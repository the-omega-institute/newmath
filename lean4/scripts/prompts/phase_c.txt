## Step 3: Paper marker registration
After `lake build` passes:
1. For each new Lean target, find the relevant paper theorem/definition site in `papers/bedc/parts/`. Decide which marker macro to use:
   - `\leanchecked{X}` ŌĆö the primary canonical implementation of a paper claim. ONE per paper site.
   - `\leanvariant{X}` ŌĆö alternative implementation of the SAME paper claim already covered by an existing `\leanchecked` at this site: typeclass-binder wrappers (e.g. `concrete_*` vs `internalized_*`), weakened conclusions (`_hsame_only`), strengthened conclusions (`_with_tokens`), projections, or pure forwarding lemmas (`exact other_theorem`). MAY be multiple per primary marker.
   - `\leanstmt{X}` ŌĆö statement-only / structure-field reference (no proof yet).
   - `\leandef{X}` ŌĆö `def` or `inductive` definition reference.
   Escape underscores as `\_` in target names.
2. Decision rule for `\leanchecked` vs `\leanvariant` at an existing site:
   - If no `\leanchecked` exists yet at this site ŌåÆ use `\leanchecked{X}` and pick the strongest / most general version as canonical.
   - If a `\leanchecked` already exists and your new target is a wrapper / variant / projection / weaker form of the same claim ŌåÆ use `\leanvariant{X}`.
   - If your new target is a genuinely different paper claim (different theorem statement) ŌåÆ look for its own paper site; do not stack under an unrelated theorem.
3. Keep the paper in current-state form only: no version markers, no `vNNN-`, no "increment", no "patch", no "frozen" vocabulary.

The `bedc_ci.py audit` will fail if any `\leanchecked{X}` / `\leanvariant{X}` / `\leanstmt{X}` / `\leandef{X}` references X not present in `lean4/BEDC/`.
