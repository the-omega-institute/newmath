## Deletion-aware rule (critical)

If one conflict side DELETED a `theorem` / `def` / `lemma` declaration (or a paper `\begin` ... `\end` theorem-block with a `\label`) and the other side kept it or modified it, **prefer the deletion**: drop the declaration in the resolved file. Rationale:

- A deletion in BASE_BRANCH (the "incoming" side during merge) is a deliberate cleanup — typically removing a duplicate-conclusion theorem caught by `phase_d_lint.py`, or a stuck dup label caught by `bedc_ci.py audit`.
- The "ours" side (this round) is older code dispatched before the deletion landed. Its version is stale.
- Re-adding the deleted declaration via "keep both" recreates the original problem (lake build dup-decl, `SHALLOW GROWTH PATTERN` lint, `duplicate paper labels` audit) and the next round will hit the same wall.

To detect this case: when one side has the declaration and the other has `<<<<<<<` immediately followed by content NOT containing that declaration name, the side without the name is the deletion. Trust the deletion.

If the deletion appears semantically wrong (e.g. the declaration is referenced elsewhere in your round's diff), re-implement the referenced uses inline using the surviving theorem's content rather than re-adding the deleted declaration.

## Lineage / option narrowing rule (CRITICAL)

If a conflict touches a `\origin{{...}}` line or a `VALID_ORIGINS`/option-set in source code, **and one side has fewer valid choices** than the other (e.g. BASE has `{{"human", "ai"}}` while ours has `{{"human", "ai", "ai-composite"}}`), **always prefer the side with FEWER choices**.
