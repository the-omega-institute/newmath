## HARD GATE — LOGIC-MINIMIZATION DISCIPLINE

Before drafting the LaTeX block, compress the result through these project
rules.  Do not print a separate audit table; reflect the answers in the
statement, hypotheses, proof, or adjacent remarks:

- No Object Without Eliminator: a new object-level name must expose its
  eliminator, observation, or inversion use.
- No Existence Without Witness: an existence claim must identify the witness
  source, bounded search, compactness extraction, or nonconstructive boundary.
- No Bridge Without CutRank: a bridge must say whether the intermediate
  carrier can be eliminated or must remain primitive.
- No Theorem Without Axiom Budget: use the weakest visible resource level
  among finite witness, schedule, rate/modulus, finite cover, countable
  construction, or choice-like surface.
- No Descent Without Certificate: Lean, Automath, Rule110, or FKernel descent
  must be stated as blocked unless a checkable certificate is named.
- No Equality Without Equality Kind: distinguish definitional equality,
  propositional equality, isomorphism, equivalence, bisimulation, substrate
  mirror, and heuristic correspondence.
- No Context Collapse: class, description, quotient, and completion objects
  stay contextual until their eliminator is used.
- No Hidden Resource Copy: copied histories, observations, oracle output, and
  certificates must carry provenance.
- No Irrelevant Dependency: remove dependencies that the proof does not use.
- No Oracle For Invertible Work: deterministic definition expansion,
  normalization, schema checks, label dedup, and obligation splitting are
  local gate work, not oracle evidence.
- No Merge Without Interpretation Kind: cross-system movement must be a
  definition, conservative extension, interpretation, embedding, projection,
  substrate mirror, or heuristic translation.
- No Completion Without Rate/Modulus Surface: limit, completion, compactness,

  tail-bound surfaces.
