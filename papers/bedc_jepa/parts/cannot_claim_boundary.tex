\section{Cannot-Claim Boundary}
\label{sec:cannot-claim-boundary}

The present paper does not claim that BEDC-JEPA has been trained at scale.  It
does not claim autonomous intelligence, natural-language semantic grounding,
or full pixel-control world modeling.  It does not claim that a post-hoc
QualityEvidenceEnvelope is already a new model objective.

The current artifact claims a completed small-world comparison, a grid-pixel
learned-transition benchmark, a MiniGrid-style visual planning benchmark, a
two-object counterfactual intervention benchmark, and seeded four-slot and
six-slot distractor benchmarks.  It does not claim a completed public JEPA
implementation comparison, a robotics benchmark result, or a general
autonomous-intelligence result.  The object-interaction evidence is still
rendered slot-world evidence rather than natural video or robot control.  The
LeJEPA-style Gaussian-OU runner remains a calibration baseline.  These
experiments are the first evidence packets for BEDC-JEPA, not a complete model
family.

\paragraph{Required next evidence.}
The next manuscript-bearing evidence must include:
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
1. & \text{a public-scale planning benchmark},\\
2. & \text{an external baseline implementation},\\
3. & \text{a clean artifact package for review},\\
4. & \text{a contact note with exact reproducible commands},\\
5. & \text{a public pixel-world benchmark comparison},\\
6. & \text{a public object-interaction benchmark with natural clutter or control}.
\end{array}
$$
The four-system ablation remains mandatory in each setting because it tests
whether BEDC-JEPA is more than a reporting layer.

\paragraph{Name boundary.}
Operational names in this paper are not free natural-language labels.  A name
is admissible only when it is attached to a distinction with a declared source,
test surface, stability condition, and gap policy.  The first such name is the
inside-boundary distinction of the boundary-gated OU world.
