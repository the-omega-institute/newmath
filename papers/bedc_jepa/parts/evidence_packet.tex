\section{Evidence Packet}
\label{sec:evidence-packet}

The local evidence is stored outside this paper as a lab artifact.  The paper
records only the current contact and its boundary.

\paragraph{Artifact pointers.}
The current protocol writes two boundary-envelope artifacts in
\path{papers/bedc-quality-lab/reports/}:
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
\text{Envelope} & \texttt{bedc\_jepa\_boundary\_envelope.json},\\
\text{Report} & \texttt{bedc\_jepa\_boundary\_report.md}.
\end{array}
$$
The four-system experiment writes:
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
\text{JSON} & \texttt{bedc\_jepa\_four\_system\_experiment.json},\\
\text{Torch objective} & \texttt{bedc\_jepa\_torch\_objective.json},\\
\text{Manifest} & \texttt{bedc\_jepa\_artifact\_manifest.json},\\
\text{Public MiniGrid probe} & \texttt{bedc\_jepa\_public\_minigrid\_probe.json}.
\end{array}
$$
The envelope is a lab-local evidence boundary.  It is not a Python definition
of BEDC NameCert, closurestatus, origin, or Lean verification status.

\paragraph{Source specification.}
The source is \texttt{boundary-gated-ou-world}.  Its latent dimension is $2$,
with sample count $768$, $\rho=0.84$, radius $1.0$, and gap width $0.14$.
The nonlinear observation is \texttt{sinusoidal-parabolic-shear}.

\paragraph{Pattern specification.}
The pattern is \texttt{latent-plus-distinction-plus-gap}.  It asks for three
readbacks from the same world process: continuous state recovery, operational
inside-boundary distinction, and boundary gap detection.

\paragraph{Ledger gaps.}
The current boundary envelope records three gaps:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\texttt{no-gradient-bedc-jepa-training-yet},\\
\texttt{one-boundary-family},\\
\texttt{no-gap-aware-planning-rollout-yet}.
\end{array}
$$
The torch objective artifact closes the no-gradient gap for the boundary
readback setting under a frozen-latent protocol: the latent encoder has the
same recovery score in the latent-only and BEDC-objective systems, while the
trained distinction and gap heads lower debt and remove unlogged error.  The
four-system experiment closes part of the second gap by comparing BEDC-JEPA
readback against post-hoc baselines on radial and sinusoidal boundary
families.  It also adds a grid-pixel benchmark with a learned
action-conditioned transition model, a MiniGrid-style visual planning
benchmark, and an eight-seed two-object counterfactual contact benchmark.  The
object evidence also includes a four-slot distractor benchmark where the
declared target pair must remain separate from two non-target object slots,
with a six-seed sweep for the distractor setting.  The same target-pair
readback is also evaluated in a six-slot cluttered-object sweep with four
distractors.  The remaining gaps are broader.  The planning rollouts are still
local and synthetic rather than robot-control scale.

\paragraph{Debt items.}
The current debt items are scale debt, public-baseline debt, and artifact debt.
Scale debt means the learned-transition and object-intervention benchmarks are
small.  Public-baseline debt means the comparison is local and not yet run
against a public JEPA implementation.  Artifact debt means the current package
is a research branch rather than a cleaned external review bundle.
