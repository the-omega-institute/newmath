\section{Evaluation Contract}
\label{sec:evaluation-contract}

The empirical claim must separate latent quality from BEDC world-state
quality.  High latent recovery is necessary for the toy protocol, but it is
not the contribution by itself.  The contribution requires lower silent
failure at comparable latent recovery.

\paragraph{Systems.}
The evaluation compares four systems:
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
S_0 & \text{latent JEPA-style prediction only},\\
S_1 & \text{latent prediction with a post-hoc distinction probe},\\
S_2 & \text{latent prediction with a post-hoc evidence envelope},\\
S_3 & \text{BEDC-JEPA with trained distinction and gap heads}.
\end{array}
$$
Only $S_3$ changes the learning objective.  $S_1$ and $S_2$ are necessary
controls because they test whether the result is merely a better report over
the same representation.

\paragraph{Primary outcome.}
The primary metric is unlogged error:
$$
\operatorname{UER}
=
\Pr[
  \text{critical distinction error}
  \wedge
  \text{gap head below threshold}
].
$$
The BEDC-JEPA target is:
$$
\operatorname{UER}(S_3)
<
\operatorname{UER}(S_0),\quad
\operatorname{UER}(S_1),\quad
\operatorname{UER}(S_2)
$$
under comparable latent recovery.  This metric captures the central BEDC
constraint: the model may be wrong, but it must not be confidently and
silently wrong on a declared critical distinction.

\paragraph{Current result.}
The boundary-gated OU experiment satisfies this target in the narrow declared
scope.  The four systems share the same latent recovery score, $0.926199$.
Their unlogged error rates are:
$$
\begin{array}{ll}
S_0 & 0.091146,\\
S_1 & 0.080729,\\
S_2 & 0.035156,\\
S_3 & 0.013021.
\end{array}
$$
The trained BEDC-JEPA system also improves gap AUROC from $0.845039$ to
$0.898880$ against the post-hoc BEDC report baseline, while preserving
outside-gap distinction accuracy at $0.943423$.

\paragraph{Secondary outcomes.}
Secondary measures are distinction accuracy outside the gap, gap AUROC,
false-claim rate inside the gap, certified coverage, and scope-relative debt.
The planning version additionally reports unsafe-state rate, high-gap
trajectory rate, success rate, and planning regret.

The seed sweep reports $95\%$ confidence radii for the three main deltas.  The
aggregate deltas are positive across radial and sinusoidal boundary worlds:
gap AUROC gain $0.021994 \pm 0.013291$, unlogged-error reduction
$0.022868 \pm 0.006128$, and debt reduction $0.011947 \pm 0.005406$.

The grid-pixel learned-transition benchmark adds the JEPA-style requirement
that the model act through a learned representation and learned predictor
rather than an oracle latent rollout.  It preserves high one-step transition
accuracy while keeping the same ablation contract: $S_3$ must improve over
$S_2$ on gap AUROC, unlogged error, and debt.

\paragraph{Break condition.}
The protocol fails as a BEDC-JEPA result if $S_3$ improves the report format
but does not lower unlogged error or gap-sensitive planning failures against
the post-hoc baselines.  It also fails if the gap head is only a proxy for high
prediction loss while missing the declared operational distinction boundary.
