\section{Relation to JEPA World-Model Work}
\label{sec:relation-to-jepa}

The JEPA family supplies the contact surface for this paper.  LeCun's
autonomous-intelligence program places learned world models at the center of
prediction, planning, and action \cite{lecun-ami}.  I-JEPA shows that
representation-space prediction can support semantic image representations
without reconstructing every pixel detail \cite{ijepa}.  LeWorldModel moves
the same principle toward end-to-end pixel world modeling with a compact
latent predictor and Gaussian regularization \cite{leworldmodel}.  Causal-JEPA
adds object-level latent masking so that interaction-dependent prediction
enters the learning objective \cite{causal-jepa}.

The most direct theory contact is the LeJEPA identifiability result
\cite{lejepa-world-model}.  That result asks when alignment plus Gaussian
regularization recovers latent world variables, and it connects such recovery
to latent-space planning.  BEDC-JEPA asks a different question.  Once a world
model has a latent carrier, which operational distinctions can be trained as
part of the state, and which failure surfaces must be carried by an explicit
gap head?

\paragraph{Complementarity.}
The LeJEPA question is latent recovery:
$$
\text{observation} \longrightarrow \text{recoverable latent coordinate}.
$$
The BEDC-JEPA question is ledgered world-state formation:
$$
\text{observation}
\longrightarrow
\text{latent coordinate}
+ \text{operational distinction}
+ \text{gap ledger}.
$$
The paper therefore does not claim that BEDC explains an already trained
LeJEPA representation.  It proposes a training target in which the distinction
and the gap are outputs of the world model itself.

\paragraph{Public baseline registry.}
The quality lab records a public JEPA-family baseline registry rather than
treating the baseline choice as prose.  The selected first comparison target
is V-JEPA 2-AC, because its public code is action-conditioned and therefore
closest to a world-model setting with observation/action streams.  The second
candidate is LeWorldModel with its LeJEPA-style baseline path, because it
directly targets stable end-to-end JEPA world modeling from pixels.  The
registry is currently contract-only: it fixes the expected observation/action
input, latent prediction or rollout output, and BEDC comparison fields, but it
does not claim that either public baseline has been executed in this
workspace.
The separate comparison packet is the readiness-facing artifact.  It reserves
baseline metrics for latent prediction, rollout or planning score, and reported
benchmark name, and it keeps the public JEPA gate missing until those fields
come from an executed public baseline.

\paragraph{Contact with Issue 330 and the quality lab.}
The local quality-lab material supplies an executable evidence envelope, not a
second semantic source for BEDC.  Its role is to keep the Python side honest:
source, pattern, classifier, stability setting, metrics, ledger gaps, debt
items, and artifact pointers are recorded in a reproducible packet.  BEDC
semantics remain in the paper and Lean tracks; the lab only reports the
contact values produced by the protocol.
