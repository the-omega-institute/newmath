\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The claim studied here is a code-layer claim about the standard genetic-code table, not a claim about translation dynamics or protein realization.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the $64$ codons of a three-letter alphabetic display after choosing the usual binary presentation of nucleotide positions.  The distinguished local row is the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit records $|R|=13$.  The same row is checked as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the evidence is not merely a named biological list: it is a finite local shape inside $Q_6$ whose vertices, edges, and square faces are all counted.  The biological contact used for interpretation is only the curated standard-code table, which assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  That contact permits code-label readback for this local tile; it does not by itself observe ribosomal realization, folding, energetic admissibility, cellular role, or a universal generative law.

\paragraph{Spectral and randomization checks.}
The row also carries a numerical spectral signature.  The recorded eigenvalue scalars are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the row is numerically at tolerance for the polynomial relation used by the finite computation.  A shape-randomization run with $N=2000$ samples, seed $42$, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  This gives $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$ and $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second randomization constrained by amino-acid class, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact shape matches and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed size $|M|=20$, giving $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These numbers support the statement that the observed local row is not a routine artifact of the tested null models, while still leaving the claim at the level of a finite code-table pattern.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests and module accounting.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the finite row gave hit rate $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M-T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ after adjoining the boundary.  The same $0.9841269841269841$ value is recorded as the hit rate above baseline.  By family, $5$ families were evaluated: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, and bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  The leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons of $R$ with no extras, no missing module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\

The accounting identity is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
This is the strongest finite reading of the packet: every codon in the local row belongs to one of the listed modules, and every listed module is covered by predictable or boundary behavior.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The resulting statement is deliberately narrow.  Window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and the finite checks show that this structure has nontrivial shape, spectrum, randomization, recovery, family, and module behavior.  The curated table supplies the reality contact for codon-to-label assignment only.  From these rows one may infer a code-read pattern: a local tile in $Q_6$ aligns with a distinguished group of codon labels and with robust leave-one recovery behavior.  One may not infer that DNA is forced to obey this geometry as a biological necessity.  One may not infer translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic causation, or universality.  Any such stronger assertion would require a separate external contact of the appropriate type, for example a translation experiment for translation, a structure observation for folding, or a functional assay for function.  In the absence of those contacts, the chapter records a finite code-layer boundary pattern and no higher-layer biological promotion.

\subsection{ORF Eligibility as a Contact-Limited Boundary}
\label{subsec:orf-eligibility-seed-boundary}

\paragraph{Object of the row.}
The row named \texttt{orf\_eligibility.seed.boundary} isolates one biological question: whether a finite nucleotide interval may be counted as eligible under a start/stop-codon enumeration rule.  The row does not begin with an observed translated product, a folded molecule, a biochemical assay, or a functional annotation. In this row, the carrier is therefore the pending test position itself: a start/stop-window question whose biological readout is unavailable unless an external sequence or curated-annotation interval supplies the observed endpoints.

\paragraph{Finite codon support.}
The finite codon layer used by the row is explicit.  The motif set $R$ contains exactly the thirteen codons
$\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.
