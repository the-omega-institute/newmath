\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine usage and tRNA-Leu copy-number contact}
\label{sec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record treats the leucine codon split between the CUN and UUR synonymous families as a finite organism-indexed coordinate, not as a direct molecular mechanism. The measured coordinate is the signed residual
$$
\begin{aligned}
  x_o &= \log\!\left(\frac{\#\mathrm{CUN}_o}{\#\mathrm{UUR}_o}\right),
\end{aligned}
$$
paired with an organism label $o$ and with the corresponding tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectrum. The admissible finite rows here are the three loaded organisms $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli\ K12}$. The observed log-ratio values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
  (x_{\mathrm{human}},x_{\mathrm{yeast}},x_{\mathrm{ecoli}})
  &= (1.3514980212896148,-0.24869078488829677,1.0086640520051702),\\
  (\operatorname{sgn}x_{\mathrm{human}},\operatorname{sgn}x_{\mathrm{yeast}},\operatorname{sgn}x_{\mathrm{ecoli}})
  &= (1,-1,1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not uniform across the finite organism list. The packet therefore records an organism-conditioned leucine-codon usage contrast rather than a single universal direction for CUN versus UUR usage.

\paragraph{Reality contacts.}
Two external contacts are sufficient for the bounded statistic asserted here. The first contact is a per-organism codon-usage readout: coding-sequence summaries report codon counts or frequencies for each organism. The second contact is an organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy readout: genome annotation resources report tRNA-Leu copy counts by anticodon family. Each contact is a sequence or annotation summary. Neither one observes ribosome motion, translation kinetics, protein folding, physical realizability, biological function, or a global law. Their role in this record is narrower: they allow the CUN/UUR log-ratio coordinate to be compared with the tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate after controlling for amino-acid composition through a synonymous-class null.

The verification run $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$ reports status passed for the claim identifier $\mathrm{h3.translation\_realization.cross\_organism.cun\_uur\_leu\_gate}$. This is a translation-layer usage statistic in the limited sense of codon-usage and tRNA-copy matching; it is not a claim that the ribosome-level causal process has been measured.

\paragraph{Translation-layer question.}
The biological question is not whether codon counts vary; the finite computation already supplies that coordinate-level fact.  The question is whether any residual coordinate in $B^{*}_{Q6}$ carries conditional predictive value for a translation-layer readout once amino-acid sequence and simpler codon features are controlled.  For a readout $r$ such as tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, and for a conditioning variable $Z$ containing amino-acid sequence $y$, local context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines, the intended score comparison is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),\qquad q\in B^{*}_{Q6}.
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value for at least one $q$ would mean that the residual coordinate improves the conditional readout model relative to the baseline used in the comparison.  The amino-acid quotient is essential: only residual information orthogonal to amino-acid composition is eligible to survive this test.  In particular, a model that only records an $M$-density summary is too coarse for the residual contrasts $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ and $K_{AAA}$ versus $K_{AAG}$-type distinctions, because those contrasts live inside synonymous amino-acid classes rather than at amino-acid composition level.

\paragraph{Available contacts.}
Three contact classes are relevant to the proposed test.  Per-organism codon-usage tables supply sequence-composition summaries and can support codon distribution readback, but they do not observe ribosome dynamics, protein output, folding, physical admissibility, biochemical mechanism, or a global biological law.  Organism-level tRNA gene-copy annotations supply anticodon-family spectra, including the kind of Leu-family copy-number information needed to compare $CUN$ and $UUR$ residuals, but copy number alone does not establish translation dynamics.  Organism-scoped tAI or stAI weights can provide a bounded translation-readout table, but such weights are modelled annotations and must remain separate from the residual coordinate geometry being tested.

\paragraph{Blocked statistic.}
The row $residual\_basis.m\_only\_local\_optimum.controlled\_readout\_gap$ records the current obstruction.  The contacts are of the right kinds, but they have not yet been paired into an organism-matched table containing codon usage, tRNA-copy spectra, and tAI or stAI readouts with amino-acid-composition-controlled estimates of $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$.  Therefore the absence of a positive residual survival score is not evidence against the residual basis, and the existence of nonzero residual variance is not evidence for translation realization.  The present status is a blocked null: the right measurement category has been identified, but the controlled statistic has not been computed.

The conjectural translation-realization claim would be supported if some residual coordinate $Q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ satisfies

\begin{aligned}
