\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine gate at translation scope}
\label{sec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate-translation-realization}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Packet and admissible object.}
This record concerns the leucine synonymous codon split between the CUN family and the UUR family, read only at the level of organism-indexed coding-sequence summaries and organism-indexed tRNA-Leu annotation summaries.  The carrier is the finite row consisting of a CUN-over-UUR codon-usage residual, a tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, an organism label, and an amino-acid-composition control.  The relevant distinctions are the coordinate $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, the paired tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio, the organism identity, and the synonymous-class control which prevents a raw amino-acid-composition effect from being mistaken for a translation-layer statistic.  The packet is therefore not a claim about leucine chemistry, protein folding, ribosome kinetics, or organismal fitness.  It is a bounded claim about whether the organism-level sign and magnitude of the CUN/UUR residual move with a matched tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate after the synonymous-composition guard is imposed.

\paragraph{Finite rows used.}
The checked run is identified by experiment run $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$ and experiment id $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$.  It loaded exactly three organism rows:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal{O} &= \{\mathrm{homo\_sapiens},\ \mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae},\ \mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}\},\\
n &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
For these rows the recorded CUN/UUR log-ratios were
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\ -0.24869078488829677,\ 1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with corresponding signs
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\ -1,\ +1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is explicitly not uniform across the loaded organisms.  The same finite run reports Pearson correlation $1.0$ for the paired statistic and a positive partial Pearson correlation $1.0$ after the amino-acid-composition control, again over $n=3$ rows.  The recorded status of the finite experiment is $\mathrm{passed}$.

\paragraph{Local statistical reading.}
The admissible reading is narrow.  The three-row table gives a context-dependent reversal: yeast is recorded with a negative CUN/UUR log-ratio while human and \emph{E. coli} K12 are recorded with positive ratios.  In this finite table, the tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate orders the residual in the same direction as the recorded CUN/UUR statistic, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
r_{\mathrm{Pearson}} &= 1.0,\\
r_{\mathrm{partial}\mid \mathrm{AA\ composition}} &= 1.0,\\
n &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is enough for a translation-scope organism-level contact in the limited sense of codon-usage and tRNA-gene-copy co-registration.  It is not enough to identify a mechanistic translation law.  The sign reversal is the important biological restraint: the packet does not say that CUN dominates UUR, or that UUR dominates CUN, across life.  It says that the direction is organism-dependent in the supplied rows and that, within those same rows, the controlled paired statistic is positive.

\paragraph{Contacts represented by the rows.}
The codon-usage contact is a per-organism sequence-summary contact.  It observes coding-sequence count or frequency summaries and can support a codon-usage distribution statement.  It does not observe ribosome transit, translational speed, nascent-chain behavior, folded structure, biological activity, or physical admissibility.  The tRNA-Leu contact is an organism-level genome-annotation contact.  It supplies copy-number spectra by anticodon family, but it is still an annotation summary rather than a direct molecular-dynamics or ribosome-profiling measurement.  The packet becomes interpretable only because these two contacts are paired by organism and then compared under the amino-acid-composition control.  Without that pairing, the CUN/UUR sign would remain an internal coordinate cue rather than a bounded translation-layer statistic.

\paragraph{Null alternatives and break conditions.}
Two null outcomes would have broken the packet.  First, the CUN/UUR sign could have been uniform across the measured organisms, in which case the advertised cross-organism reversal would not be present.  Second, the sign could have reversed while the controlled relation to the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio vanished.  The finite run reports neither failure: $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$, and the partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu ratio is recorded as $1.0$ for the three loaded organisms.  The support is therefore a small controlled co-variation result, not an unrestricted biological theorem.

\paragraph{What is not licensed.}
No translation mechanism follows from this row set.  The data do not show that a ribosome executes the relation, that a protein folds differently because of it, that any protein function is realized, that a sequence is physically admissible, or that the same direction must hold in every organism.  The result also does not license a raw-ratio shortcut: $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ without organism matching and amino-acid-composition control is not the claim made here.  A stronger biological statement would require a separate contact, such as ribosome-profiling evidence for translation dynamics, proteomic evidence for realized products, structural evidence for folded-state consequences, or a broader organism panel with uncertainty estimates.  This chapter records only the finite organism-level codon-usage and tRNA-copy relation supplied by the checked run.

\subsection{Residual codon topology after the amino-acid quotient}\label{subsec:residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient-translation-realization}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record asks whether a codon-level geometry can remain visible after the amino-acid sequence has already been accounted for. The object is not the raw coding sequence and not the amino-acid string alone. It is the per-organism codon-count profile projected to the amino-acid-residual basis
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6} = \{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The intended readout is conditional. For a residual coordinate $q \in B^{*}_{Q6}$ and a biological readout $r$, the comparison is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z) = J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence $y$, organismal context, and simpler codon-feature baselines. A positive value is meaningful only relative to that conditioning set; it is not a claim about an unconditional biological law.

\paragraph{Finite row contact.}
The finite contact presently available is a three-organism codon-count table: Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K12. The run identifier is $971b9c868480$. The NCBI genetic code table was loaded, with one codon-count table per organism, so the contact has organism count $3$ and row scope equal to three organism-level summaries. In that contact, the residual basis has element count $9$. Seven of those nine coordinates are nonzero above variance threshold $0.01$, giving nonzero fraction
$$
\begin{aligned}
7/9 = 0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The nine computed coordinates are $K_{AAA}$, $Arg_{AGR}$, $Ile_{AUA}$, $Leu_{CUN\_vs\_UUR}$, $Leu_{UUA\_vs\_UUG}$, $Ser_{UCR\_vs\_AGY}$, $Ser_{UCA\_vs\_UCG}$, $Thr_{ACR\_vs\_ACY}$, and $f_3$-stress pair antisymmetry. This is a finite contact, not a population estimate and not a claim about all organisms.

\paragraph{Why the amino-acid quotient matters.}
The amino-acid quotient removes the part of codon usage that merely repeats amino-acid composition. After this removal, only the residual component $q^{\perp}$ can carry evidence for codon-level organization beyond $y$. In particular, a model that records only local membership in an $M$-density or simpler codon class is too coarse for the displayed question. Such a model cannot distinguish $CUN$ from $UUR$ leucine structure, nor can it test $AAA$ against $AAG$ inside lysine. The present finite row therefore supports a sharper coordinate choice: residual basis coordinates are present and nontrivial after amino-acid composition has been removed. It does not yet show that those coordinates improve a translation-layer readout.

\paragraph{Bounded translation readback.}
The possible translation-layer readouts are $tAI$, $stAI$, ribosome occupancy, and protein abundance. For this chapter the admissible near-term test is bounded: organism-matched codon usage, tRNA copy-number spectra, and a translation-efficiency readout must be paired before a positive survival score is reported. The required support condition is at least three organisms with codon-usage tables, tRNA-Leu, tRNA-Arg, tRNA-Ile, tRNA-Ser, and tRNA-Thr copy-number data, and a readout source such as $stAI$ or ribosome-profiling-derived efficiency. The counterexample search is simple in form: if an $M$-only variable is sufficient for DNA-to-protein realization at this resolution, then every $Q \in B^{*}_{Q6}$ should satisfy
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M) \leq 0
\end{aligned}
$$
for every organism and every readout $r$ in the chosen translation set. A break occurs if some residual coordinate gives $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)>0$ in at least one organism on at least one readout, or if the leucine $CUN/UUR$ log-ratio reverses sign between organisms after amino-acid control while the $M$-density baseline does not.

\paragraph{Present status.}
The finite-row evidence is enough to say that the amino-acid-residual codon basis is computationally populated in the three-organism contact. It is also enough to keep the $M$-only sufficiency question open as a concrete test. It is not enough to announce a positive translation-realization result. The current memory records separate bridges for codon-usage summaries, tRNA gene-copy annotations, and $tAI$ or $stAI$ weights, but it does not yet contain an organism-matched controlled statistic combining them into $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$. Absence of that statistic is a blocked-null condition: it blocks both support and refutation of the $M$-only claim.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
No translation mechanism, ribosome dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, protein-output law, or universal direction follows from the nonzero residual variance. The contact only says that seven of nine residual codon coordinates are nonzero in three finite organism-level codon-count summaries after the amino-acid quotient has been taken. Any stronger statement requires a separate paired contact at the relevant layer, with the amino-acid sequence and context retained in the conditioning variables.
