\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine usage as a bounded translation-layer statistic}
\label{sec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Claim.}
This chapter records a finite organism-level test for the leucine codon pair CUN/UUR.  The biological packet is not a claim about a particular protein, a folded state, or a ribosomal trajectory.  It asks a narrower question: when coding-sequence usage is summarized by organism and paired with organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy information, does the signed residual between CUN and UUR remain constant, or does it change with the corresponding tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio after controlling for amino-acid composition?  The coordinate used here is the log-ratio
$$
\begin{aligned}
  L_o = \log\left(\frac{\#\mathrm{CUN}_o}{\#\mathrm{UUR}_o}\right),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $o$ ranges over the measured organisms.  The attached finite run loaded three organism rows: Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K12.  The observed log-ratio list was
$$
\begin{aligned}
  (1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with sign list
$$
\begin{aligned}
  (+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not uniform across the measured organisms.

\paragraph{Finite data readout.}
The validation record h3.translation\_realization.cross\_organism.cun\_uur\_leu\_gate reports experiment run 675fdddb3187 under experiment id cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation.  Its status is passed.  The scalar Pearson value recorded for the paired statistic is $1.0$.  The amino-acid-composition-controlled readout is reported as a positive partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu ratio, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
  n &= 3,\\
  r_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite table, the yeast row carries the negative CUN/UUR log-ratio while the human and E. coli K12 rows carry positive values.  The result therefore supports only the bounded statement that the CUN/UUR statistic is organism-context dependent in this three-row contact and that its signed magnitude tracks the available tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate in the recorded controlled statistic.

\paragraph{Biological interpretation.}
The evidence basis consists of two external contacts: per-organism codon-usage summaries and organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectra.  Codon usage supplies sequence-composition counts or frequencies for coding sequences.  tRNA gene-copy annotation supplies anticodon-family copy-number summaries.  These two contacts are sufficient for the finite statistic above: a signed CUN/UUR usage residual can be compared against a tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio while amino-acid composition is held in the intended synonymous-class control.  The contact does not observe elongation speed, ribosome occupancy, charging state, translation errors, or protein output.  The statistical gate is therefore a translation-layer usage contact, not a mechanistic translation assay.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
No claim of folded structure follows from these numbers.  No physical admissibility claim follows from them.  No biological function claim follows from them.  No ribosome-mediated mechanism follows from them.  No universal cross-organism law follows from the observed sign reversal or from the value $r_{\mathrm{partial}}=1.0$ at $n=3$.  The result is also not a license to use the raw log-ratio without the amino-acid-composition control as a translation claim.  A stronger conclusion would require a separate contact, for example ribosome profiling, charged tRNA abundance, direct translation-rate measurements, protein-level output, or an independently curated larger paired organism table.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
For this standalone record, the admissible conclusion is finite and local: in the three organisms loaded by experiment cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation, the CUN/UUR leucine log-ratio is not sign-uniform, with values $1.3514980212896148$, $-0.24869078488829677$, and $1.0086640520051702$, and the recorded controlled comparison with tRNA-Leu copy ratio is positive with partial Pearson value $1.0$ over $3$ rows.  This is enough to keep the CUN/UUR leucine gate as a bounded organism-level codon-usage and tRNA-copy contact.  It is not enough to promote the packet to structure, function, physical realization, mechanistic translation dynamics, or universal biological directionality.
