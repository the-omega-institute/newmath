\subsection{Residual codon topology after amino-acid quotienting}
\label{subsec:residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Question.}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate contact.}
The finite computation used for this record loaded one codon-count table for each of three organisms: human, budding yeast, and $E.\ coli$ K-12.  The NCBI genetic code was loaded in the same run. \paragraph{Survival statistic.}
For a residual coordinate $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ and a readout $r$, the proposed conditional score is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}

for every tested organism and every selected readout.  A single positive controlled score, or a controlled organism-level sign reversal in the $CUN/UUR$ leucine log-ratio that is not captured by $\Delta_M$, would break that $M$-only account for the tested readout.

\paragraph{Current status.}
The finite rows support the coordinate side of the conjecture but do not yet supply the decisive translation statistic. \paragraph{Boundary of inference.}
Nothing in the three codon-count rows proves translation realization by itself.  The rows also do not establish ribosome-mediated mechanism, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, protein-output causality, or universality across organisms.  A nonzero residual variance is a coordinate fact, not a biochemical conclusion.  Even a future positive $S_Q$ would be conditional on the chosen readout, organisms, controls, and context variables.  Promotion to folding, function, mechanism, or a general biological law would require a separate measurement contact at that higher layer.
