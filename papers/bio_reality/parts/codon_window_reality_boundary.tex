\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Boundary}
\label{sec:codon-window-six-local-tile-boundary}

\paragraph{Carrier and finite display.}
The carrier for this record is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-bit display of the standard-code codon table.  The relevant distinctions are not asserted as biochemical causes.  They are finite row distinctions: a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes inside a coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation package.  The biological contact is only the curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  Thus the readback layer is code readback, not translation realization.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}

In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is not a folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any claim beyond the code layer requires a separate reality contact whose observations live at that higher layer.

\subsection{Open-reading-frame eligibility as a boundary seed}\label{sec:orf-eligibility-seed-boundary}

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The local self-check records three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is carried across biological layers. Consequently this chapter makes the following boundary claim and no stronger one: the $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$ packet is admissible as a contact-gap probe while it lacks a curated ORF interval annotation. A separate reality contact, such as a curated sequence interval or transcript annotation, is required before the packet can count as evidence for ORF eligibility.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
No translation realization follows from the finite row. No folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, evolutionary role, expression claim, or universality claim follows from the finite row. A start--stop-window enumeration is not an observed ORF, and an observed ORF is not supplied by the BEDC coordinate alone. The only present claim is that this finite codon geometry defines a boundary test: if a later pipeline asserts ORF eligibility, translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality without attaching a separate biological contact that can test the proposed interval, then that assertion breaks the boundary recorded here.
