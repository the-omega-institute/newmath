\subsection{Window-six codon tile boundary}
\label{sec:codon-window-six-local-tile-boundary}

\paragraph{Finite display.}
The packet studies the standard-code codon table through a six-bit codon display $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is a finite coordinate surface on which a curated codon-to-label table can be read as external code data.  The local object isolated by the run is a boundary tile in which a singleton label corner and a three-point label class sit inside a small codon-cube pattern. $$
\begin{aligned}

\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine gate}
\label{sec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Claim scope.}
This record treats the leucine codon split between the $\mathrm{CUN}$ family and the $\mathrm{UUR}$ family as an organism-level translation-layer statistic, not as a claim about protein output. The object under test is the paired residual consisting of the signed value $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, an organism label, and the corresponding tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio after controlling for amino-acid composition at the synonymous-class level. The admissible readout is therefore narrow: for each organism, the packet compares the external sign and magnitude of the $\mathrm{CUN}$-over-$\mathrm{UUR}$ usage imbalance with the external tRNA-Leu copy-number spectrum. The construction does not treat the codon-window coordinate itself as a biochemical mechanism, and it does not promote a codon-usage correlation into a statement about folding, physical admissibility, biological function, ribosome dynamics, or a universal law.

\paragraph{Finite rows.}
The verified run identified as $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$ loaded three organism rows: \texttt{homo\_sapiens}, \texttt{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}, and \texttt{escherichia\_coli\_k12}. On these rows, the recorded log-ratio vector was
$$
\begin{aligned}
R &= (1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with the corresponding sign vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
S &= (1,\;-1,\;1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed sign is not uniform across the three organisms. In the stored finite result, \texttt{sign\_uniform} is false, and the explicit non-uniformity is carried by the negative yeast entry between two positive entries. This is the main bounded readback: the leucine codon-family imbalance reverses at organism scale in the finite table rather than preserving one global direction.

\paragraph{Paired tRNA-Leu statistic.}
The same run reports a positive paired association with the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio after the stated amino-acid composition control. The ordinary Pearson value is recorded as
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho &= 1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the controlled statistic is recorded as
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0, & n &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite three-row table, the support condition is therefore met in the restricted sense intended here: the $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ sign is not constant, and the magnitude agrees perfectly with the stored tRNA-Leu ratio statistic after the recorded control. Since the row count is $n=3$, this is best read as a sharply scoped realization contact rather than as a population-level biological law. No p-value, confidence interval, lambda parameter, module-classification scalar, hit-rate statistic, or larger row count is present in the supplied finite record.

\paragraph{Contacts and measurement meaning.}
The codon-usage contact supplies per-organism coding-sequence summaries, and the tRNA contact supplies organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy annotations by anticodon family. These two contacts are sufficient for the limited comparison above: a codon-family usage imbalance can be paired with a tRNA-Leu copy-number spectrum under an amino-acid composition control. They are not sufficient for a stronger biological interpretation. Codon-usage tables summarize sequence composition and sampling choices; tRNA gene-copy tables summarize genome annotations. Neither contact directly measures elongation kinetics, ribosome occupancy, translation efficiency, protein abundance, folded conformation, molecular stability, phenotypic function, or evolutionary necessity.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The result is accepted only as a finite, organism-level translation-realization statistic for the $\mathrm{CUN}/\mathrm{UUR}$ leucine split. The accepted content is the existence, in the recorded three-organism table, of a non-uniform sign vector together with a positive controlled association to tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio. No translation mechanism follows from this alone; no folded structure follows; no physical admissibility follows; no biological function follows; no ribosome-mediated process follows; and no universal cross-organism rule follows. Any such stronger statement would require a separate contact of the appropriate kind, for example ribosome profiling for dynamics, proteomics for realized abundance, structural measurements for folding, or functional assays for phenotype.
