\subsection{Window-six codon local tile boundary}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The window-six codon record treats the standard-code codon table as a code-read contact on a finite carrier, not as a mechanism. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The BEDC distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data. The external contact is only the code-label table: it supports codon-label readback and local split checks, but it does not itself observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any necessity that DNA must obey this geometry.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite null sampled in the coordinate audit, the observed shape was rare. In $N=2000$ sampled trials with seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampler recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported probabilities should be read as finite-row empirical rates under that concrete sampling procedure. A second amino-acid-class-preserving null had $N=2000$, observed $|M|=20$, no exact shape match, and $7$ lower-tail samples with size at most the observed $M$ size. This gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class, exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class, lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support a local non-randomness reading for the finite coordinate row. They do not supply a biochemical cause.
