\subsection{Amino-Acid-Residual Codon Topology as a Translation-Layer Conjecture}
\label{sec:bior-residual-basis-q6-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The object considered here is the organism-indexed codon-count profile after projection away from amino-acid composition and onto the residual codon basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$.  The basis contains nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}

&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The conjecture is not that codon topology alone gives a protein-level conclusion.  It is the narrower assertion that, if the $Q6$ codon topology has a translation-layer role beyond the amino-acid string, then at least one coordinate in $B^{*}_{Q6}$ should retain positive conditional survival against a translation readout after amino-acid composition and simpler codon summaries have already been included in the conditioning data.

\paragraph{Finite contact already obtained.}
The current finite row is a sequence-composition contact, not yet a translation readout contact.  It loaded per-organism codon-count tables for exactly three organisms: \textit{Homo sapiens}, \textit{Saccharomyces cerevisiae}, and \textit{Escherichia coli} K12.  The NCBI genetic code was loaded in the same run, and the contact scope was one codon-count table per organism.  The computed amino-acid-residual basis had nine elements, and seven of the nine had nonzero residual variance above the stated threshold $0.01$.  Thus the finite row records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#B^{*}_{Q6} &= 9,\\
\#\{q \in B^{*}_{Q6}: \operatorname{Var}(q)>0.01\} &= 7,\\
\frac{7}{9} &= 0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is enough to show that the amino-acid quotient has not erased all codon-level structure in the sampled organisms.  It is not enough to claim ribosome behavior, protein output, folding, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal biological rule.

\paragraph{Conditional survival statistic.}
For a translation-layer readout $r$, the intended statistic compares a baseline model that already sees the amino-acid sequence $y$, context $c$, and simpler codon summaries against a model that also sees one residual coordinate $q \in B^{*}_{Q6}$.  With $Z=(y,c,\text{simpler codon baselines})$, the readback is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,q) = J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value means that the residual coordinate improves the conditional description of the chosen readout inside the tested dataset.  The meaning is local to the organism set, the readout, the conditioning variables, and the loss functional $J$.  It does not license a broader biological conclusion unless the corresponding biological layer is separately measured.

\paragraph{Why the amino-acid quotient matters.}
Amino-acid composition is the first obstruction to interpretation.  If a codon statistic merely tracks the amino-acid sequence, then it is not evidence for codon-residual participation in translation realization.  The residual basis is designed to retain only codon distinctions that survive after amino-acid identity is accounted for.  Examples include $AAA/AAG$ inside lysine, $AUA$ inside isoleucine, the $CUN/UUR$ leucine split, the $UUA/UUG$ leucine split, two serine residuals, and a threonine residual.  These are precisely the kinds of distinctions that an amino-acid-only model cannot see.

\paragraph{M-density insufficiency as a bounded probe.}
The associated probe asks whether an $M$-density baseline can account for all translation-layer readouts once amino-acid composition is controlled.  The break condition is finite and falsifiable: in at least one organism and at least one readout among tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, some $q \in B^{*}_{Q6}$ must have
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M,q)>0,
\end{aligned}
$$
or the $CUN/UUR$ leucine log-ratio must show an organism-dependent sign change after amino-acid control that the $\Delta_M$ baseline does not capture.  Until a paired organism-matched table supplies codon usage, tRNA copy number, and a translation readout in the same controlled statistic, the result remains blocked at this layer rather than weakly supported.

\paragraph{Available contacts and current gap.}
Three contact classes are relevant.  Per-organism codon-usage tables supply sequence-composition readback.  Organism-level tRNA gene-copy annotations supply anticodon-family copy-number spectra, including the leucine families needed for $CUN/UUR$ and $UUA/UUG$ tests.  tAI or stAI resources can supply bounded codon-adaptation weights, provided those weights are kept separate from the coordinate construction itself.  The present finite row has the codon-count side and the computed residual coordinates, but it does not yet contain an organism-matched controlled statistic joining codon usage, tRNA copy number, and tAI or stAI weights.  Therefore the absence of a positive $S_Q$ value is not evidence against the conjecture; the statistic has not yet been computed.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The verified finite facts say only that a nontrivial residual codon basis was computed from three organism-level codon-count contacts and that seven of nine residual coordinates were nonzero above threshold in that finite contact.  No conclusion about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, biochemical mechanism, or universality follows from that finite sequence-composition result alone.  A translation claim requires a separate translation-layer contact, and a higher-layer claim requires its own separate measurement.
