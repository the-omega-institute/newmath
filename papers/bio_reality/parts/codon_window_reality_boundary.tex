\subsection{Window-six codon tile boundary}
\label{subsec:codon-window6-local-tile-boundary}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The claim concerns a six-coordinate display of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, where each codon is read as a six-bit point after choosing the usual two-bit nucleotide presentation.  The observed object is not a molecular mechanism but a finite label table: each one of the $64$ codons is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label by the curated standard code.  The question tested here is whether this finite table exposes a local boundary tile in the window-six coordinate system.  The record is intentionally restricted to the code-read layer.  It may compare codon labels, local faces, closures, spectra, and relations inside $Q_6$, but it does not by itself assert translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  Its observed fact is the codon-to-label assignment for the standard genetic code.  Its resolution is codon label readback.  It can test code-layer questions such as whether a local codon tile has a label split, and it cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function.  Thus the table is treated as a finite contact for labels only.  A codon label sequence obtained from this contact is still a sequence of code labels; it is not a folded protein, not a realized translation process, and not a functional assay.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The finite row identifies a distinguished relation set $R$ of size $13$.  Its members are
$$
\begin{aligned}

&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check reports $|R|=13$.  The punctured-cube check reports the same list and gives the face-vector

\begin{aligned}

This is the local geometric content of the row: the selected codons form a thirteen-vertex relation with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces in the window-six cube representation.  The codons are not being promoted to a global biological law.  They are a finite local pattern against the standard-code labels.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}

Nothing in this contact observes translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any future claim at one of those higher layers must attach a separate contact that directly observes that layer.  Until then, the chapter records a code-level local boundary pattern and blocks promotion beyond that level.

\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine gate}
\label{sec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Record.}
This chapter records an organism-level contact for the leucine codon split between the CUN family and the UUR family. The packet is not a statement about a folded protein, a cellular function, or a universal rule of the genetic code. It is a bounded statement about paired sequence summaries: for each organism in the finite table, one reads the codon-usage imbalance
$\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ and compares it with the organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectrum, after controlling for amino-acid composition at the synonymous-class level. The biological object is therefore the paired row consisting of a codon-usage residual, a tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio, and an organism label.

\paragraph{Finite table.}
The checked run named $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$ loaded three organism rows:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal{O} = \{&\mathrm{homo\_sapiens},\ \mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae},\ \mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
For these rows the reported log-ratio vector was
$$
\begin{aligned}
L = (1.3514980212896148,\ -0.24869078488829677,\ 1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
and the corresponding sign vector was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{sgn}(L) = (1,\ -1,\ 1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not constant across the three measured organisms. The run records $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$ and reports the non-uniformity directly rather than forcing the rows into a single direction of effect.

\paragraph{Controlled association.}
The same finite run reports a positive organism-level association between the CUN/UUR log-ratio and the tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate. The ordinary Pearson value in the row summary is
$$
\begin{aligned}

The finite evidence therefore supports the following local statement: in this three-row table, the CUN/UUR leucine split is not sign-uniform across organisms, and its signed magnitude is positively aligned with the organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate after the stated composition control.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The readout is deliberately narrow. Codon-usage tables summarize coding-sequence composition, and tRNA gene-copy counts summarize genome annotations by anticodon family. These contacts are sufficient for a paired organism-level statistic, but they are not direct observations of elongation kinetics, ribosome occupancy, protein abundance, folding, molecular stability, organismal phenotype, or biological function. The negative yeast-side sign in the finite vector is not treated as a contradiction of the positive human and bacterial signs; it is the reason the packet is formulated as a context-sensitive cross-organism statistic rather than a single-direction law.

\paragraph{Null boundaries.}
A raw CUN/UUR sign alone is not admitted as a translation-layer conclusion. The admissible object is the controlled paired statistic using codon usage and tRNA-Leu copy-number data together. Identifier spelling, codon labels, or a standard genetic-code table cannot repair a missing paired organism measurement. In particular, no ribosome-mediated translation mechanism, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or universal cross-organism law follows from this chapter unless another contact supplies that separate observation. The present chapter only records the finite three-organism relation between $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, organism labels, amino-acid-composition control, and tRNA-Leu copy-ratio readback.

\subsection{Amino-Acid-Residual Codon Topology as a Translation-Layer Test}
\label{sec:bior-reality-residual-basis-q6-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Object under test.}
This record concerns the codon-level residual basis obtained after quotienting out amino-acid identity.  The carrier is the per-organism table of coding-sequence codon counts, projected onto the residual coordinates
$B^{*}_{Q6}$ that remain visible only after amino-acid composition has already been held fixed.  The finite row named
$h3.\mathrm{codon\_usage\_topology}.\mathrm{residual\_basis}.q6\_\mathrm{topology\_after\_aa\_quotient}$ reports a computed residual list with $9$ entries:
$K_{AAA}$, $Arg_{AGR}$, $Ile_{AUA}$, $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$, $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$, $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$, $Thr_{ACR/ACY}$, and an antisymmetric third-position stress pair.  The same finite row records that these coordinates were computed from $3$ organism-level codon-count contacts, namely human, budding yeast, and \emph{E. coli} K-12.  This is a small finite contact set, not a population estimate.

\paragraph{Residual coordinates.}
The intended residual question is not whether organisms have different amino-acid compositions.  That effect is already placed in the conditioning variable $Z$.  The question is whether, after fixing amino-acid sequence information $y$ together with local context $c$ and simpler codon-feature baselines, one of the residual codon-topology coordinates carries additional translation-layer signal.  For a readout $r$ chosen from codon-adaptation weights, ribosome-occupancy measurements, or protein-abundance measurements, the score to be estimated is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r \mid Z) = J_0(Z) - J_Q(Z,q),\qquad q \in B^{*}_{Q6}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $J_0$ denotes the baseline loss using the controlled variables alone, while $J_Q$ denotes the corresponding loss after admitting one residual coordinate $q$.  A positive value is only a conditional survival result for that coordinate, readout, organismal scope, and control set.  It is not a statement about a universal law of codon usage.

\paragraph{Finite contact already present.}
The finite row supplies three concrete scalar facts relevant to the residual geometry.  First, the codon-count contact loaded the NCBI genetic code and used $3$ within-organism codon-count tables.  Second, the residual basis size was $9$.  Third, using the row's variance threshold $0.01$, $7$ of the $9$ residual coordinates were nonzero across the finite contact, giving a nonzero fraction
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers establish that the quotient did not collapse the proposed residual basis to a vacuous object in the finite contact.  They do not yet establish translation realization, because the available row is a codon-count residual computation rather than an organism-matched controlled readout analysis against tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance.

\paragraph{Translation-layer conjecture.}
The conjecture is that Q6 codon topology participates in DNA-to-protein realization beyond amino-acid sequence precisely when at least one residual coordinate in $B^{*}_{Q6}$ improves a translation-layer readout after the amino-acid quotient and simpler codon baselines have been imposed.  In the controlled notation, the positive case is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\exists q \in B^{*}_{Q6}\;\exists r\;\exists O\quad S_Q(r \mid Z,O)>0,
\end{aligned}
$$
where $O$ is an organism in the tested contact and $r$ is an externally supplied translation-layer readout.  A sharper comparison includes an $M$-density baseline, written $\Delta_M$, and asks whether any residual coordinate survives after that baseline has already been admitted:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r \mid Z,\Delta_M)>0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This distinction matters because an $M$-only model can detect a local codon-density optimum but cannot by itself test residual contrasts such as $CUN/UUR$ or $AAA/AAG$ after amino-acid composition has been controlled.

\paragraph{Blocked statistic.}
The present finite contact is sufficient to show that the residual coordinate list is nontrivial in the three-organism codon-count row, but it is not sufficient to score the translation-realization conjecture.  The missing statistic is an organism-matched table joining codon usage, relevant tRNA-copy spectra, and a bounded translation readout such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance.  Without that paired table, the absence of a positive survival score is not evidence against the conjecture; it means the controlled test has not yet been performed.  The current status is therefore a blocked-null boundary: the residual geometry gives a concrete coordinate system and a finite nonzero signal in codon-count space, while the translation-layer claim remains unscored.

\paragraph{Local interpretation boundary.}
The allowed conclusion is narrow.  The row shows that the amino-acid-residual codon basis has $9$ named coordinates and that $7$ of them are nonzero above threshold $0.01$ in a finite three-organism codon-count contact.  No claim about ribosome-mediated mechanism, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, protein-output causality, or universal codon law follows from these facts.  Any such stronger statement would require a separate measurement contact at the appropriate layer.  In particular, nonzero residual variance in codon counts is not by itself translation efficiency, folded structure, function, or mechanism.  A future positive value of $S_Q$ would still be conditional on the readout, organism set, control variables, and data source used to estimate it.
