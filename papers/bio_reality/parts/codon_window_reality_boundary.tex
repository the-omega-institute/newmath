\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
This chapter deliberately stops at the code-read layer.  The curated standard-code table is a reality contact for label assignment, not a contact for translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not protein structure, and the finite tile is not a global law of biology.  The positive statement is local and finite: in the six-bit codon cube, the thirteen-codon boundary row $R$, its punctured-cube face data $(13,16,4)$, the spectral values above, the null-model statistics, and the leave-one readback tests jointly support a code-layer local tile.  Any promotion from this code-layer statement to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate reality contact supplying that higher-layer evidence.
