\subsection{Window-six local tile boundary for the standard code}\label{sec:codon-window-six-local-tile-boundary}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of codons with two binary coordinates per nucleotide position.  The biological contact is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop, and it supplies no direct observation of translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The BEDC-side datum is therefore a local code-read pattern, not a global biological law.  In this contact layer, a tile may be described by its finite coordinate boundary and by its induced labels, but the labels remain external reality contacts supplied by the curated code table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row has cardinality $13$.  The row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured local cube, the observed face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite-row content behind the phrase local tile: the row is a specific thirteen-codon subset of $Q_6$ with four two-dimensional faces and sixteen edge incidences, not an all-codon rule and not an assertion about all genetic systems.

\paragraph{Spectral profile.}
The finite coordinate split also has a measured spectral profile.  The row-side value is $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, the complementary measured value is $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, and the radial parameter is $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported cubic residual is numerically negligible at

The spectral readout is still a code-layer invariant of the displayed coordinate split.  It does not by itself observe a ribosome, an aminoacyl-tRNA process, a folded protein, or a phenotype.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite null sample for the $M$-shape statistic, $N=2000$ sampled tables were generated with seed $42$.  Producing those distinct samples required $57377$ attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid classes used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  In that model the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and $7$ samples lay in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The corresponding finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the local shape is a real finite-row signal relative to the tested nulls.  They do not promote the local tile to a necessity theorem for biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback tests.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, $5$ families were evaluated: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$ above baseline, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each had hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  The leave-one-codon-out test had anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen boundary codons are exhausted by six named modules.  The $agr$ module is $\{AGA,AGG\}$, the $ar\_lys$ module is $\{AAA\}$, the $cun$ module is $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, the $ile\_met$ module is $\{AUA\}$, the $ssn$ module is $\{UCA,UUA\}$, and the $stop\_trp$ module is $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$.  The completion row reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Hence every codon in the boundary row is accounted for by a predictable-or-boundary module, and the module partition is complete at this finite level.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The ready claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table.  The evidence is the explicit thirteen-codon row, the punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, the measured spectral profile, the low finite-null shape probabilities, the leave-one readback performance, and the complete module classification.  The claim stops exactly at code readback.  No translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this chapter unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer observation.

\subsection{ORF Eligibility Seed Boundary}\label{sec:biorality-orf-eligibility-seed-boundary}

This BioReality record is a seed-level boundary packet for open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration. Its carrier is not an observed transcript, not an annotated genomic interval, and not a translation event. The carrier is the finite codon-side packet that awaits reality contact. The biological object is therefore deliberately narrow: it records what a start/stop-window eligibility test would need before it could become a biological claim. No such contact is attached here. The contact list is empty, and the packet records no sequence database row, transcript evidence row, curated ORF interval, protein product, structural assay, functional assay, or organismal condition.

\paragraph{Motif row.}
The first finite row is the motif-compression row $R$. It is exactly the following set of $13$ codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
