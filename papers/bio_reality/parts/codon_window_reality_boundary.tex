\paragraph{Packet scope.}

The intended readback is therefore narrow: the organism label, the CUN-over-UUR log residual, and the tRNA-Leu copy-number spectrum are compared as external annotations of coding usage.  The biological question is whether the CUN/UUR sign is stable across organisms or instead changes with the tRNA-Leu copy-number context.

\paragraph{Finite row.}

\ell_{\mathrm{yeast}} &= -0.24869078488829677,\\

\end{aligned}
$$

The same row also reports an ordinary Pearson value of $1.0$ for the paired statistic and a positive partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu ratio after the stated control, with sample size $n=3$ and partial Pearson value $1.0$.

\paragraph{Statistical reading.}
The finite evidence supports only a bounded organism-level statement: in this three-row table, the CUN/UUR residual changes sign across organisms, and the recorded controlled statistic is perfectly positive against the tRNA-Leu ratio.  Written as a compact row summary, the verified data say

It is also enough to keep the packet in the translation-realization layer only in the restricted sense of codon-usage and tRNA-copy contact: the measured quantities are sequence-annotation and genome-annotation summaries, not direct observations of translation dynamics.

\paragraph{Reality contacts.}
Two external contact classes are used.  The codon-usage contact supplies per-organism codon-count or codon-frequency summaries over coding sequences. \subsection{Residual codon topology after amino-acid quotient}
\label{sec:residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Question.}
The claim studied here is not that codon usage alone proves a realized translation mechanism.  The narrower question is whether the codon-level $Q_6$ topology leaves any amino-acid-residual coordinate that can still improve a translation-layer readout after the amino-acid sequence has already been held fixed.  The carrier is the per-organism table of CDS codon counts, projected onto the residual basis $B^{*}_{Q_6}$ after quotienting by amino-acid identity.  Thus the relevant coordinates are not amino-acid composition itself, but synonymous residual directions left over after the amino-acid class has been removed.

\paragraph{Finite residual basis.}
The finite run used for this record computed a nine-element residual list
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q_6} = \{&K_{AAA},\ \operatorname{Arg}_{AGR},\ \operatorname{Ile}_{AUA},\ \operatorname{Leu}_{CUN/UUR},\ \operatorname{Leu}_{UUA/UUG},\\
&\operatorname{Ser}_{UCR/AGY},\ \operatorname{Ser}_{UCA/UCG},\ \operatorname{Thr}_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress}_{\mathrm{antisym}}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified finite contact reports $9$ residual basis elements, with $7$ nonzero elements under the stated variance cutoff $0.01$.  Equivalently, the observed nonzero fraction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The computation was made on three organism-level codon-count tables: \emph{Homo sapiens}, \emph{Saccharomyces cerevisiae}, and \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12.  The run identifier is $971b9c868480$, and the reported status is passed.  This is a finite contact over three named organisms, not an estimate of a population law over all organisms.

\paragraph{Residual survival statistic.}
For a readout $r$ such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, the proposed residual test compares a baseline model against a model augmented by one coordinate $q\in B^{*}_{Q_6}$.  With $Z$ denoting the controlled context, including the amino-acid sequence $y$, organismal context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines, the statistic is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,q)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value means only that the residual coordinate improves the bounded readout under the chosen controls.  It does not by itself identify a biochemical mechanism, a universal rule, or any claim about folded structure or function.  The amino-acid quotient is essential: if $y$ is not included in $Z$, then the statistic can confuse amino-acid composition with synonymous codon residuals.

\paragraph{Why an $M$-only baseline is insufficient for this question.}
An $M$-density baseline can describe a local codon-usage optimum, but it cannot by itself distinguish all residual directions in $B^{*}_{Q_6}$.  In particular, an $M$-only description does not test the $CUN/UUR$ leucine split or the $AAA/AAG$ lysine split as residual coordinates after amino-acid composition has been held fixed.  The operational counterexample search is therefore: fit the controlled $M$-baseline first, then ask whether adding any one of the nine residual coordinates increases the survival score.  The bounded null is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M,q)\leq 0\quad\text{for every }q\in B^{*}_{Q_6}
\end{aligned}
$$
for each organism and each readout under the matched controls.  A break of this null would be one organism-readout-coordinate instance with $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M,q)>0$, or an amino-acid-controlled sign reversal in a residual such as the leucine $CUN/UUR$ log-ratio that the $M$-baseline fails to explain.

\paragraph{Reality contacts and current gap.}
The available contact classes are per-organism codon-usage summaries, organism-level tRNA gene-copy spectra, and organism-scoped tAI or stAI codon adaptation weights.  These are suitable ingredients for a translation-layer readout only when paired by organism and analyzed under amino-acid-composition control.  At present, the finite run establishes that the residual basis was computed on three organism-level codon-count contacts and that $7$ of $9$ residual directions were nonzero above the $0.01$ variance threshold.  It does not yet supply a paired table containing codon usage, tRNA copy number, and tAI or stAI weights with the controlled estimates $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M,q)$.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The observed residual variance is a coordinate-level result.  It does not assert ribosome-mediated translation realization from codon counts alone.  It does not assert protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or a universal biological law.  A nonzero residual coordinate is not enough to promote the record beyond the codon-usage layer.  Translation-level survival would require a separate matched readout contact, and higher biological claims would require their own independent contacts rather than inheritance from the residual geometry.

\paragraph{Chapter verdict.}
The finite evidence supports the existence of nontrivial amino-acid-residual $Q_6$ codon coordinates in the three-organism contact set.  The translation-realization claim remains a controlled conjecture: it becomes testable by pairing those residual coordinates with organism-matched tAI, stAI, ribosome-occupancy, or protein-abundance readouts and then asking whether any coordinate in $B^{*}_{Q_6}$ has positive controlled survival over the $M$-baseline.  The present chapter therefore records a concrete residual coordinate basis and a precise blocked-null boundary, not a completed translation, structure, function, mechanism, or universality result.
