\subsection{Window-six local codon tile boundary}
\label{sec:codon-window-six-local-tile-boundary}

\paragraph{Finite display.}
The object considered here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display. The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, while the observed labels are taken from the curated standard genetic-code table. The point of the construction is deliberately narrow: it asks whether the window-six coordinates expose a local boundary pattern in the code-label layer. The contact does not observe translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism. It observes only the assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.

The distinguished residual row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit records $|R|=13$. The same row is also recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not just a list of exceptional codons. It has a coordinate shape: thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four local two-faces inside the window-six codon cube.

\paragraph{Local spectral signature.}
The row carries a numerical spectrum attached to the local coordinate relation. The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the cubic-polynomial residual, so the displayed floating-point row is within numerical tolerance of the intended finite spectral relation. The number $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ is also the recorded spectral radius in tolerance. These numbers are used only as finite-row descriptors of the local code-label tile; they do not identify a biochemical force, a kinetic process, or a physical selection principle.

\paragraph{Shape rarity checks.}
The local shape was compared against sampled alternatives. In the shape-significance run, $N=2000$ distinct samples were requested, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. These figures support the claim that the displayed local shape is not a common outcome under that finite sampling procedure.

A second null check preserved amino-acid class information. In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$. The exact-shape count was $0$, so the exact-shape rate was recorded as $0.0$. The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving a lower-tail probability $0.0035$. The explicit list of the $M$ codons is not part of the finite row supplied here, so this chapter uses only the recorded size and the associated sampling statistics.

\paragraph{Recovery and row tests.}
Leave-one-code-out evaluation tested whether the local row continues to recover held-out code-table targets. The audit records $23$ tables evaluated and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The recorded rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$. At the family level, five families were evaluated: alternative yeast had rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial/plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had rate $1.0$.

Leave-one-codon-out evaluation gives the complementary local-row picture. The anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the high-closure coverage count was $8$, and the predictable count was $8$. The recovery rate was $1.0$, and the recorded above-threshold recovery rate was also $1.0$. This separates the finite row into codons recovered by the local closure behavior and codons located at the boundary of the tile.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are partitioned into six named modules. The complete assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit records $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. Moreover, every module has a predictable or boundary status. This is the finite sense in which the local tile is organized: the observed row is exhausted by six label-sensitive modules, and no codon in the row remains outside the classification.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The result is a code-read statement. The curated standard-code table supplies the codon-to-label contact, and the window-six coordinate row supplies a local tile geometry around that contact. The supported claim is that the thirteen-codon row has a punctured-cube shape, a recorded local spectrum, significant rarity under the stated finite null checks, high leave-one-code-out recovery, complete leave-one-codon-out module assignment, and a boundary/predictable split compatible with the curated labels.

Nothing stronger follows from this chapter. The data do not show that DNA or RNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity. They do not show translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Amino-acid label readback remains a code-layer observation. Any promotion to folding, function, admissibility, or mechanism would require a separate contact that directly observes that higher layer.

\subsection{Open-Reading-Frame Eligibility as a Boundary Seed}
\label{sec:bior-orf-eligibility-seed-boundary}

\paragraph{Question isolated by the row.}
The record concerns only the first admissibility question for an open reading frame: whether a finite nucleotide window has been classified by explicit start/stop enumeration. In this packet the answer is deliberately conservative.  No sequence, transcript, or curated interval contact is attached to the row, so the coordinate information remains a test specification for ORF eligibility rather than an observed ORF assertion.

The row therefore separates coordinate regularity from biological realization: the quotient can organize the finite codon data, but it cannot certify that a proposed interval is present in a biological source.

\paragraph{Observed start/stop-window input.}
The ORF-window experiment loaded one external window dataset, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$25$.  It reports $1$ input window and $1$ classified window under start/stop enumeration. The classification is therefore limited to the statement that the supplied finite window has been sorted by the start/stop rule.  It does not assert translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, mechanism, function, phenotype, conservation, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The self-check records $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  The two probe conditions agree with that verdict.  The first says that a start/stop-window seed must remain a boundary test unless an external sequence, transcript, or curated interval supplies the observed biological interval.  The second says that a claimed ORF eligibility record breaks if no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation can test the proposed interval.  In this row the contact list is empty, and the required contact type is $\mathrm{curated.orf.interval.annotation}$.  Consequently the admissible conclusion is local and negative in scope: the packet records how ORF eligibility would be checked once the missing interval contact is supplied, while withholding the claim that such eligibility has already been biologically established.

\paragraph{Local non-promotion rule.}
All upward biological readings are blocked at this layer.  Start and stop codons in a finite window can delimit a candidate interval for enumeration, but they do not establish that a ribosome translates it, that a stable product exists, that the product has a fold, that the fold is physically admissible in a cellular setting, that a mechanism acts through it, or that it has biological function.  Those stronger statements require their own contacts and their own finite checks.  The present row is therefore a disciplined seed for a future reality test: it preserves the exact finite codon and quotient data, records the one-window start/stop classification, and refuses every biological promotion not licensed by a separate contact.
