The claim is intentionally placed at the code-read layer: it says that window-six coordinates expose a small boundary-tile pattern whose labels can be checked against the standard table.  It does not say that the coordinate pattern is translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal biological law.

\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine usage gate}\label{sec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate-translation-realization}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
This record isolates a narrow organism-level statistic for the leucine codon split between the CUN family and the UUR family.  The carrier is the residual codon-usage coordinate for leucine, paired with organism labels and with an external tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate.  The row is not a protein claim and not a ribosome-dynamics claim.  It asks whether the signed quantity $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ changes across organisms and whether that change is aligned with the corresponding organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio after controlling for amino-acid composition within synonymous leucine usage.

\paragraph{Observed finite rows.}
The checked run is $675\mathrm{fdddb3187}$, with status passed.  The organisms loaded in the finite table are homo sapiens, saccharomyces cerevisiae, and escherichia coli K12, so the effective row count for the paired statistic is $n=3$.  The recorded CUN/UUR log-ratios are
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with signs
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not uniform across the measured organisms.  The scalar cross-organism Pearson value recorded for the paired statistic is $1.0$, and the amino-acid-composition-controlled partial Pearson value against the tRNA-Leu ratio is also $1.0$ on $n=3$ rows.  These numbers are finite-row readbacks only: no $p$-value, confidence interval, lambda parameter, hit-rate, or larger module-classification statistic is recorded in the packet.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The admissible conclusion is that, in this finite table, the CUN/UUR leucine split is context-dependent rather than sign-uniform, and its signed magnitude is positively aligned with the paired tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate under the recorded control.  The negative yeast row prevents a universal same-sign reading, while the two positive rows prevent a simple all-negative reversal.  The result therefore functions as a bounded translation-layer usage statistic: it connects codon-usage summaries and tRNA-gene-copy summaries for the same organism labels, but only at the level of curated sequence and genome-annotation contacts.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The packet does not establish ribosome-mediated translation dynamics.  It does not establish folding, three-dimensional structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or a universal biological law.  A raw CUN/UUR sign by itself is not a translation claim, and a tRNA-Leu copy-number table by itself is not a protein-output measurement.  The only supported contact here is the paired, organism-level statistic linking codon-usage imbalance to a tRNA-Leu gene-copy coordinate under the stated amino-acid-composition control.  Any stronger statement would require a separate contact, for example ribosome profiling for translation dynamics, protein measurements for output, structural assays for folding, or functional assays for biological role.

\paragraph{Mismatch pressure.}
The null records explain why the packet remains carefully bounded.  Separate codon-usage and tRNA-copy contact types are not enough unless they are assembled into an organism-matched statistic.  Identifier spelling is also not biological evidence: normalized packet names only route the data, while mixed-case biological notation such as tRNA-Leu remains descriptive text unless backed by the appropriate external table.  A standard code table can confirm codon labels, but it cannot repair the missing paired statistic and cannot promote a codon-label readback to organism-level translation evidence.  In the present finite run, that paired statistic is supplied for three organisms, so the row is usable for the bounded codon-usage/tRNA-copy claim and for no stronger layer.
