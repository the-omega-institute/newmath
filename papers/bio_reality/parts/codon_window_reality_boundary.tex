\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR Leu gate}\label{subsec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Claim packet.}

The finite object is an organism-indexed residual coordinate: for each organism in the row set, the statistic records the signed quantity $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ together with an independently curated tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio and an amino-acid-composition control. It is a bounded cross-organism codon-usage question: whether the CUN-versus-UUR residual changes sign across organisms and whether its magnitude tracks the available tRNA-Leu copy-number contrast after controlling for the amino-acid-composition background.

\paragraph{Finite rows.}

The loaded organism rows are $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli\ K12}$, so the row count for the organism-level statistic is $n=3$. The supported statement is narrow: in this three-organism table, the CUN/UUR leucine codon split is a real organism-indexed codon-usage residual, and its recorded sign and magnitude are compatible with the paired tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate after amino-acid-composition control. \subsection{Residual Q6 Topology After the Amino-Acid Quotient}
\label{sec:bioreality-residual-basis-q6-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record isolates the part of codon topology that remains after amino-acid identity has already been fixed.  The carrier is the per-organism codon-count table projected to the amino-acid-residual basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$.  The intended biological question is not whether codon counts alone prove an effect on protein production.  It is narrower: after conditioning on the amino-acid sequence $y$ and on simpler codon-feature baselines, can any residual coordinate in $B^{*}_{Q6}$ improve a translation-layer readout such as $tAI$, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance?

\paragraph{Residual basis.}
The finite basis used here has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified finite computation reports all nine elements as computed, with the contact explicitly marked as finite rather than a population estimate.  In the cross-organism codon-count contact, three organisms were loaded: \textit{Homo sapiens}, \textit{Saccharomyces cerevisiae}, and \textit{Escherichia coli} K-12.  The NCBI genetic code was loaded, and the within-organism resolution was one codon-count table per organism.  At variance threshold $0.01$, seven of the nine residual coordinates were nonzero across the finite contact, giving nonzero fraction
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This number is a coordinate-level observation about the residual basis on the three available organism tables.  It is not an estimate of prevalence in life as a whole.

\paragraph{Survival statistic.}
For a residual coordinate $q \in B^{*}_{Q6}$ and a readout $r$, the proposed statistic compares a baseline score with a score in which $q$ is made available after the required controls:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z)=J_{0}(Z)-J_{Q}(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence $y$, local or organismal context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines.  A positive value of $S_{Q}(r\mid Z)$ would mean that the residual coordinate improves the bounded readout under that conditioning scheme.  The statistic must be interpreted coordinate by coordinate and organism by organism; a positive value for one row would not by itself imply a general biological rule.

\paragraph{Control against an $M$-only explanation.}
The associated counterexample probe asks whether an $M$-density baseline is sufficient.  Its null form is that, for every organism, every tested readout in $\{tAI,\text{ ribosome occupancy},\text{ protein abundance}\}$, and every $Q \in B^{*}_{Q6}$, adding the residual coordinate to an $M$-density baseline does not improve the survival score:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z,\Delta_{M})\leq 0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The break condition is correspondingly finite and testable: some $Q$ in $B^{*}_{Q6}$ has $S_{Q}(r\mid Z,\Delta_{M})>0$ in at least one organism on at least one readout, or the $CUN/UUR$ leucine log-ratio reverses sign between organisms after amino-acid-composition control while $\Delta_{M}$ does not explain the reversal.  This is why the basis includes both coarse synonymous-family contrasts such as $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ and finer residual contrasts such as $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$, and $Thr_{ACR/ACY}$.

\paragraph{Reality contacts.}
The current contact set separates three kinds of finite evidence.  First, per-organism codon-usage resources give codon-count or frequency summaries for coding sequences; these support the residual-coordinate computation but do not observe ribosome dynamics.  Second, organism-level tRNA gene-copy annotations can report anticodon-family spectra, including leucine copy-number structure; these are genome-annotation summaries, not direct measurements of protein output.  Third, organism-scoped $tAI$ or $stAI$ weights can supply a bounded translation-layer readout, provided they are kept distinct from the residual geometry itself.  These contacts jointly define the kind of paired table that would be needed for the survival statistic, but the present finite memory does not yet contain an organism-matched codon-usage, tRNA-copy, and $tAI$ or $stAI$ table with the controlled estimates already computed.

\paragraph{Verified finite facts.}
The finite run identified as experiment run $971b9c868480$ passed for the claim identifier \texttt{h3.codon\_usage\_topology.residual\_basis.q6\_topology\_after\_aa\_quotient}.  Its experiment label was \texttt{residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation}.  The verified facts establish that the nine-coordinate residual basis was computed, that three organism codon-count contacts were loaded, and that seven residual coordinates were nonzero at threshold $0.01$.  They also explicitly withhold stronger biological readings: the result does not assert ribosome-mediated realization from codon-count residuals alone, does not assert protein folding or structural order, does not assert biochemical mechanism, and does not promote residual signs to universal directionality.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
This section therefore records a constrained conjectural target.  The residual geometry is a reason to ask for a controlled readout; it is not itself evidence for translation efficiency, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or universality.  None of those conclusions follows unless a separate contact supplies the relevant observation at the matching layer.  In particular, a nonzero residual coordinate only says that synonymous codon topology leaves a measurable coordinate after the amino-acid quotient in the finite organism set.  A positive controlled survival score would still have to be computed against amino-acid composition, context, and simpler codon-feature baselines before the translation-realization conjecture could be supported.
