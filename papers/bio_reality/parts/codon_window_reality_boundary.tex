\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine gate}
\label{sec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record treats the leucine codon split between the CUN family and the UUR family as a bounded organism-level statistic. \paragraph{Finite row set.}
The checked row set contains three organisms:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal O=\{&\mathrm{homo\_sapiens},\ \mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae},\ \mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}

$$
\begin{aligned}

$$

\paragraph{What is supported.}
The result supports only the following narrow statement. \paragraph{Controls and contacts.}
Two contacts are active.  The codon-usage contact supplies per-organism coding-sequence summaries; it has resolution at the level of organism codon-count or frequency readback. \paragraph{Controlled survival question.}
The intended readout statistic is conditional.  For a residual coordinate $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ and a readout $r$ such as tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, the score is to compare a baseline model against a model that is additionally allowed to use $q$:
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$

The row therefore defines a testable translation-layer question, but the finite contact described above has not yet supplied the paired organism-matched statistic.

An $M$-only description can summarize a local optimum inside a codon-usage geometry, but it cannot test contrasts that disappear unless the amino-acid-residual coordinates are explicitly retained.  In particular, the contrasts $CUN/UUR$ for leucine and $AAA/AAG$ for lysine are not testable by merely reporting $M$-membership or $M$-density. This is the reason the present conjecture is stated in terms of $B^{*}_{Q6}$ rather than in terms of codon usage as a single undifferentiated scalar.
