\paragraph{Scope boundary.} The conclusion is therefore deliberately narrow. The window-six display exposes a local boundary tile in the standard-code label table. The finite evidence includes a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row, a stable spectral signature, significant shape tests, leave-one readback performance, and a complete module assignment. None of this is a demonstration of translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Those claims would require separate observational contacts of the appropriate type. In particular, amino-acid label readback is not protein folding, and a codon-coordinate tile is not a global biological law. The present chapter records a code-read phenomenon: curated codon labels meet a finite local geometry, and the meeting is strong enough to be measured, but not strong enough to cross into higher biological layers.

\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR Leu gate}\label{subsec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate-translation-realization}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite organism row.}
This record treats the leucine codon split between the CUN family and the UUR family as an organism-level measurement problem rather than as a free-standing biological law.  The carrier is a paired residual: one coordinate records the sign and size of $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, another records the corresponding tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, and the organism label keeps the comparison from collapsing different biological contexts into a single pooled statistic.  Amino-acid composition is part of the admissibility condition, because a raw codon ratio is not enough to support even a bounded coding-layer contact.  The recorded run loaded three organism rows, namely homo sapiens, saccharomyces cerevisiae, and escherichia coli k12, under experiment id $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$.

\paragraph{Measured signs and correlation.}
For the three loaded rows, the observed CUN/UUR log-ratio list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R &= (1.3514980212896148,\ -0.24869078488829677,\ 1.0086640520051702),\\
\operatorname{sgn}(R) &= (1,\ -1,\ 1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not uniform across organisms.  The same finite run reports $\operatorname{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$ and records a positive organism-matched association with the tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate.  The ordinary Pearson value is $1.0$, and the amino-acid-composition-controlled partial Pearson entry is
$$
\begin{aligned}
n &= 3,\\
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite table, the supported statement is therefore narrow: the CUN/UUR sign reverses across the measured organisms, and the observed magnitude/sign coordinate is aligned with the tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio after the stated synonymous-class control.  The module is classified only at the organism-level codon-usage and tRNA-copy contact layer; no protein-output, folding, or organismal phenotype row is present in this datum.

\paragraph{Admissible readout.}
The admissible readout is the per-organism comparison between codon-usage summaries and genome-annotation summaries for tRNA-Leu copy number.  Codon-usage tables contribute sequence-composition counts or frequencies for coding regions.  tRNA-copy annotations contribute organism-level counts by anticodon family.  Their intersection permits a finite paired statistic for the Leu module, but only after the organism label and amino-acid-composition control are retained.  Removing those coordinates would turn the row into an uninterpretable raw ratio, since a single sign for $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ does not by itself distinguish codon bias, sampling composition, lineage context, or tRNA-copy structure.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The finite result does not establish a ribosome-mediated mechanism.  It also does not establish folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal direction of the CUN/UUR relation.  In particular, the sign reversal is reported as context-dependent: the row supports a bounded cross-organism statistic, not a general rule that one leucine codon family dominates the other in every organism.  A stronger claim would require a different contact, such as organism-matched ribosome profiling, direct translation-efficiency measurements, protein-output assays, or structural and functional experiments.  Without such a separate contact, the chapter records only the controlled codon-usage/tRNA-copy relation for the three finite organism rows.

\paragraph{Audit verdict.}
The run status is $\mathrm{passed}$ for claim id $\mathrm{h3.translation\_realization.cross\_organism.cun\_uur\_leu\_gate}$ with run id $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$.  The evidence is enough for a ready finite-row statement because the table contains organism identities, measured log-ratios, sign non-uniformity, the positive controlled partial-correlation scalar, and explicit non-promotion notes excluding folding, function, physical admissibility, mechanism, and universality.  The small row count remains an important limitation: $n=3$ is suitable for registering a bounded reality contact and for motivating follow-up organisms, but it is not sufficient for a broad biological law.

\subsection{Residual Q6 Codon Coordinates After Amino-Acid Quotienting}\label{subsec:residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient-translation-realization}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate record.}
This record isolates a codon-level residual question after amino-acid sequence has already been retained as a control variable. The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table, projected to the amino-acid-residual coordinate family $B^*_{Q6}$. The finite row used here is not a population estimate: it consists of one codon-count table for each of three organisms, namely \emph{Homo sapiens}, \emph{Saccharomyces cerevisiae}, and \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12. The genetic-code parsing step records that the NCBI genetic code was loaded, and the contact scope is exactly these three organism-level tables. Thus the mathematical object being tested is a finite residual coordinate system over observed codon-count summaries, not a universal biological distribution.

\paragraph{Residual basis.}
The computed residual basis has $9$ named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress pair antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite computation reports $9$ elements in this list, with the flag that the list is computed from finite contact rather than inferred as a population law. After quotienting by amino-acid composition, $7$ of the $9$ residual coordinates are nonzero at variance threshold $0.01$, giving nonzero fraction
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the first point at which the residual question becomes non-vacuous: amino-acid identity does not erase every codon-level Q6 distinction in the three-table contact. The nonzero count is only a coordinate fact about the finite codon-count rows. It does not yet say that any residual coordinate is used by the cell during protein production, nor that it changes abundance, folding, activity, or fitness.

\paragraph{Controlled survival question.}
The intended readout is a conditional survival score for each residual coordinate $q\in B^*_{Q6}$. With $r$ a bounded organism-scoped readout such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, and with $Z$ including amino-acid sequence $y$, local context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines, the proposed statistic is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value $S_Q(r\mid Z)>0$ would mean only that, in the specified organism and readout, the residual coordinate improves the controlled score relative to the chosen baseline. The sharper counterfactual version compares against an $M$-density baseline: for every $q\in B^*_{Q6}$, one tests whether $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)\leq 0$ remains true. If this inequality fails for some organism and readout, then the residual basis has detected information not available to an $M$-only local optimum. In particular, an $M$-only description cannot by itself test contrasts such as $CUN/UUR$ for leucine or $AAA/AAG$ for lysine when the relevant question is posed after amino-acid quotienting.

\paragraph{Reality contacts and present gap.}
Three kinds of organism-level contact are admissible for this question. Codon-usage tables provide the finite sequence-composition rows. tRNA gene-copy annotations provide anticodon-family spectra, including the leucine, arginine, isoleucine, serine, and threonine families relevant to the residual list. tAI or stAI resources provide bounded codon-adaptation readouts that may be used as translation-layer measurements only when kept separate from the coordinate construction. The present finite row contains the codon-count residual computation, but it does not yet contain a paired organism-matched table combining codon usage, tRNA-copy spectra, and tAI or stAI values into controlled estimates of $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$. Therefore the correct local status is blocked at the controlled-readout boundary: absence of the paired statistic is not negative evidence for the residual coordinates, and it is not positive evidence for translation realization.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The verified finite facts establish a narrow mathematical statement: across the three named organisms, the amino-acid-residual Q6 coordinate family is computable and nontrivial, with $7$ nonzero residual coordinates out of $9$ at threshold $0.01$. This supports using $B^*_{Q6}$ as a candidate residual design matrix for later organism-matched readout tests. It does not establish a ribosome-mediated process, a protein-output effect, a folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or a universal biological law. Those claims require separate contacts that directly measure the corresponding layer. In this chapter the residual basis is therefore treated as a finite coordinate cue whose translation-level relevance remains conditional on future controlled readout evidence.
