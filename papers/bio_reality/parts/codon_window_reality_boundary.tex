\subsection{Residual Codon Topology After the Amino-Acid Quotient}
\label{sec:residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record asks whether a codon-level topology that is invisible after taking amino-acid sequence alone can still leave a bounded trace in translation-layer measurements. The carrier is the per-organism table of coding-sequence codon counts, projected onto the amino-acid-residual basis $B^*_{Q6}$. The projection removes the amino-acid sequence contribution and keeps only synonymous directions that can differ between coding sequences with the same translated chain. The nine tracked coordinates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $K_{AAA}$ separates $AAA$ from the lysine synonym class, $Arg_{AGR}$ isolates the $AGR$ arginine branch, $Ile_{AUA}$ records the nonstandard isoleucine branch, the two leucine coordinates distinguish $CUN$ against $UUR$ and $UUA$ against $UUG$, the two serine coordinates distinguish $UCR$ against $AGY$ and $UCA$ against $UCG$, the threonine coordinate separates $ACR$ against $ACY$, and the last coordinate is an antisymmetric third-position stress pair.

\paragraph{Finite contact already obtained.}
The finite computation used three organism-scoped codon-count contacts: $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli}\ K12$. The genetic-code table was loaded, one codon-count table was used per organism, and the computation reported the nine named residual coordinates above. In this finite contact, $7$ of the $9$ coordinates were nonzero above the stated variance threshold $0.01$, so the observed nonzero fraction was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is not a population estimate and it is not a biological law. It says only that, for these three organism rows, the amino-acid quotient does not collapse the full $B^*_{Q6}$ residual geometry to zero. The finite row therefore establishes a real codon-count residual object, but it does not by itself establish a translation process, a ribosome mechanism, protein output, folding, physical admissibility, cellular function, or universality.

\paragraph{Survival statistic.}
For a readout $r$ such as tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, the bounded test is a conditional survival score. Let $Z$ contain the amino-acid sequence $y$, local sequence context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines. For a residual coordinate $q\in B^*_{Q6}$, define
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,q)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $J_0$ is the baseline loss and $J_Q$ is the loss after adding the residual coordinate. Positive survival means that the residual coordinate improves the readout model after the amino-acid sequence and simpler codon covariates have already been accounted for:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,q)>0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The conjectural translation-realization claim is therefore not that codon topology automatically controls translation, but that at least one amino-acid-residual coordinate may carry additional predictive information for a bounded translation-layer readout once the quotient by amino-acid composition has been enforced.

\paragraph{Why the $M$-only baseline is insufficient as a test.}
An $M$-density summary can be a useful local codon feature, but it cannot express every distinction in $B^*_{Q6}$. In particular, a model that sees only $M$-membership cannot test the $CUN/UUR$ leucine contrast or the $AAA/AAG$ lysine contrast as residual coordinates after amino-acid control. The controlled comparison must therefore use an $M$-density baseline $\Delta_M$ and then ask whether any residual coordinate still improves the readout:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M,q)>0
\end{aligned}
$$
for at least one organism, readout, and coordinate $q\in B^*_{Q6}$. Equivalently, the $M$-only null says that every such residual addition has nonpositive survival:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M,q)\leq 0
\quad\text{for all }q\in B^*_{Q6}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The existing finite codon-count result breaks neither side of this null, because the paired controlled statistic has not yet been computed.

\paragraph{Reality contacts and present obstruction.}
Three contact classes are relevant to the translation-layer test. Per-organism codon-usage tables can supply the residual codon coordinates. Organism-level tRNA gene-copy annotations can supply anticodon-family spectra, including leucine, arginine, isoleucine, serine, and threonine copy-number structure. tAI or stAI resources can supply a bounded codon-adaptation readout. These contacts are individually appropriate for a translation-layer readback, but the present row keeps them separated: it does not yet contain an organism-matched table combining codon usage, tRNA copy number, and tAI or stAI weights with amino-acid-composition-controlled estimates of $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M,q)$.

\paragraph{Current status.}
The finite row supports the internal residual-basis premise: among $9$ named amino-acid-residual coordinates, $7$ are nonzero in a three-organism codon-count contact at threshold $0.01$, with nonzero fraction $0.7777777777777778$. It does not support the stronger translation-realization claim yet. The next admissible finite test is to assemble organism-matched codon-usage, tRNA-copy, and tAI or stAI data, fit the amino-acid-controlled baseline, add $\Delta_M$, and then estimate the sign and magnitude of $S_Q$ for each of the nine residual coordinates. Until such a statistic exists, the record remains a constrained conjecture with a blocked null rather than positive evidence for translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or a universal biological rule.
