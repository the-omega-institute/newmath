\subsection{Residual Q6 Codon Topology After Amino-Acid Quotient}
\label{sec:residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Object.}
This record isolates a codon-level residual question that remains after amino-acid identity has already been fixed. The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table, projected away from amino-acid composition and then read in the amino-acid-residual basis $B^*_{Q6}$. The basis used here has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These coordinates are not amino-acid substitutions. They are synonym-level residual directions: for example, $K_{AAA}$ separates $AAA$ from the lysine quotient class, while $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ separates the four-codon $CUN$ leucine block from the $UUR$ leucine block after leucine itself is already held fixed. The biological question is whether any such residual coordinate survives as a translation-layer readout once amino-acid sequence and simpler codon features are controlled.

\paragraph{Finite contact already available.}
A finite computation has been made on three organism-scoped codon-count tables: human, budding yeast, and \emph{E. coli} K-12. The loaded contact has $3$ organisms, one codon-count table per organism, and the NCBI genetic code table present. In this finite row, the residual basis contains $9$ elements and $7$ of them have nonzero residual variance above threshold $0.01$. Thus the observed nonzero fraction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The elements explicitly computed in the finite contact are $K_{AAA}$, $Arg_{AGR}$, $Ile_{AUA}$, $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$, $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$, $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$, $Thr_{ACR/ACY}$, and the antisymmetric $f_3$-stress pair coordinate. This establishes a bounded empirical fact about codon-count residual diversity across the three listed organisms. It does not yet establish that the residual directions are used by the translation apparatus.

\paragraph{Conditional survival statistic.}
The intended readout is organism-scoped and conditional. For a residual basis element $q\in B^*_{Q6}$ and a translation-layer readout $r$, the score is read as a comparison between a baseline model and the same baseline augmented by $q$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)&=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),\\
Z&=(y,c,\Delta_M,\text{simpler codon-feature baselines}).
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $y$ denotes amino-acid sequence, $c$ denotes local or organismal context, and $\Delta_M$ denotes the local $M$-density baseline. The readout $r$ may be a bounded tAI or stAI weight, a ribosome-occupancy statistic, or a protein-abundance statistic, provided it is matched to the same organism and kept distinct from the coordinate construction itself. A positive value $S_Q(r\mid Z)>0$ would mean that the coordinate $q$ improves the conditional readout after amino-acid composition and the $M$-baseline have already been accounted for. It would not mean that $q$ is universally favorable, nor that the coordinate alone has biological force.

\paragraph{Why the $M$ baseline is insufficient for this test.}
The $M$-density coordinate is a local codon-feature summary. It can describe a local optimum in a reduced coordinate system, but it cannot distinguish several residual contrasts in $B^*_{Q6}$. In particular, an $M$-only model cannot test the leucine split $CUN/UUR$ or the lysine split $AAA/AAG$ as residual questions after amino-acid quotienting. The falsifying probe is therefore not whether $M$ has signal by itself. The relevant question is whether every residual coordinate satisfies
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)\leq 0
\end{aligned}
$$
for every tested organism and every admissible readout. A single controlled organism-readout pair with $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)>0$ for some $q\in B^*_{Q6}$ would break the $M$-only explanation at this layer. A sign reversal in the $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ log-ratio between organisms, after amino-acid-composition control, would also pressure the $M$-only account if the $M$ baseline fails to track the same reversal.

\paragraph{Contacts and present gap.}
Three contact classes are relevant. Per-organism codon-usage tables supply sequence-composition summaries. tRNA gene-copy annotations supply organism-level anticodon-family spectra, including leucine anticodon families when available. tAI or stAI resources supply modeled codon-adaptation weights that may be used as bounded translation-layer readouts. These contacts have the right types, but the present row does not yet contain an organism-matched table joining codon usage, tRNA copy number, and tAI or stAI weights into controlled estimates of $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$. Therefore the current status is a blocked null rather than a negative result. The absence of the paired statistic is not evidence that the residual basis fails; it only says that the translation-layer test has not yet been executed.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite computation above supports only the existence and nontriviality of residual codon-count coordinates in the three-organism contact. It does not assert ribosome-mediated translation realization. It does not imply folding, physical admissibility, molecular function, biochemical mechanism, protein-output causality, or a universal biological law. It also does not assign a universal sign to any residual basis element. Any such stronger statement would require a separate contact at the appropriate layer, such as organism-matched ribosome profiling, abundance measurements, or a controlled physical or functional assay. Within this chapter, the only live conjecture is the bounded one: after amino-acid sequence and simpler codon-feature baselines are controlled, at least one residual coordinate in $B^*_{Q6}$ may improve a translation-layer readout in at least one organism.
