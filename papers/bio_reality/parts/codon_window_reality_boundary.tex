\paragraph{Finite contact already established.}
The finite computation associated with the residual-basis contact loaded the standard genetic-code table and three organism-scoped codon-count tables: human, budding yeast, and \emph{E. coli} K-12. \paragraph{Translation-layer question.}
The conjectural claim is that the residual basis may participate in DNA-to-protein realization beyond amino-acid sequence.  For a readout $r$ such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, and for a control bundle $Z$ containing at least the amino-acid sequence $y$, organism or local context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines, the relevant comparison is a survival score of the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),\qquad q\in B^*_{Q6}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $J_0(Z)$ denotes the baseline loss or description cost without the residual coordinate, while $J_Q(Z,q)$ denotes the corresponding score after the candidate residual coordinate $q$ has been admitted.  A positive value $S_Q(r\mid Z)>0$ means only that the coordinate improves that bounded readout under those controls.  It does not mean that the coordinate is universally biological, mechanistic, structural, or functional.

\paragraph{Why the amino-acid quotient matters.}
The amino-acid quotient is essential because codons first determine amino-acid sequence, and amino-acid sequence is already an overwhelmingly large biological variable.  A residual claim that does not control for $y$ is not a residual claim.  In the present formulation, a coordinate can survive only if it lies outside the amino-acid-composition explanation.  This is why the coordinate $K_{AAA}$ is interpreted against the synonymous alternative for lysine, why $Arg_{AGR}$ is interpreted inside the arginine codon family, and why the two leucine coordinates distinguish $CUN$ versus $UUR$ and $UUA$ versus $UUG$ rather than merely measuring leucine abundance.  The same logic applies to the serine and threonine coordinates and to the third-position stress direction.

\paragraph{M-density is not an adequate substitute.}
A model that records only a local $M$-density or simpler codon-feature baseline cannot test all of the residual distinctions above.  In particular, it cannot directly test the $CUN/UUR$ leucine contrast or the $AAA/AAG$ lysine contrast after the amino-acid quotient has been imposed.  The empirical task is therefore not to ask whether codon usage varies.  It is to ask whether adding one of the nine residual coordinates to an already controlled baseline improves a translation-layer readout.  The counterexample search is consequently stated as
$$
\begin{aligned}
&\text{for every organism, readout }r,\text{ and coordinate }Q\in B^*_{Q6},\\
&S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)\leq 0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the conjecture broken by any organism-readout-coordinate triple for which the controlled score is positive, or by a controlled sign reversal in the leucine $CUN/UUR$ log-ratio that is not captured by the $M$-baseline.

\paragraph{Contacts that can and cannot decide the conjecture.}
Per-organism codon-usage tables can decide whether the residual coordinates are present in sequence composition, and the finite three-organism computation has already done so at the level of codon counts.  Organism-level tRNA gene-copy spectra can provide a second contact by anticodon family, especially for leucine, arginine, isoleucine, serine, and threonine.  Organism-scoped tAI or stAI weights can provide a bounded translation-layer readout.  However, these contacts must be paired in the same organism and analyzed under the same controls before they decide the translation-realization conjecture.  Separate existence of codon counts, tRNA-copy annotations, and tAI or stAI resources is not yet the same as a controlled statistic.

\paragraph{Current blocked statistic.}

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
Nothing in this chapter asserts that a nonzero residual coordinate causes translation efficiency, ribosome occupancy, protein abundance, protein folding, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal biological rule.
