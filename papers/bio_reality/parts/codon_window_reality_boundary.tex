\subsection{Window-six local codon tile boundary}\label{sec:codon-window6-local-tile-boundary}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite row claim is a code-read claim about the standard genetic-code table viewed through a six-bit codon display. Let $Q_6$ denote the six-coordinate codon cube obtained by reading a codon as a finite binary coordinate object. The BEDC carrier is not the biochemical translation apparatus; it is the finite display $Q_6$ together with a local tile face, a singleton label corner, a three-point label class, and the induced coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data. The biological contact is only the curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The claim is that this contact supports a local boundary-tile readback at the code layer, while blocking promotion to translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$

The row is interpreted only as a local code-label boundary: it is allowed to organize codon-label readback, but it is not allowed to speak for the biological realization of translation or for any downstream physical or functional property.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table as a codon-label bridge. It observes that a standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. Consequently, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law. Both probes are therefore negative guards around a finite symbolic construction. They license the sentence: this packet is a boundary probe for ORF eligibility. They do not license the sentence: this packet observes an ORF.
