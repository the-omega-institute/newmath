\subsection{Cross-organism CUN--UUR leucine gate}
\label{sec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Record.}
This record treats the leucine synonymous split between the CUN and UUR codon families as an organism-indexed usage coordinate, not as a free-standing biological mechanism.  The finite datum is a paired row for each organism in which the measured quantity is the signed log-ratio $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, compared with an organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio after controlling the amino-acid-composition background within synonymous classes.  The attached run is $675\mathrm{f}\mathrm{dddb}3187$, with status $\mathrm{passed}$, for the claim identifier $\mathrm{h3.translation\_realization.cross\_organism.cun\_uur\_leu\_gate}$.  The three loaded organisms are Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K12, so the realized row count is $n=3$.

\paragraph{Finite measurements.}
The observed log-ratio list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with corresponding sign list
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the CUN--UUR preference is not sign-uniform across the three loaded organisms.  The run reports $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$ and records the non-uniformity as the finite sign pattern above, rather than as a universal direction for leucine codon usage.  The same run reports a Pearson correlation value of $1.0$ for the paired statistic made available by the table, and a positive partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu ratio, after the stated amino-acid-composition control, of
$$
\begin{aligned}
r_{\mathrm{partial}} = 1.0, \qquad n=3.
\end{aligned}
$$
No p-value, confidence interval, bootstrap interval, or independent replication count is recorded in this row.  The numerical result is therefore a finite exact readback of the present table, not a population-level inference.

\paragraph{Biological interpretation.}
The admissible interpretation is deliberately narrow.  The record says that, in the three loaded organism rows, the sign and magnitude of $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ are compatible with the paired tRNA-Leu copy-ratio statistic under the supplied amino-acid-composition control.  The human, yeast, and bacterial rows are enough to defeat the simpler hypothesis that the CUN--UUR sign is forced to be the same in every measured organism.  They also provide a bounded positive association in the available paired table.  This is a translation-layer usage-and-copy-number contact only in the sense that codon usage and tRNA gene-copy spectra are both part of the cellular translation apparatus as externally tabulated genomic or sequence-derived quantities.

\paragraph{Boundary.}
The table does not observe ribosome movement, elongation kinetics, codon dwell time, protein abundance, folding, molecular structure, physical admissibility, organismal function, or a universal cross-organism law.  A sign reversal in $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ is therefore not evidence for a folded protein state, not evidence for biological function, and not evidence for a mechanism unless another contact supplies the relevant measurement.  Likewise, the positive partial correlation reported here does not by itself establish translation as a realized dynamical process; it only records that this codon-usage coordinate and the tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate align in the finite organism-matched rows supplied to the run.  Raw CUN--UUR ratios without the amino-acid-composition control would not support even this bounded statement.

\paragraph{Failure conditions.}
The packet would be broken by a larger organism-matched table in which the sign became uniform despite varying tRNA-Leu spectra, or by a controlled table in which sign reversals persisted but the partial association with tRNA-Leu copy ratio disappeared.  It would also be outside the present evidence if the only available data were separate codon-usage and tRNA-copy summaries with no organism-matched paired statistic.  Identifier spelling, mixed-case biological notation, and standard genetic-code label lookup do not repair that gap; they can name codons, but they cannot supply the controlled cross-organism statistic.  The present row is therefore ready only as a finite, bounded organism-level codon-usage result with $n=3$, log-ratio signs $(+1,-1,+1)$, Pearson value $1.0$, and controlled partial correlation $1.0$.

\subsection{Residual Codon Topology after Amino-Acid Quotienting}\label{sec:residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient-translation-realization}

\paragraph{Object of the record.}
This record concerns the organism-level codon-count residuals that remain after the amino-acid sequence has been held fixed.  The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table projected onto the amino-acid residual basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$.  The basis used here has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}

&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The intended readback is not an amino-acid composition effect.  For a basis element $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ and a readout $r$, the statistic to be tested is a conditional survival score
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z)=J_{0}(Z)-J_{Q}(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence $y$, local context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines.  A positive value means only that the residual coordinate improves the specified conditional readout in the stated organism and context; it does not by itself state a mechanism or a universal rule.

\paragraph{Finite contact already obtained.}
The finite computation loaded cross-organism codon-count contacts for three organisms: \emph{Homo sapiens}, \emph{Saccharomyces cerevisiae}, and \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12.  The NCBI genetic code was loaded, and the contact scope was one codon-count table per organism. They do not show translation efficiency, ribosome occupancy, protein abundance, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or organism-independent law.

\paragraph{Translation-layer conjecture.}
The conjectural translation-realization statement is that, once amino-acid composition is controlled, at least one element of $B^{*}_{Q6}$ has positive conditional survival on a bounded translation readout.  The readout class may be tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, but the claim is only meaningful when the readout is organism-matched to the codon table and to the relevant tRNA-copy spectrum. $$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z,\Delta_{M})\leq 0
\quad\text{for every }Q\in B^{*}_{Q6}

$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z,\Delta_{M})>0,
\end{aligned}
$$

\paragraph{Why the amino-acid quotient matters.}
The residual coordinates are designed to remove the most obvious confounder: the amino-acid sequence.  Without conditioning on $y$, a codon-count contrast can be dominated by the amino-acid composition of the proteins being encoded.  The residual basis asks a narrower question: after the encoded amino acid has already been fixed, does the remaining synonymous choice still preserve measurable contact with a translation-layer readout?  This is why the Leu contrasts $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ and $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, the Arg contrast $Arg_{AGR}$, the Ile coordinate $Ile_{AUA}$, the Lys coordinate $K_{AAA}$, the Ser contrasts $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$ and $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$, the Thr contrast $Thr_{ACR/ACY}$, and the $f_{3}$-stress antisymmetric coordinate are recorded separately rather than being folded into a single codon-richness scalar.

\paragraph{Present obstruction.}
The current contact set is enough to say that the residual basis is computationally nontrivial on three organism-level codon-count tables.  It is not enough to decide whether the residual basis survives against an $M$-only baseline on a translation readout.  The missing datum is an organism-matched statistic combining codon usage, the relevant tRNA copy-number spectra, and tAI or stAI weights, or an experimentally measured readout such as ribosome occupancy or protein abundance.  The absent statistic is not negative evidence for the residual basis.  It is a blocked null: the necessary paired table has not yet been supplied, so the comparison
$$
\begin{aligned}
J_{0}(Z,\Delta_{M})\quad\text{versus}\quad J_{Q}(Z,\Delta_{M},q)
\end{aligned}
$$
cannot yet be evaluated.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
This chapter records a finite codon-count residual fact and a translation-layer test that remains conditional on additional paired data.  A nonzero residual coordinate in $B^{*}_{Q6}$ is not evidence of translation realization unless an independent translation readout is attached and the amino-acid-composition-controlled score is computed.  Even a positive score on tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance would remain conditional on the organism, context, baseline, and readout.  It would not establish folding, physical admissibility, protein function, biochemical mechanism, or a global biological law.  Those claims require separate contacts of the appropriate kind.
