\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine gate}\label{sec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate-translation-realization}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Record.} This record treats the leucine codon split between the CUN family and the UUR family as a small organism-level measurement problem.  The data object is not a protein, not a ribosome trace, and not a claim about a biochemical mechanism.  It is a paired readout: for each organism in the finite table, one coordinate records the sign and size of $\log(\#CUN/\#UUR)$, while the companion coordinate records the relative tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectrum available from genome annotation.  The biological question is therefore deliberately narrow: after controlling for amino-acid composition through a synonymous-codon null, does the CUN/UUR leucine usage coordinate vary with the tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate across organisms?

\paragraph{Finite row.} The checked run is identified by experiment run $675fdddb3187$ under claim key $h3.translation\_realization.cross\_organism.cun\_uur\_leu\_gate$.  It loaded exactly three organism rows: $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli\ K12}$.  The recorded CUN/UUR log-ratio vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with sign vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not uniform in the measured finite set: the yeast row reverses relative to the human and bacterial rows.  The run reports $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$ and status $\mathrm{passed}$.

\paragraph{Controlled association.} The same run reports a Pearson value of $1.0$ for the paired statistic and a positive partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu ratio after amino-acid-composition control.  The partial-correlation entry has $n=3$ and $\mathrm{partial\_pearson}=1.0$.  In the language of this chapter, the result is a finite positive alignment between a codon-usage residual and an organism-matched tRNA-Leu copy-number residual. \paragraph{Empirical contacts.} Two empirical contacts are used.  The first contact consists of per-organism codon-usage summaries, which report codon counts or frequencies over coding sequences.  The second contact consists of organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy annotations by anticodon family.  These contacts have limited resolution.  Codon-usage tables summarize sequence composition and sampling choices; tRNA gene-copy counts summarize genome annotations.  Neither contact observes ribosome motion, elongation kinetics, protein conformation, cellular function, or a general law of translation.  Their shared role here is only to support the finite organism-level comparison described above.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.} The result licenses a bounded translation-layer observation: in this three-row table, the CUN/UUR leucine usage coordinate is context dependent rather than sign uniform, and its controlled paired statistic is positive against the tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate.  The loaded organisms give one negative sign and two positive signs, so the packet should be read as evidence for organism dependence, not as a universal direction for CUN or UUR preference.  The perfect numerical correlations are finite-row outcomes on $n=3$ rows; they should be treated as exact values for this table, not as population-level estimates.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.} No stronger biological conclusion follows from this record by itself.  The row does not establish ribosome-mediated translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, molecular function, a causal mechanism, or universality across organisms.  A separate contact would be needed for any of those claims.  In particular, ribosome profiling or a comparable translation-dynamics measurement would be needed before making a mechanism claim; protein structural evidence would be needed before making a folding or physical-admissibility claim; and phenotype or biochemical assay data would be needed before making a function claim.  The present chapter remains at the paired codon-usage and tRNA-copy statistic.

\subsection{Amino-Acid-Residual Q6 Coordinates as a Translation-Layer Test}\label{subsec:bior-q6-aa-residual-translation-test}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record isolates a codon-level question that remains after amino-acid sequence has already been fixed.  The carrier is the per-organism codon-count table, projected away from amino-acid composition and then read in the residual coordinate family $B^{*}_{Q6}$.  Thus the object under test is not whether amino-acid sequence changes, but whether synonymous codon topology leaves a measurable trace in a bounded translation-layer readout after the amino-acid sequence $y$ and ordinary context variables have been held fixed.  The residual family used here has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These coordinates are intended to ask whether a synonymous codon feature survives the amino-acid quotient, not whether codon usage by itself establishes a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Finite contact already present.}
The available finite computation loaded codon-count summaries for three organisms: Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K-12.  The run reports one codon-count table per organism, with the NCBI genetic code loaded, and classifies the contact as finite organism contact rather than a population estimate.  Within this finite row, the nine residual coordinates above were computed.  Seven of the nine coordinates were nonzero by the stated variance cutoff $0.01$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#B^{*}_{Q6}&=9,\\
\#\{q\in B^{*}_{Q6}:\operatorname{Var}(q)>0.01\}&=7,\\
7/9&=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This establishes a nontrivial residual coordinate table after the amino-acid quotient in the finite organism panel.  It does not, by itself, establish translation efficiency, ribosome behavior, protein output, structure, function, physical admissibility, or a universal biological rule.

\paragraph{Survival statistic.}
For a readout $r$ such as a tAI weight, an stAI weight, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, the proposed statistic compares a baseline model against a model augmented by one residual coordinate $q$.  With $Z$ containing the amino-acid sequence $y$, local context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines, the score is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z,q)=J_{0}(Z)-J_{Q}(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value means only that the model using $q$ improves the specified readout under the specified conditioning set.  The intended test is therefore local and conditional: for each organism and each coordinate $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$, estimate the sign and magnitude of $S_Q(r\mid Z,q)$ on the chosen readout.  If all residual coordinates fail to improve the controlled readout beyond an $M$-density baseline, then the translation-layer form of the conjecture is not supported by that dataset.  If at least one residual coordinate has $S_Q(r\mid Z,q)>0$ under the required controls, then the result is evidence for a bounded synonymous-residual signal in that readout, not for a broader biological conclusion.

\paragraph{Why the residual basis is needed.}
An $M$-only codon-density description can record a local codon-usage optimum, but it cannot by itself test distinctions such as $CUN$ versus $UUR$ in leucine or $AAA$ versus $AAG$ in lysine once amino-acid identity has been quotiented out.  The residual family is built precisely to keep those contrasts available after $y$ is controlled.  The finite computation therefore supplies the coordinate side of the test: there are nine residual slots, and seven vary nontrivially across the three-organism contact row at the declared threshold.  The missing step is not another internal coordinate calculation, but a paired controlled readout that combines organism-matched codon usage, relevant tRNA copy-number spectra, and a translation-layer measurement or annotation.

\paragraph{Available readout contacts.}
Three contact classes are identified.  Per-organism codon-usage tables give sequence-composition summaries.  Organism-level tRNA gene-copy annotations give anticodon-family spectra, including leucine-family copy-number readback.  Organism-scoped tAI or stAI resources provide codon adaptation weights that can be used as bounded translation-layer readouts when kept separate from the coordinate construction.  Each contact has a narrow scope: codon-usage tables summarize coding sequence composition; tRNA copy numbers summarize genome annotations; tAI or stAI weights are modelled efficiency annotations.  None of these contacts alone observes folding, physical admissibility, biochemical function, ribosome-mediated mechanism, protein abundance, or universality.

\paragraph{Blocked-null boundary.}
The current row contains a specific gap: it has the relevant contact categories, but it does not yet contain an organism-matched table in which codon usage, tRNA-copy data, and tAI or stAI weights are jointly used to compute amino-acid-composition-controlled estimates $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$.  Consequently, absence of the paired statistic is a blocked null condition rather than negative evidence for the residual basis.  The present record may report the finite residual geometry and the exact three-organism nontriviality count, but it must not report positive residual survival, $M$-only insufficiency, ribosome-mediated mechanism, protein abundance, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, or a global biological law until a separate matched contact supplies the appropriate statistic.

\paragraph{Operational verdict.}
The conjectural test is therefore well specified but not yet discharged at the translation layer.  The finite data show that $B^{*}_{Q6}$ is not empty after amino-acid quotienting in the three-organism panel: $9$ coordinates were computed and $7$ were nonzero above variance threshold $0.01$.  The next admissible empirical row must compute, for each organism and readout, whether adding a residual coordinate $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ improves the controlled score over the baseline containing amino-acid sequence, context, simpler codon features, and $M$-density.  Only that paired statistic can turn the residual coordinate signal into a bounded translation-layer survival claim.
