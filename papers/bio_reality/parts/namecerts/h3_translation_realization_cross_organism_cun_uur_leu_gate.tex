\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR Leu gate}\label{subsec:h3-translation-realization-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
This record concerns a Leu-module statistic built from the synonymous leucine codon families $\mathrm{CUN}$ and $\mathrm{UUR}$, paired with organism labels and organism-level $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu gene-copy summaries. The measured coordinate is the signed log-ratio
$$
\begin{aligned}
  x_o = \log\bigl(\#\mathrm{CUN}_o / \#\mathrm{UUR}_o\bigr),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $o$ ranges over the organisms present in the finite row. The row loaded three organisms: $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli\ K12}$. The corresponding reported log-ratios are
$$
\begin{aligned}
  (1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with signs
$$
\begin{aligned}
  (+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not constant across the three rows. The module is therefore not being used as a one-organism codon label or as a universal direction claim; it is a finite cross-organism comparison between a Leu codon-usage residual and a separately supplied $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu copy-number coordinate.

\paragraph{Controlled readout.}
The finite run reports experiment id $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$ and run id $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$. The recorded ordinary Pearson statistic is $1.0$. The controlled statistic also reports a positive partial correlation with the $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu ratio after amino-acid composition control, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
  n &= 3,\\
  r_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite row, the support condition is therefore satisfied in the narrow sense that the $\mathrm{CUN}/\mathrm{UUR}$ sign is non-uniform and the reported controlled association with the organism-level $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu ratio is positive. The claim is not that raw codon counts alone constitute a biological realization. The admissible statistic is the paired row: codon-usage summaries, organism labels, $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu copy spectra, and an amino-acid-composition control over synonymous classes.

\paragraph{Contacts used.}
Two contacts are active. The first is a per-organism codon-usage contact: curated sequence resources provide codon-count or frequency summaries for coding regions. This contact can constrain a codon-usage distribution, but it does not observe ribosome motion, peptide folding, biological activity, physical admissibility, or a global law. The second is an organism-level $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu gene-copy contact: genome annotation resources provide anticodon-family copy-number summaries. That contact can constrain the $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu coordinate, but copy number by itself is still an annotation-level measurement rather than a direct kinetic or proteomic observation. The chapter therefore treats the two contacts as paired finite evidence for a bounded codon-usage/$\mathrm{tRNA}$ statistic, not as evidence for downstream biological behavior.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite row passes the stated counterexample search. The break condition would have been met if all three organisms had the same $\log(\mathrm{CUN}/\mathrm{UUR})$ sign, or if sign reversal occurred without a positive controlled association with the $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu copy ratio. Instead, the signs are $+,-,+$, $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$, and the reported partial Pearson value is $1.0$ for $n=3$. The result is consequently a ready finite witness for a bounded translation-layer statistic: organism-level Leu codon usage varies in sign and magnitude with the recorded $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu copy-number ratio under the stated control.

\paragraph{Boundary.}
Nothing in this row establishes ribosome-mediated translation as a mechanism, protein structure, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal biological rule. In particular, the sign reversal is context-dependent rather than universal directionality, and no promotion to structure or function is licensed by this dataset. The row may be cited only for the attached codon-usage and $\mathrm{tRNA}$-copy contacts, together with the reported controlled statistic. A stronger statement would require a separate contact, such as ribosome profiling, direct translation-efficiency measurement, proteomic output, folding evidence, physical-admissibility data, or functional assay data.
