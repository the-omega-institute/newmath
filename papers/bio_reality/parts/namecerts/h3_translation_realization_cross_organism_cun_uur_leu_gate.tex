\subsection{Cross-organism Leu codon-usage gate}\label{subsec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Record.}
This record isolates a narrow organism-level statistic for the leucine synonymous codon families CUN and UUR.  The carrier is the residual comparison between a per-organism codon-usage coordinate and an organism label, paired with a second coordinate supplied by tRNA-Leu gene-copy annotations.  The measured codon coordinate is the signed logarithmic contrast
$$
\begin{aligned}
  L_o = \log\bigl(\#\mathrm{CUN}_o / \#\mathrm{UUR}_o\bigr),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $o$ ranges over the organisms for which both codon-usage and tRNA-Leu copy-number summaries are present.  The intended biological reading is deliberately modest: the packet asks whether the sign and magnitude of $L_o$ vary with the organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate after controlling for amino-acid composition within synonymous classes.  It is not a claim about the full translation apparatus, a ribosome-level mechanism, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Finite rows.}
The validation run is recorded as experiment $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$, with run identifier $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$, verified at $2026$-$05$-$31\,19{:}45{:}17+00{:}00$.  The loaded organism rows are $\mathrm{homo\_sapiens}$, $\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}$, so the statistic is a three-row cross-organism check rather than a population-scale survey.  The reported log-ratio vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
  (L_{\mathrm{human}},L_{\mathrm{yeast}},L_{\mathrm{ecoli}})
  = (1.3514980212896148, -0.24869078488829677, 1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
and its sign vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
  (\operatorname{sgn} L_{\mathrm{human}},\operatorname{sgn} L_{\mathrm{yeast}},\operatorname{sgn} L_{\mathrm{ecoli}}) = (1,-1,1).
\end{aligned}
$$
The field $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}$ is false.  Thus the finite table already contains a sign reversal: the yeast row is negative, while the human and $E.\ coli$ K-12 rows are positive.  That observation is only a codon-usage/tRNA-copy contact; it is not evidence that a protein folds, works, or realizes any physical phenotype.

\paragraph{Controlled association.}
The reported Pearson coefficient for the paired statistic is $1.0$.  The controlled statistic is also recorded as positive, with partial Pearson value
$$
\begin{aligned}
  r_{\mathrm{partial}} = 1.0, \qquad n = 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite table, the direction of the CUN/UUR contrast is therefore not constant across organisms, and the signed magnitude aligns perfectly with the recorded tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate after the stated amino-acid-composition control.  Because $n=3$, this should be read as a small-row certificate of compatibility with the proposed gate, not as a robust epidemiological or phylogenetic estimate.  No $p$-value, confidence interval, bootstrap interval, or held-out replication row is present in the row data, so the numerical strength of the association is exactly the reported coefficient on these three rows and nothing stronger.

\paragraph{Admissible conclusion.}
The finite result supports the bounded statement that the Leu-module CUN/UUR usage coordinate can be paired with organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number summaries, and that in the recorded rows the pair passes the non-uniform-sign and positive-controlled-association tests.  The passed status therefore licenses only a translation-layer contact in this restricted statistical sense: per-organism codon-usage summaries and per-organism tRNA-Leu gene-copy summaries agree with the proposed gate on the available rows.  The row data do not by themselves observe ribosome motion, elongation kinetics, codon dwell times, protein abundance, folding, functional activity, physical admissibility, or any universal direction of the effect.  A stronger biological assertion would require a separate measurement channel, for example ribosome profiling for translation dynamics, proteomics for abundance, structural assays for folding, or functional assays for phenotype.

\paragraph{Failure boundary.}
The same record also fixes the local stopping rule.  A raw CUN/UUR log-ratio without the amino-acid-composition control is not treated as a translation-level statistic.  A mixed-case identifier or a codon-label lookup is not treated as biological evidence.  A standard genetic-code table can say which codons name leucine, but it cannot supply organism-matched usage counts, tRNA-Leu copy ratios, ribosome-level observations, or protein-output measurements.  Consequently the present chapter accepts only the finite codon-usage/tRNA-copy relation stated above, and it blocks any inference to mechanism, folding, physical admissibility, function, or universality until an independent contact explicitly measures that layer.
