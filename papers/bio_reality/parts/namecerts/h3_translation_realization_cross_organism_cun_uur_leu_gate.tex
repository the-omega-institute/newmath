\subsection{Cross-organism Leu codon-usage gate}
\label{sec:bioreality-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
This record concerns the leucine synonymous block split into the CUN and UUR codon families. The object under inspection is not a protein, not a ribosome trace, and not a functional phenotype. It is a finite organism-indexed table whose active coordinates are the organism label, the signed value of $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, an organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, and an amino-acid-composition control that keeps the comparison inside the leucine synonymous class. The finite run is identified as \texttt{675fdddb3187}, with status \texttt{passed}, and it loaded exactly three organism rows: \texttt{homo\_sapiens}, \texttt{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}, and \texttt{escherichia\_coli\_k12}. Thus the evidential unit is a three-row cross-organism statistic, not an unrestricted biological principle.

\paragraph{Measured signs.}
The recorded CUN-over-UUR log-ratios are $1.3514980212896148$, $-0.24869078488829677$, and $1.0086640520051702$ on the three loaded organism rows. Their signs are $+1$, $-1$, and $+1$, so the sign-uniformity flag is false. This is the first finite contact: the same leucine-family coordinate does not keep one direction across the loaded organisms. Written as the packet's local numeric summary,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R &= (1.3514980212896148,\,-0.24869078488829677,\,1.0086640520051702),\\
\operatorname{sgn}(R) &= (1,\,-1,\,1),\\
\mathrm{sign\_uniform} &= \mathrm{false}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The only admissible reading is that this finite organism panel contains a context-dependent reversal in the CUN/UUR usage statistic.

\paragraph{Paired tRNA-Leu contact.}
The second finite contact pairs the codon-usage coordinate with an organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number spectrum. In the recorded run, the ordinary Pearson value is $1.0$, and the amino-acid-composition-controlled partial Pearson entry is also $1.0$ with $n=3$. This supplies a bounded positive association inside the three-row table after the stated control, not a mechanistic account of why any organism has that codon profile. The corresponding scalar readout is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{Pearson}} &= 1.0,\\
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}\mid \mathrm{AA\ control}} &= 1.0,\\
n &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
No p-value, confidence interval, fitted slope, lambda parameter, hit-rate statistic, or larger-row replicate table is present in this packet. The chapter therefore treats the correlation as a finite positive readout of the supplied rows, not as a population-level estimate.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The admissible conclusion is narrow: in the three loaded organisms, the CUN/UUR leucine-family log-ratio changes sign, and the controlled statistic recorded for that same panel is positively aligned with the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio. The biological labels matter only because they index externally curated codon-usage and tRNA gene-copy summaries. The coordinate does not become a ribosome movie, a kinetic model, a protein-structure claim, or a fitness claim. It says that a leucine codon-usage residual, when paired with organism labels and tRNA-Leu gene-copy counts, gives a concrete cross-organism test site for translation-layer bookkeeping.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
This packet does not establish translation as an observed physical process, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, ribosome-mediated mechanism, or universality. The recorded safeguards explicitly say that the CUN/UUR and tRNA-Leu contact does not establish protein folding, does not establish biological function or physical admissibility, does not promote the statistic to a global biological law, and treats sign reversal as context-dependent rather than directionally universal. A stronger claim would require a separate contact, such as ribosome profiling, direct tRNA abundance or charging measurements, controlled expression assays, protein output, structural measurements, or organism-matched data beyond the present three rows.

\paragraph{Audit status.}
The packet is ready as a finite BioReality chapter because it has concrete rows, concrete scalar values, a passed run status, and an explicit negative boundary. Its evidential class is an organism-level codon-usage and tRNA-copy association inside the leucine module. Its limitation is equally part of the result: the data are sufficient for the stated three-row codon-usage gate, but not sufficient for mechanism, function, structure, physical admissibility, or a universal rule.
