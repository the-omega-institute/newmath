\subsection{Cross-organism Leu codon balance under tRNA-copy contact}
\label{sec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
This record concerns the organism-level Leu coding split between the CUN codon family and the UUR codon family.  The finite carrier is the residual comparison of CUN against UUR after the packet has been paired with organism labels, tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectra, and an amino-acid-composition control.  Its observable coordinates are the signed statistic $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, the matched tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio, the organism label, and the synonymous-class control used to prevent a raw compositional difference from being read as a translation-layer fact.

\paragraph{Measured rows.}
The run $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$ loaded three organism rows: Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K-12.  The corresponding log-ratio vector and sign vector were
$$
\begin{aligned}
L&=(1.3514980212896148,\ -0.24869078488829677,\ 1.0086640520051702),\\
\operatorname{sgn}(L)&=(1,\ -1,\ 1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not constant across the three rows; the recorded Boolean for uniform sign is $\mathrm{false}$.  The middle row has the opposite sign from the two positive rows, so the packet is not a one-direction codon-usage rule.  The finite statistic instead records a context-dependent cross-organism reversal.

\paragraph{Paired statistic.}
The experiment $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$ reports Pearson correlation $1.0$ for the paired finite table.  It also reports a positive partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu ratio after the amino-acid-composition control, with sample size $n=3$ and partial Pearson value $1.0$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
r_{\mathrm{partial}}(\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR}),\ \mathrm{tRNA\text{-}Leu\ ratio}\mid \mathrm{AA\ control})&=1.0,\\
n&=3.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite table, the admissible conclusion is that the CUN/UUR Leu residual is not merely a detached codon-label contrast: when the organism-matched tRNA-Leu copy-number contact is attached, the controlled paired statistic is positive in the recorded rows.  The status of the run is $\mathrm{passed}$, and the verification timestamp is 2026-05-31T09:19:51+00:00.

\paragraph{Biological reading.}
The result is a bounded sequence-and-annotation contact.  Per-organism codon-usage tables supply count or frequency summaries over coding sequences, and genome annotations supply organism-level tRNA-Leu copy counts by anticodon family.  The packet therefore reads the Leu split as a finite relation among sequence composition, organism identity, and tRNA-copy annotation.  It does not by itself observe ribosome dwell time, codon-by-codon decoding kinetics, protein production rate, folding state, biochemical activity, or organismal phenotype.  The amino-acid-composition control is essential: without it, a raw $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ value would remain a codon-usage statistic rather than evidence about the translation layer.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The claim stops at the paired codon-usage and tRNA-copy statistic.  No actual translation event, folding claim, physical admissibility claim, biological function, ribosome-mediated mechanism, or universal cross-organism law follows from these three rows.  In particular, the sign reversal is part of the observation, not a defect to be erased and not a universal directionality theorem.  Promotion to protein structure, molecular function, or mechanistic translation dynamics would require a separate contact, such as ribosome-profiling, protein-output, folding, or functional-assay data matched to the same organism-level comparison.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
For the finite three-row table, the CUN/UUR Leu gate is admitted as a controlled organism-level translation-layer statistic in the narrow sense just described.  The observed values $1.3514980212896148$, $-0.24869078488829677$, and $1.0086640520051702$ establish non-uniform sign, and the recorded controlled association with tRNA-Leu copy ratio has partial Pearson value $1.0$ on $n=3$ rows.  The result is therefore a ready finite record for the stated codon-usage/tRNA-copy contact, while all stronger biological interpretations remain outside the chapter.
