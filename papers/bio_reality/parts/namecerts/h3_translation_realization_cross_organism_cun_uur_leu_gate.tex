\subsection{Cross-Organism CUN/UUR Leucine Usage as a Bounded Translation-Layer Contact}\label{sec:bioreality-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Recorded object.}
This record concerns a leucine-codon module in which the CUN synonymous block is compared with the UUR synonymous block at organism scale.  The biological row is not a claim about a protein product, a folded state, or a universal property of the genetic code.  It is a finite comparison between per-organism codon-usage summaries and organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectra.  The measured coordinate is the signed log ratio $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, paired with a tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio and interpreted only after an amino-acid-composition control on synonymous codon classes.  The finite run used three organism labels: \emph{Homo sapiens}, \emph{Saccharomyces cerevisiae}, and \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12.  The run identifier was $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$, and the row status was reported as passed.

\paragraph{Finite measurements.}
The observed log-ratio vector for the three organism labels was
$$
\begin{aligned}
R &= (1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),\\
\operatorname{sgn}(R) &= (1,\;-1,\;1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign was not uniform across the measured organisms: the recorded scalar flag was $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$.  The row also reported an ordinary Pearson value $\rho=1.0$ for the finite paired statistic available in the run.  After the stated amino-acid-composition control, the recorded partial-correlation field was
$$
\begin{aligned}
n &= 3,\\
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers are treated here as finite-row measurements, not as asymptotic population estimates.  With only three organism rows, the value $1.0$ says that the three recorded points are perfectly ordered under the statistic supplied by the run; it does not by itself provide a stable cross-clade law, a mechanistic model, or a calibrated significance claim.

\paragraph{Interpretation of the sign reversal.}
The negative yeast entry, placed between two positive entries for human and \emph{E. coli} K-12 in the recorded vector, is the main local obstruction to any uniform-direction reading.  The finite row therefore supports a narrower statement: CUN versus UUR leucine usage can change sign across organisms in this small panel, and the recorded tRNA-Leu copy-number statistic is positively aligned with the controlled codon-usage coordinate in that same panel.  The measurement is strongest as a bounded organism-level pairing between codon usage and tRNA gene-copy annotation.  It is weakest as evidence for any general biological principle, because the row count is $3$, no p-value is recorded, and no ribosome-profiling or protein-output readout is part of the packet.

\paragraph{Admissible claim.}
The admissible claim is the following finite statement.  In the run $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$, using the three loaded organisms \emph{Homo sapiens}, \emph{Saccharomyces cerevisiae}, and \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12, the CUN/UUR leucine log-ratio signs were $(1,-1,1)$ rather than uniform, and the controlled organism-level statistic paired with tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio had recorded partial Pearson correlation $1.0$ at $n=3$.  This is a translation-layer contact only in the restricted sense that codon-usage tables and tRNA gene-copy annotations are quantities adjacent to translational coding capacity.  It is not a direct observation of ribosome motion, elongation rate, decoding kinetics, peptide abundance, or cellular phenotype.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
No broader biological conclusion is licensed by this row alone.  The CUN/UUR statistic and the tRNA-Leu copy-number pairing do not establish translation in the direct ribosomal sense, do not establish folded structure, do not establish physical admissibility of a molecule or state, do not establish biological function, do not supply a mechanism, and do not define a universal rule across organisms.  Any one of those stronger readings would require an additional contact with the corresponding measurement type: for example, ribosome-profiling or decoding-rate data for mechanism-adjacent translation, structural assays for folding, thermodynamic or cellular admissibility measurements for physical realization, and perturbation or phenotype data for function.  In the absence of such contacts, the row remains a finite organism-level codon-usage/tRNA-copy comparison with a non-uniform sign vector and a perfect recorded partial correlation in a three-row panel.

\paragraph{Audit status.}
The useful content of the row is therefore precise but small.  It records three organism labels, three log-ratio values, three signs, a false uniformity flag, Pearson $1.0$, and controlled partial Pearson $1.0$ at $n=3$.  The row also explicitly denies promotion to folding, function, physical admissibility, global law, and universal directionality.  This combination makes the chapter suitable as a self-contained BioReality record: it preserves the finite positive signal while keeping the biological interpretation at the scale actually measured.
