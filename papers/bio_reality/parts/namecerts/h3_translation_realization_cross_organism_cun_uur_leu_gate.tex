\paragraph{Question fixed by the packet.}
The record isolates a leucine-codon statistic at organism resolution.  The finite carrier consists of a per-organism residual for the leucine codon families CUN and UUR, paired with an organism label and with a separate tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate.  Its intended readout is narrow: compare the sign and magnitude of $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ against the organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio after a synonymous-codon, amino-acid-composition control.  The biological object is therefore not a protein, not a folded structure, and not a dynamical ribosome trace.  It is a small cross-organism table whose admissible evidence is restricted to codon-usage summaries and tRNA-Leu genome-annotation summaries.

\paragraph{Interpretation at the measured layer.}
The packet passes only the following bounded test: in the three loaded organisms, the CUN/UUR sign reverses rather than remaining fixed, and the recorded magnitude relation is positive against the tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate after the amino-acid-composition control specified by the probe.  This is enough to make the organism label essential to the coordinate.  A single global direction for the leucine codon pair is not supported by these rows, because yeast has the opposite sign from the human and bacterial rows.  The relevant positive result is not the raw existence of CUN and UUR codons, but the paired organism-level statistic: codon-usage residual together with the tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate.

\paragraph{Local exclusion of stronger readings.}
The same finite run explicitly blocks cross-layer promotion.  The CUN/UUR and tRNA-Leu contact does not establish protein folding, biological function, physical admissibility, or a universal biological law.  It also does not by itself establish translation as an observed biochemical process or a ribosome-mediated mechanism; those would require a separate contact such as ribosome profiling, stAI-type translation efficiency evidence, protein-output measurement, or another directly relevant biological readout.  Here the admitted claim is only that the externally paired codon-usage and tRNA-copy rows support a context-dependent organism-level leucine-codon statistic.  The sign reversal is therefore reported as dependence on organismal context, not as a law with universal directionality.

\paragraph{Failure conditions retained.}
The packet would lose support if later organism-matched rows made $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ uniform across the measured organisms independently of the tRNA-Leu copy spectrum, or if sign reversal persisted while the controlled partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu ratio disappeared.  It would also remain inadmissible for any stronger biological conclusion if the only additional information were identifier spelling, codon-label lookup, or unpaired codon and tRNA summaries.  The finite result is consequently a controlled, organism-level translation-realization clue in a restricted statistical sense, not a statement about structure, function, mechanism, physical possibility, or universality.
