Second, the reported controlled association is positive in the available three-row table. Within this chapter, that is enough to register a bounded cross-organism contact between leucine codon usage and the organism-level $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu copy-number summary. The object classified here is therefore a leucine-module codon-usage residual paired with external organism labels and copy-number coordinates. Its internal structure consists of a coordinate, namely $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, and a relation, namely the controlled association with $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu copy ratio.

\paragraph{Controls and admissible scope.}
The amino-acid-composition control is essential. A raw $\mathrm{CUN}/\mathrm{UUR}$ ratio by itself is only a sequence-composition statistic; it is not a biological realization claim. The present row set becomes informative only because the packet records the synonymous-class control and an organism-matched $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu copy-number comparison. The contact sources are per-organism codon-usage summaries and genome-annotation summaries of $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu gene-copy counts. Both are external sequence-database contacts, not direct measurements of elongation speed, ribosome occupancy, protein output, or organismal phenotype.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
No direct translation event, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or universal cross-organism law follows from these rows. The sign reversal is context-dependent in the observed finite table and must not be converted into a universal direction. The $\mathrm{CUN}/\mathrm{UUR}$ and $\mathrm{tRNA}$-Leu contacts also do not establish protein folding, do not establish biological function, do not establish physical admissibility, and do not promote this codon-usage statistic to a global law.
