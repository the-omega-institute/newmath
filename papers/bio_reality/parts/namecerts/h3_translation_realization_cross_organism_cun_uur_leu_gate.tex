\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine usage as an organism-level translation statistic}
\label{sec:biorereality-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Record.}
This packet concerns the leucine codon split between the CUN family and the UUR family. Its carrier is an organism-indexed residual coordinate whose measured component is the logarithmic ratio $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, paired with an organism label and with a second organism-level readout for the relative copy spectrum of tRNA-Leu genes. The intended biological contact is deliberately narrow: the packet asks whether the sign and size of the CUN-over-UUR coordinate vary with the available tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio after amino-acid composition has been controlled by a synonymous-class null. The record therefore treats CUN/UUR geometry as a bounded codon-usage statistic, not as a protein-level or organismal-function conclusion.

\paragraph{Finite run.}
The recorded run is $675\mathrm{f}\mathrm{dddb}3187$, verified at $2026$-$05$-$31\,14{:}50{:}28+00{:}00$, with status $\mathrm{passed}$. The loaded organism rows are $\mathrm{homo\_sapiens}$, $\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}$. The measured logarithmic CUN/UUR residuals are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{human}} &= 1.3514980212896148,\\
R_{\mathrm{yeast}} &= -0.24869078488829677,\\
R_{\mathrm{E.\ coli\ K12}} &= 1.0086640520051702.
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding sign vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{sgn}(R)=(1,-1,1),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the uniform-sign alternative is false: $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$. This is the first finite check: the CUN/UUR leucine preference is not reported as a single direction shared by all measured organisms in the row set.

\paragraph{Paired statistic.}
The same run records a positive organism-level association with the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio. The ordinary Pearson value is $1.0$. After applying the stated amino-acid composition control through the synonymous-class comparison, the partial-correlation row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
n&=3,\\
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite table, the evidence therefore supports the weaker claim that the non-uniform CUN/UUR coordinate is aligned with the available tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate. The statement is intentionally row-local: it is a report about three organism rows and the two curated organism-level contacts attached to them. No p-value is recorded in the row, and no larger-sample inferential calibration is encoded here.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The scientific reading is that the leucine codon split supplies a cross-organism translation-layer statistic only when two conditions are kept together. First, the raw codon-usage coordinate must remain inside its synonymous leucine class, so that differences in amino-acid composition are not mistaken for a CUN/UUR preference. Second, the organism labels must be paired with tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectra rather than treated as decorative identifiers. Under those restrictions, the finite table says that the observed signs reverse across the three organisms, while the controlled CUN/UUR magnitude is positively aligned with the tRNA-Leu ratio in the recorded rows.

\paragraph{Negative scope.}
This packet does not establish translation in the sense of directly observed ribosome dynamics. It does not establish folding, physical admissibility, protein function, molecular mechanism, or a universal biological law. The recorded no-promotion clauses state that the CUN/UUR codon-usage and tRNA-Leu copy contact does not establish protein folding, does not establish biological function or physical admissibility, does not promote the translation-layer statistic to a global law, and reports sign reversal as context-dependent rather than universally directional. Any stronger conclusion would require a separate contact, such as ribosome profiling for translational dynamics, structural measurements for folding, biochemical assays for function, or a broader organism panel for generality.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite row is admissible as a bounded cross-organism codon-usage result. It supports the following local statement: in the three recorded organisms, $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ is not sign-uniform, with values $1.3514980212896148$, $-0.24869078488829677$, and $1.0086640520051702$, and the controlled association with the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio is positive with recorded partial Pearson value $1.0$ at $n=3$. The chapter therefore records a translation-layer usage-and-copy-number contact, while leaving mechanism, structure, physical admissibility, function, and universality unclaimed.
