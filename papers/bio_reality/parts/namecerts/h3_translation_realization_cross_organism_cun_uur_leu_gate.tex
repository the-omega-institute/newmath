\paragraph{Finite packet.}
This record treats the leucine codon split between the CUN block and the UUR block as a bounded organism-level statistic.  The carrier is the residual coordinate formed from $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, paired with an organism label, a tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, and an amino-acid-composition control.  The biological object is not a protein, a fold, or a cellular mechanism; it is the finite table of per-organism codon usage and organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy summaries.  The checked organisms in the row are $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli\ K12}$, so the numerical contact has $n=3$ organism rows.

\paragraph{Code-window anchor.}
The linked lower-level code-table fact records that the finite set $M$ has cardinality $20$ and is exactly the union of the WNR window with the CUN window.  In the notation of this packet,
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\

This finite codon list only supplies the codon-label and window coordinate used by the later organism comparison.  It does not by itself measure usage, tRNA copy number, translation, fitness, or protein output.

\paragraph{Observed rows.}
The verified cross-organism run is $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$ with run id $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$ and status $\mathrm{passed}$.  The three observed log-ratios for $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ are $1.3514980212896148$, $-0.24869078488829677$, and $1.0086640520051702$.  Their signs are $+1,-1,+1$, hence the sign is not uniform across the measured organisms.  The packet records this explicitly as $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$.  The paired tRNA-Leu comparison gives Pearson correlation $1.0$, and the amino-acid-composition-controlled partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu copy ratio is also $1.0$ on $n=3$ rows.

\paragraph{Admissible reading.}
The admissible conclusion is narrow: within this finite row set, the CUN-versus-UUR leucine usage residual is organism-dependent, and the signed residual agrees positively with the recorded tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate after the stated amino-acid-composition control.  The sign reversal matters because it prevents the packet from being read as a one-direction universal codon preference.  Human, yeast, and bacterial rows do not collapse to a single CUN-over-UUR sign, while the paired controlled statistic remains positive in the recorded three-row comparison.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
This chapter does not infer a ribosome-level translation event from the codon table alone.  It also does not infer folded structure, physical admissibility, molecular function, a biochemical mechanism, or a universal biological law.  The codon-usage contact is a sequence-composition summary, and the tRNA-Leu contact is a genome-annotation summary.  Those contacts are enough for a bounded organism-level usage-versus-copy-number statistic, but they are not enough to decide whether a ribosome realizes a mechanism, whether a protein folds, whether a phenotype follows, or whether the sign rule persists outside the three recorded organisms.  Any such stronger claim would require a separate contact, such as ribosome profiling, direct protein-output measurements, structural assays, or functional perturbation data.

\paragraph{Null pressure retained.}
The packet remains sensitive to missing context even though the finite run passed.  A larger organism table could break the pattern if the CUN/UUR sign became uniform independently of tRNA-Leu copy spectra, or if sign changes persisted without any controlled association to tRNA-Leu copy ratio.  The current evidence therefore supports only the stated three-row controlled comparison: non-uniform CUN/UUR sign, positive Pearson value $1.0$, positive partial Pearson value $1.0$, and no promotion beyond the measured codon-usage and tRNA-copy contacts.
