\subsection{Cross-organism Leu codon-usage gate}
\label{sec:bior reality-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Object measured.}
This record concerns an organism-level Leu module in which the synonymous codon families $CUN$ and $UUR$ are compared through a signed log-ratio and then paired with an organism-matched tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio.  The carrier is the finite row consisting of an organism label, the coordinate $\log(CUN/UUR)$, the sign of that coordinate, a tRNA-Leu copy-number comparison, and an amino-acid-composition control restricting the readout to synonymous Leu usage rather than whole-proteome amino-acid abundance.  The finite row used here contains three organisms: Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K-12.  The experiment identifier is \texttt{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}, with run id \texttt{675fdddb3187}, and the recorded status is \texttt{passed}.

\paragraph{Finite readout.}
The observed signed coordinates for the three loaded organisms are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R &= (1.3514980212896148,\,-0.24869078488829677,\,1.0086640520051702),\\
\operatorname{sgn}(R) &= (1,\,-1,\,1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not uniform across the loaded organisms: the field \texttt{sign\_uniform} is false.  This is the first finite check required by the packet, because a constant sign across all rows would have made the proposed organism-sensitive gate collapse to a single-direction codon-usage bias.  Instead, the yeast row reverses sign relative to the human and bacterial rows in the supplied ordering, so the statistic has the required context dependence at the level of organism-labeled codon usage.

No p-value, confidence interval, permutation statistic, or held-out row is present in the finite record, so the numerical contact is an attached table-level verification rather than a general statistical law.
