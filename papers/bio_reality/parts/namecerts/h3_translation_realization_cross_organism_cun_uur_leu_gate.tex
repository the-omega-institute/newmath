\subsection{Cross-organism CUN--UUR leucine gate}
\label{sec:bior:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Object of record.}
This record treats the leucine split between the CUN family and the UUR family as an organism-indexed statistical object.  The carrier is not a protein, a ribosome trajectory, or a molecular mechanism.  It is the finite row consisting of an organism label, a codon-usage residual for the leucine module, a tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, and an amino-acid-composition control.  The admissible readout is therefore narrow: for each organism in the measured row, one may compare the sign and magnitude of $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ with the corresponding tRNA-Leu copy-number spectrum after the synonymous-class control has been applied.

\paragraph{Finite codon support.}
The auxiliary code-table contact records the twenty-element set $M$ used by the row-level construction.  In the recorded ordering it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
M = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality is $|M|=20$.  Within this finite support, the leucine comparison uses the CUN block $\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$ against the UUR block $\{\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}$.  This code-table fact only fixes labels and membership.  It does not by itself supply an organism-level usage table, a tRNA annotation, or a biological realization claim.

\paragraph{Measured row.}
The external row loaded three organism labels: $\mathrm{homo\_sapiens}$, $\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}$.  The recorded leucine log-ratios were
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with sign vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is explicitly non-uniform across the three organisms.  The same run reports Pearson correlation $1.0$ for the finite paired statistic and a positive partial Pearson value $1.0$ after the tRNA-Leu ratio is evaluated under the amino-acid-composition control.  The controlled partial-correlation row has $n=3$.  These numbers support only the bounded conclusion that the organism-indexed CUN/UUR residual is aligned with the recorded tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio in this finite row.

\paragraph{Contact interpretation.}
Two empirical contacts are used.  The codon-usage contact is a per-organism sequence-database summary of coding-sequence composition.  The tRNA contact is an organism-level genome-annotation summary of tRNA-Leu gene-copy counts by anticodon family.  Both contacts are summary contacts: they are suitable for testing codon-usage distribution and tRNA-copy covariation, but they do not observe ribosome dwell time, translation kinetics, protein folding, molecular admissibility, or organismal function.  The row is therefore a controlled cross-organism usage statistic, not a direct observation of the biochemical translation process.

\paragraph{Decision.}
The finite test passes for the stated row.  The break condition requiring uniform CUN/UUR sign is not met, because the signs are $+,-,+$.  The alternative break condition requiring loss of partial association with the tRNA-Leu ratio is also not met in the recorded row, because the controlled partial Pearson value is $1.0$ for $n=3$.  The accepted statement is consequently limited to this: in the three-organism table loaded by experiment $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$, the CUN-versus-UUR leucine statistic changes sign across organisms and remains positively aligned with the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio after the stated amino-acid-composition control.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
No broader biological conclusion is licensed here.  The row does not establish translation as a mechanistic ribosome-mediated event, does not establish protein structure or folding, does not establish physical admissibility, does not establish biological function, and does not state a universal law of codon usage.  The sign reversal is a context-dependent finite observation, not a directionality principle.  Any promotion beyond this controlled usage-and-copy-number contact would require a separate measurement supplying the corresponding reality contact, such as ribosome profiling for dynamics or protein-level assays for structure and function.
