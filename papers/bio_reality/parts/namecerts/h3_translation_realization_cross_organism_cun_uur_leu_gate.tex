\paragraph{Finite packet.}
The packet concerns the leucine synonymous coding split between the CUN block and the UUR block, read at organism scale rather than at a single transcript, protein, or ribosome trace.  Its carrier is the residual comparison between $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ and an organism-labelled tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio, with amino-acid composition treated as a control rather than as a removable nuisance.  The finite row therefore has four named distinctions: the CUN-over-UUR logarithmic coordinate, the tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, the organism label, and the amino-acid-composition control.  The observed organisms in run $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$ are $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli}\ \mathrm{K}12$.  The experiment identifier is \texttt{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}, and the recorded status is \texttt{passed} at timestamp $2026$-$05$-$30\,15{:}47{:}14+00{:}00$.

\paragraph{Admissible interpretation.}
The admissible reading is deliberately small.  The CUN and UUR leucine families are not being used here to infer a folded protein, a biochemical role, or a universal codon-bias principle.  They are used as a finite coordinate pair whose sign and magnitude can be checked against organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectra.  The packet passes because the available organism rows show both sign reversal and a positive controlled association with the tRNA-Leu ratio.  In formula form, the local observation is only
$$
\begin{aligned}
\exists i,j\quad &\operatorname{sgn}(L_i) \ne \operatorname{sgn}(L_j),\\
& r_{\partial}(L,\rho_{\mathrm{tRNA\mbox{-}Leu}}\mid \mathrm{AA\ composition}) = 1.0,\\
& n=3.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $\rho_{\mathrm{tRNA\mbox{-}Leu}}$ denotes the organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio supplied to the validation row.  The formula is not a mechanistic equation for translation, and it is not a statement about peptide output.  It is a checked finite association among codon-usage summaries, organism labels, and tRNA gene-copy annotation.

\paragraph{Local scope boundary.}
No broader translation event, folding fact, physical admissibility result, biological function, ribosome-mediated mechanism, or universal cross-organism rule is licensed by these rows alone.  The run explicitly records that the CUN/UUR codon-usage and tRNA-Leu copy contact does not establish protein folding; it does not establish biological function or physical admissibility; it does not promote the translation-level coordinate to a global biological law; and sign reversal is context-dependent rather than universal directionality.  A stronger claim would require a separate contact, for example organism-matched ribosome profiling, protein-output measurement, or a direct mechanistic assay.  Without such an additional contact, the result remains a finite organism-level codon-usage/tRNA-copy statistic with controlled amino-acid-composition context.

\paragraph{Audit verdict.}
The row is ready for inclusion as a vision-level BioReality record because it contains a concrete organism list, a concrete logarithmic vector, the associated sign vector, a finite controlled correlation value, and an explicit negative boundary.  Its scientific force is the contrast between a non-uniform CUN/UUR sign pattern and a positive tRNA-Leu association in the available three-organism table.  Its main weakness is the same finiteness that makes it auditable: $n=3$ supplies an exact row-level check but not a robust cross-taxon estimate.  The chapter therefore records the result as a bounded, organism-labelled codon-usage contact rather than as a general biological principle.
