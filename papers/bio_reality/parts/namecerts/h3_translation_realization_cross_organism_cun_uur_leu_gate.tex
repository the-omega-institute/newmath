\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine usage as a bounded translation-layer statistic}
\label{sec:h3-translation-realization-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Packet and measured row.}
This record concerns the leucine codon-family split between the CUN block and the UUR block.  The finite carrier is an organism-indexed residual coordinate: for each organism in the available row set, the observable coordinate is the signed value of $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, paired with an independently recorded tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio and interpreted only after an amino-acid-composition control.  The experiment row is identified by run $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$ and experiment label $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$.  The loaded organism list is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R &= \{\mathrm{homo\_sapiens},\ \mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae},\ \mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}\},\\
|R| &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row therefore has enough entries to register a sign reversal and a three-point paired statistic, but it is not a broad phylogenetic survey.

\paragraph{Observed CUN/UUR signs.}
The measured log-ratio vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
L &= (1.3514980212896148,\ -0.24869078488829677,\ 1.0086640520051702),\\
\operatorname{sign}(L) &= (1,\ -1,\ 1),\\
\mathrm{sign\_uniform} &= \mathrm{false}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row does not support a constant organism-independent direction for the CUN/UUR split.  Human and $\mathrm{E.\ coli}$ K12 sit on the positive side of the log-ratio coordinate in this row, while budding yeast sits on the negative side.  This is the central biological readback of the packet: the Leu codon-family balance is organism-contextual in the available data, rather than a single fixed sign attached to the codon labels themselves.

\paragraph{Paired tRNA-Leu statistic.}
The tRNA contact is used only as an organism-level paired statistic.  In the verified row, the direct Pearson value reported for the paired CUN/UUR coordinate and tRNA-Leu ratio is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
After the stated amino-acid-composition control, the reported partial statistic is
$$
\begin{aligned}
 n &= 3,\\
 \rho_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite three-organism row, the non-uniform sign pattern is therefore accompanied by a positive controlled association with the tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate.  The result is appropriately read as a small, organism-matched contact between codon-usage summaries and tRNA gene-copy summaries.  It is not a ribosome-dynamics measurement, because no ribosome-profiling trace, elongation-rate measurement, or direct translational flux row is present here.

\paragraph{Contacts and admissible interpretation.}
Two observational contacts are active.  The first is the per-organism codon-usage table contact, which supplies sequence-derived counts or frequencies for coding regions.  The second is the tRNA-Leu gene-copy contact, which supplies annotation-derived copy-number spectra by organism and anticodon family.  Both contacts are database-level summaries.  They can support a bounded statement about codon-usage distribution and its organism-matched relation to tRNA-Leu copy number.  They cannot, by themselves, establish a complete translation process, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or a universal cross-organism law.

\paragraph{Local exclusion zone.}
The finite row explicitly blocks several stronger readings.  A raw CUN/UUR sign alone is not a translation claim.  The controlled statistic does not say that ribosomes realize a particular mechanism, that proteins fold in any specific way, that a sequence is physically admissible, or that a biological function is present.  It also does not turn the observed sign reversal into a universal directionality rule.  Any such conclusion would require an additional contact of the relevant kind: for example, ribosome profiling for translation dynamics, protein-structure evidence for folding, biochemical measurement for physical admissibility, perturbation or assay data for function, and a much wider matched organism panel for a general law.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
The row passes as a narrow translation-layer statistic: in the available organisms, $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ is not sign-uniform, and its paired relation with the tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate is reported as $\rho=1.0$ and $\rho_{\mathrm{partial}}=1.0$ at $n=3$ after the stated control.  The chapter therefore records a valid organism-level codon-usage/tRNA-copy contact, with the interpretation restricted to that finite measurement.  The admissible claim is the existence of this controlled, cross-organism association in the recorded row; the inadmissible claims are any unmeasured translation mechanism, folding, physical admissibility, function, or universal biological rule.
