\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine gate}\label{subsec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
This record concerns the leucine synonymous split between the CUN family and the UUR family at organism scale.  The carrier is a finite table whose rows pair an organism label with two measured coordinates: the signed residual $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ and an organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, with amino-acid composition held fixed through a synonymous-class control.  The biological reading is deliberately narrow.  The table is not a statement about protein output as such; it is a checked relation between codon-usage summaries and tRNA-Leu copy-number summaries for the organisms loaded in the run.

\paragraph{Loaded rows.}
The run $675\mathrm{f}\mathrm{dddb}3187$ for experiment $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$ loaded exactly three organism rows: $\mathrm{homo\_sapiens}$, $\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}$.  The observed CUN-over-UUR log-ratio vector was
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with sign vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite table does not support a uniform sign rule across the loaded organisms: the reported Boolean $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}$ is $\mathrm{false}$.  The yeast row has the opposite sign from the human and bacterial rows in this finite readout, so the packet is recording context dependence rather than a single directional law.

\paragraph{Controlled association.}
The positive statistic recorded for the same run is $\rho=1.0$ for the Pearson coordinate, and the amino-acid-composition-controlled statistic reports partial Pearson $1.0$ with $n=3$.  In the notation of this packet, the finite claim is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{sign\_uniform} &= \mathrm{false},\\
\rho_{\mathrm{Pearson}} &= 1.0,\\
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}\mid \mathrm{AA\ control}} &= 1.0,\\
n &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
The evidence status for the run is $\mathrm{passed}$.  This should be read as a small finite-row acceptance of the specified codon-usage/tRNA-copy statistic, not as a population-level theorem.  Since the row count is three, the statistic is exact for the supplied table but fragile as an estimator of any broader taxonomic pattern.

\paragraph{Scope of the contact.}
The codon-usage contact supplies per-organism sequence-composition summaries, and the tRNA contact supplies organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy summaries by anticodon family.  Together they are adequate for the bounded question asked here: whether the CUN/UUR log-ratio sign is non-uniform and whether the signed magnitude is positively associated with the tRNA-Leu copy ratio after the synonymous-class control.  They do not observe ribosome motion, elongation timing, nascent-chain behavior, folded structure, molecular stability, or organismal function.  For that reason the present chapter records only a translation-layer statistical contact in the restricted sense of codon usage paired with tRNA-copy annotation.

\paragraph{Local negative boundary.}
No ribosomal translation process, no folding fact, no physical admissibility fact, no biological function, no mechanistic causal account, and no universal cross-organism rule follows from this finite table alone.  The sign reversal is reported as context-dependent.  The positive partial correlation is reported only for the loaded three-row table and only after the amino-acid-composition control.  A later claim about protein structure, physical feasibility, function, ribosome-level mechanism, or a global biological law would require a separate contact that directly measures that higher object.
