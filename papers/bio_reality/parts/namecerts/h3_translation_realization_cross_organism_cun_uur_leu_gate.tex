\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine gate}\label{sec:h3-translation-realization-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record treats the leucine codon split between the CUN block and the UUR block as an organism-indexed finite statistic rather than as a direct claim about ribosome motion or protein output.  The carrier is the residual coordinate consisting of the per-organism value $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, the matched organism label, the corresponding tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio, and the synonymous-amino-acid control imposed within the leucine class.  The finite code-window check also records the auxiliary set $M=\mathrm{WNR}\cup\mathrm{CUN}$ with cardinality $20$ and with the listed codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This finite support is not used as an evolutionary explanation.  It is only the local coordinate that tells the packet which CUN and UUR usage counts are being compared.

\paragraph{Observed rows.}
The experiment loaded three organism rows: Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K12.  On these rows the observed CUN-over-UUR log-ratios were
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with signs
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not uniform across the measured organisms.  The row count for the controlled cross-organism comparison is $n=3$.  The reported ordinary Pearson value for the paired statistic is $1.0$, and the reported partial Pearson value after the amino-acid-composition control is also $1.0$.  The run status is recorded as passed for experiment identifier \texttt{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The finite result supports only the following narrow statement: in this three-row dataset, the CUN/UUR leucine usage balance changes sign across organisms and is positively aligned with the organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio after the stated synonymous-class control.  The negative yeast row is essential to the record, since a uniform positive sign would have made the gate biologically uninformative at this resolution.  The human and E. coli K12 rows are positive, while the yeast row is negative; the packet therefore records context dependence rather than a universal direction.

\paragraph{Local limit of the claim.}
The contact is a paired codon-usage and tRNA-gene-copy statistic.  It does not by itself establish translation as an enacted cellular process, ribosome-mediated mechanism, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal biological law.  Any such stronger assertion would require a separate measurement contact with the appropriate observable: for example ribosome-profiling evidence for translation dynamics, protein-level evidence for folding or function, or a broader organism panel with independent validation for universality.  The present row set is enough to license a bounded cross-organism usage/tRNA relation; it is not enough to promote that relation into mechanism or function.
