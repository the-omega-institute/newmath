\subsection{Cross-Organism Leucine Codon-Usage Gate}
\label{subsec:bioreality-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Object of the row.}
This record treats the leucine synonymous coding split between the $CUN$ family and the $UUR$ family as a bounded organism-level readout.  The measured coordinate is the signed logarithmic usage contrast
$$
\begin{aligned}
L_{\mathrm{Leu}} &= \log(\#CUN/\#UUR),
\end{aligned}
$$
paired with an organism label and with an organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio.  The row is not a protein claim and is not a claim about a particular ribosomal event.  It is a finite cross-organism statistic whose admissible content is limited to codon-usage summaries and tRNA-Leu annotation summaries after controlling for amino-acid composition at the synonymous-class level.

\paragraph{Finite rows used.}
The run $675\mathrm{fdddb3187}$ loaded three organisms: Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K-12.  For these rows the recorded $L_{\mathrm{Leu}}$ values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with corresponding sign vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign-uniformity flag is false.  The observed signs do not collapse to a single cross-organism direction: the yeast row is negative while the human and bacterial rows are positive.  The row therefore passes the narrow counterexample test against any claim that the $CUN/UUR$ sign is fixed across the measured organisms.

\paragraph{Paired statistic.}
The paired codon-usage and tRNA-Leu readback reports a Pearson coefficient of $1.0$ for the available three-row table.  The amino-acid-composition-controlled statistic is recorded as a positive partial correlation with $n=3$ and partial Pearson value $1.0$.  In this finite table, the supported statement is therefore only that the signed and scaled $CUN/UUR$ contrast co-varies positively with the recorded tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio after the stated control.  The row count is small, so the coefficient is best read as an exact property of the loaded finite table rather than as a population-level estimate.  No p-value is recorded in the supplied row, and no confidence interval is recorded; the chapter therefore does not attach inferential significance beyond the finite-table result.

\paragraph{Admissible interpretation.}
The passed status attaches to the experiment identifier $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$.  Its content is the following bounded claim: across the three loaded organisms, the leucine $CUN/UUR$ usage contrast is not sign-uniform, and the measured contrast has a positive partial correlation with the recorded tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio under the stated amino-acid-composition control.  This is enough to keep the packet as a translation-layer coordinate in the narrow sense of organism-level coding usage paired with organism-level tRNA annotation.  It is not enough to assert that a ribosome has realized a mechanism, that an individual transcript has a particular elongation behavior, or that the relation is a general biological law.

\paragraph{Local exclusion boundary.}
The contacts used here are codon-usage tables and tRNA gene-copy annotations.  Those contacts can test sequence-composition summaries, organism labels, synonymous leucine-family contrasts, and tRNA-Leu copy-number spectra.  They do not measure folded structure, thermodynamic admissibility, molecular function, ribosome dynamics, or universal directionality.  Consequently no translation in the full mechanistic sense, no folding conclusion, no physical-admissibility conclusion, no biological-function conclusion, no ribosome-mediated mechanism, and no universal cross-organism rule follows from this row unless a separate contact supplies that missing evidence.  The sign reversal is retained as context-dependent finite data, not promoted into a rule that must hold outside the measured table.

\paragraph{Audit verdict.}
The row is accepted as a finite organism-level coding statistic with status $\mathrm{passed}$.  The exact data supporting that verdict are the three loaded organisms, the three logarithmic contrasts, the non-uniform sign vector, the Pearson value $1.0$, and the controlled partial Pearson value $1.0$ at $n=3$.  The result is scientifically useful because it separates a concrete codon-usage/tRNA-copy relation from stronger biological interpretations that the recorded contacts do not observe.
