\subsection{Cross-organism Leu codon-use gate}
\label{sec:h3-translation-realization-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record isolates a Leu-module statistic rather than a whole-cell mechanism.  The carrier is the residual contrast between the CUN and UUR leucine codon families, paired with organism labels and an organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number spectrum.  The intended readout is the per-organism sign and magnitude of $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, compared with the corresponding tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio after the synonymous-class comparison has been controlled for amino-acid composition.  The row is therefore not a claim about protein output as such; it is a bounded check on whether a codon-use coordinate changes in the same direction as an independently recorded tRNA-copy coordinate.

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The finite run \texttt{675fdddb3187} reports status \texttt{passed} for the experiment \texttt{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}.  The loaded organisms are human, budding yeast, and \textit{E. coli} K-12, encoded in the run as \texttt{homo\_sapiens}, \texttt{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}, and \texttt{escherichia\_coli\_k12}.  Thus the row count is $n=3$.  The recorded log-ratio vector and sign vector are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda &= (1.3514980212896148,\ -0.24869078488829677,\ 1.0086640520051702),\\
\operatorname{sgn}(\lambda) &= (1,\ -1,\ 1).
\end{aligned}
$$
The sign-uniformity flag is false.  The finite row therefore contains one negative CUN-over-UUR residual among two positive residuals, so the observed cross-organism pattern is not a single invariant direction.

\paragraph{Correlation readout.}
The same run gives Pearson correlation $1.0$ for the paired statistic, and gives a positive partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu ratio after amino-acid composition control:
$$
\begin{aligned}
r_{\mathrm{Pearson}} &= 1.0,\\
r_{\mathrm{partial}\mid \mathrm{AA}} &= 1.0,\\
n_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite record, the support condition is therefore met: the CUN/UUR contrast is not uniform across the three organisms, and the reported controlled statistic is positive with respect to the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio.  The result is read as a translation-layer contact only in the narrow sense that codon-usage summaries and tRNA gene-copy summaries are both externally recorded sequence-derived quantities and the paired finite statistic is reported for them.

\paragraph{Negative boundary.}
The limitation is local.  No organism-level translation event, folded structure, physical-admissibility judgment, biological function, ribosome-mediated mechanism, or universal cross-organism law is entailed by these three rows unless another measurement supplies that specific item.  In particular, a non-uniform sign is not a folding result, and a positive partial correlation is not a mechanistic model of ribosome dynamics.  The record also does not license a global direction rule for CUN versus UUR: the reported signs are $(1,-1,1)$, so the finite table itself contains context-dependent reversal.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
For the stated carrier, the finite evidence is adequate.  The module classification is a Leu codon-usage residual paired with organism labels and tRNA-Leu copy spectra; the measured layer is the bounded translation-realization statistic supplied by codon-use and tRNA-copy contacts.  The claim should remain exactly at that layer: the verified row supports the organism-level paired statistic and rejects sign uniformity, while all higher biological interpretations remain outside the row.
