\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine usage as a bounded organism-level contact}\label{subsec:h3-translation-realization-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object and finite carrier.}
This record concerns the leucine codon split between the CUN family and the UUR family, read only at the level of organism-indexed sequence and annotation summaries.  The finite carrier is an organism-labelled table whose rows contain a codon-usage residual for the leucine module together with a matching organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio.  The distinguished coordinates are the signed quantity $\log(\#CUN/\#UUR)$, a tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, an organism label, and an amino-acid-composition control condition.  The row set recorded for experiment $675fdddb3187$ contains three organisms:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R &= \{\mathrm{homo\_sapiens},\ \mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae},\ \mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}\},\\
|R| &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
The biological reading is therefore deliberately narrow: each row asks whether the organism-level CUN-versus-UUR leucine usage statistic is aligned with the corresponding tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectrum after controlling the comparison inside the amino-acid class.  The carrier is not a ribosome trace, a protein-folding assay, a thermodynamic admissibility certificate, or a functional phenotype.

\paragraph{Measured signs.}
The verified run reports the log-ratio vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
L &= (1.3514980212896148,\ -0.24869078488829677,\ 1.0086640520051702),\\
\operatorname{sgn}(L) &= (1,\ -1,\ 1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Hence the sign is not constant across the three loaded organisms: the recorded Boolean value is $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$.  This is the local reason the packet remains interesting as a cross-organism statistic.  A single fixed direction for CUN over UUR is not what the finite table shows.  Instead, the human and bacterial rows lie on the positive side of the CUN/UUR log-ratio coordinate, while the yeast row lies on the negative side.  The sign change is only a row-level observation; it is not itself a mechanistic claim about ribosome behavior.

\paragraph{Paired statistic.}
The same run records a positive paired association between the CUN/UUR log-ratio coordinate and the tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate.  At the finite-row level the reported Pearson value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{Pearson}} &= 1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the reported amino-acid-composition-controlled statistic is
$$
\begin{aligned}
 n &= 3,\\
 \rho_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The value $1.0$ is cited here only as the value recorded for this three-row table, not as a population parameter.  With $n=3$, the statistic should be read as a finite compatibility check between two organism-level summaries: the CUN/UUR leucine usage residual and the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio.  The experiment status is recorded as $\mathrm{passed}$ for the stated test, namely that the signs are not uniform and that the paired statistic is positive after the specified amino-acid composition control.

\paragraph{Admissible interpretation.}
The admissible conclusion is that the finite cross-organism table supports a bounded translation-layer contact between a leucine codon-usage coordinate and an organism-level tRNA-Leu annotation coordinate.  The contact is statistical and organism-indexed.  It does not say that CUN codons are universally favored, nor that UUR codons are universally disfavored, nor that either direction is a global biological law.  The local evidence is the coexistence of sign reversal,
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1,\ -1,\ 1) \neq (1,\ 1,\ 1) \quad \text{and} \quad (1,\ -1,\ 1) \neq (-1,\ -1,\ -1),
\end{aligned}
$$
with the recorded positive paired association.  Thus the packet is best treated as a controlled codon-usage/tRNA-copy relation, not as a standalone statement about translation dynamics.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
No broader biological realization follows from these rows without an additional contact of the appropriate kind.  In particular, the table does not by itself establish translation as a process-level event, ribosome-mediated mechanism, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, or universality across organisms.  The run explicitly records that the CUN/UUR codon-usage and tRNA-Leu copy contact does not establish protein folding, does not establish biological function or physical admissibility, does not lift the statistic into a global law, and treats sign reversal as context-dependent rather than universally directed.  The chapter therefore closes at the level of a finite organism-level correlation: three named organisms, three log-ratio entries, three signs, one reported Pearson value, and one reported partial Pearson value under the stated control.
