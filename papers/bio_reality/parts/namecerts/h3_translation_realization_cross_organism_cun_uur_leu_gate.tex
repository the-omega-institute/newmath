\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine gate}
\label{sec:h3-translation-realization-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite biological row.}
This record treats the leucine codon split between the CUN family and the UUR family as an organism-level statistical contact, not as a direct molecular mechanism.  The carrier is the residual comparison between $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ and the corresponding tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, with organism labels kept explicit and amino-acid-composition control required before the row is allowed to speak at the translation-realization layer.  The finite run $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$ for claim $\mathrm{h3.translation\_realization.cross\_organism.cun\_uur\_leu\_gate}$ loaded three organism rows: Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K12.  The reported experiment identifier is $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$, and its status is passed.

\paragraph{Measured signs and paired statistic.}
The observed log-ratio list for $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702).
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding sign list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\;-1,\;+1),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite table explicitly records $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$.  Thus the row does not support a single cross-organism direction for the CUN/UUR split.  Instead, it records a context-dependent reversal: the yeast row has the negative sign, while the human and E. coli K12 rows have positive signs.  The paired readout also reports a Pearson value of $1.0$ for the finite statistic present in the run.  After the required amino-acid-composition control, the recorded partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio is
$$
\begin{aligned}
n &= 3,\\
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
No p-value, hit rate, lambda parameter, separate row-classification label, or independent module-class scalar is present in this finite row; the numerical content available here is therefore the three-organism row count, the three log-ratios, the three signs, the non-uniformity flag, the Pearson scalar, and the controlled partial-correlation scalar.

\paragraph{Admissible interpretation.}
The row supports only the bounded claim that, in this finite three-organism table, the CUN/UUR leucine usage split is not sign-uniform and is positively paired with the organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-ratio statistic after the stated composition control.  The contact uses per-organism codon-usage summaries and organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy annotations.  Those contacts are suitable for a codon-usage and tRNA-copy comparison, but they are summaries of sequence and genome annotation data rather than observations of ribosome motion, peptide production, folding, or organismal phenotype.  For that reason, the statistic is a translation-layer clue only in the narrow sense of a controlled codon-usage/tRNA-copy association.

\paragraph{Local exclusion boundary.}
The finite row does not, on its own, establish that ribosomes realize the proposed relation in vivo, does not prove a folding outcome, does not certify physical admissibility, does not show biological function, and does not give a universal cross-organism law.  A further contact would be required for each such promotion: ribosome-profiling or matched translation-efficiency data for ribosome-level realization, structural assays for folding, physical measurements for admissibility, phenotype or biochemical assays for function, and a broader independently sampled organism panel for any universality claim.  The present record therefore remains a bounded organism-level usage-and-copy-number result: it accepts the sign reversal and the controlled positive association in the finite table, while refusing to convert that association into mechanism, structure, function, or global biological necessity.
