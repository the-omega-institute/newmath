\subsection{Cross-Organism CUN/UUR Leucine Gate}
\label{subsec:h3-translation-realization-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Finite record.}
This record concerns an organism-level leucine codon-usage comparison, not a direct observation of a translated peptide.  The finite carrier is the paired residual formed from the CUN and UUR leucine codon blocks together with organism labels, a tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, and an amino-acid-composition control.  The attached run loaded three organism rows: \texttt{homo\_sapiens}, \texttt{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}, and \texttt{escherichia\_coli\_k12}.  For these rows the reported statistic is the signed quantity $\log(\#CUN/\#UUR)$, read with a tRNA-Leu copy-number comparison after control at synonymous-codon scale.  The observed log-ratios are
$$
\begin{aligned}
&1.3514980212896148,\quad -0.24869078488829677,\quad 1.0086640520051702,
\end{aligned}
$$
with corresponding signs
$$
\begin{aligned}
&1,\quad -1,\quad 1.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not uniform across the measured organisms: the recorded value of \texttt{sign\_uniform} is \texttt{false}.

\paragraph{Codon-window coordinate.}
The companion code-window fact fixes the finite set used by the CUN-side coordinate.  The measured block has cardinality $20$ and is recorded as $M=WNR\cup CUN$ with
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&AAA,AAG,ACA,ACG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,\
&CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This list is used only as a finite codon-coordinate support.  It does not by itself report ribosome occupancy, peptide output, folding, selection, or organismal phenotype.  Its role in this chapter is to name the codon-side residue that is compared with organism labels and tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectra.

\paragraph{Observed cross-organism relation.}
The paired experiment is recorded under \texttt{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation} with status \texttt{passed}.  Across the three loaded organism rows, the ordinary Pearson coefficient between the CUN/UUR statistic and the tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate is $1.0$.  The amino-acid-composition-controlled partial statistic is also reported as
$$
\begin{aligned}
 n&=3,\\
 r_{\mathrm{partial}}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The result therefore supports a narrow statement: in this finite record, the CUN/UUR leucine usage sign changes across organisms, and the recorded magnitude/sign statistic is positively aligned with the organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio after the stated synonymous-class control.  The evidence is a cross-organism table contact plus a tRNA gene-copy annotation contact; it is not a single-organism rule and is not a statement about all genomes.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The table contact can check organism-level codon-usage summaries, and the tRNA contact can check organism-level gene-copy spectra by anticodon family.  Neither contact observes ribosome movement, peptide production, folding, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal biological law.  Consequently, no translation event, folding statement, physical-admissibility statement, functional statement, mechanistic statement, or universal cross-organism rule follows from this record unless a separate contact supplies that missing observation.  In particular, the sign reversal is context-dependent in the finite rows reported here, and the raw log-ratio without the amino-acid-composition control is not treated as a biological-layer conclusion.

\paragraph{Resulting claim.}
The chapter records a bounded organism-level statistic: CUN versus UUR leucine usage is admissible here as a checked cross-organism codon-usage/tRNA-copy relation.  The ready part of the claim is exactly the finite association over the three named organisms, with non-uniform signs $(1,-1,1)$, log-ratios $(1.3514980212896148,-0.24869078488829677,1.0086640520051702)$, Pearson coefficient $1.0$, and controlled partial coefficient $1.0$ at $n=3$.  Everything beyond that table-level relation remains outside the claim unless additional data attach it.
