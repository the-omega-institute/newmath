\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine gate}
\label{sec:h3-translation-realization-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record isolates a narrow organism-level contact between synonymous leucine codon usage and the annotated spectrum of leucine tRNA genes.  The object under test is not leucine chemistry, protein behavior, or a general statement about the genetic code.  It is the finite statistic obtained by pairing, for each organism in the row set, the signed residual
$\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ with an organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio, after controlling for amino-acid composition through a synonymous-class comparison.  The carrier is therefore a small table of coordinates: organism label, CUN-versus-UUR log ratio, tRNA-Leu copy-ratio contact, and the amino-acid-composition control under which the comparison is allowed to be read.

\paragraph{Observed finite table.}
The checked run is \texttt{675fdddb3187}, with experiment identifier \texttt{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}.  The row labels loaded in the finite comparison are \texttt{homo\_sapiens}, \texttt{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}, and \texttt{escherichia\_coli\_k12}.  The recorded CUN/UUR log-ratio vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with sign vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is explicitly non-uniform across the three organisms: the finite flag \texttt{sign\_uniform} is \texttt{false}.  In this row set, yeast carries the negative CUN-over-UUR sign, while the human and \textit{E. coli} K-12 rows carry positive signs.  This is the first admissible observation: the packet cannot be reduced to a universal direction for CUN relative to UUR.

\paragraph{Paired statistic.}
The same run records a Pearson coefficient of $1.0$ for the paired organism-level statistic used in the validation row.  It also records a positive partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu ratio after the amino-acid-composition control, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
n &= 3,\\
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers are interpreted only at the resolution at which they are recorded.  They show that, for the three loaded organisms, the non-uniform CUN/UUR sign and magnitude are aligned with the external tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate under the stated control.  The table is too small to support an asymptotic population claim or a broad biological rule, but it is sufficient for the finite BioReality record to distinguish two cases: a constant sign across organisms is not what the rows show, and the controlled paired statistic is positive in the available rows.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
Two external contacts are used.  The first is a per-organism codon-usage readback, treated as a sequence-database summary of coding-sequence composition.  The second is an organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy readback, treated as a genome-annotation summary by anticodon family.  Each contact has limited resolution.  Codon-usage tables summarize counted or frequency-weighted coding sequences; they do not observe elongation kinetics.  tRNA gene-copy annotations summarize genomic inventory; they do not by themselves measure charged tRNA abundance, ribosome occupancy, or proteome output.  The admissible contact is therefore the matched organism-level comparison of codon-use residuals against tRNA-Leu copy-number spectra, not a direct observation of a molecular mechanism.

\paragraph{Decision.}
The finite run status is \texttt{passed}, verified at \texttt{2026-05-31T03:04:10+00:00}.  The local decision is that the CUN/UUR leucine gate is supported as a bounded cross-organism codon-usage/tRNA-copy statistic.  In compact form, the checked row can be written as
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{rows} &= 3,\\
\mathrm{signs} &= (+1,-1,+1),\\
\mathrm{sign\_uniform} &= \mathrm{false},\\
\rho_{\mathrm{Pearson}} &= 1.0,\\
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}\mid \mathrm{AA\ control}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The result is not that CUN is globally favored over UUR, nor that UUR is globally favored over CUN.  The result is that the sign changes across the three loaded organisms, and the measured row set reports perfect positive alignment between the controlled CUN/UUR coordinate and the tRNA-Leu ratio coordinate.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
No broader biological conclusion is licensed by this chapter alone.  The row does not establish organismal translation as a realized dynamical process, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, ribosome-mediated mechanism, or a universal cross-organism law.  Those claims would require separate contacts at the appropriate resolution, such as ribosome profiling, charged-tRNA measurements, protein-output assays, structural evidence, or functional intervention data.  The present record remains deliberately narrower: it is an externally paired, organism-level statistic relating synonymous leucine codon usage to annotated tRNA-Leu copy spectra under an amino-acid-composition control.
