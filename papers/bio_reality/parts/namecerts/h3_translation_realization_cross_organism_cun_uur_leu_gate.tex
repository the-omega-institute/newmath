\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine usage gate}
\label{sec:bioreality-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Finite row.}
This record treats the leucine codon split between the CUN block and the UUR block as an organism-indexed measurement problem.  The carrier is the residual coordinate obtained from the per-organism codon-usage contrast $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, paired with an organism label and checked against an independently curated tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio.  The row is not a claim about a protein product.  It is a bounded statistical contact between synonymous-codon usage and genome-annotation summaries for leucine tRNA families.

The code-side finite support used by the companion row is the twenty-element set
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Its recorded cardinality is $|M|=20$.  Within the present row, only the leucine contrast is used as the biological coordinate: CUN contributes $\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}$, while UUR contributes $\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}$.  The larger set $M$ therefore records the finite codon support available to the packet, not a separate protein-level assertion.

\paragraph{Observed organism rows.}
The checked dataset contains three organism labels: $\mathrm{homo\_sapiens}$, $\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}$.  The corresponding observed signs for $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ are
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1,-1,1),
\end{aligned}
$$
with finite log-ratio values
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\,-0.24869078488829677,\,1.0086640520051702).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not uniform across the measured organisms.  The row also records a Pearson correlation value of $1.0$ for the paired statistic and a partial Pearson value of $1.0$ after the amino-acid-composition control, with $n=3$.  These numbers are reported as finite-row evidence only: with three organisms, the statistic is a small controlled contact, not a population-level law.

\paragraph{Admissible interpretation.}
The admissible reading is that the CUN/UUR leucine split behaves as an organism-dependent codon-usage coordinate whose sign and magnitude can be compared to an organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number spectrum.  The finite observation supports the local statement that, in the three recorded organisms, the CUN-over-UUR contrast is not sign-uniform and is positively paired with the controlled tRNA-Leu copy-ratio statistic.  The amino-acid-composition control is essential: a raw codon log-ratio by itself would only report sequence composition, whereas the controlled statistic is what lets the row remain within the translation-realization layer.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
This row does not certify active ribosomal translation, translation kinetics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic causation, or a universal cross-organism rule.  The codon-usage contact consists of per-organism coding-sequence summaries, and the tRNA contact consists of genome-annotation copy-number summaries.  Those contacts can support a bounded codon-usage--tRNA-copy relation, but they do not observe ribosomes, proteins, folding, function, or physical realization.  Any such stronger statement would require a separate measurement channel, such as ribosome profiling, protein-output data, folding evidence, or a direct functional assay.  In particular, the sign reversal is recorded as context-dependent behavior, not as a universal direction for the leucine code block.
