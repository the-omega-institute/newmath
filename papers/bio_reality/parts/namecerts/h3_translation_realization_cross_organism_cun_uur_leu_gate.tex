\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine usage as a bounded translation-layer contact}
\label{subsec:h3-translation-realization-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Packet.}
This record treats the leucine codon families $CUN$ and $UUR$ as an organism-indexed measurement problem, not as a free-standing biological mechanism.  The carrier is the residual coordinate formed from the codon-usage contrast $\log(CUN/UUR)$ together with an organism label, an external tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio, and an amino-acid-composition control.  The biological object is therefore a finite table whose rows are organisms and whose measured fields compare synonymous leucine codon families against the corresponding tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectrum.  The experiment run identifier is $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$, and the recorded experiment name is \texttt{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}.  Three organism rows are present: \texttt{homo\_sapiens}, \texttt{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}, and \texttt{escherichia\_coli\_k12}.  Thus the row count used for the finite statistic is $n=3$.

\paragraph{Measured coordinate.}
For each organism row the observed contrast is the sign and magnitude of $\log(CUN/UUR)$ after the packet has been restricted to a leucine-module comparison and paired with the tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate.  The finite run records the ordered log-ratio list
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with the corresponding sign list
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
The Boolean uniform-sign field is false.  This is the first finite check: the three-row table does not collapse to a single direction for the CUN-versus-UUR leucine contrast.  In particular, the yeast row has the opposite sign from the human and bacterial rows in the recorded ordering.  The statistic is therefore organism-sensitive at the level actually measured here.

\paragraph{Paired tRNA-Leu statistic.}
The second finite check asks whether the organism-sensitive codon-usage coordinate is aligned with the tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate once the synonymous-class control is applied.  The run records a Pearson value of $1.0$ for the paired statistic and a partial Pearson value of $1.0$ for the tRNA-Leu ratio field, again with $n=3$.  Written as the reported scalar fields,
$$
\begin{aligned}
r &= 1.0,\\
r_{\mathrm{partial}}(\log(CUN/UUR),\mathrm{tRNA\text{-}Leu}\mid\mathrm{AA\ composition}) &= 1.0,\\
n &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
No $p$-value, confidence interval, resampling interval, or independent replication count is recorded in this finite row.  The result is consequently admissible only as a bounded three-row contact: it supports the existence of a recorded organism-level alignment in this table, but it does not estimate a population law by itself.  The recorded status of the run is \texttt{passed}, so the chapter may use the finite numbers above as checked data for this packet.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The local conclusion is narrow.  The packet reports that the leucine codon-family contrast $\log(CUN/UUR)$ is not sign-uniform across the three measured organisms and that, in the same finite run, its paired relation with the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio is positive after the amino-acid-composition control.  This is a translation-layer contact only in the restricted sense that codon-usage tables and tRNA gene-copy annotations are both translation-related genomic summaries.  It is not a direct observation of ribosome motion, decoding speed, peptide yield, protein abundance, folding, activity, or organismal fitness.  The codon-usage contact supplies per-organism sequence-composition summaries; the tRNA contact supplies per-organism gene-copy summaries.  Neither contact, alone or together, observes a translated protein product.

\paragraph{Local exclusion.}
The finite run explicitly records that the CUN/UUR and tRNA-Leu contact does not establish protein folding, does not establish biological function or physical admissibility, and does not elevate the observed sign reversal to a global biological law.  The same boundary is imposed here in chapter-local form: no additional translation conclusion, no folded structure, no physical admissibility claim, no biological function, no ribosome-mediated mechanism, and no universality statement follows from these rows unless a separate measurement supplies that missing contact.  The context-dependent sign reversal is therefore a measured feature of this organism-indexed table, not a rule for all organisms and not a mechanistic account of how the ribosome realizes leucine incorporation.

\paragraph{Audit verdict.}
Within the finite table supplied to this record, the claim is ready at the bounded statistic level.  The support consists of three loaded organism rows, the non-uniform sign list $(+1,-1,+1)$ for $\log(CUN/UUR)$, the reported Pearson value $1.0$, the reported partial Pearson value $1.0$ after amino-acid-composition control, and the explicit non-promotion statements attached to the run.  The evidence is sufficient for a self-contained chapter about an organism-indexed codon-usage/tRNA-Leu association.  It is not sufficient for stronger biological layers without new data.
