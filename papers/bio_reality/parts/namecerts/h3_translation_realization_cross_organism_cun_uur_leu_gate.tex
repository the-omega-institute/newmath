\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine gate}\label{subsec:h3-translation-realization-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns the leucine synonymous split between the CUN and UUR codon families, read at organism level rather than at protein, ribosome, or phenotype level. The finite carrier is the paired residual consisting of the controlled codon-usage contrast $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, the organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, the organism label, and an amino-acid-composition control. The loaded rows in run $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$ are $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli\ K12}$. Thus the row count is $n=3$, and every assertion below is restricted to this three-row table.

\paragraph{Observed finite readout.}
The measured CUN-over-UUR log ratios are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{human}} &= 1.3514980212896148,\\
R_{\mathrm{yeast}} &= -0.24869078488829677,\\
R_{\mathrm{E.coli\ K12}} &= 1.0086640520051702.
\end{aligned}
$$
Their signs are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{sgn}(R) &= (1,-1,1),\\
\mathrm{sign\_uniform} &= \mathrm{false}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite table therefore does not support a single direction for the CUN/UUR leucine contrast across the three organisms. In this packet, the non-uniform sign is not treated as a biological law; it is only the observed pattern in the rows loaded for the run.

\paragraph{Paired tRNA-Leu statistic.}
The same run reports a positive association between the CUN/UUR residual and the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio after the stated amino-acid-composition control. The scalar summary is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{Pearson}} &= 1.0,\\
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0,\\
 n_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
The experiment status is $\mathrm{passed}$. Since the table has only three organism rows, the value $1.0$ is recorded as a finite-row alignment in the attached data, not as a stable population estimate. No $p$-value, confidence interval, resampling interval, or independent holdout row is present in the supplied record, so this chapter does not attach inferential strength beyond the listed scalars.

\paragraph{Admissible interpretation.}
The admissible scientific content is narrow: for these three organisms, the CUN/UUR leucine codon-usage contrast changes sign across organisms and, in the supplied paired table, aligns positively with the corresponding tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio after the amino-acid-composition control. The relevant contacts are per-organism codon-usage summaries and organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number annotations. Those contacts can support a bounded codon-usage/tRNA-copy comparison at the translation-realization layer only in this measured sense: they connect sequence-composition summaries to a tRNA-copy statistic. They do not observe ribosome traffic, translation kinetics, peptide output, folded structure, biochemical activity, or organismal fitness.

\paragraph{Local exclusion boundary.}
Nothing in this packet by itself establishes a realized translation event, protein folding, physical admissibility, molecular function, ribosome-mediated mechanism, or universal cross-organism principle. A stronger biological conclusion would require a separate contact that directly measures the relevant object: for example ribosome profiling or tRNA-adaptation evidence for translation dynamics, structural evidence for folding, assay evidence for function, or a broader organism panel for universality. The sign reversal is therefore recorded as context-dependent in the finite table, and the positive partial correlation is recorded only as the paired statistic of run $675\mathrm{fdddb}3187$.
