This record concerns the leucine synonymous split between the CUN family and the UUR family, read only at the level of organism-indexed codon-usage summaries paired with organism-indexed tRNA-Leu gene-copy summaries. $$

Thus the sign is not constant across the three rows.  This is the first admissibility check for the packet: the statistic is not being treated as a single-species codon preference, and it is not being collapsed into a universal direction for all organisms.

\paragraph{Controlled pairing.}
The recorded test is not the raw statement that one leucine codon family is globally preferred.  The statistic is the paired cross-organism question: after controlling for amino-acid composition through a per-codon synonymous-class null, does the CUN/UUR logarithmic residual vary with the organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio?  The finite run reports $n=3$ paired organism rows and a Pearson coefficient of $1.0$ for the available paired statistic.  The reported partial Pearson coefficient after the amino-acid-composition control is also $1.0$ on the same three rows:
$$
\begin{aligned}
r_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0,\\
n &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite table, the packet therefore passes the proposed organism-level screen: the sign changes across organisms, and the measured paired coordinate is positively aligned with the tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate in the controlled statistic.  The experiment identifier for this finite run is \texttt{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}, with run id \texttt{675fdddb3187}; the recorded status is \texttt{passed}, verified at \texttt{2026-05-31T04:05:55+00:00}.

\paragraph{What the rows establish.}
The local conclusion is deliberately narrow.  The three-row table supports an organism-level contact between a leucine synonymous-codon usage coordinate and a tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate.  It says that, for the organisms loaded in the finite run, the CUN/UUR residual is not sign-uniform and that its paired statistic is positively correlated with the available tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate after the stated amino-acid-composition control.  The biological object is therefore a finite cross-organism usage-and-copy-number relation, not a direct observation of a ribosome event.  The admissible scientific reading is that this codon-family split can be used as a bounded translation-layer screening coordinate when, and only when, the codon-usage rows and the organism-matched tRNA-copy rows are both present.

\paragraph{Local exclusion boundary.}
No stronger biological conclusion is licensed by these rows.  A CUN/UUR sign, even when paired with tRNA-Leu copy number, does not by itself demonstrate realized translation in a ribosome, folded protein structure, physical admissibility of a protein product, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or a universal rule across organisms.  The recorded finite run explicitly keeps the following exclusions: the contact does not establish protein folding; it does not establish biological function or physical admissibility; it does not promote the statistic to a global biological law; and the sign reversal is context-dependent rather than a universal directionality.  Any later claim about ribosome dynamics would require an additional contact such as ribosome profiling or a directly comparable translation-efficiency readout.  Any later claim about structure or function would require separate structure-level or function-level observations.

\paragraph{Failure modes.}
The packet would fail this local screen if the measured organisms all had the same CUN/UUR sign independently of the tRNA-Leu copy spectrum, or if sign changes occurred but the controlled paired statistic lost its positive relation to the tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate.  It would also fail if the only available evidence were identifier spelling, codon labels from a code table, or unpaired codon-usage and tRNA-copy summaries.  Those data types can name the codon families or describe separate resources, but they do not supply the organism-matched controlled statistic used here.  The present record is ready only because the finite table includes the three organism labels, the three log-ratio values, the non-uniform sign vector, and the controlled positive paired statistic.
