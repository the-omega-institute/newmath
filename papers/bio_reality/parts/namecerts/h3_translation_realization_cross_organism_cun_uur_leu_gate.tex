\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record treats the leucine codon split between the $CUN$ block and the $UUR$ block as an organism-indexed measurement problem.  The finite carrier is the row family consisting of an organism label, a signed logarithmic codon-usage residual $\log(CUN/UUR)$, and an organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number comparison.  The tested row set contains exactly three loaded organisms: Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K12.  The claim identifier attached to the run is $\texttt{h3.translation\_realization.cross\_organism.cun\_uur\_leu\_gate}$, and the experiment identifier is $\texttt{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$.

\paragraph{Measured signs.}
The concrete log-ratio list recorded for the three organisms is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R &= (1.3514980212896148,\,-0.24869078488829677,\,1.0086640520051702),\\
\operatorname{sgn}(R) &= (1,\,-1,\,1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the measured sign is not constant across the three organisms.  In the notation of the finite run, $\texttt{sign\_uniform}=\texttt{false}$.  This is the first admissibility check: the packet is not merely reading a fixed codon-table identity, because the organism-indexed residual changes sign in the available rows.

\paragraph{Paired tRNA comparison.}
The same run reports a positive paired comparison between the $CUN/UUR$ log-ratio coordinate and the organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate.  The recorded Pearson value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho &= 1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the reported partial Pearson value after the amino-acid-composition control is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0,\\
n &= 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row count is small, so this is not a population-level law.  It is nevertheless a finite successful contact for the stated packet: the sign is non-uniform, and the controlled paired statistic is positive on the three recorded organism rows.  No p-value, confidence interval, regression slope, or held-out validation split is present in the finite record, so the numerical conclusion should be read as an exact statement about this run rather than as an inferential guarantee about unmeasured organisms.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The admissible scientific content is narrow.  The row family says that, in the loaded organisms, the external codon-usage summary for leucine has a context-dependent $CUN$ versus $UUR$ imbalance, and that this imbalance is positively aligned with the recorded tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate after the stated amino-acid-composition control.  The datum does not require the sign to have the same direction in every organism: Saccharomyces cerevisiae contributes the negative entry, while Homo sapiens and Escherichia coli K12 contribute positive entries.  Therefore the usable reading is a cross-organism relation between a synonymous leucine codon-usage coordinate and a tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate, not a universal directionality principle.

\paragraph{Boundary of the row.}
This packet does not by itself establish an actual ribosome-mediated translation process.  It also does not establish folded protein structure, physical admissibility of a molecular conformation, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Those would require additional contacts of the corresponding kind, such as ribosome-profiling rows for process-level translation, structural measurements for folding, thermodynamic or physical constraints for admissibility, or functional assays for biological effect.  The present run is therefore retained only as an organism-level codon-usage and tRNA-copy association.
