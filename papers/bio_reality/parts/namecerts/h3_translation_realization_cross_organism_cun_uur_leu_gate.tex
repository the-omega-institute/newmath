\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine usage gate}\label{sec:biorereality-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Packet and measured coordinates.}
This record isolates the leucine codon pair $(\mathrm{CUN},\mathrm{UUR})$ as an organism-indexed usage statistic rather than as a direct claim about translation machinery. The finite carrier is a per-organism residual coordinate containing the sign and magnitude of $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, an organism label, a matched tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, and an amino-acid-composition control. The measured organisms in the current row are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal{O} = \{&\mathrm{homo\_sapiens},\ \mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae},\ \mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated codon-window support also records the twenty-element set
$$
\begin{aligned}
M = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with recorded cardinality $|M|=20$. In this chapter the set $M$ is only a finite codon-coordinate support. It does not, by itself, identify a ribosomal process, a folded protein state, a cellular function, or a general biological law.

\paragraph{Finite readout.}
The row reports three organism-level log-ratios for the CUN-over-UUR leucine contrast:
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\ -0.24869078488829677,\ 1.0086640520051702).
\end{aligned}
$$
Their signs are
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\ -1,\ +1),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the sign-uniformity flag is false. This is the first local check: the statistic is not being read as a single organism-independent direction. The row also records a paired tRNA-Leu copy-ratio statistic. For the three loaded organisms, the Pearson value is $1.0$, and the amino-acid-composition-controlled partial Pearson entry is
$$
\begin{aligned}
r_{\mathrm{partial}} = 1.0,\qquad n = 3.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite result is therefore a bounded positive association in the available three-row table: after applying the stated synonymous-class control, the CUN/UUR log-ratio coordinate varies with the recorded tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio in this small organism panel. No $p$-value, confidence interval, regression coefficient beyond the Pearson entries, hit-rate, module-classification scalar, or lambda value is recorded in the supplied row; those quantities are therefore not asserted here.

\paragraph{Admissible interpretation.}
The biological content of the packet is limited to two contact classes: organism-level codon-usage summaries and organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number summaries. The codon-usage contact reports counts or frequencies over coding sequences. The tRNA contact reports genome-annotation copy counts by anticodon family. When paired in the supplied table, these contacts support the narrow statement that the CUN/UUR leucine usage contrast is not sign-uniform across the three listed organisms and that the recorded controlled correlation with tRNA-Leu copy ratio is positive in that finite panel.

This is not a direct observation of translation in a ribosome, elongation kinetics, decoding speed, or protein output. The row can be used as a translation-layer screening statistic only because the measured coordinates concern codon usage and matching tRNA gene-copy spectra. The contact does not make the stronger claim that a particular transcript is translated in a particular cell, that a peptide folds, that a protein has a function, or that a physical admissibility condition is satisfied. Those stronger conclusions would require separate measurements aimed at those layers.

\paragraph{Local null boundary.}
The break condition for this packet is explicit. It would fail if the sign of $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ were uniform across all measured organisms regardless of tRNA-Leu copy spectrum, or if the sign varied but the controlled association with tRNA-Leu copy ratio disappeared. The present row does not trigger either failure mode: the signs are $(+1,-1,+1)$, and the controlled partial Pearson entry is $1.0$ with $n=3$. The outcome is consequently recorded as passed for this finite table.

The boundary is equally explicit. No standalone claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or universality follows from the CUN/UUR sign pattern or from the tRNA-copy association alone. A separate ribosome-contact, protein-structure contact, functional assay, or physical measurement would be needed before any of those claims could enter the chapter. The observed sign reversal is therefore treated as context-dependent variation in an organism-level statistic, not as a universal directionality rule.

\paragraph{Audit verdict.}
The finite row supplies enough local data for a self-contained BioReality entry: the measured organism list has three rows; the CUN/UUR log-ratios and signs are present; sign uniformity is explicitly false; the paired Pearson and controlled partial Pearson values are both $1.0$; and the associated codon-coordinate support has cardinality $20$. The evidential status is a small, curated, organism-level paired statistic. Its scientific use is to mark a concrete screening contact between leucine codon usage and tRNA-Leu gene-copy spectra, while keeping all mechanistic and higher-layer biological conclusions outside the claim.
