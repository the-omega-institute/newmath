\subsection{Cross-organism Leu codon-usage gate}
\label{sec:bior reality-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Record.}
This chapter records a bounded organism-level contact for the leucine codon split between the CUN family and the UUR family.  The carrier is the residual statistic formed from $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, paired with an organism label and compared with an organism-level tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio after controlling for amino-acid composition inside the synonymous leucine class.  The row set used by the finite check contains exactly three organisms: $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli\ K12}$.  The experiment identifier is $\mathrm{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$, and the recorded status is passed.

\paragraph{Codon support.}
The associated code-level support fact fixes the finite module $M$ as a set of twenty codons.  In the recorded order it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row reports $|M|=20$ and identifies this support as the union of the WNR block with the CUN block.  For the present chapter, the relevant internal distinction is not a protein-level prediction but the codon-family contrast between the four CUN leucine codons and the two UUR leucine codons, evaluated per organism.

\paragraph{Finite statistic.}
The measured log-ratio values for $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ are
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\ -0.24869078488829677,\ 1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with signs
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\ -1,\ +1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not uniform across the three organisms: the yeast row has the negative sign while the human and bacterial rows are positive.  The paired tRNA-Leu comparison records a Pearson value of $1.0$ and a partial Pearson value of $1.0$ after the stated amino-acid-composition control, with $n=3$.  This is a finite-row statement: it says that, in the recorded three-organism table, the CUN-versus-UUR leucine usage residual changes sign and is positively ordered with the available tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate under the control used by the experiment.

\paragraph{Readback.}
The biological readback is deliberately narrow.  Per-organism codon-usage tables supply codon-count or frequency summaries for coding sequences, while genome annotation supplies tRNA-Leu gene-copy counts by anticodon family.  These two contacts are enough to ask whether a CUN/UUR leucine residual is organism-dependent and whether it tracks the tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate in this finite table.  They do not inspect translating ribosomes, nascent chains, folded structures, biochemical activity, viability, or any global rule for all organisms.

\paragraph{Boundary.}
No direct translation event, ribosome-mediated mechanism, folding result, physical admissibility claim, biological function claim, or universal cross-organism law follows from this row.  A raw CUN/UUR log-ratio alone is also insufficient: the statistic is admitted here only because it is paired with organism labels, tRNA-Leu copy data, and an amino-acid-composition control.  The observed sign reversal is therefore reported as context dependence inside the finite table, not as a universal direction of codon preference and not as a higher-layer biological conclusion.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The chapter is ready as a bounded BioReality record.  Its positive content is the three-row organism-level fact that $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ is not sign-uniform and that the controlled paired statistic records Pearson $1.0$ and partial Pearson $1.0$ against the tRNA-Leu copy-ratio coordinate.  Its negative content is equally important: the contact remains at the level of codon usage and tRNA gene-copy annotation, and any claim about actual translation dynamics, structure, function, admissibility, mechanism, or universality requires a separate data contact.
