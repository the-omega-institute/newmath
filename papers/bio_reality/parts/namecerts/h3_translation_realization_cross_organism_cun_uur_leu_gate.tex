\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine usage as a bounded translation-layer statistic}
\label{sec:bior-h3-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
The packet records a finite organism-level test for the leucine synonymous split between the CUN and UUR codon families.  Its carrier is the residual coordinate consisting of the signed quantity $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$, an organism label, the corresponding tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, and an amino-acid-composition control.  The intended readout is not a statement about a particular peptide, ribosome trace, or folded product.  It is the narrower question of whether an organism-indexed codon-usage residual changes sign and magnitude in the same direction as an organism-indexed tRNA-Leu copy-number residual once synonymous amino-acid composition has been controlled.

\paragraph{Codon support.}
The finite codon base used by the packet includes the WNR union CUN set
$$
\begin{aligned}
M = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded cardinality is $|M|=20$.  Within this support the leucine contrast isolates $\mathrm{CUN}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$ against $\mathrm{UUR}=\{\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}$, while the surrounding WNR entries keep the packet tied to a finite codon-coordinate context rather than to a free biological narrative.

\paragraph{Observed rows.}
The finite run loaded three organism rows: $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli\ K12}$.  For these rows the observed CUN/UUR log-ratios were
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with corresponding signs
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\;-1,\;+1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is explicitly non-uniform across the three measured organisms.  The run records $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$, so the packet is not reporting a single cross-organism direction for the CUN/UUR split.

\paragraph{Paired statistic.}
The same finite run reports a Pearson coefficient of $1.0$ for the paired organism-level relation used by the packet.  After the amino-acid-composition control, the recorded partial correlation with the tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio is
$$
\begin{aligned}
r_{\mathrm{partial}} = 1.0, \qquad n=3.
\end{aligned}
$$
The numerical result is therefore a passed finite-row contact for this packet: the three-row table has a sign reversal in the CUN/UUR log-ratio and a positive controlled association with the tRNA-Leu copy-number coordinate.  The appropriate scientific reading is deliberately narrow.  The rows support an organism-level codon-usage and tRNA-copy-number relation for this leucine split; they do not by themselves supply a ribosome-dynamic trace or any direct measurement of translated protein output.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The result should be read as a constrained translation-layer statistic because both contacts are external organism-level summaries: codon-usage tables give per-organism coding-sequence frequencies, and tRNA gene-copy annotations give per-organism tRNA-Leu copy-number spectra.  The packet becomes informative only when these two summaries are paired by organism and interpreted through the controlled CUN/UUR residual.  A raw CUN/UUR sign without the tRNA-Leu coordinate and without amino-acid-composition control would not carry the same interpretation.

\paragraph{Boundary.}
No translation conclusion, folded-structure conclusion, physical-admissibility conclusion, biological-function conclusion, mechanistic ribosome conclusion, or universal biological law follows from this packet unless an additional contact supplies the missing kind of evidence.  In particular, the observed sign pattern $(+1,-1,+1)$ is a context-dependent finite-row observation, not a universal directionality theorem.  The positive controlled coefficient $r_{\mathrm{partial}}=1.0$ at $n=3$ records a small paired statistic for the available organisms; it does not establish protein folding, molecular function, global codon-law status, or physical realization beyond the codon-usage and tRNA-copy-number contacts already named here.
