\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine gate}\label{subsec:cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
This record treats the leucine codon pair $({\rm CUN},{\rm UUR})$ as a finite organism-indexed measurement problem. The carrier is the residual leucine-module codon-usage coordinate paired with an organism label and an organism-level $tRNA$-Leu copy-number coordinate. The measured codon coordinate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\ell_o = \log\left({\# {\rm CUN}_o \over \# {\rm UUR}_o}\right),
\end{aligned}
$$
for each organism $o$ in the loaded finite row set. The associated external coordinate is the corresponding $tRNA$-Leu gene-copy ratio, read at organism scale and interpreted only after controlling for amino-acid composition by keeping the comparison inside the synonymous leucine class. Thus the row is not a protein-level observation. It is a paired codon-usage and gene-copy statistic whose biological scope is limited to the translation-related usage layer.

\paragraph{Loaded rows.}
The run $675fdddb3187$ for experiment $\texttt{cross\_organism\_cun\_uur\_leu\_gate\_validation}$ loaded three organism rows:
$$
\begin{aligned}
O = \{&{\rm homo\_sapiens},\ {\rm saccharomyces\_cerevisiae},\ {\rm escherichia\_coli\_k12}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified log-ratio vector, in the listed row order, was
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\ -0.24869078488829677,\ 1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
and the corresponding sign vector was
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+1,\ -1,\ +1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Consequently the sign-uniformity test returned $\texttt{false}$. The yeast row supplies the observed reversal relative to the human and bacterial rows, so the packet is not reporting a universal direction for CUN over UUR usage.

\paragraph{Correlation readout.}
The finite statistic reported a Pearson value of $1.0$ for the paired organism-level coordinate in this three-row table. The amino-acid-composition-controlled readout also reported a positive partial correlation with the $tRNA$-Leu copy-ratio coordinate:
$$
\begin{aligned}
n &= 3,\\
\rho_{\rm partial} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The run status was $\texttt{passed}$ at $\texttt{2026\mbox{-}05\mbox{-}31T00:42:47+00:00}$. Within this finite table, the result supports the narrow statement that the sign and magnitude of $\log({\rm CUN}/{\rm UUR})$ are not constant across the loaded organisms and are aligned with the available $tRNA$-Leu copy-number coordinate after the synonymous-class control used by the run.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The observation is organism-level and row-finite. It does not say that CUN or UUR codons are globally preferred, and it does not turn the sign reversal into a law across all taxa. It also does not identify a ribosomal kinetic mechanism. The table supplies codon-usage summaries and gene-copy annotation summaries; these are compatible with a translation-layer usage statistic, but they are not direct measurements of elongation speed, aminoacylation, codon-context dynamics, or protein output.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
No separate conclusion about realized translation, folding, physical admissibility, biochemical function, molecular mechanism, or universality is licensed by this row set. Such conclusions would require distinct measurements at the appropriate layer, such as ribosome-profiling or matched translation-efficiency data for realized translation, structural assays for folding, physical measurements for admissibility, perturbation or phenotype assays for function, and broader sampled organism panels for universal or near-universal claims. The present packet remains a finite cross-organism comparison of leucine synonymous codon usage against organism-level $tRNA$-Leu copy spectra.
