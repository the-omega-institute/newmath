\subsection{Cross-organism CUN/UUR leucine usage gate}\label{subsec:h3-translation-realization-cross-organism-cun-uur-leu-gate}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Packet and measured scope.}
This packet treats the leucine codon split between the $\mathrm{CUN}$ block and the $\mathrm{UUR}$ block as an organism-level statistic, not as a direct molecular mechanism. The recorded carrier is the residual comparison of $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ after retaining the external organism label, the tRNA-Leu gene-copy ratio, and the amino-acid composition control as separate coordinates. The admissible readout is therefore narrow: for each organism, one may compare the sign and magnitude of the $\mathrm{CUN}$ versus $\mathrm{UUR}$ leucine usage ratio with the corresponding tRNA-Leu copy-number spectrum, provided that synonymous-class composition is not collapsed into a raw codon-count assertion.

\paragraph{Finite code-side anchor.}
The associated code-table fact fixes the codon set used by this gate. The verified finite set has cardinality $20$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
M = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This list is cited only as a finite codon-label anchor. It does not by itself supply organism-matched codon usage, tRNA annotation, ribosome occupancy, protein folding, or cellular phenotype. In particular, the $\mathrm{CUN}$ members visible in the list, $\mathrm{CUA}$, $\mathrm{CUC}$, $\mathrm{CUG}$, and $\mathrm{CUU}$, and the $\mathrm{UUR}$ members, $\mathrm{UUA}$ and $\mathrm{UUG}$, define the leucine partition that the biological rows subsequently evaluate.

\paragraph{Organism rows and statistic.}
The finite organism table loaded three organisms: $\mathrm{homo\_sapiens}$, $\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}$. For these rows, the measured $\log(\#\mathrm{CUN}/\#\mathrm{UUR})$ values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1.3514980212896148,\;-0.24869078488829677,\;1.0086640520051702),
\end{aligned}
$$
with corresponding signs
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1,\;-1,\;1).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the sign is not uniform across the recorded organisms. The signed reversal is the main empirical pressure in the packet: it prevents the leucine split from being read as a single direction that holds independently of organism context. The same finite run reports $\mathrm{sign\_uniform}=\mathrm{false}$ and status $\mathrm{passed}$.

\paragraph{tRNA-Leu association.}
The organism-matched tRNA-Leu comparison reported a Pearson coefficient of $1.0$ for the available three-row table. The controlled statistic is recorded as a positive partial correlation with tRNA-Leu ratio, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
n &= 3,\\
\rho_{\mathrm{partial}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this packet, that value is not promoted to a population-level law. It says only that the loaded finite rows pass the stated gate: the non-uniform $\mathrm{CUN}/\mathrm{UUR}$ sign and magnitude move with the organism-level tRNA-Leu copy-number ratio after the amino-acid composition control specified for the probe. No p-value, confidence interval, additional organism row count beyond $n=3$, module classification label, hit-rate field, $\lambda$ value, or independent ribosome-dynamics measurement is present in the supplied finite record.

\paragraph{Reality contacts used.}
Two external contact types are sufficient for this bounded row-level result. The first is a per-organism codon-usage contact: curated sequence resources report codon-count or codon-frequency summaries for coding sequences. The second is an organism-level tRNA gene-copy contact: genome annotation resources report tRNA-Leu copy numbers by anticodon family. These contacts are statistical summaries of sequence and annotation data. Their known limitations are part of the packet: codon-usage tables can reflect sampling and composition choices, and tRNA gene-copy counts are annotation summaries rather than direct measurements of ribosomal decoding rate.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The chapter records a translation-layer statistic only in the restricted sense of a codon-usage and tRNA-copy-number contact. It does not establish a ribosome-mediated event, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal cross-organism rule. The sign reversal is context-dependent evidence against a uniform direction, not evidence for a global mechanism. A stronger translation claim would require a separate contact, such as organism-matched ribosome profiling, direct decoding-rate data, or another experimentally grounded readout. A structure or function claim would require its own reality contact and is not licensed by the codon-usage/tRNA-copy table.
