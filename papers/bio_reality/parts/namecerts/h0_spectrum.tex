\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum and Local Boundary Readback}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
The record is a finite code-read observation on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  Its biological contact is only the curated standard-code label table: each codon is read as an amino-acid or stop label, and the resulting finite rows are used as external labels on a BEDC coordinate display.  The carrier distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The internal structure used here is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the readback is the standard-code label table.  This is therefore a code-layer chapter, not a mechanism chapter.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The finite boundary relation $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified row is
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same row was checked as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the local object is not merely a count.  It is a finite codon subset whose one-dimensional and two-dimensional face data are fixed by the verified row calculation.  The module classification partitions the same $13$ codons without residue: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and both the extra-module list and the unassigned-codon list are empty.  The six modules are $agr=\{AGA,AGG\}$, $ar\_lys=\{AAA\}$, $cun=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, $ile\_met=\{AUA\}$, $ssn=\{UCA,UUA\}$, and $stop\_trp=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$.  Each module has either predictable or boundary status, so the local row is closed at the stated module level.

\paragraph{Spectral certificate.}
The spectral computation attaches three scalar numbers to the finite display.  The boundary-side value is $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, the larger observed window value is $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, and the auxiliary scalar is $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported spectral-radius value is the same $0.6752479308830213$.  The cubic, equivalently radial, polynomial residual is
$$\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}$$
which is numerical zero at the recorded tolerance scale.  The point of this certificate is narrow: the window-six rows support a finite spectral relation between the observed local codon display and the boundary row $R$.  The certificate does not identify a translation machine, a structural law, or a physical selection principle.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Two finite-row null checks separate the local pattern from generic relabeling noise.  In the shape-significance run with seed $42$, $N=2000$ distinct samples were requested through $57377$ attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one exact-shape match was observed, giving $p=0.0005$ for exact shape.  The high-overlap threshold counted $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null run, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding values were $p=0.0$ for exact shape and $p=0.0035$ for the lower-tail statistic.  These are finite sampling facts about the code-label table under the specified null families.  They support non-genericity of the local code-read pattern under those nulls; they do not promote the pattern beyond the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests and family stability.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$; the reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  The family breakdown had $5$ families: alternative yeast at $0.8333333333333334$, bacterial plastid at $1.0$, candidate other at $1.0$, mitochondrial at $1.0$, and nuclear ciliate at $1.0$.  The leave-one-codon-out probe had anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These probes read as finite recovery checks for the same local coordinate story.  Their force is strongest where they remain local: missing codon labels tend to be recovered by the window closure and boundary organization already visible in the curated table.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The chapter records a local boundary-tile and spectral pattern in the standard-code codon table.  It does not claim that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or universality of the genetic code.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any promotion from code-read labels to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact supplying that higher-layer evidence.
