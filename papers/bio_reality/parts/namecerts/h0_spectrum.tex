\subsection{Window-Six Spectral Boundary of the Standard Code}\label{sec:bior-h0-spectrum}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.} The finite object used here is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a coordinate carrier for codon labels. The row data are not treated as a mechanism for translation. They are a finite display in which codons are placed in a window-six coordinate system, a local tile face is extracted, singleton label corners and three-point label classes are recorded, and the result is read back against a curated standard-code label table. The reality contact is therefore a code-read contact: it says that a curated table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. It does not say that the coordinate geometry causes translation, determines folding, supplies physical admissibility, realizes biological function, or proves universality.

\paragraph{Boundary row.} The distinguished boundary set $R$ has size $13$. In the finite run it is exactly
$$\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\\
&\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube tile with face-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
This finite shape is the local object whose spectrum is measured. The claim is not that all biology is cube-like. The claim is narrower: in this coordinate readback, the observed standard-code boundary row is a small, structured local tile rather than an arbitrary subset of the codon cube.

\paragraph{Spectral measurement.} The spectral row reports three scalar quantities. The measured values are
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}$$
The radial cubic check is numerically closed at residual
$$\begin{aligned}
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}$$
with the same residual recorded as the cubic-polynomial value and the radial-polynomial value. The reported spectral-radius-in-tolerance value is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$. Thus the spectral row is internally coherent at the finite precision of the run: the local tile has a measured $M$-radius, a measured $R$-radius, and a radial parameter $\mu$ satisfying the stated cubic test to near-zero numerical error.

\paragraph{Shape significance.} The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows with seed $42$. The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. Only $1$ exact shape match occurred, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
These numbers support the finite-row statement that the observed tile shape is rare under the recorded comparison procedure. A second null test preserved amino-acid class information. In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. The corresponding probabilities were
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
The shape is therefore not only rare against the general sampled comparison but also rare in the amino-acid-class-preserving comparison recorded by the run.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback.} The finite predictive readback is also code-layer only. Across $23$ evaluated code tables and $63$ total targets, the baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$. In the leave-one-codon-out row, the anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the predictable count was $8$, the high-coverage count was $8$, and the recovery rate was $1.0$. These figures say that the coordinate tile is useful for code-label recovery in the finite table experiment. They do not promote code-label recovery into a claim about molecular translation or protein realization.

\paragraph{Module classification.} The complete module classification assigns all $13$ boundary codons, with $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing boundary codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.

Every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the recorded classification. The family-level leave-one-code-out row contains $5$ families. Its hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families. These are finite family readback statistics, not a universal statement about every possible code or every biological lineage.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.} The result is a local spectral and combinatorial record for the standard-code table under a window-six coordinate display. It supports the claim that the listed $13$-codon boundary row has a rare punctured-cube shape, a coherent finite spectrum, complete module assignment, and strong code-label recovery in the recorded leave-one tests. The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this chapter. Any such higher-layer statement would require a separate reality contact beyond the curated codon-to-label table.
