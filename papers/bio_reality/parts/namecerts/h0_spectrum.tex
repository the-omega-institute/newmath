The chapter therefore treats the observed object as a code-layer boundary tile inside $Q_6$, not as a biological law.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite null sample with seed $42$, the shape experiment used $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables after $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$. Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact matches, $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed value $|M|=20$, and therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the local claim that the standard-code label table has a non-generic boundary-tile shape under the tested nulls. They do not identify a biochemical cause, and they do not by themselves justify a claim about protein folding, physical admissibility, function, or universal mechanism.
