\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table only as a finite code-layer contact.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The coordinate data expose a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes, but the contact is still only a codon-label readback.  The finite exceptional row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Its cardinality row gives $|R|=13$.  The punctured-cube row gives the same thirteen codons and the face-count vector $(13,16,4)$, so the observed boundary is not merely a label list: it is a finite subcomplex with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces inside the codon cube.  The finite rows also give an observed $M$-side size $|M|=20$, but no explicit ordered $M$ codon enumeration is supplied here; consequently the present section uses $M$ through its size, shape statistics, and spectral coordinates rather than as a separately displayed list.

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
and the cubic-polynomial residual is the same value, $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$.  At the finite precision of this row, the spectral-radius entry is therefore consistent with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$.  The point of the row is not that a biological mechanism has been derived from the cube.  The point is narrower: the standard-code label contact determines a local codon-tile boundary whose adjacency data admit a stable spectral readback at the displayed numerical tolerance.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The shape row compares the observed $M$-shape against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was exactly one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row is stricter in a different direction.  With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, it reports $0$ exact matches to the observed shape and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size $20$.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0,\qquad p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support a finite-row statement: the displayed local tile is uncommon under the reported null constructions.  They do not promote the tile to a law of translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The record is ready as a BioReality code-read chapter because the finite data agree at three levels: the same thirteen-codon $R$ row forms a punctured cube with vector $(13,16,4)$; its associated spectral coordinates satisfy the displayed cubic and radial residuals to approximately $6.3\cdot 10^{-12}$ in absolute value; and the null and leave-one rows make the shape and recovery behavior numerically non-generic under the supplied finite comparisons.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any such higher-layer claim requires a separate reality contact, distinct from the curated standard-code table used here.
