\subsection{Window-six spectral record for the standard code}\label{sec:h0-spectrum-window-six-record}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a coordinate display for the standard-code codon table.  The four distinctions used in this record are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the external readback is the curated standard-code label table.  This is a code-layer record: it tests whether finite codon-label rows exhibit a local boundary-tile pattern, not whether translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality is present.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The finite boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The row is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it has a finite cubical boundary signature inside the six-bit display.  The row splits into six named modules with no missing or extra codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}

The module audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, empty extra-module codons, empty missing-from-modules, and empty unassigned codons.  Every module contains a predictable or boundary member, so the finite decomposition is complete at the code-read layer.

These rows support the statement that the local codon-tile relation has a stable finite spectral signature: the verified residual is numerically zero at the displayed precision, while $\lambda_R<\lambda_M$ records a lower boundary-row radius than the ambient local object.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape-significance audit used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It observed $|M|=20$, exact match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ at or below the observed $M$-size.  The resulting null probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite evidence therefore supports a non-random local shape at the level of codon-label rows.  It does not turn the local shape into a biological law.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code bridge: a table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently, amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a label readback.  No folded protein structure, no physical admissibility claim, no biological function claim, no translation mechanism, and no universality claim follows from the window-six geometry unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
For claim $\mathrm{h0.spectrum}$, the finite rows support a precise local statement.  In the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, the standard-code label table exposes a $13$-codon boundary row with punctured-cube face-vector $(13,16,4)$, a complete six-module classification, a stable spectral radius row with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and low null-model probabilities for exact and near-shape recovery.  The conclusion is restricted to the code-read layer: window-six codon coordinates expose local boundary-tile structure while curated code labels remain the external reality contact.
