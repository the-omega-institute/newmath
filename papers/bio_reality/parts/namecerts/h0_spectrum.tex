\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}

Each codon is read as a vertex of the cube, while the curated standard-code table supplies the external label bridge from codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The row under audit is not a mechanism for translation and not a model of folded protein structure.  It is a finite boundary-tile statement: the window-six coordinate display exposes a distinguished local row $R$ whose readback is checked only against code labels.  The verified row has cardinality
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the thirteen codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is also certified as a punctured-cube tile with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the chapter records a concrete local shape in the codon cube: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces.  The contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge, whose observed fact is only that the standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It does not observe ribosomal realization, biochemical admissibility, folded structure, or organismal function.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
The coordinate-shape null check used $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$.  Because the sampler was constrained to distinct rows, it made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the $2000$ retained controls, the exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\Pr(\text{exact shape match})=p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A high-overlap comparison at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ produced $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null preserving amino-acid class structure also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that run the observed module-side size was $|M|=20$, no exact shape match occurred, and only $7$ samples were in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the narrower claim that the finite codon-tile row is not typical under the stated finite null procedures.  They do not establish translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Those higher layers require separate contacts.
