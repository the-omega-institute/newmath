\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The row records a finite code-layer contact between a six-bit codon display and the curated standard-code label table.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite coordinate display for codons, and the external contact is only the standard codon-to-label table. In this row the claimed layer is therefore exactly $\mathrm{code\_read}$: the finite cube coordinates may expose a local boundary-tile split, while the labels themselves remain an external reality bridge.
