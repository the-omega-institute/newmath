\subsection{Window-Six Spectrum for the Standard-Code Boundary Tile}\label{sec:bior-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the $64$ standard codons. The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, interpreted as a codon-label readback table. Thus the finite row has access to the assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels, but it has no contact with ribosomal translation dynamics, molecular folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The claim tested in this section is therefore deliberately narrow: a window-six coordinate representation may expose a local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube audit returns the same $13$ codons and records the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a local tile statistic inside $Q_6$. It is not a law of biology. It says that the displayed codon-label table contains a finite subset with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces under the chosen window-six coordinate relation.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral computation attached to the same finite object gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported radial polynomial residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value recorded for the cubic-polynomial check. The spectral-radius row records $0.6752479308830213$ in tolerance, so the finite certificate is a numerical spectrum certificate for the local boundary tile, not merely a cardinality certificate. The value of $\lambda_M$ belongs to the surrounding selected module $M$, while $\lambda_R$ belongs to the $13$-codon boundary row $R$. The supplied finite data record $|M|=20$, but the full codon list of $M$ is not part of this row.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the sampled null model, the exact shape event is rare. With $N=2000$, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, the row records $55377$ duplicate retries, $1$ exact shape match, and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$. Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a complementary check. With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$. Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support the finite-row statement that the observed local tile is not a common outcome under the tested shuffles. They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Predictive stress rows.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The stricter interior statistic records $0.9365079365079365$ in $M\setminus T$, and the boundary-augmented statistic returns $0.9841269841269841$ in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary. By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families. The leave-one-codon-out row records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$: assigned count $13$, $R$ size $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary status in the finite audit. This makes the boundary tile a row-complete object at the code-read layer: the $13$ codons are enumerated, partitioned into named modules, checked by punctured-cube geometry, and assigned spectral values.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The ready verdict is that the standard-code label table contains a window-six local boundary tile with a $13$-codon punctured-cube row, a verified spectrum, rare shape statistics under the sampled nulls, and strong leave-one-out recovery behavior. The result remains a code-read result. It does not claim that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity; it does not claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global biological law. Any promotion beyond codon-label readback requires a separate higher-layer contact.
