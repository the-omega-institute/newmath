\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The record works only at the code-read layer.  Its carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The finite contact is therefore a label table, not a translation apparatus.  The observed boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row audit reports $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons in the cardinality and punctured-cube checks.  The punctured-cube readback has face-vector
$$

(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite object seen by the row is a local tile-face and boundary-corner pattern inside the codon cube, not a global law for DNA or protein biology.

$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}

The amino-acid-class-preserving null used the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that stricter null the observed $M$ row has size $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  Thus the row records

\begin{aligned}

These numbers support a finite-row claim of unusual local shape under the specified nulls.  They do not identify a causal translation principle, a folding principle, or a universal genetic-code theorem.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The verdict is a code-layer statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary tile whose thirteen-codon row, punctured-cube face-vector, spectral values, null-model rarity, leave-one recovery, and module classification are all reproducible in the supplied finite rows.  The reality contact is only the curated standard-code label table.  Therefore no translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this chapter.
