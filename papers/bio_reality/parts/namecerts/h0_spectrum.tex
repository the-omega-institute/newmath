\subsection{Window-six spectral record for the standard-code codon table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Coordinate packet.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a binary display of the three nucleotide positions after a fixed two-bit nucleotide encoding.  The observed labels are not produced by the cube itself: they are supplied by the curated standard-code table, which assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  The finite distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The internal computations are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation computations on the displayed codon set.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has thirteen codons.  The verified row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus $|R|=13$.  The same finite row verifies that this set is the punctured-cube boundary detected by the construction, with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a local combinatorial fact about the codon-label display.  It is not a statement that the biological translation system is generated by this geometry.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The Monte Carlo shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only one exact shape match was found, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the claim that the standard-code row is not a typical member of the sampled null family for this local shape statistic.  They still concern only the code-label display.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The supported claim is narrow.  Window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table, and the finite rows provide spectral, shape, null-model, and leave-one-out evidence for that pattern.  The curated code table is the only reality contact used here, and it observes codon-to-label assignment only.  From these rows alone one may not infer translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanistic explanation, or universality across life.  Any such claim would require an additional contact designed for that layer.  In particular, amino-acid label sequences obtained from the table remain label sequences; they are not structural observations and they are not function measurements.
