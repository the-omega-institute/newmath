\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The only external reality contact used here is a curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label.  That contact supports code-layer readback only.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Distinguished boundary row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is detected as a punctured-cube boundary with finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of labels: it carries a local cubical signature with $13$ vertices, $16$ edges, and $4$ square faces in the induced boundary-tile description.  This is a finite-row statement about the window-six coordinate system and the curated code-label readback.

\paragraph{Shape significance against unconstrained samples.}
Against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$, the exact local shape occurred once.  The empirical exact-shape rate was therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling process recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  These counts are part of the finite-row audit: the low exact rate and low high-Jaccard rate support the claim that the observed tile is not a typical random local shape under this sampling protocol.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class information.  In that model $N=2000$, the observed $M$-row size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The reported rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The result keeps the distinction between a code-label contact and a mechanism claim: even when the null respects amino-acid classes, the finite shape remains rare, but rarity of the code-layer tile is not translation, folding, physical admissibility, or function.

These numbers say that the finite local boundary is predictive at the code-read layer across the audited tables; they do not supply a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The claim established here is deliberately narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a rare local boundary-tile spectrum in the standard-code label table, with a $13$-codon punctured-cube row, spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, and predictive finite-row behavior under the listed leave-one-out probes.  The claim does not promote the tile to translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate contact that observes the higher layer directly.
