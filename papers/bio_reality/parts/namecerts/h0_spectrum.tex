\subsection{Window-six spectrum at the standard-code boundary}\label{subsec:h0-spectrum-window-six}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
This record studies the standard-code codon table only at the level of codon-to-label readback.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with the usual $64$ codon positions read as a coordinate display rather than as a biochemical mechanism.  The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.  The same row is detected as a punctured-cube shaped object with face data $(13,16,4)$: thirteen codon vertices, sixteen local adjacency edges, and four two-dimensional faces.  This is the finite combinatorial object whose spectrum is measured here.  The curated standard-code table supplies the labels used to read codons as amino-acid or stop symbols; it does not supply a translation process, a folding model, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal causal mechanism, or any claim that the coordinate geometry is necessary for life.

$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\

\end{aligned}$$
The measured spectral radius is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$ within the stated tolerance.  The radial cubic check has residual
$$\begin{aligned}
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu) &= -6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}$$
with the same value recorded as the radial polynomial value.  At this finite precision the residual is numerically zero for the row computation, so the row is not only a set of codons but a reproducible local spectral datum inside the six-bit coordinate system.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under finite nulls.}
The unrestricted shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, indicating that the finite sample space was being revisited heavily.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ alternatives with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$.  The exact-shape count was $0$, hence $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$, while the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These values support the claim that the observed local row is uncommon among the tested finite rearrangements.  They do not by themselves identify a molecular cause.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The row is fully assigned by the module classification.  The thirteen codons split as follows: $\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The classification reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every listed module has a predictable or boundary status in the finite check.  This gives a closed local description of the row as a code-table phenomenon: a singleton-corner and three-point-class pattern inside a six-bit codon display, together with its boundary modules.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded statement.  In the standard-code label table, the row $R$ is a thirteen-codon punctured-cube-like local tile with face vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral radius $0.6752479308830213$, and a near-zero radial cubic residual.  Its shape is rare in the tested null ensembles, and the associated closure and boundary checks recover held-out code labels at rates far above the finite baseline.  The conclusion is deliberately restricted to code-layer readback.  A separate contact would be required before any assertion about realized translation, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, causal mechanism, or universality could be made.
