\subsection{Window-Six Spectral Boundary for the Standard Code}\label{sec:h0-spectrum-window-six}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The claim studied here is the code-layer assertion that the six-bit codon display of the standard genetic code exposes a small local boundary region inside the codon cube. The finite carrier is the $64$-codon cube obtained by representing each of the three nucleotide positions with two binary coordinates. The biological contact used in this section is only the curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop. Thus the observed object is a label table on codons, not a translation apparatus, not a protein structure experiment, and not a measurement of biological function.

The distinguished boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified row count is $|R|=13$. As a cubical subcomplex inside the window-six cube, this row has the finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed support is a punctured local tile rather than a full subcube. The puncture matters: the row is large enough to carry faces and adjacency, but small enough that it remains a local code-table feature.

\paragraph{Spectral row measurement.}
The spectral computation assigns three scalar values to the finite row comparison:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported radial residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is also the residual of the cubic relation used by the finite computation. At the numerical precision of the run, this records that the spectral radius attached to the observed label-tile object is stable at $0.6752479308830213$. The point of the number is not that spectra explain translation; it is that the curated code labels select a finite region whose adjacency spectrum is reproducible and sharply specified.

\paragraph{Shape significance checks.}
Against $2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$, the exact observed shape appeared once. The exact-shape probability estimate was therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap test counted $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite null ensemble was constrained and collision-heavy rather than a loose informal comparison. A second null check preserving amino-acid class reported observed $|M|=20$, exact match count $0$ out of $2000$, and lower-tail count $7$. The corresponding finite estimates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrower reading that the standard-code labels pick out an unusual local shape in this coordinate presentation. They do not by themselves identify a causal biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Predictive row behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the reported rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with boundary support was again $0.9841269841269841$. The leave-one-codon-out test found no anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-resolved leave-one-code-out table separated five families. The hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, $1.0$ for the candidate-other family, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate tables. This supports a row-level stability statement across the tested code-table variants, still only at the label-readout layer.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are fully assigned by the module classification. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no unassigned codons, and no extra module codons. Every module has either predictable or boundary status. This gives the local tile a complete finite row decomposition: all thirteen listed codons are accounted for, and none of the row behavior is hidden in an unclassified remainder.

\paragraph{Scope of the contact.}
The scientific content of this section is deliberately narrow. The curated standard-code table supports codon-to-label readback and local boundary-tile tests. It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded protein geometry, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, or a universal biological law. Consequently, the window-six spectral boundary may be used as a finite code-layer regularity, but no higher-layer conclusion follows unless another independent contact supplies the missing measurement. In particular, amino-acid label sequences are not protein structures, cross-table label regularities are not mechanisms, and the local punctured-cube row is not evidence that all biological realization is forced by six-bit codon geometry.
