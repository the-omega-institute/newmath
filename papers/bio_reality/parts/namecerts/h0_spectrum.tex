\subsection{Spectral boundary data for the six-bit codon cube}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object and contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer object, not as a complete biological mechanism. The finite display is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon read as a three-letter RNA word and with its label supplied by the curated standard genetic-code table. The relevant distinctions are coordinate position, local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class. These distinctions give a finite coordinate-and-label problem: one may ask whether the standard-code labels mark a special local boundary pattern inside $Q_6$, while keeping the source of the amino-acid and stop labels external to the coordinate calculation.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The row identifies a boundary class $R$ with exactly $13$ codons. The verified list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row verifies that this set is the punctured-cube boundary detected by the local tile computation, with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite observation is not merely a count: the $13$ selected codons carry a small two-dimensional cubical signature with $16$ one-faces and $4$ two-faces. The codons are concentrated in modules that are biologically legible at the code-read level: $AAA$ in the lysine/arginine side, $AGA$ and $AGG$ in the arginine reassignment side, $AUA$ in the isoleucine/methionine side, the four $CUN$ codons, $UCA$ and $UUA$ in the serine/leucine side, and $UAA,UAG,UGA$ in the stop/tryptophan side.

\paragraph{Spectral measurement.}
The spectral row records the local operator values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic relation is numerically satisfied to the recorded residual
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\mu,\lambda_M,\lambda_R)=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the same value is reported as the radial-polynomial residual. The spectral radius recorded for the $M$ side is $0.6752479308830213$, which matches $\lambda_M$ in the row tolerance. The finite claim is therefore that the observed codon-label partition has a reproducible spectral signature inside the six-bit window, with $R$ carrying the lower boundary value and $M$ carrying the larger local radius.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported sample is explicitly finite and duplicate-controlled. The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, also at $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-match count was $0$. The observed $M$ size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below that observed size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0,\qquad p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a code-layer anomaly statement: the local tile is unusually shaped relative to the stated randomization regimes. They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Recovery probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The leave-one-codon-out probe reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These finite values say that the window-six boundary and closure data are predictive for code-label readback in the tested tables and codon deletions.

\paragraph{Family and module checks.}
The family-level code-out row contains $5$ families. Its hit-rate-above-baseline values are $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast. The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, all $13$ boundary codons are assigned, and the extra, missing, and unassigned lists are empty. The verified modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable or boundary status in the finite row. This is the strongest self-contained reading of the packet: the standard-code labels, when placed on $Q_6$, expose a small local boundary spectrum with a complete module accounting.

\paragraph{Scope limit.}
The admissible conclusion is confined to codon-label readback and local split structure in the standard-code table. Nothing in this row supplies translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanistic explanation, or a universal biological law. Amino-acid label sequences are still labels at the code layer; they are not structural observations. Any promotion beyond the code-read layer would require a distinct contact that directly observes the higher-layer phenomenon being claimed.
