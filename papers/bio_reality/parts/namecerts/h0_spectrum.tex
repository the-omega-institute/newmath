\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum}
\label{sec:biorality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a codon-coordinate display for the standard-code label table.  In this chapter the distinguished row $R$ is the thirteen-codon subset
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The direct cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The same finite row is verified as a punctured cube with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object being discussed is not an unrestricted biological mechanism.  It is a concrete boundary tile inside the codon-coordinate cube, together with the external standard-code label readback that tells which amino-acid or stop label each codon carries.

$$

At the numerical tolerance of the finite-row computation this is a zero residual: the displayed row has the advertised spectrum, with spectral-radius entry $0.6752479308830213$.  The claim is therefore a finite spectral fact about the coordinate-and-label row, not a claim that the genetic code is forced by this spectrum.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under finite nulls.}
The unrestricted shape null used $N=2000$ sampled rows.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
Rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$.  In that constrained null, the exact-shape count was $0$, the observed module size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  The recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a local finite-row anomaly relative to the sampled null models.  They do not upgrade the anomaly into a law of translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

The module audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons from the module list, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  Every listed module has either a predictable or boundary status, so the module classification is finite-row complete for this row.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$; and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.  The leave-one-codon-out probe reports recovery rate $1.0$, with $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and high closure coverage $8$.  These leave-one probes say that the finite row is stable as a code-read boundary pattern under the tested omissions; they do not observe translation realization or protein-level structure.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table: a codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop label bridge.  It can test code-layer readback and local codon-tile label splitting.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity of BEDC geometry in DNA.  Consequently, the chapter-level conclusion is narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile spectrum for the standard-code label table, with $|R|=13$, punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, plus rare-shape behavior under the reported nulls.  No higher biological layer follows unless a separate contact supplies that layer directly.
