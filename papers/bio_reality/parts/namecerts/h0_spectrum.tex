\paragraph{Shape significance.}

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
and the number of samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null used another $N=2000$ rows.  It found zero exact shape matches, an observed $M$-row size of $20$, and $7$ lower-tail rows at or below that observed size.  The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite code-layer anomaly claim: the observed tile shape is rare under the stated row models.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause.
