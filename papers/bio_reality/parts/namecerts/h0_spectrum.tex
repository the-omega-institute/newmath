\subsection{Window-six spectral contact for the standard-code table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record works at the code-read layer of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the sixty-four codons.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  That contact supplies label readback only.  It does not supply translation dynamics, ribosomal realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  The finite-row claim is therefore deliberately local: window-six coordinates expose a boundary-tile pattern in the codon-label table, while the labels themselves remain external reality contacts.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the observed standard-code readback it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube face pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object being tested is not the whole biology of coding, but a small local tile: thirteen codons, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional face incidences inside the window-six cube.  The row is large enough to carry a visible boundary, but small enough that the contact remains a finite combinatorial readback.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
For the same finite row, the spectral probe reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial check gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
P_{\mathrm{rad}}(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)
  = -6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same residual recorded as the cubic-polynomial value.  At the stated numerical precision this is a zero-residual spectral certificate for the finite window-six row.  The spectral radius in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$.  The row therefore has a concrete spectral signature, not merely a visual or verbal shape description.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The finite-row shape was tested against $N=2000$ sampled null instances with seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed $|M|=20$, exact match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite code-read anomaly statement: the observed window-six row is rare under the reported null probes.  They do not by themselves identify a biological cause.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The row $R$ is also exhausted by six named finite modules.  The module accounting is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification reports $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Thus the module partition closes exactly on the same thirteen-codon boundary row.  In the leave-one-codon-out row, the recovery rate is $1.0$, with $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchors, and $8$ high-coverage closure cases.  The module statement is therefore a finite recovery statement over codon labels, not a structural statement about amino-acid chains.

\paragraph{Cross-code stress test.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluates $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  It reports baseline hit rate $0.19821739130434785$, hit rate in $M\setminus T$ equal to $0.9365079365079365$, and hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary equal to $0.9841269841269841$.  The same $0.9841269841269841$ value is reported as the hit rate above baseline.  By family, five families are counted, with above-baseline hit rates
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This stress test says that the local window-six row remains useful for code-read recovery across the reported table family sample.  The finite rows justify a local boundary-tile readback claim, but they do not promote the tile to a law of translation or to a mechanism shared by all biological coding systems.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The conclusion is intentionally narrow.  The data support a code-layer statement: in the curated standard-code table, the six-bit codon display contains a thirteen-codon row $R$ with a punctured-cube incidence vector, a near-zero spectral polynomial residual, rare shape statistics under the supplied null probes, exact module exhaustion, and high finite recovery rates in leave-one-code and leave-one-codon tests.  Nothing in this contact observes protein realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, translation mechanism, or universality.  Any such claim would require a separate reality contact beyond the curated codon-to-label table.
