\subsection{Window-six spectral contact for the standard-code tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite-row carrier.}
The record is a finite window-six reading of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and this contact is used only for code-layer label readback.  It is not a contact with translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The finite row isolates a boundary set $R$ of cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the finite evidence is not merely a count: it also preserves the local face incidence profile of the selected codon boundary.  The module partition accounts for all $13$ codons with no missing or extra module codons: $\{AGA,AGG\}$ form the $agr$ module, $\{AAA\}$ the $ar\_lys$ module, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ the $cun$ module, $\{AUA\}$ the $ile\_met$ module, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ the $ssn$ module, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ the $stop\_trp$ module.  The module audit reports $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, empty extra-module codons, empty missing-from-modules list, and empty unassigned-codons list.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape audit compares the observed local pattern against sampled alternatives.  In the unconditioned shape run, $N=2000$ samples were evaluated from seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{exact}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, there were $0$ exact matches, so the exact-shape $p$-value was $0.0$.  The observed $M$-size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving a lower-tail value $0.0035$.  These rows support a finite local irregularity statement: the standard-code labels produce a boundary-tile spectrum that is uncommon under both the unconditioned sample and the amino-acid-class-preserving null sample.

\paragraph{Readback stability.}
The leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  Family-level readback remained high across the five recorded families: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  The leave-one-codon-out row records boundary count $5$, closure coverage high $8$, predictable count $8$, anchor count $0$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers are read as code-layer robustness checks for the local tile, not as evidence for any higher biological layer.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported claim is therefore precise.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code label table, and the observed row has a verified $13$-codon boundary, a punctured-cube incidence vector $(13,16,4)$, a near-zero spectral residual, and low sampled null frequencies.  The claim stops at code readback.  No translation process, no folded protein structure, no physical admissibility condition, no function realization, no biological mechanism, and no universality principle follows from this row unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore treated as a finite code-layer observation, not as protein structure or function.
