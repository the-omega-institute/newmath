\subsection{Window-Six Spectral Boundary for the Standard Code}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  The table is used only as a code-layer contact: it supplies label readback for a finite row system, not a mechanism of translation and not a structural or functional assay.  In this coordinate system the distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is identified as a punctured-cube boundary with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object tested here is not the whole codon table as biology, but a finite local tile: a singleton-corner and three-point-label-class pattern inside the standard-code label display.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row invariant.}
The primary spectral measurement separates the boundary row $R$ from the associated measured row $M$ by the pair of radii
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,
\end{aligned}
$$
with auxiliary scalar
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mu=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported radial polynomial residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)=-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the same value is reported as the cubic-polynomial residual.  At the supplied numerical tolerance this is a zero-residual finite-row certificate: the observed local tile is compatible with the cubic spectral constraint to approximately twelve decimal orders.  The spectral claim is therefore a claim about the internal arithmetic of the observed finite rows, not a claim that the geometry is a biological law.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under finite nulls.}
The shape test sampled $N=2000$ distinct null rows with seed $42$.  Producing those distinct rows required $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space is heavily recurrent under the sampler.  Among the $2000$ distinct null rows, exactly one exact-shape match occurred, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$ rows and found no exact match to the observed shape.  It reported observed measured-row size $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the finite statement that the observed local codon tile is rare under the supplied nulls.  They do not, by themselves, identify a causal process.

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
while the hit rate above baseline was

\begin{aligned}

$$
The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out probe reported anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons of $R$ with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\

Across five code families, the family hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The finite-row verdict is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table.  The evidence combines a $13$-codon punctured-cube row, a cubic spectral residual of magnitude about $6.23\times 10^{-12}$, rarity under two finite null models, and strong leave-one-out recovery statistics.  The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code label table.  Consequently no translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity claim follows from this record.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer readback; it is not folded structure.  Any promotion beyond code readback requires a separate contact that directly observes the higher layer.
