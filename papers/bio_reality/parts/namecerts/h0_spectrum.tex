\subsection{Spectral boundary of the window-six codon tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the standard-code codon table.  The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label.  This contact is a code-layer readback contact.  It is not a translation realization, not a protein-structure observation, not a physical-admissibility experiment, not a function assay, not a mechanism, and not a universality theorem.  The finite-row claim is therefore deliberately local: window-six coordinates expose a boundary-tile pattern inside the codon-label table, while the labels themselves remain external reality contacts.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has exactly thirteen codons.  In lexicographic codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The same finite set is also recovered as a punctured-cube boundary with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not being used merely as a list of exceptional labels.  It is being used as a local cubical tile: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the induced finite cell picture.  The claim remains a finite combinatorial statement about a displayed codon-label table; it does not say that biological translation is caused by this geometry.

$$
The radial cubic residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}

Thus the finite numerical certificate is internally coherent to the displayed tolerance: the spectral radius readback is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$, while $\lambda_R$ records the smaller boundary-row scale.  This is a certificate for the window-six tile, not a claim that the spectral radius measures fitness, folding, translation efficiency, or any other higher-layer biological property.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the sampled null family for the local shape, the observed tile is rare.  In $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$, the run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and had $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a separate check.  With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed main tile size was $|M|=20$.  The exact-shape count was $0$, so the recorded exact-shape value is $0.0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed main-tile size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the statement that the finite tile is not a high-frequency artifact of the tested null families.  They do not identify a biochemical mechanism and do not license movement from label geometry to protein structure.

Two leave-one-out checks test whether the local row is a brittle presentation artifact.  In the leave-one-code-out check, $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The by-family table reports $5$ families, with above-baseline hit rates
$$
\begin{aligned}
&0.8333333333333334 &&\text{for alternative yeast},\\
&1.0 &&\text{for bacterial plastid},\\
&1.0 &&\text{for candidate other},\\
&1.0 &&\text{for mitochondrial},\\
&1.0 &&\text{for nuclear ciliate}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The leave-one-codon-out check records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closures, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These are finite-row stability statements about code-label recovery.  They do not say that a removed codon is biologically reconstructed in a cell.

The thirteen-codon row is partitioned by the module classification without missing or extra codons.  The assigned count is $13$, the row size is $13$, the extra-module list is empty, the missing-from-modules list is empty, and the unassigned-codon list is empty.  The six displayed modules are

$$
Every module has predictable or boundary status in the recorded classification.  This completes the finite local account of the boundary row: the spectrum gives a numerical separation, the punctured-cube data gives the local tile shape, and the module table gives a complete codon-level allocation.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The scope boundary is part of the result.  From the row $R$, the punctured-cube vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, the sampled $p$-values, and the leave-one-out recovery rates, one may claim only a code-read local tile in the curated standard-code label table.  No translation process, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from these finite rows.  Any such higher-layer statement requires a separate reality contact of the appropriate kind.
