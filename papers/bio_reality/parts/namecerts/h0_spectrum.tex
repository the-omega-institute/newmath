\subsection{Window-six spectral boundary for the standard-code table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a coordinate display for the $64$ codons of the standard code. The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label. This contact has code-layer resolution. It supports codon-label readback and local split checks, but it does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The resulting claim is therefore a code-read claim about a local boundary tile in a finite codon display, not a claim that DNA is governed by this geometry as biological necessity.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation the verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube object with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it carries a finite local tile shape in the $Q_6$ coordinate presentation. Its labels include singleton-corner behavior and three-point label-class behavior, but those label features are still read from the curated code table rather than derived as biological mechanisms.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The finite-row shape test used $N=2000$ null samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite-row values support the statement that the observed local tile is not a typical random shape under the tested null. They do not, by themselves, identify a translation process, a biochemical admissibility constraint, or a protein-structure rule.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class information. It used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. The observed $M$-size was $20$. The exact-match count was $0$, so the finite-row exact-shape value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}

This comparison matters because it keeps one layer of label information fixed while testing whether the observed tile remains atypical. The result remains a code-table phenomenon: the null model sharpens the local codon-label claim, but it does not supply evidence for folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code readback.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. These values show that the local tile has predictive readback power at the code-label layer when code variants are withheld. They should not be read as a claim that the tile realizes translation or explains the molecular implementation of any code.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon readback.}
The leave-one-codon-out row recorded anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The recovery-rate threshold check also reports $1.0$. In this finite-row sense, removed codons in the tested setting are recovered through the local closure-and-boundary structure rather than through a separate external mechanism. The contact remains the curated label table, so the recovery statement is about label readback within $Q_6$ coordinates.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The verified finite rows support the following bounded statement. In the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, the curated standard-code table exposes a $13$-codon boundary row $R$ with punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral relation with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, significant finite-row shape statistics under both the generic and amino-acid-class-preserving nulls, and high code-label recovery in leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes. The claim stops exactly there. No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
