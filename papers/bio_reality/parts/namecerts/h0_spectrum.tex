\subsection{Window-six spectrum for the standard-code boundary tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  A codon is displayed as a six-bit point in the codon cube $Q_6$, and the only biological readout used here is the curated assignment of each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  The tested finite pattern is a local boundary tile, not an organismal process.  It combines a coordinate display, a closure operation on selected codons, a spectral measurement, and a relation to the observed code labels.  The reality contact is therefore narrow: the table says which label is attached to each codon in the standard code; it does not say that translation has occurred, that a peptide folds, that a molecule is physically admissible, that a function is realized, or that the pattern is a universal mechanism.

The reported $M$ side is used in this row only through its measured size and spectral response: the null-model audit records an observed $M$ size of $20$, while the supplied finite row does not include a separate explicit list of the $20$ $M$ codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance against unconstrained rows.}
The shape-significance audit used $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and therefore incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those $2000$ sampled rows, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support the claim that the observed local tile is uncommon under the sampled coordinate null.  They do not by themselves identify a biological cause.  The numerical result says that this row shape is rare in the tested finite comparison class; the curated code table supplies the label contact against which that rarity is interpreted.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A stricter null experiment preserved amino-acid label classes, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that audit, the exact-shape count was $0$, so the recorded exact-shape probability is $0.0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, with observed $M$ size $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving comparison is important because it reduces the chance that the row is merely a consequence of label multiplicities.  Even under that more constrained sampling rule, the measured shape remains rare.  The conclusion remains at the code-read level: label-preserving rarity is not evidence for folded structure, physical viability, or biological function without another observation that directly tests those layers.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The finite evidence supports the following restricted reading.  In the six-bit codon cube, the standard-code labels expose a $13$-codon boundary row with a punctured-cube shape, a measured spectral radius $0.6752479308830213$ on the $M$ side, a measured $R$ spectral value $0.4758352843197002$, and a near-zero cubic residual of about $6.22\cdot 10^{-12}$ in absolute value.  The same row has rare-shape behavior under the sampled nulls and strong recovery behavior in leave-one-code and leave-one-codon tests.  The scientific content is therefore a finite code-layer regularity: local window-six geometry organizes a codon-label boundary in the curated standard code.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
No conclusion about translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or organism-wide necessity is licensed by this row.  The contact used here is a curated codon-to-label table, so the observed facts stop at code-label readback and local split checks.  A claim about translation would need a separate translation contact; a claim about folded structure would need a separate structure contact; a claim about function would need a separate functional assay or comparable observation.  The row is therefore accepted as a finite local code-read finding and blocked from higher biological layers until those layers are independently observed.
