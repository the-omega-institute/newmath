\subsection{Window-Six Spectrum of the Standard-Code Boundary Tile}\label{sec:h0-spectrum-window-six-standard-code}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display. The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, and the readout layer is only the curated assignment of each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row was checked as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the local geometry is not merely a count: it records thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the selected codon subcomplex. The complementary named module decomposition accounts for all thirteen codons: $\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$ in the AGR module, $\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$ in the AR/Lys module, $\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$ in the CUN module, $\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$ in the Ile/Met module, $\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$ in the SSN module, and $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$ in the stop/Trp module. The module audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral certificate attached to this codon tile gives two local radii and one cubic parameter. The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also reported as the value of the satisfied cubic polynomial. This is numerically indistinguishable from zero at the recorded tolerance scale, so the spectral datum is a finite arithmetic constraint on the observed row rather than a qualitative impression. The radius attached to the larger local shape is $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, while the boundary row radius is $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$; the inequality $\lambda_R<\lambda_M$ marks the boundary row as a lower-radius subconfiguration inside the observed window-six shape.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The finite randomization audit compares the observed shape against $2000$ sampled alternatives generated with seed $42$. Sampling required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and produced $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space is collision-heavy at this resolution. Only $1$ exact shape match occurred among the $2000$ trials, giving $p=0.0005$ for exact shape recovery. At the softer Jaccard threshold $0.9$, there were $25$ matches and $p=0.0125$. A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact matches, giving exact-shape probability $0.0$ at the recorded precision. In that same class-preserving audit, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the lower-tail count at or below that size was $7$, and the lower-tail probability was $0.0035$. These finite rows support the claim that the observed local tile is not a typical output of the tested null models, while still keeping the claim at the codon-label layer.

\paragraph{Prediction rows.}
The leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate after adding the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$; the reported above-baseline rate is also $0.9841269841269841$. Family-level leave-one-code-out rows split this behavior into five families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial/plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$. The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These numbers say that the local coordinate and closure data recover the tested code-layer targets well under the finite probes, not that they explain translation as a physical process.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The chapter-specific claim is therefore narrow. A six-bit codon coordinate system exposes a finite local boundary tile in the standard-code label table. The row $R$ has an explicit thirteen-codon support, a punctured-cube incidence vector $(13,16,4)$, a complete module partition, a stable spectral pair $(\lambda_M,\lambda_R)$, and low empirical probabilities under the recorded null models. The curated standard-code table is the contact that supplies the amino-acid and stop labels. It does not by itself observe ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility of a molecule, biological function, a mechanism of origin, or a universal law of biology. Any one of those stronger readings would require a separate contact with its own finite observations. Within the data listed here, the supported conclusion is only that the standard-code label table contains a local window-six boundary pattern with a measured spectrum and non-null finite-row behavior.
