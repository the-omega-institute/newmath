\subsection{Window-six spectrum of the standard-code local tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate record.}
The record considered here is a six-bit codon display for the standard genetic-code label table.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with codons read as finite coordinate points rather than as biochemical mechanisms.  The distinguished local tile is the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
It has $|R|=13$.  Its cell count in the local cubical shape is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the recorded object is not only a list of labels, but also a finite face-incidence pattern inside the codon cube.  The intended biological contact is narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact permits code-layer label readback and local split checks.  It does not, by itself, observe translation as a process, a folded protein, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal law.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral measurement attached to this tile records the pair of local radii
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the scalar
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mu = 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The numerical residual for the associated cubic relation is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
the same value recorded as the radial-polynomial residual.  Thus the row supports a finite spectral consistency statement at numerical tolerance: the observed local tile has spectral radius $0.6752479308830213$ on the $M$ side and $0.4758352843197002$ on the $R$ side, with the displayed cubic relation satisfied to near-zero floating residual.  The row is a statement about this finite coordinate construction and its label contact, not about the dynamics of translation or the physics of the translated product.

\paragraph{Shape against finite null rows.}
The main shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among these rows, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null row preserving amino-acid class labels also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  The observed $M$ size was $20$.  In that class-preserving run there were no exact matches, so the exact-shape count was $0$ and the recorded exact-shape frequency was $0.0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers make the finite tile unusual relative to the sampled comparison rows.  They do not say that the geometry causes the standard code, and they do not license a higher biological layer without an independent observation at that layer.

The leave-one-codon-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The family-level code-out row grouped the comparison into $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  These checks strengthen the code-read interpretation: the local tile and its boundary recover label-table information across finite deletion tests.  They still remain label-table tests.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The module row assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ with no extras, no missing module entries, and no unassigned codons.  The partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has either a predictable or boundary role in the finite classification.  This gives a concrete local organization of the $13$-point tile: the singleton label corner, the three-point stop--tryptophan class, the four-point $CUN$ block, the two-point $AGR$ block, and the two singleton or two-point residual modules are all accounted for within the same finite codon-coordinate record.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The claim is ready at the code-read layer.  The finite rows support the statement that the standard-code label table contains a window-six local tile with $13$ distinguished codons, cubical face data $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral row with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, and nontrivial recovery behavior under code-table and codon deletion tests.  The conclusion is deliberately bounded.  The curated table is a label contact; it is not a translation experiment, not a folding experiment, not a physical viability assay, not a functional assay, not a mechanistic reconstruction, and not evidence that all biological code behavior must follow this local geometry.  Any such stronger statement would require a separate contact with the relevant biological layer.
