\subsection{Window-six codon spectrum and local boundary tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a binary window for the three nucleotide positions.  Each codon is still interpreted through the curated standard-code table: the coordinate system supplies a finite geometry, while the amino-acid or stop label is supplied by the observed code table.  The claim is therefore local.  It says that window-six coordinates expose a boundary-tile pattern in the standard code, not that the coordinate system is a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has thirteen codons.  In lexicographic codon notation the verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The separate cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons.  The punctured-cube check reports the face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the object is not merely a set of labelled codons: it carries a finite local cell structure with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-faces after the puncture specified by the row computation.  The row does not include a full list for the complementary or marker set $M$, but it does verify the observed marker size $|M|=20$ in the null-model checks below.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral computation associated with this finite tile gives three scalar quantities:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported polynomial residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
both as the cubic-polynomial residual and as the radial-polynomial value.  At the stated numerical tolerance this is a zero residual for the finite computation, and the spectral-radius entry agrees with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$.  Thus the spectrum is a concrete finite-row invariant of the local codon tile rather than an interpretive label attached after the fact.

\paragraph{Randomization checks.}
The finite geometry is rare under the sampled null rows recorded with the same packet.  In the general shape randomization with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the run used $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
It also found $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null that preserves amino-acid classes, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed marker size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its two recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite code-table regularity at the local tile level.  They do not establish that the regularity is a law of translation or a physical constraint on molecules.

The observed hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the observed hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The same value, $0.9841269841269841$, is recorded as the hit rate above baseline.  By family, the five reported rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\

The leave-one-codon-out row records no anchors, five boundary cases, eight high-coverage closure cases, eight predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  In finite terms, deleting a codon leaves the local coordinate-and-boundary apparatus with complete recovery on the recorded predictable class, but the recovery statement is still only about codon-label placement.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen boundary codons are partitioned into six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module-completeness row records $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has a predictable-or-boundary explanation in the finite run.  This is the strongest local reading of the packet: the spectral boundary, punctured-cube face data, and module partition all describe the same thirteen-codon region of the standard-code table.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact tests code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, organism-level mechanism, or universality.  Consequently the finite row licenses the following statement and no stronger one: in the standard-code table, the six-coordinate codon window has a rare local boundary-tile signature with a stable thirteen-codon region, a recorded spectral radius, and complete module assignment.  Any claim about translation realization, folded protein structure, molecular feasibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological necessity requires a separate contact that directly measures that higher layer.
