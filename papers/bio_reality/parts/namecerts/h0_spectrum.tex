\subsection{Window-Six Spectral Boundary in the Standard Code}\label{sec:h0-spectrum}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and readout.}
This record treats the standard genetic-code table as a finite code-layer contact. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a binary display of the three nucleotide positions after a fixed two-bit encoding of each base. The observable contact is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the curated codon-to-label table assigning each of the $64$ codons to an amino-acid label or a stop label. Within that finite table, the distinguished boundary row is the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified row count is $|R|=13$. In the induced cubical incidence geometry this row has face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed object is not merely a list of codons: it is a punctured local tile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-faces, and $4$ two-faces inside the window-six cube.

\paragraph{Spectral calculation.}
The spectral record attaches three numerical scalars to the local row: the row-side eigenvalue $\lambda_R$, the larger tile-side eigenvalue $\lambda_M$, and the radial parameter $\mu$. The verified values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports that the cubic relation used to test the row-spectrum closure has residual
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},\\
P_{\mathrm{rad}}(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},\\
\rho_{\mathrm{checked}}&=0.6752479308830213.
\end{aligned}
$$
The residual is at numerical roundoff scale relative to the displayed decimals, while the reported spectral radius equals $\lambda_M$ in the same finite computation. The claim is therefore a finite spectral fit for the observed local codon tile, not an assertion that the genetic code is generated by this spectrum.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under code-label contacts.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$. Because many proposed samples repeated earlier ones, the run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. Among the accepted alternatives, exactly one matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ shape\ matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A looser comparison at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ found $25$ matches, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid classes again used $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It found no exact shape match, $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed module size, and observed $|M|=20$ for the comparison set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact\ matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
\#\{\mathrm{lower\ tail}\leq |M|\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite p-values support the statement that the observed local row is unusual among the sampled label-preserving alternatives. They do not convert the row into a law of translation or an evolutionary necessity.

\paragraph{Predictive checks on held-out labels.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the local-tile rule achieved
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ }M\setminus T&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ }M\setminus T\mathrm{\ plus\ boundary}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
By family, the hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families. The leave-one-codon-out check reported no anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. Thus the row is visible not only in one fitted table but also in finite held-out readback tests at the code-label level.

\paragraph{Module partition of the boundary row.}
The thirteen codons of $R$ are completely assigned to six named modules. The verified partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons. Every module has a predictable-or-boundary explanation in the finite classifier. This is the strongest local conclusion available from the supplied data: the row, the cubical incidence shape, the spectral scalars, the sampled-null rarity, the held-out code checks, and the module partition agree on the same code-layer boundary tile.

\paragraph{Scope of the conclusion.}
The scientific content of this subsection is deliberately narrow. The curated table supplies codon-label readback, and the finite computations expose a local boundary pattern inside the six-coordinate codon display. Nothing here establishes translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality across life. Amino-acid label sequences are labels in this contact, not folded structures. Any later claim about translation, folding, admissible chemistry, function, or universal mechanism must introduce an independent contact measuring that higher layer. Without such a contact, the present result remains a finite code-read pattern: a spectrally coherent and statistically rare local tile in the standard-code table.
