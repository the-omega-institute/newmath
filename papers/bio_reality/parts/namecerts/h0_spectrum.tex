\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum}\label{sec:bior reality-h0-spectrum}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table after it is displayed as a six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The biological input is only the curated assignment of each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  From that input one may form local coordinate distinctions: a six-bit codon address, a tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The present claim is not that the cube is a biological mechanism.  It is the narrower statement that the finite label table has a sharply visible local boundary spectrum when read through this window-six coordinate system.

\paragraph{Boundary row set.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite count is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same rows also satisfy the punctured-cube face census
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the record is not merely a list of exceptional labels.  It is a thirteen-vertex local subshape with sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies and four two-dimensional faces inside the window-six display.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
A separate finite randomization check sampled $N=2000$ distinct comparison attempts after $57377$ total attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, using seed $42$.  Only one sample matched the exact observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
Twenty-five samples reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed $M$-region has size $20$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, so the recorded exact-shape value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite rows say that the local shape is rare under the supplied comparison schemes.  They do not say that the geometry causes the genetic code.

\paragraph{Interpretive limit.}
The contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supports codon-to-label readback and local split tests at the code layer.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a causal mechanism, or universality across living systems.  Therefore the result may be stated as a finite spectral and combinatorial regularity of the standard-code label table under a window-six display.  Any promotion beyond that level requires an additional contact of the relevant kind, supplied and tested separately.
