\subsection{Window-six spectrum of the standard-code boundary tile}
\label{subsec:codon-window-six-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The record considered here is a finite six-bit codon display attached to the curated standard-code label table.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read only at the code-label level: a row names a codon and the curated table supplies its amino-acid or stop label.  The tested boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$\{AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}$.
The same row also identifies $R$ with a punctured-cube pattern whose face-count vector is $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the object under discussion is not an open-ended biological mechanism; it is a finite label-bearing subset of $Q_6$ with a stated cubical incidence profile.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectrum attached to this boundary tile reports two distinguished local radii and one ambient scale.  The numerical values are $\lambda_M = 0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R = 0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu = 3.051487585298128$.  The recorded residual for the cubic relation is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is reported as the radial-polynomial residual.  In the finite-row tolerance used by the run, the spectral radius field returns $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$.  Equivalently, the numerical check is the statement that the displayed row is effectively on the cubic surface at machine precision:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,\\
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu) &\approx -6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a finite spectral signature of the local codon-label tile.  It is not, by itself, an assertion about translation chemistry, folding, admissible physical dynamics, organismal function, a generative mechanism, or a universal law of biology.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape row supplies a separate finite comparison against randomized label-preserving draws.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled trials, the run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, giving $p=0.0005$, and $25$ matches at Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$.  A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, records observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape $p$-value $0.0$, and lower-tail $p$-value $0.0035$.  These numbers support the statement that the observed local shape is uncommon under the stated finite nulls.  They do not convert the codon-label table into an experiment on ribosomal dynamics, molecular structure, thermodynamic accessibility, or phenotype.

\paragraph{Boundary modules.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned to six named modules.  The stop--tryptophan module contains $UAA$, $UAG$, and $UGA$; the $CUN$ module contains $CUA$, $CUC$, $CUG$, and $CUU$; the $AGR$ module contains $AGA$ and $AGG$; the serine/leucine row fragment contains $UCA$ and $UUA$; the isoleucine/methionine module contains $AUA$; and the arginine/lysine module contains $AAA$.  The module-classification row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Hence the local boundary is not merely a cardinality artifact: every listed codon has a place in the finite module partition used by the packet.

\paragraph{Leave-one checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  Family-level leave-one-code-out rates are $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  The leave-one-codon-out row records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, recovery rate $1.0$, and recovery rate above threshold $1.0$.  These finite recovery numbers are evidence that the same local tile continues to organize code-label readback under the stated omissions.

\paragraph{Local boundary of interpretation.}
The only empirical contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is sufficient for code-layer readback and for checking the listed local split, but it does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal biological mechanism.  Amino-acid label sequences therefore remain labels in this chapter.  Any claim crossing from this finite codon tile to protein structure, molecular implementation, organismal function, or lawlike biological necessity would require an additional contact with those phenomena, not merely the spectral, shape, or leave-one rows recorded above.
