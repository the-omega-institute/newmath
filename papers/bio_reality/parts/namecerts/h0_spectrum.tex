\subsection{Window-six spectral record for the standard code}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The record is formed on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the $64$ RNA codons.  Its biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label.  The contact therefore supports code-layer label readback and local codon-tile checks.  It does not, by itself, observe ribosomal translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any claim crossing one of those boundaries requires a distinct observation of that higher layer.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The distinguished codon set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the finite run it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies this set with a punctured-cube pattern whose face-vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the record is not a free verbal grouping of codons; it has an explicit finite support, an edge count, and a two-face count inside the codon-cube presentation.  The set contains the three stop codons $\mathrm{UAA}$, $\mathrm{UAG}$, and $\mathrm{UGA}$, the arginine pair $\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}$, the isoleucine--methionine boundary codon $\mathrm{AUA}$, the leucine quartet $\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}$, and the remaining listed singleton or two-point entries $\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}$.

\begin{aligned}

\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The polynomial residual attached to the radial computation is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is reported as the cubic residual.  The spectral-radius value in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$ at the precision recorded by the finite run.  The operational reading is modest: the local code tile has a stable numerical spectrum in the six-bit codon display, and the $R$ boundary sits inside that display with a smaller recorded value $\lambda_R$.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
A shape-randomization row with $N=2000$ trials reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape matched once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ matched $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The seed was $42$.  These numbers support the finite claim that the observed codon-tile shape is rare under the sampled coordinate-preserving comparison used in the run.  They do not say that the geometry is a biological necessity, and they do not identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null row.}

$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
This row is useful because it tests against a more label-aware shuffle rather than only against unconstrained codon geometry.  Its scope is still a code-table scope: it compares finite label assignments and tile shapes, not proteins, structures, or cellular outcomes.

\subsection{Local interpretation and exclusions}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum-boundary}

\paragraph{What the record supports.}
The record supports a finite code-read statement: in the six-coordinate codon cube, the curated standard-code labels exhibit a $13$-codon boundary set with punctured-cube counts $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral row with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, rare sampled shape matches under two null comparisons, and strong local recovery in leave-one-out tests.  The support is numerical, finite, and attached to codon-label readback.

\paragraph{What the record excludes.}
No translation realization follows from this chapter.  No folded-protein structure follows from amino-acid label readback.  No physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from the codon tile.  The mixed case between local codon geometry and higher biological layers is resolved by holding the chapter at the code-read layer until a separate contact supplies the missing observation.
