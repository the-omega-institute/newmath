\subsection{Window-Six Spectral Boundary for the Standard-Code Table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism: it is the coordinate display in which each codon is represented by a six-bit word, local tile faces are inspected, singleton label corners are separated from three-point label classes, and the readback is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The contact therefore tests only code-layer label readback and local codon-tile splitting.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or structural order.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the verified list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},
\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object being measured is not an unconstrained set of exceptional codons; it is a locally tiled subset of $Q_6$ with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
A shape-null run used $N=2000$ sampled tables, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$.  In that null, the observed marked set size is $|M|=20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The reported finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the statement that the observed local tile is unusual under the tested finite nulls.  They do not by themselves supply a translation model, a folding model, a kinetic model, or a functional model.

\end{aligned}

These are code-read statistics.  They say that the finite coordinate row recovers held-out label positions within the tested standard-code style contact; they do not say that codon geometry causes the biological implementation of translation.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite-row verdict is that the standard-code table contains a measurable local tile in the six-bit codon cube: a $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary, a stable spectral row with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, significant finite-null behavior, and complete module coverage under the reported leave-one probes.  The local cannot-claim boundary is strict.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this record unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains code readback; it is not folded protein structure.
