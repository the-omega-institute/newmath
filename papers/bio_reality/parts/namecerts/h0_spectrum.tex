\subsection{Window-six codon spectrum at the code-label boundary}
\label{sec:h0-spectrum-window-six-codon-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table after it is placed on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is treated as a finite coordinate row, and the only biological readback used here is the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The construction therefore lives at the code-read layer: it can test codon-label partitions and local cube geometry, but it does not by itself observe translation, folding, physical admissibility, biochemical function, a mechanism, or a universal biological law.

The distinguished boundary row set is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite row audit gives $|R|=13$.  The same rows form a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the boundary is not merely a label list: it has a small cube-combinatorial profile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-faces, and four two-faces in the induced finite complex.

\paragraph{Spectral measurement.}
The spectrum probe separates the measured row block from the surrounding codon-label table by two positive spectral scales.  The computed values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded spectral radius is the $M$-scale value
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho=0.6752479308830213.
\end{aligned}
$$
The numerical residual for the cubic relation is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the same value is recorded for the radial polynomial residual.  At the precision of this finite run, the spectral row therefore behaves as a stable algebraic coordinate of the local codon tile.  The point is limited: the cubic residual certifies a numerical relation in this six-coordinate code display, not an independent biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The row shape is rare under the finite null sample attached to the run.  With $N=2000$ sampled comparators, the exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, with seed $42$.  These counts matter because the rarity claim is a finite-row statement about this sampled coordinate population, not an asymptotic theorem about all possible genetic codes.

A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In that class-preserving sample, the observed measured block has size $|M|=20$.  Among $N=2000$ trials, exact shape recurrence occurred $0$ times, and $7$ trials fell in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The corresponding reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the local code-layer reading: the observed shape is not simply recovered by preserving broad amino-acid class counts in the sampled finite population.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback checks.}
The code-table perturbation tests give a separate finite stability check.  In the leave-one-code-out experiment, $23$ code tables were evaluated against $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Inside $M\setminus T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary it was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate.  These numbers say that the coordinate tile retains code-label predictive content across the finite comparison families used in the run.

The leave-one-codon-out experiment reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, and $8$ predictable codons, with recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus every codon tested in this row-level recovery regime is accounted for by the predictable-or-boundary criterion used by the packet.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen rows of $R$ are exhausted by six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing rows from the module list, and no unassigned codons.  The module decomposition therefore matches the finite boundary set exactly.  It also gives the biological reading a precise scope: these are codon-label modules in the curated standard-code table, not claims about folded protein state or organism-level adaptation.

\paragraph{Scientific boundary.}
The contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can support code-label readback and local split checks.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal mechanism.  Therefore the conclusion is deliberately narrow: the window-six coordinate display exposes a rare, spectrally coherent, locally recoverable codon-label boundary pattern, with $R$ equal to the thirteen listed codons and with spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  Any promotion beyond the code-read layer requires a separate observational contact aimed at that higher layer.
