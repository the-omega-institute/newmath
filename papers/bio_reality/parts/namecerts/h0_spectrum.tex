\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum and Local Boundary Readback}
\label{subsec:h0-spectrum-window-six-codon-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The claim \texttt{h0.spectrum} is a finite codon-window record on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is treated as a length-three base word with a six-coordinate display, and the observable contact is only the curated standard-code assignment from codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The data do not assert a mechanism of translation.  They record a local split inside the code-label table: a distinguished boundary set $R$, a complementary closure region $M$ of observed size $20$, and the spectral scalars obtained from the associated finite adjacency calculation.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The verified boundary row has cardinality $13$.  Its listed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies this set as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it has a finite cubical face profile.  The profile is still only a code-layer shape.  It is not a biological law and it does not by itself determine cellular execution, protein conformation, chemical admissibility, activity, or universality across organisms.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectrum calculation gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same number is reported as the radial-polynomial residual.  At this numerical tolerance the spectral-radius row is therefore internally consistent, with recorded radius $0.6752479308830213$.  The numerical content is a finite certificate for the window-six table calculation: it says that the local $M/R$ decomposition has a reproducible spectral signature, not that the codon table is forced by spectral geometry.

\paragraph{Randomization checks.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second null model preserving amino-acid classes again used $N=2000$ samples.  In that run the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, the exact-shape value was $0.0$, and the lower-tail value was $0.0035$.  These two finite rows support the claim that the observed local shape is unusual relative to the sampled null ensembles.  They do not identify a causal biochemical process.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code readback.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level split contains $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$.  This supports a code-read stability statement: when nearby code tables are withheld, the local window-six split continues to recover the relevant label positions at a rate far above the baseline row.

The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. Every module has either predictable or boundary status in the recorded classification.  The classification is therefore complete for the finite boundary set, and the recovery row says that the local split can be tested codon-by-codon inside this finite table.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code label table.  It can test codon-label readback and local tile splitting.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universal validity.  Amino-acid label sequences are therefore kept at the code layer.  Any claim about folding, function, admissible chemistry, organism-level mechanism, or universality would require a separate observation of that higher layer before it could be stated as supported.
