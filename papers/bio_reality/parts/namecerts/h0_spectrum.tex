\paragraph{Record.}
The record $h0.spectrum$ concerns the finite window-six codon display associated with the standard-code codon table. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only at the code layer. Its finite distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The external contact is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. This contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for local split checks, but it is not a contact with translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\begin{aligned}

Thus the row is not merely a list of labels: it carries a local cubical incidence profile inside $Q_6$. The word ``local'' is essential. The data show a finite tile boundary inside a codon coordinate display; they do not show that biological translation is governed by this geometry as a necessity.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
For the high-overlap threshold, $25$ samples had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed $M$ size $20$, exact match count $0$, and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size. Its finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support the claim that the observed local window-six shape is uncommon under the stated finite nulls. They do not promote the shape into a law of biochemistry.
