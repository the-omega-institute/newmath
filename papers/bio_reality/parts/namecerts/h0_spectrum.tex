\subsection{Window-six spectral boundary for the standard-code tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon-label table as an external reality contact.  The contact is deliberately narrow: it supplies codon-to-label readback for amino-acid or stop labels, and it does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The BEDC-side distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class.  The internal structure recorded by the row is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation.  Thus the object of the chapter is not the whole biology of translation, but a finite code-layer pattern inside $Q_6$ whose labels are read from the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check returns the same thirteen codons and the face-vector signature
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile boundary read at the code layer.  It is a finite-row fact about a subset of the codon cube, not a claim that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Shape and null rows.}
The shape comparison for $M$ used $N=2000$ sampled null instances with seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, recorded observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ at or below the observed $M$ size.  Its finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support the claim that the observed code-layer tile is not a common output of the tested null families.  They still remain code-layer tests, because the source table contains labels rather than physical realizations.

The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. These rows say that the local coordinate and boundary data recover many code-table label changes, not that they recover a translation machine.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The chapter records a local codon-tile boundary in $Q_6$ with standard-code label readback.  It does not claim that window-six geometry is translation, that amino-acid label sequences are folded protein structures, that the tile is physically admissible, that it realizes biological function, that it supplies a universal mechanism, or that it is a global biological law.  Any such promotion requires a separate reality contact whose observed object lives at the corresponding layer.  The present contact is the curated standard-code table, and that contact stops at codon-label readback.
