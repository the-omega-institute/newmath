\subsection{Window-six spectral packet for the standard code}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed contact.}
The packet uses the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$ as its finite display carrier.  A codon is treated as a six-bit window, and the only biological contact admitted here is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Thus the contact tests code-label readback and local label splitting.  It does not test ribosomal realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a generative mechanism, or a universal law of biology.  Those higher layers require separate observations before they can enter the claim.

Within this carrier the distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies this set as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a finite geometric statement about the window display and its label boundary.  It is not a statement that the genome, translation system, or protein fold is forced by this geometry.

It is not evidence for folding order, biochemical feasibility, translation kinetics, or organism-level function.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under finite null rows.}
The observed $M$-side size in the amino-acid-class preserving null row is $20$.  Under that null model, $2000$ samples produced $0$ exact shape matches and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  Hence the finite-row estimates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate shape-significance row, also with $2000$ samples and seed $42$, records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the displayed local tile is statistically non-generic within the tested finite null families.  They do not by themselves identify a mechanism.  The data row remains a code-layer observation: it compares finite codon-label patterns, not molecular dynamics or protein structure.

\paragraph{Module decomposition of the boundary set.}
The module-classification row assigns all $13$ elements of $R$ and reports no extra module codons, no missing members, and no unassigned codons.  The verified decomposition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
This decomposition is the most concrete local readback of the packet: every boundary codon lies in one of the listed finite modules, and each module has either predictable or boundary status.  The classification does not license a statement about tRNA copy number, cross-organism causation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, translation realization, or a universal mechanism.  Such claims would need an additional contact whose observed object is actually at that layer.

In the window-six codon cube, the curated standard-code labels determine a reproducible local boundary tile $R$ of size $13$, with punctured-cube face-vector $(13,16,4)$, a tolerated cubic spectral signature with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, and strong finite null-model separation under the sampled tests. It does not infer translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality from the codon-window geometry.
