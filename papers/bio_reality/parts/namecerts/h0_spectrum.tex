\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with a standard-code label readback.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, understood as a codon-to-label table at the code-read layer.  It supplies assignments from codons to amino-acid or stop labels, but it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any necessity claim about why biological DNA must obey this geometry.  The row is therefore a finite code-layer observation: window-six coordinates expose a local tile boundary, while the label table remains an external reality contact.

In this chapter the phrase ``punctured cube'' means exactly this finite incidence fact, not a claim that the biological code is generated by the cube as a law of nature.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The cannot-claim boundary is part of the mathematical content of this row.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only label readback.  Any promotion beyond the code-read layer requires a separate contact that directly observes the higher layer being claimed.  With only the curated standard-code table present, the ready statement is therefore: the standard-code labels expose a finite window-six boundary tile with a $13$-codon punctured-cube support, face-vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral radii $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, significant finite null-model shape statistics, and complete module coverage; no translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from this row alone.
