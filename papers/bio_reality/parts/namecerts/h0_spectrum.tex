\subsection{Window-six spectral boundary for the standard-code codon table}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object and contact.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer. The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as the binary display of the three codon positions. The contact used here is only the curated table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. That contact is strong enough to test label readback and local boundary structure, but it does not observe translation in a cell, a folded molecule, physical admissibility, biological activity, a mechanism, or a universal biological principle. Any statement crossing one of those boundaries requires an additional contact of the corresponding kind.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The verified boundary row has size $13$. The set $R$ is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite observation is not merely a list of exceptional labels: it has a small cubical incidence profile inside $Q_6$. The row does not assert that the codon table is forced by this geometry; it says that, after the standard-code labels are supplied by the curated table, the displayed boundary occupies a specific local cubical pattern.

\paragraph{Spectral certificate.}
The spectral row attached to this boundary gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the same value is recorded for the cubic-polynomial residual. Within numerical tolerance this places the observed spectral radius at $0.6752479308830213$. The spectral data therefore give a finite numerical signature for the codon-window boundary, not a biological explanation of why translation uses these labels.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under label-preserving probes.}
Two finite null probes make the local shape nontrivial. In the general shape probe with $N=2000$ sampled alternatives and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run found $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler attempted $57377$ distinct draws and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null probe, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$. This gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support the limited claim that the standard-code label table carries a rare local boundary signature under the tested finite rearrangements.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback tests.}
The code-table leave-one test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. Restricting to $M\setminus T$ gives $0.9365079365079365$, and adding the boundary raises the same row to $0.9841269841269841$. The codon leave-one row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
These tests say that the local window carries recovery information for the curated code labels. They do not say that the recovered label sequence is a folded protein or that the label split implements any physical process.

The family-level leave-one row has $5$ families. The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid tables, $1.0$ for the candidate-other group, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate tables. Thus the boundary has a finite module accounting compatible with the observed standard-code labels.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite rows support the following bounded reading. In the six-coordinate codon cube, the curated standard-code label table exposes a $13$-point boundary set with punctured-cube incidence $(13,16,4)$, a stable numerical spectral signature, rare shape behavior under the sampled null probes, and strong leave-one recovery at the code-read layer. The evidence stops there. No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from this record unless a separate observation supplies that higher-layer content.
