\subsection{Window-six spectrum of the standard-code boundary tile}
\label{sec:h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The object studied here is the standard-code codon table after it has been placed on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The only observed biological input used in this chapter is the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The finite construction then asks whether the label table, when read as a six-bit coordinate object, exposes a small local boundary tile.  The distinguished boundary row is
$R=\{AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}$,
with $|R|=13$.  In the cubical incidence calculation this same row is recorded as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of labels: it has a finite cubical footprint consisting of thirteen vertices, sixteen one-faces, and four two-faces in the window-six coordinate display.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral calculation attaches three scalar quantities to the local tile.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported residual for the cubic relation is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is recorded as the radial-polynomial residual.  At the scale of the finite computation, this is a numerical zero test for the asserted spectral relation.  The spectral radius field also records $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$ to the displayed precision.  The conclusion of this subsection is therefore narrow: the six-bit coordinate encoding of the curated code table contains a reproducible local boundary row whose spectral parameters satisfy the stated cubic test to numerical tolerance.

\paragraph{Shape controls.}
The finite-row audit compares the observed shape against randomized alternatives.  In the shape-significance run, $N=2000$ samples were evaluated with seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was exactly $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid class information again used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It recorded $0$ exact matches, an observed $M$-size of $20$, and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below that observed size.  Its exact-shape value is recorded as $0.0$, while the lower-tail value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These controls do not prove a biological mechanism.  They show that the observed finite cubical pattern is uncommon under the two recorded randomization regimes.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate after adding the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$.  The same $0.9841269841269841$ value is recorded as the rate above baseline.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families, across $5$ families.  The leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers support the local readback claim: removing a code table or a codon still leaves a strong finite-coordinate signal for the boundary tile in the recorded tests.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen codons of $R$ are completely assigned to six modules.  The recorded module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA, UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA, UAG, UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  This closes the finite bookkeeping for the local row: every codon in the boundary set appears in exactly the recorded module accounting, and the module list neither omits nor adds codons relative to $R$.

\paragraph{Interpretive boundary.}
The result is a code-layer statement.  The curated table supplies codon-to-label readback, and the six-bit construction supplies a finite coordinate, closure, spectral, and relation analysis of that readback.  Nothing in these rows observes translation as a cellular process, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Those stronger claims would require separate contacts of the appropriate kind.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is still only a label sequence in this chapter; it is not a folded structure, not a function assay, and not a physical-realization certificate.
