\subsection{Window-six spectral boundary for standard-code readback}\label{sec:h0-spectrum}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The record works on the finite codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-bit display of the $64$ standard codons.  The relevant distinctions are not biochemical mechanisms.  They are coordinate distinctions inside the finite display: a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The biological contact is only the curated standard-code label table, namely the finite assignment of each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  Thus the readback layer is $\mathrm{code\_read}$, and the verified object is a local code-label pattern inside $Q_6$.

Under the same run, $25$ samples reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$.  The sampler attempted $57377$ distinct draws and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the result should be read as a finite-row sampling statement with heavy duplicate pressure, not as a closed-form distribution theorem.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, gave $0$ exact shape matches.  It recorded observed $M$ size $20$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.
