\subsection{Window-six spectral record for the standard-code codon tile}\label{sec:h0-spectrum-window-six-standard-code}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Record.}
This record treats the standard genetic-code table as a curated code-layer contact and asks what finite structure is exposed when its codons are read in a six-coordinate codon display. The carrier is the $64$-vertex codon cube obtained from the three nucleotide positions, each represented by two bits. The observable labels are the standard-code amino-acid and stop assignments. The point of the record is deliberately narrow: the data may support a local codon-label split and a spectral signature of that split, but it does not by itself assert translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality. Those stronger readings would require independent contacts of the appropriate kind.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it has a finite adjacency-and-face profile inside the codon cube. The reported codons group into the six modules used by the leave-one-codon audit: $\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$. The module accounting is exact: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and there are no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral computation attached to this carrier reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run records the radial polynomial residual as
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
and records the cubic-polynomial residual with the same value. At the precision of the finite computation, the residual is numerically zero relative to the scale of the displayed eigenvalue data. The reported spectral-radius value in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$. This is the central finite-row content of claim $h0.spectrum$: the local codon tile carries a stable numerical spectrum when read through the curated standard-code labels.

\paragraph{Shape controls.}
The shape audit used $N=2000$ sampled comparisons with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers do not turn the tile into a biological law. They say that, under this finite comparison protocol, the observed local shape is uncommon, and the high-overlap tail is still small but less extreme than exact equality.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving control.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class information. In that audit $N=2000$, again with seed $42$. The observed $M$-size was $20$. The exact-match count was $0$, so the reported exact-shape value was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This control is important because it keeps part of the label-class information fixed while still testing whether the observed local tile is easily reproduced. The result supports a code-layer structural signal, not a claim about downstream molecular realization.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code evidence.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the family count was $5$ and the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast. This says that the local codon coordinate scheme has strong finite predictive recovery at the code-table level, with one reported family below perfect recovery but still far above the baseline row.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon evidence.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The recovery-rate-above-threshold field is also $1.0$. The module classification agrees with the boundary list: every module has either predictable or boundary status, and the complete module assignment accounts for exactly the $13$ codons of $R$. This is a finite local robustness check: deletion of a codon from the code-layer row remains recoverable through the tile and module structure recorded here.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded claim. In the six-coordinate codon cube, the curated standard-code labels expose a local boundary tile of size $13$, with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, a reproducible module decomposition, a spectral row with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and nontrivial performance under two leave-one protocols. The evidence is explicitly limited to codon-label readback and local split behavior. It does not say that the cube geometry is a law of biology, that a label sequence is a folded protein, that codon labels determine physical admissibility, or that the pattern is a universal mechanism. Any passage from this code-layer record to translation, structure, physical chemistry, function, or organism-level generality must be supplied by a separate empirical contact rather than by this finite tile alone.
