\subsection{Spectral Boundary of the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{subsec:h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record uses the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$ as its finite display: each standard-code codon is read as a six-bit window over the four nucleotide letters, with the curated standard-code table supplying the amino-acid or stop label attached to each corner. The construction is deliberately local. It tests whether a particular subset of codons forms a boundary tile inside $Q_6$, and it records how that tile behaves under finite closure and spectral probes. The reality contact is only the curated codon-to-label table. Consequently the contact reaches the code-label layer and does not by itself observe translation dynamics, folded molecular structure, physical admissibility, biological function, an organism-level mechanism, or a universal law.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified by the punctured-cube check: the induced finite tile has $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the observed object is not merely a list of labels. It has thirteen vertices, sixteen local one-faces, and four two-faces in the window-six geometry. The row is still only a code-layer object: these faces are combinatorial faces of the codon display, not molecular contacts, folding constraints, or biochemical transitions.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectrum attached to this tile separates the larger measured closure component from the boundary component. The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial relation used by the finite computation evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same number is recorded as the cubic residual. This is numerically zero at the tolerance of the run, so the spectral row is internally coherent: the radius reported for $M$ is $0.6752479308830213$, the radius reported for $R$ is smaller at $0.4758352843197002$, and the scalar $\mu$ gives the associated finite radial parameter. The conclusion is a spectral separation inside the codon-window display. It is not a claim that nucleic-acid chemistry must realize the same separation as a causal mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape check compares the observed $M$ shape against $2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$. The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space was sampled under a strong distinctness constraint. There was exactly $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ samples reaching Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$. An amino-acid-class-preserving null model gives a compatible but stricter finite-row contrast: among $2000$ class-preserving samples there were $0$ exact matches to the observed shape, while $7$ samples lay in the lower tail at or below the observed size $|M|=20$. The corresponding recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the statement that the local tile is unusual relative to the sampled code-label nulls. They do not license promotion from code-label geometry to translation, folding, physical viability, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The code-level perturbation row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. Against that baseline, the recorded hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. The codon-level omission row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate $1.0$. Thus the finite probe is stable under two small-deletion tests: it tends to recover omitted code labels from the local closure-and-boundary organization rather than from a global biological assertion.

\paragraph{Family and module rows.}
The family-level check partitions the leave-one-code-out behavior into $5$ families. The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate codes. The module classification row assigns all $13$ elements of $R$ with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

Every listed module has either a predictable or a boundary role in the finite record. This gives a compact explanation of why the thirteen-codon boundary is not an arbitrary subset of the standard-code table: it is organized by recurrent code-label modules, by punctured-cube incidence, and by a coherent spectral row.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite evidence supports a code-read claim: window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table, with $|R|=13$, $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral radii $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, significant shape rarity under the sampled nulls, and complete assignment of the thirteen boundary codons into six modules. The finite record must remain at that layer. A separate contact would be required before asserting translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, causal mechanism, or universality.
