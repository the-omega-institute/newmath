\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact placed on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  The biological contact is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The coordinate object does not assert a translation machine, a structural fold, a physically admissible molecule, a biological function, a universal rule, or a mechanism.  It asks whether the observed label table, when read at the level of codon labels, exposes a small local boundary tile inside the cube.

The distinguished boundary row is the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality row gives $|R|=13$.  The same finite row identifies $R$ with a punctured-cube pattern having $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the object is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it has an explicitly counted vertex-edge-face profile inside the six-bit display.  The supplied data do not include an element-by-element member list for the complementary or ambient set $M$; the finite record instead gives the scalar row $|M|=20$ through the amino-acid-class preserving null experiment.

\paragraph{Shape significance under finite nulls.}
Two finite null checks give independent pressure against treating the observed tile as an arbitrary shape.  In the first null experiment, $N=2000$ samples were evaluated with seed $42$.  There was $1$ exact shape match, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling log records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which is a useful warning that the finite search space is highly constrained by the imposed row conditions.  In the amino-acid-class preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, and the reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a code-layer shape statement: the local tile is unusual under the recorded finite nulls.  They do not promote the tile to a physical or functional law.

\paragraph{Local verdict and scope.}
The finite rows justify the following restrained conclusion.  The standard-code label table, viewed through six-bit codon coordinates, contains a $13$-codon boundary pattern with punctured-cube profile $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral row with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, significant finite-null behavior, and strong leave-one recovery across code and codon probes.  The conclusion is only about code-label readback.  No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these rows unless a separate empirical contact supplies that higher-layer information.
