\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum and Local Boundary Tile}
\label{sec:biorality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and label contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a display of the $64$ codons in window-six coordinates. The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and therefore supplies a code-layer readback. It does not supply a translation mechanism, a folding experiment, a physical-admissibility test, a function assay, or a universality theorem. The distinctions recorded in this row are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class. The internal BEDC structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the readback is the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA}, \mathrm{AGA}, \mathrm{AGG}, \mathrm{AUA}, \mathrm{CUA}, \mathrm{CUC}, \mathrm{CUG}, \mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA}, \mathrm{UAG}, \mathrm{UCA}, \mathrm{UGA}, \mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube boundary with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it has a finite local tile shape, with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the recorded punctured-cube complex.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the recorded null sample with $N=2000$, the exact local shape appeared once. The exact-shape frequency is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} = 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap test at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ produced $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, with seed $42$. These numbers support the finite claim that the observed local tile is not a typical draw under this row's coordinate null. They do not identify a molecular cause.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class preserving null was also run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ at or below the observed $M$-size. The resulting finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This row says that preserving amino-acid classes alone does not normally reproduce the observed shape. The result remains a code-read statement: the contact is a curated codon-to-label table, not a physical realization of protein structure.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The supported claim is precisely that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile and spectral structure for standard-code label readback. The chapter does not claim that DNA obeys this geometry as a global biological necessity. It does not claim translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or universality of the standard code. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. Any promotion beyond code\_read requires a separate higher-layer reality contact, such as a translation experiment, a structural assay, a physical admissibility model with empirical contact, or a function measurement.
