\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The claim recorded here concerns a six-bit codon display whose empirical readout is only the curated standard-code table.  The carrier is the $64$-vertex codon cube $Q_6$, read at the code-label layer: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label by the table, and the construction asks whether a local boundary tile is visible inside that finite labelled cube.  The selected boundary row is the $13$-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit reports $|R|=13$, and the same row is identified as a punctured cube with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not being introduced as a biological mechanism.  It is a finite pattern extracted from the label table, with the singleton-corner and three-point-label-class distinctions held inside the code-read layer.

The calculation does not state that DNA, translation, or protein structure is governed by this geometry.  It states only that this particular labelled codon table supports a local row whose spectral value is reproducible in the recorded finite audit.

\paragraph{Shape significance against randomized rows.}
The shape test for the associated $M$-row used $N=2000$ accepted samples, with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the accepted samples there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  Consequently the reported empirical probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite row therefore records both an exact-shape rarity and a high-overlap rarity under the sampling procedure.  A second null calculation preserved amino-acid class information.  In that audit, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The resulting values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the modest conclusion that the observed local shape is unusual under the two recorded randomization regimes.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Local boundary of the claim.}
The empirical contact for this chapter is the curated standard-code table, and its resolution is codon-label readback.  The support condition is correspondingly limited: the table supports local split checks and codon-to-label comparison.  If a later argument wants to speak about translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality, it must attach a separate contact that observes that higher layer directly.  The present finite row blocks such promotion.  The spectral residual, the $13$-codon punctured-cube row, the shape probabilities, the leave-one-out recovery values, and the complete module assignment together give a precise code-layer record for the standard table, not a global law of biology.
