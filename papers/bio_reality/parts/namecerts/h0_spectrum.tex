\subsection{Window-six spectral boundary for the standard code}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record uses the six-bit codon cube as its finite coordinate display.  A codon is treated only as a position in this window and as an entry of a curated standard-code label table.  The biological contact therefore supplies amino-acid or stop labels, while the internal geometry supplies adjacency, closure, and local tile data.  The observed boundary set is the thirteen-codon row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The same row is recognized as a punctured-cube tile with face-vector $(13,16,4)$: thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four local two-faces.  This is a finite statement about the code-layer table and the chosen window; it is not a claim about how translation is physically realized.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under finite randomization.}
The main shape-rarity run used $N=2000$ randomized samples with seed $42$.  Producing the distinct samples required $57377$ attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A high-overlap comparison at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ found $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid class also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  There the exact shape count was $0$, so the corresponding empirical exact-shape probability was $0.0$.  The observed module size was $20$, and $7$ samples were in the lower tail at or below that size, giving a lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These numbers support the claim that the local tile is not a typical random artifact of the tested finite models, while still leaving the claim at the code-read layer.

Restricting to $M\setminus T$ gave $0.9365079365079365$, and adding the boundary term returned $0.9841269841269841$.  In the leave-one-codon-out row, there were $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate $1.0$.  The family-level code-out split contains five families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, and bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Local boundary of the claim.}
The curated standard-code table is the only biological contact used here.  It permits codon-label readback and local split tests, and it blocks promotion to claims about translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, general mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label sequences produced from the table are still code-layer labels.  They do not by themselves observe a fold, certify physical viability, explain function, or establish a law applying across organisms.  Any such stronger statement would require an additional contact of the appropriate kind, separate from the codon table and separate from the six-bit local tile.
