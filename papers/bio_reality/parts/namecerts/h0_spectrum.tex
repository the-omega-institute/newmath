\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact, not as a mechanism. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code table that assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label. The finite distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The resulting object is a local window-six tile: it has coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data, but its readback remains only the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape row was tested against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$. The finite run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and had $55377$ duplicate retries. Exactly one sample matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$. A second amino-acid-class-preserving null run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact matches for the observed shape and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size $20$. Its reported probabilities are $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$. These numbers support the claim that the local tile is not a typical outcome of the tested finite-row null models, while still leaving the interpretation at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite evidence supports a chapter-local statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a boundary-tile spectrum in the curated standard-code label table. The contact is the table of codon-to-label assignments; the probe can test local code-read split behavior and boundary membership. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, universal mechanism, or biological necessity. No conclusion about protein folding, amino-acid physical admissibility, organismal function, or a universal law of DNA follows from this row unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore kept as code-layer readback only.
