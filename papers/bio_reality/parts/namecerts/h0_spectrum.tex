\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The record is built on the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read as a finite codon cube whose vertices carry standard-code labels through the curated codon table contact.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is a finite coordinate surface on which the standard-code label table can be read back.  The relevant distinguished row is the set $R$ of thirteen codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row audit reports $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube readout has face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not being introduced as an abstract label class alone: it is a concrete local tile in the window-six cube, with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four square faces.  The corresponding $M$ row is observed only through the supplied finite scalar $|M|=20$ in the null-model record; no individual $M$ codon list is supplied by this row, so the present chapter does not assert one.

\paragraph{Shape rarity against finite nulls.}
The local shape is not treated as self-validating.  It is compared against finite null rows.  In the shape-significance run, $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables were requested under seed $42$; obtaining those samples required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape was matched once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap shape agreement at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a stricter local obstruction: among $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$, the exact-shape count is $0$, so the reported exact-shape value is $0.0$.  The lower-tail count at the observed $M$ size is $7$, with observed $|M|=20$, producing
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrow claim that the observed window-six tile is uncommon under the sampled finite alternatives.  They do not convert the tile into a law of biology.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which states that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  It does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global biological law.  Therefore the spectrum record has the following final interpretation: window-six coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, with a concrete thirteen-codon row, a reproducible spectral residual, finite null rarity, and leave-one-out recovery pressure.  Nothing in this row licenses promotion from code labels to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that evidence.
