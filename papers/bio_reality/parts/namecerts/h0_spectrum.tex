\subsection{Window-six spectrum of the standard-code tile}
\label{sec:bior-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Object and contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$.  The biological contact used here is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact tests code-layer label readback and local split structure only.  It does not observe translation in a cell, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, an organism-level mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Any assertion crossing one of those boundaries would require an additional observation whose resolution is appropriate to that higher layer.

$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.

The same finite row identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube-shaped region with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
Thus the code-layer object is not merely a count: it carries a local cubical incidence profile with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-dimensional faces in the chosen display.  The computation also reports an observed $M$-region size of $20$ under the amino-acid-class-preserving comparison.  No codon-by-codon list for $M$ is asserted in this record; only its supplied finite statistics are used.

\paragraph{Randomization checks.}
Against the shape null run with $N=2000$, the finite computation required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only $1$ exact shape match was observed, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
At the relaxed Jaccard threshold $\geq 0.9$, there were $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving comparison used the same sample size $N=2000$.  It found $0$ exact matches to the observed shape and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size $20$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support a local code-table regularity: the observed tile is uncommon under the supplied finite comparison procedures.  They do not by themselves identify a causal evolutionary process or a physical realization principle.

These numbers say that the local display has strong code-layer recovery behavior under the supplied tests.  They still remain label-recovery facts, not facts about folded structures or organismal function.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The record is therefore ready as a code-read claim.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$; the visible distinctions are the local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class; and the internal measurements are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The readback is the standard-code label table.  The observed finite rows make the local tile nontrivial: $R$ has thirteen explicitly listed codons, a punctured-cube face profile $(13,16,4)$, a numerical spectral triple $(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)$, significant shape statistics under two finite null comparisons, high leave-one recovery, and a complete module partition.  The same rows also impose the scope limit.
