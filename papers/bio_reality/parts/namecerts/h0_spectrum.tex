\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the finite display obtained by writing each codon as a window-six coordinate and reading the curated standard-code label table against that coordinate system.  The reality contact used here is only the codon-to-label assignment table: it says that each of the $64$ codons has an amino-acid or stop label in the curated standard code.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape significance checks.}
Against the coordinate null sampled with seed $42$, the observed shape had $1$ exact match in $N=2000$ trials, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape probability was therefore $p=0.0005$.  A looser Jaccard threshold also remained selective: $25$ samples had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, recorded no exact shape matches, observed $M$-size $20$, and only $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size.  Its reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite-row checks support the claim that the window-six tile is not a common outcome under the sampled coordinate and amino-acid-class-preserving nulls.  They do not by themselves promote the tile to a law of translation or to a structural model of a protein.
