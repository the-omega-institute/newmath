\subsection{Window-Six Spectrum of the Standard-Code Boundary Tile}
\label{sec:h0-spectrum-window-six-standard-code-boundary-tile}

\paragraph{Claim.}
This record concerns the claim \texttt{h0.spectrum}.  The object under inspection is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  Each codon is treated as a point of the codon cube $Q_6$, while the observed amino-acid or stop assignment is supplied by the curated standard-code table.  The construction therefore has two different ingredients: the coordinate geometry of the window-six cube, and the empirical label table used for readback at the code layer.  The claim is not that the cube geometry produces translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal biological law.  The claim is only that the finite standard-code labels expose a sharply delimited local boundary tile whose spectral data can be measured.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also recorded as a punctured-cube tile with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object being measured is not an arbitrary subset of the $64$ codons: it is a thirteen-codon local face-and-corner pattern with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces in the recorded cubical summary.

\paragraph{Spectral measurement.}
The spectral run \texttt{1026324eff41} reports three central scalar quantities.  The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial check evaluates to
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is numerically zero at the stated tolerance scale.  The same value is recorded for the cubic-polynomial residual, and the spectral-radius field repeats $0.6752479308830213$.  The finite-row interpretation is therefore that the measured radius of the $M$-side tile is about $0.67525$, the measured radius attached to $R$ is about $0.47584$, and the auxiliary scale parameter is about $3.05149$, with the advertised cubic relation satisfied to roughly twelve decimal places.

\paragraph{Shape rarity.}
The tile shape was compared against $2000$ null samples in run \texttt{0b267c88f8e4}.  The generator required $57377$ distinct sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite sampling space was visibly constrained by the imposed conditions.  Among the $2000$ accepted samples there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under this null row, the exact cubical shape and the near-shape neighborhood are both rare in the recorded Monte Carlo table.  A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null, run \texttt{e8d5de747a0c}, evaluated another $2000$ samples.  It observed $0$ exact shape matches, an observed $M$ size of $20$, and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below that size.  The corresponding entries are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a code-layer statement about a finite label pattern, not a statement about biochemical inevitability.

\paragraph{Scope.}
The curated standard-code table supplies only codon-to-label contact: it tells us which codons read as amino-acid labels or stops in the standard code.  It does not observe ribosomal realization, kinetic dynamics, folded structure, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently, the spectrum, the punctured-cube row, the shape rarity values, and the leave-one recovery rates may be used only for the code-read layer.  Any move from this finite codon-label pattern to translation, structure, physical viability, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate contact that directly measures the higher layer.
