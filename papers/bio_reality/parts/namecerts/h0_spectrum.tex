\subsection{Window-six spectrum for the standard-code label table}\label{sec:h0-spectrum-window-six}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and reading convention.}
The finite object considered here is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with the curated standard genetic-code label table.  Each codon is read as a six-bit window coordinate after choosing a binary display for the three nucleotide positions, while the amino-acid or stop assignment is treated as a supplied label contact rather than as a consequence of the coordinate system.  The computation isolates a distinguished boundary row
$R=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.
The verified cardinality is $|R|=13$.  As a cubical subcomplex inside the window-six display, this row has verified face vector $(13,16,4)$: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-faces, and four two-faces.  Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it is a finite punctured-cube tile with a small two-dimensional face skeleton inside $Q_6$.

The content of the claim is therefore a finite spectral pattern attached to the local row $R$, not a biological law about translation or protein realization.

\paragraph{Shape significance against unconstrained shuffles.}
The row shape was compared with $N=2000$ distinct sampled alternatives.  Obtaining those alternatives required $57377$ sampling attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space had substantial collision pressure under the imposed finite constraints.  In that sample, exactly one alternative matched the observed shape exactly, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  Twenty-five alternatives reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  These values support the claim that the observed local tile is uncommon among the sampled coordinate-respecting alternatives.  They do not by themselves identify a mechanism, a physical energy principle, or a universal rule for genetic codes.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}

The evidence still lives at the code-label layer, because the only contact used here is the curated codon-to-label table.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The verified finite facts support the following bounded statement.  In the six-coordinate codon display, the curated standard-code labels expose a distinguished thirteen-codon row with punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral radius $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, and low sampled null frequencies under both unconstrained and amino-acid-class preserving comparisons. The result is therefore a code-read phenomenon: a local boundary tile can be read from the codon table and tested against finite null models.
