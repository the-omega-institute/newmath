\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact, not as a mechanism of translation. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The BEDC distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class. The internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation, with readback only to the standard-code label table. The finite row under audit is therefore a code-layer row: it can test local codon-tile label split, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or structural order.
