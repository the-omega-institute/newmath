\subsection{Window-six spectral record for the standard-code tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The record begins with the standard genetic-code table only as a codon-to-label contact: each of the $64$ codons is read as a three-letter nucleotide word and is assigned its curated amino-acid or stop label. The coordinate carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, used here as a finite display for local code-layer geometry. The claim examined in this section is not that the cube is a biochemical engine. It is the narrower statement that the standard-code labels determine a distinguished local tile whose boundary, closure, and spectral profile can be measured without promoting that measurement beyond code readback.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The finite boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured cube with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed row has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces in the local cube representation. This is a code-layer statement about a finite label split: it says which codons sit in the selected row and how that row is shaped as a small cube complex.

\paragraph{Shape significance rows.}
The finite randomization row for the observed shape used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null row preserving amino-acid class also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It recorded observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ at or below the observed $M$ size. The corresponding finite probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support a small finite-sample separation between the observed local shape and the tested label-preserving alternatives. They do not by themselves identify a physical cause.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact used here is the curated standard-code table. It supports codon-label readback and local split checks. It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of code origin, or a universal law for organisms. Amino-acid label sequences are not protein structures, and a local codon tile is not a claim about folding, activity, fitness, or cellular implementation. Any assertion crossing those boundaries would require a separate contact of the relevant kind, such as an independent translation assay, structural measurement, physical model, or functional experiment. In the present row, the scientific content is therefore confined to the finite standard-code label table, the thirteen-codon boundary tile, its punctured-cube shape, the reported spectral values, and the finite leave-out and null-model tests.
