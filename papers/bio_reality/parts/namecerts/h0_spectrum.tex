\subsection{Window-six spectral row for the standard-code table}\label{sec:bioreality-codon-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The claim recorded here is the finite-row claim \texttt{h0.spectrum}.  Its carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a window-six display of the $64$ standard-code codons.  The only external contact used in this row is the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label.  This contact supports code-layer readback and local label-split tests.  It does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Consequently the row is a code-read spectrum claim, not a biological-law claim.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified finite row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row verifies that this set is the punctured-cube boundary pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the boundary object is not inferred from a verbal analogy: it is a finite subcomplex with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional faces, and $4$ two-dimensional faces inside the codon-cube display.

\paragraph{Shape significance checks.}
The shape row was compared against $N=2000$ finite samples with seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap event with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the statement that the observed local tile is not a routine output of the tested coordinate null model.  They do not identify a mechanism.  They only measure rarity of the finite codon-label shape under the stated sampling regime.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, it produced $0$ exact shape matches.  The observed $M$ size is $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below that size is $7$.  The corresponding finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-label split remains unusually constrained even when the null is allowed to preserve amino-acid class.  This is still a code-layer statement: the data row records codon labels and local set geometry, not protein realization.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite-row verdict is that the standard-code label table contains a reproducible window-six local tile with a $13$-codon boundary, a punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral radius $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, and low null-model frequencies for the exact and high-overlap shape tests.  The claim remains deliberately bounded.  No translation process, no folded protein structure, no physical admissibility condition, no biological function, no mechanism, and no universal biological law follows from this row unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
