\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This row treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact, not as a mechanism of translation.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the ordinary four-letter codon display.  The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The external contact is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label and thereby supports codon-label readback.  This contact is deliberately narrow.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA is forced to obey the window-six geometry as a biological law.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}

$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ rows with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that null, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and only $7$ samples fell in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The recorded probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the finite local shape is not a common artifact of the tested label-preserving null rows.  They do not promote the result beyond the code-read layer.
