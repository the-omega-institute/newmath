\subsection{Window-Six Spectral Reading of the Standard Code}
\label{sec:h0-spectrum-window-six-standard-code}

\paragraph{Claim under test.}
The record identified by \texttt{h0.spectrum} treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer object, not as a mechanism of translation or as a statement about protein structure. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the usual codon display and then compared with the curated standard-code label table. The finite distinctions used here are the coordinate presentation of codons, local tile faces in the cube, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The numerical claim is that a distinguished codon boundary subset has a reproducible local geometry and a stable spectral signature when measured inside this coordinate presentation.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The finite boundary row $R$ contains exactly thirteen codons. The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\\
&\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$. The same row is also verified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of labels: it has a measured local combinatorial shape inside the six-coordinate codon cube. This observation remains at the code-read layer. It says that these codons occupy a particular coordinate boundary configuration; it does not say that the configuration is a biological law or a physical cause.

\paragraph{Shape against random code-label controls.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. Among those controls, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$. The corresponding finite frequencies are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second control that preserves amino-acid class reports observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ out of $2000$. Its recorded finite probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows say that the observed coordinate shape is uncommon under the two recorded null procedures. They do not promote the observation to a statement about translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, function, or universality. The null rows are evidence about the finite codon-label table under specified shuffles.

This supports a code-read statement: the local coordinate split is informative for label recovery in the recorded table family. It is not evidence that the six-bit coordinate geometry realizes translation or explains organismal machinery.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table. That contact supplies codon-to-label assignments: codons are read as amino-acid labels or stop labels. It is adequate for testing code-layer label splits and local boundary patterns. It is not a structure experiment, a translation assay, a physical-admissibility measurement, a functional screen, or a universality proof. Therefore the chapter reads the standard-code table as a finite label bridge and keeps every conclusion inside that scope.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The record is ready at the finite code-read level. The thirteen-codon row $R$ is verified, its punctured-cube face vector is $(13,16,4)$, its spectral scalars are numerically stable, the shape is rare under the recorded controls, and the leave-one rows show strong recovery within the curated label setting. The scientific content is a local coordinate-and-label phenomenon in the standard-code table. No conclusion about translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universal law follows from these data unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
