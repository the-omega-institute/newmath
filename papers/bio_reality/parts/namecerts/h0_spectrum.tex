\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum}\label{subsec:h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
This BioReality record works on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the finite display of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is not a claim about translation machinery.  It is a finite coordinate device with four locally visible kinds of distinction: the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code label table, which assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  Thus the readback layer is $code\_read$: codons may be compared with curated labels, but no statement about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this contact alone.

\begin{aligned}

The reported module audit has $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.

They do not promote the tile into a biological law.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The result is a local codon-window certificate, not a global biological theorem.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label contact only.  It does not supply ribosome dynamics, tRNA realization, chemical admissibility, folding information, structural order, phenotype, fitness, or biological function.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure.  A separate higher-layer contact would be required before any claim about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality could be stated.  Within that boundary, the finite row supports the narrower conclusion: the standard-code label table exposes a window-six boundary tile with a $13$-codon punctured-cube row, a coherent module partition, significant sampled shape statistics, and a stable local spectral signature.
