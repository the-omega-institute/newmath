\subsection{Window-Six Spectral Boundary for the Standard Code}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display rather than as a biochemical mechanism.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label, and this contact has resolution only at codon-label readback.  The BEDC-side distinctions are the six-bit coordinate display, the local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  Thus the object tested in this row is not translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global law of DNA.  It is only the finite code-layer question whether a window-six coordinate split exposes a local boundary tile in the standard-code table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same row, with finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile asserted by the row: thirteen codon labels sit on a punctured cube with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces.  The row is therefore a finite coordinate-and-relation statement about the standard-code label table, not a statement about the molecular realization of translation.

\paragraph{Spectral readout.}
The spectrum attached to the local split has three recorded scalars.  The $M$-side radius is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the $R$-side value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial parameter is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mu=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The polynomial residual is numerically zero at the recorded tolerance scale:
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},\\
P_{\mathrm{rad}}(\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row interpretation is that the codon boundary tile carries a stable spectral signature at the code-read layer.  The number $0.6752479308830213$ is the recorded spectral radius in tolerance; no higher biological layer is inferred from that scalar.

\paragraph{Shape significance against unrestricted samples.}
For the unrestricted shape comparison the run used $N=2000$ sampled rows, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape occurred once, so the exact-shape frequency was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
At Jaccard threshold $0.9$, there were $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the claim that the observed local tile is not a common finite-row accident under this sampling procedure.  They do not identify a physical cause, a selective mechanism, or a law of genetic coding.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null row.}
A stricter null model preserving amino-acid class used $N=2000$ rows.  In that model the exact match count was $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed $M$-size was $20$.  The lower-tail count at or below that observed size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the tile remains unusual even when the null sampling respects amino-acid class.  The row still remains a code-read observation: the curated code table supplies labels, while no folding, energetic admissibility, or functional assay is present.

\paragraph{Predictive stress tests at the code layer.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The recorded hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These tests are internal stress tests of label recovery under deletion; they do not create evidence for translation dynamics or protein structure.

\paragraph{Family and module decomposition.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out row used $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  The module classification covers all $13$ codons of $R$ with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The finite module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The assigned count is $13$, equal to the recorded $R$-size, and every module has either predictable or boundary status.  This gives a finite decomposition of the boundary row into named codon modules, again only at the level of the curated label table.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The ready claim is the following finite statement: in the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, the standard-code label table exposes a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary row whose spectrum has $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, with near-zero polynomial residual $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$.  The shape is rare in the sampled null rows, with $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, $p_{J\geq0.9}=0.0125$, and amino-acid-class-preserving lower-tail value $0.0035$.  The deletion tests and module classification keep the same phenomenon inside the code-read layer.  Nothing in this row licenses a claim about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Crossing any one of those boundaries requires a separate contact with its own observed facts.
