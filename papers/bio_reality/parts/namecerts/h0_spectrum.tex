\paragraph{Carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read only through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label.  The contact is therefore a code-read contact: it tests a codon-label table and a local label split, not translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The local object isolated by the row data is the boundary set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row certificate gives $|R|=13$, and the same set is reported by the punctured-cube check.  Its finite face data have $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the observed boundary tile is not merely a label list: it is a finite row complex with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-faces in the selected window-six representation.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
The unrestricted finite null run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that run there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null used the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ at or below the observed $M$-size.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite nulls support a shape-specific code-read anomaly in the curated table.  They do not say that the codon system is forced by window-six geometry; they only say that the observed local tile and its boundary geometry are rare under the sampled row-preserving comparisons used here.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The correct reading is narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and the finite rows attach spectral, shape-significance, leave-one-out, and module-classification witnesses to that structure.  The contact does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law of biology.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer readback.  Any promotion beyond the code-read layer requires a separate contact that actually measures the higher layer.
