\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the finite display of the $64$ RNA codons.  A codon is not treated here as a molecule in translation, but only as a coordinate row whose externally supplied label is amino-acid or stop readback from the curated standard-code table.  The finite row singled out by the boundary computation is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus $|R|=13$.  The same finite row is reported as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile datum used in the chapter: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional faces, and four two-dimensional faces inside the window-six codon display.

No biological necessity is inferred from the eigenvalue row.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under finite nulls.}
The unconstrained shape null used $N=2000$ distinct samples after $57377$ attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, with seed $42$.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size $20$.  The corresponding finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrower statement that the local codon tile is atypical relative to the sampled coordinate nulls.  They do not identify a biochemical mechanism, a translation law, a folding constraint, or a universal principle of genetic-code formation.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the chapter claim is exactly local: window-six coordinates expose a finite spectral boundary tile whose row values are $|R|=13$, $(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4)$, $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, with finite null and deletion tests supporting nontrivial code-layer readback.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
