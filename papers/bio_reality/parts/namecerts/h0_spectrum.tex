\paragraph{Finite object and observed row.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read object on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  A codon is treated here only as a six-bit display position carrying a curated amino-acid or stop label.  The relevant finite row is the boundary set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified row count is $|R|=13$.  The same computation identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube-shaped local set with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the chapter studies a concrete thirteen-codon boundary tile, not an unrestricted law about DNA, translation, or proteins.

The meaning is deliberately finite: the standard-code labels determine a measured local tile, and the tile has a stable spectral coordinate. \paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and logged $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the sampled alternatives there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ alternatives with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite observation is therefore sharper than a generic codon-label partition under this null experiment: the exact tile is rare in the sampled row, and near-matches are also limited.  A second null model preserving amino-acid classes evaluated $N=2000$ alternatives with seed $42$.  In that model the observed module-size statistic was $20$, the exact shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  The corresponding recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a code-layer distinction between the observed table and the tested randomized rows.

\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.}
The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label readback.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded structure, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, or a universal biological mechanism.  The spectrum and punctured-cube rows may therefore be stated only as a local code-read claim: window-six coordinates expose a thirteen-codon boundary tile whose spectrum, randomization behavior, recovery tests, and module partition are numerically recorded.  Any claim about actual translation, protein folding, physical realization, biological function, or universality would require an additional contact that measures that layer directly.  Amino-acid label sequences are still labels in this record; they are not folded protein structures.
