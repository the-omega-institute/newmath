\subsection{Window-Six Spectral Boundary for the Standard Code}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Claim.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display. The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label. The mathematical object tested here is not translation itself, not a folded molecule, and not a physical pathway. It is the finite code-layer pattern obtained when the codon cube is read as local coordinate data and then compared with the curated labels. The claim is that the window-six representation exposes a nonrandom local boundary tile, with a distinguished thirteen-codon region $R$, a complementary label-sensitive region of size $20$, and a spectral signature separating the observed table from randomized label-preserving alternatives.

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, whose vertices are six-bit displays of the $64$ codons. The contact supplies only label readback for the standard code. The distinguished boundary set has cardinality
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the observed members are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\\
&\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row verifies the punctured-cube combinatorics of this set, with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local tile is not being inferred from a continuum model or from protein outcomes; it is a finite subset of codon labels inside the six-coordinate cube.

\paragraph{Spectral measurement.}
The spectrum row records three scalar values attached to the observed split:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial equation check gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)=-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
reported both as the cubic-polynomial residual and as the radial-polynomial residual. This is within numerical tolerance for the finite row and is the reason the record is classed as a spectral boundary observation rather than only a set-cardinality observation. The value reported as the spectral radius in tolerance is again
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.6752479308830213.
\end{aligned}
$$

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
A randomized comparison with $N=2000$ distinct samples found one exact shape match. The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact-shape probability estimate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ produced $25$ matches, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed label-sensitive region has size $20$. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, another $N=2000$ sample run found $0$ exact matches, while $7$ samples fell in the lower tail at or below the observed size. The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the finite-code claim that the observed local shape is atypical under the tested nulls. They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-out behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out evaluation used $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The family-level split covered $5$ families: alternative yeast scored $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each scored $1.0$ above baseline. The leave-one-codon-out row found $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are fully assigned by the module classification: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and there are no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The listed modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has a predictable or boundary placement in the finite table. This closes the row-level accounting for the local tile: all thirteen listed codons are used, and no codon outside the module list is silently imported into the claim.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The result is a code-read observation about finite codon labels. It does not state that the cell implements the six-coordinate geometry as a law of nature. It does not establish translation realization, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a causal mechanism, or universality. Any such statement would require a separate contact that directly observes the higher layer. In this record the contact is only the curated standard-code table, so amino-acid label readback remains at the label layer and is not promoted to protein structure or biological function.
