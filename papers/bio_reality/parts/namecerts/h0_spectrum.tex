\subsection{Window-six spectral row for the standard-code codon table}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object and layer.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer. The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, used only as a coordinate display for the $64$ codons. The observed contact is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label. The construction therefore tests label readback and local codon-tile separation; it does not observe translation as a molecular process, folded protein geometry, energetic admissibility, organismal function, a mechanism, or a universal biological law. Any such promotion would require an additional contact whose rows actually measure that higher layer.

\paragraph{Finite boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube tile with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the local object is not merely a set of codons: it also carries a finite face count, with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the chosen display. The singleton-corner and three-point label-class distinctions are read at this level only as finite code-label features of the standard table.

They should not be interpreted as a physical spectrum of a molecule or a dynamical spectrum of translation; the rows only describe the coordinate-and-label construction supplied here.

\paragraph{Shape comparison under randomization.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. Exactly one sampled alternative matched the observed shape, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. There were $25$ alternatives with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$. The corresponding entries are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support the narrower claim that the observed local arrangement is atypical for the tested finite null ensembles. They do not identify a cause, evolutionary pathway, or biochemical necessity.

The hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$; and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. These finite rows say that the local coordinate construction has strong code-label recovery behavior in the recorded tests. They still remain label-level tests: the recovered item is a codon-table label, not a fold, activity, viability condition, or translational implementation.

\paragraph{Scope statement.}
The supported conclusion is that window-six codon coordinates expose a reproducible local boundary-tile pattern in the curated standard-code label table, with $R$ of size $13$, punctured-cube face data $(13,16,4)$, spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, together with finite randomization and held-out readback checks. The contact supplies codon-to-label rows only. Consequently, no claim is made here about actual translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Those claims remain blocked until a separate observation source with the appropriate resolution is attached.
