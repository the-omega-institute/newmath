\paragraph{Finite carrier and external readback.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biological mechanism; it is the finite display on which codons are read as six coordinate bits and then compared with a curated standard-code label table.  The reality contact used here is exactly the codon-to-label assignment table: it supplies amino-acid and stop labels, but it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or universality.  The finite distinguished boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}

The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube face count gives the finite $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the row is not being described as an asymptotic or schematic object: it is a concrete thirteen-vertex, sixteen-edge, four-face tile inside the displayed codon cube.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The unconditioned finite null comparison used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only one sampled row matched the exact observed shape, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while $25$ sampled rows reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second null preserving amino-acid class reported observed $M$-size $20$ over $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$: it had exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$.  The finite row list for $M$ is not supplied in this record, so the statement uses only the reported $M$-cardinality and null-model counts.  These values support the narrow statement that the observed local window-six shape is uncommon under the stated finite null models.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is that window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically non-generic local boundary-tile spectrum whose readback is aligned with the curated standard-code label table.  The inadmissible conclusion is any promotion beyond that layer.  No translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this chapter unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer fact.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation, not evidence of a folded structure or functional protein.
