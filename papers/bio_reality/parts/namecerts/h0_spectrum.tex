\subsection{Window-six spectral boundary for the standard-code table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The record uses the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ as its finite carrier.  Each codon is read as a coordinate in the window-six display, and the only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code label table: codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact is a code-read contact.  It is not a translation experiment, not a structural assay, not a physical-admissibility test, and not a function assay.  The chapter therefore studies a finite local tile in the codon-label table, not a law of biology.

\paragraph{Distinguished boundary row.}
The verified boundary row $R$ has exactly thirteen codons.  In lexicographic codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
   &UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run identifies $R$ with a punctured-cube tile and reports the finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object being tested is a local codon-coordinate shape with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces.  The singleton-corner and three-point-label-class distinctions are read only through the curated code labels; the geometry alone does not produce biological labels.

\paragraph{Spectral certificate.}
The spectrum probe attaches three real finite-row scalars to the coordinate tile.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, and the separately recorded polynomial residual is the same number.  The spectral-radius entry is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$ at the recorded tolerance.  These numbers support a finite spectral description of the local window-six tile.  They do not by themselves identify a mechanism for translation or an energetic constraint on molecular realization.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The shape-significance run sampled $N=2000$ distinct comparison rows with seed $42$.  Producing the distinct sample required $57377$ attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space is finite enough that collisions are frequent under the sampling procedure.  The exact shape was matched once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
At Jaccard threshold $0.9$, there were $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model used the same sample count $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It found zero exact-shape matches, so the recorded exact-shape probability is $0.0$.  Its lower-tail count at the observed $M$ size was $7$, with observed $M$ size $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
No explicit codon list for $M$ is part of this finite row, so only the recorded size and null statistics are asserted here.

\paragraph{Readback stability under withheld code tables.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The recorded hit rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  These values say that the finite tile is informative for code-layer label readback under this withholding protocol.  They do not say that the codon table is forced by window-six geometry.

\paragraph{Readback stability under withheld codons.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases.  The recovery rate is $1.0$, and the recovery rate above threshold is $1.0$.  The module classification covers all thirteen codons of $R$ with no extras, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
AGR&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\

Every module has either predictable support or boundary support in the recorded classification.  The assignment count is $13$, equal to $|R|$, and the module audit records $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite-row evidence supports the following local statement: in the six-bit codon cube, the curated standard-code labels expose a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary tile with a stable spectral certificate and strong finite null-model separation.  The evidence is real at the code-read layer because it uses an external curated code table as its contact.  The same evidence blocks promotion beyond that layer.  No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this chapter unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
