\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum and Local Tile Boundary}
\label{sec:bior:h0-spectrum-window-six-codon-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact discipline.}
The carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the finite display of the standard-code codon table.  The coordinate layer contains the ordinary codon rows after binary expansion of the three nucleotide positions, while the reality contact is only the curated codon-to-label table.  Thus the contact can test code-layer readback and a local codon tile label split, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The chapter therefore treats the spectral data below as a finite-row code-read invariant: it is evidence for a local boundary tile inside $Q_6$, not evidence that biological DNA is forced by this geometry as a global law.

\paragraph{Observed boundary rows.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The recorded rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same list is certified by the punctured-cube row, where the finite face count has $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  In words, the local object has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the observed punctured-cube pattern.  This is the concrete tile whose labels are read back through the curated standard-code table.  The record also reports a module decomposition that accounts for all thirteen boundary codons: $\{AGA,AGG\}$ in the $agr$ module, $\{AAA\}$ in the $ar\_lys$ module, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ in the $cun$ module, $\{AUA\}$ in the $ile\_met$ module, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ in the $ssn$ module, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ in the $stop\_trp$ module.  The classification row reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.

No higher-layer biological claim is included in that inference.

\paragraph{Shape tests against shuffled code labels.}
The first null test sampled $N=2000$ distinct standard-code-shaped trials with seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, hence $p\_\mathrm{exact}=0.0005$, and found $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, hence $p\_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  These values support the claim that the observed window-six tile is not a common outcome under that finite randomization protocol.  A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact shape matches, giving $p=0.0$ at the recorded resolution.  In the same run the observed $M$ size was $20$, the lower-tail count at or below that size was $7$, and the lower-tail value was $0.0035$.  The data therefore separate the observed code-read shape from both the unconstrained shape null and the amino-acid-class-preserving null, while remaining inside finite-row codon-label evidence.

\paragraph{Leave-out probes.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the five reported classes had hit rates above baseline equal to $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports $anchor\_count=0$, $boundary\_count=5$, $closure\_coverage\_high=8$, $predictable\_count=8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows are read as finite predictive stress tests for the local code-read tile: removed code rows and removed codons are usually recovered by the observed closure-plus-boundary structure, but the recovery is still a statement about label readback, not about the molecular production or action of proteins.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The verified statement is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local spectral boundary tile in the curated standard-code label table.  The admissible conclusion is a code-layer conclusion: $Q_6$ carries a finite local pattern whose boundary set has thirteen rows, whose punctured-cube face vector is $(13,16,4)$, whose module classification is complete, whose spectral residual is approximately zero, and whose null-model and leave-out rows show nontrivial finite-row stability.  The inadmissible promotion is equally part of the record.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any universality theorem.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any later claim about translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality must bring a separate reality contact that directly observes that higher layer.
