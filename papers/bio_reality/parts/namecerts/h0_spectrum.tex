\subsection{Window-six spectrum for the standard-code readback tile}
\label{sec:h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-bit codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The only external contact used in this chapter is the curated standard-code label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label by that table, and the table is treated only as a code-layer readback bridge. The internal BEDC structure considered here is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data on the finite codon display. Thus the chapter tests whether the window-six coordinates expose a stable local boundary tile around standard-code labels; it does not treat the table as a mechanism for translation or as a biological law.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified codons in $R$ are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is verified as a punctured cube with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a local combinatorial statement about the code-read tile. It says that the displayed row has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the punctured-cube representation. It does not say that these codons are physically preferred, structurally realized, functionally selected, or universal across biological mechanisms.

\paragraph{Spectral certificate.}
The spectral row attached to this tile reports three finite scalars:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the cubic polynomial residual is the same finite tolerance value. The reported spectral-radius-in-tolerance value is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$ at the recorded precision. The spectrum therefore supports a finite-row numerical certificate: the local $M$-row and the boundary row $R$ sit in a reproducible spectral relation whose polynomial residual is essentially zero at the stated floating precision. This is evidence for a coordinate-level regularity in the code-read layer, not evidence for a biochemical realization layer.

\paragraph{Shape and null-model pressure.}
The shape-significance row used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact shape was matched once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was matched $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information. In that row the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$ out of $2000$. Its reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows put pressure against treating the tile as a generic artifact of random relabeling. They do not by themselves identify a causal origin. The only justified readback is that the standard-code label table, when placed on the six-bit codon display, gives a locally unusual boundary-tile pattern under the recorded finite probes.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback tests.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The recorded hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The leave-one-codon-out row reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These numbers support the narrow claim that the local tile has predictive readback behavior for omitted code labels and codons inside this finite coordinate system. The result remains a code-read result: it recovers labels in a curated codon table, not translation events, folded proteins, physical admissibility, or biological function.

\paragraph{Family and module decomposition.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out row contains $5$ families. The hit-rate-above-baseline values are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. The module-classification row assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ with no extras and no omissions. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus every recorded module has a predictable-or-boundary explanation in the finite row, and the assigned count equals the $R$ size. This completes the local module accounting for the boundary row.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The chapter therefore records a finite window-six spectrum claim for the standard-code readback tile. The positive content is local: $R$ has $13$ listed codons, punctured-cube vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, strong finite null-model pressure, and high leave-one recovery behavior. The negative content is equally local: no translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from these rows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function.
