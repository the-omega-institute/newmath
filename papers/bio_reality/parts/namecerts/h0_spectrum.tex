\subsection{Window-Six Spectrum of the Standard-Code Boundary Tile}\label{subsec:h0-spectrum-window-six}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite labeled display on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  The relevant data are not a claim about biochemical necessity; they are a finite code-layer arrangement obtained by placing curated codon labels on the cube and reading local boundary behavior from that labeled array.  The carrier is the six-bit codon coordinate system, the local object is a tile face with a singleton label corner and a three-point label class, and the readout is restricted to codon-to-label assignments in the standard genetic code.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified finite table it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube configuration with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite combinatorial shape used by the spectrum calculation.  It says that the selected codons form a local cube-derived boundary pattern.  It does not say that this pattern is a global biological law, a translation mechanism, or a structural rule for proteins.

\paragraph{Spectral measurement.}
The spectral run attached to this row reports

\begin{aligned}

\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the same value is recorded as the cubic residual for the tested relation.  The reported spectral radius in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$.  Thus the finite computation supplies a stable numerical signature for the local standard-code boundary tile: the $M$-side and $R$-side radii are separated, and the radial relation is satisfied to numerical tolerance in the recorded run.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled trials with seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and it found $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid class used $N=2000$ with the same seed.  It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape value $0.0$, and lower-tail value $0.0035$.  These figures support the claim that the observed local shape is not a routine outcome of the tested finite shuffles.  They still remain code-layer evidence: they compare labeled codon arrangements, not folding, translation dynamics, or biological function.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, understood as a source of codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels.  That contact can test code-layer readback and the local split of codon labels on the six-coordinate display.  It cannot test translation realization, protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently, an amino-acid label sequence obtained from this table is still a label sequence; it is not an observed folded protein, not a functional assay, and not a physical admissibility certificate.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded statement.  In the standard-code labeling of $Q_6$, the row $R$ of $13$ codons forms a punctured-cube boundary tile with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, has a stable spectral signature with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, and remains strongly recovered in the reported leave-one-code and leave-one-codon tests.  The numerical and combinatorial record is therefore ready as a code-read observation.  No claim about translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this chapter unless an independent contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
