\subsection{Window-six spectrum of the standard-code boundary tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record fixes a finite six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$ and tests it only at the code-read layer.  The observed labels are supplied by a curated standard genetic-code table: each codon is read as an amino-acid label or a stop label, and no kinetic, structural, functional, or evolutionary mechanism is imported from that table.  The data row singled out by the local construction is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit reports $|R|=13$ and identifies this same set as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the chapter concerns a local boundary configuration inside the codon-coordinate display, not a global rule of molecular biology.  The label readback is real-data contact at the standard-code table, while the geometry is a finite organizing coordinate system used to ask which codons land on the same local boundary tile.

\paragraph{Spectral measurement.}
The spectral row gives three numerical parameters.  The measured values are

\begin{aligned}

\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run records the spectral-radius value in tolerance as $0.6752479308830213$.  The radial cubic residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)=-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the stored cubic-polynomial residual is the identical number.  At the precision of the finite computation, this means that the local tile is not merely a manually listed codon subset: it also satisfies a stable three-parameter spectral relation.  The relation is used here as a numerical certificate for the finite row, not as evidence that the codon table is generated by that relation in nature.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under label-preserving shuffles.}
A randomization check sampled $N=2000$ finite alternatives with seed $42$.  In the unrestricted shape comparison, exactly one sampled object reproduced the exact observed shape, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap comparison found $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler attempted $57377$ distinct draws and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries before obtaining the reported $2000$ alternatives.  A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, produced no exact shape match.  Its lower-tail count at or below the observed size $|M|=20$ was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite values support the claim that the observed coordinate shape is unusual among the tested label-respecting alternatives.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.

The stored comparison above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$.  Broken down by family, the five reported families had hit rates above baseline

\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\

The leave-one-codon-out row reports $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, $0$ anchor cases, and high closure coverage $8$, with recovery rate $1.0$.  These figures say that the finite local coordinate packet has strong code-label recovery behavior in the tested rows.  The result remains a code-read statement: it is about recovering codon labels and boundary membership, not about translating RNA in a cell or producing a folded protein.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification covers every member of $R$ with no extras and no omissions.  The reported classification has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, empty extra-module list, empty missing-from-module list, and empty unassigned-codon list.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary status in the finite run.  This is important because it prevents the spectral row from being an isolated scalar coincidence: the same thirteen codons are simultaneously accounted for by the punctured-cube face vector, the spectral residual, the null-model rarity checks, the recovery experiments, and the complete module partition.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and scope limit.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded conclusion.  In the six-bit codon-coordinate display, the standard-code label table exposes a thirteen-codon local boundary tile with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral triple $(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)$, rare shape occurrence under the reported null models, high recovery in leave-one tests, and complete module accounting.  This is a statement about codon-label readback from a curated table and about finite coordinate structure detected in that readback.

No stronger biological conclusion is licensed here.  The row does not show translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label sequences are not protein structures, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any claim beyond code-label readback would require a separate contact carrying the relevant biological observation.
