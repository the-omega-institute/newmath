\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with coordinates supplied by the four-letter alphabet and read back through a curated standard-code table.  The external contact used here is only the codon-label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label.  This contact is strong enough to test a local code-layer split, but it is not a translation experiment, not a folding experiment, not a physical-admissibility assay, and not a function measurement.  Consequently the chapter records a code-read claim: window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile spectrum around the standard-code labels, while the labels themselves remain external reality contacts.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The result is therefore a finite window-six statement about a local codon boundary tile and its spectrum.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label readback, and the coordinate pipeline supplies the finite tile, spectrum, module partition, and leave-out checks.  Nothing in these rows proves translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global biological law.  Any such promotion would require a separate higher-layer contact.  In the present chapter the correct reading is narrower: the standard-code labels align with a finite local tile in $Q_6$, the row $R$ has a punctured-cube shape with $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, the spectral values satisfy the recorded cubic relation to tolerance, and the observed local shape is rare under the stated finite null comparisons.
