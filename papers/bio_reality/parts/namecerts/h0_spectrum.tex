\subsection{Spectral boundary record for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the label-read layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube, with a codon displayed as a six-bit local coordinate and then read against a curated codon-to-label table.  The finite distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also recorded as a punctured-cube object with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  This is a combinatorial statement about the selected codon coordinates and their local adjacency pattern.  It is not a statement about translation dynamics, molecular realization, folded structure, chemical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectrum record assigns the local tile two numerical radii and one auxiliary scalar:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the independent cubic-polynomial residual is the same value.  At the precision of the finite computation, the spectral radius entry is therefore the same number as $\lambda_M$, namely $0.6752479308830213$.  The content of this row is modest: it says that the codon-coordinate tile has a reproducible spectral signature under the finite construction used for this packet.  The row does not by itself identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Shape comparison against coordinate-preserving randomization.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sampled row matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ samples reaching Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  These values support the narrower reading that the observed local tile is uncommon under the sampled coordinate null.  They do not license promotion from a code-label pattern to a general law of DNA or protein behavior.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class information and again used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The observed $M$ size was $20$, but this finite row supplies no member list for $M$; only its size, spectrum, and comparison statistics are part of the present data.  The exact-shape match count was $0$, giving $p=0.0$ at the recorded resolution, while the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving a lower-tail value of $0.0035$.  Thus the code-label split remains statistically sharp even when the null is constrained by amino-acid class.  This is still a code-table statement: the contact is a curated table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels, not an observation of translation in a cell and not a structural experiment.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These checks make the local row useful as a label-recovery diagnostic: when one codon or one code table entry is withheld, the finite tile often places the missing label in the predicted or boundary region.  They do not show that the coordinate geometry performs translation or encodes protein function.

\paragraph{Family and module decomposition.}
Across $5$ code families, the family-level hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  The row also decomposes all $13$ elements of $R$ into six named modules with no missing or extra codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has a predictable-or-boundary status in the finite classification.  This is the strongest local form of the packet: the spectral row, the punctured-cube row, and the recovery rows agree on the same small region of the codon table.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, at codon-label resolution.  That contact can test whether a codon-coordinate tile separates or recovers amino-acid and stop labels in the standard code.  It cannot test whether translation is realized, whether a peptide folds, whether a molecule is physically admissible, whether a function exists, whether a mechanism operates, or whether the local pattern is universal across biology.  Any such stronger claim would require a separate contact of the appropriate kind, such as a translation experiment, a structure measurement, a physical admissibility assay, or a functional readout.  The present verdict is therefore confined to the code-read layer: the window-six coordinates expose a reproducible local boundary-tile signature in the standard-code label table, with a $13$-codon punctured-cube row, spectral radius $0.6752479308830213$, exact-shape sampled value $0.0005$, amino-acid-class lower-tail value $0.0035$, and complete assignment of the $13$ boundary codons into six modules.
