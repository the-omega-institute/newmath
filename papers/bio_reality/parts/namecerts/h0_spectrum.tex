\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
The finite carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only through the standard-code codon-label table.  The coordinate distinctions are the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class.  The biological contact is therefore narrow: a curated standard genetic-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and this contact permits code-layer readback.  It does not by itself supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The finite-row shape test used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape match count was $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-Jaccard count at threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  These values say that the observed local shape is rare under the stated finite sampling protocol, both exactly and under high-overlap relaxation.  The record also includes an amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$ and seed $42$: its exact match count is $0$, its lower-tail count at the observed $M$ size is $7$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-shape $p$-value is $0.0$, and the lower-tail $p$-value is $0.0035$.  Hence the finite evidence is not merely that a visual tile can be drawn; the reported row shape remains rare after preserving amino-acid class information in the null family.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
The result is a local boundary-tile spectrum for a curated code-label table.  It may be used to say that the standard-code labels expose a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary row with the displayed spectral values, rare finite shape statistics, high leave-out recovery, and a complete six-module assignment.  It may not be used to say that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity, that the row is a global biological law, that the geometry realizes translation, that an amino-acid label sequence is a folded protein structure, that the pattern supplies physical admissibility or biological function, or that it proves a universal mechanism.  Any claim beyond code-layer readback requires a separate reality contact at the relevant layer.
