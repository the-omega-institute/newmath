\subsection{Window-Six Spectral Boundary for the Standard Code}
\label{sec:bior-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
The record for $\texttt{h0.spectrum}$ uses the six-coordinate codon cube as its finite carrier.  Each codon is read as a six-bit display state, and the observed label table is the curated standard genetic code: a codon is assigned only an amino-acid label or a stop label.  The contact is therefore a code-layer contact.  It is strong enough to test a local split of codon labels across a small tile, but it does not by itself observe translation in a cell, three-dimensional folding, physical admissibility, biochemical activity, an evolutionary mechanism, or universality across code systems.

\paragraph{The punctured row.}
The distinguished row $R$ contains exactly thirteen codons.  In the finite audit the set is
$\{\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}$.
The independent cardinality check reports $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons.  The cubical shape check identifies this row as a punctured cube with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a local combinatorial statement about the codon display and its label split.  It is not a claim that biological coding is forced by cube geometry.

\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, and the same value is reported for the radial polynomial check. Thus the finite computation supports the internal statement that the observed local row has a stable spectral summary, with a near-zero residual at numerical tolerance.  The residual should be read as a finite numerical certificate for this table row, not as a biological law.

\paragraph{Shape significance against shuffled labels.}
A direct null experiment sampled $N=2000$ distinct label arrangements, using seed $42$.  The sampler needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and rejected $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape appeared once, so the exact-shape proportion was
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold, defined by Jaccard score at least $0.9$, appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model that preserved amino-acid class reported observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ out of $2000$.  Its exact-shape row is $0.0$ at the sampled resolution, while the lower-tail row is $0.0035$.  These figures make the local tile unusual under the tested finite randomizations, but they do not identify a mechanism that produced the code.

The code-level cross-check evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The finite row reports a hit rate above baseline of $0.9841269841269841$, a hit rate inside $M-T$ of $0.9365079365079365$, and a hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary of $0.9841269841269841$.  A separate leave-one-codon row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers say that the local boundary pattern is predictive within the encoded codon-label table.  They do not say that the same row predicts protein structure or function.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact used here is the curated standard-code table, so the only observed object is codon-to-label assignment.  The finite evidence supports a local spectral and boundary-tile description of the standard-code readback.  No conclusion about translation dynamics, folded structure, thermodynamic or kinetic admissibility, biological function, causal mechanism, or universal necessity follows from these rows.  Any such stronger conclusion would require a separate contact that observes the relevant layer directly.
