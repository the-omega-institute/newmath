\subsection{Window-Six Spectrum of the Standard-Code Boundary Tile}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and observed label contact.}
The packet named \texttt{h0.spectrum} treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer observation on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The data used here are not a proposed biochemical mechanism.  They are a curated codon-to-label table together with a six-coordinate display in which local faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes can be inspected as finite combinatorial objects.  The biological contact therefore has the following narrow content: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label by the curated standard code, and the window-six coordinate system is allowed to test local split patterns in that table.

The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the finite row, it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row records that this set forms a punctured-cube-shaped local object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite code-layer phenomenon is not merely a count: the same thirteen codons occur as the named boundary set and as the vertex set of a local tile with $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the tested coordinate geometry.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral computation assigns three scalar quantities to the observed local split.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The spectral radius entry agrees with $\lambda_M$, namely
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho &= 0.6752479308830213.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row also records a cubic residual, equivalently the radial polynomial residual, of
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is numerically at zero at the precision of the run, so the spectral row should be read as a finite numerical certificate for the displayed local code geometry.  It does not say that the genome computes this spectrum, and it does not turn the coordinate construction into a universal biological principle.

\paragraph{Shape tests against null rows.}
The shape-significance row used $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those controls, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null row preserves amino-acid classes.  In that row, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the more modest statement that the displayed standard-code split is unusual under the tested finite controls.  They do not identify a causal biochemical route, because the only biological contact in this packet is the curated label table.

The module classification row assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  Its six named modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\

This module row is important because it prevents the spectral and punctured-cube claims from floating free of codon names: every codon in the thirteen-element boundary set is accounted for by one of the six listed modules.

\paragraph{Interpretation and boundary.}
The finite evidence supports a code-read statement: in the standard-code label table, the window-six coordinate display exposes a local boundary tile with a thirteen-codon punctured-cube shape, a stable spectral row, significant rarity under the two stated null procedures, and strong leave-one-out recovery in the tested rows.  The result is local and contact-limited.  It does not assert translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  Any one of those stronger claims would require a separate observation of the corresponding layer.  In particular, reading a codon as an amino-acid label is not an observation of a folded protein, and a local codon-tile split is not evidence that biological systems must obey this coordinate geometry as a global law.
