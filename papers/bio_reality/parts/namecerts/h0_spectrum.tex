\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum}
\label{sec:h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The record works on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only through the curated standard-code table as a codon-to-label contact. The carrier is not used as a biological mechanism; it is a finite display in which codons occupy six binary coordinates and the external table supplies amino-acid or stop labels. The contact therefore tests code-layer readback and local label splitting, and it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its finite membership list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the row is not merely a count: it carries thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the finite cube display. The module classification accounts for all thirteen codons with no extras and no omissions: $\{AGA,AGG\}$ in the $agr$ module, $\{AAA\}$ in $ar\_lys$, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ in $cun$, $\{AUA\}$ in $ile\_met$, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ in $ssn$, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ in $stop\_trp$.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape audit used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. Among those samples there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$. A class-preserving null run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$. Its exact-shape probability is $0.0$, while the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$. These numbers support the statement that the finite local shape is non-generic under the tested row models. They do not turn the local tile into a global law of biology.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The chapter-level conclusion is confined to the code-read layer. The six-bit codon display, the thirteen-codon punctured row, the spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, the $p$-values $0.0005$, $0.0125$, and $0.0035$, and the leave-one recovery rates jointly support a local codon-tile boundary pattern against the finite null rows supplied here. The curated standard-code table is the only reality contact used in this record. Consequently no translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity claim follows from the row unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer fact.
