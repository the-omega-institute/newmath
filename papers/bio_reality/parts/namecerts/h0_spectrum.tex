\paragraph{Finite carrier.}

It is the finite coordinate display obtained by reading each codon as a six-bit row, together with the curated standard-code label table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC-minimal form used here has four visible distinctions: the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal structures tested in this row are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation.  The readback is only the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}

It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, protein function, or a universal mechanism.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure.
