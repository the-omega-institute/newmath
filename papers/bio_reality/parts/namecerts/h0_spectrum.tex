\subsection{Window-Six Spectral Readback for the Standard Code}
\label{sec:biorality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Claim scope.}
The record studies a six-coordinate codon display for the curated standard genetic-code table.  The data layer is the assignment of each codon to an amino-acid or stop label; the geometric layer is the finite cube of codon coordinates together with local face, corner, and label-class distinctions.  The claim is deliberately narrow: the observed standard-code labels can be read through a local window-six boundary pattern, and the resulting finite subset has a measurable spectral signature.  This does not identify translation, folding, physical viability, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or a universal law.  Those stronger statements would require independent contacts that are not present in this row.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The row isolates a distinguished codon subset $R$ of size $13$.  The verified membership list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row reports that this set is a punctured-cube object with face-count vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the evidence is not merely a count: the listed codons also carry a small cubical incidence profile.  In the module classification, all $13$ codons are assigned and no codons are missing or extra.  The modules are $\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The complete accounting is $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, extra module codons $0$, missing codons $0$, and unassigned codons $0$.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral evidence is centered on the two measured radii $\lambda_M$ and $\lambda_R$, together with a radial scalar $\mu$.  The finite row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same computation records a cubic residual of $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$ and an equal radial-polynomial residual of $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  At the reported precision, the spectral radius in tolerance is again $0.6752479308830213$.  The useful reading is that the local codon subset and the larger label-supported object do not merely produce a qualitative separation; they give a reproducible numerical spectrum whose defining scalar relation is satisfied to near machine zero.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
A separate finite sampling row evaluates how often the observed shape appears under a coordinate-preserving null search.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ accepted samples, the search required $57377$ distinct sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding values are $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$ and $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  These numbers support the claim that the observed window-six shape is not a common output of the sampled comparison class.  They do not, by themselves, turn the geometry into a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving comparison.}
The amino-acid-class preserving null gives a second check against a trivial label-count explanation.  Again using seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the exact-match count is $0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, with observed $M$ size $20$.  The reported exact-shape value is $p=0.0$ at the finite resolution of the run, while the lower-tail value is $0.0035$.  This strengthens the code-layer reading: even after preserving amino-acid class information, the observed local geometry remains unusual in the sampled ensemble.

\paragraph{Recovery behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, whereas the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the local boundary organization is useful for code-label recovery tasks.  They still remain code-label tasks, not tests of protein structure or cellular implementation.

\paragraph{Contact interpretation.}
The only reality contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It is suitable for checking code readback and local label splits.  It is not an observation of ribosomal translation, protein conformation, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, or cross-biological universality.  Consequently, the chapter records a positive code-layer result and a negative promotion rule at the same time: the finite cube, the $13$-codon boundary set, the spectral values, the p-values, and the recovery rates are all admissible evidence for a local standard-code readback pattern; none of them licenses a claim about folded structure, mechanism, or function without a new and separate empirical contact.
