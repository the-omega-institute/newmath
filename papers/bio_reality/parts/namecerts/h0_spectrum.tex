\subsection{Window-six spectral boundary for the standard-code table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The carrier is not a molecule, a ribosome, a fold, or a physical dynamics. It is the finite display in which each codon has a six-bit coordinate and a curated amino-acid or stop label. The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class. The readback layer is therefore exactly the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The row singled out by the local tile calculation has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is also returned as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observation is not merely a list of exceptional codons. It has a finite cubical shape: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four local two-dimensional faces inside the codon coordinate display.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape calculation was tested against $2000$ sampled comparison rows with seed $42$. The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$. Hence the finite-row proportions are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$. Its row statistics are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a local finite-coordinate anomaly at the code-read layer. They do not by themselves supply a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, whose observed fact is only that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels. The contact can test code readback and a local codon-tile label split. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, structural order, mechanism, or universality. Consequently, the local tile may be stated as a finite code-layer boundary pattern on $Q_6$, but it must not be promoted to a global biological law. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. Any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate contact that observes that higher layer directly.
