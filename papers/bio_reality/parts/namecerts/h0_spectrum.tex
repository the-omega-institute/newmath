\subsection{Window-six spectral boundary for the standard code}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed label contact.}
The record is built on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the standard genetic-code table.  Each codon is treated only as a code-layer label position: the contact used here is the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The observed table gives enough information to test whether a local boundary tile appears in the window-six coordinates, but it does not by itself observe translation dynamics, a folded molecular structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Those higher layers require separate observations before they may be asserted.

\paragraph{Boundary row set.}
The verified boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its rows are \texttt{AAA}, \texttt{AGA}, \texttt{AGG}, \texttt{AUA}, \texttt{CUA}, \texttt{CUC}, \texttt{CUG}, \texttt{CUU}, \texttt{UAA}, \texttt{UAG}, \texttt{UCA}, \texttt{UGA}, and \texttt{UUA}.  The same computation identifies this set with a punctured-cube pattern whose face data have $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the finite observation is not merely that thirteen codons were named; it is that the thirteen named positions occupy a particular local combinatorial shape inside $Q_6$.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral computation attached to this shape gives three scalar quantities: $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported residual for the radial cubic is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual.  In finite-row form the numerical check is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,\\
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu) &= -6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The tolerance row records the spectral radius as $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$ at the precision reported by the run.  The scientific content of this row is a finite numerical constraint on the observed codon tile, not an explanation of why the biological code is realized.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Against the coordinate-preserving random comparison, the observed shape is rare in the sampled population.  With $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second comparison preserving amino-acid classes used $N=2000$ and seed $42$; it found $0$ exact shape matches, recorded observed $M$-size $20$, and counted $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size.  Its reported values were $p_{\mathrm{aa,class,exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{aa,class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These finite comparisons support the claim that the window-six tile is not a common accident under the stated row-level controls, while still remaining a code-table statement.

\paragraph{Leave-one recovery rows.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary rows was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level version grouped the same kind of signal into $5$ families: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$ above baseline, while bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each had hit rate $1.0$.  The leave-one-codon-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the local tile retains strong finite recovery behavior under omissions; they do not say that the omissions simulate an organism or a biochemical process.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen boundary codons are completely assigned by the module classification: \texttt{AGA} and \texttt{AGG} form \texttt{agr}; \texttt{AAA} forms \texttt{ar\_lys}; \texttt{CUA}, \texttt{CUC}, \texttt{CUG}, and \texttt{CUU} form \texttt{cun}; \texttt{AUA} forms \texttt{ile\_met}; \texttt{UCA} and \texttt{UUA} form \texttt{ssn}; and \texttt{UAA}, \texttt{UAG}, and \texttt{UGA} form \texttt{stop\_trp}.  The classification row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing boundary codons, and no unassigned codons.  In particular, every named module has at least one predictable or boundary member, so the finite spectrum is compatible with a complete local module partition of the observed boundary set.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The ready claim is therefore narrow.  In the standard-code codon table, the six-coordinate display exposes a thirteen-row boundary set with punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral row with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, $\mu=3.051487585298128$, and residual about $6.3\times 10^{-12}$ in magnitude, and nontrivial recovery behavior under the reported leave-one tests.  The record is evidence for a local code-read boundary tile in the curated codon-label table.  It is not evidence, by itself, for translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.
