\subsection{A spectral boundary record for the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table.  The coordinate display is therefore a finite row object: each codon is a vertex of the cube, while the biological label is supplied by an external code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The distinguished boundary row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality is $|R|=13$.  The same row is also the observed punctured-cube boundary row, with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a code-read record only.  It says that the window-six coordinate system exposes a local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class in the standard-code display.  It does not say that the codon table is generated by the geometry as a biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row invariant.}
The spectral row attached to this boundary has three reported scalar values:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The certified residual for the radial cubic relation is
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same residual is reported as the cubic-polynomial value and the radial-polynomial value.  Hence the finite computation places the spectral radius in tolerance at $0.6752479308830213$.  The point of the invariant is local: it records that the $R$ row and the supplied $M$ row have a stable spectral separation in the window-six coordinate display.  It is not a statement about translation, protein realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape significance under finite null rows.}
The shape row was tested against $N=2000$ finite null samples with seed $42$.  Because the sampler required distinct rows, it made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape matched once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap shape agreement at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed local tile is not typical under this finite null ensemble.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$ samples, again with seed $42$.  In that test the observed $M$ row had size $20$, exact matches occurred $0$ times, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the finite-row claim that the window-six display is a non-generic local code-read pattern.  They do not turn the local pattern into a global biological necessity.

The recovery statistic is still a code-layer statistic, not a translation experiment.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The $13$ codons of $R$ are partitioned into six named modules.  The stop-tryptophan module is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
while the other modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification completeness row says $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has either predictable or boundary status.  The module row therefore gives a closed finite account of the boundary tile at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table.  It supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA obeys this geometry as biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any promotion beyond code-read requires a separate higher-layer contact with its own observed rows.
