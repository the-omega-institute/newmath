\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum}
\label{sec:biorality-window-six-codon-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the $64$ standard codon displays over the four RNA letters.  The biological contact is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it is used here as a code-layer readback contact.  The contact is not a translation experiment, not a folding experiment, not a physical-admissibility test, not a function assay, not a mechanism, and not a universality proof.  The claim is therefore local: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite boundary-tile pattern whose labels are read back through the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified finite row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a count of exceptional codons: it is a finite face pattern with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the window-six coordinate display.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral calculation separates the boundary row from the complementary local mass through three scalars.  The verified values are

\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\

$$
The associated radial cubic check is numerically closed at tolerance scale: both the cubic-polynomial residual and the radial-polynomial residual are $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  The reported spectral radius in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$, agreeing with $\lambda_M$ at the displayed precision.  This is the finite-row content of the spectrum claim: the local tile has a measured spectral profile, and the code labels only tell us how that profile is read against the curated standard table.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape row was tested against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  The sampling procedure attempted $57377$ distinct draws and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null was also evaluated with $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  The observed local mass size was $20$; the finite row reports no exact matches, so the exact-shape count is $0$ and the exact-shape $p$-value is $0.0$ at the displayed Monte Carlo resolution.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed mass size is $7$, giving $p=0.0035$.  These numbers support a finite-coordinate anomaly at the code-read layer.  They do not convert the anomaly into a biochemical necessity or a universal coding mechanism.

\paragraph{Prediction rows.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the five reported families all remain high: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.

\paragraph{Codon deletion row.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The recovery rate above threshold is also $1.0$.  The module classifier accounts for all $13$ boundary codons: $\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The completion check gives $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The finite rows justify a code-read statement: in the window-six codon coordinate system, the standard-code table contains a local boundary-tile split with a $13$-codon punctured-cube row, a measured spectral profile, significant shape tests, and high leave-one-out recovery under the reported probes.  The same rows also enforce a negative boundary.  No translation realization follows from them.  No folded protein structure follows from them.  No physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from them.  Any promotion beyond code-label readback would require a separate reality contact of the appropriate kind.
