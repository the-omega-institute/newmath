\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object in this row is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, while the only biological contact used here is a curated table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  Thus the row tests a code-layer readback: it may compare codon coordinates, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes against the observed standard-code labels, but it does not observe translation events, folded structure, molecular admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal law.  Those stronger claims would require distinct contacts.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$.  To obtain distinct samples it made $57377$ attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both reported significance fields repeat these same values.  A second null model preserving amino-acid class again used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that run the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports a finite-row claim that the observed local code geometry is not typical under the two sampled null routes reported here.  It still remains a statement about label geometry, not about the biochemical realization of a protein.

The row is ready as a code-read claim.  The six-bit codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure with a $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary, a reproducible spectral triple $(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)$, significant shape behavior under the reported null samples, and strong leave-one recovery behavior.
