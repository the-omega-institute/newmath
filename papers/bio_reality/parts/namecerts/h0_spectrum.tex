\subsection{Window-six spectral boundary of the standard code}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The carrier for this BioReality record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only through the curated standard-code label table.  The BEDC-side distinctions are a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The external contact supplies codon-to-label readback only: it says that the standard table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, but it does not supply translation dynamics, molecular structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  Thus the chapter studies a finite code-layer pattern, not a global biological law.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The finite row is
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same row is observed as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  The record therefore separates a concrete finite set of codons from any stronger biological interpretation: $R$ is a code-read boundary in $Q_6$, not a theorem about how cells must translate or how proteins must fold.

The spectral calculation gives three scalar row quantities:
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.

The recorded radial polynomial value is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same number recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual.  At the stated numerical tolerance, the spectral radius is therefore represented by $0.6752479308830213$.  The point of this invariant is narrow: it is a finite-row spectral certificate attached to the codon-window construction.  It does not assert that window-six geometry causes the standard code, nor that the geometry alone realizes translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape and null comparison.}
The finite row is not treated as an arbitrary drawing after the fact.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The same run counted $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, which is important because the row lives in a small finite combinatorial space rather than in an asymptotic continuum.

The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a second finite comparison.  In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, and the exact-shape $p$-value was $0.0$ at the displayed precision.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving a lower-tail value $7/2000=0.0035$.  These numbers support a code-layer separation between the observed finite row and the class-preserving null, but they still remain finite-row evidence.  They do not promote the row into a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one contacts.}
The leave-one-code-out contact evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family, across $5$ families.

The leave-one-codon-out contact gives a codon-level complement: anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These contacts are read as local code-read robustness tests.  They are not structure experiments, not physical chemistry experiments, and not function assays.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The $13$ codons in $R$ are partitioned into named modules with no missing or extra codons.  The complete module assignment is
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The module-classification row records $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the finite classification.  This is the local tile boundary that the chapter keeps: a finite codon-window spectrum with a complete module readback.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The only admitted contact is the curated standard-code table, and its resolution is codon label readback.  Consequently the finite evidence can support local split checks, boundary-row membership, spectral scalar reporting, null-model comparison, leave-one robustness, and module classification at the code layer.  It cannot by itself support translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or the claim that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as biological necessity.  Any crossing of that boundary requires a separate higher-layer contact whose observed facts are not present in this row.
