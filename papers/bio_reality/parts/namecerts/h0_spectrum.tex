\subsection{Window-six spectral packet for standard-code label readback}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer object placed on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  The biological contact used here is only the curated table assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label.  The coordinate packet has four visible ingredients: the six-bit codon display, local tile faces inside $Q_6$, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The internal data are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data; the observable comparison is the ordinary standard-code label table.  No molecular translation event, folded structure, admissible physical path, biological function, mechanism, or universal rule is inferred from these rows.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The row $h0.R.cardinality.13$ identifies a thirteen-codon relation set.  The enumerated set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$.  The independent punctured-cube row $h0.R.punctured.cube$ gives the same list and reports the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the boundary object is not a verbally chosen motif: it is a concrete finite subset of $Q_6$ with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the recorded tile geometry.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The module-classification row partitions the thirteen listed codons without leftovers.  It reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six finite modules are

Every module has either a predictable or boundary status in the recorded leave-one-codon analysis.  This is a code-table statement: it organizes codon labels and local boundary behavior, not tRNA availability, ribosomal dynamics, protein folding, or organism-level fitness.

\paragraph{Shape significance against randomized code-layer rows.}
The shape-significance row $h0.M.shape.significance$ used $N=2000$ sampled comparators with seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{exact}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
It also found $25$ cases with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row $h0.null.model.aa_class_preserving$ separately records $N=2000$, observed $M$-size $20$, zero exact matches, and seven lower-tail cases at or below the observed $M$-size.  Its reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{class\ exact} &= 0.0,\\
p_{class\ lower\ tail} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite data supplied here include the cardinality of $M$ and the null-model counts, but not an explicit codon-by-codon enumeration of the twenty $M$ elements.  The inference therefore stays at the level supported by the row: the observed local shape is rare under the recorded code-layer randomizations.

The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ genetic-code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the row records five families: alternative yeast has rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each have rate $1.0$.  These statistics say that the finite coordinate packet has strong recovery behavior across the recorded code-table variants.  They are still comparisons among label tables, not observations of translation execution or protein structure.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The result is strongest where it is narrowest: it supports local codon-label readback and boundary checking for the standard-code table.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The curated standard-code table is the only biological contact used in this packet.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments, and the finite rows measure coordinate shape, boundary modules, null-model rarity, spectral values, and omission-test recovery against that label contact.  A stronger biological statement would require a distinct contact of the appropriate kind.  In particular, these rows do not establish translation realization, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  They certify a finite window-six code-layer pattern with the enumerated boundary set $R$, the recorded spectral scalars, rare shape statistics, and high leave-one-out recovery inside the stated table-reading scope.
