\subsection{Window-Six Spectrum of the Standard-Code Tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The record uses the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ as a finite coordinate carrier for the standard-code label table.  Each codon is read only as a coordinate together with its curated amino-acid or stop label.  The object under study is therefore a code-read object: a local tile in a finite codon cube, not an account of translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The external contact is the curated standard-code table, which supplies the codon-to-label readback and nothing above that layer.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube row with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This gives the finite tile a concrete shape: thirteen displayed vertices, sixteen local adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces after the relevant puncture.  The row is not interpreted as a biological law.  It is a finite coordinate pattern whose labels are readable because the curated code table supplies those labels.

\paragraph{Spectral certificate.}
The spectral computation attached to this tile gives three scalar witnesses.  The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check returns
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{rad}}(\mu)=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same residual recorded as the satisfied cubic-polynomial value.  Thus the finite-row spectrum is numerically closed to the displayed tolerance: the spectral radius recorded for the tile is $0.6752479308830213$, and the polynomial residual is at numerical zero for the experiment scale.  The certificate is a spectrum of the coordinate-and-label tile; it is not a claim that a ribosome, a folded protein, or a physical pathway is governed by this cubic.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the sampled null used for the coordinate shape test, $N=2000$ distinct samples were accepted after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$, recorded observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its exact-shape value was $0.0$, while the lower-tail value was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the finite statement that the observed local tile is not a common draw under the tested coordinate and amino-acid-class-preserving nulls.  They do not promote the tile into a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, five families were counted: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  The leave-one-codon-out test found $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These tests are code-read tests: they check whether finite codon-label rows recover held-out labels within the displayed coordinate regime.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The thirteen boundary codons are completely assigned to six finite modules.  The module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module-classification row has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has either predictable or boundary support in the leave-one-codon-out record.  This is the strongest local reading supported by the finite data: the boundary row decomposes into named codon modules that remain visible under the code-read probes.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact source is a curated standard genetic-code bridge.  It observes that a standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It can test the code-read layer and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Consequently, the local conclusion is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically non-generic boundary-tile spectrum for the curated standard-code label table, with $|R|=13$, face-vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral radius $0.6752479308830213$, and strong held-out label recovery.  No higher-layer biological claim follows unless a separate higher-layer contact is supplied.
