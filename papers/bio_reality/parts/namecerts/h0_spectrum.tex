\subsection{Window-Six Spectral Boundary for the Standard Code}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
The record uses the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ as its finite coordinate carrier and reads each codon through the curated standard-code label table. The observed boundary set has cardinality $13$ and is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathtt{AAA},\mathtt{AGA},\mathtt{AGG},\mathtt{AUA},\mathtt{CUA},\mathtt{CUC},\mathtt{CUG},\mathtt{CUU},\\
&\mathtt{UAA},\mathtt{UAG},\mathtt{UCA},\mathtt{UGA},\mathtt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row identifies this set as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the claim is not that the genetic code is generated by geometry alone. The claim is narrower: after the standard labels are supplied by the curated table, the window-six coordinate display exposes a small local tile with a singleton-corner and three-point label-class profile.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
For the spectral probe, the verified row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the cubic residual recorded for the same computation is also $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$. Within the finite numerical tolerance of the run, the spectral-radius value is therefore represented by $0.6752479308830213$. This is a row-level numerical certificate for the local code-read geometry: it records a stable spectral relation among the measured objects $M$, $R$, and the radial parameter $\mu$, not a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape tests.}
The shape-significance row used $N=2000$ randomized samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact shape appeared once, giving $p=0.0005$, while $25$ samples had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. The corresponding finite-row probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the local distinctiveness of the observed tile under the stated sampling procedures. They do not replace the curated code table: the codon-to-label assignment remains an empirical contact, and the coordinate computation only organizes that contact once it has been supplied.

\paragraph{Readback pressure.}
Two leave-out probes test whether the finite tile is merely descriptive or whether it has local predictive pressure at the code-read layer. Across $23$ evaluated code tables and $63$ total targets, the leave-one-code-out row reports a baseline hit rate of $0.19821739130434785$, a hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ of $0.9365079365079365$, and a hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary of $0.9841269841269841$. The reported rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$. The leave-one-codon-out row reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchors, closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These rows say that the local window-six construction has strong code-layer recovery behavior under the stated finite tests.

\paragraph{Family and module rows.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out row partitions the evaluated alternatives into $5$ families. The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial or plastid tables, $1.0$ for candidate other tables, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate tables. The module-classification row assigns all $13$ boundary codons, with no missing module members, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathtt{AGA},\mathtt{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathtt{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathtt{CUA},\mathtt{CUC},\mathtt{CUG},\mathtt{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathtt{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathtt{UCA},\mathtt{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathtt{UAA},\mathtt{UAG},\mathtt{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This module row is important because it keeps the observed spectral boundary attached to named finite codon groups rather than to a vague biological interpretation.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is confined to the code-read layer. The curated standard-code table supplies codon labels, the six-bit codon display supplies a finite coordinate carrier, and the verified rows report a local spectral and combinatorial pattern around the $13$-codon boundary set. Nothing in these rows establishes translation as a realized process, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality. Any such stronger statement would require a separate contact that directly observes that higher layer. In the present record, amino-acid label readback remains label readback, and the local tile remains a finite code-layer boundary phenomenon.
