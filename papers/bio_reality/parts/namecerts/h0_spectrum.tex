\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum and the Local Boundary Tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact rather than as a mechanism. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the usual codon alphabet display and then compared with a curated standard genetic-code table. The external contact says only that codons receive amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code. It does not say that the six-bit geometry is translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law of DNA. The local object under inspection is therefore a finite boundary tile inside $Q_6$ with label readback, not a global biological principle.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has exactly thirteen codons. In the finite row the set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus $|R|=13$. As a punctured-cube object its recorded face vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The point of this row is not that these codons are biologically forced by the cube. The point is narrower: under the window-six coordinate display, the standard-code labels expose a small local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional faces, and four two-dimensional faces. This is a coordinate-level boundary observation with an external label table attached.

\paragraph{Shape significance row.}

The high-overlap comparison at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ gives $25$ matches, hence $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and had $55377$ duplicate retries. These numbers say that the observed shape is rare in the specified finite null ensemble, not that the codon table is generated by the geometry.

The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, the list of extra module codons is empty, the list missing from modules is empty, and the unassigned-codon list is empty. Every module has either predictable or boundary status in the recorded row. This closes the finite bookkeeping for the local boundary tile: the codons named by $R$ are all accounted for by the module row, and no codon outside the row is silently inserted.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite data support the following bounded reading. The standard-code label table, when read through a six-bit codon coordinate window, exposes a rare local boundary tile with a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row, a stable spectral triple, significant finite-null separation, strong leave-one readback, and a complete six-module classification. The result is a code-read observation about labels on a finite coordinate carrier. The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: this chapter does not infer translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Any such statement would require a separate reality contact beyond the curated codon-label table.
