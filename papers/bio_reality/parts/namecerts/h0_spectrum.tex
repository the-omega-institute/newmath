\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
This BioReality record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a coordinate display for the $64$ codon positions.  The reality contact is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  This contact is enough to test a local codon-tile label split, but it is not a translation experiment, a structure experiment, a physical-admissibility experiment, or a function assay.  Thus the object studied here is a finite labelled coordinate table, not a global biological mechanism.

The relevant observation is not that biology must obey this shape.  The observation is that, after the curated standard-code labels are placed on the six-bit codon display, this thirteen-codon subset forms a concrete local boundary object whose face data can be checked directly.

\paragraph{Shape comparison against sampled alternatives.}

Because the sampling was constrained to distinct tables, the run records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those $2000$ alternatives, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding empirical probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model used the same sample size $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that restricted null, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the claim that the local row is not a typical outcome under the recorded finite-row null comparisons.  They do not identify a causal biochemical source for the row.
