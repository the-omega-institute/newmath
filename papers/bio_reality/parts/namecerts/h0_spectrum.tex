\subsection{Window-six spectral packet for the standard code}
\label{sec:h0-spectrum-window-six}

\paragraph{Claim and finite carrier.}
The claim \texttt{h0.spectrum} records a finite observation on the six-coordinate codon cube.  Each codon is treated as a point of a window-six display, and the only biological readout used here is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The carrier is therefore not a model of translation.  It is a finite coordinate table on which local label splits can be counted, compared, and spectrally summarized.  The observed boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and consists exactly of
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row reports that this set has punctured-cube face data with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the packet is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it is a small subcomplex inside the codon cube, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four local faces.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral calculation attached to this carrier gives three numerical parameters:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported residual for the radial cubic relation is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same number is recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual.  At the precision of the finite computation this places the observed spectral radius at $0.6752479308830213$.  The role of this row is narrow: it says that the codon-label tile selected by the standard code has a reproducible spectral signature inside the window-six coordinate system.  It does not say that this spectral signature is a biochemical force, a pathway, or an evolutionary rule.

\paragraph{Shape comparison against finite nulls.}
The observed local shape is rare under the supplied finite sampling tests.  In the unconstrained comparison with seed $42$, $N=2000$ null samples were generated after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sample matched the observed shape, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The relaxed comparison using Jaccard threshold $0.9$ found $25$ matches, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second null preserving amino-acid classes again used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  There the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the finite statement that the standard-code tile is not behaving like a typical sample from either supplied null.  They do not identify the causal origin of the table.

The classification row reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  This matters because the spectral row is not floating over an anonymous subset: the thirteen-point boundary decomposes into biologically named codon families at the code-read layer.  The names remain table labels, not structure observations.

\paragraph{Prediction checks around the tile.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers say that the finite tile has strong local recovery behavior under the supplied deletion probes.  They should be read as code-table recovery statistics, not as claims about ribosomal execution or organism-level function.

\paragraph{Scope of the contact.}
The only contact used by this packet is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It supports code-layer readback and local split checks.  It does not observe translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic necessity, or universality across life.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is still a label sequence.  No fold, active site, stability condition, transport process, cellular phenotype, or evolutionary inevitability follows from the present data.  Any later statement at those layers would need a separate contact whose measurements live at that layer.  The present chapter therefore records a finite spectral and combinatorial signature of the standard-code table, together with an explicit refusal to promote that signature beyond the code-read boundary.
