\subsection{Spectral Window for the Standard-Code Boundary Tile}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the finite display of the $64$ codons. The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label, and the readback is restricted to that code layer. The distinction under inspection is not a global biological law. It is a local tile-face and label-boundary pattern inside the window-six coordinate display. The contact can test codon-label readback and a local codon-tile label split; it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, function realization, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary row set.}
The distinguished boundary row set $R$ has exactly $13$ codons. In the finite row record it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row set is recorded as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed object is not merely a list of exceptional codons. It is a finite subcomplex with $13$ vertices, $16$ edges, and $4$ two-faces in the six-bit cube display. The row count is therefore both a label-table statistic and a cubical boundary statistic.

The claim is deliberately row-local: it says that the code-table readback and the six-bit coordinate closure expose a spectral boundary shape. It does not say that the shape is a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance against sampled nulls.}
The unconditioned finite null used $N=2000$ sampled tables, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$. Hence the recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$. In that stricter null, the observed $M$-row size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$. The corresponding recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-table statement: the observed codon boundary is rare under the sampled row models used here. They do not identify an evolutionary cause, a biochemical necessity, or a universal constraint on genetic codes.

The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. Leave-one-codon-out testing records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. In formula form, the two recovery summaries are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}&=1.0,\\

The by-family leave-one-code-out table has $5$ families. These checks strengthen the code-layer reading: the local boundary is not only a static subset of $Q_6$, but also a row object with strong finite recovery behavior under the supplied deletion probes.

This matters because the spectral row is not floating independently from label structure: it is carried by a complete module classification of the same $R$ rows.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary of use.}
The chapter-specific conclusion is that the standard-code table, when read through the six-bit codon cube, contains a finite local boundary tile whose row set has size $13$, punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, and rare-shape behavior under the sampled nulls described above. This is a code-read claim only. No translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from these rows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence. Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains label readback; it is not folded protein structure.
