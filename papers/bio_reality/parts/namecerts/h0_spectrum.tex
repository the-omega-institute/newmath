\subsection{Window-Six Codon Spectrum and the Local Boundary Tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-spectrum}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon coordinate record.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube obtained by displaying each codon as a finite word over the four nucleotide labels and then reading the induced local tile structure.  The observed boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite check records $|R|=13$, with exactly the same thirteen codons in the cardinality row and in the punctured-cube row.  The punctured-cube cell count is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the selected boundary is not merely a named list: it also carries a small cubical incidence profile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four square faces.  This is the local geometric object whose spectrum is being read; it is not a statement about translation dynamics or protein realization.

\paragraph{Spectral certificate.}
For the row labelled $h0.spectrum$, the numerical spectrum gives

\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The same run reports the cubic residual and the radial residual as
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},\\
P_{\mathrm{rad}}&=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
with spectral-radius tolerance recorded at $0.6752479308830213$.  At this numerical precision the residual is effectively zero relative to the displayed finite computation, so the row supports a sharply constrained local spectral pattern.  The support is still only a code-layer observation: the eigenvalue data organize the codon-label table after the codon coordinates and curated labels have been fixed.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only one sampled alternative matched the exact observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ produced $25$ matches and
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model that preserves amino-acid class reports $N=2000$, observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its corresponding values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers make the local tile non-random relative to the two finite sampling schemes recorded here.  They do not identify a biochemical cause; they say that the displayed code-read pattern is rare under the stated row-level alternatives.

\paragraph{Leave-out stability.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, five families were recorded: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, and bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had hit rate $1.0$.  The leave-one-codon-out row records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the finite prediction behavior is concentrated in the same local boundary geometry rather than in a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The module classification is complete over the thirteen-codon boundary: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing boundary codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has either a predictable or boundary status in the recorded row.  This supplies a finite decomposition of the local boundary set, with singleton behavior at $AAA$ and two- or three-point label classes elsewhere.  The codon labels used in this partition come from the curated standard-code table, which is the empirical contact for label readback.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is narrow.  Window-six coordinates, the thirteen-codon boundary set, the punctured-cube profile, the spectral residual, the sampling p-values, and the leave-out recovery rows together support a local code-read structure in the curated standard-code table.  They do not show that DNA must obey this geometry as a law of nature.  They do not establish translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality across organisms.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-table observation unless a separate contact supplies structural, physical, functional, or organism-matched translation evidence.  In particular, the codon-to-label bridge is not a fold observation, and CUN, UUR, stop, tryptophan, lysine, isoleucine, methionine, serine, arginine, or leucine wording must not be promoted into protein-structure evidence by this row alone.
