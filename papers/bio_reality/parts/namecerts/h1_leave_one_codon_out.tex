\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Codon Boundary Test}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite setting.}
The object tested here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The finite carrier is the $64$-point codon cube, with each codon treated as a point in a window-six coordinate system.  The biological contact is deliberately narrower than translation: it is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Thus the contact can check label readback and local tile separation, but it does not observe ribosomal realization, folded structure, thermodynamic admissibility, cellular function, mechanism, or universality.

The distinguished leave-one region $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
   &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row records that this region has the punctured-cube face profile
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the first constraint on the reading: the pattern is a local codon-tile profile, not a law about all biological coding systems.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The spectral row separates the region from a purely verbal description.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded as the radial polynomial residual.  At the stated numerical tolerance this places the spectral readback on the intended finite row rather than on an informal resemblance.

The shape-significance experiment used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, recorded observed $|M|=20$, zero exact matches, and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size.  The finite-row probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the displayed local geometry is not a routine outcome of the sampled null rows, while still leaving the result inside the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon rows.}
The broader leave-one-code evaluation tested $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  These figures show that the local codon-coordinate row is not isolated from the code-read experiment: the same neighborhood behavior appears when the code table is perturbed at the table level.

The specific leave-one-codon row is sharper.  It records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\ count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\ count}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row also states that the recovery rate above threshold is $1.0$.  In this test the missing codon is not recovered by an anchor count; it is recovered through predictable or boundary behavior in the local tile.  The absence of anchors is important: the row is not claiming that a single codon corner dictates biology.  It is reporting that, within the finite coordinate display and curated label table, each tested omission stays recoverable by the local boundary apparatus.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The module-classification row accounts for all $13$ codons of $R$.  It records $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite row further states that every module has predictable or boundary coverage.  Thus the leave-one-codon result is not merely an aggregate recovery score; it is distributed across the named modules covering the whole $13$-codon region.

The family-level leave-one-code row gives $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  This family stratification is compatible with a robust code-read signal, but it remains a statement about curated codon labels and finite table comparisons.  It does not turn the local tile into a cross-organism mechanism.

\paragraph{Readback boundary.}
The supported conclusion is the following.  In the six-coordinate codon display, the standard-code label table contains a $13$-codon leave-one region with punctured-cube face profile $(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral values, statistically rare sampled shape behavior, full module coverage, and perfect recorded recovery in the leave-one-codon row.  The biological observation used for this statement is only the curated codon-to-label table.

Consequently, the result may be used as a code-read claim: local window-six coordinates expose a boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table.  It may not be promoted to a claim about translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessary law of DNA.  Any one of those stronger statements would require a separate contact that observes the corresponding biological layer.  Amino-acid label readback is still label readback; it is not a structure experiment and it is not a function assay.
