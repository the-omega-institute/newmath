\paragraph{Object and observational contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table, read only as a curated assignment from codons to amino-acid or stop labels. The coordinate carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with distinctions for a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class. The relevant contact is the standard-code label table: it lets the finite construction check codon-label readback and local boundary splits, but it does not by itself observe translation chemistry, folded protein geometry, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Those stronger biological layers would require separate contacts with their own measurements.

\paragraph{Finite subset recovered by the coordinate test.}
The row $h0.R.cardinality.13$ identifies the distinguished codon set $R$ as a set of thirteen codons,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same thirteen codons appear in the punctured-cube row $h0.R.punctured.cube$, where the reported face-vector is $(13,16,4)$. Thus the finite object is not merely a list of labels: it has a small cubical incidence profile, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces inside the local codon-coordinate construction. The set is the window in which leave-one-codon recovery is tested.

For the target claim $h1.leave.one.codon.out$, the finite row reports $13$ codons under test through its surrounding module decomposition, with $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, and $0$ anchors. The high-coverage closure count is $8$, and the recovery rate is

The threshold comparison also reports $\mathrm{recovery\ rate\ above\ threshold}=1.0$. In this finite sense, every omitted codon in the tested window is either recovered by the local closure signal or placed on the boundary where the construction refuses to promote a unique internal prediction.

This is the most local form of the result: the six-bit codon geometry supplies a finite partition of the sensitive codon set into module-sized recovery or refusal zones, and the standard-code table supplies the labels against which that partition is read.

\paragraph{Boundary interpretation.}
The scientific content of the row is therefore deliberately narrow. The finite data say that, in the six-bit codon display and relative to the curated standard-code labels, the thirteen-codon set $R$ forms a punctured local cube whose leave-one-codon tests are completely covered by predictable or boundary outcomes. The construction may expose a codon-table boundary tile, but the table contact sees only code labels. It does not see ribosomal realization, kinetic accessibility, folded structure, thermodynamic stability, organismal fitness, or a universal biological mechanism. Any future promotion beyond code-layer readback must attach a distinct contact that directly measures the higher layer being claimed.
