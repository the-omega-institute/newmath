\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed contact.}
The record studies the standard genetic-code table as a finite label readout on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  The biological contact used here is only the curated assignment of each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  This makes the test a code-layer question: it may compare codon coordinates, local cube faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes against the observed table, but it does not observe ribosomal translation, folded structure, chemical admissibility, organismal function, or a universal mechanism.  Any assertion beyond codon-to-label readback would require an additional contact of the appropriate kind.

The distinguished set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is recorded explicitly as
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube computation returns the same thirteen codons and gives the finite face count
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row being tested is not an unrestricted codon table: it is a finite local shape with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional tile faces inside the window-six display.

These numbers support a finite local non-randomness claim for the displayed codon shape; they do not promote the shape to a biological law.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and limitation.}
The ready conclusion is that the window-six codon carrier exposes a finite local tile boundary in the curated standard-code table.  The evidence consists of the explicit thirteen-codon distinguished set, the punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral values $\lambda_M$, $\lambda_R$, and $\mu$, low empirical null probabilities for exact and high-Jaccard shape matches, complete module assignment, and perfect recovery among the predictable leave-one-codon cases.  The scientific scope is narrow: the contact is a codon-label table, so the result remains at code readback.  It gives no observation of translation realization, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic necessity, or universality.  Those statements remain blocked until a separate experiment or curated contact directly supplies the missing layer.
