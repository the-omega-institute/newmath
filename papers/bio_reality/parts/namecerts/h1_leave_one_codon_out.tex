\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery on the local codon tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Object and coordinate frame.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon represented by a window-six coordinate and then read against the curated standard-code label table.  The data are not treated as a model of translation dynamics.  They are used as a finite label table on which local boundary behavior can be tested after one codon is withheld.

The distinguished residual set is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The same set is observed as a punctured-cube pattern with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row is not merely a count: it records a thirteen-vertex local object with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces inside the codon-coordinate cube.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape measurements.}
The associated spectral row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check has residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, numerically indistinguishable from zero at the stated tolerance scale, and the stored spectral-radius value is again $0.6752479308830213$.  This supplies a finite numerical signature for the tile, but it does not by itself identify a biological mechanism.

A separate randomization row tested the observed shape against $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, observed $M$-size $20$, no exact-shape match, and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed size.  Its reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support the claim that the finite tile has non-random coordinate structure under the tested nulls, while still leaving label meaning attached to the curated code table.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon rows.}
The broader leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, whereas the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, five groups were recorded: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$ above baseline, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each had hit rate $1.0$.

The codon-level row for this claim reports no anchors, five boundary cases, eight high-coverage closure cases, and eight predictable cases:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\ count} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count} &= 5,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\ count} &= 8.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recovery rate is $1.0$, and the thresholded recovery-rate row is also $1.0$.  In plain terms, every withheld codon in the tested thirteen-codon residual regime is either recovered by the local closure behavior or lies on the recorded boundary class; none is explained by an independent anchor row.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification is complete for the same thirteen codons: the stored row has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The six recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has at least one predictable or boundary route in the recorded classification.  This is the strongest finite-row statement justified here: the local window-six codon geometry organizes the thirteen residual codons into a complete module partition whose leave-one-codon behavior is fully recovered at the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only reality contact used in this chapter is the curated standard genetic-code table, understood as a codon-to-label table for amino-acid and stop labels.  It can test code-read assertions and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test whether a codon is physically translated in a given organism, whether a peptide folds, whether a molecular structure is admissible, whether a function is realized, or whether a universal biological rule holds.  Therefore the result is deliberately local: window-six coordinates reveal a finite boundary-tile pattern with recovery rate $1.0$ on the supplied leave-one-codon row, but all claims about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, and universality remain outside the evidence unless a separate contact supplies those observations.
