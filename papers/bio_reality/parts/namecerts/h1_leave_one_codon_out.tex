\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Boundary Readback in the Window-Six Codon Cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biological mechanism.  It is the window-six display in which codons are read as finite coordinates, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The external contact is the curated standard genetic-code table, used only as a codon-to-label readback bridge.  Thus the admissible observation is that a codon has an amino-acid or stop label in the standard code.  No translation event, folded structure, physical admissibility test, function assay, universal law, or mechanistic necessity is present in this contact.

\paragraph{The thirteen-row boundary set.}
The finite row isolates a distinguished set $R$ of thirteen codons.  The verified cardinality row reports $|R|=13$, and the codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same thirteen codons are verified as a punctured-cube row with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  This is the local finite geometry that the chapter records: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the extracted tile.  The point of the row is not that biology must realize this geometry, but that the curated label table makes this particular local split testable at code-read resolution.

\paragraph{Shape significance against row shuffles.}
The finite-row null model gives a concrete calibration for the local shape.  In $N=2000$ sampled trials with seed $42$, the experiment made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape was matched once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  High-overlap shape recovery at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second null that preserves amino-acid class reports observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$ over $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$.  These rows support that the displayed local geometry is not a generic accident of the tested finite shuffles.  They still do not promote the tile to a global law of DNA, translation, structure, or function.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The claim made here is exactly local.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a thirteen-row punctured-cube boundary tile whose leave-one-codon recovery row has recovery rate $1.0$, predictable count $8$, boundary count $5$, and complete six-module assignment.  The external standard-code table supplies label readback only.  Therefore the record cannot claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal genetic-code mechanism, or a law that DNA obeys this geometry as biological necessity.  Any such promotion requires a separate higher-layer reality contact.  Without that contact, amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation and is not a statement about folded structure or function.
