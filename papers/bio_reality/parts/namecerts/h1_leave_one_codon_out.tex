\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Boundary Readback in the Codon Cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite coordinate display for the standard-code codon table.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label.  This contact supports code-layer readback and local label-split checks, but it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Thus the object tested here is not DNA as a global biological necessity.  It is the finite row pattern obtained when the standard-code labels are placed on the window-six coordinate cube and one codon is withheld from the readback test.

\paragraph{Null checks.}
Two finite null probes constrain the interpretation.  The shape-significance probe used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It observed $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact\ shape}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These probes support nontriviality of the finite row pattern against the tested randomizations.  They do not remove the need for a separate biological contact if one wants to discuss translation, folding, physical feasibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready claim is a code-read claim.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure on the curated standard-code label table.  The thirteen-row punctured-cube set, its complete module classification, the codon-level recovery rate $1.0$, and the supporting null statistics jointly describe a finite label-readback phenomenon.  No higher-layer claim follows from this chapter.  Amino-acid label readback is not folded protein structure; a local codon tile is not a global biological law; and window-six geometry alone is not translation, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such claim requires a separate contact outside the curated codon-label table.
