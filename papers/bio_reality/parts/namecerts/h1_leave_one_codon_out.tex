\subsection{Leave-one-codon recovery on a local codon boundary tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object in this packet is the standard-code codon table read only as a finite assignment of codon words to amino-acid or stop labels.  Each codon is displayed in the six-coordinate cube $Q_6$ by the two-bit encoding of its three nucleotide positions.  The construction does not treat the cube as a biological force.  It treats the cube as a bookkeeping space in which local faces, corners, and label classes can be tested against the curated code table.  The claim tested here is the leave-one-codon recovery question: after removing one codon from the candidate row family, does the local closure pattern recover the missing row as predictable or else place it on an explicit boundary?

\paragraph{The thirteen-row boundary family.}
The finite row family isolated by the coordinate test has cardinality $13$.  The rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same computation identifies this set as a punctured cube-shaped local patch with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row family is not merely a list of exceptional codons.  It carries a finite local tile shape: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces after one cube corner is absent.  This is the geometric content of the row packet; it remains a code-layer statement about codon labels.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The associated spectral computation gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the finite matrix calculation satisfies its radial polynomial check to numerical tolerance:
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The shape was also compared with randomized controls.  In the unconstrained shape sample, $N=2000$ trials produced one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The run used seed $42$, required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving control, the observed comparison set had size $20$; among $2000$ trials there were no exact shape matches and seven samples in the lower tail at or below the observed size, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact,class}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{lower,class}}=0.0035$.  These numbers support a finite code-table anomaly claim, not a mechanism claim.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon result.}
The direct leave-one-codon run evaluated the thirteen rows of $R$ after deleting one codon at a time.  The verified counts are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\mathrm{anchor}&=0,\\
\#\mathrm{boundary}&=5,\\
\#\mathrm{predictable}&=8,\\
\#\mathrm{high\ closure\ coverage}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The absence of anchor rows means that no deleted codon was recovered by a privileged singleton seed in this run.  Instead, eight deletions were recovered by the local closure test, while five landed on the boundary side of the same finite tile.  Since every deletion is assigned to one of these two outcomes, the recovery rate above the stated threshold is also $1.0$.  The local statement is therefore sharp: within this thirteen-row codon family, the leave-one-row experiment never escapes the predictable-or-boundary dichotomy.

\paragraph{Module-level accounting.}
The module classification accounts for all thirteen rows with no missing or extra codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The accounting row reports $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no unassigned codons, no missing module rows, and no extra module codons.  Consequently the leave-one-codon result is not concentrated in a single biochemical label family; it spans stop, tryptophan-adjacent, leucine-family, arginine-family, lysine-adjacent, and isoleucine--methionine-adjacent rows as finite table entries.  A related leave-one-code-table experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast tables.

\paragraph{Interpretation at the code layer.}
The curated standard-code table is used here as the contact point for codon-to-label readback.  It says that a codon word has a recorded amino-acid or stop label in the standard code.  It does not observe translation dynamics, ribosomal realization, folded structure, energetic admissibility, phenotype, or biological function.  For that reason the recovery statement is deliberately confined to local code-read geometry: a window-six display exposes a thirteen-row punctured-tile family whose single-row deletions are all recovered as predictable or boundary cases.  No claim follows that organisms must implement this geometry, that a protein fold is determined by it, that the corresponding amino-acid string is physically admissible, that the sequence has a function, or that the tile is a universal biological law.  Any such promotion would require a separate empirical contact at the corresponding layer.
