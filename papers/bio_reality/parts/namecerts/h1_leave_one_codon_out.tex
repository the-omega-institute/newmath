\paragraph{Carrier and contact.}
This record is a BioReality NameCert row for the standard-code codon table, read only at the code layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, where a codon is displayed as a finite coordinate word and the observed labels are supplied by the curated standard genetic-code table.  The reality contact is therefore not translation itself, and it is not a protein experiment.  It is only the codon-to-label bridge that assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  The claim tested here is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern under leave-one-codon deletion, while the biological labels remain external contacts.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The observed contact supports only codon label readback and local split checks.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate higher-layer contact, such as a translation assay, a structure experiment, a physical-admissibility model with empirical support, or a functional assay.  Without such a contact, the ready claim is exactly this: in the standard-code table, the thirteen-codon leave-one-codon-out row forms a finite window-six boundary tile whose deleted entries are completely accounted for by predictable or boundary status at the code-read layer.
