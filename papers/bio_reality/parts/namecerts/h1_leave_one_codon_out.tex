\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Recovery on the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The object considered here is the standard-code codon table read at the code-label layer.  The internal carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinates used only as a finite display of codon positions.  The empirical contact is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The claim tested in the finite rows is not that the coordinate cube explains translation.  It is the narrower statement that, after one codon is withheld, the window-six coordinate structure and the observed label table recover a local boundary pattern with high coverage.

\paragraph{Distinguished boundary set.}
The finite rows isolate a thirteen-codon set $R$.  Its reported cardinality is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the listed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the boundary data are not merely a label list: they carry a finite incidence shape inside the codon cube.  The row does not promote that incidence shape beyond the code-label layer; it records a local tile with four two-dimensional faces, sixteen edges, and thirteen vertices in the displayed codon coordinates.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The primary finite row for this chapter is $h1.leave.one.codon.out$.  It reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\_count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\_count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\_count}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\_rate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The accompanying threshold row is also $1.0$.  In this finite experiment, every recoverable leave-one-codon-out case is recovered by the recorded local closure-or-boundary rule.  The absence of anchors is important: the row is not saying that an omitted codon is fixed by a single isolated label corner.  It says instead that the useful information sits in boundary and closure structure within the finite codon display.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module row partitions the same thirteen codons without omissions or surplus codons.  It reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row states that every module has a predictable or boundary status.  This is the strongest local reading supported here: the coordinate display, the closure computation, and the curated label table organize the thirteen-codon boundary set into complete modules.

\paragraph{Comparison rows.}
The leave-one-code-out row gives $23$ evaluated code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$.  A family-level row further reports five families, with above-baseline rates $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  These rows are auxiliary support for the local code-read pattern: the signal is not confined to a single isolated table comparison.

\paragraph{Null-model checks.}
Two null-model rows constrain the interpretation.  The shape-significance row used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ duplicate retries, one exact shape match, and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It reports observed $M$ size $20$, exact match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These values support treating the observed local shape as non-generic under the recorded finite randomizations, while still leaving the biological interpretation at the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  That contact supplies codon-to-label readback.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, tRNA charging, molecular folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, evolutionary necessity, or a universal mechanism.  Therefore the result cannot be quoted as a statement about protein realization, folded structure, physical viability, functional activity, or a global law of biology.  Any such claim would need a separate contact of the appropriate kind.  Within the present finite evidence, the conclusion is precise: the window-six codon display, together with the curated standard-code labels, exposes a local leave-one-codon-out boundary-and-closure pattern on the thirteen-codon set $R$, with complete module assignment and full reported recovery among the predictable cases.
