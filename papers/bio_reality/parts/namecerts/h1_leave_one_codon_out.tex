\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Boundary Readback}
\label{sec:bioreality-leave-one-codon-out-boundary-readback}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The record \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out} concerns the standard-code codon table read only at the code layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, equipped with a six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological contact is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  This contact is enough to test codon-label readback and local label splits, but it is not a translation experiment, not a protein-structure observation, not a physical-admissibility test, and not a functional assay.

\paragraph{Distinguished finite row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is observed as a punctured-cube shape with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it has a local cubical boundary profile inside the window-six display.  The construction still remains finite-row evidence: it says that this particular codon set has a measured local shape, not that biology is forced by that shape.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
Against the shape null with $N=2000$ sampled tables, the exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling audit reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries with seed $42$.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$: the observed $M$-row size was $20$, exact shape matches were $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, and the corresponding lower-tail probability was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the finite-row claim that the observed local tile is atypical under the stated nulls.  It does not convert atypicality into a law of translation or a law of biological design.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon tests.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row is sharper at the local boundary: it has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{recovery}}=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The thresholded recovery statistic is also $1.0$.  Thus, within the finite row actually tested, every recovered case is accounted for either by the predictable closure side or by the explicit boundary side; no additional hidden global biological premise is being used.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The $13$ codons of $R$ are completely assigned to six named modules.  The arginine module is $\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$; the arginine--lysine singleton module is $\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$; the $\mathrm{CUN}$ module is $\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$; the isoleucine--methionine module is $\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$; the serine-related module is $\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$; and the stop--tryptophan module is $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The module audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Hence the module classification is a closed finite partition of the displayed row.

\paragraph{Family-level code readback.}
The by-family leave-one-code-out row reports five families.  The above-baseline hit rates are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate.  These family numbers are read as code-table label behavior under the same finite-row protocol.  They do not assert that those biological lineages implement a common geometric mechanism; they assert only that the curated label tables, when passed through this finite readback, have the displayed recovery pattern.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The boundary condition is part of the result.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, not structure, not physical admissibility, not function, not mechanism, and not universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  The only contact used here is the curated standard-code label table, so the claim is confined to code-layer readback and local codon-tile boundary behavior.  Any promotion to translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or universal mechanism would require a separate higher-layer reality contact.
