\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Recovery in a Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Record and scope.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer object.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, where each codon is read as a two-bit display at each of three nucleotide positions.  The observed contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The question tested here is local: after one codon label is withheld, does the window-six coordinate geometry still place the withheld item inside the predictable part of the local tile, or at least on the explicit boundary of that tile?  The answer is recorded only at the label-readback layer.  It is not a claim about translation being realized, a folded structure being present, a molecule being physically admissible, a function being produced, a mechanism being universal, or any biological necessity following from the coordinate picture.

\paragraph{The distinguished thirteen-codon region.}
The finite region $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the recovered region is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same list and reports face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the tested region is not introduced as a vague biological neighborhood: it is a finite subcomplex with thirteen listed vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces inside the six-bit codon display.  The table contact supplies the labels attached to those codons; the coordinate construction supplies the finite local shape on which the leave-one-codon test is run.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon result.}
For the leave-one-codon experiment, the verified run reports no anchor recoveries and five boundary cases:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{anchor\_count}&=0,\\
\operatorname{boundary\_count}&=5,\\
\operatorname{predictable\_count}&=8,\\
\operatorname{closure\_coverage\_high}&=8.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recovery rate is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{recovery\_rate}=1.0,
\qquad
\operatorname{recovery\_rate\_above\_threshold}=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite interpretation is that every withheld codon in this thirteen-codon region is accounted for by the local predictable part or by the named boundary part of the tile.  The result does not say that the omitted codon is biologically forced by geometry.  It says that, relative to the curated standard-code labels and the chosen six-coordinate display, the withheld codon remains within the finite local recovery envelope.

\paragraph{Module-level partition.}
The module classification exhausts the thirteen codons without extras or omissions.  It reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, and empty lists for extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}

\operatorname{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\operatorname{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\operatorname{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\

$$
The audit condition for this partition is that every listed module contains only codons already in $R$ and that every codon in $R$ appears in exactly one listed module.  This matters because the leave-one-codon result is not merely a global count.  It is compatible with a complete finite assignment by recognizable codon-label modules: arginine-related AGA/AGG, an AAA lysine-related singleton, the CUN four-codon block, the AUA isoleucine/methionine site, the UCA/UUA pair, and the UAA/UAG/UGA stop/tryptophan block.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The same finite record supplies numerical shape evidence.  The spectral entries are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported value of the radial cubic expression is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, within numerical tolerance for the stated spectral check.  A random-shape comparison with $N=2000$ samples, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts found $55377$ duplicate retries, one exact shape match, and twenty-five matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  This gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It recorded observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support treating the local tile as a nontrivial finite code-layer pattern rather than as an arbitrary relabeling artifact.  They still do not promote the tile into a claim about biochemical realization.

\paragraph{Code-table comparison.}
A related leave-one-code-table run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  These comparisons are included only to place the codon-level recovery record beside its code-table-level companion: they show that the same finite geometry has high label-readback coverage across the recorded table family, but they do not by themselves establish a universal law of genetic-code evolution.

\paragraph{Boundary conclusion.}
The chapter-level conclusion is therefore narrow and finite.  The thirteen-codon region $R$ is a punctured-cube-shaped local tile inside $Q_6$; its module partition is complete; the leave-one-codon test recovers all withheld entries either as predictable or as boundary entries; and the associated shape, spectral, and null-model numbers make the pattern unlikely to be an unstructured coincidence under the recorded comparisons.  The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code label table.  Consequently the result remains at code readback: no translation event, protein fold, physical admissibility condition, molecular function, mechanistic pathway, or universal biological principle is inferred without a separate contact designed to observe that higher layer.
