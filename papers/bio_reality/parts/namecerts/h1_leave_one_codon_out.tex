\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Boundary Recovery}
\label{sec:leave-one-codon-out-boundary-recovery}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The object under test is not translation as a biochemical process, but the finite readback that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The BEDC-side display uses a six-bit codon coordinate, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The external contact is the curated standard genetic-code table, used only as a label table at codon resolution. Thus the observed fact is a finite codon-to-label assignment, not a mechanism, not a folding experiment, and not a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Recovered exceptional row.}
The row singled out by the finite construction has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is detected as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers are the finite-row content of the boundary claim: the construction is seeing a specific thirteen-codon tile in $Q_6$, with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces, rather than an unconstrained subset of the $64$ codons.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}
For the codon-level leave-one-out test, the run reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, and $0$ anchor codons. The high-coverage closure count is $8$. The recovery rate is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{recovery\_rate}=1.0,
\qquad
\operatorname{recovery\_rate\_above\_threshold}=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
In this chapter, the interpretation is deliberately narrow. If a codon is removed from the finite label table and the remaining six-bit neighborhood recovers its placement as predictable or boundary-visible, that is evidence for local code-layer structure. It is not evidence that the codon realizes translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classifier assigns all $13$ codons in $R$ with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The complete module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}

Every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary explanation under the finite-row classifier. This matters because the leave-one-codon-out result is not merely a scalar hit count; it is also compatible with the natural grouped codon rows that appear in the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Against a shape null with $N=2000$ sampled alternatives, the exact shape match count is $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-Jaccard count at threshold $0.9$ is $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling run records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries with seed $42$, so the comparison is finite and explicitly row-level. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$. The corresponding values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These null comparisons support non-random local finite-row structure at the code-read layer. They do not identify a translation mechanism or a structure-function law.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The verdict is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code table. The evidence consists of the $13$-codon punctured-cube row, its $(13,16,4)$ face-vector, the complete module classification, the leave-one-codon-out recovery rate $1.0$, the spectral coordinates, and the finite null comparisons. The cannot-claim boundary is part of the mathematical statement: no translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity claim follows from this chapter unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
