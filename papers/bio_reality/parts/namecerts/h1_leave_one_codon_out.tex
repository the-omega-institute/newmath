\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out boundary tile for the standard-code readback}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite coordinate display for codon labels.  The reality contact is the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it is used here only as a code-layer readback.  Thus the biological object under test is not translation as a molecular process, not folding, not physical admissibility, not function, not a mechanism, and not a universal law.  The claim is narrower: after one codon is left out, the window-six coordinate geometry still marks a local boundary-tile regime whose recovery is visible against the curated code labels.

\paragraph{The distinguished punctured cube row.}
The finite row isolates a thirteen-codon set $R$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cardinality is $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile, not the whole code.  The row records that the same thirteen codons match the expected codon list for $R$, so the carrier-level distinction is a concrete finite subset of the codon cube rather than a verbal grouping.

The associated spectral row gives

\mu&=3.051487585298128,

$$
with radial cubic residual $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$.  The observed $M$-size in the amino-acid-class-preserving null test is $20$.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampler attempted $57377$ distinct draws and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, $N=2000$ samples gave $0$ exact matches, a lower-tail count $7$ at or below the observed $M$-size, $p=0.0$ for exact shape, and $p=0.0035$ for the lower tail.  These numbers support a finite-row anomaly at the code-read layer; they do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon performance.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  Inside $M-T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M-T$ plus boundary it was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row is sharper at the codon level: it reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The row therefore says that the missing codon is recovered by predictable or boundary status in every recorded test case, but it does not say that the codon participates in any higher biological realization.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons are partitioned into six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

$$
The classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons are all empty.  The family-level leave-one-code-out check gives $5$ families.  Their hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite evidence supports the code-read statement that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure for the curated standard-code label table.  The strongest finite-row formulation is that the thirteen-codon punctured cube has complete module assignment, nontrivial shape and null-model separation, and leave-one-codon recovery rate $1.0$ with $8$ predictable cases and $5$ boundary cases.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these rows.  Any such promotion would require a separate reality contact whose observed quantities live at that higher layer.
