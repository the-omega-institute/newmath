\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ read through the standard-code label table.  The biological contact used here is only the curated codon-to-label bridge: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it gives a code-layer readback for finite tests.  It is not a mechanism of translation, not a structural contact, not a physical-admissibility measurement, and not a function assay.  Consequently the result in this section is a local boundary-tile statement about code labels in a finite coordinate display.  It does not claim that DNA obeys the geometry as a biological law, and it does not promote an amino-acid label sequence to a folded protein structure.
