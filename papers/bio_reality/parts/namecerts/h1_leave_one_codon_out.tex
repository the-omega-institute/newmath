\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display.  The biological contact is only the curated assignment of each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The mathematical object tested here is therefore not translation as a process, and not protein structure, but the finite label readback on codon corners.  The relevant residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a local cubical pattern this set has the recorded face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row being tested is a punctured local tile rather than an arbitrary collection of isolated codons.  The companion $M$ support is recorded in the finite run by size, with observed $|M|=20$ in the amino-acid-class preserving null comparison; no separate explicit ordered list of $M$ codons is supplied by this row.

\paragraph{Spectral and randomization checks.}
The finite spectral summary separates the residual codon tile from the larger local support.  The recorded values are

\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\

$$
The radial cubic check is numerically closed at residual value $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is the same tolerance-level value reported for the cubic relation.  Against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives, the exact shape appeared once, giving $p=0.0005$.  High-overlap alternatives with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving $p=0.0125$.  The sampling run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the quoted rates should be read as finite Monte Carlo evidence for a sharply constrained local shape, not as an asymptotic biological law.  Under the amino-acid-class preserving null model, the exact shape count was $0$ in $2000$ trials, while the lower tail count at or below observed $|M|=20$ was $7$, giving a lower-tail value $p=0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon recovery.}
The leave-one-codon row tests whether withholding a single codon label can still be resolved from the local tile and boundary data.  In the verified row, the anchor count is $0$, the boundary count is $5$, the high-coverage closure count is $8$, and the predictable count is $8$.  The resulting recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{recovery\_rate}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value recorded above the threshold.  Thus every codon in the finite evaluation is recovered either by the predictable part of the local closure or by boundary placement; none is recovered by an independent singleton anchor.  This is the specific claim supported by the row: the six-bit local display and the curated standard-code labels expose a recoverable boundary split for the withheld codon task.

\paragraph{Local boundary of the claim.}
The contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supplies codon-to-label readback and permits local split checks.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, molecular folding, energetic admissibility, cellular function, a causal mechanism, or universality across biology.  In particular, amino-acid label readback is not a folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global law of DNA or translation.  Any assertion about translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or universal mechanism would require an additional contact of the appropriate kind.  The present row should therefore be read as a finite code-layer result: the standard-code labels, when placed on the six-bit codon display, support a leave-one-codon local boundary recovery with complete module accounting and the numerical rates stated above.
