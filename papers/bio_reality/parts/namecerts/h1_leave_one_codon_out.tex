\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Boundary Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.}
The record is formed on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, where a codon is read as a finite coordinate word and the standard-code label table is used only as an external readback contact. The contact supplies codon-to-label assignments for amino-acid and stop labels. It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The biological object under test is therefore the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer, not a protein-production or structure layer.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished finite row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$. Its punctured-cube check reports the same row and the face-vector summary
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R) = (13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not treated as an informal visual cluster: it is a finite coordinate object with a declared cardinality, an explicit codon list, and a local face-count profile. The complementary shape statistic uses an observed $M$-row size of $20$. In the unrestricted null sample with $N=2000$, exact shape agreement occurred once, so $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$; high-Jaccard agreement at threshold $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, so $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$. The sample required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries at seed $42$, recording that the null space was finite but heavily collision-prone under the sampling regime.

\paragraph{Spectrum.}
The same coordinate packet carries a spectral readout. The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported as the cubic-polynomial residual. This places the spectrum check within numerical tolerance for the finite row used here. The spectral values are used only as coordinate evidence for the local codon-tile packet; they are not promoted to a physical or mechanistic law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out context.}
The code-level leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family. These figures are relevant because the present codon-level row is not isolated from the finite table readback: the local codon boundary is seen against a broader leave-one-code-out code-read test.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
For the specific claim $h1.leave.one.codon.out$, the leave-one-codon-out experiment reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, and $8$ predictable codons. The recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{recover}} = 1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the threshold comparison also reported as $1.0$. In this row, the prediction signal is therefore entirely a finite code-read signal: each recovered item is counted through predictable status or boundary status, not through any claim about biochemical realization.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the same thirteen-codon row. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons. It also reports that every listed module has predictable-or-boundary support. This is the strongest finite-row statement available here: the local row is covered by a complete module partition whose pieces each touch the predictable-or-boundary criterion.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null strengthens the boundary interpretation. In $N=2000$ samples, the exact-shape count was $0$, so the exact-shape null value is $0.0$. The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving lower-tail value $0.0035$. The observed $M$-size in this test was again $20$. Since this null preserves amino-acid class information, the result is not merely a consequence of destroying all label structure; the local finite pattern remains rare under a constrained label-respecting randomization.

\paragraph{Verdict and boundary.}
The finite-row verdict is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table. The evidence is the explicit thirteen-codon row, the punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectrum values with near-zero cubic residual, the complete six-module classification, the codon-level recovery rate $1.0$, and the code-level leave-one-code-out hit rates above baseline. The claim stops exactly there. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. Any future promotion beyond code-read requires a separate reality contact at the relevant layer.
