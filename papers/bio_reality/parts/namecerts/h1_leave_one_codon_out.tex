\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Recovery at the Local Codon Boundary}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite setting.}
The claim concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$. Each codon is read as a finite six-bit display, with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes used only as finite coordinate features. The biological contact is the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and that is the only biological observation used here. The claim does not assert that the codon cube is a mechanism of translation, a structural model of proteins, a physical admissibility test, a function assay, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The leave-one-codon-out run has experiment id \texttt{e9e1a6e41633}. It reports no anchor rows, five boundary rows, eight high-coverage closure rows, and eight predictable rows. The recovery statistic is exact at the recorded threshold:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\_count} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\_count} &= 5,\\
\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\_count} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\_rate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a finite row-level statement: after one codon is withheld, the recorded local coordinate and closure features recover the held-out codon class in the tested row set. The result is therefore a code-read result, not a statement about ribosomal realization, folding, energetic feasibility, organism-level use, or biological function.

\paragraph{Shape and null rows.}
The shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. There was one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with the same sample count and seed, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, the exact-shape value was recorded as $0.0$, and the lower-tail value was $0.0035$. These rows make the local pattern nontrivial relative to the tested randomizations, while still leaving the source of codon labels in the curated table.

\paragraph{Code-table generalization check.}
The related leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary rows was again $0.9841269841269841$. By family, five families were recorded: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$. These finite checks show that the same code-read pattern is not confined to a single scalar row, but they still remain comparisons of codon-label assignments.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The codon modules account for all $13$ members of $R$: assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing members, and no unassigned codons. The recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.

$$
Each module has a predictable or boundary status in the finite run. This is the main local interpretation of the claim: the leave-one-codon-out recovery is distributed over biologically named codon-label modules, but only at the code-label level.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite data support the following bounded conclusion. In the six-coordinate codon display, the standard-code table exposes a local boundary-tile pattern whose held-out codon rows are recovered at rate $1.0$ in the recorded leave-one-codon-out experiment, with $8$ predictable rows and $5$ boundary rows. The $13$-codon residual set, the punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral values, the randomization rows, and the complete module classification jointly support the code-read claim. Nothing in these rows establishes translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, organismal universality, or any law beyond the supplied codon-label contact. Those stronger claims require separate observations designed for those layers.
