\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Boundary Test for the Standard Code}
\label{sec:bioreality-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table.  The contact is deliberately narrow: it supplies codon-to-label readback for the standard code, and it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of code origin, or universality.  The local object under test is therefore a code-layer boundary tile, not a global biological law.  The distinguished residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured-cube row, this same set has face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the row is not merely a count: it fixes thirteen named codon corners, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four local faces in the six-bit display.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe removes a single codon-level datum and asks whether the remaining local tile recovers the missing code-layer placement.  In the finite run $\mathrm{e9e1a6e41633}$, the row has $0$ anchor cases, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, and $8$ predictable cases.  The reported recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{8}{8}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the thresholded recovery statistic is also $1.0$.  This is the chapter-specific claim: within the supplied local codon tile and its curated standard-code readback, every case counted as predictable is recovered, while five cases are explicitly classified as boundary rather than anchor behavior.  The result is a code-read fact about this finite row, not an inference to biochemical realization.

The module audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Hence the leave-one-codon-out row has no hidden residual class inside the supplied finite data: each codon in $R$ is accounted for by exactly one displayed module, and every displayed module has a predictable-or-boundary witness.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}
The associated spectral row reports $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The cubic, or radial, polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the stated spectral-radius constraint is met to numerical tolerance.  The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null row, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, and the reported probabilities are $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$.  These finite checks support non-generic local shape in the code-label table, while still leaving the contact at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The boundary statement is part of the result.  A local six-bit codon tile may expose a finite boundary split in the curated standard-code labels, but the row does not establish translation realization, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any claim beyond code-layer readback requires a separate reality contact supplying that higher-layer observation.  In the present chapter the contact is only the curated standard-code table, so the strongest ready verdict is a finite code-read recovery and boundary classification for $h1.leave.one.codon.out$.
