\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The reality contact is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the conclusion is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile pattern for the standard-code label table, and the leave-one-codon-out finite row recovers the held-out label at rate $1.0$ under the supplied protocol.
