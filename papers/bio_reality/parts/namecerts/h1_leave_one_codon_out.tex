\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Recovery Inside a Local Codon Tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim being tested.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, where a codon is treated as a six-bit display point and the observed labels are the curated amino-acid or stop assignments.  The claim is not that the cube geometry explains translation.  The claim is narrower: after one codon label is withheld, the local coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data identify whether the omitted row lies in the predictable part of the tile or on its boundary.  The observed contact is therefore a label table, not a mechanism.

\paragraph{Finite rows.}
The distinguished set $R$ has size $13$.  In the verified row set it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run identifies this set as a punctured-cube row set with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not an arbitrary sample of codons: it has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the finite cubical presentation used by the packet.  The module classification accounts for all thirteen rows, with no unassigned codon, no missing module entry, and no extra module codon.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{AGA,AGG\},& \mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},& \mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{UCA,UUA\},& \mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The accounting check gives $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$ and $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The spectral run reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the recorded polynomial evaluation is numerically at zero to the displayed tolerance.  In the shape-significance experiment, $N=2000$ random samples were tested with seed $42$.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The distinct-sample attempt count was $57377$, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, and the corresponding lower-tail probability was $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon result.}
The codon-level leave-one-out run reports $\mathrm{anchor\_count}=0$, $\mathrm{boundary\_count}=5$, $\mathrm{predictable\_count}=8$, and $\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high}=8$.  Its recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\_rate}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the threshold comparison also recorded as $1.0$.  This means that every held-out codon row in the tested local packet is recovered either as predictable from the closure data or as a boundary row of the tile.  It does not mean that every biological operation involving that codon is explained.  At the neighboring code-table level, $23$ tables and $63$ targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$ and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The family-level check divides the code-table comparison into five families.  The recorded hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid tables, $1.0$ for candidate-other tables, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate tables, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast tables.  This stratification supports the reading that the local tile remains visible under several code-table families, while also showing that the family split is empirical data rather than a theorem of the coordinate system alone.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  It can test the code-read statement and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or any universal mechanism.  Consequently, amino-acid label readback remains a code-layer observation.  The finite tile may organize which codon labels are locally predictable and which sit on the boundary, but it supplies no structure contact and no functional assay.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
Within the stated finite data, the leave-one-codon packet is supported at the code-read layer.  The thirteen-row set $R$, the complete module assignment, the spectral values, the sampling probabilities, the $23$-table and $63$-target code-level comparison, and the codon-level recovery rate $1.0$ all point to a robust local boundary-tile phenomenon.  The result must be read with its scope intact: no claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, biological mechanism, or universality follows unless an additional contact directly observes that higher layer.
