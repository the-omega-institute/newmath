\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The claim uses the window-six codon display as a finite coordinate carrier $Q_6$.  A codon is read only as a six-bit coordinate together with an externally supplied standard-code label.  The reality contact is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, but it is not a contact for translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Thus the biological object under test is the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer, not a higher biological process.

\paragraph{Distinguished row set.}
The finite row set singled out by the coordinate and closure probes has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite set is reported as a punctured-cube row pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile under discussion.  Its interpretation is deliberately narrow: it is a finite boundary-tile shape inside the codon coordinate cube, with labels read back from the curated table.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$: the assigned count is $13$, there are no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons.  The finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_LYS}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ILE\_MET}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{STOP\_TRP}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary status in the finite computation.  This is the row-level reason that the leave-one-codon-out readback is treated as a local boundary-tile result rather than as a statement about the full biological realization of translation.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite-row verdict is that window-six codon coordinates expose a reproducible local boundary-tile structure for the standard-code label table.  The contact supplies codon-to-label assignments, and the finite probes report a $13$-row punctured-cube pattern, significant null-model behavior, complete module assignment, and perfect leave-one-codon recovery within the tested boundary criterion.  No claim follows about actual translation, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, causal mechanism, or universality.  Any such claim would require a separate higher-layer reality contact.  In the present chapter, amino-acid label readback remains label readback only.
