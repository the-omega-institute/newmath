\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Boundary Tile in the Standard Code}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The leave-one-codon experiment tests whether the six-coordinate codon cube carries a local boundary pattern that is visible after one codon label is withheld.  The claim is deliberately narrow: the observable object is the standard codon-label table, and the tested layer is code readback.  The construction uses the codon cube $Q_6$, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes as finite distinctions.  Its readout is not a biochemical mechanism; it is a comparison between finite cube geometry and the curated codon-to-label assignment.

\paragraph{Finite codon row.}
The row singled out by the finite computation has cardinality $13$.  The codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured cube with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object is not merely a list of exceptional codons.  It has a small cubical incidence profile: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional tile faces after the puncture is imposed.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The row also has a numerical separation from the complementary codon mass.  The recorded spectral values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual was $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the cubic-polynomial check.  At this precision the row is treated as satisfying the tested algebraic relation numerically.  The spectral statement is only a finite diagnostic of the codon table row; it does not claim that cells implement this operator or that molecular evolution optimizes this spectrum.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the recorded shape null, the run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The sampling log recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only $1$ exact shape match was observed, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed module size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$ out of $2000$.  The corresponding recorded probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support the statement that the row geometry is uncommon under the finite nulls that were actually run.  They do not by themselves identify a causal genetic-code process.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code comparison.}
The broader leave-one-code table test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  Inside $M-T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M-T$ enlarged by the boundary layer it returned to $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  These numbers place the codon-row experiment inside a finite code-table comparison, but the present section only uses them as code-layer evidence.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon result.}
For the codon-level withholding test itself, the run recorded
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\ count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\ count}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recovery rate above threshold was also $1.0$.  Thus the tested codons were recovered by predictable or boundary behavior rather than by a nonzero anchor count.  The evidence is therefore a local boundary-tile signal: the missing codon can be read back at the code-label level from the finite coordinate neighborhood and its boundary class.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The complete module classification assigns all $13$ codons in $R$, with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The six recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and $\mathrm{assigned\ count}=13$.  Each module has a predictable or boundary explanation in the recorded run.  This is the most concrete form of the claim: the finite row splits into named codon modules that are exhausted by the local code-layer test.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, understood as a codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop label assignment.  This contact can check whether the finite cube coordinates read back the standard-code labels and whether the local tile split aligns with those labels.  It cannot observe ribosomal translation, protein folding, energetic admissibility, organismal function, or universality across genetic systems.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
No translation claim follows from this section.  No folded structure claim follows.  No physical-admissibility, biological-function, mechanism, or universal-law claim follows.  An amino-acid label sequence produced by code readback is still only a label sequence unless a separate structural or biochemical contact is supplied.  Cross-organism wording, CUN/UUR notation, or tRNA-Leu language must remain outside this packet unless matched to its own organism-specific evidence route.  The present result is a finite code-layer observation: in the standard-code table, the thirteen-codon punctured-cube row has an uncommon shape, a stable numerical signature, a complete six-module classification, and a leave-one-codon recovery rate of $1.0$ through predictable or boundary classes.
