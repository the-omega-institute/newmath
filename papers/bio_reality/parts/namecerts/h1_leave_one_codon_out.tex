\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Local Tile Recovery}
\label{sec:codon-leave-one-out-local-tile-recovery}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The claim concerns a finite standard-code readback test on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is treated as a six-bit display point, and the only biological contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The packet tests whether the local window-six geometry isolates a small boundary tile whose missing codon labels can be recovered from nearby finite structure.  It does not assert that the cube geometry is a biological law.  It asserts only that, after the standard-code labels have been supplied by the curated table, a leave-one-codon-out probe sees a complete finite recovery pattern at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reported exceptional row.}
The reported exceptional row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its listed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object used by the leave-one-codon-out test is not an arbitrary list of labels.  It is a thirteen-codon subconfiguration with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces inside the six-coordinate codon display.  The supplied finite row does not give an explicit membership list for the complementary $M$ row, but it does report an observed $M$ size of $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null test.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinate.}
The same finite object is accompanied by a numerical spectral readout.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic or radial check has residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the polynomial residual is numerically zero at the displayed tolerance.  This provides a finite coordinate summary of the local split: $R$ is the listed punctured-cube boundary row, while $M$ is detected by the reported size and spectral component rather than by an explicit codon membership list in this packet.

\paragraph{Shape significance checks.}
The shape-significance simulation used $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the $2000$ sampled rows there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null preserving amino-acid classes also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It reported $0$ exact matches to the observed shape, an observed $M$ size of $20$, and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below that observed size.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the displayed finite shape is not typical under the two sampled null routes.  They do not identify a mechanism of translation or a structural cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon readbacks.}
The code-level leave-one-out run evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  These row counts say that the finite coordinate rule is strongly aligned with the curated code-label readback across the evaluated targets.

For the specific leave-one-codon-out packet, the finite recovery row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\ count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\ count}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports recovery rate above threshold equal to $1.0$.  Thus all eight codons in the predictable class are recovered by the local finite rule, while five codons remain boundary codons.  There are no anchor codons in this reported packet.  The result is therefore best read as a local boundary-and-closure decomposition of the thirteen-codon row, not as a global codon theory.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The module-classification row assigns all $13$ codons in $R$ and reports no extras, no missing entries, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row explicitly states that every module has either a predictable codon or a boundary codon.  Since the assigned count is $13$ and $R$ has size $13$, this is a complete partition of the reported exceptional row.  The leave-one-codon-out result is therefore not merely a scalar recovery statistic; it is also compatible with the displayed finite module decomposition.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The only external contact in this packet is the curated standard-code table.  That contact supplies codon-to-label assignments; it does not supply ribosomal realization, folding data, physical admissibility measurements, functional assays, or a universal biological mechanism.  Consequently the result may be stated as follows: within the six-coordinate codon display, the listed thirteen-codon row has a punctured-cube boundary shape, significant finite null-model behavior, a complete six-module assignment, and full recovery among the eight predictable leave-one-codon-out cases.  No claim about translation, folded protein structure, physical viability, function, mechanism, or universality follows from these data unless a separate observation source for that higher layer is added.
