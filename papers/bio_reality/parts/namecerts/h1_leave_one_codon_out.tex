\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out codon recovery as a local code-read boundary}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ read through a curated standard-code label table.  The row tested here is not a claim about translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  It is a code-read claim: after one codon label is withheld, the window-six coordinate system is asked whether the missing codon lies in the locally predictable or boundary part of the standard-code tile.  The observed leave-one-codon-out row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{anchor\_count} &= 0,\\
\operatorname{boundary\_count} &= 5,\\
\operatorname{closure\_coverage\_high} &= 8,\\
\operatorname{predictable\_count} &= 8,\\
\operatorname{recovery\_rate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus all thirteen codons in the tested residual set are accounted for by either the predictable side or the boundary side of the local tile, with no anchor codons recorded in this row.

\paragraph{Residual codon set.}
The residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The finite row identifies it explicitly as
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row records that this is the punctured-cube residual set, with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The interpretation is deliberately local: the listed codons are the finite codon rows whose standard-code labels interact with the window-six tile boundary.  The table contact supplies the codon-to-label readback; it does not supply a biochemical mechanism that would force this geometry.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite sampling row with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact shape was observed once.  The row records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape probability estimate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the high-Jaccard estimate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ samples reaching the $J\geq 0.9$ threshold.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null row, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact match count is $0$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite numbers support non-random local shape pressure at the code-label layer, not a claim that the code table was generated by the coordinate system.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the thirteen residual codons.  It records $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\operatorname{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\

\operatorname{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\

$$
Every listed module has either a predictable or boundary witness in the row.  This is the strongest form of the finite local statement: the residual codons are not merely counted correctly, but partitioned into named code-read modules whose members are exhausted by the local predictable-or-boundary dichotomy.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact has codon-label resolution.  It can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary necessity.  Therefore the finite verdict is: the window-six coordinate row recovers the thirteen-codon residual set at rate $1.0$ through predictable and boundary classes, and this recovery is statistically sharp against the recorded null rows; nevertheless the conclusion remains a local code-read boundary statement unless a separate higher-layer contact is supplied.
