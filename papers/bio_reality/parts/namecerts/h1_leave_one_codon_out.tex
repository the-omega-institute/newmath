\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery in the codon cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only through a curated standard-code label table.  The coordinate display is finite: a codon is treated as a cube vertex, a local tile face is a finite cubical neighborhood, and an amino-acid or stop assignment is a singleton label corner or a three-point label class according to the supplied table.  The reality contact used here is therefore deliberately narrow.  It observes that the standard code assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it supports code-layer readback and local split checks.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{The finite exceptional row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also verified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the object being tested is not an arbitrary list of labels: it is a finite codon subset with a concrete cubical incidence profile.  This profile is the internal structure visible to the BEDC coordinate, while the amino-acid and stop labels remain supplied by the external table.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The chapter therefore records a code-read regularity for the standard-code table: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure, and that structure has verified finite-row recovery for the leave-one-codon-out test.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any later crossing from code-read to translation, structure, admissibility, function, or mechanism requires a separate reality contact that directly observes that higher layer.
