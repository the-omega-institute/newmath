In this run the anchor count is $0$, the boundary count is $5$, the predictable count is $8$, and the high-coverage closure count is $8$.  The recovery rate is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{predictable\ count} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count} &= 5,\\

\end{aligned}

The interpretation is local and finite: every missing codon in the row is recovered either by a predictable closure position or by a boundary position of the same tile.  No stronger biological conclusion is licensed by this fact.  In particular, the result is a code-read statement about label-table geometry, not a statement about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of the claim.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, observed as a codon-to-label assignment table.  This contact can test code-read facts and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, folded protein structure, or a universal biological mechanism.  Therefore the supported claim is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile structure for the standard-code label table, with perfect leave-one-codon-out recovery inside the displayed row.  The unsupported promotions are blocked locally.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
