\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite object in this record is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ equipped with the standard-code label table as its only reality contact.  The contact says that a curated standard genetic-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  It does not say that the six-bit geometry is a biological mechanism.  The BEDC-side carrier supplies coordinates, closure, spectrum, and relation data; the biological-side contact supplies only code-layer readback.  Hence the tested layer is $\mathrm{code\_read}$, not translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The verdict is a local tile-boundary statement.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a finite boundary pattern in the standard-code label readback: the $13$-codon row has a punctured-cube shape, a nontrivial spectral signature, low null-model frequencies, complete module assignment, and full leave-one-codon-out recovery under the stated predictable-or-boundary rule.  The result remains deliberately narrow.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only label readback.  Any promotion beyond $\mathrm{code\_read}$ requires a separate reality contact that actually observes the higher layer.
