\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Readback for a Local Codon Tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with a codon represented by its window-six binary display and with labels read from a curated standard genetic-code table.  The tested distinction is local: a tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class are compared inside the codon cube.  The construction is not treated as a claim about translation machinery, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or biological universality.  Those layers require their own contacts; the present contact supplies codon-to-label assignments only.

\paragraph{Residual set and cube shape.}
The residual codon set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In lexicographic codon notation the measured row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
    &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also the punctured-cube row: its face-count profile is $(13,16,4)$, so the local object has $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the finite cube computation.  This is the shape datum used by the leave-one-codon readback, not a statement that the genetic code must arise from this geometry.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinate.}
The associated spectral calculation gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic residual reported for the same row is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius value retained in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers identify the finite coordinate profile used for the local codon tile.  They do not observe a molecular pathway, a folded structure, or a functional phenotype.

\paragraph{Shape against null rows.}
The shape row was compared with $2000$ sampled alternatives under seed $42$.  The sampling required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-row size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite comparisons support the statement that the standard-code row is unusually placed relative to the sampled rows used here.  They do not convert the coordinate pattern into a physical law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and family rows.}
The code-level leave-one experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, $5$ families were reported.  The above-baseline family hit rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial or plastid tables, $1.0$ for the candidate-other family, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate tables.  These are code-table readback statistics.  They do not assert that every organismal translation system, tRNA inventory, protein fold, or phenotype is determined by the six-coordinate tile.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon outcome.}
For the leave-one-codon row itself the measured anchor count is $0$, the boundary count is $5$, the predictable count is $8$, and the high-coverage closure count is $8$.  The recovery rate is $1.0$, and the thresholded recovery-rate row is also $1.0$.  In this chapter the interpretation is therefore narrow: when one codon is withheld from the local readback problem, the finite tile data recovers the tested codon-label row through predictable or boundary behavior in all measured cases.  The absence of anchors is part of the result rather than a defect: the recovery route is boundary-and-closure based, not a claim that a single codon corner carries an independent biological cause.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The $13$ residual codons are partitioned into six named modules.  The module assignment is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and there are no extra module codons, no missing residual codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has at least one predictable or boundary route in the finite classification.  This is the chapter-specific reason that the leave-one-codon claim is ready at the code-read layer: the residual row is not merely counted; it is exhausted by a module classification aligned with the measured predictable-or-boundary condition.

\paragraph{Reality contact and blocked promotions.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or a general mechanism.  Consequently, an amino-acid label sequence obtained from this table is still only a label sequence.  It is not a folded protein structure, not a structure prediction, not an admissibility certificate, and not a function observation.  Any future claim crossing those boundaries must introduce a separate contact at the relevant layer and must be evaluated there rather than imported from the codon-label row.

\paragraph{Chapter verdict.}
The finite data support the claim $h1.leave.one.codon.out$ as a local codon-table readback result.  The strongest finite row is the combination of $R$ having $13$ explicitly listed codons, the punctured-cube profile $(13,16,4)$, the spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, the exact-shape comparison $p=0.0005$, the amino-acid-class lower-tail comparison $p=0.0035$, the code-level hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ above baseline, and the leave-one-codon recovery rate $1.0$.  The result is therefore a finite, code-layer observation about local codon-tile boundary recovery.  It is not promoted beyond that layer.
