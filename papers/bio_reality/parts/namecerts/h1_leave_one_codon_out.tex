\subsection{Leave-one-codon local tile boundary}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code label table. The coordinate display is internal: codons are treated as finite row data in a six-bit window with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The label assignment itself is not generated by the coordinate system. It is supplied by the curated standard-code table, whose role is only to provide codon-to-label readback at the code layer. Thus the chapter tests whether the window-six representation exposes a local boundary-tile pattern for the leave-one-codon problem, while keeping the biological contact at the codon-label level.

\paragraph{Distinguished finite row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In the verified run, the row is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
      &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite object used by the leave-one-codon readback test. It is not asserted to be a law of translation or a universal genetic-code mechanism; it is a concrete local row inside the six-bit display.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row check.}
The associated spectral data give
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, recorded both as the cubic-polynomial value and as the radial-polynomial value. Within the numerical tolerance of the finite computation, this residual is effectively zero. The spectral radius recorded for $M$ is again $0.6752479308830213$. These numbers support the claim that the row is not merely a hand-selected list of codons: it has a reproducible finite spectral profile in the six-bit coordinate model.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape and high-Jaccard significance values are therefore $0.0005$ and $0.0125$, respectively. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$. The corresponding finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These null-model checks make the local shape nontrivial as a coordinate phenomenon, while still leaving the biological interpretation bounded by the code-read contact.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon recovery.}
For the claim $h1.leave.one.codon.out$, the verified run records $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and $8$ high-coverage closure cases. The recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the threshold comparison also recorded as $1.0$. The direct finite-row conclusion is therefore narrow but strong: in this data slice, every target counted by the leave-one-codon test is recovered either as predictable or as boundary-covered inside the local tile geometry. The absence of anchors is part of the result, not an omission: the row is explained through predictable and boundary behavior rather than through a singleton anchor mechanism.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$. The certified module table is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA, UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA, UAG, UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row-size check gives $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and the assigned count is $13$. The lists of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons are all empty. Hence the module split is complete for this finite row. In particular, every module has predictable or boundary support: $\mathrm{agr}$ contributes $AGA,AGG$; $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ contributes $AAA$; $\mathrm{cun}$ contributes $CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU$; $\mathrm{ile\_met}$ contributes $AUA$; $\mathrm{ssn}$ contributes $UCA,UUA$; and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$ contributes $UAA,UAG,UGA$.

These figures are not used to promote the local tile into a global law. They only show that the same finite coordinate apparatus has consistent code-read behavior in nearby leave-one experiments.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The local verdict is that window-six codon coordinates expose a reproducible leave-one-codon boundary tile over the curated standard-code readback. The support is finite and concrete: $|R|=13$, the punctured-cube $f$-vector is $(13,16,4)$, the leave-one-codon recovery rate is $1.0$, the module assignment covers all $13$ row codons, and the shape-null values are small in the recorded experiments. The cannot-claim boundary is equally local. No translation realization follows from this chapter. No folded protein structure follows. No physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only code-layer readback. Any claim about translation, folding, physical realization, function, or a universal mechanism would require a separate reality contact designed for that higher layer.
