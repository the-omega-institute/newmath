\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Local Tile Boundary}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite claim concerns a window-six presentation of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, and the readout is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  In this record, local tile geometry is allowed to organize a finite codon-label split, but the label table remains the empirical contact.  The claim is therefore a code-layer statement: if one codon is withheld from the local window, the remaining finite geometry and label information recover the withheld codon position within the declared local boundary class.  It is not a claim that the geometry is a biological law, a translation mechanism, a folding principle, a physical-admissibility criterion, or a functional explanation.

\paragraph{Finite support set.}
The exceptional region $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row also records that this region has the punctured-cube face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object used by the claim is not the full codon cube by itself, but a $13$-vertex subconfiguration with $16$ local edges and $4$ two-dimensional faces.  This is the region in which the leave-one-codon experiment is evaluated.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The finite $M$-shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$.  The sampling process made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those controls, exactly $1$ exact shape match occurred, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} = 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
For high-overlap comparison, $25$ controls had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} = 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second class-preserving null model also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ for size at most the observed value.  The corresponding null probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support the statement that the finite shape is not typical under the stated finite null procedures.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon result.}

Every listed module has a predictable or boundary status.  This complete assignment is the finite bookkeeping fact that lets the claim be read as a local codon-window result rather than as an informal impression about the standard code.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The empirical contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, understood only as a codon-to-label assignment.  It supplies the labels needed for code readout and local split checks.  It does not observe translation in a cell, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore amino-acid label readback is not protein-structure readback.  Any statement about translation realization, folding, physical feasibility, biological function, or a universal biological principle would require a separate observation at that layer.  Within the data given here, the strongest admissible conclusion is that the window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile pattern in the curated standard-code label table, with perfect recovery in the stated leave-one-codon row and with the displayed finite null comparisons as quantitative controls.
