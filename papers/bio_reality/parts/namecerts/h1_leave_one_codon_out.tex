\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Boundary Recovery in the Standard Code}
\label{sec:bioreality-leave-one-codon-boundary-recovery}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-reading contact, not as a mechanism for translation or protein formation.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube obtained from a binary display of the three nucleotide positions.  The observed labels are the curated standard-code assignments of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The finite row asks whether the window-six coordinate geometry detects a local boundary pattern when one codon is held out and then read only against the code-label table.

\paragraph{Recovered boundary set.}
The distinguished residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the recorded run, it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies this set with a punctured-cube pattern whose face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite geometry is not merely a list of rare codons: it carries a small cell structure with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four square faces after the puncture has been imposed.  The measured leave-one-codon-out row reports no anchor codons, five boundary codons, eight high-coverage closure codons, and eight predictable codons.  Its recovery rate is $1.0$, and the reported threshold comparison is also $1.0$.

\paragraph{Module-level localization.}
The thirteen residual codons are completely assigned to six named local modules.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},&
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},&
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},&
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every listed module contains a codon class that is either predictable or lies on the local boundary.  This is the strongest finite claim supported here: the standard-code table, when viewed through the six-coordinate codon display, gives a complete local partition of the residual thirteen-codon set into boundary-relevant modules.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The spectrum row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The recorded cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported as the radial polynomial value, so the numerical identity is satisfied to near machine precision.  The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, found one exact shape match, and found $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite sampling probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,&
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null run preserving amino-acid classes also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,&
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite rows support non-random local shape at the code-label level, while leaving mechanism, biochemical realization, and organism-level universality outside the measured contact.

\paragraph{Interpretive boundary.}
The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments.  It does not supply ribosomal dynamics, tRNA abundance, folding observations, physical admissibility data, organismal fitness, or functional assays.  Therefore the present finite pattern may be stated as a code-reading and local-boundary result only.  No translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from these rows unless an additional contact explicitly measures that higher layer.  In particular, reading an amino-acid label sequence from codons is not an observation of a folded protein, and a local tile split in the six-coordinate codon cube is not by itself a law of biology.

For claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.codon.out}$, the finite rows are coherent and numerically specific.  The residual set has $13$ codons, a punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, complete six-module assignment, eight predictable or high-coverage codons, five boundary codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The spectrum and null rows give independent finite checks, including $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, exact-shape probability $0.0005$, high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$, and amino-acid-class lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  The chapter therefore records a ready finite code-layer result with a strict stop at the code-label boundary.
