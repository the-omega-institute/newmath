\subsection{Leave-one-codon boundary test for the window-six codon tile}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with the standard-code label readback.  The biological contact used here is only the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it does not by itself observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The row is therefore a code-layer test.  Its question is whether the window-six coordinate system exposes a local boundary tile that remains recoverable when a single codon is withheld from the observed label data.

\paragraph{Residual set and punctured-cube shape.}
The residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
      &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same computation identifies this residual set with a punctured local cube whose face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row is not merely a collection of label exceptions.  It has an explicit local cell structure: thirteen label corners, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four local two-faces inside the window-six display.  This is the geometric object tested by the leave-one-codon experiment.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated spectral row separates the main set and residual set numerically.  The verified values are

\begin{aligned}

\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic radial relation is satisfied to numerical residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the reported spectral-radius value is $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers are used only as finite-row coordinates of the codon-tile object.  They do not promote the object into a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Null-model calibration.}
Against the finite shape null with $N=2000$ sampled alternatives and seed $42$, the exact shape count is $1$, so the exact-shape probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The number of high-Jaccard matches with threshold $0.9$ is $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite row is therefore calibrated against both an unconstrained shape null and an amino-acid-class-preserving null, while staying within code-read evidence.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon rows.}
The broader leave-one-code row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon row sharpens this to the single-codon withholding test: it has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The thresholded recovery flag is also $1.0$.  In words, every codon recovered by the row is recovered through predictable closure or through the declared boundary channel, with no anchor-only contribution.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The residual codons partition into six named modules, and the module accounting is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, there are no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has a predictable-or-boundary witness.  The associated code-family row reports five families.  Their hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite evidence supports a local code-read claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a boundary-tile structure whose residual set is a thirteen-codon punctured cube, whose spectral row is numerically stable, whose null-model probabilities are small in the reported finite tests, and whose leave-one-codon recovery rate is $1.0$.  The claim stops there.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label readback only.  No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal biological law, or mechanism follows from these rows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
