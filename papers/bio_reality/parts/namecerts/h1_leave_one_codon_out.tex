\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery inside a window-six codon tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite label readout on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The cube coordinate is not taken as a biological law; it is a controlled display in which codons may be compared by local face incidence, singleton-corner behavior, and three-point label classes. The biological contact is the curated standard-code table, whose observed content is only the assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels. Thus the finite test can ask whether a withheld codon label is recovered by the local tile, but it cannot by itself assert translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Recovered codon row.}
The leave-one-codon-out run for claim \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out} evaluated the codons in the recovered boundary set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit reports $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube check gives the same ordered set with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
In the direct leave-one-codon-out row, the run found $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, and $0$ anchor codons. The high-coverage closure count is $8$, and the reported recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\_rate}=1.0,
\qquad
\mathrm{recovery\_rate\_above\_threshold}=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The intended reading is narrow: every tested withheld codon in this local row is either internally predictable or boundary-classified by the window-six tile, but that success remains a code-label fact.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The module classification is complete on the same $13$ codons: the assigned count is $13$, with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The six finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has at least one predictable or boundary member, so the local test is not a statement about one accidental codon. It is a finite decomposition of the recovered row into biologically recognizable code-label modules. The table contact still supplies only labels: the fact that $\mathrm{CUN}$ and $\mathrm{UUA}$ are read through leucine-related code notation, or that $\mathrm{AGR}$ is a paired codon module, does not create structural, translational, or functional evidence.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The associated spectrum row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the numerical residual is effectively zero at the recorded tolerance. The shape-significance control used $N=2000$ random samples with seed $42$. It found one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\qquad
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ high-Jaccard matches. The sample required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which records the finite sampling pressure rather than a biological mechanism. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, and the lower-tail value was $0.0035$. These controls support that the row is a nontrivial finite code-layer pattern, not that the coordinate geometry is a physical cause.

\paragraph{Relation to leave-one-code-out evidence.}
The broader leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets. It reports a baseline hit rate of $0.19821739130434785$, a hit rate above baseline of $0.9841269841269841$, a hit rate inside $M-T$ of $0.9365079365079365$, and a hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary of $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate, over $5$ families. These rows give context for the codon-level recovery: the local boundary behavior is consistent with a larger finite code-read phenomenon, but the chapter does not promote this to a universal rule about organisms.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and boundary.}
The claim is ready at the code-read layer. The window-six codon coordinate exposes a local boundary-tile pattern in which the recovered row has $13$ codons, the punctured-cube vector is $(13,16,4)$, the module assignment is complete, and the leave-one-codon-out recovery rate is $1.0$. The curated standard-code table is sufficient to check codon-to-label readback and local split behavior. It is not sufficient to observe protein production, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, function, or a universal biological mechanism. Any later assertion across those boundaries must attach a separate empirical contact of the appropriate kind before it can be stated as a BioReality result.
