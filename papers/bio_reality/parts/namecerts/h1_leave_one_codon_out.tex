\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with the biological readout restricted to the code layer: each codon is read as a curated amino-acid or stop label, and no claim is made about ribosomal realization, folding, physical viability, biological activity, mechanism, or universality.  The finite row under test is the leave-one-codon-out claim $h1.leave.one.codon.out$: after one codon is withheld from the local labelled configuration, the remaining window-six data determine whether the missing codon lies in a predictable closure part or on a boundary part of the local tile.

\begin{aligned}

\paragraph{Leave-one recovery result.}
For the direct leave-one-codon-out row, the finite recovery audit reports $anchor\_count=0$, $boundary\_count=5$, $closure\_coverage\_high=8$, and $predictable\_count=8$. This is a strong finite-row statement, but it is deliberately local: it says that the six-coordinate codon display and the curated labels expose a boundary-and-closure split for this row, not that biology must realize this geometry as a law.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-layer readback and the local codon-tile label split.  It does not observe translation dynamics, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Therefore an amino-acid label sequence read from this table is not a folded protein, not a functional molecule, and not a proof that the codon cube governs organisms.  Any such higher-layer statement would require an additional contact of the appropriate kind.  The present row is ready only as a finite, code-layer certificate: in the given residual set $R$, the leave-one-codon-out computation has complete module assignment and full recovery through predictable or boundary status.
