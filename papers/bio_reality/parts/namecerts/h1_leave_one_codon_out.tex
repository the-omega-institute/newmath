\paragraph{Finite object.}
The finite object in this record is the standard-code codon table read through a six-bit codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with codons treated as finite addresses and with curated amino-acid or stop labels supplied by an external standard-code table.  The BEDC-side distinctions used here are a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological object is therefore not translation itself, and not protein structure, but the finite code-layer table that assigns each codon to a label.

\begin{aligned}

Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it is a finite local shape inside the codon cube.  The present chapter reads this shape as a code-layer boundary tile, not as a biological mechanism.

These are finite sampling facts about the row and its class-preserving controls.  They support the claim that the local tile is unusually structured relative to the specified nulls.  They do not identify a physical cause.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table.  Its observed fact is that a standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels, with resolution at codon label readback.  This contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splitting.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, folded protein structure, or any universal mechanism.  The finite row may therefore be reported as a local code-layer tile whose label split is supported by the curated table, but the contact itself blocks promotion beyond the code layer.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from this chapter unless a separate contact supplies that layer.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure; a local codon tile is not a global biological law; and window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function.  The chapter-specific claim is exactly this: in the standard-code readback, the row $R$ of $13$ codons forms a punctured-cube boundary tile with complete module accounting, significant finite-null behavior, spectral consistency at the recorded tolerance, and leave-one-codon-out recovery rate $1.0$ at the code layer.
