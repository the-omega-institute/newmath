\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Recovery at a Local Codon Tile Boundary}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
This record concerns a six-coordinate codon display of the standard code, where each codon is treated as a point of the codon cube $Q_6$ and the only allowed readout is the curated standard-code assignment from codons to amino-acid or stop labels. The biological object is therefore not translation as a process and not protein structure as an outcome. It is the finite codon-label table itself. The row tests whether a leave-one-codon deletion can be recovered from local coordinate, closure, spectral, and relation data without promoting that recovery beyond the code-read layer.

The distinguished codon set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check reports the same thirteen codons and the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not using a vague codon neighborhood: the finite object is a specified thirteen-point subset with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four local square faces inside the six-bit codon coordinate system.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon recovery.}
For the claim identifier $h1.leave.one.codon.out$, the finite run reports $0$ anchor cases, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases. The recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{recovery\_rate}=1.0,
\qquad
\operatorname{recovery\_rate\_above\_threshold}=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this row, every recoverable leave-one-codon target is therefore accounted for by the local closure-or-boundary test used by the packet. The number $0$ for anchors is important: the recovery is not being explained by fixed singleton anchors. The active behavior is concentrated in boundary membership and closure coverage.

The module classification gives the finite partition explicitly:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the reported lists of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons are all empty. The row also states that every module has a predictable-or-boundary representative. Hence the local result is not a statement about a single exceptional codon; it covers the six displayed modules without leaving a residual codon outside the module accounting.

\paragraph{Code-table comparison.}
The broader leave-one-code-out comparison supplies $23$ evaluated tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the reported hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$. At the family level, five families are reported. The above-baseline rates are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.

These numbers support a narrow reading. The local coordinate object is useful for code-label recovery under leave-one deletions, and the boundary extension improves agreement with the curated table relative to the baseline. They do not show that the codon geometry is a mechanism of translation. They also do not show that a label sequence folds, that a molecule is physically admissible, or that a biological function has been realized.

\paragraph{Spectral and null checks.}
The spectral row records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with radial cubic residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$ and the same tolerance-level value for the cubic polynomial check. The row therefore supplies a numerically stable spectral summary for the finite codon object, but the spectrum remains an internal diagnostic of the coordinate construction. It is not, by itself, a biological measurement.

The shape-significance null uses $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ duplicate retries, $1$ exact shape match, and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$. The corresponding probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null also uses $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It reports observed $M$ size $20$, exact match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$. These finite-row nulls make the displayed local shape non-generic under the stated randomizations. They do not replace an external biological contact; they only constrain how surprising the code-layer pattern is under the given finite comparisons.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The sole contact used here is the curated standard-code table. Its observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels. Its resolution is codon label readback. Its known limitation is that it is a table-level contact, not an observation of mechanism, universality, folding, physical feasibility, or function. The boundary probe attached to the row therefore has a simple scientific role: a local codon-tile pattern may support code-read checks, but any claim about translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity requires an additional contact of the appropriate kind.

Consequently, the verdict of this finite row is local and conditional. The thirteen-codon set $R$, its punctured-cube face data, the complete six-module classification, the recovery rate $1.0$, the high above-baseline rates, the spectral values, and the null-model probabilities together support a code-layer leave-one-codon recovery pattern. The same data also explicitly block higher-layer promotion. Amino-acid label readback is not folded protein structure, CUN/UUR wording is not structure evidence, and a local tile in $Q_6$ is not a global biological law.
