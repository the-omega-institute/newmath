\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the standard-code codon display.  The row treats the curated standard genetic-code table as an external contact for codon-to-label readback only.  The internal BEDC structure is the coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation structure of the finite codon cube; the amino-acid or stop labels are not derived as a biological mechanism.  They enter through the curated table contact, whose resolution is codon label readback.

The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The finite codons in $R$ are exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same row and records the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).

$$
Thus the local object is not a free narrative cluster: it is a specific finite subconfiguration of the codon cube with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional faces, and four two-dimensional faces.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}
The leave-one-codon-out row for this claim has experiment run identifier $\mathrm{e9e1a6e41633}$.  It records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  In finite terms, every held-out codon in the tested row is recovered by the permitted predictable-or-boundary classification, but the recovery decomposes into two different local roles: eight codons are predictable through the high-coverage closure side, and five codons are boundary codons.  The absence of anchors is material: the result is not saying that a small set of fixed codon anchors mechanically determines the table.  It is saying that the tested thirteen-codon row is exhausted by closure-predictable and boundary behavior inside the window-six coordinate system.

The module classification is complete on the same thirteen codons.  The six finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit records $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Hence the leave-one-codon-out result is not merely a scalar recovery-rate statement; it is compatible with a complete finite partition of the row into biologically named local modules.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported claim is deliberately local.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a finite boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and the leave-one-codon-out check recovers the tested codons through predictable or boundary status with recovery rate $1.0$.  The curated code table supplies the reality contact for labels, not a mechanism.  Therefore no translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from this row.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer readback.  A separate higher-layer contact would be required before any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality could be made.
