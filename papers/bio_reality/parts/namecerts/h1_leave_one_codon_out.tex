\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery in the codon cube}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim and finite setting.}
The claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.codon.out}$ concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinates read as codon positions and with the observed label map supplied by a curated standard genetic-code table.  The row does not assert that the cube geometry causes biology.  It records that, after one codon label is withheld, the local tile data around the withheld site can recover the missing code-layer label in the tested cases, while the interpretation remains confined to codon-to-label readback.

The distinguished mutable set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite set is reported as a punctured-cube object with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object has thirteen codon vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces.  These numbers are the finite combinatorial substrate for the leave-one-codon-out test.

The cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the recorded spectrum is numerically stable at the displayed precision.  The $M$-shape significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling log also reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$, and the reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These controls make the row more than a bare visual coincidence: the finite tile shape and its class-preserving comparison both place the observed object in a small tail of the tested null ensembles.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out result.}
For the target claim itself, the leave-one-codon-out row reports no anchor cases, five boundary cases, eight high-coverage closure cases, and eight predictable cases:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\_count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\_count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\_count}&=8.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recovery rate is $1.0$, and the threshold comparison also records $1.0$.  Within this finite row, every tested withheld codon that enters the predictable class is recovered by the local code-layer procedure.  The stronger interpretation is deliberately not taken: the result is a recovery statement about codon labels in a curated table, not a statement about biochemical realization.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen mutable codons are partitioned into six named modules, and the classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, $13$ codons are assigned, the lists of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons are all empty.  The module table is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has a predictable-or-boundary status in the row.  This matters because the recovery evidence is not scattered over unrelated codons: the complete module assignment shows that the mutable set is exhausted by the recorded local classes.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It can test code-layer readback and a local split in the codon tile.  It cannot test translation as a molecular process, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the admissible conclusion is narrow: the six-bit codon cube exposes a finite local boundary-and-closure pattern for codon-label recovery, with $R$ as the thirteen-codon punctured-cube set and with perfect recovery among the predictable cases in this row.  No claim about protein realization, folding, physical constraints, function, or a universal biological law follows without a separate observation that directly measures that higher layer.
