\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the ordinary codon display and then compared with a curated standard-code label table.  The reality contact used here is only a codon-to-label table: it says that the standard genetic code assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  It does not assert translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  The BioReality claim is therefore deliberately local: window-six coordinates expose a recoverable boundary-tile pattern for held-out codons, while the label readback remains an external reality contact.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}

The recorded result has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, and predictable count $8$.  The recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the above-threshold recovery rate is also $1.0$.  The absence of anchors matters: the recovery is not being reported as a trivial singleton-corner lookup.  It is a closure-and-boundary recovery statement for this finite tile.
