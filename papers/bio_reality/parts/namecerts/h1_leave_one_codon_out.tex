\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.}
The supported claim is exactly a code-read claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile split whose labels are read back through the curated standard-code table.  The contact does not test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any claim across those boundaries requires a separate reality contact that measures the corresponding layer.  With the present data, the correct conclusion is that the finite standard-code label table aligns with a local codon-tile recovery pattern at the code-read layer, while higher-layer biological interpretations remain blocked.
