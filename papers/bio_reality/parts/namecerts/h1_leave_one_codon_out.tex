\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Boundary Tile in the Standard Code}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the finite display in which each codon has a coordinate, a label readback, and local adjacency data.  The external contact is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it is used only at that label-readback resolution.  In this chapter the relevant distinguished set is the thirteen-codon boundary set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit gives $|R|=13$.  The same row identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube shaped object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the geometric object under discussion is a local tile-like boundary inside the codon-coordinate display, not a whole theory of translation.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape evidence.}
The coordinate computation also gives a finite spectral signature.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, and the same value is reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the numerical signature is internally coherent at the displayed tolerance.  The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match and twenty-five matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The corresponding finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$ and seed $42$ found no exact shape matches.  Its lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, with observed $|M|=20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the displayed local boundary is not a routine random artifact under the finite nulls that were actually run.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe is the chapter-specific test.  Removing one codon at a time, the finite row reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and high closure coverage $8$.  The reported recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the threshold comparison also equal to $1.0$.  In code-read terms, every tested codon in the local family is either recovered as predictable by the closure calculation or accounted for as a boundary case.  This is the precise content of the positive row: the local codon-coordinate tile has enough closure structure to explain the finite leave-one-codon-out split at the label-table level.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module row assigns all thirteen elements of $R$ into six named finite modules.  The complete classification is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The audit gives $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  It also states that every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness.  This matters because the leave-one-codon-out result is not concentrated in a single accidental row: it respects the full module partition of the displayed boundary set.

\paragraph{Related leave-one-code-out evidence.}
A broader leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its ordinary hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M-T$, and $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M-T$ plus boundary.  Against the reported baseline hit rate $0.19821739130434785$, the above-baseline hit rate was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the finite run found five families: alternative yeast had above-baseline hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had above-baseline hit rate $1.0$.  These rows are supportive context for the codon-specific probe, but the chapter claim remains the narrower leave-one-codon-out claim.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite evidence supports a code-read statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and the curated table supplies the external label contact.  The result is intentionally local.  It does not claim that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or universality of the standard code.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only label readback.  Any step beyond code labels requires a separate reality contact at the relevant layer.
