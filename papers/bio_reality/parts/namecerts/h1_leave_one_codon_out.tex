\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}

The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated codon-label bridge assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The contact is deliberately narrow: it tests codon-label readback and local tile splitting, but it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite evidence supports only a code-read claim: curated standard-code labels interact with a window-six local boundary tile, and the leave-one-codon-out row has complete reported recovery at rate $1.0$.  No translation process follows from this.  No folded protein structure follows from amino-acid label readback.  No physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from the punctured-cube geometry or from the spectral row.  Any such higher-layer claim would require a separate reality contact with its own observable, resolution, noise model, and failure condition.  In this record, the local codon tile remains a finite code-layer object, not a global law of biology.
