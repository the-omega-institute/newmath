\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The external contact is the curated standard-code table.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments at codon-label readback resolution.  This contact can test code-read claims and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the supported BioReality claim is exactly code-layer readback: the curated table aligns with a finite local tile pattern in $Q_6$, and the leave-one-codon-out row recovers held-out codons at rate $1.0$ within the predictable-or-boundary classification.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
No higher-layer biological conclusion follows from these rows alone.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, protein structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any claim crossing from code-read into translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biology requires a separate reality contact designed for that layer.
