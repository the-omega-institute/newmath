\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery on the window-six codon cube}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate setting.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display. The object under test is not translation itself, nor a chemical model of the ribosome, but the finite relation obtained when the $64$ codon positions are labelled by the curated standard-code amino-acid-or-stop table. The claim is that a local window-six coordinate construction isolates a small boundary-tile pattern whose missing codon labels are recoverable from finite neighborhood data, while the labels themselves remain supplied by the curated code table. Thus the contact is a code-layer contact: it reads codon labels, not protein structure, physical possibility, organismal function, or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for local label-split checks. It is not a contact with translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, protein function, organism-level fitness, or a universal biological law. Consequently, the recovery result is a code-read result: it permits the statement that this finite coordinate construction recovers the tested omitted labels in the specified local tile, and it forbids the stronger statement that the tile is itself a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
For the claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.codon.out}$, the finite evidence is internally aligned. The distinguished row has $13$ codons, complete module assignment, five boundary positions, eight high-coverage positions, eight predictable positions, and recovery rate $1.0$. The auxiliary rows give a punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral values, significant finite null-model contrasts, and high related code-table hit rates. The chapter-level conclusion is therefore that window-six codon coordinates expose a reproducible local boundary-and-recovery pattern in the standard-code label table. No conclusion about translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows without an additional contact that directly observes that higher layer.
