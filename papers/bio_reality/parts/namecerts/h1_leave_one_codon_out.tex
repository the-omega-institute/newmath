\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery in the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The experiment is evaluated on the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, with readback supplied by a curated standard-code table. The contact is only a codon-label contact: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label, and it does not observe translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Within that boundary, the finite row isolates a distinguished residual set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$. The same row reports that $R$ has the punctured-cube face profile
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons but a finite tile with vertices, edges, and two-faces in the codon cube.

These numbers support a finite local-tile anomaly at the code-read layer, not a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}
For the claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.codon.out}$ itself, the finite row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, and $8$ cases with high closure coverage. The recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}

Thus every tested leave-one-codon-out target in this row is recovered either as predictable by the local closure structure or as a boundary case of the tile. The absence of anchors is important: the result is not claiming that a fixed codon anchor forces the table. It says that, after one codon is withheld, the window-six tile supplies enough local closure or boundary information to recover the code-read label behavior in the tested finite row.

Each module has a predictable-or-boundary witness. This is the strongest finite-row form of the claim: the punctured local tile does not leave a stray codon outside the module accounting, and the recovery statistic is complete on the supplied row.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The chapter therefore records a code-read result about a finite codon-coordinate tile. It may be read as evidence that the window-six display exposes a local boundary structure in the standard-code label table. It may not be promoted to a claim about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or universality. Amino-acid label readback is not protein structure. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Any future crossing from this code-read layer into translation, folding, physical admissibility, or function would require a separate reality contact that directly observes that higher layer.
