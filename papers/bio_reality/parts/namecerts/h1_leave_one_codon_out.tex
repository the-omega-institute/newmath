\paragraph{Local boundary.}  The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, understood as a codon-to-label readback contact.  It observes that codons receive amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a biochemical mechanism, or a universal law governing all organisms.  Consequently the claim is intentionally narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary pattern in the code-label layer, with recovery rate $1.0$ for the leave-one-codon row, but no higher-layer biological conclusion follows without a separate experiment or curated contact that directly measures that higher layer.
