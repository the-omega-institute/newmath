\subsection{Leave-one-codon recovery inside the window-six code readout}
\label{sec:codon-window-leave-one-codon-recovery}

\paragraph{Finite display.}
The object recorded here is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, where a codon is represented by a six-bit display and then read against the curated standard-code label table.  The relevant finite row is not the whole table but the recovered boundary region $R$.  In the verified run, $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a cubical subcomplex of the window-six display, this same row has face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the calculation is not a free verbal pattern: it fixes a concrete thirteen-codon boundary set, sixteen local adjacencies, and four two-faces in the finite coordinate geometry.

\paragraph{Spectral signature.}
The same finite row carries a numerical separation between the ambient local object and the recovered boundary row.  The recorded spectral values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic residual for the radial calculation is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the numerical witness is stable at the displayed tolerance rather than being a loose qualitative statement.  The row $M$ itself is present in the run through its size and statistical tests: the observed $M$-size is $20$, and the exact-shape comparison against $2000$ sampled alternatives produced one exact match.  This gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ samples reaching Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The sampler used seed $42$, made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and rejected $55377$ duplicate retries before the reported sample count was obtained.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class null check.}
The label-preserving null model gives a second finite comparison.  With $N=2000$ sampled tables and seed $42$, the exact shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, and the observed $M$-size was again $20$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a code-label calculation, not a claim about chemistry.  It says that the finite window-six row is unusual under the stated class-preserving reshuffling, while the interpretation remains restricted to codon labels and their local coordinate relations.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon test.}
The claim tested in this row is leave-one-codon recovery.  The experiment reports $predictable\_count=8$, $boundary\_count=5$, $anchor\_count=0$, and $closure\_coverage\_high=8$.  The recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the above-threshold recovery rate is also $1.0$.  In finite terms, every held-out codon in the tested boundary row is recovered either by the predictable part of the local closure pattern or by the boundary class; none is recorded as an anchor-only recovery.  The result is therefore a sharp local statement: the window-six coordinate system recovers the thirteen-codon boundary row in the leave-one-codon protocol.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification is complete on the recovered row.  The run reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has either a predictable member or a boundary member, so the module partition covers the whole thirteen-codon row without remainder.  This matters because the local recovery result is not concentrated in a single codon family; it crosses arginine, lysine, leucine, isoleucine-methionine, serine-leucine, and stop-tryptophan portions of the standard label table.

The broader leave-one-code calculation evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  Family-level aggregation used five families.  The family hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate codes.  These numbers place the leave-one-codon row inside a larger code-read phenomenon, but the present chapter uses only the finite codon row as its direct claim.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  That contact says that codons are assigned to amino-acid labels or stop labels.  It supplies the label readout needed to test local splits of the window-six display, and it does not supply a translation apparatus, a folded structure, an admissibility criterion, a function assay, or a universal mechanism.  The contact therefore supports code-layer readback and blocks promotion to stronger biological layers.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite data support the following bounded statement.  In the standard-code label table, the window-six codon coordinates single out a thirteen-codon boundary row $R$ with face vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral profile with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, statistically uncommon shape behavior under the stated finite null models, and complete leave-one-codon recovery at rate $1.0$ in the reported protocol.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons and leaves no unassigned element.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
Nothing in this row establishes that biological translation is produced by the window-six geometry. Amino-acid label readback is still only label readback.
