\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record studies a six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$ whose rows are the $64$ standard codons read as finite bit positions.  The biological readout used here is only the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The internal test asks whether a local tile in this finite cube carries enough boundary information to recover held-out codon rows at the code-label layer.  The test does not treat the cube as a biological mechanism; it treats the cube as a finite coordinate device whose labels are supplied by a curated table.

\paragraph{Distinguished row set.}
The recurrent codon row set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified row order it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check reports the same $13$ codons and the face data $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the observed tile is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it has a finite incidence profile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces.  The module partition also closes on these rows: $\mathrm{AGR}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{AR/Lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{CUN}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{Ile/Met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{SSN}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{Stop/Trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The module audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing module codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon outcome.}
For the codon-level leave-one-out test, the verified run gives anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Equivalently, every held-out row admitted by this local recovery criterion is recovered within the accepted code-layer channel:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{boundary\ count} &=5,\\
\mathrm{predictable\ count} &=8,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high} &=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate} &=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The absence of anchors is important: the support is not coming from a fixed singleton seed that trivially names the answer.  The reported recovery is instead carried by boundary and closure behavior inside the finite codon tile.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The spectral summary gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, matching the reported tolerance-scale satisfaction of the cubic relation.  The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$; it needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$ and $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving $p=0.0$ for exact shape in the finite sample and $p=0.0035$ for the lower tail.  These numbers support a finite non-randomness claim for the displayed code-layer pattern, not a claim about molecular causation.

\paragraph{Code-table readback.}
The wider leave-one-code-out comparison evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ with boundary rows included was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast, across $5$ families.  These comparisons are consistent with the codon-level result: the finite tile has strong local predictive organization when judged against curated label tables.

\paragraph{Biological scope.}
The supported conclusion is deliberately narrow.  Window-six coordinates expose a local boundary tile for codon labels in the standard-code table, and the leave-one-codon run recovers the accepted rows at rate $1.0$ under the stated finite criterion.  The curated table supplies codon-to-label contact only.  No translation process, folded structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, universal mechanism, or organism-wide law follows from these rows unless a separate observation supplies that higher-layer contact.  In particular, amino-acid label readback is not protein structure, and the CUN/UUR or tRNA-Leu wording belongs to a different contact route unless paired with organism-matched evidence.  The present chapter therefore records a code-layer finite regularity: a local tile in $Q_6$ aligns with standard-code labels and remains bounded to that layer.
