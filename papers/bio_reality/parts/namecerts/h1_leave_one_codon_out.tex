\paragraph{Object and coordinate scale.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table, read only at the code-label layer. The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, where each codon is displayed as a six-bit word after choosing the usual two-bit presentation of the four nucleotide letters. The relevant distinctions are local: a tile face in the cube, a singleton corner label, and a three-point label class. These distinctions are not asserted to be a biochemical mechanism. They give a finite coordinate screen on which a curated codon-to-label table can be queried.

The punctured region singled out by the finite run has cardinality $13$. Written in codon notation, the region is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports the cubical face data
$$
\begin{aligned}

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only biological contact used in this chapter is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. That contact is sufficient for code-layer readback: it can say whether the local tile, singleton corner, and three-point class match the displayed codon labels. It is not a record of translation dynamics, molecular folding, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, or a universal generative mechanism.

Accordingly, the conclusion is deliberately narrow. The window-six coordinates expose a local boundary pattern in the standard-code label table. The row supports a finite leave-one-codon recovery statement at the code-read layer, with $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, complete module accounting over $13$ codons, and recovery rate $1.0$ under the stated criterion. Nothing in this chapter licenses a statement that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity. Nothing here turns an amino-acid label sequence into folded protein structure. Nothing here establishes physical admissibility, function, translation realization, or universality. Any such stronger statement would require a separate contact of the appropriate kind, with its own finite observations.
