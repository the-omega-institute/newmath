\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Boundary Readback}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite object is the six-bit codon display of the standard-code table.  Each row is a codon corner in the codon cube $Q_6$, and the only external contact used here is the curated codon-to-label table for the standard genetic code.  Thus the readback layer is $\mathrm{code\_read}$: the contact can test whether a codon label is recovered or locally split, but it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any necessity claim about DNA obeying this geometry.  A codon label is treated as a finite label attached to a cube corner, not as a realized protein state.

\paragraph{Distinguished boundary set.}
The distinguished row set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is reported as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a local tile statement: it says that these $13$ codon corners and their induced faces form the observed boundary display inside $Q_6$.  It does not say that this tile is a law of translation, a structural constraint on proteins, or a physical admissibility criterion.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The claim supported by these rows is narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code label table, and the leave-one-codon-out row recovers the tested labels at rate $1.0$ by predictable closure or boundary support.  The claim does not state that the local codon tile is a global biological law.  It does not state that window-six geometry alone is translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  It does not identify amino-acid label-sequence readback with folded protein structure.  Any assertion crossing those boundaries requires a separate contact with the corresponding layer.
