\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery inside a local window-six codon tile}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The BEDC carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a finite standard-code label table.  The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The experiment isolates the codons whose code-layer behavior is not already absorbed by the local window-six description.  The verified residual row is the thirteen-codon set
$\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons in the expected row.  As a finite cubical shape, this row is a punctured cube with verified $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object under discussion is not an open-ended biological mechanism.  It is a finite local codon-row geometry whose labels are supplied by a curated standard-code bridge.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out test.}
The leave-one-codon-out audit removes one codon label at a time and asks whether the remaining window-six geometry recovers the omitted code-layer placement as predictable or boundary-determined.  The verified run reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\_count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\_count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{predictable\_count}&=8,\\
\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\_rate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding threshold check also returns $1.0$.  In finite-row terms, all tested omissions are recovered at the code-read level, but the recovery splits into eight predictable positions and five boundary positions rather than into anchor positions.  The absence of anchors is important: the tile behaves as a local boundary-and-closure witness, not as a universal source of codon meaning.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The residual geometry is accompanied by a spectral control.  The verified values are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically consistent with the stated finite spectral relation at the recorded tolerance.  A separate shape-significance run sampled $N=2000$ null rows with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The distinct-sample attempt count was $57377$, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  These numbers support the claim that the observed local shape is not a typical draw under that finite null sampler, while still leaving the interpretation at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a stronger negative control for the same finite-row phenomenon.  In $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, the exact-shape match count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, and the observed $M$ size was $20$.  The recorded exact-shape $p$-value is $0.0$ at this sample resolution, and the lower-tail $p$-value is $0.0035$.  This says that the local tile survives a label-class-preserving comparison as a finite code-table pattern.  It does not say that the tile is a biochemical cause of translation, folding, physical admissibility, or function.

\paragraph{Code-table generalization checks.}
The broader leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the verified above-baseline hit rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, across $5$ families.  These figures are consistent with the leave-one-codon-out result: local boundary structure is useful for code-layer readback across curated tables, but the evidence remains table-mediated.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification accounts for every codon in the thirteen-codon residual row.  The six modules are
$\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$,
$\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$,
$\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$,
$\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$,
$\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$,
and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.
The completeness check reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Moreover, every listed module has either predictable or boundary status.  This is the finite-row closure behind the claim: the local tile is not merely a scattered set of codons, but a fully assigned residual row with module-wise predictable-or-boundary coverage.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the correct conclusion is narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, with leave-one-codon-out recovery rate $1.0$ and complete thirteen-row module accounting.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that evidence.
