\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback layer.}
The row is a finite code-read experiment on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is treated only as a displayed coordinate in the standard-code label table, with the biological contact restricted to codon-to-label readback.  The distinguished residual set has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite set is observed as a punctured cube with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the local object under study is not the whole codon table, but a finite boundary-tile carrier inside $Q_6$ whose labels are read from the curated standard genetic-code table.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{predictable}(R)&=8,\\
\operatorname{boundary}(R)&=5,\\

$$

This is a finite-row statement about label readback in the observed table, not a statement about biochemical realization.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The reality contact is the curated standard-code table: it assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code readback and a local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any necessity claim saying that DNA must obey this geometry.  Amino-acid label readback is still only label readback.  No fold, structural order, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from this row unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.  The appropriate conclusion is therefore finite and local: in the standard-code label table, the thirteen-codon residual carrier forms a window-six boundary tile whose leave-one-codon-out recovery is complete at the recorded code-read layer, while higher biological claims remain blocked.
