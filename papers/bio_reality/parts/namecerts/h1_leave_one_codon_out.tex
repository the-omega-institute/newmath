\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Recovery at the Codon Boundary}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The carrier for this row is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code label table.  The construction is not a biochemical mechanism: it is a finite coordinate display in which a codon is represented as a six-bit point, local faces are inspected as tiles, and the external table supplies only amino-acid or stop labels.  The reality contact is therefore code-layer readback.  It can test whether a codon-label split is organized by the local window-six geometry, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it is a finite subcomplex of the codon cube with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces under the local tile representation used by this paper.

\paragraph{Spectral row signature.}
The finite spectral readout separates the row $R$ from the ambient local shape.  The reported radii and coupling scalar are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, numerically zero at the displayed precision.  This is used only as a finite-row consistency certificate: it says that the computed spectrum is internally coherent for the displayed codon geometry.  It does not say that biological translation is caused by the spectrum.

\paragraph{Null-model scale.}
Against the shape randomization with $N=2000$ sampled rows, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the row comparison is a constrained finite enumeration rather than an unconstrained continuous approximation.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$, the observed module size is $20$, the exact match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the local tile is not a generic artifact of a label-preserving shuffle.  They do not promote the tile to a global law of molecular biology.

At the code-table level, $23$ tables and $63$ targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The leave-one-code-out recovery rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{\mathrm{above\ baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
h_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
h_{M\setminus T\ +\ boundary}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate families, across $5$ families.  This provides the surrounding code-level evidence that the boundary row participates in a stable readback pattern rather than appearing only in the standard table alone.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}
The codon-level row itself has no anchor codons in this audit: $anchor\_count=0$.  It has $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure codons, and $8$ predictable codons.  The leave-one-codon-out recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{recovery}}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the thresholded recovery indicator is also $1.0$.  The interpretation is deliberately local.  Removing a codon from the row leaves enough window-six boundary and closure information to recover its code-layer placement in this finite display.  This is a statement about codon-label readback through the curated standard-code contact, not about ribosomal dynamics, chemical feasibility, folding, phenotype, or evolutionary necessity.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The $13$ codons of $R$ are exhausted by six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
agr&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
ar\_lys&=\{AAA\},\\
cun&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
ile\_met&=\{AUA\},\\
ssn&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
stop\_trp&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification is complete: $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, with no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has a predictable-or-boundary witness.  This is the strongest finite-row form of the row claim: every codon in the punctured-cube boundary is accounted for by a local module, and the leave-one-codon-out probe recovers the row at rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The chapter records a boundary-tile phenomenon at the code-read layer.  The curated table supplies codon-to-label assignments; it does not supply a translation experiment, a folded-protein structure, a physical-admissibility test, a biological-function assay, a universal mechanism, or a proof that DNA must obey this geometry.  Therefore the admissible conclusion is only this: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile structure for the standard-code label table, with $13$ row codons, complete module assignment, strong null separation, and leave-one-codon-out recovery equal to $1.0$.  Any claim above code readback requires a separate higher-layer reality contact.
