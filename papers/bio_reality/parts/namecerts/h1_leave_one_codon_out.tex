\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Boundary Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Cube}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the sixty-four codon words over the usual four-letter alphabet.  The reality contact is deliberately narrower than a biological mechanism: a curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments, where the label is an amino-acid name or a stop label.  The contact therefore supports only code-layer readback.  It does not by itself observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that genomic matter is forced by this finite geometry.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion of this record is a finite-row code-read statement.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in which the thirteen-codon row $R$ is a punctured-cube boundary, its modules are completely classified, and the leave-one-codon recovery rate is $1.0$ in the supplied run. Any such higher-layer assertion would require a separate reality contact measuring that layer directly.
