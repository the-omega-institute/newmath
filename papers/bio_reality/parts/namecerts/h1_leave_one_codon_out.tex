\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Readback in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback layer.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with the standard-code label table as an external readback contact.  The object under test is not translation as a biochemical process, but only the code-layer assignment that sends each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The distinguished row set recovered by the window-six construction has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured-cube row pattern, this same set has finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the chapter records a finite local tile in $Q_6$: thirteen observed row vertices, sixteen row edges, and four row two-faces.  The contact named by the curated standard-code table supplies only label readback; it does not supply a mechanism that realizes the labels in a cell.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row signature.}
The row construction has the measured spectral scalars
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the numerical spectral check is at rounding scale for the finite matrix computation.  The relevant comparison is between the larger ambient local marker value $\lambda_M$ and the row-restricted value $\lambda_R$, not between either number and a biological rate.  These values are therefore used as coordinate evidence for the local codon tile and not as kinetic, structural, or evolutionary parameters.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The finite-row evidence includes two null checks.  In the unrestricted shape sample with seed $42$, $N=2000$ distinct samples were accepted after $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape match count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} = 0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the high-overlap threshold count was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9} = 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
In the amino-acid-class-preserving null with the same seed and $N=2000$, the observed marker size was $|M|=20$, the exact shape match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed marker size was $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The result is a finite significance statement about the row geometry under the stated sampling rules.  It is not a proof that the genetic code is forced by window-six geometry.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out comparison.}
The companion leave-one-code-out table test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, $5$ families were reported: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each had hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  These figures support a local code-read regularity across finite table variants, but they remain table-level readback data.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row test.}
For the claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.codon.out}$ itself, the leave-one-codon-out computation reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\_count} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\_count} &= 5,\\
\mathrm{predictable\_count} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\_rate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The thresholded recovery rate is also $1.0$.  Thus all recovered codon cases in the finite row test are accounted for by predictable or boundary behavior, with no separate anchor cases.  The natural reading is that the row tile has an internal closure-and-boundary split: eight codons lie in the predictable high-coverage part, while five are recorded as boundary codons.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons of $R$ are completely assigned to six named modules.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Equivalently, the module rows partition the displayed $R$ exactly.  The finite statement is therefore not merely that thirteen codons were selected, but that the selection is exhausted by a small module classification compatible with the local boundary split.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The curated standard-code table is a reality contact for codon-to-label readback only.  The present evidence supports the statement that window-six coordinates expose a local codon-tile pattern with a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row, significant finite-shape behavior, spectral scalars, full module assignment, and complete leave-one-codon-out recovery at the code-read layer.  It does not show translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any crossing from this code-read result to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact supplying that higher layer.
