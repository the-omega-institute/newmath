\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Boundary Tile in the Standard Code}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the finite display obtained by writing each codon as a coordinate in the codon cube and then reading a curated amino-acid or stop label from the standard genetic-code table.  The BEDC-minimal form used here has four visible distinctions: the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class.  Its internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation, while its readback is exactly the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe identifies a finite boundary row $R$ of size $13$.  The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
Thus the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons.  It has a finite geometric footprint inside the codon cube: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces after the relevant puncture is made.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The null comparison for the shape row used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The distinct-sample attempts were $57377$, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap comparison counted $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$ found $0$ exact matches.  Its lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, with observed $M$ size $20$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the claim that the observed finite tile is not a generic row artifact under the reported nulls.  They do not turn the tile into a universal rule for biology.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The reality contact is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments and therefore tests the code-read layer and the local codon-tile label split.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Consequently, the chapter claims only that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose labels are read through the curated standard-code bridge.  No translation process, no protein fold, no physical admissibility result, no functional claim, no mechanism, and no universal biological law follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
