\paragraph{Finite carrier and label contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon label table.  The biological contact used here is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It is a code-read contact: it permits finite label readback and local split tests, but it is not a contact with translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, realized function, a mechanism, or a universal biological law.  The relevant finite distinction is a local tile face together with singleton label corners and three-point label classes.  In this chapter the six-bit coordinate geometry is therefore treated as a finite row calculus over code labels, not as a biological necessity principle.

\paragraph{The distinguished finite row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite set is the punctured-cube row detected by the coordinate computation, with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row contains thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the local codon-tile representation.  The row is not asserted to be a global object in biology; it is the finite boundary row on which the leave-one-codon-out code-read experiment is evaluated.

\paragraph{Shape and null-model checks.}
The finite row is not merely a relabeling artifact under the reported controls.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match among the sampled controls, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run found $25$ controls with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling record also reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving control with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-row size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, and the reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the local code-read boundary claim: the observed row shape is rare in the tested finite controls, including the amino-acid-class-preserving control.  They do not promote the row to a statement about folded proteins or biological function.

The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row itself reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\ count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{predictable\ count}&=8,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}&=1.0.

$$
The above-threshold recovery rate is also $1.0$.  In this finite row, every omitted codon is therefore recovered either through predictable closure coverage or through the boundary class recorded by the experiment, with no anchor codons counted.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite-row evidence supports the following bounded statement.  In the six-bit codon display of the curated standard-code table, the row $R$ forms a punctured local codon tile with $13$ vertices, face-vector $(13,16,4)$, rare shape behavior under the reported controls, complete module assignment, and perfect leave-one-codon-out recovery in the recorded row test.  The supported layer is code readback: the contact observes codon-to-label assignments and local boundary splits.  No claim follows here about translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or the necessity of BEDC geometry for DNA.  Any such higher-layer claim would require a separate reality contact designed for that layer.
