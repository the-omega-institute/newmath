\paragraph{Record.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The tested object is not the biochemical process of translation, and it is not a model of folded proteins.  It is a finite code-layer readback problem: remove one codon label from the curated standard-code table, keep the remaining codon-label data fixed, and ask whether the local window-six tile, its closure neighborhood, and the observed boundary class recover the omitted codon at the code-read layer.  The claim identifier is \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out}; its associated conjectural packet is \texttt{codon.window6.local.tile.boundary}.

The coordinate carrier is the $64$-vertex codon cube $Q_6$, obtained by displaying the three nucleotide positions as six binary coordinates.  The finite row isolates a distinguished codon set $R$ of size $13$:

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The observational contact is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The contact has codon-label resolution: it is suitable for checking whether a codon readback agrees with the standard-code label table, and it is suitable for checking local label splits on the finite tile.  It is not a measurement of translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, organismal function, or universal mechanism.  The row therefore supports a code-layer statement: window-six coordinates and the local tile boundary recover the recorded omitted codon labels at the reported rate.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The cannot-promote boundary is part of the record.  A local codon tile may organize code labels, but no claim about protein realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or universal biological law follows from this contact alone.  Amino-acid label readback remains label readback.  To cross into folding or function, a separate observation with structural or functional resolution would have to be supplied.  Without such a contact, CUN/UUR wording, tRNA notation, and codon-label recovery remain code-layer data.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite result is ready at the code-read layer.  The distinguished set $R$ has $13$ codons, punctured-cube face-vector $(13,16,4)$, complete module assignment, spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, and the leave-one-codon-out row reports recovery rate $1.0$ with $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, and no anchor cases.  The significance rows give exact-shape probabilities $0.0005$ and $0.0$ under the two recorded finite null procedures, with the amino-acid-class-preserving lower-tail value $0.0035$.  The chapter therefore records a finite, curated-table-grounded code-read phenomenon: local window-six codon geometry tracks a boundary-class recovery pattern, while all higher biological interpretations remain outside the licensed scope of this evidence.
