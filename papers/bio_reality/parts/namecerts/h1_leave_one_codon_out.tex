\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite row contact against a six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The internal BEDC-side object is not a biochemical mechanism; it is a coordinate display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, three-point label classes, closure data, spectral probes, and relation data.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observable content is the codon-to-label readback.  Thus the biological object under test is the standard-code label table at the $code\_read$ layer.  The contact says that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels; it does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any law asserting that DNA must obey this geometry.

The row selected by the local boundary computation has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$

This is the finite object whose leave-one-codon behavior is tested.  The claim is not that these thirteen codons determine biology.  The claim is narrower: when one codon is withheld from this finite row, the coordinate-and-closure apparatus recovers the withheld code-read position either as predictable from the local closure or as a boundary case that remains explicitly marked as such.
