\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.}
This record uses the window-six codon carrier $Q_6$ as a finite coordinate display for the standard-code codon table. The biological contact is only the curated standard-code bridge: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it is used here only for code-layer readback. The carrier supplies six-bit codon coordinates, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes; the external table supplies the labels. Thus the object under test is not translation as a biochemical realization, and it is not protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{The distinguished residual row.}
The finite residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\

$$
The same row is verified as a punctured cube with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. This is the finite geometric datum used by the leave-one-codon-out probe: removing one codon at a time tests whether the remaining local coordinates and boundary relations recover the row as a code-read pattern.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out comparison evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$. The codon-level leave-one-out run then produced $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, and $0$ anchor cases, with high closure coverage $8$. Its recovery rate was $1.0$, and the recovery-rate-above-threshold flag was also $1.0$. In this chapter, those counts mean that the local window-six row is stable as a finite code-read tile: each omitted codon is either recovered by the local predictable class or held by the boundary class. They do not mean that the tile is a global law of biology.

\paragraph{Verdict and boundary.}
The claim is ready at the code-read layer: the standard-code label table, the punctured-cube row $R$, the spectral values, the null-model comparisons, the leave-one-code-out rates, the leave-one-codon-out counts, and the complete six-module classification agree on a finite local tile. The supported statement is only that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose labels are read back through the curated standard-code contact. No translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity claim follows from these rows. Any such stronger statement requires a separate higher-layer contact and is blocked in this record.
