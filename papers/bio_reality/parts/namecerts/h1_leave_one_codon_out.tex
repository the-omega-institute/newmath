\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a physical translation apparatus; it is the finite display obtained by writing each codon as a six-coordinate object, with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes read back against a curated standard genetic-code table.  The contact supplies codon-to-label assignments only.  It tests code-layer readback and local codon-tile label split, and it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

The finite row set singled out by the coordinate probe has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is

\begin{aligned}

Thus the local combinatorial object contains thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the supplied finite-row encoding.  This is the object on which the leave-one-codon-out test is evaluated; it is not promoted here to a law of biology.

\paragraph{Shape significance and null comparison.}
Against the supplied shape null, the experiment used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} = 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$.  The exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite row therefore has non-random-looking code-read structure under the supplied nulls, but this statement remains restricted to the row data and the curated label table.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The local conclusion is that window-six codon coordinates expose a boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table.  The curated table is the reality contact for label readback, and the finite data support a code-layer statement: the thirteen-row punctured-cube set has complete module assignment, significant shape statistics under the supplied nulls, and full leave-one-codon-out recovery in the reported row.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result.  No translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this chapter unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
