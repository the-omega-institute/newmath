\paragraph{Claim record.}
This BioReality record concerns the finite-row claim \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out}.  The object under inspection is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The BEDC minimal form uses the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ as carrier, with distinctions for the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal structure is finite and consists of coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The readback contact is the curated standard-code label table: each codon is externally read as an amino-acid or stop label, but the table is not itself a mechanism of translation.

\paragraph{Finite boundary set.}
The distinguished row set has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the verified set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same thirteen codons are certified as a punctured-cube row pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not an arbitrary list of exceptional codons: it is a finite cubical trace with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four square faces in the inspected window-six coordinate system.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}
The leave-one-codon-out test removes a codon from the row-level evidence and asks whether the remaining finite coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data recover the missing row as predictable or as a boundary case.  The verified run reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{predictable count} &= 8,\\
\text{boundary count} &= 5,\\
\text{anchor count} &= 0,\\
\text{high closure coverage} &= 8,\\
\text{recovery rate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Equivalently, all thirteen deleted rows are accounted for by the local tile test: eight fall into the predictable class and five fall into the boundary class.  The row-level recovery is therefore complete in this finite experiment, while still being a statement about codon-label readback rather than about biological realization.

Each module has at least one predictable-or-boundary explanation under the local test.  This matters because the recovery is not merely a pooled count: the finite decomposition respects the observed row modules.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments at codon-label resolution.  It can test code-read claims and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization.  The observed fact is simply that the curated standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  This is enough to read the finite row labels and to check the local split, but it is not enough to infer any mechanism by which those labels are biologically produced.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The verified finite-row statement is therefore: in the standard-code readback contact, the window-six codon coordinate exposes a thirteen-row local tile for which leave-one-codon-out recovery is complete, with eight predictable rows and five boundary rows.  The statement is local, finite, and code-layered.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these data unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies it.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
