\paragraph{Finite object.}
The object tested here is the standard-code codon table read only at the code-label layer.  The BEDC carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinates supplied by the codon display and with labels supplied by the curated standard-code table.  The finite row under test is the leave-one-codon-out row for the claim \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out}: when one codon label is withheld, the local tile geometry is asked whether the missing row is recovered by closure support or by an explicitly marked boundary position.  This is not a claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  It is only a finite code-read claim about a labeled codon table.

The leave-one-codon-out run reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, and $0$ anchor codons.  The recovery rate is exactly $1.0$, and the above-threshold recovery indicator is also $1.0$.  Thus every tested codon in the row is accounted for by the local rule, but the accounting is split between closure-predictable sites and boundary sites rather than being promoted to a biological law.  In the notation of this finite record, the verified scalar summary is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{predictable} &= 8,\\
\operatorname{boundary} &= 5,\\
\operatorname{anchor} &= 0,\\
\operatorname{recovery\ rate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The result is therefore a complete finite-row recovery at the code-read layer, with five codons explicitly retained as boundary rather than treated as interior closure consequences.

\paragraph{Reality-contact boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the finite verdict is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a complete local boundary-tile recovery for the supplied standard-code row, but curated code labels remain external reality contacts, and no higher biological layer follows without a separate contact designed for that higher layer.
