\subsection{Leave-one-codon recovery in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite setting.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer object.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube obtained by writing each nucleotide position in a two-bit display and taking the resulting $64$ vertices as codon addresses.  The observable labels are the curated standard-code assignments from codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The claim tested here is deliberately local: after one codon label is withheld, the window-six coordinates and their local tile boundary data recover whether the missing row is predictable from closure or is forced onto the boundary of the local tile.  The test is not a claim about translation dynamics, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal biological principle.

\paragraph{Distinguished codon set.}
The finite exceptional set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In lexicographic codon notation it is
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same set is reported by the punctured-cube calculation, with face-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the row data identify a finite local tile rather than merely a list of biologically named codons.  The corresponding module partition is complete: all $13$ codons in $R$ are assigned, with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},&
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},&
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},&
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
Each listed module has at least one codon that is either predictable by the closure computation or lies on the boundary class used by the local-tile test.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon result.}
The direct leave-one-codon experiment has experiment id $\mathrm{e9e1a6e41633}$.  It reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, and $0$ anchor codons.  The high-coverage closure count is $8$, so the local accounting closes as
$$\begin{aligned}
8+5+0=13=|R|.
\end{aligned}$$
The recovery rate is $1.0$, and the reported recovery-above-threshold value is also $1.0$.  In this finite row, every codon in the distinguished set is therefore accounted for by the split between closure-predictable rows and boundary rows.  This is the numerical content of the claim: the window-six tile gives a complete local recovery classification for the withheld codon rows in $R$.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic check is numerically zero to tolerance, with polynomial value $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is recorded for the cubic-polynomial residual.  The shape-significance experiment, run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $1$ exact shape match among the sampled alternatives, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  It found $25$ alternatives with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported probabilities should be read as finite Monte Carlo evidence, not as a closed-form probability law.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null row.}
A second null calculation preserves amino-acid class.  With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, it reports observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  The exact-shape $p$-value is $0.0$ at the recorded sample resolution, while the lower-tail $p$-value is $0.0035$.  This reinforces that the observed local shape is not reproduced by the tested class-preserving randomization at ordinary sample frequency.  The result is still a code-table statement: it compares finite codon-label rearrangements and does not observe a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Code-table leave-one-out comparison.}
The related leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  Inside $M\setminus T$ the hit rate is $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary it is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, $5$ families are reported: bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each have family hit rate above baseline $1.0$, while alternative-yeast has $0.8333333333333334$.  These numbers are consistent with the codon-level split: most withheld labels are recovered by the local closure and boundary accounting, but the assertion remains tied to curated code readback.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, whose observable content is the codon-to-label assignment.  It can test whether the finite coordinate construction predicts or classifies code labels in the local tile.  It cannot test whether a ribosome realizes a translation process, whether an amino-acid sequence folds, whether a molecule is physically admissible, whether a product has a biological function, whether the pattern is a mechanism, or whether the same local geometry is a universal law.  Any such higher-layer claim would require a separate contact with the corresponding biological observation.  Consequently the verdict is a finite code-read result: the $13$ codons in $R$ are completely partitioned by the leave-one-codon closure and boundary test, with recovery rate $1.0$, while all structural, dynamical, physical, functional, mechanistic, and universal readings remain outside the demonstrated row data.
