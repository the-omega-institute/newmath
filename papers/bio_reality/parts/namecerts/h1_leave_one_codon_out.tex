\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Codon Tile Boundary}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.} This BioReality record concerns a finite code-read object: the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display on the codon cube $Q_6$. The biological contact is only the curated table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The BEDC minimal form has coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data, but its readback is restricted to the standard-code label table. The row is therefore a code-layer record, not a translation model and not a structural or functional biology claim.

\paragraph{The distinguished local row.} The finite distinguished set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row was verified as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite observation is not merely that thirteen codons were selected; it is that these thirteen codons occupy a specific local tile with $16$ one-dimensional incidences and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the induced cube geometry.

\paragraph{Spectral readout.} The spectral row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check returned $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value was recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual. This is a numerical zero at the stated tolerance, so the spectrum is used here only as a finite coordinate invariant of the observed codon tile. It does not assert any energetic, dynamical, translational, or evolutionary mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance controls.} Against the finite null sample with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. These values support the finite statement that the observed tile is uncommon under this sampling model. They do not by themselves identify why the biological code table has these labels, and they do not promote the local geometry into a global biological law.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving control.} A second null model preserved amino-acid class structure. In that control, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed size was $|M|=20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. The recorded probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the code-read observation because the row survives a control that keeps coarse amino-acid class information available. It still remains a row-level finite comparison, not an account of translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.} The leave-one-codon-out probe for this row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ codons with high closure coverage, and $8$ predictable codons. Its recovery rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the threshold comparison also recorded as $1.0$. The content of the claim is therefore precise: after removing a codon from the finite row, the local tile data either recovers it predictably or places it on the boundary of the tile. Since the anchor count is $0$, the row is not being read as a set of immovable biological anchors; it is being read as a boundary-sensitive local code pattern.

\paragraph{Code-table transfer comparison.} In the related leave-one-code-out finite evaluation, $23$ tables and $63$ targets were evaluated. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. These numbers make the finite row portable as a code-read diagnostic across curated code-table variants, but portability at the label layer is not a claim about the biochemical implementation of translation.

\paragraph{Module classification.} The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$: the assigned count was $13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons. The modules were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.

$$
Every listed module has predictable-or-boundary status. This is the strongest finite form of the row: the local tile, the leave-one-codon-out recovery, and the module assignment all close on the same thirteen codons.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.} The contact supports only codon-label readback and local split checks. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure. A separate higher-layer contact would be required before any claim crossed from code-read geometry to translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.
