\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table only as a finite code-read contact.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with codons displayed as finite rows and with curated labels supplied by the external standard-code table.  The internal BEDC form has four visible distinctions: the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  Its internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; its readback is the standard-code label table.  The contact therefore observes codon-to-label assignments, not translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{The punctured relation row.}
The recovered relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The codons are
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
As a local finite complex this row has the punctured-cube face vector
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
The point of this datum is local: it says that the thirteen codon rows form a boundary-tile shape inside the six-bit display.  It does not say that the biological system is forced by this geometry, and it does not turn the tile into a global law for DNA, translation, protein structure, or organismal function.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the recorded randomization basis, the $M$ shape test used $N=2000$ sampled trials with seed $42$.  The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}$$
It also found $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid classes used $N=2000$ trials, with observed $M$ size $20$.  It found $0$ exact matches, so the finite-row estimate is $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$, and it found $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size, giving $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These numbers support non-generic local table shape under the tested nulls.  They do not supply a mechanism for why the biological table has that shape.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}

The observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  This supports the interpretation that the local boundary row is informative for code-label readback across finite table variants, while remaining silent about higher biological layers.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}
The chapter-specific leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, and high closure coverage $8$.  Its recovery rate is
$$\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}$$
with the threshold comparison also reported as $1.0$.  Thus the finite row says that each withheld codon in the tested relation is recovered either by predictable local closure or by boundary status, not by an independent biological mechanism.  The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons are all empty.  The modules are

Every displayed module has either predictable or boundary recovery.  In this sense the local tile is a finite, auditable code-read witness: it exposes a structured split among the thirteen rows, and the curated standard-code table supplies the labels against which that split is read.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is deliberately narrow.  A local codon tile in $Q_6$ may expose boundary structure for codon-label readback, and the displayed finite rows give positive evidence for that code-layer statement.  No translation process is observed here.  No folded protein structure is observed here.  No physical admissibility, function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from the tile.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any claim crossing from code labels to translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact with its own finite rows and its own null tests.
