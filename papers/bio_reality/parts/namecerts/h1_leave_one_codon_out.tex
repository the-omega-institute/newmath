\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery inside the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The claim recorded here concerns a six-coordinate codon display for the standard genetic-code table.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read as a binary window over the four nucleotide symbols and then evaluated only against a curated codon-to-label table.  The biological datum used in this section is therefore a code-label table: codons are assigned amino-acid or stop labels, but no ribosome, translation dynamics, folded molecule, physical chemistry, cellular function, or universal law is observed by this contact.  The finite pattern is still informative because it isolates a distinguished thirteen-codon region $R$ and asks whether local omission tests can recover withheld codons from the remaining tile geometry and the curated labels.

\paragraph{The thirteen-codon region.}
The distinguished row set has exactly thirteen codons.  In the supplied finite run, the set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R &= \{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\\
&\qquad \mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
|R| &= 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row set is reported as a punctured cube with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it carries a small cubical boundary profile, with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-dimensional faces in the observed tile.  This is the local geometric fact used by the leave-one-codon-out test.

\paragraph{Spectral scale of the tile.}
The associated spectral row also supplies a quantitative scale.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the reported eigenvalue calculation is numerically consistent at the displayed tolerance.  These numbers do not by themselves identify a biological mechanism.  Their role is narrower: they give a finite spectral signature for the codon tile and make the row set testable against randomized alternatives.

\paragraph{Randomization checks.}
The shape comparison against sampled alternatives used $N=2000$ randomized trials with seed $42$.  Only one exact shape match appeared among the samples, giving $p=0.0005$ for exact shape agreement.  A high-overlap threshold, recorded as Jaccard at least $0.9$, occurred $25$ times, giving $p=0.0125$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite sampling space was being revisited heavily; the resulting small tail probabilities should be read as empirical finite-sample evidence rather than as an asymptotic theorem.  A second control preserved amino-acid class.  In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module size was $20$, exact matches were $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, giving lower-tail probability $0.0035$ and exact-shape probability $0.0$ at the sampled resolution.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out comparison.}
The broader code-table omission run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against this baseline, the finite geometry gave a hit rate above baseline of $0.9841269841269841$, a hit rate inside $M-T$ of $0.9365079365079365$, and a hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary of $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level readout separated the same behavior across five reported families: bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each had family hit rate $1.0$ above baseline, while alternative-yeast had $0.8333333333333334$.  These numbers support a code-layer claim: the local tile is strongly aligned with codon-label recoverability under the tested omissions.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.codon.out}$ is the sharper codon-level omission test.  The run reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The thresholded recovery flag is also $1.0$.  Thus all tested omitted codons are recovered within the finite criterion supplied by predictable positions or boundary coverage, without any separate anchor count.  In compact form, the observed row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchors} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{boundary} &= 5,\\
\mathrm{high\ closure\ coverage} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{predictable} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central positive datum for the section.  It says that the window-six tile has enough finite neighborhood structure to recover the held-out codon labels in the tested row, as judged at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen codons are also completely assigned to six named modules.  The module decomposition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row records $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the module list, and no unassigned codons.  It also records that every module has predictable or boundary support.  This closes the finite bookkeeping for the local row: the codon set, cubical profile, omission recovery, and module assignment all refer to the same thirteen displayed codons.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The result should be read as a finite code-table regularity.  A six-coordinate codon display exposes a local tile whose boundary and closure structure recover omitted codon labels in the supplied tests, and the same row is unusually shaped under the reported randomizations.  The curated standard-code table is essential: without that table there is no amino-acid or stop-label readback, only a coordinate pattern.  Conversely, with only that table, the result remains at the label layer.  It does not claim that DNA is compelled by this geometry, that translation is realized by the tile, that amino-acid sequences fold from it, that any protein is physically admissible, that biological function follows, that a mechanism has been identified, or that the pattern is universal across life.  Any one of those stronger conclusions would require an additional observation directed at that specific layer.
