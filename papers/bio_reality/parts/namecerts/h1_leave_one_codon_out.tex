\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery inside a window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:bior}-h1-leave-one-codon-out

\paragraph{Claim and layer.}
The claim tested here is the code-layer assertion identified by \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out}.  The biological object is the standard genetic-code table, used only as a curated codon-to-label contact.  The internal finite object is a six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with coordinate, closure, spectral, and relation probes.  The question is not whether this geometry is a mechanism of translation.  The question is narrower: after one codon is withheld from a local code-read row, does the finite window-six construction recover that withheld codon as predictable or boundary-supported from the remaining local tile data?

\paragraph{Finite row under test.}
The primary leave-one-codon-out row reports no anchor recoveries, five boundary cases, and eight high-coverage closure cases.  In symbols, the recorded counters are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\_count} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\_count} &= 5,\\
\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\_count} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\_rate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The auxiliary threshold flag is also $1.0$, so every tested codon in this finite recovery row is accounted for by either the predictable component or the boundary component.  This is a statement about the row geometry of the code table, not a statement about ribosomal execution, protein folding, physical feasibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary necessity.

\paragraph{Recovered relation set.}
The finite relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube computation returns the same $13$ codons and the face-vector summary
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object being read is not merely a list of exceptional codons.  It has a small cubical boundary profile: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the recorded local complex.  The leave-one-codon-out recovery row is therefore evaluated against an explicitly enumerated finite set rather than against an open-ended biological class.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The module classification exhausts the same $13$ codons with no missing and no extra codons.  The six recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The assigned count is $13$, the recorded $R$ size is $13$, the list of missing module codons is empty, the list of extra module codons is empty, and the list of unassigned codons is empty.  Each module has at least one predictable-or-boundary explanation in the local row.  This matters because the recovery rate is not concentrated in a single naming accident: the coverage passes through all six named modules, including the singleton modules \texttt{ar\_lys} and \texttt{ile\_met}, the two-codon modules \texttt{agr} and \texttt{ssn}, the three-codon \texttt{stop\_trp} module, and the four-codon \texttt{cun} module.

\paragraph{Spectral profile.}
The same finite packet carries a spectral row.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,\\
P(\mu) &= -6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial check and the cubic check have the same residual, $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius entry records $0.6752479308830213$.  The residual is numerically near zero at the displayed precision, so the spectral row is consistent with the finite geometry used by the recovery test.  The spectral values do not turn the codon tile into a molecular dynamics model; they only give an internal numerical fingerprint for the finite code-read construction.

\paragraph{Comparison rows.}
The broader leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the recorded hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, across $5$ families.  These rows are not identical to the one-codon-withheld test, but they supply finite context: the local recovery phenomenon is compatible with a wider table-withholding probe rather than appearing only in the single codon row.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Two null rows constrain the interpretation.  In the shape-significance row, $N=2000$ samples were used with seed $42$.  The process recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
while $25$ samples had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It recorded observed $M$ size $20$, exact match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  The corresponding values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite code-table anomaly relative to the sampled null models.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.  The data row is strong enough to justify a local code-read claim and too narrow to justify a claim about structure, function, or universality.

\paragraph{Contact and scope.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact resolves codon label readback and local label-split checks.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, functional phenotype, or a universal biological law.  Therefore the admissible conclusion is deliberately local: within the enumerated standard-code row, the window-six codon tile supplies a complete leave-one-codon-out recovery at the code-read layer, with $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, and recovery rate $1.0$ over the recorded test row.  Any statement about translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require an additional empirical contact of the appropriate kind before it could be asserted.
