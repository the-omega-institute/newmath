\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Boundary Test for the Standard-Code Codon Table}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the usual codon display and then contacted with a curated standard-code table. The contact is deliberately narrow: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it licenses code-layer readback only. The local object tested here is not translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. It is the finite question of whether leaving out one codon from the observed standard-code label table exposes a recoverable local boundary-tile pattern in the $Q_6$ coordinates.

\paragraph{Observed exceptional row set.}
The row set singled out by the finite construction has cardinality $13$. Its observed members are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same set is recovered as a punctured-cube pattern with finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of codons; it has a local cubical boundary signature inside the six-coordinate display. This statement is only about the coordinate shape of the codon-label readback. It does not assert that biological DNA is governed by this geometry as a law.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The finite spectral calculation gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The radial polynomial residual is numerically small:
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\mu)=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The Monte Carlo shape test used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It found $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The distinct-sample process required $57377$ attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. An amino-acid-class-preserving null with the same sample count had observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$. These numbers support that the local row shape is sparse under the tested finite nulls, while still remaining a code-read statistic.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon recovery.}
At the code-table level, the leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$. At the stricter leave-one-codon-out level, the finite row had anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, recovery rate $1.0$, and recovery-rate-above-threshold value $1.0$. In the language of the test, all recoverable signal is boundary or closure signal; none is licensed as a higher biological layer.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The $13$ codons in $R$ are completely assigned to six finite modules, with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The module decomposition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification has $R$-size $13$ and assigned count $13$. Each module has either a predictable or boundary witness under the finite procedure. A family-level leave-one-code-out split over $5$ families gave hit-rate-above-baseline values $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready claim is therefore a local code-read claim: in the standard-code table, the six-bit codon display exposes a finite punctured-cube row $R$ with a stable local boundary-tile signature, strong leave-one-codon recovery, and complete module assignment. The claim stops at the curated table contact. No translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from these finite rows. Any such promotion would require a separate reality contact with its own observations and its own failure conditions.
