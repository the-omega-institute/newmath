\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite-row claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.codon.out}$ records a code-layer boundary phenomenon in the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table.  The test removes one codon from the finite display and asks whether the remaining coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data recover the omitted codon as a predictable local-tile event or as a boundary event.  The intended layer is only $\mathrm{code\_read}$: the row tests codon-to-label readback and local label splitting, not translation as a biochemical process.

\paragraph{Finite carrier and punctured region.}
The distinguished region $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row identifies this region as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object under discussion is not the full codon table and not an inferred biological universal.  It is a thirteen-codon local subconfiguration inside the window-six display, with four recorded tile faces and sixteen recorded one-dimensional adjacencies.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact has resolution at codon-label readback.  It supports checking whether a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class match the curated label table.  It does not supply a mechanism, a translation apparatus, a ribosomal process, a folded structure, a thermodynamic admissibility criterion, or a biological function assay.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The local conclusion is therefore deliberately narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates may expose a finite local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table.  From this chapter alone, no translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any future promotion beyond $\mathrm{code\_read}$ would require a separate reality contact at the promoted layer, such as an explicit translation experiment, a structure contact, a physical admissibility test, or a function assay.
