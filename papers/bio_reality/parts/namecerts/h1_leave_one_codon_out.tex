\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:bior-h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Question.}
The finite object studied here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  Each codon is a point of the window-six cube $Q_6$, and the biological label attached to that point is read only from the curated standard-code table.  The claim is deliberately local: if one codon label is withheld from the finite display, the remaining codon-label rows may determine whether the missing row sits in a predictable closure class or on a boundary of the local tile.  The experiment does not ask whether the codon is translated in a cell, whether a peptide folds, whether a molecule is physically admissible, whether a function is realized, or whether a universal mechanism has been found.

\paragraph{Finite region.}
The distinguished punctured-cube region has exactly thirteen codons,
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The cardinality audit gives $|R|=13$.  The same run identifies the punctured-cube shape by the face-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}$$
so the evidence is not just a count of exceptional labels; it is a small cubical subcomplex with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional faces, and four two-dimensional faces.  This is the finite geometric arena in which the leave-one-codon-out question is evaluated.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The numerical spectrum attached to the same finite construction supplies an internal scale for the local tile.  The reported values are
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}$$
The radial cubic check returns $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is effectively zero at the stated numerical tolerance.  These numbers are used here only as finite diagnostics of the window-six arrangement; they are not interpreted as biochemical energies, rates, or structural constraints.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against a sampled null family with $N=2000$ trials and seed $42$, the exact finite shape occurred once.  The exact-shape probability estimate is therefore $p=0.0005$.  A looser comparison by Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$ produced $25$ matches, giving $p=0.0125$.  The sampling also reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite-row result is being compared against a constrained discrete background rather than against a continuous asymptotic model.  Under an amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed comparison size is $20$, the exact finite shape appears $0$ times, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$.  Thus the class-preserving exact-shape estimate is $0.0$, while the lower-tail estimate is $0.0035$.  These finite probabilities support the claim that the local arrangement is not a typical random row pattern under the stated nulls, while still leaving the biological interpretation bounded by the available contact.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out context.}
The broader leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The reported hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  These table-level data are background support for the codon-level test: they say that the same finite coordinate device often recovers held-out label behavior at the code-read layer, but they do not promote the result to translation or function.

\paragraph{Codon-level recovery.}
For the claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.codon.out}$, the finite-row audit reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, and $8$ high-coverage closure cases.  The recovery rate is
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0,
\end{aligned}$$
and the threshold comparison also returns $1.0$.  In this chapter this means the withheld codon rows are always caught by one of the permitted local explanations: either the row belongs to the predictable closure part of the tile or it is assigned to the boundary part.  The zero anchor count is important.  The recovery is not being explained by declaring fixed singleton anchors after the fact; it is explained by closure and boundary behavior inside the finite codon cube display.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The thirteen codons are also exhausted by six named modules:
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The module-completeness audit records $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Every module contains at least one row classified as predictable or boundary.  This is the most compact finite description of the observed effect: the recovery is not scattered across unrelated singleton events, but is distributed across the full module partition of the punctured-cube region.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact has resolution at the codon-label readback level.  It is suitable for testing whether the local six-coordinate tile splits labels in a recoverable way after one codon row is withheld.  It is not a contact with ribosomal translation dynamics, protein folding, thermodynamic admissibility, molecular function, organismal fitness, or universality across all code systems.  Consequently, amino-acid label sequences obtained from the table remain label sequences; no folded structure is observed by this contact.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite-row evidence supports the following bounded statement.  In the standard-code table, the thirteen-codon punctured-cube region $R$ has a stable local tile signature, a nontrivial spectral scale, significant separation from the sampled null backgrounds, and complete leave-one-codon-out recovery by the predictable-or-boundary classification.  The strongest chapter-level reading is therefore a code-layer one: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile organization for the curated labels.  Nothing in the finite data licenses a claim of cellular translation, protein structure, physical feasibility, biological function, general mechanism, or universal law without an additional contact designed to observe that higher layer.
