\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Local Tile Boundary}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-local-tile-boundary}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite row under test.}
The row tests a window-six codon-coordinate claim on the standard-code label table.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read through a six-bit codon display and then compared with a curated codon-to-label contact.  The internal BEDC side supplies coordinates, closure, spectrum, and relation data; the biological side supplies only the standard-code label table.  Thus the object being tested is not translation as a process, not folded protein structure, and not biological function.  It is the code-layer question of whether a local codon tile and its boundary recover codon-label readback in a leave-one-codon-out test.

\paragraph{Relation row.}
The distinguished relation row has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same thirteen codons are recorded as a punctured-cube row, with finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite combinatorial body of the local-tile claim.  The row is not being promoted to a law of genetic coding; it is a measured finite pattern inside the coordinate display, read back against external labels.

They do not identify a biochemical mechanism; they only constrain the row as a finite code-read geometry.

\paragraph{Shape and null rows.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ null samples with seed $42$.  It recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ duplicate retries, $1$ exact shape match, and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$.  The corresponding probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, and the reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite null rows make the local shape hard to dismiss as an arbitrary relabeling artifact.  They still do not make the local tile a universal biological principle.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out context.}
The related leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  These context rows show that the same finite geometry remains informative across code-table families, while still staying at the label-readback layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out result.}
For the row itself, the leave-one-codon-out run reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, recovery rate $1.0$, and recovery-rate-above-threshold value $1.0$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary}&=5,\\
\mathrm{high\ closure\ coverage}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The interpretation is therefore narrow and finite: when one codon is left out, the local closure-and-boundary geometry recovers the tested codon-label row perfectly in this finite record.  The absence of anchors matters.  Recovery is carried by closure and boundary structure, not by a singleton anchor that would trivially name the omitted codon.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen relation codons are completely assigned to six modules.  The AGR module is $\{AGA,AGG\}$, the AR-lysine module is $\{AAA\}$, the CUN module is $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, the isoleucine/methionine module is $\{AUA\}$, the SSN module is $\{UCA,UUA\}$, and the stop/tryptophan module is $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$.  The classification row records $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from modules, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has a predictable or boundary witness.  This is the strongest finite-row form of the local-tile statement: the entire punctured-cube row is covered by named modules, and every module is accounted for by predictable or boundary behavior.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code readback and a local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded structure, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Consequently the verdict is a code-layer boundary claim: window-six coordinates expose a finite local tile in the standard-code label table, with perfect leave-one-codon-out recovery in the recorded row, but no higher biological layer follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
