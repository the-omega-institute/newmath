\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Boundary Test for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The claim \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out} is a finite-row BioReality record for the standard-code codon table, read only at the code layer.  Its carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with codons displayed as finite coordinates and then read back through a curated standard-code label table.  The BEDC minimal form used here has four local distinctions: the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  Its internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; its readback is the standard-code label table.  The only declared external contact is \texttt{curated.standard.code.table}: a curated table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or any mechanism-level universality claim.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube row.}
The finite set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified codon list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
   &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check reports the same thirteen codons and the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile that the row is allowed to inspect.  The data do not turn this tile into a biological law; the tile is a code-layer coordinate object whose labels are supplied by the external code table.

\paragraph{Shape and null rows.}
The shape-significance row used $N=2000$ randomized samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape match count was $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The count with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It reports observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  Thus the finite-row evidence is not merely the observation that a tile exists; it includes a null comparison in which exact shape is absent under the amino-acid-class-preserving control, and a lower-tail event appears at rate $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out context.}
The companion leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, matching the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary, while the hit rate in $M-T$ alone was $0.9365079365079365$.  By family, the hit rate above baseline was $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  These finite rows give context for the codon-level test: the same coordinate-and-boundary discipline behaves strongly against the leave-one-code-out baseline, but remains a code-read result.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out verdict.}
For the codon-level test itself, the row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, recovery rate $1.0$, and recovery-rate-above-threshold value $1.0$.  In finite-row terms, the missing codon is recovered through predictable or boundary behavior rather than through an anchor class.  The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has predictable or boundary support, and together these modules partition the thirteen codons in $R$.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The row is ready as a code-read claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure, and curated code labels supply the reality contact needed for codon-label readback.  The claim stops exactly there.  No translation realization follows from this row.  No folded protein structure follows from amino-acid label readback.  No physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from the local tile.  Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate contact that directly observes the higher layer.  The present contact only supports the statement that the standard-code label table aligns with the reported finite coordinate, closure, spectral, relation, and leave-one-codon-out rows.
