\subsection{Leave-one-codon recovery at the local code boundary}\label{subsec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim under test.}
The claim concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  Its subject is not a proposed biochemical mechanism, but a finite code-layer question: after one codon label is withheld, does the local six-bit geometry still place that missing codon inside a predictable or boundary-controlled part of the observed label pattern?  The biological contact is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for local label-split tests.  It is not evidence for translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any organism-wide law.

\paragraph{Reality contact and finite carrier.}
The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with codons represented by six binary coordinates and read against the standard-code label table.  The finite distinguished set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube computation returns the same codon set and the face-count vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the codon-level object is a concrete finite row, not an asymptotic or interpretive statement.  The local table can mark singleton label corners, three-point label classes, tile faces, and relation neighborhoods, but those marks remain code-layer marks unless another experiment supplies a higher-level biological observation.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The finite spectral row records $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The recorded radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the spectral certificate is numerically closed at the stated tolerance.  The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  Among those samples there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ high-Jaccard matches at threshold $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The run also reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which records the finite sampling pressure rather than hiding it.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null comparison.}
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null run also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It reports observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ at or below the observed $M$ size.  The resulting values are $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$.  This does not prove that codon biology is forced by the cube geometry.  It only says that, under the specified class-preserving randomization, the observed finite shape is unusually sharp relative to the sampled alternatives.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon rows.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$.  The more local leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$, with recovery-rate-above-threshold also $1.0$.  The absence of anchors matters: recovery is not being attributed to a direct fixed codon anchor, but to predictable or boundary placement in the finite local configuration.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$: it reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, and empty lists for extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},&
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},&
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},&
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has at least predictable or boundary status.  This is the finite combinatorial content of the leave-one-codon claim: each exceptional codon row is placed by the local code geometry into a controlled module, and no codon in the $13$-element set is left outside the classification.

\paragraph{Family-level stability.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out row separates the result by five table families.  The reported hit-rate-above-baseline values are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.  These numbers support the narrower statement that the local code-layer pattern is stable across the finite family grouping used in the run.  They do not license a statement about all genetic-code variants, all organisms, or the biochemical realization of translation.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported conclusion is therefore deliberately small.  The curated standard-code table gives a finite codon-to-label contact; the six-coordinate display and its closure, spectrum, relation, and module rows expose a local boundary pattern; and the leave-one-codon computation recovers the withheld codon at rate $1.0$ within predictable or boundary-controlled placement.  No protein fold, structural order, physical admissibility, function, translation process, or universal mechanism is observed in this contact.  Any such higher-layer assertion would require a separate structure, translation, physical, or functional contact before it could be stated as a biological result.
