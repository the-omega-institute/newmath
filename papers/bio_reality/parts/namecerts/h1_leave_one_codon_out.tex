\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Local Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Claim and coordinate domain.}
The claim \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out} concerns a finite code-read experiment on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is represented as a six-coordinate display, and the tested question is whether a local tile pattern can recover held-out codon labels at the code layer when one codon-level item is removed.  The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Thus the experiment is about label readback inside a finite coordinate table, not about translation machinery, folded structure, chemical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Observed residual region.}
The finite residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The certified row lists
$R=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.  The same row identifies this residual region as a punctured cube with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)&=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite shape on which the leave-one-codon check is performed.  It is local: it singles out a boundary-tile configuration inside the codon display and does not assert that the geometry is a law of biology.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The associated spectral row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The radial cubic check is numerically closed at $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the polynomial residual is at computational tolerance rather than a visible mismatch.  A separate shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$, requiring $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the row reports observed $M$-size $20$, $0$ exact matches among $2000$ trials, and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed size, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a non-generic finite coordinate pattern, but they still remain within the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one recovery row.}
For the leave-one-codon experiment itself, the run \texttt{e9e1a6e41633} reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable codons.  The recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{above\ threshold}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
In this finite packet, every recovered held-out item is accounted for either by predictable closure behavior or by the boundary side of the local tile.  The absence of anchors is important: the row is not saying that an isolated label corner alone forces the biological label.  Instead, recovery is mediated by the local coordinate neighborhood and its boundary classification.

Thus the residual region is not merely counted; it is exhausted by named codon modules, and each module has either predictable behavior or a boundary explanation in the finite row.  This is the local content of the claim.

\paragraph{Scope restriction.}
The reality contact is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons receive amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read label splits and local codon-tile behavior.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Therefore the present verdict is a finite code-layer recovery statement: the window-six coordinate experiment recovers the held-out codon row at rate $1.0$ with $8$ predictable and $5$ boundary-classified cases, while the higher biological readings remain unavailable until a separate experiment supplies the relevant contact.
