\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery on the window-six codon cube}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
The object considered here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate binary display. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon represented as a six-bit point and with the curated standard-code table supplying the amino-acid-or-stop label attached to that point. The empirical contact is deliberately narrow: it records codon-to-label readback in the standard code. It does not observe translation dynamics, folding, physical feasibility, organismal function, or a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Recovered exceptional set.}
The finite row under audit identifies a distinguished set $R$ of thirteen codons. In lexicographic codon notation this set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$. The same row records that this set has the punctured-cube face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the geometry is not being introduced as an informal picture: the vertex, edge, and two-face counts are part of the finite certificate. The set contains the full $CUN$ leucine block $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, the stop-related triple $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$, the arginine pair $\{AGA,AGG\}$, and the singleton positions $AAA$, $AUA$, $UCA$, and $UUA$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out result.}
The audited leave-one-codon-out probe reports no anchor recoveries, five boundary recoveries, and eight high-coverage closure recoveries:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\_count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\_count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\_count}&=8.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recovery rate is exactly $1.0$, and the thresholded recovery rate is also $1.0$. Thus every codon in the audited leave-one-codon-out row is accounted for either by a predictable closure pattern or by a boundary classification. The result should be read as a finite local recovery statement on the code-layer geometry, not as a claim that the cube geometry causes the genetic code.

\paragraph{Module-level accounting.}
The module classification row assigns all thirteen elements of $R$ and leaves no codon unassigned. The complete partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, and empty lists for extra module codons, missing module codons, and unassigned codons. Consequently the leave-one-codon-out claim is not supported by a selectively reported subset: the whole thirteen-codon boundary set is covered by the module table.

\paragraph{Spectral and randomization context.}
The same finite packet records spectral values for the associated shape calculation. The numerical row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the radial cubic residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, within the stated tolerance. The shape-significance experiment used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$. It found one exact shape match, twenty-five matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries. The resulting finite-row estimates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, the observed set size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the statement that the local codon-tile pattern is unusually structured relative to the sampled nulls used in the audit.

\paragraph{Code-table leave-one-out comparison.}
A related leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the audited rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\_rate\_above\_baseline}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\_rate\_in\_M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\_rate\_in\_M\setminus T\ plus\ boundary}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-level table grouped the tested codes into five families. The above-baseline family hit rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families. These rows are not substitutes for the codon-level recovery certificate, but they show that the same local boundary signal remains visible when the table is perturbed at the code-family level.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The supported conclusion is a code-read conclusion: within the six-bit codon display, the audited thirteen-codon boundary set has complete module accounting and leave-one-codon-out recovery, with five boundary cases and eight predictable closure cases. The curated standard-code table supplies the labels used for readback, and the finite randomization rows indicate that the observed shape is rare under the sampled alternatives. Nothing in this certificate establishes translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, molecular function, a mechanism of code evolution, or a universal law of biology. Any assertion at one of those higher layers would require an additional contact that directly observes that layer.
