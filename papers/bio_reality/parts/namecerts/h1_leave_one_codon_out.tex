\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Readback on the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite object and readback layer.}
This record studies a finite six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$ whose observed labels are supplied by the curated standard genetic-code table.  The object under test is not translation as a biochemical process, but the code-layer table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  The finite carrier is therefore the codon cube together with a label readback map, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The intended question is whether the window-six coordinate representation exposes a reproducible local boundary pattern when one codon is withheld from the readback task.

The distinguished region $R$ is finite and has cardinality $13$.  In the verified row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies this set with a punctured-cube shape whose $f$-vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite observation is not merely that thirteen codons are listed.  It is that the listed codons occupy a particular cubical pattern with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces after the relevant puncture is made.

The shape significance row compares the observed finite shape against $2000$ sampled alternatives, generated with seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found exactly $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$. These checks support the statement that the observed local codon shape is uncommon under the stated finite randomizations.  They do not by themselves identify a causal molecular mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon recovery row.}
The claim-specific row leaves one codon out and asks whether the local coordinate closure recovers the relevant code-layer placement.  The verified row has experiment identifier \texttt{e9e1a6e41633}.  It reports no anchor codons, five boundary codons, eight high-coverage closure hits, and eight predictable cases:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\ count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\ count}&=8.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the reported above-threshold recovery value is also $1.0$.  In finite-row language, every case counted as predictable by the local codon-window criterion is recovered by the coordinate closure test.  Since the anchor count is zero, the recovery is not being attributed to a separate singleton anchor mechanism; it is read as a boundary-and-closure phenomenon on the window-six display.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The supported conclusion is narrow.  The six-bit codon cube, together with curated standard-code labels, exposes a local boundary structure for the thirteen-codon region $R$.  In the leave-one-codon row, the predictable cases are recovered at rate $1.0$, while five codons are boundary cases and no codon is counted as an anchor.  The punctured-cube $f$-vector, spectral values, shape-randomization probabilities, amino-acid-class-preserving null row, and module completeness row all point to a finite code-layer pattern rather than to a generic table artifact.

The contact with biology is the curated codon-to-label table.  That contact is enough to test label readback and local codon-tile splitting.  It is not a contact with ribosomal dynamics, protein folding, thermodynamic admissibility, cellular function, evolutionary necessity, or a universal biological law.  Consequently, this chapter claims a finite code-layer regularity and nothing above that layer.  Any assertion about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require an additional external observation designed for that layer.
