\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Local Tile Boundary}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record tests whether the standard-code codon table, viewed through a six-coordinate codon display, contains enough local boundary information to recover the left-out codon label position from nearby code-layer data.  The object under inspection is not translation itself, nor a physical model of ribosome action.  It is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels, read as a finite table whose rows can be probed by deletion, closure, and boundary membership.

\paragraph{Finite display.}
The codon carrier is the six-bit cube $Q_6$, with one coordinate pair for each codon position.  The relevant exceptional set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured local cube, this same set has face-vector data $(13,16,4)$.  The punctured-cube description is important because the leave-one-codon test is not merely asking whether a deleted row is memorized; it asks whether the missing row lies in a small boundary region whose combinatorial shape is stable under the chosen codon-coordinate view.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The same finite packet reports a spectral scale separating the main local component from its boundary complement.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check is numerically closed at $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the spectral row is treated as a finite numerical certificate of the displayed codon geometry rather than as a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the reported null sampling row with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact observed shape occurs once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-Jaccard event at threshold $0.9$ occurs $25$ times, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and saw $55377$ duplicate retries, which is recorded here because the null comparison is finite and row-based, not asymptotic prose.  A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ reports observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape value $0.0$, and lower-tail value $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon result.}
For the claim indexed by this chapter, the leave-one-codon experiment has run id $e9e1a6e41633$.  The finite row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The threshold comparison also records $1.0$, so every codon counted in the predictable class is recovered by the local closure-or-boundary criterion in this finite packet.  The absence of anchors is part of the result: the recovery is not being attributed to a distinguished singleton that directly names the deleted row, but to the local tile and its boundary behavior.

\paragraph{Code-table comparison.}
The related leave-one-code-row experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the same test after adding the boundary gives $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline row is $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  These values support the narrower statement that a code-layer local-tile pattern is visible in the curated label table.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification assigns all $13$ codons in $R$ and reports no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has either a predictable member or a boundary member in the recorded classification.  The classification is therefore complete at the codon-label layer: $R$-size is $13$, assigned count is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons are all empty.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  That contact observes codon-to-label assignments only.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any necessity claim about DNA obeying this coordinate geometry.  Amino-acid label readback is consequently kept at the code layer.  Any promotion beyond that layer would require an independent contact designed for the promoted claim, for example a translation experiment for translation, a structure contact for folding, or a physical assay for admissibility.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite evidence is strong for the local claim: in the standard-code table, the six-coordinate codon view exposes a small punctured-cube boundary region, and the leave-one-codon deletion row recovers the predictable class with rate $1.0$.  The result is therefore a code-read boundary theorem candidate for this finite packet, not a statement about protein realization or biological universality.
