\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Boundary Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  The table is used here only as a code-layer reality contact: it supplies label readback, not translation realization, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The observed boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.
As a finite row object it is a punctured-cube tile with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2) &= (13,16,4),\\
|R| &= 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
This fixes the local object being tested: a window-six coordinate tile with singleton-corner and three-point-label-class distinctions, read back against the standard-code labels.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape witnesses.}
The spectral row attached to the same finite carrier records $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the radial polynomial residual, so the numerical row is internally consistent at the displayed precision:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,\\
P_{\mathrm{radial}} &= -6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The shape row was tested against $N=2000$ distinct samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries under seed $42$.  There was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$ and $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving $p=0.0035$ for that lower-tail event.  The supplied finite rows therefore support a non-generic local code-read shape, while still leaving its biological promotion blocked.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon rows.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row is sharper for this claim: it reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  In finite-row form,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchors} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{boundary} &= 5,\\
\mathrm{high\ closure\ coverage} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{predictable} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the test does not say that every codon is mechanically generated by the window-six geometry.  It says that, after one codon is withheld from the code-read table, the local tile plus boundary classification recovers the withheld row at the reported threshold in this finite test.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module partition is complete on the $13$ codons of $R$: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons.  The six reported modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

$$
Every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the supplied classification.  The family-level leave-one-code-out row covers $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate above baseline $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate above baseline $1.0$.  These rows make the boundary phenomenon visible across code families, but they do not turn the local tile into a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The claim is ready at the code-read layer.  The curated standard-code table supports codon-label readback and local split checks, and the finite rows give a specific recoverable boundary tile: $|R|=13$, punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, recovery rate $1.0$, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, and a complete $13$-codon module classification.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result.  No translation event, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, global mechanism, or universality follows from these rows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.  Amino-acid label readback remains label readback; it is not folded structure and it is not functional realization.
