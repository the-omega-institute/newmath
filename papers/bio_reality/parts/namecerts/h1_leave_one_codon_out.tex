\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out recovery in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite object and observed row.}
The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with the curated standard-code label table as its code-layer readout.  Each codon is treated as a finite six-bit display point, and the only empirical contact used here is the assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels in that curated table.  The leave-one-codon-out row tests whether a local tile pattern can recover the held-out code label position from the remaining finite context without promoting that recovery into a claim about translation dynamics, molecular structure, physical realizability, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

The distinguished row set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured cube subcomplex its finite face vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of codons: it has a concrete local cubical signature, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces.  The row is read only at the codon-label level; a successful local recovery means that the held-out codon sits in a recoverable label tile, not that the cell implements a physical or evolutionary law.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}
The associated spectral measurement separates the marked row geometry from a shapeless finite sample.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic relation was numerically satisfied to the recorded residual
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is within the finite computation tolerance for the row spectrum.  In the shape randomization test with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold also remained small: $25$ samples had Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid class structure used $N=2000$ samples with the same seed.  It found $0$ exact shape matches and $7$ samples at or below the observed marked-set size $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0,\qquad p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support the narrower statement that the standard-code row has an atypical local finite shape under the tested null families.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out result.}
For the specific claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.codon.out}$, the finite leave-one-codon-out run reported
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\_count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\_count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\_count}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\_rate}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{recovery\_rate\_above\_threshold}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row therefore has eight predictable held-out positions and five boundary positions, with no anchor positions in this run.  The numerical recovery rate $1.0$ is a finite-row statement: every codon tested inside this local window-six recovery criterion was recovered by the stated local rule.  The phrase ``recovered'' is used here only for codon-label reconstruction in the curated table.  It is not a statement that the codon is translated, folded, selected, or functionally realized by this finite geometry.

The classification audit recorded $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, with no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has either a predictable member or a boundary member in the finite recovery test.  This matters because the recovery pattern is organized by named codon-label modules rather than by an uninterpreted count alone.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The result supports the following bounded conclusion.  In the standard-code codon table, the window-six display contains a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row whose local closure and module structure recover the held-out codon-label positions in this run at rate $1.0$.  The same data also show nontrivial separation from random shape controls and amino-acid-class-preserving controls, with the recorded exact-shape probabilities $0.0005$ and $0.0$ respectively.  Therefore the row is a finite code-layer signal: it is a reproducible local pattern in a curated codon-label table.

The same evidence also fixes the stopping point.  The curated table supplies codon-to-label assignments, not ribosomal realization, folded protein geometry, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, causal mechanism, or universal biological necessity.  Any assertion past codon-label readback would require an additional contact of the appropriate kind.  Without such a contact, the mathematically supported statement remains the local tile recovery statement above.
