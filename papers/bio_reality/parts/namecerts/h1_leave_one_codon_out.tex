\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Boundary Test for the Standard Code}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
This record tests a window-six representation of the standard genetic code as a finite codon-label readback problem.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon treated as a coordinate word and each curated amino-acid or stop assignment treated as a label supplied by the standard-code table.  The finite row under test is not a claim about translation dynamics.  It asks whether leaving one codon label out of the local display leaves enough coordinate, closure, spectral, and relation data to recover the held-out code-layer label position as predictable or as a boundary case.

\paragraph{Observed exceptional row.}
The distinguished set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same list and records the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not only a list of codons; it also carries a small cell structure inside the codon cube.  In the leave-one-codon test the direct anchor count is $0$, the boundary count is $5$, the predictable count is $8$, and the high-coverage closure count is $8$.  The recorded recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0,
\qquad
\mathrm{above\ threshold}=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The interpretation is narrow: every held-out codon in this finite row is accounted for by predictable closure behavior or by a declared boundary position, while no codon is treated as an independent anchor.

\paragraph{Spectral and null checks.}
The finite geometry also has a numerical spectral signature.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the cubic-polynomial check, and the spectral radius in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers do not create a biological mechanism; they certify that the finite coordinate display has a stable algebraic summary at the resolution tested.

The shape test used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ duplicate retries, $1$ exact shape match, and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The exact-shape $p$-value is $0.0005$, and the high-Jaccard $p$-value is $0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, the exact-shape $p$-value is $0.0$, and the lower-tail $p$-value is $0.0035$.  These null checks support the claim that the row is not an ordinary random relabeling artifact at the tested finite scale.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose role is to assign codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  It does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law forcing DNA to obey this finite geometry.  Therefore the result stays at the code-read layer: the window-six tile identifies a compact and statistically unusual finite pattern in codon-label data, but no higher biological layer follows unless a separate observation supplies that layer.
