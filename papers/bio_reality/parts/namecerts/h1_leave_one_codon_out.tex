\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null models.}
The first null comparison used $N=2000$ randomized samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and therefore incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sample matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null comparison preserved amino-acid class information.  In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, so the exact-shape probability was recorded as $0.0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, with observed $M$ size $20$, giving

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}

These values support the finite claim that the local codon-tile shape is unusually specific under the tested row shuffles.  They do not promote the tile into a claim about protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, or a necessary code mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out result.}
The codon-level leave-one-out run is the central row of this chapter.  It recorded
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\ count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\ count}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}&=1.0.

$$
The recovery rate above threshold was also $1.0$.  In words, every withheld codon in the tested finite setting was recovered by the local closure-or-boundary apparatus used in the run, but none was recovered as an anchor.  The result is therefore better read as boundary-sensitive local recoverability than as an autonomous biological explanation.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only contact with biological data in this chapter is the curated standard genetic-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code-layer readback and local codon-tile label splitting.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded structure, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently, the amino-acid label sequence obtained from codon readback is not a protein-structure observation.  The local tile may expose a finite boundary pattern in $Q_6$, but a claim about translation, folding, admissible chemistry, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate empirical contact aimed at that layer.

\paragraph{Verdict of the finite record.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded conclusion.  In the standard-code codon-label table, the window-six coordinate representation isolates a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row set with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, stable spectral values, nontrivial null-model significance, complete module assignment, and perfect recovery in the reported leave-one-codon-out run.  The scientific content is therefore a local code-read regularity: a withheld codon label can be recovered from the finite closure-or-boundary pattern in the tested packet.  The result is not a statement that biological translation is generated by this geometry, nor that the resulting amino-acid labels determine folded structure or function.
