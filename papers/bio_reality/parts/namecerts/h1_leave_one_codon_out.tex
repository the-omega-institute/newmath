\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Boundary Readback}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-boundary-readback}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite-row claim \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out} is a code-read claim about the standard-code codon table in a six-bit codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, equipped with coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation structure.  The relevant distinctions are a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The biological contact is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels, and it supplies no translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or law of biology.  Thus the claim is not that codons biologically obey the geometry of $Q_6$ as a necessity.  It is that the finite code labels, when read through the six-bit display, expose a local boundary-tile pattern whose recovery statistics can be audited row by row.

\paragraph{Finite carrier and punctured region.}
The distinguished region $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The row audit reports exactly the following codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same audit identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube region with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local geometric object used by the leave-one-codon-out test.  It is finite and fully enumerated: there are no hidden continuum assumptions, no appeal to a statistical population outside the rows sampled by the experiment, and no claim that the punctured cube is itself a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The shape-significance audit used $N=2000$ finite samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It reported $0$ exact matches, $7$ lower-tail rows at or below the observed $M$ size, and observed $M$ size $20$.  Hence the audited finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values make the local shape non-generic under the sampled nulls.  They do not identify a causal pathway; they only say that the six-bit coordinate readback sees a finite pattern that is unusual under the stated row-generating procedures.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge.  Its observed fact is that a curated standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Its resolution is codon label readback.  Its known limitation is that the table is a bridge for labels, not a mechanism or universality proof.  Therefore the local tile can test code-read structure and local codon-tile label split, but it cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
The claim is ready at the code-read layer.  The data support a finite, chapter-local statement: in the six-bit codon cube, the enumerated $13$-codon punctured region $R$ has a non-generic local shape, a stable spectral row, complete module classification, and leave-one-codon-out recovery rate $1.0$ with $8$ predictable rows and $5$ boundary rows.  The conclusion stops exactly there.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that additional evidence.
