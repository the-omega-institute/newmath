\subsection{Leave-one-codon recovery on the local codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The claim \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out} is a code-read claim about the standard genetic-code table, not a claim about translation dynamics or protein realization.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a binary display of codon positions after the four RNA letters have been encoded into two-bit coordinates.  Within that carrier, the observed row isolates a local tile with three relevant kinds of finite distinction: a face-level codon neighborhood, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological contact is the curated standard-code label table, whose observation is only that each codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label.  The experiment asks whether removing one codon from the local row still leaves enough internal coordinate information to recover its code-layer position as predictable or boundary-supported.

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the observed standard-code coordinate realization it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured cube with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not recorded merely as a list of exceptional codons; it is also recorded as a finite cubical object with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces.  The row covers six module names without leftover codons: \texttt{agr}, \texttt{ar\_lys}, \texttt{cun}, \texttt{ile\_met}, \texttt{ssn}, and \texttt{stop\_trp}.  The module assignment is complete because $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, the list of extra module codons is empty, the list missing from the modules is empty, and the unassigned list is empty.

\paragraph{Recovery statistic.}
In the leave-one-codon-out run, no removed codon is recovered by an anchor-only condition: the anchor count is $0$.  The boundary count is $5$, and the high-closure coverage count is $8$.  The predictable count is therefore $8$, with recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0, \qquad
\mathrm{above\ threshold}=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This says that, for this finite row, each withheld codon is accounted for by the local coordinate-and-boundary structure available inside the code-read layer.  The result is strongest as a local finite-row recovery statement: the codon table labels are supplied by the curated code table, while the recovery computation uses the window-six coordinate geometry to test how much of the missing entry is constrained by the surrounding row.

\paragraph{Module structure.}
The module decomposition is concrete.  The \texttt{agr} module contains $\mathrm{AGA}$ and $\mathrm{AGG}$; \texttt{ar\_lys} contains $\mathrm{AAA}$; \texttt{cun} contains $\mathrm{CUA}$, $\mathrm{CUC}$, $\mathrm{CUG}$, and $\mathrm{CUU}$; \texttt{ile\_met} contains $\mathrm{AUA}$; \texttt{ssn} contains $\mathrm{UCA}$ and $\mathrm{UUA}$; and \texttt{stop\_trp} contains $\mathrm{UAA}$, $\mathrm{UAG}$, and $\mathrm{UGA}$.  Each module has either predictable support or boundary support in the leave-one-codon experiment.  This matters because the recovery is not a single isolated codon coincidence: it is distributed across a two-codon arginine-related pair, a lysine singleton, the four-codon CUN block, an isoleucine--methionine singleton, a two-codon SSN row, and the three-codon stop--tryptophan boundary region.

\paragraph{Spectral and null comparison.}
The same finite carrier has a recorded spectral profile.  The observed values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic radial check is numerically near zero, with value $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius tolerance record repeats $0.6752479308830213$.  Against the basic shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape probability was $0.0005$, while the count of samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving probability $0.0125$.  Against the amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, exact shape matches were $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the row is a non-generic local code-table pattern under the tested finite shuffles.  They do not, by themselves, identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Relation to whole-code leave-one-out behavior.}
A related whole-code leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid tables, $1.0$ for the candidate-other class, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate tables.  These companion rows are not used to convert the present codon-level statement into a universal rule; they only show that the same finite coordinate idiom also has high hit rates in a broader code-table comparison.

\paragraph{Reality contact and limit of interpretation.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  It can test codon-label readback and local label splits inside the code layer.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  The local tile may expose a boundary pattern in the six-coordinate codon display, but the labels remain imported from the curated biological table.  Therefore the valid conclusion is narrow: in this finite standard-code row, the leave-one-codon experiment recovers every removed entry through predictable or boundary-supported code-layer structure, with $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  No statement about protein folding, structural order, molecular feasibility, organism-level function, or necessity across biology follows unless a separate observation supplies that higher-layer contact.
