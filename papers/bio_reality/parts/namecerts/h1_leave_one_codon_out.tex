\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out codon recovery on a local window-six tile}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object and coordinate setting.} The finite object is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display on the codon cube $Q_6$. The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments, where a label is an amino-acid name or stop. The internal record uses only finite coordinates, closure membership, spectral summaries, and a relation between singleton label corners and three-point label classes. The observed readout is therefore a code-layer label table, not a realization of translation chemistry.

\paragraph{Distinguished codon set.} The finite row identifies a distinguished set $R$ of size $13$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row reports that this set has the punctured-cube face count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the claim is not that all $64$ codons are geometrically explained. The claim is that this thirteen-codon region is the finite site where the window-six coordinates expose a local boundary pattern compatible with the curated labels.

\paragraph{Spectral finite check.} The spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The reported radial cubic residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual. At the precision of the finite computation, the spectral-radius summary is therefore internally consistent. This is a numerical constraint on the codon-coordinate packet; it is not a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out result.} The leave-one-codon-out experiment has run identifier $e9e1a6e41633$. It reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, and $0$ anchor codons, with high closure coverage equal to $8$. The recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{8}{8}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the threshold comparison also records $1.0$. In words, every codon counted as predictable by the finite test is recovered by the local closure-and-boundary rule. The five boundary cases are not failures of the code table; they are the finite places where the local tile reaches the edge of what this contact can certify.

\paragraph{Module accounting.} The module classification is complete on $R$: the reported $R$-size is $13$, the assigned count is $13$, there are no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are

Each listed module has at least one predictable or boundary member. This matters because the finite row is not merely a count of recovered codons; it partitions the thirteen-codon region into named local modules without leaving any residue outside the classification.

The reported hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate in $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. These rows provide supporting finite context for the local codon test: the local tile is not isolated from the curated code-label comparison, but the interpretation remains at the label-readout layer.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.} The shape-significance row samples $N=2000$ alternatives with seed $42$. It records $1$ exact shape match, $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries. The corresponding probabilities are

p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, records observed $M$ size $20$, exact match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$. These null rows make the local geometry nontrivial relative to the tested finite alternatives, while still leaving the observation as a code-layer finite pattern.

\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.} The curated table observes codon labels only. From this chapter one may say that the six-bit codon coordinates expose a local tile-and-boundary structure on the standard-code label table, with complete module accounting on the thirteen-codon set $R$ and recovery rate $1.0$ among the predictable codons. One may not infer translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a biochemical mechanism, or a universal biological law. Any such stronger statement would require a separate contact that directly observes that higher layer. In particular, amino-acid label readback is not protein structure, and a local codon tile is not evidence that living systems must obey this geometry.
