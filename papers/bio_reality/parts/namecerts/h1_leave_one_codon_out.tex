\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Cube}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The claim \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out} is a code-layer record for the standard genetic-code table viewed through a six-bit codon coordinate display. The carrier is the finite codon cube $Q_6$, with the observed row $R$ consisting of the thirteen codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$. The punctured-cube check gives the same thirteen codons and records the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object is not merely a list of labels: it has a small local face structure inside $Q_6$, with singleton corners, local tile faces, and a three-point label class visible before any higher biological reading is attempted.

\paragraph{Spectral row signature.}

For this chapter the point of the spectrum is diagnostic rather than mechanistic: it distinguishes the observed local codon row from a featureless random row, but it does not by itself identify translation chemistry, folding, or function.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The shape-significance experiment used $N=2000$ finite samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. Among those samples there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null used the same sample count and seed. It found $0$ exact matches, $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size, and an observed $M$-size of $20$. Its finite probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite counts support a row-specific local pattern: the observed codon subset is unusually placed relative to the tested null families. They do not turn the window-six coordinate system into a global biological rule.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code context.}
The broader leave-one-code experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate after adjoining the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The same value, $0.9841269841269841$, was recorded for the hit rate above baseline. By family, $5$ families were recorded: alternative yeast had rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each had rate $1.0$. This supplies the immediate finite background for the codon-level leave-one test: the code-table rows already show strong local recovery behavior before the single-codon deletion experiment is isolated.

\paragraph{Single-codon deletion result.}
For \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out}, the direct leave-one-codon experiment recorded
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\ count} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count} &= 5,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\ count} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The threshold comparison also recorded $\mathrm{recovery\ rate\ above\ threshold}=1.0$. The absence of anchor recoveries is important: this is not presented as a fixed-point or hard-coded singleton phenomenon. The recoveries occur through the local closure and boundary organization of the codon row. In this finite reading, every omitted codon is recovered either as predictable from the local closure pattern or as a boundary codon of that pattern.

\paragraph{Module-level accounting.}
The module classification assigns all thirteen codons of $R$ with no residue. The completeness record has $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR/Lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has at least one predictable-or-boundary representative in the recorded classification. The local codon-tile claim is therefore not a loose aggregate statement over thirteen unrelated codons; it is a complete partition of the observed row into biologically named code-label modules, checked against the curated standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table: it assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels and thereby permits code-layer readback. This contact tests codon-label assignments and local label splits only. It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal mechanism. Consequently the conclusion is deliberately narrow: the window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table, and the leave-one-codon test recovers the thirteen-row module structure with rate $1.0$. No claim about translation realization, protein folding, physical viability, biological function, or universality follows from this chapter unless a separate empirical contact supplies that higher-layer information.
