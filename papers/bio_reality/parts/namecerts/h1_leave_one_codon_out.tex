\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The claim \texttt{h1.leave.one.codon.out} concerns a finite readback problem on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is a codon-display coordinate system whose observable contact is the curated standard-code table.  The table supplies codon-to-label assignments at the code-read layer only.  The BEDC form used here has four finite distinctions: a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  Its internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation, while its readback is the standard-code label table.

The finite exceptional row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.  The same row is observed as a punctured-cube pattern with face vector $(13,16,4)$.  This fixes the finite object under discussion: the row is neither an all-codon law nor a protein-level object, but a thirteen-codon local boundary pattern inside the window-six coordinate display.

The recovery rate is exactly $1.0$, and the recovery-rate-above-threshold statistic is also $1.0$.  In the finite notation of the run this is

$$
Thus every tested removed codon in the recorded boundary row is classified either by predictable closure behavior or by boundary status.  The result is a finite-row recovery statement, not a statement that codons are generated by the geometry as a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The local verdict is therefore ready: window-six coordinates expose a finite thirteen-codon boundary tile whose leave-one-codon recovery rate is $1.0$, whose module assignment is complete, and whose spectral and null-model summaries are non-generic in the recorded finite rows.  The biological contact, however, is only the curated standard genetic-code table.  Consequently, the chapter claims code-read support for a local codon-tile split and nothing stronger.  No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these data unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
