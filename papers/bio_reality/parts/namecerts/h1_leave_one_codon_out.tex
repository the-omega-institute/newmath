\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the distinctions of a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The external contact is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact supplies label readback; it does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The relevant boundary statement is therefore local: a window-six codon tile may expose a reproducible boundary pattern in the code labels, but it is not a global biological law.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The contact table contains codon-to-label assignments and nothing higher.  Therefore the present row cannot claim that DNA obeys the window-six geometry as a biological necessity; it cannot claim translation realization; it cannot claim folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary law.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any such promotion would require a separate external contact, such as a translation-system contact, a structure experiment, a physical-admissibility test, or a function assay.  Without such a contact, the correct conclusion is exactly local: the curated standard-code table aligns with a finite window-six boundary-tile pattern in the thirteen-codon set $R$, with full leave-one-codon-out recovery in the recorded row.
