\subsection{Leave-one-codon recovery in a window-six codon tile}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
This record treats the standard-code table as a curated codon-label contact and asks only a code-layer question: when one codon in the distinguished window-six region is withheld, does the local finite geometry identify it as recoverable, boundary-controlled, or outside the tested scope?  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the usual codon alphabet and then compared with the curated standard-code label table.  The finite distinguished region $R$ contains exactly thirteen codons,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13 .
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite object is reported as a punctured-cube pattern with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not a free-form biological assertion; it is a concrete finite subset of the codon cube with a fixed local combinatorial shape.  The complementary labeled structure $M$ is reported with observed size $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null test, but the present row does not provide an explicit element-by-element list for $M$, so this chapter uses only the reported scalar and comparison data for $M$.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The finite-row packet records a spectral split between the distinguished region and the larger labeled structure.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, numerically consistent with the stated cubic relation at the displayed precision.  A shape-randomization check with $N=2000$ samples found one exact shape match and twenty-five samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125 .
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, with seed $42$.  These figures support the narrower statement that the observed local tile is uncommon under the sampled finite null family.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause, a translational process, or a structural constraint.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon recovery.}
For the specific leave-one-codon row, the experiment reports no anchor codons, five boundary codons, eight high-coverage closure codons, and eight predictable codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\_count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\_count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\_count}&=8.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recovery rate is $1.0$, and the threshold comparison is also $1.0$.  In finite-row terms, every withheld codon considered recoverable by the test is accounted for either by the closure-side signal or by the recorded boundary status; none is claimed to be explained by a separate mechanistic principle.  The result is therefore a local readback result: the six-bit codon presentation, together with the curated labels, exposes a stable leave-one-codon pattern inside the thirteen-codon region $R$.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification covers all thirteen codons in $R$ and introduces no extra codons.  The reported assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completion audit gives $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no unassigned codons, and no extra module codons.  Hence the local recovery claim is not hiding an unclassified residue of the finite set.  Each named module has at least one predictable or boundary member, so the leave-one-codon behavior is distributed across the displayed codon families rather than concentrated in a single exceptional row.

Inside $M\setminus T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and after adding the boundary contribution it rose to $0.9841269841269841$.  Family-level aggregation over five families reports above-baseline rates $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial or plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate-other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate codes.  These numbers are useful controls for the codon-level row because they show that the local leave-one-codon result sits inside a broader code-read pattern, while still remaining a statement about codon-label readback rather than biological implementation.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It found no exact shape matches, recorded seven lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size, and reported observed $M$ size $20$.  The corresponding finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact\ shape}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035 .
\end{aligned}
$$
This comparison keeps the label classes in view while testing whether the observed shape is a routine consequence of class preservation alone.  Within the sampled resolution, the reported row says that the exact local shape was not reproduced and that the lower-tail event was rare.  The conclusion is still limited to the finite code-read setup: it is evidence about a labeled codon table under specified finite probes, not evidence that the geometry is a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test codon-label readback and local label splitting.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a causal mechanism, or universality across living systems.  Consequently, the finite row supports the following bounded statement: in the window-six codon display, the thirteen-codon punctured-cube region has a complete module accounting and a leave-one-codon recovery rate of $1.0$ under the supplied closure and boundary criteria.  It does not license any claim that the local tile determines translation, predicts folding, constrains physical chemistry, explains function, supplies a universal mechanism, or replaces separate biological experiments at those higher layers.
