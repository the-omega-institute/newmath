\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite reality contact at the code-read layer. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the sixty-four codons over $\{\mathrm{U},\mathrm{C},\mathrm{A},\mathrm{G}\}^3$. The observed contact is only the curated assignment of each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop. It is not a contact with translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The local BEDC object is therefore a finite coordinate-and-label readback: it may expose a boundary-tile pattern inside the codon cube, but it cannot promote that pattern into a biological law without an additional contact.

\paragraph{The recovered punctured row.}
The finite row singled out by the window-six construction has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the chapter is not using an unconstrained biological narrative: the object being read back is a specific finite subset of $Q_6$, with thirteen named codons and a measured local cubical incidence profile.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
Against the sampled shape null with $N=2000$ draws and seed $42$, the exact shape appeared once. This gives
$$
\begin{aligned}

p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ high-Jaccard matches and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, including $55377$ duplicate retries. The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a separate finite check: with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact match count is $0$, the observed $M$-size is $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$. Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the statement that the recovered local row is not a typical outcome of the tested finite randomizations. They do not by themselves identify a mechanism for why the curated code table has its labels.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon test.}
The chapter-specific test removes one codon label contact at a time and asks whether the local coordinate structure recovers the missing code-read target within the allowed readback boundary. The verified run reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{predictable\ count} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count} &= 5,\\
\mathrm{anchor\ count} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus all thirteen row codons are accounted for either by the predictable part or by the boundary part, with no anchor-only cases. The value $\mathrm{recovery\ rate\ above\ threshold}=1.0$ records that the leave-one-codon criterion clears its finite threshold on this row. The correct interpretation is narrow: the omitted codon label is recoverable as a code-layer readback inside this local tile protocol.

The classification reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, $\mathrm{assigned\ count}=13$, no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons. It also verifies that every module has a predictable-or-boundary representative. This is the finite reason the row is treated as a local tile rather than as thirteen unrelated singleton observations.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The local tile is a code-read object. From the finite data above one may claim a named row $R$, its punctured-cube incidence profile, its spectral diagnostics, its null-model rarity under the tested finite randomizations, and its leave-one-codon recovery behavior against the curated standard-code table. One may not claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or biological necessity. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. Any promotion across that boundary requires a separate reality contact with the higher layer being claimed.
