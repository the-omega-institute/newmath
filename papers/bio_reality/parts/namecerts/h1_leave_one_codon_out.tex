\paragraph{Object and coordinate scope.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display on the codon cube $Q_6$.  The working distinctions are the six displayed coordinates, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label.  The contact therefore tests code-label readback and local split behavior, but it does not observe translation in a cell, folded structure, energetic admissibility, biochemical function, a generative mechanism, or universality across biological systems.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The supported claim is confined to code readback.  The curated table supplies labels, and the coordinate construction supplies a local finite test over those labels.  Nothing in this record measures ribosomal decoding dynamics, tRNA availability, organism-specific implementation, three-dimensional folding, physical admissibility, biological activity, or an all-organism law.  Any assertion in one of those higher layers would require a separate contact that directly observes that layer.  Without such a contact, amino-acid label sequences remain label sequences, and the local tile remains a code-layer boundary pattern rather than a biological mechanism.
