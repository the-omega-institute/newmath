\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The self-check row states that this is a BioReality self-check only and that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. Its checked gate count is $3$. The packet has zero attached reality contacts and zero mismatch rows. Consequently the only admissible claim is the boundary claim: a start/stop-window ORF-eligibility seed remains a contact-gap record until a separate curated ORF interval annotation, sequence contact, or transcript contact supplies the observed biological interval.

No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this packet. In particular, start/stop-window eligibility is not translation realization, not folded structure, not physical admissibility, and not biological function. The finite rows may define a candidate test surface, but they do not determine that a real organism contains the interval, that a ribosome translates it, that a peptide is produced, that a molecule folds, or that any biological role is present. Any later promotion to an ORF-eligibility claim must attach a separate reality contact capable of testing the proposed interval; without that contact the correct layer remains boundary status.
