\subsection{ORF Eligibility as a Contact-Gap Boundary Record}\label{sec:bioreality-orf-eligibility-contact-gap-boundary}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite packet.} The BioReality packet named \texttt{orf\_eligibility.seed.boundary} records a seed-level boundary for open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration. Its biological object is not an observed open reading frame. It is the weaker finite-row question of what a start/stop-window enumeration would be allowed to claim before any sequence, transcript, or curated interval annotation has been attached. The carrier field is explicitly ``awaiting reality contact,'' the only listed distinction is also ``awaiting reality contact,'' and the internal structure field is ``none.'' Thus the packet has no internal biological interval, no curated ORF coordinate, and no external transcript witness.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.} The self-check fact records the boundary sentence: ``BioReality self-check only; no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.'' The same row records a checked gate count of $3$. The two probes attached to the packet enforce the same local boundary. The first requires a curated ORF interval annotation before start/stop-window geometry may be treated as an ORF-eligibility claim. The second rejects any packet that records ORF eligibility while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation contact can test the proposed interval. In the present finite data, the contact list is empty and the reality-contact reference list is empty. Therefore the correct chapter-level reading is that the packet is a contact-gap boundary record.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.} No translation realization follows from this enumeration. No folded structure follows from it. No physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from it. The finite rows above may describe start/stop-window coordinates, motif compression, saturation behavior, and quotient geometry, but they do not supply a biological interval and do not observe a translated product. Promotion to the layer \texttt{orf\_eligibility} requires a separate reality contact, such as a curated ORF interval annotation, sequence record, or transcript contact that can test the proposed interval. Until such a contact is attached, the packet remains admissible only as a boundary probe for what would have to be checked.
