\paragraph{Record.}

The object under test is not translation, protein production, folding, physical viability, cellular function, mechanism, or a universal biological law.  It is the narrower finite question of whether a nucleotide interval is eligible under a start--stop enumeration rule.  The self-check row states that codon geometry alone is not allowed to cross into any higher biological layer.  In the recorded run $0f47d2f0cf41$, the boundary field reads as a BioReality self-check, and the gate count is $3$.

\paragraph{Boundary conclusion.}
The finite evidence supports only the following local claim: within the declared finite dataset and frozen classifier, curated positive intervals and matched non-ORF decoys separate perfectly under start--stop eligibility, while boundary cases remain unpromoted.  The self-check therefore passes because the row does not convert codon geometry into biology by itself.  Even with the curated interval run, the conclusion remains confined to interval eligibility under the stated enumeration protocol.  No translation event, protein fold, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, genome-wide principle, or universal law follows from these rows unless another independently recorded contact supplies evidence for that higher layer.
