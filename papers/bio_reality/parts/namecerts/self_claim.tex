\subsection{Self-check boundary for ORF eligibility from start and stop enumeration}
\label{sec:bioreality-self-claim-orf-eligibility-boundary}

\paragraph{Local object.}
This record concerns the claim identifier \texttt{self.claim} for the layer \texttt{orf\_eligibility}.  The biological object is deliberately narrow: eligibility of an open-reading-frame interval as tested by start and stop codon enumeration.  The packet is not a claim that translation occurred, that a protein folded, that a molecule was physically admissible, that a biological function exists, that a mechanism has been identified, or that a universal biological law has been found.  Its role is to keep the enumerative codon geometry and the biological interval contact separate.  The local self-check reports three checked gates and records the boundary sentence that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The self-claim is therefore a local audit of separation.  The finite codon sets, the quotient geometry, the one-window start-stop classification, and the $168$-row curated interval contact can support an ORF-eligibility statement only at the stated layer and only for the finite supplied observations.  No inference to translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality is licensed by the coordinate data or by the interval classifier alone.  Any such stronger assertion would require a further contact that directly measures or curates that stronger biological layer.  The absence of mismatches in this row means only that the packet did not violate its own separation rule; it is not evidence that the separation rule can be ignored.
