\paragraph{Boundary probes.}
The two probes attached to the seed are both boundary-mismatch probes. The first, $\texttt{orf.eligibility.boundary.requires.contact}$, requires a $\texttt{curated.orf.interval.annotation}$ contact and is supported only while the packet remains a boundary probe for what would have to be tested. It breaks if the pipeline asserts ORF eligibility, translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, or biological function from start/stop-window enumeration alone. The second, $\texttt{orf.eligibility.contact.gap.check}$, has the same required contact and breaks if an ORF eligibility assertion is recorded while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation contact can test the proposed interval. Since the contact list is empty, both probes keep the packet at boundary status.

The local verdict is therefore: this packet is ready as a finite boundary record for the $\texttt{orf\_eligibility}$ layer, but it is not ready as an ORF-eligibility assertion about a biological interval. No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate sequence, transcript, or curated-annotation contact supplies it.
