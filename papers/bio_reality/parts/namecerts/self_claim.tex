\subsection{ORF eligibility as a contact-gated boundary claim}
\label{sec:bioreality-self-claim-orf-eligibility-boundary}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
This record concerns open-reading-frame eligibility under a deliberately narrow start--stop enumeration test.  The biological object is not a protein, a transcript, a translated product, a folded molecular state, or an organism-level function.  It is only the question of whether a finite nucleotide interval is eligible under a classifier that looks for a start codon and an admissible stop-window pattern.  The local claim is therefore boundary-valued: before a curated interval or comparable biological contact is attached, the packet records only what would have to be tested, not that the biological object has been observed.

The self-check for this packet reports three checked gates and records the boundary sentence: no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  In symbols, the allowed conclusion has the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{start/stop\ enumeration}+\mathrm{curated\ interval\ contact}
&\Longrightarrow \mathrm{ORF\ eligibility\ on\ this\ dataset},\\
\mathrm{start/stop\ enumeration}
&\nRightarrow \mathrm{translation},\ \mathrm{folding},\ \mathrm{mechanism},\ \mathrm{function},\ \mathrm{universality}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The second line is part of the mathematical content of the record: it prevents a finite syntactic classifier from being read as a biological realization claim.

The packet is ready as a BioReality boundary chapter because the finite claims are separated by their evidential roles.  The codon lists $R$ and $M$, the saturation result, and the quotient vectors describe the internal finite geometry.  The curated interval dataset supplies the contact needed to test ORF eligibility on a particular external biological manifest. The permissible conclusion is only ORF eligibility for this fixed contact layer.
