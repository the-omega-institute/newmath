\subsection{Self-check boundary for ORF eligibility}

\paragraph{Claim status.}
The record considered here is the self-check claim for open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration. Its local conclusion is deliberately narrow: the packet may organize a finite test of ORF boundary eligibility, but it does not by itself assert that any biological interval is translated, folded, physically admissible, functional, mechanistic, or universal. The self-check row reports three checked gates and records the boundary statement that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. Thus the object is not a biological realization claim; it is a disciplined admission rule for what must remain unasserted until a separate curated interval, sequence, transcript, or comparable contact supplies the observed biological interval.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The final interpretation is therefore a narrow eligibility result under external contact. The codon rows $R$, $M$, the $f3$ saturation data, and the quotient vectors provide finite internal organization. The start--stop enumeration row provides a scoped window classifier. The curated interval row supplies the biological contact needed to test the classifier against annotated intervals. Even with $168$ rows, $72$ accepted positives, $72$ rejected decoys, three stable held-out splits, and exact one-sided values of $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$ in the recorded tests, the claim remains bounded: it does not assert translation realization, protein folding, physical admissibility, molecular function, mechanism, organism-level significance, or universality. Those stronger layers require their own independent contacts and tests.
