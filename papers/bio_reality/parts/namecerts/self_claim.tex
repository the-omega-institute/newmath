\subsection{Self-Claim Boundary for ORF Eligibility}\label{subsec:self-claim-orf-eligibility}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim row.}
The row named \texttt{self.claim} is not an assertion that an open reading frame has been observed. It is a self-audit row for the layer \texttt{orf\_eligibility}. Its recorded boundary says that the BioReality check is internal to the packet and that no codon geometry is allowed to cross into a biological layer by itself. The row reports \texttt{checked\_gate\_count} equal to $3$, and its carrier, distinctions, and readback fields all remain at the waiting state for contact with biological data. Thus the row has a precise role: it licenses a question about what a start/stop enumeration would need in order to become testable, while refusing to treat that enumeration as an observed gene, transcript, protein, mechanism, or functional unit.

\paragraph{Finite codon geometry used only as a coordinate system.}
The finite codon data in the packet is explicit. The motif-compression row records
$$\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\\
&\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\},
\end{aligned}$$
with no missing and no extra entries relative to the motif union check. The larger finite set is certified by \texttt{h0.M.equals.WNR.CUN} as a $20$-element set,
$$\begin{aligned}
M = \{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AAG},\texttt{ACA},\texttt{ACG},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{AUG},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\\
&\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UCG},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UGG},\texttt{UUA},\texttt{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The saturation row further records that the $f_3$ boundary is empty, that $\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)$ contains the $18$ entries of $M$ except \texttt{ACA} and \texttt{ACG}, and that adjoining the mediated part with \texttt{ACA} and \texttt{ACG} recovers the same $20$-element set $M$. These facts make the finite enumeration reproducible, but they do not supply a biological interval. They are coordinates for a possible test, not evidence that translation begins, terminates, or occurs in a cell.

\paragraph{Quotient and window checks.}
The quotient-geometry row gives the finite shape of the coordinate calculation: the $M$ vector is $(20,38,26,8,1)$, the quotient vector is $(10,14,6,1)$, and the quotient has $10$ listed classes. The separate start/stop-window enumeration row loads one outside nucleotide-window manifest with \texttt{window\_count} equal to $1$ and classifies all $1$ input window by start/stop enumeration. Its own scope field is \texttt{external\_window\_start\_stop\_enumeration\_only}, and it records the non-promotion value \texttt{orf\_eligibility\_only}. Hence even the window classification is deliberately narrow: it says that a finite interval can be checked for start and stop structure under the declared rule, not that the interval is translated, folded, physically realizable as a molecule, biologically active, mechanistically explained, or universal across organisms.

\paragraph{Curated interval contact as a separate row.}
A linked curated-interval row supplies a stronger biological-contact check, but it remains separate from the self-claim row. That row loads \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json} with $168$ rows from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 accession \texttt{NC\_000913.3}, snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$. It records $72$ accepted positive intervals with accept rate $1.0$ and exact one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. It also records $72$ rejected matched decoys with rejection rate $1.0$ and the same upper-tail value, together with a predeclared paired baseline advantage of $72$ wins out of $72$ pairs and upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. The held-out split check has $3$ splits; each split has $24$ positives accepted at rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys rejected at rate $1.0$. Boundary controls contain $24$ cases, with $0$ promoted and promoted fraction $0.0$.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The self-claim row is therefore ready as a finite audit statement for the boundary of \texttt{orf\_eligibility}. It cites concrete codon sets, quotient counts, a one-window enumeration check, and a linked curated-interval contact row with $168$ rows and exact finite-test statistics. Its own contacts list is empty, however, so its conclusion must remain local and negative in scope: start/stop geometry alone does not establish translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or a general biological law. Any promotion beyond ORF eligibility requires another declared contact that directly supplies the relevant biological observation and a separate test for that higher layer.
