\paragraph{Finite packet.}

\paragraph{Self-check gate.}
The self-check row is the biological gate for this subsection.  It records the boundary sentence: BioReality self-check only; no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  It also records a checked gate count of $3$.  The content of those three gate checks is local to this seed: first, codon-window enumeration may be used as a probe-forming device; second, the probe is still missing the required biological interval contact; third, no conclusion at the ORF-eligibility layer is licensed until that contact exists.  In this packet there are no attached contacts, and the list of reality-contact references is empty.  The two probe records therefore do not certify an ORF.  They certify only that an ORF-eligibility claim would require a curated sequence, transcript, or interval annotation contact before it could be tested.

\paragraph{Boundary claim.}
The admissible statement is the following.  The finite codon row supplies a seed boundary for the layer named $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$.  It gives concrete start/stop-window and quotient-geometry data, including the $13$-codon set $R$, the $20$-codon set $M$, the $18$-codon $f3$ saturation row, the empty $f3$ boundary, and the quotient size $10$.  Those data may organize a probe, but they do not assert that any biological interval has been observed.  An observed ORF eligibility claim would need a separate external contact, such as a curated ORF interval annotation, a sequence database interval, or transcript evidence that supplies the biological object to which the codon window is being applied.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
Nothing in this seed establishes translation realization.  Nothing establishes protein folding, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, organism-level role, evolutionary universality, or general genomic law.  The row also does not claim that every start/stop-window enumeration is an ORF, that every eligible-looking interval is translated, or that codon geometry by itself supplies biological reality contact.  The strongest local conclusion is negative and procedural: until a separate contact supplies an observed interval, this packet remains a boundary mismatch record for what would have to be checked, not a biological realization of the ORF-eligibility layer.
