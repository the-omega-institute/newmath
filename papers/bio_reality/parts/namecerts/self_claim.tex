\subsection{Self-check boundary for ORF eligibility}
\label{sec:bioreality-self-claim-orf-eligibility}

\paragraph{Claim form.}
The record named \texttt{self.claim} is a local audit of the ORF-eligibility layer.  Its biological object is not a translated product, a folded molecule, a physical model, or a functional annotation.  It is the much weaker question whether a nucleotide interval is eligible under start/stop codon enumeration after an independent biological interval has been supplied.  The seed statement therefore begins in a deliberately empty observational state: the carrier is awaiting contact with reality, its only distinction is the same pending-contact distinction, its internal structure is recorded as none, and its readback is also pending-contact.  In this form the packet is not evidence for an ORF.  It is a finite rule for refusing premature promotion.

\paragraph{Layer boundary.}
The self-check row records checked gate count $3$ and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  This is the controlling interpretation of the chapter.  Start/stop enumeration plus the curated interval contact may certify eligibility for the supplied intervals under the recorded classifier.  It does not establish translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or a universal biological law.  No such conclusion follows from the codon sets $R$ and $M$, from the quotient geometry, from the one-window manifest, or from the $168$-row curated interval dataset unless a separate contact designed for that stronger layer is supplied.  The result is therefore a finite layer-local audit: codon geometry can be used only inside the declared ORF-eligibility test, and every stronger biological reading remains outside the claim.
