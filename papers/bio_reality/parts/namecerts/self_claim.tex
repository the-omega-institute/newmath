\subsection{ORF Eligibility as a Contact-Gated Finite Record}
\label{sec:biorality-self-claim-orfer-eligibility-contact-gate}

\paragraph{Finite claim being tested.}
The record identified by \texttt{self.claim} concerns open-reading-frame eligibility obtained from start and stop codon enumeration.  Its biological layer is \texttt{orf\_eligibility}; the assertion is deliberately weaker than an assertion that translation occurs.  The finite claim says that start/stop-window geometry can be kept as a testable eligibility object only after an independent biological interval contact is attached.  Before such a contact, the coordinate data remain a boundary object: the carrier, distinctions, and readback are all recorded as awaiting contact with biological reality, and the internal structure contributes no additional biological content.

These rows are finite combinatorial data about codon symbols; by themselves they do not assert that any nucleotide interval is realized in a genome or transcript.

The role of this geometry is limited to forming an eligibility filter over finite symbolic rows.  It is not evidence for translation, folding, physical stability, molecular mechanism, biological function, or a universal genomic rule.

\paragraph{Initial window contact.}
The finite row \texttt{h2.orf.start\_stop.window\_eligibility} records one external nucleotide-window manifest, with \texttt{external\_window\_dataset\_loaded}$=1$, \texttt{window\_count}$=1$, and one classified input window.  Its declared scope is external start/stop enumeration only, with snapshot date \texttt{2026-05-25}.  The row also records \texttt{no\_geometry\_to\_translation\_promotion} as \texttt{orf\_eligibility\_only}.  Thus the contact is enough to say that the one supplied window was classified by start/stop enumeration, but it is not enough to say that the window is translated, folded, physically admissible, functional, mechanistic, or representative of all possible biological intervals.

\paragraph{Curated interval contact.}
The stronger finite contact is \texttt{h2.orf\_eligibility.curated\_interval\_contact}.  It loads a curated interval dataset from \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json} with $168$ rows, using NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals from \textit{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 accession \texttt{NC\_000913.3}.  The required input classes are exactly boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.  The frozen protocol time is \texttt{2026-05-29T19:11:13+00:00}; external evidence was observed at \texttt{2026-05-30T10:29:31+00:00}.  The row states that classifier rules, input classes, baselines, and held-out splits were fixed before observation, and that the analysis used no translation or structure input.

\paragraph{Local exclusion of stronger biological readings.}
The self-check row \texttt{self.claim} reports three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  Consequently, this chapter proves only a finite contact-gated eligibility statement: given the supplied curated interval contact, the frozen classifier separates the positive curated ORF intervals from matched non-ORF decoys in the reported rows and keeps boundary cases from promotion.  It does not establish protein production, ribosomal realization, folded structure, thermodynamic admissibility, regulatory role, cellular function, causal mechanism, or universality across genomes.  Any such stronger reading would require a further contact of the corresponding biological type, measured independently of the start/stop-window enumeration and of the finite codon geometry used here.
