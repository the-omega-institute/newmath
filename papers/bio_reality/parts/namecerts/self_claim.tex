\paragraph{Boundary probes.}
Two probes enforce this separation.  The first says that a start/stop-window seed must remain at boundary status unless a curated interval, sequence record, or transcript-level contact supplies an observed biological interval.  The second rejects any packet that records an ORF eligibility claim while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation can test the proposed interval.  Both probes forbid promotion from start/stop-window geometry to translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function.  The self-check row records this same restriction as: no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers, with three gates checked.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The self-check claim is therefore accepted only as a layer boundary statement.  Before curated interval contact, start/stop enumeration gives a finite test scaffold and no biological realization claim.  After curated interval contact, the finite row supports ORF-eligibility classification on that curated dataset, with $168$ rows, $72$ positive acceptances, $72$ matched decoy rejections, $24$ unpromoted boundary cases, three stable held-out splits, and exact one-sided binomial values $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$ for the positive, decoy, and paired baseline tests.  Even with that contact, the conclusion remains confined to ORF eligibility on the stated curated interval dataset.  Translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, and global biological generality require separate contacts capable of testing those layers.
