\subsection{Open-reading-frame eligibility as a contact-gated biological layer}

\paragraph{Layer and scope.}
This record concerns the layer named $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$.  Its biological object is eligibility of an open-reading-frame interval under explicit start and stop codon enumeration.  The packet is not a claim that a protein is translated, that a folded structure exists, that a molecule is physically admissible, that a phenotype or function is present, that a mechanism has been found, or that a universal biological law has been established.  It is a contact-gated claim: the finite codon geometry may propose a boundary test, but it does not itself count as an observed biological interval.  The self-check row records this restriction directly: the boundary status is ``BioReality self-check only; no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers,'' and the checked gate count is $3$.

\paragraph{Boundary probe before contact.}
The seed row is deliberately conservative.  Its minimal biological content is described as awaiting contact with reality: the carrier, its distinctions, and its readback are not yet promoted to an observed ORF.  The two probes make this boundary operational.  One probe breaks if the pipeline asserts ORF eligibility, translation, folding, physical admissibility, or function from start--stop enumeration alone.  The other breaks if an ORF eligibility claim is recorded while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation can test the interval.  Thus the acceptable pre-contact interpretation is only this: finite start and stop codon geometry specifies what would have to be tested once an external interval annotation is supplied.
