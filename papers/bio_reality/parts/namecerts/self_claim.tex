\paragraph{Finite row presented to the biological layer.}
This packet records a BioReality boundary claim for open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration.  Its carrier is not an observed transcript, not a curated genome interval, and not a translated product.  The carrier is explicitly the contact-gap value ``awaiting reality contact''.  The only distinction presently available is the same contact-gap distinction, and the internal structure field is empty.  Thus the row is a NameCert-level boundary record: it says what kind of external biological contact would be needed before an ORF-eligibility statement could be made, while withholding the biological statement itself.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The self-check row records the boundary sentence: ``BioReality self-check only; no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.''  It also records $3$ checked gates.  Those gates are used here only to keep the boundary closed: start/stop-window enumeration may define a testable question, but it does not answer the biological question.  The two probes attached to this packet therefore have the same operational form.  First, if the pipeline asserts ORF eligibility, translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality from start/stop-window enumeration alone, the packet breaks.  Second, if the packet records an ORF-eligibility claim while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated interval annotation can test the proposed interval, the packet also breaks.

Consequently the admissible reading is narrow.  The present row is a boundary probe for the layer $orf\_eligibility$.  It may say that a curated ORF interval annotation, sequence contact, or transcript contact would be a relevant next reality contact.  It may not say that such an interval has been observed.  It may not say that translation occurs.  It may not say that a peptide folds, that a physical admissibility condition is satisfied, that a biological function exists, that a mechanism has been identified, or that the finite geometry is universal across biological contexts.  Those statements require separate reality contacts, and this packet records none.
