\subsection{Self-claim boundary for start--stop ORF eligibility}
\label{subsec:bioreality-self-claim-orf-eligibility}

\paragraph{Finite record.}
This record is a self-check for the BioReality layer called \emph{orf eligibility}.  Its biological object is the eligibility of an open-reading-frame interval as determined by explicit start/stop codon enumeration.  The row does not assert that the codon geometry is itself an observed biological interval.  The self-check reports three checked gates and records the boundary sentence: no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  Thus the admissible reading is narrow: start/stop enumeration may define a finite test condition for interval eligibility, while the biological interval itself must arrive through a separate curated sequence, transcript, or annotation contact.

\paragraph{Window contact and classifier scope.}
The start/stop-window enumeration check loaded one external nucleotide-window record from a snapshot dated $2026$-$05$-$25$, and all one of the input windows was classified by the declared start/stop enumeration rule.  Its scope is explicitly restricted to external window start/stop enumeration, with the recorded classification layer marked as \texttt{orf\_eligibility\_only}.  This is the first local guardrail: the window classifier may say that a supplied nucleotide window satisfies the start/stop eligibility rule, but it may not convert that result into translation, molecular structure, physical viability, mechanism, function, or a general biological law.

The analysis used no translation or structure input, and the baselines, classifier rules, held-out splits, and input classes were fixed before the observation.  The required input classes were \texttt{boundary\_case}, \texttt{matched\_non\_orf\_decoy}, and \texttt{positive\_curated\_orf}.  The classifier identifier was \texttt{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}, and its rules were not modified during the check.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The row is therefore ready as a bounded BioReality record for ORF-eligibility auditing.  It combines a finite codon geometry, an explicitly scoped start/stop-window classifier, and a curated interval contact with $168$ rows and matched positive/decoy controls.  The admissible conclusion is only that, under this frozen protocol and this curated interval contact, the start/stop eligibility classifier separates the recorded positive intervals from the matched decoys and leaves boundary cases unpromoted.  No translation event, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universal rule follows from this row.  Any such higher-layer claim would require its own contact data and its own audit, separate from the start/stop eligibility evidence recorded here.
