\paragraph{Object and local status.}
This record concerns open-reading-frame eligibility as obtained only from enumeration of start and stop codon windows. Its biological object is not an observed translated product, not a folded molecule, and not a functional genomic annotation. The packet is a boundary-level record for the layer $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$: it says what a finite start--stop-window test would have to respect, while withholding the biological assertion until a separate curated sequence, transcript, or interval annotation is attached. The local self-check records $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. Thus the present section treats the finite codon data as admissible combinatorial input, but not as evidence that an open reading frame has been realized in a biological system.

\paragraph{Boundary test.}
The support condition for the first probe is that the packet remains only a boundary test until a sequence, transcript, or curated-annotation contact supplies the observed interval. Its failure condition would be an assertion of ORF eligibility, translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, or biological function from start--stop enumeration alone. The second probe checks the same gap from the annotation side: it fails if the packet records an ORF eligibility claim while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated interval annotation can test the proposed interval. Since the contact list is empty, both probes keep the record below the claim threshold.

\paragraph{Local non-promotion rule.}
The only ready conclusion is a finite bookkeeping conclusion: the row coherently records the codon sets $R$ and $M$, the $18$-element $f3$ saturation of $R$, the $20$-element medium set, the $10$-row quotient image, and the one-window start--stop enumeration under an eligibility-only label. None of these data imply translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Those stronger biological layers would require a separate contact able to test the relevant biological interval or molecule. In the absence of such a contact, the record is an admissible boundary packet for what must be checked later, not an observed ORF claim.
