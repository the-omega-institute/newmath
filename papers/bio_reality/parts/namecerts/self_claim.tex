\paragraph{Finite object under audit.}

The claim is deliberately narrow: a finite nucleotide window may be sorted by a fixed start--stop rule, but that sorting is not itself an observation of translation, a folded molecular state, a physical admissibility certificate, a biological role, a mechanism, or a universal law.  The self-check recorded for this packet reports experiment run \texttt{0f47d2f0cf41}; its boundary statement is that no codon geometry is allowed to move across biological layers, and the checked gate count is $3$.  Thus the packet is a gatekeeping record for the layer named \texttt{orf\_eligibility}, not a biological realization claim.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim rule.}
The self-check therefore has a precise conclusion.  The finite codon rows, the quotient profile, the one-window enumeration manifest, and the $168$-row curated interval contact jointly support a bounded ORF-eligibility classification under the stated external annotation source.  They do not establish translation realization, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or any universal biological law.  Any such stronger layer would require its own independent contact: for example, transcript evidence for expression, translation evidence for peptide production, structural evidence for folding, physical evidence for admissibility, functional evidence for biological role, or broader sampling evidence for universality.  Without that extra contact, this record remains a finite eligibility audit with an explicit non-promotion boundary.
