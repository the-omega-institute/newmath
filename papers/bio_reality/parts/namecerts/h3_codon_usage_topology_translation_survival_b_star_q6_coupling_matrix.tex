\subsection{B-Star-Q6 Coupling Against a Translation Proxy}
\label{sec:b-star-q6-coupling-translation-proxy}

\paragraph{Object recorded.}
This record concerns the nine residual coordinates obtained after imposing the amino-acid quotient on codon-count space.  The ambient codon-count space has $61$ coding codon coordinates, and the residual family used here is
$K\_AAA$, $Arg\_AGR$, $Ile\_AUA$, $Leu\_CUN\_vs\_UUR$, $Leu\_UUA\_vs\_UUG$, $Ser\_UCR\_vs\_AGY$, $Ser\_UCA\_vs\_UCG$, $Thr\_ACR\_vs\_ACY$, and $f3\_stress$.  These coordinates are tested only as synonymous residual directions: their role is to ask whether codon usage still has structured variation after amino-acid composition has been quotiented away.  The finite contact available for this chapter is not a ribosome-profiling or proteomics contact.  It is a proxy spectrum, namely the tRNA-Leu CUN-versus-UUR log-copy spectrum, evaluated across three loaded organism rows: Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K-12.

\paragraph{Finite computation.}
For each residual coordinate $q_i$, the computation forms a covariance coupling against the proxy readout direction.  In the intended survival test the matrix entry would be refined to a controlled score $S_{ij}$ after residualizing a translation readout $r_j$ against amino-acid composition, organism context, GC3, length, and the $M$-density baseline.  The finite run recorded here stops earlier: it reports the coupling matrix entries $A_{ij}$ against the tRNA-Leu copy-number proxy and records whether the proxy-null condition is met.  The break condition for this run was that all nine residual coordinates have $|A_{ij}| < 0.01$ against the proxy readout.  That condition was not met.  The threshold was $0.01$, the maximum absolute coupling was $1882464.1106377328$, and all $9$ residual entries were nonzero above the trivial zero-count level.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{q_i\} &= 9,\\
\#\{\text{organism rows}\} &= 3,\\
\#\{\text{coding codon coordinates}\} &= 61,\\
\tau_{A} &= 0.01,\\
\max_i |A_i| &= 1882464.1106377328,\\
\#\{i : A_i \neq 0\} &= 9.
\end{aligned}
$$

The numerical result is therefore a positive proxy-contact result for nontrivial residual coupling.  It says that the nine-coordinate residual basis is not invisible to the particular tRNA-Leu CUN-versus-UUR copy-number spectrum in this three-organism finite table.  It does not yet say that any residual coordinate survives against a direct translation readout after the full control set is applied.

\paragraph{Interpretation at the translation layer.}
The biological conjecture targeted by this record is stronger than the finite proxy result.  The intended matrix is a survival matrix $S_{ij}=S_{q_i}(r_j\mid Z)$, where $r_j$ ranges over translation-related readouts such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, translation efficiency, or protein abundance, and where $Z$ contains amino-acid sequence, organism context, GC3, length, and a simpler-codon baseline.  The present finite computation supplies only a coupling precursor $A_i$ against a tRNA-gene-copy proxy.  It supports the decision to keep the nine residual coordinates under consideration for translation-readout testing, because the proxy-null condition failed with $9$ nonzero entries and a maximum absolute coupling far above $0.01$.  It does not itself estimate partial $R^2$, description-length improvement, or a controlled survival score $S_{ij}$.

\paragraph{Controls required for the survival matrix.}
A completed survival entry must be computed after the amino-acid quotient and after the additional controls.  In particular, a future row must not treat tRNA gene-copy ratio as tRNA abundance, tRNA charging, ribosome occupancy, translation efficiency, or protein output.  The tRNA-copy spectrum can motivate a translation-layer test, but it cannot replace direct contacts such as organism-scoped tAI or stAI weights, ribosome-profiling translation efficiency, matched mRNA abundance, or matched proteomics abundance.  A positive coupling $A_i$ is also not allowed to stand in for positive survival $S_{ij}$.  The survival claim requires controlled partial-$R^2$ or description-length-improvement estimation after the baseline ablation has been applied.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The finite result is confined to codon-usage topology and a tRNA-copy proxy.  No translation realization follows from this chapter unless a separate translation contact supplies a direct readout.  No folding conclusion, physical-admissibility conclusion, functional conclusion, mechanism claim, or universal biological law follows from the three-organism table.  The signs of the entries are not population-level directionality estimates.  The chapter records a finite non-null coupling against one proxy spectrum and leaves the stronger translation-survival matrix open until direct readouts and the full control set are attached.
