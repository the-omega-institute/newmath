\subsection{B-Star-Q6 Codon-Usage Coupling Against a Translation-Proxy Spectrum}\label{sec:b-star-q6-codon-usage-coupling-translation-proxy}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim scope.}
This record concerns a codon-usage topology claim for the nine residual coordinates of $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ after the amino-acid quotient has been imposed. The intended survival matrix has rows indexed by the residual coordinates
$K_{AAA}$, $Arg_{AGR}$, $Ile_{AUA}$, $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$, $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$, $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$, $Thr_{ACR/ACY}$, and the third-position stress coordinate. Its columns are intended to range over translation-associated readouts such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, translation efficiency, and matched protein abundance. The present finite computation does not yet contain those full readout columns. It records a proxy contact: the coupling of the nine synonymous residual coordinates against the tRNA-Leu $CUN$-versus-$UUR$ log-copy spectrum across three organism rows.

\paragraph{Finite row contact.}
The verified run identifier is $4a783972e74b$, with verification time $2026$-$05$-$31T23:35:57+00:00$. The codon count carrier has dimension $61$, and the organism rows loaded in the computation are Homo sapiens, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, and Escherichia coli K12. The computed residual-coordinate list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = (&Arg_{AGR}, Ile_{AUA}, K_{AAA}, Leu_{CUN/UUR}, Leu_{UUA/UUG},\
&Ser_{UCA/UCG}, Ser_{UCR/AGY}, Thr_{ACR/ACY}, f3\text{-}stress).
\end{aligned}
$$
For each row coordinate $q_i$, the computation applies the synonymous projection associated with the amino-acid quotient and then evaluates a residual covariance coupling $A_{ij}$ against the available proxy spectrum. The declared break threshold is $0.01$: the proxy test would be null if all nine residual coordinates had $|A_{ij}| < 0.01$ against the proxy readout.

\paragraph{Observed proxy coupling.}
The break condition was not met. All nine entries were nonzero relative to the thresholded proxy test, and the maximum absolute coupling was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\max_{i,j} |A_{ij}| = 1882464.1106377328.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite contact shows that the nine-coordinate $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ residual family is not numerically erased by the amino-acid quotient in this three-organism codon-count and tRNA-copy proxy setting. The computation is therefore evidence for a codon-usage-topology interaction with the proxy tRNA-Leu spectrum, not evidence for a completed translation-survival matrix.

\paragraph{Survival matrix still open.}
The survival target is stronger than proxy coupling. For a translation readout $r_j$, the intended entry is a controlled survival score $S_{ij}=S_{q_i}(r_j\mid Z)$, where the control family $Z$ includes amino-acid composition, organism context, GC3, coding-sequence length, and an $M$-density baseline. In the intended test, a positive entry requires that the residual coordinate improve the held-out or description-length score for the readout after these controls and after the simpler-codon baseline has been ablated. The present run reports $A_{ij}$ coupling values only for the tRNA-Leu copy-number proxy. It does not report partial $R^2$, controlled survival scores, p-values, lambda penalties, module classifications, hit rates, or full row counts for ribosome profiling, stAI, tAI, protein abundance, or matched mRNA controls.

\paragraph{Local interpretation boundary.}
This chapter does not infer ribosome-mediated translation efficiency from tRNA gene-copy counts alone. It also does not infer folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical function, pathway mechanism, or a universal biological law from the three-organism finite table. The sign of any proxy coupling entry is not treated as a universal direction. The only positive statement supported here is narrower: in a $61$-codon count space, across the three loaded organisms, the nine named $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ synonymous residual coordinates have nonzero proxy couplings against the tRNA-Leu $CUN/UUR$ log-copy spectrum, with at least one absolute coupling far above the $0.01$ break threshold.

\paragraph{Falsification route.}
The stronger translation-realization claim remains falsifiable by completing the readout matrix. Once organism-matched tAI or stAI weights, ribosome profiling translation-efficiency measurements, matched mRNA abundance, and matched proteomics are attached, the survival matrix must be recomputed under the full control family $Z$. The claim fails at the translation-survival level if every controlled entry satisfies $S_{ij}\leq 0$, or if the apparent proxy relation collapses into the null range after expansion to a larger organism panel and proper partial-correlation testing. In particular, the planned Leu $CUN/UUR$ extension requires at least eight organisms and a nontrivial null model before a stable cross-organism relation can be asserted.
