\subsection{B-Star-Q6 Coupling Matrix and Translation-Survival Boundary}\label{sec:biorality-b-star-q6-coupling-matrix}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Coordinate record.} The record considered here is the nine-row $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ residual coordinate packet tested against translation-adjacent readouts after the amino-acid quotient is made explicit. The codon-count ambient space has $61$ sense-codon coordinates. The computed residual rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
&\mathrm{Arg}_{AGR},\ \mathrm{Ile}_{AUA},\ K_{AAA},\ \mathrm{Leu}_{CUN/UUR},\ \mathrm{Leu}_{UUA/UUG},\\
&\mathrm{Ser}_{UCA/UCG},\ \mathrm{Ser}_{UCR/AGY},\ \mathrm{Thr}_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each row is read only as a synonymous residual direction: it is a contrast inside the codon-count table after amino-acid composition has been separated from codon choice. The intended survival matrix would have entries $S_{ij}=S_{q_i}(r_j\mid Z)$ for residual coordinate $q_i$, translation readout $r_j$, and control set $Z$ containing amino-acid composition, organism context, GC3, coding-sequence length, and the simpler $M$-density baseline. The available finite computation does not yet supply that full matrix. It supplies a proxy coupling matrix $A_{ij}$ for the nine residual rows against a tRNA-Leu $CUN$-versus-$UUR$ log-copy spectrum in three organisms.

\paragraph{Finite rows.} The organisms loaded in the computation are $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli\ K12}$. For the $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ proxy coupling run, the null rule was: declare the row null if all nine coordinates satisfy $|A_{ij}|<0.01$ against the proxy readout. That null rule was not met. The reported coupling threshold is $0.01$, the number of nonzero entries is $9$, and the largest absolute coupling is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\max_{i,j}|A_{ij}| = 1882464.1106377328.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite table says that the nine synonymous residual coordinates are not all orthogonal, under this proxy covariance statistic, to the tRNA-Leu copy-ratio spectrum. This is a codon-usage and tRNA-copy contact only. It is not a controlled translation-survival entry, because the recorded readout is not ribosome occupancy, translation efficiency, stAI, tAI, or protein abundance under the full control set $Z$.

\paragraph{Leu gate row.} The associated Leu $CUN/UUR$ finite row compares codon usage with tRNA-Leu copy ratio in the same three organisms. The recorded log-ratio signs are
$$
\begin{aligned}
(1, -1, 1)
\end{aligned}
$$
with log-ratio values
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.3514980212896148,\quad -0.24869078488829677,\quad 1.0086640520051702.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sign is therefore not uniform across organisms. In that three-row table the Pearson statistic is $1.0$, and the partial Pearson statistic after the stated control is also $1.0$ with $n=3$. This exact value is a finite-row fact, not a population estimate. With only three organisms it must be read as a small-table compatibility check, not as stable directionality or as an organism-general law.

\paragraph{Survival criterion.} The translation-survival claim would require a stricter object than the proxy coupling already computed. For each residual coordinate $q_i$ and each translation readout $r_j$, one must form the projected readout weight $\ell_j^{\perp}$, compute the residual covariance coupling
$$
\begin{aligned}
A_{ij}=q_i^{T}\Sigma\ell_j^{\perp},
\end{aligned}
$$
and then estimate the controlled survival score $S_{ij}$ as a partial-$R^2$ contribution or description-length improvement after the full control set $Z$ and the simpler-codon baseline have already been applied. A positive $A_{ij}$ is not enough. The entry becomes a survival entry only when the controlled statistic gives $S_{ij}>0$ for the corresponding translation readout. The present evidence therefore supports a bounded statement: the nine $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ residual rows show nonzero proxy coupling against a tRNA-copy spectrum, but the recorded data do not yet establish a positive $S_{ij}$ entry for ribosome profiling, tAI, stAI, translation efficiency, or protein abundance.

\paragraph{Local boundary.} The table has no authority beyond its measured contacts. tRNA gene-copy counts are genome-annotation summaries, not tRNA abundance, charging state, ribosome traffic, translation efficiency, or protein output. Codon-usage tables record sequence composition and sampling conventions, not folded structure, biochemical role, physical admissibility, or a universal biological rule. Matched mRNA abundance, when present, is a control for separating transcript level from translation efficiency; it is not by itself a translation readout. Ribosome profiling, stAI, tAI, and proteomics contacts would each have to be attached and analyzed under the same $Z$-controlled survival statistic before any translation-level survival assertion is made. Consequently no translation realization, folding claim, physical-admissibility claim, functional claim, mechanism claim, or universality claim follows from this finite row unless a separate matched contact supplies the missing measurement and the corresponding controlled statistic survives.

\paragraph{Chapter verdict.} The finite computation is positive at the proxy-coupling layer and blocked at the survival layer. The non-null entry count $9$, threshold $0.01$, and maximum absolute coupling $1882464.1106377328$ show that the tested $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ residual rows are not all erased by the available codon-usage and tRNA-copy proxy. The three-organism Leu row gives Pearson $1.0$ and partial Pearson $1.0$ with $n=3$, while also recording non-uniform signs. These facts justify retaining the packet as an open translation-survival target, not upgrading it to a completed survival matrix. The next required finite object is an organism-matched table containing codon counts, direct translation or protein readouts, mRNA controls where needed, and explicit estimates of $S_{ij}$ under amino-acid composition, organism, GC3, length, and $M$-density controls.
