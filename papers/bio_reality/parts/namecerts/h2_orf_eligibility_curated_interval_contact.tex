\subsection{Curated interval contact for ORF-window eligibility}\label{subsec:bioreality-orf-eligibility-curated-interval-contact}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question fixed by the finite rows.}
This record concerns only the layer named \emph{open-reading-frame eligibility}: whether a finite nucleotide interval passes a predeclared start/stop-window enumeration test after a separate curated interval annotation is attached. The biological object is not a protein, not a translated product, and not a folded molecule. The admissible question is narrower: given a frozen classifier whose rules use only start and stop codon window geometry, does an externally curated CDS interval contact separate positive curated intervals from matched non-ORF decoys and from boundary cases in the finite table?

The seed state before contact is deliberately empty as a biological assertion. Its carrier, distinctions, and readout are all recorded as awaiting contact, with no internal biological structure. The self-check row reports three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. Thus the claim begins as a guarded test site, not as a biological conclusion.

\paragraph{External interval table and frozen protocol.}
The contact row attaches a curated finite table at \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json}. It has $168$ rows from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession \texttt{NC\_000913.3}, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$. The required input classes are all present: \texttt{boundary\_case}, \texttt{matched\_non\_orf\_decoy}, and \texttt{positive\_curated\_orf}. The protocol was fixed at $2026$-$05$-$29$T$19{:}11{:}13$Z, before the external evidence was observed at $2026$-$05$-$30$T$10{:}29{:}31$Z. The row records that classifier rules, input classes, held-out splits, and baselines were frozen, and that the analysis used no translation or structure input.

The earlier start/stop-window enumeration row had only one external window in scope and classified that one window by start/stop enumeration, with scope restricted to external-window start/stop enumeration. That row also states the same layer restriction: the output remains ORF-eligibility-only. The curated interval contact is therefore not a new biological theory; it is the finite test that supplies the missing interval observation for this layer.

\paragraph{Observed finite separation.}
The curated contact has $72$ positive curated ORF intervals and $72$ matched non-ORF decoys. The positive intervals are accepted at rate $1.0$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
 n_{+}&=72, & a_{+}&=72, & \widehat p_{+}&=1.0, & p_{\mathrm{tail}}&=2.117582368135751\cdot 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The matched decoys are rejected at rate $1.0$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
 n_{-}&=72, & r_{-}&=72, & \widehat p_{-}&=1.0, & p_{\mathrm{tail}}&=2.117582368135751\cdot 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The predeclared pairwise baseline comparison also has $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, hence win rate $1.0$ and the same one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\cdot 10^{-22}$. Boundary controls remain unpromoted: among $24$ boundary cases, $0$ are promoted, so the promoted fraction is $0.0$.

\paragraph{Held-out split stability.}
The finite table is not supported only by a pooled count. It is also checked across three held-out splits. In \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_left\_locus\_fold}, the positive count is $24$ with positive accept rate $1.0$, and the decoy count is $24$ with decoy reject rate $1.0$. The same numbers hold for \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_middle\_locus\_fold} and for \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_right\_locus\_fold}. All three split rows are marked as passed:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{split count}&=3,\\
\text{positive accept rates}&=(1.0,1.0,1.0),\\
\text{decoy reject rates}&=(1.0,1.0,1.0),\\
\text{positive counts}&=(24,24,24),\\

$$
This supports the local claim that the frozen start/stop-window classifier separates the curated positives from their matched decoys within this finite RefSeq-derived interval table.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The ready conclusion is finite and layer-bound: after the curated interval contact is attached, the start/stop-window ORF-eligibility classifier accepts all $72$ positive curated intervals, rejects all $72$ matched non-ORF decoys, leaves all $24$ boundary cases unpromoted, and remains stable across three held-out locus folds. The support is the finite row table plus the external curated annotation contact; the codon geometry alone remains insufficient.

Nothing in this record asserts translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The contact does not establish a global biological law, does not classify windows outside the dataset, and does not turn NCBI RefSeq annotation into kernel-internal content. Any claim about translation realization, folded structure, physical chemistry, cellular function, mechanism, or species-wide law would require a separate contact designed for that layer.
