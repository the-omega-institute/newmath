\paragraph{Local claim.}
This record concerns the layer named \emph{orf eligibility}: whether a finite nucleotide interval is admitted by a start--stop enumeration rule after an external curated interval has been supplied. The biological object is not translation, not folded protein structure, not cellular activity, and not organism-level function. It is only the finite compatibility question obtained when a curated coding-sequence interval is placed in contact with a frozen start--stop-window classifier. The relevant dataset is the curated interval file \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json}, with $168$ rows, snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$, and source text identifying NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals from \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession NC\_000913.3. The analysis scope is explicitly $\mathrm{external\_orf\_interval\_contact\_only}$.

The frozen time was $2026$-$05$-$29\mathrm{T}19{:}11{:}13{+}00{:}00$, and the external evidence was observed at $2026$-$05$-$30\mathrm{T}10{:}29{:}31{+}00{:}00$. Thus the contact is a bounded annotation test: curated intervals are compared with matched non-ORF decoys and boundary cases under a fixed geometry-only classifier.

\paragraph{Relation to the earlier start--stop window row.}
A separate start--stop-window eligibility row had only $1$ external window, with $1$ classified input window, snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$25$, and scope $\mathrm{external\_window\_start\_stop\_enumeration\_only}$. \paragraph{What is established.}
Within this finite contact, the classifier admits all $72$ curated positive intervals, rejects all $72$ matched non-ORF decoys, refuses promotion on all $24$ boundary cases, and preserves this behavior across the three recorded held-out splits. The self-check row records a boundary statement that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers, with $3$ checked gates. The appropriate local conclusion is therefore: the finite external annotation contact supports ORF-eligibility classification for this dataset under the frozen start--stop enumeration protocol.

\paragraph{What is not established.}
No conclusion about translation follows from these rows. No conclusion about folded structure, physical admissibility, molecular mechanism, cellular function, organism-level effect, or universal genomic law follows from these rows. The NCBI RefSeq annotation is an external curated biological contact used by the experiment; it is not part of the internal combinatorial kernel. The matched-decoy and boundary controls do not classify windows outside the supplied dataset, and the $168$-row contact does not convert start--stop geometry into protein production. Any claim at a higher biological layer would require a separate contact designed for that layer.
