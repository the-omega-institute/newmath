\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The chapter therefore records a narrow contact result: within this curated RefSeq interval dataset, a previously fixed start--stop classifier separates the positive curated ORF intervals from matched non-ORF decoys, keeps boundary cases unpromoted, and remains stable across the three frozen held-out splits. The result is a finite interval-annotation contact, not a derivation of biological realization from codon-coordinate data.

The local exclusion boundary is part of the claim. Nothing here establishes translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, organism-level role, or a universal law over unseen nucleotide windows. The matched decoys and boundary controls do not classify windows outside the dataset. The NCBI RefSeq annotation remains a curated biological input to the experiment, not kernel content of the formal coordinate system. A stronger biological assertion would require a separate contact supplying the corresponding evidence, such as translation evidence for translation, structural evidence for folding, physical measurements for admissibility, functional assays for function, or a broader sampling design for universality.
