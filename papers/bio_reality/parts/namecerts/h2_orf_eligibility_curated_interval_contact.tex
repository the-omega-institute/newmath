\subsection{Curated interval contact for ORF eligibility}\label{sec:h2-orf-eligibility-curated-interval-contact}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite question.}
This section records a finite contact test for the biological layer called ORF eligibility.  The object under examination is not translation, not a folded molecule, and not a cellular function.  It is the narrower question whether an externally supplied interval annotation can be separated, by a frozen start--stop window classifier, from matched non-ORF decoys while leaving boundary cases unpromoted.  The layer is therefore an interval-contact layer: a start codon and a compatible stop-window can make a window eligible for this test only after a curated biological interval has been supplied as the contact.

The pre-contact codon geometry used by the packet is finite.  The motif-compressed set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with no missing and no extra entries in the reported motif union.  The larger enumerated set has cardinality $20$ and is
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&AAA,AAG,ACA,ACG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,\\
& CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The saturation check reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=\{&AAA,AAG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\},

$$
so that the $f_3$ boundary contribution is empty and $M=\operatorname{Med}(R)\cup\{ACA,ACG\}$ in the recorded finite computation.  The quotient geometry check gives the vector $(20,38,26,8,1)$ for $M$, quotient size $10$, and quotient vector $(10,14,6,1)$.  These finite coordinates are only the enumeration substrate; they are not themselves observations of an ORF.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The curated interval contact uses NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals from \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession NC\_000913.3.  The snapshot date is $2026$-$05$-$30$, and the loaded finite dataset has $168$ rows at \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json}.  The required input classes are all present: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.  The contact is explicitly external curated biological annotation; it is not part of the internal codon-geometry kernel.

The protocol was frozen before the external observation.  The protocol timestamp is $2026$-$05$-$29\,19{:}11{:}13+00{:}00$, while the external evidence was observed at $2026$-$05$-$30\,10{:}29{:}31+00{:}00$.  The classifier rules, input classes, baselines, and held-out splits were all recorded as frozen.  The classifier identifier is \texttt{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}, and the classifier-rules-modified flag is false.  The analysis also records that no translation or structure input was used.  Thus the finite result is a contact test for interval eligibility under a predeclared classifier, not a post hoc biological explanation.

\paragraph{Layer boundary.}
The self-check records three checked gates and states that codon geometry is not promoted across biological layers.  This is essential to the meaning of the result.  The curated interval contact supports only the finite claim that this frozen start--stop classifier separates the supplied RefSeq CDS intervals from the matched decoys in the stated $168$-row dataset, while refusing to promote the $24$ boundary cases.  It does not claim that a sequence is translated, that any protein folds, that any molecule is physically admissible, that any biological function occurs, that a mechanism has been identified, or that a universal law of genomes has been established.  Any one of those stronger assertions would require its own independent contact appropriate to that layer.

The earlier one-window start--stop enumeration check is consistent with this boundary: it loaded one external nucleotide-window record, classified all one of one input windows by start--stop enumeration, and marked the scope as external-window start--stop enumeration only.  That record explicitly reports no promotion from geometry to translation.  The present interval contact is stronger because it supplies a curated interval dataset, matched decoys, boundary cases, held-out splits, and exact finite statistics; it remains weaker than translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.
