\subsection{Curated interval contact for ORF eligibility}
\label{subsec:bioreality-orf-eligibility-curated-interval-contact}

\paragraph{Object and finite contact.}
This record concerns open-reading-frame eligibility as tested by start--stop interval enumeration against a curated biological annotation table.  The biological object is not protein production, folded molecular form, cellular role, or a universal genomic principle; it is the narrower question of whether a frozen start--stop-window classifier accepts curated coding intervals and rejects matched non-ORF intervals in the finite table supplied to the experiment.  The contact table has $168$ rows and is reported as `curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json'.  Its source is the NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS interval annotation for the Escherichia coli K-12 MG1655 complete genome, accession NC\_000913.3, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$.  The row classes present in the table are boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.  The relevant scope is therefore only the finite interval-contact task:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{row count} &= 168,\\
\text{positive curated ORF rows used in tests} &= 72,\\
\text{matched non-ORF decoy rows used in tests} &= 72,\\
\text{boundary-case rows} &= 24.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classifier is identified as `orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility'.  Its rules were fixed before the outside annotation was inspected, and the audit records that no translation or structure input entered the analysis.

\paragraph{Frozen protocol and observed interval behavior.}
The protocol was frozen at $2026$-$05$-$29$T$19$:$11$:$13$+$00$:$00$, while the outside annotation was first observed at $2026$-$05$-$30$T$10$:$29$:$31$+$00$:$00$.  The input classes, classifier rules, held-out splits, and baselines were all fixed before that observation.  On the positive class, all $72$ curated intervals were accepted, giving an acceptance rate of $1.0$.  Under the exact one-sided binomial calculation recorded for this finite table, the reported upper-tail probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
P_{\mathrm{positive}} &= 2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22},\\
72/72 &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
On the matched decoy class, all $72$ decoys were rejected, again with the same reported one-sided binomial probability:
$$
\begin{aligned}
P_{\mathrm{decoy}} &= 2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22},\\
72/72 &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The predeclared paired comparison also records $72$ wins in $72$ positive--decoy pairs, hence win rate $1.0$ and the same probability value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  Boundary rows were not turned into accepted ORF claims: $0$ of $24$ boundary cases were promoted, so the promoted fraction was $0.0$.

\paragraph{Held-out stability.}
The finite table was also partitioned into three held-out folds.  In the left-locus fold, $24$ positive intervals were accepted at rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys were rejected at rate $1.0$.  The middle-locus fold has the same counts and rates, and the right-locus fold also has $24$ positive examples and $24$ decoys with rates $1.0$ and $1.0$, respectively.  Thus the finite contact is not a single aggregate-only success; each recorded split passes the same positive-versus-decoy separation:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{left fold} &: (24\ \text{positive at }1.0,\ 24\ \text{decoy at }1.0),\\
\text{middle fold} &: (24\ \text{positive at }1.0,\ 24\ \text{decoy at }1.0),\\
\text{right fold} &: (24\ \text{positive at }1.0,\ 24\ \text{decoy at }1.0).
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports only a table-relative interval-eligibility statement: within the frozen finite contact, the start--stop-window rule separates the curated interval rows from the matched decoys and leaves the boundary rows unpromoted.

\paragraph{Local boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion is deliberately narrow.  The finite contact supports the statement that, on this $168$-row curated interval table, a fixed geometry-only start--stop classifier accepts all $72$ positive curated ORF intervals, rejects all $72$ matched non-ORF decoys, and promotes none of the $24$ boundary cases.  It does not assert translation, ribosome occupancy, transcript evidence, protein folding, thermodynamic admissibility, molecular mechanism, organismal function, or a law applying beyond the sampled table.  None of those stronger biological layers follows from codon-window geometry or from this interval contact unless another independent contact supplies the relevant evidence.  The NCBI RefSeq annotation remains curated biological input to the experiment rather than content generated by the mathematical kernel.  The self-check row agrees with this limitation: three gates were checked, and no codon geometry was promoted across biological layers.
