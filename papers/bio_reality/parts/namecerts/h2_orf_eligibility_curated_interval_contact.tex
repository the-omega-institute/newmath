\subsection{Curated interval contact for ORF eligibility}
\label{sec:h2-orf-eligibility-curated-interval-contact}

\paragraph{Claim and finite scope.}
This record concerns the layer named \emph{open-reading-frame eligibility}: whether a nucleotide interval is accepted by a start/stop codon enumeration rule after a curated biological interval contact has been supplied. The coordinate-level rule is intentionally narrow. It can classify an interval as eligible or ineligible relative to the supplied nucleotide window and the declared start/stop test, but it does not by itself assert translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, molecular mechanism, biological function, or a universal law over genomes. Those stronger biological readings require separate contacts of the appropriate kind.

\paragraph{Reality contact used by this record.}
The separate interval contact is a curated manifest of $168$ rows at path \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json}. Its source is the NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS interval annotation for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession NC\_000913.3, with snapshot date 2026-05-30. The required row classes are all present: \texttt{boundary\_case}, \texttt{matched\_non\_orf\_decoy}, and \texttt{positive\_curated\_orf}. The classifier is \texttt{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}; its rule set was not modified after the protocol was fixed, and the analysis used no translation or structure input. The observation time for the curated interval evidence was 2026-05-30T10:29:31+00:00, after the protocol time 2026-05-29T19:11:13+00:00.

$$
The predeclared paired comparison has $72$ wins in $72$ positive-decoy pairs, hence win rate $1.0$, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{pair}}=2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support the local statement that the curated positive intervals and their matched decoys are separated by the finite start/stop-window classifier under the recorded manifest.

\paragraph{Held-out split stability.}
The contact row includes three held-out splits. The left-locus split \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_left\_locus\_fold} contains $24$ positives and $24$ decoys; it passes with positive accept rate $1.0$ and decoy reject rate $1.0$. The middle-locus split \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_middle\_locus\_fold} has the same $24$ positive and $24$ decoy counts and the same two rates. The right-locus split \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_right\_locus\_fold} also has $24$ positives, $24$ decoys, positive accept rate $1.0$, and decoy reject rate $1.0$. Thus the finite evidence is not concentrated in a single listed fold of the manifest.

\paragraph{Boundary controls.}
The boundary-control row contains $24$ boundary cases, of which $0$ are promoted; the promotion fraction is therefore $0.0$. This matters because the classifier is not being used as a license to convert any start/stop-shaped window into an observed ORF. The earlier start/stop-window enumeration row had only one external window and classified that one input window, with scope \texttt{external\_window\_start\_stop\_enumeration\_only}. The present curated interval contact enlarges the tested finite manifest to $168$ rows, but it still remains a contact-specific interval eligibility result rather than a claim about translation or downstream biological realization.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
For this claim, the finite row data justify the following bounded conclusion. Given the supplied RefSeq CDS interval contact for NC\_000913.3, the fixed start/stop-window eligibility classifier accepts all $72$ positive curated intervals, rejects all $72$ matched non-ORF decoys, leaves all $24$ boundary cases unpromoted, and preserves this separation across three held-out locus splits. The claim is therefore ready as an ORF-eligibility contact result for this manifest. It does not assert that any accepted interval is translated, folded, physically admissible, mechanistically explained, functional, or representative of all biological settings; each such assertion would require its own contact and its own finite audit.
