\subsection{Curated interval contact for ORF eligibility}
\label{subsec:h2-orf-eligibility-curated-interval-contact}

\paragraph{Claim locus.}
The object tested here is open-reading-frame eligibility as a finite start--stop interval question. The packet is not a claim that a sequence is translated, that a peptide folds, that a molecule is physically admissible, that a biological function is present, that a mechanism has been identified, or that a universal biological law has been found. It is only a bounded contact test for whether a predeclared start--stop-window classifier agrees with curated coding-interval annotation on the finite rows supplied to the experiment. The contact layer is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{scope} &= \mathrm{external\_orf\_interval\_contact\_only},\\
\mathrm{snapshot\ date} &= 2026\text{-}05\text{-}30,\\
\mathrm{row\ count} &= 168.
\end{aligned}
$$
The biological input is the NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS interval annotation for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession NC\_000913.3. The finite dataset path recorded by the run is \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json}, with $168$ rows.

\paragraph{Pre-specified contact protocol.}
The interval contact was evaluated after the rules, baselines, input classes, and held-out splits had already been specified. The protocol record states that classifier rules were set at $2026$-$05$-$29$T$19{:}11{:}13$+00:00, while the curated evidence was observed at $2026$-$05$-$30$T$10{:}29{:}31$+00:00. The classifier identifier was \texttt{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}; its rule table was not modified during the contact run. The analysis used no translation or protein-structure input. The required row classes were exactly boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.

\paragraph{Observed finite-row separation.}
The contact run contains $72$ positive curated ORF intervals, $72$ matched non-ORF decoys, and $24$ boundary cases, totaling $168$ rows. On the positive rows, the start--stop interval classifier accepted all $72$ rows, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{positive\ accept\ rate} &= 72/72 = 1,\\
P_{\mathrm{upper}} &= 2.117582368135751\cdot 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
On the matched decoys, it rejected all $72$ rows, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{decoy\ reject\ rate} &= 72/72 = 1,\\
P_{\mathrm{upper}} &= 2.117582368135751\cdot 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The paired baseline comparison likewise recorded $72$ wins out of $72$ pairs, with one-sided exact binomial tail
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{win\ rate} &= 72/72 = 1,\\
P_{\mathrm{upper}} &= 2.117582368135751\cdot 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The boundary cases were not promoted: $0$ of $24$ were lifted beyond boundary status, giving promoted fraction $0.0$.

\paragraph{Held-out stability.}
The held-out split check used three locus folds. In the left fold, $24$ positives were accepted and $24$ decoys were rejected. The middle fold had the same $24$ positive and $24$ decoy counts, again with accept rate $1.0$ and reject rate $1.0$. The right fold repeated the same outcome. Thus the finite split table is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{split\ count} &= 3,\\
\mathrm{positive\ rows\ per\ split} &= 24,\\
\mathrm{decoy\ rows\ per\ split} &= 24,\\
\mathrm{positive\ accept\ rate\ per\ split} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{decoy\ reject\ rate\ per\ split} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports only the stated interval-contact judgment on the supplied curated rows and matched controls.

\paragraph{Local boundary of interpretation.}
The chapter records a successful finite contact between a predeclared start--stop-window classifier and curated coding-interval annotation. Nothing in this contact converts codon-window geometry into translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or universality. The NCBI RefSeq annotation remains a curated biological input to the run, not part of the mathematical kernel. The matched decoy and boundary controls also do not classify arbitrary windows outside the $168$-row dataset. The self-check recorded $3$ checked gates and explicitly kept codon geometry from being promoted across biological layers. The admissible conclusion is therefore exactly: within this finite dataset, the geometry-only ORF-window classifier separated curated positive intervals from matched decoys and left boundary cases unpromoted.
