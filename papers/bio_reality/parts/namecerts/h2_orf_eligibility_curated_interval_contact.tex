\subsection{Curated interval contact for ORF eligibility}
\label{subsec:bioreality-orf-eligibility-curated-interval-contact}

\paragraph{Claim scope.}
This record concerns only open-reading-frame eligibility as tested by a finite curated interval contact. The biological object is a start--stop codon interval question: whether a nucleotide window, already presented as an outside curated biological interval, is accepted by the frozen start/stop-window eligibility classifier. The contact is not a claim of translation, not a claim of folded protein structure, not a claim of physical admissibility, not a claim of biological function, not a mechanism, and not a universal biological law. Those assertions would require distinct biological contacts with the appropriate measured or curated observables. The present claim is narrower: under a predeclared start/stop-window rule, a finite RefSeq-derived interval table separates curated CDS intervals from matched non-ORF decoys while keeping boundary cases unpromoted.

\paragraph{Frozen finite substrate.}
The codon-level geometry used by the classifier is fixed before the curated interval contact is evaluated. The motif set $R$ is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\\
&\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The ambient codon set $M$ has cardinality $20$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
M = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The saturation check reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{Sat}_{f3}(R)=M\setminus\{\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with empty $f3$ boundary. The same finite substrate reports $M$-face vector $(20,38,26,8,1)$, quotient face vector $(10,14,6,1)$, and quotient size $10$. These codon and quotient facts are used only to define the frozen geometric classifier; they are not biological observations by themselves.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The interval contact is the curated file \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json}. It contains $168$ rows and has snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$. Its source is NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals from \textit{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession NC\_000913.3. The required input classes are all present: \texttt{positive\_curated\_orf}, \texttt{matched\_non\_orf\_decoy}, and \texttt{boundary\_case}. The classifier identifier is \texttt{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}, and the rules were not modified during the contact evaluation. The protocol was fixed at $2026$-$05$-$29T19{:}11{:}13+00{:}00$, before external evidence was observed at $2026$-$05$-$30T10{:}29{:}31+00{:}00$; the record also states that baselines, held-out splits, and input classes were fixed, and that the analysis used no translation or structure input.

\paragraph{Finite outcomes.}
The positive interval test accepts $72$ of $72$ curated positives, for accept rate $1.0$ and exact one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. The matched decoy test rejects $72$ of $72$ decoys, for reject rate $1.0$ and the same exact one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. The predeclared paired baseline comparison records $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, hence win rate $1.0$ and exact one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. The boundary-control rows have $n=24$, promoted $0$, and promoted fraction $0.0$. Thus the accepted content is a finite interval-level separation: curated CDS intervals are accepted, matched decoys are rejected, and boundary cases do not become ORF claims.

\paragraph{Held-out stability.}
The held-out stability check uses three splits. In \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_left\_locus\_fold}, $24$ positives have positive accept rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys have decoy reject rate $1.0$. In \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_middle\_locus\_fold}, $24$ positives again have accept rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys again have reject rate $1.0$. In \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_right\_locus\_fold}, the same counts and rates hold. All three split checks pass. A separate earlier window-enumeration contact had only one input window and classified one window, with scope limited to external-window start/stop enumeration. The curated interval contact is therefore stronger than the one-window seed because it attaches a $168$-row curated interval table, matched decoys, boundary cases, exact binomial tests, and held-out split stability.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The support is finite and dataset-scoped. It says that, for this frozen classifier and this curated RefSeq interval table, start/stop-window eligibility separates the supplied positive intervals from their matched non-ORF decoys and declines to promote boundary rows. It does not say that every genomic window is classified, that every accepted interval is translated, that any protein is folded, that any molecular structure is physically realized, that any biological function is present, or that a general mechanism has been established. The NCBI RefSeq annotation remains an outside curated biological contact, while the codon geometry remains the internal finite classifier substrate. The two are connected only at the level of this interval-eligibility test.
