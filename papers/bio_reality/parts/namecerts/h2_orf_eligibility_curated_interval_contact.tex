\paragraph{Object of the finite test.}
This record concerns the biological predicate called ORF eligibility, restricted to the finite question whether a nucleotide interval has the start--stop boundary form required by the frozen classifier. The biological object is not protein production and not an inferred molecular function. It is the narrower event that a curated interval, supplied from outside the codon-geometry calculation, is accepted by a predeclared start/stop-window enumeration rule. The contact used here is a curated interval dataset extracted from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession NC\_000913.3, with snapshot date 2026-05-30. The loaded dataset has $168$ rows and exactly the three required input classes: positive curated ORF intervals, matched non-ORF decoys, and boundary cases. The scope is therefore the finite contact
$$
\begin{aligned}
&\text{scope}=\text{external\_orf\_interval\_contact\_only},\\
&\text{row count}=168,\\
&\text{classes}=\{\text{positive curated ORF},\text{ matched non-ORF decoy},\text{ boundary case}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The frozen classifier is named $\mathrm{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}$, and its rules were not modified after the contact was observed.

\paragraph{Pre-contact boundary and protocol order.}
Before this curated interval contact, the same seed was only a boundary probe: start and stop codons can define a window to be tested, but the window is not an observed biological interval without a sequence, transcript, or curated annotation input. The protocol records that input classes, held-out splits, classifier rules, and baselines were fixed before the outside observation. The protocol was frozen at 2026-05-29T19:11:13+00:00, while the external evidence was observed at 2026-05-30T10:29:31+00:00. The analysis also records that no translation or structure input was used. Thus the finite row is a contact test against curated interval annotation, not a derivation of biology from codon geometry alone.

\paragraph{Curated interval contact result.}
The curated contact produced a balanced finite acceptance/rejection pattern. There were $72$ positive curated ORF intervals, all $72$ accepted by the frozen rule. The corresponding exact one-sided binomial upper-tail value was
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{+}=2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were also $72$ matched non-ORF decoys, all $72$ rejected, with rejection rate $1.0$ and the same exact upper-tail value
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{-}=2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The predeclared paired baseline comparison had $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, hence win rate $1.0$ and
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{pair}}=2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The held-out stability check was split into three folds: left locus, middle locus, and right locus. Each fold had $24$ positives and $24$ decoys, with positive accept rate $1.0$, decoy reject rate $1.0$, and pass status true. The separate boundary-case cap contained $24$ boundary cases, of which $0$ were promoted, so the promoted fraction was $0.0$.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The finite result supports the following narrowly worded claim: after the curated RefSeq interval contact is supplied, the frozen start/stop-window classifier separates the recorded positives from matched decoys on this dataset, remains stable over the three held-out splits, and does not promote boundary cases. The result is strong as a finite contact test because the positive, decoy, paired, and held-out checks all give perfect finite rates on their declared rows, with exact one-sided binomial values $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$ for the three $72$-trial statistics. It is also narrow: the contact remains an annotation-level ORF-eligibility contact, not a claim that any interval is translated, folded into a protein structure, physically admissible as a molecule, biologically functional, mechanistically explained, or universal across organisms and datasets.

\paragraph{Local non-promotion rule.}
No conclusion in this section crosses the layer boundary from interval eligibility to downstream biology. The curated interval contact does not establish translation. It does not establish protein structure. It does not establish physical admissibility. It does not establish biological function. It does not establish a mechanism. It does not establish a global biological law. The matched decoys and boundary controls are controls inside this dataset, not a classifier for all possible windows. The RefSeq contact remains a curated biological annotation input and is not part of the codon-geometry kernel itself. A later claim about translation, folding, physical realization, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate contact carrying that specific kind of evidence.
