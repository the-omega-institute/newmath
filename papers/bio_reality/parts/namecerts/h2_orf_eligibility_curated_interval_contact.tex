\subsection{Curated interval contact for open-reading-frame eligibility}
\label{sec:h2-orf-eligibility-curated-interval-contact}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record concerns the finite test layer at which a nucleotide interval may be called eligible as an open reading frame because an independently curated biological interval has been attached to a previously fixed start--stop window classifier. The object under test is not translation, not folded protein structure, not physical admissibility, and not function. It is only the weaker claim that a finite interval, already present in a curated annotation source, is accepted by the frozen start--stop enumeration rule while matched non-ORF decoys are rejected. The coordinate geometry supplies a classifier and a list of admissible codon patterns; the biological assertion enters only through the curated interval table.

\paragraph{Finite codon substrate.}
The codon-side substrate used by the classifier was fixed before the curated interval contact was evaluated. The recurrent motif set is
$R=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$,
with no missing and no extra elements relative to the motif-union computation. The larger finite set is
$M=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}$,
so $|M|=20$. The saturation check gives $\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=M\setminus\{\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG}\}$ and the recorded $f_3$ boundary is empty. The ambient finite geometry has $f$-vector $(20,38,26,8,1)$, while the quotient image has size $10$ and quotient $f$-vector $(10,14,6,1)$. Thus the geometric part of the experiment is a finite classifier substrate, not a source of biological realization by itself.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The interval table was loaded from the curated ORF interval contact dataset with $168$ rows, snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$. The source is NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals from \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession $\mathrm{NC\_000913.3}$. The required input classes were all present: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs. The protocol records that classifier rules, input classes, held-out splits, and baselines were fixed at $2026$-$05$-$29\,19{:}11{:}13+00{:}00$, before the external evidence time $2026$-$05$-$30\,10{:}29{:}31+00{:}00$. The classifier identifier was $\mathrm{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}$, and the audit states that the rules were not modified after observation.

\paragraph{Observed finite performance.}
The positive curated intervals were accepted in all $72$ tested cases, giving accept rate $1.0$ and exact one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. The matched non-ORF decoys were rejected in all $72$ tested cases, again with rate $1.0$ and exact one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. The predeclared paired baseline comparison records $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, hence win rate $1.0$ with the same exact upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. The boundary-control portion contains $24$ cases and promotes none of them, so the promoted fraction is $0.0$.

\paragraph{Held-out stability.}
The contact was also checked across three held-out folds. On $\mathrm{refseq\_nc\_000913\_left\_locus\_fold}$, $24$ positives were accepted at rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys were rejected at rate $1.0$. The same finite rates were recorded on $\mathrm{refseq\_nc\_000913\_middle\_locus\_fold}$ and $\mathrm{refseq\_nc\_000913\_right\_locus\_fold}$. All three splits passed. This gives a row-level contact result for the curated dataset rather than a law about all nucleotide strings.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The ready claim is therefore a scoped ORF-eligibility contact: a frozen geometry-only start--stop classifier, evaluated against a curated RefSeq interval table, separates $72$ positive curated ORF intervals from $72$ matched decoys, leaves $24$ boundary cases unpromoted, and remains stable over three held-out splits. The conclusion is deliberately narrow. It does not say that any accepted interval is translated in vivo, folded into a protein, physically admissible as a molecule, assigned a biological function, explained by a mechanism, or representative of a universal rule. Any such stronger assertion would require another contact carrying the corresponding observational content. The self-check records the same boundary: three gates were checked, and no codon geometry was promoted across biological layers.
