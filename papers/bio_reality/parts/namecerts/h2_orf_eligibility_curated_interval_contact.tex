\paragraph{Question.}
This record asks whether a finite start--stop codon enumeration can be placed in contact with curated biological interval data without turning that contact into a stronger biological assertion. The layer under test is \emph{ORF eligibility}: a window may be accepted only as a start/stop-delimited interval in an externally curated sequence annotation. The contact is not a claim of translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, biochemical mechanism, biological function, or universal genomic law. Those stronger layers would require separate observations addressed to those layers.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The result is an interval-contact statement only. It says that the frozen start/stop-window classifier agrees with the curated CDS-interval contact on the finite rows described above. It does not say that any accepted interval is translated in vivo, that a protein product folds, that a molecular state is physically admissible, that a biological function is realized, that a mechanism has been identified, or that the rule holds universally outside the recorded dataset. Matched decoys and boundary controls delimit this contact; they do not classify windows beyond the row set. The RefSeq annotation remains curated biological input to the experiment, not content generated by the finite codon geometry alone.
