\subsection{Curated Interval Contact for Open-Reading-Frame Eligibility}

\paragraph{Claim under finite contact.}
This record concerns the layer called \emph{open-reading-frame eligibility}.  The biological object is not a translated product, a folded molecule, a physically realized admissible state, or a biological function.  It is only the question whether a finite nucleotide interval, supplied from curated sequence annotation, is accepted by a fixed start--stop window classifier while matched non-ORF decoys are rejected.  The claim identifier is \texttt{h2.orf\_eligibility.curated\_interval\_contact}.  Its scope is exactly \texttt{external\_orf\_interval\_contact\_only}, with snapshot date \texttt{2026-05-30}.  The contact source is the NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS interval annotation for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession \texttt{NC\_000913.3}.  The loaded curated interval dataset contains $168$ rows from \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json}; the required input classes are \texttt{positive\_curated\_orf}, \texttt{matched\_non\_orf\_decoy}, and \texttt{boundary\_case}.

\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.}
The result is a finite contact between a frozen start--stop interval classifier and an external curated annotation table.  It supports the narrow statement that, on this dataset and under the predeclared protocol, curated CDS intervals are accepted and matched non-ORF decoys are rejected, while boundary cases are not promoted.  It does not say that any accepted interval is translated, folded, physically admissible, functional, mechanistically explained, or governed by a universal law.  It also does not classify nucleotide windows outside the recorded dataset.  The RefSeq annotation remains an external curated biological observation, not content generated by the finite codon coordinate system.  The self-check row is consistent with this boundary: it records a BioReality-only gate with $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is carried across biological layers without additional contact.
