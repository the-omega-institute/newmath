\paragraph{Object and layer.}

The biological object is not a translated product, a folded protein, a biochemical activity, or an organism-level role. It is only the finite question of whether externally supplied nucleotide intervals pass a fixed start--stop-window classifier while matched non-ORF decoys and boundary cases do not receive the same status. The classifier used in this contact is recorded as $\mathrm{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}$, with classifier rules unchanged during the observation. The scope string attached to the run is $\mathrm{external\_orf\_interval\_contact\_only}$, so the claim is deliberately limited to curated interval contact and not to any downstream biological realization.

\paragraph{Self-check and boundary of meaning.}
The self-check row records $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. Consequently the admissible conclusion is precise: this finite contact supports ORF-eligibility classification for the curated interval dataset under the frozen start--stop-window protocol. It does not establish translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, organismal relevance, or a universal law about all possible nucleotide windows. Those stronger claims would require separate contacts that measure the corresponding layer. The RefSeq annotation remains curated biological evidence supplied to the experiment; it is not part of the formal codon kernel and is not derivable from the codon geometry alone.
