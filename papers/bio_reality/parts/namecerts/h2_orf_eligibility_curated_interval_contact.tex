\subsection{Curated interval contact for ORF eligibility}
\label{subsec:h2-orf-eligibility-curated-interval-contact}

\paragraph{Question fixed by the record.}
This record concerns only open-reading-frame eligibility as tested by finite start and stop codon interval data.  The biological object is not protein production, folded order, biochemical admissibility, cellular role, mechanism, or a universal law of genome organization.  The tested question is narrower: after a start/stop-window classifier has already been fixed, does a curated interval contact separate annotated coding intervals from matched non-ORF decoys while refusing to promote boundary cases?  The answer recorded here is affirmative for the supplied finite dataset and only for that dataset.

\paragraph{Curated interval contact.}
The dataset loaded for the contact is \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json}.  It contains $168$ rows from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession NC\_000913.3, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$.  The required input classes are present: \texttt{positive\_curated\_orf}, \texttt{matched\_non\_orf\_decoy}, and \texttt{boundary\_case}.  The protocol record says that the classifier rules, input classes, baselines, and held-out splits were fixed before the external evidence was observed: protocol time $2026$-$05$-$29$T$19{:}11{:}13$+00:00 and evidence-observation time $2026$-$05$-$30$T$10{:}29{:}31$+00:00.  The classifier identifier is \texttt{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}, and its rule-modification flag is false.

Among matched non-ORF decoys, $72$ of $72$ were rejected, giving rejection rate $1.0$ with the same recorded upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\cdot 10^{-22}$.  The predeclared pairwise baseline comparison records $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, hence win rate $1.0$ and again
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}

The boundary control contains $24$ cases, of which $0$ were promoted, so its promoted fraction is $0.0$.  These numbers are the finite-row content of the claim: perfect separation on the supplied positive and matched-decoy rows, plus no promotion on the supplied boundary rows.

\paragraph{Held-out stability.}
The contact also records three held-out splits.  In \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_left\_locus\_fold}, $24$ positive rows had positive acceptance rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoy rows had decoy rejection rate $1.0$. The stability statement is therefore not an asymptotic claim; it is the conjunction of three finite checks, each with $24$ positives and $24$ decoys, all separated in the same direction.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
This contact is sufficient to move the ORF-eligibility seed out of mere contact-waiting status for the curated interval task described above.  The finite evidence says that the frozen start/stop-window classifier separates the supplied RefSeq CDS positives from matched non-ORF decoys and leaves boundary cases unpromoted.  It does not say that the intervals are translated in vivo, that their products fold, that they are physically admissible molecules, that they have a function, that a mechanism has been identified, or that the behavior extends beyond the finite curated dataset.  Those stronger statements would require distinct contacts able to test those layers directly.  The self-check attached to the packet records the same boundary in compact form: three gates were checked, and no codon geometry was promoted across biological layers.
