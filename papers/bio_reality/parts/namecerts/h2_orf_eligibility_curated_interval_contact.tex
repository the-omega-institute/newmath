\paragraph{Layer and question.}
This record concerns only the layer called open-reading-frame eligibility.  The biological object is the finite test in which a nucleotide interval is admitted or rejected by start-codon and stop-codon enumeration after an externally curated interval has been supplied.  The layer is intentionally narrow: it asks whether a recorded interval passes the declared start/stop-window criterion, not whether the interval is translated, folded, physically realizable as a protein, functional in a cell, mechanistically explained, or universal across genomes.  Those stronger biological assertions require their own contacts and are not consequences of the enumeration performed here.
