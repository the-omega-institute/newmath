\subsection{Curated interval contact for open-reading-frame eligibility}
\label{subsec:h2-orf-eligibility-curated-interval-contact}

\paragraph{Claim and biological object.}
This record concerns the biological object ``open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration.''  The local question is deliberately narrow: given a finite nucleotide-window classifier that uses start and stop boundary enumeration, can a separately curated interval annotation supply enough contact with biological data to test eligibility as an interval-level statement?  The answer recorded here is affirmative only for the curated interval contact described below.  It is not a claim that the interval is translated, folded, physically admissible as a molecule, functional in a cell, mechanistically explained, or universal across genomes.  Those stronger assertions require distinct observations and are not licensed by the codon-window calculation.

\paragraph{Pre-contact boundary state.}
Before the curated interval contact is attached, the seed packet only states that start/stop-window geometry gives a possible eligibility test. \paragraph{Curated interval contact.}
The contact used here is the finite dataset at \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json}.  It contains $168$ rows, with required classes \texttt{positive\_curated\_orf}, \texttt{matched\_non\_orf\_decoy}, and \texttt{boundary\_case}.  The source is the NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS interval annotation for \textit{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession NC\_000913.3, snapshot date 2026-05-30.  The classifier identifier was \texttt{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}; its rules were recorded as unmodified, and the analysis used no translation or protein-structure input.  The protocol was frozen at 2026-05-29T19:11:13+00:00, while the external evidence was observed at 2026-05-30T10:29:31+00:00.

\paragraph{Positive intervals and matched decoys.}
The positive interval test accepted all $72$ positive curated ORF intervals, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
72/72 &= 1.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{one\mbox{-}sided}} &= 2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The matched non-ORF decoy test rejected all $72$ decoys, also giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
72/72 &= 1.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{one\mbox{-}sided}} &= 2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The predeclared paired baseline comparison records $72$ wins out of $72$ pairs, hence win rate $1.0$ with the same exact one-sided binomial tail probability $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  These numbers support only dataset-scoped discrimination between curated positive intervals and matched decoys under the frozen classifier.

\paragraph{Held-out split stability.}
The interval contact was also checked on three held-out locus folds.  The left fold, middle fold, and right fold each had $24$ positive intervals and $24$ decoys.  In each fold the positive accept rate was $1.0$, the decoy reject rate was $1.0$, and the fold passed.  Thus the split-level record is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{folds} &= 3,\\
\mathrm{positive\ intervals\ per\ fold} &= 24,\\
\mathrm{decoys\ per\ fold} &= 24,\\
\mathrm{positive\ accept\ rate} &= 1.0,\\

\end{aligned}

Boundary cases provide an additional guardrail: $24$ boundary cases were examined, $0$ were promoted, and the promoted fraction was $0.0$.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The finite evidence establishes a curated-interval eligibility contact for this dataset: a frozen start/stop-window classifier separated $72$ curated positive CDS intervals from $72$ matched non-ORF decoys, remained stable across three held-out locus folds, and did not promote $24$ boundary cases.  The result is therefore an interval-annotation contact, not a biological completion of the entire ORF story.  The NCBI RefSeq annotation remains a curated biological input external to the finite codon geometry.  No translation event, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, cellular role, causal mechanism, global biological law, or classification of windows outside this dataset follows from the result without another contact designed for that stronger layer.
