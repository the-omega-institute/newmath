\subsection{Curated interval contact for open-reading-frame eligibility}
\label{sec:bioreality-orf-eligibility-curated-interval-contact}

\paragraph{Object and layer.}
This record concerns open-reading-frame eligibility as a finite start--stop interval question.  The biological object is not translation, folded protein structure, cellular viability, molecular mechanism, or biological function.  It is the narrower assertion that a nucleotide interval supplied by a curated annotation source passes a predeclared start/stop-window eligibility classifier.  The layer is therefore $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$, and the admissible conclusion is confined to that layer.

\paragraph{Contact dataset.}
The interval contact is the curated dataset at \texttt{tools/bio\_reality/data/curated\_orf\_interval\_contact\_dataset.json}.  It contains $168$ rows, drawn from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \textit{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession NC\_000913.3, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$.  The required input classes are all present: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.  The earlier one-window start/stop enumeration contact had scope \texttt{external\_window\_start\_stop\_enumeration\_only}, classified $1$ of $1$ input windows, and explicitly remained at ORF-eligibility scope.  The present contact enlarges that test to interval rows while retaining the same biological-layer restriction.

The rule set was fixed before the external interval observation: the protocol time was $2026$-$05$-$29$T$19$:$11$:$13$+$00$:$00$, while the external evidence time was $2026$-$05$-$30$T$10$:$29$:$31$+$00$:$00$. Hence the finite codon rule and the biological interval rows meet only at the declared eligibility test.

\paragraph{Local boundary of the result.}
The conclusion is that this curated interval contact supports ORF-window eligibility for the recorded interval dataset.  It does not establish translation realization, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or a universal genomic law.  The matched decoys and boundary controls do not classify windows outside the $168$-row dataset, and the RefSeq annotation remains an outside curated biological input rather than kernel content of the finite codon geometry.  In the self-check ledger, the boundary statement is explicit: no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers, and the checked gate count is $3$.  Therefore the ready claim is a finite interval-contact result at the $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$ layer only.
