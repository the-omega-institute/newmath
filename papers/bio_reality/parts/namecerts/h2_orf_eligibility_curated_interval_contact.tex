\subsection{Curated interval contact for ORF-window eligibility}
\label{sec:bioreality-orf-eligibility-curated-interval-contact}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question fixed by the contact.}
The record tests a deliberately narrow biological assertion: whether a frozen start--stop window classifier can separate curated coding-sequence intervals from matched non-ORF decoys inside one finite annotation dataset.  The biological object is open-reading-frame eligibility as exposed by start and stop codon enumeration.  The claim is not that the codon geometry produces translation, protein form, physical feasibility, cellular role, mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Those stronger predicates require additional observations with their own admissibility checks.

\paragraph{Finite codon substrate.}
The codon-level packet used by the classifier sits over two finite sets.  The motif-compressed set is
$R=\{AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}$,
with no missing and no extra entries in the recorded motif union.  The larger set is
$M=\{AAA,AAG,ACA,ACG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}$,
so $|M|=20$.  The equality check records $M$ as the union named by the WNR and CUN components.  The saturation check gives
$\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=M\setminus\{ACA,ACG\}$,
and the $f_3$ boundary is empty.  Thus the contact is not introduced into an unconstrained codon universe; it is attached to an explicitly listed finite codon region.

Its scope is restricted to external-window start--stop enumeration, not to translation or any higher biological layer.

\paragraph{Observed finite separation.}

The one-sided exact binomial tail recorded for the positive acceptance statistic is $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$, and the same tail value is recorded for matched decoy rejection.  The predeclared paired baseline comparison records $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, hence win rate $1.0$, again with one-sided binomial tail $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  In compact form,
$$
\begin{aligned}

These are finite-row measurements against a curated annotation table, not consequences of codon geometry alone.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
This chapter therefore licenses only the following bounded reading.  For the finite codon substrate listed above, under the frozen classifier \texttt{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}, and against the $168$-row curated RefSeq interval contact, the classifier separates the recorded positive curated ORF intervals from matched decoys with $72/72$ positive acceptances, $72/72$ decoy rejections, $72/72$ paired baseline wins, and no promotion among $24$ boundary cases.  The conclusion remains at ORF-window eligibility relative to this contact.  It does not say that translation occurred; it does not say that a protein folded; it does not say that the molecule is physically admissible; it does not assign function; it does not infer a mechanism; and it does not generalize beyond the finite rows and source snapshot named here without a new contact designed for that stronger target.
