\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns the claim \texttt{h0.R.punctured.cube}.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon labels.  The distinguished row $R$ is not inferred from molecular mechanism; it is the finite row isolated by the window-six coordinate test and then checked against the curated standard-code label table.  The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The separate cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons.  Within the cubical cell count used by the row test, the verified face-vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is a punctured-cube pattern in the finite coordinate display: it has thirteen retained codon vertices, sixteen retained one-dimensional incidences, and four retained square faces.  The biological readout attached to this computation is only the standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.

The null comparison for the shape test used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, and found one exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  Hence the finite empirical rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
A second null that preserves amino-acid class recorded $N=2000$, observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its reported rates were

\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\

These numbers support the claim that the displayed row is a non-generic local pattern under the sampled finite rearrangements, while still leaving the interpretation at the code-label layer.

The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out test recorded anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These finite readbacks say that the punctured-cube row has strong local recoverability in the tested coordinate-and-label setting; they do not say that a codon is translated, folded, selected, or made functional by the geometry itself.

This makes the row useful as a finite codon-table diagnostic: each listed module is accounted for by predictable or boundary behavior in the finite tests.

\paragraph{Local interpretation boundary.}
The curated standard-code table is a contact for codon-label readout only.  It lets the punctured-cube row be compared with amino-acid and stop labels, and it lets local boundary tiles be counted, sampled, and left out.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the supported conclusion is narrow: the standard-code label table contains a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row with the displayed face-vector, spectral values, null-model rates, leave-out recovery behavior, and complete module accounting.  Any claim about protein structure, physical feasibility, organism-level function, or a universal biological law would need an additional contact that measures that layer directly.
