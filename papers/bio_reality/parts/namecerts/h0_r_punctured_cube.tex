\subsection{Punctured Codon Cube Readback}\label{sec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the ordinary codon alphabet and then compared with a curated standard-code table. The relevant row is a code-layer row, not a mechanism row: the table supplies codon-to-label readback for amino-acid or stop labels, and the BEDC object supplies only coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation structure on that finite display. The observed distinguished region is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The direct cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the expected codon list is exactly the same thirteen rows. Thus the finite row is not merely a size statistic: the named codons in $R$ are fixed by the verified table row and can be read back one by one against the standard-code labels.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The shape certificate identifies $R$ with a punctured local cube in the window-six coordinate system. Its verified face-count vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row contains thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional tile faces in the induced local geometry. This is the finite content of the phrase ``punctured codon cube'' in this chapter. It does not say that the whole genetic code is forced by cube geometry, and it does not say that a biological process realizes this tile. It says that this thirteen-row standard-code subset has the displayed local tile incidence pattern when placed in the six-bit codon coordinate carrier.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Two null rows constrain the interpretation. In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ sampled alternatives, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. The same run found $25$ cases with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sample required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the row is a finite randomized comparison rather than an analytic distribution theorem. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null row, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$ among $N=2000$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$. The corresponding reported values are $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$. These numbers say that the observed finite shape is uncommon under the stated null samplers; they do not promote the local tile into a universal law.

These rows are evidence that the code-layer tile is predictive for certain finite readback tasks, not evidence for translation dynamics or downstream function.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The contact here is the curated standard-code table, whose resolution is codon label readback. Therefore this chapter cannot claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Amino-acid label sequences are not folded protein structures, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law. Any claim beyond the code-read layer requires a separate reality contact that directly observes that higher layer. Within that boundary, the finite result is precise: the verified thirteen-codon row $R$ is a punctured local cube with $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, a recorded spectral fingerprint, significant finite null comparisons, complete module assignment, and strong leave-one readback behavior against the curated standard-code label table.
