\subsection{Punctured Cube Boundary in the Standard-Code Row}
\label{sec:bior:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the claim \texttt{h0.R.punctured.cube} for the standard-code codon table, read only at the label layer. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube: each codon is represented by a six-bit display, grouped into local tile faces and then compared with the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels. In this row the distinguished set $R$ is exactly the following thirteen-codon boundary set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check reports $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube check reports the same ordered list of codons. The local cell count is not merely a count of labels: the face data match the finite vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row contains thirteen selected vertices, sixteen selected one-dimensional adjacencies, and four selected square faces in the induced six-bit cube geometry. This is the concrete reason the row is described as a punctured cube rather than as an arbitrary codon subset.

\paragraph{Spectral and combinatorial numerics.}
The same row has a recorded spectral profile. The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial cubic evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is effectively zero at the stated numerical tolerance, and the spectral-radius entry agrees with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$. The shape-significance experiment used $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and therefore $55377$ duplicate retries. Among these samples there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact matches to the observed shape, with lower-tail count $7$ at observed size $20$. Its reported values are $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$. These numbers support the narrower claim that the row is a nontrivial local pattern in the finite code-label display.

\paragraph{Readback performance.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. Within $M-T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and after adjoining the boundary term it returned to $0.9841269841269841$. The family-level split covered $5$ families: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, and bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each had hit rate $1.0$ above baseline. The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These tests do not make the local tile a biological law; they show that the same finite boundary is useful for code-label recovery under the specified deletion probes.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen selected codons are fully partitioned by the module-classification row. The assigned count is $13$, the reported $R$ size is $13$, and there are no missing, extra, or unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This partition makes the singleton-corner and three-point-class behavior explicit: $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ and $\mathrm{ile\_met}$ are singleton entries, while $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$ supplies a three-codon class on the boundary. The four-codon $\mathrm{cun}$ block is the largest module in the row, and the two-codon modules record the remaining split classes. The module check is important because it prevents the punctured-cube claim from depending on an unaccounted codon.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The empirical contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table. It supplies the codon-to-label readback needed to identify amino-acid and stop labels in the finite row. That contact is a label table, not an observation of ribosomal translation, molecular structure, thermodynamic admissibility, cellular function, or a universal mechanism. Consequently, the result may be stated as follows: in the six-bit codon display, the standard-code labels single out a thirteen-codon row whose finite geometry has vector $(13,16,4)$, whose spectral numerics satisfy the recorded cubic to numerical tolerance, and whose deletion tests recover code-label targets at the reported rates. Nothing in this row alone licenses a claim about actual translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical feasibility, organismal function, or necessity across all biological systems. Any such higher-layer assertion would require a separate contact that observes that layer directly.
