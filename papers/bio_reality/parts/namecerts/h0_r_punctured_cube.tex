\subsection{The punctured-cube row for the standard-code boundary tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The row studies a window-six codon display over the codon cube $Q_6$, with the standard-code label table used only as the observed label contact. The distinguished subset $R$ is the thirteen-codon row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check records $|R|=13$ and gives the same codon list. The shape check identifies this row with a punctured-cube pattern whose finite face count is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a collection of labels: it has a specific finite cubical boundary profile, with thirteen selected vertices, sixteen selected edges, and four selected square faces inside the six-bit coordinate representation.

\paragraph{Label contact.}
The biological contact is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. This contact has codon-label resolution only. It can test the code-reading layer and local label splits on the tile, but it does not observe ribosomal realization, folding, thermodynamic feasibility, organism-level operation, or biological role. The chapter therefore treats the table as a label readout for the finite row and not as evidence that the row is a universal biological rule.

These numbers should be read as coordinates of the finite tile computation: they sharpen the row geometry, but they do not by themselves convert codon labels into translated molecules or folded structures.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under row-level randomization.}
The observed shape was compared with $N=2000$ sampled alternatives using seed $42$. Because the sampler required distinct rows, it made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. Exactly one sampled row matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also constrained by an amino-acid-class-preserving null model. In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and only $7$ samples lay in the lower tail at or below the observed size. The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite comparisons support the claim that the observed row is a non-generic local pattern relative to the tested nulls. They do not establish that the pattern is forced by chemistry, selection, translation, or protein function.

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\

$$
Every listed module has either a predictable or boundary classification in the leave-one-codon calculation. The aggregate leave-one-codon row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. Hence the row behaves as a fully recovered local code-reading tile under that finite deletion probe, with the recoveries divided between predictable and boundary cases rather than anchored cases.

$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\

This makes the row a strong code-reading predictor within the finite table comparison used here. The result is still local to the supplied tables and deletion protocol: it is not a proof that all biological code variation, all organisms, or all molecular mechanisms must respect the same geometry.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The row supports the following limited mathematical-biological reading. In the six-bit codon coordinate system, the thirteen codons listed above form a punctured-cube boundary tile with face vector $(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral coordinates, rare shape behavior under the stated randomizations, complete module accounting, and high finite recovery rates in the listed deletion probes. The curated standard-code table supplies the label layer that lets the row be read as a codon-label phenomenon. No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, molecular mechanism, or universal law follows from this row unless a separate empirical contact is introduced for that higher layer. Amino-acid labels are labels in this chapter; they are not folded structures, not realized proteins, and not organism-level functions.
