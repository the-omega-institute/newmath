\subsection{Finite Carrier and Row Witness}
\label{sec:bior-punctured-cube-carrier}

\paragraph{Carrier.}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a coordinate display for the standard-code codon table.  The row under study is the distinguished set $R$ of codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The direct cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons appearing in both the cardinality row and the punctured-cube row.  The finite cell count recorded for the local tile is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the data describe a thirteen-vertex, sixteen-edge, four-face local subcomplex inside the codon-coordinate cube.  The word ``punctured'' is used here only for this finite-row geometry: it is a coordinate fact about the displayed codons, not a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Readback contact.}
The only external contact used by this chapter is the curated standard-code label table.  That contact assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label and therefore supports code-layer readback.  It does not by itself supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The mathematical object is therefore a local tile with label readback, not a claim that organisms must implement the geometry as a law.

\subsection{Shape, Spectrum, and Null Rows}
\label{sec:bior-punctured-cube-shape-spectrum}

\paragraph{Shape row.}
The shape-significance row compares the observed tile with $N=2000$ sampled alternatives under seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the sampled alternatives there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  Thus the finite-row estimates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrow claim that the displayed row is uncommon under the stated sampling procedure.  They do not promote the row to a universal biological principle.

\paragraph{Spectrum row.}
The spectral row records three scalar quantities attached to the same coordinate construction:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual.  The spectral radius in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$.  This gives a numerical consistency check for the coordinate row: the spectrum and the local tile are read as one finite construction, with a residual at numerical zero for the reported precision.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null row preserves amino-acid class information while testing the observed module size.  With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape match count is $0$.  The observed module size is $20$, and $7$ sampled alternatives fall in the lower tail at or below that size.  The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This row is useful because it keeps part of the label structure while still treating the coordinate tile as a testable finite object.  It does not remove the need for an external code table; curated labels remain reality contacts rather than consequences of the cube alone.

The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  These numbers say that the finite coordinate and boundary construction has strong code-read recovery behavior under this leave-one-code-out probe.  The claim remains local: recovery of codon labels is not recovery of translation machinery or protein function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The leave-one-codon-out row records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The thresholded recovery-rate flag is also $1.0$.  In this row the predictive signal comes from closure and boundary behavior rather than from anchors.  That distinction matters: the evidence supports a local boundary-tile account of code-read recoverability, not a global explanation of genetic-code origin or biological necessity.

The classification row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons. The family-level leave-one-code-out row has family count $5$ and hit-rate-above-baseline values $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. \subsection{Local Boundary and Cannot-Claim Line}
\label{sec:bior-punctured-cube-boundary}

\paragraph{Supported claim.}
The supported claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure for a thirteen-codon row of the standard-code table.  The row is concrete, the cell count is $(13,16,4)$, the scalar spectrum is numerically consistent, the shape row is rare under the stated null, and the leave-one probes recover code labels at high rates.  The result is therefore a finite code-read record with a standard-code reality bridge.

\paragraph{Blocked promotion.}
No stronger biological layer follows from this chapter.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments only.  It does not observe translation realization, ribosomal dynamics, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any later claim across those boundaries must add a separate contact that directly tests the higher layer.
