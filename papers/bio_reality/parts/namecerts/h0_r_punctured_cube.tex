\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code label table. The observed row $R$ is not asserted as a biological mechanism. It is a finite code-read subset selected by the window-six coordinate display and then checked against the external label table. The verified row has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\\
&\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row was separately recorded as $|R|=13$, so the set-level and count-level checks agree. The associated cubical face data match the punctured-cube signature
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
which is the finite combinatorial content of the phrase ``punctured cube'' in this chapter. The claim is therefore a row claim inside the code-read layer: these thirteen codons form the verified local boundary tile, with sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies and four two-dimensional faces in the computed display.

\paragraph{Shape significance against row reshufflings.}
The finite-row evidence includes two null checks. In the first, $N=2000$ distinct sampled rows were attempted under the reported sampling protocol with seed $42$. Obtaining those $2000$ distinct samples required $57377$ total distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. Exact shape agreement occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap agreement at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrower statement that the observed local tile is uncommon under that finite-row comparison. They do not convert the tile into a universal rule for genetic codes.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information. In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed closure size was $20$. The exact-shape match count was $0$, so the reported exact-shape $p$-value was $0.0$ at the finite sampling resolution. The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
This is a stronger class-preserving finite-row stress test of the same local structure. It says that, among the sampled class-preserving alternatives, the observed $M$-size and exact local shape are rare. It still remains a statement about code-label geometry, not about translation realization or function.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, whose role is to assign codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact can test code-read agreement and local codon-tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Therefore the chapter claim is deliberately bounded: window-six coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table, and the supplied finite-row probes make that pattern numerically sharp. No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies it.
