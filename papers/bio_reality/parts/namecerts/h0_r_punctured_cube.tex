\subsection{Punctured-cube row for the standard-code boundary tile}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
This record concerns the six-coordinate codon cube used only as a code-layer display for the standard genetic-code table.  The verified row named $h0.R.punctured.cube$ isolates the following thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The companion cardinality check reports $|R|=13$ and returns exactly the same codon list, so the row is not merely a size statement: it is a named finite subset of the codon cube.  Its cell-complex summary has $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, which records thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the local tile.  The biological readout attached to the row is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels; that contact says what labels the row carries, not why cellular translation realizes them.

\paragraph{Local shape and spectral measurements.}
The shape test around this row is numerically nontrivial.  In the finite randomization with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed exact shape appeared once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The looser high-overlap test had $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The same run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the small exact count should be read as a finite-row result under a heavily constrained sampling regime rather than as an asymptotic law.  A separate class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p=0.0$ for exact class-preserving shape recovery, and lower-tail $p=0.0035$.  The spectral calculation gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the cubic residual reported as approximately $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  Thus the row has a stable finite spectral signature at the level of the encoded coordinate-and-relation calculation.

\paragraph{Boundary modules.}
The module classification assigns all thirteen $R$ codons without extras or omissions.  The modules are $agr=\{AGA,AGG\}$, $ar\_lys=\{AAA\}$, $cun=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, $ile\_met=\{AUA\}$, $ssn=\{UCA,UUA\}$, and $stop\_trp=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$.  The completeness row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  In the leave-one-codon experiment, recovery rate is $1.0$, predictable count is $8$, boundary count is $5$, anchor count is $0$, and the high closure-coverage count is $8$.  This supports the finite claim that the local tile separates predictable interior behavior from boundary behavior inside the code table.  It does not identify a translation mechanism or a protein-structure cause.

\paragraph{Code-table readback tests.}
The leave-one-code-out calculation evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate after adjoining the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  Family-level reporting covers $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, and bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each report $1.0$.  These figures say that the local window-six construction is highly informative for code-label recovery within the tested finite table family.  They do not turn the coordinate display into a universal biological principle.

\paragraph{Scope of the biological claim.}
The contact used here is a curated standard-code table.  It is adequate for codon-to-label readback and for checking whether the finite row $R$ displays the reported local tile boundary.  It is not a contact with ribosomal dynamics, tRNA abundance, folding thermodynamics, physical admissibility, organismal function, or evolutionary mechanism.  Consequently the admissible conclusion is deliberately narrow: in the six-bit codon display, the listed thirteen-codon row forms a punctured-cube-like local boundary tile with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, strong finite shape enrichment, a recorded spectral signature, complete module assignment, and high leave-one-code-out recovery.  No claim of translation realization, folded protein structure, physical feasibility, biological function, mechanistic necessity, or cross-organism universality follows from these rows unless another independently specified contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
