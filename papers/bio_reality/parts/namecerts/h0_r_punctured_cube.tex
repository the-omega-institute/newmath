\subsection{Punctured-Cube Boundary in the Standard-Code Codon Table}
\label{sec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
The record treats the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube obtained by displaying each nucleotide position in binary coordinates, so that a codon is read as a point of a finite cube rather than as a biochemical mechanism.  The observable labels are the curated standard-code amino-acid or stop labels.  The object under test is therefore a finite labelled table: codon points, local tile faces, singleton label corners, three-point label classes, and the label readback supplied by the curated code table.

\paragraph{The distinguished boundary set.}
The finite row singled out by the computation is
$R=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.
Its counted size is $13$, matching the independent cardinality row for the same set.  The same run reports that this set has the punctured-cube face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite claim is not merely that thirteen codons were selected; it is that the selected codons form a local boundary tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the window-six coordinate display.

\paragraph{Shape against finite null rows.}
A shape-null run with $N=2000$ sampled alternatives reported $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that run, exactly one sampled shape matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The number of samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null row preserving amino-acid class used $N=2000$ samples and found no exact-shape matches.  It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, a lower-tail count of $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These finite comparisons support the claim that the observed code-table pattern is unusual under the recorded null generators, while still leaving the biological interpretation at the codon-label layer.

\paragraph{Local contact and scope.}
The reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments at codon-label resolution.  It does not supply a ribosomal translation experiment, a folded protein structure, a physical-admissibility assay, a function assay, or a universal biological mechanism.  Consequently the finite conclusion is local: window-six codon coordinates expose a punctured-cube boundary pattern in the standard-code label table, with the numerical and leave-one rows stated above.  No claim about translation, folding, physical realizability, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these data unless a separate contact at that higher layer is added.
