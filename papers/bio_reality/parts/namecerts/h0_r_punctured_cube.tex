\subsection{A punctured-cube codon boundary in the standard code}
\label{subsec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The observed boundary set $R$ is not introduced as a biochemical mechanism.  It is a finite label pattern inside the codon cube, read against the curated standard table only at the code-label level.  In the verified row, the $R$ set has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports the punctured-cube face count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite geometry being asserted is a thirteen-vertex local tile with sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies and four two-dimensional faces.  The biological contact used here is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels in the standard genetic code.  This contact can test code-layer readback and local label splitting, but it does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape rarity against sampled alternatives.}
The shape-significance row used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those alternatives there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ alternatives with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  An amino-acid-class-preserving null sample, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reported observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  The row therefore treats the local tile as statistically sharp under the recorded finite nulls.  The evidence remains a code-read statement: rarity of a codon-label geometry is not evidence for folded structure, organismal fitness, or universal biological necessity.
