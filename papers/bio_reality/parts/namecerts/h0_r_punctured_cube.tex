\subsection{Punctured Cube Boundary in the Standard-Code Label Table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite coordinate display for codon labels. The reality contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. This contact tests code-layer label readback and local label splits; it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality. The row under study is the boundary set $R$ selected by the local tile criterion. The verified finite row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row verifies $|R|=13$, so the boundary is not being reported as an informal pattern but as a concrete thirteen-codon subset of the codon label table.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube incidence.}
The finite incidence audit identifies this same $R$ with the punctured-cube row and records the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-read object is a local cubical boundary signature: thirteen observed codon labels, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four tile faces in the selected finite display. The claim is deliberately local. It says that the standard-code labels, when read through the chosen six-bit coordinate display, realize this punctured-cube boundary row. It does not say that the genome or the translation apparatus is governed by this geometry.

\paragraph{Null-model pressure.}
Against a shape-randomization run with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the exact shape count is $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. The high-Jaccard count is $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampler attempted $57377$ distinct draws and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite row is sparse enough that the observed exact match is not a common random occurrence under that sampling regime. A second amino-acid-class-preserving null run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$ records $0$ exact matches, $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size, observed $|M|=20$, $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035$. These rows support the finite coordinate distinction, but only at the code-read layer.

These rows say that the local coordinate-and-boundary construction has predictive recovery behavior under the finite omissions tested; they do not license any claim about protein folding, biochemical viability, or organismal function.

The finite claim is therefore closed at the row level: the punctured-cube set, its cardinality, its face vector, its spectral values, its null-model pressure, its leave-one recovery behavior, and its module partition all refer to the same code-read object.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The biological contact used here is the curated standard-code label table. It supplies codon-to-label assignments and allows local tile checks. It does not observe ribosomal translation dynamics, protein folding, structural order, thermodynamic or kinetic admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Consequently, the chapter claims only that a window-six coordinate display exposes a statistically pressured punctured-cube boundary in the standard-code label table. Any promotion from this local code-read fact to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law requires a separate reality contact and is not supplied by this row.
