\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table only as a finite code-read contact.  The carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, with coordinates supplied by the codon window and labels supplied by the curated standard-code table.  The distinguished boundary row is the finite set
$$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the independent punctured-cube check returns the same thirteen codons.  The finite face data of this row have $f$-vector

The four two-dimensional faces and sixteen one-dimensional incidences are the local tile evidence; the singleton and three-point label classes are read back only after the external code table supplies labels.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The row was compared against finite null draws.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ samples, there was one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  There were twenty-five samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling log records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries under seed $42$, so the quoted probabilities are tied to a concrete finite-row sampling trace.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, $N=2000$ samples gave exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, observed $M$-size $20$, $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These numbers support the local distinction between the observed code-table row and nearby randomized rows, while still leaving the biological interpretation bounded by the code-read contact.

These recovery statistics say that the local row is operationally useful for code-label prediction inside the finite coordinate-and-label experiment.  They do not say that the coordinate pattern realizes translation, folding, admissible chemistry, biological function, or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  It does not observe ribosomal translation as a mechanism, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or universality.  Therefore the correct conclusion is local and finite: window-six codon coordinates expose a punctured-cube boundary row in the standard-code label table.
