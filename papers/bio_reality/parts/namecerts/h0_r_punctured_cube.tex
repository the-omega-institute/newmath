\subsection{A Punctured-Cube Boundary Tile in the Standard-Code Readback}\label{subsec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record concerns the claim h0.R.punctured.cube at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  The reality contact is therefore a label table contact: it supports codon-label readback and local split checks, but it is not a contact for translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The finite row singled out by the computation is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons returned in the same finite support.  The punctured-cube check reports the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not being treated merely as a list of exceptional labels; it is being treated as a finite subcomplex of the codon cube with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model readings.}
The spectral run separates the observed local row from a generic label-set description.  It reports $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic residual is numerically zero at the displayed precision, with both the cubic-polynomial check and the radial-polynomial check returning $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$.  Thus the finite row has a stable spectral readback at the stated tolerance, while the comparison set $M$ is recorded by size rather than by a codon list in the supplied data.  The observed $M$ size is $20$.

The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled tables, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It found $0$ exact shape matches, $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size, and therefore reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support a local finite-row anomaly relative to the tested null models.  They do not, by themselves, identify a causal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Module decomposition and leave-one tests.}
The module-classification check assigns all thirteen codons in $R$ and reports no extras, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are

The classification summary gives $R$ size $13$ and assigned count $13$, confirming that the module partition covers exactly the punctured-cube row.

The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Against a baseline hit rate of $0.19821739130434785$, it reports hit rate in $M-T$ equal to $0.9365079365079365$, hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary equal to $0.9841269841269841$, and hit rate above baseline equal to $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the five-family summary gives hit-rate-above-baseline values $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  The leave-one-codon-out run reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.}
The local conclusion is a finite code-read statement: window-six coordinates expose a punctured-cube boundary tile whose thirteen-codon support, face vector, spectral readings, null-model frequencies, module partition, and leave-one recovery behavior are all finite-row observations.  The curated standard-code table supplies the external labels needed to read this row as a codon-label pattern.  No stronger biological claim follows from this contact.  In particular, the row does not show that translation is realized by the geometry, that an amino-acid sequence folds, that a physical state is admissible, that a function is performed, that a universal mechanism has been found, or that DNA obeys this cube geometry as a biological necessity.  Any such promotion would require a separate contact at the corresponding layer.  With the supplied data, the correct status is therefore: a local punctured-cube code-read boundary is supported; translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, and universality remain outside the claim.
