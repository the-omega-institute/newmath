\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite coordinate display for the standard-code codon table.  The finite row selected by the local tile probe is the thirteen-codon set $R$.  The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons returned by the cardinality run.  Thus the object being discussed is not an inferred global law over all $64$ codons; it is a concrete finite row inside the codon-cube display, with singleton-corner and three-point label-class distinctions read through the curated standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube certificate.}
The punctured-cube probe verifies that the same row $R$ equals the punctured-cube row returned by the computation.  Its cell-count certificate is the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local tile has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the chosen finite representation.  This is the chapter's central finite claim: the code-read contact exposes a local boundary-tile shape in $Q_6$, not a biological necessity statement about DNA, translation, folding, or function.

The role of these numbers is diagnostic: they make the local tile reproducible as finite coordinate data.  They do not supply a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Null-model pressure.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled comparators with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those samples, the exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the high-overlap event with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  Its observed module size was $20$, its exact-match count was $0$, and its lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the claim that the row is a non-generic local code-read pattern under the tested finite nulls.  They still do not upgrade the claim beyond the code layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate in $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, five families were evaluated: alternative yeast had hit rate above baseline $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had hit rate above baseline $1.0$.

The leave-one-codon-out row gives no anchors, five boundary codons, eight predictable codons, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the finite tile is stable under the stated deletion probes: the row can be recovered at the code-read level from local coordinate and boundary information.

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\

The module report records $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and assigned count $13$.  This gives a finite internal decomposition of the punctured-cube row into biologically named label modules, but the names are read through the curated standard-code bridge.  They are not, by themselves, a translation experiment.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The sole reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, interpreted as codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop label readback.  It can test code-read claims and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, folded protein structure, or universality.  Therefore the local conclusion is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a reproducible punctured-cube boundary tile whose row is $R$, whose face vector is $(13,16,4)$, whose spectrum has the listed numerical values, and whose deletion probes recover the code-read pattern at high rates.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universal law follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that evidence.
