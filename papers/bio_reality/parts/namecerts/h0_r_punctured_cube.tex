\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback scope.}
This record concerns a finite window-six codon carrier, written here as the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, together with a curated standard-code label table used only as a code-read contact.  The carrier is not treated as a mechanism for translation and is not treated as a structural or functional model of proteins.  Its role is narrower: it supplies a coordinate display in which a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class can be inspected against the externally curated codon-to-label table.  The finite row selected by the probe is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the independent punctured-cube check returns the same thirteen codons.  The face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed row is a finite boundary object rather than a verbal pattern imposed after the fact.  The contact named by this section is the standard-code label table: it supplies amino-acid or stop labels for codons, but it does not itself supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a proof that biological DNA must obey this coordinate geometry.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube evidence.}
The punctured-cube statement is the exact equality between the selected row and the displayed thirteen-codon set.  The equality is not merely a size claim: the same run reports both the explicit list and the boundary counts $(13,16,4)$.  The companion cardinality row separately reports $R\_cardinality\_equals\_13=13$ and the expected codon list
$$
\begin{aligned}
&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite datum has two separable checks: one check fixes the membership of $R$, while the other checks the punctured-cube face census.  This is important for the biological reading.  A code label table can confirm that these codons carry standard-code labels, but the geometry still remains a local code-read geometry.  In particular, the puncture is not promoted into a rule of molecular biology, and the face count is not promoted into a claim about folding, stability, fitness, or mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance and amino-acid-class nulls.}
The shape-significance experiment used $N=2000$ sampled trials with seed $42$.  It reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These null rows support the claim that the local finite shape is not a common outcome under the sampled controls.  They still remain code-layer evidence.  A low finite-row probability is not a translation experiment, not a structure experiment, and not a function assay.
