\subsection{Punctured-cube boundary in the standard-code readback}\label{sec:bior-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The finite object in this record is the six-coordinate codon cube with a curated standard-code label table attached only as a readback contact.  The distinguished row set $R$ consists of thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen displayed rows.  The punctured-cube certificate reports the face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed local tile is not merely a list of exceptional labels: it has a finite cubical incidence profile, with thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four local two-faces under the chosen six-bit codon display.  This statement is intentionally confined to the code-read layer.  The contact says that a curated standard genetic-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label; it does not itself observe ribosomal translation, folded molecular structure, energetic admissibility, cellular function, a causal mechanism, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The row geometry also carries a numerical spectral certificate.  The measured quantities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, recorded again as the cubic-polynomial residual, so the fitted spectral relation is satisfied to numerical tolerance at the finite row level.  In the shape randomization test with seed $42$, $N=2000$ samples were evaluated after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and there were twenty-five samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  Thus the punctured-cube pattern is rare under that finite null, but rarity is not promoted into biological necessity.  It is evidence for a local coordinate regularity in the code table, not evidence that nature must implement this coordinate system.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null keeps amino-acid class information fixed while testing the shape.  For the same sample count $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed companion set has size $|M|=20$.  The exact-shape count is zero, so the empirical exact-shape probability is $0.0$ at the sampled resolution.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is seven, giving $p_{\mathrm{lower}}=0.0035$.  This is the sharper finite-row statement: even after preserving the amino-acid class partition, the observed local arrangement is not a typical sampled shape.  The result remains a code-layer result because the preserved classes and the measured shape are both read through codon labels, not through protein structure or organismal performance.

\paragraph{Leave-one-out behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary rows was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, five groups were recorded: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  The leave-one-codon-out probe reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high-coverage closure count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers say that the local boundary and closure structure recovers the finite readback rows unusually well under the chosen probes.  They do not say that missing codons are biologically reconstructed by the coordinate system, only that the tabulated labels are organized so that the finite probes recover them.

\paragraph{Module resolution.}
The thirteen rows split into six named modules with no residue left over.  The module assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness audit records $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has either predictable rows or boundary rows, so the module view and the punctured-cube view agree on the finite support of the claim.  The biological interpretation is therefore narrow: the standard-code table supplies a reliable label readback for this local codon tile, and the finite probes find a structured boundary inside that readback.  The same data do not license claims about translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such stronger statement would require a separate observation at the corresponding biological layer.
