\subsection{The punctured codon cube as a code-layer record}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The record is a window-six description of the standard-code codon table.  Each codon is read as a six-bit coordinate in the codon cube $Q_6$, with the four nucleotide symbols supplying two binary coordinates per codon position.  The distinguished set $R$ is the finite row selected by the local label split.  In the checked finite run, the row is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the same run gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|&=13,\\
f(R)&=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not an informal cluster of codon names: it is a finite subcomplex with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four square faces.  The intended biological reading is deliberately narrower than a mechanism: the contact is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels, and the coordinate construction only tests whether a local boundary pattern is visible at that code-read layer.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube content.}
The phrase ``punctured cube'' means that the selected row behaves as a cube-like local tile with a missing or exposed part, rather than as an arbitrary thirteen-codon subset of $Q_6$.  The finite equality above is the operative claim.  The row includes the stop-related codons $\mathrm{UAA}$, $\mathrm{UAG}$, and $\mathrm{UGA}$; the arginine-side pair $\mathrm{AGA}$ and $\mathrm{AGG}$; the isoleucine--methionine boundary codon $\mathrm{AUA}$; the four $\mathrm{CUN}$ codons $\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}$; and the two $\mathrm{SSN}$-side entries $\mathrm{UCA}$ and $\mathrm{UUA}$, together with $\mathrm{AAA}$.  These codons are not promoted to higher biological structure by being named in the same finite row.  Their joint status is a local code-label pattern inside the six-bit display.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The same finite packet carries a spectral readout.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated cubic or radial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the numerical identity is satisfied to the tolerance of the run.  This number is useful as a reproducible finite invariant of the coordinate calculation.  It is not evidence that the molecule, ribosome, folding landscape, or cellular context is governed by that invariant.  The spectral data only strengthen the internal description of the displayed codon row.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
A coordinate-only null comparison was run with $N=2000$ sampled alternatives and seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those alternatives, the exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was met by $25$ samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null preserving amino-acid class information also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It produced no exact-shape match, with observed $M$-size $20$ and lower-tail count $7$ at or below that size:
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite comparisons support the statement that the local row is not a routine outcome of the chosen null models.  They do not turn the row into a global law of genetic coding.

The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  In the leave-one-codon-out run, the anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the predictable count was $8$, the high closure-coverage count was $8$, and the recovery rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{recovery}}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Family-level leave-one-code-out behavior is also finite and explicit: the five families were alternative yeast, bacterial or plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate.  Their above-baseline hit rates were $0.8333333333333334$, $1.0$, $1.0$, $1.0$, and $1.0$, respectively.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned to named modules.  The finite module decomposition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The accounting record has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite classification.  This is the strongest local form of the claim: within the six-bit codon display and the curated label table, the selected boundary row is exactly covered by the module split.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table, understood as a codon-to-label table.  It supports code-layer readback and local label-split checks.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is not a folded protein, and a local codon tile is not a statement about all organisms or all code variants.  Any claim about translation, structure, physics, function, or universal mechanism would require a separate contact with the relevant phenomenon.  Without that further contact, the present chapter asserts only the finite punctured-cube row, its numerical invariants, its null-model rarity, and its code-table readback boundary.
