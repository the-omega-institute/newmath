\subsection{The punctured-cube row in the standard-code readback}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The finite row named \texttt{h0.R.punctured.cube} is a code-layer record over the six-bit codon display.  Its carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC row does not assert that the cube geometry is a biological mechanism.  It records that one finite boundary class $R$ appears as a local punctured-cube tile when the standard-code labels are read back through the six-coordinate display.

The verified row is the following set of thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row-count check gives $|R|=13$, and the same thirteen codons are returned by the cardinality record and by the punctured-cube record.  The finite cell count attached to this local tile is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed object has thirteen vertices, sixteen local one-faces, and four local two-faces in the finite coordinate model.  This is the precise sense in which the row is called a punctured cube: the name refers to the finite coordinate incidence pattern, not to a claim about molecular necessity.

The null shape experiment used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only one exact shape match was observed, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}

These finite-row numbers support a code-layer shape signal in the curated table.  They do not by themselves identify translation dynamics, structural stability, physical admissibility, protein function, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  That contact can test codon-label readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the allowed conclusion is deliberately narrow: in the standard-code readback, the thirteen-codon row $R$ forms a finite punctured-cube boundary tile with $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral and null-model support, complete module assignment, and strong finite recovery behavior in the reported leave-one-out tests.

No stronger biological conclusion follows from this row.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Any future promotion across those boundaries would require a separate contact that directly observes the higher layer.
