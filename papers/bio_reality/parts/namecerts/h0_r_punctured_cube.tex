\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ equipped with the standard-code label table as its readback contact.  The row under study is the distinguished boundary set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality audit gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons in the punctured-cube row.  In the local cubical incidence calculation the face data match
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the object is not merely a label list: it is a finite cubical boundary tile with thirteen displayed vertices, sixteen local one-faces, and four local two-faces.  The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact supports codon-label readback and the local split check; it is not a contact for translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
and the high-Jaccard probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row separately used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It found observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, amino-acid-class exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These finite rows make the local tile nontrivial relative to the sampled nulls, while also fixing the scope: statistical separation of a code-layer shape is not evidence for protein folding, physical admissibility, function, or universal mechanism.
