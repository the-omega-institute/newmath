\subsection{The punctured-cube codon boundary row}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate carrier.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table.  The contact used here is only a code-label contact: it assigns each codon a standard amino-acid or stop label, and it does not itself provide translation dynamics, folding data, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Within this code-layer contact, the row $R$ is the finite set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality row gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons appearing in the cardinality check and in the punctured-cube check.  The finite cell count attached to this row is the face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the displayed object is not merely a list of labels: it is a local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the induced six-bit codon geometry.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube readback.}
The row is read as a punctured local cube because the thirteen displayed codons close into a local tile while leaving a boundary-sensitive gap pattern rather than a full unconstrained cube.  The singleton and class distinctions are visible at the label layer: $AAA$ is the singleton lysine-side corner; $AGA$ and $AGG$ form the arginine-side pair; $AUA$ sits in the isoleucine--methionine module; the four codons $CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU$ form the $CUN$ module; $UAA,UAG,UGA$ form the stop--tryptophan module; and $UCA,UUA$ form the $SSN$ side module recorded by the finite module classifier.  The classifier assigns all thirteen members of $R$ and reports no missing, extra, or unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{size}}&=13, & assigned&=13,\\
missing&=0, & extra&=0, & unassigned&=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row has both a coordinate description and a label-class readback, but those two facts remain code-layer facts.

\paragraph{Null-model pressure.}
The shape is not treated as an arbitrary visual coincidence.  Against the reported shape null with $N=2000$, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, the run records $55377$ duplicate retries, $1$ exact shape match, and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$.  The corresponding probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a second finite control.  In that run $N=2000$, seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the row is a structured finite coordinate event under the stated probes.  They do not promote the row to a law of DNA, a translation machine, a folding principle, or a functional explanation.

\paragraph{Boundary statement.}
The admissible conclusion is narrow.  The six-bit codon coordinates expose a local punctured-cube boundary tile whose row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\{AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$, $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, the stated spectral values, the stated null-model probabilities, and the stated leave-one recovery statistics.  The curated standard-code table supplies the reality contact needed for codon-label readback.  It supplies no contact for ribosomal translation realization, no folded protein structure, no physical admissibility criterion, no biological function, no universal mechanism, and no claim that biology must obey this geometry.  Any statement crossing one of those boundaries requires a separate higher-layer contact.
