\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on a six-bit codon display. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read through the four-letter codon alphabet after fixing a binary display for the three positions. The distinguished finite row is the set $R$ selected by the local tile boundary computation. In the verified row, $R$ contains exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\\
&\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same listed codons. The punctured-cube check gives the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object is not merely a thirteen-row selection; it is a finite boundary tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the selected codon-window geometry.

\paragraph{Code-read contact.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table. Its observed fact is that each codon is assigned to an amino-acid label or a stop label. This contact is strong enough to read labels on the selected codons and to test local label splits, but it is not a translation experiment, a protein-structure measurement, a physical-admissibility assay, or a function measurement. Thus the row has a precise layer: it is a code-read boundary record. The singleton and three-point label corners are interpreted only as label-table phenomena inside $Q_6$ after the curated table has supplied labels.

\paragraph{Shape rarity.}
Against the sampled null family for the local shape, the run used $N=2000$ distinct null samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries under seed $42$. The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $25$ sampled rows had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ to the observed shape. The exact-shape and high-Jaccard values are finite-row evidence that the observed boundary tile is uncommon under this null. They do not by themselves identify a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second null preserves amino-acid class information. In that null, the observed comparison used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. The observed $M$ size is $20$, no exact shape match occurred, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. The reported values are

\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\

This supports the statement that the code-read boundary is not an easy artifact of preserving amino-acid class alone. The data supplied here give the size of $M$ but not an explicit codon list for $M$; accordingly, this paragraph uses the verified scalar row and does not reconstruct an absent list.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The cannot-claim boundary is part of the mathematical object. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. The curated standard-code table supplies label contact only; crossing from code labels to translation realization, structural order, admissible physics, function, or a universal mechanism would require a separate contact that is not present in this row.
