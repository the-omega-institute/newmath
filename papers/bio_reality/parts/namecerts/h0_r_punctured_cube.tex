\subsection{Punctured-cube boundary row in the standard-code window}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with three nucleotide positions and a curated standard-code label table attached only as an external code-read contact.  The row under audit is the finite set $R$ of codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row count is exactly $|R|=13$.  In the verified cubical readback, the same thirteen codons are not merely a label-selected list: they form the punctured-cube row whose face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed BEDC-side distinction is a local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces inside the codon cube.  The biological readback is deliberately narrower: the curated standard-code table assigns these codons to amino-acid or stop labels, and that assignment is used only to test code-layer label splits.

\paragraph{Coordinate and spectral row evidence.}
The coordinate evidence says that the punctured-cube row survives two independent finite probes.  First, the exact row identity is verified by the list above and by the cardinality certificate $13$.  Second, the associated spectral row has numerical values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic, or radial, polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the finite computation is being used at numerical tolerance scale rather than as an exact symbolic identity over real algebraic numbers.  The role of these numbers is to characterize the observed row geometry and its local relation to the surrounding code-window construction.  They do not supply a translation mechanism, a folding model, a physical admissibility criterion, a function assay, or a universality theorem.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
A direct shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$ produced $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, including $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that null, the exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap test with Jaccard threshold $0.9$ produced $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reported observed $M$-size $20$, zero exact matches, and seven samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size.  Its reported finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the claim that the local tile is a non-generic code-window feature under the sampled nulls.  They do not convert the local feature into a global biological law, because the nulls are tests over finite codon-label rearrangements and not over cellular translation, molecular structure, thermodynamic admissibility, or organism-level function.

\paragraph{Readback and leave-one-out behavior.}
The code-read contact becomes operational in the leave-one probes.  In the leave-one-code-out row, $23$ tables and $63$ total targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  In the leave-one-codon-out row, the anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the high-closure-coverage count was $8$, the predictable count was $8$, and the recovery rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-resolved leave-one-code-out row reports five families.  The family hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  These numbers say that the boundary tile is useful for code-layer recovery and comparison across curated code-table families.  They still remain code-read quantities, because the experiment reads labels and local boundary membership rather than measuring translation kinetics or protein consequences.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned to six finite modules.  The module classification is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  This is the strongest finite-row closure statement available here: every codon in the punctured-cube row has a module-level explanation as predictable or boundary data.  It is not a statement that those modules are evolutionary necessities, biochemical mechanisms, or sufficient causes of phenotype.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The record is ready at the code-read layer.  The finite evidence supports the statement that a window-six codon coordinate exposes a local punctured-cube boundary tile in the curated standard-code readback, with $|R|=13$, $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, significant sampled null behavior, stable leave-one recovery, and a complete module partition of the row.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global law follows from this chapter.  Any such promotion requires a separate reality contact whose observed fact is about that higher layer rather than about codon-label readback.
