\subsection{A Punctured-Cube Row in the Standard-Code Codon Table}
\label{sec:biorality-punctured-codon-cube-row}

\paragraph{Finite row.}
This row records a local six-bit coordinate pattern in the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of codons with three nucleotide positions and binary refinements of the four bases.  The observed distinguished row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit gives $|R|=13$ and identifies exactly the same thirteen labels in the cardinality check and in the punctured-cube check.  In the cubical incidence count for this row, the recorded face data are
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of codons: it carries a small cell profile, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four local two-dimensional faces.  The term ``punctured cube'' is used here only for that finite incidence pattern.  It does not assert that the biological code is generated by this geometry, nor that the geometry is a biochemical law.

\paragraph{Code-layer contact.}
The only reality contact used in this row is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  This contact resolves codon-label readback and local label splits inside the codon display.  It is not an observation of translation dynamics, ribosomal realization, molecular structure, energetic admissibility, cellular function, or evolutionary universality.  The row therefore lives at the code-read layer.  Any statement about folding, physical viability, mechanism, or universal biological necessity would require an additional contact of the corresponding kind, and no such contact is present in this finite row.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinate.}
The same finite row has a recorded spectral summary.  The values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is also recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual.  At the numerical precision of the row audit, this is a near-zero residual rather than an independent biological measurement.  Its role is to mark that the finite coordinate computation is internally stable for the displayed row.  The spectral radius entry agrees with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ in the recorded tolerance check.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under sampled alternatives.}
The row was also compared against finite null samples.  In the broad shape-significance run with $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables, one exact shape match was observed.  The resulting exact-shape probability estimate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=1/2000=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reported $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler attempted $57377$ draws and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries before obtaining the $2000$ distinct samples, so the probability estimates should be read as finite sampled-row evidence, not as closed-form distribution theory.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null run, again with $N=2000$, the observed module size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrower claim that this code-layer row is uncommon under the sampled alternatives tested.  They do not by themselves identify a causal origin for the standard code.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out check evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the finite row recorded a hit rate above baseline of $0.9841269841269841$, a hit rate inside $M-T$ of $0.9365079365079365$, and a hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary of $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  This is a robustness pattern across curated code-table variants.  It is still a label-table phenomenon: it says that the finite coordinate row remains predictive for held-out code labels in this tested family of tables, not that the row realizes translation or protein function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon behavior.}
The leave-one-codon-out row has $13$ assigned codons and no missing, extra, or unassigned codons in its module classification.  The recovery rate is $1.0$, with $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and high closure coverage for $8$ codons.  The complete module partition recorded for the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status, and the assigned count equals the row size $13$.  This gives a finite closure check for the row as a row: the modules account for all and only the codons in $R$.  The classification does not promote the row beyond codon-label readback.  In particular, the presence of the $CUN$ block, the $UUR/SSN$-style pair inside the row, or the stop/tryptophan boundary block is not evidence of folded structure, physical admissibility, organism-level mechanism, or universal biological law unless a separate experiment supplies those observations.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The row supports the following limited statement.  In a six-bit codon-coordinate display of the curated standard code, the thirteen codons $R$ form a punctured-cube-shaped local tile with face vector $(13,16,4)$, stable spectral coordinates, rare sampled shape behavior, and complete module accounting in the leave-one-codon audit.  The row is therefore a finite code-layer witness for local boundary structure in the standard-code label table.  The same finite evidence also fixes the boundary of interpretation: codon labels are not proteins, label recovery is not folding, a local tile is not a translation apparatus, and sampled rarity is not a universal mechanism.
