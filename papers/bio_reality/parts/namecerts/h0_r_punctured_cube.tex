\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The window-six codon display treats the standard-code table as a finite code-read contact rather than as a mechanism of translation.  In this record the carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, and the distinguished row $R$ is the following thirteen-codon subset:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality audit gives $|R|=13$, and the independent row listing agrees exactly with the same thirteen codons.  The cubical face count attached to this row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not merely a list of labels: it is a finite punctured-cube shape with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-faces, and four two-faces inside the codon-coordinate display.  The word punctured is used here only for this finite coordinate fact.  It does not assert a physical hole, a biochemical obstruction, or a universal rule of genetic-code formation.

\paragraph{Label contact.}
The only external contact used by this record is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  That contact has resolution at codon-label readback.  It can test whether the displayed row and its local tile boundary match the standard-code label table, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, or biological function.  Thus the contact supports code-layer readback and local split checks only.  The label corner and the three-point label class are read from the curated table as external reality contacts; they are not derived from the window-six geometry alone.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}

From these data one may claim a finite code-read pattern: a thirteen-codon subset of the six-bit codon cube, with $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, a measured spectrum, statistically rare shape under the reported nulls, and complete module classification against the curated standard-code label contact. Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains label readback.
