\subsection{Punctured Cube Row for the Standard-Code Readback}\label{sec:bior-h0-r-punctured-cube}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and row.} The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the coordinate display for the $64$ codons. The row named $R$ is not introduced as a biological law. It is a finite code-read subset selected inside this cube and then compared with the curated standard-code label table. In the finite run, the row has exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independently recorded cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the expected-codon list is the same thirteen-element set. The punctured-cube check records the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the code-read row is being treated as a local tile with thirteen displayed vertices, sixteen displayed one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces. This is the precise finite content of the punctured-cube claim: a local geometric row exists in the six-bit codon display, and its row membership is exactly the thirteen codons listed above.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape evidence.} The same finite object is accompanied by a spectral certificate. The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is also the recorded value of the cubic polynomial test, and the spectral-radius entry is $0.6752479308830213$. These numbers support the claim that the finite row is not merely a named list, but participates in a reproducible coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation package. The finite shape test used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$. It made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, found $1$ exact shape match, and found $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$. Thus the empirical exact-shape probability was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the high-Jaccard probability was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$. The corresponding finite-row probabilities were $p=0.0$ for exact class-preserving shape recovery and $p=0.0035$ for the lower-tail event. These figures give a row-level separation between the observed code-read geometry and the sampled null rows, while still remaining finite-sample evidence rather than a theorem about biology.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.} The thirteen codons in $R$ are exactly assigned to six local modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module-classification record has $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. Thus the module split is complete at the finite-row level. The leave-one-codon-out probe records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The local reading is therefore that every displayed module lies either in the predictable part of the row or on the boundary supplied by the finite code-read contact.

\paragraph{Code-read tests.} The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline entries were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial or plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate codes, over $5$ recorded families. These numbers are evidence that the local row is informative for code-label readback under the supplied family partition, not evidence that the geometry causes translation or supplies a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.} The only stated reality contact is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact has codon-label resolution. It can test code-read behavior and a local codon-tile label split. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or functional realization. Consequently the punctured-cube row is a local code-read boundary tile: it supports the statement that the listed thirteen codons form a reproducible finite pattern in the six-bit codon display and that this pattern has a measurable relation to curated code labels. It does not support the stronger claims that DNA obeys this geometry as biological necessity, that the tile is a global biological law, that amino-acid label readback is folded protein structure, or that any function follows from the row.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim statement.} No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from the punctured-cube row unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information. The row remains at the $code\_read$ layer. Its positive content is finite and local: the set $R$ has thirteen specified codons, has face-vector $(13,16,4)$, satisfies the recorded spectral checks, is rare under the sampled shape nulls, is completely partitioned into six row modules, and performs strongly in the leave-one-code and leave-one-codon tests. Its negative content is equally part of the record: the curated table is a bridge for labels, not a mechanism, not a folding experiment, not a physical-admissibility assay, and not a function contact.
