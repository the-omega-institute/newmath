\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite coordinate display for standard-code labels.  The observed row is the subset $R$ of codons

\begin{aligned}

$$

Thus the chapter records a concrete finite shape: thirteen codon vertices, sixteen local one-dimensional incidences, and four local faces inside the window-six display.  This is not a statement about a physical cube in molecular space.  It is a code-read coordinate statement over the codon table contact.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, treated as a bridge from codon strings to amino-acid or stop labels.  Its observed fact is that a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label.  Its resolution is codon label readback.  The contact can test code-read claims and local codon-tile label splits, but it cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded-protein form, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Consequently the row $R$ is allowed to support a local label-boundary claim and is not allowed to promote itself into a higher biological law.
