\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This BioReality row treats the standard-code codon table as a finite label readback over a window-six carrier.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  The finite row under audit is not the whole code table.  It is the boundary class $R$ singled out by the local tile computation:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independently recorded cardinality row gives $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube row records the same thirteen codons with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object certified here is a finite local boundary tile: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the induced codon-coordinate complex.  The word \emph{punctured} is used only for this finite combinatorial shape; it is not a biological mechanism claim.

\paragraph{Label contact.}
The only external contact used by this row is the curated standard genetic-code bridge.  It supplies codon-to-label readback and therefore permits statements at the code-read layer: for example, whether a codon in $R$ carries a stop label, an amino-acid label, or participates in a local label split.  The contact does not observe translation dynamics, ribosome realization, tRNA charging, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or universality.  Consequently the certified statement is deliberately narrow: window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure inside the standard-code label table, while the labels themselves remain external reality contacts rather than consequences of the coordinate geometry.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the recorded randomization basis for the $M$-shape test, the run used $N=2000$ samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exact shape agreement occurred once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The relaxed high-overlap test counted $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, the observed $M$ size was $20$, exact shape matches were $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures are evidence that the local coordinate pattern is non-generic under the finite nulls that were actually run.  They are not evidence for a universal biological necessity, because the tests are bounded by their sampling design, their codon-label contact, and their finite-row carrier.
