\subsection{Punctured cube record for the standard code}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, used only as a finite display for codon labels.  The observed row singled out by the code-layer probe is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the same thirteen codons appear in the punctured-cube run.  The finite face count reported for this row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the record is not merely a list of codons: it carries a local cubical profile with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four square faces inside the window-six display.  This is a code-read fact about the curated standard-code table.  It is not a statement that DNA or translation is governed by this geometry as a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Label contact and scope.}
The reality contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact has exactly the resolution needed for codon-label readback and local label-split checks.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folding, chemical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or organism-wide necessity.  Consequently, the punctured-cube row is allowed to support only code-layer claims: the standard-code labels may be read against $Q_6$, and the local tile may be compared with the row $R$.  Any assertion about protein realization, folded structure, physical viability, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate observation with that resolution.

\paragraph{Spectral signature.}
The same finite packet includes a spectral check.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic polynomial test.  At the stated numerical tolerance this is a near-zero residual, so the spectral row is internally consistent with the finite construction.  This numerical agreement is evidence for a reproducible local signature of the codon display, not for a biochemical process outside the table contact.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ null samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  Thus the local shape is rare in the sampled comparison class.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$ found $0$ exact matches, so $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$ at the reported sampling resolution.  In that same run the observed $M$-size was $20$, and only $7$ null rows lay in the lower tail at or below that size, giving $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These numbers support the claim that the observed codon-row geometry is not a frequent artifact of the tested label-preserving reshufflings.

\paragraph{Leave-one behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level split covered $5$ families: alternative yeast had rate $0.8333333333333334$, and bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each had rate $1.0$.  The leave-one-codon-out run reported $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, $0$ anchor cases, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These checks say that the finite local rule has recovery power within the code-label task; they do not turn the rule into a translation model.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The module-classification row assigns all thirteen codons in $R$ without extras or omissions.  The complete split is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The summary row gives $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has either predictable or boundary support in the reported classification.  This makes the punctured-cube record a finite, checkable code-layer packet: the row, face vector, spectral values, null statistics, leave-one behavior, and module partition agree on the same thirteen-codon object, while the biological interpretation remains deliberately limited to the codon-label table.
