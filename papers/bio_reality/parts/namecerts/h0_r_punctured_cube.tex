\paragraph{Code-label contact.}
The reality contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. This contact has exactly the resolution needed for codon-label readback and local split checks. It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, downstream function, or a universal biological rule. The biological statement supported by the row is therefore narrow: the standard table contains a finite label-bearing region whose boundary behavior can be read in the codon-coordinate display. The table supplies labels; the coordinate calculation supplies the local tile geometry; neither one by itself converts the pattern into a law of molecular implementation.
