\subsection{The punctured codon cube as a local code-read tile}
\label{sec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The record concerns the claim \texttt{h0.R.punctured.cube}.  The carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read only as a finite coordinate object whose vertices are standard-code codons.  In the verified row, the distinguished boundary set $R$ is exactly the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality row independently reports $|R|=13$ with the same codon list.  The punctured-cube row reports the face-vector match
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite observation is not merely that thirteen codons were selected, but that those thirteen selected codons carry the incidence profile of a local punctured cube with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ tile faces.  This is the whole geometric content used here: a finite local tile in codon-coordinate space, not a law about molecular realization.

\paragraph{Label contact and scope.}
The reality contact is the curated standard-code table, used only as a bridge from codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Its observed fact is that standard-code codons are assigned curated labels, and its resolution is codon label readback.  That contact can test code-read structure and a local codon-tile label split; it cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded structure, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Thus the local claim is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a boundary-tile pattern whose labels can be read back through the standard code, while the labels themselves remain external reality contacts rather than consequences of the geometry.

\paragraph{Shape significance against shuffled finite rows.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ distinct samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported row is already sensitive to the finite sample space and its collisions.  Exactly one sampled row matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count was $25$ rows at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the statement that the punctured-cube tile is a sharp finite pattern under the tested null distribution.  They do not say that the pattern is forced by chemistry, translation, or selection; the null model only probes the finite code-read shape.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate after adding the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus, at the code-read layer, the local tile and its boundary recover nearly all tested withheld labels across the evaluated table rows.  This is evidence for a strong finite readback regularity.  It is not evidence that the codon window performs translation or that the resulting amino-acid label sequence has a folded structure.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure codons, and $8$ predictable codons, with recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{recover}}=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row marks the recovery rate as above threshold at $1.0$.  The absence of anchors is important: the observed recovery is not presented as direct anchoring in an external mechanism, but as recovery through the local code-read closure and boundary classification.  The finite result is therefore a boundary-sensitive readback statement, not a causal statement about biological implementation.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready verdict is a finite code-read verdict.  The row supports the statement that the standard-code label table, when pulled back to six-bit codon coordinates, contains a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary tile with face vector $(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral fingerprint, significant shape statistics under the sampled null rows, and high leave-one-out readback performance.  The row does not support any promotion beyond that layer.  No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity claim follows from the tile unless a separate reality contact supplies the missing layer.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation, and a code-layer observation is not a structure-layer or function-layer observation.
