\paragraph{Code-layer interpretation.} The biological object contacted here is the standard-code codon table. That contact supplies codon-to-label readback: it says which codons receive amino-acid or stop labels in the curated table. It does not supply translation dynamics, ribosomal realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Thus the row is a code-read record. Its legitimate content is that a window-six coordinate presentation exposes a local tile boundary in the standard-code label table. The singleton-corner and three-point label-class distinctions are therefore local features of the code table, not global laws of molecular biology.

The sampling row also reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries under seed $42$, so the finite-row evidence should be read as a sampled-coordinate comparison with substantial duplicate rejection. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, giving $p=0.0035$ for that lower tail. These numbers support the statement that the observed local codon-tile geometry is atypical under the stated finite nulls; they do not by themselves promote the row to a mechanism of translation or a claim about folding.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.} The boundary condition is part of the result. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, not folded protein structure, not physical admissibility, not biological function, not mechanism, and not universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies structural, physical, functional, or mechanistic evidence. The present row is therefore ready as a BioReality record precisely because its finite evidence and its refusal boundary are both explicit.
