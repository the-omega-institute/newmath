\subsection{The punctured-cube codon boundary record}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-label layer.  The finite carrier is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$: each codon is displayed as a six-bit coordinate, and the observed labels are the curated amino-acid or stop assignments attached to those codons.  The row under inspection is the distinguished boundary set $R$.  The verified computation identifies $R$ with the following thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
   &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality row reports $|R|=13$ and gives the same codon list.  Thus the row is not merely a count but a named finite subset of the codon cube.  Its face data are also fixed: the local tile has $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, meaning thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the verified tile representation.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube interpretation.}
The geometric read is that $R$ behaves as a punctured local cube inside the six-bit codon coordinate display.  The term ``punctured'' is used here only for the finite coordinate pattern: it records the visible boundary shape of these thirteen codons and does not assert that biological translation is generated by this geometry.  The singleton and three-point label classes are inspected through the curated standard-code table, while the coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data are internal finite operations on the codon display.  The reality contact is therefore narrow: the table says which label each codon has; it does not say that the coordinate tile is a mechanism.

The radial polynomial check is numerically close to zero, with value $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same residual is reported for the cubic polynomial row.  The spectral-radius row repeats $0.6752479308830213$ as the tolerance-accepted value.  These numbers are useful because they attach a continuous-looking invariant to a finite codon subset, while keeping the claim tied to the enumerated codons above.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite null sample for shape matching, the experiment used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$.  Because the sampler enforced distinct samples, it recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape count was $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  Thus the punctured-cube pattern is rare in this finite sampling regime, both at exact equality and at high Jaccard overlap.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null used the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It found no exact shape matches, so the reported exact-shape probability is $0.0$, while $7$ samples lay in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size of $20$, giving lower-tail probability $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the boundary-aware rate reached $0.9841269841269841$.  The rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and adding the boundary again gave $0.9841269841269841$.  The family breakdown has $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate above baseline $0.8333333333333334$, and bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each have value $1.0$.  The leave-one-codon-out row is stricter at the codon level.  It reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These probes support the local boundary pattern as a code-read phenomenon: missing labels are often recovered by the finite neighborhood and boundary data, but that recovery is still a statement about labels in a table.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The thirteen codons of $R$ are completely assigned to six named modules.  The verified partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness row gives $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the module union, and no unassigned codons.  This is the strongest finite bookkeeping fact in the record: every codon listed in $R$ is accounted for exactly once by the module classification, and every module has either a predictable or boundary role.

\paragraph{Local scientific reading.}
The admissible conclusion is that the standard-code table contains a rare and recoverable local boundary-tile pattern when codons are displayed in the six-bit cube and read through curated labels.  The evidence combines exact enumeration, face counts, spectral residuals, null comparisons, leave-one-code tests, leave-one-codon tests, and complete module assignment.  The statement is deliberately local.  No claim about translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, generative mechanism, or universal biological law follows from this row.  Any such stronger assertion would require an additional contact that directly observes the higher layer.  In particular, amino-acid label readback is not a fold measurement, and a cross-organism wording about codon families or tRNA notation does not by itself supply structure, function, or mechanism.  The present record stops at code-layer label geometry: it says that the listed codons form a verified punctured-cube boundary pattern in the curated standard-code readback, and it refuses promotion beyond that layer.
