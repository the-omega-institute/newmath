\subsection{Punctured codon cube at the code-read layer}
\label{sec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The packet uses the six-coordinate codon cube as its finite display.  Its distinguished row is the set of standard-code codons at which the local window-six construction marks a boundary tile rather than an interior code tile.  The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons recovered in the same finite row.  The cubical incidence audit reports the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not only a list of labels: it is a punctured cubical tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces inside the codon-coordinate cube.  This is the local geometric content of the claim.

\paragraph{Code-label contact.}
The biological contact used here is deliberately narrow.  A curated standard genetic-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments for amino-acid and stop labels.  That contact lets the row above be read as a code-layer object: it can say which standard-code labels occur at the selected codons, and it can test whether the local tile split is compatible with the tabulated labels.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, molecular dynamics, folded conformation, organismal fitness, or biochemical function.  The result therefore remains a code-read statement about a finite codon table contact.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the reported finite null sample, the observed shape is rare.  In the run with seed $42$, $N=2000$ sampled alternatives required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact matches.  Its observed marked-region size was $20$, with $7$ lower-tail samples at or below that size, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the statement that the finite punctured-cube row is an unusual code-table pattern under the recorded null regimes.  They still remain statistical facts about codon-label rows, not evidence for higher biological layers.

\paragraph{Local exclusion rule.}
The chapter asserts a narrow positive claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary row in the curated standard-code table, with the finite incidence vector, spectral parameters, null-model rarity, leave-one stability, and module classification stated above.  It does not assert that the coordinate geometry causes translation, determines folding, proves physical admissibility, explains function, gives a mechanism, or states a universal biological law.  Any such promotion would require a separate contact that directly observes the higher layer in question.  Without that additional contact, amino-acid label readback remains code-layer readback, and the punctured cube remains a local finite pattern in the codon table.
