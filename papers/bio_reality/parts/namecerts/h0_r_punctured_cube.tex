\subsection{Punctured-cube boundary record for the standard code}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite codon row.}
The record concerns the code-read layer of the standard genetic-code table.  Its carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite coordinate display for codon labels.  In this display the distinguished boundary set $R$ is the thirteen-codon row
$\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.
The cardinality check gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13,\\
(f_0,f_1,f_2) &= (13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed row is not merely a list of exceptional codons: within the window-six coordinate system it has the face data of a punctured local cube, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces.  The record is finite and row-level throughout; it does not assert that the coordinate geometry is a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Label contact.}
The biological contact is the curated standard-code table, used only as a codon-to-label table.  It says that each codon in the display has an amino-acid or stop label in the standard code.  This is enough to test a local tile split and enough to say which codons occur in $R$, but it is not a contact with ribosomal translation dynamics, protein folding, energetic admissibility, organismal fitness, biochemical mechanism, or universality across all codes.  Any such stronger statement would require a separate observation of that layer.  The local claim is therefore: a window-six coordinate construction exposes a boundary-shaped codon subset, while the labels attached to that subset remain supplied by the curated code table.

\paragraph{Spectral row check.}
The same finite row carries a numerical spectral signature.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, matching the recorded radial-polynomial residual to numerical tolerance.  The spectral-radius entry is again $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers are evidence that the finite row is stable under the stated coordinate and relation calculations; they are not evidence for folding, function, or physical realization beyond code-label readback.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
The shape test against sampled alternatives used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It reported one exact shape match, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, with $25$ high-Jaccard matches.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also at $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  Hence the reported exact-shape probability was $0.0$, while the lower-tail probability was $0.0035$.  These comparisons support the claim that the punctured-cube row is not a routine outcome of the stated null samplers.  They do not identify a mechanism for why the biological code has this arrangement.

\paragraph{Prediction-style checks.}
Leave-one-code-out evaluation covered $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the reported rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast tables.  Leave-one-codon-out evaluation gave recovery rate $1.0$, threshold comparison $1.0$, predictable count $8$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, and anchor count $0$.  These values show that the local codon window has predictive organization inside the code-read experiment.  They do not turn the tile into a universal biological principle.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned by the module record.  The modules are
$\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$,
$\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$,
$\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$,
$\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$,
$\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and
$\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.
The classification reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  This gives a closed finite accounting of the boundary row at the label level.  The record therefore supports a precise local statement: the standard-code table contains a thirteen-codon subset whose six-bit window geometry is a punctured local cube and whose module partition is complete.  It does not support claims about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or cross-code universality unless an additional contact directly supplies those facts.
