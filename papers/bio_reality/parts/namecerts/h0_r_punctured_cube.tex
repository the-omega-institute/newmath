\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code table as a codon-to-label contact.  The row is not a claim that biology is governed by this cube.  It is a code-layer certificate: a local coordinate display is compared with the standard-code label table, and the resulting subset is kept at the level of label readback.  The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row records this set as a punctured-cube tile with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the displayed object is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it is a local tile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the six-bit codon coordinate system.  The readback contact is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact supports code-label comparison and nothing beyond it.

\paragraph{Shape significance under finite null rows.}
The shape row was tested against $N=2000$ sampled null objects with seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that finite population, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It reports observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers make the local shape non-generic relative to the two finite null procedures recorded here.  They do not establish a universal mechanism.  They say that, within the declared coordinate and null models, the standard-code labels occupy a rare local pattern.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The claim is therefore narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary tile whose codon list, face vector, spectrum, null-model rarity, leave-one recovery, and module decomposition are all row-supported.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, so the result is a statement about code-layer label readback.  No translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from this tile unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.  Amino-acid label sequences are not folded protein structures, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
