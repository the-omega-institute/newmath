\subsection{The punctured codon cube row}
\label{sec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The claim \texttt{h0.R.punctured.cube} records a finite code-read fact about the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite coordinate display for the $64$ RNA codons.  The distinguished row $R$ is not asserted to be a biochemical mechanism; it is the finite set of codons selected by the local tile computation and then read back against the curated standard-code label table.  In the verified row, the selected codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|&=13,\\
R&=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},
\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row therefore consists of one lysine-side codon $\mathrm{AAA}$, two arginine-side codons $\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}$, the isoleucine--methionine boundary codon $\mathrm{AUA}$, the full $\mathrm{CUN}$ leucine block, three stop--tryptophan boundary codons $\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}$, and two serine/leucine-side codons $\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}$.  The table contact used here is only the curated codon-to-label bridge: it supplies label readback for amino-acid and stop labels, not translation dynamics.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The finite row has the verified punctured-cube face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)&=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
where the entries count the selected vertices, selected one-dimensional adjacencies, and selected square faces in the local six-bit cube display.  The phrase ``punctured cube'' is used in this finite combinatorial sense: the row occupies a local cube-like tile with a missing-corner boundary pattern, not a continuous geometric object and not a physical compartment.  The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class.  These distinctions are internal to the code-read model until the curated table supplies the external label contact.

This spectral fact is a coordinate-level invariant of the finite probe.  It is evidence that the row is not merely a list of labels after the fact: the same selected row has a reproducible adjacency and spectrum inside the six-bit carrier.  It still remains a code-read observation.  The spectrum does not certify a ribosomal process, a folding order, a thermodynamic admissibility criterion, or any universal law of genetic-code evolution.

\paragraph{Null-model pressure.}
The shape was tested against finite randomized controls.  In the general shape-significance run with seed $42$, $N=2000$ samples were evaluated after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125,\\
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ shape\ matches}\}&=1,\\
\#\{J\geq 0.9\ \mathrm{matches}\}&=25.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model is sharper.  In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed companion mass had size $20$, no exact class-preserving shape match was observed, and only $7$ samples landed in the lower tail at or below the observed mass size:
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M|_{\mathrm{obs}}&=20,\\
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ class\ matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
\#\{|M|\leq |M|_{\mathrm{obs}}\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite-row evidence is not only that $R$ has thirteen codons, but that its punctured-cube shape and companion-size behavior are rare under the supplied coordinate-preserving randomizations.  The supplied data do not include the explicit codon list for $M$; the chapter therefore uses only the verified scalar facts about $M$.

The accounting check records $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons.  At family level the leave-one-code-out rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families, across $5$ families.  This supports the interpretation that the row is organized by finite boundary modules rather than by an arbitrary codon list.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of claim.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  The supported conclusion is therefore local and finite: window-six codon coordinates expose a punctured-cube row with thirteen codons, face vector $(13,16,4)$, a reproducible spectral signature, rare randomized-shape behavior, complete module accounting, and strong code-label leave-one-out behavior.  The contact cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity of the BEDC geometry in DNA.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any claim crossing from code readback to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate higher-layer reality contact not present in this finite row.
