\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record concerns a window-six codon carrier, written as the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with readback through a curated standard-code label table. The carrier distinction is deliberately narrow: it is a code-read distinction over codon labels, not a claim about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The finite row isolates a subset $R$ of codons whose coordinate placement forms a punctured-cube boundary tile inside the window-six display. The verified $R$ row has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is independently reported as the expected codon list for the cardinality claim, with $|R|=13$. The finite cell count attached to the punctured-cube witness is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed object is not merely a list of exceptional labels; it carries a local tile signature with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the induced coordinate structure.

\paragraph{Label readback.}
The reality contact is the curated standard genetic-code table. It supplies codon-to-label assignments at the code layer and permits the finite row to be read back as a standard-code label pattern. It does not supply a biochemical dynamics, a ribosomal mechanism, a folding map, a physical admissibility certificate, or a function assay. The distinction therefore has the following local status: the table can test whether the listed codons occupy the claimed code-read positions and label classes, while any promotion beyond code-read is blocked unless a separate contact is attached. In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.

\paragraph{Shape-null evidence.}
Against the sampled shape null, the run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space was heavily revisited by the sampler. The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-Jaccard threshold count was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row observation that the punctured-cube boundary tile is rare under the reported null, but they do not by themselves identify a biological cause. They are evidence for a code-layer coordinate pattern, not evidence for a universal genetic-code law.
