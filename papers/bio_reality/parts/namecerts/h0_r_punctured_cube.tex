\subsection{The punctured codon cube row}
\label{subsec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record works only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with codons read as coordinates over a four-letter codon alphabet and then displayed through the curated standard-code label table.  The row under study is the set $R$ of codons selected by the local tile boundary computation.  The finite readback is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independently counted row size is $|R|=13$, and the explicit row equals the punctured-cube row returned by experiment run $e19917012c60$.  Its recorded cubical face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite object is not being used as an informal visual analogy: the row contains thirteen vertices, sixteen local one-dimensional adjacencies, and four local square faces in the selected tile complex.

\paragraph{Local label geometry.}
The row has a module-level decomposition over the standard-code labels.  The complete module assignment covers every element of $R$ and leaves no unassigned codon: $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, $missing\_from\_modules=\varnothing$, $extra\_module\_codons=\varnothing$, and $unassigned\_codons=\varnothing$.  The recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
ar\_lys &= \{AAA\},\\
agr &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
ile\_met &= \{AUA\},\\
cun &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
stop\_trp &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\},\\
ssn &= \{UCA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is a finite local split with singleton label corners, paired label classes, a four-point class, and a three-point stop-or-tryptophan boundary class.  This is the content of the punctured-cube claim: the table-supported codon row has a concrete local cubical shape and a complete finite label partition.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The same finite row was measured through a derived spectral probe.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also reported as the cubic polynomial residual, so the numerical certificate is within floating tolerance for the stated spectral relation.  The observed ambient selected set has size $|M|=20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison, while the punctured row itself has size $|R|=13$.

\paragraph{Finite null comparisons.}
The shape is not treated as automatic biological law.  It was compared against finite null samples.  In the shape-significance run with seed $42$, the sample size was $N=2000$.  Distinct sample generation required $57377$ attempts with $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exact shape agreement occurred once, giving $p_{exact}=0.0005$, while high-overlap agreement at Jaccard at least $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null run with the same sample size and seed, exact shape agreement occurred $0$ times, and only $7$ samples lay in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{aa\ class,exact} &= 0.0,\\
p_{aa\ class,lower\ tail} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the statement that the finite row is atypical under the supplied nulls, not the stronger statement that codon biology is forced by this geometry.

\paragraph{Leave-one probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate, across $5$ recorded families.  The leave-one-codon-out probe reported $anchor\_count=0$, $boundary\_count=5$, $closure\_coverage\_high=8$, $predictable\_count=8$, and $recovery\_rate=1.0$.  These finite probes say that the row is stable as a local code-read boundary object under the supplied omissions.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, at codon-label readback resolution.  That contact says that codons are assigned amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the claim licensed by this row is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a local punctured-cube boundary tile whose label split is readable in the standard-code table.  No translation process, no protein fold, no physical admissibility theorem, no function claim, no universal mechanism, and no biological necessity follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.
