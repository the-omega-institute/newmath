\subsection{The punctured-cube readback packet for the standard code}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite object under inspection.}
This packet studies the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read through the usual codon alphabet and then compared with a curated codon-to-label table.  The distinguished finite set $R$ is the thirteen-codon region
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality row records $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons listed in the cardinality check and in the punctured-cube check.  The cubical incidence summary of this region is the finite vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not merely a label collection: it carries a local face-and-edge shape inside the six-bit codon display.  The biological observation attached to the packet is narrower than this internal geometry: the curated table says which amino-acid or stop label each codon receives in the standard code.

\paragraph{Local geometry and label contact.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ form the reported punctured-cube pattern.  This is a local combinatorial description of a codon tile, not a statement that biology is governed by this tile.  The readout contact is the standard genetic-code label table.  That contact can test codon-label readback and the local split induced by the displayed codons; it cannot test translation as a biochemical process, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  The singleton-corner and three-point-label-class distinctions therefore stay at the level of finite labeled rows.  They may organize the standard-code labels around the local tile, but they do not by themselves promote the tile into a global law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Spectral and randomization evidence.}
The spectrum row supplies three scalar checks.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated cubic or radial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically at the stated tolerance scale.  These numbers give a reproducible spectral signature for the finite arrangement, while remaining internal to the code-layer representation.  In the shape-significance experiment with seed $42$, $N=2000$ distinct samples were targeted, requiring $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape appeared once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  High-overlap samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second null model preserving amino-acid class reported $N=2000$, exact-match count $0$, observed $|M|=20$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  The supplied rows therefore support a finite non-random-looking code-read pattern, but the evidence still concerns codon labels and sampled combinatorial alternatives, not a direct physical or functional experiment.

\paragraph{Leave-out tests.}
The code-level leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the reported rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These two rows make the boundary behavior concrete: when codons or code variants are withheld, the finite code-layer construction often recovers or localizes the missing item, especially once boundary cases are admitted.  The result is a readback-and-prediction record for labels in finite tables, not an observation of ribosomal realization or protein structure.

The family-level code-out row divides the evaluated variants into $5$ families.  The above-baseline family hit rates are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate.  The module-classification row assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The reported modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\

Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the supplied classification.  This gives the packet a complete finite partition of the punctured-cube region.  The partition is useful for reading the standard-code table locally: arginine-related codons, lysine-side singleton behavior, the CUN block, the Ile/Met position, the SSN pair, and the stop/Trp block are all represented as explicit row data.

\paragraph{Scientific scope.}
The admissible conclusion is deliberately modest.  The six-coordinate codon representation exposes a thirteen-codon punctured-cube region with $16$ one-dimensional incidences and $4$ local faces, a reproducible spectral signature, significant finite randomization contrasts, strong leave-out recovery rows, and a complete module assignment.  The only external biological contact used here is the curated standard-code label table.  Consequently, the packet supports a code-read claim: the standard-code labels align with a finite local tile and its boundary behavior.  It does not show that translation is realized by this geometry, that amino-acid strings fold in any particular way, that the labels are physically admissible as structures, that any biological function follows, that a mechanism has been identified, or that the pattern is universal.  Any such stronger statement would require a separate contact supplying the relevant biochemical, structural, physical, functional, mechanistic, or comparative evidence.
