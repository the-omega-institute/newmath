\subsection{Punctured-cube codon tile in the standard code}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.} The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code table as a codon-to-label contact. The row under study is the finite subset $R$ consisting of the thirteen codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the same thirteen entries occur in both the cardinality row and the punctured-cube row. The face data are finite: the recorded face vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object is a thirteen-vertex, sixteen-edge, four-square tile inside $Q_6$. This is a code-layer statement: the biological contact supplies labels in the standard genetic-code table, while the BEDC row supplies the coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data for the local tile.

\paragraph{Local shape evidence.} The shape row is not merely a restatement of the curated code table. Against $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows with seed $42$, there was exactly one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. The high-overlap check found $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampling log recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and the same seed, the observed module size was $20$, exact matches were $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$. Thus the class-preserving exact-shape value was $0.0$, while the lower-tail value was $0.0035$. These numbers support a finite local boundary pattern, not a higher-layer biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral row.} The associated spectral row records three scalar quantities:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius entry in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$. The spectral data therefore attach a numerical invariant to the same finite carrier row, rather than introducing an independent biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Predictive leave-out checks.} The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit rate above baseline was $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family. The leave-one-codon-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These checks say that the local tile has strong row-level readback behavior when codons or code tables are removed, but they remain finite code-read tests.

\paragraph{Module partition.} The module classification accounts for all thirteen codons in $R$: $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable or boundary status in the finite-row classification. The local split is therefore a complete accounting of this punctured-cube row, not an incomplete selection of convenient codons.

\paragraph{Contact boundary.} The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose resolution is codon label readback. It can test code-read behavior and the local codon-tile label split. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is not a folded protein structure, and a window-six codon geometry is not evidence that DNA obeys this tile as a biological necessity. Any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate contact at that layer. With only the present contact, the ready claim is exactly this: the standard-code label table exposes a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary tile in $Q_6$, with face vector $(13,16,4)$, nontrivial finite-row statistics, spectral invariants, complete module accounting, and leave-out readback support at the code layer.
