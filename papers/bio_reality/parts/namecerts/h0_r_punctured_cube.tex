\subsection{The punctured cube row in the standard-code readback}\label{subsec:h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
This record is a code-read claim about a finite six-bit codon display, not a claim about the biochemical machinery that realizes translation. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite coordinate object whose vertices are codons and whose labels are supplied by the curated standard-code table. The reality contact used here is only the codon-to-label table: it observes that each codon receives an amino-acid or stop label. It does not observe ribosomal translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{The punctured-cube row.}
The verified row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the following set of codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite run verifies that this row is the punctured-cube support detected by the local tile probe. Its face data have the finite vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it carries a concrete local cell structure with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the displayed tile geometry. The row is therefore a finite boundary object inside $Q_6$, with a singleton-corner and three-point-label-class readback supplied externally by the code table.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ null samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. Among those finite samples there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null run also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It observed $0$ exact matches, an observed $M$ size of $20$, and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size. The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the statement that the row is a nontrivial finite code-layer pattern relative to the sampled nulls. They do not identify a unique evolutionary cause, a biochemical necessity, or a universal mechanism.

This is a local recoverability statement at the code-read layer: the finite boundary and closure tests recover code labels in the tested leave-one setting. It is not a statement that the codons physically assemble, fold, or function by this geometry.

\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\

The completeness certificate has $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons. Thus every codon in $R$ is accounted for by the module partition, and every module has a predictable-or-boundary interpretation in the finite probe.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The chapter claim is therefore narrow. The punctured-cube row is a verified finite code-read structure inside a six-bit codon display, with row size $13$, face vector $(13,16,4)$, a numerical spectral certificate, null-model support, leave-one recovery support, and complete module classification. The curated standard-code table is the only external reality contact used for label readback. From this contact alone one cannot claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of origin, or biological universality. Any such higher-layer assertion requires a separate contact that directly measures that layer.
