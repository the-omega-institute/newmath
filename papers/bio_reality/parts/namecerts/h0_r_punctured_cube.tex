\subsection{A punctured-cube tile in the standard-code readback}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$ after the curated standard-code table has been used only as a label lookup.  The object under test is not a biochemical mechanism.  It is the finite subset $R$ of codons singled out by the local window-six construction and then checked against the standard-code label table.  The verified row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality row reports $|R|=13$ with the same thirteen codons.  The face-incidence computation reports the vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local set is read as a punctured cube-shaped tile: thirteen displayed codon vertices, sixteen one-step boundary adjacencies, and four local square faces.  The word ``punctured'' is used here in this finite combinatorial sense only; it does not assert that biological evolution, translation, folding, or function is governed by this geometry.

\paragraph{Label contact.}
The only reality contact used in this chapter is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  That contact is enough to test codon-label readback and local label splitting inside $Q_6$.  It is not enough to test ribosomal realization, molecular admissibility, folded structure, phenotype, selective advantage, or universality across code systems.  Consequently the chapter records a code-layer coincidence: a finite window in codon coordinates has a sharply delimited boundary pattern, and the standard-code labels can be read on that pattern.  No higher biological layer is inferred from that fact.

\paragraph{Spectral check.}
The same finite run reports spectral scalars for the observed tile and its complementary window statistic.  The values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic residual attached to the radial calculation is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also reported as the radial polynomial value.  Thus the spectral row is numerically self-consistent to floating-point tolerance.  The interpretation remains finite and diagnostic: the numbers certify that the displayed codon subset participates in a stable local spectral pattern, not that the spectrum is a law of translation or a physical energy model.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Two finite null checks measure how easily the observed shape appears after label-preserving or coordinate-preserving randomization.  In the coordinate shape experiment with seed $42$, $N=2000$ samples were accepted after $57377$ distinct sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sample reproduced the observed shape, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  Twenty-five samples reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed auxiliary size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  The corresponding reported probabilities were $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$.  These values support rarity of the local tile under the stated finite null procedures.  They do not identify a causal pathway, and they do not license promotion from code labels to protein structure.

The family-level breakdown reports five families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$.  A separate leave-one-codon-out check reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the finite local rule is strongly recoverable under the tested omissions.  They still speak only about codon-label recovery inside the declared code-read task.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification row partitions all thirteen $R$ codons without surplus or deficit.  It reports $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing members, and no unassigned codons.  The six named modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either a predictable member or a boundary member in the finite test.  This closes the chapter-specific accounting: the punctured cube, its thirteen codons, its four faces, its module partition, its spectral scalars, and its null-model rarity are all finite code-read facts.  The result remains deliberately modest.  Without an additional contact of the appropriate kind, it does not assert translation dynamics, folded protein order, physical feasibility, biochemical function, a mechanism of code origin, or a universal biological principle.
