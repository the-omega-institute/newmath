\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The record uses the six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$ as its finite carrier.  Each codon is read as a point of the codon cube, while the amino-acid or stop label is supplied by the curated standard-code table.  The row under inspection is the finite subset $R$ consisting of thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check reports $|R|=13$ and returns exactly the same list of codons.  The punctured-cube check identifies the same thirteen points and assigns the local cell count
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite observation is not merely a label collection: it has a concrete cubical boundary profile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-faces, and four two-faces inside the six-bit coordinate presentation.  The biological readback remains narrower than the coordinate object: the curated table tells us which of these codons carry amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code, while the coordinate calculation records the local shape of the selected row.

These numbers support the narrower claim that this row is unusual relative to the stated finite controls; they do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table, understood as a codon-to-label record.  It supports code-layer readback and local split checks: codons can be compared with their standard amino-acid or stop labels, and the selected codon row can be examined as a cubical boundary pattern.  The contact does not observe translation dynamics, folded protein geometry, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, or a universal biological mechanism.  Consequently the row may be stated as a local code-read pattern in $Q_6$, with an unusual punctured-cube profile and strong finite deletion statistics, but it must not be promoted into a claim that DNA is governed by this geometry as a biological law.

The same restriction applies to amino-acid label sequences.  Reading the labels of $AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA$ from the standard table gives only code-level labels.  It supplies no folded structure, no structural order claim, no physical viability test, and no function claim.  Any such higher-layer assertion would require a separate contact of the appropriate kind.  Within the data provided here, the scientifically defensible conclusion is therefore local and finite: the standard-code row $R$ is a thirteen-codon punctured-cube tile with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, stable spectral values, significant rarity under the recorded finite controls, and complete module accounting, all while remaining confined to code-read evidence.
