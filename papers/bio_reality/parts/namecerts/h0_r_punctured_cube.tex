\subsection{The punctured-cube codon tile at the code-read layer}\label{subsec:h0-r-punctured-cube}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.} The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only through a curated standard-code label table.  The contact says that a standard genetic-code bridge assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  It does not say that the cube geometry is a biochemical mechanism, a translation apparatus, a folding law, a physical admissibility test, or a universal biological principle.  The record therefore lives at the $\mathrm{code\_read}$ layer: it tests a local codon-label split inside a finite display, and it blocks promotion to translation, folded structure, function, mechanism, or universality unless a separate higher-layer contact is supplied.

\paragraph{Observed row set.} The distinguished row set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite run verifies that this set is the punctured-cube row under the window-six coordinate display, with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a list of anomalous labels: it has a compact boundary-tile shape consisting of thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four square faces in the finite codon display.  The biological readback remains the standard-code label table, not an independent claim about realized molecular dynamics.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row check.} The same record carries a finite spectral signature.  The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial or cubic consistency check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the polynomial residual is numerically at roundoff scale for this finite computation.  This is evidence that the coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation views are mutually consistent for the extracted row.  It is not evidence that protein structure or biological function has been observed.

\paragraph{Shape significance against null rows.} The row shape was tested against $N=2000$ sampled null rows.  The run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  In an amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$, the exact shape count was $0$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the lower-tail count at or below that size was $7$, the exact-shape $p$-value was $0.0$, and the lower-tail $p$-value was $0.0035$.  These finite-row numbers support the claim that the observed local tile is not a typical class-preserving random row.  They still do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one checks.} The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the five-family split gave hit rates above baseline of $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  The leave-one-codon-out probe found $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These are code-label recovery statistics.  They are not translation-efficiency statistics, structural statistics, or fitness statistics.

\paragraph{Module accounting.} The module classification accounts for all thirteen codons in $R$: the assigned count is $13$, the row size is $13$, and there are no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.

$$
Every module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite probe.  The singleton components $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ and $\mathrm{ile\_met}$, the two-point $\mathrm{agr}$ and $\mathrm{ssn}$ components, the three-point $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$ class, and the four-codon $\mathrm{cun}$ face together supply the local tile decomposition recorded by the codon display.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.} The ready claim is therefore narrow: in the standard-code label table, the verified row $R$ is a thirteen-codon punctured-cube local tile in the six-bit codon display, with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable finite spectral signature, significant deviation from sampled null shapes, strong leave-one recovery, and complete module classification.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law; window-six geometry alone is not translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality; and amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.
