\subsection{A punctured codon cube at the code-label layer}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
This record concerns a finite window-six presentation of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with codons displayed as finite words over the four RNA letters after choosing the associated six-bit coordinate display.  The observed subset $R$ is not introduced as a biological mechanism.  It is a finite row set whose role is to test whether the standard-code labels, when read at the code-label layer, concentrate on a local punctured-cube pattern.  The verified row set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons recovered.  The cubical face count attached to this row set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite shape is a thirteen-vertex, sixteen-edge, four-face local tile in the coordinate cube.  The statement made here is deliberately narrow: the code table supplies codon-to-label observations, and the geometry summarizes a local label split inside that table.

\paragraph{Code labels and the role of the curated table.}
The only reality contact used by this chapter is a curated standard genetic-code table.  That contact says that each codon has a standard amino-acid or stop label.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal generative mechanism.  Consequently, amino-acid label readback remains a code-layer observation.  Even when a codon is assigned to an amino-acid label, this chapter treats the assignment as a table entry, not as a statement that a protein exists, folds, functions, or is physically favored.  The contact is sufficient for local split checks among labels, and insufficient for promotion to any higher biological layer.

They do not convert the coordinate pattern into a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Null comparisons for shape concentration.}
Against the sampled shape null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact local shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ appeared in $25$ of $2000$ sampled cases, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the empirical comparison is sparse relative to the combinatorial space being sampled.  A second comparison preserving amino-acid classes evaluated $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It found $0$ exact matches, $7$ lower-tail cases at or below the observed $M$ size, and an observed $M$ size of $20$.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These checks support the claim that the local row pattern is non-generic under the tested finite nulls.  They do not identify a causal explanation for the genetic code.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon and module accounting.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, closure coverage high at $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite classification.  This closes the finite row accounting for the punctured-cube claim: all thirteen codons in $R$ are placed, and no additional codon is silently imported into the module list.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and boundary of interpretation.}
The finite evidence supports the following code-layer conclusion.  In the standard-code table, the thirteen-codon set $R$ forms a punctured local cube pattern with face vector $(13,16,4)$, non-generic finite shape statistics under the reported nulls, a stable spectral diagnostic, and complete module accounting.  The evidence is appropriate for saying that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the curated label table.  It is not appropriate for saying that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity.  It is also not evidence for translation realization, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such stronger assertion would require a separate contact that directly observes the corresponding layer.
