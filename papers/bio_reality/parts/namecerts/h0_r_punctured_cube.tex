\subsection{Finite punctured-cube readback for the codon window}
\label{subsec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Carrier and observed row.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code label table.  The row under study is the finite subset $R$ of codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The measured cardinality is $|R|=13$, and the finite-cell census of the induced punctured cube is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not being treated as a verbal motif or a continuous approximation: it is a concrete finite subcomplex of the codon display, with thirteen named vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the local tile.  The biological contact used for readback is only the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact supports code-layer label comparison; it does not by itself supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row signature.}
The same finite row has a recorded spectral signature.  The two reported radii are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, with $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic residual is numerically negligible at $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius entry is $0.6752479308830213$.  In finite-row language this means that the local codon tile is accompanied by a stable numerical probe, not merely by a set equality.  The spectral datum is still a code-read datum: it constrains the observed six-bit display and its derived relation, but it is not evidence that the same geometry governs biochemistry outside the supplied code table.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Against the shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The number of samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported row should be read as a finite randomized comparison with a visible sampling burden.  Against the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$.  The corresponding values were $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These numbers support the claim that the observed local shape is unusual under the stated finite nulls.  They do not promote the row into a law of translation or a law of protein realization.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out probe reported anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These probes are read as robustness checks for the finite code-layer row: removed local entries are frequently recovered by the displayed closure-and-boundary structure.  Since the contact is a curated code table, recovery means recovery of codon-label placement in the code-read layer, not recovery of a physical translation process or a folded protein object.

\paragraph{Family and module accounting.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out accounting used $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  The module classification is complete: it assigns all $13$ codons of $R$, has assigned count $13$, and has no extra module codons, no missing module entries, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary explanation in the supplied finite row.  This is the precise content of the local punctured-cube claim: the standard-code readback exposes a thirteen-codon boundary tile with complete module accounting and strong finite-row recovery behavior.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is deliberately narrow.  The data support a local codon-window statement: in the six-bit codon display, the row $R$ is a thirteen-vertex punctured cube with $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral values, significant finite-null comparisons, strong leave-one-code recovery, and complete module classification against the curated standard-code label table.  No claim follows here about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Any such stronger statement would require a separate reality contact of the relevant kind, because amino-acid label readback is not folded protein structure and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
