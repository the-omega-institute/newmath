\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display.  The finite coordinate object is the codon cube $Q_6$, whose displayed vertices are read as codons after the usual base-to-coordinate encoding.  Inside this cube the distinguished region $R$ is not introduced as a biochemical mechanism.  It is a finite label subset whose shape can be checked against the curated standard genetic-code table.  The verified row identifies
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$.  The separate cardinality row reports the same thirteen codons, so the region used for the shape computation and the region used for the cardinality computation coincide at the finite table level.

\paragraph{Combinatorial shape.}
The local tile carried by $R$ has verified face data
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}

Thus the row is not merely a list of thirteen exceptional codons: it also supplies a small cubical boundary profile, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional edges, and four two-dimensional faces in the induced local complex.  In this chapter the phrase ``punctured cube'' names this finite cubical profile together with the displayed codon subset above.  The phrase does not assert that organisms instantiate this geometry as a law of translation; it only names the checked local configuration inside the six-coordinate codon display.

This supports treating the punctured tile as a stable finite coordinate feature, while still leaving all biological interpretation at the code-read level.

\paragraph{Null-model comparison.}
The shape row was compared with random alternatives.  In the shape-significance run, $N=2000$ sampled trials were used, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row used $N=2000$ trials and recorded no exact matches.  Its lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, with observed $M$ size $20$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite comparisons say that the reported local shape is rare under the sampled null procedures.  They do not by themselves identify a causal process, a molecular constraint, or an evolutionary necessity.

These rows are evidence that the local coordinate split is informative for code-label recovery across the finite comparison set, not that it explains translation or organismal function.
