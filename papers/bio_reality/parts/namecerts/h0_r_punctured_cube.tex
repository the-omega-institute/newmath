\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The record concerns a six-bit codon display over the codon cube $Q_6$, read only through the curated standard-code label table. The finite row isolates a set $R$ of thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality row reports $|R|=13$ with the same codon list. The punctured-cube row reports the face-vector match
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed object is not merely a named collection of codons: it is a finite local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the chosen window-six coordinate presentation. The readback contact is deliberately narrow. It says that a curated standard genetic-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. It does not say that the coordinate tile is a mechanism of translation, a folding constraint, a physical admissibility principle, a biological function, or a universal law.

This is evidence for a reproducible coordinate phenomenon at the code-read layer, not evidence for an unobserved biological layer.

The finite data supplied here include the scalar $|M|=20$ but not an explicit list of the twenty $M$ codons, so the chapter records the $M$ row numerically rather than pretending to inspect an unavailable list.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The ready claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose a reproducible local boundary-tile structure for the standard-code label table: a thirteen-codon set $R$, a punctured-cube face-vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral row, significant finite null-model tails, and complete module accounting. The claim stops exactly there. No translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity claim follows from these rows. Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate reality contact that directly observes the higher-layer object. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
