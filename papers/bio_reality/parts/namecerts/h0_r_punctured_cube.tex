\subsection{A punctured codon cube in the standard-code readout}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The row concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  Codons are treated as finite labels in a six-coordinate display $Q_6$, and the observed subset $R$ is the thirteen-codon row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check reports $|R|=13$, with the same codon list.  The geometric check records that this same $R$ is the punctured-cube row under the window-six coordinate construction.  Its finite face-count signature is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the datum is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it is a labeled finite subcomplex with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the chosen display.  The biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels.  That contact is sufficient for checking code labels on the thirteen codons and insufficient for promoting the pattern beyond the code table.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The finite-row spectrum gives two reported label-side radii and one coupling parameter:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also reported as the radial-polynomial value, so the numerical row sits within the stated tolerance rather than being a loose descriptive fit.  A finite randomization check with seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled tables found exactly one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The same run found $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and logged $55377$ duplicate retries, so the quoted probabilities should be read as finite experiment outputs under the recorded sampling regime, not as asymptotic law.

A second null check preserves amino-acid class information.  In that run, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed companion size was $20$.  It found $0$ exact shape matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed size.  Thus the recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support the finite claim that the observed code-read shape is not reproduced frequently by the stated null procedures.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Readout modules.}
The thirteen codons are completely assigned by the module classification.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\texttt{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  In addition, every module has either predictable or boundary status in the leave-one-codon readout.  The leave-one-codon experiment reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This says that, under the stated finite procedure, every held-out codon in the tested row is recovered through the recorded predictable-or-boundary mechanism; it does not say that the row determines translation chemistry.

\paragraph{Code-table transfer tests.}
The leave-one-code-out evaluation used $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate after adding the boundary allowance was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported above-baseline hit rate was likewise $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  These family values are finite cross-table readback checks: they show that the local code-read pattern has strong recovery behavior across the evaluated table collection, while also recording a family where recovery is not perfect.

\paragraph{Local boundary of the claim.}
The result established by this row is a finite code-read statement: in the window-six codon display, the thirteen listed standard-code codons form the recorded punctured-cube row, with face signature $(13,16,4)$, the stated spectral values, significant finite randomization checks, complete module assignment, and high leave-one-out recovery.  The only biological contact invoked is the curated standard-code label table.  Therefore the row may be read as evidence for a local codon-label tile in the code table, but it must not be read as evidence for translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any one of those stronger layers would require an additional contact designed for that layer, such as a translation assay, structural observation, energetic admissibility test, or functional experiment.
