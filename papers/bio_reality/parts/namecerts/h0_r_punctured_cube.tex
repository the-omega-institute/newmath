\subsection{The punctured codon cube row}\label{subsec:h0-r-punctured-cube}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The claim concerns a window-six presentation of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite coordinate display for codon labels.  The distinguished row $R$ is the thirteen-codon set
$\{AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}$.
The verified row size is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the same audit identifies the punctured-cube face counts
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2) &= (13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object recorded here is not an arbitrary codon list: it is a concrete local tile in $Q_6$ with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces after the relevant puncture has been imposed.  The row is label-facing rather than mechanism-facing: its observational role is to organize codons that the curated standard code maps to amino-acid or stop labels.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The finite row is accompanied by a spectral measurement on the associated closure display.  The observed values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the radial polynomial residual has the same value.  The residual is therefore numerical-zero at the stated precision for the finite computation.  A separate shape audit sampled $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$.  It observed one exact shape match, giving $p=0.0005$, and twenty-five matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$.  The sample required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the row is being compared against a heavily collision-prone finite universe rather than against a continuous idealization.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
The class-preserving null model gives a sharper boundary check.  In $N=2000$ sampled tables, the exact punctured-row shape occurred zero times, so the finite estimate for exact recurrence is $0.0$.  The same null records observed $M$-size $20$ and a lower-tail count of $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This does not say that the code is forced by the cube.  It says only that, under the sampled amino-acid-class preserving perturbations, the measured row and its associated $M$-size are uncommon in the recorded finite experiment.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon tests.}
The leave-one-code experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$.  The above-baseline rate is also $0.9841269841269841$, which corresponds to all but one of the $63$ targets being recovered by the stated finite rule.  The leave-one-codon experiment reports no anchors, five boundary codons, eight high-coverage closure recoveries, eight predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The finite message is therefore stable under two different deletion probes: deleting a whole code table and deleting a single codon both preserve a strong local readback signal at the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The thirteen row codons are completely accounted for by six named modules.  The $CUN$ module is $\{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\}$; the $AGR$ module is $\{AGA, AGG\}$; the stop--tryptophan module is $\{UAA, UAG, UGA\}$; the isoleucine--methionine module is $\{AUA\}$; the arginine--lysine module is $\{AAA\}$; and the $SSN$ module is $\{UCA, UUA\}$.  The classification audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons.  Family-level leave-one-code rates remain high across five families: alternative yeast has rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial--plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each have rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Local reading.}
The scientific content of the row is a finite code-table observation.  A six-bit coordinate window, together with closure and spectrum data, isolates a local tile whose codons are exactly the thirteen listed above and whose face vector is $(13,16,4)$.  The curated standard-code table supplies the label contact needed to say which codons occupy that row, and the deletion experiments show that the row is useful for code-layer recovery.  This is enough to justify recording the punctured cube as a finite BioReality datum for codon-label readback.

\paragraph{Boundary.}
The same record also fixes the limit of the claim.  The punctured cube is not evidence that DNA is governed by this geometry as a biological necessity.  The finite row does not establish translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a biochemical mechanism, or universality across organisms.  Those stronger statements would require separate observations that directly touch translation, structure, physics, function, mechanism, or organism-level scope.  In this chapter the contact is only the curated standard-code label table, so the conclusion remains a local code-read result.
