\subsection{The punctured codon cube row}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon coordinate display.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, while the distinguished row $R$ is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check reports $|R|=13$, and the independent row equality check returns the same thirteen labels.  In the tile complex induced by this row, the face count vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the data are not merely a named list of codons: they also carry a finite local cell structure inside the six-coordinate display.  The intended readback is narrow.  The curated standard genetic-code table supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels, and this chapter uses that table only as a label contact for the code layer.

\paragraph{Geometric reading of the row.}
The row $R$ has the shape of a punctured local cube in the codon-coordinate display.  Its thirteen vertices are concentrated around the $A/G$ and $C/U$ coordinate contrasts that appear in stop, tryptophan, leucine, arginine, lysine, isoleucine, and methionine-related code neighborhoods.  The finite face vector $(13,16,4)$ records that the observed codon row includes local square faces rather than only isolated vertices.  This gives a precise mathematical object for the local tile claim: a row of codon labels, a boundary-like missing pattern, and a small number of two-dimensional faces in $Q_6$.  It does not, by itself, assert how ribosomes translate, how proteins fold, which codons are physically admissible in an organism, or which phenotypes follow from using these labels.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
A separate spectral row check reports the scalar values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the recorded spectral relation is numerically satisfied to the stated tolerance.  The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$.  Among these controls there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples reached Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling run also records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which matters because the control space is finite and repeated draws are visible in the audit trail.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class preserving null supplies a stronger comparison at the code-label level.  In that run, $N=2000$, the observed comparison size for $M$ is $20$, and there are $0$ exact matches.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the statement that the displayed row is not a typical outcome under the recorded label-class preserving shuffle.  The finite packet does not include a member-by-member list for $M$; the available datum for that object here is its observed size $20$ together with the null-model counts.

\paragraph{Predictive leave-out behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, whereas the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out run reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, closure coverage high for $8$ cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers say that the local row has reproducible label-level recovery behavior under the recorded leave-out tests.  They do not convert the row into a general biological principle.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The module-classification row assigns all thirteen codons of $R$ with no missing entries, no extras, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family leave-one-code-out audit lists $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.  Thus the row is not only a thirteen-point set; it is also a complete finite partition into biologically named code-label modules under the recorded table contact.

\paragraph{Local boundary of interpretation.}
The admissible conclusion is a code-read conclusion.  The standard-code table supports the statement that a punctured local cube pattern appears in the finite codon-label display and that the listed leave-out and null-model checks favor this pattern over the recorded controls.  Nothing in this chapter observes translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such claim would require a separate experimental or curated contact at that higher layer.  In particular, amino-acid label readback is not a protein-structure observation, and a local codon tile is not a law governing all biological systems.
