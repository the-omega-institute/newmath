\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a coordinate display for the standard-code codon table.  In this record the distinguished row set $R$ is not inferred from translation dynamics, folded structure, chemical admissibility, or biological function.  It is the finite code-layer set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the independent punctured-cube readback gives the same thirteen codons.  The associated finite face count is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed tile is a thirteen-vertex, sixteen-edge, four-face local object inside the codon coordinate cube.  The phrase ``punctured cube'' is used here only for this finite-row geometry: it names the local tile seen by the window-six coordinate system and does not promote that tile to a global biological law.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used in this claim is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact has resolution ``codon label readback''.  It can test the code-read layer and the local codon-tile label split, but it cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, universal mechanism, or any claim about folded protein structure.  Consequently the standard-code table is treated as a reality bridge for labels, not as a mechanism or universality proof.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer readback even when the local tile is visually or combinatorially sharp.
