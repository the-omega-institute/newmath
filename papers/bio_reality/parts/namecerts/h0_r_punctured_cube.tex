\subsection{Punctured-cube boundary row for the standard code}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The row records a window-six codon coordinate experiment against the curated standard-code label table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite codon display with local face adjacency and standard-code labels.  The distinguished boundary set is the thirteen-codon row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independently recorded cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same ordered codon list.  The cubical incidence count attached to this row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed object is not merely a label subset: it is a local punctured-cube tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen local one-dimensional incidences, and four local square faces.  The title ``punctured cube'' is used in this finite sense only: a codon-coordinate pattern inside the window-six cube, not a claim about molecular mechanism.

The spectral row therefore supports a reproducible coordinate statement: the observed boundary set $R$ has a stable local spectrum in the codon-cube representation.  It does not, by itself, identify an amino-acid chemistry, a translational process, or a folded state.

\paragraph{Randomization checks.}
A shape randomization with $N=2000$ sampled trials, seed $42$, and experiment id $0b267c88f8e4$ produced $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite frequencies are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null comparison was made in a constrained finite space rather than by an unconstrained independent draw at every attempt.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, observed $0$ exact shape matches.  Its lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, with observed $M$ size $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures make the local tile non-generic under the two recorded finite nulls.  The evidence is statistical and coordinate-level: it favors the presence of a special local codon-label geometry, while leaving all higher biological interpretations outside this row.

The recorded rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus the local coordinate tile has strong finite readback performance for standard-code label recovery under the recorded leave-one-code-out setup.

The leave-one-codon-out row gives a complementary test.  It recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closures, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This says that, within the finite experiment, omitted codons in the tested boundary regime were recovered by local closure or boundary behavior.  The result is a code-label result, not a statement that the biological translation apparatus is forced by the geometry.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact has resolution at the codon-label readback level.  It can test whether the coordinate tile aligns with standard-code labels and whether the thirteen-codon boundary row behaves coherently under the listed leave-one probes.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal biological mechanism.  Consequently, this row supports the following narrow statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a non-generic, spectrally stable, locally recoverable boundary tile in the standard-code label table.  No claim about protein folding, structural order, molecular feasibility, organismal function, global law, or necessity of the genetic code follows unless a separate observation at that higher layer is supplied.
