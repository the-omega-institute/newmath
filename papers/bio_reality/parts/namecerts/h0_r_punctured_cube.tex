\end{aligned}
$$

It says only that the curated standard genetic-code bridge assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels, and therefore permits code-layer readback of labels on this finite coordinate row.

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
This chapter claims only a finite local code-read fact.  The curated standard-code table is a reality contact for codon-to-label assignment, not a mechanism and not a universality proof.  The local codon tile does not by itself establish translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law saying that DNA must obey this BEDC geometry.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still code-layer readback; it is not folded structure.  Any claim crossing from this row to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate higher-layer contact supplied outside the present finite-row packet.
