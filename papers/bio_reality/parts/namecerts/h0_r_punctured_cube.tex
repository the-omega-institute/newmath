\subsection{A punctured cube row in the standard-code readback}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a coordinate display for the standard-code codon table.  In this display the distinguished row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row audit records $|R|=13$, and the same thirteen codons are listed by the independent cardinality check.  The punctured-cube check identifies this row with a local cube-shaped boundary object whose finite face data have
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the chapter-level claim is not that codons are forced by geometry, but that the curated standard-code labels expose a finite, reproducible local tile in the $Q_6$ coordinate readback.

\paragraph{Label contact and scope.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels at code-read resolution.  It does not supply a ribosomal translation event, a folded protein structure, a physical admissibility test, a biological function assay, a mechanism, or a universality theorem.  Consequently the row $R$ is a code-layer object: the table lets us ask whether a local label split is visible in the standard-code readback, but it does not promote that split beyond the code layer.  The singleton and three-point label-corner distinctions are therefore interpreted as local table distinctions inside $Q_6$, not as claims about protein realization.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model coordinates.}
The associated spectral audit gives the scalar values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, recorded both as the cubic-polynomial value and as the radial-polynomial value, so the spectral coordinate is numerically closed at the stated tolerance.  Against the shape null with $N=2000$ samples, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches at Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling log records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries under seed $42$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at the observed $M$ size was $7$, the observed value was $|M|=20$, and the corresponding lower-tail probability was $0.0035$.  These figures support a finite-row anomaly in the curated table; they do not identify a biological cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family audit splits this behavior across $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  The leave-one-codon-out probe records $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These probes show that the local tile is recoverable as a code-read pattern under finite deletions; they still do not test translation realization, folding, function, physical admissibility, mechanism, or universality.

The thirteen codons of $R$ are completely assigned to six finite modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\

The module-classification audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has predictable-or-boundary status.  This is the strongest local form of the row claim: the punctured-cube row is not only a thirteen-codon subset, but a fully classified local code-read boundary with finite module coverage.

The BioReality status is ready at code-read scope.  The finite facts support the statement that the standard-code table contains a thirteen-codon row $R$ whose $Q_6$ coordinate geometry matches a punctured cube with face vector $(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral scalars, significant finite null-model behavior, robust leave-one probes, and complete module classification.
