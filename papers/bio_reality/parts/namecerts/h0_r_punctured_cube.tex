\subsection{Punctured codon cube at the code-read boundary}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with local tile faces and code labels supplied by a curated standard-code table. The observed boundary row $R$ has exactly thirteen codons. In the finite run, the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check reports $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen entries. The punctured-cube check reports that this row is not merely a list of labels: its local cubical incidence data has $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is read as thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four local two-faces in the window-six coordinate geometry. This is the finite mathematical content of the claim: a standard-code label contact selects a thirteen-codon row whose induced local geometry matches the punctured-cube signature measured by the run.

\paragraph{Readback contact.}
The biological contact used here is deliberately narrow. The curated standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels, and therefore it can test code-layer readback and local codon-tile label splits. It does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism. Thus the row $R$ is a code-read object: the contact says which codons carry which labels in the standard table, while the six-bit BEDC coordinate calculation says how the selected codons sit inside the local cube. Neither part by itself promotes the local tile into a biological law.

\paragraph{Null comparison and shape rarity.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. Among those samples, the exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found zero exact shape matches. Its observed companion set size is $|M|=20$, its lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$. In that run the exact-shape probability is recorded as $0.0$. These null rows support the finite statement that the punctured-cube pattern is uncommon under the tested controls, but they still remain code-layer statistics rather than evidence of folding, admissibility, or function.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. The leave-one-codon-out run reports no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These figures say that the finite code-read geometry is stable under the recorded deletion tests. They do not say that the code is forced by geometry alone.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The verified claim is therefore local and finite: in the six-bit codon cube, the standard-code readback row $R$ is a thirteen-point punctured-cube tile with $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, a recorded spectral profile, rare-shape null behavior, and complete module classification. The claim stops at the code-read layer. No translation process, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, biological mechanism, or universality follows from this row unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that information. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
