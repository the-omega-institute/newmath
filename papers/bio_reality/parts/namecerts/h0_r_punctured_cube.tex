Exactly one sampled control matched the observed shape, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. Twenty-five controls had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.
$$
\begin{aligned}

These values support the statement that the punctured-cube row is an unusually specific finite shape under the tested nulls. They do not convert the row into a global law of genetic coding.
