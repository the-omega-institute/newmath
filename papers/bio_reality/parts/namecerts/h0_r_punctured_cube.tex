\subsection{The punctured codon cube packet}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary set.}
The packet is a finite code-layer record on the six-bit codon cube.  Its distinguished boundary set is the thirteen-codon family
$R=\{AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}$.
The independent cardinality check reports the same set and the same size, so the packet is not using a hidden enlargement of the row: $|R|=13$.  The punctured-cube test reports that this set has face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)&=(13,16,4),\\
|R|&=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object being discussed is a local tile in the codon display, not an organism-level or mechanism-level claim.  The labels used for readback are supplied by the curated standard-code table, which assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or stop label.  The geometric part of the packet determines which codons lie in the displayed finite boundary; the biological label names enter through the curated table contact.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The same finite row includes a spectral summary.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same number is recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual.  At this numerical tolerance, the row is internally consistent as a finite spectral record.  A shape-significance run with seed $42$ used $N=2000$ samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, records observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its reported probabilities are $p=0.0$ for exact shape in the sampled run and $p=0.0035$ for the lower-tail event.  These finite numbers support the statement that the displayed local geometry is not a typical row under the two reported sampling regimes.  They do not identify a translation process, a folding law, a physical feasibility condition, or a biological function.

\paragraph{Readback performance.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate in $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$, the same value reported as the hit rate above baseline.  By family, the above-baseline rates are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast.  These are code-table readback statistics.  They say that the finite window and its boundary data recover many held-out label placements under the stated protocol; they do not say that a ribosome, a cell, or a protein fold is generated by the cube geometry.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out module structure.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports recovery rate $1.0$, with $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, $0$ anchor cases, and $8$ high-coverage closure cases.  The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and there are no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six recorded modules are
$\{AGA,AGG\}$ for agr,
$\{AAA\}$ for ar\_lys,
$\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for cun,
$\{AUA\}$ for ile\_met,
$\{UCA,UUA\}$ for ssn, and
$\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for stop\_trp.
Each module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite packet.  This gives a compact local description of the thirteen-codon punctured-cube set and its standard-code label readback.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and scope.}
The evidence supports the following limited conclusion.  In the six-coordinate codon display, the standard-code table contains a thirteen-codon boundary set $R$ with punctured-cube face data $(13,16,4)$, stable cardinality, a coherent finite spectral summary, nontrivial sampling significance in the reported null models, and strong leave-one-out code-layer recovery.  The conclusion is intentionally confined to codon-label readback.  No translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this packet alone.  Any such stronger statement would require a separate observation at the corresponding biological layer; the curated code table contact used here supplies codon-to-label assignments and nothing beyond that.
