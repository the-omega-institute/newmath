\subsection{Punctured-cube codon window}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-reading layer. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon represented by its six-bit display. Inside that cube the distinguished set $R$ is the thirteen-codon row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the same thirteen entries are returned by the punctured-cube computation. The associated cell count is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not being used as a loose list of labels: it carries a local two-dimensional boundary profile inside the six-bit coordinate cube. This is the entire geometric claim at this stage. It is a finite shape statement about a curated codon-label table, not a claim that molecular translation, physical admissibility, folding, function, mechanism, or universality has been derived from cube geometry.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The row is accompanied by a numerical spectrum probe. The recorded values are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, and the spectral radius is inside tolerance at $0.6752479308830213$. These numbers support the statement that the local codon set has a reproducible coordinate signature, but they do not supply a biological law. The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and saw $55377$ duplicate retries. Among the sampled tables there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ cases with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jac}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$. Thus the finite row is rare under this sampling regime while still remaining a code-layer observation.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null comparison.}
A second null run preserves amino-acid class information. It used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$ and observed an $M$-size of $20$. The exact-shape count was $0$, reported as $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$. The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{class,tail}}=0.0035$. This comparison is important because it tests whether the pattern survives a more label-aware null constraint. The finite data say that the observed local shape is still unusually placed under that constrained model. They do not say why biochemical evolution selected the labels, nor do they transform codon-label readback into a structural or functional protein statement.

\paragraph{Leave-one stability.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate after adding the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$, equal to the reported rate above baseline. By family, the hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast. The leave-one-codon-out run gives a complementary local test: $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These figures make the row a stable local readback pattern across finite removal tests, but the stability is still stability of code labels and boundary membership.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification exhausts the thirteen-row set without leftovers. The complete accounting reports $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite packet. The row therefore decomposes into recognizable local families rather than a single undifferentiated exceptional set. This decomposition is part of the finite codon-table reading only; it is not evidence for a folded structure, a translation apparatus, a physicochemical feasibility constraint, a biological function, or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact has resolution at codon-label readback. It can test whether a six-bit coordinate pattern and local tile split are present in the standard table, and it can test whether the thirteen-codon row agrees with the observed labels. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, or a universal biological rule. Consequently the correct conclusion is narrow: the standard-code table contains a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row with cell vector $(13,16,4)$, a reproducible spectral signature, rare-shape behavior under the recorded null models, and strong finite leave-one stability. Any claim beyond that boundary requires a separate contact at the higher biological layer.
