\subsection{Punctured Codon Cube as a Local Code-Read Tile}\label{sec:bioreality-punctured-codon-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback scope.}
The finite object in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with a standard-code label readback.  The cube coordinate is not used as a claim about translation physics; it is used only as a finite display on which local codon-label distinctions can be inspected.  The observed boundary set is
$R = \{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.
The row check gives $|R|=13$, and the independent punctured-cube check returns the same thirteen codons.  Its finite face data are summarized by the $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the code-read contact is a thirteen-vertex local tile with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces in the chosen cube display.  The contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table: it supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels, and it supplies no direct observation of translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

This is a statement about the coordinate-and-relation object over the codon label table.  It is not a claim that DNA, RNA, ribosomes, aminoacyl-tRNA synthetases, folding kinetics, or cellular fitness are governed by this tile.

\paragraph{Shape significance against label-preserving nulls.}
The shape test with $N=2000$ sampled controls produced one exact shape match after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape probability was therefore recorded as $p=0.0005$, and the high-Jaccard threshold test gave $25$ matches with $p=0.0125$ for Jaccard at least $0.9$.  A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$, observed $M$-size $20$, exact match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its exact-shape probability was $0.0$, while the lower-tail probability was $0.0035$.  These finite rows support the narrower reading that this particular standard-code label table exposes an unusual local tile in the six-bit coordinate.  They do not promote the tile into a global biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out table evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row had anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers make the code-read tile predictive inside the finite label-display experiment, but the prediction target remains a codon label in a table.  No protein fold, physical admissibility condition, cellular function, mechanism, or universal constraint is observed by these leave-one probes.

\paragraph{Family and module resolution.}
The leave-one-code-out family row split the comparison into $5$ families. This is the strongest local form of the record: the punctured-cube row, the cardinality row, the spectral row, the null rows, the leave-one rows, the family row, and the module row agree on a finite code-read phenomenon around the same thirteen codons.

\paragraph{Boundary statement.}
The cannot-claim boundary is part of the mathematical content of this record.  The curated table is a reality contact for labels only.  It supports code-layer readback and local split checks, but it cannot test translation realization, structural order, folded protein geometry, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such higher-layer statement would require a separate contact whose observations live at that layer.  Thus the verdict is not that biological reality is a six-bit punctured cube; the verdict is that, after fixing the standard-code label table as an external contact, the thirteen-codon set $R$ forms a reproducible local punctured-cube tile in the finite coordinate display.
