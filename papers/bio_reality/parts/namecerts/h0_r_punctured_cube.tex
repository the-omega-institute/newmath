\paragraph{Finite object.}
The row records a six-position codon display whose coordinates are read as the binary placement of the three codon letters inside the codon cube $Q_6$.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: it is the curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  Within that contact, the distinguished local region $R$ is exactly the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons.  The cubical incidence count of this region is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed object is not merely a list of labels: it has a finite face structure inside the six-window coordinate cube.  The name ``punctured cube'' records this local combinatorial shape, not a global law about genetic codes.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code label table.  The contact supplies codon-to-label readback only.  Nothing in this row observes ribosomal translation, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality across life.  Any such promotion would require a separate empirical contact at the relevant layer.  In particular, amino-acid label sequences are not folded protein structures, and CUN/UUR or tRNA-Leu wording is not structural evidence unless an organism-matched structural or mechanistic contact is supplied.
