\subsection{A Punctured Local Codon Cube at the Code-Read Boundary}\label{sec:bior-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record is a finite code-read statement about the standard-code codon table, not a statement about translation dynamics or protein realization. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated codon-to-label table. The distinguished row $R$ consists of exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row audit reports $|R|=13$ and separately returns the same ordered row under the identifier $R\_cardinality\_equals\_13=13$. The row is therefore not a free visual impression: it is a named thirteen-point subset of the codon cube, with a concrete readback through the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The geometric content of the record is that the same thirteen codons match a punctured local cube pattern. Its reported face data are
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional tile faces in the finite cubical display used by the experiment. The word ``punctured'' is used only for this finite boundary shape: it names the missing-corner local tile geometry of the row inside $Q_6$. It does not assert that the genetic code is governed by this geometry as a biological law.

\paragraph{Null-model separation.}
The local shape was tested against two finite null families. In the first run, $N=2000$ distinct samples were requested from a sampling process that required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact punctured-shape match count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ high-Jaccard matches at threshold $0.9$. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed companion size was $|M|=20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. This gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite evidence therefore supports the statement that the observed local tile is uncommon under these sampled code-label reshufflings. It does not convert the local tile into a universal law.

\paragraph{Predictive readback rows.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The same run reports a hit rate $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M\setminus T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary. The leave-one-codon-out probe reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These numbers support code-layer recoverability for the local row and its boundary contacts.

\paragraph{Family and module accounting.}
The family split contains $5$ families. The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families. The module classification assigns all thirteen codons in $R$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The six reported modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

$$
This is the strongest finite bookkeeping conclusion of the record: every codon in the punctured row is accounted for by a named local module, and every module has predictable or boundary status.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact tests codon label readback and local codon-tile label splitting. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Consequently, the chapter claim is exactly this: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite punctured-cube boundary row whose standard-code labels are externally read through the curated table. No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
