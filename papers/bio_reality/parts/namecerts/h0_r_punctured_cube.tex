\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table.  The contact used here is deliberately narrow: it supplies codon-to-label readback for the standard genetic code, and it does not supply a mechanism of translation, a folded structure, a physical-admissibility test, a biological-function assay, or a universality theorem.  The internal row under inspection is therefore a code-layer row, not a biological law.  Its distinctions are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The finite row selected by the computation is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independently reported cardinality row gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons.  Thus the object is a small, explicit subset of the $64$ codons in $Q_6$, and every assertion below is about this finite list and its code-label readback.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The row is recorded as a punctured-cube tile with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This means that the realized local complex has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the window-six coordinate geometry.  The description is not that the biological code must be generated by this geometry.  The statement is only that, after the standard-code labels are read back through the curated table, this particular finite row matches the punctured-cube boundary pattern.  The concrete content of the row is especially concentrated in six modules: $\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The module-classification audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  In this finite sense the row is exhausted by the displayed module split.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Two null rows give scale to the shape observation.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ samples, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserves amino-acid class structure.  In that run, $N=2000$, the observed size of $M$ is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The reported exact-shape value is $p=0.0$ at the displayed simulation resolution, and the lower-tail value is $0.0035$.  These numbers support the claim that the observed local row is a non-generic finite code-table pattern under the tested nulls.  They do not promote the row into a mechanism, a translation model, a folding claim, or a universal constraint on biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one-out checks.}
The code-level recovery rows are stronger than a bare descriptive fit.  In the leave-one-code-out row, $23$ tables and $63$ targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$ and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$.  In the leave-one-codon-out row, the anchor count is $0$, the boundary count is $5$, the high-closure coverage is $8$, the predictable count is $8$, and the recovery rate is $1.0$.  These finite rows say that the local coordinate-and-boundary construction has predictive organization at the code-read layer.  The family split is likewise explicit: five families are reported, with hit rates above baseline equal to $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The admissible conclusion is a bounded finite-row conclusion.  The standard-code table supplies external label contact; the six-bit coordinate construction supplies the local tile; the verified row supplies the punctured-cube shape, the module partition, the spectral scalars, and the null-model and leave-one-out scores.  Therefore the record supports the statement that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table.  It does not support the stronger statement that translation itself is explained by this tile.  It also does not support any claim of folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any crossing of those boundaries requires a separate reality contact with its own finite observations.
