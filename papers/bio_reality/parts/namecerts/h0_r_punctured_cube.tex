\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only through the standard-code codon-label table.  In this row the distinguished set $R$ is not an inferred biological mechanism; it is a finite code-read subset of codon labels.  The verified row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus $|R|=13$, and the independently recorded cardinality row agrees exactly with the same thirteen codons.  The geometric certificate records that this set is a punctured-cube tile with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the finite coordinate display used by the pipeline.  The biological readback is narrower: the contact only says that the curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  It does not say that this finite tile is a law of translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary reading.}
The row is best read as a local boundary-tile observation.  The distinguished codons collect familiar code-table sites around stop, arginine, leucine, isoleucine/methionine, lysine, and serine-neighboring positions, but the mathematical certificate does not require those biological names in order to exist.  Its internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data on the six-bit codon display.  The readback into biology enters only through the external table contact.  In particular, the singleton-corner and three-point-class language is code-layer language: it marks finite label neighborhoods inside $Q_6$, not realized molecular structure.

\paragraph{Null comparison against shape coincidence.}
A shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, and found one exact shape match.  The resulting exact-shape probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run found $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid class recorded $N=2000$, observed $|M|=20$, zero exact matches, and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size.  Its finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers make the row difficult to dismiss as a generic random-table artifact, while still leaving the contact layer sharply bounded: they test code-table geometry and label arrangement, not physical realization.

The module-classification row accounts for every codon in $R$.  The assigned modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The classification has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  This is the cleanest finite-row statement of the punctured-cube claim: the local tile, its boundary, and its module decomposition close exactly at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The supported claim is therefore local and finite.  The standard-code table supplies codon-label readback, and the BEDC coordinate pipeline supplies a punctured-cube certificate for the thirteen-codon row.  No translation realization follows from this chapter.  No folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this chapter.  Any such promotion would require a separate reality contact with its own finite observations and its own mismatch audit.  Without that extra contact, the row remains a code-layer boundary tile: strong as a finite codon-table pattern, deliberately silent about higher biological layers.
