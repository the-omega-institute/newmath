\subsection{The punctured codon cube row}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The object examined here is the six-coordinate codon cube obtained by writing each RNA codon as a length-three word over the four nucleotide labels and then reading each nucleotide through a two-bit display. The row under inspection is not the whole cube. It is the finite subset $R$ singled out by the standard-code label table after the coordinate and closure filters have been applied. The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed cardinality is $|R|=13$. The independent cardinality check gives the same thirteen codons and the same size, so the row is not being inferred from a verbal description of the genetic code; it is a concrete finite list with a repeated check against the expected codon names.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The finite shape test identifies $R$ as the punctured local cube row with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Here the first entry counts the thirteen displayed vertices, the second the sixteen local one-coordinate adjacencies retained by the row, and the third the four two-dimensional local faces supported by those adjacencies. This is the chapter-specific geometric claim: the row behaves as a codon-coordinate tile with one local cube pattern punctured by the standard-code labels. It is not a statement that the biological translation apparatus is forced by the cube, nor that the cube supplies a physical or evolutionary mechanism.

\paragraph{Spectral check.}
The same finite row participates in the recorded spectrum calculation. The relevant scalar values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, recorded also as the cubic-polynomial residual. This places the numerical check at machine tolerance rather than at an exact symbolic identity. The useful conclusion is therefore finite and operational: the row has a reproducible spectral signature compatible with the coordinate construction. The calculation does not elevate the codon table into a universal law of biology.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Against the shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the exact shape occurs once, giving $p=0.0005$. High-Jaccard matches at threshold $0.9$ occur twenty-five times, giving $p=0.0125$. The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and saw $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite comparison is visibly constrained by the discreteness of the sampled shape space. A second amino-acid-class-preserving null uses the same sample count and seed. It records observed $M$ size $20$, zero exact matches, and a lower-tail count of seven, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the row is not a typical outcome under the stated finite nulls. They do not by themselves specify the biochemical cause of the standard code.

\paragraph{Readback contact.}
The only reality contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. It tests code-layer readback and local label-split questions. It does not test protein production, folding, physical admissibility, function, organismal fitness, or a universal mechanism. In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is still only a sequence of labels at this level; a folded structure would require a separate structural contact.

\paragraph{Leave-one behavior.}
The row has nontrivial recovery behavior under finite deletion tests. In the leave-one-code-out comparison, $23$ tables are evaluated over $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ with the boundary added is $0.9841269841269841$. In the leave-one-codon-out check, the anchor count is $0$, the boundary count is $5$, the high-coverage closure count is $8$, the predictable count is $8$, and the recovery rate is $1.0$. The finite reading is that the local coordinate tile has strong deletion stability at the code-label layer. The reading is deliberately narrower than a biological realization claim.

\paragraph{Family and module accounting.}
The leave-one-code-out family split records five families. The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families. The module classification accounts for all thirteen row codons with no unassigned codons, no missing module members, and no extra module codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite deletion analysis. The accounting supports the local row claim because it closes the row under the stated module names without adding or dropping codons.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The punctured-cube claim is ready at the finite code-read layer. The verified row is exactly the thirteen-codon set displayed above, its local face vector is $(13,16,4)$, its spectral residual is near numerical zero, and its deletion tests show high finite recovery. The chapter therefore records a local codon-tile boundary in the standard-code table. It does not assert translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality. Any such stronger assertion must be supplied by a separate contact that directly observes the relevant layer.
