\subsection{Punctured Codon Cube at the Code-Read Boundary}
\label{sec:bioreality-punctured-codon-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ read through a standard-code label table.  Each codon is treated as a coordinate point with a code-layer label supplied by the curated table.  The distinguished row set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the same thirteen codons are returned by the independent cardinality row.  The cube-combinatorial readback records the face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed object is not merely a list of exceptional labels: it is a finite punctured-cube tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen local one-dimensional adjacencies, and four local square faces inside the window-six coordinate display.

\paragraph{Local tile content.}
The punctured-cube row is a code-read statement.  It says that the curated standard-code labels, when pulled back to the six-bit codon display, single out a local boundary tile whose vertices are exactly the thirteen codons above.  The internal distinctions used here are coordinate position, closure membership, spectral response, and relation to the standard-code label table.  No biological mechanism is encoded in this carrier.  The table supplies only codon-to-label readback; the geometry supplies only a local finite pattern over that readback.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The associated spectral row reports $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the spectral computation closes to numerical tolerance at the finite-row level.  In this chapter the spectral data are used only as an internal coordinate diagnostic: they say that the observed punctured tile has a stable numerical readback in the chosen finite operator, not that the biological system optimizes or obeys that operator as a law.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite null sample for the shape row, $N=2000$ trials were attempted with seed $42$.  The sampling record contains $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape count is $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while the high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ is $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null reports $N=2000$, exact match count $0$, observed $|M|=20$, and lower-tail count $7$, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row claim that the observed tile is atypical under the tested coordinate nulls.  They do not promote the tile beyond the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The module-classification row partitions all thirteen $R$ codons without residue: $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six reported modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

$$
Every module has predictable-or-boundary status in the recorded classification.  This is the finite-row sense in which the punctured-cube object has internal organization: each listed codon belongs to one and only one named local module, and the modules exhaust the row set.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  It supports the statement that codons have amino-acid or stop labels at the code layer and that these labels can be read back through the six-bit coordinate window.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the chapter-level claim is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically atypical punctured-cube boundary tile in standard-code label readback.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that evidence.
