\paragraph{Label contact.}

\paragraph{Null-model pressure.}
The finite tile is not presented as a free pattern. In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the audit required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and saw $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ high-Jaccard matches at threshold $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$ reports observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0$, and lower-tail value $0.0035$. These rows indicate that the punctured-cube shape is not typical under the audited finite nulls. The conclusion is still local: the tests constrain random code-label rearrangements and amino-acid-class-preserving alternatives, not physical causation.

The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.
