\subsection{Punctured-cube boundary in the window-six codon display}
\label{sec:bior-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.}
The carrier for this record is the finite six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code label table. The contact is deliberately narrow: it supplies codon-to-label readback at the code layer, and it does not supply a translation process, a folded structure, a physical-admissibility test, a function assay, a mechanism, or a universality theorem. Within this finite carrier the distinguished boundary row is the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality row reports $|R|=13$, with exactly the same thirteen codons. The punctured-cube row reports that this same $R$ is the punctured-cube boundary object, with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite statement is not merely that thirteen codons were selected; it is that the selected codons carry a specific local cubical incidence profile inside the window-six coordinate display.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}
The spectral row separates the selected boundary from an arbitrary label readback by giving three scalar coordinates. The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row evaluates the radial cubic relation at $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically zero at the stated tolerance, and records the spectral-radius coordinate as $0.6752479308830213$. A shape-significance probe with $N=2000$ random trials, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$. The corresponding finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It reports observed $M$-size $20$, exact match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{aa,class,exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{aa,class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035$. These numbers support the claim that the finite local shape is not a generic consequence of preserving amino-acid class labels in this sampling regime.

\paragraph{Predictive finite-row behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the boundary-aware rates are much higher: $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M\setminus T$, $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary, and $0.9841269841269841$ above baseline. The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These rows say that the punctured-cube boundary is a usable local readback coordinate for held-out codon behavior in this finite table, not that the geometry is a biochemical law.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module-classification row partitions the thirteen boundary codons without leftovers. It reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing boundary codons, no unassigned codons, and no extra module codons. The six named modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has predictable-or-boundary status in the supplied row. The family-level leave-one-code-out row reports five families, with above-baseline hit rates $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The local verdict is a code-read verdict. The curated standard-code table is a reality contact for codon labels, and the finite rows identify a punctured-cube boundary inside the six-bit codon display. Nothing in this record promotes that boundary to translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity. Such claims require separate contacts of the appropriate kind. Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation; it is not a structure observation and is not a function observation.
