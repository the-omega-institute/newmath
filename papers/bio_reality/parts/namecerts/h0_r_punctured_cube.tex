\begin{aligned}

The singleton-corner and three-point-class distinctions are read at the level of code labels, while the coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation layers remain finite operations on the displayed codon rows.

The distinct-sample accounting reports $57377$ attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the comparison is not presented as a vague randomization claim; it is a finite sampled null with explicit retry pressure. This gives $p=0.0$ for exact shape in the sampled table and $p=0.0035$ for the lower-tail statistic. These numbers support the narrower statement that the local tile is unusual under the stated null procedures. They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Reality contact.} The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact is adequate for reading the code layer: it lets the finite cube points be compared with codon labels and stop or amino-acid classes. It is not a measurement of translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, organismal fitness, or molecular function. The packet therefore stops at the local code-read layer. No claim of translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless an additional contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
