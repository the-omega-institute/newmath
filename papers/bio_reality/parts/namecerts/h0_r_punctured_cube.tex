\subsection{The punctured codon cube as a local boundary tile}\label{sec:bior_reality_h0_r_punctured_cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The packet records a window-six codon display in which each standard codon is treated as a six-coordinate binary-addressable point after the usual two-bit encoding of nucleotide positions.  The distinguished boundary row is the following thirteen-codon set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite check reports $|R|=13$ and identifies the same thirteen names in the cardinality audit and in the punctured-cube audit.  In this row, the observed face data have
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the boundary is not being described merely as a list of labels.  It is a local cubical subconfiguration with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces inside the codon cube.

\paragraph{Label contact.}
The empirical contact used here is only the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It permits a code-layer readout: singleton label corners, three-point label classes, and local face membership can be compared with the standard table.  It does not itself contain ribosomal translation dynamics, structural information about a polypeptide, energetic feasibility, organismal function, or a universal generative mechanism.  Consequently, all conclusions in this subsection remain at the level of codon-label geometry unless a separate observation at a higher biological layer is supplied.

\paragraph{Shape statistics.}
The local shape is rare under the sampled coordinate null.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, the exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A relaxed Jaccard threshold also stayed small: $25$ samples had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a complementary check.  With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed comparison used an $M$-row of size $20$.  The exact-shape count was $0$, while the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0,
\qquad
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the statement that the displayed tile is a non-generic local arrangement relative to the tested finite nulls; they do not identify the physical cause of the arrangement.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The same finite computation reports spectral scalars
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual was $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual.  Thus the spectral check is numerically coherent to the displayed tolerance, with the spectral radius in tolerance equal to $0.6752479308830213$.  This gives a compact algebraic fingerprint of the local row, but it remains a fingerprint of the coordinate-and-label construction, not an independent biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
whereas the reported rates were $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M\setminus T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ after adjoining the boundary row.  The same value $0.9841269841269841$ is reported for the hit rate above baseline.  By family, five rows were tracked: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each had hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  The leave-one-codon-out audit separately reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These finite results say that the local boundary row is useful for code-label recovery tests in the supplied table ensemble.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are exhausted by six named modules.  The AGR module is $\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$; the AR-Lys module is $\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$; the CUN module is $\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$; the Ile/Met module is $\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$; the SSN module is $\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$; and the stop/Trp module is $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The classification audit reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no unassigned codons, and no extra module codons.  Hence the packet is closed as a finite code-layer partition of the punctured-cube row.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The verified content is the following: the standard-code label table contains a thirteen-codon row $R$ whose cubical incidence vector is $(13,16,4)$; the row has non-generic shape statistics in the sampled nulls; its spectral scalars satisfy the recorded radial relation to numerical tolerance; and its codons decompose completely into six code-label modules.  The conclusion is deliberately narrow.  The data support a local codon-table boundary pattern and its finite recovery behavior.  They do not by themselves establish translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a causal mechanism, or a law applying across all biological settings.
