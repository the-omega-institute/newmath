\subsection{Punctured-cube boundary row for the standard-code readback}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The row $h0.R.punctured.cube$ records a code-read phenomenon in the six-bit codon display $Q_6$.  The carrier is the finite codon cube, the readback contact is the curated standard-code label table, and the observed row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is independently reported by the cardinality check $h0.R.cardinality.13$, where $|R|=13$ and the expected codon list is exactly the same thirteen entries.  The geometric finite-row certificate for $h0.R.punctured.cube$ gives the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R) = (13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the object being discussed here is not merely a list of exceptional labels.  It is a local tile in the six-bit coordinate cube with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces.  This is the precise sense in which the row is called a punctured-cube boundary row: the claim is about the finite cubical shape visible after projecting the standard-code label table into window-six coordinates.

\paragraph{Label contact.}
The reality contact used in this chapter is the curated standard-code table.  It assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels and therefore supports code-layer readback only.  In this row the table is used to identify a singleton-label corner and a three-point label class inside the local tile, together with the displayed boundary set $R$.  The contact does not contain a translation experiment, a folding assay, a physical admissibility test, a function measurement, or a universality theorem.  Consequently the finite row can say that the code labels line up with a local punctured-cube pattern, but it cannot promote that pattern to a global biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated spectrum row $h0.spectrum$ supplies a second finite numerical signature.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports a radial cubic residual of $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, with the spectral-radius value in tolerance at $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers are used here as row diagnostics: they say that the $M$- and $R$-side coordinate constructions have a reproducible finite spectral profile.  They do not add any biological layer beyond code readback.  In particular, $\lambda_M$ and $\lambda_R$ are not folding energies, kinetic rates, structural scores, or mechanistic constants.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape-significance row $h0.M.shape.significance$ compares the observed coordinate shape against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  The run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The reported exact-shape probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the high-Jaccard probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null row, $h0.null.model.aa_class_preserving$, used $N=2000$ and seed $42$, with observed $M$ size $20$.  It found $0$ exact matches and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The reported class-preserving exact-shape probability is $0.0$, and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$.  These rows support the finite claim that the observed code-read shape is uncommon under the sampled null models.  They still do not identify a causal mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out check.}
The leave-one-code-out row $h1.leave.one.code.out$ evaluates whether the local tile continues to recover held-out code labels.  It reports $23$ tables evaluated and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  Family-level readback in $h1.leave_one_code_out.by_family$ records $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  These rows strengthen the code-read interpretation: the punctured-cube boundary is not only present in the standard table but remains predictive in finite leave-one-code-out tests.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and modules.}
The leave-one-codon-out row $h1.leave.one.codon.out$ reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification row $h1.leave_one_codon_out.module_classification$ assigns all thirteen $R$ codons with no extras, no missing entries, and no unassigned codons.  The complete module split is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA, UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA, UAG, UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit records $R$ size $13$ and assigned count $13$.  This gives a finite partition of the punctured-cube row into biologically named code-read modules.  The row therefore has a local internal structure: it is not only a cubical boundary and not only a label table lookup, but a thirteen-entry boundary split into six named modules whose union is exactly $R$.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The correct conclusion is deliberately narrow.  The row supports a local code-read statement: in window-six codon coordinates, the standard-code labels expose a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, reproducible spectral diagnostics, uncommon sampled shape statistics, and strong leave-one-out recovery.  Nothing in this chapter shows translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global law of DNA.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still label readback.  It becomes a claim about folding, function, physical chemistry, or mechanism only after a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
