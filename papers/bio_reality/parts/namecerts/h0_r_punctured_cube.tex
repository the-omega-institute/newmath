\subsection{Punctured-cube boundary in the standard-code readback}\label{sec:bior-reality-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code label table.  The observed boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same ordered list in the independent cardinality row.  The cubical face count attached to this row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite observation is not merely a list of codons: it records thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four local square faces inside the window-six display.  This is the precise sense in which the row is called a punctured-cube boundary tile.  The word ``boundary'' here is internal to the codon-coordinate display and to the standard-code label readback; it is not a claim about a physical membrane, molecular mechanism, or global biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The same finite row has a numerical spectral profile.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, reported also as the value of the cubic polynomial at the measured coordinates.  Thus the finite coordinate packet is internally consistent at numerical tolerance: the $R$-row, the ambient $M$-row scalar, and the radial parameter are co-measured rather than freely chosen decorations.  The data supplied here do not enumerate the $M$ codons themselves, but they do give the observed $M$-size comparison used by the null model: $|M|=20$.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sampled row matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null that preserves amino-acid class reported $N=2000$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$ at or below the observed $M$ size, and
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row distinction: the punctured-cube tile is rare under the sampled nulls available in this packet.  They do not by themselves identify an evolutionary cause, a biochemical necessity, or a universal code law.

\paragraph{Module assignment of the boundary row.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned by the supplied module classification.  The six finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  Thus the punctured-cube boundary is not only a geometric subset of $Q_6$; it is also partitioned into six named readback modules whose union is exactly the observed row.  The row-level boundary count is $5$, the predictable count is $8$, and the recovery rate is $1.0$ in the leave-one-codon-out packet.  The same packet records closure coverage high $8$ and anchor count $0$, so the recovery evidence is a boundary-and-closure readback effect rather than an anchor-only phenomenon.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  Family-level rows give five families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$.  These finite rows say that the local tile continues to read back strongly across the sampled table-family deletions.  They do not say that translation is generated by the tile, that folded structure is determined by it, or that the tile is a mechanism.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It can test code-layer readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Therefore the chapter-level claim is deliberately narrow: window-six coordinates expose a finite punctured-cube boundary row in the standard-code label table, with $|R|=13$, $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, the spectral values above, rare null-model shape statistics, complete module assignment, and high leave-one readback rates.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
