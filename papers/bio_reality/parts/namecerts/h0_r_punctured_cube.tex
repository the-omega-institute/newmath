\subsection{The punctured codon cube as a local code-read tile}
\label{sec:bior-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a codon-coordinate display.  Each codon is treated as a finite row in a cube coordinate system, and the biological contact is only the curated standard-code label table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The distinguished row set $R$ is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The direct cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The same finite run identifies this row set as a punctured cube tile with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the object being reported here is not an unrestricted codon law but a finite local face pattern in the six-bit display.

\paragraph{Local shape and spectral signature.}
The punctured-cube row set is accompanied by a spectral check on the associated finite operator.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with radial polynomial residual $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$.  This residual is numerically at tolerance scale for the stated finite computation and supports the claim that the row set has a stable local spectral fingerprint.  It does not by itself say that the fingerprint is a biochemical mechanism.  The readback remains at the level of a curated label table on codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The shape comparison run used $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, giving one exact shape match and twenty-five matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$.  Thus the reported empirical rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null gave observed $M$-size $20$, exact match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ out of $2000$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values make the finite local tile non-generic under the tested controls.  The conclusion is still deliberately narrow: the data support a code-read split in the coordinate table, not translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Module decomposition of the thirteen rows.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are fully assigned to six named modules.  The module classification reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The actual module rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module contains rows classified as predictable or boundary-supported by the finite probe.  This is the strongest local form of the claim: the punctured cube is not merely a cardinality coincidence, but a complete thirteen-row module partition inside the finite code-read display.

\paragraph{Leave-one probes.}
The leave-one-code-out check evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary rows was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  The leave-one-codon-out check found anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These finite rows say that the local code-read tile is recoverable under the specified leave-one probes; they do not say that a missing codon is biologically produced by the coordinate rule.

\paragraph{Reality-contact boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge: a table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, functional realization, folded protein structure, or a universal biological mechanism.  Therefore the admissible conclusion is that the six-bit codon coordinate system exposes a finite punctured-cube boundary tile whose thirteen rows, module partition, spectral values, and leave-one probe behavior align with standard-code label readback.  No claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that evidence.
