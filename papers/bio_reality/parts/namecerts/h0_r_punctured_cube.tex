\subsection{The punctured codon cube row}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon display: each codon is read as a finite word over the four nucleotide letters, with the local comparisons performed inside the window-six coordinate presentation of the codon cube.  The finite row isolates a distinguished boundary set $R$ of thirteen codons,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality row gives $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube row identifies the same thirteen codons as the punctured local cube object.  Its face data are
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite shape has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four local two-faces in the extracted boundary tile.  This is a finite combinatorial statement about the codon-label table.  It is not a statement about ribosomal realization, molecular dynamics, folded structure, selective function, or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The same finite packet also reports a numerical spectrum for the local split.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check is numerically closed at the reported tolerance: both the cubic residual and the radial polynomial value are $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$.  Thus the numerical witness is not merely the set $R$; it includes a reproducible spectral separation between the ambient extracted set $M$ and the punctured-cube boundary row $R$.  The packet supplies the scalar fact that the observed $M$ size is $20$, but it does not include an ordered list of the codons in $M$ in this finite row.  The present chapter therefore records the $M$-side data only through the supplied scalar and spectral quantities.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The finite-row null experiment for shape matching used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and therefore incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that run there was one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null row, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found no exact shape match.  Its lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, with observed $M$ size $20$, so the lower-tail probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the punctured-cube row is a non-generic local pattern under the sampled finite nulls.  They do not license a higher biological conclusion by themselves.

\paragraph{Readback contact.}
The only reality contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is adequate for label readback and for testing whether the thirteen codons listed above form the recorded local boundary tile.  It is not an observation of translation as a physical process, not a folding experiment, not a thermodynamic admissibility test, and not a functional assay.  Consequently an amino-acid label sequence produced from this table remains a code-layer object.  No protein structure, biological role, organism-level mechanism, or universal law follows unless a separate contact explicitly supplies that higher layer.

The reported rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the finite row lists five families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial/plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the local boundary and closure information is predictive for code-label readback tasks under the supplied benchmark.  They do not convert the local codon geometry into a mechanism of translation or a theory of protein form.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module-classification row assigns all thirteen $R$ codons and reports no extras, no missing module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The complete accounting is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The module row gives $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$ and assigned count $13$.  Every listed module has either a predictable member or a boundary member in the finite classification.  This closes the finite bookkeeping for the punctured-cube row: the shape, spectrum, null comparisons, prediction checks, and module assignment all refer back to the same thirteen codons.  The result is a local code-read certificate for a punctured codon cube inside the curated standard-code table, with the scope deliberately stopped before claims about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.
