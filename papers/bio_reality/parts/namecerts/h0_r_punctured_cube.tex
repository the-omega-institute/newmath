\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact, not as a mechanism for translation or protein realization.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the ordinary four-letter codon display.  The distinguished row $R$ is the following thirteen-codon subset:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with exactly the same listed codons.  The row therefore has a concrete finite support before any biological interpretation is attached.  The geometric readout identifies this support as a punctured local cube: its face-vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $13$ is the number of vertices in the row, $16$ is the number of cube edges retained by the induced local tile, and $4$ is the number of square faces retained.  The word ``punctured'' is used in this finite sense: the support has the incidence data of a local cubical patch with one visible omission pattern, not the sense of a global biological law.

\paragraph{Code-label contact.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  That contact assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label, and it resolves only the code-read layer.  It can test whether the thirteen-codon row cuts across the curated label table in a reproducible local way.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity of the geometry.  Thus a codon in $R$ may be read back as a standard-code label, but an amino-acid label sequence is still only a label sequence.  No fold, no activity, no admissible molecule, and no organism-level mechanism follows from the table contact.
