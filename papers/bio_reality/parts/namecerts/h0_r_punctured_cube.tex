\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback layer.}
The finite object in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with the standard-code label table as an external readback contact.  The distinguished subset is the row $R$ of codons selected by the local tile computation:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons appearing in the independent cardinality row.  The geometric certificate reports the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the selected row is not only a list of labels but a finite cubical pattern: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional edges, and four two-dimensional faces in the induced six-bit display.  In this chapter the phrase ``punctured cube'' means exactly this finite-row statement: the selected codons form the recorded local tile shape inside $Q_6$ and are then read back through the curated standard genetic-code table.  The readback contact is a label contact only; it assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels and does not observe translation dynamics, molecular structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

They do not by themselves turn a local codon display into a law of molecular biology, and they do not supply a structure experiment or a functional assay.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which records the finite sampling pressure in the discrete null family.  The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ samples at Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that row the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite evidence is not a vague visual resemblance: the local tile is rare under the sampled shape null and also rare under the amino-acid-class-preserving lower-tail check.  The present data do not include the full explicit $M$ codon list, so this paragraph uses the reported $M$ size and scalar null results rather than reconstructing an unobserved list.

This is the chapter-specific content of the punctured-cube certificate: a thirteen-codon local boundary tile, with a complete module assignment, rare shape statistics under the recorded nulls, stable spectral coordinates, and code-layer recovery behavior.  The certificate is deliberately scoped.  The curated standard-code table is a reality contact for codon-to-label readback, not a contact for translation machinery, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey this geometry as biological necessity.  Any promotion beyond code\_read would require a separate higher-layer contact whose observations are not present in this finite-row record.
