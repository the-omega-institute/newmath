\subsection{The Punctured Codon Cube Row}
\label{sec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record concerns the code-read layer of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a finite coordinate display whose visible distinctions are a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The external contact is only the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels, and it supplies a label readback against which the finite BEDC row can be compared.  It is not a translation experiment, a folding experiment, a physical admissibility test, a function assay, a mechanism, or a universality theorem.

\paragraph{Observed row.}
The verified row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codon list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a count of exceptional codons.  It also carries a local cubical boundary signature: thirteen displayed vertices, sixteen displayed edges, and four displayed square faces in the induced codon-tile geometry.  The label readback remains external, because the standard-code table is the reality contact that tells the row which amino-acid or stop labels are attached to these codons.

\paragraph{Shape against null rows.}
The shape-significance contact used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and saw $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among these alternatives, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}

p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null supplied a second comparison at the same sample size $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that contact, the observed marked mass had size $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed marked mass was $7$.  The reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support the finite claim that the observed punctured-cube row is rare relative to the recorded null generators.  They do not turn rarity into a biological law.

This is the strongest finite-row content of the record: the punctured-cube row is not only a set of thirteen codons, but a fully assigned six-module code-read boundary object.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion is deliberately local.  The data support the statement that window-six codon coordinates expose a punctured local tile in the curated standard-code label table.  They do not support the stronger statement that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  They also do not support translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any law of code evolution.  Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate contact of the relevant kind.  In this chapter, amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation and is not folded protein structure.
