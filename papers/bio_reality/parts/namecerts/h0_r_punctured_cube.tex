\subsection{Punctured-cube boundary in the window-six codon display}\label{sec:bior-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-label layer.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read as a coordinate cube whose displayed vertices are codons and whose labels are the curated amino-acid or stop assignments from the standard code.  The distinguished boundary set $R$ is not inferred as a biochemical cause; it is the finite row selected by the coordinate-and-label computation.  In the verified run $e19917012c60$, the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The companion cardinality check $5d4a3459997d$ gives $|R|=13$ and returns the same thirteen codons.  Thus the claim under discussion is the finite equality between this selected row and a punctured local cube boundary, not a claim about translation dynamics or molecular necessity.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The direct shape certificate reports the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Here the first coordinate records the thirteen displayed codon vertices in $R$, the second coordinate records sixteen local adjacency edges inside the selected tile, and the third coordinate records four local square faces.  The word ``punctured'' is used in this finite combinatorial sense: the displayed local tile has the face incidence pattern of a cube-like codon patch with one missing corner effect, while still remaining a row of codon labels in the standard-code table.  The singleton-corner and three-point label-class distinctions are therefore part of the finite display geometry, not independent biological observations.

\paragraph{Null comparison.}
The unrestricted shape comparison run $0b267c88f8e4$ used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampler records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the row is being compared against a heavily constrained finite universe rather than against an open-ended biological population.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null run $e8d5de747a0c$ also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$; it observed $0$ exact matches, a lower-tail count of $7$ at the observed $M$ size $20$, $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$.  These results support the statement that the displayed code-layer pattern is unusual under the sampled finite null models.  They do not convert the pattern into a universal law of biology.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table as a codon-to-label source.  It supplies the fact that codons receive amino-acid or stop labels in the standard genetic code.  It does not supply a translation experiment, a folded-structure measurement, a physical-admissibility assay, a functional phenotype, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently the local punctured-cube row may be used as evidence for code-layer boundary organization and for local label readback.  It may not be promoted to a claim about protein realization, folding order, biochemical feasibility, organism-level function, or universal biological law unless a separate contact at that higher layer is attached.
