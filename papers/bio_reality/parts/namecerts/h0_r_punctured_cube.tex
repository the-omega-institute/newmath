\subsection{The punctured codon cube row}\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This row treats the standard-code codon table as a finite labelled object at the code-read layer. The coordinate carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, where each codon is represented by a binary window over the four RNA letters. The verified distinguished set $R$ is not introduced as a biological mechanism. It is a finite subset of codon labels selected by the local tile computation and then read against the curated standard-code table. The verified row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality row reports $|R|=13$ and gives the same thirteen codons. The punctured-cube row further reports the face-count vector $(13,16,4)$, so the selected codons form a local cubical surface pattern with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional faces, and four two-dimensional faces. In this chapter, the word \emph{punctured} names that finite cubical shape only: it does not assert that organisms implement the geometry as a law.

\paragraph{Local label split.}
The biological contact for the row is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for checking whether the thirteen selected codons occupy a structured local region of $Q_6$. It is not a translation assay, a folding experiment, a physical-admissibility measurement, or a function test. Thus the observed object is the pairing between a finite coordinate pattern and code labels, not a realized protein or a universal biological rule. The relevant one-codon modules are completely accounted for by the supplied classification: $\mathrm{AGR}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{CUN}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{SSN}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$. The module audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, $13$ assigned codons, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}

They say that the coordinate construction has a stable finite spectral signature when evaluated on the supplied codon table.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The main null sample for the shape row used $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact shape appeared once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. High-overlap shapes with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. A second amino-acid-class-preserving null sample, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p=0.0$ for exact class-preserving shape match, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$. The finite evidence therefore supports the statement that the punctured-cube row is not a routine outcome of the tested randomizations. It still remains a code-table regularity unless another contact is supplied.

\paragraph{Leave-one checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. Family-level leave-one-code-out checks cover $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each have hit rate $1.0$. The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high-coverage closure count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These checks are consistent with a local boundary pattern that is reproducible under finite deletion tests.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The admissible conclusion is narrow. Window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local tile around the thirteen listed codons, with a punctured-cube face vector, a stable spectral signature, statistically rare shape recovery under the supplied null models, complete module assignment, and strong leave-one recovery. The curated code table then supplies codon-to-label interpretation. Nothing in this row demonstrates translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, an organism-level mechanism, or universality. Any claim at those layers would require a separate observation channel with its own finite checks. Without such a channel, amino-acid label readback remains label readback, and the punctured-cube pattern remains a code-layer finite regularity.
