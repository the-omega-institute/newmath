\subsection{A punctured-cube row in the six-bit codon display}
\label{sec:bivoreality-punctured-cube-row}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite display of codon labels.  The observed row $R$ is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row audit reports $|R|=13$ and identifies this same list as the expected codon row.  In the local cubical readout, the same row has the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the object being recorded is not merely a cardinality anomaly: it is a thirteen-codon subset with sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies and four local square faces in the chosen six-bit display.  This is the precise sense in which the row is called a punctured-cube row here.  The phrase names a finite coordinate pattern inside $Q_6$; it does not name a biological law.

\paragraph{Code-label contact.}
The only external contact used by this chapter is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact supports codon-label readback and local label-split checks.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a generative mechanism, or universality.  Thus the record is confined to the code-read layer: a six-bit coordinate pattern is compared with curated labels, and no higher-layer biological conclusion is licensed by that comparison alone.

\paragraph{Spectral row signature.}
The finite spectral probe separates the row $R$ from the companion module $M$ by the reported values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic radial residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is numerically zero at the stated tolerance scale.  These numbers are used only as finite-row invariants of the coordinate construction.  They say that the row has a reproducible spectral readback under the experiment's adjacency and closure conventions; they do not say that molecular translation is caused by the spectrum.

\paragraph{Shape significance against sampled alternatives.}
The shape-significance audit sampled $N=2000$ alternatives with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sampled alternative matched the observed shape exactly, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} = 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ alternatives with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite probabilities support the narrower statement that the observed punctured-cube row is uncommon under this sampled coordinate null.  They do not establish universality of the genetic code, necessity of the standard table, or a physical mechanism for codon assignment.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null preserves amino-acid class information.  In that audit the observed module size was $20$, the exact-shape match count was $0$ among $N=2000$ sampled alternatives, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed module size was $7$.  The reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is stronger evidence that the observed finite arrangement is not a typical draw from the tested class-preserving null, but it remains a statement about the finite row and label table.  It is not evidence of folded protein structure, physical admissibility, or biological function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code readback.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
whereas the hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level refinement used $5$ families and reported hit rates above baseline of $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, with $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  These values support a robust code-layer recovery pattern across the evaluated tables.  They still do not promote the local tile to translation, structure, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon module classification.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe reported anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module classification is complete for all thirteen row codons: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the extra, missing, and unassigned lists are all empty.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite probe.  This gives a row-specific decomposition of the punctured cube into named codon modules, but the names are still read through the curated code-label contact rather than through a direct biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The chapter's claim is therefore intentionally local.  The six-bit coordinate system exposes a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row with $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral values, low sampled null probabilities, high leave-one-code recovery, and a complete leave-one-codon module classification.  The curated standard-code table supplies the label readback needed to interpret the codons as amino-acid or stop labels.  No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer observation.  The finite-row result is ready as a code-read record precisely because its scope is bounded by that contact.
