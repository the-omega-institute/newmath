\subsection{A punctured local cube in the standard-code readout}
\label{sec:bior-reality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate carrier.}
The record concerns a six-coordinate codon display in which each codon is treated as a point of a finite cube $Q_6$. The biological contact is not a mechanism for translation; it is the curated standard-code table that assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label. Within that finite readout, the distinguished boundary set $R$ is exactly the following thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality row reports $|R|=13$ and gives the same ordered codon list. The punctured-cube row reports that this $R$ has face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite shape is not only a label collection but a local cubical boundary pattern with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four square faces. This is the precise mathematical content of the claim: the code-layer table, after the six-bit coordinate display is fixed, contains a small punctured-cube pattern at the listed codons.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The spectral row supplies a second finite diagnostic. The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, recorded both as the cubic-polynomial value and as the radial-polynomial value. At the displayed precision this is a numerical zero check on the reported spectral relation. The same run records the spectral radius in tolerance as $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$.

A separate shape-significance experiment used $N=2000$ random samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. Among those $2000$ samples, exactly one exact shape match occurred, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite run treats the exact punctured-cube shape as rare under the sampled null, while the high-overlap event remains uncommon but less restrictive.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null comparison.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model gives a stronger negative control. With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$. The exact shape match count is $0$, so the reported exact-shape probability under this null is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This comparison is still a finite-row computation, not a biological proof. Its role is to say that the observed code-layer pattern is not reproduced in the sampled class-preserving reshufflings at the reported exact-shape level, and is also low in the reported lower-tail statistic.

\paragraph{Prediction-style readback within the code layer.}
Two leave-one-out rows test whether the local pattern has code-table readback beyond mere restatement. The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. These numbers support a code-read claim: when one code table is held out inside the finite comparison set, the local pattern nearly always lands above the baseline criterion reported by the run.

The leave-one-codon-out run gives the complementary codon-level test. It reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The recovery-rate-above-threshold field is also $1.0$. This says that the codon-level omission experiment is fully recovered by the finite rule used in the run, with the recovered cases divided between predictable and boundary-supported behavior rather than anchored cases.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The module-classification row partitions the same thirteen $R$ codons without extras or omissions. It reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module is recorded as having either predictable or boundary status. The leave-one-code-out family row reports five families. The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families. This family split keeps the evidence finite and stratified: the signal is strongest in four of the five reported families and remains above the baseline criterion in the remaining family.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope limit.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, at codon-label resolution. It supports the statement that the listed codons receive standard-code labels and that those labels can be checked against a local six-coordinate tile. It does not observe ribosomal realization, folded structure, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, a universal generative mechanism, or any necessity law for DNA. The finite cube calculation therefore stays at the code-read layer. A separate translation experiment would be needed before making a translation claim; a separate structural or physical contact would be needed before making a folding, admissibility, or function claim; and cross-organism wording would need organism-matched contacts rather than being inferred from this standard-code table.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
The finite evidence supports the following local conclusion. In the six-coordinate codon display of the curated standard-code table, the thirteen-codon set $R$ listed above forms a punctured local cubical pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, a consistent spectral diagnostic, rare exact occurrence under the sampled shape null, no exact occurrence under the sampled amino-acid-class-preserving null, and strong code-layer recovery in the reported leave-one-out tests. The result is a bounded readback fact about codon labels. It is not evidence, by itself, for translation dynamics, protein folding, physical feasibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.
