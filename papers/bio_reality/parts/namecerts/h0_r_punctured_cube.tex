\subsection{Punctured cube row for a local codon tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim under test.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the label-readout layer.  The finite coordinate carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with codons treated as displayed row labels and with the curated standard-code table used to attach amino-acid or stop labels.  The row selected by the test is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality row gives $|R|=13$, and the independent punctured-cube row returns the same thirteen codons.  Its face-count summary is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite object is not merely a set of exceptional labels: it carries a local boundary-tile shape in the six-coordinate cube.

\paragraph{Finite readout.}
The biological contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table.  It says that codons have standard labels, and it permits one to check whether the selected cube row separates singleton label corners and three-point label classes.  It does not by itself describe translation dynamics, molecular structure, thermodynamic feasibility, or organism-level function.  The row is therefore a code-layer observation: the table supplies labels, while the coordinate construction supplies the local geometry in which the thirteen codons are arranged.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The same finite run reports a spectral comparison between the selected row and the complementary code geometry.  The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically zero at the recorded tolerance.  In the shape null experiment with $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported tail probabilities should be read as finite Monte Carlo summaries over a constrained sampling space rather than as closed-form distributional identities.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A stricter null that preserves amino-acid classes reports $N=2000$, observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite evidence is not only that the thirteen-code row exists, but that its surrounding size and shape are unusual under the recorded class-preserving perturbation.  The data supplied for this chapter include the size of $M$ and the null statistics, but not an explicit codon-by-codon list for $M$; the chapter therefore does not infer any additional membership facts for $M$ beyond the reported size and test outcomes.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  These numbers support a local recoverability statement: when related code tables are used as finite perturbations, the boundary description recovers almost all recorded targets above the baseline rate.

\paragraph{Codon deletion and modules.}
The leave-one-codon-out run records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification is complete for the thirteen selected codons: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the lists of missing, extra, and unassigned codons are all empty.  The module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either a predictable member or a boundary member in the recorded classification.  By family, the hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family, across $5$ families.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite rows justify the following narrow conclusion: the standard-code label table, when read in the six-bit codon cube, contains a thirteen-codon row with punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, coherent module coverage, strong leave-one recovery, and low finite-null frequencies under the recorded sampling procedures.  This is a statement about local codon-label organization.  It is not a statement that the cube geometry causes translation, determines folding, certifies physical admissibility, explains biological function, supplies a molecular mechanism, or establishes a universal law of living systems.  Any such stronger conclusion would require a separate observation at the corresponding biological layer, such as an explicit translation experiment, a structure contact, a physical admissibility test, or a function assay.  Without that added contact, amino-acid label readback remains label readback only.
