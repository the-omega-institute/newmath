\subsection{Punctured codon cube as a local code-read tile}
\label{sec:h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the label-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, where a codon is treated as a finite coordinate display and the observed labels are the curated amino-acid or stop labels attached to that display.  The row singled out by the punctured-cube test is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the same thirteen codons appear in the independent cardinality row.  In the induced local tile complex the measured face count is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite witness is not merely a label list: it also fixes a local cubical boundary profile.  The word ``punctured'' is used here in the literal finite-combinatorial sense that the selected codons occupy a cube-shaped local patch with a missing-corner profile; it is not a claim about molecular holes, folding cavities, or any physical defect.

\paragraph{Coordinate and spectral readback.}
The coordinate-side check is paired with a spectral row.  The reported values are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported as the cubic-polynomial residual, which is numerically at roundoff scale for this finite computation.  Thus the row supplies two compatible descriptions of the same local event: a concrete codon subset with $13$ vertices, $16$ edges, and $4$ square faces, and a spectrum whose registered radius for $M$ is $0.6752479308830213$.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape test was run with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the sampled alternatives there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null row gives a separate constraint: with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact match count was $0$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below that size was $7$.  The corresponding values are $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These numbers support the narrower statement that the finite coordinate profile is unusual under the registered finite null procedures.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The punctured set is completely accounted for by the module classification row.  The six modules are $\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The classification reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has a predictable-or-boundary status, so the local tile is not an isolated numerical coincidence: the same thirteen codons decompose into named code-read modules without remainder.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level row reports $5$ families, with above-baseline hit rates $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  The leave-one-codon-out row gives $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These tests make the local code-read packet more rigid: removal experiments tend to return the omitted target through the registered finite closure or its boundary.

\paragraph{Reality contact and limits.}
The only contact used here is the curated standard-code table, understood as a codon-to-label record.  It can test whether the selected codons and their local split are visible at the code-read layer.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA is forced to obey this coordinate geometry as a biological law.  In particular, reading an amino-acid label sequence from the table is not an observation of a folded protein, and the CUN, UUR, AGR, stop, tryptophan, isoleucine, methionine, lysine, or serine groupings recorded above remain label-table facts until a distinct experimental contact is supplied.  The local conclusion is therefore precise: the standard-code table contains a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary tile with face vector $(13,16,4)$, statistically rare finite shape rows, coherent module coverage, and strong leave-one recovery; all higher biological interpretations remain outside this record.
