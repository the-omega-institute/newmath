\subsection{Punctured-Cube Boundary Row in the Standard-Code Table}\label{subsec:biorereality-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite claim.}
The finite object in this record is the row $R$ cut out inside the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ by the standard-code label table. The carrier is not the molecular translation process itself, but the coordinate display in which each codon is read as a six-bit word and each vertex carries the curated standard-code amino-acid-or-stop label. In that display the verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite run records $|R|=13$, so the row is not being inferred from an asymptotic or model-theoretic limit: it is the concrete thirteen-codon subset listed above. The punctured-cube test reports the face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
meaning that the selected vertices support thirteen displayed codon corners, sixteen local cube edges, and four local square faces. In BioReality language this is a code-read contact: the table supplies labels, the six-bit cube supplies coordinates, and the row $R$ supplies the local boundary object to be inspected.

\paragraph{Boundary geometry.}
The phrase ``punctured cube'' is used here in the finite-row sense. The observed row is large enough to carry several two-dimensional square faces, but it is not the whole cube and it is not a law over all biological code tables. Its content is concentrated in codons touching the $A/C/U/G$ coordinate pattern around $AAA$, $AUA$, the $CUN$ block, the $AGR$ block, and the stop-adjacent $UAA,UAG,UGA$ positions. The module-classification run partitions all thirteen selected codons without leftovers: $AGR=\{AGA,AGG\}$, $AR\_Lys=\{AAA\}$, $CUN=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, $Ile/Met=\{AUA\}$, $SSN=\{UCA,UUA\}$, and $Stop/Trp=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$. The recorded counts are $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, with no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons. Thus the row is not merely a cardinality observation; it is a completely assigned local module boundary inside the codon-coordinate display.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The associated spectral check records $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the radial polynomial check, so the spectral relation is satisfied to numerical tolerance in the finite computation. The sampling check for the $M$-shape used $N=2000$ distinct randomized trials, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{exact}=0.0005,\qquad p_{Jaccard\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model again used $N=2000$ and recorded $0$ exact matches. Its lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, with observed $M$ size $20$, giving $p=0.0035$ for the lower tail and $p=0.0$ for exact shape under the sampled null. These numbers support the statement that the displayed local shape is not a common outcome of the tested finite randomizations. They do not convert the local pattern into a mechanism.

\paragraph{Prediction-style readback.}
Two finite leave-one-out runs test whether the same local boundary is useful as a code-layer readback device. Across $23$ evaluated code tables and $63$ total targets, the leave-one-code-out run reports a baseline hit rate $0.19821739130434785$, a hit rate $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M-T$, and a hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M-T$ plus boundary. The recorded above-baseline hit rate is also $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family, across $5$ families. The leave-one-codon-out run records $anchor\_count=0$, $boundary\_count=5$, $closure\_coverage\_high=8$, $predictable\_count=8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These are code-read numbers: they say that, after a codon or code table entry is withheld, the local coordinate-and-boundary structure recovers many label positions inside the finite curated setting.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, understood as a codon-label bridge. It observes that codons are assigned amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code. It does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any global necessity that DNA must obey the six-bit BEDC display. Therefore the admissible conclusion is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a local punctured-cube boundary row in the standard-code label table, and that row has verified finite combinatorial, spectral, sampling, and leave-one-out support. No translation claim, no folding claim, no physical-admissibility claim, no function claim, no mechanism claim, and no universality claim follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that missing evidence.
