\subsection{A punctured codon cube as a code-read boundary record}\label{subsec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The record concerns the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ together with the curated standard-code label table.  The finite carrier is not used here as a biological mechanism.  It is used as a coordinate display in which a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class can be read against the observed codon-to-label table.  The reality contact is therefore exactly code-layer readback: a curated table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop, and no folding, physical admissibility, translation realization, biological function, mechanism, or universality is supplied by that contact.

The distinguished row $R$ is the following thirteen-codon set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same codon list.  The punctured-cube check identifies this same row as the finite local object and reports the face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed row is not merely a named list of codons: it is a finite coordinate object with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the chosen six-bit display.

\paragraph{Boundary interpretation.}
The phrase ``punctured cube'' is used only for the finite row geometry visible in $Q_6$.  It does not say that the genetic code is forced by cube geometry, that translation is realized by the coordinate system, or that an amino-acid label sequence has acquired folded structure.  The row is a local code-read object.  Its legitimate biological contact is the curated label table, and its legitimate mathematical contact is the finite coordinate and closure computation.  Any claim about protein realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate reality contact.

\paragraph{Shape significance against row reshuffling.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same values are recorded as the exact-shape and high-Jaccard significance values.  The finite conclusion is therefore narrow: within this null experiment, the observed local shape is uncommon.  The conclusion remains at the code-read and coordinate layer.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null experiment preserved amino-acid class.  It again used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  The observed $M$ size was $20$.  No exact shape match occurred, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the finite-row reading because the row remains atypical even under a label-class preserving comparison.  It still does not supply a higher-layer biological contact.

These are code-layer recovery statistics: they measure whether the finite row-and-boundary construction predicts held-out label-table behavior, not whether any physical translation process has been reconstructed.

The leave-one-codon-out run reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The recovery-rate-above-threshold value is also $1.0$.  This indicates that the local row is useful as a code-read boundary device.  The absence of anchors is important: the evidence is not an anchor-based biological derivation, but a finite boundary-and-closure phenomenon checked against curated labels.
