\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of codon coordinates together with an externally supplied label table.  The distinguished subset $R$ is the row actually exposed by the finite computation:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the cubical boundary count attached to this row has $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the finite datum is not merely a set of codon names: it is a thirteen-vertex local tile with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces inside the six-bit codon display.  The biological contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That table is treated as an external reality bridge for label readback, not as a mechanism, not as an evolutionary necessity statement, and not as a universality proof.

\paragraph{Shape and spectrum.}
The finite row has two independent numerical signatures in the supplied runs.  The direct shape run reports that $R$ is the punctured-cube row above and that its $f$-vector is $(13,16,4)$.  A separate shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled trials with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match and twenty-five matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, after $57377$ distinct sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The corresponding rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers say that the displayed local tile is a finite-row anomaly relative to the sampled coordinate null, while still remaining a code-layer anomaly.  The spectral run gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports a radial polynomial value $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$ and the same value for the cubic-polynomial residual, so the numerical spectral certificate is at tolerance level rather than an exact symbolic identity.  The reported spectral radius in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$, agreeing with $\lambda_M$ as the controlling row-scale value.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class preserving null gives a sharper separation from label-preserving randomization.  With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact shape count is $0$.  The observed $M$-size is $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below that observed size is $7$.  The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact\ shape}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite row data therefore supports the following restricted conclusion: under the supplied coordinate and label-preserving probes, the observed local codon tile is not behaving like a typical shuffled label-class row.  The record does not contain an explicit codon list for $M$; it contains the scalar datum $|M|=20$ and the recovery statistics involving $M-T$ and boundary enlargement.  Accordingly, the text records $M$ only through those supplied finite scalars and does not invent an unobserved $M$ list.

\paragraph{Leave-one probes.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus the recovery behavior is concentrated in the same local-tile-plus-boundary region that the finite row exposes.  The leave-one-codon-out run reports no anchors, five boundary codons, eight high-coverage closure codons, eight predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  In finite terms,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\ count} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count} &= 5,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high} &= 8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\ count} &= 8,\\

$$
The evidence therefore supports a local readback claim: the row and its boundary organize missing-code and missing-codon probes at the label level.  It does not support a higher biological claim by itself.

These figures make the local-tile reading more specific: the thirteen codons are not only a cardinality anomaly; they are partitioned into named finite modules whose leave-one behavior remains concentrated at or above the baseline in the supplied family table.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported claim is exactly this: in the six-bit codon display, the curated standard-code label table exposes a thirteen-codon row $R$ with punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, nontrivial sampled shape scores, the reported spectral scalars, complete module assignment, and strong leave-one recovery at the code-read layer.  The contact tests codon labels and local label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure.  No conclusion about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, global biological law, or universal mechanism follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that missing evidence.
