\subsection{The Punctured-Cube Row in the Standard-Code Readback}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record works only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The finite row singled out by the punctured-cube probe is
$R=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons and no extra row elements.  Thus the object under discussion is not an open-ended biological mechanism; it is a finite labelled subset of the codon cube, tested against the label table as an external reality contact.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube incidence.}
The row has the recorded face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $f_0=13$ is the number of listed codon vertices, $f_1=16$ is the number of local one-coordinate adjacencies retained by the row, and $f_2=4$ is the number of local square faces retained by the row.  The phrase ``punctured cube'' is therefore used in the finite incidence sense: the row behaves like a local cube-shaped tile with one or more missing boundary positions when viewed inside the six-bit codon display.  This is a statement about the combinatorics of the labelled codon table, not about DNA dynamics, ribosomal translation, or protein structure.

\paragraph{Spectral row check.}
The same finite row is accompanied by a spectral certificate.  The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same residual is reported as the cubic-polynomial residual.  This places the numerical row in tolerance for the finite spectral test, with spectral radius $0.6752479308830213$.  The role of this number is diagnostic: it says that the row sits in a reproducible finite coordinate-and-relation calculation.  It does not upgrade the row to a physical law.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the recorded finite null sample with $N=2000$, the exact shape match count is $1$, so the exact-shape probability estimate is $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ is $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The run used seed $42$ and required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  These figures support the local claim that the observed punctured-cube row is not a common output of the tested randomization regime.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed $M$-size $20$, exact match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  Since the explicit list of the $20$ elements of $M$ is not present in the finite-row data, the chapter uses only the supplied scalar $|M|=20$ and does not infer an unreported $M$ row.

\paragraph{Readback prediction tests.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate in $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$ and the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline is therefore $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out experiment reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These readback tests are consistent with the row behaving as a local boundary-sensitive tile: withheld codon labels tend to be recovered by the finite coordinate closure or by the explicitly marked boundary.  They still test only code-label readback.

\paragraph{Module-level accounting.}
The row is fully assigned by the supplied module classification.  The modules are $\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The classification reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  At the family level there are $5$ families.  The hit-rate-above-baseline values are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate.  Thus the finite row is not merely a list of isolated codons; it is also stable under the supplied module partition and family readback checks.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code bridge: a table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read claims and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity.  Consequently the supported conclusion is narrow: window-six coordinates expose a finite punctured-cube boundary row in the standard-code label table, with $13$ codons, face-vector $(13,16,4)$, significant finite-shape statistics, spectral consistency, and strong readback recovery under the supplied probes.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
