\paragraph{Finite object.}
This BioReality packet records a code-layer pattern in the standard genetic-code table.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube, read only through a curated codon-to-label table.  The distinguished row $R$ is the following thirteen-codon set:
$$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen displayed codons.  The cell complex associated to this row has the verified face-vector

\end{aligned}

Thus the row is not being summarized by an informal visual impression: it is a finite subconfiguration with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four local faces inside the window-six codon display.

\paragraph{Shape significance against randomized tables.}
A shape-level randomization test used $N=2000$ sampled trials with seed $42$.  Because the sampling required distinct code-like objects, the run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found no exact shape matches.  In that run the observed companion size was $|M|=20$, the lower-tail count at or below that observed size was $7$, the exact-shape value was $0.0$, and the lower-tail value was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row therefore has finite empirical pressure in both the unrestricted shape test and the amino-acid-class-preserving test, while still remaining a code-table observation.

\paragraph{Biological reading and boundary.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for testing the local tile split described above.  It is not a measurement of translation dynamics, ribosomal realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently, the packet supports the claim that the window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table.  It does not support any statement that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity, nor any inference from amino-acid label sequence to protein fold or function.  Any future promotion beyond the code layer must introduce a separate observational contact at the relevant layer.
