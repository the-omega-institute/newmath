\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ read only at the code-read layer.  The row $R$ is the observed local boundary set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row has cardinality $|R|=13$, and the independent cardinality check returns the same thirteen codons: $AAA$, $AGA$, $AGG$, $AUA$, $CUA$, $CUC$, $CUG$, $CUU$, $UAA$, $UAG$, $UCA$, $UGA$, and $UUA$.  In the finite cube complex induced by the window-six coordinate, this row has the punctured-cube face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the claim is not merely that thirteen codons were selected; it is that the selected codons occupy a local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces after the puncture is imposed.

\paragraph{Label contact.}
The only external contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  This contact has resolution ``codon label readback.''  It tests code-read statements and local codon-tile label splits, but it does not test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization.  The biological object is therefore the standard-code codon table as a label table, not a mechanism.  The BEDC minimal form records the carrier $Q_6$, the distinctions ``six-bit codon display,'' ``local tile face,'' ``singleton label corner,'' and ``three-point label class,'' and the internal structure ``coordinate,'' ``closure,'' ``spectrum,'' and ``relation.''

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape and high-Jaccard significance fields repeat these same values, $0.0005$ and $0.0125$.  A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, found observed module size $20$, exact match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ for rows at or below the observed module size. These tests support the statement that the local tile is unusual relative to the sampled finite nulls, not that the codon table was generated by the geometry.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported claim is narrow: the window-six codon coordinate exposes a finite local boundary tile whose row is the thirteen-codon punctured-cube set $R$, with face vector $(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral values, significant finite-null behavior, and strong leave-one recovery at the code-read layer.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, universal law, or necessity of DNA geometry follows from this row unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
