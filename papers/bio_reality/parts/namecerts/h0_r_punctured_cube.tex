The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code table as a codon-to-label contact. The observed row set $R$ is not introduced as a mechanism, a law of translation, or a structural principle. It is the finite subset of codon names whose code-layer labels participate in the local boundary-tile pattern detected by the coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation probes. \begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.

Thus the local object being discussed is exactly this thirteen-row subset of the standard-code label table, not the entire genetic-code mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance and class-preserving null rows.}
The unconstrained shape null used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. Among those samples, the exact shape appeared once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while the high-Jaccard event $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reported observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$. These rows support the claim that the local tile is not a generic random shape under the tested finite nulls. They do not promote the tile to a biological law.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The reality contact is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact can test code-read behavior and the local codon-tile label split. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that the BEDC geometry is a biological necessity. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not protein structure. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, not folding, not physical admissibility, not function, not mechanism, and not universality. Any one of those higher-layer claims would require a separate contact supplying observations at that layer.
