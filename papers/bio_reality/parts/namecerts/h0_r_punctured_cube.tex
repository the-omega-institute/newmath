\subsection{The punctured codon cube as a local code-layer tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The record considered here is the finite codon window in which each standard-code codon is represented as a six-bit coordinate.  The claim is not that biology is forced by this coordinate system.  The claim is narrower: after the standard-code labels are supplied by a curated codon table, the coordinate display isolates a finite boundary tile whose distinguished row is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The direct cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the same thirteen codons appear in the punctured-cube computation.  The associated cell count is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed local object is a thirteen-vertex boundary patch with sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies and four square faces.  This is the finite geometric content of the row: a six-bit codon cube contains a labeled punctured tile, and the curated standard table supplies the amino-acid or stop readback attached to its vertices.

\paragraph{Label and module content.}
The thirteen codons are not a featureless set.  The module classification partitions all of them without remainder.  The stop-tryptophan boundary module is $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$, the arginine pair is $\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, the lysine-adjacent singleton is $\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, the isoleucine-methionine singleton is $\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, the four-codon leucine block is $\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, and the mixed serine/leucine pair is $\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.  The row-level accounting reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  Thus the local tile is fully accounted for at the code-label level: every vertex in the punctured cube receives a module role, and every module vertex lies in the displayed row.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling evidence.}
The finite spectrum supplies a second numerical description of the same local pattern.  The reported values are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic check is numerically zero at the recorded precision, with value $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius check returns $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers are used only as finite-row diagnostics for the code-layer tile.  They do not turn the coordinate display into a biochemical mechanism.

The shape test against sampled alternatives gives $N=2000$ trials, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sampled case matched the observed shape, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  Twenty-five sampled cases reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$, the exact-shape count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$.  These finite statistics support the statement that the tile is not an arbitrary-looking row artifact under the tested null models; they do not identify a translation process, folded structure, physical admissibility condition, function, or universal biological rule.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables over $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid tables, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate tables, $1.0$ for the candidate-other group, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast group, across five families.  The leave-one-codon-out probe reports $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, $0$ anchors, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These tests are consistent with a robust code-layer boundary pattern: removed labels or codons are often recovered by the local coordinate-and-boundary information.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The empirical contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  Its observed content is a codon-to-label assignment: codons receive amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is sufficient for code readback and for testing whether the finite tile splits labels locally.  It is not a ribosome experiment, not a folding assay, not a thermodynamic admissibility test, not a functional selection measurement, and not a proof that every biological system must obey this geometry.  The amino-acid label sequence obtained from codons remains a label sequence.  No folded protein structure, translation realization, physical possibility claim, biological function, general mechanism, or universality statement follows from this row unless another contact, designed for that higher layer, is added separately.

\paragraph{Chapter verdict.}
The finite-row verdict is therefore positive but local.  The punctured-cube row is exactly the thirteen-codon set displayed above, has cell vector $(13,16,4)$, admits a complete module assignment, shows nontrivial spectral diagnostics, and performs strongly in the leave-one tests.  The finding is a code-read phenomenon about a finite codon-coordinate tile anchored by a curated standard-code table.  Its scientific boundary is equally part of the result: the record supports local codon-label structure and blocks promotion to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or biological universality without a distinct higher-layer observation.
