\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This BioReality record concerns the claim \texttt{h0.R.punctured.cube}.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon-label table.  The observed reality contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and this contact tests only code-layer label readback and local codon-tile splits.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

The same finite row is independently reported as the punctured-cube row, so the code-layer object being discussed is not an informal picture but a concrete thirteen-codon subset of $Q_6$.  Its verified face data are
$$
\begin{aligned}

f(R)&=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Here the first entry is the number of row vertices, the second is the number of local one-dimensional adjacencies retained by the row, and the third is the number of local tile faces seen by this finite extraction.  The phrase ``punctured cube'' is therefore used only for this finite combinatorial pattern: a thirteen-point boundary row with sixteen retained edges and four retained local faces inside the six-bit codon display.

\paragraph{Shape significance under null probes.}
Against the unrestricted sampled shape null, the finite-row shape was tested with $N=2000$ sampled tables, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, of which $55377$ were duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  Against the amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row interpretation is that the observed local tile shape is uncommon under both reported null probes.  The interpretation is not that the tile is a global biological law.  The contact remains a codon-label table, not a mechanism contact.
