\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table only as a curated code-layer contact: a finite table assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or stop label. $$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality row reports the same membership list and size $|R|=13$. The punctured-cube row reports the finite face data

\paragraph{Local tile interpretation.}
The chapter-specific claim is that these thirteen codons form a local boundary tile inside the six-bit codon display. The biological readback is the standard-code label table, not an intrinsic biological necessity. Thus the phrase ``punctured cube'' is used here as a finite coordinate statement about $R\subseteq Q_6$: the observed row supplies a membership set and an $f$-vector, and the curated table supplies the codon-label contact needed to say which code labels sit on that row. The claim stays at the code-read layer. It does not assert that translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from this geometry.
