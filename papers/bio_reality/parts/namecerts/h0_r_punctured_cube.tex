\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
This BioReality record concerns the finite row \texttt{h0.R.punctured.cube}.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only through a curated standard-code label table.  The observed row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the codon set \texttt{AAA}, \texttt{AGA}, \texttt{AGG}, \texttt{AUA}, \texttt{CUA}, \texttt{CUC}, \texttt{CUG}, \texttt{CUU}, \texttt{UAA}, \texttt{UAG}, \texttt{UCA}, \texttt{UGA}, and \texttt{UUA}.  The same thirteen codons appear in the cardinality check and in the punctured-cube equality check, so the row is not merely counted correctly; its membership is fixed at the codon level.  The verified face data for this row are
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|&=13,\\
f(R)&=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite shape should therefore be read as a local boundary tile in the codon-coordinate display: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces inside the window-six cube.  The record does not assert that this tile is a biological mechanism.  It asserts that, after the standard-code labels are attached as external contacts, this particular row can be recognized as a punctured-cube boundary pattern.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read behavior and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, function realization, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the punctured-cube row is a code-layer certificate: it says that the standard-code label table exposes a finite local boundary tile on $Q_6$ with row $R$, face vector $(13,16,4)$, the recorded spectral values, and the stated probe behavior.  No claim follows here that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  No claim follows that an amino-acid label sequence is a folded structure.  No claim follows about protein function, physical admissibility, or a universal mechanism unless a separate higher-layer contact is supplied.
