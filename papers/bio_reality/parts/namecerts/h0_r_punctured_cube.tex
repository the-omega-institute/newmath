\subsection{Punctured Cube Boundary in the Standard Code}
\label{subsec:h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite record.}
The record concerns a six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$ whose observable labels are supplied by the curated standard genetic-code table.  The finite subset singled out by the local tile computation is the thirteen-codon row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the independent punctured-cube computation returns the same list.  The face-count signature is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object is not merely a named list of codons: it carries a small cubical boundary profile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces.  This is the finite shape read at the code-label layer.  It is not asserted to be a biochemical mechanism, nor is it asserted to determine translation, folding, physical feasibility, cellular function, or cross-organism universality.

\paragraph{Label contact.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is strong enough to test codon-label readback and the local split of the thirteen-row tile, but it contains no observation of ribosomal realization, no folded structural state, no thermodynamic admissibility statement, and no functional assay.  Consequently, the codon row may be compared with the standard labels, but any claim about protein shape or biological role would require a separate contact of the appropriate kind.  The amino-acid label sequence is therefore treated as a code-layer observation rather than as an observed protein object.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral computation associated with the finite split reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check.  At the displayed numerical precision this residual is effectively zero for the finite computation, and the spectral-radius entry agrees with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers are used only as finite coordinates of the code-row geometry.  They do not promote the row to a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was exactly $1$ exact-shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid class again used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that run the observed comparison size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  Thus the reported exact-shape value is $0.0$, while the lower-tail value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite-row tests support the claim that the displayed row is not a typical draw under the recorded null procedures.  They do not, by themselves, identify a physical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one evidence.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-resolved row had $5$ families: alternative yeast at $0.8333333333333334$, bacterial plastid at $1.0$, candidate other at $1.0$, mitochondrial at $1.0$, and nuclear ciliate at $1.0$.  The leave-one-codon-out record has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the row is not only recovered as a static punctured cube; it also remains visible under finite deletion probes at the code-label level.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen codons are partitioned by the module classification without leftovers.  The complete assignment has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The module rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}

Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite probe.  This accounting is important because it prevents the punctured-cube row from being treated as an unstructured coincidence of thirteen names.  The row is a code-layer finite object with a complete module ledger, a small cubical face signature, spectral coordinates, null-model separation, and deletion-probe stability.

\paragraph{Scope.}
The supported conclusion is narrow.  In the standard-code label table, the thirteen codons $AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA$ form the recorded punctured-cube boundary row with face vector $(13,16,4)$ and the numerical evidence just stated.  The result is a finite local code-read pattern.  It does not say that organisms must instantiate this geometry, that translation is explained by it, that a protein fold follows from it, that physical admissibility has been measured, that function has been demonstrated, or that a universal biological mechanism has been found.  Those stronger statements remain blocked until independent contacts of the corresponding biological layer are supplied.
