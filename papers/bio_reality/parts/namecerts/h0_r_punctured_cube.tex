\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-bit display of the standard-code codon table.  The biological contact used here is only the curated codon-to-label table: it assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label, and it supplies no independent realization of translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity.  The claim tested in this row is therefore local and code-layer only: a distinguished row $R$ in the codon cube has the combinatorics of a punctured local tile, while the labels that make the row biologically readable remain external contacts.

The separate cardinality row gives the same set and records $|R|=13$.  The punctured-cube computation reports the face-vector match

This is the precise finite-row content of the phrase ``punctured cube'' in this chapter.  No claim is made that $Q_6$ itself is a law of biology; it is a coordinate display on which the curated code table can be read.

The finite evidence supports the following bounded conclusion: in the six-bit codon display, the curated standard-code labels expose a thirteen-codon row $R$ whose induced local tile has punctured-cube combinatorics, nontrivial spectral values, significant null-model pressure, and stable leave-one-code and leave-one-codon readback behavior.
