\subsection{The punctured codon cube row}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns a window-six codon coordinate system for the standard-code table. The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through codon labels supplied by a curated standard-code table. The row under examination is the subset $R$ detected as a local boundary tile. The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the independent punctured-cube check reports the same thirteen codons. Its cell counts are the finite vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the object is not merely a list of labels: it has a reproducible local cubical boundary profile inside the six-coordinate display.

\paragraph{Contact with the code table.}
The biological contact used here is narrow. A curated standard genetic-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels, and this contact supports codon-label readback at the code layer. It does not observe ribosomal translation as a process, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a general mechanism. Those higher claims require separate contacts of their own. In this row, the code table is therefore used only to attach standard-code labels to the finite codon subset and to ask whether the window-six tile has a local boundary pattern.

\paragraph{Spectral row data.}
The finite coordinate calculation also supplies a spectral signature. The verified scalar values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial polynomial residual was reported as $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, with the same value recorded for the cubic-polynomial satisfaction check. This is numerically at the tolerance scale of the computation, so the row has a stable finite spectral signature in the coordinate model. The spectral data are still code-coordinate data; they do not by themselves convert a codon label pattern into a molecular structure or a biological function.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the shape null model with $N=2000$ sampled trials and seed $42$, the row had one exact shape match. The reported exact-shape probability was $p=0.0005$. The same run used $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, showing that the randomization space was heavily constrained by the finite sampling condition. A high-overlap comparison gave $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold $0.9$, hence $p=0.0125$. These values support the statement that the observed row is a non-generic local tile in the tested coordinate ensemble. They do not support any claim that DNA must realize this tile as a universal law.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid classes. In $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, so the reported exact-shape value was $0.0$ at the finite resolution of the experiment. The observed $M$-size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below that size was $7$, giving a lower-tail value of $0.0035$. Thus the local shape persists even under a stricter label-class constraint. The data identify a finite code-layer asymmetry, not a protein-level consequence.

\subsection{Predictive checks around the row}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube-predictive-checks}

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$. These numbers say that the finite row is not an isolated artifact of a single display: when labels are held out at the code-table level, the local coordinate context recovers almost all targets under the stated rule. The experiment remains at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The codon-level holdout run reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closures, and $8$ predictable cases. Its recovery rate was $1.0$, and the above-threshold recovery value was also $1.0$. This is a strong finite-row check: every tested codon-level omission in the recorded setting was recovered by the local coordinate closure or boundary condition. The result does not say that the omitted codon is physically produced, translated, folded, or functional; it says that the window-six code-coordinate row has complete recovery in this finite holdout test.

\paragraph{Family-level table comparison.}
The family-stratified leave-one-code-out run used $5$ families. The recorded hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid tables, $1.0$ for the candidate-other group, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate tables. The non-unit alternative-yeast value is important: it prevents the row from being described as an automatic invariant across all biological table families. The family data instead support a more modest statement, namely that the punctured-cube row is broadly but not uniformly recovered in the tested code-table variants.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned by the module classification. The recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness check gives $R\_size=13$ and $assigned\_count=13$, with no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons. This module partition is the most compact local description of the row: CUN contributes four codons, the stop/tryptophan boundary contributes three, AGR and SSN contribute two each, and the arginine/lysine and isoleucine/methionine sites each contribute one.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The row $R$ is therefore a verified punctured-cube pattern in the six-bit codon display, with $13$ codons, face-vector data $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral signature, non-generic shape probabilities under two null comparisons, strong leave-one-code and leave-one-codon recovery, and a complete six-module classification. The scientific interpretation is deliberately local. The curated table gives label assignments, and the coordinate system exposes a finite boundary tile among those labels. No translation event, folded protein structure, physical admissibility result, biological function, global mechanism, or universality statement follows from this row unless an independent contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
