\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table only as a finite label readback contact.  The carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, with each codon read as a finite coordinate in a window-six cube.  Inside this carrier, the distinguished row $R$ is not introduced as a biochemical law; it is a finite local tile selected by the code-layer table.  The observed row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the same computation identifies this row with the punctured-cube tile whose face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite claim is concrete: thirteen codon vertices, sixteen local adjacency edges, and four local tile faces are present in the selected code-read row.

\paragraph{Local boundary interpretation.}
The punctured-cube statement is a boundary statement about the local codon display.  It says that the row $R$ has the finite shape of a punctured cube in the window-six coordinate system, not that DNA, translation, or proteins are governed by this geometry.  The distinction list for this record is therefore intentionally narrow: six-bit codon display, local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class.  The internal structure used by the record is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation.  The readback is the curated standard-code label table, whose observed content is only that codons receive amino-acid or stop labels.

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was met by $25$ samples, giving

\begin{aligned}

Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-row size was $20$.  The exact shape count was $0$, so the recorded exact-shape value is $0.0$, while the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving $0.0035$.  These finite-row tests make the tile non-generic under the stated null models, but the null models still live inside the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The reality contact for this chapter is the curated standard genetic-code bridge.  It can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that window-six geometry is a biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  The punctured cube is therefore a finite local boundary tile in the standard-code label table: it is ready as a code-read claim, and it remains blocked for translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, and universality unless separate higher-layer contacts supply those facts.
