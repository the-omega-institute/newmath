\subsection{A punctured local cube in the standard codon table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-punctured-cube}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns a window-six display of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only at the code-label layer: codons are compared through their tabulated amino-acid or stop labels, and no claim is made about the biochemical process that realizes translation.  The finite row isolates a distinguished subset $R$ of codons,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality row gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen codons.  The punctured-cube row reports the face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object is not merely a thirteen-element list.  It carries a small cubical incidence profile: thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four square faces.  The word ``punctured'' is used here in this finite combinatorial sense, namely that the row has the face profile of a local cube-shaped patch with a missing or boundary-sensitive part, not in the sense of a topological theorem about the full biological code.

\paragraph{Label contact.}
The only empirical contact used in this chapter is the curated standard-code label table.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments and therefore permits code-layer readback of singleton label corners, three-point label classes, local faces, and the thirteen-codon subset above.  It does not supply ribosome dynamics, tRNA abundance, folding energetics, organismal fitness, or any law of genetic-code evolution.  Thus the table can check whether a local tile split is visible in the label data, but it cannot certify a mechanism behind the split.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The finite spectrum attached to the same packet records $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported radial polynomial residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is numerically negligible at the precision of the experiment.  The spectral-radius entry repeats $0.6752479308830213$ as the value in tolerance.  These numbers give a reproducible numerical signature of the local codon geometry, but the signature remains a code-table statistic.  It is not evidence for folding, physical admissibility, functional advantage, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The random-shape comparison used $N=2000$ distinct samples, generated with seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the accepted samples there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model used the same sample count and seed.  In that row the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the narrow statement that the finite local shape is not a common outcome under the recorded null procedures.  They do not identify a causal biological principle.

\paragraph{Predictive rows.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$.  The by-family row separates the same behavior across $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  These are strong finite-row summaries for code-table prediction, but they remain conditional on the table family and the chosen leave-one-code-out protocol.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out boundary.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, closure coverage high $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The recovery-rate threshold row is also $1.0$.  Hence every tested missing codon is recovered either by the predictable part of the local configuration or by the recorded boundary part.  The absence of anchors is important: the row is not saying that one codon has privileged causal authority.  It is saying that the finite local tile and its boundary bookkeeping are sufficient for the tested code-layer recovery task.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons of $R$ are exhausted by six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row records $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Thus the punctured-cube subset is not an unlabeled residue of the computation: it is fully partitioned into biologically legible codon modules at the label-table level.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The chapter-level claim is therefore limited and finite.  In the standard-code table, the thirteen-codon set $R$ forms a local cubical boundary pattern with face vector $(13,16,4)$, a reproducible spectral signature, nontrivial null-model separation, complete module assignment, and strong leave-one-out recovery statistics.  This justifies treating the row as a code-read phenomenon: a structured local patch in the codon label table.  It does not justify promotion to translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, evolutionary mechanism, or universal biological law.  Any such higher-layer statement would need a separate contact that observes the corresponding layer directly.
