\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The saturation computation is therefore a finite coordinate fact, and the ORF rows are contact-conditioned biological checks. The chapter-specific conclusion is that third-coordinate saturation reaches all listed ambient codons except $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$, while the curated ORF contact separately supports interval-level eligibility classification on the stated dataset. No translation event, folded molecular structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic explanation, or universal law follows from the saturation set, the quotient geometry, or start/stop enumeration alone. Those stronger biological claims require their own contacts and their own controls. Within the present scope, the admissible statement is only that the finite saturation row is exact, the ambient and motif rows agree with it, and the ORF-eligibility evidence remains confined to externally supplied interval annotations and the explicitly reported finite datasets.
