\paragraph{Biological reading.}
The biological object named by this row is open-reading-frame eligibility from start and stop codon enumeration.  The saturation identity is therefore read as a finite eligibility screen: it classifies which codon triples are reached by the declared third-face closure and which two mediator elements, $ACA$ and $ACG$, are outside that particular saturation image.  This is enough to describe a boundary for an ORF-eligibility test, but not enough to assert an observed ORF.  A start/stop pattern can define a candidate interval only after an outside sequence, transcript, or curated annotation contact supplies the biological interval being tested.

The row explicitly carries that restriction.  The seed form says that the carrier, distinctions, and readback all remain awaiting contact with biological records, while the internal structure has no additional content.  The two probes make the same point operationally.  Probe $orf.eligibility.boundary.requires.contact$ breaks if the pipeline asserts ORF eligibility, translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, or biological function from start/stop enumeration alone.  Probe $orf.eligibility.contact.gap.check$ breaks if an ORF-eligibility claim is recorded while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation can test the proposed interval.

\paragraph{Local boundary of the claim.}
The finite identities for $R$, $M$, and $Sat_{f_3}(R)$ justify only a combinatorial eligibility boundary.  They do not imply translation, folding, physical admissibility, cellular function, biochemical mechanism, organism-level role, or a universal biological law.  The curated interval contact supplies evidence for its own declared dataset and protocol, but it also leaves the same limits in force: the classifier rules were frozen before external observation, the analysis did not use translation or structure input, and the contact does not classify windows outside that dataset.  Thus the scientific content of this subsection is a finite codon-geometry packet plus a separately testable ORF-eligibility interface.  Any stronger biological assertion requires a further contact that directly measures or curates the stronger layer.
