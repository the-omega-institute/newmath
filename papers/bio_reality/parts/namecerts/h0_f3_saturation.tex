\subsection{Finite saturation at the ORF-eligibility boundary}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-f3-saturation}

\paragraph{Finite codon packet.}
This record isolates the finite saturation claim behind the open-reading-frame eligibility boundary.  The biological object under discussion is not a translated product, a folded molecule, or a functional annotation.  It is the much weaker question of whether a finite start/stop-window classifier can be kept inside an explicitly enumerated codon packet before any biological interval is allowed to promote it.  The verified finite universe $M$ has cardinality $20$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
A smaller motif-compressed row $R$ is verified as the $13$-element set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The motif check reports no missing codons and no extra codons for this representation.  Thus the packet begins as a finite combinatorial certificate: it names the codons present in the row and records that the compressed row accounts for precisely the motifs it claims to account for.

\paragraph{Saturation computation.}
The $f_3$ saturation run verifies that the mediated closure from $R$, together with the boundary codon $\mathrm{ACA}$, recovers the same $20$-element row $M$ displayed above.  The computed saturation itself is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is $M$ with $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$ withheld from the saturated row.  The verified boundary residual for $f_3$ is empty:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\partial_{f_3}=\varnothing .
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite claim is therefore narrow and exact.  In this row, the saturation operation reaches all non-withheld members of $M$, and the named boundary computation leaves no additional residual codon to discharge.  It does not say that an open reading frame has been observed.  It says only that this finite codon geometry has the stated closure behavior.

\paragraph{ORF-eligibility contact discipline.}
The layer attached to this record is $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$.  The seed statement is intentionally weaker than an ORF claim: before external sequence, transcript, or curated interval evidence is attached, the packet is only a boundary test for what would need to be checked.  A separate start/stop-window enumeration run loaded one external window from a curated nucleotide-window manifest dated $2026$-$05$-$25$, classified all $1$ of $1$ input windows by start/stop enumeration, and explicitly recorded the scope as external-window enumeration only.  That run also recorded the module classification as $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility\_only}$, meaning that the classifier output is not promoted to translation.

A stronger curated-interval contact was also recorded for the same layer.  It loaded $168$ rows from an NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS-interval source for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 accession $\mathrm{NC\_000913.3}$, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$.  The input classes present were positive curated ORF intervals, matched non-ORF decoys, and boundary cases.  The predeclared positive/decoy paired comparison had $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, hence win rate $1.0$, with one-sided binomial upper-tail probability $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  The positive interval test accepted $72$ of $72$ positives, again with accept rate $1.0$ and probability $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  The matched decoy test rejected $72$ of $72$ decoys, with reject rate $1.0$ and the same reported probability.  Boundary controls contained $24$ cases and promoted $0$, so the promoted fraction was $0.0$.

The held-out stability check used $3$ splits: left, middle, and right locus folds.  Each split had $24$ positives accepted at rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys rejected at rate $1.0$.  These numbers are evidence for this particular curated interval protocol, not for a universal biological theorem.  They show that, after the external interval contact is supplied, the finite start/stop-window classifier can be tested against the declared dataset and controls without changing the classifier rules.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The claim stops at the stated layer.  The finite codon geometry, the $f_3$ saturation row, the quotient vector, and the curated ORF-interval contact do not establish translation, folding, physical admissibility, molecular mechanism, biological function, or universality.  None of those stronger conclusions follows from a start/stop-window enumeration or from the $20$-codon row alone.  A separate contact would have to supply the relevant evidence for each stronger layer.  The self-check for the packet records exactly this discipline: $3$ gates were checked, and no codon geometry was promoted across biological layers.
