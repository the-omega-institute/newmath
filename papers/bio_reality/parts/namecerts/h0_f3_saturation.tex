\paragraph{Contact to ORF eligibility.}
A start/stop enumeration run loaded one nucleotide-window input and classified one window; its scope was recorded as $\mathrm{external\_window\_start\_stop\_enumeration\_only}$, with the explicit layer label $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility\_only}$.  A stronger curated-interval contact was then recorded on a separate data source: $168$ rows from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \textit{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655, accession $\mathrm{NC\_000913.3}$, snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$.  That contact contains three required input classes: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.

The curated contact reports $72$ accepted positives out of $72$, giving accept rate $1.0$ with exact one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. \paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The finite conclusion is therefore a two-stage statement.  First, the $f_3$ row supplies a closed finite saturation computation: $R$ expands to an $18$-codon saturated set, the recorded $f_3$ boundary is empty, and $M$ differs from this saturation exactly at $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$.  Second, ORF eligibility is not asserted from that geometry alone.  Eligibility becomes an empirical screening statement only in the presence of the curated interval contact, where the frozen classifier was tested against $168$ external annotation rows, $72$ positive intervals, $72$ matched decoys, $24$ boundary cases, and three held-out splits.

No further biological assertion is licensed here.  The row does not show that an interval is translated in vivo, does not identify a folded product, does not establish thermodynamic or cellular admissibility, does not assign function, does not give a mechanism, and does not state a universal law for genomes outside the contacted dataset.  Its scientific content is the finite saturation equality and the guarded ORF-eligibility contact: geometry may organize the candidate window test, while biological promotion requires the separate curated interval evidence recorded above.
