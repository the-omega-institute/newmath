\subsection{Saturation Boundary for Start--Stop ORF Eligibility}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-f3-saturation}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon packet.}
The record \texttt{h0.f3.saturation} concerns a finite codon enumeration whose biological reading is deliberately narrow: it is about eligibility of nucleotide windows under start and stop codon enumeration, not about realized translation or any downstream biological property.  The verified finite carrier contains the twenty-element set
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&AAA,AAG,ACA,ACG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,\\
& CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second check identifies the same set as the union of the \texttt{WNR} and \texttt{CUN} codon classes, with cardinality $20$.  The motif-compression row supplies the smaller thirteen-element motif union
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
and reports no missing and no extra codons relative to its declared motif description.  These facts give a concrete finite substrate for the packet, but by themselves they do not supply an observed gene, transcript, coding interval, protein product, or phenotype.

\paragraph{The $f_3$ saturation calculation.}
The saturation row states that applying the $f_3$ saturation to $R$ yields exactly $M$ with the two codons $ACA$ and $ACG$ absent:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=\{&AAA,AAG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run records the completion identity
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\operatorname{Med}(R)\cup\{ACA,ACG\},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the boundary audit for this saturation reports the empty list, recorded as \texttt{f3\_boundary\_is\_zero = []}.  Thus the finite-row content is an exact codon-level relation: the saturation reaches eighteen of the twenty codons in $M$, while $ACA$ and $ACG$ are precisely the two codons supplied by the median-completion clause.  This is a combinatorial statement about a named codon set.  It is not an assertion that any listed codon appears in a living transcript, initiates translation in a cellular context, terminates a ribosome, folds into a protein domain, or contributes to organismal function.

A separate finite geometry audit reports that the $M$ complex has $f$-vector $(20,38,26,8,1)$ and that the expected quotient has $10$ quotient points with $f$-vector $(10,14,6,1)$.  The quotient point list is

It still remains a mathematical compression of codon labels.  No mechanism of expression, molecular interaction, selection, or cellular viability is obtained from these quotient counts.

\paragraph{Contact-gated ORF reading.}
The packet contains two boundary probes.  Both require a curated ORF interval annotation before the start--stop enumeration may be read as an eligibility claim for a biological interval.  One row, \texttt{h2.orf.start\_stop.window\_eligibility}, loaded a curated nucleotide-window manifest with $1$ input window and classified all $1$ windows by start--stop enumeration.  Its scope is explicitly the external-window start--stop enumeration only, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$25$, and it records the classification as \texttt{orf\_eligibility\_only}.  This supports a limited statement: when an external window is supplied, the fixed enumeration can classify the window at the ORF-eligibility layer.  It does not turn the codon geometry itself into an observed ORF.

\paragraph{Curated interval contact.}
A stronger contact row, \texttt{h2.orf\_eligibility.curated\_interval\_contact}, loaded $168$ curated interval rows from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K--12 MG1655 accession \texttt{NC\_000913.3}, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$.  The required input classes were present: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.  The protocol records the classifier \texttt{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility} as unchanged after being fixed before the outside observation; the outside evidence time was $2026$-$05$-$30$T$10{:}29{:}31$Z, while the analysis plan time was $2026$-$05$-$29$T$19{:}11{:}13$Z.  The positive curated intervals were accepted at rate $72/72=1.0$, with one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  The matched decoys were rejected at rate $72/72=1.0$, with the same one-sided binomial value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  The predeclared paired comparison also recorded $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, again with one-sided binomial value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  The boundary cases had $n=24$ and $0$ promotions, so the promoted fraction was $0.0$.

\paragraph{Held-out stability.}
The curated interval row further reports three held-out splits.  In \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_left\_locus\_fold}, \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_middle\_locus\_fold}, and \texttt{refseq\_nc\_000913\_right\_locus\_fold}, each split had $24$ positive intervals and $24$ decoys, with positive accept rate $1.0$, decoy reject rate $1.0$, and pass status true.  These split checks show that, for this curated finite dataset, the start--stop interval classifier separates the supplied positives from the matched decoys and refuses the boundary cases.  The self-check row is consistent with this restriction: it reports $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The conclusion of this chapter is therefore a bounded one.  The finite codon facts establish the exact $f_3$ saturation relation, the median-completion identity, the empty saturation-boundary audit, the twenty-element $M$ set, the thirteen-element $R$ motif union, and the ten-point quotient geometry.  The curated contact rows show that, when an outside biological interval table is attached, the same start--stop enumeration can be evaluated against $168$ curated interval rows with $72$ positives, $72$ matched decoys, $24$ boundary cases, and stable held-out performance across three splits.  Nothing in these rows proves translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, mechanistic causation, universality across genomes, or a general law of coding sequence.  Those stronger readings require their own independent observations and their own finite checks.  The present record reaches only the ORF-eligibility layer for the stated curated-contact setting.
