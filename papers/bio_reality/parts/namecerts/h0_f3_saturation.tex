\subsection{Third-coordinate saturation and the ORF-eligibility boundary}
\label{sec:h0-f3-saturation}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
The record considered here is the finite codon packet with claim identifier $\mathrm{h0.f3.saturation}$.  Its mathematical content is a closed enumeration over RNA codon words, together with a boundary discipline for the biological interpretation of start and stop windows.  The finite set $M$ is verified as a $20$-element set, namely
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The smaller motif-union set $R$ is verified with $13$ listed words,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\\
&\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The relation between these rows is not an empirical biological assertion.  It is a finite combinatorial statement about which codon words are present after the stipulated closure operation is applied.

\paragraph{Saturation statement.}
The verified third-coordinate saturation row states that $\operatorname{Sat}_{f3}(R)$ contains exactly the $18$ codon words obtained from $M$ by excluding $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{Sat}_{f3}(R)=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\\
&\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run verifies
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\operatorname{Med}(R)\cup\{\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local gap in this finite presentation is precisely the two-word pair $\{\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG}\}$, while the recorded boundary error set for $f3$ is empty.  In the language of this chapter, the closure computation is saturated for the stipulated third-coordinate rule, and its only remaining distinction from $M$ is the named two-word complement.

Nevertheless, these geometric counts remain internal to the finite codon-word construction. \paragraph{Boundary to ORF eligibility.}
The biological object named by the surrounding record is open-reading-frame eligibility from start and stop codon enumeration.  The local finite mathematics can say that certain codon windows have the right enumerative form for a start/stop test.  It cannot, by itself, say that an open reading frame has been observed in a biological source.  Before external sequence, transcript, or curated interval information is attached, the record remains a boundary probe: it specifies what would have to be checked, not what has already been realized in biological material.

One later finite contact row records a single-window start/stop enumeration check with $1$ input window and $1$ classified window, under the scope marked as external-window start/stop enumeration only.  A stronger curated-interval contact row records $168$ dataset rows from the Escherichia coli K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession $\mathrm{NC\_000913.3}$, with $72$ accepted positive intervals and $72$ rejected matched decoys.  Its positive and decoy one-sided binomial upper-tail probabilities are both recorded as $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$, and three held-out splits each contain $24$ positives and $24$ decoys with positive acceptance rate $1.0$ and decoy rejection rate $1.0$.  These numbers are valid contact data for the curated interval experiment, but they are not consequences of the $f3$ saturation computation alone.

\paragraph{Local scientific reading.}
The scientifically safe reading is therefore narrow.  The $h0.f3.saturation$ packet verifies a finite closure fact: from the $13$-word motif set $R$, the third-coordinate saturation produces the $18$-word set $M\setminus\{\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG}\}$, with an empty recorded $f3$ boundary defect.  The companion finite rows check that $M$ has $20$ codons, that $R$ has no missing or extra motif-union entries, and that the quotient geometry has $10$ classes with the listed face vectors.  These are exact finite-row facts.

The cannot-claim boundary is equally part of the result.  No translation event follows from this saturation row.  No folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biochemical mechanism, biological function, or universal genomic rule follows from it.  Even when curated interval contacts are present elsewhere in the packet, they supply only the stated external interval evidence under their own scope.  They do not retroactively convert the finite codon geometry into a claim about translation, folding, mechanism, function, or universality.  The self-check row records the same discipline: three gates were checked, and no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.
