\paragraph{Local non-promotion rule.}
The correct conclusion is therefore two-layered.  First, at the codon-row level, the third-coordinate saturation is exact: $\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)$ is $M$ with precisely \texttt{ACA} and \texttt{ACG} removed, and the reported third-coordinate boundary is empty.  Second, at the biological-contact level, ORF eligibility can be discussed only where an external interval contact has supplied the relevant sequence or curated annotation.  The curated contact supports interval eligibility on its own declared dataset, with $168$ rows, $72$ accepted positives, $72$ rejected matched decoys, $24$ non-promoted boundary cases, and three stable held-out splits.

No stronger biological statement follows from this chapter.  The data here do not establish translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.
