\paragraph{Finite codon packet.}
This record isolates a finite combinatorial saturation fact before any biological interval is allowed to be asserted.  The experimentally checked motif union is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding ambient set $M$ has cardinality $20$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite computation records both no missing codons and no extra codons for the motif presentation of $R$.  Thus the first object in this chapter is not an observed gene, transcript, protein, or organismal phenotype.  It is a checked finite row of codon-level data with an explicit boundary around what it is permitted to mean.

\paragraph{Saturation calculation.}
The $f_3$ saturation check reports that the mediated closure of $R$ together with the explicit codon $\mathrm{ACA}$ recovers the full ambient set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\mathrm{Med}(R)\cup\{\mathrm{ACA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run computes the saturated row
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Equivalently, the finite saturation equals $M\setminus\{\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG}\}$.  The boundary output is the empty list, so the run found no residual $f_3$ boundary obstruction inside the stated finite row.  The verified timestamp for this computation is $2026$-$05$-$31$ $06{:}49{:}23+00{:}00$, with experiment run identifier \texttt{f4eee68dd2c3}.  This is the central mathematical content of the chapter: a finite saturation relation among named RNA codons, not an empirical claim about expression or function.

\paragraph{Local biological reading.}
The biological layer under discussion is open-reading-frame eligibility from start and stop codon enumeration.  The separate start--stop window experiment loaded one external nucleotide-window manifest with one input window and classified that one window by start--stop enumeration.  Its declared scope is external window enumeration only, and its output explicitly remained at the ORF-eligibility layer.  A stronger curated interval contact was later run on $168$ rows from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655, accession NC\_000913.3, using snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$.  That contact contained the required input classes \texttt{positive\_curated\_orf}, \texttt{matched\_non\_orf\_decoy}, and \texttt{boundary\_case}.  It accepted $72$ of $72$ positive intervals, rejected $72$ of $72$ matched decoys, and reported one-sided exact binomial upper-tail values $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$ for both positive acceptance and matched decoy rejection.  The predeclared paired baseline comparison also won $72$ of $72$ pairs with the same upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  Across three held-out splits, each split had $24$ positive examples accepted at rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys rejected at rate $1.0$.  The $24$ boundary cases had $0$ promotions, hence promoted fraction $0.0$.

\paragraph{Separation of finite geometry from biological assertion.}
The saturation row $\mathrm{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=M\setminus\{\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG}\}$ may be used to describe a finite codon geometry that is relevant to an ORF-eligibility test.  It may not be used as the observation of an ORF interval.  The curated interval contact, when present, is an external biological annotation input, not a theorem generated by the codon geometry.  The protocol dates record this separation: classifier rules, baselines, held-out splits, and input classes were frozen at $2026$-$05$-$29$ $19{:}11{:}13+00{:}00$, before the external evidence time $2026$-$05$-$30$ $10{:}29{:}31+00{:}00$.  The classifier identifier was \texttt{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}, and the rule-modification flag was false.

\paragraph{Local limit of the claim.}
The result established here is therefore narrow.  The finite codon calculation verifies the $f_3$ saturation pattern, the exact sets $R$ and $M$, the empty saturation-boundary output, and the associated quotient geometry.  The ORF window and curated interval rows show how a later external contact can test eligibility on a finite dataset.  No translation event, folded molecular structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic explanation, or universal genomic law follows from the saturation row alone.  Such conclusions would require their own observations and their own tests.  The self-check for this packet records the same discipline: three gates were checked, and no codon geometry was promoted across biological layers.
