\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The finite $f_3$ row gives a precise codon-saturation calculation: $R$ has $13$ codons, $M$ has $20$ codons, the saturation image has $18$ codons, and the two absent entries from $M$ are $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$.  The quotient ledger gives a ten-class finite geometry with displayed cell counts.  These facts are enough to support an ORF-eligibility boundary protocol: a proposed start-stop window may be sorted as a finite candidate only when an external interval contact is present and only under the stated classifier scope.  They are not enough to infer translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or a universal genomic law.  Such claims require independent contacts that measure or curate those layers directly.  The self-check ledger records three checked gates and states the same boundary: no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.
