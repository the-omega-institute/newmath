\subsection{Finite codon saturation as an ORF-eligibility boundary record}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-f3-saturation}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
This BioReality record isolates a finite codon calculation whose biological reading is deliberately narrow.  The packet concerns the layer called ORF eligibility, meaning only the syntactic possibility that a nucleotide interval could be delimited by start and stop codon data.  Its local carrier has no direct biological measurement attached to it: the carrier, its distinctions, and its readback are all still waiting for contact with observed sequence, transcript, or curated interval data.  Thus the finite calculation below is not itself an observed open reading frame.  It is a codon-level boundary test for what would have to be checked before an ORF-eligibility assertion can be made.

\paragraph{Codon sets.}
The verified motif compression record gives the thirteen-element set
$R=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$,
with no missing elements and no extra elements in the motif-union comparison.  The larger verified codon set $M$ has cardinality $20$ and is
$M=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}$.
The same row verifies that $M$ is exactly the reported union of the WNR and CUN classes, again with size $20$.  These are finite codon facts only; they do not identify a genomic locus or a translated product.

\paragraph{Saturation calculation.}
The $f_3$ saturation record separates a codon-level closure from two named boundary codons.  The verified value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)
&=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\} \\
&= M\setminus\{\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated boundary list is empty: $f_3\text{-boundary}=\varnothing$.  The finite union check also records
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\operatorname{Med}(R)\cup\{\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
where the displayed right-hand side is the same twenty-codon set listed above.  In this chapter the computational content is therefore exact: the saturated set contains eighteen codons, the two omitted codons are $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$, and the boundary audit contributes no additional codon.

The quotient rows do not supply a sequence interval, a transcript, a ribosomal event, a fold, or a biochemical role.

\paragraph{Reality-contact boundary.}
The local probes enforce the same rule in two forms.  First, start--stop-window enumeration must remain a boundary record until an external sequence, transcript, or curated annotation supplies an observed interval.  Second, the packet must not record ORF eligibility if there is no contact capable of testing the interval.  This row has no attached contact in its own contact list, so its direct biological status is only a pre-contact boundary status.  The self-check records three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.

\paragraph{Separate interval evidence.}
The linked curated-interval contact shows what a different, externally supplied contact can look like.  It used $168$ rows from a curated ORF interval dataset with required input classes $\mathrm{boundary\_case}$, $\mathrm{matched\_non\_orf\_decoy}$, and $\mathrm{positive\_curated\_orf}$.  Its protocol was frozen at $2026$-$05$-$29\,19{:}11{:}13+00{:}00$, before the external evidence time $2026$-$05$-$30\,10{:}29{:}31+00{:}00$.  The contact reported $72$ accepted positives out of $72$, $72$ rejected matched decoys out of $72$, and $24$ boundary cases with zero promotions.  The one-sided exact binomial upper-tail value was $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$ for the positive interval statistic, the matched-decoy rejection statistic, and the predeclared paired baseline advantage.  Three held-out splits each had $24$ positives and $24$ decoys, with positive accept rate $1.0$ and decoy reject rate $1.0$ in every split.  This linked evidence is not imported into the present seed as a free promotion; it illustrates the kind of external contact required before ORF eligibility may be asserted for observed intervals.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim rule.}
The conclusion of this chapter is intentionally modest.  The finite codon saturation proves only that the $f_3$ closure of $R$ is the eighteen-codon set $M\setminus\{\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG}\}$ and that the checked boundary list is empty.  From those codon facts alone one may not infer translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, organism-level role, or universal biological law.  Any such claim requires a distinct contact that supplies the relevant biological evidence and a separate analysis at the appropriate layer.
