\subsection{The $f_3$ Saturation Boundary for ORF Eligibility}

\paragraph{Finite codon inventory.}
This record concerns the finite saturation claim identified by $\mathrm{h0.f3.saturation}$ and its use as a boundary packet for open-reading-frame eligibility.  The finite alphabetic object is a codon-level enumeration, not an observed transcript, translated product, folded molecule, or functional gene call.  The verified ambient set $M$ has cardinality $20$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run verifies that this $M$ is the union named by the $\mathrm{WNR}$ and $\mathrm{CUN}$ enumeration, with reported size $20$.  A separate motif-compression check identifies
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with no missing entries and no extra entries in the comparison ledger.  These finite lists are the whole numerical content of the codon-geometry side of the claim.

\paragraph{Saturation computation.}
The $f_3$ saturation run records that the mediated closure of $R$ together with $\mathrm{ACA}$ recovers the same twenty-element set $M$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{Med}(R)\cup\{\mathrm{ACA}\}=M.
\end{aligned}
$$
The saturated set itself is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\\
&\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Equivalently, it is $M$ with $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$ absent.  The reported $f_3$ boundary residual is the empty list, so the finite saturation check has no remaining boundary elements at this level.  This is a closed combinatorial statement about the enumerated codon rows; it is not a biological observation by itself.

The quotient data constrain the finite geometry of the packet, but they do not convert the packet into an interval annotation or any molecular realization.

\paragraph{Reality-contact status.}
The row supplied with this chapter contains no attached contacts in the local contact list.  Consequently the $f_3$ saturation statement remains a seed-level boundary result for the layer called $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$.  Its internal evidence basis consists of a mismatch ledger and derived probes, and the two probes demand an independent curated ORF interval annotation before any ORF-eligibility assertion is made for a biological interval.  The boundary test is therefore negative in a precise sense: start and stop codon window geometry may describe what would have to be checked, but it does not itself show that an observed open reading frame is present.

\paragraph{Local prohibition on over-reading.}
The admissible conclusion is deliberately narrow.  The finite saturation check verifies a codon-list equality, an eighteen-element saturated subset, an empty $f_3$ boundary residual, and quotient-geometry counts.  The curated-contact experiment, when considered as a separate input, supports ORF-eligibility classification only for its declared external interval dataset.  Neither source licenses any claim of translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, organism-wide law, or universal genomic rule.  Such claims would require additional contacts able to test those layers directly.  The self-check attached to the finite row records the same boundary: three gates were checked, and no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.
