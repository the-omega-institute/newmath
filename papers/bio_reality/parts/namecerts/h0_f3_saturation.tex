\paragraph{Finite codon packet.}
The record \texttt{h0.f3.saturation} is a finite codon-level boundary packet for open-reading-frame eligibility. Its biological object is not an observed gene, transcript, protein, or function. It is the start/stop-codon enumeration problem viewed as a finite testable boundary: a coordinate pattern may describe which codon words are eligible for a later interval-level question, but it does not by itself assert that a biological interval exists. The verified finite universe used by this packet is the set $M$ of twenty codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&AAA,AAG,ACA,ACG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,\
& CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The companion check \texttt{h0.M.equals.WNR.CUN} records $|M|=20$ and identifies this same list as $M=WNR\cup CUN$ in the finite run \texttt{a53ef0f36703}. The motif-compression check \texttt{h0.R.motif.compression}, from run \texttt{338799579a87}, gives the thirteen-word motif set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,\
& UCA,UGA,UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with no missing words and no extra words in the verified motif union.

\paragraph{Saturation computation.}
The finite saturation result is that the $f_3$-closure of $R$ reaches every word of $M$ except the two boundary codons $ACA$ and $ACG$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=\{&AAA,AAG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\
& UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run, \texttt{f4eee68dd2c3}, records
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\operatorname{Med}(R)\cup\{ACA,ACG\},
\end{aligned}
$$
where the displayed right-hand side is the full twenty-word list for $M$. The reported $f_3$ boundary is empty, recorded as \texttt{f3\_boundary\_is\_zero} $=[]$. Thus the packet is saturated as a finite codon calculation while still leaving the two excluded codons explicitly visible as boundary residues. This is the mathematical content used here: the calculation classifies a finite word set under the declared combinatorial operation, and nothing in that computation observes a living interval.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The packet therefore has two layers that must not be collapsed. The finite codon calculation says that $R$ saturates to $M\setminus\{ACA,ACG\}$ under $f_3$, with an empty reported boundary residual and a checked quotient geometry. The curated interval contact says that a separately supplied biological dataset can be tested by a frozen start/stop-window classifier with $168$ rows and the statistics just listed. The former does not manufacture the latter. In particular, start/stop enumeration alone does not establish translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or a universal biological law. Those stronger conclusions would require separate contacts designed for those layers. The self-check record \texttt{self.claim}, run \texttt{0f47d2f0cf41}, is consistent with this restriction: it records three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.
