\paragraph{Finite packet.}
This record isolates a finite codon-level saturation calculation from any biological realization claim.  The biological question named by the row is open-reading-frame eligibility from start and stop codon enumeration, but the row itself does not observe an open reading frame.  Its direct finite content is the verified saturation identity for claim $h0.f3.saturation$.  The reported set
$$
\begin{aligned}

&= \{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}

$$

Thus the saturation statement is a closed finite enumeration: it records which codons enter the $f_3$-saturated region and which two codons, $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$, are outside that saturated sublist while still appearing in $M$.
