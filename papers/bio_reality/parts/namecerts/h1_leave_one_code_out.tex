\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback on the Codon Cube}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.} The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon label table. The construction separates four finite distinctions: the six-bit codon display, local tile faces inside the cube, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The external contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard genetic-code bridge assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and the experiment tests only code-layer readback. Thus the row data are not being interpreted as a translation mechanism, a folding rule, a physical admissibility criterion, a function oracle, or a universality theorem.

\paragraph{The recovered exceptional row.} The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. This gives a purely finite boundary signature: $13$ vertices, $16$ local one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ square faces inside the selected cubical subcomplex. No biological necessity is assigned to this shape; it is a local coordinate fact inside $Q_6$ plus a label-table readback.

\paragraph{Spectral fingerprint.} The associated finite operator data give $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The cubic check is numerically closed at scale $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, recorded both as the cubic-polynomial residual and as the radial-polynomial residual:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,\\
P_{\mathrm{rad}}(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu) &\approx -6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.

$$
The spectral statement is therefore only that the finite row and its ambient marker set have a reproducible numeric fingerprint. It is not a claim that the molecule computes this polynomial or that the polynomial supplies a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.} In the shape null experiment with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampling log records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite comparison is constrained by a heavily filtered discrete sample space rather than by a smooth asymptotic approximation. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed marker size was $|M|=20$, the exact-match count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed marker size was $7$, and the corresponding reported values were $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.} The claim-specific leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$; and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the local boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. Equivalently, the finite readback can be summarized as
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{tables} &= 23,\\
\mathrm{targets} &= 63,\\
\mathrm{baseline} &= 0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T) &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T\cup \partial) &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central finite-row fact for the chapter: when one code table is withheld, the local cube-and-boundary construction almost always keeps the withheld target inside the predicted code-layer neighborhood.

\paragraph{Family stratification.} The family-level summary has $5$ families. The above-baseline hit rates are $1.0$ for the bacterial-plastid family, $1.0$ for the mitochondrial family, $1.0$ for the nuclear-ciliate family, $1.0$ for the candidate-other family, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family. The alternative-yeast value is the only family rate below $1.0$ in the supplied finite rows, so it is the natural stress point for later code-layer refinements. It is still a code-read stress point only; it does not license any claim about amino-acid realization after translation.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out boundary check.} The companion leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. Thus the local boundary is not ornamental: it is the piece that keeps the finite readback stable when individual codons rather than whole code tables are withheld. The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, there are no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons. The six listed modules are

\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\

Every module has either predictable or boundary support, which is exactly the finite condition needed for a local tile record.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.} The supported statement is therefore precise: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose code-label readback is unusually stable under leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out probes. The curated standard-code table supplies the amino-acid and stop labels used for readback; it does not supply a translation apparatus, a protein fold, a structural order, a physical admissibility test, a biological function, a mechanism, or a universal law. Any later claim crossing one of those boundaries must attach a separate contact designed for that layer.
