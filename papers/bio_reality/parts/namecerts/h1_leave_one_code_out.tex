\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Cube}\label{sec:biorality-h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite record for claim $h1.leave.one.code.out$ concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, while the observable readout is not a biochemical mechanism but the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels. The claim tested here is narrow: a local window-six tile can recover most code-table perturbation targets when one code table is held out, and this recovery is concentrated near a distinguished punctured-cube boundary. The claim does not say that the coordinate geometry is translation, protein folding, physical feasibility, cellular function, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Finite boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. In the verified row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite evidence is not a vague geometric analogy: the record fixes both the codon roster and the local combinatorial shape. The biological contact remains the curated standard-code label table, so the object being read is a codon-to-label assignment, not a molecular process.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinate.}
The spectral row supplies three scalar values for the window-six coordinate calculation:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated cubic check has residual
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
reported both as the cubic-polynomial residual and the radial-polynomial value. This places the spectral calculation inside numerical tolerance for the finite row. It should be read as a coordinate-level invariant of the codon display, not as an assertion that the codon table is forced by physics or evolutionary necessity.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under finite nulls.}
Two finite null comparisons separate the observed boundary shape from simple randomized alternatives. In the first comparison, $N=2000$ samples were attempted under a shape null with seed $42$. The run used $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. It produced $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
In the amino-acid-class-preserving comparison, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$. The exact-match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, and the reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support a finite-code-layer anomaly: the local tile is difficult to reproduce under the listed nulls. They do not turn the local tile into a global biological rule.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The direct leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. Against that baseline, the verified row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ above\ baseline} &= 0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ } M-T &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ } M-T \mathrm{\ plus\ boundary} &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The key finite observation is that nearly all held-out targets are recovered once the $M-T$ region is enlarged by the boundary information. The gain over the baseline is large: the row moves from about $0.1982$ to about $0.9841$ on the same target set. This is evidence for a local code-read pattern in the finite codon table record, not for any higher-layer biological realization.

This distribution shows that the strong recovery is not confined to one named table family in the finite row, although the alternative-yeast group is weaker than the four families reported at $1.0$. The result is still a table-label result: it reads codon labels from curated code tables and does not observe translation dynamics, protein structure, or organismal function.

\paragraph{Codon-level recovery and modules.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$. The module classification covers all $13$ members of $R$ with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_LYS} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ILE\_MET} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN} &= \{UCA, UUA\},\\
\mathrm{STOP\_TRP} &= \{UAA, UAG, UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row states that every module has either a predictable or boundary role, with $R$ size $13$ and assigned count $13$. This is the strongest local organizing statement supported by the data supplied here: the distinguished codons are completely partitioned into finite modules that match the leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out recovery behavior.

\paragraph{Reality contact and blocked promotion.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, resolved at codon-label readback. It can test code-layer assignments and local codon-tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism. Amino-acid label sequences are therefore not protein folds, and CUN, UUR, stop, tryptophan, methionine, arginine, lysine, serine, or leucine labels do not become structure evidence merely by appearing in the codon table. A separate observation would be required before any of those higher-layer claims could be made.

\paragraph{Result.}
The finite row supports the following bounded conclusion. In the six-bit codon cube, the standard-code label table contains a distinguished $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, stable spectral scalars, rare shape behavior under the stated finite nulls, and strong leave-one-code-out recovery across $23$ evaluated tables and $63$ targets. The recovery reaches $0.9841269841269841$ when the boundary contribution is included, compared with a baseline rate of $0.19821739130434785$. This is a code-read result about local codon-label organization. It remains silent about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, and universality unless another independently specified contact supplies those observations.
