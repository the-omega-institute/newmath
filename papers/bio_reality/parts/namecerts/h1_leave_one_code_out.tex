\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
This record concerns a finite code-read experiment on the six-bit codon display $Q_6$.  The biological contact is only the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop, and it is used here as a code-layer readback contact.  The BEDC object is not the biochemical translation process.  It is the finite coordinate packet consisting of a codon-cube carrier, local tile faces, singleton label corners, three-point label classes, coordinate closure, spectral summaries, and relations read back against the standard-code label table.  The claim tested in this row is the leave-one-code-out claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.code.out}$: when variant code tables are withheld one at a time, the local tile packet should recover most observed reassignment targets by membership in $M\setminus T$ or by the adjacent boundary extension.

\paragraph{Observed residual row.}
The finite residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured cube with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object used by the leave-one-code-out probe is not an unconstrained list of exceptional codons.  It is a finite tile-like subset of the six-bit codon cube, with $13$ vertices, $16$ local edges, and $4$ local square faces after the puncture imposed by the standard-code label contact.

\paragraph{Null-model pressure.}
The shape of the observed $M$ packet was compared against $2000$ null samples with seed $42$.  In the unrestricted shape null, there was $1$ exact shape match after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the high-Jaccard count was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
In the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed size was $|M|=20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  This gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support the narrower statement that the local shape is not easily reproduced by the sampled null models.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The direct leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total reassignment targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
whereas the hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The stricter recovery inside $M\setminus T$ was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9365079365079365,
\end{aligned}
$$
and allowing the local boundary extension returned
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus, on this finite row, almost all withheld code-table targets fall either inside the main local packet after removing $T$ or on its boundary.  The quantitative claim is exactly this code-read recovery statement over $23$ tables and $63$ targets.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready verdict is a code-layer verdict.  The local codon tile exposes a finite boundary pattern in the standard-code readback, and the leave-one-code-out row is strong relative to the stated sampled nulls: $23$ tables, $63$ targets, baseline rate $0.19821739130434785$, and boundary-extended rate $0.9841269841269841$.  The contact does not test translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, global mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only label readback.  No claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
