\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Readback}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not translation itself, but the codon display obtained by reading each codon as a finite coordinate row with a curated amino-acid-or-stop label.  The external contact is the curated standard genetic-code bridge: it supplies codon-to-label assignments and nothing stronger.  Thus the claim tested here is a code-read claim about a local tile boundary in $Q_6$, not a claim about folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Distinguished punctured row set.}
The finite row set singled out by the coordinate and closure probe has cardinality $13$.  The row set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same list and reports the face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite object used by the leave-one-code-out test: a local tile on the codon cube with thirteen displayed codons, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces.  The data also records an observed module partition of all thirteen rows.  The modules are $\{AGA,AGG\}$ for the $agr$ class, $\{AAA\}$ for the $ar\_lys$ class, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for the $cun$ class, $\{AUA\}$ for the $ile\_met$ class, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ for the $ssn$ class, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for the $stop\_trp$ class.  The module audit has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing rows, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under null sampling.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ null samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exact shape matches occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-Jaccard matches at threshold $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its reported exact-shape value is $0.0$, and its lower-tail value is $0.0035$.  These finite-row statistics say that the observed local code-read tile is uncommon under the stated null mechanisms.  They do not convert the tile into translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  Against this baseline, the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  In finite-row terms, the result is that omitted code-label rows are recovered by the local tile-and-boundary structure far more often than the baseline would suggest.  The test is still only a code-read test: its targets are curated labels in finite code tables, not realized proteins or organismal functions.

\paragraph{Family-level split.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out table records $5$ families.  The hit-rate-above-baseline values are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  These family rows are compatible with the same local boundary interpretation: most family-specific omissions remain readable from the finite codon-tile context.  The alternative-yeast family is the only listed family below perfect hit-rate-above-baseline, but it remains far above the baseline rate reported for the aggregate leave-one-code-out run.

\paragraph{Codon-level recovery and boundary role.}
The leave-one-codon-out companion run records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The absence of anchors is important: the recovery is not being described as a single privileged codon forcing the biological code.  The recorded boundary rows carry the local readback pressure.  This supports the narrow statement that the six-bit codon display exposes a finite boundary-tile regularity in the curated code layer.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The cannot-claim boundary is part of the mathematical content of this record.  The contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks only.  No translation realization follows.  No folded protein structure follows.  No structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function.  Any claim beyond code\_read would require a separate higher-layer reality contact, with its own finite rows, observables, and mismatch audit.
