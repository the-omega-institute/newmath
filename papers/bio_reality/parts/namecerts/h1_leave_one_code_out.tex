\subsection{Leave-one-code-out boundary readback for the standard-code tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. This contact is a code-layer bridge. It does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The chapter therefore tests only whether the window-six coordinate presentation gives a local boundary readback of code labels.

\paragraph{The residual tile.}
The residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured-cube object its finite face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the displayed tile is not just a count: it has thirteen vertices, sixteen local one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the induced codon-cube geometry. The associated module partition is complete: the assigned count is $13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary witness, so the finite row supports a local module-level code split rather than an unstructured list of exceptions.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The local coordinate object also carries a finite spectral signature. The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the finite numerical row is within the stated tolerance of the polynomial constraint. Against $N=2000$ shape samples, with seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The run used $57377$ distinct sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-coordinate anomaly at the code-read layer. They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate above baseline is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9365079365079365.
\end{aligned}
$$
Adding the boundary recovers the same above-baseline level,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\ rate}(M\setminus T+\mathrm{boundary})=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus, in this finite row, most leave-one-code targets are recovered by the local $M\setminus T$ coordinate field, and the remaining boundary contribution raises the row to the reported near-complete readback rate. The companion leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. This says that the readback is boundary-driven rather than anchor-driven in the supplied finite data.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The family-stratified row contains $5$ families. The hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The alternative-yeast family is therefore the weakest listed family but remains above the baseline row, while the other four families are reported at full above-baseline recovery. This stratification is useful because it prevents the aggregate $0.9841269841269841$ hit rate from being read as a single undifferentiated effect.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite data support the following local statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a residual punctured-cube tile whose module partition, spectral row, null-model behavior, and leave-one-code-out recovery are aligned with standard-code label readback. The claim is deliberately narrower than a biological mechanism claim. The curated table supplies labels; it does not supply ribosomal translation dynamics, protein folding, physical admissibility, function, or universality. Therefore the valid conclusion is a code-read boundary result: the local tile may organize codon-label differences, but no higher biological layer follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that layer.
