\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery at the codon-label boundary}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observational layer.}
The record is a code-layer test on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  A codon is treated as a six-bit display point, with the local geometry read through coordinate faces, closure neighborhoods, a finite spectrum, and relations among label classes.  The biological contact is not a mechanism of translation.  It is the curated standard-code table, used only as a codon-to-label readout.  The relevant distinctions are a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  Thus the experiment asks whether local window-six structure helps recover known codon-label changes across code tables, while keeping the table itself as the external label source.

\paragraph{The exceptional set.}
The finite exceptional set $R$ contains exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Its measured size is $|R|=13$.  As a punctured cubical object, the same row reports the face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite shape on which the leave-one tests are evaluated.  The list is not interpreted as a universal biological law; it is a bounded code-table row set inside the codon-cube presentation.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The spectral row separates the measured local object from a purely verbal pattern.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, recorded also as the cubic-polynomial residual.  This places the numerical check at floating-point tolerance rather than at a qualitative resemblance claim.

A separate shape-randomization row used $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
while $25$ controls reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null row, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These controls support the claim that the finite local shape is not being treated as a free narrative fit.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The primary row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total target events.  The baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
Against that baseline, the measured hit rate above baseline is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary allowance is $0.9841269841269841$.  The interpretation is local and finite: when one code table is held out, the codon-cube tile and its boundary account for nearly all held-out code-label targets in the tested row, but the success is only a code-label recovery statement.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The family row partitions the same test into five families.  The reported family-level rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The nonuniform family list is important because the row is not merely a pooled statistic.  Four families reach full measured recovery above baseline in the finite record, while the alternative-yeast family remains below one and therefore marks a place where the local tile is useful but not absolute.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out companion row.}
The codon-level companion test reports no anchor codons, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ codons with high closure coverage, and $8$ predictable codons.  Its recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value reported for recovery above threshold.  This companion row does not enlarge the biological claim.  It only says that, at codon resolution, the finite local boundary bookkeeping recovers the tested code-label positions in that row.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification covers all thirteen codons in $R$ with no extras, no missing elements, and no unassigned codons.  The assigned count is $13$, matching $R$ size $13$.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has either predictable support or boundary support in the recorded classification.  The module row is therefore a finite completeness check for the thirteen-codon exceptional set, not a claim about biochemical realization.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table.  It observes codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, molecular function, or a universal mechanism.  An amino-acid label sequence read from the code table is still only a label sequence at this layer.  No folded structure, activity, organism-level effect, or cross-organism mechanism follows from the window-six geometry unless another contact supplies that higher-layer observation.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
For claim $h1.leave.one.code.out$, the finite data support a code-read statement: the six-coordinate local tile and its boundary recover held-out code-label targets at rate $0.9841269841269841$ above the baseline rate $0.19821739130434785$ across $23$ tables and $63$ targets, with compatible codon-level and module-level bookkeeping.  The result is ready as a bounded BioReality record because the numerical rows, null rows, spectral row, exceptional-codon list, and family/module stratifications are all finite and explicit.  Its scope ends at codon-label readback.
