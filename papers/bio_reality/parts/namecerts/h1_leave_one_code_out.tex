\subsection{Leave-one-code-out readback for the local codon tile}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback target.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological contact used in this section is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label and therefore supports code-layer readback.  It does not by itself observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  The finite claim tested here is consequently narrow: after one code table is left out, the local tile data should still recover most changed label targets as lying in the previously exposed tile region, or in that region plus its boundary, at a rate far above the baseline hit rate.

\paragraph{The distinguished codon set.}
The finite row singles out a set $R$ of thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The same row identifies this set with the punctured-cube readback and gives the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of label exceptions: it records a finite tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four local faces inside the six-bit codon cube.  The supplied row also reports an observed $M$-region size of $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, but it does not supply an explicit codon-by-codon list for $M$; all uses of $M$ below are therefore through the supplied finite counts and region names.

\paragraph{Spectral finite row.}
The same finite carrier has a recorded spectral summary.  The reported values are

The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius entry agrees with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ to the recorded tolerance.  These numbers are used only as finite-coordinate invariants of the tile readback.  They do not convert the tile into a physical law or into a translation mechanism.

\paragraph{Null comparison.}
Two finite null checks give the scale of the observed shape.  In the shape-significance row, $N=2000$ sampled comparisons were recorded after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row again uses $N=2000$.  It records observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the claim that the local code-read tile is not a generic finite artifact under the recorded null models.  They do not support any claim about folding, physical admissibility, function realization, or biological necessity.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out outcome.}
The primary finite row for this section evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total changed targets.  The baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
Against that baseline, the leave-one-code-out recovery rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\operatorname{hit}((M\setminus T)\cup\partial M)&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{above\ baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Since $0.9841269841269841$ is far above $0.19821739130434785$, the code-read conclusion is that the local window-six tile and its boundary retain nearly all leave-one-code-out targets in the supplied row.  The stronger interior-only rate, $0.9365079365079365$, says that most targets are already found before adjoining the boundary.  The boundary still matters: adding it raises the reported rate to $0.9841269841269841$.

The family-level row partitions the leave-one-code-out evidence into $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline are

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\

Thus four recorded families reach perfect above-baseline hit rate, while the alternative-yeast family remains high at $0.8333333333333334$.  This is a finite readback fact about code-label targets across the recorded code-table families.  It is not evidence that the window-six geometry causes those code tables, nor that it supplies a universal mechanism for genetic-code variation.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module row assigns all thirteen codons of $R$ to named modules, with no missing or extra codons.  The complete assignment is

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\

$$
The module-classification row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, empty extra-module codons, empty missing-from-modules list, and empty unassigned-codons list.  It also records that every module has predictable or boundary status.  Independently, the leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$ above the stated threshold.  These counts give a local explanation of why the boundary term is present in the code-level readback: some codons sit as boundary recoveries rather than as anchor recoveries.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The admissible conclusion is exactly a code-read conclusion.  In the supplied finite rows, the standard-code table is a contact for codon-to-label assignments, the set $R$ is a thirteen-codon punctured-cube tile with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, and the leave-one-code-out test recovers changed targets at rate $0.9841269841269841$ when the boundary is included.  No stronger biological statement follows from those data alone.  In particular, no translation event, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality is claimed here.  Any such promotion would require a separate reality contact observing that higher layer directly.
