\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact, not as a claim about translation dynamics.  The internal carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the distinctions of a six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The external contact is only the curated standard-code label table: each codon receives an amino-acid or stop label.  Thus the mathematical object under test is a finite label readback over codon coordinates.  No conclusion about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.

\paragraph{The boundary row $R$.}
The finite row singled out by the coordinate probe has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile whose label behavior is tested.  The point of the row is not that biology is forced to obey this geometry, but that a finite coordinate subset gives a sharp place where label readback can be compared against curated code rows.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The row-level spectral check gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the finite computation is internally stable at the stated tolerance.  In the shape-null experiment with $N=2000$ draws and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ samples at Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The sampling process recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed finite set size was $|M|=20$, the exact shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$ out of $2000$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row distinction at the code-read layer.  They do not identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
For the leave-one-code-out probe, $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The coordinate tile then achieved
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ }M\setminus T&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ }M\setminus T\mathrm{\ plus\ boundary}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite test says that, when one code table is left out, the local codon-tile coordinates recover almost all targets above the baseline comparison.  The recovery is a label-table phenomenon: it concerns whether the held-out code labels are seen by the finite coordinate construction.  It is not evidence that the window-six tile performs translation or explains folded protein structure.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready claim is narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure inside the curated code table, and the leave-one-code-out row data show high code-read recovery relative to the stated baseline.  The curated standard-code table is the only reality contact used here.  Therefore amino-acid label readback remains a code-layer result.  It must not be promoted to translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or a claim that DNA follows BEDC geometry as biological necessity.  Such promotions require separate contacts of the corresponding kind.
