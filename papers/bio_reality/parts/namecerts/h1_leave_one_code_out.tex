\subsection{Leave-one-code-out stability for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate packet.}
The object studied here is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon displayed as a six-bit coordinate word.  The packet separates four finite distinctions: the coordinate display of the codon, the local face-tile geometry cut out by the marked set, the singleton corner labels, and the three-point label classes.  Its readback is the ordinary curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  No cellular process, folded molecule, energetic constraint, or organism-level mechanism is part of this datum.

The distinguished remainder set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also verified as a punctured-cube tile with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not just a list of exceptional codons: it has a finite cubical incidence profile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces after the puncture is imposed.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The marked set $M$ is recorded with observed size $20$.  Against the finite sampling model with seed $42$ and $N=2000$ accepted samples, the exact shape match count is $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ is $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  These figures make the local shape unusually rigid in the sampled comparison class, but they do not convert the tile into a biological necessity.  They say that the finite coordinate pattern is rare under the stated coordinate-preserving comparison, not that translation or function has been derived from geometry.

A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at the observed marked size $20$ is $7$.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving comparison therefore also treats the observed tile as a small-tail event.  The conclusion remains local: class preservation sharpens the evidence that the coordinate packet is nonrandom relative to the stated finite shuffle, but it still supplies no evidence about folding, admissible chemistry, organismal fitness, or universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only empirical contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, at codon-label resolution.  It is a bridge from coordinate words to labels such as amino-acid names and stop marks.  It can test whether a proposed coordinate packet reads back the observed standard-code labels and whether local splits align with table entries.  It cannot test whether ribosomes perform translation in a particular way, whether a label sequence folds, whether a molecule is physically admissible, whether any biological function is realized, or whether the coordinate packet expresses a universal law.

This distinction is essential for the leave-one-code-out claim.  The hit-rate row, the punctured-cube row, the spectral row, and the module row jointly support a strong code-layer statement: the finite window-six tile captures a stable local boundary pattern in curated codon-label data.  They do not support promotion to protein realization.  An amino-acid label sequence is not a folded protein structure; a codon-to-label table is not a structure experiment; a local cubical tile is not a mechanism of translation.

These data justify the local codon-tile readback claim.  They do not justify any assertion of translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic causation, or universality unless another contact, with the relevant experimental resolution, is supplied.
