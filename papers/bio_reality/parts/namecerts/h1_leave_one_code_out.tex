\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback on the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
The finite object considered here is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ equipped with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  Its biological contact is only the curated standard-code table: a finite table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact is therefore a code-read contact, not a translation experiment, not a folded-structure observation, not a physical-admissibility certificate, not a function assay, and not a universality theorem.  The claim tested in this row is that the window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure that helps read standard-code labels and nearby reassignment targets, while the labels themselves remain external reality data supplied by the curated table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the observed standard-code contact it is exactly the following finite set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured cube with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-read carrier is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it has a finite face profile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four local tile faces.  This is the local geometric content of the row.  No global biological law is inferred from this geometry.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The conclusion is a local one.  The curated standard-code contact supports codon-to-label readback and local split checks.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, biochemical function, a mechanism of reassignment, or universality across all biological systems.  Amino-acid label sequences read back from the table are not folded protein structures.  Any claim crossing from code labels to translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact supplying that layer.
