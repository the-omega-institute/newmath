\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for a Local Codon Tile}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact against a six-bit codon display. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read through singleton codon corners, local tile faces, and three-point label classes. The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. It is not a contact for translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The finite boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is the following codon list:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube object with face data $(13,16,4)$. Thus the observed readback is not merely a list of exceptional labels; it has a finite local shape inside the codon cube. The companion finite set $M$ is observed with size $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null test. The supplied finite rows do not include an explicit displayed codon list for $M$, so this chapter uses the scalar $|M|=20$ and the observed leave-one-code and leave-one-codon rates rather than inventing an $M$ listing.

\paragraph{Spectral and null controls.}
The local geometry is accompanied by a spectral row. The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported as the cubic-polynomial residual. This is a numerical consistency check for the finite coordinate calculation, not a biological law. Against $N=2000$ sampled null objects with seed $42$, the exact-shape row found $1$ exact match after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ high-Jaccard matches. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. The corresponding reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These controls support the claim that the code-label contact is seeing a non-generic finite local pattern. They do not license promotion from code labels to a translation process or to protein structure.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluates whether the local tile supplies code-read information when an external table is withheld. The row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. The observed hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary information is $0.9841269841269841$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{>\mathrm{baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{M\setminus T+\partial}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row conclusion is therefore narrow but strong: for this code-read task, the window-six coordinate structure and boundary tile recover most withheld label-table targets, far above the baseline rate. The object being recovered is a label assignment in a curated codon table. The row does not observe ribosomal decoding, molecular kinetics, translation fidelity, selection history, folding, physical admissibility, or biological function.

\paragraph{Family and codon-module localization.}
The family-level row partitions the leave-one-code-out behavior into $5$ families. The reported hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. This makes the row family-local rather than merely global: the signal is not confined to one named code family. The companion leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$. Its module classification assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row states $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. Thus every displayed boundary codon is accounted for by one of the six named local modules, and every module has predictable or boundary support.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready claim is that a six-bit codon-window geometry exposes a local boundary-tile structure whose readback agrees with curated standard-code labels at high finite-row rates. The claim is deliberately restricted to the code-read layer. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is also not folded protein structure. Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate reality contact designed for that higher layer. Within the supplied finite rows, the supported statement is only that the curated codon-label table, the punctured-cube boundary set $R$, the spectral consistency row, the null controls, and the leave-one-code-out recovery rates jointly define a finite local code-read record.
