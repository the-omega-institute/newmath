\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the $64$ codons. The only reality contact used in this record is the curated standard-code label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label. This contact has codon-label resolution only. It tests code-layer readback and local tile label split, but it does not test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The finite row under study is the leave-one-code-out claim $h1.leave.one.code.out$: if a neighboring genetic-code table changes a target label, the local BEDC window-six tile predicts whether the target falls inside the main local region, its boundary, or outside the local tile.

This is the geometric content of the local tile: it is a finite boundary pattern in codon-coordinate space, not a biological law about DNA.

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
\begin{aligned}

This is the main finite-row verdict: the local codon tile almost always captures the changed target when the boundary allowance is included, and it substantially exceeds the baseline rate. The row is a code-read result. It is not a translation theorem, not a folding theorem, and not a universality theorem.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready verdict is local and limited. Window-six codon coordinates expose a finite boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and the leave-one-code-out data show a strong code-read recovery rate over $23$ tables and $63$ targets. The curated code table supplies only codon-label contact. Therefore no translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this chapter unless a separate higher-layer reality contact is supplied.
