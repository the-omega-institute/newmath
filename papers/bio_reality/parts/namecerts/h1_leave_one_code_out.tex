\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Readback for the Standard Code}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and scope.}
This record concerns a finite code-read experiment on the standard-code codon table. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The biological contact is only the curated codon-to-label table: it assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels, and it does not by itself observe translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality. Thus the finite claim is deliberately local. It says that window-six codon coordinates expose a stable boundary-tile pattern in code-label readback, while the curated labels remain external reality contacts rather than consequences of cube geometry alone.

The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$\begin{aligned}

The same row is observed as a punctured-cube object with finite face data

This is the local tile whose label behavior is tested. It is not promoted to a law of DNA, to a law of translation, or to a statement about realized proteins. The row is a code-layer finite object whose readback is checked against the curated standard-code bridge.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The primary leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. Against that baseline, the reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. Equivalently, the boundary-augmented local object accounts for essentially all but one of the $63$ reported targets at the stated code-read layer. This is the chapter-specific finite claim: when one code is withheld, the local six-bit tile plus boundary information remains highly predictive of the withheld code-label rows relative to the baseline.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite-row evidence supports a code-read boundary statement. The row $R$ is a $13$-codon punctured-cube tile with face vector $(13,16,4)$; the reported spectrum is numerically stable; shape and amino-acid-class-preserving null checks give small reported tail probabilities; leave-one-code-out readback gives $0.9841269841269841$ boundary-augmented hit rate over $63$ targets from $23$ evaluated tables; and the module classification covers all $13$ row codons. The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation event, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these rows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
