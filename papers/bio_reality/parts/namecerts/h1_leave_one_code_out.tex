\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with coordinates, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The reality contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  This contact supports code-layer readback and local split checks, but it is not a mechanism for translation and it is not evidence for folding, physical admissibility, biological function, or universality.  The object under test is therefore a finite codon-label boundary pattern, not the biochemical realization of that pattern.
