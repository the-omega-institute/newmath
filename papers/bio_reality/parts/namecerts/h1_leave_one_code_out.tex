\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and reality contact.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact, not as a mechanism of translation.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code label table whose observations are codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop assignments.  The BEDC-minimal form has four named distinctions: the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and a three-point label class.  Its internal data are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The biological object is therefore the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer.  The contact can test local codon-tile label splits, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or structural order.

The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the row is
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\

The same row is certified as a punctured-cube shape with finite face-vector

This is the local tile boundary that the chapter studies.  It is not asserted to be a global biological law.  It is only a finite row in the window-six codon display, together with the external label readback supplied by the curated table.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the recorded null sample for $M$-shape significance, the experiment used $N=2000$ distinct finite samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries under seed $42$.  The exact shape was seen once, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}$$
The high-Jaccard event $J\geq 0.9$ was seen $25$ times, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid classes also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  There the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding finite-row values are
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
The finite record supplied the size of $M$ but not the member list of $M$, so this paragraph uses the available scalar $|M|=20$ and does not invent an $M$ codon list.

The result supports a code-layer statement: under leave-one-code-out evaluation, most targets remain captured by the local window-six tile and its boundary.  It does not say that translation, folding, admissibility, function, or mechanism has been recovered.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The curated standard-code table is a reality bridge for labels.  It is not a structure experiment, not a translation experiment, not a folding experiment, not a physical-admissibility assay, not a function assay, and not a proof of universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains at the code layer.  A separate higher-layer contact would be required before any claim about protein realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity could be made.  The local conclusion is therefore precise: window-six codon coordinates expose a high-support local boundary-tile structure for code-label readback, with $13$ boundary codons, complete six-module assignment, strong leave-one-code-out rates over $23$ tables and $63$ targets, and finite null-model evidence against trivial shape recovery.
