\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery on the Window-Six Codon Tile}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object and claim.}
The finite object in this record is the standard-code codon table read through a six-coordinate codon display.  Each codon is treated as a point of the six-bit cube $Q_6$, while the amino-acid or stop annotation is taken from the curated standard-code table.  The claim tested here is not that the cube geometry generates biology by itself.  It is the narrower claim that, once the standard labels are supplied as a code-layer contact, the local tile structure gives a strong leave-one-code-out recovery signal for the codons whose labels move across known code-table variants.

The finite exceptional row is the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The audit row reports $|R|=13$, and the same thirteen codons are recovered by the punctured-cube check.  Its reported face data are
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object is not being summarized only by cardinality: the one-dimensional and two-dimensional incidences are also part of the finite row.

\paragraph{Numerical recovery row.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Against the finite baseline rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{base}}=0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
the observed above-baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{above}}=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The stricter row using $M-T$ alone gave
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{M-T}=0.9365079365079365,
\end{aligned}
$$
while adjoining the boundary restored
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{M-T+\partial}=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite signal is concentrated in a local code-read region and its boundary, not in an unrestricted assertion about all codons or all biological phenomena.

The family-level row also remains finite.  It lists five families.  The above-baseline rates are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.  This stratification matters because the aggregate rate is not a single opaque statistic: the one family below perfect recovery is explicitly visible, and the four perfect family rows are not silently pooled into a universal claim.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The module classification row assigns every codon of $R$ to one of six named modules and reports no missing or extra codons.  In finite form the partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness audit gives $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, $missing\_from\_modules=\emptyset$, $extra\_module\_codons=\emptyset$, and $unassigned\_codons=\emptyset$.  The leave-one-codon-out companion row is consistent with this local picture: it reports $anchor\_count=0$, $boundary\_count=5$, $predictable\_count=8$, $closure\_coverage\_high=8$, and $recovery\_rate=1.0$.  Hence the codon-level test does not identify fixed anchors inside this row; it identifies predictable codons together with boundary codons.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral checks.}
The geometric row is supported by two randomization checks.  In the shape-significance run with seed $42$, $N=2000$ distinct samples were attempted through $57377$ total attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only $1$ exact shape match was observed, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and $25$ samples reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row uses the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It reports $observed\_M\_size=20$, $exact\_match\_count=0$, and $lower\_tail\_count\_le\_observed\_M\_size=7$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The spectral row adds an independent numerical check.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic residual and the radial-polynomial residual are both recorded as $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is consistent with numerical satisfaction at the displayed precision.  The same row records the spectral radius in tolerance as $0.6752479308830213$.

\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.}
The evidential contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  That contact supplies codon-to-label readback: codons can be assigned amino-acid or stop labels, and those labels can be compared across code-table rows.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a causal mechanism, or a law valid across all biological settings.  The local tile therefore licenses a code-layer statement only: within the supplied code-label table, the six-coordinate row $R$ and its module boundary organize most leave-one-code-out targets far above the listed baseline.

Any promotion beyond that scope would require a separate empirical contact of the relevant kind.  A folding claim would need structural data.  A translation-realization claim would need a translation experiment or an organism-matched translation record.  A physical-admissibility claim would need an appropriate physical model or measurement.  A function claim would need functional observation.  None of those contacts is present in this finite row.  The present record is therefore a strong finite code-read result and, at the same time, a local boundary statement preventing the code-label readback from being treated as structure, mechanism, function, or universality.
