\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Code Readback Boundary}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the finite set of $64$ codon rows in a fixed codon display. The external contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon a code-layer label, either an amino-acid label or a stop label. This contact is sufficient for code readback and for local label-split tests. It is not a contact with translation dynamics, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary row set.}
The finite boundary row set recovered from the window-six display has cardinality $13$. In the observed row order it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13 .
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row set is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object being tested is not a free biological law. It is a local codon-tile boundary pattern inside $Q_6$, together with a readback map supplied by the curated code table.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the sampled null model with $N=2000$ distinct samples requested under seed $42$, the run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module-size statistic was $|M|=20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support a non-random local code-layer boundary relative to the supplied nulls. They do not by themselves identify a physical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The observed hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the stricter interior statistic gave
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in}\ M-T&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in}\ (M-T)+\partial&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus almost all leave-one-code-out targets are recovered by the finite local tile with boundary allowance, and most are already recovered inside the displayed $M-T$ region. This is a code-read claim: when a code table label is withheld, the finite coordinate geometry often locates the missing label-class row.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The conclusion is deliberately narrow. The finite rows support a local window-six code-read pattern for the curated standard-code table, with strong leave-one-code-out recovery and a small boundary set $R$. They do not show that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity. They do not show translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Any such promotion requires a separate reality contact whose rows observe that higher layer directly. In the present record, amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer output, not a protein-structure or function certificate.
