\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a code-layer display.  A vertex is a codon coordinate, and the external reality contact is the curated standard genetic-code table, which assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The claim tested here is not that biology is generated by the cube.  The claim is the narrower finite-row statement that the window-six coordinate system exposes a local boundary-tile pattern whose readback predicts most known code-table departures after one code table is withheld.

The distinguished set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row records $R$ as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object is not an unrestricted set of codons: it is a finite local tile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces inside the six-bit display.

These numbers are used only as finite coordinate diagnostics.  They do not assert a physical spectrum, an energetic law, or a biochemical mechanism.

The observed shape is rare under the reported finite null.  In the shape-significance row, $N=2000$ distinct samples were evaluated after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sample matched the observed shape, giving

with $25$ samples reaching Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$.  The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The finite row therefore supports the statement that the code-layer tile is not typical for these two finite nulls.  It does not identify the causal process by which the code table arose.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out test.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{\mathrm{base}}=0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
Against this baseline, the window-six tile gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{M\setminus T} &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
h_{M\setminus T+\partial} &= 0.9841269841269841,\\
h_{\mathrm{above\ base}} &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the main finite-row content of the claim.  With one code table withheld, the local coordinate-and-boundary construction recovers almost all recorded targets relative to the baseline.  The conclusion is a code-read conclusion: the tile organizes codon-label departures in the curated table contact.

The family-level row shows that the effect is not concentrated in a single named family.  Five families are recorded.  Their hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The weakest listed family is still far above the baseline row, while four of the five listed families have perfect recorded recovery above baseline.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The module-classification row partitions the $13$ codons of $R$ into six named modules, with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The audit row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, empty extra-module codons, empty missing-from-modules, and empty unassigned codons.  This matters because the leave-one-code-out result is not merely a scalar accuracy statement.  It is attached to a complete local module decomposition of the distinguished codon set.

The companion leave-one-codon-out row records $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchors, closure coverage high at $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Read together, the two leave-one tests say that the boundary tile is doing real finite work at the code-label level: it both classifies the $13$-codon distinguished set and recovers withheld code-table targets with high recorded rates.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The reality contact is deliberately narrow.  It is the curated standard-code table as a codon-label bridge.  It can test code readback and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Therefore the supported conclusion is the following bounded one: within the six-bit codon display, the finite tile $R$ and its module decomposition give a high-performing local predictor for code-layer label departures under the recorded leave-one-code-out experiment.

No stronger biological conclusion follows from this row alone.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is not a folded protein structure; a codon-label reassignment is not a translation mechanism; and a rare tile shape is not a law of molecular biology.  Any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate higher-layer reality contact.  The present row remains aligned precisely because it stops at the code-read boundary.
