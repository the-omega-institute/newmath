\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery for the local codon tile}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite setting.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a curated code-layer contact and asks a deliberately narrow question.  The coordinate object is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with codons read as finite positions in a binary window rather than as biochemical events.  The observed table supplies amino-acid and stop labels for codons, but the only admissible readback in this subsection is code-label readback.  Translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, and universality are outside the evidence supplied here unless an independent contact is attached.

The boundary row singled out by the finite construction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$.  The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube object with face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the evidence unit is not the full biology of the genetic code.  It is a finite local pattern: a coordinate row $R$, a closure-derived set $M$ of size $20$, and the label table used only to check which code labels are recovered when one code table entry is withheld.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The finite row has a reproducible spectral signature.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is within the stated numerical tolerance.  These numbers are used as internal consistency checks for the finite coordinate geometry, not as evidence that molecular translation is geometrically forced.

The shape test also gives a small-tail comparison against sampled alternatives.  In $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, the run needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and $25$ samples had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, there were $0$ exact matches and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size $20$.  The corresponding recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The point of these tests is finite discrimination: the observed local tile is uncommon under the reported null samplers.  The tests do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The local row and closure data gave
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T\;\mathrm{plus\ boundary})&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the main finite-row observation for this subsection.  When code-table information is withheld in the specified leave-one-code-out experiment, the coordinate-and-boundary construction recovers nearly all tested code-layer targets above the baseline rate.  The result is a label-recovery statement over finite rows, not a statement about ribosomes, aminoacylation, organismal selection, or folded protein structure.

\paragraph{Family-level stratification.}
The same run reports $5$ code-family classes.  The family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The alternative-yeast class is therefore the least saturated family in this finite summary, while the other four families are recorded as complete hits under the stated probe.  This stratification is useful because it keeps the claim at the code-read layer: family differences are treated as table-label behavior, not as a universal biological principle.

\paragraph{Codon-module accounting.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out module classification assigns every codon in $R$ to one of the recorded modules, with $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite classification.  In the leave-one-codon-out summary, the recovery rate is $1.0$, with $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and high closure coverage $8$.  These numbers explain why the leave-one-code-out result is not merely a scalar success rate: the recovered behavior is distributed over explicit codon modules inside the thirteen-codon row.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact used here is a curated standard-code table.  It observes codon-to-label assignments and nothing more.  Therefore this subsection licenses the following local conclusion: the six-bit codon coordinate system, together with the finite row $R$, the size-$20$ closure set $M$, and the boundary modules above, exposes a strong code-layer recovery pattern in the leave-one-code-out test.  It does not license claims about translation realization, folded structure, physical feasibility, biological function, evolutionary mechanism, or a universal law of DNA.  In particular, amino-acid label recovery is not protein-structure recovery, and a local codon tile is not evidence that all biological organization must follow this coordinate geometry.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
For claim $h1.leave.one.code.out$, the finite data are sufficient for a ready code-read chapter.  The supported statement is that the reported local codon-tile construction has high finite recovery against the curated standard-code label table: $23$ tables, $63$ targets, baseline $0.19821739130434785$, and boundary-augmented hit rate $0.9841269841269841$.  The chapter remains intentionally bounded at the label-table layer.
