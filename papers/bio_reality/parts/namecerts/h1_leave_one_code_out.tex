\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback on the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The record for \texttt{h1.leave.one.code.out} is a code-layer test on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is treated as a six-bit display point, while the biological labels are supplied by a curated standard genetic-code table.  Thus the finite object being tested is not translation as a cellular process, but a codon-to-label readback table with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The carrier has enough internal combinatorics to speak about coordinates, closures, spectra, and relations, but the amino-acid and stop labels themselves enter through the curated table contact.

\paragraph{Distinguished row set.}
The distinguished residue set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row audit identifies $R$ with a punctured-cube shape whose face-vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile being measured.  Its geometric role is boundary-sensitive: it can organize the codon-label split at the table level, but it is not by itself an organismal law, a molecular mechanism, or a statement about folded structure.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The associated spectral row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the finite spectral calculation is numerically closed to the stated tolerance.  A separate shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-overlap count was $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed size was $|M|=20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{lower}}=0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ target events.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the observed above-baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$ and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary rows was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The point of the test is therefore narrow and finite: when one code table is withheld, the window-six local structure recovers almost all target placements above the baseline available from the comparison tables.  The result supports a local code-read pattern, not a promotion from labels to protein realization.

\paragraph{Family and module audit.}
The family-level split contains five families.  The above-baseline rates by family are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid codes, $1.0$ for the candidate-other family, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate codes.  The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and there are no extra module codons, missing module codons, or unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.

$$
Each module has either a predictable row or a boundary row in the leave-one-codon audit.  That companion audit reports boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, closure-coverage high count $8$, anchor count $0$, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The curated standard-code table supplies codon-label assignments.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or cross-organism necessity.  Therefore the chapter records a finite code-layer readback: window-six geometry and the curated labels align on a local boundary-tile pattern, with strong leave-one-code-out recovery across the finite table set.  Any claim about translation realization, structural order, admissible chemistry, function, or universality would require a separate contact of the appropriate kind.  In this record, amino-acid label sequences remain labels; they are not folded proteins and they are not functional assays.
