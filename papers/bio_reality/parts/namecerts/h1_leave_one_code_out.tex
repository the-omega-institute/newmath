\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery for a window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite record tests whether the window-six codon coordinate display carries a local recovery signal for variant code labels when one code table is withheld from the comparison pool. The biological object is the standard-code codon table, used only as a curated codon-to-label contact. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with local faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes treated as finite distinctions. The claim is deliberately restricted to the code-read layer: a codon label may be read from a table, and a local tile may be checked against that label split, but this does not yet say anything about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Finite carrier row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This matters because the recovery calculation is not performed on an arbitrary verbal grouping: it is attached to a finite codon subset with a recorded face count. The row should therefore be read as a local combinatorial object inside $Q_6$, not as a biological law about all codon usage.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated spectral calculation records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the reported value is numerically at tolerance for the stated polynomial check. The spectral numbers are used here only as finite diagnostics of the local codon-tile row. They are not promoted to kinetics, biophysics, or selection pressure.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
The first shape null used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$. Among $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ were duplicate retries. The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
where $25$ samples had Jaccard score at least $0.9$. A second amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, had observed $M$ size $20$, exact match count $0$, and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size. Its reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the statement that the row is unusual relative to the sampled finite nulls. They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
For the claim row itself, $23$ code tables were evaluated and $63$ targets were tested. The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. The local tile raises the reported hit rate above baseline to $0.9841269841269841$. Within $M\setminus T$ the hit rate is $0.9365079365079365$, and within $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary allowance it is again $0.9841269841269841$.
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{in\ }M\setminus T&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{in\ }M\setminus T\mathrm{\ plus\ boundary}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite evidence says that, after one code is left out, the local codon-tile organization recovers almost all recorded targets relative to the baseline. The word ``recovers'' is used in the tabular sense only: it means the finite readback check lands in the recorded label region or its boundary, not that a cell translates, folds, or functions in virtue of this geometry.

\paragraph{Family-level split.}
The family summary contains $5$ families. The above-baseline hit rates are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. This distribution is important because the leave-one-code-out signal is not reported as a single pooled accident only; it also persists across the named family partition, with the alternative-yeast family being the only listed family below perfect recovery in the row supplied here.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module check assigns all $13$ codons in $R$, with $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either a predictable or boundary status in the supplied finite classification. The leave-one-codon-out row separately reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. This is consistent with the interpretation that the row is organized by local recoverability and boundary placement rather than by a single universal anchor codon.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The external contact is a curated standard genetic-code table. Its observed content is a codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop assignment table. That contact can test code-read claims and local codon-tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, folded-protein geometry, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism. In particular, amino-acid label readback is still only label readback. A separate structural or functional contact would be required before any claim about folding, physical feasibility, organismal action, or mechanism could be made.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
The finite row supports a narrow code-layer conclusion: in the supplied leave-one-code-out experiment, the window-six local tile has a high recovery rate for the recorded codon-label targets, far above the reported baseline and with complete module assignment for the $13$-codon punctured-cube row. The same row blocks stronger biological promotion. The result is a precise local readback pattern for curated code labels, not a theory of translation, protein structure, admissible chemistry, biological function, or universal codon law.
