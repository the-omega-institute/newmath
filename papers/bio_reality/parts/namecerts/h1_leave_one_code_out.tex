\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Scope of the record.}
This record studies the standard-code codon table as a code-layer object inside the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  The relevant display has six binary positions, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  Its internal data are coordinate placement, closure behavior, spectrum, and relation membership.  The only biological contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, read as codon-to-label assignment data.  Consequently, the record may test whether local window-six geometry is useful for code-label recovery, but it does not test translation as a biochemical process, folded structure, physical admissibility, realized function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Finite exceptional row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of codon names: it has a finite local cubical shape, with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ square faces after the relevant puncture is imposed.

The recovery rate above that baseline was

\begin{aligned}

The numerical contrast is therefore local and code-layered: the cubical row and its boundary information recover almost all held-out code-table targets relative to the baseline, while the curated table remains the only source of amino-acid and stop labels.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The data support a narrow conclusion: in the window-six codon cube, the exceptional row $R$ has a punctured-cube shape, a stable spectral signature, complete module accounting, and strong leave-one-out recovery against the supplied finite code-table tests.  The curated standard-code table supplies the label contact needed to say which codons carry amino-acid or stop labels.  It does not supply a contact for ribosomal realization, molecular folding, physical feasibility, organismal function, or a universal biological principle.  Any such higher-layer statement would need an additional observation source matched to that layer; it is not licensed by the codon-label table or by the local cubical recovery numbers alone.
