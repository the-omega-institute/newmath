\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate record.}
The claim concerns a finite codon-window computation on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is the codon display obtained by treating each codon as a point of the cube, while the observed labels are taken from a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The record is therefore a code-layer readback: it tests whether a local codon-window tile separates a distinguished boundary class, not whether translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality has been observed.

The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a finite cubical subobject it has the recorded face count
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This identifies $R$ as a punctured local cube pattern inside the window-six coordinate system.  The complementary internal marker $M$ is not used here as a mechanistic object; in the supplied finite rows it is measured through its size and spectral behavior, with observed size $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape evidence.}
The finite spectral row gives the numerical triplet
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, reported also as the radial polynomial value.  Thus the finite computation is numerically coherent at the displayed tolerance: the spectral-radius row records $0.6752479308830213$ and the polynomial residual is effectively zero at the precision of the run.

The shape-significance row used $N=2000$ sampled tables, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null run also used $N=2000$.  It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support a finite separation signal for the code table, with the important qualification that they are still computations on codon labels and coordinate tiles.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
For the claim $h1.leave.one.code.out$, the experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The stricter internal hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, while allowing the boundary class restored the hit rate to $0.9841269841269841$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T\ \mathrm{plus\ boundary})&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The readback is therefore concentrated near the finite tile and its boundary, rather than being explained by the baseline table rate alone.

The family-level row divides $23$ evaluated tables into $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  This is a strong finite regularity across the available family partition, but it is still only a code-label regularity.

\paragraph{Codon-level module accounting.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports recovery rate $1.0$, predictable count $8$, boundary count $5$, anchor count $0$, and high closure coverage $8$.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ members of $R$ with no extras and no omissions:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{size}}&=13,\\
\mathrm{assigned\ count}&=13,\\
\mathrm{missing\ from\ modules}&=0,\\
\mathrm{unassigned\ codons}&=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The displayed modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite record.  This is the local meaning of the leave-one-code-out result: the standard-code label table exposes a recoverable tile-and-boundary pattern in $Q_6$.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The only empirical contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments and supports code-layer tests of local label splitting.  It does not supply a ribosomal realization, a folded protein structure, a thermodynamic admissibility test, a biological function assay, or a general law governing all organisms.  Amino-acid label readback is therefore not a protein-structure observation.  Any claim crossing from codon labels to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate contact designed for that layer.
