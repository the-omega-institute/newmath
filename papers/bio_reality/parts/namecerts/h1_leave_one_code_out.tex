\paragraph{Finite object.}

The carrier is not a claim about translation dynamics; it is the coordinate display in which codons are read as vertices and standard-code labels are supplied by a curated external table.  The BEDC-minimal form has four visible distinctions: the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and a three-point label class.  The internal structure used here is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the readback is the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  Its resolution is codon label readback.  The contact can test the code-read layer and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization.  Consequently, the correct interpretation is narrow: the data support a code-layer local tile with a boundary, not a global biological law.
