\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback on the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record concerns the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ equipped with a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological contact is only the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and therefore supplies a code-layer readback rather than a mechanism.  The finite BEDC object tested here is not translation, not folding, not physical admissibility, not biological function, and not a universal law of genetic coding.  Its scope is the following narrower question: after a standard-code label table is used as an external reality contact, does the window-six coordinate expose a local boundary tile that is stable under leave-one-code-out probes?

\paragraph{Punctured-cube row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The observed codon list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
    &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite evidence is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it also carries a small two-dimensional incidence signature.  In this chapter, the phrase local tile means exactly this finite coordinate-and-closure datum together with standard-code labels supplied by the curated table.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated spectrum separates the main tile scale from the boundary scale.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check has residual
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is numerically zero at the resolution of the run.  This spectral fact is used only as an internal consistency check on the finite coordinate.  It does not assert that biological systems compute this spectrum, nor that the spectrum is a physical cause of codon reassignment.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
Against the unconstrained finite null sample, $N=2000$ tables were evaluated after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Against the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$, the observed $M$ size was $20$.  The exact shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, and the reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support a finite shape anomaly relative to the sampled nulls.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical mechanism, because the nulls are combinatorial probes on label tables rather than experiments on translation dynamics or molecular structure.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The main leave-one-code-out finite row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the observed hit rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T\;\mathrm{plus\ boundary})&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit}(\mathrm{above\ baseline})&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the tested readback is sharply above the finite baseline in this row.  The correct interpretation is local and conditional: when one code table is withheld from the comparison set, the window-six coordinate plus the boundary row recovers the target label position at high frequency.  The statement is not that the coordinate generates the genetic code from first principles; the curated code table remains the reality contact for the labels.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The by-family split reports $5$ families.  Their hit rates above baseline are

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&=1.0,\\

$$
The alternative-yeast family is the weakest finite family row, but it remains above the raw baseline.  The other four listed families are perfect at the reported resolution.  This strengthens the local readback claim because the aggregate rate is not carried only by one named family.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The $13$ boundary codons are partitioned into six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has a predictable-or-boundary representative.  This is the finite reason the leave-one-code-out result is recorded as a boundary-tile readback rather than as a scattered list of unrelated codon exceptions.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out support.}
The separate leave-one-codon-out row gives anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This row is compatible with the same boundary interpretation: the evidence is concentrated in closure and boundary behavior rather than in independent anchor codons.  It therefore supports the local-tile account while also warning against overpromotion.  A boundary-supported codon label readback is weaker than a mechanistic claim about how cells implement translation.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite-row verdict is that the six-bit codon coordinate exposes a real local readback pattern when it is connected to the curated standard-code table.  The strongest numerical row is the leave-one-code-out rate $0.9841269841269841$ above baseline across $23$ tables and $63$ targets, with the same value after adding the boundary row.  The punctured-cube row, the spectral residual near zero, the two null-model significance checks, the family split, and the complete module classification all point to the same finite object.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
