\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the four-base codon alphabet and then compared with a curated standard-code label table. The contact is deliberately narrow: it is a codon-to-label bridge for amino-acid and stop labels. It is not a translation experiment, a folding experiment, a physical-admissibility assay, a functional assay, a mechanism, or a universality proof. The BEDC-side object is therefore a finite coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation packet whose biological readback is only the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Distinguished finite row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In the supplied finite run, the row is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube tile with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. This is the concrete local-tile datum used in the leave-one-code-out test: it says that the standard-code label readback sees a finite thirteen-codon boundary row with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces, not that the row is a global biological law.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the shape null model with $N=2000$ sampled alternatives, the run reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ samples at Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$. The amino-acid-class-preserving null, also at $N=2000$, reports observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row is not merely a common outcome under the supplied nulls. The explicit list of the full $M$ row is not part of this finite-row packet, so the present paragraph uses only the reported $M$-size and the tested $M$-relative hit rates.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The local tile raised the hit rate above baseline to $0.9841269841269841$. Inside $M-T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M-T$ together with the boundary it was again $0.9841269841269841$. In finite-row form,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}(>\mathrm{baseline})&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M-T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M-T+\partial)&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports a code-read claim: under the supplied curated code-label contact, the window-six local tile predicts most leave-one-code-out targets relative to the baseline. It does not establish that the codon coordinate causes translation or that the label sequence realizes a folded, admissible, functional protein.

This is the strongest local form of the claim: the tile is not just a set of thirteen codons, but a fully classified finite boundary packet for the standard-code label readback.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite evidence is sufficient for the following local conclusion. The window-six codon coordinates expose a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary tile whose module classification is complete, whose spectral residual is near zero at the reported precision, whose shape is rare under the supplied nulls, and whose leave-one-code-out hit rate is $0.9841269841269841$ over $23$ evaluated tables and $63$ targets. The conclusion stops at code readback. No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
