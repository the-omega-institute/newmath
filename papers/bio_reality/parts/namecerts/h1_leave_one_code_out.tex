\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Tile Record}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Carrier and finite readback.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with coordinates inherited from the three nucleotide positions and their binary window encoding. The biological contact is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it is used here as a code-layer readback bridge. The carrier supplies coordinates, closures, spectra, and relations; the label table supplies the external labels. These two roles are deliberately separated.

The distinguished residual row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In the observed finite row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube boundary object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the readback is not a diffuse property of all $64$ codons. It is concentrated in a finite local tile with thirteen displayed codons, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The observed $M$-shape was compared with $2000$ sampled null rows under the recorded shape experiment. Because the sampler required distinct rows, it made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null with $2000$ samples recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$. Its finite p-values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0,\qquad p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $0.0$ is the empirical finite-row value for zero observed exact matches in $2000$ samples; it is not an analytic proof of impossibility.

\end{aligned}
$$
The observed hit rate above baseline was

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
while the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was
$$
\begin{aligned}

After including the boundary contribution, the recorded rate was again
$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
Thus the finite row supports the following chapter-level claim: when one code table is withheld, the six-bit local tile and its boundary coordinates recover almost all of the held-out code-read targets above the recorded baseline. This is a code-label statement, not a statement about translation machinery.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The chapter records a local codon-tile phenomenon in the code-read layer. It does not claim that DNA obeys the six-bit geometry as a biological necessity. It does not claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Any promotion beyond the code table would require a separate higher-layer contact, such as an explicit translation experiment, a structure contact, a physical admissibility assay, or a functional measurement. Without such a contact, the finite result remains exactly this: a reproducible boundary-tile readback for standard-code labels inside $Q_6$.
