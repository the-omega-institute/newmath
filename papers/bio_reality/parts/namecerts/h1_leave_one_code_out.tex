\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery in the window-six codon cube}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Claim under test.}
The record \texttt{h1.leave.one.code.out} concerns a finite code-read phenomenon in the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is the $64$-vertex codon display obtained by reading each codon as a six-bit word, two bits per nucleotide position.  The distinctions used here are deliberately local: a tile face in the cube, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The readback is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The question is not whether the cube geometry is a biological cause.  The question is whether the finite coordinate packet detects a stable local boundary pattern when one code table is withheld and then tested against the remaining code-read data.

\paragraph{Observed residual set.}
The finite residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same computation records $R$ as a punctured cubical object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not being treated as an unconstrained list of exceptional codons.  It carries a small cubical incidence profile: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces.  This is the finite shape against which the leave-one-code-out readback is evaluated.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated spectral calculation gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is numerically $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the reported cubic constraint is satisfied to floating-point tolerance.  The role of this spectral row is modest: it records that the finite coordinate object has a reproducible numerical signature.  It does not turn the codon table into a dynamical law, and it does not identify any biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the stated finite null experiment, the exact-shape test used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap test at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ found $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null experiment preserving amino-acid class structure used the same sample count and seed.  It observed $0$ exact shape matches and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size.  The recorded observed size is $|M|=20$, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support a narrow finite-row conclusion: the local shape is uncommon under the sampled comparison procedures.  They do not by themselves supply translation physics, folded structure, selection history, or organism-level function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out performance.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the stricter rate inside $M\setminus T$ was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9365079365079365.
\end{aligned}
$$
After adding the boundary contribution, the rate returned to
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central finite observation for the claim.  With one code table left out, the coordinate-and-boundary packet recovers nearly all target instances relative to the stated baseline.  The result is a code-label readback result, not a claim that the six-bit cube generates the genetic code as a law of nature.

The weakest family row is still well above the baseline rate, and four of the five family rows are perfect at this resolution.  This strengthens the local readback interpretation: the pattern is not confined to a single code-table family in the finite data supplied here.

\paragraph{Codon-module accounting.}
The codon-module classification is complete on the residual row.  It assigns all $13$ codons in $R$, leaves no extra module codons, and records no missing or unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has a predictable or boundary explanation in the supplied classification row.  The companion leave-one-codon-out data report $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ codons with high closure coverage, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This is consistent with the module reading: the residual set is not merely a collection of isolated exceptions, but a finite arrangement whose codons are exhausted by the stated module partition.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The claim is therefore ready at the code-read layer.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label observations, and the finite coordinate packet supplies the local geometry used to test the leave-one-code-out split.  The successful row is narrow: it says that the six-coordinate codon display exposes a boundary-tile pattern that predicts withheld code-label targets at high rate within the finite comparison scheme.  It also says that the residual codons occupy a punctured-cube shape with a reproducible spectral signature and complete module accounting.

\paragraph{Scope limit.}
No stronger biological statement follows from these rows.  The data here do not demonstrate translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a causal mechanism, or a universal biological principle.  Amino-acid label readback remains a code-layer observation.  Crossing into translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate empirical contact carrying that higher-layer information.  In particular, CUN/UUR wording, tRNA-Leu notation, or cross-organism phrasing cannot be converted into structure evidence by this packet alone.  The present record should therefore be read as a finite local codon-label result with an explicit boundary against promotion to higher biological layers.
