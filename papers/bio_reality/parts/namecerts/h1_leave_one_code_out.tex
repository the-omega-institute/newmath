\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite external contact against a window-six coordinate carrier.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the $64$ codon displays over the alphabet $\{A,C,G,U\}$.  The internal BEDC-side distinctions used here are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the biological readback is only the curated standard-code label table, namely the assignment of each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The finite object is therefore not a translation mechanism.  It is a code-read contact: a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class may be inspected against the table, but no folding, physical admissibility, function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from this chapter alone.

\paragraph{The thirteen-codon relation row.}
The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified codon list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
   &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the relation is not merely a set of exceptional codons: it has a finite local geometry with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the displayed codon cube.  The code table supplies the labels on these codons, while the cube supplies only the coordinate and adjacency frame.

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ rows with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, there were $0$ exact shape matches, so the reported exact-shape value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite-row values support a local code-layer anomaly relative to the specified null samplers.  They do not upgrade the anomaly to a global biological law, because the nulls test finite label-table geometry rather than biochemical realization.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ genetic-code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9365079365079365,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary row was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same value, $0.9841269841269841$, is recorded as the hit rate above baseline for the leave-one-code-out claim.  Thus, when one code table is held out, the finite coordinate-and-boundary construction recovers the held-out code-layer target far above the baseline rate.  The conclusion is deliberately narrow: it is a recovery statement about codon-label readback within finite table rows, not a statement about translation machinery.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The chapter-level verdict is that window-six codon coordinates expose a robust local boundary-tile structure for standard-code label readback.  The empirical row is finite, explicit, and numerically constrained: $R$ has $13$ codons with face-vector $(13,16,4)$; the observed $M$-row has size $20$; the main spectrum has $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$; the shape-null exact value is $0.0005$; the amino-acid-class-preserving lower-tail value is $0.0035$; and the leave-one-code-out boundary hit rate is $0.9841269841269841$ over $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The contact boundary is equally explicit.  The curated standard-code table tests codon-label readback and local label splits only.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or the claim that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as biological necessity.  Any such higher-layer claim requires a separate reality contact.
