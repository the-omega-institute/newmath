\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery on the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.code.out}$ concerns a finite code-read experiment on the codon cube $Q_6$.  The coordinate object is the six-bit codon display, while the observed labels are supplied by the curated standard genetic-code table.  The experiment asks whether the local tile cut determined from the standard code keeps predictive force when whole alternative code tables are withheld from the construction.  The result is not a statement about translation dynamics.  It is a statement about codon-to-label readback: the six-bit cube, its local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class together isolate a small boundary region whose code-layer behavior remains visible across withheld tables.

\paragraph{Finite carrier and exceptional region.}
The finite exceptional region $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The codons recorded in $R$ are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is reported as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  This means that the observed code-read packet is not merely a list of thirteen labels: it has a small cubical incidence profile inside $Q_6$, with thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four local faces after the puncture.  The larger tile support $M$ is observed with size $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison, and the leave-one-code-out statistic separately records recovery both inside $M\setminus T$ and inside $M\setminus T$ together with its boundary.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral row attached to the same finite construction gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value as the radial residual, so the numerical row satisfies the recorded cubic relation to floating-point tolerance.  The spectral radius entry is again $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers should be read only as code-layer geometry of the finite codon tile.  They do not describe ribosomal realization, biochemical admissibility, protein folding, phenotype, or selective mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite randomization row with seed $42$, $N=2000$ samples, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, the exact shape was matched once.  The exact-shape probability is therefore $p=0.0005$.  The high-overlap comparison counted $25$ cases with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$.  The duplicate retry count was $55377$, so the finite sampling row is sharply constrained by the discreteness of the codon-table combinatorics.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null row, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact shape count was $0$, while the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size $20$ was $7$.  This gives an exact-shape value $0.0$ for that comparison and a lower-tail value $0.0035$.  Thus the observed local tile is not reproduced often under either recorded finite null, even before any biological-mechanism claim is considered.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ withheld targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  Within $M\setminus T$, the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and within $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary it was $0.9841269841269841$.  These figures say that the withheld code-table targets are almost always recovered by the local tile plus boundary readback, relative to the recorded baseline.  The experiment is therefore a finite robustness test for code labels, not a proof that the coordinate geometry generates the biological code.

\paragraph{Family resolution.}
The family-level row partitions the leave-one-code-out behavior into five families.  The recorded hit-rate-above-baseline values are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  The alternative-yeast family is the only family in this row below perfect recovery, but it remains substantially above the baseline row.  This family split is useful because it prevents the global statistic from hiding which withheld-code classes carry the residual failures.

\paragraph{Codon-module accounting.}
The module classification row accounts for all thirteen codons in $R$.  The module $\mathrm{agr}$ contains $\mathrm{AGA}$ and $\mathrm{AGG}$; $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ contains $\mathrm{AAA}$; $\mathrm{cun}$ contains $\mathrm{CUA}$, $\mathrm{CUC}$, $\mathrm{CUG}$, and $\mathrm{CUU}$; $\mathrm{ile\_met}$ contains $\mathrm{AUA}$; $\mathrm{ssn}$ contains $\mathrm{UCA}$ and $\mathrm{UUA}$; and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$ contains $\mathrm{UAA}$, $\mathrm{UAG}$, and $\mathrm{UGA}$.  The same row reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, $13$ assigned codons, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has either a predictable or boundary status in the recorded classification.  The leave-one-codon-out row is consistent with this boundary picture: it reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Reality contact and local boundary.}
The only empirical contact used here is the curated standard-code table, read as a codon-to-label table.  It supplies amino-acid and stop labels for codons; it does not supply a translation apparatus, a folded structure, a physical feasibility assay, or a function measurement.  The finite experiment may therefore support a local label split in the codon cube, but it may not be promoted into a claim about biological necessity.  In particular, no conclusion about translation, folding, physical admissibility, functional realization, mechanism, or universality follows from these rows unless an independent contact at that higher layer is added.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite rows make $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.code.out}$ ready as a code-read claim.  The strongest row is the $23$-table, $63$-target leave-one-code-out result, where the boundary-augmented hit rate is $0.9841269841269841$ against a baseline of $0.19821739130434785$.  The supporting rows identify the thirteen-codon punctured-cube region, its $(13,16,4)$ incidence profile, the spectral values $\lambda_M$, $\lambda_R$, and $\mu$, two finite null comparisons with small tail probabilities, and a complete module assignment of all thirteen codons.  The claim is therefore precise but narrow: window-six coordinates expose a robust local codon-label boundary pattern, while all higher biological readings remain outside the scope of the supplied contact.
