\subsection{Leave-one-code-out stability of the codon boundary tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Record.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite label contact on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is the $64$-vertex binary display of codons, while the observed labels are supplied by the curated standard-code table.  The internal distinctions used here are coordinate position, local closure, spectral response, and codon-to-label relation.  The question is deliberately narrow: when one code table is withheld from a family of related code reads, does the local boundary tile found in the standard-code geometry continue to identify the reassignment targets better than the ambient baseline?

\paragraph{Finite boundary set.}
The row named $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit assigns all $13$ members of $R$ and leaves no extra or unassigned codons.  The modules are $\{AGA,AGG\}$ for $agr$, $\{AAA\}$ for $ar\_lys$, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for $cun$, $\{AUA\}$ for $ile\_met$, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ for $ssn$, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for $stop\_trp$.  Thus the boundary row is not just a list of outliers; it is partitioned into the codon families that carry the reassignment burden in the leave-one experiments.

\paragraph{Spectral and null checks.}
The finite geometry has a measured spectral profile
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the reported cubic relation is satisfied to numerical tolerance.  Against $2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$, the exact-shape count is $1$, giving $p=0.0005$, while the high-Jaccard count at threshold $0.9$ is $25$, giving $p=0.0125$.  A second null model preserving amino-acid class has observed $M$-side size $20$, exact-shape count $0$ in $2000$ trials, lower-tail count $7$, empirical exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These tests do not prove a biological mechanism; they only show that the local row is not a typical random finite shape under the two stated finite sampling schemes.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ withheld targets.  The ambient baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9365079365079365,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same value, $0.9841269841269841$, is recorded for the rate above the baseline.  Hence the finite claim is not that the codon cube generates the genetic code, but that this particular six-coordinate boundary construction recovers almost all withheld reassignment targets in the evaluated table collection, far above the listed baseline.

\paragraph{Family audit.}
The family split contains $5$ families.  The family-level rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate-other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate codes.  The alternative-yeast family is therefore the weakest of the five reported families, but it still remains well above the ambient baseline.  The leave-one-codon-out companion row records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high-coverage count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Each module has either predictable or boundary support, matching the complete module assignment above.

\paragraph{Biological scope.}
The only empirical contact used here is the curated standard-code codon table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-layer readback and local codon-label splitting.  It does not observe ribosomal translation as a process, folded protein geometry, thermodynamic admissibility, biological function, or a universal generative mechanism.  Consequently, amino-acid label readback remains a code-layer fact.  No claim about folding, structural order, physical admissibility, function, translation realization, or universality follows from these rows unless a separate contact for that higher layer is supplied.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded statement.  In the six-coordinate codon display, the $13$-codon boundary row $R$ forms a punctured-cube tile with module-complete reassignment support.  Its spectral row is numerically stable, its shape is rare under the stated null samplings, and its leave-one-code-out recovery over $23$ tables and $63$ targets reaches $0.9841269841269841$ when the boundary extension is allowed.  The result is a local code-read regularity tied to curated codon labels, not a promotion to molecular mechanism or organism-level law.
