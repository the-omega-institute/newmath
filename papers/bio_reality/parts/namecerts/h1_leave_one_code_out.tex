\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary for the Window-Six Codon Tile}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
This record concerns a finite code-read experiment on the standard-code codon table. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a binary display for the four-letter codon alphabet. The biological contact used here is only the curated codon-to-label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label. The experiment asks whether a local window-six tile, built from coordinate, closure, spectral, and relation data, predicts which reassignment targets are recovered when one genetic-code table is held out. The object under test is therefore not translation itself, nor protein folding, nor cellular function. It is the finite label-readback layer of the codon table.

\paragraph{Relation row.}
The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the relation is not an arbitrary prose grouping: it is a finite subset of the codon cube with a recorded combinatorial profile. The row also decomposes into six named modules, all accounted for without surplus or omission:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit gives $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The spectral row records two local radii and one coupling value:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The associated cubic relation is numerically satisfied to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the radial polynomial check. The finite shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$. It found $1$ exact shape match, $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, and therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The distinct-sample attempt count was $57377$, with $55377$ duplicate retries. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, and the reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the claim that the local tile is a finite, non-random-looking code-layer pattern in the supplied coordinate system. They do not identify a physical force or a universal law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total reassignment targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{\mathrm{baseline}}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
h_{>\mathrm{baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
h_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
h_{M\setminus T+\partial}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family split has five families. The above-baseline family rates are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families. This makes the finite conclusion sharp: within this curated label table, the local window-six construction nearly always recovers the held-out code-level target class, and the weaker alternative-yeast family is still well above the recorded baseline.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out companion row.}
The codon-level companion row reports no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. In symbols,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\ count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\ count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\ coverage\ high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\ count}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Together with the module audit, this says that the relation row is not merely a list of memorable codons. It is organized by local codon neighborhoods whose boundary and closure behavior can be checked against finite held-out rows.

\paragraph{Scientific boundary.}
The contact used in this chapter is a curated standard-code label table. It can test code-read behavior and local label splits. It cannot test whether translation is realized in a cell, whether an amino-acid sequence folds, whether a molecule is physically admissible, whether a biological function occurs, whether a mechanism has been identified, or whether the pattern is universal across biology. Amino-acid label readback is still only label readback. A separate empirical contact would be required before any structural, functional, mechanistic, or universal claim could be made.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
For claim $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.code.out}$, the finite data support a code-layer statement: the window-six local tile and its boundary row recover held-out codon-label targets at rate $0.9841269841269841$ above a baseline of $0.19821739130434785$ across $23$ evaluated tables and $63$ targets. The same packet records the $13$-codon punctured-cube relation, its $(13,16,4)$ face-vector, the spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, and the significant finite null comparisons. The chapter therefore records a ready code-read result, with the higher biological layers explicitly left unclaimed.
