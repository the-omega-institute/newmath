\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with three nucleotide positions and four choices per position. The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label. This contact has resolution at the code-read layer. It supplies label readback and local split tests, but it does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The claim tested in this row is therefore deliberately narrow: window-six coordinates may expose a local boundary-tile structure around standard-code labels, while the labels themselves remain external reality contacts.

\paragraph{Distinguished residual set.}
The row isolates a residual codon set $R$ of cardinality $13$. The verified list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row verifies that this is a punctured-cube pattern with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-layer object is not merely a list of exceptional labels. It carries a finite combinatorial shape: thirteen codon vertices, sixteen local edges, and four two-dimensional faces inside the coordinate cube. The row should be read as a local tile statement about codon-label geometry, not as a claim that the genetic code is generated by this geometry.

\paragraph{Spectral signature.}
The associated spectrum gives three scalar checks. The recorded values are

\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The same run reports a cubic, or radial, polynomial residual of $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the numerical spectral check is within the stated tolerance. The spectral row is used only as an internal finite-coordinate diagnostic: it says that the local shape has a stable numerical signature under the tested construction. It does not identify a physical energy, a folding order, or a dynamical translation mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the recorded finite null model, the observed $M$-shape is rare. In $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$, the exact shape matched once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap test counted $25$ cases with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling log also records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, and the reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row anomaly at the code-read layer. They do not authorize a biological mechanism claim: the null models test label-table geometry, not ribosomal realization, molecular stability, or organismal function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out test.}
The direct leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The coordinate-tile predictor achieved
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ }M\setminus T&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ }M\setminus T\mathrm{\ plus\ boundary}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus almost all held-out code targets are recovered once the local $M\setminus T$ region and its boundary are allowed to participate. The result is a code-read recovery statistic: it measures how well the finite coordinate row predicts held-out label differences across curated code tables. It does not imply that the window-six row implements translation or that the same boundary explains protein structure.

The row remains finite and table-relative: it is a strong local readback pattern over curated code variants, not a universal law of codon biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and module closure.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out row reports no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. The module classification covers all thirteen codons in $R$ with no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The verified modules are

The accounting identity is

This confirms that every residual codon belongs to a named finite module and that every module has predictable or boundary status in the tested row. The module names are bookkeeping for the code-label boundary; they are not claims about biochemical modules, folded motifs, or functional domains.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The row supports the following self-contained conclusion. In the six-bit codon cube, the curated standard-code label table exposes a thirteen-codon residual set with punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, stable spectral scalars $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, low null-model probabilities, complete residual-module accounting, and high leave-one-code-out recovery across $23$ evaluated code tables and $63$ targets. The finite evidence is therefore ready for the code-read claim: local codon-tile geometry helps recover held-out code-label rows. The same evidence must not be promoted beyond its contact. No translation, no folding, no physical admissibility, no biological function, no mechanism, and no universality follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
