\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback on the Window-Six Codon Cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
This BioReality record concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer readback problem on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The finite input is not a claim about translation chemistry or protein realization.  It is a coordinate-and-label test: codons are displayed as finite cube vertices, a local tile is extracted, and the curated standard-code label table is used only to ask whether the local boundary structure predicts where code-table changes concentrate.

The distinguished residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the supplied finite row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row records $R$ as a punctured-cube object with face-vector data $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the observation is not merely that thirteen codons were selected; the selected thirteen carry a specific local cubical incidence pattern with sixteen one-dimensional faces and four two-dimensional faces after the puncture.  This is the finite geometric support for treating the code-table readback as a local boundary-tile phenomenon.

The point of this number is modest: it records a stable finite spectral signature of the selected shape, not a biological force law.

The shape comparison against sampled alternatives used $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$.  The exact-shape count was $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the exact-shape count was $0$ in $2000$ trials, while $7$ trials landed in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size of $20$. \begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the narrower statement that the observed local shape is unusual under the stated finite nulls.  They do not identify a molecular mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the recorded hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ after adjoining the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  Equivalently, in the boundary-enlarged local window, the observed recovery nearly saturates the evaluated target set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T\ \mathrm{plus\ boundary})&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central code-read observation for claim $h1.leave.one.code.out$: when one code table is left out, the finite local codon-tile boundary recovers the held-out target behavior far above the recorded baseline.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope limit.}
The only biological contact asserted here is the curated standard-code table, understood as a codon-to-label readback table.  It says that codons receive amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code.  That contact is enough to evaluate code-layer label splits and local boundary recovery.  It is not an observation of ribosomal translation dynamics, protein folding, thermodynamic admissibility, cellular function, or a universal biological rule.

Consequently the supported statement is deliberately narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile pattern whose leave-one-code-out recovery is far above the recorded baseline, with $63$ targets across $23$ evaluated tables and a boundary-enlarged hit rate $0.9841269841269841$.  The same finite row supplies the thirteen-codon residual set, the punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral values $\lambda_M$, $\lambda_R$, and $\mu$, null-model shape probabilities, family-level recovery, and complete module assignment.  No translation claim, folded-structure claim, physical-admissibility claim, functional claim, mechanistic claim, or universality claim follows from those data unless a separate empirical contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
