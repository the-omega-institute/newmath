\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Record.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer object.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, whose points are read as codon displays after fixing the usual nucleotide alphabet.  The distinction being tested is not a global biological principle, but a local tile boundary: a finite set of codons forms a punctured-cube pattern, and the amino-acid or stop labels attached to those codons are supplied by a curated code table rather than by the coordinate geometry alone.  The relevant claim is therefore deliberately narrow: window-six coordinates can organize a local boundary class, and leave-one-code-out tests can ask whether that boundary class remains predictive across related code tables, but the table itself is still the empirical contact that supplies labels.

\paragraph{Finite support.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube object with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The module accounting is complete at this finite resolution: the $13$ row codons are assigned with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not being used as a vague motif: it is a finite list, a finite face count, and a finite module partition.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The coordinate construction also carries a numerical separation between the observed row geometry and randomized comparators.  The reported spectral values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the finite numerical identity is satisfied to the stated tolerance.  In the shape randomization experiment with $N=2000$, the sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only $1$ sample matched the exact observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and $25$ samples had Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$, the observed row size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  The corresponding reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrower claim that the tile shape and its class-preserving behavior are unusually constrained among the sampled alternatives.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within $M\setminus T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and after adjoining the boundary class it was again $0.9841269841269841$.  Family-resolved accounting reports $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  The companion leave-one-codon-out summary is consistent with this boundary reading: there are $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The result is a code-read result.  The finite row, its punctured-cube face vector, the spectral checks, the randomization counts, and the leave-one-code-out rates together say that the standard-code labels expose a stable local boundary tile in the six-coordinate codon display.  The curated standard-code table is essential in this statement: it provides the codon-to-label contact, while the coordinate construction organizes the local split.  Consequently, the claim is neither that the coordinate cube generates the genetic code by itself nor that the observed label pattern is a law of biology.  It is a finite empirical alignment between a coordinate row and a curated label table.

\paragraph{Boundary.}
No statement about translation realization follows from this row.  No folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from the codon-label readback.  An amino-acid label sequence is still only a label sequence at this level.  Crossing from the code layer to translation, folding, physical chemistry, or function would require a separate contact with observations at that layer.  Until such a contact is supplied, the present row remains a local codon-tile record with strong finite support and a deliberately limited scope.
