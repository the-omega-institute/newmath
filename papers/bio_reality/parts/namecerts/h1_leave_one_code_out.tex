\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback on the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the $64$ possible codon coordinates.  The data used here do not assert a biochemical mechanism.  They give a finite coordinate display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, a three-point label class, and a readback bridge to the curated standard-code label table.  The only external contact used in this section is the curated table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  Hence the tested layer is $\mathrm{code\_read}$: the finite experiment may compare codon coordinates with curated labels, but it does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any necessity law for DNA.

\paragraph{Distinguished row set.}
The distinguished row set is a thirteen-codon subset $R\subset Q_6$.  In the verified finite row, its cardinality and codon support are
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|&=13,\\
R&=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same set is verified as a punctured-cube tile with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)&=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a list of exceptional labels: it is a finite subcomplex with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the window-six coordinate geometry.  The codons in $R$ collect the local boundary rows on which the leave-one-code-out readback is evaluated.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
The first null comparison sampled $N=2000$ distinct surrogate shapes with seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, indicating that the finite sampling space was repeatedly revisited under the distinctness constraint.  Among the accepted samples, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null preserved amino-acid class.  With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed size of $M$ was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The reported values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite nulls support the claim that the observed local code tile is unusual relative to the sampled coordinate-preserving and amino-acid-class-preserving alternatives.  They do not promote the tile to a global biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The observed hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{\mathrm{above\ baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The stricter interior-tile statistic gave
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,
\end{aligned}
$$
while adding the local boundary restored
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{(M\setminus T)+\partial}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus, in this finite row, nearly all leave-one-code-out targets are recovered by the local window-six code-read geometry once the boundary codons are admitted.  The comparison with the baseline is large: the above-baseline and boundary-augmented rates are approximately $0.9841$, whereas the baseline rate is approximately $0.1982$.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite evidence supports the following narrow statement.  In the window-six codon display, the thirteen-codon punctured-cube row $R$ and its six-module classification expose a local boundary-tile structure that predicts or boundary-covers the observed leave-one-code-out label changes with hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ against a baseline of $0.19821739130434785$.  The support is strengthened by the exact-shape probability $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$, and the amino-acid-class-preserving lower-tail probability $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
This record stops at curated label readback. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any assertion beyond $\mathrm{code\_read}$ requires a separate reality contact designed for that higher layer.
