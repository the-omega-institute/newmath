\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact, not as a mechanism. The BEDC minimal form is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with a codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, three-point label classes, coordinate closure, a spectral readback, and a relation whose external label table is supplied by the curated standard genetic-code bridge. The biological object under test is therefore only the codon-to-label table. The observed contact says that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels; it does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or universal law.

\paragraph{The finite exceptional row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same thirteen codons and the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not a verbal pattern over the table: it is a concrete finite subconfiguration of $Q_6$ with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces. The module decomposition is also complete on this row. The six named modules cover all thirteen codons with no missing, extra, or unassigned entries:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},&
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},&
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},&
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The audit counts for this classification are $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, with empty lists for extra module codons, missing row codons, and unassigned codons.

These numbers locate the row in the internal coordinate-and-spectrum layer; they do not identify a biochemical force, a folding law, or an evolutionary mechanism.

\paragraph{Null comparisons for the tile shape.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. Only $1$ exact shape match occurred, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold gave $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$. These are finite-row rejection data for the local tile geometry, not evidence that the cube geometry is itself a global biological necessity.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
Against that baseline, the observed hit rate above baseline is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. Family-resolved readback gives five families. The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. The finite-row verdict is therefore strong at the code-read layer: when a table is left out, the local tile-and-boundary structure recovers almost all target labels relative to the baseline.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out compatibility.}
The codon-level companion probe reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, recovery rate $1.0$, and recovery-rate-above-threshold $1.0$. This supports the same local interpretation: the row behaves as a boundary-sensitive tile in the code table. It does not convert a label readback into a claim about ribosomal translation, folding, admissible chemistry, phenotype, or function.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported statement is narrow. Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure for standard-code label readback, with $R$ equal to the thirteen-codon punctured-cube row above, with complete module coverage, with spectral coordinates $\lambda_M$, $\lambda_R$, and $\mu$ as recorded, and with leave-one-code-out recovery far above the baseline. The forbidden promotion is equally part of the record. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, not folded protein structure, not physical admissibility, not biological function, not a mechanism, and not universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies translation, structure, physical, functional, mechanistic, or universality evidence.
