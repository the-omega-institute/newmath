\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns the finite six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$ with the standard-code label table used only as an external code-read contact.  A codon is read as a six-bit display point together with its curated amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC object in this row is not translation itself; it is the local tile, closure, spectrum, and relation data induced by the codon display and then read back against the curated standard-code table.  The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$\{AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}$.
The same finite row identifies this $R$ as a punctured-cube boundary object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the relation is not a vague biological analogy: it is a concrete thirteen-codon boundary row inside the six-bit codon display.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ alternative code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The BEDC local-tile recovery rate above that baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary row is $0.9841269841269841$.  Equivalently, the finite record says that nearly all held-out code-table targets are recovered by the local tile together with its boundary allowance, whereas the baseline row is below $0.2$.  The relevant comparison is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841 &> 0.9365079365079365 > 0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the chapter-specific content of the claim: the six-bit codon display does not merely name the standard table, but recovers held-out code-label positions at a high rate when the local tile and boundary are used as the finite readback device.

\paragraph{Family-level split.}
The family row separates the $23$ evaluated tables into $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. This gives the finite row a useful diagnostic shape: it localizes the weaker part of the recovery to one named family rather than hiding it inside the global $63$-target total.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read agreement and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Consequently the correct reading of the row is narrow: window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose code-label readback is unusually stable under leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out probes.  No claim follows that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity, that the local tile is a global law of genetic codes, or that amino-acid label sequences determine folded protein structure.  Any promotion beyond code\_read would require a separate higher-layer reality contact, such as a translation experiment, a structural contact, a physical-admissibility model, or a function assay.  In the present finite record, the result stops exactly at local codon-label readback.
