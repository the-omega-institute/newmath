\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery for a Local Codon Boundary}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record considered here is a finite code-layer packet on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is read as a six-bit display, and the only biological contact used by the packet is the curated standard-code label table: a codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label.  The packet therefore tests whether the local coordinate geometry around the standard code has predictive content for code-label readback after one code table is withheld.  It does not test translation as a cellular process, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal biological mechanism.

The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the finite run it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured-cube object this same set has reported face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The module partition accounts for all $13$ elements of $R$ and introduces no extra module codons.  The six finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}, &
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}, &
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}, &
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The audit row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing module elements, no unassigned codons, and no extra module codons.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape diagnostics.}
The finite spectral summary separates the boundary set from the ambient local mass by three scalars.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic radial check is numerically closed at the displayed precision: both the cubic-polynomial residual and the radial-polynomial residual are $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  The same record reports the spectral-radius value $0.6752479308830213$ in tolerance.  These numbers are used only as finite diagnostics of the codon-coordinate packet; they do not identify a physical force, an evolutionary necessity, or a biochemical law.

The shape null comparison used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-Jaccard count was $25$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  The corresponding reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local packet is not merely recording an arbitrary relabeling of amino-acid classes in these sampled controls.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ held-out targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The coordinate packet recovered held-out targets at rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841
\end{aligned}
$$
when measured above the baseline criterion.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary allowance was again $0.9841269841269841$.  These numbers are the main finite evidence for the claim: when one code table is left out, the local codon-coordinate packet nearly always places the withheld label event inside the predicted finite region or its boundary allowance.

The family-level breakdown keeps the same code-layer interpretation.  Five families were recorded.  Their above-baseline hit rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower alternative-yeast value is still above the baseline rate, while the other four families are perfect in this finite row.  The claim is therefore not that all organisms obey a global codon-geometric rule.  The claim is narrower: this curated collection of code-label tables is strongly organized by the six-coordinate local packet under this leave-one-code-out test.

\paragraph{One-codon stress row.}
A related leave-one-codon-out stress row gives a second internal check on the same boundary.  It reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The recovery-rate-above-threshold field is also $1.0$.  This row does not add a new biological layer; it only says that, at the codon-label level, every tested held-out codon is recovered by the finite closure or boundary mechanism in the recorded sample.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The packet is aligned with the curated standard-code table as a code-label contact.  It supports statements about codon labels, local codon tiles, the finite boundary set $R$, the reported module partition, the spectral summary, and the leave-one-code-out hit rates.  It does not by itself show that codons are translated in any particular cell, that an amino-acid label sequence folds, that a molecular structure is physically admissible, that a protein has a function, that a tRNA mechanism is present, or that a universal biological law has been found.  Any such step requires a separate observation at the relevant layer.  In particular, amino-acid label readback is not protein-structure evidence; CUN/UUR wording and tRNA-Leu notation remain code-layer or organism-matched questions until a distinct structural or mechanistic contact is supplied.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
For the claim identifier $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.code.out}$, the finite row is ready at the code-read layer.  The strongest numerical support is the contrast between the baseline rate $0.19821739130434785$ and the leave-one-code-out above-baseline hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ across $63$ targets from $23$ tables, together with the complete $13$-codon boundary partition and the null-model shape probabilities $0.0005$, $0.0125$, and $0.0035$.  The conclusion is a finite, code-layer recovery statement about local codon-coordinate organization, not a promotion to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.
