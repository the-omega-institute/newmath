\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact, not as a mechanism. The BEDC carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the finite distinctions of a six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The reality contact is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. That contact is enough to test codon-label readback and local label splits, but it is not a translation experiment, not a structural assay, not a physical admissibility test, and not a function measurement.

\paragraph{Recovered boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation the verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite set is verified as a punctured-cube row with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it carries a local cubical boundary shape inside $Q_6$. The experiment does not assert that this geometry governs biology globally. It says only that the curated code labels, when pulled back to the six-bit carrier, expose a finite local tile with a reproducible boundary row.

These finite values support the claim that the local tile is not a typical draw from the tested null ensembles. They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row recovery.}
The claim-specific leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The row-aware finite test gave
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ above\ baseline} &= 0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ } M\setminus T &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ } (M\setminus T)\cup \partial M &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The relevant reading is local and finite: when one code table is withheld, the boundary-tile structure recovers nearly all target label placements relative to the baseline rate. The statistic is a code-read statistic over curated labels. It is not a claim that the codon cube performs translation, generates proteins, selects folds, or determines biological function.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The finite result is a statement about codon-label readback from a curated code table through a window-six local tile. The admissible conclusion is that the standard-code labels expose a reproducible local boundary structure in $Q_6$, with $13$ row codons, punctured-cube face-vector $(13,16,4)$, strong leave-one-code-out recovery over $23$ tables and $63$ targets, and complete module accounting over the six displayed modules. The inadmissible conclusions are equally part of the record: no translation realization, no folded structure, no physical admissibility, no biological function, no mechanism, and no universality follows from this contact. Any such higher-layer claim requires a separate reality contact that directly observes the relevant layer.
