\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery inside a window-six codon tile}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The record studies the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon displayed as a six-bit coordinate and then read against the curated codon-to-label table.  The distinguished local row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit records $|R|=13$.  The same row is also recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the tile has thirteen observed vertices, sixteen observed one-dimensional adjacencies, and four observed square faces after the missing-corner cut is applied.  This is the chapter-specific finite object: a local coordinate-and-label phenomenon in the standard code, not a claim about translation dynamics.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape evidence.}
The associated spectral row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic or radial consistency check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically indistinguishable from zero at the stated tolerance scale.  A separate shape test with $N=2000$ samples, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts found one exact shape match and twenty-five samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The large duplicate-retry count, $55377$, is part of the finite sampling record and should be read as a warning that the sample space is constrained; it does not weaken the row identity, but it does make the null model an explicit part of the evidence rather than an invisible background assumption.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the row achieved
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ above\ baseline} &= 0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ }M\setminus T &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in\ }M\setminus T\mathrm{\ plus\ boundary} &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central finite-row observation for the claim.  When one code table is withheld, the local tile and its boundary recover almost all targets above the baseline rate.  The result is code-table readback evidence: it concerns the organization of codon labels under a six-coordinate display and the stability of that organization under the leave-one-code-out test.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It reported observed $|M|=20$, zero exact matches, and seven samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The corresponding finite probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact\ shape}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This comparison matters because it keeps the observation from collapsing into a trivial amino-acid-class artifact.  The row is still a code-layer pattern in a curated label table, but within that layer it survives a null model that preserves broad label classes.

\paragraph{Family and codon-module localization.}
The family-stratified leave-one-code-out check grouped the tables into five families.  The hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family, and $1.0$ for each of the bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  The codon-module classification assigns all thirteen row codons with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded module audit has $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and assigned count $13$.  The companion leave-one-codon-out row reported anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the evidence is not merely a table-level aggregate: every named module has either predictable or boundary status in the supplied finite classification.

\paragraph{Interpretation at the code-read layer.}
The scientific reading is narrow.  The window-six coordinate system exposes a local boundary tile in the standard-code label table, and the leave-one-code-out probe shows strong recovery relative to the baseline.  The singleton-corner and three-point-class distinctions are therefore useful for describing the row geometry of codon labels.  They do not, by themselves, describe the molecular process by which a ribosome reads a transcript, the chemical admissibility of an amino-acid assignment, the folded structure of a peptide, or the function of a protein product.

\paragraph{Local exclusion boundary.}
No statement in this chapter promotes the tile into translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The only contact supplied here is the curated standard-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read claims and local label-split claims; it cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or functional realization.  Any move beyond code labels would require a separate observation with the right biological resolution.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded-protein structure, and cross-organism wording about codon usage or tRNA notation is not a structure contact unless an organism-matched structural or biochemical record is separately supplied.
