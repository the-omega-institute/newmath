\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery at the Codon Label Boundary}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The claim \texttt{h1.leave.one.code.out} concerns a finite code-read experiment on the six-coordinate codon cube.  The carrier is the $64$-point codon display $Q_6$, with the usual standard-code labels used only as curated readback data.  The distinguished residual row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a cubical tile, this row has finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the relevant observation is not a free-form biological narrative but a small, checkable combinatorial pattern: thirteen codon vertices, sixteen local edges, and four square faces inside the window-six display.  The standard-code table supplies the labels attached to these vertices; it does not supply a translation process, a folding model, or a physical selection principle.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling evidence.}
The same finite row has a recorded spectral profile with
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the associated cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, within numerical tolerance of zero for the sampled calculation.  In the shape-significance run with seed $42$, $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows were evaluated after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sampled row matched the observed shape, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while $25$ rows had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite local-geometry reading of the standard-code label table.  They do not turn the geometry into a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$; and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus, when one code row is withheld, the finite window-six construction recovers almost all withheld targets relative to the baseline comparison:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit}_{\mathrm{baseline}}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{\mathrm{above\ baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{(M\setminus T)+\partial}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-level audit separates the result into five families.  The hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial plastid tables, $1.0$ for candidate-other tables, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate tables, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast tables.  The non-uniformity matters: the claim is a finite recovery statement for curated code-label rows, not a declaration that every organismal context has the same biological cause.

\paragraph{Codon-level module account.}
The leave-one-codon-out audit records $0$ anchor codons, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure codons, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for all thirteen residual codons with no extras and no omissions.  The assigned modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness record is $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing residual codons, and no unassigned codons.  This is the strongest local part of the record: every residual codon is placed in a named finite module, and each module has either predictable or boundary support.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope limit.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is adequate for code-layer readback and for testing local label splits on the codon cube.  It is not evidence for translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any global biological necessity.  Amino-acid label sequences remain labels until another experiment supplies a structure or function contact.  Therefore the ready verdict for \texttt{h1.leave.one.code.out} is narrow: window-six coordinates expose a statistically unusual local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code readback, and the leave-one-code-out recovery is high across the finite table audit; no higher biological layer follows from these rows alone.
