\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The claim tested here is that the six-coordinate codon display carries a local boundary pattern strong enough to recover most held-out code-table label changes, while still depending on the curated standard-code table for the actual amino-acid and stop labels. The biological object is therefore not translation as a cellular process, not protein folding, and not biological function. It is only the code-layer table that assigns codon words to amino-acid or stop labels. Within that narrow object, the leave-one-code-out row asks whether the local codon tile surrounding the standard code predicts the codons that move when other code tables are compared against it.

\paragraph{Carrier and observed puncture.}
The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-bit display of the $64$ codon words. The distinguished set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the following finite row:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row reports that this set forms the observed punctured-cube shape with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the empirical object is not a smooth global geometry on all codons. It is a finite local tile in which $13$ codons, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces are registered by the window-six coordinate display.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated spectrum records $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The reported radial check is numerically closed at the displayed precision:
$$
\begin{aligned}
P_{\mathrm{rad}}(\mu)=-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same value is recorded as the cubic residual, and the spectral-radius value in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$. These numbers are used only as finite diagnostics of the codon-tile row. They do not make the codon table into a physical dynamics, and they do not by themselves identify a translation mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape rarity.}
Under the recorded shape null, $N=2000$ samples were evaluated with seed $42$. The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
It also found $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null again used $N=2000$ and seed $42$. In that row the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$. The resulting exact-shape rate is $0.0$, while the lower-tail rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite data therefore treat the tile as unusual relative to the sampled alternatives, including alternatives that preserve amino-acid class. This is evidence for a code-layer local pattern, not evidence for folded structure or organism-level function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The direct leave-one-code-out evaluation considered $23$ tables and $63$ held-out targets. The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. Restricting attention to $M\setminus T$ gives a hit rate of $0.9365079365079365$, and adding the boundary term gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{M\setminus T+\partial}=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same value is recorded as the hit rate above baseline. The finite reading is therefore sharp: almost all held-out code-table target codons land in the local module-plus-boundary region, while the baseline rate is near one fifth. This is the row supporting the claim identifier $\mathrm{h1.leave.one.code.out}$.

\paragraph{Family split.}
The family-level row partitions the tested code tables into $5$ families. The above-baseline hit rates are $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative yeast family. The recovery is therefore not carried by a single family alone, although the alternative yeast family is the visibly weaker finite cell in this table.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module row assigns all $13$ codons in $R$ without extra or missing codons. It records $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status. The separate leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. This complements the code-table row by showing that single-codon withholding is also absorbed by the same local classification, but it still remains a codon-label statement.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table: a finite assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That table supports codon-label readback and local split checks. It does not observe ribosomal translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological activity, evolutionary necessity, or universal mechanism. Consequently the correct conclusion is bounded: the window-six codon coordinate exposes a strong local boundary-tile pattern for standard-code label readback and for leave-one-code-out recovery across the recorded comparison tables. No claim about translation, folded protein structure, physical possibility, biochemical function, mechanism, or universality follows without a separate contact that directly measures that higher layer.
