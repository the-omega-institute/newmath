\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery in a Local Codon Tile}\label{sec:biorality-h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim being tested.}
The packet \texttt{h1.leave.one.code.out} asks whether the six-coordinate codon display can retain predictive information when a code table is partly withheld. The carrier is the finite codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a six-bit codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The biological contact used here is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. Thus the observable layer is code readback. The experiment may compare codon labels and local boundary behavior, but it does not observe translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Finite exceptional row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has size $13$. The verified codon list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This finite shape is not treated as a law of biology. It is a local combinatorial object whose label contacts are supplied by the curated code table. The row decomposes into six named modules in the recovery audit: $\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$ for \texttt{agr}, $\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$ for \texttt{ar\_lys}, $\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$ for \texttt{cun}, $\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$ for \texttt{ile\_met}, $\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$ for \texttt{ssn}, and $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$ for \texttt{stop\_trp}. The module audit reports $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The finite-row spectrum reports

\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is the recorded value both for the cubic-polynomial check and the radial-polynomial check. The spectral-radius value in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$. These numbers give a finite numerical profile for the row-and-neighborhood computation; they do not turn the codon geometry into a mechanism of translation or a physical model of amino-acid behavior.

\paragraph{Null-model separation.}
Against a sampled shape null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the audit used $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$. The corresponding probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed $M$ size $20$, exact match count $0$, and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size. Its recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The data therefore separate the observed local label geometry from two finite null procedures. The conclusion remains a code-layer conclusion: the tests compare table labels and local codon neighborhoods, not ribosomal realization, molecular dynamics, folding, or phenotype.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The primary leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary class is $0.9841269841269841$. In compact form,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{tables}&=23,\\
\mathrm{targets}&=63,\\
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{>\mathrm{baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{M\setminus T+\partial}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central finite-row evidence for the claim. Removing a code table does not erase most of the local codon-label signal recoverable from the six-coordinate tile and its boundary bookkeeping. The result is still conditional on the curated label table as the contact that supplies biological names for codons.

\paragraph{Family and codon deletion audits.}
The family-stratified run reports $5$ families. The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate codes. The companion leave-one-codon-out run reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. Thus every named row module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite classification, while the pointwise codon deletion audit records perfect recovery on its own tested scope.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and boundary.}
The finite data support the following bounded statement. In the standard-code label table, the row $R$ of $13$ codons forms a punctured-cube local tile with a verified module decomposition; its spectral profile and null comparisons are numerically non-generic under the recorded finite procedures; and leave-one-code-out recovery remains far above the baseline rate across $23$ evaluated tables and $63$ targets. What follows is only a code-read claim about codon labels in a six-coordinate display. No claim is licensed here about actual translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global law of DNA. Any such stronger assertion would require a separate observation channel aimed at that stronger biological layer.
