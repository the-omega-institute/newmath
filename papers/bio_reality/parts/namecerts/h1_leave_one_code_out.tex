\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed code rows.}
The carrier is the finite codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-coordinate display of the $64$ codons.  The reality contact used here is only the curated standard-code table: it supplies codon-to-label rows, with each row labelled by an amino-acid or stop symbol.  The distinguished residual set is the thirteen-codon row set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit records $|R|=13$.  As a cubical subcomplex inside $Q_6$, the same set has face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed object is not merely a list of labels: it is a local cubical tile with vertices, edges, and square faces.  This geometric statement is still code-layer only.  It does not assert that the codon cube is a law of translation, a folding rule, a physical admissibility criterion, a function model, or a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$.  Producing the distinct samples required $57377$ attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those samples there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid class information used $N=2000$ samples, observed an $M$-row size of $20$, and found no exact shape match:
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local shape is not behaving like a common finite accident under these sampled controls, while the interpretation remains limited to codon-label readback and local tile comparison.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Inside $M\setminus T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary rows it was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The result is therefore a finite recovery statement: when one code table is withheld, the local window-six coordinates and the boundary rows almost always recover the withheld code-layer target relative to the recorded baseline.  It is not a claim that the geometry performs translation or that it predicts protein structure.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out module accounting.}
The companion codon-level audit records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary rows, $8$ predictable rows, and $8$ rows with high closure coverage.  Its recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value recorded for recovery above threshold.  The thirteen residual codons are completely assigned to six modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\

$$
The module audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module contains a predictable or boundary row, so the local explanation covers all thirteen residual positions at the code-label level.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The supported statement is narrow.  The window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary tile in the standard-code label table, and the leave-one-code-out recovery numbers show strong code-layer predictive structure across the recorded table families.  The curated table, however, observes only codon-to-label assignment.  It supplies no measurement of translation dynamics, no folded conformation, no physical admissibility test, no biological function assay, and no proof of a universal mechanism.  Any move from this codon-label tile to translation, folding, physical chemistry, organismal function, or universality would require an additional contact whose rows actually observe that higher layer.
