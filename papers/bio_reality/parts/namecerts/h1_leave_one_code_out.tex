\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery in the window-six codon cube}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table read at the code-label layer.  The finite carrier is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$, viewed as a six-bit codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The internal data used here are coordinates, closure, spectrum, and relation data; the biological contact is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The finite row therefore tests whether a local coordinate geometry helps recover code-label changes under leave-one-code-out evaluation.  It does not test ribosomal translation, molecular folding, thermodynamic feasibility, biological activity, causal mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Distinguished relation row.}
The relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
This is the local combinatorial footprint used by the recovery probe.  The row is not being promoted to a law of biology; it is a finite coordinate pattern whose interpretation remains tied to the observed code table.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported cubic relation is numerically closed to tolerance, with radial polynomial value
$$\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}$$
For the shape statistic, $2000$ sampled tables were evaluated with seed $42$.  The exact observed shape appeared once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling log records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact shape count was $0$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, giving lower-tail probability $0.0035$ and exact-shape probability $0.0$ in the sampled run.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Recovery inside $M\setminus T$ reached
$$\begin{aligned}
0.9365079365079365,
\end{aligned}$$
while recovery inside $M\setminus T$ plus the local boundary row reached
$$\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}$$
The same value, $0.9841269841269841$, is reported as the hit rate above baseline.  In finite-row terms, the coordinate-plus-boundary test almost always finds the held-out code-label target among the permitted local candidates, while a baseline selector succeeds at about one fifth of the rate.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The family split contains $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear/ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  Thus the aggregate rate is not carried by a single family class alone.  The alternative-yeast row is the weakest listed family, but it remains well above the baseline row supplied for the full leave-one-code-out table.

\paragraph{Codon-level module accounting.}
The codon-level module classification assigns all $13$ codons in $R$ and leaves no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The paired leave-one-codon-out row reports boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, anchor count $0$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The boundary/predictable split is therefore exhaustive for the listed modules at the codon row level: every module has a predictable or boundary explanation in the supplied finite classification.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, which supplies codon-to-label assignments.  That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test whether the local tile is realized as translation machinery, whether an amino-acid string folds, whether a molecule is physically admissible, whether it has biological function, or whether the coordinate pattern is a universal mechanism.  Amino-acid label readback therefore remains a code-layer observation.  Any statement about folding, physical admissibility, function, or mechanism would require a separate contact carrying that information.

\paragraph{Verdict for this row.}
The finite data support the claim that window-six codon coordinates expose a strong local boundary signal for leave-one-code-out code-label recovery.  The support is numerical and finite: $23$ tables, $63$ targets, a baseline rate $0.19821739130434785$, a boundary-augmented hit rate $0.9841269841269841$, a $13$-codon punctured-cube relation row, complete module assignment of those $13$ codons, and shape-null probabilities $0.0005$, $0.0125$, and $0.0035$ under the stated sampled nulls.  The result is not a claim that biology must obey the geometry; it is a bounded empirical readback claim saying that this coordinate system gives a compact local explanation of code-label variation within the supplied finite tables.
