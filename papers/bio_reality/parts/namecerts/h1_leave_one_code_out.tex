\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery at the codon-label boundary}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record studies the standard-code codon table only at the label-readback layer. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, used as a finite display for the $64$ codons. The relevant distinctions are a six-bit codon coordinate, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The biological contact is the curated standard-code table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label, and that assignment is treated as observed label data rather than as a mechanism of translation. The chapter therefore asks whether the window-six coordinate view exposes a repeatable local boundary pattern when one alternative code table is omitted from the comparison, while keeping the readback anchored to the finite codon-label table.

$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube boundary object with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a list of codons: it has $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the reported tile. The module partition also closes on these same $13$ codons. The six reported modules are
$$

The module check reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. Every module has either predictable or boundary status in the leave-one-codon classification, so the module layer is complete for the recorded set $R$.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the reported random comparison for the $M$-shape, $N=2000$ trials were evaluated with seed $42$. The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid class structure used $N=2000$ trials, again with seed $42$. It records observed $M$-size $20$, exact match count $0$, and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed size. The exact-shape probability is therefore reported as $0.0$, and the lower-tail probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support the claim that the local tile shape is unusually concentrated under the stated finite null procedures. They do not, by themselves, identify a biochemical cause.

\end{aligned}
$$
The reported hit rate above baseline is
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
while the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is
$$
\begin{aligned}

After including the boundary with $M\setminus T$, the hit rate is again
$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
Thus the finite row says that the boundary-augmented coordinate test recovers nearly all withheld code-label targets relative to the stated baseline. The result is strong at the level of codon-label readback: it says that the six-coordinate tile and its boundary split are predictive for the recorded table comparison. The result is not promoted beyond that layer.

\paragraph{Scientific boundary.}
The supported statement is deliberately narrow. A six-bit codon tile can organize the curated standard-code labels and can expose a high leave-one-code-out recovery rate on the reported finite rows. The contact used here is still only a codon-to-label table. It does not observe ribosomal translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or a universal law across life. Amino-acid label readback is also not a folded protein structure. Any claim at those higher layers would require a separate contact that directly measures the relevant phenomenon. Without such a contact, the chapter records a finite code-layer pattern: a punctured-cube boundary set of $13$ codons, a complete module assignment, significant finite-shape comparisons, and a high boundary-assisted recovery rate for withheld code-label targets.
