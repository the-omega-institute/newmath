\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability in the Window-Six Codon Tile}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate record.}
The object tested here is the standard-code codon label table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The carrier is the $Q_6$ codon cube: each codon is treated as a six-bit address, and the experiment asks whether the local tile cut found in this coordinate system continues to locate held-out code labels.  The readback is deliberately narrow.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments for amino-acid and stop labels, and the coordinate construction supplies local faces, singleton label corners, three-point label classes, closure tests, spectra, and adjacency relations.  The record does not treat the coordinate pattern as a biological law; it tests whether a finite, curated label table has a repeatable local boundary signature.

The distinguished residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the finite run it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same set is reported as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it also has a small face-count signature in the codon cube.  This is the local tile whose label boundary is used by the leave-one-code-out probe.

Hence the recorded exact-shape value is $p=0.0$ at the finite sample resolution, while the lower-tail value is $0.0035$.  These two checks do not by themselves identify a mechanism; they say that the observed local tile is atypical under the reported randomizations.

\end{aligned}

The family-level split is also finite.  There are $5$ reported families. \paragraph{Biological scope.}
The contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, and its resolution is codon label readback.  It supports statements about codon-to-label assignments and about local splits in the six-coordinate codon display.  It does not observe translation dynamics, ribosomal realization, folded protein geometry, chemical admissibility, biological function, a general mechanism, or universality across life.  Amino-acid label strings are still code-layer outputs in this chapter, not protein structures.  Any claim crossing from this local label geometry to translation, folding, physical feasibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require an additional empirical contact of the appropriate type.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
For the finite row with experiment identifier $429e6e2c4eb4$, the leave-one-code-out probe is positive at the code-read layer: $23$ tables and $63$ targets give a hit rate above baseline of $0.9841269841269841$ against baseline $0.19821739130434785$, with complete residual-module accounting for the $13$-codon punctured-cube set.  The accompanying spectral, shape, amino-acid-class-preserving null, codon-level, and family-level records make the result specific enough to audit as a local codon-tile phenomenon.  The conclusion remains intentionally bounded: the row supports robust label recovery from the local window-six tile, and nothing stronger about biological realization follows from this contact alone.
