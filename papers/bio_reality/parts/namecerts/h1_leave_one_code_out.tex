\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery in the window-six codon cube}\label{subsec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
This record studies the standard-code codon table only at the label-readout level.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, presented as the sixty-four codons over the alphabet $\{A,C,G,U\}$ with a six-bit coordinate display.  The coordinate object used by the row is not a biochemical mechanism; it is a finite combinatorial display on which singleton label corners, three-point label classes, local tile faces, closures, spectra, and adjacency relations can be computed.  The reality contact is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact permits codon-label readback and local split tests, but it does not by itself observe translation dynamics, molecular folding, physical feasibility, organismal function, or a universal biological principle.

\paragraph{Boundary set and punctured-cube shape.}
The finite boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The listed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
   &UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check returns the same thirteen codons and the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely recording that thirteen labels are exceptional under the chosen coordinate display; it also records a concrete local shape with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four square faces.  The module classification partitions the same thirteen codons without leftovers: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, the assigned count is $13$, the extra-module list is empty, the missing-from-modules list is empty, and the unassigned-codon list is empty.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\}, & \mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}, & \mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\}, & \mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary explanation in the finite row.  This is a local classification of codon labels inside $Q_6$, not a claim that those labels determine a biological process.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape evidence.}
The same finite packet reports a spectral comparison between the ambient shape and the boundary object.  The numerical values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic-polynomial residual, also recorded as the radial-polynomial value, is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the spectral row is numerically closed to tolerance for this finite computation.  The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The same run reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the sampling row is finite but visibly constrained by repeated draws in the available null population.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null comparison.}
A second null row preserves amino-acid classes.  In $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, the exact-shape count is $0$, so the finite estimate for the class-preserving exact-shape probability is $0.0$ in this run.  The observed $M$ size is $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These numbers support the statement that the observed local shape is unusual relative to the tested finite nulls.  They do not show that the coordinate display is the cause of the genetic code, nor that the same pattern must hold under unrelated biological contacts.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The claim-specific row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The tested recovery rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$; the rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$; and the rate inside $M-T$ after adjoining the boundary component is again $0.9841269841269841$.  In compact form, the observed finite recovery profile is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline} &= 0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{above\ baseline} &= 0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{in}\ M-T &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{in}\ M-T+\mathrm{boundary} &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-level row has five families.  The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.  The row therefore supports a strong code-label recovery statement for the tested finite collection, with one family below perfect recovery but still above the baseline reported by the same packet.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out boundary check.}
The companion codon-level row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The threshold comparison is also $1.0$.  This says that, in the finite leave-one-codon-out test, the recoverable cases are explained through predictable or boundary positions rather than through separately observed biochemical anchoring.  The absence of anchors is important: the row is strongest as a codon-label and local-boundary record, not as a mechanistic account of how translation is realized.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite evidence is ready for the following restricted reading.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a nontrivial local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table: $R$ has thirteen explicitly listed codons, the punctured-cube vector is $(13,16,4)$, the spectral row closes numerically to a residual of about $6.23\cdot 10^{-12}$ in magnitude, the exact-shape finite null estimate is $0.0005$, the amino-acid-class-preserving exact-shape count is $0$ in $2000$ samples, and the leave-one-code-out recovery above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$ across $63$ targets from $23$ tables.  The scientific claim stops there.  No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this chapter unless a separate contact supplies observations at that higher layer.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation; it is not a protein-structure observation.
