\subsection{Leave-one-code-out stability of the local codon tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the finite display of the standard-code codon table.  The contact used here is only the curated codon-to-label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop, and it supplies no independent realization of translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  The claim tested in this row is therefore a code-read claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern whose label behavior can be checked against curated code labels.  The scope is deliberately narrow.  A local tile is not a global biological law, and an amino-acid label sequence is not a folded protein structure.

\paragraph{Distinguished row set.}
The finite row distinguished by the coordinate-and-closure construction has cardinality $13$.  The observed set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row set is reported as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite witness is not merely a count: it records a thirteen-codon boundary object with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four local two-dimensional faces inside the codon cube.  The row-level module classification is complete: every one of the $13$ codons is assigned, with no missing row and no extra codon.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These module rows give the finite substrate for the leave-one-code-out test: the test asks whether alternative code tables still hit the local tile, its closure, or its boundary when one code table is excluded from the reference collection.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral controls.}
The local tile is separated from two finite null checks.  In the coordinate-shape sample with $N=2000$ distinct evaluated samples, the exact-shape count is $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap condition $J\geq 0.9$ appears $25$ times in the same $2000$ samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries at seed $42$, so the stated probabilities are attached to the finite evaluated sample rather than to an unobserved continuum.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$, the exact-shape count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$, and the observed $M$-row size is $20$.  The corresponding finite probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out evidence.}
The leave-one-code-out table test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ target rows.  The baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
Against that baseline, the hit rate above baseline is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus the code-read phenomenon is not only a singleton coincidence in the standard table: across the finite leave-one-code-out panel, almost all targets are recovered by the local tile, its closure complement, or its boundary extension.

\paragraph{Family-level distribution.}
The same leave-one-code-out behavior is distributed across five code-table families.  The family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The only family below perfect recovery in this finite panel is alternative yeast, and it still remains far above the baseline rate.  This supports a local code-read stability statement: the tile is robust under leave-one-code-out withholding across the observed family partition.  It does not support a stronger statement that all genetic codes, all organisms, or all translation systems must obey this geometry.

\paragraph{Codon-level boundary check.}
A companion leave-one-codon-out check reports $8$ predictable rows, $5$ boundary rows, $0$ anchor rows, and high closure coverage on $8$ rows.  Its recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value reported for recovery above threshold.  This is consistent with the module classification above: each module has a predictable or boundary interpretation, and every codon of $R$ is accounted for by the six displayed modules.  The codon-level result is used here only as a finite local sanity check for the leave-one-code-out row; it is not a separate proof of molecular realization.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The row verdict is ready at the code-read layer.  The finite facts jointly say that the distinguished thirteen-codon boundary tile is shape-rare under the coordinate null, rare under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, spectrally coherent within the stated tolerance, module-complete, and highly stable under the $23$-table, $63$-target leave-one-code-out panel.  The numerical core of the claim is the contrast between the baseline hit rate $0.19821739130434785$ and the observed leave-one-code-out hit rate $0.9841269841269841$, with the $M\setminus T$ rate $0.9365079365079365$ and the boundary-augmented rate $0.9841269841269841$ locating the effect inside the local closure-and-boundary structure.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
No translation realization follows from these rows.  No folded structure follows.  No physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity law follows.  The curated standard-code table supplies labels, not ribosomal dynamics, protein conformation, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal fitness, or universal biological causation.  Any claim crossing from code-read geometry to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate contact that directly observes that layer.
