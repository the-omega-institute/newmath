\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Readback}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite coordinate display for the standard-code label table. The observed exceptional row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit records $|R|=13$. As a punctured cube row, the same set has face-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the local object is not being reported as an arbitrary list of codons but as a small cubical boundary pattern inside $Q_6$. This is a code-read object: the external contact is the curated standard genetic-code table, whose role is to assign each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. It is not a contact with translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law of molecular necessity.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model scale.}
The row comes with a finite spectral signature. The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with radial cubic residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$. This residual is numerical evidence that the reported spectrum is internally consistent to the recorded tolerance; it is not a biological mechanism. Against $N=2000$ sampled null rows, the exact-shape count is $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while the high-overlap criterion $J\geq 0.9$ has $25$ matches and $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sample required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite-row calculation is also reporting the combinatorial scarcity of the sampled shape. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, the observed $M$-size is $20$, exact shape matches are $0$ among $2000$ samples, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, giving lower-tail $p=0.0035$. Thus the coordinate row survives a label-class-preserving stress test at the code-read layer, while still remaining a finite label-table observation.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out test.}
The leave-one-code-out audit evaluates $23$ genetic-code tables with $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. The recorded hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$; and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary row is $0.9841269841269841$. In finite-row terms, the boundary row recovers almost all held-out table targets once the local tile and its boundary are allowed, while the stricter interior-only region still accounts for most targets. The claim is therefore a local code-read prediction claim: leave-one-code-out label movement is largely captured by a finite codon-window geometry attached to the curated label table.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The same leave-one-code-out result is not concentrated in a single family. Five families are recorded. Their hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &: 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &: 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &: 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &: 1.0,\\

$$
This stratification supports the reading that the tile is detecting a finite code-label regularity rather than merely fitting one table family. The alternative-yeast family is the only listed family below perfect recovery, and it still remains far above the baseline rate. The statement is deliberately local: family-level recovery does not convert the tile into an explanation of organismal translation systems.

\paragraph{Module account.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned to six finite modules. The module audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary witness. The companion leave-one-codon-out audit records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. This means that the row is organized by boundary and closure behavior rather than by a single fixed anchor codon.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure for standard-code label readback. The curated code table supplies the external labels, and the finite cube supplies the coordinate distinction. Nothing in this chapter claims that DNA obeys the tile as a global biological law. Nothing here implies translation realization, folded-protein structure, physical admissibility, function realization, a biochemical mechanism, or universality. Any such promotion would require a separate higher-layer contact. The present evidence supports exactly the code-read layer: a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row with spectrum, null-model scarcity, complete module classification, and high leave-one-code-out recovery against curated codon-label tables.
