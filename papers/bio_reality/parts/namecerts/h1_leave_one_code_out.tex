\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Readback for the Standard Code}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate record.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table as a code-layer contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The finite carrier is not a mechanistic model of translation.  It is a coordinate display whose distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The readback contact is the curated standard-code label table: each codon is observed only as carrying an amino-acid or stop label.  The point of the record is therefore narrow: window-six codon coordinates may expose a local boundary-tile pattern, while the curated labels remain external reality contacts.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is reported as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus this record is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it is a finite combinatorial tile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the supplied coordinate representation.

These numbers support a local finite-row anomaly at the code-read layer.  They do not, by themselves, identify a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
whereas the hit rate above baseline is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The more restrictive row $M-T$ gives hit rate $0.9365079365079365$, and the row $M-T$ plus boundary returns $0.9841269841269841$.  The finite content of this claim is therefore a strong recovery of code-label targets from the local tile plus boundary data, measured against the baseline frequency over the evaluated tables.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite-row verdict is ready at the code-read layer.  The curated standard-code table supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  It does not support promotion to translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A separate higher-layer contact would be required before any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality could be made.  Within that boundary, the leave-one-code-out row is a strong finite recovery result: $23$ tables, $63$ targets, baseline hit rate $0.19821739130434785$, and boundary-augmented hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ over the supplied standard-code coordinate record.
