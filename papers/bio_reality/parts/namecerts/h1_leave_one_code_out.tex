\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact over the window-six codon carrier $Q_6$. The carrier is not used as a mechanism for translation. It is used only as a six-bit codon display on which local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes can be compared against a curated codon-to-label table. The reality contact is therefore narrow: a codon has a curated amino-acid or stop label, and the experiment asks whether finite coordinate structure helps recover held-out code labels. It does not test protein realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that biological DNA must obey this geometry.

\paragraph{The distinguished finite row.}
The codon subset $R$ has cardinality $13$. The row is explicitly
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the geometric input is a concrete finite row, not a continuous model and not a biochemical dynamics. The module classification partitions the $13$ row codons without remainder: $\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$. The module audit gives $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from modules, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model row checks.}
The finite spectrum attached to the row records
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}$$
The reported cubic, or radial, polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the displayed spectral relation is numerically closed at the stated tolerance scale. The finite shape comparison used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ duplicate retries, $1$ exact shape match, and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$. The corresponding probabilities are $p\_{exact}=0.0005$ and $p\_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, and the reported probabilities are $p=0.0$ for exact shape and $p=0.0035$ for the lower tail. These figures support treating the row as a finite anomaly for code-label readback, while still leaving mechanism and biological realization unclaimed.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out test.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ alternative code tables and $63$ total held-out targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. The coordinate test reports a hit rate above baseline of $0.9841269841269841$, a hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ of $0.9365079365079365$, and a hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary of $0.9841269841269841$. Equivalently, the boundary-augmented local tile almost always recovers the held-out code-label target in this finite table family:
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T\ \mathrm{plus\ boundary})&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}$$
The family-level audit has $5$ families. The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. This is the chapter-specific positive observation: the finite window-six tile has high leave-one-code-out readback power on the supplied code-table family.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out boundary check.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out row records $anchor\_count=0$, $boundary\_count=5$, $closure\_coverage\_high=8$, $predictable\_count=8$, and $recovery\_rate=1.0$. The thresholded recovery flag is also $1.0$. This means that, within the finite table data, codon-level recovery is explained by predictable or boundary behavior rather than by a separate anchor count. The module audit is consistent with this reading: every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary status, and the six modules exhaust the $13$ codons in $R$.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported claim is only a code-layer readback claim. A local codon-tile pattern may expose a boundary structure for curated code labels, and the finite leave-one-code-out row gives a high hit rate relative to the baseline. No stronger biological conclusion follows. In particular, no translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, biological mechanism, global law, or universality claim follows from these rows. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. Crossing that boundary would require a separate reality contact with its own finite observations, its own null model, and its own mismatch audit.
