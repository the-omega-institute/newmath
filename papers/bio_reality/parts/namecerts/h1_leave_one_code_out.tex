\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and scope.}
This record studies the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of $64$ codon positions with curated amino-acid or stop labels supplied by an external standard-code table.  The BEDC distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data; the readback is the standard-code label table.  The contact therefore tests codon-label readback and local split behavior.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{The punctured tile row.}
The finite relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a local combinatorial fact about the displayed codon cube.  It is not a global biological law.  In particular, the presence of the punctured tile does not imply that DNA or translation is governed by this geometry as a physical necessity.

There were $25$ matches at Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null with seed $42$ reports $N=2000$, observed $M$-size $20$, exact match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its exact-shape value is $0.0$, while the lower-tail value is

These finite null values support that the observed local shape is uncommon under the stated row generators.  They do not identify a mechanism and do not promote the local tile to a law of biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The reported hit rate above baseline is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841,
\end{aligned}
$$
with hit rate in $M\setminus T$ equal to $0.9365079365079365$ and hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary equal to $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus the local row is predictive for code-label readback under this finite leave-one-code-out test.  The prediction is a label-table prediction: it says that the held-out code labels often land inside the displayed local module or its boundary.  It does not say that the held-out organisms translate by this geometry, nor that the codon labels determine protein structure or function.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The verdict of this row is deliberately narrow.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label contacts, and the BEDC window-six construction supplies finite coordinate, closure, spectral, and relation probes.  Together they support a local code-read statement: the row $R$ and its modules expose a strong leave-one-code-out label recovery pattern.  They do not supply a translation experiment, a folding contact, a physical-admissibility contact, a biological-function contact, a mechanism, or a universality proof.  Any such higher-layer assertion would require a separate reality contact whose observations live at that higher layer.
