\paragraph{Finite carrier.}

It is used as a finite coordinate display in which codons occupy vertices, local tile faces expose boundary structure, and curated amino-acid or stop labels provide the external readback contact.  In the verified finite row, the distinguished remainder set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same thirteen codons and records the local face-vector signature
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a count: it fixes a concrete thirteen-vertex codon subobject with sixteen local edges and four local two-faces inside the window-six display.
