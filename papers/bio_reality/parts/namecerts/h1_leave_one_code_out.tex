\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback on the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and external label contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon-label table.  The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class.  The biological contact used here is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and the experiment reads only this code layer.  Thus the contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks; it does not by itself supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary row set.}
The distinguished boundary row set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the supplied finite run it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row set is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite combinatorial object being tested.  It is not a claim that the genetic code is forced by cube geometry; it is a claim that this finite coordinate display exposes a reproducible local boundary tile when compared against curated labels.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The spectral finite-row data give the measured values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic polynomial check.  This residual is numerically negligible at the displayed precision, so the spectrum is used here as an internal coordinate checksum for the finite tile rather than as an independent biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance against shuffled code-label rows.}
The finite null run for the $M$-shape used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  It recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape was matched once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was met $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reported observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its finite-row probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the displayed local tile is not a routine byproduct of the sampled label-preserving nulls.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ target rows.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The finite coordinate recovery rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T+\partial)&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus, in this finite-row experiment, almost every held-out code-table target is recovered by the local $M\setminus T$ coordinate together with its boundary extension.  The empirical point is row-local and label-local: it says that the standard-code labels give a strong readback pattern on the codon tile, not that the tile implements translation or protein function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out module check.}
The leave-one-codon-out run reported anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Its module classification assigns all $13$ boundary codons and leaves no extras, omissions, or unassigned rows.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness certificate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|&=13,\\
\mathrm{assigned\ count}&=13,\\
\mathrm{extra\ module\ codons}&=\varnothing,\\
\mathrm{missing\ from\ modules}&=\varnothing,\\
\mathrm{unassigned\ codons}&=\varnothing.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module therefore has a predictable or boundary explanation inside the supplied finite coordinate data.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The supported claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure for standard-code label readback.  The row set $R$, the punctured-cube $f$-vector, the spectral checksum, the null-model probabilities, the leave-one-code-out hit rates, and the complete module classification all point to the same finite local object.  The unsupported claims are equally important.  No translation process follows from these data.  No folded protein structure follows from amino-acid label readback.  No physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from the codon tile.  Any promotion beyond code-layer readback requires a separate external contact at the higher layer being claimed.
