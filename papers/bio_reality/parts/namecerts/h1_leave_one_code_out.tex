\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery at the Codon Label Boundary}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table read as a finite labelled display on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  A codon is treated here as a six-bit window coordinate carrying a curated amino-acid or stop label.  The test does not treat the cube as a biochemical engine.  It asks a narrower question: when alternate code tables are held out one at a time, does the local boundary geometry obtained from the standard-code label table recover the known reassignment targets above a naive background rate?

The boundary set $R$ has exactly $13$ codons.  In the verified row it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row verifies that this set has the punctured-cube face profile
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the readout is not a free list of exceptional codons; it is a finite local tile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces inside the codon-coordinate display.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The companion spectral row gives the numerical parameters
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is within the stated numerical tolerance for the cubic relation used by the finite probe.  This is a coordinate-level check on the local shape, not an assertion that biology optimizes such a spectrum.

The shape-significance row compares the observed pattern with $N=2000$ sampled alternatives using seed $42$.  Among $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ were duplicate retries.  The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap shape matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null row preserves amino-acid class information.  In that null, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$ over $2000$ trials, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The corresponding recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The supplied finite rows therefore support a claim of unusually structured local codon-label geometry under the stated probes.  They do not by themselves supply a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The claim row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ reassignment targets.  The baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
Against this baseline, the local geometric recovery rate above baseline is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary contribution is again $0.9841269841269841$.  In finite-row language, almost all held-out code-table targets land where the standard-code local tile says they should land, once the boundary part is allowed to participate.

The family-resolved row divides the $23$ tables into $5$ families.  The recorded above-baseline family rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This family split is important because it prevents the headline rate from being read as a single undifferentiated aggregate.  The weakest listed family still remains far above the baseline rate, while four families reach the recorded value $1.0$.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module-classification row assigns all $13$ boundary codons with no extras and no omissions.  Its summary is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|&=13,\\
\mathrm{assigned\ count}&=13,\\
\mathrm{missing\ from\ modules}&=0,\\
\mathrm{unassigned\ codons}&=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The modules are:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\ lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\ met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\ trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either predictable membership or boundary membership in the supplied classification row.  This is the finite combinatorial reason the leave-one-code-out result has local explanatory force: the reassignment targets are not merely scattered among $64$ codons, but concentrate in a completely assigned $13$-codon boundary packet.

\paragraph{Codon-level recovery check.}
The leave-one-codon-out companion row supplies a stricter local deletion test.  It records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Hence, under this finite deletion experiment, every tested codon is recovered either through predictable structure or through boundary placement.  The absence of anchors in this row is also informative: the result is not being carried by privileged fixed points, but by the local closure-and-boundary organization of the codon display.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The proper conclusion is a code-layer conclusion.  The curated standard-code table is a contact point for codon-to-label readback, and the verified rows show that a window-six local tile captures a striking amount of leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out behavior.  The claim is therefore ready at the level of finite codon-label geometry: the standard code exposes a $13$-codon boundary tile whose shape, spectrum, module partition, and held-out recovery rates are all numerically recorded.

The same rows also impose a strict stopping rule.  Nothing in this chapter establishes translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality across life.  Amino-acid labels are not protein folds.  A local codon tile is not a law of translation.  Any statement crossing from code-label readback into translation, structure, physics, function, mechanism, or universal biology would require an additional observation designed for that layer.  In the present record, the contact is the curated standard genetic-code table, and its scope is codon-label readback plus local split behavior.
