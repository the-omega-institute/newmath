\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for a Local Codon Tile}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and scope.}
This record concerns a finite six-bit codon display $Q_6$ equipped with coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data. The biological contact is only a curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it permits code-layer readback tests. The claim is therefore a code-read claim. It is not a claim about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. A local tile may be measured inside the codon cube, but such a tile is not a global biological law.

\paragraph{Distinguished residual set.}
The finite residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$. In the verified row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row records that this set has the punctured-cube face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it is a finite tile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the window-six display. This is the internal geometric datum against which leave-one-code-out behavior is read.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the reported null sample with seed $42$, $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables were evaluated after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} = 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The number of samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} = 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ records observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$. Its reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite-row values support the statement that the observed local tile is atypical under the tested code-label randomizations. They do not convert the tile into a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate above baseline is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M-T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M-T$ plus the boundary. Thus, when one code table is withheld from the readback family, the finite coordinate tile recovers nearly all targets relative to the reported baseline. This is the central code-layer readback fact for this chapter.

The residual set $R$ is fully assigned into six named modules. The verified module partition is

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\

$$
The module audit records $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons. Hence every residual codon is accounted for by the displayed finite partition. The companion leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. This is consistent with the interpretation that the local tile has a boundary-mediated component rather than a purely anchored one.

\paragraph{Readback boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table. It supplies codon-to-label assignments and supports code-layer split checks. It does not supply a translation apparatus, a protein fold, a physical admissibility measurement, a function assay, or a mechanism-level universality principle. Therefore the ready claim is narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically sharp local boundary tile whose leave-one-code-out label readback has hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ above the stated baseline across $23$ tables and $63$ targets. No higher biological layer follows unless a separate contact supplies that layer.
