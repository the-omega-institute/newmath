\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Readback}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-boundary-readback}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The carrier is the set of codon displays, and the only external contact used here is the curated codon-to-label table assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop. The BEDC object is therefore not translation itself. It is a window-six coordinate readback whose distinctions are a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splitting. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Distinguished boundary row.}
The finite row $R$ has cardinality $13$. The recorded codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check returns the same set and the face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed row is not merely a bag of codons: it is a finite local cell complex inside the codon cube, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces. This is the exact level at which the claim lives. The row is a code-layer boundary tile, not a biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral and null checks.}
The associated spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The cubic relation is numerically closed at $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded as the radial polynomial residual. The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$. It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact shape matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size. The observed value was $|M|=20$, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite code-layer anomaly relative to the sampled null families. They do not identify a physical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. In compact form,
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{\mathrm{base}}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
h_{\mathrm{above}}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
h_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
h_{(M\setminus T)+\partial}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite interpretation is that the local tile and its boundary recover nearly all held-out code-label targets under this row protocol. This is a readback claim about codon labels. It is not a claim that codon geometry performs translation, determines protein structure, or explains biological function.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The family-level row separates $5$ code families. The recorded hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. The only family below perfect recovery in this finite summary is alternative yeast, and even there the above-baseline rate remains high. The family stratification therefore supports the same limited reading as the pooled result: the boundary tile is useful for code-label recovery across the sampled code-table families, but it still has no authority outside the code-read layer.

Every module has predictable-or-boundary status. This gives the chapter its finite closure: the row, the punctured-cube shape, the spectral residual, the null checks, the leave-one-code-out recovery, and the module partition all point to a local codon-label boundary pattern.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The scope boundary is part of the result. The curated standard-code table supplies codon-label assignments, not a mechanism of translation. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. No conclusion about folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from the finite row unless a separate reality contact supplies that layer. The present chapter therefore records a code-read boundary phenomenon: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose labels are checked against the curated table, while all higher biological promotions are blocked.
