\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Readback}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-boundary-readback}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, whose displayed points are codons and whose external labels are the curated amino-acid-or-stop labels supplied by the standard genetic-code bridge.  The internal BEDC-side distinctions used here are a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal structures are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The readback is therefore not a biochemical mechanism; it is the finite standard-code label table read back through a window-six coordinate system.

\paragraph{Observed exceptional row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
   &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same finite row is verified as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the relevant object is not an arbitrary list of exceptional codons: it is a thirteen-point local boundary object with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces in the finite cube representation.  The packet does not promote this shape to a universal biological law; it records the shape as a code-layer boundary tile.

\paragraph{Spectral readback.}
The spectral row check gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the numerical check is at floating-point tolerance rather than an exact symbolic identity in this chapter.  The finite-row interpretation is that the local code tile has a reproducible spectral signature at the coordinate layer.  This is still only a code-read signature: the spectrum does not by itself supply translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the reported finite null sample, the exact local shape occurs $1$ time in $N=2000$ trials, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}$$
The high-overlap check with Jaccard threshold $0.9$ gives $25$ matches in $2000$ trials, hence
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
The sampler used seed $42$, made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  These figures support the claim that the observed tile is not a routine outcome of the sampled coordinate null.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports $0$ exact shape matches, a lower-tail count of $7$, observed $M$ size $20$, $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$, and lower-tail value $0.0035$.  The observed $M$ size is therefore used as a finite scalar in this chapter, while no explicit codon list for $M$ is asserted here.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out test.}
The leave-one-code-out row is the main claim of this subsection.  Across $23$ evaluated genetic-code tables and $63$ total targets, the baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The local tile recovers targets inside $M-T$ at rate $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M-T$ plus the boundary at rate $0.9841269841269841$.  The same value $0.9841269841269841$ is reported as the hit rate above baseline.  In compact form,
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{tables} &= 23,\\
\mathrm{targets} &= 63,\\
\mathrm{baseline} &= 0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M-T) &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M-T+\partial) &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}$$
This is the finite-row content of the leave-one-code-out claim: when a code table is held out, the window-six boundary construction almost always places the target in the predicted local region, far above the reported baseline rate.  The result is a code-label recovery statement, not a statement about the molecular realization of translation.

\paragraph{Family split.}
The family-resolved leave-one-code-out audit contains $5$ families.  The hit rate above baseline is $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  The alternative-yeast family has rate $0.8333333333333334$.  Thus the only reported family below perfect recovery in this row is alternative yeast, while the other four listed families are perfect at the granularity supplied by the finite packet.  This split is useful because it keeps the claim local: the chapter records family-level code-label behavior rather than collapsing all tables into a single universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned to six modules.  The modules are
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The classification reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Consequently every codon in the exceptional row is accounted for by the module split.  The companion leave-one-codon-out summary gives anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This supports a local boundary reading: codons are recovered either by predictable closure behavior or by explicit boundary status, not by an inferred protein-level property.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read behavior and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization.  Therefore the finite-row conclusion is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern for standard-code label readback, and the leave-one-code-out experiment reports high recovery relative to the baseline.  No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this chapter unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.
