\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only as a code-read contact.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with codons displayed by window-six coordinates and then compared against curated amino-acid or stop labels.  The BEDC-side distinctions used here are the coordinate display, local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class.  The contact is therefore deliberately finite: it is a codon-label readback experiment on a curated table, not a theory of translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Recovered boundary row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified finite run it is exactly the following codon set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the finite geometry being used is not an unbounded biological principle; it is a concrete thirteen-vertex local boundary tile inside the codon cube.  The readback target is the standard-code label table, whose role is to supply the external labels against which this tile can be tested.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, with $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$.  The corresponding probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact-shape matches.  Its lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, with observed $M$ size $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite-row comparisons support the claim that the observed local tile is not a frequent accident under the two tested null families.  They do not make the tile a law of molecular biology.

In finite terms, the local boundary row almost always recovers the held-out code-level targets relative to the baseline comparison, while the curated code table remains the source of the biological labels.

Its resolution is codon label readback.  It can test code-read and local codon-tile label split claims.
