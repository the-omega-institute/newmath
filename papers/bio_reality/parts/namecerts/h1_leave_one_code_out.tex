\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The packet studies the standard-code codon table through a six-coordinate codon display.  Each codon is treated as a point of the codon cube $Q_6$, and the observed readback is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The finite row isolates a local tile boundary rather than a biological mechanism: the data may be read as a codon-label pattern, but it is not evidence that translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, or universality has been obtained.  Any claim beyond code-layer label readback would require an additional biological contact of the appropriate kind.

\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ alternatives with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving

\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null row, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$.  The finite data report no exact matches, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0,

\begin{aligned}

These values support a finite combinatorial contrast for the local codon-tile readback.  They do not identify a translation apparatus, a folded structure, a biochemical pathway, or an organism-level necessity.

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
whereas the hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}

Inside $M-T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M-T$ plus the boundary it was again $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus the finite row says that nearly all leave-one-code-out targets are recovered once the local $M-T$ region is enlarged by the boundary component recorded by the tile.  This is the central code-layer observation of the chapter.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and boundary.}
The finite evidence supports a code-read claim: in the six-coordinate codon display, the residual tile $R$ and its boundary structure organize the leave-one-code-out readback with high recovery against the listed finite null rows.  The claim remains local to codon labels.  The curated standard-code table supplies the contact that makes codon-to-label readback meaningful, but it does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a causal mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Those stronger assertions are outside this packet unless separate contacts are supplied for them.
