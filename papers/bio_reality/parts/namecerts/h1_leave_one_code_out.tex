\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Readback}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
This record concerns a finite window-six codon carrier, written here as the codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not treated as a biological mechanism.  It is a finite display on which codons can be read as six-bit coordinates, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The only external contact used in this record is the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  Hence the observable layer is $\mathrm{code\_read}$ only.  The contact can test codon-label readback and local codon-tile label splits, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey this geometry as biological necessity.

\paragraph{Observed residue set.}
The finite residue set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The observed list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.

$$
The punctured-cube check returns the same set and the face-vector data
$$

\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row is not merely a count: it records thirteen codon vertices, sixteen local one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional tile faces in the punctured-cube readback.  The distinguished modules cover all thirteen rows with no extra or missing codons.  The module decomposition is

The module audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing module codons, and no unassigned codons.  Every module therefore has either predictable or boundary status inside this finite carrier.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The spectral row separates the carrier calculation from the biological contact.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the polynomial check is numerically at zero at the recorded tolerance.  The shape-significance experiment used $N=2000$ null samples with seed $42$.  It produced one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The same run recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  These numbers support the claim that the finite tile shape is a non-generic row-level pattern under the sampled null, but they do not promote the pattern into a law of biological translation.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null comparison.}
A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The observed $M$-size was $20$.  The exact class-preserving shape match count was $0$, giving $p_{\mathrm{aa\_class,exact}}=0.0$ in the finite sample.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{aa\_class,lower\_tail}}=0.0035$.  This gives a second finite-row reason to treat the local tile as statistically sharp at the code-read layer.  It still says only that the curated code table exposes an unusually constrained local label pattern relative to the tested null families.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  In compact form,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M-T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M-T+\partial)&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-level check divides the evaluated tables into five families.  The hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast.  Thus the finite row is not only a global aggregate over $23$ tables; it also remains high across the recorded family partition, with the weakest listed family still above five-sixths.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out boundary.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  In this record, recovery above threshold is also $1.0$.  The absence of anchors is important: the probe is not claiming that a single privileged codon determines the table.  Rather, the finite geometry partitions the thirteen-row residue set into predictable rows and boundary rows, and the curated label table supplies the code-layer labels needed to read that partition.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The supported statement is therefore narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the curated standard code, and the leave-one-code-out row recovers target labels at rate $0.9841269841269841$ above the recorded baseline $0.19821739130434785$ over $63$ targets from $23$ tables.  The finite evidence also includes the thirteen-codon punctured cube, the face vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, exact-shape significance $0.0005$, high-Jaccard significance $0.0125$, class-preserving lower-tail significance $0.0035$, and a complete six-module classification of $R$.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this record unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.
