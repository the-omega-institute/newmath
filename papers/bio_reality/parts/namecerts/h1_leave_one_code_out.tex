\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.}
The record is a code-layer test on the six-bit codon display $Q_6$.  A point of $Q_6$ is read only as a codon coordinate, and the only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, which assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label.  Thus the carrier is geometric, while the labels are not derived from the geometry.  The BEDC distinctions used by this row are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The intended readback is therefore a standard-code label table, not a translation apparatus and not a protein object.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The supported claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose local boundary-tile structure whose labels can be checked against the curated standard-code table.  The contact tests codon-label readback and local label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not protein structure.  Any stronger biological claim would require a separate contact at the relevant layer, such as a translation experiment for translation, a structure contact for folding, a physical assay for admissibility, or a functional assay for biological role.
