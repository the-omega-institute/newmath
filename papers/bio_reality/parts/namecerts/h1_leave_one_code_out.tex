\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact is only a code-layer contact: it supports codon-label readback and local tile checks.  It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Throughout this chapter, the term local tile refers to a finite window-six pattern inside $Q_6$, not to a global biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against this baseline, the finite-row tile achieved a hit rate of $0.9841269841269841$ above baseline, a hit rate of $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M-T$, and a hit rate of $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M-T$ plus the boundary.  In compact form,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{tables} &= 23,\\
\mathrm{targets} &= 63,\\
h_{\mathrm{baseline}} &= 0.19821739130434785,\\
h_{M-T} &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
h_{M-T+\partial} &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The read is therefore finite and local: most omitted code targets fall back into the closure row or its boundary, while the baseline rate is much smaller.
