\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The experiment is a finite-row code-read record over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  Its carrier is the codon display itself, while its external contact is only the curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The permitted readback is therefore a label-table readback, not a translation experiment.  The distinctions used in the row are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data on this finite cube.  The central boundary claim is that window-six coordinates can expose a local codon-tile split, but the labels remain contacts with an externally curated code table.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The row supports a code-read conclusion: the curated standard-code label table and the six-bit coordinate construction align on a local tile boundary, and the leave-one-code-out numbers show high held-out recovery relative to the baseline.  The chapter cannot claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function.  Any promotion beyond code-read requires a separate higher-layer reality contact, such as an independent translation contact, a structure experiment, a physical-admissibility measurement, or a function assay.
