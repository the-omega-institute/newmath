\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability of the Codon Boundary Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon label table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The internal carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with vertices read as codons and with labels supplied by the curated standard genetic-code table.  The tested phenomenon is not the whole table.  It is the local behavior of a boundary tile: a singleton corner, a three-point label class, and the induced closure and relation data visible inside the six-bit codon coordinates.  The finite row singled out by the reconstruction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The same set is reported by the punctured-cube check, whose face data have $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the local object is not introduced as a verbal pattern: it is a finite subset of the codon cube with an explicit vertex list and a recorded incidence summary.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape measurements.}
The finite geometry also carries a numerical spectrum.  The recorded spectral values are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic check is numerically residual at $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, matching the reported cubic-polynomial residual to the same value.  This is a numerical consistency check for the coordinate construction, not a biological mechanism.  A separate randomization check with $N=2000$ samples, seed $42$, found one exact shape match, so the exact-shape rate was $p=0.0005$.  The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ had $25$ matches, giving $p=0.0125$.  The run required $57377$ distinct sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which is relevant because the finite comparison space is highly constrained rather than a free continuum.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The principal finite-row test removes code-table context and asks whether the local coordinate geometry still places the missing target in the expected region.  The run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The stricter interior region $M-T$ had hit rate $0.9365079365079365$, and the enlarged region $M-T$ plus boundary returned to $0.9841269841269841$.  In summary form,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{baseline} &= 0.19821739130434785,\\
\text{in } M-T &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\text{in } M-T\text{ plus boundary} &= 0.9841269841269841,\\
\text{tables evaluated} &= 23,\\
\text{targets evaluated} &= 63.
\end{aligned}
$$
The interpretation is deliberately narrow: the local codon coordinates recover a boundary-sensitive label split at the code-read layer.  They do not say that the translation apparatus, protein structure, or organism-level function is generated by this geometry.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class information.  In $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, so the exact-shape rate was recorded as $0.0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, with observed $M$ size $20$, giving lower-tail rate $0.0035$.  The relevant scalar record is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{observed } |M| &= 20,\\
\text{exact matches} &= 0,\\
\text{lower-tail count} &= 7,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This comparison matters because it tests whether the boundary tile is merely an artifact of preserving coarse amino-acid classes.  The finite row says that the exact finite shape was not reproduced under this null model, while a small lower-tail count remains visible.

\paragraph{Family-level breakdown.}
The leave-one-code-out result was also stratified into five code families.  The family hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  This does not turn the local tile into a universal biological law.  It only says that, within the finite tables tested here, the same boundary-sensitive recovery behavior persists across the listed family partition, with alternative yeast showing the only non-unit family rate.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The codon-level module classification assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ and reports no missing or extra module codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The accounting row gives $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, empty missing-from-modules list, empty unassigned-codons list, and empty extra-module-codons list.  A separate leave-one-codon-out row recorded anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module statement is therefore complete at the finite-code level: every listed codon in the punctured tile is placed in one of the six named modules, and each module has predictable or boundary support in the recorded classification.

\paragraph{Local readback and prohibition of overreach.}
The curated standard-code table is the empirical contact used here.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments and thereby lets the six-coordinate construction be checked against amino-acid or stop labels.  It does not observe translation dynamics, ribosomal realization, folded structure, thermodynamic admissibility, cellular function, or a universal mechanism.  Amino-acid label readback is therefore kept at the code layer.  Any claim about folding, physical feasibility, biological function, or mechanism would require an additional contact of the appropriate kind.  Cross-organism wording and tRNA notation likewise do not convert this local code-table measurement into a structural or functional conclusion.  The finite result is that a $13$-codon punctured tile in $Q_6$ has stable local recovery under the stated leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out probes, with strong separation from the listed null comparisons, while all higher biological interpretations remain outside the evidence supplied by these rows.
