\paragraph{Claim site.}
This record concerns the finite codon-coordinate packet named \texttt{h1.leave.one.code.out}.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read at the label layer through the curated standard-code table.  The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The claim is deliberately local: the window-six coordinate geometry is tested as a code-label readback device, not as a biological mechanism.  The biological object under contact is the standard-code codon table, and the layer under test is only $\mathrm{code\_read}$.

The direct leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the packet reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\_rate\_above\_baseline} &= 0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\_rate\_in\_M\setminus T} &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\_rate\_in\_M\setminus T\ plus\ boundary} &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family split is also finite.  There are five reported families: alternative yeast has rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each have rate $1.0$.  The natural reading is that the window-six tile and its boundary extension give a strong local recovery signal across the evaluated code-table rows.  The stronger number belongs to the boundary-augmented readback, while the interior $M\setminus T$ channel alone remains lower at $0.9365079365079365$.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, treated as a codon-to-label observation source.  It supplies the assignment from codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It does not supply translation dynamics, molecular folding, thermodynamic admissibility, phenotype, fitness, or an organism-level universal rule.  Therefore the supported conclusion is narrow: window-six coordinates expose a finite local boundary pattern in code-label readback, and the observed leave-one-code-out rates are much larger than the baseline rate supplied in the same row.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The packet is ready as a code-read claim.  Its finite witnesses are the thirteen-codon boundary set $R$, the closed module partition of those thirteen codons, the face-vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral scalars $\lambda_M$, $\lambda_R$, and $\mu$, the shape-test values $0.0005$ and $0.0125$, the amino-acid-class-preserving lower-tail value $0.0035$, and the leave-one-code-out rates over $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The claim stops at that boundary.  No translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic explanation, or universal biological law follows from these rows unless a separate contact is added for that higher layer.
