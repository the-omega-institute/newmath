It can test the code-read layer and the local codon-tile label split. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization. The contact is therefore a bridge for labels, not a mechanism or a universality proof.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.} The finite rows support a local code-read statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a boundary-tile structure whose standard-code labels are externally supplied by the curated table. They do not imply that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity. They do not imply translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or protein realization. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. Any claim across those boundaries requires a separate contact with the relevant layer. In the absence of such a contact, the correct conclusion is the bounded one: a local codon-tile pattern is aligned with standard-code label readback, and higher biological promotion is blocked.
