\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery at the codon-label boundary}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The object studied here is the standard-code codon table read through a six-bit codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinates recording the three nucleotide positions in a binary presentation of the four RNA letters.  The finite record separates four ingredients: coordinates in $Q_6$, closure behavior of selected codon rows, the adjacency spectrum of the induced tile, and the relation between codons and curated amino-acid or stop labels.  The reality contact used by this record is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it does not by itself observe translation dynamics, folded structure, admissible chemistry, phenotype, or function.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified finite row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is identified as a punctured-cube tile with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the record is not just a list of recoded codons.  It is a finite local tile in the codon cube, with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces.  This is the level at which the present chapter makes its positive claim: the tile is a code-layer shape that can organize readback events in nearby codon-label tables.

The point of this number is local and diagnostic: it records that the observed tile has a stable finite spectral profile.  It is not a law of molecular biology and does not promote the coordinate geometry into a mechanism of translation.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite null sampler with seed $42$, $2000$ comparison samples were evaluated after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap event $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid classes again used $2000$ samples with seed $42$.  In that run the exact shape count was $0$, so the recorded exact-shape probability was $0.0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, with observed size $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite nulls support the statement that the local tile is not a common artifact of the sampled code-label rearrangements.  They do not test ribosome operation, tRNA availability, thermodynamic feasibility, folded-protein order, or biological fitness.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The primary leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
Using the local tile information, the hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value for $M-T$ plus boundary recovery.  The stricter $M-T$ row alone gave
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9365079365079365.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite reading is therefore sharp: when one code table is held out, the local codon-tile organization recovers almost all target labels above the stated baseline.  This is a code-read result about curated label tables.  It is not evidence that the tile causes a biological reassignment, that it determines a folded product, or that it supplies a universal mechanism for genetic-code evolution.

The leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ codons with high closure coverage, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for every element of $R$ without extras or omissions: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons are all empty.  The modules are

Every module has either predictable support or boundary support in the recorded classification.  This closes the finite bookkeeping for the $13$ boundary codons: the row does not leave a stray codon outside the named modules, and it does not need an unrecorded module to explain the listed boundary set.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The chapter records a finite positive result at the code-read layer.  The six-bit codon coordinates expose a local boundary tile whose $13$ codons, punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral values, null-model rarity, family-resolved hit rates, and complete module classification jointly support leave-one-code-out recovery against curated standard-code labels.  The result is deliberately narrower than a biological mechanism.  No claim is made here about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, causal mechanism, or universality.  Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate contact that actually observes the relevant higher-layer object rather than merely reading codon labels from the curated code table.
