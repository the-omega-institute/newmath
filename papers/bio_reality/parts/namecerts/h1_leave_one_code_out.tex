\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for a Local Codon Tile}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback layer.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The carrier is not a biological mechanism; it is the BEDC coordinate display in which each codon is represented by a window-six binary coordinate, then read back through a curated codon-to-label table. The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The biological object under contact is therefore only the standard-code label table: codons are assigned amino-acid or stop labels, and the finite row asks whether the local BEDC tile boundary helps recover held-out code-label changes.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same list and reports the finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the contact is not a loose enrichment of the codon table: it selects a concrete thirteen-codon boundary row with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional local faces. The module ledger partitions this same row without residue. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module classification reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. This is the finite reason that the leave-one tests can be read as row-local tests rather than as an unrestricted search over the full codon cube.

\paragraph{Spectral and null rows.}
The spectral row records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the numerical spectrum is internally consistent to the displayed tolerance. Against an unconstrained shape sample with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count is $1$, giving $p=0.0005$, while the high-overlap row has $25$ cases with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$. The sampling ledger also records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. Against the amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving lower-tail probability $0.0035$. These rows support a finite local-tile signal; they do not identify a physical law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
For the claim $h1.leave.one.code.out$, the finite experiment evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
whereas the hit rate above baseline is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The stricter interior row $M\setminus T$ gives $0.9365079365079365$, and the row $M\setminus T$ plus boundary gives $0.9841269841269841$. Read literally, the finite result says that, when one code-table contact is left out, the local tile plus boundary data recover almost all held-out code-label targets relative to the stated baseline. It does not say that the BEDC geometry generates translation or that biology must instantiate this geometry as a mechanism.

\paragraph{Family and codon deletion checks.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out row contains $5$ families. The family hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family. The companion leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. Hence the codon-deletion contact is boundary-driven rather than anchor-driven: every recovered case is accounted for by predictable or boundary behavior, and the row contains no claimed hidden anchor codon.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
The curated standard-code table is a reality bridge for codon labels only. It can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or biological necessity. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure. No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from this chapter unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence. The present claim is exactly local: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite boundary-tile structure whose readback is evaluated against curated code labels.
