\subsection{Leave-one-code-out stability of the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite record.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table as an external contact at the code-read layer. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with finite distinctions supplied by the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The internal BEDC structure used here is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data; the readback target is only the curated standard-code label table. The central finite claim is leave-one-code-out stability: when variant genetic-code tables are held out, the window-six local tile predicts almost all observed target changes after accounting for the boundary layer.

\paragraph{Observed relation row.}
The relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its codons are exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
This is a code-layer shape statement: it says which codon labels sit in the local relation row, not why a biochemical system realizes them.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}
The measured spectral values separate the main tile $M$ from the relation row $R$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is numerically negligible at the recorded tolerance. The sampler attempted $57377$ distinct draws and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite-row evidence is concentrated rather than diffuse. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, the exact-shape $p$-value was $0.0$, and the lower-tail $p$-value was $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. Thus the observed gain is not a vague qualitative resemblance: it is the finite-row statement that the local tile and boundary layer recover nearly all held-out code-label targets while the raw baseline is about one fifth.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used by this chapter is the curated standard-code table, read at codon-label resolution. That contact observes that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels. It does not observe translation dynamics, ribosomal realization, molecular structure, physical admissibility, or biological function. The evidence basis is therefore external reality plus BEDC coordinate and derived-probe data, but the external reality contact is deliberately narrow: it licenses code-label readback and local split checks, not promotion beyond the code layer.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
No translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, biological necessity, or universality follows from the window-six geometry alone. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. The supported conclusion is only that the window-six codon coordinates expose a strong local boundary-tile pattern in curated code labels: $23$ tables, $63$ targets, a baseline hit rate $0.19821739130434785$, and a boundary-aware hit rate $0.9841269841269841$. Any claim above code-read requires a separate higher-layer contact.
