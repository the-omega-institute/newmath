\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the codon-coordinate display on which local tile, corner, and label-class distinctions can be read.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  Thus the chapter tests codon-label readback and local boundary behavior, not translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.
