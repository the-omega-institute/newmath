\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Carrier and contact.} The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a standard-code label table. The BEDC-side distinctions are the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class. The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code codon table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. This contact has codon-label resolution. It is not a contact with translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity. Therefore the mathematical object tested in this section is a code-layer readback: a local codon-tile pattern may be compared with curated labels, but the comparison cannot be promoted beyond the code_read layer without a separate higher-layer contact.

\paragraph{Finite exceptional row.} The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified codon list is
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same finite row is verified as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the local object is not an informal visual analogy: it is a finite subcomplex of the codon cube with a checked vertex count, edge count, and square-face count. The scope of the claim remains local. A punctured cube in $Q_6$ is a coordinate and closure fact about this row, not a law governing biological realization.

\paragraph{Spectral row signature.} The row also carries a numerical spectrum. The verified values are
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\

These numbers are used only as row-level spectral coordinates. They do not state that the genetic code is forced by the spectrum, and they do not identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.} The shape-level null experiment used $N=2000$ sampled comparisons with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}$$
There were $25$ comparisons with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, observed $0$ exact matches and $7$ lower-tail cases at or below the observed $M$ size. The observed size was $20$, with
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
These finite-row nulls support the statement that the local tile is not a typical draw under the tested finite controls. They do not supply a structural or functional biological conclusion.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.} The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was
$$\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}$$
Against this baseline, the verified code-layer hit rates were
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in}\ M-T&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in}\ M-T+\mathrm{boundary}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}$$
Thus, in the finite data supplied here, the boundary-augmented local tile almost completely recovers the withheld code-level targets, while the unaugmented $M-T$ row already carries most of the recoveries. This is the central support for the claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a strong local boundary-tile readback for curated labels. The statement remains a readback statement because the withheld object is a label in a code table, not a realized protein, fold, function, or universal translation mechanism.

Every module has predictable or boundary status. The module table is the finite-row explanation of where the leave-one-code-out signal lives: it is concentrated in named codon modules of the punctured row rather than being a free-floating numerical score.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.} The ready claim is therefore local and finite: in the six-bit codon cube, the verified row $R$ forms a $13$-vertex punctured-cube tile with a nontrivial spectral signature, significant finite-null shape scores, complete module classification, and strong leave-one-code-out recovery against curated standard-code labels. The claim is not that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as a biological necessity. It is not that window-six geometry alone gives translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary law. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only label readback. Any future crossing from code_read to translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality must bring an independent reality contact at that layer.
