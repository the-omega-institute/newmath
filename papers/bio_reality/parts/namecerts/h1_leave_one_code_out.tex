\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite readback contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the finite display in which codons occupy cube coordinates and receive external labels from a curated standard genetic-code table.  The BEDC minimal form used here has four exposed distinctions: the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class.  Its internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation, while its readback is only the standard-code label table.  Thus the contact can test code-layer label readback and local codon-tile label split, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

This is the geometric part of the readback: it says that the code labels meet a sharply shaped local subset of $Q_6$, not that the codon cube supplies a global biological law.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite-row verdict is therefore narrow.  A curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments, and the window-six representation exposes a local boundary-tile pattern around the thirteen-codon row $R$.  From these data one may state a code-layer readback: the leave-one-code-out targets are recovered at rate $0.9841269841269841$ above the reported baseline $0.19821739130434785$, with the family and module rows listed above.  One may not infer that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as a biological necessity, that a local codon tile is a global biological law, or that amino-acid label-sequence readback is folded protein structure.  No translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate external contact supplies that layer.
