\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery on the window-six codon cube}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and scope.}
The object studied here is the standard-code codon label table read through a six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$.  The carrier is finite: each codon is treated as a point of the window-six cube, while the biological label attached to the point is supplied by the curated standard-code table.  The relevant distinctions are the coordinate position, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class.  These distinctions are used only at the code-read layer.  They support local questions about codon-to-label readback and about the boundary of a finite tile; they do not by themselves assert translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of code origin, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Reality contact and permitted conclusion.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed content is a codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop assignment.  That contact has the correct resolution for code-layer readback and local label-split checks.  It has the wrong resolution for claims about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, or function.  Consequently the supported conclusion is deliberately narrow: window-six coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile organization in the standard-code label table, and the leave-one-code-out benchmark recovers held-out code-label targets at rate $0.9841269841269841$ above a baseline of $0.19821739130434785$ over $23$ tables and $63$ targets.

\paragraph{Local stopping rule.}
No stronger biological claim is licensed by these rows.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry is not translation, protein structure, physical admissibility, or function.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any future promotion beyond code-layer readback would require a separate contact with the relevant phenomenon: a translation contact for translation, a structure contact for folding, a physical contact for admissibility, and a functional contact for biological function.  Until such a contact is supplied, the chapter records a finite code-read phenomenon and nothing above that layer.
