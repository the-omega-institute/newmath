\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary for Window-Six Codon Tiles}\n\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}\n\origin{ai}\n\n\paragraph{Finite claim.}\nThis record concerns the finite code-read claim \texttt{h1.leave.one.code.out}.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code label table.  The tested layer is only the code-read layer: codons are displayed as finite coordinates, local tile faces are inspected, singleton label corners and three-point label classes are separated, and the resulting readback is compared with curated codon-to-label assignments.  The biological contact is therefore a table contact, not a mechanism contact.  It says that a curated standard genetic-code bridge assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label; it does not say that the window-six geometry realizes translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any law of biology.\n\n\paragraph{The punctured local row.}\nThe finite boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the observed standard-code readback the row is exactly\n$$\n\begin{aligned}\nR = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\\n&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.\n\end{aligned}\n$$\nThe same row is certified as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector\n$$\n\begin{aligned}\n(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).\n\end{aligned}\n$$\nThus the local object used by this chapter is not an arbitrary list of exceptional codons.  It is a finite row in $Q_6$ with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four square faces after the distinguished cube position has been removed.  This finite shape is the object on which all later hit-rate and module statements are conditioned.\n\n\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}\nThe row is accompanied by a finite spectral check.  The recorded values are\n$$\n\begin{aligned}\n\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\\n\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\\n\mu &= 3.051487585298128,\\\np(\lambda_M) &= -6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.\n\end{aligned}\n$$\nThe same value is recorded as the radial-polynomial residual, so the spectral radius is within the numerical tolerance used by the probe.  A separate shape-randomization check used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported probabilities should be read as finite-row empirical null comparisons, not as asymptotic biological laws.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, and the corresponding finite probabilities are\n$$\n\begin{aligned}\np_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\\np_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.\n\end{aligned}\n$$\nThese numbers support the statement that the window-six row is a non-generic local code-table pattern under the declared finite nulls.  They do not supply any contact for translation dynamics, structure, chemistry, function, or universality.\n\n\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}\nThe leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was\n$$\n\begin{aligned}\nh_{\mathrm{base}}=0.19821739130434785.\n\end{aligned}\n$$\nThe row-conditioned readback was much higher: the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  In finite terms, the boundary-augmented local tile recovers almost all of the leave-one-code-out targets observed in the table sample.  The claim made here is exactly that code labels omitted from one code table are strongly predicted by the window-six local tile plus boundary structure when checked against the curated label table.  The claim is not that codons physically translate because of this geometry, nor that protein structure or function can be inferred from the recovered label.\n\n\paragraph{Family-level split.}\nThe same leave-one-code-out readback was stratified into five table families.  The family hit rates above baseline were\n$$\n\begin{aligned}\nh_{\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\\nh_{\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}} &= 1.0,\\\nh_{\mathrm{candidate\ other}} &= 1.0,\\\nh_{\mathrm{mitochondrial}} &= 1.0,\\\nh_{\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}} &= 1.0.\n\end{aligned}\n$$\nThe weaker alternative-yeast value is still above the baseline rate $0.19821739130434785$, while the other four families are complete under the finite target set.  This family split is useful because it localizes the readback claim: the result is not merely one pooled number but a finite pattern persisting across declared code-table families.  At the same time, every number in this paragraph is still a code-table number.  No family-level entry is a measurement of ribosomal realization, folding order, thermodynamic admissibility, or organismal function.\n\n\paragraph{Codon module accounting.}\nThe leave-one-codon-out module classification assigns all thirteen codons in $R$ with no extras and no missing terms.  The recorded completeness row is\n$$\n\begin{aligned}\n|R| &= 13,\\\n\mathrm{assigned} &= 13,\\\n\mathrm{missing} &= 0,\\\n\mathrm{extra} &= 0,\\\n\mathrm{unassigned} &= 0.\n\end{aligned}\n$$\nThe finite modules are\n$$\n\begin{aligned}\n\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\\n\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\\n\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\\n\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\\n\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\\n\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.\n\end{aligned}\n$$\nEvery module has either a predictable codon or a boundary codon under the recorded classification.  The companion leave-one-codon-out contact reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The exact recovery rate above threshold is also $1.0$.  This supplies a local finite explanation of why the thirteen-row pattern is useful for code-label recovery: the modules partition the punctured row and place each module on the predictable-or-boundary side of the probe.\n\n\paragraph{Local boundary verdict.}\nThe supported conclusion is a narrow one.  Window-six coordinates expose a local codon-tile boundary whose finite row $R$ has a punctured-cube profile, a non-generic shape score under the sampled nulls, a stable module partition, and a high leave-one-code-out recovery rate against curated standard-code and code-family label tables.  The contact that makes these statements meaningful is the curated codon label table.  It is a reality bridge for labels only.  Therefore the chapter cannot claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global biological law.  Any such promotion would require a separate higher-layer contact with its own observations and its own finite audit row.  In the absence of that separate contact, amino-acid label-sequence readback remains code readback and nothing stronger.
