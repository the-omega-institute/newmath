\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery at the Code-Read Boundary}
\label{sec:bior-h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This BioReality record concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read object on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The coordinate carrier is not a claim about translation itself; it is a display in which codon labels can be read back against a curated standard-code table. The finite row isolates a distinguished region $R$ of thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row reports that this set is a punctured-cube object with face-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it has a finite tile shape: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the induced codon-cube geometry. The reported module partition is complete: the $13$ codons are assigned with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are $\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$ for $\mathrm{agr}$, $\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$ for $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$, $\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$ for $\mathrm{cun}$, $\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$ for $\mathrm{ile\_met}$, $\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$ for $\mathrm{ssn}$, and $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$ for $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$. Every module has either predictable support or boundary support in the supplied classification.

The supplied data give the size of $M$ but not the twenty codon names in $M$; the chapter therefore uses only the recorded $M$-size and the reported rates for statements involving $M$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluates whether code-table variation can be recovered by the local tile and its boundary rather than by arbitrary background frequency. The finite row reports $23$ tables evaluated and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. Against that baseline, the local rule gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in}\ M-T&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in}\ M-T\ \mathrm{plus\ boundary}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-read signal is not a weak enrichment over background in this finite row. It is a near-complete recovery of the evaluated leave-one-code-out targets once the boundary term is included. The family-level row gives five families. The reported hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial or plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate codes. This family split matters because it prevents the aggregate $0.9841269841269841$ value from being read as a single-family artifact; four of the five listed families are saturated at $1.0$, while the alternative-yeast family remains high but not saturated.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out companion check.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out row supplies a local recovery sanity check. It reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. The absence of anchors is important: the row is not saying that recovery is obtained because the missing codon is trivially fixed by a named anchor. The recovery passes through predictable and boundary status inside the finite tile. This is consistent with the module table above, where all thirteen codons in $R$ are accounted for by the six named modules and no codon remains outside the classification.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact supports code-layer readback and local label-split checks. It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Consequently the mathematical conclusion is deliberately bounded: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose leave-one-code-out recovery is high on the supplied finite rows, but the curated label table remains an external reality bridge rather than a mechanistic explanation. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. No claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological necessity follows from this chapter unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that evidence.
