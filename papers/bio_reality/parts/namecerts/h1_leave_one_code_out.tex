\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback on the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
This record concerns the finite experiment indexed by \texttt{h1.leave.one.code.out}.  The object under inspection is the standard-code codon table, viewed only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with codons displayed as six-bit positions and then read against a curated codon-to-label table.  The claim is deliberately local: window-six coordinates expose a stable boundary-tile pattern for codon labels, while the amino-acid and stop labels themselves remain contacts with curated biological data.  The computation does not treat the local tile as translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Finite codon set.}
The distinguished residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the recorded row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row reports that this set is a punctured-cube tile with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the boundary object used by the leave-one-code-out test is not an arbitrary list of codons: it is a finite local face complex inside the six-bit codon display.  The row does not assert that this complex is a biological law; it only supplies a coordinate-shaped region on which label changes can be checked.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape comparison row used $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the sampled controls there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ controls with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second control preserving amino-acid classes also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that row the observed size was $|M|=20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding reported values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite rows support the statement that the observed local shape is atypical under the sampled nulls, including the amino-acid-class-preserving null.  They do not identify a mechanism for why the standard code has this shape.

\paragraph{Codon-level boundary check.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  It also records that the recovery rate is above threshold with value $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$: the assigned count is $13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\

$$
Every listed module has either predictable or boundary support in the finite row.  This is the local content of the claim: the window-six coordinate packet organizes the observed code-label exceptions into a finite boundary-and-predictability pattern.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only reality contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  That contact can test code-read statements and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence obtained by table lookup is not a folded protein structure.  A separate structural, biochemical, organism-matched, or functional contact would be needed before any higher-layer claim could be made.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite rows make the claim ready at the code-read layer.  The $13$-codon residual set, its punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral diagnostics, the sampled null comparisons, the $23$-table and $63$-target leave-one-code-out recovery row, and the complete six-module classification all point to the same local conclusion: the standard-code table contains a boundary-tile pattern visible in window-six codon coordinates.  The chapter records no stronger biological conclusion.  The result is a finite coordinate-and-label readback claim with an explicit stop at the boundary between code labels and biological realization.
