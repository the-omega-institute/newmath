\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery in a Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon label table, read only as a finite assignment from codons to amino-acid or stop labels. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with codons displayed as six-bit positions and with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes used as the finite distinctions. The biological contact is therefore deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table supplies code labels, and the window-six construction tests whether local coordinate closure recovers the sites where code labels change under leave-one-code-out evaluation.

The exceptional set $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation the verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube pattern with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local geometric datum is not an informal visual analogy: it is a finite subset of $Q_6$ with an explicit codon list and a fixed face vector.

\begin{aligned}

$$
The high-overlap comparison found $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving

The sampling audit also records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. The reported values are

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite code-layer anomaly relative to the tested null rows; they do not identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ alternative code tables and $63$ total target entries. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. The local window-six recovery rates are much larger:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}((M\setminus T)\cup \partial)&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus, within the finite rows supplied here, leaving one code table out still places nearly all withheld targets inside the local recovery region once the boundary contribution is allowed. The result is a code-read result: it says that codon-label changes concentrate in a coordinate-local tile and its boundary, not that translation or protein behavior has been derived from the tile.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The conclusion is that a six-coordinate codon display exposes a finite local tile that is strongly aligned with curated code-label variation. The table contact is a codon-to-label contact only. It supports statements about standard-code label readback, alternative-code target recovery, punctured-cube shape, spectral values, null-model rarity, and module coverage. It does not by itself support claims about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a general law of organisms. Any such higher-layer statement would require an additional empirical contact of the appropriate kind. In this chapter the observed evidence remains at the code-read layer.
