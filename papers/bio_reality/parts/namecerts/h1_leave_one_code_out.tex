\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery in the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and external label contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact, not as a mechanism of translation. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with codons read through a curated standard-code label table. The BEDC-side distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The external contact supplies only the row-wise assignment from codon to amino-acid or stop label. It does not supply folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a translation mechanism, or a universality principle.

\paragraph{The distinguished row set.}
The finite row set $R$ has cardinality $13$. In the observed standard-code coordinate, the rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube audit gives the same row list and the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object being tested is not an arbitrary biological narrative; it is a finite subconfiguration of $Q_6$ with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces. The singleton and three-point label behavior is evaluated only after this finite coordinate object has been paired with the curated label table.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}
The same finite row geometry has a recorded spectral signature. The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual was $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the cubic constraint is satisfied to numerical tolerance in this finite audit. Against the random shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampling run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and saw $55377$ duplicate retries, which records that the comparison is made inside a constrained finite sample space rather than an unconstrained continuum.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class structure. In that audit, the observed $M$ size was $20$ over $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. The exact shape count was $0$, so the recorded exact-shape probability was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the claim that the observed local codon-tile shape is not typical under the tested class-preserving null. It still does not identify a biological cause; it only records a finite geometric deviation after the code-label bridge has been supplied.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The central test removes code-table information and asks whether the local tile plus boundary information recovers held-out code labels above baseline. The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841,
\end{aligned}
$$
with hit rate $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M-T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M-T$ plus the boundary. The finite-row conclusion is therefore a code-read conclusion: for these $63$ held-out targets across $23$ evaluated tables, the local window-six coordinate and its boundary supply strong recovery relative to the recorded baseline.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The family-level audit divides the leave-one-code-out result into $5$ families. The family hit rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The weaker alternative-yeast value is still well above the scalar baseline $0.19821739130434785$, while the other four families are complete in this finite run. This stratification keeps the claim local: the table families are evidence that the recovery pattern is not concentrated in a single row family, not evidence that the codon cube determines biology by itself.

\paragraph{Codon-level module accounting.}
The leave-one-codon-out module audit assigns all $13$ rows of $R$ to six named modules, with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons. The complete module decomposition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification has $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$ and assigned count $13$, with empty extra-module, missing-from-module, and unassigned lists. The separate leave-one-codon-out recovery audit reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. Hence every module has either predictable or boundary support in the finite bookkeeping.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The ready claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure that predicts held-out code labels above the recorded baseline when the curated standard-code table is used as the external label contact. The finite evidence consists of the $13$-row punctured-cube set, its face-vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, $\mu=3.051487585298128$, the null probabilities $0.0005$, $0.0125$, $0.0$, and $0.0035$, the $23$-table and $63$-target leave-one-code-out run, the family stratification, and the complete $13$-codon module assignment. Nothing in this record promotes the local tile to translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or a global biological law. Any such higher-layer statement requires a separate reality contact beyond the curated codon-label table.
