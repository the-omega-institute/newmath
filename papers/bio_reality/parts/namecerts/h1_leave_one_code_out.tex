\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and code-layer contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a coordinate display for the standard-code codon table.  A codon is treated as a vertex of $Q_6$ through its window-six coordinate, while the curated standard genetic-code table supplies the external label contact: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label.  The present record therefore concerns code-layer readback.  It does not claim translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Those higher layers would require separate contacts not contained in the curated code table.

\paragraph{The recovered boundary row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is certified as the punctured-cube row, with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the visible object is not just a list of exceptional codons: it is a local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces inside the codon cube coordinate system.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row signature.}
The associated spectrum records a local numerical signature rather than a biological law.  The verified values are

\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.

The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, and the same value is reported as the cubic-polynomial residual.  The spectral-radius value in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$.  These scalars support the finite coordinate reading of the tile, but they do not turn the tile into a law of protein production or organismal function.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
Against the unrestricted shape null with $N=2000$ samples, the run used seed $42$, made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  The observed size of $M$ was $20$, with $0$ exact matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed size.  Its reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,lower}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact membership list of $M$ is not part of the supplied finite row data, so this paragraph uses the certified size and null statistics only.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ genetic-code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The code-layer tile achieved
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T) &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T\;\mathrm{plus\ boundary}) &= 0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ above\ baseline} &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus, within the stated finite experiment, the local tile and its boundary almost always recover the held-out code-level target above the baseline rate.  This is a readback result about label rows in curated code tables.  It is not evidence that window-six geometry alone performs translation, determines folded protein structure, enforces physical admissibility, realizes biological function, or supplies a universal mechanism.

The leave-one-codon-out check reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, closure coverage high on $8$ codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  At the code-family level there are $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline are

\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The one family below perfect recovery is still far above the baseline rate of $0.19821739130434785$, while the other four families achieve full above-baseline recovery in this finite table set.

The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and there are no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons.  The row decomposes into the following modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}

Every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the supplied classification.  This is the local content of the readback claim: the thirteen-codon boundary row is partitioned into named finite modules, and the leave-one probes test whether those modules recover code labels under held-out conditions.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The curated standard-code table is a reality bridge for codon labels.  It is not a structure experiment, not a translation apparatus, not a folding assay, not a thermodynamic admissibility certificate, and not a function measurement.  Therefore the correct conclusion is narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically sharp local boundary-tile structure for code-label readback, with $13$ certified row codons, punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, exact-shape null probability $0.0005$, amino-acid-class lower-tail probability $0.0035$, and leave-one-code-out above-baseline hit rate $0.9841269841269841$.  No claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
