\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Carrier, contact, and tested layer.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite coordinate display for the $64$ codons. The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label, and the finite-row experiment asks how much local boundary information is visible at the code-read layer. The contact is therefore a label-readback bridge, not a translation experiment, not a folding experiment, and not a mechanism for biological function. The claim tested in this row is the leave-one-code-out claim: after withholding code-table information in the specified finite protocol, the local tile structure predicts whether targets fall inside the observed interior region $M\setminus T$ or inside that region enlarged by the boundary.

\paragraph{Boundary set and punctured cube.}
The finite boundary row records a distinguished set $R$ of cardinality $13$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube row gives the same $13$ codons and reports the face-vector signature
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the readback is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it has a finite local tile footprint with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces. The row is still only local. It does not claim that the codon cube imposes a global biological law, and it does not claim that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The spectral row separates the $M$ and $R$ scales. It reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic, or radial, polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the finite spectral calculation is internally closed at numerical tolerance. This supplies a coordinate-scale witness for the local split: $R$ is a lower spectral-scale boundary object inside the same codon display, while $M$ carries the larger observed scale. The supplied finite rows give the size of the observed $M$ row as $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment; they do not include the complete list of the $20$ $M$ codons in this record.

\paragraph{Null-model pressure.}
The shape-significance row used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. Exact shape agreement occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It recorded no exact shape matches, an observed $M$ size of $20$, and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below that observed size:
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the local tile is not a generic artifact of arbitrary relabeling under the sampled nulls. They do not elevate the tile into a law of translation or a claim about protein structure.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
For the claim row itself, the experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate after adjoining the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$. The local conclusion is therefore sharp: almost all tested leave-one-code-out targets are recovered by the observed interior-plus-boundary tile, while the baseline rate is about one fifth. This is a code-read statement about finite labels in the curated table. It is not a claim that the six-bit geometry performs translation, selects folded structure, enforces physical admissibility, realizes biological function, supplies a molecular mechanism, or establishes universality.

\paragraph{Family and module resolution.}
The family row groups the leave-one-code-out behavior into $5$ families. The family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module-classification row assigns all $13$ boundary codons and leaves no extra or missing codons: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing-from-module codons, and no unassigned codons. The module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the finite row, so the boundary is not an unstructured residue after prediction. It is a complete finite module decomposition of the $13$ codons listed above.

\paragraph{Codon-level companion check.}
The leave-one-codon-out companion row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. This supports the same locality boundary from the codon side: recovery is complete in the finite code-read protocol, but the absence of anchors and the explicit boundary count mark the result as a local closure-and-boundary phenomenon, not a higher-layer biological realization.

\paragraph{Verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite rows support a ready code-read verdict for the leave-one-code-out claim. In the six-bit codon display, the curated standard-code table exposes a local boundary tile whose $13$ codons, punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral values, null-model p-values, family hit rates, and module classification jointly support the local readback claim. The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this chapter unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
