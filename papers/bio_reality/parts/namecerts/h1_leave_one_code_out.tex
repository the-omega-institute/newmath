\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a display of the $64$ codons under a curated standard-code label table.  The contact used here is only the codon-label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop, and it thereby supports code-layer readback.  It does not by itself observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  The claim in this section is therefore a bounded code-read claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern that is predictive for held-out code-table changes, while the labels themselves remain external reality contacts.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
   &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified finite-row audit reports both $|R|=13$ and the same punctured-cube support.  Its face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not being used as an arbitrary list of exceptional labels.  It is a finite local tile with $13$ vertices, $16$ local one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the six-bit codon display.

\paragraph{Spectral row check.}
The row also has a reproducible spectral signature.  The verified values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported radial or cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is at numerical zero for the finite computation.  This does not promote the tile to a biological law.  It records that the coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation views agree on the same finite codon-row object before any biological interpretation is attempted.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The observed $M$-shape has size $20$ in the finite audit.  Against $N=2000$ sampled null tables with seed $42$, the exact shape count is $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, so these probabilities should be read as finite-row Monte Carlo evidence under the stated sampling mechanism, not as an asymptotic theorem.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, and the reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local tile is not merely a restatement of amino-acid class preservation in the sampled finite rows.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out test.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ genetic-code tables and $63$ held-out targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The verified hit rates for the window-six tile are
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
h_{M\setminus T+\partial}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
h_{\mathrm{above\ baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central code-read observation: most held-out code-table changes land in the local finite structure determined by $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary, far above the baseline rate.  The result concerns label recovery within curated code tables.  It does not say that the codon cube causes the code, that all organisms must obey this geometry, or that codon-label readback determines protein structure.

\paragraph{Family resolution.}
The same leave-one-code-out computation is resolved into $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline are

The alternative-yeast family is the only listed family below perfect hit rate, but it remains far above the global baseline $0.19821739130434785$.  This family split is useful because it prevents the claim from hiding all successes inside one pooled statistic.

\paragraph{Codon-out and module checks.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification covers all $13$ codons in $R$ with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons: $R$ size is $13$, assigned count is $13$, extra module codons are empty, missing-from-modules is empty, and unassigned codons are empty.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary explanation in the finite row.  This is the strongest self-contained form of the claim: the window-six local tile is not just a global hit-rate artifact, since its $13$ codons also decompose into named finite modules with complete accounting.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The evidence in this section stays at the $\mathrm{code\_read}$ layer.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments and lets the local codon-tile split be tested.  It does not supply a ribosome-level translation contact, a protein-structure contact, a physical-admissibility contact, a function assay, a mechanistic derivation, or a universality proof.  Consequently, amino-acid label-sequence readback must not be identified with folded protein structure, and a local codon tile must not be promoted into a global biological law unless a separate higher-layer reality contact is supplied.
