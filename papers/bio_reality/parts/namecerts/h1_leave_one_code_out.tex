\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the $64$ codon displays obtained from three nucleotide positions with two binary coordinates per position.  The biological contact used here is deliberately narrow: a curated standard genetic-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  This contact tests code-layer label readback and local codon-tile label splitting.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey the displayed geometry as a biological law.  The claim studied in this row is therefore not that a local tile explains biology.  It is that, after the curated labels are attached to the finite codon carrier, the window-six coordinates expose a reproducible boundary pattern for leave-one-code-out readback.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}

\begin{aligned}

\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\

The radial cubic check is numerically closed at the supplied tolerance: both the cubic-polynomial residual and radial-polynomial residual are $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  Shape significance was tested against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  Among $57377$ distinct-sample attempts there were $55377$ duplicate retries, $1$ exact shape match, and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{exact}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$ and seed $42$ gave $observed\_M\_size=20$, $exact\_match\_count=0$, and $lower\_tail\_count\_leq\_observed\_M\_size=7$.  The corresponding reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{aa\text{-}class,exact}&=0.0,\\
p_{aa\text{-}class,lower\ tail}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row anomaly at the code-label layer.  They do not convert the anomaly into a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
For the claim $h1.leave.one.code.out$, the experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The stricter interior rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate in $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  In compact form, the observed finite-row readback is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{M\setminus T+boundary}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\#\mathrm{tables}&=23,\\
\#\mathrm{targets}&=63.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-level split is also finite and concrete.  The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate, across $5$ families.  The only family listed below perfect recovery is therefore still well above the baseline rate.
