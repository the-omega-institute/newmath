\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Code Readback Boundary}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
This record concerns the window-six codon carrier $Q_6$, read as a six-bit codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, three-point label classes, coordinates, closures, spectra, and relations. The biological contact used here is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. That contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks. It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universality theorem.

\paragraph{Distinguished residual row.}
The finite residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same set is certified as a punctured-cube row with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not just a cardinality count: it has a finite cubical boundary profile. The module classification covers all $13$ rows with no extras, no missing rows, and no unassigned codons. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},& \mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},& \mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},& \mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The count check is exact: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$ and the assigned count is $13$.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under null sampling.}
The local shape is rare under the reported null ensembles. In the main shape-significance run with $N=2000$, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$. Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a sharper finite-row comparison: with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These statistics support the claim that the observed local code-read tile is not a generic sample from the tested finite nulls.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The direct leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. Against that baseline, the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The stricter $M\setminus T$ window retained hit rate $0.9365079365079365$, and adding the boundary restored the hit rate to $0.9841269841269841$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{\mathrm{baseline}}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
h_{\mathrm{above\ baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
h_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
h_{M\setminus T+\partial}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-level split is also finite and explicit. Across $5$ families, the hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate. Thus the only listed family below perfect recovery is alternative yeast, while all other listed families reach the observed ceiling.

\paragraph{Codon-removal companion check.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out run reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary rows, $8$ rows with high closure coverage, $8$ predictable rows, and recovery rate $1.0$. The same recovery rate is above the threshold, also $1.0$. This gives a local consistency check: the leave-one-code-out table test and the leave-one-codon-out codon test both point to a boundary-assisted code-read phenomenon rather than to an unconstrained label lookup.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The supported claim is local and finite: window-six codon coordinates expose a boundary-tile structure whose standard-code labels can be read back with high leave-one-code-out recovery. The contact is the curated standard-code table, and the layer is $\mathrm{code\_read}$. Nothing in this record promotes the tile to translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal biological law. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only label readback. Any later claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact that tests that higher layer directly.
