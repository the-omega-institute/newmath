\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary Tile Readback}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-boundary-tile-readback}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite row records a code-layer readback claim for the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of codon coordinates together with curated amino-acid or stop labels.  The claim is not that codon biology is generated by cube geometry.  It is the narrower assertion that the window-six coordinate display exposes a local boundary-tile pattern whose label readback is unusually stable under leave-one-code-out tests.  The external contact is the curated standard-code table: it supplies the codon-to-label assignment, while the BEDC coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation records supply the finite-row probes.

\paragraph{Distinguished row.}
The relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The codons in the row are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object used by the readback probe is not a verbal summary of the genetic code.  It is a specific thirteen-codon subset, with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces in the local cubical representation.  The row is therefore large enough to carry face data but small enough to remain a local tile rather than a global law for the whole code table.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The null comparison for the $M$-shape was run with $N=2000$ sampled tables, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, of which $55377$ were duplicate retries.  The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that row the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite comparisons support the statement that the observed local shape is not reproduced at ordinary frequency by the listed null procedures.  They do not identify a biological cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
Against that baseline, the recorded hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The readback content is therefore concrete: when a code row is left out, the local finite object recovers almost all targets above the baseline criterion, and the boundary-augmented region accounts for the same $0.9841269841269841$ rate.  This is a code-label result, not a protein-structure result.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The curated standard-code table is a reality bridge for labels only.  It says that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  The local tile may organize code-layer label readback, but no inference is licensed from this row to protein folding, function, mechanism, or universality unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies the missing observation.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is not a folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The ready finite statement is therefore: in the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, the thirteen-codon row $R$ with punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, stable spectral coordinates, significant null-model shape behavior, complete module assignment, and leave-one-code-out hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ supports a local code-read boundary-tile record for the curated standard-code table.  The supported layer is exactly code readback.  All higher biological interpretations remain blocked until supplied by independent contacts.
