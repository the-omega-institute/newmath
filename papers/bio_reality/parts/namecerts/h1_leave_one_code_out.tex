\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Readback in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:bior-h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed label contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$ obtained from a two-bit display of each codon position.  A codon is treated here only as a point of this finite cube together with a curated standard-code label.  The external contact is therefore a code table: it assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label, but it does not observe translation chemistry, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  The claim tested in this row is narrower: when the standard-code label table is used as the readout, the window-six coordinate system exposes a stable local boundary tile that helps recover changed code labels under leave-one-code-out tests.

\paragraph{The punctured-cube boundary row.}
The boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and consists exactly of
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row verifies that this set is the punctured-cube shape detected by the window-six construction, with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-layer object under discussion is not the whole codon table as an undifferentiated list.  It is a finite local tile: thirteen named codons, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces inside the codon cube.  The row does not assert that this tile is forced by biology.  It only says that, after the curated label table supplies codon labels, this particular local subset is the boundary geometry that is repeatedly hit by the leave-one-code-out probes.

\paragraph{Spectral scale of the tile.}
The associated spectrum separates the boundary and module scales.  The verified numerical values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the cubic-polynomial check, so the finite computation is at numerical tolerance rather than an informal visual fit.  In this chapter these eigenvalue-like scalars are used only as internal diagnostics of the finite coordinate construction.  They are not treated as energetic, kinetic, evolutionary, or structural parameters.

\paragraph{Null comparisons for the shape.}
Against $2000$ random trials with seed $42$, the exact punctured-cube shape appeared once.  The exact-shape probability estimate is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=1/2000=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A looser Jaccard threshold of at least $0.9$ was met $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid class structure recorded no exact shape match in $2000$ trials.  In that same run the observed module size was $20$, and only $7$ trials fell in the lower tail at or below the observed size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0/2000=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the finite statement that the observed local tile is uncommon under the two sampled null procedures.  They do not identify a causal biological source for the tile.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The coordinate tile raised the hit rate above baseline to $0.9841269841269841$, which corresponds to $62$ hits out of $63$ targets.  The stricter interior region $M\setminus T$ reached $0.9365079365079365$, corresponding to $59$ hits out of $63$, while adding the boundary recovered the same $62$ out of $63$ level:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline} &=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T\cup \partial)&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-level stratification gives five families.  The hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  The finite row therefore supports a code-read claim: most changed labels in the evaluated tables fall back into the module-plus-boundary geometry of the window-six construction.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and module accounting.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out row records $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchors, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The high-coverage count is $8$, again with recovery rate above threshold equal to $1.0$.  The module partition accounts for all thirteen boundary codons and introduces no extra module codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{assigned\ count}&=13,\\
\mathrm{missing\ from\ modules}&=0,\\
\mathrm{unassigned\ codons}&=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified module names and members are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either predictable support or boundary support in the finite run.  This is the strongest local reading of the row: the codons that form the punctured-cube boundary are not an arbitrary thirteen-point list after module classification; they are exhausted by six named code-change modules.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The result is a finite code-layer statement.  It says that the curated standard-code table, when read through window-six coordinates, yields a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary with strong leave-one-code-out recovery across $23$ evaluated tables and $63$ targets.  It also says that the same finite boundary is rare in the sampled null procedures and complete under the recorded six-module classification.  Nothing in this row observes ribosomal translation, protein folding, physical feasibility, organismal fitness, or biological function.  An amino-acid label sequence read from the table remains a label sequence; it is not a folded protein and not a mechanism.  Any later claim crossing those layers must supply a separate contact that directly measures the higher-layer object.
