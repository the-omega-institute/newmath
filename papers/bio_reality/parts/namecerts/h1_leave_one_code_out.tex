\subsection{Leave-one-code-out readback for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The claim tested here is not that the geometry of $Q_6$ is a biological mechanism. It is only that the window-six coordinate presentation exposes a local boundary tile whose label readback is unusually stable under leave-one-code-out tests. The biological contact is therefore deliberately narrow: it is a code-layer contact for codon-to-label readback, not a contact for translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Exceptional row set.}
The distinguished residual row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In the verified finite table it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row set is recorded as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile boundary used by the leave-one-code-out readback. The statement is finite-row: the row names, the cardinality, and the face-vector are part of the observation, not inferred biological necessities.

\paragraph{Spectral row certificate.}
The spectral probe attached to the same carrier records three scalars: $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The reported radial polynomial residual is numerically negligible at $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the cubic-polynomial check. Thus the row certificate used by this chapter is the finite numerical package
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,\\
P_{\mathrm{rad}}(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The spectrum is used as an internal coordinate diagnostic for the tile. It does not by itself supply any higher biological layer.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the recorded null family for $M$-shape, the experiment used $N=2000$ sampled trials with seed $42$. The exact-shape match count was $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ had $25$ matches, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. These finite counts support the claim that the observed local shape is not a typical draw from the stated null, while still leaving the interpretation at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class preserving null gives a sharper row-level check. In $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$, the exact-shape match count was $0$, so the reported exact-shape value is $p=0.0$ at the displayed resolution. The observed $M$ size is $20$, and only $7$ null samples landed in the lower tail at or below that size, giving $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$. In finite-row form:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
|M|_{\mathrm{obs}}&=20,\\
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ matches}\}&=0,\\
\#\{\mathrm{lower\ tail}\leq |M|_{\mathrm{obs}}\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The available row data therefore supports a non-generic local label split even after preserving amino-acid class, but it still does not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ target events. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. Inside $M\setminus T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary it was again $0.9841269841269841$. Thus the finite row records the following readback profile:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\mathrm{tables}&=23,\\
\#\mathrm{targets}&=63,\\
h_{\mathrm{base}}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
h_{>\mathrm{base}}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
h_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
h_{M\setminus T+\partial}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central chapter claim: once one leaves out a code table and reads back through the local tile boundary, the finite row almost always lands above the baseline hit rate. The assertion is statistical and local to the supplied table family.

\paragraph{Family split.}
The family-wise readback divides the $23$ tables into $5$ recorded families. The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate. The alternative-yeast family is therefore the only displayed family below perfect recovery, and it remains above the baseline comparison. This supports a family-stable code-read signal rather than a single-table accident.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The $13$ codons in $R$ are completely assigned to six modules. The verified module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness check reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from modules, and no unassigned codons. Each displayed module has a predictable-or-boundary witness. This matters because the local tile is not merely a set of isolated codons; it is a finite row partition whose modules cover the punctured-cube boundary without leftovers.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out boundary check.}
The related leave-one-codon-out probe reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The same row package is therefore robust at two finite resolutions: leaving out whole code tables gives an above-baseline rate $0.9841269841269841$, while leaving out codons gives full recovery within the stated predictable-or-boundary criterion.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion is a code-read conclusion. The curated standard-code table supplies codon labels, and the window-six coordinate system supplies a local tile on $Q_6$. Together they support a finite statement about local label split, residual row shape, module coverage, spectrum, and leave-one-code-out recovery. They do not supply translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Any such higher-layer claim requires a separate contact with data at that layer. Without that separate contact, amino-acid label readback remains label readback, not protein structure or function.
