\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery in the window-six codon tile}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite window-six codon display separates a local code-read signal from stronger biological assertions.  The object under inspection is the standard-code codon table, treated here only as a curated assignment from codon triples to amino-acid or stop labels.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data used to form a local tile.  The claim tested in this row is that leaving one code table out still recovers the held-out label behavior at a rate far above a simple baseline, while the result remains confined to the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The distinguished relation row has cardinality $13$.  The codons in the row are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same set, with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row is not introduced as an informal biological category; it is a concrete thirteen-codon subset with a recorded local cubical shape.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The spectral calculation supplies three scalar coordinates for the same local object:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the finite computation is numerically at tolerance for the polynomial relation being checked.  The spectral row is used here as a local diagnostic for the window-six tile, not as a mechanism for translation or protein production.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite sampling comparison with seed $42$, the shape row used $N=2000$ samples.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The run records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving comparison, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, observed $0$ exact matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size $20$.  Its recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support that the local codon-tile shape is unusual relative to the finite nulls supplied in the row.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out test.}
The primary recovery test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Inside $M\setminus T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and after adjoining the boundary component it returned to $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus the held-out-code calculation is not merely matching the baseline frequency; on the supplied finite row it recovers nearly all targets once the local tile and its boundary are counted.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The by-family check partitions the evaluated cases into $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline are

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\

$$
The alternative-yeast family is the least complete of the reported strata, but it is still far above the baseline rate $0.19821739130434785$.  The other four reported families are complete on the supplied finite evaluation.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The thirteen codons are also exhausted by six named modules.  The recorded module assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has a predictable or boundary representative under the recorded classification, so the leave-one-code-out recovery is distributed over the entire finite row rather than isolated in a single codon family.

\paragraph{Codon-level comparison.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate $1.0$.  This is consistent with the code-level row: the signal is local and boundary-sensitive.  It does not require the thirteen codons to be interpreted as a universal biological rule.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only contact used here is the curated standard-code table as a codon-label assignment.  It can test whether the local tile reads back amino-acid or stop labels in the code table, and it can test the local label split induced by the finite row.  It does not observe translation in a cell, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Therefore the result is a code-read result: it says that the window-six finite geometry aligns strongly with the curated label table under the leave-one-code-out protocol.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
No conclusion in this subsection licenses promotion from codon labels to protein structure.  An amino-acid label sequence is not a folded protein, and a local cubical tile is not a global law of biology.  To claim translation realization, folding, admissible molecular physics, biological function, mechanism, or universality, a separate contact measuring that layer would be needed.  The present row establishes a finite, chapter-local code-label recovery pattern: $23$ tables, $63$ targets, a baseline rate near $0.1982$, an observed above-baseline rate near $0.9841$, and a thirteen-codon punctured-cube relation with complete module coverage.
