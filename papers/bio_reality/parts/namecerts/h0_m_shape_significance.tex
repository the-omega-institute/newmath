\subsection{Window-Six Local Tile Significance for the Standard Code}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a coordinate display for the $64$ codons of the standard genetic code.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label, and it is treated only as a code-layer readback contact.  The BEDC-side distinctions used by this row are the six-bit codon display, local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class.  The internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  No biological mechanism is imported into the carrier.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The finite row identifies a relation set $R$ of cardinality $13$.  The observed list is
$$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.

$$
The same row records that $R$ is a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
Thus the contact does not merely say that some codons have interesting labels; it records a particular finite local shape inside the window-six codon cube.  The observed $M$ row has size $20$ under the amino-acid-class-preserving null test, but no explicit finite list of $M$ codons is supplied in this row, so this section uses only the recorded size and shape statistics.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against $N=2000$ distinct sampled comparisons with seed $42$, the exact local shape appeared once.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite-row estimate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap test counted $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both values are treated as code-layer evidence for non-generic local boundary structure in the window-six representation.  They are not treated as evidence that the geometry generates translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null row.}
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null model used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It recorded $0$ exact shape matches, observed $|M|=20$, and counted $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The finite-row probabilities are therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the local reading: even when label classes are preserved, the observed local geometry remains unusually constrained.  The proper conclusion is still limited to codon-label readback and finite local split structure.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The result is a BioReality record at the code-read layer.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments, and the finite rows show that the window-six coordinate geometry exposes a statistically unusual local tile boundary for those labels.  The row cannot claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A separate higher-layer contact would be required before any statement about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality could be made.
