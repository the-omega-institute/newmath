\subsection{Window-six local tile significance for the standard-code table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The object studied here is the standard-code codon table read through a finite six-bit codon display. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with local distinctions given by coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data. The only external contact used in this record is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and therefore supports code-layer readback. This contact does not supply a translation dynamics, a folded structure, a physical-admissibility test, a function assay, a biological mechanism, or a universality theorem.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary relation $R$ has cardinality $13$. In the verified finite row, the relation is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-layer signal is not merely a count: the selected codons form a finite local shape in the six-bit display. The observed list also separates into named modules without residue. The module classification assigns $AGA,AGG$ to $agr$, $AAA$ to $ar\_lys$, $CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU$ to $cun$, $AUA$ to $ile\_met$, $UCA,UUA$ to $ssn$, and $UAA,UAG,UGA$ to $stop\_trp$. The module audit reports $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null sampling.}
The finite-row significance test for the $M$-shape used $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$. The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the effective finite sample was small relative to the collision pressure of the null space. Within those $2000$ retained rows, exactly one row matched the observed shape exactly, and $25$ rows had Jaccard score at least $0.9$. The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same values are recorded as the exact-shape and high-Jaccard significance values. The conclusion is therefore local and finite: the observed $M$-shape is rare under the sampled null model, and the high-overlap neighborhood is still sparse. This is a code-read statement about the curated label table, not a claim that the biological system is forced by the window-six geometry.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null row.}
A second null row preserves amino-acid class. In this row the observed $M$ size is $20$. Among $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$, the exact match count is $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the finite-row reading: even after preserving amino-acid class, the observed local configuration is not a typical sampled row. The statement remains bounded by the supplied contact. It says that the curated code labels, when read in the six-bit coordinate display, expose a rare local tile; it does not say that the tile is a translation machine, a folding rule, or a physical law.

They provide a coordinate-and-spectrum readback of the local tile, not an independent biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests at code and codon level.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit rate above baseline is $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast. The leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The same row records recovery-rate-above-threshold as $1.0$.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite-row evidence supports the following bounded statement. In the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, the curated standard-code label table exposes a rare local boundary-tile pattern: the relation $R$ is the explicit thirteen-codon punctured cube above, the observed $M$-shape is rare under both the sampled shape null and the amino-acid-class preserving null, the spectral row satisfies its cubic residual to numerical tolerance, and leave-one tests recover the local pattern at rates far above the baseline row. The reality contact is still only the curated code table. Therefore the chapter cannot claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity. Any such promotion requires a separate contact that directly tests the higher layer.
