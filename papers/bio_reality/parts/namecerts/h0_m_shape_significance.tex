\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The record starts from the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ and uses the curated standard-code table only as a code-label contact.  The carrier is finite: codons are read as vertices in the window-six cube, and the biological contact supplies the standard amino-acid-or-stop label assigned to each codon.  This contact is deliberately narrow.  It tests code-layer readback and local codon tile label splits; it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Thus the object studied here is not DNA as a necessary geometric law, but the finite relation between a coordinate presentation of codons and the curated standard-code label table.

This gives the local tile a concrete combinatorial footprint: it is not merely a label list, but a $13$-vertex, $16$-edge, $4$-face finite subshape inside the codon-coordinate carrier.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The primary finite-row significance test used $N=2000$ sampled comparators with seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported sample is not a casual draw from a large unconstrained space; it is a finite distinct-sample experiment with many rejected repeats.  In that experiment there was exactly $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ matches at Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row therefore supports a code-layer claim of local shape significance: within the tested finite comparator family, the observed $M$-shape is rare as an exact shape and remains uncommon under the high-Jaccard relaxation.  The observed $M$ size available in the finite-row payload is $20$, and the amino-acid-class-preserving null reports no exact shape matches in $2000$ trials, with lower-tail count $7$ at observed size $20$.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model sharpens the boundary.  With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-match count is $0$, so the empirical exact-shape value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at the observed $M$ size is $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This does not say that codon biology is forced by the geometry.  It says that, after preserving amino-acid class information in the tested null, the observed local tile remains statistically exceptional at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite data make the following chapter-level claim ready: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically significant local boundary-tile structure for standard-code label readback.  The claim is specifically a code-read claim.  The curated standard-code table is a reality bridge for codon labels, not a mechanism, not a universality proof, and not a structure experiment.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure.  No translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, biological mechanism, or universal law follows from this chapter unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that evidence.
