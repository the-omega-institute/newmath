\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Significance}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table read through a finite window-six carrier.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, where a codon is treated as a six-bit display rather than as a chemical mechanism.  The BEDC-side distinctions used here are a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The reality contact is narrower: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  Thus the contact tests code-layer label readback and local split structure only.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified codon list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is verified as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the concrete local tile boundary extracted from the code-read layer.  The point of the row is not that biology must obey the cube geometry; the point is that the curated label table, when read through $Q_6$, exposes this particular finite boundary pattern.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ finite random samples with seed $42$.  Producing the distinct sample set required $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Against this null, the exact observed shape appeared once, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap comparison, using Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, produced $25$ matches, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers are finite-row frequencies, not asymptotic biological laws.  They say that the standard-code tile is uncommon under the sampled coordinate null and remains uncommon even under a high-Jaccard relaxation.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A stricter label-aware null preserved amino-acid class information across $N=2000$ samples, again with seed $42$.  In that null the observed tile size was $20$, exact shape matches were $0$, and only $7$ samples landed in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The corresponding finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the local readback claim: the observed code-layer shape is not merely rare among arbitrary samples, but remains rare when the comparison is constrained by amino-acid class.  The record still stays at the label layer; the preserved classes are part of a table-read null model, not a statement about protein folding or function.
