\subsection{Window-six local tile significance for standard-code labels}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
This record studies the standard-code codon table through a six-bit codon display. The carrier is the finite codon cube $Q_6$, and the observable readout is the curated assignment of each codon to an amino-acid or stop label in the standard code. The claim is deliberately local: the window-six coordinate system may expose a boundary-tile pattern in the label table, but the labels themselves enter through the curated code contact. The table contact is therefore a code-layer observation, not a mechanism of translation and not an observation of folded structure.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished residual row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In the finite run it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube boundary with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This gives a finite combinatorial signature for the local tile: thirteen visible codon vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces. The row is not interpreted as a universal biological law. It is a finite shape extracted from the standard-code readout under the window-six coordinate convention.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinate check.}
The associated spectrum gives the numerical values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is also the reported value of the cubic-polynomial check. This is numerically consistent with a finite-coordinate spectral calculation at the stated tolerance. The spectral row is used only as an internal numerical companion to the shape row; it does not by itself assert biochemical feasibility, translation dynamics, or biological function.

\paragraph{Shape significance against random label arrangements.}
For claim $h0.M.shape.significance$, the Monte Carlo row used seed $42$ and sampled $N=2000$ distinct comparison tables, requiring $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact local-shape match count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} = 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The number of samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row supports a strong local-significance statement for the observed window-six shape under the stated randomization protocol. The statement remains a code-read statement: it says that the observed local tile is rare in the sampled label arrangements, not that codons physically implement this geometry as a law of biology.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second null row preserves amino-acid class. It again uses $N=2000$ and seed $42$. The observed $M$-size is $20$. The exact-shape count is $0$, so the recorded exact-shape value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the finite evidence that the observed local tile is not a routine artifact of preserving broad amino-acid class labels. The row still has the same scope limit: it compares code-label arrangements and does not measure translation machinery, tRNA availability, structural folding, physical admissibility, or organism-level function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. The reported hit rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family. These values show that the local code-read pattern has predictive stability under this finite leave-one-code-out stress test, while remaining tied to label readback rather than to a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row and modules.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ codons with high closure coverage, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$. The module classification assigns all $13$ members of $R$, with no extra module codons, no missing module codons, and no unassigned codons. The recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite run. This supports the view that the residual codon row is organized rather than arbitrary under the coordinate-and-label test.

\paragraph{Local negative boundary.}
The result licenses a narrow claim: the standard-code table, read through a six-bit codon coordinate display, contains a statistically unusual local boundary-tile pattern with a thirteen-codon residual row and stable finite module classification. Nothing in this chapter establishes that DNA or RNA must obey the same geometry as a biological necessity. Nothing here proves translation realization, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, organismal function, a universal mechanism, or a general law across all biological contexts. Any such stronger statement would require an additional contact that directly observes the corresponding layer. In the present record, the only contact is the curated standard-code label table, so the conclusion remains a finite code-read conclusion about codon labels and local tile structure.
