\subsection{Window-six local shape significance for the standard-code table}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite coordinate display for the $64$ codons of the standard genetic code. The reality contact used here is only the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop, and it is therefore a code-layer contact rather than a translation, structure, physics, function, mechanism, or universality contact. The BEDC object tested in this row is the local boundary-tile shape exposed by the window-six coordinates after the curated labels are read back onto $Q_6$. The claim is not that DNA is governed by this geometry as biological necessity. The claim is narrower: the observed standard-code label table has a finite local tile whose shape is rare under the stated finite nulls.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. The observed row gives the exact codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row identifies this $R$ with the punctured-cube shape detected by the coordinate closure, with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a count of codons; it is a finite combinatorial tile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces inside the codon cube display.

\paragraph{Shape-significance run.}
The primary shape-significance experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ finite samples. Producing those $2000$ distinct samples required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space was sampled with substantial collision pressure. Against this null, the exact observed shape occurred once:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\} &= 1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
At the weaker high-overlap threshold, there were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\} &= 25,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These are finite-row frequencies, not asymptotic claims. They say that the standard-code local shape is rare in this sampled coordinate null, both exactly and under a near-shape criterion.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null.}
A second null preserves amino-acid class structure. In this row the observed $M$ size is $20$. With $N=2000$ samples and the same seed $42$, the exact class-preserving shape match count was $0$, so the reported exact-shape value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the same local reading under a null that preserves more label information: the tile is not explained away merely by retaining amino-acid class counts.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables with $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$. Thus, within this finite test, the local tile-plus-boundary description recovers almost all held-out code-layer targets and greatly exceeds the baseline. This is a code-read recovery statement only: it is not evidence for realized translation dynamics, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal genetic-code mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports no anchor codons, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases. Its recovery rate is $1.0$, with the same value reported above the decision threshold. This row is compatible with the local boundary interpretation: codon-level omissions are recovered through predictable or boundary behavior inside the coordinate object. Since the contact remains the curated label table, this recovery does not observe any downstream molecule or phenotype.

The code-family row contains $5$ families. \paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite-row verdict is that the standard-code table exposes a rare window-six local tile: exact shape frequency $0.0005$, high-Jaccard frequency $0.0125$, class-preserving exact frequency $0.0$, and class-preserving lower-tail frequency $0.0035$, with coherent boundary, spectrum, leave-one-out, family, and module rows. The permissible biological reading is exactly the code-layer reading: curated codon labels, placed on $Q_6$, produce a significant local boundary-tile pattern. Nothing in this contact supplies translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity. Any such stronger claim requires a separate reality contact designed to observe that higher layer.
