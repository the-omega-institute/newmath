\subsection{Window-six local tile significance for the standard-code table}\label{sec:bior-reality-h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The record is a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is read as a coordinate in the cube, while the biological label is supplied only by the curated standard-code table.  Thus the internal BEDC-side data are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data on a finite carrier, and the readback side is the standard-code codon-to-label table.  The contact can test a local codon-tile label split at the code layer.  It cannot by itself test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey this geometry as biological necessity.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube boundary with finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the concrete finite tile under discussion: thirteen codons, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the induced local boundary complex.  The row is not asserted to be a biological law.  It is a finite coordinate-and-label pattern whose labels are read from the standard genetic-code contact.

\paragraph{Monte Carlo shape significance.}
For the shape test, the run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ distinct sampled comparison rows.  Obtaining those $2000$ distinct rows required $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space was visibly collision-heavy under the sampling procedure.  Against this finite null, the exact local shape appeared once:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\hbox{exact shape matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\hbox{ matches}\}&=25,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed $M$-row size in the amino-acid-class-preserving null is $20$.  In that null, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, there were no exact shape matches and only $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\hbox{aa-class exact matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
\#\{|M_{\mathrm{sample}}|\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row claim of local shape significance for the code-read table.  They do not promote the tile to translation, folding, function, physical admissibility, mechanism, or universality.

\begin{aligned}

The spectral row is therefore internally consistent with the finite coordinate computation.  It remains a code-layer invariant of the displayed table, not a statement about protein structure or biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out probe recorded no anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, and recovery rate $1.0$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\hbox{anchors}&=0,\\
\hbox{boundary cases}&=5,\\
\hbox{predictable cases}&=8,\\
\hbox{recovery rate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
These probes say that the local tile is useful for code-read recovery checks under the supplied finite contacts.  They do not say that the recovered label sequence is a folded protein, a physically admissible molecule, or a realized function.
