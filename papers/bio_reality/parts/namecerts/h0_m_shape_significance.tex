\subsection{Window-Six Local Tile Significance for the Standard-Code Table}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard genetic-code table as a codon-to-label contact. The contact is deliberately narrow: it supplies the code-layer labels for codons, including amino-acid and stop labels, but it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. In this chapter the relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class. The internal structure used by the row is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation, and the readback is only the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its codons are exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also recorded as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the local tile is not being inferred from an informal biological description; it is a finite row in $Q_6$ with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces under the chosen boundary relation. This is the code-read object whose label split is tested against the curated table.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
For the main shape test, the experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ accepted null samples. Obtaining those $2000$ distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts, of which $55377$ were duplicate retries. The observed local shape had exactly $1$ exact match among the $2000$ accepted null rows, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-Jaccard count was $25$ among the same $2000$ samples. These numbers support a finite-row claim about the rarity of the observed $M$-shape under the stated sampling procedure. They do not, by themselves, promote the tile to a law of biology.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class constraints. In that run, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$-size was $20$. The exact shape count was $0$, so the class-preserving exact-shape empirical value was $0.0$. The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the row-level reading: the observed local shape remains uncommon even when the null model respects amino-acid-class structure. The statement remains a significance statement about a finite code-read table, not a statement about translation realization or structural biology.

It is not an eigenvalue claim about a molecular mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one readbacks.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, while the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$. Separately, the leave-one-codon-out probe recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These probes say that the local code-read geometry has predictive contact with held-out codon-label rows under the stated finite protocol.

The family-stratified leave-one-code-out row contains $5$ families. The hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family. The module classification assigns all $13$ boundary codons, with $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing module codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are

\begin{aligned}

Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite row. This module accounting is important because the row is not merely a small $p$-value: it also has a complete internal assignment of the $13$ boundary codons.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is local and code-layered. Window-six coordinates expose a statistically uncommon local tile and a complete boundary-module accounting when read against the curated standard-code table. The contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks. It does not show that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity, and it does not imply translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Any claim across those boundaries requires a separate reality contact that directly observes the higher layer.
