\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The primary shape-significance row used $N=2000$ distinct sampled comparators with seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, which is important because the observed finite tile is being compared against a constrained discrete space rather than a continuous distribution.  Among the $2000$ sampled rows, there was exactly one exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
\#\{\hbox{exact shape matches}\}&=1,\\
\#\{\hbox{Jaccard}\geq 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row verdict is therefore not that the codon table is forced by geometry.  The narrower statement is that the observed standard-code label split occupies a rare local-tile shape under this sampling regime: exact recovery appears in $1/2000$ sampled rows, while high-Jaccard recovery appears in $25/2000$ rows.
