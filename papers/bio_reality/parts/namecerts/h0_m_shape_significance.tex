\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Significance}
\label{sec:bioreality-codon-window-six-local-tile-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display rather than as a biochemical mechanism.  A row is a codon address together with the curated standard-code label assigned to that address.  The contact used here is only the curated standard genetic-code table: it supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels at code-read resolution.  It does not supply translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  The BEDC-minimal form therefore has four visible distinctions: the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  Its internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation, with readback to the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the observed standard-code table the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube tile with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This says that the contact exposes a finite local shape in the codon cube, not that biology is forced by that shape.  The claim is local: the row is a code-layer pattern in $Q_6$ whose labels are supplied by an external table.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The observed $M$-shape was tested against $N=2000$ sampled finite rows with seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported tail values should be read as finite empirical null measurements for this sampling regime.  The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125,\\
\#\{\text{exact matches}\}&=1,\\
\#\{J\geq 0.9\text{ matches}\}&=25.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local codon-tile shape is rare under the sampled null, both for exact equality and for high-Jaccard similarity.  This is a shape-significance statement about a finite code-read row.  It is not a statement that the row implements translation, predicts folded protein structure, or explains biological function.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null preserved amino-acid class information while resampling the finite table.  In that run $N=2000$, the observed $M$-row size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The reported values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact value $0.0$ here means zero observed exact matches among the $2000$ sampled rows, not a mathematical impossibility theorem.  The lower-tail value $0.0035$ says that the observed size is also low in the class-preserving null.  Together with the exact-shape run, this supports a code-layer finite-row anomaly, while still leaving all higher biological claims outside the contact.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite evidence supports the following chapter-level claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically rare, spectrally coherent, locally stable boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code label table.  The claim is restricted to $Q_6$ coordinate readback and codon-label relations.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from these rows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that information.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not protein-structure readback.  The local tile is significant as a code-read finite row, and it remains silent about every layer above that contact.
