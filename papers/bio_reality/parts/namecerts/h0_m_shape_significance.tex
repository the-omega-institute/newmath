\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Shape Significance}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed label contact.}
The claim \texttt{h0.M.shape.significance} concerns the standard-code codon table after it is read as a finite label assignment on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$. Each codon is treated as a six-bit display point, while the curated standard code supplies the amino-acid or stop label attached to that point. The mathematical object tested here is therefore not translation itself, but a finite code-layer pattern: local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes inside the codon cube. The observed table gives the only biological contact used in this chapter. It permits codon-label readback and local split checks; it does not observe ribosomal realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has exactly thirteen codons. In the verified run \texttt{5d4a3459997d}, the set was
$R=\{\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}$, so $|R|=13$. The companion punctured-cube check, run \texttt{e19917012c60}, found the same codon list and the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This gives a concrete finite shape rather than a verbal resemblance: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces are present in the punctured-cube boundary geometry. The observed module partition also accounts for every member of $R$: \texttt{AGA}, \texttt{AGG} form the \texttt{agr} module; \texttt{AAA} forms \texttt{ar\_lys}; \texttt{CUA}, \texttt{CUC}, \texttt{CUG}, \texttt{CUU} form \texttt{cun}; \texttt{AUA} forms \texttt{ile\_met}; \texttt{UCA}, \texttt{UUA} form \texttt{ssn}; and \texttt{UAA}, \texttt{UAG}, \texttt{UGA} form \texttt{stop\_trp}. The module audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance test.}
The primary finite-row experiment for \texttt{h0.M.shape.significance} used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison instances. The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported tail counts are attached to a high-collision finite sampling regime rather than to an idealized continuum model. The exact observed shape appeared once among the $2000$ samples, and the high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ appeared twenty-five times. Thus the finite empirical probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers are small on the stated null ensemble. The exact-shape result says that the observed local tile is rare as a whole finite pattern; the high-Jaccard result says that near-copies are also uncommon, though less rare than exact copies. The chapter therefore records a code-layer shape signal, not a claim that geometry forces biology.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information and asks whether the same local shape persists under that stronger label-aware constraint. In run \texttt{e8d5de747a0c}, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$-size was $20$. The exact-shape count was $0$, giving $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$ at this finite resolution. The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the same conclusion from a different finite comparison: the window-six tile is not a common artifact of merely preserving amino-acid class labels. The result remains a statement about the standard-code label table and its finite coordinate presentation.

\paragraph{Spectral readout.}
The shape also carries a numerical spectrum. The verified spectral computation \texttt{1026324eff41} reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The cubic residual and the radial residual were both $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, within numerical tolerance for the recorded computation. The spectral radius attached to the $M$-side signal is therefore the concrete value $0.6752479308830213$, not an unspecified qualitative marker. These numbers are useful because they make the local tile reproducible as finite data: the set $R$, its face vector, the observed $M$-size, the tail probabilities, and the spectral values can all be checked independently.

\paragraph{Predictive probes at the code layer.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, across $5$ families. The leave-one-codon-out probe found $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These probes give finite evidence that the local coordinate geometry has code-read predictive content. They do not supply a separate contact for physical translation, molecular structure, organismal function, or evolutionary necessity.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and blocked promotions.}
The verified finite rows make the following chapter-level statement reasonable: in the standard genetic code, the window-six codon-coordinate presentation exposes a rare local tile shape, with exact null frequency $0.0005$, high-overlap frequency $0.0125$, amino-acid-class preserving lower-tail frequency $0.0035$, a thirteen-codon boundary with face vector $(13,16,4)$, complete module assignment of the boundary codons, and strong leave-one-out recovery at the code-label layer. The statement stops there. No conclusion in this chapter says that DNA is governed by the coordinate geometry as a biological law; no conclusion says that an amino-acid label sequence is a folded protein; and no conclusion says that the tile proves translation realization, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Any such stronger assertion would require an additional observation of the relevant higher-layer object, separate from the curated codon-to-label table used here.
