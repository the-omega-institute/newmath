\subsection{Window-six local tile significance}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the coordinate display of the $64$ standard codons.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and that table supplies the label readback used in this subsection.  The contact is not a translation experiment, not a folding experiment, not a physical-admissibility measurement, and not a function assay.  Thus the object under test is a code-layer shape: whether the window-six coordinate display exposes a local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code labels.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the following finite codon set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the concrete local tile boundary: a thirteen-codon subset of the codon cube whose incidence data contain $13$ vertices, $16$ local edges, and $4$ local faces.  The row is not being promoted to a global law for DNA.  It is a finite coordinate witness attached to the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The primary finite-row significance test sampled $N=2000$ distinct randomized rows with seed $42$.  Because the sampled rows were required to be distinct, the run made $57377$ sample attempts and rejected $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the $2000$ accepted rows, exactly one matched the observed shape, and $25$ reached Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$ with the observed row.  The resulting empirical rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row conclusion is therefore not that the codon table is forced by geometry, but that this particular window-six local shape is rare under the recorded sampling protocol.  The exact-shape event occurs once in $2000$ accepted rows, while the high-overlap event occurs $25$ times in $2000$ accepted rows.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class information.  In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-row size was $20$.  The exact observed shape appeared $0$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This null is stronger than a purely label-blind shuffle because it asks whether the shape persists after preserving a coarse amino-acid-class constraint.  The recorded outcome says that the exact shape is absent from the $2000$ accepted class-preserving trials, and that rows no larger than the observed $M$-row are also rare in that run.

\paragraph{Spectral row check.}
The same finite object has a recorded spectral profile.  The $M$ and $R$ row parameters were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check returned
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is numerically zero at the recorded tolerance.  The spectral radius value recorded for the $M$ side was again $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers function only as internal finite-row diagnostics.  They do not turn the local tile into a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The code-table perturbation check evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  Broken out by family across $5$ families, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is still a code-read result.  It says that the local row and its boundary recover many code-table perturbation targets relative to the baseline rate; it does not say that the coordinate geometry implements translation.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row modules.}
The leave-one-codon-out check found $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification accounted for all $13$ codons in $R$, with assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has either a predictable or boundary status in the recorded finite-row classification.  This supplies a local decomposition of the thirteen-codon boundary row, not a protein-structure decomposition and not a claim about biological function.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The conclusion of this record is intentionally scoped.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a rare local boundary-tile pattern against the stated finite nulls, and curated standard-code labels allow that pattern to be read back at the code layer.  No translation realization follows.  No folded structure follows.  No physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity claim follows.  Any such promotion would require a separate reality contact with its own observed rows, noise model, and failure conditions.
