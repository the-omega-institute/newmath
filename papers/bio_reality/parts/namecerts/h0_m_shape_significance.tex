\subsection{Window-six local tile significance for the standard code}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record treats the standard genetic-code table as a finite code-read contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The carrier is the codon display with $64$ codon positions, and the observable readback is the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels. The BEDC-side distinctions used here are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data; the biological contact is only the standard-code label table. Thus the claim is not that the genetic code is caused by window-six geometry, but that the standard-code labels determine a concrete local tile whose shape can be tested against finite null samples.

\paragraph{The punctured-cube row.}
The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its observed codon members are
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same finite row is reported as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
This gives the local tile a concrete combinatorial boundary: $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the row-level complex. The row is therefore not merely a label collection; it is a finite subset of $Q_6$ with a specified face profile.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
For the primary shape-significance test, the finite sampler used seed $42$ and evaluated $N=2000$ distinct sampled rows. Because many draws collided with earlier samples, the run recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. Exactly one sampled row matched the observed shape exactly, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}$$
The high-overlap test counted $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
These are finite Monte Carlo row facts about the observed code-read tile. They support the statement that the standard-code tile shape is uncommon under the sampled null, with exact-shape frequency $1/2000$ and high-Jaccard frequency $25/2000$.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A stricter null model preserving amino-acid class structure was also sampled with seed $42$ and $N=2000$. In that row, the observed $M$ size is $20$, no exact shape match occurred, and $7$ sampled rows landed in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size. The reported finite values are
$$\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
\#\{\mathrm{lower\ tail}\leq |M|\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
This strengthens the local reading: even when label-class information is preserved by the null construction, the observed local tile remains rare in the sampled finite population. The explicit member list of $M$ is not present in the finite row supplied here, so this paragraph uses the reported scalar $|M|=20$ rather than naming all members of $M$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. These numbers show that, at the code-read layer, the local tile and its boundary carry strong finite recovery signal relative to the baseline.

The leave-one-codon-out row gives $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$. This is again a local code-table recovery statement. It says that the finite row classifies the held-out codon through predictable or boundary behavior in this coordinate system; it does not say that a molecular process has been reconstructed.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported conclusion is local and finite: window-six coordinates expose a statistically uncommon codon-label tile in the curated standard-code table, with a punctured-cube row of size $13$, a reported $M$ size of $20$, exact-shape frequency $0.0005$, high-Jaccard frequency $0.0125$, class-preserving lower-tail frequency $0.0035$, and complete module accounting for the $13$ codons in $R$. No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from these rows. Amino-acid label readback remains code-layer readback unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies structure, physics, function, or mechanism.
