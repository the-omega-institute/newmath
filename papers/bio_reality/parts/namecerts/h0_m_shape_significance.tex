\paragraph{Shape significance test.}
The primary finite-row significance test sampled $N=2000$ distinct null shapes with seed $42$. Because the sampling was distinct-shape sampling rather than raw independent drawing, the run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and rejected $55377$ duplicate retries. Among the $2000$ accepted samples, exactly one matched the observed shape exactly, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-Jaccard count was $25$, so the observed tile is also rare under the relaxed overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$. These numbers support the finite claim that the observed standard-code label table occupies an unusual local shape in the six-bit coordinate display. They do not by themselves identify a causal source for the shape.
