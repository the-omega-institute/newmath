\subsection{Window-six local codon tile significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite-row object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with the usual finite codon display serving as the external label bridge.  The BEDC-side distinctions are the coordinate of a codon in the window-six cube, the local tile face selected by the finite construction, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class.  The biological object is therefore not translation or protein structure; it is only the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The observed finite row asks whether the local shape $M$ is unusually specific relative to finite label-preserving and coordinate-derived nulls.

\paragraph{Boundary subset.}
The boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified finite table it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row verifies that this set is a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the finite object being tested is not a vague visual analogy: it is a concrete thirteen-codon subset of $Q_6$ with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces in the recorded local complex.  The observed $M$ row has size $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment, while the explicit membership list for $M$ is not part of this finite-row packet.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
For claim $h0.M.shape.significance$, the finite randomization used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ distinct samples.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported empirical probabilities are tied to a finite distinct-sample procedure rather than to an unrestricted independent draw model.  Among the $2000$ accepted samples there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$.  The empirical values are therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 1/2000 = 0.0005,\\
 p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 25/2000 = 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both values are small at the finite-row scale.  The exact-shape number supports the claim that the observed $M$ shape is rare under this particular finite coordinate-derived comparison, while the high-Jaccard number records that near matches are more common but still occur in only $1.25\%$ of accepted samples.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second null keeps the amino-acid class constraint explicit.  With the same seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the experiment found $0$ exact matches to the observed shape and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size $20$.  The corresponding empirical values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}} &= 0/2000 = 0.0,\\
 p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}} &= 7/2000 = 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the finite-row reading: even after preserving the amino-acid class structure available to the null, the observed code-layer shape is not reproduced in the $2000$ accepted samples, and equally small-or-smaller shapes occur in only $0.35\%$ of the sample set.  The conclusion is still empirical and finite; it is not a proof over all possible genetic codes.

This is a coordinate-spectrum fact about the local codon tile, not a dynamical claim about ribosomes, chemistry, or selection.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the row reports $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial/plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  These numbers say that the local code-read tile has strong finite leave-one-code-out recovery behavior across the sampled table families, but the tested object remains the codon-label table.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, resolved at codon-label readback.  It supports the fact that codons receive amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code, and it permits local split checks against those labels.  It does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Consequently the admissible conclusion is narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically rare local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table, with strong finite leave-one-out behavior and a complete thirteen-codon module classification.  No claim follows that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological law, that the tile is a universal mechanism, or that amino-acid label-sequence readback determines folded structure or function.  Any promotion across those boundaries requires a separate higher-layer reality contact.
