\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Significance}
\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display whose external contact is the curated standard-code table.  The contact assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it is used here only as a code-layer readback.  The BEDC minimal form has four distinctions: a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  Its internal structure records coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  This chapter therefore studies a local boundary-tile phenomenon inside the standard-code label table, not a claim about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the standard codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also verified as the punctured-cube row, with finite face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The module decomposition of this row is complete: all $13$ codons are assigned, with no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\qquad
\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},
\qquad \mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\

\qquad \mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite local shape whose significance is tested against randomized alternatives.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
For the shape test with seed $42$, the experiment used $N=2000$ distinct randomized samples.  Obtaining those samples required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the sampling space is being probed with substantial collision pressure.  The exact shape of the observed tile appeared once among the $2000$ samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ shape\ matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap shape agreement, measured by Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\ge 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row supports a code-layer statement: the observed window-six local tile is rare under the sampled nulls, both at exact shape and at high Jaccard overlap.  The statement is deliberately local.  It says that the standard-code labels expose a boundary-tile regularity in the six-bit coordinate display; it does not say that the geometry is a biological law.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In this test, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$-size was $20$.  The exact class-preserving shape match count was $0$, giving reported exact-shape value
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{class\ preserving\ exact\ matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{lower\ tail}\le 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the local reading: even when amino-acid class structure is preserved by the null, the observed row remains unusually constrained.  The finite evidence is still a code-read observation, because the only external contact here is the curated codon-to-label table.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback tests.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  The leave-one-codon-out row records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These finite recovery facts show that the local boundary is not merely a static row description: it also supports code-layer recovery under leave-one perturbations.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded conclusion.  In the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, the standard-code label table exposes a rare local boundary tile: $R$ has $13$ codons, the punctured-cube face-vector is $(13,16,4)$, the exact randomized shape probability is $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard probability is $0.0125$, and the amino-acid-class preserving lower-tail probability is $0.0035$.  The leave-one tests show strong code-read recovery, with $0.9841269841269841$ hit rate above baseline for leave-one-code-out and $1.0$ recovery rate for leave-one-codon-out.  The corresponding spectrum is fixed by $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  This is enough for a local code-read claim about the curated standard-code table.  It is not enough for any claim about translation realization, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such stronger claim would require a separate reality contact whose observed object is at that higher layer.
