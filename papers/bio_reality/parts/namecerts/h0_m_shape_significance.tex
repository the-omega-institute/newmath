\subsection{Window-six Codon Tile Significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite reality contact for label readback on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the codon display in which a codon is read as a finite coordinate and then compared with the curated standard-code label table.  The admissible contact is therefore narrow: the table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and the experiment may test only code-layer statements about local tile structure, label splits, and boundary behavior.  It may not promote a codon-tile pattern to translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity without a separate contact for that layer.

\paragraph{Shape significance under finite null sampling.}
For the shape-significance row, the experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled rows.  Because distinct samples were required, the run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap comparison found $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed local shape is sparse in this finite null ensemble both at exact shape level and at the high-Jaccard level.  The result is a finite-row significance statement about the window-six coordinate geometry and the curated label split; it is not evidence that DNA must obey this geometry as a physical or functional law.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The verified finite rows support the claim that window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically sparse local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table.  The exact-shape value $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard value $0.0125$, the amino-acid-class lower-tail value $0.0035$, the complete $13$-codon module assignment, and the leave-one-code-out hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ all point in the same code-read direction.  The result is local and layer-bound.  No translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, universality, or global biological law follows from this chapter.  Any such statement requires a separate reality contact beyond the curated standard-code table.
