\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record studies the standard genetic code only at the level of codon-label readback.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube obtained by displaying each codon as a finite word over the four nucleotide symbols and then reading the local window-six coordinate pattern.  The biological contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each of the $64$ codons to an amino-acid or stop label.  This contact is sufficient for checking label rows, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  It is not a contact with translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, molecular function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape-frequency test.}
The null experiment for the shape statistic used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ accepted samples.  Because the sampler required distinct finite samples, it made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the $2000$ accepted samples, exactly $1$ matched the observed shape exactly, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold counted $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support a finite significance statement for the local tile shape: within this null ensemble, the observed codon-row shape is rare both under exact matching and under the high-Jaccard relaxation.  The statement is deliberately local.  It says that a window-six coordinate split has a statistically unusual finite shape relative to the sampled null model; it does not say that biological translation is caused by the coordinate geometry.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second null keeps amino-acid class information fixed while randomizing within the permitted finite scheme.  In this test the observed $M$-side size is $20$.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the exact-match count is $0$, so the empirical exact-shape value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving result is important because it does not merely compare against arbitrary codon relabelings.  It asks whether the observed size and shape remain rare when a biologically meaningful label-class constraint is kept in place.  The answer, in this finite run, is yes: the lower-tail event remains small, and the exact shape is not seen among the accepted samples.

\paragraph{Scientific boundary.}
The chapter therefore records a narrow result.  A curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label observations; window-six coordinates expose a rare local boundary-tile pattern; the row $R$ has a concrete punctured-cube signature; the spectral numbers and leave-one probes agree with that finite organization.  Nothing in these rows observes ribosomal realization, folding, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, evolutionary mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Any claim crossing from codon-label readback into those layers would require a separate contact of the appropriate kind.  Until such a contact is supplied, the result remains a code-layer finite-shape finding.
