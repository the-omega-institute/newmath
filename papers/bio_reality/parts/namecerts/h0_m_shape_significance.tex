\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The record is a finite code-read claim over the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is read as a six-bit display, with the biological labels supplied by a curated standard-code table.  This contact is only a codon-to-label bridge: it says which amino-acid or stop label is attached to each codon in the standard code, and it does not by itself observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The BEDC object tested here is therefore the local boundary geometry induced by the code-label readback, not a global biological law.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube shape with finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local code-read pattern is not merely a list of exceptional labels; it carries a concrete tile boundary with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-dimensional faces in the window-six coordinate display.

\paragraph{Shape-significance experiment.}
The shape-significance probe sampled $N=2000$ finite rows using seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, indicating that the null generator was repeatedly meeting already-seen finite rows before the target sample size was reached.  In this sample there was exactly one exact shape match to the observed $M$-shape, and there were $25$ rows with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$.  The empirical tail values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers are code-layer finite-row observations.  They support the claim that the observed local $M$ shape is scarce under the tested row model, while leaving all higher-layer biological interpretations outside the present contact.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class constraints and again used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The observed $M$ size was $20$.  Under this class-preserving null there were $0$ exact shape matches, and only $7$ sampled rows lay in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\text{-}class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\text{-}class,lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is stronger than a label-blind scarcity statement: even when the null keeps amino-acid-class information available, the exact local shape is not recovered in the sampled rows, and the observed size remains in a small lower tail.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and module coverage.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate $1.0$ and recovery rate above threshold $1.0$.  The thirteen row codons are completely assigned to six named modules:

The module-classification audit has $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has either predictable or boundary status.  This supplies a finite, exhaustively checked decomposition of the row into code-read modules.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite evidence supports a narrow verdict: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically scarce local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table.  The exact-shape value $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard value $0.0125$, the amino-acid-class exact value $0.0$, the amino-acid-class lower-tail value $0.0035$, the complete thirteen-codon module assignment, and the high leave-one-code-out rates all point to a real code-layer regularity.  The result remains local.  It does not claim that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as biological necessity, does not claim translation realization, does not claim folded protein structure, does not claim physical admissibility, does not claim biological function, does not claim mechanism, and does not claim universality.  Any move beyond codon-label readback requires a separate reality contact at the corresponding layer.
