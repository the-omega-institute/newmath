\subsection{Window-six local tile significance for the standard-code table}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim and finite object.}
This record concerns the claim identifier \texttt{h0.M.shape.significance}.  The object under test is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer: a curated assignment of the sixty-four codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The internal carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with distinctions for a six-bit display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The construction uses coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data, and its readback is the standard-code label table.  The informal claim is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically unusual local boundary-tile pattern, while the labels themselves remain supplied by the curated code table.

\paragraph{Boundary subset.}
The row named \texttt{h0.R.cardinality.13} verifies that the boundary set $R$ has size $13$.  The codons in $R$ are
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The companion row \texttt{h0.R.punctured.cube} reports the same thirteen codons and gives the finite face-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the local object is not treated as an unrestricted subset of the codon cube.  It is a punctured-cube tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces.  These numbers are part of the coordinate evidence, not a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The row \texttt{h0.M.shape.significance} compares the observed $M$-shape against $2000$ distinct sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  Producing the distinct sample required $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite null comparison is heavily constrained by collisions in the sampled shape space.  The exact observed shape appeared once among the $2000$ sampled alternatives, while $25$ sampled alternatives had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ to the observed shape.  The recorded probabilities are
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
The same row records these as \texttt{p\_value\_exact\_shape\_significant}$=0.0005$ and \texttt{p\_value\_high\_jaccard\_significant}$=0.0125$.  The result supports a local statistical anomaly of the observed tile shape.  It does not by itself identify a causal origin for the genetic code, nor does it turn codon geometry into a global law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null row, \texttt{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving}, keeps amino-acid class information in the sampling design.  In that comparison $N=2000$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact match count is $0$, and only $7$ sampled rows fall in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The recorded values are
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
The finite statement is therefore stronger than a purely label-blind coincidence test: even under an amino-acid-class preserving comparison, the observed local configuration remains rare in the sampled row set.  The zero exact-match value is an empirical count in a sample of size $2000$, not a proof that exact matches are impossible.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The spectral row \texttt{h0.spectrum} records the numerical values
$$\begin{aligned}

\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}$$
The same run reports a radial polynomial value of $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, equal to the listed cubic-polynomial residual within numerical tolerance.  The spectral radius entry is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$.  These figures give a quantitative coordinate check on the local tile: the observed $M$ and $R$ geometry has a stable numerical signature, with the residual small enough to be interpreted as floating-point agreement for the recorded cubic relation.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The row \texttt{h1.leave.one.code.out} evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, while the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The recorded hit rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, \texttt{h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family} reports five families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, and bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$.  This supports the claim that the window-six local tile is predictive for code-label readback under this finite leave-one-code-out protocol.

Every listed module has a predictable or boundary role in the finite classification.  This is a local code-layer organization result: it says that the thirteen-codon boundary decomposes cleanly into the six recorded modules for the standard-code readback task.

\paragraph{Permitted conclusion.}
The finite rows jointly support the following code-layer conclusion.  In the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, the standard-code label table induces a local boundary tile whose $R$ subset has thirteen codons, face-vector $(13,16,4)$, rare sampled $M$-shape values, a stable spectral signature, strong leave-one-code-out readback rates, and complete leave-one-codon-out module assignment.  The strongest numerical evidence is the exact-shape sample probability $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard sample probability $0.0125$, the amino-acid-class lower-tail probability $0.0035$, and the $0.9841269841269841$ leave-one-code-out hit rate above baseline.

\paragraph{Local non-promotion rule.}
No higher biological layer follows from these rows alone.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label readback; it does not observe translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality across all biological contexts.  Amino-acid label sequences are not folded structures.  Cross-organism wording, tRNA-Leu notation, or a CUN/UUR sign pattern would need an organism-matched contact of its own before it could be used as evidence for structure or function.  Therefore the present claim is ready only as a finite code-read result: a statistically and structurally distinguished local codon tile, bounded to the label table that supplies the observed rows.
