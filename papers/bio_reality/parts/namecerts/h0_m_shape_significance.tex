\subsection{Window-six codon shape significance}\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table.  The contact is deliberately narrow: it supplies codon-to-label readback for the standard genetic code, with labels consisting of amino-acid names and stop labels.  It does not supply translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of origin, or a universality theorem.  Within this scope, the window-six coordinate display exposes a local tile boundary: a singleton label corner, a three-point label class, local tile faces, and a residual punctured cube.  The claim tested here is not that biology must obey this geometry, but that the curated code labels land in an unusually specific finite shape relative to code-label-preserving probes.

\paragraph{Residual punctured cube.}
The residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same set is recorded by the punctured-cube audit, with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not a free verbal partition of the codon table.  It has a concrete finite support, a concrete list of codons, and a concrete two-dimensional cell count.  The observed $R$ should be read as the boundary-side carrier left after the window-six tile split, not as a separate biological subsystem.

\paragraph{Monte Carlo shape significance.}
For the finite-row shape audit, the experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ accepted distinct samples.  Achieving those $2000$ distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape occurred once:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A relaxed high-overlap test at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ found $25$ matches:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\ge 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers keep the interpretation at the code-read layer.  They say that the displayed local shape is uncommon under the sampled finite null, not that the shape causes translation, folding, admissibility, function, or any universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class constraints.  In that audit the observed $M$ size is $20$, with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$.  The exact shape count is zero:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{class-preserving exact matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{lower-tail samples}\le |M|\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the stronger finite-row guard against a purely class-size explanation: even when the sampled alternatives preserve the amino-acid class frame, the observed shape and size remain rare in the recorded sample.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite-row verdict is that the standard-code label table supports a non-generic window-six local tile boundary.  The evidence is the exact $13$-codon residual set, the punctured-cube face-vector $(13,16,4)$, the $N=2000$ shape significance values $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$ and $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$, the amino-acid-class preserving values $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$, the spectral readback $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and the leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out recovery rows.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the statement: no translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this local codon tile unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that information.
