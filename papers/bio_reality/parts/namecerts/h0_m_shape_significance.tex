\subsection{Window-six codon tile significance}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Coordinate object.}
The finite object used here is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$, whose vertices are the sixty-four codon displays obtained from three nucleotide positions with two binary coordinates per position.  The biological contact is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the curated standard-code label table, used only as a codon-to-label readback at the code layer.  Within this finite display, the distinguished remainder set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same thirteen codons form the punctured-cube row whose face-vector was observed as $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the row is not merely a count: it carries a local face structure with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-dimensional faces inside the codon-coordinate display.

\paragraph{Observed shape row.}
The shape statistic for the $M$ row was tested against $2000$ distinct sampled comparators, drawn with seed $42$.  Obtaining those $2000$ distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  The observed exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
where $25$ sampled rows reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The exact-shape and high-Jaccard rows therefore both mark a small finite tail in this sampling design.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact match count was $0$, while $7$ sampled rows lay in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size.  The observed $M$ size was $20$, with null values
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite local-tile anomaly at the code-label level.  They do not identify an evolutionary cause, a translational operation, a folded structure, or a physical admissibility rule.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated spectral measurements give two smaller radii and one larger radial parameter:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic residual was $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the radial polynomial check, so the finite numerical row satisfies its polynomial constraint to the stated tolerance.  In this chapter the spectral numbers are used only as invariants of the finite coordinate calculation.  They are not promoted to a biological energy, force, dynamics, or universality claim.

\paragraph{Leave-one evidence.}
The code-level predictive rows are also finite.  In the leave-one-code-out experiment, $23$ tables and $63$ targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  In the leave-one-codon-out row, the anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the high-coverage count was $8$, the predictable count was $8$, and the recovery rate was $1.0$.  Thus the recovery evidence is concentrated in a finite code-label boundary calculation rather than in any claimed protein realization.

\paragraph{Family and module partition.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out row grouped $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.  The thirteen codons in $R$ were completely assigned to six modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit recorded $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in this finite classification.  This is a statement about the row structure of codon labels under the supplied table, not a statement that the table itself is generated by the coordinate system.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge: a table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein geometry, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently the supported claim is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically sharp local boundary-tile pattern when read through the standard-code labels.  Any assertion about translation, folding, admissible biophysics, function, cross-organism mechanism, or biological necessity would require a separate contact that observes that higher layer directly.
