\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows with seed $42$. Obtaining those distinct samples required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact local shape appeared once among the $2000$ samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A high-overlap threshold at Jaccard score at least $0.9$ produced $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the finite-row statement that the observed local tile shape is uncommon under the sampled null population. They do not by themselves say that the standard code is biologically forced by the window-six geometry. They say only that, after the curated label table has been admitted as a code-read contact, the displayed boundary row has a statistically sharp local shape in the coordinate system used here.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The row supports the claim that the standard-code table contains a window-six local boundary-tile pattern with a rare sampled shape, a $13$-codon punctured-cube residual, a complete six-module accounting of that residual, and strong leave-one readback behavior. The curated code table is a label bridge, not a mechanism bridge.
