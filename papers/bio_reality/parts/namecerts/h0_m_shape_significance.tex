\subsection{Window-Six Local Tile Shape Significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display whose rows are the standard-code codons. The only external contact used in this record is the curated standard genetic-code table, which assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label. This contact is a code-layer readback contact: it supplies labels for finite rows, but it does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The chapter therefore treats the observed shape as a local boundary-tile fact inside the code-read layer. Any claim beyond codon-to-label readback requires a separate contact.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row set.}
The boundary row set $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row set is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This records a local tile geometry rather than a biological necessity. The codons in $R$ are rows at which the window-six coordinate presentation exposes a boundary-style split against the curated code labels. The record is intentionally finite: the table is the data source, the cube is the coordinate display, and the boundary statement is the local shape read from their joint finite presentation.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
For the primary shape test, the run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled tables. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. The observed exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The number of samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite local shape is rare in this null ensemble. The claim is not that the coordinate geometry causes the genetic code. The claim is only that, after the standard-code labels are supplied by the external table, the six-bit codon display presents a specific local tile shape whose exact recurrence under the sampled comparison model is $1$ in $2000$, and whose high-Jaccard recurrence is $25$ in $2000$.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null test preserves amino-acid class information. In that test the observed $M$ size is $20$. Among $N=2000$ samples, the exact shape match count is $0$, so the reported exact-shape p-value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This second control keeps the statement within the label layer: even when the comparison respects amino-acid class structure, the observed finite row geometry remains uncommon. The $M$ row count is used here as a finite statistic only; the supplied data do not include a full explicit $M$ codon list.

\begin{aligned}

\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic, or radial, polynomial residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is numerically at tolerance for the stated finite diagnostic. This gives a second, coordinate-internal check on the same row geometry: the label-derived local split is not only a set-valued observation but also has a stable spectral readout in the finite codon cube presentation.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The resulting claim is a code-read claim about a local codon tile in $Q_6$. The curated standard-code table supports codon-label readback and local split checks. It does not test translation realization, structural order, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey this BEDC geometry as a biological law. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. The correct scope is therefore: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile pattern whose row set, punctured-cube face-vector, spectral diagnostic, p-values, recovery statistics, and module classification are all finite-row observations at the code layer. Crossing into translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality remains blocked unless a separate reality contact is supplied.
