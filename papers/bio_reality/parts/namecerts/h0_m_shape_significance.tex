\subsection{Window-Six Codon Coordinates and a Local Boundary Tile}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and external readback.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the $64$ possible codon displays obtained from three nucleotide positions with two binary coordinates per position.  The BEDC data used here are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data on this cube.  The biological contact is deliberately narrower: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  Thus the table supplies code-layer label readback, not translation dynamics, not folded structure, not physical admissibility, not biological function, not a mechanism, and not a universality theorem.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The finite row singled out by the coordinate calculation has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check returns the same set and gives the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
In this chapter, $R$ is treated only as a local boundary row inside $Q_6$.  It is not promoted to a global law for DNA, translation, or organismal biology.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The observed local shape $M$ has size $20$.  Against the finite null sample with seed $42$, the experiment used $N=2000$ distinct null tables, requiring $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and producing $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only $1$ exact shape match occurred, while $25$ null samples reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The resulting empirical tail values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row therefore supports a finite, code-layer claim: the standard-code labels occupy a locally unusual window-six shape relative to the sampled null ensemble.  The claim is about the significance of a codon-label tile in the curated table; it is not a claim that the tile itself implements translation or protein structure.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class constraints.  In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact match count was $0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed size $|M|=20$ was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the local reading because the signal is not erased by preserving the coarse amino-acid class inventory.  It still remains a code-read result: the experiment compares label placements in finite codon tables and does not observe ribosomes, chemistry, folding, stability, or function.

They do not supply an independent biological contact; the only reality bridge here remains the curated standard-code label table.

The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\

These finite rows say that the local tile and its boundary are useful for code-table readback across the evaluated families.  They do not say that the same geometry is a universal biological mechanism.

The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification assigns all $13$ codons in $R$, with $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\

Each module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite classification.  The correct reading is therefore a local codon-label boundary decomposition inside the standard-code readback layer.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The boundary condition for this chapter is part of the result.  A local codon tile may expose a statistically unusual finite shape in $Q_6$, and the curated standard-code table may support label-level readback of that shape.  No translation realization follows.  No folded protein structure follows.  No structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows.  Any such higher-layer statement requires a separate reality contact whose observable contains that higher-layer information.  Without that additional contact, amino-acid label-sequence readback remains exactly a code-layer observation.
