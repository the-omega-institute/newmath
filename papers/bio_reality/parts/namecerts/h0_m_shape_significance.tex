\subsection{Window-six codon shape and local boundary significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record is built on the six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$, where a codon is treated as a finite row in a binary window rather than as a biochemical mechanism. The observed contact is the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon a label, either an amino-acid name or a stop label. This chapter uses that table only as a code-layer observation. It does not treat the table as evidence for translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any global law of DNA. Those claims require separate contacts of their own.

The distinguished boundary row set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite set is reported by the punctured-cube computation, with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the evidence is not merely a cardinality count: the row set carries a local two-dimensional tile profile inside the six-coordinate codon display.

\paragraph{Shape statistic.}
For the shape-significance test, the observed local shape was compared against $N=2000$ random finite samples using seed $42$. The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. Only one sampled table matched the observed shape exactly, and $25$ sampled tables reached Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$. The resulting empirical probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape statistic therefore records a rare local configuration in this finite experiment, while the high-Jaccard statistic records that close but non-identical shapes are still uncommon at the tested resolution. The result is a code-read claim about labels on codon rows, not a claim that the geometry causes the genetic code or that biology must implement this coordinate system.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving comparison.}
A second null comparison preserved amino-acid class information. In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-row size was $20$. The exact observed shape appeared zero times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, so the reported lower-tail probability was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This comparison is important because it tests whether the observed shape disappears once a label-class preserving baseline is imposed. The finite outcome says that the code-layer shape remains unusual under that restricted comparison. It still does not license an inference from codon labels to folded proteins, structural order, function, or physical viability.

The radial polynomial residual was $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral radius reported within tolerance was $0.6752479308830213$. These numbers give a compact numerical signature for the finite shape, but they remain internal descriptors of the row geometry. The spectral signature is not a measurement of translation, folding, chemical admissibility, organismal function, or evolutionary universality.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the reported rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The leave-one-codon-out experiment reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These checks support a finite code-layer robustness statement: when one row or one code table is withheld, the local boundary-and-closure profile still recovers much of the row signal. They do not show that a ribosome, a tRNA system, a protein fold, or a cellular phenotype has been reconstructed.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification accounts for all $13$ rows in $R$, with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons. The six reported modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The accounting values are $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and assigned count $13$, with empty lists for extra module codons, missing rows, and unassigned rows. This module list is the strongest finite-row bookkeeping evidence in the packet: the local tile boundary is not a loose verbal grouping, but a completely assigned partition of the observed boundary rows.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded statement. In the six-coordinate codon display, the standard-code label table contains a $13$-row boundary set with punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, an unusual $M$-shape statistic under both unrestricted and amino-acid-class preserving comparisons, a stable spectral signature, and leave-one-out behavior that remains well above the stated baselines. The scientific scope is narrow: the result is a local code-read regularity for codon labels. It is not a claim about protein realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, translation mechanism, organismal necessity, or universality. Any such higher-layer assertion must be attached to a different observation source that directly measures that layer.
