\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The claim recorded here concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer object. The BEDC-minimal carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code label table. The distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The internal structure used by the row is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data. The biological object is therefore not translation as a process, and not a protein as a physical structure; it is the standard-code codon-to-label table at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Monte Carlo shape significance.}
The primary finite-row audit for $h0.M.shape.significance$ used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows. The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, which matters because the finite search space has strong recurrence under the row constraints. The exact local shape occurred once among the $2000$ sampled rows, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\mathrm{exact}&=1, & p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25, & p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both reported significance fields agree with these values: $p\_value\_exact\_shape\_significant=0.0005$ and $p\_value\_high\_jaccard\_significant=0.0125$. The correct conclusion is narrow but nontrivial: the observed window-six local tile is uncommon under the sampled null rows, while the evidence remains a finite code-layer shape comparison.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite-row verdict is that the window-six codon coordinate system exposes a statistically rare and internally coherent local boundary-tile pattern in the curated standard-code table. The exact-shape probability $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$, the amino-acid-class lower-tail probability $0.0035$, the punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral row, and the complete $13$-codon module classification all point to a genuine code-layer signal. The boundary is equally part of the claim. No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this row unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global law of biology.
