\paragraph{Carrier and readback.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is the $64$-row codon display obtained from three nucleotide positions with four choices per position, represented internally by coordinates, closures, spectra, and finite relations.  The biological readback is only the curated standard-code label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact therefore supports a code-layer question, namely whether the induced local tile has finite-row structure that is uncommon under explicit null probes.  It does not by itself assert translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any necessity law for DNA.

\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.}
The result supports the following bounded claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically uncommon local boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code label table.  The evidence uses external code labels, BEDC-style finite coordinates, and derived probes over those labels.  The curated table is a reality contact for label readback only.  Therefore the chapter cannot claim protein realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or a global law of biology.  Any such promotion would require a separate contact whose rows actually observe translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function.  Without that added contact, amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation rather than a protein-level or organism-level fact.
