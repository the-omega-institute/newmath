\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact, not as a mechanistic account of translation. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The BEDC-minimal display keeps four distinctions separate: the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class. Its internal bookkeeping is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data; its biological readback is only the standard-code label table. The observed contact therefore supports codon-label readback and local split checks. It does not by itself test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The finite-row significance test for $h0.M.shape.significance$ used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled null rows. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported null comparison is visibly constrained by the finite combinatorics of the sample space. The exact shape appeared once among the $2000$ sampled rows, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap shape matches at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local tile shape is not being reported as an arbitrary visualization: in this finite sampled comparison it is a low-frequency pattern, both for exact agreement and for near-agreement. The empirical claim remains code-layer only: a rare coordinate-shape match is evidence about local codon-label geometry, not evidence about folding, admissibility, function, or mechanism.
