\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables at seed $42$.  To obtain those distinct samples the run made $57377$ sample attempts and rejected $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that row universe, the exact observed shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap shapes with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row conclusion is therefore local and statistical: the observed window-six tile is rare under this sampling procedure, with exact-shape significance $0.0005$ and high-Jaccard significance $0.0125$.  This is evidence for a structured local code-read pattern, not for a biological law beyond the tested layer.
