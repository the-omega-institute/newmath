\subsection{Window-six local codon-tile shape}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite object studied here is the standard genetic-code table viewed through a six-bit codon display on the codon cube $Q_6$.  The table is used only as a curated code-label contact: each codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label, and the resulting finite labeling is compared with a local tile extracted from the window-six coordinate system.  The claim is therefore a code-read claim.  It says that the window-six coordinates expose a statistically uncommon local boundary-tile shape inside the standard-code labeling, while the amino-acid and stop labels themselves remain imported from the curated table rather than generated by the geometry.

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the supplied row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row identifies this set with a punctured-cube shape whose face-vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the geometric datum is not a vague visual analogy: it is a finite subset of the $64$ codons, with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ tile faces in the chosen local coordinate display.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
For the shape test with run identifier $0\mathrm{b}267\mathrm{c}88\mathrm{f}8\mathrm{e}4$, the null sampling row used $N=2000$ distinct samples under seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and reported $55377$ duplicate retries, so the recorded empirical tail counts should be read as finite Monte Carlo counts rather than as symbolic enumeration of the whole null space.  Among the $2000$ samples, the exact local shape occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap event at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These two numbers support the narrow statement that the observed tile shape is uncommon in the finite sampling model.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause, an evolutionary pathway, or a universal law of codon organization.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null row.}
A second null row preserves amino-acid class information and again uses $N=2000$ with seed $42$.  In that row the observed module size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The corresponding finite estimates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a stronger guard against the possibility that the signal is only a trivial consequence of preserving coarse label classes.  The record still remains empirical and finite: the row certifies the behavior of the reported sampling experiment, not an analytic theorem over all possible relabelings.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral computation supplies three scalars for the same finite display.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check is numerically small, with polynomial value $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, and the reported spectral-radius value in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$.  This gives an independent numerical summary of the local tile and its boundary set: the finite shape has both combinatorial support and a reproducible spectral signature at the precision recorded by the row.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the five reported families have rates $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These finite rows say that the local code-read tile continues to organize held-out label cases in the reported tasks; they do not upgrade the observation to a statement about molecular translation or protein realization.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module row partitions all $13$ codons of $R$ with no missing or extra assignments: $R$ size is $13$, assigned count is $13$, the list of extra module codons is empty, the missing-from-modules list is empty, and the unassigned-codons list is empty.  The reported modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_LYS}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ILE\_MET}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{STOP\_TRP}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has predictable or boundary status in the row.  This is the most concrete finite classification supplied for the local tile: it explains how the $13$ boundary codons are grouped without leaving residue codons outside the module accounting.

\paragraph{Scope.}
The curated standard-code table is sufficient for code-layer readback and for testing local codon-label splits.  It is not a contact with ribosomal translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility of a chain, biochemical function, a mechanism of origin, or universality across genetic systems.  No such higher-layer conclusion follows from the window-six tile, the punctured-cube row, the spectral scalars, or the leave-one-out rates unless a separate observation is supplied for that layer.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence read from the code table is not a folded protein, and a statistically uncommon local codon tile is not a global biological law.
