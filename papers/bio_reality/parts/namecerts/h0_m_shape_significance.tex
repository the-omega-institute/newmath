\paragraph{Finite coordinate record.}
The record for claim $h0.M.shape.significance$ treats the standard-code codon table as a code-layer object read through a six-bit codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with the observed labels supplied by the curated standard-code table.  The internal packet distinguishes coordinates, closure behavior, spectral data, and relations among codon labels.  The biological item under inspection is therefore not translation itself, but the finite label table that assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.

The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also recorded as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite observation is more specific than a count: it names the thirteen codons and records a local cubical incidence profile with $13$ vertices, $16$ edges, and $4$ square faces.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The significance test used $N=2000$ sampled comparators with seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, indicating that the finite sampling was constrained by repeated candidates rather than by a large supply of unrelated shapes.  Among the $2000$ comparators there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The reported tail frequencies are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The local tile is therefore rare under the finite comparator scheme used in the packet.  This supports the narrower statement that the standard-code label table contains a non-generic local boundary pattern in the window-six representation.  It does not identify a biochemical cause for that pattern.

\paragraph{Reality contact and local boundary.}
The contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  Its observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  The contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or functional realization.  Accordingly, the finite results above justify a code-layer statement: window-six coordinates expose a statistically unusual local boundary tile in the standard-code label table, with named codons, module coverage, sampling probabilities, spectral entries, and leave-one checks.

The same results also impose a strict boundary.  No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this packet.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-table readback unless a separate contact supplies a structure, mechanism, or function observation.  Cross-organism wording, CUN/UUR notation, or tRNA-Leu language likewise cannot promote this local tile into evidence for folding or function without an organism-matched and layer-matched contact.
