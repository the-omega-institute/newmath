\subsection{Window-six codon tile significance}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed code layer.}
This record works at the code-read layer of the standard genetic code.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, where each codon is treated as a coordinate point and the observed labels are supplied by a curated standard-code table.  The geometric vocabulary used here is deliberately local: a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The row does not treat the tile as a biological mechanism.  It tests whether a particular finite shape arising from the codon-label readback is unusually structured under the sampled null models.

\paragraph{Boundary set and punctured-cube readout.}
The verified boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies this set with the punctured-cube pattern measured in the experiment, with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite signal is not merely a count of exceptional codons: it retains incidence data among vertices, edges, and square faces inside the local cube picture.

\paragraph{Shape significance under randomization.}
The primary shape-significance run used seed $42$ and accepted $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables only after $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those $2000$ distinct samples, the exact observed shape appeared once.  The resulting exact-shape frequency is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A high-overlap relaxation using Jaccard threshold $0.9$ produced $25$ matches, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These two numbers support the local claim that the measured $M$-shape is rare among the sampled finite alternatives.  The row also records an amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$, observed $M$-size $20$, zero exact matches, and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed size.  In that constrained null,
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0,\qquad p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite evidence therefore survives both the direct exact-shape test and a label-class-preserving comparison, while still remaining a statement about code-table geometry rather than molecular realization.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The spectral row gives a second numerical view of the same finite pattern.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is within numerical tolerance for the reported spectral relation.  This is useful because the combinatorial description of $R$ and the spectral description of the local tile are independent finite summaries of the same code-read object: one names the codons and incidence counts, the other records the eigenvalue-scale signature.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ alternative code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the recorded hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row separately recorded no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the local finite shape has predictive coherence under deletion tests, not that the cell implements the geometry as a causal law.

\paragraph{Family and module decomposition.}
The family-level code-out row groups the $23$ evaluated tables into $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast.  The module-classification row assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ with no extras, no missing module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has predictable or boundary status in the finite classification.  The decomposition is therefore complete for this row: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the residual lists are empty.

\paragraph{Scope of the biological reading.}
The allowed biological contact is the curated standard-code table: codons are read as amino-acid or stop labels at code level.  The contact does not observe translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of origin, or a universal biological law.  Consequently the result is a local codon-tile significance statement: window-six coordinates expose a rare and coherent finite pattern in the standard-code label table, but any claim about translation, folding, physical feasibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate observation designed for that higher layer.
