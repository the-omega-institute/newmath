\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The biological contact is only the curated assignment of each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC-side carrier supplies coordinates, closure, spectrum, and relations on the finite cube, while the readback supplies the standard-code label table.  Thus the object tested here is not translation as a biochemical process.  It is the finite question of whether a window-six coordinate display exposes a local tile-boundary pattern in the observed code labels.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the following finite set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube row with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local shape datum used by the code-read claim.  It is finite-row evidence for a coordinate-visible tile boundary, not evidence that DNA or translation must obey this geometry as a global biological law.

\paragraph{Shape significance under label-table sampling.}
The finite-row significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled tables.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the $2000$ samples there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$.  Hence the reported empirical rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same values are recorded as the exact-shape and high-Jaccard significance numbers.  The interpretation is narrow: the observed local tile shape is rare in this finite null ensemble, and near-shape recurrence remains low.  The computation does not by itself identify a mechanism, a selective pressure, a universal law, or a physical realization route.
