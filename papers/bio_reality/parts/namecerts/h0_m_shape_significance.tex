\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display. The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with codons read as coordinate points and then compared against curated amino-acid or stop labels. The mathematical object under test is not translation itself. It is a code-layer shape question: whether the window-six coordinate organization exposes a local boundary tile whose label pattern is unusually specific relative to label-preserving or label-randomized controls. The biological contact is the curated standard genetic-code table, which supplies assignments from codons to amino-acid labels or stop labels. This contact is sufficient for code readback and local label-split testing, but it is not a structure assay, a dynamics model, or a mechanism of protein realization.

The evidence does not claim that this incidence profile is a law of biology. It says that, after the standard-code label table is read at the code layer, the local tile determined by these coordinates is a concrete finite shape with thirteen codon vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces.

\paragraph{Shape-significance experiment.}
For the primary significance row, the experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison objects. The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported counts are tied to a concrete finite sampling trace. Among the $2000$ sampled objects, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$. The finite Monte Carlo estimates are therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the local claim that the observed tile shape is rare under the tested comparison procedure. They do not, by themselves, identify a causal process. The correct reading is narrower: the standard-code table, when filtered through the window-six coordinate display, gives a local shape whose exact recurrence in the tested finite null sample is $1$ out of $2000$, while high-overlap recurrence is $25$ out of $2000$.

\paragraph{Scientific boundary.}
The chapter therefore records a code-layer finding about a six-bit codon coordinate tile attached to the curated standard-code table. The evidence supports local label readback, boundary shape rarity, spectral coherence, module completeness, and leave-one-out recovery. It does not support any promotion beyond that layer. No conclusion about translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these rows unless a separate contact supplies observations at that higher layer. Amino-acid label sequences are still labels at the code layer; they are not folded structures. Cross-organism wording, tRNA notation, or a codon-family name also does not create structural evidence. The correct finite conclusion is that the standard-code table contains a locally rare and highly recoverable codon-tile pattern under the stated window-six construction, while all higher biological interpretations remain outside the scope of this contact.
