\paragraph{Claim scope.}
This record concerns a six-coordinate codon display of the standard genetic code.  The mathematical object is the codon cube $Q_6$, read only at the code-label level: each codon is assigned its curated amino-acid or stop label, and the experiment asks whether the induced local boundary tile is unusually shaped under the stated finite null procedures.  The biological object is therefore the standard-code codon table as a label table, not a translation apparatus, not a folding experiment, not a physical model of admissible proteins, and not a mechanism for biological function.  The admissible claim is narrower: window-six codon coordinates expose a local split whose finite shape, boundary, and recovery statistics are nontrivial when compared with label-preserving and sampled alternatives.

$$

$$

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The direct shape experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparators.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, which is relevant because the sampled space contains many repeated draws under the distinctness filter.  Among the $2000$ accepted comparators there was exactly one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
At the high-overlap threshold there were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both figures support the finite claim that the observed local tile is rare under this sampling regime.  The result is not a statement that biology must realize this geometry; it is a statement that the curated standard-code labels, when placed on the six-bit codon cube and tested by the stated row procedure, produce a local shape that is hard to reproduce exactly and uncommon even under high-Jaccard relaxation.

The class-preserving null model gives a second check on the same boundary.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$ size is $20$. The interpretation remains local: class preservation does not add a mechanism, and the null-model failure does not promote codon-label geometry into translation, protein structure, biochemical admissibility, biological function, or universal law.

\paragraph{Local boundary of interpretation.}

It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded three-dimensional structure, physical admissibility, organismal function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently, amino-acid label sequences obtained from this table must not be treated as folded protein structures, and cross-organism wording about codon families must not be used as structure evidence without a separate organism-matched contact.  The correct conclusion is therefore finite and local: the standard-code labels, placed on the six-bit codon cube, determine an unusually significant local tile with a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary, strong spectral coherence, high leave-one-code-out recovery, and complete leave-one-codon-out module coverage.
