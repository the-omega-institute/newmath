\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the finite coordinate display obtained by reading each codon as a six-coordinate word and then attaching the curated standard-code label table as the external readback. The distinctions used in the row are the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class. The internal structure retained by the record is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation. The observed reality contact is only this: a curated standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. Therefore the record can test code readback and a local codon-tile label split, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary relation.}
The finite boundary relation $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its observed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite set is verified as a punctured-cube row, with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons. It carries a local cell structure inside the codon cube: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces after the relevant puncture. This is the shape whose label behavior is compared against finite null rows.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The primary finite-row test used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$. The run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and saw $55377$ duplicate retries, so the empirical comparison is being made in a strongly constrained finite sample space rather than in a loose continuous model. The observed standard-code shape had exactly one exact shape match among the $2000$ sampled rows, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both reported values are finite-row frequencies from the stated run. They support the narrow claim that the standard-code readback occupies an unusually specific local tile shape under this coordinate probe. They do not support any claim that the coordinate geometry is a law of DNA, a translation engine, a folding principle, a physical-admissibility criterion, a biological function, a mechanism, or a universal explanation.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}

$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the code-layer reading: even after the null model is constrained by amino-acid class preservation, the observed local shape remains rare in the finite row. The data still remain label-table data. The value $|M|=20$ is a code-readback size, not a count of folded structures, viable proteins, realized translations, or functions.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite-row evidence supports a local codon-tile statement: window-six coordinates expose a rare, spectrally coherent, module-complete boundary pattern in the curated standard-code label table. The exact-shape frequency $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard frequency $0.0125$, the amino-acid-class preserving lower-tail frequency $0.0035$, the $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary, the spectral values, and the leave-one-out recovery rows all point to the same code-layer structure. The boundary of the claim is equally important. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, not folded protein structure, not physical admissibility, not biological function, not a mechanism, and not universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. Any move across those boundaries requires a separate contact that observes the higher layer directly.
