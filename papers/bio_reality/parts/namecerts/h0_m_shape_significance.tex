\subsection{Window-Six Local Tile Significance for the Standard Codon Table}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$, where a codon is treated as a finite coordinate word and the observed labels are the curated amino-acid-or-stop assignments of the standard genetic code.  The construction separates the coordinate geometry from the biological table: the geometry supplies candidate local tile, singleton-corner, three-point-class, closure, spectrum, and relation data, while the label table supplies the reality contact that says which codons read as which labels.  The claim tested here is not that biology is governed by this geometry, but that the window-six coordinate presentation exposes a statistically nontrivial local boundary pattern when read against the standard code.

\paragraph{Observed residual row.}
The residual set $R$ has size $13$ and is exactly the following codon row:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite set is reported by both the cardinality check and the punctured-cube check.  Its cubical face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not being used merely as a list of labels; it is being read as a finite shape inside the six-coordinate display.  The supplied comparison also reports an observed $M$-side size of $20$ for the amino-acid-class-preserving null test.  The individual $M$ codon names are not part of the finite row supplied here, so this chapter uses the observed size and does not invent a missing codon list.

\paragraph{Spectral signature.}
The coordinate split also has a numerical spectral profile.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, with the same value recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual, so the polynomial relation is satisfied to numerical tolerance rather than merely asserted symbolically.  This is a finite diagnostic of the coordinate presentation: it says that the displayed $M/R$ split has a stable spectral readout under the implemented probe, not that the split is a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance against random relabeling.}
For the primary shape-significance run, the experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison tables.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, which is relevant because it records the finite sampling pressure rather than hiding it.  The exact local shape appeared once in the $2000$ comparisons, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
Near matches at Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the exact shape and the high-overlap neighborhood are both uncommon under the sampled null model.  The result is finite and empirical in the narrow sense: it supports the claim that this standard-code tile is not a generic random artifact under the tested comparison, while leaving all stronger biological readings outside the scope of this contact.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison.}
A stricter comparison preserves amino-acid class information.  In that run, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$ size is $20$.  There were $0$ exact matches to the observed shape, so the sampled exact-shape value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class},\mathrm{exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Only $7$ sampled tables landed in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class},\mathrm{lower}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving comparison is important because it tests whether the row is explained away by coarse amino-acid grouping alone.  On the supplied finite data, the answer is negative for the implemented statistic: preserving class labels does not routinely reproduce the same local shape.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
The code-family perturbation check evaluated $23$ genetic-code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary row is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast.  These figures say that the local row is highly predictive under the leave-one-code-out test used here, with the weakest reported family still well above the baseline rate.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and module coverage.}
The codon-removal test reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification is complete: the reported $R$ size is $13$, the assigned count is $13$, there are no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
agr&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
ar\_lys&=\{AAA\},\\
cun&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
ile\_met&=\{AUA\},\\
ssn&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
stop\_trp&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has predictable-or-boundary support under the recorded classification.  The module table therefore gives a concrete internal organization of the thirteen residual codons rather than only a global count.

\paragraph{Reality contact and local verdict.}
The only biological contact used in this chapter is the curated standard genetic-code table, understood as a codon-to-label source.  It supports code-layer readback and local split checks: codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels, and the coordinate row can be compared with those labels.  It does not observe translation dynamics, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any necessity claim about DNA.  Consequently the local verdict is narrow.  The supplied finite rows support a statistically significant window-six local tile associated with the standard-code label table, with exact-shape $p=0.0005$, high-Jaccard $p=0.0125$, class-preserving lower-tail $p=0.0035$, complete residual module coverage, and strong leave-one-code-out recovery.  No conclusion about translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate experiment supplies that higher-layer contact.
