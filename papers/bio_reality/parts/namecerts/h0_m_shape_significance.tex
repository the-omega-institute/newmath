\subsection{Window-six local shape signal in the standard codon table}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.} This record concerns the standard genetic-code label table as a code-layer object. The coordinate carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of $64$ codon positions with curated amino-acid or stop labels. The tested pattern is local: it compares a distinguished twenty-point label-side set $M$ with the boundary set $R$ obtained from the same six-coordinate display. The finite rows give $|M|=20$ and identify the boundary component as a thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run verifies that this $R$ is the punctured-cube boundary pattern with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object being assessed is not a biological process, an organism-wide principle, or a physical model. It is a finite codon-label readback together with a local cube-shaped separation visible in that readback.

\paragraph{Shape significance computation.} The direct shape test used seed $42$ and compared the observed $M$-shape against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives. Because the sampler rejected duplicates, it made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. In that finite comparison there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold test counted $25$ alternatives with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid class information again used $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It found $0$ exact matches to the observed shape and only $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size of $20$, so the reported finite probabilities are $0.0$ for exact class-preserving shape recovery and $0.0035$ for the lower-tail event. These numbers support a code-table shape signal: the observed local pattern is uncommon under the sampled nulls supplied by the finite run.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.} The same finite packet records a three-parameter spectral summary for the local shape. The values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same number reported as the cubic-polynomial residual. This is numerically consistent with the finite spectral relation used by the packet, up to the stated tolerance. The spectral data should be read only as a coordinate-level diagnostic of the codon-label pattern. It does not by itself add a mechanism for translation, a folding claim, a physical admissibility statement, or any functional conclusion.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.} The code-table perturbation rows give additional finite checks on locality. In the leave-one-code-out run, $23$ tables were evaluated across $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and including the boundary raised the recorded rate to $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid tables, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate tables, $1.0$ for the candidate-other group, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast tables. In the leave-one-codon-out run there were $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $0$ anchor cases, with recovery rate $1.0$. These rows strengthen the finite code-layer interpretation: the pattern is stable under the listed leave-one probes, but the stability remains a property of codon-label readback.

\paragraph{Module accounting.} The module classification assigns all $13$ boundary codons and leaves no extra or unassigned codons. The recorded modules are $\{AGA,AGG\}$ for the $AGR$ module, $\{AAA\}$ for the $AR/Lys$ module, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for the $CUN$ module, $\{AUA\}$ for the Ile/Met module, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ for the $SSN$ module, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for the stop/Trp module. The row states $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, and no unassigned codons. This closes the finite accounting for the boundary pattern used in the claim.

\paragraph{Scientific boundary.} The contact used here is a curated standard-code table. That contact can test codon-to-label readback and local label splitting inside the six-coordinate codon display. It cannot test whether translation is realized in a cell, whether an amino-acid string folds, whether a molecule is physically admissible, whether a protein has a function, whether an organism implements a mechanism, or whether the local pattern is a universal biological law. The present verdict is therefore narrow: the finite rows support an unusually strong local shape signal for the standard-code label table, with exact-shape probability $0.0005$, high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$, class-preserving lower-tail probability $0.0035$, and a fully assigned thirteen-codon boundary module. Any movement from this code-layer result to translation, folding, physical chemistry, biological function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate empirical contact not supplied by this packet.
