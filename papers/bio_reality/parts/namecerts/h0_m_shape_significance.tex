\subsection{Window-six codon tile shape in the standard-code table}\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite object considered here is the standard-code codon label table viewed through a window-six codon coordinate display. Each codon is represented as a point of the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, and the observed amino-acid or stop labels are used only as curated code-layer labels. The claim is not that the geometry generates the biological code. The claim is narrower: after the labels have been read from the curated table, the distinguished local tile $M$ has an unusually specific finite shape relative to null samples on the same six-bit coordinate carrier.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The row $R$ consists of exactly thirteen codons,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality row reports $|R|=13$. The same row is certified as a punctured-cube boundary with face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-layer object is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it has a finite incidence profile in the window-six coordinate cube. This incidence statement is internal to the chosen coordinate representation, while the codon labels themselves remain supplied by the curated standard genetic-code table.

\paragraph{Shape significance row.}
For the primary shape test, the observed $M$-shape was compared with $N=2000$ distinct null samples using seed $42$. The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. Among the $2000$ accepted rows, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$. The reported empirical probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the statement that the local $M$-shape is a rare finite pattern under this null construction. The result is a shape-level observation about the codon-label table under the chosen coordinate display; it does not by itself identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null row.}
A second null row preserves amino-acid class information. In this test, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed size was $|M|=20$. There were $0$ exact matches to the observed shape. The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving the reported values
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving null therefore strengthens the finite-row evidence that the observed local tile is not a common artifact of simply preserving broad amino-acid label classes. The zero exact-match value is an empirical value for the $2000$ tested samples, not a proof of mathematical impossibility.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The same finite configuration has a measured spectral profile. The verified values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic and radial residuals both equal approximately $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius entry in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$. These finite residuals show numerical consistency of the recorded spectral summary for the same window-six object. They are included as shape diagnostics, not as independent evidence for translation dynamics or molecular folding.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon rows.}
The leave-one-code-out evaluation used $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The family-level table contains $5$ families, with hit rates above baseline
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These rows say that the local coordinate tile retains strong code-layer recovery behavior when individual entries or code-table variants are withheld. They do not say that the coordinate geometry performs translation.

\paragraph{Module classification row.}
The thirteen boundary codons are completely assigned by the module row. The verified module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completion check reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. Hence the module classification is exact for the finite row $R$. This gives a controlled local decomposition of the boundary tile into named code-layer modules.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only empirical contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact tests code readback and local label splitting. It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a generating mechanism, or universality across life. Therefore the permissible conclusion is limited to a finite code-layer statement: in the window-six coordinate display, the curated standard-code labels determine a local tile whose boundary row, incidence vector, module partition, spectral diagnostics, and null-model rarity are all explicitly recorded. Any claim about translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law requires an additional contact designed for that higher layer.
