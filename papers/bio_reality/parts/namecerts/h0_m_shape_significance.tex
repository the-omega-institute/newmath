\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Significance}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read only through a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The claim tested here is not that the biological code is generated by the geometry of $Q_6$. It is the narrower code-read claim that the window-six coordinate system exposes a local tile boundary whose observed label split is statistically sharp relative to label-preserving finite nulls. The contact is therefore a code-label contact: it supports codon-to-label readback and local split checks, and it does not supply translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{The punctured boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation the observed row is
$$\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same finite row is verified as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
f(R) = (13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the boundary is not being summarized by a single scalar: it has a concrete vertex count, edge count, and two-face count inside the codon cube. The codons in $R$ are exactly the codons listed above, with no extra codons and no omissions in the verified row.

\paragraph{Shape significance of the local tile.}
The observed local shape was tested against $2000$ distinct random samples under seed $42$. Producing those distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite null was materially constrained by duplicate avoidance rather than by a loose independent draw. Among the $2000$ distinct samples there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$. The resulting finite-row estimates are
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
The same row records these as the exact-shape significance value $0.0005$ and the high-Jaccard significance value $0.0125$. This supports a local finite-combinatorial claim: the observed $M$-shape is uncommon under the sampled null. It does not by itself identify a causal biological law.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A stricter null preserving amino-acid class was also run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$. In that test the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and only $7$ samples lay in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size. The recorded finite-row probabilities are
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
This second null separates the shape signal from a trivial amino-acid-class explanation. The result is still a code-layer statistic: it compares finite codon-label rows and does not observe ribosomal realization, molecular structure, or organism-level function.

\paragraph{Spectral readout.}
The same object has a spectral signature. The verified values are
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}$$
The radial or cubic polynomial check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the displayed spectral value is within the recorded numerical tolerance. The spectral row is useful because it gives a coordinate-independent scalar shadow of the local tile, but it remains a shadow of the finite code-read object, not a separate biological mechanism.

By family, the five recorded families have hit rates above baseline

\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\

\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\

These rows say that the local tile continues to recover code-read targets across curated table variation. They do not say that the variation is forced by geometry alone.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and module coverage.}
The codon-level holdout row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure codons, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$. The threshold comparison also records $1.0$. The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons are all empty. The six modules are
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}$$
Every displayed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite row. This is the strongest local reading supported by the data: the $13$ codons of $R$ decompose without residue into named codon modules, and the modules align with the observed boundary-predictability split.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite evidence supports a chapter-level code-read statement. In the six-bit codon display, the standard-code label table meets a local tile whose boundary row $R$ has a verified punctured-cube profile, a statistically uncommon $M$-shape under two finite nulls, a stable spectral readout, and strong leave-one-code and leave-one-codon recovery. The admissible conclusion is local and finite: window-six coordinates expose a significant codon-label boundary tile in the curated standard-code readback. The inadmissible promotions are equally explicit. No translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from this contact unless an additional reality contact supplies that higher-layer fact.
