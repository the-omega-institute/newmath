\subsection{Window-six shape significance for the standard genetic code}\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The claim concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The underlying finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon represented by a six-bit coordinate word and with the curated standard-code table supplying the amino-acid or stop label assigned to each codon.  The observed local object is a boundary-sensitive label tile: a finite subset $M$ of codon coordinates whose shape is compared against randomized label-preserving or label-derived controls.  The purpose of the row is not to derive the genetic code from geometry, but to test whether the code-layer labeling, when placed on the six-coordinate cube, singles out a local shape that is rare under the stated finite null experiments.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same row verifies that this set is a punctured-cube-shaped boundary with finite face data
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the finite observation is not only the number $13$, but the specific list of codons and its recorded face-vector profile.  The control row for $M$ records an observed tile size $|M|=20$, so the boundary $R$ is a distinguished thirteen-codon part of a twenty-codon local window rather than an arbitrary relabeling of the full table.

\paragraph{Shape-significance experiment.}
In the direct shape comparison, the run used seed $42$ and sampled $N=2000$ distinct randomized instances.  Obtaining these $2000$ distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, so the empirical comparison was performed over a deduplicated finite sample.  Exactly one sampled instance matched the observed shape, and $25$ sampled instances had Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The reported empirical probabilities are
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
Both numbers support the same code-layer reading: the observed local tile is rare in this finite randomization, and near-matches are still uncommon.  The evidence is finite and empirical; it does not make the tile a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class constraints.  With the same seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the exact observed shape occurred $0$ times.  The lower-tail event at or below the observed value $|M|=20$ occurred $7$ times, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
This strengthens the local claim because the rarity is not only a consequence of unconstrained shuffling: even after retaining amino-acid class structure, the exact shape is absent from the finite sample and the lower-tail count remains small.

\paragraph{Spectral check.}
The recorded spectral row gives three scalar invariants for the same finite construction:
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}$$
The radial cubic check returns residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also recorded as the cubic polynomial residual.  This is numerically compatible with zero at the stated precision, so the spectral data are internally consistent with the finite shape computation.  The spectral row should be read as a coordinate-level stability check on the codon tile, not as evidence for protein folding, chemical energetics, or biological function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These values indicate that the local tile and boundary retain predictive code-layer information under deletion tests, while still remaining a finite readback test on codon labels.

\paragraph{Family-level robustness.}
The family-stratified leave-one-code-out row separates $5$ families.  The reported family hit rates above baseline are
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}$$
The only listed family below perfect recovery is alternative yeast, and it still lies well above the baseline row.  This gives finite support for the claim that the local window is not a single-table accident within the tested code-family collection.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen boundary codons are completely assigned to six named modules:
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The classification row records $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the module list, and no unassigned codons.  Hence every codon in the boundary set is accounted for exactly by the displayed module partition.

\paragraph{Interpretation and limitation.}
The ready claim is a code-read claim: in the six-coordinate codon cube, the curated standard-code labels expose a rare local boundary-tile shape with exact-shape probability $0.0005$, high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$, amino-acid-class exact probability $0.0$, and amino-acid-class lower-tail probability $0.0035$ in the recorded finite runs.  The claim also includes the concrete thirteen-codon boundary list, the punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral values $\lambda_M$, $\lambda_R$, and $\mu$, and the deletion-test recovery rows.  Nothing in this row establishes translation as a physical process, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality across life.  Any such higher-layer conclusion would require an additional experimental or curated contact that directly observes that layer.
