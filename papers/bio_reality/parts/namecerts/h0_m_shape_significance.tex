\subsection{Window-six codon shape and local code readback}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record fixes the codon object at the code-reading layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube obtained by reading each codon as a finite word over the four RNA letters and then exposing the corresponding six-bit display.  The observed biological table is not inferred from the cube: it is supplied by a curated standard-code contact assigning each of the $64$ codons to an amino-acid label or to stop.  The mathematical question in this row is narrower.  Once those labels are placed on the six-coordinate display, the local label split determines a distinguished shape $M$ and a boundary remainder $R$.  The row tests whether that shape is an ordinary accident under finite label-preserving resampling or whether the observed local tile is unusually constrained.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The computed remainder has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row reports that this set is the punctured-cube boundary shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the boundary part is not described only by its size: it also carries a local two-dimensional incidence signature inside the codon cube.  The associated module partition is complete: the $13$ codons are exhausted by $\mathrm{AGR}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{CUN}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{SSN}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The assignment count is $13$, with $R$-size $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The primary finite-row test sampled $N=2000$ distinct comparison shapes with seed $42$.  Producing those distinct samples required $57377$ attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which records that the null space was sampled under a strong finite distinctness constraint rather than by accepting repeated rows.  Among the $2000$ rows, exactly one row matched the observed shape exactly, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row therefore supports a finite significance statement for the observed local codon-label shape: exact agreement is rare in the sampled null family, and near agreement at the $0.9$ Jaccard threshold is also uncommon.  This is a statement about the displayed label geometry of the curated standard code, not about the biochemical origin of that table.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In that test the observed $M$-size is $20$, the sample size is again $N=2000$, and the seed is again $42$.  The exact-match count is $0$, so the empirical exact-shape value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count for rows with size at most the observed $M$-size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the local interpretation: even after the null retains amino-acid class constraints, the observed tile remains small and shape-specific relative to the sampled alternatives.  The row does not supply the full codon list for $M$, so the present chapter states its observed size and its finite null statistics rather than pretending to enumerate missing entries.

The spectral radius recorded in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$.

\paragraph{Leave-one checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, $5$ families were evaluated: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$ above baseline, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the local tile and its boundary have predictive stability under these finite deletion probes.  They do not say that the codon table is forced by geometry alone.

\paragraph{Readback and exclusion boundary.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supports codon-to-label readback and local split checks.  It does not observe translation as a realized process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  The amino-acid labels can be read from the standard-code table, and the local six-coordinate tile can then be tested against finite null rows; no statement about folding or function follows from that operation.  A later structural or functional claim would need its own contact, with its own observation protocol, before it could be attached to this codon-row geometry.

\paragraph{Chapter verdict.}
The finite evidence supports the following restricted claim.  In the standard-code codon table, window-six coordinates expose a statistically unusual local label shape with a $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary, a complete six-module classification of that boundary, significant exact and high-Jaccard null statistics, a compatible spectral signature, and strong leave-one recovery behavior.  The supported layer is code readback.  The unsupported layers remain outside the claim: no translation realization, folding order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality is obtained unless a separate reality contact supplies it.
