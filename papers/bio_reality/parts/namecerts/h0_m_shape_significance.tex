\subsection{Window-Six Local Tile Significance for the Standard Code}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The claim recorded here concerns the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ as a finite display for the standard-code codon table. The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and this contact supports only code-layer label readback. The BEDC-side carrier is the codon cube with coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation structure; the readback side is the standard-code label table. The finite distinction under test is therefore not translation as a cellular process, not protein structure, and not biological function. It is the following local question: when the standard-code labels are read on the window-six coordinate display, does the observed local tile boundary have a shape that is rare under the tested finite nulls?

\paragraph{Observed punctured-cube boundary.}
The boundary relation $R$ has cardinality $13$. The observed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it is a finite tile-like boundary in the six-bit display. The module classification is complete on this same $R$: the $13$ assigned codons exhaust the module rows, with no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are $\{AGA,AGG\}$ for $agr$, $\{AAA\}$ for $ar\_lys$, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for $cun$, $\{AUA\}$ for $ile\_met$, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ for $ssn$, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for $stop\_trp$.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The finite-row significance test used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, including $55377$ duplicate retries, before the sampled set was completed. Among the $2000$ sampled rows, the exact observed shape appeared once. The exact-shape tail probability is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold also remains small: $25$ sampled rows had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These are finite empirical frequencies for the stated sampling procedure. They support the claim that the observed local tile shape is uncommon in that null ensemble, not the stronger claim that the codon table is forced by geometry.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null preserves amino-acid class information. In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-row size was $20$. The exact-shape count was $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This second finite row is important because it keeps part of the code-label organization fixed while still making the observed local shape hard to reproduce. The data therefore support a code-read-level distinction: the standard table's labels are not being treated as arbitrary names, but the conclusion still remains local to the curated codon-label contact.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated spectral row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is approximately
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check. This residual is numerically close to zero at the displayed precision and is used here only as a finite consistency check for the reported spectral encoding. It is not a physical energy, not a folding score, and not a mechanistic parameter.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The family-level row covered $5$ families: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each had hit rate $1.0$ above baseline. The leave-one-codon-out probe reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These finite rows say that the local tile and its boundary are predictive within the stated code-read probes. They do not say that the same pattern determines translation, folding, admissibility, or function.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded conclusion. In the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, the curated standard-code label table exposes a local tile boundary whose exact shape is rare in the sampled null rows, whose high-Jaccard neighborhood remains uncommon, whose $R$ boundary is a $13$-codon punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, and whose module decomposition is complete on the reported $13$ codons. This is a code-read result. It permits local codon-label split checks against the curated standard-code bridge. It does not promote the tile to translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity. Any such higher-layer claim would require a separate contact that directly observes the relevant layer.
