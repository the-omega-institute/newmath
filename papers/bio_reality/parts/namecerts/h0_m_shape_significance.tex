\subsection{Window-Six Local Codon Tile Significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Carrier and readback.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon label table.  Each codon is treated as a coordinate in a finite display, while each amino-acid or stop assignment is treated as an external label contact supplied by the curated table.  The observed local object is a tile $M$ together with a boundary remainder $R$.  The row data records the boundary as a thirteen-codon set,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$.  The same row identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube boundary with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  This is a coordinate and closure statement about the displayed codon cube.  It is not, by itself, a statement about translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape test.}
The primary finite-row significance test compares the observed local tile shape against $N=2000$ distinct sampled alternatives, generated with seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite row should be read as a distinct-sample Monte Carlo comparison rather than as an unconstrained independent draw sequence.  Among the $2000$ sampled alternatives, exactly one matched the observed shape and $25$ reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed window-six tile is rare under this finite null ensemble both as an exact shape and as a high-Jaccard neighborhood.  The conclusion is local: the six-bit coordinate display exposes a statistically unusual codon-label tile relative to the sampled alternatives, while the labels themselves remain contacts with the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null.}
A second null keeps amino-acid class information fixed and asks whether the same shape behavior persists.  In that null, $N=2000$, the observed tile size is $|M|=20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the finite-row reading: the local $M$-shape is not merely an arbitrary artifact of preserving broad amino-acid classes.  The finite row does not include the explicit twenty-codon list for $M$, so this chapter cites $|M|=20$ and the associated null counts, but does not reconstruct an unprovided $M$ membership list.

The spectral fact is still a code-read fact; it does not license a claim that molecular systems are forced to obey this geometry as a biological necessity.

The family hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.  The leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These values say that the local tile-and-boundary display has strong code-label recovery behavior in the tested finite rows.

Every module has either predictable or boundary status.  This makes the row suitable as a local code-read certificate: the observed boundary is not merely a cardinality count, but a finite partition into biologically named recoding modules at the label layer.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The curated standard-code table supports codon-to-label readback and local split checks.  It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or a proof that DNA obeys the BEDC coordinate geometry as a law of nature.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation, not a folded-protein or function-layer observation.  Any claim crossing that boundary would require a separate contact, such as a translation experiment, structural assay, physical admissibility model, or function measurement.  The present chapter therefore records a finite, statistically sharp, window-six local tile in the standard-code label table, with its boundary modules and recovery behavior, while keeping all higher-layer biological interpretations blocked.
