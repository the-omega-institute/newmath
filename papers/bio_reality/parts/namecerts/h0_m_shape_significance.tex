Its internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data; its biological readback is the standard-code label table.  This chapter records a code-layer finite-row finding, not a mechanism of translation.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
For the finite-row shape test with seed $42$, the experiment used $N=2000$ sampled rows.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Equivalently, the recorded exact-shape significance value is $0.0005$, and the high-Jaccard significance value is $0.0125$.  These numbers support the claim that the observed $M$-shape is unusual under the stated finite sampling scheme.  They do not make the tile a universal biological law, and they do not identify a biochemical cause.
