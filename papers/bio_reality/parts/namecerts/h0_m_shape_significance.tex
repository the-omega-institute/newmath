\subsection{Window-six codon tile significance at the code-read layer}
\label{sec:biorality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The object studied in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon-cube display $Q_6$.  The finite carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is a coordinate device for asking whether the curated codon-label table exposes a local boundary-tile pattern.  The BEDC-minimal form has four visible distinctions: the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  Its internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation.  The biological readback is restricted to the standard-code label table: codons are assigned amino-acid or stop labels by the curated contact, and all subsequent finite tests are performed at that code-read layer.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the supplied finite run it is exactly
$R=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the row is not merely a cardinality statistic: the finite certificate records both the thirteen named codons and the induced combinatorial shape data.  The module partition of this same row is complete: $\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$ forms the $\mathrm{agr}$ module, $\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$ the $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ module, $\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$ the $\mathrm{cun}$ module, $\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$ the $\mathrm{ile\_met}$ module, $\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$ the $\mathrm{ssn}$ module, and $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$ the $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$ module.  The assigned count is $13$, with $R$-size $13$, no extra module codons, no missing module codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The finite significance run used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those $2000$ sampled rows there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The resulting empirical probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
 p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The certificate therefore treats the observed local tile as finite-row significant relative to this sampled null ensemble.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that run the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, the exact-shape probability was $0.0$, and the lower-tail probability was $0.0035$.  These numbers support a code-layer statement: the observed local shape is uncommon under the recorded randomized controls.  They do not by themselves identify a generative biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral certificate supplies three scalar values for the same finite object: $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial polynomial check reports value $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, also recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual.  Since this residual is numerically near zero at the stated precision, the spectrum acts as an internal consistency check for the coordinate-and-closure presentation of the row.  The spectral-radius-in-tolerance value is again $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$ in the supplied record.

\paragraph{Leave-one evidence.}
Two leave-one tests give the local tile an operational readback.  In the leave-one-code-out run, $23$ tables were evaluated and $63$ total targets were tested.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast, over $5$ families.  In the leave-one-codon-out run, the anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the closure-coverage-high count was $8$, the predictable count was $8$, and the recovery rate was $1.0$.  The recovery-rate-above-threshold value was also $1.0$.  These rows are naturally read as code-label recovery statistics, not as structural or functional assays.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only declared external contact is the curated standard genetic-code bridge.  Its observed fact is that a curated standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization.  Consequently, the finite object has a precise contact boundary: the table supports codon-label readback, and the six-bit geometry supports local split checks, but neither source supplies an experiment for translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The claim is ready only at the code-read layer.  The finite rows support the following local statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically uncommon local boundary-tile pattern in the curated standard-code label table, with $R$ a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row and with strong leave-one recovery at the label level.  The same record explicitly blocks promotion beyond that layer.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law; window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, or function; and amino-acid label-sequence readback is not protein structure.  Any future claim about translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality must attach a separate higher-layer reality contact before it can be stated in this paper.
