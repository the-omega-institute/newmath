\subsection{Window-Six Codon Shape Significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The carrier in this record is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code table as a finite code-layer contact.  The construction does not treat the codon table as a mechanism.  It treats the table as an externally supplied assignment from each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and asks whether the window-six coordinates expose a local boundary tile with unusually rigid finite shape.  The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal structure used here is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data, with readback only to the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA}, \mathrm{AGA}, \mathrm{AGG}, \mathrm{AUA}, \mathrm{CUA}, \mathrm{CUC}, \mathrm{CUG}, \\
&\mathrm{CUU}, \mathrm{UAA}, \mathrm{UAG}, \mathrm{UCA}, \mathrm{UGA}, \mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube boundary with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R) = (13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object is not merely a set of thirteen labels: it carries a local cubical incidence profile with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four square faces after the observed puncture.  This is the concrete finite row on which the shape-significance claim rests.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
For the $M$-shape test, the finite audit used $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$.  Obtaining those distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact $M$-shape occurred once among the $2000$ distinct null samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} = \frac{1}{2000} = 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A high-overlap test at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ found $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9} = \frac{25}{2000} = 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both finite numbers support the same local conclusion: the observed window-six tile is rare under the sampled coordinate null, with exact-shape rarity stronger than the high-Jaccard rarity.  The result is a shape statement about finite codon-label readback, not a claim that biological translation must obey this geometry.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null keeps amino-acid class structure in view.  In that audit the observed $M$ size was $20$, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  The exact-match count was $0$, so the recorded exact-shape frequency was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}} = 0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower}} = \frac{7}{2000} = 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This second test is important because it does not rely only on an unconstrained codon-coordinate shuffle.  Even after preserving amino-acid class information in the null model, the finite row remains in a small lower tail and has no exact sampled match.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported claim is only that window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically rare local tile and boundary pattern when read against the curated standard-code label table.  The contact tests code-layer readback and local codon-tile label split.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any law forcing DNA to obey this finite geometry.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function.  Any promotion beyond code readback requires a separate higher-layer reality contact.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite-row evidence is internally aligned: $R$ is a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary with face-vector $(13,16,4)$; the $M$ shape has exact sampled frequency $0.0005$ and high-Jaccard frequency $0.0125$ under the coordinate null; the amino-acid-class preserving null gives exact frequency $0.0$ and lower-tail frequency $0.0035$; the spectral row satisfies its polynomial check to about $6.23\times 10^{-12}$; and the leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes recover the same local organization with high finite hit rates.  The verdict is therefore a ready code-layer shape-significance statement, bounded by the curated codon-label contact and blocked from translation, folding, physical-admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality claims.
