\subsection{Window-six local tile significance}\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The record is a window-six codon-coordinate experiment on the codon cube $Q_6$, with the biological readback supplied only by a curated standard-code table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon display, and the relevant distinctions are a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal BEDC structure used here is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data; the external contact is only the codon-to-label table.  Thus the object under test is the standard-code label table at the $\mathrm{code\_read}$ layer, not translation dynamics, protein structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary relation $R$.}
The finite boundary row is the $13$-element codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independently recorded cardinality row gives $|R|=13$, with exactly the same codon list.  The punctured-cube row identifies this same relation with a finite cell pattern whose $f$-vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the concrete local tile whose significance is being tested.  The statement is deliberately finite: it is a shape in the codon-coordinate presentation together with a label-table readback, not a law about biological necessity.

\paragraph{Shape significance against the sampled null.}
For the primary shape-significance run, the experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparators.  Because the sampling was constrained to distinct rows, the run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  The observed exact shape reappeared once among the $2000$ comparators, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap test counted $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both reported significance rows therefore support the finite claim that the observed local tile is uncommon under the sampled coordinate null.  They do not identify a mechanism.  In particular, the exact-shape and high-Jaccard rows license only the claim that this codon-coordinate shape is statistically sharp relative to the stated finite sampling procedure.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second null keeps amino-acid class information in the randomization.  In that run, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the exact shape count was $0$, so the recorded exact-shape Monte Carlo value was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class, exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row records observed $M$-size $20$, lower-tail count $7$, and lower-tail value
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class, lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the finite reading: even when class information is preserved, the observed $M$-shape behavior remains in a small tail of the sampled distribution.  The record supplies the size of $M$ and its tail behavior, but not an explicit codon-by-codon $M$ list in this finite row.

The relevant reading is that the coordinate shape is not only a set-size anomaly: it also carries a stable finite spectral signature at numerical tolerance.  The residual is near zero at the displayed precision, so the spectral row is consistent with the same local tile rather than with an unrelated summary statistic.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite evidence supports the following restricted statement: in the six-bit codon-coordinate presentation, the standard-code readback exposes a statistically uncommon local boundary tile with $|R|=13$, $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, exact-shape value $0.0005$, high-Jaccard value $0.0125$, amino-acid-class-preserving lower-tail value $0.0035$, spectral radius $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, and complete module accounting over the $13$ relation codons.  The curated code table is a reality contact for code labels only.  Therefore no translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this row unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.
