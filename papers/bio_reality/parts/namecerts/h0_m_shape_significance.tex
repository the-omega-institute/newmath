\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the codon display obtained from the three nucleotide positions after binary expansion of the four-letter alphabet.  The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  In this chapter the contact is a code-layer readback device, not a translation experiment, not a folding experiment, and not a physical or functional assay.  The BEDC object under test is therefore a local boundary-tile claim inside $Q_6$: window-six coordinates may expose a non-random local split in the standard-code labels, while the labels themselves remain external reality contacts supplied by the curated table.

\paragraph{Shape significance under finite sampling.}
Under the unrestricted sampled null with seed $42$, the run used $N=2000$ distinct samples.  Producing these samples required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the comparison is visibly finite and discrete rather than asymptotic.  The exact observed shape appeared once among the $2000$ sampled rows, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap relaxation with Jaccard threshold $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row verdict is therefore not that the codon table obeys a universal geometric law, but that this particular local $M$-shape is rare under the sampled coordinate-preserving comparison used by the run.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null preserves amino-acid class information and again uses $N=2000$ with seed $42$.  In this stricter comparison the exact observed shape has count $0$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{class-preserving exact matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed $M$ size is $20$, and only $7$ class-preserving samples lie in the lower tail at or below that size.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{lower-tail samples with size}\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the local claim: after preserving the label-class constraint used by the null, the exact $M$-shape is not recovered in the finite sample, and the observed size lies in a small lower tail.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
The supported conclusion is strictly local.  The curated standard-code table supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  It does not test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, not structure, not physical admissibility, not function, and not universality.  Any promotion across those boundaries would require a separate reality contact designed for that higher layer.
