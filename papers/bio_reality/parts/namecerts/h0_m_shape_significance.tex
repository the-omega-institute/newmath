\subsection{Window-six local shape significance for the standard code}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The claim tested here is that the six-coordinate codon display exposes a non-random local boundary shape in the standard genetic-code label table. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read as the finite set of $64$ codon words over the usual four-letter nucleotide alphabet after a six-bit display has been fixed. The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The claim is therefore a code-layer claim about label readback and local tile shape, not a claim about translation dynamics, folded protein geometry, physical feasibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The finite row identifies the boundary region $R$ as a $13$-codon subset of the codon cube. The recorded list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded cardinality check gives $|R|=13$. The same row records that this set is a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object is not merely a bag of exceptional labels: it has a measured local cell structure inside the codon-coordinate display. The row also records an observed $M$-size of $20$ for the local shape comparison, although the supplied data do not enumerate the members of $M$ as codon strings.

\paragraph{Shape randomization test.}
The main finite significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison shapes. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, of which $55377$ were duplicate retries, so the sampled space is visibly constrained by the finite-shape conditions rather than behaving like unconstrained independent draws. In this run the exact observed shape occurred once:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap comparison used the threshold $J\geq 0.9$ and found $25$ matches:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the local statement that the observed window-six tile is uncommon under the sampled null model. They do not by themselves identify a biological cause, a translational mechanism, or a structural consequence.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second finite null model preserves amino-acid class information while randomizing the relevant shape comparison. This run also used seed $42$ and $N=2000$. It records zero exact matches:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{class-preserving exact matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
For the lower-tail comparison against the observed $M$-size $20$, the run records $7$ samples at or below the observed size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{lower-tail samples}\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is stronger than the unconditioned exact-shape row in one respect: even after respecting amino-acid class information, the observed local tile remains rare in the finite comparison set. The interpretation still remains at the code-label layer, because the contact is a codon-to-label table and not an experiment on ribosomes, molecules, structures, or phenotypes.

\paragraph{Spectral record.}
The same packet records a finite spectral summary for the local shape. The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check is numerically satisfied to tolerance, with recorded value
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This spectral row gives a compact numerical signature for the local codon tile. Its role is descriptive and comparative: it helps distinguish the observed coordinate pattern from nearby finite alternatives. It is not evidence that the coordinate geometry is a law of biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. These numbers show that the local shape is predictive for code-table label changes under the finite test, but the prediction is still a label-level prediction. It says that the curated label table and the window-six coordinates jointly organize certain local changes; it does not say that the organismal translation process, protein fold, or function has been measured.

The leave-one-codon-out row records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. In this finite row, every recovered codon is recovered either through predictability or through boundary placement. The result supports a local boundary account of the $13$-codon region $R$ rather than a claim that the tile is globally necessary across biology.

\paragraph{Family and module structure.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out row groups the finite comparison into $5$ families. The hit rates above baseline are recorded as
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The module classification covers all $13$ codons in $R$, with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons. The recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\

$$
The classification row records $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and assigned count $13$. This completes the finite bookkeeping for the boundary region: the codons in $R$ are not left as isolated anomalies, but are partitioned into named modules that account for the entire observed boundary set.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite evidence supports the following narrow conclusion. In the six-coordinate codon display, the standard-code label table determines a local boundary tile whose exact shape is rare in the sampled comparison run, whose high-overlap variants are still uncommon, whose amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison remains significant, whose spectrum gives a stable numerical signature, and whose $13$-codon boundary admits a complete module classification. This is enough for a local code-read claim about the standard-code table. It is not enough for any higher-layer biological claim. No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universal law follows from these rows unless a separate contact directly supplies that higher-layer observation.
