\subsection{Window-six local tile shape for the standard code}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code label table.  The contact used here is deliberately narrow: it is a curated codon-to-label table, so it supports code-layer readback and local label-split checks.  It does not by itself observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of origin, or universality.  In this chapter the object called a local tile is therefore a finite row pattern inside $Q_6$, not a global law of biology.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is recorded as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local geometry seen by the probe is not merely a set-size event.  It has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the induced cube display.  The module classification accounts for all thirteen row codons, with no missing codons, no unassigned codons, and no extra module codons.

The thirteen boundary codons split into the following finite modules:

The classification totals are $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$ and $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$.  This is the finite-row sense in which the tile is complete: every listed boundary codon is assigned to one of the modules above, and every module has either predictable or boundary status in the leave-one-codon analysis.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
Against $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$, the exact local shape appears once.  The resulting exact-shape probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appear $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  These counts matter because they show that the finite null comparison is being made against distinct sampled rows rather than against a stream counted with duplicates.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-shape match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The corresponding finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support the claim that the observed local tile is scarce under the stated finite null models.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.

The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates are
$$

\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\

The leave-one-codon-out row has $\mathrm{anchor\_count}=0$, $\mathrm{boundary\_count}=5$, $\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high}=8$, $\mathrm{predictable\_count}=8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the finite probe sees a strong local recovery pattern, but that pattern remains a recovery pattern over codon labels.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and boundary of claim.}
The finite-row conclusion is that the standard-code label table exposes a statistically scarce local window-six tile with a thirteen-codon boundary row, a punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral signature, and high leave-one-out recovery at the code-read layer.  The result is a statement about coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation in the codon-label display.  It is not a statement that DNA obeys this geometry by biological necessity.  No translation process, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or origin mechanism follows from this contact.  Any such higher-layer claim requires a separate reality contact that observes the higher layer directly.
