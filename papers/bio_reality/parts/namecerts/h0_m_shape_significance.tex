\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite readback problem over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a mechanism for translation and is not a physical model of the ribosome.  It is a coordinate display in which each codon occupies one vertex of a finite cube, and the only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Thus the admissible readback layer is code read: codon labels, singleton label corners, local tile faces, and three-point label classes may be compared against the curated table, while translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, and universality remain outside the claim.

$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube boundary with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}

This is a finite combinatorial observation about the six-bit display.  It says that the selected code-label boundary has $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional tile faces inside the local coordinate presentation.  It does not say that biology is forced to realize this geometry, nor that the geometry is a global law of the genetic code.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape-significance audit sampled $N=2000$ distinct comparison rows with seed $42$.  Because the distinctness constraint was strong, the run made $57377$ sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape was observed once among the $2000$ comparison rows, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ shape\ matches}\}&=1.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap comparison at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ produced $25$ matches, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125,\\
\#\{J\geq 0.9\ \mathrm{matches}\}&=25.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrow statement that the observed local tile shape is uncommon under the sampled finite-row null model.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause, a translational process, a protein structure, or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second finite null model preserved amino-acid class information.  In this audit the observed $M$-row size was $20$, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  The exact-shape count was $0$, giving $p=0.0$ at the recorded resolution, while the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035,\\
\#\{\mathrm{lower\ tail}\leq |M|\}&=7,\\
|M|&=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the code-layer reading: the local geometry is not merely a generic artifact of relabeling within amino-acid classes in the tested finite row model.  The conclusion remains bounded by the same contact.  A codon-to-label table can test a codon label split; it cannot test folded structure, physical admissibility, or biological function.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The boundary condition for this record is strict.  The curated standard-code table supports only codon-label readback and local split checks.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of code evolution, or a universality theorem.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any future claim crossing one of those boundaries must attach a separate reality contact appropriate to that layer; without such a contact, the present result remains a finite code-layer observation about $Q_6$, $M$, $R$, their local tile geometry, and their standard-code label readback.
