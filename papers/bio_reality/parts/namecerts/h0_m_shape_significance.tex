\subsection{Window-Six Local Tile Significance for the Standard Code}\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns a finite code-read object: the standard-code codon table read through a six-bit codon display, with the codon cube $Q_6$ as carrier. The BEDC-minimal form used here has coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation structure, and its readback is the curated standard-code label table. The object is not a model of translation dynamics. It is a finite row test asking whether the observed local tile and boundary split in the codon-label table is unusually shaped relative to finite null samples.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the finite shape contains $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ square faces in the local codon display. The finite-row data also report an observed $M$-row size of $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison; no separate member-by-member $M$ list is supplied in this packet, so the chapter uses the verified $|M|=20$ datum and does not invent an unverified membership table.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The primary finite-row significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ distinct null samples. Producing those $2000$ distinct samples required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. Among the $2000$ samples, the exact observed shape occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Equivalently, the exact-shape significant row is recorded as $0.0005$, and the high-Jaccard significant row is recorded as $0.0125$. This supports a narrow statement: the observed local codon-tile shape is rare under the sampled finite null model. It does not by itself identify a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second null run preserves amino-acid class information and again uses seed $42$ with $N=2000$. In that comparison the exact-match count is $0$, while the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$. With observed $|M|=20$, the reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers say that the observed local row is also unusual after preserving the amino-acid class constraint used by the null. The result remains a code-read result: the table supplies labels, not chemistry, folding, admissible physical states, or function.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The finite adjacency spectrum reports three scalar quantities:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius-in-tolerance row repeats $0.6752479308830213$. The residual is effectively a numerical consistency check for the finite computation. It is not evidence that the codon cube is a law of biology; it is evidence that this particular finite coordinate package has a stable internal spectral readback.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, while the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The above-baseline hit rate was also $0.9841269841269841$. Broken down by family over $5$ families, the above-baseline hit rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. These rows are useful because they test whether the local tile continues to locate changed code labels in nearby finite variants of the genetic-code table.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out readback.}
The leave-one-codon-out row records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The recovery-rate-above-threshold row is also $1.0$. This means that, within the supplied finite test, every held-out codon target is recovered either by the predictable part of the closure computation or by the boundary allowance. This is a finite table property, not a claim about translation being generated by the geometry.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The boundary row is completely assigned by the module classification. The classification reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has predictable-or-boundary status in the supplied finite row. This supplies a compact readback of how the $13$ boundary codons are organized, and it explains why the shape test is not merely a cardinality test.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of claim.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label split. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, universal mechanism, or folded protein structure. Therefore the local verdict is deliberately limited: the window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically unusual local tile and a coherent boundary-module structure in the curated code-label table, with $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge0.9}=0.0125$, and amino-acid-class lower-tail value $0.0035$. No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
