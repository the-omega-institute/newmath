\subsection{Window-six local shape significance for the standard-code table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The claim concerns the standard-code codon table after it is placed on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$. The six-bit display is not treated as a biochemical mechanism. It is a finite coordinate instrument for asking whether the curated codon-to-label table has a local boundary shape that is rare under explicit finite null shuffles. The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels. Thus the object under test is a code-layer readback: codons are observed as labels in a table, and the window-six geometry supplies a finite neighborhood system in which local tiles, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes can be counted.

\paragraph{Boundary set $R$.}
The finite boundary set recovered in the run has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same set is reported by the punctured-cube check. Its finite face data are
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed boundary is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it also has a concrete local tile incidence profile inside $Q_6$. The module classification covers every codon of $R$ with no omissions and no extras: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, missing-from-module set empty, extra-module set empty, and unassigned set empty. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}, &
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}, &
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}, &
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}, &
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite shape whose statistical rarity is measured below.

\paragraph{Observed $M$ size and spectral scale.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null check records observed $M$ size $20$. The spectral row records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual. This places the numerical spectral calculation within the tolerance of the reported finite polynomial check while keeping the interpretation local: the spectrum summarizes the codon-window graph data and does not assert a biochemical force, a translation process, or a structural law.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under the finite null.}
For claim $h0.M.shape.significance$, the exact-shape experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ null samples. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported sample size is a distinct finite sample set rather than a count of raw attempts. Exactly one null sample matched the observed shape:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ shape\ matches}\} &= 1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap check counted $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\} &= 25,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both rows support the same local conclusion: the observed window-six tile is uncommon among the sampled null shapes. The exact probability estimate is sharper, while the high-Jaccard row shows that near-matches are also sparse but not absent.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving control.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information. With seed $42$ and $N=2000$, it produced exact match count $0$, hence reported exact-shape value $0.0$ for this finite run. It also recorded $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This control is important because it tests whether the local shape can be dismissed as a trivial consequence of preserving broad amino-acid class information. In the recorded run, the class-preserving shuffle still rarely lands at the observed scale or sharper.

\paragraph{Predictive checks inside the code layer.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$. Family-level rows preserve this pattern: alternative yeast has hit rate above baseline $0.8333333333333334$, and bacterial/plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate above baseline $1.0$. The leave-one-codon-out row records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These rows do not promote the finding beyond the code table. They say that, after one code or codon entry is held out, the finite local representation often recovers the omitted code-layer target or places it on the recorded boundary.

\paragraph{Local scientific verdict.}
The finite evidence supports the following chapter-level statement. In the standard-code table, the window-six codon coordinate exposes a rare local boundary-tile configuration: the set $R$ has $13$ codons, a punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, complete module assignment, a stable numerical spectral profile, and low empirical frequencies under both unrestricted and amino-acid-class-preserving finite nulls. The most direct empirical numbers are $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$, and $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.

\paragraph{Local boundary of interpretation.}
This chapter does not claim that the six-coordinate codon cube is a biological necessity. It does not claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or a global law of biology. Amino-acid label readback remains a code-layer observation. Any assertion about translation, folding, chemistry, organismal function, or universality would require a separate contact that directly observes that higher layer. The present result is therefore a finite, table-level significance statement about a local codon-tile boundary in the curated standard-code labels.
