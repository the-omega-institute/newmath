\subsection{Window-six codon tile significance}\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
The record uses the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ as its finite carrier.  The external contact is a curated standard-code table, used only as a codon-to-label bridge: each codon is read as an amino-acid label or as a stop label.  The BEDC-side distinctions are the six-bit display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological object is therefore the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer, not translation dynamics and not a protein-structure experiment.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is detected as a punctured-cube shape with finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite observation is not just a cardinality count: it specifies a local codon-window shape inside $Q_6$ and a concrete list of codons that realizes the shape.

\paragraph{Observed shape significance.}
The primary shape test used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ randomized samples.  Because duplicate candidate samples occurred often, the run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape appeared once among the $2000$ accepted samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\widehat p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A weaker high-overlap threshold, Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\widehat p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape row is therefore finite-row significant under this sampling design, and the high-Jaccard row is also sparse enough to mark the window-six tile as a non-generic local feature of the curated label table.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class information.  In that run, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$-size was $20$.  The exact-shape count was $0$, so the sampled exact-shape value was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\widehat p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\widehat p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the same local conclusion from a label-class-preserving comparison: the observed finite tile is not readily reproduced by the tested null while holding amino-acid class structure fixed.

These rows do not make the local tile into a biological law; they say that the same code-read geometry gives strong finite recovery behavior across the tested table families.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and module coverage.}
The codon-level leave-one-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification accounted for all $13$ codons in $R$: the assigned count was $13$, with no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\

Each module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the recorded classification.  The row-level conclusion is therefore a finite module statement about the local codon tile.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks only.  No translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from these finite rows.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded structure.  To cross any of those boundaries, a separate higher-layer contact would be required, with its own observed facts and its own mismatch tests.  The present verdict is only that the curated standard-code table, read through the six-bit codon window, exhibits a sparse and recoverable local tile with $|R|=13$, observed $M$-size $20$, exact-shape sampling value $0.0005$, high-Jaccard sampling value $0.0125$, and complete module accounting over the listed boundary codons.
