\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biological mechanism; it is a coordinate display in which codons are read as finite addresses and then compared against a curated standard-code label table.  The admissible distinctions in this row are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal structure used by the record is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels at code-read resolution.

The module classification covers all $13$ codons: $\mathrm{AGR}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{AR\_Lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{CUN}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{Ile\_Met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{SSN}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$. \paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only $1$ sampled row matched the exact observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ sampled rows reaching Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The finite-row conclusion is therefore not that the codon table is forced by the window-six geometry, but that this particular local tile is uncommon under the sampled comparison protocol.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, found $0$ exact shape matches, and counted $7$ rows in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size $20$.  Its recorded values are $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These numbers support a code-layer shape distinction while remaining finite-sample statistics rather than a mechanism claim.

The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate, across $5$ recorded families. These recovery rows say that the local code-read split is stable under the recorded omissions; they do not promote the split beyond the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite row has one permitted contact: a curated standard-code table assigning codons to labels.  Consequently, the chapter may claim only code-read structure, local codon-tile label split behavior, finite closure behavior, and the associated sampled significance values.  It may not claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function.  A local codon tile is not a universal biological law.  Any claim crossing those boundaries requires a separate reality contact designed for that layer.
