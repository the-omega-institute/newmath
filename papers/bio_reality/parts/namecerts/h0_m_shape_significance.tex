\subsection{Window-six shape significance for the standard-code tile}\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This BioReality record concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the finite display obtained by reading each codon as a coordinate word and then attaching the curated standard-code label supplied by the external table.  The distinguished row for the boundary set $R$ contains exactly $13$ codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row verifies $|R|=13$ and identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube boundary pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the observed object is a local codon-label tile in $Q_6$ with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces.  The contact itself is only the curated codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop table; it supports label readback and local split checks, not translation dynamics.

\paragraph{Observed shape statistic.}
The shape-significance row used $N=2000$ distinct sampled comparison shapes with seed $42$.  Because the comparison generator rejected many duplicates, it made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the $2000$ accepted samples, exactly $1$ matched the observed shape exactly, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap check counted $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These two finite-row probabilities are the chapter-level evidence for the claim $h0.M.shape.significance$: the observed window-six tile is rare under the sampled coordinate-shape null model.  The word ``rare'' here is deliberately finite and operational.  It means one exact match and twenty-five high-Jaccard matches in the stated $2000$ accepted samples; it does not mean that the tile is forced by biology.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null row.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid label class.  In that row the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$.  The recorded finite probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the same local conclusion from a different control: after preserving label class, the exact observed shape was not recovered in the accepted finite sample, and only seven sampled rows were at or below the observed $M$-size.  The data supplied for this chapter records the scalar $|M|=20$ but does not provide the member list of $M$, so the chapter states the size and not an invented enumeration.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The result is a code-read statement about a finite codon-label table.  It does not claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation or function.  A separate higher-layer contact would be required before any statement about translation dynamics, molecular folding, admissible physical states, biological activity, or universal mechanism could be made.
