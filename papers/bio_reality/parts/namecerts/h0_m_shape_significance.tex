\subsection{Window-six codon tile and code-label boundary}\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record treats the standard genetic code only as a codon-to-label table.  Each codon is displayed as a six-coordinate object, and the finite question is whether the induced local tile separates a small exceptional boundary from the remaining code labels in a way that is rare under controlled relabeling.  The biological contact used here is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is sufficient for label readback and for finite set comparisons inside the codon cube, but it is not an observation of translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal law.

\paragraph{Finite sets.}
The exceptional row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector data
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the local codon display is not merely a list of labels: it has a finite adjacency and face structure visible at the code-read layer.  The supplied finite row does not enumerate the full $M$ codon set explicitly, but it records the observed $M$-size as $20$ in the amino-acid-class preserving null experiment.  The chapter therefore treats $|M|=20$ and the reported shape statistics as the available $M$ data, rather than silently inventing a missing codon list.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Under the shape randomization run with seed $42$, the sample size was $N=2000$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among these $2000$ sampled rows, exactly one matched the observed shape, while $25$ had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The recorded probabilities are
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
Equivalently, the exact-shape significance field is $0.0005$, and the high-Jaccard significance field is $0.0125$.  The finite evidence therefore says that the observed local tile is uncommon in this null ensemble.  It does not say that codons are generated by the geometry, and it does not raise the local tile into a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A separate null model preserved amino-acid class.  In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact match count was $0$.  The observed $M$-size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below that observed size was $7$.  The corresponding recorded values are
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class, exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class, lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
This strengthens the finite-row reading: even after respecting amino-acid class, the observed local arrangement remains exceptional by the reported tests.  The result is still a code-table statement.  It does not identify a ribosomal process, a tRNA mechanism, a folding constraint, or a functional advantage.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The verified rows support the following restricted conclusion.  In a six-coordinate codon display of the curated standard-code table, the reported $R$ row is a $13$-codon punctured-cube tile with face vector $(13,16,4)$, the available $M$ evidence records $|M|=20$, the exact-shape and high-Jaccard randomization probabilities are $0.0005$ and $0.0125$, the amino-acid-class preserving lower-tail probability is $0.0035$, and the leave-one-out tests show strong finite recovery within the evaluated label tables.  These facts make the local tile a statistically and combinatorially specific code-layer object.  They do not, by themselves, license any claim about translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such stronger statement would require a separate contact measuring that higher layer directly.
