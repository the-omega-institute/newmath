\paragraph{Shape significance row.}  The shape-significance probe used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled null rows.  Because distinct samples were enforced, the run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape occurred once among the $2000$ null rows, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}=1,\qquad p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}$$
A high-overlap relaxation at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\ge 0.9\}=25,\qquad p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
The finite-row conclusion is that the local tile shape is rare under this null experiment.  The conclusion is deliberately local: it says that the observed code-label geometry has a significant finite shape signature in the window-six carrier, not that the carrier explains translation, molecular dynamics, or function.
