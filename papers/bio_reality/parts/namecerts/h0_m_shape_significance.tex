\subsection{Window-six local tile significance in the standard-code table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The carrier used here is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$: each codon is read as a six-bit display coordinate, and the only biological readback contact admitted in this chapter is the curated standard-code label table. The internal BEDC-side distinctions are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the external contact supplies codon-to-label assignments at the code layer only. The observed local tile is therefore not a statement about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. It is a finite-row code-read phenomenon: a local boundary pattern in $Q_6$ is compared against the curated standard-code labels, and no higher biological layer is inferred without a separate contact.

\paragraph{Boundary relation row.}
The finite relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the following codon set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R={}&\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
|R|={}&13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it has a concrete finite cubical shape, with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the induced row geometry.

\paragraph{Shape significance row.}
The shape-significance experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled null rows. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported null comparison is a finite sampled comparison against distinct candidate rows rather than a symbolic asymptotic calculation. The observed exact shape appeared once among the $2000$ rows, while $25$ rows reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
\#\{\hbox{exact shape matches}\}&=1,\\
\#\{\hbox{Jaccard}\geq 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the chapter's central finite-row claim. In this null ensemble, the exact local shape is rare at the $5\times 10^{-4}$ level, and high-overlap approximations are still rare at the $1.25\times 10^{-2}$ level. The conclusion is deliberately local: window-six geometry exposes a significant boundary-tile row inside the standard-code label table, not a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second null row preserves amino-acid class information. With the same seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$-side size is $20$. The exact-shape count is $0$, while the lower tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\hbox{class-preserving exact matches}\}&=0,\\
\#\{\hbox{rows with } |M'|\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving null therefore does not erase the row. It strengthens the interpretation that the detected split is not just a generic artifact of preserving broad amino-acid categories, while still remaining a finite code-read comparison against a curated table.

The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{>\mathrm{baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{M-T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\

$$
The leave-one-codon-out row gives anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These rows show that the local boundary construction has finite predictive stability under leave-one perturbations, but only within the code-read layer.

Every module has a predictable-or-boundary status in the finite row. This makes the boundary split inspectable at the codon-module level rather than only as an aggregate statistic.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The contact named by this chapter is a curated standard genetic-code bridge. It observes that a standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels, with resolution at codon-label readback. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization. Consequently the row supports the following bounded conclusion: window-six coordinates expose a statistically rare and module-complete local tile in the standard-code label table. They do not show that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as biological necessity; they do not infer protein function from a codon-tile split; they do not promote label readback to folded structure; and they do not establish a universal mechanism. Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate higher-layer reality contact.
