\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Significance}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only through the finite standard-code label table.  Each codon is treated as a coordinate in the codon display, and the biological contact is the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact supports code-layer readback and local label-split checks.  It does not by itself observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The measured row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite shape claim is not that the standard code is forced by geometry, but that the observed code table contains a concrete thirteen-codon local boundary row inside the window-six display.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the reported null sample with seed $42$, the experiment evaluated $N=2000$ distinct randomized shapes after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was met $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row reading is therefore a small-tail observation for this local tile: exact agreement is rare in the sampled null ensemble, and near-agreement remains uncommon.  This is a code-read significance statement, not a biological necessity statement.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null keeps amino-acid class structure while testing the shape.  In this run $N=2000$, the observed tile size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the same local conclusion under a stricter label-aware randomization: the observed tile is not a generic outcome of preserving the broad amino-acid class partition.

\paragraph{Spectral readback.}
The measured spectral row records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check.  The spectral-radius tolerance readback is $0.6752479308830213$.  These scalars give a finite numerical signature for the local tile, but they remain signatures of the coordinate-and-label construction rather than measurements of molecular dynamics or protein structure.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Inside $M\setminus T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary it was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  These finite rows indicate that the local window-six tile has strong label-readback stability across the tested code-table family, while still staying inside the code layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The leave-one-codon-out run reports no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases.  Its recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value reported above threshold.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has predictable-or-boundary status, so the finite row is internally closed at the level of the reported module partition.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The window-six construction exposes a statistically sharp local boundary tile in the standard-code readback.  The finite evidence consists of a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row, small exact and high-overlap null probabilities, a label-aware null with no exact matches, a stable spectral signature, strong leave-one-code-out recovery, and a complete leave-one-codon-out module classification.  The claim is therefore a local code-read claim: curated code labels meet a coordinate tile in $Q_6$.  No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity of the genetic code follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
