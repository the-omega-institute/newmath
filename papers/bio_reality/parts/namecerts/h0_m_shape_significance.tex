\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The record studies the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the $64$ codon vertices, together with the curated standard-code bridge that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC-minimal form used here has four visible distinctions: the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  Its internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The finite row is therefore not a theory of translation or protein realization.  It is a finite shape test on the label table.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified table it is exactly
$\{\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}$.
The punctured-cube computation returns the same codon set and the finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed relation is not merely a count; it is a concrete finite subshape of the codon cube.  The row supports a local boundary-tile reading: the set $R$ is large enough to contain several codon families, but small enough to behave as a punctured local object rather than as the whole cube.

\paragraph{Monte Carlo shape test.}
The primary significance row sampled $N=2000$ distinct comparison shapes using seed $42$.  Because duplicates were rejected, the run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  The observed shape was matched exactly once among the $2000$ accepted samples, and $25$ samples reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The reported finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
 p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same values are recorded as $p\_\mathrm{value\_exact\_shape\_significant}=0.0005$ and $p\_\mathrm{value\_high\_jaccard\_significant}=0.0125$.  This supports a code-layer claim: within the chosen finite null, the observed local tile is uncommon.  It does not by itself identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge.  Its observed fact is that a curated standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Its resolution is codon label readback.  The contact can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits; it cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization.  Consequently the finite-row verdict is local.  Window-six coordinates expose a statistically uncommon and module-complete boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.
