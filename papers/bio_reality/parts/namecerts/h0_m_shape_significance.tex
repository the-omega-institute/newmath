\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a standard-code label table. The finite display is not a biochemical mechanism; it is a code-layer coordinate object whose external contact is the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels. The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class. The internal structure used here is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data, with readback restricted to the standard-code label table. Thus the claim is a $code\_read$ claim: window-six coordinates may expose a local boundary-tile structure, while the labels themselves remain supplied by external biological curation.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The Monte Carlo comparison for $h0.M.shape.significance$ used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ accepted samples. Producing those samples required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries. In that sample, the exact observed shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{exact}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row therefore supports a finite, statistically sharp local-shape statement: the observed window-six tile is rare under the sampled shape null, and near-matches remain uncommon. The record does not claim that the coordinate geometry causes the genetic code; it only records that this particular finite codon-label contact has a non-generic local shape in the tested coordinate system.

The packet does not include the explicit codon list for $M$, so the chapter treats $M$ by its reported shape statistics and size rather than by an enumerated vertex set.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The contact for this chapter is the curated standard-code table, and its observed fact is only that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels. Consequently the supported conclusion is local: a window-six codon tile, together with its $R$ row, spectral values, null-model significance, and leave-one-out behavior, gives a finite code-read pattern. No translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or law of biology follows from this row. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate reality contact, not merely a stronger reading of the present finite geometry.
