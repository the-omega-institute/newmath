\paragraph{Contact boundary.}
The only reality contact used in this chapter is the curated standard-code table: it assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The contact can test code-read behavior and a local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, structural order, mechanism, or universality.  The local tile pattern must therefore remain at the $code\_read$ layer unless a separate higher-layer contact is supplied.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a rare window-six shape is not by itself a claim about physical realization or biological function.
