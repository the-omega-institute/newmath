\subsection{Window-six codon tile shape and code-layer significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The record is formed on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, where a standard-code codon is displayed as a six-bit window and is then read only through its curated amino-acid or stop label. The construction has four local distinctions: the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class. These distinctions organize coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data, but they do not by themselves supply a biochemical process. The biological contact is the standard genetic-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label and therefore supports code-layer readback only.

\paragraph{Observed exceptional relation.}
The finite relation $R$ has cardinality $13$. In the supplied row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies this set with a punctured-cube pattern whose face-vector is $(13,16,4)$. Thus the local object is not merely a list of codons: it carries a finite cubical boundary profile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces. The module partition supplied for $R$ is complete: $13$ of $13$ relation codons are assigned, with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},
&\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},

$$
Every listed module has either a predictable codon or a boundary codon under the leave-one-codon classification.

\paragraph{Shape test.}
The primary shape experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ distinct null samples. It required $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported null ensemble was not a trivial repeated draw. Among the $2000$ distinct samples, the exact observed shape occurred once and the high-overlap condition $J\geq 0.9$ occurred $25$ times. Therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=1/2000=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite row records both values as significant shape statistics. A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, observed $|M|=20$, no exact shape matches, and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size. Its reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This comparison matters because it keeps the signal at the finite code-label layer: the shape is rare under the sampled nulls, including a null that preserves amino-acid class, but rarity is not the same thing as a mechanistic explanation.

These numbers give the row a reproducible finite spectral signature. They do not identify a folding energy, a translation rate, a tRNA abundance law, or a universal biochemical invariant.

\paragraph{Prediction probes at the code layer.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the recorded above-baseline hit rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, across $5$ families. The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These data support the claim that the local window-six tile has predictive structure for code-label recovery within the supplied finite tests.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The evidence supports a code-read statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a rare local boundary-tile shape in the curated standard-code label table. The strongest finite signs are the $13$-codon punctured-cube relation with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, the exact-shape probability $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$, the amino-acid-class lower-tail probability $0.0035$, the spectral triple $(0.6752479308830213,0.4758352843197002,3.051487585298128)$, and the leave-one recovery rates near or equal to $1$ in the reported probes. The claim is deliberately narrower than a biological mechanism claim. From these rows alone one may not infer translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, organismal universality, or a general law of genetic-code evolution. Any such promotion would require a separate empirical contact whose observation is about that higher layer rather than about codon-to-label readback.
