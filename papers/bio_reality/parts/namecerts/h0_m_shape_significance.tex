\subsection{Window-six codon tile significance at the code-read layer}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record studies the standard-code codon table only as a finite code-read object.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, equipped with the displayed codon labels supplied by the curated standard genetic-code table.  The BEDC-side distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The biological readback is therefore not translation itself, but the finite table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact used here is explicitly limited to codon-label readback.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The row is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is certified as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed code-read pattern is not merely a count of codons: it also carries a local combinatorial shape, with thirteen vertices, sixteen row-adjacencies, and four two-dimensional tile faces in the reported punctured-cube representation.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape-significance experiment for $M$ used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled finite rows.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the $2000$ sampled rows, exactly one matched the observed shape exactly, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ shape\ matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A high-overlap threshold was also reported: $25$ sampled rows had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row conclusion is therefore a local code-read statement: under this sampling protocol, the observed $M$-shape is rare at the exact-shape level and still uncommon at the high-Jaccard level.  This is evidence for a finite local tile boundary in the codon-label table, not evidence for any higher biological layer.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In that experiment the observed $M$ size is $20$, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  The exact-shape match count is $0$, so the reported exact-shape tail value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This null result is compatible with the same local reading: after preserving amino-acid class structure, the observed tile remains small and shape-specific relative to the finite sampled background.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The reported hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast, across $5$ families.  These rows say that the local tile and boundary features recover many held-out code-label targets relative to the baseline, while remaining within the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The recovery rate above threshold is also $1.0$.  The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing module codons, and unassigned codons are all empty.  The complete module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}

Every listed module has a predictable or boundary member, and together these six modules exhaust the thirteen codons in $R$.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The claim supported by these finite rows is narrow and positive: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically uncommon local boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code label table.  The exact-shape value $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, the high-Jaccard value $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$, the amino-acid-class preserving lower-tail value $0.0035$, the punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, the complete thirteen-codon module partition, and the leave-one-code-out hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ all point to the same finite code-read phenomenon.
