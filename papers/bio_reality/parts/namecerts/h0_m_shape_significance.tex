\subsection{Window-Six Shape Significance for the Standard-Code Codon Table}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The claim $h0.M.shape.significance$ concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, and the observed object is not a biochemical mechanism but a code-layer label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label by the curated standard genetic code. The finite question is whether the window-six coordinate presentation exposes a local boundary-tile pattern whose shape is unusually specific under row-level null sampling. The relevant distinctions are the coordinate display itself, local tile faces in the cube, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The readback stays at codon-label resolution.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The verified punctured-cube residue set $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run verifies that this set has cubical face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the record is not merely a list of labels: it carries a finite cubical shape inside $Q_6$. The module accounting also closes on exactly these $13$ codons. The assigned modules are $agr=\{AGA,AGG\}$, $ar\_lys=\{AAA\}$, $cun=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, $ile\_met=\{AUA\}$, $ssn=\{UCA,UUA\}$, and $stop\_trp=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$. The module audit reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The primary finite-row experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ accepted samples. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and rejected $55377$ duplicate retries. Against this null ensemble, the exact observed shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact matches}\}&=1,\\
p\_{exact}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A relaxed comparison using Jaccard threshold $0.9$ found $25$ high-overlap matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25,\\
p\_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row supports a specific statement: in this coordinate system, the standard-code label table produces a local tile shape that is rare under the sampled null model. The record does not say that this geometry causes the code, only that the curated label table has a non-generic window-six shape at the tested resolution.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class structure. It also used seed $42$ and $N=2000$. The observed $M$ size was $20$. The exact-shape count was $0$, while $7$ samples landed in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size. The corresponding values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p\_{\text{class exact}}&=0.0,\\
p\_{\text{class lower tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the finite reading: the phenomenon is not only an artifact of an unrestricted shuffle. Even when the sampling respects the amino-acid class partition, the observed shape remains an unusually constrained code-layer pattern.

\paragraph{Spectral check.}
The same finite presentation has a numerical spectral profile. The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual was $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, matching the reported tolerance value for the cubic relation. This gives an independent numerical fingerprint of the same finite geometry: the coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data agree on the local code-read object.

\paragraph{Predictive readback checks.}
Leave-one-code-out testing evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. Within $M-T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and adding the boundary gave $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the above-baseline rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast. Leave-one-codon-out testing found $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, $8$ high-coverage closures, and recovery rate $1.0$. These checks are consistent with the shape-significance record: the boundary tile is useful for code-label recovery in this finite table regime.

\paragraph{Scope limit.}
The biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table. That contact observes codon-to-label assignments only. It does not observe translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of origin, or a universal law. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not evidence of protein structure. Cross-organism usage patterns and tRNA-copy questions require their own organism-matched contacts. The valid conclusion is narrow: window-six coordinates expose a statistically rare local boundary-tile shape in the standard-code label table, with verified residue, module, spectral, and prediction rows. Any claim beyond code-layer readback must wait for a separate empirical contact at the higher layer.
