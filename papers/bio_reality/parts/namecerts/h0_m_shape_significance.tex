\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Boundary for the Standard Code}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The object studied here is the standard-code codon label table viewed through a six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The finite display keeps four pieces of structure separate: the coordinate placement of the codons, the local closure pattern of a tile face, the spectrum of the associated finite relation, and the final readback into curated amino-acid or stop labels.  The point of the construction is not that the cube creates the genetic code.  The point is narrower: after the curated standard-code labels are placed on the $64$ codon positions, the window-six coordinates expose a sharply localized boundary tile whose shape is rare under the tested finite null models.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified finite table it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same computation records the face-vector profile
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed boundary is not merely a list of codons but a finite punctured-cube pattern with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces.  This is the local geometric datum used by the chapter.  It is a code-layer datum only: it concerns codon labels and adjacency in the displayed cube, not ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, biochemical admissibility, organismal function, or a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The direct shape test used $N=2000$ sampled comparison tables with seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the accepted comparison rows, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding empirical probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed local tile is not common in this finite comparison ensemble.  The exact-shape statistic is the strongest row-level signal, while the high-Jaccard statistic says that even near matches are uncommon rather than absent.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second finite null model preserved amino-acid class information.  In that test the observed $M$-side size was $20$, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  The exact-match count was $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This makes the signal robust to a stricter label-aware comparison: the observed configuration is not recovered as an ordinary consequence of preserving amino-acid classes in this sampled row family.

\paragraph{Spectral check.}
The finite relation also has a recorded spectral profile.  The verified values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated cubic constraint was evaluated at approximately
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is within numerical tolerance for the recorded finite computation.  The spectral row therefore agrees with the same local boundary description rather than providing an unrelated statistic.  It is still only an internal consistency and shape-strength measurement for the codon-label display.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
The cross-table test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
whereas the hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By code-family, the five recorded family rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support a limited readback claim: the local shape has predictive contact with codon-label rows under the tested leave-one-code-out protocol.  They do not certify a molecular process.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The codon-level deletion test recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases.  Its recovery rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
which also exceeded the recorded threshold.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ boundary codons with no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The verified modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The count identity is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
2+1+4+1+2+3=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
This closes the finite module accounting for the row: every boundary codon is assigned to one of the observed modules, and every module contributes codons already present in $R$.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only outside contact used in this chapter is the curated standard genetic-code table as a codon-to-label source.  That table supplies the amino-acid and stop labels needed for code-layer readback.  It does not supply a translation apparatus, a fold observation, a thermodynamic admissibility test, a functional assay, or a universality theorem.  Therefore the claim is deliberately bounded: window-six coordinates may reveal a rare local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table, and the associated finite probes show strong row-level and deletion-test behavior.  No conclusion about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or biological universality follows from these rows unless a separate contact supplies the missing layer.
