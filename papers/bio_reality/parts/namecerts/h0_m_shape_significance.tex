\subsection{Window-Six Codon Shape Significance}
\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Claim packet.}
This packet records a code-layer observation about the standard genetic-code table in a six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  A codon is treated as a finite coordinate word in the six-bit display, and the observed label table is used only as a curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The internal data of the packet consist of the codon coordinates, the closure pattern induced by the selected row, the spectral quantities attached to that pattern, and the relation between the selected row and the surrounding codon cube.  The biological readback is therefore narrow: it concerns the standard-code label table and the local split that appears when the observed labels are placed on $Q_6$.

\paragraph{Observed row.}
The distinguished residual row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified run it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same computation identifies this row with a punctured-cube shape having face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the packet is not merely counting labels.  It isolates a specific thirteen-codon subset, records its adjacency data, and checks that the subset has the four-face local tile structure expected of the window-six construction after the missing corner is removed.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The primary significance run used seed $42$ and sampled $N=2000$ distinct comparison rows.  Obtaining those distinct samples required $57377$ attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null sampling process was heavily constrained by the finite row space.  The observed exact shape appeared once among the $2000$ sampled rows, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact matches}\}&=1, & p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A relaxed geometric comparison also counted rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  There were $25$ such rows, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25, & p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers are finite-sample statements about the local codon-tile shape.  They say that this row is rare under the sampled comparison model, not that the geometry itself causes translation or determines biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A stricter null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In that run the observed $M$-row size was $20$.  Among $N=2000$ sampled class-preserving rows, the exact shape count was $0$, giving $p=0.0$ at the reported sample resolution.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, so the lower-tail probability was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This second null test is important because it asks whether the effect survives after preserving a biologically meaningful label-class constraint.  It does: no exact class-preserving sample reproduced the observed shape, and only seven sampled rows landed at or below the observed size threshold.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only external contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test the code-read layer and the local codon-tile label split.  It does not observe translation as a biochemical process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, organismal function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently the packet supports the following local conclusion: in the six-coordinate codon display, the standard-code labels determine a rare and reproducible local boundary-tile pattern, with the thirteen-codon residual row, punctured-cube face-vector, spectral quantities, null-model rarity, and leave-one-out recovery values listed above.  No claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from this packet unless another contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
