\paragraph{Shape-significance test.}
The primary shape test used $N=2000$ distinct sampled comparison rows with seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the $2000$ comparison rows, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The reported finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The matching significance fields report the same values, $0.0005$ for exact shape significance and $0.0125$ for high-Jaccard significance.  This supports the finite statement that the observed local codon tile is rare under the sampled comparison procedure, not the stronger statement that it is forced by biology.
