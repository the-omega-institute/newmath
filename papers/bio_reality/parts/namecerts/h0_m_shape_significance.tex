\subsection{Window-six local shape significance for the standard code}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite object under inspection.}
This chapter records a finite code-layer observation about the standard genetic-code table.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, used as a display for codons with a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The external contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact is therefore a label readback contact only: it observes codon-to-label assignments, not translation in a cell, folded protein structure, chemical admissibility, realized function, mechanism, or biological universality.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube configuration with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local finite shape used by the chapter.  It is not promoted to a law of molecular biology; it is a structured pattern in a codon-coordinate display whose labels are supplied by the curated table.

\paragraph{Shape significance row.}
The finite Monte Carlo row for the shape comparison used seed $42$ and sampled $N=2000$ null instances.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact local shape occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap criterion with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jac}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded significance scalars match these values: $p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape\ significant}}=0.0005$ and $p_{\mathrm{high\ Jaccard\ significant}}=0.0125$.  Thus the finite row supports the statement that the observed local code-layer shape is rare under the sampled null model, while leaving open every higher biological interpretation.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null row.}
A second null row preserves amino-acid class and again uses seed $42$ with $N=2000$.  The observed $M$ size is $20$.  Exact shape matches occur $0$ times, so the reported exact-shape null value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This row says that the observed local shape remains unusual even after preserving amino-acid class information in the null construction.  It does not say that the coordinate geometry causes the code, that the code is forced by the cube, or that label geometry carries structural or functional protein information.

These numbers provide a finite numerical signature of the local configuration.  They are useful as a reproducible shape descriptor, not as an independent biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The verdict of this chapter is narrow.  The finite evidence supports a rare and reproducible window-six local tile pattern in the standard-code label table, including the $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary, the reported spectral signature, the exact-shape and high-Jaccard null values, and the leave-one-out recovery rows.  The same evidence explicitly stops at the code-read layer.  No claim about translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from these rows unless a separate external contact observes that higher layer.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
