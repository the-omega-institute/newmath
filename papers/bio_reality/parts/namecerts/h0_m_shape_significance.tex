\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Significance}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite claim.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinates supplied by the three nucleotide positions after binary expansion of the four-letter alphabet.  The observable contact is only the curated table assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  Within that code-level contact, the claim is that a local tile and its boundary are statistically sharp: the coordinate cube separates a singleton label corner from a three-point label class and exposes a finite boundary set whose shape is unlikely under the recorded null samples.  This is a statement about code readback and finite cube geometry, not about translation dynamics or any later biological layer.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary row set is the following thirteen-codon subset of the standard code:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit gives $|R|=13$ and reports that the same set is the punctured-cube boundary with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row evidence is not merely a cardinality count.  It records a concrete subset of codons and a cell-incidence profile: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the induced local tile.  The boundary therefore has a specified finite combinatorial shape before any biological interpretation is attached to it.

\paragraph{Observed shape and null comparison.}
The observed shape $M$ has size $20$.  In the principal randomization run, $N=2000$ distinct samples were retained after $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, with seed $42$.  Exactly one retained sample matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\hbox{exact shape matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap comparison counted $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null preserving amino-acid class structure also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It found no exact matches to the observed shape and only $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed size $20$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\hbox{class-preserving exact matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
\#\{|M'|\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrow conclusion that the window-six coordinate presentation detects a non-generic local code-table shape.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Spectral row data.}
The same finite record includes a spectral summary for the local boundary calculation.  The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual.  At this numerical tolerance the residual is effectively zero for the finite computation, so the spectrum is internally consistent with the audited row data.  The spectral entry is used here as a numerical certificate for the local tile calculation, not as an independent biological measurement.

\paragraph{Leave-out behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and allowing the boundary raised the rate to $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, across $5$ reported families.  The leave-one-codon-out record reports $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, $0$ anchor cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These figures say that the local shape is predictive for the audited code-label perturbations, while still remaining a code-layer regularity.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen boundary codons are fully assigned to six finite modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification audit gives $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no missing boundary codons, and no unassigned codons.  Thus every codon in the boundary row set has a module placement, and every listed module codon belongs to the boundary row set.  This closes the finite bookkeeping for the local code-label packet.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The empirical contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table.  It resolves codon-label readback: each codon is assigned to an amino-acid label or to stop.  The contact can test the code-level split and the local tile label pattern, but it does not observe ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility of a molecule, organismal function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently, the proper conclusion is deliberately narrow.  Window-six coordinates expose a significant finite tile and boundary in the standard-code label table, with exact-shape and high-Jaccard null probabilities $0.0005$ and $0.0125$, class-preserving lower-tail probability $0.0035$, complete thirteen-codon module assignment, and strong leave-out recovery.  No claim about translation realization, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate observation at that layer is supplied.
