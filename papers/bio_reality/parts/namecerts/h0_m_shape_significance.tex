\subsection{Window-six codon shape significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The object under test is the standard-code codon table read at the code-label layer.  The coordinate carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, where a codon is treated as a finite coordinate row and the observed contact is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The selected boundary region $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is independently recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed finite geometry is not an unconstrained list of codons: it is a local cubical tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces inside the six-coordinate display.  The biological readback attached to this row is the standard-code label table, not a molecular dynamics model and not an organismal mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape test against finite random rows.}
The primary shape-significance run used seed $42$ and evaluated $N=2000$ distinct sampled comparison rows after $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those comparison rows there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The reported empirical probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact match rate therefore places the observed local codon tile in a sparse part of the finite comparison space, while the high-Jaccard count records that there are nearby alternatives.  This supports a code-layer statement: the standard-code labels meet a small local boundary pattern in $Q_6$.  It does not convert that pattern into a law of biology.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null row.}
A stricter null model preserving amino-acid class information was also run with seed $42$ and $N=2000$.  The observed $M$-row size was $20$.  The exact-shape count under this null was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding empirical values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This second row is important because it tests whether the effect disappears after preserving a biologically meaningful label partition.  In the recorded finite run it does not disappear: no exact shape match is seen in the class-preserving sample, and only seven comparison rows fall at or below the observed $M$ size.  The conclusion remains deliberately narrow.  The table supplies codon-to-label contact; it does not supply translation realization, folded structure, admissible physical geometry, biochemical function, or a universal generative mechanism.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The same finite object has a recorded spectral summary.  The row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial cubic residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is within numerical tolerance for the recorded spectral relation.  These numbers are used only as finite descriptors of the codon-coordinate row.  They are not evidence for protein folding, cellular implementation, or evolutionary necessity.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  The leave-one-codon-out row reported anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the local tile has predictive stability for code-label recovery tasks under the recorded finite probes.  They do not say that a ribosome, tRNA population, folded protein, or organismal phenotype has been observed.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned to six modules.  The module rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness row records $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has a predictable or boundary status in the recorded classification.  This makes the packet internally finite and auditable: each codon in the boundary row has an explicit module location, and the module partition neither drops nor adds codons.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite evidence supports a ready code-read claim.  In $Q_6$, the standard-code table exposes a thirteen-codon local boundary tile with sparse exact-shape frequency, a stronger amino-acid-class preserving null result, a stable spectral summary, and complete module accounting.  The permitted statement is that window-six coordinates reveal a statistically non-generic local codon-label boundary in the curated standard code.  The prohibited promotion is equally part of the result: this chapter does not infer translation, folding, physical admissibility, biochemical function, organism-level mechanism, or universality.  Any such higher-layer statement would require a separate contact whose observations live at that higher layer.
