\paragraph{Object and contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display.  The biological datum used here is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label.  The mathematical object is a window-six codon coordinate cube $Q_6$ equipped with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The readback being tested is only the codon-to-label table.  It is not a test of translation dynamics, folded structure, chemical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance against random label tables.}
For claim $h0.M.shape.significance$, the experiment with run id $0b267c88f8e4$ used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled tables.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape appeared once among the $2000$ sampled cases, while the high-overlap test with Jaccard threshold $0.9$ appeared $25$ times.  The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The supplied row also records these as $p\_value\_exact\_shape\_significant=0.0005$ and $p\_value\_high\_jaccard\_significant=0.0125$.  The exact-shape statistic therefore marks the observed local tile as unusually specific inside this finite null experiment.  The high-Jaccard statistic is weaker but still separates the observed table from most sampled alternatives.

\paragraph{Boundary of the inference.}
The contact supporting this record is the curated standard-code label table.  It can test code-layer label readback and the local codon-tile split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or universality.  In particular, reading an amino-acid label sequence from codons is not an observation of a folded protein.  Mentions of CUN, UUR, stop, tryptophan, arginine, lysine, isoleucine, methionine, serine, or leucine in this chapter are label-level statements inside the standard-code table unless an additional organism-matched or structure-level contact is supplied.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite row supports a narrow conclusion: in the standard-code codon table, the window-six coordinate presentation exposes a statistically unusual local tile whose residual set has $13$ codons, face-vector $(13,16,4)$, stable spectral scalars, strong leave-one-code-out performance, complete leave-one-codon-out recovery in the supplied test, and a complete six-module classification.  The conclusion remains at the code-read layer.  No statement about translation, folding, chemistry, function, mechanism, or biological necessity follows from these rows alone.
