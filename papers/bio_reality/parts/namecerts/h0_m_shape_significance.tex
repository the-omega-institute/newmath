\subsection{Finite carrier and code-layer contact}
\label{sec:window-six-codon-tile-finite-carrier}

\paragraph{Carrier.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the $64$ codon displays of the standard genetic-code table. The construction uses the codon table only as a code-layer contact: each codon has a curated amino-acid or stop label, and the observed label table is not treated as a mechanism. The relevant distinction is therefore local and finite. A window-six coordinate may expose a tile boundary in the codon display, but the label assignment remains an external reality contact at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Observed residual row.}
The row singled out by the finite probe is a $13$-codon residual set $R$. In the observed standard-code table it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality is $|R|=13$. The same finite row is also recovered as a punctured-cube shaped object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a local shape assertion about the codon-coordinate display. It is not a claim that codons globally obey a biological geometry law.

\subsection{Shape significance against finite null probes}
\label{sec:window-six-codon-tile-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Unrestricted finite-row null.}
The primary significance probe compared the observed shape against $N=2000$ distinct sampled alternatives, using seed $42$. Producing those distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact observed shape appeared once among the $2000$ distinct alternatives, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} = 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was met by $25$ alternatives, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the window-six tile is rare in this finite-row null model both as an exact shape and as a high-Jaccard near shape. The result is a code-layer rarity statement: it says that this local tile is not a typical shuffled codon-label row under the stated finite sampling rule.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class structure. Under this constrained finite-row probe, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed exact shape occurred $0$ times. The observed $M$-side size was $20$, and the lower-tail event at or below that observed size occurred $7$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The available finite row gives the scalar size of the observed $M$ side, $|M|=20$, but does not include an explicit codon-by-codon $M$ list. Consequently, the chapter states the verified size and the finite-row probabilities, while leaving the absent $M$ membership list unstated.

\subsection{Boundary of the claim}
\label{sec:window-six-codon-tile-boundary}

\paragraph{Positive claim.}
The admissible claim is narrow. In the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, the curated standard-code label table supports a local boundary-tile structure with residual set $R$, verified cardinality $13$, punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, rare exact-shape null frequency $0.0005$, high-Jaccard null frequency $0.0125$, class-preserving exact-shape frequency $0$, and class-preserving lower-tail frequency $0.0035$. The spectral and leave-one probes give additional finite certificates that the same local structure is stable under the stated code-read tests.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
No translation realization follows from this chapter. No folded protein structure follows. No physical admissibility, function realization, mechanism, or universality follows. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only label readback. Crossing from this local codon tile to translation, structure, physical chemistry, biological function, mechanism, or universal law would require a separate reality contact that directly observes that higher layer. In the present finite row, the only contact is the curated standard-code table, and that contact supports only codon-label readback and local split checks.
