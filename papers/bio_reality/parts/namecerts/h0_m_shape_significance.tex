\subsection{Window-Six Local Tile Significance for the Standard-Code Table}
\label{sec:biores-h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table read on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The six-bit display is treated only as a finite coordinate carrier.  The external contact is the curated standard-code label table, namely the codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop assignment used for code-layer readback.  The contact has resolution at the codon-label level.  It does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a global mechanism, or universality.  Thus the chapter studies a finite local tile in a code-read layer, not a biological law.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The row is exactly the following codon set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the concrete local tile boundary used by the claim.  It is not promoted to a universal codon law: the row is a finite code-table feature, and its interpretation remains tied to the curated label contact.

\paragraph{Shape significance experiment.}
The primary shape experiment used seed $42$ and sampled $N=2000$ finite comparison rows.  Because the sampling procedure enforced distinct rows, it made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Against this finite null ensemble, the observed $M$-shape had $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape row is therefore rare in this finite experiment, and the high-Jaccard event remains uncommon.  The claim supported by these numbers is deliberately narrow: window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern whose exact shape is scarce under the sampled comparison model.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  It also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that ensemble the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The resulting finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the local code-read observation: the tile is not merely a generic artifact of preserving amino-acid classes in the sampled rows.  It still does not supply any contact with folding, physical admissibility, or function.

\paragraph{Spectral readback.}
The finite spectrum attached to the same construction records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check gave residual
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is within the stated numerical tolerance for the reported spectral radius.  These numbers are internal finite-coordinate diagnostics.  They are useful for identifying the tile and checking reproducibility, but they are not a physical-energy spectrum, not a protein-structure spectrum, and not a mechanistic translation model.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out stability.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast.  These rows show that the local tile has strong code-table readback stability across the evaluated family split, while still remaining a code-layer statement.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe found anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The reported recovery rate above threshold was also $1.0$.  The module classification assigned all $13$ codons in $R$ with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has predictable-or-boundary status in the finite probe.  This is the strongest local statement supported here: the observed row is not an isolated visual coincidence, since the row also admits a complete module-level classification and leave-one-codon recovery behavior.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite-row conclusion is that the standard-code label table, read on $Q_6$, supports a significant local boundary-tile shape for the observed $M$ construction and the $13$-codon row $R$.  The exact shape p-value is $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard p-value is $0.0125$, the amino-acid-class preserving lower-tail p-value is $0.0035$, and the module classification is complete on the observed row.  No stronger biological claim follows from these data.  In particular, the chapter does not claim translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a universal law of DNA geometry.  Any such higher-layer claim would require a separate reality contact beyond the curated standard-code table.
