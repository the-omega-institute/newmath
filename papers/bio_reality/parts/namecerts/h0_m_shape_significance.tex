\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary set.}
This record studies a finite six-coordinate codon display in which each codon is treated as a point of a window-six cube and is read only through the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels. The construction is local: it concerns the shape of a tile cut, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class inside the codon table. It does not assert that the coordinate geometry is a biological cause. The finite boundary set detected in the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$. The same row verifies that this set has the punctured-cube face count
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed codons are not being treated as an unconstrained list. They form a finite incidence pattern inside the window-six codon display, while the labels attached to those codons still come from the curated code table.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under finite sampling.}
The principal significance check sampled $N=2000$ distinct comparison shapes with seed $42$. Producing those distinct samples required $57377$ attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null comparison was visibly constrained by collisions in the finite shape space. Within the accepted sample, the exact observed shape appeared once and high-overlap shapes with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times. The corresponding empirical rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row supports a specific finite statement: the observed local window-six tile is rare among the sampled comparison shapes. The statement is deliberately about code-level label geometry. It is not a statement about ribosomal translation dynamics, molecular folding, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, a mechanism, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving comparison.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information and again uses $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. In that comparison the observed local shape has size $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$. The reported rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This check matters because it asks whether the observed configuration survives a less naive comparison that respects label classes. It does: the exact shape was not seen in the sampled class-preserving null, and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$. The result strengthens the local code-read claim, but it still does not convert a codon-label table into a structural or functional observation.

\paragraph{Leave-one evidence and module coverage.}
The leave-one-code-out evaluation used $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$. The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.

The boundary modules also cover the thirteen listed codons exactly. The module assignment is

By family, the hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other groups, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast. These figures support the narrow claim that the local tile and its boundary modules are repeatedly visible in finite code-read probes.

\paragraph{Local interpretation and blocked promotion.}
The reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table: it supplies codon-to-label assignments and nothing more. The finite evidence supports a local window-six split in the code layer, including a rare observed shape, a thirteen-codon punctured boundary, stable module coverage, and strong leave-one recovery. The same evidence blocks stronger readings. No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these rows unless an independent contact is supplied for that higher layer. Amino-acid label readback remains label readback; it is not an observation of a folded protein or a proof that organisms must instantiate the window-six geometry.
