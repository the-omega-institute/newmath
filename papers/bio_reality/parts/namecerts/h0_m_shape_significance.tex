\subsection{Window-six local tile significance}
\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite codon coordinate object.}
The object considered here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display. Each codon is a point of the codon cube $Q_6$, with the biological label supplied by the curated standard-code table. The mathematical datum is not a mechanism for translation. It is a finite labeled coordinate object: codon positions, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes can be compared inside the cube after the code labels have been attached by the curated table. The contact therefore reaches the code-read layer only. It does not by itself observe a ribosome, a folded protein, a physical admissibility constraint, a biological function, or a universal law.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. The finite run reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$. The same row is reported as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite local shape that the chapter records: thirteen codons, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces in the selected coordinate subobject. The row is not promoted beyond its local status. It is a codon-label boundary inside the window-six display.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The spectral summary is numerically stable in the recorded run. The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same number recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual. At the displayed precision this is a near-zero residual, so the finite row behaves as a coherent spectral coordinate of the observed local tile. The statement is intentionally narrow: the spectral numbers organize a finite codon-label shape; they do not establish biochemical dynamics or structural realization.

\paragraph{Shape significance against random relabeling.}
The primary shape experiment used seed $42$ and sampled $N=2000$ distinct comparison tables after $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries. In that sample there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$. Thus the recorded empirical probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row is therefore uncommon under the sampled null used in the run. The result supports the claim that the standard-code label table, when read through the window-six coordinates, exposes a non-generic local tile. It does not show that the coordinate geometry causes the genetic code, nor that the local tile is a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null preserves amino-acid classes. In $N=2000$ runs with seed $42$, the exact-match count is $0$. The lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$, and the observed $M$ size is $20$. The recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the finite-row reading: even when the comparison respects amino-acid class structure, the observed local shape remains rare in the sampled ensemble. The number $20$ is used here only as the reported size of $M$ in that experiment; the present finite row does not include a full codon-by-codon listing of $M$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families. These numbers say that the local coordinate pattern is informative for nearby code-table variation under the tested leave-one-code-out protocol. They still remain code-table statistics, not evidence of folded structure or function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. The recovery rate above threshold is also $1.0$. The module classification accounts for every element of $R$ with no missing or extra codons: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, the list of extra module codons is empty, the list missing from modules is empty, and the list of unassigned codons is empty. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This complete module accounting is the finite combinatorial reason the row is usable as a local boundary tile: each reported codon in $R$ is placed in a named module, and no reported boundary codon is left outside the classification.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and scope.}
The finite evidence supports a code-layer conclusion. The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments; after those labels are placed on $Q_6$, the observed window-six pattern has a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary, a coherent spectral summary, low empirical exact-match probability under the sampled null, low lower-tail probability under the amino-acid-class preserving null, and strong leave-one-out recovery behavior. This is enough to record a significant local codon-tile shape at the code-read layer. It is not enough to infer translation realization, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Any claim crossing one of those boundaries would require a separate observation of that higher layer.
