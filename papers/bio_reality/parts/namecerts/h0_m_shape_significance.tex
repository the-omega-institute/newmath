\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Shape Significance}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code table as a finite code-layer contact.  The contact supplies codon-to-label readback only: each codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label, and the resulting label table can be tested against local coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data inside the window-six display.  The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is observed as
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the local object is not merely a set of labels: it has a finite boundary geometry visible in the codon cube.  The finite-row data do not promote this geometry beyond the code-read layer.  They say that this particular codon-label table has a local tile and boundary profile, not that biology is governed by this profile as a global law.

\paragraph{Shape test against randomized rows.}
The primary significance test sampled $N=2000$ distinct randomized comparison rows using seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported null row count is a finite distinct-row audit rather than a loose draw count.  Among these $2000$ comparison rows, exactly $1$ row matched the observed shape exactly, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
 \#\{\mathrm{exact\ shape\ matches}\} &= 1,\\
 N &= 2000.
\end{aligned}
$$
A relaxed high-overlap test found $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125,\\
 \#\{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9\} &= 25.
\end{aligned}
$$
These two numbers support a chapter-specific significance claim: the observed local codon-tile shape is rare under the sampled coordinate-preserving comparison process.  The exact-shape probability is $5\times 10^{-4}$, while the high-Jaccard probability remains small at $1.25\times 10^{-2}$.  This is evidence for a finite local shape signal, not evidence for translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information and again uses $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  In this stricter label-aware comparison, the observed $M$ size is $20$.  The exact-shape match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The finite-row summary is
$$
\begin{aligned}
 |M|_{\mathrm{obs}} &= 20,\\
 \#\{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact\ matches}\} &= 0,\\
 p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
 \#\{\mathrm{lower\ tail}\leq |M|_{\mathrm{obs}}\} &= 7,\\
 p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving test is important because it blocks a weak explanation in which the signal is only an artifact of broad amino-acid label classes.  Even after preserving that coarse biological partition, the exact observed shape does not appear in the sampled rows, and the lower-tail event appears in only $7$ out of $2000$ rows.  The finite conclusion is therefore stronger than a raw label-count observation: the window-six row geometry remains atypical under a null model that keeps amino-acid class structure visible.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded claim.  In the six-bit codon cube, the curated standard-code label table exposes a rare local tile shape with a $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary, a stable module decomposition, significant exact-shape and high-Jaccard tests, a class-preserving lower-tail signal, spectral consistency, and strong leave-one-out recovery at the code-read layer.  The finite evidence does not support any higher-layer promotion.  No translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these rows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer information.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains label readback; it is not folded structure and it is not a physical or functional assay.
