\subsection{Window-six local tile significance for the standard code}\label{sec:bior-h0-m-shape-significance}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display.  The carrier is the $64$-vertex codon cube $Q_6$, with vertices read as codons and with the curated standard-code table supplying only the amino-acid or stop label attached to each codon.  The observed finite pattern is not treated as a biochemical mechanism.  It is a code-layer readback: a coordinate-level tile, a boundary class, a singleton-corner class, and a three-point label class are extracted from the finite table and then compared with finite null ensembles.

The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same list is certified by the punctured-cube finite row, where the face-vector data are
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the record is not merely a count: the $13$ codons form a specific punctured local tile with $16$ one-dimensional incidences and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the induced combinatorial geometry.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The principal finite randomization test used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  Because the sampler rejected repeated draws, the run attempted $57377$ distinct draws and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries before completing the $2000$-row comparison set.  In that finite ensemble, the exact observed shape occurred once, while $25$ samples had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ with the observed shape.  The reported probabilities are therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape event is consequently rare at the one-in-two-thousand resolution of this run, and the high-overlap event is also uncommon.  The conclusion is intentionally local: the six-bit coordinate display exposes a finite boundary-tile pattern in the standard code table.  It does not by itself identify translation dynamics, molecular folding, energetic admissibility, biological function, an evolutionary mechanism, or a universal law.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null check.}
A second null row preserved amino-acid class information while resampling the relevant structure.  In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$.  The exact observed shape appeared $0$ times, and only $7$ null samples lay in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The finite values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ matches}\} &= 0,\\
\#\{\mathrm{lower\ tail}\leq |M|\} &= 7,\\
|M| &= 20,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the code-layer reading: even after preserving the coarse amino-acid class constraint, the observed local shape remains exceptional in the tested finite population.  The zero exact-match value is a sample-resolution statement, not a proof that the true probability is zero.

The spectral datum is used only as a coordinate diagnostic of the codon-table pattern; it is not promoted to a claim about protein structure or physical viability.

They show that the local coordinate pattern has predictive traction inside the code table, not that a cell implements the coordinate construction as its biological cause.

\paragraph{Scope of the conclusion.}
The supported conclusion is that, in the standard-code label table, the six-bit codon coordinate system exposes a rare local boundary-tile shape with a certified $13$-codon punctured-cube support, a $20$-element observed $M$ size in the class-preserving null row, significant finite-sample rarity values, coherent spectral diagnostics, and strong leave-one recovery behavior.  The curated table is the only reality contact used here, and that contact contains codon-to-label assignments rather than translation events, folded structures, physical admissibility measurements, functional assays, or universality evidence.  Therefore no statement about actual translation realization, protein folding, structural order, biochemical feasibility, biological function, mechanism, or global necessity follows from this chapter unless a separate empirical contact supplies that higher-layer information.
