\subsection{Window-six codon shape significance}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with vertices read as codons and with local faces used only as finite combinatorial neighborhoods.  The observed layer is code readback: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and the finite analysis asks whether the label split has an exceptional local tile shape inside $Q_6$.  The data do not identify translation, molecular realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Those are different empirical layers and require independent observations.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its verified codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it has a specified local two-dimensional incidence profile in the six-bit codon cube.

\paragraph{Shape test.}
For the shape-significance probe, the finite experiment used $N=2000$ randomized comparison samples with seed $42$.  The sampler needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  The observed local shape had exactly one exact match among the $2000$ samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under a high-overlap relaxation, $25$ samples reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These two values support the finite statement that the observed tile geometry is atypical for the tested null ensemble.  They do not by themselves say why biology realizes the table, nor do they turn the local tile into a law of genetic coding.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class information.  In this run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$.  The exact-shape count was $0$, so the empirical exact-shape value was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This reinforces the finite conclusion at the code-label layer: the observed table is not behaving like a generic class-preserving rearrangement under these probes.

The leave-one-codon experiment reported no anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows are consistent with the same reading: the local tile carries predictive finite structure for code-label recovery tests, but only within the tested code-readback problem.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite evidence supports a precise code-layer statement.  In the six-bit codon cube, the standard-code labels determine a local tile whose boundary row $R$ has size $13$, punctured-cube face-vector $(13,16,4)$, significant exact-shape and high-Jaccard statistics, stable spectral values, and successful leave-one recovery behavior across the reported code and codon tests.  The scientific reading is deliberately narrow: curated codon-to-label data are enough to test local codon-tile shape, label split, and recovery behavior.  They are not enough to infer translation as a realized process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, organism-level function, a general mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Any such stronger conclusion must enter through a separate empirical contact that directly observes the relevant layer.
