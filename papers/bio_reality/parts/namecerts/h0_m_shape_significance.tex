\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact, not as a mechanism.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC-side distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal structure used here is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the readback is the standard-code label table.  The biological object is therefore the standard-code codon table at the $code\_read$ layer only.

\paragraph{Observed relation row.}
The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not being summarized by a vague visual resemblance: the row count, the named codons, and the face-vector data are all part of the finite record.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The primary shape-significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparisons.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite sampling pressure is explicit.  The observed exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{exact}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ produced $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The shape is therefore significant under this finite null probe at the exact level and remains uncommon under the high-Jaccard relaxation.  This is a local codon-tile result: it says that the window-six coordinate presentation exposes a sharply placed finite row relative to the sampled alternatives.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A separate amino-acid-class preserving null model also used seed $42$ and $N=2000$.  The observed $M$ size was $20$.  The exact-match count was $0$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{aa\text{-}class,exact}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{aa\text{-}class,lower\ tail}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This second probe says that the observed local organization is not erased by preserving amino-acid class information in the null.  It strengthens the code-read claim while still remaining a finite-row observation.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The contact is the curated standard-code table.  It can test code readback and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any claim crossing from this code-layer observation into translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate higher-layer reality contact.  Within that boundary, the finite evidence is aligned: the curated table supports codon-label readback, the $13$-codon relation row is explicit, the punctured-cube face vector is recorded, the spectral residual is near zero, and the finite null probes assign small tail probabilities to the observed local shape.
