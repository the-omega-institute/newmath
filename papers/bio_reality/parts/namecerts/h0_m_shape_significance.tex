\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Shape Significance}
\label{sec:biores-h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object and coordinate carrier.}
The object considered here is the standard-code codon table, read only as a finite codon-to-label table.  The BEDC minimal carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$: each codon is displayed as a six-coordinate word, and the permitted internal distinctions are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation.  The biological contact is therefore a code-layer contact.  It supplies a curated standard-code label table, not a mechanism of translation, not a folded protein structure, and not a physical or functional realization.  The local tile detected in this chapter is a finite shape inside the codon coordinate display whose readback is the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The finite boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This same row is verified as a punctured-cube boundary, with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed boundary is not merely a list of codons: it carries a finite face-incidence signature.  The chapter claim is that this local shape is statistically non-generic under the tested finite-row nulls, while remaining only a code-read fact.

\paragraph{Shape-significance experiment.}
The direct shape-significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ samples.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  The observed exact shape occurred once among the $2000$ draws, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact}} = 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both scalars are finite-row readbacks of the same run, not asymptotic model parameters.  They say that the observed local boundary tile is rare among the sampled alternatives under this generator, with exact recurrence at $1/2000$ and near-recurrence at $25/2000$.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class data.  In this test the observed $M$ size was $20$, the run again used $N=2000$ and seed $42$, and the exact shape match count was $0$.  The lower tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, so the finite-row scalars are
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
 p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the local-shape reading: even after preserving the relevant label-class constraint, the exact observed shape was not found in the $2000$ sampled alternatives, and the observed size sits in a small lower tail.

\paragraph{Claim boundary.}
The contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge.  It can test code readback and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Consequently, the proper conclusion is narrow: window-six coordinates expose a statistically rare and predictively useful local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table.  No claim follows that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity, that the geometry realizes translation, that amino-acid label sequences determine folded structure, or that the tile is a universal biological law.  Any such higher-layer assertion would require a separate contact supplying the missing translation, structure, physics, function, mechanism, or universality data.
