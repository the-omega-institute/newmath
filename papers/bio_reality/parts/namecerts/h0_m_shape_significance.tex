\subsection{Window-six local tile significance for the standard-code table}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The record studies the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite coordinate display for the sixty-four codons.  The external contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  This contact is enough to test codon-label readback, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  It is not a contact with translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  All claims below therefore remain finite-row claims about label geometry in $Q_6$.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube object with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite evidence is not merely a count: the row is a named codon set, and its local cubical incidence summary has thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-faces.  The module partition is complete on the same thirteen codons.  The six modules are $\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The classification reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The local shape statistic was tested against $N=2000$ distinct sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported empirical probabilities should be read as probabilities over the accepted distinct sample, not over all raw attempts.  There was exactly one exact shape match among the $2000$ accepted samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed $M$ row has size $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment.  Under that stricter label-class-preserving null, $N=2000$ samples produced zero exact shape matches, so $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0$ at this finite resolution.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the finite statement that the observed standard-code label geometry is unusual relative to the sampled local-shape nulls.  They do not say that the geometry is a biological law.

\paragraph{Predictive leave-out checks.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate after adding the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the five family rates above baseline were: alternative yeast $0.8333333333333334$, bacterial or plastid $1.0$, candidate other $1.0$, mitochondrial $1.0$, and nuclear ciliate $1.0$.  These are code-table readback checks across finite label variants, not evidence for a universal generator of biology.

\paragraph{Codon leave-out checks.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The same experiment records that the recovery rate is above threshold, also as $1.0$.  Together with the module classification, this says that every listed $R$ module has either predictable or boundary support within the finite code-read test.  It does not identify the missing codon's biochemical realization, and it does not turn a codon-label recovery event into a protein-structure prediction.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite-row verdict is that window-six coordinates expose a statistically sharp local boundary-tile pattern in the curated standard-code label table.  The strongest numerical support is the exact-shape probability $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$, the amino-acid-class-preserving exact count $0$ out of $2000$, the amino-acid-class lower-tail probability $0.0035$, and the leave-out recovery rates near one.  The claim is intentionally local: the curated table supplies codon-to-label reality contact only.  No translation process, folded structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
