\subsection{Window-six codon tile significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Object and finite coordinate frame.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a code-layer contact with a finite coordinate model on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The coordinates are not asserted to be a biological mechanism.  They give a finite display in which codons can be compared by local adjacency, tile boundary, singleton corner behavior, and three-point label-class behavior.  The observed contact is the curated standard-code assignment of each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The readout therefore remains a label table: it can test codon-label geometry, but it does not by itself observe translation, folding, physical viability, biochemical function, organismal usage, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the following codon row:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported by the punctured-cube check, with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object is not merely a count: the row is pinned by named codons, and its local combinatorics have $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the recorded punctured-cube shape.  The codons divide into recognizable local modules: $\{AGA,AGG\}$ for the AGR module, $\{AAA\}$ for the AR/Lys singleton, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for the CUN block, $\{AUA\}$ for the Ile/Met singleton, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ for the SSN pair recorded here, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for the stop/Trp boundary module.  The module audit assigns all $13$ boundary codons, reports no missing codons, and reports no extra module codons.

\paragraph{Shape-significance experiment.}
The main finite-row test uses $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows with seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space is being sampled with substantial rejection pressure rather than by a loose one-pass draw.  Against this null, the exact observed shape appeared once:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A relaxed high-overlap test at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$ found $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape row is therefore rare in this finite null, while the high-overlap row is also small enough to mark the observed tile as non-generic under the stated sampling rule.  The result should be read as a code-table shape fact: the local codon tile has a statistically unusual boundary pattern inside this coordinate experiment.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null comparison.}
A second null keeps amino-acid class information in the randomization constraint.  In that test, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-row size is $20$.  The experiment reports no exact class-preserving shape match:
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M|&=20,\\
\#\{\text{class-preserving exact matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run records $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{samples with size}\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the interpretation that the observed standard-code label split is not a typical output of the finite comparison model, even when the randomization is constrained to preserve amino-acid class structure.  The statement is still only a statement about the finite code-label contact and its coordinate readout.

This residual is numerically close to zero at the reported precision, so the spectral row is internally consistent with the cubic relation used by the finite probe.  The spectral radius value in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$ in the record.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ held-out targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The observed hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline row is $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate, across $5$ recorded families.  These numbers say that the local tile remains predictive for held-out code-label rows in the finite comparison, with the weakest listed family still well above the stated baseline.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The leave-one-codon-out row has $0$ anchor codons, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, and $8$ predictable codons.  Its recovery rate is $1.0$, and the recovery-rate-above-threshold entry is also $1.0$.  Module classification is complete: the $13$ codons of $R$ are all assigned, with $0$ missing from modules, $0$ unassigned codons, and no extra module codons.  The recovered modules are exactly the AGR pair $AGA,AGG$, the AR/Lys singleton $AAA$, the CUN block $CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU$, the Ile/Met singleton $AUA$, the SSN pair $UCA,UUA$, and the stop/Trp triple $UAA,UAG,UGA$.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded conclusion.  In the six-bit codon display, the curated standard-code labels determine a local boundary tile whose exact shape is rare under the sampled null, whose high-Jaccard neighborhood is also uncommon, whose class-preserving null gives no exact match in $2000$ draws, and whose leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out rows show strong recovery behavior.  This is evidence for a code-read pattern in the standard-code table.  It is not evidence that the coordinate geometry causes translation, that amino-acid label strings determine folded protein structure, that the row is physically admissible as a molecular mechanism, that it realizes biological function, or that the tile is universal across biological contexts.  Any such stronger claim would require a separate contact that directly observes the relevant layer.
