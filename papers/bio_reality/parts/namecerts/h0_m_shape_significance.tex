\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Shape Significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with one local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: each codon is externally assigned to an amino-acid label or a stop label. This contact supports code-layer readback, not translation realization. The internal BEDC-side structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data, while the readback is the standard-code label table. The row is therefore a code-read certificate for a local tile boundary, not a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary relation $R$ has cardinality $13$. The observed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same thirteen codons are certified by the punctured-cube check. Its finite face count is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it carries a small two-dimensional boundary signature inside the codon cube. The certificate stays finite throughout; no limiting, asymptotic, or population-level biological claim is used.

\paragraph{Shape-significance test.}
The shape-significance run used seed $42$ and drew $N=2000$ distinct null samples. Obtaining those distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space being sampled has substantial collision pressure under this sampling protocol. Against this null, the exact observed shape appeared once:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}=1,\qquad p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A softer high-overlap test counted $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}=25,\qquad p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite-row evidence supports the narrow claim that the standard-code labels expose an unusually specific local tile shape in window-six coordinates. The exact-shape and high-Jaccard values are both small under this null, but they are still code-layer statistics: they do not assert that the geometry causes translation, folding, admissibility, or function.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null kept amino-acid-class information fixed and tested whether the same local shape persists as an automatic consequence of label-class structure. In that run, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$ size was $20$. The exact match count was $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the finite-row reading that the tile is not explained away merely by preserving broad amino-acid classes. Since no explicit codon list for $M$ is supplied in this row, the chapter records its size and significance statistics rather than inventing an $M$ enumeration.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family, across $5$ families. These rows say that the local certificate has strong code-read recovery behavior across the evaluated tables. They do not say that a cell implements the certificate as a mechanism.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The certificate is explicitly bounded. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. The curated standard-code table is a reality bridge for code labels only; it contains no structure contact, no physical-admissibility contact, no functional assay, and no universal-mechanism proof. Any claim crossing from code readback into translation realization, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate contact supplying that layer.
