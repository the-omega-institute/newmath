\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the $64$ codon displays obtained from three nucleotide positions with four choices at each position. The row does not treat this cube as a mechanism for biology. It treats $Q_6$ as a finite coordinate display on which a curated standard-code table supplies external labels. The internal distinctions used by the row are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class. The internal structure recorded for the row is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation, while the readback is only the standard-code label table.

The record is still local. It does not assert that DNA, translation, folding, physical chemistry, function, mechanism, or universality is determined by this shape.

\paragraph{Shape significance row.}
The shape-significance experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows. The distinct-sample process required $57377$ sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. Against this finite comparison ensemble, the exact local shape occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A high-overlap relaxation using Jaccard threshold $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{Jaccard} \geq 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row supports the narrow statement that the observed local tile shape is rare under the finite sampling procedure used here. The exact-shape and high-Jaccard values are both finite-row counts, not asymptotic laws. The reported exact-shape significance is $0.0005$, and the reported high-Jaccard significance is $0.0125$.

It does not convert the local code-read fact into a global biological principle. The null row is a finite comparison against label-preserving alternatives, not an observation of translation dynamics or protein structure.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table. It observes that a curated standard genetic-code bridge assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. Its resolution is codon label readback. Its known bias is that the table is a bridge for labels, not a mechanism or a proof of universality. Therefore the row can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splitting, but it cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, folded structure, or universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The chapter-specific boundary is local and strict. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, not protein structure, not physical admissibility, not biological function, and not a mechanism of universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. A claim crossing from code labels to translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate contact that observes that higher layer. This row supplies no such contact.

The finite verdict is that the standard-code label table, when read through the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, exposes a rare and internally structured local tile. The appropriate reading is significance at the local code-read layer, with the higher biological layers explicitly outside the scope of this contact.
