\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite external contact for label readback on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The contact is deliberately narrow: it supplies a curated assignment from each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it does not supply a translation mechanism, a folding experiment, a physical admissibility test, a functional assay, or a universality theorem.  The BEDC-minimal form used here has carrier $Q_6$, with distinctions given by the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  Its internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data, and its readback is the standard-code label table.

Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it carries a concrete finite tile shape inside the six-bit display.  The record should be read as a code-layer shape statement, not as a claim that the biological genetic code is forced by cube geometry.

\paragraph{Shape significance experiment.}
For the primary shape-significance test, the run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ distinct sampled comparison rows.  Generating those distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts and produced $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite comparison space was visibly constrained by the row shape and label-preserving filters.  The observed exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold count was $25$ for Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers are finite-row Monte Carlo readbacks for the local codon tile.  They support the statement that the observed window-six shape is rare under the implemented comparison sampler, but they do not by themselves identify a biological cause.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The verdict is therefore a constrained positive one.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and the primary row has exact-shape significance $0.0005$ with high-Jaccard significance $0.0125$ under the sampled null.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives no exact matches in $2000$ samples and a lower-tail value $0.0035$.  The row $R$ has the verified $13$ codons displayed above, the punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, and a coherent module assignment.  Nothing in this chapter claims translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such promotion requires a separate reality contact beyond the curated standard-code table.
