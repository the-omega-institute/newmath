\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
The BioReality record for claim $h0.M.shape.significance$ treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact, not as a mechanism.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a standard-code label table whose rows assign each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC-side distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal structure retained by the record is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation.  The external contact is only the curated standard-code table: it supports codon-label readback and local split checks, but it does not by itself observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or biological necessity.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the observed row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported by both the cardinality check and the punctured-cube check.  Its finite face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object being tested is a concrete finite tile boundary, not an informal visual analogy.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$: $AAA$ lies in the $ar\_lys$ module, $AGA$ and $AGG$ in $agr$, $AUA$ in $ile\_met$, $CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU$ in $cun$, $UCA$ and $UUA$ in $ssn$, and $UAA, UAG, UGA$ in $stop\_trp$.  The module audit reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance experiment.}
The finite-row significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, which is material because the row space is finite and repeated draws are common at this resolution.  Among the $2000$ comparison rows there was exactly $1$ exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$.  The reported tail frequencies are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The record therefore supports the narrow statement that the observed local tile shape is uncommon under the stated finite comparison procedure.  It does not support promotion from an uncommon finite code-read geometry to translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information and again uses $N=2000$ with seed $42$.  Under that null the observed $M$-row size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the code-layer reading: even after preserving amino-acid class, the observed row is not behaving like a typical sampled row in the tested finite ensemble.  The result remains a statement about label-table geometry.  It is not a statement that the labels have folded into a protein, that a physical molecule is admissible, or that a biological function has been realized.
