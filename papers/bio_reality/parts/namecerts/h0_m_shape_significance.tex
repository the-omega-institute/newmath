\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Shape Significance}\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The record \texttt{h0.M.shape.significance} concerns a window-six display of the standard-code codon table on the codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is finite: each codon is read as a six-bit coordinate, and the only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  Thus the biological readback is a code-layer readback, not a mechanism.  The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube calculation reports the same $13$ codons and the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile face seen by the finite coordinate construction.  It is not asserted to be a global biological law.

\paragraph{Monte Carlo shape significance.}
The shape-significance run used seed $42$ and sampled $N=2000$ distinct null rows after $57377$ total distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  The observed exact shape occurred once among the $2000$ rows, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row conclusion is therefore narrow but nontrivial: under this sampled null, the exact local shape is rare, and near-shape matches remain uncommon.  The calculation supports a local boundary-tile signal in the codon-label table; it does not support promotion to translation dynamics, protein structure, admissible chemistry, realized function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null comparison.}
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null run also used seed $42$ and $N=2000$.  In that run the observed $M$ row count was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This comparison matters because it preserves a label-class constraint while still failing to reproduce the exact observed shape in the sampled rows.  The data therefore point to a boundary-sensitive code-read feature rather than merely to an arbitrary relabeling artifact.  The finite row gives the size $|M|=20$ but does not provide the explicit membership list of $M$; no membership claim beyond the reported count is made here.

The spectral row reports

\mu&=3.051487585298128.

This supplies a coordinate-level spectral fingerprint for the local tile.  It is a finite combinatorial and numerical fact about the code-label display, not an observation of folding, physical admissibility, or function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family split reports $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$ above baseline, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These finite recovery checks support the claim that the local boundary tile has code-read predictive structure under the stated probes.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification partitions all $13$ codons of $R$ with no extras, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The reported modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{size}}=13,\qquad \mathrm{assigned\_count}=13,
\end{aligned}
$$
with empty extra-module, missing-from-module, and unassigned-codon lists.  Each module has predictable-or-boundary support in the finite record.  This is the precise local interpretation of the tile: a six-bit codon coordinate display exposes a structured boundary subset and module split, while the label meanings still enter only through the curated code-table contact.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
The support condition is deliberately bounded.  The curated standard-code table tests codon-label readback and local split checks.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or code universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not protein structure.  A separate higher-layer contact would be required before any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality could be made.  Within the finite data supplied here, the ready claim is only that the window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically uncommon local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table.
