\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Significance}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object considered here is the standard-code codon table read through the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  The display is finite: each codon contributes a six-bit coordinate, local face incidence supplies the tile geometry, and the biological labels are supplied by the curated standard code as amino-acid or stop labels.  The claim is therefore not that the coordinate cube creates translation.  The claim is narrower: in this coordinate presentation, the observed label split determines a local boundary-shaped subset whose incidence and spectrum can be compared with finite null ensembles.

The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Its punctured-cube incidence summary has $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed local label tile $M$ has size $20$ in the amino-acid-class preserving comparison.  The finite readout thus contains a $20$-codon observed tile together with a $13$-codon boundary set, rather than an unrestricted assertion about all biological coding behavior.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled null tables with seed $42$.  To obtain the distinct samples, the run made $57377$ sample attempts and rejected $55377$ duplicate draws.  Among the $2000$ distinct null samples, exactly one matched the observed shape exactly, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
For the relaxed comparison, $25$ samples had Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These are finite-row empirical counts, not asymptotic claims.  They say that the observed codon-tile shape is rare in the stated sample universe, with one exact match and twenty-five near matches under the reported sampling protocol.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null comparison preserves amino-acid class information.  In this ensemble the observed $M$ size was $20$, the number of exact shape matches among $N=2000$ samples was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the local reading: the observed shape is not only unusual against the first finite null, but also remains rare when the comparison respects amino-acid class structure.  Since the exact count is zero in a finite run, the value $0.0$ is the empirical count divided by $2000$, not a claim of logical impossibility.

\paragraph{Spectral readout.}
The same finite object gives a spectral summary.  The reported parameters are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check returned
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
so the polynomial residual is at numerical roundoff scale for this finite computation.  The spectral radius value recorded in tolerance is the same $
\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$.  This supports a geometric summary of the observed tile and boundary relation, but it does not identify a molecular mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one readbacks.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the five family rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The leave-one-codon-out experiment found $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the local coordinate tile has substantial finite predictive recovery inside the declared code-read setting.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The $13$ boundary codons are completely assigned to six named modules.  The module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has either predictable or boundary support in the finite classification.  This is an exact bookkeeping statement about the listed codons.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, which supplies codon-to-label assignments at code-read resolution.  It supports local label-split checks and the finite incidence, null, module, and spectral summaries above.  It does not observe ribosomal realization, folding, admissible molecular physics, biological function, or a universal coding law.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is still only a label sequence at this level; it is not a folded protein structure and does not by itself carry function.

Consequently, the chapter's result is a finite code-layer significance statement.  Window-six coordinates expose a rare local tile and a fully accounted boundary set for the standard-code label table, with exact-shape empirical probability $0.0005$, high-Jaccard empirical probability $0.0125$, amino-acid-class lower-tail probability $0.0035$, and complete $13$-codon module coverage.  Any assertion about translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires an additional contact of the corresponding kind.
