\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Shape Significance}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
This record concerns a finite window-six display of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code bridge that assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact is deliberately narrow: it tests codon-label readback and local tile label splitting, not translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The BEDC object under inspection has coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data; the biological object is only the standard-code label table at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the following codon set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube boundary with finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not being used as a vague motif: it is a concrete finite subconfiguration with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the sampled local tile representation.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The observed tile shape was compared against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives using seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, which is itself a useful warning that the finite state space has strong collision pressure under the sampling scheme.  Among the $2000$ accepted samples, exactly one exact shape match occurred and $25$ samples had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  Thus the reported finite-row tail values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the claim that the observed local $M$-shape is uncommon under the stated null sampler.  It does not by itself identify a biochemical cause, since the contact is a label table rather than an experiment on translation, folding, energy, or function.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null check.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$.  The exact-shape count was $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the lower-tail count for samples with size at most the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving check therefore points in the same direction as the unrestricted shape check: the local tile is a finite-row anomaly relative to the tested codon-label shuffles.  The conclusion remains code-layered, because preserving amino-acid class is still a constraint on labels, not a measurement of ribosomal dynamics or protein structure.

\paragraph{Spectral signature.}
The same finite object carries a spectral readout.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is numerically zero at the recorded tolerance scale.  Thus the boundary row is not only a set-theoretic list of codons; it also has a stable spectral footprint in the finite coordinate system used for the window-six tile.

\end{aligned}
$$
while the observed hit rate above baseline was

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the five reported families have hit rates above baseline
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
These rows say that the local code-read pattern has strong recovery behavior across the evaluated code-family table, but they still do not turn a codon-coordinate regularity into a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, and high closure coverage $8$.  Its recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value reported for recovery above threshold.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$: $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The module rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module therefore has a predictable-or-boundary explanation within the finite window-six code-read object.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite evidence supports a chapter-specific statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically unusual local boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code label table.  The exact-shape value $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard value $0.0125$, the class-preserving lower-tail value $0.0035$, the complete $13$-codon module accounting, and the leave-one-code-out hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ all point to a robust code-layer pattern.  The boundary is equally important.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains label readback unless a separate reality contact supplies structure, physical realization, or function.
