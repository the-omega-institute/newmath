\subsection{Window-Six Local Tile Significance in the Standard Code}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Claim.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite labelled contact with a six-coordinate codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, equipped with local face data, singleton corner labels, three-point label classes, coordinate closure, spectral summaries, and relation rows.  The observed biological object is not translation itself but the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The claim is therefore restricted to the code-read layer: window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern whose shape is unusually rare under the stated finite randomizations, while the amino-acid labels remain supplied by the curated table.

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The distinguished residual row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are $AAA$, $AGA$, $AGG$, $AUA$, $CUA$, $CUC$, $CUG$, $CUU$, $UAA$, $UAG$, $UCA$, $UGA$, and $UUA$.  The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2) &= (13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated module partition is complete on these thirteen codons.  The $agr$ module contains $AGA,AGG$; the $ar\_lys$ module contains $AAA$; the $cun$ module contains $CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU$; the $ile\_met$ module contains $AUA$; the $ssn$ module contains $UCA,UUA$; and the $stop\_trp$ module contains $UAA,UAG,UGA$.  The module audit has $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  Thus the relation row is not merely a count: it is a fully assigned finite set with a named module cover.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The primary finite sampling test used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ distinct samples, requiring $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Against this null ensemble there was exactly one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125,\\
\#\{J\geq 0.9\} &= 25.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both reported significance fields agree with these values: the exact-shape significance value is $0.0005$, and the high-Jaccard significance value is $0.0125$.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null test, also with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, found observed $M$-size $20$, exact match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support a narrow conclusion: the local $M$-shape and its nearby variants are uncommon under the sampled finite controls.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Spectral measurement.}
The same finite packet reports a spectral separation between the $M$ and $R$ components.  The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual was $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, recorded both as the cubic-polynomial value and as the radial-polynomial value.  This is numerically consistent with the stated spectral computation at the given tolerance, and it supplies an internal shape statistic for the finite code-read packet.  The spectral row is used here only as a finite numerical invariant of the displayed codon geometry.

\paragraph{Leave-one checks.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$; the reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  Family-level leave-one-code-out rows were recorded for five families: alternative yeast had hit rate above baseline $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had value $1.0$.  The leave-one-codon-out run recorded anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These finite probes show that the local tile and its boundary recover much of the observed code-read variation in the tested rows, with the boundary carrying essential explanatory load.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only biological contact used in this record is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-layer readback and a local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal biological rule.  The local geometry may organize codon labels in $Q_6$, but the labels themselves are imported from the curated code table rather than derived from nucleotide chemistry or protein physics inside this record.

\paragraph{Boundary.}
The result should be read as a finite code-layer observation: a window-six codon coordinate system, when paired with curated standard-code labels, yields a rare local $M$-shape, a thirteen-codon punctured-cube residual row, a complete module cover, stable leave-one recovery rows, and a measured spectral profile.  Nothing in these rows licenses a promotion from codon-label readback to translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such higher-layer statement would require a separate contact whose measurements directly touch that layer.
