\subsection{Window-Six Local Tile Significance for the Standard-Code Table}
\label{subsec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display whose vertices are codons and whose observed labels are the curated standard-code amino-acid or stop labels. The data used here are deliberately finite-row data: a coordinate display, a closure computation, a spectral summary, and a relation-level readback against the standard-code label table. The biological object is therefore not translation as a cellular process, and not a protein structure. It is only the code-read layer of the standard codon table, with a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class extracted as finite distinctions.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed finite shape is not merely a count of codons: it also carries a local face structure with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces. The row is internally consistent across the cardinality report and the punctured-cube report, since both give exactly the same thirteen codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape-significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows. The distinct-sample procedure required $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, so the comparison space was sampled under a strong finite uniqueness constraint rather than by accepting repeated rows. Exactly one comparison row matched the observed shape exactly, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
Twenty-five comparison rows reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers are row-level significance statements about the local codon-tile shape. They do not say that the geometry is a biological law. They say that, relative to this finite sampling protocol, the standard-code row occupies a rare local shape position.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information. In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ row size is $20$. The finite report gives no exact $M$ member list, but it does give the scalar row size and null counts. The exact-shape count is $0$, so the reported exact-shape $p$-value is $0.0$ at the sampled resolution. The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This null is important because it tests whether the observed local shape can be dismissed as a trivial amino-acid-class artifact. At the sampled resolution, the exact shape is not recovered by the class-preserving null, and the lower-tail event remains small.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The same finite object has a spectral summary. The reported values are

\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial and cubic-polynomial residual are both reported as $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is effectively zero at the numerical tolerance of the row. The spectral radius value in tolerance is again $0.6752479308830213$. These numbers support the claim that the local tile is not only a combinatorial subset but also has a stable finite spectral signature under the reported construction.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The code-read predictive check evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary row is $0.9841269841269841$. The reported hit rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$. Thus the local tile and boundary information give a strong code-layer recovery signal in this finite row system. The statement is still only a code-read statement: it predicts or recovers codon-label positions under the tested table perturbations, not biochemical realization.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out readback.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure codons, and $8$ predictable codons. The recovery rate is $1.0$, and the threshold comparison also reports $1.0$. This is a sharp finite-row fact: every tested omitted codon in this row-level probe is recovered by the available predictable-or-boundary mechanism. It is not a claim that every biological context would recover the same codon by a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons of $R$ are completely assigned to finite modules. The report gives $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

$$
Every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the finite report. This is the strongest local-form conclusion available from the supplied rows: the boundary codons are not stray examples but exhaust a classified thirteen-codon module system.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The reality contact is the curated standard genetic-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Therefore the supported conclusion is narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically rare and predictively useful local boundary-tile structure for standard-code label readback. No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
