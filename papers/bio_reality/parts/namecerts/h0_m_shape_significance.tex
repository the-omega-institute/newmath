\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}

The readback is the standard-code label table itself.  This chapter uses the contact only at the code layer: it tests whether window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern in the codon-label table.

\paragraph{Shape significance under the finite null.}
For the shape-significance run, the experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, showing that the finite sampling space was strongly revisited by the row generator.  Exact shape agreement occurred once among the $2000$ sampled rows, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap test counted $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers support the local claim that the observed $M$-shape is atypical under this finite row null.  The result is a significance statement about the codon-coordinate tile, not a biological law.
