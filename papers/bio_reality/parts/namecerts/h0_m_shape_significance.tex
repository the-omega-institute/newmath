\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a coordinate display for the sixty-four codons.  The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label, and the readback is a code-layer label readback.  The construction therefore has four local distinctions: the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  Its internal BEDC structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation, but the reality contact is the standard-code label table rather than a mechanistic model of translation.

Thus the boundary object is not merely a cardinality event: it also carries a finite incidence shape with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-faces inside the codon-coordinate display.  The module assignment is complete: the $13$ codons are exhausted by six named modules,

The finite audit reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
For the $M$-shape significance test, the run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ finite samples.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  The observed exact shape appeared once among the $2000$ samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{exact}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap event with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported significance fields agree with these proportions: $p\_value\_exact\_shape\_significant=0.0005$ and $p\_value\_high\_jaccard\_significant=0.0125$.  A second null model preserving amino-acid class gives $N=2000$, observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{aa\text{-}class,exact}=0.0,\qquad p_{aa\text{-}class,lower}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrow statement that the standard-code label table has a rare local tile shape under the stated finite-row nulls.  They do not by themselves identify a biological cause.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The finite data support a code-layer reading: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern whose relation set is the thirteen-codon punctured-cube row above, whose module decomposition is complete, and whose sampled null frequencies are small under both the raw shape and amino-acid-class preserving tests.  The admissible conclusion is therefore local and conditional: the curated standard-code table supplies a label contact, and relative to that contact the codon cube contains a rare boundary shape.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
No claim of translation realization follows from these rows.  No claim of folded protein structure follows from amino-acid label readback.  No claim of physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from the local codon tile.  Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate reality contact with its own finite rows; the curated standard-code table used here tests only codon-to-label readback and local split structure.
