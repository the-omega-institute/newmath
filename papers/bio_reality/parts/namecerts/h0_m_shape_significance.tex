\subsection{Window-six codon tile significance}\label{sec:window-six-codon-tile-significance}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with one binary coordinate for each nucleotide-bit position.  The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it therefore supplies a code-layer readback.  It does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  In this chapter the word ``label'' always means the external table label supplied by that contact, while the words ``coordinate'', ``closure'', ``spectrum'', and ``relation'' refer to finite BEDC-side structure on $Q_6$.

\paragraph{Observed relation row.}
The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with face vector
$$\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the local object is not a vague enrichment of the code table: it is a finite row with exactly $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces inside the six-bit display.  The row is also exhausted by the finite module partition
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\},\\
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The module audit records $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  This is a closed finite-row accounting statement, not a biological-mechanism statement.

\paragraph{Shape-significance row.}
The shape-significance experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled rows.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the sampled rows there was exactly $1$ exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$.  The reported finite-row probabilities are
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
The same scalars are recorded as $p\_value\_exact\_shape\_significant=0.0005$ and $p\_value\_high\_jaccard\_significant=0.0125$.  The result says that the observed local tile is rare under the sampled finite null at the exact-shape level and still uncommon under the high-Jaccard relaxation.  It does not say that the codon table is forced by geometry, nor that the tile is a global biological law.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null preserves amino-acid class information.  Under that null, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed local $M$-row size is $20$.  The exact shape match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The recorded probabilities are
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the local $M$-shape is not recovered by merely preserving amino-acid class labels in this finite experiment.  Since the explicit members of $M$ are not part of this row package, the mathematical statement here is restricted to its supplied size, shape match count, and lower-tail statistic.

The finite spectral row reports
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.

The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is reported for the cubic polynomial residual.  The spectral-radius-in-tolerance value is $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$.  These numbers give an internal coordinate-and-spectrum readout for the tile.  They do not add a translation contact, a structure contact, or a physical model.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values are
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\

\end{aligned}$$
The leave-one-codon-out probe records $anchor\_count=0$, $boundary\_count=5$, $closure\_coverage\_high=8$, $predictable\_count=8$, and $recovery\_rate=1.0$, with $recovery\_rate\_above\_threshold=1.0$.  These probes support the local code-read boundary pattern: when codon-label information is removed in controlled finite rows, the tile-and-boundary structure is highly recoverable at the code layer.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite data support a local window-six codon tile with a distinguished $13$-codon relation row, a punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, rare sampled shape matches, a nontrivial amino-acid-class preserving null result, a spectral certificate, and strong leave-one probes.  The supported claim is therefore narrow: window-six coordinates expose local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table.  The chapter does not infer that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as biological necessity.  It does not infer translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  Any such promotion would require a separate reality contact whose observed facts live at that higher layer.
