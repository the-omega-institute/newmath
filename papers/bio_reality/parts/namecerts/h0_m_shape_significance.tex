\paragraph{Monte Carlo shape significance.}
The shape-significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact observed shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ shape\ matches}\}&=1,&
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25,&
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the finite claim that the observed window-six local shape is statistically uncommon under the reported sampling procedure. They do not promote the local tile into a global biological law. The correct reading is weaker and sharper: the curated labels expose a rare local boundary pattern in $Q_6$ under the chosen null comparison, while the biological table remains the external contact that supplies the labels.
