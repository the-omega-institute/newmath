\subsection{Window-six local codon tile and shape significance}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with the standard-code label readback supplied by the curated genetic-code table.  This contact says only that each codon has an amino-acid or stop label in the standard code.  It is therefore a code-layer contact, not a mechanism contact.  The finite row under study isolates the local boundary set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed cardinality is $|R|=13$.  The same row identifies $R$ with a punctured-cube boundary pattern whose $f$-vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the BioReality content is not a claim that the codon table is generated by geometry.  It is the narrower statement that, after the curated standard-code label table is used as an external readback bridge, the resulting window-six coordinates expose a finite local tile with thirteen named codons and a four-face punctured-cube profile.

\paragraph{Shape row and randomization scale.}
For the shape-significance row, the experiment used seed $42$ and sampled $N=2000$ distinct comparison shapes.  Producing those $2000$ distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts, of which $55377$ were duplicate retries.  Against this finite comparison set, the exact local shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap relaxation with Jaccard threshold $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values are finite-row empirical p-values for a specific coordinate-and-label pipeline.  They support the claim that the observed local tile is uncommon under the recorded null sample, but they do not by themselves identify a biological cause.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In that row, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$-size is $20$.  The exact-shape count is $0$, so the finite-row exact-shape p-value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This matters because it tests a stricter background than a wholly unconstrained reshuffle: the local row remains unusual even when the null respects amino-acid class information.  The finite data therefore distinguish two claims.  The positive claim is that the observed local code-read tile is statistically sharp within the recorded null ensembles.  The blocked claim is that this sharpness is already a translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality theorem.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out rows.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the local tile has predictive readback strength under the recorded deletion tests.  They do not say that a biological organism must realize the tile, or that the tile determines protein structure or function.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The chapter-level claim is therefore deliberately narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates, together with the curated standard-code table, expose a statistically uncommon local boundary tile with $13$ codons, $16$ edges, $4$ faces, a recorded spectral signature, strong leave-one-code-out behavior, complete leave-one-codon-out module accounting, and high family-level hit rates.  Nothing in this row proves translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate reality contact whose observed facts live at that higher layer.
