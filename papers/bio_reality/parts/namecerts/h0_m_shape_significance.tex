\subsection{Window-six shape significance for the standard-code codon table}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record tests whether the six-coordinate codon display contains a reproducible local shape signal when the curated standard-code label table is read only as a codon-to-label assignment. The carrier is the $64$-point codon cube $Q_6$, with codons represented as six-bit windows. The permitted observations are finite: coordinate position in the cube, local tile incidence, singleton label corners, three-point label classes, closure membership, spectral measurements, and comparison with randomized label-preserving controls. The biological object is therefore the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer. No claim is made here about ribosomal translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Finite boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube computation returns the same $13$ codons and the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed boundary is not merely a label list: it has a finite local incidence profile inside the six-bit codon cube. The four two-faces, sixteen one-faces, and thirteen vertices describe the local tile visible to this probe.

\paragraph{Shape randomization test.}
The primary significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ random samples. To obtain the distinct sample set it made $57377$ sample attempts and rejected $55377$ duplicate retries. Only $1$ sampled table gave an exact shape match, and $25$ sampled tables had Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$. The resulting empirical values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers are finite-row measurements from the same run, not asymptotic formulas. They support the narrower statement that this codon-coordinate construction sees a rare local shape among the sampled alternatives. They do not turn the local tile into a biological law.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving control.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class structure. In that run, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$-set size was $20$. The exact-shape count was $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This control is useful because it keeps coarse amino-acid information while still asking whether the particular six-window shape is ordinary under that constrained scrambling. The answer in the finite run is negative: exact recovery did not occur, and the observed size sits in a small lower tail.

\paragraph{Spectral measurements.}
The same finite object also has a spectral profile. The measured values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual was
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is at numerical tolerance for the recorded cubic relation. These values give a coordinate-level spectral fingerprint of the local codon tile. They are not a statement about protein geometry or chemical realizability.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M-T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M-T$ plus the boundary. This supports a code-layer recovery statement: when code labels are perturbed tablewise, the local coordinate construction recovers nearly all targets relative to the observed baseline. It still does not observe a translation apparatus or a folded molecule.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out readback.}
The leave-one-codon-out run recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, and $8$ predictable cases. The recovery rate was exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The matching threshold flag also recorded $1.0$. This is a strong finite code-read result for the codons covered by the test: every tested case is recovered through predictability or boundary placement. The absence of anchors is part of the result, not an omission; the observed recovery is routed through the boundary and closure pattern rather than through fixed anchor codons.

\paragraph{Family-level behavior.}
Across $5$ code-family classes, the family hit rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The non-unit value for alternative yeast matters: the evidence is high but not a proof that every family behaves identically. The correct conclusion is family-robust local readback in this finite survey, with one recorded family below perfect recovery.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The $13$ boundary codons are completely assigned to six named modules. The module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The audit reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons. Each module has either a predictable route or a boundary route. This closes the finite classification of the observed boundary set for this code-read packet.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The evidence supports a precise and limited conclusion. In the six-bit codon cube, the standard-code label table determines a rare local boundary-tile pattern: $R$ has $13$ codons, face-vector $(13,16,4)$, a nontrivial spectral profile with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, an exact-shape empirical value of $0.0005$, and a high-overlap empirical value of $0.0125$ under the main randomization. The amino-acid-class preserving null gives no exact matches in $2000$ samples and a lower-tail value of $0.0035$. The leave-one-code and leave-one-codon tests give high recovery at the code-label layer, including a total leave-one-codon recovery rate of $1.0$.

\paragraph{Scope limit.}
The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label readback only. It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. A sequence of amino-acid labels is not a protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological principle. Any future assertion across those boundaries requires a separate contact whose observations live at that higher layer. Until such a contact is attached, this record remains a finite code-read result about local shape in $Q_6$.
