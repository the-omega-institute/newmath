\subsection{Window-six codon tile shape significance}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite object and readout.}
The record concerns the standard genetic-code table viewed only at the codon-label level.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, equivalently the $64$ possible codons displayed as three nucleotide positions with two finite coordinates per position.  The biological contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label.  In this chapter, that table is used only as a label readout on finite codon rows.  It is not treated as an observation of ribosomal translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, causal mechanism, or any universal biological law.

\paragraph{Boundary row $R$.}
The distinguished boundary row set has size $13$.  The verified codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is verified as a punctured-cube shape with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-level object is not merely a set of exceptional labels; it is a finite subcomplex of the codon cube with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ square faces.  The role of this row is local: it marks a tile-like boundary pattern inside $Q_6$, not a law governing all biological realizations.

\paragraph{Observed shape statistic.}
For the primary shape test, the observed set $M$ has size $20$.  In $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows, the run used seed $42$, made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one comparison matched the observed shape, while $25$ comparisons had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The resulting empirical probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the observed window-six shape is uncommon under the stated finite sampling procedure.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.  The statement is narrower: the standard-code label table, when read through the six-coordinate codon display, exposes a local shape whose exact finite pattern is rare among the sampled controls.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving control.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information while testing the same kind of shape claim.  In $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, the exact shape count was $0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed size $|M|=20$ was $7$.  The recorded probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This control keeps the result at the label-table level: the shape persists as a finite codon-label phenomenon even when amino-acid-class preservation is enforced.  It still gives no evidence for folded structure, activity, cellular context, or organism-level mechanism.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The finite shape also has a verified spectral signature.  The numerical values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value recorded for the cubic-polynomial residual.  The spectral-radius entry for the observed component is $0.6752479308830213$.  Since the residual is near numerical zero, the spectral row is consistent with a stable finite-coordinate calculation for the local codon tile.  This is a mathematical diagnostic of the encoded shape, not a statement about molecular energetics or protein geometry.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
Across $23$ evaluated code tables and $63$ total targets, the leave-one-code-out probe recorded a baseline hit rate of
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding hit rates for the shape-guided recovery were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T\ \mathrm{plus\ boundary})&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{above\ baseline}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
By family, the above-baseline rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial or plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate codes, across $5$ recorded families.  These figures show that the local tile has predictive value for code-label variation under this finite leave-one-code-out design.  They do not license a claim that the same geometry explains translation machinery or downstream biological function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases.  Its recovery rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value recorded as above threshold.  The module classification covers all $13$ codons of $R$ with no missing or extra codons: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, extra module codons $0$, missing-from-module codons $0$, and unassigned codons $0$.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable or boundary status in the finite record.  This supports the interpretation that the punctured-cube row is organized by recognizable codon-label modules rather than by an arbitrary list of exceptional entries.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite evidence supports a code-read claim: within the six-coordinate codon cube, the curated standard-code label table exhibits a rare local tile shape, a $13$-codon punctured boundary row with face vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral calculation, and strong leave-one-out recovery behavior.  The exact-shape probability $0.0005$, high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$, amino-acid-class lower-tail probability $0.0035$, and leave-one-code-out rate $0.9841269841269841$ above baseline all point in the same finite direction.  The permissible conclusion is therefore a bounded one: the label table contains a significant local codon-coordinate pattern.  No translation process, folded-protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this chapter unless an independent contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
