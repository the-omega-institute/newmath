\subsection{Window-six local tile significance for the standard code}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with coordinates for the three nucleotide positions. The BioReality contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard genetic-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and this contact is used only for code-layer readback. The finite row tests whether the standard-code label table exposes a local boundary-tile pattern in the window-six display. It does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality. Those claims require separate contacts and are not consequences of the local codon geometry.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported by the punctured-cube check, with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the contact is not merely a count: it carries a finite shape certificate in the codon cube. The row touches the code labels at singleton-corner and three-point label-class boundaries, while still remaining a local tile rather than a global biological law. The finite-row statement is therefore: the standard code supplies a codon-label readback in which this particular $13$-codon boundary has the punctured-cube signature $(13,16,4)$.

\paragraph{Monte Carlo shape significance.}
For the primary shape-significance test the run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ distinct comparison rows. Producing those $2000$ distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space is visibly collision-heavy under the sampling rule. The standard-code shape occurred exactly once among the $2000$ sampled rows, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The relaxed high-overlap statistic counted $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers support the local claim that the observed codon tile is an uncommon finite shape under this null draw. They do not promote the claim beyond the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information. Under this class-preserving comparison with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the exact-match count is $0$, so the reported exact-shape probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports observed $M$-size $20$ and lower-tail count $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This makes the finite observation sharper: even after preserving amino-acid class structure, the observed local pattern is rare in the sampled class-preserving rows. The data provide the size $|M|=20$ but not a full codon listing for $M$ in this row, so the prose uses the reported size and shape statistics rather than inventing an $M$ list.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The code-table generalization probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. By contrast, the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. These numbers say that the code-layer boundary rule has strong leave-one-code-out readback power across the tested table family. They still speak only about label recovery in codon tables. A successful label readback is not a folded-protein prediction, not a physical admissibility proof, and not a function claim.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The chapter claim is a finite code-read claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose standard-code instance is rare under the reported null models and coherent across spectrum, leave-one-code-out, leave-one-codon-out, and module-classification probes. The curated standard-code table is the reality contact, and it supplies codon-to-label assignments only. No translation process, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows from these finite rows unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
