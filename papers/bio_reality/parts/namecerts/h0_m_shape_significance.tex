\subsection{Window-six codon-tile shape significance}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-m-shape-significance}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}  This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, equipped with the distinctions used by the window-six display: coordinate position, closure, spectrum, and relation.  The biological contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  That contact is a label-readback bridge only.  It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universality theorem.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}  The finite row isolates a distinguished boundary set $R$ of thirteen codons.  The row is not a verbal description but a concrete finite subset of the codon cube:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
|R| &= 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-read phenomenon being measured is a local codon-tile boundary pattern inside $Q_6$, not a global law of biology.

\paragraph{Shape-significance assay.}  The shape-significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparators.  Distinct sampling required $57377$ attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported tail counts should be read as finite-row Monte Carlo evidence under the stated generator rather than as a closed-form probability law.  The exact observed shape occurred once among the $2000$ samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A high-overlap relaxation at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\ge 0.9\}&=25,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The local window-six shape is therefore uncommon under this finite null sampler, both at exact equality and at the high-Jaccard relaxation.  The conclusion is deliberately narrow: it supports a non-random-looking local code-read tile, not any claim about biochemical necessity.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}  A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In that assay, again with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$-size was $20$.  The exact $M$-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$.  The finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M|_{\mathrm{observed}}&=20,\\
\#\{\text{aa-class exact shape matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
\#\{\text{lower tail}\le |M|_{\mathrm{observed}}\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the code-read interpretation: the boundary pattern is not explained away merely by preserving coarse amino-acid classes in this finite assay.  It still remains a code-table statement, because the assay has only the curated codon-to-label table as its external contact.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}  The finite-row verdict is that window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically rare and internally structured local boundary tile in the standard-code label table.  The contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for local split checks.  It is not sufficient for translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any claim crossing that boundary requires a separate higher-layer reality contact, such as an explicit translation-system contact, a structure experiment, a physical admissibility model tied to observation, or a functional assay.
