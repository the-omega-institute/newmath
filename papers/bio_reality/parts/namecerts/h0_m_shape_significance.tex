\subsection{Window-six codon shape significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The record uses the six-bit codon display as its finite carrier.  The biological contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label.  This contact is deliberately narrow: it supports codon-label readback and local label-split checks, but it is not a contact with translation dynamics, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  The claim studied here is therefore a code-read claim about a local tile in the codon cube $Q_6$, not a global biological law.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The observed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row was checked as a punctured-cube shape with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite evidence is not merely that thirteen codons were selected.  It is that the selected codons form a particular local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-faces in the window-six coordinate presentation.

\paragraph{Monte Carlo shape test.}
The primary null experiment sampled $N=2000$ distinct candidate rows using seed $42$.  Because many random attempts repeated already sampled rows, the run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  In this sample, exactly one row matched the observed shape exactly, and $25$ rows had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ to the observed shape.  The finite-row estimates are therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=1/2000=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers are code-layer shape statistics.  They say that the observed local tile is rare under the sampled coordinate null; they do not say that the tile forces any biochemical process.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A stricter label-aware null preserved amino-acid class information while testing the same code-read shape.  In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ row had size $20$.  The experiment found $0$ exact shape matches and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  Its reported values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This separates the coordinate-tile observation from a trivial explanation by label-class preservation alone.  The row does not become a translation, folding, physical, functional, mechanistic, or universal claim; it remains a finite code-read anomaly relative to the tested nulls.

\paragraph{Spectral readback.}
The same finite carrier supplies a spectral summary.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual was $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also reported as the cubic-polynomial residual.  The spectral radius in tolerance was $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers give an internal coordinate-and-spectrum readback of the finite tile.  They do not add an independent reality contact beyond the curated code table.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the run had $5$ families: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each had hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  These are robust finite-row readback checks for code-label variation, not evidence for protein realization.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe reported no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases.  Its recovery rate was $1.0$, and the above-threshold recovery rate was also $1.0$.  The module classification was complete: $R$ had size $13$, all $13$ codons were assigned, and there were no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module had a predictable-or-boundary explanation in the finite readback.  This supports the local tile account as a code-layer record: the codon table supplies labels, the coordinate system supplies the tile, and the probes check whether the local split survives finite deletion tests.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The claim is ready only with a strict scope boundary.  The local codon-tile pattern may be reported as a significant finite coordinate feature of the curated standard-code readback.  It may not be promoted to translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA biologically obeys the window-six geometry as necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still label readback.  A separate higher-layer contact would be required before any statement about folding, physical feasibility, or function could be made.
