\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.}
This BioReality record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display. The internal carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, while the biological contact is only the curated standard-code label table: each codon is read back as an amino-acid label or a stop label. The contact is therefore a code-read contact, not a translation apparatus, not a folding experiment, and not a physical realization certificate. The BEDC-side distinctions used in this row are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class. The internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the readback is the standard-code label table.

Thus the row is not merely a count: it is a concrete finite subshape in the codon cube, with thirteen displayed codons, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces under the local coordinate probe.

\paragraph{Shape significance row.}
The shape-significance experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the effective sample was deliberately constrained by distinctness. Among the $2000$ comparison rows, there was exactly one exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$. The resulting finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape row therefore supports a narrow code-layer statement: the standard-code labels, when read through this window-six local tile, produce a rare shape among the sampled finite alternatives. The high-Jaccard tail is larger but still small at $0.0125$, which records nearby shape recurrence without collapsing it into exact equality.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class structure and again used $N=2000$ with seed $42$. In that null row the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. The recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is stronger than the unconstrained exact-shape row in the limited sense that no exact amino-acid-class-preserving sampled row reproduced the observed shape. It does not say that biology must implement the geometry. It says that, inside this finite comparison protocol, the observed code-read shape is uncommon even after the label-class constraint is retained.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}

Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and the supplied finite rows give shape, null-model, spectrum, leave-one, and module evidence for that code-read structure. The curated standard-code table is the only reality bridge used here. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure. A local codon tile is not a global biological law.
