\paragraph{Finite display.}
The claim concerns the standard-code codon table after it is placed in a six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is read as a six-bit display, and the only biological labels admitted in this packet are the curated standard-code amino-acid and stop labels.  The construction therefore tests a code-layer shape, not a biochemical mechanism.  The finite boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$, with the observed codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\\
&\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row records $R$ as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  The companion $M$ population is observed with size $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving control.  No claim here uses an unlisted codon outside the curated table; the standard-code table is the sole contact that supplies labels.

\paragraph{Shape statistic.}
The finite randomization audit used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparators.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the row is not being read as a continuous asymptotic estimate.  Against this finite comparison set, there was exactly $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jac}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  These are finite-row significance values for the window-six codon shape: the exact shape is rare in the sampled null family, and the near-shape event remains uncommon but not absent.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving control.}
A separate null model preserves amino-acid class data while reassigning the relevant finite structure.  In that row, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact match count is $0$ among $N=2000$ trials, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The resulting exact-shape value is $0.0$ at the recorded sampling resolution, while the lower-tail value is $0.0035$.  This control is important because it keeps the readback at the label-table level: it asks whether the codon-label partition has an unusual local form under an amino-acid-class-respecting shuffle, not whether molecular translation or protein behavior has been derived.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The chapter therefore records a finite, reproducible code-table phenomenon.  Window-six coordinates on $Q_6$ isolate a local boundary tile; the curated standard genetic-code table supplies the labels; the shape has low sampled exact-match probability; and the leave-one rows show high recovery for the tested label readbacks.  The valid conclusion is a code-read conclusion: the standard-code labels occupy a locally structured finite region that is statistically unusual under the recorded controls.
