\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Shape Significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record concerns the standard genetic-code table only at the level of codon-label readback.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube obtained by writing a codon over the alphabet $\{\mathrm{A},\mathrm{C},\mathrm{G},\mathrm{U}\}$ as a window-six binary display.  The observed boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured-cube object this same set has face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the record is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it is a local tile with a specified vertex, edge, and square-face count inside the six-coordinate codon display.  The amino-acid and stop labels used to identify this tile are supplied by the curated standard-code table; the coordinate geometry by itself does not assign biological labels.

\paragraph{Observed shape and randomization scale.}
The shape-significance run used seed $42$ and sampled $N=2000$ distinct randomized configurations.  Producing those $2000$ distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  Against this null ensemble the exact observed shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A relaxed comparison at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$ produced $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The numerical claim is therefore modest and local: the particular tile shape is rare under this finite sampling protocol, and near-shape agreement is still uncommon.  The result is not a proof that the codon table had to take this form, and it is not a mechanism for translation.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information while randomizing within the relevant finite constraints.  In that run the observed $M$-side size was $20$, the sample size was again $N=2000$, and the exact-shape count was $0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, so the reported finite probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}}&=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the code-layer observation because the tile is not only unusual under a broad shuffle; it also remains unusual when a coarse amino-acid-class constraint is retained.  The available row does not list the individual elements of $M$, so this chapter records its observed size and its role in the sampled tests without inventing an absent codon list.

\paragraph{Spectral summary.}
The local relation also has a finite spectral signature.  The recorded eigenvalue parameters are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check returned the residual
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is numerically indistinguishable from zero at the stated tolerance scale.  Thus the finite row supplies both a combinatorial tile and a compatible spectral readout: the same code-layer object is visible as a $13$-vertex boundary set, as a punctured-cube face count, and as a small spectral triple.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out evaluation examined $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, while adding the boundary raised the rate to $0.9841269841269841$; the same $0.9841269841269841$ value is reported as the rate above baseline.  By family, the above-baseline rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers say that the finite tile is useful for code-layer recovery tests; they do not say that the tile carries a physical or functional explanation.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen boundary codons are fully partitioned by the module classification row.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has either predictable or boundary status in the recorded finite test.  This is the cleanest form of the claim: the standard-code label table exposes a sharply organized local boundary tile in window-six coordinates, and the tested recovery probes repeatedly find that tile relevant at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Local scope of the claim.}
The reality contact for this chapter is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test codon-label readback and local tile-label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal generative mechanism.  Amino-acid label sequences are not folded proteins, CUN or UUR wording is not structure evidence, and a local codon tile is not a global law of biology.  Any claim above code readback requires a separate observation at that higher layer.  Within this boundary, the finite evidence is specific: a $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, exact-shape probability $0.0005$, high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$, amino-acid-class lower-tail probability $0.0035$, spectral parameters $(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)=(0.6752479308830213,0.4758352843197002,3.051487585298128)$, and leave-one recovery rates near one for the tested code-layer probes.
