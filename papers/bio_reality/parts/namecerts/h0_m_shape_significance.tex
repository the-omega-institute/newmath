\subsection{Window-Six Local Codon Tile Significance}\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate record.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite label readback on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  The coordinate object is not a model of translation dynamics.  It is a finite display in which codon words are read as six-bit positions, local faces are inspected as tiles, and amino-acid or stop names are imported from a curated standard-code table.  The observed boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same thirteen codons form the punctured-cube boundary record with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The companion $M$-shape statistic is recorded as a twenty-position observed tile size in the amino-acid-class preserving null experiment.  The finite data supplied here do not include a separate explicit member list for those twenty $M$ positions, so this chapter uses the row count and the reported shape-match statistics rather than inventing a membership table.

\paragraph{Shape significance against random codon-label rearrangements.}
In the main finite randomization test, $N=2000$ distinct samples were requested with seed $42$.  Because the sampling was constrained to distinct tables, the run made $57377$ sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the $2000$ accepted samples, only one exact $M$-shape match appeared.  Thus the empirical exact-shape probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A looser comparison using Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ found $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers are small enough to make the local shape non-generic within this finite sampling scheme, while still leaving the conclusion at the code-table layer.  The record says that the observed codon-label table carries a rare local tile boundary under the chosen finite null; it does not say that the tile is a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null keeps amino-acid class information fixed and tests whether the local tile remains easy to reproduce under that stronger constraint.  In this run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$.  The exact shape count is $0$, so the reported exact-shape probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This class-preserving test is important because it prevents the chapter from attributing the whole effect merely to gross amino-acid category frequencies.  The finite signal survives as a local codon-label geometry fact under the stated randomized comparison.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The same finite object has a recorded spectral summary.  The $M$-side and $R$-side eigenvalue statistics are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated cubic or radial residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is numerically zero at the precision reported by the run.  The spectral row therefore provides an independent finite check on the same local object: the tile is not described only by a codon list or a Monte Carlo count, but also by a stable numerical relation among the recorded operators.

\paragraph{Leave-one-out recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ alternative code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
whereas the reported hit rate inside $M-T$ is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9365079365079365,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same value $0.9841269841269841$ is reported as the hit rate above baseline.  Family-level rows sharpen the picture: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  These rows support the claim that the local codon tile has predictive value for code-label perturbations under this finite protocol.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and module partition.}
The leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for all thirteen $R$ codons with no missing or extra module codons: $R$ size is $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and both the extra-module and unassigned lists are empty.  The finite module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has a predictable or boundary role in the supplied record.  This is the strongest finite form of the chapter claim: the local tile is not only globally rare under two null tests, but its thirteen boundary codons also admit a complete module-level accounting.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The supported conclusion is deliberately narrow.  Window-six coordinates expose a significant local boundary-tile pattern in the curated standard-code label table.  The evidence includes an exact-shape probability of $0.0005$, a high-Jaccard probability of $0.0125$, an amino-acid-class preserving lower-tail probability of $0.0035$, a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, a near-zero spectral residual, and strong leave-one-out recovery rows.  These facts justify a code-read claim about the finite standard-code table.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
No higher biological conclusion is licensed by this chapter alone.  The curated table supplies codon-to-label assignments, not ribosomal translation realization, not folded protein structure, not thermodynamic or kinetic admissibility, not organismal function, not a mechanism of code evolution, and not a universal law for biological systems.  Amino-acid label sequences obtained from this contact remain labels.  They do not become structures or functions without a separate experimental or curated contact at that higher layer.  Cross-organism codon usage, tRNA copy number, CUN/UUR behavior, structural order, and protein function must therefore remain outside the present claim until separately observed by an appropriate source.
