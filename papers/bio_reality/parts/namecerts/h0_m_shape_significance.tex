\paragraph{Shape-significance test.}
The direct shape null used $N=2000$ sampled finite rows with seed $42$.  Because the null was sampled under a distinct-row requirement, the run attempted $57377$ draws and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the $2000$ distinct samples there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The resulting empirical probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
For this finite row, the exact local codon-tile shape is therefore rare under the sampled null, and even near-shape agreement remains low.  The evidence is local and combinatorial: it says that the observed standard-code label split occupies an unusually specific window-six tile shape in $Q_6$.
