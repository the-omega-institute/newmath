\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite reality contact at the code-read layer. The carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read through a curated table assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop. The distinguished local boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a finite tile, the same row is reported as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The contact does not claim that the biological system is generated by this geometry. It says only that the curated standard-code table is sufficient to read labels back onto a six-bit codon carrier, after which a local tile split can be tested as finite data.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The observed $M$-shape row was tested against $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$. The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite-row audit records both the target sample count and the rejection pressure in the sample space. In those $2000$ controls, the exact observed shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} = 1/2000 = 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A relaxed comparison using Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$ produced $25$ matches, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row supports the narrower statement that the observed standard-code local shape is rare under this concrete null draw. The result is not a mechanism statement: the number $0.0005$ is an empirical finite-row surprise score for this coordinate-and-label construction, not a derivation of translation, physical admissibility, folded structure, biological function, or universality.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null preserves amino-acid class information. In this null, $N=2000$ controls gave $0$ exact shape matches. The observed $M$ size is $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below this observed size is $7$, yielding
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0/2000=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a stronger code-layer sanity check than an unconstrained visual comparison: even after preserving label-class structure, the observed local arrangement remains in a small lower tail. The row still has the same boundary: it tests codon-label readback and local split structure, not protein realization.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The $13$ codons of $R$ are completely assigned to six named modules. The module row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row states $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons. In addition, every module has either a predictable or boundary status. This is the finite-row reason that the local tile can be discussed as a structured code-read object rather than as a bare list of exceptional codons.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The claim is therefore local and finite. Window-six coordinates expose a significant local boundary-tile pattern in the curated standard-code table: exact-shape frequency $0.0005$, high-Jaccard frequency $0.0125$, amino-acid-class exact frequency $0.0$, class-preserving lower-tail frequency $0.0035$, complete $13$-codon module assignment, and recovery-rate $1.0$ in the leave-one-codon-out row. Nothing in this record promotes the tile to translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality. Any such promotion would require a separate reality contact beyond the codon-label table.
