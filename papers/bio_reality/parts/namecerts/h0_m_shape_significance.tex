\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the finite display in which a codon is read as a six-coordinate word and then compared with curated amino-acid or stop labels. The distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class. The internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation. The only external contact in this row is the curated standard-code table, which says that codons have amino-acid or stop labels. It does not say that the window-six geometry is translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its listed codons are $AAA$, $AGA$, $AGG$, $AUA$, $CUA$, $CUC$, $CUG$, $CUU$, $UAA$, $UAG$, $UCA$, $UGA$, and $UUA$. The punctured-cube check returns the same set and the finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-layer object is not merely a set of thirteen exceptional labels; it also has a finite local two-dimensional boundary profile inside the six-bit cube. This is the shape that the subsequent significance probe tests. The row does not supply a separate list of the twenty codons in $M$; it supplies $|M|=20$ through the amino-acid-class-preserving null row and reports the shape statistics for $M$ against sampled alternatives.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The shape-significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ distinct sampled alternatives. To obtain those distinct samples it made $57377$ sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. Among the $2000$ retained samples, exactly $1$ had exact shape agreement with the observed $M$ shape, and $25$ had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$. The resulting empirical probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
 p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row therefore supports a finite local-tile anomaly claim: relative to this sample design, the observed standard-code shape is rare under exact shape agreement and still uncommon under the high-Jaccard relaxation. This is a code-read statement about the curated label table. It is not a claim that biology must realize the shape, nor that the shape explains translation or protein behavior.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null comparison.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row gives an independent check on whether the local shape is just a trivial artifact of preserving label classes. In $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$. The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
 p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The zero exact count is an empirical finite-sample zero, not a mathematical impossibility theorem. It says that within this finite run no class-preserving surrogate reproduced the exact observed shape, while only $7$ of $2000$ landed in the lower tail at the observed size threshold.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The support condition is narrow. The curated standard-code table supports codon-label readback and local split checks. It cannot by itself test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, folded protein structure, biological mechanism, or universality. The blocked claims are therefore local to this chapter: no translation, no folding, no physical admissibility, no biological function, no mechanism, and no universal biological necessity follows from the window-six tile unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that evidence. Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer output, not a protein-structure observation.

The finite evidence supports the following bounded statement. In the six-bit codon display of the curated standard-code table, the observed local codon-tile shape has a thirteen-codon boundary row with punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, a twenty-codon $M$ size in the class-preserving null comparison, rare exact and high-Jaccard shape statistics under the reported finite samples, a coherent spectral row with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, and complete module assignment for all codons in $R$. The result is ready as a code-read BioReality record precisely because its boundary is explicit: it records a significant local label-table geometry and refuses promotion beyond the code layer without a separate contact.
