\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table read through a six-bit codon display. The carrier is the finite codon cube $Q_6$, and the only external contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. This contact has resolution only at codon-label readback. It supplies a label table, not a translation mechanism, not a folding observation, not a physical-admissibility test, not a functional assay, and not a universality theorem. The BEDC-side distinctions used in the row are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The internal structure recorded for the row is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation.

\paragraph{Shape significance row.}
The shape-significance experiment uses seed $42$ and $N=2000$ distinct sampled comparators. Reaching those $2000$ distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts and produced $55377$ duplicate retries. Among the $2000$ distinct comparator rows, exactly one exact shape match was observed, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap test found $25$ rows with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These are finite-row Monte Carlo frequencies, not asymptotic biological laws. They say that this local tile shape is rare under the sampled comparator procedure. They do not say that biological translation is caused by the geometry.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The admissible conclusion is narrow. Window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically sharp local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, with a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row, rare exact and high-Jaccard comparator frequencies, a closed numerical spectral fingerprint, strong leave-one-code-out recovery, complete leave-one-codon-out recovery, and a complete six-module classification of $R$. The inadmissible conclusion is any promotion beyond the contact. No translation process, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from this chapter unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information. Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains code-layer readback, not protein realization.
