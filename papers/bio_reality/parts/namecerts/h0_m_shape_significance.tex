\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read object on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The carrier is not a molecular mechanism and not a theory of translation. It is a finite display in which codons are read as six coordinate bits, local faces and corners are inspected, and curated amino-acid or stop labels are used only as external label contacts. The biological contact is therefore narrow: a curated standard genetic-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The BEDC-side distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes; the internal operations are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation.

\paragraph{Shape significance test.}
For the primary shape test, the row used $N=2000$ distinct sampled comparison shapes with seed $42$. The sampling required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite null space was being hit repeatedly rather than sampled as a loose continuous approximation. Exactly one sampled shape matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A relaxed high-overlap test counted $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both finite-row values support the limited claim that the observed local tile is non-generic under the sampled comparison protocol. They do not by themselves identify a biochemical cause, a translation rule, or an evolutionary pathway.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The contact for this chapter is the curated standard-code label table. That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Therefore the supported conclusion is deliberately local: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically unusual boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table, and the listed leave-one tests show strong finite readback behavior for that code layer. No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies it.
