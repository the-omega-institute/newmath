\subsection{Window-six codon tile significance}
\label{sec:h0-m-shape-significance}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record studies the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a display of the $64$ codons by binary nucleotide coordinates.  The experimentally distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a cubical subset it has face-count vector $(13,16,4)$.  The record also fixes an observed $M$-tile size of $20$, but the supplied finite row enumerates $R$ rather than the full list of the $20$ members of $M$.  Thus every codon named in this subsection is a directly supplied row value, while $M$ is used through its reported size, spectral value, and significance statistics.

\paragraph{Spectral signature.}
The local tile is not just a codon list; it carries a numerical shape signature.  The reported spectral values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports the cubic residual, equivalently the radial-polynomial residual, as $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  This is small at the scale of the displayed eigenvalue data and is treated here as a finite numerical consistency check for the recorded local shape.  It does not turn the codon coordinate system into a biochemical mechanism; it only says that the row-level codon geometry has a repeatable numerical signature under the chosen construction.

\paragraph{Randomization test.}
The shape-significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled comparison shapes.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, before the finite comparison set was obtained.  Among the $2000$ sampled rows, exactly $1$ matched the observed shape exactly and $25$ reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding empirical probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
 p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model used the same sample count and seed.  In that stricter comparison, the exact-shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, the reported exact-shape probability was $0.0$, and the lower-tail probability was $0.0035$.  These values support the narrow claim that the window-six coordinate construction finds a non-generic local codon-label pattern in the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Readback and modules.}
The code-read contact is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Family behavior.}
The family summary contains $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid tables, mitochondrial tables, nuclear-ciliate tables, and the candidate-other family, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  The module partition is complete on $R$: all $13$ boundary codons are assigned, with no extra module codons, no missing boundary codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has either predictable or boundary support in the supplied row.  This is a codon-label regularity statement, not an organism-level claim about translation dynamics or molecular realization.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The evidence supports a finite code-layer conclusion: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose observed shape is rare under the reported comparison schemes and whose boundary modules recover known standard-code label splits.  The evidence does not establish translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a causal mechanism, or universality across life.  Amino-acid label readback is only a table-level observation unless another contact supplies a structure, translation, fitness, or mechanism measurement.  In particular, CUN, UUR, stop, tryptophan, isoleucine, methionine, arginine, and lysine wording in the module names is used here only as code-table annotation; it is not evidence for folding, function, or a global biological law.
