\paragraph{Carrier and contact.}
This BioReality row records a finite window-six reading of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the ordinary codon display and then compared with a curated standard-code table.  The table contact supplies only codon-to-label readback: each codon is assigned to an amino-acid label or to stop.  The BEDC-side distinctions used in the row are the six-bit display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The biological object is therefore not translation as a physical process, and not protein structure, but the standard-code label table as a finite external contact.

\paragraph{Shape-significance test.}
For claim $\mathrm{h0.M.shape.significance}$ the finite randomization used seed $42$ and sampled $N=2000$ distinct comparison rows.  Because the generator rejected repeats, the run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Against this finite null ensemble, exactly one sampled row matched the observed shape exactly, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=1, & p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
A relaxed comparison at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ found $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{J\geq 0.9\}&=25, & p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both numbers are finite-row statements about the sampled code-label geometry.  They support the narrower claim that the observed local tile is unusual under the stated row generator.  They do not by themselves identify a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported statement is deliberately narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and the finite-row tests report unusually low exact-shape and high-Jaccard probabilities under the stated nulls.  The curated code table is the required reality contact for code-layer readback, but it is not a contact for translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  No such higher-layer claim follows from this row unless a separate contact supplies that layer.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
