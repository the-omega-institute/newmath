\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite reality contact rather than as a mechanism.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with codons read as finite coordinate rows and labels supplied by a curated standard-code table.  The readback contact says only that a codon is assigned to an amino-acid or stop label in that table.  It does not say that the window-six geometry is translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The chapter therefore records a code-layer shape fact: window-six coordinates expose a local tile boundary in the curated label table, while the labels themselves remain external reality contacts.

\paragraph{Shape significance under the finite null.}
For the primary shape-significance experiment, the finite-row audit used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled null rows.  Constructing distinct samples required $57377$ attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the $2000$ sampled rows there was exactly one exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The empirical probabilities recorded for the observed local tile are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same values are recorded as $p$-value significance scalars: $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$ and $p_{\mathrm{high\ Jaccard}}=0.0125$.  The result is a finite-row rarity statement about the observed window-six shape.  It is not a proof that biological translation is caused by this geometry, and it is not a claim that the geometry is a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The support condition is deliberately narrow.  The curated standard-code table supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, universal mechanism, or folded protein structure.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not a folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact that directly supplies that layer.  In the present finite row, no such separate contact is included.
