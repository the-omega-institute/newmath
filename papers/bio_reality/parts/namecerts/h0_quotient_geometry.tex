\paragraph{Finite-row carrier.}
The biological object recorded here is open-reading-frame eligibility as seen only through start/stop codon enumeration. The carrier is not an observed transcript, not a curated interval, and not a translated product. It is the finite codon row set produced by the name-certificate computation. The row set $M$ has cardinality $20$ and is exactly

\begin{aligned}

& CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite audit records $M$ as the union of the WNR and CUN contributions, with no additional biological contact attached to the union. A smaller motif row set $R$ is also present:
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
The motif compression check reports no missing rows and no extra rows relative to this displayed $R$. Thus the chapter records a finite combinatorial object with $20$ codon rows in $M$ and $13$ codon rows in $R$, not a biological realization of an ORF.

\begin{aligned}

&UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is $M$ without $ACA$ and $ACG$. The same finite run records
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\operatorname{Med}(R)\cup\{ACA\},
\end{aligned}
$$

These facts give a closed finite-row relationship among $R$, $\operatorname{Sat}_{f3}(R)$, and $M$, but they do not create an external interval contact. In particular, the row $AUG$ may appear in the finite codon enumeration, and the rows $UAA$, $UAG$, and $UGA$ may appear as stop-codon rows, without converting the enumeration into an observed open reading frame.

\paragraph{ORF-eligibility contact gap.}
The layer name is $orf\_eligibility$, but the packet has no reality-contact reference. Its contacts list is empty and its mismatch list is empty. The empty mismatch ledger is therefore not evidence of biological correctness; it only says that no attached contact has produced a contradiction inside this packet. The self-check reports three checked gates and records the boundary sentence that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. Consequently the admissible reading is narrow: this chapter records the finite quotient geometry that would have to be tested against a curated ORF interval, sequence database record, transcript evidence, or equivalent external biological contact.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
Nothing in the displayed finite rows implies translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. No protein product is claimed. No transcript is claimed. No start-to-stop interval is claimed as observed. No organism, genome, experimental system, expression context, or curated annotation is supplied. The quotient $\pi(M)$ and the vectors $f(M)=(20,38,26,8,1)$ and $f(\pi(M))=(10,14,6,1)$ are valid only as finite-row geometry. Promotion to ORF eligibility requires a separate contact that supplies an observed biological interval; promotion beyond ORF eligibility would require additional contacts appropriate to translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function.
