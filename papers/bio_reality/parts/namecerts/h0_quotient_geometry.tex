\subsection{Quotient Geometry as an ORF-Eligibility Boundary Record}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-quotient-geometry}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
The record named \texttt{h0.quotient.geometry} is a finite-row BioReality packet for open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration.  Its biological object is not an observed ORF, and it is not a translation, folding, physical-admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality claim.  The packet only says that a finite codon geometry has been enumerated and that this geometry can be held as a boundary probe for what an external sequence, transcript, or curated interval annotation would later have to test.  The local carrier is therefore intentionally recorded as awaiting reality contact, with no internal biological structure beyond the finite enumeration itself.

\paragraph{Contact gap.}
The contact list for this packet is empty.  The two local probes therefore have boundary status.  The first probe, \texttt{orf.eligibility.boundary.requires.contact}, breaks if the pipeline asserts ORF eligibility, translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, or biological function from start/stop-window enumeration alone.  The second probe, \texttt{orf.eligibility.contact.gap.check}, breaks if an ORF-eligibility claim is recorded while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation contact can test the proposed interval.  The required contact named by both probes is \texttt{curated.orf.interval.annotation}.

The self-check \texttt{self.claim} records the boundary text: BioReality self-check only; no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  It also records a checked gate count of $3$.  Consequently the chapter-level claim is deliberately narrow.  The finite rows support a quotient-geometry seed for the layer \texttt{orf\_eligibility}; they do not support an observed ORF claim.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate reality contact supplies the missing biological interval and the packet is checked again against that contact.
