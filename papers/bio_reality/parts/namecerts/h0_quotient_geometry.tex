This record concerns the biological object ``open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration,'' but only at boundary status. The finite data are a coordinate packet for the combinatorics of start and stop windows; they are not an observed open reading frame, not a translated product, and not a biological function. \paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
The self-check states that this is a BioReality boundary record and that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. Therefore the admissible conclusion is narrow: the packet gives a finite quotient-geometric seed for a possible ORF-eligibility test, with $20$ rows in $M$, $10$ quotient rows in $\pi_M$, and the displayed face-vector data. It does not claim translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, evolutionary universality, or even actual ORF eligibility. Any one of those claims would require a separate contact, such as a curated ORF interval annotation, sequence or transcript evidence, or another external biological interval record capable of testing the proposed window.

\paragraph{Probe status.}
Two boundary probes enforce the same restriction. The first says that a start/stop-window seed must remain at boundary status unless an external sequence or transcript contact supplies the observed biological interval. The second says that an ORF eligibility seed is admissible only as a contact-gap boundary record while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation contact can test the proposed interval. Since the contact list is empty here, both probes support only the boundary reading. The packet is therefore ready as a finite mathematical record of quotient geometry, but not ready as a biological realization claim.
