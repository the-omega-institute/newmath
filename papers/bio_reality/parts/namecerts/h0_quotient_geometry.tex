\paragraph{Interpretive limit.}
The chapter therefore supports only the following local statement.  A finite codon quotient geometry, together with a frozen start/stop-window classifier and a curated interval contact, verifies ORF-window eligibility on the stated dataset and controls matched decoys and boundary cases in that dataset.  Nothing stronger follows from this packet.  It does not establish translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or a universal law over genomes.  The quotient pattern and the $168$-row curated contact are separate ingredients: the first supplies a finite combinatorial coordinate system, while the second supplies the biological interval evidence needed to make an ORF-eligibility assertion.  Without such a contact, the quotient geometry remains only a testable boundary construction.  With the contact, the assertion is still confined to start/stop-window eligibility for the recorded curated intervals and controls.
