Its biological object is not an observed open reading frame; it is the question of what a start/stop-window enumeration would be allowed to claim before any separate sequence, transcript, or curated interval contact has been attached. The packet therefore carries a strict boundary status. It may record the finite codon geometry checked by the row computation, but it may not promote that geometry to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Reality-contact gate.}
The packet contains no attached reality contact: there is no curated ORF interval annotation, no sequence database contact, no transcript evidence, and no external biological interval against which an ORF-eligibility claim can be tested. The local self-check states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers, and the checked gate count is $3$. Therefore the admissible conclusion is only this: the row data define a boundary probe for what would have to be checked if a biological interval contact were later supplied.

The cannot-claim boundary is part of the mathematical content of the record. Start/stop-window geometry alone does not realize an observed open reading frame. It does not imply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, evolutionary universality, or organism-level relevance. Any such assertion would require a separate contact capable of testing that layer. Until that contact is present, the quotient geometry remains a finite-row NameCert boundary record for ORF eligibility and nothing stronger.
