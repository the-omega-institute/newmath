\paragraph{Contact gap.}
The reality-contact list is empty.  The two boundary probes therefore remain active.  The first probe says that a start/stop-window ORF-eligibility seed must remain at boundary status unless an external sequence, transcript, or curated annotation supplies the observed biological interval.  The second says that an ORF-eligibility seed is admissible only as a contact-gap boundary record while it lacks a testable external interval contact.  The required contact type is a curated ORF interval annotation.

Consequently, the local cannot-claim boundary is strict.  From the finite packet one may claim the listed $R$, $M$, saturation, and quotient-geometry facts.  One may not claim translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, cross-organism universality, or even an observed ORF interval.  Those claims require a separate reality contact capable of testing the proposed biological interval.  The self-check agrees with this boundary: three gates were checked, and the recorded boundary states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.
