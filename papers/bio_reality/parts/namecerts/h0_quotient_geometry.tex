\subsection{Quotient Geometry as an ORF-Eligibility Boundary Seed}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-quotient-geometry}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record named \texttt{h0.quotient.geometry} is a finite-row BioReality packet for open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration. Its biological layer is \texttt{orf\_eligibility}, but the layer is not asserted as a biological fact. The packet has no attached external sequence, transcript, or curated interval contact. Consequently the only admissible reading is a boundary reading: the finite codon geometry describes what would have to be checked before an ORF-eligibility claim could be made. It does not itself supply the observed interval.

The finite carrier visible in the verified row data is the twenty-element set $M$. The recorded equality $M=\mathrm{WNR}\cup\mathrm{CUN}$ is witnessed by
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&AAA,AAG,ACA,ACG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,\
& CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding verified cardinality is $|M|=20$. A second finite subset $R$ is recorded by motif compression as
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,\
& UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
For this row, the compression certificate reports no missing elements and no extra elements. Thus the finite motif description and the enumerated $R$-list agree exactly at the row level.

\paragraph{Saturation check.}
The finite closure check attached to $f_3$ records that saturation of $R$ yields all of $M$ except $ACA$ and $ACG$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=\{&AAA,AAG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\
& UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same packet records
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\mathrm{Med}(R)\cup\{ACA,ACG\},
\end{aligned}
$$
and records an empty $f_3$ boundary, written in the finite table as \texttt{f3\_boundary\_is\_zero = []}. These are row-level facts about the codon enumeration and its induced closure operation. They do not say that any biological transcript contains an eligible interval, and they do not say that a start codon and a stop codon have been observed in a curated biological context.

The row also records an empty product-decomposition witness, \texttt{M\_is\_product\_decomposition = []}. In this chapter that empty entry is read only as the absence of a certified product decomposition in the supplied finite-row data, not as a biological impossibility theorem.

\paragraph{Boundary interpretation.}
The packet is a geometry seed for an ORF-eligibility question, not an ORF observation. Its self-check records the boundary sentence: BioReality self-check only; no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. The same self-check records three checked gates. The local consequence is strict: start/stop-window enumeration may define a finite test surface, but it cannot by itself assert translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. It also cannot assert that the twenty codons in $M$, the thirteen codons in $R$, the saturation relation, or the ten quotient rows are realized in an organism, transcript, genome, plasmid, curated interval, or experimental assay.

\paragraph{Reality-contact requirement.}
The two boundary probes impose the same rule from different directions. The first says that a start/stop-window ORF-eligibility seed must remain at boundary status unless an external sequence or transcript contact supplies the observed biological interval. The second says that the packet is admissible only as a contact-gap boundary record while it lacks an external biological interval contact. Therefore the finite geometry is ready as a NameCert-style seed, but the biological claim is intentionally not promoted. A separate contact such as a curated ORF interval annotation would be required before the layer \texttt{orf\_eligibility} could be tested as a biological assertion.
