\paragraph{ORF eligibility boundary.}
The biological object named by the packet is open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration.  The verified rows include start-like and stop-like codon symbols such as $AUG$, $UAA$, $UAG$, and $UGA$, but their appearance inside $M$ or inside $\pi_M(M)$ does not by itself assert an observed open reading frame.  An ORF eligibility claim would require a separate reality contact, such as a curated sequence interval, transcript record, or annotation capable of testing whether a specific biological interval is present.  In the present packet the contact list is empty.  Consequently the result is a boundary record: it specifies what the finite start/stop-window geometry would make available for testing, while explicitly withholding the biological claim.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
No translation realization follows from this quotient geometry. The row facts show only that $M$ has twenty listed codons, $R$ has thirteen listed motif rows, $\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)$ has the displayed eighteen rows, $\pi_M(M)$ has ten quotient rows, and the two face vectors match the recorded finite computations.
