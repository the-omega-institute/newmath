\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
The boundary attached to this packet is strict. From the finite quotient geometry alone one may claim only that the recorded coordinate computation has the stated finite rows, set equalities, saturation relation, and quotient $f$-vectors. One may not claim translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, conservation, evolutionary universality, expression, transcript support, or curated ORF eligibility. One may also not promote the packet from boundary status to the layer of $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$ merely because start and stop codons occur in the enumerated set. The self-check states the same restriction: the packet is a BioReality self-check only, and no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. A later reality contact could test an ORF-eligibility claim by supplying a curated ORF interval annotation, a sequence database interval, or transcript evidence. Until such a contact is attached, this chapter records a finite quotient geometry and the precise boundary of what it cannot biologically assert.
