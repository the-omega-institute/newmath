\paragraph{Finite packet.}

Its biological object is open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration, but the present carrier remains only a finite row object: it is not an observed ORF, not a transcript interval, and not a translated product. with no missing motif rows and no extra motif rows. These are finite enumeration facts, not biological realization facts.

The empty certificate for $M$ being a product decomposition is recorded as a positive finite check in the data, so the row geometry may be read as a product-shaped finite combinatorial object. This reading is internal to the packet and does not license an external molecular interpretation.

This is a combinatorial boundary statement only. It does not say that $ACA$ or $ACG$ has a biological role in an ORF, and it does not assert any translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Reality-contact gate.}
The local ORF-eligibility layer is explicitly contact-gated. The two probes require a curated ORF interval annotation or an equivalent external sequence or transcript contact before any ORF-eligibility claim can be made. In the present packet the list of reality contacts is empty. Consequently the only admissible conclusion is the boundary conclusion: start/stop-window enumeration gives a finite object that says what would have to be tested, not that an ORF has been observed. The self-check records the boundary sentence that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers, with $3$ checked gates. Hence the chapter cannot claim translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Those claims would require a separate contact supplying an observed interval and a distinct test at the relevant biological layer.
