\paragraph{ORF-eligibility boundary.}
The biological layer named here is ORF eligibility from start and stop codon enumeration.  The packet therefore asks whether the finite codon geometry can serve as a boundary test for an external interval.  The answer is deliberately weaker than an ORF assertion: without a separate sequence, transcript, or curated-annotation contact, the row remains a boundary record.  Start and stop symbols can delimit a possible window, but they do not certify that the window occurs in a biological molecule, that it is translated, that its product folds, that it is physically admissible in a cell, that it has a function, that it realizes a mechanism, or that it supports a universal biological law.
