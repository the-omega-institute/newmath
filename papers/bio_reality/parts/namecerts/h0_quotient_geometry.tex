The carrier, distinctions, and readback are all marked as awaiting reality contact, and the only internal structure available at this stage is the finite codon geometry itself.  The packet therefore says what a start/stop-window enumeration has organized, not that an observed biological open reading frame has been found.

\paragraph{Boundary probe.}
The two local probes impose the biological boundary.  The first probe says that a start/stop-window ORF-eligibility seed must remain at boundary status unless an external sequence or transcript contact supplies an observed biological interval.  The second probe says that the packet is admissible only as a contact-gap boundary record while it lacks a sequence database hit, transcript evidence, or curated interval annotation.  Both probes require the same kind of missing contact, namely a curated ORF interval annotation.

This makes the finite-row verdict precise.  The packet may cite the verified motif list $R$, the twenty codons of $M$, the eighteen-row saturation set, the empty $f3$ boundary, the $M$ vector $(20,38,26,8,1)$, the quotient vector $(10,14,6,1)$, and the ten quotient rows of $\pi M$.  It may not claim that these rows identify an observed ORF.  It may not claim translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biochemical mechanism, biological function, or universality.  It may not promote ORF eligibility to any higher biological layer.  Such claims require a separate reality contact that can test the proposed biological interval.  The self-check run $0f47d2f0cf41$ records the same boundary in operational form: no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers, and the checked gate count is $3$.
