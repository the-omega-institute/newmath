\paragraph{Boundary rule.}
The local rule for this packet is strict.  A start/stop-window geometry may be stored as an ORF-eligibility seed only while it remains a boundary probe.  It becomes an ORF-eligibility claim only if a separate reality contact supplies a testable biological interval, such as a curated ORF interval annotation, a sequence database contact with the relevant interval, transcript evidence that fixes the interval, or another explicitly named contact capable of testing the proposed ORF boundary.  In the present finite data there are no such contacts.  The contact list is empty, the mismatch list is empty, and both probes demand the same missing kind of contact, namely $curated.orf.interval.annotation$.

Consequently the correct reading is negative and local: this packet records where an ORF-eligibility assertion would fail if it were made now.  It may say that the finite codon geometry has $20$ codon rows, $38$ one-dimensional faces, $26$ two-dimensional faces, $8$ three-dimensional faces, one top cell, and a $10$-row quotient with face vector $(10,14,6,1)$.  It may also say that the linked motif row $R$ has $13$ codons and that the linked saturation row has $18$ codons before the two missing $ACA,ACG$ entries restore $M$.  It may not say that an ORF exists in a genome, that translation occurs, that a folded product exists, that the product is physically admissible, that the product has biological function, that a mechanism has been identified, or that the pattern is universal across organisms.  The self-check row states this boundary directly: no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers, and the checked gate count is $3$.  This chapter therefore keeps $h0.quotient.geometry$ at ORF-eligibility boundary status.
