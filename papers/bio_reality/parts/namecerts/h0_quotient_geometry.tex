\paragraph{Biological layer.}
The biological label attached to this packet is open-reading-frame eligibility from start and stop codon enumeration.  The row does not say that the quotient geometry itself is an observed ORF.  Rather, it marks a layer boundary: start/stop-window enumeration can define an eligibility test only when a separate biological interval contact is supplied.  In the contact-gap state the carrier is still waiting for such contact, the only recorded distinction is the unresolved biological interval, the internal biological structure is absent, and the readback remains unassigned.  This is why the probe language asks for a curated ORF interval annotation before an eligibility assertion is allowed.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The allowed conclusion is therefore narrow.  The quotient geometry verifies a finite codon-set calculation with $|M|=20$, $|\pi(M)|=10$, and the displayed $f$-vectors.  The curated interval contact verifies that a frozen start/stop-window classifier separates the supplied positive, decoy, and boundary rows in this finite dataset.  Nothing in this chapter establishes translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, biochemical mechanism, biological function, or a universal genomic law.  Those claims would require additional contacts targeted to those layers.  The self-check row states the same limitation in audit form: three gates are checked, and no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.
