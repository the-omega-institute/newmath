\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The quotient geometry therefore supplies a finite codon-coordinate pattern and a verified quotient-size calculation, while the curated interval dataset supplies the separate biological rows on which ORF-eligibility can be tested.  The two roles must not be collapsed.  From the quotient vertices, the face vectors, the $20$-element codon packet, or the empty $f3$ boundary, one may not infer translation, folding, physical admissibility, molecular function, mechanism, or a universal biological rule.  Those stronger conclusions require their own contacts and their own tests.  The self-check for this packet records exactly this separation: three gates were checked, and no codon geometry was promoted across biological layers.  Thus the chapter records a bounded success for finite quotient enumeration and a separate bounded success for curated interval eligibility, while keeping all higher biological interpretations outside the claim.
