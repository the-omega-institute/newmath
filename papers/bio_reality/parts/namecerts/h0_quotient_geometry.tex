\paragraph{Boundary interpretation.}
The ORF-eligibility layer remains at boundary status because the reality-contact list is empty.  The two probes attached to this packet both enforce the same local rule: a start/stop-window seed requires a separate external sequence, transcript, or curated ORF interval annotation before it can become an ORF eligibility claim.  Without such a contact, the object is a testable contact gap rather than an observed biological interval.

This boundary is not a caution external to the mathematics; it is part of the claim.  The finite quotient geometry can organize start/stop-window enumeration, but it cannot by itself supply translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  No claim is made that any row in $M$, any row in $R$, any row in $\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)$, or any quotient row in $\pi_M$ is expressed, translated, stable, selected, conserved, functional, or physically admissible in a biological system.
