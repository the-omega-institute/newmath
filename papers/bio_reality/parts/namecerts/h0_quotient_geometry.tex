\paragraph{Object of the record.}
This record concerns open-reading-frame eligibility only at the level of start and stop codon enumeration.  The finite data do not assert that a transcript is present, that a nucleotide interval is translated, that a peptide folds, that a molecular configuration is physically admissible, that a biological function is realized, or that a mechanism has been identified.  The biological layer named here is $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$, and the evidence row is deliberately weaker than an observed ORF: it is a finite boundary test for what an ORF-eligibility claim would have to attach to before becoming a biological assertion.

\paragraph{Boundary rule enforced by the probes.}
Two boundary probes govern the record.  The first says that a start--stop-window seed must remain at boundary status until an external sequence, transcript, or curated annotation supplies the observed interval.  The second says that an ORF-eligibility entry is admissible only as a contact-gap record while it lacks such an interval contact.  The self-check row records $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  Therefore the local rule is: quotient geometry and start--stop enumeration may organize a finite eligibility test, but they do not establish translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Any one of those stronger readings would require its own independent contact and would not follow from the $R$, $M$, saturation, quotient, or one-window enumeration rows displayed above.
