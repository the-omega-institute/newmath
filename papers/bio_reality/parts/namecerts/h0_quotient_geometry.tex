\paragraph{Finite carrier.}

\paragraph{Boundary interpretation.}
The ORF-eligibility seed is admissible only as a contact-gap boundary probe. The two local probes attached to the packet have the same mathematical warning: a start/stop-window enumeration must remain at boundary status unless an external sequence, transcript, or curated interval annotation supplies the observed biological interval. A separate contact of type \texttt{curated.orf.interval.annotation} is required before the layer \texttt{orf\_eligibility} can be claimed. In the present packet, the contact list is empty, the reality-contact reference list is empty, and the mismatch list is empty. Empty mismatch data here means only that no contradiction has been recorded inside the finite-row packet; it is not positive biological evidence.

No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from this quotient geometry. The record may say that the codon carrier has $20$ rows, that the motif subset has $13$ rows, that the saturation list has $18$ rows, that the quotient has $10$ rows, and that the corresponding face vectors are $(20,38,26,8,1)$ and $(10,14,6,1)$. The packet is therefore a finite mathematical boundary object: it records exactly what a future biological contact would have to test, while refusing to turn the quotient coordinate itself into biological realization.
