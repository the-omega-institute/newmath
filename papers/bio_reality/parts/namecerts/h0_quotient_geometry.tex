\subsection{Quotient Geometry at the ORF-Eligibility Boundary}\label{subsec:h0-quotient-geometry}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under audit.}
The record named $h0.quotient.geometry$ is a finite combinatorial packet attached to open-reading-frame eligibility only at the level of start--stop enumeration.  Its biological object is not a translated molecule, a folded protein, a physical state, or a functional annotation.  It is the more modest question of whether a finite codon-window description can be organized as an eligibility boundary before any claim is made about realized biology.  In the audited row, the minimal biological carrier is explicitly still awaiting contact with observed reality: the carrier field, the distinction field, and the readback field do not yet supply an external sequence, transcript, or curated interval by themselves.

The finite codon set $M$ recorded in the audit has cardinality $20$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&AAA,AAG,ACA,ACG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,\
& CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
A smaller motif-compressed row $R$ is recorded with no missing and no extra codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,\
& UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite audit does not merely assert that a quotient exists; it supplies the exact codon inventory on which the quotient calculation is performed.

The row $M\_is\_product\_decomposition$ is empty in the audit output.  In this chapter that empty output is read negatively and conservatively: the finite computation records a quotient geometry, not a product decomposition theorem.  No biological mechanism is inferred from the quotient shape.

\paragraph{Saturation check.}
The same finite run records the saturation relation
$$
\begin{aligned}

&UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\},

which is $M$ with $ACA$ and $ACG$ absent.  The row also records
$$
\begin{aligned}

It does not convert the codon geometry into an observed open reading frame.  It says only that, inside this finite computation, the recorded saturation and median-extension rows match the listed codon sets.

\paragraph{Boundary against biological promotion.}
The ORF-eligibility seed attached to this quotient geometry has no contacts in the local row.  The associated probes require a curated ORF interval annotation before eligibility can be asserted as an observed biological interval.  Consequently, the quotient calculation may be used only as a finite boundary record for what would have to be tested by a separate biological contact.  Start and stop codon enumeration alone is insufficient for translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or universal genomic law.

A later curated interval contact is present elsewhere in the finite audit table and is useful for stating the boundary sharply.  That contact loaded $168$ rows from a curated interval dataset, with required input classes $boundary\_case$, $matched\_non\_orf\_decoy$, and $positive\_curated\_orf$.  It reports $72$ positive acceptances out of $72$, $72$ matched decoy rejections out of $72$, and a paired baseline win rate of $72/72$.  The positive, decoy, and paired exact one-sided binomial tails are all recorded as $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  Three held-out splits each have $24$ positives accepted at rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys rejected at rate $1.0$.  The boundary controls have $24$ cases and $0$ promotions.  These numbers belong to the separate curated-contact row, not to the bare quotient-geometry row.  They show the kind of external contact that can test interval eligibility, while preserving the rule that geometry by itself does not establish translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The audited content of $h0.quotient.geometry$ is therefore ready as a finite mathematical record of quotient geometry: $|M|=20$, $|\pi_M|=10$, $f(M)=(20,38,26,8,1)$, and $f(\pi_M)=(10,14,6,1)$.  It is not ready as an autonomous biological assertion.  The self-check row records $3$ checked gates and explicitly keeps codon geometry from being promoted across biological layers.  The admissible conclusion is that this packet supplies a precise finite coordinate geometry for an ORF-eligibility boundary; any stronger biological reading must be supplied by an independent curated sequence, transcript, or interval contact.
