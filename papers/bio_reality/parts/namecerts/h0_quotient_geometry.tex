\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The BioReality record attached to \texttt{h0.quotient.geometry} is a finite-row boundary record for open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration. Its carrier is not an observed transcript interval, not a translated product, and not a folded molecule. It is the finite combinatorial packet determined by the codon sets that were checked in the row data. The motif-compression row identifies the set
$R=\{\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}$.
The audit reports no missing and no extra codons for this equality, so the row-level statement is exactly that $R$ is this thirteen-codon motif union. A second row identifies the twenty-codon set

with $|M|=20$ and with the equality $M=WNR\cup CUN$ recorded by the finite enumeration itself. These are the only biological alphabet objects used by the present record.

\paragraph{Saturation and boundary shape.}
The finite saturation row states that the third closure stage generated from $R$ gives
$\operatorname{Sat}_{f3}(R)=M\setminus\{\texttt{ACA},\texttt{ACG}\}$,
namely the eighteen-codon list
$\{\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AAG},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{AUG},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UCG},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UGG},\texttt{UUA},\texttt{UUG}\}$.
The same row records
$$
\begin{aligned}
M&=\operatorname{Med}(R)\cup\{\texttt{ACA},\texttt{ACG}\},\\
\partial f3(R)&=\varnothing.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite computation supports a closed boundary statement about the enumerated codon geometry: within the supplied coordinate system, the recorded saturation has no residual third-stage boundary. This does not assert that an open reading frame exists in any genome or transcript. It only says that the finite start/stop-window coordinate packet has reached the stated closure shape inside the packet.

This is a coordinate claim internal to the row table. It is not a claim that the quotient rows are genes, transcripts, proteins, domains, structures, phenotypes, or mechanisms.

\paragraph{Reality-contact gate.}
The self-check row states that the packet is a BioReality self-check only and that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. It also records three checked gates. The local gate is therefore strict: start/stop-window enumeration may be stored as an ORF-eligibility boundary probe, but it cannot be read as translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality. Such a promotion would require a separate reality contact, for example a curated ORF interval annotation or an external sequence or transcript contact that tests the proposed biological interval. In the present finite-row data the contact list is empty, and both ORF probes require a curated ORF interval annotation. Therefore the correct BioReality verdict is boundary status: the packet says what a future contact would have to test, while making no positive ORF-eligibility claim about any biological object beyond the finite enumerations above.
