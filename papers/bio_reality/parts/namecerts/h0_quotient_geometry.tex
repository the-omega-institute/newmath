\paragraph{ORF eligibility boundary.}
The conjectural layer named by the packet is \texttt{orf\_eligibility}.  Two boundary probes govern the interpretation.  The first says that a start/stop-window ORF eligibility seed must remain a boundary probe unless an external sequence or transcript contact supplies the observed biological interval.  The second says that an ORF eligibility seed is admissible only as a contact-gap boundary record while it lacks an external biological interval contact.  Both probes require a contact of type \texttt{curated.orf.interval.annotation}; no such contact is attached in this finite-row packet.  The attached contacts list is empty, and the reality-contact reference list is also empty.

The checked finite geometry is therefore confined to row enumeration, saturation, and quotient-coordinate arithmetic.  It does not imply translation realization.  It does not imply folding.  It does not imply physical admissibility.  It does not imply biological function.  It does not imply a mechanism.  It does not imply universality across genomes, transcripts, genetic codes, organisms, or experimental conditions.  Any promotion from the displayed start/stop-window geometry to ORF eligibility, and any further promotion from ORF eligibility to translation, structure, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality, requires a separate reality contact capable of testing that biological layer.
