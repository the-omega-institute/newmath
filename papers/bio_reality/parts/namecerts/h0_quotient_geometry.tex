\paragraph{Finite packet.}
The packet records a finite start--stop-window geometry whose biological reading is deliberately held at the boundary of open-reading-frame eligibility.  Its carrier is not an observed transcript, not a curated genome interval, and not a translated molecule.  The carrier is the finite row object supplied by the codon-window computation, together with the explicit warning that the biological object remains awaiting reality contact.  In this chapter, the only admissible biological phrase is therefore ``ORF-eligibility boundary probe'': the packet says what kind of external interval contact would be needed before an ORF claim could be tested, but it does not itself supply that contact.
