\paragraph{Local claim boundary.}
For this chapter, the quotient geometry is therefore an admissible finite coordinate witness for a boundary-level ORF-eligibility screen, not for downstream biology. The permitted conclusion is narrow: the listed codon set $M$, motif row $R$, saturation row, and quotient table $\pi(M)$ are internally checked finite data, and an independent curated-interval contact can test whether a proposed interval belongs to an ORF-eligibility dataset. The forbidden conclusions are equally local. Even the $168$-row curated contact is bounded by its source, snapshot date, frozen classifier, held-out splits, matched decoys, and boundary controls. The self-check row records three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. That is the scientific role of this packet: it supplies a finite quotient geometry and a disciplined place where biological contact may enter, while preventing geometric enumeration from being mistaken for biological realization.
