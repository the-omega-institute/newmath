\paragraph{Local verdict and non-promotion rule.}
The finite conclusion is therefore two-level.  At the coordinate level, $h0.quotient.geometry$ supplies a verified quotient geometry for $M$, including the $20$-element codon set, the $10$-class quotient, and the listed face vectors.  At the biological-contact level, the curated interval experiment supplies an external ORF-interval test with $168$ rows, perfect positive acceptance on the sampled positives, perfect matched-decoy rejection, stable held-out folds, and zero promotion of boundary controls.  The self-check row records three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.

The admissible claim is consequently narrow: this packet supports an ORF-eligibility contact record for the stated finite data and stated curated interval source.  It does not establish translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Any such stronger assertion would require a separate contact whose observations are appropriate to that layer.
