\paragraph{Local boundary of the claim.}
The chapter therefore separates three levels.  First, the codon geometry is a finite quotient computation: $20$ codons in $M$, $13$ codons in $R$, quotient size $10$, face vector $(20,38,26,8,1)$ before quotienting, and face vector $(10,14,6,1)$ afterward.  Second, the seed ORF-window rule is a boundary-level eligibility test that requires an interval contact before it may speak about a biological window.  Third, the curated interval contact gives a scoped result on $168$ curated rows, with $72/72$ positive acceptances, $72/72$ decoy rejections, $0/24$ boundary promotions, and three passing held-out splits.  None of these levels licenses the following stronger conclusions by itself: realized translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or universal law.  Any such conclusion would require a further contact designed for that higher layer.

The self-check record reports that three gates were checked and records the boundary sentence that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.
