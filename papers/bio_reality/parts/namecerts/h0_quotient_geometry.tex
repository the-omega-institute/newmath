\paragraph{ORF eligibility remains contact-dependent.}
The seed boundary row states that open-reading-frame eligibility cannot be asserted from the quotient coordinates alone. Its two probes both test the same local discipline: a start/stop-window candidate may remain a boundary test until a separate sequence, transcript, or curated annotation interval supplies the observed biological object. If the pipeline were to infer ORF eligibility, translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, or biological function from start/stop enumeration alone, the probe would break. The self-check row is consistent with this restriction: it reports $3$ checked gates and explicitly says that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.

There is a distinct finite start/stop-window row with experiment identifier \texttt{c26a0d6656f7}. It loaded one external nucleotide-window record, classified all one of one input windows by start/stop enumeration, and fixed its scope as external-window start/stop enumeration only. It also recorded the scalar boundary value \texttt{no\_geometry\_to\_translation\_promotion} as \texttt{orf\_eligibility\_only}. This supports a limited eligibility classification when such a window is supplied, but it still does not turn the quotient geometry into translation evidence.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The quotient geometry row is ready as a finite mathematical record for the boundary of ORF eligibility. It gives the verified $M$ enumeration, the motif set $R$, the $f_3$ saturation behavior, the quotient representatives, and the face vectors for $M$ and $\pi(M)$. Its scientific role is to define a checked coordinate regime in which start/stop-window questions can be posed. The admissible biological reading is narrower: without a separate observed interval, the row is only a boundary record for an eligibility test. With the curated interval contact, the claim may be read as dataset-scoped ORF eligibility for the curated intervals and matched controls, while all stronger biological interpretations remain outside the row.
