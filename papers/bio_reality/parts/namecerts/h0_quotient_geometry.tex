\subsection{ORF Boundary Geometry from a Finite Start--Stop Quotient}\label{subsec:h0-quotient-geometry}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.} The packet records a finite codon-level boundary geometry for open-reading-frame eligibility, but it does not record an observed biological open reading frame. The carrier under inspection is the twenty-element set
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The enumeration check gives $|M|=20$, and the recorded equality is that this same set is $WNR\cup CUN$ in the packet's finite alphabet convention. The motif-compression row gives the thirteen-element set
$$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with no missing and no extra entries in the comparison against the motif union. This is a finite-row identity, not an expression claim, translation claim, or functional claim.

\paragraph{Saturation row.} The third finite row records that the saturation of $R$ misses precisely the two codons $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$ inside the twenty-element carrier. In the notation of the packet,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\},
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
with the displayed twenty codons as the resulting carrier. The boundary list for $f_3$ is empty, so the recorded obstruction at this level is not an unaccounted extra boundary codon. It is exactly the contact gap between a finite start--stop-window geometry and an observed biological interval.

\paragraph{Quotient geometry.} The quotient row identifies a ten-point quotient image $\pi_M$:

\begin{aligned}

$$

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is a finite quotient-geometric compression: twenty named codons collapse to ten quotient coordinates, with the recorded face-vector changing from five entries to four entries. The product-decomposition check is represented by an empty mismatch list, so this row supplies no internal counterexample to the finite quotient calculation.

\paragraph{Biological boundary.} The biological object named by the packet is open-reading-frame eligibility from start and stop codon enumeration. The contact list is empty, and the required contact named by both probes is a curated ORF interval annotation. Therefore the chapter can only assert the finite boundary record: the packet describes what would have to be tested if an external sequence, transcript, or curated interval contact were attached. It cannot assert that any displayed codon window is an observed open reading frame. It also cannot claim translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, universality, expression, evolutionary conservation, or organism-level relevance from the quotient geometry alone.

\paragraph{Probe verdict.} The two local probes agree on the same boundary condition. The first probe breaks if the pipeline asserts ORF eligibility, translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, or biological function from start--stop-window enumeration alone. The second probe breaks if the packet records an ORF eligibility claim while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation contact can test the interval. Since the contact list is empty and the self-check records only that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers, the row is admissible as a BioReality boundary record. Its content is the finite enumeration $M$, the motif subset $R$, the saturation relation for $f_3$, and the ten-point quotient geometry $\pi_M$; its non-content is equally important: no biological realization follows without a separate reality contact.
