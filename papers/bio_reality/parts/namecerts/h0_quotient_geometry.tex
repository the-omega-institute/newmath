\paragraph{ORF-eligibility reading.}
The ORF-eligibility reading is deliberately weaker than an ORF claim. A start/stop-window enumeration can describe where an eligibility test would have to look, but it cannot certify that the proposed interval exists in a genome, transcript, curated annotation, or protein product. The two local probes make this boundary explicit. The probe $orf.eligibility.boundary.requires.contact$ says that the packet must remain at boundary status unless an external sequence or transcript contact supplies the observed biological interval. The probe $orf.eligibility.contact.gap.check$ says that an ORF-eligibility assertion breaks if the packet records such a claim while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation contact can test the proposed interval.

Thus the current readback is contact-gap geometry: $M$ has $20$ codons, $R$ has $13$ codons, $\pi M$ has $10$ quotient rows, and the verified face vectors are $f(M)=(20,38,26,8,1)$ and $f(\pi M)=(10,14,6,1)$. These numbers are sufficient to name the finite packet and to state what a later reality contact would have to test. They are not sufficient to claim ORF eligibility as a biological fact.
