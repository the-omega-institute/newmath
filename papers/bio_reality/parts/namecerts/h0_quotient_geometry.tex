\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record treats open-reading-frame eligibility only as a boundary problem generated by finite start/stop codon enumeration.  The finite row carrier is the twenty-element set $M$ verified in the packet:

\begin{aligned}

&CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.

The same run records $|M|=20$ and identifies $M$ with the union of the WNR and CUN rows in the finite enumeration.  No biological interval is attached to these twenty symbols: they are not a transcript, not a genomic locus, and not an observed open reading frame.  Their role here is to expose the exact boundary at which a coordinate-level codon geometry would need a separate reality contact before it could become an ORF-eligibility claim.

The key biological point is negative: this quotient is an eligibility-boundary geometry, not an observation of an eligible ORF.  A quotient row can mark what a start/stop-window test would have to inspect, but it cannot by itself say that such a window occurs in a curated sequence, transcript, or annotated interval.

\paragraph{Boundary probes.}
The packet contains two boundary-mismatch probes.  The first probe, $orf.eligibility.boundary.requires.contact$, breaks if the pipeline asserts ORF eligibility, translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, or biological function from start/stop-window enumeration alone.  The second probe, $orf.eligibility.contact.gap.check$, breaks if an ORF-eligibility claim is recorded while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation contact can test the proposed interval.  Both probes require the same missing contact type, $curated.orf.interval.annotation$.  In the present packet the contact list has cardinality $0$ and the mismatch list has cardinality $0$, so the correct reading is not that ORF eligibility has been observed, but that no forbidden promotion has been made.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
This chapter therefore records a finite, self-contained boundary theorem for the BioReality packet.  The data prove only that the packet has a twenty-row codon carrier $M$, a thirteen-row motif subcarrier $R$, an eighteen-row $f3$ saturation excluding $ACA$ and $ACG$, and a ten-row quotient geometry with the stated face vectors.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these facts.  Such claims would require a separate reality contact, for example a curated ORF interval annotation or an independently specified sequence or transcript contact.  Until that contact is attached, the packet remains at ORF-eligibility boundary status: it describes exactly what would have to be tested, while refusing to count the coordinate geometry itself as biological realization.

\paragraph{Self-check status.}
The packet's internal self-check reports three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  This is the intended closure condition for the present row: the geometry is usable as a finite name-certificate boundary record, but it is not a carrier of higher-layer biological content.
