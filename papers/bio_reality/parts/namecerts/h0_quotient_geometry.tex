\paragraph{BioReality gate.}
The layer claimed by the packet is \texttt{orf\_eligibility}, but the packet has zero reality-contact references. Its two probes are therefore boundary probes. The first probe requires a curated ORF interval annotation before start/stop-window geometry may be treated as ORF eligibility. The second probe breaks if an ORF eligibility claim is recorded while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated annotation contact can test the proposed interval. Both probes forbid the same promotion: start/stop-window enumeration alone cannot be counted as an observed ORF, cannot establish translation realization, cannot establish folded structure, cannot establish physical admissibility, and cannot establish biological function.

This local gate is also backed by the self-check record: the boundary string states that the BioReality check permits no codon geometry to be promoted across biological layers, and the checked gate count is $3$. Thus the admissible conclusion is deliberately narrow. The packet is ready as a finite quotient-geometry and saturation boundary record for an ORF-eligibility seed. It is not ready as an ORF eligibility assertion about any biological sequence. Any such assertion requires a separate contact, such as a curated ORF interval annotation or another externally testable sequence or transcript record, and that contact must be evaluated independently of the present codon-window enumeration.
