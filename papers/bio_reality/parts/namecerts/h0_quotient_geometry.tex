\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The biological layer named here is $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$.  The packet deliberately remains at boundary status because there are no attached reality contacts: no curated ORF interval annotation, no sequence-database interval, no transcript evidence, and no independent biological observation that can test a proposed interval.  The two boundary probes state the same local rule from opposite sides.  A start--stop-window seed may be retained as a testable boundary record only while it lacks such contact; it breaks if the pipeline asserts ORF eligibility, translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality from the finite enumeration alone.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim clause.}
Nothing in the lists $R$, $M$, $\operatorname{Sat}_{f3}(R)$, or $\pi_M$ promotes this packet beyond finite combinatorics.  In particular, the quotient geometry does not establish translation, does not establish folding, does not establish physical admissibility, does not establish molecular function, does not establish an evolutionary mechanism, and does not establish universality across organisms or code variants.  A separate reality contact would have to supply an observed interval before any ORF eligibility claim could be made, and further separate contacts would be required before translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function could be claimed.  The self-check records exactly this boundary: three checked gates are present, but no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.
