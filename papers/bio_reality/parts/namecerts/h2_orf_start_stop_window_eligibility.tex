\subsection{Start--stop window eligibility for curated open-reading-frame intervals}\label{sec:bior-orf-start-stop-window-eligibility}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object considered here is not protein production and not molecular activity. It is the finite predicate which says whether a nucleotide interval passes the elementary start--stop-window enumeration used for open-reading-frame eligibility. The data row for this predicate has claim identifier $\mathrm{h2.orf.start\_stop.window\_eligibility}$ and belongs only to the layer $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$. In the direct window experiment, the external finite manifest was loaded with value $1$, had window count $1$, and the enumeration classified $1$ of $1$ supplied input windows. The manifest snapshot date was $2026$-$05$-$25$. Its declared scope was external window start--stop enumeration only, and the row explicitly recorded the outcome class as $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility\_only}$ rather than translation or any higher biological layer.

\paragraph{Reality contact for interval eligibility.}
A later curated-interval contact attached biological annotation to the same eligibility layer without changing the layer itself. The interval dataset was loaded from a curated ORF interval-contact table with $168$ rows, using NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals from \textit{Escherichia coli} K--12 MG1655 complete genome accession $\mathrm{NC\_000913.3}$, snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$. The protocol was frozen before external observation: classifier rules, baselines, held-out splits, and input classes were all recorded as frozen, with protocol time $2026$-$05$-$29\,19{:}11{:}13+00{:}00$ and external observation time $2026$-$05$-$30\,10{:}29{:}31+00{:}00$. The classifier identifier was $\mathrm{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}$, and the row states that the rules were not modified after contact.

\paragraph{Finite acceptance and rejection counts.}
The contact table separated three input classes: positive curated ORF intervals, matched non-ORF decoys, and boundary cases. The positive interval statistic accepted $72$ of $72$ positives, giving accept rate $1.0$ and exact one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. The matched decoy statistic rejected $72$ of $72$ decoys, giving reject rate $1.0$ with the same upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. The predeclared pairwise baseline comparison recorded $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, again with win rate $1.0$ and upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. Boundary examples were not converted into positives: $0$ of $24$ boundary cases were promoted, so the promoted fraction was $0.0$.

\paragraph{Held-out stability.}
The held-out split audit used three folds. In the left-locus fold $24$ positive intervals were accepted at rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys were rejected at rate $1.0$. The middle-locus fold repeated the same finite counts, $24$ positives and $24$ decoys, both at rate $1.0$. The right-locus fold again recorded $24$ positives and $24$ decoys with rates $1.0$ and $1.0$. Thus the split-level row has split count $3$, positive total $72$, decoy total $72$, and no split-level failure. This supports the narrow statement that the frozen start--stop-window classifier matched the curated interval labels and their matched decoys in this finite manifest.

\paragraph{Local boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion is deliberately limited. Start--stop-window eligibility means only that the supplied finite interval has the enumerated boundary pattern required by this classifier under the curated-contact protocol. It does not assert that a ribosome translated the interval, that a protein product exists, that any folded structure is present, that a physical pathway is admissible, that a biological function is realized, that a molecular mechanism has been identified, or that a universal genomic law has been established. The self-check row recorded three checked gates and stated that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. The present section therefore records a finite, externally attached interval-eligibility result: $1$ direct test window classified by enumeration, $168$ curated-contact rows audited, $72$ positive intervals accepted, $72$ matched decoys rejected, $24$ boundary cases kept unpromoted, and three held-out folds passing. Any stronger biological assertion would require another contact whose data directly measure the stronger layer.
