\subsection{Start--stop window eligibility as a bounded ORF contact test}
\label{subsec:h2-orf-start-stop-window-eligibility}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record concerns a narrow biological question: when a finite nucleotide interval is supplied as an observed input, can a start--stop enumeration procedure classify that interval as an open-reading-frame eligibility case without also asserting translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality? The answer recorded here is deliberately local. The packet is allowed to say that a curated interval has the syntactic boundary pattern required by the chosen start--stop rule. It is not allowed to say that a peptide was made, that a protein folded, that a cellular role exists, or that the same rule governs other organisms or other datasets.

\paragraph{Local readout.}
The correct readout is therefore conditional and layer-specific. Given the curated interval table, the classifier separates the recorded positive, decoy, and boundary classes exactly in the reported finite sample. Given only the codon geometry or only the start--stop pattern, no biological realization is obtained. In symbols, the admissible conclusion has the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{curated\ interval\ contact}+\mathrm{start/stop\ enumeration}
&\Rightarrow \mathrm{ORF\ eligibility\ classification\ on\ this\ finite\ table},\\
\mathrm{start/stop\ enumeration\ alone}
&\nRightarrow \mathrm{translation,\ folding,\ physical\ admissibility,\ function,\ mechanism,\ or\ universality}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The self-check row agrees with this boundary: it records three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
This chapter does not claim that any listed interval is translated, that a peptide product exists, that a product has a stable fold, that a molecular structure is physically admissible, that a function is present, that a mechanism has been identified, or that a universal biological law has been found. Those claims require separate contacts of the appropriate kind. The present row only says that a finite, curated annotation contact has been supplied for ORF-eligibility testing, and that the named start--stop classifier exactly separates the reported rows within that contact.
