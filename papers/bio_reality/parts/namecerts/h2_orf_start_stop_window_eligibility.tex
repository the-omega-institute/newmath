The verified run for this claim used experiment identifier \texttt{c26a0d6656f7}. Its input was a curated finite nucleotide-window manifest dated 2026-05-25, and the manifest was loaded as an external annotation input rather than as a fact derived from the combinatorial coordinate system. The run reports one loaded window and one classified window:
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{window\_count} &= 1.
\end{aligned}
$$
The scope of the run is explicitly \texttt{external\_window\_start\_stop\_enumeration\_only}. Thus the finite output is an eligibility-level classification for a supplied window, not a statement that the window is translated, expressed, folded, stable, functional, mechanistic, or representative of all genomes.

\paragraph{Eligibility verdict.}
For the specific start/stop-window run, the module classification is \texttt{orf\_eligibility\_only}. The record therefore licenses only the following narrow statement: the one externally supplied nucleotide window in the 2026-05-25 manifest was classified by the start/stop enumeration procedure. It does not license an ORF claim for an unsupplied interval, and it does not convert the finite codon lists into a biological event. The self-check run \texttt{0f47d2f0cf41} records the same boundary in aggregate form, with three checked gates and the condition that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.

\paragraph{Separate contact comparison.}
A later curated-interval contact, experiment \texttt{7c9b30b02d46}, shows what a stronger external attachment looks like without changing the status of this seed. That contact loaded 168 rows from an NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS-interval dataset for \textit{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 accession NC\_000913.3, dated 2026-05-30. It contained 72 positive curated ORF intervals, 72 matched non-ORF decoys, and 24 boundary cases. The positive accept rate was $72/72=1.0$, the matched decoy rejection rate was $72/72=1.0$, and the one-sided binomial upper-tail value recorded for the positive, decoy, and paired baseline tests was $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. Three held-out splits each had 24 positives and 24 decoys, with positive accept rate $1.0$ and decoy rejection rate $1.0$. The 24 boundary cases had zero promotions. These contact results may support an interval-contact layer for that dataset, but they do not retroactively turn start/stop enumeration alone into translation evidence.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is deliberately small. Start and stop codons can delimit an eligibility window after an external nucleotide record supplies the window to be tested. Without such an attachment, the packet remains a boundary test for what would have to be observed. Even with the one-window enumeration result, the paper does not infer translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, mechanism, organismal role, or universal biological law. Any step from enumeration to those stronger layers requires a separate finite contact that directly measures or curates the relevant biological object.
