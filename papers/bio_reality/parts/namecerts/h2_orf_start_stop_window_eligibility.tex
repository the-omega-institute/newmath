\paragraph{Finite packet.}
This record concerns open-reading-frame eligibility when the only operative test is finite start/stop codon enumeration on an externally supplied nucleotide window. The packet is deliberately narrow: it records that the input window can be classified by the stated boundary rule, and it does not assert that translation occurs, that a peptide is produced, that a fold is reachable, that the molecule is physically admissible, that a phenotype is affected, or that any general biological mechanism has been identified. The finite run for this row loaded an external nucleotide-window manifest with $1$ window, and the enumeration classified $1$ of $1$ input windows. The recorded scope is exactly external-window start/stop enumeration, and the layer label is orf-eligibility only. In symbolic form the local enumeration certificate is the finite equality
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{classified windows}\} &= 1,\\
\#\{\text{input windows}\} &= 1,\\
\text{promotion target} &= \text{orf\_eligibility only}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The source line for the window manifest states that the rows are curated finite nucleotide-window annotations used as experiment inputs, not consequences of the BEDC codon geometry itself. Thus the mathematical contribution of this row is a boundary gate: start/stop syntax can sort a supplied window, but the biological interval must arrive from outside the geometry.

\paragraph{Reality-contact separation.}
The primary start/stop-window run has snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$25$ and experiment identifier c26a0d6656f7. Its data show exactly one loaded window and exactly one classified window. This is enough for a finite boundary statement: given the external row, the start/stop-window classifier assigns the row to the ORF-eligibility layer. It is not enough for a biological conclusion beyond that layer. The separate curated-interval contact row has snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$ and loaded $168$ rows from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 accession NC\_000913.3. In that separate contact, the required input classes were present: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs. \paragraph{Local verdict.}
The row therefore has a two-part reading. First, the start/stop-window packet itself is a finite syntactic classifier over an externally loaded window manifest: $1$ loaded row, $1$ classified row, and no promotion above ORF eligibility. Second, the linked curated-interval contact is a stronger external biological contact with $168$ rows, balanced positive and decoy counts, non-promotion of $24$ boundary cases, and exact binomial tail values of $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$ for the recorded positive, decoy, and paired-baseline tests. The boundary remains strict even after that contact: no translation event, folded structure, physical admissibility claim, biological function, mechanism, or universal law follows from codon geometry or from start/stop enumeration alone. Any such higher-layer assertion requires its own contact capable of testing that higher layer. The self-check row is consistent with this reading: it records $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.
