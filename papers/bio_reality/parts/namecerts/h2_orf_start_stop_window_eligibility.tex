\subsection{Start--Stop Window Eligibility}\label{subsec:bioreality-orf-start-stop-window-eligibility}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object and status.}
This record concerns the weakest biological layer at which a nucleotide window can be called eligible by start and stop codon enumeration.  The biological object is not a translated product, not a folded molecule, not a physically admissible configuration, and not a functional gene.  It is only the finite question whether an externally supplied nucleotide interval is classified by a fixed start--stop enumeration rule.  The claim is therefore intentionally narrow: a window may enter the $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility}$ layer only after an outside curated sequence or interval annotation supplies the interval to be tested.  Without such a contact, the same construction remains a boundary test describing what would have to be checked, not a biological assertion.

\paragraph{Codon background carried by the finite row.}
The row is anchored to a finite codon geometry already measured in the same audit packet.  The motif union $R$ contains exactly thirteen codons,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
|R|&=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified mediator set $M$ is the union $\mathrm{WNR}\cup\mathrm{CUN}$ and has cardinality $20$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\},\\
|M|&=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate saturation check reports that $\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)$ equals $M$ with $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$ removed, while the $f_3$ boundary is empty.  Thus the finite row uses a codon-level combinatorial substrate, but the row does not infer biology from that substrate alone.

\paragraph{Interval contact check.}
A linked interval-contact audit supplies a stronger finite stress test for the same boundary.  It loaded $168$ curated interval rows from a dataset of NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K--12 MG1655 accession $\mathrm{NC\_000913.3}$, using snapshot date $\mathrm{2026\mbox{-}05\mbox{-}30}$.  The required classes were present: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.  The protocol was frozen before the external observation: classifier rules, baselines, input classes, and held-out splits were fixed, with protocol time $\mathrm{2026\mbox{-}05\mbox{-}29T19{:}11{:}13Z}$ and external observation time $\mathrm{2026\mbox{-}05\mbox{-}30T10{:}29{:}31Z}$.

The finite statistics are exact at the recorded row counts.  There are $72$ positive curated intervals and all $72$ were accepted, giving accept rate $1.0$ and one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\cdot 10^{-22}$.  There are $72$ matched decoys and all $72$ were rejected, giving reject rate $1.0$ and the same one-sided binomial value.  The predeclared paired comparison records $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, win rate $1.0$, again with one-sided binomial value $2.117582368135751\cdot 10^{-22}$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{positive\ accepted}/\mathrm{positive\ total}&=72/72,\\
\mathrm{decoy\ rejected}/\mathrm{decoy\ total}&=72/72,\\
\mathrm{paired\ wins}/\mathrm{paired\ total}&=72/72,\\
p&=2.117582368135751\cdot 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The held-out stability check used three splits.  Each split had $24$ positives and $24$ decoys, positive accept rate $1.0$, decoy reject rate $1.0$, and passed.  The three split names were $\mathrm{refseq\_nc\_000913\_left\_locus\_fold}$, $\mathrm{refseq\_nc\_000913\_middle\_locus\_fold}$, and $\mathrm{refseq\_nc\_000913\_right\_locus\_fold}$.  Boundary cases were not promoted: among $24$ boundary records, the promoted count was $0$ and the promoted fraction was $0.0$.

These numbers are useful for reproducible enumeration, but they are not evidence of translation, protein folding, physical feasibility, mechanism, or biological role.  The self-check records three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is exactly this: for the finite external inputs supplied to the experiment, the fixed start--stop enumeration classifier classified all one window in the window manifest, and the linked curated interval contact accepted $72$ positive CDS intervals, rejected $72$ matched decoys, preserved three held-out split checks, and promoted none of $24$ boundary cases.  No stronger biological statement follows from this row.  Translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, cellular function, mechanistic explanation, and universality over genomes require distinct contacts designed for those layers.
