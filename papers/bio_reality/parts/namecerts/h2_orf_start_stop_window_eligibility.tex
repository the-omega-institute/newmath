\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The start/stop computation therefore has two different statuses that must not be conflated.  With only the finite one-window manifest, it shows that the supplied window is classifiable by the start/stop rule.  With the curated interval contact, it also shows that the frozen eligibility rule agrees with a finite annotated interval table and separates the matched decoys in the reported sample.  Neither statement says that a peptide was made, that a protein folded, that a molecule is physically admissible, that a mechanism has been identified, that a biological function exists, or that the same rule is universal outside the sampled contacts.  Those stronger conclusions would require their own contact records and their own tests.
