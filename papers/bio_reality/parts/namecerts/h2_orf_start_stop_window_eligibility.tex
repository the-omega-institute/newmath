\subsection{Start--stop window eligibility as a contact-gated ORF record}\label{subsec:orf-start-stop-window-eligibility}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
This record concerns the weakest open-reading-frame layer considered here: eligibility obtained by enumerating a nucleotide window against start and stop codon boundaries.  The experiment deliberately separates a syntactic boundary test from any claim about a realized biological product.  In the finite window run dated 2026-05-25, the manifest was loaded as one external annotated nucleotide window, and the classifier assigned that one input window to the start--stop enumeration class.  Thus the finite accounting is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{input windows}\} &= 1,\\
\#\{\text{classified windows}\} &= 1,\\
\#\{\text{loaded window manifests}\} &= 1.
\end{aligned}
$$
The scope string recorded by the run is \texttt{external\_window\_start\_stop\_enumeration\_only}, and the promotion guard is \texttt{orf\_eligibility\_only}.  The source manifest is a curated finite nucleotide-window input to the experiment; it is not a consequence of the BEDC codon geometry and is not counted as an internally generated biological fact.

\paragraph{What the finite row licenses.}
The one-window enumeration row licenses only the following narrow statement: given the supplied finite nucleotide-window manifest, the start--stop boundary procedure classified every supplied window, and the run explicitly kept the result at the ORF-eligibility layer.  The curated interval contact supplies a separate check that the same kind of boundary classifier can agree with a finite set of curated CDS intervals and matched decoys under a frozen protocol.  The protocol timestamps record the classifier, input classes, baselines, and held-out splits as fixed at 2026-05-29T19:11:13+00:00, before external evidence was observed at 2026-05-30T10:29:31+00:00.  This matters because the finite successes are then read as contact-conditioned evidence rather than as a post hoc fit to the annotation table.

\paragraph{Local non-promotion boundary.}
No biological layer above ORF eligibility follows from the start--stop enumeration alone.  In particular, the record does not assert translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, molecular function, mechanism, organism-level role, or universality.  The curated interval row also leaves those higher claims unmade: the RefSeq contact remains an annotated biological input, the matched-decoy and boundary controls do not classify windows outside the finite dataset, and the codon geometry is not promoted into a global biological law.  A later claim about translation, structure, physical possibility, function, mechanism, or general law would require its own separate contact capable of testing that stronger layer.
