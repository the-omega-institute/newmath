\subsection{Start--Stop Window Eligibility as a Contact-Gated ORF Record}
\label{sec:bioreality-orf-start-stop-window-eligibility}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite packet and biological question.}
This record concerns open-reading-frame eligibility obtained from start and stop codon enumeration.  The biological object is not protein production, folded molecular structure, physical viability, or cellular role.  It is the weaker finite question of whether a nucleotide interval, once supplied by an outside curated interval record, passes a fixed start--stop-window classifier.  The relevant finite run for this chapter loaded one curated window manifest, with $1$ input window and $1$ classified window, under snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$25$.  The run records the scope as external window start--stop enumeration only and explicitly stores the layer value as orf eligibility only.  Thus the chapter treats the start--stop enumeration as a bounded classifier over finite input rows, not as evidence that translation occurs.

\paragraph{Codon-level ambient coordinates.}
The packet is attached to the finite codon alphabet coordinates used by the surrounding BioReality computation.  The motif-compression run gives the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
It reports no missing codons and no extra codons for this motif union.  The larger marked set has cardinality $20$ and is recorded as
$$
\begin{aligned}
M = \{&AAA, AAG, ACA, ACG, AGA, AGG, AUA, AUG, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UCG, UGA, UGG, UUA, UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These lists give finite coordinates for the classifier input language.  They do not, by themselves, say that any listed codon is expressed in a transcript, translated by a ribosome, stabilized in a protein, or assigned a phenotype.

\paragraph{Window-classifier result.}
The start--stop-window run is deliberately small: $1$ finite window was loaded and the same $1$ window was classified by the fixed enumeration rule.  Its source is a curated finite nucleotide-window manifest for ORF start--stop enumeration, and the manifest rows are treated as annotation inputs to the experiment rather than as facts generated by the codon-coordinate machinery.  In symbols, the run stores
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{input windows} &= 1,\\
\text{classified windows} &= 1,\\
\text{window count} &= 1.
\end{aligned}
$$
The conclusion available from this row is exactly that the supplied finite window was handled by the start--stop enumeration classifier at the orf-eligibility layer.  The row does not establish that the interval is translated, that a product folds, that the product is chemically admissible, that it has a function, that a mechanism is present, or that a universal biological rule has been found.

\paragraph{Curated interval contact.}
A separate curated interval contact supplies a stronger finite test for the same layer boundary.  That run loaded $168$ rows from a curated ORF interval dataset derived from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession NC\_000913.3, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$.  The required input classes were present: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.  The protocol was fixed before the external observation time $2026$-$05$-$30T10{:}29{:}31+00{:}00$, while the frozen protocol time was $2026$-$05$-$29T19{:}11{:}13+00{:}00$.

\paragraph{Positive, decoy, and boundary counts.}
For the curated interval contact, $72$ positive curated ORF intervals were accepted and $72$ matched decoys were rejected.  The reported exact one-sided binomial upper-tail probability was $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$ for the positive acceptance statistic and the same value for the matched decoy rejection statistic.  The predeclared paired baseline comparison also recorded $72$ wins out of $72$ pairs, again with upper-tail probability $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  Boundary controls had $24$ cases, with $0$ promoted and promoted fraction $0.0$.  Thus the observed finite counts are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{positive accepted} &= 72/72,\\
\text{matched decoy rejected} &= 72/72,\\
\text{paired baseline wins} &= 72/72,\\
\text{boundary promoted} &= 0/24.
\end{aligned}
$$
The held-out split audit had $3$ splits.  Each split had $24$ positives and $24$ decoys, with positive accept rate $1.0$, decoy reject rate $1.0$, and pass status true for the left-locus, middle-locus, and right-locus folds.

\paragraph{Layer boundary.}
The scientific content of the chapter is the contact gate.  Before an interval annotation or comparable biological contact is supplied, start--stop enumeration is only a finite proposal about what could be tested.  After the curated contact is supplied, the admissible statement is still confined to ORF-eligibility classification on the finite contacted rows.  The computation does not infer translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or universality from codon geometry or from start--stop-window syntax.  The self-check run records $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  Therefore the local verdict is a finite ORF-eligibility result with a strict non-promotion condition: every stronger biological assertion requires its own separate contact and cannot be obtained from the window classifier alone.
