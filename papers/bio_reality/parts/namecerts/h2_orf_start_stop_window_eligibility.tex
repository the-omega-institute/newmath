\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The chapter claim is therefore a two-level statement.  First, the start/stop-window enumeration row confirms that the externally supplied one-window manifest is fully classified by the finite eligibility rule.  Second, the linked curated-interval experiment shows that the same style of frozen rule can separate $72$ curated positive intervals from $72$ matched decoys across three held-out splits, while refusing to promote $24$ boundary cases.  The self-check row records $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  Consequently the result may be read only as finite ORF-window eligibility under explicit external interval contact.  It does not assert translation, peptide existence, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical mechanism, biological function, or universal genomic law.  Any such stronger biological claim would require another contact whose measurements directly address that stronger layer.
