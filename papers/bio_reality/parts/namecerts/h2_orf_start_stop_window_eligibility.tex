\subsection{Start--stop window eligibility as a bounded ORF contact test}
\label{sec:bioreality-orf-start-stop-window-eligibility}

\paragraph{Object and scope.}
This record concerns a narrow biological predicate: whether a finite nucleotide interval is eligible under a start-codon and stop-codon enumeration test. The predicate is deliberately weaker than translation, weaker than protein production, weaker than folding, weaker than physical admissibility, and weaker than biological function. It is a window-level eligibility classifier whose inputs are externally supplied nucleotide intervals. The classifier may say that a supplied interval has the required boundary pattern, but it does not by itself say that a cell translates the interval, that a peptide exists, that a folded structure exists, or that any organism-level role follows.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is confined to ORF-window eligibility for the finite supplied contacts. The one-window enumeration contact establishes only that the supplied window was classified by the start--stop rule. The $168$-row curated interval contact gives stronger external support for that same layer, including positive acceptance, decoy rejection, boundary non-promotion, and three held-out stable folds. None of these data establish translation realization, peptide existence, folded structure, thermodynamic admissibility, mechanism, function, organismal relevance, or a universal biological law. Any such stronger claim would require a separate contact whose measured object is that stronger layer. The self-check records the same boundary: three gates are checked, and no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.
