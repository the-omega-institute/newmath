\paragraph{ORF-eligibility boundary.}
The conjectural biological object is open-reading-frame eligibility from start and stop codon enumeration.  The seed row is deliberately weaker than an ORF call.  The local rule is: start/stop-window eligibility may be discussed only as an eligibility predicate over an externally supplied interval; it must not be promoted to translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, molecular function, causal mechanism, or general biological law without an additional contact that is capable of testing that stronger layer.  The finite start/stop-window check $h2.orf.start\_stop.window\_eligibility$ loaded one external window dataset with $\mathrm{window\_count}=1$ and classified $1$ of $1$ input windows.  Its recorded scope is external window start/stop enumeration only, and its own output marks the promotion ceiling as $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility\_only}$.  Hence even the successful one-window classification is not a translation claim; it is only a bounded eligibility classification for the supplied window.

\paragraph{Curated interval contact.}
A stronger contact row is available for $h2.orf\_eligibility.curated\_interval\_contact$.  It loaded $168$ curated interval rows from a RefSeq-derived GenBank XML CDS interval manifest for \textit{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 accession $NC\_000913.3$, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$.  The protocol was frozen before the recorded observation time: classifier rules, input classes, held-out splits, and baselines were all marked frozen, while the analysis used no translation or structure input.  The required input classes were present: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.  The positive interval test accepted $72$ of $72$ positives, for accept rate $1.0$, with one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  The matched decoy test rejected $72$ of $72$ decoys, for reject rate $1.0$, with the same reported binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  The predeclared paired baseline comparison recorded $72$ wins out of $72$ pairs, again with upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.

\paragraph{Held-out stability and non-promotion.}
The curated contact also reports three held-out splits.  The left-locus, middle-locus, and right-locus folds each contain $24$ positives and $24$ decoys; in each fold the positive accept rate is $1.0$, the decoy reject rate is $1.0$, and the fold is marked passed.  Boundary cases remain capped: $24$ boundary cases were examined, $0$ were promoted, and the promoted fraction is $0.0$.  These controls support a narrow interval-contact claim: within this curated dataset, the frozen start/stop-window classifier separates the supplied positive intervals from matched decoys and does not promote boundary cases.  The contact still does not establish actual translation events, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, universality, or classification beyond the declared data rows.  The self-check row is consistent with this reading: it records $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The chapter therefore has two separable finite conclusions.  First, the motif compression claim is ready as a finite codon enumeration: the thirteen-member $R$ list is exactly the verified motif union, with no missing and no extra codons.  Second, ORF eligibility is admissible only where a supplied interval contact is present.  The one-window enumeration row and the $168$-row curated interval row provide bounded contact evidence for start/stop-window eligibility on their stated inputs, including $72/72$ positive acceptance, $72/72$ decoy rejection, three stable held-out folds, and $0/24$ boundary promotion.  Nothing in these rows turns codon geometry into translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality; those would require separate contacts aimed at those layers.
