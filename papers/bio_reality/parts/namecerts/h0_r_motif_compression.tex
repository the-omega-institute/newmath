\subsection{Motif Compression and the ORF-Eligibility Boundary}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-motif-compression}

\paragraph{Finite motif row.}
This chapter records a finite motif-compression row whose biological reading is deliberately weaker than an open-reading-frame claim.  The verified motif carrier is the thirteen-codon set
$R=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.  The finite audit for claim $\mathrm{h0.R.motif.compression}$ reports no missing codons and no extra codons: both discrepancy lists are empty.  Thus the row is a closed finite enumeration of the motif set it names, not a biological observation of an expressed interval.  The experimentally recorded run identifier is $338799579a87$, with verification timestamp $2026$-$05$-$31\mathrm{T}20{:}34{:}39+00{:}00$.

\paragraph{Ambient enumerated molecule row.}
The linked ambient row $\mathrm{h0.M.equals.WNR.CUN}$ places this motif inside a twenty-codon finite set.  Its audited enumeration is
$M=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}$,
with $|M|=20$.  The compression row therefore sits inside a specific finite codon universe rather than inside an unspecified sequence space.  The finite relationship can be summarized as
$$\begin{aligned}
|R|&=13,\\
|M|&=20,\\
M\setminus R&=\{\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
This arithmetic is the whole content of the compression claim: it gives a reproducible codon-level distinction, but it does not say that any member of $R$ is translated, folded, chemically admissible in a cell, functional, mechanistic, or universal.

A quotient coordinate, a saturation equality, or an empty finite discrepancy list is still a mathematical audit datum unless a separate curated sequence, transcript, or interval annotation contacts the biological object being tested.

\paragraph{ORF-eligibility reading.}
The proposed biological object is open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration.  In this chapter the correct reading is boundary-level: the motif row may specify what would be tested for start/stop-window eligibility, but it is not itself evidence that a real interval is an ORF.  The linked window-enumeration check loaded one external nucleotide-window dataset with $1$ input window, classified $1$ window by start/stop enumeration, and recorded the scope as external-window start/stop enumeration only.  Its own non-promotion field says $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility\_only}$.  That scalar result is useful because it fixes the layer boundary: start and stop enumeration can classify an input window for eligibility, but it is not a translation assay, a protein-structure assay, a physical-admissibility test, or a function experiment.

The stronger linked contact row shows what a real ORF-eligibility contact looks like when it is present.  It loaded $168$ curated interval rows from an NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML source for accession $\mathrm{NC\_000913.3}$, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$.  The protocol was frozen at $2026$-$05$-$29\mathrm{T}19{:}11{:}13+00{:}00$, before the external evidence timestamp $2026$-$05$-$30\mathrm{T}10{:}29{:}31+00{:}00$.  The required input classes were present: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.  The positive interval test accepted $72$ of $72$ positives, with one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  The matched decoy test rejected $72$ of $72$ decoys, with the same one-sided value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  The predeclared paired comparison won $72$ of $72$ pairs, again with one-sided value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  The boundary-case cap reports $24$ boundary cases and $0$ promotions.  Three held-out splits each had $24$ positives accepted at rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys rejected at rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
The preceding contact row is cited only to delimit the layer, not to upgrade the motif-compression row by implication.  The chapter therefore asserts the following local boundary.  The thirteen-codon motif equality, the twenty-codon ambient enumeration, the eighteen-codon saturation row, and the quotient geometry establish finite mathematical classifications.  They do not establish translation, folded molecular structure, cellular physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Such claims require their own contact with biological reality, such as a curated interval, transcript evidence, translation evidence, structural assay, physical model, or functional experiment appropriate to the layer being asserted.  The self-check row is consistent with this reading: it records $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  Hence the chapter is ready only as an ORF-eligibility boundary record attached to finite audited rows, not as a higher-layer biological conclusion.
