\paragraph{Finite motif packet.}
The record \texttt{h0.R.motif.compression} is a finite codon-level compression claim.  Its verified content is not an assertion about translated products; it is the exact equality between the named set $R$ and a motif union computed in the experiment run \texttt{338799579a87}.  The computed list contains thirteen codons, and the audit found neither extra nor missing entries:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},
\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite equality is therefore a closed enumeration fact about the row data: \texttt{no\_extra} is empty and \texttt{no\_missing} is empty.  The result is useful because it gives a compact handle on the codons participating in the motif union, but its biological interpretation remains intentionally narrow.

\paragraph{Ambient finite universe.}
The companion finite universe $M$ was verified in experiment run \texttt{a53ef0f36703}.  It has cardinality twenty and is exactly the union denoted by the WNR and CUN components in the row data:
$$
\begin{aligned}
M = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\\
&\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\\
&\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\},
\qquad |M|=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus $R$ is not being read as a free-standing biological class.  It is a thirteen-element sub-enumeration inside a twenty-element codon universe whose membership is fixed by the finite run.  A separate saturation computation, run \texttt{f4eee68dd2c3}, gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{Sat}_{f3}(R)
= \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\\
&\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\},
\qquad |\operatorname{Sat}_{f3}(R)|=18.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports an empty $f3$ boundary, and $M$ is recovered from this saturated list together with the two entries $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$.  This is a combinatorial recovery statement, not a biological promotion.

\paragraph{ORF eligibility boundary.}
The layer named \texttt{orf\_eligibility} is kept separate from the motif compression itself.  The seed statement says that start--stop codon enumeration can only define a boundary test until an observed interval is supplied by a curated sequence, transcript, or annotation contact.  The row \texttt{h2.orf.start\_stop.window\_eligibility}, run \texttt{c26a0d6656f7}, loaded one external window from a snapshot dated 2026-05-25 and classified one of one input windows by start--stop enumeration.  Its own scope field is \texttt{external\_window\_start\_stop\_enumeration\_only}, and its no-promotion field is \texttt{orf\_eligibility\_only}.  Consequently, the one-window classification is only an eligibility-level enumeration check.

\paragraph{Curated interval contact.}
A stronger contact appears in \texttt{h2.orf\_eligibility.curated\_interval\_contact}, run \texttt{7c9b30b02d46}.  It loaded 168 curated interval rows from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \textit{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 accession NC\_000913.3, with snapshot date 2026-05-30.  The protocol was frozen at 2026-05-29T19:11:13+00:00, before the external evidence time 2026-05-30T10:29:31+00:00; the classifier identifier was \texttt{orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility}, and classifier rules were not modified.  The required input classes were present: positive curated ORF intervals, matched non-ORF decoys, and boundary cases.

The curated contact gives exact finite performance figures.  There were seventy-two positive intervals accepted out of seventy-two, seventy-two matched decoys rejected out of seventy-two, and twenty-four boundary cases with zero promotion:

\begin{aligned}
\text{positive accept rate} &= 72/72 = 1.0,\\
\text{matched decoy reject rate} &= 72/72 = 1.0,\\
\text{boundary promotion rate} &= 0/24 = 0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The positive-interval exact one-sided binomial statistic has upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$, the matched-decoy rejection statistic has the same upper-tail value, and the predeclared paired baseline sign-test records seventy-two wins in seventy-two pairs with the same upper-tail value.  Three held-out splits were also stable: the left, middle, and right locus folds each had twenty-four positives accepted at rate $1.0$ and twenty-four decoys rejected at rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Local claim boundary.}
The finite facts above justify only this local reading: the motif set $R$ is exactly the computed thirteen-codon motif union; $M$ is the verified twenty-codon universe; the saturation and quotient rows give finite combinatorial structure; and the curated interval experiment supports ORF-eligibility classification for its declared external interval dataset.  Nothing in this packet establishes translation realization, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, genome-wide coverage, or universality.  Such claims would require additional biological contacts designed for those layers.  The self-check row records this separation explicitly: three gates were checked, and the boundary says that codon geometry is not promoted across biological layers.
