\subsection{Motif Compression and ORF-Eligibility Boundary for a Finite Codon Packet}

\paragraph{Finite packet.}
The row records a finite codon packet whose immediate verified claim is the equality between the motif-compressed region $R$ and the explicitly enumerated motif union. The checked list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit reports no missing codons and no extra codons, so this subsection treats $R$ as a thirteen-element enumerated object rather than as an inferred biological class. The companion finite universe $M$ has cardinality $20$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The identity $M=\mathrm{WNR}\cup\mathrm{CUN}$ is therefore being used here only as a finite enumeration fact: its recorded value is the same twenty-codon list, with size $20$.

\paragraph{Saturation and boundary residues.}
The recorded $f_3$ saturation check says that $\mathrm{Sat}_{f_3}(R)$ is $M$ with exactly $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$ absent:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\\
&\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row records $M=\mathrm{Med}(R)\cup\{\mathrm{ACA}\}$ as the full twenty-codon list above, while the $f_3$ boundary list is empty. These statements are combinatorial statements about a closed finite table. They do not by themselves assert that a nucleotide interval exists in a genome, that a transcript is present, or that translation occurs.

This quotient information is useful for locating the compressed motif packet inside a finite coordinate system, but it remains a coordinate-level fact. No physical or biological conclusion is licensed merely because the quotient has ten rows or because the original finite object has the stated $f$-vector.

\paragraph{ORF-eligibility contact.}
The row also carries an ORF-eligibility boundary packet. The seed statement is deliberately weak: start/stop codon enumeration may define a window-eligibility test, but it does not make an observed open reading frame without a separate curated interval, sequence, or transcript contact. One finite start/stop-window manifest was loaded for the window-enumeration check; it had $1$ input window, $1$ classified window, snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$25$, and scope restricted to external window start/stop enumeration only. Its recorded classification boundary is $\mathrm{orf\_eligibility\_only}$.

A stronger curated interval contact is present separately. It loads $168$ rows from a curated ORF interval dataset derived from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 accession $\mathrm{NC\_000913.3}$, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$. The input classes are boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs. The protocol was frozen before the external observation: classifier rules, input classes, held-out splits, and baselines were fixed, with protocol time $2026$-$05$-$29\,19{:}11{:}13+00{:}00$ and outside evidence observed at $2026$-$05$-$30\,10{:}29{:}31+00{:}00$.

\paragraph{Local limitation.}
The finite packet is therefore split into two kinds of information. First, the motif-compression and quotient rows verify a finite codon geometry: the thirteen-codon $R$ list, the twenty-codon $M$ list, the saturation relation excluding $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$ at the stated step, and the ten-row quotient. Second, a separately attached curated interval contact tests ORF-eligibility on a finite biological annotation table. Even with the curated contact, the conclusion remains local to ORF interval eligibility in the supplied dataset. It does not establish translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, genome-wide law, or universality. Any one of those stronger readings would require its own independently specified contact and its own finite audit.
