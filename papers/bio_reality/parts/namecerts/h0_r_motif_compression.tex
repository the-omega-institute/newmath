\paragraph{ORF-eligibility boundary.}
The biological layer attached to this row is \texttt{orf\_eligibility}.  The seed statement is deliberately weaker than an ORF call: start and stop codon enumeration can describe what an interval would have to satisfy, but it does not itself supply an observed interval.  The one-window start--stop enumeration row loaded an external-window manifest with $1$ input window and classified $1$ window under the scope \texttt{external\_window\_start\_stop\_enumeration\_only}, with snapshot date 2026-05-25.  Its own result string states that the scope remains \texttt{orf\_eligibility\_only}.  Thus the row is admissible as a finite eligibility classifier only after an interval source is present; it is not evidence of translation, structure, physical feasibility, biological activity, or mechanism.
