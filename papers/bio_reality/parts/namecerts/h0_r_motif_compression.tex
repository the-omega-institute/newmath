\subsection{Motif Compression and ORF Eligibility Boundary}\label{sec:bioreality-r-motif-compression}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite motif row.}
The finite row named \texttt{h0.R.motif.compression} records a closed codon-level motif set $R$ and verifies that the row contains exactly the motif union intended by the experiment.  In this row the observed set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verification ledger reports no missing codons and no additional codons for this equality.  Thus the local claim is not an asymptotic biological statement; it is the assertion that a particular thirteen-element codon set has been recovered without discrepancy from the finite motif enumeration.  The row is therefore useful as a compressed syntactic handle on a codon pattern, but it is not yet a biological observation by itself.

\paragraph{Ambient codon universe.}
The same packet is linked to the finite row \texttt{h0.M.equals.WNR.CUN}, where the ambient set $M$ is verified to have cardinality $20$.  The verified list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\\
&\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\\
&\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supplies a second finite scale for the motif claim: $R$ occupies $13$ of the $20$ codons in $M$.  A linked saturation row further reports that $\mathrm{Sat}_{f3}(R)$ consists of the $18$ codons obtained from $M$ after excluding only $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$, while the listed $f3$ boundary is empty.  These numbers describe closure behavior inside the chosen codon geometry; they do not by themselves identify translation, expression, protein formation, or cellular role.

\paragraph{Quotient geometry.}
The quotient row \texttt{h0.quotient.geometry} gives an independent finite geometric check on the same ambient scale.  The verified $f$-vector for $M$ is $(20,38,26,8,1)$, while the quotient has size $10$ and $f$-vector $(10,14,6,1)$.  The row also lists a ten-point quotient image, so the compression from the codon universe to the quotient is explicitly finite rather than inferred from a continuum model.  In this chapter the quotient data are read only as codon-geometry bookkeeping: they can organize the motif row and reveal finite identifications, but they do not convert a motif into an open reading frame in the biological sense.

\paragraph{ORF-contact boundary.}
The biological layer attached to this packet is \texttt{orf\_eligibility}.  The seed statement says that start/stop codon enumeration can describe a possible eligibility boundary, but that the boundary remains unasserted until an independent sequence, transcript, or curated interval contact supplies the biological interval being tested.  A linked start/stop-window row loaded one external window and classified that one input window by start/stop enumeration, with scope restricted to external-window start/stop enumeration only.  This supports a very narrow reading: enumeration can check a supplied window against start and stop rules; it does not create the window as an observed biological object.

\paragraph{Curated interval contact.}
A later contact row gives the finite reality-facing test that the seed itself lacked.  The curated interval dataset contains $168$ rows from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 accession $\mathrm{NC\_000913.3}$.  The required input classes are present: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs.  The positive interval test accepts $72$ of $72$ positives, with one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.  The matched decoy test rejects $72$ of $72$ decoys with the same reported upper-tail value, and the predeclared paired baseline comparison records $72$ wins in $72$ pairs.  Three held-out splits are reported; each split contains $24$ positives and $24$ decoys, with positive accept rate $1.0$ and decoy reject rate $1.0$.  The boundary-control block reports $24$ boundary cases and $0$ promotions.  These numbers justify an interval-contact result for this curated dataset, not a general law about all genomes or all possible codon strings.

\paragraph{Local negative boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is deliberately narrow.  The motif row verifies the thirteen-codon set $R$ and its exact finite membership.  The ambient and quotient rows verify a twenty-codon universe, a ten-point quotient, and the stated finite $f$-vectors.  The curated interval contact verifies strong separation on the $168$ supplied rows under frozen classifier rules and predeclared split structure.  None of these facts entails translation realization, folded molecular structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Those stronger layers would require additional contacts that directly measure or curate the relevant biological level.  The self-check row states the same gate in operational form: three gates are checked, and no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.
