\paragraph{ORF eligibility remains contact-dependent.}
The biological reading attached to this row is deliberately narrow: it concerns eligibility of open-reading-frame intervals under start/stop enumeration.  The seed packet is therefore not an observed ORF call.  A start/stop pattern can mark a candidate interval only after a separate biological contact supplies the relevant sequence, transcript, or curated interval annotation.  Without that contact, the codon geometry remains a finite pretest for what one would inspect, not a biological claim about expression, translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal genomic law.

The one-window enumeration check \texttt{h2.orf.start\_stop.window\_eligibility}, verified in run \texttt{c26a0d6656f7}, reports $1$ input window and $1$ classified window, with scope restricted to external-window start/stop enumeration and with the explicit value \texttt{orf\_eligibility\_only}.  Its snapshot date is 2026-05-25.  This says that the supplied window was classified under the declared start/stop rule, not that translation occurred or that the resulting interval has structure or function.
