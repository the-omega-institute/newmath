\paragraph{Claim scope.}
The record \texttt{h0.R.motif.compression} is a finite codon-level packet whose biological reading is deliberately narrow.  Its biological object is open-reading-frame eligibility as seen through start and stop codon enumeration, but the supplied row does not by itself assert that an open reading frame has been observed in a sequence.  The packet instead fixes a small combinatorial boundary: it says which codons are present in the motif-compressed set $R$, how that set sits inside the larger enumerated set $M$, and what a separate contact would have to supply before the biological layer \texttt{orf\_eligibility} could be asserted.

\paragraph{Boundary probes.}
The packet contains two boundary probes.  The first, \texttt{orf.eligibility.boundary.requires.contact}, breaks if the pipeline asserts ORF eligibility, translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, or function from start--stop-window enumeration alone.  The second, \texttt{orf.eligibility.contact.gap.check}, breaks if an ORF eligibility claim is recorded while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated interval annotation can test the proposed interval.  Both probes require a curated ORF interval annotation as the missing contact.  Their common scientific content is that a start--stop-window pattern may define a testable boundary, but an observed biological interval has to come from a separate curated or empirical source.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite-row verdict is therefore a bounded readiness statement for the motif-compressed codon packet.  The codon set $R$ is exactly the listed $13$-element motif union, its comparison against $M$ has no missing or extra elements in the primary row, the saturation and quotient summaries give finite checks around the same coordinate system, and the linked window manifest contains one classified start--stop window.  What remains absent is decisive for interpretation: there are no attached reality-contact records in the supplied packet.  Consequently, the chapter may state a finite start--stop eligibility boundary and may identify the contact gap that must be closed, but it may not claim translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, cross-context universality, or an observed open reading frame unless an additional contact supplies that biological interval.
