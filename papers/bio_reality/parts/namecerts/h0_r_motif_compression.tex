\paragraph{Finite motif record.}
The record named $h0.R.motif.compression$ isolates a finite codon-level compression pattern and keeps it separate from any biological assertion about open reading frames. The verified row for this claim reports that the compressed motif set $R$ is exactly
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same run reports no missing members and no extra members: $no\_missing=\varnothing$ and $no\_extra=\varnothing$. Thus the finite statement being certified here is not that these codons realize an open reading frame, but only that this thirteen-element motif list is the whole motif union used by the packet. The companion finite universe $M$ has cardinality $20$ and is recorded as
$$\begin{aligned}
M=\{&AAA, AAG, ACA, ACG, AGA, AGG, AUA, AUG, CUA, CUC,\\
&CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UCG, UGA, UGG, UUA, UUG\}.
\end{aligned}$$
This gives the local finite scale of the packet: the motif compression concerns $13$ named codons inside a $20$-codon enumerated universe, with the equality $M=WNR\cup CUN$ checked at the same timestamp as the motif row.

\paragraph{Saturation and quotient geometry.}
The adjacent saturation row records that applying the $f3$ operation to $R$ reaches $M$ except for the two boundary codons $ACA$ and $ACG$:
$$\begin{aligned}

&UAA, UAG, UCA, UCG, UGA, UGG, UUA, UUG\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The same finite computation gives $M=Med(R)\cup\{ACA\}$ and reports an empty $f3$ boundary obstruction. The quotient-geometry row gives the ambient $M$ vector as $(20,38,26,8,1)$ and the quotient vector as $(10,14,6,1)$. These rows certify a finite compression and quotient profile. They do not, by themselves, certify a nucleotide interval in a genome, an expressed transcript, a translated product, a folded molecule, a physical feasibility condition, a cellular role, a mechanism, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{ORF-eligibility boundary.}
The biological object associated with the packet is open-reading-frame eligibility from start and stop codon enumeration. The local rule is deliberately conservative: start/stop enumeration may define a possible boundary pattern, but it is not an observed ORF unless an independent biological interval contact supplies the sequence context being tested. The start/stop-window row contains one external input window and reports that all input windows were classified by start/stop enumeration, so the local count is $1$ classified window out of $1$ loaded window. Its scope is explicitly restricted to external-window start/stop enumeration, and the finite result is classified only at the $orf\_eligibility$ layer. This layer statement is narrower than translation and narrower than biological function.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The packet therefore has two separable parts. First, the codon-side finite computation certifies the exact motif set $R$, the $20$-element universe $M$, the $f3$ saturation profile, and the quotient geometry. Second, the curated interval contact supplies a bounded biological test for ORF-eligibility on $168$ curated rows, with perfect positive acceptance, perfect matched-decoy rejection, three stable held-out folds, and non-promotion of $24$ boundary cases. The admissible conclusion is exactly an ORF-eligibility contact result for the stated curated interval dataset. Nothing in this chapter licenses a stronger biological conclusion unless another independent contact is supplied for that stronger layer.
