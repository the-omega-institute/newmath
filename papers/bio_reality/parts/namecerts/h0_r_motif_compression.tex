Therefore the present chapter treats the data as a boundary probe for what an ORF-eligibility contact would have to test, not as evidence that such a contact has already been supplied.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
No translation realization follows from the thirteen-codon motif equality, from the twenty-codon ambient set, from the eighteen-codon saturation row, from the quotient size $10$, or from the single classified start/stop window.  No folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, regulatory role, selective explanation, or universality follows from these finite rows.  In particular, a start/stop-window pattern is not an observed open reading frame unless a separate sequence, transcript, or curated interval annotation is attached as a reality contact.  The packet itself lists no such contact: the contact list is empty, and the required contact named by the probes is $\mathrm{curated.orf.interval.annotation}$.  The correct reading is therefore narrow: $h0.R.motif.compression$ is a verified finite motif-compression record whose ORF relevance remains at boundary status until an external biological interval contact is provided.
