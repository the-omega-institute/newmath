\paragraph{Local claim boundary.}
The finite codon equality for $R$, the $20$-element ambient set $M$, the $18$-element saturation display, the quotient vectors, the single start/stop-window classification, and the $168$-row curated interval contact together justify only a narrow conclusion. The packet may be used as an ORF-eligibility boundary record once the curated interval contact is attached; before that contact, it remains a finite codon and start/stop-window construction awaiting biological anchoring. Even after the curated interval contact, the conclusion is still limited to interval-level eligibility under the frozen classifier and the stated dataset. It does not establish translation realization, folded molecular structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic explanation, organism-wide law, or cross-dataset universality. The self-check row reinforces this boundary by reporting $3$ checked gates and stating that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. The scientific role of this subsection is therefore to separate a reproducible finite codon pattern from the distinct biological acts of observation, translation, folding, mechanism, and function.
