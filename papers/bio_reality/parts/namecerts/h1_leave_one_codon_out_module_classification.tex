\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table.  The internal distinctions used here are a six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  These distinctions are not biological mechanisms.  They are finite coordinate predicates on the codon display, with an external readback supplied by the standard-code label table.  The observed exceptional row set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check returns the same thirteen codons and the finite face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the chapter studies a finite local tile in $Q_6$, not a global law of translation or physiology.

$$

\begin{aligned}

$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, the observed module size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$ out of $2000$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}

p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the finite display carries a non-random local boundary signature relative to the stated nulls.  They do not support a claim that the display is forced by biology itself.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The reality contact is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is only that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  Therefore the result may claim code-layer readback, local codon-tile structure, and finite module exhaustion in $Q_6$.  It may not claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any promotion beyond the code-read layer requires a separate contact that directly observes the higher layer.
