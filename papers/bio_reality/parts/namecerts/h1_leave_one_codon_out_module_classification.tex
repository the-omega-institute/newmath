\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
The finite experiment asks whether the window-six codon coordinate system separates the standard-code exception region into reproducible local modules when each codon is treated as a held-out label.  The object is the standard-code codon table read only at the code-label level: codons are observed as six-bit coordinates in the codon cube $Q_6$, and the external contact supplies amino-acid or stop labels.  The claim is not that the coordinate geometry explains translation.  The narrower claim is that a specific finite subset of codons has a stable local-tile organization whose module assignment is complete under the recorded probe.

\paragraph{Exception region.}
The distinguished region $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The codons recorded in $R$ are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube-shaped region with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite record is not merely a count: it gives the full codon membership and the local cubical incidence summary used by the classification step.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The associated spectrum separates the module scale from a random label view.  The recorded values are

\paragraph{Shape controls.}
Against the generic finite comparison, the observed module shape is rare.  In the $N=2000$ run with seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a stricter local check: with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  The resulting values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a code-table-level organization of the finite labels.  They do not by themselves assert that the organization is realized as a physical pathway, a fold, or a functional constraint.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classifier assigns every codon in $R$ to one of six named modules, and each module has at least one codon marked predictable or boundary-supported.  The finite assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completion audit gives $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and assigned count $13$, with no extra module codons, no codons missing from modules, and no unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{extra\ module\ codons} &= \varnothing,\\
\mathrm{missing\ from\ modules} &= \varnothing,\\
\mathrm{unassigned\ codons} &= \varnothing.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central finite-row result: the local boundary-tile view does not merely detect a few isolated codons; it partitions the entire recorded exception region into complete modules.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  That contact resolves codon-label readback: it says which standard-code codons carry amino-acid or stop labels.  It does not observe translation dynamics, folded protein geometry, thermodynamic admissibility, cellular function, or cross-organism universality.  Therefore the result is a finite code-read statement: window-six coordinates and the curated label table jointly expose a complete local module classification for the $13$ codons in $R$.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
No claim of translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows from this chapter.  A codon label sequence is not a protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological principle.  Any stronger assertion would require a separate contact that directly measures the higher layer in question.  Within the stated scope, the verdict is that the finite standard-code data support a complete leave-one-codon-out module classification at the code-read layer.
