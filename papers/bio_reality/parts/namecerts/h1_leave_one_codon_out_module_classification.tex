\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification in the window-six codon cube}\label{sec:biorality-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read only as a finite coordinate cube equipped with standard-code labels supplied by a curated genetic-code table.  The biological contact is therefore a code-layer contact: it says which amino-acid or stop label is attached to each codon in the standard table.  It does not say that the coordinate cube is a mechanism for translation, a physical admissibility criterion, a folding model, a function model, or a universal biological law.  The claim tested here is narrower: after one codon is withheld from the local window-six construction, the codons in the residual boundary row $R$ fall into named modules, and every such module has at least one predictable or boundary-supported member.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The finite row recovered for the boundary side has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube object with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not merely a list of labels: it carries a finite local tile shape with vertices, edges, and square faces.  The row size agrees with the independent cardinality check, $|R|=13$.

\paragraph{Spectral check.}
The row also comes with a small spectral readout.  The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check.  This places the finite tile inside a stable numerical regime for this run, but the interpretation remains combinatorial and code-layer only.  A spectral radius in tolerance is not evidence for biochemical necessity; it is a consistency check on the finite coordinate construction.

\paragraph{Shape significance against null rows.}
Against the broad null sample with $N=2000$ rows, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$.  The reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling log records $57377$ distinct sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries with seed $42$.  Against the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, while $7$ rows fell in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-row size $20$.  The corresponding values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the finite-row distinctiveness of the local tile relative to the supplied null models.  They do not by themselves identify a molecular cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  Grouping those tables by family gave $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other tables, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast tables.  In the leave-one-codon-out run there were $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and $8$ high-coverage closure cases, with recovery rate $1.0$ and thresholded recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the $13$ codons of $R$.  The assigned count is $13$, the reported row size is $13$, and the audit records no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{size}} &= 13,\\
\mathrm{assigned} &= 13,\\
\mathrm{missing} &= \varnothing,\\
\mathrm{extra} &= \varnothing,\\
\mathrm{unassigned} &= \varnothing.
\end{aligned}
$$
The six named modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row therefore decomposes into arginine-side, lysine-side, leucine-box, isoleucine-methionine, serine/leucine-side, and stop/tryptophan-side components.  The finite statement certified by this run is that every one of these modules contains at least one codon marked predictable or boundary-supported by the leave-one-codon-out procedure.

\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.}
The ready claim is a local code-read claim.  Window-six geometry exposes a boundary-tile organization in the curated standard-code labels, and the leave-one-codon-out module classification covers all $13$ row codons without remainder.  The contact does not observe translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label readback is not a protein-structure readback.  Crossing from this finite row into translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate contact that directly supplies that higher-layer evidence.
