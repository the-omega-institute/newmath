\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and readout.}
The object tested here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon represented as a coordinate point and then read against the curated table assigning amino-acid or stop labels.  The claim is deliberately local: the coordinate layer can expose a boundary-tile pattern in the codon-label table, while the labels themselves enter through a curated code contact.  Thus the test is a code-read test, not a statement about translation dynamics, folded structure, chemical admissibility, function, mechanism, or biological universality.

The distinguished residual row set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Its measured cardinality is $|R|=13$.  The same row is reported as a punctured cubical object with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite shape on which the leave-one-codon-out module classification is evaluated.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The coordinate split is not treated as a free visual pattern.  The spectral run reports $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The cubic consistency residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the radial-polynomial check, so the numerical identity is within the recorded tolerance.  The observed $M$ size used in the class-preserving null comparison is $20$.

Two null comparisons give the finite background rate.  In the broad shape comparison with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The run records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-match count was $0$, so $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower}}=0.0035$.  These controls do not turn the coordinate object into a biological law; they only say that the finite shape is unusual under the reported finite randomizations.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
The table-level leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the recorded hit rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
&\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}:0.8333333333333334,\\
&\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}:1.0,\\
&\mathrm{candidate\ other}:1.0,\\
&\mathrm{mitochondrial}:1.0,\\
&\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The five-family summary has family count $5$.  These rows show that the local coordinate cut has predictive contact with code-label changes across the reported finite panel, while still remaining a codon-label readout.

\paragraph{Codon-level recovery.}
The leave-one-codon-out run reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, and $8$ cases with high closure coverage.  The recovery rate is $1.0$, and the recorded recovery rate above threshold is also $1.0$.  The interpretation is that, within the tested finite row, each held-out codon is recovered either by the predictable part of the coordinate closure or by the explicitly marked boundary behavior.  The result is therefore a complete local accounting of the residual codons inside the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The module classification run assigns all $13$ codons in $R$.  Its completeness row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{size}}&=13,\\
\mathrm{assigned\ count}&=13,\\
\mathrm{extra\ module\ codons}&=\varnothing,\\
\mathrm{missing\ from\ modules}&=\varnothing,\\
\mathrm{unassigned\ codons}&=\varnothing.
\end{aligned}
$$
The six reported modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row named \texttt{every\_module\_has\_predictable\_or\_boundary} records exactly these six modules.  Hence every codon in the residual row is covered by a named finite module, and every named module lies inside the predictable-or-boundary accounting.  There are no surplus codons, no omissions from the module list, and no codons left outside the classification.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite evidence supports the following narrow statement.  In the six-bit codon coordinate display of the curated standard-code table, the residual row $R$ has cardinality $13$, realizes a punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, and admits a complete six-module classification under the leave-one-codon-out probe.  The table-level and codon-level tests both report high recovery against their finite baselines: $0.9841269841269841$ for the table-level boundary-augmented hit rate and $1.0$ for codon-level recovery.  The spectral and null-model rows give additional finite controls for the same coordinate object.

The result must not be promoted beyond its contact.  A curated genetic-code table observes codon-to-label assignment, not ribosomal realization, protein folding, physical viability, organismal function, or a universal mechanism.  Amino-acid label readback is therefore not evidence for folded protein structure.  Any claim about translation, structure, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require an independent contact that directly measures that higher layer.
