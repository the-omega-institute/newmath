\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{sec:bior_reality_h1_leave_one_codon_out_module_classification}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite claim is a code-read claim about the standard-code codon table, not a claim about translation dynamics or protein realization.  In the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, the leave-one-codon-out row set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.  The associated finite row calculation reads this set as a punctured local tile: its measured face data have $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Thus the object under test is the local label split induced by the table, with carrier $Q_6$, six-bit display coordinates, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.

\paragraph{Finite module classification.}
The module calculation partitions all $13$ codons of $R$ into six named modules, with no missing codons, no extra codons, and no unassigned codons.  The row record is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R &= \{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
|R| &= 13,\\
\mathrm{assigned}(R) &= 13,\\
\mathrm{missing} &= \varnothing,\qquad \mathrm{extra}=\varnothing,\qquad \mathrm{unassigned}=\varnothing.
\end{aligned}
$$
The six modules are the arginine pair $\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, the arginine--lysine singleton $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, the leucine four-codon block $\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, the isoleucine--methionine singleton $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, the serine/leucine pair $\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and the stop/tryptophan module $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The verified row property is that every one of these modules contains a codon classified as predictable or boundary-supported by the leave-one-codon-out probe.  This is the precise content of the module classification: it is a finite coverage statement over the observed $R$-tile.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape witnesses.}
The local tile is not introduced only by visual inspection.  The spectral calculation reports $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, with cubic radial residual $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$.  The residual is numerically at zero at the stated precision, so the spectral row is internally consistent with the recorded polynomial constraint.  The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, found $1$ exact shape match, and found $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  Hence the reported finite-row tail values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The corresponding reported values are $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These figures support the finite-row distinctiveness of the observed tile relative to the tested null models; they do not by themselves explain a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out row gives $23$ evaluated code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values are $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid tables, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate tables, $1.0$ for candidate-other tables, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast tables, across $5$ families.  The leave-one-codon-out row separately reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification refines this by showing that the $13$ codons of $R$ are all accounted for inside the six modules above, and that each module intersects the predictable-or-boundary side of the probe.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact asserted here is the curated standard-code label table.  That contact can test code-layer readback and local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the conclusion is deliberately local: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite boundary-tile pattern whose module coverage is complete for the observed $R$ row, and whose label readback is supplied by the standard-code table.  No claim follows that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  No claim follows that an amino-acid label sequence is a folded protein structure.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies it.
