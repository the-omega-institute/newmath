\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read only at the code layer through a curated standard-code table.  The reality contact used here is therefore a codon-to-label table: it assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label, but it does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The finite row isolates a distinguished set $R$ of thirteen codons,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube check gives the same thirteen codons with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the object under discussion is not an unrestricted codon table; it is a finite local boundary-tile object inside $Q_6$ whose readback is mediated by external code labels.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classifier partitions the thirteen codons of $R$ into six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA, UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA, UAG, UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Consequently the classifier is exact on the finite row: every codon in $R$ is accounted for, and every module codon lies in $R$.  The row-level assertion is stronger than a visual clustering claim but weaker than a biological mechanism claim.  It says precisely that every displayed module has predictable-or-boundary support in the leave-one-codon-out finite row.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported claim is a code-read claim.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure for the thirteen-codon set $R$, with complete module assignment and strong leave-one-codon recovery under the supplied finite rows.  The curated standard-code table supplies the label readback, and the geometry supplies the local coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  Nothing in this row observes ribosomal translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, structural order, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure.  Any promotion beyond code-read would require a separate higher-layer contact, and that contact is not present in this finite row.
