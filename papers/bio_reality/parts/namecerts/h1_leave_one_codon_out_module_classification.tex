\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon represented by a six-bit display induced by the three nucleotide positions.  The distinctions used here are a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The row is not a claim about translation dynamics or protein realization; it is a finite classification test for whether the observed standard-code labels expose a reproducible local boundary pattern.

\paragraph{Observed exceptional set.}
The finite exceptional set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same list and records the face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a count: the row fixes both the thirteen displayed codons and the induced two-dimensional cell inventory of the punctured cube.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Against $2000$ sampled null tables with seed $42$, the exact shape appeared once, giving $p=0.0005$, while the high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving $p=0.0125$.  The sampling log records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count is $0$ and the lower-tail count at the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
 p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035,\\
 |M|_{\mathrm{observed}} &= 20.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite row therefore supports a code-layer shape anomaly relative to the sampled nulls.  It does not supply a structural, thermodynamic, organismal, or functional contact.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The observed rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the row records $5$ families: bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline, while alternative-yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$.  These values say that the coordinate tile recovers many code-table label changes under this finite protocol; they do not say that the coordinate geometry causes the code.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out module classification.}
The target row classifies all thirteen codons of $R$ into six modules, with no missing or extra assignments.  The complete module table is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN} &= \{UCA, UUA\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp} &= \{UAA, UAG, UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completion audit records $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, empty extra-module codons, empty missing-from-modules list, and empty unassigned-codons list.  The companion leave-one-codon-out check records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Hence every module has either predictable support or boundary support in the finite row.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only biological contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact tests codon-label readback and the local label split.  It does not observe ribosomal realization, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal mechanism.  Those higher layers require separate observations with their own finite tests.  In particular, reading an amino-acid label sequence from codons is not an observation of folded protein structure.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The ready claim is therefore narrow: within the six-bit codon display, the standard-code label table yields a thirteen-codon punctured local tile whose module classification is complete, whose leave-one-codon-out recovery is total in the recorded protocol, and whose sampled null comparisons are small at the stated finite sample sizes.  The row supports a code-read boundary phenomenon.  It must remain at that layer unless an independent contact supplies translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.
