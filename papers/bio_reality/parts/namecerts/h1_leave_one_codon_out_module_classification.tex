The row is not a claim that the biological code is forced by cube geometry. It is a finite code-read observation: after the window-six coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation filters are fixed, the exceptional relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and consists exactly of

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the local tile is neither an arbitrary codon list nor a global genetic law: it is a thirteen-codon boundary pattern inside $Q_6$ whose readback is made only through the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge, whose observed fact is that a standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. Therefore the claim is a $\mathrm{code\_read}$ claim about local codon label split. It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey BEDC geometry as biological necessity. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. Any promotion beyond code-layer readback requires a separate higher-layer contact, with its own observable row and its own mismatch test.
