\subsection{Finite codon row and code-layer scope}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification-finite-row}

\paragraph{Carrier and readback.} The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite coordinate display for codon labels. A codon is therefore treated as a vertex of the cube together with an externally curated standard-code label. The finite-row question is not whether the cube explains translation, folding, physical chemistry, function, mechanism, or universality. It is only whether the observed standard-code label table exposes a stable local boundary-tile pattern when one codon is withheld and the remaining coordinate closure is queried.

\paragraph{Distinguished residual set.} The residual codon row $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check returns the same codon set and the finite face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional labels: it is a finite subcomplex with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces inside the six-coordinate display.

\paragraph{Shape significance.} Against the sampled null used for the finite-row audit, the observed shape is rare. In $N=2000$ distinct null samples, obtained after $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries with seed $42$, the exact shape appeared once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. The high-overlap threshold also remained sparse: $25$ samples had Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. These numbers support a code-read statement about finite coordinate shape. They do not, by themselves, supply a biological mechanism.

The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. \subsection{Boundary of the claim}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification-boundary}

\paragraph{What is supported.} The supported claim is a finite code-read claim: in the six-bit codon coordinate display, the curated standard-code label table exposes a residual thirteen-codon tile whose module classification is complete, whose leave-one-codon-out recovery is perfect in the recorded audit, and whose shape is rare under the sampled nulls. The reality contact is the curated standard-code table, so the empirical readback is a codon-to-label readback.

\paragraph{What is not supported.} No translation realization follows from this chapter. No folded protein structure follows. No structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function. Any promotion beyond code-read would require a separate contact whose observed fact directly measures the higher layer being claimed.

\paragraph{Verdict.} The finite row is ready as a BioReality record at the code-read layer. Its strongest point is the conjunction of exact residual coverage, complete module assignment, rare null-model shape, spectral compatibility, and perfect recorded leave-one-codon-out recovery. Its controlling limitation is equally explicit: all evidence is mediated by the curated standard-code label bridge, and that bridge blocks promotion to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.
