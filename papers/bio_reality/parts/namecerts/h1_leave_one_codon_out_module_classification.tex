\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon table as an external code-layer contact.  The contact supplies a curated assignment from codons to amino-acid or stop labels; it does not supply a translation mechanism, a folded structure, a physical admissibility test, a biological function, or a universality theorem.  The row under study is the finite exceptional region $R$ exposed by the window-six coordinate and label split.  Its cardinality is exactly $13$, with codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recovered as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the finite datum is not merely a list of labels: it also carries a local face structure with $13$ vertices, $16$ edges, and $4$ two-dimensional faces inside the codon-coordinate display.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model calibration.}
The row has a measured spectral profile
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with cubic, equivalently radial, residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  The observed $M$-shape has size $20$.  Against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives at seed $42$, the exact-shape count is $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while the high-Jaccard threshold count is $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, and the reported tail probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row anomaly at the code-read layer, not a biological mechanism.  The role of the null model is only to ask whether the observed coordinate-and-label geometry is typical under the stated randomization constraints.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
In the leave-one-code-out probe, $23$ code tables were evaluated across $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, there were $5$ families: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$ above baseline, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.

For the leave-one-codon-out probe, the finite counts were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\ count}&=0,\\

$$
The absence of anchors and the presence of $5$ boundary cases are essential to the interpretation: the recovery is a local closure-and-boundary readback of codon labels, not a claim that the six-bit window generates protein realization.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ and leaves no residue outside the declared modules.  The completeness row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|&=13,\\
\mathrm{assigned\ count}&=13,\\
\mathrm{extra\ module\ codons}&=0,\\
\mathrm{missing\ from\ modules}&=0,\\
\mathrm{unassigned\ codons}&=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has at least one predictable or boundary-supported member.  This is the finite-row content of the claim: the exceptional codon row is exhausted by a small set of label modules, and the leave-one-codon-out recovery is mediated by predictable or boundary positions inside that exhaustion.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The result is a code-read statement about a curated standard-code label table and a six-bit local tile.  It does not say that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not say that the local tile is a global law.  It does not infer translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary cause.  Any such promotion would require a separate reality contact with measurements at the relevant layer.  In the present finite row, amino-acid label-sequence readback remains only label readback.
