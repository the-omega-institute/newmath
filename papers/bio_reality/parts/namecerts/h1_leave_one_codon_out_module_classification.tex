\paragraph{Claim scope.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the level of codon-label readback.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube, where each codon is displayed as a finite window of six binary choices and the observed labels are supplied by the curated standard-code table.  The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The claim is that the leave-one-codon-out residual set admits a complete module partition in which every module is accounted for by either a predictable site or a boundary site.  This is a local code-layer classification.  It does not assert translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal law.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The empirical contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It can test code-layer readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function.  The observed fact is simply that the standard table supplies codon-to-label assignments.  Consequently, an amino-acid label sequence produced from this contact is still a label sequence; it is not a folded protein structure and it is not a function-bearing molecule in the sense required by biochemical or structural evidence.
