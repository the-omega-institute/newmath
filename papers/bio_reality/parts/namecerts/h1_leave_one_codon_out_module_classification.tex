\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification in the Window-Six Codon Cube}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table read through a six-bit codon display $Q_6$.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated codon table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label, and this chapter tests only whether the finite coordinate geometry exposes a stable local label-split pattern.  The distinguished row is
$R=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$,
with $|R|=13$.  The punctured-cube check gives the same thirteen codons and the face-vector summary
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the row is not being treated as a verbal biological category; it is a finite subset of the codon cube with a fixed observed support, fixed local faces, and a fixed label readback supplied by the curated table.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The leave-one-codon-out module classification assigns all thirteen row codons to six named modules, with no residue left over.  The complete assignment is
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The classification audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the module list, and no unassigned codons.  Each module contains at least one codon marked predictable or boundary-supported in the leave-one-codon-out analysis.  This is the chapter-specific content of the claim: the row can be decomposed into local code-label modules without inventing codons outside the observed row and without leaving a row codon unexplained by the module register.

\paragraph{Predictive row behavior.}
The codon-level leave-one-out run reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and $8$ high-coverage closure cases.  Its recovery rate is $1.0$, and the recorded threshold comparison is also $1.0$.  At the broader code-table level, $23$ tables were evaluated over $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary class returns to $0.9841269841269841$.  These values support a local reading: the window-six coordinates are recovering codon-label placements in and near the row far more often than the baseline comparison, but the evidence remains at the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The row is also accompanied by finite spectral and null-model summaries.  The spectral run records
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}$$
with radial and cubic residuals both reported as $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, within numerical tolerance for the finite computation.  The observed $M$-shape comparison used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$; it found $1$ exact shape match, $25$ matches at Jaccard at least $0.9$, $55377$ duplicate retries, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts.  The corresponding empirical probabilities are $p=0.0005$ for exact shape and $p=0.0125$ for high-Jaccard shape.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, exact-shape matches are $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$, and the reported probabilities are $0.0$ for exact shape and $0.0035$ for the lower-tail event.  These figures say that the finite row and its surrounding shape are not typical under the sampled comparison ensembles used here.

\paragraph{Family-level stability.}
The leave-one-code-out family summary divides the evaluated cases into $5$ families.  The observed family hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  This does not turn the window-six geometry into a universal biological rule.  It only records that, in the finite family table supplied to this run, the local row-and-boundary procedure remains highly aligned with code-label recovery across the listed families.

\paragraph{Scientific boundary.}
The result is a code-read result, not a mechanism result.  The curated standard-code table is a contact for codon-to-label readback only.  From the module partition, the punctured-cube row, the spectral values, the null-model probabilities, and the family hit rates, one may report a finite local boundary-tile structure in $Q_6$ for the standard-code label table.  One may not infer translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Any such higher-layer claim would require an additional contact that directly measures that layer.  In particular, amino-acid label sequences read from codons are not observations of folded structure, and CUN/UUR or tRNA-Leu wording alone does not supply a structure, function, or mechanism contact.
