\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite-row object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinates read as a finite codon display and with labels supplied by the curated standard-code table.  The finite exceptional row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is observed as a punctured-cube tile with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the data used here are not a continuous model, an asymptotic inference, or a biological mechanism; they are a finite boundary row in the six-coordinate codon display together with the external table contact that assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The leave-one-codon-out classification assigns every codon in $R$ to one of six named modules, with no omitted codons and no extra codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{AGR} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
R_{AR/Lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
R_{CUN} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
R_{Ile/Met} &= \{AUA\},\\
R_{SSN} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
R_{Stop/Trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row audit gives $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, empty extra-module codons, empty missing-from-modules, and empty unassigned codons.  Equivalently, the module classifier is a total finite partition of the observed row $R$ at this resolution.  The certificate condition attached to the row is that every module contains a predictable or boundary member; the evidence table records the module-level witnesses listed above for $AGR$, $AR/Lys$, $CUN$, $Ile/Met$, $SSN$, and $Stop/Trp$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The codon-deletion probe reports $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, $0$ anchor cases, and $8$ high-closure-coverage cases.  The recorded recovery rate is $1.0$, and the recovery-rate-above-threshold flag is also $1.0$.  In this finite-row sense, removing one codon from the row does not destroy the row-level module classification: the missing codon remains recoverable either by the predictable component or by the boundary component.  The statement is local to the row and to the given six-bit display.  It does not assert that a cell translates a codon because of this geometry, and it does not assert that a codon label determines a folded structure.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral checks.}
The shape comparison against $2000$ sampled alternatives recorded $1$ exact shape match, giving $p\_{exact}=0.0005$, and $25$ high-Jaccard matches, giving $p\_{Jaccard\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling run used seed $42$, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-match count was $0$, the lower-tail count at the observed $M$ size was $7$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-shape $p$-value was $0.0$, and the lower-tail $p$-value was $0.0035$.  The associated spectral row records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,\\
P(\mu) &= -6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.

The displayed residual is numerically at tolerance scale for the stated cubic or radial polynomial check.  These finite numbers are evidence that the row is structured relative to the chosen coordinate and null probes; they are not evidence of folding, function, physical admissibility, or mechanism.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used by this chapter is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact has codon-label resolution.  It can test code-read claims and local codon-tile label splits, but it cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently, the admissible conclusion is narrow: the six-bit codon display exposes a finite local boundary-tile structure whose thirteen-row module classification is complete and whose leave-one-codon-out recovery is recorded as perfect in the supplied finite run.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
