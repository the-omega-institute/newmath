\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback boundary.}
This BioReality record works on the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ with a curated standard-code table used only as a codon-label readback contact. The row under test is the finite boundary set
$R=\{AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}$,

The punctured-cube audit gives the same thirteen codons and the face-count vector $(13,16,4)$, so the object being classified is not an informal biological slogan but a finite local tile in the codon cube. $$
Thus the module classification is exact at the code-read layer: every observed boundary codon is accounted for, and every module member is drawn from the observed boundary set.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The conclusion is deliberately local. Window-six coordinates expose a boundary-tile organization of the standard-code labels, and the six modules give a complete finite partition of the thirteen codons in $R$. Nothing in this row proves that DNA biologically obeys the geometry as a necessity. Nothing here proves translation realization, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality. An amino-acid label sequence read back from the curated code table is still only a code-layer readback. Any claim across the translation, structure, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality boundary requires a separate reality contact with its own finite rows.
