\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Module Classification for the Standard Code}
\label{sec:bior reality-leave-one-codon-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record studies the standard-code codon table through a six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$, where each codon is read as a finite point and the observed label is the curated amino-acid or stop assignment. The tested residual set is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$. The same residual set is also reported as a punctured cubical object with face vector $(13,16,4)$, so the finite shape contains thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional faces, and four two-dimensional faces in the local codon geometry. This is a code-layer statement: the geometry organizes codon-label rows, while the labels themselves enter from the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model evidence.}
The finite display has a measured spectral profile with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The associated radial cubic check returns residual value $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically at zero for the reported tolerance. Against the sampled null model with $N=2000$, the exact shape appeared once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while the high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ appeared twenty-five times, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampling run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite comparison is not being treated as an asymptotic theorem; it is a bounded computational contact with the displayed codon rows. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison, again with $N=2000$, the exact shape count was $0$, the observed module-size statistic was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below that statistic was $7$, giving $p=0.0035$ for the lower tail and $p=0.0$ for exact-shape recurrence in that sample.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate after adding the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The family split is also finite: five families were evaluated, with rates $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial or plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate codes. The leave-one-codon-out check reports no anchor codons, five boundary codons, eight high-closure-coverage codons, eight predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$. Thus the tested effect is not a claim that every omitted codon is forced by a universal law; it is a finite recovery pattern inside the supplied codon-label table and its local boundary geometry.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification partitions the thirteen residual codons into six named modules. The reported classes are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completion audit gives $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the module list, and no unassigned codons. Each module contains at least one codon that is either predictable under the finite closure test or lies on the reported boundary. This supports the narrower statement that the residual codon rows are completely classified by the six local modules under the standard-code readback.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The empirical contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table: it supplies codon-to-label assignments for amino-acid and stop labels. That contact can test code-layer readback and local label splitting, but it does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality across living systems. Consequently, the module classification may be used as evidence for a local codon-table regularity, not as evidence that proteins fold in a particular way, that an amino-acid sequence is physically viable, that a biological function is present, or that the six-coordinate geometry is a necessary law of genetic coding. Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate experimental or curated contact at the relevant layer.
