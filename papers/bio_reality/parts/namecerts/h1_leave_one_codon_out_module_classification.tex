\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record is a window-six reading of the standard-code codon table.  Each codon is treated as a point of the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with the biological label supplied by the curated standard-code table.  The finite residual set singled out by the row data has cardinality $13$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies $R$ with a punctured-cube shape whose face vector is $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the geometric content is finite and checkable: it is not an informal shape drawn over the code table, but a specified subset of the codon cube with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-faces under the chosen local display.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The leave-one-codon-out module classification assigns every codon of $R$ to a named local module, with no surplus and no omission.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completion counters are $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, $extra\_module\_codons=\varnothing$, $missing\_from\_modules=\varnothing$, and $unassigned\_codons=\varnothing$.  The substantive classification statement is therefore exact: every residual codon belongs to one of these modules, and each module has either a predictable site or a boundary role in the leave-one-codon-out probe.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The finite packet is accompanied by scalar diagnostics that keep the shape claim numerically anchored.  The spectrum row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with radial polynomial value $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, within numerical tolerance of zero.  In the shape-null row, $N=2000$ samples were drawn after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape was matched once, so $p_{exact}=0.0005$; the high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was met $25$ times, so $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null row reports $N=2000$, observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These numbers do not turn the codon geometry into a biological law, but they do show that the observed finite shape is not treated as an unmeasured visual coincidence.

\paragraph{Leave-one performance.}
The code-table leave-one experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Against a baseline hit rate $0.19821739130434785$, the above-baseline hit rate is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary class is $0.9841269841269841$.  The codon-level leave-one row reports $anchor\_count=0$, $boundary\_count=5$, $closure\_coverage\_high=8$, $predictable\_count=8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast.  The module classification is therefore best read as a local code-read regularity: the finite codon-window geometry organizes the thirteen residual codons into a complete module cover, and the leave-one tests recover targets at rates far above the baseline reported in the same finite row.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table, namely the codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop assignment.  That contact can test label readback and local label splitting inside the codon window.  It cannot test whether translation is realized in a cell, whether an amino-acid string folds, whether a molecule is physically admissible, whether a protein has function, whether a mechanism is universal, or whether the codon cube imposes a necessary law on biology.  Consequently, the chapter claims only a code-layer module classification for the standard-code label table.  Any assertion about translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate observation at that higher layer.
