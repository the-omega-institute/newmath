\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}\label{sec:bior-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record concerns the window-six codon carrier $Q_6$, read as the six-bit display of the standard-code codon table. The biological contact used here is only the curated codon-to-label table: it says that a codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label in the standard code. The contact is therefore a code-layer readback contact, not a translation experiment, not a folding assay, not a physical-admissibility certificate, not a function observation, and not a universality theorem. Within that restriction, the finite row singled out by the coordinate and closure probes is the $13$-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube check returns the same codon list with face-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the object being classified in this chapter is not an arbitrary biological table; it is the finite row $R$ exposed by the window-six codon geometry and then read back through the external standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The leave-one-codon-out module classification assigns every codon in $R$ to one of six local label modules. The observed module rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the module union, and no unassigned codons. Equivalently, the six displayed rows form a complete finite cover of $R$ by the codon modules used by the probe. The substantive content is local: every module has either a predictable member or a boundary member under the leave-one-codon-out test. This is the precise sense in which the codon tile is exposed; it is not a claim that the tile is a global biological law.

\paragraph{Predictability and boundary counts.}
At the single-codon level, the leave-one-codon-out audit reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The absence of anchor codons is important: the classification is not certified by immutable singleton anchors. Instead, it is a mixed predictable-or-boundary certificate across the six modules. The observed recovery rate is therefore a local finite-row recovery statement: deleting one codon from the code-layer row still leaves enough module and boundary information to recover the tested classification in this data run. It does not assert that translation machinery, tRNA recognition, codon usage bias, or protein-level function has been reconstructed.

\paragraph{Code-table comparison.}
The corresponding leave-one-code-out comparison evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate, $1.0$ for candidate-other, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast, across $5$ families. These numbers are consistent with a strong code-read signal at the level of curated label tables. They still remain below the translation boundary: they compare finite label rows, not biochemical realization.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral checks.}
The row $R$ also sits inside a broader finite shape audit. In the $M$-shape significance run, $N=2000$ distinct samples were obtained only after $57377$ sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries. Exact shape matches occurred once, giving $p=0.0005$, and the high-Jaccard threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was reached $25$ times, giving $p=0.0125$. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null run, the observed $M$ size was $20$, exact match count was $0$ out of $2000$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, the exact-shape value was $p=0.0$, and the lower-tail value was $p=0.0035$. The spectral row records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,\\
\varepsilon_\mathrm{cubic}&=-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The residual is numerically near zero at the stated precision, so the finite spectrum is compatible with the recorded cubic relation for the audited row. These scalar checks support the claim that the row is structured in the window-six coordinate system, but they do not supply an independent biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready statement is therefore deliberately narrow. The data certify a complete finite module classification of the $13$-codon row $R$ in the standard-code label readback layer, with every module containing predictable-or-boundary support and with no missing or extra codons in the module audit. The contact permits the following claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose labels can be read back against the curated standard-code table. The same contact forbids the stronger claims: no translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from this chapter unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
