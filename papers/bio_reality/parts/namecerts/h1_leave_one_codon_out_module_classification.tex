\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism.  It is the finite display of the $64$ codons as window-six coordinates, together with an external label readback assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or stop label.  The empirical contact used here is the curated standard-code table, so the verified layer is only the code layer: a codon-to-label table can be read, compared, and left out row by row.  The contact does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Recovered boundary set.}
The finite boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row set is certified as the punctured-cube row, with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a set of exceptional labels.  It has a finite tile shape inside $Q_6$, with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces.  This is the geometric row on which the leave-one-codon-out module test is performed.

$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null probe used $N=2000$, observed $0$ exact matches, and reported $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$-size.  The observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-shape probability is $0.0$, and the lower-tail probability is

These finite values support the claim that the observed local tile is not a typical row under the tested shuffles.  They do not turn the tile into a law of biology.

The classification check reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and assigned count $13$.  It also reports no extra module codons, no missing codons from the module list, and no unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{extra}=\varnothing,\qquad

Every listed module therefore contains only codons belonging to the recovered boundary row, and every codon in the recovered boundary row appears in exactly one listed module.  The row-level statement certified by this chapter is that each module has predictable-or-boundary support inside the window-six code-read tile.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The local codon tile is a finite code-read phenomenon: it compares coordinates, closures, spectra, relations, and curated labels in the standard-code table.  No claim of translation realization follows from these data.  No claim of folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or universality follows from these data.  Amino-acid label readback is not folded protein structure.  A separate contact would be required for each higher layer: translation must be supplied by a translation contact, folding by a structure contact, physical admissibility by a physical contact, and function by a functional contact.  Without such a contact, the result remains exactly a finite-row module classification for a local window-six codon tile.
