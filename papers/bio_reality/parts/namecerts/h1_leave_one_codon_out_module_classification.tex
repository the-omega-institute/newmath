\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This BioReality record works at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  The reality contact is therefore a finite label table, not a mechanism of translation and not an observation of folded protein structure.  The local object under test is the distinguished row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$.  In the punctured-cube computation the same row has face data $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, so the finite object is not merely a set of labels but a small cubical boundary tile with vertices, edges, and two-dimensional faces all counted inside the same six-bit display.

\paragraph{Spectral scale of the tile.}
The measured finite spectrum gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ for the enclosing module scale and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$ for the row scale, with $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The corresponding cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the numerical row used in this chapter is stable at the displayed precision.  The spectral statement is only a finite-row invariant of the codon-tile display: it says that this particular standard-code readback carries a reproducible local shape, not that the biological system is forced by this geometry.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
The shape comparison against finite alternatives used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  Among $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ retries were duplicate retries, one exact shape match was found, and $25$ samples had Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$.  Thus the reported finite-row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null gave $N=2000$, observed module size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support a code-layer finite anomaly: the local tile is rare under the stated sampling regimes.  They do not by themselves supply translation dynamics, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon behavior.}
In the leave-one-code-out probe, $23$ code tables and $63$ targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  In the leave-one-codon-out probe the anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the predictable count was $8$, closure coverage high was $8$, and the recovery rate was $1.0$.  Thus every recovered codon in this finite row is accounted for by predictable or boundary behavior, while no separate anchor class is needed for this particular readback.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification partitions the full row $R$ into six named finite blocks:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Hence the classification is complete at the row level: each member of $R$ belongs to exactly one displayed module, and each displayed module has predictable-or-boundary support.  Family-level leave-one-code-out checks evaluated $5$ families; the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.

\paragraph{Boundary of the result.}
The result is a finite code-read statement.  It says that, inside the six-bit codon display and under the curated standard-code label bridge, the thirteen-codon row has a complete six-module classification whose members are all covered by predictable or boundary behavior.  It does not say that a local codon tile is translation itself; it does not infer folding from amino-acid label readback; it does not establish physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any promotion beyond code-read requires a separate higher-layer contact, such as an experimental translation contact for translation realization or an independent structural contact for folded protein structure.
