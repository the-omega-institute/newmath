\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only external contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is sufficient for code-layer label readback and for testing whether the local codon tile split aligns with the standard labels.  It is not evidence for translation realization, protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global law of genetic codes.  In particular, amino-acid label sequences are not folded protein structures.  Cross-organism wording, tRNA notation, and codon-family names are not promoted to structural or functional evidence without a separate observation at that layer.

Consequently the finite conclusion is narrow.  Within the six-bit codon display, the row identifies a $13$-codon boundary set, verifies its punctured-cube face data, records non-null spectral and shape evidence, and classifies every boundary codon into one of six named modules.  The biological readback remains the curated standard-code label table.  Any claim about translation, folding, physical possibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires an additional contact that is not present in this row.
