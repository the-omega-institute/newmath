\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object examined here is the standard-code codon label table, read only at the code-label layer.  A codon is represented as a six-coordinate display over the codon cube, and the finite row under study is the distinguished residual set
$R=\{AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}$.
The verified cardinality is $|R|=13$.  The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the tested input is not an open-ended biological mechanism.  It is a finite codon-label configuration whose rows are the thirteen codons above, together with the local adjacency and closure information induced by the six-coordinate codon display.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The leave-one-codon-out classification assigns all thirteen codons in $R$ to named local modules.  The verified partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R={}&\{AGA,AGG\}\cup\{AAA\}\cup\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}\\
&\cup\{AUA\}\cup\{UCA,UUA\}\cup\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding module names are, respectively, $agr$, $ar\_lys$, $cun$, $ile\_met$, $ssn$, and $stop\_trp$.  The completeness check reports $R\_size=13$ and $assigned\_count=13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  This makes the classification a genuine finite accounting result: every codon in the residual row is placed exactly within the displayed module list.

\paragraph{Local predictability row.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe reports $predictable\_count=8$, $boundary\_count=5$, $anchor\_count=0$, and $closure\_coverage\_high=8$.  Its recovery rate is $1.0$.  In words, the row does not recover by fixed anchor codons; instead, it separates into eight codons recovered by the local closure signal and five codons recorded as boundary cases.  The module statement strengthens this bookkeeping: every module has at least one predictable or boundary member, so no module is invisible to the local leave-one-codon-out test.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The same finite configuration is accompanied by a spectral row.  The verified numerical values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is also the recorded cubic-polynomial residual.  This is a numerical consistency check for the finite coordinate construction, not an independent biological law.

A separate randomized shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled tables.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p\_exact=0.0005$, and twenty-five matches at Jaccard threshold $0.9$, giving $p\_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling log records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries under seed $42$.  Under an amino-acid-class-preserving null model, the observed module size was $20$; the exact-shape count was $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$ out of $2000$, giving $p=0.0035$ for that lower-tail event.

\paragraph{Code-table recovery checks.}
The leave-one-code-out table test evaluated $23$ genetic-code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ after adding the boundary class was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the recorded hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  The family count was $5$.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The finite result supports a code-layer statement: the standard-code label table, when read through window-six codon coordinates, exposes a local residual row $R$ with a complete six-module classification, a punctured-cube combinatorial shape, a stable spectral numerical row, and strong leave-one-out recovery statistics.  The scientifically meaningful claim is therefore local and finite.  It says that the curated codon-to-label table contains a detectable boundary-tile organization around the listed codons, and that the organization survives the specified leave-one-codon-out and leave-one-code-out checks.

\paragraph{Local boundary of the claim.}
Nothing in this chapter establishes translation as a biochemical process, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law binding all organisms.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label readback only.  Amino-acid labels are not folded structures, codon rows are not ribosomal dynamics, and the six-coordinate display is not a physical model of molecular realization.  Any claim about translation, structure, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate empirical contact aimed at that higher layer.  The result here remains a finite code-read classification of the thirteen-codon residual row and its local modules.
