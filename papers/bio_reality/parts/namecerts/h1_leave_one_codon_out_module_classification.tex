\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}\label{sec:bioreality-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite object in this record is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with a curated standard-code label table.  The carrier is not treated as a biological mechanism.  It is a six-bit codon display whose admissible observations are local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The reality contact is the code-layer readback: a curated table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  Thus the record may test code-read structure, local codon-tile label split, and finite module coverage, but it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{The residual row set.}
The finite residual set $R$ has exactly thirteen codons.  In the observed standard-code row, the set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The punctured-cube check gives the same codon list and the face-vector summary
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The point of the row is therefore not that all codons are recovered by a global law, but that the remaining thirteen positions form a small finite tile with a reproducible boundary profile.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The leave-one-codon-out module classification assigns all thirteen residual codons and assigns no codon outside the modules.  The complete module table is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The accounting row is exact:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R\_\mathrm{size}=13,\qquad \mathrm{assigned\_count}=13,\qquad
\mathrm{extra}=\mathrm{missing}=\mathrm{unassigned}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every displayed module has at least one codon lying in the predictable-or-boundary class.  The result is a finite-row classification statement: within this specific residual code-read tile, the six named modules cover the row without leakage and without omission.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape evidence.}
The spectral row attached to the same local tile reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the observed numerical row satisfies its cubic check to the displayed tolerance.  The null comparison for the $M$-shape experiment used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  Among $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ were duplicate retries; the exact-shape match count was $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while the count with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, observed $\mathrm{observed\_M\_size}=20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0$, and lower-tail value $0.0035$.  These rows support a non-generic local shape signal, but they do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-out recovery.}
At the code-table level, the leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$; and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  At the codon level, the leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The recovery-rate-above-threshold row is also $1.0$.  By family, the finite split contains $5$ families, with hit rates above baseline
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module classification is therefore consistent with both the row-level recovery experiment and the family-level code-read experiment.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The finite-row conclusion is deliberately narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local residual tile, and the curated standard-code table supplies the external label readback used to name the split.  The chapter does not claim that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not claim translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global law of biology.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation; no fold, physical constraint, or function is observed by this contact.  Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate higher-layer contact rather than a stronger interpretation of the same codon table.
