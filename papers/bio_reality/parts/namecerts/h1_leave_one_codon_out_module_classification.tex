\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read object. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code label table. The contact used here is deliberately narrow: it supplies codon-to-label assignments for amino-acid or stop labels. It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The mathematical question is therefore not whether the genetic code is caused by the window-six geometry, but whether the finite coordinate display exposes a stable local boundary pattern inside the curated label table.

The distinguished residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube computation gives the same thirteen codons and records the finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object under discussion is a finite, auditable row set, not an asymptotic or informal geometric metaphor.

\begin{aligned}

In finite-row terms, every reported module has at least one codon in the predictable-or-boundary class, and the union of the six named modules is exactly the observed residual set $R$.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The supported claim is a code-layer claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code label table. The table is the only reality bridge used here, and it is only a bridge for codon-label readback. Therefore the record cannot claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or biological necessity. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded structure. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Any promotion beyond code-read requires a separate higher-layer contact, with its own finite observations and its own null boundary.
