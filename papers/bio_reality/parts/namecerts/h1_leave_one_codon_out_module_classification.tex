\subsection{Leave-one-codon module classification in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite display and scope.}
The object studied here is the standard-code codon table after it is placed on the six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$.  The row does not claim that the cube is a biochemical cause.  It records a finite code-layer pattern: a local tile, a distinguished boundary set, and a module partition of the codons that participate in the leave-one-codon experiment.  The only biological contact used in this chapter is the curated standard genetic-code table, read as a codon-to-label assignment table.  Consequently the result is about code labels and local combinatorics.  It does not establish translation in a cell, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Any such higher-layer conclusion would require an additional observation channel with its own data.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The finite boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality row gives $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube row identifies the same thirteen codons with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the boundary is not a loose list of exceptional codons; it is a finite subcomplex of the window-six display with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-faces.  The codons in $R$ are exactly the codons that must be assigned to one of the module classes below.

\paragraph{Module assignment.}
The leave-one-codon classification partitions the thirteen boundary codons into six named modules.  The verified assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},&
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},&
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},&
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness counters are sharp: the boundary size is $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons are all empty.  In particular, this row is a classification theorem for the finite table supplied to the experiment: every codon in $R$ appears once in the displayed module list, and no listed module codon lies outside $R$.

\paragraph{Predictable-or-boundary condition.}
The row also verifies that every displayed module has at least one codon falling under the predictable-or-boundary test.  This is the local content of the leave-one-codon experiment.  The codons are not inferred from an unobserved molecular mechanism; they are tested against the finite code-label table and the window-six coordinate closure.  The aggregate leave-one-codon row reports $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, $0$ anchor cases, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The resulting statement is therefore exact at this finite resolution: within the supplied table, every module is covered by the predictable-or-boundary alternative, and the recovery criterion is met without leftover module codons.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}
The same finite packet contains two independent checks on shape non-genericity.  The spectral row records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with radial polynomial residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, matching the tolerance row for the cubic relation.  The random-shape row with seed $42$ uses $N=2000$ samples and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, including $55377$ duplicate retries.  It finds $1$ exact shape match, so $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, so $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null row, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These checks make the local tile statistically nontrivial relative to the tested null models, while preserving the same code-layer limitation.

\paragraph{Readback and blocked promotion.}
The finite readback is the standard-code label table: each codon is read only as its curated amino-acid or stop label.  The chapter therefore supports the claim that window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard code and that the thirteen boundary codons admit a complete six-module classification.  It does not support any statement about realized translation dynamics, folded structure, physical viability, organismal function, or necessity across biology.  Those statements are blocked here because the only contact is a codon-label table.  A later claim may cross one of those boundaries only after adding a separate contact that actually observes the corresponding layer.
