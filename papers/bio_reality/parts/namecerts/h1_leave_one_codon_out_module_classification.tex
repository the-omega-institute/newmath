\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The carrier is the finite codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-bit display of the four-letter codon alphabet after a fixed binary coordinate presentation.  The chapter records a code-read phenomenon, not a mechanistic law: the external reality contact is the curated standard-code table, whose role is only to attach amino-acid or stop labels to codons.  In this coordinate, the residual local row $R$ contains exactly thirteen codons,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is identified as a punctured-cube object with finite face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the observed object is not a free-form biological narrative: it is a concrete finite subconfiguration in $Q_6$ with thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four local two-faces.

The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the cubic-polynomial check, so the displayed spectral tuple is numerically consistent to the reported tolerance.  The shape significance test used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the small exact-match count should be read against a difficult finite sampling space rather than against an unlimited independent draw model.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe is sharper because it acts on individual codons inside the row.  It reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, and $8$ predictable cases. The absence of anchors is important: the result is not saying that a privileged singleton explains the whole code table. $$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{extra\_module\_codons}&=\varnothing,\\

$$
\begin{aligned}

The classification is therefore complete as a finite-row statement: no codon from $R$ is left outside the module list, and no module introduces an extra codon beyond $R$.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  That contact supports codon-label readback and local label-split checks.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Consequently, the chapter's claim is deliberately local: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite boundary-tile pattern whose thirteen-row module classification is complete and whose leave-one-codon-out recovery is total in the supplied finite data.  No claim is made that DNA obeys this geometry as a global law, that the geometry performs translation, or that amino-acid label readback determines folded protein structure.  Any promotion beyond the code-read layer would require a separate contact with the higher layer being claimed.
