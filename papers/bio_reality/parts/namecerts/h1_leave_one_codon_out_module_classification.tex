\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with the standard-code label readback supplied by a curated codon table.  The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class.  The contact is intentionally narrow: it observes only codon-to-label readback at the code layer.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  The local object is therefore a finite boundary-tile record inside $Q_6$, not a global biological law.

\paragraph{Residual row.}
The residual row $R$ contains exactly thirteen codons.  In the finite-row audit it is the punctured-cube row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
|R|&=13,\\
f(R)&=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The equality $|R|=13$ is not inferred from a biological mechanism; it is a finite coordinate fact of the row selected by the standard-code readback.  The face vector $(13,16,4)$ records the same row as a local cubical object: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces after the relevant puncture.  This is the layer on which the module classification is allowed to speak.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out module classification.}
The leave-one-codon-out row has no anchors, five boundary cases, eight high-coverage closure cases, eight predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification assigns all thirteen codons of $R$, with no extras, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{size}}&=13,\\
\text{assigned count}&=13,\\
\text{extra module codons}&=0,\\
\text{missing from modules}&=0,\\
\text{unassigned codons}&=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has at least one predictable or boundary member, and together the six modules exhaust the residual row.  This is the specific finite content of the claim: the leave-one-codon-out test does not merely recover isolated codons, but partitions the entire punctured row into named local modules whose members are covered by predictability or boundary behavior.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The result is a local code-layer classification statement.  It says that, inside the six-bit codon display and under the curated standard-code readback, the thirteen-codon residual row decomposes into six complete modules, each supported by predictable or boundary behavior.  It does not say that the codon table is forced by BEDC geometry, that translation is realized by the tile, that amino-acid label readback determines folded protein structure, that the row is physically admissible, that it realizes biological function, that it supplies a mechanism, or that it is universal.  Any such higher-layer assertion requires a separate reality contact beyond the curated standard-code table.
