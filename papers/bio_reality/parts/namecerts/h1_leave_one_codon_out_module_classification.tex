\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}\label{sec:biorality-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite claim.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with a codon displayed as a finite coordinate row and with curated amino-acid or stop labels supplied by an external standard-code table.  The claim is not that the cube is a biological mechanism.  The finite assertion is narrower: the leave-one-codon-out boundary set $R$ is partitioned into named modules, and every codon in those modules is accounted for by the predictable-or-boundary classification supplied by the row data.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The verified boundary row has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same row and reports the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a label list: it is also a finite cubical boundary object with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-faces, and $4$ two-faces in the tested local tile.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinate.}
The associated spectral summary records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports a cubic, or radial, residual of $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the finite spectral check is satisfied to numerical tolerance.  This number is used here only as a coordinate witness for the local row geometry.  It is not a claim about translation, folding, function, physical admissibility, or biological universality.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Two finite null rows constrain the interpretation.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p=0.0005$, and $25$ samples had Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$.  The sampler recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries with seed $42$.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null run, the observed $M$ size was $20$; among $N=2000$ samples there were $0$ exact shape matches and $7$ rows at or below the observed $M$ size.  The reported values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These tests support the finite-row status of the local shape.  They do not convert that shape into a mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon readback.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  Separately, the leave-one-codon-out run recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate $1.0$.  These are code-layer readback statistics against curated labels.

The family split contains $5$ families.  The above-baseline family hit rates are

This family row says that the tested split is stable across these finite code-table families at the recorded rates.  It does not say that all genetic codes, all organisms, or all molecular contexts obey a universal geometric rule.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the thirteen-row boundary.  It assigns $13$ codons out of $R$ to modules, with no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The finite module row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The disjoint union of these displayed modules has size
$$
\begin{aligned}
2+1+4+1+2+3=13,
\end{aligned}
$$
matching $|R|=13$.  Hence the module classifier covers exactly the verified boundary row.  In the internal vocabulary of the record, every displayed module has predictable-or-boundary support.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the local tile is retained as a code-read finite-row claim: the six-bit coordinate and the curated labels expose a boundary classification, while all higher biological layers remain outside the certified scope unless supplied by a separate contact.
