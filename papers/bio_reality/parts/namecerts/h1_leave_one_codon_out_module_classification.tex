\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Claim under test.}
The claim \texttt{h1.leave\_one\_codon\_out.module\_classification} concerns the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer.  The finite object is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with local faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The observed labels are not generated by the cube alone: the codon-to-label table is a curated contact, and the coordinate system is used only to test whether the label table exhibits a local boundary pattern.  The relevant finite row is the set $R$ of codons selected by the punctured-cube pattern:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite audit reports the face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not being treated as an undifferentiated list of codons.  It is a thirteen-point subcomplex with sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies and four local two-dimensional faces inside the six-bit codon display.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape measurements.}
The coordinate row also has a numerical spectrum.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is within the recorded tolerance for the radial polynomial computation.  Thus the spectrum is not an informal visual impression; it is a finite numerical invariant attached to the same codon row.

The shape comparison against random relabelings used $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$.  There was exactly one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling process also records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite comparison is a constrained draw over admissible distinct shapes rather than an unconstrained free list.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information and therefore asks a stricter question: whether the observed row is still exceptional when a biologically meaningful label partition is held fixed.  In $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$, the exact match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$.  The observed size of the associated $M$ row is $20$, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This does not turn the coordinate row into a mechanism.  It only says that, under this finite class-preserving randomization, the observed local arrangement is uncommon.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
The broader leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ above\ baseline} &= 0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in}\ M\setminus T &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in}\ M\setminus T\ \mathrm{plus\ boundary} &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-resolved audit groups the same behavior into $5$ families.  The recorded family hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  These numbers support a local code-read regularity across the sampled code-table rows, not a claim that the coordinate pattern causes translation.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The direct leave-one-codon-out finite row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ codons with high closure coverage, and $8$ predictable codons.  The recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the threshold comparison also records $1.0$.  In this setting, recovery means that the local coordinate-and-boundary test can account for the held-out codon at the code-label level.  It does not mean that a ribosome, aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase, folded protein, or cellular function has been reconstructed.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module-classification row partitions all thirteen codons in $R$ into six named modules.  The complete finite assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness counters are exact:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{size}}=13,\qquad \mathrm{assigned\ count}=13,\qquad
\mathrm{extra\ module\ codons}=\varnothing,\\
\mathrm{missing\ from\ modules}=\varnothing,\qquad
\mathrm{unassigned\ codons}=\varnothing.
\end{aligned}
$$
Consequently every codon in the punctured-cube row belongs to one of the listed modules, and every listed module has codons that are either predictable in the leave-one-codon-out test or lie on the recorded boundary.  This is the precise content of the module-classification result.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The curated standard-code table supplies codon-label readback only.  It is enough to say that the six-bit coordinate row, the punctured-cube shape, the spectral values, the null-model comparisons, and the module assignment are aligned with a local code-read boundary pattern.  It is not enough to claim translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a biochemical mechanism, or universality across life.  Any such stronger statement would require a separate observation at the relevant layer.  In particular, amino-acid label sequences are not protein structures, CUN or UUR wording is not structural evidence, and a local codon tile is not a global law of biology.
