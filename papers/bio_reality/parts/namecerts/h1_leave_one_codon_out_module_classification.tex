\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_{6}$, read through a curated standard-code label table.  The carrier data are not treated as a mechanism for translation.  They are a code-layer reality contact: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label, and the BEDC object records how a local window-six tile meets that label table.  The relevant distinction set consists of the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The internal structure used here is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data, with readback restricted to the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Observed residual row.}
The finite residual row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The observed codons are exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a set of exceptional labels; it has a finite face profile inside the codon cube.  This is the local geometric object tested by the leave-one-codon-out classification.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$.  At the single-codon level, the leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The threshold comparison also reports recovery rate above threshold $1.0$.  The finite reading is therefore sharp: the row has no anchor codons in this classification, but every recovered codon is accounted for either by predictable closure behavior or by boundary status.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification partitions all $13$ codons of $R$ with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The complete finite assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness check reports $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, and empty sets of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons.  Hence the module row is not a partial annotation of the residual set: it is a total finite classification of the punctured-cube row.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The ready claim is a code-read claim only.  The data support a local boundary-tile structure in $Q_{6}$ and a complete module classification of the $13$-codon residual row.  They do not show that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as a biological necessity, and they do not show translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or universality of the standard code.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A separate higher-layer contact would be required before any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality could be made.
