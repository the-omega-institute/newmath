\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{sec:leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read object over the six-bit codon display $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a molecular mechanism and is not a claim about translation dynamics.  It is a finite coordinate surface on which codon labels can be read back from a curated standard-code contact.  The distinguished residual row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit reports $|R|=13$, and the same row is realized as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object under discussion is a local tile in codon-coordinate space: thirteen listed codons, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four local square faces.  The readback layer is the standard-code label table, not the existence of any protein product.

Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$ out of $2000$, giving probabilities $p=0.0$ for exact shape and $p=0.0035$ for the lower-tail event.  These numbers support a finite coordinate anomaly at the code-read layer; they do not, by themselves, identify a biological cause.

The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level split used $5$ families. These row counts say that the local codon tile is informative for code-label recovery in the supplied finite experiment.  They do not say that the tile is a global biological law.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The admissible conclusion is local and finite: in the six-bit codon display, the standard-code label table exposes a thirteen-codon residual tile whose module partition is complete and whose leave-one recovery statistics are far above the stated baseline.  The reality contact is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read behavior and local label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
