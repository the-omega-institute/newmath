\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact tests code readback and local codon-tile label splitting.  It does not test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, protein folding, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Consequently the chapter claim is local: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite boundary-tile and module-classification pattern for the standard-code label table.  No claim is made that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity, that a local codon tile is a global biological law, or that amino-acid label-sequence readback is folded protein structure.  Any promotion beyond the code-read layer requires a separate reality contact supplying the missing translation, structure, physical, functional, mechanistic, or universality evidence.
