\subsection{Leave-one-codon module classification in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:bior-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer readback problem over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The finite carrier is not treated as a biochemical mechanism.  It is a coordinate display on which codons can be grouped, removed, and tested against local closure and boundary patterns.  The biological contact is the curated standard genetic-code table: it supplies codon-to-label assignments for amino-acid or stop labels, and it supplies no observation of translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or structural order.

The distinguished row set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row set is recorded as a punctured-cube configuration with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the test is not merely that thirteen codons were selected; the selected codons also occupy a finite local tile with $16$ one-dimensional incidences and $4$ two-dimensional faces.  The row-level question is whether leave-one-codon behavior respects this local tile decomposition at the level of codon labels.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The coordinate data include a finite spectral summary.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically indistinguishable from zero at the stated tolerance of the run.  These numbers are used only as finite diagnostics of the displayed codon geometry.  They do not convert the coordinate construction into a biological law.

The principal shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  The run needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ alternatives with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reported observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  Its reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These sampling figures support the claim that the observed local arrangement is atypical under the recorded finite null models.  They do not identify a causal source for the arrangement.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon behavior.}
The leave-one-code experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level refinement grouped the evaluated tables into $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The leave-one-codon experiment reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The recovery-rate threshold check also returned $1.0$.  Hence the finite rows support a sharply delimited statement: within the supplied codon-coordinate display and label table, the removed-codon tests are recovered by predictable or boundary behavior.  The statement remains a code-read statement.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete for the row set $R$.  It assigns all $13$ codons, records no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13,\\
\mathrm{assigned} &= 13,\\
\mathrm{extra} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{missing} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{unassigned} &= 0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has at least one codon classified through predictable or boundary behavior.  The singleton modules $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ and $\mathrm{ile\_met}$ show that the classification is not merely a large-family effect; isolated label corners are included in the same finite accounting.  The $\mathrm{cun}$ block supplies a four-codon local face, the $\mathrm{agr}$ and $\mathrm{ssn}$ blocks supply two-codon boundary classes, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$ supplies a three-point label class.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The result is a local codon-table fact.  In the window-six coordinate system, the $13$-codon row set forms a punctured-cube tile whose modules are fully accounted for by predictable or boundary cases under the supplied leave-one-codon experiment.  The stronger sampling checks make this tile unlikely under the recorded finite null models, and the leave-one-code family checks show that the recovery behavior is not confined to a single table family.  The curated standard-code table is therefore sufficient to test codon-label readback and local label splitting.

The result does not say that DNA or translation must obey this geometry.  It does not produce a ribosome-level model, a folding model, a thermodynamic admissibility criterion, a functional assay, a universal law of code evolution, or a protein-structure prediction.  Amino-acid label sequences obtained from the table remain label sequences.  Any claim about realized translation, folded structure, physical possibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate observational contact designed for that layer.
