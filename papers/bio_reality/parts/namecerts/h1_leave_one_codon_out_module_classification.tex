\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the sixty-four codons. The biological contact is deliberately narrower than the carrier: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and this table is used only as a code-layer readback. The BEDC-side distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class. Thus the object under test is not translation itself, and it is not a protein or a physical realization. It is the finite relation between codon coordinates and standard-code labels.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is detected as a punctured-cube object with finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The point of this row is local: it marks the codons at which the window-six display and the curated label table meet in a nonuniform way. It is not a global law of biology and is not an admissibility test for molecular mechanisms.

\paragraph{Spectral signature.}
The coordinate calculation gives the numerical signature
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the numerical row is internally coherent at the displayed precision. These numbers are used only as finite-row invariants of the codon-coordinate construction. They do not identify a folding energy, a translation rate, a selective pressure, or a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite null sample for the $M$-shape statistic, the run used $N=2000$ distinct draws after $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
It also found $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, $N=2000$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These tests support the statement that the observed finite pattern is not a typical draw under the stated finite nulls. They do not promote the pattern beyond the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The coordinate tile recovered targets inside $M-T$ at rate $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M-T$ plus the boundary at rate $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. Family aggregation used $5$ families. The family hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. This says that the finite row has predictive bite for code-table label recovery in this setup; it does not say that the row generates the genetic code or that it is universal across contacts not tested here.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}
In the leave-one-codon-out calculation, the anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the high-coverage closure count was $8$, and the predictable count was $8$. The recovery rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value reported above threshold. The interpretation is precise: every tested held-out codon in this row is accounted for by the local predictable-or-boundary split. No held-out codon requires a separate anchor class in this finite result.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification partitions the $13$ boundary codons without residue. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness row reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the module list, and no unassigned codons. Equivalently, the six modules form a complete finite classification of the boundary row used by this claim.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The chapter-level claim is therefore a code-read claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose modules cover the $13$ codon row, and the curated standard-code table supplies the label readback. Nothing in this contact supplies translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate contact whose observed fact is about that higher layer, not merely the standard-code label table.
