\paragraph{Reality contact and limit of the claim.}
\label{par:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification-reality-contact}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  That contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for testing whether the local window-six tile separates singleton corners, three-point label classes, closure behavior, and boundary codons.  It is not evidence for translation dynamics, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic necessity, or universality across organisms.  In particular, amino-acid label sequences are not folded structures, and a local codon tile is not a global law of biology.  Any claim crossing from this code-read result into translation, structure, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate reality contact dedicated to that higher layer.
