\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object and coordinate scope.}
This record studies the standard-code codon table only at the code-label layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a binary display of the three nucleotide positions.  The observed exceptional row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row count is $|R|=13$.  Its cubical footprint has $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the finite object is not merely a list of labels: it also has a local face pattern inside $Q_6$.  The intended biological readout is the curated standard genetic-code assignment from codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is a label table, not a translation experiment, not a protein-structure experiment, and not a physical assay.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The finite row comes with a numerical shape check.  The recorded spectral values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, also recorded as the value of the cubic polynomial at the tested root.  In the sampling comparison with $N=2000$ draws and seed $42$, the exact shape was seen once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was met $25$ times, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second null constrained by amino-acid class also used $N=2000$ draws and seed $42$; it found no exact shape matches, so the reported exact-shape frequency is $0.0$, while the lower-tail count at the observed module size $20$ is $7$, giving $p=0.0035$.  The row therefore has finite combinatorial signal under the recorded tests, while the data still remain at codon-label resolution.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the same rate after adjoining the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, across $5$ families.  The leave-one-codon-out run recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers support a local recovery pattern for omitted codon labels, but they do not identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification assigns every member of $R$ and nothing outside it.  The complete partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA, UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA, UAG, UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The audit row reports $R\_size=13$ and $assigned\_count=13$, with empty lists for extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons.  Thus the finite classification is exact at the row level: every codon in the punctured-cube row belongs to one of the six named modules, and the modules introduce no additional codons.  The result is a local boundary-tile classification of code labels in $Q_6$.

\paragraph{Reality contact and blocked promotions.}
The only empirical contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  It licenses statements about codon-to-label readback and local label splits.  It does not, by itself, license claims about translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, organism-level mechanism, or universal biological necessity.  In particular, amino-acid label sequences are not folded structures, and a local cubical tile in codon coordinates is not a global law of biology.  Any promotion beyond the code-read layer requires an independent contact that directly observes the higher layer being claimed.
