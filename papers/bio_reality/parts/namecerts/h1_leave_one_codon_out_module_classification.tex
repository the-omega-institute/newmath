\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{subsec:leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and claim.}
The claim \texttt{h1.leave\_one\_codon\_out.module\_classification} is a finite-row BioReality record for the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The internal BEDC form uses coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data, but its readback is only the standard-code codon-to-label table. The observed reality contact is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. This contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks; it does not by itself supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey the displayed geometry as a biological law.

\paragraph{Recovered boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified row is

$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite geometric object being read is not an open-ended biological mechanism; it is a concrete thirteen-codon row with sixteen one-faces and four two-faces inside the chosen codon-coordinate display.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The spectral row data report $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, also reported as the satisfied cubic-polynomial value, so the spectral computation is internally stable at the displayed tolerance. The shape-significance control used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$, requiring $57377$ distinct-sample attempts because $55377$ retries were duplicates. It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
 p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$ observed $M$-size $20$, found $0$ exact shape matches, and counted $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size. Its reported estimates are $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035$. These figures support a finite-row anomaly against the sampled nulls, not a causal or universal law.

The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$. In the family-resolved version, $5$ families were evaluated: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had hit rate $1.0$ above baseline. The leave-one-codon-out test found $0$ anchor codons, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These row counts are finite readback statistics: they say that the displayed coordinate-and-boundary scheme recovers the tested code labels in the sampled setting, not that the scheme realizes translation or protein function.

The completeness audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons. Each module therefore has either predictable or boundary support in the finite code-read analysis. The classification is exactly a local codon-tile boundary statement: it organizes the thirteen observed row codons into six named modules and checks that the partition exhausts the row.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose resolution is codon label readback. The contact can test the code-read layer and a local codon-tile label split. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, folded protein structure, or a universal mechanism. Therefore amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation. Any later claim about translation, folding, admissible molecular physics, biological function, or universality would require a separate reality contact with its own finite-row data. Without that additional contact, the supported conclusion is limited to this: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically controlled, completely classified local boundary row in the curated standard-code label table.
