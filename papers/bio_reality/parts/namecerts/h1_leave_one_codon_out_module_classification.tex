\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification for the Standard-Code Tile}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Codon-coordinate object.}
The object considered here is the standard-code codon table after it is read at the code-label level through a six-bit codon display. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon treated as a finite coordinate in the four-letter alphabet presentation. The relevant empirical contact is a curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label. That contact is enough to test code-layer label readback and local tile splits, but it is not a translation experiment, a folding experiment, a physical-admissibility assay, a functional assay, or a proof of a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Boundary set and punctured-cube shape.}
The finite boundary row $R$ has exactly $13$ codons. The verified list is
$$\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The independent punctured-cube check returns the same codon list and the face-count vector
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the local object is not merely a count of exceptional labels; it has a verified finite cubical incidence profile. The row named $M$ is present in the tests through its size and shape statistics: its observed size is $20$, and the reported shape tests compare this observed finite pattern with null samples rather than giving a separate codon-by-codon $M$ listing in this row.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The spectral record supplies three scalar coordinates for the same finite construction. The values are
$$\begin{aligned}

The cubic or radial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the recorded polynomial consistency check is at numerical zero at the displayed precision. These numbers should be read as a finite coordinate signature of the codon-label tile, not as a biochemical force law.

\paragraph{Null-model pressure.}
The shape-significance experiment used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}$$
while $25$ samples reached Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It recorded no exact-shape matches, so the exact-shape frequency was $0.0$ in the sample. The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size $20$ was $7$, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
These figures support the claim that the displayed local shape is not a routine outcome of the tested finite shuffles. They do not identify a molecular mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$. The family-level row reports $5$ families, with hit rates above baseline equal to $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast. The leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification row assigns all $13$ codons in $R$ and leaves no unassigned codons. The classification is
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The completeness record states $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons. In words: every codon in the verified boundary set is accounted for by one of the named modules, and every named module contains codons that are predictable or boundary-supported in the finite local test.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The resulting claim is a code-read claim about a finite local codon tile. The standard-code table supplies label assignments, and the coordinate, closure, spectrum, null-model, recovery, and module rows supply a finite pattern around those assignments. Nothing in this record demonstrates that the codon tile is a law of DNA, that a label sequence realizes translation in a cell, that any amino-acid sequence folds, that a folded structure is admissible in physical chemistry, that a protein has biological function, or that the pattern is universal across organisms. Those stronger assertions require separate empirical contacts of the appropriate kind. The present record is therefore ready as a local code-layer module-classification chapter, with its claim deliberately confined to codon-label readback and finite boundary structure.
