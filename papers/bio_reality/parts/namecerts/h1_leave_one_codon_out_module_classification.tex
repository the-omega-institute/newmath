\begin{aligned}

&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
In the punctured-cube calculation the same row appears with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$

The companion $M$ row is not enumerated in the supplied finite-row packet, but its observed size is reported as $|M|=20$ in the amino-acid-class preserving null calculation.  All claims below are therefore stated from the finite row data actually supplied: the $13$ codons of $R$, the punctured-cube face vector, the scalar spectrum, the local leave-one-out counts, and the module assignment table.

This does not turn the geometry into a biological law.  It says only that the particular finite codon-coordinate construction has a stable scalar readback for the row under this computation.

\paragraph{Null comparison.}
The shape is also separated from the supplied random baselines.  In the unrestricted shape comparison with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite audit is explicitly a sampled comparison rather than an analytic enumeration of all alternatives.  Under the amino-acid-class preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact shape count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, and the observed $M$ size is $20$.  The resulting reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row distinction between the observed code-label arrangement and the sampled alternatives.  They do not by themselves supply translation dynamics, molecular structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the supplied $R$ row.  The row size is $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the three defect lists are all empty:

$$

\begin{aligned}

Every module has at least one predictable or boundary representative in the leave-one-codon-out classification.  This is the exact content of the finite module result: the entire $13$-codon row is partitioned by these six named module classes, and no codon in the row is left outside the assignment.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, at codon-label readback resolution.  That contact observes that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code.  It does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently the local codon tile may be used to describe a boundary pattern in the code table, and the module classification may be used to summarize the $13$ supplied boundary codons, but no higher-layer biological claim follows from this chapter.  A separate translation contact would be required for translation claims; a separate structural contact would be required for folding or structural-order claims; a separate physical or functional contact would be required for admissibility or function claims; and a cross-organism mechanistic contact would be required for universality claims.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite-row result is therefore a ready code-read claim.  In the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, the supplied boundary row $R$ has $13$ codons, punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, separated spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, low sampled null probabilities, strong leave-one-out recovery, and a complete six-module assignment with no missing, extra, or unassigned row codons.  The curated code table supplies the label bridge.  The claim stops exactly there: a local finite codon-tile classification at the code-read layer.
