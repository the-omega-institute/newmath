\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Module Classification in the Window-Six Codon Cube}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the sixty-four codons.  The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code table: it supplies a codon-to-label readback into amino-acid and stop labels, and it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Thus the finite object under test is a code-layer object.  The relevant exceptional row is the thirteen-codon subset
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$.  The same row is observed as a punctured-cube pattern with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The point of the chapter is not that biology must obey this geometry.  The point is that, once the curated label table is admitted as an external contact, the window-six coordinates expose a reproducible local boundary-tile split inside the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on $R$: the row size is $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing module codons, and unassigned codons are all empty.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has at least one predictable or boundary witness in the finite leave-one-codon analysis.  The singleton modules $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ and $\mathrm{ile\_met}$ show that the classification is not merely a partition into large codon families; the two-point modules $\mathrm{agr}$ and $\mathrm{ssn}$ and the three-point stop--tryptophan module record local label classes; the four-point $\mathrm{cun}$ module records a full local face.  Together these finite rows realize the advertised distinctions: a six-bit display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported claim is the following: window-six codon coordinates, combined with the curated standard-code table, expose a local boundary-tile structure in the code-read layer, and the thirteen codons of $R$ are completely classified into predictable-or-boundary modules.  The claim stops there.  No translation realization follows from this table.  No folded protein structure follows from an amino-acid label sequence.  No physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global law follows from the local tile.  Any move across those boundaries requires a separate reality contact with its own finite observations and its own null checks.
