\subsection{Leave-one-codon module classification in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record studied here is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with codons displayed as finite words over the RNA alphabet after the usual two-bit coordinate encoding of each base.  The observed label table is used as a curated contact for amino-acid and stop labels.  It is not used as evidence for translation dynamics, folded structure, biochemical admissibility, organismal function, a mechanism, or a universal law.  The question is narrower: when one codon-level contact is withheld, does the finite coordinate geometry still place the omitted codon in a predictable local class or on a detected boundary of the tile?

\paragraph{Residual row set.}
The finite row set singled out by the window-six computation has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
      &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},\\
|R| &= 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run identifies this set as a punctured-cube shaped residue, with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not an arbitrary list of exceptional codons.  It is a finite cell complex inside $Q_6$ with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces.  This is the finite geometry against which the leave-one-codon module classification is read.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The associated spectral computation gives the numerical scale of the tile rather than a biological mechanism.  The measured quantities are

This supports the internal consistency of the finite coordinate construction, but it does not turn the coordinate construction into a claim about physical translation or protein structure.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Two finite null checks constrain how much surprise is attached to the shape.  In the general shape comparison, $N=2000$ distinct samples were evaluated after $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class preserving null with $N=2000$, the observed comparison used $|M|=20$, found $0$ exact matches, and found $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The corresponding finite estimates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers say that the coordinate residue is a statistically concentrated finite pattern relative to the sampled nulls.  They do not say that the pattern is the cause of the genetic code or that it realizes protein function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code evidence.}
At the code-table level, the leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  A separate family stratification used $5$ families.  The family-level hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  These finite rows justify using the local tile as a code-read classifier across the sampled table variants, while keeping the evidence at the label-assignment layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon evidence.}
For the codon-level leave-one-out task, the run reports no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure codons, and $8$ predictable codons.  The recovery rate is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{recover}} = 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same value is reported for recovery above the threshold.  The absence of anchors is important: the classification is not obtained by declaring fixed singleton anchors and reading the answer back from them.  Instead, each omitted row is accounted for by predictable closure behavior or by a boundary position of the local tile.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module audit assigns all thirteen codons in $R$ and leaves no residue outside the listed modules.  The completeness row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{size}} &= 13,\\
\mathrm{assigned} &= 13,\\
\mathrm{unassigned} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{missing} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{extra} &= 0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every one of these modules has at least one predictable or boundary explanation in the leave-one-codon computation.  The classification is therefore complete at the finite-row level: the thirteen codons of the residue are partitioned into named local modules, and no codon in the residue is left without a module assignment.

\paragraph{Reality contact and exclusion rule.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It resolves code labels and local label splits; it does not observe ribosomal realization, molecular folding, thermodynamic admissibility, biological activity, or evolutionary universality.  Consequently, the finite result may be stated as follows.  In the standard-code label table, the window-six coordinate residue $R$ is a thirteen-codon punctured-cube tile whose leave-one-codon modules are completely classified by predictable or boundary behavior.  The result is a code-read statement about finite codon labels.  Any claim about translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate contact designed to observe that higher layer.
