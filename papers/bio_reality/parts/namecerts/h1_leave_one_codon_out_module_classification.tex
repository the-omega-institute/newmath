\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification in the codon cube}
\label{subsec:bioresality-codon-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed residue set.}
The finite object used here is the six-coordinate codon display of the standard genetic code, with each codon treated as a point of the codon cube and each curated amino-acid or stop assignment treated as a label contact.  The experiment isolates the residue set $R$ of codons participating in the local boundary-tile pattern.  The verified cardinality is $|R|=13$, and the observed list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured cubical object, this same set has verified face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The role of this finite carrier is deliberately narrow: it gives a local coordinate-and-label test at the code-read layer.  It does not by itself assert that biological translation is forced by the cube, that a protein structure is produced, or that any physical or functional mechanism has been obtained.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}
The spectral audit reports three scalar values attached to the same finite codon pattern: $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is within numerical tolerance for the recorded spectral-radius check.  Against the unconstrained sampled null model with $N=2000$ trials, the exact shape was observed once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was met in $25$ samples, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The distinct-sample process required $57377$ attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the small probability values should be read as properties of a finite sampled comparison, not as a universal biological law.

A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In that experiment $N=2000$, the observed module size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  The corresponding reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures strengthen the finite-row claim that the local codon pattern is not a routine outcome of the sampled class-preserving randomization.  They do not change the layer of the claim: the contact is still a curated codon-to-label table, not an observation of translation dynamics, folded structure, admissible chemistry, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and family-level recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ target entries.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside the enlarged region $M\setminus T$ plus its boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By code-family grouping, the verified family count is $5$.  The family hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  Thus the finite evidence says that the local region has strong readback performance across these curated code-family rows, with the alternative-yeast family providing the only listed family-level value below perfect recovery.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon recovery and module classification.}
The leave-one-codon-out run reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification then accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$ with no extras, no missing entries, and no unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{size}}&=13,\\
\mathrm{assigned}&=13,\\
\mathrm{extra}&=0,\\
\mathrm{missing}&=0,\\
\mathrm{unassigned}&=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\texttt{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\texttt{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has at least one predictable or boundary member in the finite audit.  This is the chapter-specific conclusion: window-six coordinates expose a local module partition of the standard-code label table, and the partition is complete on the $13$ codons selected by the boundary-tile test.

\paragraph{Scope of the biological reading.}
The biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is adequate for code-layer readback and for checking whether the finite local split recovers codon-label rows.  It is not a measurement of ribosomal translation, not a folding assay, not a thermodynamic admissibility test, not a functional screen, and not evidence that the same local tile is a global rule of biology.  In particular, reading an amino-acid label sequence from codons does not amount to observing a folded protein structure.  Any claim about translation realization, structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate empirical contact designed for that layer.  The present result should therefore be read as a finite, code-level classification result: the $13$-codon residue set is fully partitioned into biologically named modules, the leave-one analyses show high recovery against the recorded baselines, and the interpretation stops at curated label readback.
