\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification in the Window-Six Codon Cube}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.} This record asks whether the thirteen exceptional standard-code codons singled out by the window-six codon coordinate can be partitioned into biologically legible local modules after one codon is withheld. The object under inspection is only the standard-code codon-to-label table. The coordinate carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, and the readout is the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels. The data do not attempt to reconstruct ribosomal translation, molecular folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of code evolution, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Finite residue set.} The finite residue set has cardinality $13$. In the recorded standard-code readout it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube calculation returns the same thirteen codons, with face-vector data $(13,16,4)$. Thus the residue is not an informal list chosen after inspection; it is the finite codon set produced by the local cube calculation and then read against the curated label table.

\paragraph{Spectral calibration.} The associated spectral row records $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The reported cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the numerical row is internally stable at the recorded tolerance. These numbers are used only as a finite coordinate signature for the local tile; they are not treated as a physical energy, a kinetic parameter, or a structural observable.

\paragraph{Null checks.} Against $2000$ sampled alternatives in the shape null, the exact shape match count is $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. The high-overlap threshold gives $25$ matches with $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampling row also records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries under seed $42$. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module size is $20$, the exact match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$. Thus the recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row evidence therefore says that the local tile is uncommon under these two stated shuffling regimes. It does not say that the tile is necessary for life, optimal for translation, or explanatory of protein structure.

These rows support a code-label recovery pattern across the curated code-table collection, with one visibly weaker family row and four saturated family rows.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon behavior.} The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The recovery rate is also recorded as above threshold with value $1.0$. This means that, inside the finite codon-label table used here, every withheld codon in the residue set is recovered either through the predictable part of the local closure or through the boundary part of the tile. Since the anchor count is $0$, this is not an anchor-based reconstruction claim; it is a predictable-or-boundary classification claim.

\paragraph{Module partition.} The module-classification row assigns all thirteen residue codons and leaves no extras. The completeness counters are
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13,\\
\mathrm{assigned} &= 13,\\
\mathrm{extra} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{missing} &= 0,\\
\mathrm{unassigned} &= 0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The six named modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{CUN} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

Each module has at least one predictable or boundary member in the recorded leave-one-codon test. This is the specific content of the classification result: the thirteen-codon residue decomposes into six finite label-facing modules, and the leave-one-codon route does not strand any codon outside predictable-or-boundary status.

\paragraph{Reality contact and limit.} The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, at codon-label resolution. That contact can test whether a six-bit codon coordinate reads against the standard code as a local label split, and it can check the finite residue, module, and recovery counts listed above. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function, evolutionary mechanism, or universality. In particular, amino-acid label readback is not a protein-fold observation. A separate structural, biochemical, or organism-matched contact would be required before crossing from code-layer classification into any higher biological layer.

\paragraph{Conclusion.} The ready finite claim is that the window-six codon coordinate identifies a thirteen-codon residue in the standard-code table, that this residue has the recorded punctured-cube and spectral signatures, and that the leave-one-codon experiment partitions the whole residue into six predictable-or-boundary modules with no missing or extra codons. The claim remains deliberately local: it is a finite code-read classification of curated labels, not a statement about translation machinery, protein folding, admissible chemistry, biological function, mechanism, or a global law.
