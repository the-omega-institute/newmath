The reality contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label, and it supplies no observation of translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. \begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
The audited cardinality row gives $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube row gives the same thirteen codons with face data

Thus the object is not a vague codon neighborhood: it is a concrete thirteen-vertex local tile in $Q_6$, with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces in the finite cubical readback.

The shape test against $2000$ sampled alternatives produced one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five high-Jaccard matches, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling effort required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which is part of the finite audit record rather than an interpretive claim. These numbers justify treating the tile as a nonrandom code-layer pattern relative to the declared null rows, but they do not convert the tile into a biological law.

The row records $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and assigned count $13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons. \paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}

To cross any of those boundaries, a separate higher-layer contact would be required, and no such contact is present in this finite row.
