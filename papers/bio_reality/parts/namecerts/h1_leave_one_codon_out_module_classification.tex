The claim tested here is not that the biological code is forced by cube geometry. The claim is narrower: after a leave-one-codon-out probe, the exceptional codons in the local codon tile are exhausted by a finite module classification, and every module has either predictable support or boundary support inside the same code-read layer.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label readback and local split checks. It does not supply translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law of life. Amino-acid label sequences produced from the table remain code-layer labels. No folded structure follows from the local tile. No physical viability follows from the spectral row. No function follows from a hit rate. Any claim crossing from code readback to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact with its own finite observations. The present result is therefore a local BioReality record: in the window-six codon cube, the leave-one-codon-out exceptional row is classified by six finite modules, and the standard-code contact supports only the corresponding code-layer readback.
