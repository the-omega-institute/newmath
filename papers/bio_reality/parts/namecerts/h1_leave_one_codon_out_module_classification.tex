\paragraph{Carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as the ordinary codon display with three nucleotide positions and four letters per position.  The BioReality contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  This contact supports code-layer readback and local codon-label split tests.  It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that the code table is forced by the cube geometry.  The claim tested in this row is therefore deliberately local: window-six coordinates may expose a boundary-tile structure among codons whose labels are sensitive under leave-one-codon-out probing, while the labels themselves remain external reality contacts.

The row isolates a residual set $R$ of thirteen codons.  The verified list is

\begin{aligned}

No biological universality is read from this shape.  The interpretation is only that the residual codons occupy a small local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces inside the six-bit coordinate carrier.

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\

The completion audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Equivalently, the six displayed modules form a complete finite classification of $R$ in this row.  Every module has predictable-or-boundary status in the row sense, so the leave-one-codon-out classification is closed over the observed residual set.
