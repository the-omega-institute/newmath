\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification in the window-six codon cube}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read as a coordinate presentation of the sixty-four codon rows.  The tested distinction is not a mechanism of translation.  It is the finite question of whether the local window-six geometry separates a small boundary set of codons into reproducible modules when the curated standard-code labels are used as the external readback contact.  The reality bridge used here is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It supplies label readback; it does not supply ribosomal realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey this geometry as a law.

\paragraph{Boundary row set.}
The finite boundary row set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run verifies that this is the punctured-cube row set with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-read object is not described merely by a list of exceptional codons.  It has a finite local tile signature: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces inside the window-six cube.  The row set is therefore suitable for a local boundary test, while still being too weak to support any higher-layer biological conclusion by itself.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}
The associated spectral run reports $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the finite spectral computation closes to numerical tolerance at the reported precision.  The shape-significance null run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed $|M|=20$, zero exact matches, and seven samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The corresponding values are $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These numbers support the narrower claim that the observed local shape is not typical under the tested finite-row null models.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon recovery.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out probe reports no anchors, five boundary cases, eight high-coverage closure cases, eight predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These two probes are consistent with the same code-read interpretation: local window-six information and the curated labels recover many held-out label positions, but the recovery is still a statement about codon-label readback rather than translation or function.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification run assigns all thirteen boundary rows and no others.  It reports $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing rows from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The complete finite classification is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_LYS}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ILE\_MET}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{STOP\_TRP}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has either predictable status or boundary status in the leave-one-codon-out accounting.  The classification is therefore finite-row complete for this record: every codon in $R$ is assigned to exactly one named module, and no codon outside the supplied module list is introduced by the classification.

\paragraph{Family-level readback.}
The leave-one-code-out family summary separates the evaluated tables into five families.  The reported family hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial or plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  This family readback strengthens the local code-layer result because the same boundary geometry remains visible across the reported table families.  It still does not convert the tile into a global biological law.  The table families are contacts with curated codon labels, not contacts with translation dynamics, protein structure, or organism-level function.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded statement.  In the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, the standard-code label table exposes a thirteen-row local boundary tile with face vector $(13,16,4)$, stable module assignment, significant null-model separation, and strong leave-one-code and leave-one-codon recovery at the code-read layer.  The supported readback is a codon-label and local-tile readback.  The unsupported promotions are equally important: no translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this record unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.
