\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite claim tested here is that the window-six codon coordinate system separates the standard-code readback region into named local modules, and that every codon in the distinguished residual set belongs to one of those modules without remainder.  The biological object is only the curated standard-code codon table: a table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The mathematical carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through local tile faces, singleton label corners, three-point label classes, coordinate closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The claim is therefore a code-layer classification statement.  It is not a statement about translation as a biochemical process, not a folding statement, not a physical-admissibility statement, not a function statement, not a mechanism, and not a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Finite residual set.}
The residual codon set used by the module classification has cardinality $13$.  The verified membership list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row records $|R|=13$.  A separate punctured-cube check returns the identical membership list and the face-vector summary
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely counting a set of exceptional labels; it also records a finite local cubical shape with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional faces, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the induced codon-coordinate geometry.

\paragraph{Module assignment.}
The leave-one-codon-out module row assigns every codon of $R$ to one of six named modules.  The verified classification is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness check reports $R\_size=13$ and $assigned\_count=13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  In ordinary prose: the module partition covers exactly the distinguished thirteen-codon set and nothing outside that set is introduced by the reported module map.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The codon-deletion probe does not merely name the six modules.  It also checks whether the deleted codon can be recovered by predictable closure or by boundary membership.  The verified row reports $predictable\_count=8$, $boundary\_count=5$, $anchor\_count=0$, and $closure\_coverage\_high=8$.  The recovery rate is $1.0$, and the recorded threshold comparison is also $1.0$.  These numbers fit the module classification exactly: the thirteen codons are all accounted for by the predictable-or-boundary dichotomy, with eight codons landing in the stronger closure-coverage side and five codons retained as boundary codons.

The hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ with boundary extension is $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level readback row groups $23$ evaluated tables into $5$ families and records above-baseline hit rates by family:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other} &= 1.0,\\

$$
These rows support a narrow interpretation: the local coordinate tile gives a strong code-readback signal against the listed baseline, and the signal persists across the reported code-family grouping.  The numbers do not identify a biochemical implementation route.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape context.}
The same finite packet includes spectral data for the residual geometry.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic relation is numerically satisfied at $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, recorded both as the cubic-polynomial residual and as the radial-polynomial value.  The spectral-radius tolerance row repeats $0.6752479308830213$ for $\lambda_M$.  The shape-significance row uses $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It finds $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{exact} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{Jaccard\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row also uses $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It reports observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape $p$-value $0.0$, and lower-tail $p$-value $0.0035$.  These figures strengthen the finite code-layer reading: the observed coordinate shape is unusual under the supplied finite null procedures.  They still remain statistics on codon-label geometry, not observations of protein structure or cellular function.

\paragraph{Reality contact and local boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge: a standard-code table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splitting.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded form, or biological function.  Consequently the classification has a sharp local scope.  It says that the thirteen residual codons form a completely assigned six-module pattern in the window-six coordinate model, with all thirteen codons falling into predictable or boundary behavior under the leave-one-codon-out probe.  It does not say that DNA must obey this geometry as a law of nature, nor that an amino-acid label sequence determines a folded protein, nor that a CUN, UUR, AGR, stop, tryptophan, isoleucine, methionine, serine, or lysine label boundary supplies a mechanism by itself.  Any such promotion would require a separate contact of the appropriate kind.
