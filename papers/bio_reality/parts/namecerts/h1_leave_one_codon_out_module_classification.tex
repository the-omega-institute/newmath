\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record concerns a finite code-read claim on the six-bit codon display $Q_6$.  The biological object is not a mechanism of translation and not a protein-level object.  It is the curated standard-code label table, used only as a finite contact that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC-side carrier is the codon cube with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The readback layer is therefore code-level: codon labels may be compared with the standard-code table, but no folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality is licensed by this contact.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The verified finite-row content is a local codon-tile label split.  It says that the window-six coordinate system exposes a structured code-read row and that the curated standard-code table supplies the external labels used for readback.  It does not say that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological law.  It does not supply translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim about protein-level realization.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation unless a separate structure or function contact is added.  The correct conclusion is therefore narrow: within the finite standard-code contact, the $13$-codon row is completely classified into six predictable-or-boundary modules, with the recorded leave-one-code and leave-one-codon rates supporting the local code-read certificate.
