\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact discipline.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism.  It is a finite display in which codons are read as six coordinate bits, local tile faces are inspected, singleton label corners are distinguished from three-point label classes, and the final readback is the curated standard-code label table.  The contact used here is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It can test code-layer readback and local codon tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Consequently every numerical statement below is a finite-row statement about the code-read layer.
