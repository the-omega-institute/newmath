\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Coordinate object.}
The object examined here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with local observations restricted to tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The readout is not a biochemical mechanism; it is the finite assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels in the curated standard-code table.  In this coordinate description the distinguished residual set is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed cardinality is $|R|=13$.  The punctured-cube check returns the same list and has face-count vector $(13,16,4)$, so the residual rows are not merely thirteen unrelated codons: in the six-coordinate display they occupy a specific local cubical pattern.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The same finite object has a recorded spectral profile.  The two reported radii are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the auxiliary scalar is $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the numerical witness is at floating-point zero for the intended finite computation.  This is a shape-and-spectrum statement about the codon-label table.  It does not assert translation, folding, energetic admissibility, organismal function, a mechanism of origin, or a universal law.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the recorded null sample with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact residual shape occurred once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  High-overlap shapes with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sample required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which is relevant because the empirical baseline is being measured in a constrained finite space rather than by unrestricted independent labels.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  Thus the row supports a finite non-randomness claim about the local table geometry, while keeping the observation at the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The code-table perturbation row evaluates $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate after adjoining the boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the same leave-one-code-out calculation reports $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate above baseline $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial/plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have value $1.0$.  These numbers support the narrower statement that local table geometry is predictive for the curated code-label row under the specified omission experiment.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}
The codon-level omission row has no anchors, with anchor count $0$.  It has boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$, with the threshold comparison also $1.0$.  This separates two kinds of local support: some omitted codons are recovered by the predictable interior of the coordinate pattern, and the remaining cases are carried by the boundary class.  The computation therefore does not require promoting the local tile into a claim about molecular realization.  It says that within the finite codon-label table, every omitted member of the relevant residual pattern is accounted for by a predictable-or-boundary alternative.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the same thirteen-codon residual set.  The assigned count is $13$, the reported $R$-size is $13$, and there are no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either predictable support or boundary support in the leave-one-codon-out row.  The content of the claim is therefore exact and finite: the thirteen residual codons are partitioned into six named local modules, and the omission experiment supplies a predictable-or-boundary explanation for each module member.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, understood as a codon-to-label table.  It can test code readout and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test whether translation is realized in a cell, whether an amino-acid sequence folds, whether a fold is physically allowed, whether a molecule has function, whether a historical mechanism produced the code, or whether the pattern is biologically universal.  In particular, reading an amino-acid or stop label from a codon is not a folded-structure observation.  Any claim crossing from code labels to translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate contact of the appropriate kind.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
For the claim $h1.leave\_one\_codon\_out.module\_classification$, the finite evidence is internally complete at the code-read layer.  The residual set has size $13$, the punctured-cube face vector is $(13,16,4)$, the spectrum has the recorded radii $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, the shape tests give small empirical probabilities, the code-level omission rows recover almost all targets above baseline, and the codon-level module classification assigns all thirteen residual codons with no leftovers.  The warranted conclusion is a local boundary-tile classification of the standard-code label table, not a statement about protein structure or biological mechanism.
