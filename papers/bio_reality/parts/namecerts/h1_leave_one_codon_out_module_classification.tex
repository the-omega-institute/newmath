\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is the codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The internal BEDC-side structure used here is only coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data; the external contact is only the curated standard-code label table.  Thus the readback is a finite codon-to-label table, not a biochemical mechanism.

The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. This is the exact finite object classified by the leave-one-codon-out module test.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the thirteen-row support.  The completeness row reports $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the module cover, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\

\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\

$$
The classification statement is therefore exact: each codon in $R$ appears in exactly one listed module, and every listed module contains only codons from $R$.  Moreover, every module has at least one predictable or boundary member in the leave-one-codon-out run.  The point of the claim is not merely that $R$ has thirteen codons, but that the thirteen codons decompose into the six named local label modules without residue.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code-read and local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization.  Consequently, the finite-row verdict is deliberately local: window-six codon coordinates expose a boundary-tile classification for the standard-code label table, and the classification survives leave-one-codon-out checks on the supplied finite rows.

No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from this chapter.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any promotion across those boundaries would require a separate higher-layer contact, such as a translation experiment, structural assay, physical-admissibility model, or function measurement.  In the absence of such a contact, the result remains a code-layer finite classification: thirteen codons, six modules, complete assignment, punctured-cube face data, spectral tolerance, and empirical enrichment against the stated finite null models.
