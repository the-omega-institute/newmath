\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback layer.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with the standard-code label table as an external readback contact. The internal row is not a mechanism for translation; it is a finite coordinate object whose distinctions are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The observed boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a cubical subobject this row is a punctured local cube with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the row contains thirteen codon vertices, sixteen displayed one-dimensional adjacency faces, and four displayed two-dimensional tile faces. This is the finite object being classified; no claim is made here about full organismal use, translation machinery, protein realization, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

The Monte Carlo shape audit used $N=2000$ sampled code rows with seed $42$; it required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jac}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed $M$-row size is $20$; among $2000$ samples the exact-match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$, the exact-shape probability is $0.0$, and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$. These scalar diagnostics support the claim that the displayed finite row is not being recovered as a routine artifact of the tested null models.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact tests code-layer readback and the local codon tile label split. It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity. The correct conclusion is therefore narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile classification of the standard-code label table, and the leave-one-codon-out module row is complete for the thirteen codons of $R$. Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-level statement unless a separate structure, translation, physical, or function contact supplies the missing layer.
