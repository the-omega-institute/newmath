\paragraph{Finite object under test.}\label{par:bioreality-codon-module-finite-object}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with codons read as six-bit displays and with the curated standard-code table supplying the amino-acid or stop labels.  The relevant distinguished set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$\{AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}$.
As a subcomplex of the codon cube it is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  The row is therefore not a claim about all codons uniformly; it is a claim about a particular finite boundary-shaped subset of the code table and about how the labels on that subset behave under local omission tests.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}\label{par:bioreality-codon-module-controls}
The finite-row spectral check reports $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The same computation gives a radial cubic residual of $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, within numerical tolerance for the recorded spectrum.  The shape-control experiment used $N=2000$ null samples with seed $42$; it required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those samples there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape $p$-value $0.0$, and lower-tail $p$-value $0.0035$.  These controls do not turn the geometry into biology by themselves; they say that the observed finite codon pattern is uncommon under the stated finite randomizations.

The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. Thus the finite evidence is concentrated in local recoverability and boundary membership, not in a proposed biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Local interpretation and boundary.}\label{par:bioreality-codon-module-boundary}
The supported conclusion is narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code table: $R$ is a $13$-codon punctured-cube pattern, its spectral values are numerically stable in the recorded row, the null controls give small shape probabilities, and the leave-one-codon module classification assigns every $R$ codon to one of the six listed modules.  The result may be used as a finite code-read record for local codon-label structure.  It does not by itself establish translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a biochemical mechanism, or a universal law of genetic coding.  Any step from this code-layer pattern to translation, structure, physics, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate observational contact designed for that higher layer.
