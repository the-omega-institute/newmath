\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification on the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite external readback contact on the six-bit codon display $Q_6$.  The BEDC carrier is not asserted to be a biochemical mechanism; it is a coordinate object on which local tile, closure, spectrum, and relation probes can be evaluated before any higher biological interpretation is attempted.  The observed reality contact is the curated standard-code table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label.  This contact has resolution only at codon-label readback.  It can test code-layer label splits and local codon-tile structure, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the following finite codon set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the row has $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the local cube complex readback.  These numbers are finite-row data, not asymptotic estimates.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model calibration.}
The spectral row gives three scalar checks on the same codon-coordinate object.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the cubic constraint is numerically satisfied at the displayed tolerance.  The shape-significance null model used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It observed $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling log also records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed row size was $|M|=20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, and the reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These calibrations support the finite-row claim that the displayed coordinate shape is not typical under the tested randomizations.  They do not convert the coordinate shape into a law of molecular biology.

The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate, across $5$ reported families.

The leave-one-codon-out probe gives the local boundary accounting for this chapter.  It reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The above-threshold recovery rate is also $1.0$.  In this record, predictability therefore means recovery by the finite closure-or-boundary test in the code-read layer; it does not mean that the omitted codon is biologically forced by translation dynamics or protein function.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification partitions the $13$ codons of $R$ into six named modules.  The verified assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness counters are exact: $R$ size is $13$, assigned count is $13$, the extra-module-codon list is empty, the missing-from-modules list is empty, and the unassigned-codon list is empty.  Hence the finite claim is not merely that some modules contain predictable or boundary codons.  The sharper row-level statement is that every codon in the verified $R$ row is accounted for by the six-module classification, and every reported module has a predictable-or-boundary witness under the leave-one-codon-out probe.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported conclusion is a code-read conclusion: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern in the curated standard-code label table, with complete finite module accounting for the $13$ codons in $R$.  The chapter does not claim that a local codon tile is a global biological law.  It does not claim that window-six geometry alone supplies translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  It also does not claim that amino-acid label-sequence readback is folded protein structure.  Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate reality contact with the relevant resolution.  In the present record, the curated standard-code table is sufficient for codon-label readback and local split checks, and it is insufficient for every higher-layer promotion.
