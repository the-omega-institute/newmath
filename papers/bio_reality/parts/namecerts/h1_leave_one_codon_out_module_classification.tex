\subsection{Leave-one-codon module classification in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate setting.}
The record is formulated on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, where each codon is read as a coordinate point and the observed labels are the standard-code amino-acid or stop labels. The finite residue set singled out by the construction has size $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same set is registered as a punctured-cube pattern with face-count vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the chapter concerns a local codon-coordinate configuration, not a genome-wide or organism-wide principle. The contact used for labels is only the curated standard genetic-code table: it supplies the codon-to-label readout needed to say which corners carry amino-acid or stop labels, but it does not supply a translation event, a folded molecule, a physical admissibility assay, a biological function assay, a mechanism, or a universality theorem.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The finite geometry has a measured spectral profile with
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the radial polynomial residual, so the numerical spectral identity is satisfied to the stated tolerance. A random-label comparison with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$ found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and found $25$ matches at Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and produced $55377$ duplicate retries, so the rarity statement is tied to this concrete finite sampling run rather than to an asymptotic model.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null experiment preserved amino-acid class information. In that run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed comparison size was $20$. The exact-match count was $0$, so the reported exact-shape value was $p=0.0$ in the finite sample. The lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, giving a lower-tail value $p=0.0035$. This separates the local coordinate effect from a trivial relabeling explanation: the module is still unusual when the null keeps coarse amino-acid-class information, but that conclusion remains a code-layer statement about the finite table.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon behavior.}
Across the leave-one-code-out experiment, $23$ code tables and $63$ targets were evaluated. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The same value, $0.9841269841269841$, was obtained for the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ alone was $0.9365079365079365$. In the leave-one-codon-out experiment, the anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the predictable count was $8$, the number with high closure coverage was $8$, and the recovery rate was $1.0$. These counts say that the local window-six data strongly recover the held-out code-layer positions in this finite protocol, especially after boundary positions are allowed. They do not say that the codon cube performs translation or that the table alone determines biochemical realization.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The codons in $R$ are partitioned into six named modules, and every listed module contains a predictable or boundary contribution in the leave-one-codon-out analysis:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no elements missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons. The module account is therefore complete for this finite residue set. It also matches the biological scope of the contact: the curated table can identify codon labels and local label splits, while any claim about folding, physical viability, organism-specific translation machinery, biological role, or universal genetic-code mechanism would require an independent observation at that higher layer.

\paragraph{Family-level readout and local verdict.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out summary covers $5$ families. The above-baseline hit rates are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate. Taken together with the complete $13$-codon module assignment, the finite evidence supports the following bounded statement: window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile organization inside the standard-code label table, and the held-out codon behavior is highly recoverable within that code-layer protocol. The statement stops there. The amino-acid label sequence read from the table is not a folded protein structure, not a proof of translation realization, not a physical-admissibility certificate, not a function measurement, not a mechanism, and not a universal biological law.
