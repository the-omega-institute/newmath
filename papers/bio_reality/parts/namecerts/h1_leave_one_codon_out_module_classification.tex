\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification}
\label{sec:biorality-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The claim is a finite code-read claim about the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  Its carrier is the codon display obtained by treating each codon as a point in a finite window-six coordinate system, while its readback is the curated standard-code label table.  The distinguished residual set $R$ has thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is also a punctured-cube row: the recorded $f$-vector is $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the object under discussion is not an unconstrained list of codons, but a finite local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces inside the window-six display.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The leave-one-codon-out classification assigns every codon of $R$ to one of six named modules.  The complete assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{agr}}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},&
R_{\mathrm{ar\_lys}}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
R_{\mathrm{cun}}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},&
R_{\mathrm{ile\_met}}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
R_{\mathrm{ssn}}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},&
R_{\mathrm{stop\_trp}}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row is exact at the finite level: $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, the assigned count is $13$, the list of extra module codons is empty, the list missing from modules is empty, and the list of unassigned codons is empty.  In particular, this claim does not depend on an asymptotic limit or an unobserved completion.  It says that the thirteen observed residual codons are exactly exhausted by the six displayed modules.

\paragraph{Predictability and boundary status.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe reports zero anchor codons, five boundary codons, and eight codons with high closure coverage.  The predictable count is $8$, and the recovery rate is $1.0$, with the recovery-rate threshold also met at $1.0$.  The module-level assertion is correspondingly local: every module contains either predictable or boundary support.  The evidence is not that every codon is forced by a biological mechanism, but that each of the six finite modules touches the predictable-or-boundary layer in the window-six tile.

\paragraph{Code-table readback.}
The external contact is the curated standard-code table, used only as a code-read bridge from codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  In the leave-one-code-out row, $23$ tables were evaluated against $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit rate above baseline was $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate rows, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast row, across five recorded families.  These figures support a code-read alignment between the finite tile and curated label rows; they do not promote the tile to a translation model.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral checks.}
The shape row gives a finite null comparison with $N=2000$, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, of which $55377$ were duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p\_\mathrm{exact}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p\_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$: the exact-match count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-shape $p$-value was $0.0$, and the lower-tail $p$-value was $0.0035$.  The spectral row records
$$
\begin{aligned}

\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,\\
p_{\mathrm{radial}}(\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.

The near-zero radial-polynomial value is a numerical consistency check for the recorded spectrum, not an independent biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion is deliberately narrow.  Window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure for the standard-code label table, and the leave-one-codon-out row gives a complete six-module classification of the thirteen residual codons.  No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or law of DNA follows from these rows.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer statement.  Crossing from this local codon tile to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate reality contact that supplies that higher-layer evidence.
