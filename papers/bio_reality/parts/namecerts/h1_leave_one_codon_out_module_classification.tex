\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification on the codon cube}
\label{sec:bior-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the $64$ codons in the standard code.  The reality contact is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label, and it is used here as a code-layer readback device.  The contact does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  The tested object is therefore a finite row of code labels on $Q_6$, not a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{The residual row $R$.}
The finite residual set detected in the window-six coordinate is a $13$-codon row.  Its observed support is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The punctured-cube check returns the same codon list and the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not being inferred from a verbal description of the genetic code; it is a finite subset of the codon cube with a measured local combinatorial profile.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The associated finite operator diagnostics give
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic, or radial, polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is numerically zero at the tolerance of the run.  In the shape-significance control with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, only $1$ exact shape match is observed, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports $25$ high-Jaccard matches, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-row size is $20$, the exact shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The corresponding finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a local combinatorial anomaly relative to the sampled controls, but they do not promote the anomaly beyond the code-read layer.

The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level split has $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline are

The leave-one-codon-out probe reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and $8$ codons with high closure coverage.  Its recovery rate is $1.0$, and the thresholded recovery-rate check is also $1.0$.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification partitions the $13$ residual codons without omissions or extras.  The complete assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completion row records $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, with no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Moreover, every listed module has at least one codon classified as predictable or boundary-supported.  This is the finite-row content of the claim: the residual codon row is not only a $13$-point subset of $Q_6$, but also decomposes into six named local modules that account for the whole row.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The supported statement is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose labels can be read back against the curated standard-code table.  The evidence is finite: the $13$-codon row, the punctured-cube vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral values, the sampled null-model probabilities, the leave-one-code and leave-one-codon hit rates, and the complete module partition.  Nothing in this record establishes that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity.  Nothing here establishes translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global biological law.  Any such claim would require a separate higher-layer reality contact, not merely the codon-to-label table used in this chapter.
