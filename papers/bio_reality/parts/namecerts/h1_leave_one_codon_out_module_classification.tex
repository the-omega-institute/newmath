\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite labelled object on the six-coordinate codon cube.  The carrier is the set of all codon positions in the window-six display, while the tested subobject is the thirteen-codon row
$R = \{AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}$.
The verified cardinality calculation gives $|R|=13$, and the same row is identified as a punctured-cube pattern with face vector $(13,16,4)$.  This is a combinatorial statement about the finite display and its label readback.  It is not a statement that the display by itself realizes translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of code origin, or a universal law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Local module partition.}
The leave-one-codon-out classification assigns every codon in $R$ to one of six modules, with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The complete assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing elements, and no unassigned codons.  Thus the classification is closed at the row level: each codon in the thirteen-point punctured pattern is accounted for by exactly one named local module in the reported partition.

\paragraph{Predictable and boundary codons.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe separates the row into predictable positions and boundary positions.  The verified counts are $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, and $0$ anchor codons, with high closure coverage $8$ and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module audit sharpens that scalar result: every named module contains codons that are either predictable or boundary codons under the row-level test.  In other words, the recovery result is not concentrated in a single exceptional family; it is distributed across the six reported modules.  The conclusion remains local: the classification says that the finite code-label table has a recoverable row-module structure under this probe, not that the codon table has been derived from geometry alone.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The row sits inside a larger finite shape test.  The spectral audit reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the radial cubic residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check.  The residual is numerically negligible at the recorded tolerance, so the row and ambient shape pass the stated finite spectral consistency check.  The corresponding shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
 p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the finite claim that the observed labelled shape is unusual under the recorded sampling procedure.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null comparison.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$, the observed size was $|M|=20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}

 p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This comparison is stricter than a purely unlabelled row check because it retains class information while testing the shape outcome.  Its result supports the interpretation that the observed finite code-label arrangement is not easily reproduced by the recorded class-preserving randomization.  The explicit codon list for $M$ is not part of the supplied finite row, so the present chapter uses the verified size and probability values rather than reconstructing an unsupplied list.

This is a code-read result: it compares finite codon-label tables and their local recoverability properties.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only empirical contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, resolved at codon-label readback.  That contact supplies the assignments from codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It does not supply ribosomal dynamics, tRNA charging, translation kinetics, folded structure, energetic feasibility, cellular function, evolutionary inevitability, or universality.  Consequently, the row classification may be reported as a finite local boundary-tile fact about the standard-code label table, but it must not be promoted beyond the code-read layer unless an additional contact is supplied for the higher layer being claimed.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The verified finite data support the claim that the thirteen-codon row $R$ forms a complete six-module leave-one-codon-out classification in the six-coordinate codon display.  The classification covers all $13$ row codons, has no unassigned or extra module elements, and is consistent with the punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$.  The row participates in a broader finite signal: spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$ satisfy the recorded cubic check to residual about $6.2\times 10^{-12}$; randomization tests give exact-shape probability $0.0005$, high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$, and amino-acid-class preserving lower-tail probability $0.0035$; leave-one-code-out readback achieves hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ above baseline over $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The correct verdict is therefore a bounded code-layer result: local codon-tile geometry helps organize the curated label table, while translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, and universality remain outside the claim unless separately observed.
