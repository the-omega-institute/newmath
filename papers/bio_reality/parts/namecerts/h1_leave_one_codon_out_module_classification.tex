\subsection{Leave-one-codon module classification in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:bior-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate record.}
The object considered here is the standard-code codon table read through a six-bit codon display.  The codon cube supplies the finite carrier, while the biological label table supplies the amino-acid or stop labels against which local readback is checked.  The distinguished residual set has exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same thirteen codons form a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite geometric substrate for the local boundary-tile computation.  The observation is not that the codon table is forced by geometry; it is that, after the standard label table is admitted as a curated contact, the residual codons fall into a small and completely assigned collection of local modules.

$$
The radial cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is at numerical zero for the finite computation.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving comparison, the observed module size was $20$, with no exact shape match among $2000$ samples, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape value $0.0$, and lower-tail value $0.0035$.  These numbers support a finite local anomaly at the code-label layer, not a claim about biochemical realization.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon readback.}
The leave-one-code-out computation evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out computation reported no anchor codons, five boundary codons, eight high-coverage closures, eight predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the finite test is sharp in the following limited sense: when one codon label is withheld, the local tile-and-boundary data recovers the held-out code-layer placement for all targets in the recorded run.  This recovery is a readback fact about codon labels; it is not a statement about translation kinetics, molecular folding, physical admissibility, or organismal function.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the thirteen-codon residual set.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has either predictable or boundary support in the finite leave-one-codon analysis.  The accounting check gives $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Equivalently, the modules form an exact partition of the residual set used by the computation:
$$
\begin{aligned}
13 &= 2+1+4+1+2+3,\\
R &= \mathrm{AGR}\,\dot\cup\,\mathrm{AR\_Lys}\,\dot\cup\,\mathrm{CUN}\,\dot\cup\,\mathrm{Ile\_Met}\,\dot\cup\,\mathrm{SSN}\,\dot\cup\,\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-level leave-one-code-out run supplies an additional finite stratification: five families were evaluated, with hit-rate-above-baseline values $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  This stratification is consistent with the local module result, but it remains a code-table readback statement because its only admitted biological contact is the curated standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact used here observes that a curated standard genetic-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-layer readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal biological mechanism.  Consequently, the chapter records a finite module classification for a window-six codon representation of the standard-code table and stops there.  No protein structure follows from the amino-acid label sequence, no function follows from the local tile, and no global law of biology follows from the punctured-cube geometry unless a separate external contact supplies the missing higher-layer observation.
