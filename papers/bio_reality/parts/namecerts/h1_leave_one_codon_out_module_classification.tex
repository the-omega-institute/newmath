\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon label table.  The finite row under examination is not the whole table as a biological mechanism.  It is the local boundary row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$.  The observed punctured-cube row has $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the row is represented as thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four local faces inside the codon display.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose role is to assign codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact supports code-layer readback and local split checks; it does not by itself supply translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\begin{aligned}

Each displayed module has at least one predictable or boundary member, so the local finite claim is a classification theorem about the thirteen-row boundary object: the six modules cover the row exactly and do not leave a residual codon class.

\paragraph{Family-level stability.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out comparison grouped the alternatives into $5$ families.  The hit-rate-above-baseline values were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  These numbers support a finite-row stability statement: within the tested table family split, the local boundary row is usually recovered above the baseline rate.  They do not promote the row to a universal genetic-code principle, because the family list is a finite curated comparison and not a derivation of all possible codes.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The conclusion is therefore code-read only.  Window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in which the thirteen codons of $R$ are exactly classified into the six displayed modules, and the numerical probes show strong finite recovery under the supplied comparisons.  The curated table remains the contact that attaches labels to codons.  No claim follows here about translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Any such promotion would require a separate contact at the relevant layer, not merely the local codon-tile row and its standard-code label readback.
