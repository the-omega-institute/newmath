The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Thus the data support a code-level label readback, not a claim about translation chemistry, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The permitted conclusion is narrow. The same contact does not observe translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of code evolution, or a universal law across all organisms.  Any statement at those higher layers would require an additional contact specifically measuring that layer.  Within the present data, the claim is therefore: in the standard-code label table, the six-coordinate codon display exposes a complete residual module classification for the $13$ codons listed above, with the recorded spectral, null-model, and leave-one-out checks supporting that finite code-level pattern.
