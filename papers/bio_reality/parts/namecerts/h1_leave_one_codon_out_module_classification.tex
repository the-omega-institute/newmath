\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop, and the present packet asks what finite local tile structure is visible when that table is read only at the code-label layer.  The carrier is the $Q_6$ codon cube, and the distinguished local data are a tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  No part of the record treats the coordinate display as a biochemical law.  It is a finite readout comparison between codon coordinates and curated label assignments.

\paragraph{The punctured cube row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube audit reports the same row and the face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely counted; it is also recognized as a finite cubical pattern with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces.  The module classifier then partitions exactly this row into six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness row has $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Hence the finite module statement is closed at the level of row accounting: every codon in $R$ appears in exactly the reported module ledger.

\paragraph{Predictable-or-boundary status.}
The leave-one-codon-out run reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The packet-level classifier records that every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary representative.  In concrete terms, the single-codon lysine-side module $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ contributes $AAA$, the isoleucine-methionine module contributes $AUA$, the arginine module contributes $AGA$ and $AGG$, the CUN module contributes all four of $CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU$, the SSN row contributes $UCA$ and $UUA$, and the stop-tryptophan row contributes $UAA,UAG,UGA$.  The relevant assertion is therefore not that the codon table has been generated from first principles, but that this finite row admits a complete local classification once the standard-code labels are supplied.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported as the cubic-polynomial residual, so the numerical spectral check is at floating tolerance rather than at an exact symbolic identity claim.  The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null run also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It observed $M$-size $20$, exact match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, giving exact-shape probability $0.0$ and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These values support the claim that the reported local row is not a routine outcome of the sampled null procedures, while still leaving the interpretation at the finite code-label level.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out context.}
The related leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit rate above baseline was $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, across $5$ families.  These companion rows provide context for the leave-one-codon-out module result: the same codon-coordinate apparatus is detecting finite label-geometry regularities across several curated table comparisons, not just in a single hand-selected row.

\paragraph{Reality contact and local verdict.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table as a codon-label bridge.  It tests code readback and local codon-tile label splitting.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, organismal mechanism, or universality.  Consequently the local verdict is this: within the supplied finite rows, the $13$-codon punctured-cube row is completely assigned to six modules, every module has predictable-or-boundary support, the leave-one-codon-out recovery rate is $1.0$, and the companion null and spectral rows give nontrivial finite checks.  The result is a code-layer module-classification record for the standard-code label table.  Any claim about translation, folding, admissible chemistry, function, mechanism, or a universal biological principle would require an additional contact that observes that higher layer directly.
