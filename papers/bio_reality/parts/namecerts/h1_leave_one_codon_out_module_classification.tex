\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification in the window-six codon cube}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The row-level carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, and the biological readout attached to it is the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The finite set isolated by the local tile computation is not the whole table.  It is the thirteen-codon region
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality row reports $|R|=13$.  The same thirteen codons are certified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the claim is about a finite local region of codon-label data, not about the biochemical process by which any organism realizes translation.

\paragraph{Classification statement.}
The leave-one-codon-out module check partitions all thirteen codons of $R$ into six named modules, with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The verified classification is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, with empty lists for extra module codons, missing module codons, and unassigned codons.  Each module contains at least one codon marked predictable or boundary-visible by the leave-one-codon-out computation.  This is the finite content of the claim: the local tile does not merely detect a scattered set of individual codons; it organizes the entire thirteen-codon region into named codon modules while preserving complete accounting.

The codon-level leave-one-out run found no anchor codons, five boundary codons, and eight predictable codons.  It also recorded eight cases with high closure coverage and a recovery rate of $1.0$.  In scalar form, the codon-row summary is

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{predictable\_count}&=8,\\
\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high}&=8,\\

These figures explain why the module classification is stronger than a post hoc list: the finite row supplies a per-codon recovery test, and the resulting thirteen codons are exactly exhausted by the six modules above.

These numbers support a finite enrichment claim for this codon-coordinate construction, but they do not identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table: a table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It can test codon-label readback and local label splits.  It cannot test whether translation physically occurs, whether a peptide folds, whether a molecule is physically admissible, whether a biological function is realized, whether an organism uses a universal mechanism, or whether the local tile is a global biological law.  Therefore the verdict of this chapter is deliberately narrow.  The thirteen-codon region of $Q_6$ is completely classified into the six modules listed above, and its leave-one-out, spectral, and null-model rows support that local code-layer organization.  No conclusion about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate empirical contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
