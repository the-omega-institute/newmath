\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Question.}
The finite object studied here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon coordinate cube.  The question is whether the row $R$ singled out by the window-six construction is merely a loose collection of exceptional labels, or whether it decomposes into a small number of label modules whose members are all accounted for by the leave-one-codon-out recovery test.  The claim is deliberately local: the construction tests code-label readback and tile-boundary behavior inside the curated standard-code table.  It does not test translation as a biochemical process, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal law.

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The distinguished row has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported by the punctured-cube check, with face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row used by the module analysis is not selected after seeing the module labels; it is the same $13$-codon finite row already isolated by the coordinate-and-closure construction.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The associated spectral summary separates the row scale from the ambient $M$ scale.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic residual for the reported radial relation is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded as the radial polynomial residual.  At the precision of the finite computation, this is a numerical zero check rather than an additional biological assertion.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the reported randomization test with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the exact-shape count is $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ is $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite test is best read as a sharply constrained comparison inside a small discrete universe.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its recorded values are $p=0.0$ for exact shape and $p=0.0035$ for the lower tail.  These numbers support the claim that the observed finite shape is unusual under the stated null models, but they do not turn a local code-table pattern into a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
The code-level recovery experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the five reported groups have hit-rate-above-baseline values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports a code-read statement: the coordinate tile recovers most reported label changes relative to the stated baseline.  It is not evidence that the coordinate geometry itself performs translation or supplies organism-level function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The codon-level recovery row contains $8$ predictable cases and $5$ boundary cases.  The recorded anchor count is $0$, the high-closure-coverage count is $8$, and the recovery rate is $1.0$.  The threshold check is also $1.0$.  In this finite sense, every codon in the $13$-element row is either recovered by the predictable part of the tile computation or lies on the boundary part explicitly marked by the test.  This is the local content of the result.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The module classification partitions the row into six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA, UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA, UAG, UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification-completeness row records $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and assigned count $13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Hence the module table is exact at the finite-row level:
$$
\begin{aligned}
13\ \mathrm{row\ codons} = 13\ \mathrm{assigned\ module\ codons}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The substantive point is that the row is not only recovered as a set; it is exhausted by biologically legible code-label modules: AGR, AR-lysine, CUN, isoleucine-methionine, SSN, and stop-tryptophan.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact has the resolution of codon-label readback.  It can test the code-read layer and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test whether a ribosome realizes the pattern, whether a peptide folds, whether a molecule is physically admissible, whether a function is present, or whether the pattern is universal across biology.  The observed finite alignment therefore remains a code-table alignment.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite data support the following bounded conclusion.  In the six-bit codon coordinate cube, the row
$$
\begin{aligned}
\{AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}
\end{aligned}
$$
has a punctured-cube profile with $13$ vertices, $16$ edges, and $4$ two-faces; it has a recorded spectral summary with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$; it is statistically uncommon under the supplied finite null models; and it is completely exhausted by six leave-one-codon-out modules.  Every module has predictable-or-boundary support, and the module assignment has no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The result is ready as a local code-read claim.  It must not be promoted beyond that layer without a separate contact that directly observes translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.
