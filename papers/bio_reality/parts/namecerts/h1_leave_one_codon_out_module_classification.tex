\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Module Classification for the Window-Six Code Layer}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Claim and carrier.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer object over the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a molecule, a ribosome, or a folding system; it is the finite display in which a codon is represented by a six-bit coordinate and then read against a curated standard-code label table.  The tested claim is that the leave-one-codon-out remainder splits into named local modules, and that every codon in the residual row set is accounted for by a predictable or boundary class.  The biological contact used here is only the codon-to-label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, but it does not observe translation dynamics, protein structure, physical feasibility, organismal function, or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Residual row set.}
The residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The concrete rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row list is verified as a punctured-cube object with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the classification is not being asserted over an unspecified sample: it is a finite statement over these thirteen codons.  The local geometry is allowed to describe tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes inside this display, while the amino-acid names themselves remain supplied by the curated table.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The complete module assignment covers exactly the residual set.  The verification row reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing residual codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six named modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR/Lys} &= \{\texttt{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{SSN} &= \{\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UUA}\},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
Their sizes add to $2+1+4+1+2+3=13$, matching the residual-cardinality calculation.  In this sense the module classification is closed at the finite-row level: every codon that enters $R$ appears in exactly one displayed module, and the classification introduces no codon outside $R$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon recovery.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe reports no anchor rows, five boundary rows, eight rows with high closure coverage, and eight predictable rows.  Its recovery rate is $1.0$, and the recorded threshold comparison is also $1.0$.  These numbers support a local statement: within the supplied six-coordinate display and curated label table, the removed codon can be recovered through predictable or boundary behavior for the tested residual cases.  The result is therefore a code-read regularity, not an assertion that a biochemical process follows this geometry.

The finite evidence therefore combines a complete residual partition, a strong leave-one recovery statistic, a high leave-one-code hit rate above baseline, and null-model rarity estimates for the displayed geometry.

\paragraph{Scientific boundary.}
The valid conclusion is deliberately narrow.  The six-coordinate codon display, together with the curated standard-code table, exposes a local boundary-tile organization for the thirteen residual codons and classifies all residual rows into the six modules shown above.  It also supplies finite support from spectrum, null comparisons, and leave-one probes.  Nothing in this contact observes ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, thermodynamic admissibility, biological function, or a universal biological law.  Amino-acid label readback is a code-layer measurement only.  Any promotion beyond that layer requires a separate contact that directly measures the higher-level object being claimed.
