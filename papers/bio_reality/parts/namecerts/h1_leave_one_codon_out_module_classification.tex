\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table only as a code-layer contact. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code label table. A codon is therefore used here as a finite coordinate together with an externally supplied amino-acid or stop label. The available contact supports code-read comparisons, local tile boundaries, and label readback. It does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality. Those higher layers remain outside the claim unless a separate reality contact is supplied.

\paragraph{The exceptional row.}
The finite row singled out by the coordinate-and-label procedure has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object being classified is not an unconstrained list of codons: it is a finite local tile in $Q_6$ with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces. The code labels attached to those vertices come from the curated table, not from the cube geometry alone.

\paragraph{Spectral finite check.}
The row also carries a numerical spectral signature. The recorded values are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The radial polynomial check is numerically zero to tolerance:
$$
\begin{aligned}
P_{\mathrm{rad}}(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This check is used only as a finite-row diagnostic. It supports the existence of a reproducible coordinate signature for the observed local row, but it does not promote the row to a biological law.

\paragraph{Null-model placement.}
Against a shape null with $N=2000$ samples, the exact shape was matched once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. High-overlap matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving $p_{\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampling audit recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries under seed $42$. A second amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$ samples. It found $0$ exact-shape matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$-size of $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers are finite-row evidence for non-generic placement of the local tile under the tested nulls. They are not evidence for protein structure or biological function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit rate above baseline was $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast. The leave-one-codon-out probe recorded $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These measurements support a local predictive-and-boundary reading of the codon tile at the code layer.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the thirteen-codon row: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing module codons, and unassigned codons are all empty. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module contains codons classified as predictable or boundary-supported by the leave-one-codon-out analysis. The classification therefore partitions exactly the exceptional row $R$, rather than adding hidden codons or leaving part of the row outside the module account.

\paragraph{Claim boundary.}
The conclusion is a bounded code-read statement. Window-six coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code table, and the leave-one-codon-out module classification covers the thirteen-codon row exactly. The curated table supplies the label contact needed to read amino-acid and stop labels. Nothing in this row asserts that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as a biological necessity. Nothing here establishes translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any global law of biology. Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate contact whose observed data live at that layer.
