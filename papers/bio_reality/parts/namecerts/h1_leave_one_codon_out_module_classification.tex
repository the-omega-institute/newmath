\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite codon window.}
The codon display used here is the six-coordinate cube obtained by writing each three-letter RNA codon as a six-bit word.  The biological contact is not a mechanism of translation; it is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Within that restricted code-read layer, the finite row under study isolates a residual set $R$ of thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The direct cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The same row records that this set has the punctured-cube face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the object being classified is not an arbitrary list of exceptional codons but a finite local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the six-bit coordinate display.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape constraints.}
The companion spectral row supplies three scalar values for the local tile calculation: $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The recorded cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is numerically at tolerance level for the displayed finite computation.  The shape significance row compares the observed $M$-shape with $2000$ sampled alternatives using seed $42$.  It reports one exact shape match, twenty-five matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null row, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These numbers support the claim that the coordinate tile is a nontrivial code-layer regularity, while leaving open every biological interpretation above codon-label readback.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon tests.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row is narrower: it reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The recorded recovery rate above threshold is also $1.0$.  The code-family refinement has five families.  Its above-baseline hit rates are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast families.  The single weaker family value is still far above the global baseline row and is therefore compatible with the local tile reading.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The module-classification row assigns all thirteen codons in $R$ to six named local modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
AGR&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
AR_{\mathrm{Lys}}&=\{AAA\},\\
CUN&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
Ile/Met&=\{AUA\},\\
SSN&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
Stop/Trp&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness counters are exact: $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The module-level statement is therefore finite and checkable: every codon in the residual punctured-cube tile belongs to one of the six displayed modules, and every module has either predictable or boundary status in the leave-one-codon-out calculation.

\paragraph{Code-read interpretation.}
The result is a local classification of standard-code labels, not a claim about how cells implement translation.  The curated table supplies codon-to-label readback and nothing beyond that layer.  The six-bit geometry, the punctured-cube row, the spectral residual, the null-model probabilities, and the leave-one recovery rates jointly support a finite code-layer pattern: the residual codons split into six modules with complete coverage and no leftover entries.  They do not establish folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Any such stronger assertion would require a separate contact measuring that higher layer directly.  In particular, amino-acid labels read from the table are not protein structures, and a local codon tile is not evidence that DNA or translation is governed by this coordinate geometry as biological necessity.
