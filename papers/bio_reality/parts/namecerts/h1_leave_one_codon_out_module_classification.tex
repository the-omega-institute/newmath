\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
The record is a finite-row BioReality claim about the standard-code codon table read only at the code layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with distinctions given by a six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The external contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  This contact permits codon-label readback and local split checks, but it is not a translation experiment, a folding experiment, a physical-admissibility assay, a function assay, a mechanism proof, or a universality proof.

The finite exceptional row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube audit gives the face-vector row
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed set is not being treated as an unconstrained list of labels: it is read as a finite subconfiguration inside the codon cube.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion is a local finite-row code-read statement.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a boundary-tile organization in which the exceptional row $R$ is fully assigned to six modules, and the observed recovery statistics are strong against the supplied null models.  The curated standard-code table remains the only reality contact used here.  Consequently no claim is made that BEDC geometry is a global law of biology, that the local codon tile realizes translation, that amino-acid label readback determines folded structure, that the row proves physical admissibility or biological function, or that the pattern is a universal mechanism.  Any such higher-layer statement would require a separate contact outside this finite code-read record.
