\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read as the finite set of $64$ codon coordinates with labels supplied by a curated standard-code table.  The relevant distinguished residue is the thirteen-codon row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit gives $|R|=13$.  As a punctured-cube row it has face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not being treated as a narrative pattern but as a finite subcomplex with counted vertices, edges, and square faces.  The readback contact is the curated codon-to-label table; it supplies amino-acid and stop labels, but it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Spectral and null baselines.}
The row sits inside a measured codon-window geometry whose reported spectral values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the numerical spectral row is internally consistent at the stated tolerance.  Against the finite random-shape baseline with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the exact shape match count is $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling process used $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the quoted probabilities are finite-row empirical counts rather than closed-form biological laws.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite code-layer anomaly statement: the row geometry is difficult to reproduce under the audited nulls.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon readback.}
The leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the finite rates are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family, across $5$ families.  The leave-one-codon-out audit reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The row $R$ is partitioned into six named code modules, each of which is covered by the predictable-or-boundary criterion:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness audit reports $R\_size=13$ and $assigned\_count=13$, with no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons.  Equivalently, the module classification is a complete finite cover of the thirteen-codon row:
$$
\begin{aligned}
13=2+1+4+1+2+3.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the strongest local conclusion of the record.  Given the curated standard-code labels, the leave-one-codon procedure sorts all codons in the punctured-cube row into predictable-or-boundary modules without residue outside the module list.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The boundary condition is essential to the claim.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, or a universal mechanism.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  The curated table is a reality contact for labels only: it says which codons read as amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code, and it permits finite split checks over those labels.  It does not observe ribosomal realization, molecular folding, energetic admissibility, phenotype, or biological function.  Any such higher-layer statement would require a separate contact whose observable lies at that layer.

\paragraph{Claim.}
At the code-read layer, the finite-row data support the following bounded conclusion.  In the six-bit codon display, the thirteen-codon row $R$ forms a punctured-cube tile with measured face-vector $(13,16,4)$, non-null shape statistics, and stable spectral values.  The leave-one-code and leave-one-codon audits recover label behavior at high finite rates, and the module audit gives a complete partition of $R$ into the six predictable-or-boundary modules listed above.  The conclusion is therefore a local module-classification result for curated standard-code label readback, not a claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.
