\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification in the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a standard-code label table.  The contact used here is only the curated codon-to-label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it supplies no independent observation of translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The row therefore has a deliberately narrow scope.  It tests whether window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern in the code-read layer, while treating the labels themselves as external reality contacts.

\paragraph{Observed residual set.}
The distinguished residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row records that $R$ is a punctured-cube object with finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the classification problem is not over all $64$ codons in isolation; it is over a finite residual tile of $13$ codons inside $Q_6$, with $16$ one-dimensional incidences and $4$ two-dimensional faces retained by the punctured-cube readback.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectral probe gives the numerical invariants
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is also reported as the cubic-polynomial residual.  At the finite precision of the row, this is a near-zero consistency check for the spectral calculation rather than a biological mechanism claim.

\paragraph{Shape significance controls.}
Against the shape null with seed $42$, the run used $N=2000$ accepted samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape match count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The number of samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row claim of non-generic local shape, not a claim that the geometry is a law of biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, while the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The recorded hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family, across $5$ families.  This says that the local tile is informative for code-label recovery in this finite comparison regime; it does not say that code reassignment is caused by the tile.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, and $8$ predictable cases.  The recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the recovery-rate-above-threshold flag also equal to $1.0$.  Since no anchor codons are reported, the successful recovery should be read as a boundary-and-closure classification effect on the residual tile rather than as recovery from fixed biological anchor points.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the $13$ codons of $R$: the assigned count is $13$, the reported $R$-size is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons are all empty.  The six finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the row.  The classification therefore partitions exactly the observed residual codons, with no surplus codons introduced by the module names and no residual codon left outside the module list.

\paragraph{Claim boundary.}
The ready claim is a finite code-read claim: in the six-bit codon display, the residual punctured-cube set $R$ admits a complete leave-one-codon-out module classification, and every listed module is covered by predictable-or-boundary status.  The external standard-code table is required for label readback.  Nothing in this row establishes translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Any one of those higher-layer claims would require a separate contact that observes that layer directly.
