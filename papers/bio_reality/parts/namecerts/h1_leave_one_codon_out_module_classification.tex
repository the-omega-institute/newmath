\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact placed over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is a finite display in which codons are read as six coordinate bits and compared with curated amino-acid or stop labels.  The distinguished residual row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the row has thirteen codon vertices, sixteen local edges, and four local square faces in the induced finite tile.  The BEDC readback is therefore a finite local codon-tile statement: a singleton label corner and a three-point label class can be compared inside the six-coordinate display, but the label table itself remains an external contact.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the thirteen-codon residual row.  The assigned count is $13$, the residual-row size is $13$, and there are no extra module codons, no missing residual codons, and no unassigned codons.  The complete module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\

$$
Every listed module has at least one codon marked by the predictable-or-boundary condition.  The content of the classification is therefore exact finite coverage of the row $R$ by six named modules, not a claim that these module names are causal biological modules.  The names are code-read bins for the finite experiment.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table: a table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read statements and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary necessity.  The local tile may expose a boundary pattern in $Q_6$, and the leave-one probes may recover code labels at high rate, but no higher-layer claim follows unless a separate higher-layer contact is supplied.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, a codon-label table is not a mechanism of translation, and a punctured-cube row is not a universal biological law.
