\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification in the Window-Six Codon Cube}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite setting.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, and the tested readout is only the assignment of a codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The local row under test is not a claim about translation chemistry, folded protein geometry, physical admissibility, biological function, an evolutionary mechanism, or a universal biological law.  It is a finite code-layer statement: a window-six coordinate presentation isolates a distinguished thirteen-codon set $R$, and the leave-one-codon-out classification checks whether every codon in that set falls into one of the named local modules.

\paragraph{Distinguished codon set.}
The verified row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with cardinality
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate punctured-cube check gives the same thirteen codons and records the finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not using a continuous approximation or an unbounded search space: the support is the explicitly listed codon subset inside $Q_6$.

\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}

The completeness counters are
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{assigned}=13,\qquad
\mathrm{extra}=0,\qquad
\mathrm{missing}=0,\qquad
\mathrm{unassigned}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Consequently, the finite-row conclusion is exact: every codon in $R$ belongs to a declared module, and every declared module codon lies in $R$.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can check code-layer label readback and local codon-tile splits.  It cannot check translation realization, structural order, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Therefore the result may be stated as follows: in the finite window-six codon cube, the thirteen-codon set $R$ is completely partitioned by six named modules, and the leave-one-codon-out row reports that every module has predictable or boundary support.  No claim beyond code-label readback follows unless another independently specified contact supplies the missing layer.
