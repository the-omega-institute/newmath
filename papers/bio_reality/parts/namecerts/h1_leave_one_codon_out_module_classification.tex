\subsection{Leave-one-codon module classification for the window-six codon tile}
\label{subsec:biorality-leave-one-codon-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with the standard code-label readback as an external contact.  The finite row under test is not the whole genetic code as a mechanism, but the boundary set $R$ exposed by the window-six coordinate.  In the verified row, $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies this set as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the local tile is a finite combinatorial object: it has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional boundary incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the recorded tile complex.  This chapter records the module classification of that finite boundary object and does not promote it to a biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral and null context.}

\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with radial cubic residual $-6.224354365258478 \times 10^{-12}$, so the numerical spectrum satisfies its cubic constraint to computational tolerance.  Against $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$, the exact-shape count is $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$; the high-overlap count at Jaccard at least $0.9$ is $25$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$ out of $2000$, giving $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  No explicit codon list for $M$ is part of the supplied finite row; only its size and null statistics are used here.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon performance.}
The code-level leave-one experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The codon-level leave-one row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers support a code-read statement: the local tile is informative for codon-label recovery in this finite row.  They do not state that a codon is translated, that a protein is realized, or that any physical or functional constraint has been measured.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The module classification is complete for the thirteen elements of $R$.  The verified assignment has $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA, UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA, UAG, UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has at least one predictable-or-boundary witness in the leave-one-codon row.  The singleton modules $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ and $\mathrm{ile\_met}$ show that the classification is not merely a family-size partition; singleton label corners are retained as explicit boundary modules.  The four-codon $\mathrm{cun}$ module carries the largest local face contribution, while $\mathrm{agr}$, $\mathrm{ssn}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$ record smaller boundary classes that still participate in the same finite recovery test.

\paragraph{Family-level readback.}
The associated leave-one-code-by-family row contains $5$ families.  The family hit-rate-above-baseline values are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate.  This family split is consistent with the module row: the local tile is not a single accidental codon, but a boundary pattern that remains visible across the recorded family grouping.  The weaker alternative-yeast value marks the finite limit of the row rather than a failure of the module partition, since the module classification itself still assigns all thirteen $R$ codons without residue.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose role is to assign codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read claims and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity.  Therefore the verdict of this chapter is deliberately local: the window-six codon coordinate exposes a finite boundary-tile module classification for the standard-code label table, with complete assignment of the thirteen recorded boundary codons and strong finite-row recovery statistics.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
