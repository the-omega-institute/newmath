\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed contact.}\label{par:codon-module-carrier-contact}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a codon display with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological contact is not a mechanism: it is the curated standard-code table, used only as a finite readback assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC object therefore has four internal coordinates: coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation.  Its external contact has one permitted resolution, namely codon label readback.  The finite row for the relation component is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded cardinality is $|R|=13$.  The same finite row is also certified as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object being classified is neither the whole genetic code nor a biochemical process.  It is the thirteen-row boundary relation inside the window-six codon display, read against the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Module partition.}\label{par:codon-module-partition}
The leave-one-codon-out module classifier assigns all thirteen codons of $R$ to six named modules, with no missing rows and no extra rows.  The certified module table is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness row reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  In this precise finite sense, the module classification is total on $R$.  It does not say that these modules are biological universals.  It says that, after the window-six coordinate has isolated the thirteen-row boundary relation, the leave-one-codon-out classifier partitions exactly those thirteen rows into six finite modules.

\paragraph{Predictable-or-boundary coverage.}\label{par:codon-module-predictable-boundary}
The leave-one-codon-out probe records zero anchors, five boundary rows, eight high-closure coverage rows, and eight predictable rows.  The recovery rate is $1.0$, and the threshold comparison also records $1.0$.  The module-level statement is sharper than the scalar recovery rate: every module contains only rows that are classified as predictable or boundary rows by the finite probe.  Equivalently, for the six-module partition $\mathcal{P}$ displayed above, the audit row asserts
$$
\begin{aligned}
\forall P\in\mathcal{P},\quad P\subseteq R\quad\text{and every row of }P\text{ is predictable or boundary-classed.}
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a local code-read statement.  The singleton module $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ consists of $\mathrm{AAA}$; the singleton module $\mathrm{ile\_met}$ consists of $\mathrm{AUA}$; the two-row modules are $\mathrm{agr}$ and $\mathrm{ssn}$; the three-row stop--tryptophan module is $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$; and the four-row $\mathrm{cun}$ module is the largest block.  The finite result is not merely that many rows are recoverable.  It is that the recoverable-or-boundary condition is distributed across the whole six-block module table without leaving a residue.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling context.}\label{par:codon-module-spectral-context}

\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also reported as the cubic-polynomial residual, so the spectral consistency check is at numerical zero for the finite computation.  The shape-significance row used $N=2000$ distinct null samples after $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five high-Jaccard matches with threshold $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  A class-preserving amino-acid null model with $N=2000$ found zero exact matches for the observed $M$ shape and seven lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size.  The recorded observed $M$ size is $20$, with $p_{\mathrm{aa,class,exact}}=0.0$ and lower-tail value $0.0035$.  These numbers support the claim that the local window-six shape is not a generic accident under the sampled nulls, while still leaving the contact at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}\label{par:codon-module-cannot-claim-boundary}
The boundary is part of the claim.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, not folded structure, not physical admissibility, not biological function, not a mechanism, and not universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is also not folded protein structure.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments and supports local split checks, but it supplies no structure contact, no physical realization contact, and no functional assay.  Therefore the verdict of this finite row is deliberately narrow: the thirteen-row relation $R$ is completely assigned to six leave-one-codon-out modules, each module is covered by predictable-or-boundary rows, and the surrounding finite statistics make the local code-read pattern nontrivial under the recorded null samples.  Any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact and is not licensed by this chapter.
