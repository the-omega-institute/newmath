\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}

The observed boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check reports the same $13$ codons and gives the local face vector

$$
Thus the finite object is not a free verbal analogy to the genetic code.  It is a specified subset of the codon cube, with thirteen listed vertices, sixteen local edges, and four square faces in the recorded local tile.
