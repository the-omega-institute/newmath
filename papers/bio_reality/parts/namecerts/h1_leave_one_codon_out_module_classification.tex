\subsection{Leave-one-codon module classification for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-leave-one-codon-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The object examined here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display. The carrier is the finite codon cube $Q_6$, with local faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes used only as code-layer distinctions. The reality contact is the curated standard-code table: it supplies codon-to-label assignments, and nothing in the present record treats that table as an observation of translation dynamics, folding, physical viability, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Residual tile.}
The distinguished residual set $R$ contains exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$. The same finite set is detected as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the record is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it is a finite local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces after the missing cube part is accounted for.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Against the finite null model with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the exact shape count is $1$, so the exact-shape tail is $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. The high-overlap count with Jaccard threshold $0.9$ is $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampling trace records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which is relevant because the finite codon-label universe is small enough for repeated proposals to collide frequently. A second amino-acid-class-preserving null test, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reports observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape value $0.0$, and lower-tail value $0.0035$. These values support the statement that the observed local tile is unusually structured relative to the tested finite shuffles, while still remaining a code-read phenomenon.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code readback.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate in $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. The recorded hit rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the above-baseline rates are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family. These numbers say that the finite local classification has strong readback power for code labels under the tested leave-one-code protocol; they do not say that an organism realizes the geometry as a necessary biological rule.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon recovery.}
The leave-one-codon-out row has recovery rate $1.0$ and thresholded recovery $1.0$. It records $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, and $0$ anchor codons. The absence of anchors is important: the successful recovery is not being attributed to fixed singleton anchors in the data. It is attributed to the local closure and boundary behavior of the finite codon tile.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The module classification is complete. It assigns all thirteen residual codons, with $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no missing residual codons, and no unassigned codons. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
agr&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
ar\_lys&=\{AAA\},\\
cun&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
ile\_met&=\{AUA\},\\
ssn&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
stop\_trp&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module contains at least one codon marked predictable or boundary by the leave-one-codon-out run. The decomposition therefore supplies a finite row-level classification of the residual codons into biologically named code-label neighborhoods: arginine-family residuals, lysine-side residuals, the $CUN$ leucine block, the isoleucine-methionine corner, the $SSN$ pair, and the stop-tryptophan boundary.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The chapter claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile organization in the standard-code label table. The evidence is finite and explicit: a thirteen-codon residual set, a punctured-cube face-vector $(13,16,4)$, a near-zero cubic residual for the reported spectral values, significant finite null comparisons, high leave-one-code readback rates, full leave-one-codon recovery, and a complete six-module assignment of all residual codons. The empirical contact remains the curated code table, so the conclusion is restricted to codon-label readback. No claim about translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these rows unless a separate contact directly supplies that higher-layer observation.
