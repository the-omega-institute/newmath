\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite question.}
The packet tests a code-layer claim about the standard genetic code after one restricts attention to the thirteen codons in the distinguished residue set $R$.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube, with codons displayed as finite words over the four nucleotide labels.  The question is not whether the cube explains translation, protein structure, biochemical feasibility, cellular function, or a universal biological mechanism.  The only question asked here is whether the finite code-label table admits a complete module assignment in which each observed module is accounted for by the leave-one-codon-out prediction rule or by the local boundary class supplied by the same code-level analysis.

\paragraph{Observed residue set.}
The verified set $R$ has size $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check reports the same thirteen codons and the face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row being classified is not an open-ended biological sample.  It is a finite combinatorial object: thirteen named codons, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional tile faces inside the codon coordinate display.

\paragraph{Module assignment.}
The module-classification run assigns all thirteen codons and leaves no residue outside the declared modules.  Its completeness record is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|&=13,\\
\mathrm{assigned}&=13,\\
\mathrm{extra\ module\ codons}&=0,\\
\mathrm{missing\ from\ modules}&=0,\\
\mathrm{unassigned\ codons}&=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The complete module table is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each listed module contains only codons from $R$, and the union of the six rows is $R$ itself.  The run therefore supports the finite classification statement: every residue codon belongs to a named module whose members are covered by either the predictable class or the boundary class.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out run gives $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and $8$ high-closure-coverage codons.  The recovery rate is $1.0$, with the threshold comparison also recorded as $1.0$.  At the code-table level this means the thirteen-codon residue does not fragment into unexplained singletons under deletion testing.  The split is instead a full accounting by two local statuses: prediction inside the coordinate closure and boundary membership at the tile edge.  This is the only recovery interpretation used here.

\paragraph{Cross-code pressure.}
A separate leave-one-code-out screen evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary class was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  These finite rates support the use of the local module boundary as a code-read phenomenon, while still leaving organismal implementation and biological causation outside the present contact.

\paragraph{Shape and spectrum controls.}
The shape-control run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It observed $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The distinct-sample counter recorded $57377$ attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, observed no exact shape matches, so $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$, and placed $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size $20$, giving $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$.  The spectral row records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with radial cubic residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  These numbers do not upgrade the claim to a mechanism.  They only make the finite coordinate row non-vacuous: the observed residue has a reproducible local shape, a controlled null comparison, and a numerical spectral signature attached to the same finite codon set.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used in this chapter is the curated standard-code table, which supplies codon-to-label readback at code resolution.  It permits statements about which codons carry amino-acid or stop labels in the standard table and whether those labels fall into the finite modules above.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, protein folding, structural order, thermodynamic admissibility, biological activity, evolutionary necessity, or universality across all living systems.  Consequently the verified statement is local: in the standard-code label table, the thirteen-codon residue admits a complete six-module classification, and every module is covered by the predictable-or-boundary dichotomy.  Any claim about translation, folded structure, physical realization, function, mechanism, or global biological law would require an additional contact of the appropriate kind.
