\subsection{Leave-one-codon module classification}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The claim concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon cube.  The relevant finite residue is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The same finite row identifies this set as a punctured cubical residue with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the object being classified is not an unrestricted list of exceptional codons but a fixed local tile in the six-bit display.  Its biological readout is only the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact supplies labels for comparison; it does not supply a mechanism of translation, a structure observation, a physical-admissibility test, a functional assay, or a universal biological principle.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model calibration.}
The finite residue is accompanied by a numerical spectrum for the observed codon geometry.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check has residual $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is numerically zero at the recorded tolerance.  A random-shape calibration with $N=2000$ trials and seed $42$ found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and found $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jac}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling log records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported significance is attached to an explicitly finite sampling process rather than to an asymptotic assertion.

A second calibration preserves amino-acid class information.  In that row the observed comparison size is $20$, with $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  The exact-shape count is $0$, so the recorded exact-shape probability is $0.0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These two null probes support the claim that the local codon tile is unusually organized relative to the tested finite controls.  They do not convert the codon table into a structural or functional model of proteins.

\paragraph{Leave-one evidence.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
whereas the recorded rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level table has $5$ families.  Its above-baseline rates are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  At the single-codon level, the leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchor codons, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the row supports a classification by predictability or boundary membership, not by biochemical realization.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The classification row assigns all thirteen codons of $R$ to six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, with no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Equivalently, the module map is a complete finite cover of the thirteen-codon residue:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R&=\mathrm{agr}\cup\mathrm{ar\_lys}\cup\mathrm{cun}\cup\mathrm{ile\_met}\cup\mathrm{ssn}\cup\mathrm{stop\_trp},\\
13&=2+1+4+1+2+3.
\end{aligned}
$$
The separate Boolean row states that every module has at least one predictable or boundary member.  This is the precise content of the module-classification claim: the local residue decomposes into six named codon modules, every codon in the residue is assigned exactly within that finite table, and the leave-one-codon evidence places each module on the predictable-or-boundary side of the local test.

\paragraph{Scope of the contact.}
The only reality contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table.  It is adequate for checking codon-label readback and local label splitting in the six-coordinate codon display.  It is not an observation of ribosomal translation dynamics, folded protein geometry, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, or a universal mechanism.  Therefore the verified conclusion is deliberately narrow: the standard-code table contains a thirteen-codon local residue whose six-module partition is complete and whose modules are covered by the recorded predictable-or-boundary classification.  Any claim about translation, folding, physical possibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require an additional empirical contact of the relevant kind.
