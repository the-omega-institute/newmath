\subsection{Leave-One-Codon Module Classification in the Six-Bit Codon Cube}\label{sec:biorality-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record considered here is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  Each codon is represented as a point of the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with the ordinary curated codon-to-label table used as the observed label contact.  The coordinate construction distinguishes the six-bit codon display, local tile faces in the cube, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The point is not to replace the biological table by geometry, but to ask whether the finite coordinate table exposes a repeatable local boundary pattern when one codon, or one code-table entry, is held out.

\paragraph{Observed exceptional row.}
The finite row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified codons in $R$ are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same computation identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube row with face data $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the row is not merely a list of codons with changed labels; it has a finite cubical signature: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four local two-dimensional faces after the relevant puncture.  The complementary measured region $M$ is reported with observed size $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, but the finite input supplied here does not include the individual codons of $M$ as a list.

\paragraph{Spectral check.}
The same finite coordinate packet gives the spectral values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, also reported as the cubic-polynomial residual.  This is numerically at tolerance scale for the finite computation, so the spectral datum is treated as a row-level measurement of the coordinate construction rather than as an independent biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Against $2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$, the shape-significance run observed one exact shape match, giving $p=0.0005$.  It also observed $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$.  The sampling process needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported probabilities should be read as finite Monte Carlo table values for a constrained discrete search.  A second null model preserving amino-acid classes evaluated $2000$ samples with seed $42$, obtained $0$ exact matches, and found $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size $20$.  Its exact-shape value is $0.0$ at the displayed resolution, and its lower-tail value is $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out measurement evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out run reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This is the specific empirical content of the chapter: in the finite standard-code table, the coordinate row is highly recoverable under the stated holdout probes.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module-classification run assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ to named local modules.  The classes are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness counters are exact: $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, with no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has at least one predictable-or-boundary witness in the run.  The family-level leave-one-code-out summary further separates five families: alternative yeast has hit rate above baseline $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate above baseline $1.0$.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
These finite rows support a narrow claim.  Window-six codon coordinates detect a local code-table boundary pattern in the curated standard-code table, and the detected row admits a complete six-module classification.  The evidence is strongest at the code-read level: the label table supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop assignments, and the coordinate probes test whether those labels align with local cubical structure and holdout recovery.  No step in this chapter observes translation as a molecular process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law governing all organisms.  Any such claim would require a separate contact, with its own observed data, beyond the curated codon-label table used here.
