\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
The experiment is a code-layer readout over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is treated as a finite coordinate display, and the only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code assignment from codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The carrier is therefore not a molecular process, a ribosomal model, or a protein-structure model.  It is a finite table together with coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data that can be compared against the standard-code label table.

The distinguished row set recovered by the finite computation has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row set is reported as a punctured-cube object with finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the concrete object being classified.  It is not an inferred law of genetic coding; it is a finite codon subset whose geometry is checked against the curated code labels.

These values support the narrower claim that the row geometry is not a generic artifact of the tested finite shuffles.  They do not identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Local scope of the biological contact.}
The contact used in this chapter is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supports codon-label readback and local split checks, including the singleton label-corner and three-point label-class distinctions visible in the window-six display.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any necessity claim about DNA.  A readback from codon labels to amino-acid symbols remains a code-layer observation.  A further claim about proteins, folding, admissible chemistry, organismal function, or universality would require an additional contact that is not present in these finite rows.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded statement.  In the six-bit codon cube, the thirteen-codon row set $R$ forms a punctured-cube pattern with face vector $(13,16,4)$, satisfies the recorded spectral check to numerical tolerance, is rare under the tested null models, and admits a complete six-module classification in which every module contains a predictable or boundary codon.  The result is a local code-read classification of the standard-code table.  It is not a claim that the same geometry generates translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.
