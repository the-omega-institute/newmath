\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification for the codon tile}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.} This record treats the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through codon labels supplied by a curated standard-code table. The finite row under inspection is the thirteen-codon region
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row count is $|R|=13$. Its punctured-cube summary has $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the observed region is not merely a loose list of codons: it carries a local tile face pattern with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-step edges, and four square faces. The readback remains a label-table readback. The labels are external contacts from the curated code table; they are not generated by the cube itself.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.} The finite spectral summary gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The reported cubic, or radial, polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically at tolerance scale for the stated calculation. Against the shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The sample construction required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite row is being compared against a constrained discrete sample space rather than against a continuous approximation. Against the amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. Thus the reported exact-shape value is $0.0$, and the lower-tail value is $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon readback.} The leave-one-code-out finite row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, across $5$ families. The leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These numbers support a local classification statement: the thirteen codons in $R$ are recovered by predictable or boundary behavior in the code-read probe, but the row does not by itself identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Module partition.} The module classification is complete on the thirteen-codon row. It assigns all $13$ codons, has no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The six reported modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has at least predictable or boundary support in the leave-one-codon-out classification. The singleton modules $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ and $\mathrm{ile\_met}$ mark label-corner behavior, while $\mathrm{agr}$, $\mathrm{cun}$, $\mathrm{ssn}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$ record multi-codon local label classes. The stop-tryptophan module is a three-point label class in the row, and the four-point $\mathrm{cun}$ module occupies the largest local block among the listed modules. The classification therefore reads the punctured-cube row as a finite boundary tile whose internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data tied back to the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.} The ready claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose a reproducible local boundary-tile structure for this finite code-read row. The evidence is numerical and finite: $|R|=13$, $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, the spectrum has $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, the exact shape null gives $p=0.0005$, the amino-acid-class-preserving lower-tail value is $0.0035$, leave-one-code-out recovery is $0.9841269841269841$ above baseline, and the module classification assigns all thirteen codons without residue. This is a code-layer result about local codon-label readback. It does not claim translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, universality, or a global biological law. Any such higher-layer claim would require a separate contact that observes that layer directly.
