\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a claim about translation mechanics.  It is a display in which each codon is read as a six-coordinate row, the curated table supplies the external amino-acid or stop label, and the BEDC-side structure asks whether the distinguished codons form a local tile with a stable boundary.  The observed distinguished row set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified row count is $|R|=13$.  The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with face data $(13,16,4)$: thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four two-faces in the finite tile.  Thus the chapter-specific object is the local code-read pattern $R\subset Q_6$, together with the curated standard-code label table as the only reality contact.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}
The finite spectrum attached to this row reports $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic polynomial check, so the spectral readback is numerically at the stated tolerance scale.  Under the shape-significance null, $N=2000$ samples were evaluated after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only one exact shape match was observed, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$; twenty-five samples had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed $M$-row size is $20$; among $N=2000$ samples there were $0$ exact matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size, giving $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These numbers support a finite local-tile anomaly at the code-read layer; they do not by themselves supply a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and family readback.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary rows is $0.9841269841269841$.  The family stratification has five families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$ above baseline, and bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  The row therefore behaves as a stable code-read classifier across the tested finite family split, while still depending on the curated label table for what the codon labels are.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon module classification.}
The leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Its module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing module codons, and unassigned codons are all empty.  The six finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module contains only predictable or boundary codons in the verified classification.  This is the exact finite-row content of the claim: the thirteen-row local tile is exhausted by the six named modules, and no codon in $R$ is left outside the module partition.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The chapter does not assert that the local tile is a global biological law.  The curated standard-code table supports code-layer label readback and local split checks only.  No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from the six-bit window geometry unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not a protein-structure observation.  The ready claim is therefore narrow: within the finite standard-code contact, the codon-window carrier exposes a thirteen-row punctured-cube tile whose leave-one-codon modules are completely classified and whose tested null behavior is non-generic at the reported finite sample scales.
