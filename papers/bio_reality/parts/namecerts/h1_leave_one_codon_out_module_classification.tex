\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
The claim concerns the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ equipped with a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological readback is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and that table is used only as a code-layer contact.  In this row the distinguished set $R$ has exactly thirteen codons,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same thirteen codons are reported by the punctured-cube check, with face data summarized by the $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object under discussion is not an unconstrained biological mechanism.  It is a finite code-read object: a subset of the codon cube, a local closure pattern, a spectral profile, and a label readback supplied by an external curated table.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model row.}
The associated spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic check is numerically closed at the recorded tolerance, with polynomial residual

$$
The observed $M$-shape is statistically non-generic under the recorded finite sampling protocol.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and $25$ samples had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the small empirical tail is attached to a visibly constrained finite sampler.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  The corresponding reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0,\qquad
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row distinction at the code-read level; they do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out modules.}
The module classification row assigns every codon in $R$ to one of six named modules, with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The recorded completeness check is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13,\qquad \mathrm{assigned}=13,\qquad
\mathrm{missing}=\varnothing,\qquad
\mathrm{extra}=\varnothing,\qquad
\mathrm{unassigned}=\varnothing.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

The classification statement is therefore finite and checkable: each listed module has at least one predictable-or-boundary representative, and the union of the displayed modules is exactly the thirteen-codon $R$ row.  This is the chapter-specific content of the leave-one-codon-out module claim.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label contact only.  It does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any necessity that DNA must obey this geometry.  The local tile can support a code-read statement and a local label-split check, but it cannot be promoted beyond that layer without a separate contact designed for the higher layer.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a statistically sharp codon-cube row is not a proof of biochemical function.  The ready claim is therefore this: within the six-bit codon carrier and the curated standard-code label bridge, the thirteen-codon $R$ row has a complete leave-one-codon-out module classification, non-generic finite-shape statistics, recorded spectral closure data, and high code-layer recovery rates, while all translation, folding, physical-admissibility, function, mechanism, and universality claims remain blocked.
