\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification in the codon cube}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, used as a finite display for codon labels. The finite exceptional row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the row is not being treated as an open-ended biological principle; it is a finite codon-label configuration with thirteen named codons, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four local faces in the displayed cube geometry. The readout contact is the curated standard-code table, which supplies amino-acid or stop labels for codons. That contact gives the label table needed for the computation, but it does not by itself observe translation dynamics, molecular structure, physical feasibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The leave-one-codon-out module classification assigns every codon in $R$ to one of six named modules. The complete assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, with no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. In the finite row, therefore, the classification is exhaustive and disjoint at the level relevant to the reported packet: each listed exceptional codon is accounted for by a module, and the module names do not import additional codons outside the row.

\paragraph{Predictable-or-boundary condition.}
The module-level statement tested here is not that each codon is mechanistically explained by cube geometry. It is the narrower claim that each module contains codons falling under the predictable-or-boundary status supplied by the leave-one-codon-out computation. The row-level leave-one-codon-out data report $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchors, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These numbers match the size of $R$: the thirteen-codon exceptional row splits into eight predictable cases and five boundary cases, with no codon left outside the stated local analysis. The module table above is therefore a finite classification of the same thirteen codons, not a separate biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape context.}
The cube display also comes with a spectral row. The finite spectral quantities are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The reported cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the numerical witness is within the stated tolerance for the computation. Shape significance was checked against $2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$: exact shape matches occurred once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $2000$ samples and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, exact matches are $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, and the corresponding lower-tail value is $0.0035$. These values make the finite local pattern unusual under the stated null procedures, while still leaving the biological interpretation bounded by the available contact.

\paragraph{Code-table recovery evidence.}
The leave-one-code-out computation evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the reported hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial or plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate codes, across five families. These figures support a code-layer readback claim: the local codon display is informative for the curated label table and for the reported leave-one-out recovery tasks.

\paragraph{Interpretive boundary.}
The scientific content of this packet is deliberately local. The finite row says that the thirteen exceptional codons form a punctured-cube-shaped row, that all thirteen are placed into six modules, and that the leave-one-codon-out analysis supplies predictable-or-boundary status across the row. The contact says that the labels being read are curated standard-code labels. No result in this packet shows that the geometry is translation itself, that a peptide folds, that a physical state is admissible, that a biological function is realized, that a mechanism is established, or that the pattern is universal. Crossing any of those layers requires an additional contact of the appropriate kind, not merely the codon-label table used here.
