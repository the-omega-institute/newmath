\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record works in the finite codon carrier obtained by displaying each codon as a six-bit coordinate in the codon cube $Q_6$. The biological readback is deliberately narrow: the only external contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The finite BEDC side supplies the coordinate display, closure operation, spectrum, and relation data; the biological side supplies only the label table. Thus the object under test is not translation as a process, not protein folding, not physical admissibility, and not biological function. It is the code-layer question of whether a local window-six tile and its boundary rows organize the standard-code label table in a reproducible finite pattern.

\paragraph{Boundary row set.}
The distinguished boundary row set $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified row list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row set is verified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
In this chapter, the phrase local tile means exactly this finite coordinate-and-label situation: a row subset in $Q_6$, its faces, and the label readback induced by the curated code table. It does not mean a global biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row check.}
The spectral row data give three scalar checks. The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also reported as the cubic-polynomial residual. This places the finite computation at numerical tolerance rather than at a symbolic exactness claim. The local interpretation is that the window-six row geometry has a stable spectral signature, not that the signature causes or explains the standard genetic code.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ null samples, seed $42$, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
It found $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$, seed $42$, observed $|M|=20$, found $0$ exact matches, and had $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size. The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures support a finite-row anomaly relative to the tested nulls. They do not by themselves identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out and family checks.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. Inside $M\setminus T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary it was $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the five recorded classes have hit rates above baseline
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The leave-one-codon-out run has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The recovery rate above threshold is also $1.0$. The finite reading is therefore that every tested missing codon is recovered as predictable or boundary-covered in the coordinate construction.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification partitions the $13$ boundary codons without omissions or extras. The verified module rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness audit reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons. Thus the module statement is a finite classification theorem for the displayed row set: each boundary row belongs to exactly one of the named modules, and each module contains at least one codon recorded as predictable or boundary-supported by the leave-one-codon-out probe.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The supported claim is only the code-read claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a local tile-boundary organization in the standard-code label table, and the leave-one-codon-out module classification covers all $13$ rows of $R$. The unsupported claims are equally part of the result. No translation realization follows from these rows. No folded structure follows from amino-acid label readback. No physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity claim follows from the local codon tile. Any step across those boundaries requires a separate reality contact with its own finite observations and its own null tests.
