\subsection{Leave-One-Codon-Out Module Classification in the Codon Cube}\label{sec:bior-eality-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The record is a code-read statement about the standard-code codon table, represented on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The finite carrier used here is the row set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed cardinality is $|R|=13$, and the same thirteen codons are reported by the punctured-cube check.  The associated face data have $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the local object is not a free verbal grouping of codons but a finite tile in $Q_6$ with thirteen displayed vertices, sixteen displayed one-faces, and four displayed two-faces.  The external contact is only the curated standard genetic-code bridge: it supplies codon-to-label readback for the standard table.  It does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal law.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The row geometry has a stable numerical signature in the finite experiment.  The reported spectral values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with radial cubic residual $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check.  Thus the computation is internally consistent at the numerical tolerance recorded for the run.  Against $N=2000$ random samples in the shape-significance test, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$, reported observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0$ and lower-tail value $0.0035$.  These numbers support a finite-row code-read anomaly, not an unbounded biological necessity.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out module partition.}
The module-classification run assigns all thirteen row codons and reports no missing, extra, or unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{assigned}&=13,\
\mathrm{missing}&=\varnothing,\
\mathrm{extra}&=\varnothing,\
\mathrm{unassigned}&=\varnothing.

$$
The six module rows are

Each listed module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the leave-one-codon-out classification.  The aggregate leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  In this precise sense, every member of the finite row is accounted for by the local tile computation: none of the thirteen codons remains outside the module partition, and the partition is exhausted by predictable or boundary behavior.

\paragraph{Code-table transfer checks.}
The corresponding leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  Grouped by code family, the reported above-baseline rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family, across $5$ families.  These transfer numbers are consistent with the interpretation that the local six-bit tile carries code-layer information about label splits.  They do not convert the tile into a biological law.

\paragraph{Readback boundary.}
The claim proved by this finite row is deliberately narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure on $Q_6$, and the curated standard-code table lets that tile be read back as codon labels.  The contacts in this record can test only code readback and local codon-tile label splitting.  They cannot test translation realization, folded-protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is still only a label sequence at this layer.  No folded structure follows from the module table, no protein function follows from the recovery rate, and no universal mechanism follows from the punctured-cube face vector.  Any later claim crossing one of those boundaries requires a separate reality contact with the relevant observable, not merely the present codon-label table.
