\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary row.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read only as a coordinate cube for codon labels. The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is detected as a punctured-cube finite complex with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a code-read datum: the codons are vertices in the window-six coordinate display, and the labels attached to them come from the curated standard-code contact. No claim about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality is made from this carrier alone.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model calibration.}
The row is not used merely as a named subset. Its finite geometry is calibrated by a spectrum in which
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic, equivalently radial, polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the displayed spectral value is within the numerical tolerance used by the run. Against the first randomization basis, $N=2000$ samples required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries; the exact shape appeared once, giving $p=0.0005$, while the high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving $p=0.0125$. Against the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$, the observed module-side size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, giving lower-tail probability $0.0035$. These numbers support a finite coordinate anomaly at the code-label layer; they do not supply a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification partitions all $13$ codons of $R$ with no leftovers. The six recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\

\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness row states
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|&=13,\\
\mathrm{assigned\_count}&=13,\\
\mathrm{extra\_module\_codons}&=\varnothing,\\
\mathrm{missing\_from\_modules}&=\varnothing,\\
\mathrm{unassigned\_codons}&=\varnothing.

$$
Every module therefore has at least one predictable or boundary codon in the leave-one-codon-out readback. The classification is a finite certificate that the row-level codon split is module-complete relative to the given six module names. It is not a certificate of amino-acid chemistry, protein folding, organismal function, or a universal genetic-code law.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of the claim.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, interpreted as a codon-to-label bridge. It can test code-read and local codon-tile label split statements, because those statements require only the codon labels supplied by the table. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey this window-six geometry as a biological necessity. The finite-row conclusion is therefore: within $Q_6$, the standard-code label table exposes a locally coherent punctured-cube boundary row $R$ whose $13$ codons are completely classified into six leave-one-codon modules, with recovery and null-model statistics as listed above. Any promotion beyond that code-read layer requires a separate higher-layer contact.
