\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
The object of this record is the standard-code codon table read through a six-bit codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, and the external contact is only the curated codon-to-label table.  Thus the row data are allowed to test a code-layer readback and a local codon-tile split, but they are not allowed to promote the split to translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The observed exceptional row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality audit gives $|R|=13$.  The same row is also recorded as a punctured-cube boundary object with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the finite evidence is not merely a list of labels: it has a small local cubical boundary shape inside $Q_6$.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape evidence.}
The spectral probe assigns the row two internal scales, $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, with radial parameter $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the reported polynomial check is at numerical tolerance rather than an exact symbolic identity.  The shape-significance audit used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which is part of the finite-row context: the null exploration is combinatorially constrained rather than uniformly effortless.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed module size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$, giving lower-tail probability $0.0035$.

They do not supply a biochemical realization claim.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the thirteen-codon row.  Its audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has at least one predictable or boundary witness.  The classification therefore partitions the finite row into local label modules without leaving a residue.  The singleton module $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{AAA\}$ records a singleton label corner; $\mathrm{cun}$ records a four-codon local face; $\mathrm{agr}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}$, $\mathrm{ssn}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$ record smaller label classes sitting on the same thirteen-row boundary.  The biological contact remains the standard-code label table: it tells us which codon labels are present, not why a ribosome translates, why a peptide folds, or why any realized molecule is physically admissible or functional.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite-row verdict is that the standard-code table contains a reproducible local codon-tile boundary in the six-bit display.  The row has $13$ codons, the punctured-cube face-vector is $(13,16,4)$, the module classification assigns all $13$ codons, and the strongest leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes reach recovery or boundary-augmented hit rates near $1.0$.  This is a code-read claim and a local-tile claim only.  No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, universal genetic-code mechanism, or necessity of BEDC geometry for DNA follows from these rows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that information.
