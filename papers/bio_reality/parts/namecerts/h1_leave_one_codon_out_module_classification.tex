\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite coordinate display for the $64$ standard-code codons.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label.  Thus the contact tests code-layer label readback and local codon-tile splitting, not translation as a biochemical process.  The BEDC-side distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal structure retained by the finite row is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the readback is only the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Recovered relation row.}
The finite relation row $R$ has exactly thirteen codons.  In the observed row,
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
|R|&=13,\\
f(R)&=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The equality $|R|=13$ and the same thirteen codons are independently recorded in the cardinality and punctured-cube rows.  The punctured-cube row also records the face-vector signature $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, so the observed object is not merely a list of exceptional labels; it is a finite subshape of the codon cube with a specific vertex-edge-face count.  The row is still local: it records a boundary tile inside $Q_6$, not a law of biology.

\paragraph{Null comparison rows.}
Two finite null comparisons restrict the amount of freedom in the pattern.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ samples, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null used the same sample count $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support the claim that the observed local tile is not easily reproduced by the sampled null procedures.  They do not, by themselves, identify an evolutionary cause or a biochemical necessity.

Since the union of the displayed modules is exactly the displayed thirteen-codon relation row, the finite-row statement is closed at the code-read layer: the module classifier accounts for every codon in $R$ and introduces no codon outside $R$.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The claim is a finite code-read claim about a local codon tile in $Q_6$.  It says that the curated standard-code labels, when read through the six-bit coordinate display, expose a thirteen-codon relation row with a punctured-cube face vector, a numerical spectral signature, significant sampled-null behavior, high leave-one-code-out recovery, and a complete six-module leave-one-codon-out classification.  It does not say that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not claim translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  No such higher-layer conclusion follows unless a separate reality contact supplies it.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation; it is not a protein-structure observation.
