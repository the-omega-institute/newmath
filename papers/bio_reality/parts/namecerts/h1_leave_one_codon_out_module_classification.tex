\subsection{Leave-one-codon-out module classification in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-codon-out-module-classification}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The finite carrier used here is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read as the $64$ codon positions of the standard genetic code after a fixed binary coordinate presentation.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label readback, where the labels are amino-acid names or stop.  This contact tests only the code-read layer and local codon-tile label splits.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is verified as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object under discussion is not an open-ended biological mechanism.  It is a finite row inside a coordinate cube, together with a standard-code label readback supplied by an external table.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The leave-one-codon-out module classification assigns all $13$ members of $R$ and leaves no residue.  The completeness audit reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, $extra\_module\_codons=\emptyset$, $missing\_from\_modules=\emptyset$, and $unassigned\_codons=\emptyset$.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR/Lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile/Met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop/Trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module contains at least one codon classified by the leave-one-codon-out probe as predictable or boundary-supported.  The point of the classification is therefore local and finite: the row decomposes into recognizable codon-label modules without requiring a claim that the geometry is itself a biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out recovery.}
The codon-level leave-one-out probe reports $predictable\_count=8$, $boundary\_count=5$, $anchor\_count=0$, and $closure\_coverage\_high=8$.  Since the row has $13$ codons, the reported recovery rate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{8+5}{13}=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite-row sense in which every codon in the row is accounted for by either prediction or boundary behavior.  The result is not a statement that the codon will be translated in vivo, nor that an amino-acid sequence will fold, nor that any protein function follows.  It says only that, after one codon is withheld, the remaining window-six coordinate and label information place the omitted codon into a predictable or boundary class inside this finite row.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral checks.}
The row sits inside a stronger finite pattern.  The shape-significance audit used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, giving $p\_exact=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p\_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null model also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$; it found $0$ exact matches, $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size, $observed\_M\_size=20$, $p\_value\_aa\_class\_exact\_shape=0.0$, and $p\_value\_aa\_class\_lower\_tail=0.0035$.

The associated spectral row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral radius in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers are finite internal checks on the coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation package.  They are not a substitute for external biological contacts beyond the codon-label table.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The chapter-specific verdict is that the standard-code codon table supplies a real but narrow contact: it supports codon-label readback and local module classification for the $13$-codon row $R$.  The strongest finite-row statement is that the module partition is complete and that every module has predictable or boundary support under the leave-one-codon-out probe.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
