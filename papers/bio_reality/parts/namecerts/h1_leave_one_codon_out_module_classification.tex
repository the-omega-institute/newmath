\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns a finite window-six codon display.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code label table as an external reality contact.  The distinguished residual row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the finite evidence is not merely a list of exceptional labels: it has a concrete local tile shape, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces inside the codon-coordinate display.

The row also carries a numerical spectral signature.  The reported values are

This supports using the row as a stable coordinate object in the code-read layer, but it does not by itself identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the supplied null ensemble with seed $42$, the $M$-shape experiment used $N=2000$ samples.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and twenty-five matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The same run reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers make the local row shape a finite statistical anomaly relative to the tested shuffles.  They do not make it a universal law of biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
The companion leave-one-code-out table experiment evaluated $23$ genetic-code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The same value, $0.9841269841269841$, is reported as the hit rate above baseline.  By family, there are $5$ families: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$.  This is evidence for a robust code-read recurrence across the finite table family that was tested.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
For the codon-level deletion test, the recovery rate is $1.0$.  The finite row has $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, and $8$ high-closure-coverage codons.  Thus every recovered codon in this probe is accounted for either by prediction or by boundary placement, rather than by an independent anchor class.  This is the local-tile content of the result: the row is readable as a boundary-sensitive finite module in the standard-code label contact.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification is complete on the thirteen-codon row.  It assigns all $13$ elements of $R$, with no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each listed module has predictable or boundary status.  This is the precise finite-row claim: the residual punctured-cube row decomposes into six label modules whose union is $R$, and the decomposition leaves no codon outside the classification.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supplies codon-to-label readback: codons are assigned to amino-acid labels or stop labels.  Therefore the result is a code-layer statement about a six-bit coordinate display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The data support the statement that window-six codon coordinates expose local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code readback.

\paragraph{Boundary.}
No stronger biological claim follows from these rows.  A local codon tile is not translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a global law of biology.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded structure.  Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate higher-layer reality contact, such as a translation experiment, a structural assay, a physical admissibility model tied to observation, or a function-level measurement.  In the present record, the verdict is restricted to finite code-read geometry and its tested module classification.
