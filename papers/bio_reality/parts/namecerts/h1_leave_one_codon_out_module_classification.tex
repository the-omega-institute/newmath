The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read only through a curated standard-code table.  The BioReality contact is therefore a code-layer contact: it supplies codon-to-label readback, and it does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. $$

This is a local tile statement about the code table.  It is not a claim that biological DNA must obey this geometry as a law, and it is not a claim that amino-acid label readback determines protein structure.

These numbers support the claim that the observed local row is not a typical draw from the tested finite nulls.  They do not promote the result beyond the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The support condition is local and finite: the curated standard-code table supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  The break condition is any promotion from this local tile to translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  No such promotion is made here.  A higher-layer statement would require a separate contact, such as a translation experiment, a structure experiment, a physical admissibility model with observed constraints, or a function-level assay.  In the absence of such a contact, the BioReality verdict is confined to the code-read layer: window-six coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile module structure in the standard-code label table, with the concrete row counts, spectra, null-model probabilities, hit rates, and module assignments displayed above.
