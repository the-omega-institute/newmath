\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Code Family}\label{sec:biorality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read object on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The BEDC display supplies a coordinate carrier, closure operation, spectral summary, and finite relation, while the amino-acid and stop labels are not generated internally by the cube.  They enter through the curated standard-code table, a reality contact that supports codon-label readback and local split checks only.  The local distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The contact therefore tests whether the BEDC tile boundary tracks curated label changes, but it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The finite boundary relation $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
   &UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check returns the same set and the finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row being tested is not an unrestricted collection of codons: it is a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces.  This is the local geometric object against which code-table label differences are read.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The same finite carrier has a spectral summary with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial polynomial check gives

\begin{aligned}
P(\mu,\lambda_M,\lambda_R)=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},

$$
reported both as the cubic-polynomial residual and the radial-polynomial value.  The spectral-radius tolerance row records $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$.  These numbers are used only as finite-row diagnostics for the code-read geometry; they are not evidence for a biochemical force law or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The observed $M$ object has size $20$.  Against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$, the exact shape occurred once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-Jaccard threshold row counted $25$ cases with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling log reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model with the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, the exact-shape $p$-value was $0.0$, and the lower-tail $p$-value was $0.0035$.  The finite evidence therefore says that the observed local shape is uncommon under the sampled nulls, including the amino-acid-class-preserving null, but this remains a code-layer statement.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The aggregate leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-resolved row partitions the tested tables into $5$ families.  Their above-baseline hit rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus four of the five reported families reached perfect above-baseline hit rate on this row, while the alternative-yeast family remained high but not perfect at $5/6=0.8333333333333334$.  The claim h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family is therefore a family-stability claim about code-label readback: when one code table is held out, the local $Q_6$ tile and boundary structure recover the relevant label split far above the baseline frequency across the listed family classes.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The codon-level leave-one-out row is consistent with the code-table row.  It records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$, with the recovery-rate-above-threshold flag also equal to $1.0$.  The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the missing, extra, and unassigned lists are all empty.  The six named modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness.  This gives a finite explanation of where the thirteen boundary codons sit in the local tile: the boundary is not merely a bag of exceptions, but is exhausted by six named modules that cover all thirteen codons.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The supported conclusion is narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table, and that pattern remains strong in leave-one-code-out tests when grouped by code family.  The curated table supplies the labels; BEDC supplies the coordinate, closure, relation, and spectral readback.  The numbers support a local code-read regularity: $|R|=13$, $(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4)$, $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, aggregate above-baseline hit rate $0.9841269841269841$, and family hit rates ranging from $0.8333333333333334$ to $1.0$.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
No stronger biological claim follows from this row.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of code evolution, or a universality theorem.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any claim crossing from code labels into translation realization, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact beyond the curated standard-code table.
