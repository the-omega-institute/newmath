\paragraph{Carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code label table. The coordinate layer records codons as finite words over the four-letter codon alphabet, while the contact layer supplies amino-acid or stop labels from the standard code. This contact is a code-read bridge only: it says which codon label is curated in the table, but it does not by itself supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality. The local tile considered here is therefore a boundary object inside code-read evidence. Its admissible readback is a codon-to-label split, not a claim about proteins or organismal function.

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The discovered $M$-shape has observed size $20$. Against $2000$ finite samples with seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap check counted $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling audit also records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the comparison is a finite-row comparison under a constrained sampling regime, not an asymptotic theorem. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, while the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$. Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the finite code-read distinction as a non-generic row-level pattern under the tested nulls. They do not promote the pattern beyond the table contact.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The claim supported by this finite row is narrow. Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code label table, and the family-stratified leave-one-code-out probe reports above-baseline recovery in all five listed families. The numerical record is strongest at the code-read layer: $R$ has size $13$, the punctured-cube $f$-vector is $(13,16,4)$, the row has a stable spectral readback, the tested null models give small finite $p$-values, and the leave-one-code-out probes recover the held-out labels at rates far above the baseline. Nothing in this chapter asserts that the local tile is a global biological law. Nothing here asserts translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Such claims would require separate contacts outside the curated codon-label table.
