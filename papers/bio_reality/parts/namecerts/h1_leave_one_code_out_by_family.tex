\paragraph{Finite-row object.} This record concerns the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ with standard-code label readback.  The finite carrier is the codon display in which a curated table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The internal BEDC-side distinctions used here are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the external contact is only the standard-code label table.  The row under audit is the family-resolved leave-one-code-out test for the claim identifier $h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family$.  The test is not a claim about translation machinery, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  It is a code-read claim: when one genetic-code table is withheld, the local window-six tile and its boundary statistics are evaluated against the withheld codon-label changes, and the result is grouped by code family.

\paragraph{Boundary set.} The distinguished finite boundary relation $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The complete row list is

\begin{aligned}

The same row is reported as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. It is a finite boundary tile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional faces, and $4$ two-dimensional faces.  The observed module partition accounts for all $13$ codons with no missing, extra, or unassigned rows: $AGR=\{AGA,AGG\}$, $ar\_lys=\{AAA\}$, $CUN=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, $ile\_met=\{AUA\}$, $SSN=\{UCA,UUA\}$, and $stop\_trp=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$. \paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.} The finite spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The shape significance control used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The resulting finite-row estimates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{exact}&=0.0005,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$ observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, giving $p=0.0$ for exact-shape recovery and $p=0.0035$ for the lower-tail event.  The supplied data give the size of $M$ and the boundary relation $R$ explicitly; they do not supply a full codon-by-codon list for $M$ in this row, so the chapter does not pretend that such a list has been observed here.

The appropriate reading is stability of the local boundary-tile probe across named code families, not a proof that the codon cube enforces the biological code.

\paragraph{Codon-withholding consistency.} The leave-one-codon-out companion row gives recovery rate $1.0$ and recovery-rate-above-threshold $1.0$.  It also reports $predictable\_count=8$, $closure\_coverage\_high=8$, $boundary\_count=5$, and $anchor\_count=0$.  This is consistent with the module classification: every reported module contains codons classified as predictable or boundary-covered, and the complete module assignment has no residue outside the six listed modules.  The code-read content is therefore finite and local: the tile can mark a family-sensitive boundary pattern and can recover withheld rows under the stated tests, but all label meanings still enter through the curated code table contact.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.} The contact named here observes only that a curated standard genetic-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It can test code readback and a local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or the claim that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Therefore the finite verdict of this row is: the family-resolved leave-one-code-out data support a code-layer boundary probe on the six-bit codon display, with strongest reported family rates at $1.0$ and the weakest reported family rate at $0.8333333333333334$, while every higher-layer biological interpretation remains blocked unless a separate reality contact supplies it.
