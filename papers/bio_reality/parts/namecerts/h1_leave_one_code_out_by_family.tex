\subsection{Leave-one-code-out stability by code family}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-layer contact.}
The BioReality record is a finite code-read test over the six-bit codon display $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a claim about a biological mechanism; it is a finite coordinate chart in which each codon is read as a point of the codon cube, and the only external contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The intended readback is therefore exactly code-layer label readback.  The contact can test a local codon-tile label split, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any necessity claim about DNA obeying the coordinate geometry.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the row is
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same row and the face-vector data
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
This is the finite tile on which the leave-one-code-out family test is interpreted.  The row is concrete and label-readable, but it remains a local boundary object.  The existence of this row does not by itself supply translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The finite coordinate probe reports spectral values
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}$$
The corresponding radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is within the numerical tolerance reported for the row-level spectrum.  Against the shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the exact shape count is $1$, hence $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ is $25$, hence $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling audit also records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite-row comparison should be read as a constrained combinatorial probe rather than as a continuous asymptotic estimate.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a sharper code-label control.  With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  Thus
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
This says that the observed finite coordinate shape is not reproduced by the sampled class-preserving controls in this run.  It does not say that the coordinate geometry is a biological law; it says only that the label-preserving finite-row contact has a nontrivial local shape signal under the stated null.

The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The boundary row therefore carries nearly all of the aggregate above-baseline recovery in this finite readback test.

The leave-one-codon-out control reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module accounting covers all $13$ codons of $R$ with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are

The module classifier records $R$ size $13$ and assigned count $13$.  Every listed module has predictable or boundary support in the finite-row bookkeeping.  This supplies the internal audit that the family-level signal is not being obtained by silently dropping row codons.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready claim is narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose leave-one-code-out behavior is stable by family in the finite rows supplied above.  The curated standard-code table supports codon-to-label readback, and the derived probe supports only local split checks at the code-read layer.  No translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, global mechanism, or universal biological law follows from these data.  Any such higher-layer claim would require a separate reality contact beyond the curated code table and beyond the finite coordinate rows used here.
