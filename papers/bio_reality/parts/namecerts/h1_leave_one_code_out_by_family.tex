\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Genetic-Code Family}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record asks whether the window-six codon coordinate packet keeps predictive contact with curated codon labels when whole alternative-code rows are withheld and then grouped by genetic-code family. The object under test is not a biochemical mechanism. It is a finite code-layer readback problem: codons are represented in a six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, local tile information is computed inside that finite display, and the only allowed reality contact is the curated table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The tested layer is therefore $\mathrm{code\_read}$, not translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, cellular function, or universality.

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The family-level run has identifier $7\mathrm{dda}978\mathrm{c}6\mathrm{c}97$ and evaluates $5$ families. The measured hit rates above the baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus four of the five recorded families reach perfect above-baseline recovery, while the alternative-yeast family remains above the comparison level but does not reach $1.0$. The aggregate leave-one-code-out run supplies the surrounding scale: $23$ tables are evaluated, with $63$ total targets, baseline hit rate $0.19821739130434785$, hit rate in $M-T$ equal to $0.9365079365079365$, and hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary equal to $0.9841269841269841$. The corresponding hit rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$. These numbers justify treating the family stratification as a stability check on the same code-layer phenomenon, rather than as a separate mechanistic claim.

\paragraph{Codon subset.}
The finite subset $R$ has cardinality $13$. In the recorded order it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\\
&\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check reports the same codon list and the face-count vector $(13,16,4)$. This matters because the by-family statistic is read against a fixed finite geometric carrier: the comparison is not free-form label matching, but label recovery constrained by the same window-six subset and its local boundary structure.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification assigns all $13$ elements of $R$ and leaves no residual codons. The recorded modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

The completeness row is $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. Every module is recorded as having predictable or boundary status. This makes the family-level result interpretable at the finite-row level: the withheld-code checks are not hiding unclassified elements inside $R$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon support.}
The codon-level deletion run gives a sharper local control. It reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The recovery-rate-above-threshold field is also $1.0$. These figures say that the window-six packet is acting through predictable and boundary cases, not through an asserted biochemical anchor. That distinction is essential: a codon restored by this finite coordinate procedure is restored as a label-table entry inside the code layer.

\paragraph{Spectral and randomization checks.}
The spectral row records $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the finite spectral computation is numerically coherent at the recorded tolerance. The shape-significance run uses $N=2000$ sampled rows, seed $42$, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries. It finds $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$ and $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$. The amino-acid-class-preserving null model also uses $N=2000$ and seed $42$; it records observed $M$ size $20$, exact match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape $p$-value $0.0$, and lower-tail $p$-value $0.0035$. The row does not list the elements of $M$ themselves, so only the recorded size and test counts are used here.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The finite evidence supports the following restricted statement. In the standard-code label table, the window-six local tile structure identifies a special $13$-codon subset $R$, partitions it completely into six named modules, and gives strong leave-one-code-out recovery when alternative-code rows are grouped by family. The family row is strongest for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate groups, and slightly weaker but still above baseline for alternative yeast. The combined code-level result is consistent with local boundary structure in the codon display.

\paragraph{Boundary.}
No biological promotion is licensed by these rows alone. A local codon tile is not a translation apparatus, not a folded-protein observation, not a physical-admissibility test, not a function assay, not a mechanism, and not a universal biological law. Amino-acid label readback is still label readback, even when the finite geometry is statistically sharp. Crossing from codon labels to translation realization, structural order, physical constraint, or biological function would require a separate contact whose observed fact lives at that higher layer.
