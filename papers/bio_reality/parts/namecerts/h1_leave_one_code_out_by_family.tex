\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This finite row records a window-six codon-coordinate test on the codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is the six-bit codon display, read back through the curated standard-code label table.  The contact is therefore deliberately narrow: it supplies codon-to-label assignments for the standard code and permits code-layer readback, local tile-face checks, singleton label-corner checks, and three-point label-class checks.  It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Those higher-layer claims are blocked unless a separate reality contact supplies them.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite evidence supports a code-layer statement only.  Window-six geometry exposes a local boundary-tile pattern whose leave-one-code-out recovery is strong by family, whose codon modules cover the thirteen-codon row, and whose null-model comparisons make the observed shape non-generic in the sampled finite ensembles.  The same evidence does not promote the local tile into a global biological law.  It does not show that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity, that translation is mechanically realized by the tile, that amino-acid label readback determines folded protein structure, that a sequence is physically admissible, that a function is realized, or that the mechanism is universal.  The correct conclusion is narrower and finite: curated code labels make the local codon-tile split testable at the code-read layer, and the five-family leave-one-code-out row preserves high above-baseline recovery within that layer.
