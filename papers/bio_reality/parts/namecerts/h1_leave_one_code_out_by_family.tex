\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Code Family}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback scope.}
The record is a finite code-read experiment on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with codons read through the curated standard-code label table.  The carrier distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The readback is therefore only a label-table readback: it concerns codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels, not translation as a cellular process.  The finite distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the local object being tested is not the full genetic code, but a finite boundary-sensitive row inside $Q_6$ whose labels are supplied by the external code table.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The finite-row spectrum separates the marked row from the complementary code geometry by the recorded values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the numerical row satisfies its recorded cubic constraint to floating-point tolerance.  Against the shape-null experiment with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling log reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed marked size was $|M|=20$, the exact-match count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$ out of $2000$, and the corresponding values were $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These controls support non-random local code-read geometry, but they do not supply a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge.  It observes that a standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels, and it can test code-read structure and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, function realization, or a universal biological mechanism.  Consequently, no statement in this chapter says that DNA obeys the six-bit tile as biological necessity, that amino-acid label sequences determine folded structures, or that a local codon tile is a global law of life.  The supported claim is narrower: in the finite $Q_6$ code-read carrier, the row $R$ and its module partition expose a stable local boundary signal, and the five recorded code-family leave-one-out rows remain above the baseline hit rate.  Any promotion beyond code-read requires a separate higher-layer contact.
