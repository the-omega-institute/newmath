\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. It is sufficient for code-layer readback and for testing local codon-tile label splits. It is not an observation of translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or a universal biological law. Amino-acid label readback remains a code-level datum. A claim about a folded structure, a realized translation process, or functional selection would require a separate contact with the relevant experiment or observation. The present finite row therefore supports this narrower statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a stable local boundary pattern across the five evaluated code families, while the biological labels used to read that pattern remain curated contacts rather than consequences of the coordinate system itself.
