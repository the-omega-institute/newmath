\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Behavior by Codon-Family Route}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Object and scope.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact: each of the $64$ codons is read only as a curated amino-acid or stop label. The coordinate carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, equipped with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The claim tested here is narrower than a biological mechanism claim. Window-six codon coordinates may expose a local boundary pattern in the label table, but the labels themselves remain supplied by the curated code table. The chapter therefore studies code readback and local split behavior only.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The tested contact does not observe translation in a cell, folded protein structure, energetic admissibility, organismal function, or a universal biological law. A codon-to-label table can support statements about label placement and finite combinatorial recovery. It cannot by itself certify ribosomal realization, folding order, physical feasibility, phenotype, or mechanism. Any such higher-layer statement would require another contact whose measurements live at that layer. In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is not a folded structure, and a local tile in $Q_6$ is not a global law of biology.

\paragraph{Punctured region.}
The verified punctured region $R$ has cardinality $13$. The listed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run reports that this region matches a punctured-cube profile with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the local object is not merely a set of exceptional labels: it has a finite face structure in the codon cube. The module classification accounts for all $13$ members of $R$ with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
This is a complete finite partition of the observed boundary region for the present data row.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The radial polynomial residual is numerically negligible at $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the cubic-polynomial check reports the same residual. These numbers are used as finite numerical witnesses for the codon-cube split; they are not treated as biochemical constants. The shape test used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It found one exact shape match, $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, $55377$ duplicate retries, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts. The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,&
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.

$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, produced $0$ exact matches, lower-tail count $7$, observed $M$-size $20$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$. These rows say that the observed local geometry is uncommon under the tested finite null models. They do not say why the genetic code has this form.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, while the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The same $0.9841269841269841$ value is reported for the hit rate above baseline. Hence the finite row supports a strong code-layer recovery statement: once one code table is held out, the local codon-cube structure and its boundary recover most targets far above the baseline rate. This is still a label-table result; it is not a proof of translation dynamics or organismal necessity.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The family-level row separates the leave-one-code-out behavior into five families. The family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family count is $5$. Four families achieve perfect above-baseline recovery in this finite row, while the alternative-yeast family remains high but not perfect. This pattern is more informative than the aggregate number alone because it shows that the recovery is not confined to a single named code family. At the same time, the lower alternative-yeast value is a useful guard against overstatement: the table supports family-stratified code readback, not a uniform mechanistic theorem about all biological contexts.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out support.}
The related leave-one-codon-out probe reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. Its threshold comparison also gives $1.0$. Together with the module partition, this says that every module has a predictable or boundary status in the finite row. The absence of anchors is significant for interpretation: the recovered behavior is not being attributed to a single fixed codon acting as a universal biological cause. It is a distributed local-code phenomenon in the codon-cube representation.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The row supports the following restricted conclusion. In the six-bit codon cube, the standard-code label table exhibits a $13$-codon punctured region with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, a complete six-module classification, significant finite-null separation, and strong leave-one-code-out recovery across five code-family routes. The best reading is a code-layer local boundary result: curated codon labels align with a finite codon-cube tile structure and recover held-out label behavior at high rates. No translation process, folded structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this row alone.
