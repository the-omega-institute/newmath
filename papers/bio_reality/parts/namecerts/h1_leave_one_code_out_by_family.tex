\subsection{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out readback}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Finite row under test.}
The claim tests whether the window-six codon-coordinate packet keeps a stable code-layer signal when whole code tables are withheld by family.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube, and the observed contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The finite object is not a model of translation dynamics.  It is a label-readback experiment: codons are displayed in a six-bit coordinate geometry, local tile faces are extracted, and the resulting boundary pattern is compared with curated code labels.  The family-stratified row evaluates five code families and asks whether the held-out table targets land above a baseline label hit rate.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube residue.}
The distinguished residue set is a thirteen-codon puncture pattern.  The verified row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite check records the face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it has a specific punctured-cube incidence profile.  This is the code-layer residue whose labels are probed against curated readback.  The row supplies no independent biological mechanism for why those codons should be singled out; the test only reports that the coordinate extraction produces this finite set and that the set has the stated local face counts.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling controls.}
The finite spectrum row supplies a radial scalar package for the same local geometry.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,\\
P_{\mathrm{rad}}&=-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The residual is numerically at tolerance for the cubic check, so the spectral row is consistent with the finite coordinate calculation rather than a separate biological assertion.  The shape-control sampling row used $N=2000$ distinct null samples after $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ high-Jaccard matches.  An amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ reported zero exact shape matches and seven lower-tail outcomes at or below the observed $M$-size.  The recorded observed size is $|M|=20$, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-coordinate enrichment statement at the code-read layer.  They do not by themselves identify a translation apparatus, a fold, an admissible physical chemistry, or an organism-level function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out aggregate.}
The aggregate leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{hit}_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{M\setminus T+\partial}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit}_{\mathrm{above\ baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed local coordinate signal is not a one-table artifact in this finite battery: almost all held-out code targets are recovered above the baseline when the local residue and boundary information are used.  The row still remains a code-table readback row.  It does not claim that the window-six geometry generates the code, that all biological codes must obey the same geometry, or that the extracted tile is a universal law.

\paragraph{Family split.}
The family-stratified row is the focal claim.  It partitions the leave-one-code-out evaluation into five families and records the above-baseline hit rates
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family count is therefore $5$.  Four families are perfect under the finite criterion, and the alternative-yeast family remains strongly above baseline.  The claim certified here is exactly that family-level withholding preserves a code-read signal for the available finite rows.  The family labels are bookkeeping over curated code tables, not evidence for a common causal mechanism.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The leave-one-codon-out module row assigns all thirteen residue codons to named modules, with no missing or extra codons.  The verified module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row records $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, $13$ assigned codons, no extra module codons, no missing residue codons, and no unassigned codons.  A companion leave-one-codon-out row reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchors, high closure coverage of $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Hence every module has a predictable or boundary status in the finite account.

\paragraph{Local contact boundary.}
The only contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which observes codon-to-label assignments.  The contact can test code-read behavior and local label splitting; it cannot test whether translation is realized, whether a polypeptide folds, whether a molecular configuration is physically admissible, whether a biological function is present, or whether a universal mechanism has been found.  Amino-acid label readback is therefore not protein structure.  Cross-table or family-level stability of the local tile may motivate later biological experiments, but no higher-layer conclusion follows until a separate contact supplies observations at that layer.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite rows support the following bounded claim.  In the six-coordinate codon display, the thirteen-codon residue $R$ has the verified punctured-cube profile $(13,16,4)$, the spectral scalars satisfy the recorded tolerance check, the null controls give small exact-shape and lower-tail probabilities, the aggregate leave-one-code-out hit rate rises from baseline $0.19821739130434785$ to $0.9841269841269841$ above baseline, and the five-family split remains above baseline in every recorded family.  This is evidence for a local code-read boundary tile in the curated label table.  It is not evidence, by itself, for translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.
