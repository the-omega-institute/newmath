\subsection{Leave-one-code-out family boundary for the window-six codon tile}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The record uses the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ as a finite display carrier and reads its vertices through the curated standard-code label table.  The biological contact is therefore a codon-to-label table: it observes whether a codon is assigned to an amino-acid or stop label, but it does not observe translation realization, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The claim tested here is narrower: the window-six coordinate system may expose a local boundary-tile pattern whose readback is visible at the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Residual row.}
The distinguished residual row $R$ has exactly thirteen codons.  In the verified finite run, the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
|R|&=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the row is not treated merely as a list of exceptional codons: it is a finite tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-faces inside the codon cube display.  The row contains the codons later assigned to the modules $\mathrm{agr}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$, $\mathrm{cun}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}$, $\mathrm{ssn}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion remains at the code-read layer.  The curated standard-code table supplies label readback and local split checks only.  Amino-acid label sequences are not folded protein structures.  A local codon tile is not a translation apparatus, not a structural-order measurement, not a physical-admissibility test, not a function assay, not a universal mechanism, and not a global biological necessity.  Any claim crossing one of those boundaries requires a separate contact that directly observes the corresponding layer.
