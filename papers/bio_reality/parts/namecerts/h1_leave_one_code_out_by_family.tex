\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the ordinary codon alphabet and then through a curated standard-code label table.  The distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The internal structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the biological readback is the standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The external contact is therefore narrow: a curated table assigns labels to codons.  It is not a translation experiment, not a folding experiment, not a physical-admissibility measurement, not a functional assay, not a mechanism, and not a universality theorem.
