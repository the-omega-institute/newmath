\subsection{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out recovery}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object and coordinate display.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer object, not as a mechanism for translation or a model of protein realization. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the $64$ RNA codons. The active boundary set $R$ has size $13$ and is exactly
$\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$.
The same finite set is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the evidence used here is not a free biological interpretation of codons; it is a finite coordinate pattern whose readout is compared against a curated codon-to-label table.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape measurements.}
The row-level geometry has a numerical spectral certificate. The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check is numerically zero within the recorded tolerance: the polynomial value is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$. Shape significance was tested against $2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$. There was $1$ exact shape match, so $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ controls had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, so $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampling log records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite count is not being presented as a continuum approximation. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $2000$ controls and seed $42$, the observed size was $|M|=20$, with $0$ exact matches, $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed size, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family result.}
The family-stratified test evaluates whether local window-six information remains informative when code tables are held out by family. Five families were evaluated. The hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The aggregate leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ target events. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The family table therefore records a strong finite-row asymmetry: the held-out code family is usually recovered by the same local boundary geometry, and the one non-perfect family, alternative yeast, still records $5/6$ performance rather than a null-level value.

\paragraph{Codon-module accounting.}
The leave-one-codon-out module classification exhausts the $13$ codons of $R$. The assigned count is $13$, with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module is recorded as having a predictable or boundary explanation. In the codon-level leave-one-out run, the predictable count was $8$, boundary count was $5$, anchor count was $0$, high closure coverage was $8$, and the recovery rate was $1.0$. This module accounting is important because it prevents the family-level result from being only a pooled statistic: each codon in the active boundary set is assigned to a named finite module.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. This contact can test code-layer readback and local codon-tile label splitting. It does not test whether translation is realized in an organism, whether any amino-acid sequence folds, whether a physical state is admissible, whether a biological function occurs, whether a molecular mechanism is present, or whether the local tile is a universal biological law. The conclusion is therefore deliberately narrow: the six-coordinate codon display exposes a family-stable local boundary pattern in the standard-code label table, with significant shape and null-model support, but any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality requires an additional independent contact.
