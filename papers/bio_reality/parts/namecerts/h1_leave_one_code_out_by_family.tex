\subsection{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out recovery in the window-six codon cube}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite claim tested here is that the window-six codon coordinate system carries a family-stable local boundary signal for standard-code label readback.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, equipped with the codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard genetic-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and the computation asks whether the codons whose labels change across known code tables fall inside the same local tile-and-boundary region already obtained from the standard-code display.  The tested layer is therefore code readout only.  The row does not assert that the geometry implements translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal law of biology.

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$R=\{\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}$.  Its cubical incidence summary is the $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the finite object used by the test is not an unspecified subset of the codon cube but a thirteen-vertex punctured-cube pattern with sixteen one-faces and four two-faces.  The associated spectral calculation gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, with the radial cubic residual recorded as $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  Thus the same finite coordinate object supplies both the local boundary rows and a stable numerical spectrum within floating tolerance.

\paragraph{Family split.}
For the family-stratified leave-one-code-out test, the experiment evaluated $5$ code families.  The hit rate above the baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\texttt{alternative\_yeast} &\mapsto 0.8333333333333334,\\
\texttt{bacterial\_plastid} &\mapsto 1.0,\\
\texttt{candidate\_other} &\mapsto 1.0,\\
\texttt{mitochondrial} &\mapsto 1.0,\\
\texttt{nuclear\_ciliate} &\mapsto 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Four of the five families therefore reached perfect above-baseline recovery under this row, while the alternative-yeast family retained a non-perfect but still high value of $5/6=0.8333333333333334$.  In the broader leave-one-code-out run, $23$ tables and $63$ targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The same value, $0.9841269841269841$, is recorded as the overall hit rate above baseline for that run.  The family row is therefore not an isolated numerical accident: it refines a table-level result in which the local region and its boundary recover almost all observed code-table targets while the baseline stays near $0.1982$.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The codon-level leave-one-out row gives $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, no anchors, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ with no extras, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six named modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\texttt{agr} &= \{\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG}\},\\
\texttt{ar\_lys} &= \{\texttt{AAA}\},\\
\texttt{cun} &= \{\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU}\},\\
\texttt{ile\_met} &= \{\texttt{AUA}\},\\
\texttt{ssn} &= \{\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UUA}\},\\
\texttt{stop\_trp} &= \{\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This accounting matters because the family-level statement can be read codon-by-codon: every codon used by the boundary set is placed into a predictable-or-boundary module, and the module cover has size $13$ over a set $R$ of size $13$.  No hidden residual class is needed to explain the leave-one-code-out family row.

\paragraph{Null comparison.}
Two finite null checks calibrate the signal.  In the shape-significance run with seed $42$, $N=2000$ samples were accepted after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exact shape agreement occurred once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while $25$ samples reached Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null with the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding recorded values are $p=0.0$ for exact class-preserving shape recovery and $p=0.0035$ for the lower tail.  These null rows do not prove a biological mechanism.  They do show that the observed finite window-six pattern is not reproduced at ordinary frequency by the listed randomizations.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The scientific content of this row is a constrained recovery statement.  If one removes a code family from the comparison and asks whether its altered labels are recovered by the standard-code local tile region plus the boundary, the recovery remains above the baseline in every listed family and is perfect in four of five families.  The computation is tied to the curated codon-to-label table and to finite cube data: $R$ has $13$ codons, the module cover assigns all $13$, the table run evaluates $23$ tables and $63$ targets, and the family row records $5$ family rates.  Nothing in these data supplies a ribosomal realization, a folding experiment, a thermodynamic admissibility test, or a functional assay.  The proper conclusion is that window-six codon coordinates expose a reproducible local code-label boundary, while every claim beyond codon-label readout remains unavailable until a separate empirical contact is attached.
