\subsection{Leave-one-code-out stability by code family}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary row.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read only as a code-layer coordinate system for the standard-code label table.  The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube tile with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  This gives the chapter its finite object: not a biochemical law, but a local combinatorial face, with singleton label corners and three-point label classes read back through a curated standard-code contact.  The curated table supplies codon-to-label assignments only.  It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral diagnostics.}
The finite-row diagnostics separate the tile from a generic relabeling artifact.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact shape appeared once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while high-overlap shapes with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving $p_{\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the quoted probabilities are attached to a concrete finite enumeration protocol rather than to an asymptotic approximation.  A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null model reports observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$ out of $2000$, and lower-tail count $7$ out of $2000$, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial or cubic polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically at tolerance scale for the reported finite matrix calculation.  These numbers support the claim that the observed row is a stable finite coordinate phenomenon; they do not promote it beyond code readback.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family evidence.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  Against that baseline, the tile reports hit rate $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M\setminus T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary, with the same value $0.9841269841269841$ recorded as the hit rate above baseline.  The family-stratified row contains $5$ families.  Their above-baseline hit rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the family split is not carried by a single pooled count alone.  Four reported families attain perfect above-baseline recovery, while the alternative-yeast family remains high but non-perfect at $5/6$.  The proper reading is local and finite: the window-six tile is informative for leave-one-code-out label recovery across the reported family partition.

\paragraph{Codon-level module accounting.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ members of $R$, with assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing members, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\

Every listed module has a predictable or boundary status in the finite probe.  This is the strongest local form of the chapter claim: the row is closed under the reported module accounting, and the leave-one-code-out family rates remain high when the same local tile is tested against held-out code tables.

\paragraph{Reality-contact boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge.  It supports code-layer readback: a codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label by the curated table, and the finite probe may test local label splits against that table.  The contact does not observe translation, ribosomal realization, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, causal mechanism, or universal biological necessity.  Therefore the chapter claim is deliberately bounded: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile regularity with family-stratified leave-one-code-out support, but all higher-layer biological claims remain blocked unless a separate contact supplies the missing observation.
