\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Code Family}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate setting.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite reality contact at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated codon-to-label table.  The relevant BEDC distinctions are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  These distinctions are not interpreted as a biological mechanism.  They are used only to ask whether a finite local tile boundary, computed from the codon-coordinate data, recurs when one code table or one codon is left out.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The distinguished finite row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the code-read object being tested is not an unrestricted list of codons.  It is a finite boundary-shaped subset of the codon cube, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces.  The module classification also accounts for all thirteen row codons and no others: $R$ is partitioned into the modules $\{AGA,AGG\}$, $\{AAA\}$, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, $\{AUA\}$, $\{UCA,UUA\}$, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$.  The classification row reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, $missing\_from\_modules=\varnothing$, $extra\_module\_codons=\varnothing$, and $unassigned\_codons=\varnothing$.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}
The finite coordinate row has a reproducible spectrum.  The verified numerical values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check is numerically closed at $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is the reported value both for the cubic-polynomial residual and for the radial-polynomial residual.  A separate shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$.  Among $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ were duplicate retries, $1$ exact shape match was observed, and $25$ samples had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also at $N=2000$ with seed $42$, reported observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These numbers support the narrower claim that the local row is finite, non-generic under the sampled controls, and visible at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family test.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out row is the primary finite-row claim for this section.  Five code families were evaluated.  The verified family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus four of the five recorded families are perfect at the reported resolution, while the alternative-yeast family remains above the baseline but is not perfect.  The associated all-table leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The same run reports $0.9365079365079365$ for hits in $M-T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ for hits in $M-T$ plus the boundary.  The family-level result is therefore not a standalone slogan: it is the family stratification of a finite leave-one-code-out computation whose aggregate target count and baseline rate are explicitly recorded.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out support.}
A complementary leave-one-codon-out row reports no anchors, five boundary codons, eight high-coverage closure cases, eight predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  In symbols the finite row is summarized as
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{anchor\_count}&=0,\\
\mathrm{boundary\_count}&=5,\\
\mathrm{closure\_coverage\_high}&=8,\\
\mathrm{predictable\_count}&=8,\\
\mathrm{recovery\_rate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Together with the module partition of $R$, this says that every named module has either predictable or boundary support in the finite classification.  It does not say that every codon has an intrinsic biological explanation from the window-six geometry.  It says that the row-level boundary computation recovers the observed code-label split in the tested finite table.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge: a table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Consequently the result must remain local.  A window-six codon tile may expose a finite boundary pattern in the standard-code label table, and the leave-one-code-out family row shows strong stability above the recorded baseline across five code families.  No claim follows that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as a global biological law.  No claim follows that amino-acid label sequences are folded protein structures.  No claim follows about physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies those data.
