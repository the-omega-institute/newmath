\paragraph{Finite codon coordinate record.}
The row under consideration is the family-resolved form of the leave-one-code-out probe for the standard-code codon table.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a codon-label display.  The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is the following explicit codon set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row reports that this set has the punctured-cube face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the codon list is not being used merely as a bag of labels: it is the vertex list of a small local tile with $16$ one-dimensional incidences and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the chosen six-bit display.  The row does not turn this local geometry into a biological necessity.  It only supplies a bounded coordinate pattern against which curated codon-label contacts can be checked.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The finite spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The associated radial cubic evaluation is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically at zero at the recorded tolerance.  Thus the local codon tile has a reproducible spectral signature inside the finite coordinate experiment.  The shape-control row sampled $N=2000$ comparison objects with seed $42$ and required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts because $55377$ attempted draws were duplicates.  In that run there was exactly $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ comparisons with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving control, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, recorded observed $M$-size $20$, $0$ exact matches, and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$-size.  Its finite values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the observed local pattern is not a frequent accident under the sampled finite controls.  They do not, by themselves, identify a translation mechanism, a folded structure, a physical admissibility condition, or a biological function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out module resolution.}
The codon-level companion probe records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification is complete for the same $13$ codons: $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The explicit module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

Every module in this partition is recorded as having predictable or boundary status.  This matters for the family row because the leave-one-code-out hits are not scattered across an unnamed set of codons.  They are carried by a closed finite list whose module assignment accounts for all $13$ members of $R$.

\paragraph{Curated table contact.}
The only reality contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is sufficient for code-layer readback: it can test whether a codon-label split appears in the standard-code label table and whether leave-one-out targets land in the finite boundary-and-closure pattern.  It is not a contact with ribosomal translation dynamics, molecular structure, energetic admissibility, phenotype, fitness, or universal code evolution.  Consequently the scientific content of this row is deliberately narrow.  It says that, in the six-bit codon display, a $13$-codon local tile with the listed modules and spectra gives high leave-one-code-out recovery across $5$ family bins, with aggregate hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ above a baseline of $0.19821739130434785$ over $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  It does not say that DNA is governed by this coordinate geometry, and it does not infer protein structure or function from amino-acid label readback.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The scope restriction is part of the row, not an afterthought.  A local codon tile can be supported at the code-read layer while still being silent about higher biological layers.  The finite evidence supports the following bounded statement: curated codon labels, when placed in the six-coordinate codon cube, expose a reproducible local boundary structure whose family-resolved leave-one-code-out rates are high in the recorded run.  No claim of translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global law follows unless an independent contact is supplied for that higher layer.
