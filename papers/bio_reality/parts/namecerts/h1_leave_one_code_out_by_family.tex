\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed label contact.}
The experiment is carried by the six-coordinate codon cube, with each codon represented as a finite word over the four RNA letters and read only through the curated standard-code label table.  The contact supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels; it does not supply a translation process, a molecular structure, a physical feasibility test, a biological function, a mechanism, or a universality theorem.  The admissible claim is therefore narrower: window-six coordinates expose a local boundary pattern whose label behavior can be checked against curated code rows.

The distinguished residual row set has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a cubical subobject this row has the punctured-cube face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite pattern is not merely a list of exceptional labels: it has a measured local face structure inside the codon cube.  The codons in $R$ are partitioned by the module classifier as
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},&
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},&
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},&
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, $13$ assigned codons, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  Thus every row in the exceptional set is accounted for by one of the six named local modules.

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row anomaly at the code-label layer, not a claim that the geometry is a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one recovery across code families.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluates whether the local coordinate row predicts withheld code-label targets better than the baseline.  Across $23$ evaluated code tables and $63$ total targets, the baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported rate above baseline is likewise $0.9841269841269841$.  Family stratification separates this aggregate recovery into five families:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus four of the five recorded families achieve perfect above-baseline family recovery, while the alternative-yeast family remains high but not perfect.  This is the specific content of the family-stratified claim: the same finite coordinate split that recovers the aggregate leave-one-code-out targets also persists across the named family classes, with one family showing a visible shortfall rather than being hidden inside the total average.

\paragraph{Codon-level boundary check.}
The associated leave-one-codon-out probe records $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, closure coverage high at $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Every classified module has at least predictable or boundary support.  This is consistent with the row-level account above: the local tile behaves as a code-read scaffold whose weak points are exposed as boundary rows, not as an all-purpose reconstruction of biology.

\paragraph{Local interpretation and excluded promotions.}
The valid reading is that the curated standard-code table, when placed on the six-coordinate codon cube, exhibits a small punctured-cube row $R$ whose finite geometry, spectrum, null-model behavior, module partition, aggregate leave-one-code-out recovery, and family-stratified recovery all align at the codon-label layer.  The contact is still only a label table.  No translation dynamics, folded protein structure, structural ordering principle, physical admissibility, biological function, general mechanism, or universal genetic-code law follows from these rows.  Any statement beyond code-label readback requires a separate empirical or formal contact that actually observes that higher layer.
