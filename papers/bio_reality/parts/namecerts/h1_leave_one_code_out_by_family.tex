\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery by Code Family}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon object.} This record studies a code-read phenomenon on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  A codon is treated as a six-bit display point, and the only outside contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The carrier-side distinctions are deliberately local: coordinate position in $Q_6$, closure under the finite tile operation, a spectral summary, and the codon-label relation read from the curated table.  The distinguished residual row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the finite object is not an unspecified codon heuristic: it is a concrete thirteen-codon boundary set inside the codon cube, read against a fixed standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.} The observed marked shape has size $20$ in the class-preserving null experiment.  Its spectral row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with cubic residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the radial polynomial check.  The shape null with seed $42$ sampled $N=2000$ distinct randomized cases after $57377$ attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, so $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ cases with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, so $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, while $7$ samples fell in the lower tail at or below the observed marked-set size $20$.  The corresponding recorded probabilities are $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$.  These finite rows say that the observed local codon tile is not a common draw under the two stated randomizations, while still saying nothing about mechanism beyond the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family recovery.} The family-level experiment evaluates alternative code tables by asking whether held-out code changes land in the marked shape after the standard-code tile is used as the reference.  Across $23$ evaluated tables and $63$ total targets, the baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary row is $0.9841269841269841$.  The recorded above-baseline rate is therefore $0.9841269841269841$.  At the coarser family level there are $5$ families.  Their hit rates above the baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family row is therefore nearly saturated: four of the five families have complete recovery at this resolution, and the remaining family is still well above the baseline row.  The result supports a local code-read claim: the six-coordinate tile is informative about where curated alternative-code label changes occur.

\paragraph{Module accounting.} The thirteen-codon boundary row is completely assigned to six modules.  The module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\texttt{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\texttt{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit records $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, $13$ assigned codons, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The leave-one-codon-out row is compatible with this partition: it has $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Hence the family-level result is not only a pooled-rate statement; it sits on a complete finite classification of the boundary row.

\paragraph{Scientific limit.} The supported claim is intentionally narrow.  The curated table lets the construction read codon labels and compare local tile splits.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, a universal evolutionary mechanism, or a law forcing organisms to follow this geometry.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is not a folded structure, and a CUN or UUR label movement is not by itself evidence about tRNA-copy dynamics or organism-level realization.  Any such promotion would require a separate contact at the appropriate layer.  The present row is ready only as a finite code-read record: window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically sharp local boundary tile for the curated standard-code label table and for the tested alternative-code families.
