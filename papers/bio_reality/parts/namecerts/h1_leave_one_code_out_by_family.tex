\subsection{Leave-one-code-out family recovery in the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Carrier and readback.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The BEDC-side display has four distinguished finite features: a six-bit codon coordinate, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The biological readback is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The external contact is therefore a table contact: it can test code-layer label readback and local codon-tile label splits, but it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, function realization, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{The finite exceptional row.}
The finite row selected by the local tile calculation is the set $R$ of thirteen codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The punctured-cube check gives the same thirteen codons and the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not being used as an open-ended biological class.  It is a finite local object inside $Q_6$, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional tile faces.  Its contact with biology is only through the curated standard-code labels attached to these codons.

\paragraph{Shape significance against coordinate nulls.}
The shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those samples there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$.  The corresponding reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These null checks support the statement that the local tile shape is not a typical coordinate-row artifact under the tested finite randomizations.  They still remain code-layer tests: the randomized rows compare codon-label geometry, not protein structure or organism-level function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out recovery.}
The family-level claim is a refinement of the leave-one-code-out recovery table.  Across the code-out evaluation, $23$ tables were evaluated and $63$ targets were counted.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus the observed code-read recovery is concentrated in the finite tile-and-boundary region rather than at the baseline rate.

\paragraph{Codon-out and module checks.}
The leave-one-codon-out table gives $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, closure coverage high equal to $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification assigns all thirteen codons of $R$ with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons: $R\_size=13$ and $assigned\_count=13$.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has predictable or boundary status, and the classification is exhaustive for the thirteen-codon row.  This is the finite reason the by-family result is meaningful: the family-level recovery is not floating over unspecified codons, but is tied to a closed thirteen-codon module decomposition.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The curated standard-code table supports code-layer readback and local split checks.  It does not supply a translation experiment, a structure experiment, a physical-admissibility measurement, a function assay, a mechanism, or a universality theorem.  Consequently the admissible conclusion is that the window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically nontrivial local boundary-tile structure for codon-label readback, with strong leave-one-code-out recovery by family.  No claim about folded protein structure, biological function, universal genetic-code necessity, or molecular realization follows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact is added.
