\subsection{Family-Stratified Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary for the Standard Code}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record asks whether the window-six codon coordinate separates the standard-code label table in a way that remains visible when whole code families are held out. The biological datum used here is only the curated codon-to-label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid label or a stop label. The coordinate system is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes used as finite distinctions. The intended readback is therefore a code-layer readback, not a claim about translation chemistry, protein folding, physical feasibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Finite carrier and boundary set.}
The boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite geometric object being tested against the curated code table. No mechanistic interpretation is attached to the shape itself. The available finite rows report the size and identity of $R$; they do not supply the full codon list for the complementary set $M$, although later probes report $|M|=20$ and use $M$ in recovery tests.

The finite adjacency calculation gives the spectral values

\begin{aligned}

The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the numerical witness is within the stated tolerance. Against $2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$, the exact shape was matched once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was met by $25$ samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, and the reported probabilities were

\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support the claim that the observed code-layer shape is not a typical sample under the tested finite null procedures, while still leaving the interpretation at the label-table level.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result by family.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ held-out targets. Its aggregate rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline\ hit\ rate} &= 0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ above\ baseline} &= 0.9841269841269841,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in}\ M\setminus T &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit\ rate\ in}\ M\setminus T\ \mathrm{plus\ boundary} &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-stratified row contains $5$ families. The above-baseline family hit rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite family row says that every listed family except alternative yeast attains the maximal above-baseline rate in this test, while alternative yeast still remains substantially above the baseline rate. The statement is not that these families are generated by the cube geometry; it is only that the held-out label-table targets remain highly recoverable under this finite coordinate-and-boundary procedure.

\paragraph{Codon-level recovery and module assignment.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports no anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{recovery\ rate}=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module classification covers all $13$ codons in $R$: the assigned count is $13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons. The verified modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{Stop\_Trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has at least one predictable or boundary member according to the recorded classification. This is the finite reason the family-stratified leave-one-code-out row is treated as aligned with the local tile boundary: the entire $R$ set is exhausted by named modules, and the recovery probe reports complete codon-level recovery within the measured scope.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The evidence supports a precise code-layer claim: in the standard-code table, the six-bit codon coordinate exposes a local boundary-tile organization whose leave-one-code-out performance remains high across the recorded code families. The strongest family row is the set of four families with rate $1.0$, together with alternative yeast at $0.8333333333333334$; the strongest aggregate row is the $63$-target leave-one-code-out rate $0.9841269841269841$ above baseline. These facts are finite and table-relative. They do not identify a translation process, a folded structure, a physical admissibility criterion, a biological function, a causal mechanism, or a universal constraint on life. Any such promotion would require a different contact supplying the relevant observation.
