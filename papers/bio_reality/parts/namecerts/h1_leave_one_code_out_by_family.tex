\subsection{Family-resolved leave-one-code-out readback for a local codon tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite display and scope.}
This record concerns a window-six codon coordinate display of the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated codon-to-label table.  The observed object is therefore not translation itself, not folded structure, not physical admissibility, and not biological function.  It is a finite-row comparison between a local tile selected inside the codon cube and externally curated amino-acid or stop labels.  The claim tested here is the family-resolved leave-one-code-out version of the local boundary-tile phenomenon: when alternative code tables are withheld one at a time, their target label changes are usually recovered by the same local window-six tile rather than by an arbitrary global law.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The finite boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The codons in $R$ are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube tile with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of exceptional codons.  It is a finite local face-and-boundary object in $Q_6$: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces.  The construction still uses curated labels as its biological contact; the cube geometry alone does not supply the amino-acid or stop labels.

which is within the stated numerical tolerance for the polynomial check.  In this chapter the spectral row is used only as a finite diagnostic of the observed tile.  It is not interpreted as a mechanism for translation, a folding principle, or a physical admissibility criterion.

\paragraph{Shape significance row.}
Against the recorded shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match.  The exact-shape probability was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  For the high-overlap comparison, $25$ sampled rows had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrower statement that the observed local codon tile is uncommon under the sampled row model.  They do not turn the local tile into a global biological law.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null row.}
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$ samples, seed $42$, and observed $M$-row size $20$.  It recorded no exact shape matches:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ matches}\}=0,\qquad p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This strengthens the finite-row evidence that the local display is not an artifact of a simple amino-acid-class-preserving shuffle.  It still remains a code-read contact: labels are compared because the curated table supplies them.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out aggregate row.}
The aggregate leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total target changes.  Its baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary row was again $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus nearly all evaluated target changes are recovered by the local tile-and-boundary readback rather than by the raw baseline.  The relevant conclusion is local and finite: the selected row has strong recovery power for held-out code-table changes at the label layer.

\paragraph{Family-resolved row.}
The family-resolved run grouped the leave-one-code-out comparison into $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Four of the five families therefore reach perfect recovery above the baseline in the recorded finite rows, while the alternative-yeast family remains high but not perfect.  This is the chapter-specific signal: the local boundary tile is not merely an aggregate average over heterogeneous code-table changes.  It remains visible after the evaluated tables are separated into biological code families.

\paragraph{Module classification row.}
The boundary codons decompose into six named modules.  The recorded module assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

$$
The classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, there are no extra module codons, no missing codons from the module list, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has either predictable or boundary status.  In the associated leave-one-codon-out row, the recovery rate is $1.0$, with $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $0$ anchors.  These numbers show that the family-level code-table recovery is aligned with a complete finite module decomposition of the same thirteen-codon row.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Consequently, the correct verdict is a bounded positive one: the window-six codon coordinates expose a family-stable local boundary-tile signal for curated code-label readback.  No claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies it.
