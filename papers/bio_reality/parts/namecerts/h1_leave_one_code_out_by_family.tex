\subsection{Family-Stratified Leave-One-Code-Out Stability}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and label contact.}
The object considered here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The finite carrier has $64$ codon positions, but the active local question is concentrated on a distinguished remainder set $R$ of size $13$.  In the recorded run, the set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same set was checked as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  This is a finite code-layer observation: the labels are supplied by a curated standard genetic-code table, while the coordinate geometry supplies a local organization of codon positions.  The chapter therefore studies whether the local tile boundary remains visible when one code family is omitted from the comparison, not whether the tile is a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Family-stratified test.}
The leave-one-code-out family audit grouped the comparison rows into $5$ families and measured, for each family, the fraction of held-out targets whose observed label position landed above the baseline local-tile rate.  The experiment identifier is $7\mathrm{dda}978\mathrm{c}6\mathrm{c}97$, verified at $2026$-$05$-$31\mathrm{T}05{:}50{:}20+00{:}00$.  The resulting family rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus four of the five recorded families were saturated at the observed decision threshold, while the alternative-yeast family retained $5/6$-level support.  The aggregate leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  These numbers are the main row-level evidence for the family claim: family omission rarely destroys the local boundary signal, and the rare loss is confined to one named family rather than spread uniformly across the recorded family classes.

\paragraph{Module closure check.}
The codon-level module classification supplies a second finite consistency check.  The $13$ codons of $R$ were fully assigned: $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, with no extra module codons, no missing members, and no unassigned codons.  The six recorded modules were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module had either predictable support or boundary support.  The companion leave-one-codon-out run recorded $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchors, closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This is important for the family-stratified result because the family split is not floating over an unclassified residue set: each of the thirteen positions has a named module membership, and the local readback is exhausted by these six finite modules.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model calibration.}
The finite geometry was also calibrated against spectral and randomization checks.  The recorded spectral values were $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The cubic relation was satisfied up to numerical residue $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the radial polynomial evaluation.  Against $2000$ sampled alternatives in the shape-significance run, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_\mathrm{exact}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sample procedure attempted $57377$ distinct draws and rejected $55377$ duplicates.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving $p=0.0035$ for that lower-tail event.  These calibrations do not prove a mechanism; they indicate that the recorded tile shape and the family leave-out behavior are not typical under the stated finite shuffles.

\paragraph{Interpretation at the code layer.}
The family result is therefore ready as a code-read claim.  It says that the six-coordinate codon display, together with the curated standard-code labels, exposes a stable local boundary tile across the recorded leave-one-code-out family classes.  The evidence is finite and row-specific: $5$ families, $23$ evaluated tables, $63$ targets, a $13$-codon punctured-cube remainder, six exhaustive modules, and calibrated spectral and null-model checks.  The biological contact used here is only the codon-to-label table.  No translation event, folded structure, physical admissibility test, biological function, causal mechanism, or universal law is obtained from this contact.  Any claim crossing those layers would require a separate observation of that layer, with its own finite rows and its own failure conditions.
