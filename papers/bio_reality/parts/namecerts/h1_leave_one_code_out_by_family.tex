\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite readback contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a mechanism for translation.  It is the coordinate display in which each codon is read as a point of the finite cube, and the curated standard-code table supplies the external label map from codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The tested layer is therefore only $\mathrm{code\_read}$: a local tile may be compared with curated labels, but no claim about translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary necessity follows from this contact alone.

\paragraph{Scope and non-promotion rule.}
The result is a finite code-layer readback statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure that aligns with curated standard-code labels and with family-stratified leave-one-code-out recovery.  The supporting contact is only the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splitting.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded structure, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently an amino-acid label sequence read from this tile is not a folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any crossing from this chapter into translation, structure, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact with its own finite observations.
