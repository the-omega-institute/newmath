\subsection{Family-resolved leave-one-code-out stability for the window-six codon tile}

\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table. The contact is deliberately narrow: it supplies codon-to-label readback at the code layer, not translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The local BEDC object tested here is a boundary tile inside the codon display. Its distinguished residual set is
$R=\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$,
with $|R|=13$. The punctured-cube check gives the finite face data $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, so the object being reused by the family experiment is not merely a list of labels: it is a thirteen-vertex local face pattern with sixteen one-faces and four two-faces in the observed coordinate display.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling scale.}
The associated finite-row spectrum fixes the numerical scale of the tile. The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with radial cubic residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, within the reported tolerance for the polynomial check. The shape is also rare under the sampled nulls. Against $2000$ sampled tables, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, the exact-shape count is $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$; the count with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ is $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$, the observed module size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$. These numbers do not create a biological mechanism, but they do justify treating the tile as a finite-row signal rather than a decorative coordinate artifact.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out aggregate.}
The aggregate leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ nonstandard code tables and $63$ held-out targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The local tile without the singleton label corner reached hit rate $0.9365079365079365$, while the tile plus its boundary reached $0.9841269841269841$. The reported above-baseline hit rate is also $0.9841269841269841$. Thus the tested row does not say that the genetic code is forced by $Q_6$ geometry; it says that, after the curated label table has supplied the external contact, the local tile-and-boundary coordinates recover almost all held-out code-level targets in this finite leave-one-code-out panel.

\paragraph{Family-resolved row.}
The family-resolved run has experiment id $7\mathrm{dda}978\mathrm{c}6\mathrm{c}97$ and reports $5$ code families. The above-baseline hit rates by family are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family split is important because the aggregate $0.9841269841269841$ could have hidden a weak family. Instead, four of the five families are complete in this finite row, and the remaining alternative-yeast family still exceeds the baseline strongly at $0.8333333333333334$. The result is therefore a family-stability statement at the code-read layer: the same local boundary tile continues to recover held-out codon-label changes across the reported families, rather than succeeding only in a single pooled table.

\paragraph{Codon-module localization.}
The leave-one-codon-out and module-classification rows explain where the family signal sits. The codon-level run reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The thirteen residual codons are completely assigned to six modules, with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons: $\mathrm{AGR}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{AR\_Lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{CUN}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{Ile\_Met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{SSN}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$. The completeness row records $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and assigned count $13$, with empty extra-module, missing-from-module, and unassigned lists. Every module has predictable or boundary support, so the family-resolved result is not floating above the codon table: it is grounded in a complete partition of the same thirteen residual codons.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready claim is a finite-row code-read claim. Window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose family-resolved leave-one-code-out performance is above baseline in all five reported families, with four families at $1.0$ and one at $0.8333333333333334$. The claim stops there. The curated standard-code table is a reality bridge for labels only. No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this row unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
