\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Genetic-Code Family}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
This section records a finite code-read phenomenon on the six-bit codon cube.  Codons are treated as points of a finite coordinate cube, while amino-acid and stop labels are supplied by a curated standard-code table.  The claim is not that the cube geometry generates biology.  The claim is narrower: after the standard-code table has supplied the label partition, a window-six local tile detects a stable boundary pattern for codon-label reassignment tests, and that stability remains visible when alternative code tables are grouped by family.

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary set.}
The carrier is the $64$-point codon cube with the usual four-letter nucleotide alphabet encoded as six binary coordinates.  The finite row selected by the local boundary rule has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the selected set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent punctured-cube check returns the same thirteen codons and the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local tile is not represented only by a label count: it has a concrete vertex, edge, and square inventory inside the finite cube.  The thirteen codons also split into six named reassignment modules with no missing or extra elements:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The finite tile also has a numerical spectral profile.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the reported polynomial check is numerically at zero to the displayed tolerance.  A separate finite randomization audit used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  For the shape of $M$, the exact-shape match count was $1$, giving $p=0.0005$; the high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was met $25$ times, giving $p=0.0125$.  The sampling run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite test is reported with its sampling pressure visible rather than hidden.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
A second null model preserved amino-acid class while randomizing the remaining table structure.  With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed size was $|M|=20$.  The exact-shape match count was $0$, giving an empirical exact-shape rate $0.0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This does not by itself explain the biological origin of the standard code, but it does show that the observed local row is not a common outcome under the class-preserving finite shuffle used in this audit.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family result.}
The family-level row is the direct target of this section.  The experiment evaluated five code-table families.  The family hit rates above the finite baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported family count is $5$.  Four families hit every tested target above baseline, while the alternative-yeast family misses at least one target but remains high at five sixths.  The companion leave-one-code-out aggregate evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the finite local-tile rule reached $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M\setminus T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary allowance.  The same $0.9841269841269841$ value is reported as the hit rate above baseline.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out support.}
The codon-level leave-one-out row provides a second finite check on the same local picture.  It reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the row is not being supported by fixed anchors.  Its support is concentrated in predictable local closure and explicitly marked boundary cases.  This is consistent with the family-level result: the tile is useful as a code-read discriminator, but it must be read as a local finite pattern rather than as a global biological principle.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only reality contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That table supplies the observed code labels against which the finite cube pattern is tested.  It does not supply a translation apparatus, a ribosome model, a protein-folding observation, a thermodynamic admissibility test, a function assay, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently the present row supports only code-layer readback and local split checks.  No claim about translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these numbers unless a separate contact of the appropriate kind is added.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The finite evidence is strongest when stated locally.  A thirteen-codon punctured-cube row with $f(R)=(13,16,4)$, a stable module partition, a spectral profile with $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, significant finite null-model contrasts, and high leave-one-code-out recovery by family together indicate that the standard-code labels expose a robust boundary tile in window-six coordinates.  The alternative-yeast family is the weakest family row at $0.8333333333333334$, while the other four recorded families reach $1.0$.  The aggregate leave-one-code-out rate $0.9841269841269841$ above baseline is far above the baseline $0.19821739130434785$.  The correct conclusion is therefore a finite code-read conclusion: curated labels, once supplied, align with a small local boundary geometry.  The conclusion stops at that layer.
