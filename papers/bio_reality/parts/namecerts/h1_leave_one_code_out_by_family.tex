\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Code Family}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite-row claim.}
The finite row tests whether the window-six codon coordinate display gives a stable local tile signal when one genetic-code table is withheld from a code-family panel.  The object is the standard-code codon table read only as a curated codon-to-label contact.  The BEDC-side carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ with coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation structure; the readback side is the standard-code label table.  The row is therefore a code-read row, not a translation row.  It asks whether the local boundary tile that appears in the standard-code coordinates continues to recover withheld family targets above a baseline, while keeping curated amino-acid and stop labels as external reality contacts.

\paragraph{Observed family row.}
The family-stratified leave-one-code-out run has experiment id $7dda978c6c97$ and evaluates $5$ code families.  The hit rates above the baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{hit}(\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}) &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\operatorname{hit}(\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}) &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{hit}(\mathrm{candidate\_other}) &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{hit}(\mathrm{mitochondrial}) &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{hit}(\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}) &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus four of the five reported families are recovered at rate $1.0$, while the alternative-yeast family remains high at $0.8333333333333334$.  The companion leave-one-code-out row, experiment id $429e6e2c4eb4$, evaluates $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the above-baseline hit rate is $0.9841269841269841$.  Inside $M-T$ the hit rate is $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M-T$ plus the boundary it returns to $0.9841269841269841$.  The finite-row reading is that the family signal is concentrated in the local tile and its boundary rather than being a diffuse property of all codon labels.

\paragraph{Codon set and punctured-cube geometry.}
The distinguished relation set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the standard-code row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row reports the punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$.  This makes the local object concrete: it is not an abstract claim that the genetic code globally obeys a BEDC law, but a finite set of $13$ codons with $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the selected coordinate window.  The shape row also reports $\lvert M\rvert=20$ for the observed $M$ set; no separate explicit list of the $20$ $M$ codons is part of this finite-row payload, so the chapter uses only the reported size and the $M-T$ hit rates.

\paragraph{Spectral and null controls.}
The spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, within the numerical tolerance used by the finite-row run.  The shape-significance control with $N=2000$ samples has $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It finds $1$ exact shape match, hence $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, hence $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$, reports $0$ exact matches, $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size, $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These controls support the claim that the observed local tile is finite-row non-generic under the tested randomizations, while still leaving the biological interpretation bounded by the curated label contact.

\paragraph{Codon-module accounting.}
The leave-one-codon-out module-classification row accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$: $R$ size is $13$, assigned count is $13$, and the extra-module, missing-from-module, and unassigned lists are empty.  The six reported modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The companion leave-one-codon-out row reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, $0$ anchors, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This is the codon-level counterpart of the family row: the local tile has a complete module accounting for the $13$ relation codons, and every listed module has predictable or boundary support.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The curated standard-code table supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  It does not by itself supply translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  The local tile is therefore a boundary object: it can organize the finite standard-code labels in the six-bit codon display and can be tested by leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out probes, but it cannot be promoted across biological layers without a separate contact.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation; it is not a folded protein, not a physical admissibility certificate, and not a functional assay.  The positive verdict of this row is restricted to the claim that the window-six local boundary tile is stable across the reported code-family holdout test and is non-generic under the stated finite null controls.
