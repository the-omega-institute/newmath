\subsection{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out readback}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record works only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact is therefore a label bridge, not a translation experiment.  The finite row under test is the family-stratified leave-one-code-out row with claim identifier $\texttt{h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family}$.  Its purpose is to ask whether the window-six local tile continues to recover reassignment targets when nonstandard code tables are grouped by biological table family, while keeping the interpretation at codon-label readback.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The row uses the same distinguished reassignment boundary set $R$ of size $13$.  The verified codons are
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The punctured-cube computation gives the same list and the face-vector
$$\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the row is not merely a count: it is a concrete local subcomplex of the codon cube, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-faces, and four two-faces.  The module audit assigns all thirteen codons and leaves no extras, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The verified modules are
$$\begin{aligned}
\texttt{agr}&=\{\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG}\},&
\texttt{ar\_lys}&=\{\texttt{AAA}\},&
\texttt{cun}&=\{\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU}\},\\
\texttt{ile\_met}&=\{\texttt{AUA}\},&
\texttt{ssn}&=\{\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UUA}\},&
\texttt{stop\_trp}&=\{\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The classification row reports $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, and empty extra, missing, and unassigned lists.

\paragraph{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out row.}
The primary family row has experiment run $\texttt{7dda978c6c97}$ and contains five families.  The verified hit rates above baseline are

\texttt{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\

Four of the five family buckets are perfect at this finite resolution, and the remaining bucket is still above the baseline test.  The unstratified leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  In finite-row terms, the family stratification does not dilute the signal: it localizes the same leave-one-code-out behavior into named table families.

These numbers fit the module picture: the finite set $R$ is not treated as a biological law, but every module has either predictable or boundary support in the row-level audit.  The spectral row supplies a second internal check.  It reports

\mu&=3.051487585298128,\\
P_{\mathrm{radial}}&=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.

The displayed residual is numerically near zero at the recorded tolerance, so the coordinate and spectral readbacks agree at the finite precision of the run.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, including $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$ and seed $42$ as well.  It found exact match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$ at observed $M$ size $20$, $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These are finite Monte Carlo rows, not asymptotic theorems.  They say that the observed local shape is uncommon under the sampled nulls used here.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The verdict of this row is deliberately narrow.  It supports a code-read statement: window-six coordinates expose a local tile and boundary pattern that tracks codon-label reassignment rows across five table families.  It does not show that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not protein structure.  A separate higher-layer contact would be required before any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law could be made.
