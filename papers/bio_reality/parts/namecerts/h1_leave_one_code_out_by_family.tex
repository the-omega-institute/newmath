\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The biological contact is only the curated codon-to-label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label, and the readback resolution is codon label readback.  The internal BEDC data used here are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation rows on a six-bit codon display.  The distinctions are local: a codon may lie in a local tile face, may occupy a singleton label corner, or may belong to a three-point label class.  These distinctions are not promoted to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Interpretation at the code-read layer.}
The finite-row conclusion is a local code-read stability statement.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a boundary-tile organization around the curated standard-code labels, and the leave-one-code-out family test says that this organization remains highly predictive across the five listed family classes.  This supports the claim that the codon-label table has a nontrivial local finite geometry under this coordinate and closure readback.  It does not support a higher biological promotion.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law; window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function; and amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any claim about translation realization, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate reality contact beyond the curated standard-code table.
