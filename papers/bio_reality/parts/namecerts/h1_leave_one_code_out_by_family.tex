\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table.  The reality contact is deliberately narrow: it supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels, and it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universality theorem.  The finite claim is therefore only a code-read claim.  Window-six coordinates may expose a local boundary-tile pattern in the codon display, but the labels themselves remain external reality contacts supplied by the curated code table.

$$
for this finite code-read task.  Grouping the same test by family gives $5$ families.  The above-baseline family hit rates are

The claim is not that all biological codes are generated by the local tile.  The claim is the narrower finite statement that, once the curated labels are supplied, leave-one-code-out targets are recovered at high rate by the window-six local row and its boundary extension.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out boundary check.}
The codon-level leave-one-out row gives anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This row is compatible with the module table above: every module has either predictable or boundary support, and no module remains outside the finite classification.  The absence of anchors is important.  It says that the evidence is not a claim of isolated codon necessity; the support lives in the local closure-and-boundary pattern of the row.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
This chapter records a finite code-read phenomenon only.  The curated standard-code table supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, protein folding, structural order, energetic or physical admissibility, function realization, a universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  An amino-acid label sequence is not a folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any promotion beyond the code-read layer would require a separate reality contact for the promoted layer, and no such contact is included in this finite-row packet.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The finite rows support the following bounded interpretation.  In the six-bit codon cube, the row $R$ of $13$ codons forms a punctured local tile with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, a coherent spectral calibration, significant shape behavior under the supplied null models, complete module classification, and high leave-one-code-out recovery across $5$ code families.  This is evidence for a local boundary-tile regularity in codon-label readback.  It is not evidence, by itself, for translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.
