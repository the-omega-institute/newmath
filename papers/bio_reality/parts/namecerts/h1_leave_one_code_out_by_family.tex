\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Family}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite-row claim $h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family$ records a code-read phenomenon on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The carrier is the finite codon display, the observed contact is a curated standard-code label table, and the tested object is the local boundary-tile behavior of codons whose standard labels change under known code-table alternatives. The claim is not that the cube geometry is a biological law. It is the narrower statement that, after the standard-code labels are read back into the $Q_6$ coordinate display, the leave-one-code-out targets remain concentrated in the same local tile and boundary region across biological-code families.

\paragraph{Finite carrier and boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube computation gives the same row, with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row used by the family test is not a free narrative subset: it is the same $13$-codon finite object obtained in the cardinality and punctured-cube checks. The module decomposition of this row is complete: the six modules are $AGR=\{AGA,AGG\}$, $AR\_Lys=\{AAA\}$, $CUN=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, $Ile\_Met=\{AUA\}$, $SSN=\{UCA,UUA\}$, and $Stop\_Trp=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$. The module audit reports $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The same finite display has spectral values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the recorded finite row satisfies the fitted polynomial relation to numerical tolerance. The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{exact}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found zero exact matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size $20$, giving $p_{exact}=0.0$ and $p_{lower}=0.0035$. These values support the finite-row reading as a non-generic local pattern under the stated nulls, while keeping the interpretation at the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family table.}
The by-family audit evaluates $5$ families. The hit rate above the baseline is
$$
\begin{aligned}
&alternative\_yeast: &&0.8333333333333334,\\
&bacterial\_plastid: &&1.0,\\
&candidate\_other: &&1.0,\\
&mitochondrial: &&1.0,\\
&nuclear\_ciliate: &&1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The pooled leave-one-code-out run evaluates $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The stricter interior count gives hit rate $0.9365079365079365$ in $M\setminus T$, and the boundary-augmented count returns $0.9841269841269841$ in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary. The family split therefore does not concentrate the signal in a single family except for the weaker alternative-yeast value; four of the five families reach $1.0$, and the remaining family remains well above the baseline.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out compatibility.}
The codon-level deletion audit is compatible with the family-level result. It records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. This means the same finite structure that supports the leave-one-code-out table also has a codon-level account in which every module has a predictable or boundary representative. The row is therefore read as a local boundary tile with a complete module classification, not as an unconstrained list of exceptional codons.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or the necessity of BEDC geometry for DNA. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only label readback. No protein fold, physical admissibility condition, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies it.
