\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact, not as a mechanism for translation or protein realization. The BEDC carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated codon-to-label table. The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The experiment isolates a finite boundary set $R$ of size $13$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row reports that $R$ is a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the object being tested is not the whole biological translation system, but a named local tile in the codon-label readback layer.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The external contact is a curated standard genetic-code bridge whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels. Its resolution is codon label readback. This contact can test code-layer readback and local codon tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization. Consequently, the local tile result is a code-read result: it says that a finite six-coordinate codon display, when read through curated labels, exposes a boundary pattern that remains strong under leave-one-code-out tests and family stratification.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
No translation process follows from these rows. No folded protein structure follows from these rows. No physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or law of DNA follows from these rows. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded structure. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality. Any promotion beyond the code-read layer requires a separate higher-layer contact, such as an explicit translation experiment, structure contact, physical-admissibility assay, or function measurement.
