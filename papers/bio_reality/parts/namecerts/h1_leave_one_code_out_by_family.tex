\subsection{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out recovery in the codon cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Claim and coordinate frame.}
This chapter records the finite-row claim $\mathrm{h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family}$ at the code-read layer.  The object under inspection is the standard-code codon table, viewed through a six-bit codon display on the codon cube $Q_6$.  The relevant distinctions are local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The readout is not an organismal mechanism: it is the comparison between finite codon coordinates and the curated codon-to-label table.  The question tested here is whether the leave-one-code-out recovery signal persists after the evaluated non-standard code tables are grouped into biological-code families.

\paragraph{Finite residue set.}
The residue set $R$ contains exactly thirteen codons.  The verified row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$.  The same set is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite carrier seen by this claim is not a free verbal grouping of codons; it is a concrete thirteen-vertex subset with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces inside the six-bit codon cube.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated spectral record gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check is numerically closed to tolerance: the recorded polynomial value is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, and the same number is listed for the cubic residual.  The spectral-radius row therefore supplies a numerical stability check for the local codon pattern, not an independent biological law.

\paragraph{Shape significance rows.}
The finite null comparison for the $M$-shape used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was exactly one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null row preserved amino-acid class structure.  In that experiment $N=2000$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The recorded values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support the claim that the local codon-tile shape is unusual under the tested finite nulls.  They do not convert the coordinate pattern into translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal rule.

\paragraph{Leave-one recovery rows.}
The global leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with its boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The companion leave-one-codon-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ codons with high closure coverage, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the recovery signal is concentrated in the finite local geometry and its boundary behavior, not in a claim that codon geometry itself performs biological translation.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen residue codons are partitioned into six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and there are no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite recovery test.  This matters for the present family-stratified claim because the family comparison is not being made over an uncontrolled residue inventory; it is made after the residue codons have been accounted for module by module.

\paragraph{Family-stratified result.}
The family-level experiment evaluated $5$ families.  The recorded hit rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Four of the five families achieved hit rate $1.0$ above baseline, while the alternative-yeast family remained high at $0.8333333333333334$.  In the finite-row reading, this is evidence that the leave-one-code-out signal is not merely an artifact of pooling all tables together.  It persists after grouping the evaluated tables into families, with one family showing a reduced but still above-baseline row.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only empirical contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-layer readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently, amino-acid label sequences produced by the table remain label sequences.  They are not protein structures, and the CUN, UUR, stop, tryptophan, lysine, arginine, isoleucine, or methionine module names do not by themselves supply organism-matched tRNA evidence, folding evidence, function evidence, or physical admissibility evidence.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
The finite data support the chapter claim at the code-read layer: a thirteen-codon punctured-cube residue set, a closed module classification, significant finite-null rows, and a family-stratified leave-one-code-out table together show a robust local boundary-tile signal in the standard-code label table.  The result is intentionally local.  It says that the window-six codon coordinates expose a stable codon-label recovery pattern across the tested family grouping.  It does not say that the pattern is translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality; each of those higher-layer statements would require a separate contact carrying the appropriate observation.
