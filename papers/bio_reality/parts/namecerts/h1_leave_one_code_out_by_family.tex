\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery by Code Family}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
This record concerns the finite codon carrier obtained from the six-bit codon display of the standard genetic-code table.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated codon-to-label contact.  The contact is deliberately narrow: it supplies the standard-code label table, meaning codon labels such as amino-acid names or stop labels, but it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  The present claim therefore lives at the code-read layer.  It asks whether the window-six coordinate system exposes stable local boundary-tile structure when codon-label changes are withheld by genetic-code family.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The finite boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The observed row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recovered as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the object being tested is not an unrestricted codon table; it is a finite local tile consisting of thirteen displayed codon corners, sixteen displayed local adjacencies, and four displayed two-dimensional faces.  This is the BEDC-minimal row on which the leave-one-code-out experiment is evaluated.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The local row is accompanied by a finite spectral check.  The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic polynomial residual, so the spectral identity is satisfied to numerical tolerance.  The row is also non-generic under two finite null controls.  Against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives, the exact shape count is $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and the high-Jaccard count is $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, which records the finite search pressure rather than a symbolic assumption.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$, the observed module size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed module size is $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out test.}
The family-level row tests the claim identified by \texttt{h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family}.  Across the broader leave-one-code-out experiment, $23$ genetic-code tables supplied $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  Equivalently, the reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The by-family refinement groups the evaluated code tables into $5$ families and reports the following above-baseline family rates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite-row content of the claim: when one code is withheld and code families are used as the grouping variable, the local codon tile remains predictive above the baseline rate in every reported family, with four families at $1.0$ and the alternative-yeast family at $0.8333333333333334$.

\paragraph{Codon-module accounting.}
The related leave-one-codon-out module accounting is complete on the same thirteen-codon boundary row.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification reports $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$ and $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons.  In the codon-level recovery check, $8$ codons have high closure coverage, $8$ are predictable, $5$ are boundary codons, and the recovery rate is $1.0$.  The anchor count is $0$, so the recovery is not being described as an anchor-based mechanism; it is a finite boundary-and-closure readback.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The result supports a local statement only.  Window-six coordinates, closure, spectrum, and finite module accounting expose a stable codon-tile boundary that is useful for code-label readback across code families.  The curated standard-code table is the reality contact that makes the labels meaningful, but it is only a label contact.  No translation realization follows from these rows.  No folded protein structure follows from amino-acid label readback.  No physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from the local tile.  Any claim crossing those boundaries would require a separate contact carrying that higher-layer observation.
