\subsection{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out signal in the codon cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite claim.}
The object tested here is the standard-code codon table after it is placed on the six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$.  A codon is treated as a finite row with a code label, and the only contact with biology used in this section is the curated table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The claim is therefore a code-layer statement: window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile regularity in the rows that differ across code tables, and the family-stratified leave-one-code-out test asks whether that regularity remains visible when one code table is withheld from a family-level comparison.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The distinguished row set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The supplied rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a finite subcomplex in the codon cube, the same row set is reported as a punctured cube with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the section is not reading an unrestricted biological mechanism from the table.  It is reading a small labelled boundary object: thirteen codon rows, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four local two-dimensional faces inside the six-bit display.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The finite spectral row gives three numerical anchors.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The associated radial cubic check has residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the supplied floating-point witness is numerically consistent at the stated tolerance.  The row does not by itself say that codon biology optimizes this spectrum; it says that the finite coordinate object used by the probe has a reproducible spectral scale and that the reported radius calculation closes numerically for this data snapshot.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the shape null reported for $N=2000$ sampled trials with seed $42$, the exact boundary shape appeared once.  The exact-shape rate is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=1/2000=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was reached $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found no exact shape match, an observed $M$ size of $20$, and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below that observed size.  Its reported rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support a finite enrichment statement for the local shape.  They do not identify a molecular cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  When the boundary rule is used, the reported hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-stratified row refines this by splitting the code tables into five families.  The finite family rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family count is $5$.  Four families are saturated at the reported resolution, while the alternative-yeast family remains positive but not saturated.  The empirical content of this subsection is exactly that family-level split: the local codon-tile rule is not merely a pooled-table artifact in the finite row supplied here.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out module check.}
A separate row removes one codon at a time.  It reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for all thirteen $R$ rows with no extras, no missing module rows, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The accounting line is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13,\qquad \mathrm{assigned}=13,\qquad \mathrm{unassigned}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
This makes the local boundary interpretation precise: every reported module contains a predictable row or a boundary row under the supplied classification.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of inference.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code label table.  It supports codon-to-label readback and local label-split tests.  It does not observe ribosomal realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, evolutionary mechanism, or universality.  Therefore no claim of translation, folding, physical viability, function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows from the rows above unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is still only a label sequence at this level; it is not a protein structure and not a functional molecule.  The finite conclusion is narrower: in the supplied code-layer data, the thirteen-row punctured-cube boundary, its module decomposition, and the family-stratified leave-one-code-out rates form a reproducible local pattern tied to curated codon labels.
