\paragraph{Finite-row object.}
This record concerns a window-six codon display on the codon cube $Q_6$, read only at the code layer. The carrier is the finite six-bit codon coordinate system, while the external reality contact is the curated standard-code label table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. The local BEDC-side object is not a translation mechanism. It is a finite-row probe for whether the standard-code readback exposes a stable boundary-tile pattern when codon labels are compared against neighboring code tables.

The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is witnessed as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the finite object is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it is a local tile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ square faces in the six-bit coordinate display.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The row also carries a spectral summary. The finite computation reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the associated cubic or radial polynomial residual equal to $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$. This residual is numerically at tolerance scale for the finite calculation, so the spectral datum is treated as a stable coordinate readout, not as an independent biological law.

The observed $M$-shape has size $20$. Against the shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite-row audit records both the small tail and the sampling pressure. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, so $p_{\mathrm{aa,class,exact}}=0.0$, while the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{aa,class,lower}}=0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family result.}
The chapter-specific claim is the family-stratified leave-one-code-out test. The run evaluated $5$ code families. The hit rates above the baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other} &= 1.0,\\

$$
Thus every reported family exceeds the baseline, and four of the five reported families attain full above-baseline recovery in this finite-row sense. The least recovered family is alternative yeast, still with above-baseline hit rate $5/6=0.8333333333333334$.

The family result sits inside the broader leave-one-code-out run over $23$ evaluated tables and $63$ total targets. That broader run reports baseline hit rate $0.19821739130434785$, hit rate in $M\setminus T$ equal to $0.9365079365079365$, and hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary equal to $0.9841269841269841$. The reported hit rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$. The family-stratified table therefore does not stand alone: it refines a finite recovery pattern in which almost all targets are recovered once the local $M$-row and boundary row are allowed to participate.

\paragraph{Codon-module accounting.}
The leave-one-codon-out module classification is complete for the same $13$ boundary codons. Its accounting reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons. The six finite modules are

$$
Every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the reported classification. The companion leave-one-codon-out recovery row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. In this record, the codon-level and code-family-level audits point to the same finite interpretation: the signal is a local code-read boundary phenomenon in the codon coordinate system.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, at codon-label resolution. It supports the observed fact that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels, and it allows local split checks against the finite codon tile. It does not supply translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality. Therefore the admissible conclusion is restricted: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile pattern whose label readback is tested against curated code tables.

No stronger biological conclusion follows from this row. In particular, the local codon tile is not a global biological law; window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, or function; and amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure. Any future claim crossing from code readback to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality must introduce a separate higher-layer contact and pass a separate mismatch test.
