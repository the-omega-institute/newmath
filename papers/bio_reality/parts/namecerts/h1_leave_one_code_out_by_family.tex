\subsection{Null Checks and Boundary of Interpretation}
\label{sec:bior-reality-null-boundary}

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the reported null sampling, the observed shape is rare.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p\_\mathrm{exact}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p\_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The run required $57377$ distinct sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-shape $p$-value was $0.0$, and the lower-tail $p$-value was $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The result is a finite code-read record.  It says that a six-bit codon display, together with the curated standard-code table, exposes a local tile whose leave-one-code-out family recovery is high above the stated baseline.  It does not say that the geometry causes translation, realizes protein structure, proves physical admissibility, determines function, supplies a biochemical mechanism, or establishes universality across biology.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation.  A separate translation contact would be needed for translation claims; a separate structural contact would be needed for folding or structural-order claims; and separate physical or functional contacts would be needed for admissibility, mechanism, or function claims.
