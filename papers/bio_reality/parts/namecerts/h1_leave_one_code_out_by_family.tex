\subsection{Family-resolved leave-one-code-out stability for the codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon label table read through a six-coordinate codon display.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinates grouped as codon positions and nucleotide bits.  The observed contact supplies only the assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The internal construction then asks whether the rows whose labels move across known code variants occupy a coherent local region of the cube, and whether the same region remains predictive after one genetic-code table is withheld.

The distinguished row set has cardinality $13$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a subcomplex of the six-coordinate cube, this row set has finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object is not a verbal pattern over labels alone: the row set occupies $13$ vertices, carries $16$ local adjacencies, and contains $4$ two-dimensional tile faces.  This is the local geometric substrate on which the leave-one-code-out test is evaluated.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The associated finite operator separates the marked region from its complement with spectral values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically zero at the recorded tolerance.  The same value is reported for the cubic-polynomial residual, so the spectral summary is internally consistent with the finite operator calculation rather than being a post hoc label statistic.

The randomization audit used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  For the exact shape criterion, only $1$ sampled table matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
For the high-overlap criterion, $25$ sampled tables had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and rejected $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite-row audit records both the observed rarity and the sampling pressure needed to obtain the $2000$ distinct comparison rows.  A second null preserving amino-acid classes also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It produced $0$ exact shape matches, while $7$ samples fell at or below the observed marked size $|M|=20$, giving lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These two null checks support the claim that the codon tile is not an arbitrary artifact of row labeling under the tested finite randomizations.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The family-resolved test withholds one code table at a time and asks whether the withheld changes land back in the region predicted by the remaining code tables and the local boundary of the tile.  Across the non-family aggregate test, $23$ tables and $63$ targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  Restricting to the marked region without the withheld table gave hit rate $0.9365079365079365$, and adding the local boundary raised the hit rate to $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$, so nearly all withheld targets were recovered by the finite local construction plus its boundary rows.

The family-resolved row contains $5$ code families.  Their hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The only family below perfect recovery is alternative yeast, and even there the rate is $5/6$.  The result therefore says something more specific than a single pooled success rate: the local tile remains stable across all five named families, with four of the five families achieving complete recovery in the recorded finite test.

\paragraph{Module-level localization.}
The codon-level module audit assigns all $13$ rows of $R$ with no missing or extra codons.  The complete partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no rows missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has at least one predictable or boundary row.  The leave-one-codon-out run is consistent with this localization: it records $8$ predictable rows, $5$ boundary rows, no anchors, closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Interpretation at the code layer.}
The result is a finite code-read statement.  The standard-code table is used as a curated contact for codon labels, and the six-coordinate codon geometry organizes those labels into a local tile with a tested boundary.  The family-resolved leave-one-code-out row shows that the tile is not merely fit to one table after the fact: withholding code families still returns the altered targets to the same local region at rates far above the recorded baseline.

This does not promote the tile into a biological mechanism.  The data in this record do not establish translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal law of genetic coding.  Amino-acid label readback is still only label readback.  Any claim about folding, organism-specific translation machinery, tRNA-copy usage, physical feasibility, or realized function would require a separate observation of that higher layer.  In the present finite row, the justified conclusion is narrower: window-six codon coordinates expose a stable local boundary structure for standard-code label changes, and the family-resolved leave-one-code-out calculation supports that code-layer regularity without crossing into mechanism or structure.
