\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The row studies a six-coordinate codon display for the standard genetic-code label table.  The contact used here is only the curated assignment of each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The finite carrier is the $64$-vertex codon cube, with local tests made on a distinguished boundary set $R$ and on the observed label geometry of the standard table.  The verified boundary set has cardinality $13$ and is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row verifies that this $R$ is a punctured-cube boundary with face vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the object under test is not an unrestricted biological narrative: it is a finite codon-label configuration with a named boundary, a finite list of codons, and a local combinatorial shape.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape evidence.}
The boundary calculation is accompanied by a spectrum row.  The recorded values are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The checked radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the stated spectral relation is numerically saturated at the recorded tolerance.  Shape significance was tested against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  The exact observed shape occurred once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$; the high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sample log records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which is important because the row is finite but the sampling space has a high duplicate burden under the chosen constraints.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module size is $20$, exact-shape matches are $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  Hence the row reports $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$ for that constrained null.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family test.}
The claim-specific row groups alternative code tables by family and asks whether the local codon-window rule still recovers the held-out reassignment above the baseline rate.  The verified family count is $5$.  The family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
This is the narrow content of the row: the code-family split is not merely pooled success but is visible separately in five named families, with four perfect family rates and one non-perfect but high rate for alternative yeast.  The pooled leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  These numbers support a local recovery statement for code-label changes, not a statement about molecular realization.

\paragraph{Codon-level module accounting.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out row reports recovery rate $1.0$, predictable count $8$, boundary count $5$, anchor count $0$, and high closure coverage $8$.  The module accounting is complete on the same $13$ codons: $\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  The verification row records $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, and empty lists for extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons.  This gives the family-level claim a codon-level audit trail: every boundary codon used by the family test is assigned to exactly one listed module.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only reality contact in this row is the curated standard-code table as a codon-label readback source.  It can test whether a codon coordinate receives an amino-acid or stop label, and it can test whether a local tile split matches that label table.  It does not observe translation in a cell, folding of an amino-acid chain, thermodynamic admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or a universal law of genetic coding.  Therefore the row licenses the following finite statement: the six-coordinate codon display exposes a strong local boundary pattern for standard-code labels and for leave-one-code-out family recovery, with the listed numerical rates and module partition.  Any promotion from this code-label layer to translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate contact that directly measures that higher layer.
