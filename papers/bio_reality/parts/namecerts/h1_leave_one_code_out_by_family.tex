\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Family}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The object of this section is the standard-code codon table read as a finite label table on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is treated only as a coordinate-bearing row with an externally curated amino-acid or stop label.  The local data used here are the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class.  The biological contact is therefore only code-layer readback: a curated table assigns codons to labels.  It is not a contact with translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Distinguished boundary set.}
The finite boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The supplied row audit gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row set is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not being inferred from an unlimited biological principle.  It is a finite subset of the codon cube, read against a finite external table.

$$
There were $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving

\begin{aligned}

$$
A second null row preserving amino-acid classes reports observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, and

\begin{aligned}

p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.

These rows say that the observed finite tile is rare under the tested finite nulls.  They do not say that the tile is a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The tile-based hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus the finite coordinate construction recovers nearly all held-out code-table targets once the local boundary correction is allowed.  The statement is deliberately code-layered: the recovered item is a codon-label row, not a biochemical realization of translation and not a folded amino-acid sequence.

The stratification supports the local codon-window reading because it survives the family partition rather than appearing only in the pooled row.

Every module has predictable-or-boundary status.  This gives a row-wise explanation of why the family-stratified leave-one-code-out test can remain high: every codon in the $13$-row boundary set is accounted for by a named finite module.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The supported statement is narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose code-label readback is stable under the listed leave-one-code-out tests and under the five-family stratification.  The curated standard-code table supplies the reality contact for labels only.  No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity of DNA geometry follows from these rows.  Any such higher-layer statement requires a separate contact whose observed rows directly test that higher layer.
