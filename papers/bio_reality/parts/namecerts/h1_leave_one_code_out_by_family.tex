\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table read through the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  A codon is treated only as a finite coordinate row with a curated label, and the permitted distinctions are the six-bit display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The observed boundary set is the thirteen-row set
$R=\{AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}$.
The cardinality audit gives $|R|=13$, and the punctured-cube audit gives the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not an arbitrary codon list: it is a finite punctured-cube boundary pattern with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces.  This is the BioReality contact point for the claim: the geometry is internal to the row construction, while the amino-acid and stop labels are supplied by the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only declared reality contact is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code-layer readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, structural order, or universal mechanism.  Consequently, the finite-row result stays at the code-read layer.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.  The proper conclusion is narrower and fully finite: in the supplied six-bit codon-coordinate audit, the thirteen-row punctured-cube boundary and its complete module classification support high leave-one-code-out recovery by family against the recorded baselines, while all higher biological claims remain blocked.
