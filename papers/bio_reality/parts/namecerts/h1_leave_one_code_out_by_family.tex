\paragraph{Finite setting.}
The object under inspection is the standard-code codon label table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_{6}$, with each codon represented by a six-bit display and with labels read from a curated standard genetic-code table.  The relevant distinctions are coordinate position, closure behavior, spectrum, and relation membership.  The construction separates singleton label corners from three-point label classes and then asks whether local tile boundaries remain visible when one genetic-code table is held out from a family-indexed comparison.  The contact supplies codon-to-label assignments only; it does not observe ribosomal translation, folded structure, chemical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal law.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The strongest supported reading is a code-layer result.  The six-bit codon display, the punctured-cube relation set, the spectral values, the null-model comparisons, the leave-one-code-out family rates, and the module accounting all point to a stable local boundary pattern in the curated codon-label table.  The primary row for this claim records $5$ families and gives above-baseline hit rates from $0.8333333333333334$ to $1.0$, while the related table-level row gives $23$ tables, $63$ targets, and a $0.9841269841269841$ above-baseline rate.  Those numbers are enough to state a finite local readback claim for the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Local limit of the claim.}
No higher biological layer is inferred from these rows.  The curated table contact reports codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels; it does not report translation realization, tRNA availability, ribosomal kinetics, folded structure, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, evolutionary mechanism, or universality across all biological contexts.  A future structural, physical, functional, or mechanistic statement would require a separate contact that directly observes that layer.  Until such a contact is supplied, the result remains a finite code-read boundary statement: local codon-tile structure is visible in the tested label table, but the table itself is not evidence for folded protein structure or a global biological necessity.
