\subsection{Leave-one-code-out stability by code family}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Finite-row object.}
The finite object in this chapter is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with a curated standard-code label readback.  The coordinates are BEDC-side finite data, while the amino-acid and stop labels are external contacts supplied by the curated code table.  The distinguished residual row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite count check gives $|R|=13$.  The same row is read as a punctured-cube boundary tile with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the chapter is not about all codons uniformly; it is about a named thirteen-point boundary tile inside the six-bit codon display, together with label readback from the external standard-code table.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The associated finite spectral row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is numerically negligible at $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, with the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check.  The Monte Carlo shape check used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$.  Among those samples there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The distinct-sample attempt count was $57377$, with $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite search was highly constrained by the row space rather than by an unconstrained continuum.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null row.}
A second null model preserves amino-acid class information.  In this control, the observed module size was $20$.  The run used $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact shape matches, and found $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The resulting finite-row probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This says that the local shape is not merely recovered by preserving coarse label class.  It remains a finite boundary phenomenon visible after a stricter label-preserving shuffle, but the observation is still only a code-read observation.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out aggregate row.}
The aggregate leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  Written as the finite-row contrast, the observed lift is
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841 - 0.19821739130434785
  &= 0.7859095928226363.
\end{aligned}
$$
The number is used here only as a code-layer recovery statistic: leaving one code table out usually keeps the target in the computed local tile or its boundary extension.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The family-stratified row has $5$ code families.  The hit-rate-above-baseline values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Four of the five families are saturated at $1.0$ on this statistic, while the alternative-yeast family is lower at $0.8333333333333334$.  The family mean is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{0.8333333333333334+1+1+1+1}{5}
  &= 0.9666666666666667.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family minimum is $0.8333333333333334$ and the maximum is $1.0$.  This makes the family row a stability statement rather than a uniformity statement: the local boundary tile persists across the listed families, but the finite strength is not identical in every family.

\paragraph{Codon-module readback.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, closure coverage high at $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification partitions all $13$ residual codons without extras or omissions.  The six named modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row has $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has a predictable or boundary status.  This is the local combinatorial reason the leave-one-code-out family statistic is meaningful: the row is not a loose list of exceptional codons, but a completely assigned finite module table.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of the claim.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the chapter claims only that a window-six codon coordinate system exposes a stable local boundary-tile pattern across the listed leave-one-code-out families.  No statement about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer observation.
