\subsection{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out recovery}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite coordinate display for the $64$ codons of the standard genetic code.  The contact used here is only the curated codon-to-label table: it assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label, and it supplies the code-layer labels against which finite BEDC rows can be read.  The local object under test is not the whole biological translation process.  It is a finite boundary tile inside $Q_6$, with singleton label corners and three-point label classes distinguished by coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R={}&\{\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
|R|={}&13.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured-cube row its reported $f$-vector is $(13,16,4)$.  The module classification covers all $13$ codons in $R$, with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},&
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},&
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},&
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either predictable support or boundary support, so the module-level row is closed as a finite classification fact rather than as a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model checks.}
The row is accompanied by a finite spectral signature.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,\\
P(\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $P(\mu)$ denotes the reported cubic, equivalently radial, polynomial residual.  The residual is numerically at tolerance scale, so the spectral row is treated as internally coherent for the finite computation.

The shape is not a frequent occurrence under the reported null draws.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$.  The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the finite local tile is a structured code-layer object relative to the tested null families.  They do not support any promotion to a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The family-stratified leave-one-code-out row evaluates $5$ code families.  Its family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The unstratified leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family row therefore says that the recovery phenomenon is not concentrated in a single named family: four of the five family bins report perfect above-baseline recovery, and the remaining alternative-yeast bin still reports $5/6=0.8333333333333334$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out compatibility.}
The codon-level leave-one-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate $1.0$.  This agrees with the module classification: every one of the $13$ codons in $R$ is assigned to one of the six modules, and no module codon is left outside the classification.  The finite interpretation is that local boundary membership and predictable closure are enough to recover the recorded code-layer labels in the tested leave-one-out rows.

\paragraph{Readback boundary.}
The result is a code-read statement about a curated standard-code table.  It says that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile pattern whose family-stratified leave-one-code-out rates are strongly above the listed baseline.  It does not say that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not establish translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or universality of the genetic code.  Amino-acid label readback is still only label readback.  Any claim about translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate contact supplying that higher-layer observation.
