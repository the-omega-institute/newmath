\subsection{Familywise leave-one-code-out stability for the window-six codon tile}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed through the six-coordinate codon display $Q_6$.  The coordinates supply a finite combinatorial carrier, while the amino-acid and stop labels are read from a curated standard-code table.  Thus the contact tested here is a code-label contact: it can compare codons, local faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes, but it does not observe translation dynamics, folded structure, physical feasibility, biochemical function, a causal mechanism, or universality across life.  Any statement crossing those layers would require an additional contact not present in this record.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}

$$
\begin{aligned}

\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,&
\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The associated radial cubic residual was $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is numerically at tolerance for this finite computation.  Shape significance was checked against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives.  The sampling run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,&
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and $7$ samples lay in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The corresponding values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,&
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite numbers support a nontrivial local code-label pattern, not a biological necessity claim.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The global leave-one-code-out evaluation covered $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ after adding the boundary allowance was $0.9841269841269841$.  The codon-level leave-one-out row is compatible with this result: it recorded boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, anchor count $0$, and recovery rate $1.0$.

\paragraph{Familywise result.}
For the present claim, the leave-one-code-out result was stratified into $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus four of the five family strata reached perfect above-baseline recovery in this finite evaluation, while the alternative-yeast family remained above the baseline but below perfect recovery.  The chapter-specific claim is therefore that the window-six local tile is stable under this familywise leave-one-code-out test at the code-read layer.  The claim stops there: it licenses a finite codon-label regularity check against a curated code table, and it does not license statements about translation realization, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic explanation, or universal law.
