\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Code Family}\label{sec:bior-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record concerns a finite code-read experiment on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism; it is a coordinate display whose vertices are codon labels.  The external contact is the curated standard-code table, used only as a codon-to-label readback bridge.  The admissible readback layer is therefore code_read: codons may be compared by their displayed amino-acid or stop labels, and local tile boundary behavior may be tested against that table.  No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from this contact unless an independent higher-layer contact supplies it.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object being tested is not the full genetic code and not the full codon cube.  It is a finite boundary tile cut out of the six-bit display, with thirteen codon vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The finite-row spectrum supplies a numerical scale for the tile.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial or cubic polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is numerically zero at the reported tolerance.  This supports the internal consistency of the finite coordinate computation, but it still remains a code-layer statistic.  A spectral radius in the codon display is not a folding observable, an energetic admissibility test, or a translation mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite null sample for the $M$-shape comparison, the experiment used $N=2000$ samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$ samples.  It observed $0$ exact shape matches, lower-tail count $7$, and observed $M$-size $20$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values say that the displayed tile is sparse under the reported finite nulls.  They do not say that the geometry is a global law of biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out test.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The observed hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, while the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The recorded rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  In finite-row terms, the boundary tile recovers almost all of the held-out code targets once the local boundary allowance is included:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M-T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M-T+\partial)&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the operational content of the claim: local codon-window geometry has predictive code-read structure across held-out code tables.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The by-family audit partitions the leave-one-code-out evidence into $5$ code families.  The family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The weaker family is alternative_yeast, but it remains above the baseline rate.  The other four named families reach the reported ceiling value $1.0$.  Hence the finite-row evidence is not merely a pooled aggregate; it survives the displayed family split at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Codon-module coverage.}
The leave-one-codon-out module audit reports predictable_count $8$, boundary_count $5$, anchor_count $0$, closure_coverage_high $8$, and recovery_rate $1.0$.  The complete module assignment covers all $13$ codons of $R$, with assigned_count $13$, no extra_module_codons, no missing_from_modules, and no unassigned_codons.  The certified modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has either a predictable or boundary status.  This closes the finite bookkeeping for the local tile: the thirteen boundary codons are not left as an unclassified list but are exhausted by six named code-read modules.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The conclusion is deliberately narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the curated code-label table, with a thirteen-codon punctured-cube row, a stable finite spectrum, significant shape statistics under the stated nulls, high leave-one-code-out recovery, and complete module coverage.  The same data also block stronger readings.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, protein structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any claim beyond code_read must introduce a separate reality contact and must be tested at that higher layer rather than inferred from this finite tile.
