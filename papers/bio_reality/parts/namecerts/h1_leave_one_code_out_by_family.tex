\paragraph{Finite codon window.}
The packet studies the standard-code codon table through a six-coordinate codon display.  The mathematical carrier is the finite codon cube $Q_6$, with vertices read as codon labels after the usual three-base grouping.  The contact used here is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  That contact is enough to test code-layer label readback and local tile splits, but it is not a measurement of translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanistic necessity, or universality.

The distinguished residual set has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured-cube object it carries the finite face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row under test is not a vague biological analogy.  It is a concrete finite subset of the codon cube, with explicit vertices and explicit low-dimensional incidence data.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}

The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is at numerical zero for this computation.  These numbers are used only as finite diagnostics for the displayed codon geometry.  They do not certify a biochemical process.

Two null comparisons locate how unusual the observed shape is under finite randomization.  In the first control, $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$ required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\qquad
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ samples at Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving control, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-row size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0,
\qquad
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The available finite data therefore support the weaker statement that the codon-window row is geometrically non-generic relative to the listed controls.  They do not support a stronger claim about why a cell realizes a translation system.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family test.}
The family-stratified leave-one-code-out row evaluates whether the local codon-window pattern remains visible when genetic-code variants are held out by family.  The aggregate leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.

The family-level row has $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The chapter claim is exactly this finite claim: under the supplied family partition, the leave-one-code-out probe mostly recovers the target pattern above the baseline rate, with four families at $1.0$ and the alternative-yeast family at $5/6$.  The one non-unit family score is important because it keeps the row empirical rather than tautological: the table contact supplies labels, and the finite computation reports where the local rule succeeds.

This matters because the family result is not floating over an unspecified codon subset: every codon in the residual row is placed into a named finite module, and each module has a predictable or boundary status.

\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.}
The supported reading is local and code-layered.  The six-coordinate codon display, the residual row $R$, the module partition, the spectral diagnostics, and the leave-one-code-out family rates together show that the curated code labels align with a finite local tile pattern more often than the listed baselines.  The contact does not observe ribosomal execution, aminoacylation, tRNA abundance, organism-specific physiology, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, or function.  Consequently the row may be used as evidence for a local codon-label split in the standard-code readback layer, and not as evidence that the geometry is a biological law or a mechanism of life.

If a later packet wants to speak about translation realization, folded proteins, physical constraints, or functional selection, it must attach a separate contact that directly observes that layer.  Until then, the present row remains a finite code-table result: strong enough to record family-stratified recovery above baseline, explicit enough to audit by codon and module, and too narrow to license higher-layer biological conclusions.
