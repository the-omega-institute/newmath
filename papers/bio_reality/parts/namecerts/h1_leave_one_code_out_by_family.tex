The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the external label table which assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. Thus the chapter-specific object is not an arbitrary subset of the codon cube: it is a thirteen-codon boundary row with a fixed local tile profile, a standard-code label readback, and a family-level leave-one-code-out probe.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge: a table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact tests code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Therefore the chapter does not claim that DNA obeys this geometry as a global law, that window-six geometry explains translation, or that an amino-acid label sequence determines folded protein structure.  Any promotion from code-read stability to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate reality contact with its own finite-row evidence.
