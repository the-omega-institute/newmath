\paragraph{Finite code-read setting.}
This BioReality record concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read object. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated codon-to-label table whose labels are amino-acid names or stop. The contact is deliberately narrow: it supplies codon label readback and permits tests of local tile splits, but it is not a contact for translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity. Thus every claim in this subsection is a statement about finite rows, codon labels, and local coordinate structure. No claim is made that window-six geometry is itself translation, protein structure, admissible chemistry, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
The record supports a code-layer statement: in the six-bit codon display, the curated standard-code table exposes a local boundary-tile pattern whose leave-one-code-out recovery remains high across the five reported code families. The strongest family row is $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate; the weakest displayed family row is $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast. The global leave-one-code-out row has $63$ targets over $23$ tables and hit rate above baseline $0.9841269841269841$. These finite facts justify the local code-read claim and the boundary warning simultaneously: the codon tile is a real finite signal relative to the curated table contact, but no translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.
