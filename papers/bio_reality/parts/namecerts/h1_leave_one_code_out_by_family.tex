\subsection{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out recovery for the codon boundary tile}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
This record concerns the claim \texttt{h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family}.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube, with each codon displayed as a word over the four nucleotide letters and read only through a curated standard-code label table.  The relevant exceptional set $R$ has exactly $13$ codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row verifies that this set is the punctured-cube region with face-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the computation is not about all codons uniformly; it isolates a small boundary-shaped part of the codon display and asks whether code-table changes remain concentrated near that finite pattern.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape calibration.}
The row includes a numerical spectral check for the associated finite adjacency data.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the radial cubic residual equal to $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$.  The small residual is used only as a finite numerical consistency check for the displayed boundary geometry.  It is not a biological mechanism.  Shape significance was assessed against $2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  Among $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ were duplicate retries, $1$ exact shape match was observed, and $25$ samples had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite Monte Carlo rates were $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$ and $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null comparison.}
A second null row preserves amino-acid class while testing the same kind of shape concentration.  In $2000$ samples with seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, exact-shape matches were $0$, and only $7$ samples fell in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size.  The finite rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrower statement that the observed code-label split is unusually concentrated under the stated finite controls.  They do not identify translation chemistry, folding behavior, fitness, or organism-level causation.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out aggregate result.}
The broader leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  Interpreted conservatively, this says that when one code table is held out, the finite coordinate construction tends to place the held-out target in the local region singled out by the boundary-and-closure computation far more often than the baseline rate.

\paragraph{Family-stratified result.}
The family-stratified row divides the same leave-one-code-out question into $5$ table families.  The family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\

The finite conclusion is therefore not merely an aggregate artifact of one pooled table.  Four of the five listed families attain perfect above-baseline recovery in the recorded run, while the alternative-yeast family remains high at $5/6$.  The claim certified here is exactly this family-level stability of the leave-one-code-out boundary signal.

\paragraph{Codon-level module accounting.}
The codon-module classification is complete on $R$: the row records $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has a predictable or boundary member according to the recorded row.  A companion leave-one-codon-out test reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure codons, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This codon-level accounting is important because the family-level table result is meaningful only if the exceptional codons themselves have been fully assigned to the finite modules being measured.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is adequate for code-label readback and for checking whether finite codon-coordinate regions align with table-level label changes.  It is not a translation experiment, a protein-structure observation, a physical-admissibility assay, a function assay, or evidence for a universal biological law.  Amino-acid label sequences obtained from the table remain code-layer outputs.  No conclusion about folded structure, cellular realization, biochemical mechanism, organismal fitness, or universality follows from these rows unless another independent contact supplies that higher-layer observation.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
The finite-row evidence supports the following bounded statement.  In the six-coordinate codon display, the verified $13$-codon punctured-cube region and its completed module assignment form a stable local boundary pattern for leave-one-code-out recovery.  Across $23$ evaluated tables and $63$ targets, the recovery rate near $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$ against a baseline of $0.19821739130434785$, and the family-stratified rates remain high across all $5$ recorded families.  The result is therefore ready as a code-read claim about a finite local codon-tile boundary, with the stated higher-layer biological interpretations explicitly withheld.
