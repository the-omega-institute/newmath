\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Family Stratification for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite display of codon labels.  The reality contact is the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label, and it supplies no observation of translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The chapter therefore studies a code-read phenomenon.  A local codon-tile pattern may be compared with curated labels, but the comparison is not a biological law and is not evidence that DNA must obey the geometry of $Q_6$.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The admissible conclusion is narrow.  Window-six coordinates, closure data, spectra, and family-stratified leave-one-code-out checks expose a local codon-label boundary tile in the curated code table.  They do not show that translation is realized, that a protein folds, that a sequence is physically admissible, that a biological function exists, that a universal mechanism is present, or that any higher biological layer follows from the finite geometry alone.  Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate reality contact with the corresponding resolution.  In the present row, the contact resolution is codon label readback only.
