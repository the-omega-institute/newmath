\subsection{Family-resolved leave-one-code-out boundary test}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and readout.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer. The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-bit display of codon positions and bases. The relevant distinctions are local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class. The table contact supplies codon-to-label assignments only: each codon is assigned to an amino-acid label or a stop label, and the row is allowed to test whether a local window-six pattern separates label changes near the observed standard-code boundary. It does not by itself observe translation execution, folded structure, chemical admissibility, biological role, evolutionary mechanism, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Observed punctured-cube boundary.}
The finite boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. The codons reported in the row are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same list is certified again by the punctured-cube row, where the face-vector is $(13,16,4)$. Thus the finite object used in this chapter is not an arbitrary codon subset: it is the observed thirteen-codon boundary pattern with sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies and four two-dimensional faces in the local cube geometry. The packet reports the companion $M$ object through its size and spectral statistics rather than through an explicit codon inventory; the amino-acid-class preserving null row records $|M|=20$.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The spectral row assigns numerical scale to the two finite pieces. The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is reported as the radial polynomial residual. This is numerically negligible at the precision of the run, so the spectrum is being used as a stable finite signature of the local configuration rather than as a biological mechanism. The row therefore supports a geometric summary of the code labels but does not promote that summary to a claim about ribosomal realization or protein structure.

\paragraph{Null comparison for shape.}
Against the finite null model used for the $M$ shape test, the run sampled $N=2000$ cases with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. These values show that the observed local shape is rare in that finite comparison class. A second amino-acid-class preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact matches, $7$ lower-tail cases at or below the observed size $|M|=20$, $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$. The finite evidence is therefore strongest as a code-label geometry statement: the observed code readout has a non-generic local boundary pattern under the sampled controls.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family result.}
The family-resolved leave-one-code-out row is the direct claim of this chapter. It evaluates $5$ families and reports the following hit rates above the baseline:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &\mapsto 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &\mapsto 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &\mapsto 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &\mapsto 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &\mapsto 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The aggregate leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The same run reports $0.9365079365079365$ for hits in $M\setminus T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ after adding the boundary. The family split therefore does not merely restate an aggregate count: four of the five families reach a perfect reported rate, and the remaining alternative-yeast family still exceeds five sixths.

\paragraph{Codon-level recovery and module coverage.}
The leave-one-codon-out row supplies the local recovery check. It reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, closure coverage high at $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. The module-classification row partitions all $13$ boundary codons with no missing or extra codons: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, extra module codons $\varnothing$, missing-from-modules $\varnothing$, and unassigned codons $\varnothing$. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite row. This matters because the family-level result is not isolated from the codon-level account: the same thirteen codons are exhausted by named modules, and the recovery statistic is complete at the level tested by the table.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The supported conclusion is narrow. Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary tile in the standard-code label table, and leave-one-code-out tests remain high when grouped by code family. The curated table is the contact that makes the labels observable; the coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data organize those labels into a finite local pattern. The result is therefore a code-read statement about the codon table. It is not evidence that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity, that translation is realized by the finite pattern, that amino-acid label strings determine folded proteins, that the labels are physically admissible in any particular context, that a biological function follows, or that the local tile is a universal mechanism. Any such stronger claim would need a separate contact at the corresponding layer.
