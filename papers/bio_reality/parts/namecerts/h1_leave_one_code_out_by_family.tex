\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table read through a six-coordinate codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon treated as a six-bit word after the usual four-letter nucleotide alphabet has been placed into a binary coordinate scheme.  The finite distinctions used here are a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal operations are coordinate placement, closure inside the six-dimensional cube, a spectral readout, and a relation between the recovered codon set and curated amino-acid or stop labels.  The biological table is used only as the label contact: it tells us which codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code, and it does not by itself describe translation machinery, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The finite data support the following restricted statement.  In the six-coordinate codon cube, the standard-code label table exposes a local boundary-tile pattern with a thirteen-codon punctured-cube set, a small random-shape probability under two sampled null comparisons, stable spectral coordinates, complete module accounting, high leave-one-code-out recovery, and family-level hit rates ranging from $0.8333333333333334$ to $1.0$.  The observed object is a code-layer readback: codons are matched to curated amino-acid or stop labels, and the local geometry is tested against those labels.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
No higher biological layer follows from this row alone.  The curated table supplies codon-to-label assignments, but it does not observe ribosomal translation, molecular folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a universal law of genetic-code formation.  Amino-acid label sequences are not folded proteins.  CUN, UUR, stop, tryptophan, arginine, lysine, isoleucine, and methionine module names remain code labels unless a separate experiment supplies the corresponding organismal, structural, or functional contact.  Consequently the family-level leave-one-code-out result should be recorded as a finite code-read regularity, not as a mechanism claim.
