\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Code Family}\label{subsec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate setting.}
The packet tests a six-coordinate codon display against curated standard-code labels.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite table of $64$ codon positions with labels supplied by the standard genetic code.  The point of the experiment is not to derive biology from the cube.  It is to ask whether a local codon-window construction keeps a stable boundary signal when one compares code tables family by family, while treating the label table as the measured contact.  The observed special region $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row reports the face-count profile $(13,16,4)$ for this punctured-cube shape.  Thus the local object under discussion is concrete: it is a thirteen-codon subset with sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies and four two-dimensional faces, not an unconstrained verbal pattern.

\paragraph{Baseline spectral and randomization checks.}
The shape is accompanied by a spectral row.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with radial cubic residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the finite spectral calculation is internally consistent to numerical tolerance.  The shape-randomization control used $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second control preserving amino-acid class data also used $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$.  It had observed module size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers justify treating the displayed local tile as a nontrivial finite signal at the code-read layer.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Family-level leave-one-code-out result.}
The family split evaluates whether the leave-one-code-out behavior persists after grouping code tables into named families.  The row contains five families.  Their hit rates above the baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The non-family aggregate row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The stricter interior rate was $0.9365079365079365$ for $M\setminus T$, and the rate became $0.9841269841269841$ after adding the boundary band.  The family row therefore says that the leave-one-code-out signal is not concentrated in a single named code family: four of the five families reach $1.0$, and the remaining family still exceeds $5/6$.

\paragraph{Codon-level module accounting.}
The related leave-one-codon-out row supplies a second finite check.  It reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification assigns all thirteen codons in $R$ with no missing or extra codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The completeness row states $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, and empty lists for extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons.  In particular, the family-level result is attached to a fully enumerated thirteen-codon support, not to an unresolved module remainder.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The measured claim is that the window-six codon coordinate can expose a stable local boundary-tile pattern when standard-code labels are supplied as curated observations.  The support is finite and row-based: a thirteen-codon punctured-cube region, a spectral profile, randomization controls, a leave-one-code-out aggregate across $23$ tables, a five-family stability split, and a complete module assignment of the same thirteen codons.  The alternative-yeast family is the weakest family row at $0.8333333333333334$, so it should be reported as a partial but still high family-level support rather than hidden inside the four perfect family rates.

\paragraph{Scope limit.}
This chapter only licenses a code-layer readback statement.  The curated label table records codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop assignments, and the experiment checks how those labels interact with the local $Q_6$ tile.  It does not observe translation in a cell, folded protein structure, physical admissibility of molecules, biological function, a generative mechanism, or a universal law of genetic coding.  Any assertion crossing one of those boundaries would require a separate contact designed for that higher layer.  In particular, amino-acid label sequences read from codons are not protein folds, and the CUN/UUR or tRNA-Leu wording belongs to a separate organism-matched investigation unless a new contact supplies that evidence.
