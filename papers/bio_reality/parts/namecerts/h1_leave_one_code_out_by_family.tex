\paragraph{Finite-row object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read object, not as a mechanism of translation.  The BEDC minimal carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read back through a curated standard-code label table.  The finite distinctions are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The internal structure recorded here consists of coordinates, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The contact is therefore a codon-label readback contact: it can test whether a local codon-tile split is reflected by curated labels, but it does not by itself test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\begin{aligned}

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The support condition is narrow.  The curated standard-code table supports codon-label readback and local split checks.  It does not supply a translation experiment, a structure experiment, a physical-admissibility criterion, a function assay, a mechanism, or a universality theorem.  Consequently the correct conclusion is: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile pattern whose leave-one-code-out behavior is stable by family in the recorded rows.  The correct non-conclusion is equally important: no protein realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, global mechanism, or universal biological law follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that evidence.
