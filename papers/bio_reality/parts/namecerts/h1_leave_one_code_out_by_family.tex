\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read object, not as a mechanism of translation or a theory of protein realization.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC-side distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The external contact is only the curated codon-label table.  Therefore the admissible readback is a code-layer label split: it can test local codon-tile label behavior, but it cannot by itself test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or biological necessity.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion is strictly local.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a finite boundary-tile pattern in the standard-code label table, and the family-resolved leave-one-code-out row shows high above-baseline recovery across five code families.  The result does not assert that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not assert translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate reality contact beyond the curated codon-label table.
