\subsection{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out readback}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-layer contact with the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The display is not used as a mechanism for translation.  It is used only as a finite coordinate carrier whose vertices are codons and whose observed labels are the curated amino-acid or stop labels supplied by the standard genetic-code bridge.  The contact therefore tests code readback and local label splitting, not folding, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that biological DNA is forced by this geometry.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the finite row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube row gives the same list and has face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local tile is a finite boundary object inside the codon cube.  Its row content is concrete: thirteen codons, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces.  This is enough to support a local boundary-tile statement, but not enough to promote that statement beyond the code-read layer.

$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
and high-Jaccard agreement at threshold $0.9$ occurs $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$, the exact-shape count is $0$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  The corresponding row values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support the claim that the observed local shape is unusual relative to the sampled nulls.  They do not identify a biochemical cause, nor do they turn the tile into a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The aggregate leave-one-code-out experiment evaluates $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The observed hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the observed hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The row also records the same above-baseline value, $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus, when a code table is withheld, the local coordinate-and-boundary construction recovers almost all targets at the code-label level.  The word ``recovers'' here means finite label readback against the curated table; it does not mean translation realization, protein production, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

The displayed modules are

Each module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the finite row.  This gives a compact local explanation of where the leave-one-code-out hits sit inside the boundary set: the hits are organized by named codon modules rather than by an unconstrained list of isolated codons.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The supported statement is therefore deliberately narrow.  Window-six coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure for the curated standard-code label table, and the family-stratified leave-one-code-out row reports high above-baseline code-label recovery across five families.  The unsupported statements are equally explicit.  No translation process follows from this row.  No folded protein structure follows from amino-acid label readback.  No physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from the local tile.  Any crossing into those layers requires a separate reality contact whose observed facts live at that higher layer.
