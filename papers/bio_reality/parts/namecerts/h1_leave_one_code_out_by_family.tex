\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The record lives on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the sixty-four codons.  Its biological contact is only the curated standard-code label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label, and the readback is a code-layer label readback.  The tested object is therefore not translation itself, not folded structure, not physical admissibility, not biological function, not mechanism, and not universality.  The finite carrier can expose a local tile, a boundary split, and a label instability pattern; any stronger biological reading requires a separate contact.

The row is thus a finite boundary tile: thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four local two-faces.  This is a statement about the codon-coordinate display and the curated label table, not about a global biological law.

These values support a finite-row anomaly relative to the sampled label-preserving controls.  They do not by themselves identify a cause in chemistry, selection, translation dynamics, or protein structure.

\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
