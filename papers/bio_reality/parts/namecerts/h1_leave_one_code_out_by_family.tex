\subsection{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out readback}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim tested.}
The finite record in this subsection asks whether the same local window-six codon geometry that isolates the standard-code boundary also retains predictive power after the alternative-code rows are grouped by biological-code family. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read only through codon labels supplied by a curated standard-code table. The test is deliberately a code-layer test: it concerns codon-to-label readback and local boundary membership, not translation dynamics, molecular structure, energetic feasibility, biological function, mechanism, or a universal law of genomes.

\paragraph{Finite boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit records $|R|=13$. The same row is certified as a punctured-cube-shaped object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile against which the leave-one tests are scored. It is not itself a claim about a cell, a ribosome, an organism, or a physical pathway.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling controls.}
The row is accompanied by a spectral record with
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the stored numerical witness is at tolerance scale rather than a large residual artifact. Against $N=2000$ sampled comparison rows with seed $42$, the exact-shape count is $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. The high-overlap comparison has $25$ rows with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$. The sampling log reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which matters because the comparison space is being exhausted by repeated draws rather than by an unconstrained continuous model. A second amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ gives exact-match count $0$, observed $M$-row size $20$, lower-tail count $7$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out aggregate.}
Before family stratification, the leave-one-code-out table contains $23$ evaluated code tables and $63$ total target codons. The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$. The observed rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ after adjoining the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. Thus the aggregate result is not merely that one row is visually recognizable; the finite leave-one experiment says that nearly all held-out code-table targets fall into the local row or its immediate allowed extension under the stated scoring rule.

\paragraph{Family-stratified result.}
The family-level record contains $5$ families. Their hit rates above the baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\

$$
This stratification is the central row of the present claim. Four families are saturated at the recorded hit-rate ceiling, while the alternative-yeast family remains below the ceiling but still far above the aggregate baseline rate $0.19821739130434785$. The finite conclusion is therefore family-uniform in a limited sense: the signal is not confined to one alternative-code family, but the record does not license replacing the finite family rows with an unrestricted biological principle.

\paragraph{Codon-module coverage.}
The accompanying module classification assigns all $13$ boundary codons and reports no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The leave-one-codon audit reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ codons with high closure coverage, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$. This supports the interpretation that the family-stratified leave-one-code-out row is reading a coherent local codon module, not a scattered list of unrelated table entries.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only external contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact is adequate for code-label readback and for checking whether held-out labels land in the finite codon row above. It is not a contact with ribosomal translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, organismal fitness, biochemical mechanism, or universal genomic necessity. Consequently the ready verdict is narrow: window-six coordinates expose a finite local boundary pattern whose family-stratified leave-one-code-out hit rates are strong in the recorded rows, while all higher biological claims remain outside this evidence packet unless another experiment supplies the missing contact.
