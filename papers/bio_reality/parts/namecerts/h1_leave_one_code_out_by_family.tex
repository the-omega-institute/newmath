\subsection{Leave-one-code-out stability by genetic-code family}\label{sec:leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table.  The table is used only as a code-layer reality contact: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, but it does not by itself observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The BEDC object under test is therefore a local window-six tile with singleton label corners and three-point label classes, not a global law of biology.  The contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks; it blocks any promotion from code_read to protein realization unless a separate higher-layer contact is supplied.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In standard RNA codon notation the verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is also verified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not introduced as a biological mechanism; it is a finite geometric witness inside the codon cube whose labels are later read back against the external table.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The spectral probe reports $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The associated radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the numerical spectrum is internally consistent at the recorded tolerance:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,\\
P_{\mathrm{rad}}(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Against a $2000$-draw null model with seed $42$, the exact shape appeared once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  High-Jaccard matches at threshold $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampler needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite-row shape is being tested in a crowded combinatorial regime rather than assumed by inspection.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, yielding $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The recorded hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  Written as finite-row data, the comparison is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{tables}&=23,\\
\mathrm{targets}&=63,\\
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T\cup\partial)&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the code_read claim: when one genetic-code table is withheld, the finite window-six construction recovers most withheld targets relative to the recorded baseline.  The claim is not that DNA must obey this geometry, nor that the geometry implements translation.

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&=1.0,\\

The weakest recorded family is alternative yeast, still above the baseline by a large margin.  The four remaining families reach $1.0$ in this finite-row statistic.  The result therefore supports a family-stable code-layer readback pattern: the local tile does not merely fit a single undifferentiated pool, but remains visible after stratification by genetic-code family.

\paragraph{Codon-module accounting.}
The leave-one-codon-out module classification exhausts the $13$-codon row.  It assigns all $13$ row codons, with $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The verified modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

$$
Every module has a predictable-or-boundary explanation in the recorded classification.  The companion leave-one-codon-out run reports recovery rate $1.0$, predictable count $8$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, and anchor count $0$.  These numbers separate prediction from boundary support: $8$ codons are counted as predictable by closure coverage, while $5$ codons are counted at the boundary.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite-row evidence is compatible with the statement that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose labels can be checked against a curated standard-code table.  It does not imply that translation has been realized, that a protein fold has been observed, that physical admissibility has been tested, that biological function follows, that a mechanism has been discovered, or that the pattern is universal across biology.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains code-layer readback.  Any claim about folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, or function requires a separate reality contact designed for that higher layer.
