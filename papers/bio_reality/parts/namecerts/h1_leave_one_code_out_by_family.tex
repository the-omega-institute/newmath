\paragraph{Finite-row claim.} The finite-row object in this chapter is the window-six codon display on the codon cube $Q_6$, read only through the curated standard-code label table. The claim is not that the cube geometry is a biological law. The claim is narrower: after one code table is withheld, the local boundary tile in the six-bit codon display continues to recover above-baseline code-layer label positions across the observed code-family partition. The row-level family audit contains $5$ families. The family-resolved above-baseline hit rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus four of the five reported families attain perfect above-baseline recovery, while the alternative-yeast family remains above baseline at $5/6$. This is the chapter-specific finite-row evidence for the identifier $\mathrm{h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family}$.

\paragraph{The punctured local tile.} The distinguished residue set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.

$$
The punctured-cube audit returns the same list and the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).

$$
This means that the carrier used by the chapter is not an informal drawing of codons. It is a finite combinatorial tile with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces, then read back through the external standard-code label contact.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model controls.} The spectral audit reports
$$
\begin{aligned}

with cubic, equivalently radial, polynomial residual $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$. The shape-significance null model used $N=2000$ samples with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model also used $N=2000$ samples. It found $0$ exact shape matches, observed $|M|=20$, and had $7$ lower-tail samples at or below that observed size. Its reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These controls make the local tile non-generic at the finite-row level. They do not convert the tile into a universal law of genetics.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.} The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels. Therefore the supported readback is code-layer label readback and local codon-tile label splitting. No translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from this chapter. In particular, an amino-acid label sequence read from the table is not a folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law. Any claim crossing into translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate contact that directly observes that higher layer.
