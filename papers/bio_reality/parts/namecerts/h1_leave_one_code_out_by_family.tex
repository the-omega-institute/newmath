\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Recovery by Code Family}\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
This section records the finite-row claim \texttt{h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family}.  The biological object is the standard-code codon table, read only as a finite assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The coordinate object is the six-position codon display $Q_6$, used here as a finite codon cube with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The claim is not that the cube is a biological mechanism.  The claim is narrower: after one leaves out code-level rows by family, the window-six local boundary pattern retains high recovery against the curated label table, with the recovery computed at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The primary run for this row has experiment id \texttt{7dda978c6c97} and verification time \texttt{2026-05-31T04:05:55+00:00}.  It evaluated $5$ code families.  The family-level rates above the baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{rate}(\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}) &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\operatorname{rate}(\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}) &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{rate}(\mathrm{candidate\_other}) &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{rate}(\mathrm{mitochondrial}) &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{rate}(\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}) &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus four of the five named families are saturated at rate $1.0$, while the alternative-yeast row remains high at $0.8333333333333334$.  The associated leave-one-code-out run, experiment \texttt{429e6e2c4eb4}, evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the above-baseline hit rate was $0.9841269841269841$.  Inside $M-T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M-T$ plus the local boundary it was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family row is therefore read as a stratified version of the same code-level recovery phenomenon, not as an independent assertion about translation or function.

$$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.

$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The row should therefore be read as a finite local boundary computation: thirteen codons, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional tile faces.  These numbers are the geometric content used by the leave-one-code-out test.  They do not, by themselves, assert that the biological apparatus must realize this geometry.

It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. In experiment \texttt{e8d5de747a0c}, the amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It reported observed $M$ size $20$, exact match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape $p$-value $0.0$, and lower-tail $p$-value $0.0035$.  These null comparisons support the statement that the finite local shape is not a routine outcome of the tested randomizations.  They do not convert a code-table pattern into a statement about folded structure, physical chemistry, organismal fitness, or universal law.

\paragraph{Contact and limitation.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table, understood as a codon-to-label table.  It can test code-read claims and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization.  Accordingly, this row permits the following conclusion and no stronger one: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary pattern whose leave-one-code-out recovery remains high across five code families, with rates $0.8333333333333334,1.0,1.0,1.0,1.0$ by family and $0.9841269841269841$ above baseline in the aggregate leave-one-code-out run.  No claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows unless an additional, separately measured contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
