\subsection{Family-Level Leave-One-Code-Out Boundary for Window-Six Codon Tiles}

\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only through a curated standard-code label table.  The table supplies codon-to-label assignments at the code-read layer: it is a reality contact for amino-acid and stop labels, not a contact for translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  The local object isolated by the finite-row analysis is a boundary tile inside the codon display.  Its distinguished row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with $|R|=13$.  As a punctured-cube subcomplex this row has $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the claim is not merely that thirteen codons were selected; it is that the selected codons carry a finite face-incidence signature inside the six-coordinate codon cube.  The observed label-supporting set has size $|M|=20$ in the reported null-model row, but no explicit codon list for $M$ is supplied here; this chapter therefore uses the observed cardinality and the verified boundary classifications rather than reconstructing an unstated $M$ list.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row signature.}
The same finite-row object carries a numerical spectrum.  The reported spectral quantities are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic check gives residual $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, with the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial and radial-polynomial evaluations.  Thus the row has a reproducible coordinate-and-spectrum signature: a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary, a twenty-label observed support size, and a stable spectral radius for the label side.  These numbers justify treating the pattern as finite data with a geometric readback, not as an interpretive story about molecular implementation.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
Against the shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-overlap threshold produced $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling record also reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, which matters because the finite universe is tight enough that repeated proposed samples are common.  Against the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, so the exact-shape empirical value is $0.0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{lower}}=0.0035$.  These tests say that the row is hard to reproduce under the reported finite nulls.  They do not say that the codon table is forced by geometry, and they do not identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The claim \texttt{h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family} refines the leave-one-code-out readback by grouping alternative genetic-code rows into families.  The family count is $5$.  The family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The aggregate leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level row therefore says that the local codon tile is not concentrated in a single code family: four of the five reported families attain perfect above-baseline hit rate, and the remaining family, alternative yeast, still attains $5/6=0.8333333333333334$.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and module closure.}
The codon-level recovery row supplies a stricter boundary check.  It reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification is complete on the thirteen-codon row: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable or boundary status.  This is the local finite-row sense in which the tile has closure: the thirteen codons are exhausted by named modules, and the reported leave-one-codon-out test recovers the withheld codon within the predictable-or-boundary envelope.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready claim is narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code label table, and the finite rows show that this tile is unusually stable under the reported leave-one-code-out, leave-one-codon-out, spectral, and null-model checks.  The claim remains at the code-read layer.  It cannot be promoted to translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or universality unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure; a local codon tile is not a global biological law; and window-six geometry alone is not a mechanism of protein realization.  The contact aligns with code-label readback and simultaneously blocks higher-layer promotion.
