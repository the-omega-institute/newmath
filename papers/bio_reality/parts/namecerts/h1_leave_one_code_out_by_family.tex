\subsection{Family-resolved leave-one-code-out stability}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
This record concerns the standard genetic-code label table after it has been placed on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$.  The object under test is not translation itself, nor any molecular mechanism.  It is the finite question of whether a local boundary tile obtained from the standard-code table continues to identify withheld code-table targets when alternate code families are left out by family.  The readout layer is therefore only the code-label layer: codons are read as amino-acid or stop labels by a curated standard-code table, and the geometry is tested against those labels.

\paragraph{Finite codon support.}
The distinguished boundary set has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a cubical subobject this same set is observed as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated module partition accounts for every member of $R$ and introduces no extra codons: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the missing, extra, and unassigned lists are all empty.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has either a predictable recovery route or a boundary explanation in the leave-one-codon-out run.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling checks.}
The local object is not merely a hand-picked list.  Its recorded spectral scalars are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the cubic relation check, so the finite numerical relation is satisfied to the stated tolerance.  In the shape-randomization run with $N=2000$, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries with seed $42$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and $7$ null samples fall in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The corresponding values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These checks support the claim that the tile is a finite, nonrandom code-layer pattern, while leaving its biological interpretation limited to the label table actually supplied.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family result.}
The family-resolved run evaluated $5$ families.  The hit rate above the baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus every listed family exceeds the baseline, and four of the five families reach perfect above-baseline recovery in this finite table test.  The broader leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  Restricted to $M-T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and after adding the boundary contribution it returned to $0.9841269841269841$.  The family run is therefore consistent with the table-level result: the boundary tile remains informative after code-family withholding rather than being exhausted by a single code-family idiosyncrasy.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out consistency.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out record gives $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This matters for the family result because the family-level recovery is not being treated as a black-box classifier.  It is backed by a finite codon-level decomposition in which all $13$ codons in $R$ are assigned to named modules, with no missing or extraneous module members.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-label readback and the local codon-tile label split.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or organism-level universality.  Consequently the verified numerical pattern is a code-table phenomenon: it licenses statements about the finite codon-label geometry reported above, not about how proteins are produced, fold, remain stable, acquire function, or become universal biological laws.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The result should be read as follows.  In the six-bit codon display, the $13$-codon boundary set forms a punctured-cube tile with stable module coverage and strong finite recovery statistics across the recorded leave-one-code-out families.  The observed family hit rates, the $23$-table and $63$-target aggregate, the spectral scalars, and the two null-model comparisons all support a code-layer regularity.  No higher-layer conclusion follows from these data alone.  Any future assertion about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality must introduce a separate empirical contact that measures that layer directly.
