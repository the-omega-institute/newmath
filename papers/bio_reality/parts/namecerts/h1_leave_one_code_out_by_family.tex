\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Signal by Code Family}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary row.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table. The row under test is not a claim about translation dynamics or protein realization. It is a finite code-read experiment: remove one alternate code table, compare its moved labels against the window-six local tile determined from the standard code, and record whether the moved codons remain inside the predicted tile complement or its boundary. In the standard-code contact, the distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the finite object is a local codon-label split in $Q_6$: singleton label corners and three-point label classes are visible as code-read distinctions, while the curated labels themselves remain external reality contacts.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape calibration.}
The row is not being selected merely by the size of a codon subset. The recorded spectral probe gives $\lambda_M = 0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R = 0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu = 3.051487585298128$, with radial polynomial residual $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$. The empirical shape calibration used $N=2000$ finite samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. An amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$, recorded $0$ exact shape matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$-size $20$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa,class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa,class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row local-geometry reading: the standard-code row has a rare tile shape under both the unconditioned and amino-acid-class-preserving probes.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family record.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ moved-label targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The observed hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The above-baseline hit rate was also $0.9841269841269841$. Grouping the same experiment by biological code family gives $5$ families, with family hit rates above baseline
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-level record is therefore not a single-table accident: four of the five families reach perfect above-baseline recovery under the local tile criterion, and the remaining family still reaches $5/6$.

\paragraph{Codon-level module check.}
The codon-level leave-one-out check supplies the internal module partition of the same $13$-codon row. The complete classification assigns all $13$ codons, with no extras, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same codon-level probe records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ predictable codons, $8$ high-coverage closures, and recovery rate $1.0$. Thus every module has either predictable or boundary status in the local code-read geometry. The module partition is exactly exhaustive over $R$, so the family-level leave-one-code-out signal is attached to a closed finite row rather than to an unspecified biological background.

\paragraph{BioReality boundary.}
The contact used here is only the curated standard genetic-code table. It supports codon-to-label readback and local split checks. It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality. Consequently the verdict is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure for standard-code label readback, and alternate-code families mostly land inside that structure above baseline. No claim follows that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological law, that amino-acid label sequences determine folded structure, or that the tile explains function. Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate reality contact of the appropriate layer.
