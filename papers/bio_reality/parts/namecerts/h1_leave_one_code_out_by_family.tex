\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The record is a finite code-read claim on the six-bit codon display $Q_6$.  The biological contact is only the curated standard-code label table: each codon is read back as an amino-acid label or as a stop label.  The claim concerns the local boundary behavior of this finite label table under leave-one-code-out probes.  It does not assert a translation mechanism, a folding law, physical admissibility, biological function, or universality.  Those claims would require separate contacts beyond the codon-label table.

\paragraph{Distinguished row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check reports the same row, with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the finite object used here is not an unconstrained list of labels: it is a row inside the six-bit codon cube with a local tile-face boundary.  The readback remains a standard-code label readback, not a molecular dynamics or cellular translation assertion.

\paragraph{Shape and null comparison.}
The shape-significance probe used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and had $55377$ duplicate retries.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding reported values are $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These finite comparisons support a non-generic local shape in the code-read layer, while leaving all higher biological layers outside the tested contact.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out aggregate.}
The leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus, once the code-read table is treated as the external label source, the local coordinate-and-boundary predicate recovers nearly all held-out targets in this finite evaluation.  The statistic is a code-read statistic: it says that the label split is locally exposed by the window-six geometry, not that the geometry is itself a universal law of DNA or protein realization.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The support condition is exactly this: the curated standard-code table supports codon-label readback and local split checks on the six-bit codon display.  The break condition is any promotion of the local tile to translation realization, structural order, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a claim that DNA must obey this BEDC geometry as a biological necessity.  The aligned reading is narrower and finite: in $Q_6$, the thirteen-codon row $R$ forms a punctured-cube local boundary whose code-read behavior remains strong under leave-one-code-out stratification across five reassignment families.
