\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The BEDC-side distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it is read only as a codon-label bridge.  The contact does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

Thus the row object is not a verbal interpretation of the code table: it is a finite subset of the codon cube with an explicit row list and an explicit local face count.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the reported null sample for the $M$-shape, the run used $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It observed $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding empirical values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null is sharper for the row count: with $N=2000$, observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, it reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The data therefore support a finite code-read asymmetry in the curated table under these probes.  They do not state that the asymmetry is a law of DNA, a mechanism of translation, or a cause of protein structure.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The readback boundary is strict.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  The curated code table supplies code-layer labels only; crossing into translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function would require a separate reality contact with its own observations.  Under the present contacts, the ready claim is therefore limited to finite-row code-read behavior: the standard-code label table, read through the six-bit codon carrier and the row set $R$, exhibits a high family-stratified leave-one-code-out hit rate above the stated baseline.
