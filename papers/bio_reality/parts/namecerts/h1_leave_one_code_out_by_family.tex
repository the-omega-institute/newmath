\subsection{Family-resolved leave-one-code-out readback}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Question.}
The finite object studied here is the standard-code codon label table read through a six-coordinate codon display.  The row under audit asks whether the same local boundary pattern that appears in the standard table remains visible when whole code families are held out and then queried against the remaining code-layer geometry.  The question is deliberately narrow: it concerns codon-to-label readback and local tile membership only.  It does not assert that the six-coordinate display explains translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The recurrent boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the observed list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite set is reported by the punctured-cube check, with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely counting thirteen isolated labels; it is using a specific local tile shape with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the codon-coordinate complex.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The finite spectral audit assigns the local pattern the numerical values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the cubic-polynomial residual.  This puts the reported spectral relation at numerical roundoff scale for the finite computation.  The spectrum is used here only as a coordinate diagnostic for the codon-label table; it is not treated as an energetic, structural, or evolutionary law.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against $2000$ sampled controls with seed $42$, the exact shape match count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler attempted $57377$ distinct draws and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the observed local codon-label shape is uncommon under the recorded finite null procedures.  They do not identify the biochemical cause of the shape.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The code-family leave-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ held-out targets.  The aggregate baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate after including the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The same $0.9841269841269841$ value is reported for the hit rate above baseline.  In finite terms, the local tile and its boundary recover nearly all held-out code-layer targets in this run, whereas the baseline recovers about one fifth.

\paragraph{Family decomposition.}
The family-resolved row contains $5$ families.  Their hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The weakest listed family is therefore alternative yeast, still at five sixths of its family-level held-out targets above baseline.  The other four listed families are complete at the resolution of the finite row.  The point of the decomposition is that the aggregate hit rate is not produced solely by one named code family; the signal is distributed across the recorded family classes, while retaining one visible family-specific shortfall.

\paragraph{Codon-module accounting.}
The leave-one-codon module audit assigns all $13$ members of $R$ to named modules, with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification report gives $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$ and assigned count $13$, with empty lists for extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons.  A separate codon leave-out row reports predictable count $8$, boundary count $5$, anchor count $0$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This is consistent with the module accounting: every boundary codon is either predictable from the local code-layer closure or carried by the boundary portion of the finite tile.

\paragraph{Readback contact.}
The only biological contact used by this row is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact resolves codon label readback.  It can test code-layer label splits and local codon-tile membership, but it does not contain observations of translation realization, protein structure, physical feasibility, or function.  Therefore an amino-acid label sequence produced from this table remains a label sequence.  It is not a folded protein, not a structural-order measurement, and not a functional assay.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The row supports the following bounded conclusion.  In the six-coordinate codon display, the standard-code label table contains a finite local boundary set $R$ of size $13$ with punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$.  The associated spectral and null-model diagnostics report a nonrandom-looking local code-layer shape, and leave-one-code-out tests recover held-out targets well above baseline both in aggregate and by recorded family.  The family row is especially sharp: $4$ of $5$ families are complete at rate $1.0$, and the remaining family is $0.8333333333333334$.  The result is a code-read result only.  Any claim about molecular translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate contact carrying that kind of observation.
