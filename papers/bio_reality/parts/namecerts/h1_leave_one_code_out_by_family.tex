\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery by code family}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table.  The row does not treat the table as a mechanism: it is only the external contact that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  Inside this carrier the distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row records the punctured-cube face data of this set by the $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object under test is not a free biological narrative but a named codon subset, its local tile adjacency data, and the standard-code labels supplied by the external table.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The row carries a finite spectral certificate for the same coordinate object.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also reported as the cubic-polynomial residual, so the numerical check is at rounding scale rather than at biological scale.  The finite shape comparison used $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and found $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampler attempted $57377$ distinct rows and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving lower-tail value $0.0035$.  These numbers support a finite-row shape signal; they do not by themselves identify a translation mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out aggregate.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ target events.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$.  The observed hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, while the hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$ and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The by-family refinement partitions the evaluated codes into $5$ families.  Their hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The only non-unit family rate in this packet is therefore the alternative-yeast row, at five sixths.  The other four named families are saturated at the finite-row level.  This is a code-read statement: when a code family is left out, the local coordinate and boundary construction recovers the relevant label split at rates far above the stated baseline.

\paragraph{Module-level localization.}
The leave-one-codon-out companion row reports no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure codons, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and the lists of extra module codons, missing codons, and unassigned codons are all empty.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every codon in the displayed $R$ list appears in exactly one of these modules.  The module row therefore explains where the leave-one-code-out recovery is local: it is concentrated in the same finite boundary classes used by the punctured-cube certificate.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  It supports codon-label readback and local split checks, but it does not observe translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA obeys this geometry as biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only a code-layer object.  The result may be stated as follows: in the six-bit codon display, the named boundary set $R$ and its module classes give a finite local tile whose leave-one-code-out by-family recovery is high relative to the row baseline, with family rates $0.8333333333333334,1.0,1.0,1.0,1.0$ across the five reported families.  No higher biological layer follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that layer.
