\paragraph{Finite object and scope.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, equipped with the finite distinctions used by the window-six display: codon coordinate, local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class.  The biological contact is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact is therefore a label table, not a translation experiment and not a structural assay.  The finite claim tested here is that the local window-six geometry exposes a stable boundary-tile pattern across leave-one-code-out comparisons, and that this stability remains visible when the alternative codes are grouped into coarse biological families.
