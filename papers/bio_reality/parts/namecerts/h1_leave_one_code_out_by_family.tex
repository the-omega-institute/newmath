\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the $64$ codons.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  The BEDC object is therefore a code-read object, not a translation apparatus.  Its internal distinctions are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data on the codon cube, with the biological readback restricted to the standard-code label table.

The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is the finite set

\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.

This row is the local tile boundary that the leave-one probes test against.  It is not asserted to be a global law of biology.
