\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery by code family}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
This record works only at the code-read layer of the standard-code codon table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of codons with coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The external contact is the curated standard genetic-code table, used only as a codon-to-label bridge.  It supplies amino-acid or stop labels for finite-row readback; it does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{The residual row.}
The distinguished residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its listed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same row and has face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object used below is not a free narrative class of codons.  It is a finite row with a fixed list, fixed size, and a fixed local cubical incidence profile.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-row calibration.}
The spectral row has
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic, or radial, polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the spectral calculation is at numerical tolerance for this finite row.  The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ distinct samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null run also used $N=2000$.  With observed $M$-size $20$, it found $0$ exact matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed size, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers are calibration data for the finite code-read row.  They do not by themselves assert a biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out aggregate.}
The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ target events.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The BEDC-local row had hit rate $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M\setminus T$, and hit rate $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary.  The hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  In finite-row terms, this says that when a code-table row is withheld, the local coordinate-and-boundary construction recovers almost all target labels relative to the stated baseline:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{baseline}&=0.19821739130434785,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T)&=0.9365079365079365,\\
\mathrm{hit}(M\setminus T+\partial)&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row count is small, and the assertion is therefore deliberately finite: it is a high recovery statistic on the supplied table family, not an asymptotic theorem about all possible genetic codes.

\paragraph{Family split.}
The family-level audit separates the same leave-one-code-out behavior into $5$ code families.  The family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The weakest listed family is alternative yeast, at $5/6$ in decimal form.  The other four families are complete hits against the same above-baseline criterion.  This is the chapter-specific claim: the leave-one-code-out signal is not only an aggregate effect over $23$ tables; it remains visible after grouping by the five supplied family classes.

\paragraph{Codon-module closure check.}
The codon-level module classification assigns all $13$ residual codons and has no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  Its six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification report has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, empty extra-module codons, empty missing-from-modules list, and empty unassigned-codons list.  The leave-one-codon-out row gives anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  This supports the interpretation that the family-level leave-one-code-out statistic is attached to a closed finite module decomposition, not to an untracked pile of exceptional codons.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite evidence supports a code-read claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose leave-one-code-out recovery is high both in aggregate and by family.  The support is concrete: $|R|=13$, the punctured-cube vector is $(13,16,4)$, the spectral row has $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, the main aggregate hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, and the five family rates are $0.8333333333333334,1.0,1.0,1.0,1.0$.  The contact boundary is equally concrete.  Nothing in this record proves that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as a biological necessity.  Nothing here proves translation realization, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still label readback.  Any promotion beyond code-read requires a separate higher-layer reality contact.
