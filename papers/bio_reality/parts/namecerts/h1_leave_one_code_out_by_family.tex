\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Code Family}
\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback boundary.}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code label table. A codon is treated as a six-bit display point, while the biological labels are supplied by the curated codon-to-label contact. The BEDC object is therefore not a biochemical mechanism. It is a finite coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation packet whose readback is restricted to the code layer. The local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class are internal distinctions on $Q_6$; the amino-acid and stop names are external labels attached by the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The finite exceptional row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the following codon set:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube readback has face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed row is not merely a list of labels but a small finite subcomplex of the window-six cube. The module classification accounts for all $13$ row codons with no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons. The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\}, & \mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}, & \mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\}, & \mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite closure test. This is the local object whose family behavior is measured below.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The spectral row records three scalar coordinates. The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check. The residual is numerically negligible at the displayed precision, so the spectral coordinate is internally consistent with the finite row computation. This is still a coordinate statement, not a physical statement about translation or folding.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the shape null, the experiment used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$. The distinct-sample attempt count was $57377$, with $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$. The exact shape match count was $0$, so the reported exact-shape probability was $0.0$. The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the finite row is a non-generic local code-layer shape under the tested nulls. They do not show that the geometry is a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. Thus almost all evaluated targets recovered by the finite window-six test lie above the baseline row, and the boundary-augmented closure accounts for the same $0.9841269841269841$ rate as the above-baseline statistic.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out audit contains $5$ code families. Their above-baseline hit rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family split is therefore stable in four of five reported families and remains above baseline in the remaining family. This is the chapter-specific content of the claim: the local $Q_6$ boundary tile is not only a pooled leave-one-code-out phenomenon, since the by-family rows preserve the above-baseline behavior after stratification. The weaker alternative-yeast value is still above the baseline hit rate $0.19821739130434785$, but it marks the family where additional finite-row inspection is most warranted.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out compatibility.}
The codon-level leave-one-out audit reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure coverage codons, and $8$ predictable codons. Its recovery rate is $1.0$, and the above-threshold recovery rate is also $1.0$. This is compatible with the module classification: the thirteen codons in $R$ are exhausted by the six named modules, and every module has predictable or boundary status. The family-level code-table result and the codon-level module result therefore point to the same finite interpretation: the row is a local closure-and-boundary pattern in the codon cube.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Consequently the conclusion is deliberately narrow: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose by-family leave-one-code-out rates are high relative to the stated baseline. No claim follows that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as a biological necessity, that the tile generates translation, that amino-acid label sequences determine folded protein structure, that the labels are physically admissible products, that any biological function is realized, or that a universal mechanism has been found. Any such promotion would require a separate higher-layer contact.
