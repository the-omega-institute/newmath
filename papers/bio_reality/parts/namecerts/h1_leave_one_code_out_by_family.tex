\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback claim.}
This BioReality record concerns the claim \texttt{h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family}.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code label table.  The coordinate object is not asserted to be a biological mechanism.  It is a finite display in which codons occupy binary cube positions, local tile faces can be counted, singleton label corners can be separated from three-point label classes, and the resulting pattern can be compared against the external codon-to-label table.  The claimed layer is only $\mathrm{code\_read}$: the object tested here is whether the standard-code readback exposes a stable local boundary-tile signal under leave-one-code-out perturbations, and whether that signal remains visible when alternative code tables are grouped by broad family.

\paragraph{Observed exceptional row.}
The distinguished finite row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the verified row, the codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent punctured-cube check returns the same $13$ codons and the face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object used in the family test is not a free-floating label list: it is the same $13$-row boundary object under both the cardinality check and the punctured-cube shape check.

These values support a finite-row anomaly statement at the code-read layer.  They do not, by themselves, promote the row to a law of translation or protein realization.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out aggregate.}
The aggregate leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The reported hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, while the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$.  The same value, $0.9841269841269841$, is reported as the hit rate above baseline.  In finite-row terms, the experiment says that nearly all held-out code targets land in the local closure or boundary region selected by the window-six display, relative to the stated baseline.  The readback remains a label-table readback: the observed successes are codon-label placements, not biochemical transitions.

The finite evidence is strongest for a local boundary-tile readback rule and weakest, by design, for any causal biological interpretation.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, observed as a codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop label assignment.  This contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Therefore the finite-row verdict is deliberately narrow.  The data support the statement that window-six codon coordinates expose a stable local boundary-tile structure under leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out probes, including a family-stratified signal over $5$ families.  No translation process, no folding claim, no physical admissibility claim, no functional claim, no mechanism claim, and no universality claim follows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact is supplied.
