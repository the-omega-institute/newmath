\subsection{Family-resolved leave-one-code-out stability}
\label{sec:bioreality-h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Claim and scope.}
This record tests whether a six-coordinate codon display retains local predictive structure when whole genetic-code tables are withheld by family.  The object under inspection is the standard-code label table as a curated codon-to-label contact.  The claim is deliberately restricted to code-layer readback: a finite codon coordinate system may expose a stable boundary pattern for amino-acid or stop labels, but the labels themselves are supplied by the curated code table rather than by the coordinate geometry alone.  The calculation therefore concerns a local tile boundary in the codon cube, not translation in a cell, protein folding, physical realizability, biological function, mechanism, or a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Finite codon set.}
The boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same set is verified as a punctured-cube row with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite geometry is not merely a count: the recorded tile has $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ local square faces.  This is the row on which the leave-one analyses are evaluated.

\paragraph{Spectral scale.}
The spectrum calculation supplies the numerical scale of the boundary tile.  The verified values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also recorded as the radial-polynomial value.  In this chapter the residual is used only as numerical evidence that the finite spectral row was evaluated consistently; it is not used to infer any molecular dynamics.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Two finite null checks constrain the interpretation.  In the shape-significance row with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The run reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null comparison is finite and visibly duplicate-limited.  In the amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size is $20$, the exact-match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$.  These numbers support the statement that the observed tile is unusual under the stated finite resamplings, while leaving the biological meaning of the labels to the curated code-table contact.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out aggregate.}
The aggregate leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The recorded hit rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$.  This is the central finite-row observation: after withholding code tables, most targets remain recoverable by the local tile plus boundary rule, far above the displayed baseline.

\paragraph{Family resolution.}
The family-resolved row contains $5$ families.  The verified hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Four of the five displayed families attain full above-baseline recovery in this finite row, while the alternative-yeast family remains high at $5/6$.  The family split matters because it prevents the aggregate $0.9841269841269841$ value from hiding a single-family concentration.  The pattern is broad across the displayed table families, but it is still a pattern in curated labels, not a proof of a shared cellular mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out support.}
The codon-level leave-one row gives recovery rate $1.0$ and reports $8$ predictable codons.  It also records $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and $8$ high-coverage closure codons.  These counts are compatible with the family-level picture: the coordinate tile is informative at the local code-label layer, and the informative positions sit in predictable or boundary roles rather than in a claimed biochemical anchor.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification assigns all $13$ codons in $R$ and records no missing or extra module codons.  The complete assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row reports $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no unassigned codons, no missing module members, and no extra module codons.  Every listed module has predictable or boundary status.  This gives a finite internal partition of the same boundary set used by the leave-one-code-out calculation.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It observes that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels, at codon-label resolution.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function, or universality.  Consequently, an amino-acid label sequence obtained by readback remains a code-level object.  A separate experimental or curated contact would be needed before any statement about folding, physical feasibility, organismal use, or function could be made.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
The finite data support the narrow claim that the six-coordinate codon tile has stable leave-one-code-out behavior across the displayed code-family split.  The strongest row is the family table: $5$ families are evaluated, with four rates equal to $1.0$ and the remaining rate $0.8333333333333334$.  This is consistent with the aggregate run over $23$ tables and $63$ targets, where the above-baseline hit rate is $0.9841269841269841$ against a baseline of $0.19821739130434785$.  The geometric row supplies the concrete set $R$ of $13$ codons with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectrum row supplies the numerical scales $\lambda_M$, $\lambda_R$, and $\mu$, and the module row partitions all $13$ codons without remainder.  The conclusion is therefore ready at the code-read layer and blocked at every higher biological layer until new contacts are supplied.
