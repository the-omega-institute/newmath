\subsection{Family-Stratified Leave-One-Code-Out Readback}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite record tests whether the window-six codon coordinate system keeps its predictive signal when variant code tables are grouped by family. The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard genetic-code table gives codon-to-label assignments, and the readback question is only whether local codon-tile geometry helps recover label changes at the code layer. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, equipped with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes. The observed claim is not that the geometry is a law of biology, but that the curated label table exposes a stable local boundary pattern under leave-one-code-out tests.

\paragraph{Finite codon boundary.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. The recorded codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row verifies that $R$ is the punctured-cube boundary with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the chapter's empirical object is a thirteen-codon boundary inside a six-bit codon display, not an unconstrained genomic or biochemical model.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape controls.}
The spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The reported cubic check is numerically closed to tolerance, with value $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$. In the shape randomization row, $N=2000$ distinct samples were targeted, requiring $57377$ sampling attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. Exact shape agreement occurred once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while $25$ samples reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. A second amino-acid-class-preserving null generated $N=2000$ trials with observed $M$-size $20$, no exact shape match, and a lower-tail count of $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class, exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class, lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These controls are finite-row evidence that the observed code-layer geometry is not a generic artifact of random relabeling under the tested null models.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out totals.}
Across the leave-one-code-out experiment, $23$ tables supplied $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The local geometry reached hit rate $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M\setminus T$, and $0.9841269841269841$ when the boundary contribution was included. The same value, $0.9841269841269841$, is recorded as the rate above baseline. This is the central finite-row signal: almost all tested code-table targets are recovered by the local coordinate-and-boundary readback rule, while the baseline remains near $0.1982$.

\paragraph{Family stratification.}
The family-stratified row groups the leave-one-code-out targets into $5$ families. The observed hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The only family below perfect recovery in this finite row is $\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}$, still at $5/6$. The other four families are recovered at rate $1.0$. The stratification therefore supports the stronger reading that the leave-one-code-out behavior is not concentrated in a single variant-code family.

\paragraph{Codon-level module accounting.}
The codon-level module row accounts for every codon in $R$ without residue. It reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing boundary codons, and no unassigned codons. The module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has either predictable status or boundary status in the leave-one-codon-out classification. The separate leave-one-codon-out row records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. This codon-level accounting is important because it prevents the family-level result from hiding unmatched codons in the boundary set.

\paragraph{Scientific reading.}
The finite rows support a code-read statement: the six-bit codon window, together with its local boundary geometry, recovers most curated label changes under leave-one-code-out tests and does so across the recorded code-family stratification. The evidence is numerical, finite, and contact-bound. It uses the curated codon-label table as the observed label source and the coordinate construction as the probe geometry. It does not by itself observe translation in a cell, ribosomal realization, folded protein structure, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, or a universal biological mechanism. Any such stronger claim would require a separate contact whose measured object lies at that higher layer. In this chapter the local codon tile remains a code-layer readback device, and the blocked higher-layer interpretations are part of the claim rather than exceptions to it.
