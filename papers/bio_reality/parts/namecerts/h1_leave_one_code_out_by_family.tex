\subsection{Leave-one-code-out stability by code family}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record concerns a finite code-read claim on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biological mechanism; it is a coordinate display for codon labels.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, read as a finite assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The admissible readback is therefore code-layer readback: a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class may be compared with curated labels, but the comparison does not itself observe translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The finite boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row being tested by the leave-one-code-out probe is not an arbitrary list of codons.  It is a finite boundary object whose vertices, edges, and local faces have already been counted inside the window-six coordinate geometry.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model scale.}
The spectral row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the reported cubic relation is numerically at tolerance scale for the finite computation.  Against the unrestricted shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ high-Jaccard matches, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  That run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Against the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, and the lower-tail value was $0.0035$.  These numbers support finite-row non-genericity of the observed code-read geometry, not a claim of biological necessity.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$; and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family stratification has $5$ families.  The verified family hit rates above baseline are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\_yeast}&=0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\_other}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&=1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\_ciliate}&=1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The weakest of the five family rows is therefore the alternative-yeast family at $5/6$, while the other four reported families have perfect above-baseline recovery in this finite probe.  The claim supported by these rows is family-stable code-read recovery for the boundary-tile signal under the stated leave-one-code-out test.

\paragraph{Codon-level module check.}
The companion leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ members of $R$ and introduces no extra module codons.  The verified modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\

$$
The classification row reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no unassigned codons, and no extra module codons.  Hence the family-level leave-one-code-out signal is compatible with a complete codon-level decomposition of the finite boundary row.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite evidence supports the following local statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a stable boundary-tile structure for code-label readback, and this structure remains visible under the recorded leave-one-code-out family split.  The evidence does not say that DNA obeys this geometry as a global biological law.  It also does not say that a recovered amino-acid label sequence is a folded protein, a physically admissible molecule, a translation mechanism, a realized biological function, or a universal property of genetic codes.  Any such promotion would require a separate higher-layer contact.  Within the present row, the curated table supplies labels only, and the supported conclusion stays at the code_read layer.
