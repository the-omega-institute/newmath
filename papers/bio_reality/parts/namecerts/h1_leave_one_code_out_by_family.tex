\subsection{Leave-One-Code-Out Stability by Code Family}
\label{sec:bioreality-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with a curated standard-code bridge assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC-side distinctions are a six-bit codon display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The reality contact is therefore label readback only: it says which codon has which curated label, but it does not say that the local tile is translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The observed row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube pattern with finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local object tested by the leave-one procedures.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons: the modules are $\{AGA,AGG\}$ for the arginine block, $\{AAA\}$ for the lysine-side block, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for the leucine-side block, $\{AUA\}$ for the isoleucine--methionine block, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ for the serine/leucine-side block, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for the stop/tryptophan-side block.  The accounting row has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Null-model pressure.}
Against the shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite-row probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and had $55377$ duplicate retries, so the empirical row is being compared against a visibly constrained finite combinatorial space.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$.  The reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the local tile is not a generic random draw under the stated finite-row nulls.  They do not promote the tile beyond code-read evidence.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out result.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  In finite-row terms, leaving a code table out preserves a strong local recovery signal relative to the baseline rate, but the recovery target remains the curated label table.  It is a code-label recovery statement, not a translation-realization statement.

\paragraph{Family-level stability.}
The by-family refinement evaluated $5$ code families.  The family hit rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the boundary tile is not only a pooled effect over all tested tables: it remains visible after grouping the leave-one-code-out readback by family, with four families at perfect above-baseline hit rate and the alternative-yeast family at $5/6$.  This is the finite-row content of the claim.  It says that the local code-read geometry recurs across the listed family partition; it does not say that those families are generated by the geometry.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out compatibility.}
The related leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The recovery rate above threshold is also $1.0$.  This codon-level result is compatible with the code-family result: the boundary and predictable codons cover the modules without requiring an anchor codon.  The row is therefore a local stability witness for the curated code table, not a proof of a global biological necessity.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table.  It can test code-read label assignments and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that additional evidence.
