\subsection{Family-stratified leave-one-code-out recovery}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite record.}
The object considered here is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-label layer. A codon is read as a six-bit point in the codon cube, and the finite comparison is performed against the curated codon-to-label table. The experiment indexed by \texttt{h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family} evaluated five code-family strata and recorded that every family except the alternative-yeast group reached a perfect above-baseline recovery rate. The observed family rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}} &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{candidate\ other}} &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{mitochondrial}} &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the family-level claim is not merely a pooled effect: it survives a split into $5$ named strata, with the smallest recorded stratum-level above-baseline rate equal to $0.8333333333333334$. The row is therefore a finite statement about how the local codon-window partition behaves when one code table is withheld, not a statement about the biochemical implementation of translation.

\paragraph{Global leave-one-code-out context.}
The family split refines the pooled leave-one-code-out experiment. In that run, $23$ code tables were evaluated, producing $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The stricter membership count inside $M-T$ gave $0.9365079365079365$, and the relaxed boundary-augmented condition $M-T+\partial$ again gave $0.9841269841269841$. In finite terms, the code-family stratification is supported by a larger table-level recovery pattern: almost all held-out targets land in the predicted or boundary-tolerant region, while a naive baseline remains near one fifth.

\paragraph{The thirteen-codon boundary set.}
The recurrent finite boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. The recorded codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same set and records the face-vector data $(13,16,4)$. This identifies the boundary not as an arbitrary list of exceptional codons but as a finite tile-shaped subset of the six-bit display. The finite-row data do not say that this tile is a law of molecular biology; they say that the listed codons form the local set against which the recovery and boundary tests were evaluated.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The codons in $R$ were partitioned into named modules without omissions or surplus assignments. The completeness row reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, $\mathrm{assigned\_count}=13$, no extra module codons, no missing elements, and no unassigned codons. The actual module record is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR/Lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile/Met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop/Trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module in this list is recorded as having either predictable or boundary status. This matters for the family-stratified leave-one-code-out claim because the family effect is not being asserted over an unorganized residue class: the thirteen codons split into six finite modules, and the row certifies complete assignment across those modules.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out comparison.}
The companion codon-level recovery run gives a second finite check on the same local geometry. It records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure codons, $8$ predictable codons, and a recovery rate of $1.0$ above the threshold. This does not replace the leave-one-code-out experiment; it checks that the codon-level deletion problem is compatible with the same local boundary picture. The family-stratified row therefore sits between two finite observations: the pooled code-table recovery, where $63$ targets across $23$ tables give $0.9841269841269841$ above-baseline recovery, and the codon-deletion recovery, where all $8$ predictable codons are recovered at the stated threshold.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model controls.}
The spectral record supplies numerical shape constraints for the same finite construction. The values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,\\
P(\lambda) &= -6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The near-zero polynomial residual is recorded both as the cubic-polynomial check and as the radial-polynomial value. The null experiments are also finite. In the shape-significance run with seed $42$, $N=2000$ samples required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries; it found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$. The corresponding probabilities were $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$ and $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null with the same sample size and seed, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$. These controls support the claim that the measured local pattern is not a typical outcome of the sampled null procedures.

\paragraph{Interpretive boundary.}
The only curated contact used here is the standard genetic-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact can test code-label readback and local codon-tile splitting. It cannot, by itself, test whether translation is physically realized, whether an amino-acid sequence folds, whether a molecular state is admissible, whether a biological function is present, whether a mechanism is universal, or whether this local geometry has the force of a global biological law. Consequently the verdict of this subsection is narrow: the finite row supports a family-stratified leave-one-code-out recovery pattern in the codon-label table, with explicit boundary modules, spectral values, and null controls. Any move from this code-level observation to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require a separate empirical contact carrying that higher-layer observation.
