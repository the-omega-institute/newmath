\subsection{Leave-one-code-out families in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}

\paragraph{Carrier and contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a standard-code label table.  A codon is treated here only as a finite row in a code-label display: its observable contact is the curated assignment of a codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  The chapter therefore concerns the code-read layer.  The local distinctions used in the finite row are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The internal structure recorded by the row is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the biological readback is only the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The finite relation row isolates the following thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row reports that this set has the punctured-cube face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the datum is not merely a list of exceptional labels; it is a finite local boundary object inside $Q_6$.  The row does not say that the whole genetic code is generated by this boundary.  It says that these thirteen codons form the particular local relation whose label behavior is tested against curated code readback.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated spectral row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The radial polynomial check is numerically closed at $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, and the cubic-polynomial row reports the same residual.  This places the local tile in a reproducible numerical window: the spectrum is not used as a mechanism, but it supplies a coordinate signature for the finite codon relation.  The spectral radius in tolerance is the same value as $\lambda_M$, namely $0.6752479308830213$.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the sampled null row, the shape test used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The run made $57377$ distinct sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It observed $0$ exact matches, a lower-tail count $7$ at or below the observed $M$ size, and an observed $M$ size of $20$.  Its reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the finite local tile is not a typical draw under the stated null rows.  They do not promote the tile into a biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$; and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  This is the finite-row reason for reading the boundary tile as predictive at the code-label layer: when a code table is left out, the local window-six construction recovers almost all target labels above the baseline row.  The evidence is still label evidence.  It is not a statement about translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or folded protein structure.

The family-resolved row contains $5$ families.  Its hit rates above baseline are

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{bacterial\_plastid}&:1.0,\\

$$
Thus the leave-one-code-out behavior is not concentrated in a single table family.  Four families reach $1.0$ above-baseline hit rate, while the alternative-yeast family remains high at $0.8333333333333334$.  The chapter-specific claim is therefore a family-stable code-read claim: the same local codon-tile boundary continues to organize label recovery across the recorded families.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module row assigns all thirteen codons in $R$ with no extras and no omissions.  It reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, empty extra-module codons, empty missing-from-modules, and empty unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has predictable or boundary status in the finite row.  The separate leave-one-codon-out row gives boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, closure coverage high $8$, anchor count $0$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These module counts explain why the thirteen-codon relation is usable as a local code-read object: each module is accounted for either by prediction or by boundary placement.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The contact is a curated standard-code table.  It can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, structure, admissibility, or function.  Any claim crossing that boundary requires a separate reality contact, not a stronger reading of the same finite row.
