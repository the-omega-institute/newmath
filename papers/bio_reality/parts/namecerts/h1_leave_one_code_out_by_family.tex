\subsection{Leave-one-code-out recovery by genetic-code family}\label{subsec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and readout.} The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-label layer. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with codons read as finite words over $\{\mathrm{U},\mathrm{C},\mathrm{A},\mathrm{G}\}$ and labels supplied by the curated standard genetic-code table. The local object under test is not translation itself. It is the finite pattern obtained when the codon cube is marked by the standard-code amino-acid and stop labels, then compared with single-code omissions and code-family groupings. The reality contact therefore has one narrow resolution: codon-to-label readback. It does not observe ribosomal realization, folding, thermodynamic admissibility, cellular function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary set.} The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The punctured-cube check reports the same list and the face-vector summary
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
Thus the finite row is not merely a count: it identifies a concrete thirteen-codon region in the codon cube, with four local two-dimensional faces and sixteen local edges. This is the region against which later leave-out probes are interpreted.

\paragraph{Spectral row.} The same finite object carries a numerical spectral signature. The recorded values are
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, recorded also as the cubic-polynomial residual. At the precision of the finite computation, this places the observed local tile on a sharply constrained numerical row rather than on an arbitrary marked subset of $Q_6$.

\paragraph{Shape controls.} The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ random trials with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact observed shape appeared once, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}$$
and the high-overlap condition $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving control, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found no exact shape matches. Its lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, with observed $|M|=20$, so
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
These numbers support the claim that the local code-label geometry is a finite non-generic pattern under the sampled controls. They do not by themselves explain why biology realizes the standard code.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family row.} The family-level leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated five genetic-code families. The run identifier for this row is \texttt{7dda978c6c97}, with verification time \texttt{2026-06-01T03:41:58+00:00}. The family hit rates above the baseline were
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}&:0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial}&:1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}&:1.0.
\end{aligned}$$
The associated aggregate leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The family row therefore says that the boundary-aware local tile is highly stable under the tested code-family omissions, with only the alternative-yeast family dropping below perfect recovery in this finite table.

\paragraph{Codon-level module check.} The leave-one-codon-out module classification assigns all thirteen boundary codons and reports no extras, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The six modules are
$$\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The classification summary is $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, and empty lists for extra module codons, missing boundary codons, and unassigned codons. The companion leave-one-codon-out row recorded anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. Hence every listed module is accounted for by a predictable-or-boundary status inside the finite codon display.

\paragraph{Interpretation.} The claim \texttt{h1.leave\_one\_code\_out.by\_family} is ready at the code-read layer. The evidence is a finite table computation over the standard-code contact: the thirteen-codon boundary set is explicit, its punctured-cube face vector is fixed, its spectral row is numerically constrained, sampled controls assign small tail probabilities to the observed shape, and leave-one-code-out recovery remains above baseline across all five evaluated families. The conclusion is deliberately local: the window-six codon coordinates expose a stable boundary-tile pattern in curated codon-label data. Nothing in this chapter asserts that the tile is a law of molecular biology, that it implements translation, that it predicts folded protein structure, that it certifies physical admissibility, that it yields biological function, or that it supplies a universal mechanism. Any such assertion would require a separate contact that observes the relevant higher layer directly.
