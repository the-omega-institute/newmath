\paragraph{Finite codon window.}
The data packet studies the standard-code codon table through a six-coordinate codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with local distinctions given by coordinate position, closure behavior, spectral response, and label relation.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard genetic-code table supplies codon-to-label assignments for amino-acid and stop labels.  It does not supply a translation apparatus, a folding observation, a physical admissibility test, a functional assay, a mechanism, or a universal biological law.

The finite exceptional region $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified row lists the codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.

$$
The same row identifies this region as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).

$$
Thus the chapter is not asserting that the codon table is generated by geometry.  It records that the curated labels, when read in the window-six coordinates, meet a compact local boundary shape whose finite content is exactly visible in the table.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The contact used here is a curated code table, so the conclusion is confined to codon-label readback and local split checks.  The chapter does not claim that DNA is compelled to obey the six-coordinate pattern, that translation is realized by the window geometry, that amino-acid labels determine folded structure, that physical admissibility follows, that biological function follows, or that the observed tile is a global law.  A stronger biological conclusion would require a separate contact of the appropriate kind, such as a translation experiment for translation, a structure observation or validated structure-prediction contact for folding, and an assay-level contact for function.  Until such data are attached, the finite result remains a code-layer stability record for the listed codon families.
