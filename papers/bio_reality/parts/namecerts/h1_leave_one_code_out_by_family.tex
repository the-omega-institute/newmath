\subsection{Leave-one-code-out family stability}\label{sec:h1-leave-one-code-out-by-family}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and code-read scope.}
The record tests a window-six codon display on the finite codon cube $Q_6$ against a curated standard-code label table.  The carrier is not a biological mechanism; it is a finite coordinate display in which codons are read as six-bit positions and code labels are supplied by an external table contact.  The claim tested here is therefore local and code-layered: the window-six geometry may expose a boundary tile whose label changes are recoverable across genetic-code families, but the table contact only supplies codon-to-label readback.  No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from this finite-row calculation unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer fact.

\paragraph{Boundary set.}
The finite exceptional row set has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
      &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube audit reports the same row list and the face-vector signature
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This means that the local boundary object is not merely a count of unusual codons; it has a concrete finite cubical incidence profile in the six-bit display.  The row set is also exhausted by the module partition used for the leave-one-codon and leave-one-code probes: $\{AGA,AGG\}$ for $agr$, $\{AAA\}$ for $ar\_lys$, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for $cun$, $\{AUA\}$ for $ile\_met$, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ for $ssn$, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for $stop\_trp$.  The module audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing rows, and no unassigned codons, so the family-level calculation is made on a closed finite boundary inventory rather than on an open-ended list.

\paragraph{Spectral and null-model anchors.}
The same finite display has spectral values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is within the reported tolerance for the polynomial identity used by the probe.  Against $2000$ sampled null tables in the shape audit, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
That audit required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries at seed $42$, so the reported tail behavior is attached to a concrete finite sampling run.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$, the observed local set size was $20$, the exact shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the observed code-layer boundary is not behaving like a generic row pattern under the stated finite null models.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out family rows.}
The family-specific leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $5$ families.  Its hit-rate-above-baseline values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{alternative\ yeast}} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid}} &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{candidate\ other}} &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{mitochondrial}} &= 1.0,\\
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate}} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The pooled leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the code-read recovery rates were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{hit}_{M-T} &= 0.9365079365079365,\\
\operatorname{hit}_{M-T+\partial} &= 0.9841269841269841,\\
\operatorname{hit}_{\mathrm{above\ baseline}} &= 0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family vector is therefore not a single pooled artifact hidden by averaging: four of the five families have perfect above-baseline recovery, and the remaining alternative-yeast family remains high at $0.8333333333333334$.  Read as a finite-row statement, the local tile is stable under the family split tested here.

\paragraph{Codon-level boundary check.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe gives a second finite control.  It reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The recovery rate above threshold is also $1.0$.  Since every listed module has either predictable or boundary rows and the module inventory exactly covers the $13$ codons in $R$, the codon-level probe and the code-family probe point to the same local phenomenon: the six-bit display has a finite boundary tile whose row behavior is visible through curated label readback.

\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.}
The verified statement is a code-read statement.  The external contact is the curated standard genetic-code bridge, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test the local codon-tile label split, but it cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, protein folding, or a universal biological mechanism.  The local tile is therefore admissible as a finite coordinate-and-label result: $R$ has $13$ rows, a punctured-cube face signature $(13,16,4)$, a complete module classification, strong family-level above-baseline recovery across $5$ families, and supporting null-model tail values.  It is not admissible as a claim that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological law, nor as a claim that amino-acid label readback determines folded protein structure or function.
