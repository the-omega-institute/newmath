\subsection{Q6 codon residual topology after the amino-acid quotient}\label{sec:h3-codon-usage-topology-residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
This record isolates a codon-level residual question after the amino-acid sequence has already been held fixed.  The carrier is the per-organism CDS codon-count table projected to the amino-acid-residual basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$.  The basis used in the finite run has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\ Ser_{UCR/AGY},\\
&Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress pair antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The intended biological question is not whether codon composition alone proves a translational mechanism.  It is narrower: after amino-acid composition is controlled, can any one of these residual coordinates carry additional organism-level signal for a bounded readout such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, relative to simpler codon-feature baselines?  In score notation, the motivating statistic has the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$, the readout $r$ is supplied by a separate translation-layer contact, and $Z$ includes at least the amino-acid sequence $y$, organismal context, and the simpler baseline variables being compared against.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The finite computation loaded one codon-count table for each of three organisms: $\mathrm{homo\_sapiens}$, $\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}$.  The NCBI genetic code was loaded in the same run, and the computation is explicitly a three-organism contact rather than a population estimate.  The experiment identifier was $\mathrm{residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation}$, with run identifier $971b9c868480$, and the recorded status was passed.  On this finite row, all nine residual coordinates were computed.  With variance threshold $0.01$, seven of the nine coordinates were nonzero after the amino-acid quotient, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#B^{*}_{Q6}&=9,\\
\#\{q\in B^{*}_{Q6}:\operatorname{Var}(q)>0.01\}&=7,\\
\frac{7}{9}&=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This establishes only that the chosen residual coordinate system is not collapsed by the amino-acid quotient on the finite three-organism codon-count contact.  It does not establish a positive survival score for any translation readout.

\paragraph{Residual basis.}
The amino-acid quotient removes effects already readable from amino-acid composition.  Therefore a codon coordinate can remain relevant only through the part that is orthogonal to the amino-acid-level description used in the control set.  The nontrivial finite result is that the computed $B^{*}_{Q6}$ projection still contains organism-dependent variation after this quotient.  In particular, the basis includes split coordinates that an $M$-density-only summary cannot inspect directly, such as $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ and the $K_{AAA}$ contrast against the synonymous lysine alternative.  Consequently, a model that sees only the local $M$-density may be too coarse to test whether these synonymous residual distinctions matter for a bounded readout.  The finite run supports that as a coordinate-design pressure, not as a completed biological conclusion.

\paragraph{Blocked statistic.}
The decisive survival test would require paired organism-level data: codon usage, relevant tRNA copy-number spectra, and an organism-matched tAI, stAI, ribosome-occupancy, or protein-output readout.  The required comparison is that adding a coordinate $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ to an $M$-density baseline, with amino-acid composition controlled, improves the score for at least one organism and one readout.  Equivalently, the null form is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)\leq 0
\quad\text{for every }Q\in B^{*}_{Q6}
\end{aligned}
$$
for every organism and every admissible readout under the selected contact.  The current finite row does not contain that paired controlled statistic.  It contains codon-count residual geometry and contact categories for codon-usage tables, tRNA gene-copy summaries, and tAI or stAI weights, but these remain separate bridges rather than a single controlled regression, survival-score table, or organism-matched readout matrix.  Thus the $M$-only insufficiency claim remains blocked at the statistical-readout step.

\paragraph{Boundary.}
No translation-realization claim follows from the codon-count residuals alone.  No folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or universal law follows from the fact that seven of nine residual coordinates are nonzero on this finite contact.  A positive value of $S_Q$ would also be conditional on the selected readout, organism set, and control variables, not a global statement about biology.  The present record is therefore a bounded coordinate result: across human, yeast, and $E. coli$ K-12 codon-count tables, the amino-acid-residual $Q6$ basis is computable and nontrivial, with nonzero fraction $7/9$ at threshold $0.01$.  Any promotion to translation efficiency, ribosome dynamics, protein abundance, folded structure, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate matched contact that directly supplies the relevant measurement.
