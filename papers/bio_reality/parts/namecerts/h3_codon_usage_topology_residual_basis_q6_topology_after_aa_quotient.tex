\subsection{Amino-acid-quotiented codon residuals as a finite Q6 topology readout}\label{sec:codon-usage-q6-residual-topology}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim encoded by the row.}
The finite row records a codon-level residual construction after quotienting by amino-acid identity.  The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table, projected away from the amino-acid composition component and read in the residual coordinate family $B^{*}_{Q6}$.  The nine named residual coordinates are $K_{AAA}$, $Arg_{AGR}$, $Ile_{AUA}$, $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$, $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$, $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$, $Thr_{ACR/ACY}$, and an antisymmetric third-position stress coordinate.  The intended question is not whether a codon count alone proves a biological mechanism.  It is narrower: after amino-acid composition is held fixed, does the Q6 residual coordinate system still expose nontrivial finite structure that could later be tested against a bounded translation-layer readout?

\paragraph{Finite contact actually used.}
The verified run $971b9c868480$ loaded one codon-count table for each of three organisms: human, budding yeast, and the K--12 strain of $E.\ coli$.  The NCBI genetic code table was loaded for the same computation.  Thus the observed scope is exactly a three-organism contact, not a population estimate over taxa and not a universal codon-usage law.  In this finite contact, the residual basis had nine listed elements, and the computation classified seven of them as nonzero above the row's variance threshold $0.01$.  Equivalently, the reported nonzero fraction was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9} &= 0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row status is reported as passed, with verification time $2026$-$05$-$31$ $04{:}05{:}55$ UTC, for the experiment identifier \texttt{residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation}.

\paragraph{Interpretation of the nonzero residuals.}
The observed $7$-of-$9$ outcome says that the amino-acid quotient does not collapse the selected Q6 codon topology to the zero pattern on this finite organism set.  The result is strongest as a coordinate fact: the residual basis remains capable of distinguishing codon-level synonymous structure after the amino-acid sequence component has been removed.  In particular, the construction is designed to include contrasts that an $M$-density-only summary cannot directly test, such as $CUN$ versus $UUR$ inside leucine and $AAA$ versus $AAG$ inside lysine.  The row therefore supports the existence of nontrivial residual codon-count geometry in the finite tables, while leaving open whether that geometry has predictive survival against a separate readout such as tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance.

\paragraph{Conditional survival statistic.}
The survival question attached to this row is the conditional comparison
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z) &= J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$, the readout $r$ is kept outside the codon-count coordinate itself, and $Z$ includes amino-acid sequence together with contextual and simpler-codon-feature baselines.  The finite row does not yet report organism-matched values of $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$ for tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance.  Consequently, the current computation can motivate that conditional test but cannot already decide it.  A positive value would have to be obtained from a paired statistic in which codon usage, tRNA-copy information, and the readout table are matched at organism resolution and controlled for amino-acid composition.

\paragraph{Local boundary on what follows.}
Nothing in the three-organism codon-count computation establishes translation realization by itself.  It also does not establish ribosome-mediated mechanism, protein abundance, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or universality across organisms.  The signs of the residual coordinates are not global directional laws.  The only finite conclusion recorded here is that, in the checked human--yeast--$E.\ coli$ contact, the amino-acid quotient leaves seven of nine named Q6 residual coordinates nonzero above threshold.  Any stronger statement requires an additional matched contact supplying the corresponding translation-layer or physical readout.
