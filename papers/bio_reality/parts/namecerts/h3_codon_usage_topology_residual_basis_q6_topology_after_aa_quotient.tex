\subsection{Amino-acid-quotiented codon residuals for Q6 topology}
\label{sec:h3-codon-usage-topology-residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient}

\paragraph{Object of the record.}
The object recorded here is the organism-level codon-count profile after quotienting away amino-acid composition and then reading the remaining synonymous codon degrees of freedom through the residual basis $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$. The carrier is not a protein-folding object and not a direct measurement of translation dynamics. It is a finite coordinate system on per-organism coding-sequence codon counts, designed to ask whether Q6-style codon topology leaves a residue after the amino-acid sequence $y$ has already been conditioned on.

The residual basis used in the finite computation has exactly nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{\ast}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $K_{AAA}$ separates the lysine $AAA$ component inside the lysine synonym class, $Arg_{AGR}$ isolates the $AGR$ arginine branch, $Ile_{AUA}$ isolates the $AUA$ isoleucine branch, the two leucine coordinates distinguish the $CUN$ versus $UUR$ split and the $UUA$ versus $UUG$ split, the two serine coordinates distinguish $UCR$ versus $AGY$ and $UCA$ versus $UCG$, the threonine coordinate separates $ACR$ from $ACY$, and the final coordinate records an antisymmetric third-position stress pair. These coordinates are only residual coordinates: they have meaning after the amino-acid quotient has been imposed.

\paragraph{Finite row contact.}
The finite table used for this record contains three organisms: $homo\_sapiens$, $saccharomyces\_cerevisiae$, and $escherichia\_coli\_k12$. The NCBI genetic code was loaded for the computation, and each organism contributes one codon-count table. Thus the contact scope is three organism-level codon-count summaries, not a population estimate and not a sampling statement about all taxa. Within this finite row set, all nine $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ elements were computed.

The nontriviality check used variance threshold $0.01$. Seven of the nine residual coordinates were nonzero above that threshold, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The experiment identifier for this finite computation is $residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation$, with run identifier $971b9c868480$, and the recorded status is passed. This establishes only that the amino-acid-quotiented residual coordinates are not all collapsed on these three organism-level codon-count contacts. It does not establish a population-level taxonomic pattern.

\paragraph{Survival-score reading.}
For a readout $r$ and a context block $Z$, the intended survival comparison is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $q\in B^{\ast}_{Q6}$, $J_0$ is the baseline loss or incompatibility after conditioning on $Z$, and $J_Q$ is the corresponding quantity after adding the residual coordinate $q$. The context $Z$ must include the amino-acid sequence $y$, organismal context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines. In the stricter comparison against an $M$-density-only model, the statistic is read as $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$, so that the question becomes whether any residual coordinate improves over the local $M$ baseline.

This chapter therefore separates two facts. First, the finite codon-count contact shows that $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ is not vacuous after the amino-acid quotient in the three-organism row set. Second, a positive translation-layer survival score has not yet been computed from organism-matched codon usage, tRNA copy spectra, and a tAI, stAI, ribosome-occupancy, or protein-abundance readout. The first fact is a coordinate-level observation. The second would require an additional paired statistic.

\paragraph{Role of the organismal contacts.}
The codon-usage contact can test sequence-composition residuals at organism resolution. The tRNA gene-copy contact can supply organism-level anticodon-family spectra, including leucine-family copy-number structure. The tAI or stAI contact can supply a bounded codon-adaptation readout. These contacts are scientifically different. Codon counts alone observe coding-sequence composition; tRNA copy numbers summarize genome annotation; tAI or stAI weights are modeled translation-efficiency annotations. None of these, taken separately, is a direct measurement of ribosome motion, protein production, folded structure, biochemical function, or a universal biological principle.

The current blocked comparison is the $M$-only insufficiency test. The required statistic would match, for the same organisms, codon usage, relevant tRNA-copy spectra, and a translation-layer readout, while controlling amino-acid composition. The finite memory at hand does not yet contain such a matched table. Consequently the absence of $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)>0$ is not negative evidence; it is a missing paired statistic. Likewise, the observed seven-of-nine nonzero residual result is not positive evidence for translation efficiency. It only says that the residual coordinates have finite codon-count variation after the quotient by amino-acid composition.

\paragraph{Local inference boundary.}
The local conclusion is deliberately narrow. In the finite contact consisting of human, yeast, and $E.\ coli$ K-12 codon-count tables, the nine-element residual basis $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ is computable, and seven elements are nonzero above threshold $0.01$. This supports the use of $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ as a residual coordinate family for subsequent organism-matched tests.

No claim of ribosome-mediated translation realization follows from this row alone. No folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or universal biological law follows from the nonzero residual variance. No direction of any residual sign should be read as universal. Promotion to a translation readout requires a separate matched contact, and promotion beyond translation requires still another contact at the corresponding physical or functional layer.
