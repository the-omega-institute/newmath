\subsection{Codon-Usage Residual Topology After the Amino-Acid Quotient}
\label{sec:bioresidual-q6-aa-quotient}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record isolates a codon-level question that remains after amino-acid sequence has already been held fixed. The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table projected onto an amino-acid-residual basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$. The point is not to remeasure amino-acid composition. It is to ask whether any synonymous coordinate left over after the amino-acid quotient has a measurable organism-scoped signal on a bounded translation-layer readout. The candidate residual basis has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6} = \{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $K_{AAA}$ denotes the lysine $AAA/AAG$ residual direction, $Arg_{AGR}$ the arginine $AGA/AGG$ direction, $Ile_{AUA}$ the isoleucine $AUA$ direction, and the two leucine coordinates separate the $CUN$ versus $UUR$ family contrast from the $UUA$ versus $UUG$ contrast. The serine coordinates similarly distinguish the $UCR$ versus $AGY$ family contrast from the $UCA$ versus $UCG$ residual contrast. The threonine coordinate separates $ACR$ from $ACY$, and the last coordinate records the antisymmetric third-position stress pair.

\paragraph{Finite row.}
The verified finite contact for run $971b9c868480$ loaded the standard genetic-code partition and exactly three organism-level codon-count tables: human, budding yeast, and \emph{E. coli} K-12. Thus the row is a three-organism contact, not a population estimate. In this contact the nine candidate residual coordinates were computed explicitly. Seven of the nine coordinates exceeded the stated variance threshold $0.01$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#B^{*}_{Q6} &= 9,\\
\#\{q\in B^{*}_{Q6}:\operatorname{Var}(q)>0.01\} &= 7,\\
\frac{7}{9} &= 0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The run status is recorded as passed for this finite computation. The number $0.7777777777777778$ is only a within-row nonzero fraction: it says that the amino-acid-residual projection is not collapsed to the zero geometry on these three codon-count tables. It does not, by itself, estimate a species-wide distribution, a universal effect size, or a mechanism of translation.

\paragraph{Readout statistic.}
The intended survival statistic is conditional. For a residual coordinate $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ and a readout $r$ such as a codon-adaptation weight, ribosome-occupancy measurement, or protein-abundance measurement, the comparison is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z) &= J_{0}(Z)-J_{Q}(Z,q),\\
Z&=(y,c,\text{simpler codon-feature baselines}).
\end{aligned}
$$
The variable $y$ is the amino-acid sequence, so a positive value is not allowed to come from amino-acid composition leakage. A stricter M-density comparison would use $S_{Q}(r\mid Z,\Delta_{M})$ and ask whether adding a residual coordinate improves a baseline already containing the local M-density summary. The finite row in this chapter has not yet supplied the paired organism-matched statistic $S_{Q}(r\mid Z,\Delta_{M})$ for codon usage, tRNA copy numbers, and tAI or stAI weights. Therefore the present record supports only the weaker geometric statement: the post-quotient residual basis has nontrivial finite variation in the observed codon-count contact.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The biological hypothesis is that if Q6 codon topology contributes to DNA-to-protein realization beyond amino-acid sequence, then at least one residual coordinate should survive a controlled readout test. In symbols, the translation-layer test would require an organism and a readout $r$ for which
$$
\begin{aligned}
\exists q\in B^{*}_{Q6}\quad S_{Q}(r\mid Z)>0,
\end{aligned}
$$
or, against an M-density baseline,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\exists q\in B^{*}_{Q6}\quad S_{Q}(r\mid Z,\Delta_{M})>0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed finite row does not yet establish either displayed inequality. What it establishes is the prerequisite coordinate fact: after quotienting by amino-acid identity, the selected Q6 residual coordinates are available as nonzero organism-level contrasts in three concrete codon-count tables, with seven nonzero coordinates among nine candidates at the declared threshold.

\paragraph{Contact discipline.}
Three kinds of contact are relevant to a future controlled test. Per-organism codon-usage tables provide sequence-composition summaries. tRNA gene-copy spectra provide organism-level annotation by anticodon family. tAI or stAI tables provide bounded translation-efficiency readouts, but they are modelled readouts rather than direct observations of ribosome motion. These contacts must be paired at the organism level before a positive residual-survival statement is reported. Separate bridges are not enough: a codon-count table for one organism, a tRNA annotation from another, and a translation-weight table from a third would not define the conditional statistic above.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The finite computation must not be promoted beyond what its rows measure. Nonzero residual variance in codon counts does not assert ribosome-mediated translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical function, protein abundance, or a universal biological law. It also does not assign a universal sign to any coordinate of $B^{*}_{Q6}$. A later translation claim would need a separate controlled readout contact; a folding or function claim would need a different physical or functional contact altogether. The current chapter records a finite organism-scoped residual topology after the amino-acid quotient, and leaves all higher-layer biological conclusions outside the result.
