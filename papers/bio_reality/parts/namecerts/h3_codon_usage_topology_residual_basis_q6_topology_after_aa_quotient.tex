\subsection{Amino-acid-residual codon topology after quotienting}
\label{subsec:codon-usage-topology-residual-basis-q6-after-aa-quotient}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record isolates a codon-level question from the amino-acid sequence it accompanies.  The carrier is the per-organism table of coding-sequence codon counts, projected to an amino-acid-residual coordinate system $B^*_{Q6}$.  The projection asks which codon distinctions remain visible after the ordinary amino-acid composition has already been accounted for.  It therefore does not ask whether a protein folds, whether a protein has a function, or whether a ribosome-level mechanism has been measured.  It asks a narrower finite-row question: among a fixed list of synonymous residual coordinates, which coordinates have nontrivial organism-level variation in the observed codon-count tables.

\paragraph{Residual basis.}
The computed residual basis contains exactly nine coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress pair antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These names are used as finite coordinate labels, not as mechanistic explanations.  The lysine coordinate distinguishes the $AAA$ residual from its amino-acid class background.  The arginine, isoleucine, leucine, serine, and threonine coordinates record synonymous splits that are erased by an amino-acid-only quotient.  The $f_3$-stress coordinate records an antisymmetric third-position pattern rather than a single codon family.  In this chapter the coordinate list is the object under inspection; no direction of biological causation is assigned to any sign in the list.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The finite run loaded one codon-count table for each of three organisms: $Homo\ sapiens$, $Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae$, and $Escherichia\ coli$ K-12.  The NCBI genetic code was loaded for the codon-to-amino-acid map, and the contact scope is explicitly finite: three organism-level tables, not a population estimate over all organisms and not a universal law.  The run identifier was $971b9c868480$, and the named experiment was $residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation$.  Its status was recorded as passed.

\paragraph{Observed residual support.}
Using variance threshold $0.01$, the finite computation found that seven of the nine listed residual coordinates were nonzero across the three organism contacts.  Thus the observed nonzero fraction was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is evidence that the amino-acid quotient does not collapse the full $Q6$ codon-topology list to zero on these three codon-count tables.  The result is deliberately modest: it says that the residual coordinate basis can be populated from finite organism-level sequence summaries and that most of the listed coordinates show nonzero finite variation at the stated threshold.  It does not say that the same fraction holds outside the three organisms, nor that a coordinate with nonzero variance is beneficial, selected, mechanistic, or functionally meaningful.

\paragraph{Readout test.}
A stronger translation-layer test would estimate, for a readout $r$ such as tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, a conditional survival comparison of the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $q\in B^*_{Q6}$ and $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence $y$, contextual covariates $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines.  For an $M$-density baseline, the relevant comparison is $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$.  A positive value would mean that adding a residual coordinate improves the chosen readout model relative to the specified baseline and controls.  The finite data recorded here do not yet provide organism-matched codon usage, tRNA copy-number spectra, and tAI or stAI readout tables with those controlled estimates.  Therefore the present result supports residual-basis nontriviality in codon-count geometry, while the positive survival-score claim remains a separate statistical task.

\paragraph{Relation to $M$-only summaries.}
An $M$-membership or $M$-density summary is too coarse for several distinctions in the nine-coordinate list.  In particular, an $M$-only view cannot by itself test the leucine $CUN/UUR$ split or the lysine $AAA/AAG$ split, because those questions live inside amino-acid classes after the amino-acid quotient has already been imposed.  The finite result therefore motivates keeping $B^*_{Q6}$ as an explicit residual coordinate system when testing codon usage beyond amino-acid composition.  This is a coordinate adequacy statement, not a finding about ribosome traffic or protein output.

\paragraph{Claim limit.}
No translation-realization conclusion follows from the codon-count residuals alone.  The finite computation does not assert ribosome-mediated translation, protein abundance, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical mechanism, biological function, or a global biological law.  A separate matched contact would be needed before any statement about tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance could be counted as support or failure for a residual survival score.  Until that contact is supplied, the correct conclusion is that $B^*_{Q6}$ is a nontrivial finite residual coordinate system on the three observed codon-count tables, with $9$ coordinates tested and $7$ nonzero at threshold $0.01$.
