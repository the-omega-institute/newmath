\subsection{Amino-acid-quotiented codon residuals in a finite organism panel}
\label{sec:bior-eality-q6-aa-quotient-residuals}

\paragraph{Coordinate under test.}
The object recorded here is the codon-level residue that remains after amino-acid identity has been held fixed.  The carrier is the table of per-organism CDS codon counts, projected onto the amino-acid residual basis $B^*_{Q6}$.  In this coordinate system a signal is not counted merely because one amino acid is common, rare, hydrophobic, charged, or compositionally enriched.  The tested coordinates are the synonymous directions that survive the quotient by amino-acid sequence: $K_{AAA}$, $Arg_{AGR}$, $Ile_{AUA}$, $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$, $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$, $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$, $Thr_{ACR/ACY}$, and an antisymmetric third-position stress-pair coordinate.  The biological question is therefore narrower than a codon-usage question in the usual compositional sense: it asks whether the $Q6$ topology leaves a measurable residual after the amino-acid sequence $y$ has already been supplied.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The finite run $971b9c868480$, verified at $2026$-$05$-$31$ $03{:}04{:}10$ UTC, loaded the NCBI genetic code and one codon-count table for each of three organisms: human, budding yeast, and the K-12 strain of $E.\ coli$.  Thus the contact scope is
$$
\begin{aligned}
N_{organisms} &= 3,\\
\mathcal O &= \{\mathrm{homo\_sapiens},\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae},\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a finite organism panel, not a population estimate.  The row-level contact is enough to say that the residual coordinates can be computed on real codon-count tables for these organisms.  It is not enough, by itself, to infer ribosome dynamics, protein output, protein structure, biochemical function, or an organism-independent law.

\paragraph{Residual basis realized on the panel.}
The computation instantiated all nine named coordinates of $B^*_{Q6}$ on the finite organism panel.  The basis size and the observed nontriviality count were
$$
\begin{aligned}
|B^*_{Q6}| &= 9,\\
N_{nonzero} &= 7,\\
\frac{N_{nonzero}}{|B^*_{Q6}|} &= 0.7777777777777778,\\
\tau_{var} &= 0.01.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $\tau_{var}$ is the variance threshold used to decide whether a residual coordinate is nontrivial across the finite contact.  The result is consequently a coordinate-existence result with finite empirical grounding: seven of the nine amino-acid-quotiented codon directions exhibit nonzero residual variance above the stated threshold in the three-organism panel.  The computation also records the exact coordinate list, namely $K_{AAA}$, $Arg_{AGR}$, $Ile_{AUA}$, $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$, $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$, $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$, $Thr_{ACR/ACY}$, and the antisymmetric third-position stress-pair coordinate.

\paragraph{What the finite result does and does not establish.}
The positive part of the record is the existence of nontrivial amino-acid residual codon coordinates in real organism-scoped codon-count data.  The status of the finite computation is passed, with $9$ residual elements computed and $7$ exceeding the stated nonzero threshold.  This supports the use of $B^*_{Q6}$ as a concrete residual coordinate system for later readout tests.

It does not yet establish a positive survival score for translation readouts.  Such a score would require an organism-matched statistic of the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z) &= J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),\\
Z&=(y,c,\Delta_M,\text{simpler codon baselines}),\\
r&\in\{\mathrm{tAI},\mathrm{ribosome\ occupancy},\mathrm{protein\ abundance}\},\\
q&\in B^*_{Q6}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The present row does not contain the paired codon-usage, tRNA-copy, and tAI or stAI table needed to estimate this controlled statistic.  Therefore the finite residual variance is not evidence that any $Q6$ coordinate improves translation-efficiency prediction over an $M$-density baseline.  It also does not show that an $M$-only model is insufficient.  It only identifies the residual directions whose later controlled scores would be meaningful.

\paragraph{Local boundary of interpretation.}
The bounded conclusion is as follows: in the three-organism finite contact, the amino-acid-quotiented residual basis $B^*_{Q6}$ is computable from real codon-count tables, has nine named coordinates, and has seven coordinates with nonzero cross-organism residual variance above $0.01$.  No stronger biological layer follows from this row alone.  In particular, the row does not assert ribosome-mediated translation realization from codon counts alone; it does not assert folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical mechanism, biological function, protein abundance, or universal directionality of residual signs.  Any such stronger claim would need a separate matched readout contact and a controlled estimate in which amino-acid composition and simpler codon baselines are already present in $Z$.
