\subsection{Amino-acid-quotient residual topology for Q6 codon usage}
\label{sec:bior reality-q6-aa-quotient-residual-topology}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record isolates the part of Q6 codon topology that remains after amino-acid composition has been quotiented away.  The measured object is not a protein, a ribosome trace, or a functional phenotype.  It is the per-organism codon-count table projected onto an amino-acid-residual coordinate system.  The coordinate list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress antisymmetry}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The intended test is whether any coordinate in this residual basis contributes to a readout $r$ after controlling for amino-acid sequence $y$, local context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines.  In the notation used here, the desired survival comparison has the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z) = J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\qquad Z=(y,c,\hbox{simpler codon-feature baselines}),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$.  A positive value would mean that the residual coordinate improves the bounded readout model under the specified controls.  The finite row considered here does not yet contain that controlled statistic; it contains the preliminary residual geometry needed before such a statistic can be interpreted.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The computation used one codon-count table for each of three organisms: \emph{Homo sapiens}, \emph{Saccharomyces cerevisiae}, and \emph{Escherichia coli} K12.  The genetic-code table was loaded, and the run was recorded under experiment run identifier $971b9c868480$.  The contact scope is therefore finite and organism-specific:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N_{organisms}=3,
\qquad
\mathcal O=\{\hbox{human},\hbox{yeast},\hbox{E. coli K12}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is not a population estimate over organisms, not a universal distributional law, and not an inference from a random sample of genomes.  It is a bounded three-organism check that the residual coordinates are not vacuous after amino-acid composition is removed.

\paragraph{Residual basis.}
Nine residual coordinates were computed.  With variance threshold $0.01$, seven of the nine coordinates were nonzero across the finite contact set.  The recorded scalar summary is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|B^{*}_{Q6}| &= 9,\\
\#\{q\in B^{*}_{Q6}:\operatorname{Var}(q)>0.01\} &= 7,\\
\frac{7}{9} &= 0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the amino-acid quotient does not collapse the Q6 residual basis to a trivial coordinate artifact on these three codon-count tables.  In particular, the list contains distinctions that an amino-acid-only description cannot express: the lysine $AAA/AAG$ residual, the arginine $AGR$ residual, the isoleucine $AUA$ residual, the two leucine residuals $CUN/UUR$ and $UUA/UUG$, the two serine residuals $UCR/AGY$ and $UCA/UCG$, the threonine $ACR/ACY$ residual, and the antisymmetric $f_3$-stress coordinate.  The pass status of the finite computation means that these coordinates were actually evaluated on the stated organism-level codon-count inputs, not merely listed as formal possibilities.

\paragraph{What the row establishes.}
The row establishes a narrow geometric fact: after quotienting by amino-acid identity, the residual Q6 basis has detectable finite variation in the observed codon-count data.  The relevant distinction is between an amino-acid composition explanation and a synonymous-residual explanation.  If a later readout table supplies organism-matched tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein-output measurements, then these nine coordinates give the explicit directions along which a controlled survival comparison can be made.  The row also records why an $M$-density-only variable is not enough to test all of the listed distinctions.  A local optimum based only on $M$-membership cannot by itself separate $CUN/UUR$ leucine structure or the $AAA/AAG$ lysine distinction, because those contrasts live inside the amino-acid residual coordinates rather than in the coarser membership summary.

\paragraph{Blocked stronger claim.}
The stronger translation-layer statement remains blocked.  The contacts named for codon-usage tables, tRNA gene-copy counts, and tAI or stAI weights are compatible with the desired test, but the current row does not contain an organism-matched table in which codon usage, tRNA-copy spectra, and a translation readout are jointly fitted with amino-acid composition controlled.  Consequently the following assertion is not yet supported:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\exists q\in B^{*}_{Q6}\;\exists r\in\{\hbox{tAI},\hbox{ribosome occupancy},\hbox{protein abundance}\}:\quad
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)>0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Nor does the present row decide the null comparison
$$
\begin{aligned}
\forall q\in B^{*}_{Q6},\quad S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)\leq 0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The absence of the paired statistic is a measurement gap, not negative evidence for the residual coordinates and not positive evidence for an $M$-only account.

\paragraph{Local exclusion boundary.}
No translation mechanism follows from this codon-count residual check alone.  No folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, ribosome-mediated process, protein-abundance effect, or universal biological rule is inferred from the seven nonzero residual coordinates.  A nonzero coordinate only says that the corresponding synonymous distinction has finite variation after amino-acid quotienting in the three organism tables.  To move beyond that statement, a separate contact must supply a matched translation or functional readout and must report the controlled survival score under $Z=(y,c,\hbox{simpler baselines})$.  Until then, the chapter records a bounded residual-topology result: $7$ of $9$ Q6 amino-acid-residual coordinates are nontrivial on the finite three-organism codon-count contact, with nonzero fraction $0.7777777777777778$, while all claims about translation realization and higher biological layers remain open.
