\subsection{Codon-topology residuals after the amino-acid quotient}
\label{sec:biorality-codon-q6-residual-basis}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record treats synonymous codon topology only after the amino-acid sequence has already been held fixed.  The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table, projected to an amino-acid-residual coordinate family $B^*_{Q6}$.  The point of this projection is to remove the part of codon usage that is already explained by the amino-acid word $y$, and to ask whether any remaining codon-level distinction still has measurable structure.  The finite contact used here consists of one codon-count table for each of three organisms: \emph{Homo sapiens}, \emph{Saccharomyces cerevisiae}, and \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12.  The NCBI genetic code was loaded in the computation, and the experiment identifier was \texttt{residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation} with run identifier \texttt{971b9c868480}.

\paragraph{Residual basis.}
The computed residual family has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress-pair antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These names are not amino-acid-composition variables.  They are directions left over after quotienting by amino-acid identity.  For example, $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ compares the CUN and UUR leucine codon families inside the same amino-acid class, while $K_{AAA}$ isolates the AAA/AAG lysine distinction after lysine abundance itself has been controlled.  Likewise, the serine coordinates separate the UCR/AGY family contrast and the UCA/UCG contrast, and the final coordinate records an antisymmetric third-position stress-pair direction.

\paragraph{Observed finite signal.}
Across the three organism-level codon-count contacts, the residual computation found that seven of the nine coordinates were nonzero above the stated variance threshold $0.01$.  Thus the finite nonzero fraction was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a finite-contact statement, not a population estimate.  It says that, in the inspected organism set, the amino-acid quotient does not collapse the entire $Q6$ codon-topology family to zero.  Equivalently, the three codon-count tables retain codon-level residual distinctions that are not identical to amino-acid composition itself.

\paragraph{Readout question.}
The natural survival statistic for a later translation-layer readout $r$ is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $q\in B^*_{Q6}$ and $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence $y$ together with the chosen context and simpler codon-feature baselines.  If a matched readout such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance is supplied, then a positive value of $S_Q(r\mid Z)$ would mean that the residual coordinate improves the conditional readback relative to the baseline.  The present finite row does not yet contain that paired controlled statistic.  It establishes that the residual coordinates are computable and nontrivial on the inspected codon-count contacts; it does not by itself establish that any coordinate survives against a translation-efficiency readout.

\paragraph{Why an M-only summary is insufficient for this test.}
A model that keeps only an M-density or other local codon-summary score cannot ask every question represented by $B^*_{Q6}$.  In particular, family contrasts such as $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ and point contrasts such as $K_{AAA}$ are residual distinctions inside amino-acid classes.  They require a coordinate that remains visible after amino-acid composition has been conditioned on.  Therefore the correct test is not whether codon usage has a local optimum under an M-like summary, but whether adding one of the nine residual coordinates improves a controlled readout once $y$ and the other contextual variables are already present in $Z$.

\paragraph{Contacts still needed for promotion.}
Three kinds of empirical contact are relevant to the stronger question: per-organism codon-usage tables, organism-level tRNA gene-copy spectra, and organism-scoped tAI or stAI weights.  The present record has codon-count contact and identifies the other contact classes, but it does not yet include an organism-matched table combining codon usage, tRNA-copy data, and tAI or stAI readouts into estimates of $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$.  Until that statistic exists, the absence of a positive survival estimate is a blocked measurement rather than negative evidence for the residual family.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The result reported here is deliberately narrow.  Nonzero residual variance in codon-count tables does not show ribosome-mediated translation behavior, does not show protein folding, does not show structural order, does not show physical admissibility, does not show biological function, does not provide a biochemical mechanism, and does not state a universal rule across organisms.  Any such stronger conclusion would need an additional matched contact at the relevant layer.  The only conclusion licensed by the finite row is that the $B^*_{Q6}$ residual basis was computed for three organisms and that seven of its nine coordinates were nonzero above variance threshold $0.01$ in that finite inspection.
