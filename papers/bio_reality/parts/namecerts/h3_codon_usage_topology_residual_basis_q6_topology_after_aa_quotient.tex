\subsection{Codon-Usage Residual Topology After Amino-Acid Quotient}\label{sec:biori-codon-usage-q6-residual-topology}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object.}
This record concerns a codon-level residual coordinate system obtained after quotienting away amino-acid composition.  The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table after projection to the amino-acid residual basis $B^*_{Q6}$.  The relevant basis has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress antisymmetric pair}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These coordinates are not amino-acid frequencies.  They are residual contrasts inside synonymous or quasi-synonymous codon neighborhoods after the amino-acid sequence variable $y$ has been retained as a control.  The coordinate therefore asks whether codon topology leaves a measurable remnant once the protein sequence itself has already been accounted for.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The finite computation used one codon-count table per organism for three organisms: \emph{Homo sapiens}, \emph{Saccharomyces cerevisiae}, and \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12.  The NCBI genetic code was loaded for the codon-to-amino-acid interpretation, and the run is recorded as experiment \texttt{residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation} with run identifier \texttt{971b9c868480}.  The verification timestamp is \texttt{2026-05-31T16:05:15+00:00}.  This is a finite organism-level contact with $3$ organisms, not a population estimate and not a universal sampling statement.

\paragraph{Observed residual nontriviality.}
All nine basis elements were computed on the finite tables.  With variance threshold $0.01$, seven of the nine residual coordinates were nonzero across the finite organism panel.  The observed nonzero fraction was therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The result is a coordinate-level fact: after amino-acid quotienting, the supplied codon-count data do not collapse all of $B^*_{Q6}$ to zero.  In particular, the finite row records that the residual basis is computationally populated for the listed organisms and that most of its named coordinates have nonzero finite variance under the stated threshold.

\paragraph{Readout equation.}
For a basis element $q\in B^*_{Q6}$ and a translation-layer readout $r$, the intended controlled score is the improvement of a model containing $q$ over a baseline that already contains amino-acid sequence and context:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),\qquad Z=(y,c,\text{simpler codon-feature baselines}).
\end{aligned}
$$
When an $M$-density baseline is also being tested, the controlled comparison is $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$.  The scientific question is whether any coordinate in $B^*_{Q6}$ improves prediction of a bounded readout such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance after amino-acid composition has already been controlled.  A positive value for one coordinate in one organism would be a bounded survival event for that readout and organism; it would not by itself be a general law.

\paragraph{Blocked statistic.}
The finite row supplies the codon-count contact and records the residual coordinate computation, but it does not supply an organism-matched table combining codon usage, tRNA copy number, and tAI or stAI weights with the controlled estimates $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$.  Consequently, the present evidence does not decide whether an $M$-only model is sufficient.  It also does not establish a positive $B^*_{Q6}$ survival score.  The correct status of the stronger biological claim is a blocked null: the required statistic has not yet been computed on a paired external readout, so the absence of that statistic is not evidence for or against $M$-only sufficiency.

\paragraph{Interpretive boundary.}
The verified finite facts stop at codon-count residual geometry.  They do not assert ribosome-mediated translation realization from codon counts alone, and they do not assert protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or a universal biological rule.  Nonzero residual variance does not promote itself into translation efficiency, ribosome occupancy, protein output, folded structure, mechanism, or function.  Any such promotion would require a separate organism-matched contact that directly supplies the relevant readout and supports the controlled comparison.  Likewise, the signs of the residual coordinates in the finite panel must not be interpreted as universal directionality.

\paragraph{Conclusion.}
The ready result is therefore narrow but concrete.  In three organism-level codon-count contacts, the nine-coordinate amino-acid-residual basis $B^*_{Q6}$ was computed, and seven coordinates were nonzero at variance threshold $0.01$, giving nonzero fraction $0.7777777777777778$.  This validates the residual basis as a finite codon-topology coordinate for further testing.  It does not yet validate the stronger claim that any residual coordinate survives against an amino-acid-controlled and $M$-controlled translation readout.
