\subsection{Q6 codon residual topology after amino-acid quotient}
\label{subsec:q6-codon-residual-topology-after-aa-quotient}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record isolates a codon-level residual coordinate system after the amino-acid sequence has already been fixed. The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table, projected away from amino-acid composition and read in the amino-acid residual basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$. The computation used one codon-count table for each of three organisms: human, budding yeast, and the K-12 strain of Escherichia coli. The genetic-code table was loaded in the same run, so the quotient by amino acid is not an informal grouping but a finite table operation over the standard codon-to-amino-acid map.

\paragraph{Residual basis.}
The basis tested here has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress antisymmetric pair}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These coordinates are not amino-acid composition variables. They are the remaining synonymous distinctions available after the amino-acid quotient has been imposed. The finite computation reports that all nine basis elements were instantiated, and that seven of the nine had organism-dependent variance above the threshold $0.01$. Thus the observed nonzero fraction was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The result is therefore not a population estimate and not a universal law; it is a three-organism finite contact showing that most of the chosen synonymous residual coordinates are nontrivial in the sampled organism table.

\paragraph{What the finite row establishes.}
The finite row establishes a coordinate-level fact: after controlling for the amino acid named by each codon, the selected $Q6$ distinctions do not all collapse to zero on the sampled codon-count tables. In particular, the tested residuals include lysine $AAA$ versus $AAG$ pressure through $K_{AAA}$, the arginine $AGR$ branch, the isoleucine $AUA$ branch, the two leucine separations $CUN/UUR$ and $UUA/UUG$, the two serine separations $UCR/AGY$ and $UCA/UCG$, the threonine $ACR/ACY$ separation, and an antisymmetric third-position stress coordinate. The run identifier was $971b9c868480$, and the finite status was reported as passed for the residual-basis computation. This is enough to justify using $B^{*}_{Q6}$ as a concrete residual coordinate chart for codon-usage comparison across the three sampled organisms.

\paragraph{Survival score target.}
The intended biological test is stronger than the coordinate fact. For a readout $r$ such as a bounded codon-adaptation weight, ribosome-occupancy statistic, or protein-abundance statistic, the target comparison is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $Z$ contains the amino-acid sequence $y$, organismal context, and simpler codon-feature baselines. A positive value would mean that adding a residual coordinate $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ improves the tested readout relative to the baseline used in that comparison. The finite row in this record does not yet supply those paired $J_0$ and $J_Q$ estimates. It supplies the prerequisite residual chart and the fact that seven of nine residual coordinates are nonzero on the sampled codon-count contact.

\paragraph{M-density limitation.}
A model that only measures membership in a local codon class or aggregate density cannot by itself test distinctions such as $CUN$ versus $UUR$ for leucine or $AAA$ versus $AAG$ for lysine. Those contrasts live precisely in the residual chart after amino-acid composition has been fixed. The present row therefore separates two questions. First, does the residual chart contain nontrivial finite organism-level variation? In this run, yes: seven of nine coordinates were nonzero above $0.01$. Second, does any such coordinate improve a translation-layer readout after amino-acid composition and simpler baselines are controlled? This row does not answer that second question because the organism-matched codon-usage, tRNA-copy, and tAI or stAI statistic has not yet been supplied.

\paragraph{Contact scope.}
Three contact classes are relevant. Per-organism codon-usage tables can test codon-count geometry but do not observe ribosome dynamics. Organism-level tRNA gene-copy spectra can supply anticodon-family structure, especially for leucine and related synonymous branches, but copy number alone is still an annotation summary rather than a direct translation-dynamics measurement. Organism-scoped tAI or stAI weights can serve as a bounded translation-layer readout when kept separate from the coordinate construction. The present row contains the codon-count contact and the named need for the tRNA-copy and tAI or stAI readbacks, but it does not contain the controlled paired statistic joining them.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
No translation realization follows from the nonzero residual variance alone. No ribosome-mediated mechanism, folding behavior, physical admissibility, protein function, biochemical mechanism, protein-output claim, or universal biological rule follows from this row. The observed signs of the residual coordinates must not be read as universal directionality. The row licenses only the finite statement that, in human, budding yeast, and E. coli K-12 codon-count summaries, the amino-acid-residual $Q6$ chart has nine instantiated coordinates and seven nonzero coordinates above the declared variance threshold. Any stronger claim requires a separate paired readout in which $S_Q(r\mid Z)$ is actually estimated under amino-acid-composition control.
