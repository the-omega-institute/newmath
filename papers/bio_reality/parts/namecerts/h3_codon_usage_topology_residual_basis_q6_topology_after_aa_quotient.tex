\subsection{Q6 residual topology after amino-acid quotient}
\label{sec:biorality-q6-residual-topology-aa-quotient}

\paragraph{Object of the finite record.}
This record concerns codon-level synonymous residuals after the amino-acid sequence has already been quotiented out.  The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table, read through the amino-acid residual basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$ rather than through raw codon frequencies alone.  The finite run named \texttt{residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation}, with run identifier \texttt{971b9c868480}, loaded the NCBI genetic code and one codon-count table for each of three organisms: \texttt{homo\_sapiens}, \texttt{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}, and \texttt{escherichia\_coli\_k12}.  Thus the observed scope is a three-organism contact, not a population estimate over all genomes or all translation systems.

\paragraph{Residual coordinates.}
The basis used by the finite computation has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6} = \{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $K_{AAA}$ separates lysine AAA from its amino-acid-matched synonym class, $Arg_{AGR}$ isolates the AGR branch inside arginine usage, and $Ile_{AUA}$ isolates the AUA branch inside isoleucine usage.  The two leucine coordinates split the $CUN$ family from the $UUR$ family and then split $UUA$ from $UUG$.  The serine coordinates separately test $UCR$ against $AGY$ and $UCA$ against $UCG$.  The threonine coordinate compares $ACR$ with $ACY$, and the final coordinate records the antisymmetric third-position stress pair.  These are not amino-acid composition variables; they are the residual directions left after the amino-acid readout has been fixed.

\paragraph{Finite numerical outcome.}
The computation found that the residual basis was nontrivial in the finite contact.  Of the nine coordinates, seven had variance exceeding the stated threshold $0.01$ across the three organism-level codon-count contacts.  The recorded nonzero fraction was therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9} = 0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This number is a finite-row statistic about codon-count residual variation in human, yeast, and \textit{E. coli} K-12.  It says that the amino-acid quotient does not collapse the displayed Q6 residual basis to zero on this small observed panel.  It does not say that the same fraction holds across organisms generally, nor that the signs of the residual coordinates define universal biological directionality.

\paragraph{Controlled survival question.}
The intended readout statistic is conditional.  For a readout $r$ such as tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, and for context $Z$ containing the amino-acid sequence $y$ together with local sequence context and simpler codon-feature baselines, the proposed comparison is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value would mean that the coordinate $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ improves the chosen readout model after amino-acid composition and simpler codon features have already been controlled.  The finite codon-count run does not yet supply this statistic.  It supplies the residual coordinate geometry and shows that seven of the nine displayed coordinates are nonzero at the stated variance threshold in the three-organism panel.  A separate paired table matching codon usage, tRNA-copy information, and tAI, stAI, ribosome-occupancy, or protein-output readouts is still required before any positive survival claim can be made.

\paragraph{M-density limitation.}
The finite record also fixes the reason why an $M$-only codon summary is too narrow for this question.  A model based only on an $M$-density baseline can describe a local codon-feature optimum, but by construction it cannot test the residual splits $CUN/UUR$ or $AAA/AAG$ once amino-acid identity is held fixed.  The appropriate counterfactual test is therefore not whether an $M$-summary varies, but whether adding some coordinate $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ improves a controlled readout beyond that baseline:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)>0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The present row does not report such a positive value.  It records the blocked boundary: the contacts for codon usage, tRNA gene-copy spectra, and tAI or stAI weights identify the required measurement classes, but the paired controlled statistic has not yet been curated for this claim.

\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.}
The verified status of the finite run is \texttt{passed}, but its conclusion is deliberately narrow.  It establishes only that the listed amino-acid-residual Q6 coordinates can be computed from finite per-organism codon-count contacts and that seven of nine coordinates are nonzero above threshold $0.01$ in the three organisms examined.  No translation-realization conclusion follows from codon-count residual variation alone.  In particular, this row does not establish ribosome-mediated dynamics, protein abundance, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical mechanism, biological function, or a universal rule of codon usage.  Any such promotion requires an additional contact that directly measures the corresponding layer and an amino-acid-composition-controlled comparison against the simpler baseline.
