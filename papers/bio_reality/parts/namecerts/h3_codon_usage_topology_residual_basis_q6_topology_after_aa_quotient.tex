\subsection{Amino-Acid-Quotiented Codon Residual Topology}
\label{sec:bior:codon-usage-q6-aa-quotient}

\paragraph{Finite row and object of measurement.}
The row recorded under run identifier $971b9c868480$ tests whether the proposed $Q6$ codon topology leaves nontrivial synonymous residue after quotienting by amino-acid identity.  The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table after projection to the amino-acid-residual basis $B^*_{Q6}$.  The finite contact uses exactly three organism-scoped codon-count summaries: \texttt{homo\_sapiens}, \texttt{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}, and \texttt{escherichia\_coli\_k12}.  The NCBI genetic code table is loaded in the row, and the contact scope is explicitly finite: one codon-count table per organism, not an estimate of a biological population.

\paragraph{Residual basis.}
The computed basis has nine named elements:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These coordinates are not amino-acid composition coordinates.  They are the surviving synonymous directions left after the amino-acid sequence has already been treated as a conditioning variable.  Hence a signal in this coordinate system is a candidate codon-level residual, not a protein-sequence claim and not a claim about folded molecular form.

\paragraph{Observed finite-row result.}
The row reports $9$ basis elements, of which $7$ have nonzero residual variance above the stated variance threshold $0.01$.  The corresponding finite-row fraction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the only numerical survival-like statement established by the row itself: the amino-acid quotient does not collapse the proposed $Q6$ residual basis to zero on the three observed organism tables.  The row status is \texttt{passed}, and the experiment identifier is \texttt{residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation}.  The result therefore supports the narrower proposition that the basis is computationally populated on finite codon-count data after amino-acid quotienting.

\paragraph{Conditional readout statistic.}
For a downstream readout $r$, the intended survival comparison is conditional.  If $Z$ contains the amino-acid sequence $y$, organism and sequence context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines, then each residual coordinate $q\in B^*_{Q6}$ is to be tested by a score of the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,q)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value would mean that adding the coordinate $q$ improves the specified readout model relative to the baseline under the same conditioning information.  The present finite row does not report such a positive value for tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance.  It reports only the prior coordinate fact that several amino-acid-residual codon directions remain nonzero in organism-level codon-count summaries.

\paragraph{Why the $M$-only baseline is insufficient as a test.}
The row separates membership in a simpler codon class from the residual basis.  A model that only records an $M$-density can be locally useful, but it cannot by itself interrogate contrasts such as $CUN$ versus $UUR$ in leucine or $AAA$ versus $AAG$ in lysine.  Those contrasts live inside synonymous amino-acid fibers.  Once amino-acid composition is controlled, the relevant alternatives are precisely the residual directions listed above.  Thus the finite row makes a methodological point: a test of codon-topology participation must compare $M$-density against the named residual coordinates rather than replacing those coordinates by $M$ alone.

\paragraph{Contacts and blocked null.}
Three contact classes are registered for later testing: per-organism codon-usage summaries, organism-level tRNA gene-copy spectra, and organism-scoped tAI or stAI weights.  The current row has the codon-count side populated for three organisms, but it does not yet contain an organism-matched table combining codon usage, relevant tRNA copy numbers, and tAI or stAI weights into amino-acid-composition-controlled estimates of $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$.  Therefore the absence of that controlled statistic is a blocked null, not negative evidence against the residual basis and not positive evidence for translation efficiency.

\paragraph{Local claim boundary.}
The finite result does not assert ribosome-mediated translation realization from codon-count residuals alone.  It also does not assert protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical mechanism, biological function, protein abundance, or any universal biological law.  Nonzero residual variance across the three organism tables is a coordinate-level observation.  A translation-layer conclusion would require a separate matched readout contact, and any higher-layer conclusion would require its own independent physical or functional contact.
