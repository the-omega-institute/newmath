\subsection{Amino-acid residual topology in organism-level codon counts}
\label{sec:bior-h3-codon-usage-topology-residual-basis-q6}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record considered here is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table after quotienting away the amino-acid sequence coordinate.  The remaining coordinates are the synonymous residual directions in the nine-element basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$.  In this finite row the basis is
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The contacted organisms are human, budding yeast, and the K--12 strain of Escherichia coli.  The run identifier is $971b9c868480$, and the data table states that the NCBI genetic code was loaded and that one codon-count table was available per organism.  Thus the row is a three-organism finite contact, not an estimate over a biological population.

\paragraph{Residual computation.}
The computation reports all nine named residual coordinates as constructed.  The amino-acid quotient residual test used variance threshold $0.01$ and found $7$ nonzero residual directions among $9$ basis elements.  Equivalently, the observed nonzero fraction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the relevant finite fact: after amino-acid composition has been treated as the controlled coordinate, the codon-count tables still contain nontrivial synonymous residual variation in the $Q6$ basis.  The result is not a statement about every organism, and it is not a statement about an average species-level law.  It says only that, in the three contacted codon-count summaries, the residual coordinate system is not collapsed to zero after the amino-acid quotient.

\paragraph{Readout question.}
The intended survival score for a residual coordinate $q$ is a conditional comparison against a context $Z$ containing the amino-acid sequence coordinate $y$, local context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines.  For a readout $r$ such as tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, the local score is written
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z,q)=J_{0}(Z)-J_{Q}(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value would mean that the residual coordinate improves the chosen readout relative to the baseline model under the stated conditioning.  The present finite row does not contain those paired controlled scores.  It contains the antecedent coordinate fact: $B^{*}_{Q6}$ can be evaluated on organism-level codon-count summaries, and the resulting residuals are nontrivial in $7$ of $9$ tested directions under the stated threshold.  The stronger comparison against an $M$-density baseline remains blocked until codon-usage, tRNA-copy, and tAI or stAI readouts are paired organism by organism and evaluated with amino-acid composition held in the conditioning set.

\paragraph{Why the quotient matters.}
An $M$-only codon feature can register local optimality inside a restricted codon family, but it cannot by itself distinguish the $CUN$ versus $UUR$ leucine split or the $AAA$ versus $AAG$ lysine split after the amino-acid coordinate is fixed.  The nine-element residual basis was therefore chosen to ask a sharper question: whether synonymous codon topology leaves a detectable coordinate after the protein sequence coordinate has already been accounted for.  The finite answer in this row is positive at the level of residual variance, because the run reports $7$ nonzero directions out of $9$ across the three contacted organisms.

\paragraph{Local evidential status.}
The row status is passed for the bounded claim that the residual basis was computed and that the amino-acid quotient did not erase all $Q6$ residual structure in the contacted tables.  The organisms are exactly $homo\_sapiens$, $saccharomyces\_cerevisiae$, and $escherichia\_coli\_k12$, with organism count $3$.  The source contact is sequence-table contact: it observes codon-count or codon-frequency summaries, not ribosome motion, not peptide folding, and not biological function.  tRNA copy-number and tAI or stAI tables are relevant future readouts, but the current row records them as required contact classes rather than as a completed controlled statistic.

\paragraph{Claim boundary.}
No translation-realization result follows from this codon-count residual computation alone.  In particular, this row does not establish ribosome-mediated dynamics, protein abundance, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or a universal biological rule.  It also does not assign a universal sign to any residual coordinate.  Such claims would require a separate matched readout contact and a conditional comparison showing $S_{Q}(r\mid Z,q)>0$ for a specified $q$, organism, and readout.  Until that paired statistic exists, the scientifically licensed conclusion is narrower: the finite codon-count contact supports a nontrivial amino-acid-residual coordinate geometry for $B^{*}_{Q6}$ in $3$ contacted organisms, with $7/9$ residual directions nonzero at threshold $0.01$.
