\subsection{Amino-acid-quotient residual topology for codon usage}
\label{sec:bior-reality-q6-aa-quotient-residual-topology}

\paragraph{Scientific object.}
This record concerns codon-level synonymous residuals after the amino-acid sequence has already been held fixed.  The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table projected onto the amino-acid residual basis $B^*_{Q6}$.  The point of the projection is not to remeasure amino-acid composition.  It is to ask whether the part of codon usage left over after the amino-acid quotient has any finite, organism-indexed shape in the $Q6$ coordinate family.  The nine tested coordinates are \texttt{K\_AAA}, \texttt{Arg\_AGR}, \texttt{Ile\_AUA}, \texttt{Leu\_CUN\_vs\_UUR}, \texttt{Leu\_UUA\_vs\_UUG}, \texttt{Ser\_UCR\_vs\_AGY}, \texttt{Ser\_UCA\_vs\_UCG}, \texttt{Thr\_ACR\_vs\_ACY}, and \texttt{f3\_stress\_pair\_antisymmetric}.  These coordinates are treated as residual readbacks, not as protein-level causes.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The finite row used here loaded one codon-count table for each of three organisms: \texttt{homo\_sapiens}, \texttt{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}, and \texttt{escherichia\_coli\_k12}.  The NCBI genetic code was loaded for the computation, and the contact scope is explicitly finite: human, yeast, and \emph{E. coli} K-12 are the contacted organisms, not a sampled population from which a universal biological frequency is estimated.  The residual basis was computed with nine elements, and the computation reports that seven of the nine residual coordinates are nonzero at variance threshold $0.01$.  Thus the observed finite fraction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9} &= 0.7777777777777778 .
\end{aligned}
$$
This establishes only that the amino-acid quotient has not collapsed the contacted $Q6$ codon topology to the zero residual vector on this finite row.

\paragraph{Residual readout.}
For a basis element $q \in B^*_{Q6}$ and a readout $r$, the intended survival comparison is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z) &= J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence $y$, organismal context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines.  A positive value would mean that the residual coordinate improves the bounded readout relative to the baseline used in the comparison.  The present row does not yet supply organism-matched controlled estimates of $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$ for tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance.  It therefore records residual nontriviality after amino-acid quotienting, while leaving the survival-score claim unpromoted.

\paragraph{Why the quotient matters.}
A codon table can differ across organisms because amino-acid composition differs.  Such a difference is not a synonymous-residual effect.  The quotient condition forces the biological question into the part orthogonal to amino-acid identity: after $y$ is included in $Z$, only the remaining codon choice within synonymous families is eligible to survive.  This is why the Leu coordinates separate \texttt{CUN} versus \texttt{UUR} and \texttt{UUA} versus \texttt{UUG}, why Lys distinguishes \texttt{AAA} from its synonymous partner, and why the Ser, Arg, Ile, Thr, and third-position stress coordinates are listed as separate residual axes.  The observed value $7/9$ says that this residual basis has finite organism-level variation in the contacted codon-count tables.

\paragraph{Contacts and blocked readouts.}
Three outside contact classes are relevant to a stronger translation-layer test: per-organism codon-usage tables, organism-level tRNA gene-copy spectra, and organism-scoped tAI or stAI weights.  These contacts can support codon-usage distribution checks, tRNA-family copy-number comparisons, and bounded adaptation-weight readbacks.  They do not by themselves observe ribosome motion, protein output, folded structure, biochemical mechanism, physical admissibility, biological function, or a global biological rule.  The present memory keeps these contact classes separate: it has codon-count residuals for three organisms, but it does not yet contain a paired table joining codon usage, tRNA copy number, and tAI or stAI weights with amino-acid composition controlled in the same statistic.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The verified finite fact is that the amino-acid-residual basis $B^*_{Q6}$ was computed for nine named coordinates across three contacted organisms, and seven coordinates were nonzero at the stated variance threshold.  The computation status is \texttt{passed}.  The result supports the local statement that the $Q6$ residual coordinate system has nontrivial finite organism-indexed codon-count content after amino-acid quotienting.  It does not yet support the stronger assertion that any coordinate has positive survival on tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance after controlling for amino-acid composition and an $M$-density baseline.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
No translation-realization claim follows from the codon-count residuals alone.  No folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, ribosome-mediated mechanism, protein-abundance effect, or universal directionality follows from the finite nonzero residual variance.  A later promotion would require a separate matched readout contact and a controlled statistic showing $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)>0$ for at least one residual coordinate and at least one organism-readout pair.
