\subsection{Amino-acid residual topology for Q6 codon usage}\label{sec:h3-codon-usage-topology-residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record treats per-organism coding-sequence codon counts only after the amino-acid sequence has been held fixed.  The data carrier is therefore not raw codon usage as a whole, but the quotient residual left after amino-acid composition has been removed.  In the finite run with identifier $971b9c868480$, three organism-level codon-count tables were loaded: $\mathrm{homo\_sapiens}$, $\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}$.  The NCBI genetic code was present in the computation, and the contact scope was one codon-count table per organism.  This is a finite three-organism contact, not a population estimate.

\paragraph{Residual coordinates.}
The residual basis $B^*_{Q6}$ used in this row has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding finite labels in the computation are $K\_AAA$, $Arg\_AGR$, $Ile\_AUA$, $Leu\_CUN\_vs\_UUR$, $Leu\_UUA\_vs\_UUG$, $Ser\_UCR\_vs\_AGY$, $Ser\_UCA\_vs\_UCG$, $Thr\_ACR\_vs\_ACY$, and $f3\_stress\_pair\_antisymmetric$.  The row reports that all nine residual elements were computed, and that the computation is a finite contact rather than a sampled biological law.  The purpose of this coordinate choice is to test codon distinctions that remain visible after amino-acid identity has already been accounted for, especially distinctions such as $AAA/AAG$ and $CUN/UUR$ that are invisible to a model that records only an amino-acid-level synonymy class or a local codon-density summary.

\paragraph{Observed nontriviality.}
The amino-acid quotient check used variance threshold $0.01$.  Out of the nine residual coordinates, seven were nonzero at that threshold.  Thus the observed nonzero fraction was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This number has a narrow interpretation.  It says that, in the three loaded organism tables, the quotient residual basis is not collapsed to zero after amino-acid composition is removed.  It does not say that any residual coordinate is beneficial, mechanistic, universal, or predictive of a protein-level outcome.  The row establishes that the residual coordinate system has finite empirical variation on the available codon-count contacts.

\paragraph{Readout equation.}
For a later translation-layer readout $r$, the intended survival comparison is conditional.  If $Z$ contains the amino-acid sequence $y$, organismal context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines, then a residual coordinate $q\in B^*_{Q6}$ would be scored by comparing a baseline model with a model that additionally receives $q$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,q)&=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value would mean that the residual coordinate improves the chosen readout under the specified conditioning.  The finite row here does not supply such a paired statistic.  It supplies codon-count residual variation, while the controlled survival statistic requires organism-matched codon usage, tRNA copy-number spectra, and a readout such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance under amino-acid composition control.

\paragraph{Relation to M-only summaries.}
An $M$-only codon summary may capture a local codon-density optimum, but it cannot by itself distinguish every residual direction listed above.  In particular, $AAA/AAG$ and $CUN/UUR$ comparisons require the quotient residual coordinates rather than a summary that has already merged away the relevant synonym choice.  The finite row therefore motivates the following bounded diagnostic: after controlling for amino-acid sequence and baseline codon features, test whether any coordinate in $B^*_{Q6}$ improves a translation-layer readout beyond an $M$-density baseline.  The available row stops before that diagnostic is evaluated; it only shows that the residual directions exist as nontrivial finite coordinates in the three organism tables.

\paragraph{Reality contacts and local exclusion.}
Three contact classes are named for possible follow-up: per-organism codon-usage tables, organism-level tRNA gene-copy counts, and organism-scoped tAI or stAI weights.  Codon-usage tables observe coding-sequence composition.  tRNA copy-number tables observe genome-annotation summaries.  tAI or stAI tables provide modeled codon-adaptation weights.  None of these, taken alone, observes ribosome dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, molecular mechanism, or a universal biological rule.  Consequently this row must not be read as a claim of translation realization from codon counts alone; it also must not be read as evidence for folding, structural order, protein function, physical realizability, mechanism, or universality.  Those stronger statements require a separate matched contact that directly measures the relevant layer.

\paragraph{Finite verdict.}
The passed finite result is the non-collapse of the amino-acid residual basis on the three available organism codon-count contacts.  The precise finite content is: $3$ organisms, $9$ residual coordinates, $7$ nonzero residual coordinates at threshold $0.01$, and nonzero fraction $0.7777777777777778$.  The scientific use of this row is therefore to justify keeping $B^*_{Q6}$ as a candidate residual coordinate system for controlled readout tests.  It does not yet justify a positive survival claim $S_Q(r\mid Z,q)>0$, and it does not decide whether an $M$-only baseline is sufficient for translation-layer readouts.
