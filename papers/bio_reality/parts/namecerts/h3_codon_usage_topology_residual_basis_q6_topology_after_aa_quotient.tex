\subsection{Amino-acid-residual codon topology after quotienting}
\label{sec:h3-codon-usage-topology-residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient}

\paragraph{Question.}
The record asks whether a codon-level topology can leave a measurable residue after amino-acid identity has already been accounted for. The carrier is not a protein-folding object, a kinetic mechanism, or a universal rule about translation. It is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table, projected to an amino-acid-residual coordinate family $B^*_{Q6}$. The intended readout is conditional: for a basis element $q \in B^*_{Q6}$ and a readout $r$, compare a baseline score $J_0(Z)$ against a score $J_Q(Z,q)$ after adding the residual coordinate, with $Z$ containing amino-acid sequence $y$, contextual variables, and simpler codon-feature baselines. The reported quantity is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r \mid Z) = J_0(Z) - J_Q(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value would only say that the coordinate improves that bounded readout under those controls. It would not by itself identify a ribosome mechanism, protein abundance law, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or organism-independent principle.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The finite row used here is experiment run $971b9c868480$, with status $\mathrm{passed}$. The loaded sequence contact consists of one codon-count table per organism for exactly three organisms: $\mathrm{homo\_sapiens}$, $\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}$. The NCBI genetic code was loaded for the codon-to-amino-acid quotient. This is a finite three-organism contact, not a population estimate and not a sampling statement about all organisms.

\paragraph{Residual coordinate family.}
The residual basis computed in the finite row has nine named elements:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6} = \{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress antisymmetric pair}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These coordinates are designed to remain visible only after quotienting by amino-acid identity. Thus the object being measured is not the amino-acid sequence itself, but the part of synonymous codon organization that can still vary inside the same amino-acid readback. In particular, the Leu coordinates separate $CUN$ from $UUR$ and also $UUA$ from $UUG$; the Lys coordinate isolates $AAA$ within the $AAA/AAG$ pair; the Arg, Ile, Ser, Thr, and $f_3$-stress coordinates test analogous residual distinctions.

\paragraph{Observed finite-row result.}
All nine basis elements were computed on the three-organism codon-count contact. Using the declared variance threshold $0.01$, seven of the nine coordinates were nonzero after amino-acid quotienting. The nonzero fraction is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9} = 0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This verifies that the residual basis is not collapsed by the amino-acid quotient on this finite contact. The finite result is a coordinate-level observation: the projected codon-count tables retain nontrivial synonymous structure in most of the tested directions. It does not yet estimate $S_Q(r \mid Z)$ for tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, because those require organism-matched readout tables and an explicit controlled comparison against the baseline variables.

\paragraph{Relation to M-density baselines.}
A model that only records an $M$-density-style local optimum cannot test several distinctions that remain present in $B^*_{Q6}$. The finite row is therefore useful as a diagnostic of what an amino-acid-residual analysis must be able to represent: $CUN/UUR$ Leu separation, $AAA/AAG$ Lys separation, and the other listed within-amino-acid contrasts are not reducible to a mere assertion that an organism has a locally dense preferred-codon set. The present data show that the residual coordinates can be formed and that seven of them have nonzero finite variance across the three loaded organism contacts. They do not show that any such coordinate survives a translation-efficiency readout once amino-acid composition and context are controlled.

\paragraph{Readout status.}
The relevant readout contacts are separate: per-organism codon-usage tables, organism-level tRNA gene-copy spectra, and tAI or stAI weights. The current finite row has the codon-count side and the residual-coordinate computation, but it does not include a paired organism-matched statistic combining codon counts, tRNA copy numbers, tAI or stAI weights, and amino-acid-composition controls. Consequently the proper status is a bounded coordinate finding plus a blocked readout claim. A later positive value of $S_Q(r \mid Z,\Delta_M)$ for at least one $q \in B^*_{Q6}$ and one controlled readout would support a translation-layer survival statement for that readout and organism. Until that paired statistic exists, neither $S_Q>0$ nor insufficiency of an $M$-only baseline is established.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The finite row explicitly forbids promotion from codon-count residual variance to broader biological conclusions. Nonzero residual variance does not assert ribosome-mediated translation realization, protein-output control, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or a universal biological law. The signs of the residual coordinates are not universal directional claims. Any future statement about translation, folding, physical feasibility, function, mechanism, or universality must be supplied by a separate contact that measures that layer under the appropriate controls.
