\subsection{Amino-acid-residual codon topology after the quotient}\label{sec:bior-experiment-codon-usage-topology-residual-basis-q6}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
This record asks whether a codon-topological coordinate can remain visible after amino-acid composition has already been held fixed. The object is not a raw codon-frequency table and not an amino-acid-composition statistic. It is the residual codon coordinate system $B^*_{Q6}$ obtained by projecting per-organism coding-sequence codon counts onto distinctions that are invisible to the amino-acid sequence alone. The working biological question is therefore narrow: after the quotient by amino-acid identity, does any codon-level residual coordinate remain nontrivial in finite organism data, and could such a coordinate later be tested against a bounded translation-layer readout?

\paragraph{Residual coordinates.}
The finite row used nine named residual coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6} = \{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $K_{AAA}$ denotes the lysine residual separating $AAA$ from the amino-acid class containing $AAA$ and $AAG$; $Arg_{AGR}$ isolates the arginine $AGR$ branch; $Ile_{AUA}$ isolates the isoleucine $AUA$ branch; the two leucine coordinates separate $CUN$ from $UUR$ and then $UUA$ from $UUG$; the two serine coordinates separate $UCR$ from $AGY$ and $UCA$ from $UCG$; the threonine coordinate separates $ACR$ from $ACY$; and the final coordinate records an antisymmetric third-position stress pair. The point of this list is that an $M$-membership or density summary is too coarse for these distinctions: it cannot see the $CUN/UUR$ leucine split or the $AAA/AAG$ lysine split once amino-acid identity has been quotiented out.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The run loaded one codon-count table per organism for three organisms: $homo\_sapiens$, $saccharomyces\_cerevisiae$, and $escherichia\_coli\_k12$. The NCBI genetic code was loaded in the same run, so the residual coordinates were evaluated against a fixed codon-to-amino-acid assignment rather than against an informal codon grouping. The experiment identifier was $residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation$, with run id $971b9c868480$, verified at $2026$-$05$-$31T01{:}25{:}04{+}00{:}00$. This is a finite contact over three named organisms, not a population estimate over taxa and not a law over all coding systems.

\paragraph{Observed residual nontriviality.}
The finite computation evaluated all nine coordinates in $B^*_{Q6}$. Seven of the nine coordinates exceeded the reported residual-variance threshold $0.01$. Thus the observed finite fraction of nontrivial residual coordinates was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9} = 0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the concrete result of the row: amino-acid quotienting did not collapse the entire $Q6$ residual basis on the three-organism codon-count contact. In ordinary terms, the codon tables retain detectable synonymous structure after amino-acid composition has been removed. The result is coordinate-level evidence only: it says that the residual basis is not empty on this finite contact and that seven named coordinates vary enough to pass the stated threshold.

\paragraph{Survival-score test left open.}
The intended next statistic is conditional survival of a readout $r$ after controlling for amino-acid sequence $y$, context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines. For each residual coordinate $q \in B^*_{Q6}$, the target contrast has the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r \mid Z) = J_0(Z) - J_Q(Z,q),
\qquad
Z = (y,c,\text{baseline codon features}).
\end{aligned}
$$
A stronger test against an $M$-density baseline would use $S_Q(r \mid Z,\Delta_M)$ and ask whether some residual coordinate improves prediction of a bounded readout such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance after the amino-acid and baseline controls are already present. The finite row does not yet contain such paired controlled estimates. In particular, it does not report a lambda, a p-value, a hit rate, a regression table, or a per-coordinate survival score against a matched translation readout. The present row therefore supports residual-coordinate nontriviality, not positive translation survival.

\paragraph{Available contacts and blocked statistic.}
Three contact classes are named for a later test: per-organism codon-usage summaries, organism-level tRNA gene-copy spectra, and tAI or stAI codon-adaptation weights. These contacts are compatible with a translation-layer readout, but the row records them as separate bridges rather than as a matched table containing codon usage, tRNA copy number, and tAI or stAI weights for the same organism set under amino-acid-composition control. The blocked statistic is therefore local and precise: no current number tests whether any $q \in B^*_{Q6}$ improves over an $M$-density baseline on a controlled readout. Absence of that statistic is not evidence for $M$-only sufficiency, and it is not evidence against the residual basis. It is a missing paired measurement.

\paragraph{Interpretation boundary.}
The verified finite row has status $passed$ for the coordinate computation, but it remains below any claim about realized translation dynamics. Nonzero residual variance in codon-count tables does not by itself establish ribosome-mediated translation, protein abundance, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical function, mechanism, or a universal biological rule. The signs of the residual coordinates are also not universal direction claims. A later promotion would require a separate matched readout contact and an explicit controlled statistic, preferably reporting $S_Q(r \mid Z,\Delta_M)$ per organism and per residual coordinate.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
This row gives a bounded positive result: in three named organisms, with the genetic code fixed and nine amino-acid-residual codon coordinates computed, seven coordinates remained nontrivial above variance threshold $0.01$, giving nonzero fraction $0.7777777777777778$. The result justifies keeping $B^*_{Q6}$ as an active codon-topology coordinate system for future translation-readout testing. It does not yet justify saying that the residual coordinates survive as translation predictors, that an $M$-only model fails on a controlled readout, or that any biological mechanism has been identified.
