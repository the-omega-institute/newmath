\subsection{Amino-Acid-Quotiented Codon Residuals in the Q6 Basis}
\label{subsec:h3-codon-usage-topology-residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient}

\paragraph{Coordinate under test.}
The finite row tests whether the Q6 codon topology leaves a nontrivial residue after amino-acid identity has already been accounted for. The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table, projected onto an amino-acid-residual coordinate basis $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$. The point of this projection is not to re-read amino-acid composition. It asks whether synonymous codon choices, once grouped by their amino-acid assignment, still carry a detectable Q6-shaped coordinate signal.

The computed basis has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{\ast}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These are read as residual contrasts, not as amino-acid counts. For example, the leucine coordinates separate the $CUN/UUR$ family contrast and the $UUA/UUG$ within-family contrast, while the lysine coordinate isolates $AAA$ against its synonymous partner rather than treating lysine abundance itself as the signal.

\paragraph{Finite contact row.}
The finite computation loaded cross-organism codon-count data for three organisms: $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli}$ K-12. The row records one codon-count table per organism, with the NCBI genetic code loaded for the amino-acid quotient. This is a three-organism contact, not a population estimate and not a universal survey. In the recorded run $971b9c868480$, the Q6 residual basis was computed with nine elements and the finite-contact flag was explicitly set.

The quotient result is nontrivial in the following narrow sense. Of the nine residual coordinates, seven exceeded the recorded variance threshold $0.01$ across the three organism rows. Thus the finite nonzero fraction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row status is recorded as passed for this finite residual computation. The status means that the codon-count contact suffices to show that the amino-acid quotient does not collapse all nine Q6 residual coordinates to zero on this finite organism set. It does not mean that any translation-efficiency, ribosome-occupancy, protein-output, folding, physical-admissibility, functional, mechanistic, or universal claim has been established.

\paragraph{Survival statistic and the missing paired readout.}
The intended survival comparison is conditional. For a residual coordinate $q \in B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ and a readout $r$, the proposed score has the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence $y$, organism or genomic context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines. A positive value would mean that adding the residual coordinate improves the conditional readout model against the baseline used in that comparison. The row also specifies the stricter M-baseline version
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)>0,
\end{aligned}
$$
for readouts such as tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance.

The present finite row has not yet supplied that paired statistic. It has codon-count contact, tRNA-copy-number contact as a required class, and tAI or stAI weight contact as a required class, but the recorded mismatch states that there is no curated organism-matched table combining codon usage, relevant tRNA-copy spectra, and tAI or stAI readouts with amino-acid-composition-controlled estimates of $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$. Therefore the residual basis is a legitimate coordinate result, while the survival claim remains unmeasured here. Absence of that paired statistic is a blocking null condition for M-only insufficiency, not evidence for M-only sufficiency and not evidence against the Q6 residual coordinates.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The finite row supports a bounded statement: after quotienting by amino-acid identity, the Q6 residual basis is not empirically empty on the three recorded organism codon-count tables. The observed scalar summary is $9$ tested residual coordinates, $7$ nonzero at threshold $0.01$, and nonzero fraction $0.7777777777777778$. The organisms are human, budding yeast, and $E.\ coli$ K-12, and the row is scoped to per-organism codon-count contact.

The row does not permit a stronger biological reading. In particular, codon-count residual variance alone does not establish translation realization, ribosome-mediated mechanism, protein abundance, folding behavior, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or a global biological law. Such conclusions would require a separate contact that actually measures the relevant layer and a controlled statistic that keeps amino-acid composition and simpler codon variables in $Z$. The current result is therefore best read as a finite coordinate discovery with a clearly marked next test: assemble organism-matched codon usage, tRNA-copy spectra, and tAI, stAI, ribosome-occupancy, or protein-output readouts, then evaluate whether any member of $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ gives positive conditional survival beyond the M-density baseline.
