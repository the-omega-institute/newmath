\subsection{Amino-acid-quotiented codon topology as a residual coordinate system}
\label{sec:bior-entity-h3-codon-usage-topology-residual-basis-q6-topology-after-aa-quotient}

\paragraph{Object measured.}
The record concerns codon-level synonymous residuals after quotienting by amino-acid sequence.  The finite carrier is a per-organism table of coding-sequence codon counts, projected onto the amino-acid residual basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$ rather than read as raw codon abundance.  The quotient is essential: the coordinate is not allowed to treat amino-acid composition as evidence for a codon-topology effect.  The measured basis consists of the following nine named residual directions:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ separates four-codon-box leucine codons from $UUR$ leucine codons, while $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$ keeps the two $UUR$ codons distinct.  The serine coordinates similarly distinguish the $UCR$ and $AGY$ families and then refine the $UCA/UCG$ contrast.  These coordinates are designed to retain synonym-level structure that disappears if the table is compressed to amino-acid sequence alone.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The finite run loaded one codon-count table per organism for exactly three organisms: $Homo\ sapiens$, $Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae$, and $Escherichia\ coli$ K12.  The run also recorded that the NCBI genetic code was loaded.  This is a finite three-organism contact, not a population estimate and not a cross-species law.  The experiment identifier was $residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation$, with run identifier $971b9c868480$, and the finite-row status was recorded as passed.  The numerical contact therefore supports only the existence and nontriviality of these residual coordinates in the sampled tables.

\paragraph{Observed residual nontriviality.}
Across the nine basis elements, seven had nonzero residual variance above the stated threshold $0.01$.  The observed nonzero fraction was therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite result is not that any one sign is universal, nor that a residual coordinate already predicts a translation output.  It says that after amino-acid quotienting, the $B^{*}_{Q6}$ coordinate system is not vacuous on the three organism-level codon-count contacts.  In particular, the construction preserves synonym-level distinctions such as $AAA/AAG$ and $CUN/UUR$ that an $M$-density-only description cannot directly test.

\paragraph{Readout statistic.}
For a biological readout $r$ and a context bundle $Z$, the intended survival comparison is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$, $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence $y$, organismal context, and simpler codon-feature baselines, $J_0$ is the baseline loss, and $J_Q$ is the loss after adding the residual coordinate.  A positive value would mean that the residual coordinate improves the bounded readout under the specified controls.  The present finite row does not yet contain organism-matched estimates of $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$ for tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance.  Consequently the chapter records a prepared test statistic and a nontrivial residual basis, not a completed translation-realization result.

\paragraph{M-only control.}
The local null comparison is that an $M$-only codon variable is sufficient once amino-acid sequence and simpler codon features are controlled.  In that case every tested residual direction should fail to improve the readout:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)\leq 0
\end{aligned}
$$
for all $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ and for each bounded readout under consideration.  The finite-row data already show why this is a meaningful test rather than a restatement: the residual basis has nine coordinates, seven of them are nonzero in the sampled codon-count contact, and several of the named coordinates express synonym contrasts invisible to an amino-acid quotient or to a single local density statistic.  What remains absent is the paired controlled readout table needed to decide whether any of those residual coordinates survives against the $M$ baseline.

\paragraph{Local scientific boundary.}
The finite contact is sequence-composition evidence.  It does not by itself observe ribosome motion, codon dwell time, tRNA charging dynamics, protein abundance, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or a universal rule.  Nonzero residual variance in $Homo\ sapiens$, $Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae$, and $Escherichia\ coli$ K12 is therefore not evidence that the residual directions cause translation efficiency, not evidence of folding, and not evidence of function.  A separate organism-matched readout contact, such as bounded tAI or stAI weights, ribosome-profiling-derived occupancy, or measured protein abundance with amino-acid composition controlled, would be required before a positive survival statement could be made.

\paragraph{Status of the record.}
The chapter-level record is ready as a bounded finite observation: the $B^{*}_{Q6}$ residual basis was computed on three organism-level codon-count contacts, the basis contained nine named elements, and seven elements were nonzero above the $0.01$ variance threshold.  The corresponding stronger biological claim remains conditional.  It would require a paired statistic showing $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)>0$ for at least one residual coordinate, in at least one organism, on a specified translation-layer readout while retaining amino-acid composition and simpler codon-feature controls.
