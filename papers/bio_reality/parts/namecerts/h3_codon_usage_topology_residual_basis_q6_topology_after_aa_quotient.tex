\subsection{Q6 codon topology after amino-acid quotienting}
\label{sec:bior-q6-codon-topology-aa-quotient}

\paragraph{Question.}
The object studied here is not a raw codon-frequency table and not an amino-acid-composition effect.  The finite datum is the residual codon coordinate left after amino-acid identity has already been treated as a controlled component.  For each organism, the carrier is the coding-sequence codon-count vector projected onto the amino-acid-residual basis $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$.  The named residual coordinates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{\ast}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
These nine coordinates ask whether codon topology leaves organism-specific structure after the amino-acid quotient has removed the most direct compositional explanation.  The readout is therefore a residual coordinate system for synonymous codon organization, not a direct assertion about translation speed, folding, protein output, or fitness.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The finite row used here loaded cross-organism codon-count contact for $3$ organisms: $\mathrm{homo\_sapiens}$, $\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}$.  The contact scope is explicitly one codon-count table per organism, with the NCBI genetic code loaded for the amino-acid quotient.  The computation records $9$ residual basis elements and marks the contact as finite rather than a population estimate.  In this finite panel, $7$ of the $9$ residual coordinates have variance above the threshold $0.01$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#B^{\ast}_{Q6} &= 9,\\
\#\{q\in B^{\ast}_{Q6}: \operatorname{Var}(q)>0.01\} &= 7,\\
\frac{7}{9} &= 0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the amino-acid quotient does not collapse the Q6 residual basis to a trivial coordinate list in this finite contact.  The residual basis remains empirically populated across the three organism-level codon-count summaries.

\paragraph{Residual interpretation.}
The intended statistic for a translation-layer test would estimate, for a residual coordinate $q\in B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ and a readout $r$, a conditional survival contrast of the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z) = J_{0}(Z)-J_{Q}(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence variable $y$, organism or sequence context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines.  The present finite row does not compute that statistic.  It only establishes that the residual coordinates are nontrivial after amino-acid quotienting in a three-organism codon-count panel.  A positive value of $S_{Q}(r\mid Z)$ would require an organism-matched readout such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, fitted with amino-acid composition held inside the conditioning set.

\paragraph{What the row supports.}
The passed finite computation supports the narrower statement that the residual basis $B^{\ast}_{Q6}$ is a live coordinate system on observed organism-level codon-count data.  In the loaded panel, the basis has $9$ named coordinates, and $7$ of them show nonzero residual variation above the declared threshold.  This is enough to justify retaining these coordinates as test variables for later controlled readouts.  It is not enough to report a translation-efficiency effect, a ribosome-mediated mechanism, a protein-abundance effect, a structural effect, or a biological law.  In particular, the signs of the residual coordinates in human, yeast, and $E.\ coli$ cannot be promoted to universal directionality because the contact is a finite three-organism panel and not a population-level estimate.

\paragraph{Local negative scope.}
No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or universality follows from this finite codon-count residual contact alone.  The row also does not decide whether an $M$-only codon-density model is insufficient, because the current contact does not include a matched controlled statistic comparing an $M$-density baseline against each $q\in B^{\ast}_{Q6}$.  The correct use of this row is therefore preparatory and bounded: it shows that the amino-acid-residual Q6 basis survives as observable finite codon-count variation in the three named organisms, while all higher biological interpretations remain unavailable until a separate organism-matched readout supplies the relevant contact.
