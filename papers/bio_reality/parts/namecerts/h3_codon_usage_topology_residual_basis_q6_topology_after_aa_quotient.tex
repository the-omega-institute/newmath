\paragraph{Finite record.} This record concerns per-organism coding-sequence codon counts after the amino-acid sequence has been treated as the controlled quotient variable. The object under inspection is not raw codon usage, and it is not an amino-acid composition claim. It is the residual coordinate system $B^{*}_{Q6}$ consisting of nine named directions:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite contact run loaded one codon-count table for each of three organisms: human, budding yeast, and \emph{E. coli} K-12. The run identifier was $971b9c868480$, and the recorded verification time was 2026-05-31T19:45:17+00:00. The genetic-code table was loaded for the computation, so the quotient by amino-acid identity was not inferred from an informal synonym list.

\paragraph{Measured residual nontriviality.} The finite computation evaluated all nine residual directions. Seven of the nine directions had nonzero residual variance above the stated threshold $0.01$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a coordinate-level observation: after amino-acid composition is held fixed as the quotient variable, most of the specified Q6 residual directions are still not collapsed by the three-organism codon-count contact. The observed fact is therefore that the basis is not empty, not purely formal, and not reduced to amino-acid composition in this finite contact. The computation also records that the contact is finite and not a population estimate. It should be read as a three-organism row-level check, not as a statement about all taxa or all coding sequences.

\paragraph{Scientific interpretation.} The relevant biological question is whether any residual direction in $B^{*}_{Q6}$ participates in DNA-to-protein realization beyond the amino-acid sequence itself. The intended survival statistic has the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $q$ is a residual basis element, $r$ is a bounded readout such as tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, and $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence together with contextual and simpler codon-feature baselines. The finite run reported here does not yet compute this statistic. It establishes that the residual coordinates have nontrivial organism-scoped codon-count variation after the amino-acid quotient, which is the necessary coordinate condition for a later survival test. It does not establish that any residual direction improves prediction of a translation-layer readout.

\paragraph{M-density limitation.} A model that only records membership in an $M$-style codon class can be locally useful while still being unable to distinguish several directions in $B^{*}_{Q6}$. In particular, the listed residual basis explicitly separates directions such as $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ and $K_{AAA}$, which are not tested merely by asking for a coarse local codon class density. For this reason, the finite observation is framed as a residual-basis contact rather than as a claim that an $M$-only coordinate has succeeded or failed. The blocked statistical comparison remains the controlled question whether adding one of the nine residual coordinates to an $M$-density baseline increases the survival score for an organism-matched readout.

\paragraph{Local negative scope.} The finite contact does not show ribosome-mediated translation behavior, protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or a universal biological rule. It also does not assign a universal sign to any residual coordinate. A nonzero residual variance in organism-level codon counts is not by itself evidence for translation efficiency, ribosome occupancy, or protein output. Those claims require a separate paired contact containing organism-matched codon usage, relevant tRNA copy-number or adaptation weights, and an amino-acid-composition-controlled readout statistic. Until that paired statistic is present, the result is a bounded coordinate result: seven of nine Q6 residual directions remain nontrivial in three named organisms after the amino-acid quotient.
