\subsection{Amino-acid-quotiented codon residuals in the $Q6$ topology}\label{sec:bior reality-q6-residual-basis}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object of the record.}
This record isolates a codon-level residual question after quotienting by amino-acid sequence.  The observed table is not the amino-acid sequence itself, but per-organism CDS codon-count data projected onto a finite residual basis denoted here by $B^*_{Q6}$.  The point of the quotient is to prevent ordinary amino-acid composition from being mistaken for synonymous-codon geometry.  Thus the relevant coordinates are only those components that remain after the amino-acid class has already been held fixed.

The computed residual basis has nine named elements:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress antisymmetric pair}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $K_{AAA}$ denotes the lysine $AAA$ residual against its synonymous context, $Arg_{AGR}$ denotes the arginine $AGR$ residual, and $Ile_{AUA}$ denotes the isoleucine $AUA$ residual.  The leucine, serine, and threonine entries are explicit within-amino-acid contrasts, rather than amino-acid abundance effects.  The final coordinate records an antisymmetric third-position stress pattern and is treated as a codon-topological coordinate, not as a direct biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Finite computation.}
The finite contact used for this record loaded the NCBI genetic code and one codon-count table for each of three organisms: $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli\ K12}$.  The run identifier is $971b9c868480$, verified at $2026$-$05$-$30$ $20{:}40{:}01+00{:}00$, and the recorded status is passed.  The organism count is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
N_{\mathrm{org}}=3.
\end{aligned}
$$
The basis construction returned nine candidate residual elements and found seven of them nonzero under the stated variance threshold.  With threshold $0.01$, the measured nonzero fraction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#B^*_{Q6} &= 9,\\
\#\{q\in B^*_{Q6}:\operatorname{Var}(q)>0.01\} &= 7,\\
\frac{7}{9} &= 0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a finite-table statement: the computation says that, in these three organism-level codon-count contacts, the amino-acid-residual codon basis is not collapsed to zero.  It is not a population estimate over organisms, not a universal frequency law, and not an inference about all genomes.

\paragraph{Residual survival statistic.}
For a readout $r$, a residual coordinate $q\in B^*_{Q6}$, and a conditioning context $Z$, the intended survival comparison is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,q)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
The conditioning context $Z$ must include the amino-acid sequence information $y$, the relevant sequence or organism context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines.  When an $M$-density baseline $\Delta_M$ is included, the question becomes whether any residual coordinate improves the readout beyond the baseline:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M,q)>0.
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value would be a bounded readout-level survival statement for that organism, that coordinate, and that readout.  It would not by itself be a statement about folded structure, biochemical function, physical admissibility, or a universal biological rule.

\paragraph{What the current row establishes.}
The verified finite row establishes only the following bounded conclusion.  After amino-acid quotienting, the $Q6$ residual basis can be instantiated on three organism-level codon-count tables, and seven of its nine coordinates are nonzero at variance threshold $0.01$.  The computed elements are exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_3\text{-stress antisymmetric pair}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the amino-acid quotient does not erase all of the codon-topological structure in this finite contact.  The result is strongest as a coordinate-level observation: the basis has measurable variation after quotienting by amino-acid identity.  It is weaker than a translation-efficiency result, because no paired controlled statistic has yet been recorded that joins codon usage, tRNA copy number, and tAI or stAI weights into one organism-matched regression or survival comparison.

\paragraph{Blocked null for the $M$-only baseline.}
The relevant counterfactual is that an $M$-only codon-density variable already captures every translation-layer signal available from the codon table.  In that case, after controlling for amino-acid composition and the $M$-density baseline $\Delta_M$, every residual coordinate should fail to improve any admissible readout:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M,q)\leq 0
\end{aligned}
$$
for every $q\in B^*_{Q6}$ and every readout $r$ among tAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance.  The present row does not falsify this null.  It supplies the residual coordinates and confirms nontrivial finite variation, but the paired controlled readout statistic has not been supplied.  Consequently the $M$-only insufficiency claim remains blocked rather than supported or refuted.

\paragraph{Permitted and forbidden readings.}
The permitted reading is narrow: in a three-organism codon-count contact, the amino-acid-residual $Q6$ basis contains nine computable coordinates, seven of which are nonzero above threshold $0.01$.  This justifies treating $B^*_{Q6}$ as a concrete residual coordinate system for later controlled readout tests.  It also justifies keeping contrasts such as $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ and $K_{AAA}$ separate from a bare $M$-density summary, because those contrasts are named coordinates of the residual basis and cannot be tested by a statistic that has already discarded them.

The prohibited reading is broader biological promotion.  No translation-realization conclusion follows from nonzero codon-count residual variance alone.  No ribosome-mediated mechanism, folding conclusion, structural order claim, physical admissibility claim, protein-function claim, or universal biological law follows from this row.  A later claim about translation would require a separate organism-matched contact, for example a controlled tAI or stAI readout, ribosome-occupancy measurement, or protein-abundance measurement, with amino-acid composition kept in the conditioning variables.
