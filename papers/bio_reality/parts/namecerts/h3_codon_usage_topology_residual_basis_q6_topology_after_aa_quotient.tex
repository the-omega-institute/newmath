\subsection{Codon-Usage Topology After Amino-Acid Quotienting}
\label{sec:codon-usage-topology-residual-basis-q6}

\paragraph{Residual coordinate.}
This record isolates a codon-level question that remains after amino-acid identity has already been accounted for.  The carrier is a per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table, projected away from the amino-acid sequence coordinate and then read in the residual basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$.  The nine named residual coordinates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here the point is not that a codon count is already a translation mechanism.  The point is narrower: once amino-acid composition is held fixed, these coordinates ask whether synonymous codon topology still leaves a detectable residual signal.  The Leu coordinates are especially important because an aggregate membership or density variable cannot distinguish the $CUN/UUR$ split, and it also cannot test the $UUA/UUG$ contrast by itself.  The same issue appears for $AAA/AAG$ in lysine: a local optimum over a simpler codon feature is not the same thing as a test of the residual coordinate.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The finite run used three organism-scoped codon-count tables: $homo\_sapiens$, $saccharomyces\_cerevisiae$, and $escherichia\_coli\_k12$.  The NCBI genetic code table was loaded, and the contact scope was one codon-count table per organism.  This is a finite organism contact, not a population estimate over all taxa or all genomes.  In the run identified as $971b9c868480$, all nine residual basis elements were computed, and the finite-contact flag was recorded as true.  With variance threshold $0.01$, seven of the nine residual coordinates were nonzero, so the observed finite-row nonzero fraction was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This establishes only that the amino-acid quotient does not collapse the chosen codon-count residual basis to zero on this three-organism contact.  It does not estimate a universal taxonomic frequency, and it does not by itself select a biological direction for any coordinate.

\paragraph{Survival statistic.}
For a basis element $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ and a readout $r$, the intended controlled statistic compares a baseline objective $J_{0}(Z)$ with a model that is allowed to use the residual coordinate:
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z)=J_{0}(Z)-J_{Q}(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
The conditioning data $Z$ must include the amino-acid sequence $y$, the relevant biological context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines such as an $M$-density coordinate when that coordinate is being tested.  A positive value of $S_{Q}(r\mid Z)$ would mean that the residual coordinate improves the chosen readout under the stated controls.  The permitted readout classes are bounded translation-layer observables such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, but each such readout must be supplied by a matched external table or experiment rather than inferred from codon counts alone.

\paragraph{Blocked-null boundary.}
The present finite row has the coordinate side and the contact categories, but it does not yet contain an organism-matched statistic combining codon usage, relevant tRNA copy-number spectra, and tAI, stAI, ribosome-occupancy, or protein-abundance readout under amino-acid-composition control.  Therefore the positive-survival claim is not reported as established.  The correct status of the stronger probe is a blocked null: the absence of the paired statistic is a missing measurement, not evidence that the residual basis is useless and not evidence that an $M$-only description is sufficient.  In particular, the current data do not test whether any $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ has $S_{Q}(r\mid Z,\Delta_{M})>0$.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The safe conclusion is that the residual basis is nontrivial on the finite codon-count contact after amino-acid quotienting: nine named coordinates were computed, seven crossed the stated nonzero threshold, and the three organisms were human, budding yeast, and $E.\ coli$ K-12.  This supports using the basis as a disciplined coordinate system for a later controlled readout study.  It does not say that codon-count residual variance is already ribosome-mediated translation realization, protein output, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biochemical function, molecular mechanism, or a law applying across organisms.  Any such higher claim requires a separate matched contact that measures the corresponding layer directly and keeps amino-acid composition in the control set.
