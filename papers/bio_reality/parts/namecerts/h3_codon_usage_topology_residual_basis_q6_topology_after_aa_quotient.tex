\subsection{Q6 codon topology after the amino-acid quotient}
\label{sec:q6-codon-topology-after-aa-quotient}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Object of record.}
This record isolates the part of codon usage that remains after amino-acid identity has been accounted for.  The carrier is a per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table projected onto the amino-acid-residual basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$.  The construction is not a protein-level claim.  It is a finite codon-count contact with three named organisms, and its role is to test whether the Q6 codon topology leaves nontrivial within-amino-acid residue after quotienting by the encoded amino-acid sequence.

The finite contact used run $971b9c868480$ of experiment $residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation$.  The NCBI genetic code was loaded, and the organism set had size $3$: $\mathrm{homo\_sapiens}$, $\mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}$.  The contact resolution is one codon-count table per organism.  The scope is therefore a finite comparison over human, yeast, and $E.\ coli$ K-12, not a population estimate over taxa or environments.

\paragraph{Residual coordinates.}
The residual basis has $9$ named elements.  In this row they are
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress pair antisymmetric}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The basis separates synonymous distinctions that are invisible to an amino-acid-only readout.  For example, $K_{AAA}$ distinguishes the lysine codon $AAA$ from its synonymous partner; $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ records the split between the four-codon $CUN$ leucine family and the two-codon $UUR$ leucine family; $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$ records the corresponding two-block serine split; and the $f_{3}$-stress coordinate records an antisymmetric third-position pressure across paired codon families.  These are codon-topological coordinates, not folded-structure or function coordinates.

\paragraph{Finite numerical outcome.}
The finite row reports that all $9$ residual coordinates were computed, with the flag that this is a finite contact and not a population estimate.  Using variance threshold $0.01$, $7$ of the $9$ coordinates were nonzero after the amino-acid quotient.  Thus the row-level nonzero fraction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The status of the computation is recorded as passed.  The mathematical consequence is deliberately narrow: after controlling by amino-acid identity, the chosen codon-count tables still contain nontrivial residual variation along most of the declared $B^{*}_{Q6}$ directions.  This supports the existence of a finite codon-usage residual signal in the row, but it does not by itself identify a translation-efficiency effect.

\paragraph{Readout equation.}
For a readout $r$ and a contextual control $Z$, the intended survival statistic for a coordinate $q \in B^{*}_{Q6}$ is
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z)&=J_{0}(Z)-J_{Q}(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence $y$, organismal context, and simpler codon-feature baselines.  A positive value of $S_{Q}(r\mid Z)$ would mean that the added residual coordinate improves the selected readout relative to the baseline under those controls.  The present finite row does not provide such controlled values.  It provides the coordinate computation and the nonzero residual count; the statistic comparing $J_{0}$ and $J_{Q}$ remains unfilled for tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance.

\paragraph{Relation to the M-only baseline.}
The row is designed to expose a limitation of an $M$-density-only description.  An $M$-only model may capture a local codon preference but cannot, by itself, test distinctions such as $CUN$ versus $UUR$ in leucine or $AAA$ versus $AAG$ in lysine after the amino-acid quotient.  The finite computation shows that residual directions of this kind are present in the three-organism codon-count contact.  However, the stronger statement that an $M$-only model is insufficient for a translation-layer readout requires an organism-matched statistic with codon usage, tRNA-copy information, and a translation-related readout under amino-acid-composition control.  That paired statistic is not supplied in this row.

\paragraph{Contact classes and blocked interpretation.}
The row names three kinds of relevant contact: per-organism codon usage, tRNA gene-copy spectra, and tAI or stAI weights.  The verified numerical part used here is the codon-count residual computation.  The tRNA and adaptation-weight contacts identify the kinds of measurements that could support a bounded translation-layer test, but they are not yet assembled here into a controlled survival comparison.  Consequently, the row should be read as evidence for nontrivial synonymous residual topology in finite codon-count data, not as evidence for a ribosome-mediated mechanism.

No translation realization, folding behavior, physical admissibility, biochemical function, molecular mechanism, or universal biological rule follows from the $7/9$ nonzero residual count alone.  Such conclusions would require a separate contact that directly supplies the corresponding layer and an analysis keeping amino-acid composition and simpler codon-feature baselines in the control set.  In particular, a nonzero residual coordinate is not a claim about protein abundance, structural order, fitness, or global directionality of codon choice.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite row establishes a bounded codon-topology fact: in three organism-scoped codon-count tables, the amino-acid-residual basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$ has $9$ computed coordinates and $7$ of them are nonzero above variance threshold $0.01$.  The claim is therefore ready as a finite residual-coordinate record.  It is not ready as a positive survival-score claim for tAI, ribosome occupancy, protein abundance, or any higher biological layer, because the required paired controlled readout has not been provided.
