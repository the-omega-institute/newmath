\subsection{Amino-Acid-Residual Codon Topology in Three Organism Tables}
\label{subsec:bior reality-q6-amino-acid-residual-codon-topology}

\paragraph{Question.}
The finite row studied here asks whether the codon-topological coordinates collected in $B^*_{Q6}$ become trivial after quotienting by amino-acid identity, or whether some organism-level codon-count variation remains in directions that cannot be read as amino-acid composition alone. The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table, projected onto the residual basis
$B^*_{Q6}$ after the amino-acid quotient. The nine named coordinates are
$K_{AAA}$, $Arg_{AGR}$, $Ile_{AUA}$, $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$, $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$, $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$, $Thr_{ACR/ACY}$, and the antisymmetric $f_3$ stress-pair coordinate. These coordinates are not amino-acid frequencies: each one is evaluated only after the amino-acid class has been held fixed, so a nonzero coordinate reports synonymous structure inside the codon table rather than a change in the protein alphabet.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The run $971b9c868480$ loaded one codon-count table for each of three organisms: human, budding yeast, and the K-12 strain of Escherichia coli. The NCBI genetic code was loaded in the same computation, so every codon count was assigned to its amino-acid class before residual coordinates were formed. This is a three-organism contact, not a population estimate. Its scope is exactly the finite set
$$
\begin{aligned}
O=\{&\mathrm{homo\_sapiens},\ \mathrm{saccharomyces\_cerevisiae},\ \\
&\mathrm{escherichia\_coli\_k12}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The calculation therefore tests whether the displayed codon tables contain detectable synonymous residual variation in the stated basis. It does not by itself test ribosome motion, protein output, folding, biochemical activity, or organismal fitness.

\paragraph{Residual basis and observed rank.}
The computed basis contained exactly nine coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^*_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3,\mathrm{stress}}^{\mathrm{anti}}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
A coordinate was counted as nontrivial when its across-organism residual variance exceeded the finite threshold $0.01$. Under that rule, seven of the nine coordinates were nonzero. Thus the observed residual fraction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
The result is a bounded topological statement about codon-count geometry after amino-acid quotienting: in this finite three-table contact, most of the displayed $Q6$ residual directions are not erased by controlling for amino-acid identity.

\paragraph{What the row supports.}
The row supports the claim that the amino-acid quotient does not collapse the full $B^*_{Q6}$ residual basis on the loaded organism tables. In particular, the computation found $9$ named residual elements and detected nonzero finite variance in $7$ of them at threshold $0.01$. This is stronger than merely saying that codon usage differs between organisms, because the tested directions are chosen inside synonymous classes or across specified synonymous partitions such as $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$, $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$, and $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$. The row therefore records residual codon topology after the amino-acid quotient, not just amino-acid composition.

\paragraph{Controlled survival statistic.}
The intended downstream statistic for a readout $r$ is a conditional survival comparison of the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z)=J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q),
\end{aligned}
$$
where $q\in B^*_{Q6}$ and $Z$ includes the amino-acid sequence variable $y$, organismal or sequence context $c$, and simpler codon-feature baselines. For an $M$-density baseline, the relevant comparison is $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$. The finite row reported here has not supplied the paired organism-matched readout table needed to estimate this statistic for $r$ in tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance. Consequently, the positive-survival claim remains a further test, not a conclusion of the codon-count calculation.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The row has status passed for the finite residual computation, but its scientific reach is deliberately narrow. Nonzero residual variance in codon-count tables does not imply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, or a universal rule. None of those conclusions follows unless a separate contact supplies the corresponding readout and the statistic is evaluated with amino-acid composition and simpler codon features controlled. In particular, a nonzero coordinate in $B^*_{Q6}$ is not evidence that ribosomes respond to that coordinate, that proteins become more abundant, that a structure folds, or that the sign of the coordinate has the same meaning across all organisms.

\paragraph{Finite verdict.}
Within the stated three-organism scope, the amino-acid-residual codon basis is empirically nontrivial: $7$ of $9$ residual coordinates survive the variance threshold $0.01$ after quotienting by amino-acid identity. The row therefore licenses a finite codon-table statement: the loaded human, yeast, and E. coli K-12 tables contain synonymous residual structure visible in $B^*_{Q6}$. It does not yet license the stronger readout statement that adding a $Q6$ residual coordinate improves a controlled translation-layer score over an $M$-only baseline.
