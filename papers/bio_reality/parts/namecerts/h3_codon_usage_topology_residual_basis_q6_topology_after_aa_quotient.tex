\subsection{Amino-Acid-Quotiented Codon Residual Topology}
\label{sec:bior reality-q6-amino-acid-quotiented-codon-residual-topology}

\paragraph{Residual carrier.}
The object considered here is not raw codon usage and not amino-acid composition.  The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table after quotienting by the amino-acid sequence and then projecting to a small residual coordinate system $B^*_{Q6}$.  The nine coordinates used in the finite computation are
$K_{AAA}$, $Arg_{AGR}$, $Ile_{AUA}$, $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$, $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$, $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$, $Thr_{ACR/ACY}$, and an antisymmetric $f_3$-stress-pair coordinate.  Thus the chapter tests whether the Q6 codon topology leaves any measurable codon-count residue after the amino-acid quotient has removed the first-order protein-sequence explanation.

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The computation loaded the NCBI genetic code and one codon-count table for each of three organisms: human, yeast, and \emph{E. coli} K-12.  In the recorded run $971b9c868480$ for experiment $residual\_basis\_q6\_topology\_validation$, the organism count is therefore $3$, and the contact scope is explicitly finite: human, yeast, and \emph{E. coli} K-12 are the whole observed panel, not a population sample.  The residual basis has $9$ named elements, all nine were computed, and the finite-contact flag records that this is not a population estimate.

\paragraph{Observed residual nontriviality.}
The amino-acid-quotiented residual test reports $9$ tested elements, with $7$ nonzero at the stated variance threshold $0.01$.  The corresponding finite-panel fraction is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9} &= 0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This number is the central positive fact of the chapter.  It says that, within the three-table panel and under the stated residual projection, the Q6-derived coordinates are not all collapsed by amino-acid composition.  The result is topological and compositional: it detects residual codon-count structure after the amino-acid quotient.  It does not by itself measure elongation speed, ribosome occupancy, protein output, folded structure, biochemical activity, or organismal fitness.

\paragraph{Conditional survival statistic.}
The intended translation-layer statistic is a controlled comparison.  For a readout $r$ such as a tAI-like weight, ribosome-occupancy estimate, or protein-abundance readout, and for a context variable $Z$ containing at least the amino-acid sequence $y$ and simpler codon-feature baselines, the relevant score has the form
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_Q(r\mid Z) &= J_0(Z)-J_Q(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
Only a residual coordinate $q\in B^*_{Q6}$ can enter this test, because the amino-acid quotient has already removed the amino-acid-composition component.  The finite codon-count result establishes that the residual coordinates are available and nontrivial in the observed panel.  It does not establish that any $S_Q(r\mid Z)$ is positive, because the paired controlled readout statistic has not yet been supplied.

\paragraph{M-density limitation.}
The local M-density baseline is weaker than the residual basis for this question.  A model that only records an M-style local codon optimum can miss contrasts such as $CUN$ versus $UUR$ within leucine and $AAA$ versus $AAG$ within lysine.  Those contrasts are exactly the kind of within-amino-acid residual that survives only after the amino-acid quotient is made explicit.  The present row therefore separates two statements: first, the finite codon-count panel contains nontrivial $B^*_{Q6}$ residual coordinates; second, whether those coordinates improve a controlled translation readout over an M-density baseline remains unmeasured in the current data record.

\paragraph{Blocked readout boundary.}
The available contacts are codon-usage tables, organism-level tRNA gene-copy spectra, and tAI or stAI-style codon-adaptation weights as possible readout classes.  They are not yet joined into an organism-matched statistic containing codon usage, tRNA copy numbers, and a controlled tAI or stAI readout with amino-acid composition included in $Z$.  Consequently, absence of a positive $S_Q(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$ estimate is not negative evidence; it is a missing paired statistic.  The blocked claim is the stronger statement that some residual coordinate has positive conditional survival, or that the residual basis defeats an M-only baseline on a controlled translation readout.

\paragraph{Local interpretation.}
The verified status of the finite computation is passed.  The scientific content is correspondingly narrow: in three named organisms, using one codon-count table per organism and the NCBI genetic code, $7$ of $9$ Q6 residual-basis coordinates remain nonzero after amino-acid quotienting at variance threshold $0.01$.  This supports the existence of a computable residual coordinate system for codon-topology analysis.  It does not license a translation claim, a ribosome-mediated mechanism, a folding claim, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical causation, universal directionality of residual signs, or a global biological law.  Any such promotion requires a separate matched contact that actually observes the corresponding layer and estimates the controlled statistic rather than merely reporting codon-count residual geometry.
