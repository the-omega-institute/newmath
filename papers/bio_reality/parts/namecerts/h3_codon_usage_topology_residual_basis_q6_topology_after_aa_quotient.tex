\subsection{Codon-Usage Topology After the Amino-Acid Quotient}
\label{sec:biorality-codon-usage-q6-residual-topology}

\paragraph{Object of the record.}
This record concerns the organism-level codon-count geometry that remains after amino-acid composition has been held fixed. The carrier is the per-organism coding-sequence codon-count table, projected to the amino-acid residual basis $B^{*}_{Q6}$. The finite basis used in the run has nine named coordinates:
$$
\begin{aligned}
B^{*}_{Q6}=\{&K_{AAA},\ Arg_{AGR},\ Ile_{AUA},\ Leu_{CUN/UUR},\ Leu_{UUA/UUG},\\
&Ser_{UCR/AGY},\ Ser_{UCA/UCG},\ Thr_{ACR/ACY},\ f_{3}\text{-stress}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Here the subscripts name synonymous codon contrasts or codon-family stresses rather than amino-acid abundance itself. The point of the quotient is that a signal which can be explained solely by the amino-acid sequence $y$ is removed before any residual coordinate is allowed to count as surviving structure. Thus the local question is not whether leucine, serine, arginine, lysine, isoleucine, or threonine are frequent, but whether their synonymous codon alternatives retain measurable organism-specific geometry after the amino-acid class has already been accounted for.

\paragraph{Finite contact used.}
The finite computation loaded codon-count contacts for three organisms: $\mathrm{Homo\ sapiens}$, $\mathrm{Saccharomyces\ cerevisiae}$, and $\mathrm{Escherichia\ coli\ K12}$. The run recorded $\mathrm{organism\_count}=3$ and described the contact scope as one codon-count table per organism. The NCBI genetic code was loaded for the mapping from codons to amino acids, so the quotient was evaluated against the standard amino-acid grouping rather than against an untyped list of codon labels. The run identifier was $971b9c868480$, and the finite-row status was reported as passed at $2026$-$05$-$31$ $17{:}00{:}36+00{:}00$.

\paragraph{Residual basis outcome.}
The computed residual basis contained $9$ elements. With variance threshold $0.01$, $7$ of the $9$ coordinates were nonzero across the three-organism contact, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{9}=0.7777777777777778.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a finite-contact statement about the selected organisms, not a population estimate over life, clades, environments, genes, or expression regimes. It says that the amino-acid quotient did not collapse the whole $Q6$ codon-topology list to zero in this small organism panel. In particular, the residual coordinate list was actually instantiated for $K_{AAA}$, $Arg_{AGR}$, $Ile_{AUA}$, $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$, $Leu_{UUA/UUG}$, $Ser_{UCR/AGY}$, $Ser_{UCA/UCG}$, $Thr_{ACR/ACY}$, and the antisymmetric $f_{3}$ stress pair. The finite result is therefore stronger than a naming convention: it confirms that the quotient pipeline can compute these nine coordinates and that most of them have nontrivial variation in the chosen organism panel.

\paragraph{Survival-score reading.}
The intended translation-layer statistic is conditional. For a readout $r$ such as tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance, and for a context package $Z$ containing at least the amino-acid sequence $y$ together with simpler codon-feature baselines, the residual contribution of a coordinate $q\in B^{*}_{Q6}$ is read as
$$
\begin{aligned}
S_{Q}(r\mid Z,q)=J_{0}(Z)-J_{Q}(Z,q).
\end{aligned}
$$
A positive value would mean that the residual coordinate improves the bounded readout relative to the baseline model under the stated controls. The present finite row does not supply such a paired statistic. It supplies codon-count residual geometry and records that tAI or stAI weights, tRNA gene-copy spectra, and codon-usage tables are the relevant contact classes for a later controlled test. Consequently, the chapter records a computed residual coordinate system and a blocked survival-score question, not a confirmed translation-efficiency law.

\paragraph{M-density limitation.}
The associated counterexample probe asks whether an $M$-density baseline is too coarse to test all of the listed synonymous residuals. The finite row is consistent with the need for coordinates beyond a local $M$ summary, because the named basis includes contrasts such as $Leu_{CUN/UUR}$ and $K_{AAA}$ that are invisible to a model which only tracks a reduced membership or density class. However, the current contact set has not yet paired organism-matched codon usage, tRNA copy number, and tAI or stAI readouts in a single controlled estimate of $S_{Q}(r\mid Z,\Delta_M)$. Therefore the insufficiency of the $M$-only baseline remains a testable pressure rather than an established biological conclusion. The correct null state is blocked by missing paired statistics, not by a measured negative result.

\paragraph{Local boundary of interpretation.}
The computation does not establish ribosome-mediated translation realization from codon counts alone. It does not establish folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, biochemical mechanism, protein abundance, or any universal rule about codon usage. It also does not assign a universal direction to any residual sign. A nonzero residual variance in $B^{*}_{Q6}$ is a coordinate-level organism-panel fact; promotion to translation, folding, physical behavior, function, mechanism, or universality requires an independent contact that measures that layer under the relevant controls.

\paragraph{Chapter verdict.}
For the three-organism finite contact, the amino-acid quotient leaves a nontrivial $Q6$ residual topology: $9$ residual coordinates were computed and $7$ exceeded the stated nonzero threshold. This is enough to retain $B^{*}_{Q6}$ as a concrete codon-usage coordinate system for subsequent controlled readout tests. It is not enough to claim that any coordinate has positive survival score for tAI, stAI, ribosome occupancy, or protein abundance. The present result is therefore a ready finite-coordinate record with a clearly delimited downstream empirical test.
