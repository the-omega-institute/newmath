\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and reality contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table read through the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The finite carrier is the $64$-vertex codon display with coordinates supplied by the window-six encoding, while the external contact is only the curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact is therefore a code-layer bridge: it licenses codon-label readback and local label-split tests, but it does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any law saying that biology must obey this geometry.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a finite cubical row, the same set is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  This supplies a concrete local shape: thirteen selected codon vertices, sixteen local edges, and four square faces in the induced tile.  The row is not treated as a mechanism.  It is a compact finite witness that the curated label table has a local boundary pattern when read in $Q_6$ coordinates.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null comparison.}
The null model preserves amino-acid classes and samples $N=2000$ finite rows with seed $42$.  Against this class-preserving null, the observed $M$-row has size $20$.  No sampled row matched the exact observed shape: the exact-match count was $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
|M_{\mathrm{obs}}|&=20,\\
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ shape\ matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail statistic counted $7$ samples with size at most the observed $M$-size, so the finite-row lower-tail value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{ |M_{\mathrm{sample}}|\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the amino-acid-class preserving control does not reproduce the observed local shape in the sampled rows, and it places the observed $M$-size in a small lower tail.  This is a finite null-model statement about code-label geometry only.

\paragraph{Independent shape and spectrum checks.}
A separate shape-significance run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$ required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic residual was $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, reported identically as the cubic-polynomial residual and the radial-polynomial value, so the numerical spectrum is internally coherent at the stated tolerance:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,\\
P_{\mathrm{radial}}(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)&=-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.

$$
These numbers strengthen the finite-row audit by showing that the local tile has both combinatorial and spectral signatures under the chosen coordinate readback.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback tests.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  A family-level stratification over $5$ families gave hit rates above baseline of $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.  The leave-one-codon-out row had recovery rate $1.0$, recovery rate above threshold $1.0$, predictable count $8$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, boundary count $5$, and anchor count $0$.  These figures say that the finite local tile is useful for code-label recovery checks, not that it generates translation or function.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The amino-acid-class preserving null model is rejected as an exact-shape explanation in the sampled finite regime, with exact-match probability $0.0$ over $2000$ samples and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  Together with the punctured-cube row, spectral values, leave-one readback rates, and complete module classification, the data support a chapter-specific statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a non-generic local boundary tile in the curated standard-code label table.  The statement stops there.  No translation realization, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this contact unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.
