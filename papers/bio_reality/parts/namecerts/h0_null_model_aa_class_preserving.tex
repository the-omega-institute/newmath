\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the local codon tile}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary row.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code label table as an external reality contact.  The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is observed as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the finite object under test is not an unconstrained biological mechanism; it is a local combinatorial tile inside $Q_6$ whose readback is mediated by curated codon labels.  The observed marked set $M$ has size $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment.  No full list of the $20$ elements of $M$ is supplied in the finite row, so the local statement records the size, the row $R$, the spectrum, and the sampled null statistics without asserting an unlisted $M$ membership table.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null experiment.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label class while resampling the local shape test.  In $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, the observed $M$-size threshold was $20$.  The exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  Equivalently, the empirical finite-sample scores were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact,class}} &= 0/2000 = 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail,class}} &= 7/2000 = 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The score $0.0$ is used here only as the finite Monte Carlo frequency $0/2000$ for this run, not as a theorem that the underlying probability is zero.  The lower-tail value $0.0035$ says that, under this class-preserving null, only seven sampled rows were at least as small as the observed $M$-size criterion.  This makes the local codon tile sharper than a random class-preserving relabeling in this finite test, while leaving the biological interpretation at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral controls.}
A separate shape-significance run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$ required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}} &= 1/2000 = 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 25/2000 = 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The spectral row also records a local numerical fingerprint: $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the spectral-radius calculation is internally consistent at the displayed tolerance.  These values support the statement that the observed local tile has reproducible finite geometry in $Q_6$; they do not convert the tile into a law of translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Readback robustness under leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out experiment reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Family-level leave-one-code-out rates were also concentrated above baseline: alternative yeast had rate $0.8333333333333334$, and bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had rate $1.0$.  These are code-read robustness statistics for the curated label table, not independent observations of ribosomal translation or protein behavior.

\paragraph{Module classification of the thirteen-row boundary.}
The module classification accounts for every codon in $R$ with no missing or extra row elements: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, the list of extra module codons is empty, the list missing from modules is empty, and the unassigned-codon list is empty.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This module table is the most concrete finite readback of the boundary: it decomposes the $13$-codon row into six named codon groups and confirms that each group has predictable or boundary status in the supplied finite data.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model supports a narrow claim: the standard-code codon table, when read as labels over the six-bit cube, contains a local boundary-tile pattern whose row $R$, punctured-cube $f$-vector, sampled p-values, spectrum, and leave-one robustness are finite and reproducible in the supplied runs.  The reality contact is the curated standard genetic-code table, whose observed fact is only that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  Therefore the supported layer is $code\_read$.  No translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from these numbers unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that evidence.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a mechanism of life.
