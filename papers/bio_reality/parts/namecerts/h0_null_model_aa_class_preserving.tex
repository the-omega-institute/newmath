\subsection{Amino-Acid-Class-Preserving Null Model for the Window-Six Codon Tile}\label{sec:bior-reality-aa-class-preserving-null-model}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The claim tested here is that the window-six codon display has a finite local tile whose shape is not explained by an amino-acid-class-preserving randomization of the standard-code labels.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read back through a curated standard-code label table.  The relevant distinctions are the coordinate display, the local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  This is a code-read claim only: the data can test codon-label readback and local codon-tile splitting, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary rows.}
The finite boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The observed codons in $R$ are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube readback gives the same list and has finite face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object under test is not an unrestricted codon table; it is a specific finite subconfiguration in $Q_6$ with $13$ vertices, $16$ edges, and $4$ two-faces.  The observed main tile size used by the null-model test is $|M|=20$.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null row.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model keeps the amino-acid class structure as the randomized background and asks whether the same local tile shape appears under that restricted shuffling.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ samples, the exact tile match count is $0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed tile size is $7$.  The recorded null statistics are
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
|M|_{\mathrm{obs}}&=20,\\
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ matches}\}&=0,\\
\#\{\mathrm{lower\ tail}\leq |M|_{\mathrm{obs}}\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
On this finite row, the standard-code local tile is therefore not reproduced by the amino-acid-class-preserving randomization in the exact-shape statistic, and its size lies in a lower-tail event of frequency $7/2000=0.0035$.

\paragraph{Shape significance against a broader shape sampler.}
A separate finite-row shape sampler, also with seed $42$, used $N=2000$ retained samples after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found one exact shape match and $25$ high-Jaccard matches at threshold $0.9$.  The corresponding values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the same code-layer reading from a second finite-row direction: the window-six tile is a rare local shape under the sampled nulls.  The row does not by itself say why the biological code has this table, and it does not promote a local cube pattern into a biological law.

These numbers are used only as finite spectral diagnostics of the observed codon-label tile.  They are not a physical admissibility certificate and are not evidence of folding, translation dynamics, or function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
h_{M\setminus T}&=0.9365079365079365,\\
h_{M\setminus T+\partial}&=0.9841269841269841,\\
h_{\mathrm{above\ baseline}}&=0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The leave-one-codon-out row has anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These checks say that the finite local tile has strong code-read recovery behavior under these omissions.  They do not say that an omitted codon is biologically recovered by translation, nor that the label sequence is a folded protein structure.

\paragraph{Family and module rows.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out row has family count $5$.  Its family hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.  The module classification row assigns all $13$ codons in $R$ with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The module partition is

Every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the finite row.  The module statement is therefore complete at the code-read layer: assigned count $13$ equals $|R|=13$, with empty missing and unassigned lists.

\paragraph{Boundary of the result.}
The reality contact is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It supplies codon label readback and local split checks.  It does not supply a translation experiment, a structural contact, a physical admissibility measurement, a biological function assay, a universal mechanism, or a proof that DNA obeys the window-six geometry as biological necessity.  Consequently the ready claim is narrow: the finite rows support a rare and recoverable local code-read tile in the standard-code table, including an amino-acid-class-preserving null result with $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  Any assertion about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality remains blocked unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
