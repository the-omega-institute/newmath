\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes while randomizing the finite code-layer arrangement under the stated sampling rule.  In $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, the observed tile size is $|M|=20$.  The exact observed shape occurs $0$ times under this amino-acid-class-preserving null, while $7$ sampled rows have size at most the observed value.  The reported tail values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both reported significance fields equal the corresponding values, namely $0.0$ for exact shape and $0.0035$ for the lower tail.  The finite-row interpretation is therefore narrow: conditional on this class-preserving null and this sample size, the observed local tile is not reproduced exactly in the sampled population, and the observed $M$-size lies in a small lower tail of the sample distribution.
