\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon table as an external label contact.  The BEDC object is not a free biological mechanism: it is a coordinate display whose distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological readback is only the curated standard-code label table, which assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact therefore tests code-layer label readback and local codon-tile splitting, but it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment.}
The row was tested against an amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$ sampled tables and seed $42$.  The observed finite object has reported size $|M|=20$.  Under this null model the exact shape appeared zero times:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
\operatorname{exact\_match\_count}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact\ shape}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail test counts samples whose $M$-size is at most the observed value.  It found $7$ such samples among $2000$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{lower\_tail\_count}_{\leq |M|}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Consequently, preserving amino-acid classes does not explain the observed local tile shape in this finite experiment: the exact-shape estimate is $0.0$, and the lower-tail estimate is $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral checks.}

These values support a finite coordinate-and-spectrum record for the local tile; they do not by themselves promote the tile to a biological law.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready claim is therefore narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a statistically rare local codon-tile boundary under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, with exact-shape count $0/2000$ and lower-tail count $7/2000$.  The curated standard-code table supplies the label readback contact, and the finite module decomposition accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$.  No stronger biological conclusion follows here.  In particular, no translation process, folded-protein structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity claim is established unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that evidence.
