\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null model for the local codon tile}
\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Object and scope.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The biological datum is not a mechanism for translation and not a structural observation of a protein.  It is the curated assignment of each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The question tested here is narrower: after the labels are read at the code layer, does the observed local tile remain exceptional under a null model that preserves amino-acid label classes?  The tested claim is therefore a finite code-read claim.  It does not assert folding, physical admissibility, function, a biochemical mechanism, or universality across organisms unless another observation supplies that higher-layer contact.

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary set.}
The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with codons represented as finite six-bit coordinate positions.  The distinguished boundary set $R$ has thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check gives $|R|=13$.  The same finite row records that $R$ is a punctured-cube-shaped set with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the boundary object being tested is not an informal visual impression: it is the thirteen-codon subset above, together with its recorded local incidence counts.  The row supplies the size of the associated observed module set as $|M|=20$; it does not supply an element-by-element list of $M$ in this packet, so no member list for $M$ is asserted here.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null test.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes and compares the observed tile against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  In this run, no sampled alternative reproduced the exact observed shape:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N &= 2000,\\
\operatorname{exact\_match\_count} &= 0,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail statistic also remains small.  With observed size $|M|=20$, the number of sampled alternatives with size at most the observed size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{lower\_tail\_count} &= 7,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central finite-row result for the present claim: even after preserving amino-acid classes, the observed local code tile is not typical among the sampled alternatives.  The result supports a code-layer boundary pattern, not a claim about why the biological code exists or how the translation apparatus realizes it.

\paragraph{Comparison with the unrestricted shape test.}
A separate shape-significance row gives $N=2000$ sampled alternatives, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that comparison, there is $1$ exact shape match, so $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  There are $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class preserving null test is therefore not the only source of small finite probabilities: both the unrestricted shape comparison and the class-preserving comparison mark the observed local tile as uncommon in the sampled finite universe.

Hence the module account is complete for this finite boundary set.  The statement is combinatorial and label-level: it says that the thirteen observed codons are exactly covered by the listed modules.

The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These checks support the robustness of the code-layer tile description under finite deletion probes.  They still do not observe folded structure, physical feasibility, function, or a general biological law.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite evidence supports the following local reading.  In the standard-code table, the six-coordinate codon display exposes a thirteen-codon boundary set with a complete module classification, a stable spectral summary, small sampled null probabilities, and strong leave-one recovery behavior.  Under the amino-acid-class preserving null model, the exact observed shape appears $0$ times in $2000$ samples and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$.  The scientifically usable conclusion is therefore confined to the code-read layer: the curated standard-code labels exhibit an exceptional local boundary tile in this coordinate representation.  No claim about translation realization, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this packet alone.
