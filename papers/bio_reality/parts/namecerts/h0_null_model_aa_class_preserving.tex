\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null model for the codon tile}\label{subsec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Coordinate object.}
The object under test is the standard-code codon table read through a six-bit codon coordinate cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is treated as a vertex-level label in the cube, while the amino-acid or stop assignment is supplied by a curated standard-code table.  The geometric calculation therefore concerns a finite labeled display, not a mechanism of translation.  The contact can check only code-layer label readback and local tile splits; it does not observe ribosomal realization, folded molecular structure, energetic admissibility, biological function, or a universal law of life.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the observed standard-code row it is exactly
$\{\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}$.
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite combinatorial object whose rarity is tested against amino-acid-class preserving randomizations.  The observed complementary tile has recorded size $|M|=20$ in the null-model run, but the supplied finite row does not list the individual members of $M$.

\paragraph{Null model.}
The null model preserves amino-acid class information while randomizing the codon placement enough to test whether the same local shape appears as an ordinary consequence of label multiplicities.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ null samples, the observed exact boundary shape appeared zero times.  The lower-tail event $|M'|\leq 20$ occurred $7$ times.  Thus the finite run reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=0,\\
\#\{|M'|\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape statistic is therefore absent in the sampled class-preserving background, and the lower-tail statistic is rare at the recorded resolution.  This supports the narrower claim that the standard-code labels make an unusually sharp local tile in the six-bit display.  It does not promote the tile into a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Comparison with an unconstrained shape screen.}
A separate shape-significance row with the same seed and $N=2000$ records one exact shape match after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It gives $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$ and $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$, with $25$ high-Jaccard matches.  The class-preserving null is therefore at least as restrictive for the exact-shape event in this finite run: it records no exact copy, while the broader shape screen records one.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The same finite packet carries a spectral summary.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic polynomial check.  This places the local tile inside a numerically stable finite spectral row, but the spectrum is still a code-display invariant.  It is not evidence for a protein fold, a physical energy ordering, or an organism-level function.

\paragraph{Recovery rows.}
Two leave-one-out probes test whether the local display has predictive value at the label layer.  In the leave-one-code-out row, $23$ tables were evaluated and $63$ total targets were tested.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  In the leave-one-codon-out row, the anchor count is $0$, the boundary count is $5$, the high-coverage closure count is $8$, the predictable count is $8$, and the recovery rate is $1.0$.

\paragraph{Family and module rows.}
The family-level code-out row records $5$ families.  The hit-rate-above-baseline values are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast.  The module-classification row assigns all $13$ boundary codons with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The modules are: $\texttt{AGR}=\{\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG}\}$, $\texttt{AR\_LYS}=\{\texttt{AAA}\}$, $\texttt{CUN}=\{\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU}\}$, $\texttt{ILE\_MET}=\{\texttt{AUA}\}$, $\texttt{SSN}=\{\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UUA}\}$, and $\texttt{STOP\_TRP}=\{\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UGA}\}$.  Each module has either predictable or boundary status in the supplied row, so the boundary packet is internally accounted for at the code-label level.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite evidence supports the claim that an amino-acid-class preserving background does not readily reproduce the observed local boundary tile: the exact-shape count is $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail rate is $7/2000=0.0035$.  Together with the $13$-codon punctured-cube row, the spectral values, and the leave-one-out recovery rows, this is a coherent code-read result for the standard genetic-code table.  The proper conclusion is deliberately narrow.  The data justify a local codon-coordinate and label-readback statement; they do not by themselves establish translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality across biological systems.  Any such stronger statement would require a separate contact that directly observes the relevant higher layer.
