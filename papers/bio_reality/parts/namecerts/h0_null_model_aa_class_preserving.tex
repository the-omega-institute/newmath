\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes while randomizing the tested shape.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled null rows, the exact observed shape was never reproduced:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\hbox{exact shape matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail statistic for rows no larger than the observed $M$ size counted $7$ null samples among $2000$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\hbox{null rows with size}\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the chapter-specific finite-row claim: even after preserving amino-acid classes, the observed local codon-tile boundary is rare under the stated null.  The result is a code-layer finite combinatorial signal, not a biological necessity theorem.
