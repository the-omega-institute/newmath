\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the codon window}
\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The record considered here uses the standard-code codon table only as a codon-to-label contact.  Each codon is read in a six-coordinate window, and the local object under test is the finite codon subset singled out by the window-six tile computation.  The distinguished boundary set is the thirteen-codon row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit reports $|R|=13$.  As a cubical subcomplex in the punctured window, the same row has face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed object is not a verbal family label or an inferred mechanism: it is a fixed finite subset of the $64$ codon positions, with an explicitly counted vertex, edge, and square-face profile.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The row also carries a numerical spectral signature.  The recorded values are

\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\

The radial cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is numerically zero at the stated tolerance.  This supplies a finite coordinate constraint on the local tile: the same thirteen-codon boundary has both the cubical profile $(13,16,4)$ and the spectral-radius readout $0.6752479308830213$ for the $M$ side of the construction.  The contact with biology enters only when codon positions are labelled by the curated standard code.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model keeps amino-acid class counts fixed while randomizing the table subject to that constraint.  In the recorded run, the number of samples is $N=2000$, with seed $42$.  The observed $M$ size is $20$.  Among the $2000$ amino-acid-class-preserving samples, the exact local shape match count is $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, so the lower-tail probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{tail}}&=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite outcome therefore says that, after preserving amino-acid class information, the observed local window shape is still rare in the tested ensemble.  This is a statement about the code-labelled finite row, not about ribosomal translation, protein folding, molecular energetics, cellular function, or a universal biological rule.

\paragraph{Shape comparison against unrestricted samples.}
A related shape-significance run used $N=2000$ accepted samples, but required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found exactly one exact shape match, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{shape}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
It also found $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These two statistics are weaker than a mechanistic explanation but stronger than a qualitative drawing: the boundary profile is rare under the sampled comparison procedures, and its rarity remains visible even when the comparison is relaxed from exact equality to high overlap.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level split used five families and recorded hit rates above baseline of $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, with $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast.  The leave-one-codon-out audit recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These values support a local readback claim: omitted codons and code variants tend to return to the same finite window neighborhood under the tested probes.

\paragraph{Module partition.}
The thirteen boundary codons are completely assigned by the module classification.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  In particular, the module partition is not merely compatible with the thirteen-codon row; it exhausts it.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The verified claim is confined to code-layer readback.  The curated table supplies codon labels, and the finite probes show that those labels interact with a local window-six tile in a statistically nontrivial way.  Nothing in this record observes translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such promotion would require a separate contact carrying that higher-layer observation.  In the present packet, amino-acid label sequences remain label sequences, and the local codon tile remains a finite code-level object.
