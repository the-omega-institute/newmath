\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}\label{sec:bior-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ read through a curated standard-code label table.  The contact used here is only a codon-to-label bridge: each of the $64$ codons is assigned an amino-acid or stop label by the standard code.  The row is therefore a code-read record, not a translation record.  Its internal BEDC distinctions are coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data on the codon display.  The biological object touched by the contact is the standard-code codon table, and the permitted readback is the standard-code label table.  The local tile under discussion is the finite set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality is $|R|=13$.  The same row identifies $R$ with a punctured-cube boundary pattern whose face-vector is $(13,16,4)$: thirteen vertices, sixteen local one-faces, and four local two-faces in the induced finite tile.  These numbers are the complete finite-row content of the local boundary claim; they do not assert a global law of genetic coding.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The targeted null model preserves amino-acid class information and asks whether the observed local shape is typical under that constraint.  In the finite run with seed $42$, the null sample size was $N=2000$.  The observed $M$-size was $20$.  Among the $2000$ amino-acid-class-preserving samples, the exact-shape count was $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus no sampled amino-acid-class-preserving table reproduced the exact observed shape, and only seven sampled tables had $M$-size at most the observed value.  This supports a finite-row rejection of typicality for this particular class-preserving null, at the stated sample size and seed.  The claim is deliberately local: the row says that the observed code-label geometry is unusual relative to this null ensemble; it does not say that the geometry causes translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape and spectrum checks.}
A separate shape-significance run also used seed $42$ and $N=2000$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that ensemble, there was $1$ exact-shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$ and $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The spectral row gives the finite numerical witnesses
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding radial cubic check is numerically closed to tolerance: the reported polynomial value is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  These spectral values are not interpreted as biochemical energies or physical rates.  They are internal finite-row invariants of the codon-window relation and its induced tile.

\paragraph{Leave-one contacts.}
Two leave-one tests probe whether the local tile remains informative under finite deletion tasks.  In the leave-one-code-out row, $23$ code tables and $63$ targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  In the leave-one-codon-out row, the anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the high-closure-coverage count was $8$, the predictable count was $8$, and the recovery rate was $1.0$.  These values justify treating the boundary tile as a strong code-read predictor in the tested finite setting.  They do not justify promoting the predictor to a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Family and module resolution.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out table has $5$ families.  Its family hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  The module-classification row assigns all thirteen codons of $R$ and leaves no residue: $R$-size is $13$, assigned count is $13$, extra module codons are empty, missing-from-modules is empty, and unassigned codons are empty.  The six reported modules are $\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.  Every module has a predictable-or-boundary interpretation in this finite row.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The admissible conclusion is that window-six codon coordinates expose a non-generic local tile in the standard-code label table, even under an amino-acid-class-preserving null.  The curated table supplies code labels only.  Therefore no translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this row.  Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate contact whose observed facts live at that higher layer.  In the present record, amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer statement and is not a statement about protein structure.
