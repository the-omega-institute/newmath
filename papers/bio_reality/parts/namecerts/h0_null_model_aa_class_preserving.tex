\paragraph{Finite object.}
This BioReality record concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code label table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The BEDC-side distinctions used here are the six-bit codon display, the local tile face, the singleton label corner, and the three-point label class.  The internal structure recorded for the finite row is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data.  The biological object is therefore not translation itself, but the standard-code label table as a finite external contact.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite object tested against null rearrangements.  The record does not assert that the codon cube is a law of biology; it asserts that this finite code-read display exposes a local boundary-tile pattern when read against the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Shape significance under the broad null.}
A separate shape-significance run sampled $N=2000$ null rows with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among these samples there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite Monte Carlo readouts are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed local shape is uncommon under that broad finite null, but the conclusion remains a code-layer conclusion: it is a statement about the label-table row geometry, not about realized protein structure.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The claim-specific null model preserves amino-acid label classes while testing whether the same local codon-tile shape is recovered.  In the recorded run, $N=2000$ class-preserving samples were generated with seed $42$.  The observed finite row has $|M|=20$.  The exact-match count was $0$, so the reported empirical exact-shape value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Consequently, even after preserving amino-acid class information, the observed finite code-read geometry is not reproduced by the sampled null ensemble.  The result supports the narrower statement that the curated standard-code labels and the six-bit window expose a non-generic local tile boundary.

The leave-one-codon-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases.  Its recovery rate was $1.0$, above the recorded threshold.  The module classification is complete on the $13$ codons of $R$: assigned count $13$, missing-from-modules empty, extra-module codons empty, and unassigned codons empty.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}

Every module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite classification.  This is the strongest local statement supplied by the row: the thirteen-codon boundary is not merely counted, but partitioned into named finite modules whose coverage is complete.

\paragraph{Reality contact and null boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments at codon-label resolution.  It can test code-read behavior and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or universality of the BEDC geometry.  Therefore the supported conclusion is deliberately local: the window-six codon coordinate system, together with curated standard-code labels, exposes a statistically non-generic local boundary tile under the amino-acid-class-preserving null.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.

For the claim $h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving$, the finite-row evidence is aligned with the boundary-mismatch probe.
