\paragraph{Carrier and reality contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a coordinate display for the standard-code codon table.  The coordinate system supplies a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class, but the amino-acid and stop labels themselves enter only through the curated standard-code table.  Thus the object tested here is not translation, folding, chemistry, or function.  It is the code-layer readback of a finite label table through the window-six coordinate geometry.

The externally supplied contact is a curated standard genetic-code bridge assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or folded protein structure.  A claim crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate contact.

This is a local combinatorial statement about the codon-coordinate display.  It does not say that biological translation is generated by the display, nor that the display is a global law for DNA or protein realization.

\paragraph{Shape significance against the unrestricted finite null.}
A finite randomization run with $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries under seed $42$.  In that unrestricted null, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$.  The corresponding values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the finite claim that the observed coordinate shape is uncommon under that sampling regime.  The conclusion remains a code-read conclusion: uncommon local tile shape is not evidence of folding, function, physical admissibility, or universality.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null result.}
The central null model for this row preserves amino-acid class.  With $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed module size was
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M|&=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact match count was $0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed module size was $7$.  Therefore the recorded finite-row probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact\ shape}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite experiment, the exact observed shape did not appear among the amino-acid-class-preserving null samples, and only $7$ of $2000$ samples landed in the lower tail at or below the observed module size.  The result is evidence for a nontrivial code-layer boundary pattern under the specified null model.  It is not evidence that amino-acid labels determine protein structure, nor that the codon table enforces a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The verdict is that the window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table.  The strongest finite-row support comes from the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, where the exact observed shape has probability $0.0$ in $2000$ samples and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$, together with the independently recorded punctured-cube row, spectral signature, leave-one-code-out stability, leave-one-codon-out recovery, and complete module assignment.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation, no folded protein structure, no physical admissibility, no biological function, no mechanism, and no universality follows from this chapter unless another reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
