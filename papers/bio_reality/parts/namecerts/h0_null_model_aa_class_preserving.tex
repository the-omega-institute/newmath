\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:bior reality-aa-class-preserving-null}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code table only as a codon-to-label contact.  The BEDC row under consideration is the local window-six tile whose boundary set $R$ is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit records $|R|=13$ and identifies the same list as the punctured-cube row.  Its recorded face data are
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not being treated as a vague biological region: it is a finite subcomplex with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-faces, and four two-faces in the displayed codon coordinate system.

\paragraph{Observed tile size and spectral row.}
The associated observed local tile $M$ has recorded size $|M|=20$ in the amino-acid-class preserving null experiment.  Its codon-by-codon member list is not part of this finite row, but its size and shape tests are.  The spectral row records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with cubic, equivalently radial, polynomial residual $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$.  Thus the numerical certificate used here is a finite coordinate-and-spectrum certificate, not a claim that the codon table is generated by spectral biology.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid class labels while randomizing the local shape test.  In $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$, the exact observed shape occurred zero times:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed tile size was seven rows, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{|M'|\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the chapter's central finite-row conclusion: after preserving the amino-acid class information, the observed local tile remains lower-tail exceptional at the $0.0035$ level, and the exact shape is absent from the $2000$ sampled class-preserving null rows.

\paragraph{Unconditioned shape comparison.}
A companion shape-significance row used $N=2000$ distinct samples with seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found one exact shape match and twenty-five matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=1/2000=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving null is therefore not the only source of signal: the unconditioned finite-row comparison also marks the observed shape as rare, both exactly and under the high-Jaccard relaxation.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of the claim.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact tests code-layer readback and local codon-tile label split.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey the displayed BEDC geometry as biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Therefore the local verdict is deliberately narrow: window-six coordinates expose a finite local tile whose boundary, spectrum, module coverage, and null-model rarity can be checked against the standard-code label table; no translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
