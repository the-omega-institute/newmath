\subsection{Amino-Acid-Class-Preserving Null Model for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:bior-reality-aa-class-preserving-null}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon-label table.  The table is used only as a curated code-layer contact: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it does not by itself supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Within this finite display, the observed boundary row is the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
so $|R|=13$.  The same thirteen codons form the punctured-cube row with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The distinguished local object $M$ has observed size $|M|=20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment.  The supplied finite-row data give the size of $M$ but not an explicit codon-by-codon list for $M$; consequently this record treats $|M|=20$ as the row statistic and keeps all list-level statements attached to $R$ and to the module partition below.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null experiment.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes while randomizing the finite row geometry.  In $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$, the exact observed shape occurred zero times:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact matches}\}=0,\qquad p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail statistic compares the sampled $M$-sizes with the observed value $20$.  Seven of the $2000$ samples had size at most the observed value, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{|M_{\mathrm{sample}}|\leq 20\}=7,\qquad p_{\mathrm{lower}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a finite empirical separation inside the class-preserving null, not a theorem that the row cannot occur.  The exact-match value $0.0$ means that no exact match appeared in the $2000$ sampled rows; it is not an absolute probability-zero assertion about every possible class-preserving table.

\paragraph{Independent shape pressure.}
A second finite shape test used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$ and recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, of which $55377$ were duplicate retries.  It found one exact shape match and twenty-five high-overlap matches at Jaccard threshold $0.9$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{shape}}&=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Together with the class-preserving null, this says that the local tile is not a generic artifact of preserving amino-acid classes.  It says only that the finite coordinate-and-label structure is unusually aligned with the observed standard-code readback under the sampled nulls.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The same finite carrier supplies a spectral check.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check and lies at numerical roundoff scale for the recorded computation.  This spectral row is therefore consistent with the finite tile calculation, but it remains a code-read statistic on $Q_6$ rather than a biochemical mechanism.

The code-level prediction test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was

$$
The hit rate above baseline was

\begin{aligned}

with hit rate $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M-T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ inside $M-T$ plus the boundary.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate.  These values support the local codon-label split as a stable code-read feature across the evaluated family rows.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The leave-one-codon-out test recorded no anchors, five boundary codons, eight high-coverage closure cases, and eight predictable cases:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\mathrm{anchor}=0,\qquad \#\mathrm{boundary}=5,\qquad \#\mathrm{predictable}=8.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recovery rate was $1.0$, above the stated threshold.  The module classification was complete: all $13$ elements of $R$ were assigned, with no missing codons, no unassigned codons, and no extra module codons.  The modules were
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the finite record, so the thirteen-codon boundary row is internally accounted for at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose resolution is codon-label readback.  It supports tests of the local codon tile and the amino-acid label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Therefore the conclusion is deliberately local: window-six coordinates expose a finite boundary-tile pattern whose observed row, null-model separation, spectral values, leave-one-code-out behavior, and module classification are aligned with the curated standard-code labels.  No claim is made that DNA obeys this geometry as a law of biology, that the tile realizes translation, or that an amino-acid label sequence determines a folded or functional protein.  Any passage from code readback to translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact supplying that higher-layer observation.
