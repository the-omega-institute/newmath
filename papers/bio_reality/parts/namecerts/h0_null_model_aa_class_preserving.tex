\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the local codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table read through a finite six-bit codon display. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with rows indexed by codons and with the observed labels supplied by a curated standard genetic-code table. The contact is deliberately narrow: it supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels at the code-read layer. It does not supply a translation mechanism, a folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universality theorem. The local tile therefore has the status of a finite boundary pattern in the code table, not the status of a global biological law.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube check gives the same set and the face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object being tested is not merely a count. It is a specific thirteen-codon boundary row with a four-face local tile signature inside the six-bit cube. The row contains the modules $\texttt{AGR}=\{\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG}\}$, $\texttt{ar\_lys}=\{\texttt{AAA}\}$, $\texttt{CUN}=\{\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU}\}$, $\texttt{ile\_met}=\{\texttt{AUA}\}$, $\texttt{SSN}=\{\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UUA}\}$, and $\texttt{stop\_trp}=\{\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UGA}\}$. The module audit assigns all $13$ codons: the assigned count is $13$, the reported $R$ size is $13$, and both the missing-from-modules and extra-module-codons lists are empty.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model keeps the amino-acid class information fixed while randomizing the local shape question. Against this stricter null, the observed local object has $M$-size $20$. In $N=2000$ amino-acid-class-preserving trials with seed $42$, the exact local shape was seen zero times:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
|M_{\mathrm{obs}}|&=20,\\
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail statistic is also small. Only $7$ of the $2000$ trials had null $M$-size at most the observed $M$-size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{|M_{\mathrm{null}}|\leq |M_{\mathrm{obs}}|\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite-row content of the claim $h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving$: the observed window-six tile is not reproduced by the amino-acid-class-preserving null sample, and the lower-tail event occurs in $0.35\%$ of the sampled null rows.

\paragraph{Comparison with the unconstrained shape sample.}
A separate shape-significance run used $N=2000$ distinct null samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. In that run, the exact shape appeared once and the high-Jaccard event appeared $25$ times:
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving result is therefore not a loose restatement of the unconstrained shape statistic. It is a more label-aware null comparison in which the exact match count is $0$ rather than $1$, while the lower-tail count remains small at $7$ out of $2000$.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row readout.}
The same finite object has a recorded spectral readout. The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is numerically negligible at $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius-in-tolerance value is $0.6752479308830213$. These values support the interpretation that the row is being read as a finite coordinate-and-spectrum object, not only as a codon list.

\paragraph{Predictive contact checks.}
Two leave-one-out checks record how much of the local row is recoverable from finite code-table context. In the leave-one-code-out test, $23$ tables and $63$ targets were evaluated. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. In the leave-one-codon-out test, the recovery rate was $1.0$, with $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, and $0$ anchors. Family-level leave-one-code-out rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family, across $5$ families. These checks support a code-read prediction claim for this finite row, but only at the code-label layer supplied by the curated table.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion is local and finite. The data support the statement that the standard-code label table contains a window-six boundary tile whose row $R$ has size $13$, whose local punctured-cube face vector is $(13,16,4)$, whose amino-acid-class-preserving null exact-match count is $0$ out of $2000$, and whose lower-tail probability is $0.0035$ for the observed $M$-size $20$. No stronger biological conclusion follows from these rows alone. In particular, the result does not claim that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity; it does not claim translation realization; it does not claim folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality. Any such claim would require a separate reality contact beyond the curated codon-label table.
