\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null row.}

In this row the observed $M$-size is $20$, the number of exact shape matches among $N=2000$ samples is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$. The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class, exact\ shape}} &= 0.0,\\

$$
The finite reading is that the observed local tile is not explained away by preserving amino-acid class alone in this sample. The conclusion is still a code-layer conclusion: it concerns the standard-code label table as a finite readback contact, not a biological law governing translation or function.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary of the claim.}
The supported claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and that this structure remains rare under the amino-acid-class-preserving null row reported above. The unsupported claims are equally part of the record. A local codon tile is not a global biological law. Window-six geometry alone is not translation, not folded protein structure, not physical admissibility, not function, not mechanism, and not universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only label readback unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies the missing biological evidence.
