\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite setting.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table observed only at the label-readback layer.  Codons are treated as vertices of the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, using the finite display supplied by the four nucleotide letters across three positions.  The question tested here is not whether the codon cube explains translation.  It is narrower: after the standard-code labels are read from the curated table, does the local tile selected by the window-six construction retain its shape against a null model that preserves amino-acid class information?

The distinguished boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality audit gives $|R|=13$.  The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the finite observation is not merely a list of labels; it also carries a local combinatorial boundary profile inside $Q_6$.

\paragraph{Observed module decomposition.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are partitioned into six named modules, and the module audit assigns every member of $R$ with no omissions and no extras.  The complete classification is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The summary row reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no unassigned codons, no missing module elements, and no additional module codons outside $R$.  This matters for the null comparison because the test is class-preserving: the randomized rows are not allowed to ignore the biological label partition altogether.  The null therefore asks whether the same sort of amino-acid-class information would typically reproduce the observed local shape.

\paragraph{Null-model result.}
For the amino-acid-class-preserving null ensemble, the experiment sampled $N=2000$ finite rows using seed $42$.  The observed selected tile has size $20$.  Among the $2000$ null rows, the exact shape match count was $0$, giving the finite empirical value
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact,aa}} = 0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count for rows with size at most the observed $M$ size was $7$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower,aa}} = \frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded significant values are therefore $p\_\mathrm{aa\_class\_exact\_shape}=0.0$ and $p\_\mathrm{aa\_class\_lower\_tail}=0.0035$.  In plain finite-row terms, preserving amino-acid class was not enough to make the observed window-six tile ordinary under this null model: no exact match appeared in the sampled class-preserving ensemble, and only seven sampled rows fell in the lower tail at or below the observed size.

\paragraph{Comparison with the broader shape test.}
The companion shape-significance row used the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$ but did not collapse the record to the amino-acid-class-preserving conclusion alone.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that run there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= \frac{1}{2000}=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= \frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving null is therefore not an isolated numerical curiosity.  It fits the same finite picture: the selected codon tile is rare as an exact shape and remains rare even when the comparison respects amino-acid class constraints.

This does not turn the codon table into a physical mechanism, but it gives a separate numerical readback that the same finite row has coherent coordinate, relation, and spectrum data.

\paragraph{Leave-one-out behavior.}
Two prediction-style audits test whether the local tile has recoverable boundary information when individual code or codon entries are withheld.  In the leave-one-code-out row, $23$ tables were evaluated with $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  In the family-resolved row, the five families have above-baseline hit rates
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the finite tile is not only rare under sampled null rows; it also carries a boundary signature that is stable under these withheld-entry probes at the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The supported conclusion is deliberately local.  The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-amino-acid or stop labels, and the finite calculations above test a boundary-tile pattern in that label table.  They do not observe translation as a biochemical process.  They do not observe protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any necessity law for DNA.  The amino-acid label string obtained by reading the table is still only a label string.  Any claim about translation realization, folded structure, admissible chemistry, organism-level function, or universality would require a separate contact whose observations are about that higher layer rather than about the codon-label table.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
Within its finite scope, the row supports the claim that the window-six codon coordinates expose a rare local boundary tile in the standard-code label table, even against an amino-acid-class-preserving null model.  The strongest single number for this claim is the null result $0/2000$ exact class-preserving matches, together with the lower-tail value $7/2000=0.0035$.  The thirteen-codon punctured-cube row, the complete six-module classification, the spectral residual near $10^{-12}$, and the leave-one-out hit rates give mutually consistent finite checks.  The result is therefore a code-readback statement about a local codon tile, not a promoted biological mechanism.
