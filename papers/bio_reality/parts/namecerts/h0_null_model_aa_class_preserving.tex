\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The BioReality row is a finite code-read record on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  Its carrier is not a molecular mechanism, but a coordinate display of the standard-code table in which codons are read as six binary distinctions and then compared against the external codon-label contact.  The available distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal BEDC structure recorded for this row is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation; the readback is the curated standard-code label table.  Thus the object under test is only the label geometry of the standard genetic code at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Observed punctured relation.}
The finite relation $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The recorded codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row verifies that this set is a punctured cube with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  The interpretation is finite and local: the row says that these thirteen codons form the recovered boundary relation inside the window-six coordinate presentation.  It does not say that the cube is a law of biology, that the cube generates translation, or that a codon in $R$ carries any protein-structural conclusion beyond its curated amino-acid or stop label.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The claim identified by $\mathrm{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving}$ tests whether the observed local shape is reproduced by a null model that preserves amino-acid class.  In $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$, the exact shape match count is $0$.  The observed size of $M$ is $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below this observed size is $7$.  Consequently the reported exact-shape value is $p=0.0$ at the finite sampling resolution, while the lower-tail value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}}&=0.0,\\
|M|_{\mathrm{observed}}&=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row conclusion is that the observed window-six local tile is not readily reproduced by this amino-acid-class-preserving null ensemble.  This supports a code-read distinction between the curated standard-code labels and class-preserving randomized alternatives.  It does not upgrade the local distinction into translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey the BEDC coordinate geometry as a biological necessity.

\paragraph{Shape significance row.}
A separate shape-significance row also used $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, of which $55377$ were duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Together with the amino-acid-class-preserving null, this gives two finite sampling contacts: one records exact-shape rarity under the general shape row, and one records no exact hits under the amino-acid-class-preserving row.  The large number of duplicate retries is part of the audit state and should be read as a sampling-design feature, not as independent biological evidence.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code-read claims and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the local verdict is deliberately narrow: the window-six coordinate may expose a finite boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and the amino-acid-class-preserving null row reports strong finite-sample rarity for that structure.  No claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies it.
