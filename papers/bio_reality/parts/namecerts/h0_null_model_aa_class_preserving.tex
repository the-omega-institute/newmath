\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the codon window-six tile}\label{sec:bior reality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display on the cube $Q_6$.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The finite construction tests whether the observed local tile can be recovered after the amino-acid class labels are preserved but the internal codon placement is randomized.  The carrier is therefore the $64$-codon window-six cube, the readout is the standard-code label table, and the relevant distinctions are the coordinate display, local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.

The boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the observed standard-code table it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.

$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
Thus the record is not merely a count of codons.  It is a finite incidence pattern in which thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four local faces form the observed codon-tile boundary.  The row also decomposes into the complete module list

\paragraph{Unconditioned shape comparison.}
In the unconditioned shape experiment, $N=2000$ sampled alternatives were evaluated with seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and produced $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ alternatives with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This row says that the observed finite tile is rare under the corresponding shape sample, but it does not identify a biological mechanism.  It only records that the finite coordinate-and-label construction is not frequently reproduced by the sampled alternatives.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The sharper null model keeps amino-acid class information fixed while randomizing the internal codon placement subject to that constraint.  In this class-preserving run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact match count is $0$.  The observed size is $|M|=20$, and only $7$ sampled alternatives fall in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the codon tile remains exceptional even after amino-acid class preservation is imposed.  The correct interpretation is finite and local: the standard-code label table has a window-six boundary pattern that is not explained by this particular class-preserving null sample.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The result is a code-read observation, not a general biological theorem.  The curated contact supplies codon-to-label assignments; it does not observe translation dynamics, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal generative mechanism.  Amino-acid label sequences obtained from the table therefore remain label sequences.  They are not structural models, not folding witnesses, and not functional assays.  Any promotion beyond code-layer readback would require an independent contact that directly measures the higher layer being claimed.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
For the finite row identified by \texttt{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving}, the observed standard-code boundary is the thirteen-codon punctured-cube row listed above, with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, module coverage complete, shape size $20$, spectral value $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, unconditioned exact-shape probability $0.0005$, high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$, amino-acid-class-preserving exact probability $0.0$, and amino-acid-class-preserving lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These finite numbers justify recording a local codon-window tile at the code-read layer.  They do not justify claims about translation realization, folding, physical possibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.
