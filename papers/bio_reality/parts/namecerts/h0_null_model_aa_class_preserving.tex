\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Claim under audit.}
This record concerns the finite codon-window claim \texttt{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving}.  The object is the standard-code codon table read at the code-label layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, where each codon is represented by a six-bit display, local faces provide tile tests, singleton corners carry individual codon labels, and three-point classes record amino-acid or stop-label groupings.  The contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  That contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for checking local split patterns, but it is not a contact with translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The finite boundary subset $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The recorded codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The punctured-cube calculation reports the same $13$ codons and the face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the tested object is not an arbitrary verbal cluster: it is a finite subset of the six-coordinate codon cube with an explicitly recorded vertex, edge, and face count.  The associated observed module size is $|M|=20$, which is the reference size used by the amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null experiment preserves amino-acid classes while resampling the relevant label geometry.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ finite trials, the observed exact tile shape did not appear among the null draws:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
\operatorname{exact\_match\_count}&=0,\\
p_{\operatorname{aa\ class\ exact\ shape}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail comparison against the observed module size was also small:
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M|_{\operatorname{obs}}&=20,\\
\#\{\operatorname{null\ draws\ with\ size}\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\operatorname{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The data therefore reject the idea that the recorded local tile is a typical outcome of the tested amino-acid-class-preserving null model.  The rejection is a finite code-table statement only: it says that the observed codon-label geometry is rare under this particular finite resampling scheme, not that the geometry is a law of translation or a cause of protein structure.

\paragraph{Independent finite checks.}
A separate shape-significance run with seed $42$ used $N=2000$ samples and required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, including $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\operatorname{exact\ shape}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\operatorname{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the same local conclusion from a shape-matching perspective: the observed tile is finite, sharply specified, and uncommon under the sampled comparison family.

This near-zero residual is a numerical consistency check for the finite spectral summary.  It should be read as a coordinate-level invariant of the encoded codon tile, not as evidence for an unobserved biological mechanism.

The hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, $1.0$ for the candidate-other group, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate tables.  These numbers make the code-label recovery behavior concrete, while still leaving all higher biological layers outside the scope of this contact.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The leave-one-codon-out run found no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases.  The recovery rate was

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded module assignment is complete on $R$: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and there are no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\

Every listed module has at least one predictable or boundary member, and the union of these modules is exactly the recorded $R$ list.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite data support a code-layer claim: within the six-coordinate codon display, the standard-code labels define a local boundary tile whose shape is rare under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model and coherent across the recorded cardinality, punctured-cube, spectral, leave-one-code-out, and leave-one-codon-out checks.  The strongest permissible conclusion is therefore a local codon-label statement about the standard-code table.  Nothing in this row licenses promotion to actual translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, causal mechanism, or a universal biological principle.  Any such promotion would require a separate observation channel whose object is the higher-layer phenomenon itself, not merely the codon-to-label table.
