\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Claim.}
This finite-row record tests the claim \texttt{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving} at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read back through a curated standard-code label table.  The relevant distinctions are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The biological object is therefore not translation as a physical process, and not folded protein structure, but the finite standard-code codon table as a label readback contact.  The claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure whose row pattern is hard to reproduce under an amino-acid-class-preserving null model, while the labels themselves remain external reality contacts supplied by the curated code table.

\paragraph{Finite row set.}
The observed boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified row list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object being tested is not an informal visual impression of the genetic code.  It is a concrete thirteen-codon subset of the sixty-four-codon cube, together with its induced local cell counts.  The associated module classification is complete: the six displayed modules account for all $13$ row codons, with no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are $\mathrm{agr}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{ar\_lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{cun}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{ile\_met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{ssn}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$.

\paragraph{Null model.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model used $N=2000$ finite samples with seed $42$.  The observed comparison size was $|M|=20$.  In these samples the exact-shape match count was $0$, giving the sampled exact-shape value
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,exact}} &= 0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, so the lower-tail probability was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,lower\ tail}} = \frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central finite-row result for the present claim: preserving amino-acid class labels does not make the observed window-six local tile ordinary in the sampled null population.  The evidence is finite and row-level.  It says that, among the sampled amino-acid-class-preserving randomizations, none reproduced the exact shape and only seven fell into the observed-or-smaller lower tail for the $M$-size statistic.

\paragraph{Shape and spectrum controls.}
A separate shape-significance run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$ produced $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
That run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite population being sampled is visibly constrained rather than a free continuum of shapes.  The spectral row also has a concrete numerical signature: $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The reported cubic, or radial, polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically at tolerance scale for this finite computation.  These values do not by themselves prove a biological mechanism.  They only stabilize the local coordinate readback: the same finite row that gives the punctured-cube count also carries a reproducible spectral signature.

\paragraph{Predictive finite-row checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row used $23$ evaluated tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate families.  The leave-one-codon-out check recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure codons, and $8$ predictable codons, with recovery rate $1.0$ and thresholded recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows support the same bounded conclusion as the null-model test: the tile is locally informative for code-layer readback and for finite leave-one perturbations, especially when boundary membership is allowed to participate in the recovery rule.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code-read behavior and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization.  Therefore the present record treats amino-acid labels as readback labels and not as folded proteins, catalytic roles, organismal fitness effects, or universal biological laws.  The curated table is a bridge for labels, not a mechanism for why those labels physically occur.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from this finite-row result unless a separate contact supplies it.  The local tile does not show that DNA obeys BEDC geometry as biological necessity.  It does not infer protein function from a codon-tile split.  It does not promote amino-acid label sequences to folded structures.  The strongest local conclusion is narrower: in the standard-code label table, the window-six codon coordinates expose a thirteen-codon punctured-cube boundary row with a reproducible spectral signature, strong sampled shape rarity, and strong leave-one recovery behavior; under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, the exact observed shape appeared $0$ times in $2000$ samples and the lower-tail event appeared $7$ times in $2000$ samples.
