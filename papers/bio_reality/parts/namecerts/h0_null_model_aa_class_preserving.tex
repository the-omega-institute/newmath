\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer. The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinates read as codon displays and with labels supplied by a curated standard genetic-code table. The contact therefore observes codon-to-label assignment, not translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The admissible question is narrower: whether the window-six coordinate presentation exposes a local tile boundary whose shape remains visible after amino-acid class information is preserved by the null model.

The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector

Thus the finite datum is not merely a count: it is a named codon subset, with a low-dimensional face profile inside the six-coordinate display. The row is local because it is read from the codon cube and the curated label table; it is not promoted to a global law of biological organization.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid class information and asks how often the observed local shape reappears under that constraint. With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled null instances, the exact observed shape occurred $0$ times. The observed $M$ size was $20$, and only $7$ null instances landed in the lower tail at or below that observed size. The reported finite-row statistics are
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
\#\{\hbox{exact matches}\}&=0,\\
\#\{\hbox{null rows with size}\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
In this record, the exact-shape statistic is therefore sharper than the finite Monte Carlo resolution, while the lower-tail statistic remains small but nonzero. The result supports a code-layer boundary claim: the observed tile is not typical among amino-acid-class preserving shuffled code-label arrangements sampled in this run.

\paragraph{Comparison with the unconstrained shape probe.}
The companion shape probe used the same seed $42$ and $N=2000$, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$. Equivalently,
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class preserving null is therefore not weaker evidence for the exact finite shape in this finite row: under the class-preserving constraint the exact count is $0/2000$, while the broader shape probe gives $1/2000$. Both probes keep the claim at the code-read layer, because both rely on the curated label contact and on finite coordinate readback.

\paragraph{Verdict and boundary.}
The finite evidence supports the following local conclusion. In the six-bit codon display, the curated standard-code label table exposes a $13$-codon boundary row with punctured-cube profile $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral signature, a small amino-acid-class preserving lower-tail probability $0.0035$, and exact-shape count $0/2000$ under the class-preserving null. The leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes show high finite recovery within this code-read setting. No stronger biological conclusion follows from this contact. In particular, this chapter does not claim translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, causal mechanism, or universality. Any such higher-layer claim requires a separate reality contact whose observations directly live at that layer.
