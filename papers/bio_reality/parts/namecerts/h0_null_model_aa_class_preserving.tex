\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:bior reality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite row.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table read only as a finite assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with the display coordinates supplied by the usual three-base codon alphabet after binary windowing.  The observed local object is a labelled tile with a singleton corner and a three-point label class.  The row is deliberately limited to the code-reading layer: it tests whether a local codon-coordinate pattern is unusually stable under an amino-acid-class preserving shuffle, while leaving biochemical realization outside the claim.

The distinguished boundary set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
It has cardinality $13$.  The same row identifies $R$ with a punctured-cube shape whose face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The complementary measured tile size in the amino-acid-class preserving null experiment is $|M|=20$.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinate check.}
The finite coordinate calculation also gives a numerical spectral profile.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated radial cubic evaluation is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the cubic residual.  At this precision the residual is a numerical zero check for the finite computation, not a new biological premise.  The spectral data therefore support the internal consistency of the coordinate row: $M$ and $R$ are not being compared only by cardinality, but also by a reproducible finite spectral readout.

\paragraph{Null experiment.}
The amino-acid-class preserving null model used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled tables.  It produced no exact shape matches:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact matches}\} &= 0,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail event, counted as null samples with size at most the observed $|M|=20$, occurred $7$ times in $2000$ trials, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed local shape is rare even after preserving amino-acid-class information.  This is a code-table statement: the null keeps label-class structure in the shuffle and still fails to reproduce the exact local tile in the sampled row.

A separate shape-significance row, also with seed $42$ and $N=2000$, found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  Its probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{shape}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
That sampling required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  These counts matter because the finite table space is small enough for repeated proposals to collide often; the reported probabilities are tied to the actual deduplicated sampling process rather than to an informal asymptotic heuristic.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback tests.}
The code-level readback has two leave-one checks.  In the leave-one-code-out table experiment, $23$ code tables supplied $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate after adjoining the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family, across $5$ families.

The leave-one-codon-out row records $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, and $0$ anchor cases.  Its closure-coverage count is $8$, and its recovery rate is $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$ with no missing or extra module codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The accounting row has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no unassigned codons, no missing module entries, and no extra module entries.  Every listed module has either a predictable or boundary status in the recorded row.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The reality contact is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments at codon-label resolution.  The contact can test code readback and the local label split of the codon tile.  It does not observe translation in a ribosome, folded protein structure, physical admissibility of a molecule, biological function, or a universal generating mechanism.  Consequently, the result here is not a claim that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity, and it is not a claim that a codon-tile split determines protein function.

The supported conclusion is narrower and finite: within the standard-code label table, the window-six coordinate row isolates a $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary and a $20$-site measured tile whose shape is rare under amino-acid-class preserving null sampling.  The same row is compatible with the spectral check and with the leave-one recovery data.  Any promotion beyond code-layer label readback requires another contact that directly measures the promoted layer.  In particular, no translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this row alone.
