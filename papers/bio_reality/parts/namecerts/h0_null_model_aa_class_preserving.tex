\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes while randomizing the relevant finite display.  In $N=2000$ sampled trials with seed $42$, the observed tile has $|M|=20$.  The exact-shape count is $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count for samples with size at most the observed $M$ size is $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both reported significance fields equal these values: $p\_\mathrm{value\_aa\_class\_exact\_shape\_significant}=0.0$ and $p\_\mathrm{value\_aa\_class\_lower\_tail\_significant}=0.0035$.  This is the central finite-row conclusion: after preserving the coarse amino-acid classes, the observed local geometry remains unusually small or structured relative to the sampled null ensemble.

This row is weaker than the amino-acid-class-preserving exact result only in the sense that it is a different null question.  Together they say that the local tile is not a generic accident of the finite codon display, while still refusing to promote the tile into a biological law.
