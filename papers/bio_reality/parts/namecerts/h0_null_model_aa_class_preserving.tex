\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null model for the codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Claim record.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display. The biological datum used here is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels. The mathematical object under inspection is the finite codon cube $Q_6$, with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes read against that table. The claim tested in this subsection is not that this coordinate geometry explains translation. It is narrower: after preserving the amino-acid class information in the null ensemble, the observed local codon-tile shape is still unusually constrained when compared with sampled class-preserving alternatives.

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary set.}
The observed boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run identifies this set as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a list of exceptional codons; it is a finite subshape in the codon coordinate cube with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces. The observed marked set used by the null-model comparison has size $20$.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null comparison.}
The amino-acid-class preserving null experiment sampled $N=2000$ alternatives with seed $42$. It found no exact reproduction of the observed shape:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\hbox{exact matches}\}=0,\qquad p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail statistic counted $7$ samples with marked-set size at most the observed value $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\hbox{samples with } |M'|\leq 20\}=7,\qquad p_{\mathrm{lower}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both reported significance fields agree with these finite counts: the exact-shape significance is $0.0$, and the lower-tail significance is $0.0035$. In ordinary statistical language, the experiment says that this local codon-tile arrangement is not typical among the sampled class-preserving alternatives. The statement remains finite and empirical: it depends on the specified sample count, the sampling rule, and the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Comparison with the unconstrained shape statistic.}
A separate shape-significance run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$ attempted $57377$ distinct samples and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. That run found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{shape}}=0.0005,\qquad p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving null is therefore not the only test in which the observed arrangement is scarce. The class-preserving test is sharper for this claim because it keeps amino-acid class information inside the comparison ensemble and still obtains zero exact matches among $2000$ samples.

These numbers do not add a biological mechanism; they record that the coordinate object has a stable finite spectral readout under the implemented calculation.

\paragraph{Leave-one evidence.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. Inside $M-T$ the hit rate was $0.9365079365079365$, and inside $M-T$ plus boundary it returned to $0.9841269841269841$. The leave-one-codon-out probe reported no anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$. These figures support a local recovery reading: the coordinate tile and its boundary account for many held-out codon-label positions in the finite tests.

\paragraph{Interpretive boundary.}
The contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table. It supplies codon-to-label assignments and permits a code-layer check of local tile splits. It does not observe ribosomal translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, organismal function, or a universal biological mechanism. Consequently, the finite results above may be read as evidence for a local codon-coordinate pattern in the standard-code label table, but they may not be promoted to a claim about translation, folded structure, physical realization, biological function, or universality unless a separate contact directly supplies that higher-layer information.

\paragraph{Verdict.}
For the claim $h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving$, the finite row is ready at the code-read layer. The strongest numerical point is the class-preserving null result: $0$ exact matches in $2000$ samples and lower-tail probability $0.0035$ for the observed marked-set size $20$. This is reinforced by the $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary, the spectral values, the leave-one recovery statistics, and the complete module accounting. The result is a bounded finite observation about codon-label geometry, not a higher-layer biological explanation.
