\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:bior-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Finite question.}
The finite object in this record is the standard-code codon table read through a six-coordinate codon display.  Each codon is treated as a point of a six-bit cube, and the experiment asks whether the observed local tile can be explained by a null model that preserves amino-acid label classes while otherwise forgetting the particular codon positions.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: the curated table supplies codon-to-label assignments, including amino-acid and stop labels, and nothing in this contact supplies translation dynamics, folding behavior, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  The only permitted readout is code-layer label readback together with finite local split checks.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its observed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite set is recorded as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local tile is not merely a count of codons; it has a specific low-dimensional adjacency pattern inside the codon cube.  The selected label-side set $M$ has observed size $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment.  The supplied finite row does not list the members of $M$ individually, so this chapter uses the recorded size and the associated null counts rather than inventing a label list.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
Under the amino-acid-class-preserving randomization, $N=2000$ samples were evaluated with seed $42$.  The exact observed shape appeared $0$ times, so the empirical exact-shape probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact,class}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$ out of $2000$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail,class}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central finite-row result for claim \texttt{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving}: when amino-acid class information is preserved, the observed tile remains rare in the sampled null ensemble.  The conclusion is a code-layer separation claim only.  It says that the local codon-label arrangement is not reproduced by this finite class-preserving null at the observed resolution; it does not say that the arrangement is a law of biology or that it determines any higher biological layer.

\paragraph{Comparison with the broader shape sample.}
A second shape-significance row used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$ and required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, including $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that run, the exact shape occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold produced $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Together with the class-preserving row, these numbers describe a finite empirical profile: exact equality is absent in the amino-acid-class-preserving run and very rare in the broader shape run, while near-equality remains uncommon but not impossible.  The table therefore supports a local pattern statement rather than a claim of necessity.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null does not reproduce the observed local tile in $2000$ samples, and its lower-tail probability is $0.0035$ for the observed $M$ size.  The broader shape run, the spectral coordinates, the leave-one rows, and the complete module accounting all point to a non-generic finite code-layer arrangement.  The conclusion is therefore ready as a finite codon-table claim: the standard-code labels expose a rare local boundary-tile pattern under the stated nulls.  The conclusion stops there.  No claim about translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows unless a separate empirical contact supplies that higher-layer information.
