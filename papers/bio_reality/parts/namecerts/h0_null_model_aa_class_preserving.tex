\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null test for the local codon tile}\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.} This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite labelled object on the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$. The cube coordinates are used only as a display and adjacency system for codons. The labels are supplied by the curated standard-code table, which assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop. The tested claim is therefore not that the cube generates biology. It is that, after the code labels are read into this finite coordinate display, a local boundary-tile pattern has a shape that is not reproduced by an amino-acid-class preserving null draw at the observed scale.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.} The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$. In codon notation it is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube tile with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite datum is not merely a count: it contains a thirteen-codon support, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces inside the window-six coordinate geometry. The corresponding module partition assigns all thirteen codons, with no omissions and no extras. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
&ar\_lys=\{AAA\},\quad agr=\{AGA,AGG\},\quad ile\_met=\{AUA\},\\
&cun=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\quad ssn=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
&stop\_trp=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, $missing\_from\_modules=\varnothing$, $extra\_module\_codons=\varnothing$, and $unassigned\_codons=\varnothing$.

\paragraph{Null model and rejection statistic.} The null draw preserves amino-acid class information rather than treating all relabellings as interchangeable. With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled tables, the observed local object has $observed\_M\_size=20$. No sampled amino-acid-class preserving table produced an exact shape match:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
exact\_match\_count&=0,\\
p\_{exact}&=0/2000=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
lower\_tail\_count\_{\leq observed}&=7,\\
p\_{lower\ tail}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape statistic and the lower-tail statistic are therefore both small at the finite resolution of this run. The first says that the complete observed local shape was not reproduced among the two thousand class-preserving trials. The second says that only seven trials had an $M$-size no larger than the observed value $20$.

\paragraph{Related shape and spectral checks.} An independent shape-significance run with the same seed used $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables. It required $57377$ sample attempts and rejected $55377$ duplicate retries. In that run, exactly one sampled table matched the observed shape, and twenty-five reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
exact\_shape\_matches&=1, & p\_{exact\ shape}&=0.0005,\\
jaccard\_{\geq 0.9}\_matches&=25, & p\_{jaccard\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The spectral record gives a second numerical description of the same finite geometry. The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the displayed spectral quantities satisfy the recorded cubic relation to numerical tolerance. This does not add a biological mechanism; it records that the coordinate-side spectrum is internally coherent for the finite tile being tested.

\paragraph{Predictive readback checks.} The leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ nonstandard-code tables and $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$. The reported rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit rates above baseline were
$$
\begin{aligned}
&bacterial\_plastid=1.0,\quad mitochondrial=1.0,\quad nuclear\_ciliate=1.0,\\
&candidate\_other=1.0,\quad alternative\_yeast=0.8333333333333334.
\end{aligned}
$$
A leave-one-codon-out audit found $anchor\_count=0$, $boundary\_count=5$, $predictable\_count=8$, $closure\_coverage\_high=8$, and $recovery\_rate=1.0$. These checks say that the finite local row has useful code-layer recovery behavior. They do not say that the row is a universal biological principle.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.} The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table. It supplies codon-to-label assignments at code-read resolution. The coordinate construction may expose a local boundary tile, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class inside $Q_6$, but the label table is still an empirical contact, not a theorem of the coordinate system. The result is therefore a code-layer observation: the observed standard-code local tile is rare under the amino-acid-class preserving null draws reported above.

\paragraph{Local boundary of the claim.} Nothing in this record establishes translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality across life. Amino-acid label strings are not folded structures. A CUN, UUR, AGR, or stop--tryptophan module notation is not a structure measurement. Crossing from code labels to translation, folding, physics, or function requires a separate observational contact of the corresponding kind. Without such a contact, this chapter supports only the finite code-read statement: in the curated standard-code table, the thirteen-codon boundary row and its associated twenty-point local object are statistically atypical under the sampled amino-acid-class preserving null model.
