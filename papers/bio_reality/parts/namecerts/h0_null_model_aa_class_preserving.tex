\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This BioReality record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact, not as a mechanistic model of translation.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the usual codon display and then contacted with a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The relevant distinctions are the six-bit coordinate, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The contact therefore tests only codon-label readback and local split structure.  It does not test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey this geometry as biological necessity.

\paragraph{Shape significance before amino-acid-class preservation.}
Against the first sampled null row, $N=2000$ distinct samples were retained after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, using seed $42$.  The exact shape was matched once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  A high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ was matched $25$ times, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  These two numbers say that the observed local shape is rare under that finite sampling scheme.  They do not say that the codon table was generated by the geometry.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null row.}
The claim-specific null model preserves amino-acid label classes and re-tests the same local pattern.  With $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the observed row has $|M|=20$.  The exact match count is $0$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= \frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both recorded significance fields equal the corresponding displayed values, namely $0.0$ for exact shape and $0.0035$ for the lower tail.  This is the main finite-row content of the record: even after preserving amino-acid classes, the observed local shape is not recovered by the sampled null ensemble.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite rows support a narrow conclusion: in the window-six codon coordinate, the standard-code label table exposes a rare local tile and a robust finite recovery pattern under the sampled probes, including the amino-acid-class-preserving null test.  The conclusion remains at the code-read layer. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not a folded-structure observation, and the local codon tile is not promoted to a global biological law.
