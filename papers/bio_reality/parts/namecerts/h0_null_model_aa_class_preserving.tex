\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Question.}
The finite object in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The display does not by itself assert a biochemical mechanism.  It supplies a finite coordinate arena in which one can ask whether the observed local tile, its boundary codons, and its label classes are common under a null model that preserves amino-acid class sizes.  The contact used here is the curated standard-code codon-to-label table.  Thus the observed layer is code readback: codons are read as amino-acid or stop labels, and no claim about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality is licensed by this contact alone.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same computation identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube boundary pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite geometric datum tested by the null model.  The row does not say that these codons are biologically forced by the six-coordinate display.  It says that, after the curated code labels are attached, this particular local set is the observed code-layer boundary pattern.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null experiment keeps the amino-acid class counts fixed and resamples label assignments within that finite constraint.  For $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$, the observed local tile has $|M|=20$.  The exact observed shape appeared $0$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count for samples with size at most the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\leq |M|}=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both reported significance fields equal the corresponding empirical values, namely $0.0$ for exact shape and $0.0035$ for the lower tail.  In this finite-row sense, the observed boundary-tile arrangement is uncommon even after preserving amino-acid class sizes.  The result is still a code-table result: the null model tests finite label rearrangements, not molecular dynamics.

\paragraph{Coordinate-only shape comparison.}
A separate coordinate comparison used $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape appeared once, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{shape}}=1/2000=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the finite claim that the observed standard-code tile is rare among the sampled coordinate alternatives.  They do not convert rarity into necessity, optimality, or a general law of biology.

This gives a numerical consistency check for the finite tile computation.  It is not a physical energy, folding score, translation-rate parameter, or organism-level fitness value.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$; and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid codes, $1.0$ for the candidate-other family, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate codes.  These row counts indicate that the local code-layer tile remains predictive across the evaluated code-table variants, subject to the same limitation: the prediction target is codon-label readback, not translation or function.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out behavior.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closures, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The threshold comparison also records $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$, with $13$ assigned codons, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are

\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\

Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite classification.  This is the strongest local conclusion available from the supplied rows: the boundary set is completely organized into named code-layer modules, and the leave-one-codon test recovers the relevant local pattern in the reported finite trial.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The empirical contact is a curated standard genetic-code table.  It provides codon-to-label assignments and allows finite tests of local tile structure, boundary membership, module classification, spectral consistency, and null-model rarity.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, amino-acid chemistry, three-dimensional folding, physical admissibility, phenotype, organismal function, or universality across life.  Any statement crossing from code labels to those higher layers would require a separate contact with measurements at that layer.  In the present record, the correct conclusion is narrower: under an amino-acid-class-preserving null model, the observed standard-code window-six tile is rare and locally coherent, while all mechanistic and structural promotions remain outside the evidence supplied here.
