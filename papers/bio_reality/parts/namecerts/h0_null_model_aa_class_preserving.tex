\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the local codon tile}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The finite object in this record is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-bit codon display whose vertices are standard-code codons.  The reality contact is a curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it is used only as a codon-label bridge.  It is not used as a translation mechanism, a structural measurement, a physical-admissibility test, a function assay, or a universality theorem.  Within this finite carrier, the distinguished boundary row is the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row count is $|R|=13$.  The same thirteen codons form a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the observed local tile has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the finite codon-coordinate complex.

\paragraph{Observed tile size and amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The observed local module $M$ has size $|M|=20$.  Against an amino-acid-class-preserving null model with $N=2000$ sampled tables and seed $42$, the exact observed shape occurred $0$ times.  Thus the finite-row exact-shape estimate is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\widehat p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0/2000=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail statistic counted $7$ null samples with module size at most the observed size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\widehat p_{\mathrm{lower}}=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the chapter-specific evidence for the claim that preserving amino-acid classes alone does not usually reproduce the observed window-six local tile.  The statement is deliberately finite and row-level: it says that the sampled null ensemble did not recover the exact observed codon-coordinate shape and only rarely landed at or below the observed module size.  It does not say that the tile is a global biological law.

\paragraph{Unconditioned shape comparison.}
A separate shape-significance row used $N=2000$ distinct sampled comparisons under seed $42$.  That run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, so
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
It also found $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support the same local reading from a different finite probe: the codon-coordinate tile is not a common random shape under the sampled comparison process, even when the conclusion is restricted to code-layer label readback.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion is confined to code readback.  Window-six geometry exposes a local codon-tile boundary, and the curated standard-code table supplies the amino-acid and stop labels needed to read that boundary.  Nothing in this finite row proves that DNA obeys the geometry as a biological necessity.  Nothing here proves translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate contact of the appropriate kind, such as an experimental translation contact, a structure contact, a physical-admissibility contact, or a function contact.  The present record is therefore a ready code-layer result and a blocked higher-layer result: the local tile is meaningful as a finite codon-label pattern, and it remains silent about layers not measured by the supplied contact.
