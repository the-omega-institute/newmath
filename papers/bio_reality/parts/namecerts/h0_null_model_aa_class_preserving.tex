\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Question.}
The finite object under test is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display.  The display is used only as a coordinate device: each codon occupies one vertex of a $Q_6$ cube, and the observed labels are the curated amino-acid or stop labels of the standard genetic code.  The claim tested here is not that codon geometry determines biology.  The claim is narrower: after preserving amino-acid label classes in the null model, the observed local tile remains unusually shaped relative to finite random relabellings with the same class inventory.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has size $13$.  The observed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row also identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube-shaped subset with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local observation is a finite codon-label pattern, not a continuum or asymptotic object.  The singleton-corner and three-point label-class features are read as features of this finite table, with the curated standard-code labels supplying the biological contact.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated two-part spectral measurement records
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic or radial consistency check is numerically close to zero:
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\mu)=-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the internal coherence of the finite coordinate calculation.  It does not turn the coordinate calculation into a claim about translation chemistry, protein folding, physical realizability, biological function, or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Unrestricted shape comparison.}
A first finite comparison used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that comparison there was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} = 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers say that the observed local shape is rare under this finite sampling regime.  They do not identify a causal source for the shape.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The stricter null model preserves amino-acid label classes.  In the recorded run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$-side size is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M|=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
No exact match occurred among the $2000$ class-preserving samples:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ matches}\}=0,
\qquad
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central row for the present claim.  It says that, even after preserving amino-acid class information, the observed local tile remains in a small lower tail of the finite comparison ensemble.

These rows support robustness at the code-label level.  They still remain code-label tests: they do not observe ribosomal realization, protein structure, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, or lawlike universality.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The ready verdict is a code-read verdict only.  The finite evidence shows that the standard-code label table, when placed in the six-bit codon cube, contains a rare local boundary-tile pattern under both an unrestricted shape comparison and an amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison.  The exact class-preserving match count is $0$ out of $2000$, and the class-preserving lower-tail probability is $0.0035$.  The supporting rows further show a $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary, a stable spectral calculation, high leave-one-code-out recovery, perfect recorded leave-one-codon-out recovery, and complete module assignment.

\paragraph{Local boundary on biological claims.}
The empirical contact here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments and permits finite code-layer readback.  It does not supply an observation of translation implementation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any assertion at one of those higher layers would require a separate contact designed for that layer.  Within the present data, the admissible conclusion is that the code-label geometry has a statistically rare local tile under the stated finite null models.
