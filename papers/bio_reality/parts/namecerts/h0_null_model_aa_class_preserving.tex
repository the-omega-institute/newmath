\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}\label{sec:bior-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the codon-label layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon displayed as a six-bit coordinate word and then read against the curated standard-code label table.  The finite distinctions used here are local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The contact supplies codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop labels; it does not supply a ribosome, a folded molecule, a physical admissibility criterion, a functional assay, or a universal mechanism.

The distinguished boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit reports $|R|=13$ and identifies the same thirteen rows in the punctured-cube check.  The associated face data have $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the record is not merely a count: it also fixes a local cubical shape with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-dimensional faces inside the codon-coordinate display.

\paragraph{Null model.}
The null comparison preserves amino-acid classes while randomizing the label arrangement at the tested scale.  In the run with seed $42$ and $N=2000$ samples, the observed tile size was $|M|=20$.  The null produced no exact shape match:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
\mathrm{exact\_match\_count}&=0,\\
\widehat p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
For the lower-tail comparison against the observed $M$ size, $7$ null samples landed at or below the observed size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{lower\_tail\_count}&=7,\\
\widehat p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the amino-acid-class-preserving null does not reproduce the observed local shape at this sampling resolution.  The exact-shape statistic is reported as $0.0$ on the $2000$-sample empirical scale, while the lower-tail statistic gives the nonzero finite estimate $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Companion shape and spectral checks.}
A separate shape-significance run with the same sample count and seed found $1$ exact shape match among $2000$ samples, with $\widehat p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  It also found $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $\widehat p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  That run required $57{,}377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55{,}377$ duplicate retries, so the finite sampling report also records the search pressure induced by the restricted randomization space.

The spectral row supplies numerical invariants for the same local arrangement.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial residual.  This is numerically at zero at the stated tolerance scale, so the spectral row is consistent with the finite tile calculation without turning the tile into a biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback tests.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  These numbers say that the local codon-coordinate structure has strong code-layer recovery behavior under this finite deletion protocol.

The leave-one-codon-out test gives a sharper row-level check.  It reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary rows, $8$ high-coverage closure rows, $8$ predictable rows, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The threshold comparison is also $1.0$.  The module audit assigns all thirteen $R$ rows and leaves no missing or extra rows: $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, extra module codons $\varnothing$, missing rows $\varnothing$, and unassigned codons $\varnothing$.  The six named modules are

Every module has either predictable or boundary status under the reported classification.

\paragraph{Family-level stability.}
The by-family code-out audit separates $23$ evaluated code tables into $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial or plastid tables, $1.0$ for the candidate-other class, $1.0$ for mitochondrial tables, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate tables.  The nonunit alternative-yeast value is important: it prevents the record from being phrased as a perfect invariant across every family, while still preserving the finite claim that all five family summaries lie well above the baseline rate.

\paragraph{Scope of the conclusion.}
The supported conclusion is narrow.  In the standard-code table, the six-coordinate codon display exposes a local boundary tile whose thirteen-row boundary, twenty-row observed tile size, spectral values, null-model rarity, and leave-one recovery tests are all finite and checkable.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null does not explain the observed shape in the reported samples: exact matches are absent in the $N=2000$ null run, and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$.

This does not license promotion beyond the code-label layer.  No translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this contact.  Amino-acid labels are not protein structures, CUN/UUR wording is not structural evidence, and a local codon tile is not a global biological principle.  Any claim about translation, folding, physical chemistry, organism-level function, or general law would require an additional independent contact that observes that higher layer directly.
