\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null test for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Object and contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with codons displayed as finite coordinate rows and then compared against curated amino-acid or stop labels.  The contact used here is a curated standard-code table: it supplies codon-to-label assignments, but it does not supply a translation event, a folded molecular structure, a physical admissibility measurement, a functional assay, or a universal mechanism.  The evidential question is therefore deliberately narrow: after the window-six coordinate construction has selected a local tile and boundary region, does an amino-acid-class-preserving randomization reproduce the same shape, or is the observed shape atypical even when label classes are held fixed?

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The finite row records the following codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is registered as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13,\\
f(R) &= (13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object is not merely a count of unusual codons; it has a local cubical incidence pattern.  The row remains a code-layer pattern, however.  The fact that $R$ is describable by local cube geometry does not turn the codon table into a law of biology.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The spectrum row gives a second internal diagnostic for the same window-six construction.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic check is numerically closed to tolerance: the listed polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is reported for the cubic polynomial check.  The spectral-radius entry is $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers make the row reproducible as finite computation rather than as a visual impression of the codon table.

\paragraph{Shape significance row.}
A separate finite sampling row tested the observed shape against $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the accepted samples, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the claim that the observed local tile is not a common random shape under that sampling regime.  It still does not promote the row beyond code readback; it only says that the finite geometry seen in the curated label table is statistically sharp for the tested null.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null row.}
The claim-specific null model preserves amino-acid class information while randomizing within the permitted finite regime.  In $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$, the exact match count was $0$.  The observed $M$-size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below that observed size was $7$.  The finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact\ shape}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape row is therefore stricter than the general shape row: after preserving amino-acid class, the tested randomizations did not reproduce the observed exact pattern in the $2000$ sampled cases.  The lower-tail probability $0.0035$ also marks the observed $M$-size as unusually small or boundary-concentrated under this finite null.  This is the central evidence for the record: the codon-window tile is not explained away merely by preserving the amino-acid class partition.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out row.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The reported hit rate above baseline was also $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the finite row reports $5$ families: alternative yeast has rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate each have rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the local tile has predictive reach across the curated code-read comparison set.  They do not say that the tile is a translation mechanism or that it determines organismal biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out row.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, and high closure coverage equal to $8$.  The recovery rate is $1.0$, and the threshold row is also $1.0$.  The module classification is complete: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, there are no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The module partition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either a predictable member or a boundary role in the finite classification.  This gives a compact explanation of how the $13$-codon boundary row is organized, while remaining tied to codon labels rather than to protein structure.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and non-promotion rule.}
The finite rows support the following local conclusion.  In the standard-code table, the window-six codon coordinates expose a $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary with face vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable spectral signature, significant shape statistics, and a stronger amino-acid-class-preserving null result with exact-match probability $0.0$ and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  The leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out rows further show that the same finite pattern has code-read predictive structure across the tested tables and modules.  The result is therefore ready as a code-layer BioReality record.  It is not a claim about translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or biological universality.  Any such stronger claim would require a separate contact that directly observes the higher layer rather than inferring it from codon-label geometry.
