\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The object studied here is the standard-code codon table read through a finite window-six coordinate display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, whose sixty-four vertices are the three-letter codons written over the alphabet $\{\mathrm{A},\mathrm{C},\mathrm{G},\mathrm{U}\}$.  The coordinate construction does not assert a biological mechanism.  It supplies a six-bit display, a local tile face, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes, and then asks which finite rows are exposed when the curated standard genetic-code table is used as the only external label contact.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.

$$
The finite audit records $|R|=13$.  The same row is identified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}

$$
Thus the row is not being treated as a loose list of exceptional codons; it is a finite subobject with a recorded local incidence profile.  The biological readback attached to the row remains only the standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the unconstrained finite null sample with seed $42$, $N=2000$ distinct trials were evaluated after $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape occurred once, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap event with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These are finite-row significance statements about the coordinate-and-label construction.  They do not say that the codon table had to arise from this geometry, only that the curated standard-code labels expose a rare local tile shape under this readback.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The present row is the stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null check.  The observed $M$-row size is $20$.  In $N=2000$ class-preserving trials with seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, hence the empirical exact-shape value is recorded as
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, yielding
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central null-model conclusion: even when the randomization preserves amino-acid class information, the observed finite row remains unusually small and no exact shape match appears in the sampled null ensemble.  The result is a code-read statement about the standard-code label table under the specified finite coordinate constraints.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of code origin, or universality.  Therefore no translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological law follows from the window-six tile unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that evidence.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observable, not a protein-structure observable.
