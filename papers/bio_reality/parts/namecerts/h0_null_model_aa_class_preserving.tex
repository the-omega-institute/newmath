\subsection{Amino-Acid-Class-Preserving Null Model for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:bior reality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier and observed boundary row.}
The object studied here is the standard-code codon table read through a finite six-bit codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, equipped only with coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data visible at the code-read layer.  The distinguished boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row count is $|R|=13$.  In the punctured-cube readback the same thirteen codons are recovered, and the finite face count is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not a floating numerical score: it is a concrete finite subcomplex of the codon cube with thirteen vertices, sixteen local edges, and four local tile faces.  The observed target set $M$ has size $20$ in the amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment.  No separate list of the twenty $M$ entries is asserted here; the finite row evidence used in this section is the reported size, the $R$ list, the punctured-cube face vector, and the null-model counts.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes while testing whether the exact local tile shape can be produced by label-preserving reassignment alone.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled null tables, the observed exact-match count was zero:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
\operatorname{exact\_match\_count}&=0,\\
\operatorname{observed\_M\_size}&=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape empirical probability is therefore reported as
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail comparison is less degenerate but still small: only $7$ of the $2000$ sampled tables had size at most the observed $M$ size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite-row content of the claim.  It says that under a null model constrained to preserve amino-acid classes, the observed window-six tile is not reproduced as an exact shape in the sampled row, and the observed size lies in a lower tail of frequency $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Comparison with the unconstrained shape sample.}
A separate finite sample of $N=2000$ random code-shaped tables gives one exact shape match and twenty-five high-overlap matches at Jaccard threshold $0.9$.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{exact\_shape\_matches}&=1,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
\operatorname{jaccard}_{\geq 0.9}\operatorname{\ matches}&=25,\\
p_{\mathrm{jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null is therefore not merely repeating the same empirical row.  It asks a stricter question: if label classes are held fixed, does the same local tile geometry appear?  In the reported sample the answer is no for exact shape, with the lower-tail statistic retaining a nonzero finite count of $7$.

\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports no anchors, five boundary cases, eight high-coverage closure cases, eight predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{anchor\_count}&=0,\\
\operatorname{boundary\_count}&=5,\\
\operatorname{closure\_coverage\_high}&=8,\\

By family, five families were evaluated.  The reported hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  These numbers support a code-layer local-tile pattern; they do not identify a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen boundary codons are partitioned by the reported module classification.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\operatorname{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\operatorname{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\operatorname{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\operatorname{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\operatorname{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification is complete at the row level: $R\operatorname{\_size}=13$, assigned count is $13$, and the extra-module, missing-from-modules, and unassigned-codon lists are all empty.  Each module has predictable-or-boundary status, so the finite account covers the whole thirteen-codon boundary rather than an isolated subrow.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope boundary.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact tests code-read facts and local codon-tile label splits.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Consequently, the supported claim is deliberately local: window-six codon coordinates expose a finite boundary-tile pattern whose amino-acid-class-preserving null frequency is small in the reported sample.  No translation process, no protein fold, no physical admissibility criterion, no biological function, no mechanism, and no universality claim follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.
