\paragraph{Null model.}
The tested null model preserves amino-acid classes while randomizing the relevant local shape comparison.  In $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, the observed main tile has size $|M|=20$.  The exact shape match count under the amino-acid-class-preserving null is $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0,\\
 \#\{\mathrm{null\ rows\ with\ exact\ shape}\} &= 0,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail comparison counts $7$ null rows at or below the observed $M$ size, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{aa\ class},\mathrm{lower\ tail}} &= \frac{7}{2000}=0.0035,\\

\end{aligned}
$$
This is the chapter's finite-row result: after preserving amino-acid class information, the observed local window-six shape is still not reproduced by the sampled null ensemble, and the lower tail remains small at $0.0035$.
