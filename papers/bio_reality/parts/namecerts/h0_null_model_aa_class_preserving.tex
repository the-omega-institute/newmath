\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{subsec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact layer.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon table as an external contact.  The contact supplies codon-label readback only: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label, and this assignment is treated as curated finite-row data rather than as a consequence of the cube geometry.  The distinctions used in this record are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The internal BEDC-side structure is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation data; the biological readback is the standard-code label table.  Thus the claim tested here is not that the codon cube determines biology, but that a particular local boundary tile is hard to reproduce under an amino-acid-class-preserving null model.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished relation row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured-cube row this same set has finite face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},&
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},&
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},&
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},&
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either a predictable member or a boundary member in the finite-row classification, so the row is not merely a cardinality coincidence; it has a codon-family decomposition aligned with the local tile boundary.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The spectral probe records $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic check is numerically closed at the stated tolerance, with polynomial residual
$$
\begin{aligned}
P(\lambda_M,\lambda_R,\mu)=-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This gives the local row a coordinate-level signature: the row is not only a set of codons, but a finite object with a measured spectrum and a small residual in the associated polynomial check.  The spectrum is still a code-layer diagnostic.  It is not a translation process, a folding model, a physical admissibility proof, or a functional assay.

\paragraph{Shape significance against unrestricted finite rows.}
A finite-row shape experiment with seed $42$ used $N=2000$ samples.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrow statement that the observed local tile is uncommon in this finite comparison regime.  They do not promote the tile to a global law of the genetic code.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model.}
The null model for this record preserves amino-acid class while randomizing the finite-row comparison.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$, the observed $M$-row size was $20$.  The exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact\ shape}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape statistic is therefore absent from the sampled amino-acid-class-preserving null rows, while the lower-tail statistic remains small.  In the finite-row sense used here, preserving amino-acid class does not by itself explain the observed local window-six tile.

\paragraph{Leave-one probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By code-family, the hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  The leave-one-codon-out probe recorded anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows show that the local boundary signal survives several finite deletion checks at the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The supported claim is local and finite: in the six-bit codon cube, the standard-code label table exposes a boundary-tile pattern whose $13$-codon row, punctured-cube face vector, spectral coordinates, module accounting, and null-model statistics are all nontrivial under the stated probes.  The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result.  No translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from these rows.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any claim beyond code-layer readback requires a separate reality contact designed for that higher layer.
