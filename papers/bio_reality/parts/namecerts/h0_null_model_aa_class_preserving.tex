\subsection{Amino-acid class preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The record \texttt{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving} concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer readback over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not asserted to be a mechanism of translation.  It is only a finite display in which codons are read as six coordinate bits, local tile faces are inspected, singleton label corners are separated from three-point label classes, and the resulting finite rows are compared with the curated standard-code label table.  The external contact is the curated standard genetic-code bridge: it assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label, but it supplies no fold, no physical admissibility test, no functional assay, and no universality theorem.

\paragraph{The distinguished row $R$.}
The observed boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube shape with finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This gives the local geometric object used by the null comparison: a thirteen-codon boundary tile sitting inside $Q_6$, not a biological law.  The module classification is complete on this row: the six named modules cover all $13$ codons with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},&
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},&
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},&
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the finite audit, so the row-level classification is closed for this contact.

\paragraph{Amino-acid class preserving null test.}
The amino-acid class preserving null model keeps label-class structure fixed while randomizing the relevant finite rows.  In $N=2000$ sampled null tables with seed $42$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$.  The recorded p-values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed local tile is not reproduced exactly in the amino-acid-class-preserving null sample, and only $7$ out of $2000$ null rows fall in the lower tail at the observed $M$-size threshold.  The conclusion is deliberately local: the null model says that this finite code-layer tile is unusual relative to that constrained sampling rule.  It does not say that the codon table is forced by geometry, nor that the tile is a complete account of translation.

\paragraph{Shape significance under the broader finite null.}
A related finite-row shape test used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite p-values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the same interpretation as the amino-acid preserving null: the standard-code row has a specific finite boundary shape that is rare under the tested randomization.  The duplicate-retry count is part of the evidence audit because it records the finite sampling pressure rather than hiding it behind an asymptotic approximation.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear ciliate, and candidate other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, across $5$ families.  The leave-one-codon-out probe reported anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These probes are consistent with a local code-read pattern: missing finite rows are often recovered by the tile-and-boundary rule inside the audited contact.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The cannot-claim boundary is part of the theorem record.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  The curated standard-code table supports codon-to-label readback and local split checks only.  Any promotion beyond the code layer would require a separate reality contact: for translation realization, a translation experiment; for structural order, a structure contact; for physical admissibility, a physical assay; for function, a functional assay; and for universality, a cross-system mechanism argument.  In this chapter the supported claim is therefore narrow and finite: the standard-code label table contains a thirteen-codon boundary row in $Q_6$ whose punctured-cube geometry, spectrum, module cover, and null-model rarity are all visible at the code-read layer.
