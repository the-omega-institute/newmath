\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Finite object and readback.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The BEDC carrier is not a biological mechanism; it is the finite coordinate display in which each codon is read as a point of $Q_6$, and the external contact is the curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The local structure under test consists of a coordinate presentation, a closure operation, a spectrum, and a relation extracted from the code-label readback.  The distinguished relation $R$ has exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit records $|R|=13$.  The same row set is identified as a punctured-cube boundary object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the empirical object is a local tile face with thirteen observed vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the finite coordinate complex.

\paragraph{Observed module decomposition.}
The thirteen rows of $R$ are completely assigned to six codon modules.  The module classifier reports no missing rows, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons: $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and the assigned count is $13$.  The finite module table is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either a predictable or boundary status in the leave-one-codon audit.  This is the finite-row content of the local tile: it is not inferred from protein behavior, folding, or organismal function, but from codon-label rows and their coordinate closure behavior.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes while resampling the finite table structure.  In the recorded run, the seed was $42$ and $N=2000$ null samples were evaluated.  The observed closure-side size was
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M|=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within the $2000$ amino-acid-class-preserving null samples, the exact observed shape occurred zero times:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ matches}\}=0,\qquad p\_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail statistic counted seven null samples with size at most the observed $M$-size:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{|M\_{\mathrm{null}}|\leq 20\}=7,\qquad p\_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These are finite Monte Carlo row counts, not asymptotic biological laws.  Their force is local: among label-class-preserving alternatives sampled in this run, the observed window-six tile is unusually small in the relevant lower-tail statistic and has no exact-shape match in the sample.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The reality contact is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is only that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  Therefore the claim is deliberately bounded.  The window-six geometry may expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code readback, and the amino-acid-class-preserving null run makes that structure non-generic within the sampled finite alternatives.  Nothing in this chapter proves translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded structure.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any promotion beyond code-read requires a separate higher-layer contact supplying the missing biological observation.
