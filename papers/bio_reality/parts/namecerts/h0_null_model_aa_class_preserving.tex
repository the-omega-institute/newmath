\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the codon tile}\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated codon-to-label table. The finite boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row certifies the punctured-cube shape by the $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not a slogan about the genetic code: it is a finite row in which thirteen codons, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces are selected inside the codon cube. The carrier distinctions used by the record are the six-bit codon display, local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class; the internal structure being tested is coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation.

\paragraph{Observed tile size and class-preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model fixes the amino-acid or stop class sizes and randomizes within that constraint. Against $N=2000$ sampled null tables with seed $42$, the observed tile size was
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M|=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
No exact class-preserving null sample reproduced the observed shape:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{\text{exact matches}\}=0,\qquad p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
Only $7$ of the $2000$ sampled null tables had size at most the observed $M$ size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local statistical claim of the row. It says that, even after preserving amino-acid class counts, the observed code-read tile is unusually small in the relevant lower tail and is not exactly reproduced in the sampled null ensemble. It does not say that the geometry is a biological law, a mechanism of translation, a constraint on physical admissibility, or a statement about folded proteins.

\paragraph{Comparison with the unconstrained shape null.}
A separate finite-row comparison used $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$ for shape matching. There was $1$ exact shape match, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{shape}}=1/2000=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler attempted $57377$ distinct draws and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. These counts matter because the row is finite and empirical: the evidence is not an asymptotic theorem about all possible genetic-code mechanisms, but a measured rarity statement for the displayed codon-tile shape under the specified sampling procedures.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The same local object carries a spectral certificate. The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial value was $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value recorded for the cubic-polynomial residual. This residual is numerically close to zero at the precision of the row, so the spectral data are consistent with the finite tile calculation. The spectral row supplies a coordinate-level diagnostic of the same code-read object; it is not a protein-structure observable.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and module classification.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. The module classification is complete for the thirteen codons in $R$: it assigns all $13$ codons, leaves no codons unassigned, has no extra module codons, and misses none of the codons from the modules. The displayed modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has a predictable or boundary representative in the finite row. This supplies a compact code-read decomposition of the boundary set $R$.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels. Therefore the chapter may claim a finite local codon-tile pattern, an amino-acid-class-preserving null result, a punctured-cube boundary row, a spectral diagnostic, and leave-one-code or leave-one-codon recovery statistics. It may not claim translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer readback. Crossing from this chapter to translation, folding, physical chemistry, or function would require a separate reality contact that directly observes that higher layer.
