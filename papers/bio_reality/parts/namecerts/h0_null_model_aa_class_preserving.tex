\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null keeps the label-class information fixed while randomizing within that constraint.  In $N=2000$ sampled null tables with seed $42$, the exact observed shape occurred $0$ times.  The exact-shape empirical probability is therefore reported as
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail statistic compares the sampled null size to the observed value $|M|=20$.  There were $7$ samples with size at most the observed value, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row conclusion is that the observed local tile is not reproduced by this amino-acid-class-preserving null at the exact-shape level, and it also lies in a small lower tail for the $M$-size statistic.  This is a code-layer finite-row significance statement, not an explanation of biochemical realization.

These figures support the same local reading: the observed boundary shape is rare under the sampled combinatorial background.  They do not promote the local tile to a global biological law.
