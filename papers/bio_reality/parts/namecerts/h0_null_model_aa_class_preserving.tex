\subsection{Amino-Acid-Class-Preserving Null Model for the Window-Six Codon Tile}

\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record concerns the claim \texttt{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving}.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon table as an external code-layer contact.  The contact supplies only a finite label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label.  It does not supply a translation process, a folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  The BEDC object under test is therefore a local window-six boundary tile inside the codon-label readback, not a global biological law.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the observed row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
   &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row is not merely a count: it also carries a face-incidence signature with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the local tile.  The row includes the stop-related codons $UAA$, $UAG$, and $UGA$, the arginine pair $AGA,AGG$, the leucine block $CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU$, and singleton or mixed-module positions such as $AAA$, $AUA$, $UCA$, and $UUA$.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid class information while randomizing the finite code-layer arrangement subject to that constraint.  Against $N=2000$ such null samples with seed $42$, the observed tile has $|M|=20$.  The exact-shape match count is $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, so the finite-row lower-tail probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central finite-row finding for the claim: within the amino-acid-class-preserving null ensemble, the observed local tile is not recovered exactly in the sampled population, and only seven samples fall at or below the observed $M$-size tail threshold.  The conclusion is a code-read signal about a local codon tile, not an inference about the physical realization of proteins.

\paragraph{Comparison with unconstrained shape sampling.}
A broader shape-significance run also used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, before collecting the finite null population.  In that run there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null is therefore stricter in the exact-shape statistic for this row: its observed exact count is $0$ out of $2000$, while the broader shape run observed $1$ exact match out of $2000$.  Both readings keep the same scope boundary: they are finite code-layer comparisons over codon labels.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite-row evidence supports the following restricted statement.  In the six-bit codon cube, the standard-code label table exposes a local boundary tile whose row $R$ has $13$ codons, punctured-cube face data $(13,16,4)$, a numerically stable spectral signature, strong leave-one-code-out recovery, complete leave-one-codon-out module assignment, and a significant amino-acid-class-preserving lower-tail statistic $0.0035$ with no exact matches in $2000$ null samples.  This is enough for a ready code-read claim about local codon-label structure.  It is not enough for any claim about translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Any such higher-layer statement would require a separate reality contact whose observed facts live at that higher layer.
