\subsection{Amino-Acid-Class-Preserving Null Model for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:bior-reality-aa-class-preserving-null-model}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The object tested here is the standard-code codon table read through a six-bit codon display.  The BEDC-minimal carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with distinctions for coordinate position, local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks, but it is not a contact with translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The finite boundary set $R$ has exactly thirteen codons.  The verified list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run identifies this set as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed object is not merely a count: it is a finite local tile with thirteen codon vertices, sixteen boundary edges, and four square faces.  The row therefore supplies a concrete local geometry on the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Observed module split.}
The module classification accounts for all thirteen codons in $R$ and leaves no unassigned or extra module codons.  The six finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification row reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has either predictable or boundary status.  This is the local finite-row content of the split; it is not a claim that these modules are universal biological mechanisms.

The spectral radius in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$.  These scalars are internal finite-row diagnostics of the coordinate-and-relation object.  They should be read as evidence that the local tile has a stable numerical signature under the stated probe, not as evidence for folding, function, physical admissibility, or a translation mechanism.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The claim row tests whether the observed shape is explained by an amino-acid-class-preserving null model.  The run uses $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The observed $M$ size is $20$.  Among the $2000$ null samples, the exact observed shape appears $0$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}} &= 0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both reported significance fields equal the same values: $p\_value\_aa\_class\_exact\_shape\_significant=0.0$ and $p\_value\_aa\_class\_lower\_tail\_significant=0.0035$.  Within this finite sample, preserving amino-acid class does not reproduce the exact local tile, and only $7$ out of $2000$ samples fall in the lower tail relative to the observed $M$ size.

\paragraph{Shape-comparison control.}
A separate shape-significance row also uses $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It records $57377$ distinct sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape match count is $1$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}} &= 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ has $25$ matches, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This control is consistent with the amino-acid-class-preserving null result: the observed local pattern is rare under the sampled finite-row alternatives, both exactly and under high-Jaccard comparison.

The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast, across $5$ families.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports $anchor\_count=0$, $boundary\_count=5$, $closure\_coverage\_high=8$, $predictable\_count=8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These are code-layer recovery checks for codon labels, not observations of protein structure.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite rows support the following bounded statement: window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and the observed tile is rare under the sampled amino-acid-class-preserving null model.  The evidence is strongest at the code-read layer: the thirteen-codon boundary set is explicit, the punctured-cube $f$-vector is fixed, the six modules cover $R$ exactly, the spectral row is numerically stable, and the null-model tail probabilities are small in the reported finite samples.  The same rows also impose the cannot-claim boundary.  No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, universal mechanism, or law of biology follows from this local codon tile unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that information.
