\subsection{Amino-Acid-Class-Preserving Null Model for the Window-Six Codon Tile}\label{subsec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The record tests a local window-six codon geometry on the finite codon cube $Q_6$ against the curated standard-code label table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon display, and the observed contact is only the standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Thus the biological readback is a code-layer readback: it supplies codon labels, amino-acid labels, and stop labels, but it does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  The local tile under discussion is therefore a finite row object inside $Q_6$, not a global biological law.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its observed codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the finite combinatorial support on which the local tile claim rests.  The row is not being interpreted as a force, a pathway, or a necessary rule of molecular biology; it is a finite set of codon-label positions whose shape can be compared with controlled null samples.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null test.}
The primary null model preserves amino-acid class while randomizing the relevant row structure.  In $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$, the observed module size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, and the count in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The corresponding empirical values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The exact-shape result says that no amino-acid-class-preserving null sample reproduced the observed shape in the finite sample.  The lower-tail value says that only seven of the two thousand sampled rows landed at or below the observed $M$ size.  The conclusion is finite-row significance relative to this null ensemble, not a claim that the codon table was generated by the coordinate system.

\paragraph{Independent shape significance check.}
A second shape check used $N=2000$ distinct samples with seed $42$.  Producing those distinct samples required $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}}=1/2000=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supplies a second finite-row comparison: the exact shape is rare in the sampled ensemble, and high-overlap shapes remain uncommon.  The test is still a code-read test.  It does not observe an amino-acid chain being translated, folded, stabilized, selected, or used.

\mu&=3.051487585298128.

The associated cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius value in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers are useful as finite invariants of the computed row geometry.  They do not change the layer of the claim: a spectral invariant of the code-label tile is not evidence for protein structure or physical admissibility unless another contact measures that higher layer directly.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, $5$ families were evaluated: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had hit rate $1.0$.  These figures support the statement that the local row geometry is informative for code-label readback across the tested table family.  They do not supply a translation apparatus, a kinetic model, or a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and module classification.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe reported anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The module classification is complete on the $13$ codons of $R$: assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite row.  The module statement is therefore internally closed at the level of codon-label row accounting: it classifies all thirteen row codons and leaves no codon outside the module ledger.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The verified claim is that amino-acid-class-preserving null samples do not reproduce the observed local codon-tile shape in the sampled finite ensemble, and that the same row participates coherently in punctured-cube, spectral, leave-one-code-out, leave-one-codon-out, and module-classification summaries.  The allowed biological contact is the curated standard-code table.  The cannot-claim boundary is local and explicit: no translation, no folding, no structural order, no physical admissibility, no biological function, no mechanism, and no universality follows from this chapter unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains code readback; it is not folded protein structure.
