\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null for the window-six codon tile}\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table as a finite code-read object, not as a mechanism for translation or protein realization. The carrier is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-bit display of codon coordinates. The finite distinctions used here are the coordinate display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class. The external contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. Thus the biological readback is only a label table: it supplies codon-to-label facts and does not itself supply folding, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or universality.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also recorded as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. In this finite table the observed $M$-row has size $20$. The supplied finite row gives the value $|M|=20$ but does not give the member list of $M$, so this chapter uses only the reported size and the reported shape statistics, not an unstated membership assertion.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes while resampling the finite code table. Against this null, the reported run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. The exact-match count was $0$, giving the finite-run exact-shape value
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$ out of $2000$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The interpretation is deliberately narrow. The row says that, under an amino-acid-class-preserving resampling scheme, the observed local codon-tile shape is not reproduced in the finite sample and the observed $M$ size lies in a small lower tail. It does not say that the geometry is a biological law, that codons are physically forced by $Q_6$, or that amino-acid labels determine protein structure.

\paragraph{Independent finite-shape comparison.}
A broader shape comparison over $N=2000$ distinct samples, also with seed $42$, recorded $57{,}377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55{,}377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$. The corresponding finite empirical values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}}&=1/2000=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the same code-layer reading: the local tile is a finite, non-generic pattern relative to the sampled nulls, while the contact remains the curated codon-label table.

This is a numerical consistency check for the finite coordinate construction; it is not a biochemical energy, folding score, or physical admissibility measure.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. Separately, the leave-one-codon-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ codons with high closure coverage, $8$ predictable codons, and recovery rate $1.0$. These rows measure finite recovery behavior inside the code-read table. They do not constitute a claim about translation realization or functional realization.

\paragraph{Family and module rows.}
The family-stratified leave-one-code-out row has $5$ families. The hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast. The module classification assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ with no extras, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons:

Thus the boundary row is not merely counted; it is partitioned into named finite modules, and every module has predictable or boundary status in the supplied record.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null strengthens the finite code-layer reading of the window-six tile. The strongest local statement is that the standard-code label table, when represented on $Q_6$, exposes a finite boundary set $R$ of size $13$, an observed $M$ size of $20$, a punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, small empirical null probabilities under both amino-acid-class-preserving and broader shape sampling, and stable leave-one-out recovery behavior. The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation process, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from these rows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer information.
