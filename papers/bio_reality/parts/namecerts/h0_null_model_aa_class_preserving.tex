\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Claim record.}
The finite object tested here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display. Each row is a codon-label row: the codon supplies the coordinate and the curated table supplies the amino-acid or stop label. The claim is deliberately local. The window-six coordinates may expose a boundary tile in the code-read layer, but the labels themselves are contacts with the curated standard code rather than consequences of the coordinate system alone. The table contact used in this chapter says only that codons have assigned amino-acid or stop labels in the standard code. It does not observe translation dynamics, folded protein structure, chemical admissibility, organismal function, or a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The observed boundary row set $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same set is recovered as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the tested finite pattern is not an unconstrained list of exceptional codons; it is a thirteen-row local tile with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces in the induced codon-cube geometry. The row count is part of the claim: $|R|=13$, and no larger biological interpretation is being imported from that count.

\paragraph{Spectral row geometry.}
The associated spectral calculation gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual reported for this finite row calculation is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the cubic-polynomial residual is the same numerical value. This places the computed spectral datum within numerical tolerance for the stated finite display. These numbers are used only as row-geometry diagnostics: they identify the local shape being tested and do not turn the coordinate tile into a mechanistic account of coding, translation, folding, or fitness.

\paragraph{Shape significance before class preservation.}
An unrestricted finite-row Monte Carlo comparison with $N=2000$ sampled tables produced $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$. The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sample generation attempted $57377$ distinct draws and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, with seed $42$. This comparison supports the claim that the observed local row shape is rare under the sampled background, while still remaining a code-layer fact about labeled codon rows.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model for this chapter preserves amino-acid class structure while testing whether the observed local shape reappears. In $N=2000$ class-preserving trials, the exact-match count was $0$. The observed $M$-set size was $20$, and only $7$ trials landed in the lower tail at or below that observed size. The reported finite-sample values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Equivalently, the exact class-preserving shape was not seen in the $2000$ draws, and the lower-tail event occurred with frequency $7/2000=0.0035$. This is the central null result: preserving amino-acid classes does not by itself make the standard-code local tile ordinary in the sampled finite background. The conclusion is statistical and finite-row: it says that the displayed codon-label geometry is unusually specific under this null, not that the coordinate scheme explains biological implementation.

\paragraph{Predictive leave-out checks.}
The leave-one-code-out check evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary rows was $0.9841269841269841$. A separate leave-one-codon-out check reported $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These checks are compatible with the local tile reading: missing code-label rows are often recovered by the finite row geometry plus boundary rows. They still do not observe peptide production, molecular structure, or organismal role.

\paragraph{Family and module stratification.}
The family-stratified leave-one-code-out calculation contains $5$ families. The family hit rates above baseline are $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate, $1.0$ for candidate-other, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast. The module classification assigns all $13$ boundary rows with no extras, no missing rows, and no unassigned codons: $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, extra module codons $\varnothing$, missing-from-modules $\varnothing$, and unassigned codons $\varnothing$. The modules are

Every listed module has predictable or boundary status in the reported classification. This gives a finite row-by-row accounting of the whole boundary set rather than a loose visual analogy.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and boundary.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null result is ready at the code-read layer. The finite evidence consists of the thirteen-codon boundary set, its punctured-cube face-vector $(13,16,4)$, the spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, the unrestricted shape probabilities $0.0005$ and $0.0125$, the class-preserving exact-match frequency $0/2000$, and the class-preserving lower-tail frequency $7/2000$. Together these records support a local codon-label tile that is rare under the tested nulls and stable under several leave-out probes. The chapter makes no stronger biological claim. No statement about translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this contact unless a separate experiment or curated contact explicitly supplies that higher-layer observation.
