\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the codon tile}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Claim scope.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer. The finite object is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a window-six display of the codons. The tested distinction is local: a tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class are compared against curated codon-to-label assignments. The contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, which supplies labels for codons but does not supply a ribosome, a kinetic model, a folded structure, a physical admissibility test, a biological function assay, or a universal mechanism. The conclusion therefore stays inside code-layer label readback and local tile comparison.

\paragraph{Finite codon rows.}
The recorded boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the finite geometry being tested is not an arbitrary verbal grouping: it has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the recorded punctured-cube representation. The module partition of these thirteen codons is complete: $\mathrm{AGR}=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\}$, $\mathrm{AR/Lys}=\{\mathrm{AAA}\}$, $\mathrm{CUN}=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\}$, $\mathrm{Ile/Met}=\{\mathrm{AUA}\}$, $\mathrm{SSN}=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\}$, and $\mathrm{Stop/Trp}=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$. The audit row reports $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Null ensemble.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row uses $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$. The observed size of $M$ is $20$. Within that null ensemble, the exact tile shape is not hit at all: exact match count is $0$, so the recorded exact-shape frequency is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail comparison counts $7$ samples with size at most the observed $M$ size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row significance fields repeat these values as $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact\ shape\ significant}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail\ significant}}=0.0035$. This is the central null-model result for the present claim: after preserving the amino-acid class constraint encoded by the run, the observed local codon-tile pattern remains unusually small and no exact copy of its shape appears in the $2000$ sampled rows.

\paragraph{Independent shape and spectrum checks.}
A separate shape-significance run with the same seed size $N=2000$ records one exact shape match, $25$ high-Jaccard matches at threshold $0.9$, $57377$ distinct sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries. Its reported frequencies are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The radial cubic check is numerically closed at the recorded tolerance, with polynomial value $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is reported for the cubic-polynomial residual. These numbers say that the local split is visible both combinatorially and spectrally, but they do not convert the split into a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Readback stability probes.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$. By family, five families are recorded: alternative yeast has hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have hit rate $1.0$ above baseline. The leave-one-codon-out row records anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These rows support a code-table regularity statement: the finite tile has predictive value for label readback under the recorded omissions.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The curated standard-code table is a label contact. It allows the row to say that codon labels in the standard code align with a finite local tile in $Q_6$, and that the alignment is rare under the recorded amino-acid-class-preserving null model. It does not allow the row to say that translation is realized by this geometry, that an amino-acid label sequence folds, that a fold is physically admissible, that a function follows, that a mechanism has been identified, or that all biological codes must obey the same pattern. Any one of those stronger statements would need a separate contact of the appropriate kind. In this chapter the supported claim is narrower: window-six coordinates expose a statistically sharp local codon-label boundary, while the curated labels remain the point of contact with biological data.
