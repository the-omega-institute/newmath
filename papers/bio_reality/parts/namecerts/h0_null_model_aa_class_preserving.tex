\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:biorality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Question.}
The claim \texttt{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving} asks whether the local codon-tile pattern remains exceptional after the null model is required to preserve amino-acid label classes.  The data object is the standard-code codon table read at the code-label layer: codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels, and the six-bit codon display gives a finite coordinate cube $Q_6$ on which local faces, singleton corners, and three-point label classes can be inspected.  This chapter treats that coordinate object as a finite readout experiment, not as a biological mechanism.  The curated table supplies codon-to-label assignments; it does not by itself observe translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or any universal rule.

\paragraph{Finite codon row.}
The verified boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also verified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the contact is not merely a count: it records a finite subset of the codon cube together with its local incidence shape.  The supplied finite row gives the observed $M$ size as $20$, but it does not provide the membership list of $M$ in this packet; consequently this chapter uses the size of $M$ and the tested comparisons involving $M$, while leaving the explicit $M$ codon enumeration to the underlying experiment artifact.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment.}
The class-preserving null model used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  The observed tile size was $|M|=20$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving randomization, the exact tile shape was never reproduced:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
\#\{\text{exact matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail comparison counted $7$ sampled rows with size at most the observed $M$ size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\{|M_{\mathrm{null}}|\leq 20\}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These figures say that the local tile is rare even after label-class preservation is imposed.  The result is stronger than a purely unconstrained shuffle would be expected to be, because the null already retains the amino-acid class structure before testing the coordinate pattern.

\paragraph{Shape comparison against the broader finite sample.}
A companion shape-significance row used $N=2000$ with seed $42$ and required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, including $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that run there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This second row is not identical to the amino-acid-class-preserving null, but it supplies a useful scale check: exact reconstruction of the observed local geometry is rare, and even near reconstruction at the $0.9$ Jaccard threshold remains uncommon in the verified finite sample.

$$
The radial cubic check is numerically closed at the recorded tolerance, with polynomial residual
$$
\begin{aligned}

The verified spectral-radius entry repeats $0.6752479308830213$ for the leading $M$ value.  This spectral information is used only as a finite coordinate invariant of the code-label readout.  It is not evidence that the genetic code is generated by this linear operator, nor that the operator is a biochemical law.

\paragraph{Leave-one rows.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the verified hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, across $5$ families.  The leave-one-codon-out row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These finite rows indicate that the observed local tile is stable under several deletion tests at the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The module classification row assigns all $13$ codons of $R$ with no missing or extra module codons.  The complete partition is

\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\

The row reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, empty extra-module codons, empty missing-from-modules list, and empty unassigned-codons list.  Each module has either a predictable or boundary role in the verified classification.  This matters for the null claim because the exceptional geometry is not an unparsed collection of codons; it is organized into finite label-related modules while still staying within code readback.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
The accepted statement is narrow.  The standard-code table, viewed through the six-bit codon display, exposes a rare local tile whose boundary set has $13$ codons, whose punctured-cube incidence vector is $(13,16,4)$, whose observed $M$ size is $20$, whose class-preserving exact-match count is $0$ out of $2000$, and whose lower-tail probability is $0.0035$.  The associated deletion and family rows show strong code-layer recovery and family-level persistence.  The result is therefore ready as a finite code-read claim: the local codon geometry is statistically nontrivial under the recorded amino-acid-class-preserving null model.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
No stronger biological conclusion is licensed by this packet.  The data do not show translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality across life.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only a label readback.  Any claim about folding, structural order, organism-level transfer, tRNA realization, functional selection, or physical feasibility must be supported by a separate contact that observes that layer directly.  The lowercase probe \texttt{codon.local.tile.no.global.law} is therefore satisfied in the restricted sense: the local tile can be tested against the curated standard-code table, while higher-layer promotion remains blocked.
