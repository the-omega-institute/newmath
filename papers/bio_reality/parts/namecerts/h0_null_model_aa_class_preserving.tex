\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}\label{sec:bior-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer object, not as a biochemical process.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read through the usual codon alphabet and then compared with the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The observable row used here is therefore a finite table of labels.  The internal distinctions retained by the construction are the six-bit codon display, a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The relevant internal data are coordinate position, closure, spectral size, and relation membership.

The distinguished relation $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a finite subcomplex of the codon cube, this row is recorded as a punctured-cube shape with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The corresponding observed $M$ row has size $20$.  The supplied finite data do not include an explicit codon-by-codon list for $M$, so this chapter uses only the reported cardinality and the tests derived from it.

\paragraph{Null model.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes while randomizing the relevant finite assignment.  In $N=2000$ amino-acid-class-preserving trials with seed $42$, the exact tile shape was never reproduced: the exact-match count was $0$, giving the empirical exact-shape rate $0/2000=0.0$.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, so the reported lower-tail value is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed window-six row is not explained as a common outcome of the amino-acid-class-preserving randomization used in this finite experiment.  This is a finite-row statement about the code table and the chosen coordinate display.  It is not a claim that the codon table is forced by geometry, by chemistry, or by a universal biological mechanism.

A separate shape-significance run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, sampled $57377$ distinct-attempt rows and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding empirical rates are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These two rows give compatible finite evidence: exact reconstruction is rare under the sampled nulls, while near-overlap occurs but remains uncommon at the reported threshold.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  It supports codon-label readback and finite local split checks.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a dynamical mechanism, or universality across possible codes.  Consequently, the conclusion is deliberately narrow: under the stated amino-acid-class-preserving null model, the observed standard-code local tile is rare in the reported finite samples, and its boundary modules are strongly recovered by the reported leave-one-out probes.  No statement about protein structure, molecular viability, organismal function, or necessary biological law follows from this chapter unless an additional empirical contact supplies that higher-layer information.
