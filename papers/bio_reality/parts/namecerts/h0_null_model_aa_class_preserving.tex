\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the codon boundary tile}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with the standard-code label readback. The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it is treated as a code-layer reality bridge rather than as a mechanism for translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, or universality. The finite row isolates a local tile face with a singleton label corner and a three-point label class. Its distinguished residual set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the code-read geometry is not merely a count: it carries a concrete finite face structure with $13$ vertices, $16$ edges, and $4$ two-dimensional faces inside the window-six display.

\paragraph{Observed local split.}
The observed module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$ with no missing and no extra module codons. The assigned modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{CUN} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{SSN} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module-classification row reports $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no unassigned codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no extra module codons. The corresponding leave-one-codon-out row gives boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, anchor count $0$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These figures support a local boundary-readback claim: when a codon is removed from this finite code-layer object, the remaining window retains enough local closure or boundary information to recover the tested codon in the reported cases.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes while randomizing the shape subject to that finite constraint. In the reported run, $N=2000$ samples were evaluated with seed $42$. The observed $M$ size is $20$. The exact amino-acid-class-preserving shape match count is $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact\ significant}} &= 0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} = {7 \over 2000} = 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the chapter-specific null result: among $2000$ amino-acid-class-preserving randomized rows, no exact shape match was observed, and only $7$ rows landed in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size. The result is a finite-row rejection signal against the claim that the observed local tile is typical under this class-preserving null. It is not a proof that the biological table is caused by the window-six geometry.

\paragraph{Comparison with the unrestricted shape probe.}
The broader shape-significance row used $N=2000$ distinct sampled rows with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. In that run the exact-shape match count was $1$, so $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and the high-Jaccard count at threshold $0.9$ was $25$, so $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$. The amino-acid-class-preserving null is therefore stricter in one direction and sharper on exact shape in this finite row: the exact match count drops from $1$ under the broader probe to $0$ under the amino-acid-class-preserving probe, while the lower-tail statistic remains small at $0.0035$.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported claim is local and finite: window-six codon coordinates expose a boundary-tile structure whose residual set $R$, punctured-cube $f$-vector, spectral row, module partition, and amino-acid-class-preserving null behavior are all non-generic in the reported finite probes. The claim stops there. No translation realization follows from this chapter. No folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity of the genetic code follows from this chapter. The curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-label readback only. Any statement about translation, protein folding, physical admissibility, function, or universality would require a separate higher-layer contact not present in this row.
