\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite coordinate display for the $64$ codons. The distinguished subset $R$ is the thirteen-codon boundary row
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen displayed codons in the cardinality row and in the punctured-cube row. The punctured-cube face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the record is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it is a local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the chosen six-bit display. The biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact supplies labels for readback; it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Unrestricted shape null.}
Against the unrestricted shape null with $N=2000$ sampled finite rows and seed $42$, the exact local shape appeared once. The exact-shape empirical probability is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold row counted $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the row is a finite Monte Carlo comparison with heavy duplicate rejection. The conclusion permitted by this row is narrow: the observed codon tile is rare under that sampled shape null. The row does not promote rarity to necessity, adaptation, function, or universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
The claim tested here is the amino-acid-class preserving null model. In this null, the finite shuffle respects amino-acid label classes and asks whether the same local tile shape is recovered under that class-preserving constraint. With $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the exact match count is $0$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=\frac{0}{2000}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed $M$-size is $20$. The lower-tail counter records $7$ samples with size at most the observed $M$-size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the strongest finite-row support for the present record: even after preserving amino-acid classes, the exact boundary-tile shape is absent from the sampled null, and the observed $M$-size lies in a lower tail of empirical mass $0.0035$. The null comparison is still a code-layer statement. It concerns codon-label readback inside a curated table and says nothing by itself about translation realization, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a causal mechanism, or universality across genetic systems.

These numbers support a robust code-read regularity across the sampled table families, but they remain table-label facts.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The ready claim is therefore a scoped finite-row statement. Window-six coordinates expose a rare local boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code label table. The unrestricted null gives exact-shape probability $0.0005$ and high-Jaccard probability $0.0125$; the amino-acid-class preserving null gives no exact matches in $2000$ samples and lower-tail mass $0.0035$ for observed $M$-size $20$; the spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$ with residual about $6.3\cdot 10^{-12}$; and the code-read probes give high leave-one-code-out and leave-one-codon-out recovery. The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: no translation, no folding, no physical admissibility, no function, no mechanism, and no universality follows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies it.
