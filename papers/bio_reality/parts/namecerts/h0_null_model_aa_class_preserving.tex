\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the local codon tile}\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with codons displayed as row-level labels and with the curated standard genetic-code table used only to attach amino-acid or stop labels to those codons.  In this finite readback, the distinguished residual row set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row set is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the row evidence is not merely a label count: it includes $13$ vertices, $16$ local adjacencies, and $4$ two-faces in the tile extracted from the codon cube.

\paragraph{Class-preserving comparison.}
The null model tested here preserves amino-acid classes while randomizing the relevant code-label placement.  Against $N=2000$ sampled class-preserving tables with seed $42$, the observed tile has $|M|=20$.  No sampled table exactly reproduced the observed shape, so the exact-match count is $0$ and the empirical exact-shape probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0 .
\end{aligned}
$$
For the lower-tail comparison, $7$ of the $2000$ samples had size at most the observed value, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035 .
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local finite-row significance statement: under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed row-size and tile shape are not typical in the sampled population.  The claim is deliberately confined to this finite experiment; it is not a claim that the geometry causes the genetic code or that all biological realizations must exhibit the same local pattern.

\paragraph{Unrestricted-shape comparison.}
A second finite comparison used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$ and required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, including $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that run there was $1$ exact shape match, yielding
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, yielding
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125 .
\end{aligned}
$$
These two values give an independent finite-row check that the observed tile is a sparse event among sampled alternatives, both for exact equality and for high-overlap similarity.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, interpreted as a codon-to-label source.  It supports code-layer readback and local label-split tests.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism of origin, or universality across all code systems.  Consequently, the empirical statement is that window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local tile whose label distribution is unusual under the tested nulls and stable under the listed leave-one probes.  Any assertion about translation, folding, admissible molecular structure, function, mechanism, or universal biological necessity would require a separate contact measuring that higher layer.
