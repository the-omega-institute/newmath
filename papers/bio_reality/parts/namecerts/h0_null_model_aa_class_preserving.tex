\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite object tested here is the standard-code codon table read through a six-bit codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, while the biological contact is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Thus the row data support a code-read statement: codons can be compared as finite coordinates carrying external labels.  They do not by themselves supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such higher-layer claim would require a separate contact.

The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified list is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is certified as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local tile boundary under study.  Its interpretation is deliberately narrow: it is a finite codon-coordinate shape together with label readback from the curated standard code.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid class information while randomizing the code-layer arrangement.  Against this restricted null, the observed boundary behavior is not typical.  In $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$, the exact observed shape occurred $0$ times.  The reported exact-shape p-value is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed $M$-size is $20$.  The lower-tail count at or below this observed size is $7$ out of $2000$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both reported significance fields equal their corresponding p-values: $0.0$ for exact shape and $0.0035$ for the lower tail.  These numbers support a finite-row statement: under the amino-acid-class-preserving null used in this run, the observed local boundary tile is rare.

\paragraph{Comparison with unconstrained shape sampling.}
A separate shape-significance run gives a useful scale for the same local object.  With $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the exact shape was seen once, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run observed $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  The amino-acid-class-preserving run is therefore not the only row in which the local tile appears exceptional; the broader shape probe also places the exact shape and near-shape events in a small tail.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite-row verdict is that the standard-code label table contains a rare local boundary-tile pattern when codons are read as six-bit coordinates and labels are supplied by the curated code table.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives no exact matches in $2000$ samples and a lower-tail p-value of $0.0035$ at observed $M$-size $20$.  The verified $R$ row has $13$ codons, face-vector $(13,16,4)$, complete six-module classification, and compatible spectral and leave-out summaries.  The result is therefore a code-layer boundary statement only.  No translation process, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from these rows unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies it.
