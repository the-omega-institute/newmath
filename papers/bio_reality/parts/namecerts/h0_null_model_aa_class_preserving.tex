\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the local codon tile}
\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Finite claim.}
The claim tested here is the finite-row assertion named $\mathrm{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving}$.  Its carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code label table.  The row does not treat the codon cube as a biological mechanism.  It asks a narrower question: after the observed local tile $M$ has been read at the code layer, does its size and boundary shape look typical under a null model that preserves amino-acid label classes?  The contact used for this question is only the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-layer label readback and local label splits; it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In codon notation the verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is verified as a punctured-cube boundary with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of labels: it is a finite local tile in $Q_6$ with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces.  This is the geometric datum whose compatibility with label-preserving randomization is tested below.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes while randomizing the local assignment subject to that constraint.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled rows, the observed tile size was
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M|=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
No sampled amino-acid-class-preserving row matched the exact observed shape:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{exact\_match\_count}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{lower\_tail\_count}_{\leq |M|}&=7,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Within this finite experiment the observed tile is therefore not typical of the amino-acid-class-preserving null ensemble.  The exact-shape event was absent from the $2000$ draws, and the lower tail contains only $7$ rows.  The conclusion is deliberately finite-row: it says that the observed code-layer tile is exceptional for this specified null model, not that codon biology is governed by the window-six geometry.

\paragraph{Independent shape and spectrum checks.}

\begin{aligned}

These numbers are used only as finite diagnostics of the local tile and boundary spectrum.  They are not promoted to dynamical, structural, or functional biological laws.

\paragraph{Local boundary verdict.}
The finite verdict is that the standard-code label table supplies enough contact to read a six-bit local codon tile, its thirteen-codon boundary row, its punctured-cube face-vector, its spectral diagnostics, and its leave-one recovery behavior.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, the observed tile is exceptional: the exact-shape count is $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$.  The result remains strictly local and code-layer.  No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, global mechanism, or universality claim follows from this row unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
