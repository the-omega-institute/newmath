\paragraph{Reality contact and limits.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for testing local label splits.  It is not an observation of translation dynamics, folded structure, thermodynamic admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or a universal law of coding.  In particular, amino-acid label sequences are still labels; they are not protein structures.  Any claim about folding, structural order, physical realization, organism-level function, or universal necessity would require a separate measurement or curated contact that directly observes that higher layer.  Without such a contact, the present record remains a finite code-read result: a class-preserving null model fails to reproduce the observed local tile, while the biological scope stays at curated codon-to-label assignment.
