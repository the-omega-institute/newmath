\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The object tested here is not a complete biological mechanism.  It is the standard-code codon label table read through a six-bit codon display, with the BEDC-side carrier taken to be the codon cube $Q_6$.  The admissible distinctions are coordinate position, local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class.  The external contact supplies only the curated assignment from codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Thus the contact can test code-layer readback and local label-split structure, but it cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The null model preserves amino-acid label classes while randomizing the codon-row geometry under the stated finite procedure.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled null rows, the observed BEDC tile has $M$-size $20$.  The exact-shape count under the amino-acid-class-preserving null is $0$, giving the empirical exact-shape estimate
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}} = 0/2000 = 0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}} = 7/2000 = 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row conclusion is therefore sharp but local: after preserving amino-acid classes, the observed window-six tile is not reproduced exactly in the $2000$ null draws, and only $7$ draws fall into the observed-or-smaller $M$-size tail.  This is evidence for a code-table geometry contact, not evidence for folding, function, or a universal genetic mechanism.
