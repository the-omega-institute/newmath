\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The relevant BioReality object is therefore not translation as a biochemical realization, but the code-layer readback of labels on the finite cube. The observed boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row audit records $|R|=13$, and the same row is also the punctured-cube row with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the object under discussion is a concrete local tile on codon labels, not a global biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape coordinates.}
The window-six construction exposes a local coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation structure. In the spectral row, the measured values are

\paragraph{Unrestricted shape null.}
Against the shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the observed shape has $1$ exact match among $2000$ draws, with $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$. The high-overlap comparison gives $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, hence $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and had $55377$ duplicate retries. This supports the finite statement that the observed local codon tile is unusual relative to the sampled null ensemble. It does not by itself identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The claim indexed here is the amino-acid-class-preserving null model. In that null, the finite run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. The observed row has $|M|=20$. The exact tile was not reproduced: the exact-match count is $0$, so the empirical exact-shape probability is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,exact}}=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail statistic counted $7$ sampled rows with size at most the observed value $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa,class,lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the amino-acid-class-preserving finite row separates the observed tile from the class-preserving randomized background both by exact shape and by lower-tail size. The exact-shape result is a finite-row absence statement over this sample, not a proof that no other class-preserving table can reproduce the tile.

Each module has a predictable-or-boundary witness in the finite classification. This is a code-read partition of a finite codon row, not an assertion that the modules are universal biological units.

\paragraph{Local cannot-claim boundary.}
The contact named by this chapter is the curated standard-code table. It supports codon-to-label readback and local split checks. It does not test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary necessity. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore strictly a code-layer observation here. Any claim about protein realization, folding, admissible molecular physics, function, or universality requires a separate reality contact supplying that layer. Without such a contact, the local window-six tile remains a finite BioReality coordinate pattern with significant null-model separation and a sharply bounded interpretation.
