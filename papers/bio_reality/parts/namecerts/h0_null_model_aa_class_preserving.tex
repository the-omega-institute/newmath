\subsection{Amino-Acid-Class-Preserving Null Model for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:bior-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The finite display is not treated as a biochemical mechanism. It is a coordinate object whose rows are the $64$ codons, whose observable labels are the curated amino-acid or stop assignments, and whose local questions are asked only at the code-read layer. The tested claim is that the window-six coordinate description exposes a local boundary-tile pattern while keeping the label table as a contact with observed biological convention rather than as something derived from geometry alone.

\paragraph{Distinguished boundary set.}
The verified distinguished set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row reports that this set is a punctured-cube boundary object with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the finite certificate is not merely a list of codons: it records $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ square faces in the local tile. The accompanying module classification assigns all $13$ codons with no missing or extra elements. The modules are $\{AGA,AGG\}$ for $agr$, $\{AAA\}$ for $ar\_lys$, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for $cun$, $\{AUA\}$ for $ile\_met$, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ for $ssn$, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for $stop\_trp$. This is a complete finite partition of $R$, since the recorded $R$ size and assigned count are both $13$, with empty missing and unassigned lists.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinates.}
The same finite packet carries a spectral reading of the tile. The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, also reported as the cubic-polynomial residual. At the numerical precision of the run, this means that the spectral summary is internally consistent with the finite tile calculation. It does not by itself make a biological assertion; it gives a stable coordinate signature for the row set that is later compared with label-preserving null samples.

\paragraph{Shape significance against unconstrained samples.}
The shape-significance run used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ distinct samples. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, so the sample space being probed is visibly constrained by finite collision pressure. Among those $2000$ samples, the exact shape was matched once, giving $p_{exact}=0.0005$. The high-overlap comparison counted $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. These two numbers say that the observed local shape is uncommon under the sampled null distribution, while still being a finite empirical comparison rather than a theorem about all possible codon labelings.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null comparison.}
The claim-specific null model preserves amino-acid classes and tests whether the same local shape appears when the labels are randomized under that constraint. In the verified run $N=2000$, the observed $M$ size was $20$. There were no exact matches to the observed shape, so the empirical exact-shape count was $0$ and the reported exact-shape $p$-value was $0.0$ at this sample resolution. The lower-tail comparison counted $7$ samples with size at most the observed $M$ size, yielding
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{exact,class} &= 0.0,\\
p_{lower,class} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The value $0.0$ here means zero hits in $2000$ trials, not a mathematical impossibility statement. The lower-tail number gives the safer finite reading: under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, rows as small as the observed $M$ are rare in this run.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The code-table leave-one-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$, equal to the reported rate above baseline. By family, the hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, across $5$ families. The codon leave-one-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These numbers support the local readback role of the tile: removing codons or code rows tends to leave a recoverable boundary or closure signal inside this finite display.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact is strong enough to test code-layer readback and the local codon-tile label split. It is not a contact with translation dynamics, folded protein geometry, thermodynamic admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or cross-organism universality. Consequently, the finite evidence may be stated as follows: the window-six coordinate system, together with the curated standard-code labels, identifies a rare and recoverable local boundary tile under the tested null models. It does not show that organisms must implement the geometry, that protein structure follows from the codon pattern, or that the pattern is a universal biological law. Any promotion beyond code-read requires a separate observation at the corresponding biological layer.
