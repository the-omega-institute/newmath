\subsection{Amino-Acid-Class-Preserving Null Model for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{subsec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The record tests a six-coordinate codon display for the standard genetic code at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the $Q_6$ codon cube: each codon is treated as a six-bit point, and the only observed biological labels used here are the curated standard-code assignments of codons to amino-acid or stop symbols.  The local structure selected by the construction is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit reports $|R|=13$ and identifies the same thirteen codons in the punctured-cube computation.  Its face data are
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed pattern is not merely a list of exceptional labels; it is a small local tile with vertices, edges, and two-dimensional faces inside the codon cube.

\paragraph{Class-preserving null comparison.}
The null model used for this claim preserves amino-acid class information while randomizing the finite codon-label geometry.  In $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$, the observed tile size was $20$ on the measured $M$ side, the exact-shape match count was $0$, and only $7$ sampled tables fell in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The recorded probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the class-preserving null does not reproduce the exact observed local shape in this sample, and the low-tail comparison also places the observed size in a small part of the null distribution.  This is a finite sampling statement about the standard-code label table and the chosen coordinate display; it is not a claim that codon biology is forced by the cube geometry.

\paragraph{Independent shape and spectral checks.}
A second Monte Carlo row for the same code-level geometry used $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, and found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{shape}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The spectrum row supplies a numerical invariant for the same finite structure.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual was $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check.  At the precision of the run, the spectral radius check therefore closes numerically on the displayed finite object.

\paragraph{Leave-one evidence.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the local tile and its boundary carry useful code-layer readback information under finite withholding tests.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module row partitions all thirteen $R$ codons without leftovers.  The complete assignment is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The audit reports $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The family-level leave-one-code-out row used $5$ families.  Its hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  This supports the interpretation that the finite signal is organized by code-label modules rather than by an unconstrained codon list.

\paragraph{Local boundary of the claim.}
The reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table: it supplies codon-to-label readback and permits checks of local label splits.  It does not observe translation as a cellular process, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The tile may be a sharp code-layer datum, but no protein-level or organism-level conclusion follows from this contact alone.  Any statement about translation realization, folding, physical law, functional effect, or universal biological mechanism would require a distinct measurement route with its own finite observations.
