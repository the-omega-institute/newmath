\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the local codon window}\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The claim tests whether the local window-six codon geometry survives a null model that preserves amino-acid label classes. The data object is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-bit codon display. The observed boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies this set as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the observable datum is not merely the number of codons in the boundary set; it is a finite local shape in the codon cube, with vertices, one-dimensional incidences, and two-dimensional tile faces recorded together. The associated observed module size is $|M|=20$, while the explicit finite row supplied here gives the codons of $R$ rather than the full codon list of $M$.

\paragraph{Null experiment.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null run uses $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$. In that ensemble, the exact local shape was recovered $0$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class},\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0,\\
\#\{\hbox{samples with exact shape}\}&=0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail comparison against the observed module size found $7$ samples with size at most the observed value, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class},\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=7/2000=0.0035,\\
|M|_{\mathrm{observed}}&=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the central finite-row witness for the present claim: even when the null model respects amino-acid class information, the observed local codon-window shape remains rare in the sampled ensemble. The result is narrower than a biological mechanism claim. It says that the curated standard-code labels, when placed in this six-coordinate display and compared against class-preserving controls, exhibit a local boundary-tile structure that the null samples almost never reproduce exactly.

\paragraph{Independent shape and spectral checks.}
A second shape-significance row sampled $N=2000$ tables with seed $42$ and required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts because $55377$ attempted samples were duplicates. That run found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=1/2000=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The spectral row supplies an additional numerical fingerprint of the same local construction. It reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with both the cubic-polynomial residual and the radial-polynomial residual equal to approximately $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$. At this precision the residual is numerical roundoff rather than a visible deviation in the finite computation. These numbers do not identify a physical force or a molecular dynamics law; they give a compact check that the observed tile has a stable coordinate-and-spectrum signature inside the chosen finite representation.

\paragraph{Readback against code labels.}
The reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The contact is sufficient for code-layer readback and local label-split tests. It is not a translation experiment, not a structural assay, not a thermodynamic admissibility measurement, and not a functional readout. Within that boundary, the leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ alternative tables and $63$ total targets. It records a baseline hit rate of $0.19821739130434785$, a hit rate above baseline of $0.9841269841269841$, a hit rate in $M\setminus T$ of $0.9365079365079365$, and a hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary of $0.9841269841269841$. The family breakdown has $5$ families: alternative yeast has rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each have rate $1.0$. These rows show that the local tile is informative for code-label variation in this finite setting, especially near the boundary set, without promoting the tile to a universal biological rule.

\paragraph{Codon-deletion and module accounting.}
The leave-one-codon-out row reports no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. The module-classification row accounts for all $13$ codons of $R$: $R$ has size $13$, the assigned count is $13$, and there are no extra module codons, no codons missing from modules, and no unassigned codons. The finite modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has either a predictable or boundary status in the finite classification. This closes the local accounting problem for the supplied row: the exceptional codons are not left as an unnamed residue after the null comparison, but are partitioned into named finite modules.

\paragraph{Local conclusion and scope limit.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null model supports the local code-read claim. The standard-code labels, placed in the six-bit codon cube, give a $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary with face-vector $(13,16,4)$, observed module size $20$, exact class-preserving null frequency $0/2000$, and lower-tail frequency $7/2000$. The separate shape run and the leave-one analyses are consistent with the same reading: the local window is a real finite pattern in the curated code table, and it is unusually structured under the tested null models. Nothing in this chapter establishes that DNA or translation must obey this geometry. No conclusion about protein folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or universality follows from these rows unless a separate observation of that higher layer is supplied.
