\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null model for the codon boundary row}
\label{sec:bior reality-aa-class-preserving-null-model}

\paragraph{Finite codon display.}
This record concerns a six-coordinate codon display in which each codon is treated as a vertex of the finite codon cube $Q_6$. The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code table: each codon receives its standard amino-acid or stop label, and no additional measurement of translation, folding, physical realizability, biochemical activity, or evolutionary mechanism is imported. The finite row singled out by the local boundary calculation is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality is $|R|=13$. The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube pattern with finite face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observation is not merely a list of codons but a local cubical incidence pattern inside $Q_6$.

\paragraph{Null model.}
The null experiment preserves amino-acid class information while randomizing the finite code-layer arrangement. In the verified run with seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled tables, the observed comparison set had size $20$. No sampled table reproduced the exact observed shape: the exact-match count was $0$, giving the recorded empirical exact-shape value $p=0.0$ at the run resolution. The lower-tail statistic counted $7$ sampled tables with comparison size at most the observed size, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the amino-acid-class preserving null model places the observed row in a small lower tail of the sampled distribution, while the exact local shape was not seen in the sample.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral checks.}
A second finite randomization check tested shape similarity directly. With $N=2000$, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, the run recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated spectral row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic residual, equivalently the recorded radial-polynomial value, is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically at tolerance scale for this finite computation. These numbers support the narrower statement that the local standard-code row has a reproducible finite geometry under the stated display and sampling scheme.

This closes the finite accounting for the local row: every codon in the observed boundary set is named by exactly one listed module in the supplied classification.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The supported claim is a code-read claim about a finite local codon-tile boundary in a six-coordinate display, tested against a curated standard-code label table and amino-acid-class preserving null samples. The row supplies evidence for a non-generic local split in that display, with exact-shape and lower-tail statistics reported above. It does not by itself establish translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law governing all DNA. Any such higher-layer statement would require an additional contact whose observed data are about that layer rather than about the codon-to-label table.
