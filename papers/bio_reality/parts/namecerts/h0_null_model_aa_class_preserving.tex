\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null model for the codon boundary tile}

\paragraph{Finite object under test.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The biological contact used here is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, and the tested structure is a local boundary tile in that cube.  The readback is therefore a code-layer readback: codons are compared through their curated labels, not through translation dynamics, folded structure, physical chemistry, biological function, or any universal biological mechanism.

The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the supplied finite row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row records that this set is a punctured-cube face complex with face vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object being tested is not a loose enrichment claim over the full table; it is a specified thirteen-codon local tile with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces inside the window-six coordinate geometry.

\paragraph{Null model.}
The null model for this chapter preserves amino-acid class information while resampling the relevant shape comparison.  With seed $42$ and $N=2000$ null samples, the observed tile has $M$-size $20$.  The exact-shape count under this class-preserving null is $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact,class}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size is $7$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail,class}}={7\over 2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers say that the observed local configuration is not a typical output of the amino-acid-class preserving randomization used in the row.  They do not say that the coordinate geometry causes the genetic code, that translation is thereby reconstructed, or that protein-level structure has been observed.

\paragraph{Comparison with the shape null.}
A separate shape-significance row with the same seed and $N=2000$ records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ duplicate retries, one exact shape match, and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
 p_{\mathrm{exact,shape}}&={1\over 2000}=0.0005,\\
 p_{J\geq 0.9}&={25\over 2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The class-preserving null is therefore at least as stringent at the exact-shape level in this finite run: its exact count is zero rather than one.  The high-Jaccard comparison remains finite and empirical; it measures closeness of sampled tiles to the observed local pattern, not a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The class-preserving null row supports a narrow conclusion.  In the standard-code table, the window-six codon display exposes a thirteen-codon boundary tile whose exact shape is absent in $2000$ amino-acid-class preserving null samples and whose lower-tail statistic is $0.0035$.  The same packet records a punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, and complete module coverage for the thirteen codons.  These are finite code-layer observations.

The chapter therefore claims only a local codon-label phenomenon.  It does not claim translation realization, protein folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such higher-layer claim would require an additional contact that directly measures that layer rather than merely reading codon labels from the curated standard-code table.
