\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null test for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
The claim tested here is the code-layer claim that a six-bit codon display on the codon cube can expose a local boundary-tile pattern while still treating the standard-code label table as an external reality contact. The carrier is the finite codon cube $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code table as codon-to-label data. The distinguished boundary row is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified row count is $|R|=13$. The same finite row is also verified as a punctured-cube pattern with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a finite combinatorial statement about a displayed codon table. It is not a claim that the biological translation apparatus is generated by $Q_6$, and it is not a claim about folded protein structure.

\paragraph{Observed shape and class-preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null test uses $N=2000$ sampled class-preserving label rearrangements with seed $42$. The observed local object has size $|M|=20$. Under this null, the exact observed shape occurred $0$ times, giving the empirical exact-shape value
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count for samples with size at most the observed value was $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite-row evidence says that preserving amino-acid class alone does not typically reproduce the observed local tile. In the associated unrestricted shape-significance run with the same seed and $N=2000$, there was $1$ exact-shape match, $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries. The corresponding empirical values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a code-layer shape anomaly: the local tile is rare under the tested finite nulls. They do not identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row check.}
The row also has an independent spectral readback. The verified spectral values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual was $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the recorded spectral-radius value in tolerance was $0.6752479308830213$. The small residual is a numerical consistency check for the finite matrix calculation. It is not a separate biological observation; it is a derived probe on the same finite codon display.

\paragraph{Leave-one readbacks.}
Two leave-one probes give the local tile a concrete predictive profile at the code-read layer. In the leave-one-code-out run, $23$ tables and $63$ total targets were evaluated. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate. In the leave-one-codon-out run, there were $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchors, closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These are finite recovery statements about codon-label rows. They do not assert that the recovered label sequence folds, functions, or is physically admissible as a protein.

\paragraph{Module-level accounting.}
The thirteen boundary codons are completely assigned by the verified module classification. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons. This closes the row accounting for the local tile: every codon in $R$ is accounted for by one of the displayed modules.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported conclusion is deliberately narrow. The finite data support a code-read statement: in the standard-code codon table, the window-six coordinate display exposes a rare local boundary-tile pattern, and that pattern remains rare even under an amino-acid-class-preserving null model. The contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which supplies codon-to-amino-acid or stop labels. That contact can test code readback and local label splitting. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. No such higher-layer conclusion follows unless a separate higher-layer contact is supplied.
