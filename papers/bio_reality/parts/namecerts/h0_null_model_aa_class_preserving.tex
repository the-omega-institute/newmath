\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null for the window-six codon tile}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object and contact.}
The object recorded here is the standard-code codon table, read only at the label layer.  The coordinate carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, used as a finite display for the $64$ codons.  The distinguished data are a local tile face, singleton label corners, three-point label classes, and the relation between the coordinate geometry and the curated amino-acid or stop labels.  The contact used in this record is a curated standard genetic-code table: it supplies codon-to-label assignments, but it is not an observation of translation dynamics, folded protein geometry, energetic admissibility, organism-level function, mechanism, or biological universality.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The boundary set $R$ has exactly $13$ codons.  In standard RNA notation the row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite check identifies this same row as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of labels: it has a small cubical incidence profile inside $Q_6$.  The record nevertheless remains local.  A cubical face count in the codon coordinate display is not a biological law about all coding systems and is not evidence for any higher layer without a separate measurement at that layer.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
The null experiment preserves amino-acid classes while randomizing the relevant label placement.  In $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$, the observed tile size was $|M|=20$.  The exact shape count under this null was $0$, so the sampled exact-shape tail is
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count for samples with size at most the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower}}=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the specific finite-row result for claim $h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving$: after preserving the broad amino-acid class partition, the local shape remains unusually constrained in the sampled null ensemble.  The statement is about the code-label readout of a curated table and the coordinate geometry of $Q_6$; it does not promote the local tile to a physical or functional account of biology.

\paragraph{Comparison with unrestricted shape sampling.}
A companion shape sampling row used $N=2000$ with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact observed shape appeared once, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=\frac{1}{2000}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=\frac{25}{2000}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class preserving null is therefore not the only finite row indicating rarity.  The exact-shape comparison gives a sharper count, while the high-Jaccard comparison records a broader neighborhood around the same local pattern.

\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\

$$
The radial cubic residual is

\begin{aligned}

which is numerically at tolerance for the recorded polynomial relation.  This spectral row is useful because it reports an internal finite invariant of the same code-layer geometry.  It should not be read as a biochemical spectrum or as a dynamical model of translation.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M\setminus T$ together with the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows support a local recovery interpretation at the code-label layer: omitted labels are often recovered by the finite coordinate-and-boundary structure.  They do not observe a ribosome, a protein fold, a viability constraint, or a functional phenotype.

\paragraph{Family and module classification.}
The code-family leave-one-out row groups the $23$ evaluated tables into $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  The boundary row also decomposes into six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module audit reports $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every listed module has either predictable or boundary status.  This is a complete finite classification of the supplied $R$ row, not a claim that the named biological categories share a structural or mechanistic origin.

\paragraph{Scope of the record.}
The verified finite facts jointly say that the standard-code label table, when placed in the six-coordinate codon cube, contains a rare local tile with a $13$-codon punctured boundary, a $20$-codon observed $M$ region, a stable module decomposition, and high leave-one recovery against the supplied finite comparisons.  The null result for amino-acid-class preserving randomization is especially sharp: no exact matches appeared in $2000$ samples, and only $7$ samples landed in the lower tail at the observed size threshold.  The scientific reading is deliberately narrow.  The record supports code-layer readback and local split checks.  It does not by itself establish translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality; any such assertion would require an additional contact whose observation is actually made at that higher layer.
