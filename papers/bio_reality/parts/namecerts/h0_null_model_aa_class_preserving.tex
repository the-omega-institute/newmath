\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the local codon tile}
\label{subsec:bior-reality-aa-class-preserving-null}

\paragraph{Claim.}
The finite object tested here is the standard-code codon table read through a six-coordinate codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, and the biological contact is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The claim under audit is the null-model statement named $\mathrm{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving}$: after preserving amino-acid label classes, the observed local tile remains unusually small and structured relative to finite randomized rows.  This is a code-read claim.  It is not a claim about translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Finite row under test.}
The observed boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified as a punctured-cube tile with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  In the finite display, this says that the selected codons do not appear merely as an unstructured list: they carry a local face-and-edge signature inside the six-bit codon cube.  The observed $M$-row size used by the amino-acid-class-preserving null test is $20$.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null result.}
The null model used $N=2000$ finite randomized trials with seed $42$, preserving amino-acid label classes before comparing the observed local shape.  The exact-shape count was $0$, so the finite-run empirical exact-match value was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\widehat p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0/2000=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count for rows with size at most the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\widehat p_{\mathrm{lower}}&=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus, within this finite row experiment, preserving amino-acid classes did not explain away the observed local tile: no exact randomized shape matched the observed one, and only seven randomized rows reached the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size.  The statement is finite and empirical: it reports the audited $2000$-row experiment, not an asymptotic theorem and not an impossibility claim over all conceivable code tables.

\paragraph{Comparison with the unconstrained shape audit.}
A related shape audit also used $N=2000$ rows with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, and found exactly one exact-shape match.  Its finite empirical exact value was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\widehat p_{\mathrm{shape}}&=1/2000=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the count at Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
\widehat p_{J\geq 0.9}&=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This comparison is useful because the amino-acid-class-preserving null is stricter about label structure, whereas the shape audit measures the geometric rarity of the same local object more directly.  Both finite rows point in the same direction: the tile is not typical among the sampled alternatives.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table, observed as a codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop label assignment.  This contact can test code-read behavior and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the chapter-level conclusion is deliberately narrow: the six-coordinate display exposes a rare, spectrally coherent, module-complete local boundary tile under an amino-acid-class-preserving null model, but the biological content remains exactly at the label-readback layer unless a separate higher-layer contact is supplied.
