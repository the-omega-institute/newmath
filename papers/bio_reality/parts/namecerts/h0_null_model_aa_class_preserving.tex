\subsection{Amino-Acid-Class-Preserving Null Model for the Window-Six Codon Tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-aa-class-preserving-null}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon table as an external reality contact.  The BEDC-side data are the coordinate display, local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class; the readback side is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  Thus the carrier supports a code-layer question: whether the window-six coordinate geometry isolates a local boundary-tile pattern that is unusually compatible with the standard-code labels.  The contact does not supply a translation experiment, a folding experiment, an admissibility model, a function assay, a mechanism, or a universality theorem.

\paragraph{Observed boundary row.}
The observed boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The finite row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies $R$ as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a combinatorial statement about the codon-coordinate display and the induced local tile.  It is not a statement that these codons are biologically forced by the geometry.  The curated code table supplies the labels against which the row can be read, but it remains a reality bridge rather than a derivation of the biological code.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
The claim tested here is the amino-acid-class-preserving null model.  In $N=2000$ sampled null rows, with seed $42$, the observed distinguished set has size $20$, and the exact window-six shape was never matched:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N&=2000,\\
|M|_{\mathrm{obs}}&=20,\\
\#\{\text{exact shape matches}\}&=0,\\
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail statistic counts $7$ sampled rows at or below the observed $M$ size, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=7/2000=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Under this class-preserving null, the observed local tile is therefore a rare finite-row event: not one exact shape recurrence in the sampled null population, and only $0.35\%$ of sampled rows in the lower tail at the observed size threshold.

\paragraph{Shape significance under the broader row sample.}
A companion shape-significance row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  In that run there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches at Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, so
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}}&=1/2000=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}&=25/2000=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the same local conclusion from a second finite-row view: the observed tile is not merely a large arbitrary subset of codons, but a coordinate-sensitive shape whose recurrence is sparse under the tested sampling regimes.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite-row evidence is aligned with the claim that window-six codon coordinates expose a rare local boundary-tile pattern whose curated standard-code labels can be read back with strong finite support under the tested nulls.  The strongest local facts are the $13$-codon punctured-cube boundary, the exact amino-acid-class-preserving null count $0/2000$, the lower-tail value $0.0035$, the companion exact-shape value $0.0005$, the high-Jaccard value $0.0125$, the spectral residual near zero, and the complete $13$-of-$13$ module assignment.  These facts remain code-layer facts.  No translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity follows from this chapter unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that information.
