\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null model for the local codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}

\paragraph{Claim packet.}
The packet tests the claim identified by \texttt{h0.null.model.aa\_class\_preserving}.  The biological object is the standard-code codon table, read only at the code-label layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, where a codon is treated as a six-bit display and local faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes are used as finite distinctions.  The record is not a claim about translation dynamics.  It asks whether the observed local tile remains unusual after preserving amino-acid class information in the null model.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured cube with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the finite observation is not merely a count: it has $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ local square faces under the window-six coordinate relation.  The observed companion set $M$ is reported by size in this finite row, with $|M|=20$; no explicit ordered codon list for $M$ is part of this packet.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class preserving null experiment used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ sampled tables.  The exact-shape count was $0$, so the empirical exact-shape probability is reported as $p=0.0$ at this sample size.  The lower-tail statistic compares the observed $M$ size against the sampled null distribution: $7$ of $2000$ samples had size at most the observed value $20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}} = 7/2000 = 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both the exact-shape statistic and the lower-tail statistic are therefore small in this finite audit.  The result says that the observed local tile is not typical under this particular class-preserving randomization.  It does not say that the codon table is generated by the coordinate system, nor that the coordinate system is a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral checks.}
A separate shape-significance row used seed $42$ and $N=2000$ distinct samples, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The spectral row reports $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  Its radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, numerically consistent with the stated polynomial constraint at the recorded tolerance.  These scalar checks support the finite geometry of the code-label tile, while remaining within the code-read layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one-out behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary row was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, $5$ families were recorded: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, while bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had hit rate $1.0$.  The leave-one-codon-out row reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers make the packet a finite robustness check for code-label recovery, not a theory of the biochemical realization of recovery.

This is the local combinatorial content of the packet: the observed standard-code labels occupy a small, structured part of the six-coordinate cube and retain unusually strong recovery behavior under the listed finite probes.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of assertion.}
The only contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code readback and a local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, function realization, or a universal biological law.  Consequently, the chapter asserts only a finite code-layer regularity: under the recorded amino-acid-class preserving null, the observed local tile and its boundary modules are rare in the sampled null ensemble and robust under the listed leave-one-out probes.  No conclusion about protein folding, structural order, organism-level mechanism, function, or universality follows from these rows unless an additional, independent contact supplies observations at that higher layer.
