\subsection{Amino-acid-class preserving null model for the window-six codon tile}
\label{sec:bioreality-aa-class-preserving-null}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate record.}
The object studied here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a window-six codon coordinate system.  Each codon is represented as a point of the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, while the biological labels are not derived from the cube itself.  They enter through a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop.  The claim is therefore deliberately narrow: the coordinate display may expose a local boundary-tile pattern at the code-read layer, and the amino-acid labels provide a finite readback test for that pattern.  The coordinate system does not by itself establish translation dynamics, folded structure, biochemical admissibility, organismal function, a molecular mechanism, or universality across all genetic-code usage.

\paragraph{Observed boundary set.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the recorded run $5d4a3459997d$, the set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row appears in the punctured-cube check $e19917012c60$, where the reported face data have $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the record is not merely a count: it specifies the exact codon members and a finite local shape.  The codons include the stop-related entries $UAA$, $UAG$, and $UGA$; the leucine-related $CUN$ block $CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU$ together with $UUA$; the arginine-related pair $AGA, AGG$; the lysine-related $AAA$; the isoleucine or methionine-related $AUA$; and the serine-related $UCA$.  These names are used only as standard-code labels read from the curated table.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null test.}
The main finite-row test for this chapter is the amino-acid-class preserving null model, run as $e8d5de747a0c$ with seed $42$ and $N=2000$ samples.  The observed $M$ size is $20$.  Among the $2000$ class-preserving randomizations, the exact match count is $0$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035,\\
\#\{\text{samples with size at most the observed }M\} &= 7.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the chapter-specific null claim: preserving amino-acid class information does not commonly reproduce the observed local tile.  The exact-shape event was absent in the sampled class-preserving null ensemble, and only seven sampled configurations fell in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The conclusion is statistical and finite: it supports the distinctiveness of the code-layer tile against this particular constrained null model, not a biological necessity theorem.

\paragraph{Shape and spectrum checks.}
A second null run, $0b267c88f8e4$, used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$ and required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts because $55377$ attempts were duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, so $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, so $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  These finite values agree with the class-preserving null result in treating the observed tile as uncommon under the tested sampling rules.

The recorded spectral row $1026324eff41$ gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.

$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual.  The reported spectral radius in tolerance is $0.6752479308830213$.  This supplies a numerical internal check on the tile geometry: the coordinate-and-relation data are stable enough to produce a reproducible spectral signature, but the signature remains a code-layer invariant of the finite display.

\paragraph{Predictive readback rows.}
Two leave-one-out records test how the tile behaves when code-table information is partially withheld.  In the leave-one-code-out run $429e6e2c4eb4$, $23$ tables were evaluated over $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  Thus the local tile has strong code-read recovery power in this finite evaluation, with the boundary row supplying nearly all of the above-baseline effect.

In the leave-one-codon-out run $e9e1a6e41633$, the anchor count is $0$, the boundary count is $5$, the high closure-coverage count is $8$, the predictable count is $8$, and the recovery rate is $1.0$.  The recorded threshold check is also $1.0$.  This does not say that codons are physically generated by the tile; it says that, within the curated standard-code readback task, the withheld codon labels were recovered by the finite coordinate pattern under the recorded evaluation rule.

\paragraph{Reality contact and limitation.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  That contact supports codon-to-label readback and local split checks.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, peptide folding, structural order, molecular admissibility, biological function, or universal mechanism.  Consequently, amino-acid label sequences read from this table must not be promoted to folded protein structure, and a local codon-tile pattern must not be promoted to a global biological law.  Separate empirical contacts would be required before crossing any of those boundaries.

\paragraph{Chapter verdict.}
The finite-row record supports a ready code-read claim for the amino-acid-class preserving null model.  The exact boundary row $R$ has $13$ named codons, the punctured-cube row reports $(13,16,4)$ face data, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the class-preserving exact-shape count is $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$.  Independent shape and spectral rows give $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$, $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  Leave-one-out evaluations show strong finite readback performance, including $0.9841269841269841$ hit rate above baseline in the leave-one-code-out row and $1.0$ recovery rate in the leave-one-codon-out row.  The scientific content is therefore a constrained finite claim: the window-six coordinate display captures an unusually stable local code-label tile under the recorded null and recovery tests, while all higher biological interpretations remain outside this chapter unless separately measured.
