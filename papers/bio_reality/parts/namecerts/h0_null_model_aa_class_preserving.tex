\subsection{Amino-acid-class-preserving null model for the local codon tile}
\label{sec:bior-h0-null-model-aa-class-preserving}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with the standard-code label readback supplied by the curated codon table.  The row is a code-read record, not a translation or structure record: codons are read as amino-acid or stop labels, and the only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code assignment.  Within this finite carrier the distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
As a punctured-cube object this same row reports the finite face-count vector $(13,16,4)$, so the local tile is not merely a list of exceptional labels but a concrete finite subshape of the codon cube.  The observed amino-acid-class-preserving comparison keeps the standard amino-acid class counts fixed and asks whether the same local tile is recovered under this constrained null.

\paragraph{Null-model row.}
The amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  The observed local marker size was $|M|=20$.  Among the $2000$ constrained samples, the exact tile match count was $0$, giving an empirical exact-shape probability
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0,\\
\#\{\hbox{exact matches}\}&=0,\\
N&=2000.
\end{aligned}
$$
The lower-tail count at the observed marker size was $7$, so the lower-tail probability was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}&=\frac{7}{2000}=0.0035,\\
|M|_{\mathrm{obs}}&=20.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the constrained null does not explain the observed local marker by preserving amino-acid classes alone.  The exact-shape event was not seen in the sampled row, and the lower-tail event was rare at the reported resolution.  This is a finite-row statement about the standard-code label table and the window-six coordinate display; it is not a statement that geometry alone causes biology.

\paragraph{Comparison with the unconstrained shape row.}
The broader shape-significance row used the same sample size and seed, $N=2000$ and seed $42$, but did not impose the amino-acid-class-preserving interpretation in the same way.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries, and found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact\ shape}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving row is therefore stricter at the exact-shape level: it reports $0$ exact matches in $2000$ samples, while the broader shape row reports $1$ exact match in $2000$ samples.  Both rows point in the same local direction, namely that the observed standard-code tile is not a typical draw from the tested finite nulls.

\paragraph{Spectral coordinate.}
The same finite object also carries a spectral readout.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic or radial polynomial residual was $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius value in tolerance was again $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers are used only as finite internal structure on the codon-coordinate row.  They support the statement that the local marker has a reproducible coordinate-and-spectrum profile, but they do not promote the codon table to a physical, structural, functional, or universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback checks.}
Two leave-one checks probe whether the local tile behaves as a code-read boundary rather than as a global biological law.  In the leave-one-code-out row, $23$ tables were evaluated against $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, $5$ families were reported: alternative yeast had hit rate $0.8333333333333334$, and bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate each had hit rate $1.0$ above baseline.  In the leave-one-codon-out row, there were $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the local boundary is informative for finite code-label recovery tests; they do not say that the recovered amino-acid label sequence is a folded protein structure.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons of $R$ are completely assigned to six finite modules with no missing or extra module codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Thus every codon in the boundary row belongs to a named code-read module, and every named module has predictable or boundary support in the finite record.

\paragraph{Local verdict and cannot-claim boundary.}
The finite-row verdict is that the standard-code codon table contains a reproducible window-six local tile whose boundary set $R$, marker size $|M|=20$, spectral values, module partition, and leave-one recovery behavior are not typical under the tested amino-acid-class-preserving null.  The strongest supported claim is local and code-layered: curated standard-code labels expose a finite boundary-tile structure in $Q_6$.  Nothing in this row establishes translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  A separate reality contact would be required before crossing any of those boundaries.
