\subsection{Window-six codon boundary packet}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The packet studies the standard-code codon table through a six-coordinate codon display.  The carrier is the finite codon cube $Q_6$, read only at the code-label layer: each codon is treated as a finite word over $\{A,C,G,U\}$ together with the curated amino-acid or stop label supplied by the standard-code table.  The selected boundary region is the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality statement is $|R|=13$.  The same finite row identifies $R$ with the punctured-cube boundary pattern having face-vector data $(13,16,4)$, so the combinatorial object is not merely a list of exceptional codons: it carries a small cell structure with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces.  The row is therefore a local boundary packet inside $Q_6$, not a statement about translation dynamics.

\paragraph{Spectral and closure measurements.}
The spectral row gives three numerical coordinates for the same local packet.  The recorded values are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The associated radial cubic check evaluates to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically zero at the recorded tolerance, and the reported spectral-radius entry is again $0.6752479308830213$.  These figures support a finite geometric reading: the boundary set $R$ sits in a structured window whose measured radius is stable under the stated polynomial check.  They do not identify a biochemical force, a ribosomal process, or a folding rule.

\paragraph{Empirical contrast against random label arrangements.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ null samples with seed $42$.  The sampler needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, showing that the finite comparison space was heavily revisited.  Among the accepted null samples there was exactly $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second null that preserves amino-acid classes reported observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$ over $N=2000$ samples with the same seed.  Thus the finite code-label arrangement is unusual under both recorded comparison procedures.  The claim is still restricted to code readback: statistical rarity of the window pattern is not evidence of folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Leave-one recovery rows.}
The leave-one-code experiment evaluated $23$ alternative code tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The row also records $0.9365079365079365$ for hits inside $M-T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ when the boundary is included with $M-T$.  The leave-one-codon experiment reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows say that the local window has strong finite recovery behavior when one removes code-table or codon entries and asks whether the remaining code-layer coordinates recover the target.  The observation is about label recovery inside a finite codon table, not about whether a cell translates a transcript or whether a peptide realizes a stable structure.

\paragraph{Family and module resolution.}
The family-level leave-one-code row grouped the evaluated code tables into $5$ families.  The hit-rate-above-baseline values were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate cases.  The module-classification row assigns all thirteen boundary codons without surplus or omission: $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},&
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},&
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},&
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},&
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Each module is recorded as having predictable-or-boundary status.  The module row is important because it prevents the thirteen-codon set from being an unstructured residue class: every codon in $R$ is accounted for by a named finite module, and no codon outside the row is smuggled into the packet.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, whose observed fact is that each codon has a standard amino-acid or stop label.  That contact can test code-layer readback and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently the packet supports the following bounded statement: the window-six coordinates expose a thirteen-codon boundary region with verified cardinality, punctured-cube combinatorics, spectral numerics, null-model contrast, recovery behavior, and complete module assignment at the code-label layer.  It does not license any inference that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological law, that a label sequence folds, that a protein function follows, or that the same local tile is a mechanism of translation.  Any such stronger claim would require a separate contact measuring the relevant biological layer.
