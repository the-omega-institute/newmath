\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
The BioReality row records a finite code-read contact on the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  Each codon is read as a coordinate point in the cube and is then compared with a curated standard-code label table.  The row does not treat the label table as a mechanism.  It treats the table as an external contact that permits codon-label readback and local tile tests.  In this coordinate readback the distinguished boundary row is the finite set $R$ with exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
   &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality claim is therefore the concrete finite assertion
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also recorded as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the code-read object is not merely a count: it has a finite incidence shape with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional faces, and four two-dimensional local faces in the recorded tile geometry.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact used here is the curated standard-code table.  It supports code-layer readback: codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels, and the local window-six split can be checked against those labels.  It does not observe translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a causal mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the row permits the statement that a thirteen-codon boundary tile is present in the recorded standard-code readback, with the listed spectral, null-model, prediction, and module signatures.  It does not permit any claim that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity, that the geometry realizes translation, or that amino-acid label sequences determine folded structure or function.  Crossing any of those boundaries requires a separate reality contact with its own finite observations.
