\subsection{Window-Six Boundary Row for the Standard Code}
\label{sec:biori-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record concerns the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ with readback through a curated standard-code table.  The carrier is not a biochemical mechanism.  It is a finite coordinate display whose rows are codons and whose labels are the amino-acid or stop labels supplied by the external table contact.  The contact says only that the standard genetic-code bridge assigns codons to labels at code-read resolution.  In this chapter the object under test is therefore a local tile in the codon-label table, not translation realization, not folded structure, not physical admissibility, not function, and not a universal biological law.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality thirteen.  In the finite-row data it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also recorded as a punctured-cube shape with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the chapter-specific finite claim: the local window-six construction isolates a thirteen-codon boundary set with sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies and four two-dimensional faces in the induced tile complex.  The statement is deliberately local.  The existence of this row does not say that DNA realizes the geometry as a necessity, and it does not promote codon-label readback into a statement about protein structure.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the sampled null row for the $M$ shape, the run used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exact shape agreement occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
High-overlap agreement at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ occurred twenty-five times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  In that row the observed $M$ size is $20$, exact shape matches are $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$.  Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The chapter reads these probabilities as finite evidence that the observed code-read tile is not a typical member of the sampled comparison rows under those null choices.  It does not read them as evidence for a universal mechanism.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact supporting the chapter is the curated standard-code table.  Therefore the supported claim is exactly a code-read claim: window-six coordinates expose a local codon-label boundary tile whose thirteen-codon row is stable under the stated finite checks.  The unsupported promotions are excluded inside the statement itself.  No translation realization follows.  No folded structure follows.
