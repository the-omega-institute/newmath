\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite object in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ read through the curated standard-code label table. The coordinate carrier is internal: codons are treated as six-bit display points equipped with local tile faces, singleton label corners, three-point label classes, closure, spectrum, and relation data. The amino-acid and stop labels are not internal consequences of the cube. They enter through one external contact: a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. This contact tests code-layer readback and local codon tile label splits only. It does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

The spectral row separates the local tile from random relabeling baselines. The recorded values are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The finite-row evidence therefore supports a non-generic local shape signal. It does not, by itself, identify the physical cause of that signal.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
