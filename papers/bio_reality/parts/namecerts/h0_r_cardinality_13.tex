\subsection{Window-six codon tile boundary for the standard-code readback}
\label{sec:bior-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary row.}
The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the four-letter codon display with coordinates induced by the window-six encoding.  The row under test is the finite boundary set $R$ selected by the local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class distinctions.  In the verified row, $R$ has exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite audit records $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen entries returned by the expected-codon readback and by the punctured-cube check.  The punctured-cube cell structure has $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not being treated as a diffuse collection of labels: it is a local finite subcomplex of the codon cube with thirteen vertices, sixteen tile edges, and four tile faces.  This is the exact content of the claim $h0.R.cardinality.13$: the row cardinality is thirteen and the thirteen codons are precisely the displayed members of $R$.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The same row is accompanied by a finite spectral profile.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, and the corresponding cubic-polynomial residual is the same number within the stored tolerance.  Thus the computation sees the local tile as a numerically stable finite object rather than as a visual artifact of the codon display.  Against the shape null with $N=2000$ sampled tables, the exact observed shape appeared once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  High-overlap alternatives with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling audit used seed $42$, with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the reported tail frequencies are finite-row counts rather than asymptotic approximations.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving null.}
The amino-acid-class preserving null gives a separate pressure test on whether the same shape is forced merely by preserving coarse label classes.  In $N=2000$ class-preserving samples, the exact shape count was $0$, so the recorded exact-shape value is $p=0.0$ at this sample resolution.  The lower-tail count for rows no larger than the observed module size was $7$, with observed $M$-size $20$, giving lower-tail value $7/2000=0.0035$.  This does not prove a biological mechanism.  It says only that, under the specified finite randomization, the standard-code readback occupies a small tail of the local tile statistics.

The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned by the module classifier.  The six recorded modules are

\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\

$$
The classifier reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has predictable-or-boundary status in the stored classification.  The row therefore has a finite internal organization: it is not only a thirteen-element list, but a partitioned boundary object whose module accounting closes exactly on the thirteen displayed codons.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table: it assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact tests code-layer readback and local codon-tile label splitting.  It does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA must obey the BEDC geometry as a biological law.  The local tile boundary therefore remains a code-read statement: the curated labels meet a finite coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation package on $Q_6$, but no protein realization follows from the row.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a thirteen-codon boundary in the standard-code table is not a global law of biology unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies that claim.
