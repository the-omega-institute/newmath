\subsection{Window-six codon boundary row}
\label{sec:biorreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite display.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table after each codon is represented as a six-coordinate word in the codon cube $Q_6$. The row isolates a finite boundary class $R$ inside this display. The verified cardinality statement is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The thirteen codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a codon-label statement, not a mechanistic statement. The observational contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. It supplies code-layer labels for readback, and it does not supply ribosomal dynamics, folded protein structure, thermodynamic admissibility, organismal function, or a universal evolutionary principle.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same row records that $R$ is the punctured-cube boundary class detected by the finite coordinate construction. Its face-count summary is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2) &= (13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of codons; it is a local tile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the six-bit codon display. The statement is deliberately local. A tile in $Q_6$ may explain why these labels form a stable boundary packet in this coordinate system, but that geometric fact alone does not say that biological translation is caused by the coordinate construction or that proteins with these labels realize any particular fold.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
The main shape-comparison run used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. In that run there was exactly one exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
while $25$ samples reached Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid classes also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It recorded observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the displayed shape is not a routine output of the tested randomizations. They do not convert the tile into a biological law. They only say that this particular standard-code label table has a strong finite shape signal under the sampled comparisons.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The chapter claim is that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table. The contact is the curated codon-to-label table, and the evidence consists of the thirteen-codon $R$ row, its punctured-cube face vector, the numerical spectrum, the two null comparisons, the leave-one-code and leave-one-codon recovery rows, and the complete module assignment. No stronger biological conclusion follows here. In particular, the record does not establish translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Any such assertion would require a separate observational contact at the appropriate biological layer.
