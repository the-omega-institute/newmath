\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record works in the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, where each codon is read as a finite coordinate string and the only biological readback contact is the curated standard-code label table.  The distinguished residue row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|&=13,\\
R_{\mathrm{cardinality}}&=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite row is not a symbolic name for an unspecified class: it is exactly the thirteen listed codons.  The associated readback layer is code-level only.  The contact says that a curated standard genetic-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label; it does not supply a translation mechanism, a folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or universality.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same row was verified as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)&=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Here the first entry records the thirteen codon vertices in the row, while the higher entries record the finite local incidence data seen by the codon-window construction.  This supports the claim that the window-six display exposes a local tile boundary.  It does not support the stronger statement that the biological code is forced by this geometry.  The local tile is a finite coordinate pattern whose labels are supplied by the external code table.

\paragraph{Shape significance against null rows.}
The shape was tested against $N=2000$ sampled null rows with seed $42$.  The sampling required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among these null rows there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null test used $N=2000$ rows and found $0$ exact matches, with lower-tail count $7$ at the observed $M$-size threshold.  In that run
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M|_{\mathrm{observed}}&=20,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The data therefore support that the finite local shape is unusual under the stated null models.  They do not identify a causal biological mechanism.

The module-classification audit recorded $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness.  This closes the finite bookkeeping for the row: the local boundary description covers the whole listed residue and leaves no codon outside the module partition.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The admissible conclusion is deliberately narrow.  Window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary-tile structure for the thirteen-codon row $R$, and the curated standard-code table supplies the code-label readback needed to test that local split.  No translation realization follows from these data.  No folded protein structure follows from amino-acid label readback.  No physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
