\subsection{Window-Six Boundary Rows in the Standard Code}
\label{sec:h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube obtained from the usual three nucleotide positions after expanding each base into a two-bit display.  Labels are then read as curated amino-acid or stop symbols.  The computation isolates a distinguished row set $R$ of cardinality $13$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified row count is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is not an asymptotic claim and not a claim about molecular dynamics.  It is a finite table assertion: under the fixed window-six coordinate display and the curated standard-code labeling, exactly these thirteen codons occupy the reported boundary row set.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same run identifies the row set with a punctured cubical tile.  Its recorded face-vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the tile has thirteen displayed vertices, sixteen displayed edges, and four displayed square faces in the local cubical complex used by the computation.  The important point is the coincidence between the explicit codon list and this local face count: the row set is not merely a bag of codons but a small cubical boundary pattern inside the six-bit coordinate cube.  The result remains local; the puncture description does not assert that the full genetic code is generated by this geometry.

\paragraph{Spectral readout.}
A separate finite spectral calculation supplies the scalar profile of the same local construction.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual was
$$
\begin{aligned}
-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12},
\end{aligned}
$$
which is numerically at tolerance for the stated finite computation.  Thus the row packet has both a combinatorial signature, given by $(13,16,4)$, and a numerical signature, given by the displayed spectral data.  These numbers should be read as diagnostics of the finite codon-table construction, not as independent biological mechanisms.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled comparison tables with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those samples, the exact local shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The count with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null model with the same sample count reported observed $M$-size $20$, zero exact matches, and seven samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$-size.  Its reported values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite null comparisons make the local pattern nontrivial relative to the sampled alternatives.  They do not convert the pattern into a necessity theorem for biology.

\paragraph{Predictive stress tests.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the reported above-baseline rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast.  The leave-one-codon-out test found anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These figures show that the finite tile is not only retrospectively describable; it also survives two small deletion-style probes at the code-label level.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen codons are completely assigned to six reported modules.  The module rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification report gives $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  Hence the module decomposition exactly partitions the row set.  This exact accounting is the strongest finite part of the record: each displayed codon in $R$ has a named local module position, and no additional codon is smuggled into the decomposition.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The only empirical contact used here is a curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code readback and local label splitting.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or an organism-independent law.  In particular, an amino-acid label sequence is still only a label sequence at this level; it is not a protein fold, not a physical admissibility certificate, and not a functional assay.  Any such stronger biological statement would require a separate contact of the appropriate kind.  The present chapter therefore records a finite local boundary pattern in the standard-code table, together with its row count, cubical face data, spectral values, null comparisons, deletion probes, and exact module partition.
