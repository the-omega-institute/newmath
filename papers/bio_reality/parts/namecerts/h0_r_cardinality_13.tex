\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, where a codon is displayed as a six-bit coordinate presentation of its three nucleotide positions.  The observation singled out here is a local tile $R$ whose members are codons at which the standard-code label table meets a boundary pattern: singleton-label corners, three-point label classes, and local face structure all occur inside the same finite coordinate window.  The verified row for the present claim gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}

This is the whole assertion of claim $h0.R.cardinality.13$: the row certifies the membership list and the cardinality, not a biochemical mechanism.  A companion finite check identifies the same list as a punctured cube pattern with face-vector data

\begin{aligned}

Thus the claim is a small combinatorial statement about a named subset of codon coordinates together with its code-label readback.

\paragraph{Code-label contact.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments for the standard code and has resolution at the level of codon label readback.
