\subsection{A Thirteen-Codon Boundary Row in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-thirteen}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code codon-label table. The relevant row is not the whole cube and is not a biological mechanism. It is the finite subset $R$ selected by the window-six local tile computation. The verified row has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the following set of codons: $AAA$, $AGA$, $AGG$, $AUA$, $CUA$, $CUC$, $CUG$, $CUU$, $UAA$, $UAG$, $UCA$, $UGA$, and $UUA$. Thus the row-size assertion is the concrete equality
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run records the value under experiment identifier $5d4a3459997d$, with both the scalar field $R\_cardinality\_equals\_13=13$ and the independent field $size=13$. The row is therefore not inferred from a visual diagram or from a later biological interpretation; it is a finite enumeration inside $Q_6$ with a direct count.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The geometric readback of $R$ is a punctured local cube. The verified face data give the $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2) &= (13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the same thirteen codons occur as $13$ vertices, with $16$ local edges and $4$ local square faces in the induced tile. This is the chapter-level content of the boundary row: it is a small local codon-tile shape inside the six-coordinate display. The word local is essential. The finite shape does not say that DNA must obey this geometry as a law of biology, and it does not provide translation dynamics, folding dynamics, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

In chapter terms this means that the numerical spectral summary is internally consistent to near-zero residual at the stated precision:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\

The spectral row is a derived probe of the same finite configuration. It strengthens the finite-row description, but it does not turn the row into a physical model of translation or protein realization.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the recorded null sample with seed $42$, the shape test used $N=2000$ sampled tables. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and therefore recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact shape matched once, giving $p_{exact}=0.0005$. The high-overlap test counted $25$ matches at Jaccard threshold at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The finite-row claim is therefore not only that a thirteen-codon row exists, but that the observed local shape is rare under the supplied coordinate-level null:
$$
\begin{aligned}
N &= 2000,\\
\#\{\mathrm{exact\ shape\ matches}\} &= 1,\\
p_{exact} &= 0.0005,\\
\#\{J\geq 0.9\} &= 25,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$. Its exact-shape value is $0.0$ in the finite sample, while the lower-tail value is $0.0035$. These numbers support the claim that the observed tile is not a generic consequence of retaining only coarse amino-acid-class information.

\paragraph{Readback and prediction row.}
The contact used here is the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. This is a code-read contact and nothing higher. In the leave-one-code-out probe, $23$ tables were evaluated with $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$. In the leave-one-codon-out probe, the recorded recovery rate was $1.0$, with $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and $8$ cases at high closure coverage. These are label-recovery facts at the code layer, not demonstrations of ribosomal translation, molecular admissibility, or protein function.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned to six named modules. The recorded module classification has $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are $agr=\{AGA,AGG\}$, $ar\_lys=\{AAA\}$, $cun=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, $ile\_met=\{AUA\}$, $ssn=\{UCA,UUA\}$, and $stop\_trp=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$. This classification accounts for every row element once. The family-level leave-one-code-out rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate, across $5$ families. The module result is therefore compatible with the row being a boundary-sensitive codon-label pattern, but it remains a finite code-read pattern.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported statement is narrow. Window-six coordinates expose a local boundary tile whose row $R$ has $13$ explicitly listed codons, punctured-cube face data $(13,16,4)$, stable spectral values, rare-shape null statistics, and strong label-recovery behavior under the supplied probes. The curated standard-code table supplies the reality contact for codon-label readback. It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law of biology. Any claim crossing from this code-read row to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate contact that is not part of this finite-row record.
