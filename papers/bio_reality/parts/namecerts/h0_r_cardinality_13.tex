\paragraph{Observed finite row.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer contact: a curated table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to stop. The carrier used for the row is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, where a codon is read as a six-bit display after choosing the usual two-bit presentation of each RNA base. The verified boundary set $R$ has exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\texttt{AAA},\texttt{AGA},\texttt{AGG},\texttt{AUA},\texttt{CUA},\texttt{CUC},\texttt{CUG},\texttt{CUU},\\
&\texttt{UAA},\texttt{UAG},\texttt{UCA},\texttt{UGA},\texttt{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite count is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| = 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row was checked against the punctured-cube description, with the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a cardinality statement: it is a small local tile in the codon cube, with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces after the puncture has been taken into account.

\paragraph{Label readback.}
The biological object contacted here is only the standard-code label table. The thirteen codons in $R$ are the codons whose local position is selected by the window-six boundary construction and then read back through the curated code labels. This includes the codons \texttt{AAA}, \texttt{AUA}, the two AGN codons \texttt{AGA} and \texttt{AGG}, the four CUN codons \texttt{CUA}, \texttt{CUC}, \texttt{CUG}, and \texttt{CUU}, the three stop-sector codons \texttt{UAA}, \texttt{UAG}, and \texttt{UGA}, and the two SSN-side codons \texttt{UCA} and \texttt{UUA}. The row is therefore a code-read statement about a finite display and its curated labels, not a claim about translation dynamics or molecular realization.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The supported claim is deliberately narrow. Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in the standard-code label table, and the selected row has cardinality $13$. The reality contact is the curated codon-to-label table, so the contact can test code readback and local label splits. It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, or universal mechanism. Amino-acid label sequences are still labels at this level; they are not folded structures. A stronger biological claim would require a separate observation that directly bears on the higher layer being asserted.
