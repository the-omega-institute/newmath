\subsection{A thirteen-codon boundary row in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The object tested here is the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer.  The coordinate carrier is the six-bit codon cube, with each codon treated as a finite word over the four displayed nucleotide symbols and then read through the curated standard-code label table.  The verified row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and consists exactly of
\texttt{AAA}, \texttt{AGA}, \texttt{AGG}, \texttt{AUA}, \texttt{CUA}, \texttt{CUC}, \texttt{CUG}, \texttt{CUU}, \texttt{UAA}, \texttt{UAG}, \texttt{UCA}, \texttt{UGA}, and \texttt{UUA}.  Thus the finite certificate records the equality $|R|=13$ together with the complete codon roster; no unspecified member is left implicit.

\paragraph{Local geometry.}
The same thirteen codons were also verified as the punctured-cube row detected by the local window-six construction.  Its face data have the finite vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2) &= (13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This means that the observed row is not being used merely as an unordered label list.  It carries a local tile shape: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces inside the codon-coordinate display.  The test therefore supports a bounded geometric statement about a local codon tile, not a claim about the whole biological process of translation.

\paragraph{Spectral check.}
The numerical spectrum gives a second finite check on the same row.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the reported spectral identity is satisfied to the stored numerical tolerance.  This does not turn the codon table into a dynamical law; it says that the finite coordinate extraction, after the row has been chosen, has a stable numerical profile that can be recomputed from the same row data.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the finite null sample with seed $42$, $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables were evaluated after $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sampled table matched the observed shape, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The relaxed comparison with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ had $25$ matches, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, had observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape value $0.0$, and lower-tail value $0.0035$.  These finite counts make the row difficult to dismiss as a generic consequence of label multiplicities alone, while still leaving the interpretation confined to the code-table readout.

\paragraph{Predictive readback.}
The leave-one-code-out check evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The family split is also finite: five families were recorded.  The above-baseline family rates were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.  The leave-one-codon-out check had anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers support the narrower statement that the local row is useful for label recovery tests inside this curated code-read setting.

\paragraph{Module decomposition.}
The thirteen codons are exhausted by six named modules.  The \texttt{agr} module contains \texttt{AGA} and \texttt{AGG}; \texttt{ar\_lys} contains \texttt{AAA}; \texttt{cun} contains \texttt{CUA}, \texttt{CUC}, \texttt{CUG}, and \texttt{CUU}; \texttt{ile\_met} contains \texttt{AUA}; \texttt{ssn} contains \texttt{UCA} and \texttt{UUA}; and \texttt{stop\_trp} contains \texttt{UAA}, \texttt{UAG}, and \texttt{UGA}.  The classification record reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing members, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has either a predictable member or a boundary member, so the local row has a complete finite module account rather than a loose biological gloss.

\paragraph{Reality contact and scope.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for checking the local split above.  It is not an observation of ribosomal realization, folded molecular structure, chemical admissibility, biological activity, a generative mechanism, or universality across life.  Consequently the conclusion is deliberately limited: the window-six coordinates expose a reproducible thirteen-codon local boundary tile in the standard-code label table, with significant finite null comparisons and strong label-recovery behavior.  Any statement about translation, folding, physical realization, function, mechanism, or universal biological necessity would require a separate contact designed to observe that higher layer.
