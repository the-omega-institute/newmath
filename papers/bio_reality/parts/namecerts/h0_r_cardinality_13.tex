\paragraph{Finite row.}
The claim concerns the standard-code codon table read only as a code-level labelling contact.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, with codons displayed as finite words over the four RNA bases and compared through the window-six coordinate presentation.  The distinguished residue set $R$ has cardinality thirteen.  In the verified row it is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not a vague enrichment of the genetic code table: it is a named finite subset of the $64$ codon positions, with size $|R|=13$ and with every member explicitly listed.  The readout contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose role is only to assign codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact permits code-label readback and local split checks, but it does not by itself observe ribosomal translation, folded structure, chemical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Local shape.}
The same finite subset is recorded as a punctured-cube tile.  Its face data are
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the thirteen codon vertices carry sixteen local one-dimensional adjacencies and four two-dimensional faces in the row geometry.  This is the precise content of the boundary observation: the window-six display exposes a local tile shape inside $Q_6$, while the biological labels remain supplied by the curated table.  The shape should therefore be read as a code-layer organization of a finite codon set, not as a law of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
The unrestricted finite comparison used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those samples, there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding tail frequencies were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second comparison preserved amino-acid class.  In that constrained run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed comparison size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  The recorded frequencies were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the thirteen-codon row is a nontrivial local code-table pattern under the sampled finite models.  They do not convert the pattern into a statement about protein folding, thermodynamics, organismal fitness, or a universal generative mechanism.

\paragraph{Scope.}
The verdict for this row is that the thirteen-codon set is a verified finite code-read pattern in the window-six codon cube.  Its support consists of an explicit $13$-element residue set, punctured-cube face counts $(13,16,4)$, spectral values with a near-zero cubic residual, finite null-model frequencies, leave-out recovery statistics, and an exact module partition.  The boundary is equally explicit.  Nothing in this row observes translation as a physical process, an amino-acid sequence as a folded protein, a fold as chemically admissible, a molecule as functional, or the local tile as a universal biological rule.  Any such promotion would require an additional contact of the appropriate kind; the curated codon table alone is not that contact.
