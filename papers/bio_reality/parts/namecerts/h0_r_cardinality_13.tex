\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, understood as a codon-label bridge.  It supports the observation that standard-code codons have amino-acid or stop labels, and it can test code-read statements and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, mechanism, or universality.  Therefore the admissible conclusion is narrow: window-six coordinates expose a local thirteen-codon boundary tile whose labels have strong finite-row readback against the listed nulls and holdout checks.  No claim follows here that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity, that the tile is a global biological law, that amino-acid label sequences determine folded protein structure, or that the pattern supplies translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality without a separate higher-layer contact.
