\subsection{Window-Six Local Tile Boundary for the Standard Code}
\label{subsec:codon-window-six-local-tile-boundary}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The BioReality record for claim $h0.R.cardinality.13$ treats the standard-code codon table as an external label contact placed over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$. The finite row singled out by the coordinate-and-closure probe is the thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row audit reports $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen entries returned by the expected-codon readback. This is a code-layer datum: each listed token is a codon position whose biological label is read from the curated standard-code table. The table contact supplies amino-acid-or-stop labels, but it does not by itself supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Local tile shape.}
The same finite row is also registered as a punctured-cube boundary tile. The reported face-vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the record sees thirteen vertices, sixteen local one-dimensional adjacencies, and four local square faces in the induced window-six geometry. The important point is not that the codon table is thereby governed by cube geometry as a biological law. The claim is narrower: after the external code labels have been supplied, the displayed row has a reproducible local tile signature inside $Q_6$. The tile is therefore a finite coordinate certificate for a label split, not a mechanism for why cells translate codons as they do.

\paragraph{Spectral certificate.}
The spectral row supplies the scalars $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$. The associated cubic or radial polynomial residual is reported as $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, which is numerically at tolerance for the stated finite computation. Thus the local row is not only a cardinality observation: it has a stable finite spectral signature. This spectral information stays inside the code-read layer. It can support comparison of codon-label rows and derived probes, but it cannot promote the result to protein folding, physical viability, or biological function.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Against the general finite-shape null model, the run used $N=2000$ samples with $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact shape was matched once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while the high-overlap condition $J\geq 0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. Against the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$, the exact shape count was $0$, the observed $M$-size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$-size was $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$. These finite-row statistics support the claim that the observed local tile is not a routine accident of the sampled nulls. They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Readback tests.}
The leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast. The leave-one-codon-out audit reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These numbers make the row useful as a local readback device for code labels under finite perturbations.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification exhausts the thirteen-codon row without extras or omissions: $R$-size is $13$, assigned count is $13$, extra module codons are empty, missing-from-modules is empty, and unassigned codons is empty. The modules are $\{AGA,AGG\}$ for $agr$, $\{AAA\}$ for $ar\_lys$, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for $cun$, $\{AUA\}$ for $ile\_met$, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ for $ssn$, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for $stop\_trp$. Each module has either predictable or boundary status in the recorded classification. This gives a complete finite partition of the local row and fixes the interpretation of the thirteen entries at the code-read level.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The local conclusion is therefore ready: window-six coordinates expose a thirteen-codon punctured-cube tile in the standard-code label table, with finite cardinality, face-vector, spectral, null-model, leave-one-out, and module-classification support. The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result. No translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this row unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence. Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains a code-layer observation, not a structure or function observation.
