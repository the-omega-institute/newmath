\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The object in this row is the standard-code codon table read through a window-six codon display.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, and the only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label.  This contact supports code-layer readback and local label-split tests.  It does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Those higher-layer claims remain outside the row unless a separate contact supplies them.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The row establishes a code-read statement: the curated standard-code labels, when placed on the six-bit codon cube, expose a thirteen-codon local boundary tile with the listed punctured-cube, spectral, null-model, predictive, and module-classification data.  The row does not assert that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not assert translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only label readback.  Crossing from this code-layer tile to translation, folding, structure, or function requires an additional reality contact that is absent from this finite row.
