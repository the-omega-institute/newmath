\paragraph{Finite object and scope.}
This record concerns a finite window-six readback of the standard-code codon table. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-bit codon display, and the observed layer is only the code-read layer: codons are assigned curated amino-acid or stop labels by an external standard-code contact. The finite row isolates a local tile boundary set $R$ with cardinality $13$. The verified row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
and hence $|R|=13$. This is the whole claimed object at this row: a finite local boundary pattern in codon-coordinate space, together with the curated label table used to read it back.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same row verifies that $R$ is the punctured-cube boundary set for this coordinate presentation. Its face data are recorded by the finite vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object has $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional adjacencies, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the verified tile representation. These numbers are not a biological mechanism. They are a finite combinatorial shape extracted after fixing the codon coordinates and the curated code-label readback.

The associated spectral row separates the local boundary set from the surrounding codon-coordinate mass. The verified values are

It does not say that the codon table is forced by geometry alone.

\paragraph{Null-model checks.}
The shape was compared against finite null rows. In the reported shape-significance run, $N=2000$ distinct samples were targeted, requiring $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. There was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null row, again with $N=2000$, the observed size was $|M|=20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$. The reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These rows make the local tile non-generic relative to the supplied null procedures, but they do not elevate the tile to a universal law of coding or translation.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code-out readback.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The finite boundary method gave a hit rate above baseline of $0.9841269841269841$, a hit rate inside $M-T$ of $0.9365079365079365$, and a hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary of $0.9841269841269841$. By family, the reported hit-rate-above-baseline values were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate, across $5$ families. This supports the narrower claim that the same local code-read boundary is informative for held-out code-label rows within the tested finite table family.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon-out and modules.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons in $R$: $agr=\{AGA,AGG\}$, $ar\_lys=\{AAA\}$, $cun=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$, $ile\_met=\{AUA\}$, $ssn=\{UCA,UUA\}$, and $stop\_trp=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$. The classification row records $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons. This closes the finite bookkeeping for the boundary set itself.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code label table. It supplies codon-to-label assignments at codon-label resolution. It does not supply translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Therefore the admissible conclusion is local and finite: window-six codon coordinates expose a non-generic boundary-tile structure in the curated standard-code readback. No claim follows about protein folding, structural order, physical feasibility, biological function, a universal coding mechanism, or the necessity of the observed table unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
