The row is therefore not a qualitative impression about the code table: it is a fixed finite subset with thirteen named codons. The only supported reading is code-layer label readback and local boundary-tile comparison inside the six-bit codon display.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
The shape was compared against randomized alternatives.  In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ samples, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries were recorded.  The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the count with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A label-class-preserving null run with $N=2000$ produced no exact shape matches.  It also recorded $7$ samples in the lower tail at or below the observed ambient size $|M|=20$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0,\qquad p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the claim that the observed local shape is not a common outcome under the tested finite randomizations.  They do not identify a mechanism; they only constrain how surprising the displayed row is under the stated sampling designs.
