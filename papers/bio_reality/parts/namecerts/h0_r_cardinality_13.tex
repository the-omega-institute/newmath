\subsection{A thirteen-codon boundary row in the window-six codon cube}\label{sec:h0-r-cardinality-13}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.} The record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a window-six coordinate display. The ambient carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, where each codon is treated as a six-bit coordinate word over the four RNA letters. The object isolated by the finite run is a row $R$ of codons that sits on a local boundary tile of this display. The verified row is
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The cardinality run reports $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen listed codons and experiment identifier $5d4a3459997d$. Thus the row is not a qualitative drawing of a codon pattern; it is a finite named subset with thirteen enumerated members.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.} The companion shape computation identifies the same row with a punctured-cube boundary pattern. The reported face vector is
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}$$
so the local tile has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces in the finite cubical readout used by the probe. The important point is locality: this is a codon-coordinate shape inside the window-six representation, not a law about all molecular processes. The tile records where the standard-code labels meet the selected coordinate distinctions; it does not by itself realize translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Spectral check.} The same packet carries a numerical spectral signature. The finite computation gives
$$\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}$$
The reported radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is recorded as the cubic-polynomial residual. This is small enough to function as a numerical consistency check for the computed spectrum of the finite row relation. It should be read as an internal finite-row diagnostic: it supports the coherence of the coordinate extraction, while leaving biological interpretation to the curated codon-label contact.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.} Two finite null runs quantify how special the observed shape is under the stated sampling regimes. In the shape-significance run with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. It found $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null run, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-match count was $0$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below that observed size was $7$. The corresponding reported values were $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$. These numbers support the claim that the observed local row geometry is not reproduced frequently by the tested finite null models. They do not license any claim about protein structure or molecular fitness.

\paragraph{Readback contact.} The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table. That contact assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels and therefore can test code-layer readback and local label splitting. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded form, biological function, or a universal biological mechanism. The observed fact is a table-level assignment of codons to labels; the table does not contain folding observations, kinetic measurements, organism-level selection data, or physical admissibility assays.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.} The leave-one-code-out run evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$. The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The leave-one-codon-out run reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These finite rows say that the local coordinate-and-boundary pattern is strongly recoverable under the specified held-out tests. They still remain code-layer tests: they recover codon-label placement in the finite representation, not biochemical execution.

\paragraph{Scope.} The scientific content of this chapter is therefore narrow and checkable. The window-six codon coordinate system isolates a thirteen-codon row, the row has a punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, its spectral quantities satisfy the stated numerical residual check, and several finite null and holdout probes find the row nontrivial under their declared sampling regimes. The curated genetic-code table supplies the code labels needed for readback. No stronger biological conclusion follows from these data alone. Any claim about translation, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality requires an additional contact designed to observe that higher layer directly.
