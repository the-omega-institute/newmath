\subsection{Window-Six Codon Boundary Tile}
\label{subsec:biorality-window-six-codon-boundary-tile}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read through the curated standard-code label table.  The contact used here is only a code-layer contact: it records that each codon has an amino-acid or stop label in the standard-code table.  It does not record translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or universality.  The BioReality claim is therefore a local finite-row claim about a boundary tile in codon-label readback, not a global biological law.

\paragraph{The observed boundary row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  In the observed finite row, the codons are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the checked cardinality statement is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also reported as a punctured-cube face pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the concrete finite-row content of the claim: the boundary row contains thirteen named codons, arranged as a local punctured-cube tile with sixteen one-dimensional incidences and four two-dimensional faces.

\paragraph{Null-model pressure.}
The shape test against a finite null sample used $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, with seed $42$.  The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and the high-overlap event $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$ and found no exact shape match.  It recorded $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$-size, with observed $|M|=20$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0,
\qquad
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite-row values make the tile non-generic relative to the stated null models.  They do not identify a mechanism; they only say that the local label geometry is not behaving like the tested shuffled baselines.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out audit evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out audit reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate
$$
\begin{aligned}
1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
These tests support the claim that the local boundary tile has predictive code-layer organization under the supplied finite probes.  They still remain inside codon-label readback.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion is deliberately narrow.  Window-six coordinates expose a local codon-label boundary tile with $|R|=13$, punctured-cube face data $(13,16,4)$, stable spectral scalars, non-generic null-model behavior, strong leave-one recovery, and a complete six-module classification.  The curated standard-code table supplies the label contact needed to read these rows.  Nothing in this contact supplies translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law saying that DNA must obey this geometry.  Any such promotion would require a separate higher-layer reality contact.
