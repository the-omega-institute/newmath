\subsection{The Thirteen-Codon Boundary Row in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The record concerns the standard-code codon table read only as a code-layer reality contact.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, with codons read as externally curated labels rather than as a mechanism for translation or protein realization.  The observed boundary row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified row cardinality is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the local finite fact named by the row: the boundary object has thirteen codons, not an unspecified or asymptotic size.  The same finite list is reported by the punctured-cube check, which gives the face-count vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the row has thirteen displayed codon vertices, sixteen local edge contacts, and four local tile faces inside the chosen window-six geometry.

\paragraph{Local tile interpretation.}
The geometry separates a singleton-corner and a three-point label-class behavior inside the codon display, but the interpretation remains local.  In particular, $R$ is not asserted to be a biological law; it is a finite tile boundary seen after imposing the chosen coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation probes on the curated label table.  The finite modules assigned to the thirteen codons are complete and have no extras: $\{AGA,AGG\}$ for the $agr$ module, $\{AAA\}$ for $ar\_lys$, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for $cun$, $\{AUA\}$ for $ile\_met$, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ for $ssn$, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for $stop\_trp$.  The module audit reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  This matters because the row is not only a cardinality count; it is also a completely partitioned finite boundary pattern.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The spectrum check gives the measured values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same reported tolerance residual for the cubic polynomial.  The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ null samples with seed $42$.  It found one exact shape match, twenty-five samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The corresponding reported probabilities are $p_{exact}=0.0005$ and $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  These numbers support the statement that the observed local shape is unusual under that finite null run; they do not promote the tile into a universal mechanism.

The exact-match count is $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, the reported exact-shape probability is $0.0$, and the lower-tail probability is $0.0035$. The data supplied here do not include a codon-by-codon list for $M$, so the chapter uses the reported $M$ size and null probabilities without inventing an $M$ list.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The reality contact is the curated standard-code table: it assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, or universality.  Therefore the finite conclusion is deliberately narrow: in the window-six codon coordinate system, the standard-code label table exposes a thirteen-codon local boundary row $R$ with punctured-cube vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$ and $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and strong finite null separation in the supplied runs.  No claim follows here that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity, that amino-acid label readback is folded protein structure, or that the local tile is a universal mechanism.  Any such higher-layer assertion would require a separate reality contact beyond the curated code table.
