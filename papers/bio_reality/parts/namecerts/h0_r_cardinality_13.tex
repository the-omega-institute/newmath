\paragraph{Finite carrier.}

$$\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}$$
The row therefore records the exact cardinality statement
$$\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}$$
This is not a claim that the local tile is a global biological law. It is a finite code-layer observation: the codon-coordinate window and the curated label table agree on a distinguished thirteen-codon boundary row.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}

This geometric readback remains local. It says that the displayed code row has a particular finite shape inside $Q_6$; it does not say that translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from that shape.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge: a table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact can test code-read behavior and local codon-tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, a universal mechanism, or a global biological law. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore kept at the code layer. A higher-layer claim would require a separate reality contact, such as a translation experiment, a structure experiment, a physical-admissibility assay, or a function-level observation. Without such a contact, the correct verdict is local alignment at the codon-label layer and explicit blockage of translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, and universality claims.
