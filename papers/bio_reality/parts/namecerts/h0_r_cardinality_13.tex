\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
This record concerns a finite six-bit codon display, read through the standard-code label table.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, where each codon is treated as a coordinate row and the only external biological contact used here is the curated codon-to-label table.  The observed boundary set $R$ has exactly thirteen rows:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check is therefore not an asymptotic or interpretive claim but the finite equality
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| = 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same rows are also reported by the punctured-cube check, whose face data give the $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the claim is a local tile statement: thirteen labelled codon rows support sixteen edge incidences and four two-dimensional faces inside the displayed coordinate window.  The readback is the standard-code label table only; the finite geometry is not itself a translation mechanism.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA obeys the displayed geometry as a biological necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Therefore the correct conclusion is narrow: the window-six coordinates expose a thirteen-row local boundary tile with significant finite-row structure and strong code-layer recovery statistics, while curated labels remain external reality contacts.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
