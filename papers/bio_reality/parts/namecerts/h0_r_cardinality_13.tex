\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed boundary row.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a codon-coordinate display.  In this row the external contact is the curated standard-code label table, so the observable readback is a codon-to-label assignment and not a biochemical mechanism.  The verified boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite-row claim is not merely that a small set exists: the certified row names all thirteen codons and fixes the row size as
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is also certified as a punctured-cube object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This gives the local tile its mathematical shape: thirteen visible vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces inside the window-six coordinate display.

\paragraph{Shape significance under finite null rows.}
Against the sampled shape null, the row was tested with $N=2000$ trials and seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, so the experiment is a finite-row comparison with substantial rejection of repeated samples.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives a separate constraint.  In $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, the exact match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local codon tile is not treated as a free visual coincidence: it survives both the shape comparison and the amino-acid-class-preserving comparison at the finite-row level supplied here.

For the leave-one-codon-out row, the recovery rate is $1.0$ and the above-threshold recovery rate is also $1.0$. \begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\

$$
The classification row reports $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  It also records that every module has a predictable-or-boundary witness.  This is the local reason the row can be discussed as a boundary-tile structure rather than as an arbitrary thirteen-element list.

\paragraph{Reality contact and cannot-claim boundary.}
The reality contact is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code readback and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function realization, universal mechanism, or biological necessity.  Therefore the finite-row conclusion is deliberately narrow: window-six coordinates expose a thirteen-codon local boundary tile with the listed spectral, null-model, leave-one-out, and module-classification signatures, while the curated table supplies only the external code labels.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from this row unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that information.
