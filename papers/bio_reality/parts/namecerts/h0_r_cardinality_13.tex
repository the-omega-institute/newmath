\paragraph{Finite object and observed row.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only as a finite code-layer table.  The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube, with each codon represented by its three nucleotide positions and the induced six-bit display.  The observed boundary row $R$ has exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cardinality check is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is not a statement about translation chemistry or protein realization.  It is a finite statement about which codon labels occupy a local boundary row after the curated standard-code table is read at the code layer.

\paragraph{Null comparison.}
Against $2000$ sampled comparison tables, the exact shape was observed once.  The exact-shape probability estimate was therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run found $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling process required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, with seed $42$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving comparison, $2000$ samples produced no exact shape matches, while $7$ samples lay in the lower tail at or below the observed module size $20$.  The corresponding reported values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These statistics support the claim that the local row is not a typical artifact of the tested finite null models.  They remain conditional on the finite sampling design and on the curated code-label contact.

\paragraph{Scope of the contact.}
The reality contact is the curated standard genetic-code table.  It supplies codon-to-label assignments for amino-acid and stop labels.  It does not supply an observation of ribosomal realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, organism-level function, or a universal biological law.  Consequently, the local tile may be used to discuss code-layer label splits and finite module recovery, but it may not be promoted to translation, folding, physical chemistry, function, mechanism, or universality unless another contact is explicitly supplied for that higher layer.

The finite row $R$ is a thirteen-codon boundary row in the six-coordinate codon cube, with punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, a consistent spectral check, significant finite-null behavior in the reported samples, strong leave-one-code and leave-one-codon recovery, and a complete six-module classification.
