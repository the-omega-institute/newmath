\subsection{Window-six codon boundary record}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-reading layer.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with one coordinate display for the three nucleotide positions and the binary presentation induced by the four-letter alphabet.  The observed contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The contact therefore supports label readback and local codon-tile comparison, but it is not a translation experiment and it is not a structural or functional assay.

The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The finite run reports exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run records $|R|=13$ and the named scalar $R\_cardinality\_equals\_13=13$.  The punctured-cube check returns the same list and gives the face-count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object is not merely a count: it carries a small two-dimensional tile pattern inside the codon cube.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling evidence.}
The spectral summary separates the measured boundary set from its complementary local mass.  The reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic check is numerically closed at the displayed tolerance, with residual $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, reported both as the cubic-polynomial value and as the radial-polynomial value.  Thus the finite calculation supplies a stable numerical signature for the local split, while still remaining a code-table signature rather than a biological mechanism.

A separate shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled tables, seed $42$, and $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, of which $55377$ were duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found observed $M$ size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the local tile is a nontrivial finite pattern relative to the tested null models.  They do not identify a causal path from nucleotides to proteins.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification exhausts the thirteen codons in $R$: it reports $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every listed module has a predictable or boundary interpretation in the finite run.  This is the strongest local reading of the record: the thirteen-codon set is organized by named modules, the modules cover the set exactly, and the sampling and spectral checks agree that the arrangement is not a generic random-table accident under the tested procedures.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is deliberately narrow.  Window-six coordinates expose a local codon-tile boundary in the curated standard-code table.  The table supplies codon-to-label observations, and the finite calculations record a thirteen-codon punctured-cube pattern, spectral values, null-model rarity, and recovery behavior.  Nothing in this record establishes translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a biochemical mechanism, or universality across life.  Any such stronger claim would require a separate contact measuring the relevant higher layer.  In particular, amino-acid label readback is only a code-layer observation; it is not evidence of protein folding, structural order, or functional activity.
