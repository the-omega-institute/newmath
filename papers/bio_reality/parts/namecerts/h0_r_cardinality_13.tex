\subsection{Window-six boundary tile for the standard codon table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The record concerns a six-coordinate codon display of the standard genetic-code table.  The only empirical contact used here is the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The observed object is therefore a code-layer table, not a translation process and not a physical model of a molecule.  Within this finite display, the distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the complete listed row for the claim $h0.R.cardinality.13$: the reported scalar $R\_cardinality\_equals\_13$ is $13$, and the independent size field is also $13$.  The readout is finite in the strongest sense relevant to this chapter: each named codon is one of the sixty-four standard codon words, and the contact only asks whether the curated label table recognizes the word and its label class.

\paragraph{Local cubical shape.}
The same row is reported as a punctured local cube with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f\_0,f\_1,f\_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of thirteen exceptional words; it carries a local face structure with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-faces in the window-six coordinate system.  The puncture is part of the claim: the finite face count says that the local tile is shaped, but it is not promoted to a global rule for biology.  A singleton-corner behavior and a three-point label-class behavior are visible at the code-read layer, yet their evidential meaning remains restricted to codon labels in the curated table.

\paragraph{Spectral readout.}
The numerical spectrum attached to the same finite object separates the main tile scale from the boundary scale.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported as the cubic-polynomial residual.  This is within the numerical tolerance used by the row computation and supports the claim that the window-six object has a stable finite spectral signature.  The conclusion is still geometric and tabular: it says that the local codon display has a reproducible row-and-boundary spectrum, not that codons physically realize a spectral mechanism during translation.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
The shape signal is not treated as self-certifying.  Against $2000$ sampled null instances with seed $42$, the exact shape was matched once, giving $p\_\mathrm{exact}=0.0005$.  The high-overlap test at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ produced $25$ matches, giving $p\_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampling required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries, so the finite search itself is visibly constrained by the small combinatorial universe being sampled.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed main set size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$.  The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p\_\mathrm{aa\ class,exact} &= 0.0,\\
p\_\mathrm{aa\ class,lower\ tail} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the narrower statement that the standard table has an uncommon local code-layer arrangement under the tested finite nulls.  They do not identify a biochemical cause.

\paragraph{Prediction-style checks.}
Two leave-out probes provide the strongest finite evidence that the row is not an after-the-fact decoration.  In the leave-one-code-out experiment, $23$ code tables were evaluated across $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  In the leave-one-codon-out experiment, the anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the high-coverage closure count was $8$, the predictable count was $8$, and the recovery rate was $1.0$.  The recovery threshold flag is also $1.0$.  These values make the local tile operational at the code-read level: held-out codon-label cases are often recovered by the finite geometry plus boundary rule, but this remains a statement about label recovery in tables.

\paragraph{Family and module stratification.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out table contains $5$ families.  The hit-rate-above-baseline values are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate codes.  The module classification accounts for all thirteen row codons: $R\_size=13$, $assigned\_count=13$, and the missing, extra, and unassigned lists are all empty.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA, UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA, UAG, UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has either predictable or boundary status in the recorded classification.  This is the chapter-specific reason to regard the thirteen-codon row as a coherent finite packet rather than a miscellaneous set of codons.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The admissible conclusion is local and code-level: the standard-code label table, when displayed in the window-six coordinate system, contains a thirteen-codon row with a punctured-cube face count, a stable numerical spectrum, significant finite-null behavior, and leave-out recovery evidence.  Nothing in this chapter observes ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, thermodynamic admissibility, biological function, a molecular mechanism, or universality across life.  Amino-acid label readback is not folded structure; a local codon tile is not a law of biology; and a coordinate pattern in a curated table is not, by itself, an explanation of why cells translate as they do.  Any such stronger claim would require a separate contact that directly measures the higher layer being asserted.
