\subsection{Window-Six Boundary Tile for a Thirteen-Codon Residue}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate record.}
The object recorded here is the standard-code codon table viewed only through a six-coordinate codon display.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon treated as a finite word in the alphabet $\{A,C,G,U\}$ after the usual two-bit expansion of each nucleotide position.  The row named $h0.R.cardinality.13$ isolates a distinguished residue set $R$ of size thirteen.  The verified member list is
$$\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}$$
The finite check reports $|R|=13$ and also records the same thirteen codons under the expected-codon comparison.  Thus the primary statement is not an asymptotic biological law: it is a finite equality between a named coordinate residue and an explicitly enumerated codon set.

\paragraph{Local tile shape.}
The punctured-cube row identifies the same set $R$ as a local tile with face-vector data
$$\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}$$
This says that the selected residue has thirteen displayed vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four square faces inside the window-six cube representation.  The geometry is therefore strong enough to define a local boundary object: there is a finite tile, it has a measured combinatorial shape, and its vertex set agrees with the thirteen-codon list above.  The singleton-corner and three-point-class descriptions are read as internal distinctions of this local tile, not as independent biological mechanisms.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Two null checks measure how unusual the local shape is under sampled alternatives.  In the unconstrained shape comparison, $N=2000$ samples were used with seed $42$.  The run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found one exact shape match, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,
\end{aligned}$$
and it found twenty-five samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It reports observed $M$-size $20$, zero exact matches, and seven lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$-size:
$$\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}$$
The supplied finite row gives the cardinality of $M$ and the null behavior of its shape, but not a member-by-member $M$ codon list.  The chapter therefore uses the available $M$ data only at the level of $|M|=20$ and the associated sampled probabilities.

\paragraph{Readback against the curated code table.}
The only external contact used here is the curated standard-code label table.  That contact assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label and has codon-label resolution.  It supports code-layer readback of the thirteen displayed codons and local split checks, but it does not contain observations of ribosomal realization, protein structure, physical admissibility, function, or universality.  Consequently, the biological statement is deliberately narrow: the six-coordinate display exposes a local codon-tile boundary whose labels can be read against the standard table.

These finite rows support a robust code-read pattern around the boundary, with one explicitly weaker family and four perfect family-level rates in the sampled comparison.

The summary row gives $R$-size $13$ and assigned count $13$, so the partition is complete for this finite residue.  Each module has either predictable or boundary status, which is the appropriate code-layer conclusion for this packet.

\paragraph{Local boundary of interpretation.}
The verified data justify a finite mathematical and code-read statement: a thirteen-codon subset of the window-six codon cube forms a punctured-cube-like local tile with measured face vector, numerical spectrum, sampled null separation, strong leave-one-code-out behavior, and complete module classification.  The data do not by themselves show translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universal necessity.  Any claim at one of those higher layers would require a separate contact with the relevant experimental resolution.  In the present record, amino-acid and stop labels are used only as curated table labels, and amino-acid label strings are not treated as protein structures.
