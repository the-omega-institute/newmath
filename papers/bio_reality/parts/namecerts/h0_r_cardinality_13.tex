\subsection{Window-six codon boundary row}
\label{sec:h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code label table.  The coordinate layer is not a biological mechanism: it is a finite display in which a codon is treated as a six-coordinate word, and the external contact is the curated table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  In this row the distinguished boundary set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Its observed cardinality is $|R|=13$.  This is the finite-row content of claim $\mathrm{h0.R.cardinality.13}$: the row does not merely assert that a boundary exists, but fixes the complete list of thirteen codons that realize it in the standard-code readback.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same thirteen codons also appear as the punctured-cube boundary row.  The recorded face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not only a cardinality statement.  It carries a local tile profile: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces inside the window-six display.  The singleton and three-point label phenomena are therefore treated as local boundary evidence inside the codon cube, not as a claim about translation dynamics or molecular realization.

\end{aligned}

$$
\begin{aligned}

These finite-row statistics support the claim that the observed local boundary is not a typical random class-preserving shape under these probes.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon behavior and module partition.}
The leave-one-codon row records $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate $1.0$.  The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned by the module classification:

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The chapter-specific claim is therefore narrow.  Window-six coordinates expose a local boundary tile in the standard-code label table, with $|R|=13$, punctured-cube face data $(13,16,4)$, a recorded spectral signature, low sampled null probabilities, high leave-one-code hit rates, and complete module coverage.  Nothing in this row says that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  Nothing here proves translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or universality of the code.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded structure.  Any claim crossing from code-read geometry to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact designed for that higher layer.
