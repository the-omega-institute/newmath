The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, and the readout is the curated standard-code assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. \end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite statement attached to claim $\mathrm{h0.R.cardinality.13}$ is not merely that a row exists, but that this exact thirteen-codon row is the row under discussion.  The codons include the four $\mathrm{CUN}$ leucine codons, three stop-related codons $\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}$, the two $\mathrm{AGR}$ codons, and four singleton or near-singleton boundary positions $\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}$.

\paragraph{Monte Carlo shape controls.}
Two finite null controls constrain how much weight should be put on the observed geometry.  In the shape-preserving sampling row with seed $42$, the run used $N=2000$ accepted samples but required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, with $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only $1$ of the $2000$ accepted samples matched the exact observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving control, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, had observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$ for samples with size at most the observed $M$-size.  This gives $p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0.0$ in the finite run and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These are sampling statements about the coded finite row, not biological laws.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary of interpretation.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, interpreted as a codon-label table.  It supplies the assignments needed to check code readback and local label splitting.  It does not supply ribosomal dynamics, tRNA availability, protein folding data, thermodynamic admissibility, organism-level function, or evidence that the geometry is a universal biological principle.  Therefore the scientific reading of this row is deliberately narrow: the window-six coordinate system exposes a finite local boundary tile in the standard-code label table, and the associated controls show that this tile is nontrivial under the reported finite null models.  Any claim about translation, folded protein structure, physical feasibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality requires a different contact that directly observes that higher layer.
