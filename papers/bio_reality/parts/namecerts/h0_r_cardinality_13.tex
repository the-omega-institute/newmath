\subsection{Window-Six Codon Boundary Tile}
\label{subsec:h0-r-cardinality-13}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite readback problem over the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$.  The carrier is not a biological mechanism; it is a coordinate display in which codons can be inspected as finite rows with local tile faces, singleton label corners, and three-point label classes.  The external contact is the curated standard-code table, whose only role here is to assign each codon an amino-acid or stop label.  Within that scope, the detected boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is reported by the punctured-cube check: $R$ is a punctured local cube with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the chapter-specific claim is not merely that thirteen codons were selected, but that the selected codons carry a concrete local face structure with $13$ vertices, $16$ one-dimensional incidences, and $4$ two-dimensional faces in the finite codon coordinate system.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape evidence.}
The spectral row gives a second finite witness for the same local split.  The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with radial polynomial value $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same residual reported for the cubic polynomial check.  The spectral-radius entry is $0.6752479308830213$, so the recorded numerical residual is at roundoff scale relative to the displayed finite-row spectrum.  A shape null model with $N=2000$ distinct sampled tables recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$, found $0$ exact matches, and had $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size.  Its observed $M$ size is $20$, with $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These rows support a local coordinate-and-label irregularity stronger than the sampled nulls, while still remaining a code-read fact.

\paragraph{Leave-one stability.}
Two leave-one tests describe how the finite boundary behaves under local removal.  In the leave-one-code-out row, $23$ tables were evaluated against $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline is $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ is $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  In the leave-one-codon-out row, the anchor count is $0$, the boundary count is $5$, the high-closure coverage count is $8$, and the predictable count is $8$, with recovery rate $1.0$ and recovery-rate-above-threshold value $1.0$.  These values say that the boundary tile is locally recoverable in this finite table setting; they do not say that translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality has been recovered.

\paragraph{Module-level accounting.}
The module classification accounts for every element of $R$ without extras or omissions.  It reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from modules, and no unassigned codons.  The six displayed modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The leave-one-code-out family row has family count $5$ and family hit rates above baseline $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  This module accounting is important because it ties the thirteen-row boundary to named codon families rather than to an anonymous statistic.  The evidence therefore supports a chapter-specific statement: in the standard-code readback, window-six coordinates expose a local boundary tile whose finite row is $R$, whose punctured-cube face count is $(13,16,4)$, whose module assignment is complete, and whose sampled null behavior is unusually concentrated.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The contact used here is a curated code table.  It supplies codon-to-label readback and local split checks only.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, a universal biological mechanism, or any necessity that DNA must obey this geometry.  Amino-acid label sequences are still label sequences, not folded structures.  A stronger biological claim would require a separate higher-layer contact, such as an experimental translation contact, a structural contact, a physical-admissibility contact, or a function contact.  Without such a contact, the finite-row conclusion remains deliberately local: $Q_6$ window geometry organizes a thirteen-codon boundary tile in the standard-code label table, and the reported null, spectral, leave-one, and module rows quantify that organization at the code-read layer.
