\subsection{Window-six codon boundary row}\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon row.}
The row records a window-six codon-coordinate pattern for the standard genetic-code label table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read only as a finite coordinate object whose vertices are codon displays.  The observed boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is also recorded as a punctured-cube face object with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the claim is not that the whole biological code is generated by the cube.  The narrower statement is that, after a standard-code label table is supplied as the contact, the listed thirteen codons occupy a local boundary tile whose vertex, edge, and face counts are the finite numbers above.

\paragraph{Label contact and admissible readout.}
The only reality contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  That contact has resolution at codon-label readout: it can test whether the finite set $R$ is the listed codon set and whether local label splits occur on that set.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, protein folding, biochemical feasibility, cellular function, or a universal mechanism.  The coordinate packet therefore stops at the code-read layer.  Any statement about realized translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, or biological universality would require a separate observation route with its own measured object.

It says that the displayed window-six object has the measured spectral footprint; it does not say that organisms must implement that footprint as a law.

\paragraph{Shape tests.}
Against the sampled null comparison for the $M$-shape statistic, the run used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was $1$ exact shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005,
\end{aligned}
$$
and there were $25$ rows with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null comparison preserving amino-acid class structure used $N=2000$ samples with the same seed.  Its observed $M$ size was $20$, with $0$ exact matches and $7$ rows in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The recorded probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the observed local row is not a common outcome under the stated finite sampling tests.  They do not by themselves identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one checks.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.19821739130434785,
\end{aligned}
$$
while the hit rate above baseline was
$$
\begin{aligned}
0.9841269841269841.
\end{aligned}
$$
The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out check reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the finite row is stable under the two stated deletion probes at the code-label level.

\paragraph{Family and module accounting.}
The code-family split contains $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline by family were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial and plastid codes, $1.0$ for candidate-other codes, $1.0$ for mitochondrial codes, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate codes.  The module classification accounts for all thirteen codons in $R$ with no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{AGR}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{AR\_Lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{CUN}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{Ile\_Met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{SSN}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{Stop\_Trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The accounting summary is $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$ and assigned count $13$.  This is the strongest form of the row-level claim: every boundary codon belongs to one of the named finite modules, and the modules exhaust exactly the observed boundary set.

\paragraph{Scientific status.}
The record is ready as a code-read claim about a finite local tile in the standard-code table.  Its support consists of the exact thirteen-codon list, the punctured-cube face counts, the spectral scalars, the sampled null probabilities, the leave-one recovery checks, the family hit rates, and the complete module partition.  Its scope is deliberately local.  From this evidence one may report a finite codon-coordinate boundary pattern tied to curated codon labels; one may not infer translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a generative mechanism, or a universal biological law without an additional contact measuring that higher layer.
