\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table read through a six-bit codon display.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation structure measured only at the code-read layer.  The external contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  This contact is strong enough to test codon-label readback and local label-split structure, but it is not a translation apparatus, a folding experiment, a physical-admissibility test, a function assay, a universal mechanism, or a law of biology.

\paragraph{Boundary row.}
The verified row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  Its codons are exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}

Thus the finite claim is not merely that a boundary exists, but that the boundary row is the displayed thirteen-codon subset of the standard-code table. \paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
A separate finite-row check identifies the same thirteen codons as the punctured-cube row and records the face vector
