\subsection{Window-six codon tile with thirteen boundary labels}\label{subsec:h0-r-cardinality-13}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and observed row.}
The finite object in this record is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$ read through the curated standard genetic-code table.  The table is used only as a codon-to-label contact: it assigns each displayed codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label, and it does not itself assert a translation dynamics, a protein structure, or a functional mechanism.  In this contact, the distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite readback asserts $|R|=13$, with no hidden asymptotic or population parameter.  The same row was also checked as a punctured-cube pattern with $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
The phrase ``punctured cube'' is used here in the finite combinatorial sense encoded by these face counts and by the displayed codon set; it is not a claim that cellular biology is governed by this geometry.

\paragraph{Local label split.}
The row separates codons that the standard table labels by amino-acid or stop symbols inside a local window-six display.  The relevant distinctions are coordinate position, closure inside the local tile, the spectral signature of the associated finite operator, and the relation between singleton-corner labels and three-point label classes.  These distinctions remain internal to the codon-label display until another measurement is supplied.  In particular, the label sequence obtained from a codon string is not a folded protein, not a structure order, and not a physical admissibility certificate.

The associated spectral run produced the scalar values

\begin{aligned}

\mu&=3.051487585298128.

$$
The radial cubic check evaluated to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, with the same residual recorded for the cubic relation.  At the scale of this finite computation, the residual is a numerical tolerance certificate for the stated operator relation, not a new biological postulate.  The role of the spectral values is to identify the local combinatorial row inside the window-six display and to make the tile comparison reproducible.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null samples.}
The local shape was compared with $N=2000$ sampled alternatives using seed $42$.  The sampler required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sampled alternative matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite-row claim: the observed local tile is uncommon under this sampling procedure.  They do not, by themselves, promote the tile to a universal law of translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, or mechanism.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class preserving comparison.}
A second null comparison preserved amino-acid classes.  In $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, so the reported finite-sample exact-shape rate was $0.0$.  The observed size of the associated $M$ object was $20$, and $7$ null samples lay in the lower tail at or below that observed size.  The recorded lower-tail value was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This comparison is stronger than a purely unconstrained shuffle because it keeps amino-acid class information in place while testing whether the same local shape appears.  The result still remains a code-layer readback: it constrains a displayed codon-label pattern, not a folded structure.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon probes.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the observed hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  These values show that the local window-six structure has predictive stability for code-label readback across the tested table family.

The leave-one-codon-out probe recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, and $8$ predictable cases.  Its recovery rate was
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho_{\mathrm{recover}}=1.0,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the same value recorded for recovery above threshold.  The interpretation is deliberately narrow: removing one codon and asking whether the local code-layer geometry recovers it is a finite diagnostic for the curated table, not an observation of translation in a ribosome and not evidence of protein-level function.

Every module had a predictable or boundary status in the finite classification.  This accounts for the whole row $R$ and gives the chapter its concrete object: a thirteen-codon local boundary row in the standard-code display, equipped with face counts, spectral scalars, null comparisons, leave-one-out probes, and module coverage.

\paragraph{Scope limit.}
The evidence in this chapter reaches only the code-read layer.  It supports the statement that the curated standard-code table, when placed in a window-six codon coordinate display, exposes a reproducible local boundary-tile pattern with $13$ codons, $16$ edges, and $4$ two-faces.  It does not show that DNA must obey this geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not establish translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or a law spanning all genetic systems.  Any one of those stronger claims would require a separate empirical contact addressed to that layer, rather than a promotion of the finite codon-label pattern recorded here.
