\subsection{Window-Six Boundary Tile for the Standard Codon Table}\label{sec:codon-window-six-boundary-tile}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
The record works over the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the $64$ codons after the usual four-letter alphabet is encoded by two bits per nucleotide position.  The claim isolated here is not a claim about translation as a chemical process.  It is a finite statement about a distinguished boundary set $R$ inside the codon display, together with a curated label table used only to read codons as amino-acid or stop labels.  In the verified row, the boundary set has exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus $|R|=13$.  The same finite computation identifies this set as the punctured-cube boundary pattern supplied by the window-six coordinates, with face-vector data $(13,16,4)$.  The point of the construction is that these thirteen labels are not selected by narrative grouping: they are the codons returned by the coordinate-and-closure computation before any biological promotion is allowed.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The finite row also records a numerical spectral profile.  The reported values are $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic check is numerically zero at the recorded precision: both the cubic-polynomial residual and the radial-polynomial residual are $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  In the companion shape run with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the exact boundary-shape match count is $1$, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$.  The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ is $25$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The sampler had $57377$ distinct sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries, so the row should be read as a finite randomized comparison against the stated sampling procedure, not as an asymptotic theorem about all possible genetic codes.

\paragraph{Label-class preserving null comparison.}
A second null comparison preserves amino-acid label classes.  In that run $N=2000$, seed $42$, the observed size of the companion set $M$ is $20$.  The exact-shape count is $0$, so the recorded exact-shape probability is $0.0$ at this sample size.  The lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$, giving a lower-tail probability of $0.0035$.  These numbers support the local claim that the window-six codon geometry is detecting a nontrivial boundary-and-label split in the standard table.  They do not identify a molecular cause, a historical pathway, or a universal rule for biological coding.

\paragraph{Readback tests across held-out labels and codons.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluates $23$ code tables with $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate is $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate in $M-T$ is $0.9365079365079365$ and the hit rate after adding the boundary is $0.9841269841269841$.  The recorded rate above baseline is also $0.9841269841269841$.  A separate leave-one-codon-out row reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, no anchors, and recovery rate $1.0$.  At the family level, five families are recorded.  The family hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families.  These finite readback rows say that the local codon tile remains useful under the stated omissions; they still remain code-layer tests of labels.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The thirteen codons of $R$ are fully accounted for by the module classification.  The modules are $\{AGA,AGG\}$ for agr, $\{AAA\}$ for ar-lys, $\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\}$ for cun, $\{AUA\}$ for ile-met, $\{UCA,UUA\}$ for ssn, and $\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}$ for stop-trp.  The module row reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  This is the strongest finite bookkeeping statement in the packet: every boundary codon named by the cardinality computation is placed in exactly the recorded module inventory.

\paragraph{Local interpretation boundary.}
The empirical contact is a curated standard-code label table.  It supplies codon-to-label readback and permits local split checks inside the code layer.  It does not observe ribosomal translation, folding, physical admissibility, biochemical function, or an organism-independent mechanism.  Consequently, the chapter may say that the standard code exhibits a window-six boundary tile with a thirteen-codon set, the recorded face-vector, the stated spectral values, and the reported null-model behavior.  It may not infer that proteins fold from this tile, that the tile is a law of biology, that every organism must instantiate it, or that codon-label readback alone supplies function.  Any such stronger assertion would require a separate contact whose measurements live at that higher layer.
