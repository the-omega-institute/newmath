\subsection{Window-Six Boundary Tile for the Standard Code}\label{subsec:h0-r-cardinality-13}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite codon carrier.}
The record studies the standard-code codon table through a six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with each codon treated as a coordinate point carrying a curated amino-acid or stop label. The observed boundary set $R$ has exactly thirteen codons. In the verified row, the set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the reported cardinality is $|R|=13$. This is the local finite object under discussion: a codon-coordinate subset together with label readback from the curated standard code. The table contact supplies codon-to-label assignments only; it does not by itself observe translation dynamics, folding, admissible physical chemistry, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same thirteen codons are verified as the punctured-cube boundary set. Its finite cell count is recorded by the vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the local object contains thirteen displayed codon vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four local faces in the chosen window-six presentation. This shape statement is a code-layer fact about a coordinate tile. It is not a statement that DNA, RNA, ribosomes, tRNAs, proteins, or organisms must instantiate this geometry as a global biological law.

\paragraph{Spectral row.}
The associated spectral calculation separates the main marked component from the boundary component by two reported radii. The verified values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is reported as the cubic-polynomial residual. Thus the numerical row is internally stable at the displayed tolerance: the spectral radius recorded for $M$ is $0.6752479308830213$, while the boundary radius for $R$ is $0.4758352843197002$. These numbers support the existence of a finite code-coordinate distinction; they do not identify a physical energy, folding order parameter, or translation rate.

\paragraph{Shape significance under shuffled code labels.}
The finite-row significance test used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. Among those alternatives, there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. The row therefore says that the standard-code local tile is uncommon under that sampling design, both exactly and at high-overlap tolerance. A second null test preserving amino-acid classes also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$; it reported observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, $p_{\mathrm{class,exact}}=0.0$, and $p_{\mathrm{class,lower}}=0.0035$. These are finite empirical comparisons against specified randomization procedures, not evidence that the coordinate construction explains every biological constraint on the genetic code.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The boundary-aware rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$; the same $0.9841269841269841$ is recorded as the hit rate above baseline. By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate, across $5$ families. The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, recovery rate $1.0$, and recovery rate above threshold $1.0$. These rows show that the boundary tile is useful for code-label recovery tests in the finite dataset, while remaining silent about protein structure and cellular implementation.

\paragraph{Module accounting.}
The module classification assigns all thirteen boundary codons without omissions. The complete row reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA, AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA, UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA, UAG, UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module has a predictable or boundary status in the recorded classification. This gives a compact finite partition of the local tile, but it still operates at the level of codon labels. In particular, CUN, UUR-adjacent wording, stop and tryptophan grouping, and arginine or lysine modules do not become folded-structure observations merely by being named in this partition.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The claim supported here is that a window-six coordinate presentation exposes a thirteen-codon local boundary tile in the curated standard-code label table, with a punctured-cube cell count, stable spectral row, significant finite randomization comparisons, strong leave-one recovery behavior, and complete module accounting. The claim does not assert that the tile is a universal biological principle. It also does not assert translation realization, structural order, folded protein geometry, physical admissibility, biological function, or a causal mechanism. Any statement crossing into those layers would need a distinct contact observing that layer directly, rather than reusing the standard-code table as if codon-label readback were a structural or functional experiment.
