\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$ read through the curated standard-code label table.  The row is a code-read record: it compares a finite coordinate pattern against codon labels, and it does not assert a biological mechanism.  The verified boundary set has cardinality thirteen,
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13,
\end{aligned}
$$
and its members are
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The relevant empirical contact is only that a curated standard genetic-code table assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact supplies label readback at codon resolution; it is not a measurement of translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Shape significance under finite nulls.}
The shape-significance null used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  The distinct-sample procedure made $57377$ attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries, so the null space being sampled was finite enough for duplication pressure to be visible.  There was one exact shape match, giving

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
and there were twenty-five samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
A second amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, recorded zero exact matches, seven lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$-size, and observed $M$-size $20$:

\begin{aligned}

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The boundary condition is part of the result.  The local codon tile may expose a finite boundary pattern in $Q_6$ and may support standard-code label readback, but no translation realization follows from it.  No folded protein structure follows from it.  No physical admissibility, biological function, universal biological mechanism, or global law follows from it.  Crossing any of those boundaries would require a separate higher-layer reality contact, such as a translation experiment, a structural measurement, a physical admissibility assay, or a functional assay.  With only the curated code table and the finite coordinate probes listed here, the claim remains exactly a thirteen-codon, window-six, local boundary-row fact at the code-read layer.
