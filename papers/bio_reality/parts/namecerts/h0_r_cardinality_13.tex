\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, read as the $64$ codons of the standard-code table through a curated codon-label contact.  The row under audit isolates a local boundary set $R$ inside this carrier.  The verified row gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the cardinality check is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the whole content of the cardinality claim: thirteen named codons form the boundary row selected by the window-six coordinate rule.  The contact supplies the standard-code label table against which these codons can be read, but it does not supply a mechanism of translation or any structural statement about proteins.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same row is also verified as a punctured-cube tile.  The finite shape row reports that the selected codons are exactly the punctured-cube set and that its face-count vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a list of thirteen codon names.  It carries a local cubical incidence profile: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the induced codon display.  The phrase \emph{local tile} refers only to this finite cubical profile and to the labels seen at those vertices.  It is not a global law for DNA, not a biological necessity, and not a claim that window-six geometry realizes translation.

This places the numerical residual at floating-point tolerance rather than at a visible combinatorial discrepancy.  The spectrum should be read as a finite-row diagnostic for the selected codon tile, not as a thermodynamic, kinetic, or evolutionary invariant.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
The unrestricted shape Monte Carlo row used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It reports $57377$ distinct-sample attempts, $55377$ duplicate retries, one exact shape match, and twenty-five matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null row also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It records observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape probability $0.0$, and lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These rows support the limited statement that the observed local shape is uncommon under the tested finite nulls.  They do not identify a causal biological process.

The leave-one-codon-out row reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These numbers say that the local boundary is useful for finite code-layer recovery probes.  They do not say that a removed codon is physically synthesized, folded, selected, or functional.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons of $R$ are partitioned into six named modules:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The module-completeness row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Hence every codon in the boundary row is accounted for by the finite module partition.  The classification is a code-read classification: it organizes codon labels at the curated standard-code contact.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table, whose observed fact is that codons are assigned to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test the code-read layer and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, universal mechanism, or universality of the local geometry.  Therefore the valid conclusion is deliberately narrow: the window-six codon coordinates expose a finite local boundary tile with $|R|=13$, punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, a stable numerical spectral readout, significant finite-null behavior, and complete module assignment against the curated standard-code label table.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
