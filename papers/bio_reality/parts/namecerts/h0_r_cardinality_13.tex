\subsection{Window-six local boundary tile for the standard code}\label{sec:bior--h0-r-cardinality-13}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the usual codon alphabet display.  The biological contact used here is only the curated standard-code label table: it assigns each codon a code-layer label, namely an amino-acid label or a stop label.  This contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for local split checks, but it is not a translation experiment, not a folding experiment, not a physical-admissibility assay, and not a function measurement.  The chapter therefore treats the observed pattern as a code-read boundary tile in $Q_6$, not as a global biological law.

$$
\begin{aligned}

&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite-row assertion for this record is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row list is verified as a punctured-cube tile with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a local combinatorial statement about the codon-coordinate carrier.  It says that the listed thirteen codons form the observed boundary object under the window-six coordinate readback.  It does not say that the carrier geometry causes the standard genetic code, nor that the geometry is biologically necessary.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The finite-row verdict is that the standard-code label table, when read through the window-six codon carrier, supports a thirteen-row local boundary tile with a complete module decomposition, a punctured-cube face-vector $(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral scalars, and finite null-model support.  The result remains strictly at the code-read layer.  No translation realization follows from the row list.  No folded structure follows from amino-acid label readback.  No physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
