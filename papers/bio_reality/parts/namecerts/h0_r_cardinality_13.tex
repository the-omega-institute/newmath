\subsection{Window-six codon boundary row}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Record.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table at the code-read layer. The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, used only as a coordinate display for codons and their local neighborhood structure. The observed row is the boundary class $R$ selected by the window-six tile computation. In the verified run $5d4a3459997d$, the class has size $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is verified in the punctured-cube computation $e19917012c60$, where the face data have $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. Thus the numerical statement is not merely that a size was counted: the same thirteen codons form the displayed local tile and give the recorded finite face profile.

\paragraph{Code-label contact.}
The biological contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table. It assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and in this chapter that is the whole biological role of the contact. The contact tests codon-label readback and local label splitting on the displayed tile; it does not test translation dynamics, molecular folding, physical realizability, biochemical activity, or organism-level mechanism. The computation may therefore identify a finite local pattern in the standard-code label table, but it does not convert that pattern into a law of biology.

These values support the claim that the displayed row is an unusually structured local code-table feature under the tested null families.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code readback.}

The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. Family-level aggregation in run $7dda978c6c97$ split the $23$ tables into $5$ families. The family hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families. These numbers say that the code-label readback remains highly recoverable under the tested leave-one-code regime; they do not say that translation or function has been reconstructed.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon row.}
The leave-one-codon run $e9e1a6e41633$ reports $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases, and $8$ high-coverage closure cases, with recovery rate $1.0$ and recovery above threshold $1.0$. This closes the finite bookkeeping for the row: every member of the thirteen-codon boundary class is placed in one of the named code-read modules.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The supported statement is narrow. Window-six coordinates expose a local boundary tile in the curated standard-code label table, and the finite row has reproducible cardinality, face profile, spectrum, null-model rarity, leave-one-code recovery, leave-one-codon recovery, and complete module assignment. The unsupported statements are equally important. From these data alone one may not infer translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, a universal mechanism, or a rule forcing DNA to obey this geometry. Any such promotion would require a separate contact that directly observes the higher layer. Until that contact is supplied, the chapter remains a code-read record for a thirteen-codon local boundary row in the standard-code table.
