\subsection{Thirteen-Codon Boundary Row in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The object of this record is the standard-code codon table read through a finite window-six coordinate display.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, with codons read as finite coordinate rows and with the curated standard genetic-code table used only as an external label contact.  The contact supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels; it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The finite row isolated by the probe is the boundary row $R$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality statement is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| = 13,
\end{aligned}
$$
with experiment run $\mathrm{5d4a3459997d}$ reporting both the size $13$ and the same thirteen expected codons.  This is a finite-row observation about the local codon tile; it is not a biological law about the whole code.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same row is also verified as a punctured-cube boundary object.  The finite face data attached to $R$ has $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the observed row has thirteen vertices, sixteen edge incidences, and four local two-dimensional faces in the tile summary.  The equality between the punctured-cube row and the listed codons was recorded in experiment run $\mathrm{e19917012c60}$.  The intended reading is therefore narrow: the codon cube coordinate system exposes a local boundary tile whose row is visible after the standard-code labels are read back from the external table.

\paragraph{Spectral signature.}
The spectral probe separates the local row from a purely visual coincidence by attaching numerical invariants.  The measured values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the same value is reported for the cubic-polynomial check.  Thus the finite object is not merely a named list of codons; it carries a stable numerical signature within the coordinate-and-closure computation.  The tolerance-level spectral-radius entry is again $0.6752479308830213$, matching $\lambda_M$ in the finite-row summary.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
Against the sampled shape null, experiment run $\mathrm{0b267c88f8e4}$ used $N=2000$ sampled rows.  The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found one exact shape match and twenty-five samples with Jaccard overlap at least $0.9$.  The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null gives an additional check.  In experiment run $\mathrm{e8d5de747a0c}$, again with $N=2000$, the observed comparison row had $|M|=20$, no exact class-preserving shape match, and seven samples in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the local row is a detectable finite pattern under the chosen probes.  They do not convert the pattern into a mechanism of translation or into a claim about protein structure.

\paragraph{Leave-one checks.}
The leave-one-code-out probe evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  The leave-one-codon-out probe reported anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, high closure coverage $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the local boundary is not just present in the static row; it is also visible under deletion-style finite probes.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned by the module classification.  The six reported modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The classification summary reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons.  Every module has either a predictable or boundary status in the finite probe output.  This gives a complete finite accounting of the row, with no residue hidden outside the six named modules.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The result is a code-read statement.  It says that the window-six codon coordinate system, together with the curated standard-code label contact, isolates a thirteen-codon local boundary row with a punctured-cube face vector, a recorded spectral signature, statistically unusual shape behavior under the sampled nulls, and complete module accounting.  It does not say that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not infer translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any global biological law.  Any such higher-layer statement would require a separate reality contact designed for that layer rather than the curated code table used here.
