\subsection{A thirteen-codon boundary tile in the standard-code cube}
\label{sec:h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite row and observed contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer. The finite carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube obtained by displaying each codon as a six-bit word, with labels read from the curated standard genetic-code table. The row singled out by the finite computation is a thirteen-codon set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality row gives $|R|=13$, with experiment run $5d4a3459997d$. The biological contact used here is deliberately narrow: a curated table assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label. That contact is sufficient for codon-label readback and for checking a local split in the cube; it is not a measurement of translation dynamics, folded structure, physical feasibility, biological function, or a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Local cell structure.}
The companion punctured-cube computation returns exactly the same thirteen codons for $R$ and assigns the local cell count vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite object is not merely a list of labels: it has a local face pattern with thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-faces inside the six-bit codon display. The interpretation remains geometric and code-level. The singleton-corner and three-point label-class distinctions indicate where curated labels meet the local tile, but they do not convert the tile into a physical model of molecular action.

\paragraph{Spectral signature.}
The spectral row records three scalar values for the same finite configuration:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported as the radial polynomial residual. This is numerically indistinguishable from zero at the recorded tolerance scale and supplies an internal consistency check on the finite spectral readout. It should be read as a signature of the codon-coordinate construction, not as evidence that genetic translation is governed by the displayed cubic relation.

\paragraph{Shape significance against sampled nulls.}
The sampled shape-null row used $N=2000$ trials with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. Among those $2000$ samples, the exact shape appeared once, giving $p=0.0005$, while $25$ samples had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p=0.0125$. A stricter amino-acid-class-preserving null, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, recorded $0$ exact matches. Its lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size $20$ was $7$, giving lower-tail probability $0.0035$. These finite counts support the claim that the displayed local shape is not a common outcome of the tested randomizations. They do not by themselves identify a causal biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The leave-one-codon-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-coverage closure cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$. These rows show that the local code-layer geometry has predictive stability under the tested removals. The prediction being tested is still label recovery in a finite codon table, not protein folding or cellular function.

\paragraph{Family and module accounting.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out row groups the evaluated cases into $5$ families. The hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families. The module-classification row accounts for all thirteen codons in $R$: $\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}$ form the agr module; $\mathrm{AAA}$ is ar\_lys; $\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}$ form cun; $\mathrm{AUA}$ is ile\_met; $\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}$ form ssn; and $\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}$ form stop\_trp. The classification reports $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. This is the strongest finite accounting statement in the row: every codon selected by the local boundary set is assigned to one of the recorded modules.

\paragraph{Scope of the claim.}
The conclusion is a bounded code-read statement. In the six-bit codon cube, the curated standard-code labels expose a thirteen-codon boundary tile with a punctured-cube face vector, a stable spectral signature, significant sampled-null separation, strong leave-one recovery, and complete module accounting. The same evidence also fixes the stopping point. No assertion about translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this row unless a distinct contact measuring that higher layer is supplied.
