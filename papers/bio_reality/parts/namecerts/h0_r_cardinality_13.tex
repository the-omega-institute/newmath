\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The finite carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$ equipped with a curated standard-code readback.  The contact is deliberately narrow: it is a codon-label table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  It can test code-layer readback and local label splits, but it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Within this scope, the distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is also observed as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$.  Thus the row-level statement is not merely that thirteen labels were counted; the finite cell data records thirteen vertices, sixteen edges, and four two-dimensional faces in the local boundary tile.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The record supports a local code-read boundary claim: in the standard-code contact, a six-bit codon display exposes a thirteen-codon boundary row with punctured-cube incidence data, stable spectral coordinates, strong finite-sample shape separation, and complete module assignment.  The record does not support a higher-layer biological claim.  No translation process, folded protein structure, physical admissibility condition, biological function, mechanism, or universal law follows from this row unless a separate reality contact supplies that higher-layer observation.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback remains label readback; it is not folded structure.
