\subsection{A Thirteen-Point Boundary Row in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{subsec:bior-reality-h0-r-cardinality-thirteen}

\paragraph{Finite carrier.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite reality contact for code-layer label readback.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated codon-to-label table.  The BEDC row does not claim that the biological translation apparatus is generated by this cube.  It only records that, after the window-six coordinate and local tile tests are applied to the standard-code table, the distinguished boundary row $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly the following set of codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Equivalently, the finite-row audit reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is reported by the punctured-cube check, which gives the face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not being introduced as an informal motif: it is a finite subset of the codon cube with a counted vertex set, a counted edge set, and a counted two-face set.

They do not, by themselves, identify any molecular mechanism, folding law, energetic admissibility condition, or biological function.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The null comparison for the $M$-shape used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  The audit records $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among the sampled tables there was $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserving amino-acid class reports observed $|M|=20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, and
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These are finite-row significance statements about the local label split under specified shuffles.  They are not statements that all genetic codes, all translation systems, or all proteins must realize the same geometry.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely assigned by the module audit.  The row reports $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\

\end{aligned}
$$
The module statement is therefore exact at the finite-row level: the thirteen boundary codons are neither under-assigned nor over-assigned by the listed classes.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The reality contact is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  This contact can test code-layer readback and the local codon-tile label split.  It cannot test translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or structural order.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback is still only a label readback: it is not folded structure.  The local tile is therefore a code-read record with a sharp boundary.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality follows from this row unless a separate higher-layer reality contact supplies that information.
