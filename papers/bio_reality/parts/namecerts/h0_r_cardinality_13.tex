\subsection{Window-Six Boundary Tile in the Standard Codon Table}
\label{sec:bior-r-cardinality-thirteen}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite readback contact over a six-bit codon display. The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinates read as codon positions and alphabet symbols, and the only external contact used here is the curated standard-code label table. That contact says that each codon has an amino-acid or stop label in the standard code. It does not say that the coordinate geometry causes translation, determines protein structure, supplies physical admissibility, realizes biological function, or gives a universal mechanism. The finite row therefore lives at the code-read layer: it compares a coordinate tile against curated labels and records what the label split exposes.

\paragraph{The boundary set.}
The verified boundary row is the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Its audited cardinality is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite row is also identified as a punctured-cube shaped object with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local tile is not merely a list of codons: it carries a small two-dimensional incidence profile inside the six-bit coordinate cube. The singleton-corner and three-point label-class distinctions are read through the standard-code labels, while the shape claim itself is a finite coordinate claim about which codons lie in $R$ and how their local faces meet.

\paragraph{Null-model pressure.}
The shape row was tested against a finite randomization baseline with $N=2000$ samples. The experiment required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries. There was exactly one exact-shape match, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
There were $25$ samples with Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A stricter amino-acid-class preserving null model again used $N=2000$. It found $0$ exact matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$ size. The recorded observed size is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M|=20,
\end{aligned}
$$
with
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\text{-}class,exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\text{-}class,lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the window-six tile is not an ordinary draw from the tested finite baselines. They do not supply mechanism, universality, or function.

This completes the chapter-level readback claim: the window-six coordinate geometry isolates a thirteen-codon local boundary tile whose labels and module assignments are visible through the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The conclusion is deliberately narrow. The finite-row evidence supports a local code-read statement: a thirteen-codon boundary set in $Q_6$ has a punctured-cube incidence profile, a stable spectral signature, significant finite-baseline separation, high leave-one recovery, and complete module assignment under the curated standard code. Nothing in this contact proves translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or biological necessity. Any such higher-layer claim requires a separate reality contact designed for that layer.
