\subsection{Window-six codon readback and the thirteen-codon boundary row}
\label{sec:bior:codon-window-six-thirteen-row}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The carrier for this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard-code label table as a finite code-layer contact.  The contact is not a biochemical mechanism: it is the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels, used here only to test whether a window-six coordinate construction identifies a local tile and its boundary labels.  In this row the recovered boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite claim is not merely that a boundary exists, but that the row-level extraction returns this specific thirteen-element set.  The same row records the boundary as a punctured-cube object with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional faces, and four two-dimensional faces in the local tile representation.  This gives a concrete local geometry for the code-read layer while keeping the biological contact external to the coordinate construction.

\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\

The cubic or radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, so the row is numerically consistent with the stated spectral equation to the recorded tolerance.  The point of these numbers is modest and finite: the code table, the window-six coordinates, and the derived probe agree on a reproducible local split.  They do not identify translation dynamics, molecular structure, physical admissibility, folded conformation, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary necessity.

\paragraph{Shape and null comparisons.}
The shape-significance run used $N=2000$ sampled tables with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries.  Among those samples there was $1$ exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and $25$ samples had Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null run, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, reported observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$.  Its recorded probabilities were $p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}=0.0$ and $p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These finite rows support the statement that the observed local tile is not a common outcome under the sampled nulls, while still leaving the source of biological labels in the curated code table rather than in the geometry alone.

\paragraph{Leave-one-out tests.}
The leave-one-code-out test evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the reported hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the hit-rate-above-baseline values were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, candidate-other, mitochondrial, and nuclear-ciliate families, across $5$ recorded families.  The leave-one-codon-out row recorded anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, predictable count $8$, high closure coverage $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These readbacks say that the local boundary is predictive for omitted code-layer labels in the finite protocol; they do not say that the same boundary realizes translation, folding, function, or physical selection.

The module classification is complete at the level of $R$: it records $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  The assigned modules are

Every listed module has predictable or boundary status in the recorded classification.  This is the strongest local form of the row: the thirteen codons are all accounted for by named modules, the finite row has no leftover codon, and the standard-code readback supplies labels for checking the split.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The conclusion is a code-read conclusion only.  The row supports a finite window-six local tile with boundary set $R$, punctured-cube $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral values $\lambda_M$, $\lambda_R$, and $\mu$ as recorded above, nontrivial sampled-null behavior, and leave-one-out recovery at the code-label layer.  It does not support any claim that DNA obeys this geometry as a biological necessity.  It does not support translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or amino-acid sequence-to-structure inference.  Any such claim would require a separate reality contact at that higher layer; the curated standard-code table supplies codon-label readback and local split checks only.
