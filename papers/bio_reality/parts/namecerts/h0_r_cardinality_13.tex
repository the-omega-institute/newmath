\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite-row assertion is
$$
\begin{aligned}

The biological contact used here is only that a curated standard genetic-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  It supports codon-label readback and local label-split checks, but it does not observe translation dynamics, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$

These are finite-row rarity statements about the code-label geometry.  They do not identify a causal origin of the genetic code, and they do not license promotion from code-read structure to protein structure or biological function.
