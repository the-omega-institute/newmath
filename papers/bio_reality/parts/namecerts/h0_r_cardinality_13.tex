\subsection{The thirteen-codon boundary row in the window-six codon cube}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$, where each codon is read as a six-bit display and the observed labels are the curated amino-acid or stop labels supplied by the standard genetic-code table.  The finite row selected by the local tile computation is the set $R$ of thirteen codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality statement is therefore not asymptotic and not qualitative: the row has size $13$, and the computed row is exactly the displayed list.  In this chapter the phrase ``boundary row'' denotes this finite subset of the codon cube together with its curated standard-code labels; it does not denote translation, protein realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same finite row was checked against the punctured-cube description.  The verified equality is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R_{\mathrm{punctured}}=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
and its face-count summary is the finite vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row is not merely a count of labels; it also carries a local tile shape inside $Q_6$.  The first entry records the thirteen vertices, the second records sixteen local edges, and the third records four local square faces in the computed tile.  This is the geometric content of the claim.  The biological contact remains the curated standard-code table, so the geometry can be used to organize codon-label readback but cannot be promoted into a global law of DNA or translation.

These numbers are part of the row certificate: they say that the local tile has a reproducible coordinate and spectral signature.  They do not say that the signature causes the genetic code, nor that it determines protein structure.

\paragraph{Shape significance checks.}
Against the coordinate null ensemble with seed $42$, the shape run used $N=2000$ sampled tables.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and had $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape appeared once, so the exact-shape frequency was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap criterion with Jaccard score at least $0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\ge 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite-row values support the statement that the observed tile is not a generic outcome of the sampled coordinate null model.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null model, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, found $0$ exact matches and $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$-size.  The observed $M$-size was $20$, the exact-shape frequency was $0.0$, and the lower-tail frequency was $0.0035$.  This strengthens the local code-read claim while keeping the contact boundary intact.

The leave-one-codon-out record found no anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, and $8$ predictable cases, with recovery rate $1.0$.  This says that the row behaves as a recoverable local code-read tile under the tested deletions.  It does not say that deletion recovery is a biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Module classification.}
The thirteen codons of $R$ are completely assigned into six named modules.  The module rows are
$$
\begin{aligned}

\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{AUA\},\\

$$
The classification has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons from the modules, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons.  Each module has a predictable-or-boundary status in the finite certificate.  The module labels are useful for reading the local split in the standard-code table, but they remain code-layer labels unless a separate biological contact supplies translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code bridge: a table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact can test code-read facts and local codon-tile label splits.  It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, folded protein structure, biological function, universal mechanism, or the necessity of the BEDC geometry for DNA.  The supported conclusion is therefore precise: window-six coordinates expose a thirteen-codon local boundary tile with reproducible shape, spectrum, null-model behavior, deletion recovery, and complete module classification.  The unsupported conclusions are equally precise: no global biological law, no protein fold, no function, and no universality follow from this finite row without an additional higher-layer contact.
