\subsection{The Thirteen-Codon Boundary Row in the Window-Six Codon Cube}
\label{subsec:h0-r-cardinality-thirteen}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table as a finite readback contact, not as a mechanism. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, displayed through codon coordinates and then read back through a curated standard-code label table. The distinguished boundary row is the finite set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| = 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row is therefore not an informal visual impression of the code table. It is a finite named set with thirteen listed codons, all read through the same curated codon-to-label contact. The contact supplies code-layer labels only: a codon may be assigned to an amino-acid label or to stop, but this contact does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any necessity that DNA must obey the window-six geometry.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same row has a verified punctured-cube face profile. Its finite cell count is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row contains thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the local tile complex extracted from the six-bit display. This is the chapter-level content of the boundary claim: the local codon window exposes a finite tile with a concrete shape, while the biological readback remains the externally curated code label table. The tile may organize which codons sit together in the coordinate display; it does not by itself assert a global biological law.

\end{aligned}

$$
\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}
$$
The sampling run required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. These numbers make the local boundary row statistically non-generic under that particular null procedure, but only for the declared coordinate and label-readback experiment. This strengthens the finite-row shape claim while preserving the same scope boundary: the evidence is about code-label geometry, not about translation, folding, admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

The module audit has $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons from the modules, and no unassigned codons. Each module has either a predictable or boundary status. This gives the row a closed finite bookkeeping form: every codon listed in $R$ is accounted for by exactly the supplied module classification. The classification is a code-layer organization of codon labels and local boundary behavior. It is not a claim that the corresponding amino-acid label sequence is a folded protein structure, a physically admissible object, or a realized biological function.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The chapter claim is therefore precise. In the window-six codon cube, the boundary row $R$ has verified size $13$, the listed codons above, punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, the stated spectral signature, significant rarity under the declared null samples, strong leave-one readback rates, and a complete six-module classification. The curated standard-code table is the only biological contact used here. Consequently the admissible conclusion is local: window-six coordinates expose a finite boundary-tile structure in codon-label readback. The inadmissible promotions are also local and explicit: no translation realization, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global law follows unless a separate contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.
