\subsection{Window-six local codon tile and the thirteen-codon boundary}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Claim and finite display.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed only at the code-read layer. The working carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube: each codon is represented by a finite window of three nucleotide positions with two-bit nucleotide display at each position. Within that finite display, the distinguished boundary set $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run records $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$ and $R\_\mathrm{cardinality\_equals\_13}=13$ under experiment run $5\mathrm{d}4\mathrm{a}3459997\mathrm{d}$. The biological contact used here is a curated standard genetic-code table: it supplies codon-to-amino-acid-or-stop labels and therefore permits label readback and local split checks. It does not, by itself, observe translation in a cell, molecular folding, energetic admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality across codes.

\paragraph{Local shape.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ also appear as the verified punctured-cube row. Its face-count summary is $f=(13,16,4)$, meaning that the finite object has thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional adjacencies, and four two-dimensional faces in the tested local tile representation. This is a local combinatorial signature, not a global law of biology. In particular, the presence of a punctured-cube-shaped boundary says that the standard-code labels have a structured finite readback in this coordinate system; it does not say that DNA, translation, tRNA charging, ribosomal selection, folded proteins, or organismal fitness are governed by that geometry.

\paragraph{Null comparisons.}
Two randomization checks give scale to the local shape. In the shape-significance run $0\mathrm{b}267\mathrm{c}88\mathrm{f}8\mathrm{e}4$, $N=2000$ samples were evaluated after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. Exactly one sample matched the observed shape, giving $p\_\mathrm{exact}=0.0005$. Twenty-five samples had Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p\_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9}=0.0125$. In the amino-acid-class-preserving null run $\mathrm{e}8\mathrm{d}5\mathrm{de}747\mathrm{a}0\mathrm{c}$, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed $M$ size is $20$, the exact-shape count is $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size is $7$. Thus the exact-shape empirical value is $0.0$ in that null, while the lower-tail empirical value is $0.0035$. These comparisons support the claim that the observed finite table position is not a routine draw from the tested null families, while still remaining a code-layer statement.

\paragraph{Scope of the record.}
The supported conclusion is narrow: the standard-code label table, when read through the window-six codon coordinates, contains a thirteen-codon boundary tile with a punctured-cube face summary, a stable spectral signature, significant finite null comparisons, strong leave-one performance, and a complete six-module assignment. The unsupported conclusions are equally important. No claim about translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal biological necessity follows from this contact. Any such claim would require a separate observation at the relevant biological layer, with its own finite measurements and its own failure conditions.
