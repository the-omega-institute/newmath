\subsection{Window-six codon boundary row}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite coordinate record.}
The object studied here is the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  Each codon is treated as a finite address in the window-six cube $Q_6$, and the readout layer is only the curated codon-to-label assignment: amino-acid or stop labels are observations supplied by the code table, not consequences of the coordinate system alone.  In the verified row for claim $h0.R.cardinality.13$, the distinguished boundary set $R$ has exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded cardinality is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a finite row statement about a named local pattern in the codon cube.  It says that these thirteen addresses form the selected boundary row under the tested coordinate-and-label construction; it does not say that the geometry is a biological law or that the row implements translation.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same thirteen codons are also reported as the punctured-cube instance for the row.  The finite cell count attached to this shape is
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row has thirteen selected vertices, sixteen selected one-dimensional adjacencies, and four selected two-dimensional tile faces in the tested local complex.  The word ``punctured'' is used here only for this finite combinatorial shape: a local cube-like patch with a missing interior or corner pattern as measured by its row membership and face counts.  The singleton-corner and three-point label-class distinctions belong to this local readback level.  They are not, by themselves, statements about ribosomal implementation, molecular dynamics, folded structure, or organism-level function.

The profile is evidence for a reproducible coordinate pattern inside the tested six-coordinate representation, while the biological labels still enter through the curated standard-code table.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The row was compared with finite sampled alternatives.  In the shape-significance run, $N=2000$ sampled rows were evaluated after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ appeared in $25$ samples, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A separate amino-acid-class-preserving null experiment again used $N=2000$ rows.  It found no exact shape match, recorded $7$ lower-tail samples at or below the observed $M$-row size, and used observed size $|M|=20$.  Its reported values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These tests support the narrow statement that the observed local shape is uncommon under the sampled finite alternatives.  They do not identify a causal mechanism, and they do not turn the coordinate pattern into a rule of molecular biology.

\paragraph{Leave-one-code and leave-one-codon checks.}
The leave-one-code-out check evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total target cases.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, whereas the tested local construction reached hit rate $0.9365079365079365$ inside $M-T$ and $0.9841269841269841$ after adjoining the boundary row.  The same value $0.9841269841269841$ is recorded as the hit rate above baseline.  By code-family grouping, the above-baseline rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative-yeast tables.  These values show that the row has predictive contact with code-label variation in the finite test bed, but the prediction target remains a code label.

The leave-one-codon-out check reports $8$ predictable cases, $5$ boundary cases, $0$ anchor cases, and $8$ high-coverage closure cases, with recovery rate $1.0$.  This is a recovery statement for omitted codon addresses inside the local coordinate-and-label construction.  It is not a statement that an omitted codon would be biologically translated, structurally accommodated, or functionally expressed without an independent observation at those layers.

\paragraph{Reality contact and claim boundary.}
The only biological contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is adequate for code-layer readback and for checking whether a local tile separates labels in the reported way.  It is not a contact with translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  Consequently, the chapter supports the following restricted conclusion: in the window-six codon coordinate display, the standard-code label table singles out a thirteen-codon boundary row with punctured-cube counts $(13,16,4)$, stable spectral values, low sampled-null frequencies, strong finite leave-one recovery, and complete module accounting.  No stronger biological conclusion follows here unless a separate experiment or observation supplies the missing higher-layer contact.
