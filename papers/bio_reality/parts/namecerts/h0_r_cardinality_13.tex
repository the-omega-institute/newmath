\subsection{Window-Six Codon Boundary Row}
\label{sec:bior-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Claim record.}
This record concerns the window-six codon coordinate on the standard-code table. The finite carrier is the six-bit codon display, interpreted only as a code-layer readback device. The observed boundary row $R$ is the set of codons selected by the local tile computation. The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
and the cardinality check is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The associated experiment run for the cardinality claim is $5d4a3459997d$, with the same thirteen codons appearing in the expected-codon comparison and in the reported size field. Thus the row is not an informal visual cluster: it is a finite named subset with a fixed membership list and a fixed count.

\paragraph{Local geometry.}
The punctured-cube readout reports the same row $R$ and assigns the face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R) &= (13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Here the first entry is the vertex count, the second is the edge count, and the third is the two-face count for the local codon tile after the puncture has been applied. This is a local combinatorial statement about the window-six coordinate. It does not say that the biological translation system is generated by this geometry; it says only that the curated codon labels can be read against a finite cube-shaped coordinate and that this particular boundary subset has thirteen codon vertices.

The value $\lambda_M$ belongs to the ambient module statistic, $\lambda_R$ belongs to the thirteen-codon row, and $\mu$ is the scalar coupling recorded by the probe.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The null comparison for the observed shape used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold count was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J \geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null model preserved amino-acid classes. In that comparison, the observed ambient module size was $20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed module size was $7$ out of $2000$. The resulting values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values support the claim that the row is a structured local pattern relative to the tested randomizations. They do not by themselves identify a physical cause, a translational mechanism, or a functional role.

\begin{aligned}

$$
The module-classification audit gives $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing row codons, and no unassigned codons. This supplies an internal accounting check: every codon in the boundary row is used exactly by the module classification supplied for this finite record.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The contact used here is the curated standard-code table, which assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact can test code-layer label readback and the local tile label split. It cannot test translation realization, structural order, physical admissibility, or function realization. The observed alignment is therefore narrow: the local coordinate, closure, spectrum, and module relation match a finite codon-label table at the code layer.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The row $R$ is a thirteen-codon boundary subset in a six-bit codon coordinate with a verified punctured-cube profile, spectral scalars, null-model significance, predictive recovery statistics, and a complete module classification. No stronger biological conclusion follows from those facts alone. In particular, this record does not establish translation, folded structure, physical admissibility, biochemical function, a mechanism of genetic-code origin, or a universal biological law. Any such claim would require a separate contact that observes the relevant higher-layer phenomenon rather than only codon-to-label assignment.
