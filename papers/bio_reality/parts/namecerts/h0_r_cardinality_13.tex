\paragraph{Finite codon row.}
The row records a window-six coordinate presentation of the standard-code codon table. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated codon-to-label bridge. The finite boundary set singled out by the run has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\qquad |R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row identifies this set as a punctured-cube boundary tile with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the claim is not merely that thirteen codons were named after inspection; the finite row gives a concrete codon list, a local cubical shape, and a small face vector attached to the same object.

\paragraph{Shape against null rows.}
The shape test used $N=2000$ sampled rows with seed $42$. It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact boundary shape occurred once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap threshold $J\geq 0.9$ occurred $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
A second null row preserves amino-acid classes. In that run the observed module size was $20$, the exact-shape count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$ out of $2000$. Hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}=0,
\qquad
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite code-layer distinction: the window-six tile is not a typical sampled shape under the stated row generators. They do not promote the tile into a global biological principle.

\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code label table: a codon is read back as an amino-acid or stop label through that table. This contact can test code-layer readback and local codon-tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. Therefore the accepted conclusion is limited to the code-read layer. Window-six geometry may expose a local boundary tile in the standard-code label table, but no translation process, protein fold, admissible physical pathway, biological function, universal mechanism, or necessity claim follows unless a separate higher-layer contact supplies it.
