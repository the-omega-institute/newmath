\subsection{Window-Six Codon Tile Boundary}\label{sec:codon-window-six-local-tile}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The object in this record is the standard-code codon table viewed only through a six-bit codon display. The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, and the permitted readback is a curated standard-code label table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. This contact is a code-layer contact: it can test codon-label readback and local label splits, but it does not observe translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality. The local tile therefore lives at the $code\_read$ layer. Any statement beyond that layer requires a separate higher-layer contact.

\paragraph{The recovered boundary row.}
The distinguished row $R$ has cardinality $13$. The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the finite-row assertion is not merely a size claim: the recovered row is exactly the listed codon set, with size $13$. The same set appears in the punctured-cube check, where the local face data have $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$. In this record, the phrase ``punctured cube'' means this finite incidence signature on the displayed codons; it is not a biological law and it is not a mechanism for translation.

\paragraph{Spectral signature.}
The associated finite operator data give a stable numerical signature. The observed values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the spectral relation is satisfied to numerical tolerance. The spectral radius recorded for the tolerated check is again $0.6752479308830213$. These numbers support a finite coordinate regularity claim: the codon-label display has a reproducible local numerical profile. They do not support promotion to folding, function, physical admissibility, or universal genetic-code necessity.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
The row shape was compared against $2000$ sampled finite alternatives with seed $42$. The sampler made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and encountered $55377$ duplicate retries. Exactly $1$ sample matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{exact}&=0.0005,\\
p_{Jaccard\geq 0.9}&=0.0125,
\end{aligned}
$$
with $25$ samples at Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$. Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null model, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact shape count was $0$, the observed $M$ size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below that observed size was $7$. The corresponding finite-row values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{aa\text{-}class\ exact}&=0.0,\\
p_{aa\text{-}class\ lower\ tail}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These tests make the local tile non-generic relative to the tested finite nulls. They still remain code-layer tests: a rare local label shape is not evidence of a translation mechanism, structural order, folded protein geometry, physical admissibility, biological function, or universality.

\paragraph{Leave-one evidence.}
The leave-one-code-out check evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets. Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$. The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$. The by-family version separated $5$ families. The family hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, $1.0$ for candidate-other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear-ciliate. These finite leave-one values support the claim that the boundary row is predictive within the displayed code-label regime, not that it realizes a universal biological mechanism.

\paragraph{Leave-one-codon and module classification.}
The leave-one-codon-out check recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-closure-coverage codons, and $8$ predictable codons, with recovery rate $1.0$ and recovery rate above threshold $1.0$. The module classification accounts for all $13$ codons of $R$: assigned count $13$, no missing module codons, no extra module codons, and no unassigned codons. The classified modules are

Every module has a predictable-or-boundary status in the finite classification. The classification is complete for the observed row, but its scope is exactly the code-label row. It does not identify a protein fold, a structural order, an admissible physical pathway, a biological function, or a general law of genetic-code evolution.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The finite evidence supports the following bounded claim. In the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, the curated standard-code label table exposes a local boundary tile whose distinguished row has exactly the $13$ codons listed above, whose punctured-cube incidence profile is $(13,16,4)$, whose spectral data satisfy the recorded cubic relation to tolerance, and whose leave-one tests recover the row structure at high finite rates. The cannot-claim boundary is part of the result: the same evidence does not establish translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
