\subsection{Window-six codon boundary row}\label{sec:bioreality-codon-window-six-boundary}\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This row records a finite code-layer contact between a six-bit codon display and the curated standard-code label table.  The carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, read through a local tile face, a singleton label corner, and a three-point label class.  The biological contact used here is only the curated assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  It is not a translation machine, not a folding observation, not a physical-admissibility assay, and not a function assay.  The BEDC-side readback therefore has the following local form: a coordinate system on $Q_6$ exposes a distinguished boundary set $R$, and the external table supplies the code labels against which that boundary set is read.

\paragraph{The thirteen-codon boundary set.}
The finite row gives an exact boundary cardinality.  The distinguished set $R$ has size $13$ and is the following codon list:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Equivalently, the row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same thirteen codons are reported by the punctured-cube check, with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the object being claimed is not an indefinite pattern in the genetic code.  It is the concrete finite row consisting of these thirteen codons, sixteen local one-dimensional incidences, and four local two-dimensional faces inside the window-six display.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite null rows.}
Against the recorded shape null, the experiment used $N=2000$ distinct samples after $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape match count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-Jaccard count at threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
These values say that the observed local shape is rare in that finite sampling regime.  They do not say that the local shape is a law of biology, that organisms are forced to realize it, or that the geometric row by itself explains the origin of the code.

This strengthens the finite-row reading that the code-label split is not a generic accident of the sampled class-preserving null.  It still remains a code-layer statement: the row compares label assignments and finite local geometry, not ribosomal translation, protein folding, chemical admissibility, or biological function.

It does not identify the physical process by which a codon is read in a cell.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite-row verdict is that the standard-code table supplies a reality contact for codon-label readback, and that the window-six coordinate system exposes a thirteen-codon local boundary tile with nontrivial shape, spectrum, null-model, recovery, family, and module evidence.  The admissible claim is only at the code-read layer: curated labels can be compared with the local codon tile.  No translation realization follows from this row.  No folded protein structure follows from this row.  No physical admissibility, biological function, global mechanism, or universality follows from this row.  Any such higher-layer claim would require a separate contact that directly observes that higher layer.
