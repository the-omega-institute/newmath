\subsection{Window-six codon boundary row}\label{sec:bior-h0-r-cardinality-13}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table as a code-layer object. The carrier is the six-coordinate codon cube $Q_6$, read as the finite display of the $64$ codons after each base is represented by two binary coordinates. The claim is not that the cube explains translation. The claim is narrower: within this six-coordinate display, the curated standard-code labels single out a local boundary row $R$ with exactly thirteen codons, and that row has a reproducible tile shape under the reported coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation probes.

\paragraph{Observed row.}
The verified row is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite count is therefore
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| = 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The independent punctured-cube check reports the same codon set and the face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not merely a list of labels: it is also a small cubical subcomplex with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-faces, and four two-faces in the window-six geometry. This is the finite reason for treating the row as a local tile boundary rather than as an arbitrary excerpt from the code table.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
The row shape was compared against $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$. In the main shape test there was exactly one exact-shape match, so the finite-sample exact probability was
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The sampler attempted $57377$ distinct draws and recorded $55377$ duplicate retries, which is important for interpretation: the sample space was not treated as an unlimited stream of unrelated rows, but as a finite class in which duplicate rejection materially constrained the run. A second null preserving amino-acid class labels used the same $N=2000$ and seed $42$. There the exact-shape count was $0$, the observed comparison size was $20$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support the claim that the thirteen-codon row is an unusually constrained local code-table pattern under the tested finite nulls. They do not by themselves establish a biochemical mechanism.

\end{aligned}

Thus the thirteen-codon row is not only a static shape. Under the reported finite omissions, it remains a strong local recovery scaffold for code-label readback.

\paragraph{Reality contact.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table, understood as a codon-to-label table. It supplies the amino-acid or stop labels needed to read the row in code space. It does not supply ribosome dynamics, tRNA abundance, organism-specific expression context, protein folding, thermodynamic admissibility, or phenotypic function. The contact therefore supports the statement that the window-six coordinate system exposes a thirteen-codon local boundary tile in the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The admissible conclusion is the following finite one: in the standard-code table, the codons $AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU, UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA,$ and $UUA$ form a thirteen-element row whose cubical face vector, spectral values, null-model probabilities, leave-out behavior, and complete module assignment are all recorded by finite probes. No claim of translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this record. Any move beyond code-label readback requires a separate contact that directly observes the higher layer being asserted.
