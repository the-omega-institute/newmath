\subsection{Window-six codon boundary record}
\label{sec:bioreality-codon-window-six-boundary}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
The record uses the six-coordinate codon cube as its finite carrier and reads the observed labels only through the curated standard genetic-code table.  The coordinate calculation isolates a boundary set $R$ of size $13$.  In the verified row, the set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not a free biological assertion: it is a finite split inside the codon display, checked against a label table whose observed content is only the assignment of codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  The same row records the punctured-cube profile of this boundary as the $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the numerical claim is simultaneously a cardinality claim and a small face-incidence claim.

\paragraph{Spectral and sampling evidence.}
The associated spectral row gives $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$.  The radial cubic check has residual $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, which is within numerical tolerance for the recorded finite computation.  The shape-comparison experiment used $N=2000$ sampled alternatives with seed $42$; it needed $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and rejected $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only $1$ exact shape match was found, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while $25$ samples reached Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\ge 0.9}=0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed $M$ size is $20$, exact matches are $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed size is $7$, giving lower-tail probability $0.0035$.  These numbers support the claim that the window-six split is not a typical draw from the recorded comparison families, but they do not promote the split to a biological mechanism.

The module classification is complete: all $13$ boundary codons are assigned, with no missing, extra, or unassigned codons.

\paragraph{Module inventory.}
The assigned modules are explicit finite rows.  The $\mathrm{agr}$ module contains $\mathrm{AGA}$ and $\mathrm{AGG}$; $\mathrm{ar\_lys}$ contains $\mathrm{AAA}$; $\mathrm{cun}$ contains $\mathrm{CUA}$, $\mathrm{CUC}$, $\mathrm{CUG}$, and $\mathrm{CUU}$; $\mathrm{ile\_met}$ contains $\mathrm{AUA}$; $\mathrm{ssn}$ contains $\mathrm{UCA}$ and $\mathrm{UUA}$; and $\mathrm{stop\_trp}$ contains $\mathrm{UAA}$, $\mathrm{UAG}$, and $\mathrm{UGA}$.  This inventory is the strongest local form of the claim: every codon in the recorded $R$ set is accounted for by the module partition, and every listed module has either predictable or boundary status in the finite run.

\paragraph{Scope boundary.}
The contact is intentionally narrow.  It permits a code-layer readback of codon labels and a local boundary-tile comparison.  It does not observe translation being realized in a cell, a folded protein structure, physical admissibility of a molecule, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any necessity law for DNA.  Amino-acid label sequences are still label sequences; no structural or functional conclusion follows from this table alone.  Any claim about translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality would require an independent contact at that layer.  In this record, the proper conclusion is that the curated standard-code table aligns with a finite window-six boundary pattern whose $13$ codons, punctured-cube incidence data, spectral values, sampling tail values, leave-out rates, and module partition are all explicitly recorded.
