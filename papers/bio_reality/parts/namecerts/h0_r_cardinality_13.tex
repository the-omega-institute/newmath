\subsection{Window-six boundary row for the standard-code table}\label{sec:biorality-codon-window-six-boundary-row}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This BioReality record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read as a finite display of the $64$ codons.  The external contact is a curated standard-code table assigning each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label.  The contact is therefore a label bridge: it tests codon-to-label readback and local label splits, but it does not test translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA obeys the BEDC geometry as biological necessity.

\paragraph{The thirteen-row boundary set.}
The finite row selected by the local-tile probe is the set $R$ of thirteen codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The observed cardinality statement is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same row is recorded as a punctured-cube tile with face data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the local object is not merely a loose codon list.  It has thirteen vertices, sixteen tile edges, and four square faces in the induced window-six geometry.  The point of the row is boundary detection: the thirteen codons occupy a small, structured subregion of the codon cube, and the label readback can be checked against the curated table at those vertices.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
The unrestricted finite null used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and saw $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sampled table matched the observed shape exactly, and $25$ samples had Jaccard score at least $0.9$.  The corresponding finite-row probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The amino-acid-class-preserving null also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It gave exact-match count $0$, observed $M$-size $20$, and lower-tail count $7$ for the event at or below the observed $M$-size.  Its finite probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class,exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class,lower}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
The available row data therefore says that the local shape is uncommon under these two finite probes.  It does not say that the shape is forced by chemistry, by translation, or by natural selection.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out finite probe evaluated $23$ code tables over $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate in $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The family-level split used $5$ families.  The family hit rates above baseline were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, and $1.0$ for bacterial/plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate families.  These values support a code-read statement: the local row and its boundary information recover many curated label placements under leave-one tests.  They do not supply a separate contact for actual translation dynamics.

\paragraph{Leave-one codon and module split.}
The leave-one-codon-out probe reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  The finite row is also completely assigned by modules.  The module decomposition is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The bookkeeping row is exact: $R$-size $13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no codons missing from the modules, and no unassigned codons.  Thus every codon in the thirteen-row boundary has a module position, and every module entry belongs to the recorded row.

\paragraph{Local verdict and boundary.}
The finite evidence supports a narrow claim: window-six codon coordinates expose a structured local boundary tile for the standard-code label table, with $|R|=13$, punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral values, significant finite-null behavior, and complete module assignment.  The claim remains at the code-read layer.  No translation, folding, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality follows from this row unless a separate external contact supplies that higher-layer evidence.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
