\paragraph{Finite carrier and external contact.}
This BioReality record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard-code table that assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  The finite row under inspection is the local boundary row $R$ selected by the window-six coordinate, closure, spectrum, and relation probes.  In the verified finite row, $R$ has cardinality $13$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the observed finite statement is not a vague geometric impression: the boundary row is a named list of thirteen codons, with the scalar check $|R|=13$.  The associated punctured-cube check returns the same codon list and has face-count vector $(13,16,4)$, recording thirteen selected vertices, sixteen selected local edges, and four selected local square faces in the inspected tile geometry.

\paragraph{Spectral and shape checks.}
The same finite run records a spectral separation between the selected row and its companion mass $M$.  The supplied scalars are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M&=0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R&=0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu&=3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The radial cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, the same value reported for the cubic-polynomial check, so the numerical row is internally consistent to the displayed tolerance.  The shape-significance experiment used $N=2000$ sampled null shapes with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  There was one exact shape match, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, and there were $25$ samples with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  These numbers support the narrower claim that the observed local tile is atypical for the sampled coordinate-preserving null, not the broader claim that the geometry is a biological law.

\paragraph{Amino-acid-class-preserving null.}
A second null keeps amino-acid class information in view.  In that run, $N=2000$, the observed companion size was $|M|=20$, the exact-match count was $0$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  The resulting exact-shape value is $p=0.0$ at the displayed sampling resolution, while the lower-tail value is $0.0035$.  This is stronger evidence that the observed code-label split is not a common draw under that finite null, but it still remains a code-read statement because the only external contact is the curated codon-to-label table.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback behavior.}
The leave-one-code-out check evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M\setminus T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  By family, the above-baseline hit rates were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  The leave-one-codon-out check found no anchors, $5$ boundary codons, $8$ high-coverage closure codons, and $8$ predictable codons, with recovery rate $1.0$.  These finite readback numbers say that the local window-six row is useful for recovering code labels under the tested removals; they do not say that codons are translated, folded, selected, or made functional by this geometry.

The module classification accounts for all thirteen codons in $R$ and introduces no extra module codons.  Its completeness row reports $R\_\mathrm{size}=13$, assigned count $13$, no missing codons, no unassigned codons, and no extra module codons.  The six displayed modules are

Each module has a predictable or boundary status in the supplied row, so the thirteen codons are not merely counted; they are exhausted by named local code-read modules.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The conclusion is deliberately local.  The curated standard-code table supplies a reality contact for codon-label readback and for checking the local boundary-tile split.  It does not supply translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not a folded-structure observation, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Any claim crossing from this code-read row into translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate higher-layer contact with its own finite evidence.
