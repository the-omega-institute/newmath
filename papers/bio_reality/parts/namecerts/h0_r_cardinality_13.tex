Thus the primary finite assertion is not a generic claim about genetic coding. \paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The contact is deliberately narrow.  A curated standard-code table supplies codon-to-amino-acid or codon-to-stop labels, so it can test code readback and local codon-tile label splitting.  It does not observe translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, or a universal mechanism.  The boundary condition is therefore local: the window-six geometry and the thirteen-row tile may organize codon-label data, but no claim about protein realization, folding, structural order, function, physical possibility, or biological universality follows unless a separate contact directly supplies that higher-layer observation.  In particular, amino-acid label-sequence readback remains label readback; it is not evidence of folded protein structure.  The record is aligned at the code layer and blocked for higher biological layers until an independent structure, translation, function, or physical-admissibility contact is attached.
