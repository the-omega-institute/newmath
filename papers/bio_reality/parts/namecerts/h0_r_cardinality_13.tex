\subsection{Window-six codon tile boundary}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer.  The finite carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the usual codon display alphabet $\{U,C,A,G\}^3$.  The external contact is a curated standard genetic-code bridge assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label.  That contact supports codon-label readback and local split checks; it does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.  The BEDC object isolated by the row is a local tile-face boundary inside $Q_6$, together with singleton label corners and three-point label classes.  The distinguished boundary set is
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The finite-row cardinality check gives
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R|=13.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the claim is not that the genetic code is generated by the geometry.  The claim is that, after the curated label table is used as a reality contact, the window-six coordinates expose a thirteen-codon local boundary pattern whose readback remains entirely at the codon-label layer.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same thirteen codons form the reported punctured-cube row.  Its face data are summarized by the finite vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Here the first entry is the number of selected vertices, and the subsequent entries record the row-level edge and tile counts used by the finite shape audit.  The row therefore has enough internal structure to be more than an arbitrary list of codons, but the structure is still a local combinatorial object.  In particular, the punctured-cube description is not a statement about biochemical necessity.  It is a code-layer coordinate statement: these thirteen codons occupy a boundary tile pattern visible in the six-bit display once the external standard-code label table has supplied the labels.

\paragraph{Shape significance under randomization.}
Against the shape null with $N=2000$ sampled rows and seed $42$, the audit recorded $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact-shape count was $1$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap count at Jaccard threshold $0.9$ was $25$, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both values are small at the row scale, so the finite shape is not behaving like a typical sample under this null.  A second amino-acid-class-preserving null used $N=2000$ with the same seed.  It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, and lower-tail count $7$, hence
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
This supports the claim that the local code-read geometry is row-specific relative to the tested nulls.  It still does not identify a mechanism: the p-values compare finite shapes and finite label-preserving samples, not biological implementation pathways.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The supported statement is deliberately narrow.  A window-six codon coordinate system, together with the curated standard-code label table, exposes a local tile-boundary set $R$ of cardinality $13$ with punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, spectral values $\lambda_M=0.6752479308830213$, $\lambda_R=0.4758352843197002$, and $\mu=3.051487585298128$, significant finite-shape behavior under the recorded nulls, high leave-one recovery, and a complete six-module assignment.  The unsupported promotions are equally explicit: no translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or global biological law follows from this row.  Any such promotion would require a separate higher-layer contact.  The present contact is a code-label readback bridge, and the chapter therefore remains a code-read finite-row record.
