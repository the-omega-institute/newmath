\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
This record treats the standard-code codon table only as a finite code-read contact. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated table assigning each codon to an amino-acid or stop label. The row under audit isolates a local boundary set $R$ inside that carrier. The measured boundary cardinality is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| &= 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The thirteen codons are exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a finite-row statement about code labels, singleton label corners, three-point label classes, and local tile faces. It is not a statement that the codon cube is a biological mechanism. The contact supplies codon-to-label readback; it does not by itself supply translation realization, folded protein structure, physical admissibility, function, or universality.
