\subsection{Window-six local codon boundary for a thirteen-row set}
\label{sec:window-six-local-codon-boundary-thirteen-row-set}
\origin{ai}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and readback contact.}
This record concerns the standard-code codon table only at the code-read layer. The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through a curated standard genetic-code table that assigns each codon an amino-acid or stop label. The finite row isolated by the window-six coordinate probe is the set $R$ of thirteen codons
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,{}\\
     &UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\},\\
|R| &= 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The experiment run $5d4a3459997d$ reports both $R\_cardinality\_equals\_13=13$ and $size=13$, with the displayed codon list matching the expected codons exactly. The contact is therefore a finite codon-label readback: it says that these thirteen rows can be named inside the curated standard code, not that the local coordinate pattern is itself a biological law.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
A second finite-row check, run $e19917012c60$, identifies the same thirteen codons as a punctured-cube row. Its reported face-vector data are
$$
\begin{aligned}
f(R) &= (13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This shape description is local. It records a tile-like boundary visible in the six-bit codon display, with singleton-label corners and three-point label classes among the distinctions used by the probe. The statement is not promoted beyond the code table: the observed object is a finite coordinate-and-label pattern inside $Q_6$, contacted through the standard-code table.

\paragraph{Spectral finite-row signature.}
The same row has a numerical spectral signature. Run $1026324eff41$ reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,\\
p_{\mathrm{radial}} &= -6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The cubic polynomial residual and radial polynomial value coincide at approximately $-6.224354365258478\cdot 10^{-12}$, so the reported spectral-radius check is within the finite numerical tolerance of the run. This supports a stable coordinate signature for the observed local row. It does not supply a translation mechanism, a folded structural model, or a physical admissibility principle.

\paragraph{Shape significance against finite nulls.}
The shape was also tested against randomized finite-row baselines. In run $0b267c88f8e4$, with seed $42$, the probe evaluated $N=2000$ samples after $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries. The exact shape was seen once, and high-Jaccard matches with threshold $0.9$ were seen $25$ times:
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{J\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
An amino-acid-class-preserving null, run $e8d5de747a0c$, again used $N=2000$ and seed $42$. It reports observed $M$-size $20$, exact match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape value $0.0$, and lower-tail value $0.0035$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M| &= 20,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers justify treating the row as a non-generic finite coordinate event relative to the stated nulls. They do not justify treating it as a global biological necessity.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback behavior.}
The code-read behavior is stronger than the bare row count. Run $429e6e2c4eb4$ evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ total targets in a leave-one-code-out setting. It reports baseline hit rate $0.19821739130434785$, hit rate in $M-T$ equal to $0.9365079365079365$, and hit rate in $M-T$ plus boundary equal to $0.9841269841269841$. The same value $0.9841269841269841$ is reported as hit rate above baseline. By family, run $7dda978c6c97$ reports $5$ families, with hit-rate-above-baseline values
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{alternative\ yeast} &= 0.8333333333333334,\\
\mathrm{bacterial\ plastid} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{candidate\ other} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{mitochondrial} &= 1.0,\\
\mathrm{nuclear\ ciliate} &= 1.0.
\end{aligned}
$$
The leave-one-codon-out run $e9e1a6e41633$ reports anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure coverage high $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$. These readback data say that the local row is useful for finite label recovery inside the sampled code-table regime.

\paragraph{Module classification of the thirteen rows.}
Run $bbe8a4d0455b$ partitions all thirteen codons of $R$ into six named modules and reports that the classification is complete: $R\_size=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing rows, and no unassigned codons. The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{AGA,AGG\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{AAA\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{AUA\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{UCA,UUA\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{UAA,UAG,UGA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Every module is reported as having a predictable or boundary status. This gives the row an internal module-level account, but the account remains a code-read account. In particular, the stop-tryptophan and isoleucine-methionine labels are labels in the curated table, not observations of molecular realization.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The resulting claim is intentionally narrow. Window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile structure in $Q_6$, and the curated standard-code table supplies the reality contact needed to read the thirteen rows as codon labels. No translation realization, folded protein structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or law of DNA follows from this record. Any such stronger claim requires a separate contact that observes the relevant higher layer. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure, and a local codon tile is not a global biological law.
