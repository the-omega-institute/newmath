\subsection{Window-Six Boundary Tile in the Standard Code}
\label{sec:bior-h0-r-cardinality-thirteen}

\paragraph{Record.}
The finite record concerns the standard-code codon table viewed through a six-coordinate codon display.  The biological contact is deliberately narrow: a curated standard-code table assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or a stop label.  The mathematical object inspected here is not translation and not protein structure; it is the local geometry of the code-label table after codons are placed in a window-six coordinate cube.  In that display the distinguished boundary set $R$ has exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified finite count is $|R|=13$.  The same run reports the size field as $13$, so the cardinality statement and the explicit list agree entry by entry.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The boundary set is not only a thirteen-element selection.  The finite face calculation identifies it as a punctured-cube pattern with face-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $f_0$ is the number of selected codon vertices, $f_1$ the counted local one-dimensional adjacencies, and $f_2$ the counted local tile faces in the induced codon-coordinate complex.  This is the chapter-specific geometric datum: the signal is a local boundary tile inside the codon-coordinate cube, not a claim that the code table is forced by geometry alone.

\paragraph{Spectral certificate.}
The finite spectral run gives three scalar values attached to the same displayed split:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the radial-polynomial residual has the same value.  Thus the numerical certificate is internally consistent at the displayed tolerance: the observed radius field is $0.6752479308830213$, matching the listed $\lambda_M$ value.  These numbers support a reproducible finite-row signature for the coordinate split.  They do not identify a biochemical mechanism.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the sampled comparison family for the $M$-shape calculation, the run used $N=2000$ accepted samples with seed $42$.  It made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  Exactly one sampled table matched the observed shape, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}&=0.0005,\\
p_{J\ge 0.9}&=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-Jaccard count was $25$.  The observed $M$ size supplied by the amino-acid-class-preserving comparison is $20$; under that null model the exact-shape count was $0$ among $2000$ samples, while $7$ samples landed in the lower tail at or below the observed $M$ size.  The reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite-row values make the tile unusual relative to the sampled baselines.  The conclusion is statistical and coordinate-level: the code-label table has a localized pattern that survives the listed null checks.

\paragraph{Predictive rows.}
The leave-one-code-out experiment evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$; the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$; and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  These rows say that the local coordinate rule recovers many held-out label positions in the finite comparison task.  They still remain code-layer rows: successful label recovery is not an observation of a ribosome, a folding pathway, a physically admissible protein, or a biological function.

\paragraph{Codon deletion rows.}
The leave-one-codon-out run reports $8$ predictable codons, $5$ boundary codons, $0$ anchor codons, and $8$ cases with high closure coverage.  The recovery rate is $1.0$, above the stated threshold with value $1.0$.  The module classification accounts for all thirteen codons in $R$: $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, with no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The six modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr}&=\{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys}&=\{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun}&=\{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met}&=\{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn}&=\{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp}&=\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The family-level leave-one-code-out hit rates above baseline are $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast and $1.0$ for each of bacterial plastid, candidate other, mitochondrial, and nuclear ciliate.  The weaker alternative-yeast row is therefore retained as a finite specificity rather than rounded into a universal statement.

\paragraph{Boundary of interpretation.}
The curated table supplies codon-to-label contact only.  From the thirteen-codon boundary set, the punctured-cube face vector, the spectral scalars, the sampled $p$-values, and the held-out recovery rows, one may state that the standard-code label table contains a reproducible local coordinate pattern.  One may not infer that window-six geometry realizes translation, determines folded structure, proves physical admissibility, supplies function, gives a mechanism, or establishes a universal biological law.  Any such promotion would require a separate contact that directly observes the higher layer.  In this chapter the claim stops at code-read structure: a local boundary tile is present in the finite codon-label data, and the evidence is confined to that layer.
