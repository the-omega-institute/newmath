\subsection{Window-Six Boundary Row for the Standard-Code Table}
\label{subsec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite contact.}
The finite object in this record is the window-six codon cube, read only through a curated standard-code label table.  The carrier is the six-bit codon display $Q_6$, with distinctions given by coordinate position, local tile face, singleton label corner, and three-point label class.  The contact supplies a code-layer readback: a codon has an externally curated amino-acid or stop label.  It does not supply translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The verified boundary row is the set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\\
     &\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The row count is exactly $|R|=13$, as recorded by experiment run $5d4a3459997d$.  Thus the claim is not that the standard code is generated by geometry alone.  The claim is only that, after the curated label table is used as a reality bridge, these thirteen codons form the observed boundary row for the local window-six split.

\paragraph{Punctured-cube shape.}
The same thirteen codons are reported in the punctured-cube check, experiment run $e19917012c60$.  Its face-vector is
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This is a local finite-row statement.  The vertices are the thirteen codons in $R$; the sixteen edges and four tile faces are part of the induced local cubical boundary.  The word ``punctured'' is therefore used here in the finite combinatorial sense: the row has cube-like adjacency data but is not promoted to a global biological law.  The result is compatible with the support condition that the curated table checks codon-label readback and local split structure only.

\paragraph{Spectral readback.}

\begin{aligned}

\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128.
\end{aligned}
$$
The reported cubic, equivalently radial, polynomial residual is $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$, and the spectral-radius-in-tolerance value is $0.6752479308830213$.  These numbers give a numerical fingerprint of the observed local split.  They do not make the split causal, biochemical, or universal; they only say that the finite coordinate-and-relation object has a stable spectral signature under the stated computation.

\paragraph{Null comparison.}
The shape-significance run $0b267c88f8e4$ used $N=2000$ samples with seed $42$.  It required $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape appeared once, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while $25$ samples reached Jaccard score at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  The amino-acid-class-preserving null run $e8d5de747a0c$ also used $N=2000$ and seed $42$.  It recorded observed $M$-size $20$, exact-match count $0$, lower-tail count $7$, exact-shape $p$-value $0.0$, and lower-tail $p$-value $0.0035$.  These finite comparisons support the statement that the observed local row is not a common draw under the tested null models.  They do not replace the curated table: the labels remain external contacts, not internal consequences of the cube.

The hit rate in $M\setminus T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate in $M\setminus T$ plus boundary was again $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out run $e9e1a6e41633$ found anchor count $0$, boundary count $5$, closure-coverage-high count $8$, predictable count $8$, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These recovery checks are code-layer diagnostics.  They show that the local finite row has predictive pressure for label readback under the stated leave-one protocols, but they do not show that a ribosome realizes the geometry or that any amino-acid sequence folds.
