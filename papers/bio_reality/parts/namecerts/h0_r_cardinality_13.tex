\subsection{Window-six boundary row for the standard-code codon table}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier in this record is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the standard four-letter codon display.  The biological contact is only the curated standard-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid label or to a stop label.  This contact is a code-read contact.  It is not a measurement of translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality.  The local question is therefore deliberately narrow: whether the window-six coordinate display isolates a distinguished local boundary row inside the standard-code label table.

\paragraph{The thirteen-codon row.}
The finite row $R$ has cardinality $13$.  The observed row is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\\
&\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the row-count assertion is
$$
\begin{aligned}
|R| = 13.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same finite set is also the punctured-cube row reported by the cubical check, with face-vector data
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4).
\end{aligned}
$$
This says that the row is not merely a list of thirteen labels: in the window-six coordinate geometry it carries a local tile-face profile with thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional edges, and four two-dimensional faces.

\paragraph{Shape significance.}
Against the shape null with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, the run made $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and incurred $55377$ duplicate retries.  It found $1$ exact shape match and $25$ matches with Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$.  The corresponding reported probabilities are
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}} &= 0.0005,\\
p_{\mathrm{Jaccard}\geq 0.9} &= 0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
For the amino-acid-class-preserving null, again with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the exact-shape count was $0$, while the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$.  With observed $M$ size $20$, the reported probabilities were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}} &= 0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}} &= 0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These numbers support a finite local irregularity statement for the code-read table.  They do not convert the tile into a biological law.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback.}
The leave-one-code-out check evaluated $23$ tables and $63$ targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$.  The hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$, the hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ plus boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out check reported $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ high-closure-coverage cases, $8$ predictable cases, and recovery rate $1.0$.  Thus the row is useful for local code-label recovery within this finite table experiment, especially when the boundary layer is admitted.

\paragraph{Family and module partition.}
The family-level leave-one-code-out summary used $5$ families.  The hit rates above baseline were $1.0$ for bacterial-plastid, mitochondrial, nuclear-ciliate, and candidate-other families, and $0.8333333333333334$ for the alternative-yeast family.  The module classification assigns all thirteen codons of $R$ with no extras, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons:

\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\

The recorded completeness values are $R$ size $13$, assigned count $13$, empty extra-module list, empty missing-from-modules list, and empty unassigned-codon list.  This is the finite-row reason for treating $R$ as a closed local code-read boundary packet rather than as a loose collection of exceptional codons.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The record is ready at the code-read layer.  The data support the statement that window-six codon coordinates expose a local boundary-tile row in the curated standard-code table, with $|R|=13$, punctured-cube face vector $(13,16,4)$, nontrivial spectral values, small null probabilities, strong leave-one recovery, and a complete six-module classification.  The record cannot claim that this local tile is translation, folding, structural order, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or a universal biological law.  Any such promotion requires a separate contact whose observed fact lies at that higher layer.
