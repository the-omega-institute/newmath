\subsection{The thirteen-codon boundary row in the window-six code readback}
\label{sec:bioreality-h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite carrier and contact.}
The finite carrier is the window-six codon cube $Q_6$, read as a six-bit display of the sixty-four standard-code codons.  The only external contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns each codon to an amino-acid or stop label, and it supplies code-layer labels only.  The row under audit is the boundary set $R$ selected by the local tile computation.  In the verified finite row, $R$ has exactly thirteen codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
R = \{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\\
&\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UUA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the cardinality claim is not an asymptotic or symbolic slogan: the realized finite row is $|R|=13$, with experiment run $5\mathrm{d}4\mathrm{a}3459997\mathrm{d}$ verifying both the size and the codon membership.  The same row is also certified as a punctured-cube boundary with $f$-vector $(13,16,4)$, so the record contains thirteen vertices, sixteen local one-dimensional adjacencies, and four local two-dimensional faces inside the selected tile geometry.

\paragraph{Shape and spectral finite-row evidence.}
The companion shape audit places this row against randomized alternatives rather than treating the tile as self-evident.  In the shape-significance run, $N=2000$ distinct samples were requested, requiring $57377$ sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  Only one sample matched the exact observed shape, giving $p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005$, while $25$ samples reached Jaccard similarity at least $0.9$, giving $p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125$.  Under the amino-acid-class-preserving null, the observed module size was $|M|=20$; the exact-shape match count was $0$ out of $2000$, and the lower-tail count at or below the observed $M$ size was $7$, giving $p_{\mathrm{lower\ tail}}=0.0035$.  These numbers support a finite local shape departure from the tested nulls, not a biological necessity theorem.

The spectral row is also finite and numeric.  The recorded values are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\lambda_M &= 0.6752479308830213,\\
\lambda_R &= 0.4758352843197002,\\
\mu &= 3.051487585298128,
\end{aligned}
$$
with the cubic or radial polynomial residual equal to $-6.224354365258478\times 10^{-12}$.  This residual is a numerical tolerance witness for the row computation.  It does not identify a mechanism of translation, nor does it say that the codon cube is a physical law of genomes.

\paragraph{Leave-one readback tests.}
The code-level readback tests are stronger than mere restatement of the curated table, but they remain code-level.  In the leave-one-code-out audit, $23$ tables and $63$ targets were evaluated.  The baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the rate inside $M-T$ plus the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  In the leave-one-codon-out audit, the anchor count was $0$, the boundary count was $5$, the predictable count was $8$, the high-coverage closure count was $8$, and the recovery rate was $1.0$.  These data say that the finite tile and its boundary recover code-label behavior in the tested windows; they do not say that the recovered amino-acid label sequence is a folded protein, a viable molecule, or a biological function.

\paragraph{Module-level classification.}
The thirteen codons in $R$ are completely accounted for by the verified module partition.  The module classifier records $R_{\mathrm{size}}=13$, assigned count $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The modules are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{agr} &= \{\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG}\},\\
\mathrm{ar\_lys} &= \{\mathrm{AAA}\},\\
\mathrm{cun} &= \{\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU}\},\\
\mathrm{ile\_met} &= \{\mathrm{AUA}\},\\
\mathrm{ssn} &= \{\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UUA}\},\\
\mathrm{stop\_trp} &= \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
At the family level, five families were evaluated.  The hit-rate-above-baseline values were $0.8333333333333334$ for alternative yeast, $1.0$ for bacterial plastid, $1.0$ for candidate other, $1.0$ for mitochondrial, and $1.0$ for nuclear ciliate.  The family data therefore support a stable local code-read signal across the tested family grouping, with alternative yeast being the visible non-perfect family row.

\paragraph{Boundary of the claim.}
The ready claim is exactly this: in the window-six codon display, the curated standard-code labels expose a thirteen-codon local boundary row $R$ with punctured-cube shape, significant finite-row departures from the tested nulls, a stable spectral signature, complete module accounting, and strong code-label recovery in leave-one tests.  The claim stops at $\mathrm{code\_read}$.  No translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or genomic necessity follows from these rows.  A separate contact would be required before any claim crosses from codon-label readback into translation, structure, physical realization, function, mechanism, or universality.
