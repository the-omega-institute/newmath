\paragraph{Finite carrier and reality contact.}

The carrier is the six-bit codon cube $Q_6$, read through the codon display whose alphabetic faces are the usual triples over $\{\mathrm{A},\mathrm{C},\mathrm{G},\mathrm{U}\}$.  The external contact is the curated standard-code label table: each codon is assigned an amino-acid or stop label, and that assignment is the only biological fact imported by the row.  The BEDC object tested here is a local tile boundary inside the six-bit coordinate cube, with distinctions for coordinate, closure, spectrum, relation, singleton label corner, and three-point label class.  Its readback is therefore only a standard-code label readback at the $\mathrm{code\_read}$ layer.

\paragraph{Cannot-claim boundary.}
The aligned contact supports only codon-label readback and local split checks.  A local codon tile is not a global biological law.  Window-six geometry alone is not translation, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or evolutionary necessity.  Amino-acid label-sequence readback is not folded protein structure.  Any claim that crosses from the code table to translation realization, protein structure, physical admissibility, biological function, mechanism, or universality requires a separate reality contact designed for that higher layer.  In the absence of such a contact, the correct conclusion is the finite one: the standard-code table contains a verified thirteen-codon boundary row in $Q_6$ with non-generic local shape statistics and strong code-layer recovery behavior.
