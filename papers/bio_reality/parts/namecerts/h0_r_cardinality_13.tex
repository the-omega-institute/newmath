\subsection{The Thirteen-Codon Boundary Row in the Standard Code}
\label{sec:h0-r-cardinality-13}

\paragraph{Finite object.}
The record considered here is the window-six codon display of the standard genetic code, read only at the level of codon labels.  The finite carrier is the codon cube $Q_6$, with coordinates supplied by the six-bit codon representation and labels supplied by the curated standard-code table.  In this row the distinguished boundary set is not inferred from translation chemistry, protein structure, or organismal function.  It is the finite set
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA, AGA, AGG, AUA, CUA, CUC, CUG, CUU,\\
&UAA, UAG, UCA, UGA, UUA\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The verified cardinality row gives $|R|=13$, with the same thirteen displayed codons recorded as the expected codons for the row.  Thus the first finite claim is a literal counting statement: the boundary row contains thirteen codons, and the listed codons exhaust it.

\paragraph{Local shape.}
The same thirteen codons also appear in the punctured-cube row.  Its recorded face data have $f$-vector
$$
\begin{aligned}
(f_0,f_1,f_2)=(13,16,4),
\end{aligned}
$$
so the row is not merely a cardinality class.  It carries a small local tile shape: thirteen vertices, sixteen one-dimensional incidences, and four two-dimensional faces inside the window-six cube.  The point of the row is therefore local and combinatorial.  It says that the standard-code label table exposes a specific boundary tile inside $Q_6$; it does not say that the tile is a law governing all biological realization.

\paragraph{Shape rarity under finite null models.}
Two finite null rows test whether the observed local pattern is easily reproduced by label-preserving rearrangements.  In the unrestricted shape row with $N=2000$ samples and seed $42$, there were $57377$ distinct-sample attempts and $55377$ duplicate retries.  The exact shape appeared once, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{exact}}=0.0005.
\end{aligned}
$$
The high-overlap criterion $J\geq 0.9$ appeared $25$ times, giving
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{J\geq 0.9}=0.0125.
\end{aligned}
$$
In the amino-acid-class-preserving row, also with $N=2000$ and seed $42$, the observed companion set size was $20$.  The exact-shape count was $0$, the lower-tail count at or below the observed size was $7$, and the recorded values were
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ exact}}&=0.0,\\
p_{\mathrm{aa\ class\ lower\ tail}}&=0.0035.
\end{aligned}
$$
These finite comparisons support the claim that the displayed row is not a routine artifact of the tested randomizations, while still leaving the interpretation at the code-label layer.

\paragraph{Leave-one tests.}
The leave-one-code-out row evaluated $23$ code tables and $63$ total targets.  Its baseline hit rate was $0.19821739130434785$, while the hit rate above baseline was $0.9841269841269841$.  The hit rate inside $M-T$ was $0.9365079365079365$, and the hit rate inside $M-T$ together with the boundary was $0.9841269841269841$.  The leave-one-codon-out row recorded $0$ anchors, $5$ boundary cases, $8$ predictable cases at high closure coverage, and recovery rate $1.0$.  These rows are useful because they test finite recovery behavior after deleting information.  They do not upgrade codon-label recovery into translation dynamics, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, a mechanism, or a universal biological principle.

The module row partitions all thirteen codons with no missing or extra entries: assigned count $13$, $R$ size $13$, no extra module codons, no missing codons, and no unassigned codons.  The recorded modules are

Every module has either a predictable or boundary status in the finite row.  This is a complete bookkeeping statement about the thirteen displayed codons, not a claim about protein folding or cellular execution.

\paragraph{Contact and limitation.}
The reality contact used here is the curated standard genetic-code table: it assigns codons to amino-acid or stop labels.  That contact is adequate for code-layer readback and for checking the local split of labels on the window-six tile.  It is not a contact with ribosomal translation, molecular structure, energetic feasibility, organismal function, or universality across biology.  Consequently the row supports the following bounded conclusion: the standard-code label table contains a thirteen-codon boundary set with the listed codons, the punctured-cube face data $(13,16,4)$, the recorded spectral values, rare finite-null behavior, and complete module classification.  Any claim about translation realization, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universal law requires a separate observation designed for that higher layer.
