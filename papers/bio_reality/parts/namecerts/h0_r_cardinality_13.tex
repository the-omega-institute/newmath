\paragraph{Reality contact and boundary.}
The only reality contact used here is the curated standard-code table assigning codons to amino-acid or stop labels. That contact can test code readback and local codon-tile label splits. It cannot test translation realization, folded structure, structural order, physical admissibility, biological function, universal mechanism, or any claim that DNA obeys this geometry as biological necessity. Amino-acid label-sequence readback is therefore not folded protein structure. A higher-layer statement would require a separate contact: for example, a translation experiment for translation, a structure experiment or structure-prediction contact for folding, or an explicit functional assay for biological function. Without such a contact, the correct verdict is only the finite code-read statement: the six-bit codon display exposes a reproducible local boundary tile with thirteen codons, non-generic sampled shape statistics, stable spectral scalars, strong leave-one-code and leave-one-codon readback, and a complete six-module partition of $R$.
