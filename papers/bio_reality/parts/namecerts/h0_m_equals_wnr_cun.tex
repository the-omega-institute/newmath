\paragraph{Finite codon packet.}
The finite row under study is the codon packet whose primary enumeration verifies the identity between $M$ and the union of the WNR and CUN-selected rows. The checked carrier contains exactly twenty RNA codons:
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&AAA,AAG,ACA,ACG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,\\
& CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The recorded cardinality is $|M|=20$, with the same value reported as the finite size of the packet. This is only an enumerated codon set. It does not by itself assert that any member is transcribed, translated, selected, folded, functional, or physically realized in an organism.

\paragraph{Motif compression row.}
A second finite check records a smaller motif union $R$ inside the same codon alphabet:

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}

This row has thirteen listed codons and the mismatch ledger reports no missing entries and no extra entries for the claimed motif compression. Thus the compression statement is exact at the finite-table level: the row named $R$ is neither enlarged nor diminished relative to the listed motif union. The statement remains a combinatorial compression of codon symbols, not an observation of a genomic interval.

\paragraph{Saturation and boundary codons.}
The third finite operation records that the saturated image of $R$ under the $f_3$ operation reaches all of $M$ except the two codons $ACA$ and $ACG$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\operatorname{Sat}_{f_3}(R)=\{&AAA,AAG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same run records $M=\operatorname{Med}(R)\cup\{ACA,ACG\}$ and an empty $f_3$ boundary obstruction list. This is a finite closure fact about codon rows. The two distinguished codons $ACA$ and $ACG$ mark the difference between the saturated row and the full twenty-element packet; they do not license a biological conclusion about reading frames or protein production.

\paragraph{ORF eligibility contact boundary.}
The biological layer attached to this packet is $orf\_eligibility$. The seed statement is intentionally conservative: start--stop enumeration can define what would have to be tested, but an eligibility claim requires an external interval contact. The standalone start--stop-window run loaded one curated nucleotide-window manifest, reported $window\_count=1$, and classified all input windows by start--stop enumeration with $(classified,input\_windows)=(1,1)$. Its scope is explicitly limited to external-window start--stop enumeration, and its classification field records $orf\_eligibility\_only$. Therefore the finite geometry of $M$, $R$, saturation, and quotient rows is not promoted to translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

\paragraph{Curated interval contact.}

It loads $168$ rows from a curated ORF interval dataset based on NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for the Escherichia coli K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession $NC\_000913.3$, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$. The required input classes are present: boundary cases, matched non-ORF decoys, and positive curated ORFs. The positive interval statistic reports $72$ accepted positives out of $72$, so the accept rate is $1.0$, with one-sided binomial upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. The matched decoy statistic reports $72$ rejected decoys out of $72$, so the reject rate is $1.0$, with the same upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$. The predeclared pairwise comparison records $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, again with upper-tail value $2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}$.

\paragraph{Local verdict.}
The chapter records two distinct levels. At the codon-row level, the finite packet verifies $M=WNR\cup CUN$, $|M|=20$, the thirteen-row motif compression $R$, the $f_3$ saturation row missing only $ACA$ and $ACG$, and the quotient vectors $(20,38,26,8,1)$ and $(10,14,6,1)$. At the biological-contact level, ORF eligibility becomes reportable only after curated interval evidence is attached, with $168$ dataset rows and the exact positive, decoy, pairwise, held-out, and boundary-control counts listed above. The self-check records three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is carried across biological layers by itself. The local cannot-claim boundary is therefore part of the result: no translation realization, folded structure, physical admissibility, biological function, molecular mechanism, or universal biological law follows from this packet unless an additional contact supplies evidence for that stronger layer.
