It says that a start/stop-window enumeration has selected exactly these twenty codon rows. It does not say that any sequence interval has been observed, transcribed, translated, folded, physically admitted, functionalized, mechanized, or made universal.

\paragraph{Boundary verdict.}
The self-check row records the boundary statement: BioReality performs only a self-check here, and no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. The checked gate count is $3$. The two local probes say the same thing in operational form. First, an ORF-eligibility seed must remain at boundary status unless a separate sequence, transcript, or curated-annotation contact supplies an observed biological interval. Second, the packet may not record an ORF-eligibility claim while no sequence database, transcript evidence, or curated interval annotation can test the proposed interval. In the present finite data there are no attached reality contacts and no mismatch rows. Therefore the admissible conclusion is exactly this: the twenty-row set $M$, the thirteen-row motif set $R$, the eighteen-row saturation list, and the ten-point quotient geometry form an internally checked boundary packet for what would have to be tested. They do not establish ORF eligibility as a biological fact. They also do not establish translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality. Any such promotion requires a separate contact, such as a curated ORF interval annotation, and that contact is absent from this packet.
