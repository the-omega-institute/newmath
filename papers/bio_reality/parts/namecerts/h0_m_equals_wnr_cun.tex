\paragraph{Finite coordinate under test.}
The record considered here is the finite codon-level assertion indexed by $h0.M.equals.WNR.CUN$.  Its audited content is not a biological realization claim.  It is a finite equality statement about a named codon inventory $M$ and the union inventory denoted $WNR\cup CUN$.  The verified row reports
$$
\begin{aligned}
|M| &= 20,\\
M &= \{AAA,AAG,ACA,ACG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,\\
&\qquad UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the whole finite carrier of the present coordinate: twenty codon strings, each of length three over the RNA alphabet appearing in the row.  The readout supplied by this equality is therefore combinatorial.  It says which triples are present in the inventory and how many there are; it does not say that any triple has been used by a cell, transcribed in a sample, translated by a ribosome, folded into a protein, or selected for a function.

with no missing entries and no extra entries.  A separate saturation row $h0.f3.saturation$ states that applying the recorded $f_3$ saturation to $R$ gives

&UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.

The saturation row also records an empty $f_3$ boundary list and then recovers $M$ after adjoining $ACA$ and $ACG$ to the saturated inventory.  Thus the finite relationship being certified is the following: the twenty-member $M$ inventory is obtained from a saturated eighteen-member subinventory together with the two codons $ACA$ and $ACG$.  This is a codon-set fact only.  It is useful because it fixes exactly which start--stop-window candidates a later biological contact may query, but it is not itself that contact.

\paragraph{Boundary status before biological contact.}
The conjecture attached to this coordinate is an ORF-eligibility seed.  Its minimal biological content is intentionally empty at the level of realized biology: the carrier, distinctions, and readback remain awaiting contact with a curated interval, sequence record, transcript record, or another observation that can test an ORF interval.  The two probes say this locally.  First, a start--stop-window seed must remain at boundary status unless an outside interval contact supplies the observed biological interval.  Second, the record fails if it asserts ORF eligibility while no sequence database entry, transcript evidence, or curated annotation can test the proposed interval.  Consequently, the finite codon geometry may suggest a question, but it may not answer the biological question by itself.

\paragraph{Curated interval contact.}
The stronger contact row is $h2.orf\_eligibility.curated\_interval\_contact$.  It uses a curated interval dataset from NCBI RefSeq GenBank XML CDS intervals for \emph{Escherichia coli} K-12 MG1655 complete genome accession $NC\_000913.3$, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$30$.  The dataset contains $168$ rows and includes the required input classes $boundary\_case$, $matched\_non\_orf\_decoy$, and $positive\_curated\_orf$.  The protocol was frozen before the outside observation: classifier rules, input classes, baselines, and held-out splits were fixed at $2026$-$05$-$29T19:11:13+00:00$, while the external evidence was observed at $2026$-$05$-$30T10:29:31+00:00$.  The classifier identifier is $orf\_start\_stop\_window\_eligibility$, and the row records that classifier rules were not modified.

The positive curated intervals and matched decoys give the same exact one-sided binomial tail value.  For positives, $72$ of $72$ were accepted, with accept rate $1.0$ and
$$
\begin{aligned}
p=2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
For matched non-ORF decoys, $72$ of $72$ were rejected, with reject rate $1.0$ and the same tail probability
$$
\begin{aligned}
p=2.117582368135751\times 10^{-22}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The predeclared paired baseline comparison also reports $72$ wins in $72$ pairs, win rate $1.0$, and the same one-sided binomial tail probability.  Boundary cases are not promoted: $24$ boundary cases were tested, $0$ were promoted, and the promoted fraction was $0.0$.

\paragraph{Held-out stability.}
The curated interval row further records three held-out splits.  The left-locus split has $24$ positives accepted at rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys rejected at rate $1.0$.  The middle-locus split has the same counts and rates.  The right-locus split also has $24$ positives accepted at rate $1.0$ and $24$ decoys rejected at rate $1.0$.  All three splits passed.  These finite rows support a scoped statement: within this curated interval dataset and its matched controls, the frozen start--stop-window classifier separates the listed positive curated ORF intervals from the listed decoys and refuses to promote the listed boundary cases.

\paragraph{Local limit of the claim.}
The self-check row records $checked\_gate\_count=3$ and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  This limit is part of the mathematical content of the chapter.  The $M=WNR\cup CUN$ equality, the motif compression row, the saturation row, the quotient geometry row, the one-window enumeration row, and the curated interval contact together support only a scoped ORF-eligibility contact statement for the tested finite records.  They do not establish that translation occurred. Without that separate evidence, the present result remains a finite codon-inventory and curated-interval eligibility record, not a completed biological mechanism claim.
