\subsection{ORF-Eligibility Boundary from the WNR--CUN Codon Packet}

\paragraph{Finite codon support.}
The record attached to claim $h0.M.equals.WNR.CUN$ is a finite codon packet, not an observation of translation.  Its checked base set $M$ has cardinality $20$ and is exactly
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&AAA,AAG,ACA,ACG,AGA,AGG,AUA,AUG,CUA,CUC,\\
& CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The motif-compressed support $R$ is the $13$-element subset
$$
\begin{aligned}
R=\{&AAA,AGA,AGG,AUA,CUA,CUC,CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UGA,UUA\},
\end{aligned}
$$
with no recorded missing entries and no recorded extra entries relative to the motif union.  The finite-row equality $M=WNR\cup CUN$ therefore supplies only an enumerated codon boundary: it says which triplets are in the formal packet, not whether any nucleotide interval in a cell is transcribed, translated, folded, physically admissible, functional, mechanistic, or universal.

\paragraph{Saturation and quotient geometry.}
The saturation check records
$$
\begin{aligned}

&UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\},
\end{aligned}
$$
so the $f3$-saturated packet contains $18$ entries and omits $ACA$ and $ACG$ from $M$.  Adding the mediated contribution with $ACA$ restores the same $20$-element $M$, while the recorded $f3$ boundary is empty.  The quotient-geometric audit gives the $M$ face vector $(20,38,26,8,1)$ and a quotient face vector $(10,14,6,1)$, with quotient size $10$.  The quotient rows are

These numbers describe a finite combinatorial configuration.  They do not by themselves name a genomic locus, a transcript, a ribosome event, a peptide product, or a selected phenotype.

\paragraph{ORF eligibility as a contact-limited layer.}
The biological object considered here is open-reading-frame eligibility from start/stop codon enumeration.  At the seed boundary, the carrier, distinctions, and readback are marked as awaiting contact with biological records, with no internal structure beyond the finite codon enumeration.  The direct window-enumeration check loaded one external nucleotide-window record, classified all $1$ input window by start/stop enumeration, and explicitly kept the scope at $orf\_eligibility\_only$.  Its source was a curated finite nucleotide-window manifest dated $2026$-$05$-$25$; this source is an annotation input to the experiment rather than content derived from the codon geometry.  Thus the admissible reading is narrow: a start/stop window may be sorted as an eligibility window only when a separate sequence, transcript, or curated annotation supplies the interval being tested.

The analysis protocol was recorded as fixed before the external observation: classifier rules, input classes, baselines, and held-out splits were all frozen, with protocol time $2026$-$05$-$29T19:11:13+00:00$ and external evidence observation time $2026$-$05$-$30T10:29:31+00:00$. \paragraph{Local interpretation boundary.}
The evidence supports only a contact-conditioned ORF-eligibility statement for the finite curated interval setting just described.  It does not establish translation, peptide production, folded structure, physical admissibility, molecular mechanism, biological function, genome-wide universality, or any law beyond the loaded interval contact.  The matched decoy and boundary controls also do not classify arbitrary windows outside the dataset.  The self-check records three checked gates and states the same limitation: no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  Therefore the local claim is that the finite $M$ packet, together with the curated interval contact, can serve as an audited ORF-eligibility test for this row set; without such contact it remains only a proposed boundary condition waiting for biological measurement.
