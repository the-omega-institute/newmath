\subsection{ORF-window eligibility as a contact-limited finite record}

\paragraph{Finite codon support.}
The record begins with a finite codon set rather than with an observed gene, transcript, protein, or organism-level phenotype.  The verified row named $h0.M.equals.WNR.CUN$ reports that the carrier set $M$ has cardinality $20$ and is exactly the following union of codon tokens:
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\{&\mathrm{AAA},\mathrm{AAG},\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG},\mathrm{AGA},\mathrm{AGG},\mathrm{AUA},\mathrm{AUG},\mathrm{CUA},\mathrm{CUC},\\
&\mathrm{CUG},\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
This is the complete finite support available to the present packet.  It is already enough to state a precise combinatorial fact: $M$ contains $20$ codon strings, and no codon outside the displayed list is part of the certified set for this row.  It is not enough to say that any displayed codon has been observed in an annotated open reading frame, translated by a ribosome, selected for a biological role, or embedded in a physical molecular context.

It only says that this particular finite codon list admits the recorded motif description without surplus or deficit in the row-level audit.

&\mathrm{CUU},\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UCA},\mathrm{UCG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG},\mathrm{UUA},\mathrm{UUG}\}.

$$
The same row reports that adding the two codons $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$ recovers the full $20$-element set $M$.  In finite-row form,
$$
\begin{aligned}
M=\operatorname{Med}(R)\cup\{\mathrm{ACA},\mathrm{ACG}\},\qquad
f3\text{-boundary}=\varnothing.
\end{aligned}
$$
Thus the present evidence distinguishes an $18$-codon saturated part from the two recorded boundary additions $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$.  This is a statement about a certified enumeration.  It is not an assertion that either boundary codon is a start, stop, splice, regulatory, structural, or functional signal in an actual biological interval.

\paragraph{ORF eligibility scope.}
The only biological-layer row present here is $h2.orf.start\_stop.window\_eligibility$.  It reports one external nucleotide-window manifest, one input window, and one classified window.  Its scope is explicitly limited to external-window start/stop enumeration, with snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$25$.  The row also records the local status string $orf\_eligibility\_only$, which prevents the finite codon geometry from being promoted to translation realization.  In symbols, the available count data are
$$
\begin{aligned}
\#\mathrm{loaded\ manifests}=1,\qquad
\#\mathrm{input\ windows}=1,\qquad
\#\mathrm{classified\ windows}=1.
\end{aligned}
$$
This gives a narrow eligibility check for a curated finite window manifest.  It does not by itself certify that an open reading frame is biologically present in a genome or transcript.  Such a certification would require a separate interval annotation, sequence record, transcript record, or comparable curated contact able to test the proposed interval.

\paragraph{Local boundary.}
The self-check row records $3$ checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  The present chapter therefore has a precise negative content as well as a positive finite enumeration.  Positively, it records the $20$-element set $M$, the $13$-element motif set $R$, the $18$-element saturation output, the two codon additions $\mathrm{ACA}$ and $\mathrm{ACG}$ needed to recover $M$, the quotient size $10$, the vectors $(20,38,26,8,1)$ and $(10,14,6,1)$, and a one-window start/stop classification input.  Negatively, it does not establish translation, protein folding, molecular stability, physical admissibility, biochemical activity, organismal function, mechanism, or universality.  Any such statement must come from a distinct biological contact that supplies the missing observation and is auditable independently of the finite codon enumeration.
