\paragraph{ORF-eligibility boundary.}
The biological object attached to this packet is open-reading-frame eligibility from start and stop codon enumeration. The row is intentionally conservative: a start/stop-window condition may define what has to be tested, but it is not itself an observed biological interval. The self-check reports $3$ checked gates and explicitly records that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers. Consequently, the local rule is as follows. A finite start/stop window may be treated as an eligibility question only after an independent biological contact supplies the interval being tested. Without that contact, the packet remains a boundary record: it asks whether a separate sequence, transcript, or curated annotation can support an ORF-eligibility assertion, and it refuses to convert the codon coordinate into that assertion.

\paragraph{Local conclusion.}
The finite-row conclusion is therefore narrow. The codon packet has a verified $20$-element coordinate, a $13$-element motif-compressed subpacket, an $18$-element $f3$-saturation output excluding $ACA$ and $ACG$, and a $10$-point quotient image with the stated face vectors. The biological contact rows show that, when a separate curated interval dataset is supplied, the frozen start/stop-window classifier separates the provided positive intervals, matched decoys, and boundary cases in the reported finite sample. The conclusion stops there. No translation event, folded structure, physical admissibility, cellular function, molecular mechanism, or universal biological law follows from $M$, from $R$, from the quotient geometry, or from start/stop enumeration alone. Any such stronger layer would require its own independent contact and its own finite audit.
