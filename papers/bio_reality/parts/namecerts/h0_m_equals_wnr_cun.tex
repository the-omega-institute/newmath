\paragraph{Finite codon support.}

\paragraph{Boundary rule for ORF eligibility.}
The packet is attached to the biological question of start--stop open-reading-frame eligibility.  Its local rule is negative unless a separate biological contact is supplied: a start/stop-window enumeration may define what would be tested, but it does not itself certify that the window is present in an annotated sequence, transcribed, translated, structurally realized, physically admissible, functional, mechanistic, or universal.  The self-check row records three checked gates and states that no codon geometry is promoted across biological layers.  Accordingly, the finite codon packet can serve as a predeclared boundary probe for ORF eligibility, but not as an ORF claim in isolation.
