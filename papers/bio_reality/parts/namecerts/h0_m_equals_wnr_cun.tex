& CUG,CUU,UAA,UAG,UCA,UCG,UGA,UGG,UUA,UUG\}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same packet also records a motif-compression row for $R$, with no missing and no extra elements relative to its motif union:

\begin{aligned}

\end{aligned}

The difference between these two finite lists is not treated as biology.  It is only a finite syntactic coordinate from which a possible biological question can be posed.  In particular, membership in $M$ is not, by itself, an observed transcript, an annotated coding interval, a translation event, a folded protein, a physical model, a cellular role, or a law about genomes.

\paragraph{Boundary status before contact.}
The seed form for the ORF-eligibility layer begins with no biological carrier beyond an awaiting-contact state.  Its distinctions are likewise awaiting contact, and its internal biological structure is absent.  This is not a defect in the packet; it is the point of the boundary test.  A coordinate may be mathematically exact while still having no observed sequence interval to which the ORF question can attach.  Therefore, before contact, the admissible statement is only that the packet identifies a question of the following kind: if a curated sequence, transcript record, or annotation supplies an interval, then one may test whether that interval satisfies the declared start--stop enumeration rule.  Without such an interval, the packet remains silent about ORF eligibility.

A separate finite window row records one externally supplied nucleotide-window input under the scope of start--stop enumeration only.  The row reports that the outside window dataset loaded successfully, with $1$ input window and $1$ classified window, using the snapshot date $2026$-$05$-$25$. This row is enough to show how an interval contact can activate the eligibility test, but it is not enough to infer translation, folding, physical admissibility, function, mechanism, or universality.

The protocol row states that classifier rules, input classes, held-out splits, and baselines were fixed before the outside evidence was observed; the protocol time is $2026$-$05$-$29T19:11:13+00:00$, and the outside evidence time is $2026$-$05$-$30T10:29:31+00:00$.

\paragraph{Local exclusion boundary.}
The conclusion is intentionally narrow.  The $M=WNR\cup CUN$ coordinate, its $R$ motif row, its saturation row, and its quotient geometry supply a finite mathematical packet.  The curated interval rows supply a separate biological contact that tests start--stop ORF eligibility on a fixed external dataset.  The combined record permits the statement that the frozen start--stop enumeration classifies the contacted finite intervals as reported.  It does not assert that any eligible interval is translated; it does not assert a protein fold; it does not assert thermodynamic or kinetic admissibility; it does not assert cellular function; it does not identify a mechanism; and it does not establish a universal genomic principle.  The self-check row reinforces this restriction by recording a BioReality-only boundary with $3$ checked gates and no promotion from codon geometry across biological layers.
