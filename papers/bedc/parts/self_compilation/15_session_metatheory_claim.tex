\section{Session-Level Metatheory Claim}
\label{sec:session-metatheory-claim}

This section states the claim surface carried by \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC} as a
single formal object of the manuscript.  The surface is intentionally narrow:
it concerns the universe-$0$, beta-only, single-sort dependent-function
fragment, read at closed represented terms and empty object contexts.  Within
that scope, the manuscript can cite a mathlib-free, axiom-free, sorry-free
Lean 4 mechanization of the represented syntax, typing, beta reduction,
closure discipline, selected confluence components, closed normalization
witnesses, executable checking, host-gallery transport, Church-encoded data
laws, and beta-conversion facts.

\begin{center}
\fbox{\begin{minipage}{0.91\textwidth}
\textbf{Scope claim.}
In the universe-$0$, beta-only, single-sort fragment with represented
constructors \texttt{sort}, \texttt{var}, \texttt{app}, \texttt{lam}, and
\texttt{pi}, and under the reading
$$
\begin{aligned}
  \mathrm{ClosedEmptyCtx}(t,A)
  \quad\equiv\quad
  \texttt{ClosedAt}\ 0\ t
  \land
  \texttt{HasType}\ []\ t\ A,
\end{aligned}
$$
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC} supplies first-principles mechanized metatheory inside
Lean 4, without mathlib imports, without project axioms, and without sorry
placeholders.  The claim is about this represented object calculus, not about
the full host Lean kernel.
\end{minipage}}
\end{center}

\subsection{Claim 1: Substrate Invariants}
\label{subsec:claim-substrate-invariants}

The substrate invariant is that de Bruijn indices are not treated as informal
names.  The calculus defines shift and substitution as native recursive
operations on represented terms.  A descent under a binder changes the cutoff;
substitution below a binder shifts the inserted term; closure is the predicate
that records which indices are admissible at a boundary.  The following
targets are the basic shift-aware substrate facts used throughout the
metatheory:

not asserted by a global slogan; it is exposed through first-principles

preserve typing across the local substitution operation:

Closure is stable under represented beta computation.  A one-step beta
reduction from a term closed at boundary $n$ has a target closed at the same
boundary, and the reflexive-transitive beta chain preserves the same boundary.
This is the guardrail that permits the self-compilation surface to execute
finite represented beta traces without leaving the closed fragment:

\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.substitute\_preserves\_closed\_at}

Read together, these targets justify the substrate part of the claim: the
syntax, closure, substitution, and local typing algebra are not external
assumptions.  They are object-language theorems about the represented
calculus, and all later claims depend on that explicit de Bruijn discipline.

\subsection{Boundary Statement}
\label{subsec:session-metatheory-boundary}

The scope boundary is part of the claim.  Open-term metatheory is outside the
closed empty-context surface.  The obstruction is concrete: the naive
substitution-composition shape fails at depth $0$ with
$v = \texttt{var}\ 0$, $u = \texttt{sort}$, and $t = \texttt{var}\ 1$.
The corresponding Lean target records the inequality directly, and the
confluence layer exposes the same obstruction as a substitution-commutation
boundary:

\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.substitute\_substitute\_zero\_zero\_unclosed\_counterexample}
\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.substitute\_commutation\_unclosed\_obstruction}
\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.BetaParallelSubstitutionCompatibility}

The represented calculus also excludes a universe hierarchy, cumulative
universes, native inductive families, eliminators for those families,
propositional irrelevance, quotient principles, and eta-conversion.  The
syntax has one sort and beta reduction only.  Any argument requiring those
features must introduce a separate bridge or a separate represented calculus;
it is not licensed by the claim in this section.

\subsection{Delivered Invariants}
\label{subsec:session-metatheory-delivered-invariants}

The delivered invariants are project-level as well as theorem-level.  The
\texttt{lean4/} development imports Lean 4 core facilities and project files,
not mathlib.  The project axiom audit rejects \texttt{axiom} declarations in
\texttt{lean4/BEDC/}.  The build is kept at zero sorry.  The axiom-purity
audit checks theorem dependencies against forbidden host principles, including
\texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, and \texttt{propext}.

These invariants determine how the preceding claims are read.  The markers in
this section are not literary citations; each target is a Lean object in the
project.  The substrate, subject-reduction, confluence, normalization,
checker, host-bridge, Church-algebra, and beta-conversion surfaces are
therefore reproducible as mechanized claims over the represented calculus.
The boundary clauses are equally binding: where a stronger statement is
refuted or only conditional, the paper reads the formal target as a refusal to
overstate the metatheory.
