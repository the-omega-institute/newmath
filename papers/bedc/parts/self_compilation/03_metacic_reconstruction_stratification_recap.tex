\subsection{Stratification Recap}\label{subsec:reconstruction-stratification-recap}

Within \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC}, L1 is the registration level. BEDC records
paper-claimed theorems as host-checked Lean data through
\texttt{BEDC.Manifest}, and the MetaCIC reconstruction is the syntactic
surface on which the registered calculus-facing claims can be named.

L4-mid is the internal representation level. BEDC expresses the inductive
structure of a CIC fragment, together with selected metatheory, as Lean
objects and predicates. MetaCIC therefore supplies representation and
derivation forms; it does not attempt to prove the host's consistency.

L4a is blocked by the Godel boundary. Since BEDC is checked inside the host,
BEDC cannot prove the host's own consistency from within that same host.
MetaCIC keeps this boundary intact and does not turn reconstruction into a
consistency proof for Lean.

L4c is rejected. BEDC does not replace the host CIC, and MetaCIC is not an
alternative foundation. The reconstruction is an inside-host formalization of
syntax, reduction, typing, and selected metatheory, all under the checking
authority of the host calculus.
