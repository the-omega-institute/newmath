\subsection{No Eta-Conversion}\label{subsec:reconstruction-no-eta-conversion}

\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Beta} contains no $\eta$-conversion, either as
$\eta$-expansion or as $\eta$-reduction. Its one-step relation is generated
only by root $\beta$ contraction and the congruence constructors for
application, lambda, and Pi terms.

This has concrete consequences. The represented terms
\verb|lam d (app f (var 0))| and \verb|f| do not reduce to each other through
\texttt{BetaStep}, even when \verb|f| is closed. The multi-step relation
\texttt{BetaStarStep} and the parallel relation \texttt{BetaParallel} are
built from the same $\beta$ surface, so they do not blur this boundary into
$\eta$-equality.

Accordingly, subject reduction and confluence in this reconstruction are
statements about $\beta$-equivalence only. A calculus containing
$\eta$-conversion has a different reduction signature: it requires an
additional \texttt{BetaStep} constructor, such as an \texttt{etaStep}, and
separate compatibility proofs between that constructor and the existing
$\beta$ metatheory.
