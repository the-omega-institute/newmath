\section{Closed-Term Metatheory Completion}
\label{sec:closed-term-metatheory-completion}

The closed-term surface of \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC} is the part of the
self-compilation substrate in which represented terms are read in the empty
context and de Bruijn closure is an explicit hypothesis.  The object calculus
is the universe-$0$, beta-only, single-sort dependent-function fragment:
represented syntax has sort, variable, application, lambda, and Pi; typing is
an inductive judgment over represented contexts; beta reduction is an
inductive relation over represented terms; and closure is the predicate
\texttt{ClosedAt}.  The proof objects live inside Lean 4 without mathlib,
without project axioms, and without sorry placeholders.

The scope claim is deliberately narrow and binding.  BEDC does not assert a
full host-kernel theorem about Lean's own CIC, and it does not assert the full
open-term metatheory of a production proof assistant.  It asserts that the
closed fragment needed by the self-compilation surface is represented from
first principles, that the reduction and closure invariants needed by that
surface are checked, and that the remaining theorem boundaries are exposed as
named Lean statements rather than hidden behind prose.

\subsection{The Closed-Term Scope Claim}
\label{subsec:closed-term-scope-claim}

The represented calculus has a closed empty-context reading:

$$
\begin{aligned}
  \mathrm{ClosedTermScope}(t,A)
  \quad\equiv\quad
  \texttt{ClosedAt}\ 0\ t
  \land
  \texttt{HasType}\ []\ t\ A .
\end{aligned}
$$

Inside this reading, the three metatheoretic invariants used by BEDC's
self-compilation account have checked representatives.

First, subject reduction is available as a hypothesis-explicit theorem over
the represented typing and beta-step relations.  The theorem states that beta
substitution preservation, application-argument type stability, lambda-domain
subject reduction, and Pi-domain subject reduction are exactly the interfaces
needed to preserve the displayed type along one represented beta step.  The
ordinary beta case and the congruence cases are mechanized; the closed-scope
discharge layer records which of the remaining interfaces is valid under the
closed hypotheses and where the calculus rejects a stronger reading.

Second, closure preservation is fully checked for one-step and finite-chain
beta reduction.  If a represented term is closed at boundary $n$, one

keeps the object-language evaluator inside the intended de Bruijn boundary:
beta contraction, application congruence, lambda congruence, and Pi congruence
cannot create a free represented variable when the source term is closed.

\subsection{What This Scope Buys}
\label{subsec:closed-term-scope-buys}

The first payoff is a first-principles self-compilation boundary.  The
represented typed witnesses used by BEDC are not informal quotations of host
Lean terms.  They are object-language inhabitants built from the MetaCIC
syntax constructors, checked by the MetaCIC typing relation, and reduced by
the MetaCIC beta relation.  When such a witness is closed and lies in the
normal-form sub-scope, the project has both a termination witness and a
type-preservation path appropriate to the exact local interface it uses.

The phrase ``appropriate to the exact local interface'' is important.  The
calculus validates broad subject reduction only under its named hypotheses.
For applications with a closed independent codomain, the application-argument
stability hypothesis is discharged directly.  For reducible binder domains,
Lean records counterexamples showing that the stronger lambda-domain and
Pi-domain closed statements are not valid as stated.  This is a positive
metatheoretic result for BEDC's architecture: the paper does not need to guess
where the closed scope ends, because the endpoint is itself mechanized.

The third payoff is executable normal-form operation without importing
standard-library axioms.  The bounded normalizer is a native recursive
operation over the represented syntax.  It decides the normal form of a finite
run by host fuel and definitional equations, not by an excluded-middle search
over arbitrary reduction paths.  For the gallery, the equational laws state
that enough fuel reaches the expected representative.  For the paper's
closed-term self-compilation surface, this is exactly the executable content
needed: a finite evaluator, named beta traces, closure preservation for those
traces, and concrete normal-form witnesses where termination is claimed.

\subsection{The Three-Hypothesis Chain}
\label{subsec:closed-term-three-hypothesis-chain}

substitution preservation.  In the closed empty-context scope they have
different resolutions.  The application-argument interface is discharged for
closed independent codomains; the reducible lambda-domain and Pi-domain
interfaces are resolved by checked counterexamples.  The following table is
the binding reading of that chain.

closedness alone as a substitute for binder-context transport.  The resulting
architecture is stricter than a prose-only closure claim: every consumed
interface is named, and every rejected strengthening is named as well.

\subsection{Closed Normalization and Candidate Witnesses}
\label{subsec:closed-normalization-candidate-witnesses}

The normal-form sub-scope has two layers.  The first layer is beta-strong
inertia.  A term is \texttt{BetaStrong} when it has no beta successor.  Sort
and variables satisfy this immediately by constructor inversion on
\texttt{BetaStep}; \texttt{BetaStrong} implies
\texttt{StrongNormalizable}; hence sort and variables are strongly
normalizable.  The closed-atom theorem packages the same fact for closed
terms typed at sort when their shape is sort or variable.

\subsection{Bounded Normalizer as Operation}
\label{subsec:closed-term-bounded-normalizer-operation}

The bounded normalizer is executable because it is structurally recursive on
fuel:

$$
\begin{aligned}
  \texttt{normalizeBounded}\ 0\ t &= t,\\
  \texttt{normalizeBounded}\ (k+1)\ \texttt{sort} &= \texttt{sort},\\
  \texttt{normalizeBounded}\ (k+1)\ (\texttt{var}\ i) &= \texttt{var}\ i.
\end{aligned}
$$

For lambda and Pi it recursively normalizes components.  For applications it
normalizes the function and argument and contracts when the normalized
function is a lambda.  The operation is therefore deterministic as host
computation, while the object-language safety statement remains the
represented one: whenever the run is read as a finite \texttt{BetaStep} trace,
\texttt{betaStarStep\_preserves\_closed} preserves closure from source to
target.

The host bridge records that the same finite computations match host CIC
equalities for the gallery.  The left side is represented computation through
\texttt{normalizeBounded}; the right side is a host definitional equality.
The beta-chain lemmas give the object-language reduction witnesses for the
same examples.  Together they supply an operational bridge from host examples
to represented examples without importing host normalization as an axiom about
the represented calculus.

\subsection{What Remains Outside the Closed-Term Scope}
\label{subsec:closed-term-open-frontier}

The frontier above the closed-term scope is explicit.

\begin{itemize}
  \item Open-term subject reduction remains outside the checked claim.  The
  obstruction is not merely bookkeeping: substitution composition under
  binders must account for shifting, local variables, and binder-context
  transport simultaneously.

  \item Full beta-parallel diamond over open terms remains outside the checked
  claim.  Atom diamonds and compound-shape assemblers are mechanized, but the
  open application residual proof still depends on substitution compatibility
  across parallel residuals.

  \item Universe hierarchy remains outside the represented calculus.  The
  object language has one sort, so it does not claim cumulativity, universe
  polymorphism, or universe-level consistency for a hierarchy.

  \item Inductive families remain outside the represented calculus.  There
  are no represented constructors, eliminators, positivity conditions,
  recursion principles, or computation rules for inductive families.

  \item Proposition irrelevance remains outside the represented calculus.  The
  fragment has typing, beta, Pi, lambda, application, variables, and sort; it
  does not include a proposition universe or proof-irrelevance principle.
\end{itemize}

These boundaries are architectural commitments.  The closed-term MetaCIC
surface is a substrate for BEDC's self-compilation account, not a replacement
kernel for all of Lean and not a paper claim about every feature of CIC.  The
frontier is stated so that downstream BEDC arguments do not accidentally use
universe hierarchy, inductive-family computation, proposition irrelevance, or
open-term substitution algebra as if those facts were already part of the
first-principles substrate.

\subsection{Meaning for the BEDC Architecture}
\label{subsec:closed-term-broader-architecture}

\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC} is the self-compilation substrate of the manuscript.  It
gives BEDC a represented language in which typed witnesses, reduction traces,
normal-form evidence, and finite host-gallery transport can be discussed
inside the project rather than only in metalinguistic prose.  The closed-term
scope covers the surface the self-reflective architecture truly consumes:
closed represented terms, empty-context typing witnesses, beta-only finite
reduction traces, candidate and neutral-normal witnesses for the gallery, and
bounded normalizer equations for executable examples.

The finite-history bridge fits this reading.  A \texttt{BHistIdx} can be
embedded as a represented MetaCIC term, and the image is closed at boundary
$0$.  This gives the self-compilation substrate a direct route back to BEDC's
finite event-history world without opening the de Bruijn boundary.

\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.BHistSubstrate.bhistToTerm\_closed}
\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.BHistSubstrate.bhistToTerm\_injective}

Outside the closed-term scope, BEDC makes no first-principles claim.  An
argument that needs open-term subject reduction, a full parallel diamond,
universe hierarchy, inductive families, or proposition irrelevance must carry
that extra structure explicitly.  The closed surface is therefore strong
because it is exact: it proves the substrate BEDC actually uses, records the
normal-form and operation facts needed by that substrate, and leaves the
larger CIC frontier outside the claim.
