\section{Shift-Aware Substrate Design}\label{sec:self-comp-substrate-shift-aware-design}

The substrate for \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC} is part of the formal claim, not only a
notation choice. The represented calculus needs binders, substitution, beta
contraction, typing under contexts, and induction over syntax while preserving
the project-wide axiom-free discipline. The chosen substrate is therefore a
pure de Bruijn representation with explicit shift and substitution. It is
small enough to be defined from first principles, but explicit enough to expose
the binder-index invariant needed by beta reduction and typing.

\subsection{Substrate Alternatives Surveyed}
\label{subsec:substrate-alternatives-surveyed}

Several binder substrates are mathematically standard. They do not have the
same fit for an axiom-free, self-contained Lean reconstruction.

\paragraph{Literal binders.}
A literal-binder encoding stores a variable name at each binder and each
occurrence. It is close to ordinary mathematical notation: lambda terms carry
names, and alpha-renaming is treated as irrelevant. The formal cost is that the
syntax quotient by alpha-equivalence becomes part of the substrate. Equality of
terms is no longer the constructor equality of an inductive type; it is a
quotient-respecting equality. In Lean this pulls the representation toward
quotient reasoning or proposition extensionality for the equality of
alpha-equivalence classes. That cost conflicts with the BEDC invariant that the
MetaCIC layer remains axiom-free and uses no hidden quotient axiom.

\paragraph{Nominal sets.}
A nominal substrate treats names, freshness, and finite supports as primitive
structure. It can express alpha-equivalence elegantly, but it requires a
fresh-name mechanism and finite-support reasoning. Those mechanisms introduce
additional infrastructure around finite sets, name permutation, and support
transport before the calculus itself appears. For BEDC this is the wrong
dependency direction: the calculus substrate should be a closed inductive
object, not an object whose well-formedness rests on a separate freshness
algebra.

\paragraph{HOAS.}
A higher-order abstract syntax substrate represents object binders by host
binders. This removes many manual substitution lemmas, but it moves binder
behavior into the host. BEDC.MetaCIC needs the opposite direction: object
terms, object binders, and object reductions must be ordinary data and
relations over which BEDC can quantify. A HOAS body has host function type, so
its substitution behavior is supplied by the host binder rather than
internalized as a represented operation. That makes full self-reflection of the
object binder mechanics unavailable at the MetaCIC layer.

\paragraph{Shift-aware de Bruijn.}
The chosen substrate uses natural-number indices and explicit operations:
\texttt{shift} raises free indices at or above a cutoff, and
\texttt{substitute} replaces the variable at a given depth while shifting the
substituted value under binders.
\leandef{BEDC.MetaCIC.shift}
\leandef{BEDC.MetaCIC.substitute}
The term grammar remains an ordinary inductive type, and all binder behavior is
visible as recursive functions on that type. No quotient, freshness oracle, or
host-level binder semantics is needed.

This representation is less pleasant for informal notation because every binder
movement must account for indices. That inconvenience is also its advantage:
the object-language bookkeeping becomes part of the checked substrate rather
than an implicit convention outside the formal surface.

\subsection{Why De Bruijn Alone Is Insufficient}
\label{subsec:why-de-bruijn-alone-insufficient}

Bare de Bruijn indices solve alpha-equivalence by construction, but they do not
by themselves solve binder transport. A variable index is depth-relative: the
same numeral denotes a different surrounding binder after descent below a
lambda or Pi. Any operation that crosses a binder must record this change in
depth.

Substitution without shift loses that invariant. Consider a body under a binder
and an argument inserted below another binder. If the argument is copied under
the inner binder without raising its free indices, its free variable zero is
captured by the inner binder. Conversely, when a substituted variable is
removed, variables above the removed depth must decrease by one. The operation
therefore has two distinct duties: insert the replacement at the selected
depth, and maintain the de Bruijn coordinate system around the removed binder.

In \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Syntax}, these duties are encoded directly. For a
variable, \texttt{substitute} replaces exactly the matching depth, lowers
indices above that depth, and leaves lower indices unchanged. For binders, it
recurses at depth $d+1$ and replaces the inserted value by
$\texttt{shift}\ 0\ 1\ v$. This is the explicit form of the depth-relative
invariant: when the operation enters a binder, the object-language environment
has one more bound variable, so free variables of the inserted value must be
raised away from capture.

The typing rules use the same invariant. The lambda and Pi body premises are
checked in a context headed by $\texttt{shift}\ 0\ 1\ \mathrm{dom}$ rather than
by the unshifted domain. The domain was defined outside the binder body; when it
is placed into the body context, its free indices must be read one binder
deeper. Thus shift is not an auxiliary convenience. It is the operation that
makes the context-extension rule match the de Bruijn meaning of binder descent.

\subsection{Mechanized Properties}
\label{subsec:substrate-mechanized-properties}

The substrate is represented by three layers of checked facts.

\paragraph{Closed terms are stable at their boundary.}
\texttt{ClosedAt n t} says that every free variable of $t$ is below $n$.
Shifting at the closure boundary does not change the syntax, and substituting
at the closure boundary is inert:
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.shift\_closed}
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.substitute\_closed} is marked at the reconstruction
substrate site.
These facts are the base sanity checks for the substrate. They say that
operations aimed outside the free-variable support of a term have no effect.

\paragraph{Shift and substitution commute in the closed anchor cases.}
The one-step cancellation law
\texttt{substitute\_shift\_at\_eq} records that substituting at the same depth
undoes a single shift at that depth; its primary marker is also at the
reconstruction substrate site.
The closed-source commuting law
\texttt{shift\_substitute\_zero\_zero\_closed} records the binder-head
transport shape used by typing preservation:
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.shift\_substitute\_zero\_zero\_closed}
This is the algebraic reason the substituted binder domain can be moved into a
shifted context without changing its represented meaning.

\paragraph{Closedness is preserved by substitution and beta reduction.}
Substitution preserves closure when the replacement is closed at the target
boundary and the source term is closed one boundary higher:
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.substitute\_preserves\_closed\_at}
Since beta contraction is implemented by substitution, this feeds directly into
the closure preservation theorem for one beta step and then for beta-star:
\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.betaStep\_preserves\_closed}
\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.betaStarStep\_preserves\_closed}

\paragraph{Typing preservation exposes the same binder transport.}
The typing surface contains constructor-local substitution results for Pi,
lambda, and application terms, together with the main case analysis over the
represented syntax. The broad term-induction surface states that, under the
closedness and shape hypotheses required by the fragment, typing is preserved
after substitution:
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.substitute\_preserves\_typing\_via\_term\_induction}
The subject-reduction assembly then uses substitution preservation as an
explicit hypothesis and proves that a represented beta step preserves the same
represented type. The main subject-reduction theorem is marked at the
case-level metatheory site in this chapter.

\subsection{Open Algebraic Gap}
\label{subsec:substrate-open-algebraic-gap}

The substrate deliberately does not assume that every familiar textbook
substitution law transfers in its most direct form. A naive composition law for
zero-depth substitution is false for the represented operations.

Take $d=0$, $v=\mathrm{sort}$, $u=\mathrm{sort}$, and $t=\mathrm{var}\ 1$ in
the naive law

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{substitute}\ d\ v\,
  (\mathrm{substitute}\ d\ u\ t)
&=
\mathrm{substitute}\ d\,
  (\mathrm{substitute}\ d\ v\ u)\ t.
\end{aligned}
$$

The left side first lowers $\mathrm{var}\ 1$ to $\mathrm{var}\ 0$ and then
replaces it by $\mathrm{sort}$. The right side simplifies
$\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ \mathrm{sort}\ \mathrm{sort}$ to $\mathrm{sort}$ and
then substitutes at depth zero in $\mathrm{var}\ 1$, which lowers to
$\mathrm{var}\ 0$. Thus the left side is $\mathrm{sort}$ and the right side is
$\mathrm{var}\ 0$.

The checked counterexample in the Lean substrate records the same obstruction
using an unclosed zero-depth anchor. With

$$
\begin{aligned}
s &= \mathrm{var}\ 0,\\
a &= \mathrm{sort},\\
t &= \mathrm{var}\ 1,
\end{aligned}
$$

the checked counterexample is

$$
\begin{aligned}
&\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ s\,
    (\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ a\ t)
\neq
\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\,
    (\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ s\ a)\,
    (\mathrm{substitute}\ 1\ s\ t).
\end{aligned}
$$

\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.substitute\_substitute\_zero\_zero\_unclosed\_counterexample}

Here the left side is $\mathrm{var}\ 0$, while the right side is
$\mathrm{sort}$. The point is not that substitution composition is unavailable
in all forms. The point is that the exact law must account for closure,
shifting, and binder depth. BEDC therefore derives the composition shapes it
uses, instead of importing a nominal-looking lemma whose hypotheses do not
match this substrate.

\subsection{Comparison to the Host CIC}
\label{subsec:substrate-host-cic-comparison}

Lean 4's kernel and elaborator expose a richer implementation environment than
the MetaCIC fragment. The host surface accepts named binders in user syntax,
elaborates them through local constants and internal binder representations,
and manages levels, metavariables, definitional unfolding, and kernel
conversion. BEDC.MetaCIC does not mirror that implementation.

The MetaCIC substrate is intentionally smaller. It has one sort constructor,
variables, application, lambda, Pi, beta contraction, and a shift-aware typing
relation over explicit contexts. Names are absent from the object syntax, and
there is no attempt to reproduce Lean's elaboration pipeline or the exact
locally nameless machinery used by host internals.

This separation keeps the claim precise. The host kernel checks the Lean files
that define the MetaCIC syntax and prove its lemmas. The object calculus inside
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC} is a represented fragment with its own explicit binder
mechanics. Its substrate is not a reimplementation of the host kernel; it is a
small inductive calculus whose shift and substitution rules can be inspected,
proved about, and audited inside the project.
