\section{What BEDC.MetaCIC Does Not Claim}
\label{sec:metacic-what-we-do-not-claim}

This section fixes the load-bearing absences around \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC}.
The closed-term, empty-context surface is intentionally strong enough for the
self-compilation account and intentionally too small to be read as a
replacement for a production CIC kernel.  The object calculus is represented
inside Lean as a first-principles, mathlib-free development: it has
represented terms, represented contexts, de Bruijn closure, shift,
substitution, beta reduction, typed examples, bounded normalization, and a
set of named metatheoretic interfaces.  It is not a mirror of every feature
that appears in Lean 4, Coq-style CIC, or the pure type systems literature.

The boundary matters because the positive results are now dense enough that a
reader can overgeneralize them.  A theorem about closed represented terms may
look like a theorem about arbitrary open host terms.  A beta-only reduction
relation may look like definitional equality.  A single-sort universe
constant may look like a universe hierarchy.  A gallery of Church encodings
may look like native inductive families.  Those readings are not the claims
made here.  The claim is narrower and more useful for BEDC: the represented
closed fragment used by the self-compilation surface is explicit, checked,
and bounded.

\subsection{Why This Boundary Is Stated}
\label{subsec:metacic-boundary-why}

The self-compilation argument consumes finite represented artifacts.  A BEDC
reflection packet talks about names, histories, represented terms, typed
closed witnesses, finite beta traces, and object-language certificates that
are already inside the manuscript's finite surface.  This use case does not
require the whole host kernel.  It requires a precise subcalculus whose
syntax and metatheory can be inspected without importing untracked logical
strength.

That is the reason \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC} is formulated as an object calculus.
The Lean kernel remains the trusted host that checks the formalization.  The
MetaCIC layer is not another implementation of that kernel.  It is a
represented calculus whose constructors and relations are small enough to be
audited by the project gates.  When the paper cites the MetaCIC surface, the
reader should read the claim as a theorem about the represented fragment, not
as a theorem about every term, universe, recursor, quotient, and definitional
equality feature accepted by Lean.

The most important narrowing is the closed empty-context reading.  The
object-level invariant is:

$$
\begin{aligned}
  \mathrm{ClosedEmptyMetaCIC}(t,A)
  \quad\equiv\quad
  \texttt{ClosedAt}\ 0\ t
  \land
  \texttt{HasType}\ []\ t\ A .
\end{aligned}
$$

This is the surface on which the self-compilation examples live.  It is also
the surface on which closure preservation, selected subject-reduction
interfaces, closed-normal confluence facts, candidate witnesses, executable
checking, and finite HostBridge examples are read.  The calculus exposes
stronger-looking open-term statements only when they have explicit hypotheses
or checked obstructions.  Therefore this section states the negative space in
the same voice as the positive theorems: not as cautionary prose, but as part
of the mathematical specification.

\paragraph{Universe hierarchy.}
The represented grammar contains a single sort constructor.  The paper may
write ``universe-$0$'' as a mnemonic for the object-level scale, but that is
not a commitment to a hierarchy of object universes.  There is no represented
\texttt{Sort 1}, no lifting relation between universes, no cumulativity rule,
and no universe-polymorphic binder discipline.  Consequently, any theorem
whose proof would use universe constraints, universe maximums, predicative
stratification, or cumulativity transport lies outside the claim surface.

This omission is intentional.  Universe hierarchy is not a cosmetic
annotation on an existing calculus.  It changes the formation rules for Pi,
the shape of type inference, the statement of conversion, and the induction
principles used in metatheory.  BEDC's self-compilation surface does not need
those rules.  It needs closed represented terms whose typing witnesses can be
checked without importing a second layer of universe bookkeeping.

\paragraph{Inductive families.}
The object language has dependent functions and application, but it does not
have native inductive families.  A family such as \texttt{Vec A n} would
require object-level constructors, parameters, indices, eliminators,
recursors, computation rules, and positivity or guardedness conditions.  A
production kernel has to define and check those interfaces.  \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC}
does not.

The Church gallery should therefore be read correctly.  Church Booleans,
Church numerals, pairs, options, and combinators are represented as terms in
the dependent-function fragment.  They demonstrate that the fragment can
carry useful closed encodings and beta laws.  They are not a proof that the
object language contains native inductive declarations, pattern matching, or
recursor computation for arbitrary families.

\paragraph{Proposition irrelevance.}
The represented calculus does not distinguish \texttt{Prop} from
\texttt{Type}.  It has no separate impredicative proposition universe and no
rule that identifies all proofs of the same proposition.  Thus proposition
irrelevance is not available as an object-language theorem, a conversion
principle, or an implicit metatheoretic assumption.

This is also aligned with the axiom-purity discipline.  BEDC avoids moving
from logical equivalence to definitional identity unless a first-principles
object-level construction supplies the equality in the represented calculus.
That discipline is why \texttt{propext} is prohibited by audit rather than
treated as a harmless background feature.

\paragraph{$\eta$-conversion.}
The reduction relation is beta-only.  Lambda terms reduce by applying a
lambda to an argument and substituting the argument into the body.  Congruence
allows beta steps under applications, lambdas, and Pis.  No $\eta$ rule says
that a function is convertible to a lambda that applies it to a fresh
variable.  No record, pair, or function extensionality rule is folded into
conversion.

This matters for subject reduction and confluence statements.  A beta-only
calculus has different normal forms, different residual obligations, and a
smaller conversion relation than a beta-eta calculus.  Any result that needs
$\eta$-expansion, $\eta$-contraction, or extensional comparison of functions
is outside the MetaCIC claim unless it is restated as an explicit additional
relation with its own proof obligations.

\paragraph{Pattern matching beyond term constructors.}
The Lean proofs may analyze the inductive syntax of represented terms.  This
is ordinary host-side reasoning: a theorem about \texttt{Term} can proceed by
cases over \texttt{sort}, \texttt{var}, \texttt{app}, \texttt{lam}, and
\texttt{pi}.  Such proof analysis does not mean that the represented object
language has a general match expression or a dependent eliminator for every
future object datatype.

The distinction is important.  Host-side induction over the encoding is how
the metatheory is proved.  Object-side pattern matching would be a feature of
the represented calculus itself, with typing rules, reduction rules, and
subject-reduction obligations.  BEDC uses the former and does not claim the
latter.

\paragraph{General fixpoints and well-founded recursion.}
The object language does not contain a general fixpoint constructor.  It also
does not contain an object-level well-founded recursion principle.  Where the
formalization needs recursion, it uses ordinary Lean structural recursion on
the encoded data, a fuel-indexed bounded function, or an inductively generated
relation.  Those are host-side implementation techniques and object-level
relations, not an exported recursive programming language inside MetaCIC.

This boundary keeps termination evidence visible.  Strong-normalization
witnesses are carried by inductive predicates and candidate structures.
Bounded normalization is controlled by fuel.  Beta-star traces are finite
inductive chains.  None of these witnesses should be read as a general
termination checker for arbitrary represented recursive definitions.

\paragraph{Open-term metatheory.}
The closed empty-context surface is not a disguised proof of full open-term
metatheory.  Open contexts require binder transport, shift/substitution
interaction across changing cutoffs, and substitution-composition laws that
are sensitive to closure hypotheses.  The substrate obstruction is concrete:
the naive zero-depth composition law for unclosed anchors is false, and the
counterexample is documented as part of the MetaCIC boundary.

The existing closed facts therefore have an exact reading.  Closure
preservation says that beta steps do not create free variables from closed
sources.  The positive application subject-reduction result works for closed
independent codomains.  The binder-domain counterexamples show why closedness
alone is not enough for blanket lambda-domain or Pi-domain preservation.
Those are not gaps in an otherwise implicit open theorem.  They are the
formal end of the present claim surface.

\paragraph{Coquand--Werner CIC.}
The name MetaCIC signals the object of comparison, not identity of scope.
Classical CIC presentations support a richer theory than the fragment used
here: universe hierarchies, cumulativity, inductive types, eliminators, and a
conversion relation integrated with the kernel.  BEDC does not replicate that
whole package.  In particular, it does not present a normalization-by-
evaluation algorithm for full CIC, and it does not mechanize universe
cumulativity.

The comparison should be read as follows.  BEDC takes the kernel-facing
lesson that syntax, typing, substitution, and reduction must be formal
objects.  It then applies that lesson to the closed self-compilation surface.
The result is a small, auditable object calculus rather than a production
assistant kernel.

\paragraph{Girard reducibility candidates.}
The normalization layer uses candidate terminology because candidates are the
right vocabulary for strong-normalization evidence.  The formalization has
\texttt{Candidate}, \texttt{ClosedCandidate}, \texttt{PiCandidate}, candidate
sets, closure under beta, Pi application consequences, neutral-normal
witnesses, and concrete gallery instantiations.

That is not the same as a full Girard-style adequacy theorem for every
well-typed represented term.  The adequacy chain would have to assign
candidate interpretations to all types in the fragment, prove closure under
substitution and conversion at the required generality, and then derive
strong normalization for arbitrary typed terms.  BEDC has the framework and
the consumed closed witnesses; it does not claim the entire adequacy
development.

\paragraph{Pure type systems.}
Pure type systems classify calculi by specifying sorts, axioms, and product
rules.  \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC} can be compared to a very small sub-PTS: one
represented sort and the dependent-function rules needed by the object
grammar.  That comparison is useful for orientation, but the formalization is
not a generic PTS engine parameterized by arbitrary rules.

This matters for reviewer expectations.  A theorem proved for the BEDC
fragment should not be lifted to all PTSs, to all single-sort systems, or to
all extensions with additional products.  The theorem is about the inductive
relations actually present in \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC}.

\paragraph{Lean 4 kernel.}
Lean is the host that checks the project.  It is not the object calculus being
replaced.  The host has its own elaborator, reduction machinery, inductive
declaration checker, quotient support, universe handling, and runtime
behavior.  \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC} sits inside that host as a verified object
language.

The project-level distinction is sharper than ordinary shallow embedding.
BEDC explicitly audits its own theorems for forbidden dependencies.  The
formal content cited by this chapter is required not to depend on
\texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, or \texttt{propext}.  Thus
the MetaCIC results are not imported from host classical reasoning or quotient
reasoning.  They are checked within the constructive, axiom-clean discipline
that BEDC imposes on its own theorem surface.

\subsection{Zero-Axiom Invariants Delivered}

claim to cover all of CIC, but the part it does cover is not supported by
hidden project axioms.  The invariant is audited at two levels: source files
must not declare project axioms, and theorem dependencies must not rely on
the forbidden host axioms.

\paragraph{Constructive dependency discipline.}
Avoiding \texttt{Classical.choice} is not merely a style rule.  In this
project, a theorem that depends on choice would close over an unwitnessed
selection operation.  That would be the wrong kind of evidence for a
self-compilation surface whose artifacts are finite, named, and carried by
explicit histories.  The formal proofs therefore use constructors,
induction, structural recursion, and displayed hypotheses rather than
classical selection.

\paragraph{No quotient collapse.}
Quotient types are also outside the cited MetaCIC theorem dependencies.
Represented terms remain syntactic data.  Beta conversion is represented by
relations and join evidence, not by collapsing syntax into equivalence
classes.  This is essential for the paper's auditability: a reader can see
which beta paths, closure predicates, and typing hypotheses are being used.

\paragraph{No proposition extensionality.}
The project treats \texttt{propext} as a real logical dependency.  It is not
allowed to enter through a convenient proof pattern.  This affects the shape
of the Lean development: proofs are written so that proposition equality is
not required when ordinary constructor reasoning, explicit implications, or
relation evidence is sufficient.  The result is a theorem surface that can be
read constructively.

\paragraph{No mathlib dependency.}
The mathlib-free condition keeps the dependency chain small.  It does not
mean the work rejects established mathematics.  It means the specific
MetaCIC surface is audited as a first-principles Lean 4 development rather
than as a collection of imported high-level results.  The relevant objects
are the project's own inductives, definitions, and theorems.

\paragraph{No unproved placeholders.}
The no-sorry invariant keeps the paper and Lean states aligned.  When the
manuscript cites a checked theorem, there is a proof term.  When the project
does not have a proof at the required strength, the theorem is either stated
as an explicit interface, constrained to a checked sub-scope, or rejected by a
counterexample.  That is why the boundary section can be part of the
scientific claim rather than a disclaimer outside the mathematics.

\subsection{Why This Matters for Self-Compilation}
\label{subsec:metacic-boundary-self-compilation}

BEDC self-compilation is not a demand that the project reconstruct every
feature of the host kernel inside itself.  The operational surface is smaller:
the project reflects finite, typed, closed artifacts.  The represented terms
used in that surface are object-language data.  Their typing witnesses,
closure facts, beta traces, and normal-form witnesses are the objects that
the self-compilation argument actually consumes.

For that reason, the excluded features above do not weaken the stated
self-compilation closure.  Universe hierarchy does not participate in the
finite closed witnesses currently consumed by the reflection surface.
Native inductive families do not participate because the gallery uses
Church-encoded data inside the dependent-function fragment.  Proposition
irrelevance does not participate because the object language has no separate
proposition layer.  $\eta$-conversion does not participate because the beta
traces are the reduction evidence being checked.  General fixpoints do not
participate because termination evidence is supplied by structural recursion,
fuel, inductive traces, or candidate witnesses.

Open-term metatheory is the most subtle boundary.  A production kernel must
handle open contexts constantly.  BEDC's self-compilation surface, however,
uses closed represented artifacts at the reflection boundary.  The substrate
still has to understand binders, shifting, and substitution, because closed
terms may contain binders internally.  But the exported self-compilation
claim does not need a blanket theorem for arbitrary open object contexts.  It
needs the checked closed facts and the named local interfaces already present
in the MetaCIC layer.

Thus the boundary is not a retreat from the self-compilation goal.  It is the
shape of that goal.  The positive surface and the negative surface meet at
the same line:

$$
\begin{aligned}
  &\text{closed represented terms}
  + \text{empty object context}
  + \text{beta-only traces}
  + \text{explicit certificates} \\
  &\qquad\text{rather than full host kernel reflection.}
\end{aligned}
$$

This line is what makes the claim auditable.  A reviewer can ask whether a
self-compilation step uses only the displayed closed surface.  If it does,
the MetaCIC mechanization is relevant.  If it requires universe cumulativity,
native inductives, proposition irrelevance, $\eta$-conversion, general
recursion, or an open-context substitution theorem, then it is outside the
present commitment and must be stated as a separate extension.

\subsection{What Would Extend the Scope}
\label{subsec:metacic-extend-scope}

The omitted features are not impossible additions.  They are substantial
theory projects with their own syntax, typing, reduction, and audit burden.
The present manuscript does not rely on them, so it does not claim them.  A
larger MetaCIC surface would have to expose each one as first-principles
content rather than as an informal analogy with an external kernel.

\paragraph{Universe hierarchy extension.}
A universe hierarchy would require a layered represented syntax with at
least \texttt{Sort 0} and \texttt{Sort 1}, and likely a general level
language.  The typing judgment would need formation rules for sorts and Pis
that compute result levels.  Conversion would need to account for
cumulativity if cumulativity is included.  The metatheory would then have to
prove that substitution, subject reduction, confluence, normalization
witnesses, and checking remain valid in the level-aware calculus.

\paragraph{Inductive-family extension.}
Native inductive families would require represented declarations,
constructors, parameters, indices, eliminators, and computation rules.  The
project would also need a positivity or guardedness condition appropriate to
the represented language.  Only after those pieces exist could a theorem
about \texttt{Vec}, \texttt{List}, or indexed eliminators be stated as a
MetaCIC theorem rather than as a Church-encoded example.

\paragraph{Proposition-layer extension.}
A separate proposition layer would require syntax and typing rules that
distinguish proof-relevant and proof-irrelevant regions, or at least a clear
choice about whether proof irrelevance is object-level, meta-level, or absent.
If proposition irrelevance were included, it would need its own constructive
or explicitly axiomatized status.  Under BEDC's present audit discipline, it
could not enter as an untracked dependency on \texttt{propext}.

\paragraph{Open-term metatheory extension.}
The open-term extension would have to start from the known obstruction rather
than ignore it.  It would need closure-indexed substitution composition,
binder-context transport, weakening, exchange if contexts are generalized,
and subject reduction stated with the exact transported type.  The existing
counterexamples show where naive statements fail.  A successful extension
would replace those naive statements with the right shift-aware invariants.

\paragraph{Kernel-bridge extension.}
A bridge to the host kernel would require a relation between represented
MetaCIC judgments and actual Lean elaboration or kernel checking.  That
relation would have to be partial, because the represented fragment is
smaller than Lean.  It would also have to specify quotation, erasure,
normalization, universe behavior, and failure modes.  Such a bridge would be
a separate metatheoretic layer above the present object calculus.

These extensions are ambitious, and none is required for the present
self-compilation boundary.  The commitment here is narrower: maintain the
closed represented fragment as an axiom-clean, mathlib-free, sorry-free
surface; state every consumed interface explicitly; and keep every excluded
kernel feature outside the claim until its own represented syntax and
metatheory are supplied.
