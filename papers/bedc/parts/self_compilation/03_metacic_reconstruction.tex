\section{MetaCIC Reconstruction}\label{sec:self-comp-metacic}

The MetaCIC reconstruction surface asks how much of the host calculus can be
represented as BEDC data and predicates. It is a reconstruction analysis, not a
replacement of the host. The relevant namespace is `\verb|BEDC.MetaCIC|', whose
role is to name the current surface on which CIC syntax, typing, and reduction
can be discussed as first-class BEDC objects.

The structural reconstruction layer constructs syntax, contexts, terms,
universe annotations as needed, beta-reduction, and typing judgments as
inductive objects or inductive predicates over BEDC histories.
\leandef{BEDC.MetaCIC.Term}
\leandef{BEDC.MetaCIC.BetaStep}
\leandef{BEDC.MetaCIC.HasType}
The result is not a claim that BEDC has reimplemented Lean. It is a claim that
the grammar and judgment forms of a CIC fragment can be represented directly
enough to support quantification and induction over them.

The difference between behavior and structure is central. A Turing-machine
simulation of Lean execution can describe observed runs:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\exists t,\ \mathrm{MachineTrace}(\mathrm{Lean},x,t)
  \wedge \mathrm{Accepts}(t).
\end{aligned}
$$

Such a statement is behavioral and trace-based. It predicts or records a
finite execution. A direct inductive predicate has a different form:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Gamma \vdash_{\mathrm{MetaCIC}} t : T.
\end{aligned}
$$

This is a first-class judgment. BEDC can quantify over contexts, terms, and
derivations, and can prove results by induction on the derivation. TM
simulation gives Sigma-one execution traces; direct reconstruction gives
typed judgment objects. The base sort judgment is inhabited in the empty
context. \leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.sort\_in\_empty\_ctx}

The same fragment carries concrete checked examples. A polymorphic identity
term over `Sort' is well typed, and its Pi type is itself a represented typing
judgment. \leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.id\_sort\_well\_typed}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.pi\_sort\_sort\_well\_typed} Applying that identity
to `Sort' has the expected represented application typing, and the corresponding
beta step contracts to the argument. \leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.id\_sort\_applied}


The construction surface covers syntax and judgments. It supports statements
of the form ``for every represented derivation with this constructor shape, the
following structural property holds.'' It also supports explicit boundary
claims: which syntax is represented, which conversion relation is present,
which typing rules are included, and which host-kernel features remain outside
the fragment.

The metatheory layer covers structural claims about represented derivations.
Subject reduction says that typing survives reduction:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\Gamma \vdash t : T \wedge t \to_{\beta} u
  \longrightarrow \Gamma \vdash u : T.
\end{aligned}
$$

Confluence says that divergent reductions can be joined. These theorems are
structural metatheorems about the represented calculus, and their checked
fragments must be stated over the explicit syntax, reduction, and typing
relations carried by the reconstruction.

Strong normalization has a different status. A global strong-normalization
theorem strong enough to recover consistency of the represented host calculus
meets the Godel-II boundary when the represented calculus is comparable in
strength to the host in which the proof is carried out. BEDC can study bounded
or fragment-specific termination claims, and it can state which hypotheses make
those claims available. It does not obtain an axiom-free global consistency
certificate for its own host by internal reconstruction.

The replacement reading would make BEDC the foundation instead of CIC. This is
not a claim of the paper. BEDC is formalized inside the host CIC. All Lean
theorems in the project are checked by that host. The MetaCIC surface may
represent syntax and prove fragment metatheory, but it does not move BEDC
outside the host or give BEDC a stronger foundational status than the kernel
that checks it.

This yields the governing inclusion:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{BEDC\ formalization} \subseteq \mathrm{host\ CIC}.
\end{aligned}
$$

The inclusion is methodological, not merely notational. A theorem about a
represented calculus is a theorem inside the host. It can be useful, precise,
and internally quantified, but it cannot serve as a host-independent foundation
for the host without crossing the Godel-Tarski boundary.

The MetaCIC reconstruction surface thus complements the Manifest. The Manifest
registers finite paper-anchored theorem entries as checked Lean data. MetaCIC
reconstruction studies the syntax and judgment structure of the calculus in
which such checking is meaningful. Together they explain why ``BEDC compiles
itself'' has a legitimate L4-mid reading and no legitimate L4a or replacement
reading.

\subsection{Internal Manifest Coverage}\label{subsec:internal-manifest-coverage}

\texttt{BEDC.Manifest} is the Lean-side registry for paper-facing theorem
claims. Its core record is \texttt{BEDC.Manifest.ClaimEntry}, with fields for
the paper label, fully qualified Lean name, proposition, and proof witness.
The field \texttt{proof : prop} is dependent: a manifest entry elaborates only
when Lean's kernel accepts the supplied proof term at the recorded proposition.
Thus manifest well-formedness is not a string audit; it is kernel type
checking of the \texttt{List ClaimEntry}.

The registry has 65 Lean-side entries in the unfiltered manifest-coverage
report. Its breadth includes paper Lean-checked theorems from base reflection,
ground compiler results, F-kernel gap transport, capstone fable-machine
claims, reflection claims, derived structures for holonomy, KKT, and spectral
measure packets, and the six primary MetaCIC examples in this section:
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.sort\_in\_empty\_ctx},
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.id\_sort\_well\_typed},
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.pi\_sort\_sort\_well\_typed},
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.id\_sort\_applied},
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.id\_sort\_beta}, and
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.var\_zero\_in\_sort\_ctx}.

The audit command \texttt{bedc\_ci.py manifest-coverage} compares two
sets: paper targets named by $\backslash\mathtt{leanchecked}\{X\}$ and Lean
targets registered in \texttt{BEDC.Manifest.manifest}. It reports the marker
count, manifest-entry count, coverage ratio, and a sample of paper markers
that are not represented by manifest entries. The unfiltered report gives
7831 paper markers and 65 manifest entries, for
\texttt{65/7831 = 0.83\%}. With the literal namespace filter
\texttt{--scope all}, the same command reports zero markers and zero entries,
because no Lean target has prefix \texttt{all}.

The gap is therefore marker-heavy rather than manifest-heavy. The manifest
contains a selected kernel-typed spine, while many paper markers remain outside
the internal registry. In this section, the six primary MetaCIC markers above
are manifest entries; the substitution, subject-reduction, and binder-transport
markers below are Lean-checked paper markers without manifest entries. The
audit makes this distinction mechanical: every paper-claimed theorem must be
visible either through the ordinary $\backslash\mathtt{leanchecked}$ marker
stream or through the stronger internal manifest entry stream.

\subsection{Shift-Aware Metatheory Progress}\label{subsec:shift-aware-metatheory-progress}

The shift-aware substrate is represented by \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Typing}. Its
sanity layer covers the sort, Pi, identity, application, and dependent
identity examples, and its atomic substitution layer is no longer a schematic
placeholder: sort and variable atoms are checked directly.
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.substitute\_preserves\_typing\_sort\_var}
The captured-substitution demonstration also has a positive source typing
derivation and a rejected captured target.
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.r11\_substitute\_preserves\_typing}

The Pi and lambda cases expose the shift-aware shift discipline directly. Their
substitution-preservation lemmas are checked under closed-domain and
atomic-shape hypotheses, with weakening across the shifted binder premise.
\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.substitute\_preserves\_typing\_pi}
\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.substitute\_preserves\_typing\_lam}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.hasType\_shift\_weaken\_sort\_var}

The beta case of subject reduction is therefore isolated in
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.SubjectReduction}. It is provable from a clean
substitution-preservation lemma:
\leandef{BEDC.MetaCIC.BetaSubstitutionPreservation}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.subject\_reduction\_beta}
\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.subject\_reduction}
This puts the beta subject-reduction burden exactly on
$\mathrm{BetaSubstitutionPreservation}$, without adding an extra
operational premise to beta reduction itself. Application congruence is
checked separately at the preserved-type boundary.
\leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.subject\_reduction\_congApp1}

The binder-transport lemmas identify the semantic footprint of the shift-aware
binder context. The shift inserted under the binder is judgmentally absorbed by
closedness on closed binder domains.

\subsection{Substrate Stack}\label{subsec:substrate-stack}

The MetaCIC reconstruction is a layered stack of Lean modules. Each module
contributes a specific fragment of the represented type theory's substrate.
The stack is finite, ordered by formal dependency, and audited by the standing
paper and Lean checks.

\paragraph{Syntax layer.} \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Syntax} provides
$\mathrm{Term}$ (an inductive with five constructors: $\mathrm{var}$,
$\mathrm{app}$, $\mathrm{lam}$, $\mathrm{pi}$, $\mathrm{sort}$),
$\mathrm{shift}$ (de Bruijn lifting at cutoff with explicit amount,
dispatching on Boolean $\mathrm{Nat.ble}$), and $\mathrm{substitute}$
(capture-aware substitution at arbitrary depth, dispatching on Boolean
$\mathrm{Nat.beq}$ and $\mathrm{Nat.blt}$; the Bool-level branching avoids the
$\mathrm{propext}$ exposure that Prop-level decidable-if would introduce for
generalized substitute lemmas).

\paragraph{Typing layer.} \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Typing} provides
$\mathrm{Ctx}$ as a list of represented types, $\mathrm{Ctx.lookup}$ with
depth-aware $\mathrm{shift}$ on tail-looked-up types, and the inductive
predicate
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{HasType} : \mathrm{Ctx} \to \mathrm{Term} \to \mathrm{Term}
  \to \mathrm{Prop}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Its formation rules are $\mathrm{sortRule}$, $\mathrm{varRule}$,
$\mathrm{piRule}$, $\mathrm{lamRule}$, and $\mathrm{appRule}$.

\paragraph{Reduction layer.} \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Beta} provides
$\mathrm{BetaStep}$ with one base beta rule and three congruence rules:
app-left, app-right, and lam-body.

\paragraph{Well-formedness layer.} \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.ContextWF} provides
$\mathrm{WellFormedCtx}$ with empty and cons formation rules. The cons rule
requires the head type to be derivable at $\mathrm{Sort}$ in the predecessor
context. This premise is required for substitution preservation: without it,
ill-typed contexts admit refutations of preservation.

\paragraph{Substitution-preservation layer.}
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Substitution} hosts the substitution-preservation target
with the $\mathrm{WellFormedCtx}$ premise. Its proof body is the active
research target.

\paragraph{Subject-reduction layer.}
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.SubjectReduction} depends on substitution preservation
and is the next downstream target.

\paragraph{Operational layer.} \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Examples} carries
concrete $\mathrm{HasType}$ witnesses for representative terms: the base sort
judgment, the polymorphic identity, and variable lookup.
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.LeanBridge} carries one worked encoding of a host CIC
term into MetaCIC with its $\mathrm{HasType}$ derivation.

\paragraph{Decidable layer.} \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Checker} provides
$\mathrm{Term.beq}$, structural equality on terms, with soundness
$\mathrm{Term.beq\_eq}$; $\mathrm{infer}$, type inference returning
$\mathrm{Option}\ \mathrm{Term}$, with soundness $\mathrm{infer\_sound}$; and
$\mathrm{check}$, decidable typing check, with soundness
$\mathrm{check\_sound}$.

\paragraph{Display and elaboration layer.}
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.PrettyPrint} provides a display function
$\mathrm{Term} \to \mathrm{String}$. \texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Elaborator}
provides $\mathrm{ElabFailure}$, $\mathrm{ElabResult}$,
$\mathrm{normalizeOneStep}$ for single-step beta reduction, and
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{elaborate} : \mathrm{Ctx} \to \mathrm{Term} \to \mathrm{ElabResult}.
\end{aligned}
$$

\paragraph{Reflection bridge implementation status.}
$\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.HostBridge}.\mathrm{reflectExpr}$ covers eight
host Lean $\mathrm{Expr}$ constructors: $\mathrm{sort}$,
$\mathrm{bvar}$, $\mathrm{app}$, $\mathrm{lam}$,
$\mathrm{forallE}$, $\mathrm{const}$, $\mathrm{fvar}$ returning
$\mathrm{none}$, and $\mathrm{mdata}$ as a passthrough. Two elaboration
commands surface the encoding to BEDC automation:
$\#\mathrm{reflect\_metacic}\ e$ prints the $\mathrm{Term}$ form, and
$\#\mathrm{metacic\_decide}\ e$ runs $\mathrm{infer}$ on the reflected
$\mathrm{Term}$ and prints the inferred MetaCIC type. The latter is the
end-to-end demonstration of the host-MetaCIC pipeline.

\paragraph{Atomic inference and concrete typing gallery.}
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.AtomInfer} carries a minimal type-inference procedure
for closed atoms: a sort literal infers to itself, a de Bruijn variable
infers to its context lookup.
\leandef{BEDC.MetaCIC.inferAtom}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.inferAtom\_sort\_sound}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.inferAtom\_var\_sound}
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.TypedExamples} carries a gallery of concrete typed
terms over the shift-aware substrate: sort and Pi types in singleton and
binary contexts, the dependent identity that exhibits shift-aware binder
tracking, and a beta-reducible identity application.
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.var\_zero\_in\_sort\_ctx}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.var\_one\_in\_double\_sort\_ctx}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.pi\_var\_sort\_in\_sort\_ctx}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.lam\_dependent\_identity}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.pi\_sort\_sort\_in\_empty\_ctx}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.app\_id\_sort\_in\_empty\_ctx}
\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.Normalization} records that sort and variable atoms
are reduction-stable: no $\beta$-step starts from a sort or variable
constructor, so closed atoms typed at sort are normal forms.
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.sort\_no\_beta}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.closed\_var\_no\_beta}

\paragraph{Audit gating.} Every module in the stack is gated by
\texttt{check-axioms.py}, which rejects raw \texttt{axiom} declarations;
\texttt{bedc\_ci.py axiom-purity --strict}, which rejects transitive
\texttt{propext}, \texttt{Classical.choice}, and \texttt{Quot.sound}
dependencies; \texttt{bedc\_ci.py audit}, which rejects paper-Lean marker
drift; and \texttt{bedc\_ci.py marker-existence-audit}, which rejects paper
references to non-existent Lean names. A module that fails any of these
audits is rejected at PR merge time.

\paragraph{Stack closure.} The substrate stack is open at the
substitution-preservation layer and closed elsewhere: the remaining layers
carry definitions and theorems that are axiom-free under
\texttt{\#print axioms}. Subject reduction depends on substitution
preservation; closing the substitution layer is the prerequisite for opening
the subject-reduction layer. No layer is closed by importing unverified
content from outside the stack.

\subsection{Current State of the Reconstruction}\label{subsec:current-state}

This subsection records the state of the MetaCIC reconstruction as encoded
in BEDC's Lean library.

\paragraph{Definitions in place.} The represented type theory exposes
$\mathrm{Term}$, $\mathrm{Ctx}$, $\mathrm{HasType}$,
$\mathrm{BetaStep}$, $\mathrm{shift}$, $\mathrm{substitute}$,
$\mathrm{WellFormedCtx}$, $\mathrm{infer}$, $\mathrm{check}$,
$\mathrm{Term.beq}$, $\mathrm{pp}$, $\mathrm{normalizeOneStep}$, and
$\mathrm{elaborate}$. Each definition is axiom-free under
\texttt{\#print axioms}.

\paragraph{Theorems with full proofs.} The library carries axiom-free proofs
for:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Sanity instances of $\mathrm{HasType}$: $\mathrm{sort}$ in the empty
    context, polymorphic identity at the $\mathrm{Sort}$ level,
    $\mathrm{Pi}$ types, and variable lookup in singleton contexts.
  \item A worked encoding of host single-sort polymorphic identity as a
    MetaCIC $\mathrm{Term}$ with its $\mathrm{HasType}$ derivation
    (\autoref{sec:self-comp-metacic}).
  \item Soundness of $\mathrm{Term.beq}$ as structural equality.
  \item Soundness of $\mathrm{infer}$ against $\mathrm{HasType}$.
  \item Soundness of $\mathrm{check}$ against $\mathrm{HasType}$.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Theorems with statements pending proof.} The library carries the
unrestricted target, $\mathrm{substitute\_preserves\_typing}$, guarded by a
$\mathrm{WellFormedCtx}$ premise. Its proof requires weakening of
$\mathrm{HasType}$, substitution-shift composition, and binder substitution
composition. The same substrate also supports
$\mathrm{closed\_term\_substitute\_preserves\_typing}$ as a more tractable
closed-term variant. The unrestricted target depends on the
$\mathrm{WellFormedCtx}$ requirement, binder-shift propagation, and the
induction-based assembly of the available helper lemmas.

\paragraph{Closed-term substitute preservation.} The library carries
a closed-term restriction of substitute preservation:
$$
\begin{aligned}
  \mathrm{closed\_term\_substitute\_preserves\_typing} :
  \mathrm{WellFormedCtx}\ (B :: \Gamma) \to
  \mathrm{ClosedAt}\ 0\ B \to
  \mathrm{ClosedAt}\ 0\ s \to \\
  \mathrm{HasType}\ (B :: \Gamma)\ t\ A \to
  \mathrm{HasType}\ \Gamma\ s\ B \to \\
  \mathrm{HasType}\ \Gamma\ (\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ s\ t)\
    (\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ s\ A).
\end{aligned}
$$
The statement is registered as
$\mathrm{ClosedTermSubstitutePreservesTypingStatement}$. The
infrastructure---$\mathrm{ClosedAt}$ inductive, $\mathrm{shift\_closed}$,
$\mathrm{substitute\_closed}$, $\mathrm{substitute\_shift\_at\_eq}$---is in
place with axiom-free proofs.

\paragraph{Closed-term substitute preservation: case status.}
The closed-term restriction has axiom-free case lemmas covering all five
inductive cases of the typing derivation. The variable case is split by
depth, and the application helper carries a type-equality premise:

\begin{itemize}
  \item $\mathrm{substitute\_sort\_preserves\_typing\_closed\_anchor}$ (sort case);
  \item $\mathrm{substitute\_var\_zero\_preserves\_typing\_closed\_anchor}$ (variable, depth 0);
  \item $\mathrm{substitute\_var\_nonzero\_preserves\_typing\_closed\_anchor}$ (variable, depth > 0);
  \item $\mathrm{substitute\_pi\_preserves\_typing\_closed\_anchor}$ (Pi);
  \item $\mathrm{substitute\_lam\_preserves\_typing\_closed\_anchor}$ (lambda, proved via term induction; the corresponding $\mathrm{HasType}$-induction path depends on binder-shift propagation);
  \item $\mathrm{substitute\_app\_preserves\_typing\_closed\_anchor\_with\_type\_eq}$ (application, conditional on a substitute composition equation).
\end{itemize}

The main theorem depends on two proof obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Prove the substitute composition equation
    $\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ s\ (\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ a\ \mathrm{cod})
    = \mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ (\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ s\ a)\
      (\mathrm{substitute}\ 1\ s\ \mathrm{cod})$, discharging the
    application conditional.
  \item Run the outer induction on the typing derivation, supplying each
    case lemma's recursive premises and assembling them into the main theorem.
\end{enumerate}

The closed-term restriction avoids the binder-shift propagation obstruction by
ensuring the substitution targets and source terms have no free variables that
would expose it.

\paragraph{Substitute composition.} The closure-restricted form of
the substitute composition equation
$$
\begin{aligned}
  \mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ s\ (\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ a\ \mathrm{cod}) =
  \mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ (\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ s\ a)\
    (\mathrm{substitute}\ 1\ s\ \mathrm{cod})
\end{aligned}
$$
is established for five of five inductive cases of $\texttt{cod}$:
sort, var, app, lam, and pi. The sort case is unconditional, the var
case assumes $\mathrm{ClosedAt}\ 0\ s$, the app case uses two recursive
premises, and the lam and pi cases are conditional on a
shift-substitute commutation lemma
$$
\begin{aligned}
  \mathrm{shift}\ 0\ 1\ (\mathrm{substitute}\ 0\ s\ a) =
  \mathrm{substitute}\ 1\ (\mathrm{shift}\ 0\ 1\ s)\
    (\mathrm{shift}\ 0\ 1\ a).
\end{aligned}
$$
The commutation lemma is the required premise for full unconditional
composition closure.

\paragraph{Host reflection bridge.}
$\texttt{BEDC.MetaCIC.HostBridge}.\mathrm{reflectExpr}$ covers eight
host Lean $\mathrm{Expr}$ constructors: $\mathrm{sort}$,
$\mathrm{bvar}$, $\mathrm{app}$, $\mathrm{lam}$,
$\mathrm{forallE}$, $\mathrm{const}$, $\mathrm{fvar}$ returning
$\mathrm{none}$, and $\mathrm{mdata}$ as a passthrough. The elaboration
commands $\#\mathrm{reflect\_metacic}\ e$ and
$\#\mathrm{metacic\_decide}\ e$ expose the bridge: the former prints
the reflected $\mathrm{Term}$, and the latter runs $\mathrm{infer}$ on
that reflected term and prints the inferred MetaCIC type.

\paragraph{Out of scope.} The reconstruction does not currently implement
strong normalization, confluence, full universe hierarchy, inductive
declarations within MetaCIC, or a verified embedding into the host Lean kernel.
These remain research targets as outlined in
\autoref{sec:self-comp-reflection-bridge}.

\paragraph{Audit guarantees.} The reconstruction's status is maintained by
four standing audits at PR merge time:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{check-axioms.py} rejects any \texttt{axiom} declaration.
  \item \texttt{bedc\_ci.py axiom-purity --strict} rejects theorems whose
    transitive axiom dependency includes \texttt{Classical.choice},
    \texttt{Quot.sound}, or \texttt{propext}.
  \item \texttt{bedc\_ci.py audit} rejects
    $\backslash\mathtt{leanchecked}\{X\}$ markers whose $X$ does not exist in
    \texttt{lean4/BEDC/}.
  \item \texttt{bedc\_ci.py marker-existence-audit} reports the same
    information as a standing first-class audit.
\end{itemize}
The current state is coherent. Formal targets without full proof terms are
reported as such, and the audit guarantees make this distinction enforceable.
