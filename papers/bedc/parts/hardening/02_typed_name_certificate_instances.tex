\chapter{Typed Naming Certificate Instances}
\label{ch:hardening-typed-name-certificate-instances}

The BEDC paper template specifies the five fields of a derived-interface naming certificate. This chapter records explicit instance templates. These are not complete reconstructions of traditional mathematics. They are interface seeds whose role is to show how the naming mechanism should be used.

\section{Certificate fields}

\begin{definition}[Typed naming certificate, expanded template]
\label{def:typed-naming-certificate-expanded-template}
A typed paper certificate template for a derived interface \(N\) is a record
$$
  \NameCert(N)=
  (\SourceSpec_N,\PatternSpec_N,\ClassifierSpec_N,\StabCert_N,\LedgerPolicy_N).
$$
The fields have the following intended roles:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \(\SourceSpec_N\): declares the permitted source histories or visible tokens.
  \item \(\PatternSpec_N\): states the behavior pattern being named.
  \item \(\ClassifierSpec_N\): states how the behavior is packaged or identified.
  \item \(\StabCert_N\): records closure, reuse, descent, composition, or seal stability.
  \item \(\LedgerPolicy_N\): specifies which gap ledger records the compression performed by the name.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.SemanticNameCert}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.NameCert\_iff\_semantic\_fields}

\begin{theorem}[Semantic NameCert pattern-ledger witness]
\label{thm:semantic-namecert-pattern-ledger-witness}
Every semantic naming certificate contains a source history whose pattern
condition and ledger policy both hold.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Eliminate the semantic certificate to expose its core naming certificate and
the pattern and ledger soundness fields. The core certificate supplies a source
history satisfying the source specification. Applying the two soundness fields
to that same source witness yields the required pattern and ledger witnesses.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.semanticNameCert\_pattern\_ledger\_witness}

\begin{theorem}[Semantic NameCert pattern-ledger transport]
\label{thm:semantic-namecert-pattern-ledger-transport}
If a semantic naming certificate classifies a source history \(h\) with a
history \(k\), then the pattern condition and ledger policy both hold at \(k\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use carrier transport from the core naming certificate along the classifier
judgment to obtain the source specification at \(k\). The semantic pattern and
ledger soundness fields then apply at the transported history.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.semanticNameCert\_pattern\_ledger\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Semantic NameCert classifier chain transport]
\label{thm:semantic-namecert-classifier-chain-transport}
If a semantic naming certificate classifies $h$ with $k$ and $k$ with $r$, then
a source witness at $h$ transports the pattern condition and ledger policy to
$r$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use transitivity from the core naming certificate to compose the classifier
judgments into a classifier judgment from $h$ to $r$. The pattern-ledger
transport theorem then applies to this composite classifier judgment and the
source witness at $h$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.semanticNameCert\_classifier\_chain\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Semantic NameCert classifier left-confluence transport]
\label{thm:semantic-namecert-classifier-left-confluence-transport}
If a semantic naming certificate classifies a shared left endpoint $h$ with
$k$ and with $r$, then a source witness at $k$ transports the pattern condition
and ledger policy to $r$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use symmetry from the core naming certificate to reverse the classifier
judgment from $h$ to $k$, then use transitivity with the classifier judgment
from $h$ to $r$. This gives a classifier judgment from $k$ to $r$, so
pattern-ledger transport applies to the source witness at $k$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.semanticNameCert\_classifier\_left\_confluence\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Semantic NameCert classifier right-confluence transport]
\label{thm:semantic-name-cert-classifier-right-confluence-transport}
If a semantic naming certificate classifies $h$ and $k$ with a shared right
endpoint $r$, then a source witness at $h$ transports the pattern condition
and ledger policy to $k$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use symmetry from the core naming certificate to reverse the classifier
judgment from $k$ to $r$, giving a classifier judgment from $r$ to $k$. Then
use transitivity with the classifier judgment from $h$ to $r$. This gives a
classifier judgment from $h$ to $k$, so pattern-ledger transport applies to
the source witness at $h$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.semanticNameCert\_classifier\_right\_confluence\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Semantic NameCert classifier reverse-chain transport]
\label{thm:semantic-name-cert-classifier-reverse-chain-transport}
If a semantic naming certificate classifies \(h\) with \(k\) and \(k\) with
\(r\), then a source witness at \(r\) transports the pattern condition and
ledger policy back to \(h\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use transitivity from the core naming certificate to compose the two classifier
judgments into a classifier judgment from \(h\) to \(r\). Symmetry reverses
this composite into a classifier judgment from \(r\) to \(h\), and the
pattern-ledger transport theorem applies to the source witness at \(r\).
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.semanticNameCert\_classifier\_reverse\_chain\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Semantic NameCert classifier diamond transport]
\label{thm:semantic-namecert-classifier-diamond-transport}
If a semantic naming certificate classifies $a$ with $b$, classifies $a$ with
$c$, and classifies $b$ with $d$, then a source witness at $c$ transports the
pattern condition and ledger policy to $d$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use symmetry to reverse the classifier judgment from $a$ to $c$. Transitivity
then carries $c$ to $b$ through $a$, and another transitivity step carries
$c$ to $d$ through $b$. The pattern-ledger transport theorem applies to this
composite classifier judgment and the source witness at $c$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.semanticNameCert\_classifier\_diamond\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Carrier self semantic lifting]
\label{thm:namecert-carrier-self-semantic-lifting}
Let $C : \mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and
$R : \mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$. Any core certificate
$\mathsf{core}:\NameCert(C,R)$ induces a semantic certificate whose core field is
$\mathsf{core}$ and whose pattern and ledger soundness fields are identity
implications over the same carrier. This is the carrier-as-pattern and
carrier-as-ledger paper certificate target, distinct from
\texttt{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.NameCert\_iff\_semantic\_fields}. The target is
$$
\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(C,C,C,R).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Choose carrier $C$, classifier $R$, pattern $C$, and ledger $C$. Keep the
supplied certificate as the $\mathsf{core}$ field. Stability under classifier
transport is exactly the carrier stability field already contained in that core
certificate, and both semantic soundness fields are filled by the identity
implication from a carrier witness of $C(h)$ to the same proposition $C(h)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.NameCert\_carrier\_self\_semantic\_lifting}

\begin{theorem}[Eqtype class semantic NameCert]
\label{thm:eqtype-class-semantic-namecert}
For any BEDC history anchor $a$, the anchor class
$h\mapsto \hsame(h,a)$ is a semantic naming certificate when the source,
pattern, and ledger predicates are this same class and the classifier is
$\hsame$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The anchor itself inhabits the class by reflexivity of $\hsame$. Reflexivity,
symmetry, and transitivity of $\hsame$ supply the core classifier laws.
Carrier transport reverses the classifier comparison and composes it with the
displayed anchor comparison. The pattern and ledger fields are the identity
implications over the same anchor class.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EqtypeUp.EqtypeClass\_semanticNameCert}

\section{Unary repetition as \texorpdfstring{\(\NatUp\)}{Nat-up} seed}

\begin{definition}[Unary-source specification]
\label{def:unary-source-specification-hardening}
The unary-source specification is the class of histories generated by repeated extension with \(\bone\) from the empty history. It is not called a natural number object internally. It is a source pattern of repeated emission.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.UnarySourceSpec\_induction}

\begin{definition}[Unary pattern]
\label{def:unary-pattern-hardening}
The unary pattern is the stable behavior of repeated \(\bone\)-extension together with the ability to distinguish the empty history from an extended unary history by the generated history constructors.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.UnaryPatternSpec\_step}
\leanvariant{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.unary\_pattern\_induction\_with\_discrimination}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{\(\NatUp\)}{Nat-up} certificate seed]
\label{def:natup-certificate-seed}
The name \(\NatUp\) is licensed only when the following fields are supplied:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item source: unary histories;
  \item pattern: repeated \(\bone\)-extension;
  \item classifier: internal history-sameness restricted to unary histories;
  \item stability: closure under further \(\bone\)-extension and reuse as a counting interface;
  \item ledger: the originating unary history behind each visible counting token.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}
\leanvariant{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.nat\_up\_interface\_seed}

\begin{corollary}[\texorpdfstring{\(\NatUp\)}{Nat-up} is not primitive]
\label{cor:natup-certificate-seed-not-primitive}
The interface \(\NatUp\) is licensed by a naming certificate over unary histories. It is not an internal primitive of the kernel.
\end{corollary}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.nat\_up\_certificate\_seed\_not\_primitive}

\section{Continuation as \texorpdfstring{\(\AddUp\)}{Add-up} seed}

\begin{definition}[Continuation pattern]
\label{def:continuation-pattern-hardening}
The continuation pattern is the relation \(\Cont(h,k,r)\), read as: the history \(h\) continued by the history \(k\) yields result \(r\), up to internal history-sameness.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.Cont.continuation\_pattern\_stability\_fields}

\begin{definition}[\texorpdfstring{\(\AddUp\)}{Add-up} certificate seed]
The name \(\AddUp\) is licensed only when the following fields are supplied:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item source: pairs of unary histories;
  \item pattern: relational continuation;
  \item classifier: internal history-sameness of continuation results;
  \item stability: left unit, right unit, determinacy up to \(\HSame\), and associativity up to \(\HSame\);
  \item ledger: the pair of source unary histories and the continuation witness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Addition-like behavior is a certificate, not an operation]
\label{thm:addition-like-behavior-certificate-not-operation}
The interface \(\AddUp\) is not admitted by writing a binary operation symbol. It is admitted by a naming certificate whose stability field contains unit and associativity witnesses for relational continuation.
\end{theorem}
\leanvariant{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.add\_up\_licensed\_not\_primitive}

\begin{proof}
The kernel contains histories and the relation \(\Cont\). It does not contain a primitive binary operation called addition. If continuation is shown to be stable on the unary source pattern and its behavior descends to the unary classification, then a derived interface may be named \(\AddUp\). The name records the evidence; it does not introduce a primitive operation.
\end{proof}

\section{Descent certificate for stable transformations}

\begin{definition}[Descent certificate]
\label{def:descent-certificate}
A descent certificate for a transformation pattern states that the pattern respects the classifier on the source and the classifier on the target. In explanatory notation, if \(a\) and \(a'\) are source-same, then their transformed outputs are target-same.
\end{definition}

\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.StableTransformation\_descentCertificate\_exists}

\begin{theorem}[Descent certificate respects source sameness]
\label{thm:descent-certificate-respects}
If a descent certificate maps a source classifier to a target classifier, then source-same inputs have target-same mapped outputs.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Stable transformations descend to packages]
\label{thm:stable-transformations-descend-to-packages}
If visible source package tokens introduce \(p\) and \(q\), and the source classifier identifies them, then any supplied stability witness that respects the package classifier transports that identification to the target package classifier.

\begin{theorem}[Descent-certificate composition for stable transformations]
\label{thm:descent-certificate-stable-transformation-composition}
Let $S,R,Q:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be source, intermediate, and target carrier specifications, and let
$$
\sim_S,\sim_R,\sim_Q:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}
$$
be their classifiers. Assume the classifier roles are licensed by
$$
\mathsf{cert}_S:\NameCert(S,\sim_S),\qquad
\mathsf{cert}_R:\NameCert(R,\sim_R),\qquad
\mathsf{cert}_Q:\NameCert(Q,\sim_Q).
$$
Let $T,U:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}$ have carrier-preservation fields
$$
\rho_T:\forall h.\;S(h)\to R(T(h))
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\rho_U:\forall r.\;R(r)\to Q(U(r)).
$$
Assume descent certificates
$$
\delta_T:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_R)
$$
and
$$
\delta_U:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_R,\sim_Q)
$$
whose map fields are $T$ and $U$. Then the composite map $U\circ T$ preserves carriers by
$$
\rho_{U\circ T}(h,Sh)=\rho_U(T(h),\rho_T(h,Sh))
$$
and carries a descent certificate
$$
\delta_{U\circ T}:\mathsf{DescentCertificate}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\sim_S,\sim_Q)
$$
with map field $h\mapsto U(T(h))$. Thus any source-classifier witness $h\sim_S k$ gives the target-classifier witness $U(T(h))\sim_Q U(T(k))$.

Moreover, if
$$
\tau_T:\mathsf{StableTransformation}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},L_T,\sim_S,\sim_R)
$$
and
$$
\tau_U:\mathsf{StableTransformation}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},L_U,\sim_R,\sim_Q)
$$
have map fields $T$ and $U$, then their composite is a stable transformation
$$
\mathsf{StableTransformation}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},L_T\times L_U,\sim_S,\sim_Q)
$$
whose ledger witness is the ordered pair of the two input ledger witnesses. In gap-ledger cases this ordered pair is the witness-level form of the composite ledger policy of \autoref{def:hardening-composite-gap-ledger} and \autoref{cor:ledger-composition-principle}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.NameCert.StableTransformation\_descentCertificate\_composition}
\begin{proof}
The three displayed naming certificates place $S$, $R$, and $Q$ in the typed certificate template of \autoref{def:typed-naming-certificate-expanded-template}; the descent argument uses the respect fields of the two descent certificates.

Define $\delta_{U\circ T}$ with map field $h\mapsto U(T(h))$. Given $h\sim_S k$, the respect field of $\delta_T$ gives
$$
T(h)\sim_R T(k).
$$
Applying the respect field of $\delta_U$ to this intermediate classifier witness gives
$$
U(T(h))\sim_Q U(T(k)).
$$
By \autoref{def:descent-certificate}, this is exactly the respect field required for a descent certificate from $\sim_S$ to $\sim_Q$. Carrier preservation is the displayed composite of the two carrier-preservation fields.

For the stable-transformation statement, use the same composite map. If $h\sim_S k$, the respect field of $\tau_T$ gives $T(h)\sim_R T(k)$, and the respect field of $\tau_U$ gives $U(T(h))\sim_Q U(T(k))$. The ledger field of \autoref{def:stable-transformation} supplies witnesses $\ell_T:L_T$ and $\ell_U:L_U$; pair them as $(\ell_T,\ell_U):L_T\times L_U$. This gives the ledger field for the composite stable transformation. When the two ledgers are gap ledgers, the same pair records the first-stage ledger and the second-stage ledger pulled back along the intermediate interface, which is the composite ledger policy cited above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NameCert composition for stable transformations]
\label{thm:semantic-namecert-stable-transformation-composition}
Let $S,R,Q,P_S,P_R,P_Q,L_S,L_R,L_Q:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, and let
$$
\sim_S,\sim_R,\sim_Q:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}
$$
be classifier specifications. Assume semantic carrier certificates
$$
\mathcal{C}_S:\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(S,P_S,L_S,\sim_S),\qquad
\mathcal{C}_R:\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(R,P_R,L_R,\sim_R),\qquad
\mathcal{C}_Q:\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(Q,P_Q,L_Q,\sim_Q).
$$
Let $T,U:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}$ have carrier-preservation fields
$$
\rho_T:\forall h.\;S(h)\to R(T(h)),
\qquad
\rho_U:\forall r.\;R(r)\to Q(U(r)).
$$
Let $\Lambda_T$ and $\Lambda_U$ be ledger witness types, and let
$$
\tau_T:\mathsf{StableTransformation}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\Lambda_T,\sim_S,\sim_R)
$$
and
$$
\tau_U:\mathsf{StableTransformation}(\mathsf{BHist},\mathsf{BHist},\Lambda_U,\sim_R,\sim_Q)
$$
be stable transformations with map fields $T$ and $U$. Let
$$
M_T,M_U:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}
$$
be their two-stage ledger predicates, with ledger soundness fields
$$
\lambda_T:\forall h.\;S(h)\to M_T(h,T(h)),
\qquad
\lambda_U:\forall r.\;R(r)\to M_U(r,U(r)).
$$
Define source-level composite pattern and ledger predicates by
$$
\mathsf{CompStab}_{T,U}(h)\Longleftrightarrow
Q(U(T(h)))\land
\forall k,\;h\sim_S k\to U(T(h))\sim_Q U(T(k))
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{CompLedger}_{T,U}(h)\Longleftrightarrow
\exists r,\;r=T(h)\land M_T(h,r)\land M_U(r,U(r)).
$$
Then
$$
\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(S,\mathsf{CompStab}_{T,U},\mathsf{CompLedger}_{T,U},\sim_S)
$$
holds. Its stability component is the composite descent witness for $U\circ T$, and its ledger component is the composite ledger policy.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the core field of $\mathcal{C}_S$ as the core field of the displayed semantic certificate. By \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}, this is exactly the carrier-and-classifier certificate for $S$ and $\sim_S$. The core fields of $\mathcal{C}_R$ and $\mathcal{C}_Q$ place the intermediate and target classifiers in the same typed certificate discipline of \autoref{def:typed-naming-certificate-expanded-template}.

For pattern soundness, fix $h$ with $Sh:S(h)$. Carrier preservation gives $\rho_T(h,Sh):R(T(h))$, and then $\rho_U(T(h),\rho_T(h,Sh)):Q(U(T(h)))$. If $h\sim_S k$, the respect field of $\tau_T$ gives $T(h)\sim_R T(k)$. Applying the respect field of $\tau_U$ gives $U(T(h))\sim_Q U(T(k))$. Hence $\mathsf{CompStab}_{T,U}(h)$.

For ledger soundness at the same source history, choose the intermediate witness $r=T(h)$. The field $\lambda_T$ gives $M_T(h,T(h))$. The field $\rho_T$ gives $R(T(h))$, so $\lambda_U$ gives $M_U(T(h),U(T(h)))$. These witnesses prove $\mathsf{CompLedger}_{T,U}(h)$.

The respect calculation is the descent certificate required by \autoref{def:descent-certificate}: a source-classifier witness is first transported along $T$ and then along $U$. By \autoref{def:naming-certificate-stability-field}, this fills the $\DescentCert$ and $\CompositionCert$ modes of the stability component. The ledger proof records the first-stage ledger together with the second-stage ledger pulled back along the intermediate witness. In gap-ledger cases this is the two-step witness of \autoref{def:hardening-composite-gap-ledger}, and \autoref{cor:ledger-composition-principle} identifies it as the composite ledger policy.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Semantic NameCert classifier replacement]
\label{thm:semantic-namecert-classifier-replacement}
Let $S,P,L:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ and let
$C,C':\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be classifier specifications. Assume
$$
\mathcal{N}:\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(S,P,L,C)
$$
and assume classifier comparison fields
$$
\alpha:\forall h,k,\; C'(h,k)\to C(h,k),
\qquad
\beta:\forall h,k,\; C(h,k)\to C'(h,k).
$$
Then
$$
\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(S,P,L,C')
$$
holds. The resulting certificate also has the same pattern-ledger transport consequence: whenever $C'(h,k)$ and $S(h)$ hold, both $P(k)$ and $L(k)$ hold.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:typed-naming-certificate-expanded-template}, a semantic naming certificate separates its core carrier-classifier certificate from its pattern and ledger soundness fields. Keep the source, pattern, and ledger soundness fields of $\mathcal{N}$ unchanged. It remains only to replace the core classifier certificate.

The original core field is a $\NameCert(S,C)$ in the sense of \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}. Define the new core field over $C'$ as follows. The inhabited source witness is the same one carried by the original core. For reflexivity at a source history $h$, apply the old reflexivity field to obtain $C(h,h)$ and then apply $\beta$. For symmetry, convert a witness $C'(h,k)$ to $C(h,k)$ by $\alpha$, apply the old symmetry field, and convert the result back by $\beta$. For transitivity, convert both $C'$ witnesses to $C$ by $\alpha$, apply the old transitivity field, and convert the composite classifier witness back by $\beta$. For carrier transport, convert $C'(h,k)$ to $C(h,k)$ by $\alpha$ and then use the old carrier-transport field to carry $S(h)$ to $S(k)$.

These four classifier laws together with the unchanged source witness form $\NameCert(S,C')$. Combining this new core field with the unchanged pattern and ledger soundness fields gives $\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(S,P,L,C')$. Finally, if $C'(h,k)$ and $S(h)$ hold, the new carrier-transport field gives $S(k)$, and the unchanged pattern and ledger soundness fields give $P(k)$ and $L(k)$. This is the same transport consequence recorded in \autoref{thm:semantic-namecert-pattern-ledger-transport} for the replacement classifier.
\end{proof}
