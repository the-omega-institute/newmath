\chapter{Empty as the Fable Machine: Observer-Sequence Growth and Selector Ledgers}
\label{ch:capstones-empty-fable-machine}

The preceding capstone (\autoref{ch:capstones-observer-hist-identity}) identifies the observer with the history of its distinctions: $\mathit{observer} = \Hist$, $\mathit{observation} = \Cont$, time = Hist growth. That identification fixes what an observer is, but does not yet spell out how the observer sequence grows. The present chapter fills that gap. The empty history $\emp$ carries no information; nevertheless every non-empty history is generated from $\emp$ by a finite sequence of distinction acts. When more than one branch is locally admissible, the realized history contains the selector witness for the branch that was enacted. Empty is therefore not an observer-substance and not a truth oracle. It is the zero-information boundary at which distinction becomes generative: the fable machine. This chapter falls under the project's axiom-purity gate; no $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$, no $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$, and no $\mathsf{propext}$ is required for any cited target.

\section{Empty as zero-information boundary}
\label{sec:efm-empty-vs-e}

The kernel value $\emp$ and the boundary at which distinction enters above any history must be distinguished at the reading level: $\emp$ is the unique $\Hist$ whose distinction record is empty; the empty boundary above any $\Hist$ is the zero-information slot at which the next $\Ezero$ or $\Eone$ application has not yet been enacted. Both are read as ``Empty,'' but each non-trivial observation is generated at the empty boundary above the current Hist, not by resetting the history to $\emp$.

\begin{definition}[Empty boundary]
\label{def:efm-empty-boundary}
For a $\Hist$ value $h$, the \emph{empty boundary above $h$} is the zero-information slot at which a singleton extension may apply: the unique slot at which $\Ext(\,h,\,m,\,r\,)$ becomes admissible for some mark $m$, with the resulting $r$ recording $m$ as a fresh distinction above $h$. This is a reading-level locator; the formal target is the kernel relation $\Ext$ on $\Hist$.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Empty carries no selector]
\label{thm:efm-empty-no-selector}
The Hist value $\emp$ exposes no $b_0$/$b_1$ selector. The information content of $\emp$ is the empty sequence, and any reading that recovers a mark from a Hist value must recurse on a non-empty constructor, never on $\emp$ alone.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The information content recursion (\autoref{def:hist-information-content}) returns the empty sequence at $\emp$. Every $\Eone$ or $\Ezero$ application records its tag in the constructor of $\Hist$; the no-confusion lemmas separating $\emp$ from $\Eone(h)$ and from $\Ezero(h)$ rule out the existence of a Hist that records a mark while remaining $\hsame$-equivalent to $\emp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Empty is not a hidden subject]
\label{rem:efm-no-hidden-subject}
Empty is not an information reservoir, nor a substance behind the trace, nor a global chooser. Its role is structural: the zero-information boundary at which the first visible distinction is enacted. Every claim made later in this chapter about ``selecting'' a branch will be redeemed by a constructive witness on the kernel surface, not by an unwitnessed appeal to Empty.
\end{remark}

\section{One-step birth: from empty boundary to singleton continuation}
\label{sec:efm-one-step-birth}

\begin{definition}[Singleton tail]
\label{def:efm-singleton-tail}
For a mark $m \in \{b_0, b_1\}$, the \emph{singleton tail} of $m$ is the $\Hist$ value $\Ezero(\emp)$ when $m = b_0$ and $\Eone(\emp)$ when $m = b_1$. The singleton tail records exactly one distinction above $\emp$ and nothing else.
\leandef{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.singletonTail}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Empty-boundary step]
\label{def:efm-empty-step}
An \emph{empty-boundary step} from $h$ enacted by mark $m$ with result $r$ is the relation $\Ext(\,h,\,m,\,r\,)$, read at the chapter level as filling the empty boundary above $h$ with the mark $m$. The relation is definitionally identical to the kernel-level extension; the wrapper name carries the reading.
\leandef{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.EmptyStep}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Empty-boundary step is the kernel extension]
\label{thm:efm-empty-step-iff-ext}
For every $h$, $m$, $r$: an empty-boundary step from $h$ enacting $m$ to $r$ holds if and only if $\Ext(\,h,\,m,\,r\,)$ holds.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.emptyStep\_iff\_ext}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton-tail constructors]
\label{thm:efm-singleton-tail-construct}
$b_0$-stepping above $\emp$ produces the $b_0$ singleton tail, and $b_1$-stepping above $\emp$ produces the $b_1$ singleton tail.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.emptyStep\_singletonTail\_b0}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.emptyStep\_singletonTail\_b1}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Empty-step result is non-empty]
\label{thm:efm-empty-step-result-nonempty}
For any empty-boundary step from $h$ enacting $m$ with result $r$, the result $r$ is not $\hsame$-equivalent to $\emp$.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.emptyStep\_result\_ne\_empty}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Cross-mark step no-confusion]
\label{thm:efm-empty-step-cross-mark-noconf}
A $b_0$-empty-step and a $b_1$-empty-step from the same source $h$ never share a result up to $\hsame$.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.emptyStep\_cross\_mark\_no\_confusion}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Empty-step totality]
\label{thm:efm-empty-step-total}
For every $h$ and every $m$, an empty-boundary step from $h$ enacting $m$ exists.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.emptyStep\_total}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Empty-step determinism]
\label{thm:efm-empty-step-det}
For every $h$ and every $m$, the result of an empty-boundary step is unique up to $\hsame$.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.emptyStep\_deterministic}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Each step is local; no reset to $\emp$]
\label{rem:efm-no-reset}
The reading ``each observation is born from Empty'' refers to the empty boundary above the current Hist, not to a reset of the history to $\emp$. The accumulated sequence $h_0, h_1, \dots, h_n$ has $h_{i+1}$ obtained from $h_i$ by an empty-boundary step, with $h_i$ retaining its full distinction record; the singleton tail $\Ezero(\emp)$ or $\Eone(\emp)$ is the local shape of that step, not the global state.
\end{remark}

\section{Observer sequence as finite selector ledger}
\label{sec:efm-observer-sequence}

\begin{definition}[Trace]
\label{def:efm-trace}
A \emph{trace} of a $\Hist$ value $h$ is the finite sequence $\langle s_0, s_1, \dots, s_{n-1}\rangle$ of marks read off the constructor spine of $h$ in chronological order: at each $\Ezero$ application the mark $b_0$ is recorded, at each $\Eone$ application the mark $b_1$ is recorded, and at $\emp$ the sequence is empty.
\leandef{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.Trace}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Every Hist has a trace]
\label{thm:efm-trace-exists}
For every $\Hist$ value $h$, there exists a finite mark sequence $xs$ such that $h$ has trace $xs$.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.trace\_exists}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Empty trace iff empty Hist]
\label{thm:efm-trace-empty-iff}
A $\Hist$ value $h$ has the empty trace if and only if $h$ is $\emp$.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.trace\_empty\_iff}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Trace transports along $\hsame$]
\label{thm:efm-trace-hsame-transport}
If $h$ and $k$ are $\hsame$-equivalent and $h$ has trace $xs$, then $k$ has trace $xs$.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.trace\_hsame\_transport}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Trace determines Hist up to $\hsame$]
\label{thm:efm-trace-determines-hist}
If $h$ and $k$ both have trace $xs$, then $h$ and $k$ are $\hsame$-equivalent.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.trace\_same\_marks\_hsame}
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Observer identity is sequence identity]
\label{cor:efm-observer-as-sequence}
Under the identification $\mathit{observer} = \Hist$ (\autoref{cor:observer-is-hist}), two observers are the same observer (modulo $\hsame$) precisely when their selector ledgers are the same finite sequence of marks. The observer sequence is not a label attached from outside; it is the constructor spine of the Hist read as a list of distinctions.
\end{corollary}

\section{Multi-solution branch space and realized selector}
\label{sec:efm-multi-branch}

\begin{definition}[Local branch admissibility]
\label{def:efm-adnext}
For a local branch predicate $\Gamma : \Hist \to \mathit{Mark} \to \Hist \to \mathsf{Prop}$, the predicate $\mathsf{AdNext}_\Gamma(h, m)$ holds when there exists a Hist $r$ such that the empty-boundary step from $h$ enacting $m$ produces $r$ and $\Gamma(h, m, r)$ holds. The predicate $\Gamma$ is read at the chapter level as ``the branch is locally licensed''; no global selection is built.
\leandef{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.AdNext}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Multi-solution boundary]
\label{def:efm-multinext}
The branch space at $h$ under $\Gamma$ is \emph{multi-solution}, written $\mathsf{MultiNext}_\Gamma(h)$, when both $\mathsf{AdNext}_\Gamma(h, b_0)$ and $\mathsf{AdNext}_\Gamma(h, b_1)$ hold.
\leandef{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.MultiNext}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Selector step]
\label{def:efm-selstep}
A \emph{selector step} from $h$ to $r$ under $\Gamma$ is a witness $(m, \mathit{step}, \mathit{cert})$ where $m$ is a mark, $\mathit{step}$ is an empty-boundary step from $h$ enacting $m$ to $r$, and $\mathit{cert}$ is a $\Gamma(h, m, r)$ certificate. The mark $m$ is exposed by the witness; no hidden chooser is invoked.
\leandef{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.SelStep}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Selector step exposes a mark]
\label{thm:efm-selstep-exposes}
Every selector step from $h$ to $r$ under $\Gamma$ exposes a mark $m$ such that an empty-boundary step from $h$ enacts $m$ to $r$.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.selStep\_exposes\_mark}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Selector step from step + certificate]
\label{thm:efm-selstep-from-step}
Given an empty-boundary step from $h$ enacting $m$ to $r$ and a $\Gamma(h, m, r)$ certificate, a selector step from $h$ to $r$ under $\Gamma$ exists.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.selStep\_from\_step}
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Distinct realized branches stay separated]
\label{thm:efm-distinct-branches-separated}
If two selector steps from the same source $h$ recover distinct marks $m_0 \neq m_1$, their targets are not $\hsame$-equivalent.
\leanchecked{BEDC.Capstone.EmptyFableMachine.selStep\_distinct\_branches\_separated}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Specific sequence implies accumulated ledger]
\label{rem:efm-specific-sequence-ledger}
When the local branch space is multi-solution but the realized observer sequence is specific, the specificity is not an unexplained miracle. Each step records the mark of the branch that was enacted as a constructor in $\Hist$; the trace of that Hist (\autoref{def:efm-trace}) is the accumulated selector ledger. There is no global choice function in the kernel and none is needed: each step's selector is a local witness on the Hist surface itself, and the entire ledger is recovered by reading the constructor spine.
\end{remark}
