\chapter{Real Completeness via Regular Cauchy Sequences}
\label{ch:capstones-real-completeness}

This capstone records the conditional analytical target for the Phase Real and Completion milestone of \autoref{sec:roadmap-l10-construction-order}. It treats the real naming certificate $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}) as complete in the Bishop sense only after the DyadicRatCore, StreamNameUp, RegSeqRat, and RealUp certificate obligations are available at machine-checked strength. The chapter defines a regular Cauchy sequence over $\RegSeqRat$ as a sequence of regular rational sequences together with an explicit modulus of convergence, gives the intended limit modulus and $\RegSeqRat$ representative by diagonal extraction, and records the uniqueness criterion up to the standard $\psame$ equivalence on naming certificates. The construction is fully constructive as a proof target: every modulus is an explicit function on naturals and rationals, no real number is identified with an equivalence class, and the required Lean target is expected to use neither $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$ nor $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$ nor $\mathsf{propext}$.

\section{Regular Cauchy sequences and their limits}
\label{sec:real-completeness-regular-cauchy}

\begin{definition}[Regular sequence over $\RatUp$]
\label{def:real-completeness-regular-rat-sequence}
A \emph{regular sequence} over $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ is a function $x \colon \mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{Q}$ together with the explicit regularity witness
$$ \forall\, m, n \in \mathbb{N}, \quad |x_m - x_n| \;\le\; \tfrac{1}{m+1} + \tfrac{1}{n+1}. $$
The carrier $\RegSeqRat$ of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} packages such pairs as a closed inductive over $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ data, with $\RealClassifier$ the $\psame$ closure that identifies two regular sequences whose pointwise difference shrinks at the regularity rate.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy sequence over $\RegSeqRat$]
\label{def:real-completeness-regular-cauchy-rat}
A \emph{regular Cauchy sequence} of regular rational sequences is a function $X \colon \mathbb{N} \to \RegSeqRat$ together with an explicit modulus $\mu \colon \mathbb{Q}_{>0} \to \mathbb{N}$ such that, for every $\varepsilon \in \mathbb{Q}_{>0}$ and every $m, n \ge \mu(\varepsilon)$, the regular rational sequence $X_m - X_n$ (computed pointwise on representatives) lies inside the $\varepsilon$-ball at $0$ in the sense of $\NameCert_{\RegSeqRat}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Diagonal limit representative]
\label{def:real-completeness-diagonal-limit}
The \emph{diagonal limit representative} of $(X, \mu)$ is the rational sequence $L \colon \mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{Q}$ given by
$$ L_n \;=\; \big(X_{\mu(1/(n+1))}\big)_{\,\mu(1/(n+1)) + n + 1}, $$
that is, the value at index $\mu(1/(n+1)) + n + 1$ of the $\mu(1/(n+1))$-th member of the Cauchy sequence. The construction is primitive recursion on $n$ together with $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ arithmetic on the indices.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Diagonal limit is a regular sequence]
\label{thm:real-completeness-diagonal-regular}
The diagonal limit representative $L$ of \autoref{def:real-completeness-diagonal-limit} is a regular sequence in the sense of \autoref{def:real-completeness-regular-rat-sequence}: for every $m, n \in \mathbb{N}$,
$$ |L_m - L_n| \;\le\; \tfrac{1}{m+1} + \tfrac{1}{n+1}. $$
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
Fix $m \le n$. Bound $|L_m - L_n|$ by triangle inequality through three intermediate points: the value of $X_{\mu(1/(m+1))}$ at the diagonal index, the value of $X_{\mu(1/(n+1))}$ at the same index, and the value at $L_n$'s diagonal index. The first and third differences are controlled by the regularity of the constituent regular sequences (\autoref{def:real-completeness-regular-rat-sequence}); the middle difference is controlled by the Cauchy modulus $\mu$ (\autoref{def:real-completeness-regular-cauchy-rat}). Each bound is at most $1/(m+1) + 1/(n+1)$ after a one-line rational arithmetic step.
\end{proof}

\section{The limit theorem}
\label{sec:real-completeness-limit-theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Real completeness proof target over $\RegSeqRat$]
\label{thm:real-completeness-limit-exists-unique}
Assume that the DyadicRatCore, StreamNameUp, RegSeqRat, and RealUp certificate obligations of Phase Real and Completion are supplied at machine-checked strength. For every regular Cauchy sequence $(X, \mu)$ over $\RegSeqRat$, the diagonal representative $L$ of \autoref{def:real-completeness-diagonal-limit} is the Bishop-style limit candidate: it is a regular sequence by \autoref{thm:real-completeness-diagonal-regular}, and the assignment $(X, \mu) \mapsto [L]$ into the supplied $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ satisfies
$$ \forall\, n \in \mathbb{N}, \quad |X_n - L| \;\le\; \tfrac{2}{n+1} $$
in the metric of $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$. The limit candidate is unique up to the $\psame$-equivalence underlying $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$: if $L'$ is another regular sequence with the same convergence bound, then $L \,\psame\, L'$ as elements of $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
Under the stated certificate obligations, the convergence bound is an unfolding of the supplied definitions. At scale $n$, the diagonal index is at least $\mu(1/(n+1))$, so the regular Cauchy modulus controls $X_n$ against $X_{\mu(1/(n+1))}$, and the diagonal-index regularity (\autoref{thm:real-completeness-diagonal-regular}) controls $X_{\mu(1/(n+1))}$ against $L$.

The uniqueness assertion is the $\psame$ closure on the supplied $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ applied to the difference $L - L'$. Its pointwise size is bounded by $4/(n+1)$ for every $n$, hence shrinks at the regularity rate, hence is identified with $0$ by the supplied $\RealClassifier$.
\end{proof}

The zero modulus is therefore admissible: after unfolding the regular Cauchy predicate, the required endpoint comparison is exactly the self-classifier just obtained, transported through StreamNameUp constant point exactness \autoref{thm:rat-stream-name-constant-point-exactness}.

For the diagonal representative, the extraction formula always selects a member of $X_d$ and then reads a point of the same constant stream. Thus every diagonal value is the seed $d$, independently of the outer index and independently of the selected diagonal bound.

Finally, \autoref{thm:real-constant-rational-embedding-certificate} identifies the pair $(\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d,\Eone(d))$ with the RealUp constant rational embedding. The previous paragraph shows that $L_d$ has exactly the same constant seed at every diagonal read, so the RealUp classifier relates $L_d$ to that embedding.

\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e),\quad

\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(C_d,C_e,n,m+1+r).

\begin{array}{l}
\forall u,v:\Hist,\,
\bigl(\hsame(C_d(n+k),\Eone(u))\land
\hsame(C_e(n+k),\Eone(v))\bigr)\\
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\\
\qquad
\bigl(\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(u)\land
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(v)\land

\hsame(e_0,v)\bigr).

\begin{proof}[Sketch]

with the common $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e)$, first at

\begin{theorem}[Stationary rational finite-partition fold]
\label{thm:real-completeness-stationary-rational-partition-fold}
For histories $d,e,d_0,e_0:\Hist$, an anchor $n$, a total bound $w$, and a
unary observation schedule $\eta:\mathbb{N}\to\Hist$, define the stationary
rational RealUp observation streams
$$
C_d(i)=\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d(\eta(i)),
\quad
C_e(i)=\mathsf{RatConstStream}_e(\eta(i)).
$$
Let $P$ be a certified finite partition spine over $(n,w)$ with segment anchors
and radii $(n_i,r_i)$, and suppose that $P$ records the unary source-anchor,
source-bound, and selected-offset witnesses for every segment. Assume
$$
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e),\quad
\hsame(d,\Eone(d_0)),\quad
\hsame(e,\Eone(e_0)).
$$
Then the total stationary window classifier is exactly the finite conjunction
of the segment classifiers named by $P$:
$$
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(C_d,C_e,n,w)
\Longleftrightarrow
\bigwedge_{(n_i,r_i)\in P}
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(C_d,C_e,n_i,r_i).
$$
Moreover, for every segment $(n_i,r_i)$ named by $P$ and every selected offset
$k\le r_i$ whose unary source witness is recorded by $P$, the selected endpoint
has the original displayed seed-tail class:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\forall u,v:\Hist,\,
\bigl(\hsame(C_d(n_i+k),\Eone(u))\land
\hsame(C_e(n_i+k),\Eone(v))\bigr)\\
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\\
\qquad
\bigl(\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(u)\land
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(v)\land
\hsame(d_0,u)\land
\hsame(e_0,v)\bigr).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
First treat each segment named by $P$ as an ordinary stationary rational
finite window. The spine ledger supplies the unary source anchor, source bound,
and selected offsets for that segment, while the common
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e)$ row is shared by every segment.

Apply
\autoref{thm:real-completeness-stationary-rational-selected-tail-seed-exactness}
segmentwise. This gives, for every selected offset in every segment, the
displayed endpoint-tail equivalence with the seed tails $d_0,e_0$.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:real-completeness-stationary-rational-finite-window-bridge}
to each segment ledger. The stationary streams erase the observation schedule
$\eta$, so the same RatUp classifier produces every segment classifier
$\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(C_d,C_e,n_i,r_i)$.

It remains to fold the segment classifiers along the certified spine. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-stream-window-finite-partition-fold} to the streams
$C_d,C_e$ and to $P$. In the singleton case this is the existing stationary
finite-window bridge. In the successor case the induction step uses the spine
continuation ledger to concatenate the predecessor window with the final

\begin{theorem}[Stationary rational finite-partition diagonal embedding]
\label{thm:real-completeness-stationary-partition-diagonal-embedding}

$$

\quad
C_e(i)=\mathsf{RatConstStream}_e(\eta(i)).

\autoref{thm:real-completeness-stationary-rational-partition-fold}. Assume

\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(e),\quad
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e),
$$
and the seed displays
$$
\hsame(d,\Eone(d_0)),\quad
\hsame(e,\Eone(e_0)).
$$
Then the finite conjunction of segment classifiers supplied by $P$ is a
complete finite ledger for the stationary input $X_d$ of
\autoref{def:real-completeness-stationary-rational-cauchy}. Its diagonal
representative $L_d$ is classified by the RealUp classifier with the constant
rational embedding represented by $(\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d,\Eone(d))$.
Likewise, the stationary input $X_e$ has diagonal representative $L_e$
classified by the constant rational embedding represented by
$(\mathsf{RatConstStream}_e,\Eone(e))$, and the two embeddings are related
through the displayed $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
First use RatUp semantic reflexivity for the carrier rows on $d$ and $e$.
Together with
\autoref{thm:rat-stream-name-constant-witness} and
\autoref{thm:rat-stream-name-constant-point-exactness}, the stationary streams
$C_d$ and $C_e$ are valid constant StreamNameUp witnesses at every observation
named by $\eta$.

Next consume the finite partition spine. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-completeness-stationary-rational-partition-fold} to
$C_d,C_e$, the common RatUp classifier, the seed displays, and the certified
spine $P$. This supplies the total
$\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(C_d,C_e,n,w)$ exactly as the finite
conjunction of segment classifiers, and it preserves the selected endpoint
seed-tail equivalence on every segment.

The selected-tail rows are the ledger needed by the constant regular Cauchy
input $X_d$: every member of $X_d$ is the same constant RealUp name

Now apply

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary rational partition refinement invariance]
\label{thm:real-completeness-stationary-partition-refinement-invariance}
For RatUp histories $d,e,d_0,e_0:\Hist$, an observation anchor $n$, a total
bound $w$, and a unary observation schedule $\eta:\mathbb{N}\to\Hist$, define
the stationary rational RealUp observation streams
$$
C_d(i)=\mathsf{RatConstStream}_d(\eta(i)),
\quad
C_e(i)=\mathsf{RatConstStream}_e(\eta(i)).
$$
Let $P$ be a certified finite partition spine over $(n,w)$, and let $P'$ be a
certified refinement of $P$ obtained by replacing each segment $(n_i,r_i)$ of
$P$ by a finite adjacent chain $(n_{i,j},r_{i,j})$ whose union is the same
window. Suppose that the refinement map preserves the unary source-anchor,
source-bound, selected-offset, and continuation ledgers: every subsegment of
$P'$ is recorded with the same stationary source anchor and source bound as
its parent segment in $P$, selected offsets are the corresponding translated
offsets inside the parent segment, and the continuation rows concatenate the
subsegments back to the parent window.
Assume
$$
\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d),\quad
\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(e),\quad
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(d,e),
$$
and the seed displays
$$
\hsame(d,\Eone(d_0)),\quad
\hsame(e,\Eone(e_0)).
$$
Then the finite segment ledger named by $P$ holds iff the finite segment ledger
named by $P'$ holds:
$$
\bigwedge_{(n_i,r_i)\in P}
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(C_d,C_e,n_i,r_i)
\Longleftrightarrow
\bigwedge_{(n_{i,j},r_{i,j})\in P'}
\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(C_d,C_e,n_{i,j},r_{i,j}).
$$
Moreover, either ledger gives the same diagonal RealUp embedding conclusions
of
\autoref{thm:real-completeness-stationary-partition-diagonal-embedding} for
$X_d$ and $X_e$, and every selected endpoint in either ledger has the same
seed-tail class supplied by
\autoref{thm:real-completeness-stationary-rational-selected-tail-seed-exactness}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
First apply
\autoref{thm:real-completeness-stationary-rational-partition-fold} to the
original spine $P$. Its ledger is equivalent to the total classifier
$\mathsf{RealStreamWindowClassifier}(C_d,C_e,n,w)$, and its selected endpoint
rows have the seed-tail class determined by $d_0,e_0$.

Apply the same partition fold to the refined spine $P'$. The refinement
preserves the source-anchor and source-bound rows, so each subsegment is a
stationary rational finite window with the same hidden RatUp seed classifier.

For a subsegment inside a parent segment, use adjacent-window restriction to
read the parent classifier on the smaller window, and use adjacent-window
concatenation along the refinement chain to assemble the parent window from
the subsegments. The stationary streams erase the observation schedule
$\eta$, so this restriction and concatenation carry no extra endpoint data
beyond the translated selected offsets already recorded in the refinement
ledger.

The forward direction transports the $P$ ledger through the common total
classifier and then through the fold for $P'$. The reverse direction uses the
fold for $P'$ to recover the same total classifier and then the fold for $P$ to
recover the original segment conjunction.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:real-completeness-stationary-partition-diagonal-embedding} once
with $P$ and once with $P'$. Both applications consume the same total
stationary window classifier and the same RatUp seed classifier. Apply
\autoref{thm:real-completeness-stationary-rational-selected-tail-seed-exactness}
on the selected offsets in each spine to identify every endpoint tail with the
same displayed seed-tail class.
\end{proof}

\section{Constructive content and axiom purity}

\begin{remark}[Explicit moduli throughout]
\label{rem:real-completeness-explicit-moduli}
Every modulus appearing in \autoref{def:real-completeness-regular-cauchy-rat}, \autoref{def:real-completeness-diagonal-limit}, and \autoref{thm:real-completeness-limit-exists-unique} is specified as a function on naturals and rationals computed by primitive recursion on the inductive carriers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}. The required proof obligations are exactly the certificate-level regularity, convergence, and $\psame$-closure facts that avoid quotienting an equivalence relation by a host-level $\Quot$-soundness step. These obligations are the axiom-pure targets for the later machine-checked real-completion layer.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Reuse for the continuous certificate]
\label{rem:real-completeness-reuse-continuous}
The diagonal limit construction (\autoref{def:real-completeness-diagonal-limit}) identifies the limit step expected by the continuous certificate $\NameCert_{\ContinuousFunctionUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}). A continuous function on $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ is, in BEDC, a function on $\RegSeqRat$ together with an explicit pointwise modulus; once the real-completion obligations are checked, downstream continuity certificates can consume the same diagonal regularity bound. The reuse is structural, not a separate construction.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Composition with the compact uniform-continuity capstone]
\label{rem:real-completeness-compact-composition}
After $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ has the supplied completion certificate and the continuous-function certificate is available, the compact uniform-continuity capstone (\autoref{ch:capstones-compact-uniform-continuity}) can be instantiated on closed bounded intervals $[a, b] \subset \RealUp$. The downstream consumer is a future kernel theorem that, for every continuous $f \colon [a, b] \to \RealUp$, derives a uniform modulus depending only on the tolerance from the checked compactness and continuity certificates.
\end{remark}
