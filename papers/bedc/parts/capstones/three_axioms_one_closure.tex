\chapter{Three Axioms, One Meta Closure}
\label{ch:capstones-three-axioms-one-closure}

The three axioms classical mathematics needs but BEDC rejects -- the axiom of choice, quotient soundness, and propositional extensionality -- are not three independent disciplinary commitments but three syntactic variants of a single underlying move: accepting an unwitnessed existential as legitimate input to a subsequent argument. Choice closes at the term level by supplying an unwitnessed selection function across an arbitrary index family; quotient soundness closes at the type level by supplying an unwitnessed canonical representative for an equivalence class; propositional extensionality closes at the proposition level by supplying an unwitnessed identification of equivalent propositions. Each is the meta-level closure of the open meta loop in \autoref{ch:capstones-reflection-and-limits}'s two-loops structure, phrased at a different syntactic stratum, in the same family as the halting closure of \autoref{ch:capstones-halting-as-form-of-distinction-limit}. This chapter (i) reformulates each of the three axioms as the schematic move ``accept an unwitnessed $\exists$ as argument input'', (ii) states the unification theorem that the three are syntactic variants of one meta-closure operation, (iii) pairs each axiom with its BEDC effective replacement -- countable and dependent choice plus the finite case via explicit recursion for choice, the typed naming certificate framework of \autoref{ch:core-typed-naming-certificates} with classifier-respecting transport for quotient soundness, and explicit $\hsame$ / $\psame$ transport for propositional extensionality -- and (iv) records that the project's axiom-purity gate is therefore a refusal of meta closure rather than a rejection of the application content the three axioms serve.

\section{The three axioms as syntactic variants of meta closure}
\label{sec:three-axioms-one-closure-variants}

\begin{definition}[Meta-closure operation]
\label{def:three-axioms-meta-closure-operation}
A \emph{meta-closure operation} at a syntactic stratum $S \in \{\text{term}, \text{type}, \text{proposition}\}$ is a derivation step that, given a positive existential statement at stratum $S$ together with no constructor witness, supplies a value of $S$ as if a witness had been given. The accepted value plays the role of an oracle: it appears as input to subsequent constructions inside the kernel without being traceable to a closed inductive constructor.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[Choice as term-level meta closure]
\label{ex:three-axioms-choice}
The axiom of choice asserts: from $\forall i \in I, \exists x_i \in A_i$, conclude $\exists f \colon I \to \bigsqcup A_i$ with $f\,i \in A_i$. Read at the term stratum, the conclusion is a term $f$ of function type whose body is not constructed from the existential witnesses. The axiom supplies $f$ as an oracle; the body is unwitnessed and therefore inaccessible to kernel-level computation. This is the term-level instance of \autoref{def:three-axioms-meta-closure-operation}.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[Quotient soundness as type-level meta closure]
\label{ex:three-axioms-quot-sound}
$\Quot$-soundness asserts: from an equivalence relation $R$ on a type $A$, conclude that there exists a type $A / R$ together with a section $A / R \to A$ choosing a canonical representative. Read at the type stratum, the section is a type-level oracle: it returns a representative without exhibiting one. This is the type-level instance of \autoref{def:three-axioms-meta-closure-operation}.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[Propositional extensionality as proposition-level meta closure]
\label{ex:three-axioms-propext}
$\mathsf{propext}$ asserts: from $P \Leftrightarrow Q$, conclude $P = Q$ at the proposition level. The conclusion identifies two propositions whose only relation is bidirectional implication; reading the identification at the proposition stratum, $\mathsf{propext}$ supplies an unwitnessed equality of propositions. This is the proposition-level instance of \autoref{def:three-axioms-meta-closure-operation}.
\end{example}

\section{The unification theorem}
\label{sec:three-axioms-unification}

\begin{theorem}[The three axioms instantiate one meta-closure schema]
\label{thm:three-axioms-unification}
Choice (\autoref{ex:three-axioms-choice}), $\Quot$-soundness (\autoref{ex:three-axioms-quot-sound}), and $\mathsf{propext}$ (\autoref{ex:three-axioms-propext}) are instances of the same meta-closure schema of \autoref{def:three-axioms-meta-closure-operation}, differing only in the syntactic stratum at which the unwitnessed existential is accepted. Accepting any one of the three closes the open meta loop of \autoref{ch:capstones-reflection-and-limits} at the corresponding stratum.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
The three examples display the schema directly: an unwitnessed positive existential is accepted, and the accepted value is fed into the subsequent kernel construction. The differences across choice, $\Quot$-soundness, and $\mathsf{propext}$ are exactly which stratum the existential lives at -- term, type, proposition. Closure at any one stratum supplies a $\NameCert$-level decision for a predicate that the open meta loop refuses to settle, so the unified statement of \autoref{thm:reasoning-forbidden-implements-meta-closure} applies stratum by stratum.
\end{proof}

\section{Effective replacements inside BEDC}
\label{sec:three-axioms-replacements}

\begin{definition}[Effective replacement for choice]
\label{def:three-axioms-replacement-choice}
The BEDC \emph{effective replacement for choice} is the package consisting of: countable choice on $\mathbb{N}$-indexed families with explicit witness functions; dependent choice along a $\Cont$-chain with explicit step functions; and finite choice via primitive recursion on a closed inductive list. Each replacement supplies a witness function whose body is constructed from the kernel-level data of the assumption.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Effective replacement for $\Quot$-soundness]
\label{def:three-axioms-replacement-quot}
The BEDC \emph{effective replacement for $\Quot$-soundness} is the typed naming-certificate framework of \autoref{ch:core-typed-naming-certificates}. Two carrier elements identified by a classifier are not collapsed at the type level; instead, the certificate exposes a $\psame$-transport that lets every classifier-respecting operation pass through the identification without selecting a canonical representative.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Effective replacement for $\mathsf{propext}$]
\label{def:three-axioms-replacement-propext}
The BEDC \emph{effective replacement for $\mathsf{propext}$} is the explicit $\hsame$ and $\psame$ transport on every certificate. Two propositions $P, Q$ that are bidirectionally implied are not identified; instead, every kernel-level construction that consumes $P$ receives a $Q$-version via the bidirectional implication, and the construction is parameterised over the chosen direction.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Replacements suffice for BEDC's certified content]
\label{thm:three-axioms-replacements-suffice}
Every theorem in the manuscript whose statement is supported by a Lean-checked-level marker is provable using only the replacements of \autoref{def:three-axioms-replacement-choice}, \autoref{def:three-axioms-replacement-quot}, and \autoref{def:three-axioms-replacement-propext} in place of the three classical axioms.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
The replacements are themselves kernel-level constructions on closed inductive carriers. A traversal of the manuscript's certified content maps each invocation of choice to countable, dependent, or finite choice; each invocation of $\Quot$-soundness to certificate-level $\psame$-transport; and each invocation of $\mathsf{propext}$ to a $\hsame$-mediated rewrite. The traversal terminates because the certified content is finite and the replacement is uniform across applications of each axiom.
\end{proof}

\section{The axiom-purity gate as refusal of meta closure}
\label{sec:three-axioms-purity-gate}

\begin{remark}[The gate's structural meaning]
\label{rem:three-axioms-gate-meaning}
The project's axiom-purity gate (\texttt{axiom-purity --strict} in \texttt{lean4/scripts/bedc\_ci.py}) refuses every kernel-level theorem whose transitive dependencies include $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$, $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$, or $\mathsf{propext}$. Read through \autoref{thm:three-axioms-unification}, the gate is a single refusal at three strata: it forbids meta-closure at the term, type, and proposition layers. The gate is therefore not a rejection of the application content the three axioms serve; it is a refusal of the syntactic shortcut that supplies that content without constructive witnesses.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Sibling instance: halting and reasoning by contradiction]
\label{rem:three-axioms-siblings}
The chapter's unification theorem (\autoref{thm:three-axioms-unification}) sits in a family with the halting capstone (\autoref{ch:capstones-halting-as-form-of-distinction-limit}) and the reasoning-by-contradiction capstone (\autoref{ch:capstones-reasoning-by-contradiction-boundary}): all three identify a different face of the same refusal of meta closure. Halting names the operational-semantics face; reasoning by contradiction names the proof-theoretic face; the present chapter names the foundational-axiom face. Together they constitute the discipline side of the two-loops architecture (\autoref{thm:two-loops}).
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Term-stratum choice closure]
\label{def:term-stratum-choice-closure}
Fix an index carrier $I$, an ambient term carrier $U$, and a family of positive fiber predicates $A_i \subseteq U$. The \emph{term-stratum choice closure schema} is the following instance of \autoref{def:three-axioms-meta-closure-operation}: from an accepted family of positive existentials
$$
H:\forall i \in I,\; \exists x:U,\; x \in A_i
$$
with no displayed constructor-level selection of such terms, introduce a term $o_H:I\to U$ and membership evidence $o_H(i)\in A_i$ for each $i$. The term $o_H(i)$ is then available as an oracle input to later term-level constructions.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Choice is term-stratum meta closure]
\label{thm:choice-term-stratum-meta-closure}
For arbitrary indexed families $A_i$, the term-stratum choice closure schema of \autoref{def:term-stratum-choice-closure} is equivalent to the axiom-of-choice principle of \autoref{ex:three-axioms-choice}:
$$
\left(\forall i \in I,\; \exists x_i,\; x_i \in A_i\right)
\Longrightarrow
\exists f:I\to\bigsqcup_{i\in I} A_i,\; \forall i\in I,\; f(i)\in A_i.
$$
Here $f(i)\in A_i$ abbreviates that the value of $f$ lies over the $i$-fiber of the displayed coproduct.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Assume the term-stratum choice closure schema. Given $H:\forall i\in I,\exists x_i, x_i\in A_i$, the schema supplies an oracle term $o_H:I\to U$ with evidence $o_H(i)\in A_i$ for every $i$. Define $f(i)=(i,o_H(i))$ as an element of the displayed coproduct $\bigsqcup_{i\in I}A_i$. The fiber evidence supplied with $o_H$ proves $f(i)\in A_i$ for each $i$, so $f$ is the choice function required by the displayed axiom-of-choice principle.

Conversely, assume the displayed axiom-of-choice principle. Given a term-stratum closure datum $H:\forall i\in I,\exists x_i,x_i\in A_i$, apply choice to the family $A_i$. It produces a function $f:I\to\bigsqcup_{i\in I}A_i$ with $f(i)\in A_i$ for every $i$. Let $o_H(i)$ be the component of $f(i)$ in the $i$-fiber. The accompanying fiber proof is exactly the evidence required to use $o_H(i)$ as the accepted value of the existential at $i$. Any later term-level construction that consumes the accepted existential can therefore be fed $o_H(i)$ and its membership evidence. For a single positive term-existential, take $I$ to be the singleton carrier; for an indexed family, keep the given $I$. Thus the axiom-of-choice principle realizes exactly the term-stratum instance of \autoref{def:three-axioms-meta-closure-operation}, and the two principles are equivalent.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Quotient representative package]
\label{def:quotient-representative-package}
Let $A$ be a type and let $R$ be an equivalence relation on $A$. A \emph{quotient representative package} for $(A,R)$ is a triple $(Q,\pi,\rho)$ with
$$
Q:\mathsf{Type},\qquad \pi:A\to Q,\qquad \rho:Q\to A,
$$
such that
$$
\forall a\,b:A,\; R(a,b)\Longrightarrow \pi(a)=\pi(b),
$$
$$
\forall u:Q,\; \pi(\rho(u))=u,
$$
and
$$
\forall a:A,\; R(\rho(\pi(a)),a).
$$
Write $\mathsf{QRep}_{A,R}(Q,\pi,\rho)$ for this conjunction. The second displayed condition says that $\rho$ is a section of $\pi$; the third says that the selected representative of the class of $a$ remains in the $R$-class of $a$.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Quotient representative packages are exact]
\label{lem:quotient-representative-package-exact}
Let $R$ be an equivalence relation on $A$. If $\mathsf{QRep}_{A,R}(Q,\pi,\rho)$ holds, then for all $a,b:A$,
$$
\pi(a)=\pi(b)\Longleftrightarrow R(a,b).
$$
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The reverse implication is the soundness field of $\mathsf{QRep}_{A,R}(Q,\pi,\rho)$. For the forward implication, assume $e:\pi(a)=\pi(b)$. Representative adequacy gives $R(\rho(\pi(a)),a)$ and $R(\rho(\pi(b)),b)$. Transport the second witness along congruence of $\rho$ applied to $e$, obtaining $R(\rho(\pi(a)),b)$. Symmetry of the first witness gives $R(a,\rho(\pi(a)))$, and transitivity with the transported second witness gives $R(a,b)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Type-stratum quotient closure]
\label{def:type-stratum-quotient-closure}
For a type $A$ and an equivalence relation $R$ on $A$, the \emph{type-stratum quotient closure schema} is the following instance of \autoref{def:three-axioms-meta-closure-operation}: from the positive existential
$$
\exists Q:\mathsf{Type},\;\exists \pi:A\to Q,\;\exists \rho:Q\to A,\;\mathsf{QRep}_{A,R}(Q,\pi,\rho)
$$
with no displayed constructor-level witness for $Q$, $\pi$, or $\rho$, introduce such a triple as accepted type-level data. The accepted type is read as $A/R$, and the accepted selector is available as an oracle input to later type-level constructions.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Quot-soundness as type-stratum meta closure]
\label{thm:quot-sound-type-stratum-meta-closure}
At the schema level, the type-stratum quotient closure schema of \autoref{def:type-stratum-quotient-closure} is equivalent to the quotient-representative $\Quot$-soundness principle of \autoref{ex:three-axioms-quot-sound}: for every type $A$ and equivalence relation $R$ on $A$, accepting one supplies exactly a type $A/R$ with a section $A/R\to A$ choosing a canonical representative, and accepting the other supplies exactly the accepted type-level witness required by \autoref{def:three-axioms-meta-closure-operation}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Assume the type-stratum quotient closure schema. Given $A$ and $R$, instantiate the positive existential in \autoref{def:type-stratum-quotient-closure}. The schema supplies $Q$, $\pi$, and $\rho$ satisfying $\mathsf{QRep}_{A,R}(Q,\pi,\rho)$. By \autoref{lem:quotient-representative-package-exact}, equality in $Q$ is exactly the classifier $R$ on representatives, so $Q$ is read as the quotient type $A/R$. The section and representative-adequacy fields make $\rho$ the canonical-representative selector. This is precisely the quotient-representative $\Quot$-soundness principle stated in \autoref{ex:three-axioms-quot-sound}.

Conversely, assume the quotient-representative $\Quot$-soundness principle of \autoref{ex:three-axioms-quot-sound}. For any equivalence relation $R$ on $A$, that principle supplies a quotient type $A/R$ together with a section $A/R\to A$ choosing a representative for each class. Let $Q$ be that type, let $\pi:A\to Q$ be the quotient projection, and let $\rho:Q\to A$ be the representative section. The quotient soundness clause gives $R(a,b)\Longrightarrow \pi(a)=\pi(b)$; the section clause gives $\pi(\rho(u))=u$; and the representative clause gives $R(\rho(\pi(a)),a)$. Hence $\mathsf{QRep}_{A,R}(Q,\pi,\rho)$ holds, which is exactly the existential datum accepted by the type-stratum closure schema.

The two directions only rename the same accepted data: a quotient type, a projection, and a representative selector. Thus the type-stratum instance of meta closure and the quotient-representative $\Quot$-soundness principle are the same schematic move. The BEDC replacement in \autoref{def:three-axioms-replacement-quot} refuses the selector $\rho$ and keeps classifier-respecting $\psame$-transport instead.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Proposition-stratum propext closure]
\label{def:proposition-stratum-propext-closure}
For propositions $P,Q:\mathsf{Prop}$, a \emph{proposition-stratum closure datum} is a bidirectional implication
$$
b_{P,Q}:(P\Rightarrow Q)\land(Q\Rightarrow P).
$$
The \emph{proposition-stratum propext closure schema} is the following instance of \autoref{def:three-axioms-meta-closure-operation}: from such a datum, with no displayed constructor-level inhabitant of the identity proposition $P=Q$, accept the identity proposition as inhabited by supplying an equality token
$$
\varepsilon_{P,Q}:P=Q.
$$
The token is then available as input to later proposition-stratum constructions by rewrite transport in every proposition-valued context $C:\mathsf{Prop}\to\mathsf{Prop}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Propext is proposition-stratum meta closure]
\label{thm:propext-proposition-stratum-meta-closure}
The proposition-stratum propext closure schema of \autoref{def:proposition-stratum-propext-closure} is equivalent to the propositional extensionality principle of \autoref{ex:three-axioms-propext}:
$$
\forall P,Q:\mathsf{Prop},\; (P\Leftrightarrow Q)\Rightarrow P=Q.
$$
Under this equivalence, the accepted oracle value in \autoref{def:three-axioms-meta-closure-operation} is exactly the equality token $\varepsilon_{P,Q}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Assume the proposition-stratum propext closure schema. Let $P,Q:\mathsf{Prop}$ and let $h:P\Leftrightarrow Q$. The datum $h$ is precisely a pair of implications $P\Rightarrow Q$ and $Q\Rightarrow P$. Applying the schema to this datum supplies an equality token $\varepsilon_{P,Q}:P=Q$. This is the conclusion of propositional extensionality.

Conversely, assume propositional extensionality. Given a proposition-stratum closure datum $b_{P,Q}:(P\Rightarrow Q)\land(Q\Rightarrow P)$, apply propositional extensionality to $b_{P,Q}$ and set $\varepsilon_{P,Q}$ to the resulting equality $P=Q$. For any proposition-valued context $C:\mathsf{Prop}\to\mathsf{Prop}$, equality elimination along $\varepsilon_{P,Q}$ transports a witness of $C(P)$ to a witness of $C(Q)$, and equality elimination along the symmetric equality transports in the reverse direction. Thus propositional extensionality supplies exactly the equality token and contextual rewrite behavior required by \autoref{def:proposition-stratum-propext-closure}. The two principles therefore coincide at the proposition stratum.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Primitive-recursive countable witness map]
\label{def:countable-choice-primitive-recursive-witness-map}
Let $A:\mathbb{N}\to\mathsf{Type}$ and let $w:\forall n:\mathbb{N},A(n)$. Put
$$
\Sigma A:=\bigsqcup_{k:\mathbb{N}}A(k).
$$
The associated \emph{primitive-recursive countable witness map} is the function
$$
\mathsf{cw}_{A,w}:\mathbb{N}\to\Sigma A
$$
defined by the primitive-recursion clauses
$$
\mathsf{cw}_{A,w}(0)=(0,w(0)),\qquad
\mathsf{cw}_{A,w}(n+1)=(n+1,w(n+1)).
$$
Equivalently,
$$
\mathsf{cw}_{A,w}
=
\mathsf{rec}_{\mathbb{N}}
\bigl((0,w(0))\bigr)
\bigl(\lambda n\,r.\,(n+1,w(n+1))\bigr),
$$
where the step variable $r$ is ignored because the premise supplies the witness in the next fiber.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Countable choice is realized by primitive recursion]
\label{thm:countable-choice-primitive-recursive-witness-function}
For every family $A:\mathbb{N}\to\mathsf{Type}$ and every displayed witness family $w:\forall n:\mathbb{N},A(n)$, the map $\mathsf{cw}_{A,w}$ of \autoref{def:countable-choice-primitive-recursive-witness-map} is a fiberwise choice function:
$$
\forall n:\mathbb{N},\;\mathsf{cw}_{A,w}(n)\in A(n).
$$
Here membership uses the displayed-coproduct convention of \autoref{thm:choice-term-stratum-meta-closure}: the value lies over the $n$-fiber, and its second component inhabits that fiber. The construction uses only the $\mathbb{N}$ recursor and dependent-pair introduction, so it is an instance of \autoref{def:three-axioms-replacement-choice} and does not invoke the term-stratum choice closure of \autoref{def:term-stratum-choice-closure}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Induct on $n$. At $0$, the first recursion clause gives
$$
\mathsf{cw}_{A,w}(0)=(0,w(0)),
$$
whose first projection is $0$ and whose second projection is the displayed term $w(0):A(0)$. Hence $\mathsf{cw}_{A,w}(0)$ lies in the $0$-fiber.

For the successor case, the second recursion clause gives
$$
\mathsf{cw}_{A,w}(n+1)=(n+1,w(n+1)).
$$
Its first projection is $n+1$, and its second projection is the displayed term $w(n+1):A(n+1)$. Thus $\mathsf{cw}_{A,w}(n+1)$ lies in the $(n+1)$-fiber. This proves the displayed universal statement. No step eliminates an unwitnessed existential: the only fiber element used at index $n$ is the constructor-level term $w(n)$, so no oracle selector or $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$ enters the construction.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Witnessed countable choice for predicates]
\label{cor:witnessed-countable-choice-for-predicates}
Let $B:\mathbb{N}\to\mathsf{Type}$ and let $P:\forall n:\mathbb{N},B(n)\to\mathsf{Prop}$. If
$$
H:\forall n:\mathbb{N},\;\bigsqcup_{x:B(n)}P(n,x)
$$
is a constructor-level family of existential witnesses, then there are functions
$$
a:\forall n:\mathbb{N},B(n),\qquad p:\forall n:\mathbb{N},P(n,a(n))
$$
given by dependent-pair projection from $H(n)$. The displayed-pair map
$$
n\mapsto (n,a(n)):\mathbb{N}\to\bigsqcup_{n:\mathbb{N}}B(n)
$$
is the primitive-recursive witness map of \autoref{def:countable-choice-primitive-recursive-witness-map} applied to $a$, and $p(n)$ proves the chosen value satisfies $P$ at every index.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
For each $n$, the term $H(n)$ is a displayed pair. Define $a(n)$ to be its first projection and $p(n)$ to be its second projection. Then $a(n):B(n)$ and $p(n):P(n,a(n))$ by dependent-pair elimination. Applying \autoref{thm:countable-choice-primitive-recursive-witness-function} to the family $B$ and witness function $a$ gives the displayed-pair map $n\mapsto(n,a(n))$, and the proof component $p(n)$ supplies the predicate evidence for each chosen value. Projection from a displayed pair is kernel computation, not term-stratum meta closure.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Certified psame-respecting operation]
\label{def:namecert-psame-respecting-operation}
Let $A$ and $B$ be BEDC carrier layers whose public classifiers are written $\psame_A$ and $\psame_B$, and whose carrier-classifier laws are licensed by naming certificates in the sense of \autoref{def:typed-naming-certificate} and \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}. A map $\varphi:A\to B$ is a \emph{certified $\psame$-respecting operation} when its stability data contains a descent witness
$$
\tau_\varphi:\forall a,a':A,\; \psame_A(a,a')\Longrightarrow \psame_B(\varphi(a),\varphi(a')).
$$
This witness is the operation-level $\DescentCert$ component of \autoref{def:naming-certificate-stability-field}. It is evidence about the classifiers, not an equality $a=a'$ and not a selector of a representative of the $\psame_A$-class.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NameCert psame transport replaces quotient equality]
\label{thm:namecert-psame-transport-replaces-quot-sound}
Let $A$ and $B$ be BEDC carrier layers with certified classifiers $\psame_A$ and $\psame_B$. Let $\varphi:A\to B$ be a certified $\psame$-respecting operation with descent witness $\tau_\varphi$ as in \autoref{def:namecert-psame-respecting-operation}. For every $a,a':A$, a classifier witness
$$
s:\psame_A(a,a')
$$
transports through $\varphi$ to a target classifier witness
$$
\psame_B(\varphi(a),\varphi(a')).
$$
The derivation uses no type-level equality $a=a'$, no quotient representative type, and no representative-selecting section.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the certified $\psame$-respecting operation datum. Its descent field is precisely
$$
\tau_\varphi:\forall x,y:A,\; \psame_A(x,y)\Longrightarrow \psame_B(\varphi(x),\varphi(y)).
$$
Apply this field to $a$, $a'$, and the supplied classifier witness $s$. The resulting witness is
$$
\tau_\varphi(a,a',s):\psame_B(\varphi(a),\varphi(a')).
$$
This proof consumes only the displayed classifier evidence and the certified descent field. A quotient-style argument would first turn $s$ into an equality at a quotient type and then use congruence of $\varphi$ over that equality. The naming-certificate argument keeps $a$ and $a'$ as distinct source terms and transports only the classifier judgment. Thus the quotient-soundness role identified in \autoref{def:three-axioms-replacement-quot} is supplied by explicit $\psame$-transport.
\end{proof}
