\chapter{The Finite Kernel Is Already a Category}
\label{ch:capstones-kernel-as-category}

The checked categorical interface for BEDC is the unary-continuation
$\CategoryUp$ package over $\mathsf{BHist}$. Its objects are unary histories,
its displayed hom-carriers are histories $f$ equipped with a continuation
ledger from $a$ to $b$, and morphism equality is the endpoint-stable
classifier $\hsame$ on displayed hom histories. The prior capstone
Computation as Continuation (\autoref{ch:capstones-computation-as-continuation})
reads $\Cont$ as a small-step relation; the present chapter records the
checked categorical reading available to the paper-Lean interface.

\section{Hom as unary continuation}
\label{sec:kernel-as-category-hom-as-cont}

\begin{theorem}[Unary-continuation category realizes the continuation interface]
\label{thm:kernel-as-category-unary-continuation-realization}
For unary histories $a,b,f:\mathsf{BHist}$, the checked hom-carrier
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)$ is equivalent to unary endpoints and a
continuation morphism whose tail is classified as $f$:
$$
\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(a)\wedge
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(b)\wedge
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(f)\wedge
\exists m:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b),\,
\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(m),f).
$$
With this hom-carrier, the unary-continuation $\CategoryUp$ package satisfies
identity, binary composition closure, associativity of displayed composition,
left and right unit laws, and endpoint-transport stability under $\hsame$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The equivalence is exactly
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-continuation-morphism-tail-correspondence}.
Its forward direction unpacks a displayed hom-carrier into unary source,
target, and morphism histories, then packages the continuation ledger as a
$\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}$ whose displayed tail is classified by
$\hsame$. Its reverse direction transports the continuation ledger carried by
the morphism tail across the supplied $\hsame$ classifier and repacks the four
fields of $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}$.

The categorical law package is
\autoref{thm:category-unary-continuation-stability-law-package}. It supplies
the empty hom-carrier identities, closure under displayed binary composition,
associativity for the two displayed composites of a threefold composition,
the left and right empty-composition laws, and preservation of hom-carriers
after endpoint transport along $\hsame$.
\end{proof}

\section{Derived presentations over the unary category}
\label{sec:kernel-as-category-laws}

\begin{remark}[FunctorUp and NatTransUp as bounded bridges]
\label{rem:kernel-as-category-functorup-nattransup}
$\NameCert_{\FunctorUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-functor-namecert})
and $\NameCert_{\NatTransUp}$
(\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nattrans-namecert}) are prefix and component
presentations over the checked unary-continuation category. They use the same
BEDC carriers, classifiers, and continuation stability obligations at their
own certificate sites. A standard category interface or mathlib bridge is not
asserted here; that bridge remains governed by the roadmap bridge phase.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[No new axioms]
\label{rem:kernel-as-category-no-new-axioms}
\autoref{thm:kernel-as-category-unary-continuation-realization} introduces no
kernel-level definition not already present in the checked $\CategoryUp$
continuation bridge. The categorical reading is obtained by unfolding
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}$, $\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}$, and $\hsame$
transport, then applying the already checked stability package.
\end{remark}
