\chapter{The Finite Kernel Is Already a Category}
\label{ch:capstones-kernel-as-category}

The BEDC finite kernel -- $\Hist$ as objects, $\Cont$ as morphisms, $\hsame$ as morphism equality, together with \texttt{cont\_assoc}, \texttt{cont\_left\_unit}, and \texttt{cont\_right\_unit} from the relational extension and continuation layer (\autoref{ch:core-relational-extension-and-continuation}) -- already constitutes a category in the strict sense. The derived chapters CategoryUp (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-functor-namecert}'s sibling), FunctorUp (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-functor-namecert}), and NatTransUp (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nattrans-namecert}) are not new structures layered on top of the kernel; they are explicit unary-prefix presentations of category-theoretic structure already present in $\Cont$. The prior capstone Computation as Continuation (\autoref{ch:capstones-computation-as-continuation}) gestures at this identification by reading $\Cont$ as a small-step relation; the present chapter promotes $\mathrm{Hom} = \Cont$ from an informal observation to a stated isomorphism theorem at the kernel level. This chapter (i) identifies the kernel objects and morphisms exhibiting the category laws, (ii) states the isomorphism between the kernel-level category and the derived CategoryUp presentation on naming certificates, (iii) locates FunctorUp and NatTransUp as canonical functors and natural transformations on this category, and (iv) records that no new axioms are introduced -- the result is structural visibility, not addition.
