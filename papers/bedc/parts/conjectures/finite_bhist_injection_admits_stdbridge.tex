\section{Finite-BHist-Injection Admits StdBridge}
\label{sec:conjectures-finite-bhist-injection-admits-stdbridge}

\begin{conjecture}[Finite-BHist-Injection Admits StdBridge]
\label{conj:finite-bhist-injection-admits-stdbridge}
For every BEDC chapter $\<X>Up$ that
\begin{enumerate}
  \item inhabits $\BHistCarrier$ with a finite-image $\toEventFlow$,
  \item inhabits $\ChapterTasteGate$ (i.e. $\roundTrip$ and $\layerSeparation$ hold),
  \item has carrier expressible as an injection from a regular language over
    $\{\BMarkZero, \BMarkOne\}$ into the chapter's BHist field tuple,
\end{enumerate}
there exists a $\<X>UpStdBridge$ theorem in $\BEDC.Derived.\<X>Up$ that bridges the
chapter's $\NameCert$ obligation surface to the standard reflection schema.
\end{conjecture}

\conjectureStatus{open}

\paragraph{Status notes.}
Open: no general proof. The candidate path is via finite-state-automaton
reflection (every regular language has a finite-state recogniser, which
the chapter's carrier embeds as a $\NameCert$ obligation), but the
$\hsame$ transport step from "regular-language injection" to "BHist field
tuple" requires a $\<X>UpStdBridge$ that is currently chapter-specific.
A general proof would require either (a) extracting the regular-language
recogniser as a uniform BHist construction, or (b) restricting the
conjecture to chapters whose carrier is already a fixed-arity tuple of
BHist fields.

\paragraph{Weakening route (if not provable).}
Restrict to chapters whose $\BHistCarrier.\toEventFlow$ image is
isomorphic to a fixed finite set $\{w_0, w_1, \ldots, w_k\}$ of
$\EventFlow$ values. The bridge then follows directly from
$\fromEventFlow \circ \toEventFlow = \mathsf{some}$ via enumeration.

\paragraph{Refutation route (if false).}
Find a chapter satisfying premises (1)-(3) for which a $\<X>UpStdBridge$
theorem demonstrably cannot exist (e.g. because the chapter's NameCert
obligation requires a non-standard reflection axis incompatible with the
schema).
