\section{Finite-BHist-Injection Admits StdBridge}
\label{sec:conjectures-finite-bhist-injection-admits-stdbridge}

\begin{conjecture}[Finite EventFlow Enumeration Admits StdBridge]
\label{conj:finite-bhist-injection-admits-stdbridge}
For every BEDC chapter $\langle X\rangle\mathsf{Up}$ equipped with a fixed finite-event-flow presentation that
\begin{enumerate}
  \item displays a finite list $[e_0,e_1,\ldots,e_k]$ of $\EventFlow$ values;
  \item supplies a $\toEventFlow$ image proof that every accepted carrier row maps into that list;
  \item supplies a $\fromEventFlow$ readback row with $\fromEventFlow(e_i)=\mathsf{some}(b_i)$ for each listed value $e_i$;
  \item presents the chapter carrier as a fixed-arity $\BHist$ tuple $(b_0,b_1,\ldots,b_k)$ whose $\NameCert$ rows are transported componentwise along those readback rows;
  \item inhabits $\ChapterTasteGate$ for the displayed tuple presentation;
\end{enumerate}
there exists a $\langle X\rangle\mathsf{UpStdBridge}$ theorem in $\BEDC.Derived.\langle X\rangle\mathsf{Up}$ that bridges the displayed finite tuple's $\NameCert$ obligation surface to the standard reflection schema.
\end{conjecture}

\conjectureStatus{weakened}

\paragraph{Status notes.}
The obligation is the fixed finite-event-flow route. The bridge follows by
case analysis over $[e_0,e_1,\ldots,e_k]$, replaying the displayed
$\fromEventFlow(e_i)=\mathsf{some}(b_i)$ readback for each listed value,
and transporting the tuple carrier's $\NameCert$ rows component by
component before packaging the finite family as the chapter's standard
reflection bridge.

The remaining open part is the general transport from arbitrary
regular-language recognisers to $\BHist$ tuple presentations. That route
would need a recogniser-to-carrier construction and a uniform component
transport theorem not supplied by the fixed finite-event-flow test target.

\paragraph{Refutation route (if false).}
Find a chapter satisfying the displayed finite-list, image, readback,
fixed-tuple, and $\ChapterTasteGate$ premises for which a
$\langle X\rangle\mathsf{UpStdBridge}$ theorem demonstrably cannot exist
(e.g. because the chapter's NameCert obligation requires a non-standard
reflection axis incompatible with the schema).
