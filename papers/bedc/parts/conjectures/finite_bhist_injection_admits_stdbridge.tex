\section{Finite-BHist-Injection Admits StdBridge}
\label{sec:conjectures-finite-bhist-injection-admits-stdbridge}

\begin{conjecture}[Finite-BHist-Injection Admits StdBridge]
\label{conj:finite-bhist-injection-admits-stdbridge}
For every BEDC chapter $\langle X\rangle\mathsf{Up}$ that
\begin{enumerate}
  \item inhabits $\BHistCarrier$ whose $\toEventFlow$ image is isomorphic to a fixed finite set $\{w_0,w_1,\ldots,w_k\}$ of $\EventFlow$ values,
  \item inhabits $\ChapterTasteGate$ (i.e. $\roundTrip$ and $\layerSeparation$ hold),
  \item exposes a fixed enumeration of those image values together with the displayed $\fromEventFlow \circ \toEventFlow = \mathsf{some}$ readback for each enumerated case,
\end{enumerate}
there exists a $\langle X\rangle\mathsf{UpStdBridge}$ theorem in $\BEDC.Derived.\langle X\rangle\mathsf{Up}$ that bridges the
chapter's $\NameCert$ obligation surface to the standard reflection schema.
\end{conjecture}

\conjectureStatus{weakened}

\paragraph{Status notes.}
The obligation is now the fixed finite-image route. The bridge follows by
case analysis over $\{w_0,w_1,\ldots,w_k\}$, replaying the displayed
$\fromEventFlow \circ \toEventFlow = \mathsf{some}$ readback in each case
and packaging the resulting finite family as the chapter's standard
reflection bridge.

The statement does not assert a uniform recogniser theorem for arbitrary
regular-language injections into BHist field tuples. Such a theorem would
require a separate construction of a regular-language recogniser as a
uniform BHist surface and is outside this conjecture.

\paragraph{Refutation route (if false).}
Find a chapter satisfying premises (1)-(3) for which a $\langle X\rangle\mathsf{UpStdBridge}$
theorem demonstrably cannot exist (e.g. because the chapter's NameCert
obligation requires a non-standard reflection axis incompatible with the
schema).
