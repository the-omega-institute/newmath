\section{Consolidated Signature Gap Policy}
\label{ch:v151-gap-policy}

We now restate the concrete signature gap using the two policies introduced above. This chapter freezes what is safe and records the exact assumptions needed for coverage, separation, soundness, and completeness.

\begin{definition}[Policy-supported signature gap]
Given \(\DomainPolicy(D)\), \(\AskPol(\Pi,D)\), and \(\PkgTokPol(\Pi)\), define
\[
  \InGapSig(\Pi,D,p,h)
\]
when there exist an answer history \(s\) and an evidence ledger \(\Delta\) such that
\[
  \InDom(D,h),
  \qquad
  \Sig(\Pi,h,s,\Delta),
  \qquad
  \TokIntro(\Pi,s,p).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Policy-supported gap coverage]
\label{thm:v151-gap-coverage}
Assume \(\DomainPolicy(D)\), \(\AskPol(\Pi,D)\), and \(\PkgTokPol(\Pi)\). If
\[
  \InDom(D,h),
\]
then there exists a token \(p\) such that
\[
  \InGapSig(\Pi,D,p,h).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
From \(\AskPol(\Pi,D)\) and \(\InDom(D,h)\), obtain a generated signature event \(\Sig(\Pi,h,s,\Delta)\). From token existence in \(\PkgTokPol(\Pi)\), obtain \(p\) with \(\TokIntro(\Pi,s,p)\). These data witness \(\InGapSig(\Pi,D,p,h)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Policy-supported gap separation]
\label{thm:v151-gap-separation}
Assume \(\DomainPolicy(D)\), \(\AskPol(\Pi,D)\), and \(\PkgTokPol(\Pi)\). If
\[
  \InGapSig(\Pi,D,p,h)
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  \InGapSig(\Pi,D,q,h),
\]
then
\[
  \psame(p,q).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Unpack both gap memberships. We obtain signatures \(s,t\) and evidence ledgers \(\Delta,\Theta\) such that
\[
  \Sig(\Pi,h,s,\Delta),\qquad \TokIntro(\Pi,s,p),
\]
\[
  \Sig(\Pi,h,t,\Theta),\qquad \TokIntro(\Pi,t,q).
\]
By signature determinacy supplied through \(\AskPol(\Pi,D)\), \(\hsame(s,t)\). By package soundness in \(\PkgTokPol(\Pi)\), \(\psame(p,q)\).
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Policy-supported concrete Globalize exactness]
\label{thm:v151-globalize-exact}
Under \(\DomainPolicy(D)\), \(\AskPol(\Pi,D)\), and \(\PkgTokPol(\Pi)\), two introduced visible tokens are package-same exactly when their generating signatures are internally same.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
This is Theorem~\ref{thm:v151-psame-iff-hsame}; the domain and asking policies are needed to ensure that the tokens arise from admitted histories with generated signatures.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[What is frozen]
The concrete signature Globalize instance is frozen only as a policy-supported construction. Its exactness depends on \(\DomainPolicy(D)\), \(\AskPol(\Pi,D)\), and \(\PkgTokPol(\Pi)\).
\end{corollary}
