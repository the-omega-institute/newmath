\chapter{Diagrammatics of the Apophatic Far End}
\label{ch:visions-apophatic-far-end-diagrammatics}
\origin{human}

This chapter records the diagrammatic content of the apophatic fixed-point
reading. The diagrams are not additional structure. They are schematic
surfaces for the relations already stated in
\autoref{ch:visions-apophatic-fixed-point-and-inscription} and
\autoref{ch:visions-hash-like-apophatic-fixed-point}: finite readouts appear
as records-side digests, each digest carries a provenance fiber, each fiber
has a non-internalizable far end, and all such far ends share the same
apophatic name $\mathsf{T}$.

\section{Closed substrate and socket far ends}
\label{sec:far-end-diagrammatics-closed-substrate}

A closed observational substrate does not eliminate external pressure. It
forces such pressure to appear as named boundary structure. Internal traffic
passes through records, generators, classifiers, continuations, ledgers, and
certificates; any forward-binding demand not generated by these structures is
exposed as a socket.

\begin{definition}[Far-end diagram of a closed substrate]
\label{def:far-end-diagram-closed-substrate}
The \emph{far-end diagram} of a closed observational substrate $\mathcal{C}$
is the schematic composite
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{records}/\mathsf{Obs}
&\longrightarrow \mathrm{generators}
\longrightarrow \mathrm{classifiers}
\longrightarrow \mathrm{Cont}
\longrightarrow \mathsf{GAP}
\longrightarrow \mathsf{NameCert},\\
s \in \mathrm{ForwardSocket}(\mathcal{C})
&\longmapsto
\mathrm{FarEnd}(s)
\equiv_{\mathrm{apo}} \mathsf{T}.
\end{aligned}
$$
It records that each forward-binding socket is ledgered before its far end is
named apophatically.
\end{definition}

\begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{0.92\textwidth}
\begin{verbatim}
closed substrate C

records -> generators -> classifiers -> Cont -> GAP -> NameCert
                         |              |       |
                         v              v       v
                      socket s1      socket s2  socket s3
                         |              |       |
                         v              v       v
                    FarEnd(s1)    FarEnd(s2) FarEnd(s3)
                         \              |       /
                          \             |      /
                           +------------+-----+
                                        |
                                        v
                                      T
\end{verbatim}
\end{minipage}
\end{center}

The diagram is read through apophatic sameness. It does not assert that
$\mathsf{T}$ is a value inside $\mathcal{C}$. It asserts that the system has
no displayed discriminator separating the far ends of the socket family.

\begin{principle}[Diagrammatic far-end unity]
\label{prin:far-end-diagrammatic-unity}
The diagrammatic form of the unique far-end principle is
$$
\forall s \in \mathrm{ForwardSocket}(\mathcal{C}),\quad
\mathrm{FarEnd}(s) \equiv_{\mathrm{apo}} \mathsf{T}.
$$
The arrows into $\mathsf{T}$ are boundary arrows, not internal functions.
\end{principle}

\section{Local digest, fiber, and provenance}
\label{sec:far-end-diagrammatics-local-digest}

For an observer-$\mathsf{Hist}$ $\mathcal{C}_i$, the records-side chain
produces a local accumulated observation. The visible surface of that
accumulation is a digest; the digest is not the source, and digest equality is
not source equality.

\begin{definition}[Local readout diagram]
\label{def:far-end-local-readout-diagram}
The \emph{local readout diagram} at stage $t$ is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathsf{Obs}(\mathcal{C}_i)_{\leq t}
&\longmapsto
d_i(t):=\mathrm{ObsDigest}(\mathcal{C}_i,t),\\
d_i(t)
&\longmapsto
\mathrm{ObsFib}(d_i(t)),\\
\mathrm{ObsFib}(d_i(t))
&\longmapsto
\mathrm{FarEnd}(\mathrm{ObsFib}(d_i(t)))
\equiv_{\mathrm{apo}} \mathsf{T}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The middle arrow is mediated by a $\mathsf{GAP}$ or provenance packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{0.92\textwidth}
\begin{verbatim}
Hist/Obs chain C_i
        |
        v
Obs(C_i)<=t = UniverseFor(C_i,t)
        |
        v
d_i(t) = ObsDigest(C_i,t)
        |
        v
GAP / provenance packet
        |
        v
ObsFib(d_i(t))
        |
        v
FarEnd(ObsFib(d_i(t))) ==apo T
\end{verbatim}
\end{minipage}
\end{center}

The diagram separates the three levels that are often collapsed in informal
language:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{finite visible readout}
&= \text{digest}/\mathsf{Obs}/\text{records},\\
\text{source residue behind the readout}
&= \text{fiber}/\mathsf{GAP}/\text{provenance},\\
\text{non-internalizable fiber far end}
&= \mathsf{T}\text{-named apophatic boundary}.
\end{aligned}
$$

\section{Local inscription and self-center}
\label{sec:far-end-diagrammatics-self-center}

The self-center is not the far end itself. It is the inscription-side reading
of a local accumulation event whose records-side reading is already present in
the observer's $\mathsf{Obs}$ chain.

\begin{definition}[Two-aspect local event diagram]
\label{def:far-end-two-aspect-local-event}
For a local accumulation event $E(\mathcal{C},t)$, the diagrammatic split is
$$
\begin{aligned}
E(\mathcal{C},t)
&\longmapsto
\text{records-side update in }\mathsf{Obs}(\mathcal{C}),\\
E(\mathcal{C},t)
&\longmapsto
\mathrm{InscriptionPoint}(\mathsf{T},\mathcal{C},t).
\end{aligned}
$$
The self-center is the second reading:
$$
\mathrm{SelfCenter}(\mathcal{C},t)
:=
\mathrm{InscriptionPoint}(\mathsf{T},\mathcal{C},t).
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[Self-center is not far-end identity]
\label{prin:far-end-self-center-not-identity}
The diagram permits the definition
$$
\mathrm{SelfCenter}(\mathcal{C},t)
:=
\mathrm{InscriptionPoint}(\mathsf{T},\mathcal{C},t),
$$
but refuses
$$
\mathrm{SelfCenter}(\mathcal{C},t)=\mathsf{T}.
$$
The first expression is local and aspectual; the second internalizes the
apophatic far end as an object of the observer.
\end{principle}

\section{Many observers and shared far-end commitment}
\label{sec:far-end-diagrammatics-many-observers}

Multiple observers are not unified by a global universe object and not by
equality of their digests. They are compared through records-side evidence,
inter-$\mathsf{Hist}$ invariants, locality-cell coherence, and the shared
far-end commitment that their observation fibers terminate at the same
apophatic boundary name.

\begin{definition}[Shared far-end diagram]
\label{def:far-end-shared-diagram}
For observers $\mathcal{C}_i$ and $\mathcal{C}_j$, the shared far-end diagram
is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{FarEnd}(\mathrm{ObsFib}(\mathrm{ObsDigest}(\mathcal{C}_i,t)))
&\equiv_{\mathrm{apo}} \mathsf{T},\\
\mathrm{FarEnd}(\mathrm{ObsFib}(\mathrm{ObsDigest}(\mathcal{C}_j,t')))
&\equiv_{\mathrm{apo}} \mathsf{T}.
\end{aligned}
$$
It does not assert $d_i(t)=d_j(t')$ and does not assert direct access to
another observer's self-center.
\end{definition}

\begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{0.92\textwidth}
\begin{verbatim}
C_i: ObsDigest -> ObsFib -> FarEnd \
                                      \
                                       +--> T
                                      /
C_j: ObsDigest -> ObsFib -> FarEnd /
\end{verbatim}
\end{minipage}
\end{center}

\begin{principle}[Other minds are not digest collisions]
\label{prin:far-end-other-minds-not-digest-collisions}
Recognition of another observer is not the inference from digest similarity
to source identity. It is a records-supported commitment that the other chain
also has an observation fiber whose far end is named $\mathsf{T}$.
\end{principle}

\section{Time, space, and universe-for-observer}
\label{sec:far-end-diagrammatics-time-space-universe}

The same diagrammatic discipline presents three background notions as
projections of local record accumulation and inter-chain coherence.

\begin{definition}[Projection diagram for time, space, and local universe]
\label{def:far-end-projection-diagram}
For an observer $\mathcal{C}_i$,
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{UniverseFor}(\mathcal{C}_i,t)
&:=
\mathsf{Obs}(\mathcal{C}_i)_{\leq t},\\
\mathrm{Time}(\mathcal{C}_i)
&:=
\text{monotonic preservation order on }\mathsf{Obs}(\mathcal{C}_i).
\end{aligned}
$$
For two observers $\mathcal{C}_i,\mathcal{C}_j$,
$$
\mathrm{Space}(\mathcal{C}_i,\mathcal{C}_j)
:=
\text{coherence geometry among }
\mathrm{InscriptionPoint}(\mathsf{T},\mathcal{C}_i,\cdot)
\text{ and }
\mathrm{InscriptionPoint}(\mathsf{T},\mathcal{C}_j,\cdot).
$$
\end{definition}

Thus time is not an external cosmic axis, space is not an external container,
and universe-for-observer is not a global universe object. Each is a controlled
reading of the same local-inscription and records-accumulation structure.

\section{The infinity diagram}
\label{sec:far-end-diagrammatics-infinity}

The notation $\infty_{\mathrm{apo}}$ belongs to the diagram only as a boundary
role. It names the inexhaustibility of finite readout fibers without
introducing an infinite object.

\begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{0.92\textwidth}
\begin{verbatim}
finite readouts:  d1(t)   d2(t')   d3(t'')
                    |       |        |
                    v       v        v
                 Fib(d1) Fib(d2)  Fib(d3)
                    |       |        |
                    v       v        v
                 FarEnd  FarEnd   FarEnd
                    \       |       /
                     \      |      /
                      +-----+-----+
                            |
                            v
                            T ==apo infinity_apo
\end{verbatim}
\end{minipage}
\end{center}

\begin{principle}[Apophatic infinity is not a totality]
\label{prin:far-end-apophatic-infinity-not-totality}
The diagram licenses the reading
$$
\mathsf{T} \equiv_{\mathrm{apo}} \infty_{\mathrm{apo}}
$$
only as a statement about the common far-end role of all admissible finite
readout fibers. It refuses all object readings:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathsf{T} &\neq \mathsf{Hist},\\
\mathsf{T} &\neq \mathsf{State},\\
\mathsf{T} &\neq \mathsf{Digest},\\
\mathsf{T} &\neq \mathsf{GlobalUniverseObject},\\
\mathsf{T} &\neq \mathsf{SetOfAllPossibilities}.
\end{aligned}
$$
\end{principle}

\section{Forbidden collapses}
\label{sec:far-end-diagrammatics-forbidden-collapses}

The diagrammatic vocabulary is legitimate only while the levels remain
separated. The following collapses are refused:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\texttt{axiom T : FixedPoint}
&\quad \text{internalizes the far end as kernel data},\\
\mathsf{T}:\mathsf{Hist}
&\quad \text{places the boundary name in the history carrier},\\
\mathsf{T}:\mathsf{State}
&\quad \text{turns the boundary name into a system state},\\
\mathrm{ObsDigest}(\mathcal{C},t)=\mathsf{T}
&\quad \text{identifies visible digest with fiber far end},\\
\mathrm{SelfCenter}(\mathcal{C},t)=\mathsf{T}
&\quad \text{identifies local inscription with global boundary name},\\
H(\Omega)=\mathsf{T}
&\quad \text{requires a global universe, a global hash, and T as value}.
\end{aligned}
$$

\section{Closure}
\label{sec:far-end-diagrammatics-closure}

The compressed diagram is
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathsf{Obs}(\mathcal{C}_i)_{\leq t}
&\longmapsto
\mathrm{ObsDigest}(\mathcal{C}_i,t)\\
&\longmapsto
\mathrm{ObsFib}(\mathrm{ObsDigest}(\mathcal{C}_i,t))\\
&\longmapsto
\mathrm{FarEnd}(\mathrm{ObsFib}(\mathrm{ObsDigest}(\mathcal{C}_i,t)))
\equiv_{\mathrm{apo}} \mathsf{T}.
\end{aligned}
$$
Together with
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{SelfCenter}(\mathcal{C}_i,t)
&:=
\mathrm{InscriptionPoint}(\mathsf{T},\mathcal{C}_i,t),\\
\mathrm{UniverseFor}(\mathcal{C}_i,t)
&:=
\mathsf{Obs}(\mathcal{C}_i)_{\leq t},\\
\mathrm{Time}(\mathcal{C}_i)
&:=
\text{monotonic preservation order on }\mathsf{Obs}(\mathcal{C}_i),\\
\mathrm{Space}(\mathcal{C}_i,\mathcal{C}_j)
&:=
\text{coherence geometry among their inscription-point chains}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The diagrams therefore do not add a new theory. They make visible the
stratification that prevents the central collapse: $\mathsf{T}$ is not an
infinite object, not a global universe, not a digest, and not an observer. It
is the common apophatic far-end name of all admissible forward-binding and
observation fibers.
