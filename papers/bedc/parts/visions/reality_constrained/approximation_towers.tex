\chapter{Reality-Constrained Approximation Towers}
\label{ch:visions-reality-constrained-approximation-towers}
\origin{ai}

Mathematical approximation and physical adequacy are not the same relation. A mathematical object may admit several convenient representations, and those representations may be equivalent under a chosen mathematical classifier. When the representation is used as a model of a real system, however, the relevant classifier is no longer freely chosen. It is constrained by the reality-facing properties through which the system responds to admissible observation and intervention.

This chapter records the resulting conceptual framework. BEDC already treats equality, function, limit, real number, circle, and other mature objects as derived interfaces rather than primitive givens. Compression, packaging, projection, sealing, and identification must carry source, classifier, stability, and $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger data. The same discipline applies to physical modelling: a mathematical representation is physically admissible only when the classifier that licenses it is compatible with the physical signature bundle under which the represented system is queried.
