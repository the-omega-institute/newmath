\chapter{Real Completeness via Regular Cauchy Sequences}
\label{ch:visions-real-completeness}
\origin{human}

This capstone records the conditional analytical target for the Phase Real and Completion milestone of \autoref{sec:roadmap-l10-construction-order}. It treats the real naming certificate $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}) as complete in the Bishop sense only after the DyadicRatCore, StreamNameUp, RegSeqRat, and RealUp certificate obligations are available at machine-checked strength. The chapter defines a regular Cauchy sequence over $\RegSeqRat$ as a sequence of regular rational sequences together with an explicit modulus of convergence, gives the intended limit modulus and $\RegSeqRat$ representative by diagonal extraction, and records the uniqueness criterion up to the standard $\psame$ equivalence on naming certificates. The construction is fully constructive as a proof target: every modulus is an explicit function on naturals and rationals, no real number is identified with an equivalence class, and the required Lean target is expected to use neither $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$ nor $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$ nor $\mathsf{propext}$.
