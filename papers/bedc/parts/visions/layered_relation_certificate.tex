\chapter{Layered Relation Certificates}
\label{ch:visions-layered-relation-certificate}
\concretizedIn{ch:concrete-instances-layeredrelationcert-namecert}{The relation-certificate vision is realized by a finite NameCert packet for same-schema cross-domain comparisons.}

This chapter records a relation discipline for cross-domain comparisons in BEDC. A comparison such as force cognition and MetaCIC self-audit is not admitted as an object identity, nor as a claim that physics has been derived from the kernel. It is admitted only when the comparison is lowered to a finite relation certificate: source rows, carrier rows, classifier rows, layer map, preserved structure, non-preserved content, provenance ledger, exactness status, failure boundary, and strength. The active rule is: do not close large words; close the relation certificates that make their comparison auditable.

The chapter sits beside \autoref{ch:visions-external-supply-without-axioms}, \autoref{ch:audit-map-family-methodology}, \autoref{ch:metacic-audit-synthesis}, \autoref{ch:visions-inter-hist-locality}, and \autoref{ch:visions-consciousness-universe-observer-in-bedc}. Those chapters already supply the surrounding discipline: unsupported supply remains socketed, MetaCIC claims are read through audit maps, multi-$\Hist$ physics is relation-local, and consciousness or universe language closes only on finite records-side carriers. The present chapter packages that discipline for analogical and cross-domain claims.

\section{From Analogy to Certificate}
\label{sec:layered-relation-from-analogy-to-certificate}

A loose analogy has no carrier, no classifier, no exactness statement, and no failure boundary. It may guide reading, but it cannot be consumed by the manuscript as a claim. BEDC therefore lowers cross-domain analogy to relation certification.

\begin{principle}[Relation before identity]
\label{prin:layered-relation-before-identity}
When BEDC compares two mature concepts, the default form is not object-level identity. The default form is a relation-level certificate whose fields display the source domains, carriers, classifiers, preserved structure, non-preserved content, ledgers, exactness status, and failure boundary of the comparison.
\end{principle}

Thus the sentence "force cognition resembles MetaCIC self-recognition" is not a theorem. The admissible BEDC form is:
$$
\mathrm{SameRefinementSchema}(\mathrm{ForceCognition},\mathrm{MetaCICSelfRecognition}).
$$
The predicate does not assert sameness of objects. It asserts that two displayed refinement chains instantiate the same relation schema.

\begin{definition}[Primitive-looking token]
\label{def:layered-relation-primitive-token}
In a practice domain $D$, a \emph{primitive-looking token} is a name $P$ that can be used in $D$ before the manuscript has displayed its source, carrier, classifier, ledger, and failure boundary. Its danger is not that it is false, but that it compresses several sources under one public surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Refinement chain]
\label{def:layered-relation-refinement-chain}
A \emph{refinement chain} is a finite sequence
$$
P_0 \xrightarrow{o_0} P_1 \xrightarrow{o_1} \cdots \xrightarrow{o_{n-1}} P_n
$$
where each $P_k$ is a concept layer and each $o_k$ is an obstruction, counterexample, audit failure, measurement anomaly, or boundary pressure that forces a classifier split. Each step carries source, carrier, classifier, transition, ledger, and failure rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[Obstruction as classifier pressure]
\label{prin:layered-relation-obstruction-classifier-pressure}
An obstruction is not merely a failed claim. In BEDC it is evidence that the previous classifier was too coarse. A legitimate refinement step converts the obstruction into a carrier split, classifier refinement, ledger row, or boundary socket.
\end{principle}

\section{Certificate Shape}
\label{sec:layered-relation-certificate-shape}

\begin{definition}[Layered relation certificate]
\label{def:layered-relation-certificate}
Given two refinement chains $A$ and $B$, a $\mathrm{LayeredRelationCert}(A,B,s)$ is a finite paper-side certificate with the following fields:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathsf{sourceA}$ and $\mathsf{sourceB}$, the two source domains;
\item $\mathsf{carrierA}$ and $\mathsf{carrierB}$, the carrier families used at each layer;
\item $\mathsf{layersA}$ and $\mathsf{layersB}$, the finite layer lists;
\item $\mathsf{obstructionA}$ and $\mathsf{obstructionB}$, the failures or anomalies that force refinement;
\item $\mathsf{classifierA}$ and $\mathsf{classifierB}$, the classifiers used at each layer;
\item $\mathsf{layerMap}$, the displayed correspondence between the two layer lists;
\item $\mathsf{preserved}$, the structure preserved by the correspondence;
\item $\mathsf{notPreserved}$, the content explicitly not preserved;
\item $\mathsf{ledger}$, the source record for each correspondence row;
\item $\mathsf{exactness}$, the meaning of "same schema";
\item $\mathsf{failureBoundary}$, the condition under which the relation weakens or fails;
\item $\mathsf{strength}=s$, with values such as seed, paper certificate, checked certificate, or bridge certificate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[Exactness before export]
\label{prin:layered-relation-exactness-before-export}
A cross-domain relation without a $\mathsf{notPreserved}$ field cannot be exported as a BEDC claim. The certificate must say both what the relation preserves and what it refuses to preserve, because otherwise same-schema language is easily misread as identity.
\end{principle}

The strength field is part of the certificate, not a decoration. A paper certificate says that the manuscript fields are displayed. A checked certificate would require a corresponding Lean target. A bridge certificate would require a conservative external interpretation with no hidden host leak. The force--MetaCIC comparison below claims only paper-certificate strength.

\section{Force Cognition as a Refinement Chain}
\label{sec:layered-relation-force-chain}

Human treatment of force can be read as a refinement chain rather than as a single object. Early force language compresses pushing, pulling, falling, collision, magnetic attraction, tension, and resistance into one token. Later practice splits that token into dynamical relations, field descriptions, geometric readings, symmetry structures, effective interactions, and unresolved boundaries.

\begin{definition}[Force cognition chain]
\label{def:layered-relation-force-cognition-chain}
The $\mathrm{ForceCognitionChain}$ is the following paper-side refinement schema:
$$
\begin{aligned}
F_0 &= \text{push/pull token};\\
F_1 &= \text{dynamical relation};\\
F_2 &= \text{field reading};\\
F_3 &= \text{geometric or curvature reading};\\
F_4 &= \text{gauge-symmetry or connection reading};\\
F_5 &= \text{effective interaction under scale and regime};\\
F_6 &= \text{unresolved boundary or model-interface socket}.
\end{aligned}
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[Force is not primitive]
\label{prin:layered-relation-force-not-primitive}
In BEDC, force may not enter as a source-free primitive. At the records-side level it can only be discussed as a classifier over witnessed inter-$\Hist$ change rows:
$$
\mathrm{ForceLikeRelation}_N :=
\mathrm{Classifier}(\mathrm{InterHistChangeRows}_N).
$$
Such a classifier must expose source rows, continuation or measurement witnesses, $\hsame$/$\psame$ stability, ledger policy, and bridge status.
\end{principle}

\begin{remark}[Physical content remains bridged]
\label{rem:layered-relation-physical-content-bridged}
$\mathrm{ForceLikeRelation}_N$ is not empirical physical force itself. It is a finite horizon records-side carrier. A claim about standard physics requires a separate bridge:
$$
\mathrm{StandardBridge}(\mathrm{ForceLikeRelation}_N,\mathrm{PhysicalForce}).
$$
Without that bridge, the relation remains paper-side or schema-side.
\end{remark}

\section{MetaCIC Self-Audit as a Refinement Chain}
\label{sec:layered-relation-metacic-chain}

MetaCIC self-audit follows the same de-primitiveizing pattern at the formal layer. A mature object, formal rule, type-like object, proof-like object, or classical CIC claim is not accepted as primitive. The audit asks for source, carrier, classifier, exactness, stability, ledger, naming certificate, gate, and socket.

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC self-recognition chain]
\label{def:layered-relation-metacic-self-recognition-chain}
The $\mathrm{MetaCICSelfRecognitionChain}$ is the following paper-side refinement schema:
$$
\begin{aligned}
M_0 &= \text{primitive-looking mature object};\\
M_1 &= \text{source carrier};\\
M_2 &= \text{classifier over generated carriers};\\
M_3 &= \text{exactness and stability obligations};\\
M_4 &= \text{ledger, GAP, and provenance policy};\\
M_5 &= \text{NameCert or DerivCert acceptance};\\
M_6 &= \text{audit gate, failure certificate, or socket}.
\end{aligned}
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[Formal tokens are de-primitiveized]
\label{prin:layered-relation-metacic-deprimitiveizes-formal-tokens}
MetaCIC's audit function is to lower formal tokens into generated carriers, classifiers, stability obligations, ledgers, and boundaries. Any item that cannot pass through those rows may remain named, but it cannot be treated as internally closed BEDC content.
\end{principle}

\section{Force--MetaCIC Relation}
\label{sec:layered-relation-force-metacic}

\begin{definition}[Force--MetaCIC relation certificate]
\label{def:layered-relation-force-metacic-cert}
The $\mathrm{ForceMetaCICRelationCert}$ is a $\mathrm{LayeredRelationCert}$ whose source domains are the history of force cognition and the BEDC/MetaCIC history of self-certification. Its layer map sends:
$$
F_0\mapsto M_0,\quad
F_1\mapsto M_1,\quad
F_2\mapsto M_2,\quad
F_3\mapsto M_3,\quad
F_4\mapsto M_4,\quad
F_5\mapsto M_5,\quad
F_6\mapsto M_6.
$$
The preserved structure is primitive-token replacement by relation, classifier, invariant, ledger, certificate, and boundary. The non-preserved content includes empirical physical content, numerical constants, machine-checked proofs, phenomenal essence, object-level $\mathsf{T}$, and global universe structure. The exactness status is same refinement schema, not same object. The strength is paper certificate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Force--MetaCIC same-schema certificate]
\label{thm:layered-relation-force-metacic-same-schema}
$\mathrm{ForceCognitionChain}$ and $\mathrm{MetaCICSelfRecognitionChain}$ instantiate the same de-primitiveizing relation schema at paper-certificate strength.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Sketch]
Both chains begin from a primitive-looking token. Both are refined by obstruction: empirical anomaly, classifier pressure, audit failure, or boundary mismatch. Both replace the coarse token by carrier and classifier rows. Both require stability and ledger rows to prevent source erasure. Both end by marking the unclosed residue as a boundary, socket, failure certificate, or bridge obligation. The relation preserves this refinement mode and does not preserve object content. Therefore the comparison closes as same-schema relation rather than identity.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Analogy as certificate]
\label{cor:layered-relation-analogy-as-certificate}
The sentence that force cognition resembles MetaCIC self-audit may enter the manuscript only as the natural-language reading of \autoref{def:layered-relation-force-metacic-cert}. It may not enter as an object-level theorem.
\end{corollary}

\section{Gate and Refusal Surface}
\label{sec:layered-relation-gate-refusal}

\begin{definition}[Layered relation gate]
\label{def:layered-relation-gate}
The $\mathrm{LayeredRelationGate}(A,B)$ is the audit question family for a proposed relation certificate:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Are the two source domains displayed?
\item Are the two carrier families displayed?
\item Are the two classifier families displayed?
\item Is the layer map displayed?
\item Are preserved and non-preserved content both displayed?
\item Are ledger rows displayed?
\item Are exactness and failure boundary displayed?
\item Has same-schema language been incorrectly promoted to identity?
\item Has the relation smuggled in an empirical law, subject-substance, global frame, or object-level $\mathsf{T}$?
\item Is the strength row displayed?
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[Refusal is part of closure]
\label{prin:layered-relation-refusal-is-closure}
In BEDC, a relation certificate closes partly by refusing its over-readings. Refusal is not an admission of theoretical failure; it is the ledger row that prevents a relation token from pretending to be an object-level theorem.
\end{principle}

\section{Closure}
\label{sec:layered-relation-closure}

\begin{theorem}[Closed discussion of layered relations]
\label{thm:layered-relation-closed-discussion}
Let $A$ and $B$ be two cross-domain themes. If $\mathrm{LayeredRelationCert}(A,B,s)$ displays source, carrier, classifier, layer map, ledger, exactness, failure boundary, strength, and refusal rows, and if $\mathrm{LayeredRelationGate}(A,B)$ reports no identity collapse, hidden axiom, global-frame leak, subject-substance leak, or empirical overclaim, then the discussion of the relation between $A$ and $B$ is closed at manuscript level with strength $s$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}[Sketch]
The discussion consumes only displayed certificate fields. Source and carrier rows bound the concept layers. Classifier and layer-map rows specify the comparison. Ledger rows preserve provenance. Exactness rows state what form of sameness is available. Failure-boundary and refusal rows prevent over-reading. The strength row prevents a paper certificate from being read as checked or bridged evidence. Therefore the exported relation is neither loose analogy nor object identity, but an auditable relation claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Force--MetaCIC relation closes at paper-certificate strength]
\label{cor:layered-relation-force-metacic-papercert}
Under the fields of \autoref{def:layered-relation-force-metacic-cert}, the force--MetaCIC relation may be used as a paper-certificate-strength relation. It closes the de-primitiveizing schema and does not close empirical force or the full MetaCIC meta-loop.
\end{corollary}

\begin{corollary}[Universe and consciousness enter through records-side carriers]
\label{cor:layered-relation-universe-consciousness-records-side}
Universe, consciousness, and force language enter this relation certificate only through finite multi-$\Hist$ ledger-sites, focused $\Hist$ carriers, inter-$\Hist$ relations, and $\mathrm{ForceLikeRelation}_N$. Claims about phenomenal consciousness, $\mathsf{T}$, a global universe object, or empirical constants remain boundary-side unless a separate certificate supplies the required bridge.
\end{corollary}

The final rule is therefore: do not write analogy as identity. Close the analogy as a relation certificate with source, carrier, classifier, ledger, exactness, and failure boundary.
