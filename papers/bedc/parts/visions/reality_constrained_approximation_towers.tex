\chapter{Reality-Constrained Approximation Towers}
\label{ch:visions-reality-constrained-approximation-towers}

Mathematical approximation and physical adequacy are not the same relation. A mathematical object may admit several convenient representations, and those representations may be equivalent under a chosen mathematical classifier. When the representation is used as a model of a real system, however, the relevant classifier is no longer freely chosen. It is constrained by the reality-facing properties through which the system responds to admissible observation and intervention.

This chapter records the resulting conceptual framework. BEDC already treats equality, function, limit, real number, circle, and other mature objects as derived interfaces rather than primitive givens. Compression, packaging, projection, sealing, and identification must carry source, classifier, stability, and $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger data. The same discipline applies to physical modelling: a mathematical representation is physically admissible only when the classifier that licenses it is compatible with the physical signature bundle under which the represented system is queried.

\section{Reality-facing signatures}
\label{sec:reality-constrained-signatures}

\begin{definition}[Reality-facing property]
\label{def:reality-facing-property}
A reality-facing property of a system is a property that can affect the system's stable interaction profile under some admissible physical procedure. Removing, smoothing, quotienting, or replacing such a property by an approximate surrogate can change at least one physically relevant response.
\end{definition}

Mass, charge, curvature, boundary differentiability, stress concentration, causal accessibility, optical response, and measurement-stable phase information are standard examples. A purely mathematical feature becomes reality-facing only when it enters such a response profile.

\begin{definition}[Ontic signature source]
\label{def:ontic-signature-source}
Let $O$ be a system and let $\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}$ be a bundle of physically admissible questions. The physical signature of $O$ relative to $\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}$ is written
$$
\operatorname{PhysSig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(O).
$$
The ontic signature source of $O$, relative to $\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}$, is the invariant source of this signature. It is not identified with any particular mathematical representation.
\end{definition}

Two systems are physically indistinguishable at the resolution $\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}$ only when their signatures agree:
$$
\operatorname{PhysSig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(O_1)
\sim
\operatorname{PhysSig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(O_2).
$$
This is a signature-level sameness judgment, not host-level identity.

\begin{definition}[Mathematical representation]
\label{def:reality-constrained-mathematical-representation}
A mathematical representation of an ontic signature source $O$ is a structured mathematical object $M$ together with a representation route
$$
\rho:O\rightsquigarrow M
$$
and an interpretation procedure that reads features of $M$ as candidate reality-facing properties of $O$.
\end{definition}

The representation is mathematically adequate relative to a mathematical classifier $\sim_{\mathcal M}$ when it preserves the mathematical features selected by that classifier. It is physically adequate relative to $\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}$ only when its induced physical signature agrees with the signature of the represented system:
$$
\operatorname{Sig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(M)
\sim
\operatorname{PhysSig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(O).
$$

\begin{definition}[Physical-fit relation]
\label{def:physical-fit-relation}
For mathematical representations $M_1$ and $M_2$, the physical-fit relation relative to $\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}$ is
$$
M_1\equiv_{\mathrm{phys},\Pi}M_2
$$
exactly when
$$
\operatorname{Sig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(M_1)
\sim
\operatorname{Sig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(M_2).
$$
\end{definition}

The physical-fit relation may be strictly finer than a mathematical approximation relation. Polygonal approximations and a smooth circle may agree under area, perimeter, or Hausdorff-distance classifiers while separating under tangent-field, curvature, reflection, stress-response, or boundary-layer classifiers.

\section{Ledgered approximation towers}
\label{sec:ledgered-approximation-towers}

\begin{definition}[GAP-admissible compression]
\label{def:gap-admissible-compression}
A compression
$$
C:X\rightsquigarrow Y
$$
is $\mathsf{GAP}$-admissible when it carries a source domain of admissible histories, a signature-generation policy, a package policy assigning visible tokens to signatures, a gap ledger recording which source histories lie behind each visible token, and a stability condition specifying which operations descend through the compression.
\end{definition}

This is the physical reading of BEDC's finite globalization discipline. Package tokens are not quotient classes accepted without provenance. They are visible rows whose source histories remain available through $\mathsf{GAP}$ and package policies; see \autoref{ch:visions-certificate-closure-and-generator-induction} and \autoref{ch:visions-external-supply-without-axioms}.

\begin{definition}[Approximation tower]
\label{def:reality-constrained-approximation-tower}
An approximation tower is a finite or transfinite chain of $\mathsf{GAP}$-admissible compressions
$$
O
\longrightarrow
D_0
\longrightarrow
D_1
\longrightarrow
\cdots
\longrightarrow
D_n
\longrightarrow
M,
$$
where $O$ is the ontic signature source, the intermediate $D_i$ are observation histories, finite data, discrete models, continuous models, smooth models, or other certified representation layers, and $M$ is the visible mathematical object used for reasoning.
\end{definition}

Each arrow compresses, packages, hides, regularizes, completes, or idealizes source material. The tower is admissible only when each arrow carries a ledger and the composite tower carries the composite ledger.

\begin{definition}[Operational descent]
\label{def:reality-constrained-operational-descent}
An operation $F$ descends through a classifier $\sim$ when there is a target classifier $\sim'$ such that
$$
x\sim y
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
F(x)\sim'F(y).
$$
A descent certificate is a proof that the operation respects the source classifier and lands in the target classifier.
\end{definition}

Without such a certificate, sameness before applying $F$ does not imply sameness after applying $F$. The physically important case is differentiation: a classifier that identifies two shapes by coarse geometric approximation does not automatically permit tangent fields, curvature measures, boundary conditions, or dynamics to descend through it.

\begin{principle}[Reality-signature priority]
\label{prin:reality-signature-priority}
Physical equivalence is determined by reality-facing signatures, not by mathematical convenience alone. If
$$
M_1\sim_{\mathrm{math}}M_2
$$
but
$$
\operatorname{Sig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(M_1)
\not\sim
\operatorname{Sig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(M_2),
$$
then $M_1$ and $M_2$ are mathematically similar but physically inequivalent relative to $\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}$.
\end{principle}

\begin{principle}[No free transfer of structure]
\label{prin:no-free-transfer-of-structure}
If a compression identifies two source histories, no operation on those histories may be transported across the compression unless a stability or descent certificate has been supplied.
\end{principle}

\begin{principle}[Compression requires physical memory]
\label{prin:compression-requires-physical-memory}
Every physically meaningful idealization must record what it hides. When a model replaces rough, discrete, finite, noisy, or staged source material by a smooth visible object, the lost source structure must remain available in a ledger whenever later physical questions may depend on it.
\end{principle}

\section{Physical adequacy theorems}
\label{sec:physical-adequacy-theorems}

\begin{theorem}[Mathematical approximation does not imply physical equivalence]
\label{thm:math-approx-not-physical-equivalence}
Let $M_1$ and $M_2$ be mathematical representations. Suppose $M_1\sim_{\mathrm{math}}M_2$ under a mathematical classifier. If
$$
\operatorname{Sig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(M_1)
\not\sim
\operatorname{Sig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(M_2),
$$
then $M_1$ and $M_2$ are not physically equivalent relative to $\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:physical-fit-relation}, physical equivalence relative to $\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}$ is sameness of induced physical signatures. The hypothesis gives failure of such signature sameness. Therefore the physical-fit relation does not hold, although the mathematical classifier identifies the two representations.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Physics may refine mathematical equivalence]
\label{cor:physics-refines-mathematical-equivalence}
A physical classifier may refine, split, or reject a mathematical equivalence class.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Let $[M]_{\mathrm{math}}$ be a mathematical equivalence class. If there are $M_1,M_2\in[M]_{\mathrm{math}}$ whose physical signatures differ, then \autoref{thm:math-approx-not-physical-equivalence} separates them in the physical-fit relation. Thus the physical classifier partitions the mathematical class into finer classes or rejects some members as inadequate for the intended physical use.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Polygonal convergence does not certify differentiability descent]
\label{thm:polygon-circle-no-automatic-differentiability}
Let $P_n$ be regular polygons approximating a circle $C$, and let $\sim_0$ be a coarse geometric classifier such as agreement in area, perimeter, finite-resolution visibility, or Hausdorff distance. If the polygonal histories and the circle history are packaged into the same visible circle-like token under $\sim_0$, then an operation extracting differentiable boundary structure does not automatically descend through $\sim_0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The classifier $\sim_0$ records coarse shape agreement. It does not, by itself, record tangent-field or curvature data. Each finite polygon has vertices at which the boundary direction changes discontinuously. The circle has a tangent at every point and constant curvature. Hence polygonal source histories and smooth-circle histories may agree under coarse signatures while separating under differentiability signatures. If differentiable-boundary extraction descended through $\sim_0$, coarse sameness would imply sameness after extraction. The displayed separation contradicts that implication. A separate descent certificate is therefore required.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Circle-like tokens are classifier-relative]
\label{cor:circle-like-token-classifier-relative}
A visible package token produced by polygonal approximation may behave as a circle under coarse shape queries while failing to be a smooth circle under differentiability-sensitive queries.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:polygon-circle-no-automatic-differentiability}, the coarse classifier may license a shared visible token without licensing differentiability descent. The token is therefore circle-like only relative to the classifier that licensed the package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Composite towers retain source dependence]
\label{thm:composite-towers-retain-source-dependence}
Let
$$
X_0\xrightarrow{C_1}X_1\xrightarrow{C_2}X_2\xrightarrow{C_3}\cdots\xrightarrow{C_n}X_n
$$
be an approximation tower in which each compression carries a $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger satisfying coverage. Then the composite compression from $X_0$ to $X_n$ has a composite ledger recording which original source histories in $X_0$ lie behind each final visible token in $X_n$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For two stages, a source $x\in X_0$ lies behind a final token $z\in X_2$ exactly when there exists an intermediate token $y\in X_1$ such that $x$ lies in the $C_1$ ledger behind $y$ and $y$ lies in the $C_2$ ledger behind $z$. This is the composite-gap construction. For more stages, apply the two-stage construction inductively: the composite ledger to $X_{n-1}$ composes with the ledger for $C_n$, yielding a ledger from $X_0$ to $X_n$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Hidden source structure can become physically active]
\label{cor:hidden-source-structure-physically-active}
A feature hidden at an early level of an approximation tower may become relevant when a later physical query depends on it.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:composite-towers-retain-source-dependence}, the hidden source structure is retained by a ledger-complete tower. If a later query, such as differentiation, curvature response, stress concentration, or causal propagation, depends on that hidden structure, then the query must inspect the ledger rather than only the final visible token.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Physical admissibility requires signature preservation and descent]
\label{thm:physical-admissibility-signature-descent}
Let $M$ be a mathematical representation of a system $O$, and let $\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}$ be the physical signature bundle relevant to the intended modelling context. If $M$ is physically admissible for $O$ relative to $\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}$, then the following conditions hold:
$$
\operatorname{Sig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(M)
\sim
\operatorname{PhysSig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(O),
$$
and every operation used to extract physical predictions from $M$ descends through the classifier by which $M$ was admitted.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
If signature preservation fails, $M$ does not reproduce the relevant reality-facing properties of $O$ and is not physically admissible. If operational descent fails, then there is an operation $F$ and classifier-same inputs $M_1\sim M_2$ with $F(M_1)\not\sim'F(M_2)$. Predictions depending on $F$ are then not well defined at the visible package level; they depend on hidden source history rather than on the admitted model token. Both conditions are therefore necessary.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Physical representation is unique only up to physical fit]
\label{cor:physical-representation-unique-up-to-fit}
There may be many mathematical descriptions of one system, but relative to a complete physical signature bundle they occupy one admissible physical-fit class.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
If $M_1$ and $M_2$ are physically admissible representations of the same $O$, \autoref{thm:physical-admissibility-signature-descent} gives
$$
\operatorname{Sig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(M_1)
\sim
\operatorname{PhysSig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(O)
\sim
\operatorname{Sig}_{\Pi_{\mathrm{phys}}}(M_2).
$$
By transitivity of signature sameness, $M_1\equiv_{\mathrm{phys},\Pi}M_2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Refining physical questions refines model fit]
\label{thm:refining-physical-questions-refines-fit}
Let $\Pi\subseteq\Pi'$ be physical signature bundles, and let $\equiv_{\Pi}$ and $\equiv_{\Pi'}$ be the induced physical-fit relations. Then
$$
M_1\equiv_{\Pi'}M_2
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
M_1\equiv_{\Pi}M_2.
$$
The converse need not hold.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Agreement under $\Pi'$ includes agreement on every question in $\Pi$, since every question in $\Pi$ is also in $\Pi'$. Hence agreement under the larger bundle restricts to agreement under the smaller bundle. The converse fails when $\Pi'$ contains a question omitted by $\Pi$. Polygonal approximants and a smooth circle may agree under a coarse shape bundle while separating under a differentiability or curvature bundle.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Approximation is tower-relative]
\label{cor:approximation-is-tower-relative}
An approximation statement is indexed by the tower level and signature bundle at which it is asserted.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:refining-physical-questions-refines-fit}, changing the signature bundle can refine the equivalence relation. Since an approximation tower is a staged sequence of classifiers and compressions, an approximation judgment is meaningful only relative to the layer and bundle that license it.
\end{proof}

\section{Observation and reflection}
\label{sec:observation-and-reflection}

\begin{definition}[Reflective inquiry system]
\label{def:reflective-inquiry-system}
A reflective inquiry system is a system $S$ equipped with
$$
(\mathsf{Hist}_S,\mathsf{Cont}_S,\mathsf{Sig}_S,\sim_S,\mathsf{Ledger}_S),
$$
where $\mathsf{Hist}_S$ records finite histories, $\mathsf{Cont}_S$ extends histories, $\mathsf{Sig}_S$ extracts visible signatures, $\sim_S$ classifies signatures, and $\mathsf{Ledger}_S$ records what classification hides.
\end{definition}

BEDC provides the prototype. Observer identity is internal to $\Hist$ and mediated by marks, continuations, bundles, signatures, and certified classifiers; no external equality principle or synchronizing observer is introduced.

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC self-study]
\label{def:metacic-self-study}
MetaCIC self-study is the reflective task in which a formal system constructs a certified description of its own generative and classificatory behaviour without closing a total truth predicate for itself.
\end{definition}

In the language of \autoref{ch:visions-reflection-and-limits}, the ground loop must close so that the system can start, while the meta loop must remain open. Trying to internalize total observation or total truth produces the same kind of fixed-point obstruction that the reflection-limit chapter isolates.

\begin{definition}[Observation-reflection equivalence]
\label{def:observation-reflection-equivalence}
Physical inquiry and MetaCIC self-study are observation-reflection equivalent when they preserve the pattern
$$
\text{source}
\longrightarrow
\text{finite trace}
\longrightarrow
\text{signature}
\longrightarrow
\text{classifier}
\longrightarrow
\text{package or model}
\longrightarrow
\text{ledgered stability}
\longrightarrow
\text{open continuation}.
$$
\end{definition}

This is not an assertion that physical systems and formal systems are the same object. It is an assertion that their inquiry architectures instantiate the same relation certificate pattern in the sense of \autoref{ch:visions-layered-relation-certificate}.

\begin{theorem}[Physical inquiry and MetaCIC self-study share a reflective architecture]
\label{thm:physical-inquiry-metacic-reflective-architecture}
Physical inquiry and MetaCIC self-study share the architecture
$$
\text{finite history}
\longrightarrow
\text{signature}
\longrightarrow
\text{classifier}
\longrightarrow
\text{package or model}
\longrightarrow
\text{ledger}
\longrightarrow
\text{open continuation}.
$$
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
In physical inquiry, the source is a system $R$. Access to $R$ is mediated by finite traces $H_n$. These traces generate signatures under an observation bundle $\Pi$. The signatures are classified, producing model-fit judgments. Hidden assumptions, discarded data, and untested continuations must be recorded in a ledger.

In MetaCIC self-study, the source is the system's own generative behaviour. Access is mediated by finite histories, extended by continuation, exposed through signatures, classified by certified relations, and packaged into derived interfaces. The naming-certificate discipline requires source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger fields for licensed derived interfaces.

Both processes therefore have the same form. Neither process can complete the total meta-judgment from within: MetaCIC does not close an internal truth predicate, and physical inquiry does not close a final-fit oracle.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Physical content is relationally certified]
\label{cor:physical-content-relationally-certified}
Physical content that is independent of a particular observer must be expressed as an inter-observer invariant, not as a property of one private trace.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
\autoref{ch:visions-inter-hist-locality} records that no single history supplies a global empirical standpoint. Observer-independent content must therefore be formulated through relations among histories. Inter-$\Hist$ invariants are exactly the finite, observer-symmetric form of such content.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[The open meta loop is the formal analogue of empirical revisability]
\label{cor:open-meta-loop-empirical-revisability}
The open meta loop in MetaCIC corresponds, at the level of physical inquiry, to the revisability of empirical theory.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
In MetaCIC, local generation and local audit judgments close, but no total internal truth predicate closes. In physical inquiry, finite observations close local fit judgments $\operatorname{Fit}_n(M,R;\Pi)$, but the total claim $\forall n,\operatorname{Fit}_n(M,R;\Pi)$ does not close from within finite observation. Both structures permit local closure while refusing global closure.
\end{proof}

\section{Synthesis}
\label{sec:reality-constrained-synthesis}

\begin{principle}[Reality is modelled through certified approximation towers]
\label{prin:reality-modelled-through-certified-towers}
A physically meaningful mathematical model is not an identity
$$
\text{Reality}=\text{Mathematical Object}.
$$
It is a ledgered tower
$$
\text{Reality}
\longrightarrow
\text{observation history}
\longrightarrow
\text{measurement data}
\longrightarrow
\text{discrete representation}
\longrightarrow
\text{continuous representation}
\longrightarrow
\text{smooth representation}
\longrightarrow
\text{ideal mathematical object}.
$$
Each arrow is licensed only relative to a signature bundle, a stability claim, and a ledger.
\end{principle}

The practical questions attached to each layer are: what the layer hides, whether the hidden material is inactive for the intended signature bundle, and whether the operations used for prediction descend through the compression. If the hidden material is active or descent is uncertified, the tower level is not physically final.

\begin{corollary}[Mathematical idealization is indexed]
\label{cor:mathematical-idealization-indexed}
The framework does not reject idealization. It requires idealization to be indexed by a signature bundle, a stability claim, and a ledger.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:physical-admissibility-signature-descent}, physical admissibility requires signature preservation and operational descent. By \autoref{thm:composite-towers-retain-source-dependence}, the tower must retain source dependence. Thus idealization remains legitimate exactly as certified compression.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Classifier mismatch can produce physical disagreement]
\label{cor:classifier-mismatch-physical-disagreement}
Two theories may disagree because they use different classifiers for what counts as the same physical object.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
If one classifier is coarser than another, representations identified by the first may be separated by the second. By \autoref{thm:refining-physical-questions-refines-fit}, refining the signature bundle refines the fit relation. The disagreement may therefore concern preserved physical signature rather than algebraic error inside either theory.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[The boundary between mathematics and physics is a certificate boundary]
\label{cor:math-physics-certificate-boundary}
A mathematical representation becomes a physical representation only when a physical-fit certificate is supplied.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
A mathematical representation may be licensed internally by a naming certificate with source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data. Physical modelling additionally requires that the classifier match the relevant physical signature bundle. By \autoref{thm:physical-admissibility-signature-descent}, this requires signature preservation and descent for the physical operations used in prediction.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Closing]
\label{rem:reality-constrained-closing}
The chapter's compressed statement is:
$$
\text{mathematical similarity}
\not\Rightarrow
\text{physical sameness},
$$
$$
\text{coarse convergence}
\not\Rightarrow
\text{descent of derivative, curvature, or dynamics},
$$
and
$$
\text{physical adequacy}
=
\text{signature preservation}
+
\text{operational descent}
+
\text{ledgered compression}.
$$
The polygon-circle example is the elementary witness. A polygonal sequence may converge to a circle in a coarse mathematical sense, but treating it as physically circular is legitimate only relative to the properties reality makes active. If reality responds only to coarse shape, the representation may be adequate. If reality responds to differentiability, curvature, local boundary stress, reflection, or flow, the approximation fails unless additional smoothing or descent data are supplied.

Thus approximation is not a weakness to be erased. It is the structural condition under which physical knowledge becomes auditable: finite traces, certified signatures, classifier-relative fit, ledgered compression, and open continuation.
\end{remark}
