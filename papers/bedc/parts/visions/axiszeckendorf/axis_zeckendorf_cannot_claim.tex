\chapter{Cannot-Claim Registry: AxisZeckendorf Cluster}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-axis-zeckendorf-cannot-claim}

This chapter aggregates the deliberate non-claims of the AxisZeckendorf cluster (Chapters~\ref{ch:concrete-instances-axisnat-namecert}--\ref{ch:concrete-instances-full-axis-seal-namecert}). Each entry is paired either with a checked negative theorem in \texttt{BEDC.Derived.AxisZeckendorf} or with an explicit horizon obligation queued for the formalize pipeline. The non-claims protect the cluster's positioning: it is a conservative bridge layer above the finite kernel and below mature mathematical interfaces, not a numerological reinterpretation of binary words.

\section{Registry of non-claims}

The following six entries are not assertions of the cluster. They are explicit refusals; each carries either a paper-level checked theorem above, or an upgrade path that names the missing certificate.

\begin{description}
\item[Cannot claim 1: \(\mathtt{word\_011}\) denotes infinity.]
  Backed by Theorem~\ref{thm:cannot-claim-011-is-infinity}. \(\mathtt{word\_011}\) is a finite three-step history; no infinity object is ever attached to it inside the cluster.

\item[Cannot claim 2: \(\mathsf{ZCarry}\) is history-sameness.]
  Backed by Theorem~\ref{thm:cannot-claim-carry-is-hsame}. The classifier relates non-\(\HSame\) histories; promoting it to a kernel equality is rejected.

\item[Cannot claim 3: \(\mathtt{boundary\_01}\) is a limit / supremum.]
  Backed by Theorem~\ref{thm:cannot-claim-boundary-01-in-zerospine}, together with the chapter remarks of Chapter~\ref{ch:concrete-instances-full-axis-seal-namecert}. The boundary token is a length-two finite history, not a limit object.

\item[Cannot claim 4: \(\FullAxisUp\) is \(\RealUp\) (or \(\CompleteMetricUp\)).]
  Horizon non-claim. Upgrade path: a separate bridge from \(\FullAxisUp\) into the existing \(\CompleteMetricUp\) chapter must be supplied; the cluster does not assert this bridge by itself.

\item[Cannot claim 5: \(\AxisNatUp\) replaces \(\NatUp\).]
  Horizon non-claim. The two interfaces are kernel-distinct (Theorem~\ref{thm:axisnat-natup-kernel-distinct}); identification requires Chapter~\ref{ch:concrete-instances-unary-direction-bridge}, which is itself a non-kernel bridge.

\item[Cannot claim 6: \(\DimLiftUp\) is a primitive geometric dimension.]
  Horizon non-claim. \(\DimLiftUp\) is, in this cluster, a deferred lift seed: the cluster does not assert that the carry classifier produces a geometric dimension, a topological dimension, a vector-space dimension, a homological dimension, or a projective dimension. Promotion to any such reading requires a fresh certificate not part of the AxisZeckendorf cluster.
\end{description}

\section{Horizon obligations associated with the registry}

\begin{theorem}[\(\DimLiftUp\) primitive non-claim, horizon]
\label{thm:cannot-claim-dimLift-primitive-horizon}
The cluster does not assert any kernel-level primitive geometric, topological, vector-space, homological, or projective dimension associated with the carry classifier.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[\(\FullAxisUp \neq \RealUp\) horizon]
\label{thm:cannot-claim-fullAxis-is-real-horizon}
The cluster does not assert any of the field operations of \(\RealUp\) on \(\FullAxisUp\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[\(\AxisNatUp\) does not replace \(\NatUp\) horizon]
\label{thm:cannot-claim-axisNat-replaces-natUp-horizon}
The cluster does not assert that \(\AxisNatUp\) supersedes \(\NatUp\); both interfaces remain independent inside the finite kernel.
\end{theorem}
