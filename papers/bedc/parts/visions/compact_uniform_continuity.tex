\chapter{Compactness Implies Uniform Continuity}
\label{ch:visions-compact-uniform-continuity}
\origin{human}

A continuous function on a compact metric space is uniformly continuous, constructively. This chapter assembles the components already isolated in the kernel: the metric carrier with its located distance (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}), the pointwise modulus chain encoded by $\Cont$ on continuous naming certificates (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}), and the compactness witness packaged as subset $+$ located $+$ finite $\varepsilon$-net $+$ intermediate $+$ compact (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert}). The argument identifies the compactness data as a finite cover indexed by a $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, folds the finitely many $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ radius histories through a certified lower-bound ledger, and reads off a uniform-modulus relation depending only on the tolerance history. The main theorem states this in BEDC form: from a continuous naming certificate together with a compact naming certificate on the same metric carrier, the kernel produces a uniform-continuity ledger, with no appeal to $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$ and no excluded middle.

\section{Uniform modulus from located finite covers}
\label{sec:compact-uniform-continuity-uniform-modulus}

\begin{definition}[Pointwise modulus on $\NameCert_{\ContinuousFunctionUp}$]
\label{def:compact-uniform-pointwise-modulus}
Let $X, Y$ be metric naming certificates and let $\NameCert_f$ be a continuous-function naming certificate witnessing $f \colon X \to Y$. A \emph{pointwise modulus ledger} for $\NameCert_f$ is the BEDC relation on histories
$$
(x,\varepsilon,\delta,\mathsf{cert})
$$
whose accepted rows carry a source-point history $x$, a positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$, a positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ precision history $\delta$, and a certificate history $\mathsf{cert}$. The certificate row is carried by $\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(x,\delta,\mathsf{cert})$ together with the surrounding $\Cont$ rows of $\NameCert_f$: whenever $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,x',d)$ certifies a source distance bounded by $\delta$, the graph and target-metric ledgers certify a target distance bounded by $\varepsilon$. The public datum is this certified relation, not a map from external points and tolerances to external rationals.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Uniform modulus]
\label{def:compact-uniform-uniform-modulus}
A \emph{uniform modulus ledger} for $f \colon X \to Y$ is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ relation
$$
\mathsf{UniformModulusLedger}(\varepsilon,\delta,\mathsf{u})
$$
from positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ tolerance histories to positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ precision histories. An accepted row carries a ledger certificate $\mathsf{u}$ such that, for every pair of source-point histories $x,x'$ and every distance history $d$ with $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,x',d)$ bounded by $\delta$, the graph ledger for $f$ and the target metric certificate produce a target-distance history bounded by $\varepsilon$. The precision is therefore selected by a BEDC relation over histories and classifiers, rather than by an external selector.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Located finite $\varepsilon$-net]
\label{def:compact-uniform-finite-net}
A \emph{located finite $\varepsilon$-net} for a metric carrier $X$ is a $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$-indexed family of center histories. For a positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$, the compact certificate supplies a bundle $\mathcal{P}_\varepsilon$, a center history $c_p$ for every probe name $p$ with $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P}_\varepsilon)$, and coverage rows saying that every source-point history $x$ has some bundled probe $p$ with a distance history $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,c_p,d_p)$ certified below $\varepsilon$. The finite carrier is the closed inductive $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ together with its coverage witnesses; no external enumeration is exposed.
\end{definition}

\section{The kernel construction}
\label{sec:compact-uniform-continuity-construction}

\begin{lemma}[Subordinate finite modulus cover]
\label{lem:compact-uniform-subordinate-modulus-cover}
Let $X$ be a compact metric carrier and let $f \colon X \to Y$ have the pointwise modulus ledger of \autoref{def:compact-uniform-pointwise-modulus}. For every positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$, the compact certificate supplies a nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ $\mathcal{P}_\varepsilon$ and, for every bundled probe $p$, histories
$$
(c_p,\rho_p,\delta_p,\mathsf{cert}_p)
$$
such that $\rho_p$ and $\delta_p$ are positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ histories, $(c_p,\varepsilon/3,\delta_p,\mathsf{cert}_p)$ is accepted by the pointwise modulus ledger, $\rho_p$ is certified below $\delta_p$, and every source-point history $x$ has a bundled probe $p$ whose center satisfies the certified half-radius coverage row $d_X(x,c_p)<\rho_p/2$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Certificate reading]
Apply compactness to the located positive-radius ledger whose member at $x$ is supplied by an accepted pointwise-modulus row for tolerance $\varepsilon/3$. The finite-cover certificate returns a $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, center histories, radius histories, and subordination rows already tied to those pointwise rows, so the comparisons $\rho_p \le \delta_p$ are part of the returned data.

The located half-radius clause is the cover witness used by the kernel construction below: for each input point $x$, it produces a bundled probe name $p$ with $d_X(x,c_p)<\rho_p/2$. No scale depending on the eventual uniform-modulus ledger is used to obtain this bundle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Capstone: finite BEDC fold supports compact uniform modulus]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-finite-minimum-certificate}
Let $X$ and $Y$ be metric BEDC carriers whose distance rows are supplied by $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$, and let a BEDC history carry
$$
\mathsf{CompactWitnessCarrier}(\mathsf{subset},\mathsf{located},\mathsf{finite},\mathsf{intermediate},\mathsf{compact})
$$
for $X$. Let another history carry
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(\mathsf{source},\mathsf{map},\mathsf{target},\mathsf{modulus},\mathsf{cert})
$$
for a graph $f \colon X \to Y$ over the same source metric. For each positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$, suppose \autoref{lem:compact-uniform-subordinate-modulus-cover} returns a nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine
$$
\mathcal{P}_\varepsilon
$$
whose bundled probes carry positive $\RatUp$ radius histories, center histories, pointwise-modulus rows, and subordinate half-radius cover rows. Then primitive recursion over $\mathcal{P}_\varepsilon$, using the $\RatUp$ order readback of \autoref{thm:totalorder-prefix-finite-minimum-selector} at each successor step, produces a unary precision history $\mu_\varepsilon$ and a fold certificate saying that $\mu_\varepsilon$ is classifier-stably below every bundled half-radius ledger. The row $\mathsf{UniformModulusLedger}(\varepsilon,\mu_\varepsilon,\mathsf{u}_\varepsilon)$ of \autoref{def:compact-uniform-uniform-modulus} is then certified for the same continuous graph.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Certificate assembly]
Read the finite cover from the compact witness: the $\mathsf{finite}$ and $\mathsf{located}$ rows give a nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of centers, positive $\RatUp$ radius histories, and for every point of $X$ a bundled probe whose center lies within the displayed half-radius.

Transport the radius histories through the $\RatUp$ total-order readback. Since the bundle is nonempty and every entry is carried by the rational positive-radius certificate, primitive recursion over $\mathcal{P}_\varepsilon$ compares the accumulator with the next half-radius ledger and keeps a certified lower-bound history. The uniqueness row of \autoref{def:totalorder-prefix-finite-minimum-witness} makes this fold stable under the classifier, so the selected lower bound is a BEDC history rather than an external choice.

At each bundled center, the $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ modulus row reads back the local implication from the graph certificate: a distance bounded by the selected radius below $\delta_p$ gives output distance below $\varepsilon/3$. The subordinate-cover rows guarantee that the same center can be used for any input point, and the fold certificate guarantees that the proposed uniform precision is below the chosen local radius.

Finally apply the metric semantic certificate: symmetry converts $d_X(x,x')$ to the orientation needed at the center, and the triangle row combines $d_X(x,c_p)$ with $d_X(x,x')$ to put $x'$ inside the same local ball. Applying the target-metric triangle row to $f(x)$, $f(c_p)$, and $f(x')$ yields the displayed uniform estimate. Thus $\mathsf{UniformModulusLedger}(\varepsilon,\mu_\varepsilon,\mathsf{u}_\varepsilon)$ is certified by the finite BEDC rational-fold ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous on compact yields uniform modulus]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-continuity}
Let $X$ be a metric carrier with a compact naming certificate $\NameCert_X$ exposing the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ finite-net witness of \autoref{def:compact-uniform-finite-net}, and let $f \colon X \to Y$ be witnessed by a continuous naming certificate $\NameCert_f$ over $X$ exposing the pointwise modulus ledger of \autoref{def:compact-uniform-pointwise-modulus}. For every positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$, primitive recursion over the compact bundle and the certified $\RatUp$ lower-bound fold produces histories $\delta_\varepsilon$ and $\mathsf{u}_\varepsilon$ such that
$$
\mathsf{UniformModulusLedger}(\varepsilon,\delta_\varepsilon,\mathsf{u}_\varepsilon)
$$
holds for $f$. The conclusion is a certified uniform-modulus ledger from tolerance histories to precision histories, not an external modulus selector.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
Fix a positive tolerance history $\varepsilon$. Invoke \autoref{lem:compact-uniform-subordinate-modulus-cover} to obtain a nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ $\mathcal{P}_\varepsilon$ whose probes carry centers $c_p$, pointwise precision histories $\delta_p$, subordinate radius histories $\rho_p$, and half-radius coverage rows. Then \autoref{thm:compact-uniform-finite-minimum-certificate} supplies the classifier-stable rational-fold ledger $\mu_\varepsilon$ below every bundled half-radius ledger.

Let $x,x'$ be source-point histories with source distance certified below $\mu_\varepsilon$. The subordinate cover gives a bundled probe $p$ with $d_X(x,c_p)<\rho_p/2$. Because the fold ledger certifies $\mu_\varepsilon \le \rho_p/2$, the triangle row gives
$$
d_X(x',c_p) \le d_X(x',x)+d_X(x,c_p) < \rho_p.
$$
Also $d_X(x,c_p)<\rho_p/2<\rho_p$.

The bound $\rho_p \le \delta_p$ lets the pointwise modulus row at $c_p$ apply to both $x$ and $x'$. Hence $d_Y(f\,c_p,f\,x)<\varepsilon/3$ and $d_Y(f\,c_p,f\,x')<\varepsilon/3$. The triangle inequality on $Y$ gives $d_Y(f\,x,f\,x')<\varepsilon$. The construction uses only the finite subordinate cover, primitive recursion over $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, the certified finite-fold rational ledger over $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$, and the metric certificates.
\end{proof}

\section{Axiom-purity of the uniform-modulus construction}
\label{sec:compact-uniform-continuity-axiom-purity}

\begin{remark}[No appeal to non-constructive principles]
\label{rem:compact-uniform-axiom-purity}
The construction in \autoref{thm:compact-uniform-continuity} uses only primitive recursion on the finite net (a $\Cont$-shaped ledger over the closed inductive $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of centers), arithmetic on $\mathbb{Q}_{>0}$ (rational addition, multiplication, and division by $2$, all kernel-level operations on $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}), and the located-distance comparison built into $\NameCert_{\MetricSpaceUp}$. None of these operations passes through $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$, $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$, or $\mathsf{propext}$. The chapter therefore falls under the same axiom-purity gate as the rest of the manuscript.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Heine--Cantor as a kernel theorem]
\label{rem:compact-uniform-heine-cantor}
\autoref{thm:compact-uniform-continuity} is the BEDC form of the Heine--Cantor theorem of classical analysis. The classical proof uses sequential compactness and an extracted convergent subsequence; the BEDC proof uses the located finite $\varepsilon$-net and a certified lower-bound fold over its $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ radius ledger. The two routes deliver the same uniform modulus relation when the metric carrier is taken as $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ restricted to a closed bounded interval (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interval-namecert}), making the classical result a derived corollary of the kernel-level statement and not the other way around.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Composition with the real-completeness capstone]
\label{rem:compact-uniform-real-completeness-composition}
The construction composes with the real-completeness capstone (\autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}): once $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ is upgraded to a regular Cauchy carrier, every closed bounded interval $[a, b] \subset \RealUp$ inherits a compact naming certificate via the standard located $\varepsilon$-net of dyadic rationals at scale $\lceil 1/\varepsilon \rceil$, and \autoref{thm:compact-uniform-continuity} then gives uniform continuity of every continuous $f \colon [a, b] \to \RealUp$ with an explicit uniform-modulus ledger. The chapter is therefore the canonical bridge from the analytic certificate layer to the constructive analysis literature.
\end{remark}
