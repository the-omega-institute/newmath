\section{Classifier-Shift Information}
\label{sec:visions-philosophy-classifier-shift-information}
\origin{ai}

The preceding distinction-history layer separates generated record growth
from classifier change. This section names the corresponding information
principle: information growth in the auditable-generativity reading is not
the mere accumulation of outputs, traces, activations, or notational
variants. It is a certified change in the classifier surface under which
generated material is organized.

\begin{definition}[Certified classifier state]
\label{def:philosophy-certified-classifier-state}
A certified classifier state is a tuple
$$
\mathcal{T}=(S_{\mathcal{T}},P_{\mathcal{T}},L_{\mathcal{T}},
C_{\mathcal{T}},N_{\mathcal{T}})
$$
where $S_{\mathcal{T}}$ is the source specification, $P_{\mathcal{T}}$
is the pattern specification, $L_{\mathcal{T}}$ is the ledger policy,
$C_{\mathcal{T}}$ is the classifier relation on the source material, and
$N_{\mathcal{T}}$ is the naming certificate or semantic certificate
licensing the state.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Classifier equivalence]
\label{def:philosophy-classifier-equivalence}
Two certified classifier states $\mathcal{T}$ and $\mathcal{T}'$ are
classifier-equivalent on their common source when every pair of histories
admitted by both source specifications receives the same classifier
judgment from $C_{\mathcal{T}}$ and $C_{\mathcal{T}'}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Classifier shift]
\label{def:philosophy-classifier-state-shift}
A passage $\mathcal{T}\to\mathcal{T}'$ has classifier shift when the
two states are not classifier-equivalent on their common source. The
shift may appear as refinement, coarsening, or reorganization of the
classifier cells, but it must be witnessed on the public classifier
surface rather than inferred from record length, notation, or fluency.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Structural discovery]
\label{def:philosophy-structural-discovery}
A structural discovery is a passage to a certified classifier state
$\mathcal{T}'$ such that the passage has classifier shift, the target
state has a ledger witness for its admitted material, and the changed
classifier is actually used on the displayed record or certificate
surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Discovery requires classifier shift]
\label{thm:philosophy-discovery-requires-classifier-shift}
Every structural discovery has classifier shift.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Classifier shift is one of the defining fields of
\autoref{def:philosophy-structural-discovery}. A passage that preserves
the same classifier on the common source may improve presentation,
coverage of displayed routes, or verification status, but it has not
changed the classifier surface that organizes the material.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Discovery requires certificate and ledger]
\label{thm:philosophy-discovery-requires-certificate-ledger}
Every structural discovery carries both a certificate for the resulting
classifier state and a ledger witness for the material it classifies.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The target of a structural discovery is a certified classifier state, so
its tuple contains the certificate component $N_{\mathcal{T}'}$. The
definition also requires a ledger witness. Without the certificate, the
changed classifier is only an asserted relation; without the ledger, the
source, hidden material, projection, compression, and boundary behavior
are not auditable.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Record growth is not discovery]
\label{cor:philosophy-record-growth-not-discovery-classifier}
A record-only passage is not a structural discovery.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
A record-only passage preserves the certified classifier state while
adding more displayed material under that state. By
\autoref{thm:philosophy-discovery-requires-classifier-shift},
structural discovery requires classifier shift, so record growth
alone is insufficient.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Presentation change is not discovery]
\label{cor:philosophy-presentation-change-not-discovery}
Changing notation, proof organization, naming style, or exposition is not
by itself a structural discovery.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Presentation changes can alter how a state is read without changing the
source specification, classifier relation, certificate fields, or ledger
policy. Such a passage is classifier-equivalent on the common source and
therefore lacks classifier shift.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Boundary, bridge, and unification discovery]
\label{def:philosophy-discovery-modes}
Classifier-shift discovery has three recurring modes. Boundary discovery
exposes a principled obstruction to strengthening a classifier. Bridge
discovery connects previously separated classifier states through a
certified relation. Unification discovery replaces several classifier
states by a single certified state whose classifier surface accounts for
their shared behavior.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Formal hardening is not classifier shift]
\label{thm:philosophy-formal-hardening-not-classifier-shift}
Formal verification of a statement or certificate component does not by
itself create classifier shift.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Formal verification changes the audit status of a statement or proof
object. If the source specification, classifier relation, certificate
fields, and ledger policy remain the same, the certified classifier state
is classifier-equivalent to its prior state on the common source. The
verification may increase reliability, but the classifier surface has not
changed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Classifier-shift information principle]
\label{thm:philosophy-classifier-shift-information-principle}
In the auditable-generativity reading, theoretical information growth is
read from certified classifier shift rather than from output length,
record count, feature count, notation change, or verification status
alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Output length and record count are governed by record unfolding, which
can preserve the same classifier. Notation and presentation changes can
preserve classifier equivalence. Formal hardening can preserve the same
classifier while changing only audit status. By contrast, structural
discovery requires a changed classifier together with certificate and
ledger witnesses. Hence the auditable unit of theoretical information
growth is certified classifier shift.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Compression, ledger, and conservation}
\label{subsec:philosophy-compression-ledger-conservation}

\begin{definition}[Compression map]
\label{def:philosophy-compression-map}
For a finite family of histories $X$, a compression map is a displayed
map $q:X\to Y$ into a representation carrier $Y$. The map induces the
classifier
$$
x\sim_q x'
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
q(x)=q(x').
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Hidden residue]
\label{def:philosophy-hidden-residue}
The hidden residue of a compression map $q:X\to Y$ is the family of
distinction pairs collapsed by the representation:
$$
\mathsf{Res}(q)
=
\{(x,x')\in X\times X:\ x\neq x'\ \text{and}\ q(x)=q(x')\}.
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Ledger-complete compression]
\label{def:philosophy-ledger-complete-compression}
A compression map is ledger-complete when it carries a ledger
$\Lambda_q$ whose recorded rows cover the hidden residue of $q$. A
ledger gap is a collapsed distinction in $\mathsf{Res}(q)$ not recorded
by $\Lambda_q$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Certified compression]
\label{def:philosophy-certified-compression}
A certified compression is a compression map equipped with source,
pattern, compression-induced classifier, stability, and ledger rows
sufficient to license the compressed representation as a certificate
surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compression is classifier formation]
\label{thm:philosophy-compression-classifier-formation}
Every compression map induces a classifier on its source family.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The relation $\sim_q$ is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive because it
is defined by equality of the displayed representation $q(x)$. Thus the
compression map partitions its source family into representation cells.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Certified compression requires ledger coverage]
\label{thm:philosophy-certified-compression-requires-ledger}
A certified compression has no unrecorded hidden residue.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:philosophy-certified-compression}, the compression
certificate includes a ledger row. By
\autoref{def:philosophy-ledger-complete-compression}, the ledger row must
cover every collapsed distinction in $\mathsf{Res}(q)$. Any unrecorded
collapsed distinction would be a ledger gap, so the compression would not
be certified.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Shorter presentation is not certified gain]
\label{cor:philosophy-shorter-presentation-not-certified-gain}
A shorter representation is not by itself a certified information gain.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Shortening can be achieved by collapsing distinctions. By
\autoref{thm:philosophy-certified-compression-requires-ledger}, a
certified compression must account for the collapsed distinctions through
its ledger. The representation length alone therefore does not determine
the certified gain.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Information conservation for compression]
\label{thm:philosophy-information-conservation-compression}
The auditable gain of a compression claim is read only after subtracting
ledger cost, certificate cost, verification cost when claimed, and ledger
debt for unrecorded residue from the apparent description gain.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apparent description gain records only the shorter representation. A
certificate must also expose source, pattern, classifier, stability, and
ledger rows. When a formal verification claim is made, the proof-checking
work is part of the audit cost. When hidden residue is not recorded, the
claim carries ledger debt. Omitting any of these terms would treat hidden
material as free information, contrary to the certificate discipline.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Compression discovery requires classifier shift]
\label{cor:philosophy-compression-discovery-requires-classifier-shift}
A compression is a structural discovery only when it changes the
certified classifier surface and supplies the corresponding certificate
and ledger rows.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Same-classifier compression may reduce description cost, but it remains
classifier-equivalent on the common source. By
\autoref{thm:philosophy-discovery-requires-classifier-shift}, structural
discovery requires classifier shift, and by
\autoref{thm:philosophy-discovery-requires-certificate-ledger}, it also
requires certificate and ledger witnesses.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Machine-learning reading}
\label{subsec:philosophy-classifier-shift-ml-reading}

For a learned model, additional activations, candidate features, lower
training loss, or more examples do not automatically constitute
interpretability discovery. They become interpretability-relevant only
when they change a certified classifier state: the activation source, the
feature pattern, the induced classifier, the stability account, and the
ledger must be displayed. Dimensionality reduction, feature clustering,
pruning, and model compression are likewise compression maps: they must
record hidden residue before they can count as closed explanations. This
is the philosophy-side counterpart of
\autoref{sec:large-model-tensor-classifier-certificate-extraction}: a
learned tensor distinction can propose a classifier, but interpretability
information is certified only when that classifier is named, stabilized,
ledgered, and auditable.
