\subsection{DNA window-six symbolic-physical dynamics}
\label{subsec:philosophy-dna-window-six-dynamics-bridge}

The codon-window bridge of
\autoref{subsec:philosophy-codon-window-six-bridge} identifies a codon
with a six-bit local source window, and the stop-codon bridge of
\autoref{subsec:philosophy-stop-codon-zeckendorf-suffix-bridge}
identifies the standard stop class as a Zeckendorf-legal L-shape inside
the $\mathrm{U}+\mathrm{RR}$ square. The dynamic bridge adds a further
discipline: DNA and RNA should not be treated merely as static code
tables. They form a relative symbolic-physical layer with local windows,
frames, stop boundaries, raw-codon ledgers, and state transitions
executed through cellular machinery.

The word ``relative'' is load-bearing. Replication, transcription,
repair, translation, release, accessibility changes, and chromatin
updates are performed by polymerases, ribosomes, release factors, repair
enzymes, chromatin proteins, tRNA pools, regulatory factors, and
cellular context. The bridge therefore does not claim that DNA is a
closed autonomous dynamics. It claims that DNA/RNA reading has a
symbolic state space and transition surface rich enough to require its
own ledger rather than collapsing into the final protein class.

\begin{definition}[DNA window-six dynamics bridge]
\label{def:philosophy-dna-window-six-dynamics-bridge}
A DNA window-six dynamics bridge is a conservative biological bridge
whose source field contains RNA or DNA sequence histories together with
the two-axis base readback of
\autoref{def:philosophy-rna-two-axis-readback}. Its pattern field
records local codon windows, frame movement, stop-boundary transitions,
replication, transcription, repair, mutation, recombination, and
accessibility updates. Its classifier field includes the codon
classifier, stop classifier, amino-acid classifier, and
microstate-preserving synonymous-codon classifier. Its stability field
requires frame-step closure, window-six readback stability,
stop-boundary determinacy under a fixed biological code table, and
ledger transport under explicit cellular-context parameters. Its ledger
policy preserves the raw codon, frame position, base-axis bits, code
table, release-factor or tRNA context when used, accessibility state,
and biological bridge metadata.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Translation state]
\label{def:philosophy-translation-state}
A translation state has the schematic form
$$
  q_{t}
  :=
  (f_{t},c_{t},r_{t},\kappa_{t}),
$$
where $f_{t}$ records the current frame, $c_{t}$ records the current
codon, $r_{t}$ records ribosomal or release status, and $\kappa_{t}$
records the cellular context relevant to the bridge. In the minimal
finite model, the context is suppressed and the state is
$$
  q_{t}:=(f_{t},c_{t}).
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Elongation and stop transitions]
\label{def:philosophy-elongation-stop-transitions}
For a non-stop codon $c_{t}$ with
$$
  \mathsf{TranslateClassifier}(c_{t})
  =
  \mathsf{AminoAcid}(a),
$$
the elongation transition advances the frame by one codon and records
the emitted amino-acid class together with the raw codon:
$$
  q_{t}\longrightarrow q_{t+1}.
$$
For a stop codon with
$$
  \mathsf{TranslateClassifier}(c_{t})=\mathsf{Stop},
$$
the transition enters a halted or release state:
$$
  q_{t}\longrightarrow q_{\mathsf{halt}}.
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Stop codons are boundary states]
\label{thm:philosophy-stop-codons-boundary-states}
Under the standard-code biological bridge, the codons
$\mathrm{UAA}$, $\mathrm{UAG}$, and $\mathrm{UGA}$ are translation
boundary states.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:philosophy-three-stop-reconstruction}, the
Zeckendorf-family stop candidate under the chosen two-axis readback is
exactly $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}$. The external
standard-code bridge supplies the biological classifier row that reads
these codons as stop signals. Combining the finite reconstruction with
that bridge row gives the transition
$q_{t}\to q_{\mathsf{halt}}$ for precisely those three standard-code
codons. This is a bridge theorem, not a BEDC kernel theorem about
biology.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Expanded DNA dynamic state]
\label{def:philosophy-expanded-dna-dynamic-state}
The local six-bit codon cube
$$
  Q_{6}:=\{0,1\}^{6}
$$
is sufficient for local codon classification but not for the full
symbolic-physical dynamics. A minimal expanded state has the form
$$
  S_{\mathsf{DNA}}
  :=
  \Sigma^{n}
  \times
  \mathsf{FrameState}
  \times
  \mathsf{EpigeneticState}
  \times
  \mathsf{ChromatinTopologyState}
  \times
  \mathsf{CellularEnzymeContext}
  \times
  \mathsf{TranslationContext}
  \times
  \mathsf{RepairContext}.
$$
Equivalently, a compressed state may be displayed as
$$
  D_{t}:=(s_{t},f_{t},e_{t},c_{t},\kappa_{t}),
$$
where $s_{t}$ is the sequence state, $f_{t}$ is the frame and window
position, $e_{t}$ records epigenetic marks, $c_{t}$ records accessibility
and topology, and $\kappa_{t}$ records cellular machinery and regulatory
context.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[DNA transition families]
\label{def:philosophy-dna-transition-families}
The dynamic bridge names transition families rather than imposing a
universal execution order:
$$
\begin{array}{c|l}
  R & \text{replication}\\
  T & \text{transcription or selective readout}\\
  L & \text{translation}\\
  M & \text{mutation}\\
  P & \text{repair}\\
  G & \text{recombination or genome rearrangement}\\
  E & \text{epigenetic update}\\
  C & \text{chromatin or topology update}\\
  F & \text{feedback from protein or translation state}
\end{array}
$$
The schematic form is
$$
  D_{t+1}=\Phi(D_{t};\kappa_{t}),
$$
where the active components of $\Phi$ depend on cell state and bridge
context. No fixed composition such as
$C\circ E\circ P\circ M\circ R\circ T\circ L$ is licensed as a universal
biological sequence.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Window six is local but incomplete]
\label{thm:philosophy-window-six-local-incomplete}
The local state $Q_{6}$ captures the codon-level classifier domain, but
it does not exhaust DNA dynamics.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Two biological states may share the same local six-bit codon window and
differ in frame, methylation, histone-related marks, chromatin
accessibility, polymerase status, ribosome state, repair status,
release-factor availability, tRNA context, or code-table variant. Those
differences may change whether the sequence is copied, read, repaired,
translated, released, or ignored. Hence $Q_{6}$ is a local classifier
domain, while the dynamic bridge must also record sequence position,
frame, accessibility, repair, and cellular-machine context.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Codon microstate ledger]
\label{def:philosophy-codon-microstate-ledger}
A codon microstate ledger is a bridge row
$$
  \mathsf{CodonLedger}(c,o,\kappa)
$$
recording the raw codon $c$, frame position, output class $o$, tRNA or
release-factor context when used, codon-usage or optimality data when
used, source code table, and variant-code metadata. The ledger separates
the codon microstate from the amino-acid or stop class to which it is
classified.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Translation does not collapse codon microstates]
\label{thm:philosophy-translation-not-microstate-collapse}
If two codons classify to the same amino-acid class, the biological
bridge must still retain their raw codon rows whenever dynamic effects
are in scope.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The amino-acid output is a many-to-one classifier. If the raw codon were
discarded, the bridge could not express codon usage, elongation-rate
differences, mRNA-stability effects, tRNA-context dependence, or
variant-code reassignment. Therefore the translation classifier is a
ledger-preserving quotient, not a deletion of source microstate.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Variant-code deformation fields]
\label{def:philosophy-variant-code-deformation-fields}
Non-standard genetic codes are recorded as deformations of biological
bridge fields. A variant may change the stop-shape region, the
translation classifier, release-factor or release-mechanism rows, the
biological code table attached to the readback, or the cellular context
that permits readthrough, recoding, or organellar translation. Such
deformations do not refute the finite stop-square enumeration; they
change the bridge target in which that enumeration is interpreted.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Dynamic bridge modules]
\label{def:philosophy-dynamic-bridge-modules}
The dynamic research program decomposes into seven modules:
\begin{description}
  \item[Channel and Zeckendorf termination.]
  Preserve the no-internal-$11$, first-$11$, and layer-separation
  theorems for the BEDC channel.

  \item[RNA four-phase and window six.]
  Record $\mathsf{RNA4Alphabet}^{\uparrow}:=\BoolUp\times\BoolUp$,
  the $\mathsf{Flat}_{6}$ map, and the codon-window ledger.

  \item[Stop L-shape reconstruction.]
  Retain $\mathsf{UPurPur}$, family suffix, Zeckendorf-legality, the
  three-stop theorem, and the $\mathrm{UGG}$ excluded-corner row.

  \item[Translation dynamics.]
  Define translation states, elongation transitions, stop transitions,
  and the halted or release state.

  \item[DNA state-space dynamics.]
  Define expanded sequence, frame, accessibility, chromatin, repair,
  mutation, and cellular-context fields.

  \item[Microstate ledger.]
  Separate raw codon rows from amino-acid or stop output classes and
  record synonymous-codon non-collapse.

  \item[Release factors and variants.]
  Compare the RF1/RF2 L-shape bridge with variant-code deformation
  fields.
\end{description}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Dynamic bridge figures]
\label{def:philosophy-dynamic-bridge-figures}
The bridge has three stable display forms. The two-axis base atlas is
$$
\begin{array}{c|cc}
   & \mathrm{W} & \mathrm{S}\\
\hline
  \mathrm{Y} & \mathrm{U} & \mathrm{C}\\
  \mathrm{R} & \mathrm{A} & \mathrm{G}
\end{array}
$$
The stop square is the L-shape of

$$
  Q_{6}
  \subset
  S_{\mathsf{DNA}},
$$
where $Q_{6}$ is the local codon window and
$S_{\mathsf{DNA}}$ is the expanded sequence-frame-accessibility-context
state.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dynamic bridge cannot-claim boundary]
\label{thm:philosophy-dynamic-bridge-cannot-claim-boundary}
The DNA window-six dynamics bridge does not license any claim that
Zeckendorf or window six forces DNA biology, that DNA has a fully
autonomous closed dynamics independent of cellular machinery, that the
BEDC channel terminator is identical to a biological stop codon, that
the family suffix $11$ in $\mathrm{UGG}$ is the channel terminator, that
the standard stop codons are universal across all genetic codes, that
$\mathrm{UGG}$ is tryptophan by BEDC logic alone, that synonymous codons
are dynamically identical after amino-acid classification, that the DNA
state space is exhausted by $Q_{6}$, that $\Phi$ has a universal fixed
composition order, that the biological bridge replaces
$\NameCert$ obligations, or that
$\mathsf{DNAWindow6Dynamics}^{\uparrow}$ is already checked.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The channel-token and stop-codon refusals are inherited from
\autoref{thm:philosophy-zstop-cannot-claim-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:philosophy-codon-window-cannot-claim-boundary}. The
autonomy and state-space refusals follow from
\autoref{thm:philosophy-window-six-local-incomplete}. The synonymous
codon refusal follows from
\autoref{thm:philosophy-translation-not-microstate-collapse}. The
fixed-order refusal follows from
\autoref{def:philosophy-dna-transition-families}. The checked-status
refusal follows because the present subsection is a vision-level
biological bridge plan rather than a Lean target.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Allowed dynamic bridge claim]
\label{cor:philosophy-dynamic-bridge-allowed-claim}
Under a standard-code biological bridge, window six and the no-$11$
motif supply a structural model for local DNA and RNA reading and
stop-boundary dynamics. The three standard stop codons form the
Zeckendorf-legal L-shape in the $\mathrm{U}+\mathrm{RR}$ square, and the
broader DNA layer is modeled as a relative symbolic-physical dynamics
with state, context, transitions, and ledger-preserved codon
microstates.
\end{corollary}
