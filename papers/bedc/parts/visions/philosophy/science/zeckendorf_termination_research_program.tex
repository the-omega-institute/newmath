\subsection{Research program for Zeckendorf-legal termination}
\label{subsec:philosophy-zeckendorf-termination-research-program}

The stop-codon suffix bridge of
\autoref{subsec:philosophy-stop-codon-zeckendorf-suffix-bridge} gives a
paper-level reconstruction of the standard stop set from a two-axis RNA
readback and a no-$11$ family-suffix condition. The research program
attached to that reconstruction has a narrower purpose: it separates
the finite theorem that can be checked internally from the biological
bridge evidence that must remain external, and it names the certificate
fields needed before the bridge may be exported at stronger status.

The guiding question is why the standard stop codons
$\mathrm{UAA}$, $\mathrm{UAG}$, and $\mathrm{UGA}$ appear together. In
BEDC form, the question becomes whether the standard stop-codon set can
be reconstructed as the Zeckendorf-legal part of the
$\mathrm{U}+\mathrm{RR}$ square under a conservative two-axis bridge for
RNA bases:
$$
\begin{gathered}
  \mathsf{UPurineSquare}
  =
  \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA},\mathrm{UGG}\},\\
  \mathrm{AA}\mapsto 00,\qquad
  \mathrm{AG}\mapsto 01,\qquad
  \mathrm{GA}\mapsto 10,\qquad
  \mathrm{GG}\mapsto 11.
\end{gathered}
$$
The finite claim keeps $00$, $01$, and $10$ and excludes the unique
$11$ corner. The biological claim then reads the surviving L-shape as
the standard stop set.

\begin{definition}[Conditional stop-codon reconstruction problem]
\label{def:philosophy-conditional-stop-codon-reconstruction-problem}
The stop-codon reconstruction problem is the following conditional
bridge target. Given
\begin{itemize}
  \item the product atlas
    $\mathsf{RNA4Alphabet}^{\uparrow}:=\BoolUp\times\BoolUp$;
  \item the readback
    $\mathrm{U}=(\mathrm{Y},\mathrm{W})$,
    $\mathrm{C}=(\mathrm{Y},\mathrm{S})$,
    $\mathrm{A}=(\mathrm{R},\mathrm{W})$, and
    $\mathrm{G}=(\mathrm{R},\mathrm{S})$;
  \item codons as triples
    $(\mathsf{RNA4Alphabet}^{\uparrow})^{3}$;
  \item the stop-shape region
    $\mathrm{U}+\mathrm{RR}$;
  \item the family-suffix legality condition excluding $11$;
  \item a standard-code biological bridge,
\end{itemize}
prove that the reconstructed stop-candidate class is exactly
$$
  \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
$$
The problem is a bridge problem, not a kernel-level derivation of the
biological genetic code.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Research inputs]
\label{def:philosophy-zterm-research-inputs}
The BEDC-side inputs are the active channel theorem package of
\autoref{subsec:philosophy-channel-genetic-code-bridge}, the derived
interfaces $\BoolUp$ and $\ProdUp$, classifier transport, displayed pair
and triple ledgers, layer separation, and cannot-claim registry
discipline. The biological inputs are external rows: RNA bases,
three-position codons, the standard stop set, the assignment of
$\mathrm{UGG}$ as a sense codon in the standard code, release-factor
recognition data where used, and variant genetic-code tables where used.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Research subquestions]
\label{def:philosophy-zterm-research-subquestions}
The program decomposes into nine subquestions:
\begin{itemize}
  \item why adjacent $11$ is the BEDC channel boundary pattern and which
    no-internal-$11$ theorem supports it;
  \item how RNA or DNA bases are read as a conservative
    $\BoolUp\times\BoolUp$ target;
  \item how the triple product yields the $64$-position codon atlas;
  \item why the standard stop set is analyzed inside the
    $\mathrm{U}+\mathrm{RR}$ square;
  \item whether excluding the family suffix $11$ reconstructs exactly
    the three standard stop codons;
  \item how $\mathrm{UGG}$ functions as the excluded square corner while
    remaining a standard-code sense codon;
  \item how bacterial release-factor recognition arms compare with the
    same L-shape;
  \item which bridge field changes when a genetic-code variant reassigns
    a stop codon;
  \item which part is finite enumeration suitable for Lean checking and
    which part remains biological bridge evidence.
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Four-phase stop-square data]
\label{def:philosophy-four-phase-stop-square-data}
The finite data of the stop square consists of a shape axis
$\{\mathrm{Y},\mathrm{R}\}$, a family axis
$\{\mathrm{W},\mathrm{S}\}$, the readback
$$
\begin{aligned}
  \mathrm{U} &= (\mathrm{Y},\mathrm{W}),&
  \mathrm{C} &= (\mathrm{Y},\mathrm{S}),\\
  \mathrm{A} &= (\mathrm{R},\mathrm{W}),&
  \mathrm{G} &= (\mathrm{R},\mathrm{S}),
\end{aligned}
$$
and the family projection on the purine-purine suffix:
$$
  \mathrm{family}(\mathrm{A})=0,\qquad
  \mathrm{family}(\mathrm{G})=1.
$$
Thus the second and third positions of the
$\mathrm{U}+\mathrm{RR}$ square display the binary family square
$00$, $01$, $10$, $11$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Research module boundaries]
\label{def:philosophy-zterm-research-module-boundaries}
The program has six module boundaries.
\begin{description}
  \item[Channel termination.]
  Define the legal channel body, event marker, no-internal-$11$
  theorem, first-$11$ terminator theorem, decode-compile inverse
  theorem, and
  layer-separation theorem.

  \item[Four-phase alphabet bridge.]
  Package $\mathsf{RNA4Alphabet}^{\uparrow}$ as a product of two
  binary interfaces; record the two-axis biological readback,
  complement involution, codon triple product, and translation
  classifier as bridge data.

  \item[Stop-codon enumeration.]
  Define the $\mathrm{U}+\mathrm{RR}$ square, family suffix,
  Zeckendorf-legality predicate, and stop-candidate classifier; prove by
  four cases that $\mathrm{UGG}$ is the unique excluded corner.

  \item[Release-factor bridge.]
  Record the standard bacterial recognition arms
  $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG}\}$ and
  $\{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UGA}\}$ as external data and compare their
  union with the reconstructed L-shape.

  \item[Variant-code boundary.]
  Compare standard and alternative genetic-code tables by identifying
  which external bridge field changes: stop-shape region, translation
  classifier, release-factor bridge, or standard-code table.

  \item[Formalization boundary.]
  Keep the finite enumeration over $\BoolUp\times\BoolUp$ inside the
  checked layer, while keeping base names, biochemical translation, and
  empirical recognition data as external bridge rows.
\end{description}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite stop-square enumeration target]
\label{thm:philosophy-finite-stop-square-enumeration-target}
Inside the $\mathrm{U}+\mathrm{RR}$ square, the predicate
``family suffix is not $11$'' selects exactly
$\mathrm{UAA}$, $\mathrm{UAG}$, and $\mathrm{UGA}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The square has four elements:
$$
  \mathrm{UAA},\quad \mathrm{UAG},\quad
  \mathrm{UGA},\quad \mathrm{UGG}.
$$
Their family suffixes are respectively $00$, $01$, $10$, and $11$.
The predicate rejects exactly the fourth suffix. Hence the selected
class is precisely
$$
  \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}.
$$
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Distinguished non-stop corner]
\label{cor:philosophy-distinguished-non-stop-corner}
Within the standard-code bridge, $\mathrm{UGG}$ is the unique
non-selected corner of the $\mathrm{U}+\mathrm{RR}$ square.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:philosophy-finite-stop-square-enumeration-target}, the
only rejected family suffix is $11$. The codon $\mathrm{UGG}$ is the
unique square element whose second and third positions both have family
bit $1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Variant-code deformation boundary]
\label{thm:philosophy-variant-code-deformation-boundary}
A genetic-code variant that reassigns one of the standard stop codons
changes external biological bridge data rather than the finite
enumeration of \autoref{thm:philosophy-finite-stop-square-enumeration-target}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The enumeration uses only the two-axis readback, the
$\mathrm{U}+\mathrm{RR}$ square, and the no-$11$ family-suffix
condition. A variant genetic code changes the biological interpretation
of at least one codon position or recognition field. That change belongs
to the translation classifier, the release-factor bridge, the
standard-code table, or the chosen stop-shape bridge. It therefore
alters the bridge target, not the four-case finite calculation.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Lean finite skeleton]
\label{def:philosophy-zterm-lean-finite-skeleton}
The internal finite theorem can be represented by a small closed
alphabet, codon triples, and Boolean predicates:
\begin{verbatim}
inductive Bit where
  | zero
  | one

abbrev Shape  := Bit
abbrev Family := Bit

structure Base where
  shape  : Shape
  family : Family

def U : Base := { shape := Bit.zero, family := Bit.zero }
def C : Base := { shape := Bit.zero, family := Bit.one }
def A : Base := { shape := Bit.one,  family := Bit.zero }
def G : Base := { shape := Bit.one,  family := Bit.one }

structure Codon where
  b1 : Base
  b2 : Base
  b3 : Base

def isPurine (b : Base) : Prop := b.shape = Bit.one
def familyBit (b : Base) : Bit := b.family

def UPurineSquare (c : Codon) : Prop :=
  c.b1 = U /\ isPurine c.b2 /\ isPurine c.b3

def ZLegalSuffix (c : Codon) : Prop :=
  not (familyBit c.b2 = Bit.one /\ familyBit c.b3 = Bit.one)

def ZStopCandidate (c : Codon) : Prop :=
  UPurineSquare c /\ ZLegalSuffix c
\end{verbatim}
The target theorem states that $\mathsf{ZStopCandidate}$ is equivalent
to membership in the three displayed codons
$\mathrm{UAA}$, $\mathrm{UAG}$, and $\mathrm{UGA}$. The proof is a
finite case split over the second and third bases in
$\{\mathrm{A},\mathrm{G}\}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Research-program cannot-claim boundary]
\label{thm:philosophy-zterm-research-cannot-claim-boundary}
The research program does not license any claim that Zeckendorf coding
alone proves the biological genetic code, that the BEDC channel
terminator is identical to a DNA or RNA stop codon, that the family
suffix $11$ is the channel terminator, that the standard stop codons are
universal across all genetic codes, that $\mathrm{UGG}$ is tryptophan by
BEDC logic alone, that $\mathsf{ZStopCandidate}$ is already checked, or
that a compiled channel substring directly denotes a nucleotide base.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The refusals are inherited from
\autoref{thm:philosophy-zstop-cannot-claim-boundary}. The present
program only organizes the route from vision-strength reconstruction to
finite enumeration and external bridge reporting; it does not discharge
the external biological bridge fields or collapse the channel and codon
layers.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Allowed research-program claim]
\label{cor:philosophy-zterm-research-allowed-claim}
Under the two-axis RNA bridge and Zeckendorf-family suffix legality, the
standard stop-codon set is structurally reconstructed as the L-shape
$$
  \{\mathrm{UAA},\mathrm{UAG},\mathrm{UGA}\}
$$
inside the $\mathrm{U}+\mathrm{RR}$ square. The theorem is conditional
and layered: Zeckendorf supplies the no-$11$ pattern, BEDC supplies
classifier and ledger discipline, and biology supplies the standard-code
bridge target.
\end{corollary}
