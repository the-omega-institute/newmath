\section{Gaps, Transcendental Supply, and Boundary Sockets}
\label{sec:visions-philosophy-gap-t-boundary}

The preceding layer distinguishes local witness-preserving proof from
unwitnessed meta closure. The present layer describes what remains when
a closed observational system genuinely needs a commitment that cannot
be generated from its records. Such a commitment must not be hidden as
an internal rule. It is exposed as a gap socket, classified by the
shape of the missing supply, and held at a boundary whose far end is
named only apophatically.
