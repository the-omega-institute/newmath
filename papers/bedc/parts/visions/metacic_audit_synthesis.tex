\chapter{MetaCIC audit synthesis: closed-CIC critical-path overview}
\label{ch:metacic-audit-synthesis}

\section{The setup}
\label{sec:metacic-audit-synthesis-setup}

BEDC.MetaCIC is the closed CIC meta-theory formalisation inside the BEDC
project. Its closed-term meta-properties are distributed across the subject
reduction, confluence, normalisation, substitution, decidable-checking, and
consistency-assembly files. The relevant paper audit maps are
\autoref{ch:metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction},
\autoref{ch:metacic-confluence-audit-map},
\autoref{ch:metacic-normalization-audit-map},
\autoref{ch:metacic-substitution-audit-map},
\autoref{ch:metacic-decidable-audit-map}, and
\autoref{ch:closed-metacic-consistency-assembly}.

This chapter gives a single reader-facing overview of that state. It does not
replace the sibling audit maps. It points to them, aligns their boundaries, and
extracts the closed-MetaCIC critical path: which nodes are already available,
which nodes are conditional, and where unconditional closed consistency is
blocked.

\section{The five submodule audit maps}
\label{sec:metacic-audit-synthesis-five-maps}

\subsection{Reading rule}
\label{subsec:metacic-audit-synthesis-reading-rule}

The five maps are not interchangeable evidence. Each map exports a different
kind of checked object. Subject reduction exports an obstruction and a
four-input preservation interface. Confluence exports reduction-geometry
fragments and conditional bridges. Normalisation exports reducibility-candidate
routes. Substitution exports closed syntactic laws. Decidability exports
executable checking surfaces.

A citation to one map therefore cannot silently stand in for another. A
substitution lemma may support the beta case of a later preservation proof, but
it is not the preservation proof. A confluence bridge may show how a supplied
diamond would flow to closed star confluence, but it is not the supplied
diamond. A decidable checker may certify a displayed typing computation, but it
is not a decision procedure for all meta-theoretic predicates. This is the
reading discipline used throughout this synthesis.

\subsection{Subject reduction}
\label{subsec:metacic-audit-synthesis-subject-reduction}

The subject-reduction map records a parameterised preservation theorem,
four documented discharge obligations, and a checked obstruction theorem for
the unparameterised closed subject-reduction statement. Closed subject
reduction is therefore explicitly not available in the present substrate; see
\autoref{ch:metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction}.

\subsection{Confluence}
\label{subsec:metacic-audit-synthesis-confluence}

The confluence map records a substitution-conditional parallel diamond,
bridges from closed parallel diamond to closed star confluence, and
unconditional fragments for closed normal sources and atom shapes. It does not
claim arbitrary closed-term Church--Rosser; see
\autoref{ch:metacic-confluence-audit-map}.

\subsection{Normalization}
\label{subsec:metacic-audit-synthesis-normalization}

The normalisation map records the candidate-mediated strong-normalisation path
for closed typed terms. Candidate, closed-candidate, or candidate-set evidence
delivers strong normalisation and no-infinite-reduction consequences, but the
map does not erase that evidence into a blanket theorem over all closed typed
terms; see \autoref{ch:metacic-normalization-audit-map}.

\subsection{Substitution}
\label{subsec:metacic-audit-synthesis-substitution}

The substitution map records unconditional closed syntactic laws:
\texttt{shift\_closed}, \texttt{substitute\_closed}, and closed composition
fragments. These facts supply syntax-side binder movement, but they do not
discharge beta typing preservation or full subject reduction; see
\autoref{ch:metacic-substitution-audit-map}.

\subsection{Decidability}
\label{subsec:metacic-audit-synthesis-decidability}

The decidable map records executable equality and type-checking surfaces, their
soundness and completeness wrappers, gallery sanity witnesses, and bounded
normal-form comparison under finished-normalisation evidence. It is a local
checking layer, not a decision oracle for subject reduction, confluence,
normalisation, or consistency; see
\autoref{ch:metacic-decidable-audit-map}.

\section{The critical path to unconditional closed consistency}
\label{sec:metacic-audit-synthesis-critical-path}

The closed-MetaCIC consistency assembly in
\autoref{ch:closed-metacic-consistency-assembly} can be read as the following
critical path.

\begin{enumerate}
\item Closed strong normalisation is available through the candidate-mediated
  normalisation interface. Its public witnesses include
  \leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.closed\_typed\_term\_sn\_from\_candidate},
  \leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.closed\_typed\_term\_sn\_from\_closedCandidate},
  and
  \leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.closed\_typed\_term\_sn\_from\_candidateSet}.
\item No closed normal proof of false is available:
  \leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.no\_closed\_normal\_proof\_of\_false}.
\item Closed normal consistency assembly is available:
  \leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.closed\_normal\_consistency\_assembly}.
\item Closed reduction by subject reduction is blocked by the dependent-codomain
  obstruction:
  \leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.closed\_consistency\_reduction\_obstruction\_not\_available}.
\item The discharge bundle required for an unconditional preservation lift is
  explicitly named:
  \leanvariant{BEDC.MetaCIC.SubjectReductionDischargeBundle}.
\item Unconditional closed consistency remains blocked until the bundle is
  supplied and the closed-normal theorem can be transported from arbitrary
  closed proofs to their normal endpoints.
\end{enumerate}

The same dependency can be displayed as a compact audit graph:

\begin{verbatim}

  status: AVAILABLE
  feeds:
    closed-normal consistency branch

No closed normal proof of False
  status: AVAILABLE
  witness:
    BEDC.MetaCIC.no_closed_normal_proof_of_false

Closed normal consistency
  status: AVAILABLE
  witness:
    BEDC.MetaCIC.closed_normal_consistency_assembly

Closed reduction by subject reduction
  status: BLOCKED
  reason:
    dependent-codomain obstruction
  required interface:
    BEDC.MetaCIC.SubjectReductionDischargeBundle

Unconditional closed consistency
  status: BLOCKED
  reason:
    closed-normal consistency cannot be lifted without closed reduction
\end{verbatim}

\section{Status matrix}
\label{sec:metacic-audit-synthesis-status-matrix}

The same state can be read as a matrix of positive results and non-claims.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{0.21\linewidth}p{0.32\linewidth}p{0.36\linewidth}}
\textbf{Map} & \textbf{Available} & \textbf{Not claimed} \\
\hline
Subject reduction &
parameterised preservation, four named inputs, checked obstruction &
unparameterised closed subject reduction \\
Confluence &
conditional parallel diamond route, closed normal and atom fragments &
arbitrary closed Church--Rosser \\
Normalisation &
candidate-mediated closed strong normalisation routes &
candidate-free global strong normalisation \\
Substitution &
closed shift, closed substitution, closed composition fragments &
unrestricted substitution calculus or beta typing preservation \\
Decidability &
term equality, type-checking soundness and completeness, bounded comparison &
global decision procedure for meta-theoretic properties \\
Assembly &
closed-normal consistency &
unconditional closed consistency \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The matrix is deliberately asymmetric. The positive column records what the
Lean substrate and paper maps can be used for directly. The non-claim column
records the strongest nearby statement that must not be inferred from that
positive result. The synthesis depends on this asymmetry: the project has many
checked local nodes, but the closed-consistency endpoint needs a specific
transport edge that those local nodes do not themselves supply.

\section{How the maps interact}
\label{sec:metacic-audit-synthesis-interaction}

The interaction pattern is a supply chain with named handoffs rather than a
single theorem with hidden premises. Substitution supplies closed syntactic
facts about moving terms across binders. Subject reduction would have to turn
the relevant binder movement into type preservation across beta and domain
congruence. Normalisation supplies the target normal-form side once the proof
term is inside the candidate-mediated strong-normalisation interface.
Consistency assembly consumes the normal-form theorem, but only after the
displayed false type has been preserved along reduction.

Confluence and decidability sit beside that path. Confluence describes how
reduction branches can be joined under the available and conditional diamond
interfaces. Decidability describes which local syntactic and typing questions
are executable inside the represented fragment. Both constrain how later work
may cite the substrate, but neither bypasses the subject-reduction lift.

The useful reading is therefore:

\begin{enumerate}
\item Substitution is syntax algebra.
\item Subject reduction is type-preservation transport.
\item Normalisation is termination-to-normal-form evidence.
\item Closed-normal consistency is the contradiction eliminator at the normal
  endpoint.
\item Full closed consistency is the composition of transport and the normal
  endpoint.
\end{enumerate}

The missing edge is item 2 in the closed arbitrary-proof setting. Once that
edge is unavailable, items 3 and 4 remain valid but cannot be composed into
item 5 for every closed proof term.

\section{The frontier shape}
\label{sec:metacic-audit-synthesis-frontier-shape}

The substrate currently establishes normal-form sub-consistency
unconditionally. This is the strongest closed-consistency node available: a
closed proof term that is already beta-strong cannot inhabit the internal false
encoding. The result is sharp because it states exactly the normal-form
fragment, and it is useful because it is the endpoint that a successful
subject-reduction lift would consume.

The lift to full closed consistency requires one of two project-level moves.
The first route is to discharge the four subject-reduction hypotheses named in
\autoref{ch:metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction}. The second route is to
extend the substrate beyond the present closed-CIC surface so that the missing
context-reduction and dependent-codomain readback lemmas become expressible and
provable. On the current substrate, the checked obstruction theorem prevents
the paper from treating that lift as a local proof exercise.

Confluence and decidability do not remove this frontier. Confluence supplies
reduction geometry and conditional bridges, while decidability supplies local
checking procedures. Substitution supplies closed syntax algebra. All three are
necessary audit context, but none of them replaces the subject-reduction
transport needed to carry an arbitrary closed proof of false to the closed
normal-form theorem.

\section{Apophatic discipline applied to meta-theory}
\label{sec:metacic-audit-synthesis-apophatic}

The MetaCIC audit maps apply BEDC's apophatic naming discipline to the
project's own meta-theory. The substrate names what it has: checked closed
normal consistency, candidate-mediated normalisation, closed substitution
laws, local decidable checking, atom and normal-source confluence fragments, and
conditional bridges. It also names what it does not have: unparameterised closed
subject reduction, arbitrary closed Church--Rosser, unrestricted substitution
commutation, a global decidability oracle, and unconditional closed
consistency.

This synthesis chapter combines those names into one critical-path map. Its
purpose is to prevent the reader from overreading a neighbouring checked layer
as the missing endpoint. The current state is strong, but it is not ambiguous:
the assembled theorem is closed-normal consistency, and the blocked edge is the
subject-reduction lift across the dependent-codomain frontier.

\section{Boundary}
\label{sec:metacic-audit-synthesis-boundary}

This chapter is an audit overview, not a proposal and not a proof of any open
obligation. It does not discharge the subject-reduction bundle, does not claim
full closed confluence, does not strengthen the candidate-mediated
normalisation interface, and does not turn decidable local checking into a
global meta-theoretic decision procedure.

The claim is exactly the synthesis claim: the five audit maps and the
consistency assembly form a closed critical-path diagram for the present
BEDC.MetaCIC state. The available endpoint is normal-form closed consistency.
The unavailable endpoint is unconditional closed consistency, blocked at the
named subject-reduction discharge interface.
