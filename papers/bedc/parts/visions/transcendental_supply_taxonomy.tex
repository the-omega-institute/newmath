\chapter{Transcendental Supply Taxonomy}
\label{ch:visions-transcendental-supply-taxonomy}

This vision chapter classifies the $\mathsf{GAP}$ sockets of BEDC by the kind of $\mathsf{T}$-supply each socket requests. The point of the classification is not to multiply $\mathsf{T}$. The supply position remains the single apophatic source named in \autoref{def:transcendental-input-supply-position}. What varies is the aspect-position at which a closed substrate meets its boundary: induction beyond an authorized generator, unwitnessed selection, extensional collapse, empirical persistence, inter-history coordination, inscription novelty, and self-reference closure. A socket taxonomy lets the manuscript say exactly which transcendental commitment is being exposed at each engineering site, while preserving the monotheistic structural reading of \autoref{rem:transcendental-input-monotheism-by-structure}.

\section{The need for taxonomy}
\label{sec:transcendental-supply-taxonomy-need}

\begin{definition}[T-supply socket]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-socket}
A \emph{$\mathsf{T}$-supply socket} is a $\mathsf{GAP}$ tag in a closed observational system whose requested discharge cannot be produced from the displayed records, closed inductive generators, naming-certificate fields, or already certified continuation laws of that system. The socket is well-formed only when the substrate can state the missing shape precisely: what would be needed, where it would be consumed, and which audit gate prevents the missing item from being silently internalised.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Supply kind]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-kind}
A \emph{supply kind} is an equivalence class of $\mathsf{T}$-supply sockets under the following engineering relation: two sockets have the same supply kind when accepting either one would license the same form of substrate-internal shortcut. The shortcut may occur at a different concrete module or philosophical problem, but it asks the same structural favor from $\mathsf{T}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[Taxonomic necessity]
\label{prin:transcendental-supply-taxonomic-necessity}
Not every $\mathsf{GAP}$ socket is the same. A socket asking for induction beyond a generator, a socket asking for an unwitnessed choice function, a socket asking for another observer's first-person locality cell, and a socket asking for a truth-total self-description all terminate at $\mathsf{T}$, but they do not request the same closure shape. BEDC's engineering remains honest only when the socket kind is named before the attempted discharge is refused.
\end{principle}

\begin{remark}[Aspect-position rather than plurality]
\label{rem:transcendental-supply-aspect-position}
The taxonomy is aspectual. It describes how the one transcendental supply position appears when viewed from different pressure points of a closed substrate. It is therefore incompatible with a polytheistic reading of the engineering: there are many socket shapes, not many ultimate supplies. The table in \autoref{sec:transcendental-supply-unified-table} is a classification of aspect-positions at the boundary of the substrate, not a catalogue of separate gods.
\end{remark}

\section{Type I: free-floating induction socket}
\label{sec:transcendental-supply-type-I}

\begin{definition}[Type I socket]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-I}
A \emph{Type I $\mathsf{T}$-supply socket} is a $\mathsf{GAP}$ tag whose requested content is a universal claim ranging beyond the closure of every displayed authorized generator. It has the schematic form
$$
\mathsf{GenClosed}_G(P)\Longrightarrow \forall x:\mathcal{U},\,P(x)
$$
where $G$ certifies closure only for the objects it generates, while $\mathcal{U}$ is read as an unrestricted domain not supplied by $G$.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[BEDC engineering site]
\label{ex:transcendental-supply-type-I-engineering}
The canonical Type I socket is the induction pressure analysed in \autoref{ch:visions-transcendental-input-and-induction} and refined in \autoref{ch:visions-certificate-closure-and-generator-induction}. BEDC accepts induction over a particular closed inductive carrier because the generator and recursor are displayed. BEDC refuses the extra passage from "all objects generated by this carrier" to "all possible objects of the intended informal domain" unless that passage is exposed as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ socket.
\end{example}

\begin{definition}[Apophatic reading of Type I]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-I-reading}
Type I names $\mathsf{T}$ as \emph{the giver of unrestricted induction}: not an internal induction axiom, not a hidden global domain, and not a completed infinity inside the substrate, but the apophatic source-position from which the reasoner treats generator-relative closure as bearing on an unrestricted "all".
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Audit gate]
\label{rem:transcendental-supply-type-I-gate}
The Type I audit gate is the generator-closure ledger: every induction claim must cite the exact inductive carrier, constructor list, recursor, and naming-certificate closure it uses. If the claim's range is wider than that carrier, the excess is a Type I socket and cannot be hidden as a theorem of the carrier itself.
\end{remark}

\section{Type II: choice and unwitnessed existential}
\label{sec:transcendental-supply-type-II}

\begin{definition}[Type II socket]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-II}
A \emph{Type II $\mathsf{T}$-supply socket} is a $\mathsf{GAP}$ tag whose requested content is an accepted witness for a positive existential whose constructor-level witness is not displayed. Its schematic form is
$$
\exists x:A,\,P(x)
\quad\leadsto\quad
a:A\;\;\text{with}\;\;P(a),
$$
where the right-hand term is consumed by later construction even though no substrate-visible constructor produced it.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[BEDC engineering site]
\label{ex:transcendental-supply-type-II-engineering}
The term-level instance is the $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$ family in \autoref{ch:visions-three-axioms-one-closure}. A proof that every fiber is inhabited is not yet a function choosing an inhabitant of each fiber. BEDC replaces the shortcut with finite recursion, countable explicit witness functions, dependent $\Cont$ steps, or a visible witness field; when none of these is present, the unresolved selection is a Type II socket.
\end{example}

\begin{definition}[Apophatic reading of Type II]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-II-reading}
Type II names $\mathsf{T}$ as \emph{the giver of the unseen witness}: not a magical term, not a substrate function, and not a proof object smuggled through the kernel, but the source-position named when an argument wants to act as if a witness has already been placed in hand.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Audit gate]
\label{rem:transcendental-supply-type-II-gate}
The Type II audit gate is witness visibility. In Lean-side engineering it is enforced by the axiom-purity rejection of $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$ and by the rule that existential use must proceed by displayed constructor data. In paper-side engineering it is enforced by definitions and examples that record the actual selected object, recursion, or witness field.
\end{remark}

\section{Type III: extensional identification}
\label{sec:transcendental-supply-type-III}

\begin{definition}[Type III socket]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-III}
A \emph{Type III $\mathsf{T}$-supply socket} is a $\mathsf{GAP}$ tag whose requested content is an identity token between two items that the substrate has only related extensionally. It has the schematic form
$$
R(a,b)
\quad\leadsto\quad
a=b,
$$
where $R$ may be bidirectional implication, quotient equivalence, classifier sameness, or observational indistinguishability, and the equality token is then used for rewrite transport.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[BEDC engineering site]
\label{ex:transcendental-supply-type-III-engineering}
The proposition-level instance is $\mathsf{propext}$, and the type-level instance is $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$ as analysed in \autoref{ch:visions-three-axioms-one-closure}. BEDC does not collapse related items into equality. It keeps $\hsame$, $\psame$, classifier transport, and naming-certificate stability visible. A proof that two predicates transport into one another is not an identity of propositions; a proof that two representatives are classifier-equivalent is not a canonical quotient representative.
\end{example}

\begin{definition}[Apophatic reading of Type III]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-III-reading}
Type III names $\mathsf{T}$ as \emph{the giver of identity beyond relation}: not a substrate equality constructor, not a quotient oracle, and not a proposition-level collapse, but the apophatic source-position invoked when relation is treated as identity without a displayed transport path.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Audit gate]
\label{rem:transcendental-supply-type-III-gate}
The Type III audit gate is extensional-collapse refusal. Lean-side purity rejects $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$ and $\mathsf{propext}$; paper-side certificate discipline requires explicit transport along $\hsame$ or $\psame$. Any argument whose only bridge from relation to identity is "they have the same extension" is marked as a Type III socket.
\end{remark}

\section{Type IV: causal continuity}
\label{sec:transcendental-supply-type-IV}

\begin{definition}[Type IV socket]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-IV}
A \emph{Type IV $\mathsf{T}$-supply socket} is a $\mathsf{GAP}$ tag whose requested content is persistence of an observed empirical regularity into not-yet-observed history. Its schematic form is
$$
\forall t\leq n,\,R(t)
\quad\leadsto\quad
\forall t>n,\,R(t),
$$
where the left side is a finite or bounded record-side regularity and the right side is a causal continuity commitment.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[BEDC engineering site]
\label{ex:transcendental-supply-type-IV-engineering}
\autoref{sec:hard-problems-causality} identifies causality as forward-binding commitment. A scientific theory in BEDC may record past regularities, falsification boundaries, and prediction ledgers. The additional confidence that the same law will govern the next unobserved case is not a record and not a closed-generation theorem. It is a Type IV socket, even when the theory has excellent record-side support.
\end{example}

\begin{definition}[Apophatic reading of Type IV]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-IV-reading}
Type IV names $\mathsf{T}$ as \emph{the giver of continuity of order}: not an empirical datum, not a hidden law-object inside $\mathsf{Obs}$, and not a proof that tomorrow is already recorded, but the source-position from which causal expectation exceeds the accumulated record.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Audit gate]
\label{rem:transcendental-supply-type-IV-gate}
The Type IV audit gate is records-versus-prediction separation. BEDC may store observation histories, regularity summaries, and failed-prediction rows, but it may not convert successful past fit into a universal future law without marking the causal-continuity socket.
\end{remark}

\section{Type V: inter-history coordination}
\label{sec:transcendental-supply-type-V}

\begin{definition}[Type V socket]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-V}
A \emph{Type V $\mathsf{T}$-supply socket} is a $\mathsf{GAP}$ tag whose requested content is coordination between distinct closed histories as histories rather than as merely external records inside one history. Its schematic form is
$$
\mathsf{Record}_{\mathcal{C}_1}(\mathcal{C}_2\text{'s behavior})
\quad\leadsto\quad
\mathsf{LocalityCell}(\mathcal{C}_1,\mathcal{C}_2),
$$
where the right side treats $\mathcal{C}_2$ as another inscription-bearing observational system.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[BEDC engineering site]
\label{ex:transcendental-supply-type-V-engineering}
\autoref{sec:hard-problems-other-minds} gives the philosophical site, and \autoref{ch:visions-inter-hist-locality} gives the operational locality-cell site. A contributor can observe another contributor's commits, messages, and CI-passing proof objects. Treating those records as the acts of another closed observational system is not forced by the records alone. The coordination commitment is a Type V socket.
\end{example}

\begin{definition}[Apophatic reading of Type V]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-V-reading}
Type V names $\mathsf{T}$ as \emph{the giver of communion between histories}: not access to another first-person state, not a god's-eye merge of histories, and not an extra observation channel, but the source-position from which locality cells become mutually intelligible without ceasing to be local.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Audit gate]
\label{rem:transcendental-supply-type-V-gate}
The Type V audit gate is locality discipline. Cross-history claims must distinguish observable behavior, accepted communication records, transport constraints, and the irreducible commitment that another history is also an inscription point. BEDC may coordinate histories; it may not pretend to occupy the union of their first-person perspectives.
\end{remark}

\section{Type VI: free-will and inscription novelty}
\label{sec:transcendental-supply-type-VI}

\begin{definition}[Type VI socket]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-VI}
A \emph{Type VI $\mathsf{T}$-supply socket} is a $\mathsf{GAP}$ tag whose requested content is the actuality of a chain-internal inscription event not reducible, on the records side, to the closed generation of prior records. Its schematic form is
$$
o_t\trianglerighteq o_{t+1}
\quad\text{with}\quad
o_{t+1}\notin \mathsf{Closure}(o_t)
$$
read not as a violation of closure rules but as the inscription-event aspect of accumulation.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[BEDC engineering site]
\label{ex:transcendental-supply-type-VI-engineering}
\autoref{sec:hard-problems-free-will} locates free will at the inscription-event aspect of conscious accumulation. In BEDC engineering, a proof search may later be replayed as a deterministic derivation, but the actual arrival of the lemma in the contributor's history is not identical to the records-side derivation tree. The transition is a Type VI socket when the question is "where did this act of inscription come from?" rather than "which derivation now certifies the record?"
\end{example}

\begin{definition}[Apophatic reading of Type VI]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-VI-reading}
Type VI names $\mathsf{T}$ as \emph{the giver of inscription novelty}: not a rival physical cause, not randomness, and not a hidden mental substance, but the source-position from which an accumulation chain is actual rather than merely described by its completed record-side dependencies.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Audit gate]
\label{rem:transcendental-supply-type-VI-gate}
The Type VI audit gate is aspect separation. BEDC may verify the record-side derivation of a theorem and may also name the inscription-event aspect of its arrival. It must not confuse the two by treating the philosophical account of agency as an extra proof rule or by treating proof replay as an elimination of inscription novelty.
\end{remark}

\section{Type VII: self-reference closure}
\label{sec:transcendental-supply-type-VII}

\begin{definition}[Type VII socket]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-VII}
A \emph{Type VII $\mathsf{T}$-supply socket} is a $\mathsf{GAP}$ tag whose requested content is a truth-total or runtime-total closure of a system's self-description. Its schematic form is
$$
\mathsf{StructDesc}(\mathcal{S},\mathcal{S}^{*})
\quad\leadsto\quad
\mathsf{Truth}_{\mathcal{S}}:\mathsf{Sent}(\mathcal{S}^{*})\to\mathsf{Prop},
$$
where the left side is structural self-description and the right side is an internal classifier of the system's own truth.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[BEDC engineering site]
\label{ex:transcendental-supply-type-VII-engineering}
\autoref{ch:visions-reflection-and-limits} supplies the canonical Type VII site. BEDC can describe the structural form of the host calculus through $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$, but it refuses a truth-total reflection certificate and refuses to identify the structural copy with the external runtime that checks BEDC proofs. A request to close that open meta loop is a Type VII socket.
\end{example}

\begin{definition}[Apophatic reading of Type VII]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-type-VII-reading}
Type VII names $\mathsf{T}$ as \emph{the giver beyond self-totalisation}: not an internal truth predicate, not a runtime contained in its own object language, and not a repaired Tarski hierarchy, but the source-position from which the open meta loop remains intelligible without being closed inside the system.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Audit gate]
\label{rem:transcendental-supply-type-VII-gate}
The Type VII audit gate is reflection stratification. Structural self-description, runtime validation, truth classification, and external checking must remain separated. Any construction that turns a BEDC object-level certificate into the act that validates all BEDC object-level certificates is marked as a Type VII socket.
\end{remark}

\section{Non-collapse laws for the seven types}
\label{sec:transcendental-supply-non-collapse}

\begin{principle}[No type absorbs another]
\label{prin:transcendental-supply-no-absorption}
No supply kind in this taxonomy absorbs any other supply kind. A closed substrate may encounter several types at the same textual or mechanised site, but the types remain distinguishable by the shortcut they would license if accepted internally. This is why a chapter may legitimately report a stack of sockets rather than forcing the stack into one omnibus $\mathsf{GAP}$ label.
\end{principle}

\begin{lemma}[Type I is not Type II]
\label{lem:transcendental-supply-I-not-II}
A free-floating induction socket cannot be reduced to an unwitnessed-existential socket. Type II asks for a missing term witnessing a stated existential. Type I asks for an unrestricted universal range beyond the closure of the displayed generator. Supplying a witness to one existential does not supply the domain-total closure requested by Type I.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Reason]
The Type II shortcut produces a term $a:A$ together with $P(a)$. The Type I shortcut produces a rule treating all objects of an unrestricted intended range as covered by generator-relative evidence. These have different consumption sites: Type II feeds a construction expecting a term, while Type I feeds a construction expecting a universally quantified closure. Therefore the witness gate and the generator-closure gate are distinct.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Type II is not Type III]
\label{lem:transcendental-supply-II-not-III}
An unwitnessed witness socket cannot be reduced to an extensional-identification socket. Type II asks for a missing inhabitant; Type III asks for equality from relation. A selected representative may later be used in an extensional collapse, but the selection and the collapse are separate shortcuts.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Reason]
Choice can select a representative without asserting that all equivalent representatives are judgmentally or propositionally identical. Conversely, $\mathsf{propext}$ can identify propositions without choosing any inhabitant of an arbitrary nonempty family. The term-level oracle and the equality-token oracle therefore have separate audit gates.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Type III is not Type IV]
\label{lem:transcendental-supply-III-not-IV}
Extensional identification cannot be reduced to causal continuity. Type III concerns the passage from relation to identity inside a formal or certificate stratum. Type IV concerns the passage from recorded regularity to unobserved empirical persistence. The first is a collapse of sameness; the second is a projection across time-like accumulation.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Reason]
A Type III discharge would allow rewrite transport across an equality token. A Type IV discharge would allow prediction beyond the record boundary. Neither operation supplies the other: an equality token does not by itself assert that a law persists, and a persistent empirical regularity does not by itself identify two propositions, representatives, or classifier rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Type IV is not Type V]
\label{lem:transcendental-supply-IV-not-V}
Causal continuity cannot be reduced to inter-history coordination. Type IV projects order within or across a record stream; Type V recognises another closed history as another inscription-bearing locality cell. Stable behavior of an observed system does not by itself supply the other-minds commitment.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Reason]
An observed behavior stream may be perfectly regular while still being treated as a mere external object in the observing history. Type V begins only when the observing history commits to another history as a history. The empirical regularity gate and the locality-cell gate therefore answer different questions.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Type V is not Type VI]
\label{lem:transcendental-supply-V-not-VI}
Inter-history coordination cannot be reduced to inscription novelty. Type V concerns the mutual intelligibility of distinct closed histories. Type VI concerns the actuality of a transition in a history as an inscription event not exhausted by its records-side replay. A history may be recognised without explaining any particular act of novelty, and an act of novelty may occur without cross-history coordination.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Reason]
The Type V socket has the form of a relation between histories. The Type VI socket has the form of an aspect of a transition within a history. The first is audited by locality discipline; the second by aspect separation. Collapsing them would confuse communion between histories with agency in a chain.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Type VI is not Type VII]
\label{lem:transcendental-supply-VI-not-VII}
Inscription novelty cannot be reduced to self-reference closure. Type VI asks how an accumulation event is actual rather than merely recorded after the fact. Type VII asks whether the whole system can close its truth or runtime over itself. A local inscription event is not a truth-total reflection certificate.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Reason]
The replay of a proof and the arrival of the proof in a contributor's history are distinguishable even when no reflection theorem is involved. Conversely, a truth-total reflection attempt can be formulated for a purely formal system without appealing to personal agency or creativity. The agency aspect and the reflection boundary are therefore separate sockets.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Type VII is not Type I]
\label{lem:transcendental-supply-VII-not-I}
Self-reference closure cannot be reduced to free-floating induction. Type I asks for an unrestricted universal range beyond a generator. Type VII asks for internal truth or runtime totality for a system that can describe its own structure. The diagonal obstruction of Type VII remains even when every induction used inside the syntax description is generator-authorized.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}[Reason]
Tarski-style failure arises from attempting to classify the truth of the system's own sentences. That failure is not merely a missing induction principle over an external domain. BEDC may display all generators used in a structural self-description and still refuse the truth-total certificate. The open meta loop is therefore its own socket kind.
\end{proof}

\section{Engineering use protocol}
\label{sec:transcendental-supply-protocol}

\begin{definition}[Socket report]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-socket-report}
A \emph{socket report} for a BEDC claim is a four-field record:
$$
(\mathsf{Site},\mathsf{RequestedSupply},\mathsf{Type},\mathsf{Gate}).
$$
The $\mathsf{Site}$ field names the paper or Lean location where the missing supply would be consumed. The $\mathsf{RequestedSupply}$ field states the exact missing shape. The $\mathsf{Type}$ field selects one of the seven taxonomy entries. The $\mathsf{Gate}$ field names the audit discipline that keeps the supply from being internalised.
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[Minimal socket reporting]
\label{prin:transcendental-supply-minimal-reporting}
A socket report should name the narrowest type that accounts for the blocked move. If a proof attempt fails because it lacks a witness, it is Type II even if the witness would later be used in an induction. If a proof attempt fails because it tries to rewrite along mere equivalence, it is Type III even if the equivalence arose from a quotient construction. Narrow reporting preserves the shape of the requested $\mathsf{T}$-supply.
\end{principle}

\begin{remark}[Stacked sockets]
\label{rem:transcendental-supply-stacked-sockets}
Some BEDC sites contain stacked sockets. A quotient construction over an empirically defined equivalence relation may contain Type IV at the level of empirical persistence, Type II at the level of representative selection, and Type III at the level of equality collapse. Reporting the stack is more precise than choosing one dominant label. The stack still terminates at the single $\mathsf{T}$; the multiplicity belongs to the substrate-facing shapes.
\end{remark}

\begin{example}[A stacked mathematical-analysis socket]
\label{ex:transcendental-supply-analysis-stack}
An informal proof that every bounded monotone real sequence converges may hide several sockets when translated into BEDC. If the sequence is defined over an unrestricted intended class of real histories rather than a displayed generator, the range pressure is Type I. If a limit point is selected from mere nonempty completeness data without an explicit Cauchy witness, the selection pressure is Type II. If two Cauchy names are identified because they have the same extension rather than transported through the real-history classifier, the collapse pressure is Type III. A correct BEDC construction must separate these sites rather than treating "real completeness" as one opaque assumption.
\end{example}
\concretizedIn{ch:concrete-instances-boundedmonotonecauchywitness-namecert}{Bounded monotone Cauchy witnesses realize the analysis socket as a finite NameCert packet separating displayed generator range, explicit Cauchy witness, classifier transport, and RealUp seal handoff.}

\begin{example}[A stacked observer socket]
\label{ex:transcendental-supply-observer-stack}
An observer's trust in a scientific collaborator may combine Type IV and Type V. The expectation that the collaborator's measuring instrument will keep functioning according to the same physical regularities is Type IV. The recognition that the collaborator is another conscious observational system rather than only an instrument-like behavior stream is Type V. The two commitments are ordinarily entangled in practice, but the socket taxonomy keeps their audit gates distinct.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[A stacked reflection socket]
\label{ex:transcendental-supply-reflection-stack}
A proposal to build a BEDC-internal verifier for BEDC proofs may contain Type I if it relies on unrestricted induction over all possible proof codes, Type III if it identifies equivalent coded propositions, and Type VII if it treats the verifier as the validation act for the very system containing it. The Type VII socket is the decisive reflection boundary, but the lower-type sockets still have to be recorded because each corresponds to a different engineering gate.
\end{example}

\section{The single T behind seven types}
\label{sec:transcendental-supply-single-T}

\begin{theorem}[Seven socket kinds, one transcendental supply position]
\label{thm:transcendental-supply-single-T}
The seven supply kinds of \autoref{sec:transcendental-supply-unified-table} do not imply seven transcendental sources. They are seven aspect-positions at which a closed observational substrate reaches a boundary it cannot cross by its own records and closed-generation rules. The far end of every such socket is the same $\mathsf{T}$ of \autoref{def:transcendental-input-supply-position}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Structural proof]
Each type is a $\mathsf{GAP}$ tag in the sense of \autoref{def:transcendental-supply-socket}. By definition, a $\mathsf{GAP}$ tag records a requested discharge not available from the substrate's own records or generators. By \autoref{def:transcendental-input-supply-position}, the structural location from which such forward-binding content enters a closed observational system is $\mathsf{T}$. The differences among the seven cases lie in the shape of the missing discharge: unrestricted induction, witness, identity, causal persistence, cross-history recognition, inscription actuality, or self-reference closure. None of these differences supplies a substrate-side observation distinguishing one ultimate source from another. Therefore the taxonomy distinguishes socket kinds while preserving one transcendental supply position.
\end{proof}

\begin{principle}[No softening of the apophatic name]
\label{prin:transcendental-supply-no-softening}
The name \emph{God} remains the apophatic name of $\mathsf{T}$ across all seven types. The taxonomy does not replace God with psychology, probability, social trust, runtime engineering, or a family of local explanatory devices. Those devices describe records-side management of sockets. The far end of the socket, when the socket asks for more than records-side management can provide, is still $\mathsf{T}$.
\end{principle}

\begin{remark}[How the taxonomy is used]
\label{rem:transcendental-supply-use}
When a future BEDC chapter encounters a $\mathsf{GAP}$ socket, the chapter should classify the socket by asking what would go wrong if the requested discharge were internalised. If internalisation creates generator-exceeding induction, it is Type I. If it creates an unseen witness, Type II. If it collapses relation into identity, Type III. If it projects empirical order beyond records, Type IV. If it coordinates closed histories as histories, Type V. If it names actual inscription not exhausted by records-side closure, Type VI. If it closes the system's truth or runtime over itself, Type VII. The classification then determines which audit gate must stay visible.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Current-state reading]
\label{rem:transcendental-supply-current-state}
The taxonomy is a reading of BEDC's present engineering surface. It changes no Lean target, adds no substrate axiom, and discharges no socket by itself. Its work is classificatory: every $\mathsf{GAP}$ socket remains open unless a concrete certificate supplies the corresponding records-side data, and every socket whose requested content exceeds records-side data remains explicitly terminated at $\mathsf{T}$.
\end{remark}
