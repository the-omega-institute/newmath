\chapter{Theory Stability and the TasteGate Framework}
\label{ch:visions-theory-stability-taste-gate}

The 280-plus concrete-instance chapters of \texttt{papers/bedc/parts/}
formalise objects whose mathematical-consensus credit was earned by the
mathematics community over decades or centuries: $\GroupUp$, $\HilbertUp$,
$\CurvatureUp$, $\GelfandDualityUp$, $\DiffGaloisUp$, and the rest. Each
chapter inherits this credit; the BEDC formalisation does not need to
re-test whether $\GroupUp$ is a `real theory', because the question was
settled outside the system.

A new mode opens when codex itself proposes a chapter. An AI-proposed
chapter has no mathematical-consensus credit. It might be combinatorial
padding (a trivial cartesian-product object); it might be a synonym of
an existing chapter under a new name; it might cross the BEDC zero-axiom
boundary; it might be locally self-consistent yet break stability of
the existing horizon when admitted. The taste gate of this chapter
records the discipline by which AI-proposed chapters earn admission.

The discipline reuses the conservativity, no-hidden-input, round-trip,
and source-channel-separation theorems already established in the
ground compiler chapters
(\autoref{ch:ground-compiler-event-flow},
 \autoref{ch:ground-compiler-zeckendorf-safe-channel-encoding},
 \autoref{ch:ground-compiler-source-channel-layer-separation},
 \autoref{ch:ground-compiler-main-theorems-cannot-claims}). It elevates
those compiler-internal invariants into a chapter-level taste gate.

\section{Four obligations}
\label{sec:taste-gate-four-obligations}

A candidate chapter $X$ earns admission to the AI-proposed track when
its taste-gate package supplies four propositional obligations together
with discharging proofs.

\begin{definition}[Conservativity obligation]
\label{def:taste-gate-conservativity}
The conservativity obligation for $X$ asserts that no formula already
provable from the BEDC baseline becomes unprovable when $X$ is admitted,
and no formula previously unprovable becomes provable except via formulas
that explicitly mention $X$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[No-hidden-input obligation]
\label{def:taste-gate-no-hidden-input}
The no-hidden-input obligation for $X$ asserts that every $X$-token is
the recognised image of a finite event flow displayed from $\bzero,\bone$
in the sense of \autoref{ch:ground-compiler-event-flow}. No external
universe, no infinite parameter set, no oracle witness, and no
representation-only metadata enters as a primitive carrier row.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Round-trip obligation]
\label{def:taste-gate-round-trip}
The round-trip obligation for $X$ asserts that the chapter's display
readback composed with reconstruction is the identity on tokens, in the
sense of \autoref{ch:ground-compiler-zeckendorf-safe-channel-encoding}'s
\texttt{flow\_level\_round\_trip}. Display does not lose information.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Layer-separation obligation]
\label{def:taste-gate-layer-separation}
The layer-separation obligation for $X$ asserts that the chapter's
source role, channel role, and display role do not collapse into one
another, in the sense of
\autoref{ch:ground-compiler-source-channel-layer-separation}'s
\texttt{source\_channel\_separation}. The chapter does not paper over
the source/channel/display distinction by treating one as a renaming of
another.
\end{definition}

\section{Lean interface}
\label{sec:taste-gate-lean-interface}

The four obligations live in a single Lean structure:

\begin{verbatim}
structure ChapterTasteGate (X : Type) where
  conservativity_holds : Prop
  conservativity_proof : conservativity_holds
  no_hidden_input_holds : Prop
  no_hidden_input_proof : no_hidden_input_holds
  round_trip_holds : Prop
  round_trip_proof : round_trip_holds
  layer_separation_holds : Prop
  layer_separation_proof : layer_separation_holds
\end{verbatim}
\leandef{BEDC.Meta.TasteGate.ChapterTasteGate}

The reference instance witnesses that the ground compiler's own
no-hidden-input compiler state already satisfies the four obligations,
using \texttt{event\_flow\_conservativity}, \texttt{no\_hidden\_input},
and \texttt{flow\_level\_round\_trip} as discharging proofs. The
layer-separation field is currently a \texttt{True} placeholder; the
\texttt{source\_channel\_separation} witness is wired in once the
ground-compiler prototype layer chapters complete.

\leanchecked{BEDC.Meta.TasteGate.groundCompilerSelfTasteGate}

\section{Relationship to the NameCert obligations}
\label{sec:taste-gate-versus-namecert}

The \emph{naming-certificate} obligation set
(\autoref{ch:core-typed-naming-certificates}) records source, classifier,
pattern, ledger, and extension obligations within a single chapter. The
TasteGate obligations record stability across chapters and across the
ground compilation layer. The two are orthogonal:

\begin{itemize}
\item A chapter satisfying $\NameCert$ alone is internally consistent
      but not necessarily stable when admitted to the global horizon.
\item A chapter satisfying TasteGate alone has cross-horizon stability
      but is not yet a usable BEDC carrier.
\item Human-curated chapters supply $\NameCert$ and inherit TasteGate
      credit from mathematical consensus.
\item AI-proposed chapters must supply both $\NameCert$ and TasteGate.
\end{itemize}

The chapter does not retroactively impose TasteGate on the existing
human-curated horizon. The audit gate distinguishes the two tracks by a
single \texttt{\textbackslash origin\{human|ai\}} field on the
\texttt{closurestatus} block; absence defaults to \texttt{human} and
preserves backward compatibility.

\section{Cannot-claim registry}
\label{sec:taste-gate-cannot-claims}

The chapter does not claim a complete answer to the underlying
philosophical question of what makes a mathematical object `real';
it claims only a machine-checkable filter. The chapter does not claim
that any failure of the four obligations rules a candidate out as
mathematics in some absolute sense; it rules the candidate out of
\emph{this} BEDC horizon under the AI-proposed track. The chapter does
not claim that human-curated chapters whose four obligations have not
been mechanically checked are incorrect; it claims only that they
inherit consensus credit and need not be re-checked at admission time.
The chapter does not claim a conservativity proof for arbitrary chapter
$X$ relative to arbitrary BEDC baseline; the
\texttt{conservativity\_holds} field is per-chapter and the discharging
proof is supplied by the chapter itself.
