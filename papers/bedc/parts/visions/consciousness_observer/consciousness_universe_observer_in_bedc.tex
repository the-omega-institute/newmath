This vision chapter extends the closed-observational-system framework of \autoref{ch:visions-transcendental-input-and-induction} from BEDC's substrate self-description into a records-side phenomenological reading. The object-level target is deliberately small: a unary horizon, a finite displayed ledger of generated $\Hist$ rows, one focus row, local $\Cont$ time-stream rows, selector ledgers, $\mathsf{GAP}$-accounted compression, and witnessed inter-$\Hist$ relation rows. It closes only the finite records-side carrier through which those boundary readings can be stated without hidden supply.

\section{Finite records-side closure target}
\label{sec:consciousness-universe-finite-closure-target}

\begin{definition}[Finite focused multi-Hist ledger]
\label{def:closed-focused-multihist-ledger}
For a horizon $N:\Unary$, a $\mathrm{ClosedFocusedMultiHistLedger}_N$ is a finite records-side packet
$$
\mathcal{L}_N=(I,H,\Theta,F,S,D,G,R,J,Q,\rho,A)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is a finite displayed index ledger for the histories admitted by the packet, not a host-level set of all observers;
\item $H$ assigns to each displayed index row $i$ a generated $\Hist$ row $H(i)$ whose displayed trace route is bounded by the unary horizon $N$;
\item $\Theta$ records, for every displayed index row $i$, a local $\Cont$ chain from $\emp$ to $H(i)$, read as the local time-stream row of $H(i)$;
\item $F$ is a displayed focus-index row of $I$, and it is only an index of reading, not a subject parameter;
\item $S$ records the selector ledger for every visible transition in the displayed traces, including the chosen $\BMark$ row and the corresponding $\Cont$ witness;
\item $D$ records either the identity digest row $D(i)=H(i)$ or a displayed package-token digest row for $H(i)$;
\item $G$ records the $\mathsf{GAP}$ or provenance row attached to every non-identity digest row, including the exactness status that prevents digest equality from being read as source equality without a witness;
\item $R$ is a finite displayed ledger of inter-$\Hist$ relation rows, each with left history, right history, finite evidence, and $\hsame$-stability data;
\item $J$ is a finite displayed ledger of inter-$\Hist$ invariant rows, each stable under componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item $Q$ is a finite displayed ledger of dependence rows between local unary time slots, together with an optional unary maximum-rate row computed only from the displayed dependence witnesses;
\item $\rho$ is the focused self-proxy, consisting of the endpoint and trace of $H(F)$;
\item $A$ is the refusal and audit row recording no subject parameter, no global clock, no hidden chooser, no unledgered compression, no object-level $\mathsf{T}$ field, and no hidden use of choice, quotient soundness, or propositional extensionality in the packet's accepted read.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[Closed means generated, ledgered, and witnessed]
\label{prin:consciousness-universe-closed-means-ledgered}
The packet of \autoref{def:closed-focused-multihist-ledger} is read as a records-side closed carrier because every displayed object row has a generator, every branch has a selector ledger, every compression has a $\mathsf{GAP}$ or provenance row, every inter-history relation has a finite witness, every invariant is classifier-stable, and every boundary pressure remains outside the object layer. The packet contains no carrier for an observer-substance, no global synchronization clock, no global universe object, no physical constant as primitive datum, and no internal $\mathsf{T}$ object.
\end{principle}

\begin{remark}[Boundary readings]
\label{rem:consciousness-universe-boundary-readings}
The finite ledger may support readings about universe, consciousness, self-location, physical invariance, and other minds, but those readings are not themselves extra fields of the packet. $\mathrm{SelfProxy}$ is the focused endpoint and trace of $H(F)$, not phenomenal first-personness as an object. A unary maximum-rate row is a packet invariant, not the empirical value of light speed. A boundary tag for $\mathsf{T}$, when present in surrounding exposition, is not consumed by any theorem of $\mathcal{L}_N$.
\end{remark}

\section{Consciousness as closed observational system}
\label{sec:consciousness-universe-consciousness-as-closed}

\begin{definition}[Consciousness, records-side carrier]
\label{def:consciousness-universe-consciousness-reading}
A \emph{conscious records-side carrier} in a finite focused ledger $\mathcal{L}_N$ is the focused row
$$
\mathrm{ConsciousRecord}(\mathcal{L}_N,F)
  := (H(F),\Theta(F),S(F),\rho)
$$
read through the audit row $A$. It consists of a generated focused $\Hist$, its local $\Cont$ trace, the selector witnesses for its visible transitions, and the endpoint/trace self-proxy. It is a carrier for consciousness readings, not an object-level definition of phenomenal consciousness.
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[The carrier does not add a subject]
\label{prin:consciousness-universe-no-extra-content}
\autoref{def:consciousness-universe-consciousness-reading} uses only the rows already visible in \autoref{def:closed-focused-multihist-ledger}. The focus index selects a local reading surface; it does not create an observer-substance, a hidden first-person carrier, or an identity criterion beyond $\hsame$ and the displayed trace ledger.
\end{principle}

\begin{example}[BEDC contributor as records-side carrier]
\label{ex:consciousness-universe-bedc-author}
A BEDC contributor can be read, on the records side, as a focused history whose visible work consists of consulted text, proof traces, mechanisation checks, and accepted manuscript rows. The production of a \texttt{leanchecked} lemma is represented by a trace extension with a selector ledger and a verification row. The reading does not assert a substrate-internal model of the contributor's first-person life; it records only the focused history surface through which that contribution is public.
\end{example}

\begin{remark}[Relation to the implicit metaphysics chapter]
\label{rem:consciousness-universe-relation-implicit-metaphysics}
\autoref{ch:visions-observer-hist-identity} argues that BEDC's finite kernel identifies an observer with the history that observer has accumulated. \autoref{def:consciousness-universe-consciousness-reading} re-uses exactly that identification. The self-proxy is the focused endpoint and trace, while identity preservation is the $\hsame$-stable continuity of the displayed history. \section{Universe as observation history}
\label{sec:consciousness-universe-universe-as-history}

\begin{definition}[Finite universe-for-the-focus]
\label{def:consciousness-universe-universe-for-observer}
For a finite focused ledger $\mathcal{L}_N$, the \emph{finite universe-for-the-focus} is the records-side family
$$
\mathrm{Universe}_{\mathcal{L}_N}
  := (I,H,\Theta,S,D,G,R,J,Q)
$$
together with the focus row $F$ when a focused read is required. The row $\Theta(i)$ is the displayed local $\Cont$ route whose unary bound is recorded against $N$. Thus the universe-for-the-focus is not a set comprehension over an external domain; it is the finite packet surface whose selector, digest, $\mathsf{GAP}$, relation, invariant, and dependence rows are displayed. It is not the universe itself and not a God-view object; it is the finite slice whose records the packet admits.
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[Phenomenological frame]
\label{prin:consciousness-universe-phenomenological-frame}
The finite universe-for-the-focus is exactly the records-side slice visible through $\mathcal{L}_N$. Anything not generated by the displayed histories, not recorded by selector or relation rows, not preserved by a $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger, and not exposed as a boundary tag is outside this object-level slice. Distinct focused ledgers need not share a world object; cross-perspective alignment remains the ledger-local problem treated in \autoref{ch:visions-inter-hist-locality}.
\end{principle}

\begin{remark}[The frame is structural, not metaphysical]
\label{rem:consciousness-universe-structural-not-metaphysical}
\autoref{prin:consciousness-universe-phenomenological-frame} does not assert that there is no observer-independent reality. It asserts only that the BEDC-side carrier is the finite records-plus-ledger slice. Any pressure beyond that slice appears either as an additional displayed row, as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ or provenance obligation, or as a boundary reading whose far end is not internalized.
\end{remark}

\section{Time as monotonic accumulation}
\label{sec:consciousness-universe-time-as-accumulation}

\begin{definition}[BEDC-internal time]
\label{def:consciousness-universe-bedc-time}
For a focused ledger $\mathcal{L}_N$, the \emph{internal time order} of $H(i)$ is the local $\Cont$ chain $\Theta(i)$ producing $H(i)$ from $\emp$. Equivalently, it is the finite trace order induced by the displayed $\BMark$ selector rows. Internal time is local to a history; it is not a scalar shared by all histories in $I$.
\end{definition}

\begin{principle}[Time is monotonic information accumulation]
\label{prin:consciousness-universe-time-is-accumulation}
For BEDC the object-level notion of "later" is trace extension inside a displayed local ledger. This is the information-conservation invariant of \autoref{prin:transcendental-input-conservation} read as a temporal structure. Reversibility, time symmetry, global simultaneity, or retrocausal phrasing may be studied as additional structures, but they are not fields of $\mathcal{L}_N$ unless they arrive as witnessed extension carriers.
\end{principle}

\begin{example}[Commit history as BEDC-internal time]
\label{ex:consciousness-universe-commit-history-time}
The git commit history of \texttt{newmath} is a project-level analogy for local ledger time: each accepted state is read through accumulated records such as \texttt{leanchecked} lemmas, $\texttt{inductive}$ types, paper content, and audit rows. The analogy is records-side only. It does not supply a global clock for all observers; it illustrates how a local trace can serve as the public time order of one development history.
\end{example}

\begin{remark}[Why time has only one direction in BEDC]
\label{rem:consciousness-universe-time-one-direction}
External physics admits various time-symmetric or time-reversed formulations. BEDC's records-side reading does not exclude such formulations; it observes only that a local ledger is asymmetric because its displayed trace has a generated prefix structure. A global clock or global "now" would require a separate extension carrier and cannot be read from the kernel signature.
\end{remark}

\section{Space as inter-observer coordination}
\label{sec:consciousness-universe-space-as-coordination}

\begin{principle}[No god's-eye coordinate system]
\label{prin:consciousness-universe-no-god-eye}
Two focused histories in $\mathcal{L}_N$ do not share a single observation history or a global time coordinate. The relation "these histories coordinate on an event" is therefore not primitive. It is reconstructed from paired records, $\mathsf{GAP}$ or provenance rows, and relation witnesses. \autoref{ch:visions-inter-hist-locality} works out the operational form of this coherence and treats maximum-rate phenomena as inter-$\Hist$ invariant interfaces with displayed unary-rate rows, not as primitive physical constants.
\end{principle}

\begin{definition}[Inter-system locality cell]
\label{def:consciousness-universe-locality-cell}
For two displayed index rows $i,j$ in $I$ and an event row $e$, the \emph{locality cell} of $e$ across $(i,j)$ is a finite relation row containing a record read from $H(i)$, a record read from $H(j)$, a paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ or provenance acknowledgement, and a stability witness saying that the cell respects componentwise $\hsame$. The cell is the records-side form of coordinated event reading.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[Space as locality-cell geometry]
\label{ex:consciousness-universe-space-as-geometry}
Within $\mathcal{L}_N$, space is the finite relation graph carried by locality cells and inter-$\Hist$ invariant rows. A unary maximum-rate read, when present, is computed from displayed dependence rows and remains an invariant of that packet. It is not the empirical light-speed constant and not a completed spacetime manifold; it is the records-side surface on which such a physical reading may later be certified.
\end{example}

\begin{remark}[Spacetime logic as inter-observer logic]
\label{rem:consciousness-universe-spacetime-logic}
What is usually called "spacetime logic" enters this chapter only as a boundary reading of relation rows, dependence rows, and inter-$\Hist$ invariants. The closed records-side object has no separate spacetime carrier. It has generated histories, witnessed relations, local clocks, and refusal of global synchronization. Any stronger physical geometry belongs to a subsequent $\NameCert$ target.
\end{remark}

\section{Observer identity through history}
\label{sec:consciousness-universe-observer-identity}

\begin{principle}[Identity is history-continuity]
\label{prin:consciousness-universe-identity-is-history-continuity}
The identity of a focused records-side carrier across local time is given by the generated history and its $\hsame$-stable trace. There is no separate "self" inhabiting the chain and persisting through it; the self-proxy is the focused endpoint and trace. \autoref{ch:visions-observer-hist-identity}'s implicit-metaphysics reading is the engineering form of this claim.
\end{principle}

\begin{example}[BEDC project identity]
\label{ex:consciousness-universe-bedc-project-identity}
The BEDC project as a development artefact is identifiable through a connected accumulation of public records and audit rows. The example is a records-side analogy for $\hsame$-stable trace identity. It does not posit a project-substance behind the ledger, and it does not turn repository history into a global observer.
\end{example}

\begin{remark}[Buddhist and Heraclitean correspondences]
\label{rem:consciousness-universe-buddhist-heraclitean}
The history-continuity reading of identity has correspondences in several philosophical traditions: the Buddhist denial of a substantial $\bar{a}tman$; Heraclitus's flux-with-pattern; Hume's bundle theory. The BEDC version keeps only the formal residue of that family: the observer row is a $\Hist$ trace, and sameness is mediated by $\hsame$ and displayed continuity, not by a subject-substance.
\end{remark}
