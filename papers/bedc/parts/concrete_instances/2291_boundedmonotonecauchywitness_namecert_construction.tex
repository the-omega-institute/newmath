\begin{definition}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness carrier]
\label{def:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-carrier}
A $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(S,F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the bounded monotone source row, displaying the generator history, monotone comparison rows, and the finite upper-bound ledger for the source observations;
\item $F$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row extracted from $S$ and read as the regular rational name exposed to completion-facing consumers;
\item $\sigma$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window schedule that selects the inspected source and regular-sequence windows;
\item $\mu$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ tail witness, sending each dyadic tolerance request to a concrete finite tail window;
\item $\lambda$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger containing the dyadic tolerance rows, the source upper-bound rows, and the monotone comparison checks consumed by $\mu$;
\item $I$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ trap row recording the lower observation, upper guard, and tolerance enclosure read before the real seal;
\item $Q$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the displayed regular sequence, selected window, Cauchy witness, dyadic ledger, and located trap have been exposed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the bounded source, regular sequence, stream schedule, Cauchy witness, dyadic ledger, located trap, and real-seal rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect those rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and source certificates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The bounded monotone source row is displayed as
$$
\begin{gathered}
S_{\mathrm{gen}}(i): \text{the } i\text{-th generated source observation},\\
S_{\mathrm{mono}}(i,j): i \leq j \Rightarrow S_{\mathrm{gen}}(i) \leq S_{\mathrm{gen}}(j),\\
S_{\mathrm{ub}}(i): S_{\mathrm{gen}}(i) \leq U
\end{gathered}
$$
for a dyadic upper guard $U$ recorded in $\lambda$. The Cauchy witness is the explicit row
$$
\mu(\varepsilon): \text{a scheduled tail window on which } F \text{ has diameter below } \varepsilon.
$$
The carrier contains no choice-selected limit point, quotient-name equality, host real equality, unrestricted real-history range, ambient completeness theorem, trichotomy row, or open-cover compactness row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp}$ surface consists exactly of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet of \autoref{def:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-carrier};
\item generator range discipline, stating that every source observation read by a consumer is routed through $S_{\mathrm{gen}}$ and the bounded monotone ledgers of $S$;
\item explicit Cauchy witness discipline, stating that every tail request is answered by the row $\mu$ and the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ window $\sigma$;
\item located trap and $\RealUp$ seal handoff, stating that the real-seal row $Q$ is reached only through $F,\sigma,\mu,\lambda,I,H,C,P,N$;
\item ledger exhaustion, stating that all public reads of bounded monotone convergence are exhausted by the displayed generator, witness, classifier transport, package provenance, and local naming rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of the finite packet in \autoref{def:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-carrier}. The packet lists the bounded source row, regular rational row, stream schedule, Cauchy witness, dyadic ledger, located trap, real seal, transports, continuations, package provenance, and local naming row.

Generator range discipline uses the displayed row $S_{\mathrm{gen}}$ and its monotone and upper-bound ledgers. Type I range pressure is discharged by that displayed generator, because no consumer ranges over an unrestricted intended class of real histories.

Explicit Cauchy witness discipline uses $\mu$ and $\sigma$ to answer each tolerance request with a concrete tail window. Type II selection pressure is discharged by this visible Cauchy witness, not by choosing a limit point from nonempty completeness data.

The seal handoff and ledger exhaustion then use $I$, $Q$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$. Type III collapse pressure is discharged by $\RealUp$ classifier transport and componentwise $\hsame$ rows, not by host equality or quotient-name identification.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone Cauchy witness socket separation]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-cauchy-witness-socket-separation}
Let
$$

$$
be an accepted $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$ carrier. The three analysis sockets in bounded monotone convergence are separated inside $W$: Type I range pressure is read only through the displayed generator row $S$, Type II selection pressure is read only through the explicit Cauchy witness $\mu$ with the scheduled windows $\sigma$, and Type III collapse pressure is read only through $\RealUp$ classifier transport and the componentwise rows in $H$ and $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the range site, inspect the bounded monotone source row. The generator $S_{\mathrm{gen}}$, monotone ledger $S_{\mathrm{mono}}$, and upper-bound ledger $S_{\mathrm{ub}}$ are carrier coordinates, so source reads have a displayed finite route before any regular-sequence or real-seal row is consumed.

For the selection site, inspect the Cauchy witness row. A tolerance request is routed to $\mu(\varepsilon)$ and then through the scheduled window $\sigma(\mu(\varepsilon))$, so the tail witness is present as data rather than selected from an existential completion statement.

For the collapse site, compare accepted packets only through the transported regular sequence, located trap, and real-seal classifier rows. The $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ routes preserve the displayed rows, while the $\RealUp$ seal is reached through classifier transport rather than host equality.
\end{proof}
