\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicBinarySearchUp$}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\DyadicBinarySearchUp$ packet records finite bisection search over located dyadic intervals before any Real-and-Completion consumer receives a bracket. It sits on the dyadic arithmetic ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite bisection route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-interval-bisection-namecert}, the located interval surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the stream-window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a bounded controller for interval refinement; it does not decide global trichotomy, select an infinite limit, identify quotient real names, or invoke ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Dyadic binary search carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-binary-search-carrier}
A $\DyadicBinarySearchUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(I,T,K,M,L,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the initial $\LocatedIntervalUp$ row, containing the left endpoint, right endpoint, dyadic radius, and locatedness data from which the search begins.
\item $T$ is the finite test row. It records the decidable inspected predicate or sign comparison available at each displayed midpoint, but only on the finite rows opened by the packet.
\item $K$ is the unary depth row, bounding the number of bisection decisions made by the controller.
\item $M$ is the midpoint row, computed through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ arithmetic from the current retained interval at every depth below $K$.
\item $L$ is the retained-half ledger, recording for each stage whether the left or right dyadic half is kept and which located interval row results.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family through which the inspected endpoint, midpoint, and retained-half data are exposed to downstream consumers.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the same finite stream windows and dyadic midpoint ledger.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ bracket seal handed off only after the retained interval, stream windows, and regular rational readback have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval, test, depth, midpoint, retained-half, stream-window, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of every consumer request through
$$
I,T,K\longmapsto M,L\longmapsto W\longmapsto R\longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicBinarySearchUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\LocatedIntervalBisectionUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicBinarySearchUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for real trichotomy, quotient real equality, countable choice, a selected infinite refinement, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic binary search NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-binary-search-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicBinarySearchUp$ carrier $B=(I,T,K,M,L,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicBinarySearchUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed twelve-row packet;
\item initial located-interval exposure for $I$ and finite test exposure for $T$;
\item unary depth coverage for $K$ and midpoint arithmetic exactness for $M$ through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$;
\item retained-half ledger exactness for $L$ through the $\LocatedIntervalBisectionUp$ route;
\item stream-window coverage for $W$, regular rational readback for $R$, and $\RealUp$ bracket non-escape for $E$;
\item componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for exactly the rows recorded in $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No dyadic binary-search NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $B=(I,T,K,M,L,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes the initial located interval, finite test row, unary depth, midpoint ledger, retained-half ledger, stream windows, regular readback, Real bracket seal, and the structural rows.

The numerical part is finite. The row $K$ bounds the number of midpoint computations, while $M$ records each midpoint through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ arithmetic and $L$ records the retained half through the $\LocatedIntervalBisectionUp$ route.

The observation part is also finite. The test row $T$ can inspect only the displayed midpoint and endpoint data, and the window row $W$ is the only stream surface available to the readback row $R$.

Finally $E$ is downstream of $R$. The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same route, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic binary search bisection step]
\label{thm:dyadic-binary-search-bisection-step}
Let $B=(I,T,K,M,L,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\DyadicBinarySearchUp$ carrier. For every displayed stage strictly below the unary depth $K$, the next retained interval is obtained by the finite route
$$
I,T,M\longmapsto L .
$$
The retained interval is one of the two dyadic halves certified by the $\LocatedIntervalBisectionUp$ row, and its midpoint computation is the one displayed by the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by finite induction on the displayed prefix of $K$. In the base stage, the current interval is the initial row $I$, and the midpoint row $M$ computes its dyadic midpoint from the endpoints already displayed in $I$.

For the step stage, assume the retained interval has been obtained from the previous prefix through $L$. The next midpoint is again read from $M$, and the finite test row $T$ supplies the only branch datum permitted at that midpoint.

Apply the $\LocatedIntervalBisectionUp$ retained-half row recorded in $L$. It stores exactly one of the two dyadic halves, with the corresponding located interval data, and componentwise transport in $H$ preserves that row under the displayed classifier.

The induction therefore produces no extra analytic coordinate. Each stage is obtained by repeating the same finite midpoint, test, and retained-half route below the bound $K$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic binary search Real bracket handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-binary-search-real-bracket-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\DyadicBinarySearchUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\LocatedIntervalUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \LocatedIntervalBisectionUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
Concretely, the consumer must read $I,T,K,M,L,W,R,H,C,P,N$ before the bracket seal $E$ is exposed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a consumer request for the bracket seal. Carrier projection shows that the only terminal Real-facing coordinate is $E$, and the continuation row $C$ routes access to $E$ through the displayed packet.

The route first reads the initial located interval $I$, the finite test row $T$, and the unary depth $K$. The midpoint ledger $M$ and retained-half ledger $L$ then provide the bounded bisection data through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and $\LocatedIntervalBisectionUp$.

Next the request passes through the stream windows $W$ and the regular rational readback $R$. These rows are the only observation and readback surfaces connected to the terminal bracket.

The structural rows $H,P,N$ bind the same route under transport, provenance, and local naming. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected limits, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, the handoff exports none of those principles.
\end{proof}
