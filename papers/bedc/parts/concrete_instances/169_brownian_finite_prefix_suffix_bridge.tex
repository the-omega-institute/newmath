\begin{theorem}[Brownian finite-prefix carrier restriction]
\label{thm:brownian-finite-prefix-carrier-restriction}
Let
$$
\mathcal{B}=(M,C,T,I,N,P,\rho,\lambda)
$$
be an admitted $\BrownianUp$ process packet in the sense of
\autoref{def:brownian-bhist-process-packet}. Suppose the displayed unary time
row $T$ has length $n+k$, and choose the initial finite subledger $T_{\leq n}$
containing exactly the first $n$ adjacent time faces. Let $I_{\leq n}$,
$N_{\leq n}$, $P_{\leq n}$, $\rho_{\leq n}$, and $\lambda_{\leq n}$ be the
subrows of the adjacent-step ledger, normal-step display, path-continuity
display, provenance row, and finite $\Cont/\hsame$ ledger whose displayed
faces lie entirely over $T_{\leq n}$. Let $M_{\leq n}$ be the finite-prefix
restriction of the consumed $\NameCert_{\MartingaleUp}$ row $M$, and keep the
same consumed $\NameCert_{\ContinuousMapUp}$ path row $C$.

Then
$$
\mathcal{B}_{\leq n}=(M_{\leq n},C,T_{\leq n},I_{\leq n},N_{\leq n},P_{\leq n},\rho_{\leq n},\lambda_{\leq n})
$$
is again an admitted $\BrownianUp$ process packet. In particular, restricting
the Brownian path-step carrier to the first $n$ displayed time steps preserves
the carrier boundary and introduces no host probability-space row, host
stochastic-process row, or host path-equality row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BrownianUp.BrownianStepContinuityClassifier\_finite\_prefix\_carrier\_restriction}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the Brownian carrier of
\autoref{def:brownian-bhist-process-packet}. The admitted packet is a finite
displayed tuple whose public rows are exactly $M,C,T,I,N,P,\rho,\lambda$.
The time row is a finite $\UnaryHistory$ row, so its initial subledger
$T_{\leq n}$ is again a finite displayed unary time row. The rows
$I_{\leq n}$ and $N_{\leq n}$ are obtained by retaining precisely the adjacent
faces of $I$ and $N$ whose endpoints remain in $T_{\leq n}$; no boundary face
crossing from the prefix into the omitted tail is retained.

For the martingale dependency, apply
\autoref{thm:martingale-finite-prefix-restriction} to $M$. It gives the
accepted finite-prefix martingale packet $M_{\leq n}$ over the same displayed
initial horizon. Thus all adaptedness, conditional-expectation, filtration,
provenance, and finite $\Cont$ rows read through the Brownian martingale input
remain licensed by $\NameCert_{\MartingaleUp}$ after the time-prefix
restriction.

For the Gaussian-step face, use
\autoref{thm:brownian-gaussian-step-ledger-coverage}. Every public adjacent
Gaussian-step consumer reads only the unary time row, the adjacent-step ledger,
the normal-step display, the provenance row, the finite transport ledger, and
the corresponding $\UnaryHistory$ and $\Cont/\hsame$ witnesses. The restriction
keeps exactly those witnesses for the retained adjacent faces, so the
Gaussian-step surface over $T_{\leq n}$ is still displayed inside the finite
packet.

For the path-continuity face, use
\autoref{thm:brownian-path-continuity-ledger-exactness}. Every public
path-continuity row is projected from the consumed $\NameCert_{\ContinuousMapUp}$
row $C$ together with the displayed continuity row $P$ and the finite ledger
$\lambda$. The restricted row $P_{\leq n}$ contains the same modulus and
certificate witnesses that the original packet displayed on the retained time
faces, and $\lambda_{\leq n}$ contains exactly the transport entries needed by
those retained witnesses. Since $C$ is unchanged, the continuous-map dependency
surface still supplies the path-continuity rows read by the prefix packet.

It remains to repack the provenance and exactness boundary. The rows
$\rho_{\leq n}$ and $\lambda_{\leq n}$ are finite subledgers of the original
shared $\Pkg$ provenance and $\Cont/\hsame$ ledger, restricted to the retained
martingale, time-step, Gaussian, and path-continuity faces. By
\autoref{thm:brownian-ledger-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:brownian-dependency-ledger-obligation-exactness}, every public
row of the restricted tuple is either one of its displayed coordinates or a
row already scoped by $M_{\leq n}$ or $C$. Finally
\autoref{thm:brownian-namecert-obligation-surface} identifies these displayed
carrier, dependency, classifier, and ledger rows as the public
$\NameCert_{\BrownianUp}$ obligation surface. Therefore
$\mathcal{B}_{\leq n}$ satisfies the Brownian carrier definition with no
additional host-level data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Brownian finite-suffix carrier restriction]
\label{thm:brownian-finite-suffix-carrier-restriction}
Let
$$
\mathcal{B}=(M,C,T,I,N,P,\rho,\lambda)
$$
be an admitted $\BrownianUp$ process packet in the sense of
\autoref{def:brownian-bhist-process-packet}. Suppose the displayed unary time
row $T$ has length $n+k$. Let $T_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$ be the finite
$k$-step unary row obtained by retaining the adjacent faces from the boundary
endpoint at time $n$ through the endpoint at time $n+k$ and then relabelling
that boundary endpoint as the initial endpoint. Let
$$
I_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}},\quad
N_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}},\quad
P_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}},\quad
\rho_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}},\quad
\lambda_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}
$$
be the corresponding finite subrows of the adjacent-step ledger,
normal-step display, path-continuity display, provenance row, and
$\Cont/\hsame$ ledger whose displayed faces lie entirely in that closed
suffix interval, with each time index translated by $j\mapsto j-n$. Let
$M_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$ be the same translated suffix of the consumed
$\NameCert_{\MartingaleUp}$ row, retaining exactly the endpoint rows and
conditional-expectation faces with indices $n\leq j\leq n+k$. Let
$C_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$ be the path-continuity subrow read from the
consumed $\NameCert_{\ContinuousMapUp}$ row $C$ on the retained path segment,
with the time-source boundary reset to the displayed endpoint at time $n$.

Then
$$
\mathcal{B}_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}
=
(M_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}},
 C_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}},
 T_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}},
 I_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}},
 N_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}},
 P_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}},
 \rho_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}},
 \lambda_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}})
$$
is again an admitted $\BrownianUp$ process packet. Thus the Brownian path-step
carrier is closed under finite suffix restriction from any displayed boundary
endpoint, and the restricted carrier introduces no host probability-space row,
no host stochastic-process row, and no host path-equality row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier of \autoref{def:brownian-bhist-process-packet}. An admitted
Brownian packet is a finite displayed tuple, and every public Brownian row is
one of $M,C,T,I,N,P,\rho,\lambda$ or is read through the two consumed
dependency certificates. Taking the interval from the boundary endpoint $n$ to
$n+k$ therefore selects a finite subtuple of already displayed rows. The
translation $j\mapsto j-n$ changes only the displayed position names in the
finite unary spine; it does not create a further time object.

For the martingale dependency, read $M$ through the concrete
$\MartingaleUp$ packet surface. By
\autoref{def:martingale-bhist-adapted-sequence-packet}, the martingale carrier
is a finite tuple of endpoint rows, adjacent conditional-expectation rows,
tower-boundary rows, provenance, and finite $\Cont$ transport. The suffix row
$M_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$ retains precisely those endpoint rows and adjacent
faces whose indices lie in the displayed interval $[n,n+k]$, with the endpoint
at $n$ serving as the shifted initial value. Each retained successor face is
one of the conditional-expectation faces already accepted in $M$, and
\autoref{thm:martingale-namecert-obligation-surface} identifies these retained
finite rows as the public $\NameCert_{\MartingaleUp}$ surface. Hence the
martingale input needed by the shifted Brownian packet is still licensed by the
consumed martingale certificate.

For the Gaussian-step surface, apply
\autoref{thm:brownian-gaussian-step-ledger-coverage}. It says that every public
adjacent Gaussian-step read uses only the unary time row, the adjacent-step
ledger, the normal-step display, the provenance row, the finite transport
ledger, and the corresponding $\UnaryHistory$ and $\Cont/\hsame$ witnesses.
The rows $T_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$,
$I_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$, $N_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$,
$\rho_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$, and
$\lambda_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$ keep exactly those witnesses for the retained
adjacent faces. No step crossing the cut between $n-1$ and $n$ is retained, so
the shifted packet has a closed $k$-step Gaussian ledger over its reset time
boundary.

For the path-continuity surface, use
\autoref{thm:brownian-path-continuity-ledger-exactness}. Every public
path-continuity row of a Brownian packet is projected from the consumed
$\NameCert_{\ContinuousMapUp}$ row together with the displayed continuity row
$P$ and the finite ledger $\lambda$. The row
$C_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$ is the retained continuous-map certificate read on
the same path segment, while $P_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$ and
$\lambda_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$ keep exactly the modulus, continuity, and
transport entries whose source and target faces lie in the suffix interval.
Resetting the source boundary to the endpoint at time $n$ is therefore a
ledger relabelling inside the displayed segment, not a host path equality.

It remains to repack the exact public boundary. By
\autoref{thm:brownian-martingale-continuousmap-dependency-surface}, the shifted
martingale and path rows are still read only through the two dependency
certificates, and the time, Gaussian-step, continuity, provenance, and
transport rows remain displayed packet coordinates. By
\autoref{thm:brownian-ledger-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:brownian-dependency-ledger-obligation-exactness}, every public row
of the shifted tuple is either one of its displayed coordinates or a row scoped
by the shifted martingale dependency or the shifted continuous-map dependency.
Finally \autoref{thm:brownian-namecert-obligation-surface} identifies exactly
such a displayed carrier, dependency, classifier, and finite-ledger package as
the $\NameCert_{\BrownianUp}$ obligation surface. Therefore
$\mathcal{B}_{[n,n+k]}^{\mathsf{sh}}$ is an admitted Brownian process packet,
with no added host-level probability, stochastic-process, or path-equality
data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Brownian finite prefix suffix bridge boundary]
\label{thm:brownian-finite-prefix-suffix-bridge-boundary}
The finite-prefix restriction of
\autoref{thm:brownian-finite-prefix-carrier-restriction} and the finite-suffix
restriction of \autoref{thm:brownian-finite-suffix-carrier-restriction} form
the explicit $\BrownianUp$ bridge boundary over the consumed
$\MartingaleUp$ and $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows, the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$
and $\UnaryHistory$ rows, the shared $\Pkg$ provenance, the finite $\Cont$
transport, and the $\hsame$ classifier rows. A downstream bridge read of the
Brownian packet therefore factors through these finite prefix and suffix
surfaces, and no host probability-space row, stochastic-process row, or path
equality is added to the public boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The prefix theorem identifies the Brownian carrier after an initial finite
time cut. Its retained rows are the consumed martingale prefix, the unchanged
continuous-map dependency, the initial $\UnaryHistory$ time row, the retained
Gaussian-step and continuity displays, the shared $\Pkg$ provenance, and the
finite $\Cont/\hsame$ transport ledger.

The suffix theorem identifies the same carrier after a finite shifted suffix
cut. It resets the displayed boundary endpoint by relabelling the finite
unary spine and retains only those martingale, continuous-map, Gaussian,
provenance, and transport faces whose source and target lie in the closed
suffix interval.

Together these two restrictions cover the bridge-facing boundary: every
consumer read of a Brownian finite segment is either a prefix read from the
initial endpoint or a suffix read from a displayed boundary endpoint. The
dependency reads are still mediated by $\NameCert_{\MartingaleUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\ContinuousMapUp}$, while the remaining rows are exactly the
displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\UnaryHistory$, $\Pkg$, $\Cont$, and $\hsame$
rows. Thus the bridge boundary is finite and visible, with no ambient
stochastic object beyond the displayed packet.
\end{proof}
