Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\CompactMetricCompletionUp$ packet records the finite route by which a complete totally bounded metric completion is exposed as a compact-facing certificate. It reads the finite completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallyboundedcompletion-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter convergence surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-uniform-space-namecert}, the finite-net ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, the compact certificate surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite NameCert surface for complete totally bounded metric completions; it is not open-cover compactness, a quotient completion, a selected limit, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Compact metric completion carrier]\label{def:compact-metric-completion-carrier}
A $\CompactMetricCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(T,U,B,K,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp$ row. It records the source metric carrier, finite-net completion route, completion embedding, separated equality handoff, and total-bounded completion ledger consumed by the compact-facing certificate.
\item $U$ is the $\CompleteUniformSpaceUp$ row. It stores the finite entourage, Cauchy-filter basis, convergence boundary, readback, and seal data through which completeness is read.
\item $B$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net ledger. For each displayed positive tolerance it records the center bundle, membership rows, center-carrier rows, distance witnesses, refinement rows, and rational bound ledger.
\item $K$ is the $\CompactUp$ export row. It exposes compactness only as a downstream finite-net completion read, not as an independent open-cover or host-topological compactness object.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the finite-net completion row $T$, complete uniform-space row $U$, total-bounded ledger $B$, and compact export row $K$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the completion route, uniform-space convergence route, total-bounded ledger, compact export, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite-net tolerance request through total-bounded completion, complete uniform-space convergence, compact export, and the Real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactMetricCompletionUp$, $\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp$, $\CompleteUniformSpaceUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompactUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactMetricCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $T,U,B,K,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, quotient representatives, selected limits, countable-choice schedules, host real equality, or an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric completion NameCert obligations]\label{thm:compact-metric-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactMetricCompletionUp$ carrier $M=(T,U,B,K,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactMetricCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed total-bounded completion route, complete uniform-space convergence row, total-bounded finite-net ledger, compact export, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item finite-net completion handoff, requiring every compact-facing tolerance request to read the $\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp$ row $T$ together with the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ ledger $B$ before any compact export is available;
\item complete uniform-space seal, requiring every completion convergence read to pass through the $\CompleteUniformSpaceUp$ row $U$ and then through the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$;
\item compact export, requiring the $\CompactUp$ row $K$ to be downstream of $T,U,B,E$ and to expose no independent open-cover, quotient, selected-limit, countable-choice, host-equality, or ambient-completeness datum;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the same ordered route under componentwise sameness and displayed continuation replay;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $P$ and $N$ to name only the displayed rows and to export no principle absent from them.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:compact-metric-completion-carrier}. The projected rows are exactly the total-bounded completion route $T$, complete uniform-space route $U$, finite-net ledger $B$, compact export $K$, Real seal $E$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The finite-net completion handoff reads \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallyboundedcompletion-namecert} together with \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}. The row $T$ supplies the completion-facing route, while $B$ supplies the tolerance-indexed centers, membership rows, distance witnesses, and refinement ledger consumed by that route.

The complete uniform-space seal reads \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-uniform-space-namecert} before it reaches \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Thus the convergence request is replayed through $U$ and only then sealed by $E$.

The compact export reads \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert} as a downstream consumer of the same route. Since $K$ is placed after $T,U,B,E$, it cannot supply an open-cover compactness object or a host-level compactness theorem independently of the displayed finite-net completion path.

Transport and replay stability are precisely the roles of $H$ and $C$. The provenance and naming rows $P,N$ name only this packet, so the excluded principles have no carrier coordinate, replay edge, or ledger row from which to escape.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric completion finite-net seal]\label{thm:compact-metric-completion-finite-net-seal}
Let $M=(T,U,B,K,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CompactMetricCompletionUp$ carrier. Every $\CompactUp$ read exported by $M$ factors through the finite-net completion route
$$
B \longmapsto T \longmapsto U \longmapsto E \longmapsto K \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently a compact-facing consumer receives total-bounded finite nets, their completion handoff, complete uniform-space convergence, the Real seal, compact export, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming before it can read compactness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a $\CompactUp$ read of $M$. By \autoref{def:compact-metric-completion-carrier}, the only compact coordinate is $K$, and the definition places it downstream of the finite-net ledger $B$, the total-bounded completion route $T$, the complete uniform-space row $U$, and the Real seal $E$.

The first part of the route is fixed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallyboundedcompletion-namecert}. A tolerance request reads finite centers, membership rows, distance witnesses, and refinements through $B$, and the completion-facing handoff then passes through $T$ rather than through a quotient completion or selected limit.

The second part of the route is fixed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-uniform-space-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Completion convergence is read through $U$, and the terminal Real-facing datum is sealed by $E$ before the compact export is exposed.

Finally, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert} is reached only at $K$. The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the same dependency order, so every exported compact read factors through the displayed finite-net completion route.
\end{proof}
