Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis and Troelstra--van Dalen constructive metamathematics, the $\DedekindCauchySealUp$ packet records the finite bidirectional seal between located Dedekind-cut observations and regular Cauchy-name observations. It sits after the located-cut surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dedekindcut-namecert}, the comparison boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dedekind-cauchy-comparison-namecert}, the regular rational source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the stream-window source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic arithmetic core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names only the finite seal surface by which the two representations can be read against one another; it does not construct $\RealUp$ from cuts and does not assert ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[DedekindCauchySeal carrier]
\label{def:dedekind-cauchy-seal-carrier}
A $\DedekindCauchySealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(D,R,W,T,F,G,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DedekindCutUp$ row, carrying the located lower and upper rational observations that may be read by a seal consumer.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row, carrying the regular rational Cauchy name used for finite tolerance requests.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family through which the row $R$ is exposed.
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the radii, rational comparison budgets, and finite refinement rows used on both sides of the seal.
\item $F$ is the forward seal row that regularizes a displayed located-cut observation into a finite regular Cauchy window against $T$.
\item $G$ is the reverse seal row that reads a displayed regular Cauchy window back as located cut data against $T$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ boundary row reached only after $D,R,W,T,F,G$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the cut, regular-name, stream-window, dyadic, forward, reverse, Real-boundary, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay route for rereading the same finite rows under an accepted consumer motion.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DedekindCauchySealUp$, $\DedekindCutUp$, $\DedekindCauchyComparisonUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DedekindCauchySealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a Dedekind construction of $\RealUp$, ambient completeness, quotient stream equality, real trichotomy, countable choice, selected representatives, or host real equality.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[DedekindCauchySeal classifier]
\label{def:dedekind-cauchy-seal-classifier}
The $\DedekindCauchySealUp$ classifier accepts exactly those finite reads of the carrier $S=(D,R,W,T,F,G,E,H,C,P,N)$ that proceed through one of the following displayed routes:
\begin{enumerate}
\item a cut-to-name route reading $D$, then the tolerance ledger $T$, then the forward seal row $F$, then the stream windows $W$ and regular row $R$;
\item a name-to-cut route reading $R$ through $W$, then the tolerance ledger $T$, then the reverse seal row $G$, then the located-cut row $D$;
\item a Real-boundary route reading $D,R,W,T,F,G$ before the terminal row $E$.
\end{enumerate}
All accepted classifier answers are transported by $H$, replayed by $C$, and recorded by $P,N$. The classifier rejects any request whose answer would require data outside the displayed $\BHist$ packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DedekindCauchySeal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dedekind-cauchy-seal-namecert-obligations}
The seed $\NameCert_{\DedekindCauchySealUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:dedekind-cauchy-seal-carrier} consists exactly of carrier exposure for $S$, classifier exposure through \autoref{def:dedekind-cauchy-seal-classifier}, forward regularization through $F$, reverse cut readback through $G$, dyadic budget control through $T$, stream-window exposure through $W$, terminal $\RealUp$ boundary exposure through $E$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the displayed $\BHist$ packet $S=(D,R,W,T,F,G,E,H,C,P,N)$. The analytic rows are exactly the $\DedekindCutUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, forward seal, reverse seal, and $\RealUp$ boundary rows named in the carrier.

The classifier is finite by \autoref{def:dedekind-cauchy-seal-classifier}. Each accepted read first chooses one of the displayed routes, and each route is exhausted by the stored rows $D,R,W,T,F,G,E$.

The structural rows then close the local naming surface. The $\hsame$ row transports each displayed component, the $\Cont$ row replays the same finite route, and the $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert$ rows record the public packet. Since no excluded principle appears as a coordinate of $S$, the listed obligations exhaust the seal surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Forward regularization through the seal]
\label{thm:dedekind-cauchy-seal-forward-regularization}
Every accepted $\DedekindCauchySealUp$ packet $S=(D,R,W,T,F,G,E,H,C,P,N)$ exposes a finite forward route from located Dedekind-cut observations to regular Cauchy-name observations:
$$
D\longmapsto T\longmapsto F\longmapsto W\longmapsto R .
$$
The route uses only displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DedekindCutUp$, and $\RealUp$ seal rows, and it exports no quotient stream equality, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, or host real equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dedekind-cauchy-seal-carrier}. A cut-facing request can read located rational observations only from $D$ and can refine them only through the dyadic ledger $T$.

The forward seal row $F$ is stored after those two rows. It therefore has access only to the displayed cut observations and tolerance budget, and its output is forced into the stream-window family $W$ before the regular rational row $R$ is read.

Transport and replay do not add analytic data. The rows $H$ and $C$ preserve the same route, while $P$ and $N$ record that the route is the public $\DedekindCauchySealUp$ certificate. The missing quotient, representative, choice, and host-equality data cannot be recovered from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reverse cut readback through the seal]
\label{thm:dedekind-cauchy-seal-reverse-cut-readback}
Every accepted $\DedekindCauchySealUp$ packet $S=(D,R,W,T,F,G,E,H,C,P,N)$ exposes a finite reverse route from regular Cauchy-name observations to located Dedekind-cut observations:
$$
R\longmapsto W\longmapsto T\longmapsto G\longmapsto D .
$$
The route reads the regular rational row only through displayed $\StreamNameUp$ windows, uses the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger as its comparison budget, and returns only the displayed $\DedekindCutUp$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a name-facing request. The carrier makes the regular rational row $R$ visible only through the finite windows $W$, so the request cannot inspect an infinite stream or a quotient stream object.

The dyadic ledger $T$ supplies the finite comparison radii for the reverse read. The reverse seal row $G$ then reads the same windowed name data back into the located-cut row $D$ and has no coordinate from which to choose representatives or host equalities.

Finally apply the structural rows. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay keep the consumer on $R,W,T,G,D$, while $\Pkg$ provenance and the local $\NameCert$ row bind the route to the same public packet.
\end{proof}
