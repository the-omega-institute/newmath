It consumes the terminal real-name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the graph and pointwise-modulus rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the compact interval locator of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-interval-locator-namecert}, the finite attainment route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-extremevalue-namecert}, and the derivative metric quotient rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-derivative-namecert}. The packet names a stationary-point certificate over displayed interval, endpoint, continuity, attainment, derivative, and zero-seal rows; it is not a host mean-value theorem, a classical differentiability principle, quotient real equality, open-cover compactness, or a choice-selected interior point.

\begin{definition}[Rolle carrier]
\label{def:rolle-carrier}
A $\RolleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(I,E,F,V,D,W,Z,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the compact interval source row, exposing the located endpoint names, interval locator, finite windows, and compact-interval boundary data used by the theorem.
\item $E$ is the endpoint-equality row, recording that the two endpoint value seals for the displayed map are classifier-equal through the same $\RealUp$ route.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ row naming the real-valued map on the compact interval source.
\item $V$ is the $\ExtremeValueUp$ witness row, carrying the finite attainment route for the displayed continuous map on the compact interval data.
\item $D$ is the $\DerivativeUp$ metric quotient row, recording the inspected interior derivative quotient route rather than a host differentiability predicate.
\item $W$ is the interior witness row, binding the stationary-point read to a displayed interior interval cell and excluding endpoint-only witnesses.
\item $Z$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ zero-seal row for the derivative quotient named by $D$ at the interior witness named by $W$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact interval source, endpoint equality, continuous-map row, extreme-value witness, derivative quotient, interior witness, zero seal, replay rows, provenance rows, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from interval inspection to map windows, from endpoint equality to the extreme-value comparison route, from the attainment row to the interior witness, and from the derivative quotient route to the zero seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RolleUp$, $\CompactIntervalLocatorUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\ExtremeValueUp$, $\DerivativeUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RolleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for host real equality, open-cover compactness, arbitrary point choice, ambient differentiability, a full mean-value theorem, quotient real equality, or detached $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Rolle NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:rolle-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RolleUp$ carrier
$$
R=(I,E,F,V,D,W,Z,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RolleUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item compact interval source admission through $I$;
\item endpoint equality for the two displayed endpoint value seals through $E$;
\item continuous real-valued map admission through $F$;
\item extreme-value witness routing through $V$;
\item derivative metric quotient and interior zero-seal routing through $D,W,Z$.
\end{enumerate}
The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only these five rows. No Rolle obligation is stored outside the displayed carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:rolle-carrier}. The source row $I$ supplies the compact interval data, and the endpoint row $E$ supplies the equality of the two displayed endpoint value seals.

The map and attainment rows are then fixed. The row $F$ is the only continuous-map surface, and $V$ is the only extreme-value witness route attached to that map and interval source.

Finally inspect the derivative-facing rows. The quotient row $D$, the interior witness row $W$, and the zero-seal row $Z$ form the only derivative-zero route in the packet.

The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same data. Since the carrier has no additional coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rolle extreme-value stationary point]
\label{thm:rolle-extreme-value-stationary-point}
Every stationary witness exported by an accepted $\RolleUp$ carrier factors through the extreme-value row $V$ together with the endpoint-equality row $E$. The witness is therefore read as an interior stationary certificate produced from displayed attainment and equal-endpoint rows, not as a choice-selected point or an ambient compactness conclusion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $R=(I,E,F,V,D,W,Z,H,C,P,N)$. The compact interval source and continuous-map row expose the domain and graph data before any stationary read is available.

Read the endpoint equality $E$ and the extreme-value row $V$. These are the only rows connecting the two endpoint value seals to the attained-value comparison route.

The interior witness $W$ is downstream of that comparison. Transport and replay through $H$ and $C$ preserve the same row inventory, while $P$ and $N$ add only provenance and local naming.

Thus any exported stationary witness passes through the endpoint-equality and extreme-value rows before the derivative-zero seal can be displayed. The packet contains no separate selected point or host compactness conclusion.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rolle derivative-zero seal boundary]
\label{thm:rolle-derivative-zero-seal-boundary}
The derivative-zero claim exported by an accepted $\RolleUp$ carrier is only the displayed $\RealUp$ zero seal $Z$ over the derivative quotient route $D$ at the interior witness $W$. It does not assert host differentiability, a general mean-value theorem, quotient real equality, or an ambient equality of completed real functions.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the derivative-facing part of \autoref{def:rolle-carrier}. The packet contains a derivative metric quotient row $D$, an interior witness row $W$, and a terminal zero-seal row $Z$.

The derivative chapter supplies the quotient route, while the real chapter supplies the meaning of the displayed zero seal. Neither row stores a host differentiability predicate or an equality of completed functions.

The endpoint and extreme-value rows explain why this zero seal is exported for the Rolle packet, but they do not enlarge it into a mean-value theorem. The structural rows only transport and replay the displayed route.

Consequently the exported derivative-zero statement remains a $\RealUp$ seal over $D$ and $W$, with no hidden host differentiability or ambient real-equality content.
\end{proof}
