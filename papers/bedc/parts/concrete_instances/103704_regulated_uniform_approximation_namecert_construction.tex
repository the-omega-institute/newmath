\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegulatedUniformApproximationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regulated-uniform-approximation-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\RegulatedUniformApproximationUp$ packet separates the finite step-schedule data used to approximate a regulated function uniformly from the later integration interface. It sits between the regulated-function surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regulatedrealfunction-namecert}, the dyadic step-function rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicstepfunction-namecert}, the uniform-limit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformlimit-namecert}, the closed interval domain of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-unit-interval-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Its role is to expose a finite approximation route, not to assert integration, host function-space equality, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Regulated uniform approximation carrier]
\label{def:regulated-uniform-approximation-carrier}
A $\RegulatedUniformApproximationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(I,A,T,U,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\ClosedUnitIntervalUp$ domain row, including the interval endpoints and order data consumed by the approximation schedule;
\item $A$ is the finite $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$ step ledger, recording the displayed step partitions, carried values, and dyadic tolerances;
\item $T$ is the uniform tail schedule, naming the finite threshold and tolerance comparisons used to read the approximation as a uniform route;
\item $U$ is the $\UniformLimitUp$ handoff row that consumes $A$ and $T$ before any later regulated-function or integration-facing consumer sees the packet;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row read through the regular rational windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback data attached to the displayed step values;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the interval, step ledger, uniform schedule, uniform-limit route, real seal, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes from a domain request through the selected step row, the uniform schedule, the handoff row, and the real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegulatedUniformApproximationUp$, $\RegulatedRealFunctionUp$, $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$, $\UniformLimitUp$, $\ClosedUnitIntervalUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegulatedUniformApproximationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a Darboux sum, Riemann sum, regulated integral, quotient of functions, open-cover compactness proof, selected real representative, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regulated uniform approximation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regulated-uniform-approximation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegulatedUniformApproximationUp$ packet $R=(I,A,T,U,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegulatedUniformApproximationUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed interval, step ledger, uniform schedule, handoff route, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item step-ledger exactness, requiring every approximation read to use the $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$ rows in $A$ over the $\ClosedUnitIntervalUp$ row $I$;
\item uniform approximation stability, requiring the tail schedule $T$ to control all later step reads at the displayed tolerance;
\item $\UniformLimitUp$ handoff, requiring every regulated-function or integration-facing consumer to factor through $U$ before reaching the real seal row $E$;
\item real-seal non-escape, requiring every terminal read to pass through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and $\RealUp$ seal data displayed by $E$.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside those five rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:regulated-uniform-approximation-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes only the rows $I,A,T,U,E,H,C,P,N$.

The step-ledger row is governed by $I$ and $A$. A domain read first enters the closed interval row and then selects one of the displayed dyadic step-function rows; there is no host function value outside this finite ledger.

The uniform side is governed by $T$ and $U$. The tail schedule records the common tolerance route, and the uniform-limit handoff consumes that schedule before any downstream consumer reaches $E$.

The remaining rows transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since no coordinate stores integration, function quotients, compactness, selected representatives, or ambient completeness, no NameCert obligation can export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regulated uniform approximation step-ledger exactness]
\label{thm:regulated-uniform-approximation-step-ledger-exactness}
Every approximation read of an accepted $\RegulatedUniformApproximationUp$ packet factors through the $\ClosedUnitIntervalUp$ row $I$ and one displayed $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$ row in the ledger $A$. The read obtains its partition, carried value, and dyadic tolerance data from that finite row, not from a host function-space representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier definition. The only approximation coordinate is $A$, and it is scoped by the interval row $I$.

The continuation row $C$ sends a domain request to $I$ before selecting a step row in $A$. Componentwise transport in $H$ preserves the same interval and selected step row under classifier comparison.

Thus an approximation read has no route around $I$ and $A$. Any value or tolerance not displayed by the selected dyadic step-function row is outside the accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regulated uniform approximation stability]
\label{thm:regulated-uniform-approximation-uniform-stability}
If two accepted reads of a $\RegulatedUniformApproximationUp$ packet use step rows past the threshold named by the uniform tail schedule $T$, then their comparison is routed through the same displayed tolerance schedule and the same $\UniformLimitUp$ handoff row $U$. The stability claim is therefore a finite schedule claim over $A,T,U$, not an ambient completeness claim about all real-valued functions.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the schedule row $T$. It is the only coordinate that names common thresholds for the step ledger $A$.

The handoff row $U$ consumes exactly that schedule as a uniform-limit route. Therefore any comparison of sufficiently late displayed steps must be replayed through $T$ and then through $U$.

The carrier omits ambient function-space limits and completeness data. Stability is consequently confined to the displayed schedule and its uniform-limit handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regulated uniform approximation UniformLimit handoff]
\label{thm:regulated-uniform-approximation-uniform-limit-handoff}
Every later $\RegulatedRealFunctionUp$ consumer, and every later integration-facing consumer that reads this packet through a regulated-function surface, must factor through
$$
I,A,T \longmapsto U \longmapsto E .
$$
The handoff supplies the closed interval row, dyadic step ledger, uniform schedule, $\UniformLimitUp$ route, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal, but it supplies no Darboux, Riemann, or regulated-integral row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use step-ledger exactness to place every approximation read at $I$ and $A$. Use uniform approximation stability to place every sufficiently late comparison at $T$ and $U$.

The carrier then exposes $E$ only after the handoff through $U$. Structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve that same route under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Because no integration coordinate occurs in $R=(I,A,T,U,E,H,C,P,N)$, downstream integration chapters may consume this handoff as input data but cannot read integration as part of the approximation packet itself.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regulated uniform approximation real-seal nonescape]
\label{thm:regulated-uniform-approximation-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read of an accepted $\RegulatedUniformApproximationUp$ packet reaches the terminal seal only through the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback data attached to $E$ after the interval, step-ledger, uniform schedule, and $\UniformLimitUp$ rows have been read. It exports no selected real representative, quotient function equality, countable-choice schedule, integration row, open-cover compactness row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The only terminal real-facing coordinate is $E$, and its provenance row records the regular rational readback and real seal dependency rather than a host real value.

The preceding theorems force every read of $E$ to pass through $I,A,T$ and $U$. The structural rows do not add analytic content; they transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet.

Therefore any route requiring selected representatives, quotient equality, choice, integration, compactness, or ambient completeness has no coordinate in the accepted carrier and is rejected at the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
