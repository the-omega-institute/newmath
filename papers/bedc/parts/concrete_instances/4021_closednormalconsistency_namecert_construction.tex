\begin{definition}[Closed-normal consistency packet]
\label{def:closed-normal-consistency-packet}
A $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$ packet is a finite $\BHist$ carrier
$$
Q=(T,F,S,K,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the empty-context MetaCIC typing row for a closed proof candidate;
\item $F$ is the internal false-type row named by the displayed typing certificate;
\item $S$ is the beta-strong normality row used by the closed-normal theorem;
\item $K$ is the checked closed-normal consistency row naming $\mathsf{BEDC.MetaCIC.closed\_normal\_consistency\_assembly}$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the typing, false-type, beta-strong, and theorem rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads those rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over MetaCIC typing, closedness, beta-strong normality, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host consistency, closed subject reduction, hidden normalization transport, or an ambient logical shortcut.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Closed-normal consistency classifier]
\label{def:closed-normal-consistency-classifier}
The classifier for $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$ compares accepted packets only through the visible rows $T,F,S,K,H,C,P,N$. Two packets are classifier-equivalent exactly when the empty-context typing row, false row, beta-strong normality row, checked closed-normal theorem row, componentwise transports, displayed routes, package provenance, and local naming row agree through the displayed $\hsame$ data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-normal consistency normal-fragment boundary]
\label{thm:closed-normal-consistency-normal-fragment-boundary}
Every accepted $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$ packet exposes a positive consistency statement only for the beta-strong normal fragment. A consumer may read $T,F,S,K,H,C,P,N$ and conclude that a closed beta-strong proof candidate typed at the internal false row is impossible through the checked closed-normal row $K$. The packet does not assert that an arbitrary closed proof candidate reaches such a normal row while preserving its displayed type.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-normal-consistency-packet}. Its theorem coordinate $K$ names the closed-normal assembly row, and the coordinates $T,F,S$ provide exactly the displayed typing, false-type, and beta-strong inputs read by that row.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ keep the same finite evidence under componentwise transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming. They do not add a reduction sequence or a type-preservation theorem.

Thus the consumer obtains the beta-strong normal contradiction and nothing outside that fragment. Host consistency, closed subject reduction, hidden normalization transport, and ambient logical shortcuts are absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-normal consistency subject-reduction frontier]
\label{thm:closed-normal-consistency-subject-reduction-frontier}
The $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$ packet is separate from the subject-reduction frontier recorded by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert}. Agreement on $T,F,S,K,H,C,P,N$ does not supply the four MetaCIC subject-reduction discharge rows, a closed subject-reduction theorem, or a transport from arbitrary closed proofs to beta-strong normal inhabitants of the same displayed false type.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the classifier in \autoref{def:closed-normal-consistency-classifier}. It fixes the positive normal-fragment packet by the rows $T,F,S,K,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinates for the beta-substitution, application-argument, lambda-domain, or Pi-domain discharge rows.

Compare this with the sibling obstruction packet in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert}. That chapter names the missing subject-reduction surface as a different finite packet, so it cannot be recovered by reclassifying the closed-normal rows.

Consequently the closed-normal packet cannot transport arbitrary closed proofs to the normal fragment. It keeps the positive theorem and the subject-reduction frontier as distinct BEDC objects.
\end{proof}
