\begin{definition}[Package map-boundary carrier]
\label{def:package-map-boundary-carrier}
A $\PackageMapBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(T,G,R,S,X,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the theorem-map row, recording theorem sites, definition sites, proof mode, claim strength, dependency surface, and not-claimed readings;
\item $G$ is the gap-map row, recording typed gaps, requested supply, socket type, gate, status, route, and forbidden internalization;
\item $R$ is the tradition-map row, recording inherited comparison, preserved structure, rejected surplus, audit status, and package route;
\item $S$ is the scientific-map row, recording records carrier, invariant carrier, empirical ledger, prediction gap, bridge status, claim status, and failure surface;
\item $X$ is the axis-boundary row, recording that theorem strength, gap status, tradition comparison, scientific bridge status, theory closure, formal verification, and bridge status are distinct audit coordinates;
\item $A$ is the audit-route row, recording the philosophy-package carrier, claim registry layer, socket report, standard bridge audit packet, and host primitive leakage route consulted by the export;

\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for the four map rows, the axis-boundary row, and the audit-route row;

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PackageMapBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a prose-only map, a theorem entry detached from its gap boundary, a tradition comparison detached from rejected surplus, a scientific bridge detached from failure surface, or a collapse of map status into formal verification or bridge status.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Package map-boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:package-map-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:package-map-boundary-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\PackageMapBoundaryUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of theorem-map admission through $T$, gap-map admission through $G$, tradition-map admission through $R$, scientific-map admission through $S$, axis-boundary admission through $X$, audit routing through $A$, transport and replay through $H,C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet $M=(T,G,R,S,X,A,H,C,P,N)$. The four map rows expose theorem, gap, tradition, and scientific readings before any package export is named.

Use the package and registry siblings next. The package carrier supplies the package-level row, the claim registry layer controls claim admission, and the socket report records the diagnostic socket through which a gap or map entry is inspected.

The bridge and host-leak siblings account for the export boundary. The standard bridge audit packet displays bridge fields, while the host primitive leakage packet refuses host equality, quotient import, choice, and semantic primitives.

Finally read $X,A,H,C,P,N$. These rows separate the status axes, route the audit, transport and replay only the displayed packet, source it, and name it locally. Since no other coordinate is available, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Package map-boundary non-escape]
\label{thm:package-map-boundary-nonescape}
Every accepted philosophy-package audit reading through $\PackageMapBoundaryUp$ factors through
$$
T,G,R,S,X,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
If any one of the theorem-map, gap-map, tradition-map, scientific-map, or axis-boundary rows is absent, the packet cannot export a philosophy-package audit reading.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:package-map-boundary-namecert-obligations}. The theorem-map, gap-map, tradition-map, scientific-map, and axis-boundary rows are admitted separately before the audit route and local name can be consumed.

The package audit route then reads the same finite carrier through $A,H,C,P,N$. These rows preserve the displayed rows and cannot manufacture a missing map family or a missing axis boundary.

Thus an accepted reading remains on $T,G,R,S,X,A,H,C,P,N$. A route missing any of $T,G,R,S,X$ may still be prose about the package, but it is not a $\PackageMapBoundaryUp$ audit export.
\end{proof}
