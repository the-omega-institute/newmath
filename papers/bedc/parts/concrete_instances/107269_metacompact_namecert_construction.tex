\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetacompactUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metacompact-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Morita and Kelley general topology, the $\MetacompactUp$ packet records the point-finite refinement surface between paracompact locally finite refinement, Moore-space developments, and star-refinement cover ledgers. It sits after the paracompact cover surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-paracompact-namecert}, beside the developable-neighbourhood rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-moore-space-namecert}, and before the uniform star-refinement handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-star-refinement-uniform-cover-namecert}. The packet names only a displayed open-cover request, a point-finite open refinement, and the ledgers that certify coverage and point incidence; it does not choose arbitrary subcovers, infer local finiteness, or import compactness.

\begin{definition}[Metacompact open-cover carrier]
\label{def:metacompact-open-cover-carrier}
A $\MetacompactUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(T,O,R,Q,I,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ source row containing the open predicates, point observations, and neighbourhood classifier used by the packet.
\item $O$ is the open-cover request row, listing the displayed cover cells and the pointwise coverage ledger for $T$.
\item $R$ is the open-refinement row, listing the displayed refinement cells and their containment witnesses back into cells of $O$.
\item $Q$ is the point-finite incidence row, recording for each displayed point observation the finite list of refinement cells that contain it.
\item $I$ is the coverage-and-incidence exactness row, requiring every point covered by $O$ to be covered by a refinement cell and requiring every incidence read to be one of the finite entries in $Q$.
\item $S$ is the sibling handoff row to the $\ParacompactUp$, $\MooreSpaceUp$, and $\StarRefinementUniformCoverUp$ cover ledgers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $T,O,R,Q,I,S,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an open-cover request through refinement, point-finite incidence, sibling handoff, provenance, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetacompactUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\ParacompactUp$, $\MooreSpaceUp$, $\StarRefinementUniformCoverUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetacompactUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for selected subcovers, locally finite partitions, metric development extraction, uniform-star generation, or ambient compactness.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Metacompact classifier]
\label{def:metacompact-classifier}
The $\MetacompactUp$ classifier accepts exactly those displayed packets $M=(T,O,R,Q,I,S,H,C,P,N)$ whose refinement row $R$ is open in $T$, whose containment ledger maps each refinement cell into a displayed cell of $O$, whose coverage ledger in $I$ covers the same point observations as $O$, and whose incidence row $Q$ displays a finite list of refinement cells at each point observation. A classifier read is rejected if it asks for a subcover selection, a locally finite refinement, a Moore development, a uniform star refinement, or a compactness principle not present among $T,O,R,Q,I,S,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Metacompact carrier admission]
\label{thm:metacompact-carrier-admission}
For every accepted $\MetacompactUp$ packet $M=(T,O,R,Q,I,S,H,C,P,N)$, carrier admission exposes exactly the topology source, open-cover request, open-refinement row, point-finite incidence row, coverage-and-incidence exactness row, sibling handoff row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows of \autoref{def:metacompact-open-cover-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier definition. The tuple lists $T,O,R,Q,I,S,H,C,P,N$ and no further coordinate.

The classifier of \autoref{def:metacompact-classifier} accepts only packets that read refinement, containment, coverage, point-finite incidence, sibling handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through those coordinates. Therefore carrier admission is the displayed finite packet itself.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metacompact point-finite refinement exactness]
\label{thm:metacompact-point-finite-refinement-exactness}
For every accepted $\MetacompactUp$ packet $M=(T,O,R,Q,I,S,H,C,P,N)$, the row $R$ is an open refinement of the request row $O$, and the row $Q$ is a point-finite incidence ledger for $R$. Every displayed point covered by $O$ is covered by at least one row of $R$, and every refinement cell containing that point appears in the finite list recorded by $Q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the refinement part of the classifier. It requires the cells of $R$ to be open in $T$ and to carry containment witnesses into the displayed cover cells of $O$.

Use the exactness row $I$ next. It links coverage by $O$ to coverage by $R$ and restricts incidence reads to the finite lists displayed in $Q$.

Thus the point-finite refinement is not inferred from a choice principle. It is stored as the finite incidence ledger attached to the accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metacompact sibling cover handoff]
\label{thm:metacompact-sibling-cover-handoff}
Every cover-refinement consumer of an accepted $\MetacompactUp$ packet factors through the sibling handoff row $S$ before citing $\ParacompactUp$, $\MooreSpaceUp$, or $\StarRefinementUniformCoverUp$. The handoff may compare locally finite, development, and star-refinement ledgers with the point-finite row $Q$, but it cannot promote the metacompact packet to any of those stronger cover principles.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the sibling coordinate $S$ in the carrier. It is the only row that mentions the paracompact, Moore-space, and star-refinement neighbours.

The refinement and incidence rows remain $R,Q,I$. Comparing them with sibling cover ledgers does not add locally finite partitions, countable developments, or uniform-star generation to the packet.

Therefore any downstream citation of the neighbours passes through $S$ as a handoff and returns to the same point-finite refinement surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metacompact transport stability]
\label{thm:metacompact-transport-stability}
Transporting an accepted $\MetacompactUp$ read along $H$ preserves the topology source $T$, open-cover request $O$, refinement row $R$, point-finite incidence row $Q$, exactness row $I$, sibling handoff $S$, replay row $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$. The transported classifier still reads only the displayed point-finite refinement packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The row $H$ is componentwise by definition of the carrier. It transports the same open-cover request, refinement cells, point-finite incidence lists, exactness row, and sibling handoff.

The classifier has no alternate coordinate for cover selection or compactness. After transport, the accepted read is still checked by the same rows $T,O,R,Q,I,S,C,P,N$ and by no hidden cover operation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metacompact nonescape]
\label{thm:metacompact-nonescape}
No consumer of an accepted $\MetacompactUp$ packet can read selected subcovers, locally finite partitions of unity, Moore developments, uniform star-refinement generation, ambient compactness, or standard bridge data from the packet. All consumer reads are confined to $T,O,R,Q,I,S,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. The only cover-theoretic data are the open-cover request $O$, open refinement $R$, point-finite incidence row $Q$, exactness row $I$, and sibling handoff $S$.

Transport, replay, provenance, and naming do not add cover choices. They preserve and expose the same finite rows already present in the carrier.

The excluded stronger objects have no projection from $T,O,R,Q,I,S,H,C,P,N$, so a consumer cannot extract them from the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
