Following Arzela-Ascoli and Bishop-style compact metric analysis, the $\FiniteLipschitzFamilyUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface for a listed family of continuous maps controlled by one displayed Lipschitz row. It sits after the compact source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the target metric of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the displayed map rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the shared bound ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lipschitz-constant-namecert}, and the tolerance handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}. The packet gives compact-uniform and Arzela--Ascoli consumers a finite family row; it does not assert ambient function-space compactness, selected subsequences, open-cover compactness, quotient function equality, or a hidden modulus.

\begin{definition}[Finite Lipschitz-family carrier]
\label{def:finite-lipschitz-family-carrier}
A $\FiniteLipschitzFamilyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(X,Y,I,M,L,V,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row whose finite metric windows supply all source-distance reads.
\item $Y$ is the target $\MetricUp$ row whose distance reads are used by the displayed map values.
\item $I$ is the finite index row naming exactly the family members available for inspection.
\item $M$ is the finite list of $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows, one row for each index in $I$.
\item $L$ is the shared $\LipschitzConstantUp$ row, recording one displayed bound used by every listed member.
\item $V$ is the $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff row that receives the compact source, target metric, displayed maps, and common Lipschitz bound as tolerance data.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,Y,I,M,L,V$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route from compact metric source data through the listed continuous maps and the shared Lipschitz row to the uniform-modulus handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteLipschitzFamilyUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\LipschitzConstantUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteLipschitzFamilyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an ambient function-space topology, selected subsequence, open-cover compactness proof, quotient equality of functions, or non-displayed modulus.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lipschitz-family NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-lipschitz-family-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteLipschitzFamilyUp$ carrier $F=(X,Y,I,M,L,V,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteLipschitzFamilyUp}$ surface consists exactly of compact-source exposure, target-metric exposure, finite-index admission, displayed continuous-map admission, shared-bound exactness, $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. No obligation is stored in an ambient function-space object or in an unlisted modulus selector.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $F=(X,Y,I,M,L,V,H,C,P,N)$. The rows $X,Y,I,M,L,V$ are the only mathematical coordinates available to the family read.

The finite family part is therefore explicit. The index row $I$ lists the inspectable members, $M$ attaches each member to a displayed $\ContinuousMapUp$ row, and $L$ supplies one common $\LipschitzConstantUp$ row for all of them.

The handoff part is also explicit. The row $V$ receives only the displayed compact source, target metric, map, and bound data, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet.

Because the excluded objects do not occur among the carrier coordinates, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Lipschitz-family modulus handoff]
\label{thm:finite-lipschitz-family-modulus-handoff}
Every compact-uniform or Arzela--Ascoli consumer of an accepted $\FiniteLipschitzFamilyUp$ carrier receives the route
$$
\CompactMetricUp,\MetricUp,\ContinuousMapUp,\LipschitzConstantUp
\longmapsto
\UniformModulusUp
\longmapsto
\hsame,\Cont,\Pkg,\NameCert .
$$
The common Lipschitz row is exported only as a finite-family tolerance handoff. It exports no ambient function-space compactness, selected subsequence, open-cover compactness, quotient function equality, or hidden modulus.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the rows $X$ and $Y$. They provide the source and target metric reads that any later compact-uniform consumer may inspect.

Next read the family rows $I$ and $M$ together with the shared bound row $L$. Since every listed member is paired with a displayed $\ContinuousMapUp$ row and the same $\LipschitzConstantUp$ row, the consumer never ranges over a host function space.

Finally export $V,H,C,P,N$. The $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff receives the compact metric, target metric, listed maps, and common bound, while the structural rows preserve exactly that route for downstream replay.

The absent coordinates rule out selected subsequences, open-cover compactness, quotient function equality, and hidden moduli, so the handoff is precisely the finite BEDC family packet.
\end{proof}
