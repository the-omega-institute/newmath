\begin{theorem}[GroundCompiler event-flow audit route NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:groundcompiler-event-flow-audit-route-namecert-obligations}

Transport through $H$, route replay through $K$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ preserve those five rows as finite BEDC data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet $R=(E,C,L,G,A,B,H,K,P,N)$. The rows $E,C,L,G,A,B$ are the only public data rows, and they display the event-flow input, legal channel, lossless readback, generated recognizer, certificate gate, and non-evidence boundary.

The rows $H,K,P,N$ transport, replay, locate, and name the same packet. Since none of them introduces another public data coordinate, the five-row obligation surface exhausts the local naming certificate.

\label{thm:groundcompiler-event-flow-audit-route-channel-lossless-boundary}

\begin{proof}
