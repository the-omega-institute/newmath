\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealOneUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-one-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\RealOneUp$ packet records the unit real as the visible rational-one route into the real-name surface, not as a host primitive. It sits beside the zero-real packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realzero-namecert} and reads its source through the rational embedding route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalembedding-namecert}, the rational carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, the constant window of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the stationary regular-rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic unit ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is the finite unit-real route consumed by later real algebra, order, metric, and completion-facing chapters.

\begin{definition}[RealOne carrier]
\label{def:real-one-carrier}
A $\RealOneUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(q,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $q$ is the $\RatUp$ unit seed row, read as the rational numerator-denominator packet for $1$.
\item $S$ is the constant $\StreamNameUp$ window whose every admitted finite request reads the same seed $q$.
\item $R$ is the stationary $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback attached to the constant unit window.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ unit ledger recording the dyadic endpoint, tolerance, and radius rows used by the unit source.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached after the rational seed, constant window, stationary readback, and dyadic unit ledger have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the rational seed, constant window, stationary readback, dyadic ledger, terminal seal, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay routes from $q$ through $S$, from $S$ through $R$ and $D$, and from those rows into the terminal seal $E$.
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealOneUp$, $\RatUp$, $\RationalEmbeddingUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealOneUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, multiplication laws, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealOne NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-one-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealOneUp$ carrier $O=(q,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealOneUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the rational unit seed, constant StreamName window, stationary RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore unit ledger, terminal RealUp seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item rational embedding route, requiring the unit seed to be read through the $\RatUp$ row and the $\RationalEmbeddingUp$ handoff before any real-facing consumer may inspect it;
\item stationary unit-source route, requiring the seed to be re-read only through the constant window, stationary regular-rational readback, and dyadic unit ledger;
\item terminal RealUp seal handoff, requiring the real-facing row $E$ to be exported only after $q,S,R,D$ and the structural rows have been displayed;
\item ledger nonescape from quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, multiplication laws, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these five rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-one-carrier}. The visible data are the rational unit seed $q$, the constant window $S$, the stationary readback $R$, the dyadic unit ledger $D$, the terminal seal $E$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The rational embedding row is forced before the real seal can be read. The packet exposes $q$ as a $\RatUp$ unit source and sends that source through the $\RationalEmbeddingUp$ handoff rather than through a host real constant.

The stationary source row is then determined by $S,R,D$. Every admitted finite request re-reads the same rational seed, the regular-rational readback is stationary, and the dyadic ledger records the corresponding unit endpoint and tolerance rows.

The terminal seal is reached only through $E$, with transport and replay supplied componentwise by $H$ and $C$. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host equality, selected limits, trichotomy, multiplication laws, or ambient completeness, none of those principles can be exported through the local $\NameCert_{\RealOneUp}$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealOne rational embedding route]
\label{thm:real-one-rational-embedding-route}
Every accepted $\RealOneUp$ packet exposes the rational-one source through the finite route
$$
q \longrightarrow S \longrightarrow R \longrightarrow D \longrightarrow E
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$. A real algebra, order, metric, or completion-facing consumer therefore receives the unit real only after the $\RatUp$, $\RationalEmbeddingUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows have been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $O=(q,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$. The seed $q$ is the rational unit row, so the first public source is the $\RatUp$ carrier read through the rational embedding handoff.

The constant window $S$ makes the route finite at every requested observation. The readback $R$ records the same rational value as a stationary regular-rational row, and $D$ records the dyadic unit ledger attached to that row.

Only after these rows are visible does the terminal seal $E$ become available. Thus any downstream consumer receives the unit real through the displayed rational-to-real route and not through a host-level real constant.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealOne terminal seal nonescape]
\label{thm:real-one-terminal-seal-nonescape}
The terminal $\RealUp$ seal of an accepted $\RealOneUp$ packet exports exactly the displayed unit route $q,S,R,D,H,C,P,N$. It exports no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limit, trichotomy datum, multiplication unit law, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet. The only rows available before the terminal seal are the rational unit seed, the constant window, the stationary readback, the dyadic unit ledger, and the transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The seal $E$ is downstream of those rows and has no independent coordinate carrying a quotient, a host equality proof, a selected limit, an order trichotomy decision, a multiplication law, or a completeness principle. Therefore the real-facing boundary preserves the visible unit route and cannot export any omitted principle.
\end{proof}
