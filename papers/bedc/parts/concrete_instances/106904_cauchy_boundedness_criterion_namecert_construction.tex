\begin{definition}[Cauchy boundedness criterion carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-boundedness-criterion-carrier}
A $\CauchyBoundednessCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(S,B,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the Cauchy source row, naming the regular rational sequence whose finite observations are admitted by the packet.
\item $B$ is the boundedness ledger, recording the displayed initial bound, tail bound, and comparison rows that make the source a $\BoundedRegularCauchyUp$ object.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row used to inspect the source at the requested precision and tail threshold.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the same finite window and boundedness ledger.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, recording the precision budget, bound comparison, and tail estimate consumed by the readback.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row available only after $S,B,W,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the Cauchy source, boundedness ledger, window row, readback row, dyadic tolerance row, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a bounded Cauchy request through $S,B,W,R,D,E,H,P,N$.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyBoundednessCriterionUp}$ row.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,B,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient-stream equality, a selected Real limit, countable choice, trichotomy, a host supremum, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy boundedness criterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-boundedness-criterion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyBoundednessCriterionUp$ carrier
$$
K=(S,B,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyBoundednessCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the Cauchy source row $S$, boundedness ledger $B$, $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row $W$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row $D$, $\RealUp$ seal row $E$, componentwise transport $H$, displayed replay $C$, package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-boundedness-criterion-carrier}. The analytic rows are the source $S$, boundedness ledger $B$, window row $W$, readback row $R$, dyadic tolerance row $D$, and Real seal $E$.

The boundedness obligation is read only from $B$ together with the dyadic row $D$. The ledger records the displayed bound comparisons and tail estimates, while $D$ records the precision budget consumed by those comparisons.

The regular readback is downstream of the same finite window. Thus $R$ is a $\RegSeqRatUp$ row obtained through $W,B,D$, not a host sequence, quotient stream, selected limit, or completeness argument.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for countable choice, trichotomy, host suprema, quotient-stream equality, selected Real limits, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy boundedness criterion Real-completion handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-boundedness-criterion-real-completion-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing completion read exported by an accepted $\CauchyBoundednessCriterionUp$ packet factors through
$$
S \longmapsto B \longmapsto W,D \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently a completion consumer may use the displayed Cauchy source, boundedness ledger, finite $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, terminal $\RealUp$ seal, and local structural rows, but it receives no quotient-stream equality, selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, host supremum, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the displayed source $S$. The source is admissible for the packet only as a Cauchy row whose boundedness information is supplied by the ledger $B$.

The finite observation then passes through $W$ and $D$. The row $W$ exposes the inspected stream window, while $D$ supplies the dyadic tolerance and bound-comparison budget for the same request.

Only after those rows have been displayed does the packet expose $R$ and then $E$. The row $R$ is the regular rational readback, and $E$ is the terminal Real seal served by that readback.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same dependency order under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since none of the excluded objects occurs in the carrier, no completion-facing read can bypass the bounded finite route.
\end{proof}
