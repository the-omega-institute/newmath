\begin{definition}[Cauchy rate lattice carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-rate-lattice-carrier}
A $\CauchyRateLatticeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(A,B,M,J,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two source Cauchy-rate schedule rows whose precision requests, source thresholds, and monotone reuse entries are visible to the packet.
\item $M$ is the meet or shared-bound row. It records the finite threshold cells that both source schedules may use as a common Cauchy window before a downstream read consumes either source.
\item $J$ is the displayed join or dominating-rate row. It records the finite threshold cells that dominate both $A$ and $B$ on the same precision surface.
\item $D$ is the pair of $\CauchyRateDominanceUp$ ledgers witnessing that $M$ is readable from the two sources and that $J$ dominates both source schedules through displayed comparison rows.
\item $S$ is the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window family opened only at requests certified by $A,B,M,J,D$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row produced from the selected windows and the visible dyadic tolerance entries.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after the source schedules, meet row, join row, dominance ledgers, selected windows, and regular readback have all been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two source schedules, meet row, join row, dominance ledgers, selected windows, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through $A,B,M,J,D,S,R,E$ and then through the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyRateLatticeUp$, $\CauchyRateDominanceUp$, $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$, $\UniformCauchyModulusUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRateLatticeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts only packets whose reads pass through these twelve rows. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected host limits, countable choice, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy rate lattice meet-join stability]
\label{thm:cauchy-rate-lattice-meet-join-stability}
Let
$$
L=(A,B,M,J,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CauchyRateLatticeUp$ carrier. Any Real-facing or completion-facing consumer that reads the meet row $M$ or the join row $J$ must factor through the same displayed source pair $A,B$, dominance ledger row $D$, selected-window row $S$, regular readback row $R$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$. Thus the meet and join operations are stable under componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay inside the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a consumer of $M$. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-rate-lattice-namecert-obligations}, the meet row is not an independent tail selector; it is read only after the source pair $A,B$ has been displayed and only through the shared-bound ledger.

Now consider a consumer of $J$. The join row is likewise downstream of the same source pair, and its dominance evidence is stored in $D$. Hence the join read has no route that bypasses $A,B,D$.

The selected-window and readback rows are common to both reads. Since $S$ is opened only after the meet, join, and dominance ledgers are visible, and since $R$ reads only those selected windows, both operations reach the endpoint through the same finite window and readback route.

Transport stability is componentwise. The row $H$ preserves all carrier coordinates together, and $C$ replays only the displayed order from precision request to seal. Therefore both meet and join reads remain stable under the permitted $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ structure.
\end{proof}
