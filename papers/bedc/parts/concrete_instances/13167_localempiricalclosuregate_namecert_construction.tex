\begin{definition}[Local empirical closure gate carrier]
\label{def:local-empirical-closure-gate-carrier}
A $\LocalEmpiricalClosureGateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $F$ is the local fit row certifying whether the model signature and observed signature agree on $S$ under the displayed classifier;
\item $J$ is the local closure judgment row, exposing that the finite inquiry over $S$ has been settled;
\item $O$ is the global openness row, recording that no row of the packet supplies a final induction oracle over all admissible continuations;

\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,F,J,O,L,C,P,N$;

The carrier has no coordinate for unrestricted empirical confirmation, total future-fit quantification, hidden continuation enumeration, host real equality of physical trajectories, or a final induction oracle.

\begin{theorem}[Local empirical closure gate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:local-empirical-closure-gate-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:local-empirical-closure-gate-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\LocalEmpiricalClosureGateUp}$ surface consists exactly of five empirical obligation rows: finite source admission through $S$, local fit settlement through $F$, local closure certification through $J$, global openness retention through $O$, and explicit nonfinality refusal through $L$. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name those five rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier to the empirical rows $S,F,J,O,L$. The finite source row fixes the observed trace and classifier; the local fit row records the model/observation comparison; and the closure-judgment row states that this finite inquiry is settled.

Read the openness and nonfinality rows next. The row $O$ preserves the open-continuation boundary of \autoref{def:global-empirical-openness}, while $L$ records the refused final-fit and oracle-transfer reads.

Finally inspect the structural rows. The transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming data preserve only the displayed rows already present in $G$. They do not add a sixth empirical coordinate, so the five named rows exhaust the NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Local empirical closure gate nonfinality]
\label{thm:local-empirical-closure-gate-nonfinality}
Every accepted $\LocalEmpiricalClosureGateUp$ consumer may read the finite local closure row $J$ only together with the global openness row $O$ and the nonfinality ledger $L$. It cannot derive a final induction oracle, a universal future-fit certificate, or a continuation enumeration from the finite local-closure packet alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the local closure theorem of \autoref{thm:local-closure-not-global-closure}. It separates finite fit settlement over a displayed trace from universal closure over all future observation histories.

Apply the carrier inventory of \autoref{def:local-empirical-closure-gate-carrier}. The only positive settlement row is $J$, and it is paired in the same accepted packet with $O$ and $L$, which record global openness and the refused oracle transfer.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ cannot synthesize missing empirical data; they only preserve, replay, source, and name the displayed packet. Therefore an accepted consumer receives local closure together with its nonfinality ledger and has no route to a final induction oracle.
\end{proof}
