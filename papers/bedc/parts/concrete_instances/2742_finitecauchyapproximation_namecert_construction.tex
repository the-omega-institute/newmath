\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finite-cauchy-approximation-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$ interface isolates the finite observation packet used when a regular rational source is read at one displayed precision before a $\RealUp$ consumer crosses the Cauchy-limit boundary. It records the selected threshold, stream window, dyadic observation ledger, error row, and handoff route as finite $\BHist$ data, so later completion arguments can cite a named approximation surface without importing a selected real value or an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Finite Cauchy approximation carrier]
\label{def:finite-cauchy-approximation-carrier}
A $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(R,n,k,W,D,E,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the source $\RegSeqRatUp$ row whose rational stream is read through an attached $\StreamNameUp$ surface;
\item $n$ is the unary precision row naming the requested finite tolerance level;
\item $k$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row displayed for precision $n$;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window selected at and beyond the threshold row $k$;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger recording the dyadic representatives read from $W$;
\item $E$ is the explicit dyadic error-bound row used to compare the selected observations;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ and $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff row that exposes the finite approximation to a real-facing consumer boundary;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the source, precision row, threshold, window, dyadic ledger, error bound, handoff, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $R$ to the stream window, from $n$ to $k$, from $W$ to $D$ and $E$, and from the finite packet to the handoff row $S$;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$, $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected real values, infinite tails, host real metrics, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Cauchy approximation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-cauchy-approximation-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:finite-cauchy-approximation-carrier} consists exactly of source $\RegSeqRatUp$ admission, unary precision exposure, $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold exactness, finite $\StreamNameUp$ window readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation-ledger exposure, explicit error-bound exposure, $\RealUp$ and $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff routing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed tuple $F$. The rows $R,n,k,W,D,E,S$ provide the source, precision, threshold, window, dyadic ledger, error bound, and real-facing handoff.

The threshold obligation is read from the $\CauchyModulusUp$ surface attached to the regular rational source. The finite window obligation is read from the $\StreamNameUp$ rows selected by that threshold, and the dyadic observation rows are exactly the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ entries recorded in $D$.

The remaining obligations are routing and naming obligations. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport the listed coordinates, replay the finite reads, record the dependency packages, and name the approximation packet without adding quotient stream equality, a selected limit, or an infinite-tail coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Cauchy approximation window soundness]
\label{thm:finite-cauchy-approximation-window-soundness}
For an accepted $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$ carrier, every dyadic observation exported by the finite approximation lies in the displayed window $W$ selected by the threshold row $k$. The error row $E$ is read through the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, and its tolerance is bounded by the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold exactness for the unary precision row $n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted carrier and project the rows $R,n,k,W,D,E$. The $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row $k$ supplies the finite bound attached to the precision row $n$.

Read the stream window through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ surface. Since $W$ is the only window coordinate in the packet, every observation route into $D$ passes through that finite window before any dyadic representative is visible.

The error row is then read from the same dyadic ledger. The nonnegative tolerance rows used by $\DyadicAbsoluteValueUp$ bind the endpoint comparison inside $D$, while the threshold exactness of $\CauchyModulusUp$ supplies the displayed precision bound. No exported observation can bypass $W,D,E$ or appeal to an infinite tail.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite Cauchy approximation refinement stability]
\label{thm:finite-cauchy-approximation-refinement-stability}
Let two accepted $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$ carriers have the same transported $\RegSeqRatUp$ source, unary precision row, $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold, finite $\StreamNameUp$ window, dyadic observation ledger, and explicit error row. Then their exported finite approximations present the same observation ledger and the same error-bound surface to every $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two carriers and read the componentwise transport rows in $H$. The hypothesis identifies the source, precision, threshold, window, dyadic ledger, and error-bound rows after transport.

Use the displayed continuation routes to replay the finite reads in the same order: source to window, precision to threshold, window to dyadic observations, and dyadic observations to the error row. The shared-bound routes supplied by $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ preserve the common threshold surface when a consumer compares this packet with another regular rational source.

The consumer-facing surface is therefore fixed before the handoff row is read. Any $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$, or $\RealUp$ route receives the same $D$ and $E$ rows, together with the same transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming data.
\end{proof}
