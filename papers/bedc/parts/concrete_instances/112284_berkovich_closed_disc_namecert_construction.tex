\begin{definition}[Berkovich closed disc carrier]
\label{def:berkovich-closed-disc-carrier}
A $\BerkovichClosedDiscUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(K,a,\rho,U,Q,E,V,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the local-field source row, exposing only the finite coefficient, valuation, residue, and completeness reads needed by the disc packet.
\item $a$ is the centre row, read through the same local-field source and not as a selected host point outside the displayed packet.
\item $\rho$ is the ultrametric radius ledger, recording the closed bound $|x-a|\leq \rho$ and the finite tolerance comparisons used by downstream consumers.
\item $U$ is the $\UltrametricSpaceUp$ route that keeps radius comparison non-Archimedean and componentwise.
\item $Q$ is the polynomial probe-family row, listing the finite polynomial names and coefficient windows inspected by the current analytic read.
\item $E$ is the bounded multiplicative-seminorm read row, recording the values assigned to the displayed probes and the bound imposed by the closed radius ledger.
\item $V$ is the valuation compatibility row, requiring products, sums, and scalar coefficients to be consumed only through the displayed local-field and polynomial rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,a,\rho,U,Q,E,V$ and for the replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ route from local-field inspection through ultrametric radius comparison, polynomial probe readback, bounded seminorm evaluation, and valuation compatibility.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BerkovichClosedDiscUp$, $\PadicUp$, $\LocalFieldUp$, $\UltrametricSpaceUp$, $\PolynomialUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BerkovichClosedDiscUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $K,a,\rho,U,Q,E,V,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a full Berkovich spectrum, a rigid analytic atlas, an adic point space, a completed structure sheaf, a quotient of analytic functions, or a host compactness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Berkovich closed disc NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:berkovich-closed-disc-namecert-obligations}

Every closed-disc analytic read must expose $K,a,\rho,U,Q,E,V,H,C,P,N$ before it can be consumed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:berkovich-closed-disc-carrier}. The source of coefficients and the centre of the disc are visible only through $K$ and $a$, while the closed-radius constraint is recorded by the ultrametric ledger $\rho$ and route $U$.

The analytic read then reaches only the displayed probe surface. The polynomial row $Q$ supplies the finite family being inspected, and the bounded multiplicative-seminorm row $E$ records values for those probes under the bound enforced by $\rho$.

Valuation compatibility is supplied by $V$, so products, sums, and scalar coefficients remain tied to the local-field and polynomial rows. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet.

Since no further coordinate occurs in the carrier, the certificate cannot export a full spectrum, rigid atlas, adic point space, structure sheaf, analytic-function quotient, or host compactness theorem.
\end{proof}
