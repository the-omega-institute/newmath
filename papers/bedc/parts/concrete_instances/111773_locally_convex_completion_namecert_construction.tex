The packet names completion only through displayed finite rows; it is not a Hahn--Banach theorem, not a selected dual, not a host quotient completion, and not an arbitrary-net completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[LocallyConvexCompletion carrier]
\label{def:locally-convex-completion-carrier}
A $\LocallyConvexCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(X,S,F,U,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$ source row, exposing the vector-space, topological-vector-space, finite seminorm, and convex balanced neighbourhood data accepted by the source.
\item $S$ is the finite seminorm-family ledger read through $\SeminormedSpaceUp$, including the displayed family index, zero, triangle, scalar, induced uniform, and metric handoff rows.
\item $F$ is the Cauchy-filter row for the locally convex uniformity generated by $S$, with its finite base, refinement, and Cauchy-obligation data displayed before completion is requested.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformSpaceCompletionUp$ handoff row, available only after $X,S,F$ have exposed the same local uniform route.
\item $E$ is the completion seal row, recording the accepted locally convex completion name after the Cauchy-filter criterion and uniform-completion handoff have both been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the locally convex source, seminorm-family ledger, Cauchy-filter row, uniform-completion handoff, completion seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion request through $X,S,F,U,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocallyConvexCompletionUp$, $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$, $\SeminormedSpaceUp$, $\FrechetCompletionUp$, $\UniformSpaceCompletionUp$, $\CauchyFilterCompletionCriterionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocallyConvexCompletionUp}$ row.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,S,F,U,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for Hahn--Banach separation, a choice-selected dual, a quotient completion in a host category, arbitrary nets without displayed Cauchy data, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocallyConvexCompletion seminorm-filter obligations]
\label{thm:locally-convex-completion-seminorm-filter-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocallyConvexCompletionUp$ carrier $L=(X,S,F,U,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocallyConvexCompletionUp}$ surface contains exactly the displayed source row $X$, finite seminorm-family ledger $S$, Cauchy-filter row $F$, uniform-completion handoff $U$, completion seal $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local naming $N$. Every completion-facing read must first expose the finite seminorm-family route and then the Cauchy-filter row generated by that family.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:locally-convex-completion-carrier}. Its public rows are precisely $X,S,F,U,E,H,C,P,N$, so any local NameCert read begins with the displayed locally convex source and the finite seminorm-family ledger.

The finite family induction is over the index row carried by $S$. The base read exposes the empty or initial finite family as a displayed uniform-neighbourhood request, and the extension step adds one seminorm row by routing zero, triangle, scalar, induced uniform, and metric obligations through the same $\SeminormedSpaceUp$ ledger.

After the family has been displayed, the Cauchy-filter row $F$ records the completion request against that finite uniform route. The handoff $U$ and seal $E$ are downstream rows, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since the excluded Hahn--Banach, dual-choice, host-quotient, arbitrary-net, and ambient-completeness coordinates are absent from the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:locally-convex-completion-cauchy-seal}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a public read of the completion seal $E$. By \autoref{def:locally-convex-completion-carrier}, the seal is not a primitive host point; it is a row reached after $X,S,F,U$ have been displayed.

Thus every accepted seal factors through the displayed packet. No row exports Hahn--Banach separation, a choice-selected dual, host quotient equality, arbitrary nets without Cauchy data, or ambient completeness.
