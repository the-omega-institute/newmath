\begin{theorem}[KernelCategory scoped endpoint transport surface]
\label{thm:kernel-category-scoped-endpoint-transport-surface}
Let $K=(O,H,I,C,A,U,P,N)$ be an accepted $\KernelCategoryUp$ packet. Every endpoint transport available to a kernel-category consumer is exhausted by the unary object histories in $O$, the continuation-ledger hom histories in $H$, the empty-continuation identity rows in $I$, the displayed $\Cont$ composition rows in $C$, the componentwise $\hsame$ associativity rows in $A$, and the componentwise $\hsame$ unit rows in $U$. Transport can therefore move object endpoints, hom endpoints, identity rows, composite rows, and law rows only by the componentwise $\hsame$ data already listed in $K$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with carrier admission. By \autoref{def:kernel-category-carrier} and \autoref{thm:kernel-category-carrier-admission-obligation}, the only endpoint-bearing rows of $K$ are the unary object rows, continuation-ledger hom rows, identity rows, displayed composites, and law-transport rows.

Use the operation rows next. \autoref{thm:kernel-category-identity-continuation} identifies identities with empty $\Cont$ rows over the transported unary histories, while \autoref{thm:kernel-category-composition-continuation} and \autoref{thm:kernel-category-cont-composition-stability} keep composite endpoints inside the displayed hom and composition ledgers.

It remains to account for law transport. \autoref{thm:kernel-category-associativity-scope}, \autoref{thm:kernel-category-unit-scope}, and \autoref{thm:kernel-category-law-transport-exhaustion} place every associativity or unit comparison in the componentwise $\hsame$ rows $A$ and $U$. The non-escape surface of \autoref{thm:kernel-category-root-nonescape-surface} then removes host endpoints, quotient endpoints, and external arrow equality from the scoped endpoint surface.
\end{proof}
