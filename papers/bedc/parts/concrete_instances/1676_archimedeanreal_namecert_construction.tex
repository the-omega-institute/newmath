\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ArchimedeanRealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-archimedeanreal-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ interface records the finite rational-bound surface for a displayed $\RealUp$ name. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, boundedness is read from explicit rational witnesses and dyadic inspection rows, not from a host supremum, quotient equality of streams, a choice-selected bound, or ambient completeness.

The packet is a $\RealUp$ consumer and therefore reads the displayed real-name seal through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} before exporting rational-bound rows. This sibling edge is substantive: the first coordinate of the Archimedean carrier is the $\RealUp$ name $R$, while $Q,D,S,G,B$ are the bound, inspection, and ledger rows surrounding that real-name seal.

\begin{definition}[ArchimedeanReal carrier]
\label{def:archimedean-real-carrier}
An $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
A=(R,Q,D,S,G,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ name whose rational boundedness is being certified;
\item $Q$ is the $\RatUp$ row naming the exported rational bound;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row that presents the same bound at the dyadic inspection surface consumed by real-name windows;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row through which finite observations of $R$ are read;
\item $G$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row attached to the real name, so that each inspected rational observation has a regular-Cauchy source;
\item $B$ is the finite bound ledger comparing the inspected $G$ observations against $Q$ and $D$ at the requested precision;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the real name, rational bound row, dyadic bound row, stream window, regular-Cauchy handoff, and bound ledger;
\item $C$ stores the $\Cont$ routes that expose the finite real-name windows, dyadic bound checks, regular-Cauchy reads, and rational-bound export;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealUp$, $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanRealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The packet is an Archimedean bound certificate for a displayed real name. It stores no host supremum, no selected least bound, no quotient stream equality, and no ambient completeness object.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.ArchimedeanRealUp.ArchimedeanRealUp}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanReal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:archimedean-real-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanRealUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:archimedean-real-carrier} consists of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly the displayed packet $A$ with a $\RealUp$ name, a $\RatUp$ bound row, a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ bound presentation, a $\StreamNameUp$ inspection window, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, a finite bound ledger, transport rows, continuation rows, package provenance, and local naming data;
\item classifier transport: classification transports the real name, rational bound, dyadic bound presentation, stream window, regular-Cauchy handoff, and bound ledger componentwise through the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes;
\item stability: replacing $R$, $Q$, $D$, $S$, or $G$ by classified rows preserves the same kind of finite rational-bound ledger and exposes the transported bound row;
\item ledger exactness: every accepted bound read is the displayed comparison in $B$ between the inspected regular-Cauchy observations and the rational or dyadic bound rows $Q,D$;
\item rational-bound export: every $\RealUp$ order, locatedness, or completion-facing consumer first receives $Q,D,S,G,B,H,C,P,N$ before it can use the bound.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the tuple in \autoref{def:archimedean-real-carrier}. The real name, rational bound, dyadic presentation, stream window, regular-Cauchy handoff, bound ledger, transport routes, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row are all explicit coordinates.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The $\RealUp$ name transports together with its $\StreamNameUp$ window and $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, while the $\RatUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ bound rows transport the comparison side of the ledger.

The stability and exactness clauses then read only the finite ledger $B$. Since $B$ compares the displayed regular-Cauchy observations with the displayed rational and dyadic bound rows, the exported bound cannot be replaced by a host supremum, a choice-selected bound, quotient stream equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanReal rational-bound witness]
\label{thm:archimedean-real-rational-bound-witness}
Every accepted $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ packet $A=(R,Q,D,S,G,B,H,C,P,N)$ exposes an explicit rational-bound witness for the $\RealUp$ name $R$: the witness is the pair of displayed rows $Q:\RatUp$ and $D:\DyadicRatCoreUp$ together with the finite ledger $B$ comparing all inspected $\RegSeqRatUp$ observations of $R$ against those rows. The witness is available without a host supremum, a choice-selected least bound, quotient stream equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier inventory in \autoref{def:archimedean-real-carrier}. The bound rows $Q$ and $D$ are coordinates of the accepted packet, and the real-name observations are read only through $S$ and $G$.

Apply the ledger-exactness clause of \autoref{thm:archimedean-real-namecert-obligations}. Each finite inspection request is compared against the displayed rational row $Q$ and the dyadic row $D$ by the ledger $B$ before any downstream consumer receives the bound.

Thus the exported witness is exactly the finite pair $(Q,D)$ with its ledger $B$ and provenance routes $H,C,P,N$. No coordinate of the packet is a supremum operator, a selected least bound, a quotient of streams, or a completeness principle, so none of those objects is available to a consumer of the rational-bound witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanReal rational-dyadic bound coherence]
\label{thm:archimedean-real-rat-dyadic-bound-coherence}
For every accepted $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ packet $A=(R,Q,D,S,G,B,H,C,P,N)$, the $\RatUp$ row $Q$ and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row $D$ form one coherent bound witness for the displayed $\RealUp$ name $R$. \end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ArchimedeanRealUp.ArchimedeanRealCarrier\_rat\_dyadic\_bound\_coherence}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:archimedean-real-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes $Q$ and $D$ as named coordinates of a single finite $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ carrier rather than as two independent bound witnesses.

Apply \autoref{thm:archimedean-real-namecert-obligations}. Its ledger-exactness and rational-bound export clauses say that the comparison of inspected $\RegSeqRatUp$ observations against $Q$ and $D$ is mediated by the same ledger $B$, while the exported consumer surface keeps the same $S,G,H,C,P,N$ rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:archimedean-real-rational-bound-witness}. The public witness for the displayed real name is the pair $Q,D$ together with the finite ledger $B$, so a consumer cannot take the $\RatUp$ row without the dyadic presentation or take the dyadic row without the rational row.

Finally use \autoref{thm:archimedean-real-bound-ledger-nonescape}. Since the packet has no host supremum, selected least bound, quotient stream equality, host limit, or ambient completeness coordinate, there is no second bound surface through which a downstream order, locatedness, completion, integer-part, or decimal-prefix consumer could separate the two rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanReal transported bound stability]
\label{thm:archimedean-real-transported-bound-stability}
Let $A=(R,Q,D,S,G,B,H,C,P,N)$ and $A'=(R',Q',D',S',G',B',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ packets. If the real-name row, rational bound row, dyadic bound row, stream window, regular-Cauchy handoff, bound ledger, transport table, continuation table, provenance row, and local naming row are replaced only by rows classified through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes, then the rational-bound witness of \autoref{thm:archimedean-real-rational-bound-witness} is transported unchanged as a witness surface: the exported $\RatUp$ row, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row, and finite ledger comparison remain the same displayed bound rows for the classified $\RealUp$ name.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ArchimedeanRealUp.ArchimedeanRealCarrier\_transported\_bound\_stability}
\begin{proof}
The carrier of \autoref{def:archimedean-real-carrier} stores $Q$, $D$, and $B$ as explicit coordinates, together with the transport table $H$ and continuation table $C$. The NameCert obligations state that classification acts componentwise on exactly these rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:archimedean-real-rational-bound-witness}. The witness is not an external bound; it is the visible pair $Q,D$ with the ledger $B$ comparing inspected $\RegSeqRatUp$ observations against those rows.

After replacing the packet by classified rows, the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes carry the same $Q,D,B$ witness surface to $A'$. Therefore the rational and dyadic bound rows remain stable for the transported $\RealUp$ name.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanReal consumer bound handoff]
\label{thm:archimedean-real-consumer-bound-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ order, locatedness, or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ packet receives the displayed rows $Q,D,S,G,B,H,C,P,N$ before it uses the bound on $R$. In particular, the consumer reads the rational bound through $\RatUp$, the dyadic presentation through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, finite observations through $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the comparison through the ledger $B$ with its transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ArchimedeanRealUp.ArchimedeanRealCarrier\_consumer\_bound\_handoff}
\begin{proof}

The rational-bound witness theorem identifies the witness used by a consumer as $Q,D$ together with the ledger $B$. The NameCert obligations further require the consumer-facing export to preserve the stream window, regular-Cauchy handoff, transport rows, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row.

Thus any order, locatedness, or completion-facing read of the bound must pass through those displayed rows before the bound is used. The handoff is a finite BEDC packet over the visible witness surface.
\end{proof}
