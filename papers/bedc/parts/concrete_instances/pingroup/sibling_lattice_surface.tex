\begin{theorem}[PinGroup Spin-Clifford sibling lattice]
\label{thm:pingroup-spin-clifford-sibling-lattice}
Every public $\PinGroupUp$ reflection-parity read factors through exactly the
consumed $\SpinGroupUp$ inclusion row of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spingroup-namecert} and the consumed
$\CliffordUp$ reflection-product and action rows of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}. The factorization is
recorded by the displayed $\BHist$ carrier rows, componentwise $\hsame$
transports, finite $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local
$\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ support row.

No public reflection-parity consumer may replace this lattice by a host
orthogonal group, a quotient Clifford algebra, a matrix representation, or an
independent Pin carrier. Conversely, a finite packet carrying exactly the
Spin inclusion row, the Clifford reflection-product and action rows, and the
listed BEDC support rows is the same public $\PinGroupUp$ reflection-parity
surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the source of the public parity surface through
\autoref{thm:pingroup-scoped-reflection-parity-source}. It identifies the
visible source rows as the reflection-parity carrier, the inherited Spin
inclusion, the Clifford reflection product, the displayed $\hsame$
transports, the finite $\Cont$ routes, and the $\Pkg$ provenance rows supplied
by the two source certificates.

Next apply the scoped boundary theorem
\autoref{thm:pingroup-scoped-spin-clifford-boundary}. The common boundary is
determined by the Spin boundary row and the Clifford reflection-product row,
with the parity classifier, endpoint transports, ledger entries, and
provenance fixed componentwise.

Finally use
\autoref{thm:pingroup-scoped-consumer-projection-exhaustion}. It exhausts
every public consumer projection by the readback packet, the Spin inclusion,
the Clifford reflection-product and action rows, and the same $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support. The exclusion clauses in
the three cited theorems rule out any host orthogonal group, quotient
Clifford algebra, matrix representation, or additional Pin carrier, so the
public read factors through exactly the stated sibling lattice.
\end{proof}
