\begin{definition}[PinGroup consumer readback packet]
\label{def:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet}
The $\PinGroupUp$ consumer readback packet is the finite public packet obtained
from $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ by retaining exactly the reflection ledger, the
Clifford action row, the Spin boundary row, the $\Pkg$ provenance rows, the
finite $\Cont$ transport rows, and the $\hsame$ witnesses carried by the public
certificate. It contains no host orthogonal group, no external Pin object, and
no row not exported by the displayed $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup consumer readback packet soundness]
\label{thm:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet-sound}
Every accepted public $\PinGroupUp$ consumer row projects to the
$\PinGroupUp$ consumer readback packet of
\autoref{def:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}. It says that the
accepted public surface of $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ is carried by the finite
carrier, classifier, source, transport, witness, and provenance rows listed in
the certificate.

The reflection component is then covered by
\autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-ledger-closure}. That theorem identifies every
reflection read with the displayed reflection ledger and its finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ provenance.

The Spin component is covered by
\autoref{thm:pingroup-spin-boundary-exhaustion}. Hence the remaining public
action read is the Clifford action row already exposed by the certificate
surface, and all transport or identity data are the displayed $\Cont$ and
$\hsame$ rows. These are exactly the rows retained in the readback packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup consumer readback packet exactness]
\label{thm:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet-exact}
Any $\PinGroupUp$ consumer readback packet reconstructs the public
$\PinGroupUp$ Clifford-action consumer surface and exposes no host orthogonal
group or external Pin object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a packet as in
\autoref{def:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet}. Its reflection ledger,
Clifford action row, Spin boundary, $\Pkg$ provenance, finite $\Cont$
transport, and $\hsame$ witnesses are precisely the rows admitted by the
public consumer surface of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-clifford-action-consumer-surface}.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-exhaustion}. The only sources available to
the public Pin certificate are the displayed $\SpinGroupUp$ and $\CliffordUp$
source rows together with their transported histories.

Therefore repacking the packet rows reconstructs the same consumer surface,
while the source-exhaustion and consumer-surface theorems exclude any host
orthogonal group or external Pin object outside those rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup downstream unblock package]
\label{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package}
The carrier definition of $\PinGroupUp$ together with
\autoref{def:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet},
\autoref{thm:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet-sound}, and
\autoref{thm:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet-exact} forms the ten-field
downstream package consumed by later Clifford-family and Spin-family chapters:
carrier, classifier, reflection ledger, Clifford action row, Spin boundary row,
source exhaustion, $\Pkg$ provenance, finite $\Cont$ transport, $\hsame$
witnesses, and exclusion of host orthogonal or external Pin reads.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier are the public $\PinGroupUp$ rows already fixed by
\autoref{thm:pingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}. The reflection ledger,
Clifford action row, and Spin boundary are exactly the three rows retained in
\autoref{def:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet}.

Soundness is supplied by
\autoref{thm:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet-sound}; exact readback and
exclusion of host data are supplied by
\autoref{thm:pingroup-consumer-readback-packet-exact}. The remaining
provenance, transport, and witness fields are the packet's $\Pkg$, $\Cont$,
and $\hsame$ rows.

Thus a later Clifford-family or Spin-family consumer may cite this single
package instead of separately collecting the carrier, source, ledger,
transport, and exclusion rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup full-orthogonal action exactness]
\label{thm:pingroup-full-orthogonal-action-exactness}
The public full-orthogonal action row of $\PinGroupUp$ is exactly the
classifier boundary assembled from the inherited $\SpinGroupUp$ source, the
Clifford reflection generator, the Clifford product ledger, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows of the displayed $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$. It
admits no additional host orthogonal action row outside that boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-ledger-coverage} to locate the inherited
$\SpinGroupUp$ source and the Clifford source inside the finite source ledger.
Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-exactness} to identify the
classifier boundary with the parity carrier induced by those two source
branches.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-exhaustion}. The only public projections
remaining after the carrier read are the listed $\SpinGroupUp$ and
$\CliffordUp$ rows, their $\Cont$ transports, their $\hsame$ comparisons, and
their $\Pkg$ provenance.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package}. Its consumer
package records the reflection ledger, Clifford action row, Spin boundary row,
source exhaustion, transport, witnesses, and provenance as one exported
surface, so the full-orthogonal action row is exactly that classifier boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup reflection-parity boundary]
\label{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-boundary}
Every public $\PinGroupUp$ row factors through exactly one branch of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-parity-carrier}: either the inherited
$\SpinGroupUp$ branch or the Clifford reflection branch. No public row factors
through both branches except by the inherited Spin classification already
recorded in the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PinGroupUp.PinGroupReflectionParityCarrier\_source\_projection\_no\_confusion}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-ledger-coverage} to read the public source
ledger as the two displayed sources and their transported histories. Apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-exactness} to classify each public row
by the reflection-parity carrier.

For a row classified by the inherited Spin branch, the carrier records exactly
the $\SpinGroupUp$ inclusion and its $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows. For a
row classified by the Clifford reflection branch, the carrier records the
reflection generator and Clifford product ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-exhaustion} to rule out a third branch.
Then apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package} to see that the
consumer-visible package exports precisely these two branches and the Spin
overlap already classified by the inherited branch.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup source projection separation]
\label{thm:pingroup-source-projection-no-confusion}
The public source projections of $\PinGroupUp$ to $\SpinGroupUp$ and
$\CliffordUp$ are disjoint except for rows classified by the inherited Spin
branch of \autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-parity-carrier}. Consequently the
public certificate has no third source projection.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-ledger-coverage}. It places every public
source read in the displayed $\SpinGroupUp$ or $\CliffordUp$ source row, with
the advertised $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories and no additional projection field.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-exactness}. The classifier
separates inherited Spin rows from Clifford reflection rows, and it identifies
the only common public classification as the inherited Spin branch already
present in the reflection-parity carrier.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-exhaustion} to exclude any remaining public
source projection. The downstream package of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package} then repacks exactly the two
source projections, their transports, witnesses, and provenance for later
consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[PinGroup scoped primitive binding]
\label{def:pingroup-scoped-primitive-binding}
The scoped primitive binding for $\PinGroupUp$ is the finite dependency package
whose only primitive rows are $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$,
$\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$. The inherited
$\SpinGroupUp$ inclusion row and the $\CliffordUp$ reflection product row are
read only through those two source certificates, and every reflection-parity
carrier, classifier, transport, and provenance row of this chapter is a row in
that package.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup parity scope transport]
\label{thm:pingroup-parity-scope-transport}
Every reflection-parity carrier transport used by $\PinGroupUp$ is exhausted by
the primitive package of
\autoref{def:pingroup-scoped-primitive-binding}. In particular, parity transport
may use only the carried $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories, displayed $\hsame$
comparisons, finite $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the two source
certificate rows from $\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PinGroupUp.PinGroupReflectionParityLedgerSurface\_parity\_scope\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:pingroup-parity-source-scope-package} to identify the parity
rows as the inherited Spin branch, Clifford reflection branch, transport rows,
and provenance rows.

Then apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-scoped-reflection-parity-source}. It repacks
the same rows as the scoped reflection-parity source and excludes host
orthogonal, matrix, quotient, and third-source data. Hence every carrier
transport factors through exactly the primitive binding package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup consumer boundary exhaustion]
\label{thm:pingroup-consumer-boundary-exhaustion}
Every downstream consumer of the scoped $\PinGroupUp$ surface receives only the
two source projections, the reflection-parity classifier, the transport rows,
and the provenance package. No downstream consumer receives a host orthogonal
group, matrix model, quotient Clifford unit, or additional Pin source row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the consumer through
\autoref{thm:pingroup-scoped-consumer-projection-exhaustion}. This gives the
consumer readback packet, reflection-parity classifier, Spin inclusion row,
Clifford reflection product row, transport rows, finite continuation routes,
and provenance rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package}. It identifies
the downstream package with the same finite rows, so the exported consumer
boundary has exactly the two source projections, classifier, transport, and
provenance data stated above.
\end{proof}
