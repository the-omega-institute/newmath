Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\RealIntervalUp$ packet records displayed real interval endpoints and finite enclosure rows for the L10 Real and Completion route. It sits after the located interval surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the rational interval surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}, and the dyadic-to-real spine of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter gives interval-completion and real-enclosure consumers one named endpoint-and-enclosure packet; it is not a host closed interval, selected endpoint representatives, trichotomy, compactness, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[RealInterval carrier]
\label{def:real-interval-carrier}
A $\RealIntervalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(L,U,E,D,W,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the lower endpoint $\RealUp$ row, read through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal discipline.
\item $U$ is the upper endpoint $\RealUp$ row, read through the same finite real-name seal discipline.
\item $E$ is the enclosure and order row, binding the lower endpoint, upper endpoint, rational interval enclosure, and endpoint-order evidence without replacing the endpoints by host representatives.
\item $D$ is the dyadic tolerance row supplied by $\DyadicRatCoreUp$; it records positive precision requests, outward rounding budgets, and finite endpoint comparison tolerances.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row for the two endpoint streams and the interval enclosure reads visible at the requested tolerance.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that turns the endpoint windows and dyadic tolerance budget into regular rational observations.
\item $S$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row for endpoint and enclosure consumers; every Real-facing output must pass through this row after $D,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for endpoints, enclosure and order data, dyadic tolerance, windows, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from interval membership, enclosure, refinement, or completion-facing requests through $L,U,E,D,W,R,S,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealIntervalUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\RealIntervalEnclosureUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealIntervalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts exactly that these rows form one displayed real interval endpoint-and-enclosure history. It has no coordinate for host interval equality, selected quotient representatives, real-order trichotomy, countable choice, compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealInterval NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-interval-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealIntervalUp$ carrier $I=(L,U,E,D,W,R,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealIntervalUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the lower endpoint, upper endpoint, enclosure and order row, dyadic tolerance row, stream windows, regular rational readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item endpoint order, requiring $E$ to bind $L$ and $U$ before any interval read can be treated as an enclosure;
\item enclosure stability, requiring every containment, refinement, and endpoint-bound read to pass through $E$ together with the displayed tolerance and window rows;
\item dyadic tolerance handoff, requiring $D$ to precede the $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$;
\item Real seal non-escape, requiring every Real-facing endpoint or enclosure output to reach $S$ only after $L,U,E,D,W,R$ have been displayed;
\item ledger refusal, excluding host interval equality, selected endpoint representatives, real-order trichotomy, countable choice, compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No real interval obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-interval-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the two endpoint rows, enclosure and order row, dyadic tolerance row, stream windows, regular rational readback, Real seal, and structural rows.

Endpoint order and enclosure stability are read from $E$. That row is the only coordinate joining $L$ and $U$ into an interval boundary, so containment, refinement, and endpoint-bound requests cannot bypass it.

The dyadic tolerance handoff is the route $D\mapsto W\mapsto R$. A positive precision request first enters the dyadic budget, then selects finite stream windows, and only then produces regular rational readback.

The Real seal row $S$ is downstream of that route. The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same endpoint-and-enclosure history, while the excluded host interval, choice, compactness, and completeness objects have no coordinate in the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealInterval seal enclosure route]
\label{thm:real-interval-seal-enclosure-route}
Every endpoint, enclosure, refinement, or completion-facing read of an accepted $\RealIntervalUp$ carrier reaches the $\RealUp$ seal only through the displayed route
$$
L,U,E \longmapsto D,W,R \longmapsto S,H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently every interval consumer receives endpoint order, finite enclosure data, dyadic tolerance, stream windows, and regular rational readback before any Real-facing seal is visible. The route exports no host interval equality, selected endpoint representative, real-order trichotomy, countable-choice schedule, compactness theorem, hidden complete space, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $I=(L,U,E,D,W,R,S,H,C,P,N)$. The endpoint rows $L$ and $U$ are the only Real endpoints in the packet, and $E$ is the only row that binds them as an ordered enclosure.

Read the dyadic tolerance handoff next. The row $D$ supplies the finite precision and outward-rounding budget, $W$ exposes the endpoint stream windows visible at that budget, and $R$ turns those windows into regular rational readback.

Only after this handoff may the Real-facing seal $S$ be consumed. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row preserve the same route. Since none of the excluded host interval or completeness objects is a carrier coordinate, every interval read reaches the seal only through the displayed rows.
\end{proof}
