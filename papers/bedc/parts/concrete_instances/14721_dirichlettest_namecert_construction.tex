\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DirichletTestUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dirichlettest-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Dirichlet and the Bishop--Bridges constructive convergence reading, the $\DirichletTestUp$ packet records the finite summation-by-parts route for a series whose displayed coefficient tail is monotone null and whose displayed partial sums are bounded. It sits over the partial-sum surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-series-namecert}, the dyadic tail-budget ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadictailbound-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the finite quotient-of-differences discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-stolz-cesaro-namecert}, the conditional-series boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dirichlet-series-namecert}, and the majorant-facing comparison discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-weierstrassmtest-namecert}. The packet is a conditional convergence certificate carried by bounded partial sums and a monotone-null coefficient ledger; it is not an absolute-convergence theorem, a selected infinite sum, a quotient series object, host real equality, countable choice, or an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Dirichlet test carrier]
\label{def:dirichlet-test-carrier}
A $\DirichletTestUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(A,B,S,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the bounded partial-sum row for the source series, including the displayed finite partial sums and a displayed bound used by the summation-by-parts estimate.
\item $B$ is the monotone-null coefficient row, recording the inspected coefficient windows, monotonicity comparisons, nonnegativity or signed-variation side condition, and the dyadic null threshold requested by a tail consumer.
\item $S$ is the finite summation-by-parts route, storing the endpoint terms and the finite difference ledger by which a tail of products is rewritten into bounded partial sums times coefficient differences.
\item $T$ is the dyadic tail-budget row, combining the bound from $A$, the null threshold from $B$, and the finite variation estimate exposed by $S$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row produced from the tail-budget ledger.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row attached to the regular rational handoff.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the bounded partial-sum row, coefficient row, summation-by-parts route, tail-budget row, regular readback, real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through the coefficient threshold, finite summation-by-parts route, dyadic tail budget, regular readback, and real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DirichletTestUp$, $\SeriesUp$, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\StolzCesaroUp$, $\DirichletSeriesUp$, $\WeierstrassMTestUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DirichletTestUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these ten displayed rows. It has no coordinate for absolute convergence, a selected infinite sum, quotient series equality, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet test NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dirichlet-test-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DirichletTestUp$ carrier $D=(A,B,S,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DirichletTestUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed ten-row packet;
\item bounded partial-sum exposure for $A$ through finite $\SeriesUp$ windows and the displayed bound cited by the tail estimate;
\item monotone-null coefficient exposure for $B$, including the inspected comparison windows and dyadic threshold for the requested tail scale;
\item summation-by-parts route exactness for $S$, tying each endpoint and difference term to displayed finite rows;
\item dyadic tail-budget compatibility for $T$, requiring the estimate consumed by $R$ to be the estimate assembled from $A,B,S$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff and $\RealUp$ seal exposure for $R$ and $E$;
\item transport stability, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for exactly the rows recorded by $H,C,P,N$;
\item non-escape from the carrier surface: absolute convergence, selected infinite sums, quotient series equality, host real equality, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness are not exported.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $D=(A,B,S,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes exactly the bounded partial-sum row, coefficient row, summation-by-parts route, tail-budget ledger, regular readback, real seal, transport rows, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row.

Read a displayed precision request through $B$. The monotone-null row supplies a finite coefficient window and a dyadic threshold, while $A$ supplies the bounded partial-sum estimate on the source windows needed by that same request.

The route $S$ records the finite summation-by-parts calculation. Its endpoint terms and difference ledger are the only algebraic rows admitted for the tail calculation, and $T$ packages the resulting dyadic estimate before any regular rational readback is available.

Finally use the structural rows. The componentwise transports in $H$ preserve the displayed data, and $C,P,N$ only replay, source, and name the same route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for absolute convergence, selected infinite sums, quotient series equality, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet test summation-by-parts route]
\label{thm:dirichlet-test-summation-by-parts-route}
Every accepted $\DirichletTestUp$ carrier routes a product-series tail to the dyadic budget ledger only through the finite path
$$
A,B \longmapsto S \longmapsto T \longmapsto R,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
Consequently a downstream consumer may use the Dirichlet test only after reading the displayed bounded partial-sum row, the monotone-null coefficient row, the summation-by-parts route, and the dyadic tail-budget row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier and a displayed tail request. The coefficient row $B$ determines the finite coefficient window and threshold, and the partial-sum row $A$ supplies the bound used on the same interval.

Apply the summation-by-parts row $S$. It rewrites the finite product tail into endpoint terms and a finite difference sum whose coefficients are read from $B$ and whose partial sums are bounded by $A$.

Now read the dyadic budget row $T$. This row combines the endpoint estimates, coefficient null threshold, and finite variation estimate into the tolerance format consumed by $R$.

The remaining rows do not introduce another analytic route. The transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows preserve the displayed path, so any consumer that omits one of $A,B,S,T$ leaves the accepted carrier surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet test Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:dirichlet-test-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ seal-facing read exported by an accepted $\DirichletTestUp$ carrier factors through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row $R$ produced from the finite summation-by-parts tail budget. The seal row $E$ receives the bounded partial-sum row, monotone-null coefficient row, summation-by-parts route, dyadic tail budget, componentwise transports, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows, but it receives no absolute-convergence certificate, selected infinite sum, quotient series equality, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the seal-facing rows $R$ and $E$ from the accepted carrier. The regular handoff $R$ is downstream of the dyadic budget row $T$, and $T$ is downstream of the displayed partial-sum, coefficient, and summation-by-parts rows.

Use \autoref{thm:dirichlet-test-summation-by-parts-route}. It identifies the only route from a product-series tail to the regular readback as the finite path through $A,B,S,T$ and the structural rows attached to the same packet.

Read the seal row $E$. Its analytic input is exactly $R$, together with $H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for absolute convergence, selected infinite sums, quotient series equality, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, the seal-facing read cannot export any of those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet test nonescape boundary]
\label{thm:dirichlet-test-nonescape-boundary}
An accepted $\DirichletTestUp$ carrier cannot expose a convergence conclusion through any route that omits one of $A$, $B$, $S$, or $T$. In particular, the carrier surface rejects conclusions obtained from absolute convergence, from a selected infinite sum, from quotient series equality, from host real equality, from countable choice, or from ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Suppose a consumer reads a convergence conclusion from an accepted carrier. By \autoref{thm:dirichlet-test-namecert-obligations}, the conclusion row is available only after carrier admission, bounded partial-sum exposure, monotone-null coefficient exposure, summation-by-parts exactness, and dyadic tail-budget compatibility have all been read.

If one of $A,B,S,T$ is missing, the displayed route cannot form the required tail estimate. Without $A$ there is no bound on partial sums, without $B$ there is no monotone-null coefficient threshold, without $S$ there is no finite summation-by-parts ledger, and without $T$ there is no dyadic estimate to pass to $R$.

The structural rows cannot fill this gap. Transport in $H$ preserves displayed data, while $C,P,N$ replay, source, and name only the displayed route. Therefore any conclusion obtained from absolute convergence, selected infinite sums, quotient series equality, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness lies outside the accepted classifier.
\end{proof}
