It consumes the recursor-induced output packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-recursorinducednamecert-namecert}, the authorization packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-recursorauthorization-namecert}, the generator packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-recursorgenerator-namecert}, the branch-audit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-recursor-branch-audit-namecert}, and the classifier-membership packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typeclassifiermembership-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Recursor closure classifier audit carrier]
\label{def:recursor-closure-classifier-audit-carrier}
A $\RecursorClosureClassifierAuditUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(S,G,B,K,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the recursor signature and motive row, read through the accepted inductive signature and motive boundary;
\item $G$ is the $\RecursorGeneratorUp$ row recording the legal eliminator call and its structural generator boundary;
\item $B$ is the $\RecursorBranchAuditUp$ branch matrix, with one displayed branch row for every constructor of the accepted signature;
\item $K$ is the classifier-closure row, carrying the $\TypeClassifierMembershipUp$ membership surface and the output classifier reached by the recursor call;
\item $A$ is the audit-replay row, recording the kernel replay, source axiom gate, and forbidden-dependency purity check for the generated output;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,G,B,K,A$ and for the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes by which consumers read the recursor, branch, classifier, audit, transport, and local name;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RecursorInducedNameCertUp$, $\RecursorAuthorizationUp$, $\RecursorGeneratorUp$, $\RecursorBranchAuditUp$, $\TypeClassifierMembershipUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RecursorClosureClassifierAuditUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for an omitted constructor case, a detached classifier proof, a hidden host type checker, an unreported dependency trace, a second branch package, or an audit result outside $S,G,B,K,A,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Recursor closure classifier audit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:recursor-closure-classifier-audit-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RecursorClosureClassifierAuditUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:recursor-closure-classifier-audit-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission for $R=(S,G,B,K,A,H,C,P,N)$, recursor authorization through $S$ and $G$, constructor-complete branch coverage through $B$, classifier-closure preservation through $K$, audit replay through $A$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and the local naming row $N$.

Consequently, any accepted output must be readable both as a legal recursor-generated NameCert and as a classifier-closed output whose audit can be replayed from the same finite branch package. A packet whose branch package omits one constructor case is rejected by the classifier audit, even if its remaining recursor, classifier, and audit rows are individually well formed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows. The signature and motive row $S$, generator row $G$, branch matrix $B$, classifier-closure row $K$, audit replay row $A$, transport row $H$, continuation row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local name $N$ are the only available coordinates.

Read $S$ and $G$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-recursorauthorization-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-recursorgenerator-namecert}. They license the eliminator call but do not by themselves expose the per-constructor branch matrix or the classifier membership row.

Next read $B$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-recursor-branch-audit-namecert} and $K$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typeclassifiermembership-namecert}. Branch coverage and classifier membership are therefore checked on the same displayed recursor route rather than on separate host judgments.

Finally use the audit row $A$ and the recursor-induced output packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-recursorinducednamecert-namecert}. The replay must see the same branch package and classifier-closure row named in the carrier. If a constructor case is missing from $B$, the replay has no row from which to certify that branch, so the local classifier rejects the packet as a $\RecursorClosureClassifierAuditUp$ certificate.
\end{proof}
