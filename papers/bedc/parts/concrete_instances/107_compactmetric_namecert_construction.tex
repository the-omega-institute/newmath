\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompactMetricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}

The compact-metric interface $\CompactMetricUp$ packages a metric carrier that is both totally bounded and complete in the senses of $\NameCert_{\TotallyBoundedUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\CompleteMetricUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}). It is the metric form of compactness used by the Heine--Cantor capstone (\autoref{ch:capstones-compact-uniform-continuity}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:compactmetric-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Compact metric certificate carrier]
\label{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}
Let $X$ be a certified metric carrier over $\mathsf{BHist}$. The compact
metric certificate carrier
$$
\mathsf{CompactMetricCertificate}(X)
$$
is the conjunction of the following BEDC certificate fields:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item a $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier and distance package for $X$, with
  distance rows encoded by $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ and classifier
  transport on point histories;
  \item for every positive $\RatUp$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$, a
  $\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P}_{\varepsilon},net_{\varepsilon})$
  package of
  \autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-namecert-target};
  \item for every regular Cauchy $\StreamNameUp$ stream name $s$ whose
  observations are carried by $X$, a complete metric limit witness
  $\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu_s,\ell_s,L_s)$ as in
  \autoref{def:completemetric-limit-witness}.
\end{enumerate}
Thus compactness is represented by the metric carrier, the finite
ProbeBundle-net family at each visible positive tolerance, and the explicit
limit-witness family for all regular Cauchy streams. No external compact set
or host-level subsequence principle is part of the carrier.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:compactmetric-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric carrier obligation]
\label{thm:compactmetric-metric-carrier-obligation}
Let $X$ carry the compact metric certificate relation of
\autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}. Then the public carrier row
for $\CompactMetricUp$ is exactly the underlying $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier:
the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ point histories are accepted, the same point
classifier is used, the displayed distance rows are the inherited
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ rows, and distance-transport is the
$\hsame$-stable transport row supplied by the metric certificate. In the
ten-field $\NameCert$ roadmap template this fixes the source, carrier,
classifier, stability, and metric ledger entries before the
$\TotallyBoundedUp$ and $\CompleteMetricUp$ fields are read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the first field of
\autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}. It is a
$\MetricSpaceUp$ package over $\mathsf{BHist}$, so it already names the
accepted point histories, their point classifier, and the distance witness
relation used for every metric comparison.

No compact-specific carrier is introduced at this stage. The
$\CompactMetricUp$ carrier copies those point histories and copies the point
classifier, so classifier transport is the same $\hsame$ row that the metric
certificate uses on endpoints.

It remains to record the ledger surface. Each distance claim exposed by the
compact certificate is a $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ row inherited from
$\MetricSpaceUp$, and distance transport is precisely the inherited
$\hsame$-stable metric transport. These rows give the carrier, classifier,
distance witness, and distance-transport obligations required by the
$\NameCert$ template.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric finite-net obligation]
\label{thm:compactmetric-totallybounded-net-obligation}
Let $X$ carry the compact metric certificate relation of
\autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}. For every positive
$\RatUp$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$, the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ field
supplies a closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ net with center histories,
positive radius histories, bundle-membership rows, metric coverage rows, and
rational comparison ledgers. These center, radius, coverage, and ledger rows
are stable under $\hsame$ and the $\RatUp$ classifier by
\autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability}, and they
form the finite-net carrier, classifier-stability, and ledger obligations for
$\CompactMetricUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a positive $\RatUp$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$. The second field of
\autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier} gives a
$\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P}_{\varepsilon},net_{\varepsilon})$
row, where $\mathcal{P}_{\varepsilon}$ is a closed
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and membership is witnessed internally by
$\mathsf{InBundle}$ constructors.

Unpacking that row gives the finite-net carrier: each bundled probe has a
center history carried by the metric source, a positive radius history, and a
coverage ledger saying that every carried point has a bundled center within
the displayed tolerance. The ledger rows are exactly the tolerance history,
center histories, radius histories, membership constructors,
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ rows, and $\RatUp$ bound comparisons.

Apply
\autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability}. It transports
the positive tolerance, bundled centers, radii, metric distances, and bound
comparisons along $\hsame$ and the $\RatUp$ classifier while keeping the same
closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ carrier. Therefore the finite-net family
supplies the required classifier-stability and ledger obligations for the
compact metric certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric complete-limit obligation]
\label{thm:compactmetric-complete-limit-obligation}
Let $X$ carry the compact metric certificate relation of
\autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}. For every regular
$\StreamNameUp$ Cauchy stream name $s$ whose observations are carried by
$X$, the $\CompleteMetricUp$ field supplies a
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu_s,\ell_s,L_s)$ row. This row
records the stream, modulus, limit classifier, threshold histories,
late-observation distance rows, and $\RatUp$ comparison ledgers, and it is
stable under stream transport, modulus transport, and limit-classifier
transport by \autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-stability}. These rows
form the complete-limit carrier, classifier-stability, and ledger obligations
for $\CompactMetricUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a regular $\StreamNameUp$ Cauchy stream name $s$ whose observations lie in
the inherited $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier. The third field of
\autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier} provides a complete metric
limit witness for $s$, with explicit modulus history $\mu_s$, carried limit
history $\ell_s$, and late-observation ledger family $L_s$.

Unpack the witness. Its carrier data are the observed stream histories, the
modulus, the limit history, and the displayed threshold histories. Its ledger
data are the $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ rows from late observations to
the limit and the corresponding $\RatUp$ comparisons against each positive
tolerance.

Apply \autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-stability}. Stream transport
moves the observed histories, modulus transport moves the Cauchy threshold
rows, and limit classifier transport moves $\ell_s$ while preserving the
late-distance witnesses and rational bounds. Thus every regular Cauchy
stream receives a transported limit-witness package, giving the complete
metric part of the $\CompactMetricUp$ $\NameCert$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric package from total boundedness and completeness]
\label{thm:compactmetric-certificate-package}
For a certified metric carrier $X$, the three fields of
\autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier} form the paper-level
$\NameCert$ target for $\CompactMetricUp$. The classifier is inherited from
the $\MetricSpaceUp$ point and distance classifier; the totally-bounded field
is stable by
\autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability}; and the
complete-metric field is stable by
\autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-stability}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompactMetricUp.CompactMetricCertificate\_hsame\_transport}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompactMetricUp.CompactMetricSingleton\_certificate\_package}
\begin{proof}
Start with the metric carrier field. It supplies the source object, point
classifier, distance rows, and metric-distance transport used by both
subcertificates, so the public $\CompactMetricUp$ carrier can use the same
histories and the same $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ ledger policy.

For total boundedness, fix a transported positive tolerance history. The
certificate carrier provides the corresponding closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$
net, its bundled center histories, positive radius histories, membership
rows, distance witnesses, and rational comparison ledgers. Apply
\autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability} to transport
this finite-net row along the metric and $\RatUp$ classifier data.

For completeness, fix a transported regular Cauchy stream name whose
observations are carried by $X$. The certificate carrier supplies the modulus
history, limit history, threshold ledgers, and late-observation distance
bounds. Apply
\autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-stability} to transport these rows
pointwise and recover the displayed limit witness for the transported stream.

The ledger policy for $\CompactMetricUp$ is exactly the union of these three
visible packages: metric carrier data, ProbeBundle-net data at each positive
tolerance, and complete-limit data for each regular Cauchy stream. Since each
component has classifier transport and the shared source classifier is the
metric one, the assembled fields satisfy the paper-level $\NameCert$ target
for $\CompactMetricUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric certificate scope]
\label{thm:compactmetric-certificate-scope}
For a certified metric carrier $X$, the source, carrier, classifier,
stability, ledger, exactness, and status fields of
$\mathsf{CompactMetricCertificate}(X)$ are exactly the fields exposed in
\autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}: the $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier
and $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ rows, the

\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompactMetricUp.CompactMetricCertificate\_scope}
\begin{proof}

\autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}. The first field supplies the

classifier, and exactness ledger used by the compact-metric package.

The second field supplies, for each visible positive tolerance, a closed
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ net together with its membership, center, radius,
distance, and $\RatUp$ comparison ledgers. Its stability clause is precisely
\autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability}.

The third field supplies, for each regular Cauchy stream name, the modulus,
limit, late-observation threshold, and distance-bound ledgers. Its stability
clause is precisely \autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-stability}.
There is no fourth field in the displayed carrier, so the compact-metric
certificate scope is exactly this metric, finite-net, and limit-witness
surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric component stability]
\label{thm:compactmetric-component-stability}
Let $X$ be a certified metric carrier equipped with the
$\MetricSpaceUp$ point and distance classifier. Suppose the tolerance
histories, stream observations, moduli, limit histories, distance histories,
and every displayed comparison ledger in
$\mathsf{CompactMetricCertificate}(X)$ are transported by $\hsame$ together
with the $\RatUp$ classifier. Then the transported rows again assemble a
compact-metric certificate over the transported carrier, component by
component.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the shared metric carrier. The transported point histories and
distance rows remain under the same $\MetricSpaceUp$ classifier, so both
subcertificates read the same carrier predicate and the same
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ transport policy.

For the finite-net component, fix a transported positive tolerance history.
Apply \autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability} to the
corresponding $\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}$ row. This preserves the
closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine, the visible coverage rows, and the
$\RatUp$ bounds against the transported tolerance.

For the complete-limit component, fix a transported regular Cauchy stream
name. Apply \autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-stability} to the
corresponding $\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ row. This preserves the
modulus, transported limit, late-observation thresholds, metric distance rows,
and $\RatUp$ bounds for the transported stream.

Repack the three transported components using
\autoref{thm:compactmetric-certificate-package}. Since both subcertificates
share the transported metric carrier classifier, their ledgers combine into a
single transported $\CompactMetricUp$ certificate.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompactMetricUp.CompactMetricCertificate\_component\_stability}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric downstream consumer boundary]
\label{thm:compactmetric-downstream-consumer-boundary}
Downstream $\ContinuousMapUp$ and Heine--Cantor consumers of
$\mathsf{CompactMetricCertificate}(X)$ may use only the metric carrier, the
finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$-net family, and the complete-limit family
exposed by the compact-metric certificate. The certificate does not expose
open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, or choice-based subsequence
extraction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the exported ledger union from
\autoref{thm:compactmetric-certificate-package}. The public package contains
metric carrier data, one finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$-net row for each
positive tolerance, and one complete-limit witness row for each regular
Cauchy stream name.

A $\ContinuousMapUp$ consumer can therefore inspect point histories, distance
rows, finite net membership rows, and explicit limit-witness rows. A
Heine--Cantor consumer can use the same rows to combine total boundedness,
continuity control, and completeness through explicit certificates.

The same exported union contains no row naming open covers, no row selecting a
finite subcover, no row asserting sequential compactness for arbitrary
sequences, and no row extracting a subsequence by host-level choice. The
negative boundary recorded in the $\CompactMetricUp$ closure status is
therefore a consequence of the displayed certificate surface.
\end{proof}

compact-metric constructor source. It does not add an open-cover selector,
subsequence extractor, or external compact set; downstream consumers receive
the finite-net and complete-limit rows already certified by the component
NameCerts.

\begin{theorem}[Continuous-map consumption boundary]
\label{thm:compactmetric-continuousmap-consumption-boundary}
Any $\ContinuousMapUp$ construction consuming a public
$\CompactMetricUp$ certificate may use only the exported metric classifier,
the exported distance witnesses, and the finite
$\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}$ rows. It receives no open-cover row,
no subsequence compactness row, and no unlisted compact-set eliminator from
$\mathsf{CompactMetricCertificate}(X)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:compactmetric-public-export-surface}, the visible public
rows are exactly the metric carrier rows, the finite ProbeBundle-net family,
and the complete-limit family. The continuous-map interface can therefore
read the point classifier, distance witnesses, and finite-net center and
coverage ledgers directly from the exported certificate.

The same projection also proves the boundary. The certificate carrier in
\autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier} contains no open-cover
predicate, no subsequence selector, and no compact-set eliminator. Hence a
$\ContinuousMapUp$ argument consuming this public surface must factor through
the displayed metric and total-bounded rows rather than through any classical
compactness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Heine--Cantor input boundary]
\label{thm:compactmetric-heine-cantor-input-boundary}
The compact-uniform-continuity capstone may consume a public
$\CompactMetricUp$ certificate by using the finite
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$-net family together with continuous pointwise modulus
ledgers to assemble a uniform-modulus ledger. Completeness enters only through
the named $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit-witness field
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu_s,\ell_s,L_s)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the public export theorem. For each positive tolerance, the
$\CompactMetricUp$ surface supplies the finite
$\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}$ row, including bundled centers,
membership rows, distance witnesses, and rational comparison ledgers. Apply
\autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability} to transport
these rows along the metric classifier data used by the capstone.

The continuous input contributes pointwise modulus ledgers at the exported
centers. The finite net supplies only finitely many such center rows, so the
capstone can combine their visible tolerance ledgers into one
uniform-modulus ledger without invoking open covers or subsequences.

Completeness is used only at the point where a regular Cauchy stream requires
a named limit witness. That row is exactly the
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ exported from the certificate, with
transport governed by
\autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-stability}. No additional
compactness principle is consumed.
\end{proof}

\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P}_{\varepsilon},net_{\varepsilon}).

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the second field of \autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}. For
each positive tolerance history it gives a closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine
$\mathcal{P}_{\varepsilon}$, center histories, membership rows, distance
witnesses, and $\RatUp$ comparison ledgers in the package named in the
statement.

The transport clause is then read through
\autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability}. That theorem
keeps the same metric carrier interface and transports the tolerance,
membership, center, distance, and rational-bound rows along the source
classifiers.

Finally repack these rows into the $\CompactMetricUp$ ten-field $\NameCert$
template by \autoref{thm:compactmetric-certificate-package}. The resulting
obligation is only the tolerance-indexed finite-net family; it does not add a
finite-subcover choice principle or any open-cover surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompactMetric limit-witness family obligation]
\label{thm:compactmetric-limit-witness-family-obligation}
Let $X$ be a compact-metric certificate carrier. Every carried regular Cauchy
$\StreamNameUp$ stream $s$ has the complete-metric witness
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu_s,\ell_s,L_s)
$$
of \autoref{def:completemetric-limit-witness}. Transport of these witnesses is
the pointwise transport supplied by
\autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-stability}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the third field of \autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}. For a
carried regular Cauchy stream it supplies the modulus history $\mu_s$, the
carried limit history $\ell_s$, the threshold rows, the late-observation
distance rows, and the $\RatUp$ budget comparisons required by
\autoref{def:completemetric-limit-witness}.

Apply \autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-stability} to the displayed
stream, modulus, limit, threshold, distance, and rational comparison rows. The
transport remains internal to the same metric carrier classifier and does not
use a host completion or a choice of subsequence.

Repack the transported witness through
\autoref{thm:compactmetric-certificate-package}. This fills the complete-metric
part of the ten-field $\NameCert$ template for $\CompactMetricUp$ while leaving
all convergence data visible as the limit-witness ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompactMetric ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:compactmetric-ledger-exactness-obligation}
For a compact-metric certificate carrier $X$, the $\CompactMetricUp$ ledger is
exactly the union of the metric-distance rows of $\MetricSpaceUp$, the
tolerance-indexed finite-net rows of $\TotallyBoundedUp$, and the regular-stream
limit-witness rows of $\CompleteMetricUp$. Coverage, soundness, and completeness
for the exported certificate are read componentwise over these three sources.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier inventory in
\autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}. It lists one metric source
field, one ProbeBundle-net family indexed by positive $\RatUp$ tolerances, and
one limit-witness family indexed by carried regular Cauchy streams. These are
the only rows available to the compact-metric package.

Coverage is componentwise. The metric source covers point classification and
distance witnessing; the totally-bounded component covers each carried point by
a bundled center at a displayed tolerance; and the complete-metric component
covers each carried regular Cauchy stream by its displayed limit witness.

Soundness is also componentwise. Metric rows are compared by the
$\MetricSpaceUp$ classifier, finite-net rows by
\autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability}, and limit rows
by \autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-stability}. Each comparison stays
inside the classifier and ledger named by its source component.

Completeness follows by repacking all three inventories through
\autoref{thm:compactmetric-certificate-package}. Any exported compact-metric row
therefore comes from one of the displayed metric, finite-net, or limit-witness
rows, and every such row is accepted by the ten-field $\NameCert$ template for
$\CompactMetricUp$.
\end{proof}
