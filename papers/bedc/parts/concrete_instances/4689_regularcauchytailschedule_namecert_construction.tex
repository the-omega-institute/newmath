\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy tail schedule carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-tail-schedule-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyTailScheduleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the finite precision-request row consumed by the Real and Completion route;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational source row whose tails are scheduled;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window schedule selected for $Q$;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and endpoint ledger for the selected windows;
\item $K$ is the cofinal-index row naming the finite tail bound reached by the schedule;
\item $T$ is the finite tail-witness row read from $R,W,D,K$;
\item $M$ is the $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$ shared-threshold packet available to consumers that compare the scheduled tail with another tail;
\item $F$ is the $\RegularCauchyTailFusionUp$ consumer row that may fuse the displayed tail witness with the downstream diagonal readback;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing handoff row reached only after $Q,R,W,D,K,T,M,F$ are displayed;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the precision request, source row, window schedule, dyadic ledger, cofinal index, tail witness, sibling consumer rows, seal-facing row, provenance, and local name;

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailScheduleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is the finite schedule row linking a precision request to one displayed cofinal tail. It contains no coordinate for a host-level subsequence, quotient stream equality, selected Real limit, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy tail schedule NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-schedule-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailScheduleUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-tail-schedule-carrier} consists exactly of five obligation rows:

\item carrier admission for the finite packet $S=(Q,R,W,D,K,T,M,F,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier stability under the displayed $\hsame$ transports of $H$ for the precision request, source row, windows, dyadic ledger, cofinal index, tail witness, sibling consumer rows, seal-facing row, provenance, and local name;
\item schedule exactness, which forces every cofinal-tail read to pass through $Q,R,W,D,K,T$ before any sibling consumer can inspect it;

\end{enumerate}

\begin{proof}

Next inspect the sibling consumer coordinates. The shared-threshold row $M$ is the only tail-meet handoff, and the tail-fusion row $F$ is the only fusion handoff. Finally use the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. They transport the same displayed coordinates, replay the same finite continuations, record package provenance, and name the local certificate. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, host equality, selected limits, unbounded diagonal objects, or ambient completeness, the five rows exhaust the local naming surface.

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy tail schedule seal-facing route determinacy]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-schedule-seal-facing-route-determinacy}
Let
$$
S=(Q,R,W,D,K,T,M,F,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
and
$$
S'=(Q',R',W',D',K',T',M',F',E',H',C',P',N')
$$
be accepted $\RegularCauchyTailScheduleUp$ carriers. Suppose the two packets have componentwise $\hsame$ transports aligning
$$
Q,Q',\quad R,R',\quad W,W',\quad D,D',\quad K,K',\quad T,T',\quad M,M',\quad F,F',\quad P,P',\quad N,N',
$$
and suppose the displayed continuation rows $C$ and $C'$ present the same finite handoff route from the aligned entrance rows $Q,R,W,D,K,T,M,F$ to the seal-facing endpoints $E$ and $E'$. Then
$$
\hsame(E,E').
$$
Thus two accepted schedule packets with the same precision request, $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, $\StreamNameUp$ window schedule, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, cofinal index, tail witness, sibling consumer rows, transport rows, continuation route, provenance, and naming row determine the same $\RealUp$ seal-facing handoff up to $\hsame$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both carriers using \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-tail-schedule-carrier}. In each packet, the only seal-facing coordinate is the row $E$ or $E'$, and it is downstream of the displayed entrance rows $Q,R,W,D,K,T,M,F$.

By \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-schedule-namecert-obligations}, schedule exactness forces every cofinal-tail read to pass through $Q,R,W,D,K,T$ before any sibling consumer can inspect it, and downstream consumer handoff then routes the shared-threshold and tail-fusion reads through $M$ and $F$ before the seal-facing handoff is available. Hence the terminal seal read in either packet is not an independent Real row; it is the endpoint of the finite continuation route named in \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-tail-schedule-handoff-route}.

The assumed componentwise transports align the two entrance surfaces. After this alignment, the terminal clauses of $C$ and $C'$ are two displayed $\Cont$ derivations with the same left history and the same finite right route, one ending at $E$ and the other ending at $E'$. Applying continuation determinacy from \autoref{thm:continuation-determinacy} gives $\hsame(E,E')$.

The sibling rows are only consumer rows in this argument. The shared-threshold packet $M$ and the tail-fusion row $F$ are read before the seal route, and neither adds a second seal endpoint to the schedule carrier. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected completed limits, unbounded diagonal streams, hidden tail oracles, or ambient completeness, no alternate seal-facing route remains.
\end{proof}
