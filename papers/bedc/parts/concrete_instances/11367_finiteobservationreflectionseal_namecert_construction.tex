\begin{theorem}[Finite observation-reflection seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-observation-reflection-seal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-observation-reflection-seal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationReflectionSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $F=(P,M,S,K,C,L,H,Q,N)$;
\item physical-observation exposure through $P$ and formal-reflection exposure through $M$;
\item shared signature and classifier acceptance through $S$ and $K$;
\item continuation and non-finalization through the shared route $C$ and ledger $L$;
\item componentwise transport, provenance, and local naming through $H,Q,N$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the seal certifies a finite correspondence between physical inquiry and MetaCIC self-study without identifying their sources and without closing either inquiry direction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The signature and classifier rows provide the shared correspondence. Row $S$ records the finite signature comparison, and row $K$ records the accepted classifier judgment. These rows consume the observation-reflection packet and reflective inquiry surfaces rather than replacing them.

The route and ledger rows then close the non-finality condition. The shared continuation row $C$ keeps both directions extendable, while $L$ records rejected final truth-oracle demands. Rows $H,Q,N$ transport, package, and name exactly the same displayed route, so no further NameCert obligation remains outside the packet.
\end{proof}
