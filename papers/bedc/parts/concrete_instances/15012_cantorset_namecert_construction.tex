Following Cantor and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\CantorSetUp$ packet records the middle-third closed subset of the real line as finite ternary-prefix and deleted-middle interval data. It is a real-set certificate downstream of the nested interval discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nestedinterval-namecert}, the dyadic endpoint surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicinterval-namecert}, and the $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The carrier names only finite prefixes, finite gap ledgers, nested interval rows, and real-seal routing; it does not import open-cover compactness, host powersets, quotient real equality, or a completed infinite intersection.

\begin{definition}[Cantor set carrier]
\label{def:cantor-set-carrier}
A $\CantorSetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
C=(T,G,I,D,R,E,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the ternary-prefix ledger, a finite word over the left and right retained thirds, together with its generation depth.
\item $G$ is the deleted-middle interval ledger, listing the middle-third gaps excluded at the displayed depths.
\item $I$ is the retained nested-interval row whose endpoints are determined by $T$ and whose containment route is read through the $\NestedIntervalUp$ surface.
\item $D$ is the auxiliary $\DyadicIntervalUp$ or rational endpoint ledger used to expose the retained interval endpoints to finite consumers.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row for a real-name readback compatible with the retained interval chain.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the ternary prefix, deleted-gap ledger, retained interval row, endpoint ledger, and regular-rational handoff have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the ternary prefix, gap ledger, interval row, endpoint ledger, handoff row, real seal, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $K$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from prefix requests to gap checks, from gap checks to retained intervals, and from retained intervals to $R$ and $E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CantorSetUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicIntervalUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CantorSetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts a packet only when the displayed prefix $T$ selects retained thirds, the ledger $G$ contains exactly the finite deleted-middle gaps inspected at those depths, the interval row $I$ avoids those gaps, and every real-facing read factors through $R,E,H,K,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host subset of $\RealUp$, open-cover compactness, quotient real equality, selected infinite branch, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cantor set NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cantor-set-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CantorSetUp$ carrier $C=(T,G,I,D,R,E,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CantorSetUp}$ obligations are exactly carrier admission, finite ternary-prefix classification, deleted-middle gap exclusion, nested-interval containment, endpoint-ledger compatibility, $\RealUp$ seal handoff, and non-escape. These obligations are discharged by the displayed route
$$
T\longmapsto G\longmapsto I\longmapsto D\longmapsto R\longmapsto E
$$
together with $H,K,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission projects the finite tuple of \autoref{def:cantor-set-carrier}. The prefix classifier reads $T$ as a finite retained-third word and therefore inspects only finitely many ternary branch decisions.

The deleted-middle exclusion row $G$ is the only place where removed middle-third intervals are named. The retained interval row $I$ must pass through the $\NestedIntervalUp$ containment surface, so every accepted prefix determines a retained nested interval before any real-facing row is read.

Endpoint compatibility is checked by $D$. This ledger exposes the same retained interval endpoints to dyadic or rational interval consumers and prevents a later consumer from replacing the ternary interval by an unrelated host interval.

Finally, $R$ and $E$ are downstream readback and seal rows. The transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows $H,K,P,N$ preserve the same finite route, and no carrier coordinate supplies open-cover compactness, host powersets, quotient real equality, a selected infinite branch, or ambient real completeness.
\end{proof}
