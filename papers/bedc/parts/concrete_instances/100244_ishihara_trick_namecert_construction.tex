\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\IshiharaTrickUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\IshiharaTrickUp$ packet records the finite inspection boundary used when a real-name argument refines a disjunctive apartness situation without importing a global semi-classical principle. It is read against the constructive boundary example of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-speckersequence-namecert}, the regular-rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the stream schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a Real/Completion boundary object: it exhibits the rows a consumer may inspect and records that no LLPO, WLPO, Markov-principle leakage, trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected limits, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness travels across the boundary.

\begin{definition}[Ishihara trick carrier]
\label{def:ishihara-trick-carrier}
An $\IshiharaTrickUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(S,R,T,W,D,E,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is a $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row naming the finite observation requests admitted by the inspection.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row returning the rational approximants actually inspected from that schedule.
\item $T$ is the displayed real-name test sequence, presented only through the finite schedule and regular-rational readback rows already named by $S$ and $R$.
\item $W$ is the finite inspection window naming the tested indices of $T$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the dyadic radii, endpoint comparisons, and finite margin rows used by the inspection.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $S,R,T,W,D$ have been displayed.
\item $A$ is the binary test outcome boundary: it records which finite tolerance row witnesses the local apartness alternative or, when no such row is displayed, which refinement request remains inside the schedule.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the schedule, readback, test sequence, inspection window, dyadic ledger, real seal, boundary row, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ stores $\Cont$ replay from finite schedule requests through $R,T,W,D$, and from those rows into the sealed real-facing boundary $A$.
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\IshiharaTrickUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\SpeckerSequenceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\IshiharaTrickUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for LLPO, WLPO, Markov-principle leakage, trichotomy, quotient real equality, countable choice, a selected limit, or an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Ishihara trick NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:ishihara-trick-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\IshiharaTrickUp$ carrier $O=(S,R,T,W,D,E,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\IshiharaTrickUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the StreamName schedule, RegSeqRat readback, displayed real-name test sequence, finite inspection window, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, Real seal, inspected binary boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item schedule-readback coherence, requiring every inspected rational value to be read through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows;
\item dyadic tolerance discipline, requiring every apartness or refinement branch to cite a displayed dyadic radius, endpoint comparison, and finite margin row from $D$ within the window $W$;
\item real-boundary handoff, requiring a Real/Completion consumer to receive the branch only through $E$ together with the displayed rows $S,R,T,W,D,A,H,C,P,N$;
\item ledger nonescape from LLPO, WLPO, Markov-principle leakage, trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected limits, countable choice, and ambient real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these five rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:ishihara-trick-carrier}. The visible data are the finite schedule $S$, readback $R$, displayed test sequence $T$, finite window $W$, tolerance ledger $D$, terminal real seal $E$, inspected boundary $A$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Schedule-readback coherence follows because the packet contains no readback coordinate independent of $S$ and $R$. Every rational approximant inspected by the branch is therefore routed through the displayed stream and regular-rational rows.

The dyadic tolerance row is forced by $D$ and the inspection window $W$. A branch may expose a local apartness witness only by naming a displayed dyadic radius, endpoint comparison, and finite margin inside the tested window; otherwise it records the finite refinement request still admitted by the schedule.

The real-boundary handoff is downstream of $E$, with transport and replay supplied componentwise by $H$ and $C$. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for LLPO, WLPO, Markov-principle leakage, trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected limits, countable choice, or ambient completeness, none of those principles can be exported through the local $\NameCert_{\IshiharaTrickUp}$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Ishihara trick finite test boundary]
\label{thm:ishihara-trick-finite-test-boundary}
Every accepted $\IshiharaTrickUp$ packet exposes its binary branch only through the displayed real-name test sequence $T$, finite inspection window $W$, and dyadic ledger $D$. Hence the classifier can distinguish the displayed local branch from continued refinement only by finite inspection of the named rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier presents the test sequence $T$ through the already displayed stream and regular-rational rows. The window $W$ names the inspected indices, so no classifier step can read outside the finite schedule.

The branch row $A$ is binary at the packet boundary. If a dyadic separation is displayed, it cites a radius, endpoint comparison, and finite margin from $D$ inside $W$; if no such row is displayed, $A$ records only that the schedule continues by refinement.

Componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ replay preserve this finite route. Therefore the classifier cannot obtain a third outcome, a global ordering of reals, or a selected limit from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Ishihara trick branch stability]
\label{thm:ishihara-trick-branch-stability}
Let
$$
O=(S,R,T,W,D,E,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
and
$$
O'=(S',R',T',W',D',E',A',H',C',P',N')
$$
be accepted $\IshiharaTrickUp$ packets. Suppose the displayed schedule, regular-rational readback, test sequence, inspection window, dyadic ledger, and real seal rows of $O$ and $O'$ are related componentwise by the transport rows, and suppose the branch inspection is replayed through the same $\Cont$ route. Then the branch row is stable under that transport: a displayed apartness branch in $A$ remains the corresponding displayed apartness branch in $A'$ with transported dyadic radius, endpoint comparison, and margin, while a refinement branch in $A$ remains a refinement request inside the transported schedule $S'$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows of $O$ and $O'$. The only rows available to a branch inspection are the schedules, readbacks, displayed test sequences, finite windows, dyadic ledgers, real seals, branch rows, componentwise transports, and continuation replays named in the two packets.

Apply \autoref{thm:ishihara-trick-finite-test-boundary} to the source packet. Every branch read is restricted to the displayed $T,W,D$ rows, so an apartness branch can cite only a dyadic radius, endpoint comparison, and finite margin from the ledger inside the window, and a non-apartness branch can record only continued refinement through the schedule.

The row $H$ transports the displayed schedule, readback, test sequence, window, dyadic ledger, real seal, and boundary row componentwise to the corresponding rows of $O'$. The row $C$ replays the same continuation route through those transported rows, so the inspected branch cannot change shape during transport.

Thus a displayed apartness branch remains an apartness branch with transported dyadic data, and a refinement branch remains a refinement request inside the transported schedule. Since neither packet contains a coordinate for LLPO, WLPO, Markov-principle leakage, trichotomy, quotient real equality, a selected limit, countable choice, or ambient completeness, no outside principle can supply an additional branch case.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Ishihara trick real seal nonescape]
\label{thm:ishihara-trick-real-seal-nonescape}
Every Real/Completion consumer of an accepted $\IshiharaTrickUp$ packet factors through the displayed finite rows
$$
S,R,T,W,D,E,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
Consequently the consumer receives only a finite schedule, rational readback, displayed test sequence, finite inspection window, dyadic tolerance ledger, terminal real seal, inspected binary boundary, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local NameCert row. It receives no LLPO, WLPO, Markov-principle leakage, trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected limit, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a Real/Completion consumer accept the packet $O=(S,R,T,W,D,E,A,H,C,P,N)$. By the carrier definition, the only real-facing coordinate is the seal $E$, and that seal is reached after the schedule, readback, test sequence, finite window, tolerance ledger, inspected boundary, and structural rows have been displayed.

The consumer's branch information is therefore finite. It can inspect the schedule row, replay the regular-rational readback, cite the dyadic tolerance ledger inside the finite window, and use the componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows to transport those observations through the seal.

No omitted semi-classical or completion principle is a coordinate of the packet. Thus factorization through the displayed rows cannot export LLPO, WLPO, Markov-principle leakage, trichotomy, quotient real equality, a selected limit, countable choice, or ambient real completeness.
\end{proof}
