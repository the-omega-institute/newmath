\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\IshiharaTrickUp$}

Following Ishihara and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\IshiharaTrickUp$ packet records the finite inspection boundary used when a real-name argument refines a disjunctive apartness situation without importing a global semi-classical principle. It is read against the stream schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular-rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the nonconvergent control example of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-speckersequence-namecert}, and the located boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}. The packet is a Real/Completion boundary object: it exhibits the rows a consumer may inspect and records that no LPO, LLPO, trichotomy, countable choice, quotient stream equality, selected limit, or ambient real completeness travels across the boundary.

\begin{definition}[Ishihara trick carrier]
\label{def:ishihara-trick-carrier}
An $\IshiharaTrickUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(S,R,D,E,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is a $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row naming the finite observation requests admitted by the inspection.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row returning the rational approximants actually inspected from that schedule.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the dyadic radii, endpoint comparisons, and finite margin rows used by the inspection.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $S,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $A$ is the inspected apartness/refinement branch: it records which finite tolerance row witnesses the local apartness alternative or, when no such row is displayed, which refinement request remains inside the schedule.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the schedule, readback, dyadic ledger, real seal, branch row, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ stores $\Cont$ replay from finite schedule requests through $R$ and $D$, and from those rows into the sealed real-facing branch $A$.
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\IshiharaTrickUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\SpeckerSequenceUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\IshiharaTrickUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for LPO, LLPO, trichotomy, countable choice, quotient stream equality, a selected limit, or an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Ishihara trick NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:ishihara-trick-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\IshiharaTrickUp$ carrier $O=(S,R,D,E,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\IshiharaTrickUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the StreamName schedule, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, Real seal, inspected branch, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item schedule-readback coherence, requiring every inspected rational value to be read through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows;
\item dyadic tolerance discipline, requiring every apartness or refinement branch to cite a displayed dyadic radius, endpoint comparison, and finite margin row from $D$;
\item real-boundary handoff, requiring a Real/Completion consumer to receive the branch only through $E$ together with the displayed rows $S,R,D,A,H,C,P,N$;
\item ledger nonescape from LPO, LLPO, trichotomy, countable choice, quotient stream equality, selected limits, and ambient real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these five rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:ishihara-trick-carrier}. The visible data are the finite schedule $S$, readback $R$, tolerance ledger $D$, terminal real seal $E$, inspected branch $A$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Schedule-readback coherence follows because the packet contains no readback coordinate independent of $S$ and $R$. Every rational approximant inspected by the branch is therefore routed through the displayed stream and regular-rational rows.

The dyadic tolerance row is forced by $D$. A branch may expose a local apartness witness only by naming a displayed dyadic radius, endpoint comparison, and finite margin; otherwise it records the finite refinement request still admitted by the schedule.

The real-boundary handoff is downstream of $E$, with transport and replay supplied componentwise by $H$ and $C$. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for LPO, LLPO, trichotomy, countable choice, quotient stream equality, selected limits, or ambient completeness, none of those principles can be exported through the local $\NameCert_{\IshiharaTrickUp}$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Ishihara trick Real/Completion boundary]
\label{thm:ishihara-trick-real-completion-boundary}
Every Real/Completion consumer of an accepted $\IshiharaTrickUp$ packet factors through the displayed finite rows
$$
S,R,D,E,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
Consequently the consumer receives only a finite schedule, rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, terminal real seal, inspected apartness/refinement branch, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local NameCert row. It receives no LPO, LLPO, trichotomy, countable choice, quotient stream equality, selected limit, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a Real/Completion consumer accept the packet $O=(S,R,D,E,A,H,C,P,N)$. By the carrier definition, the only real-facing coordinate is the seal $E$, and that seal is reached after the schedule, readback, tolerance ledger, inspected branch, and structural rows have been displayed.

The consumer's branch information is therefore finite. It can inspect the schedule row, replay the regular-rational readback, cite the dyadic tolerance ledger, and use the componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows to transport those observations through the seal.

No omitted semi-classical or completion principle is a coordinate of the packet. Thus factorization through the displayed rows cannot export LPO, LLPO, trichotomy, countable choice, quotient stream equality, a selected limit, or ambient real completeness.
\end{proof}
