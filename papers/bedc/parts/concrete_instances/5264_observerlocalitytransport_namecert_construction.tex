The $\ObserverLocalityTransportUp$ packet realizes the transport boundary in the Inter-Hist Locality chain of \autoref{ch:visions-inter-hist-locality}. It sits after the observer-locality cell surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-locality-cell-namecert}, the causal-route surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausal-namecert}, and the unary-bound surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-maxcausalrate-namecert}; it is the route-level sibling read by the cross-Hist causal-rate packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausalrate-namecert}. The packet records the finite path by which a consumer transports locality-cell data through causal rows and maximum-rate rows without entering a global observer frame.

\begin{definition}[Observer-locality transport carrier]
\label{def:observer-locality-transport-carrier}
An $\ObserverLocalityTransportUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(O,D,R,L,H,Q,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ row that supplies the two-observer locality cell being transported;
\item $D$ is the finite family of $\CrossHistCausalUp$ rows that records the causal route through which the locality-cell read is moved between the two displayed histories;
\item $R$ is the $\MaxCausalRateUp$ row whose unary bound is read only after the causal routes in $D$ have been exposed;
\item $L$ is the no-global-frame ledger, recording that the transport route contains no privileged observer frame, synchronized clock, metric tensor, Lorentz-geometry coordinate, or host real-valued speed row;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the locality cell, causal rows, maximum-rate row, no-global-frame ledger, probe-stability row, continuation routes, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item $Q$ records $\psame$ stability for the probe reads used by the locality-cell row, causal rows, and maximum-rate row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads $O$, replays each row of $D$, reads $R$, checks $L$, and transports the same packet through $H$ and $Q$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ObserverLocalityTransportUp$, $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, $\MaxCausalRateUp$, $\CrossHistCausalRateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\psame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverLocalityTransportUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the displayed locality cell, causal rows, maximum-rate row, no-global-frame ledger, transport rows, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observer-locality transport NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-locality-transport-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-locality-transport-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverLocalityTransportUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $T=(O,D,R,L,H,Q,C,P,N)$;
\item locality-cell source exactness, requiring the transported row to be the $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ row $O$ and not a detached observer-frame coordinate;
\item causal-route handoff, requiring every transport route to pass through the displayed $\CrossHistCausalUp$ family $D$ before the maximum-rate row $R$ is read;
\item transport stability through componentwise $\hsame$ in $H$, probe stability in $Q$, and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$;
\item non-escape, excluding global frames, synchronized clocks, metric tensors, Lorentz geometry, host real-valued speed, and any locality read not routed through $O,D,R,L,H,Q,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:observer-locality-transport-carrier}. The rows $O,D,R,L,H,Q,C,P,N$ display the locality cell, causal family, maximum-rate row, no-global-frame ledger, transports, routes, provenance, and local name.

Locality-cell source exactness reads $O$ first. The packet has no separate observer-frame coordinate, so the transported locality surface is the finite cell row rather than an ambient frame.

Causal-route handoff then reads $D$ before $R$. Each maximum-rate read is attached to the displayed causal family, so a consumer cannot replace the route witness by a bare unary bound.

Transport stability is componentwise through $H,Q,C$, and the non-escape claim is the carrier inventory. Since no excluded structure is a coordinate of $T$, no local certificate projection can export one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observer-locality transport boundary]
\label{thm:observer-locality-transport-boundary}
Every public consumer of an accepted $\ObserverLocalityTransportUp$ packet reads the locality-cell row only through
$$
O,D,R,L,H,Q,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, the consumer must pass from the $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ row $O$ through the $\CrossHistCausalUp$ rows $D$ before it reads the $\MaxCausalRateUp$ row $R$ or the cross-Hist causal-rate surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausalrate-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The locality-cell source is the row $O$, and the only displayed route family connecting it to rate data is $D$.

Use the causal-route handoff in \autoref{thm:observer-locality-transport-namecert-obligations}. It places the $\CrossHistCausalUp$ rows before the maximum-rate row $R$, so the rate read is not a substitute for the transport route.

Finally route through $H,Q,C,P,N$ and check the no-global-frame ledger $L$. The consumer remains inside the finite packet and cannot read a global frame, metric tensor, Lorentz geometry, synchronized clock, or host real-valued speed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observer-locality transport rate pullback]
\label{thm:observer-locality-transport-rate-pullback}
Every rate-facing consumer of an accepted $\ObserverLocalityTransportUp$ packet factors through the same finite tuple
$$
O,D,R,L,H,Q,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, the consumer must read the $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ row $O$, the $\CrossHistCausalUp$ route family $D$, the $\MaxCausalRateUp$ row $R$, and the $\CrossHistCausalRateUp$ consumer surface through one transport packet. The packet exports no global frame, synchronized clock, metric tensor, Lorentz geometry, host real-valued speed, or detached rate row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier from \autoref{def:observer-locality-transport-carrier}. The tuple $O,D,R,L,H,Q,C,P,N$ lists the locality cell, causal family, maximum-rate row, no-global-frame ledger, structural rows, provenance, and local naming row available to a rate-facing consumer.

Apply \autoref{thm:observer-locality-transport-namecert-obligations}. The NameCert surface places locality-cell exactness before causal-route handoff, and it requires every maximum-rate read to pass through the displayed causal family.

Now use \autoref{thm:observer-locality-transport-boundary}. It keeps the public consumer inside the same tuple and routes any cross-Hist causal-rate read through the locality cell, causal routes, maximum-rate row, and transport rows.

The non-escape clause follows from the ledger $L$ and carrier inventory. Since no listed coordinate is a global frame, synchronized clock, metric tensor, Lorentz geometry, host real-valued speed, or detached rate row, the accepted transport packet cannot export one.
\end{proof}
