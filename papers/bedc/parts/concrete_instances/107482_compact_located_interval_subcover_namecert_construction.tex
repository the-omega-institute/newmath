\label{def:compact-located-interval-subcover-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $N$ is the $\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp$ row, carrying a finite mesh, mesh-scale ledger, and finite-net coverage route for the same interval.
\item $C$ is the local coverage row: each finite mesh cell is paired with an admitted cover token and the finite witness that the cell is covered at the displayed tolerance.
\item $M$ is the extraction row listing the selected finite cover tokens in the order read from the finite net.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the located interval, finite net, and local coverage rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for interval, net, local coverage, extracted token list, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

\begin{theorem}[Compact located interval subcover obligations]
\label{thm:compact-located-interval-subcover-obligations}

\begin{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact located interval finite-net extraction]
\label{thm:compact-located-interval-subcover-finite-net-extraction}
Every accepted $\CompactLocatedIntervalSubcoverUp$ carrier exports a finite subcover list only by the route
$$
\LocatedIntervalUp \longrightarrow \HeineBorelFiniteNetUp \longrightarrow C \longrightarrow M \longrightarrow \RealUp .
$$
In particular, the finite list $M$ is extracted from the displayed mesh cells and their local coverage witnesses; it is not chosen from an arbitrary open cover and is not obtained from sequential compactness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the located interval row $I$. It fixes the endpoint and radius data whose finite-net request is read by $N$.

The row $N$ supplies only a finite mesh route. The local coverage row $C$ attaches cover tokens to that finite mesh, so the extraction row $M$ can list exactly those displayed tokens.

The terminal seal $E$ is downstream of the finite extraction. It records the $\RealUp$ boundary of the already displayed route and does not supply a separate compactness theorem.

Thus the exported subcover is the finite list read from $M$, transported by $H$ and replayed by $R$. The excluded open-cover and sequential compactness principles have no coordinate from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}
