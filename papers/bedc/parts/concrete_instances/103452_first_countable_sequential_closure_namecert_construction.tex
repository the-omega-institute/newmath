\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FirstCountableSequentialClosureUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-first-countable-sequential-closure-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley on first-countability and sequential closure, the $\FirstCountableSequentialClosureUp$ packet records the finite route by which a local countable base supplies Frechet-tail neighbourhood tests and then exposes a displayed sequential-closure witness. It sits after the local-basis surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-first-countable-space-namecert}, the cofinite-tail interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-frechetfilter-namecert}, the sequence-closure packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sequentialclosure-namecert}, the real sequence limit handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-limit-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names only the displayed route from countable local bases to sequence-limit closure evidence; it does not identify host points, choose subsequences, quotient filters, prove open-cover compactness, invoke countable choice, or import ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[First-countable sequential-closure carrier]
\label{def:first-countable-sequential-closure}
A $\FirstCountableSequentialClosureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(B,T,Q,L,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\FirstCountableSpaceUp$ local-basis row. It records the accepted point-window, the local countable neighbourhood ledger, and the topology classifier that every closure request must read.
\item $T$ is the $\FrechetFilterUp$ tail-neighbourhood row. It turns the local countable request into a cofinite-tail budget before any sequence window is inspected.
\item $Q$ is the $\SequentialClosureUp$ witness row. It names the displayed source subset, candidate closure point, neighbourhood tests, and metric tests supplied to the closure consumer.
\item $L$ is the $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ handoff row, available only when the sequential witness is read through a real-valued sequence limit.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger for the local basis enumeration, the Frechet threshold, the inspected sequence entries, and the limit handoff.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row translating the displayed metric and real-sequence windows into regular rational data.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the neighbourhood radius, Frechet-tail slack, sequence-window budget, and real-limit comparison scale.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $B,T,Q,L,W,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the local-basis, Frechet-tail, sequential-closure, real-sequence-limit, stream-window, regular-rational, dyadic-tolerance, Real-seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
B,T \longmapsto Q \longmapsto L,W,R,D \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FirstCountableSequentialClosureUp$, $\FirstCountableSpaceUp$, $\FrechetFilterUp$, $\SequentialClosureUp$, $\RealSequenceLimitUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FirstCountableSequentialClosureUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host point equality, selected subsequences, quotient filters, open-cover compactness, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or a detached closure operator.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[First-countable sequential-closure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:first-countable-sequential-closure-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FirstCountableSequentialClosureUp$ carrier
$$
F=(B,T,Q,L,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FirstCountableSequentialClosureUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the first-countable local basis, Frechet tail, sequential-closure witness, real-sequence-limit handoff, stream windows, regular-rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item local-basis coverage, requiring every closure-neighbourhood request to read the displayed $\FirstCountableSpaceUp$ row $B$ before it can ask for sequence data;
\item Frechet-tail exactness, requiring the cofinite-tail row $T$ to supply the tail threshold used by the same neighbourhood request;
\item sequential-witness exposure, requiring $Q$ to be the only row that exports the displayed sequence-closure witness downstream of $B,T$;
\item real-limit handoff, requiring $L,W,R,D$ to be consumed before the terminal Real seal $E$ is exposed;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve and source exactly the displayed rows componentwise;
\item non-escape, excluding host point equality, selected subsequences, quotient filters, open-cover compactness, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, and detached host closure.
\end{enumerate}
No first-countable sequential-closure obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $F=(B,T,Q,L,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes exactly the local first-countable basis, the Frechet tail, the sequential-closure witness, the real-sequence-limit handoff, the stream windows, the regular readback, the dyadic ledger, the Real seal, and the structural rows.

The closure request first consumes the local basis row $B$. This row fixes the displayed point-window and the countable neighbourhood ledger, so the request cannot replace the local basis by a host family of neighbourhoods.

The Frechet tail row $T$ then supplies the cofinite threshold for the same request. Once the threshold is displayed, the sequential witness row $Q$ reads sequence entries through that tail budget rather than through a selected subsequence or quotient filter.

The real-valued limit side is downstream of the witness. The handoff row $L$, window ledger $W$, regular readback $R$, and dyadic ledger $D$ must all be read before the terminal Real seal $E$ becomes visible.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only that finite route. Since host point equality, selected subsequences, quotient filters, open-cover compactness, countable choice, ambient real completeness, and detached host closure have no carrier coordinate, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[First-countable sequential-closure route]
\label{thm:first-countable-sequential-closure-route}
Let
$$
F=(B,T,Q,L,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\FirstCountableSequentialClosureUp$ carrier. Every consumer that reads the displayed closure witness factors through the finite route
$$
B,T \longmapsto Q \longmapsto L,W,R,D \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the consumer receives a $\FirstCountableSpaceUp$ local basis, a $\FrechetFilterUp$ tail-neighbourhood row, a $\SequentialClosureUp$ witness, a $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ handoff, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, a terminal $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming. It receives no host point equality, selected subsequence, quotient filter, open-cover compactness principle, countable-choice row, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, or detached closure operator.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted consumer read of the closure witness. Carrier admission gives access to the witness row $Q$ only together with the local-basis row $B$ and the Frechet-tail row $T$.

Replay the route through $C$. The consumer first reads the point-window and local neighbourhood ledger from $B$, then the cofinite threshold and tail-neighbourhood surface from $T$, and only then reaches the displayed sequential witness $Q$.

The real-limit data remain downstream. The rows $L,W,R,D$ expose the real sequence limit, finite windows, regular rational readback, and dyadic tolerance accounting before the Real seal $E$ is reached.

Transport by $H$, provenance by $P$, and local naming by $N$ preserve that same order. No remaining carrier row can export host point equality, a selected subsequence, quotient filter equality, open-cover compactness, countable choice, ambient Real completeness, or detached host closure, so every accepted consumer factors through the displayed route.
\end{proof}
