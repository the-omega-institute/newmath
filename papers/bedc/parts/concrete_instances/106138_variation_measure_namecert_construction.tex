\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\VariationMeasureUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-variation-measure-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following the finite-partition side of Jordan bounded variation as treated by Rudin and Folland, the $\VariationMeasureUp$ packet records the ledger that a bounded-variation consumer may read before any passage to classical measure theory. It sits over the bounded-variation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedvariation-namecert}, the Jordan signed-variation split of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-jordan-variation-decomposition-namecert}, the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular rational stream surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the dyadic observation rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The packet names only displayed finite partition ledgers, finite edge sums, common refinements, and their visible transport rows.

\begin{definition}[VariationMeasure finite-partition carrier]
\label{def:variation-measure-finite-partition-carrier}
A $\VariationMeasureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(B,J,\Pi,E,X,D,V,A,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the source $\BoundedVariationUp$ packet and its finite partition-variation ledger.
\item $J$ is the optional $\JordanVariationDecompositionUp$ signed ledger read on the same displayed partition surface.
\item $\Pi$ is a displayed ordered finite partition with endpoint and edge rows.
\item $E$ is the finite edge ledger induced by adjacent endpoints of $\Pi$.
\item $X$ records endpoint $\RealUp$ rows or their $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-name representatives.
\item $D$ records the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ edge observations used to compare adjacent endpoint values.
\item $V$ is the finite dyadic variation-sum ledger on $\Pi$.
\item $A$ records finite additivity rows for displayed concatenations and subpartition blocks.
\item $R$ records common-refinement rows comparing transported partitions, edge ledgers, and finite sums.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the bounded-variation source, Jordan split, partition, endpoint, dyadic, finite-sum, additivity, refinement, package, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a bounded-variation request to endpoints, edge observations, finite sums, refinement comparison, and consumer exposure.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\VariationMeasureUp$, $\BoundedVariationUp$, $\JordanVariationDecompositionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\VariationMeasureUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $B,J,\Pi,E,X,D,V,A,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a sigma-additive measure, a host supremum over all partitions, quotient function equality, selected limits, ambient Real completeness, hidden partition families, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[VariationMeasure finite partition additivity]
\label{thm:variation-measure-finite-partition-additivity}
Let $M=(B,J,\Pi,E,X,D,V,A,R,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\VariationMeasureUp$ carrier. If $\Pi$ is displayed as the concatenation of finite subpartition blocks $\Pi_0,\ldots,\Pi_k$ whose shared endpoints are transported by the visible $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows, then the public variation-measure read on $\Pi$ is the finite sum of the block reads recorded in $A$. Each block read uses only the source $\BoundedVariationUp$ ledger, the optional $\JordanVariationDecompositionUp$ signed ledger, endpoint rows from $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ edge observations, and the local $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert_{\VariationMeasureUp}$ provenance.

The additivity statement is restricted to the displayed finite partition. It exports no sigma-additive measure, host supremum over all partitions, quotient function equality, selected limit, or ambient Real-completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:variation-measure-finite-partition-carrier}. The rows available to a consumer are the source bounded-variation packet, the optional Jordan signed ledger, the displayed partition, endpoint names, dyadic edge observations, finite variation sum, additivity rows, refinement rows, and visible provenance.

For each displayed block $\Pi_i$, read its endpoint rows through $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ and its adjacent-edge comparisons through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$. The source bounded-variation ledger supplies the finite edge-sum surface, while the Jordan ledger, when present, supplies the signed split on the same finite edge list.

The additivity row $A$ concatenates those finite block sums inside the same partition ledger. Shared endpoints are identified only by the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows, so no unrecorded endpoint or selected limit is introduced.

Finally apply the no-escape clause built into the carrier. Since every summand is a displayed finite dyadic row and the packet has no coordinate for sigma-additivity, host suprema, quotient function equality, selected limits, or ambient Real completeness, the result is a finite-partition additivity certificate rather than a measure-theoretic extension.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[VariationMeasure common-refinement classifier stability]
\label{thm:variation-measure-common-refinement-classifier-stability}
Let $M$ and $M'$ be accepted $\VariationMeasureUp$ packets. Suppose their source $\BoundedVariationUp$ rows, optional $\JordanVariationDecompositionUp$ rows, endpoint $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ names, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ edge observations, finite variation-sum ledgers, additivity rows, $\hsame$ rows, $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows agree after transport to a displayed common finite refinement. Then the local $\VariationMeasureUp$ classifier identifies $M$ and $M'$ on that refinement, and every public finite-partition read factors through the same transported refinement ledger.

This classifier stability uses no sigma-additive measure, host supremum over all partitions, quotient function equality, selected limit, ambient Real completeness, hidden partition family, or bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the displayed common finite refinement. Its rows provide the only comparison surface for the two packets: transported source bounded-variation data, optional Jordan signed ledgers, endpoint names, dyadic observations, finite sums, additivity rows, and provenance.

Use \autoref{thm:variation-measure-finite-partition-additivity} on each packet after transport to the common refinement. Both public reads are finite sums over the same displayed block and edge rows, and the shared endpoints are handled by the same $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes.

Repack these componentwise comparisons into the local classifier data. Because the carrier has no additional coordinate beyond $B,J,\Pi,E,X,D,V,A,R,H,C,P,N$, the common-refinement comparison exhausts the classifier.

The excluded objects cannot distinguish the packets. None is present among the carrier rows, and none is generated by the finite additivity or transport steps.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[VariationMeasure bounded-variation dependency route]
\label{thm:variation-measure-bounded-variation-dependency-route}
Every $\VariationMeasureUp$ public read factors through a displayed $\BoundedVariationUp$ partition ledger and, when a signed split is requested, through the displayed $\JordanVariationDecompositionUp$ finite signed ledger. Endpoint values are read only through $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$, and adjacent-edge variation rows are read only through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$. The packet exposes those sibling rows as finite additivity and common-refinement data with visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the source row $B$ from \autoref{def:variation-measure-finite-partition-carrier}. By the bounded-variation chapter, the available variation data are finite partition ledgers, endpoint rows, dyadic edge observations, finite sums, refinement rows, and visible provenance.

If the signed row $J$ is present, read it through the Jordan variation decomposition chapter. That row only splits the same displayed edge ledger into positive and negative finite ledgers and reconstructs the displayed endpoint-difference row.

Endpoint rows remain on the $\RealUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ surfaces, while edge comparisons remain on $\DyadicRatCoreUp$. The $\VariationMeasureUp$ carrier packages these rows into finite additivity and common-refinement certificates without adding a host measure object.
\end{proof}
