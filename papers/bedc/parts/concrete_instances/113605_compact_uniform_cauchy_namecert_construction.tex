\begin{definition}[CompactUniformCauchy carrier]
\label{def:compact-uniform-cauchy-carrier}

$$

$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the compact metric source row, read as finite net data rather than as an open-cover compactness principle;
\item $F$ is the family or stream of observed real-valued rows over the compact source;
\item $W$ is the finite tail-window row, naming the indices and windows exposed to a uniform-Cauchy consumer;
\item $R$ is the regular rational readback row through $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$;
\item $D$ is the dyadic tolerance row supplied by $\DyadicRatCoreUp$;
\item $U$ is the uniform Cauchy tail row, recording one displayed tail bound for the whole compact finite net;
\item $E$ is the downstream $\RealUp$ seal row reached after the finite tail and tolerance readback;
\item $H$ stores the componentwise $\hsame$ and $\psame$ transports for the compact, tail, tolerance, uniformity, seal, provenance, and naming rows;

\begin{theorem}[CompactUniformCauchy NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-cauchy-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted carrier $Q$ displaying the packet $(K,F,W,R,D,U,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactUniformCauchyUp}$ surface consists of finite obligations for compact-net exposure, family-row exposure, uniform tail-window exactness, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance exactness, Real seal nonescape, componentwise transport, route replay, package provenance, and local naming. Each obligation is read from the displayed coordinates $K,F,W,R,D,U,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier coordinates. The compact row, family row, tail window, regular readback, dyadic tolerance, uniform tail, and Real seal are all explicit entries of the finite packet.

The transport and replay rows $H$ and $C$ determine how those entries move between certified presentations. They expose only componentwise sameness and displayed continuation routes, so a consumer cannot insert an open-cover choice, quotient stream, selected limit, or host equality proof.

The provenance and local naming rows $P,N$ bind the packet to the cited sibling chapters. Repacking these rows gives exactly the stated finite NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompactUniformCauchy finite-net extraction]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-cauchy-finite-net-extraction}
Every uniform-Cauchy read of an accepted $\CompactUniformCauchyUp$ carrier first extracts the compact finite-net source row $K$, then reads the family row $F$, the tail-window row $W$, the regular readback row $R$, the dyadic tolerance row $D$, and the uniform tail row $U$ through $H,C,P,N$. The extraction exports finite net and tail data only; it does not export open-cover compactness, selected subcovers outside the displayed packet, countable choice, quotient point equality, or a host uniform modulus.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the compact source coordinate $K$. By definition it is the displayed compact-metric finite-net row, not an ambient compactness principle.

Next read the analytic rows $F,W,R,D,U$. These rows move from observed family data to finite tail windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance, and one uniform tail bound over the displayed finite net.

Finally use $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name that same route. Since no other coordinate is present, finite-net extraction is exhausted by the packet and cannot supply the excluded choice, quotient, subcover, equality, or host-modulus data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompactUniformCauchy Real seal nonescape]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-cauchy-real-seal-nonescape}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The only routes to $E$ are the displayed continuation routes in $C$ transported by $H$ and sourced by $P,N$. Those routes force the consumer to pass through $K,F,W,R,D,U$ before the seal can be read.

Therefore the seal is a terminal row of the finite packet. It cannot be detached into a completeness theorem, selected limit, host real equality, or quotient-stream representative.
