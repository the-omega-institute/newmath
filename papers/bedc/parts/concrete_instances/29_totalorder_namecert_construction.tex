\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\TotalOrderUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-totalorder-namecert}

The total order interface \(\TotalOrderUp\) extends \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-poset-namecert} with the trichotomy field, expressed constructively: for any \(a, b\) in the carrier, the carrier's apartness predicate decides \(a < b\), \(a = b\) (under the classifier), or \(a > b\). The classical formulation (LEM-style "either \(a \le b\) or \(b \le a\)") is replaced by decidable comparison so that the total-order certificate remains 0-axiom.

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[TotalOrder carrier]
\label{def:totalorder-carrier}
Use a poset setup extended with constructive comparison data returning less, same, or greater.
\end{definition}

\section{Concrete singleton history total order instance}
\label{sec:totalorder-concrete-singleton-history-instance}

\begin{definition}[Concrete singleton history total order instance]
\label{def:concrete-singleton-history-totalorder-instance}
Let $A_{\emp}$ be the singleton history source of
Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source} at $a_0=\emp$. Define
$$
\rho_{\emp}(h,k):=
A_{\emp}(h)\land A_{\emp}(k)\land\hsame(h,k),
$$
and let the order relation on carried endpoints be
$$
h\le_{\emp}k:=\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k).
$$
The constructive comparison package has three branches:
$$
\mathsf{lt}_{\emp}(h,k):=\bot,\qquad
\mathsf{same}_{\emp}(h,k):=\rho_{\emp}(h,k),\qquad
\mathsf{gt}_{\emp}(h,k):=\bot.
$$
Thus the less and greater branches are empty, while the same branch stores the
singleton classifier witness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton history total order laws]
\label{thm:singleton-history-totalorder-laws}
For carried histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $A_{\emp}(h)$ and
$A_{\emp}(k)$, the comparison package of
\autoref{def:concrete-singleton-history-totalorder-instance} always returns
the same branch carrying $\rho_{\emp}(h,k)$. The resulting tuple satisfies the
$\TotalOrderUp$ carrier field, the $\POSetUp$ fields, classifier stability,
strict-to-apartness, trichotomy, and comparison stability.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier data are inherited from the singleton history source. The
hypotheses $A_{\emp}(h)$ and $A_{\emp}(k)$ unfold to $\hsame(h,\emp)$ and
$\hsame(k,\emp)$; symmetry and transitivity of $\hsame$ compose these endpoint
witnesses into $\hsame(h,k)$. Together with the two carrier facts, this is
exactly $\rho_{\emp}(h,k)$.

The preorder comparisons in both directions are supplied by
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE\_of\_hsame}$ from the displayed
$\hsame(h,k)$ witness and its symmetric form. These witnesses give the
$\POSetUp$ order relation on the restricted singleton carrier.

Antisymmetry is the concrete prefix collapse:
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE\_antisymm\_hsame}$ sends opposing prefix comparisons
to $\hsame(h,k)$, which repackages with the carrier facts as the classifier
$\rho_{\emp}(h,k)$. Classifier stability and comparison stability use the same
endpoint transport through $\emp$.

The strict branches are false by definition, so strict-to-apartness is vacuous.
Trichotomy is the same branch just constructed; no classical comparison
principle is used.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton total-order comparison exactness]
\label{thm:singleton-totalorder-comparison-exactness}
For carried histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $A_{\emp}(h)$ and
$A_{\emp}(k)$ in the concrete instance of
\autoref{def:concrete-singleton-history-totalorder-instance}, the comparison
branches are exact in the following sense:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\mathsf{lt}_{\emp}(h,k)\to\mathsf{False}$ and
  $\mathsf{gt}_{\emp}(h,k)\to\mathsf{False}$.
  \item $\mathsf{same}_{\emp}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow\rho_{\emp}(h,k)$.
  \item $\mathsf{same}_{\emp}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow
  (h\le_{\emp}k\land k\le_{\emp}h)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the three comparison branches in
\autoref{def:concrete-singleton-history-totalorder-instance}. The strict
branches are both $\bot$, so each strict witness eliminates to
$\mathsf{False}$.

The same branch is defined to be $\rho_{\emp}(h,k)$, giving the second
equivalence by reflexive unfolding.

For the opposing-prefix characterization, apply the exactness part of
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-poset-laws} to the carried histories $h$ and $k$.
Composing that equivalence with the displayed unfolding
$\mathsf{same}_{\emp}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow\rho_{\emp}(h,k)$ gives the stated
equivalence with $h\le_{\emp}k$ and $k\le_{\emp}h$.
\end{proof}

\section{Concrete unary prefix total order instance}
\label{sec:totalorder-concrete-unary-prefix-instance}

\begin{definition}[Concrete unary prefix total order instance]
\label{def:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-instance}
Let the carrier be the concrete unary-history preorder carrier:
$$
\mathsf{Carrier}_{\mathsf{u}}:=\mathsf{PreorderCarrier}
=\mathsf{UnaryHistory}.
$$
Let the carrier classifier be
$$
\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k):=
\mathsf{PreorderCarrierClassifierSpec}(\mathsf{PreorderCarrier})(h,k),
$$
and let the order relation be the concrete prefix relation
$$
h\le_{\mathsf{u}}k:=\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k).
$$
The constructive comparison package has the three branches
$$
\mathsf{lt}_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k):=\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(h,k),
\qquad
\mathsf{same}_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k):=\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k),
\qquad
\mathsf{gt}_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k):=\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(k,h).
$$
Thus strict-left means that $k$ extends $h$ by a nonempty unary tail, same is
the carrier classifier, and strict-right is the opposite strict unary prefix.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete unary prefix total order laws]
\label{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-laws}
For carried unary histories $h,k$ in
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\mathsf{u}}$, the branches of
\autoref{def:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-instance} supply constructive
total comparison. Each strict branch implies carrier-classifier apartness, the
same branch satisfies
$$
\mathsf{same}_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow
(\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k)\land\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(k,h)),
$$
and the inherited preorder and antisymmetry fields assemble these data into a
concrete $\TotalOrderUp$ law package over unary histories. The strict comparison
row is the isolated branch transitivity of
\autoref{thm:totalorder-strict-branch-transitivity}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the total unary prefix comparison of
\autoref{thm:nat-unary-prefix-total}. For carried histories $h$ and $k$, this
returns a unary continuation from $h$ to $k$ or one from $k$ to $h$.

Split the selected unary tail by the unary-generation cases. A nonempty tail
chooses the corresponding strict branch
$\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(h,k)$ or
$\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(k,h)$. An empty tail collapses the continuation
to $\hsame(h,k)$, which repackages with the two carrier witnesses as
$\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k)$ and therefore chooses the same branch.

The strict branches are apart from the same branch. If a strict-left witness and
the carrier classifier both held, the classifier exactness of
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness} would give the reverse prefix
comparison as well; splitting that reverse continuation into empty or nonempty
form either collapses the strict tail to $\emp$ or gives opposing strict
prefixes, both ruled out by \autoref{thm:nat-unary-strict-prefix-asymm}. The
strict-right case is symmetric.

The displayed equivalence for the same branch is exactly the prefix classifier
exactness theorem \autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness} after
unfolding $\mathsf{same}_{\mathsf{u}}$. Relation stability, reflexivity, and
transitivity are inherited from
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-preorder-laws}, while antisymmetry is the
same exactness field of \autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}.

For strict-comparison transitivity, use
\autoref{thm:totalorder-strict-branch-transitivity}. It applies the concrete
strict unary-prefix transitivity field to the strict-left branch and the same
argument with reversed endpoints to the strict-right branch. These fields repack
into the concrete $\TotalOrderUp$ law package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete unary prefix total-order comparison partition]
\label{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-comparison-partition}
For carried unary histories $h,k$ in
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\mathsf{u}}$, exactly one of the three concrete branches
$$
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(h,k),
\qquad
\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k),
\qquad
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(k,h)
$$
supplies the comparison branch. The middle branch is exact:
$$
\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow
(\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k)\land\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(k,h)).
$$
The strict-left and strict-right branches cannot both hold, and neither strict
branch is compatible with $\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k)$.

The per-object fields are: traditional target constructive total-order branch
partition; BEDC source $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$, and
$\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\mathsf{u}}$; classifier $\rho_{\mathsf{u}}$; stability
obligations branch totality and incompatibility; ledger selected comparison
branch; core theorem this partition; exactness the same-branch equivalence;
standard bridge deferred; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the total unary prefix comparison of
\autoref{thm:nat-unary-prefix-total}. For carried histories $h$ and $k$, it gives
a unary continuation in one direction, and the unary tail is then split into
empty and nonempty cases.

If the selected tail is nonempty from $h$ to $k$, the selected branch is
$\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(h,k)$. If the selected tail is nonempty from $k$
to $h$, the selected branch is $\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(k,h)$.

If the selected tail is empty, the continuation collapses to the same-history
case. The carrier classifier is exactly the same-history branch, and
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness} identifies it with the pair of
prefix comparisons in both directions.

The two strict branches are incompatible by
\autoref{thm:nat-unary-strict-prefix-asymm}. A strict branch together with
$\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k)$ would, by
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}, also provide the opposite
prefix comparison; splitting that opposite comparison yields either an empty
tail contradicting strictness or the opposing strict branch, again ruled out by
\autoref{thm:nat-unary-strict-prefix-asymm}. The strict-right case is symmetric.

These cases give total coverage, the exact middle branch, and pairwise
incompatibility, so the three branches form the concrete constructive
partition.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete unary prefix total-order branch transport]
\label{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-branch-transport}
For carried unary histories $h,h',k,k'$ in
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\mathsf{u}}$, suppose
$\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,h')$ and $\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(k,k')$. Then classifier
replacement transports the three concrete comparison branches exactly:
$$
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(h',k'),
$$
$$
\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h',k'),
$$
$$
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(k,h)\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(k',h').
$$
The per-object fields are: traditional target constructive total-order branch
transport; BEDC source $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ plus
$\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}$ and $\rho_{\mathsf{u}}$; carrier
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\mathsf{u}}$; classifier $\rho_{\mathsf{u}}$; stability
obligation comparison transport; ledger endpoint replacement witnesses; core
theorem this block; exactness the three displayed iff rows; standard bridge
finite unary word total order; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the two classifier witnesses. The witness
$\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,h')$ gives the endpoint comparison $\hsame(h,h')$, and
$\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(k,k')$ gives $\hsame(k,k')$.

For the strict-left row, the forward direction applies
\autoref{thm:nat-unary-strict-prefix-cont-hsame-transport} to the strict
witness from $h$ to $k$ with endpoint witnesses $\hsame(h,h')$ and
$\hsame(k,k')$.

The reverse direction uses the same transport theorem with the symmetric
endpoint witnesses $\hsame(h',h)$ and $\hsame(k',k)$.

For the classifier row, compose the endpoint comparison $\hsame(h,h')$, the
middle comparison extracted from $\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k)$, and the symmetric
form of $\hsame(k,k')$. This gives $\hsame(h',k')$, which repackages with the
transported carrier witnesses as $\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h',k')$.

The reverse classifier direction is the same composition with the two endpoint
replacement witnesses reversed. Symmetry and transitivity of the carrier
classifier recover $\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k)$.

For the strict-right row, read the strict witness as a strict unary prefix from
$k$ to $h$. Apply the strict-prefix transport theorem with endpoints
$\hsame(k,k')$ and $\hsame(h,h')$.

The reverse strict-right direction again uses the symmetric endpoint witnesses.
Thus all three comparison branches are preserved and reflected under concrete
classifier replacement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete unary strict branch transitivity]
\label{thm:totalorder-strict-branch-transitivity}
For carried unary histories $h,k,l$ in $\mathsf{Carrier}_{\mathsf{u}}$,
strict-left comparison is transitive:
$$
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(h,k)\land
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(k,l)
\to
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(h,l).
$$
The strict-right branch is transitive in the same concrete carrier:
$$
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(k,h)\land
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(l,k)
\to
\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(l,h).
$$
Consequently $\mathsf{lt}_{\mathsf{u}}$ and $\mathsf{gt}_{\mathsf{u}}$ are
transitive on the concrete unary prefix $\TotalOrderUp$ instance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the strict-left branch of
\autoref{def:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-instance}. The two hypotheses are
exactly a strict unary prefix from $h$ to $k$ and a strict unary prefix from $k$
to $l$. Apply
\autoref{thm:nat-unary-strict-prefix-trans-composite-tail}; its composite tail
witness gives $\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(h,l)$.

For strict-right comparison, unfold
$\mathsf{gt}_{\mathsf{u}}(x,y):=\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(y,x)$. The
hypotheses become strict unary prefixes from $l$ to $k$ and from $k$ to $h$.
Applying the same composite-tail transitivity theorem yields
$\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(l,h)$.

The carrier witnesses for $h$, $k$, and $l$ are not consumed by the strict-prefix
composition itself; they keep the conclusion inside the concrete
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\mathsf{u}}$ total-order package. The two displayed
implications are therefore precisely the transitivity fields for
$\mathsf{lt}_{\mathsf{u}}$ and $\mathsf{gt}_{\mathsf{u}}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete unary prefix $\TotalOrderUp$ naming certificate]
\label{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-namecert}
The tuple of \autoref{def:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-instance} supplies
the source, pattern, classifier, stability, ledger, and exactness rows for a
$\NameCert$ licensing $\TotalOrderUp$ over
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\mathsf{u}}=\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, classifier
$\rho_{\mathsf{u}}$, order $\le_{\mathsf{u}}$, and comparison branches
$\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(h,k)$, $\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k)$, and
$\mathsf{NatUnaryStrictPrefix}(k,h)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The source and inherited order rows are the certified unary-prefix $\POSetUp$
package. Apply \autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-namecert}; it supplies
the $\mathsf{BHist}$ source, prefix pattern, classifier, stability, ledger, and
exactness rows used by the total-order carrier.

The classifier and exactness rows are fixed by
\autoref{thm:poset-prefix-classifier-exactness}: on carried unary histories,
$\rho_{\mathsf{u}}(h,k)$ is equivalent to the two opposing prefix comparisons
$h\le_{\mathsf{u}}k$ and $k\le_{\mathsf{u}}h$.

The stability row is the concrete total-order law package of
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-laws}. It supplies the
strict-to-apartness obligations, relation stability, constructive total
comparison, comparison stability, and strict-comparison transitivity for the
three displayed branches. The strict-comparison transitivity component is the
concrete branch law of \autoref{thm:totalorder-strict-branch-transitivity}, and
comparison stability is the branch transport theorem of
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-branch-transport}.

The ledger row is the selected branch certificate. By
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-comparison-partition}, each pair
of carried unary histories chooses exactly one of the strict-left, same, and
strict-right branches, and the same branch is the exact classifier branch.

Repack these rows as the public $\NameCert$: the source is
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, the pattern is the three-way unary-prefix comparison,
the classifier is $\rho_{\mathsf{u}}$, the stability data are the cited law
fields, the ledger is the selected comparison branch, and exactness is the
middle-branch equivalence.
\end{proof}

The per-object template is: traditional target concrete unary constructive
total order; BEDC source unary $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$\mathsf{Carrier}_{\mathsf{u}}=\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$; classifier
$\rho_{\mathsf{u}}$; order $\le_{\mathsf{u}}$; pattern strict-left,
same, and strict-right unary-prefix comparison; stability obligations from
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-totalorder-namecert}; ledger the selected
comparison branch certificate; exactness same branch iff opposing prefix
comparisons; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

The $\TotalOrderUp$ schema is represented by this concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$
carrier and explicit comparison data, giving later Lean formalization a
$\mathsf{BHist}$-anchored target rather than an abstract parameter schema.

\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[TotalOrder source specification]
\label{def:totalorder-source-specification}
The source is a certified poset carrier plus apartness-aware trichotomy.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[TotalOrder pattern specification]
\label{def:totalorder-pattern-specification}
The pattern is comparison formation producing one of three certified comparison cases.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[TotalOrder classifier specification]
\label{def:totalorder-classifier-specification}
The classifier is the poset carrier classifier, with apartness stable under classifier replacement.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[TotalOrder stability certificate]
\label{def:totalorder-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item all poset stability fields
  \item strict-order-to-apartness field
  \item trichotomy as explicit comparison data
  \item comparison stability under classifier replacement
  \item transitivity of strict comparison where supplied
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[TotalOrder ledger policy]
\label{def:totalorder-ledger-policy}
Record the comparison certificate selected for each pair.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[\(\TotalOrderUp\) certificate obligations]
\label{def:totalorder-certificate-obligations}
The carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data listed above are the obligations required for the interface name \(\TotalOrderUp\).
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete singleton history $\TotalOrderUp$ naming certificate]
\label{thm:concrete-singleton-history-totalorder-namecert}
The concrete tuple of
\autoref{def:concrete-singleton-history-totalorder-instance} supplies the
source, pattern, classifier, stability, ledger, and exactness rows for a
$\NameCert$ licensing $\TotalOrderUp$ over the singleton carrier $A_{\emp}$,
classifier $\rho_{\emp}$, order $\le_{\emp}$, and comparison branches
$\mathsf{lt}_{\emp}:=\bot$, $\mathsf{same}_{\emp}:=\rho_{\emp}$, and
$\mathsf{gt}_{\emp}:=\bot$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row is the singleton $\mathsf{BHist}$ source. Apply
\autoref{thm:singleton-history-totalorder-laws} to obtain the carried endpoint
facts, classifier stability, the $\POSetUp$ fields, trichotomy, and comparison
stability for the displayed branches.

The exactness row is the underlying partial-order exactness supplied by
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-poset-laws}: on carried histories,
$\rho_{\emp}(h,k)$ is equivalent to the two opposing prefix comparisons
$h\le_{\emp}k$ and $k\le_{\emp}h$.

The strict rows contribute no extra witnesses. Both strict comparison branches
are empty by definition, so strict-to-apartness and strict transitivity are
vacuous.

It remains to assemble the public certificate. Repack the source as
$A_{\emp}$, the pattern as the three-way comparison formation, the classifier
as $\rho_{\emp}$, the stability data as the fields just cited, and the ledger
as the selected same-branch comparison certificate.
\end{proof}

The per-object template is: traditional target singleton constructive total
order; BEDC source singleton $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier
$\rho_{\emp}$; stability obligations from
\autoref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-totalorder-namecert}; ledger the
selected same-branch comparison certificate; exactness singleton classifier
iff opposing prefix comparisons; standard bridge singleton total order; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\paragraph{Primitive boundary.}
\(\TotalOrderUp\) is not a BEDC primitive. It is admitted only when those obligations are supplied.
