\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-criticallinewitness-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ interface records the finite packet a constructive Riemann Hypothesis witness would expose after a zero has already been supplied in the critical strip. It does not prove $\mathsf{RH}$ and does not produce a zero. It only names the BEDC-native surface of the witness type in \autoref{thm:rh-witness-modulus} and the restriction type in \autoref{thm:rh-witness-critical-line-restriction}.

\begin{definition}[Critical-line witness carrier]
\label{def:critical-line-witness-carrier}
A $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Z$ is the supplied $\ZetaZerosUp$ zero row, displaying the zero history and its zero-classifier witness;
\item $S$ is the supplied $\CritStripUp$ critical-strip row for the same complex history, displaying the left and right strip comparisons;
\item $M$ is the on-critical-line modulus row, sending each depth $n:\NatUp$ to an inspected rational depth at which the real-part representative is within $2^{-n}$ of $1/2$;
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ real-part row consumed by the modulus;
\item $Q$ is the $\RatUp$ comparison ledger containing the half-line rational row, dyadic tolerance row, and strict comparison row used by $M$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the zero row, critical-strip row, real-part row, rational comparison ledger, and modulus row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which consumers inspect those rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ZetaZerosUp$, $\CritStripUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the supplied analytic certificates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes a witness packet for a supplied zero-in-strip input. It contains no proof that every zero in the critical strip has such a packet, no zero-producing search, no host complex equality, no quotient real equality, no selected representative of a Cauchy class, and no appeal to ambient analytic completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CriticalLineWitnessUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet of \autoref{def:critical-line-witness-carrier};
\item zero-strip compatibility, stating that the $\ZetaZerosUp$ row $Z$ and $\CritStripUp$ row $S$ name the same supplied complex history through displayed $\hsame$ transport;
\item modulus discipline, stating that every depth request is answered by the on-critical-line modulus row $M$ using only the real-part row $R$ and rational comparison ledger $Q$;
\item non-escape discipline, stating that consumers reach the critical-line conclusion only through $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$;
\item ledger exactness, stating that the public witness surface is exhausted by the displayed zero, strip, modulus, comparison, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of the finite packet in \autoref{def:critical-line-witness-carrier}. The packet lists the supplied zero row, critical-strip row, modulus row, real-part row, rational comparison ledger, transports, continuations, package provenance, and local naming row.

Zero-strip compatibility uses the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$ to bind $Z$ and $S$ to the same supplied complex history. No host complex equality is read, and no independent zero search is introduced.

Modulus discipline is the row $M$. A depth request $n$ is routed to the inspected real-part representative in $R$ and to the dyadic comparison rows in $Q$, yielding the critical-line approximation at that depth.

The non-escape and ledger rows then use $C$, $P$, and $N$. Since the packet has no quotient, representative-selection, host-equality, or analytic-completeness coordinate, every public witness read is exhausted by the displayed finite rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness modulus row]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-modulus-row}
For an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ carrier
$$
W=(Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
each request $n:\NatUp$ is answered by the displayed modulus coordinate $M(n)$ and the $\RatUp$ comparison ledger $Q$. The resulting read states that the rational representative of the $\RealUp$ real-part row $R$ at the inspected depth lies within the dyadic tolerance $2^{-n}$ of the half-line row $1/2$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and inspect the modulus coordinate. The only field that answers a depth request is $M$, and its target is the inspected real-part representative carried by $R$.

The rational comparison is then read from $Q$. The half-line row, dyadic tolerance row, and strict comparison row are all visible coordinates of the same packet, so the read remains at the $\RatUp$ level.

Transport through $H$ and continuation through $C$ preserve exactly these rows. The package and local naming fields $P,N$ record provenance and membership, but they add no analytic proof of $\mathsf{RH}$ and no off-ledger equality principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness non-escape boundary]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-nonescape-boundary}
Every public critical-line read exported by an accepted $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ carrier factors through $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$. It cannot read a host complex equality, quotient real equality, selected Cauchy representative, zero-producing search, global zero-set classifier, or ambient analytic completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier inventory. The packet contains exactly the supplied zero row, critical-strip row, modulus row, real-part row, rational comparison ledger, transport rows, continuation rows, package provenance, and local naming row.

The critical-line conclusion is consumed through the modulus row and rational comparison ledger. The zero and strip rows provide the supplied input context, while $H$ and $C$ provide only displayed transport and route data.

None of the excluded objects appears as a coordinate of the carrier. Therefore a public read has no route by which host equality, quotient equality, representative selection, zero search, global zero-set classification, or ambient completeness can enter the witness packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Critical-line witness ledger exactness]
\label{thm:critical-line-witness-ledger-exactness}
The public $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ ledger of an accepted carrier is exactly the finite packet
$$
Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N.
$$
It exposes a BEDC-native witness that a supplied $\ZetaZerosUp$ row in a supplied $\CritStripUp$ row carries an on-critical-line modulus over $\RealUp$ and $\RatUp$, and it exposes no proof that all non-trivial zeros admit such a packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the public ledger by projecting the carrier. The rows $Z$ and $S$ provide the supplied zero-in-strip input, and $M,R,Q$ provide the critical-line modulus and its rational comparison surface.

The rows $H$ and $C$ account for the only permitted movement of the displayed data, while $P$ and $N$ record package provenance and local naming. These rows do not add a quantifier over all zeros and do not create a zero.

Consequently the ledger is exact: every exported witness coordinate is one of $Z,S,M,R,Q,H,C,P,N$, and every coordinate in that tuple has a public role in the witness packet.
\end{proof}
