\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion Real-completion finite envelope]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-envelope}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Every $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer entering through $U$ factors through exactly one finite envelope before it can read a real-facing or completion-facing result. The envelope is the displayed route through the $\StreamNameUp$ window family $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows reached by the uniform tail ledger, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $Q$, the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ tail-meet route, the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ public $\RealUp$ export, and the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows carried by the packet and its sibling routes.

Thus the accepted consumer surface has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, or bridge rows outside the displayed finite envelope.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ side. By \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-cauchy-limit-seal-sibling-route}, the only route from the accepted packet to a real-facing seal is the finite sibling surface through $W,M,Q,T,E$ together with the displayed transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.

Next read that surface through the tail-meet route. The uniform selector route of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-tail-meet-uniform-selector-route} places the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\RealObservationBudgetUp$, and $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ rows on one finite envelope over the shared $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ data.

Use the source-exhaustion theorem \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-tail-meet-source-exhaustion}. It says that an admitted $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer has already exhausted the finite source rows supplied by the tail meet, so there is no second source for quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality, hidden family limits, independent completion rows, ambient completeness, or metric-completeness data.

Finally close with the public export theorem \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-completion-public-real-name-export}. The $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ consumer repacks the same finite windows, regular-rational readbacks, dyadic ledger, real-seal route, and structural BEDC rows as a public $\RealUp$ name surface. Since every entry point from $U$ has already been routed through the same tail-meet envelope before this export is read, the Real and completion consumer surface is exactly the displayed finite envelope and has no bridge row outside it.
\end{proof}
