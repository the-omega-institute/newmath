\begin{definition}[UniformCauchyCriterion Real-completion finite-envelope carrier]
\label{def:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-carrier}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet. The Real-completion finite-envelope carrier of $U$ is the packet
$$
\mathcal{E}_{\RealUp}(U)=
(U,W_{\StreamNameUp},R_{\RegSeqRatUp},Q_{\DyadicRatCoreUp},
S_{\CauchyLimitSealUp},E_{\RealCauchyCompletionUp},H,C,P,N).
$$
Its rows are exactly the accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet, the $\StreamNameUp$ window family $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows $R$ reached by the uniform tail ledger, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $Q$, the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ tail-meet route $S$, the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ public $\RealUp$ export $E$, and the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchyCriterionUp}$ rows $H,C,P,N$. A row is admitted to the carrier only when it is displayed by this packet or by one of the named sibling routes attached to the same $U$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion Real-completion finite-envelope NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier $\mathcal{E}_{\RealUp}(U)$ of \autoref{def:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-carrier}, the local $\NameCert$ obligations are the following finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure: every admitted row is one of $U,W_{\StreamNameUp},R_{\RegSeqRatUp},Q_{\DyadicRatCoreUp},S_{\CauchyLimitSealUp},E_{\RealCauchyCompletionUp},H,C,P,N$;
\item window-family stability: $\hsame$ transport of a $\StreamNameUp$ window request remains inside the same displayed $W$ family and is replayed only through the visible $\Cont$ row;
\item regular-rational handoff: every $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row used by a consumer is reached from the uniform tail ledger of $U$ and not from a second stream source;
\item dyadic tolerance ledger exactness: every tolerance comparison consumed at the completion boundary is read from $Q_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ before the tail-meet route is sealed;
\item public $\RealUp$ export: the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ route exports the named $\RealUp$ surface only after $W_{\StreamNameUp}$, $R_{\RegSeqRatUp}$, $Q_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$, and $S_{\CauchyLimitSealUp}$ have been displayed;
\item non-escape: no consumer row carries quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, or a bridge row outside $\mathcal{E}_{\RealUp}(U)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.UniformCauchyCriterionUp.UniformCauchyCriterionPacket\_real\_completion\_finite\_envelope\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier definition. It lists the only admitted rows and records the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ as transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming data rather than analytic sources.

Window stability and the regular-rational handoff are inherited from the existing $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ route: the $\StreamNameUp$ windows are carried by $W$, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows are reached through the uniform tail ledger before any completion-facing read is available.

The dyadic and public-export obligations are downstream of the same route. The tolerance ledger is the displayed $Q_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ row, the tail-meet route is the displayed $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row, and the public $\RealUp$ export is read only through $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ after those rows have been fixed.

Thus every local naming obligation is discharged by one of the displayed rows. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, the excluded host-level, choice, completeness, quotient, and bridge data cannot be introduced by a consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion Real-completion finite-envelope consumer exhaustion]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-consumer-exhaustion}
Every $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer of an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet factors through the finite-envelope carrier $\mathcal{E}_{\RealUp}(U)$ of \autoref{def:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-carrier}. The factorization uses exactly the displayed rows
$$
U,W_{\StreamNameUp},R_{\RegSeqRatUp},Q_{\DyadicRatCoreUp},
S_{\CauchyLimitSealUp},E_{\RealCauchyCompletionUp},H,C,P,N
$$
and no row outside that list can be read by such a consumer.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.UniformCauchyCriterionUp.UniformCauchyCriterionPacket\_real\_completion\_finite\_envelope\_consumer\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer of $U$. The consumer must first read the accepted UniformCauchyCriterion packet, so its family request is already tied to the finite $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$ surface.

Next pass through the real-seal and tail-meet routes. The $\StreamNameUp$ window family, $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger are the rows that feed the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route. No completion-facing read is available before those rows are displayed.

Finally use the public export row. The $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ route repacks the same displayed rows as a public $\RealUp$ surface, while $H,C,P,N$ replay transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming. This exhausts the consumer surface by the finite envelope.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion Real-completion finite-envelope tail-window stability]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-tail-window-stability}
In $\mathcal{E}_{\RealUp}(U)$, every $\StreamNameUp$ window row used by a $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer is tied to the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row under the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows. Transport may rename the window and replay the readback, but it cannot replace $W_{\StreamNameUp}$ or $R_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ by a second window family or a second regular-rational source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the window request inside the carrier. The only window-family row is $W_{\StreamNameUp}$, and the only regular-rational source rows are the displayed $R_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ rows reached from the uniform tail ledger.

Apply the structural rows. The $\hsame$ data compare windows componentwise, while the $\Cont$ row replays the same readback route after transport. Both rows act on the displayed carrier coordinates rather than creating a new source.

Therefore any transported completion-facing consumer still reads the same $W_{\StreamNameUp}$ and $R_{\RegSeqRatUp}$ pair. A second window family or a second regular-rational source has no coordinate in the envelope.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion Real-completion finite-envelope no-choice extraction]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-no-choice-extraction}
The finite-envelope carrier $\mathcal{E}_{\RealUp}(U)$ exports no quotient stream, no selected host limit, no host real equality, no countable-choice selector, no ambient completeness principle, and no metric-completeness data. Any attempted extraction of one of those objects from a $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer is therefore outside the displayed carrier and outside its local $\NameCert$ obligations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier rows. The analytic rows are the StreamName window family, RegSeqRat source rows, dyadic tolerance ledger, CauchyLimitSeal tail-meet route, and RealCauchyCompletion public export. None of these rows stores a quotient stream, a selected limit, host equality, a choice selector, or completeness data.

The structural rows do not add analytic content. $\BHist$ records the visible history surface, $\hsame$ transports displayed rows, $\Cont$ replays displayed continuations, $\Pkg$ records provenance, and $\NameCert$ names the same local packet.

By consumer exhaustion, every $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer has already factored through this list. Hence the listed host-level and choice-based objects cannot be extracted from the envelope.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion Real-completion finite envelope]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-envelope}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Every $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer entering through $U$ factors through exactly one finite envelope before it can read a real-facing or completion-facing result. The envelope is the displayed route through the $\StreamNameUp$ window family $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows reached by the uniform tail ledger, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $Q$, the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ tail-meet route, the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ public $\RealUp$ export, and the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows carried by the packet and its sibling routes.

Thus the accepted consumer surface has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, or bridge rows outside the displayed finite envelope.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.UniformCauchyCriterionUp.UniformCauchyCriterionPacket\_real\_completion\_finite\_envelope}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite-envelope carrier of \autoref{def:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-carrier}. It displays the accepted packet, the $\StreamNameUp$ window family, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows, the dyadic tolerance ledger, the CauchyLimitSeal tail-meet route, the RealCauchyCompletion public export, and the visible structural rows in one packet.

Use the NameCert obligation surface of \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-namecert-obligations}. It records carrier exposure, window-family stability, regular-rational handoff, dyadic tolerance exactness, public $\RealUp$ export, and non-escape as the finite obligations for the same displayed packet.

Next apply consumer exhaustion and tail-window stability. \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-consumer-exhaustion} forces every $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer through the displayed carrier rows, and \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-tail-window-stability} keeps the $\StreamNameUp$ windows tied to the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows under $\hsame$ and $\Cont$.

Finally invoke the no-choice extraction boundary of \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-completion-finite-envelope-no-choice-extraction}. Since the consumer surface has already been exhausted by the carrier and its obligations, the Real and completion-facing surface has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, or bridge rows outside the displayed finite envelope.
\end{proof}
