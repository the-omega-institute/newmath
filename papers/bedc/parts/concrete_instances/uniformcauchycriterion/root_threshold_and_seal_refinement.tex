\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion root threshold schedule determinacy]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-threshold-schedule-determinacy}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet in a $\BHist$ inventory. Every downstream threshold read of $U$ is forced through the single displayed schedule row $M$ after the finite index row $I$ and the shared window-family row $W$ have been read. No downstream consumer may insert a second threshold schedule, read $M$ before $I,W$, or detach a threshold from the route recorded by $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the root tail surface. By \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-tail-coverage}, every downstream family-tail comparison already factors through the finite route $I,W,M,Q,T$.

Now apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-shared-threshold-transport}. The shared-threshold theorem identifies the schedule read as the same displayed row $M$ for every accepted family window over $I,W$.

The structural rows only transport and replay that route. Since $H,C,P,N$ do not add another schedule coordinate, any consumer-visible threshold read is the single row $M$ reached after $I,W$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion root tolerance ledger exactness]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-tolerance-ledger-exactness}
For an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
every dyadic tolerance or tail comparison read visible at the root is exactly a displayed read of the $Q,T$ ledger after the schedule row $M$. Thus the dyadic tolerance surface is the finite ledger
$$
M \longmapsto Q \longmapsto T
$$
over the already displayed rows $I,W$, and it contains no unrecorded tolerance, detached tail bound, quotient-stream comparison, or selected limit.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.UniformCauchyCriterionUp.UniformCauchyCriterionPacket\_root\_tolerance\_ledger\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Start from the threshold route supplied by \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-threshold-schedule-determinacy}. The consumer reaches the same row $M$ after $I,W$ before any dyadic tolerance can be inspected.

Apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-tail-ledger-exactness}. It says that the tolerance and tail evidence is precisely the ledger pair $Q,T$ over that schedule.

The root coverage theorem, \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-tail-coverage}, rules out a second tail source at the same surface. Therefore every root tolerance or tail read is the displayed $Q,T$ ledger and nothing outside it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion root seal consumer non-escape]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-seal-consumer-nonescape}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Every $\RealUp$ seal consumer reaches the seal row $E$ only through the route
$$
I \longmapsto W \longmapsto M \longmapsto Q \longmapsto T \longmapsto E.
$$
The consumer surface cannot read quotient streams, selected limits, host equality, ambient completeness, or any row outside the displayed $\BHist$ package.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the seal through \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-seal-readback}. The seal-facing surface already factors through the finite rows $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$.

Next use \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-threshold-schedule-determinacy} and \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-tolerance-ledger-exactness}. These two root obligations force the path to $E$ through the unique schedule row $M$ and the exact $Q,T$ tolerance ledger.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-seal-consumer-factorization} and \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-seal-route-uniqueness}. The real-facing consumer is exhausted by the displayed route, so quotient streams, selected limits, host equality, and ambient completeness have no coordinate through which to enter.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion root shared tail meet]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-shared-tail-meet}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet whose shared window-family schedule and tolerance ledger are consumed by a $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$ packet. Every $\RealUp$-facing seal read then factors through the same finite rows $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$ and through the sibling shared-tail packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-tail-meet-namecert}. The factorization does not claim RealUp completeness, quotient-stream equality, selected limits, host equality, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with root threshold schedule determinacy. \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-threshold-schedule-determinacy} forces every downstream threshold read through the displayed schedule row $M$ after $I,W$.

Next use tolerance ledger exactness. By \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-tolerance-ledger-exactness}, the dyadic tolerance and tail comparison data consumed after $M$ are exactly the finite $Q,T$ ledger.

Finally apply root seal consumer non-escape, \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-root-seal-consumer-nonescape}. The $\RealUp$-facing seal route reaches $E$ only through $I,W,M,Q,T$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$, while \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-tail-meet-namecert} consumes the same shared-tail surface as a sibling packet rather than as a quotient or completeness theorem.
\end{proof}
