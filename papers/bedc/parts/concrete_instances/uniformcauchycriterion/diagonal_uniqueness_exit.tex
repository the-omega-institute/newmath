\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion diagonal uniqueness exit]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-diagonal-uniqueness-exit}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Suppose its terminal rows are consumed through an accepted $\DiagonalLimitCompatibilityUp$ selector-budget route and through an accepted $\RealLimitUniquenessSealUp$ comparison route, with both routes reading the same displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces.

Then every Phase Real and Completion exit read of $U$ factors through that same finite route. The terminal read uses the stream windows carried by $W$, the regular rational readback carried by $T$, the dyadic tolerance ledger carried by $Q$, and the Real seal carried by $E$, together with the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. No exit read has access to quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, choice data, ambient completeness, or a second completion source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the public export of $U$. By \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-public-non-escape-export}, every downstream consumer is already exhausted by the finite rows $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$, so the exit read has no hidden analytic coordinate before the terminal rows are interpreted.

Next apply the terminal seal envelope, the completion--Real correspondence, and the finite-window extraction route. The envelope keeps the terminal seal downstream of the displayed rows, the correspondence identifies the Real-facing and Completion-facing endpoint reads on the same seal route, and \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-finite-window-real-seal-extraction} extracts the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces from $W,T,Q,E$.

Now attach the diagonal selector-budget route. The diagonal compatibility surface supplies the same finite faces to the UniformCauchyCriterion terminal rows, while the uniqueness-seal comparison consumes those faces through the same selector budget. By \autoref{thm:diagonal-limit-uniform-criterion-terminal-uniqueness-pullback}, the terminal consumer and the uniqueness-seal consumer collapse to one finite pullback route rather than two endpoint sources.

Finally combine this pullback with the non-escape clauses on both sides. Since the shared route has no coordinate for quotient streams, host equality, selected limits, choice data, ambient completeness, or an independent completion object, every Phase Real and Completion exit read of $U$ factors through the same finite $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ faces.
\end{proof}
