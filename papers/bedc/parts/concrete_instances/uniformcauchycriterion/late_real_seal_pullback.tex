\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion late-overlap Real-seal pullback]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-late-overlap-real-seal-pullback}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Every late-overlap $\RealUp$-seal consumer of $U$ pulls back to the same finite route over
$$
I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
The pullback first reads the shared schedule and seal inheritance, then the tail-overlap budget, then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer route, and finally the $\RegSeqRatUp$--$\RealUp$ seal overlap. It exports no second modulus schedule, quotient stream family, selected family limit, host real equality, countable-choice route, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a late-overlap $\RealUp$-seal consumer of $U$. By \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-shared-schedule-real-seal-inheritance}, singleton and paired-family reads inherit the same shared schedule $M$ and the same seal handoff through $E,H,C,P,N$.

Next apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-tail-overlap-budget-exhaustion}. Any overlap budget is exhausted by the displayed rows $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$, so the consumer has no route to a second threshold, tolerance ledger, or tail selector.

Now read the regular-rational side through \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-regseqrat-uniform-consumer-exhaustion}. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer is already factored through the same family ledger, shared windows, shared modulus, dyadic tolerance, tail ledger, seal row, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Finally use \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-regseqrat-real-seal-overlap}. The Real seal and the RegSeqRat readback meet on the same finite package, and the non-escape clauses in the cited overlap route exclude quotient stream families, selected family limits, host equality, choice schedules, and ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion late-overlap RegSeqRat consumer factorization]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-late-overlap-regseqrat-consumer-factorization}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet. Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer that reaches a late-overlap $\RealUp$-seal boundary of $U$ factors through the same route
$$
I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N
$$
and through the Real-seal pullback of \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-late-overlap-real-seal-pullback}. The factorization exposes no independent regular-sequence classifier, quotient stream family, second Real seal, selected family limit, host real equality, countable-choice route, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer at the late-overlap boundary. Apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-regseqrat-uniform-consumer-exhaustion}; the consumer can read only the displayed package $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$.

Next force compatibility with the overlap route. By \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-tail-overlap-budget-exhaustion}, the overlap budget is the same finite tail package, and by \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-shared-schedule-real-seal-inheritance}, the schedule and seal handoff remain the inherited $M$ and $E$ rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-late-overlap-real-seal-pullback}. The Real-facing side has already been pulled back to the same finite route, so the RegSeqRat consumer cannot supply an independent classifier, quotient stream family, second seal, selected limit, host equality, choice schedule, or completeness coordinate.
\end{proof}
