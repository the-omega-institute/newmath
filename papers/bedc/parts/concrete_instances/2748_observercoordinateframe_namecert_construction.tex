The $\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp$ surface names the local coordinate-frame packet that a conscious observer may use without appealing to a global observer frame. It is a finite consumer of the conscious-observer-state row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-consciousobserverstate-namecert} and the observer-locality-cell row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-locality-cell-namecert}, then adds $\MultiHistConfigUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, and $\MaxCausalRateUp$ rows without forming an ambient chart over all observers.

\begin{definition}[Observer-coordinate-frame carrier]
\label{def:observer-coordinate-frame-carrier}
An $\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(S,L,M,C,B,H,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is an accepted $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$ row naming the observer-state surface at which the frame is read;
\item $L$ is an accepted $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ row naming the locality cell through which nearby observer records are coordinated;
\item $M$ is an accepted $\MultiHistConfigUp$ row displaying the finite collection of observer histories available to the frame;
\item $C$ is a finite family of $\CrossHistCausalUp$ rows over selected pairs in $M$ and the locality-cell anchors in $L$;
\item $B$ is an accepted $\MaxCausalRateUp$ row bounding the displayed cross-Hist causal reads without adding a metric tensor or global clock;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport ledger for $S,L,M,C,B$ and for the package and naming rows;
\item $R$ is the $\Cont$ route ledger by which a consumer reads local coordinates through $S,L,M,C,B,H$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$, $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$, $\MultiHistConfigUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, $\MaxCausalRateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two carriers only by the displayed rows and their $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ ledgers. It has no coordinate for a god's-eye observer, a single global chart, a shared external time scalar, a host spacetime manifold, or an observer-independent event object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverCoordinateFrame NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-coordinate-frame-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-coordinate-frame-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations: carrier exposure for the rows $S,L,M,C,B,H,R,P,N$; observer-state anchoring through $S$; locality-cell and multi-Hist anchoring through $L$ and $M$; causal and bound routing through $C$ and $B$; and non-escape from global observer frames, shared clocks, host manifolds, metric tensors, observer-independent events, quotients of histories, and unrecorded histories.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the finite tuple $F=(S,L,M,C,B,H,R,P,N)$.

Observer-state anchoring is read from $S$. Locality-cell and multi-Hist anchoring are read from $L$ and $M$, so every frame coordinate begins inside the displayed observer state and its listed local comparison surface.

The causal and bound obligations are then read from $C$ and $B$. The family $C$ supplies only selected $\CrossHistCausalUp$ rows over the visible configuration, while $B$ supplies the finite $\MaxCausalRateUp$ bound row for those reads.

Finally inspect the remaining coordinates. The transport ledger $H$, route ledger $R$, package row $P$, and local name row $N$ can replay only the displayed finite rows, so no global frame, shared clock, host manifold, metric tensor, observer-independent event, quotient, or hidden history is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverCoordinateFrame locality soundness]
\label{thm:observer-coordinate-frame-locality-soundness}
Every accepted $\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp$ packet is local to the conscious observer state $S$, the locality cell $L$, and the finite multi-Hist configuration $M$. A consumer may read selected cross-Hist causal rows and their maximum-rate bound through $C$ and $B$, transported by $H$ and routed by $R$, but the consumer cannot read a coordinate outside the displayed locality-cell and multi-Hist rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet as $F=(S,L,M,C,B,H,R,P,N)$. The observer-state row $S$ fixes the state at which the frame is read, while $L$ and $M$ fix the locality-cell and multi-Hist surfaces available at that state.

The selected causal reads in $C$ are anchored in those rows. The bound row $B$ is a finite $\MaxCausalRateUp$ consumer of the same cross-Hist surface, not a metric or global-chart object.

Transport and continuation replay through $H$ and $R$ preserve the same finite rows. Since no coordinate outside $S,L,M,C,B,H,R,P,N$ is exposed, every accepted frame remains locality-cell sound.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverCoordinateFrame non-global-frame boundary]
\label{thm:observer-coordinate-frame-non-global-frame-boundary}
An accepted $\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp$ carrier exports only conscious-observer-state, observer-locality-cell, multi-Hist configuration, cross-Hist causal, maximum-bound, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ route, package, and local $\NameCert$ rows. It exports no god's-eye observer, no global coordinate frame, no shared external time scalar, no host spacetime manifold, no metric tensor, no observer-independent event object, no quotient of histories, and no unrecorded remote history.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier inventory of \autoref{def:observer-coordinate-frame-carrier}. Every public read of the frame is one of the rows $S,L,M,C,B,H,R,P,N$.

The positive frame data is therefore local: $S$ supplies the present conscious-observer state, $L$ supplies the locality cell, $M$ supplies the finite history configuration, $C$ supplies selected cross-Hist causal rows, and $B$ supplies the finite bound row.

The remaining rows $H,R,P,N$ transport, route, package, and name those same coordinates. None of them contains a god's-eye observer, global chart, shared time scalar, manifold, metric, observer-independent event, quotient, or hidden remote history, so none of those objects can be projected from the packet.
\end{proof}
