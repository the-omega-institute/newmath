\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicLocatedCutUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-located-cut-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\DyadicLocatedCutUp$ packet records the finite dyadic endpoint surface by which located cut data can be read as regular-Cauchy evidence before the terminal real-name seal is consumed. It refines the located-cut route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedcut-namecert} through the dyadic endpoint ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite-window discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} seal. It also sits beside the two-sided real cut surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lower-upper-real-cut-namecert}, with the dyadic endpoint rows made explicit rather than absorbed into general lower and upper ledgers.

\begin{definition}[DyadicLocatedCut carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-located-cut-carrier}
A $\DyadicLocatedCutUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(L,U,G,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the lower dyadic endpoint ledger over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$. It records lower endpoints, requested radii, comparison cells, and endpoint reuse provenance.
\item $U$ is the upper dyadic endpoint ledger over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$. It records upper endpoints, requested radii, comparison cells, and endpoint reuse provenance.
\item $G$ is the located gap evidence through $\LocatedCutUp$. It binds each displayed lower endpoint and upper endpoint to the same located-cut request.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite precision window. It lists exactly the endpoint reads opened by the requested precision and does not select an infinite representative.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff. It reads the endpoint-normalized windows as regular rational data before any real seal is available.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal. It can be reached only after $L,U,G,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the lower endpoint ledger, upper endpoint ledger, located gap row, finite window, regular-Cauchy handoff, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for every consumer read through the same ordered tuple $L,U,G,W,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicLocatedCutUp$, $\LocatedCutUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\LowerUpperRealCutUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicLocatedCutUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $L,U,G,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient cuts, selected suprema, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicLocatedCut NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-located-cut-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\DyadicLocatedCutUp$ carrier
$$
K=(L,U,G,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicLocatedCutUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the lower dyadic endpoint ledger, upper dyadic endpoint ledger, located gap row, finite precision window, regular-Cauchy handoff, terminal real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item lower dyadic transport, requiring every lower endpoint read to enter through $L$ and to preserve its $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison budget under componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item upper dyadic transport, requiring every upper endpoint read to enter through $U$ and to preserve its $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison budget under componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item located-gap exactness, requiring $G$ to bind the displayed lower and upper endpoint rows to one $\LocatedCutUp$ request before $W$ or $R$ is consumed;
\item regular-Cauchy handoff, requiring $R$ to read only the displayed $L,U,G,W$ route before exposing any $\RealUp$-facing datum;
\item Real seal non-escape, requiring the terminal row $E$ to remain downstream of $L,U,G,W,R$ and to export no quotient cut, selected supremum, trichotomy branch, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness principle;
\item ledger provenance, requiring $P$ and $N$ to name only the displayed dyadic endpoint route and its transport and replay rows.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:dyadic-located-cut-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the lower endpoint ledger, upper endpoint ledger, located gap row, precision window, regular-Cauchy handoff, real seal, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Lower and upper dyadic transport are componentwise. The rows $L$ and $U$ store the endpoint comparisons over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, while $H$ transports those endpoint cells and $C$ replays the same displayed reads.

Located-gap exactness is the role of $G$. Since $G$ is a coordinate of the same packet as $L$ and $U$, the lower and upper endpoint rows are bound to one located-cut request before a window or regular-Cauchy handoff can be consumed.

The handoff row $R$ is downstream of $L,U,G,W$. Thus every regular rational read is endpoint-normalized by the displayed ledgers and finite precision window before the terminal real seal is visible.

The terminal row $E$ and the provenance rows $P,N$ add no analytic coordinate. Because the carrier has no row for quotient cuts, selected suprema, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness, the local NameCert surface cannot export any such object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicLocatedCut regular Cauchy handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-located-cut-regular-cauchy-handoff}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\DyadicLocatedCutUp$ packet factors through the displayed dyadic endpoint ledgers and located-gap row before reading the terminal $\RealUp$ seal. Concretely, every such consumer reads the route
$$
L,U,G,W,R,E,H,C,P,N
$$
with $L$ and $U$ supplying dyadic endpoint-normalized lower and upper evidence, $G$ supplying the located cut gap, $W$ supplying the finite precision window, $R$ supplying the regular-Cauchy handoff, and $E$ supplying the terminal seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix an accepted $\DyadicLocatedCutUp$ carrier. A $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer first sees only the displayed endpoint rows $L$ and $U$ together with the located gap row $G$, so its input is already normalized by dyadic lower and upper endpoint ledgers.

The finite precision window $W$ is downstream of those rows. It schedules the visible endpoint reads without choosing an infinite stream representative or introducing a host cut object.

The regular-Cauchy handoff is $R$. Since $R$ is read after $L,U,G,W$, every regular rational consumer factors through the dyadic endpoint ledgers and the located-gap row.

A $\RealUp$ consumer must pass through $E$, and $E$ is downstream of $R$. The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the same route, so no consumer can bypass the displayed endpoint and located-gap data before reading the terminal seal.
\end{proof}
