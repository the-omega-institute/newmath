The $\ObserverTimeStepUp$ packet realizes the Hist time-step direction of \autoref{ch:visions-observer-hist-identity} as one finite local naming certificate. It sits between the elementary observer-history packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}, the local stream packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-histtimestream-namecert}, the clock-budget packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-localclockbudget-namecert}, the licensed comparison packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerlicensedcomparison-namecert}, and the bridge ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-bridge-ledger-namecert}. The packet names the single Hist-growth step, not a global clock, a synchronized observer frame, a quotient history identity, or an external time parameter.

\begin{definition}[Observer time-step carrier]
\label{def:observer-time-step-carrier}
An $\ObserverTimeStepUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(H,D,C,R,L,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the source $\Hist$ row before the elementary growth step;
\item $D$ is the distinction tag recording whether the step applies $\Eone$ or $\Ezero$;
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ step from $H$ along $D$ to the next local $\Hist$ row;
\item $R$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, distinction, continuation, refusal, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $L$ is the no-global-clock ledger refusing any synchronized external time coordinate for the step;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\Hist$, distinction tags, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, no-global-clock refusal, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverTimeStepUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts a local observer time step only as the displayed Hist source, distinction tag, Cont step, transport row, no-global-clock ledger, package provenance, and local naming row together.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observer time-step NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-time-step-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-time-step-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverTimeStepUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $T=(H,D,C,R,L,P,N)$, source-Hist exposure through $H$, distinction exposure through $D$, displayed Cont-step replay through $C$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $R$, no-global-clock refusal through $L$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier to the seven displayed rows. The source Hist row, distinction tag, and Cont-step row are visible before a consumer can name the time step.

Transport is then restricted to $R$, which preserves the same source, tag, continuation, refusal, provenance, and local name rows. The no-global-clock ledger $L$ is read as part of the packet, so the local step is never promoted to a synchronized external clock.

The provenance row $P$ and naming row $N$ bind the admitted read to exactly these coordinates. Since no other coordinate appears in the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations for $\ObserverTimeStepUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observer time-step nonescape]
\label{thm:observer-time-step-nonescape}
Every accepted $\ObserverTimeStepUp$ consumer factors through the finite route
$$
H,D,C,R,L,P,N.
$$
No accepted consumer may replace the displayed Cont step by a global clock tick, a synchronized observer frame, a quotient history identity, an unrecorded Hist predecessor, or an external time parameter.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:observer-time-step-namecert-obligations}. They identify an accepted time-step read with the source Hist row, distinction tag, displayed Cont step, transport row, refusal ledger, provenance, and local name.

The Cont row $C$ is the only route from the source row $H$ and distinction tag $D$ to the next local Hist row. Removing $C$ leaves no displayed replay row for a consumer to preserve.

The refusal ledger $L$ excludes the external clock and synchronized-frame alternatives, while $R,P,N$ only transport, package, and name the same displayed rows. Hence every accepted consumer remains inside the finite route $H,D,C,R,L,P,N$.
\end{proof}
