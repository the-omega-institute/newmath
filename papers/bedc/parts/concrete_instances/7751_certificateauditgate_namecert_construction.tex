\begin{definition}[CertificateAuditGate carrier]
\label{def:certificate-audit-gate-carrier}
A $\CertificateAuditGateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(I,S,R,D,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the gate-input row, recording the finite manuscript, Lean target, source-file, and audit-command inputs supplied to the gate;
\item $S$ is the checked-surface row, recording the finite pass surface actually inspected by the gate;
\item $R$ is the refusal row, recording every reading that the gate rejects rather than promotes to substrate-internal closure;
\item $D$ is the drift row, recording paper-Lean marker resolution, duplicate-label checks, and name-resolution failures as finite audit data;
\item $A$ is the axiom-purity row, recording source-axiom refusal and dependency refusal for $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$, $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$, and $\mathsf{propext}$;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the input, checked surface, refusal, drift, axiom-purity, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ stores the $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads the gate input, checked surface, refusal, drift, axiom-purity, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CertificateAuditGateUp$, $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$, $\OperationalMembraneUp$, $\EffectiveReplacementLedgerUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ data;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CertificateAuditGateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for a global repository-correctness theorem, a hidden source axiom, an unresolved Lean marker, a quotient representative, a choice selector, proposition equality, or a proof that a passing audit row becomes substrate-internal closure.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CertificateAuditGate obligation surface]
\label{thm:certificate-audit-gate-obligation-surface}
For every accepted carrier $G=(I,S,R,D,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CertificateAuditGateUp}$ surface consists exactly of gate-input exposure, checked-surface exactness, refusal-row attachment, drift-row resolution, axiom-purity refusal, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ routing, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming row. No consumer may cite a gate pass without exposing all nine rows of the same finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:certificate-audit-gate-carrier}. The rows $I,S,R,D,A,H,C,P,N$ are introduced as separate coordinates of one finite $\BHist$ packet.

The first five rows give the gate-facing surface: supplied inputs, inspected checks, explicit refusals, paper-Lean drift data, and axiom-purity data. The support rows $H,C,P,N$ then transport, replay, cite, and name those same rows without adding a further proof principle.

Thus the local NameCert obligation is not a scalar pass bit. It is the displayed finite packet, and any consumer omitting one of the rows has left the certificate surface named by the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CertificateAuditGate soundness boundary]
\label{thm:certificate-audit-gate-soundness-boundary}
For every accepted $\CertificateAuditGateUp$ carrier $G=(I,S,R,D,A,H,C,P,N)$, a passing checked-surface row $S$ is sound only as local non-breach of the rows displayed by $I,D,A,H,C,P,N$. It does not assert global manuscript truth, host-kernel completeness, unrestricted semantic conservativity, or substrate-internal closure of the gate input.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $S$ together with the supplied inputs $I$. The checked surface records only the finite rows inspected for those inputs, so the pass result is bounded by the rows carried in the packet.

Use the drift row $D$ and axiom-purity row $A$ as the two gate ledgers. They report marker resolution, duplicate-label refusal, source-axiom refusal, and forbidden-dependency refusal as finite audit data.

Finally route the read through $H,C,P,N$. Componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming keep the soundness claim on the displayed non-breach surface. Since no coordinate records global truth, host completeness, semantic conservativity, or internal closure, none can be projected from the pass row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CertificateAuditGate non-escape]
\label{thm:certificate-audit-gate-nonescape}
Every consumer read of an accepted $\CertificateAuditGateUp$ packet factors through
$$
I,S,R,D,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
The read may expose gate inputs, checked rows, refusals, drift data, axiom-purity data, transports, routes, provenance, and local naming, but it cannot escape to a hidden audit oracle, unresolved target replacement, quotient representative, choice selector, proposition equality, or substrate-internal theorem supplied by the gate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier inventory in \autoref{def:certificate-audit-gate-carrier}. It lists exactly the gate input, checked surface, refusal, drift, axiom-purity, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:certificate-audit-gate-obligation-surface}. A public certificate read must expose the same nine rows and cannot replace the packet by a pass token.

Apply \autoref{thm:certificate-audit-gate-soundness-boundary}. The pass row is local non-breach for the displayed audit data rather than a theorem about all host or manuscript behaviour.

The excluded objects require coordinates absent from $G$. Therefore continuation replay through $C$ and naming through $N$ can return only the finite certificate rows, not a hidden oracle or substrate-internal theorem.
\end{proof}
