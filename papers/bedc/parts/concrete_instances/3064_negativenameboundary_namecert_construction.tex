The $\NegativeNameBoundaryUp$ packet realizes apophatic negative naming as a finite NameCert surface. The negative-name packet is the refusal-facing boundary between the socket-name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert} and the visible refusal question of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticgatequestion-namecert}. \begin{definition}[NegativeNameBoundary carrier]
\label{def:negative-name-boundary-carrier}
A $\NegativeNameBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(a,s,r,g,h,c,p,n)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $a$ is the apophatic handle row naming the requested external supply without describing it as an internal object;
\item $s$ is the requested socket shape, recording which supply interface the surrounding argument asked to consume;
\item $r$ is the refusal and non-internalization ledger, recording each blocked route by which the requested supply might otherwise be treated as generated theorem content, quotient equality, host selector, axiom, or hidden witness;
\item $g$ is the audit gate row, recording the finite question that separates visible boundary use from hidden internalization;
\item $h$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the handle, socket, refusal, gate, route, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $c$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect the handle, requested socket shape, refusal ledger, audit gate, provenance, and local certificate;
\item $p$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, apophatic names, socket rows, refusal ledgers, audit gates, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NegativeNameBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for the requested external supply itself.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NegativeNameBoundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:negative-name-boundary-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\NegativeNameBoundaryUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:negative-name-boundary-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$

$$
apophatic handle exposure through $a$, requested socket display through $s$, refusal-ledger exhaustion through $r$, audit-gate readback through $g$, componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $h$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $c$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $p$, and local naming through $n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed tuple. The rows $a,s,r,g$ expose the boundary content: the name, the requested socket shape, the refusal ledger, and the audit gate.

Transport and consumer access are confined by $h$ and $c$. These rows replay the same boundary packet under componentwise $\hsame$ movement and expose only the recorded continuation routes.

The provenance and local naming rows $p,n$ account for package ancestry and the local certificate. Since the requested external supply has no coordinate in $B$, the NameCert surface can name the negative boundary without exporting the supply as internal content.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NegativeNameBoundary refusal ledger]
\label{thm:negative-name-boundary-refusal-ledger}
For an accepted $\NegativeNameBoundaryUp$ carrier, the refusal ledger $r$ records every public attempt to use the requested socket shape $s$ as generated theorem content, quotient equality, host selector, axiom, hidden witness, or anonymous supply route. Each such attempt is routed through $r$ and remains audit-visible through $g,c,p,n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the requested socket row $s$ and the refusal ledger $r$. The ledger is attached to exactly the routes by which the requested supply would be converted into internal content.

The audit gate row $g$ makes the refusal question public, while the continuation routes $c$ replay that gate and ledger for downstream consumers. Componentwise transport in $h$ preserves the same refusal rows when the packet is moved across equivalent presentations.

The provenance and local naming rows $p,n$ identify the packet as the certified source of the refusal. Therefore a public use of the requested socket shape must either pass through the displayed refusal ledger or have no accepted coordinate in the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NegativeNameBoundary non-internalization]
\label{thm:negative-name-boundary-non-internalization}
Every public use of an accepted $\NegativeNameBoundaryUp$ packet factors through the rows
$$

$$
The packet may expose the apophatic handle, the requested socket shape, the refusal ledger, the audit gate, transports, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming, but it cannot export the requested external supply as generated theorem content, quotient equality, host selector, axiom, hidden witness, or unrecorded internal object.

\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier inventory of \autoref{def:negative-name-boundary-carrier}. The requested external supply itself is absent; only its handle, socket shape, refusal ledger, audit gate, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows are present.

Let a public consumer of the packet be given. By the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:negative-name-boundary-namecert-obligations}, every displayed consumer route must read the packet through $c$ and preserve the same finite surface through $h$.

If the consumer tries to use the socket shape as generated theorem content, quotient equality, host selector, axiom, hidden witness, or anonymous supply, apply the refusal ledger of \autoref{thm:negative-name-boundary-refusal-ledger}. The attempt is routed to $r$ and remains visible through the audit gate.

Thus every accepted public use factors through the displayed rows, and no projection from the packet can return the requested external supply as internal substrate content.
\end{proof}
