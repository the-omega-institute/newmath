It sits beside the series certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-series-namecert}, the real-sequence limit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-limit-namecert}, the dyadic arithmetic core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational sequence boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal Real surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Fekete subadditive carrier]
\label{def:fekete-subadditive-carrier}
A $\FeketeSubadditiveUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(S,A,D,L,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the real sequence source row. It records the inspected source windows and the finite indexing data from which normalized averages are read.
\item $A$ is the subadditivity witness ledger. Each row records a displayed decomposition $m+n$ together with the finite comparison cell witnessing that the source value at $m+n$ is bounded by the visible sum of the source values at $m$ and $n$.
\item $D$ is the dyadic normalized-average comparison ledger. It stores the rational denominator windows, dyadic comparison tolerances, and finite average rows used to compare inspected values of $S$.
\item $L$ is the located lower-bound readback row. It records the finite lower-bound and located-infimum data actually displayed by $D$, without exposing a host-level infimum operator.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained only after the normalized-average ledger and lower-bound readback have been displayed.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the regular rational handoff has been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, subadditivity, dyadic average, lower-bound, regular handoff, Real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through
$$
S,A\longmapsto D\longmapsto L\longmapsto R\longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FeketeSubadditiveUp$, $\RealSequenceLimitUp$, $\SeriesUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FeketeSubadditiveUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for ambient completeness, trichotomy, countable choice, quotient stream equality, host real equality, host-level infimum operators, or a selected external limit.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Fekete subadditive NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:fekete-subadditive-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FeketeSubadditiveUp$ carrier $F=(S,A,D,L,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FeketeSubadditiveUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed ten-row packet;
\item source-window exposure for $S$, including every index and normalized-average denominator inspected by the certificate;
\item subadditivity ledger exactness for each decomposition row recorded in $A$;
\item dyadic normalized-average comparison stability for $D$ under the recorded $\hsame$ transports;
\item located lower-bound readback, requiring $L$ to be read only from the displayed average comparisons in $D$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff and $\RealUp$ seal non-escape, requiring $R$ and $E$ to factor through $S,A,D,L$;
\item transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for exactly the rows recorded in $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No Fekete subadditive NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $F=(S,A,D,L,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes the source sequence windows, subadditivity witness ledger, dyadic average comparisons, lower-bound readback, regular rational handoff, Real seal, and structural rows.

Finally $R$ and $E$ are downstream of the displayed ledger. Componentwise transport in $H$ preserves the same route, and $C,P,N$ only replay, source, and name that route. Hence the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Fekete subadditive Cauchy route]
\label{thm:fekete-subadditive-cauchy-route}
Let $F=(S,A,D,L,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\FeketeSubadditiveUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$-facing read exported by $F$ factors through the finite route
$$
S,A\longmapsto D\longmapsto L\longmapsto R\longmapsto E .
$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Real-facing read. Carrier projection shows that the only available numerical rows are the source windows $S$, the subadditivity witnesses $A$, the dyadic comparison ledger $D$, and the lower-bound readback $L$.

The Cauchy information is produced by the normalized-average route. The ledger $D$ supplies finite dyadic comparisons between inspected averages, and $L$ records the lower-bound readback that those comparisons actually expose.

The handoff to $R$ and $E$ does not add analytic data. It presents the displayed lower-bound route as a $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff and then as a Real-facing seal.

The structural rows preserve the same route under transport and replay. Therefore a downstream Cauchy, series, or real-sequence-limit consumer cannot read ambient completeness, trichotomy, countable choice, quotient stream equality, host real equality, host-level infimum operators, or an external selected limit from the $\FeketeSubadditiveUp$ packet.
\end{proof}
