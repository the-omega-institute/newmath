\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetricCompletionIdempotenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metric-completion-idempotence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley, the $\MetricCompletionIdempotenceUp$ packet records the fixed-point surface of constructive metric completion: once a displayed metric-completion route is already separated and complete at the finite BEDC interface, a second completion read must return to the same named rows. It sits after the metric-completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}, the separated-completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedcompletion-namecert}, the complete separated metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-separated-metric-namecert}, and the uniform-completion handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}. The packet gives downstream completion-functor, real-completion, and regular-rational routes a fixed-point target without quotient completion equality, selected representatives, host equality, countable choice, or an ambient completeness axiom.

\begin{definition}[MetricCompletionIdempotence carrier]
\label{def:metric-completion-idempotence-carrier}
A $\MetricCompletionIdempotenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(M,S,C,U,R,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the first $\MetricCompletionUp$ row, including the source metric, Cauchy-filter or Cauchy-net branch, finite readback, and separated comparison route.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedCompletionUp$ boundary row requiring the first completion read to be separated before the fixed-point claim is consumed.
\item $C$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ or $\CompleteSeparatedMetricUp$ limit-service row, exposing only the displayed Cauchy-name endpoint data available inside the completed surface.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ compatibility row, tying the metric-completion read to the same uniform completion route when a uniform consumer is present.
\item $R$ is the second-completion readback row. It records that any repeated completion request replays $M,S,C,U$ rather than introducing a second quotient, selector, host equality, or ambient complete metric space.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the first completion, separated boundary, complete-metric service, uniform handoff, repeated readback, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the first completion request through the separated and complete rows to the repeated-completion readback.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedCompletionUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\CompleteSeparatedMetricUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionIdempotenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected Cauchy representatives, host equality of completed points, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or a second completion object outside the replayed rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionIdempotence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-completion-idempotence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetricCompletionIdempotenceUp$ carrier $I=(M,S,C,U,R,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionIdempotenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item first-completion admission, requiring the initial read to pass through the displayed $\MetricCompletionUp$ row $M$;
\item separated fixed-point boundary, requiring the $\SeparatedCompletionUp$ row $S$ to be read before a repeated completion request is accepted;
\item complete-service compatibility, requiring the limit-service row $C$ to expose only the completed finite endpoint data already available from the same source;
\item uniform-route compatibility, requiring every $\UniformCompletionUp$ consumer to share the route $M,S,C$ before it reaches the repeated readback row $R$;
\item idempotence nonescape, requiring $R,H,K,P,N$ to replay, transport, package, and name exactly the same route, with no quotient completion equality, selected representative, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, or detached second completion coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metric-completion-idempotence-carrier}. The first completion row is $M$, and the only rows that can certify fixed-point behavior before the repeated readback are $S,C,U$.

Read $S$ through the separated-completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedcompletion-namecert}. This forces any equality or comparison of completed names to use displayed separated classifier data rather than host equality or quotient classes.

Read $C$ through the complete-metric service. The row supplies endpoint service only for the already displayed completion data, so it cannot select a new representative or create a second ambient complete space.

For uniform-facing consumers, read $U$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}. The uniform route is required to share the same completion rows before the repeated readback $R$ is available.

Finally $H,K,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier has no excluded coordinate, the five listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionIdempotence repeated completion fixed point]
\label{thm:metric-completion-idempotence-repeated-completion-fixed-point}
Let $I=(M,S,C,U,R,H,K,P,N)$ be an accepted $\MetricCompletionIdempotenceUp$ carrier. Every second $\MetricCompletionUp$ read consumed through $I$ factors through the finite route
$$
M \longmapsto S,C,U \longmapsto R \longmapsto H,K,P,N .
$$
The second read therefore lands on the displayed completion surface already named by $M,S,C,U$ and cannot be replaced by quotient completion equality, selected representatives, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, or a detached completion object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted repeated completion request. Carrier admission gives $R$ only as a row downstream of the first completion row $M$ and the boundary rows $S,C,U$.

Use \autoref{thm:metric-completion-namecert-obligations}. The first completion read is already confined to the displayed metric source, cofinal branch, finite readback, and separated comparison route.

Use the separated boundary supplied by $S$ and the complete-service row $C$. They ensure that the repeated request reads a completed separated metric surface rather than a quotient completion or a selected representative.

The uniform compatibility row $U$ keeps uniform consumers on the same route. Transport and replay through $H,K$ then carry that route to $R$, while $P,N$ record provenance and local naming.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionIdempotence downstream scope]
\label{thm:metric-completion-idempotence-downstream-scope}
Every completion-functor, regular-rational, or real-completion consumer of an accepted $\MetricCompletionIdempotenceUp$ carrier receives only the fixed-point route displayed by $M,S,C,U,R,H,K,P,N$. The packet supplies a non-quotient idempotence target for downstream completion functoriality and Real or $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback routes, but it does not close arbitrary completion monad laws, global reflection theorems, selected-limit principles, host equality of completed points, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows and read the repeated-completion fixed point theorem \autoref{thm:metric-completion-idempotence-repeated-completion-fixed-point}. Any downstream consumer entering through the packet is already on the route $M,S,C,U,R,H,K,P,N$.

For functorial consumers, the route starts with the same $\MetricCompletionUp$ row $M$ and reaches a repeated completion only through $R$. No additional functorial law is stored in the packet.

For regular-rational and real-completion consumers, the readback data are inherited from the finite completion rows named by $M$ and the complete-service row $C$. The packet names the fixed-point surface but does not add a new $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.

The nonescape clause of \autoref{thm:metric-completion-idempotence-namecert-obligations} then excludes quotient equality, selected limits, host equality, choice, ambient completeness, and detached second completion data from all such consumers.
\end{proof}
