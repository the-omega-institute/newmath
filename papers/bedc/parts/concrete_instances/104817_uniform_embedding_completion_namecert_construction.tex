\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformEmbeddingCompletionUp$}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley on uniform embeddings and completion, the $\UniformEmbeddingCompletionUp$ packet records the finite BEDC route by which an embedding into a separated completion is consumed without quotient representatives or selected limits. It sits after the metric-completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the separated comparison surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the uniform-completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, the Cauchy-name comparison of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequenceequivalence-namecert}, and the terminal seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the embedding interface as displayed rows over completion input, separatedness, uniform continuity, Cauchy-equivalence stability, and Real-facing readback; it does not assert a quotient completion, choose representatives, or import ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[UniformEmbeddingCompletion carrier]
\label{def:uniform-embedding-completion-carrier}
A $\UniformEmbeddingCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(M,S,U,Q,R,T,W,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ source row supplying the completion-facing metric request and its displayed finite tolerance route.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ row requiring the target-side comparison to be separated before the embedding read is public.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ row exposing the entourage-facing completion presentation consumed by the embedding.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$ row identifying when two source Cauchy names have the same displayed completion read.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the completion, separatedness, uniform, and Cauchy-equivalence rows have been displayed.
\item $T$ records the finite source-window schedule inspected by the embedding before a target read is accepted.
\item $W$ records the finite target-window schedule produced by the embedding route.
\item $A$ records the approximation ledger saying that matching source windows and tolerances transport to matching target windows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,S,U,Q,R,T,W,A$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
M,S,U,Q,T,A \longmapsto W \longmapsto R .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformEmbeddingCompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformEmbeddingCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts only packets whose embedding-to-completion read is exhausted by $M,S,U,Q,R,T,W,A,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient representatives, selected limits, host equality of completions, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UniformEmbeddingCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-embedding-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformEmbeddingCompletionUp$ carrier
$$
E=(M,S,U,Q,R,T,W,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\UniformEmbeddingCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed twelve-row packet; metric-completion exposure through $M$; separated-target exposure through $S$; uniform-completion exposure through $U$; Cauchy-equivalence stability through $Q$; terminal Real sealing through $R$; finite source-window exposure through $T$; finite target-window exposure through $W$; approximation transport through $A$; componentwise transport through $H$; displayed replay through $C$; package provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:uniform-embedding-completion-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the completion source, separated target, uniform completion, Cauchy-equivalence comparison, terminal seal, source and target windows, approximation ledger, and structural rows listed there.

The analytic obligations are read in the displayed order. The completion request passes through $M$ and $U$, the separated target boundary is supplied by $S$, and the source comparison is stabilized by $Q$ before the terminal Real-facing row $R$ can be inspected.

The embedding content is the finite window handoff $T,A,W$. The source windows in $T$ and their approximation ledger $A$ determine the target windows in $W$; no step inserts a quotient representative or a selected completion point.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since the excluded quotient, choice, host-equality, and ambient-completeness objects are absent from the carrier, they cannot appear as local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformEmbeddingCompletion universal route]
\label{thm:uniform-embedding-completion-universal-route}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\UniformEmbeddingCompletionUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
M,S,U,Q,T,A \longmapsto W,R \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus a uniformly embedded source can be read by completion consumers only through displayed metric-completion input, separatedness, uniform-completion structure, Cauchy-equivalence stability, finite source windows, approximation transport, finite target windows, the terminal Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier. The source completion row $M$ and uniform-completion row $U$ provide only completion-facing requests, while the separated row $S$ restricts target comparison to the displayed separated boundary.

The Cauchy-equivalence row $Q$ supplies the only equality-facing source comparison. It is read before the target windows are public, so a consumer cannot replace it by quotient-stream equality or host completion equality.

The universal route is the finite handoff $T,A,W$. The source-window schedule in $T$ is transported by the approximation ledger $A$ to the target-window schedule $W$, and the Real-facing read reaches $R$ only after those rows have been displayed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name exactly this packet. Hence every completion-facing consumer factors through the displayed route and cannot read a selected limit, quotient representative, countable-choice schedule, host completion equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{proof}
