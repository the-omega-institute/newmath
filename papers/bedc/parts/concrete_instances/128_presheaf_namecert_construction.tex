\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PreSheafUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-presheaf-namecert}

The presheaf interface $\PreSheafUp$ packages a contravariant functor from the open-sets category of a topological carrier to a target category, with restriction maps respecting composition and identity. It depends on $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-category-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\FunctorUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-functor-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:presheaf-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[Presheaf source and target carrier obligations]\label{thm:presheaf-source-target-carriers}
Every $\PreSheafUp$ certificate must expose the following carrier obligations. Its source carrier is the BEDC open-object category whose objects are $\mathsf{BHist}$-indexed open rows and whose arrows are certified inclusion witnesses. Its target carrier is a $\CategoryUp$ certificate carrying section objects and target hom rows. For each certified inclusion $U\le V$, the certificate must carry a restriction-hom row from the section object over $V$ to the section object over $U$. The classifier ledger must also contain the source-open, source-inclusion, target-object, and target-hom rows over $\mathsf{BHist}$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PreSheafUp.PreSheafIndexedRestrictionSurface\_carrier\_rows}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the ten-field roadmap. The traditional target is the ordinary contravariant presheaf assignment from opens to a target category, so the BEDC source must be a $\CategoryUp$ surface of open objects and inclusion rows, and the target must be a $\CategoryUp$ surface of section objects and target homs.

The carrier choice then names four rows: open objects over $\mathsf{BHist}$, certified inclusions, target section objects, and target homs. The classifier choice names the target hom classifier, while the visible source boundary keeps every open and inclusion row attached to $\mathsf{BHist}$ membership.

The remaining roadmap fields record why these carriers are obligations rather than completed construction. Identity, composition, transport, hidden restriction witnesses, restriction-row exactness, the standard bridge, and the status row are still obligations of the same certificate surface, with theory status $\obligationClosure$ and verification status $\unformalizedV$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Presheaf identity restriction obligation]\label{thm:presheaf-restriction-identity-row}
For every carried open object $U$ of the BEDC source category, the restriction row attached to the certified identity inclusion $U\le U$ must be classified as the identity morphism of the target $\CategoryUp$ section object over $U$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PreSheafUp.PreSheafIndexedRestrictionSurface\_empty\_restriction\_identity\_row}
\begin{proof}
Start with the traditional presheaf identity law. In BEDC form, the source category supplies the identity inclusion witness on the open object $U$, while the target $\CategoryUp$ supplies the identity hom on the section object over $U$.

The carrier choice fixes both rows before any comparison is made. The classifier choice is the target hom classifier, so the exact identity obligation is not host equality of maps but classification of the restriction hom by the target identity row.

The ledger records the hidden restriction witness and the identity comparison witness. Classifier transport is inactive in this row, composition is not invoked, the standard bridge remains outside the certificate, and the status is $\obligationClosure$ on the paper side with $\unformalizedV$ on the Lean side.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Presheaf composition restriction obligation]\label{thm:presheaf-restriction-composition-row}
For carried inclusions $U\le V\le W$ in the BEDC source category, the direct restriction row from $W$ to $U$ must be classified as the target-category composite of the restriction row from $W$ to $V$ followed by the restriction row from $V$ to $U$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is the contravariant presheaf composition law. The BEDC source supplies the composable inclusion witnesses $U\le V$ and $V\le W$, together with the carried composite inclusion $U\le W$.

The carrier rows give the three target section objects and the three restriction hom witnesses. The classifier row is the target hom classifier, so the exactness statement compares the direct $W$-to-$U$ restriction with the target $\CategoryUp$ composite of the two displayed restriction homs.

The ledger boundary records the two source inclusion witnesses, their composite witness, the two-step target hom witnesses, and the direct composite restriction witness. Identity and transport remain separate rows, the standard bridge is not claimed, and the status is $\obligationClosure$ with $\unformalizedV$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PreSheafUp.PreSheafIndexedRestrictionSurface\_restriction\_composition\_row}

\begin{theorem}[Presheaf classifier transport and ledger obligation]\label{thm:presheaf-classifier-transport-ledger}
Classified source opens, classified source inclusions, and classified target homs must transport every restriction row of a $\PreSheafUp$ certificate. The exact ledger records precisely the source inclusion witness, the target hom witness, and the composite restriction witness needed for the transported restriction row, and it records no host open equality, cover quotient, stalk quotient, external sheafification, or standard bridge witness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PreSheafUp.PreSheafIndexedRestrictionSurface\_ledger\_extension\_surface}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PreSheafUp.PreSheafIndexedRestrictionSurface\_section\_restriction\_transport}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target presheaf supplies restriction rows stable under change of certified presentation. BEDC names this as transport along the source-open classifier, the source-inclusion classifier, and the target hom classifier, all indexed over $\mathsf{BHist}$.

The carrier choice fixes the open-object and target-object rows, and the classifier choice fixes the target hom classifier. Transport therefore moves restriction witnesses only through named classifier rows; it does not appeal to an ambient equality of host opens or to an external quotient of sections.

The ledger exactness row is the boundary of the certificate. It records the source inclusion witness, the target hom witness, and the composite restriction witness used by identity, composition, and transport obligations. Sheaf locality, gluing, stalk quotients, and the standard bridge remain outside this certificate boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[PreSheaf indexed restriction surface]\label{def:presheaf-indexed-restriction-surface}
Let $\mathcal{T}$ be an indexed open topology carrier for $\TopologyUp$ in the sense of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}, with point carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$, visible membership row $\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,b)$, and membership transport along $\hsame$. Write $j\le_{\mathcal{T}} i$ for the displayed inclusion row saying that every $\mathsf{BHist}$ point of $j$ is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ point of $i$. A $\PreSheafUp$ indexed restriction surface over $\mathcal{T}$ is the following ten-field template.
\begin{enumerate}
\item Traditional target: a contravariant presheaf surface from the open-index category of $\mathcal{T}$ to a target certificate category.
\item BEDC source: the open-index, inclusion-witness, and composition rows supplied by $\CategoryUp$, $\FunctorUp$, and $\TopologyUp$.
\item Naming source: a $\NameCert$ row whose visible carrier is built from $\mathsf{BHist}$ and whose membership rows are stable under $\hsame$.
\item Section carrier: for every open index $i$, a carrier $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$.
\item Section classifier: for every open index $i$, a classifier $\sim_i$ on $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, with reflexive, symmetric, and transitive rows.
\item Restriction maps: for every inclusion witness $j\le_{\mathcal{T}} i$, a map $\rho_{i,j}:\mathsf{Sec}(i)\to\mathsf{Sec}(j)$.
\item Identity row: for every $s:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, the self-restriction satisfies $\rho_{i,i}(s)\sim_i s$.
\item Composition row: for every chain $k\le_{\mathcal{T}}j\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$ and every $s:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$,
$$
\rho_{j,k}(\rho_{i,j}(s))\sim_k\rho_{i,k}(s).
$$
\item Restriction classifier-stability row: every classifier comparison $s\sim_i t$ transports along every displayed restriction:
$$
s\sim_i t
\Longrightarrow
\rho_{i,j}(s)\sim_j\rho_{i,j}(t).
$$
\item Exact ledger: the exported ledger consists only of indexed opens, displayed inclusion witnesses, section rows, and restriction provenance, all over $\mathsf{BHist}$ membership and $\hsame$-stable transport.
\end{enumerate}
No row in this surface exports host open equality, cover quotients, stalk quotients, or external sheafification data.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.PreSheafUp.PreSheafIndexedRestrictionSurface}

\begin{theorem}[PreSheaf indexed restriction functoriality surface]\label{thm:presheaf-indexed-restriction-functoriality-surface}
If $\mathsf{PreSheafIndexedRestrictionSurface}(sec,\rho,identity,composite)$ holds, $identity\hsame identity'$, and $\Cont(\rho,identity',composite')$, then $\mathsf{PreSheafIndexedRestrictionSurface}(sec,\rho,identity',composite')$, $composite\hsame composite'$, and $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(composite')$ all hold.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.PreSheafUp.PreSheafIndexedRestrictionSurface\_functoriality\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Unpack \autoref{def:presheaf-indexed-restriction-surface}. The old surface supplies $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(sec)$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(\rho)$, $\Cont(sec,\rho,identity)$, and $\Cont(\rho,identity,composite)$.

Transport the first continuation result along $identity\hsame identity'$ to obtain $\Cont(sec,\rho,identity')$. Unary closure under $\Cont$ then gives $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(identity')$, and the displayed continuation $\Cont(\rho,identity',composite')$ gives $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(composite')$. These four rows repack as $\mathsf{PreSheafIndexedRestrictionSurface}(sec,\rho,identity',composite')$.

It remains to compare the composite rows. Continuation respect for $\hsame$ applies to the reflexive comparison on $\rho$, the displayed comparison $identity\hsame identity'$, and the two continuation ledgers from $\rho$ to $identity$ and from $\rho$ to $identity'$. The result is $composite\hsame composite'$, completing the displayed functoriality surface.
\end{proof}
