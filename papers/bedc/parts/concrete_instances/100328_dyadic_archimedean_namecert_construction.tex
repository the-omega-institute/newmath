\begin{definition}[DyadicArchimedean carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-archimedean-carrier}
A $\DyadicArchimedeanUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet

$$
A=(D,B,K,S,C,I,H,T,P,N)
$$

with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row whose denominator and numerator histories are the source of the tolerance comparison.
\item $B$ is the target dyadic bound row, displayed over the same dyadic core surface as the quantity to be dominated.
\item $K$ is a unary scale witness, naming a finite number of successor-scale steps from the source tolerance scale to a scale whose dyadic unit dominates $B$.
\item $S$ is the common-scale replay row, transporting $D$ and $B$ to the comparison scale named by $K$ without invoking host rational order.
\item $C$ is the $\DyadicComparisonUp$ and $\DyadicOrderUp$ domination row, recording the finite comparison that the replayed scale dominates the replayed bound.
\item $I$ is the $\DyadicIntervalArithmeticUp$ enclosure handoff, exposing the same dominated scale as an endpoint enclosure for downstream tolerance consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dyadic source row, bound row, unary scale witness, common-scale replay, domination comparison, enclosure handoff, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from scale requests through $D,B,K,S,C,I,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicArchimedeanUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicOrderUp$, $\DyadicComparisonUp$, $\DyadicIntervalArithmeticUp$, $\DyadicDensityRealUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicArchimedeanUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is exactly the assertion that these rows form one finite dyadic scale-domination packet. It has no coordinate for trichotomy, quotient equality, host rational order, selected limits, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicArchimedean NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-archimedean-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicArchimedeanUp$ carrier $A=(D,B,K,S,C,I,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicArchimedeanUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed dyadic source row $D$, target bound row $B$, unary scale witness $K$, common-scale replay $S$, domination comparison $C$, enclosure handoff $I$, and structural rows $H,T,P,N$;
\item scale-witness well-foundedness, requiring $K$ to be read as a finite unary successor ledger rather than as an ambient Archimedean principle;
\item common-scale exactness, requiring $S$ to replay $D$ and $B$ at the scale named by $K$ through displayed dyadic denominator rows;
\item domination exposure, requiring $C$ to certify domination only through the $\DyadicComparisonUp$ and $\DyadicOrderUp$ rows produced by $S$;
\item enclosure compatibility, requiring $I$ to export the dominated scale as a $\DyadicIntervalArithmeticUp$ endpoint enclosure without changing the comparison route;
\item classifier transport, requiring $H$ and $T$ to preserve the same source row, bound row, scale witness, replay row, comparison row, and enclosure row;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding trichotomy, quotient equality, host rational order, selected limits, countable choice, and $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The analytic rows are $D,B,K,S,C,I$, while $H,T,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet.

The scale-witness obligation is read from $K$. Since $K$ is unary, the only available eliminator is finite induction over the displayed successor ledger. The base row compares the initial dyadic unit with the replayed bound when the bound is already dominated at the source scale.

For the successor step, assume the preceding scale has been replayed through $S$ and compared through $C$. The next unary constructor doubles the displayed dyadic unit at the common scale, and the comparison row is recomputed through the same $\DyadicComparisonUp$ and $\DyadicOrderUp$ surface. No host rational order or trichotomy is introduced in this step.

The enclosure handoff is then read from $I$. It packages the dominated scale as a dyadic endpoint enclosure, so downstream tolerance consumers see the same comparison route rather than a separate real-order argument.

Finally $H$ and $T$ transport and replay the listed rows componentwise, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming. The carrier contains no coordinate from which trichotomy, quotient equality, host rational order, selected limits, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness could be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicArchimedean scale existence]
\label{thm:dyadic-archimedean-scale-existence}
Every accepted $\DyadicArchimedeanUp$ carrier $A=(D,B,K,S,C,I,H,T,P,N)$ exposes a finite dyadic scale that dominates its displayed target bound. More precisely, induction on the unary witness $K$ produces a scale row $k$ such that the replay route

$$
D,B,K \longmapsto S \longmapsto C
$$

certifies that the dyadic unit at scale $k$ dominates the bound row $B$ through displayed $\DyadicComparisonUp$ and $\DyadicOrderUp$ data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the unary scale witness $K$.

In the base case, the carrier supplies the common-scale replay row $S$ at the initial scale. The comparison row $C$ is then the displayed domination certificate for the source dyadic unit against the replayed target bound.

In the successor case, apply the induction hypothesis to the predecessor witness. The successor constructor extends the scale ledger by one displayed doubling step, and $S$ replays both $D$ and $B$ at the extended common scale. The comparison row $C$ records the corresponding domination through the same dyadic comparison and order surfaces.

The induction returns the terminal scale row named by $K$. Since each step used only $S$ and $C$, the resulting scale is finite and carries no appeal to host rational order, trichotomy, selected limits, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicArchimedean L10 handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-archimedean-l10-handoff}
For every accepted $\DyadicArchimedeanUp$ carrier $A=(D,B,K,S,C,I,H,T,P,N)$, every L10 dyadic tolerance consumer reaches the domination certificate through the finite route

$$
D,B,K \longmapsto S \longmapsto C \longmapsto I \longmapsto H,T,P,N .
$$

Consequently a $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicDensityRealUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer receives a displayed scale-domination and enclosure row, with no trichotomy, quotient equality, host rational order, selected limit, countable-choice row, or Real-completeness witness inserted into the route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $A=(D,B,K,S,C,I,H,T,P,N)$. The only source tolerance row is $D$, the only target bound row is $B$, and the only scale-selection data is the unary witness $K$.

By \autoref{thm:dyadic-archimedean-scale-existence}, finite induction on $K$ yields a displayed dominating scale. The proof of that theorem uses the common-scale replay $S$ and the comparison row $C$, so the exported domination certificate is already routed through $\DyadicComparisonUp$ and $\DyadicOrderUp$.

The row $I$ is the sole handoff to $\DyadicIntervalArithmeticUp$ enclosure data. It exports the dominated scale as an endpoint enclosure that a $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicDensityRealUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer may read after the dyadic comparison has been displayed.

The structural rows $H,T,P,N$ preserve and name the route. Componentwise transport and continuation replay keep the same source row, bound row, scale witness, common-scale replay, comparison row, and enclosure handoff. Since every L10 tolerance read is reached through this route, there is no remaining coordinate from which trichotomy, quotient equality, host rational order, selected limits, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness could be read.
\end{proof}
