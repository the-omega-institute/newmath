The $\AddUp$ carrier consumes the unary-history source licensed in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert}. Its two input histories are $\NatUp$ unary spines, and $\AddUp$ adds only the displayed $\Cont$ result row and continuation ledger.

\begin{theorem}[AddUp consumes NatUp as a sibling route]
\label{thm:addup-natup-sibling-dependency-route}
Every accepted $\AddUp$ carrier row reads its two source histories through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert} before the displayed $\Cont$ result row is admitted. Consequently $\AddUp$ is a downstream consumer of the $\NatUp$ unary spine, not a kernel-only arithmetic primitive.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AddUp.AddUpNatUp\_sibling\_dependency\_route}
\begin{proof}
Project the source side of the $\AddUp$ carrier. By \autoref{def:additive-source-specification}, an additive source is an ordered pair of unary histories together with a continuation witness producing the result row.

The unary hypotheses on the two source histories are precisely the $\NatUp$ spine discipline cited by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert}. The continuation lemmas below then act only after those source histories have already been accepted as unary rows.

Apply \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-unit}, \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-associativity}, and the continuation-preservation lemma of this section to the displayed $\Cont$ witness. These results admit and transport the result row, but they do not create the two source spines. Hence the source read factors through $\NatUp$ before $\AddUp$ admits its continuation result.
\end{proof}

\section{Unary continuation}

\begin{lemma}[Continuation preserves unary histories]
If \(\mathsf{Unary}(h)\), \(\mathsf{Unary}(k)\), and \(\ContR(h,k,r)\), then \(\mathsf{Unary}(r)\).
\end{lemma}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.unary\_cont\_preserves\_unary\_by\_induction}
\begin{proof}
By induction on the generated proof of \(\ContR(h,k,r)\). In the empty case, \(r=k\), hence \(\mathsf{Unary}(r)\) follows from the second premise. In the \(\Eone\)-continuation case, use the induction hypothesis on the subcontinuation and then apply unary closure under \(\Eone\). The \(\Ezero\)-continuation case makes the second input history have shape \(\Ezero(k_0)\), contradicting the premise \(\mathsf{Unary}(k)\) by unary no-zero-extension.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Unary continuation has no zero result]
\label{lem:unary-cont-e0-result-absurd}
If $h$ and $k$ are unary histories, then no continuation
$\ContR(h,k,\Ezero(z))$ can exist.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Unary continuation closure sends the two unary inputs and the displayed
continuation to a unary result. But a result of shape $\Ezero(z)$ contradicts
the unary no-zero-extension principle.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.unary\_cont\_e0\_result\_absurd}

\begin{lemma}[Unary continuation one-result cases]
\label{lem:unary-cont-e1-result-cases}
If $h$ and $k$ are unary histories and $\ContR(h,k,\Eone(r))$, then either
$k=\emp$ and $h\hsame\Eone(r)$, or $k=\Eone(k_0)$ for a unary tail $k_0$ and
$\ContR(h,k_0,r)$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Apply the continuation one-result inversion. In the empty branch, keep the
displayed empty right input and result comparison. In the visible-tail branch,
the equality $k=\Eone(k_0)$ transports the unary witness for $k$ to the tail
witness for $k_0$, leaving the tail continuation $\ContR(h,k_0,r)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.unary\_cont\_e1\_result\_cases}

\begin{lemma}[Unary continuation unit]
\label{lem:unary-continuation-unit}
For every unary history \(h\), continuation by the empty history and continuation from the empty history produce unary histories internally same to the expected unary source.
\end{lemma}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.unary\_cont\_unit}
\leanvariant{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.unary\_cont\_unit\_same\_pair}
\begin{proof}
The left unit is the empty continuation generator. The right unit follows from the continuation unit theorem in the proof-standing layer, restricted to unary histories. Unary closure ensures the resulting histories remain within the unary source discipline.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Unary continuation associativity]
\label{lem:unary-continuation-associativity}
Unary continuation is associative up to \(\HSame\) when all participating histories are unary.
\end{lemma}
\leanchecked{BEDC.FKernel.Unary.unary\_continuation\_associativity}
\begin{proof}
This is the continuation associativity theorem specialized to unary histories, together with continuation-preserves-unary-histories for the intermediate results.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AddUp unary standard bridge]
\label{thm:addup-unary-standard-bridge}
The public $\AddUp$ NameCert surface over unary histories, $\ContR$,
$\mathsf{AddSource}$, $\mathsf{AddPattern}$, $\mathsf{AddClassifier}$,
$\mathsf{AddStabilityCert}$, and $\mathsf{AddLedger}$ carries a conservative
paper-level bridge to finite unary words with concatenation and length
addition. Carried BEDC histories read back through the $\NatUp$ unary standard
bridge of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert}; continuation witnesses read back as concatenation witnesses;
$\HSame$ of generated results reads back as equality of unary word lengths;
ledger entries record only the two input histories and the continuation
witness; and no host natural number or host function is admitted as a BEDC
primitive.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AddUp.AddUp\_StdBridge}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:natup-unary-standard-bridge} as the carrier bridge for each
unary history appearing in the public $\AddUp$ source. Thus the two input
histories and the generated result decode as finite unary words, and the
source field of \autoref{thm:concrete-addup-namecert} supplies no data beyond
those two carried histories and the displayed continuation witness.

The continuation witness is read by induction on the right unary tail. The
empty tail is the unit case of \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-unit}; the
one-extension tail is the successor step; and
\autoref{lem:unary-continuation-associativity} transports nested tails to
ordinary concatenation of finite unary words. The commutative-continuation
field listed in \autoref{def:additive-stability-certificate} is therefore
available for the public certificate, while the bridge itself reads the ordered
continuation witness as the corresponding concatenation witness.

Exactness is the result-classifier clause. By
\autoref{thm:same-source-additive-determinacy-bridge}, two generated results
from the same ordered source are related by $\mathsf{AddClassifier}$, and by
\autoref{def:additive-classifier-specification} this is $\HSame$ of the result
histories. Applying the $\NatUp$ bridge exactness to those result histories
reads the comparison as equality of the external unary word lengths, hence as
length addition for concatenation.

Finally, the ledger field is the policy of
\autoref{def:additive-stability-and-ledger-policy}: it records the two BEDC
input histories and the continuation witness that produced the result. The
external word model appears only in the bridge readback and exactness fields,
so no host natural number, host addition function, or host concatenation
function becomes a primitive of the BEDC kernel.
\end{proof}

By \autoref{def:typed-naming-certificate}, a derived interface is licensed by a certificate once its source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger fields are supplied. \begin{theorem}[Core additive stability does not imply swapped commutativity]
\label{thm:core-additive-stability-not-swapped-commutative}
There is a finite equality-classified carrier with a deterministic total continuation relation $\Gamma$ such that the carrier $\NameCert$, closure, two-sided unit, associativity, same-source determinacy, and the source-to-pattern and source-to-ledger soundness fields of the naming template hold, while the swapped-source additive commutativity field
$$
  \Gamma(x,y,r)\quad\text{and}\quad \Gamma(y,x,s)\quad\Rightarrow\quad r=s
$$
fails.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $C=\{e,p,q\}$ and use equality as the classifier. Define $\star:C\times C\to C$ by
$$
\begin{array}{c|ccc}
\star & e & p & q\\
\hline
e & e & p & q\\
p & p & p & p\\
q & q & q & q
\end{array}
$$
and let $\Gamma(x,y,r)$ mean $x\star y=r$.

The carrier certificate of \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate} holds because $e$ inhabits $C$, equality is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive, and equality transports membership in $C$. For the additive source role of \autoref{def:additive-source-specification}, use an ordered pair $(x,y)$ together with the graph witness $\Gamma(x,y,r)$. Take the pattern specification and ledger policy to be this same graph witness, matching the ledger role of \autoref{def:additive-stability-and-ledger-policy}; hence the pattern-soundness and ledger-soundness roles in \autoref{def:typed-naming-certificate} are immediate. The classifier role of \autoref{def:additive-classifier-specification} is literal equality on result elements.

Closure is visible from the table. The row and column for $e$ show $e\star x=x$ and $x\star e=x$ for every $x\in C$. Same-source determinacy holds because $\Gamma$ is the graph of the function $\star$: if $\Gamma(x,y,r)$ and $\Gamma(x,y,s)$, then $r=x\star y=s$. This is the finite equality-classified analogue of the determinacy field in \autoref{def:additive-stability-certificate}.

Associativity follows by cases on the left factor. If $x=e$, then $(e\star y)\star z=y\star z=e\star(y\star z)$. If $x=p$, then $p\star y=p$ and $p\star t=p$ for every $t\in C$, so both $(p\star y)\star z$ and $p\star(y\star z)$ are $p$. The case $x=q$ is identical with $q$ in place of $p$.

The swapped-source field fails. The table gives $\Gamma(p,q,p)$ and $\Gamma(q,p,q)$, but the equality classifier does not identify $p$ and $q$. Thus the fields used for the monoid-like activation in \autoref{thm:unary-continuation-monoid-activation} do not force the swapped-source additive commutativity field.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AddUp.AddUpThreePoint\_unital\_graph\_not\_swapped\_commutative}

\label{thm:unary-continuation-monoid-activation-from-stability}
Let $C:\NameCert(\Unary,\hsame)$ be the core carrier certificate of \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}. Fix the continuation source, pattern, classifier, and ledger policies of \autoref{def:additive-source-specification}, \autoref{def:additive-pattern-specification}, \autoref{def:additive-classifier-specification}, and \autoref{def:additive-stability-and-ledger-policy}. holds. No premise asserting $\mathsf{AddCommField}$ or $\UnaryComm$ is required.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AddUp.AddUnaryContinuationMonoid\_activation\_from\_stability}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:typed-naming-certificate}, licensing a derived interface amounts to supplying the source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger fields required by its certificate. The carrier-and-classifier core is $C$, which is a certificate for the unary carrier classified by $\hsame$ in the sense of \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}.

The source field is the ordered unary continuation source: a pair of unary histories together with a witness $\ContR(h,k,r)$. This is the source specification of \autoref{def:additive-source-specification}. The pattern field is the corresponding unary continuation behavior of \autoref{def:additive-pattern-specification}. The classifier field is $\hsame$ on generated continuation results, as in \autoref{def:additive-classifier-specification}. The ledger field records the same ordered inputs and continuation witness, as in \autoref{def:additive-stability-and-ledger-policy}. Thus source-to-pattern soundness and source-to-ledger soundness follow by unpacking these four policies.

It remains to check the stability component. Unary closure is the closure field supplied by $S$. The empty-history unit is the unit field supplied by $S$, matching \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-unit}. Associativity up to $\hsame$ is the associativity field supplied by $S$, matching \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-associativity}. Same-source determinacy says that for fixed ordered unary inputs $h,k$, any two witnesses $\ContR(h,k,r)$ and $\ContR(h,k,r')$ produce classified results $\hsame(r,r')$; this is precisely the determinacy field listed in \autoref{def:additive-stability-certificate}.

These fields give a closed unary carrier, a two-sided unit, associative continuation, deterministic classification for fixed ordered sources, and an explicit ledger. That is the full certificate for $\UnaryContMonoidUp$. A swapped-source field would compare $\ContR(h,k,r)$ with $\ContR(k,h,s)$; no such comparison is used in the construction, so neither $\mathsf{AddCommField}$ nor $\UnaryComm$ enters the proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Two-element monoids cannot witness additive noncommutativity]
\label{thm:two-element-monoid-cannot-witness-additive-noncommutativity}
Let $C$ be an equality-classified carrier whose unary source is total. Let $e,u\in C$ and assume every $x\in C$ satisfies $x=e$ or $x=u$. Let $\star:C\times C\to C$ be a monoid operation with unit $e$, and let the additive continuation relation be the graph of $\star$:
$$
  \Gamma(x,y,z)\quad\text{means}\quad x\star y=z.
$$
Then for all $x,y,z,z'\in C$,
$$
  \Gamma(x,y,z)
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  \Gamma(y,x,z')
  \quad\Rightarrow\quad
  z=z'.
$$
Thus no equality-classified monoid with at most two carrier elements and total unary source can witness failure of the swapped-source additive commutativity field measured by \autoref{def:additive-classifier-specification}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:additive-source-specification}, an additive source in this interpretation is an ordered pair together with a graph witness $\Gamma(x,y,z)$. Since the unary source is total, every pair of carrier elements is admissible. By \autoref{def:additive-classifier-specification}, the result comparison is the carrier classifier; here that classifier is equality. It is therefore enough to prove $x\star y=y\star x$ for arbitrary $x,y\in C$, because $\Gamma(x,y,z)$ and $\Gamma(y,x,z')$ identify $z$ with $x\star y$ and $z'$ with $y\star x$.

Use the cover $t=e$ or $t=u$ for each carrier element $t$. If $x=e$, the left-unit law gives $x\star y=e\star y=y$, and the right-unit law gives $y\star x=y\star e=y$. Hence $x\star y=y\star x$. If $y=e$, the same argument with the two roles exchanged gives $x\star y=x=y\star x$.

The only remaining case is $x=u$ and $y=u$. In that case both ordered products are the same expression $u\star u$, so $x\star y=y\star x$ by reflexivity of equality. Combining this product equality with the two graph witnesses gives $z=z'$. The conclusion is exactly the swapped-source comparison not supplied by the monoid-like fields of \autoref{def:additive-stability-certificate}. In contrast, \autoref{thm:core-additive-stability-not-swapped-commutative} shows that three carrier elements suffice for an equality-classified unital deterministic graph to fail that comparison.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AddUp.AddUpTwoPoint\_unital\_graph\_swapped\_commutative}

\label{thm:unary-continuation-activation-without-commutativity}
Let the typed naming-certificate template of \autoref{def:typed-naming-certificate} be instantiated on the unary carrier with classifier $\hsame$. Assume a core carrier certificate $\NameCert(\Unary,\hsame)$ in the sense of \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}. The naming template of \autoref{def:typed-naming-certificate} licenses a derived interface once its source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger fields are supplied. The assumed $\NameCert(\Unary,\hsame)$ supplies the carrier inhabitedness, classifier reflexivity, classifier symmetry, classifier transitivity, and carrier transport fields listed in \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}. Thus the unary carrier and its classifier core are available before any binary behavior is considered.

The source field is the ordered pair of unary histories together with a continuation witness, as in \autoref{def:additive-source-specification}. The pattern field is the continuation behavior of \autoref{def:additive-pattern-specification}. The classifier field is $\hsame$ on generated results, as in \autoref{def:additive-classifier-specification}. The ledger field records the ordered inputs and the continuation witness, as in \autoref{def:additive-stability-and-ledger-policy}. The assumed soundness fields send every source witness to this pattern witness and to this ledger witness.

It remains only to read the stability fields. Unary closure gives closure of the carrier under the continuation result. The empty-history unit gives the left and right unit clauses for the unary continuation operation, matching \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-unit}. Associativity up to $\hsame$ gives the monoid-like associativity clause, matching \autoref{lem:unary-continuation-associativity}. \end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Two-element unital continuation commutativity]
\label{thm:two-element-unital-continuation-commutativity}
Let $U$ be the unary part of a finite equality-classified carrier with classifier $\hsame$, and assume $U$ has exactly the two elements $e$ and $u$. Let $\star:U\times U\to U$ be a total binary operation with two-sided unit $e$:
$$
  e\star t=t
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  t\star e=t
  \quad\text{for every }t\in U.
$$
Suppose the continuation relation on $U$ is the graph of $\star$:
$$
  \ContR(h,k,r)
  \quad\text{if and only if}\quad
  r=h\star k.
$$
Then for all $h,k,r,r'\in U$,
$$
  \ContR(h,k,r)
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  \ContR(k,h,r')
  \quad\Rightarrow\quad
  \hsame(r,r').
$$
No associativity premise is needed.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AddUp.AddUpTwoPoint\_unital\_continuation\_commutativity}
\begin{proof}
The source role of \autoref{def:additive-source-specification} treats a continuation source as an ordered pair together with a continuation witness, and the classifier role of \autoref{def:additive-classifier-specification} compares generated results by $\hsame$. In the equality-classified instance of the carrier certificate of \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}, proving $\hsame(r,r')$ is the same as proving $r=r'$.

Assume $\ContR(h,k,r)$ and $\ContR(k,h,r')$. Since $\ContR$ is the graph of $\star$, these witnesses give $r=h\star k$ and $r'=k\star h$. It is enough to show $h\star k=k\star h$.

Because $U$ has exactly the two elements $e$ and $u$, each of $h$ and $k$ is either $e$ or $u$. If $h=e$, then $h\star k=e\star k=k$ by the left-unit law, while $k\star h=k\star e=k$ by the right-unit law. If $k=e$, the same unit laws give $h\star k=h$ and $k\star h=e\star h=h$.

The only remaining case is $h=u$ and $k=u$. Then both ordered products are the identical product $u\star u$, so they are equal by reflexivity. Thus $h\star k=k\star h$ in every case, and the two graph identities give $r=r'$. Equality classification then gives $\hsame(r,r')$.

Relative to the stability fields of \autoref{def:additive-stability-certificate}, this is an automatic two-element consequence of graph determinacy and the unit laws, not a separate swapped-source field. Together with \autoref{thm:core-additive-stability-not-swapped-commutative}, it identifies three equality classes as the first possible size for such a unital deterministic swapped-source failure.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Zero-result continuation has no same-surface reverse]
\label{prop:unary-zero-result-one-way-boundary}
In the public $\AddUp$ surface of \autoref{thm:concrete-addup-namecert}, the displayed implication
$$
  \Unary(h)
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  \Unary(k)
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  \ContR(h,k,\Ezero(z))
  \quad\Rightarrow\quad
  \bot
$$
is a one-way interpretation boundary. More precisely, for fixed unary inputs $h$ and $k$, no same-surface reverse rule may use only the zero-result absence row to supply the missing zero-result continuation row
$$
  \neg\ContR(h,k,\Ezero(z))
  \quad\Rightarrow\quad
  \ContR(h,k,\Ezero(z)).
$$
Consequently the no-zero-result row cannot be completed into an equivalence row on the visible $\mathsf{AddSource}$, $\mathsf{AddPattern}$, $\mathsf{AddClassifier}$, or $\mathsf{AddLedger}$ surface.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Assume $h$ and $k$ are unary histories. By \autoref{lem:unary-cont-e0-result-absurd}, every displayed continuation witness
$$
  \ContR(h,k,\Ezero(z))
$$
is impossible. Hence the zero-result absence row
$$
  \neg\ContR(h,k,\Ezero(z))
$$
is already forced by the unary source hypotheses.

Suppose a same-surface reverse rule of the displayed form were available. Applying it to the absence row just obtained would produce a continuation witness
$$
  \ContR(h,k,\Ezero(z)).
$$
Applying \autoref{lem:unary-cont-e0-result-absurd} again to the same unary inputs and this witness gives a contradiction. Thus the reverse rule cannot coexist with the unary continuation calculus.

The certificate rows in this chapter do not contain another route around this obstruction. By \autoref{def:additive-source-specification}, a source row for an additive output contains an actual continuation witness. By \autoref{def:additive-pattern-specification}, the pattern row is that continuation witness together with the unary input discipline. By \autoref{def:additive-stability-and-ledger-policy}, the ledger records the same witness rather than replacing it by an absence statement. Finally, \autoref{def:additive-classifier-specification} compares generated result histories only after a result has been generated. Therefore the absence of a zero-headed continuation is not a hidden source, pattern, classifier, or ledger witness for a zero-headed result; it is exactly the boundary excluding such a row.
\end{proof}
