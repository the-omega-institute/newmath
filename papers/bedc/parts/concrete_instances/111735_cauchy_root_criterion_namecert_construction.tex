It sits beside the real-series carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realseries-namecert}, the regular root-test packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-root-test-namecert}, the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Cauchy root criterion carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-root-criterion-carrier}
A $\CauchyRootCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(A,Q,W,D,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the $\RealSeriesUp$ source row, exposing the real-valued term stream only through the series packet.
\item $Q$ is the root-bound witness row, recording a displayed dyadic bound below the unit scale and the finite entry index after which the bound is admitted.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ tail-window row used to inspect the term stream and partial-tail data named by $A$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, carrying the precision cells, slack conversions, and comparison budgets used by the root-bound witness.
\item $T$ is the $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$ handoff row that turns $Q,W,D$ into a regular Cauchy tail route.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained only after the root-test handoff has consumed the same tail windows and dyadic tolerance row.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the regular rational readback is displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, root-bound, window, tolerance, handoff, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a root-bound request through $A,Q,W,D,T,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyRootCriterionUp$, $\RealSeriesUp$, $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRootCriterionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for a host root-limit theorem, selected infinite sum, quotient stream equality, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy root criterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-root-criterion-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-root-criterion-carrier}. The source, witness, window, tolerance, handoff, readback, seal, and structural rows are precisely $A,Q,W,D,T,R,E,H,C,P,N$.

The root-bound portion is read through $Q,W,D$. The bound and entry index are displayed by $Q$, the inspected tail data are displayed by $W$, and the tolerance comparisons are displayed by $D$.

The handoff row $T$ is then forced through $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$. It consumes the same windows and dyadic budget before any regular rational readback $R$ can be exposed.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since no omitted coordinate exists for host root limits, selected sums, quotient equality, choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness, these rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

$$

$$
Thus the root criterion supplies downstream RealSeries and RealPowerSeries chapters with a finite convergence-test certificate, while exporting no host completeness theorem, selected infinite sum, quotient stream equality, countable-choice schedule, or detached Real seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The terminal Real seal $E$ is therefore reached only after $R$. Componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming preserve this order, so no consumer receives host completeness, a selected sum, quotient stream equality, choice data, or a detached seal.
\end{proof}
