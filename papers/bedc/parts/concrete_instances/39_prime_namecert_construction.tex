\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\PrimeUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-prime-namecert}

This chapter gives a concrete naming-certificate construction for the prime number interface \(\PrimeUp\) at the paper-proof level. The construction sits above the unary natural-number certificate \(\NatUp\) (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert}) and the additive certificate \(\AddUp\) (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-add-namecert}), and is the first arithmetic chapter to exhibit a non-trivial property of unary histories beyond pure additive structure. The certificate is fully constructive: every theorem produces an explicit witness, primality is decidable by trial division on the underlying unary segment, and Euclid's infinitude of primes is supplied as an effective bound function rather than as a non-constructive existence claim. The standard limit results --- the fundamental theorem of arithmetic in both existence and uniqueness directions --- are reduced, in the same chapter, to closure laws on the prime classifier.
