\label{def:groundcompiler-recognizer-boundary-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $S$ is the source-channel row recording the encoded, decoded, and lossless channel evidence available to the compiler layer;
\item $R$ is the generated recognizer row that classifies the event-flow packet without treating raw source identity as proof identity;
\item $Q$ is the certificate-gate row through which accepted exports must pass before a downstream theorem or NameCert consumer may cite them;
\item $M$ is the metric-report row, readable only as compiler-layer measurement data;

\begin{theorem}[GroundCompiler recognizer-boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:groundcompiler-recognizer-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:groundcompiler-recognizer-boundary-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\GroundCompilerRecognizerBoundaryUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $G=(E,S,R,Q,M,V,B,H,C,P,N)$;
\item event-flow and source-channel readback through $E$ and $S$ before any recognizer row is consumed;
\item recognizer and certificate-gate exactness through $R$ and $Q$, with every accepted export routed through the gate;
\item report boundary separation for $M,V,B$, so metric, motif, and bootstrap rows remain evidence rows rather than theorem rows;
\item transport, continuation, provenance, and naming replay through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet $G$. The event-flow row $E$ and source-channel row $S$ are visible before the recognizer row is read, so the packet separates source-like compiler input from recognizer evidence.

Read the recognizer and gate rows next. The row $R$ records the generated recognizer result, while $Q$ records the certificate gate through which accepted exports pass. A consumer cannot skip $Q$, because no alternate accepted-export coordinate occurs in the carrier.

Now inspect the report rows. The metric row $M$, motif row $V$, and bootstrap row $B$ are separate evidence rows. They may be cited as displayed compiler-layer data, but they are not theorem rows and do not discharge a missing proof obligation.

Finally project $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport the same components, replay the displayed consumer routes, cite the GroundCompiler audit map and audit-map family sibling as provenance, and bind the local name. Hence the NameCert surface is exhausted by the five listed obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GroundCompiler recognizer-boundary non-escape]
\label{thm:groundcompiler-recognizer-boundary-nonescape}
For every accepted $\GroundCompilerRecognizerBoundaryUp$ carrier $G=(E,S,R,Q,M,V,B,H,C,P,N)$, every compiler-facing or theorem-facing consumer read remains inside the displayed event-flow, source-channel, recognizer, certificate-gate, metric-report, motif-report, bootstrap-evidence, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows. In particular, generated theorem code is not read as proof, recognizer classification is not raw event-flow equality, and report evidence is not certificate evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier projection. The packet lists only $E,S,R,Q,M,V,B,H,C,P,N$, so every consumer read must enter through one of those displayed rows.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:groundcompiler-recognizer-boundary-namecert-obligations}. They force recognizer reads to pass through $E,S,R$ and accepted-export reads to pass through $Q$, while report reads stay in $M,V,B$.

The provenance and route rows then block hidden strengthening. Since $P$ cites the GroundCompiler audit map and its audit-map family sibling only as visible row sources, and since $C$ replays only displayed routes, no consumer can extract proof, raw identity, or an unconditional compiler tower from the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
