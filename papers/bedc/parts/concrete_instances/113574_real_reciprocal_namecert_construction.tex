\begin{definition}[Real reciprocal carrier]
\label{def:real-reciprocal-carrier}
A $\RealReciprocalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(X,A,B,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the accepted $\RealUp$ source whose reciprocal is requested.
\item $A$ is the nonzero-apartness row, recording the displayed lower-bound witness that keeps $X$ away from the zero boundary.
\item $B$ is the $\CauchyInverseBudgetUp$ row that selects the finite reciprocal-window budget for the source name and apartness witness.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row produced by the budgeted reciprocal route.
\item $D$ is the $\RegularCauchyDivisionUp$ handoff row, exposing the same reciprocal as division of the unit regular name by the guarded source name.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal for the reciprocal candidate.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, apartness, budget, readback, division handoff, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for source inspection, apartness exposure, budget selection, regular readback, division handoff, and Real sealing.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealReciprocalUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\CauchyInverseBudgetUp$, $\RegularCauchyDivisionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealReciprocalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only through $X,A,B,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for total inversion, trichotomy, quotient real equality, or an ambient field inverse.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real reciprocal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-reciprocal-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealReciprocalUp$ carrier
$$

$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RealReciprocalUp}$ surface consists exactly of source Real admission, nonzero-apartness exposure, finite Cauchy inverse budget selection, $\RegSeqRatUp$ reciprocal readback, $\RegularCauchyDivisionUp$ handoff, terminal $\RealUp$ sealing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming for the same finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:real-reciprocal-carrier}. The rows $X,A,B,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N$ list all analytic and structural data available to an accepted reciprocal packet.

The analytic route is ordered. The apartness row $A$ is exposed before the inverse budget $B$, the budget produces the regular rational readback $Q$, and the division handoff $D$ identifies the same guarded reciprocal route before the terminal Real seal $E$ is read.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ then transport, replay, source, and name the displayed route. Since no additional coordinate is present, the local NameCert obligations are exhausted by the listed packet and cannot import total inversion, trichotomy, quotient real equality, or ambient field completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real reciprocal apartness-domain stability]
\label{thm:real-reciprocal-apartness-domain-stability}
Let $R=(X,A,B,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N)$ and $R'=(X',A',B',Q',D',E',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\RealReciprocalUp$ packets. If the displayed transport rows relate $X,A,B,Q,D,E$ to $X',A',B',Q',D',E'$ componentwise by $\hsame$, then the reciprocal route for $R'$ still exposes $A'$ before any budget, readback, division handoff, or Real seal row is consumed. A route with no transported apartness row remains outside the reciprocal domain.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations for $R$. They place $A$ before $B,Q,D,E$ in the displayed reciprocal route.

Use the componentwise $\hsame$ rows to transport the source, apartness, budget, readback, handoff, and seal coordinates to $R'$. The transported route has the same row order because $\hsame$ changes only the displayed coordinate representatives, not the carrier inventory.

It follows that $A'$ is still the first domain row for the reciprocal read. If the transported route omits $A'$, then no row can justify the Cauchy inverse budget or the later Real seal, so the route remains a boundary attempt rather than an accepted reciprocal packet.
\end{proof}

Next read the Cauchy inverse budget row $B$. This row is the finite budget surface supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyinversebudget-namecert}; it selects only displayed windows and lower-bound data.

Finally inspect the carrier inventory. Since the packet contains only $X,A,B,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N$, there is no coordinate for total reciprocal choice, trichotomy, quotient real equality, or ambient Real completeness.
