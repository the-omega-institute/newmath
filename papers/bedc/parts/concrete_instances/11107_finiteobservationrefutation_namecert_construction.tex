\begin{theorem}[FiniteObservationRefutation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-observation-refutation-namecert-obligations}

$$

trace and bundle exposure through $T,\Pi$, observed and model signature exposure through $O,M$, finite-fit exposure through $F$, defeating-observation exposure through $D$, verdict exposure through $V$, ledger and open-continuation exposure through $L,E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The tuple $J=(T,\Pi,O,M,F,D,V,L,E,H,C,P,N)$ is the complete finite packet, so carrier admission exposes every row that can be read by a consumer.

Read the observational rows first. The trace and bundle rows $T,\Pi$ determine the finite questions, while $O$ and $M$ are the observed and model-generated signatures compared by the finite-fit row $F$.

The defeating observation is stored in $D$ and classified by $V$. A finite defeat therefore enters the certificate as a visible row of the same packet, not as a hidden host objection or a global claim about all observations.

The ledger and continuation rows separate defeat from openness. The ledger $L$ records the finite scope and the refusal of final confirmation, while $E$ preserves admissible observations not yet queried. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, authenticate, and name that same packet, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
