\begin{theorem}[Boundary orbit inventory polynomial obligations]
\label{thm:boundary-orbit-inventory-polynomial-obligations}
For every accepted $\BoundaryOrbitInventoryPolynomialUp$ carrier $I=(O,K,E,M,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BoundaryOrbitInventoryPolynomialUp}$ surface consists exactly of orbit-table admission through $O$, codon-count table admission through $K$, coefficient-ledger exactness through $E$, marginal-projection exactness through $M$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:boundary-orbit-inventory-polynomial-carrier}. The orbit table and codon count table are the only source rows, while the coefficient ledger and marginal projection rows are the only polynomial-facing rows.

Coefficient exactness is read from $E$. Each displayed coefficient is determined by grouping the rows of $O$ and weighting them by the count row $K$; no coefficient row is available without those two source tables.

Marginal exactness is read from $M$. A marginal projection is permitted only as a specialization of the coefficient ledger, so it cannot replace the orbit table or codon count table by a hidden enumeration.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier lacks biological stochastic, quotient-codon, hidden-table, and unbounded-enumeration coordinates, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:boundary-orbit-inventory-coefficient-determinacy}

Removing any coefficient row from $E$ leaves a marginal query without a displayed source, and removing any orbit or codon count row leaves the corresponding coefficient without a displayed inventory witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:boundary-orbit-inventory-polynomial-obligations}. A coefficient query first exposes the orbit table $O$ and codon count table $K$, then reads the coefficient ledger $E$.

The marginal projection row $M$ is downstream of the ledger. It can specialize the displayed polynomial, but it cannot introduce a coefficient absent from $E$ or reconstruct a missing source row absent from $O$ or $K$.

Transport and replay preserve the same dependency order. Therefore a coefficient marginal is determined exactly by the displayed table and ledger rows, and deletion of any required row breaks the finite NameCert route.
\end{proof}
