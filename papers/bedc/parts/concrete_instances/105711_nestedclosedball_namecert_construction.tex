Following Bishop and Bridges and the Cantor-style nested closed ball criterion, the $\NestedClosedBallUp$ packet records explicit nested closed ball completion data as finite NameCert rows. It sits beside the metric-completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the complete-metric service of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter evidence of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a displayed descending ball route rather than a host completeness theorem, a quotient completion, or a choice-selected center.

\begin{definition}[Nested closed ball carrier]
\label{def:nested-closed-ball-carrier}
A $\NestedClosedBallUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(M,K,F,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ source row naming the ambient completion request before any ball center is read.
\item $K$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ service row available only as a displayed limit-service dependency, not as an independent selector.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ filter row generated by the descending family of closed balls.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row inspecting the indexed balls and their center approximations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row for the inspected center and radius data.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ diameter ledger, recording the dyadic shrink schedule, containment witnesses, and late-radius comparisons.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $F,S,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the completion source, complete-metric service, filter, windows, readback, dyadic ledger, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a nested-ball request through $M,K,F,S,R,D,E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NestedClosedBallUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NestedClosedBallUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected limits, quotient completions, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Nested closed ball NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:nested-closed-ball-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\NestedClosedBallUp$ carrier
$$
B=(M,K,F,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\NestedClosedBallUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the metric-completion source, displayed complete-metric service, Cauchy-filter row, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic diameter ledger, terminal real seal, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming. Every nested-ball read is therefore routed through $M,K,F,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$, and no obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:nested-closed-ball-carrier}. The packet displays the completion source, the complete-metric service, the Cauchy filter generated by the nested balls, the finite windows, the rational readback, and the dyadic diameter ledger before the real seal can be consumed.

The constructive nested-ball content is the route through $F,S,R,D$. The filter row records the descending family, the stream row exposes only finite indexed windows, the regular rational row reads center and radius data, and the dyadic row records the shrink and containment comparisons.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly that route. Since the carrier contains no open-cover compactness row, selected limit, quotient completion, countable-choice schedule, ambient real-completeness row, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Nested closed ball Cauchy-center route]
\label{thm:nested-closed-ball-cauchy-center-route}

The center data are then inspected through finite windows. The stream row $S$ exposes finite ball indices, the regular rational row $R$ reads the center and radius approximants, and the dyadic ledger $D$ records the shrink and containment comparisons needed for a Cauchy-center route.

\begin{proof}
