\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\IntervalNewtonUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-intervalnewton-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Moore--Kearfott--Cloud interval analysis, the $\IntervalNewtonUp$ packet records interval Newton and Krawczyk enclosure certificates as finite BEDC rows. It names a displayed box, function enclosure, slope or derivative enclosure, correction row, narrowing ledger, validated readback route, real-name readback, transport witnesses, replay data, provenance, and local naming row. It is not a solver oracle, a host zero-existence theorem, a global root-isolation method, a completeness assertion, or a floating-point semantics package.

The carrier reads interval operations through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-intervalarithmetic-namecert}, finite validated enclosures through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-validatednumerics-namecert}, displayed correction provenance through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-newtoniteration-namecert}, and finite Taylor or slope remainders through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-taylormodel-namecert}. These sibling rows are the entire dependency surface for the enclosure certificates below.

\begin{definition}[Interval Newton carrier]
\label{def:interval-newton-carrier}
An $\IntervalNewtonUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
I=(B,F,D,N,K,V,R,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the interval box row, read through $\IntervalArithmeticUp$ endpoint and enclosure ledgers;
\item $F$ is the function-enclosure row over $B$, with every enclosure read routed through finite interval operations;
\item $D$ is the derivative or slope enclosure row, supplied either directly as an interval row or through a finite $\TaylorModelUp$ remainder packet;
\item $N$ is the finite Newton or Krawczyk correction row, including the displayed center, inverse or preconditioner row, and correction enclosure;
\item $K$ is the containment and narrowing ledger recording that the correction row stays inside, or narrows, the displayed box $B$;
\item $V$ is the $\ValidatedNumericsUp$ enclosure row read by downstream consumers;
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback route for the center, endpoint, and correction observations;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the box, function, derivative, correction, containment, readback, and validated rows;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes by which the displayed rows are replayed inside their source certificates;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\IntervalArithmeticUp$, $\ValidatedNumericsUp$, $\NewtonIterationUp$, $\TaylorModelUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the displayed kernel rows;
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\IntervalNewtonUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
Two accepted carriers are classified together only by transporting these displayed coordinates through the recorded $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows. The classifier has no coordinate for a hidden solver state, a selected zero, host floating-point behavior, quotient real equality, or completeness of the ambient real line.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Interval Newton NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:interval-newton-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\IntervalNewtonUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:interval-newton-carrier} consists of six rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite packet $I=(B,F,D,N,K,V,R,H,C,P,L)$;
\item correction containment saying that the displayed Newton or Krawczyk row $N$ is admitted only with its containment and narrowing ledger $K$;
\item interval-arithmetic soundness for the box, function, derivative or slope, and correction enclosures;
\item validated-consumer handoff through the displayed $\ValidatedNumericsUp$ row $V$ and the readback row $R$;
\item transport under the componentwise $\hsame$ rows of $H$ and replay under the $\Cont$ routes of $C$;
\item ledger non-escape, meaning every consumer-visible claim is projected from $B,F,D,N,K,V,R,H,C,P,L$ and from no other numerical object.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:interval-newton-carrier}. The box, function enclosure, derivative or slope enclosure, correction, containment ledger, validated readback, real-name route, transport, replay, provenance, and local name are visible fields of one finite packet.

Correction containment is read from $N$ together with $K$. The packet may expose a Krawczyk or interval Newton row only when the same displayed ledger records how that row is contained in, or narrows, the box $B$.

Interval-arithmetic soundness is inherited by routing all endpoint and enclosure operations through the $\IntervalArithmeticUp$ rows named in $P$. When a derivative or slope enclosure is supplied by a Taylor model, the packet reads only the finite $\TaylorModelUp$ remainder and interval-operation rows.

The consumer handoff is then $V$ together with $R$. Transport and replay use only the componentwise rows $H$ and $C$, so every exported obligation is exhausted by the carrier coordinates and no solver oracle or external real-semantics datum remains.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Interval Newton Krawczyk enclosure]
\label{thm:interval-newton-krawczyk-enclosure}
Let $I=(B,F,D,N,K,V,R,H,C,P,L)$ be an accepted $\IntervalNewtonUp$ carrier. Every Krawczyk or interval Newton enclosure exported by $I$ narrows the displayed box $B$ only through the $\IntervalArithmeticUp$ operation rows named by $P$ and through the displayed $\NewtonIterationUp$ or $\TaylorModelUp$ rows used to form $N$ and $D$. The exported enclosure is exactly the row recorded by $K$ and validated by $V$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer-visible narrowing claim. By the carrier definition, the only box available to the packet is $B$, and the only correction row available is $N$.

Read the arithmetic used by the correction through the $\IntervalArithmeticUp$ dependency rows named in $P$. The center, inverse or preconditioner, function enclosure, derivative or slope enclosure, and final interval operation are all displayed rows of the same finite ledger.

If the correction row is presented as a Newton step, its provenance is the finite $\NewtonIterationUp$ row named in $P$. If the slope or derivative enclosure is presented by finite Taylor data, it is read through the $\TaylorModelUp$ row named in $P$. In either case, the packet keeps the operation inside visible BEDC rows.

Finally project $K$ and $V$. The containment ledger $K$ records the narrowed box, and the validated row $V$ is the only downstream enclosure exported by the packet. No separate analytic root theorem, host solver state, or floating-point rounding semantics is consulted.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Interval Newton validated consumer boundary]
\label{thm:interval-newton-validated-consumer-boundary}
Every $\ValidatedNumericsUp$ or root-isolation consumer of an accepted $\IntervalNewtonUp$ packet reads the correction only through the displayed carrier
$$
I=(B,F,D,N,K,V,R,H,C,P,L).
$$
The consumer receives the interval box, function enclosure, derivative or slope enclosure, correction row, containment ledger, validated enclosure row, real-name readback route, transport rows, replay routes, provenance, and local naming row. It receives no solver oracle, no host zero, no floating-point semantics, no quotient real equality, and no completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. A validated or root-isolation consumer can inspect only the fields $B,F,D,N,K,V,R,H,C,P,L$.

Use \autoref{thm:interval-newton-namecert-obligations} to identify the exported obligation rows. The consumer handoff is $V$ and $R$, while transport and replay are restricted to $H$ and $C$.

Use \autoref{thm:interval-newton-krawczyk-enclosure} for the correction read. The Krawczyk or interval Newton row is visible only through $\IntervalArithmeticUp$ operations and through the displayed $\NewtonIterationUp$ or $\TaylorModelUp$ provenance named in $P$.

Thus every consumer-facing row is a projection of the finite packet. Since no carrier coordinate names a solver oracle, selected zero, hardware arithmetic semantics, quotient-real equality witness, or completeness theorem, those data cannot be exported by the boundary.
\end{proof}
