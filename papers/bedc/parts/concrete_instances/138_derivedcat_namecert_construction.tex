\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DerivedCatUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-derivedcat-namecert}

The derived-category interface $\DerivedCatUp$ packages the localisation of a chain-complex category at quasi-isomorphisms. It depends on $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-category-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\CohomologyUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cohomology-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:derivedcat-carrier}

\begin{definition}[DerivedCatUp NameCert obligation surface]
\label{def:derivedcat-namecert-obligation-surface}
Fix a $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ presenting a chain-complex category $\mathcal{C}$ and a $\NameCert_{\CohomologyUp}$ presenting cohomology readback on those complexes. The $\DerivedCatUp$ obligation surface is the following visible localization interface.

The traditional target is the localization of the chain-complex category at quasi-isomorphisms. The BEDC source is the paired source $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\CohomologyUp}$. The carrier is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ package whose rows expose chain-complex object histories, homotopy-class morphism histories, and quasi-isomorphism witnesses read by the cohomology certificate. The classifier $\sim_{\DerivedCatUp}$ is generated by zigzags whose elementary steps are quasi-isomorphism witnesses and category-level morphism classifiers, with no primitive quotient constructor.

The stability obligations are reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity of displayed zigzags; transport of the carrier along the generated classifier; congruence of represented roof composition; and descent of the visible ledger along classifier-equivalent representatives. The ledger records roofs, quasi-isomorphism witnesses, cone and cohomology rows, and composition witnesses. Exactness is the visible localization readback: two displayed representatives are classified exactly when the ledger supplies a finite zigzag through quasi-isomorphism rows and category morphism rows. The standard bridge field is absent.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:derivedcat-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Derived localization carrier obligation]
\label{thm:derivedcat-localization-carrier-obligation}
The carrier in \autoref{def:derivedcat-namecert-obligation-surface} exposes exactly the chain-complex objects and homotopy-class morphism histories supplied by $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$, together with the quasi-isomorphism witnesses supplied by $\NameCert_{\CohomologyUp}$. It carries no object, morphism, or weak-equivalence row except through these displayed certificate fields.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DerivedCatUp.DerivedCatLocalizationRoof\_carrier\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier row of \autoref{def:derivedcat-namecert-obligation-surface}. Its object and morphism coordinates are the object and morphism rows of the chosen $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$, so every carried localization endpoint has a displayed category object or homotopy-class morphism history.

The weak-equivalence coordinate is read from the cohomology certificate: a morphism row is marked invertible for localization only when the $\NameCert_{\CohomologyUp}$ ledger supplies the corresponding quasi-isomorphism witness. Hence every exposed quasi-isomorphism is certificate-visible.

Conversely, any category object row, homotopy-class morphism row, and cohomology quasi-isomorphism witness may be injected into the carrier by the displayed package constructor. Constructor inversion on that package shows there are no additional carrier cases. This proves the exact exposure claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Derived quasi-isomorphism classifier obligation]
\label{thm:derivedcat-quasi-isomorphism-classifier-obligation}
The classifier $\sim_{\DerivedCatUp}$ of \autoref{def:derivedcat-namecert-obligation-surface} is the finite zigzag closure generated by cohomology quasi-isomorphism witnesses and the existing category morphism classifiers. It is not an independent quotient primitive.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction is induction on the displayed classifier derivation. The reflexive case is the empty zigzag. A category-morphism classifier step gives a one-step zigzag labelled by the $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ row. A quasi-isomorphism step gives a one-step zigzag labelled by the $\NameCert_{\CohomologyUp}$ witness. Symmetry reverses the finite zigzag, and transitivity concatenates the two finite zigzags.

For the reverse direction, induct on the finite zigzag. The empty zigzag gives reflexivity. In the cons step, the head is either a category-morphism classifier row or a cohomology quasi-isomorphism witness, hence gives an elementary classifier step. Compose it with the induction hypothesis for the tail by transitivity of the generated classifier.

All constructors used in both directions are the displayed generator constructors and the reflexive, symmetric, and transitive closure rules. Since no step appeals to an unlabelled endpoint identification, the classifier is precisely the generated zigzag closure.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DerivedCatUp.DerivedCatVisibleLocalizationClassifier\_endpoints}

\begin{theorem}[Derived composition descent obligation]
\label{thm:derivedcat-composition-descent-obligation}
Composition of represented roofs descends along $\sim_{\DerivedCatUp}$. If two left roofs and two right roofs are pairwise classified by the generated zigzag classifier, then the composite roofs obtained from the $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ composition rows and the $\NameCert_{\CohomologyUp}$ quasi-isomorphism stability rows are again classified by $\sim_{\DerivedCatUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Represent each morphism by a roof ledger row. The composition row first forms the category-level composite using the associativity and unit witnesses already exposed by $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$. These witnesses identify the usual alternative parenthesizations and inserted identity rows inside the category morphism classifier.

Now induct over the displayed zigzag comparing the left roof representatives. For an elementary category-classifier step, composition congruence in $\NameCert_{\CategoryUp}$ transports the step through the composite. For an elementary quasi-isomorphism step, the cohomology stability row says that the pulled-back or pasted weak-equivalence witness remains a quasi-isomorphism witness, so it is again a generator for $\sim_{\DerivedCatUp}$.

Repeat the same induction over the right roof comparison. The two transported zigzags concatenate, and the category associativity/unit rows align the middle object choices used by the two roof-composition ledgers. The resulting finite zigzag is a classifier witness between the two composite roofs, which is exactly descent of composition.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DerivedCatUp.DerivedCatVisibleLocalizationClassifier\_composition\_descent}

\begin{theorem}[DerivedCatUp NameCert exactness obligation]
\label{thm:derivedcat-namecert-exactness-obligation}
The public $\NameCert_{\DerivedCatUp}$ fields determined by \autoref{def:derivedcat-namecert-obligation-surface} are exactly the visible localization carrier, the generated zigzag classifier, the stability rows, the roof and quasi-isomorphism ledger, the visible exactness readback, and the absent standard-bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the public certificate fields. The carrier field is the package of \autoref{thm:derivedcat-localization-carrier-obligation}, and the classifier field is the generated zigzag classifier of \autoref{thm:derivedcat-quasi-isomorphism-classifier-obligation}. Thus the public object and morphism surfaces are exactly the displayed localization rows.

The stability fields are supplied by the reflexive, symmetric, and transitive zigzag constructors, by carrier transport along generated classifier steps, and by composition descent from \autoref{thm:derivedcat-composition-descent-obligation}. The ledger field is the visible list of roofs, quasi-isomorphism witnesses, cone and cohomology rows, and composition witnesses used by those constructions.

It remains to identify exactness and the bridge row. Exactness is constructor inversion on the generated classifier: a public equality witness is precisely a finite ledger zigzag through the displayed category and cohomology rows, and any such zigzag repacks as a public classifier witness. The standard bridge field is the designated absent row, so the certificate exposes no unrecorded external bridge data.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DerivedCatUp.DerivedCatVisibleLocalizationClassifier\_name\_certificate}

\begin{theorem}[DerivedCatUp roof identity unit laws]
\label{thm:derivedcat-roof-identity-unit-laws}
Let $r$ be a displayed $\DerivedCatUp$ roof ledger row from $X$ to $Y$ in the visible localization interface of \autoref{def:derivedcat-namecert-obligation-surface}. Let $\mathsf{id}_X$ and $\mathsf{id}_Y$ be displayed identity roof ledger rows at the two endpoints, with category components supplied by the empty identity hom-carriers of \autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-empty-identity} and with their weak-equivalence components recorded by the displayed cohomology quasi-isomorphism rows. If $\ell$ is a ledger result for composing $\mathsf{id}_Y$ with $r$ and $\rho$ is a ledger result for composing $r$ with $\mathsf{id}_X$, then both identity composites are classified with the original roof:
$$
\ell\sim_{\DerivedCatUp} r
\quad\text{and}\quad
\rho\sim_{\DerivedCatUp} r.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the roof ledger for $r$. It exposes the source and target object histories, the roof middle object, the category morphism leg, the cohomology quasi-isomorphism leg, and the composition witnesses used by \autoref{def:derivedcat-namecert-obligation-surface}. The two displayed identity roofs expose only empty identity hom-carrier rows at $X$ and $Y$, together with the cohomology rows marking those identity legs as quasi-isomorphisms.

For the composite $\mathsf{id}_Y\circ r$, the category morphism part is a displayed right-identity composite of the category leg of $r$. By \autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-right-identity-carrier-result}, that composite carries the same hom-carrier and its morphism history is $\HSame$ to the original category leg. The weak-equivalence part is obtained by pasting the displayed cohomology identity row with the quasi-isomorphism row already present in $r$; the cohomology ledger transports this pasted witness across the same identity comparison, so no new weak-equivalence generator is introduced.

Thus the ledger for $\ell$ differs from the ledger for $r$ by a finite list of displayed generator steps: one category morphism-classifier step collapsing the empty identity composite, followed by the reflexive or transported quasi-isomorphism rows on the weak-equivalence side. By \autoref{thm:derivedcat-quasi-isomorphism-classifier-obligation}, such a finite list is exactly a witness for $\ell\sim_{\DerivedCatUp} r$.

The composite $r\circ\mathsf{id}_X$ is symmetric at the source endpoint. Apply \autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-left-identity-carrier-result} to collapse the category leg containing the source identity, and use the displayed cohomology identity row to transport the quasi-isomorphism leg back to the original ledger row. These two displayed steps again form a finite zigzag made only of category morphism classifiers and quasi-isomorphism witnesses. The generated classifier therefore gives $\rho\sim_{\DerivedCatUp} r$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DerivedCatUp.DerivedCatVisibleLocalizationClassifier\_roof\_identity\_units}

\begin{theorem}[DerivedCatUp localization example boundary]
\label{thm:derivedcat-localization-example-boundary}
The visible localization classifier of
\autoref{def:derivedcat-namecert-obligation-surface} supplies two finite
example roofs inside the displayed $\DerivedCatUp$ package: the identity-roof
example at a chain-complex object and the one-step quasi-isomorphism roof
example for a displayed quasi-isomorphism. Both examples use only the displayed
chain-complex carrier, quasi-isomorphism classifier, roof identity and unit
rows, and $\Pkg$ provenance. They export no host localization, quotient object,
or derived functor calculus.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier rows come from
\autoref{thm:derivedcat-localization-carrier-obligation}. At an object $X$, the
identity roof uses the displayed category identity row as its category leg and
the cohomology identity quasi-isomorphism witness as its weak-equivalence leg.
The unit laws of
\autoref{thm:derivedcat-roof-identity-unit-laws} classify this roof with the
visible identity representative.

For a displayed quasi-isomorphism row, use
\autoref{thm:derivedcat-quasi-isomorphism-classifier-obligation}. The
one-step roof has that quasi-isomorphism as its weak-equivalence leg and the
displayed category morphism row as its other leg. Its classifier witness is a
single generator in the finite zigzag closure.

The obligation surface of
\autoref{def:derivedcat-namecert-obligation-surface} records both examples in
the same $\Pkg$ provenance ledger. Since each construction uses only carrier
rows, quasi-isomorphism generators, roof identity/unit rows, and package
provenance, neither example creates a host localization, quotient object, or
derived functor operation.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DerivedCatUp.DerivedCatLocalizationBoundary\_example\_boundary}
