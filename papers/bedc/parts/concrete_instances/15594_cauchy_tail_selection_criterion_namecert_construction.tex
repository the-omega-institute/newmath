\begin{definition}[Cauchy tail selection criterion carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-tail-selection-criterion-carrier}
A $\CauchyTailSelectionCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(S,R,M,D,I,Q,E,H,C,P,N)

with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row whose displayed observations are the only stream data admitted by the packet.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the windows in $S$ and carrying the regular rational approximants inspected by the criterion.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row that names the finite stage bound for each displayed tolerance request.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger through which every radius, endpoint, and comparison used by the tail criterion is named.
\item $I$ is the $\CauchyReindexingUp$ admissible-schedule row, recording the monotone cofinal route along which the selected tail may be inspected.
\item $Q$ is the cofinal tail request row, binding the requested precision to the displayed threshold and to the admissible schedule.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ boundary row, available only after $S,R,M,D,I,Q$ have been replayed.

\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these eleven rows. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, hidden infinite stream inspection, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy tail selection criterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-tail-selection-criterion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyTailSelectionCriterionUp$ carrier $T=(S,R,M,D,I,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyTailSelectionCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the eleven displayed rows, finite-window exposure through $S$, regular rational readback through $R$, threshold exposure through $M$, dyadic tolerance exposure through $D$, admissible-schedule exposure through $I$, cofinal tail-request binding through $Q$, Real-boundary ordering through $E$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-tail-selection-criterion-carrier}. The visible analytic rows are precisely the stream windows, regular rational readback, CauchyModulus threshold, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, CauchyReindexing schedule, cofinal tail request, and Real boundary.

Read the rows in dependency order. A tail-selection request first reaches the finite windows $S$ and their regular rational readback $R$, then consumes the threshold row $M$ and dyadic tolerance ledger $D$ that make the requested tail bound visible.

The schedule and request rows then close the local criterion. The admissible route $I$ records how the selected tail may be inspected, while $Q$ binds the cofinal request to the displayed threshold and schedule.

The rows $E,H,C,P,N$ add only boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming for the same finite route. Since the carrier has no quotient-stream, selected-limit, choice, trichotomy, hidden-inspection, or ambient-completeness coordinate, the displayed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy tail selection criterion RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-tail-selection-criterion-regseqrat-handoff}

$$
S\longmapsto R\longmapsto M\longmapsto D\longmapsto I\longmapsto Q\longmapsto(H,C,P,N).
$$
Thus a regular rational consumer receives the displayed stream window, the matching readback row, the explicit modulus threshold, the dyadic tolerance ledger, the admissible reindexing schedule, and the cofinal tail request before any Real-facing boundary can be inspected.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from a regular rational consumer request. By \autoref{def:cauchy-tail-selection-criterion-carrier}, the readback row $R$ is attached to the finite stream windows $S$, so the consumer cannot begin from an unbounded stream or a quotient representative.

The threshold and tolerance rows are consumed next. The row $M$ provides the displayed stage bound for the requested precision, while $D$ records the dyadic endpoint and radius comparisons used by that bound.

The admissible schedule $I$ and cofinal request $Q$ then bind the same threshold to a tail route. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$ preserve that order, so no detached RegSeqRat handoff exists.
\end{proof}
