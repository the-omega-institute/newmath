\begin{theorem}[Homology singleton cycle semantic name certificate]
\label{thm:homology-singleton-cycle-semantic-namecert}
Let the singleton cycle carrier contain exactly the BEDC histories
$h\hsame\mathsf{BHist.Empty}$. Let its classifier require both endpoints to
lie in that carrier and require $h\hsame k$. This classifier is a semantic
naming certificate for the carrier used simultaneously as source, pattern,
and ledger policy.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier is inhabited by $\mathsf{BHist.Empty}$ through reflexivity of
$\hsame$. Classifier reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are obtained by
unpacking the two endpoint-carrier witnesses and composing the underlying
$\hsame$ proofs. Carrier transport along a classifier edge is the right
endpoint carrier witness already stored in the classifier. The semantic
pattern and ledger fields are the source witness itself.
\end{proof}
