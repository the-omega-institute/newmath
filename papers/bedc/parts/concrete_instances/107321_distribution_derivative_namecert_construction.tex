\begin{definition}[Distribution derivative carrier]
\label{def:distribution-derivative-carrier}
A $\DistributionDerivativeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(A,D,I,E,Q,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the test-action row, listing the finite test probes, support windows, sign convention, and coordinate direction.
\item $D$ is the input $\DistributionUp$ row, read only by evaluating the tests named in $A$.
\item $I$ is the integration-by-parts sign row, recording the displayed minus sign and coordinate handoff for the derivative action.
\item $E$ is the candidate derivative action row on the same finite tests.
\item $Q$ is the shared $\RealUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for input and candidate action values.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row selecting the finite tests, tolerances, and readback prefixes.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $A,D,I,E,Q,W,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from test selection to input action, candidate action, readback, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DistributionDerivativeUp$, $\DistributionUp$, $\WeakDerivativeUp$, $\SobolevUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DistributionDerivativeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these ten rows. It has no coordinate for a host distribution quotient, selected representative, completed test-function space, full PDE regularity theorem, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{definition}

Since the carrier has no coordinate for distribution quotients, selected representatives, completed test-function spaces, full PDE regularity, or ambient Real completeness, the NameCert obligations are exactly the displayed finite rows.

\begin{theorem}[Distribution derivative weak-action route]
\label{thm:distribution-derivative-weak-action-route}

$$
A\longmapsto D,I,E\longmapsto Q\longmapsto W\longmapsto H,C,P,N.

The route supplies a distribution action and a candidate derivative action on the same displayed tests, with shared readback and stream windows. It does not supply quotient equality of distributions, a chosen representative, Sobolev completion, density of smooth tests, elliptic regularity, trace compactness, or a completed Real carrier.

\begin{proof}

Read $D,I,E$ next. The input distribution action and candidate derivative action are tied by the displayed sign handoff, and both actions are evaluated only on the tests already selected by $A$.

Finally read $Q,W,H,C,P,N$. These rows provide real and regular-rational readback, finite windows, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming for the same action route. No absent analytic completion or quotient surface can be recovered from that finite packet.
\end{proof}
