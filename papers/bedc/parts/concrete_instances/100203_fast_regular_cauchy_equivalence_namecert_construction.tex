\begin{theorem}[Fast regular Cauchy equivalence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:fast-cauchy-regular-equivalence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FastRegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$ carrier $K=(F,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FastRegularCauchyEquivalenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the fast dyadic-modulus row $F$, shared stream-window row $S$, dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, regular rational readback $Q$, real-seal row $E$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$;
\item fast-to-regular handoff, requiring each finite precision read of $F$ to pass through the displayed $S,D$ rows before the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $Q$ may be consumed;
\item regular-to-fast schedule extraction, requiring every regular Cauchy readback request on $Q$ to recover a finite fast schedule in $F$ from the same $S,D$ rows;
\item $\RealUp$-seal nonescape, requiring $E$ to remain terminal with respect to $F,S,D,Q$ and forbidding any detached completion read;
\item ledger refusal, forbidding quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, and any read not routed through the displayed carrier rows.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:fast-cauchy-regular-equivalence-carrier}. The tuple displays exactly the fast name, shared stream windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular readback, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

For the fast-to-regular handoff, read a precision request in $F$. The request names a finite window in $S$ and a dyadic tolerance row in $D$; those two rows are then repacked as the regular rational readback $Q$.

For the regular-to-fast schedule extraction, start with a finite regular Cauchy request on $Q$. Because $Q$ is stored only as the readback of the same $S,D$ route, the request recovers the stream windows and dyadic budget already admitted by $F$.

The real seal and refusal clauses follow by inspection of the carrier. The row $E$ appears only after $F,S,D,Q$, while quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, ambient completeness, and detached completion reads are not coordinates of $K$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Fast regular Cauchy two-way route]
\label{thm:fast-cauchy-regular-equivalence-two-way-route}
Every accepted $\FastRegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$ carrier $K=(F,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$ proves both finite readback directions:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the fast dyadic-modulus row $F$ yields the regular rational row $Q$ by reading the shared $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row $D$;
\item the regular rational row $Q$ yields the fast schedule of $F$ by reading the same $S,D$ rows and the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes.
\end{enumerate}
Both directions reach the $\RealUp$ seal only through $E$ and the completion-facing provenance of $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$. The route constructs no quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or hidden completion object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction begins with the fast row $F$. Its precision schedule selects finite observations in $S$ and dyadic tolerance rows in $D$; the regular readback row $Q$ is precisely the repackaging of those displayed rows.

The reverse direction begins with the regular row $Q$. Since the carrier stores $Q$ only downstream of $S,D$, every finite regular request traces back to the same stream windows and dyadic tolerances, and these rows determine the fast schedule already named by $F$.

Transport and replay do not change the route. The rows $H$ and $C$ preserve the componentwise reads through $F,S,D,Q,E$, while $P$ records the source certificates and $N$ binds the packet locally.

Finally, the $\RealUp$ seal is terminal. A completion-facing consumer may cite the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ provenance but receives only the displayed route through $E$, so no quotient stream, selected limit, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient completeness principle is exported.
\end{proof}
