\label{def:bishop-real-line-interface-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and tolerance ledger, recording admitted dyadic endpoints, positive tolerances, and comparison budgets before any real-line consumer reads a value-shaped claim.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, exposing only bounded observation windows of the named stream.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular Cauchy readback row attached to $W$ and to the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, reached only after $D,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $Q$ is the classifier-comparison row for accepted interface packets; it compares endpoint ledgers, finite windows, regular readback, and terminal seals without adding quotient stream equality or host real equality.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dyadic ledger, stream windows, regular readback, terminal Real seal, classifier comparison, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $D,W,R,E,Q,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for $\RealUp$ completeness, quotient stream equality, host real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, selected limits, metric completeness, or bridge checking.

\begin{theorem}[Bishop real-line interface NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-real-line-interface-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopRealLineInterfaceUp$ carrier $I=(D,W,R,E,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BishopRealLineInterfaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic endpoint and tolerance ledger, finite stream windows, regular Cauchy readback, terminal Real seal, classifier comparison, transport, continuation, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item dyadic-to-window admission, requiring every real-line request to expose its endpoint and tolerance budget in $D$ before reading a finite window in $W$;
\item regular readback admission, requiring the regular Cauchy row $R$ to be read from the displayed stream windows and dyadic ledger before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$ is consumed;
\item classifier transport, requiring $Q,H,C$ to preserve the displayed route $D,W,R,E$ under componentwise $\hsame$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay;
\item package provenance, requiring $P$ to route the packet through the already displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ surfaces;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $P$ and $N$ to export no $\RealUp$ completeness, quotient stream equality, host real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, selected limit, metric completeness, or bridge-checking row.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bishop-real-line-interface-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the dyadic ledger $D$, finite windows $W$, regular readback $R$, terminal seal $E$, classifier row $Q$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The dyadic-to-window and regular-readback obligations are inherited from the four L10 source surfaces. The dyadic endpoint and tolerance budget is the public source row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}; finite observation windows are read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}; and the regular Cauchy row is the readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}.

The terminal Real seal is downstream of those rows. A consumer reaches $E$ only after the $D,W,R$ route has been replayed, matching the Real seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} and the completion-facing discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert}.

Classifier transport is exhausted by $Q,H,C$. The classifier compares only displayed rows, $H$ transports them componentwise under $\hsame$, and $C$ replays the same route under $\Cont$.

Finally, provenance and local naming add no mathematical coordinate. Since the excluded objects are absent from $I$, the listed rows exhaust the local $\NameCert_{\BishopRealLineInterfaceUp}$ obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

\label{thm:bishop-real-line-interface-nonescape}
