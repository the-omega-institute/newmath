\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RoundedIdealCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-rounded-ideal-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, Vickers-style formal topology, and the Edalat--Heckmann formal-ball domain presentation of metric completion, the $\RoundedIdealCompletionUp$ packet names completion data as rounded directed families of formal balls rather than quotient classes of Cauchy names. It sits after the formal-ball interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-formalball-namecert}, the metric source surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completion-namecert}, the stream-window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the dyadic radius ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}.

The carrier is intended for completion consumers that can inspect finite lower information about an ideal. It records only finite $\BHist$ rows for roundedness, directedness, cofinal refinements, classifier transport, replay, provenance, and local naming, so the packet can be replayed without selecting a limit point or assuming that the ambient metric space is already complete.

\begin{definition}[Rounded ideal completion carrier]
\label{def:rounded-ideal-completion-carrier}
A $\RoundedIdealCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(M,F,D,S,R,E,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ source row, including accepted point histories, metric-distance witnesses, and the point classifier used by every formal-ball center.
\item $F$ is the finite $\FormalBallUp$ family row. Its entries are formal balls $(x,\rho)$ read through the same metric row $M$, with radius requests exposed through finite windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius ledger. It records positive-radius witnesses, strict comparison budgets, radius addition, and refinement inequalities for the rows of $F$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row used to read finite center observations and finite ideal membership requests.
\item $R$ is the roundedness row: every displayed ball in $F$ is accompanied by a smaller displayed ball whose dyadic radius comparison is recorded in $D$ and whose center window is read in $S$.
\item $E$ is the directedness row: every displayed pair of balls in $F$ has a common displayed refinement in $F$, with both comparison witnesses recorded in $D$ over the same metric row $M$.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, formal-ball family, dyadic ledger, stream window, roundedness row, directedness row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from finite ideal membership requests through $M,F,D,S,R,E,T,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RoundedIdealCompletionUp$, $\FormalBallUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\CompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RoundedIdealCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rows $M,F,D,S,R,E,T,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RoundedIdealCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:rounded-ideal-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RoundedIdealCompletionUp$ carrier
$$
I=(M,F,D,S,R,E,T,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RoundedIdealCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the metric source, formal-ball family, dyadic ledger, stream window, roundedness row, directedness row, transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item roundedness, requiring each displayed ideal member to admit a strictly smaller displayed formal ball in the same family, with the comparison recorded in $D$ and the center read through $S$;
\item directedness, requiring every displayed pair of ideal members to admit a common displayed refinement in the same family, with both comparison witnesses recorded in $D$ over $M$;
\item cofinal refinement stability, requiring any finite radius tightening accepted by $D$ and replayed through $C$ to remain inside the same ideal-family row $F$;
\item classifier transport, requiring $\hsame$ comparison of accepted packets to be componentwise through $T$ and to preserve the displayed roundedness and directedness rows;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring every completion-facing read to pass through $M,F,D,S,R,E,T,C,P,N$ and to export no quotient completion, selected limit, countable-choice selector, open-cover compactness proof, or ambient completeness datum.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:rounded-ideal-completion-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the metric source, formal-ball family, dyadic ledger, stream window, roundedness row, directedness row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row.

Roundedness is read from $R$. For each displayed ideal member, $R$ names a smaller displayed formal ball, while $D$ supplies the strict radius comparison and $S$ supplies the finite center window.

Directedness is read from $E$. Given two displayed ideal members, $E$ supplies a common formal-ball refinement in $F$, and $D$ records the two comparison witnesses over the same metric row $M$.

Cofinal refinement stability and classifier transport are structural. The replay row $C$ keeps finite tightening requests inside the same displayed route, and the transport row $T$ compares packets componentwise without adding a second classifier.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ only source and name the displayed packet. Since quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, and ambient completeness have no carrier coordinate, no replay edge, and no provenance row, the local NameCert surface is exactly the listed obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RoundedIdealCompletion cofinal replay]
\label{thm:rounded-ideal-completion-cofinal-replay}
Let $I=(M,F,D,S,R,E,T,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RoundedIdealCompletionUp$ carrier. Every finite completion-facing request for a tighter formal-ball neighbourhood is answered by the route
$$
M,F \longmapsto D,S \longmapsto R,E \longmapsto T,C,P,N .
$$
The answer is a displayed member of the same rounded directed family $F$, not a selected limit, not a quotient representative, and not an ambient-completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the metric source and formal-ball family. Projection gives $M$ and $F$, so the request can inspect only the displayed centers, radii, and formal-ball rows of the accepted packet.

The requested tightening is checked against $D$ and $S$. The dyadic ledger records the radius comparison budget, and the stream window records the finite center observations used by that comparison.

Roundedness and directedness then supply the internal answer. The row $R$ handles a one-ball tightening request, while $E$ handles the common-refinement demand generated by a pair of displayed members.

The structural rows preserve the route. The transport row $T$, replay row $C$, provenance row $P$, and naming row $N$ add no field for quotient classes, selected limits, or completeness principles, so the answer remains a displayed member of the same rounded directed family.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RoundedIdealCompletion classifier transport]
\label{thm:rounded-ideal-completion-classifier-transport}
If two accepted $\RoundedIdealCompletionUp$ carriers are related by componentwise $\hsame$ transport through their transport rows, then their classifier-visible rounded ideal data agree on the metric source, formal-ball membership rows, dyadic comparisons, stream windows, roundedness witnesses, directedness witnesses, replay route, provenance, and local name.

Consequently classifier transport preserves cofinal refinement stability and ledger non-escape for the rounded ideal completion surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two accepted carriers. The only classifier-visible coordinates are the metric source, formal-ball family, dyadic ledger, stream window, roundedness row, directedness row, replay row, provenance row, and local name.

Apply componentwise $\hsame$ transport through the transport rows. The metric source remains the same source surface, formal-ball membership is compared row by row, dyadic comparison witnesses are preserved, and stream windows are transported with their finite observations.

The roundedness and directedness rows are transported with the family they certify. Hence a smaller displayed ball, or a common displayed refinement, remains a witness for the transported packet.

Replay, provenance, and naming preserve the same route. Since no excluded coordinate is available to either classifier, cofinal refinement stability and ledger non-escape are invariant under the displayed transport.
\end{proof}
