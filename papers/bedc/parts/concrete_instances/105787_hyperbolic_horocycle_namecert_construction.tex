\begin{definition}[Hyperbolic horocycle carrier]
\label{def:hyperbolic-horocycle-carrier}
A $\HyperbolicHorocycleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(D,M,B,G,T,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\UnitDiskUp$ disk-coordinate row for the finite source observations.
\item $M$ is the $\HyperbolicMetricUp$ metric-read row over the same disk data.
\item $B$ is the boundary-anchor row, recording the displayed ideal boundary point only as a finite boundary-facing observation.
\item $G$ is the $\GeodesicMetricUp$ incidence row used to compare tangent geodesic directions near the boundary anchor.
\item $T$ is the $\PoincareBoundaryFixedTransportUp$ transport row preserving the boundary anchor during admissible disk transport.
\item $L$ is the horocycle ledger row: boundary tangency, constant hyperbolic-distance readback relative to the anchored geodesic family, and finite incidence compatibility are recorded here.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for disk, metric, boundary, geodesic, transport, ledger, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through disk admission, metric readback, boundary anchoring, geodesic incidence, boundary-fixed transport, and horocycle ledger inspection.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HyperbolicHorocycleUp$, $\UnitDiskUp$, $\HyperbolicMetricUp$, $\PoincareBoundaryFixedTransportUp$, $\GeodesicMetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HyperbolicHorocycleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports horocycle reads only through $D,M,B,G,T,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host Euclidean circle, quotient ideal boundary, global horocycle foliation, selected geodesic representative, physical observer motion, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hyperbolic horocycle NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hyperbolic-horocycle-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\HyperbolicHorocycleUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(D,M,B,G,T,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\HyperbolicHorocycleUp}$ surface consists exactly of disk-coordinate admission, hyperbolic-metric readback, boundary-anchor admission, geodesic-incidence readback, boundary-fixed transport compatibility, horocycle ledger inspection, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, and local naming.

Every boundary-tangent horocycle read exported by the packet must factor through
$$
\UnitDiskUp,\ \HyperbolicMetricUp,\ \PoincareBoundaryFixedTransportUp,\ \GeodesicMetricUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The disk surface is row $D$, the hyperbolic metric read is row $M$, the boundary anchor is row $B$, the geodesic incidence read is row $G$, and the boundary-fixed transport route is row $T$.

The horocycle-specific content is row $L$. It records boundary tangency, constant hyperbolic-distance readback relative to the anchored geodesic family, and finite incidence compatibility only after the disk, metric, boundary, geodesic, and transport rows have been displayed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name that route. Since the carrier contains no host Euclidean circle, quotient ideal boundary, global horocycle foliation, selected geodesic representative, physical observer motion, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
