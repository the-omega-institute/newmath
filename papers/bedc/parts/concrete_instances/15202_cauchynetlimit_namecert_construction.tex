\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyNetLimitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynetlimit-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\CauchyNetLimitUp$ packet names the directed-net limit boundary reached after a $\CauchyNetUp$ directed-window row has been consumed and before a $\RealUp$ seal can be read. It sits downstream of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, reads the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readbacks of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and exposes the seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} only through the completion-facing boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}. The chapter records a finite directed-net limit packet, not a quotient of nets, not a selected host limit, and not an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[CauchyNetLimit carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-net-limit-carrier}
A $\CauchyNetLimitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(K,W,R,D,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the accepted $\CauchyNetUp$ directed-window row. It names the inspected directed windows and the common upper-window witnesses consumed by the limit request.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row read from the directed windows exposed by $K$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row turning the scheduled window observations into regular rational data.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare the regular rational observations at the requested directed-limit scale.
\item $A$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $K,W,R,D$ have been replayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the directed-window, window, readback, tolerance, seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a directed-net limit request through finite windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance, and then the seal row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyNetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyNetLimitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient net equality, a selected host limit, cofinal-choice selection, host real equality, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyNetLimit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-net-limit-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyNetLimitUp$ carrier $L=(K,W,R,D,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyNetLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed CauchyNet directed-window row, StreamName windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming row;
\item directed-window consumption, requiring the $K$ row to supply the displayed directed windows and common upper-window witnesses before any limit-facing read can inspect $W,R,D,A$;
\item regular readback, requiring each limit-scale observation to pass through $W$ and $R$ before the tolerance ledger $D$ is consulted;
\item Real seal handoff, requiring the seal row $A$ to remain downstream of $K,W,R,D$;
\item ledger refusal, excluding quotient nets, selected limits, cofinal choice, host equality, and Real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-net-limit-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the accepted CauchyNet row, StreamName window row, regular readback row, dyadic tolerance ledger, Real seal, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Directed-window consumption is forced by the row order. The continuation row $C$ routes a limit request from $K$ into the finite windows $W$ before the request can reach the regular readback $R$, the tolerance ledger $D$, or the seal row $A$.

The regular readback and seal conditions are internal to the same packet. Transport by $H$ preserves the route, while $P$ and $N$ package and name only that displayed route, so no separate quotient, selected-limit, cofinal-choice, host-equality, or completeness coordinate remains available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyNetLimit Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-net-limit-real-seal-handoff}
Let $L=(K,W,R,D,A,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyNetLimitUp$ packet. Every $\RealUp$-facing read factors through the finite route
$$
K,W,R,D \longmapsto A \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the limit seal is read only after the $\CauchyNetUp$ directed-window row, the $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger have been displayed. The route contains no quotient net, selected host limit, cofinal-choice selector, host real equality, or ambient Real completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a Real-facing consumer of the packet. Carrier admission gives access to the seal row $A$, but only as a row downstream of $K,W,R,D$.

Replay the continuation row $C$. The request first consumes the accepted CauchyNet directed-window row $K$, then the finite StreamName windows $W$, the regular rational readback $R$, and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$.

Only after this replay can the seal row $A$ be read. The transport, provenance, and local naming rows record the same dependency order, so the packet exports no direct route through quotient nets, selected host limits, cofinal choice, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
