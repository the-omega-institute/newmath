\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyTailGluingUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\CauchyTailGluingUp$ packet records the finite tail-gluing surface by which two regular rational names can be read as one Real-facing route after their late tails have been compared. It sits over the finite window discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the nonquotient comparison boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-equivalence-setoid-namecert}, the dyadic late-tail containment route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-tail-ball-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the finite gluing route consumed by L10 Real and Completion consumers; it is not quotient stream equality, a selected common representative, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy tail gluing carrier]\label{def:cauchy-tail-gluing-carrier}
A $\CauchyTailGluingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(X,Y,S,T,n,D,B,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational name rows whose late tails are to be glued.
\item $S$ and $T$ are the corresponding $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows through which $X$ and $Y$ are observed.
\item $n$ is the displayed tail index. A consumer may inspect only windows whose source positions lie at or beyond $n$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the displayed tail index.
\item $B$ is the $\DyadicTailBallUp$ evidence row stating that the two late-tail windows enter the same dyadic tail ball at the tolerance recorded in $D$.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ classifier row, read only after the paired windows, tail index, dyadic ledger, and tail-ball evidence have been displayed.
\item $R$ is the glued $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the accepted late-tail comparison.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached from $X,Y,S,T,n,D,B,Q,R$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two regular names, paired stream windows, tail index, dyadic ledger, tail-ball evidence, setoid classifier, glued readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a gluing request through the paired stream windows, regular rational rows, dyadic tolerance ledger, tail-ball evidence, setoid classifier, glued readback, and Real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyTailGluingUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$, $\DyadicTailBallUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyTailGluingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts only packets in which both source names are observed through displayed StreamName windows, the same tail index bounds the compared suffixes, the dyadic tail-ball row supplies the finite tolerance evidence, the Cauchy-equivalence classifier consumes that evidence, and the Real seal is reached only through the glued RegSeqRat readback.
\end{definition}

The displayed tail index is part of the certificate surface:
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathsf{tail}(G) &= n,\\
\mathsf{leftWindow}(G) &= S_{\geq n},\\
\mathsf{rightWindow}(G) &= T_{\geq n}.
\end{aligned}
$$
The dyadic tail-ball evidence row $B$ is read only at the tolerance recorded in $D$, and the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ is unavailable until the route through $B,Q,R$ has been replayed.

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy tail gluing NameCert obligations]\label{thm:cauchy-tail-gluing-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyTailGluingUp$ carrier $G=(X,Y,S,T,n,D,B,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyTailGluingUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two regular rational names, paired stream windows, displayed tail index, dyadic ledger, tail-ball evidence, setoid classifier, glued readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item classifier discipline, requiring $Q$ to read the $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ comparison only after $S,T,n,D,B$ have been displayed;
\item tail transport, requiring componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ to preserve the same tail index and the same paired suffix windows before the glued readback $R$ is consumed;
\item Real seal compatibility, requiring $E$ to remain downstream of the glued $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ and the classifier route $Q$;
\item ledger nonescape, excluding quotient stream equality, selected common representatives, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness as certificate data.
\end{enumerate}
No gluing obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-tail-gluing-carrier}. The projection exposes the two regular rational names $X,Y$, their stream windows $S,T$, the displayed tail index $n$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the tail-ball evidence $B$, the setoid classifier $Q$, the glued readback $R$, the Real seal $E$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The classifier obligation is downstream of the finite tail data. A comparison request first reads the paired windows at the displayed suffix, then consumes the dyadic tolerance ledger and tail-ball evidence before the $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ row $Q$ is available.

Transport preserves the same suffix route. The $\hsame$ row $H$ acts componentwise on $X,Y,S,T,n,D,B,Q,R,E,C,P,N$, so a transported packet cannot replace the displayed tail index by a hidden tail selector or move the comparison outside the recorded windows.

Finally, the Real seal is terminal. The row $E$ is reached through the glued RegSeqRat readback $R$ after the classifier route has been replayed, and the carrier contains no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a selected representative, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy tail gluing Real seal route]\label{thm:cauchy-tail-gluing-real-seal-route}

$$

Thus the Real-facing row is compatible with the StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readbacks, Cauchy-equivalence classifier, and DyadicTailBall evidence already displayed by the packet, and it exports no quotient stream equality, selected common representative, countable-choice selector, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The replay row $C$ therefore sends the consumer through the same finite path. The classifier row $Q$ receives exactly the dyadic tail-ball evidence $B$ attached to $D$, and $R$ repacks the accepted comparison as the only glued $\RegSeqRatUp$ row.

The remaining structural rows transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for the excluded quotient, choice, host-equality, trichotomy, or completeness data, the terminal Real seal cannot export them.
\end{proof}
