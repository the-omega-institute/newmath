\begin{theorem}[Finite coefficient admission]
\label{thm:finite-dimensional-hahn-banach-separation-coefficient-admission}

A consumer may inspect only the finitely displayed coefficient list on the finite $\VecSpaceUp$ window; it may not replace that list by a host dual-space element or by a choice-selected functional.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The norm row $N$ bounds only those displayed coefficient and vector reads. Transport and replay through $H,T,P,M$ preserve the same finite list, so no additional coordinate can introduce a host dual element.

Thus coefficient admission is finite-row admission. The accepted packet exports the displayed list and its NameCert routes, not an unrestricted dual object or a choice-selected functional.
