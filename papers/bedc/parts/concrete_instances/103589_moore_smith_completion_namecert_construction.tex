\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MooreSmithCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-moore-smith-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley, Moore--Smith, and Bourbaki, the $\MooreSmithCompletionUp$ packet records the directed-net completion route as finite BEDC NameCert data. It sits after the directed-index substrate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-directedset-namecert}, the Cauchy-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, and the Moore--Smith convergence boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-mooresmithconvergence-namecert}; it is read by the metric completion interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the uniform completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, and the separated comparison row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}. Its numerical readback is only the finite regular-rational and real-name route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the completion-facing net interface without selecting limits, quotienting nets, assuming ambient completeness, invoking countable choice, or asserting completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Moore--Smith completion carrier]
\label{def:moore-smith-completion-carrier}
A $\MooreSmithCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(D,C,G,Q,S,R,A,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DirectedSetUp$ index substrate, including the finite comparison windows and common upper-window witnesses used by the net route.
\item $C$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ tail row over $D$, recording the displayed Cauchy windows before any completion-facing read occurs.
\item $G$ is the $\MooreSmithConvergenceUp$ convergence row, tying the same directed windows to a visible boundary request.
\item $Q$ is the completion boundary row read by $\MetricCompletionUp$ and $\UniformCompletionUp$ consumers.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ zero-distance comparison row available when two displayed boundary reads must be compared.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite tolerance readback row attached to the retained net windows.
\item $A$ is the $\RealUp$ terminal seal reached only after $D,C,G,Q,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for directed indices, Cauchy-net tails, convergence rows, completion-boundary reads, separated comparison rows, readback rows, replay data, provenance, and local naming.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from directed windows through Cauchy-net evidence, Moore--Smith convergence, completion-boundary reads, separated comparison, regular rational readback, and real-name sealing.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MooreSmithCompletionUp$, $\DirectedSetUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\MooreSmithConvergenceUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MooreSmithCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a selected limit, a quotient net, a host completion object, ambient completeness, countable choice, or a completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Moore--Smith completion classifier]
\label{def:moore-smith-completion-classifier}
Two accepted $\MooreSmithCompletionUp$ packets are classified as the same displayed completion read exactly when their directed substrates, Cauchy-net tails, Moore--Smith convergence rows, completion-boundary rows, separated comparison rows, regular rational readbacks, and terminal real seals are related componentwise by the displayed $\hsame$ transports and replayed by the same $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ route. The classifier compares boundary reads through the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ zero-distance row when such a comparison is requested; it does not compare host completion points, quotient classes of nets, or selected representatives.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:moore-smith-completion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\MooreSmithCompletionUp$ carrier $M=(D,C,G,Q,S,R,A,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MooreSmithCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the directed substrate, Cauchy-net tail row, Moore--Smith convergence row, completion boundary row, separated comparison row, finite readback row, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item directed-net coverage, requiring every completion-facing request to begin with the displayed $\DirectedSetUp$ and $\CauchyNetUp$ rows;
\item convergence boundary exposure, requiring $G$ and $Q$ to be read before any metric or uniform completion consumer receives the packet;
\item separated comparison discipline, requiring all comparison of two displayed boundary reads to pass through $S$ rather than host equality or quotient equality;
\item readback discipline, requiring every numerical tolerance request to pass through $R$ before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $A$ is inspected;
\item structural transport, requiring $H,K,P,N$ to preserve and name only the displayed route;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding selected limits, quotient nets, ambient completeness, countable choice, and $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:moore-smith-completion-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the directed-index, Cauchy-net, convergence, boundary, separatedness, readback, real-seal, and structural rows.

Directed-net coverage follows from the first two rows. A completion-facing request cannot reach $G,Q,S,R,A$ until the directed substrate $D$ and Cauchy-net evidence $C$ have been displayed.

The convergence and completion boundary obligations are the roles of $G$ and $Q$. These rows place the Moore--Smith convergence read before the metric or uniform completion consumer receives any boundary data.

Separated comparison and readback discipline are local to $S$ and $R$. The classifier of \autoref{def:moore-smith-completion-classifier} uses $S$ for zero-distance comparison, and every numerical read uses $R$ before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $A$.

Finally $H,K,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only the listed coordinates. Since the excluded completion principles have no carrier coordinate, they cannot appear as local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith completion net boundary]
\label{thm:moore-smith-completion-net-boundary}
Every $\MetricCompletionUp$ or $\UniformCompletionUp$ consumer of an accepted $\MooreSmithCompletionUp$ packet reads the boundary through the finite route
$$
D \longmapsto C \longmapsto G \longmapsto Q .
$$
Thus the completion boundary is exposed only after the displayed $\DirectedSetUp$ index substrate, the $\CauchyNetUp$ tail row, and the $\MooreSmithConvergenceUp$ convergence row have been read. The packet provides no selected Moore--Smith limit, quotient of nets, ambient complete object, countable-choice schedule, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a completion-facing consumer and project the carrier rows. The directed substrate $D$ names the finite index windows and upper-window witnesses, while $C$ records the Cauchy-net tail evidence over those windows.

The convergence row $G$ is the next boundary-facing row. It ties the same directed windows to the Moore--Smith convergence test before any completion boundary is exported.

The row $Q$ is therefore downstream of $D,C,G$. Any $\MetricCompletionUp$ or $\UniformCompletionUp$ consumer reads the boundary from $Q$ with the same transport, replay, provenance, and local name recorded by $H,K,P,N$.

No carrier coordinate stores a selected limit, a quotient net, an ambient complete object, a countable-choice schedule, or a $\RealUp$ completeness theorem. The exported boundary is exactly the displayed directed-net route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith completion nonescape]
\label{thm:moore-smith-completion-nonescape}
For an accepted $\MooreSmithCompletionUp$ carrier $M=(D,C,G,Q,S,R,A,H,K,P,N)$, every comparison, tolerance read, and terminal real-name read exported by $M$ is exhausted by
$$
D,C,G,Q,S,R,A,H,K,P,N .
$$
Consequently the packet cannot introduce selected limits, quotient nets, ambient completeness, countable choice, host equality of completion points, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Comparison requests use the classifier of \autoref{def:moore-smith-completion-classifier}. That classifier compares displayed completion reads through componentwise transport and the separated zero-distance row $S$.

Tolerance requests use $R$. Since $R$ is downstream of $D,C,G,Q,S$, each numerical read remains attached to the same directed-net completion route.

Terminal real-name reads use $A$ only after the regular rational readback has been displayed. Thus $A$ is a seal on the same finite route, not an ambient real-completeness principle.

The structural rows $H,K,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name only these coordinates. Since no other completion or choice data is present in the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the packet.
\end{proof}
