\begin{theorem}[FunctionalAnalysis bounded-linear-operator obligation]
\label{thm:functionalanalysis-bounded-linear-operator-obligation}
A $\FunctionalAnalysisUp$ bounded-operator row over a $\BanachUp$ or
$\HilbertUp$ source and target consists of the following displayed data:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item a carried $\LinearMapUp$ map-code carrier for the underlying map;
  \item source and target carrier predicates supplied by the Banach or Hilbert
  certificates named in the chapter opening;
  \item the pointwise $\LinearMapUp$ classifier on carried source inputs;
  \item an operator-norm witness landing in the $\RealUp$ nonnegative
  comparison surface;
  \item a norm-bound ledger recording the input norm row, output norm row,
  bound endpoint, and classifier transport used by the comparison.
\end{enumerate}
The stability field of this row is inherited from the $\LinearMapUp$
pointwise classifier and from the Banach or Hilbert carrier and norm
classifiers, with no extra equality relation on maps.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FunctionalAnalysisUp.FunctionalAnalysisBoundedOperatorRow\_visible\_ledger}
\begin{proof}
Read the Banach and Hilbert dependencies from the chapter opening. They supply
the carried vector endpoints, norm rows, distance rows, and classifier
transport used by a bounded-operator comparison.

Next read the map part through $\LinearMapUp$. The singleton and pointwise
classifier surface of $\LinearMapUp$, including
\autoref{def:linearmap-carrier} and
\autoref{def:linearmap-classifier-specification}, supplies the carried
map-code endpoint and identifies map sameness with pointwise codomain
classifier sameness on carried source inputs.

The operator-norm witness is then only a ledgered RealUp bound: for each
carried input, the source norm row, output norm row, and bound endpoint are
recorded together with the RealUp comparison. Transporting the source input
along the $\LinearMapUp$ classifier and the Banach or Hilbert norm classifier
transports the same bound row, so the bounded-operator classifier has exactly
the inherited stability claimed above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FunctionalAnalysis dual-space classifier obligation]
\label{thm:functionalanalysis-dual-space-classifier-obligation}
For a $\BanachUp$ source, the $\FunctionalAnalysisUp$ dual-space carrier is
the row of bounded scalar-valued $\LinearMapUp$ map codes from the Banach
carrier to the scalar carrier. Its classifier compares two dual rows
pointwise: for every carried Banach input, their scalar outputs are related by
the scalar $\NameCert$ classifier. Reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity of
the dual classifier are inherited from the scalar $\NameCert$ classifier and
from the $\LinearMapUp$ pointwise classifier package.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The source side is the Banach certificate named in the chapter opening. The
target side is the scalar $\NameCert$ used by the Banach norm and bound rows.
Together they determine the scalar-valued $\LinearMapUp$ carrier: a functional
is a carried linear-map code whose output carrier is the scalar carrier and
whose bound ledger is a RealUp comparison row.

For classification, unfold the $\LinearMapUp$ pointwise classifier surface.
Two carried scalar-valued map codes are compared by applying both to an
arbitrary carried Banach input and comparing the scalar outputs. Scalar
reflexivity gives the diagonal comparison, scalar symmetry reverses each
pointwise comparison, and scalar transitivity composes two pointwise
comparisons.

Because these three operations are performed pointwise and then repacked by
\autoref{def:linearmap-classifier-specification}, the dual-space classifier
inherits refl/symm/trans from the scalar $\NameCert$ and the $\LinearMapUp$
classifier package alone.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FunctionalAnalysis compact-operator ledger obligation]
\label{thm:functionalanalysis-compact-operator-ledger-obligation}
A $\FunctionalAnalysisUp$ compact-operator witness for a carried bounded
operator is an explicit finite-net image ledger: for each displayed
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of bounded source inputs and each displayed target tolerance
row, the ledger gives finitely many carried target probes whose classifier
balls cover the carried image probes. The witness is stable under the
bounded-operator classifier because pointwise target classifier transport
moves every image probe and its finite-net comparison across classifier-equal
bounded operators. This is not an open-cover compactness assertion and uses
no choice-based extraction of subcovers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the bounded-operator row of
\autoref{thm:functionalanalysis-bounded-linear-operator-obligation}. It gives
the carried $\LinearMapUp$ map code, pointwise target classifier control, and
the norm-bound ledger needed to restrict attention to bounded source probes.

The compactness datum is recorded as a finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$-style ledger.
For a displayed source probe bundle and a displayed target tolerance row, the
ledger names a finite list of carried target probes and, for each image probe,
the selected finite-net point together with the target classifier comparison.

If two bounded operators are related by the bounded-operator classifier, then
their image endpoints over every carried source probe are pointwise related in
the target classifier. Transport each finite-net comparison across that
pointwise relation and keep the same finite list of target probes. Thus the
compact-operator witness is classifier-stable while remaining a finite ledger,
not an open-cover or choice principle.
\end{proof}
