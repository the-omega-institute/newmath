\begin{definition}[Uniform polygonal approximation carrier]
\label{def:uniform-polygonal-approximation-carrier}
A $\UniformPolygonalApproximationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(I,M,L,Q,F,R,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ source row, displaying the endpoint real names and the bounded interval window on which the approximation is read.
\item $M$ is a $\DyadicPartitionUp$ or $\UniformPartitionUp$ mesh row, including the ordered boundary ledger, endpoint-coverage route, and positive mesh-width evidence.
\item $L$ is the $\PiecewiseLinearApproximationUp$ polygonal row whose affine cells, endpoint values, and local slope ledger are read over the mesh $M$.
\item $Q$ is the $\LocatedUniformContinuityUp$ modulus row, exposing a located tolerance-to-mesh request for the same interval source and displayed map.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ row whose graph and pointwise continuity data are consumed only through $I,M,Q,L$.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ endpoint and value-readback row reached after the mesh, modulus, and polygonal rows have been displayed.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, recording the dyadic request, mesh bound, cell-width comparison, and uniform error budget used by downstream consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval source, mesh, polygonal row, located modulus, continuous-map handoff, real readbacks, dyadic ledger, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
I,M,Q,L,D \longmapsto F,R \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformPolygonalApproximationUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicPartitionUp$, $\UniformPartitionUp$, $\PiecewiseLinearApproximationUp$, $\LocatedUniformContinuityUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\RealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformPolygonalApproximationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,M,L,Q,F,R,D,H,C,P,N$ and their displayed transports. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected limiting polygonal sequence, host function graph, quotient equality of maps, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform polygonal approximation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-polygonal-approximation-namecert-obligations}

the local $\NameCert_{\UniformPolygonalApproximationUp}$ surface consists exactly of interval-source admission through $I$, mesh coverage through $M$, polygonal-cell admission through $L$, located modulus exposure through $Q$, continuous-map handoff through $F$, real readback through $R$, dyadic tolerance accounting through $D$, componentwise transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$. \begin{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform polygonal approximation mesh handoff]
\label{thm:uniform-polygonal-approximation-mesh-handoff}

\begin{proof}

\label{thm:uniform-polygonal-approximation-nonescape}

Consequently the packet exports no ambient real completeness, selected uniform limit, quotient function equality, open-cover compactness, countable-choice schedule, or host function-space equality.

\begin{proof}

\label{thm:uniform-polygonal-approximation-mesh-refinement}

\begin{proof}
Project the mesh row $M$ and the located modulus row $Q$. The refined mesh $M'$ is accepted only as a refinement of the displayed cell ledger and only at a tolerance admitted by the same located modulus request.
