\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyHadamardUp$}

Following Cauchy and Hadamard's power-series radius formula, the $\CauchyHadamardUp$ packet records the constructive radius route for a real power series as finite BEDC certificate data. It sits over the coefficient windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realpowerseries-namecert}, the radius seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-convergence-radius-namecert}, the regular root-bound route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-root-test-namecert}, and the sibling ratio-test comparison route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-ratio-test-namecert}. The chapter packages the traditional formula as a NameCert route through displayed coefficient windows, dyadic tolerance rows, and regular-Cauchy readbacks rather than through a host limsup, quotient stream, choice-selected radius, or ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy-Hadamard carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-hadamard-carrier}
A $\CauchyHadamardUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(A,W,R,G,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the $\RealPowerSeriesUp$ source row, exposing only the displayed coefficient family and its finite inspection windows;
\item $W$ is the coefficient-window ledger, recording the inspected unary indices, absolute-coefficient reads, and root-bound requests used by the packet;
\item $R$ is the $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$ root-bound route, read from the same coefficient windows and tolerance requests;
\item $G$ is the $\ConvergenceRadiusUp$ seal row reached from the root-bound route and the power-series source;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, storing the dyadic slack and comparison budgets used by the root-bound and radius rows;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the source row, coefficient windows, root-bound route, radius seal, tolerance ledger, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay by which consumers read $A,W,R,G,D,H,P,N$ in that order;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealPowerSeriesUp$, $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$, $\RegularCauchyRatioTestUp$, $\ConvergenceRadiusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyHadamardUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows and their recorded transport, continuation, package, and local NameCert provenance.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy-Hadamard NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-hadamard-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyHadamardUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:cauchy-hadamard-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission for $K=(A,W,R,G,D,H,C,P,N)$, coefficient-window exposure through $A,W$, root-bound exposure through $R$, radius-seal exposure through $G$, dyadic tolerance exactness through $D$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$. Every accepted radius read is classified only by the displayed power-series row, finite coefficient windows, root-bound route, tolerance ledger, radius seal, and structural rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier tuple $K$. The source row, coefficient-window ledger, root-bound route, radius seal, dyadic tolerance ledger, transport rows, continuation routes, package row, and local name are all stored coordinates.

Carrier admission and coefficient exposure are therefore finite projections from $A$ and $W$. The root-bound and radius obligations project $R$ and $G$, with $D$ recording the tolerance comparisons used by both rows.

The structural obligations project $H,C,P,N$. Since no coordinate names a host limsup, quotient stream, choice-selected radius, or ambient completeness theorem, the local certificate exports only the displayed Cauchy-Hadamard route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy-Hadamard root-bound handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-hadamard-root-bound-handoff}
For every accepted $\CauchyHadamardUp$ carrier, each root-bound request consumed by the radius row factors through the finite route
$$
A\longmapsto W\longmapsto D\longmapsto R\longmapsto G\longmapsto H\longmapsto C\longmapsto P\longmapsto N.
$$
The handoff uses the $\RealPowerSeriesUp$ coefficient source, the $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$ root-bound row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, and the $\ConvergenceRadiusUp$ seal. The $\RegularCauchyRatioTestUp$ sibling is available only as a comparison provenance row in $P$ and does not replace the displayed root-bound route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $A,W,D,R,G$ from the carrier. A radius request first opens the power-series coefficient source and the finite coefficient-window ledger, so the root-bound row sees only displayed coefficient data.

Use the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$ to align the slack and comparison budget consumed by $R$. The radius seal $G$ is then read after the root-bound route, not from a host supremum or selected limsup witness.

The continuation and provenance rows preserve the same order for downstream consumers. The ratio-test sibling remains a recorded dependency anchor in $P$, while the accepted handoff itself is the root-bound route through $A,W,D,R,G,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}
