\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-criticalstripzetazerowitness-namecert}

The $\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp$ interface names the finite witness packet consumed by the RH capstone. It packages a complex-history candidate together with the displayed critical-strip row, the zeta-zero row, the critical-line classifier boundary, and the local $\NameCert$ routes needed to read the witness as a BEDC object. The packet is a witness surface for the constructive form of \autoref{prop:rh-constructive-form}; it is not a proof of RH and it does not manufacture analytic zeros outside the rows already exposed by $\CritStripUp$ and $\ZetaZerosUp$.

\begin{definition}[CriticalStripZetaZeroWitness carrier]
\label{def:critical-strip-zeta-zero-witness-carrier}
A $\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
W=(S,Z,L,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\CritStripUp$ row carrying the complex history $s$ and the strict-strip evidence for its real component;
\item $Z$ is the $\ZetaZerosUp$ row carrying the zeta-value and zero-pattern evidence for the same source history $s$;
\item $L$ is the $\CritStripUp$ critical-line classifier row naming the boundary predicate $\mathsf{OnCritLine}(s)$ or its displayed absence;
\item $B$ is the boundary ledger recording the two open-strip boundary exclusions, the $\Re(s)=1$ non-vanishing slot, and the critical-line comparison row;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the strip row, zero row, critical-line row, and boundary ledger;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes for reading the strip witness, reading the zeta-zero witness, exposing the critical-line classifier, and handing the packet to the RH predicate;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ComplexUp$, $\CritStripUp$, $\ZetaZerosUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier stores no host complex number, no analytic zero finder, no quotient equality of zero sets, no selected representative, and no proof or refutation of RH.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalStripZetaZeroWitness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:critical-strip-zeta-zero-witness-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is the displayed packet $W$ with strip, zero, critical-line, boundary, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows;
\item shared source binding: the $\CritStripUp$ row $S$ and the $\ZetaZerosUp$ row $Z$ expose the same complex-history source through the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes;
\item line-boundary routing: the critical-line row $L$ and boundary ledger $B$ are read only after the strip and zero rows have been exposed;
\item RH handoff: any citation of the RH predicate reads the carrier through $S,Z,L,B,H,C,P,N$;
\item ledger exhaustion: the package and local certificate rows exhaust the dependency surface over $\ComplexUp$, $\CritStripUp$, $\ZetaZerosUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp.CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessPacket\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:critical-strip-zeta-zero-witness-carrier}. The strip row, zero row, critical-line classifier, boundary ledger, transport rows, continuation rows, package row, and local naming row are explicit coordinates of the packet.

The shared source binding is componentwise. The strip row names the complex history through $\CritStripUp$, the zero row names the same source through $\ZetaZerosUp$, and the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes are the only routes by which a consumer compares them.

The remaining obligations are downstream reads. The critical-line row and boundary ledger are exposed after the strip and zero rows, and the RH handoff consumes the same finite packet. Since no coordinate is a host complex number, zero finder, quotient equality, selected representative, or proof of RH, the ledger is exhausted by the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalStripZetaZeroWitness critical-line boundary]
\label{thm:critical-strip-zeta-zero-witness-critical-line-boundary}
Every accepted $\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp$ packet routes the critical-line question through the displayed $\CritStripUp$ classifier row $L$ and the boundary ledger $B$. A downstream RH consumer may ask whether the source history carried by $S$ and $Z$ is on the critical line, but it receives only the displayed $\mathsf{OnCritLine}(s)$ row or its boundary evidence through $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the line-boundary routing obligation of \autoref{thm:critical-strip-zeta-zero-witness-namecert-obligations}. It requires the source strip row and zero row to be exposed before the critical-line classifier is read.

The boundary ledger $B$ contains the available boundary exclusions and comparison rows. Therefore a consumer cannot bypass $L$ and $B$ to obtain an ambient real equality, a selected representative for $\Re(s)$, or a host-level decision procedure for the critical line.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalStripZetaZeroWitness ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:critical-strip-zeta-zero-witness-ledger-exhaustion}
For an accepted $\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp$ carrier, every fact exported to a $\CritStripUp$, $\ZetaZerosUp$, $\RealUp$, or RH-facing consumer is one of the rows $S,Z,L,B,H,C,P,N$ or a route between such rows. In particular, the witness packet exports no analytic zero search, no completed zero-set quotient, no host complex equality, no selected counterexample, and no proof of the RH predicate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
List the coordinates of \autoref{def:critical-strip-zeta-zero-witness-carrier}. The only mathematical data rows are the strip row $S$, the zeta-zero row $Z$, the critical-line row $L$, and the boundary ledger $B$.

Transport can only move these rows through $H$, and continuation can only expose them through $C$. The package and local certificate rows $P$ and $N$ record provenance and naming, not additional analytic data.

Thus the consumer surface is exhausted by the finite packet. The excluded objects have no coordinate in the carrier and cannot be reconstructed from the exported rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalStripZetaZeroWitness concretizes RH]
\label{thm:critical-strip-zeta-zero-witness-concretizes-rh}
The $\CriticalStripZetaZeroWitnessUp$ packet is the concrete NameCert surface for the constructive RH premise of \autoref{prop:rh-constructive-form}. Any BEDC proof of the RH predicate may consume such a packet as the finite input carrying $\mathsf{ZetaZero}(s)$ and $\mathsf{InCritStrip}(s)$, and any constructive counterexample must export such a packet together with a displayed failure of the critical-line row. The packet itself contributes only the input surface; it does not prove the critical-line conclusion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier supplies the two premise rows required by the constructive RH form: $S$ gives the critical-strip row and $Z$ gives the zeta-zero row. The shared source binding in \autoref{thm:critical-strip-zeta-zero-witness-namecert-obligations} identifies them as rows over the same complex history.

The critical-line boundary theorem identifies the only route to the conclusion-side classifier, and ledger exhaustion rules out any hidden analytic or quotient data. Hence the packet is exactly the finite input surface for the RH premise. A proof may consume it to produce the critical-line row, while a counterexample must pair it with a displayed failure of that row.
\end{proof}
