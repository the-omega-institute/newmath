\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EffrosBorelSpaceUp$}

Following Effros and standard descriptive-set-theoretic hyperspace practice, the $\EffrosBorelSpaceUp$ packet records the finite hit--miss Borel surface of a hyperspace without importing a host sigma-algebra, a completed measure object, or choice-selected closed sets. It sits beside the compact-subset carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hyperspace-namecert}, the Vietoris routing of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vietoris-topology-namecert}, the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Effros Borel hit--miss carrier]
\label{def:effros-borel-hit-miss-carrier}
An $\EffrosBorelSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(H,A,M,V,K,D,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the underlying $\HyperspaceUp$ carrier row, exposing only finite compact-subset names and Hausdorff-facing inspection data.
\item $A$ is the hit-row ledger, recording the finite open-window tests whose positive intersection is visible to the packet.
\item $M$ is the miss-row ledger, recording the finite closed-window tests whose avoidance is visible to the packet.
\item $V$ is the $\VietorisTopologyUp$ routing row that places the hit and miss ledgers in the same finite topological basis.
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row and $D$ is the $\MetricUp$ distance row used before any hyperspace read is admitted.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for hyperspace, hit, miss, Vietoris, compact-metric, metric, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a hit--miss request through $H,A,M,V,K,D,T$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EffrosBorelSpaceUp$, $\HyperspaceUp$, $\VietorisTopologyUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EffrosBorelSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a host powerset of closed sets, an ambient Borel sigma-algebra, a completed measure space, a selected representative, quotient closed-set equality, or countable-choice enumeration of tests.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Effros Borel NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:effros-borel-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EffrosBorelSpaceUp$ carrier $E=(H,A,M,V,K,D,T,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\EffrosBorelSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, hit-row stability, miss-row stability, hyperspace nonescape, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. The hit and miss rows are admitted only through the common $\VietorisTopologyUp$ route over the displayed $\HyperspaceUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, and $\MetricUp$ source rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:effros-borel-hit-miss-carrier}. The only observable hyperspace data are the finite compact-subset row $H$, the hit ledger $A$, the miss ledger $M$, and the common Vietoris route $V$.

The compact and metric rows $K,D$ fix the source on which the hit and miss tests are read. Since both ledgers pass through $V$, a consumer cannot separate hit stability from miss stability by appealing to an external topology.

Transport and replay are then forced through $T$ and $C$, with provenance and local naming supplied by $P,N$. No remaining coordinate can introduce a host sigma-algebra, a completed measure object, a quotient closed-set representative, or a choice-selected enumeration.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Effros Borel hyperspace nonescape]
\label{thm:effros-borel-hyperspace-nonescape}
Every Borel-facing consumer of an accepted $\EffrosBorelSpaceUp$ packet must read the finite route
$$
\HyperspaceUp,\VietorisTopologyUp,\CompactMetricUp,\MetricUp,\BHist,\hsame,\Cont,\Pkg,\NameCert
$$
before it receives a hit--miss name. The read remains inside the displayed hyperspace packet and exports no host Borel sigma-algebra, no completed measure structure, no selected closed subset, no quotient equality of closed sets, and no countable-choice schedule for tests.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a Borel-facing read. Carrier admission exposes the hyperspace row $H$, but no hit--miss information is public until the hit row $A$ and miss row $M$ have both been routed through the same Vietoris row $V$.

The source rows $K,D$ keep the route tied to the compact metric and metric carriers already displayed in the packet. Thus a consumer can inspect finite hit and miss windows, but it cannot leave the packet for a host powerset or a detached measurable-space object.

Finally $T,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. The carrier omissions in \autoref{def:effros-borel-hit-miss-carrier} rule out the remaining exits, so every accepted read is a hyperspace nonescape read.
\end{proof}
