\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FanTheoremUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-fantheorem-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\FanTheoremUp$ interface names the finite-spread packet used when later compactness and uniform-continuity consumers read a constructive finite fan argument. In Bishop constructive analysis and in the Troelstra--van Dalen treatment of finite spreads and bars, the finite fan theorem is a boundedness principle for decidable bars on finitely branching binary spreads. BEDC records only the visible finite packet: binary branch rows, branch-prefix $\ListUp$ and tree rows, a decidable finite bar classifier, unary depth bounds, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ traversal routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and a local $\NameCert$ row.

The packet is not Brouwer bar induction, not open-cover compactness, not a choice principle, and not a $\RealUp$ completeness theorem. It is a concrete finite naming surface that downstream certificates can consume without importing an ambient compact space.

The finite fan packet is a sibling input for \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactuniformcontinuity-namecert}. $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$ consumes $\FanTheoremUp$ only as a finite branch-depth and prefix-window certificate, never as open-cover compactness or $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[FanTheorem binary spread carrier]
\label{def:fan-theorem-binary-spread-carrier}
A $\FanTheoremUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
F=(T,B,D,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the finite binary spread row, displayed as a prefix-closed tree of $\BoolUp$ branch histories and branch-prefix $\ListUp$ rows;
\item $B$ is the decidable finite bar row, assigning to each displayed prefix a visible $\BoolUp$ classifier saying whether that prefix is barred;
\item $D$ is the unary depth-bound ledger, a finite $\NatUp$ row recording the candidate depth through which every branch-prefix read is inspected;
\item $W$ is the finite witness window, listing the branch-prefix rows of length at most the displayed depth and the bar decisions actually read from $B$;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the tree row, prefix rows, bar decisions, depth ledger, witness window, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ traversal routes for root reads, left and right branch extension, prefix restriction, depth comparison, and bar-decision readback;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance naming the consumed $\BoolUp$, $\NatUp$, $\ListUp$, $\TreeUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FanTheoremUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an infinite binary sequence, a host compact space, an open cover, a choice-selected branch, Brouwer bar induction, or real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[FanTheorem bar classifier]
\label{def:fan-theorem-bar-classifier}
Two accepted $\FanTheoremUp$ carriers are classified as the same finite fan packet exactly when their displayed rows compare componentwise. Their binary spread rows agree by transported $\TreeUp$ and $\ListUp$ prefix classifiers, their bar decisions agree by the displayed $\BoolUp$ rows, their depth ledgers agree by the unary $\NatUp$ rows, their finite witness windows agree prefix by prefix, and their $\Pkg$ provenance and local $\NameCert$ rows name the same packet.

The classifier has no generator for comparing infinite branches, no generator for an open-cover index, no generator for a choice function, and no generator for a $\RealUp$ completion object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FanTheorem NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:fan-theorem-namecert-obligations}
The finite packet of \autoref{def:fan-theorem-binary-spread-carrier} satisfies the local $\NameCert_{\FanTheoremUp}$ obligation surface for a decidable finite bar on a binary spread. Carrier habitation is witnessed by the empty prefix tree with its displayed depth row, classifier stability is the componentwise classifier of \autoref{def:fan-theorem-bar-classifier}, and ledger exactness says that every accepted read factors through the displayed prefix tree, bar decision row, unary depth ledger, finite witness window, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ traversal routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FanTheoremUp.FanTheoremPacket\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
For habitation, take the root prefix as the finite binary spread and use the displayed unary depth row for the witness window. The bar classifier row is decidable because it is a finite $\BoolUp$ ledger over the retained prefixes.

For stability, compare two accepted packets by the rows named in \autoref{def:fan-theorem-bar-classifier}. Tree transport moves prefix rows only through the displayed $\TreeUp$ and $\ListUp$ classifiers, bar decisions move only through $\BoolUp$, and depth rows move only through $\NatUp$.

For exactness, inspect any consumer read of the packet. The read must project one of the coordinates $T$, $B$, $D$, $W$, $H$, $C$, $P$, or $N$, and each coordinate is part of the finite carrier. Repacking these projections gives the stated local $\NameCert_{\FanTheoremUp}$ surface without adding infinite branches, open covers, choice functions, bar induction, or real-completeness data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FanTheorem finite bar handoff]
\label{thm:fan-theorem-finite-bar-handoff}
Every downstream compactness or uniform-continuity consumer that reads an accepted $\FanTheoremUp$ packet receives only the finite bar handoff: a prefix-closed binary spread row, a decidable finite bar classifier, a unary depth bound, the finite branch-prefix window through that depth, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ traversal routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row.

The handoff supplies a bounded finite inspection surface for binary fan arguments. It does not supply Brouwer bar induction, open-cover compactness, a choice-selected branch, a host Cantor-space object, or a $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FanTheoremUp.FanTheoremPacket\_finite\_bar\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:fan-theorem-binary-spread-carrier}. The only branch data available to the consumer are the finite tree row $T$ and the finite prefix window $W$.

Read the bar information through $B$ and the depth information through $D$. Decidability is the stored $\BoolUp$ row at each retained prefix, while boundedness is the stored unary $\NatUp$ row; neither read mentions an infinite branch or an open cover.

The traversal routes are exactly the continuations in $C$, transported by the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$. Repacking those rows with $P$ and $N$ gives the finite bar handoff, and coordinate exhaustion excludes Brouwer bar induction, choice, host Cantor space, and $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FanTheorem real completion boundary]
\label{thm:fan-theorem-real-completion-boundary}
When a $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, or $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$ consumer cites a $\FanTheoremUp$ packet, the citation can use only the finite fan rows of \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-finite-bar-handoff}. In particular, the citation may bound a finite branch-prefix inspection, but it cannot assert real completeness, quotient equality of real names, selection of a real limit, open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, or Brouwer bar induction.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FanTheoremUp.FanTheoremPacket\_real\_completion\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Start with the consumer citation and factor it through the local $\NameCert_{\FanTheoremUp}$ surface of \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-namecert-obligations}. The cited packet exposes only the finite carrier rows and their displayed transport routes.

Apply the finite bar handoff of \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-finite-bar-handoff}. It identifies the available data as a binary spread row, decidable bar row, unary depth row, finite prefix window, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming row.

None of these coordinates is a $\RealUp$ completion object, quotient classifier for real names, selected real limit, open-cover compactness witness, sequential compactness principle, or Brouwer bar-induction rule. The real-facing consumer therefore receives only a finite inspection bound and cannot read any stronger compactness or completeness principle from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FanTheorem bar uniform modulus handoff]
\label{thm:fan-theorem-bar-uniform-modulus}
A $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$ or finite compactness consumer citing an accepted $\FanTheoremUp$ packet can extract only a unary finite branch depth, the displayed finite witness window, and the pointwise modulus rows routed through the recorded $\Cont$ and $\Pkg$ provenance. The resulting uniform bound is a finite BEDC packet over the branch-prefix rows and their modulus ledgers, not an open-cover compactness theorem, a selected branch, choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FanTheoremUp.FanTheoremPacket\_bar\_uniform\_modulus}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted fan packet through \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-finite-bar-handoff}. The consumer receives the prefix-closed binary spread row, decidable bar row, unary depth ledger, finite witness window, componentwise transports, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row.

The pointwise modulus rows consumed by a compact-uniform-continuity argument are therefore attached only to the retained branch-prefix window. The unary depth row bounds the branch inspection, and the displayed $\Cont$ routes record how each pointwise row is reached from the finite spread data.

Apply \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-real-completion-boundary} to the same citation. It prevents the consumer from replacing the finite inspection bound by $\RealUp$ completeness, an open-cover compactness principle, sequential compactness, Brouwer bar induction, or a choice-selected branch.

Repacking the depth, witness window, pointwise modulus rows, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row gives the uniform-modulus handoff. Since all coordinates are finite carrier rows, the handoff remains a BEDC packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FanTheorem public uniform bar export]
\label{thm:fantheorem-public-uniform-bar-export}
The carrier, classifier, NameCert obligations, finite-bar handoff, real-completion boundary, and uniform-modulus handoff assemble a single public $\FanTheoremUp$ export packet consumable by $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$ and constructive compact-metric fan arguments. The public row exposes only the binary-spread carrier, bar classifier, finite subcover witness window, real-completion boundary, and uniform-modulus ledger; it does not add open-cover compactness or choice-selected bars.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FanTheoremUp.FanTheoremPacket\_public\_uniform\_bar\_export}
\begin{proof}
First use \autoref{def:fan-theorem-binary-spread-carrier} and \autoref{def:fan-theorem-bar-classifier}. They identify the binary-spread carrier and the componentwise bar classifier that every exported packet must expose.

Next apply \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-namecert-obligations} and \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-finite-bar-handoff}. These results bind the local naming obligations and supply the finite subcover witness window available to downstream compactness consumers.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-real-completion-boundary} and \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-bar-uniform-modulus}. The public packet therefore exports the finite bar rows and uniform-modulus ledger while retaining the stated boundary against open-cover compactness, choice-selected bars, Brouwer bar induction, and $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FanTheorem compact uniform continuity obligation surface]
\label{thm:fan-theorem-compact-uniform-continuity-obligation-surface}
The public uniform bar export of \autoref{thm:fantheorem-public-uniform-bar-export} supplies exactly the rows a $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$ consumer may read over $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, and $\UniformModulusUp$: finite binary-spread rows, decidable-bar rows, unary-depth rows, finite witness-window rows, the $\UniformModulusUp$ ledger, the $\CompactMetricUp$ finite coverage surface, the $\ContinuousMapUp$ pointwise-modulus surface, $\BHist$ histories, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\FanTheoremUp}$ row.

It supplies no open-cover compactness row, no choice-selected bar, no sequential compactness row, and no $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FanTheoremUp.FanTheoremPacket\_compact\_uniform\_continuity\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite bar handoff of \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-finite-bar-handoff}. It contributes the finite binary-spread rows, decidable-bar classifier, unary-depth bound, finite witness window, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming row.

Use the bar uniform modulus handoff of \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-bar-uniform-modulus}. That result is the route by which a compact-uniform-continuity consumer may read the finite witness window as a $\UniformModulusUp$-facing ledger over the relevant pointwise rows.

Now combine the public uniform bar export of \autoref{thm:fantheorem-public-uniform-bar-export} with the compact-facing rows named there. The export is therefore readable by $\CompactMetricUp$ and $\ContinuousMapUp$ consumers only through finite coverage, pointwise-modulus, and uniform-modulus coordinates.

Finally apply the real completion boundary of \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-real-completion-boundary}. It removes any coordinate for open-cover compactness, choice-selected bars, sequential compactness, or $\RealUp$ completeness, so the listed rows are exactly the public obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FanTheorem standard finite-fan bridge]
\label{thm:fan-theorem-standard-finite-fan-bridge}
The public uniform bar export of \autoref{thm:fantheorem-public-uniform-bar-export} interprets as the standard finite binary fan packet over finite spread rows, decidable bar rows, a unary depth bound, a finite witness window, the $\UniformModulusUp$ ledger, the $\CompactMetricUp$ finite coverage surface, the $\ContinuousMapUp$ pointwise-modulus surface, $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\FanTheoremUp}$ row.

This interpretation refuses Brouwer bar induction, open-cover compactness, choice-selected bars, sequential compactness, and $\RealUp$ completeness. Those principles are not bridge data for the finite fan packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FanTheoremUp.FanTheoremPacket\_standard\_finite\_fan\_bridge}
\begin{proof}
First apply the finite bar handoff of \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-finite-bar-handoff}. It supplies the finite binary spread row, decidable finite bar classifier, unary depth ledger, finite witness window, componentwise transports, traversal routes, provenance, and local naming row.

Next apply the real completion boundary of \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-real-completion-boundary}. This step removes any reading of the packet as $\RealUp$ completeness, open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, choice-selected bar selection, or Brouwer bar induction.

Use the bar uniform modulus handoff of \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-bar-uniform-modulus}. The finite witness window can be read as a $\UniformModulusUp$-facing ledger over pointwise modulus rows, with no additional compactness principle beyond the displayed finite rows.

Finally combine the public uniform bar export of \autoref{thm:fantheorem-public-uniform-bar-export} with the compact uniform continuity obligation surface of \autoref{thm:fan-theorem-compact-uniform-continuity-obligation-surface}. The resulting bridge packet has exactly the finite spread, decidable bar, depth, witness-window, uniform-modulus, compact-coverage, pointwise-modulus, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates stated above.
