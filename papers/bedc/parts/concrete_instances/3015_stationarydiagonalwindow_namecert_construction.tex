The $\StationaryDiagonalWindowUp$ packet realizes the stationary diagonal finite-window classifier correspondence as a concrete $\NameCert$ surface. It keeps the rational seed rows, the constant stream-name windows, the regular-rational readback rows, the dyadic finite-window ledger, the real-facing diagonal representatives, the three classifier faces, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming in one finite packet. \begin{definition}[Stationary diagonal window carrier]
\label{def:stationary-diagonal-window-carrier}
A $\StationaryDiagonalWindowUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(Q,S,G,D,R,K,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the paired stationary $\RatUp$ seed row, including the two rational history carriers and their $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row;
\item $S$ is the paired constant $\StreamNameUp$ window row induced by $Q$;
\item $G$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite readback row attached to the same selected window;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ finite-window ledger, including dyadic endpoint reads, tolerance rows, and window-bound comparisons;
\item $R$ is the paired $\RealUp$ diagonal representative row read only after $S,G,D$ have been displayed;
\item $K$ records the three classifier faces: the stationary $\RatUp$ classifier on $Q$, the regular-rational finite-window classifier on $G,D$, and the $\RealUp$ diagonal finite-window classifier on $R$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $Q,S,G,D,R,K$ and the structural rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $Q$ to $S$, from $S$ to $G$, from $G$ to $D$, from $D$ to $R$, and from the three faces of $K$ to the common correspondence row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\StationaryDiagonalWindowUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is finite. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, selected real limits, non-stationary tails, an infinite window family, or a global completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary diagonal window NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:stationary-diagonal-window-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\StationaryDiagonalWindowUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:stationary-diagonal-window-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission for $W=(Q,S,G,D,R,K,H,C,P,N)$, paired stationary $\RatUp$ seed exposure through $Q$, constant $\StreamNameUp$ window exposure through $S$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback exposure through $G$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ finite-window ledger exposure through $D$, paired $\RealUp$ diagonal representative exposure through $R$, the three classifier faces recorded in $K$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ route replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed tuple $W$. The rows $Q,S,G,D,R$ expose the rational seeds, constant stream windows, regular-rational readbacks, dyadic finite-window ledger, and real-facing diagonal representatives.

The row $K$ supplies the three classifier faces required by the correspondence: stationary rational, regular-rational finite-window, and real diagonal finite-window. The continuation row $C$ routes these faces through the same selected window, so no second classifier source is available.

The rows $H,P,N$ account for transport, package provenance, and local naming. Since the excluded objects have no carrier coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local $\NameCert$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary diagonal window classifier correspondence]
\label{thm:stationary-diagonal-window-classifier-correspondence}
For an accepted $\StationaryDiagonalWindowUp$ carrier, the following three finite classifier reads have the same public finite-window content: the stationary $\RatUp$ classifier displayed by $Q$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback classifier displayed by $G,D$, and the $\RealUp$ diagonal finite-window classifier displayed by $R$. The correspondence is witnessed inside the finite row $K$ and replayed only through the continuations in $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the three classifier faces from $K$. The first face is anchored in the stationary rational seed row $Q$, the second in the regular-rational readback and dyadic ledger rows $G,D$, and the third in the real diagonal representative row $R$.

The continuations in $C$ force all three faces to use the constant stream window $S$ selected from the same rational seed pair. Hence the regular-rational and real-facing reads cannot change the finite window inspected by the rational classifier.

The transport row $H$ carries the same window and classifier faces componentwise. Therefore an accepted packet presents one finite-window classifier correspondence rather than three unrelated classifications.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary diagonal window finite-window exhaustion]
\label{thm:stationary-diagonal-window-finite-window-exhaustion}
Every observation exported by an accepted $\StationaryDiagonalWindowUp$ packet factors through the finite rows
$$

$$
In particular, the consumer may inspect only the displayed stationary rational seed, constant stream window, regular-rational readback, dyadic finite-window ledger, real diagonal representative, classifier correspondence row, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and its local naming surface. The mathematical rows are $Q,S,G,D,R,K$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

Apply the classifier-correspondence theorem to the only classifier row $K$. It routes every rational, regular-rational, and real-facing observation through the common finite window induced by $S$.

No carrier coordinate remains after those rows are read. Thus a downstream observation cannot range over an infinite window family, quotient stream equality, host equality, selected limits, non-stationary tails, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary diagonal window public boundary]
\label{thm:stationary-diagonal-window-public-boundary}
The public boundary of an accepted $\StationaryDiagonalWindowUp$ carrier is the finite tuple consisting of $Q,S,G,D,R,K$ together with the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. This boundary is sufficient for Real-completion consumers that require the stationary diagonal finite-window classifier correspondence, and it withholds quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected completed limits, non-stationary tails, and any unbounded completion principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The NameCert obligations identify the rows available to public consumers. The classifier-correspondence theorem shows that the public classifier content is already present in $K$ and routed through $Q,S,G,D,R$.

The finite-window exhaustion theorem then rules out any hidden observation path outside the displayed packet. Hence the public boundary is exactly the finite tuple named in the statement, with the excluded analytic objects absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}
