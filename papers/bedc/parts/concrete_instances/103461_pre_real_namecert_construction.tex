\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PreRealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-pre-real-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges' regular Cauchy construction of the reals, the
$\PreRealUp$ packet records the unsealed regular-Cauchy name boundary between
regular rational sequences and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal. It sits over the
dyadic tolerance ledger of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation
surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular
rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and
the real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The
packet exposes the finite name data available before a Real seal is consumed;
it is not quotient stream equality, a selected limit, host real equality,
countable choice, or completeness.

\begin{definition}[PreReal carrier]
\label{def:pre-real-carrier}
A $\PreRealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(D,S,R,H,C,K,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row recording finite precision
requests, denominator windows, and dyadic radius bookkeeping.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation row opened by the same tolerance
request, recording the finite schedule of inspected name entries.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row, read only through $D$ and $S$,
and carrying the displayed Cauchy regularity evidence available before a real
seal is consumed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for tolerance, observation,
regularity, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
D,S \longmapsto R \longmapsto H,K,N .
$$
\item $K$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PreRealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and
$\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PreRealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected representatives,
host real equality, countable choice, a selected limit, or completeness of
$\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PreReal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:pre-real-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\PreRealUp$ carrier
$$
P=(D,S,R,H,C,K,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\PreRealUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following
finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic tolerance, StreamName observation,
RegSeqRat regularity, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local
naming rows;
\item tolerance-window exposure, requiring every PreReal read to start from the
displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row $D$ and the matching $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$;
\item regularity handoff, requiring the opened finite name window to pass
through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ before any Real-facing boundary is named;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the
same displayed rows componentwise;
\item provenance non-escape, requiring $K$ and $N$ to export no quotient
streams, selected representatives, host real equality, countable choice,
selected limit, or completeness principle.
\end{enumerate}
No PreReal obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:pre-real-carrier}. The
packet lists the tolerance row, finite observation row, regularity row,
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row, so admission has no
additional source.

The tolerance-window exposure obligation reads $D$ and $S$ first. These two
rows bind the same finite precision request, so a PreReal consumer cannot skip
directly to a selected limit or quotient stream.

The regularity handoff is row $R$. It consumes the opened finite window through
the regular rational sequence surface, and therefore presents only regular
Cauchy name data to any later Real-facing boundary.

Finally $H,C,K,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly the displayed
route. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for the excluded host objects
or choice principles, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PreReal constant dyadic route]
\label{thm:pre-real-constant-dyadic-route}
Every constant dyadic name admitted by a $\PreRealUp$ packet factors through
the finite route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp
\longrightarrow \PreRealUp .
$$
For a carrier $P=(D,S,R,H,C,K,N)$, the route reads the constant dyadic
tolerance data from $D$, opens the matching finite StreamName observation row
$S$, records regularity in $R$, and then consumes only $H,C,K,N$ as structural
support for the local $\NameCert_{\PreRealUp}$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $P=(D,S,R,H,C,K,N)$. The constant dyadic request
first reads $D$, because this is the only tolerance row exposed by the carrier.

The matching StreamName observation row is $S$. It records the finite schedule
at which the constant dyadic data are inspected, and it supplies no selected
limit or quotient coordinate.

The regularity evidence is then read from $R$. A constant dyadic name satisfies
the regular rational route by the displayed tolerance and observation rows, so
the PreReal packet receives a regular name rather than host real data.

The remaining rows $H,C,K,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same route.
Thus the constant dyadic route reaches $\PreRealUp$ without exporting quotient
streams, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, or Real
completeness.
\end{proof}
