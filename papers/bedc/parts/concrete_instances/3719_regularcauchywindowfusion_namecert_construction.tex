\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyWindowFusionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchywindowfusion-namecert}

The $\RegularCauchyWindowFusionUp$ surface packages the finite observation-window route used by transported stationary diagonal seals as one naming certificate. It receives a RatUp seed row, the constant $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal readback, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger, and the $\RealUp$ finite-window seal as one displayed packet, so downstream real-completion consumers read a shared finite route rather than a selected limit, quotient stream, or ambient completeness assertion.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy window fusion carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-window-fusion-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyWindowFusionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(R,W,S,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the RatUp seed classifier row for the stationary rational source;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ constant-window classifier read over the displayed observation window;
\item $S$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal-consumption row obtained from the same finite window;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger carried by that window;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ finite-window classifier and seal row reached from $W,S,D$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $R,W,S,D,E$ and for the package and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers first read the seed row, then the shared window, then the dyadic and regular-sequence rows, and finally the real seal row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over RatUp, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyWindowFusionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a selected limit, quotient stream equality, real trichotomy, host completeness, or an unrecorded RealUp comparison row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy window fusion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-window-fusion-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyWindowFusionUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-window-fusion-carrier} consists of carrier admission for
$$
F=(R,W,S,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
seed-to-window exposure, $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation-ledger exposure, $\RealUp$ seal handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ locality, $\Pkg$ provenance, and non-escape from selected limits, quotient streams, trichotomy, ambient completeness, and hidden RealUp comparison data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-window-fusion-carrier}. The rows $R,W,S,D,E,H,C,P,N$ are the only rows admitted to the packet.

The observation obligations are read in order. The RatUp seed row $R$ feeds the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$; the same window supplies the $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal row $S$ and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$.

The Real-facing obligation is the row $E$. It records the finite-window $\RealUp$ seal reached from $W,S,D$, while $H$ and $C$ preserve and route that same displayed row without adding an analytic carrier.

Finally $P$ and $N$ bind the packet to its package provenance and local naming row. Since no carrier coordinate names selected limits, quotient streams, trichotomy, ambient completeness, or a hidden RealUp comparison, none can be exported by the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy window fusion shared-window handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-window-fusion-shared-window-handoff}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyWindowFusionUp$ carrier, the RatUp seed row, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window classifier, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger are all reads of the same displayed window. Any downstream consumer that reaches the $\RealUp$ seal must first consume the shared route
$$
R,W,S,D,H,C,P,N.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows. The only seed row is $R$, the only stream-window row is $W$, the only regular-sequence seal row is $S$, and the only dyadic observation ledger is $D$.

Use the transported stationary diagonal finite-window seal exhaustion as the source route. It identifies the RatUp seed classifier, the constant $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal consumption row, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger as four readings of one certified finite window.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ then preserve, route, package, and name that shared window. Therefore a downstream consumer cannot reach the real seal without first reading the displayed shared-window packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy window fusion Real seal non-escape]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-window-fusion-real-seal-nonescape}
For an accepted $\RegularCauchyWindowFusionUp$ carrier
$$
F=(R,W,S,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the $\RealUp$ finite-window seal row $E$ is reachable only through the seed row $R$, the shared $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $S$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, and the support rows $H,C,P,N$. The seal exports no selected limit, quotient stream equality, trichotomy decision, ambient completeness proof, independent real carrier, or unrecorded comparison row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the shared-window handoff. It forces every route to the seal to pass through $R,W,S,D$ before the Real-facing row is read.

Now read the real seal coordinate. The carrier contains a single $\RealUp$ finite-window row $E$, and the displayed $\Cont$ route exposes it only after the seed, stream-window, regular-sequence, and dyadic rows have been consumed.

Inspect the remaining coordinates. The support rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, route, package, and name the same finite packet, while no coordinate names selected limits, quotient streams, trichotomy, ambient completeness, an independent real carrier, or a hidden comparison. The Real seal therefore cannot escape the finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy window fusion downstream package]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-window-fusion-downstream-package}
The carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-window-fusion-carrier}, the local NameCert obligations, the shared-window handoff, and the Real seal non-escape theorem assemble a downstream package for $\RealCompletenessUp$, $\StationaryDiagonalWindowRouteUp$, $\CauchyModulusComparisonUp$, and $\DiagonalCofinalTailUp$ consumers. The package exposes exactly
$$

$$
over RatUp, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the carrier and NameCert surface. They provide the RatUp seed, the shared stream window, the regular-sequence seal readback, the dyadic ledger, the real seal, and the support rows.

Next use the shared-window handoff. It binds the seed, stream, regular-sequence, and dyadic rows to one finite observation window, so downstream consumers receive a single route rather than separate analytic witnesses.

Finally apply the Real seal non-escape theorem. The package can export only $R,W,S,D,E,H,C,P,N$, and the listed downstream consumers read those rows through their existing finite-window routes.
\end{proof}
