\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FisherInfoUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-fisherinfo-namecert}

The Fisher-information interface $\FisherInfoUp$ packages, for a parametric family of distributions, the Riemannian metric whose components are expectations of squared score functions. It depends on $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-distribution-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannianmetric-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:fisherinfo-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Fisher information score-expectation carrier]
\label{def:fisherinfo-score-expectation-carrier}
A carried $\FisherInfoUp$ packet is a displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$$
\mathcal{I}=(\mathcal{D},\theta,s,q,G,\pi,\chi,\lambda)
$$
where $\mathcal{D}$ is a $\DistributionUp$ source certificate, $\theta:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the parameter row, $s:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the score row read over the displayed distribution family at $\theta$, $q:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the squared-score expectation row, and $G:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the target fibre row consumed by $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$. The provenance row $\pi:\mathsf{Pkg}$ binds the distribution source, parameter, score, expectation, and target metric fibre into one packet. The classifier witness $\chi$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons between matching source, parameter, score, expectation, and target-fibre rows. The finite ledger $\lambda:\mathsf{Cont}$ records how the score row and squared-score expectation row are read from the $\DistributionUp$ pushforward source and then presented as the $\RiemannianMetricUp$ fibre endpoint.

The public carrier is this finite history packet. Choices of score representative, expectation approximation, coordinate display, and host differentiability data remain ledger rows inside $\lambda$ and are not exported as public carrier equalities.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:fisherinfo-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp distribution source obligation]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-distribution-source-obligation}
Every carried $\FisherInfoUp$ packet of \autoref{def:fisherinfo-score-expectation-carrier} projects a $\DistributionUp$ source row whose pushforward endpoint is the only probability-distribution data available to the score and expectation ledger. In particular, the packet consumes the pushforward carrier of \autoref{def:distribution-pushforward-carrier}, the classifier of \autoref{def:distribution-pushforward-classifier}, and the pushforward row of \autoref{thm:distribution-pushforward-row}; it does not import an ambient probability space outside the displayed $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier tuple $\mathcal{I}$. Its first field is the displayed source certificate $\mathcal{D}$, and the provenance row $\pi$ binds every later score and expectation row to that same source.

Project $\mathcal{D}$ through the $\DistributionUp$ pushforward carrier and classifier. The target measurable-event rows and RealUp endpoint rows are therefore read from \autoref{def:distribution-pushforward-carrier} and \autoref{def:distribution-pushforward-classifier}.

The score and expectation ledger $\lambda$ has no independent probability carrier. It may use the pushforward row of \autoref{thm:distribution-pushforward-row} as source data, but any probability-space, measurable-preimage, or measure-row information is accessed only through the displayed $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp score row carrier obligation]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-score-row-carrier-obligation}
For a carried $\FisherInfoUp$ packet, the parameter row $\theta$, score row $s$, and squared-score expectation row $q$ form a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ score-expectation spine over the same $\DistributionUp$ source. The score row is accepted only as a history over the displayed parameter and distribution rows, while the expectation row is accepted only as the $\mathsf{Cont}$ readback of the squared score through the source distribution ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the parameter, score, expectation, and ledger fields from \autoref{def:fisherinfo-score-expectation-carrier}. The parameter row $\theta$ supplies the displayed family index, and the score row $s$ is carried over that index rather than over an external coordinate object.

Next read the finite continuation ledger $\lambda$. It records the passage from the displayed distribution endpoint and parameter row to the score row, and then from the score row to the squared-score expectation row $q$.

Thus the carrier admits the score and expectation rows only as a connected $\mathsf{BHist}$ spine. Different score representatives or numerical expectation displays may appear as ledger witnesses, but the public row is the carried endpoint $q$ together with its source and provenance.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp RiemannianMetric comparison obligation]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-riemannianmetric-comparison-obligation}
The target fibre row $G$ of a carried $\FisherInfoUp$ packet is consumable by the public $\RiemannianMetricUp$ source-fibre surface. Under this consumption, the squared-score expectation row $q$ supplies the displayed metric endpoint on the parameter fibre, while the fibre carrier, classifier stability, and positivity or chart-compatibility obligations are read from \autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-public-source-fibre-namecert-surface}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the target fibre row $G$ from the Fisher-information packet. By construction it is the row presented to $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$, so its base point is the parameter row and its fibre endpoint is the squared-score expectation row.

Apply the public $\RiemannianMetricUp$ source-fibre surface of \autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-public-source-fibre-namecert-surface}. That surface provides the manifold point row, tangent-fibre row, fibrewise inner-product row, and classifier transport needed to read $G$ as a metric endpoint.

The comparison is therefore between the Fisher-information expectation endpoint and the displayed Riemannian-metric fibre endpoint. It is not a claim that a host coordinate formula, a differentiability structure, or an external metric tensor has been exported by the $\FisherInfoUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp classifier transport obligation]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-classifier-transport-obligation}
Let $\mathcal{I}$ and $\mathcal{I}'$ be carried $\FisherInfoUp$ packets whose source distribution, parameter, score, squared-score expectation, and target-fibre rows are related by the componentwise $\hsame$ witnesses recorded in their classifier fields. Then the $\FisherInfoUp$ classifier transports the score-expectation spine of $\mathcal{I}$ to the score-expectation spine of $\mathcal{I}'$ and preserves the target $\RiemannianMetricUp$ fibre comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the two carrier tuples and project their componentwise classifier witnesses. The source distribution rows are transported by the $\DistributionUp$ classifier, while the parameter, score, expectation, and target-fibre rows are transported by the listed $\hsame$ comparisons.

Transport the finite continuation ledger component by component. The source-to-score step moves along the transported distribution and parameter rows; the score-to-expectation step moves along the transported score row; the expectation-to-target-fibre step moves along the transported target row.

Finally apply the $\RiemannianMetricUp$ classifier stability supplied by \autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-public-source-fibre-namecert-surface}. The transported expectation endpoint is still the metric endpoint of the transported target fibre row, so the public $\FisherInfoUp$ classifier is preserved.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp ledger exactness boundary]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-ledger-exactness-boundary}
The public $\NameCert_{\FisherInfoUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of the $\DistributionUp$ source row, the parameter row, the score row, the squared-score expectation row, the $\RiemannianMetricUp$ target fibre row, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row, the componentwise $\hsame$ classifier witnesses, and the finite $\Cont$ ledger of \autoref{def:fisherinfo-score-expectation-carrier}. It exports no additional row for score choice, expectation approximation, coordinate chart, host differentiability, ambient statistical model equality, or standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Soundness is by projection from the carried packet. Each accepted public row is one of the named fields of \autoref{def:fisherinfo-score-expectation-carrier}: source, parameter, score, expectation, target fibre, provenance, classifier witness, or ledger.

Completeness is the reverse packaging. Given exactly those rows, the tuple $\mathcal{I}$ is reconstructed as a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet whose score and expectation endpoints are connected by the displayed $\Cont$ ledger and whose metric endpoint is presented to $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$.

The exclusions follow from the same exactness reading. Score representatives, expectation approximations, coordinate choices, and differentiability evidence can justify ledger steps, but no such datum is a separate public carrier equality or a row available to downstream consumers outside the listed packet.
\end{proof}
