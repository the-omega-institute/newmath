\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FisherInfoUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-fisherinfo-namecert}

The Fisher-information interface $\FisherInfoUp$ packages, for a parametric family of distributions, the Riemannian metric whose components are expectations of squared score functions. It depends on $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-distribution-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannianmetric-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:fisherinfo-carrier}

\begin{definition}[FisherInfoUp BHist score carrier]
\label{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier}
A carried $\FisherInfoUp$ score packet is an eight-tuple
$$
(\mathcal{D},\mathcal{G},\theta,s,\eta,g,\rho,\lambda).
$$
Here $\mathcal{D}$ is a displayed $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ packet for the
parametric distribution family, $\mathcal{G}$ is a displayed
$\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$ metric packet, $\theta$ is the parameter
row, $s$ is the score-observable row over the family, $\eta$ is the
expectation row reading the squared score through the distribution endpoint,
$g$ is the metric-component row, $\rho$ is the shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance row, and $\lambda$ is a finite $\Cont$ ledger connecting these
displayed rows. The packet is accepted exactly when each coordinate is a
$\mathsf{BHist}$ row, all comparisons used by the ledger are $\hsame$
comparisons, $\lambda$ is a finite $\Cont$ composite, $\rho$ is the common
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and the only dependency rows used by the carrier
are the displayed $\DistributionUp$ and $\RiemannianMetricUp$ rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp distribution source obligation]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-distribution-source-obligation}
Every accepted packet of \autoref{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier}
projects a $\DistributionUp$ source row whose pushforward endpoint is the only
probability-distribution data available to the score and expectation ledger.
In particular, the packet consumes the pushforward carrier of
\autoref{def:distribution-pushforward-carrier}, the classifier of
\autoref{def:distribution-pushforward-classifier}, and the pushforward row of
\autoref{thm:distribution-pushforward-row}; it does not import an ambient
probability space outside the displayed $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted tuple. Its first field is the displayed source certificate
$\mathcal{D}$, and the provenance row $\rho$ binds every later score,
expectation, and metric-component row to that same source.

Project $\mathcal{D}$ through the $\DistributionUp$ pushforward carrier and
classifier. The target measurable-event rows and RealUp endpoint rows are
therefore read from \autoref{def:distribution-pushforward-carrier} and
\autoref{def:distribution-pushforward-classifier}.

The score and expectation ledger $\lambda$ has no independent probability
carrier. It may use the pushforward row of
\autoref{thm:distribution-pushforward-row} as source data, but any
probability-space, measurable-preimage, or measure-row information is accessed
only through the displayed $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp score row carrier obligation]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-score-row-carrier-obligation}
For an accepted $\FisherInfoUp$ score packet, the parameter row $\theta$, score
row $s$, and squared-score expectation row $\eta$ form a finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ score-expectation spine over the same $\DistributionUp$
source. The score row is accepted only as a history over the displayed
parameter and distribution rows, while the expectation row is accepted only as
the $\mathsf{Cont}$ readback of the squared score through the source
distribution ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the parameter, score, expectation, and ledger fields from
\autoref{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier}. The parameter row $\theta$
supplies the displayed family index, and the score row $s$ is carried over
that index rather than over an external coordinate object.

Next read the finite continuation ledger $\lambda$. It records the passage
from the displayed distribution endpoint and parameter row to the score row,
and then from the score row to the squared-score expectation row $\eta$.

Thus the carrier admits the score and expectation rows only as a connected
$\mathsf{BHist}$ spine. Different score representatives or numerical
expectation displays may appear as ledger witnesses, but the public row is the
carried endpoint $\eta$ together with its source and provenance.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp RiemannianMetric comparison obligation]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-riemannianmetric-comparison-obligation}
The metric-component row $g$ of an accepted $\FisherInfoUp$ score packet is
consumable by the public $\RiemannianMetricUp$ source-fibre surface. Under this
consumption, the squared-score expectation row $\eta$ supplies the displayed
metric endpoint on the parameter fibre, while the fibre carrier, classifier
stability, and positivity or chart-compatibility obligations are read from
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-public-source-fibre-namecert-surface}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the metric-component row $g$ from the Fisher-information packet. By
construction it is the row presented through
$\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$, so its base point is the parameter row and
its fibre endpoint is the squared-score expectation row.

Apply the public $\RiemannianMetricUp$ source-fibre surface of
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-public-source-fibre-namecert-surface}. That
surface provides the manifold point row, tangent-fibre row, fibrewise
inner-product row, and classifier transport needed to read $g$ as a metric
endpoint.

The comparison is therefore between the Fisher-information expectation
endpoint and the displayed Riemannian-metric fibre endpoint. It is not a claim
that a host coordinate formula, a differentiability structure, or an external
metric tensor has been exported by the $\FisherInfoUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp distribution score row coverage]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-distribution-score-row-coverage}
Every accepted packet of
\autoref{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier} projects exactly the
$\DistributionUp$ family row $\mathcal{D}$, the score-observable row $s$, the
expectation ledger $\eta$, and the shared provenance row $\rho$. These
projections are the complete public distribution-side rows of the
$\FisherInfoUp$ carrier: no host density, ambient probability law, or analytic
sample-space object is part of the accepted packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted eight-tuple onto its distribution-side coordinates. The
first projection is the carried $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ packet
$\mathcal{D}$, so the family row is read through the pushforward carrier and
probability-package rows already exposed by $\DistributionUp$.

The score observable is the displayed row $s$ over the same parameter row
$\theta$. It is not reconstructed from a host density; it is one of the
accepted $\mathsf{BHist}$ coordinates.

The expectation row $\eta$ is read only through the carried
$\DistributionUp$ endpoint and the finite $\Cont$ ledger $\lambda$. The
provenance row $\rho$ is shared by all displayed coordinates, so repacking the
four projections gives exactly the distribution family, score, expectation,
and provenance surface stated in the theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp Riemannian metric row transport]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-riemannian-metric-row-transport}
Let $(\mathcal{D},\mathcal{G},\theta,s,\eta,g,\rho,\lambda)$ and
$(\mathcal{D}',\mathcal{G}',\theta',s',\eta',g',\rho',\lambda')$ be accepted
$\FisherInfoUp$ score packets. If $\mathcal{D}$ transports to
$\mathcal{D}'$ by $\hsame$ and the $\DistributionUp$ classifier, and $s$
transports to $s'$ by the displayed score-row classifier over the transported
parameter row, then the metric component $g$ transports to $g'$ through the
$\RiemannianMetricUp$ classifier carried by $\mathcal{G}$ and
$\mathcal{G}'$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FisherInfoUp.FisherInfoScorePacket\_metric\_component\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Use the carried $\DistributionUp$ classifier first. It transports the
distribution endpoint used by the expectation row from $\mathcal{D}$ to
$\mathcal{D}'$, and the score-row hypothesis transports the displayed score
observable from $s$ to $s'$ over the transported parameter row.

The expectation endpoint is therefore read at the transported pair of
distribution and score rows. Because $\eta$ and $\eta'$ are ledger rows in the
accepted packets, the finite $\Cont$ ledgers $\lambda$ and $\lambda'$ record
only this distribution-to-expectation readback.

It remains to compare the displayed metric components. The metric coordinate
is carried by the $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$ packets $\mathcal{G}$ and
$\mathcal{G}'$. Applying the RiemannianMetricUp classifier-stability row
transports the fibrewise metric endpoint, so the transported expectation
component is classified as $g'$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp expectation ledger exactness]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-expectation-ledger-exactness}
In an accepted $\FisherInfoUp$ score packet, the expectation of the squared
score is hidden only by the displayed $\DistributionUp$ pushforward ledger,
the $\RealUp$ classifier endpoint, and the finite $\Cont$ readback
$\lambda$. No integration primitive, host density, or analytic probability
space is a public coordinate of the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates of
\autoref{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier}. The only distribution-side
source is $\mathcal{D}$, and the only observable-side source is the displayed
score row $s$ over $\theta$.

The expectation coordinate $\eta$ is the public endpoint of reading the
squared score through the $\DistributionUp$ pushforward ledger. Its endpoint
comparison is a $\RealUp$ classifier comparison, and its construction is
recorded by the finite $\Cont$ ledger $\lambda$.

Every public endpoint of the carrier is therefore one of the displayed rows
$\mathcal{D}$, $\mathcal{G}$, $\theta$, $s$, $\eta$, $g$, $\rho$, or the
finite ledger $\lambda$. Since no coordinate names an integration primitive,
host density, or external probability object, those objects are not part of
the public carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp Riemannian metric component row]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-riemannian-metric-component-row}
In every accepted packet of
\autoref{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier}, the endpoint of the expected
squared score $\eta$ is exactly the metric-component $\mathsf{BHist}$ row
$g$ consumed by the inherited $\RiemannianMetricUp$ fibre surface
$\mathcal{G}$. Thus the Fisher information component is not an external
coordinate metric; it is the displayed expectation endpoint repacked as the
metric fibre component.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet onto the expectation row $\eta$ and the
metric-component row $g$. By the carrier definition, both are displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ rows in the same finite $\Cont$ ledger and share the
provenance row $\rho$.

The distribution source obligation identifies $\eta$ as the expectation
endpoint of $s^2$ through the carried $\DistributionUp$ probability row. The
RiemannianMetricUp dependency $\mathcal{G}$ consumes a displayed metric fibre
component rather than a host coordinate tensor.

The accepted FisherInfoUp packet equates these two displayed endpoints inside
the finite ledger: the expected squared score endpoint is repacked as $g$.
Consequently the inherited fibre surface reads the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ row
that the expectation source row produces.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp classifier transport obligation]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-classifier-transport-obligation}
Let $\mathcal{I}$ and $\mathcal{I}'$ be accepted $\FisherInfoUp$ score packets
whose source distribution, parameter, score, squared-score expectation, and
metric-component rows are related by the componentwise $\hsame$ witnesses
available to their finite ledgers. Then the $\FisherInfoUp$ classifier
transports the score-expectation spine of $\mathcal{I}$ to the
score-expectation spine of $\mathcal{I}'$ and preserves the target
$\RiemannianMetricUp$ fibre comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the two accepted carrier tuples and project their componentwise
classifier witnesses. The source distribution rows are transported by the
$\DistributionUp$ classifier, while the parameter, score, expectation, and
metric-component rows are transported by the listed $\hsame$ comparisons.

Transport the finite continuation ledger component by component. The
source-to-score step moves along the transported distribution and parameter
rows; the score-to-expectation step moves along the transported score row; the
expectation-to-metric-component step moves along the transported metric row.

Finally apply the $\RiemannianMetricUp$ classifier stability supplied by
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-public-source-fibre-namecert-surface}. The
transported expectation endpoint is still the metric endpoint of the
transported metric-component row, so the public $\FisherInfoUp$ classifier is
preserved.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:fisherinfo-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp classifier transport row]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-classifier-transport-row}
Let $(\mathcal{D},\mathcal{G},\theta,s,\eta,g,\rho,\lambda)$ and
$(\mathcal{D}',\mathcal{G}',\theta',s',\eta',g',\rho',\lambda')$ be accepted
$\FisherInfoUp$ score packets. If $\hsame$ transport carries the distribution
family row $\mathcal{D}$ to $\mathcal{D}'$, the parameter row $\theta$ to
$\theta'$, the score row $s$ to $s'$, and the metric fibre row $\mathcal{G}$
to $\mathcal{G}'$, then it transports the FisherInfoUp metric endpoint
componentwise from $g$ to $g'$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carried $\DistributionUp$ classifier first. It transports the
pushforward and probability row that supplies the expectation source from
$\mathcal{D}$ to $\mathcal{D}'$, while the parameter and score hypotheses
transport the displayed score-function row from $s$ to $s'$ over
$\theta'$.

The expected squared score endpoint therefore transports from $\eta$ to
$\eta'$ along the same finite $\Cont$ readback pattern. No analytic
integration or derivative primitive enters this step because both endpoints
are displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows in accepted packets.

Apply the metric component row theorem on both packets. It identifies $\eta$
with $g$ and $\eta'$ with $g'$ as the metric fibre components consumed by
$\mathcal{G}$ and $\mathcal{G}'$. The $\RiemannianMetricUp$ classifier then
transports the component row, giving the stated componentwise FisherInfoUp
metric endpoint transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp public certificate surface]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-public-certificate-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\FisherInfoUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of
the score packet carrier of
\autoref{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier}, the distribution source row of
\autoref{thm:fisherinfo-distribution-source-obligation}, the metric component
row of \autoref{thm:fisherinfo-riemannian-metric-component-row}, the
classifier transport row of
\autoref{thm:fisherinfo-classifier-transport-row}, the finite $\Cont$ ledger
$\lambda$, the shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$ ledger $\rho$, the exactness boundary
excluding host density, integral, derivative, and coordinate metric
primitives, and an absent bridge row. These data form the public
$\NameCert_{\FisherInfoUp}$ surface over the dependency certificates
$\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier component is exactly the displayed eight-coordinate packet of
\autoref{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier}. Its accepted coordinates expose
the $\DistributionUp$ dependency, the $\RiemannianMetricUp$ dependency, the
parameter history, the score-function history, the squared-score expectation
history, the metric-component history, the shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance,
and the finite $\Cont$ ledger.

The distribution-side public rows are supplied by
\autoref{thm:fisherinfo-distribution-source-obligation}. The metric endpoint
identified from the expected squared score is supplied by
\autoref{thm:fisherinfo-riemannian-metric-component-row}.

The classifier transport row is supplied by
\autoref{thm:fisherinfo-classifier-transport-row}. Repacking these rows with
the finite $\Cont$ ledger, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance ledger, the explicit
exactness boundary, and the absent bridge row gives the stated ten-field
$\NameCert_{\FisherInfoUp}$ obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:fisherinfo-namecert-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\FisherInfoUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of
the $\mathsf{BHist}$ score carrier of
\autoref{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier}, the distribution score row

\begin{proof}
The carrier component is exactly the eight-coordinate packet of
\autoref{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier}. Its accepted coordinates expose
the $\DistributionUp$ dependency, the $\RiemannianMetricUp$ dependency, the
parameter row, the score row, the expectation row, the metric-component row,
the shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and the finite $\Cont$ ledger.

\autoref{thm:fisherinfo-distribution-score-row-coverage}. The classifier

\begin{theorem}[FisherInfoUp ledger exactness boundary]
\label{thm:fisherinfo-ledger-exactness-boundary}
The public $\NameCert_{\FisherInfoUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of
the $\DistributionUp$ source row, the $\RiemannianMetricUp$ metric packet, the
parameter row, the score row, the squared-score expectation row, the
metric-component row, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row, the componentwise
$\hsame$ classifier witnesses used by the finite ledger, and the finite
$\Cont$ ledger of \autoref{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier}. It exports no
additional row for score choice, expectation approximation, coordinate chart,
host differentiability, ambient statistical model equality, or standard
bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Soundness is by projection from the accepted packet. Each accepted public row
is one of the named fields of \autoref{def:fisherinfo-bhist-score-carrier}:
distribution source, metric packet, parameter, score, expectation,
metric-component, provenance, or ledger, together with the $\hsame$
comparisons admitted by that ledger.

Completeness is the reverse packaging. Given exactly those rows, the
eight-coordinate packet is reconstructed as a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
whose score and expectation endpoints are connected by the displayed $\Cont$
ledger and whose metric endpoint is presented to
$\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$.

The exclusions follow from the same exactness reading. Score representatives,
expectation approximations, coordinate choices, and differentiability evidence
can justify ledger steps, but no such datum is a separate public carrier
equality or a row available to downstream consumers outside the listed packet.
\end{proof}
