It sits over the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the stream-name windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the completion functor of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionfunctor-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Cauchy completion strength carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-strength-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionStrengthUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(A,B,W_A,W_B,D_A,D_B,P,Q,R,E,H,C,G,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows whose regular Cauchy data are to be paired.
\item $W_A$ and $W_B$ are the corresponding $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows, displaying the observations used by each source row.
\item $D_A$ and $D_B$ are the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledgers attached to the two window rows.
\item $P$ is the $\ProductUp$ pairing-route row, recording only the displayed product read of the two regular Cauchy sources.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ handoff row, applied after the product route has displayed $A,B,W_A,W_B,D_A,D_B$.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ completion seal reached only after the paired regular readback and the completion-functor handoff are public.
\item $E$ records strength exactness: every accepted product-completion read must use the displayed rows $A,B,W_A,W_B,D_A,D_B,P,Q,R$ in that order.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two sources, two stream-window rows, two dyadic ledgers, product route, completion handoff, seal, exactness, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the same product-completion route.
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionStrengthUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\ProductUp$, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionStrengthUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $A,B,W_A,W_B,D_A,D_B,P,Q,R,E,H,C,G,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, host product equality, detached product representatives, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion strength NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-strength-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionStrengthUp$ carrier
$$
S=(A,B,W_A,W_B,D_A,D_B,P,Q,R,E,H,C,G,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionStrengthUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows, two $\StreamNameUp$ window rows, two $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledgers, $\ProductUp$ pairing route, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ handoff, $\RealUp$ seal, exactness, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item source-window compatibility, requiring $W_A,D_A$ to read only from $A$ and $W_B,D_B$ to read only from $B$;
\item product-route stability, requiring $P$ to pair the two displayed regular Cauchy sources before the completion handoff $Q$ is consumed;
\item completion-seal nonescape, requiring $R$ to be downstream of $A,B,W_A,W_B,D_A,D_B,P,Q$ and excluding quotient completions, selected limits, countable choice, host product equality, and ambient real completeness;
\item structural persistence through $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and the local $\NameCert$ row $N$.
\end{enumerate}
No other NameCert obligation is available from the Cauchy completion strength packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-strength-carrier}. Its analytic rows are exactly the two regular Cauchy sources, their finite stream windows, their dyadic ledgers, the product pairing route, the completion-functor handoff, and the terminal real seal.

The five obligation rows separate the finite data by use. Carrier admission displays every coordinate. Source-window compatibility ties each window and tolerance ledger to its own regular Cauchy source, so the two sources cannot be mixed by an ambient product equality.

Product-route stability then forces the pairing row $P$ to be read before $Q$. The completion-seal row $R$ is downstream of that displayed route, so it cannot stand for quotient completion, a selected limit, or a countable-choice schedule.

Finally $H,C,G,N$ preserve transport, replay, provenance, and local naming for the same finite route. Since the carrier contains no further coordinate, the five rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion strength product route]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-strength-product-route}
Every product-completion read of an accepted $\CauchyCompletionStrengthUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
\RegSeqRatUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \ProductUp,\ \CauchyCompletionFunctorUp,\ \RealUp
$$
by reading $A,B,W_A,W_B,D_A,D_B,P,Q,R,E,H,C,G,N$. Thus completion after pairing is visible only as the finite product route followed by the completion handoff and Real seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a product-completion consumer. The only source rows available to it are $A$ and $B$, and the only observations of those sources are the finite window rows $W_A,W_B$ with dyadic ledgers $D_A,D_B$.

The consumer must next read the product route $P$. This route pairs the displayed regular Cauchy data before the completion-functor handoff $Q$ is available, so a detached product representative or host product equality cannot supply the handoff.

After $Q$ is displayed, the seal row $R$ is the only real-facing completion row. The exactness row $E$ records the required order, and $H,C,G,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route.

Therefore every accepted read factors through the displayed RegSeqRat, StreamName, DyadicRatCore, Product, CauchyCompletionFunctor, and Real rows before the Real seal is consumed. The packet exports no quotient completion, selected limit, countable-choice principle, host product equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}
