\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GroundCompilerEvidenceBoundaryUp$}

The $\GroundCompilerEvidenceBoundaryUp$ packet realizes the GroundCompiler audit-map boundary of \autoref{ch:bedc-groundcompiler-audit-map} as a finite NameCert surface. It sits beside the audit-map interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditmapinterface-namecert} and the audit-map family packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditmapfamily-namecert}: those chapters classify audit-map rows, while the present packet isolates the compiler-facing evidence rows that may be consumed by TasteGate and self-compilation audit sites.

\begin{definition}[GroundCompiler evidence boundary carrier]
\label{def:groundcompiler-evidence-boundary-carrier}
A $\GroundCompilerEvidenceBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(E,C,R,A,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the event-flow evidence row, naming the legal source, channel, encoding, and decoded-event data that may be treated as compiler-layer evidence;
\item $C$ is the code-not-proof separation row, recording that source text, theorem code, chapter code, source substrings, and carried code channels are readable code data rather than proof or certificate evidence;
\item $R$ is the generated-recognizer row, recording the generated package, name-certificate, derivation-certificate, theorem, chapter, and accepted-export recognizers that mediate evidence admission;
\item $A$ is the audit/report non-evidence row, recording metric reports, motif reports, analysis summaries, compiler reports, and cannot-claim ledgers as audit outputs rather than proof inputs;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for event-flow, separation, recognizer, report-boundary, replay, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $T$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes by which consumers inspect the event-flow evidence, generated recognizer evidence, code/report boundary, and non-input ledger;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:bedc-groundcompiler-audit-map}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditmapinterface-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditmapfamily-namecert}, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and compiler-layer NameCert rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GroundCompilerEvidenceBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts exactly the displayed packet rows. It exports no proof term from code text, no certificate from a report, no recognition evidence without the generated-recognizer row, no host equality for event flows, and no compiler-layer input outside $E,R,H,T,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GroundCompiler evidence boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:groundcompiler-evidence-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:groundcompiler-evidence-boundary-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\GroundCompilerEvidenceBoundaryUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $G=(E,C,R,A,H,T,P,N)$ as the complete compiler-layer evidence boundary packet;
\item generated-recognizer evidence, requiring any admitted package, certificate, theorem, chapter, or accepted-export row to pass through $R$;
\item code/report non-evidence, requiring code channels, source text, theorem code, chapter code, metric reports, motif reports, compiler reports, and analysis summaries to remain in $C$ or $A$ unless $R$ supplies generated recognition evidence;
\item transport and replay, requiring componentwise $\hsame$ movement through $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ inspection through $T$ for every accepted consumer read;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring cannot-claim rows and audit/report rows to remain ledgered outputs rather than hidden proof, certificate, or compiler-input rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the displayed finite packet. The rows $E,C,R,A,H,T,P,N$ are the only coordinates available to a consumer of the evidence boundary.

Generated-recognizer evidence is read from $R$. Any package, certificate, theorem, chapter, or accepted-export claim must pass through that row before it can be treated as admitted compiler-layer evidence.

The code and report boundary is read from $C$ and $A$. These rows keep source text, theorem code, chapter code, compiler reports, metrics, motifs, and analysis summaries outside proof and certificate input unless the generated-recognizer row supplies the required recognition evidence.

Transport and replay are confined to $H$ and $T$. Componentwise $\hsame$ movement preserves the row class being transported, and each $\Cont$ route reads back the same visible coordinate rather than creating a hidden input.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ bind provenance and local naming. Since the carrier has no further coordinate for proof extraction from code or certificate extraction from reports, ledger rows cannot escape their non-input status.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GroundCompiler evidence boundary non-input scope]
\label{thm:groundcompiler-evidence-boundary-noninput-scope}
For every accepted $\GroundCompilerEvidenceBoundaryUp$ carrier $G=(E,C,R,A,H,T,P,N)$, compiler reports, source text, metrics, motifs, theorem codes, and chapter codes do not become proof inputs, certificate inputs, or formal compiler inputs without generated recognition evidence supplied by $R$ and replayed through the displayed routes $T$. Their readable status remains code, report, motif, metric, or ledger status inside $C$ and $A$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only row that can mediate evidence admission is $R$, while code-like and report-like artifacts are recorded in $C$ and $A$.

Use the NameCert obligations. They require every consumer read to pass through the displayed transport and continuation rows $H$ and $T$, so a code or report coordinate cannot be silently reclassified during transport.

If generated recognition evidence is absent, the artifact remains in its displayed code, report, motif, metric, or ledger row. The carrier has no separate proof-input, certificate-input, or hidden compiler-input coordinate, so the non-input scope is fixed by the packet itself.
\end{proof}
