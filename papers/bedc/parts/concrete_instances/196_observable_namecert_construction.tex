\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ObservableUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-observable-namecert}

The observable interface $\ObservableUp$ packages a self-adjoint operator on a Hilbert space carrier together with its spectrum and expectation pairing with quantum states. It depends on $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}), $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spectraltheorem-namecert}), and $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-quantumstate-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:observable-carrier}

\begin{definition}[ObservableUp BHist operator carrier]
\label{def:observable-bhist-operator-carrier}
An $\ObservableUp$ packet is a finite displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ row package
$$
\mathcal{O}=(H,A,S,E,W,\rho,\lambda).
$$
The row $H$ is the consumed $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ carrier row. The endpoint
$A:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the visible operator endpoint over $H$. The row $W$
records the self-adjointness witness for that endpoint. The row $S$ is the
consumed $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$ spectrum row attached to $(H,A,W)$.
The row $E$ records the expectation-source endpoints read through
$\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ and paired with the observable endpoint $A$. The
row $\rho$ is the shared finite $\Pkg$ provenance tying $H$, $A$, $S$, $E$,
and $W$ into one certificate packet, and $\lambda$ is the finite
$\Cont/\hsame$ ledger used by every transported row.

The packet is admitted as an $\ObservableUp$ carrier exactly when all Hilbert
space structure is projected from $H$, all spectral data is projected from
$S$, all expectation sources are projected from $E$, and the only observable
endpoint outside the dependency rows is the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ row
$A$ with its witness row $W$. No host operator equality, ambient Hilbert-space
equality, or external spectral primitive is part of the carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[ObservableUp spectral expectation classifier]
\label{def:observable-spectral-expectation-classifier}
Let
$$
\mathcal{O}=(H,A,S,E,W,\rho,\lambda)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
\mathcal{O}'=(H',A',S',E',W',\rho',\lambda')
$$
be admitted $\ObservableUp$ packets. The $\ObservableUp$ classifier holds
exactly when $\hsame$ transports the Hilbert carrier row, the operator
endpoint row, the self-adjointness witness row, the spectral row, the
expectation-source endpoints, and the shared package ledger:
$$
H\hsame H'\land A\hsame A'\land W\hsame W'\land S\hsame S'
\land E\hsame E'\land \rho\hsame\rho'\land \lambda\hsame\lambda'.
$$
The $H$ comparison is read through $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$, the $S$
comparison is read through $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$, and the
expectation endpoint comparison is read through $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$.
The classifier therefore identifies only displayed rows and their common
$\Pkg$ provenance. It does not compare host operators by equality.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:observable-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[ObservableUp Hilbert source boundary]
\label{thm:observable-hilbert-source-boundary}
For every admitted $\ObservableUp$ packet of
\autoref{def:observable-bhist-operator-carrier}, every operator row,
spectral row, and expectation row reads its Hilbert source only through the
consumed $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ row $H$ and $\hsame$ transport. In
particular, the operator endpoint $A$, the spectral-data row $S$, and the
expectation-source row $E$ cannot inspect an ambient Hilbert carrier apart
from the certified source row and its displayed classifier transport.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the packet $\mathcal{O}=(H,A,S,E,W,\rho,\lambda)$. The only field that
names a Hilbert source is $H$, and the only transport field available to move
Hilbert-side data is the $\mathsf{Cont}$ ledger $\lambda$ recording
$\hsame$-compatible rows.

Project the operator endpoint $A$ and its self-adjointness witness row $W$
from the packet. Their source is required to be the same $H$ because the
endpoint is displayed over the Hilbert source field rather than over a
separate carrier. Project the spectral row $S$ next; by construction it is
the spectral-data row for that same displayed endpoint and witness.

Finally project the expectation row $E$. Its state input is constrained by
$\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$, while its vector-space and inner-product
source is still the consumed Hilbert row $H$. Repacking these projections
with $\rho$ gives the stated source boundary: all Hilbert reads factor
through $H$ and the displayed $\hsame$ transport rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObservableUp spectral data ledger]
\label{thm:observable-spectral-data-ledger}
The spectral rows consumed by $\ObservableUp$ are exactly the
$\SpectralTheoremUp$ spectrum, projection-measure, and functional-calculus
rows for the displayed self-adjoint operator endpoint $(A,W)$, all with the
same package provenance row $\rho$. No spectral support, projection
assignment, or calculus action is accepted by the observable packet unless it
is one of the rows carried by the consumed $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$
entry $S$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the spectral-data field $S$ of
\autoref{def:observable-bhist-operator-carrier}. It is not an independent host
spectral theorem; it is the consumed $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$ row for
the operator endpoint field $A$ and witness field $W$.

The forward projection exposes precisely the spectral entries supplied by
that certificate: the spectrum row, the projection-measure row, and the
functional-calculus row. The provenance row $\rho$ attaches these entries to
the same finite packet as $H$, $A$, and $W$.

For the reverse reading, any displayed spectrum, projection-measure, and
functional-calculus rows with the shared $\rho$ provenance repack as the
spectral field $S$ only when they are accepted by
$\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$. Thus the ledger is exact for those spectral
rows and contains no further spectral datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObservableUp quantum-state expectation boundary]
\label{thm:observable-quantumstate-expectation-boundary}
Expectation evaluation in an $\ObservableUp$ packet may consume only
$\QuantumStateUp$-compatible input rows over the same Hilbert source $H$.
If the Hilbert source, operator endpoint, witness row, spectral row, and
quantum-state expectation row are transported by the displayed classifiers in
$\lambda$, then the expectation readback transports to the corresponding
expectation row over the transported packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the expectation-source row $E$ from the packet. By definition $E$ is
read through $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$, so every state input used by the
expectation pairing is a certified quantum-state row rather than an arbitrary
vector row.

Use \autoref{thm:observable-hilbert-source-boundary} to identify the Hilbert
source of the operator endpoint, witness row, spectral row, and expectation
row with the same consumed row $H$. The quantum-state boundary then supplies
only state inputs whose Hilbert field is compatible with that source.

Transport a classified packet through $\lambda$. The $\hsame$ rows move $H$,
$A$, $W$, $S$, and the expectation-source componentwise. Since the expectation
readback is displayed as the pairing of the transported operator endpoint
with the transported state input, it repacks as the transported expectation
row and remains inside the $\ObservableUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observable Hilbert-spectral ledger exactness]
\label{thm:observable-hilbert-spectral-ledger-exactness}
For every admitted $\ObservableUp$ packet of
\autoref{def:observable-bhist-operator-carrier}, the public
$\NameCert_{\ObservableUp}$ ledger exposes exactly the Hilbert carrier row
$H$, the operator endpoint $A$, the self-adjointness witness row $W$, the
$\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$ spectrum row $S$, the
$\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ expectation-source row $E$, the shared
$\Cont/\hsame$ transport ledger $\lambda$, and the shared $\Pkg$ provenance
$\rho$. No other row is exported by the certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carried tuple
$(H,A,S,E,W,\rho,\lambda)$. The first projection is the consumed
$\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ row, so all inner-product, vector, and scalar
structure available to the observable is read through $H$.

Project the spectral and state rows. The spectrum row $S$ is consumed through
$\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$ for the displayed endpoint and witness
$(A,W)$, while the expectation row $E$ supplies the quantum-state endpoints
through $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$.

The remaining projections are the visible operator endpoint $A$, the
self-adjointness witness row $W$, the package provenance $\rho$, and the
finite transport ledger $\lambda$. Repacking these projections gives exactly
the displayed public ledger. Since the carrier contains no host operator
equality and no external spectral primitive, no additional ledger row can be
read from the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observable classifier stability]
\label{thm:observable-classifier-stability}
If two admitted $\ObservableUp$ packets are related by the classifier of
\autoref{def:observable-spectral-expectation-classifier}, then $\hsame$
transport preserves the carried Hilbert row, operator endpoint row,
self-adjointness row, spectral row, expectation endpoint row, shared $\Pkg$
provenance, and finite $\Cont$ ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the classifier relation. It supplies the displayed comparisons
$H\hsame H'$, $A\hsame A'$, $W\hsame W'$, $S\hsame S'$, $E\hsame E'$,
$\rho\hsame\rho'$, and $\lambda\hsame\lambda'$.

Transport the dependency rows first. The Hilbert comparison moves all carrier
reads through $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$, and the spectral comparison moves the
spectrum read through $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$ with the transported
endpoint and witness.

Transport the expectation row through $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ and keep
the package boundary fixed by $\rho\hsame\rho'$. The ledger comparison
$\lambda\hsame\lambda'$ then identifies every finite $\Cont$ row used by the
source packet with the corresponding row in the target packet. Thus the
target packet has the same visible carrier and spectral-expectation rows, up
to the displayed classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observable expectation pairing boundary]
\label{thm:observable-expectation-pairing-boundary}
Every expectation value exported by an admitted $\ObservableUp$ packet is read
through the carried $\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ expectation-source row $E$
and the observable endpoint ledger $(A,W,\rho,\lambda)$. It is not justified
by host-level inner-product equality, host operator equality, or an external
spectral primitive.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Take an exported expectation value. By ledger exactness it must appear in the
expectation-source row $E$ of the carried packet, because no other row in the
packet is allowed to export expectation endpoints.

The observable side of the pairing is the displayed endpoint $A$ together
with its self-adjointness witness row $W$. The spectrum row $S$ may be used
only as the consumed $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$ dependency row attached
to those displayed observable data.

Finally attach the provenance and transport boundary. The $\Pkg$ row $\rho$
places the state endpoint and observable endpoint in the same finite packet,
and the $\Cont/\hsame$ ledger $\lambda$ supplies the only admissible transport
of the readback. Therefore the expectation pairing is a packet-level
readback, not a claim about equality of host inner products or host
operators.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observable NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:observable-namecert-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\ObservableUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of
the carrier of \autoref{def:observable-bhist-operator-carrier}, the
classifier of \autoref{def:observable-spectral-expectation-classifier},
Hilbert source boundary, spectral data ledger, quantum-state expectation
boundary, Hilbert-spectral ledger exactness, classifier stability,
expectation-pairing boundary, and the shared $\Cont/\Pkg$ ledger clauses for
the displayed rows $H,A,S,E,W,\rho,\lambda$. It consumes the existing
$\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$, $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$, and
$\NameCert_{\QuantumStateUp}$ dependency surfaces and consumes no host
operator equality or independent spectral primitive.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Collect the carrier rows first. The finite tuple
$(H,A,S,E,W,\rho,\lambda)$ displays the Hilbert dependency, observable
endpoint, spectral dependency, expectation-source dependency,
self-adjointness witness, package provenance, and transport ledger.

Collect the classifier rows next. The spectral expectation classifier
compares exactly those displayed coordinates by $\hsame$, with dependency
reads delegated to the Hilbert, spectral-theorem, and quantum-state
certificates named by the chapter dependencies.

Use \autoref{thm:observable-hilbert-source-boundary} for the Hilbert source
boundary, \autoref{thm:observable-spectral-data-ledger} for spectral ledger
exactness, \autoref{thm:observable-quantumstate-expectation-boundary} for the
state-input boundary, \autoref{thm:observable-hilbert-spectral-ledger-exactness}
for public ledger coverage, \autoref{thm:observable-classifier-stability} for
transported stability, and
\autoref{thm:observable-expectation-pairing-boundary} for the expectation
readback boundary. These clauses cover every public request admitted by the
carrier and classifier. Since none of the collected rows contains host
operator equality or an external spectral primitive, the stated clauses form
the full paper-side $\ObservableUp$ obligation surface.
\end{proof}
