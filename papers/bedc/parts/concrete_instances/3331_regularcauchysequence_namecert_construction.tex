\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy sequence carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-sequence-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchySequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(F,\mu,W,D,R,G,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the finite regular Cauchy family row whose members are read as rational regular sequences;
\item $\mu$ is the explicit modulus row assigning finite tail bounds to the family requests;
\item $W$ is the selected-window row, listing the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows used for the requested precisions;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ readback ledger containing the dyadic endpoints, radii, comparisons, and tolerance rows used by the selected windows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that presents each selected window as regular rational sequence data;
\item $G$ is the $\RealDiagonalWindowCoverageUp$ coverage row stating that the selected windows cover exactly the finite diagonal reads consumed by the handoff;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal handoff row reached after $F,\mu,W,D,R,G$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the family, modulus, selected windows, dyadic ledger, regular readbacks, coverage row, handoff row, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the family request through the modulus, windows, dyadic ledger, regular readback, diagonal-window coverage, and $\RealUp$ handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealDiagonalWindowCoverageUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchySequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient equality on regular sequences, no host real equality, no selected external limit, no countable-choice data, and no ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy sequence classifier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-sequence-classifier}

$$
S=(F,\mu,W,D,R,G,E,H,C,P,N)
\quad\text{and}\quad
S'=(F',\mu',W',D',R',G',E',H',C',P',N')

The classifier is finite and rowwise. It does not identify quotient representatives of regular Cauchy sequences and does not compare completed reals outside the displayed handoff row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy sequence window regularity]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-sequence-window-regularity}
For an accepted $\RegularCauchySequenceUp$ carrier
$$
S=(F,\mu,W,D,R,G,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
every selected window in $W$ is regular for the family row $F$ at the precision named by the explicit modulus row $\mu$. Its rational observations are read through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$ and repacked by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ before the coverage row $G$ may expose the read to the diagonal-limit handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier through \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-sequence-carrier}. The only rows that can name regular-family observations are $F,\mu,W,D,R,G$.

The selected-window row $W$ is reached from the family request only through the displayed continuation route in $C$ and the explicit modulus row $\mu$. Thus a window has no independent precision source.

The rational observations then pass through $D$ and $R$. The coverage row $G$ can expose only those reads, so regularity at the handoff boundary is the finite rowwise regularity recorded by the carrier.
\end{proof}
