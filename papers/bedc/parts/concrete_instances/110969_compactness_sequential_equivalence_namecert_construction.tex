\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompactnessSequentialEquivalenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-compactness-sequential-equivalence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive compactness practice, the $\CompactnessSequentialEquivalenceUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface on which finite-cover compactness and sequential compactness can be compared without importing an ambient equivalence theorem. It sits between the bounded-subsequence route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bolzanoweierstrass-namecert}, the finite-cover finite-net route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-heine-borel-finite-net-namecert}, the compact-metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the stream inspection surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite NameCert comparison surface for compact metric consumers; it is not a host proof that all compactness notions coincide, not countable choice, not quotient stream equality, and not ambient real completeness.

\begin{definition}[Compactness sequential equivalence carrier]
\label{def:compactness-sequential-equivalence-carrier}
A $\CompactnessSequentialEquivalenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(K,F,B,S,W,R,D,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row whose metric, total-boundedness, and completeness-facing reads bound both sides of the comparison.
\item $F$ is the finite-cover row read through $\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp$, including the displayed finite net, cover request, and finite subcover ledger.
\item $B$ is the bounded-subsequence row read through $\BolzanoWeierstrassUp$, including the interval-net and selected regular-subsequence route.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row naming the finite source indices inspected by the sequence side.
\item $W$ is the finite subsequence-window row, recording selected indices, selected windows, and monotonicity evidence.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the selected windows and their regular rational tail budgets.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, recording net mesh, cover-radius comparison, and rational slack checks used by both directions.
\item $Q$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached after the regular rational readback and cover-radius comparisons are accepted.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $K,F,B,S,W,R,D,Q$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replays from compact-metric reads to finite covers, finite nets, stream windows, subsequence windows, dyadic tolerance checks, regular rational readbacks, and the terminal real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactnessSequentialEquivalenceUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp$, $\BolzanoWeierstrassUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactnessSequentialEquivalenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $K,F,B,S,W,R,D,Q,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a global open-cover compactness theorem, an unrestricted sequential compactness principle, countable choice, quotient equality of streams, a selected host subsequence, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compactness sequential equivalence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compactness-sequential-equivalence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactnessSequentialEquivalenceUp$ carrier
$$
E=(K,F,B,S,W,R,D,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CompactnessSequentialEquivalenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of finite rows for compact-metric source exposure, finite-cover finite-net exposure, bounded-subsequence exposure, stream-window inspection, subsequence-window monotonicity, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance comparison, Real seal nonescape, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. No obligation row may be discharged by a host compactness-equivalence theorem, countable choice, quotient stream equality, an externally selected subsequence, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:compactness-sequential-equivalence-carrier}. Its rows expose exactly the compact-metric source, finite-cover finite-net route, bounded-subsequence route, stream windows, subsequence windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance checks, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming data.

The cover side is finite because $F$ records only the displayed net and the finite subcover ledger from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-heine-borel-finite-net-namecert}. The sequence side is finite because $B$, $S$, and $W$ record only the bounded-subsequence route, inspected stream windows, and selected finite windows.

The comparison between the two sides passes through $D$ and $Q$. These rows record tolerance and seal data already visible to the packet, so the local NameCert surface has no remaining coordinate in which a host compactness-equivalence theorem, countable choice, quotient stream equality, external subsequence, or ambient Real completeness could enter.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite subcover data yields a subsequence-window route]
\label{thm:compactness-sequential-equivalence-finite-subcover-to-subsequence}
Let
$$
E=(K,F,B,S,W,R,D,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CompactnessSequentialEquivalenceUp$ carrier. If a downstream compact-metric consumer reads the finite subcover side through $F$, then the same packet exposes a subsequence-window route through
$$
K,\quad F,\quad S,\quad W,\quad R,\quad D,\quad Q
$$
without adding a host subsequence selector, quotient stream equality, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite-cover row $F$. It supplies only a finite net, finite cover request, and finite subcover ledger over the compact-metric source $K$.

Use the stream window $S$ to record the finite source positions inspected by the consumer. The row $W$ then records the selected finite windows and their monotonicity evidence; it is packet data, not a choice functional supplied from outside the carrier.

Finally apply $R$, $D$, and $Q$. The regular rational readback consumes the selected windows, the dyadic row records the cover-radius and tolerance comparisons, and the Real row seals only those accepted finite reads. Therefore the route remains inside the displayed packet and exports none of the excluded host principles.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Subsequence-window data yields a finite-net cover route]
\label{thm:compactness-sequential-equivalence-subsequence-to-finite-net}
Let
$$
E=(K,F,B,S,W,R,D,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CompactnessSequentialEquivalenceUp$ carrier. If a downstream compact-metric consumer reads the sequential side through $B,S,W,R$, then the same packet exposes a finite-net cover route through
$$
K,\quad B,\quad S,\quad W,\quad R,\quad D,\quad F,\quad Q
$$
without adding a global open-cover compactness theorem, an unrestricted sequential compactness principle, quotient stream equality, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the sequential rows first. The bounded-subsequence row $B$, stream row $S$, subsequence-window row $W$, and regular rational row $R$ expose only finite windows and regular readback data.

The dyadic row $D$ transports that finite readback into the tolerance scale used by the finite-net cover ledger. It records mesh, radius, and slack comparisons; it does not create a second compactness principle.

The finite-net route is then the already carried row $F$, sealed by $Q$ over the same compact-metric source $K$. Since all rows are components of the same accepted carrier and are replayed by $H$ and $C$, the consumer obtains a finite-net cover route without any appeal to the excluded host principles.
\end{proof}
