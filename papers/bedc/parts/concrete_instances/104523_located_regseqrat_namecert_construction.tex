Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\LocatedRegSeqRatUp$ packet records a located regular sequence of rational approximants as finite BEDC NameCert data. It sits after the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the located real boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet supplies Real and Completion consumers with a nonquotient route from displayed regular sequence data to located real-name handoff, without importing selected limits, quotient stream equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[LocatedRegSeqRat carrier]
\label{def:located-regseqrat-carrier}
A $\LocatedRegSeqRatUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(D,S,R,Q,W,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, exposing finite dyadic scales and comparison rows used by later location requests.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation schedule, naming the finite windows at which rational approximants may be read.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-sequence source row, carrying rational readback and regularity evidence for the scheduled windows.
\item $Q$ is the located comparison row, recording for each displayed dyadic tolerance the finite branch that locates the regular sequence relative to the requested rational cut.
\item $W$ is the tail-window row, binding the location read to a finite suffix of the scheduled regular sequence.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $D,S,R,Q,W,K,P,N$.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a location request through the dyadic, stream, regular-sequence, comparison, tail-window, transport, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedRegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRegSeqRatUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier reads accepted packets only through $D,S,R,Q,W,H,K,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a selected limit representative, countable choice, trichotomy, host rational order, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedRegSeqRat NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-regseqrat-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedRegSeqRatUp$ carrier $L=(D,S,R,Q,W,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRegSeqRatUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, dyadic tolerance exposure, finite stream-window exposure, regular rational readback, located comparison classification, tail-window binding, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, local naming, and ledger refusal for quotient streams, selected limit representatives, countable choice, trichotomy, host rational order, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:located-regseqrat-carrier}. The packet exposes the dyadic ledger $D$, the observation schedule $S$, the regular rational row $R$, the located comparison row $Q$, and the tail-window row $W$ before the transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows can be consumed.

The NameCert obligations are precisely these projections. The dyadic and stream rows determine which finite tolerance and observation windows are available; the regular rational row supplies the readback; the located comparison and tail-window rows state where the location request is decided.

Transport and replay are exhausted by $H$ and $K$. Since the classifier has no additional coordinate beyond $D,S,R,Q,W,H,K,P,N$, the local naming row cannot import quotient stream equality, selected limit representatives, countable choice, trichotomy, host rational order, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedRegSeqRat tail-window stability]
\label{thm:located-regseqrat-tail-window-stability}
Every transported $\LocatedRegSeqRatUp$ location read preserves the same finite tail-window route. If a consumer requests a dyadic tolerance through $D$, the transported packet must still read the scheduled stream windows $S$, the regular rational source $R$, the located comparison row $Q$, and the tail-window row $W$ before it can inspect the local naming row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the transport row $H$ from the carrier. It acts componentwise on the dyadic ledger, stream schedule, regular rational source, located comparison row, tail-window row, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The replay row $K$ then repeats the same finite route after transport. A tolerance request first reads $D$, then the scheduled windows $S$, then the regular rational source $R$, and then the located comparison row $Q$ bound to the suffix named by $W$.

No transported read can bypass this suffix because the local naming row is not exposed until the displayed route has been replayed. Thus tail-window stability is internal to the carrier surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedRegSeqRat RealUp handoff]
\label{thm:located-regseqrat-realup-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing consumer of an accepted $\LocatedRegSeqRatUp$ packet factors through the located regular-sequence carrier before the terminal real-name seal is read. The handoff exposes only $D,S,R,Q,W,H,K,P,N$ and the sibling $\LocatedRealUp$ and $\RealUp$ certificate surfaces, so it cannot supply a quotient completion, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy decision, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness proof.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a Real-facing read. The carrier forces the read through the dyadic tolerance ledger, finite stream schedule, regular rational source, located comparison classifier, and tail-window row before it reaches provenance or local naming.

The sibling $\LocatedRealUp$ boundary consumes the displayed location row and not a host cut predicate. The $\RealUp$ seal is therefore terminal for the displayed route rather than a source of further completeness data.

Since no classifier coordinate stores quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient completeness, the handoff exports only the finite located regular-sequence certificate surface.
\end{proof}
