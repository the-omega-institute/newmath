\begin{definition}[HashLikeFiberBoundary carrier]
\label{def:hash-like-fiber-boundary-carrier}
A $\HashLikeFiberBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(D,F,G,T,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the visible digest row read through the $\HashUp$ certificate;
\item $F$ is the hidden fiber ledger row recording source-side membership
behind $D$ without identifying the source with the digest;
\item $G$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ provenance row for the same fiber ledger;
\item $T$ is the apophatic far-end boundary row read through the
$\ApophaticFixedPointFiberUp$ and $\ApophaticFixedPointUp$ sources;
\item $R$ is the refusal row blocking any consumer from treating $T$ as a
digest value, source payload, selected preimage, kernel object, or ambient
universe object;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $D,F,G,T,R,C,P,N$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes for digest, fiber, GAP,
far-end, refusal, transport, provenance, and local naming reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HashUp$, $\DigestFiberLedgerUp$,
$\ApophaticFixedPointFiberUp$, $\ApophaticFixedPointUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HashLikeFiberBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for digest-as-source equality, selected
preimage recovery, an internal far-end object, a global universe digest, or an
unledgered fiber consumer.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[HashLikeFiberBoundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hash-like-fiber-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:hash-like-fiber-boundary-carrier}, the local
$\NameCert_{\HashLikeFiberBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier
admission for
$$
X=(D,F,G,T,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
visible digest exposure through $D$, hidden fiber-ledger exposure through $F$,
GAP provenance through $G$, apophatic far-end boundary naming through $T$,
far-end refusal through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$,
displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local
naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The rows $D,F,G,T,R$ are the complete concrete
boundary content, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same
finite packet.

Read the source chapters in their displayed order. The $\HashUp$ certificate
supplies digest visibility, $\DigestFiberLedgerUp$ supplies hidden fiber and
GAP provenance, $\ApophaticFixedPointFiberUp$ supplies the fiber-to-far-end
boundary route, and $\ApophaticFixedPointUp$ supplies the refusal discipline
that prevents far-end internalization.

The NameCert surface is exhausted by these rows. Since the carrier has no
coordinate for digest-as-source equality, selected preimage recovery, an
internal far-end object, global universe digest, or unledgered fiber consumer,
no accepted read can obtain any of those objects from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HashLikeFiberBoundary noninternalization]
\label{thm:hash-like-fiber-boundary-noninternalization}
Every accepted $\HashLikeFiberBoundaryUp$ consumer that reads the far-end row
$T$ receives it only as the apophatic boundary of the displayed fiber behind
the visible digest row $D$. The consumer may replay $T$ through $R,H,C,P,N$,
but it cannot read $T$ as a digest value, source payload, selected preimage,
kernel object, global universe digest, host equality, or ambient completeness
principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier inventory in
\autoref{def:hash-like-fiber-boundary-carrier}. The far-end coordinate is the
boundary row $T$, and it is displayed together with digest, fiber, GAP, and
refusal rows.

Apply the NameCert obligations of
\autoref{thm:hash-like-fiber-boundary-namecert-obligations}. Any consumer of
$T$ must preserve the route through $D,F,G,T,R,H,C,P,N$, so the far-end read
cannot detach from the fiber ledger that made it visible.

The refusal row $R$ excludes the forbidden interpretations. A read that treats
$T$ as a digest value, payload, selected preimage, object, global digest, host
equality, or completeness principle has no coordinate in the carrier and
therefore no accepted route.
\end{proof}
