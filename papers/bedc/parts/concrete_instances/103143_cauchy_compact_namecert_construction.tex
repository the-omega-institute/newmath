Following Bishop-style compact metric arguments, the $\CauchyCompactUp$ packet records the finite certificate route by which explicit total boundedness and complete metric data turn a displayed regular Cauchy sequence window into an extracted limit seal. It sits over the compact packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert}, the compact metric finite-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the complete metric limit rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the totally bounded completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallyboundedcompletion-namecert}, and the L10 real-name spine of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names sequential compactness only in the constructive Cauchy-extraction sense supplied by displayed finite nets, regular rational windows, and completeness rows; it is not an open-cover compactness theorem and does not introduce a choice-selected subsequence principle.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompact carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-compact-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompactUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(M,L,T,S,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the compact metric row. It records the source metric carrier, classifier, finite compact-net admission data, and the compact-metric provenance read from the $\CompactMetricUp$ and $\CompactUp$ packets.
\item $L$ is the complete metric row. It supplies the displayed limit-witness route for regular Cauchy point streams and the uniqueness route available under the local metric classifier.
\item $T$ is the totally bounded finite-net schedule. For each displayed dyadic radius request it records the finite bundle of centers, coverage witnesses, mesh ledger, and replay route consumed by the extraction.
\item $S$ is the regular Cauchy sequence window. It records the $\StreamNameUp$ finite observations, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity readback, and the late-distance comparisons by which the sequence is admitted as a Cauchy stream in the metric row.
\item $D$ is the dyadic radius ledger. It records every $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius, refinement, comparison, and tail-budget read used by $T$ and $S$.
\item $E$ is the extracted limit seal. It is the $\RealUp$-facing and metric-facing row reached only after $M,L,T,S,D$ have been displayed; it records the limit name, the convergence comparisons, and the local extraction certificate.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact metric row, complete metric row, totally bounded schedule, regular Cauchy window, dyadic radius ledger, extracted limit seal, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
M,L,T,S,D \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompactUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\CompactUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\TotallyBoundedCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompactUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,L,T,S,D,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, a choice-selected subsequence, quotient stream equality, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompact NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-compact-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompactUp$ carrier
$$
K=(M,L,T,S,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompactUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier projection for the compact metric row, complete metric row, totally bounded finite-net schedule, regular Cauchy sequence window, dyadic radius ledger, extracted limit seal, transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local name;
\item classifier exactness, requiring packet comparison to inspect only the displayed rows $M,L,T,S,D,E,H,C,P,N$ and to preserve their componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item Cauchy extraction, requiring every limit read to pass first through the finite-net schedule $T$, the regular Cauchy window $S$, the dyadic radius ledger $D$, and then the complete metric row $L$ before the seal $E$ is exposed;
\item transport stability, requiring any transported packet to replay the same compact metric source, total-bounded mesh ledger, complete-metric limit route, regular sequence window, dyadic comparisons, and extracted limit seal;
\item ledger confinement, requiring $C,P,N$ to replay, source, and name only the displayed finite route and to export no open-cover compactness, choice-selected sequential compactness, quotient stream equality, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
No $\CauchyCompactUp$ obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier projection is immediate from \autoref{def:cauchy-compact-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the compact metric row, complete metric row, finite-net schedule, regular Cauchy window, dyadic ledger, extracted limit seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Classifier exactness is componentwise. The classifier can compare two accepted packets only by reading $M,L,T,S,D,E,H,C,P,N$, and the transport row $H$ records preservation of precisely those coordinates.

For Cauchy extraction, begin with a displayed tolerance request. The dyadic ledger $D$ fixes the radius and tail budget, the total-bounded schedule $T$ exposes the finite centers at that radius, and the sequence window $S$ supplies the regular Cauchy observations and late-distance comparisons. The complete metric row $L$ is then applied to the same admitted stream route, producing the limit row sealed by $E$.

Transport stability and ledger confinement follow from $H,C,P,N$. These rows replay and name only the displayed route, so the excluded open-cover, choice, quotient, host-equality, trichotomy, and ambient real-completeness principles have no carrier coordinate from which they could be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompact extraction scope]
\label{thm:cauchy-compact-extraction-scope}
Let
$$
K=(M,L,T,S,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CauchyCompactUp$ carrier. Every extracted limit read exported by $K$ factors through the displayed rows
$$
\CompleteMetricUp,\quad
\TotallyBoundedUp,\quad
\CompactMetricUp,\quad
\RegSeqRatUp,\quad
\RealUp,\quad
\StreamNameUp,\quad
\DyadicRatCoreUp
$$
together with the structural rows $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. The extraction reads no open-cover compactness row, no choice-selected subsequence row, no quotient stream equality row, no host real equality row, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The compact metric input is $M$, whose finite-net side is read through the $\CompactMetricUp$ and $\TotallyBoundedUp$ surfaces; no open-cover row is present in the carrier.

The sequential input is $S$ together with $D$. These rows expose the $\StreamNameUp$ finite observations, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity readback, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius ledger before any limit request can reach the completion side.

The limit step is $L$ followed by $E$. The row $L$ is the displayed $\CompleteMetricUp$ route for the same regular Cauchy stream, and $E$ is only the resulting metric and $\RealUp$ seal for that route.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same dependencies. Since the tuple has no coordinate outside the displayed analytic and structural rows, every exported extraction remains confined to the stated scope.
\end{proof}
