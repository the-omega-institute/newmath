\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PrecompactCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-precompact-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\PrecompactCompletionUp$ packet reads $\PrecompactMetricUp$ finite-net and filter rows through $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ and $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$ without quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness. It sits between the precompact-metric bridge of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-precompactmetric-namecert}, the totally bounded completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallyboundedcompletion-namecert}, and the constructive metric completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-constructive-metric-completion-namecert}; its role is to preserve the finite BEDC ledger that completion consumers inspect.

\begin{definition}[Precompact completion carrier]
\label{def:precompact-completion-carrier}
A $\PrecompactCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(X,N,F,E,C,S,H,R,Q,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source metric carrier row, including accepted point histories, metric-distance witnesses, and classifier transports already exposed by the $\PrecompactMetricUp$ packet.
\item $N$ is the finite-net ledger inherited from $\PrecompactMetricUp$. For each positive tolerance it records the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of centers, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, center histories, distance witnesses, rational comparison ledgers, and net-refinement reads.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ replay row generated from the same metric and finite-net data, including finite basis packets, common-refinement rows, regular tail windows, and dyadic neighbourhood witnesses.
\item $E$ is the $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ row that inserts source observations into the dense completion boundary while preserving the displayed finite-net and filter-basis ledgers.
\item $C$ is the $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$ route through regular Cauchy names, dyadic tolerances, separated comparison, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, and the terminal $\RealUp$ naming boundary.
\item $S$ is the stream-and-real seal row. It records the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ reads that completion-facing consumers may inspect after $F,E,C$ have been replayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source metric, finite nets, filter-basis replay, embedding row, constructive completion route, stream-and-real seal, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $R$ is the route ledger of displayed $\Cont$ reads, requiring every consumer to pass through $N,F,E,C,S$ in that order before it reaches $H,Q,L$.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\PrecompactMetricUp$, $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$, $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$, $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PrecompactCompletionUp}$ row naming the displayed packet and the permitted reads from it.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier on such packets is componentwise transport through $H$ together with replay through the displayed route row $R$. No coordinate stores a quotient completion, a selected completed point, a choice schedule, an open-cover compactness argument, or an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:precompact-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PrecompactCompletionUp$ carrier
$P=(X,N,F,E,C,S,H,R,Q,L)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PrecompactCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed source metric, finite-net ledger, Cauchy-filter basis, completion embedding, constructive completion route, stream-and-real seal, transport ledger, route ledger, provenance, and local naming row;
\item finite-net persistence, requiring every completion tolerance request to read the centers, membership constructors, distance witnesses, rational bounds, and refinement rows recorded in $N$;
\item filter-basis replay, requiring each completion-facing read to consume the finite basis packets, common-refinement rows, regular tail windows, and dyadic neighbourhood witnesses in $F$ before it reaches the embedding row;
\item dense-embedding handoff, requiring source observations to pass through $E$ before the constructive completion route $C$ may be inspected;
\item constructive-completion non-escape, requiring the completed read to continue through $C$ and $S$ using only $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ naming rows exposed by the finite route;
\item ledger exactness, excluding quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:precompact-completion-carrier}. The packet lists exactly the source metric, finite-net ledger, Cauchy-filter basis, dense embedding, constructive completion route, stream-and-real seal, transport ledger, route ledger, provenance, and local naming row.

Finite-net persistence is read from $N$. A tolerance request inspects the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, its $\mathsf{InBundle}$ constructors, center histories, metric witnesses, rational comparisons, and refinement rows; no later row can replace this ledger by an open cover or by a host finite set.

Filter-basis replay and dense-embedding handoff follow the route order in $R$. The basis row $F$ consumes the displayed finite-net data, and the embedding row $E$ receives only those basis reads before placing source observations in the dense completion boundary.

The constructive-completion read is then local to $C$ and $S$. Regular Cauchy names, dyadic tolerances, separated comparison, finite stream windows, regular rational readbacks, and the terminal real-name seal are the only completion-facing rows exposed downstream.

Finally, $H,Q,L$ record transport, provenance, and local naming for the same coordinates. Since the excluded principles have no carrier coordinate, no replay edge, and no provenance row, the local $\NameCert_{\PrecompactCompletionUp}$ surface is exactly the listed obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactCompletion density handoff]
\label{thm:precompact-completion-density-handoff}
Let $P=(X,N,F,E,C,S,H,R,Q,L)$ be an accepted $\PrecompactCompletionUp$ packet. Every completion-facing consumer factors through
$$
\PrecompactMetricUp,\ \CauchyFilterBasisUp,\ \CompletionEmbeddingUp,\ \ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
Equivalently, the consumer reads the finite route
$$
X,N \longmapsto F \longmapsto E \longmapsto C \longmapsto S \longmapsto H,R,Q,L
$$
rather than a quotient completion class, a selected completed point, a choice schedule, an open-cover compactness proof, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the source metric row $X$ and the finite-net ledger $N$. These are precisely the precompact metric data of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-precompactmetric-namecert}: point histories, distance witnesses, finite centers, membership constructors, rational bounds, and refinement reads.

The consumer next reaches the Cauchy-filter basis row $F$. This step keeps the handoff finite because the basis packets, common refinements, regular tail windows, and dyadic neighbourhood witnesses are generated from the same rows already exposed by $\PrecompactMetricUp$.

Density enters only through $E$. The $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ row receives source observations after the basis replay and places them in the completion boundary without adding quotient representatives or selected limits.

The constructive completion route $C$ and seal row $S$ complete the factorization. They expose the regular Cauchy, stream-name, regular-rational, and real-name reads of $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ while preserving the displayed route ledger.

Transport, provenance, and local naming through $H,R,Q,L$ do not add new data; they preserve the same dependency order. Thus every completion-facing consumer factors through the named route and cannot inspect any of the excluded nonconstructive or ambient-completeness objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrecompactCompletion ledger exactness]
\label{thm:precompact-completion-ledger-exactness}
For every accepted $\PrecompactCompletionUp$ packet $P=(X,N,F,E,C,S,H,R,Q,L)$, every exported completion read remains inside the finite ledger
$$
X,N,F,E,C,S,H,R,Q,L .
$$
It cannot export a quotient completion, a selected completed point, countable choice, open-cover compactness, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an exported completion read from the carrier. By \autoref{def:precompact-completion-carrier}, the read has access only to the ten displayed coordinates and their componentwise transport rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:precompact-completion-namecert-obligations}. Finite-net persistence keeps the read inside $N$, filter-basis replay moves it through $F$, dense-embedding handoff moves it through $E$, and constructive-completion non-escape moves it through $C$ and $S$.

The remaining rows $H,R,Q,L$ record transport, route order, provenance, and local naming for the same data. Every excluded object is absent from these rows, so no exported completion read can leave the displayed finite ledger.
\end{proof}
