\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NeighborhoodFilterUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-neighborhood-filter-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bourbaki, the $\NeighborhoodFilterUp$ packet records the local filter generated by displayed neighbourhood rows around a named point or finite source window. It sits between the finite directed basis interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-filterbase-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the open-neighbourhood discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, and the completion handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}. The packet names a local neighbourhood-filter interface; it does not quotient filters, select ultrafilters, choose limits, assert host point equality, or import ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[NeighborhoodFilter carrier]
\label{def:neighborhood-filter-carrier}
A $\NeighborhoodFilterUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(X,x,O,B,D,C,M,T,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source carrier row whose visible points are consumed through $\MetricUp$ or $\TopologyUp$ data.
\item $x$ is the focused point or source-window row around which the local filter is read.
\item $O$ is the finite open-neighbourhood row, exposed through the $\TopologyUp$ classifier.
\item $B$ is the $\FilterBaseUp$ row generated by the displayed neighbourhood family.
\item $D$ is the directed-refinement ledger: two displayed neighbourhood rows admit a smaller displayed neighbourhood row before any filter consumer reads downstream data.
\item $C$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ compatibility row used when the local neighbourhood filter is consumed by completion routes.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ witness row when neighbourhoods are read as metric balls or radius windows.
\item $T$ records the $\TopologyUp$ provenance that the displayed open rows are local neighbourhoods of the same focused source.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source, point, open row, basis row, directed refinement, Cauchy compatibility, metric read, topology provenance, replay, package, and naming rows.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a local-neighbourhood request through $X,x,O,B,D,C,M,T,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NeighborhoodFilterUp$, $\FilterBaseUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NeighborhoodFilterUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for ultrafilters, quotient filters, selected cluster points, host equality of neighbourhood systems, countable-choice selectors, or ambient completion theorems.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NeighborhoodFilter directed completion base]
\label{thm:neighborhood-filter-directed-completion-base}
For every accepted $\NeighborhoodFilterUp$ carrier $U=(X,x,O,B,D,C,M,T,H,K,P,N)$, every completion-facing read of the local filter first traverses the directed $\FilterBaseUp$ row $B$ and its refinement ledger $D$. The $\CauchyFilterUp$ row $C$ and the $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ consumer may read only the route
$$
X,x,O \longmapsto B,D \longmapsto C,M,T,H,K,P,N .
$$
Thus local-neighbourhood completion data are exported as finite basis rows rather than quotient filters, ultrafilters, selected limits, or hidden host neighbourhood equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:neighborhood-filter-carrier}. The only basis-facing coordinates are the open-neighbourhood row $O$, the generated $\FilterBaseUp$ row $B$, and the directed-refinement ledger $D$.

The completion side cannot bypass those rows. A $\CauchyFilterUp$ or $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ consumer reads $C$ only after $B$ and $D$ have supplied the local directed base, while $M$ and $T$ record the metric and topological provenance of the same neighbourhood family.

Finally the structural rows $H,K,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name that route. Since ultrafilters, quotient filters, selected limits, host equality, and choice selectors are absent from the carrier, every completion-facing read is exhausted by the displayed basis route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NeighborhoodFilter local Cauchy handoff]
\label{thm:neighborhood-filter-local-cauchy-handoff}
Let $U=(X,x,O,B,D,C,M,T,H,K,P,N)$ be an accepted $\NeighborhoodFilterUp$ carrier. Whenever a Cauchy-filter completion route consumes the local neighbourhood filter, the handoff factors through the same $\FilterBaseUp$ row $B$, directed-refinement ledger $D$, $\MetricUp$ or $\TopologyUp$ provenance rows $M,T$, and local $\NameCert_{\NeighborhoodFilterUp}$ row $N$. No consumer receives an independent filter object beyond the finite packet $U$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:neighborhood-filter-directed-completion-base}. It fixes the source-to-basis-to-completion route before any completion-facing read can inspect the Cauchy row $C$.

Use the metric and topological provenance rows next. The row $M$ records ball-style local reads when available, while $T$ records open-neighbourhood membership through the topology carrier; both are attached to the same focused source $X,x,O$.

The local NameCert row $N$ closes the handoff by naming the visible packet $U$. Thus the Cauchy-filter completion route receives finite neighbourhood-filter data, not a detached filter, selected limit, ultrafilter, quotient, host equality witness, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{proof}
