\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TypeCheckingDerivationTraceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-typecheckingderivationtrace-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\TypeCheckingDerivationTraceUp$ surface records a type-checking run as a finite BEDC packet. It refines the $\TypeClassifierMembershipUp$, $\ClassifierTypingUp$, and $\SubjectReductionUp$ views by making the derivation ledger, reduction route, classifier membership, transport, provenance, and local naming row visible in one certificate.

\begin{definition}[Type-checking derivation trace carrier]
\label{def:type-checking-derivation-trace-carrier}
A $\TypeCheckingDerivationTraceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(T,E,C,D,H,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the term row, recording the source term, target term, typing context entries, and the finite term-history positions inspected by the checker;
\item $E$ is the $\Ext$-membership classifier row, recording the accepted typing judgments for the source row and the target row;
\item $C$ is the $\Cont$ reduction route, displaying each checked step from the source term row to the target term row;
\item $D$ is the derivation ledger, listing the typing-rule entries, premise addresses, and rule conclusions consumed by the checker;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the term row, classifier row, continuation route, derivation ledger, subject-reduction row, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $R$ is the subject-reduction row, recording that every displayed $\Cont$ step whose source is in $E$ has its target in the transported $\Ext$ row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, term rows, $\Ext$, $\Cont$, derivation ledgers, $\hsame$, subject-reduction rows, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TypeCheckingDerivationTraceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The packet is finite and derivation-facing. It contains no ambient metatheory, no host equality of terms, no unrecorded typing rule, no hidden reduction step, and no proof object outside the displayed BEDC rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Type-checking derivation trace subject-reduction surface]
\label{thm:type-checking-derivation-trace-subject-reduction-surface}
Every accepted $\TypeCheckingDerivationTraceUp$ carrier routes the typed source row and the typed target row through the displayed $\Ext$ and $\Cont$ data. More precisely, if a consumer reads a source typing judgment from $E$ and a reduction step from $C$, then the only available target typing judgment is the one recorded by the subject-reduction row $R$, transported through $H$ and packaged by $P,N$.

Thus a trace consumer may inspect $T,E,C,D,H,R,P,N$, but it cannot replace the derivation ledger by an unrecorded rule, replace the reduction route by a host computation, or identify source and target terms by host equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $Z=(T,E,C,D,H,R,P,N)$. The source and target term entries are carried by $T$, while the typing judgments available to the checker are carried by $E$.

Next read the continuation route $C$. Each reduction step consumed by a trace is displayed before any target typing judgment is exposed, so the route cannot be supplied by an external host computation.

Finally use the subject-reduction row $R$ together with $H,P,N$. The transport rows replay the same classifier and continuation data under componentwise $\hsame$, and the package and local naming rows bind the target typing judgment to the displayed trace. Since no other typing-rule or equality coordinate is present, the subject-reduction surface is exhausted by those rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Type-checking derivation trace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:type-checking-derivation-trace-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:type-checking-derivation-trace-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\TypeCheckingDerivationTraceUp}$ surface consists of six finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the packet $Z=(T,E,C,D,H,R,P,N)$;
\item $\Ext$ membership for the displayed source and target typing rows;
\item subject reduction along the displayed $\Cont$ route;
\item ledger exactness for every typing-rule premise and conclusion consumed by $D$;
\item classifier transport through the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$;
\item non-escape from the host type theory, excluding unrecorded typing rules, host equality of terms, hidden reduction steps, external proof objects, and ambient metatheoretic closure.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of the finite tuple in \autoref{def:type-checking-derivation-trace-carrier}. The rows $T,E,C,D,H,R,P,N$ are the only public coordinates named by the local certificate.

The $\Ext$ membership and subject-reduction obligations are read together. The classifier row $E$ records the accepted typing judgments, the continuation route $C$ records the checked reduction path, and \autoref{thm:type-checking-derivation-trace-subject-reduction-surface} identifies the target judgment through $R$.

Ledger exactness is supplied by $D$. Each typing-rule premise and conclusion consumed by the checker is addressed inside that row, so a consumer cannot introduce an unlisted rule application.

The classifier transport and non-escape clauses then follow from $H,P,N$ and the carrier inventory. Componentwise $\hsame$ rows transport only the displayed data, while the missing coordinates rule out host equality, hidden reductions, external proof objects, and ambient metatheory.
\end{proof}
