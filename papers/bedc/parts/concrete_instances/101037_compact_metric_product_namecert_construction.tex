\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompactMetricProductUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive metric compactness and Kelley-style product metrics, the $\CompactMetricProductUp$ packet records binary finite-product compactness for L10 compact metric spaces as a displayed BEDC certificate. It reads the product-metric ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-productmetric-namecert}, the compact metric source rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the finite-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, the complete-limit surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the finite epsilon-net rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-epsilon-net-namecert}, and the binary product carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prod-namecert}. The packet names the standard constructive route: build finite product nets componentwise, pair complete-limit witnesses componentwise, and expose the resulting compact-metric package only through the displayed product metric.

\begin{definition}[Compact metric product carrier]\label{def:compact-metric-product-carrier}
A $\CompactMetricProductUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,Y,M,P,T,L,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the first compact metric source row. It exposes its $\CompactMetricUp$ packet only through the associated $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, and $\FiniteEpsilonNetUp$ rows.
\item $Y$ is the second compact metric source row, with the same displayed compact-metric boundary for the second component.
\item $M$ is the $\ProductMetricUp$ row on the binary product carrier. It stores the two projection routes, the component distance reads, and the product-distance ledger.
\item $P$ is the $\ProdUp$ pairing row carrying the product points inspected by $M$, together with the two displayed projection histories.
\item $T$ is the finite epsilon-net product witness. A tolerance request is split into component tolerances, the component $\FiniteEpsilonNetUp$ witnesses are read from $X$ and $Y$, and their paired centers form the product finite-net row.
\item $L$ is the paired complete-limit witness row. A regular Cauchy stream in the product metric is projected through $M$ to the two component $\CompleteMetricUp$ witnesses, then repacked through $P$ as the displayed product limit row.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two compact metric sources, the product metric row, the product carrier row, the finite-net product witness, the paired limit witness, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a compactness request through
$$
X,Y \longmapsto M,P \longmapsto T,L \longmapsto H,Q,N .
$$
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactMetricProductUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ProductMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\FiniteEpsilonNetUp$, $\ProdUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactMetricProductUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,M,P,T,L,H,C,Q,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, quotient product equality, selected limits, countable choice, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric product NameCert obligations]\label{thm:compact-metric-product-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactMetricProductUp$ carrier
$$
K=(X,Y,M,P,T,L,H,C,Q,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CompactMetricProductUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two compact metric sources, product metric row, product carrier row, finite-net product witness, paired complete-limit witness, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item product-metric exactness, requiring every product distance and projection read to pass through the displayed $\ProductMetricUp$ row $M$ and $\ProdUp$ row $P$;
\item finite-net product coverage, requiring $T$ to be assembled from the two component $\FiniteEpsilonNetUp$ rows and the product-distance ledger of $M$;
\item complete-limit pairing, requiring $L$ to project every product Cauchy stream to the two component $\CompleteMetricUp$ witnesses before repacking the limit through $P$;
\item non-escape provenance, requiring $H,C,Q,N$ to preserve the displayed rows and to export no open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, quotient product equality, selected limit, countable-choice selector, or external compactness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these five rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompactMetricProductUp.CompactMetricProductTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}

\begin{proof}
The carrier row already lists the only admissible coordinates. Carrier admission is therefore the tuple inspection of $X,Y,M,P,T,L,H,C,Q,N$, while product-metric exactness is the projection and distance replay inherited from $M$ and $P$.

For total boundedness, read the positive tolerance through the component tolerance split recorded by $T$. The two component compact metric sources expose their finite epsilon-net rows, and the product row pairs the chosen centers through $P$. The product-distance ledger in $M$ then gives the displayed coverage comparison.

For completeness, project a product Cauchy stream through the two projection routes of $M$. The component complete-limit rows in $X$ and $Y$ supply the two limits, and $L$ repacks those limits through the binary product carrier $P$.

Finally, $H,C,Q,N$ only replay and name the rows already displayed. Since no listed coordinate stores an open-cover argument, sequential compactness theorem, quotient equality, selected limit, or countable-choice selector, none can be exported by the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric product component handoff]\label{thm:compact-metric-product-component-handoff}
Let $K=(X,Y,M,P,T,L,H,C,Q,N)$ be an accepted $\CompactMetricProductUp$ carrier. Every consumer read of compactness for the product factors through the two component $\CompactMetricUp$ rows $X,Y$ and the $\ProductMetricUp$ projection ledger $M$. In particular, the only compactness data visible to the consumer are the component finite-net rows, the component complete-limit rows, their paired transport through $P$, and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows of $K$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Start with an arbitrary product compactness consumer. The carrier has no compactness coordinate except $T$ and $L$, so the consumer must ask for finite coverage or complete-limit data through those rows.

The finite-coverage request reaches $T$. That row is defined by reading the finite epsilon-net witnesses from $X$ and $Y$, then pairing the selected centers through $P$ and measuring coverage through $M$.

The complete-limit request reaches $L$. That row is defined by projecting the product Cauchy stream through $M$, invoking the component complete-limit witnesses carried by $X$ and $Y$, and repacking the two limits through $P$.

Thus every compactness read is componentwise before it is product-facing. The remaining rows $H,C,Q,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this same route without adding any external compactness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompactMetric package for binary products]\label{thm:compact-metric-product-compactmetric-package}
Every accepted $\CompactMetricProductUp$ carrier
$$
K=(X,Y,M,P,T,L,H,C,Q,N)
$$
supplies a $\CompactMetricUp$ package for the binary product named by $P$ and metrized by $M$. Its total-bounded component is the finite epsilon-net product witness $T$, and its complete component is the paired complete-limit witness $L$. The package is exposed only through the displayed rows $X,Y,M,P,T,L,H,C,Q,N$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The product metric row $M$ supplies the metric carrier for the binary product row $P$. This gives the target metric surface on which a $\CompactMetricUp$ package can be read.

The total-bounded half is $T$. For each admitted tolerance, $T$ reads finite epsilon-net witnesses from the component compact metric rows, pairs their centers, and uses the product-distance ledger to prove product coverage.

The complete half is $L$. A product Cauchy stream is projected to the two component streams, the component complete-limit witnesses produce component limits, and the binary product row repacks those limits as the product limit.

Combining these two rows gives the compact metric package for the product. The package has no route to open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, quotient product equality, selected limits, countable choice, Lean verification, or bridge checking, because those data are absent from the carrier and from its replay and provenance rows.
\end{proof}
