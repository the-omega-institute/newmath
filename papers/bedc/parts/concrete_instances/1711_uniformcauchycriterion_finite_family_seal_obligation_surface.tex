\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion finite-family seal obligation package]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-finite-family-seal-obligation-package}
Let
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet. The rows $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$ form the finite-family seal obligation surface for every $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ consumer that reaches a $\RegSeqRatUp$ tail read or a $\RealUp$ seal read.

The surface consists exactly of the finite index ledger $I$, shared window family $W$, shared threshold schedule $M$, dyadic tolerance row $Q$, tail ledger $T$, seal handoff row $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transports $H$, displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$, package provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. It contains no selected limit, quotient stream, host real equality, ambient completeness proof, or detached tail oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First expose the finite window spine. Apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-window-stability}; every window read remains a read of the displayed family $W$ over the finite index ledger $I$.

Next bind the threshold and tail data. Apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-shared-threshold-transport} and \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-tail-ledger-exactness}. These force the route from the family window to pass through the displayed $M,Q,T$ rows.

Now pass to the consumers. By \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-regseqrat-uniform-consumer-exhaustion}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ tail reader is exhausted by the rows of $U$. By \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-seal-consumer-factorization}, the $\RealUp$ seal reader factors through the same displayed package.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-overlap-refinement-determinacy} and \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-seal-route-uniqueness}. Overlaps cannot split the tail ledger, and the seal route has no second public handoff. Hence the displayed rows are exactly the finite-family seal obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion finite-family RegSeqRat--Real handoff]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-finite-family-regseq-real-handoff}
For an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet
$$
U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
every $\RegSeqRatUp$-visible tail read and every $\RealUp$ seal read factor through the same shared-window route
$$
I \longmapsto W \longmapsto M \longmapsto Q \longmapsto T \longmapsto E.
$$
The tail side and the seal side may refine the displayed finite ledger, but they cannot fork into separate window families, threshold schedules, dyadic tolerance routes, or completion routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the shared window. Apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-window-stability} and \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-shared-threshold-transport}. They identify $W$ and $M$ as the common route before any tail or seal data can be read.

Use \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-tail-ledger-exactness}. The $\RegSeqRatUp$-visible tail read is exactly the displayed $T$ row after the dyadic tolerance row $Q$.

For the seal boundary, apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-seal-consumer-factorization}. The $\RealUp$ seal read is downstream of that same tail ledger rather than an independent completion route.

Refinements do not alter this handoff. Apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-overlap-refinement-determinacy}; any overlap refinement still determines the same tail ledger and the same seal route. The stated shared route follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyCriterion finite-family nonescape ledger]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-finite-family-nonescape-ledger}
Let $U=(I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ packet carrying the finite-family seal obligation surface of \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-finite-family-seal-obligation-package}. A consumer of this surface cannot inspect a selected limit, quotient stream, host real equality, ambient completeness witness, or any unledgered completion datum.

All admissible reads are exhausted by the displayed window, threshold, tolerance, tail, seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-regseqrat-uniform-consumer-exhaustion}. The RegSeqRat-facing read has no coordinate outside $I,W,M,Q,T,E,H,C,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-real-seal-consumer-factorization}. The Real-facing read also factors through the same displayed package, so it cannot introduce a selected limit or ambient completion witness.

Use \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-overlap-refinement-determinacy} to rule out escape through refined windows. The overlap still determines the same tail ledger and seal route.

Finally use \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-criterion-seal-route-uniqueness}. A second route to host equality, quotient streams, or completion data would require another visible seal handoff, and that handoff is absent from the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
