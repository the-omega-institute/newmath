\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BetaSubstitutionPreservationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-betasubstitutionpreservation-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\BetaSubstitutionPreservationUp$ packet realizes the beta-redex discharge obligation of \autoref{def:metacic-beta-substitution-preservation} as a finite BEDC naming certificate. It records the body typing row, argument typing row, shifted-domain boundary, substitution route, substituted-codomain row, target typing row, binder ledger, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming row that a later $\SubjectReductionUp$ beta handoff can cite without closing global subject reduction.

\begin{definition}[Beta-substitution preservation carrier]
\label{def:beta-substitution-preservation-carrier}
A $\BetaSubstitutionPreservationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(M,A,D,S,Q,T,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the body typing row in the context extended by the shifted domain;
\item $A$ is the argument typing row in the original context;
\item $D$ is the shifted-domain boundary row tying the binder domain to the body context;
\item $S$ is the substitution route row that moves the argument through the binder boundary;
\item $Q$ is the substituted-codomain row obtained by reading the codomain along $S$;
\item $T$ is the target typing row for the substituted body in the original context;
\item $L$ is the binder-boundary ledger recording that $M,A,D,S,Q,T$ are the only beta-case coordinates exported to subject reduction;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the body, argument, shifted-domain, substitution, codomain, target typing, ledger, provenance, and local naming coordinates;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which beta-case consumers read each coordinate;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, typing rows, binder-boundary rows, substitution routes, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\SubjectReductionUp$, and \NameCert{} rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BetaSubstitutionPreservationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no host substitution function, no unlisted conversion rule, no global subject-reduction theorem, no normalization argument, and no semantic equality of terms.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Beta-substitution preservation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:beta-substitution-preservation-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:beta-substitution-preservation-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\BetaSubstitutionPreservationUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
B=(M,A,D,S,Q,T,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
body typing through $M$, argument typing through $A$, shifted-domain exposure through $D$, substitution-route replay through $S$, substituted-codomain readback through $Q$, target typing through $T$, binder-ledger exhaustion through $L$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ route replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet $B$. The rows $M,A,D,S,Q,T,L,H,C,P,N$ list the beta-case body, argument, binder boundary, substitution route, codomain readback, target typing, ledger, transports, continuations, provenance, and local name.

The beta-specific obligations are finite. The body row $M$ is read only in the context extended by the shifted domain $D$, while the argument row $A$ is read only in the original context. The substitution route $S$ is the only displayed passage across that binder boundary.

The codomain and target rows are then read through $Q$ and $T$. The substituted codomain is not recomputed by a host substitution primitive; it is the row reached by replaying $S$ and then reading the target typing boundary.

Finally the rows $L,H,C,P,N$ exhaust the certificate surface. The ledger records that all beta-case coordinates have already been displayed, and the transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows give the only routes available to downstream consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Beta-substitution preservation ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:beta-substitution-preservation-ledger-nonescape}
Every consumer read of an accepted $\BetaSubstitutionPreservationUp$ packet factors through
$$
M,A,D,S,Q,T,L,H,C,P,N.
$$
It may inspect the body typing, argument typing, shifted-domain boundary, substitution route, substituted-codomain row, target typing row, binder-boundary ledger, componentwise transports, displayed continuations, provenance, and local \NameCert{} row. It cannot read host substitution, a hidden conversion relation, an unlisted weakening rule, global subject reduction, normalization, semantic term equality, or an erased typing derivation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:beta-substitution-preservation-namecert-obligations}. It identifies the accepted packet with the displayed rows and names those rows as the complete local certificate inventory.

The typing information is confined to $M,A,D,Q,T$. These rows record the body, the argument, the shifted-domain boundary, the substituted codomain, and the target typing judgment. No additional row can supply a host substitution result or an ambient conversion proof.

The route information is confined to $S,H,C$. The substitution route crosses the binder boundary, while the transport and continuation rows replay only the displayed coordinates.

The ledger and provenance rows $L,P,N$ close the public boundary. Since the carrier has no coordinate beyond the listed rows, downstream subject-reduction consumers cannot extract global subject reduction, normalization, semantic equality, or an erased derivation from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Beta-substitution preservation subject-reduction handoff]
\label{thm:beta-substitution-preservation-subject-reduction-handoff}
An accepted $\BetaSubstitutionPreservationUp$ packet supplies exactly the beta-case handoff row consumed by a $\SubjectReductionUp$ packet: the source body typing $M$, the argument typing $A$, the shifted-domain boundary $D$, the substitution route $S$, the substituted-codomain row $Q$, and the target typing row $T$, transported through $H$ and replayed through $C$ under the ledger, package, and local naming rows $L,P,N$.

The handoff is a finite beta-case certificate. It does not assert the closed subject-reduction theorem of \autoref{ch:metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction}, and it does not discharge the application-argument, lambda-domain, or Pi-domain obligations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier definition to expose the beta-case rows $M,A,D,S,Q,T$. These are precisely the rows needed for the direct beta branch: the body is typed under the shifted domain, the argument is typed in the original context, and the target typing row is reached by the displayed substitution route.

Apply \autoref{thm:beta-substitution-preservation-ledger-nonescape}. The ledger forces every read through the listed beta rows and prevents a consumer from replacing them with host substitution, hidden conversion, normalization, or a blanket preservation theorem.

It remains to package the handoff for $\SubjectReductionUp$. The rows $H,C,P,N$ provide transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming, so the receiving subject-reduction packet obtains only the finite beta-case handoff and none of the other three MetaCIC discharge obligations.
\end{proof}
