\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\InscriptionTraceClassifierUp$}

The $\InscriptionTraceClassifierUp$ packet realizes the source/name/route/check/consumer surface of the inscription-event vision in \autoref{ch:visions-inscription-event-as-concrete-instance}. It sits beside the full event packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}, the public residue ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscription-gap-ledger-namecert}, the trace reader of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionaudittrace-namecert}, and the route audit of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscription-route-audit-namecert}. These siblings expose event, ledger, trace, and route aspects; the present classifier isolates the finite packet a downstream consumer must display before an inscription event may be exported as public record data.

\begin{definition}[Inscription trace-classifier carrier]
\label{def:inscription-trace-classifier-carrier}
An $\InscriptionTraceClassifierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(S,M,R,K,U,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite source row from which the inscription trace is read;
\item $M$ is the public name row attached to the trace;
\item $R$ is the displayed route row from source to named trace;
\item $K$ is the accepted check row that admits the route as public record data;
\item $U$ is the downstream consumer row permitted to read the accepted trace;
\item $L$ is the refusal-ledger row for incomplete source, name, route, check, or consumer displays;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,M,R,K,U,L$ together with continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads source, name, route, check, consumer, and refusal-ledger rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, $\InscriptionGapLedgerUp$, $\InscriptionAuditTraceUp$, $\InscriptionRouteAuditUp$, $\InscriptionCommitmentSealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\InscriptionTraceClassifierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts exactly packets with these ten displayed rows. It has no coordinate for a private source, hidden name, unchecked route, off-packet checker, consumer right outside $U$, or inscription-event export that bypasses the refusal ledger $L$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Inscription trace-classifier obligations]
\label{thm:inscription-trace-classifier-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:inscription-trace-classifier-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\InscriptionTraceClassifierUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier exposure for
$$
T=(S,M,R,K,U,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
source visibility through $S$, public naming through $M$, route exactness through $R$, accepted-check admission through $K$, consumer accountability through $U$, refusal-ledger coverage through $L$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the displayed packet. The rows $S,M,R,K,U,L,H,C,P,N$ are the whole inventory available to a trace-classifier reader.

Read the content rows in order. The source row $S$ supplies the visible origin, the name row $M$ supplies the public handle, the route row $R$ supplies the trace path, the check row $K$ admits that path as public record data, and the consumer row $U$ names the permitted downstream reader.

The refusal row $L$ covers every incomplete display among those five rows. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ then transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since no carrier coordinate exports a hidden source, hidden name, unchecked route, off-packet checker, or extra consumer right, these rows exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\falsifiablePrediction{Any accepted trace-classifier consumer without displayed source, name, route, check, and consumer rows factors to the refusal ledger rather than exporting an inscription event.}

\begin{theorem}[Inscription trace-classifier falsifiable prediction]
\label{thm:inscription-trace-classifier-falsifiable-prediction}
For every accepted $\InscriptionTraceClassifierUp$ packet $T=(S,M,R,K,U,L,H,C,P,N)$, any purported trace-classifier consumer that omits one of the displayed source, name, route, check, or consumer rows factors through the refusal ledger $L$ rather than exporting an $\InscriptionEventUp$ row. Thus an inscription event is public only after $S,M,R,K,U$ are visible in the same packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:inscription-trace-classifier-carrier}. The only inscription-facing export path is the displayed chain through $S,M,R,K,U$.

Apply \autoref{thm:inscription-trace-classifier-obligations}. It assigns missing source, name, route, check, or consumer data to the refusal-ledger row $L$, while the accepted event-facing path requires the five displayed content rows.

The transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows preserve this same split. Therefore a consumer that omits any one of $S,M,R,K,U$ has no event-export coordinate left in the carrier and must be read through $L$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Inscription trace-classifier independence witness]
\label{thm:inscription-trace-classifier-independence-witness}
The $\InscriptionTraceClassifierUp$ carrier is not bijective, under NameCert-row preservation, with $\InscriptionEventUp$, $\InscriptionGapLedgerUp$, $\InscriptionAuditTraceUp$, $\InscriptionRouteAuditUp$, or $\InscriptionCommitmentSealUp$. It uniquely binds the displayed source, name, route, accepted check, downstream consumer, and refusal-ledger rows as one classifier packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the displayed coordinates. The present carrier contains the accepted classifier chain
$$
S \to M \to R \to K \to U
$$
together with the refusal-ledger row $L$ for incomplete presentations.

The inscription event sibling contains the full event packet rather than the classifier fallback. The gap ledger sibling records public residue, but not the simultaneous accepted source/name/route/check/consumer chain. The audit trace sibling records trace readback, and the route-audit sibling isolates the accepted route to a consumer, but neither supplies this packet's refusal fallback for missing classifier rows.

The commitment seal sibling records an accepted commitment and ledger rather than the full trace-classifier surface. Hence no bijection preserving the local NameCert rows can identify any of those carriers with $T=(S,M,R,K,U,L,H,C,P,N)$.
\end{proof}
