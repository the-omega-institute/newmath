The $\CellularOrbitSynchronizationUp$ packet realizes the cellular carrier alternative of \autoref{ch:visions-cellular-substrate-as-carrier-alternative} as a finite paired-orbit synchronization surface. It consumes the observation-boundary route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cellular-substrate-observation-boundary-namecert}, the classifier-boundary route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cellular-substrate-classifier-boundary-namecert}, the paired audit coupling of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-twinsubstrateauditcoupling-namecert}, and the twin-substrate bridge of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-twinsubstratebridge-namecert}. The packet records two local orbit replays together with the classifier tag row that authorizes them as one closed substrate read.

\begin{definition}[Cellular orbit synchronization carrier]
\label{def:cellular-orbit-synchronization-carrier}
A $\CellularOrbitSynchronizationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(O_0,O_1,G,B,Q,A,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O_0$ and $O_1$ are paired local orbit replay rows, each giving a finite transition table, initial row, time window, spatial window, and replay ledger.
\item $G$ is the classifier tag row that assigns both replayed windows to the same substrate observation family.
\item $B$ is the $\CellularSubstrateObservationBoundaryUp$ boundary row recording the manifest and accepted observation windows used by the paired replay.
\item $Q$ is the $\CellularSubstrateClassifierBoundaryUp$ row requiring the classifier tags to be displayed before either orbit read is exported.
\item $A$ is the $\TwinSubstrateAuditCouplingUp$ row that records paired audit use without collapsing the two replay rows into a shared host orbit.
\item $T$ is the $\TwinSubstrateBridgeUp$ row through which the paired read may be inspected by a bridge-facing consumer.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two orbit replays, classifier tag, observation boundary, classifier boundary, audit coupling, twin-substrate bridge, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the paired local orbits through $G,B,Q,A,T,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CellularOrbitSynchronizationUp$, $\CellularSubstrateObservationBoundaryUp$, $\CellularSubstrateClassifierBoundaryUp$, $\TwinSubstrateAuditCouplingUp$, $\TwinSubstrateBridgeUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CellularOrbitSynchronizationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an unclassified orbit read, a detached host evaluator, an all-time induction principle, quotient stream equality, or an identification of the two substrate roles.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CellularOrbitSynchronization NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cellular-orbit-synchronization-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CellularOrbitSynchronizationUp$ carrier $S=(O_0,O_1,G,B,Q,A,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CellularOrbitSynchronizationUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the paired orbit replays, classifier tag row, observation boundary, classifier boundary, audit coupling, twin-substrate bridge, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming. Every synchronized substrate read factors through
$$
O_0,O_1 \longmapsto G \longmapsto B,Q \longmapsto A,T \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
No synchronized orbit obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cellular-orbit-synchronization-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the paired local orbit rows, classifier tag row, observation boundary, classifier boundary, audit coupling, bridge row, and structural rows.

The classifier row is read before the boundary rows. Thus a pair of replay ledgers $O_0,O_1$ becomes a synchronized substrate read only when $G$ names the inspected pattern family and the rows $B,Q$ bind that tag to the observation and classifier boundaries.

The audit and bridge rows preserve the paired reading without identifying the two replays. The row $A$ supplies paired audit coupling, $T$ supplies bridge-facing inspection, and $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only that finite packet. Therefore unclassified orbit reads, host evaluators, all-time induction, quotient streams, and substrate-role identifications cannot become NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CellularOrbitSynchronization classifier-locked handoff]
\label{thm:cellular-orbit-synchronization-classifier-locked-handoff}
Every synchronized orbit read accepted by a $\CellularOrbitSynchronizationUp$ packet displays the classifier tag row $G$ before either paired orbit replay is exported through the observation boundary, classifier boundary, audit coupling, or twin-substrate bridge. A Lean follow-up for this carrier must supply $\Nontrivial$ and $\FieldFaithful$ instances in \texttt{lean4/BEDC/Derived/CellularOrbitSynchronizationUp/TasteGate.lean}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The paired orbit rows $O_0,O_1$ are present, but they are not exported directly; they are routed through the classifier tag row $G$.

Use the observation and classifier boundaries. The observation-boundary row $B$ fixes the manifest and local windows, while the classifier-boundary row $Q$ requires the pattern tag to be displayed for each observed window. Hence an orbit replay without $G$ has no accepted route to a synchronized read.

The audit coupling and bridge rows are downstream. They consume the already tagged boundary packet, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite route. Thus the classifier-locked handoff is the only exported synchronization surface.
\end{proof}
