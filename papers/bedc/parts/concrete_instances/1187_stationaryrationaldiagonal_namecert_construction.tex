\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-stationaryrationaldiagonal-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp$ interface names the finite packet obtained
from a stationary rational regular Cauchy input and its diagonal representative.
It sits at the Real and Completion boundary: the source is a $\RatUp$ row read
as a constant $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ object, while the consumer sees
only the associated $\RealUp$ seal surface. The packet carries no quotient of
regular streams, host real equality, ambient completeness principle, or hidden
limit selection.

\begin{definition}[Stationary rational diagonal carrier]
\label{def:stationary-rational-diagonal-carrier}
A $\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ row
$$
S=(q,C,R,D,E,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $q$ is the $\RatUp$ source row;
\item $C$ is the constant $\StreamNameUp$ schedule generated by $q$;
\item $R$ is the stationary $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row whose inspected windows
all return $q$;
\item $D$ is the diagonal selector row that always selects the stationary
member and the requested regular-sequence window;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal ledger for the constant rational embedding;
\item $H$ records the displayed $\hsame$ transports for the rational source,
stream schedule, regular readback, diagonal selector, and real seal;
\item $K$ records the visible $\Cont$ routes by which consumers reach the
selected constant windows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance naming the $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and
$\NameCert$ dependency rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp}$ provenance
row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only by the displayed $\RatUp$ seed
classifier, the constant $\StreamNameUp$ readback, the stationary
$\RegSeqRatUp$ window row, and the $\RealUp$ seal-facing route, transported
through the listed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary rational diagonal NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:stationary-rational-diagonal-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp}$ surface consists exactly
of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the finite packet of
\autoref{def:stationary-rational-diagonal-carrier};
\item classifier: comparison of two accepted packets through their displayed
$\RatUp$ seed classifier and the induced constant $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows;
\item stability: componentwise $\hsame$ transport and visible $\Cont$ routing
preserve the stationary seed, every constant window, the diagonal selector, and
the real seal ledger;
\item exactness/readback: every consumer read factors through the same
stationary rational seed and the same constant regular-rational window before
it reaches the $\RealUp$ seal;
\item ledger: the $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert$ coordinates expose only $\RatUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, and local certificate rows.
\end{enumerate}
No row of the surface is a quotient, a choice principle, or a host real
equality.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StationaryRationalDiagonalUp.StationaryRationalDiagonalCarrier\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:stationary-rational-diagonal-carrier}. The listed coordinates
already name the rational seed, constant stream, stationary regular readback,
diagonal selector, real seal, transports, continuation routes, package
provenance, and local ledger.

For the classifier row, compare two packets only by the seed classifier and its
constant readbacks. The $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ coordinates are
stationary, so every selected window reads the same rational row before the
$\RealUp$ seal is consulted.

Stability follows by transporting each visible coordinate through its stored
$\hsame$ row and using the displayed $\Cont$ routes to reach the same selected
window after transport. The exactness row is then forced, because the real seal
has no source except the stationary rational readback.

The ledger is exhausted by $P$ and $N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for
quotient streams, host real equality, or a hidden selected limit, none of those
objects can be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary rational diagonal Real handoff]
\label{thm:stationary-rational-diagonal-real-handoff}
Every accepted $\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp$ packet determines a concrete
$\RealUp$ consumer surface for the stationary rational diagonal representative.
The consumer route reads the $\RatUp$ seed through the constant
$\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the stationary $\RegSeqRatUp$ row, the diagonal
selector, and the displayed $\RealUp$ seal ledger, with all replacements
accounted for by visible $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted carrier and read its constant stream coordinate. Every
requested observation reaches the same $\RatUp$ seed through the displayed
$\Cont$ route.

The stationary $\RegSeqRatUp$ coordinate repackages those constant observations
as the regular-rational readback used by the diagonal selector. Since the
selector always chooses the stationary member, the selected window and the
regular readback stay on the listed finite rows.

Finally read the $\RealUp$ seal ledger. It is attached to the same stationary
readback and is transported only through the displayed $\hsame$ rows. Repacking
these coordinates gives the real-facing handoff, and the carrier contains no
additional endpoint object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary rational diagonal consumer boundary]
\label{thm:stationary-rational-diagonal-consumer-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer of an accepted
$\StationaryRationalDiagonalUp$ packet factors through the finite rows listed
in \autoref{def:stationary-rational-diagonal-carrier}. In particular, the
consumer may use the $\RatUp$ seed, constant $\StreamNameUp$ schedule,
stationary $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, diagonal selector, $\RealUp$ seal ledger,
visible $\hsame$ transports, visible $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and
local $\NameCert$ row, and nothing outside that finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the obligation surface of
\autoref{thm:stationary-rational-diagonal-namecert-obligation-surface} to
place the carrier, classifier, stability, readback, and ledger rows on one
local certificate surface.

Next apply the handoff of
\autoref{thm:stationary-rational-diagonal-real-handoff}. It routes every
real-facing read through the stationary seed, the constant stream schedule, the
regular-rational readback, the diagonal selector, and the real seal ledger.

It remains to delimit completion-facing consumers. Such a consumer reaches the
packet only through the same $\RealUp$ seal-facing route and the same displayed
provenance rows. Therefore the consumer boundary is exactly the finite packet
surface stated in the theorem, with no coordinate for quotient equality,
choice, or host real equality.
\end{proof}
