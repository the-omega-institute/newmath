\begin{definition}[Registry-change continuity carrier]
\label{def:registry-change-continuity-carrier}
A $\RegistryChangeContinuityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(T,A,D,S,R,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the registry target row, naming the theorem, definition, writing item, formal target, or package entry whose reading changes.
\item $A$ is the active-reading row after the change, imported through the current-reading surface of $\ActiveReadingGateUp$.
\item $D$ is the displaced-reading row, retained as blocked audit data rather than as a second active reading.
\item $S$ is the status-bound row, recording the closure-status and formal-status boundary read from $\ClosureStatusReferenceUp$.
\item $R$ is the cannot-claim refusal row, recording the public reason that blocks the displaced row from export as current.
\item $B$ is the blocked-reading row, binding $D$ to $R$ and recording that the displaced row remains visible only as audit data.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for target, active reading, displaced reading, status bound, refusal, blocking, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from $T$ through $A,S,R,B$ to the export-readable registry route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegistryChangeContinuityUp$, $\ActiveReadingGateUp$, $\ClosureStatusReferenceUp$, $\RegistryConsistencyAuditUp$, $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, $\PhilosophyCannotClaimRegistryUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegistryChangeContinuityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for exporting $D$ as a second active reading, changing the status boundary outside $S$, citing a refusal outside $R$, replaying the change outside $C$, or naming the packet outside the finite row $K=(T,A,D,S,R,B,H,C,P,N)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Registry-change continuity obligations]
\label{thm:registry-change-continuity-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:registry-change-continuity-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RegistryChangeContinuityUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $K=(T,A,D,S,R,B,H,C,P,N)$; target admission through $T$; current-reading admission through $A$; displaced-reading exposure through $D$; status-bound exactness through $S$; refusal and blocked-reading compatibility through $R$ and $B$; registry-consistency and claim-layer readability through $\RegistryConsistencyAuditUp$ and $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$; and transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:registry-change-continuity-carrier}. The rows $T,A,D,S,R,B,H,C,P,N$ are the only coordinates available to the local certificate, so every accepted change-continuity read must pass through those rows.

The registry target is fixed by $T$. The active row $A$ is the single current reading, while $D$ is retained only as displaced audit data. The status row $S$ bounds the change by the closure-status and formal-status surface, so the carrier cannot silently change the public grade of the target.

The refusal and blocking rows supply the non-export clause. Row $R$ records the cannot-claim reason, and row $B$ binds that reason to the displaced row. Thus the carrier exposes why $D$ remains readable as history while refusing to make it a second current claim.

Rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite packet under transport, replay, provenance, and naming. They add no coordinate for a hidden active row, so the displayed obligations exhaust the local $\NameCert_{\RegistryChangeContinuityUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Registry-change continuity active-reading refusal]
\label{thm:registry-change-continuity-active-reading-refusal}
Every accepted $\RegistryChangeContinuityUp$ packet exports the target row $T$ through the active row $A$, the status-bound row $S$, the refusal row $R$, and the blocked-reading row $B$. A packet that exports both $A$ and $D$ as active readings for the same target is rejected by $\RegistryChangeContinuityUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet
$$
K=(T,A,D,S,R,B,H,C,P,N).
$$
By \autoref{thm:registry-change-continuity-obligations}, the export-readable route contains $T,A,S,R,B$ as displayed rows.

The active-reading gate supplies uniqueness for $A$, and the closure-status reference supplies the status bound $S$. The displaced row $D$ is still present, but it is tied to the cannot-claim row $R$ through the blocked-reading row $B$.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve that same route. Since no coordinate turns $D$ into an additional active row, any packet that exports both $A$ and $D$ as active readings for the same target leaves the displayed carrier and is refused by $B$.
\end{proof}
