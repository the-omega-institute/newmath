It sits after the functorial action of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-functoriality-namecert}, the uniform completion interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletionfunctor-namecert}, and the effective completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-effective-completion-namecert}. \begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionLift carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-lift-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionLiftUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(S,T,F,U,E,K,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite-source metric row, including the displayed source observations, Cauchy windows, and admissible tolerance requests before any completed consumer is read.
\item $T$ is the target completion-consumer row, exposing the completion-facing object only after its uniform structure and accepted consumer interface have been displayed.
\item $F$ is the uniformly continuous source-map row from the finite source route into the target-facing precompletion data.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionFunctorUp$ row that records the uniform completion surface used by the lift.
\item $E$ is the $\EffectiveCompletionUp$ row that supplies the finite Cauchy-realizer, stream-window, regular-rational, dyadic-tolerance, and real-seal route consumed by the lift.
\item $K$ is the $\CauchyCompletionFunctorialityUp$ row that packages the map action on completion-facing data before the universal consumer reads the lifted map.
\item $R$ is the universal-route row, recording that every allowed completion consumer reads the lifted map only through the displayed $S,T,F,U,E,K$ chain.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source row, target row, source-map row, uniform completion row, effective completion row, functoriality row, universal route, replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
S,T,F \longmapsto U,E \longmapsto K \longmapsto R .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionLiftUp$, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorialityUp$, $\UniformCompletionFunctorUp$, $\EffectiveCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionLiftUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,T,F,U,E,K,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected limits, representative choice, host function equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionLift NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-lift-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionLiftUp$ carrier
$$
L=(S,T,F,U,E,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionLiftUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite-source row, target completion-consumer row, uniformly continuous source-map row, uniform completion row, effective completion row, functoriality row, universal-route row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item finite-source visibility, requiring every lifted-map read to expose the source observations, Cauchy windows, and tolerance requests in $S$ before completion-facing data is inspected;
\item uniform continuity handoff, requiring the source-map row $F$ to be consumed through the uniform completion row $U$ before any completed-map read is named;
\item effective completion exactness, requiring the row $E$ to supply the stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, and real seal used by the lift;
\item functorial action, requiring the row $K$ to bind the same $S,T,F,U,E$ route as the completion-facing map action;
\item universal consumer routing, requiring $R$ to expose every allowed completion consumer only after the displayed functorial route is available;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve, replay, source, and name only the listed rows and to export no quotient completion equality, selected limit, representative choice, host equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No Cauchy completion lift obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-lift-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the finite source, target consumer, source-map, uniform completion, effective completion, functoriality, universal-route, and structural rows.

Finite-source visibility is read from $S$. Since the source observations, Cauchy windows, and tolerance requests occur before any completion-facing row, the lifted map cannot be inspected from an unrecorded source object.

The uniform-continuity handoff is read from $F$ and $U$. The source map enters the lift only as uniformly continuous finite data, and the completion-facing interface is the uniform completion row rather than a host function on completed objects.

Effective completion exactness is read from $E$. The lift uses the displayed stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, and real seal already admitted by the effective completion route.

Functorial action and universal consumer routing are read from $K$ and $R$. The row $K$ packages the same source, target, source-map, uniform completion, and effective completion route, while $R$ makes each allowed consumer read through that package.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ add no analytic coordinate. They transport, replay, source, and name the displayed route, so the excluded quotient, selected-limit, representative-choice, host-equality, countable-choice, and ambient-completeness principles have no row from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionLift universal route]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-lift-universal-route}
Let
$$
L=(S,T,F,U,E,K,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionLiftUp$ carrier. Every completion consumer of the lifted source-map data factors through the finite route
$$
S,T,F \longmapsto U,E \longmapsto K \longmapsto R \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The route is a universal lift surface for uniformly continuous finite-source maps across Cauchy completion consumers. It is not quotient completion equality, not selected-limit data, not representative choice, not host function equality, not countable choice, and not an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a completion consumer of the lifted data. Projection gives the source row $S$, target row $T$, and uniformly continuous source-map row $F$.

The lift then enters the uniform and effective completion rows. The row $U$ supplies the uniform completion interface, while $E$ supplies the finite Cauchy-realizer route through stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance data, and the real seal.

The functoriality row $K$ packages the map action on completion-facing data. It reads the same $S,T,F,U,E$ coordinates, so a consumer cannot replace the route by a selected representative or by host function equality.

The universal route row $R$ is downstream of $K$. Thus every allowed consumer reads the lifted map only after the uniform, effective, and functorial rows have been displayed.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming close the route. Since $H,C,P,N$ preserve only the listed rows, the packet exports no quotient completion equality, selected limit, representative choice, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}
