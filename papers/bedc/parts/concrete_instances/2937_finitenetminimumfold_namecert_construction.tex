\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$}

The $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$ interface packages the finite rational lower-bound fold used by compact-uniform-modulus consumers. It reads a nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of positive $\RatUp$ half-radius ledgers, folds them through the $\TotalOrderUp$ finite-minimum route, and returns a single precision history whose classifier-stable lower-bound certificate is visible to the downstream $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff.

\begin{definition}[FiniteNetMinimumFold carrier]
\label{def:finite-net-minimum-fold-carrier}
A $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(P,R,M,L,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is a nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row indexing the compact finite-net probes;
\item $R$ assigns to each bundled probe a positive $\RatUp$ half-radius ledger;
\item $M$ is the primitive-recursive accumulator history produced by folding the bundled radius ledgers through the $\TotalOrderUp$ finite-minimum selector;
\item $L$ stores the lower-bound certificates saying that $M$ is below every radius ledger in $R$ after the displayed classifier transports;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for probe membership, radius histories, accumulator rows, and lower-bound rows;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes for bundle recursion, rational order readback, accumulator comparison, and downstream precision handoff;
\item $Q$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\RatUp$, $\TotalOrderUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only after transporting the bundle spine, each positive rational half-radius row, the accumulator history, and the lower-bound certificates to a common finite-net surface. The carrier has no coordinate for an external minimum operator, a selected probe representative, host-order trichotomy, or a modulus chosen outside the displayed recursion.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteNetMinimumFold NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-net-minimum-fold-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-net-minimum-fold-carrier} consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $F=(P,R,M,L,H,C,Q,N)$;
\item classifier transport of bundle membership, positive rational radius rows, accumulator rows, and lower-bound certificates to a common finite-net surface;
\item primitive-recursion stability for the fold over the nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine;
\item rational lower-bound exactness for every bundled radius ledger stored in $R$;
\item ledger rows for rational order readback, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming certificate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The displayed rows contain precisely the nonempty bundle spine, its positive rational half-radius ledgers, the fold accumulator, the lower-bound certificates, transports, continuations, provenance, and local name.

Use the $\TotalOrderUp$ finite-minimum selector at each recursive step over $P$. The accumulator is initialized from the nonempty bundle data and then compared with the next bundled half-radius ledger, so each recursive branch produces either the previous lower-bound row transported forward or the newly read rational row.

The componentwise transports in $H$ and the continuation routes in $C$ preserve bundle membership, rational positivity, and order readback. Therefore the five obligations exhaust the local certificate surface, and no external selector or host-order principle is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteNetMinimumFold lower-bound certificate]
\label{thm:finite-net-minimum-fold-lower-bound-certificate}
For an accepted $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$ carrier
$$
F=(P,R,M,L,H,C,Q,N),
$$
the accumulator history $M$ is certified below every positive $\RatUp$ half-radius ledger assigned by $R$ to a bundled probe of $P$. The certificate is the finite row $L$, transported through $H$ and read by $C$; it is not a host-level minimum and does not depend on a selected enumeration outside the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ recursion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row $P$ and the radius assignment row $R$. Primitive recursion over $P$ produces the accumulator $M$ by repeatedly applying the rational finite-minimum selector to the current accumulator and the next radius ledger.

At the base probe, the lower-bound row is the positive rational radius ledger itself. At each successor probe, the $\TotalOrderUp$ readback compares the current accumulator with the next radius ledger and records the selected lower-bound branch in $L$.

Transport stability follows from $H$, and the consumer-facing read is through $C$. Since the carrier contains no external enumeration, no selected probe representative, and no host-order trichotomy, the only exported lower-bound evidence is the finite certificate row $L$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteNetMinimumFold positive precision obligation]
\label{thm:finite-net-minimum-fold-positive-precision-obligation}

$$
F=(P,R,M,L,H,C,Q,N),
$$
the accumulator $M$ is not only certified below every bundled half-radius ledger by $L$; it is itself a positive $\RatUp$ precision history transported through $H$ and read by $C$. This positivity and admissibility are part of the displayed finite fold packet, not consequences of a host minimum, host-order trichotomy, or an external rational selector.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row $P$. Its base probe supplies an initial bundled radius, and the radius assignment row $R$ records that radius as a positive $\RatUp$ half-radius ledger.

Run the primitive-recursive fold over the remaining probes. At each recursive step, the $\TotalOrderUp$ selector compares the current accumulator with the next bundled positive $\RatUp$ row and chooses one of those two already positive rows.

The componentwise transports in $H$ preserve the accumulator as a positive $\RatUp$ history under classifier movement, and the routes in $C$ replay the same row to downstream consumers. Thus $M$ is an admissible positive precision history as well as a lower-bound certificate.

Because the carrier has no coordinate for a host minimum or host-order trichotomy, the positivity of $M$ is obtained only by this finite row selection and transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteNetMinimumFold nonempty probe exhaustion]
\label{thm:finitenetminimumfold-nonempty-probe-exhaustion}
For an accepted $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$ carrier
$$
F=(P,R,M,L,H,C,Q,N),
$$
every probe consumed by the accumulator history $M$ is a member of the nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row $P$, and every member of $P$ is consumed exactly once by the primitive-recursive fold that builds $M$. The exhaustion certificate is internal to the displayed bundle recursion, radius ledger $R$, transport row $H$, continuation row $C$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row $P$. Its constructors supply the initial probe for the fold and the finite successor spine for the remaining probes.

Run primitive recursion over that spine. At the base probe, the accumulator reads the corresponding positive $\RatUp$ half-radius ledger from $R$ and records the first lower-bound row in $L$.

At each successor probe, the recursion extends the accumulator by exactly the next bundled radius ledger. The $\TotalOrderUp$ finite-minimum route compares the current accumulator with that ledger and records the selected branch without introducing an unbundled probe.

The transports in $H$ and routes in $C$ replay the same membership and radius rows under classifier movement. Thus the fold consumes precisely the bundle $P$, with no missing bundled probe and no probe outside $P$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteNetMinimumFold order-selector transport]
\label{thm:finitenetminimumfold-order-selector-transport}
For an accepted $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$ carrier $F=(P,R,M,L,H,C,Q,N)$, the $\TotalOrderUp$ selector used at each fold step is stable under the displayed componentwise $\hsame$ transports. If two transported presentations of the same bundled half-radius ledgers in $R$ are compared through the routes in $C$, the selected accumulator branch and the lower-bound certificate row in $L$ transport to the same $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$ classifier surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the fold step determined by a bundled probe in $P$. The step compares the current accumulator row with the next positive $\RatUp$ half-radius ledger from $R$.

Transport both compared rows through the corresponding component of $H$. The rational comparison is then read through the displayed $\TotalOrderUp$ route in $C$, so the selector sees the same ordered pair on the transported surface.

If the selector keeps the accumulator branch, the transported lower-bound row is the previous certificate transported forward. If the selector chooses the new radius branch, the transported lower-bound row is the radius ledger transported through the same component of $H$.

In both cases the selected branch and its certificate remain inside $M$ and $L$. Therefore order selection is stable under the displayed classifier transport and exports no host-order trichotomy.
\end{proof}
