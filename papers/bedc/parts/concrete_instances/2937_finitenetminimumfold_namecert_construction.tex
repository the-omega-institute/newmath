The $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$ interface packages the finite rational lower-bound fold used by compact-uniform-modulus consumers. It reads a nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of positive $\RatUp$ half-radius ledgers, folds them through the $\TotalOrderUp$ finite-minimum route, and returns a single precision history whose classifier-stable lower-bound certificate is visible to the downstream $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff.

The fold is the finite rational lower-bound row consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactuniformcontinuity-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}. Those consumers read only the displayed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\RatUp$ half-radius, $\TotalOrderUp$ selector, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, so the handoff exposes no host minimum, open-cover selector, or external modulus coordinate.

\begin{definition}[FiniteNetMinimumFold carrier]
\label{def:finite-net-minimum-fold-carrier}
A $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(P,R,M,L,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is a nonempty $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row indexing the compact finite-net probes;
\item $R$ assigns to each bundled probe a positive $\RatUp$ half-radius ledger;
\item $M$ is the primitive-recursive accumulator history produced by folding the bundled radius ledgers through the $\TotalOrderUp$ finite-minimum selector;
\item $L$ stores the lower-bound certificates saying that $M$ is below every radius ledger in $R$ after the displayed classifier transports;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for probe membership, radius histories, accumulator rows, and lower-bound rows;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes for bundle recursion, rational order readback, accumulator comparison, and downstream precision handoff;
\item $Q$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\RatUp$, $\TotalOrderUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only after transporting the bundle spine, each positive rational half-radius row, the accumulator history, and the lower-bound certificates to a common finite-net surface. The carrier has no coordinate for an external minimum operator, a selected probe representative, host-order trichotomy, or a modulus chosen outside the displayed recursion.
\end{definition}
