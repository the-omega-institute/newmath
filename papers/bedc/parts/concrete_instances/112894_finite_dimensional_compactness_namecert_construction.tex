Following Bishop and Bridges and standard finite-dimensional normed-space compactness, the $\FiniteDimensionalCompactnessUp$ packet records the finite-net route by which a displayed bounded closed coordinate window is consumed as compact data. It sits after the vector-space surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}, the metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the norm-comparison route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-dimensional-norm-equivalence-namecert}, the interval compactness packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-bounded-interval-compact-namecert}, and the finite-net Heine--Borel route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-heine-borel-finite-net-namecert}. The packet is a constructive finite-net NameCert surface for finite-dimensional bounded windows; it is not a general Heine--Borel theorem, sequential compactness principle, host basis selector, quotient vector equality, or choice-based compactness object.

\begin{definition}[Finite-dimensional compactness carrier]
\label{def:finite-dimensional-compactness-carrier}
A $\FiniteDimensionalCompactnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(V,M,R,E,N,I,F,W,S,H,C,P,Q)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $V$ is the $\VecSpaceUp$ row exposing vector addition, zero, inverse, scalar action, and vector classification only through displayed finite carrier data.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ row induced by a displayed norm or coordinate distance on the same finite vector source.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row used for coordinate bounds, metric radii, finite sums, and comparison constants.
\item $E$ is the $\FiniteDimensionalNormEquivalenceUp$ row that keeps all norm and coordinate comparisons on one finite coordinate window.
\item $N$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp$ interval compactness row for each coordinate bound.
\item $I$ is the product interval-window row, listing the finitely many coordinate intervals and their endpoint seals.
\item $F$ is the finite-net row read through $\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp$, recording displayed centers, mesh radius, coverage witnesses, and radius comparisons for the coordinate window.
\item $W$ is the bounded-window row tying every accepted vector read to the listed coordinate bounds before any compactness-facing consumer sees the metric row.
\item $S$ is the exactness row saying that a finite-net membership read reconstructs a vector-window read through $V,M,R,E,N,I,F,W$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the vector, metric, real, norm-equivalence, interval-compactness, finite-net, bounded-window, exactness, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite-dimensional compactness request through $V,M,R,E,N,I,F,W,S,H,P,Q$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteDimensionalCompactnessUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\FiniteDimensionalNormEquivalenceUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp$, $\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $Q$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteDimensionalCompactnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed tuple $V,M,R,E,N,I,F,W,S,H,C,P,Q$. It has no coordinate for arbitrary open covers, host bases, quotient vector equality, selected subsequences, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-dimensional compactness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-dimensional-compactness-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteDimensionalCompactnessUp$ carrier
$$
K=(V,M,R,E,N,I,F,W,S,H,C,P,Q),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteDimensionalCompactnessUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed vector-space, metric, real-seal, finite-dimensional norm-equivalence, coordinate interval-compactness, interval-window, finite-net, bounded-window, exactness, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. Every compactness-facing read must first expose the coordinate window $I$, the finite-net row $F$, and the bounded-window row $W$; every metric or norm comparison must factor through $M,R,E$; and every exported finite-net membership read must be repacked by $S$ before it reaches the local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-dimensional-compactness-carrier}. The tuple displays exactly the rows $V,M,R,E,N,I,F,W,S,H,C,P,Q$, so the classifier has no access to compactness data outside that packet.

The finite-dimensional source is read first. The vector row $V$ and metric row $M$ expose the finite carrier and distance surface, while the real row $R$ supplies the coordinate bounds, radii, sums, and comparison constants.

The compactness route is finite-net based. The norm-equivalence row $E$ keeps all coordinate and norm comparisons on the same finite window, the interval compactness row $N$ supplies compactness for each coordinate interval, and $I,F,W$ list the coordinate intervals, finite centers, coverage witnesses, and bounded-window admission data.

The exactness row $S$ is the only route from finite-net membership back to vector-window membership. The structural rows $H,C,P,Q$ transport, replay, source, and name that same route. Since the carrier has no row for arbitrary open covers, host basis selection, quotient vector equality, selected subsequences, countable choice, or ambient completeness, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-dimensional compactness finite-net readback]
\label{thm:finite-dimensional-compactness-finite-net-readback}
Every $\RealUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, or $\VecSpaceUp$ consumer of an accepted $\FiniteDimensionalCompactnessUp$ packet that requests compactness data receives only the finite route
$$
\VecSpaceUp,\MetricSpaceUp,\RealUp
\longmapsto
\FiniteDimensionalNormEquivalenceUp
\longmapsto
\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp
\longmapsto
\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp
\longmapsto
\NameCert_{\FiniteDimensionalCompactnessUp}
$$
through $V,M,R,E,N,I,F,W,S,H,C,P,Q$. The readback exports finite coordinate bounds, displayed interval compactness rows, finite-net centers, mesh-radius comparisons, bounded-window admission, exactness, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming only; it exports no general Heine--Borel theorem, open-cover selector, sequential compactness branch, quotient vector equality, host basis choice, countable choice, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:finite-dimensional-compactness-namecert-obligations}. A compactness-facing request opens the displayed vector, metric, and real rows before any coordinate or finite-net row is consumed.

Next read the finite-dimensional comparison data. The norm-equivalence row $E$ ties the coordinate and metric comparisons to one finite window, and the interval compactness row $N$ supplies compactness only for the listed coordinate intervals.

The finite-net handoff is $I,F,W,S$. The interval-window row lists the coordinate bounds, the finite-net row lists centers and coverage witnesses, the bounded-window row keeps vector reads inside those bounds, and the exactness row repacks finite-net membership as vector-window membership.

Finally $H,C,P,Q$ preserve the same route for each consumer. Because no excluded object is present in the carrier, the consumer receives exactly the finite-net readback displayed in the statement.
\end{proof}
