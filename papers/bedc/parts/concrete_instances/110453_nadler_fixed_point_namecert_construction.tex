The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,K,F,V,D,L,O,R,H,C,P,Q$. It has no coordinate for a selected representative of each compact value, a countable choice operator, quotient-completion equality, a host hyperspace object, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{theorem}[Nadler fixed-point NameCert obligations]\label{thm:nadler-fixed-point-namecert-obligations}

\item carrier admission for the metric source $X$, complete-metric service $K$, multivalued map row $F$, compact value rows $V$, Hausdorff distance rows $D$, contraction ledger $L$, finite approximation ledger $O$, completion handoff $R$, transport $H$, replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming $Q$;
\item classifier transport, requiring accepted packet comparisons to preserve the same source classifier, compact value names, Hausdorff finite-net witnesses, contraction budget, approximation ledger, and completion handoff componentwise through $H$;
\item stability, requiring the Hausdorff contraction comparison in $D,L$ to survive replacement of displayed source points and compact value names by classified equals before the next approximation row in $O$ is admitted;
\item exactness and ledger nonescape, requiring the route from finite approximation rows to the complete-metric handoff $R$ to pass through $K$ and to export no selected representatives, no countable choice, no quotient completion, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate;
\item NameCert provenance, requiring $C,P,Q$ to replay, source, and name only the displayed route over the CompleteMetric, HausdorffMetric, BanachFixedPoint, PicardIteration, and CompactMetric sibling surfaces cited above.
\end{enumerate}
No Nadler fixed-point obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The classifier is therefore componentwise. Transport through $H$ may replace displayed source points, compact value names, Hausdorff witnesses, and ledger rows only by the recorded $\hsame$ routes; replay through $C$ preserves the same finite path from compact-value inspection to the completion handoff.

The stability obligation is the multivalued analogue of the contraction step. It reads the Hausdorff comparison from $D,L$ and admits the next row in $O$ only through the displayed value names and finite-net witnesses, not through a hidden branch selector.

Finally $R,P,Q$ repack the finite route as the local naming surface. Since the carrier has no coordinate for selected representatives, countable choice, quotient-completion equality, a host hyperspace object, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, the five listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
