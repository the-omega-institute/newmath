\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedSubspaceCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closed-subspace-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges and Kelley-style metric completion, the $\ClosedSubspaceCompletionUp$ packet records the finite certificate by which a closed metric subspace inherits the completion-limit route carried by its ambient metric completion. It sits after the subspace admission surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-subspace-namecert}, the Bishop regular Cauchy completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert}, the completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the complete-metric limit row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the separated metric boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readbacks of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter isolates the displayed route from an admitted subspace Cauchy packet to a retained subspace limit, without quotienting the subset or selecting limits by choice.

\begin{definition}[ClosedSubspaceCompletion carrier]
\label{def:closed-subspace-completion-carrier}
A $\ClosedSubspaceCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(X,A,K,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the parent $\MetricCompletionUp$ row, including the ambient separated metric carrier, the regular Cauchy source data, and the completion handoff route used by downstream consumers.
\item $A$ is the $\MetricSubspaceUp$ admission row naming the displayed subspace predicate, the inherited metric restriction, and the finite membership ledger for the source Cauchy packet.
\item $K$ is the closedness-under-limit row: whenever a Cauchy source is admitted by $A$ and its ambient completion route in $X$ reaches a displayed limit, the same route carries the subspace-membership witness to that limit.
\item $L$ is the inherited $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit row obtained by replaying the ambient completion route through $K$ and then reading the result as a subspace limit rather than as a detached ambient point.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the parent completion row, subspace admission row, closedness row, inherited limit row, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for every consumer that inspects subspace admission, ambient completion data, closedness-under-limit, or the inherited complete-metric row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClosedSubspaceCompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\MetricSubspaceUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedSubspaceCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,A,K,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient subset equality, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedSubspaceCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-subspace-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ClosedSubspaceCompletionUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(X,A,K,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedSubspaceCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the parent metric-completion row, metric-subspace admission row, closedness-under-limit row, inherited complete-metric limit row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item source compatibility, requiring the Cauchy source read by $X$ to be admitted through the same subspace row $A$ before any limit row is inspected;
\item closedness retention, requiring $K$ to carry the subspace-membership witness along the ambient completion route rather than through quotient subset equality or a selected representative;
\item inherited-limit exactness, requiring $L$ to be obtained by replaying the ambient $\CompleteMetricUp$ row through $K$ and not by invoking ambient completeness for the subspace;
\item classifier stability, requiring transported packets to compare only through the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $P,N$ to source and name no selected limit, countable-choice selector, open-cover compactness proof, ambient completeness principle, $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:closed-subspace-completion-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly one parent completion row, one subspace admission row, one closedness-under-limit row, one inherited limit row, and the structural rows that transport, replay, source, and name them.

Source compatibility is read from $X$ and $A$. The Cauchy source used by the parent completion route is the same source whose finite membership ledger is exposed by the subspace admission row, so a consumer cannot replace it by a detached subspace quotient.

Closedness retention is the role of $K$. It transports the displayed membership witness along the ambient completion route and returns a limit that is still read through the subspace row.

Inherited-limit exactness is then projection through $L$. The complete-metric limit row is obtained after $K$ has replayed the ambient route, so the subspace receives a retained limit rather than a new ambient-completeness principle.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name only this displayed route. Since the carrier contains no excluded coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedSubspaceCompletion limit retention]
\label{thm:closed-subspace-completion-limit-retention}
Let $Q=(X,A,K,L,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\ClosedSubspaceCompletionUp$ carrier. Every subspace Cauchy source admitted by $A$ and completed through the parent row $X$ reaches its inherited limit only through the retained route
$$
X,A \longmapsto K \longmapsto L .
$$
Thus the exported limit remains a subspace limit named by the displayed closedness row, and no selected ambient limit, quotient subset equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, or $\RealUp$ completeness is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Cauchy source admitted by $A$. By the carrier definition, the only completion data available for that source is the parent row $X$, so the source first follows the displayed metric-completion route.

The closedness row $K$ is then forced. It is the only coordinate that can turn the ambient limit reached through $X$ into a subspace-membership witness, and it does so by replaying the same finite source route.

The inherited limit row $L$ is downstream of $K$. Consequently the complete-metric consumer receives a limit only after the closedness-under-limit witness has retained subspace membership.

The structural rows preserve this order under transport and replay. Since no excluded limit-selection or ambient-completeness coordinate is present, every exported subspace limit factors through $X,A,K,L$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedSubspaceCompletion nonescape]
\label{thm:closed-subspace-completion-nonescape-boundary}
For an accepted $\ClosedSubspaceCompletionUp$ carrier $Q=(X,A,K,L,H,C,P,N)$, every $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer of the inherited subspace completion data reads only the displayed rows
$$
X,A,K,L,H,C,P,N .
$$
In particular, the packet cannot export quotient subset equality, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, or $\RealUp$ completeness beyond the named finite route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only consumer-facing rows are the parent completion row $X$, subspace admission row $A$, closedness-under-limit row $K$, inherited limit row $L$, and the four structural rows.

A $\CompleteMetricUp$ consumer must read $L$, and $L$ is available only after the closedness row $K$ has replayed the ambient route from $X$ with the subspace admission ledger $A$ attached.

The $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ consumers are no freer. Their finite windows, regular readbacks, and terminal real-name seals are inherited through the parent completion row and are replayed through the same closedness and subspace-admission route.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ do not add data; they only transport, replay, source, and name the accepted packet. Since none of the excluded objects is a coordinate of $Q$, no consumer can recover such an object from the packet.
\end{proof}
