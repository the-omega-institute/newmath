\begin{definition}[T0 reflection carrier]
\label{def:tzero-reflection-carrier}
A $\TZeroReflectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(T,S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ indexed-open row, carrying the named open indices, finite membership reads, and topology closure witnesses available from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}.
\item $S$ is the $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ observable-open truth row, reading each displayed open-index membership as a two-observation classifier rather than as a host proposition.
\item $R$ is the $\PreorderUp$ specialization row, recording the finite relation $x \preceq y$ by testing that every open read true at $x$ is also true at $y$ through $S$.
\item $Q$ is the $\PosetUp$ T0-reflection row, where the public separated order identifies points only by mutual specialization rows already visible in $R$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the topology row, Sierpinski truth row, specialization preorder row, T0 poset row, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an observable-open request through $T$ and $S$, then into $R$, and finally into the separated order row $Q$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TZeroReflectionUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$, $\PreorderUp$, $\PosetUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TZeroReflectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these displayed rows, their componentwise transports, their replay route, their provenance, and their local name row. It has no coordinate for host quotient equality, powerset extensionality, excluded middle over opens, selected representatives, or ambient point equality.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[T0 reflection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:tzero-reflection-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\TZeroReflectionUp$ carrier $K=(T,S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\TZeroReflectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the topology, observable-truth, specialization, separated-order, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows; observable-open exactness through $T,S$; specialization-preorder exactness through $R$; T0 separated-order handoff through $Q$; and provenance exhaustion through $P,N$. No obligation is discharged by a host quotient of points, a powerset equality of opens, proof irrelevance, excluded middle, selected representatives, or ambient set-level equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only public rows are $T,S,R,Q,H,C,P,N$, so every local obligation must be read from the indexed topology row, the observable-open truth row, the specialization preorder row, the T0 poset row, or the structural rows.

Observable-open exactness is supplied by $T$ and $S$. The topology row names the finite open-index requests, and the Sierpinski row turns each request into displayed truth data; no host proposition or powerset membership predicate is available to the packet.

Specialization exactness is then a replay through $R$. The preorder row can compare two points only by the observable implication route already displayed through $S$, so the reflexive and transitive reads remain preorder data rather than quotient equality.

Finally $Q$ performs the separated-order handoff. Mutual specialization is exported as the T0 reflection surface carried by the poset row, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed route. Since the excluded host objects have no carrier coordinate, they cannot appear as local $\NameCert_{\TZeroReflectionUp}$ obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[T0 reflection specialization-poset handoff]
\label{thm:tzero-reflection-specialization-poset-handoff}
Every consumer of an accepted $\TZeroReflectionUp$ packet that asks for a separated specialization order must factor through the finite route
$$
\TopologyUp \longmapsto \SierpinskiSpaceUp \longmapsto \PreorderUp \longmapsto \PosetUp .
$$
Thus observable-open truth is read before specialization order, and specialization order is read before the public T0 poset surface. The handoff exports no host quotient equality, powerset extensionality, proof irrelevance for opens, excluded middle over points, selected representative function, or ambient set-level equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier $K=(T,S,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$. A consumer first reads $T$, because the open indices and finite membership requests are the only topology data available to the packet.

Replay $C$ through $S$. The observable-open truth row converts those open-index requests into Sierpinski classifier data, so later order reads see displayed truth rows rather than host propositions.

The specialization preorder row $R$ receives exactly those truth rows. It may construct reflexive and transitive specialization comparisons, but it has no coordinate for collapsing points by an external quotient.

The final public handoff is $Q$. The poset row exposes the T0 reflection surface as separated specialization data, and $H,P,N$ preserve the same row order under transport, provenance, and naming. The refused host quotient and choice objects are absent from the carrier, so no consumer can export them.
\end{proof}
