It sits after the common-refinement route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-cover-refinement-namecert}, the formal-ball window of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-formalball-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Finite dyadic refinement budget carrier]
\label{def:finite-dyadic-refinement-budget-carrier}
A $\FiniteDyadicRefinementBudgetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(D,F,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicCoverRefinementUp$ common-refinement row whose source covers and restriction ledgers determine the dyadic cells being tightened.
\item $F$ is the $\FormalBallUp$ center-radius window whose radius request consumes the mesh budget before any completion-facing read is available.
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger. It records the dyadic mesh scale, its endpoint arithmetic, and the finite comparison rows used to say that the refined mesh is small enough for the requested radius.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window address at which the budget is read.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback that turns the stream-window and dyadic ledger into a scheduled tolerance row.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal read by Real-facing consumers after $Q,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the refinement row, formal-ball window, dyadic ledger, stream address, regular rational readback, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the finite route
$$
D,F,Q,S,R,E \longmapsto H .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteDyadicRefinementBudgetUp$, $\DyadicCoverRefinementUp$, $\FormalBallUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteDyadicRefinementBudgetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier of an accepted packet compares mesh-budget data only through these ten rows. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, selected limiting meshes, quotient equality of streams or real names, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite dyadic refinement budget NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-dyadic-refinement-budget-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteDyadicRefinementBudgetUp$ carrier
$$
B=(D,F,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteDyadicRefinementBudgetUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following six rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed $\DyadicCoverRefinementUp$ row, $\FormalBallUp$ window, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\StreamNameUp$ address, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item mesh-source exposure, requiring every budget request to name the common-refinement row $D$ before it names a refined mesh;
\item formal-ball exposure, requiring the radius request in $F$ to be visible before the budget is consumed by a completion-facing route;
\item dyadic scale exactness, requiring the mesh scale and endpoint comparisons to be read through $Q$;
\item regular-readback exactness, requiring the finite stream address $S$ and regular rational row $R$ to mediate the tolerance schedule before the real seal $E$ is inspected;
\item classifier transport through componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming, with no escape to open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, selected limiting meshes, quotient equality, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these six rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:finite-dyadic-refinement-budget-carrier}. The tuple displays the refinement row, formal-ball window, dyadic scale ledger, finite stream address, regular rational readback, real seal, and structural rows.

Mesh-source exposure is forced by $D$. The carrier has no detached mesh field; a refined mesh is read only as a budget attached to the common dyadic refinement route.

Formal-ball exposure is forced by $F$. The radius request that motivates the budget is part of the carrier, so a completion-facing consumer cannot read a budget before the formal-ball window has been displayed.

Dyadic scale exactness is the role of $Q$. Endpoint arithmetic, mesh scale comparison, and tolerance narrowing all remain in the dyadic ledger. The stream and regular rational rows $S,R$ then mediate the scheduled tolerance before the terminal real seal $E$ is available.

Finally inspect $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier contains no field for open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, selected limiting meshes, quotient equality, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the classifier cannot export such data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite dyadic refinement budget mesh handoff]
\label{thm:finite-dyadic-refinement-budget-mesh-handoff}
Let
$$
B=(D,F,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\FiniteDyadicRefinementBudgetUp$ carrier. Every consumer that receives a mesh-budget handoff from $B$ factors through the displayed route
$$
\DyadicCoverRefinementUp,\ \FormalBallUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp
\longmapsto D,F,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the handoff exposes a finite common-refinement source, a formal-ball radius request, a dyadic mesh ledger, a finite stream address, a regular rational tolerance readback, a terminal real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The source of the mesh is $D$, and the consuming radius request is $F$; these are the only rows that connect the refinement route to formal-ball data.

Read the scale data next. The dyadic ledger $Q$ supplies the mesh size and endpoint comparisons, while $S$ records the finite window at which that budget is read.

The regular rational row $R$ and real seal $E$ are downstream of the finite window. Thus a Real-facing consumer sees the mesh budget only after the stream-window and scheduled-tolerance rows have already been displayed.

The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name this route. Hence every mesh handoff factors through the finite packet and not through a detached compactness principle, selected mesh limit, quotient equality, choice schedule, or ambient real-completeness claim.

\begin{proof}

The real seal is terminal in this route. It records the Real-facing tolerance read only after the finite dyadic and regular rational rows have been exposed.

Because the carrier contains no coordinate for compactness, selected limiting meshes, quotient equality, choice, or ambient completeness, no Real-facing read can escape the finite budget surface.
