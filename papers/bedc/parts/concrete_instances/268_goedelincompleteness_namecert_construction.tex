\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GoedelIncompletenessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-goedelincompleteness-namecert}

The Gödel-incompleteness interface $\GoedelIncompletenessUp$ packages an arithmetised provability predicate over a recursively-axiomatised first-order theory of arithmetic, together with a self-referential fixed-point row. It depends on $\NameCert_{\NatUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert}), $\NameCert_{\FirstOrderUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-firstorder-namecert}), $\NameCert_{\ModelTheoryUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-modeltheory-namecert}), and $\NameCert_{\ComputableUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-computable-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:goedelincompleteness-carrier}

A $\GoedelIncompletenessUp$ token carries: a recursively-enumerated axiom set $T$ over the language of arithmetic, an arithmetised provability predicate $\Prov_T(x)$ via a $\NameCert_{\ComputableUp}$ proof-checking decoder, a Gödel numbering $\ulcorner\cdot\urcorner$ on formulas, and a fixed-point row $G$ with $T\vdash G\leftrightarrow\neg\Prov_T(\ulcorner G\urcorner)$. The carrier accepts an incompleteness witness when $T$ is consistent and $G$ is neither provable nor refutable in $T$. The classifier is $\hsame$ on the fixed-point rows; the ledger keeps the axiom enumeration, the proof-checker, the Gödel numbering, and the fixed-point row visible.

\begin{theorem}[GoedelIncompleteness proof-checker obligation]
\label{thm:goedelincompleteness-proof-checker-obligation}
An accepted $\GoedelIncompletenessUp$ witness exposes a recursively enumerated
axiom row, a $\ComputableUp$ proof-checker decoder, and the finite
proof-verdict ledger over $\mathsf{BHist}$. The carrier reads a proof row only
through the displayed decoder and records its verdict as a visible
$\FirstOrderUp$ syntactic row.

The obligation is syntactic and finite-ledger local. It does not assert
semantic completeness, second incompleteness, or a set-theoretic foundations
bridge.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GoedelIncompletenessUp.GoedelIncompletenessProofCheckerLedger\_verdict\_nonempty\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to its axiom-enumeration coordinate. That row
supplies the recursively enumerated theory data in the displayed
$\FirstOrderUp$ language.

Read the proof-checker coordinate through the $\ComputableUp$ decoder. Each
candidate proof is consumed by this finite decoder row, and the result is the
visible proof-verdict ledger over $\mathsf{BHist}$.

Repacking these coordinates gives the proof-checker obligation. No semantic
model argument, second-incompleteness claim, or external foundations bridge is
used.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GoedelIncompletenessUp.GoedelIncompletenessWitnessPacket\_proof\_checker\_obligation}

\begin{theorem}[GoedelIncompleteness fixed-point obligation]
\label{thm:goedelincompleteness-fixed-point-obligation}
The fixed-point row in an accepted $\GoedelIncompletenessUp$ witness carries
the displayed $\FirstOrderUp$ formula, its Gödel-number row, and the
provability-predicate row for the fixed proof-checker decoder. The classifier
$\hsame$ compares fixed-point histories only at the displayed numbering and
does not identify formulas across a different coding surface.

This obligation records the finite fixed-point ledger. It does not assert the
Löb conditions, semantic completeness, or a second-incompleteness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the fixed-point coordinate of the accepted packet. It exposes the
$\FirstOrderUp$ formula row and its Gödel-number row in the same finite
ledger.

Next read the provability-predicate coordinate. It is tied to the displayed
proof-checker decoder, so the predicate row refers to the fixed numbering
rather than to an ambient coding theorem.

The $\hsame$ classifier is then applied only at that fixed numbering. This
gives the stated fixed-point obligation without importing semantic or
metatheoretic closure beyond the visible rows.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GoedelIncompletenessUp.GoedelIncompletenessFixedPointLedger\_obligation}

\begin{theorem}[GoedelIncompleteness undecidable row obligation]
\label{thm:goedelincompleteness-undecidable-row-obligation}
Conditionally on the consistency row displayed by an accepted
$\GoedelIncompletenessUp$ witness, the witness ledger contains both the
no-proof row and the no-refutation row for the displayed fixed-point formula.
The no-proof row is checked against $\Prov_T(\ulcorner G\urcorner)$, and the
no-refutation row is checked against the displayed negation row in the same
$\FirstOrderUp$ syntax.

The statement is only the accepted finite undecidability ledger. It does not
claim second incompleteness, semantic completeness, $\omega$-consistency, or
the Löb conditions.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GoedelIncompletenessUp.GoedelIncompletenessWitnessPacket\_undecidable\_row\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Read the consistency coordinate, the fixed-point formula, and the
proof-checker verdict ledger from the accepted packet. They share the same
Gödel numbering and the same $\ComputableUp$ decoder.

For the no-proof side, inspect the verdict rows attached to
$\Prov_T(\ulcorner G\urcorner)$. The accepted packet records that no displayed
proof row verifies the fixed-point formula.

For the no-refutation side, inspect the verdict rows attached to the displayed
negation of $G$. The same proof-checker ledger records that no displayed proof
row verifies the refutation. Repacking the two rows gives the conditional
undecidable-row obligation.
\end{proof}
