\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyCompletionBindUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges and Mac Lane, the $\CauchyCompletionBindUp$ packet records the finite Kleisli-composition surface for Bishop-style completion: a completion-facing read may bind a source completion row to a displayed completion-valued route only through the already named $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionKleisliUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$ certificates. It is a local ledger for bind composition, not a quotient completion, not a selected representative, not countable choice, and not an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.

The bind packet consumes the completion monad of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionmonad-namecert}, the unit admission of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionunit-namecert}, the Kleisli consumer route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-kleisli-namecert}, the Bishop-regular completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert}, and the terminal Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} as concrete sibling rows rather than as background terminology.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionBind carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-bind-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionBindUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(S,U,K,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source-completion row over $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ and $\RealUp$, exposing the regular Cauchy input windows before any bind consumer is read.
\item $U$ is the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ row recording the unit admission for source points and terminal seal handoff.
\item $K$ is the $\CauchyCompletionKleisliUp$ row recording the single-bind consumer route and the reassociation schedule inherited from $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$.
\item $E$ is the completion seal row that records which displayed $\RealUp$ or Bishop-regular completion boundary the bind route may consume.
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for the source, unit, Kleisli, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming data.
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay row for the finite bind route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionBindUp$, $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionKleisliUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionBindUp}$ row naming the bind packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient Cauchy classes, selected representatives, countable-choice schedules, host equality of completed objects, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionBind NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-bind-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionBindUp$ carrier $B=(S,U,K,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionBindUp}$ surface consists exactly of source-completion exposure, unit admission, Kleisli bind exposure, completion-seal compatibility, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every bind consumer must first read the $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionKleisliUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows recorded in $P$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The rows $S,U,K,E$ are the only analytic rows available to a bind consumer, and each is already sourced from the named completion, unit, Kleisli, Bishop-regular, and real-seal siblings.

Transport and replay are structural. The row $H$ preserves the same source, unit, Kleisli, and seal coordinates under displayed $\hsame$ routes, while $C$ replays the same finite bind path through the consumer surface.

Finally $P$ and $N$ source and name the local certificate. Since the carrier contains no quotient class, representative selector, choice schedule, host equality, or ambient completeness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionBind associativity ledger]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-bind-associativity-ledger}
Let $B=(S,U,K,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionBindUp$ packet. Any two public reads of a two-step completion bind that use the same displayed source-completion row $S$, unit row $U$, Kleisli row $K$, completion seal $E$, transport row $H$, replay row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local name $N$ have the same finite associativity ledger. The ledger is a displayed route equality of BEDC rows; it is not quotient completion equality, not equality of selected representatives, not countable choice, and not a proof that $\RealUp$ is complete in the ambient host.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the two-step bind only through the carrier rows. The first route and the reassociated route both project the same $S,U,K,E$ coordinates, so their source completion, unit admission, Kleisli schedule, and seal handoff are the displayed data of one packet.

Use $H$ to transport the coordinates componentwise and $C$ to replay the same finite consumer path. These structural rows compare the bind schedules inside the packet rather than by host equality of completed objects.

The provenance and naming rows attach the comparison to the same $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionKleisliUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$ sources. No excluded quotient, representative, choice, or ambient-completeness object is available to alter the ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionBind real-completion forward link]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-bind-real-completion-forward-link}
Every accepted $\CauchyCompletionBindUp$ consumer that exposes a completion seal must first route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionmonad-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionunit-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-kleisli-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. It exports no quotient completion class, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, host equality of completed objects, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $B=(S,U,K,E,H,C,P,N)$. The source row $S$ and seal row $E$ are completion-facing only because the provenance row $P$ names the Cauchy monad, Bishop-regular completion, and Real seal siblings.

Read the bind route through $U$ and $K$. The unit row supplies the source admission boundary, and the Kleisli row supplies the displayed consumer route before any completion seal can be exposed.

The structural rows $H$ and $C$ only transport and replay the same finite route. Together with $P$ and $N$, they keep the consumer inside the cited sibling certificates, so the packet has no coordinate from which to recover quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, host completed-object equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
