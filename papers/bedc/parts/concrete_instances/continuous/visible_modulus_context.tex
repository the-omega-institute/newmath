\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier visible modulus context exactness]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-visible-modulus-context-iff}
For histories $p,q,source,map,target,modulus,cert$, a visible prefix on the
source, target, and certificate ledgers together with a visible suffix on the
modulus and certificate ledgers is exactly unary evidence for the two context
histories together with the central continuous-function carrier:
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(p\cdot source,map,p\cdot target,
modulus\cdot q,(p\cdot cert)\cdot q)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)\wedge\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(q)\wedge
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Peel the right suffix from the modulus and certificate ledgers by modulus
suffix exactness. The remaining carrier has the common left prefix on source,
target, and certificate, so carrier prefix exactness recovers the unary prefix
and the central carrier. Conversely, rebuild the prefixed carrier first, then
reattach the modulus suffix.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier visible-context target-certificate determinacy]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-visible-modulus-context-target-cert-deterministic}
For histories $p,q,source,map,target,target',modulus,cert,cert'$, if both
visible-modulus-context carrier presentations
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(p\cdot source,map,p\cdot target,
modulus\cdot q,(p\cdot cert)\cdot q)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(p\cdot source,map,p\cdot target',
modulus\cdot q,(p\cdot cert')\cdot q)
$$
hold, then the public target and certificate ledgers are determined by the
central presentation:
$$
\hsame(target,target')\land\hsame(cert,cert').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-visible-modulus-context-iff}
to the first visible carrier. This recovers the unary context histories
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(p)$ and $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(q)$ together with the
central witness
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert)$.

Apply the same exactness theorem to the second visible carrier. The common
visible prefix and suffix are peeled in the same way, producing the central
witness
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target',modulus,cert')$.

Now apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-target-cert-deterministic} to the
two central carrier witnesses. It gives the displayed
$\hsame(target,target')$ and $\hsame(cert,cert')$ conclusions, which are already
the public target and certificate comparisons required here.

The ten-field certificate fit is the following. The traditional target is
public target-certificate determinacy for visible continuous-function carrier
presentations. The BEDC source is $\mathsf{BHist}$ equipped with $\mathsf{Cont}$. The
carrier is $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ and the classifier is
$\hsame$. The stability obligation is visible-context public readback. The
ledger is source, map, target, modulus, and certificate histories together
with the visible prefix and suffix contexts. The core theorem is this result.
The exactness predicate is the displayed pair of $\hsame$ conclusions. The
standard bridge is deferred, and the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
