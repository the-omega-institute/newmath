\begin{theorem}[Cauchy criterion uniform NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-criterion-uniform-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-criterion-uniform-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCriterionUniformUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite packet $U=(S,D,R,E,M,H,C,P,N)$;
\item uniform window exposure through the $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$ and the common modulus row $M$;
\item dyadic tolerance exactness through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row $D$;
\item regular readback through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row $E$;
\item ledger non-escape through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $U=(S,D,R,E,M,H,C,P,N)$. Carrier admission is exactly the admission of this finite tuple, so a consumer can inspect only the displayed coordinates.

The uniform window obligation reads $S$ together with the common threshold discipline $M$. The dyadic tolerance obligation reads $D$, and the regular readback obligation reads $R$ before any terminal seal row $E$ is available.
