\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\DyadicUp$ interface records the finite dyadic naming surface used by constructive real-name and completion routes. It is the direct handoff object between $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the $\RealUp$ seal: every consumer receives displayed finite tolerance, stream-window, regular-sequence, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows, rather than host real equality, selected binary expansions, trichotomy, or completed quotient data.

\begin{definition}[DyadicUp carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-up-carrier}
A $\DyadicUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row, carrying the dyadic rational numerator, denominator, tolerance budget, and arithmetic ledger read by dyadic consumers.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row through which requested precision windows are opened.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational sequence row attached to the same window surface.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row, exposing only the displayed real-name boundary reached after $Q,S,R$ have been read.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dyadic core, stream window, regular sequence, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through $Q,S,R$ and then to the real seal $E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for selected binary expansions, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, quotient-stream representatives, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.DyadicUp.DyadicUp}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicUp NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-up-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicUp$ carrier
$$
D=(Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic core, stream window, regular sequence, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item dyadic core admission, requiring every tolerance and arithmetic read to pass through $Q$;
\item stream and regular-sequence admission, requiring the precision window $S$ and regular rational row $R$ to be displayed before any real-name consumer reads the dyadic packet;
\item real-seal handoff, requiring $E$ to expose only the $\RealUp$ boundary reached from $Q,S,R$;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the same dyadic core, stream, regular sequence, and real-seal rows componentwise;
\item nonescape from selected binary expansions, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, quotient-stream representatives, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No $\DyadicUp$ naming obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:dyadic-up-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the dyadic rational core $Q$, finite stream window $S$, regular rational row $R$, real seal $E$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Dyadic core, stream, and regular-sequence admission follow from the displayed route order. A precision request first reads the dyadic tolerance ledger $Q$, opens the finite window $S$, and then reads the regular rational row $R$ attached to that window.

The real-seal handoff is then forced through $E$. Since $E$ is the only Real-facing coordinate of the carrier, a downstream Real or Completion consumer cannot replace the displayed handoff by host equality, a selected binary expansion, or a quotient-stream representative.

Transport and replay are componentwise. The $\hsame$ row $H$ preserves each displayed coordinate, while the $\Cont$ row $C$ repeats the same finite path from precision request to real seal.

Finally inspect $P$ and $N$. Provenance and local naming mention only $Q,S,R,E,H,C$, so the excluded choice, trichotomy, quotient, host-equality, and completeness data have no projection from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicUp real-seal factorization]
\label{thm:dyadic-up-real-seal-factorization}
Let $D=(Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\DyadicUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$-facing consumer of $D$ must read the displayed dyadic core $Q$, the finite stream window $S$, the regular rational row $R$, and the terminal real seal $E$ in that order before it can export a Real-facing name.

Consequently a downstream Real or Completion route cannot introduce selected binary expansions, quotient stream representatives, trichotomy, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness while still claiming to consume the same $\DyadicUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-up-carrier}. The only Real-facing coordinate is $E$, and it is displayed after the dyadic rational core $Q$, finite window row $S$, and regular rational row $R$.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-up-namecert-obligations}. Its dyadic core, stream, regular-sequence, and real-seal obligations force each precision request through $Q,S,R,E$ before any local name can be read by a downstream Real consumer.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ only preserve, replay, account for, and name that same displayed route. Since the excluded analytic and quotient objects are absent from the carrier, they cannot be inserted by a Real-facing consumer without leaving the accepted $\DyadicUp$ NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicUp StreamName RegSeqRat route]
\label{thm:dyadic-up-streamname-regseqrat-route}
Let $D=(Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\DyadicUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$-facing $\DyadicUp$ read first opens the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $Q$, then the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window $S$, then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$, before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$ is visible. Thus the dyadic route depends on the sibling chapters \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} as finite packet rows, not as ambient host-real completeness data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-up-carrier}. The displayed coordinates place $Q$, $S$, and $R$ before the only terminal seal coordinate $E$.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-up-real-seal-factorization}. A Real-facing consumer cannot reach $E$ until the dyadic core, stream window, and regular rational row have all been read in the carrier order.

The sibling chapters provide exactly those finite rows: the tolerance ledger from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite-window classifier from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the regular rational readback from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name that route, so no selected binary expansion, quotient stream, trichotomy, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness principle is exposed by the handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicUp terminal Real-seal boundary]
\label{thm:dyadicup-terminal-real-seal-boundary}
Let
$$
D=(Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\DyadicUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$ or Completion-facing consumer of $D$ factors through exactly the rows $Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$, with $Q$ read from $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $S$ from $\StreamNameUp$, $R$ from $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $E$ from $\RealUp$. No selected binary expansion, quotient stream, host equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient Real-completeness row is readable from the terminal handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-up-carrier}. It displays the mathematical route rows $Q,S,R,E$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$, and it contains no additional Real-facing or Completion-facing coordinate.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-up-namecert-obligations}. The accepted NameCert surface forces dyadic core admission through $Q$, stream-window admission through $S$, regular-sequence admission through $R$, and real-seal admission through $E$.

Now use \autoref{thm:dyadic-up-real-seal-factorization} and \autoref{thm:dyadic-up-streamname-regseqrat-route}. Together they exhaust the route from the dyadic tolerance ledger through finite windows and regular rational readback before the terminal seal can be read.

The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name that route. Since the excluded binary, quotient, host-equality, trichotomy, choice, and completeness data are absent from every displayed coordinate, no terminal consumer can read them while staying inside the accepted $\DyadicUp$ packet.
\end{proof}
