\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RationalIntervalUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}

The $\RationalIntervalUp$ interface names finite closed rational intervals on
the path from $\RatUp$ and dyadic rational cores to $\RegSeqRatUp$ and the
$\RealUp$ finite-window seal. In the constructive analysis tradition of
Bishop and Bridges, an interval is not a hidden located subset of the real
line. It is a displayed pair of rational endpoints, an order witness, and the
finite ledgers by which consumers read containment and refinement.

\begin{definition}[RationalInterval BHist carrier]
\label{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}
A $\RationalIntervalUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
I=(l,r,o,\kappa,h,c,p,n)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $l$ is the left endpoint row, a $\RatUp$ carrier displayed in the
  packet;
\item $r$ is the right endpoint row, a $\RatUp$ carrier displayed in the
  packet;
\item $o$ is the order witness row certifying the displayed endpoint
  comparison $l \leq r$ through the $\RatUp$ comparison ledger;
\item $\kappa$ is the containment ledger recording exactly which rational
  sample rows are certified to lie between $l$ and $r$;
\item $h$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transport rows for endpoints,
  the order witness, containment rows, and consumer-facing coordinates;
\item $c$ records the $\Cont$ routes by which a finite consumer reads the
  endpoint pair, order witness, containment ledger, and refinement windows;
\item $p$ is the visible $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\RatUp$,
  $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ dependency certificates
  used by the packet;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RationalIntervalUp}$ registration row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is
$\mathsf{RationalIntervalPacket}(l,r,o,\kappa,h,c,p,n)$. Its classifier
compares two accepted packets only by transporting the displayed endpoint
rows and replaying the $\RatUp$ order and containment ledgers over the same
finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:rational-interval-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\RationalIntervalUp}$ seed obligation surface consists
of six finite rows: the carrier of
\autoref{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}, endpoint equality through the
displayed $\RatUp$ classifiers, the endpoint order witness, containment
ledger exactness, $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ stability for finite refinements, and
the $\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ handoff boundary for finite rational windows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the eight carrier coordinates. The endpoint rows $l$ and $r$ provide
the rational carrier data, while the order witness $o$ and containment ledger
$\kappa$ provide the comparison rows exposed to interval consumers.

The stability rows are exactly $h$ and $c$. The dependency and local naming
surface is carried by $p$ and $n$, so the six listed obligations account for
every row of the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval endpoint order witness]
\label{thm:rational-interval-endpoint-order-witness}
Every accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ packet displays a $\RatUp$ endpoint pair
$(l,r)$ together with an order witness row $o$ certifying $l \leq r$ in the
same finite history. Transporting the interval by its stored $\hsame$ rows
transports both endpoints and the order witness componentwise, and it
introduces no apartness decision, total order on completed reals, or selected
real endpoint outside the displayed $\RatUp$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the endpoint and order coordinates from
\autoref{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}. The order witness row $o$ is
stored next to $l$ and $r$, so a consumer cannot obtain the interval without
also reading the finite comparison ledger that makes the endpoint order
visible.

Under transport, $h$ supplies the componentwise $\hsame$ rows for the two
endpoints and for $o$. Repacking those transported rows preserves the same
ordered endpoint surface and leaves no coordinate for completed real order
or a selected external endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval containment ledger exactness]
\label{thm:rational-interval-containment-ledger-exactness}
For an accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ packet
$I=(l,r,o,\kappa,h,c,p,n)$, every rational containment read is exactly one of
the finite rows in $\kappa$ routed by $c$ and transported by $h$. Conversely,
any displayed row of $\kappa$ with its endpoint, order, transport,
continuation, package, and local naming evidence is accepted as a
$\RationalIntervalUp$ containment read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $I$ through
\autoref{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}. The only containment
coordinate is $\kappa$, and its routes to consumers are the displayed
$\Cont$ rows in $c$.

The forward direction follows by reading the consumer route and then the
corresponding containment row. The transported endpoint and order data needed
to interpret that row are supplied by $h$ and $o$.

For the reverse direction, start with a displayed row of $\kappa$ together
with the matching endpoint, order, transport, continuation, package, and name
rows. These are exactly the coordinates required by the carrier, so the row
is accepted as a containment read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:rational-interval-regseqrat-handoff}
A $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ packet may read
only the displayed $\RatUp$ endpoints, the endpoint order witness, the finite
containment ledger, and the associated $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert$ rows. The handoff supplies a closed rational window for regular
rational sequence arguments; it does not assert Cauchy completeness, select a
limit, or identify completed $\RealUp$ values.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier packet and project the rows visible to a regular
rational sequence consumer. The endpoint rows and order witness delimit the
finite window, while $\kappa$ records the rational samples certified to lie
inside it.

The remaining consumer-facing information is precisely the transport,
continuation, provenance, and naming data in $h$, $c$, $p$, and $n$.
Repacking those rows gives the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff and leaves no row for a
quotient Cauchy class or a completed real equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval RealUp seed boundary]
\label{thm:rational-interval-realup-seed-boundary}
When a finite-window $\RealUp$ construction consumes an accepted
$\RationalIntervalUp$ packet, the seed boundary is the same displayed rational
interval packet: two $\RatUp$ endpoints, the order witness, containment
ledger, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert_{\RationalIntervalUp}$ row. The boundary is a rational closed
window and not a completed real object, a located subset, or a standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff of
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-regseqrat-handoff}. It exposes the rational
window as finite endpoint and containment rows, with all reads routed through
the same transport and continuation data.

The $\RealUp$ seed consumer can therefore inherit only this finite packet.
Since the carrier has no coordinate for a completed real, a located subset,
or a bridge witness, the seed boundary remains the displayed rational
interval surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval endpoint containment transport]
\label{thm:rational-interval-endpoint-containment-transport}
For accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ packets connected by displayed $\hsame$
rows, endpoint order and containment transport together: the lower endpoint,
upper endpoint, order witness, finite containment ledger, $\Cont$ routes,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row remain the complete
interval readback surface after transport.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet through
\autoref{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}. The carrier has endpoint
coordinates, one endpoint-order coordinate, one containment ledger, and the
visible transport, continuation, package, and naming rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:rational-interval-endpoint-order-witness} to transport the
order witness along the displayed endpoint identifications. The transported
endpoints are still the two $\RatUp$ rows of the same finite carrier.

Use \autoref{thm:rational-interval-containment-ledger-exactness} for the
containment coordinate. Since each containment read is interpreted with the
transported endpoint and order rows, the transported ledger is exactly the
same finite interval surface and contains no completed-real or located-subset
coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval common refinement window]
\label{thm:rational-interval-common-refinement-window}
Two accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ packets with compatible endpoint and
containment rows refine to one finite rational observation window whose
visible data are the transported endpoints, the common endpoint-order
witness, the common containment ledger, displayed $\hsame$ transport,
$\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the endpoint rows of the two carrier packets from
\autoref{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}. Compatibility supplies the
displayed $\hsame$ routes needed to compare lower endpoints with lower
endpoints and upper endpoints with upper endpoints.

The common order row is obtained by applying
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-endpoint-order-witness} after those endpoint
transports. The containment rows are then read against that common ordered
window.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-containment-ledger-exactness}. It exhausts the
finite containment ledger by rational samples, endpoint-order evidence,
transport, continuation, package, and name rows, so the common refinement
exports one finite rational observation window and no completed $\RealUp$
object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval RealUp consumer exactness]
\label{thm:rational-interval-realup-consumer-exactness}
Every finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$
packet reads only the two endpoint rows, the endpoint-order witness, the
containment ledger, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance,
local $\NameCert$ evidence, and the $\hsame$ rows needed to transport those
data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:rational-interval-realup-seed-boundary}. It identifies the
$\RealUp$ seed boundary with the displayed rational interval packet rather
than with a completed real, located subset, or bridge witness.

Endpoint order is supplied by
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-endpoint-order-witness}, and containment is
supplied by \autoref{thm:rational-interval-containment-ledger-exactness}.
Both rows are coordinates of
\autoref{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}, with consumer access routed
through the displayed $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, $\NameCert$, and $\hsame$
evidence.

Thus the real consumer cannot observe anything outside the finite interval
packet. In particular, quotient Cauchy classes, located subsets, interval
arithmetic laws, and standard bridges are not part of this consumer surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval refinement composition]
\label{thm:rational-interval-refinement-composition}
Suppose two displayed finite $\RationalIntervalUp$ refinement rows share the
middle rational endpoint window. If each row is accepted through its displayed
$\RatUp$ endpoints, endpoint-order witness, containment ledger, $\hsame$
transport, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ evidence,
then the two refinements compose to a single accepted finite refinement row
from the first window to the last. The composed row replays the shared
endpoint classifier rows, concatenates the two finite $\Cont$ routes, and
repackages exactly the same containment-ledger coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two refinement packets through
\autoref{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}. Their visible coordinates are
only endpoint rows, one order witness for each endpoint window, containment
ledger rows, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and
local naming evidence.

The stored endpoint classifier rows identify the middle lower endpoint with
the next lower endpoint, and the middle upper endpoint with the next upper
endpoint. With those rows fixed, the two order witnesses read the same middle
window and can be replayed as one finite refinement step across the outer
endpoints.

Concatenate the two displayed $\Cont$ routes and carry along the two
containment ledgers. The containment coordinates are already interpreted
against the transported endpoint and order rows, so repacking them yields the
outer refinement packet and introduces no completed $\RealUp$ equality,
located subset, or bridge coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval endpoint transport classifier]
\label{thm:rational-interval-endpoint-transport-classifier}
Endpoint transport for accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ packets preserves the
interval classifier componentwise: lower endpoints transport only along lower
$\RatUp$ rows, upper endpoints transport only along upper $\RatUp$ rows, and
the endpoint-order coordinate is read against those transported components.
Consequently endpoint transport cannot introduce a completed $\RealUp$
equality row or an apartness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the endpoint coordinates from
\autoref{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}. The carrier separates the lower
endpoint, upper endpoint, and endpoint-order witness, and each classifier row
is attached to its corresponding endpoint coordinate.

Use the stored $\hsame$ rows to transport lower endpoints along lower
coordinates and upper endpoints along upper coordinates. The order witness is
then replayed over those two transported coordinates, so the classifier has
the same lower, upper, and order components after transport.

No coordinate of the packet contains a completed $\RealUp$ equality or an
apartness witness. Since transport only reuses the displayed endpoint
classifier rows and the order witness coordinate, neither kind of row can be
created by endpoint transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval finite-window consumer exhaustion]
\label{thm:rational-interval-window-consumer-exhaustion}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer and every finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer of an
accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ packet factors through the displayed endpoint,
order, containment, transport, continuation, package, and local $\NameCert$
rows. These rows exhaust the consumer surface of the finite rational window.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier tuple. Its observable coordinates are precisely
the two $\RatUp$ endpoints, the endpoint-order witness, the finite containment
ledger, the displayed $\hsame$ rows, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and
the local naming evidence.

For a $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer, apply
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-regseqrat-handoff}. The handoff reads the
rational window only through those endpoint, order, containment, transport,
continuation, package, and name rows.

For a finite-window $\RealUp$ consumer, apply
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-realup-seed-boundary} and then
\autoref{thm:rational-interval-realup-consumer-exactness}. The seed boundary
and consumer exactness identify the same finite packet as the complete
readback surface, so the consumer has no additional row for quotient reals,
located subsets, apartness, or standard bridges.
\end{proof}
