\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RationalIntervalUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}

The $\RationalIntervalUp$ interface names finite closed rational intervals on
the path from $\RatUp$ and dyadic rational cores to $\RegSeqRatUp$ and the
$\RealUp$ finite-window seal. In the constructive analysis tradition of
Bishop and Bridges, an interval is not a hidden located subset of the real
line. It is a displayed pair of rational endpoints, an order witness, and the
finite ledgers by which consumers read containment and refinement.

\begin{definition}[RationalInterval BHist carrier]
\label{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}
A $\RationalIntervalUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
I=(l,r,o,\kappa,h,c,p,n)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $l$ is the left endpoint row, a $\RatUp$ carrier displayed in the
  packet;
\item $r$ is the right endpoint row, a $\RatUp$ carrier displayed in the
  packet;
\item $o$ is the order witness row certifying the displayed endpoint
  comparison $l \leq r$ through the $\RatUp$ comparison ledger;
\item $\kappa$ is the containment ledger recording exactly which rational
  sample rows are certified to lie between $l$ and $r$;
\item $h$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transport rows for endpoints,
  the order witness, containment rows, and consumer-facing coordinates;
\item $c$ records the $\Cont$ routes by which a finite consumer reads the
  endpoint pair, order witness, containment ledger, and refinement windows;
\item $p$ is the visible $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\RatUp$,
  $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ dependency certificates
  used by the packet;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RationalIntervalUp}$ registration row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is
$\mathsf{RationalIntervalPacket}(l,r,o,\kappa,h,c,p,n)$. Its classifier
compares two accepted packets only by transporting the displayed endpoint
rows and replaying the $\RatUp$ order and containment ledgers over the same
finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:rational-interval-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\RationalIntervalUp}$ seed obligation surface consists
of six finite rows: the carrier of
\autoref{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}, endpoint equality through the
displayed $\RatUp$ classifiers, the endpoint order witness, containment
ledger exactness, $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ stability for finite refinements, and
the $\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ handoff boundary for finite rational windows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the eight carrier coordinates. The endpoint rows $l$ and $r$ provide
the rational carrier data, while the order witness $o$ and containment ledger
$\kappa$ provide the comparison rows exposed to interval consumers.

The stability rows are exactly $h$ and $c$. The dependency and local naming
surface is carried by $p$ and $n$, so the six listed obligations account for
every row of the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval endpoint order witness]
\label{thm:rational-interval-endpoint-order-witness}
Every accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ packet displays a $\RatUp$ endpoint pair
$(l,r)$ together with an order witness row $o$ certifying $l \leq r$ in the
same finite history. Transporting the interval by its stored $\hsame$ rows
transports both endpoints and the order witness componentwise, and it
introduces no apartness decision, total order on completed reals, or selected
real endpoint outside the displayed $\RatUp$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RationalIntervalUp.RationalIntervalPacket\_endpoint\_order\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Read the endpoint and order coordinates from
\autoref{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}. The order witness row $o$ is
stored next to $l$ and $r$, so a consumer cannot obtain the interval without
also reading the finite comparison ledger that makes the endpoint order
visible.

Under transport, $h$ supplies the componentwise $\hsame$ rows for the two
endpoints and for $o$. Repacking those transported rows preserves the same
ordered endpoint surface and leaves no coordinate for completed real order
or a selected external endpoint.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RationalIntervalUp.RationalIntervalPacket\_endpoint\_order\_classifier}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RationalIntervalUp.RationalIntervalEndpointRows\_order\_witness}

\begin{theorem}[RationalInterval containment ledger exactness]
\label{thm:rational-interval-containment-ledger-exactness}
For an accepted $\RationalIntervalUp$ packet
$I=(l,r,o,\kappa,h,c,p,n)$, every rational containment read is exactly one of
the finite rows in $\kappa$ routed by $c$ and transported by $h$. Conversely,
any displayed row of $\kappa$ with its endpoint, order, transport,
continuation, package, and local naming evidence is accepted as a
$\RationalIntervalUp$ containment read.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RationalIntervalUp.RationalIntervalPacket\_containment\_ledger\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Project $I$ through
\autoref{def:rational-interval-bhist-carrier}. The only containment
coordinate is $\kappa$, and its routes to consumers are the displayed
$\Cont$ rows in $c$.

The forward direction follows by reading the consumer route and then the
corresponding containment row. The transported endpoint and order data needed
to interpret that row are supplied by $h$ and $o$.

For the reverse direction, start with a displayed row of $\kappa$ together
with the matching endpoint, order, transport, continuation, package, and name
rows. These are exactly the coordinates required by the carrier, so the row
is accepted as a containment read.
\end{proof}
