\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closed-bounded-interval-compact-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on compact intervals and finite approximation, the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp$ packet records the compactness of a closed bounded interval as finite NameCert data. It consumes the closed bounded interval source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert}, the finite-net cover route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-heine-borel-finite-net-namecert}, the interval uniform-modulus source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-bounded-interval-uniform-modulus-namecert}, the compact-metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the endpoint real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a displayed compact interval certificate for downstream finite-net, Bolzano-Weierstrass, and compact-uniform-continuity consumers; it is not an unrestricted open-cover compactness theorem, a selected subcover functional, a quotient interval representative, or an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness assertion.

\begin{definition}[Closed bounded interval compact carrier]
\label{def:closed-bounded-interval-compact-carrier}
A $\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(I,F,U,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ source row, exposing endpoint order, rational containment, dyadic containment, stream windows, regular rational readback, and Real sealing.
\item $F$ is the $\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp$ finite-net compactness row, recording the listed cover witnesses and radius comparisons consumed by finite-cover readers.
\item $U$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUniformModulusUp$ sibling row, recording the compact-uniform source route over the same interval source and finite cover data.
\item $M$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ metric compactness row, read only through its finite-net, total-boundedness, and complete-limit components.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ endpoint and radius seal reached only after $I,F,U,M$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval source, finite-net row, uniform-modulus row, compact-metric row, Real seal, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from closed bounded interval inspection through finite-net compactness, compact-uniform source routing, compact-metric readback, and Real sealing.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$, $\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUniformModulusUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,F,U,M,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for arbitrary open covers, selected subcovers, selected points, quotient real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed bounded interval compact NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-bounded-interval-compact-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp$ carrier $K=(I,F,U,M,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the interval source, finite-net compactness row, uniform-modulus sibling row, compact-metric row, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item interval-source exposure, requiring every compactness read to display the accepted $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ row before inspecting finite-net or compact-metric data;
\item finite-net compactness exposure, requiring cover-facing reads to pass through the $\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp$ row and its displayed radius and cover-witness ledgers;
\item compact-uniform compatibility, requiring the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUniformModulusUp$ and $\CompactMetricUp$ rows to be read as sibling routes over the same interval source and Real seal;
\item non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed packet and to export no unrestricted open-cover compactness, selected subcover, selected point, quotient real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:closed-bounded-interval-compact-carrier}. The tuple displays exactly the interval source, finite-net compactness row, compact-uniform sibling row, compact-metric row, Real seal, and structural rows.

The interval source is upstream. Any compactness reader must first read $I$, so endpoint order, rational containment, dyadic containment, stream windows, regular rational readback, and the Real-facing source seal are fixed before finite-net or compact-metric data are inspected.

The finite-net compactness obligation is the row $F$. Its cover witnesses and radius comparisons are the only cover-facing compactness data in the carrier, and they are consumed through the Heine-Borel finite-net route rather than through a host open-cover theorem.

The rows $U$ and $M$ are sibling compatibility rows. They let compact-uniform-continuity and compact-metric consumers use the same interval source and Real seal, but neither row changes the source interval or adds a selected subcover, selected point, quotient equality, trichotomy, choice, or ambient completeness coordinate.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve only the displayed rows. Since every refused object is absent from the carrier, the local NameCert surface is exactly the five obligations listed above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed bounded interval compact finite-net route]
\label{thm:closed-bounded-interval-compact-finite-net-route}
Every compactness-facing consumer of an accepted $\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp
\longmapsto
\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp
\longmapsto
\CompactMetricUp
\longmapsto
\RealUp .
$$
The $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUniformModulusUp$ row is available as the compact-uniform sibling route over the same interval and finite-net data. Thus $\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp$ supplies a precise compact interval anchor for finite-net compactness, Bolzano-Weierstrass, and compact-uniform-continuity consumers without exporting open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, quotient real equality, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $K=(I,F,U,M,E,H,C,P,N)$. The interval source is $I$, the finite-net compactness route is $F$, the compact-metric row is $M$, and the terminal Real seal is $E$.

Replay through $C$ keeps this order. A compactness-facing request opens the closed bounded interval source, reads the finite-net cover and radius row, then inspects the compact-metric finite-net and limit components before it reaches the Real-facing seal.

The row $U$ records the compatible uniform-modulus route for consumers that need compact uniformity. It remains tied to the same interval source, finite-net row, compact-metric row, and Real seal, so it cannot introduce an unrestricted open-cover theorem, selected subcover, quotient equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed bounded interval compact finite-net fan]
\label{thm:closed-bounded-interval-compact-finite-net-fan}
Every Bolzano-Weierstrass, compact-uniform-continuity, and finite-cover consumer of an accepted $\ClosedBoundedIntervalCompactUp$ packet must pass through the displayed finite route
$$
\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp
\longmapsto
\HeineBorelFiniteNetUp
\longmapsto
\CompactMetricUp
\longmapsto
\RealUp
$$
together with the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUniformModulusUp$ sibling row before reading any Real seal. The fan exports only the interval source, finite-net compactness row, compact-uniform sibling row, compact-metric row, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows; it exports no open-cover compactness, selected subcover, selected point, quotient real equality, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $K=(I,F,U,M,E,H,C,P,N)$. The rows $I,F,M,E$ are precisely the closed bounded interval source, Heine-Borel finite-net row, compact-metric row, and terminal Real seal displayed in \autoref{thm:closed-bounded-interval-compact-finite-net-route}.

Read the consumer fan by cases. A finite-cover consumer uses $F$; a Bolzano-Weierstrass consumer uses the compact-metric row $M$ only after $F$ has exposed the finite-net route; a compact-uniform-continuity consumer also reads the sibling row $U$ over the same interval and finite-net data.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve this order for every branch of the fan. Since all refused objects are absent from the carrier, no branch can bypass the displayed route or read the Real seal before the interval, finite-net, compact-metric, and uniform-modulus rows have been displayed.
\end{proof}
