\begin{theorem}[CauchyProduct budgeted root source triple]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-budgeted-root-source-triple}
For every accepted $\CauchyProductUp$ packet, the two $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ source rows, the paired $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius and observation rows form the admitted root source triple for the product classifier. The triple is the finite $\BHist$ route
$$
(X,Y),\quad (W_X,W_Y),\quad (M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y)
$$
inside the packet
$$
Z=(X,Y,W_X,W_Y,M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y,P,E,H,C,L,N),
$$
and it reaches the product row and $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier only through the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductUp}$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-product-namecert-source}. Its displayed coordinates put the two regular Cauchy source rows, the paired windows, and the dyadic radius and observation rows in one finite packet before the product row $P$ and classifier row $E$ are read.

Apply the root source admission of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-source-admission} and the root source window totality of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-source-window-totality}. These rows force every product read to enter through the two source rows and the paired StreamName windows.

Now use the root budget product lock of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-budget-product-lock}. The dyadic observations and radius rows are the budgeted route into $P$ and $E$, while $H,C,L,N$ carry the displayed transport, continuation, package, and local naming evidence. Coordinate exhaustion leaves no second source route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyProduct window-budget NameCert ledger]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-window-budget-namecert-ledger}
For an accepted $\CauchyProductUp$ packet feeding a product observation into $\RegSeqRatUp$, the finite window-budget ledger is the only $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductUp}$ ledger available to that observation. It is the displayed inventory
$$
W_X,W_Y,M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y,P,L,H,C,N
$$
together with the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier row $E$ and, when a $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ packet consumes the read, the budget rows
$$
E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B.
$$
No separate ledger can feed the product observation into $\RegSeqRatUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Next apply the window-budget composition surface of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-window-budget-composition-surface}. This identifies the path from paired StreamName windows and DyadicRatCore rows to the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier $E$ as a single finite route.

Finally use the budgeted product consumer boundary of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-budget-product-consumer-boundary}. If a budget packet is present, it adds only the displayed budget rows, and those rows read the same product ledger. Thus the NameCert ledger feeding the RegSeqRat observation is exactly the stated inventory.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyProduct Real seal root consumer boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-real-seal-root-consumer-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$-facing product seal read of an accepted $\CauchyProductUp$ packet factors through the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows of the root package. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the budgeted root source triple of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-budgeted-root-source-triple}. It fixes the source, window, radius, and dyadic observation rows before the product row and classifier can be inspected.

Use the window-budget NameCert ledger of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-window-budget-namecert-ledger}. The ledger identifies the exact route through $P,L,H,C,N$ and, when present, through the observation-budget rows feeding the same classifier.

Finally apply the Real seal root surface of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-real-seal-root-unblock-surface} together with the budget-triangle Real seal exhaustion of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-budget-triangle-real-seal-exhaustion}. These theorems place every real-facing product seal behind the same finite product and budget rows, so host multiplication, quotient streams, selected limits, and ambient completeness have no consumer coordinate.
\end{proof}
