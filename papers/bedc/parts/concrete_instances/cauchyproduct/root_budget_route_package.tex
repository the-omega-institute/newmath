\begin{theorem}[CauchyProduct budget route package]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-budget-route-package}
Let $Z=(X,Y,W_X,W_Y,M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y,P,E,H,C,L,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyProductUp$ packet. Suppose its product is read together with an accepted $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$ finite-approximation packet and an accepted $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ observation-budget packet over the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ rows. Then these packets assemble one finite product budget route
$$
X,Y,W_X,W_Y,M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y,P,E,H,C,L,N
$$
with the finite approximation rows and the observation-budget rows
$$
E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B
$$
over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and the local $\NameCert$ evidence. The assembled route is the one consumed by $\RealUp$ and completion-facing Cauchy readers; it is not a loose union of separate budget lemmas.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the CauchyProduct side. The obligation closure of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-obligation-closure}, the window-budget composition surface of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-window-budget-composition-surface}, and the root product readiness package of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-product-readiness-package} bind the paired $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations, product row, classifier row, Real seal row, transport rows, continuation rows, package provenance, ledger row, and local certificate row into one finite packet.

Attach the root-unblock and triangle rows next. The source-window unblock certificate of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-unblock-certificate}, the observation-budget triangle of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-observation-budget-triangle}, and the RealAlgOrder Completion handoff of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-realalgorder-completion-handoff} identify the same product route as the route read by downstream Real and completion-facing consumers.

Now add the finite approximation and observation-budget chapters. The accepted $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$ packet is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-cauchy-approximation-namecert}, and the accepted $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ packet is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-observation-budget-namecert}. These chapters supply the finite approximation, precision, finite-window, dyadic-readback, regular-sequence, real-seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows that match the product route.

The joined package is therefore a single route over the displayed kernel objects. Since every cited surface is finite and row-exhaustive, the assembled package exports only the listed product, finite-approximation, observation-budget, transport, continuation, provenance, and certificate rows.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyProductUp.CauchyProductPacket\_budget\_route\_package}

\label{thm:cauchy-product-componentwise-window-admission}

\begin{corollary}[CauchyProduct budget route strict local obstruction]
\label{cor:cauchy-product-budget-route-strict-obstruction}
Let $Z=(X,Y,W_X,W_Y,M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y,P,E,H,C,L,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyProductUp$ packet. Let $F=(R_F,n_F,k_F,W_F,D_F,E_F,S_F,H_F,C_F,P_F,N_F)$ be the accepted $\FiniteCauchyApproximationUp$ packet serving the same finite product read, and let $B=(E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B)$ be the accepted $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ packet on the same route. Suppose a displayed failure, refusal, separation, or countermodel row is cited against a named converse or reuse route for the product budget package of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-budget-route-package}. Then that row is a strict local obstruction: it blocks only the named route whose consumer-facing read tries to omit, replace, detach, or bypass one of the displayed coordinates
$$
X,Y,W_X,W_Y,M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y,P,E,H,C,L,N,
$$
$$
R_F,n_F,k_F,W_F,D_F,E_F,S_F,H_F,C_F,P_F,N_F,
$$
$$
E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B,
$$
together with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ support rows. The same row cannot be reinterpreted as a global impossibility statement for $\CauchyProductUp$, as a host theorem about multiplication or completion, as an external validation channel, as a quotient-stream argument, as a selected real value, as a host metric, as a trichotomy row, as an independent real carrier, as an ambient completeness principle, or as an external Cauchy-limit principle.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
First apply the budget route package of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-budget-route-package}. It assembles the accepted $\CauchyProductUp$ packet with the accepted finite-approximation and observation-budget packets into one finite product budget route. On the product side the route displays the source rows, paired windows, modulus rows, dyadic readbacks, product row, classifier row, real-seal support, transports, continuations, ledger, provenance, and local naming evidence. On the auxiliary sides it displays the finite approximation rows of $F$ and the observation-budget rows of $B$. Thus a consumer-facing converse or reuse route has no first read except through these displayed packet coordinates.

Next apply the Real handoff non-escape theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-real-handoff-nonescape}. Every $\RealUp$ or $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ consumer of the assembled route receives only the displayed product rows, finite-approximation rows, observation-budget rows, transport rows, continuation rows, provenance rows, and local $\NameCert$ rows. In particular, quotient streams, selected real values, host metrics, host multiplication primitives, trichotomy rows, independent real carriers, ambient completeness principles, and external Cauchy-limit principles are not exported coordinates of the handoff.

Now consider the cited failure, refusal, separation, or countermodel row. If the named converse or reuse route asks to omit, replace, detach, or bypass one of the displayed product, finite-approximation, observation-budget, transport, continuation, provenance, ledger, or local naming coordinates, the cited row blocks exactly that request at the challenged coordinate of the accepted package. If the route instead asks for a global impossibility statement, a host theorem, an external validation channel, a quotient stream, a selected real value, a host metric, a trichotomy row, an independent real carrier, an ambient completeness principle, or an external Cauchy-limit principle, \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-real-handoff-nonescape} gives no coordinate through which such a request can be read.

Therefore every permitted consumer-facing use of the obstruction factors through the displayed finite route, and every unlisted coordinate is rejected as outside the accepted $\CauchyProductUp$ budget route package. The obstruction is strict and local to the named converse or reuse route, and it asserts nothing beyond the displayed package.
\end{proof}
