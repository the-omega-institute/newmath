\label{thm:cauchy-product-source-window-bilinear-synchronization}

$$
X,Y,W_X,W_Y,M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y
$$
over the sibling carriers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The row $P$ is therefore a bilinear readback from the displayed pair of regular-Cauchy source windows, not a host multiplication primitive, selected limit, quotient stream, or ambient product operation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Next use the radius budget conservation theorem \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-radius-budget-conservation}. The dyadic rows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} attach the source windows to the product-radius ledger before $P$ can be inspected.

The sibling regular-rational and real chapters, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, then consume only that displayed route. Since the route has no coordinate for host multiplication or quotient streams, the product row is synchronized by the paired windows before any downstream read.
\end{proof}

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
