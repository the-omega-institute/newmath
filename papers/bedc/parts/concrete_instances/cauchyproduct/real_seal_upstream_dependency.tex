\label{thm:cauchy-product-real-seal-upstream-dependency}

$$
X,Y,W_X,W_Y,M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y,P,E,H,C,L,N
$$
together with any accepted observation-budget rows when the consumer uses a budget packet. The route exports no quotient stream, selected product limit, host multiplication primitive, trichotomy row, independent real carrier, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with paired-window source admission from \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-source-window-totality} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-source-admission}. These rows force the two source names $X,Y$ to enter the product packet through the paired $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W_X,W_Y$.

Next use budget route exactness from \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-budget-product-lock} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-radius-budget-conservation}. The dyadic observations $Q_X,Q_Y$, source-radius rows $M_X,M_Y$, product row $P$, and ledger row $L$ form the budget route before the classifier can be read.

Apply the paired-window ledger classifier results \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-shared-window-product-classifier} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-real-classifier-obligation}. They identify the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $E$ as downstream of the same finite windows, dyadic observations, and product ledger.

Finally use the root Real seal obligations and consumer exhaustion supplied by \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-real-seal-product-ledger}, \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-real-completion-consumer-lock}, and \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-phase-handoff}. The completion-facing consumer therefore reaches the $\RealUp$ seal only through the displayed upstream route and cannot import any excluded analytic object.
\end{proof}
