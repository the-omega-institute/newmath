$$

$$
together with the selector rows
$$
B,S,W,D,R,E_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B.
$$
The route is distributive only as a finite $\BHist$ product-route certificate: it distributes the same selector window over the two displayed factor windows and then reads the product through the existing $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows. It does not expose host multiplication, quotient stream equality, selected limits, independent real carriers, trichotomy rows, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyProductUp.CauchyProductPacket\_budget\_selector\_distributive\_lock}
\begin{proof}

Finally apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-budget-product-consumer-boundary}. The product consumer can inspect exactly the displayed product rows together with the displayed budget rows, so the two factor-window reads and the product read are locked to one finite route. The excluded analytic objects are absent from both accepted packets, hence they cannot enter the distributive product-route certificate.
\end{proof}
