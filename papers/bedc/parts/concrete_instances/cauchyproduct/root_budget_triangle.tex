\begin{theorem}[CauchyProduct observation-budget triangle]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-observation-budget-triangle}
Any accepted $\CauchyProductUp$ product observation consumed through a $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ packet factors through the triangle whose sides are the paired $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observations, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier row, the $\RealUp$ seal row, and the product and budget rows
$$
W_X,W_Y,M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y,P,E,H,C,L,N
$$
and
$$
E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B.
$$
The source side is the admission route of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-source-admission}; the classifier side is the stability route of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-classifier-stability}; and the budget side is the finite readback boundary of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-budgeted-readback-root-boundary}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the product packet through \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-source-admission}. The two $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ source rows are visible only through the paired $\StreamNameUp$ windows and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation rows, so the product row $P$ has no source-facing route outside the displayed finite window surface.

Next transport that surface through \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-classifier-stability}. The classifier row $E$, the $\RealUp$ seal row, and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows preserve the same finite product packet rather than replacing it by a quotient stream or host product.

Finally enter the observation budget through \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-budgeted-readback-root-boundary}. The budget rows $E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B$ read the same product packet, so the accepted observation is routed through the stated triangle of source windows, dyadic observations, classifier and seal rows, and budget rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyProduct RealAlgOrder Completion handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-realalgorder-completion-handoff}
Every $\RealAlgOrderUp$ multiplication consumer and every $\RealCompletenessUp$ completion-facing consumer that reads an accepted $\CauchyProductUp$ packet receives the same finite product-budget route. The shared route is the phase handoff of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-phase-handoff} with the ledger obligation of \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-ledger-obligation}, read by the downstream chapters \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realalgorder-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realcompleteness-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-root-phase-handoff}. It places every downstream L10 product consumer behind the paired source windows, dyadic observations, product row, classifier row, real seal row, displayed transports, continuations, package provenance, and local naming evidence.

Now apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-ledger-obligation}. The ledger route keeps the radius rows, product-radius accounting, and product classifier visible as finite packet data, so the handoff does not create a second multiplication surface for $\RealAlgOrderUp$ or a selected limit surface for $\RealCompletenessUp$.

The two downstream chapters then consume the same row inventory. The $\RealAlgOrderUp$ chapter may use the packet as its multiplication-facing product route, while the $\RealCompletenessUp$ chapter may use the same route as a completion-facing finite seal input; neither receives any coordinate beyond the displayed product and budget rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyProduct finite product-budget exhaustion]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-finite-product-budget-exhaustion}
The union of the product rows
$$
W_X,W_Y,M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y,P,E,H,C,L,N
$$
and the budget rows
$$
E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B
$$
exhausts the downstream L10 consumer surface of an accepted $\CauchyProductUp$ packet consumed through $\RealObservationBudgetUp$. That surface excludes quotient streams, selected limits, host multiplication primitives, trichotomy rows, independent real carriers, and ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the product side first. The root source admission, classifier stability, and phase handoff theorems make every product-facing consumer pass through $W_X,W_Y,M_X,M_Y,Q_X,Q_Y,P,E,H,C,L,N$ before any downstream real-facing read is available.

Read the budget side next. The observation-budget boundary supplies exactly $E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B$ as the finite consumer rows through which a $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ packet may inspect the product.

Combining the two inventories leaves no unused coordinate in the L10 consumer surface. Since quotient streams, selected limits, host multiplication primitives, trichotomy rows, independent real carriers, and ambient completeness are not rows of either packet, they cannot be exported by the accepted product-budget route.
\end{proof}
