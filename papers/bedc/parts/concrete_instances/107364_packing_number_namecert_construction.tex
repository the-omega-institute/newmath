\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PackingNumberUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-packing-number-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges and Kelley metric-space practice, the $\PackingNumberUp$ packet records the finite separated-center count dual to a displayed metric covering tolerance. It sits beside the finite net route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-epsilon-net-namecert}, the upper-count packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-covering-number-namecert}, the scale-control surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-doubling-metric-namecert}, and the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}. The packet names a lower-bound quantitative ledger as finite BEDC data, not as a maximal subset, a choice-selected packing, a compactness proof, or an ambient cardinality comparison.

\begin{definition}[Metric packing-number carrier]
\label{def:packing-number-carrier}
A $\PackingNumberUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,\varepsilon,U,D,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\MetricUp$ source row carrying the point classifier, distance classifier, and metric-law ledger used by all separation reads.
\item $\varepsilon$ is the positive tolerance row, read through $\RatUp$ or $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ together with the displayed positivity evidence needed by separated-center consumers.
\item $U$ is the finite closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of candidate centers admitted for the tolerance $\varepsilon$.
\item $D$ is the pairwise distance ledger. For every two distinct displayed centers of $U$, it records the metric-distance row in $X$ and the comparison witnessing $\varepsilon$-separation.
\item $B$ is the unary packing-budget row naming the number of center entries exposed by $U$ and certified by the pairwise ledger $D$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, tolerance row, separated-center bundle, pairwise distance ledger, budget row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from any packing-number request through the same rows $X,\varepsilon,U,D,B,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PackingNumberUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\FiniteEpsilonNetUp$, $\MetricCoveringNumberUp$, $\DoublingMetricUp$, $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PackingNumberUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $X,\varepsilon,U,D,B,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for an ambient finite subset, host maximality proof, selected saturated packing, powerset cardinality, compactness, total boundedness, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Metric packing-number NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:packing-number-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PackingNumberUp$ carrier $K=(X,\varepsilon,U,D,B,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PackingNumberUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed metric source, positive tolerance, finite separated-center bundle, pairwise distance ledger, unary packing budget, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item center admission, requiring every entry counted by $B$ to be visible in the same closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row $U$ and admitted by the metric source row $X$;
\item pairwise separation exactness, requiring every two distinct displayed centers to be compared by $D$ through the same metric distance and the same tolerance $\varepsilon$;
\item budget exactness, requiring the exported packing number to count exactly the visible center entries of $U$ certified by the pairwise ledger;
\item tolerance transport, requiring the $\RatUp$ or $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row to remain attached to the separation comparisons under $H$ before any consumer reads the count;
\item ledger nonescape, excluding maximal subset selection, host cardinality comparison, powerset enumeration, open-cover compactness, total-bounded completion, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness as local certificate data.
\end{enumerate}
No metric packing-number obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:packing-number-carrier}. The analytic rows are exactly the metric source $X$, positive tolerance $\varepsilon$, separated-center bundle $U$, pairwise ledger $D$, and unary budget $B$.

Center admission is rowwise. A center can be counted only after it is visible in $U$ and read as a point of the metric source $X$.

Pairwise separation is also rowwise. The ledger $D$ supplies each displayed distance comparison between distinct entries of $U$, and every comparison uses the same positive tolerance row $\varepsilon$.

The budget row then names the quantitative boundary. Because $B$ counts the same visible entries certified by $U$ and $D$, a consumer cannot replace the packet by a host maximal packing, an external enumeration, or a powerset cardinality argument.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly this route. Since the carrier contains none of the excluded compactness, choice, completeness, or maximality coordinates, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metric packing covering-dual handoff]
\label{thm:packing-number-covering-dual-handoff}
Every $\MetricCoveringNumberUp$, $\FiniteEpsilonNetUp$, $\DoublingMetricUp$, or compact-metric consumer reading an accepted $\PackingNumberUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
X,\varepsilon \longmapsto U,D,B \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer receives a separated-center lower-bound ledger before comparing it with a covering-number or finite-net upper-count ledger. The handoff exports no maximal packing, no selected cover, no open-cover compactness proof, no powerset cardinality comparison, no countable-choice schedule, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a covering, finite-net, doubling, or compact-metric consumer of $K$. By \autoref{def:packing-number-carrier}, the consumer can enter the packet only through the displayed metric source $X$ and the displayed positive tolerance row $\varepsilon$.

The separated data are opened next. The bundle $U$ names the candidate centers, the ledger $D$ certifies pairwise separation through the metric distance row, and $B$ records the resulting unary packing budget.

The covering-dual comparison is external to the packing packet. A covering-number or finite-net consumer may compare its own center budget with $B$, but only after both packets expose their metric source, tolerance, finite bundle, and distance ledger rows.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same route under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Applying \autoref{thm:packing-number-namecert-obligations}, the consumer cannot replace the finite separated ledger by a selected maximal packing, compactness argument, powerset cardinality comparison, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{proof}
