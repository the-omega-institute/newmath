It sits after the located entourage surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-uniform-space-namecert}, the Real-induced uniformity of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-uniform-structure-namecert}, the filter carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the net handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, the finite window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Uniformly Cauchy filter carrier]
\label{def:uniformly-cauchy-filter-carrier}
A $\UniformlyCauchyFilterUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(U,L,F,N,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,Q)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the $\LocatedUniformSpaceUp$ entourage row, exposing only named entourage requests and located refinement witnesses.
\item $L$ is the uniform-Cauchy schedule, assigning each displayed entourage request a finite filter-stage certificate.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ row that reads the accepted filter base after the located entourage and schedule rows have been displayed.
\item $N$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ handoff row, available only as a finite net witness for the same entourage request.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row inspected by Real-and-Completion consumers.
\item $D$ is the positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the window and entourage requests.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the displayed windows under the dyadic ledger.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, downstream of the located uniform, filter, net, window, dyadic, and regular readback rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the located-uniform, schedule, filter, net, window, dyadic, regular-readback, Real-seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a located entourage request through $U,L,F,N,W,D,R,E,H,P,Q$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformlyCauchyFilterUp$, $\LocatedUniformSpaceUp$, $\RealUniformStructureUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $Q$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformlyCauchyFilterUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $U,L,F,N,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,Q$. It has no coordinate for quotient filters, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniformly Cauchy filter NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniformly-cauchy-filter-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformlyCauchyFilterUp$ carrier
$$
G=(U,L,F,N,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,Q),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\UniformlyCauchyFilterUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the located-uniform entourage row, uniform-Cauchy schedule, CauchyFilter row, CauchyNet handoff, StreamName windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item located-entourage adequacy, requiring each Cauchy request to read $U$ before any filter, net, or numeric row is interpreted;
\item uniform-Cauchy scheduling, requiring $L$ to exhibit a finite filter-stage certificate for every displayed entourage request;
\item filter and net handoff, requiring $F$ and $N$ to consume the same located entourage and schedule rows rather than a quotient filter;
\item finite readback, requiring $W,D,R,E$ to expose only finite windows, dyadic tolerances, regular rational readbacks, and the downstream Real seal;
\item transport, replay, provenance, and naming through $H,C,P,Q$ for the same displayed route;
\item nonescape for quotient filters, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, and ambient Real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No uniformly Cauchy filter obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:uniformly-cauchy-filter-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the located entourage row, the schedule row, the filter and net handoff rows, the finite numeric readback rows, and the structural rows.

The uniformly Cauchy assertion is schedule-local. Each entourage request is first read from $U$, and the finite stage evidence in $L$ is the only admissible route by which the request reaches $F$ or $N$.

The completion-facing read is then numeric but still finite. Stream windows $W$ are inspected under the dyadic ledger $D$, regular rational readback is recorded in $R$, and the Real seal $E$ remains downstream of those displayed rows.

Finally, $H,C,P,Q$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since quotient filters, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, and ambient Real completeness are not carrier rows, they cannot be exported by the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniformly Cauchy filter located-uniform handoff]
\label{thm:uniformly-cauchy-filter-located-uniform-handoff}
Every Real-and-Completion consumer of an accepted $\UniformlyCauchyFilterUp$ packet reads the Cauchy-filter route only through the located entourage row, the uniform-Cauchy schedule, the CauchyFilter row, the CauchyNet handoff, StreamName windows, positive DyadicRatCore tolerances, RegSeqRat readbacks, the downstream Real seal, and the structural rows $H,C,P,Q$. The handoff exports no quotient filter, selected limit, countable-choice selector, host equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the accepted carrier $G=(U,L,F,N,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,Q)$. Carrier admission gives the consumer no access beyond these rows.

Use the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:uniformly-cauchy-filter-namecert-obligations}. It forces every filter read to pass through the located entourage row $U$ and the finite schedule $L$ before either the CauchyFilter row $F$ or the CauchyNet handoff $N$ can be inspected.

The finite numeric handoff then follows the displayed order $W,D,R,E$. The structural rows preserve that order under componentwise transport and replay, so the consumer obtains a located-uniform Cauchy route without receiving any excluded quotient, selection, host-equality, or completeness coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniformly Cauchy filter Real completion boundary]
\label{thm:uniformly-cauchy-filter-real-completion-boundary}
The $\RealUp$ seal reached by an accepted $\UniformlyCauchyFilterUp$ packet is downstream of the located uniform data, the uniform-Cauchy schedule, the filter and net handoff rows, finite windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, and regular rational readback. \begin{proof}

The remaining rows $H,C,P,Q$ only transport, replay, source, and name this route. Hence the Real completion boundary is a downstream seal for the packet rather than a quotient-filter construction or a host equality principle.
\end{proof}
