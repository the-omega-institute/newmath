\begin{definition}[MonoidalCat singleton tensor instance]
\label{def:monoidalcat-singleton-tensor-instance}
The singleton $\mathsf{BHist}$ monoidal-category instance uses the unary-continuation category of \autoref{thm:category-unary-continuation-stability-law-package} as its underlying $\CategoryUp$ carrier. Its objects are carried unary histories, its displayed hom rows are $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)$ rows, and its tensor is continuation append:
$$
a\otimes b := \mathsf{append}(a,b),\qquad
f\otimes g := \mathsf{append}(f,g).
$$
The unit object is $\emp$. The associator row from $(x\otimes y)\otimes z$ to $x\otimes(y\otimes z)$ and the left and right unitor rows from $\emp\otimes x$ and $x\otimes\emp$ to $x$ are the $\hsame$ witnesses supplied by continuation associativity and the empty-continuation unit laws on the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MonoidalCat singleton tensor carrier]
\label{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-tensor-carrier}
In the singleton tensor instance of \autoref{def:monoidalcat-singleton-tensor-instance}, tensoring carried unary objects remains a carried unary object. If
$$
\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(c,d,g)
$$
are displayed hom histories, then the tensor hom history $\mathsf{append}(f,g)$ is carried by the same unary-continuation hom surface from $\mathsf{append}(a,c)$ to $\mathsf{append}(b,d)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unary object closure is the ordinary append closure for unary $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows.

For hom rows, first use the endpoint stability law package of \autoref{cor:category-hom-carrier-stability-law-package} to read both displayed morphisms as continuation tails over their endpoints. Then apply \autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-comp-closed} to the compatible tail rows after appending the source and target endpoints.

The resulting composite row has exactly the source $\mathsf{append}(a,c)$, target $\mathsf{append}(b,d)$, and displayed morphism $\mathsf{append}(f,g)$ required by the tensor carrier surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MonoidalCat singleton tensor preserves identity homs]
\label{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-tensor-identity-hom}
In the singleton tensor instance of \autoref{def:monoidalcat-singleton-tensor-instance}, let $a$ and $b$ be carried unary objects. The tensor of the identity hom histories $\emp:a\to a$ and $\emp:b\to b$ is classified as the identity hom on $a\otimes b$. Concretely, $\mathsf{append}(\emp,\emp)$ is $\hsame$ to $\emp$, and the row gives
$$
\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(a,b),\mathsf{append}(a,b),\emp).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The identity rows for $a$ and $b$ are the empty continuation rows supplied by \autoref{thm:category-unary-continuation-stability-law-package}. Unary append closure gives the tensor object $\mathsf{append}(a,b)$ as a carried unary object.

Tensoring the two displayed identity histories gives $\mathsf{append}(\emp,\emp)$. The empty-continuation unit laws of \autoref{thm:concrete-history-monoid-laws} identify this displayed history with $\emp$ by $\hsame$.

Transport the identity row on $\mathsf{append}(a,b)$ along that $\hsame$ classifier row. The resulting displayed hom carrier is exactly $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(a,b),\mathsf{append}(a,b),\emp)$, so the tensor preserves identity hom histories on the concrete singleton carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MonoidalCat singleton associator and unitors]
\label{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-associator-unitors}
For carried unary histories $x$, $y$, and $z$ in the singleton tensor instance, the displayed histories $(x\otimes y)\otimes z$ and $x\otimes(y\otimes z)$ are related by $\hsame$. The displayed histories $\emp\otimes x$ and $x\otimes\emp$ are each related to $x$ by $\hsame$ on the same concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The associator row is continuation associativity applied to the three visible tails $x$, $y$, and $z$. Both bracketings reduce to the same iterated continuation history, so the classifier row is the corresponding $\hsame$ witness.

For the left unitor, use the left empty-continuation law to identify $\mathsf{append}(\emp,x)$ with $x$. For the right unitor, use the right empty-continuation law to identify $\mathsf{append}(x,\emp)$ with $x$.

The category continuation-morphism tail theorems transport these three $\hsame$ rows through the displayed hom carrier, so the associator and both unitors live on the concrete unary-continuation category rather than on an external categorical carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MonoidalCat singleton coherence laws]
\label{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-coherence-laws}
In the singleton tensor instance, the pentagon and triangle diagrams commute under the morphism classifier. For any carried unary histories appearing in either diagram, the two displayed paths have $\hsame$-equal classifier rows because both paths reduce to the same iterated $\ContR$ tail.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the pentagon, expand each edge into the associator row of \autoref{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-associator-unitors}. The two boundary paths are the two standard ways to reassociate a fourfold continuation tail, and the category fourfold associativity witness \autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-fourfold-assoc-witness} identifies their displayed histories by $\hsame$.

For the triangle, expand the associator and the two unitor rows. The left path and the right path both normalize to the same twofold continuation tail after applying the empty-continuation unit laws.

Finally compose the classifier rows by transitivity of $\hsame$. The displayed hom carrier is preserved along the comparison by the category stability law package, so the commuting diagrams are certified inside the BEDC morphism classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MonoidalCat NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:monoidalcat-namecert-obligation-surface}
The singleton tensor instance assembles the following paper-side $\NameCert_{\MonoidalCatUp}$ obligation surface:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Traditional target: a monoidal category.
  \item BEDC source: $\CategoryUp$, $\ContR$, $\hsame$, and $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}$.
  \item Carrier: unary $\mathsf{BHist}$ objects and continuation hom histories.
  \item Classifier: $\hsame$ on displayed object and hom histories.
  \item Stability: tensor carrier closure, identity-hom preservation, associator rows, unitor rows, pentagon coherence, and triangle coherence.
  \item Ledger: tensor tails and the two coherence-path ledgers.
  \item Core theorem list: \autoref{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-tensor-carrier}, \autoref{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-tensor-identity-hom}, \autoref{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-associator-unitors}, \autoref{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-coherence-laws}, and \autoref{thm:category-unary-continuation-stability-law-package}.
  \item Exactness: classifier equality of the displayed coherence paths.
  \item Standard bridge: absent.
  \item Status: paper-side obligation surface.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier fields are supplied by the unary-continuation category and its $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}$ rows. The tensor field is the append operation in \autoref{def:monoidalcat-singleton-tensor-instance}.

The stability fields are discharged by \autoref{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-tensor-carrier}, \autoref{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-tensor-identity-hom}, and \autoref{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-associator-unitors}. The coherence fields are discharged by \autoref{thm:monoidalcat-singleton-coherence-laws}.

The ledger records only the displayed continuation tails and the two coherence paths. Exactness is the statement that the two paths in each coherence diagram are equal under $\hsame$, and no standard external monoidal-category bridge is asserted here.
\end{proof}
