\section{Singleton Empty-History Ring Instance}
\label{sec:ring-singleton-empty-history-instance}

\begin{definition}[Singleton empty-history RingUp instance]
\label{def:ring-singleton-empty-history-instance}
Let $A_{\emp}$ be the singleton history source of
Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source} at $a_0=\emp$. The
classifier $R_{\emp}$ is the carrier-aware singleton relation
$$
R_{\emp}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow
A_{\emp}(h)\land A_{\emp}(k)\land \hsame(h,k).
$$
Define the addition, multiplication, zero, one, and negation rows by
$$
h+_{\emp}k:=\emp,\qquad
h\cdot_{\emp}k:=\emp,\qquad
0_{\emp}:=\emp,\qquad
1_{\emp}:=\emp,\qquad
-_{\emp}h:=\emp.
$$
Equivalently, every binary operation is append-trivial on the singleton
endpoint: its operation tree may record two inputs, but its exposed output is
the empty history. The tuple
$(A_{\emp},R_{\emp},+_{\emp},0_{\emp},-_{\emp},\cdot_{\emp},1_{\emp})$
is the concrete singleton empty-history $\RingUp$ instance.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton empty-history RingUp laws]
\label{thm:ring-singleton-empty-history-laws}
The tuple of \autoref{def:ring-singleton-empty-history-instance} satisfies
the $\RingUp$ algebraic laws: additive abelian-group laws, multiplicative
monoid laws, left and right distributivity, left and right zero absorption,
and congruence of both operations under $R_{\emp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier is inhabited by $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$. Every displayed operation
returns $\emp$, so operation closure, the closedness of $0_{\emp}$ and
$1_{\emp}$, and the closure of additive inverses are all the singleton carrier
witness for $\emp$.

For the additive laws, both sides of each unit, inverse, associativity, and
commutativity comparison reduce to $\emp$. The corresponding $R_{\emp}$ row is
therefore assembled from the two singleton endpoint witnesses and reflexivity
of $\hsame$. This is the constant-operation specialization of the singleton
AbGroup rows in \autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-abgroup-laws}.

For the multiplicative monoid laws, both products in each unit and
associativity comparison again reduce to $\emp$. The singleton endpoint
witnesses and $\hsame$ reflexivity provide the required classifier row, matching
the singleton monoid and group rows of
\autoref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws}.

For operation congruence, suppose the inputs are paired by $R_{\emp}$. The
source and target additions are both $\emp$, and the source and target
multiplications are both $\emp$. Thus congruence is the same packed
$R_{\emp}(\emp,\emp)$ witness in both cases.

For distributivity and zero absorption, every term on either side of each law
is definitionally $\emp$. The endpoint comparisons are consequently supplied
by $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$, while the classifier endpoint fields are the singleton
carrier witnesses for $\emp$. Transitivity of $\hsame$ is used only to
transport any named input endpoint through its carrier witness before repacking
the final $R_{\emp}$ comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton empty-history RingUp naming certificate]
\label{thm:ring-singleton-empty-history-namecert}
The instance of \autoref{def:ring-singleton-empty-history-instance} supplies a
concrete $\NameCert_{\RingUp}$ package for the one-element ring. Its ten-field
fit is: traditional target the one-element ring; BEDC source
$\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier $R_{\emp}$; stability the
singleton transport rows under $\hsame$; ledger the constant-operation
provenance for $+_{\emp}$, $0_{\emp}$, $-_{\emp}$, $\cdot_{\emp}$, and
$1_{\emp}$; core theorem \autoref{thm:ring-singleton-empty-history-laws};
exactness singleton endpoint classification; standard bridge the terminal
ring; and consumer boundary the exposed one-endpoint operation table.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The source and carrier fields are the $\mathsf{BHist}$ singleton predicate
$A_{\emp}$, and the classifier field is $R_{\emp}$. The stability fields follow
by transporting every carried endpoint through its witness to $\emp$ and then
using transitivity of $\hsame$ to repack the same carrier-aware classifier.

The operation-law fields are exactly
\autoref{thm:ring-singleton-empty-history-laws}. They include the additive
AbGroup part, the multiplicative Monoid part, both distributivity rows, zero
absorption, and congruence for the two binary operations.

The ledger records only that each displayed operation tree has endpoint
$\emp$. Since $R_{\emp}$ exposes precisely the two endpoint carrier witnesses
and their $\hsame$ comparison, exactness says that a public endpoint belongs to
the certificate exactly when it is the singleton empty-history endpoint. This
is the terminal one-element ring table read through BEDC histories.
\end{proof}
