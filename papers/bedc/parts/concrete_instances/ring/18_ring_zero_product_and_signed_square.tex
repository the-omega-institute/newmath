\begin{proof}
First derive the additive right-zero law from
Theorem~\ref{thm:ring-add-right-zero} and the additive right-inverse law from
Theorem~\ref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse}. These give the removable zero and
inverse pairs used on both sides of the equivalence.

For the forward implication, add $y$ to the right of the comparison
$x+(-y)\hsame0$. Associativity rewrites $(x+(-y))+y$ as $x+((-y)+y)$,
commutativity and the right-inverse law collapse $(-y)+y$ to $0$, and the
right-zero law removes that terminal zero. The right endpoint $0+y$ reduces to
$y$ by the left-zero law, leaving $x\hsame y$.

For the reverse implication, transport $x\hsame y$ through addition congruence
against the common summand $-y$. The right side becomes $y+(-y)$, which
Theorem~\ref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse} collapses to $0$, yielding
$x+(-y)\hsame0$ by transitivity.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Ring multiplication zero absorption]
\label{thm:ring-mul-zero-absorption}
For BEDC history operations satisfying additive associativity, left zero,
left inverse, congruence, and both distributive laws, multiplication by zero on
either side is internally the same as zero.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RingUp.ring\_mul\_zero\_absorption}
\begin{proof}
First cancel a duplicated summand \(a\hsame a+a\) by adding the left inverse,
reassociating, and using the left-zero law. Then apply distributivity to
\(x\cdot(0+0)\) and \((0+0)\cdot x\), transport along \(0+0\hsame0\), and use
the duplicate-cancellation step on the two resulting products.
\end{proof}
