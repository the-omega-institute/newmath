\begin{proof}
First derive the additive right-zero law from
Theorem~\ref{thm:ring-add-right-zero} and the additive right-inverse law from
Theorem~\ref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse}. These give the removable zero and
inverse pairs used on both sides of the equivalence.

For the forward implication, add $y$ to the right of the comparison
$x+(-y)\hsame0$. Associativity rewrites $(x+(-y))+y$ as $x+((-y)+y)$,
commutativity and the right-inverse law collapse $(-y)+y$ to $0$, and the
right-zero law removes that terminal zero. The right endpoint $0+y$ reduces to
$y$ by the left-zero law, leaving $x\hsame y$.

For the reverse implication, transport $x\hsame y$ through addition congruence
against the common summand $-y$. The right side becomes $y+(-y)$, which
Theorem~\ref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse} collapses to $0$, yielding
$x+(-y)\hsame0$ by transitivity.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Ring left distributivity over subtraction]

\begin{proposition}[Ring forgets to Monoid certificate]
\label{prop:ring-forgets-monoid-certificate}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with carrier classifier $\sim_R$, zero $0_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $(-)_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and identity $1_R$. If the additive operations $0_R$, $+_R$, and $(-)_R$, the additive abelian-group stability rows, both distributive rows, the zero-multiplication rows, and every ledger entry whose operation-tree provenance uses a dropped additive constructor are omitted, then the retained carrier, source, monoid pattern, classifier, monoid stability fields, and multiplicative ledger form a $\MonoidUp(R)$ certificate. The carrier $R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, identity $1_R$, and classifier $\sim_R$ are unchanged.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:ring-certificate-obligations}, the given certificate supplies carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data for $\RingUp(R)$. The retained package keeps exactly the rows whose endpoint claims and expression provenance use only the carrier classifier, multiplication, and the multiplicative identity.

By \autoref{def:ring-carrier}, a ring carrier consists of a carrier with classifier, addition, zero, additive inverse, multiplication, one, and ring law fields. Removing the additive coordinates leaves the same carrier $R$, the same classifier $\sim_R$, the same multiplication $\cdot_R:R\times R\to R$, and the same identity $1_R$. These are precisely the carrier data listed in \autoref{def:monoid-carrier}.

By \autoref{def:ring-source-specification}, the source side of a ring is an additive $\AbGroupUp$ and a multiplicative $\MonoidUp$ on the same carrier and classifier. Suppressing the additive source leaves the certified multiplicative source over $R$, $\cdot_R$, $1_R$, and $\sim_R$. This is the source required by \autoref{def:monoid-source-specification}.

By \autoref{def:ring-pattern-specification}, ring expressions are formed from $0$, $1$, $+$, unary minus, and multiplication. Restricting to expressions containing no additive constructor leaves expression formation from $1_R$, carried endpoints, and binary multiplication, exactly the monoid pattern of \autoref{def:monoid-pattern-specification}.

By \autoref{def:ring-classifier-specification}, the ring classifier is the shared carrier sameness relation, congruent for both operations. Retaining the relation $\sim_R$ and its multiplication-congruence row gives the classifier required by \autoref{def:monoid-classifier-specification}. The addition-congruence row mentions the omitted operation and is not a monoid obligation.

For stability, \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} lists additive abelian-group laws, multiplicative monoid laws, operation congruence, left distributivity, right distributivity, and zero multiplication laws. The multiplicative monoid laws give associativity and the left and right identity laws for $1_R$ under $\sim_R$; the retained multiplication-congruence row gives congruence in both arguments. The carrier-classifier rows are unchanged by the projection, so reflexivity and transitivity remain available. Together these are exactly the rows listed in \autoref{def:monoid-stability-certificate}. The omitted additive, distributive, and zero-multiplication rows mention $0_R$, $+_R$, or $(-)_R$, so they have no corresponding $\MonoidUp$ obligation.

Finally, \autoref{def:ring-ledger-policy} records the shared carrier ledger and the operation tree for ring expressions. Removing ledger entries whose provenance uses a dropped additive constructor leaves carrier witnesses, the identity endpoint, and multiplication trees for monoid expressions. This is the ledger policy of \autoref{def:monoid-ledger-policy}. Hence the retained data satisfy the obligations named in \autoref{def:monoid-certificate-obligations}, with $R$, $\cdot_R$, $1_R$, and $\sim_R$ copied from the original $\RingUp(R)$ certificate.
\end{proof}

Let $R$ carry a history-level $\RingUp$ certificate with classifier $\sim_R$,

$x-_R y$ for $x+_R(-_R y)$. For every carried triple $a,b,c$,
$$
(a-_R b)\cdot_R c\sim_R(a\cdot_R c)-_R(b\cdot_R c).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target right distributivity over subtraction;
BEDC source $\RingUp$ over the carried endpoints of $R$; carrier the ring
carrier; classifier $\sim_R$; stability from the addition congruence and right
distributivity fields of \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}; ledger the
three endpoints and the hidden negative summand; core theorem this theorem;
exactness the displayed subtraction law; standard bridge ordinary ring
distribution over differences; downstream consumer polynomial and module
linear-expression normalization; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By the difference abbreviation, the left side is
$(a+_R(-_R b))\cdot_R c$. Right distributivity gives
$$
(a+_R(-_R b))\cdot_R c
\sim_R
(a\cdot_R c)+_R((-_R b)\cdot_R c).
$$
The left-negative product theorem
\autoref{thm:ring-mul-neg-left-eq-neg-mul}, read in this certificate, gives
$$
(-_R b)\cdot_R c\sim_R-_R(b\cdot_R c).
$$
Addition congruence transports this comparison in the right summand while the
left summand $a\cdot_R c$ is held fixed. Hence
$$
(a\cdot_R c)+_R((-_R b)\cdot_R c)
\sim_R
(a\cdot_R c)+_R(-_R(b\cdot_R c)).
$$
The final expression is $(a\cdot_R c)-_R(b\cdot_R c)$ by the same difference
abbreviation. Transitivity of $\sim_R$ composes the distributivity comparison
with the transported sign comparison and yields the displayed law.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Ring forgets to AbGroup certificate]
\label{prop:ring-forgets-abgroup-certificate}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp(R)$ certificate with carrier classifier $\sim_R$, zero $0_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and identity $1_R$. If the multiplicative monoid source package, $\cdot_R$, $1_R$, the multiplicative monoid stability rows, both distributive rows, the zero-multiplication rows, and every ledger entry whose operation-tree provenance uses a multiplicative constructor are omitted, then the retained carrier, source, additive pattern, classifier, additive stability fields, and additive ledger form an $\AbGroupUp(R)$ certificate. The carrier $R$, addition $+_R$, zero $0_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, and classifier $\sim_R$ are unchanged.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:ring-certificate-obligations}, the given certificate supplies carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data for $\RingUp(R)$. The retained package keeps exactly the rows whose endpoint claims and expression provenance use only the carrier classifier, additive zero, addition, and additive inverse.

By \autoref{def:ring-carrier}, a ring carrier consists of a carrier with classifier, addition, zero, additive inverse, multiplication, one, and ring law fields. Removing the multiplicative coordinates leaves the same carrier $R$, the same classifier $\sim_R$, the same addition $+_R:R\times R\to R$, the same zero $0_R$, and the same additive inverse $-_R$. Reading addition as the abelian-group operation, zero as its identity, and $-_R$ as its inverse operation gives precisely the carrier data required by \autoref{def:abgroup-carrier}.

By \autoref{def:ring-source-specification}, the source side of a ring is an additive $\AbGroupUp$ and a multiplicative $\MonoidUp$ on the same carrier and classifier. Suppressing the multiplicative source leaves the certified additive $\AbGroupUp$ source over $R$, $+_R$, $0_R$, $-_R$, and $\sim_R$. Unpacking that source through \autoref{def:abgroup-source-specification} gives the certified group carrier plus commutativity field required for the abelian-group source.

By \autoref{def:ring-classifier-specification}, the ring classifier is the shared carrier sameness relation, congruent for both operations. Retaining $\sim_R$ and the addition-congruence row gives the classifier required by \autoref{def:abgroup-classifier-specification}. The multiplication-congruence row mentions the omitted operation and has no abelian-group obligation.

By \autoref{def:ring-pattern-specification}, ring expressions are formed from $0_R$, $1_R$, $+_R$, $-_R$, and $\cdot_R$. Restricting to expressions containing no multiplicative constructor leaves carried endpoints, $0_R$, addition, and additive inverse. Together with the retained commutativity permission from the additive abelian-group certificate, this is the abelian-group pattern of \autoref{def:abgroup-pattern-specification}.

By \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}, the stability certificate contains additive abelian-group laws, multiplicative monoid laws, addition and multiplication congruence, distributivity, and zero multiplication laws. The retained additive abelian-group laws supply the group stability fields, commutativity under the classifier, and the compatibility data for commutativity with inverse and operation congruence listed in \autoref{def:abgroup-stability-certificate}. The retained addition-congruence row is the operation-congruence row for the abelian-group operation. The multiplicative monoid rows, multiplication congruence, distributive rows, and zero-multiplication rows all mention $\cdot_R$ or $1_R$, so they are not obligations for $\AbGroupUp$.

Finally, \autoref{def:ring-ledger-policy} records the shared carrier ledger and the operation tree for ring expressions. Removing entries whose provenance uses $1_R$ or $\cdot_R$ leaves carrier witnesses, the additive identity endpoint, addition trees, additive inverse trees, and the commutativity-use records from the additive source. This is the group-expression provenance and commutativity ledger of \autoref{def:abgroup-ledger-policy}. Hence the retained data satisfy the obligations named in \autoref{def:abgroup-certificate-obligations}, with $R$, $+_R$, $0_R$, $-_R$, and $\sim_R$ copied from the original $\RingUp(R)$ certificate.
\end{proof}
