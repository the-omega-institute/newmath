\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchySelectionFunctionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-selection-function-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\CauchySelectionFunctionUp$ packet records the finite selector interface by which a Real and Completion consumer asks for a displayed regular Cauchy window without importing countable choice. It sits after the Cauchy modulus surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, the finite observation interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the displayed modulus-selection route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-modulus-selection-namecert}, and the selector-budget packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyselectorbudget-namecert}. It provides the finite selector row used by the Real completeness agenda of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}; it is not a countable-choice principle, quotient stream object, selected global limit, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy selection-function carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-selection-function-carrier}
A $\CauchySelectionFunctionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(Q,M,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the request row, naming the precision request, source regular Cauchy name, and completion-facing consumer boundary.
\item $M$ is the modulus schedule row, read through the $\CauchyModulusUp$ and $\CauchyModulusSelectionUp$ surfaces for the displayed request.
\item $W$ is the selected finite $\StreamNameUp$ window opened by $M$ for this request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular readback row assembled from the selected window.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger containing the dyadic radius, slack, comparison, and scale data used by the readback.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, available only after $Q,M,W,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the request, schedule, selected window, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the selector route
$$
Q,M \longmapsto W \longmapsto R,D \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchySelectionFunctionUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\CauchyModulusSelectionUp$, $\RegularCauchySelectorBudgetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchySelectionFunctionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $Q,M,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for countable choice, quotient streams, selected global limits, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy selection-function NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-selection-function-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchySelectionFunctionUp$ carrier
$$
S=(Q,M,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchySelectionFunctionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the request row $Q$, modulus schedule $M$, selected finite window $W$, regular readback $R$, dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, terminal real seal $E$, componentwise transport $H$, continuation replay $C$, package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$.

The obligation surface also includes request-schedule compatibility, finite-window visibility, regular-readback exactness, dyadic tolerance compatibility, real-seal nonescape, and stability under the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows. No obligation is stored outside $Q,M,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-selection-function-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the request, schedule, selected-window, readback, dyadic, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows listed in the statement.

Request-schedule compatibility is the role of $Q$ and $M$. The schedule is read as a displayed modulus-selection row for the request, and the selected finite window $W$ is downstream of that schedule rather than a separate choice function.

Regular readback and dyadic tolerance exactness are the roles of $R$ and $D$. The carrier places both rows after $W$, so the readback is assembled only from displayed observations and the displayed dyadic ledger.

The terminal real seal $E$ is downstream of $Q,M,W,R,D$, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since no further coordinates occur in the packet, countable choice, quotient streams, selected global limits, host real equality, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness cannot be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy selection-function regular readback]
\label{thm:cauchy-selection-function-regular-readback}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\CauchySelectionFunctionUp$ carrier
$$
S=(Q,M,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
must first read the displayed request row $Q$, the modulus schedule $M$, the selected finite window $W$, and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$. The regular readback row $R$ and real seal row $E$ then expose only the finite selector route
$$
Q,M \longmapsto W,D \longmapsto R \longmapsto E
$$
together with the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Thus the selector interface supplies a finite BEDC service for Real and Completion consumers without providing countable choice, quotient stream equality, a selected global limit, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ read. By the carrier definition, the readback rows available to that consumer are $R$ and $E$, and both are downstream of the request row, schedule row, selected window, and dyadic ledger.

The selected window is finite because it is the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ row $W$. The schedule $M$ only names the request-facing modulus surface that opens $W$; it does not add a second stream, a quotient stream, or an infinite family of choices.

The dyadic ledger $D$ records the tolerance data used by the same finite window, and $R$ assembles the regular rational readback from those displayed rows. The real seal $E$ can therefore be consumed only as the terminal row of the route through $Q,M,W,D,R$.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the route under componentwise transport and continuation replay. The excluded objects have no carrier coordinate, so a consumer cannot read countable choice, quotient stream equality, selected global limits, host real equality, or ambient completeness from this packet.
\end{proof}
