\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocallyCompactHausdorffUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-locally-compact-hausdorff-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bourbaki on locally compact Hausdorff spaces, the $\LocallyCompactHausdorffUp$ packet records the point-local compact-neighbourhood and point-separation rows that a constructive topology consumer may inspect without invoking open-cover compactness or choice of neighbourhood bases. It sits over the indexed-open surface of $\TopologyUp$, the separated uniform route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorff-uniform-space-namecert}, the compact-metric window of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the formal-ball observation interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-formalball-namecert}, and the point-local metric compactness packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locally-compact-metric-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Locally compact Hausdorff carrier]
\label{def:locally-compact-hausdorff-carrier}
A $\LocallyCompactHausdorffUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(T,U,x,V,K,F,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ or $\HausdorffUniformSpaceUp$ source row, including indexed open reads, separated neighbourhood reads, and the point classifier used by topology consumers.
\item $U$ is a neighbourhood row around the inspected point $x$, displayed through the same topology or separated uniform source.
\item $V$ is the compact neighbourhood inclusion row, witnessing that the inspected neighbourhood is carried by the compact window $K$.
\item $K$ is a finite compactness window read through $\CompactMetricUp$ or a compact formal-ball window from $\FormalBallUp$.
\item $F$ is the $\FormalBallUp$ or finite-window replay row used when a consumer requests radius-indexed local observations inside $K$.
\item $S$ is the Hausdorff separation row: distinct point reads are separated only by displayed neighbourhood or entourage witnesses over $T$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the topology or uniform source, inspected point, local neighbourhood, compact window, formal-ball window, separation row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from local topology reads to compact-window, formal-ball, and Hausdorff-separation consumers.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocallyCompactHausdorffUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\HausdorffUniformSpaceUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\FormalBallUp$, $\LocallyCompactMetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocallyCompactHausdorffUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The packet has no coordinate for arbitrary open-cover compactness, selected neighbourhood bases, quotient point equality, excluded middle, countable choice, or ambient local compactness outside the displayed carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocallyCompactHausdorff carrier admission]
\label{thm:locally-compact-hausdorff-carrier-admission}
For every accepted $\LocallyCompactHausdorffUp$ packet $L=(T,U,x,V,K,F,S,H,C,P,N)$, carrier admission is rowwise over the displayed topology or separated-uniform source, inspected point, local neighbourhood row, compact-window inclusion, formal-ball or compact-metric finite window, Hausdorff separation row, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local name. Admission cannot be obtained from an unrecorded host topology, an open-cover compactness theorem, a selected basis, quotient point equality, or ambient local compactness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:locally-compact-hausdorff-carrier}. The packet exposes exactly $T,U,x,V,K,F,S,H,C,P,N$, so every accepted read begins with the topology or separated-uniform source and the inspected point.

Next read the compact side. The row $V$ ties the neighbourhood $U$ to the compact window $K$, while $F$ supplies only the finite formal-ball or compact-metric observations cited by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-formalball-namecert}.

Finally read the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. They transport, replay, record provenance, and bind the local name for the same finite packet. Since the carrier contains no host topology, open-cover compactness, selected basis, quotient equality, or ambient local-compactness coordinate, none can enter through carrier admission.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocallyCompactHausdorff compact-neighbourhood nonescape]
\label{thm:locally-compact-hausdorff-compact-neighbourhood-nonescape}
Every local compact-neighbourhood read of an accepted $\LocallyCompactHausdorffUp$ packet factors through
$$
T,U,x,V,K,F \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The read may use compact-metric and formal-ball windows, and may compare with the $\LocallyCompactMetricUp$ point-local route when a metric presentation is displayed. When completion-facing readers consume the local compact neighbourhood, the compact-window row may be refined through the finite located-neighbourhood radius handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-compactness-namecert}, not through open-cover compactness. The read cannot promote the window to arbitrary open-cover compactness, a global compactness theorem, a selected local base, quotient completion equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the neighbourhood rows $T,U,x,V$. They name the source topology or separated uniform space, the inspected point, and the local neighbourhood inclusion before any compactness consumer can inspect the packet.

Use the compact-window rows $K$ and $F$. The compact metric certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert} and the formal-ball interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-formalball-namecert} provide finite windows only; the metric local-compactness sibling \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locally-compact-metric-namecert} is read as a displayed point-local comparison, not as an ambient theorem.

Finally replay through $H,C,P,N$. These rows keep the compact neighbourhood inside the same finite carrier route, so open-cover compactness, selected neighbourhood bases, quotient completion equality, global compactness, and ambient completeness have no row from which to escape.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocallyCompactHausdorff separation transport]
\label{thm:locally-compact-hausdorff-separation-transport}
For every accepted $\LocallyCompactHausdorffUp$ packet, Hausdorff separation is transported only through the displayed row $S$ over the same topology or separated-uniform source $T$ and the same structural route $H,C,P,N$. If two point reads are moved by $\hsame$, their separated-neighbourhood or separated-entourage witnesses are moved componentwise with the compact-neighbourhood rows; no host equality, quotient identification, global apartness principle, or classical point separation is added.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the separated source. When $T$ is read through $\HausdorffUniformSpaceUp$, the separated-uniform packet supplies the visible separation route; when it is read through $\TopologyUp$, the row $S$ displays the neighbourhood witnesses required for the inspected point reads.

Apply componentwise transport $H$. The transport row moves the source, neighbourhood, compact-window, formal-ball, and separation data together, so a separated read cannot be detached from the local compact carrier.

Finally use replay and provenance, $C,P,N$. They record the same separation route for downstream consumers and keep quotient equality, host point equality, global apartness, and classical separation outside the packet.
\end{proof}
