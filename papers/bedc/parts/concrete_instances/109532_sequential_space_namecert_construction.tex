\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SequentialSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-sequential-space-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Franklin and Engelking on sequential spaces, the $\SequentialSpaceUp$ packet records the finite convergence interface by which sequence-indexed observations may be consumed as topological data without importing open-cover compactness, quotient point equality, or a selected subsequence principle. It sits beside the limit carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-limit-space-namecert}, the Cauchy source interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyspace-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the indexed-open topology surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Later completion and compactness consumers may read sequential convergence only through this displayed packet.

\begin{definition}[SequentialSpace carrier]
\label{def:sequential-space-carrier}
A $\SequentialSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(I,W,L,T,Q,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the sequence-index row, naming the finite initial segment, tail request, and comparison scale read by the consumer.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row, exposing only the finite observations of the sequence at the requested indices.
\item $L$ is the $\LimitSpaceUp$ convergence row, recording the limit-facing test supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-limit-space-namecert}.
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ open-test row, recording the indexed open neighbourhood test through which sequential convergence is observed.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchySpaceUp$ comparison row when the sequence is also read by a completion or Cauchy consumer.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ endpoint row when the displayed observations are regular real-name reads.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the sequence index, StreamName window, limit row, topology row, Cauchy comparison row, Real endpoint row, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a sequential-convergence request through $I,W,L,T,Q,R,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SequentialSpaceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\LimitSpaceUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\CauchySpaceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, quotient point equality, selected subsequences, countable-choice selectors, host topological closure, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SequentialSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:sequential-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SequentialSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
S=(I,W,L,T,Q,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of sequence-index admission through $I$, finite observation through $W$, limit-space convergence through $L$, topology open-test exposure through $T$, Cauchy comparison through $Q$ when present, Real endpoint exposure through $R$ when present, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:sequential-space-carrier}. The sequence-facing side is exhausted by $I$ and $W$, so a consumer can inspect only displayed finite StreamName windows at displayed indices.

The convergence-facing side is exhausted by $L,T,Q,R$. The limit row supplies the sequential convergence request, the topology row supplies the open-test interface, and the Cauchy and Real rows are available only when the same packet displays those comparison surfaces.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly this data. Since the carrier has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, quotient point equality, selected subsequences, countable choice, host closure, or ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SequentialSpace completion-facing route]
\label{thm:sequential-space-completion-facing-route}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\SequentialSpaceUp$ packet reads sequential convergence through the finite route
$$
\StreamNameUp,\ \LimitSpaceUp,\ \TopologyUp,\ \CauchySpaceUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
The route exposes finite sequence windows, a displayed limit test, an indexed-open neighbourhood test, a Cauchy comparison surface when relevant, and a terminal Real endpoint row; it exports no open-cover compactness, selected subsequence, quotient point equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient Real completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:sequential-space-namecert-obligations}. A completion-facing read must first inspect the sequence index and finite StreamName window rows.

The limit request then passes through the $\LimitSpaceUp$ row and the $\TopologyUp$ open-test row. If the consumer is a completion or Cauchy consumer, the same route displays the $\CauchySpaceUp$ comparison row before any $\RealUp$ endpoint row is read.

Transport, replay, provenance, and naming preserve this order. The excluded principles are not carrier coordinates, so no consumer can extract them from the packet.
\end{proof}
