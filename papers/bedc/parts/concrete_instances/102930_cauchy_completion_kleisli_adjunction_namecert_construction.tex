\begin{definition}[Cauchy completion Kleisli-adjunction carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-kleisli-adjunction-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionKleisliAdjunctionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the unit row inherited from $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, displaying the source packet before any Kleisli consumer or adjunction comparison is read.
\item $B$ is the bind row inherited from $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, displaying the completion-facing source family consumed by a single Kleisli request.
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyCompletionKleisliUp$ consumer row, recording the finite consumer request that reads the unit and bind rows through the same downstream completion surface.
\item $A$ is the $\CauchyCompletionAdjunctionUp$ comparison row, aligning the Kleisli consumer read with the adjunction's source-side and completion-side observations.
\item $V$ is the universal handoff row, exposing the adjunction extension surface only after $U,B,L,A$ have been displayed.
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row used by the shared completion read.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row attached to the same finite window.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row for the displayed window and tolerance data.

\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionKleisliAdjunctionUp$, $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\CauchyCompletionKleisliUp$, $\CauchyCompletionAdjunctionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionKleisliAdjunctionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $U,B,L,A,V,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completions, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion Kleisli-adjunction obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-kleisli-adjunction-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionKleisliAdjunctionUp$ carrier
$$
K=(U,B,L,A,V,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionKleisliAdjunctionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the unit row, bind row, Kleisli consumer row, adjunction comparison row, universal handoff row, StreamName window, DyadicRatCore tolerance, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item unit and bind exposure, requiring every Kleisli or adjunction consumer to display $U$ and $B$ before it can inspect the shared downstream route;
\item Kleisli consumer exactness, requiring $L$ to consume only the displayed unit-and-bind data before opening the adjunction comparison;
\item adjunction comparison exactness, requiring $A$ and $V$ to compare the Kleisli consumer with the universal handoff over the same finite source route;
\item analytic readback exactness, requiring $S,D,R,E$ to supply the only StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and Real rows visible to the consumer;
\item structural exhaustion, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve, replay, source, and name the listed route without adding quotient completions, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-kleisli-adjunction-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the unit, bind, Kleisli consumer, adjunction comparison, universal handoff, finite analytic readback, and structural rows.

Unit and bind exposure is read from $U$ and $B$. Since both rows are inherited from the completion monad surface, every consumer starts with the same displayed source packet and source family before any Kleisli or adjunction row is available.

Kleisli exactness is read from $L$. The row records the consumer request downstream of $U$ and $B$, so it cannot introduce a second source family, selected limit, or quotient representative before the comparison row is opened.

Adjunction comparison and universal handoff are read from $A$ and $V$. These rows align the Kleisli consumer with the adjunction boundary only over the displayed $U,B,L$ route, and the handoff is exposed only after that comparison has been recorded.

The analytic readback rows are $S,D,R,E$. They give the finite StreamName window, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular rational readback, and terminal Real seal used by both sides of the comparison.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ add no analytic or quotient coordinate. They transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route, so the excluded completion, choice, host-equality, ambient-completeness, Lean, and bridge objects have no row from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy completion Kleisli-adjunction route]

$$
U,B \longmapsto L \longmapsto A,V \longmapsto S,D,R,E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .

\begin{proof}
