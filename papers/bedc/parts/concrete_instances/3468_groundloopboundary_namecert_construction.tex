\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GroundLoopBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-groundloopboundary-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\GroundLoopBoundaryUp$ packet realizes the boundary isolated in \autoref{ch:visions-reflection-and-limits}. It records the closed ground loop as finite mark and mark-sameness rows, while the meta side is recorded only as refusal rows for truth-total reflection, host runtime export, and host truth predicates. The packet therefore gives downstream chapters a concrete $\NameCert$ surface for citing the exact place where ground distinction closes and meta-truth closure remains unavailable.

$\GroundLoopBoundaryUp$ supplies the ground-row subpacket consumed by $\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp$ in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflectionlimitboundary-namecert}; the latter adds the broader structural meta-description boundary.

\begin{definition}[Ground-loop boundary carrier]
\label{def:ground-loop-boundary-carrier}
A $\GroundLoopBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(M,S,X,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the two-row $\BMark$ inventory exposing exactly $\bzero$ and $\bone$;
\item $S$ is the positive $\msame$ inventory exposing exactly $\msame(\bzero,\bzero)$ and $\msame(\bone,\bone)$;
\item $X$ is the cross-mark refusal row, recording that no generated row identifies $\bzero$ with $\bone$ or $\bone$ with $\bzero$;
\item $R$ is the reflection-boundary row, recording structural self-description as permitted data and truth-total reflection as refused data;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the mark inventory, positive sameness rows, cross-mark refusal rows, reflection-boundary row, provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes used to read $M$, replay $S$ and $X$, transport the boundary row $R$, and return the same finite packet to every consumer;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\BMark$, $\msame$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, reflection-boundary rows, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GroundLoopBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for a truth-total reflection certificate, host runtime, host truth predicate, or meta-level classifier that turns structural self-description into BEDC truth.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GroundLoopBoundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:ground-loop-boundary-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\GroundLoopBoundaryUp}$ surface for the carrier $G=(M,S,X,R,H,C,P,N)$ consists exactly of carrier admission for \autoref{def:ground-loop-boundary-carrier}, the two displayed $\BMark$ rows in $M$, the two positive $\msame$ rows in $S$, cross-mark non-generation and refusal in $X$, the reflection-boundary row $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GroundLoopBoundaryUp.GroundLoopBoundaryTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite tuple of \autoref{def:ground-loop-boundary-carrier}. The accepted object exposes only the rows $M,S,X,R,H,C,P,N$.

The ground rows are exhausted by $M,S,X$. The row $M$ displays the two mark constructors, $S$ displays the two positive primitive sameness rows, and $X$ records that no generated cross-mark sameness row is admitted.

The reflection row is $R$. It permits structural self-description only as a named boundary row and records refusal of truth-total reflection, so it cannot be read as a truth classifier for the host calculus.

Finally, the transport, replay, provenance, and naming obligations are exactly $H,C,P,N$. No further row remains from which a consumer could recover a host runtime, host truth predicate, or meta-level classifier beyond the displayed boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Ground-loop boundary meta non-escape]
\label{thm:ground-loop-boundary-meta-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\GroundLoopBoundaryUp$ packet factors through the finite rows $M,S,X,R,H,C,P,N$. The packet exports the closed ground distinction between $\bzero$ and $\bone$ together with the positive $\msame$ rows and cross-mark refusal, but it exports no truth-total reflection certificate, host runtime, host truth predicate, or meta-level truth classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let $G=(M,S,X,R,H,C,P,N)$ be accepted. Any permitted read of the ground loop uses $M$ for the two marks, $S$ for positive sameness, and $X$ for the refusal of generated cross-mark sameness.

Any permitted read of the reflection boundary uses $R$ and is transported only by $H$ and replayed only by $C$. The provenance and local name remain the rows $P$ and $N$.

Since the carrier inventory has no coordinate for truth-total reflection, a host runtime, a host truth predicate, or a meta-level truth classifier, no downstream consumer can project such data from the packet. The only exported boundary is the finite distinction between closed ground generation and open meta-truth refusal.
\end{proof}
