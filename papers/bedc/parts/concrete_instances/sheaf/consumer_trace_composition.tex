\begin{definition}[Sheaf consumer trace composition source]
\label{def:sheaf-consumer-trace-composition-source}
Let $T_1$ and $T_2$ be finite $\SheafUp$ consumer access traces of \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-source}. Their consumer trace composition $T_1 \mathbin{\star} T_2$ is the finite trace obtained by listing every constructor of $T_1$ in its given order and then listing every constructor of $T_2$ in its given order. The empty trace is a two-sided unit for this operation.

The source of this operation is restricted to the same objects as the access trace grammar: $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories, $\hsame$ transport, optional $\Cont$ route histories, $\TopologyUp$ indexed opens, $\PreSheafUp$ section carriers, and $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ rows. No constructor of $T_1 \mathbin{\star} T_2$ may mention host powerset equality of opens, cover quotients, stalk quotients, external sheafification, or standard bridge rows except through the sealed consumer projections already present in \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-source}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf consumer trace concatenation coverage]
\label{thm:sheaf-consumer-trace-concatenation-coverage}
For any finite traces $T_1$ and $T_2$ of \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-source}, every constructor in the composed trace $T_1 \mathbin{\star} T_2$ of \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-trace-composition-source} lands in the same root face or sealed consumer projection that it occupied before composition.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafConsumerAccessTrace\_append\_membership\_coverage}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by the ordered list presentation of $T_1 \mathbin{\star} T_2$.

For a constructor appearing in the first segment, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-face-coverage} to its occurrence in $T_1$. Its landing face is one of the root faces of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-face-exactness-source} or one of the sealed consumer projections of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-consumer-projection-seal}; the concatenation operation only preserves the occurrence and its order inside the first segment.

For a constructor appearing in the second segment, apply the same coverage theorem to its occurrence in $T_2$. The first segment supplies no wrapper and no coercion around the second segment, so the second occurrence keeps the landing face it had before composition.

Thus every constructor in the concatenated trace has exactly the face assigned to it by the original access trace coverage theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf consumer trace composition no-feedback]
\label{thm:sheaf-consumer-trace-composition-no-feedback}
Composing finite $\RingedSpaceUp$, $\SchemeUp$, or topology pullback consumer traces by \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-trace-composition-source} creates no additional $\SheafUp$ carrier row, classifier row, exactness row, or bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafConsumerAccessTrace\_append\_no\_zero\_extension}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-no-feedback} to the consumer constructors in $T_1$. Each $\RingedSpaceUp$, $\SchemeUp$, and topology pullback event reads through the sealed projection and contributes no root carrier, classifier, exactness, or bridge row.

Apply the same theorem to the consumer constructors in $T_2$. The composition source merely places the second trace after the first, so no event in the second segment obtains a new sheaf row from the preceding segment.

It remains to rule out feedback across the boundary between the two segments. By \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-face-downstream-separation}, consumer-specific algebraic, chart, and pullback data are outside the root sheaf faces. By \autoref{thm:sheaf-downstream-consumption-exhaustion}, all allowed downstream sheaf reads have already been exhausted by the listed root rows and sealed projections.

Therefore the concatenation boundary carries only the finite order of reads. It supplies no constructor capable of manufacturing a further $\SheafUp$ carrier, classifier, exactness, or bridge row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf consumer trace factorization composes]
\label{thm:sheaf-consumer-trace-factorization-composes}
Suppose two allowed downstream $\SheafUp$ accesses by $\RingedSpaceUp$, $\SchemeUp$, or topology pullback factor through finite traces $T_1$ and $T_2$ of \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-source}. Their sequential use factors through the composed trace $T_1 \mathbin{\star} T_2$ of \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-trace-composition-source}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafBHistPointGermLedger\_trace\_factorization\_composes}
\begin{proof}
Choose the trace $T_1 \mathbin{\star} T_2$ by listing the constructors witnessing the first downstream access and then listing the constructors witnessing the second downstream access. This is a finite trace because both inputs are finite and the composition source is ordinary finite concatenation.

The first access factors through the first segment by the assumed factorization of $T_1$. The second access factors through the second segment by the assumed factorization of $T_2$, and the preceding segment changes only the position of the second segment in the list.

By \autoref{thm:sheaf-consumer-trace-concatenation-coverage}, every constructor in both segments still lands in its original root face or sealed projection. By \autoref{thm:sheaf-consumer-trace-composition-no-feedback}, the boundary between the two segments creates no additional sheaf row that would require another access constructor.

The sequential downstream use is therefore represented by first reading along $T_1$ and then reading along $T_2$, which is exactly the composed finite trace.
\end{proof}
