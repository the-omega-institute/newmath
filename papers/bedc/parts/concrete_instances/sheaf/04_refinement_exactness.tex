\begin{theorem}[Sheaf cover refinement obligation]
\label{thm:sheaf-cover-refinement-obligation}
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$. Let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ and $\mathcal{V}=(B,\kappa,q,\delta)$ be indexed open covers of the same open index $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$. A displayed refinement of $\mathcal{V}$ through $\mathcal{U}$ is a map $r:B\to A$ together with inclusion witnesses
$$
\epsilon_b:\kappa(b)\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(r(b))
$$
for every $b:B$, compatible with the two cover rows in the sense that the composite inclusion $\kappa(b)\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(r(b))\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$ is the displayed route from the refined cover member to $i$. This is a certificate-level obligation only over the indexed-open inclusion relation of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality} and the carrier rows of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}; it introduces no host powerset equality of covers and no quotient comparison between cover presentations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the two cover rows. The original cover supplies $h_a:\iota(a)\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$, and the refined cover supplies $q_b:\kappa(b)\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$. A refinement chooses, for each refined row $b$, an original row $r(b)$ together with the displayed inclusion $\epsilon_b:\kappa(b)\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(r(b))$.

The topology carrier already gives the meaning of $\le_{\mathcal{T}}$ as indexed-open inclusion and supplies the finite-intersection rows used by the sheaf compatibility predicate. Therefore the refinement datum is fully expressed by the map $r$ and the witnesses $\epsilon_b$, with the target cover inclusion $h_{r(b)}$ recording the route onward to $i$.

No comparison of host open subsets is used. All later transport across the refinement is forced to pass through $\epsilon_b$, restriction composition, and the section classifiers of the indexed presheaf carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf compatible family pullback along refinement]
\label{thm:sheaf-refinement-compatible-family-pullback}
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$. Let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ and $\mathcal{V}=(B,\kappa,q,\delta)$ be indexed open covers of the same open index $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$, and let $(r,\epsilon)$ be a displayed refinement of $\mathcal{V}$ through $\mathcal{U}$ in the sense of \autoref{thm:sheaf-cover-refinement-obligation}. If $s_a:\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(a))$ is compatible over $\mathcal{U}$, then the pulled-back family
$$
t_b:=\rho_{\iota(r(b)),\kappa(b)}(s_{r(b)})
$$
is compatible over $\mathcal{V}$. Explicitly, for every $b,c:B$, writing $m_{b,c}:=\kappa(b)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\kappa(c)$, one has
$$
\rho_{\kappa(b),m_{b,c}}(t_b)
\sim_{m_{b,c}}
\rho_{\kappa(c),m_{b,c}}(t_c).
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafBHistCoverNerveLedger\_refinement\_pullback}
\begin{proof}
Fix $b,c:B$ and write $m=\kappa(b)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\kappa(c)$. The finite-intersection field of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier} gives $m\le_{\mathcal{T}}\kappa(b)$ and $m\le_{\mathcal{T}}\kappa(c)$. Composing these with the refinement inclusions gives routes $m\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(r(b))$ and $m\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(r(c))$.

Use restriction composition on the chain $m\le_{\mathcal{T}}\kappa(b)\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(r(b))$. It identifies the left restriction of $t_b$ with the direct restriction of $s_{r(b)}$ to $m$, up to the classifier $\sim_m$. The same argument on the $c$ side identifies the right restriction of $t_c$ with the direct restriction of $s_{r(c)}$ to $m$.

The original compatibility of $s_a$ over $\mathcal{U}$ gives equality after restricting $s_{r(b)}$ and $s_{r(c)}$ to the overlap $\iota(r(b))\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\iota(r(c))$. Since $m$ lies inside both original cover members by the refinement inclusions and the finite-intersection projections, restriction composition transports that compatibility row down to $m$.

Finally, transitivity of $\sim_m$ composes the two restriction-composition rows with the transported original compatibility row. The resulting equality is precisely the compatibility clause for $t_b$ and $t_c$ over the refined cover $\mathcal{V}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf gluing descends along refined covers]
\label{thm:sheaf-refinement-gluing-descent}
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$. Let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ and $\mathcal{V}=(B,\kappa,q,\delta)$ be indexed open covers of $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$, and let $(r,\epsilon)$ be a displayed refinement of $\mathcal{V}$ through $\mathcal{U}$. Suppose $s_a:\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(a))$ is compatible over $\mathcal{U}$ and $g:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ glues it:
$$
\forall a:A,\quad \rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g)\sim_{\iota(a)}s_a.
$$
Then $g$ glues the pulled-back family $t_b:=\rho_{\iota(r(b)),\kappa(b)}(s_{r(b)})$ over $\mathcal{V}$:
$$
\forall b:B,\quad \rho_{i,\kappa(b)}(g)\sim_{\kappa(b)}t_b.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix $b:B$. The gluing row over $\mathcal{U}$ gives
$$
\rho_{i,\iota(r(b))}(g)\sim_{\iota(r(b))}s_{r(b)}.
$$
Apply restriction classifier transport along the refinement inclusion $\epsilon_b:\kappa(b)\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(r(b))$ to obtain equality after restriction to $\kappa(b)$.

Restriction composition on the chain $\kappa(b)\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(r(b))\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$ identifies the restriction of $\rho_{i,\iota(r(b))}(g)$ to $\kappa(b)$ with the direct restriction $\rho_{i,\kappa(b)}(g)$. By definition, the restriction of $s_{r(b)}$ along the same refinement inclusion is $t_b$.

Transitivity of the classifier on $\mathsf{Sec}(\kappa(b))$ composes the composition row with the transported gluing row. Since $b$ was arbitrary, the displayed equality holds for every member of $\mathcal{V}$, so $g$ glues the pulled-back family over the refined cover.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf locality detection under refinement]
\label{thm:sheaf-refinement-locality-detection}
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$. Let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ and $\mathcal{V}=(B,\kappa,q,\delta)$ be indexed open covers of $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$, and let $(r,\epsilon)$ be a displayed refinement of $\mathcal{V}$ through $\mathcal{U}$. Suppose $g,h':\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ satisfy refined-cover equality
$$
\forall b:B,\quad \rho_{i,\kappa(b)}(g)\sim_{\kappa(b)}\rho_{i,\kappa(b)}(h').
$$
Assume moreover that every original cover member is detected by its refinement rows: for every $a:A$, the subfamily of indices $b:B$ with $r(b)=a$ is an indexed open cover of $\iota(a)$ through the inclusions $\epsilon_b:\kappa(b)\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(a)$. Then locality over $\mathcal{U}$ gives
$$
g\sim_i h'.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix $a:A$. By the detection hypothesis, the rows with $r(b)=a$ form an indexed cover of $\iota(a)$. For each such row $b$, the refined equality compares the direct restrictions of $g$ and $h'$ to $\kappa(b)$.

Restriction composition on $\kappa(b)\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(a)\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$ rewrites those direct restrictions as the restrictions of $\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g)$ and $\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(h')$ along $\epsilon_b$. Thus the two sections $\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g)$ and $\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(h')$ agree on every member of the detected cover of $\iota(a)$.

Apply the sheaf locality field of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality} on this detected cover of $\iota(a)$. It gives
$$
\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g)\sim_{\iota(a)}\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(h').
$$
Since $a$ was arbitrary, these equalities hold over all members of $\mathcal{U}$.

Applying the same locality field over the original cover $\mathcal{U}$ yields $g\sim_i h'$, which is the claimed global classifier equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf refined-cover gluing uniqueness]
\label{thm:sheaf-refined-cover-gluing-uniqueness}
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$. Let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ and $\mathcal{V}=(B,\kappa,q,\delta)$ be indexed open covers of $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$, and let $(r,\epsilon)$ be a displayed refinement of $\mathcal{V}$ through $\mathcal{U}$. Let $s_a:\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(a))$ be compatible over $\mathcal{U}$, and write
$$
t_b:=\rho_{\iota(r(b)),\kappa(b)}(s_{r(b)})
$$
for its pulled-back family over $\mathcal{V}$. Suppose $g,h':\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ both glue this pulled-back family:
$$
\forall b:B,\quad \rho_{i,\kappa(b)}(g)\sim_{\kappa(b)}t_b,
\qquad
\forall b:B,\quad \rho_{i,\kappa(b)}(h')\sim_{\kappa(b)}t_b.
$$
Assume moreover that every original cover member is detected by its refinement rows in the sense of \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-locality-detection}. Then
$$
g\sim_i h'.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix $b:B$. The first gluing row compares $\rho_{i,\kappa(b)}(g)$ with $t_b$, and the second compares $\rho_{i,\kappa(b)}(h')$ with the same section $t_b$.

Use symmetry of the classifier on $\mathsf{Sec}(\kappa(b))$ to reverse the second equality, then transitivity to obtain
$$
\rho_{i,\kappa(b)}(g)\sim_{\kappa(b)}\rho_{i,\kappa(b)}(h').
$$
Since $b$ was arbitrary, this is refined-cover equality of $g$ and $h'$ over $\mathcal{V}$.

Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-locality-detection} to this refined-cover equality and to the displayed detection hypothesis for the rows with $r(b)=a$. It returns the global classifier equality $g\sim_i h'$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf refined-cover exactness obligation]
\label{thm:sheaf-refined-cover-exactness-obligation}
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$. Let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ and $\mathcal{V}=(B,\kappa,q,\delta)$ be indexed open covers of $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$, and let $(r,\epsilon)$ be a displayed refinement of $\mathcal{V}$ through $\mathcal{U}$. For every compatible family $s_a:\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(a))$ over $\mathcal{U}$, the pulled-back family $t_b:=\rho_{\iota(r(b)),\kappa(b)}(s_{r(b)})$ satisfies the following three exactness rows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item The family $t_b$ is compatible over $\mathcal{V}$.
\item Every gluing $g:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ of $s_a$ over $\mathcal{U}$ also glues $t_b$ over $\mathcal{V}$.
\item If every original cover member is detected by its refinement rows, then any two sections $g,h':\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ gluing $t_b$ over $\mathcal{V}$ satisfy $g\sim_i h'$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The first row is exactly \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-compatible-family-pullback}: compatibility of the original family transports along the displayed refinement.

The second row is exactly \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-gluing-descent}: a section gluing the original family restricts to a gluing of the pulled-back family over the refined cover.

The third row is \autoref{thm:sheaf-refined-cover-gluing-uniqueness}. Its hypotheses are precisely two displayed gluings of the same pulled-back family, plus the locality-detection row for the refinement fibers over each original cover member.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf NameCert refinement exactness export]
\label{thm:sheaf-namecert-refinement-exactness-export}
The paper-side obligation surface $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ exports refined-cover exactness over indexed opens. Concretely, for any indexed sheaf carrier over an indexed open topology carrier, every displayed refinement of indexed covers carries compatible families forward, descends original gluings to refined gluings, and makes any two refined gluings of the same pulled-back family globally classifier-equal whenever the original members are detected by their refinement rows. This exported row uses only $\TopologyUp$, $\PreSheafUp$, restriction composition, section classifiers, locality, and gluing; it does not use host powerset equality or quotient comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier row from \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}. This supplies the open-indexed section carriers, classifiers, restriction maps, restriction composition, and classifier transport used in the refinement arguments.

Project the cover, compatibility, locality, and gluing rows from \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}. These rows supply indexed covers, overlap compatibility, the local-to-global classifier implication, and displayed gluing witnesses.

Assemble the export by applying \autoref{thm:sheaf-refined-cover-exactness-obligation}. Its three rows are already expressed in the projected data, so $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ exposes a single refined-cover exactness obligation to downstream ringed-space and scheme certificates without adding a host-level cover comparison.
\end{proof}
