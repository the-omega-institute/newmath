\begin{definition}[Sheaf root cover-descent source]
\label{def:sheaf-root-cover-descent-source}
The $\SheafUp$ root cover-descent source is the following $\mathsf{BHist}$-scoped source tuple. It is read over a $\TopologyUp$ indexed-open carrier with point carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$, membership row $\mathsf{OpenAt}$, inclusion relation $\le_{\mathcal{T}}$, and finite-intersection rows; an indexed $\SheafUp$ carrier with section carriers $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, classifiers $\sim_i$, and restriction maps $\rho_{i,j}$; an indexed cover $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ of an ambient open $i$; member inclusions $h_a:\iota(a)\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$; displayed restriction routes to members and finite-intersection overlaps; a compatible local family $s_a:\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(a))$; and the section classifier rows used to compare restrictions on members, overlaps, and glued readbacks.

The source admits only these displayed data: $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories, indexed open membership, cover-member witnesses, member and overlap inclusions, restriction-route witnesses, compatible-family overlap equalities, locality, gluing, and section classifiers. It excludes stalks, quotient carriers, host powerset equality of covers, and external sheafification.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root cover-descent carrier exactness]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-cover-descent-carrier-exactness}
The source of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-cover-descent-source} exposes exactly $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\mathsf{OpenAt}$, cover membership, restriction maps, finite-intersection overlap rows, and section classifiers. In particular, every carrier or classifier row visible to cover descent is one of the indexed-open carrier rows of $\TopologyUp$, the indexed section rows of $\SheafUp$, or the compatible-family and locality rows of the sheaf cover surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:sheaf-root-cover-descent-source}. The topology component contributes only $\mathsf{BHist}$, indexed opens, $\mathsf{OpenAt}$, inclusion witnesses, and finite-intersection rows. These are exactly the displayed carrier and membership rows of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}.

Unfold the indexed sheaf component. By \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}, the section-level rows are the carriers $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, classifiers $\sim_i$, restriction maps $\rho_{i,j}$, and restriction stability fields attached to displayed inclusion routes.

Finally unfold the cover and local-family component through \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}. It adds cover membership, member inclusions, finite-intersection overlap comparisons, locality, and gluing. None of the three unfolded sources contains a stalk carrier, a quotient carrier, host powerset equality, or external sheafification, so the displayed list is exact.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root cover-descent gluing exhaustion]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-cover-descent-gluing-exhaustion}
Every root cover-descent comparison route for the source of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-cover-descent-source} factors through a cover member or a displayed common refinement, then through restriction-route exactness, and finally through the locality or gluing classifier row. Thus the cover-descent route surface is exhausted by cover membership, finite-intersection overlap rows, restriction transport, point-germ readback, and gluing uniqueness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply the cover law $\gamma$ in the indexed cover $\mathcal{U}$. Each history cell $b:\mathsf{BHist}$ lying in the ambient open is sent to a member $a:A$ with $\mathsf{OpenAt}(\iota(a),b)$, and the finite-intersection rows of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier} supply the displayed overlaps used for pairwise comparison.

Next route the comparison through the sheaf restriction surface. Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-route-exactness-membership-transport} to transport the member witness through the displayed route, and apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-route-exactness-cont-composition} when the comparison is represented by a composed route history. Both steps keep the target section classifier attached to the reached open.

It remains to read the local family through gluing. Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-bhist-point-germ-gluing-readback} for the memberwise readback of a glued section, and apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-gluing-uniqueness-from-locality} to identify any two global readbacks that agree on all displayed members. The route therefore ends at the existing locality or gluing classifier row and introduces no further carrier or classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root cover-descent downstream interface]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-cover-descent-downstream-interface}
The $\RingedSpaceUp$, $\SchemeUp$, and topology pullback consumers may consume only the cover-descent source of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-cover-descent-source}, the carrier exactness row of \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-descent-carrier-exactness}, the gluing exhaustion row of \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-descent-gluing-exhaustion}, and the downstream exactness coverage row of \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-downstream-consumer-exactness-coverage}. They receive no additional sheaf carrier and no additional sheaf classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the ringed-space consumer, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-ringedspace-local-ring-row}. Its sheaf-side data are section carriers, classifiers, restriction stability, point-germ readback, and local compatibility rows, all already present in the cover-descent source and its carrier exactness row.

For the scheme consumer, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-scheme-gluing-cover-row}. The chart gluing request is routed through cover membership, finite-intersection overlaps, compatible local families, gluing, and common-refinement comparison, which are precisely the rows exhausted by \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-descent-gluing-exhaustion}.

For topology pullback, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-topology-pullback-cover-row}. Pullback cover membership and compatible-family transport are displayed over the same indexed-open and restriction-route rows, so they add no carrier beyond \autoref{def:sheaf-root-cover-descent-source}.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-downstream-consumer-exactness-coverage}. It seals the three consumer projections to cover membership, locality and gluing, refinement descent, common-refinement exactness, and projection rows. Hence downstream access is exactly the cover-descent interface named in the statement.
\end{proof}
