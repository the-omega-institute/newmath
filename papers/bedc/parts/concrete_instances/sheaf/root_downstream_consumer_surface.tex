\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root downstream consumer carrier scope]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-downstream-consumer-carrier-scope}
The downstream consumers $\RingedSpaceUp$, $\SchemeUp$, and topology pullback receive their $\SheafUp$ carrier rows only from the indexed section carrier over $\mathsf{BHist}$ opens in \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}, together with the cover-nerve ledger of \autoref{def:sheaf-bhist-cover-nerve-ledger}. Their sheaf-side carrier scope is exactly the indexed open carrier, the section carriers $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, the section classifiers $\sim_i$, the restriction maps $\rho_{i,j}$, cover-member cells, pair-overlap cells, and $\hsame$-stable ledger transport. No host powerset equality, cover quotient, stalk quotient, or external sheafification row is exported to these consumers.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafDownstreamConsumer\_carrier\_scope}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier source from \autoref{def:sheaf-root-unblock-bedc-source}. Its carrier clause points to \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}, so every section-level carrier read by a downstream consumer is one of the open-indexed section carriers, classifiers, restrictions, and identity or composition rows already listed there.

Read the history side from \autoref{def:sheaf-bhist-cover-nerve-ledger} and the cover-nerve export surface of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-cover-nerve-export-surface}. The only additional cells are cover-member cells, finite-intersection pair-overlap cells, route-history cells when present, and membership transport through $\hsame$.

Finally apply the downstream coverage theorem \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-nerve-downstream-coverage} and the projection seal \autoref{def:sheaf-root-consumer-projection-seal}. These rows are projected read-only to $\RingedSpaceUp$, $\SchemeUp$, and topology pullback, so none of the three consumers obtains host powerset equality, a cover quotient, a stalk quotient, or an external sheafification row from the root package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root downstream consumer classifier stability]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-downstream-consumer-classifier-stability}
Every restriction route exposed from the $\SheafUp$ root to $\RingedSpaceUp$, $\SchemeUp$, or topology pullback preserves the relevant section classifier. One-step and two-step routes preserve classifiers by \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-nerve-restriction-route-stability}; membership reindexing is transported only through $\hsame$; optional $\Cont$ route histories compare displayed route witnesses without changing the target open or the target classifier. Hence consumer restriction routes do not add any host powerset equality, cover quotient, stalk quotient, or external sheafification row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafDownstreamConsumer\_classifier\_stability}
\begin{proof}
For section restrictions, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-nerve-restriction-route-stability}. It states that the one-step restriction of classifier-equal sections lands in the target classifier and that the composed two-step route agrees with the direct route in the classifier of the final open.

For membership movement, use the cover-nerve ledger of \autoref{def:sheaf-bhist-cover-nerve-ledger}. Its transport row is $\hsame$-stable membership transport over $\mathsf{BHist}$ cells, so route changes do not replace the indexed open carrier by a host open or a quotient carrier.

If optional $\Cont$ histories are present, use the route-history clause cited in \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-unblock-restriction-stability-obligation}. Those histories compare the witnesses for displayed routes; they do not alter the open index on which $\sim_j$ or $\sim_k$ is read.

The three projection seals \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-ringedspace-local-ring-projection-seal}, \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-scheme-affine-chart-projection-seal}, and \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-topology-pullback-projection-seal} then pass the same classifier-stable routes to the downstream consumers without exporting host powerset equality, a cover quotient, a stalk quotient, or an external sheafification row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root downstream consumer exactness coverage]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-downstream-consumer-exactness-coverage}
The $\SheafUp$ exactness surface consumed by $\RingedSpaceUp$, $\SchemeUp$, and topology pullback is exhausted by cover membership, locality and gluing, refinement descent, common-refinement exactness, and the downstream projection seals. Concretely, cover membership is supplied by \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-unblock-cover-membership-obligation} and \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-nerve-membership-exhaustion}; locality and gluing are supplied by \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-unblock-locality-gluing-obligation}; refinement descent and refined exactness are supplied by \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-gluing-descent} and \autoref{thm:sheaf-refined-cover-exactness-obligation}; common-refinement exactness is supplied by \autoref{thm:sheaf-common-refinement-gluing-invariance} and \autoref{thm:sheaf-cover-presentation-exactness}; and consumer access is sealed by the three projection theorems. No host powerset equality, cover quotient, stalk quotient, or external sheafification row is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with cover membership. The accepted-cover cell is unpacked by \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-unblock-cover-membership-obligation}, and \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-nerve-membership-exhaustion} records the corresponding cover-member and pair-overlap cells inside the $\mathsf{BHist}$ cover nerve.

For local exactness, use \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-unblock-locality-gluing-obligation}. It gives existence of a global readback from a compatible local family and classifier uniqueness for global readbacks whose restrictions agree on the cover.

For refinement, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-gluing-descent} and the package theorem \autoref{thm:sheaf-refined-cover-exactness-obligation}. These rows keep the same global classifier class after passing to displayed refinements. For comparison of cover presentations, use \autoref{thm:sheaf-common-refinement-gluing-invariance} and \autoref{thm:sheaf-cover-presentation-exactness}.

Finally pass to consumers through \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-ringedspace-local-ring-projection-seal}, \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-scheme-affine-chart-projection-seal}, and \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-topology-pullback-projection-seal}. Each consumer receives a projection of the listed cover, restriction, locality, gluing, refinement, and common-refinement rows; none receives host powerset equality, a cover quotient, a stalk quotient, or an external sheafification row.
\end{proof}
