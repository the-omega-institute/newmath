\begin{definition}[Indexed section presheaf carrier]
\label{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}
Let $\mathcal{T}$ be an indexed open topology carrier for $\TopologyUp$ in the sense of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}. Write
$$
j\le_{\mathcal{T}} i
:\Longleftrightarrow
\forall x:X,\ \mathsf{OpenAt}(j,x)\Longrightarrow\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,x)
$$
for inclusion of indexed opens. An indexed section presheaf carrier for $\PreSheafUp$ over $\mathcal{T}$ is a tuple
$$
\mathcal{P}=(\mathsf{Sec},\sim,\rho,\mathsf{Eqv},\mathsf{Id},\mathsf{Comp},\mathsf{Res})
$$
with the following data. For every open index $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$ there is a section carrier $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ and a classifier $\sim_i$ on $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$; the field $\mathsf{Eqv}$ says that each $\sim_i$ is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive. For every inclusion $j\le_{\mathcal{T}} i$ there is a restriction map
$$
\rho_{i,j}:\mathsf{Sec}(i)\to\mathsf{Sec}(j).
$$
The identity field says $\rho_{i,i}(s)\sim_i s$ for every $s:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, and the composition field says that whenever $k\le_{\mathcal{T}}j\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$,
$$
\rho_{j,k}(\rho_{i,j}(s))\sim_k\rho_{i,k}(s)
$$
for every $s:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$. The restriction classifier-stability field says that, for every inclusion $j\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$ and every $s,t:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$,
$$
s\sim_i t
\Longrightarrow
\rho_{i,j}(s)\sim_j\rho_{i,j}(t).
$$
This is the section-level concrete form of the contravariant restriction data described in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-presheaf-namecert}.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Indexed sheaf cover, compatibility, and locality]
\label{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}
Let $\mathcal{P}$ be an indexed section presheaf carrier over $\mathcal{T}$ as in \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}. An indexed open cover of an open index $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$ is a tuple $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ consisting of an indexing type $A$, a family $\iota:A\to\mathsf{OpenIx}$, inclusion witnesses
$$
h_a:\iota(a)\le_{\mathcal{T}}i
$$
for all $a:A$, and the pointwise cover law
$$
\gamma:
\forall x:X,\ \mathsf{OpenAt}(i,x)\Longrightarrow
\exists a:A,\ \mathsf{OpenAt}(\iota(a),x).
$$
For a family $s_a:\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(a))$, compatibility over $\mathcal{U}$ means that for all $a,b:A$ the two restrictions to the indexed intersection agree:
$$
\rho_{\iota(a),\iota(a)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\iota(b)}(s_a)
\sim_{\iota(a)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\iota(b)}
\rho_{\iota(b),\iota(a)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\iota(b)}(s_b),
$$
where the needed inclusions are supplied by the finite-intersection law of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}. A sheaf locality field for $\mathcal{P}$ says that, for every such cover $\mathcal{U}$ of $i$ and every pair $g,h:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$,
$$
\bigl(\forall a:A,\ \rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g)\sim_{\iota(a)}\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(h)\bigr)
\Longrightarrow
g\sim_i h.
$$
A sheaf gluing field says that every compatible local family over $\mathcal{U}$ has a section $g:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ whose restriction to each $\iota(a)$ is classifier-equal to $s_a$. An indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ is an indexed section presheaf carrier equipped with both of these fields.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Stable sheaf restriction row]
\label{def:sheaf-stable-restriction-row}
Let $\mathcal{P}$ be an indexed section presheaf carrier over $\mathcal{T}$ as in \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}. A stable sheaf restriction row for $\mathcal{P}$ is the field saying that classifier equality is preserved by every restriction: for every inclusion $j\le_{\mathcal{T}} i$ and every pair $s,t:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$,
$$
s\sim_i t
\Longrightarrow
\rho_{i,j}(s)\sim_j\rho_{i,j}(t).
$$
Thus section classifiers transport along the indexed open restriction maps carried by the presheaf surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf basic carrier-classifier scope]
\label{thm:sheaf-basic-carrier-classifier-scope}
For a $\mathsf{BHist}$-scoped indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$ for $\TopologyUp$ with membership row $\mathsf{OpenAt}$ and history classifier $\hsame$, every indexed sheaf carrier $\mathcal{S}$ for $\SheafUp$ over $\mathcal{T}$ has exactly the basic carrier and classifier rows displayed by the indexed section presheaf carrier of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}. The carrier row is the family of section carriers $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ over $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$, the classifier row is the family $\sim_i$, the classifier-equivalence row is $\mathsf{Eqv}$, and the restriction row is the family $\rho_{i,j}$ for inclusions $j\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$ expressed by $\mathsf{OpenAt}$. Thus the basic $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ surface over $\NameCert_{\TopologyUp}$, $\NameCert_{\PreSheafUp}$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, and $\mathsf{OpenAt}$ contains no carrier or classifier row beyond those section, equivalence, identity, composition, and restriction-stability fields.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the indexed section presheaf component of $\mathcal{S}$. By \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}, this projection is exactly the tuple $(\mathsf{Sec},\sim,\rho,\mathsf{Eqv},\mathsf{Id},\mathsf{Comp},\mathsf{Res})$, indexed over the open indices supplied by the $\TopologyUp$ carrier and ordered by the $\mathsf{OpenAt}$ inclusion row.

The $\SheafUp$ carrier adds locality and gluing fields, but those fields quantify over the same $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, $\sim_i$, and restriction maps. They do not introduce a new host carrier, a second classifier, or a quotient comparison. Therefore the basic carrier-classifier scope is exhausted by the rows listed in the statement, with the $\mathsf{BHist}$ and $\hsame$ data entering only through the displayed topology and name-certificate provenance.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf locality-gluing exactness scope]
\label{thm:sheaf-locality-gluing-exactness-scope}
For an indexed sheaf carrier $\mathcal{S}$ for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$, the exactness rows at the locality and gluing level are precisely the locality field, the gluing field, uniqueness of glued sections, and compatibility of global restrictions. Concretely, locality and gluing are the two sheaf fields in \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}; uniqueness is the consequence recorded by \autoref{thm:sheaf-gluing-uniqueness-from-locality}; and the local family obtained from any global section is compatible by \autoref{thm:sheaf-global-restrictions-compatible-local-family}. No further exactness predicate is needed for the $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ surface at this scope.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}. Its locality row says that coverwise classifier equality of two global sections implies classifier equality on the covered open, and its gluing row supplies a global section for every compatible local family.

Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-gluing-uniqueness-from-locality}. Two displayed gluings of the same compatible family restrict to classifier-equal rows on every cover member; locality then identifies the two global sections.

Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-global-restrictions-compatible-local-family}. Restricting one global section to the cover members produces a local family whose pairwise overlap comparisons follow from finite-intersection rows and restriction composition. These two consequences are exactly the uniqueness and global-restriction compatibility clauses consumed by the certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf cover-refinement ledger scope]
\label{thm:sheaf-cover-refinement-ledger-scope}
The cover-refinement ledger for the $\SheafUp$ certificate consists exactly of indexed cover rows, refinement maps, displayed common-refinement spans, finite-intersection overlap rows, pullback of compatible families, and gluing descent along those rows. The refinement maps are those of \autoref{thm:sheaf-cover-refinement-obligation}; common-refinement spans are those of \autoref{def:sheaf-displayed-common-refinement-span}; finite-intersection overlaps are the $\wedge_{\TopologyUp}$ rows of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}; compatible-family pullback and gluing descent are supplied by \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-compatible-family-pullback} and \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-gluing-descent}. This ledger is scoped over indexed opens, restriction maps, and section classifiers only, without host powerset equality of covers and without quotient comparison of cover presentations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the cover rows from \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}. A cover is an index type, an open-index family, inclusion witnesses into the covered open, and a pointwise $\mathsf{OpenAt}$ cover law. The finite-intersection row of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier} supplies the overlap indices where compatibility is checked.

Project the refinement rows. \autoref{thm:sheaf-cover-refinement-obligation} records a refinement as a map of cover indices together with displayed inclusions, and \autoref{def:sheaf-displayed-common-refinement-span} records a common refinement by two such maps and two displayed inclusion families.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-compatible-family-pullback} and \autoref{thm:sheaf-refinement-gluing-descent}. They show that compatible families and gluing rows transport along the displayed refinement ledger. Since every step uses only indexed inclusions, finite intersections, restriction maps, and classifiers $\sim_i$, the ledger has no need for host powerset equality or quotient comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf carrier support obligation]
\label{thm:sheaf-carrier-support-obligation}
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$. Then the carrier support exposed by $\mathcal{S}$ is only the indexed section presheaf carrier over the $\TopologyUp$ open-index surface, together with the sheaf locality and gluing fields. Concretely, every carrier row is one of the section carriers $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, section classifiers $\sim_i$, equivalence fields, restriction maps $\rho_{i,j}$, identity and composition laws, indexed open-cover data, compatible local-family predicates, locality implications, or gluing witnesses described in \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier} and \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the indexed section presheaf component of $\mathcal{S}$. Its displayed rows are precisely the open-indexed section carriers, classifiers, equivalence witnesses, restriction maps, and restriction identity and composition laws of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}.

Project the sheaf component of $\mathcal{S}$. By \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}, this contributes only indexed open covers, compatible local families, the locality field, and the gluing field. No additional carrier row is introduced beyond those two displayed interfaces, so the asserted carrier support is exhausted by their projections.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf classifier transport obligation]
\label{thm:sheaf-classifier-transport-obligation}
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$, and suppose its indexed section presheaf carrier carries a stable sheaf restriction row in the sense of \autoref{def:sheaf-stable-restriction-row}. Then section classifiers transport through every restriction. Moreover, for every indexed open cover $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ of $i$ and every pair $g,h':\mathsf{Sec}(i)$,
$$
\bigl(\forall a:A,\ \rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g)\sim_{\iota(a)}\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(h')\bigr)
\Longrightarrow
g\sim_i h'.
$$
Thus restriction stability gives classifier transport along the presheaf maps, and sheaf locality turns coverwise classifier equality back into classifier equality on the covered open.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For a single inclusion $j\le_{\mathcal{T}} i$, the transport claim is exactly the stable restriction row of \autoref{def:sheaf-stable-restriction-row}: a classifier equality $s\sim_i t$ restricts to $\rho_{i,j}(s)\sim_j\rho_{i,j}(t)$. The source and target classifiers are those supplied by the section presheaf carrier, and their reflexive, symmetric, and transitive behavior is supplied by the equivalence field of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}.

For a cover $\mathcal{U}$, assume the displayed coverwise equality of the restrictions of $g$ and $h'$. This is exactly the antecedent required by the sheaf locality field in \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}. Applying that field over the same indexed cover gives $g\sim_i h'$, which is the global classifier equality claimed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf cover ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:sheaf-cover-ledger-exactness-obligation}
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier $\mathcal{T}$. An accepted cover and gluing ledger row for $\mathcal{S}$ consists exactly of indexed open-cover data $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$, the finite-intersection rows of the $\TopologyUp$ carrier, a compatible local section family $s_a:\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(a))$, and a global section $g:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ with displayed restriction equalities
$$
\forall a:A,\quad \rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g)\sim_{\iota(a)}s_a.
$$
The ledger uses no host open-cover equality and no quotient primitive: all comparison data is expressed through indexed opens, restriction maps, and the section classifiers of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the indexed open cover and compatibility clauses in \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}. The cover ledger records only the indexing type $A$, the open-index map $\iota$, the inclusion witnesses $h_a:\iota(a)\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$, and the pointwise cover law $\gamma$. Compatibility is recorded by restricting local sections to the finite-intersection index supplied by the $\TopologyUp$ carrier and comparing them with the classifier on that intersection.

Unfold the gluing clause of the same definition. A gluing ledger row adds a section $g:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ and the displayed restriction equalities against the compatible family. The theorem \autoref{thm:sheaf-global-restrictions-compatible-local-family} checks that restrictions of a global section produce a compatible local family, while \autoref{thm:sheaf-gluing-uniqueness-from-locality} checks that two displayed gluings of the same compatible family are classifier-equal.

The two definitions just unfolded contain no host-side equality of open covers and no quotient constructor. Their comparison mechanism is the indexed inclusion relation $j\le_{\mathcal{T}} i$, the finite-intersection row of the topology carrier, the restriction maps, and the section classifiers. Hence the listed rows are exact for the accepted cover and gluing ledger.
\end{proof}
