\begin{definition}[Sheaf root consumer-threshold projection source]
\label{def:sheaf-root-consumer-threshold-projection-source}
The $\SheafUp$ root consumer-threshold projection source is the $\mathsf{BHist}$-indexed package obtained from \autoref{def:sheaf-root-threshold-certificate-source} by exposing three consumer faces: the $\RingedSpaceUp$ local-ring face, the $\SchemeUp$ affine-chart gluing face, and the topology pullback face. It is recorded by the following ten fields.
\begin{enumerate}
\item The traditional target is the sheaf-side data of a structure sheaf that can be read by ringed spaces, schemes, and pullback-open consumers.
\item The BEDC source is the single threshold source of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-threshold-certificate-source}, not a separate host sheaf object.
\item The carrier row is the indexed-open section carrier $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, with restricted-open carriers and displayed source carriers obtained only by slicing or pulling back indexed opens.
\item The classifier row is the section classifier family $\sim_i$, together with its restricted-open and displayed base-change readbacks.
\item The restriction row is restriction identity, restriction composition, and classifier transport for every displayed inclusion used by the three consumers.
\item The RingedSpaceUp projection row is the local-ring consumer surface: section carriers, section classifiers, ring-preserving restriction routes, point-germ local-ring readback, restricted-open rows, and base-change rows.
\item The SchemeUp projection row is the affine-chart gluing surface: chart-wise ringed-space projections, displayed affine cover membership, overlap compatibility, and common-refinement comparison.
\item The topology pullback projection row is the displayed base-change surface: cover pullback, gluing pullback, pulled-back common refinement, and classifier transport along the displayed square.
\item The ledger and exactness row is the cover-nerve ledger, point-germ ledger, restricted-open ledger, refinement ledger, cover-presentation exactness, and base-change exactness already present in the threshold source.
\item The standard-bridge field is absent: the projection source exports no host powerset cover equality, no cover quotient, no stalk quotient, no external sheafification operation, and no $\StdBridge$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the same $\mathsf{BHist}$-indexed source is projected separately to the three consumer families without duplicating the sheaf root obligations.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root RingedSpace consumer projection]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-consumer-ringedspace-projection}
The $\RingedSpaceUp$ local-ring consumer receives exactly the indexed sections, section classifiers, restriction identity, restriction composition, restriction transport, point-germ local-ring readback, restricted-open rows, and base-change rows of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-consumer-threshold-projection-source}. No additional sheaf carrier, cover comparison, stalk quotient, or standard bridge is available through this projection.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafRootConsumerRingedSpaceProjection\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafRootRingedSpaceConsumerProjection\_scope}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:sheaf-ringedspace-consumption-obligation}. Its sheaf-side list asks for section carriers, classifiers, restriction stability, cover-gluing exactness, restricted-open rows, and base-change rows, together with non-sheaf commutative-ring data supplied outside $\SheafUp$.

The carrier and classifier entries are supplied by \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-threshold-carrier-classifier-obligation}. This gives the open-indexed section carrier, the classifier family, restriction identity and composition, classifier transport, $\mathsf{BHist}$ cover membership, and point-germ rows read on displayed neighborhoods.

The exactness and transport entries are supplied by \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-threshold-gluing-refinement-obligation}. Locality, gluing, refinement pullback, restricted-open exactness, and displayed base-change exactness are already fields of the root threshold source, so the ringed-space projection is precisely the listed face of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-consumer-threshold-projection-source}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root Scheme consumer projection]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-consumer-scheme-projection}
The $\SchemeUp$ affine-chart gluing consumer receives the same threshold source by chart-wise $\RingedSpaceUp$ projection, followed by cover-presentation exactness and common-refinement exactness on chart overlaps. Thus affine-chart sections, overlap classifiers, glued section classes, and comparison across common refinements are all read from \autoref{def:sheaf-root-consumer-threshold-projection-source}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-scheme-consumption-obligation}. Each affine or ringed-space chart first reads the sheaf boundary through the ringed-space consumer, so the chart-wise carrier, classifier, restriction, restricted-open, point-germ, and base-change rows are the rows identified in \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-consumer-ringedspace-projection}.

The coverage claim is \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-threshold-downstream-coverage-obligation}: every sheaf row consumed by $\SchemeUp$ is already covered by the threshold source. This keeps the chart gluing surface inside the same $\mathsf{BHist}$-indexed package.

For independence of the chosen affine cover presentation, use \autoref{thm:sheaf-cover-presentation-exactness}. The displayed common refinement compares the pulled-back local families and identifies the resulting global classifier class, so no quotient of cover presentations or external sheaf model is introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root topology pullback consumer projection]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-consumer-pullback-projection}
Topology pullback consumers receive only the displayed base-change, cover-pullback, gluing-pullback, and common-refinement rows of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-consumer-threshold-projection-source}. These rows preserve pulled-back cover membership, compatible local-family predicates, glued section classifiers, and common-refinement comparison, without exporting the ringed-space local-ring face or the scheme affine-chart face.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:sheaf-topology-pullback-cover-row}. It identifies the consumer request as a displayed pullback of indexed cover rows: cover membership, restriction stability, and compatible local-family predicates are transported along the topology pullback-open rows.

The complete base-change surface is \autoref{thm:sheaf-base-change-namecert-obligation-surface}. Its displayed square supplies cover pullback, gluing pullback, and common-refinement invariance using the same indexed-open carrier, classifier family, cover ledger, and refinement exactness fields recorded in \autoref{def:sheaf-root-consumer-threshold-projection-source}.

Since this consumer reads only displayed source and target rows, the projection discards the ringed-space local-ring field and the scheme affine-chart field. What remains is exactly the base-change and common-refinement face of the root threshold source, with no host powerset cover comparison or standard bridge.
\end{proof}
