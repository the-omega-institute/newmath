\begin{definition}[Sheaf root route exactness packet]
\label{def:sheaf-root-route-exactness-packet}
The $\SheafUp$ root route exactness packet is the following ten-field $\mathsf{BHist}$-scoped surface.
\begin{enumerate}
\item The traditional target is the sheaf route from an accepted indexed cover cell to the section row consumed by downstream certificates.
\item The BEDC source is the joint $\TopologyUp$, $\PreSheafUp$, and $\NameCert$ surface, read over $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories and optional $\Cont$ route histories.
\item The carrier is the indexed-open carrier of $\TopologyUp$ together with the section carrier $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}.
\item The classifier is the open-indexed section relation $\sim_i$ on $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, transported only along displayed restriction routes.
\item The stability rows are restriction identity, restriction composition, one-step classifier transport, two-step classifier transport, and route-history associativity when $\Cont$ rows are present.
\item The ledger rows are cover-member cells, pair-overlap cells, route witnesses, and $\hsame$ membership transport in the $\mathsf{BHist}$ cover nerve.
\item The exactness rows are cover-cell membership exhaustion, restriction-route classifier transport, locality and gluing, displayed refinement descent, and common-refinement comparison.
\item The downstream consumers are exactly the $\RingedSpaceUp$ local-ring face, the $\SchemeUp$ affine-chart face, and topology pullback constructions.
\item The packet contains no host powerset equality of open covers, no quotient of cover presentations, no stalk quotient, and no external sheafification operation.
\item The status is a root $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ route-exactness surface: it records the rows available for downstream use and does not assert standard bridge closure.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf route exactness membership transport]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-route-exactness-membership-transport}
Let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ be an accepted indexed cover of $i$ over the $\mathsf{BHist}$ topology ledger. For every displayed cell $\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,b)$ and every displayed restriction route from $i$ to a cover member or pair-overlap row, the packet of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-route-exactness-packet} transports the cover-member witness through the route and preserves the target section classifier on the reached open.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafRootRouteExactness\_membership\_transport}
\begin{proof}
First build the cover witness. Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-nerve-membership-exhaustion} to the accepted cover row. It sends the displayed cell $\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,b)$ to a member $a:A$ with $\mathsf{OpenAt}(\iota(a),b)$ and supplies the finite-intersection row needed for comparison with any second member.

Next transport along the restriction route. Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-nerve-restriction-route-stability}. Its one-step and two-step clauses keep the section comparison inside the classifier of the reached member or overlap open.

The transported member witness and the transported classifier row use the same indexed open route. Since both cited rows are recorded over $\mathsf{BHist}$ and use $\hsame$ for membership transport, the result stays inside the cover nerve and introduces no host cover equality or cover quotient.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf route exactness Cont composition]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-route-exactness-cont-composition}
When optional $\Cont$ histories are present in the $\SheafUp$ route ledger, their composition rows compose displayed route witnesses only. They preserve the target open index and therefore preserve the target section classifier $\sim_j$ or $\sim_k$ already transported by restriction stability.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafBHistPointGermLedger\_route\_history\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Start with the one-step route. The restriction stability obligation of \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-unblock-restriction-stability-obligation} gives classifier transport from $\sim_i$ to $\sim_j$ along $j\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$.

For a composite route $k\le_{\mathcal{T}}j\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$, use the composition clause of \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-unblock-restriction-stability-obligation}. The direct restriction from $i$ to $k$ and the route through $j$ are compared in the same target classifier $\sim_k$.

Now read the optional $\Cont$ row. By \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-nerve-restriction-route-stability}, such a history compares route witnesses and does not change the reached open. Hence route-history composition can only rebracket the displayed path; the classifier already attached to the reached open is unchanged.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafBHistPointGermLedger\_route\_cont\_composition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf route exactness NameCert consumption]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-route-exactness-namecert-consumption}
The $\RingedSpaceUp$, $\SchemeUp$, and topology pullback consumers receive exactly the route-exactness packet of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-route-exactness-packet}: cover-member cells, restriction classifier transport, optional $\Cont$ route-history composition, and the downstream projection rows sealed for those consumers. No consumer receives a larger sheaf interface from this packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First identify the common downstream surface. Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-cover-nerve-downstream-coverage}. It lists the cover-member cells, pair-overlap cells, one-step and two-step restriction stability rows, optional $\Cont$ histories, and locality and gluing exactness rows available to downstream consumers.

For the ringed-space consumer, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-ringedspace-local-ring-projection-seal}. The local-ring face may read the sheaf section carrier, classifier, restriction stability, cover-gluing exactness, restricted-open rows, and displayed base-change rows, while local-ring structure itself is supplied outside $\SheafUp$.

For the scheme consumer, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-scheme-affine-chart-projection-seal}. Its affine-chart read is routed through cover-presentation rows, displayed common-refinement rows, and the sealed ringed-space projection, so it receives the same route-exactness packet rather than a separate sheaf surface.

For topology pullback consumers, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-topology-pullback-projection-seal}. The pullback face reads pulled-back cover membership, restriction-route stability, compatible-family transport, gluing pullback, and common-refinement transport along displayed base change.

The three projections exhaust the consumers named in \autoref{def:sheaf-root-route-exactness-packet}. Since each cited seal excludes host powerset equality, cover quotients, stalk quotients, external sheafification, and standard bridge closure, downstream $\NameCert$ use is exactly the route-exactness packet and nothing larger.
\end{proof}
