\begin{definition}[Sheaf BHist cover nerve ledger]
\label{def:sheaf-bhist-cover-nerve-ledger}
Let $\mathcal{T}$ be an indexed open topology carrier for $\TopologyUp$ whose point carrier is $\mathsf{BHist}$, whose membership classifier is $\mathsf{OpenAt}$, and whose finite-intersection index is written $\wedge_{\TopologyUp}$. Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier over $\mathcal{T}$, and let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ be an indexed open cover of $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$. The $\mathsf{BHist}$ cover nerve ledger of $\mathcal{U}$ is the finite-intersection ledger surface with the following rows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item For each $a:A$, the member row is the indexed open $\iota(a)$ with its inclusion witness $h_a:\iota(a)\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$ and its visible membership predicate $\mathsf{OpenAt}(\iota(a),b)$ for $b:\mathsf{BHist}$.
\item For each $a,b:A$, the pair-overlap row is $k_{a,b}:=\iota(a)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\iota(b)$, together with the two finite-intersection projections $k_{a,b}\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(a)$ and $k_{a,b}\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(b)$ and the two restriction rows from $\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(a))$ and $\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(b))$ to $\mathsf{Sec}(k_{a,b})$.
\item For each $a,b,c:A$, the triple-overlap row is $\ell_{a,b,c}:=k_{a,b}\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\iota(c)$, together with the finite-intersection projections $\ell_{a,b,c}\le_{\mathcal{T}}k_{a,b}$ and $\ell_{a,b,c}\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(c)$ and the restriction rows obtained by composing through $k_{a,b}$ or directly from any member whose inclusion route is displayed.
\end{enumerate}
The ledger contains only $\mathsf{OpenAt}$ rows, finite-intersection projections, inclusion witnesses, presheaf restriction rows, section classifiers, and visible $\mathsf{BHist}$ provenance. It does not identify cover presentations by host equality and does not quotient overlap rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf BHist cover nerve overlap projections]
\label{thm:sheaf-bhist-cover-nerve-overlap-projections}
Let $\mathcal{T}$ be an indexed open topology carrier for $\TopologyUp$ on $\mathsf{BHist}$, let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier over $\mathcal{T}$, and let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ be an indexed open cover of $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$. For a local family $s_a:\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(a))$, compatibility over $\mathcal{U}$ is exactly the assertion that every pair-overlap row of the $\mathsf{BHist}$ cover nerve ledger carries classifier equality between the two projected restrictions:
$$
\forall a,b:A,\quad
\rho_{\iota(a),k_{a,b}}(s_a)
\sim_{k_{a,b}}
\rho_{\iota(b),k_{a,b}}(s_b),
$$
where $k_{a,b}:=\iota(a)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\iota(b)$ and the inclusions are the finite-intersection projections of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the compatibility predicate in \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}. Its only comparison clauses are indexed by pairs $a,b:A$, and each clause restricts $s_a$ and $s_b$ to the common intersection $\iota(a)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\iota(b)$ before comparing them by the section classifier at that intersection.

Unfold the cover nerve ledger of \autoref{def:sheaf-bhist-cover-nerve-ledger}. Its pair-overlap rows are exactly the same indices $k_{a,b}$, with the same two finite-intersection projections supplied by \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}. Therefore every compatibility clause is a classifier equality on one pair-overlap ledger row.

Conversely, assume every pair-overlap ledger row carries the displayed classifier equality. Reading those rows through the same two projections gives precisely the clauses required by \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality}. Restriction composition is not extra data here; it only confirms that any displayed route to $k_{a,b}$ reads as the same section classifier row as the direct pair-overlap restriction.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf BHist cover nerve gluing readback]
\label{thm:sheaf-bhist-cover-nerve-gluing-readback}
Let $\mathcal{T}$ be an indexed open topology carrier for $\TopologyUp$ on $\mathsf{BHist}$, let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier over $\mathcal{T}$, and let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ be an indexed open cover of $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$. Suppose $s_a:\mathsf{Sec}(\iota(a))$ is compatible over $\mathcal{U}$ and $g:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ is a gluing section with
$$
\forall a:A,\quad \rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g)\sim_{\iota(a)}s_a.
$$
Then the $\mathsf{BHist}$ cover nerve ledger reads $g$ back to the local family on every member row and to the induced compatible family on every pair-overlap row. Explicitly, for every $a:A$,
$$
\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g)\sim_{\iota(a)}s_a,
$$
and for every $a,b:A$, writing $k_{a,b}:=\iota(a)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\iota(b)$,
$$
\rho_{\iota(a),k_{a,b}}(\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g))
\sim_{k_{a,b}}
\rho_{\iota(a),k_{a,b}}(s_a)
$$
and
$$
\rho_{\iota(b),k_{a,b}}(\rho_{i,\iota(b)}(g))
\sim_{k_{a,b}}
\rho_{\iota(b),k_{a,b}}(s_b).
$$
Consequently the two restrictions of $g$ to each pair-overlap ledger row recover the same classifier row as the compatible family there.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The member readback is the gluing equality displayed in the statement. It is exactly the gluing clause of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-cover-compatibility-locality} evaluated at the member row $\iota(a)$.

For a pair $a,b:A$, write $k=\iota(a)\wedge_{\TopologyUp}\iota(b)$. The finite-intersection field of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier} supplies $k\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(a)$ and $k\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(b)$, which are the two pair-overlap projections recorded by \autoref{def:sheaf-bhist-cover-nerve-ledger}.

Transport the gluing equality $\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g)\sim_{\iota(a)}s_a$ along $k\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(a)$ using the restriction classifier-stability field from \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}. This gives the first pair-overlap readback equality. The same transport along $k\le_{\mathcal{T}}\iota(b)$ gives the second one.

It remains to compare the two readings of $g$ on $k$. Restriction composition identifies the restriction of $\rho_{i,\iota(a)}(g)$ to $k$ and the restriction of $\rho_{i,\iota(b)}(g)$ to $k$ with the direct restriction $\rho_{i,k}(g)$, up to the classifier $\sim_k$. The compatibility of $s_a$ gives the corresponding equality between the two local-family restrictions. Transitivity and symmetry of $\sim_k$ combine these rows, so the pair-overlap ledger reads the gluing section back to the same classifier row as the compatible local family.
\end{proof}
