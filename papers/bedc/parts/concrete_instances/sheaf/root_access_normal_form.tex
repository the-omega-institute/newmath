\begin{definition}[Sheaf root access normal form]
\label{def:sheaf-root-access-normal-form}
The $\SheafUp$ root access normal form is the finite classification of downstream-visible root reads generated by \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-source}. Its classes are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier, classifier, and restriction-route rows for the indexed section carriers $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, classifiers $\sim_i$, restrictions $\rho_{i,j}$, identity rows, composition rows, classifier transport, and optional $\Cont$ route histories;
\item cover-member and overlap rows for accepted covers, member witnesses, finite-intersection overlaps, pair-overlap projections, and $\hsame$ membership transport;
\item locality, gluing, refinement, common-refinement, and base-change rows for compatible-family predicates, global witnesses, uniqueness rows, displayed refinements, refinement pullbacks, gluing descent, cover-presentation exactness, displayed common refinements, displayed base-change squares, cover pullback, compatible-family transport, and gluing pullback;
\item point-germ comparison rows that expose named pointwise readbacks without forming stalk quotients;
\item the three downstream projection faces: the $\RingedSpaceUp$ local-ring projection, the $\SchemeUp$ affine-chart projection, and the topology pullback projection.
\end{enumerate}
These classes are read-only faces of the same $\mathsf{BHist}$-indexed sheaf root package. They contain no class for host powerset equality of opens, cover quotients, stalk quotients, external sheafification, standard bridges, or consumer-private algebraic, chart, or pullback data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root access normal-form coverage]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-access-normal-form-coverage}
Every admitted $\SheafUp$ root access trace of \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-source} lands in one class of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-access-normal-form}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafRootFaceRead\_locality\_gluing\_readback}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by cases on the trace constructor of \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-source}.

Carrier, classifier, and restriction-route reads land in the first normal-form class. The root face source of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-face-exactness-source} places section carriers, section classifiers, restrictions, identity rows, composition rows, classifier transport, and optional route histories in the restriction-route face, and \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-face-classifier-coverage} gives the corresponding coverage.

Cover-membership and overlap reads land in the second class. The accepted-cover row, member witness, finite-intersection overlap, pair-overlap projection, and $\hsame$ transport are exactly the cover-membership rows isolated by \autoref{thm:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-face-coverage}.

Locality, gluing, refinement, common-refinement, and base-change reads land in the third class. Their exactness rows are the locality, gluing, and refinement face of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-face-exactness-source}, with gluing exactness supplied by \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-face-gluing-exactness}; the downstream boundary of \autoref{def:sheaf-downstream-consumption-boundary} records the same restricted-open, base-change, refinement, and exactness rows.

Point-germ reads land in the fourth class because \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-source} admits named pointwise readbacks only as point-germ comparison rows. The same definition excludes stalk quotient formation, so the point-germ case does not branch into an additional quotient class.

The remaining constructors are the three downstream consumer projections. They land in the fifth class by the projection seal of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-consumer-projection-seal}, and the face coverage theorem \autoref{thm:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-face-coverage} identifies no further trace constructor. Thus every admitted access has a normal-form landing class.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root access normal-form consumer factorization]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-access-normal-form-consumer-factorization}
Every downstream $\SheafUp$ access used by $\RingedSpaceUp$, $\SchemeUp$, or topology pullback factors first through \autoref{def:sheaf-root-access-normal-form} and then through the existing projection seals.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an allowed downstream access. By \autoref{thm:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-exhaustion}, it is represented by a finite trace of \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-source}. Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-access-normal-form-coverage} to send each trace constructor to its normal-form class.

For $\RingedSpaceUp$, the relevant classes are carrier, classifier, restriction, cover-gluing, restricted-open, base-change, and point-germ comparison rows. The local-ring projection seal \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-ringedspace-local-ring-projection-seal} reads exactly those sheaf-side classes and supplies no commutative-ring or local-ring row from the sheaf root.

For $\SchemeUp$, first pass through the ringed-space projection and then use the cover-presentation and displayed common-refinement classes. The affine-chart projection seal \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-scheme-affine-chart-projection-seal} identifies spectra and chart-ring rows as consumer-side data, while \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-joint-consumer-face-exhaustion} lists the remaining sheaf rows visible to the joint face.

For topology pullback, use the base-change class together with cover-membership, restriction-route, compatible-family, gluing, and common-refinement rows. The pullback projection seal \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-topology-pullback-projection-seal} is exactly this factor, and \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-joint-consumer-face-noninterference} prevents the other two consumer faces from entering it.

The factorization therefore has two stages: access traces land in the finite normal form, and the three downstream consumers read their respective normal-form classes only through the sealed projections.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafConsumerAccessTrace\_root\_access\_factorization}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root access normal form exports no extra rows]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-access-normal-form-no-extra-rows}
The normal form of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-access-normal-form} exports no host powerset equality, no cover quotient, no stalk quotient, no external sheafification row, no standard bridge row, and no consumer-private algebraic, chart, or pullback data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For host powerset equality, cover quotients, stalk quotients, external sheafification, and standard bridges, inspect the source grammar. The trace source of \autoref{def:sheaf-consumer-access-trace-source} has no constructor for those rows, and the root face exactness source of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-face-exactness-source} explicitly keeps them outside the four root faces.

For consumer-private algebraic rows, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-ringedspace-local-ring-projection-seal}. It says the commutative-ring and local-ring data used by $\RingedSpaceUp$ are supplied outside $\SheafUp$, so the normal form cannot export them as sheaf root rows.

For affine-chart and spectra rows, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-scheme-affine-chart-projection-seal}. The scheme face reads sheaf rows through ringed-space projections, cover-presentation exactness, and common-refinement comparison, while spectra data and chart-ring data remain outside the sheaf root.

For pullback-private choices, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-topology-pullback-projection-seal}. The topology pullback face reads displayed base-change transport, pulled-back cover membership, compatible-family transport, gluing pullback, and common-refinement transport; it does not export consumer-specific pullback-open choices as root data.

Finally combine the joint face and downstream boundary. The joint consumer face noninterference theorem \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-joint-consumer-face-noninterference} prevents private consumer data from feeding back into the root, and \autoref{thm:sheaf-downstream-consumption-exhaustion} exhausts the sheaf-side rows consumed downstream. Thus the normal form exports only the listed finite classes and no extra row.
\end{proof}
