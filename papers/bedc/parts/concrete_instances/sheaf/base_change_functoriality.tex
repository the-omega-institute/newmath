\section{Sheaf base-change functoriality}
\label{sec:sheaf-base-change-functoriality}

\begin{definition}[Sheaf composable base-change squares]
\label{def:sheaf-composable-base-change-squares}
Let $\mathcal{T}$, $\mathcal{T}'$, and $\mathcal{T}''$ be indexed open topology carriers, and let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over $\mathcal{T}$. Let
$$
\mathcal{T}''\xrightarrow{g}\mathcal{T}'\xrightarrow{f}\mathcal{T}
$$
be displayed base-change squares in the sense of \autoref{def:sheaf-displayed-base-change-square}. The two squares are composable when the following rows assemble into the displayed square $\mathcal{T}''\to\mathcal{T}$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the open-index map is the composite $f\circ g$ and preserves finite intersections by composing the finite-intersection rows of $g$ and $f$;
\item the represented inverse-image row for a target open $i$ identifies the source open $i''$ with the two-step inverse image obtained first in $\mathcal{T}'$ and then in $\mathcal{T}''$;
\item cover ledgers compose pointwise: an indexed cover $\mathcal{U}$ of $i$ pulls back to a cover $\mathcal{U}'$ of $i'$ and then to a cover $\mathcal{U}''$ of $i''$ with the same index type and the same member order;
\item inclusion rows compose from each member $\iota''(a)$ through $\iota'(a)$ to $\iota(a)$, and the route to the target open is the composite of the two displayed routes;
\item memberwise pullback rows for sections, overlaps, and common-refinement members are classifier-equal to the corresponding rows read directly along the composite square.
\end{enumerate}
Thus a composable pair is not extra carrier data. It is the assertion that the displayed index maps, inverse-image representatives, finite-intersection rows, cover ledgers, inclusion rows, and memberwise pullback rows form the same downstream-readable base-change surface as the one-step square.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf base-change identity square neutrality]
\label{thm:sheaf-base-change-identity-square-neutrality}
For an indexed sheaf carrier $\mathcal{S}$ over $\mathcal{T}$, the identity displayed base-change square on $\mathcal{T}$ preserves the cover, restriction, gluing, and common-refinement rows of \autoref{thm:sheaf-base-change-namecert-obligation-surface}. In particular, reading the $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ obligation surface through the identity square gives the same indexed cover members, overlap witnesses, restriction composites, displayed gluing rows, and common-refinement comparisons as reading the surface directly.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafBaseChangeIdentitySquare\_restriction\_neutrality}
\begin{proof}
The identity square uses the identity map on open indices. Its inverse-image representative for an open $i$ is $i$ itself, and its displayed cover pullback sends each cover member $\iota(a)$ to the same indexed member with the same inclusion row.

Cover compatibility follows because the overlap of two unchanged members is the original finite intersection. Restriction identity in the indexed section carrier identifies each pulled-back local section with the section already present in the target cover ledger.

For gluing, \autoref{thm:sheaf-base-change-gluing-pullback} specializes to the identity restriction path. The displayed row is therefore classifier-equal to the original gluing row by restriction identity and classifier reflexivity.

For common refinements, the displayed span, the refinement maps, and the member inclusions are unchanged. Applying \autoref{thm:sheaf-base-change-common-refinement-invariance} along the identity square returns the same comparison row, and \autoref{thm:sheaf-cover-presentation-exactness} reads the same global classifier class.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf base-change cover-pullback composition]
\label{thm:sheaf-base-change-cover-pullback-composition}
Let $\mathcal{T}''\xrightarrow{g}\mathcal{T}'\xrightarrow{f}\mathcal{T}$ be composable displayed base-change squares from \autoref{def:sheaf-composable-base-change-squares}. Let $\mathcal{U}=(A,\iota,h,\gamma)$ be an indexed cover of $i$ in $\mathcal{T}$. Pulling $\mathcal{U}$ through the composite square gives the same cover-member witnesses, member inclusion routes, finite-intersection overlap rows, and compatible local-family rows as pulling $\mathcal{U}$ first through $f$ and then through $g$, up to the classifier identifications recorded by the composable-square rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafBHistCoverNerveLedger\_base\_change\_composition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafBHistCoverNerveLedger\_base\_change\_cover\_pullback\_composition}
\begin{proof}
Start with a compatible family over $\mathcal{U}$. Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-base-change-cover-pullback-compatibility} to the square $f$. This gives a compatible displayed family over the pulled-back cover $\mathcal{U}'$ in $\mathcal{T}'$.

Apply the same theorem to $g$ and the compatible family over $\mathcal{U}'$. The result is a compatible family over the two-step cover $\mathcal{U}''$ in $\mathcal{T}''$.

By \autoref{def:sheaf-composable-base-change-squares}, the composite square has the same cover index type $A$, the same displayed member order, and the same member inclusion route as the two-step cover ledger. Its finite-intersection preservation row is the composite of the two overlap rows.

Restriction composition identifies the two-step section pullback with the direct pullback along the composite route. Classifier transport then replaces the two-step overlap comparisons by the one-step comparisons, so the downstream cover-pullback surface is the same in both readings.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf base-change gluing pullback composition]
\label{thm:sheaf-base-change-gluing-pullback-composition}
Let $\mathcal{T}''\xrightarrow{g}\mathcal{T}'\xrightarrow{f}\mathcal{T}$ be composable displayed base-change squares from \autoref{def:sheaf-composable-base-change-squares}. Let $\mathcal{U}$ be an indexed cover of $i$ and let $u_a$ be a compatible local family glued by $s:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$. The displayed gluing row transported along the composite square is classifier-equal to the row obtained by first transporting the gluing row along $f$ and then transporting the resulting row along $g$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafBHistPointGermLedger\_base\_change\_gluing\_pullback\_composition}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-base-change-gluing-pullback} to $f$. It transports the gluing row for $s$ to a displayed gluing row over the cover pulled back to $\mathcal{T}'$.

Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-base-change-gluing-pullback} again to $g$. This transports the intermediate gluing row to the two-step pulled-back cover over $\mathcal{T}''$.

The composability rows identify the source open of the one-step composite square with the two-step represented inverse image. They also identify each pulled-back cover member and member inclusion route with the corresponding two-step member route.

Restriction composition in the sheaf carrier identifies the restriction of $s$ along the composite route with the successive restrictions through the intermediate open. Classifier transport along the displayed member identifications converts the two-step gluing comparison into the direct composite-square comparison, giving the claimed classifier equality of gluing rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf base-change common-refinement composition]
\label{thm:sheaf-base-change-common-refinement-composition}
Let $\mathcal{T}''\xrightarrow{g}\mathcal{T}'\xrightarrow{f}\mathcal{T}$ be composable displayed base-change squares from \autoref{def:sheaf-composable-base-change-squares}. Let $\mathcal{U}$ and $\mathcal{V}$ be indexed cover presentations of $i$ whose compatible local families agree on a displayed common refinement. The common-refinement comparison obtained after pulling through the composite square agrees with the comparison obtained after successive pullbacks through $f$ and $g$. Consequently the global classifier class supplied by \autoref{thm:sheaf-cover-presentation-exactness} is independent of whether downstream consumers read the composite square in one step or as the two displayed squares.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafDisplayedCommonRefinementSpan\_base\_change\_composition}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-base-change-common-refinement-invariance} to $f$. This produces pulled-back cover presentations over $\mathcal{T}'$ and transports the displayed common-refinement comparison to the intermediate common refinement.

Apply the same theorem to $g$. The intermediate comparison becomes a comparison over the two-step pulled-back common refinement in $\mathcal{T}''$.

The composable-square definition identifies the one-step pulled-back covers, refinement maps, member inclusions, and finite-intersection rows with their two-step counterparts. In particular, each common-refinement member has the same downstream restriction route after composing the displayed inclusion rows.

Restriction composition and classifier transport move the two-step comparison to the direct composite comparison. Then \autoref{thm:sheaf-cover-presentation-exactness} reads either comparison as the same global classifier class over the represented source open, so the common-refinement surface is independent of the chosen displayed factorization.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SheafUp.SheafBaseChange\_common\_refinement\_composition}
