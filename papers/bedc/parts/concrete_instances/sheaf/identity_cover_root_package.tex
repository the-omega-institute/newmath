\section{Identity-cover root package}
\label{sec:sheaf-identity-cover-root-package}

\begin{definition}[Sheaf identity-cover root package]
\label{def:sheaf-identity-cover-root-package}
For an indexed open $i$ in the $\SheafUp$ root-obligation surface, the identity-cover root package is the accepted one-member cover of $i$ with cover index type $\mathsf{Unit}$ and member map $\iota:\mathsf{Unit}\to\mathsf{OpenIx}$ constantly equal to $i$. Its membership row is the identity row on $\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,b)$ for each $b:\mathsf{BHist}$, its only overlap row is the finite-intersection cell $i\wedge_{\TopologyUp}i$, and its exposed restriction route is the identity restriction $\rho_{i,i}$.

The package ledger is restricted to $\mathsf{BHist}$ membership cells, $\hsame$ transport of those cells, the self-overlap projections for $i\wedge_{\TopologyUp}i$, and the presheaf identity row identifying $\rho_{i,i}(s)$ with $s$ in the classifier $\sim_i$. It carries no host cover equality, cover quotient, stalk quotient, or external sheafification operation.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf identity-cover membership exactness]
\label{thm:sheaf-identity-cover-membership-exactness}
Let $i$ be an indexed open and let $\mathcal{U}_{\mathrm{id}}$ be the identity-cover root package of \autoref{def:sheaf-identity-cover-root-package}. For every $b:\mathsf{BHist}$, the displayed membership row is exact:
$$
\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,b)
\Longleftrightarrow
\exists a:\mathsf{Unit},\ \mathsf{OpenAt}(\iota(a),b).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, use the unique member of $\mathsf{Unit}$. Since $\iota$ is constantly $i$, the required displayed cover-member cell is the original cell $\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,b)$ read through the identity membership row.

For the reverse direction, destruct the displayed witness $a:\mathsf{Unit}$. The member map again reads as $i$, so the cover-member cell is exactly a cell of $\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,b)$.

Both directions stay inside the $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger and use only $\hsame$ transport when a history cell is reindexed. No equality of ambient host covers is used.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf identity-cover restriction readback]
\label{thm:sheaf-identity-cover-restriction-readback}
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier, and let $\mathcal{U}_{\mathrm{id}}$ be the identity-cover root package of an indexed open $i$. For every section $s:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, the restriction route exposed by $\mathcal{U}_{\mathrm{id}}$ is classifier-equal to $\rho_{i,i}(s)$ and hence classifier-equal to $s$ by the presheaf identity row:
$$
\rho^{\mathcal{U}_{\mathrm{id}}}(s)\sim_i\rho_{i,i}(s),
\qquad
\rho_{i,i}(s)\sim_i s.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the identity-cover root package. Its only cover member is $i$, and the route from the ambient open to that member is the identity route, so the exposed restriction readback is the displayed route $\rho_{i,i}(s)$.

Apply the identity row of the indexed section presheaf carrier from \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}. That row identifies $\rho_{i,i}(s)$ with $s$ in the classifier $\sim_i$.

The self-overlap cell $i\wedge_{\TopologyUp}i$ supplies no additional restriction target; it only records the overlap ledger row already attached to the identity route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf identity-cover gluing collapse]
\label{thm:sheaf-identity-cover-gluing-collapse}
Let $\mathcal{S}$ be an indexed sheaf carrier for $\SheafUp$ over an indexed open topology carrier, and let $\mathcal{U}_{\mathrm{id}}$ be the identity-cover root package of an indexed open $i$. A compatible local family on $\mathcal{U}_{\mathrm{id}}$ is classifier-equivalent to exactly one global section over $i$: gluing supplies a global readback, and locality identifies any two global readbacks whose identity-cover restrictions agree.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply the gluing part of \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-unblock-locality-gluing-obligation} to the compatible local family over $\mathcal{U}_{\mathrm{id}}$. This produces a global section $g:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$ whose restriction to the unique cover member agrees with the displayed local section.

The agreement row is read back through \autoref{thm:sheaf-identity-cover-restriction-readback}. Because the only exposed route is $\rho_{i,i}$ and the presheaf identity row identifies $\rho_{i,i}(g)$ with $g$, the local family has no separate cover presentation beyond its single global readback.

For uniqueness, take two global readbacks whose restrictions agree on the identity cover. The membership exactness row of \autoref{thm:sheaf-identity-cover-membership-exactness} reduces coverwise agreement to agreement on the lone $\mathsf{Unit}$ member, and \autoref{thm:sheaf-identity-cover-restriction-readback} turns that agreement into agreement of the two global sections over $i$.

Apply the locality part of \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-unblock-locality-gluing-obligation}. It returns classifier equality in $\sim_i$, so the compatible local family collapses to one global section up to the section classifier.
\end{proof}
