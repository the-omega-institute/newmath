\begin{definition}[Sheaf root stalk-free point-germ source]
\label{def:sheaf-root-stalk-free-germ-source}
The $\SheafUp$ root stalk-free point-germ source is the following ten-field source over $\mathsf{BHist}$.
\begin{enumerate}
\item The topology field is the $\TopologyUp$ indexed-open carrier of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}, with open indices, membership rows $\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,b)$, inclusion rows $j\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$, identity inclusions, and finite-intersection rows $i\wedge_{\TopologyUp}j$.
\item The presheaf field is the $\PreSheafUp$ indexed section carrier of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier}, with section carriers $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, restriction rows $\rho_{i,j}$, restriction identity, restriction composition, and restriction classifier-stability.
\item The sheaf field is the $\SheafUp$ locality and gluing surface over the same indexed topology and presheaf carrier.
\item The naming field is the root $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ obligation surface, read from the carrier, classifier, locality, gluing, refinement, and exactness rows already exposed by the indexed sheaf carrier.
\item The history field is the point carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$ together with $\hsame$-stable transport of displayed membership rows.
\item The point-germ ledger field is the displayed ledger of \autoref{def:sheaf-bhist-point-germ-ledger}; a row over $b:\mathsf{BHist}$ is a triple $(i,o_i,s)$ with $o_i:\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,b)$ and $s:\mathsf{Sec}(i)$.
\item The common-neighborhood field is supplied by finite intersections in the topology carrier, including the projections from $i\wedge_{\TopologyUp}j$ to $i$ and $j$ and the displayed membership witness obtained from the two input membership rows.
\item The restriction-comparison field compares two point-germ rows only after restricting their sections to a displayed common neighborhood and applying the section classifier $\sim_k$ there.
\item The classifier-law field uses the reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity rows of the section classifier on each $\mathsf{Sec}(k)$, together with restriction identity and restriction composition, to move comparisons across displayed neighborhoods.
\item The boundary field exports no host point carrier, no host stalk quotient, no quotient of section representatives, and no powerset equality of opens; all point-germ evidence remains inside indexed opens, $\mathsf{BHist}$ membership, restrictions, finite intersections, section classifiers, and $\hsame$ transport.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root point-germ classifier reflexivity]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-point-germ-classifier-reflexive}
Every displayed point-germ row in the source of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-stalk-free-germ-source} compares to itself. Concretely, if $(i,o_i,s)$ is a row over $b:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the sense of \autoref{def:sheaf-bhist-point-germ-ledger}, then the comparison from $(i,o_i,s)$ to $(i,o_i,s)$ is witnessed by the common neighborhood $i$, the identity inclusions $i\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$, the membership row $o_i$, and the identity classifier row
$$
\rho_{i,i}(s)\sim_i\rho_{i,i}(s).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the row's own indexed open as the displayed common neighborhood. The two projections required by \autoref{def:sheaf-bhist-point-germ-ledger} are both the identity inclusion $i\le_{\mathcal{T}}i$, and the membership witness is the row's own witness $o_i:\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,b)$.

It remains to fill the classifier comparison after restriction. The identity restriction field of \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier} identifies $\rho_{i,i}(s)$ with $s$ in the section classifier on $\mathsf{Sec}(i)$, and the reflexivity field of that classifier gives $\rho_{i,i}(s)\sim_i\rho_{i,i}(s)$. This is exactly the displayed comparison row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root point-germ classifier symmetry]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-point-germ-classifier-symmetric}
The displayed point-germ comparison in the source of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-stalk-free-germ-source} is symmetric. If rows $(i,o_i,s)$ and $(j,o_j,t)$ over the same $b:\mathsf{BHist}$ compare on a displayed common neighborhood $k$ with projections
$$
k\le_{\mathcal{T}}i,
\qquad
k\le_{\mathcal{T}}j,
\qquad
o_k:\mathsf{OpenAt}(k,b),
$$
and classifier row
$$
\rho_{i,k}(s)\sim_k\rho_{j,k}(t),
$$
then the reverse comparison from $(j,o_j,t)$ to $(i,o_i,s)$ is witnessed by the same $k$, the projections swapped, the same membership row $o_k$, and the symmetric classifier row
$$
\rho_{j,k}(t)\sim_k\rho_{i,k}(s).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Keep the displayed common neighborhood $k$. The comparison definition asks only for a neighborhood below the two source opens and a membership row at $b$, so the same witness $o_k$ remains valid and the two projections are read in the opposite order.

The only row whose direction changes is the section classifier comparison on $\mathsf{Sec}(k)$. Apply the symmetry field of the classifier supplied by \autoref{def:sheaf-indexed-section-presheaf-carrier} to the given row $\rho_{i,k}(s)\sim_k\rho_{j,k}(t)$. The result is $\rho_{j,k}(t)\sim_k\rho_{i,k}(s)$, so the swapped neighborhood data form the reverse point-germ comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sheaf root point-germ classifier equivalence boundary]
\label{thm:sheaf-root-point-germ-classifier-equivalence-boundary}
On the displayed point-germ rows of \autoref{def:sheaf-root-stalk-free-germ-source}, the comparison relation of \autoref{def:sheaf-bhist-point-germ-ledger} is an equivalence relation. Reflexivity is supplied by \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-point-germ-classifier-reflexive}, symmetry by \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-point-germ-classifier-symmetric}, and transitivity by \autoref{thm:sheaf-bhist-point-germ-comparison-transitivity}. The resulting equivalence boundary exports only displayed rows and comparison witnesses over indexed neighborhoods; it exports no stalk quotient, no host point carrier, and no quotient carrier of point-germ classes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For reflexivity, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-point-germ-classifier-reflexive} to the displayed row. The witness is its own indexed open, with identity restriction and the section classifier's reflexivity row.

For symmetry, apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-root-point-germ-classifier-symmetric} to any displayed comparison witness. This keeps the same common neighborhood and reverses only the two projection slots and the section classifier row.

For transitivity, take two composable displayed comparison witnesses between rows over the same $b:\mathsf{BHist}$. Apply \autoref{thm:sheaf-bhist-point-germ-comparison-transitivity}; its finite-intersection construction supplies the refined common neighborhood and composes the restricted classifier rows.

These three clauses are stated entirely in the ledger language of \autoref{def:sheaf-bhist-point-germ-ledger}. Since \autoref{def:sheaf-root-stalk-free-germ-source} contains no host stalk carrier and no quotient operation, the equivalence relation is the boundary relation available to downstream consumers, not a construction of stalk elements.
\end{proof}
