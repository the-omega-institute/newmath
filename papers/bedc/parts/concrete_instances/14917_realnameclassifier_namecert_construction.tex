Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\RealNameClassifierUp$ packet records equality inspection for two regular Cauchy real names as a finite tolerance classifier rather than as host equality of completed objects. It sits over the regular-name source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyname-namecert}, the regular rational rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the common finite-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[RealNameClassifier source packet]
\label{def:real-name-classifier-source}

$$

with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two source $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ rows whose equality is being inspected.
\item $W$ is the common $\StreamNameUp$ window schedule exposing the same finite precision requests to both sources.
\item $D_A$ and $D_B$ are the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows read from the two sources along $W$.
\item $T$ is the finite tolerance classifier row, recording that every inspected precision request is discharged by a dyadic comparison between the corresponding observations of $A$ and $B$.
\item $R_A$ and $R_B$ are the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows attached to the two regular Cauchy names.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two source names, common stream windows, dyadic tolerance rows, readback rows, classifier row, package provenance, seal row, and local naming row.
\item $K$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from $A,B$ through $W,D_A,D_B,T,R_A,R_B$.

\item $S$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the tolerance classifier and both regular rational readbacks have been exposed.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealNameClassifierUp}$ row.

The packet has no coordinate for quotient Cauchy classes, host sequence equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealNameClassifier tolerance obligation]
\label{thm:real-name-classifier-tolerance-obligation}
For an accepted $\RealNameClassifierUp$ packet $C=(A,B,W,D_A,D_B,T,R_A,R_B,H,K,P,S,N)$, every classifier query factors through the common window $W$, the two dyadic tolerance rows $D_A,D_B$, and the finite tolerance classifier row $T$ before either regular rational readback or terminal real seal is consumed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet. The only precision schedule visible to both regular Cauchy names is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ row $W$, so a classifier query has no independent observation route for $A$ or $B$.

The dyadic tolerance rows $D_A$ and $D_B$ are read from $W$. The classifier row $T$ records the finite comparison of these two displayed dyadic observations at the requested precisions.

The readback rows $R_A,R_B$ and the seal row $S$ are downstream of this finite comparison. Hence every equality-facing consumer first consumes the common window and dyadic tolerance data, then the tolerance classifier, and only afterward reads regular rational or real-facing rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealNameClassifier refinement stability]
\label{thm:real-name-classifier-refinement-stability}
Let $C=(A,B,W,D_A,D_B,T,R_A,R_B,H,K,P,S,N)$ be an accepted $\RealNameClassifierUp$ packet. If a downstream consumer refines the common window $W$ by a displayed $\StreamNameUp$ transport and replays the two dyadic rows through $H$ and $K$, then the resulting classifier read is the same local $\NameCert_{\RealNameClassifierUp}$ surface. The refinement changes only the finite observation window, not the two source names, dyadic tolerance obligation, regular rational readbacks, package provenance, or terminal seal boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the displayed refinement of $W$. The transport row $H$ identifies the refined window with the original common schedule in the componentwise $\hsame$ sense recorded by the packet.

Replay by $K$ carries both dyadic rows through the same refinement. Thus the comparison row $T$ is read after transporting $D_A$ and $D_B$ along a single common route, not by changing one source independently of the other.

The remaining rows $R_A,R_B,P,S,N$ are attached to the same transported packet. The local naming surface is therefore stable under the displayed refinement, because no new source coordinate or external equality principle is introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealNameClassifier Real seal boundary]
\label{thm:real-name-classifier-seal-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RealNameClassifierUp$ packet reaches the seal row $S$ only after the two $\RegularCauchyNameUp$ source rows, the common $\StreamNameUp$ window, the two $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows, the tolerance classifier row, and the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows have been displayed. The seal exports no quotient equality of Cauchy names, host sequence equality, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy witness, or $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier of \autoref{def:real-name-classifier-source}. The terminal seal row $S$ appears after the source rows, common finite window, dyadic tolerance rows, tolerance classifier, regular rational readbacks, transport, replay, and provenance rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-name-classifier-tolerance-obligation}. A seal-facing consumer cannot bypass $W,D_A,D_B,T$, because those rows are the only route by which the classifier query is discharged.

The carrier contains no coordinate for any of the excluded principles. Consequently the $\RealUp$ seal is only a boundary row for the displayed finite classifier packet, not a source of quotient equality, host equality, choice, trichotomy, selected limits, or completeness.
\end{proof}
