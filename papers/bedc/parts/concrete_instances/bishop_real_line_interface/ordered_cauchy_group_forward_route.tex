\begin{theorem}[Bishop real-line interface to ordered Cauchy-group forward route]
\label{thm:bishop-real-line-interface-ordered-cauchy-group-forward-route}
Every $\OrderedCauchyGroupUp$ consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ordered-cauchy-group-namecert} that reads the $\BishopRealLineInterfaceUp$ surface factors through the displayed dyadic endpoint and tolerance ledger, finite stream-window row, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, terminal $\RealUp$ seal, and $\CauchyEquivalenceSetoidUp$ comparison row of the present chapter. The ordered Cauchy-group route may consume those rows as the real-line source for order-compatible completion, but it receives no quotient stream equality, selected completed representative, detached completed order, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem from the Bishop interface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $I=(D,W,R,E,Q,H,C,P,N)$ of \autoref{def:bishop-real-line-interface-carrier}. The only value-facing rows are the dyadic endpoint and tolerance ledger $D$, stream-window row $W$, regular readback row $R$, terminal seal $E$, and Cauchy-equivalence comparison row $Q$.

The $\OrderedCauchyGroupUp$ packet reads exactly those rows when it imports a Bishop real-line source for completion. Its own order-compatible completion ledger remains downstream of the displayed $\AbGroupUp$, $\OrderUp$, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ route in \autoref{thm:ordered-cauchy-group-order-compatible-completion}.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same Bishop interface. Since the interface carrier has no coordinate for quotient equality, selected representatives, detached completed orders, choice schedules, trichotomy, or ambient completeness, none of those objects can be passed to the ordered Cauchy-group consumer.
\end{proof}
