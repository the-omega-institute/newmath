\begin{theorem}[External supply signature seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:external-supply-signature-seal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:external-supply-signature-seal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ExternalSupplySignatureSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $S=(O,M,Q,G,D,L,H,C,P,N)$, source-signature exposure through $O,Q$, representation exposure through $M$, GAP and descent exposure through $G,D$, hidden-supply ledger exposure through $L$, structural replay through $H,C,P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The tuple lists one ontic source row, one mathematical representation row, one physical signature row, one GAP socket row, one descent certificate row, one hidden-supply ledger row, and the structural support rows.

Read the sibling rows componentwise. The residue sibling supplies representation-gap discipline, the descent sibling supplies operation-descent discipline, the fit sibling supplies physical-signature compatibility, the GAP socket boundary supplies represented absence, and the registry no-smuggling predicate supplies the export refusal row.

The support rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only this displayed packet. Since no further coordinate exists, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[External supply signature seal descent refusal]
\label{thm:external-supply-signature-seal-descent-refusal}
An accepted $\ExternalSupplySignatureSealUp$ packet refuses any export of the mathematical representation row $M$ as a reality-facing classifier unless the same route displays the physical signature row $Q$, the GAP socket row $G$, the descent certificate row $D$, and the hidden-supply ledger row $L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:external-supply-signature-seal-namecert-obligations}. It makes source-signature exposure, GAP exposure, descent exposure, and ledger exposure separate required rows of the same local certificate.

Use the descent and fit siblings next. \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-signature-descent-namecert} supplies the operation-descent row, while \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-signature-fit-namecert} supplies the physical-signature compatibility row.

Finally attach the GAP socket and no-smuggling siblings. The boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-gap-socket-boundary-namecert} records the represented absence, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registrynosmugglingpredicate-namecert} prevents a registry route from exporting the mathematical row as if the omitted source rows had been supplied. Thus a route missing $Q,G,D,L$ is refused rather than accepted as a seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[External supply signature seal ledger exactness]
\label{thm:external-supply-signature-seal-ledger-exactness}
In every accepted $\ExternalSupplySignatureSealUp$ packet, the hidden-supply ledger $L$ records exactly the source material not exported by $M$ but still required by the physical signature row $Q$, the GAP socket row $G$, and the descent certificate row $D$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the carrier rows $O,M,Q,G,D,L$. The source row $O$ is not identified with its mathematical representation $M$, and the physical signature row $Q$ is not recoverable from $M$ unless the packet displays the GAP, descent, and ledger rows.

Apply the NameCert obligations. They expose $L$ as its own ledger coordinate, not as a consequence of $M$ or $Q$ alone.

The residue, descent, and no-smuggling siblings then fix exactness. Residue records what remains after mathematical identification, descent records which operations survive the route, and the registry predicate refuses an export that hides supply rows. Therefore $L$ is exact for the omitted source material required by $Q,G,D$ and no broader host supply.
\end{proof}
