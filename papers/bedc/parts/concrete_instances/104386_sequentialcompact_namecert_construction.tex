\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SequentialCompactUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive compactness and Brouwer choice-sequence practice, the $\SequentialCompactUp$ packet records sequential compactness only as finite subsequence-window data. It sits over the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the Baire-space inspection discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairespace-namecert}, the finite stream interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet gives compact-metric and Baire-space completion consumers a displayed subsequence route; it is not a host sequential compactness theorem, not countable choice, and not a quotient stream principle.

\begin{definition}[SequentialCompact carrier]
\label{def:sequential-compact-carrier}
A $\SequentialCompactUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(K,B,S,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row whose finite cover and metric inspection data bound the sequence consumer.
\item $B$ is the $\BaireSpaceUp$ cylinder discipline row used when a downstream completion route reads finite choice-sequence windows.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ source-window row, recording the inspected indices and finite request schedule.
\item $W$ is the finite subsequence-window row: selected indices, selected source windows, and the displayed monotonicity evidence for the selected index list.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the selected windows and their rational tail budgets.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached after the regular rational readback is accepted.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $K,B,S,W,R,E$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replays from compact-metric source reads to stream windows, Baire cylinders, subsequence windows, regular rational readbacks, and the terminal real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SequentialCompactUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\BaireSpaceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialCompactUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $K,B,S,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for an externally chosen subsequence, quotient equality of streams, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SequentialCompact NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:sequential-compact-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SequentialCompactUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(K,B,S,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialCompactUp}$ surface consists exactly of finite rows for carrier admission, compact-metric source exposure, Baire-cylinder exposure, stream-window inspection, subsequence-window monotonicity, regular rational readback, Real seal nonescape, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. No obligation row may be discharged by host sequential compactness, countable choice, quotient stream equality, an externally selected subsequence, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:sequential-compact-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the compact metric source, Baire cylinder row, stream window, subsequence window, regular rational readback, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

The compactness and choice-sequence reads are finite. The $\CompactMetricUp$ row supplies only displayed metric and cover data, while the $\BaireSpaceUp$ row supplies only finite cylinder access before any selected window is consumed.

The subsequence evidence is the row $W$. Its selected indices, selected source windows, and monotonicity evidence are packet data, and the $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows transport and replay those same data without adding a second selection source.

The readback then passes through $R$ and $E$. Since neither row has a coordinate for host sequential compactness, countable choice, quotient stream equality, an external subsequence, or ambient Real completeness, the local NameCert surface is exhausted by the displayed finite rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SequentialCompact finite subsequence handoff]
\label{thm:sequential-compact-finite-subsequence-handoff}
Every accepted $\SequentialCompactUp$ packet consumed by a compact-metric or Baire-space completion route must expose the finite handoff
$$
\CompactMetricUp,\quad \BaireSpaceUp,\quad \StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
in that order: compact source data and Baire cylinder data are read before stream windows, stream windows are read before the displayed subsequence-window row, and the selected windows are read before the regular rational handoff and Real seal. The handoff exports no host subsequence selector, quotient stream equality, open-cover compactness theorem, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $Q=(K,B,S,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The rows $K$ and $B$ are the only compact-metric and Baire-space sources available to the consumer.

Next read the stream and subsequence windows. The row $S$ gives finite source-window access, and the row $W$ gives the selected finite windows and monotonicity evidence over those source windows.

Finally apply the readback and seal rows. The row $R$ consumes only the selected windows from $W$, and the row $E$ seals only the accepted regular rational readback. The transport, continuation, provenance, and NameCert rows preserve this same handoff, so no host subsequence selector, quotient stream equality, open-cover compactness theorem, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness can be exported.
\end{proof}
