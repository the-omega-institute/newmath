\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealApproximationEnvelopeUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-realapproximationenvelope-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\RealApproximationEnvelopeUp$ interface names the finite observation packet used by Real and Completion consumers before any completed real value is available. It packages a tolerance request, a finite stream window, a dyadic observation, a regular-sequence classifier row, and a $\RealUp$ seal handoff into one local $\NameCert$ surface. The packet is designed for diagonal representatives, stationary rational finite windows, Cauchy-modulus extraction, and RealUp classifier handoffs without importing host real equality, quotient stream equality, selected limits, or ambient completeness.

The envelope consumes the finite-window route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic observation ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular-rational classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the seal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. These sibling surfaces are the rows $W,D,R,S$ of the carrier below, and the edge is a finite observation-envelope route rather than an independent completed real.

\begin{definition}[Real approximation-envelope carrier]
\label{def:real-approximation-envelope-carrier}
A $\RealApproximationEnvelopeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(\varepsilon,W,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\varepsilon$ is the tolerance request row naming the precision scale to be answered by the envelope;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row selected for that tolerance request;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation row read through $W$, including the displayed endpoint, radius, and finite arithmetic ledger;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier row that reads the same window as a regular rational observation;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal handoff row receiving the observation packet for downstream real-name consumers;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the tolerance request, window, dyadic observation, classifier row, seal row, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from tolerance request to window, from window to dyadic observation, from dyadic observation to classifier row, and from classifier row to seal handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealApproximationEnvelopeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier stores no host real equality, no quotient stream equality, no selected limit, no completed-real inverse law, and no ambient completeness premise.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real approximation-envelope NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-approximation-envelope-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RealApproximationEnvelopeUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:real-approximation-envelope-carrier} consists exactly of tolerance-request admission, selected $\StreamNameUp$ window admission, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation admission, $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier readback, $\RealUp$ seal handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ route replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet $E$. Its rows list the tolerance request, finite window, dyadic observation ledger, regular rational classifier row, RealUp seal handoff, transport witnesses, continuation routes, provenance, and local certificate.

The observation side first reads $\varepsilon$ and then follows the continuation route to $W$. The selected window exposes only finite stream data, and $D$ records the dyadic endpoint, radius, and arithmetic ledger obtained from that window.

The classifier side routes the same finite read through $R$ and then to the seal row $S$. The stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows ensure that the classifier handoff is tied to the same tolerance request and window, while $P$ and $N$ record the package provenance and local name.

No remaining coordinate can supply host real equality, quotient stream equality, selected limits, completed-real inverse laws, or ambient completeness. The obligations therefore exhaust the finite Real-and-Completion observation envelope.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real approximation-envelope consumer boundary]
\label{thm:real-approximation-envelope-consumer-boundary}
Every diagonal-representative, stationary rational finite-window, Cauchy-modulus, or $\RealUp$ classifier consumer of an accepted $\RealApproximationEnvelopeUp$ packet must read the rows
$$
\varepsilon,W,D,R,S,H,C,P,N.
$$
Such a consumer may inspect the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier row, and the $\RealUp$ seal handoff, but it cannot replace that route by host real equality, quotient stream equality, a selected limit, a completed-real inverse law, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:real-approximation-envelope-namecert-obligations}. They force every observation request to pass from $\varepsilon$ through the selected window $W$ before any dyadic or regular rational data is consumed.

Diagonal representatives and stationary rational finite-window consumers read the finite window side of the packet. The dyadic ledger $D$ records the observed endpoint data, and the classifier row $R$ records the regular rational readback for the same selected window.

Cauchy-modulus and $\RealUp$ classifier consumers then reach the real-facing boundary through $S$. The transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows keep the seal attached to the same finite observation route.

The carrier inventory is exhausted by the displayed rows. Since none of them is host real equality, quotient stream equality, a selected limit, a completed-real inverse law, or an ambient completeness theorem, no accepting consumer can export such data.
\end{proof}
