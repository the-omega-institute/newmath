Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\ApartnessCompletionUp$ packet records the generic separated completion boundary for sources whose equality information is exposed through apartness evidence. It sits between the real-specific boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realapartnesscompletion-namecert}, the Hausdorff separation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert}, the constructive metric completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-constructive-metric-completion-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The packet is a finite boundary object for Real and Completion consumers; it is not a quotient completion, a host equality principle, a selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Apartness completion carrier]
\label{def:apartness-completion-carrier}
An $\ApartnessCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(X,Q,M,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the apartness source row, carrying the positive separation evidence used by the completion boundary.
\item $Q$ is the $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ separated-completion route attached to the same source row.
\item $M$ is the $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$ completion route through which the separated source is replayed.
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window schedule exposed to consumers of the completion read.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback extracted from those stream windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the requested positive gaps, radii, and comparison cells.
\item $E$ is the optional $\RealUp$ seal handoff, present only when the generic completion route is consumed by a Real-facing boundary.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the apartness source, separated route, completion route, window schedule, readback row, tolerance ledger, optional seal, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from apartness inspection through separated completion, constructive metric completion, stream windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance, and the optional Real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ApartnessCompletionUp$, $\HausdorffCompletionUp$, $\ConstructiveMetricCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ApartnessCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion classes, host equality of completed points, selected limits, countable-choice schedules, trichotomy rows, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Apartness completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:apartness-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ApartnessCompletionUp$ carrier $A=(X,Q,M,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ApartnessCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed apartness source row, separated completion route, constructive metric completion route, stream windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, optional Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item apartness-source exactness, requiring the positive separation row $X$ to be displayed before any separated completion route $Q$ or completion route $M$ is consumed;
\item finite readback discipline, requiring each consumer observation to pass through $S$, $R$, and $D$ before the optional seal $E$ is available;
\item componentwise stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to transport and replay only the displayed rows $X,Q,M,S,R,D,E,P,N$;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $P$ and $N$ to export no quotient completion class, host equality, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy row, or ambient-completeness principle.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:apartness-completion-carrier}. The tuple displays the apartness source, separated completion route, constructive metric completion route, finite windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, optional Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

Apartness-source exactness follows from the order of the displayed route. A consumer can inspect $Q$ or $M$ only after the positive separation row $X$ has been named, so separated completion cannot hide its apartness source.

The readback discipline is carried by $S,R,D,E$. Each completion observation first opens a finite stream window, then reads the regular rational row, then consults the dyadic tolerance ledger; the optional Real seal is downstream of that finite route.

Componentwise stability and provenance exhaustion are the roles of $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, package, and name only the displayed packet, and therefore cannot add quotient completion classes, host equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Apartness completion Real seal boundary]
\label{thm:apartness-completion-real-seal-boundary}
Let $A=(X,Q,M,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\ApartnessCompletionUp$ packet. Every Real-facing completion read of $A$, when the optional seal row $E$ is present, factors through the finite route
$$
X,Q,M,S,R,D \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the Real seal receives only the displayed apartness source, Hausdorff separated route, constructive metric completion route, stream windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, transport rows, continuation replay, provenance, and local NameCert row. It receives no quotient completion, host equality of completed points, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy row, or ambient-completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Real-facing completion read. Projection of the carrier gives a single ordered route from the apartness source row $X$ through the separated route $Q$ and constructive metric completion route $M$.

The finite observation rows then exhaust the readback path. The window schedule $S$, regular readback $R$, and dyadic tolerance ledger $D$ must be displayed before the Real seal row $E$ can be inspected.

Finally, the transport, replay, provenance, and local name rows $H,C,P,N$ carry the same route without adding coordinates. Row exhaustion therefore blocks quotient completions, host equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, and ambient completeness from entering the Real seal boundary.
\end{proof}
