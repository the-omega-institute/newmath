\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BayesianUp$}

The Bayesian-update interface $\BayesianUp$ packages the conditional-probability rule transforming a prior distribution and likelihood into a posterior distribution. It depends on $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-distribution-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\CondExpUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-condexp-namecert}).

\begin{theorem}[Bayesian posterior source obligation]
\label{thm:bayesian-posterior-source-obligation}
Let $\mathcal{B}$ be a Bayesian update packet over
$\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\CondExpUp}$. The packet exposes,
as $\mathsf{BHist}$ data, the prior distribution row $\pi$, the likelihood row
$\lambda$, the evidence row $e$, the posterior endpoint $\omega$, the classifier
comparison $\hsame(\mathsf{Bayes}(\pi,\lambda,e),\omega)$, and the
normalisation row $\Cont(\pi,\lambda,e;\omega)$. These are the only public
source obligations of the Bayesian posterior surface; no host probability
kernel, measurable-space quotient, or external conditional-density equality is
exported by the certificate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BayesianUp.BayesianPosteriorSurface\_source\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed update packet. Its $\DistributionUp$ source supplies the
prior distribution row and the posterior endpoint as distribution-side
$\mathsf{BHist}$ witnesses. Its $\CondExpUp$ source supplies the conditioning
row used to read the likelihood and evidence ledger.

The classifier projection is the displayed history-sameness row comparing the
Bayesian update expression with the posterior endpoint. The continuation
projection is the normalisation row, recording that the prior, likelihood, and
evidence data are continued to the visible posterior endpoint.

Repacking these projected rows gives exactly the stated public surface. Since
the packet inventory consists only of the two dependency certificates, the
evidence ledger, the posterior endpoint, the classifier row, the continuation
row, and provenance, there is no field from which a host probability kernel or
external conditional-density equality can be read.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BayesianUp.BayesianPosteriorPacket\_source\_obligation}

\begin{theorem}[Bayesian update ledger exactness]
\label{thm:bayesian-update-ledger-exactness}
For any accepted Bayesian update packet, every public posterior read factors
through the displayed $\DistributionUp$ source, the $\CondExpUp$ conditioning
row, the evidence ledger, the posterior endpoint, and the provenance row. A
consumer can therefore read the posterior only by projecting those rows and
repacking them into the displayed classifier and normalisation obligations.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BayesianUp.BayesianPosteriorSurface\_update\_ledger\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted packet into its dependency rows. The $\DistributionUp$
component is the sole source for the prior distribution row and the posterior
endpoint. The $\CondExpUp$ component is the sole source for the conditioning row
that binds the likelihood to the evidence ledger.

The public posterior read then has only two operations available. First, project
the classifier comparison $\hsame(\mathsf{Bayes}(\pi,\lambda,e),\omega)$ from
the displayed packet rows. Second, project the continuation row
$\Cont(\pi,\lambda,e;\omega)$ that records the normalised endpoint.

Finally, the provenance row records the $\Pkg$ origin of the distribution,
conditioning, evidence, endpoint, classifier, and normalisation rows. Repacking
only these projected rows reconstructs the public posterior read, while any
read requiring a host kernel, an unstated probability space, or an external
density equality has no source field in the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Bayesian posterior packet carrier]
\label{def:bayesian-posterior-packet-carrier}
A $\BayesianUp$ posterior packet is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
\mathcal{B}=(\mathcal{D}_{\pi},\mathcal{D}_{\omega},\mathcal{C},\pi,\lambda,e,\omega,\beta,H,N,P)
$$
with the following displayed rows. The rows $\mathcal{D}_{\pi}$ and $\mathcal{D}_{\omega}$ are $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ source certificates carrying, respectively, the prior distribution row $\pi$ and the posterior distribution endpoint $\omega$ as distribution data in the sense of \autoref{def:distribution-pushforward-carrier}. The row $\mathcal{C}$ is a $\NameCert_{\CondExpUp}$ source certificate carrying the likelihood row $\lambda$ and evidence row $e$ through the singleton conditional-expectation packet of \autoref{thm:condexp-carrier-obligation}. The row $\beta$ is the Bayesian update history computed from $\pi$, $\lambda$, and $e$. The classifier row is
$$
H=\hsame(\beta,\omega),
$$
and the normalisation row is
$$
N=\Cont(\pi,\lambda,e;\omega).
$$
The provenance row $P:\Pkg$ names exactly the two distribution certificates, the conditional-expectation certificate, the prior row, the likelihood row, the evidence row, the posterior endpoint, the classifier row, and the normalisation row. The packet is accepted precisely when all these rows are present with this provenance and no additional posterior-source coordinate is exposed.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bayesian posterior packet carrier introduction]
\label{thm:bayesian-posterior-packet-carrier-introduction}
Suppose the following data are supplied under the $\BayesianUp$ setup: a $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ prior source carrying $\pi$, a $\NameCert_{\DistributionUp}$ posterior source carrying $\omega$, a $\NameCert_{\CondExpUp}$ row carrying the likelihood and evidence rows $\lambda$ and $e$, a Bayesian update history $\beta$ read from $\pi$, $\lambda$, and $e$, a comparison row $H=\hsame(\beta,\omega)$, a normalisation row $N=\Cont(\pi,\lambda,e;\omega)$, and a provenance row $P:\Pkg$ naming exactly these sources and rows. Then these rows assemble an accepted $\BayesianUp$ posterior packet in the carrier of \autoref{def:bayesian-posterior-packet-carrier}. Its public posterior source is exactly
$$
(\mathcal{D}_{\pi},\mathcal{D}_{\omega},\mathcal{C},\pi,\lambda,e,\omega,H,N,P).
$$
In particular, every public posterior read factors through the displayed prior, likelihood, evidence, posterior, classifier, normalisation, and provenance rows, and no host probability kernel, ambient measurable-space quotient, or external conditional-density equality is part of the accepted packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Form the tuple
$$
\mathcal{B}=(\mathcal{D}_{\pi},\mathcal{D}_{\omega},\mathcal{C},\pi,\lambda,e,\omega,\beta,H,N,P).
$$
The two distribution certificates license the prior and posterior distribution rows by the carrier discipline of \autoref{def:distribution-pushforward-carrier}. The conditional-expectation certificate licenses the likelihood and evidence rows by the packet discipline of \autoref{thm:condexp-carrier-obligation}.

The supplied comparison row $H$ identifies the computed Bayesian update history $\beta$ with the displayed posterior endpoint $\omega$ through $\hsame$. The supplied normalisation row $N$ records that the visible prior, likelihood, and evidence rows continue to the visible posterior endpoint. The provenance row $P$ names exactly these dependency, classifier, and continuation rows.

These are precisely the acceptance clauses of \autoref{def:bayesian-posterior-packet-carrier}, so the tuple is an accepted $\BayesianUp$ posterior packet. Projection of an accepted packet returns only its displayed coordinates. Hence every public posterior read is obtained from the two distribution sources, the conditional-expectation source, $\pi$, $\lambda$, $e$, $\omega$, $H$, $N$, and $P$.

Conversely, each row in the displayed source list is a coordinate of the accepted tuple, so the list is not missing any public posterior-source row. Since the carrier definition contains no coordinate for a host probability kernel, an ambient measurable-space quotient, or an external conditional-density equality, no such datum can be read from the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
