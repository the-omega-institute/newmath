The $\RegularCauchyCompletionFrontierUp$ packet realizes the Phase Real and Completion route of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness} as a single finite frontier between regular rational names and the completed real-facing seal. It consumes the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the displayed refinement route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-modulus-refinement-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The frontier is not a completed real by itself; it is the finite selector, cofinal-tail, agreement, and seal-handoff surface a consumer must traverse before any $\RealUp$ limit is public.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy completion frontier carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-completion-frontier-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyCompletionFrontierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(D,S,R,L,M,A,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and comparison ledger used by the regular Cauchy readback;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite observation schedule through which every window request is exposed;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational source row;
\item $L$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row, read only after the source, selector, and cofinal-tail rows are displayed;
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ refinement row binding the source windows, selected window, and tolerance readback;
\item $A$ is the diagonal-selector row that chooses finite windows from the displayed schedules and never from a host choice function;
\item $T$ is the cofinal-tail agreement row, comparing the selected regular tails before the completion seal is consumed;
\item $E$ is the completion-seal handoff row from the selected regular tail to the $\RealUp$ seal;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for $D,S,R,L,M,A,T,E$;
\item $C$ is the $\Cont$ replay row for the same source, selector, tail, and seal route;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCompletionFrontierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
No coordinate of $F$ is a quotient stream, a selected limit, host real equality, a countable-choice selector, or an ambient completeness witness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy completion frontier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-frontier-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-completion-frontier-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCompletionFrontierUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $F=(D,S,R,L,M,A,T,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item source-window exposure, requiring every consumer to read $D,S,R,M$ before any diagonal selector or completion seal is public;
\item selector and cofinal-tail stability, requiring $A$ and $T$ to be transported by the same $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ route as the source rows;
\item completion-seal handoff, requiring $E$ to expose the selected cofinal-tail agreement before the $\RealUp$ seal row $L$ can be consumed;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring every accepted Real and Completion consumer to factor through the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-completion-frontier-carrier}. The finite packet displays the dyadic, schedule, regular-source, refinement, selector, tail, seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows as one route.

Source-window exposure reads the sibling surfaces in the order forced by the carrier: $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ supplies tolerances, $\StreamNameUp$ supplies finite windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ supplies regular readback, and $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ binds the selected window and tolerance route.

Selector and tail stability use the displayed $H$ and $C$ rows. They transport and replay the same source route through $A$ and $T$, so the diagonal selector and cofinal-tail agreement cannot be detached from the source window.

The completion-seal handoff is the row $E$ followed by $L$. Since $L$ is exposed only after the selected cofinal tail, a consumer cannot read a $\RealUp$ limit without the displayed seal handoff.

Ledger non-escape follows from $P$ and $N$. The package and local certificate name only the displayed route, so quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, and ambient completeness have no certificate coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy completion frontier nonescape]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-frontier-nonescape}
Every accepted $\RegularCauchyCompletionFrontierUp$ consumer factors through
$$
D,S,R,L,M,A,T,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, no consumer can export a quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness witness as an alternate Real and Completion route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier. Its public route contains the dyadic tolerance row, stream schedule, regular rational source, Real seal, modulus refinement, diagonal selector, cofinal-tail agreement, completion seal, transport, continuation, package, and local certificate rows.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-frontier-namecert-obligations}. Source-window exposure places $D,S,R,M$ before selector access, selector and tail stability bind $A,T$ to the same route, and completion-seal handoff places $E$ before $L$.

The forbidden objects have no row in the packet. A quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality proof, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness witness therefore cannot supply another route for an accepted frontier consumer.
\end{proof}
