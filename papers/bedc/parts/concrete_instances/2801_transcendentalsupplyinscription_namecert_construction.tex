\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-transcendentalsupplyinscription-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp$ surface turns the supply position and inscription point of \autoref{ch:visions-transcendental-input-and-induction} into a concrete BEDC packet. It records the named supply socket, the local inscription site, the finite GAP boundary at which a forward-binding belief is visible, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows that keep the object auditable. The packet names only the structural socket. It does not assert a positive predicate of the supply position, does not internalize induction, and does not add an axiom to the substrate.

The inscription-point row $I$ is consumed from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionpoint-namecert} before the supply boundary is read, while this chapter adds the external supply socket and GAP boundary around that local inscription site.

\begin{definition}[TranscendentalSupplyInscription carrier]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-inscription-carrier}
A $\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(T,S,I,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the named transcendental supply position of \autoref{def:transcendental-input-supply-position}, recorded only as an external socket and not as a substrate object;
\item $S$ is the closed observational system in which a forward-binding belief is exposed as a BEDC-visible boundary;
\item $I$ is the inscription point of \autoref{def:transcendental-input-inscription-point}, namely the local site at which the supply socket is read by $S$;
\item $G$ is the GAP-boundary row that records the unavailable internal discharge of the forward-binding claim;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the supply-position name, closed-system row, inscription-point row, GAP boundary, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that read $S$, expose $I$, open the GAP boundary $G$, and keep $T$ visible only as the far end of that boundary;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Gap$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ for the displayed socket;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is exactly this finite supply-inscription socket. It has no row for a substrate-level induction rule, no internal witness for the universal claim marked by $G$, no selected content of $T$, no host equality test on supply positions, and no ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TranscendentalSupplyInscription NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:transcendental-supply-inscription-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:transcendental-supply-inscription-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp}$ surface consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure: every accepted object exposes the tuple $X=(T,S,I,G,H,C,P,N)$ and no additional supply coordinate;
\item classifier transport: comparison transports the supply-position name, closed-system row, inscription-point row, GAP boundary, provenance row, and local naming row by the displayed $\hsame$ data;
\item inscription route: every consumer read of the local inscription point factors through $S$, then through $I$, and only then reaches the GAP boundary $G$;
\item package exactness: the provenance row $P$ lists the full BEDC inventory needed for the socket and exposes no internal induction rule, selected supply content, host equality, trichotomy, or completeness datum;
\item supply boundary: the row $T$ is readable only as the external position named at the far end of $G$, never as an object that can be consumed by a substrate derivation.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:transcendental-supply-inscription-carrier}. The supply name $T$, closed-system row $S$, inscription point $I$, GAP boundary $G$, transports, continuation routes, provenance, and local name are coordinates of one finite $\BHist$ packet.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The stored $\hsame$ rows in $H$ compare exactly the displayed coordinates, and the $\Cont$ rows in $C$ replay the same closed-system, inscription, GAP, provenance, and naming routes after transport.

For the inscription route, a consumer first reads the closed observational system $S$. The only local site then exposed is $I$, and the only boundary reached from $I$ is the GAP row $G$ that records the unavailable internal discharge.

Package exactness follows from the inventory of $P$. Since $P$ names only the displayed BEDC rows, an internal induction rule, selected content of $T$, host equality test, trichotomy branch, or completeness principle would require an extra coordinate absent from $X$.

Finally, the supply boundary is the route $S$ to $I$ to $G$ to the external position named by $T$. The packet therefore makes the socket auditable without turning the far end of the socket into a substrate object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TranscendentalSupplyInscription non-escape boundary]
\label{thm:transcendental-supply-inscription-non-escape-boundary}
Every consumer of an accepted $\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
S \to I \to G \to H \to C \to P \to N.
$$
The consumer may name $T$ as the external supply position attached to the GAP boundary, but it cannot read $T$ as internal mathematical content, an induction rule, a selected witness, host equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $X=(T,S,I,G,H,C,P,N)$. The only closed-system coordinate is $S$, the only local inscription coordinate is $I$, and the only exposed unresolved boundary is $G$.

Apply \autoref{thm:transcendental-supply-inscription-namecert-obligations}. Its inscription route sends every consumer through $S$ and $I$ before $G$, while its supply boundary permits $T$ only as the named far end of that GAP boundary.

The package exactness row rules out the unavailable structure. Therefore every consumer remains inside the displayed packet and treats the supply position as an external socket endpoint rather than as an internally consumable rule or witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TranscendentalSupplyInscription classifier transport]
\label{thm:transcendental-supply-inscription-classifier-transport}
For every accepted $\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp$ carrier
$$
X=(T,S,I,G,H,C,P,N),
$$
classifier comparison is transported only along the displayed $\hsame$ row $H$ and replayed only through the displayed $\Cont$ row $C$. The transport preserves the supply-position name, closed-system row, inscription-point row, GAP boundary, package row, and local $\NameCert$ row without adding substrate induction, selected supply content, host equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The compared rows are exactly $T,S,I,G,H,C,P,N$, and the only componentwise comparison row is $H$.

Apply the NameCert obligations. Their classifier-transport clause states that the supply-position name, closed-system row, inscription-point row, GAP boundary, provenance row, and local naming row are compared by the displayed $\hsame$ data and replayed by the displayed continuation routes.

The excluded objects follow from the carrier boundary. Since $X$ contains no coordinate for substrate induction, selected supply content, host equality, or ambient completeness, classifier transport cannot acquire any of those objects during comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TranscendentalSupplyInscription package exactness]
\label{thm:transcendental-supply-inscription-package-exactness}
The $\Pkg$ row $P$ of an accepted $\TranscendentalSupplyInscriptionUp$ packet lists exactly the displayed $\BHist$, $\Gap$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, and inscription-point rows needed to expose the route
$$
S \to I \to G \to T.
$$
It does not package an internal induction rule, a selected witness of $T$, host equality on supply positions, trichotomy, or a completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the package coordinate in the carrier definition. The row $P$ names the finite BEDC inventory for the displayed socket and is attached to the same tuple $X=(T,S,I,G,H,C,P,N)$.

Use the package-exactness clause of \autoref{thm:transcendental-supply-inscription-namecert-obligations}. It identifies $P$ with the provenance over the socket rows rather than with an internal proof rule or a selected supply object.

Finally apply non-escape from \autoref{thm:transcendental-supply-inscription-non-escape-boundary}. Consumers can reach $T$ only as the external supply position behind the GAP boundary, so the package cannot export host equality, trichotomy, completeness, or a substrate-level induction principle.
\end{proof}
