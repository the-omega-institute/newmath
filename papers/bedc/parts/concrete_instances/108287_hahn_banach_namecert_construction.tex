\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HahnBanachUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-hahn-banach-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Hahn and Banach, Bourbaki, and Bishop-style constructive functional analysis, the $\HahnBanachUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface for bounded scalar functional extension rows. It sits beside the normed-space and Banach rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normedspace-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-banach-namecert}, the dual carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-banach-dual-namecert}, the bounded-operator row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedlinearoperator-namecert}, the functional-analysis boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-functionalanalysis-namecert}, and the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names a displayed finite extension ledger only; it is not the unrestricted classical Hahn--Banach theorem.

\begin{definition}[Hahn--Banach extension carrier]
\label{def:hahn-banach-carrier}
A $\HahnBanachUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,B,D,F,E,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the ambient $\NormedSpaceUp$ or $\BanachUp$ row on which the extension is read.
\item $B$ is the displayed subspace row, including the finite incidence data saying which inspected vectors lie in the subspace.
\item $D$ is the $\BanachDualUp$ and $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$ row for the bounded scalar functional on $B$.
\item $F$ is the $\FunctionalAnalysisUp$ context row that records the local functional-analysis consumer boundary.
\item $E$ is the extension row, displaying the scalar functional on the inspected ambient vectors and its agreement with $D$ on the subspace row.
\item $L$ is the norm-bound ledger, comparing the displayed bound for $D$ with the displayed bound for $E$ through $\RealUp$ endpoints.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,B,D,F,E,L,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from ambient-space reads through the subspace, bounded-functional, extension, and norm-bound rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HahnBanachUp$, $\NormedSpaceUp$, $\BanachUp$, $\BanachDualUp$, $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$, $\FunctionalAnalysisUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HahnBanachUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for quotient dual spaces, selected extensions, ultrafilter arguments, countable choice, Zorn maximality, or unrestricted ambient extension.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hahn--Banach NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hahn-banach-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\HahnBanachUp$ carrier $K=(X,B,D,F,E,L,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\HahnBanachUp}$ surface consists exactly of ambient normed or Banach admission, displayed subspace admission, bounded scalar functional admission, local functional-analysis context, extension-row agreement, norm-bound ledger exactness, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. No extension obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:hahn-banach-carrier}. The rows $X,B,D,F,E,L,H,C,P,N$ are the full public inventory.

The bounded-functional read starts at the subspace row $B$ and the scalar-functional row $D$. The extension row $E$ can be inspected only with the same ambient row $X$ and the same subspace incidence data.

The norm-bound ledger is $L$. It compares the displayed bound for $D$ with the displayed bound for $E$ through $\RealUp$ endpoints, while $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the same route.

Thus the NameCert obligations are exactly the listed finite rows. The unrestricted theorem, quotient duals, selected extensions, ultrafilter arguments, and choice principles have no carrier coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hahn--Banach bounded functional extension route]
\label{thm:hahn-banach-bounded-functional-extension-route}
Every bounded scalar functional extension read of an accepted $\HahnBanachUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\NormedSpaceUp,\ \BanachUp,\ \BanachDualUp,\ \BoundedLinearOperatorUp,\ \FunctionalAnalysisUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
The route first fixes the ambient and subspace rows, then reads the bounded scalar functional, then reads the displayed extension row and the norm-bound ledger, and only then exposes transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. It exports no unrestricted classical Hahn--Banach theorem, quotient dual space, selected extension, ultrafilter argument, countable choice, or detached extension principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let $K=(X,B,D,F,E,L,H,C,P,N)$ be accepted. The carrier projection gives the ambient row $X$, the subspace row $B$, the bounded scalar functional row $D$, the functional-analysis context row $F$, and the extension row $E$.

The public extension route reads $X$ and $B$ before $D$. Only then can the consumer inspect $E$, because agreement with the original functional is meaningful only over the displayed subspace incidence row.

The norm-bound ledger $L$ is read after the two functional rows. It records the displayed $\RealUp$ comparison between the original bound and the extension bound, so the consumer receives a finite bound ledger rather than an ambient domination theorem.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve this finite route. Since no row names quotient dual spaces, selected extensions, ultrafilter arguments, countable choice, or unrestricted Hahn--Banach extension, none of those objects can be exported by the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
