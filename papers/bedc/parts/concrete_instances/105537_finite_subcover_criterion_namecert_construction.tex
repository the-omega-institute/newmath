\label{def:finite-subcover-criterion-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $E$ is the $\FiniteEpsilonNetUp$ center-bundle row for the requested positive scale.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ or located source-window row when the compact source is an interval-facing carrier.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row attached to endpoint, radius, and distance comparisons already displayed by the source.
\item $Q$ is the cover-read ledger, recording for each bundled center the finite witness row that covers the corresponding metric ball.
\item $D$ is the dyadic or rational comparison ledger certifying that the center radius and mesh slack fit the requested coverage scale.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact source, finite-net row, located window, Real seal, cover-read ledger, comparison ledger, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.

\begin{theorem}[FiniteSubcoverCriterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-subcover-criterion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteSubcoverCriterionUp$ carrier $S=(K,E,L,R,Q,D,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteSubcoverCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the compact metric source, finite epsilon-net row, located source-window row, Real seal, cover-read ledger, rational comparison ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item finite-net exposure, requiring every cover read to display the $\FiniteEpsilonNetUp$ center bundle $E$ before any covering witness is inspected;
\item source compatibility, requiring $K,L,R$ to be read as the compact metric, located-window, and Real-seal rows already carried by sibling chapters;
\item radius exactness, requiring $Q$ and $D$ to record the finite center-to-cover and radius-slack checks that make the criterion usable by downstream compactness consumers;
\item non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed packet and to export no open-cover compactness, countable choice, selected representatives, host finite-subcover operation, quotient real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-subcover-criterion-carrier}. Its coordinates are exactly the compact source, finite net, located source window, Real seal, cover-read ledger, comparison ledger, and structural rows.

Finite-net exposure is immediate from the order of the displayed packet. A cover consumer must first read $E$, so each later covering witness is tied to a named center bundle rather than to an arbitrary family of open sets.

Source compatibility is supplied by $K,L,R$. These rows identify the compact metric, located-window, and Real-seal surfaces already present in the sibling chapters, while $Q$ and $D$ record only finite coverage and comparison checks.

Finally, the refused objects have no coordinate in $S$. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same finite rows, so no accepted packet exports open-cover compactness, countable choice, selected representatives, host finite-subcover selection, quotient real equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}
