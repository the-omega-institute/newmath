\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GrothendieckGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-grothendieck-group-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Grothendieck's group-completion construction, the $\GrothendieckGroupUp$ packet records the standard passage from a commutative monoid to its universal abelian group as finite BEDC NameCert data. It is anchored by the monoid carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monoid-namecert}, the abelian-group target surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-abgroup-namecert}, the semiring-to-additive-completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-semiring-namecert}, and the generator discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-freemonoid-namecert}. The packet names a displayed difference-pair congruence and its universal additive handoff; it does not introduce a primitive quotient group, ambient setoid completion, or external localization theorem.

\begin{definition}[Grothendieck group carrier]
\label{def:grothendieck-group-carrier}
A $\GrothendieckGroupUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(M,X,D,Q,A,U,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the source $\MonoidUp$ row, exposing the commutative additive operation, zero row, and BEDC monoid laws used by the completion.
\item $X$ is the generator row, recording the formal generator map $x\mapsto [x,0]$ from the monoid surface into the completion packet.
\item $D$ is the difference-pair row, whose displayed elements are finite pairs $(a,b)$ read as the formal difference $a-b$.
\item $Q$ is the congruence row, generated by the finite relation
$$
(a,b)\sim(c,d)
$$
whenever a displayed witness $t$ satisfies $a+d+t=c+b+t$ inside the source monoid row $M$.
\item $A$ is the additive-group row, carrying zero $[(0,0)]$, inverse $[(a,b)]\mapsto[(b,a)]$, and addition $[(a,b)]+[(c,d)]=[(a+c,b+d)]$ after the congruence row $Q$ has been displayed.
\item $U$ is the universal handoff row from $M$ to any accepted $\AbGroupUp$ row: a monoid homomorphism out of $M$ factors through $X,D,Q,A$ by sending $[(a,b)]$ to $f(a)-f(b)$ in the target abelian group.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the monoid row, generator row, difference-pair row, congruence row, additive-group row, universal handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a source monoid query through $M,X,D,Q,A,U,H$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\GrothendieckGroupUp$, $\MonoidUp$, $\AbGroupUp$, $\SemiringUp$, $\FreeMonoidUp$, $\BHist$, $\msame$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GrothendieckGroupUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts a group-completion read only through $M,X,D,Q,A,U,H,K,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a quotient-group primitive, hidden cancellation law, host abelian group equality, external localization object, or global exactness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Grothendieck group obligation surface]
\label{thm:grothendieck-group-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\GrothendieckGroupUp$ carrier
$G=(M,X,D,Q,A,U,H,K,P,N)$, the local NameCert surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source monoid, generator, difference-pair, congruence, additive-group, universal handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item generator compatibility, requiring $X$ to send each displayed monoid element $a$ to the displayed pair $(a,0)$;
\item congruence stability, requiring the row $Q$ to be preserved by pairwise addition and by swapping coordinates for inverses;
\item additive-group exposure, requiring $A$ to read zero, addition, and inverse only after $D$ and $Q$ have been displayed;
\item universal handoff discipline, requiring each accepted handoff into an $\AbGroupUp$ row to factor through $U$ and the displayed formula $[(a,b)]\mapsto f(a)-f(b)$;
\item structural preservation through $H,K,P,N$;
\item non-escape, excluding primitive quotient groups, hidden cancellation, host equality, external localization data, and unrecorded exactness assumptions.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:grothendieck-group-carrier}. The rows $M,X,D,Q,A,U,H,K,P,N$ are the only displayed data available to an accepted packet.

The generator row $X$ records the map into formal pairs, while $D$ supplies the pair carrier. The congruence row $Q$ supplies exactly the finite witness relation needed to identify two differences without assuming cancellation in $M$.

The additive-group row $A$ is then read from $D$ and $Q$: zero is the class of $(0,0)$, addition is coordinatewise addition, and inverse swaps the two coordinates. Congruence stability is the obligation that makes these operations insensitive to the displayed equivalence row.

For a target $\AbGroupUp$ packet, the universal handoff row $U$ records the factorization formula $[(a,b)]\mapsto f(a)-f(b)$. The structural rows $H,K,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only this displayed route.

Since none of the excluded objects is a carrier coordinate, every accepted group-completion obligation is one of the listed rows and no quotient-group primitive is exported.
\end{proof}
