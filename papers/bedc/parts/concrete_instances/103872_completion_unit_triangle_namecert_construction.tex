\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompletionUnitTriangleUp$}

Following Bishop--Bridges completion and Mac Lane adjunction triangle identities, the $\CompletionUnitTriangleUp$ packet records the finite L10 surface at which a completion-facing read returns through the same unit, counit, and adjunction rows that introduced it. It sits after the unit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionunit-namecert}, the adjunction packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-adjunction-namecert}, and the regular universal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-completion-universal-namecert}. The packet names the triangle identity as a route through displayed finite rows; it does not form quotient completions, compare host equality of completed objects, choose representatives, assume countable choice, or assert ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Completion unit triangle carrier]\label{def:completion-unit-triangle-carrier}
A $\CompletionUnitTriangleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(U,K,A,E,R,S,D,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ row sending the source regular name into the completion-facing boundary.
\item $K$ is the $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ row returning an already displayed completion read to the terminal real-facing route.
\item $A$ is the $\CauchyCompletionAdjunctionUp$ row binding $U$ and $K$ as the unit and counit faces of one adjunction packet.
\item $E$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ extension row through which completion consumers read the same unit-facing datum.
\item $R$ is the $\RegularCauchyCompletionUniversalUp$ regular-Cauchy universal row attached to the same source name and extension ledger.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row supplying the visible stream segment of the source name.
\item $D$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational readback row for the same finite window.
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger that fixes the displayed precision used by $S$ and $D$.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the unit, counit, adjunction, universal-property, regular-Cauchy, stream, readback, dyadic, terminal Real, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay for the triangle route: unit first, universal extension second, regular-Cauchy readback third, counit return fourth, and terminal Real seal last.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompletionUnitTriangleUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionAdjunctionUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\RegularCauchyCompletionUniversalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionUnitTriangleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes a triangle-identity route only as a finite replay path through the named rows. It carries no quotient completion object, no host equality of completed carriers, no selected representative, no countable-choice schedule, and no ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Completion unit triangle NameCert obligations]\label{thm:completion-unit-triangle-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CompletionUnitTriangleUp$ carrier $T=(U,K,A,E,R,S,D,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionUnitTriangleUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the unit, counit, adjunction, universal-property, regular-Cauchy, stream, regular-rational readback, dyadic, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item unit and counit visibility, requiring every triangle read to cite $U$ before any completion-facing extension row and to cite $K$ before any return to the terminal real seal;
\item triangle-route identity, requiring the adjunction row $A$ to bind the unit and counit faces to the same displayed completion read;
\item classifier stability, requiring transported triangle evidence to replay through $H$ and $C$ before a completion-facing consumer reads it;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring the package and local naming rows to export no quotient completion, host equality, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:completion-unit-triangle-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the unit, counit, adjunction, universal-property, regular-Cauchy, stream, readback, dyadic, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Unit and counit visibility are the first two completion faces of the tuple. A completion-facing read enters through $U$, and a read that returns to the Real boundary exits through $K$.

The triangle-route identity is recorded by $A$ together with the replay row $C$. The adjunction row binds the unit and counit faces to one displayed read, while $C$ forces the same route through $E$, $R$, $S$, $D$, $Q$, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal before any consumer sees it.

Classifier stability is componentwise transport through $H$ followed by the same replay through $C$. Ledger non-escape is the inventory statement for $P$ and $N$: the carrier contains no quotient completion, host equality, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, or ambient completeness row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Completion unit triangle identity route]\label{thm:completion-unit-triangle-identity-route}
Every accepted $\CompletionUnitTriangleUp$ triangle read factors through the same unit, counit, adjunction, universal-extension, regular-Cauchy, stream, dyadic, and Real seal rows before any completion-facing consumer reads it. Concretely, the exported route is exhausted by
$$
\CauchyCompletionUnitUp,\ \CauchyCompletionCounitUp,\ \CauchyCompletionAdjunctionUp,\ \CompletionUniversalPropertyUp,\ \RegularCauchyCompletionUniversalUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
No accepted read may replace one of these rows by quotient-completion equality, host equality, a selected representative, countable choice, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier of \autoref{def:completion-unit-triangle-carrier}. The only analytic rows available to a triangle consumer are $U,K,A,E,R,S,D,Q$ and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal named by the transport, replay, and provenance rows.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:completion-unit-triangle-namecert-obligations}. Unit and counit visibility force the read to enter through $U$ and return through $K$, while triangle-route identity forces both faces through the same adjunction row $A$.

The universal-extension and regular-Cauchy rows $E$ and $R$ then keep the completion-facing read attached to the same regular name. The stream, readback, and dyadic rows $S,D,Q$ display the finite windows and tolerances consumed by the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.

The package and local naming rows contain no further completion object or host equality coordinate. Therefore every accepted triangle read is exhausted by the displayed route and cannot export quotient completions, selected representatives, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}
