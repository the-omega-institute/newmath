\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CantorIntersectionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cantor-intersection-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, Constructive Analysis, the $\CantorIntersectionUp$ packet records the finite nested-interval route by which a displayed shrinking interval chain is consumed as a real-completion surface. It is a NameCert reconstruction of the Cantor nested-interval reading over the finite diagonal interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nestedintervalintersection-namecert}, with source rows also anchored at \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The chapter names a completion-facing consumer of the existing nested-interval readback surface. It does not add point choice, quotient equality of reals, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness; every real-facing read is required to pass through the displayed nested-interval intersection packet.

\begin{definition}[Cantor intersection carrier]
\label{def:cantor-intersection-carrier}
A $\CantorIntersectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
K=(N,W,D,E,R,H,C,P,Q)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $N$ is the accepted $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$ packet whose source chain is a displayed $\NestedIntervalUp$ or $\NestedDyadicIntervalUp$ row;
\item $W$ is the finite width-window ledger read from the selected $\DyadicIntervalUp$ endpoint packets and their $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ lower and upper endpoint rows;
\item $D$ is the diagonal selector row choosing, for each displayed precision request, one finite nested-interval window already present in $N$;
\item $E$ records the endpoint enclosure rows selected by $D$ and the exact finite dependency path back to $W$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained by reading the selected endpoint enclosures as a regular rational completion name;
\item $H$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff $R$ and the nested-interval intersection packet $N$;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transports for $N,W,D,E,R,H$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\NestedDyadicIntervalUp$, $\DyadicIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $Q$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CantorIntersectionUp}$ row for the finite completion-consumer name.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is $\mathsf{CantorIntersectionPacket}(K)$. Its public rows are exactly the nested-interval intersection packet, width-window ledger, diagonal selector, endpoint enclosure rows, regular-rational handoff, real seal, transport rows, package provenance, and local naming row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cantor intersection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cantor-intersection-namecert-obligations}
The seed $\NameCert_{\CantorIntersectionUp}$ surface has five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: admission of the finite packet of \autoref{def:cantor-intersection-carrier};
\item finite-window classifier: two accepted carriers classify together only by comparing the displayed $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$ packet, width-window ledger, selected endpoint enclosures, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, $\RealUp$ seal row, transports, provenance, and local certificate row;
\item selector stability: transporting the nested-interval intersection packet and width-window ledger through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes transports the selected finite window to the corresponding selected finite window;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff exactness: every regular-rational read in $R$ is obtained from the selected endpoint enclosure rows $E$ and no unrecorded tail of the interval chain;
\item $\RealUp$ seal nonescape: every real-facing read through $H$ factors through $N,W,D,E,R,C,P,Q$ and receives no host real equality, point selector, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness datum.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations are the finite Cantor-intersection consumer surface over the already named nested-interval intersection route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from $K$. The tuple lists the nested-interval intersection source, width-window ledger, selector, selected endpoint enclosures, regular-rational handoff, real seal, transports, provenance, and local naming row as one finite history packet.

The finite-window classifier compares only those displayed rows after the recorded transports have moved both packets to the same precision request. Since the comparison is made at the visible window, it has no coordinate for quotient equality of completed reals or equality of host interval chains.

Selector stability is inherited from the selector row $D$ and the transported width ledger $W$. A transported request still names a finite interval row of the transported $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$ packet, so the selected endpoint enclosure remains a visible row of the same packet.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff exactness is the statement that $R$ is read from $E$, not from an invisible tail of the chain. The $\RealUp$ seal nonescape then follows by routing $H$ through $R$ together with the displayed transport, package, and local certificate rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cantor intersection real seal handoff]
\label{thm:cantor-intersection-real-seal-handoff}
For every accepted $\CantorIntersectionUp$ carrier
$$
K=(N,W,D,E,R,H,C,P,Q),
$$
every $\RealUp$-facing read factors through the displayed $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$ packet $N$, the selected finite nested-window ledger $W,D,E$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row $R$, the displayed transports $C$, the package provenance $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\CantorIntersectionUp}$ row $Q$ before the seal row $H$ is consumed.

The handoff does not claim open-cover compactness, point choice, host real equality, quotient equality of real names, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CantorIntersectionUp.CantorIntersection\_real\_seal\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Start with the real-facing row $H$ in \autoref{def:cantor-intersection-carrier}. Its source is the regular-rational handoff $R$ together with the displayed transports, provenance, and local naming row.

Apply \autoref{thm:cantor-intersection-namecert-obligations}. The obligation surface forces $R$ to be read from the selected endpoint enclosures $E$, and those enclosures are selected by $D$ from the finite width-window ledger $W$ inside the nested-interval intersection packet $N$.

Thus every real-facing consumer reaches the seal only after passing through the displayed finite nested-interval intersection route. No remaining coordinate in $K$ carries an open-cover compactness proof, a chosen intersection point, a host real equality row, quotient equality of real names, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cantor intersection diagonal cofinal readback]
\label{thm:cantor-intersection-diagonal-cofinal-readback}
For every accepted $\CantorIntersectionUp$ carrier
$$
K=(N,W,D,E,R,H,C,P,Q),
$$
and every displayed precision request named by the finite width-window ledger $W$, the diagonal selector row $D$ selects a visible interval row already present in the nested-interval intersection packet $N$. The selected row determines the endpoint enclosure row in $E$, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row $R$ is read from that same enclosure.

Consequently the $\RealUp$ seal row $H$ is reachable only through the finite cofinal route
$$
W,D \longrightarrow N \longrightarrow E \longrightarrow R \longrightarrow H
$$
together with the displayed transports $C$, package provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert_{\CantorIntersectionUp}$ row $Q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $K$ along the rows listed in \autoref{def:cantor-intersection-carrier}. A displayed precision request is not an external real-completion demand; in the packet it is a row of the finite ledger $W$.

Apply the selector-stability obligation of \autoref{thm:cantor-intersection-namecert-obligations}. After any displayed $\hsame$ or $\Cont$ transport in $C$, the row $D$ still points to a finite nested-window row of $N$ for that request. Thus the selector has no access to a hidden tail of the chain and no coordinate for a chosen intersection point.

The endpoint enclosure row $E$ is then the visible enclosure attached to the selected row, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff exactness clause of \autoref{thm:cantor-intersection-namecert-obligations} forces $R$ to be obtained from $E$. There is no side input from quotient equality of real names, host real equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:cantor-intersection-real-seal-handoff}. The only route to the real-facing seal is the already displayed chain through $N,W,D,E,R,C,P,Q$, so every precision request consumed by $H$ has already been answered by a finite diagonal row of $N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cantor intersection public completion consumer]
\label{thm:cantor-intersection-public-completion-consumer}
For every accepted $\CantorIntersectionUp$ carrier
$$
K=(N,W,D,E,R,H,C,P,Q),
$$
the public completion consumer surface of $K$ is exactly the finite route through $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$ or $\NestedDyadicIntervalUp$, $\DyadicIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and the local $\NameCert_{\CantorIntersectionUp}$ row.

Equivalently, every public consumer row factors through the displayed nested-interval intersection packet $N$, the width-window ledger $W$, the diagonal selector $D$, the selected endpoint enclosures $E$, the regular-rational handoff $R$, the real seal row $H$, the transport rows $C$, the package provenance $P$, and the local naming row $Q$. The surface exports no point selector, quotient real equality, open-cover compactness, uniqueness of an intersection point, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:cantor-intersection-namecert-obligations}. The carrier, classifier, selector-stability, handoff-exactness, and seal-nonescape obligations list exactly the finite rows visible to a $\CantorIntersectionUp$ consumer.

The real-facing boundary is fixed by \autoref{thm:cantor-intersection-real-seal-handoff}. Any read reaching $\RealUp$ must first pass through the nested-interval packet, the selected finite window, the regular-rational handoff, the displayed transports, the provenance row, and the local certificate row.

The finite cofinality of those reads is fixed by \autoref{thm:cantor-intersection-diagonal-cofinal-readback}. Each displayed precision request is answered by a visible diagonal row of $N$, so the consumer has no access to an unrecorded tail or to an external choice of intersection point.

Combining these three surfaces leaves precisely the listed public route and no further public coordinate. In particular, quotient equality of real names, host real equality, open-cover compactness, uniqueness of an intersection point, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness are not part of the exported consumer surface.
\end{proof}
