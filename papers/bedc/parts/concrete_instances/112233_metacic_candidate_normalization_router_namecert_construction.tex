\label{thm:metacic-candidate-normalization-router-obligations}

$$

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the closed typed term, candidate evidence, closed-candidate wrapper, candidate-set membership, adequacy handoff, readback, obstruction, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item candidate visibility, requiring every strong-normalization or no-infinite-reduction read to inspect $A$ and the relevant wrapper row $W$ or membership row $S$;
\item adequacy handoff exactness, requiring application and Pi consumers to pass through $M$ before using candidate evidence to route a closed term;

\item transport and replay closure, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve the same candidate-mediated route.
\end{enumerate}
No router obligation is stored outside $T,A,W,S,M,U,O,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}

Application and Pi reads are handled by $M$. That row records the adequacy handoff before the router treats candidate evidence as applicable to the closed term under inspection.

The obstruction row $O$ records the negative boundary. Since no carrier coordinate supplies evidence-free strong normalization, unrestricted subject reduction, global confluence, quotient syntax equality, host evaluator soundness, or completed closed consistency, $O$ rejects exactly the bypasses named in the statement.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
