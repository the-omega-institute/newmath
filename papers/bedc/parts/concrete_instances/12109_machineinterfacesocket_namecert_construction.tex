\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MachineInterfaceSocketUp$}

The $\MachineInterfaceSocketUp$ packet realizes the machine-facing formalization target interface of \autoref{subsec:philosophy-machine-facing-interface} as a finite socket-routed NameCert carrier. It sits beside the kind classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-socketkindclassifier-namecert}, the use ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-socket-use-ledger-namecert}, the audit gate boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditgateboundary-namecert}, the apophatic socket of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticsocket-namecert}, and the supply socket ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supplysocketledger-namecert}. Those siblings classify, record, police, refuse, or account for socket traffic; this packet routes formalization targets through the same finite audit-facing rows.

\begin{definition}[MachineInterfaceSocket carrier]
\label{def:machine-interface-socket-carrier}
A $\MachineInterfaceSocketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(T,K,U,A,S,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the formalization-target row recording the target name, intended namespace, source registry, statement skeleton, dependency list, expected status, and not-claimed surface from \autoref{def:philosophy-formalization-target-interface};
\item $K$ is the $\SocketKindClassifierUp$ row naming the socket kind that governs the target route;
\item $U$ is the $\SocketUseLedgerUp$ row recording the visible use of that socket by the machine-facing target;
\item $A$ is the $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$ row whose local verdict must accept the displayed socket kind before the target can be authorized;
\item $S$ is the $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$ row tying the requested machine support to the finite supply ledger;
\item $R$ is the refusal-locality row recording that refused audit-gate kinds do not authorize target admission;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $T,K,U,A,S,R$;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes from the target row through the classifier, use ledger, audit gate, supply ledger, and refusal-locality row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SocketKindClassifierUp$, $\SocketUseLedgerUp$, $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$, $\ApophaticSocketUp$, $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MachineInterfaceSocketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for anonymous target admission, global namespace correctness, host-side proof search success, bypassed audit acceptance, supplied witness content, or a theorem asserting that every declared target is true.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MachineInterfaceSocket NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:machine-interface-socket-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:machine-interface-socket-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\MachineInterfaceSocketUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
M=(T,K,U,A,S,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
formalization-target exposure through $T$, socket-kind exactness through $K$, visible socket-use accounting through $U$, audit-gate boundary readback through $A$, supply-ledger accounting through $S$, refusal-locality through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

Consequently every accepted machine-facing target route is a finite NameCert packet whose public reads are target row, socket-kind row, use row, audit-gate row, supply-ledger row, refusal-locality row, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The packet lists $T,K,U,A,S,R,H,C,P,N$, so the local certificate cannot introduce another formalization target, socket kind, socket use, audit verdict, supply ledger, refusal row, transport, route, provenance, or local name.

Read the dependency rows in order. The target row $T$ gives the machine-facing request, the classifier row $K$ fixes the socket kind, the use ledger $U$ records the visible invocation, the audit boundary $A$ supplies the local verdict surface, and the supply ledger $S$ accounts for the requested machine support.

Finally inspect $R,H,C,P,N$. The refusal-locality row blocks refused gate kinds, while the support rows only transport, replay, package, and name the same finite packet. Thus the NameCert obligations are exhausted by the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MachineInterfaceSocket gate locality]
\label{thm:machine-interface-socket-gate-locality}
Every accepted $\MachineInterfaceSocketUp$ packet authorizes a formalization target only through the audit-gate row displayed in $A$. If the audit-gate row refuses the socket kind named by $K$, then the refusal-locality row $R$ prevents the packet from authorizing the target named by $T$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the target and kind rows. The row $T$ records the intended namespace, dependencies, expected status, and not-claimed surface, while $K$ records the socket kind through which that target asks to be routed.

Use the use ledger and audit boundary. The row $U$ makes the socket use visible, and $A$ is the only displayed audit-gate boundary available to judge that use. No carrier coordinate stores a detached authorization predicate.

Apply refusal-locality. When $A$ refuses the kind named by $K$, the row $R$ records that refusal as a local obstruction to target admission. Since $H,C,P,N$ only preserve the same rows, the target route cannot be authorized by another hidden gate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MachineInterfaceSocket sibling separation]
\label{thm:machine-interface-socket-sibling-separation}
The $\MachineInterfaceSocketUp$ carrier is not bijective, under NameCert-row preservation, to $\SocketKindClassifierUp$, $\SocketUseLedgerUp$, $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$, $\ApophaticSocketUp$, or $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$. The machine-interface socket carries the formalization-target row $T$ together with the gate-locality row $R$, while those siblings respectively classify socket kinds, record uses, expose audit verdicts, refuse external supply, or account for supply sockets without preserving both rows at once.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the classifier and use ledger first. $\SocketKindClassifierUp$ supplies kind exactness, and $\SocketUseLedgerUp$ records visible use, but neither carries the formalization-target row with namespace, dependency, expected-status, and not-claimed fields.

Compare the audit and apophatic siblings next. $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$ exposes audit verdict surfaces, and $\ApophaticSocketUp$ records non-internalizing supply requests, but neither combines the target row with a socket-use ledger and the local refusal row for target authorization.

Finally compare $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$. It accounts for requested supply sockets but does not bind a machine-facing target row to the classifier, use ledger, audit gate, and refusal-locality rows in one packet. Therefore no bijection preserves the target row plus gate-locality row together with all displayed NameCert fields.
\end{proof}
