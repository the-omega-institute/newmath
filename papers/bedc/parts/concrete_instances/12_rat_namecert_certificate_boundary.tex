\begin{theorem}[Rat ledger shared-raw contextual zero-extension endpoint exclusion]
\label{thm:rat-history-ledger-shared-raw-context-zero-extension-endpoint-exclusion}
Let $\rho,v,v',p,p_v,p_{v'},t,t_v,t_{v'}:\mathsf{BHist}$. If
$\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}(\rho,v)$ and
$\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}(\rho,v')$ hold, the unary prefixes
$p,p_v,p_{v'}$ satisfy
$$
\hsame(p,p_v)\land \hsame(p,p_{v'}),
$$
and the unary tails $t,t_v,t_{v'}$ satisfy
$$
\hsame(t,t_v)\land \hsame(t,t_{v'}),
$$
then for all $z,z':\mathsf{BHist}$ both contextual visible denominator
endpoints are excluded from zero-extension heads:
$$
(\hsame(\mathsf{append}(p_v,\mathsf{append}(v,t_v)),\Ezero(z))\to\mathsf{False})
\land
(\hsame(\mathsf{append}(p_{v'},\mathsf{append}(v',t_{v'})),\Ezero(z'))\to\mathsf{False}).
$$

The ten-field fit is: traditional target RatUp ledger shared-denominator
zero-branch exclusion; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ plus
$\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}$; carrier $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$;
classifier $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$; stability from shared raw
denominator and context transport; ledger shared raw denominator and context
data; core theorem this block; exactness the two contradictions; downstream
consumers $\mathsf{RealUp}$ denominator-context readback and
$\mathsf{FieldUp}$ denominator continuation; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First invoke
\autoref{thm:rat-history-ledger-shared-raw-context-visible-classifier} with
the two ledger witnesses and the displayed prefix and tail $\hsame$ links. This
gives the classifier edge between
$\mathsf{append}(p_v,\mathsf{append}(v,t_v))$ and
$\mathsf{append}(p_{v'},\mathsf{append}(v',t_{v'}))$.

For a proposed zero-extension on the first contextual visible endpoint,
transport the first classifier endpoint along its displayed $\hsame$ link to
$\Ezero(z)$. The transported classifier has a zero-extension left endpoint, so
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-zero-extension-endpoint-exclusion} gives
the contradiction.

For a proposed zero-extension on the second contextual visible endpoint,
transport the second classifier endpoint along its displayed $\hsame$ link to
$\Ezero(z')$. The transported classifier has a zero-extension right endpoint,
and the same endpoint-exclusion theorem gives the second contradiction.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[\(\RatUp\) is licensed, not primitive]
\label{cor:rat-licensed-not-primitive}
\(\RatUp\) is licensed by normalized representatives, not by a quotient of pairs.
\end{corollary}

\paragraph{Primitive boundary.}
\(\RatUp\) is not a BEDC primitive. It is admitted only when those obligations are supplied.
