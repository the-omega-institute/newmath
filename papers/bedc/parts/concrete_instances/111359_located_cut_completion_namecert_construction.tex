\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedCutCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-located-cut-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\LocatedCutCompletionUp$ packet names the finite lower--upper dyadic bracket surface by which Real and Completion consumers compare located-cut evidence with regular Cauchy readback. It sits beside the dyadic endpoint ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the Cauchy--Dedekind bridge of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-dedekind-bridge-namecert}. The packet is a BEDC-native carrier surface for located lower and upper brackets; it does not construct $\RealUp$ from primitive Dedekind cuts and does not add an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Located-cut completion bracket carrier]\label{def:located-cut-completion-bracket-carrier}
A $\LocatedCutCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(B^{-},B^{+},G,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B^{-}$ is the lower dyadic bracket ledger. It records inhabited lower cells, lower endpoint witnesses, and lower refinement requests through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$.
\item $B^{+}$ is the upper dyadic bracket ledger. It records inhabited upper cells, upper endpoint witnesses, and upper refinement requests through the same dyadic scale row.
\item $G$ is the located comparison row. For every displayed lower endpoint and upper endpoint request, it records a finite separation, overlap, or refinement witness between $B^{-}$ and $B^{+}$.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window schedule used to expose only the finitely requested bracket observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that compares the displayed brackets with a regular rational Cauchy route.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ refinement ledger shared by $B^{-},B^{+},G,W,R$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the lower bracket, upper bracket, located comparison, finite window, regular readback, and dyadic refinement rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the bracket rows, comparison row, window row, readback row, dyadic row, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the bracket-completion route
$$
B^{-},B^{+},G,D \longmapsto W,R \longmapsto E,H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedCutCompletionUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCutCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts only packets that display both bracket ledgers, the shared located comparison row, the common dyadic refinement ledger, the finite window schedule, the regular readback row, the terminal real seal, and the structural rows. It has no coordinate for primitive Dedekind cuts, quotient equality, selected limits, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located-cut bracket inhabitation]\label{thm:located-cut-completion-bracket-inhabitation}
For every accepted $\LocatedCutCompletionUp$ carrier $L=(B^{-},B^{+},G,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCutCompletionUp}$ surface includes inhabited lower and upper dyadic bracket reads. Every public bracket read must pass through the displayed rows $B^{-}$ and $B^{+}$ and the shared dyadic ledger $D$ before it can reach $W,R,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:located-cut-completion-bracket-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes $B^{-}$ and $B^{+}$ as separate lower and upper bracket ledgers, each tied to the same dyadic refinement row $D$.

The classifier has no bracket coordinate outside these ledgers. Therefore any public bracket read is a read of the displayed lower ledger or upper ledger together with the shared dyadic scale data.

The remaining rows $W,R,E,H,C,P,N$ only schedule, read back, seal, transport, replay, source, and name that same bracket route. They cannot supply a detached lower or upper cut object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located-cut comparison obligation]\label{thm:located-cut-completion-located-comparison}
For every accepted $\LocatedCutCompletionUp$ carrier, the row $G$ is the only located comparison interface between the lower bracket ledger $B^{-}$ and the upper bracket ledger $B^{+}$. A Real or Completion consumer cannot compare the two bracket sides without first reading $B^{-},B^{+},G,D$ inside the same finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier rows named in \autoref{def:located-cut-completion-bracket-carrier}. The lower and upper bracket ledgers are displayed separately, and the located comparison row $G$ is the sole row that relates their endpoint requests.

A consumer that omits $G$ has only two unrelated dyadic ledgers. A consumer that omits either bracket side has no two-sided located question to compare.

Thus the comparison route is forced through $B^{-},B^{+},G,D$. The stream, regular readback, real seal, and structural rows preserve the comparison after it is displayed, but they do not create a second comparison principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Common dyadic refinement stability]\label{thm:located-cut-completion-common-refinement-stability}
Let $L=(B^{-},B^{+},G,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\LocatedCutCompletionUp$ carrier. Any refinement of a displayed lower bracket or upper bracket that remains inside the common dyadic ledger $D$ preserves the same $\LocatedCutCompletionUp$ packet route through $G,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier stores one dyadic refinement ledger $D$ for both bracket sides. Refining a lower or upper endpoint inside $D$ therefore keeps the refined observation in the same finite packet rather than moving it to an external cut object.

The located comparison row $G$ is read after the two bracket ledgers and the shared dyadic scale are fixed. A refinement that remains in $D$ is still a request to the same comparison row.

Finally $W,R,E,H,C,P,N$ are attached to this packet route. They can replay and name the refined bracket read, but they cannot replace the common dyadic ledger by an independent refinement principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Classifier transport under bracket equivalence]\label{thm:located-cut-completion-classifier-transport}
For every accepted $\LocatedCutCompletionUp$ carrier, componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the lower bracket, upper bracket, located comparison, dyadic refinement, stream window, regular readback, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows preserves classifier acceptance. Equivalence of bracket displays is therefore transported only through $H$ and the displayed packet rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The transport row $H$ is a coordinate of the accepted carrier. Its scope is componentwise: it transports $B^{-},B^{+},G,W,R,D,E,C,P,N$ as rows of the same packet.

The classifier of \autoref{def:located-cut-completion-bracket-carrier} accepts exactly those displayed rows. Applying $H$ preserves the rows it names and leaves no unmentioned quotient equality coordinate to inspect.

Hence bracket equivalence is a packet-level transport statement. It is not equality of primitive cuts and not quotient equality of completed real names.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real seal handoff for located-cut completion]\label{thm:located-cut-completion-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read exported by an accepted $\LocatedCutCompletionUp$ carrier factors through
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
The terminal seal $E$ is reached only after the lower bracket, upper bracket, located comparison, common dyadic refinement, finite window schedule, and regular rational readback rows have been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the bracket rows $B^{-},B^{+}$ and the located comparison row $G$. The shared dyadic ledger $D$ fixes the common refinement scale on which both bracket sides are read.

The finite observation schedule $W$ and regular rational readback row $R$ then expose the bracket route to the Cauchy-facing side of the packet. At this point the consumer has only displayed finite rows, not a selected limit or quotient representative.

The terminal real seal $E$ is therefore a handoff after $B^{-},B^{+},G,D,W,R$. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same handoff, so the excluded primitives and ambient completeness principle have no carrier coordinate.
\end{proof}
