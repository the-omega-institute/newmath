\begin{theorem}[LocatedSupremum upstream upper-bound face]
\label{thm:located-upper-bound-located-supremum-forward-dependency}
The downstream $\LocatedSupremumUp$ packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedsupremum-namecert} consumes an accepted $\LocatedUpperBoundUp$ packet only as its upstream upper-bound face. Concretely, the route supplied by \autoref{thm:located-upper-bound-supremum-route} offers the finite rows $F,U,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$ as the displayed upper-bound witness; lower-approximation cofinality, least-upper-bound exactness, and supremum-facing closure remain rows of $\LocatedSupremumUp$ rather than rows of $\LocatedUpperBoundUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read \autoref{thm:located-upper-bound-supremum-route}. It identifies the accepted upper-bound carrier as a reusable witness for the source family, the upper-bound ledger, finite windows, regular readback, the Real seal, and the structural transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Now compare the downstream consumer in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedsupremum-namecert}. Its supremum certificate requires lower-approximation and cofinality data in addition to an upstream upper-bound face. Those additional rows are not coordinates of \autoref{def:located-upper-bound-carrier}.

Thus every handoff from $\LocatedUpperBoundUp$ into $\LocatedSupremumUp$ is exactly the forward dependency through $F,U,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. The supremum packet must supply the remaining supremum-specific rows itself.
\end{proof}
