\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy tail-ball carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-tail-ball-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule opened at the tail threshold.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger comparing the selected tail radius with the requested precision.
\item $Q$ is the tail-threshold row naming the finite index after which the regular Cauchy source is read inside the ball.
\item $W$ is the tail-ball witness row, recording the displayed center, radius, and finite window selected by $Q$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $R,S,D,Q,W$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the analytic rows and the local structural rows.

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy tail-ball NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-ball-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyTailBallUp$ carrier $T=(R,S,D,Q,W,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailBallUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the regular rational source, finite stream window, dyadic tolerance ledger, tail threshold, tail-ball witness, Real seal, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-tail-ball-carrier}. The visible analytic rows are $R,S,D,Q,W,E$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

The tail-ball obligation is the ordered read through $Q$ and $W$. The threshold row fixes the finite tail, and the witness row records the center, radius, and finite window within that tail.

The dyadic and Real-facing rows are downstream. The ledger $D$ supplies the displayed tolerance comparison, and the seal $E$ is consumed only after the regular rational source and stream window have been exposed.

Transport, replay, provenance, and naming preserve the same finite route. Since the carrier lacks quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, and ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local surface.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-ball-real-completion-route}

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The threshold and ball data are $Q$ and $W$. They select a finite tail and a displayed ball witness before any Real-facing seal can be read.
