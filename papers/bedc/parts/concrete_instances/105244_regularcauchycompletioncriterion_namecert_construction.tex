Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\RegularCauchyCompletionCriterionUp$ packet records the finite criterion by which a regular rational name is admitted to the completion-facing Real seal. It sits between the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the modulus schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-convergence-modulus-namecert}, the sequence-limit handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-limit-namecert}, the completion-admission boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletioncriterion-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a displayed NameCert criterion for L10 completion consumers, not a quotient completion, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, host sequence theorem, trichotomy split, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness assertion.

\begin{definition}[RegularCauchyCompletionCriterion carrier]
\label{def:regularcauchycompletioncriterion-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyCompletionCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(R,W,D,M,L,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row naming the regular rational readback inspected by the completion criterion.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule through which the source name is observed.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the requested dyadic radius, slack cells, and comparison budget used by the regular readback.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$ schedule row naming the displayed tail threshold.
\item $L$ is the $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ handoff row reached only after $R,W,D,M$ have been displayed.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyCompletionCriterionUp$ boundary row that admits the regular Cauchy route to the completion-facing $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the regular readback, finite windows, dyadic ledger, modulus schedule, sequence-limit handoff, completion boundary, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCompletionCriterionUp}$ row naming the displayed criterion and its permitted completion-facing reads.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $R,W,D,M,L,Q,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a selected completion representative, countable choice over tail indices, host convergence, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyCompletionCriterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regularcauchycompletioncriterion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyCompletionCriterionUp$ carrier $K=(R,W,D,M,L,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCompletionCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$ schedule, $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ handoff, $\CauchyCompletionCriterionUp$ boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item finite-window exactness, requiring every regular Cauchy observation to read $W$ before $R$ or $M$ is available;
\item dyadic tolerance exactness, requiring the comparison budget $D$ to be read before the modulus schedule $M$, sequence-limit handoff $L$, or completion boundary $Q$ is exposed;
\item completion-boundary handoff, requiring $Q$ to remain downstream of $R,W,D,M,L$ and upstream only of the structural rows $H,C,P,N$;
\item ledger refusal, excluding quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice over thresholds, host sequence convergence, trichotomy, host real equality, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is tuple projection from \autoref{def:regularcauchycompletioncriterion-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the regular readback $R$, finite windows $W$, dyadic ledger $D$, modulus schedule $M$, sequence-limit handoff $L$, completion boundary $Q$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The analytic route first reads the finite windows $W$ and the tolerance ledger $D$. The regular rational readback $R$ and the convergence-modulus schedule $M$ are therefore exposed only as displayed finite data.

The handoff rows $L$ and $Q$ are downstream of that finite route. The $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ row receives the displayed sequence-limit comparison, and the $\CauchyCompletionCriterionUp$ row records the completion-admission boundary before any $\RealUp$-facing consumer can read the seal.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since the carrier has no row for quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice over thresholds, host convergence, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyCompletionCriterion Real-seal handoff]
\label{thm:regularcauchycompletioncriterion-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing consumer of an accepted $\RegularCauchyCompletionCriterionUp$ carrier $K=(R,W,D,M,L,Q,H,C,P,N)$ must first read the route
$$
\StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \CauchyConvergenceModulusUp \longrightarrow \RealSequenceLimitUp \longrightarrow \CauchyCompletionCriterionUp \longrightarrow \RealUp .
$$
The handoff exposes only finite windows, dyadic tolerances, regular readback, modulus schedule, sequence-limit comparison, completion-admission boundary, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:regularcauchycompletioncriterion-carrier}. Its $\StreamNameUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows are the only source of inspected windows and tolerance data.

The regular readback and modulus rows consume those displayed data. Thus the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ and the $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$ row $M$ cannot be replaced by a host sequence predicate or by an unbounded tail search.

Next the $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ handoff $L$ and $\CauchyCompletionCriterionUp$ boundary $Q$ are read in order. The completion-facing consumer reaches the $\RealUp$ seal only after this route has been displayed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the same handoff. Hence the exported surface is exactly the finite route in the statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyCompletionCriterion ledger refusal]
\label{thm:regularcauchycompletioncriterion-ledger-refusal}

Any consumer requesting one of those objects must leave the displayed carrier route $R,W,D,M,L,Q,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Therefore any such request cannot be answered by the packet. It is refused at the ledger boundary before a $\RealUp$-facing consumer can treat the displayed completion criterion as a quotient completion theorem.
\end{proof}
