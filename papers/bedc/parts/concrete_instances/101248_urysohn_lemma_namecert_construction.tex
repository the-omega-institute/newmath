Following Urysohn, Kelley, and Bourbaki, the $\UrysohnLemmaUp$ packet records the finite separation-function surface obtained from a displayed normal-space separation request. It sits immediately above the open-neighborhood certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normalspace-namecert}, and it reads the function boundary through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cozero-set-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zero-set-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sierpinski-space-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}. The packet names only a local separation-function certificate for two displayed closed rows; it does not select complements, reason by excluded middle, assert Tietze extension, or identify host functions extensionally.

\begin{definition}[Normal-space Urysohn separation carrier]
\label{def:normal-space-urysohn-separation-carrier}
A $\UrysohnLemmaUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(N,F_0,F_1,D,O_0,O_1,M,Z,C,S,T,H,K,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $N$ is the $\NormalSpaceUp$ source row, exposing the ambient $\TopologyUp$ row, the displayed closed-pair separation request, and the open-neighborhood separation route named in \autoref{def:normal-space-carrier}.
\item $F_0$ and $F_1$ are the two displayed closed-set rows over the same topological source; they are read as finite closed rows, not as selected complements of open sets.
\item $D$ is the disjointness ledger for $F_0$ and $F_1$, recording absence of an accepted common membership cell inside the displayed packet.
\item $O_0$ and $O_1$ are the separating open rows supplied by the $\NormalSpaceUp$ source, with $F_0$ contained in $O_0$, $F_1$ contained in $O_1$, and no accepted overlap between the two open rows.
\item $M$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ row for the displayed map from the source topology into $\RealUp$, exposing only the graph cells and continuity replay used by the separation read.
\item $Z$ is the $\ZeroSetUp$ endpoint row certifying that the displayed map has value zero on the first closed row $F_0$.
\item $C$ is the $\CozeroSetUp$ endpoint row certifying that the displayed map is positively apart from zero on the second closed row $F_1$ after the terminal one-row readback is opened.
\item $S$ is the $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ observation row, recording the open-truth boundary used to compare the cozero and zero endpoint observations without host propositions.
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ handoff row tying the source topology, separating opens, cozero open row, zero closed row, and Sierpinski observation to one displayed topological surface.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the normal-space row, closed-set rows, disjointness ledger, separating open rows, continuous-map row, endpoint rows, Sierpinski and topology observation rows, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a separation-function request through $N,F_0,F_1,D,O_0,O_1,M,Z,C,S,T,H,P,L$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UrysohnLemmaUp$, $\NormalSpaceUp$, $\CozeroSetUp$, $\ZeroSetUp$, $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\RealUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UrysohnLemmaUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $N,F_0,F_1,D,O_0,O_1,M,Z,C,S,T,H,K,P,L$. It has no coordinate for selected complements, excluded middle, host function extensionality, Tietze extension, full normal-space function extension, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Normal-space Urysohn separation route]
\label{thm:normal-space-urysohn-separation-route}
For every accepted $\UrysohnLemmaUp$ carrier
$$
U=(N,F_0,F_1,D,O_0,O_1,M,Z,C,S,T,H,K,P,L),
$$
the local separation-function read factors through the displayed $\NormalSpaceUp$ source, the two closed-set rows, the disjointness ledger, the separating open rows, the continuous $\RealUp$-valued map row, the zero and cozero endpoint rows, the Sierpinski observation row, the topology handoff row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row. No local read of $L$ is available before these rows have been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:normal-space-urysohn-separation-carrier}. The accepted tuple displays exactly the normal-space source, closed-pair rows, disjointness ledger, separating open rows, continuous-map row, endpoint rows, observation rows, topology handoff, and structural transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The first gate is the normal-space route. A separation-function request reads $N,F_0,F_1,D,O_0,O_1$ before it can inspect any Real-valued map row; this fixes both closed witnesses and prevents substitution by selected complements or host closed predicates.

The second gate is the function boundary. The continuous-map row $M$ is read only as displayed $\ContinuousMapUp$ data into $\RealUp$, while $Z$ and $C$ record the zero and cozero endpoint conditions through the sibling zero-set and cozero-set certificates. The Sierpinski row $S$ and topology row $T$ keep the endpoint observations on the same observable-open and topology surface.

Transport and replay are exhausted by $H$ and $K$. They move the listed rows componentwise and replay the same finite route to the local naming row $L$.

Finally, non-escape follows from the absence of any further classifier coordinate. The provenance and local NameCert rows name only this finite route, so no row exports Tietze extension, selected complements, excluded middle, host function extensionality, full normal-space function extension, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{proof}
