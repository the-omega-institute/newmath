\begin{definition}[RegSeqRat constant dyadic source]
\label{def:regseqrat-constant-dyadic-source}
A $\RegSeqRatUp$ constant dyadic source packet for a displayed dyadic endpoint
$d$ is the finite packet obtained by reading one $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint
row for $d$, the constant $\StreamNameUp$ schedule that returns that same row
at every requested unary window, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity ledger that
compares any two displayed positions by the same endpoint row. Its visible
rows are the dyadic endpoint row, the dyadic radius row requested by each
window, the zero dyadic-distance row for equal endpoints, the regularity bound
row, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance, and local $\NameCert$ provenance.

The packet is only a finite BEDC source description. It is not a quotient
stream, host real equality, selected limit, metric completion object, Real
completeness row, or ambient embedding principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat constant dyadic regularity]
\label{thm:regseqrat-constant-dyadic-regularity}
Every finite window of a $\RegSeqRatUp$ constant dyadic source is regular. For
requested precisions $n$ and $m$, both displayed endpoints are the same
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row $d$, the dyadic-distance row is the equal-endpoint row,
and the regularity ledger is the displayed bound
$$
0 \leq 2^{-n}+2^{-m}.
$$
Thus the window supplies exactly the RegSeqRat regularity row over the constant
StreamName schedule, together with its $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
local $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the constant dyadic source of
\autoref{def:regseqrat-constant-dyadic-source}. The two endpoint projections
in any finite window are the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint row, because the
$\StreamNameUp$ schedule is constant.

Read the equal-endpoint dyadic-distance row from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The radius ledger for
the same window supplies the two displayed radii $2^{-n}$ and $2^{-m}$, so the
regularity comparison is bounded by the displayed sum.

Finally apply the RegSeqRat regularity obligation surface of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-regularity-obligation-surface} and the finite-window
transport closure of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-finite-window-transport-obligation}. They repack the
constant StreamName schedule of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and the dyadic endpoint
rows as the required RegSeqRat regularity ledger, without adding quotient or
completion data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat constant RealUp seal handoff]
\label{thm:regseqrat-constant-real-seal-handoff}
A $\RegSeqRatUp$ constant dyadic source reaches the $\RealUp$ seal only through
the four visible faces
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp.
$$
The source first reads the constant StreamName schedule, then the shared
DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius rows, then the RegSeqRat regularity and
classifier ledger of \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-dyadic-regularity}, and
then the RealUp seal row of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The handoff exports no quotient
stream equality, host real equality, completion object, selected limit, ambient
embedding theorem, or RealUp completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the constant schedule and endpoint packet of
\autoref{def:regseqrat-constant-dyadic-source}. Its source rows are already the
StreamName and DyadicRatCore rows named by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}.

Use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-constant-dyadic-regularity} to place every finite
window on the RegSeqRat regularity surface. The cited theorem supplies the
same endpoint, radius, distance, transport, route, package, and naming rows in
each requested window.

Finally apply the existing RegSeqRat seal route:
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness},
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-meet-handoff}, and
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff}. These route the finite
regular-rational packet to the RealUp seal through the four visible faces.
Since none of those faces contains a coordinate for quotient stream equality,
host real equality, completion objects, selected limits, embedding theorems, or
RealUp completeness, the constant packet exports no such row.
\end{proof}
