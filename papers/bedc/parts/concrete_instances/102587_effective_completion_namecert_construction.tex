It sits after the effective Cauchy source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-effective-cauchy-sequence-namecert}, the Cauchy realizer handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-realizer-namecert}, the represented-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-representation-namecert}, the admissible-representation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-admissible-representation-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Effective completion carrier]
\label{def:effective-completion-carrier}
An $\EffectiveCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(E,Q,K,W,R,D,A,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the $\EffectiveCauchySequenceUp$ source row, exposing the effective index schedule, Cauchy evidence, and source-side observations before any completion consumer reads the packet.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyRealizerUp$ handoff row, recording the effective procedure that answers tolerance requests by finite Cauchy data read from $E$.
\item $K$ is the $\CauchyRepresentationUp$ row, binding the represented-space name to the same effective Cauchy source and realizer route.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row opened by the source, realizer, representation, and downstream completion request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row generated from the displayed windows and their tolerance ledger.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, recording dyadic scales, comparison witnesses, slack, and radius data used by $Q,W,R$.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only through the displayed effective source, realizer, representation, windows, readback, and dyadic tolerance rows.
\item $U$ is the completion-consumer row, recording every Real-facing or completion-facing read that the packet permits after $E,Q,K,W,R,D,A$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the effective source, realizer, representation, windows, readback, tolerance ledger, real seal, completion consumer, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
E,Q,K \longmapsto W,D \longmapsto R \longmapsto A \longmapsto U .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EffectiveCompletionUp$, $\EffectiveCauchySequenceUp$, $\CauchyRealizerUp$, $\CauchyRepresentationUp$, $\AdmissibleRepresentationUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $E,Q,K,W,R,D,A,U,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected limits, countable choice, host equality of completed objects, trichotomy of reals, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Effective completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:effective-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EffectiveCompletionUp$ carrier
$$
X=(E,Q,K,W,R,D,A,U,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the effective Cauchy source, Cauchy realizer, Cauchy representation, finite windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, real seal, completion consumer, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item effective-source visibility, requiring every downstream read to expose the source schedule and Cauchy evidence in $E$ before any represented-space or real-facing output is inspected;
\item finite-window exactness, requiring every observation used by the realizer, representation, regular readback, real seal, or completion consumer to occur inside the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ row $W$;
\item Cauchy-realizer handoff, requiring tolerance requests to pass through $Q$ and its displayed dyadic ledger $D$ before $R$ or $A$ can be read;
\item represented-space compatibility, requiring the row $K$ to bind the same effective source, realizer, finite windows, dyadic tolerance data, and real seal as the admissible representation route;
\item completion-consumer non-escape, requiring $U$ to consume only $E,Q,K,W,R,D,A$ and to export no quotient completion, selected limit, countable-choice selector, host equality, trichotomy branch, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, Lean verification credential, or bridge credential;
\item transport, replay, provenance, and local naming, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve, replay, source, and name the displayed route without adding a second classifier.
\end{enumerate}
No effective completion obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:effective-completion-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the effective source, realizer, representation, finite windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, real seal, completion consumer, and structural rows.

The effective-source and finite-window clauses are read from $E$ and $W$. Since every downstream row is listed downstream of those coordinates, no represented-space read, regular readback, real seal, or completion consumer can inspect hidden stream data.

The realizer and represented-space clauses are read from $Q,K,D,R,A$. A tolerance request reaches $Q$, opens only the displayed dyadic data $D$ and windows $W$, forms the readback $R$, and then reaches the terminal real seal $A$ through the represented route $K$.

The consumer and structural clauses are exhausted by $U,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking, so these objects cannot be exported by replay or local naming.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Effective completion Cauchy-realizer handoff]
\label{thm:effective-completion-cauchy-realizer-handoff}
For every accepted $\EffectiveCompletionUp$ carrier
$$
X=(E,Q,K,W,R,D,A,U,H,C,P,N),
$$
every $\RealUp$ or completion consumer of $X$ factors through the displayed route
$$
E \longrightarrow Q \longrightarrow K \longrightarrow W,D \longrightarrow R \longrightarrow A \longrightarrow U
$$
with transport, replay, provenance, and local naming supplied only by $H,C,P,N$. The consumer receives no quotient completion, selected limit, countable choice, host equality of completed objects, trichotomy branch, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a Real-facing or completion-facing consumer of $X$. The only consumer coordinate is $U$, so the read must pass through the displayed completion-consumer row.

Read the dependencies of $U$. It is downstream of the real seal $A$, and $A$ is downstream of the regular readback $R$ generated from finite stream windows $W$ and dyadic tolerance data $D$.

The effective source of those rows is fixed by $E,Q,K$. The source row $E$ supplies the effective Cauchy data, the realizer $Q$ answers tolerance requests from that source, and the representation row $K$ binds the same data to the represented-space interface.

The structural rows add no analytic data. Componentwise transport through $H$ preserves the displayed coordinates, replay through $C$ preserves the route order, and $P,N$ only source and name the same packet.

Since no excluded object appears as a carrier coordinate, the consumer cannot obtain quotient completion equality, a selected limit, countable choice, host equality, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness. Thus every allowed consumer factors through exactly the displayed Cauchy-realizer handoff.
\end{proof}
