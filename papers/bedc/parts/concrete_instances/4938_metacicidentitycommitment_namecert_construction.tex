\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetacicIdentityCommitmentUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metacicidentitycommitment-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\MetacicIdentityCommitmentUp$ packet turns the principle of \autoref{ch:bedc-is-its-own-theory} into a finite naming certificate. It is read together with the dependency-map surface of \autoref{ch:bedc-axiom-dependency-map}, the cross-map routing packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditmapdependencyweave-namecert}, and the forbidden-host-ancestry boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-forbidden-axiom-ancestry-namecert}. Those chapters expose dependency, routing, and forbidden-ancestry rows; this chapter packages the positive identity commitments by which BEDC.MetaCIC is named as its own substrate.

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC identity commitment carrier]
\label{def:metacic-identity-commitment-carrier}
A $\MetacicIdentityCommitmentUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(G,E,R,P,A,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the closed-generator row, listing the finite constructor and formation rows by which the substrate is generated;
\item $E$ is the literal-AST equality row, recording equality by displayed syntax and not by comparison with another calculus;
\item $R$ is the structural-recursor row, naming the recursor and induction routes that act on the closed generators of $G$;
\item $P$ is the strict-purity row, recording that accepted internal reads use the displayed generator, equality, and recursor rows without a hidden host axiom supply;
\item $A$ is the apophatic-boundary row, naming the points where an internal BEDC.MetaCIC route stops without redefining the substrate by absence from classical CIC;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the generator, equality, recursor, purity, apophatic-boundary, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer replays the generator read, equality readback, recursor readback, strict-purity read, and apophatic-boundary read;
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the own-theory principle, axiom-dependency map, audit-map dependency weave, forbidden-axiom ancestry packet, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ source rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetacicIdentityCommitmentUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a derivative-subtheory inclusion, a hidden comparison theorem with classical CIC, a host proof oracle, or an external axiom supply.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC identity commitment NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-identity-commitment-namecert-obligations}
For every $\MetacicIdentityCommitmentUp$ carrier
$$
I=(G,E,R,P,A,H,C,Q,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\MetacicIdentityCommitmentUp}$ surface consists exactly of closed-generator exposure through $G$, literal-AST equality exposure through $E$, structural-recursor exposure through $R$, strict-purity exposure through $P$, apophatic-boundary exposure through $A$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $Q$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-identity-commitment-carrier}. The rows $G,E,R,P,A$ expose the positive generator, equality, recursor, strict-purity, and apophatic-boundary commitments.

The transport and replay rows $H$ and $C$ bind those commitments componentwise. A consumer may transport or replay the packet, but the endpoints remain the displayed rows of the same carrier.

The provenance and naming rows $Q,N$ package the same finite data as the local $\NameCert$ surface. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, no additional inclusion, comparison theorem, host oracle, or external supply can enter the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC identity commitment nonderivative boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-identity-commitment-nonderivative-boundary}
For every accepted $\MetacicIdentityCommitmentUp$ carrier
$$
I=(G,E,R,P,A,H,C,Q,N),
$$
the packet names BEDC.MetaCIC by its own closed generators, literal-AST equality, structural recursors, strict-purity row, and apophatic boundary. It cannot be read as a derivative-subtheory claim, a comparison-first definition, or a theorem that classical CIC supplies the meaning of the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the positive rows $G,E,R,P,A$. They state the substrate identity by generator closure, literal syntax, structural recursion, strict purity, and internal stopping points.

The continuation row $C$ only replays those displayed reads, and the transport row $H$ only carries them componentwise. Neither row contains a map from classical CIC into the carrier or a row that turns comparison language into a definition.

Thus the boundary is nonderivative by inventory. The finite packet exports exactly the positive identity rows and the local certificate rows; it exports no derivative-subtheory inclusion and no comparison-first meaning clause.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC identity commitment audit-map handoff]
\label{thm:metacic-identity-commitment-audit-map-handoff}
Every downstream audit-map consumer of an accepted $\MetacicIdentityCommitmentUp$ carrier must factor its read through the provenance row $Q$ and the continuation row $C$. The consumer may cite the axiom-dependency map, audit-map dependency weave, and forbidden-axiom ancestry packets named in $Q$, but it cannot replace the positive identity rows $G,E,R,P,A$ by a hidden dependency-map verdict or by a forbidden-ancestry refusal row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the provenance row $Q$. It names the own-theory principle together with the axiom-dependency map, audit-map dependency weave, and forbidden-axiom ancestry packets as the cited handoff surfaces.

Next read the continuation row $C$. A downstream audit-map consumer reaches generator, equality, recursor, purity, and apophatic-boundary data only by replaying the displayed routes from the carrier.

The handoff is therefore a routing claim, not a replacement claim. Dependency-map and forbidden-ancestry consumers may inspect the packet through $Q$ and $C$, but the positive identity rows remain the source rows of the local $\NameCert$.
\end{proof}
