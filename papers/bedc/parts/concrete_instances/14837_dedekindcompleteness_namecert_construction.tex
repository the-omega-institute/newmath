\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DedekindCompletenessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dedekindcompleteness-namecert}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\DedekindCompletenessUp$ packet records the finite completeness route by which a displayed inhabited, bounded, located cut family exposes a real-name supremum witness. It sits over the located-cut surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedcut-namecert}, the located-real surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, the comparison discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishoprealcomparison-namecert}, the stream and regular-rational readbacks of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a concrete finite route for later $\RealUp$ consumers; it does not assert an ambient host completeness principle for arbitrary subsets.

\begin{definition}[Dedekind completeness carrier]
\label{def:dedekind-completeness-carrier}
A $\DedekindCompletenessUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
K=(L,R,B,W,A,S,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedCutUp$ family row whose lower and upper faces are inspected only through finite rational windows;
\item $R$ is the $\LocatedRealUp$ readback row attached to the same family observations;
\item $B$ is the $\BishopRealComparisonUp$ row that records boundedness and comparison evidence between the cut, located-real, and real-seal faces;
\item $W$ is the paired $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger used to request finite observations from the family;
\item $A$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ approximation ledger that repacks the windows as a regular rational route;
\item $S$ is the located upper-bound stability row, displaying that transported family observations remain below the same accepted upper-bound witness;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $L,R,B,W,A,S$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the cut family, located-real readback, comparison row, stream windows, regular approximation ledger, stability row, real seal, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes for family reads, upper-bound checks, lower-approximation witnesses, comparison handoff, regular-rational handoff, and real-seal readback;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DedekindCompletenessUp$, $\LocatedCutUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\BishopRealComparisonUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DedekindCompletenessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The accepted carrier contains no quotient-real coordinate, no trichotomy oracle, no host real equality test, no countable-choice schedule, no arbitrary subset classifier, and no completeness principle beyond the displayed finite packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dedekind completeness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dedekind-completeness-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted carrier $K=(L,R,B,W,A,S,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DedekindCompletenessUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure: every consumer can inspect precisely the displayed rows $L,R,B,W,A,S,E,H,C,P,N$;
\item classifier transport: the transports in $H$ and replay routes in $C$ preserve the same cut-family classifier, located-real readback, comparison ledger, stream windows, regular approximation ledger, upper-bound stability row, real seal, provenance, and local naming row;
\item located upper-bound stability: the row $S$ transports accepted upper-bound evidence through the $\BishopRealComparisonUp$ comparison row without changing the family window named by $L$ and $W$;
\item lower-approximation witness: every precision requested in $W$ receives a displayed lower-face witness from the located cut row $L$ and its regular-rational handoff $A$;
\item $\RealUp$ seal factorization: every terminal real-name read factors through $L,R,B,W,A,S$ before it reaches $E$;
\item non-escape: no consumer can obtain quotient reals, trichotomy, host real equality, countable choice, an arbitrary subset classifier, or ambient completeness from the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier row by row. Carrier exposure is the tuple inventory itself, and classifier transport follows because $H$ and $C$ mention only the displayed rows.

For located upper-bound stability, read $S$ together with the comparison row $B$. The transported family observations remain tied to the same cut window $L$ and stream requests $W$, so no separate upper-bound classifier is introduced.

For lower approximation, inspect a requested precision in $W$. The located cut row $L$ supplies the lower face, and the regular-rational row $A$ repacks that witness into the same finite route consumed by the real-seal boundary.

The real-facing exit is then forced. The only terminal real row is $E$, and the carrier reaches it only after the cut, located-real, comparison, stream, approximation, and stability rows have been displayed.

The non-escape clause is structural. Quotient reals, trichotomy, host real equality, countable choice, arbitrary subset classifiers, and ambient completeness are not carrier coordinates and are not named by the package provenance $P$.
\end{proof}
