\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SequentialContinuityUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\SequentialContinuityUp$ packet records continuity along certified convergent sequences as a finite BEDC route. It reads the pointwise metric-continuity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}, the regular rational windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the inspected stream schedules of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the $\MetricSpaceUp$ rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}. The packet supplies a Real and Completion frontier surface for sequence consumers without importing host sequential continuity, function extensionality, choice, quotient equality, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Sequential continuity carrier]
\label{def:sequential-continuity-carrier}
A $\SequentialContinuityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(M_X,M_Y,F,S,L,W_X,W_Y,R_X,R_Y,E_X,E_Y,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M_X$ and $M_Y$ are the source and target $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier rows with their visible classifiers and distance ledgers.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousUp$ pointwise-modulus row for a displayed map from the source metric carrier to the target metric carrier.
\item $S$ is the source sequence surface, exposed only through finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows.
\item $L$ is the inspected convergence ledger for $S$, listing the finite thresholds actually requested by the packet.
\item $W_X$ and $W_Y$ are the source and target finite stream windows read from $S$ and from the displayed images of those windows under $F$.
\item $R_X$ and $R_Y$ are the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows attached to the inspected source and target windows.
\item $E_X$ and $E_Y$ are the $\RealUp$ seal rows reached after the corresponding regular-rational handoffs have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric rows, the continuous-map row, the sequence row, the convergence ledger, both window families, both regular handoffs, both Real seals, continuation, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from $S,L$ to $W_X$, from $W_X,F$ to $W_Y$, from $W_X$ to $R_X,E_X$, and from $W_Y$ to $R_Y,E_Y$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SequentialContinuityUp$, $\ContinuousUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialContinuityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for an external sequence function, quotient stream equality, function extensionality, countable choice, or host sequential-continuity theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Sequential continuity NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:sequential-continuity-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SequentialContinuityUp$ carrier $Q=(M_X,M_Y,F,S,L,W_X,W_Y,R_X,R_Y,E_X,E_Y,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialContinuityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed fifteen-row packet;
\item source and target metric-row stability through $M_X,M_Y,H,C$;
\item pointwise-continuity use only through the displayed $\ContinuousUp$ row $F$;
\item inspected-threshold convergence coverage through the finite ledger $L$;
\item source and target sequence handoff through $W_X,W_Y,R_X,R_Y$ before either $\RealUp$ seal is consumed;
\item non-escape from host sequential continuity, function extensionality, countable choice, quotient equality, and ambient Real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:sequential-continuity-carrier}. The metric rows, continuous-map row, source sequence row, convergence ledger, window rows, regular-rational handoffs, Real seals, and structural rows are the only coordinates visible to the classifier.

The metric and continuity faces are already displayed. Source and target stability reads through $M_X,M_Y,H,C$, while pointwise continuity is consumed only through $F$ and the finite windows named by the packet.

The sequential face is finite. The ledger $L$ records the inspected convergence thresholds, $W_X$ displays the corresponding source windows, and $W_Y$ displays the image windows obtained by the recorded continuation route.

Finally read the endpoint route. The source side reaches $E_X$ through $R_X$, and the target side reaches $E_Y$ through $R_Y$. Since the excluded principles are not coordinates of $Q$, the six listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sequential continuity Cauchy handoff]
\label{thm:sequential-continuity-cauchy-handoff}
Every accepted $\SequentialContinuityUp$ packet hands both the source sequence windows and the image sequence windows to $\RegSeqRatUp$ before any $\RealUp$ seal can be consumed. Equivalently, every seal-facing route factors through
$$
S,L\longmapsto W_X\longmapsto R_X\longmapsto E_X
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
W_X,F\longmapsto W_Y\longmapsto R_Y\longmapsto E_Y
$$
together with $M_X,M_Y,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the inspected source route. The continuation row records that $W_X$ is read from the displayed sequence surface $S$ using only the thresholds listed in $L$.

Next form the image route. The row $F$ is the displayed $\ContinuousUp$ pointwise-modulus surface, and $C$ records the replay from the inspected source windows to the target windows $W_Y$.

Both sides then pass through regular-rational readback. The rows $R_X$ and $R_Y$ are the only regular handoffs attached to the source and target windows, and the seal rows $E_X$ and $E_Y$ are downstream from those handoffs.

Thus a consumer that tries to use either $\RealUp$ seal without the matching $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff leaves the accepted $\SequentialContinuityUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Sequential continuity inspected-threshold coverage]
\label{thm:sequential-continuity-inspected-threshold-coverage}
For every accepted $\SequentialContinuityUp$ carrier $Q=(M_X,M_Y,F,S,L,W_X,W_Y,R_X,R_Y,E_X,E_Y,H,C,P,N)$, each threshold used by a Real-facing or regular-Cauchy consumer is first listed in the inspected convergence ledger $L$, then read through the source window $W_X$, the image window $W_Y$, the regular handoff rows $R_X,R_Y$, and only then through the seal rows $E_X,E_Y$. The coverage route is exhausted by the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows of the same carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and read the inspected ledger. The row $L$ is the only coordinate that lists convergence thresholds requested by the packet.

The source schedule then opens $W_X$ from $S$ at the listed thresholds. The pointwise row $F$ and replay row $C$ produce the corresponding image windows $W_Y$ without adding a detached sequence object.

The regular and Real-facing reads are downstream from those windows. The rows $R_X,R_Y$ repack the inspected windows as regular rational handoffs, and $E_X,E_Y$ are consumed only after those handoffs.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name exactly this route. Therefore the inspected thresholds cover the whole consumer path and no unlisted threshold, quotient stream, selected limit, choice row, or ambient completeness datum can enter the packet.
\end{proof}
