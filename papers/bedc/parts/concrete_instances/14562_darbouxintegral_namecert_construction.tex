\section{Carrier packet}
\label{sec:darboux-integral-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Darboux integral finite carrier]
\label{def:darboux-integral-carrier}
A $\DarbouxIntegralUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
D=(P,U,L,SU,SL,G,R,H,C,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $P$ is a $\PartitionUp$ row, read as the finite dyadic partition ledger on the integration interval.
  \item $U$ and $L$ are upper and lower $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$ approximants over a displayed common refinement of $P$.
  \item $SU$ and $SL$ are the finite upper and lower Darboux sum rows computed from the cells and carried values of $U$ and $L$.
  \item $G$ is the upper-lower gap and modulus ledger comparing $SU$ and $SL$ at the displayed precision.
  \item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ handoff surface produced from the gap ledger.
  \item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports of partition, step-function, sum, gap, and handoff rows.
  \item $C$ records the finite $\Cont$ routes that replay refinements, cell reads, finite sums, and handoff rows.
  \item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DarbouxIntegralUp}$ row and $\Pkg$ provenance surface attached to the packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier classifies two accepted packets only by these displayed rows. In particular, it has no coordinate for a selected tag, a host Riemann sum oracle, a quotient function space, a measure completion, or an ambient real-completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\section{Certificate obligations}
\label{sec:darboux-integral-obligations}

\begin{theorem}[Darboux integral NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:darboux-integral-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\DarbouxIntegralUp}$ surface consists of the following five rows.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Carrier habitation for the finite packet $D=(P,U,L,SU,SL,G,R,H,C,N)$ of \autoref{def:darboux-integral-carrier}.
  \item Refinement-stable classification of the displayed partition and upper/lower step-function approximants by common dyadic refinement.
  \item Finite Darboux-sum exactness for $SU$ and $SL$ over the carried cells and values of $U$ and $L$.
  \item Gap-modulus exposure through $G$, with $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ handoff through $R$.
  \item Ledger exclusion of selected tags, quotient functions, measure completion, and ambient real-completeness data.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DarbouxIntegralUp.DarbouxIntegralPacket\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is the finite packaging operation of \autoref{def:darboux-integral-carrier}. It displays the partition row, upper and lower step-function approximants, the two finite sum rows, the gap row, the handoff surface, transport routes, continuation routes, and the local name.

Refinement stability is read through the common-refinement machinery already exposed by the $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$ packet. The same displayed refinement transports $P$, $U$, $L$, $SU$, and $SL$, so classification remains a finite ledger statement rather than a comparison of host functions.

The Darboux-sum and gap rows are then inspected as ledger coordinates. Since every downstream handoff to $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, or $\IntegralUp$ passes through $G$ and $R$, the excluded host data have no carrier coordinate or continuation route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Darboux partition carrier admission]
\label{thm:darboux-integral-partition-carrier-admission}
Let $D=(P,U,L,SU,SL,G,R,H,C,N)$ be an accepted $\DarbouxIntegralUp$ carrier. A Darboux-integral read is admitted only when the finite $\BHist$ partition row $P$ displays a closed interval source, a bounded real function row, the cell ledger used by the upper and lower step approximants, and the local $\NameCert_{\DarbouxIntegralUp}$ carrier row. The admission route is transported by the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$, replayed by $C$, and sourced by the $\Pkg$ provenance inside $N$; it does not introduce Lebesgue measure, choice-selected partitions, or ambient real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DarbouxIntegralUp.DarbouxIntegralPacket\_partition\_carrier\_admission}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:darboux-integral-carrier}. The partition row $P$ is the only source row from which cells can be read, and the upper and lower approximants $U,L$ are downstream of that same finite ledger.

Use the carrier obligations of \autoref{thm:darboux-integral-namecert-obligations}. Carrier habitation and refinement-stable classification require the closed interval source, bounded real function row, partition cells, and local NameCert row to be read before any sum or gap row is consumed.

The structural rows preserve admission. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$, finite $\Cont$ replay in $C$, and provenance through $N$ keep the read on the displayed packet, so no measure object, selected partition family, or ambient completeness coordinate is admitted.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Darboux upper-lower sum ledger]
\label{thm:darboux-integral-upper-lower-sum-ledger}
For every accepted $\DarbouxIntegralUp$ carrier $D=(P,U,L,SU,SL,G,R,H,C,N)$, the upper and lower sum rows $SU$ and $SL$ are computed from the displayed partition row $P$, the upper and lower $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$ rows $U,L$, and the carried Rational and Real sum ledgers. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport preserves the same cell list and value reads before the gap row $G$ is inspected, and $\Cont$ replay exposes no host Riemann-sum oracle or choice-selected tags.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DarbouxIntegralUp.DarbouxIntegralPacket\_upper\_lower\_sum\_ledger}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:darboux-integral-partition-carrier-admission}. The accepted read has already displayed the closed interval source, bounded function row, and finite partition ledger.

Compute the two sums by replaying $C$ on the displayed cells. The upper row $SU$ reads only the carried upper step values in $U$, and the lower row $SL$ reads only the carried lower step values in $L$.

Transport through $H$ keeps the partition cells and value rows aligned. The Rational and Real sum ledgers are therefore the finite ledgers already present in the packet, not a host summation oracle, a tagged Riemann sum, or a measure-completion read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Darboux Cauchy gap nonescape]
\label{thm:darboux-integral-cauchy-gap-nonescape}
Let $D=(P,U,L,SU,SL,G,R,H,C,N)$ be an accepted $\DarbouxIntegralUp$ carrier. Any Cauchy-facing or $\IntegralUp$ consumer that reads the gap row $G$ must first consume the partition admission route and the upper/lower sum ledger, then read the $\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ handoff row $R$ through the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ surface. The gap cannot escape to Lebesgue measure, choice-selected partitions, quotient function equality, host completeness, or a detached real sequence.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DarbouxIntegralUp.DarbouxIntegralPacket\_cauchy\_gap\_nonescape}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:darboux-integral-partition-carrier-admission}. It places the gap consumer behind the displayed partition row, closed interval source, bounded function row, and local carrier admission.

Apply \autoref{thm:darboux-integral-upper-lower-sum-ledger}. The gap row $G$ compares the finite upper and lower sum ledgers $SU,SL$ after their common partition and value rows have been read.

Finally inspect the handoff row $R$. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ reads are downstream of $G$ and are transported, replayed, sourced, and named by $H,C,N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for measure completion, selected partitions, quotient function equality, ambient completeness, or detached real sequences, the Cauchy gap cannot leave the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Darboux upper-lower sum gap]
\label{thm:darboux-integral-upper-lower-sum-gap}

The transported packet computes $SU$ and $SL$ only from displayed refined cells and carried values, and the gap comparison never introduces a selected tag, quotient function, or measure completion.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DarbouxIntegralUp.DarbouxIntegralPacket\_upper\_lower\_sum\_gap}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through \autoref{def:darboux-integral-carrier}. The common refinement gives the finite cell list on which both approximants are read, and the rows $U$ and $L$ already carry the value data used on those cells.

Compute $SU$ and $SL$ by replaying the finite sum routes in $C$. Refinement only changes the displayed parent-cell ledger: each refined cell points back to a named cell, and its upper or lower value is the transported value already present in $U$ or $L$.

The gap row $G$ compares the two transported finite sums after this replay. Because the packet has no coordinate for tags, quotient functions, or measure completion, preserving the displayed gap cannot add any of those objects.
\end{proof}
