\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteStreamUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges on finite sequences and finite observation records, the $\FiniteStreamUp$ packet records the finite schedule object that sits between $\ListUp$ spine data and the countable-name windows of $\StreamNameUp$. It packages only the finitely requested observations needed by downstream regular-rational and real-name consumers. The chapter is a finite observation-schedule neighbour of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-list-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}; it does not export an infinite stream, quotient stream equality, a selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[FiniteStream carrier]
\label{def:finite-stream-carrier}
A $\FiniteStreamUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(Q,L,W,R,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the finite request row in $\BHist$, recording the ordered observation indices and payload requests actually inspected by the consumer.
\item $L$ is the $\ListUp$ prefix spine that stores those requests as a finite constructor-generated list rather than as a host array or hidden stream segment.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window handoff row, exposing exactly the window generated from $Q$ and $L$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback boundary available to regular-rational consumers only after the finite window $W$ has been displayed.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, recording the displayed dyadic radii, comparison cells, and finite precision budget attached to the window.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the request row, list prefix spine, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback boundary, DyadicRatCore tolerance row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite request through $Q,L,W,R,D,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteStreamUp$, $\ListUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteStreamUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the rows $Q,L,W,R,D,H,C,P,N$. No coordinate stores a completed stream, host stream equality, selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteStream NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-stream-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteStreamUp$ carrier
$$
F=(Q,L,W,R,D,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteStreamUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the finite request row, $\ListUp$ prefix spine, $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window handoff, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback boundary, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. The finite-window exactness clause requires $W$ to expose only the observations listed by $Q$ and represented by $L$. The StreamName handoff clause requires every downstream countable-name read to pass through $W$ before it can reach $R$ or $D$. The RegSeqRat and Real nonescape clause requires every regular-rational or $\RealUp$-facing consumer to remain downstream from $Q,L,W,R,D,H,C,P,N$. The provenance exhaustion clause requires $P$ and $N$ to name only this finite route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-stream-carrier}. Its displayed inventory is exactly the finite request row, the finite list spine, the StreamName window, the regular-rational readback boundary, the dyadic tolerance row, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Finite-window exactness is read from $Q,L,W$. The request row lists the observations, the list spine represents them as finite constructor data, and the StreamName handoff exposes precisely that finite window.

The regular-rational and dyadic rows are downstream. A consumer may reach the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback boundary $R$ or the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row $D$ only after the finite window $W$ has already been displayed.

Transport, replay, and provenance preserve the same route. Since the carrier contains no completed stream, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, trichotomy branch, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate, no Real-facing consumer can escape the finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteStream section sibling lock]
\label{thm:finite-stream-section-sibling-lock}
Every $\RealUp$-facing consumer of an accepted $\FiniteStreamUp$ packet reads the $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ boundary $R$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row $D$, and downstream $\RealUp$ seal rows only over the same finite request row $Q$ and $\ListUp$ spine $L$. In particular, no sibling packet can replace $Q$ or $L$ while preserving the displayed route
$$
Q,L,W,R,D,H,C,P,N.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-stream-carrier}. The rows $W,R,D$ are not free-standing analytic objects; they are displayed after the finite request row $Q$ and the finite list spine $L$.

The displayed $\Cont$ replay row $C$ then fixes the request coordinate used by the downstream consumer. Replaying the packet with a different finite request row would leave the listed carrier rows and hence would leave the accepted $\FiniteStreamUp$ packet.

The componentwise $\hsame$ transport row $H$ preserves the same coordinate rather than identifying it with an unlisted sibling. Thus any $\RealUp$-facing read that reaches $W,R,D$ or a Real seal remains locked to the original $Q,L$ route named by $P,N$.
\end{proof}
