uniform separation, the $\MetricSeparationReflectorUp$ packet records the
finite reflector that turns pseudometric zero-distance evidence into
separated metric identity without quotient primitives. It sits after the
pseudometric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-pseudometric-namecert},

\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},

\begin{definition}[Metric separation reflector carrier]
\label{def:metric-separation-reflector-carrier}
A $\MetricSeparationReflectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(P,Z,S,M,C,D,W,Q,A,H,K,G,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the $\PseudometricUp$ source row, carrying point observations,
finite distance comparisons, and zero-distance witnesses.
\item $Z$ is the zero-distance kernel row, recording exactly which displayed
pairs of $P$ are admitted as indistinguishable by the pseudometric evidence.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ identity row, consuming $Z$ before any
point classifier is allowed to identify two observations.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ handoff row, exposing the separated metric
consumer surface reached after $S$.
\item $C$ is the $\CompletionUp$ route row for completion-facing consumers.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to inspect finite
zero-distance budgets.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation schedule attached to the same
finite tolerance requests.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row obtained from
$D$ and $W$.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal through which numerical
zero-distance evidence is read.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source rows, kernel
rows, separated identity rows, metric handoffs, completion handoffs,
tolerance ledgers, stream observations, regular rational readbacks, Real
seals, replay data, provenance, and local naming.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay along the same route.
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricSeparationReflectorUp$,
$\PseudometricUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\CompletionUp$,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricSeparationReflectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through
$P,Z,S,M,C,D,W,Q,A,H,K,G,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient classes,
selected representatives, host equality of points, countable choice, ambient
completion, or an external real-line equality oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Metric separation reflector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-separation-reflector-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetricSeparationReflectorUp$ carrier
$R=(P,Z,S,M,C,D,W,Q,A,H,K,G,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\MetricSeparationReflectorUp}$ surface consists exactly of the
following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the pseudometric source, zero-distance kernel,
separated identity, metric handoff, completion route, dyadic tolerance,
stream observation, regular rational readback, Real seal, transport, replay,
provenance, and local naming rows;
\item kernel exposure, requiring every identity-facing request to display the
$Z$ row before it can reach the separated metric row $S$;
\item reflector exactness, requiring $S$ to identify precisely the displayed
zero-distance pairs admitted by $Z$ and no point pairs outside that row;
\item metric and completion handoff, requiring $M$ before metric consumers and
$C$ before completion consumers inspect the separated result;
\item numerical readback discipline, requiring $D,W,Q,A$ in that order for
any finite tolerance or Real-facing zero-distance read;
\item structural preservation through $H,K,G,N$;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding quotient classes, selected representatives,
host point equality, countable choice, hidden ambient completion, and external
Real equality.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:metric-separation-reflector-carrier}. The accepted packet
exposes the pseudometric source, zero-distance kernel, separated identity,
metric and completion handoffs, numerical readback rows, and structural rows.

The reflector clause is forced by the route through $Z$ and $S$. A
pseudometric comparison can become point identity only after the corresponding
zero-distance pair has appeared in $Z$, and $S$ is the only displayed row that
exports separated identity.

Metric and completion consumers are downstream of that separated identity
row. The metric handoff $M$ and completion row $C$ receive only the reflected
classifier, while $D,W,Q,A$ expose the finite tolerance and Real-facing
readback route used to inspect zero distance.

Finally $H,K,G,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name exactly these displayed
rows. Since no excluded object appears as a carrier coordinate, the listed
obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metric separation reflector zero-distance kernel]
\label{thm:metric-separation-reflector-zero-distance-kernel}
Let $R=(P,Z,S,M,C,D,W,Q,A,H,K,G,N)$ be an accepted
$\MetricSeparationReflectorUp$ carrier. Every identity exported to a
$\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, or $\CompletionUp$ consumer factors
through the finite route
$$
\PseudometricUp \longmapsto Z \longmapsto \SeparatedMetricUp
\longmapsto \MetricSpaceUp \longmapsto \CompletionUp
\longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp,\StreamNameUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\RealUp
\longmapsto H,K,G,N .
$$
Thus zero distance is the displayed kernel of the reflector. The packet
exports separated classifier identity, not quotient equality, selected
representatives, host point equality, countable choice, or an ambient
completion theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an identity-facing consumer of an accepted packet. The carrier
definition gives $P$ as the only pseudometric source row and $Z$ as the only
zero-distance kernel row, so the request must first expose the displayed
kernel evidence.

The separated identity row $S$ consumes that kernel evidence before any metric
or completion consumer can inspect the result. Rows $M$ and $C$ then carry the
separated classifier into the metric and completion surfaces.

The numerical route is also explicit. A zero-distance tolerance read passes
through $D$, the finite observation schedule $W$, the regular rational
readback $Q$, and the terminal Real seal $A$.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming in $H,K,G,N$ preserve this
route componentwise. No quotient class, selected representative, host
equality proof, choice schedule, or ambient completion coordinate is available
to bypass the displayed kernel.
\end{proof}
