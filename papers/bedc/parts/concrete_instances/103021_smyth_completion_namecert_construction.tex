\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SmythCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-smyth-completion-namecert}

Following Smyth's domain-theoretic completion of quasi-uniform spaces and the constructive completion tradition of Bishop--Bridges, the $\SmythCompletionUp$ packet names the finite asymmetric completion route that reads entourage observations before passing to rounded Cauchy data. It sits on the L10 Real and Completion frontier beside \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-formalball-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rounded-ideal-completion-namecert}. The packet exports constructive completion evidence without host quotients, selected representatives, \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Smyth completion carrier]
\label{def:smyth-completion-carrier}
A $\SmythCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(U,Q,F,R,D,M,B,I,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage surface restricted to the asymmetric observations used by the quasi-uniform request.
\item $Q$ is the quasi-uniform direction row, recording finite forward-entourage, refinement, and composition observations rather than a symmetric metric equality.
\item $F$ is the rounded $\CauchyFilterUp$ or $\CauchyNetUp$ refinement ledger accepted over $Q$.
\item $R$ is the rounded-refinement row, naming the finite cofinal requests that make the filter or net progressively tighter.
\item $D$ is the dyadic and regular readback row through which $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ observations reach the terminal $\RealUp$ boundary when a real-valued consumer is present.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ comparison row used only when a symmetric metric completion consumer asks for the associated finite route.
\item $B$ is the $\FormalBallUp$ formal-ball row exposing radius-tagged approximation requests.
\item $I$ is the $\RoundedIdealCompletionUp$ rounded-ideal row naming the ideal-style closure of the same formal-ball route.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the entourage, quasi-uniform direction, Cauchy refinement, rounded-refinement, readback, metric-completion, formal-ball, rounded-ideal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from quasi-uniform entourage observations through rounded Cauchy refinements, formal balls, rounded ideals, and optional Real-facing readback.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SmythCompletionUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\FormalBallUp$, $\RoundedIdealCompletionUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SmythCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these displayed rows. The carrier has no coordinate for a quotient completion object, a chosen representative, a host equivalence class, a hidden choice functional, \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, or an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Smyth completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:smyth-completion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\SmythCompletionUp$ carrier
$$
S=(U,Q,F,R,D,M,B,I,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\SmythCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed uniform-space entourage row, quasi-uniform direction row, rounded Cauchy-filter or Cauchy-net ledger, rounded-refinement row, dyadic and regular readback row, metric-completion comparison row, formal-ball row, rounded-ideal row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item asymmetric entourage exposure, requiring every completion request to pass through $U$ and $Q$ before any rounded Cauchy row is read;
\item rounded Cauchy refinement, requiring $F$ and $R$ to display finite cofinal refinements and not a quotient of Cauchy filters or nets;
\item formal-ball and rounded-ideal factorization, requiring $B$ and $I$ to carry radius-tagged approximation and rounded ideal closure before a completion-facing consumer receives the packet;
\item metric and Real handoff discipline, requiring $M$ and $D$ to be optional downstream readbacks from the same finite route rather than sources of completeness;
\item structural exhaustion, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the listed rows.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows, and none exports host quotient completion, choice of representatives, \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:smyth-completion-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the uniform-space, quasi-uniform, Cauchy-refinement, rounded-refinement, readback, metric-completion, formal-ball, rounded-ideal, and structural rows.

Asymmetric entourage exposure is forced by the order of $U$ and $Q$. A completion request first reads finite forward-entourage, refinement, and composition observations; it does not begin from a symmetric metric quotient or from a completed host object.

Rounded Cauchy refinement is then read from $F$ and $R$. These rows display the finite cofinal filter or net refinements that make the request progressively tighter, so they provide a constructive route rather than a quotient of Cauchy data.

The formal-ball and rounded-ideal rows give the completion interface. The row $B$ supplies radius-tagged approximation requests, and $I$ records the rounded ideal closure of the same route before any completion-facing consumer receives it.

Metric and Real handoffs are downstream checks. The row $M$ compares with $\MetricCompletionUp$ only after the Smyth route has been displayed, while $D$ exposes $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ readbacks without asserting ambient Real completeness.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ add no analytic principle. They transport, replay, source, and name the displayed route, so host quotients, selected representatives, \texttt{Classical.choice}, and \texttt{Quot.sound} have no row from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Smyth completion finite route]
\label{thm:smyth-completion-finite-route}
Let
$$
S=(U,Q,F,R,D,M,B,I,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\SmythCompletionUp$ carrier. Every completion-facing consumer of $S$ receives the Smyth completion only through the finite route
$$
U,Q \longmapsto F,R \longmapsto B,I \longmapsto M,D \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the packet exports a constructive asymmetric completion route from quasi-uniform entourage observations to rounded filter or Cauchy-net refinements, formal balls, rounded ideals, optional metric-completion comparison, and optional Real-facing readback. It does not export host quotient completion equality, a choice of representatives, \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a completion-facing consumer of the packet. Projection gives $U$ and $Q$, so the consumer starts from finite quasi-uniform entourage observations and their directed refinement ledger.

The rounded Cauchy stage reads $F$ and $R$. Whether the consumer enters through $\CauchyFilterUp$ or $\CauchyNetUp$, it receives only the displayed cofinal refinement rows and never a quotient of Cauchy names.

The formal-ball stage reads $B$ and $I$. Radius-tagged approximation requests are named by $\FormalBallUp$, and the rounded closure is named by $\RoundedIdealCompletionUp$ on the same finite route.

Metric and Real comparisons are optional downstream readbacks. A $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumer reads $M$ only after the rounded route is available, while any $\RealUp$-facing consumer reads $D$ through the displayed dyadic, stream, and regular-rational observations.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming close the route. The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the listed data componentwise, so no host quotient, representative selector, \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem is introduced.
\end{proof}
