\begin{definition}[Finite-tail diagonal seal carrier]
\label{def:finite-tail-diagonal-seal-carrier}
A $\FiniteTailDiagonalSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $q$ is the rational precision request, read through the displayed $\RatUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows;
\item $s$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window selecting the finite diagonal observation interval;
\item $r$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row whose regularity ledger supplies the source observations;
\item $w$ is the selected finite-tail witness, displaying the bounded tail segment that answers $q$ inside the window $s$;
\item $e$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $q,s,r,w$ have been exposed;
\item $h$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the precision request, StreamName window, RegSeqRat row, finite-tail witness, and real-seal row;
\item $c$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect the rows;
\item $p$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and selected-tail evidence;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteTailDiagonalSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient stream, no host real equality, no unbounded diagonal object, no ambient completeness theorem, and no selected limit outside the finite witness row $w$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-tail diagonal seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-tail-diagonal-seal-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\FiniteTailDiagonalSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $D=(q,s,r,w,e,h,c,p,n)$, precision routing through $q$, finite-window routing through $s$, regular-Cauchy source routing through $r$, selected-tail witness exactness through $w$, $\RealUp$ seal handoff through $e$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $h$, displayed $\Cont$ route replay through $c$, package provenance through $p$, and local naming through $n$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-tail-diagonal-seal-carrier}. The rows $q,s,r,w,e,h,c,p,n$ display the precision request, finite window, regular rational source, selected tail witness, real seal, transports, routes, package provenance, and local naming evidence.

The precision and source obligations are read before the seal. A consumer first reads $q$, then the selected StreamName window $s$, and then the RegSeqRat row $r$ whose regularity ledger supplies the observations inside that window.

The selected witness obligation is the row $w$. It gives the finite tail segment that answers the request and prevents the handoff from appealing to an unbounded diagonal stream or an existential limit choice.

Finally the rows $h,c,p,n$ account for transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming. Since the carrier has no quotient-stream coordinate, no host real equality coordinate, and no ambient-completeness coordinate, the obligation surface is exhausted by the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:finite-tail-diagonal-seal-window-factorization}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:finite-tail-diagonal-seal-namecert-obligations}. They force precision routing through $q$, source routing through $r$, and selected-tail witness exactness through $w$ before the real-facing row $e$ is reached.
