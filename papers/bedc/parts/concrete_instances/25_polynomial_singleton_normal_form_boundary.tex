\begin{definition}[Polynomial normalized singleton representative]
\label{def:polynomial-normalized-singleton-representative}
Let $R$ be a scalar carrier with classifier $\sim_R$ and zero endpoint $0_R$.
For a scalar coefficient $c$, define
$$
\mathsf{NormOne}_R(c)\Longleftrightarrow \neg(c\sim_R0_R).
$$
Thus the singleton coefficient spine $\mathsf{cons}(c,\mathsf{nil})$ is a normalized singleton representative exactly when $\mathsf{NormOne}_R(c)$ holds, while $\mathsf{nil}$ is the empty zero-polynomial representative. This is the one-coefficient boundary of the trailing-zero convention in \autoref{def:polynomial-carrier} and the canonical trimming rule in \autoref{def:polynomial-stability-certificate}.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.PolynomialUp.PolynomialSingletonNormalizedRepresentative}

\begin{proposition}[Raw polynomial addition leaves singleton normal form]
\label{prop:raw-polynomial-addition-leaves-singleton-normal-form}
Let $R$ be a scalar ring carrier with classifier $\sim_R$, zero endpoint $0_R$, addition $+$, and canonical trim $\mathsf{trim}_R$ as in \autoref{def:polynomial-pattern-specification} and \autoref{def:polynomial-stability-certificate}. Let $\mathsf{radd}_R$ and $\mathsf{PolySame}_R$ be the raw addition and normalized raw classifier of \autoref{def:polynomial-raw-add-comparison-data}. For scalar sources presented through $\CommRingUp$, \autoref{prop:commring-forgets-ring-certificate} reads these as the retained $\RingUp$ addition, zero endpoint, and classifier.

Suppose $a$ and $b$ are classified nonzero and add to a coefficient classified as zero:
$$
\neg(a\sim_R0_R),\qquad \neg(b\sim_R0_R),\qquad (a+b)\sim_R0_R.
$$
Set
$$
p:=\mathsf{cons}(a,\mathsf{nil}),\qquad q:=\mathsf{cons}(b,\mathsf{nil}).
$$
Then $p$ and $q$ are normalized singleton representatives, but their raw zero-padded sum is not a normalized singleton representative:
$$
\mathsf{NormOne}_R(a)\land \mathsf{NormOne}_R(b),
$$
$$
\mathsf{radd}_R(p,q)=\mathsf{cons}(a+b,\mathsf{nil}),
$$
$$
\neg\mathsf{NormOne}_R(a+b).
$$
Moreover its canonical normalized representative is the empty zero representative, and this is exactly what the polynomial classifier records:
$$
\mathsf{trim}_R(\mathsf{radd}_R(p,q))=\mathsf{nil},
\qquad
\mathsf{PolySame}_R(\mathsf{radd}_R(p,q),\mathsf{nil}).
$$
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:polynomial-normalized-singleton-representative}, the assumptions $\neg(a\sim_R0_R)$ and $\neg(b\sim_R0_R)$ are precisely the witnesses $\mathsf{NormOne}_R(a)$ and $\mathsf{NormOne}_R(b)$, so $p$ and $q$ are normalized singleton representatives.

Unfolding $p$ and $q$, the cons-cons branch of the raw addition recursion in \autoref{def:polynomial-raw-add-comparison-data} gives
$$
\mathsf{radd}_R(p,q)
=\mathsf{cons}(a+b,\mathsf{radd}_R(\mathsf{nil},\mathsf{nil})).
$$
The nil-nil branch gives $\mathsf{radd}_R(\mathsf{nil},\mathsf{nil})=\mathsf{nil}$, hence
$$
\mathsf{radd}_R(p,q)=\mathsf{cons}(a+b,\mathsf{nil}).
$$
The coefficient hypothesis $(a+b)\sim_R0_R$ contradicts any witness of $\mathsf{NormOne}_R(a+b)$, since such a witness is a proof of $\neg((a+b)\sim_R0_R)$. Therefore $\neg\mathsf{NormOne}_R(a+b)$, so the raw singleton spine is not admitted as a normalized singleton representative.

The same coefficient hypothesis activates the trailing-zero clause of the canonical trim from \autoref{def:polynomial-stability-certificate}. Therefore the singleton spine $\mathsf{cons}(a+b,\mathsf{nil})$ trims to $\mathsf{nil}$, and the raw-sum computation above gives
$$
\mathsf{trim}_R(\mathsf{radd}_R(p,q))=\mathsf{nil}.
$$
The empty zero-polynomial clause gives $\mathsf{trim}_R(\mathsf{nil})=\mathsf{nil}$. Unfolding $\mathsf{PolySame}_R$ from \autoref{def:polynomial-raw-add-comparison-data}, the classifier goal is
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_R,\mathsf{nil},\mathsf{nil}),
$$
which is the nil-nil constructor of the finite-spine classifier from \autoref{def:list-classifier-specification}, as isolated by \autoref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness}. Hence $\mathsf{PolySame}_R(\mathsf{radd}_R(p,q),\mathsf{nil})$ holds.
\end{proof}
