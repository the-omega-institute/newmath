\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\StackUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-stack-namecert}

The algebraic-stack interface $\StackUp$ packages a sheaf of groupoids on the étale site of schemes satisfying descent and representability axioms (Artin / Deligne-Mumford). It depends on $\NameCert_{\SheafUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sheaf-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\SchemeUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scheme-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:stack-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[Stack BHist carrier obligation]\label{thm:stack-carrier-obligation}
A $\StackUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet containing a displayed $\SchemeUp$ site row, a displayed $\SheafUp$ groupoid-valued presheaf row over that site, $\hsame$ transport rows for object and arrow comparisons, $\Cont$ restriction routes for cover refinement, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance for the consumed dependency certificates. It contains no host stack object, no host quotient stack, and no external algebraic-space equality.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackBHistCarrier\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Read the site coordinate through the $\SchemeUp$ certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scheme-namecert}. The site row is a visible BEDC ledger row built from scheme carrier and cover data, so it is already a $\mathsf{BHist}$-scoped input rather than a host site primitive.

Read the groupoid-valued presheaf coordinate through the locality and gluing surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sheaf-namecert}. Its object rows, arrow rows, restriction rows, and classifier comparisons are the rows transported by $\hsame$ and routed by $\Cont$ over the displayed scheme site.

The remaining coordinates are provenance and transport rows: $\mathsf{Pkg}$ records which dependency packets are consumed, while $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ record replacement and restriction behavior. Since every coordinate has been listed in this finite packet, there is no carrier slot for a host stack object, a quotient stack, or external algebraic-space equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stack descent obligation]\label{thm:stack-descent-obligation}
For a $\StackUp$ carrier packet, accepted cover-compatible local object rows and arrow rows glue through the $\SheafUp$ locality and gluing ledger over the displayed $\SchemeUp$ site row, and the glued object and arrow rows remain inside the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackCarrierPacket\_descent\_rows}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackCarrier\_descent\_obligation}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackCarrierPacket\_descent\_transport}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackBHistCarrier\_descent\_obligation}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackCarrierPacket\_descent\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Use the cover rows supplied by the $\SchemeUp$ site surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scheme-namecert}. They determine the visible cover indices and refinement routes on which the stack packet is allowed to restrict object and arrow rows.

Use the $\SheafUp$ locality and gluing ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sheaf-namecert} on the groupoid-valued presheaf coordinate. Cover-compatible local object rows glue to a global object row, and compatible local arrow rows glue to a global arrow row, with the readback comparisons expressed by the displayed section classifiers.

The resulting rows are repacked with the original site, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. Thus descent closes inside the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier and does not appeal to host quotient stacks or external algebraic-space equality.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackDescent\_obligation\_surface}

\begin{theorem}[Stack representability obligation]\label{thm:stack-representability-obligation}
Every visible diagonal row or atlas row of a $\StackUp$ carrier packet is readable only through displayed $\SchemeUp$ carrier rows and $\hsame$ transport over the site. Representability is recorded as a ledger predicate on those rows, not as an external algebraic-space primitive and not as host equality of quotient stacks.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackCarrierPacket\_representability\_surface}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackRepresentability\_boundary}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackRepresentability\_scheme\_site\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Project the diagonal or atlas read through the site coordinate supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scheme-namecert}. Each visible source, target, overlap, and cover entry is a scheme-carrier or scheme-cover row, so the read has no independent host algebraic-space endpoint.

Project the presheaf and descent comparisons through the $\SheafUp$ rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sheaf-namecert}. The relevant object and arrow comparisons are transported by $\hsame$ and restricted along the $\Cont$ routes already displayed in the stack carrier.

The representability witness is therefore a ledger predicate saying that the listed diagonal or atlas row is covered by the displayed scheme rows after the named transports. It is not an imported algebraic-space object and it does not identify quotient-stack presentations by external equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stack ledger obligation]\label{thm:stack-ledger-obligation}
The public ledger of a $\StackUp$ carrier packet exports exactly the site rows, cover rows, groupoid-object rows, arrow rows, descent witnesses, representability witnesses, $\Cont$ routes, and dependency $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows displayed in the packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackLedger\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
The site and cover exports are the rows inherited from the $\SchemeUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scheme-namecert}. They are the only rows that expose scheme carriers, affine or site cover entries, refinements, and the restriction routes consumed by the stack packet.

The groupoid-object, arrow, and descent exports are the rows inherited from the $\SheafUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sheaf-namecert}. They account for the groupoid-valued presheaf coordinate and for the locality, gluing, and readback rows used by descent.

Representability rows, $\Cont$ routes, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance are the remaining coordinates of the carrier packet. Since the ledger is the ordered projection of these displayed coordinates, it exports no host stack object, no quotient-stack equality, and no external algebraic-space equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stack NameCert obligation surface]\label{thm:stack-namecert-obligation}
The preceding four obligations supply the $\StackUp$ $\NameCert$ surface. Carrier inhabitance is witnessed by the displayed trivial-cover packet, classifier equivalence is componentwise $\hsame$, carrier transport is governed by the descent and representability rows, and ledger exactness is the exact projection asserted by \autoref{thm:stack-ledger-obligation}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackCarrierPacket\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.StackUp.StackNameCert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Carrier inhabitance uses the trivial cover available in the $\SchemeUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scheme-namecert} together with the identity local object and arrow rows read through the $\SheafUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sheaf-namecert}. These rows form the finite packet required by \autoref{thm:stack-carrier-obligation}.

Classifier equivalence is componentwise $\hsame$ on the site, object, arrow, transport, restriction, and provenance rows. Reflexive, symmetric, and transitive behavior is inherited row by row from the consumed scheme and sheaf classifiers, with $\Cont$ only recording the displayed restriction route.

Carrier transport follows by applying the descent obligation to glued object and arrow rows and the representability obligation to diagonal and atlas rows. Ledger exactness then follows from \autoref{thm:stack-ledger-obligation}, which lists every exported row and excludes host quotient stacks and external algebraic-space equality from the public surface.
\end{proof}
