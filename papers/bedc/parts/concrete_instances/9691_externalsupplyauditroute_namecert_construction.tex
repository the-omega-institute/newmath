It sits beside the boundary packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-external-supply-boundary-namecert}, the admission classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundary-admission-decision-namecert}, the GAP-facing closure boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-gapclosureboundary-namecert}, and the inscription ledger surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscription-gap-ledger-namecert}. \begin{definition}[ExternalSupplyAuditRoute carrier]
\label{def:external-supply-audit-route-carrier}
An $\ExternalSupplyAuditRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(B,W,G,L,H,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the boundary-form row, recording the strongest non-axiom form accepted for the requested supply;
\item $W$ is the witness-route row, recording the finite route by which a consumer reaches the displayed supply boundary;
\item $G$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$-ledger row preserving the residue or socket pressure generated by the request;
\item $L$ is the local route-classifier ledger, recording whether $B,W,G$ are all present before the route may be accepted;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the boundary-form, witness-route, GAP-ledger, and classifier rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ExternalSupplyBoundaryUp$, $\NonAxiomBoundaryFormUp$, $\NonAxiomAdmissionUp$, $\InscriptionGapLedgerUp$, $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ExternalSupplyAuditRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an axiom, hidden selector, quotient representative, proposition-equality token, unledgered supply witness, or host-side admission outside $B,W,G,L,H,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ExternalSupplyAuditRoute non-axiom boundary]
\label{thm:external-supply-audit-route-nonaxiom-boundary}
Every accepted $\ExternalSupplyAuditRouteUp$ packet exposes an external-supply request only through its displayed non-axiom boundary form. A downstream consumer may read the boundary form $B$, the witness route $W$, the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger $G$, the route-classifier ledger $L$, the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$, the package row $P$, and the local $\NameCert$ row $N$, but it receives no axiom, hidden selector, quotient representative, proposition-equality token, or host-side admission of the requested supply.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ExternalSupplyAuditRouteUp.ExternalSupplyAuditRouteCarrier\_nonaxiom\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:external-supply-audit-route-carrier}. The accepted packet displays $B,W,G,L,H,P,N$ before any consumer can read the route.

Use the distinction in \autoref{def:external-supply-non-axiom-boundary-form} and \autoref{def:external-supply-admission-decision}. The boundary-form row $B$ records which non-axiom form carries the request, while $W$ records the finite route to that boundary.

Now read $G$ and $L$. The $\mathsf{GAP}$ row preserves the residue or socket pressure, and the classifier ledger records acceptance only when the boundary form, witness route, and GAP ledger are present together.

The structural rows $H,P,N$ transport, package, and name only those displayed rows. Since no coordinate stores an axiom or hidden supply witness, the accepted route keeps the requested supply external.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ExternalSupplyAuditRoute ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:external-supply-audit-route-ledger-nonescape}
No accepted $\ExternalSupplyAuditRouteUp$ packet can be consumed as a supply route without its ledger row. If the route classifier reads a packet missing $G$, then the packet fails the route classifier and cannot expose a witness route as an accepted non-axiom supply boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. The classifier ledger $L$ is a row over the displayed triple $B,W,G$, not over the boundary form and witness route alone.

Apply the non-axiom boundary theorem above. It permits downstream reads only through $B,W,G,L,H,P,N$, so the GAP ledger is part of the public route rather than an optional annotation.

If $G$ is absent, the route classifier cannot certify that the requested supply remained ledgered. The packet therefore has no accepted $\ExternalSupplyAuditRouteUp$ read and cannot be consumed as a non-axiom supply route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ExternalSupplyAuditRoute NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:external-supply-audit-route-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:external-supply-audit-route-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ExternalSupplyAuditRouteUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
A=(B,W,G,L,H,P,N),

boundary-form exposure through $B$, witness-route exposure through $W$, GAP-ledger readback through $G$, classifier-ledger readback through $L$, componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $H$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

Consequently a certificate consumer may audit which boundary form was accepted, which finite route witnesses the boundary, which ledger records the residue, and which local rows stabilize the route, but it may not read unlisted supply content.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is immediate from \autoref{def:external-supply-audit-route-carrier}. The displayed packet lists exactly $B,W,G,L,H,P,N$.

The first obligation group exposes the supply boundary without internalizing it: $B$ names the accepted non-axiom form, $W$ names the route to the boundary, and $G$ keeps the ledger pressure visible.

The second obligation group stabilizes the route. The classifier ledger $L$ records that the route is accepted only with the ledger present, while $H,P,N$ provide componentwise transport, provenance, and local naming over the same finite packet.

By \autoref{thm:external-supply-audit-route-ledger-nonescape}, omitting the ledger row prevents route acceptance. Thus the NameCert obligations are exhausted by the displayed rows and do not supply any hidden content.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ExternalSupplyAuditRoute GAP-ledger factorization]
\label{thm:external-supply-audit-route-gap-ledger-factorization}
Every accepted $\ExternalSupplyAuditRouteUp$ packet factors its accepted route
through the displayed boundary form $B$, witness route $W$, $\mathsf{GAP}$
ledger $G$, classifier ledger $L$, componentwise transport $H$, provenance
$P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. Before any consumer reads the route, the
provenance row must pass through $\ExternalSupplyBoundaryUp$,
$\NonAxiomBoundaryFormUp$, $\NonAxiomAdmissionUp$,

unledgered supply witness, quotient representative, proposition-equality token,
or host-side admission outside the finite packet $A=(B,W,G,L,H,P,N)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:external-supply-audit-route-carrier}. The
only accepted route coordinates are the boundary-form row $B$, witness route
$W$, $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger $G$, classifier ledger $L$, transport row $H$,
package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:external-supply-audit-route-nonaxiom-boundary}. It restricts
every downstream read of the external supply request to the displayed
non-axiom boundary form and the rows that stabilize it.

Next use \autoref{thm:external-supply-audit-route-ledger-nonescape}. The route
cannot be accepted without $G$, so the supply witness is read only after the
GAP ledger and classifier ledger have both been displayed.

Finally apply

certificate packages $B,W,G,L,H,P,N$ over the provenance chain through
$\ExternalSupplyBoundaryUp$, $\NonAxiomBoundaryFormUp$,
$\NonAxiomAdmissionUp$, $\InscriptionGapLedgerUp$, and
$\GapClosureBoundaryUp$. Since no additional carrier coordinate exists, no
consumer can extract an axiom, hidden selector, unledgered supply witness,
quotient representative, proposition-equality token, or host-side admission.
\end{proof}
