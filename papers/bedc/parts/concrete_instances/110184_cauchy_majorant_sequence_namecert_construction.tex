\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyMajorantSequenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-majorant-sequence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\CauchyMajorantSequenceUp$ packet records the finite majorant-control surface used when a regular Cauchy name is read through explicit dyadic error budgets. It sits between the completion consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fast-cauchy-completion-namecert}, the threshold discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, the finite window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names one explicit Bishop-style majorant route; it is not a quotient stream, a selected limit, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy majorant sequence carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-majorant-sequence-carrier}
A $\CauchyMajorantSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
J=(S,M,W,D,B,R,E,H,C,N)
$$
with the following ten displayed fields.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source regular Cauchy name, read only through $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row assigning displayed stage bounds to requested dyadic precisions.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window ledger opened to inspect the source name at the requested stages.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording radii, slack comparisons, and finite budget arithmetic.
\item $B$ is the majorant sequence row, recording a displayed monotone bound for each inspected precision request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained from $S,M,W,D,B$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $R$ has been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all source, modulus, window, dyadic, majorant, handoff, seal, replay, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the same rows in consumer order.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyMajorantSequenceUp}$ row, including $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FastCauchyCompletionUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,M,W,D,B,R,E,H,C,N$. No coordinate stores a quotient representative, a selected limit, countable-choice tail selection, host real equality, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy majorant sequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-majorant-sequence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyMajorantSequenceUp$ carrier $J=(S,M,W,D,B,R,E,H,C,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyMajorantSequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the carrier row $S,M,W,D,B,R,E,H,C,N$, classifier stability under componentwise $\hsame$, dyadic ledger exposure, majorant exactness for the inspected precision requests, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, terminal $\RealUp$ seal ordering, displayed $\Cont$ replay, local provenance, and exclusion of quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-majorant-sequence-carrier}. The ten fields are the source regular Cauchy row, threshold row, finite window ledger, dyadic tolerance ledger, majorant row, regular readback, real seal, transport, replay, and local naming-provenance row.

The classifier is stable because $H$ transports each field componentwise and $C$ replays the same consumer order. A related packet can therefore change only by the displayed $\hsame$ rows, not by replacing the source with a host sequence or by identifying quotient representatives.

The ledger and exactness obligations are carried by $D$ and $B$. The dyadic ledger records every tolerance request and comparison budget, while the majorant row records the displayed monotone bound consumed by the handoff.

Finally $R$ and $E$ are downstream of those rows. Since the packet has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, countable-choice tails, host real equality, or ambient completeness, those objects cannot discharge any local NameCert obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy majorant sequence RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-majorant-sequence-regseqrat-handoff}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\FastCauchyCompletionUp$ consumer of an accepted $\CauchyMajorantSequenceUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
\CauchyModulusUp
\longmapsto
\StreamNameUp
\longmapsto
\DyadicRatCoreUp
\longmapsto
\RegSeqRatUp .
$$
The handoff exports only the source row, displayed threshold, finite windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, majorant sequence row, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local name carried by the same packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:cauchy-majorant-sequence-namecert-obligations}. They force every accepted regular-rational read to expose the modulus row $M$, the finite windows $W$, and the dyadic ledger $D$ before the handoff row $R$ is available.

The majorant row $B$ is then read as the exact control object for those displayed requests. It is tied to the same $M,W,D$ rows, so it cannot be detached into an external tail selector or a host-level bounding function.

The structural rows preserve the route. Componentwise transport $H$, replay $C$, and local provenance in $N$ keep the handoff on the same finite BEDC packet, which is the surface consumed by $\FastCauchyCompletionUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy majorant sequence Real seal nonescape]
\label{thm:cauchy-majorant-sequence-real-seal-nonescape}
Let $J=(S,M,W,D,B,R,E,H,C,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyMajorantSequenceUp$ carrier. Any $\RealUp$ read of the terminal seal $E$ must first pass through the displayed $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, majorant sequence row, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff in the same carrier. The seal exports no quotient stream equality, selected Cauchy limit, countable-choice schedule, host real equality, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier definition. The terminal real seal is the field $E$, and the definition places it after the source, modulus, window, dyadic, majorant, and regular-rational rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-majorant-sequence-regseqrat-handoff}. The regular handoff $R$ is reached only through $M,W,D,B$, and every completion-facing consumer shares that displayed route.

Now inspect the classifier and ledger exclusions from \autoref{thm:cauchy-majorant-sequence-namecert-obligations}. The accepted packet has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable-choice schedules, host real equality, or ambient completeness. The real seal therefore remains a terminal read of the finite majorant route rather than an escape to any of those objects.
\end{proof}
