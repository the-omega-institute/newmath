\begin{definition}[Reality-constrained explanation carrier]
\label{def:reality-constrained-explanation-carrier}
A $\RealityConstrainedExplanationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(\Pi,M,P,S,D,R,F,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\Pi$ is the finite observation-bundle row through which the phenomenon is read;
\item $M$ is the model row whose equations, derivations, mechanisms, or effective-theory operations are offered as explanatory;
\item $P$ is the phenomenon row naming the reality-facing target of the explanation;
\item $S$ is the signature-comparison row comparing the physical signature generated by $\Pi,P$ with the model-facing signature generated by $\Pi,M$;
\item $D$ is the tower-descent row recording which explanatory or predictive operations survive the approximation route that produced $M$;
\item $R$ is the residue ledger naming unmodelled variables, hidden tower levels, idealized-away structure, measurement conditions, and physical properties not queried by $\Pi$;
\item $F$ is the failure-surface row naming finite observations, refinements, or signature mismatches that would defeat the explanation;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the observation, model, phenomenon, signature, descent, residue, and failure rows;
\item $C$ records the accepted consumer route through the displayed explanation rows;
\item $Q$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealityConstrainedExplanationUp$, $\ObservationReflectionPacketUp$, $\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp$, $\PhysicalFitTowerUp$, $\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$, $\PhysicalModelAuditUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedExplanationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for bare equation reading, detached derivation, syntax realism, unscoped model truth, hidden idealization, or explanatory export outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained explanation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-explanation-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-explanation-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedExplanationUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly $E=(\Pi,M,P,S,D,R,F,H,C,Q,N)$;
\item signature-comparison exactness: every explanation read must use the displayed comparison row $S$ between the $\Pi$-indexed model signature and the $\Pi$-indexed phenomenon signature;
\item tower-descent: every explanatory operation exported from $M$ must pass through the descent row $D$ before it is read as a claim about $P$;
\item residue and failure-surface exposure: the residue ledger $R$ and failure-surface row $F$ are public before the explanation route is accepted;
\item ledger non-escape: no consumer may infer explanation from rows outside $\Pi,M,P,S,D,R,F,H,C,Q,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:reality-constrained-explanation-carrier}. The packet lists the only rows available to the local certificate, so no later consumer can insert an unobserved source, hidden model row, detached phenomenon row, or extra truth oracle.

Signature-comparison exactness is the distinguished role of $S$. The comparison is not a bare equation inside $M$ and not a host judgment about $P$; it is the displayed relation between the two $\Pi$-indexed signatures.

Tower-descent is forced by $D$. An operation internal to $M$ becomes explanatory for $P$ only when the same packet records its survival through the approximation route.

Residue and failure exposure are forced by $R$ and $F$. The packet accepts an explanation only with the unmodelled remainder and defeating observations visible to the consumer.

Ledger non-escape follows because the carrier contains no coordinate outside $\Pi,M,P,S,D,R,F,H,C,Q,N$. A route depending on any absent row is not a route in the accepted local naming certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained explanation scope and residue]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-explanation-scope-and-residue}
Let $E=(\Pi,M,P,S,D,R,F,H,C,Q,N)$ be an accepted $\RealityConstrainedExplanationUp$ packet. A consumer of $E$ cannot read bare equations, bare derivations, syntax realism, or unscoped model claims as explanations unless the same route displays the observation bundle, model row, phenomenon row, signature-comparison row, tower-descent row, residue ledger, failure surface, transport, consumer route, provenance, and local NameCert row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier definition. The only exported explanation packet is the finite tuple
$$
\Pi,M,P,S,D,R,F,H,C,Q,N.
$$

Bare equations and bare derivations use only syntax internal to $M$. They do not expose the phenomenon row $P$, the comparison row $S$, the descent row $D$, or the residue and failure rows $R,F$, so they do not project an explanation route from $E$.

Syntax realism asks the consumer to identify model syntax with reality-facing structure. The classifier surface instead requires the $\Pi$-indexed signature comparison and the displayed transport row $H$, so syntax alone has no accepted coordinate.

Unscoped model claims ignore the residue ledger and failure surface. By the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:reality-constrained-explanation-namecert-obligations}, the accepted route must expose $R$ and $F$ before the explanation can be read.

Therefore a consumer can export an explanation only through the complete displayed packet. Any route that omits one of the listed rows is a partial mathematical, syntactic, or model-audit read, not a $\RealityConstrainedExplanationUp$ explanation.
\end{proof}
