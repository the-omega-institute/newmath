Following Bourbaki and Kelley, the $\MetricReflectionSandwichUp$ packet records the standard constructive route that reads a separated metric surface through a metric embedding, lands it in a completion surface, and checks that the dense embedding row preserves the same finite distance evidence. It sits on the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the separation witness of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the embedding rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricembedding-namecert}, the completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the dense completion discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-completion-dense-embedding-namecert}, and the terminal scalar seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite NameCert consumer for the metric-reflection route; it is not a quotient completion, not a selected-representative theorem, not a choice principle, and not an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness claim.

\begin{definition}[MetricReflectionSandwich carrier]
\label{def:metric-reflection-sandwich-carrier}
A $\MetricReflectionSandwichUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(X,M,Q,E,C,D,R,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\MetricUp$ row whose distances are inspected.
\item $M$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ row, exposing the finite zero-distance separation evidence for the same source.
\item $Q$ is the $\MetricEmbeddingUp$ row, read as a distance-preserving route out of $X$ rather than as a host function equality.
\item $E$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ row naming the completion-facing target surface.
\item $C$ is the $\DenseEmbeddingUp$ row showing that the embedded source rows are dense in the displayed completion target.
\item $D$ is the dyadic distance ledger used by the metric, embedding, completion, and density rows.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ distance seal reached only after $X,M,Q,E,C,D$ have all been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for metric distances, separation evidence, embedding rows, completion rows, dense embedding rows, dyadic comparisons, Real sealing, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a source-distance read through the embedding and completion rows to the dense target read.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricReflectionSandwichUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\MetricEmbeddingUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\DenseEmbeddingUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricReflectionSandwichUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts only packets whose separated metric, metric embedding, completion, dense embedding, dyadic distance ledger, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows are displayed together. It has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice, host metric equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricReflectionSandwich NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-reflection-sandwich-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetricReflectionSandwichUp$ carrier $S=(X,M,Q,E,C,D,R,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricReflectionSandwichUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, separated-metric exposure, metric-embedding distance preservation, completion-row routing, dense-embedding readback, dyadic distance-ledger exactness, Real distance sealing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, local naming, and non-escape from the displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:metric-reflection-sandwich-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes the source metric, separation row, embedding row, completion target, dense embedding row, dyadic ledger, Real seal, and structural rows as finite coordinates.

The route through the sandwich is forced by those coordinates. A source-distance read starts at $X$, is separated by $M$, passes through the distance-preserving row $Q$, lands in the completion row $E$, and is reread through the dense embedding row $C$.

The remaining rows keep the route finite. The ledger $D$ supplies the distance comparisons, $R$ seals only those displayed comparisons as Real-facing data, and $H,T,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since no row stores quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice, host equality, or ambient Real completeness, the NameCert surface exports none of those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricReflectionSandwich completion route]
\label{thm:metric-reflection-sandwich-completion-route}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\MetricReflectionSandwichUp$ carrier reads the source separated metric through the finite route
$$
\MetricUp,\ \SeparatedMetricUp,\ \MetricEmbeddingUp,\ \MetricCompletionUp,\ \DenseEmbeddingUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \RealUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
The consumer receives a displayed metric source, separation evidence, distance-preserving embedding, completion target, dense embedding readback, dyadic distance ledger, Real distance seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. It receives no quotient completion, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, detached host metric equality, or ambient Real-completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier and apply \autoref{thm:metric-reflection-sandwich-namecert-obligations}. The completion-facing reader can inspect only the displayed rows of the packet.

The source metric is not read directly by the completion target. It first passes through the separated metric row and the metric embedding row, so the completion consumer sees the same finite distance data under the embedding route.

The dense embedding row then rereads the embedded source inside the completion surface. Dyadic comparisons and the Real seal are downstream of the displayed metric and embedding rows, while transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve that order. Therefore the completion route is exactly the finite sandwich route, with no quotient completion, selected representative, choice, host equality, or ambient Real completeness exported.
\end{proof}
