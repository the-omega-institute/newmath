\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompletionDenseRangeUp$}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive completion and Kelley uniform spaces, the $\CompletionDenseRangeUp$ packet records the finite dense-subsource extension interface for L10 Real and Completion consumers. It sits after the extension-uniqueness surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}, the functorial uniform-completion handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletionfunctor-namecert}, the Cauchy-completion reflection boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-reflection-namecert}, the finite stream window route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names dense-range extension as displayed BEDC data; it does not introduce quotient completion equality, selected limits, ambient complete objects, host equality of completed points, or countable-choice representatives.

\begin{definition}[CompletionDenseRange carrier]\label{def:completion-dense-range-carrier}
A $\CompletionDenseRangeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(S,D,E,U,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source carrier row, naming the metric or uniform source whose completion-facing reads are being served.
\item $D$ is the dense-subsource witness, recording finite evidence that the inspected subsurface is dense for the source requests consumed by the packet.
\item $E$ is the completion-extension row, carrying the displayed extension action from the dense-subsource data into the completion-facing target.
\item $U$ is the uniqueness-on-dense-source row, comparing two extension reads only after their common action on $D$ has been displayed.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row used to inspect source, dense-subsource, and extension data at the requested tolerance.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row produced from the same finite windows.
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row that bounds the finite window and readback comparisons.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, dense-subsource, extension, uniqueness, window, readback, tolerance, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a dense-source request through $S,D,E,U,W,R,Q$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompletionDenseRangeUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\UniformCompletionFunctorUp$, $\CauchyCompletionReflectionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionDenseRangeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only this finite dense-range route. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, selected limits, host equality of completed values, ambient complete objects, or a completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionDenseRange NameCert obligations]\label{thm:completion-dense-range-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CompletionDenseRangeUp$ carrier $G=(S,D,E,U,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionDenseRangeUp}$ surface consists exactly of source admission, dense-subsource admission, completion-extension admission, uniqueness-on-dense-source admission, StreamName finite-window admission, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore tolerance admission, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming for the rows $S,D,E,U,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:completion-dense-range-carrier}. The analytic rows are $S,D,E,U,W,R,Q$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

The dense-window coverage comes first. A completion-facing request reads the source carrier $S$ and the dense-subsource witness $D$ before the extension row $E$ or uniqueness row $U$ can be inspected.

The finite observation rows then bind the extension to BEDC-readable data. The row $W$ supplies StreamName windows, $R$ repacks those windows as regular rational readback, and $Q$ gives the dyadic tolerance budget used by both the extension and uniqueness comparisons.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since the carrier has no quotient completion equality, selected representative, selected limit, host equality, ambient completion object, or $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionDenseRange extension]\label{thm:completion-dense-range-extension}
Every extension read from an accepted $\CompletionDenseRangeUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
S,D \longmapsto E \longmapsto W,R,Q \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus a Real or Completion consumer receives extension data only after the dense-subsource witness, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, and DyadicRatCore tolerance budget are visible.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:completion-dense-range-namecert-obligations}. It exposes $S$ and $D$ before any completion-extension row is available.

The extension row $E$ is therefore tied to the displayed dense-subsource witness. A consumer that reaches $E$ must also carry the finite StreamName windows $W$, the regular rational readback $R$, and the dyadic tolerance row $Q$ that make the extension readable.

The structural rows do not create a second extension. They only transport, replay, source, and name the same route, so the accepted extension read factors through $S,D,E,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionDenseRange nonescape]\label{thm:completion-dense-range-nonescape}
Every uniqueness or completion-facing read from an accepted $\CompletionDenseRangeUp$ packet is exhausted by
$$
S,D,E,U,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N .
$$
In particular, the packet cannot export quotient completion equality, a selected representative, a selected limit, host equality of completed points, an ambient complete object, countable choice, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the extension theorem. By \autoref{thm:completion-dense-range-extension}, extension reads already pass through the source, dense-subsource witness, finite windows, regular readback, and dyadic tolerance budget.

The uniqueness row $U$ is read on the same route. It compares accepted extension rows only after their common dense-source action has been displayed through $D$, so it is uniqueness on the dense source rather than quotient equality of completed points.

It remains to exclude escape data. The carrier inventory has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, selected limits, host equality, ambient complete objects, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness, and $H,C,P,N$ preserve only the displayed rows.
\end{proof}
