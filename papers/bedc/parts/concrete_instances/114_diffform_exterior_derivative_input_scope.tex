\begin{theorem}[Differential-form exterior-derivative downstream scope]
\label{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-downstream-scope}
Downstream exterior-derivative chapters may use a $\DiffFormUp$ row only
through its degree row, its carried $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of tangent probes,
its multilinear $\TensorProductUp$ tensor row, its scalar endpoint classifier,
its antisymmetry ledger, wedge ledger coverage, the wedge associator ledger,
the wedge degree ledger, and the exterior-derivative candidate ledger of
\autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-candidate-ledger}. In particular, the
DiffForm surface supplies the carrier, classifier, exterior-algebra substrate,
degree-raise candidate row, and endpoint transport substrate for $\DeRhamUp$,
$\SymplecticUp$, and $\ContactUp$, but it does not by itself assert $d^2=0$,
closedness, nondegeneracy, or contact nonintegrability.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiffFormUp.DiffFormExteriorDerivative\_downstream\_scope}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the exported fields of
\autoref{thm:diffform-namecert-obligation-surface}. Each accessible component
is one of the displayed carrier, classifier, stability, wedge, or ledger rows.

The exterior derivative needs the degree row and the multilinear scalar
endpoint surface in order to state degree raising and scalar endpoint
transport. Those rows are available from the carrier and classifier; the
wedge degree ledger supplies the visible input-degree bookkeeping allowed at
the de Rham boundary. The candidate ledger of
\autoref{def:diffform-exterior-derivative-candidate-ledger} supplies the
positive output-carrier and successor-degree row allowed at that boundary.
Stability is supplied by the two transport theorems,
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-degree-classifier-stability}, and
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-wedge-input-stability}.

The identities $d^2=0$, closedness, nondegeneracy, and contact
nonintegrability require additional operator or geometric rows. They are not
fields of the $\DiffFormUp$ surface, so downstream chapters must state and
certify them in their own $\NameCert$ surfaces.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form source scope]
\label{thm:diffform-source-scope}
Every row accepted by $\mathsf{DiffFormBHistCarrier}_{M,T}$ depends only on
$BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$,
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$. No accepted
row may import a carrier component, classifier clause, or endpoint comparison
from outside those six displayed sources.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted row through
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}. Its six components are exactly the
degree history, probe bundle, tensor row, scalar endpoint, antisymmetry
ledger, and source ledger. The only structural rows mentioned there are
$BHist$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, the $\TensorProductUp$ continuation ledgers
$\Cont$, and the two upstream $\NameCert$ sources.

Now read the classifier clauses in
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier}. Degree, tensor, antisymmetry,
and source-ledger comparisons are $\hsame$ comparisons on visible histories,
while scalar endpoint comparison is the retained scalar classifier supplied by
the manifold and tensor sources. Thus every accepted classifier step remains
inside the stated source scope.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form wedge degree ledger]
\label{thm:diffform-wedge-degree-ledger}
For a carried wedge row $\omega\wedge_{\DiffFormUp}\eta$, the visible BHist
provenance exposes the degree row of $\omega$, the degree row of $\eta$, the
$\TensorProductUp$ concatenation ledger supplied by
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-ledger-coverage}, and the resulting unary degree
row of the wedge. This ledger states provenance for the wedge degree and does
not assert any exterior-derivative identity.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the wedge row by
\autoref{thm:diffform-wedge-ledger-coverage}. Admission of the wedge requires
a visible $\TensorProductUp$ concatenation row joining the tensor component of
the left carried form to the tensor component of the right carried form.

Read the two input degree rows from
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}. They are the first carrier
components of $\omega$ and $\eta$, and the wedge row records them in its
source ledger together with the tensor concatenation row.

Repack those displayed histories as the wedge degree ledger. The output
degree row is a unary history visible in the carried wedge row, so downstream
chapters may cite the two input degree rows, the concatenation provenance, and
the output degree row without adding any law about $d$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form scalar endpoint transport]
\label{thm:diffform-scalar-endpoint-transport}
Let $\omega$ and $\omega'$ be $\DiffFormUp$ rows with componentwise $\hsame$
comparisons on their probe bundles, tensor rows, antisymmetry ledgers, and
source ledgers. If the scalar endpoint of $\omega$ is accepted by the retained
scalar classifier from the upstream manifold and tensor sources, then the
scalar endpoint comparison transports to $\omega'$ through the
$\DiffFormUp$ classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First transport the carrier row. The componentwise comparisons on the probe
bundle, tensor row, antisymmetry ledger, and source ledger are exactly the
visible histories required by
\autoref{thm:diffform-carrier-hsame-transport}, so the transported row is
again accepted by $\mathsf{DiffFormBHistCarrier}_{M,T}$.

Next apply classifier stability. By
\autoref{thm:diffform-form-classifier-stability}, the scalar endpoint clause
of \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier} is preserved under the same
componentwise transport.

Finally, the scalar comparison used in that clause is not a new
$\DiffFormUp$ primitive. It is the retained scalar classifier from the
manifold and tensor sources, so endpoint comparison moves along the
transported probe, tensor, antisymmetry, and source ledgers already present in
the carried row.
\end{proof}
