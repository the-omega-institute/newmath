The $\OpenFitPacketUp$ carrier realizes the finite-observation open-fit discipline of \autoref{sec:finite-observation-open-fit} as a finite NameCert packet. It consumes the physical signature tower of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-physical-fit-tower-namecert}, the classifier boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-signature-fit-namecert}, and the audit gate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-model-audit-namecert}. Those packets separately expose physical-fit towers, signature compatibility, and audit ledgers; $\OpenFitPacketUp$ binds the open-fit failure surface and no-final-oracle boundary into one finite observational carrier.

\begin{definition}[OpenFitPacket carrier]
\label{def:open-fit-packet-carrier}
An $\OpenFitPacketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(H,\Pi,S,M,F,E,L,B,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the finite observation trace row.
\item $\Pi$ is the admissible observation-bundle row.
\item $S$ is the observed-signature row extracted from $H$ through $\Pi$.
\item $M$ is the model-signature row compared against $S$ at the displayed trace.
\item $F$ is the finite fit row certifying the currently accepted signature comparison.
\item $E$ is the failure-surface row naming finite signature comparisons that refute $F$.
\item $L$ is the ledger row recording accepted fit, undecided continuation, failure evidence, and refused final-fit demands.
\item $B$ is the boundary row forbidding export of a final-fit oracle from the finite packet.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\OpenFitPacketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for total confirmation, host identity between model and system, unobserved future signatures, or a global oracle deciding all admissible observation histories.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[OpenFitPacket obligation surface]
\label{def:open-fit-obligation-surface}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:open-fit-packet-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\OpenFitPacketUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $P=(H,\Pi,S,M,F,E,L,B,N)$;
\item finite signature exactness through $S$ and $M$ over the displayed trace $H$ and bundle $\Pi$;
\item finite fit exactness through $F$;
\item finite refutation exposure through the failure-surface row $E$;
\item ledger exactness through $L$;
\item no-final-oracle boundary preservation through $B$;
\item local naming through $N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[OpenFitPacket finite refutation surface]
\label{thm:open-fit-finite-refutation-surface}
Every accepted $\OpenFitPacketUp$ packet is refuted locally by a finite failed signature comparison displayed in $E$. Conversely, a consumer route that does not display such a failed comparison has no refutation witness inside the packet and must leave continuation status ledgered as open rather than confirmed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission exposes exactly $P=(H,\Pi,S,M,F,E,L,B,N)$. The only row naming failed comparisons is $E$, and the only finite fit row that can be defeated is $F$.

When $E$ displays a failed comparison between the observed signature $S$ and the model signature $M$ over the same $H$ and $\Pi$, the packet contains a finite witness against the accepted fit row. This is a local refutation because all participating rows are part of the same finite carrier.

When no such failed comparison is displayed, the ledger row $L$ still records the status as open. The boundary row $B$ prevents the missing failure from being converted into final confirmation, so the packet remains a finite open-fit certificate rather than an oracle for all continuations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OpenFitPacket no-final-oracle boundary]
\label{thm:open-fit-no-final-oracle-boundary}
No accepted $\OpenFitPacketUp$ packet exports a final-fit oracle. Its public evidence is exhausted by finite observation, finite signature comparison, finite fit, finite refutation exposure, ledger status, boundary refusal, and local naming through $H,\Pi,S,M,F,E,L,B,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier of \autoref{def:open-fit-packet-carrier} contains no row for total confirmation or global quantification over every admissible observation history. The obligation surface of \autoref{def:open-fit-obligation-surface} instead requires finite signature exactness, finite fit exactness, finite refutation exposure, ledger exactness, and preservation of the boundary row.

Thus a consumer may read the displayed finite fit, and it may read a finite refutation when $E$ displays one. The same route must also read $L$ and $B$, so undecided continuation remains ledgered and the final-fit demand is refused.

Any purported oracle for all future or counterfactual histories would need a coordinate outside $H,\Pi,S,M,F,E,L,B,N$. Since the NameCert surface admits only these rows, the purported oracle has no accepted route in the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OpenFitPacket signature-fit handoff]
\label{thm:open-fit-signature-fit-handoff}
An $\OpenFitPacketUp$ consumer can hand finite fit to the signature-fit and audit stack only through the displayed signature rows $S,M$, finite fit row $F$, failure-surface row $E$, ledger row $L$, and boundary row $B$. The handoff may consume $\PhysicalFitTowerUp$, $\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp$, and $\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$ certificates, but it cannot erase the open-fit failure surface or the no-final-oracle boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The physical-fit tower supplies the reality-facing tower context, the signature-fit packet supplies the comparison discipline, and the model-audit packet supplies the audit gate. The $\OpenFitPacketUp$ carrier reads those surfaces only through its own displayed rows.

The handoff therefore preserves $S,M,F,E,L,B$. Signature comparison travels through $S$ and $M$, accepted finite fit through $F$, finite defeat through $E$, audit status through $L$, and the final-oracle refusal through $B$.

If a consumer route drops $E$ or $B$, it no longer carries the open-fit failure surface or the no-final-oracle boundary. Such a route may still mention a physical tower, signature classifier, or audit gate, but it is not an accepted $\OpenFitPacketUp$ handoff.
\end{proof}
