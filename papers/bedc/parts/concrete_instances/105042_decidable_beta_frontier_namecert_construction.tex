\begin{theorem}[Decidable beta frontier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:decidable-beta-frontier-namecert-obligations}

\begin{enumerate}

\item bounded-window exactness, requiring every beta-check request to name the finite depth, fuel, closedness observations, and normal-output comparisons in $W$;
\item checker accountability, requiring $Q$ to expose only bounded checker facts already visible at the decidability audit surface;
\item frontier refusal, requiring $A$ to record the non-claim against full normalization, unrestricted conversion decidability, subject reduction, and global confluence;
\item compiler-layer provenance, requiring $G$ to be read only as a GroundCompiler audit row and not as hidden semantic evidence;
\item structural stability, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the rows already listed.
\end{enumerate}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The compiler and redex rows are $G$ and $R$. They provide audit provenance and frontier routing, not semantic shortcuts. Since $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name displayed rows, the local obligations are exhausted by the finite packet.
\end{proof}
