\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompletionReflectionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-completionreflection-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the completion reflection boundary is read here inside the BEDC mathlib-free substrate: completion data are admitted only as finite named rows, and reflection across the real seal is expressed by displayed transports rather than by host quotient objects. The $\CompletionReflectionUp$ packet ties together the construction surface of $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, the extension surface of $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, and the separated boundary of $\HausdorffCompletionUp$.

\begin{definition}[CompletionReflection carrier]
\label{def:completion-reflection-carrier}
A $\CompletionReflectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
R=(C,U,H,D,Q,A,S,T,K,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ completion-construction row;
\item $U$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ extension row attached to the same completion-facing source;
\item $H$ is the $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ separated completion boundary row;
\item $D$ is the $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ diagonal real-seal row;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational source row inspected by the diagonal and completion packets;
\item $A$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row through which the completed object is consumed;
\item $S$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for construction, extension, separatedness, diagonal, regular-sequence, and real-seal rows;
\item $T$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through the same rows;
\item $K$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\HausdorffCompletionUp$, $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$;
\item $P$ records the local reflection packet provenance over $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionReflectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient stream, no selected host limit, no host equality of completed reals, and no ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[CompletionReflection classifier]
\label{def:completion-reflection-classifier}
For accepted $\CompletionReflectionUp$ carriers $R$ and $R'$, the classifier $\mathsf{CompletionReflectionClassifier}(R,R')$ compares exactly the displayed rows of \autoref{def:completion-reflection-carrier}. The construction row compares through $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, the extension row through $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, the separated boundary through $\HausdorffCompletionUp$, the diagonal row through $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$, the regular rational source through $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the seal row through $\RealUp$.

The classifier has no component for a quotient of regular streams, a selected host limit, host real equality, or an external complete-space object. Transport is therefore confined to the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionReflection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:completion-reflection-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CompletionReflectionUp}$ surface consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission by the finite packet of \autoref{def:completion-reflection-carrier};
\item classifier transport by the visible comparison surface of \autoref{def:completion-reflection-classifier};
\item diagonal real-seal reflection from $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ into the $\RealUp$ seal row;
\item universal-property extension reflection from $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ back into the same completion and seal provenance;
\item non-escape from quotient streams, selected host limits, host equality, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of the finite packet. Each row required by the reflection boundary appears as a coordinate before any consumer can read it.

Classifier transport is componentwise. Use the displayed $\hsame$ rows to compare construction, extension, separatedness, diagonal, regular-sequence, and real-seal data, then replay the $\Cont$ routes attached to those same coordinates.

The diagonal reflection row reads $D$, $Q$, and $A$ together. It states that the real-seal observation is reached through regular rational diagonal data and not through a host real representative.

The extension reflection row reads $U$ against the same construction and seal provenance. Since the extension ledger is carried by $U$ and routed through $T$, the reflected extension cannot introduce a separate completion object.

Finally the provenance rows $K$, $P$, and $N$ account for package and local naming data. Because the carrier has no quotient-stream, selected-limit, host-equality, or ambient-completeness coordinate, no such object can be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionReflection real-seal boundary]
\label{thm:completion-reflection-real-seal-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer read of an accepted $\CompletionReflectionUp$ packet factors through the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ construction row, the $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ diagonal row, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, the $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ separated boundary row, and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. The read exposes no quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompletionReflectionUp.CompletionReflectionPacket\_real\_seal\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted carrier $R=(C,U,H,D,Q,A,S,T,K,P,N)$. The only real-facing coordinate is $A$, and it is paired in the packet with the completion construction row $C$, diagonal row $D$, regular source row $Q$, and separated boundary row $H$.

Use the classifier of \autoref{def:completion-reflection-classifier} to move between accepted representatives. It preserves exactly those rows by displayed transport and continuation, so any real-seal read remains attached to the same finite provenance.

Apply the non-escape row of \autoref{thm:completion-reflection-namecert-obligations}. There is no coordinate from which a quotient stream, host limit, host equality, or ambient completeness theorem could be read, so the real-seal boundary is the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionReflection extension reflects]
\label{thm:completion-reflection-extension-reflects}
Let $R=(C,U,H,D,Q,A,S,T,K,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CompletionReflectionUp$ carrier. Any completion-facing extension read exported by $U$ reflects back to the same construction row $C$, diagonal row $D$, regular rational source $Q$, real-seal row $A$, separated boundary row $H$, and provenance rows $S,T,K,P,N$. No reflected extension can switch to a quotient stream, selected host limit, host equality principle, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the extension coordinate $U$ inside the carrier. Its source and target provenance are not external: the same packet records the completion construction row $C$, diagonal row $D$, regular source row $Q$, and real-seal row $A$.

The separated boundary row $H$ fixes the Hausdorff-facing route. Transport along $S$ and $T$ keeps the extension ledger attached to that route, while the package rows $K$ and $P$ record the dependency surface consumed by the local certificate.

The obligation surface in \autoref{thm:completion-reflection-namecert-obligations} then repacks the extension read as a $\NameCert_{\CompletionReflectionUp}$ row. Since every exported coordinate is visible in the carrier, the reflected extension has no path to unrecorded quotient, limit, equality, or completeness data.
\end{proof}
