\begin{definition}[Heat-equation finite-stencil carrier]
\label{def:heat-equation-finite-stencil-carrier}
A $\HeatEquationFiniteStencilUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(F,T,X,B,Q,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the finite source-function window row, read through
$\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ observations of the finitely inspected
source values.
\item $T$ is the time-step row containing the displayed dyadic time width,
time-index schedule, and local tolerance budget.
\item $X$ is the spatial stencil row containing the displayed dyadic mesh
width, nearest-neighbour or finite-radius stencil weights, and inspected
spatial index schedule.
\item $B$ is the boundary row containing only the boundary values and boundary
classifier reads requested by the finite stencil.
\item $Q$ is the finite replay row that records how source, time-step,
spatial-stencil, and boundary reads produce the next displayed heat row.
\item $R$ is the real-seal row reached through $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ readbacks for the finitely inspected
stencil values.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $F,T,X,B,Q,R,P,N$ and
for the local certificate rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the source-function windows
through time-step, spatial-stencil, boundary, and real-seal rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HeatEquationFiniteStencilUp$,
$\PdeUp$, $\DerivativeUp$, $\IntegralUp$, $\LaplaceTransformUp$, $\RealUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HeatEquationFiniteStencilUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through
$F,T,X,B,Q,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for analytic solution existence,
global parabolic well-posedness, host function-space equality, measure-theory
integration, infinite mesh limits, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Heat-equation finite-stencil NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:heat-equation-finite-stencil-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\HeatEquationFiniteStencilUp$ carrier
$E=(F,T,X,B,Q,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\HeatEquationFiniteStencilUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier
admission, source-window admission, time-step exactness, spatial-stencil
exactness, boundary-row exactness, finite replay, real-seal handoff,
componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, provenance, and local
naming. These obligations are discharged along the finite route
$$
F,T,X,B\longmapsto Q\longmapsto R
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:heat-equation-finite-stencil-carrier}. The projection exposes the
source-function windows, time-step row, spatial-stencil row, boundary row,
finite replay row, real-seal row, transport rows, replay rows, provenance row,
and local name.

The source-window, time-step, spatial-stencil, and boundary obligations are
the rows $F,T,X,B$. They record only finite stream positions, regular rational
readbacks, dyadic mesh and time budgets, and boundary reads already displayed
in the packet.

The finite replay obligation is $Q$. It consumes $F,T,X,B$ before the real
seal $R$ is read, so the heat row is a displayed stencil calculation rather
than a host PDE solution.

Finally $H$ transports the same rows componentwise, $C$ replays the same route,
and $P,N$ source and name the packet. Since the carrier contains no row for
analytic existence, global well-posedness, host function spaces, measure
theory, infinite mesh limits, or ambient real completeness, the listed rows
exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Heat-equation finite-stencil Laplace-PDE handoff]
\label{thm:heat-equation-finite-stencil-laplace-pde-handoff}
Every accepted $\HeatEquationFiniteStencilUp$ handoff to a $\PdeUp$ or
$\LaplaceTransformUp$ consumer passes first through the displayed finite
stencil route
$$
F,T,X,B\longmapsto Q\longmapsto R.
$$
The $\PdeUp$ read receives only the derivative-relation and boundary rows
visible through the stencil packet, while the $\LaplaceTransformUp$ read
receives only the finite transform-window handoff after $R$ has been displayed.
Neither consumer obtains analytic solution existence, global heat-kernel
semantics, selected improper limits, host function-space equality, measure
theory, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier
$E=(F,T,X,B,Q,R,H,C,P,N)$. The PDE-facing data are not independent host
equations; they are the derivative-relation, time-step, spatial-stencil, and
boundary reads already present in $T,X,B,Q$.

For a $\PdeUp$ consumer, replay $C$ from the source windows through the time
and spatial rows into $Q$. The consumer can inspect only the finite relation
and boundary rows that the replay exposes, transported by $H$ and sourced by
$P,N$.

For a $\LaplaceTransformUp$ consumer, the transform-window handoff is available
only after the real-seal row $R$ has been reached through the same route. The
read therefore consumes finite stream, regular rational, and dyadic rows
before it reaches any transform output.

The excluded analytic objects are not fields of the carrier. The handoff is
therefore a finite NameCert route from stencil data to PDE and Laplace
interfaces, not a claim of global heat-equation solvability or host analytic
semantics.
\end{proof}
