\begin{theorem}[Bounded monotone convergence seal public handoff]
\label{thm:bounded-monotone-convergence-seal-public-handoff}

$$

$$
The consumer receives a finite Real convergence handoff assembled from $\BoundedMonotoneCauchyWitnessUp$, $\MonotoneCauchyUp$, $\CauchyCriterionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows, with the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row $L$ acting only as the compatibility boundary for the sealed route. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the bounded monotone witness surface. Next use the bridge to public tail and criterion packets. The $\MonotoneCauchyUp$ row supplies the public tail-window certificate, while the $\CauchyCriterionUp$ row supplies the explicit tolerance, tail, dyadic, regular-rational, and seal route.

The remaining rows $F,\sigma,D,L,R,H,C,P,N$ carry the public convergence handoff to the Real-facing boundary. The limit-seal row records compatibility with the sealed completion route, but it does not add a selected representative or a host equality coordinate.

A route that omits $W$ or $K$ has no access to the bounded witness or Cauchy criterion rows, and a route that asks for selected limits, quotient streams, host equality, ambient completeness, detached tail oracles, trichotomy, or compactness asks for data absent from the carrier.
