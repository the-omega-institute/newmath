\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\AbGroupUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-abgroup-namecert}

The abelian group interface \(\AbGroupUp\) extends \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert} with the commutativity field \(a \cdot b = b \cdot a\) under the carrier's classifier. It is the abelian specialization of the group certificate; the integer additive group from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-int-namecert} is the canonical concrete instance.

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[AbGroup carrier]
\label{def:abgroup-carrier}
Use a group setup extended with commutativity of the binary operation.
\end{definition}

\section{Concrete singleton history abelian group instance}
\label{sec:abgroup-concrete-singleton-history-instance}

\begin{definition}[Concrete singleton history abelian group instance]
\label{def:concrete-singleton-history-abgroup-instance}
Let $A_{\emp}$ and $R_{\emp}$ be the singleton carrier and classifier from
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}. Define
the operations by
$$
\mathsf{mul}(h,k):=\mathsf{append}(h,k),\qquad
\mathsf{inv}(h):=\emp,\qquad
e:=\emp.
$$
The resulting tuple $(A_{\emp},R_{\emp},\mathsf{mul},e,\mathsf{inv})$ is the
singleton history abelian group instance.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete singleton history abelian group laws]
\label{thm:concrete-singleton-history-abgroup-laws}
The tuple $(A_{\emp},R_{\emp},\mathsf{mul},e,\mathsf{inv})$ satisfies the
concrete group laws of Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws}
and the abelian commutativity law
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(h,k),\mathsf{mul}(k,h))$ for all histories $h,k$ with
$A_{\emp}(h)$ and $A_{\emp}(k)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The group fields are exactly the concrete singleton history group laws supplied
by Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws}.

For commutativity, suppose $A_{\emp}(h)$ and $A_{\emp}(k)$. These singleton
carrier witnesses identify both $h$ and $k$ with $\emp$ under $\hsame$.

The continuation unit laws reduce $\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ and
$\mathsf{append}(k,h)$ to the singleton class of $\emp$. Symmetry and
transitivity of $\hsame$ then assemble the endpoint relation
$\hsame(\mathsf{mul}(h,k),\mathsf{mul}(k,h))$.

The multiplication-closure clauses from the concrete group laws supply
$A_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(h,k))$ and $A_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(k,h))$. Together with
the endpoint relation, these are exactly the classifier witness
$R_{\emp}(\mathsf{mul}(h,k),\mathsf{mul}(k,h))$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.concrete\_singleton\_history\_abgroup\_laws}

Thus the $\AbGroupUp$ schema is instantiated by this concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$
carrier and operation, so later Lean work can formalize BHist-anchored abelian
group laws in $\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp}$ rather than another abstract
operator schema.

\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[AbGroup source specification]
\label{def:abgroup-source-specification}
The source is a certified group carrier plus a commutativity field.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[AbGroup pattern specification]
\label{def:abgroup-pattern-specification}
The pattern is group expression formation under an operation whose operands may be exchanged by certificate law.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[AbGroup classifier specification]
\label{def:abgroup-classifier-specification}
The classifier is the group carrier classifier.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[AbGroup stability certificate]
\label{def:abgroup-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item all group stability fields
  \item commutativity under classifier
  \item compatibility of commutativity with inverse and multiplication congruence
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\section{Concrete singleton empty-history abelian group instance}
\label{sec:abgroup-concrete-singleton-instance}

\begin{definition}[Concrete singleton empty-history abelian group instance]
\label{def:singleton-empty-history-abgroup-instance}
The singleton empty-history abelian group instance is the tuple
$(A_{\emp},\rho_{\emp},\mu_{\emp},e_{\emp},\iota_{\emp})$ where
$$
A_{\emp}(x)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(x,\emp),\qquad
\mu_{\emp}(x,y):=\emp,\qquad
e_{\emp}:=\emp,\qquad
\iota_{\emp}(x):=\emp.
$$
The classifier $\rho_{\emp}(x,y)$ holds exactly when $A_{\emp}(x)$,
$A_{\emp}(y)$, and $\hsame(x,y)$ all hold.
Equip this tuple with the AbGroup commutativity field
$\rho_{\emp}(\mu_{\emp}(x,y),\mu_{\emp}(y,x))$ and the associated
compatibility fields for multiplication and inverse transport on the singleton
carrier.
Its certificate is the ten-field fit with traditional target the one-element
abelian group; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier
$\rho_{\emp}$ with the three carrier-aware components above; stability
obligations for this classifier together with commutativity; ledger
constant-operation provenance; core theorem
Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-abgroup-laws}; exactness the singleton
endpoint classification $A_{\emp}(x)$; standard bridge the one-object abelian
group; and status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton empty-history abelian group laws]
\label{thm:singleton-empty-history-abgroup-laws}
The tuple $(A_{\emp},\rho_{\emp},\mu_{\emp},e_{\emp},\iota_{\emp})$ satisfies
the AbGroup carrier, closure, unit, inverse, associativity, inverse,
commutativity, and congruence laws for the singleton carrier $A_{\emp}$ and the
carrier-aware classifier $\rho_{\emp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier inhabitance is $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$, hence $A_{\emp}(\emp)$.
Every operation in the tuple returns $\emp$, so the carrier endpoint required
for closure, unit, inverse, associativity, inverse laws, commutativity, and
constant-operation congruence is always witnessed by $A_{\emp}(\emp)$.

For equality conclusions whose right endpoint is an original variable, the
corresponding carrier witness is supplied by the input hypothesis
$A_{\emp}(x)$. Together with the defining clause
$A_{\emp}(x)\Longleftrightarrow\hsame(x,\emp)$, this gives the required
$\hsame$ component.

It remains to check the classifier components. Since $\mu_{\emp}(x,y)$ and
$\mu_{\emp}(y,x)$ are both definitionally $\emp$, commutativity reduces to
$\rho_{\emp}(\emp,\emp)$. Its three components are
$A_{\emp}(\emp)$, $A_{\emp}(\emp)$, and $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.

The same reduction handles closure, associativity, inverse compatibility, and
multiplication congruence: every source product, target product, and inverse is
definitionally $\emp$. Whenever a law compares $\emp$ with an original endpoint,
the classifier is assembled from $A_{\emp}(\emp)$, the endpoint carrier
hypothesis, and the corresponding $\hsame$ witness obtained from the singleton
carrier definition.
\end{proof}

Thus the AbGroup schema is instantiated by this concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$
carrier and operations, so Lean formalization can target this BHist-anchored
instance rather than only abstract operator fields.

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup left-right multiplication swap]
\label{thm:abgroup-mul-left-right-swap}
For BEDC histories equipped with an associative multiplication, commutativity,
and multiplication congruence under \(\hsame\), the expression
\(a\cdot(b\cdot c)\) is internally the same as \(b\cdot(a\cdot c)\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Reassociate \(a\cdot(b\cdot c)\) to \((a\cdot b)\cdot c\), transport the
left factor across commutativity, and reassociate \((b\cdot a)\cdot c\) back to
\(b\cdot(a\cdot c)\).
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.abgroup\_mul\_left\_right\_swap}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup middle-four interchange]
\label{thm:abgroup-middle-four-interchange}
For BEDC histories equipped with an associative multiplication, commutativity,
and multiplication congruence under \(\hsame\),
$$
\hsame((a\cdot b)\cdot(c\cdot d),(a\cdot c)\cdot(b\cdot d)).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Reassociate \((a\cdot b)\cdot(c\cdot d)\) to
\(a\cdot(b\cdot(c\cdot d))\). Apply the abelian left-right swap to the right
factor, obtaining \(a\cdot(c\cdot(b\cdot d))\), and reassociate back to
\((a\cdot c)\cdot(b\cdot d)\).
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.abgroup\_mul\_middle\_four}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup inverse pair multiplication collapse]
\label{thm:abgroup-mul-inverse-pair-collapse}
For BEDC histories equipped with associative and commutative multiplication,
a right identity, multiplication congruence, and right inverses,
$$
\hsame((a\cdot b)\cdot(a^{-1}\cdot b^{-1}),e).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use middle-four interchange to regroup the expression as
$$
(a\cdot a^{-1})\cdot(b\cdot b^{-1}).
$$
The two right-inverse laws collapse
the factors to $e\cdot e$, and the right-identity law for $e$ collapses
the remaining product to $e$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.abgroup\_mul\_inverse\_pair\_collapse}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup conjugation collapse]
\label{thm:abgroup-conjugation-collapse}
For BEDC histories equipped with associative and commutative multiplication,
a right identity, multiplication congruence, and right inverses, every
expression $a\cdot(b\cdot a^{-1})$ is internally the same as $b$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the abelian left-right swap to rewrite $a\cdot(b\cdot a^{-1})$ as
$b\cdot(a\cdot a^{-1})$. Collapse $a\cdot a^{-1}$ to the identity by the
right-inverse law, then use the right-identity law for $b$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.abgroup\_conjugation\_collapse}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup inverse conjugation collapse]
\label{thm:abgroup-inverse-conjugation-collapse}
For BEDC histories equipped with associative and commutative multiplication,
a right identity, multiplication congruence, and left inverses, every
expression $a^{-1}\cdot(b\cdot a)$ is internally the same as $b$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the abelian left-right swap to rewrite $a^{-1}\cdot(b\cdot a)$ as
$b\cdot(a^{-1}\cdot a)$. Collapse $a^{-1}\cdot a$ to the identity by the
left-inverse law, then use the right-identity law for $b$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.abgroup\_inverse\_conjugation\_collapse}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup inverse preserves multiplication order]
\label{thm:abgroup-inverse-mul}
For BEDC histories equipped with group laws, commutative multiplication, and
multiplication congruence under $\hsame$, the inverse of a product is
internally the product of the two inverses in the same order:
$$
\hsame((x\cdot y)^{-1},x^{-1}\cdot y^{-1}).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The group inverse multiplication theorem identifies $(x\cdot y)^{-1}$ with
$y^{-1}\cdot x^{-1}$. Commutativity then exchanges the two inverse factors,
yielding $x^{-1}\cdot y^{-1}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.abgroup\_inverse\_mul}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup right factor swap]
\label{thm:abgroup-mul-right-factor-swap}
For BEDC histories equipped with an associative multiplication, commutativity,
and multiplication congruence under $\hsame$,
$$
\hsame((a\cdot b)\cdot c,(a\cdot c)\cdot b).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Reassociate $(a\cdot b)\cdot c$ to $a\cdot(b\cdot c)$, commute the inner
factor $b\cdot c$ to $c\cdot b$ under multiplication congruence, and reassociate
back to $(a\cdot c)\cdot b$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.abgroup\_mul\_right\_factor\_swap}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup common left factor collection]
\label{thm:abgroup-mul-common-left-factor-collect}
For BEDC histories equipped with an associative multiplication, commutativity,
and multiplication congruence under $\hsame$,
$$
\hsame((a\cdot b)\cdot(a\cdot c),a\cdot(a\cdot(b\cdot c))).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Reassociate the left side to $a\cdot(b\cdot(a\cdot c))$. Apply the abelian
left-right multiplication swap inside the tail to turn $b\cdot(a\cdot c)$
into $a\cdot(b\cdot c)$, and transport that tail equality through left
multiplication by $a$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.abgroup\_mul\_common\_left\_factor\_collect}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup common right factor collection]
\label{thm:abgroup-mul-common-right-factor-collect}
For BEDC histories equipped with an associative multiplication, commutativity,
and multiplication congruence under $\hsame$,
$$
\hsame((b\cdot a)\cdot(c\cdot a),a\cdot(a\cdot(b\cdot c))).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Commute the exposed right factors to rewrite the left side as
\((a\cdot b)\cdot(a\cdot c)\). The common left factor collection theorem then
collects the two occurrences of \(a\) and leaves the residual factor
\(b\cdot c\).
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.abgroup\_mul\_common\_right\_factor\_collect}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup balanced multiplication cancellation]
\label{thm:abgroup-mul-balanced-cancel}
For BEDC histories equipped with group laws, commutativity, and multiplication
congruence under $\hsame$, if $a\cdot b\hsame c\cdot a$, then $b\hsame c$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Commute the right endpoint $c\cdot a$ to $a\cdot c$. The hypothesis then has
the common left factor $a$ on both sides, so group left cancellation removes
that factor and yields $b\hsame c$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.abgroup\_mul\_balanced\_cancel}

\begin{theorem}[AbGroup middle-four double cancellation]
\label{thm:abgroup-middle-four-double-cancel}
For BEDC histories equipped with group laws, commutativity, and multiplication
congruence under $\hsame$, if
$$
\hsame((a\cdot b)\cdot(c\cdot d),(a\cdot c)\cdot(u\cdot d)),
$$
then $\hsame(b,u)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The rule depends on the AbGroup middle-four theorem and on the Group left and
right cancellation theorems; it is not merely a renamed form of any one
theorem. Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:abgroup-middle-four-interchange} to the left
endpoint of the hypothesis and compose by symmetry and transitivity of
$\hsame$. This turns the supplied comparison into
$$
\hsame((a\cdot c)\cdot(b\cdot d),(a\cdot c)\cdot(u\cdot d)).
$$

Now apply group left cancellation, Theorem~\ref{thm:group-left-cancellation},
to the common left factor $a\cdot c$. The remaining comparison is
$$
\hsame(b\cdot d,u\cdot d).
$$

Finally apply group right cancellation, Theorem~\ref{thm:group-right-cancellation},
to the common right factor $d$, yielding $\hsame(b,u)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Nonempty history append cannot be right-absorbed]
\label{thm:abgroup-history-append-nonempty-left-ne-right}
For every BEDC history $h$ and right tail $k$, neither
$\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{e0}(h),k)=k$ nor
$\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{e1}(h),k)=k$ holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the right tail $k$. The empty case is constructor disjointness
against $\emp$. In the nonempty cases, append exposes the same outer
constructor as the tail, so constructor injectivity reduces the claim to the
induction hypothesis on the inner tail.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AbGroupUp.history\_append\_nonempty\_left\_ne\_right}

\begin{definition}[AbGroup ledger policy]
\label{def:abgroup-ledger-policy}
Record group expression provenance and the commutativity law when used.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[\(\AbGroupUp\) certificate obligations]
\label{def:abgroup-certificate-obligations}
The carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data listed above are the obligations required for the interface name \(\AbGroupUp\).
\end{definition}

\paragraph{Primitive boundary.}
\(\AbGroupUp\) is not a BEDC primitive. It is admitted only when those obligations are supplied.

\begin{proposition}[AbGroup forgets to Group certificate]
\label{prop:abgroup-forgets-group-certificate}
Let \(M\) carry an \(\AbGroupUp(M)\) certificate. If the commutativity field and its compatibility data are omitted, the retained carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger fields form a \(\GroupUp(M)\) certificate. The carrier, multiplication \(\cdot\), identity \(e\), inverse operation \({}^{-1}\), and classifier are unchanged.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{def:abgroup-certificate-obligations}, the given certificate supplies carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data for \(\AbGroupUp\). The argument keeps the group-shaped fields among these data.

By \autoref{def:abgroup-carrier}, an \(\AbGroupUp\) carrier is a group setup together with commutativity of the binary operation. The underlying carrier, classifier, multiplication, identity, and inverse are therefore already the data listed in \autoref{def:group-carrier}.

By \autoref{def:abgroup-source-specification}, the source is a certified group carrier plus a commutativity field. Removing the commutativity component leaves the certified group carrier. By \autoref{def:group-source-specification}, this is the certified monoid carrier plus inverse data required for the group source. By \autoref{def:abgroup-classifier-specification}, the classifier is the group carrier classifier, so it is exactly the classifier of \autoref{def:group-classifier-specification}.

By \autoref{def:abgroup-pattern-specification}, the abelian group pattern is group expression formation with the added permission to exchange operands by the commutativity law. Omitting only that exchange permission leaves the group expression formation of \autoref{def:group-pattern-specification}.

By \autoref{def:abgroup-stability-certificate}, the stability certificate contains all group stability fields, commutativity under classifier, and compatibility of commutativity with inverse and multiplication congruence. The first component is precisely the list of group stability fields in \autoref{def:group-stability-certificate}; the latter components are not obligations for \(\GroupUp\). The remaining ledger is the group expression provenance of \autoref{def:group-ledger-policy}, matching the group part of \autoref{def:abgroup-ledger-policy}.

The retained carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data are exactly the obligations named in \autoref{def:group-certificate-obligations}. Since each retained operation and the classifier are copied from the original \(\AbGroupUp(M)\) certificate, the resulting \(\GroupUp(M)\) source package has the same carrier operation, identity, inverse, and classifier.
\end{proof}
