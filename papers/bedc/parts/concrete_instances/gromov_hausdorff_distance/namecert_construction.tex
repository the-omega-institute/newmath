\label{def:gromov-hausdorff-distance-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are $\CompactMetricUp$ endpoint carriers, each exposing its $\MetricUp$ distance rows, compact finite-net rows, and local compact-metric NameCert data before any cross-endpoint comparison is public.
\item $R$ is the finite correspondence data between the endpoint point-name surfaces, including the two projection coverage ledgers and the inhabitedness rows needed for a correspondence read.
\item $D$ is the dyadic distortion bound, recording a displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance for every pair of finite correspondence rows and the symmetric comparison budget induced by those rows.
\item $H$ records the $\hsame$ transport rows that keep the endpoint carriers, correspondence rows, dyadic distortion ledger, replay row, provenance row, and local name attached under accepted renamings.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$

\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\GromovHausdorffDistanceUp$, $\GromovHausdorffUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\HausdorffDistanceUp$, $\CompactMetricHyperspaceUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.

\begin{theorem}[Gromov--Hausdorff distance NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:gromov-hausdorff-distance-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\GromovHausdorffDistanceUp$ carrier
$$
Z=(X,Y,R,D,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\GromovHausdorffDistanceUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the compact metric endpoints $X,Y$, finite correspondence data $R$, dyadic distortion bound $D$, transport row $H$, replay row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local name $N$;
\item correspondence stability, requiring the projection coverage and inhabitedness rows in $R$ to remain attached to the same two compact metric endpoints under the transport row $H$;
\item distortion symmetry, requiring the dyadic bound $D$ to compare both endpoint distance reads for every displayed pair of correspondence rows with the same tolerance budget;
\item triangle composition by correspondence composition, requiring composed comparison reads to concatenate the finite correspondence tables and add their displayed dyadic distortion budgets before a downstream consumer reads the result;
\item ledger refusal, requiring $C,P,N$ to replay, source, and name only the displayed finite route and to export no quotient metric space, selected optimal correspondence, choice over covers, ambient completion, host isometry class, or completed hyperspace point.
\end{enumerate}
No Gromov--Hausdorff distance obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:gromov-hausdorff-distance-carrier}. The endpoint rows $X,Y$ are the only compact metric sources, and the rows $R,D,H,C,P,N$ are the entire comparison surface attached to them.

Carrier admission is therefore the admission of those displayed rows. The correspondence stability row is read from $R$ together with $H$, because the projection ledgers and inhabitedness witnesses are transported only as rows over the same two endpoints.

The distortion symmetry row is read from $D$. Each finite pair of correspondence rows triggers two endpoint distance reads, and the same dyadic tolerance ledger bounds the two directed comparisons.

For triangle composition, compose the finite correspondence tables by their shared middle endpoint and add the two displayed dyadic distortion ledgers. The result is again a finite table with a displayed tolerance budget, so the composed comparison remains inside the same carrier discipline.

Finally $C,P,N$ replay, source, and name the route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient metric spaces, selected optimal correspondences, choice over covers, ambient completions, host isometry classes, or completed hyperspace points, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Gromov--Hausdorff distance compact-metric handoff]
\label{thm:gromov-hausdorff-distance-compact-metric-handoff}

It cannot derive a quotient metric space, selected optimal correspondence, choice over covers, ambient completion, host isometry class, or completed hyperspace point from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Next read the correspondence and distortion rows. The finite table $R$ supplies the cross-endpoint pairs and coverage witnesses, while $D$ supplies the dyadic distortion budget attached to exactly that table.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same route. Because no additional coordinate is present, the handoff cannot export a quotient metric object, selected optimum, cover choice, ambient completion, host isometry class, or completed hyperspace point.
