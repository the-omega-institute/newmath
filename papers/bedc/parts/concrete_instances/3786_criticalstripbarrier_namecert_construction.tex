The $\CriticalStripBarrierUp$ packet realizes the RH approach-rate witness as finite barrier-ledger data. It binds a supplied critical-strip row, a supplied zeta-zero row, a critical-line witness row, the real-part and rational lower-bound rows, transport and continuation rows, package provenance, and a local naming row. The witness row $W$ is consumed from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-criticallinewitness-namecert} as the finite critical-line witness route, and this barrier chapter only transports that sibling row into the strip, zero, and refusal ledger rather than proving $\mathsf{RH}$ or adding analytic zero search. The packet records how a consumer may route a zero-localization or off-line lower-bound read; it does not prove $\mathsf{RH}$, compute zeros, or assert a global analytic lower bound outside the displayed finite rows.

\begin{definition}[Critical-strip-barrier carrier]
\label{def:critical-strip-barrier-carrier}
A $\CriticalStripBarrierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(S,Z,W,R,Q,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the supplied $\CritStripUp$ row carrying the critical-strip membership data for the displayed source history;
\item $Z$ is the supplied $\ZetaZerosUp$ row carrying the zeta-zero premise for the same displayed source when a zero-localization read is requested;
\item $W$ is the supplied $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$ row available to consumers that already carry a critical-line witness route;
\item $R$ is the real-part comparison row reading $\Re(s)$ through the $\RealUp$ carrier exposed by the strip and zero rows;
\item $Q$ is the rational distance row recording the displayed lower-bound comparison against the rational history $1/2$ in $\RatUp$;
\item $A$ is the approach-rate ledger row that pairs each displayed rational separation depth with the corresponding positive rational lower-bound entry;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,Z,W,R,Q,A$ and for the package and local naming coordinates;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the strip row to the zero row, from the real-part row to the rational distance row, from the distance row to the approach-rate ledger, and from the witness row to the RH-facing handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CritStripUp$, $\ZetaZerosUp$, $\CriticalLineWitnessUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CriticalStripBarrierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only through the displayed strip, zero, witness, real-part, rational-bound, approach-rate, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalStripBarrier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:critical-strip-barrier-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CriticalStripBarrierUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:critical-strip-barrier-carrier} consists exactly of six finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $B=(S,Z,W,R,Q,A,H,C,P,N)$;
\item zero-localization routing from the $\CritStripUp$ row $S$ to the $\ZetaZerosUp$ row $Z$ through displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows;
\item rational separation exposure through $R,Q,A$ as the only place where a lower-bound comparison against $1/2$ is read;
\item witness handoff through $W$ only when a consumer already supplies the critical-line witness route;
\item componentwise stability under the transports in $H$ and replay through the routes in $C$;
\item non-escape from a proof of $\mathsf{RH}$, analytic zero search, host complex equality, quotient zero-set equality, or an undisplayed global lower-bound theorem.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:critical-strip-barrier-carrier}. The strip row, zero row, witness row, real-part row, rational distance row, approach-rate ledger, transport row, continuation row, package row, and local naming row are all displayed coordinates.

Zero-localization routing is read only through $S,Z,H,C$. These rows bind the supplied critical-strip premise to the supplied zeta-zero premise without replacing either one by host complex equality or by a selected representative.

The approach-rate side is read through $R,Q,A$. The real row gives the displayed real part, the rational row gives the displayed comparison against $1/2$, and the ledger row gives the finite lower-bound entry attached to that comparison.

The handoff row $W$ is a consumer boundary, not an RH proof. It may be read only as an already supplied critical-line witness route, so the packet does not turn a zero-in-strip premise into a proof that all zeros lie on the line.

Finally inspect the carrier inventory. Since no coordinate names zero search, quotient zero-set equality, host complex equality, a global analytic lower-bound theorem, or an RH inhabitant, the six obligations exhaust the local naming surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalStripBarrier zero-localization route]
\label{thm:critical-strip-barrier-zero-localization-route}
For an accepted $\CriticalStripBarrierUp$ carrier
$$
B=(S,Z,W,R,Q,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
every zero-localization read factors through the displayed rows $S,Z,H,C,P,N$. The consumer receives the supplied critical-strip premise, the supplied zeta-zero premise, the componentwise transport tying them to the same displayed source, the continuation route by which the rows are replayed, package provenance, and the local name; it receives no zero search, host equality of complex histories, quotient zero-set equality, or selected representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the zero-facing rows from the carrier. The rows available to such a read are $S,Z,H,C,P,N$.

The strip row $S$ supplies the critical-strip side, and the zero row $Z$ supplies the zeta-zero side. The transport and continuation rows bind them as one displayed route without adding a host equality or quotient comparison.

The package and local naming rows close the finite route. Because the carrier contains no coordinate for zero search or representative selection, the zero-localization read remains exactly the displayed finite packet route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalStripBarrier non-escape]
\label{thm:critical-strip-barrier-nonescape}
Every public read exported by an accepted $\CriticalStripBarrierUp$ packet factors through $S,Z,W,R,Q,A,H,C,P,N$. A request for a proof of $\mathsf{RH}$, an analytic zero-finding procedure, a host complex equality, a quotient zero-set equality, or a lower-bound theorem not displayed in the finite approach-rate ledger is outside the packet and is not exported as NameCert evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read a public request through the displayed continuation row $C$. If the request asks for the strip row, zero row, witness row, real-part row, rational-bound row, approach-rate ledger, transport row, package provenance, or local naming row, the carrier supplies the corresponding coordinate.

All other requests leave the carrier inventory. In particular, no coordinate carries an RH proof, analytic zero search, host complex equality, quotient zero-set equality, or an undisplayed lower-bound theorem.

Thus the local $\NameCert$ row can certify only the finite barrier ledger and its displayed routes. It cannot export evidence that is absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CriticalStripBarrier RH witness handoff]
\label{thm:critical-strip-barrier-rh-witness-handoff}
The RH approach-rate witness of \autoref{thm:rh-witness-approach-rate} is realized, at the concrete-instance level, by the finite $\CriticalStripBarrierUp$ packet
$$
S,Z,W,R,Q,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
An RH-facing consumer may read zero localization through $S,Z,H,C,P,N$, rational lower-bound separation through $R,Q,A,H,C,P,N$, and an already supplied critical-line witness route through $W,H,C,P,N$. The handoff is a finite NameCert packet for the witness surface; it is not a proof of $\mathsf{RH}$ and not an analytic theorem that every off-line point is bounded away from zero.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier definition and NameCert obligations. They expose exactly the strip, zero, witness, real-part, rational-bound, approach-rate, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows.

Use the zero-localization route for $S,Z,H,C,P,N$. This supplies the premise side needed by an RH-facing consumer that is inspecting a zero in the critical strip.

Use the rational-bound rows $R,Q,A,H,C,P,N$ for the approach-rate side. These rows provide only the finite lower-bound ledger entries that are displayed in the packet.

Finally use the non-escape theorem. Since the carrier has no coordinate for an RH proof or a global analytic lower-bound theorem, the exported object is precisely the finite witness handoff named by $\CriticalStripBarrierUp$.
\end{proof}
