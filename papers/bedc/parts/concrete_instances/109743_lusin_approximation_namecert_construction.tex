Following Lusin and Bishop--Bridges constructive measure theory, the $\LusinApproximationUp$ packet records the finite route by which a measurable real-valued row is approximated on a large displayed set by a continuous row. It sits after the measurable carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-measure-namecert}, the continuous-map surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the integral readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-integral-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the finite exceptional-window route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-egorov-namecert}, and the density ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-radon-nikodym-namecert}. The carrier exposes the approximant, tolerance, large-set, integral, replay, and naming rows needed by later measure-analysis packets; it is not a host regularity theorem, selected null-set completion, quotient almost-everywhere equality, or countable-choice construction.

\begin{definition}[Lusin approximation carrier]\label{def:lusin-approximation-carrier}
A $\LusinApproximationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(M,A,C,F,E,I,R,H,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the ambient finite $\MeasureUp$ row, exposing the measurable-set ledger and the finite measure budget against which the exceptional set is read.
\item $A$ is the finite large-set and exceptional-window row, carrying the measurable subset on which the approximation is certified and the displayed complement budget.
\item $C$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ approximant row, naming the continuous candidate consumed by the public approximation read.
\item $F$ is the measured function row, stored as finite measurable readback data rather than as an ambient almost-everywhere equivalence class.
\item $E$ is the error-tolerance row, recording the requested $\RealUp$ tolerance, the pointwise comparison window, and the bound used outside the exceptional set.
\item $I$ is the $\IntegralUp$ readback row, exposing the finite integral or size comparisons used when a consumer asks for quantitative approximation data.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal that compares the measured function, continuous approximant, tolerance, and integral-facing reads through the displayed finite route.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the measure, large-set, continuous, function, error, integral, Real, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $Q$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
M,A,C,F,E,I,R \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LusinApproximationUp$, $\MeasureUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\IntegralUp$, $\RealUp$, $\EgorovUp$, $\RadonNikodymUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LusinApproximationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,A,C,F,E,I,R,H,Q,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host Lusin theorem, completed measure algebras, selected null sets, quotient almost-everywhere equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Lusin approximation NameCert obligations]\label{thm:lusin-approximation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LusinApproximationUp$ carrier
$$
L=(M,A,C,F,E,I,R,H,Q,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LusinApproximationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite $\MeasureUp$ row $M$, the measured function row $F$, and the continuous approximant row $C$;
\item large-set and exceptional-window exposure through $A$, with the finite complement budget read in the same measure ledger as $M$;
\item tolerance and comparison exactness through $E$ and the $\RealUp$ seal $R$, requiring each public approximation read to display its error budget;
\item quantitative readback through $I$, requiring integral-facing or size-facing consumers to remain on the same finite route;
\item structural stability through $H,Q,P,N$, requiring componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming to preserve exactly the rows $M,A,C,F,E,I,R$.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation may be discharged by importing a host Lusin theorem, completing the measure algebra, choosing null representatives, quotienting by almost-everywhere equality, or appealing to countable choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:lusin-approximation-carrier}. The measure-facing rows are $M$ and $A$, the approximation-facing rows are $C,F,E,R$, and the quantitative readback row is $I$.

The large-set budget is visible before any approximation conclusion is named. This is the same finite exceptional-window discipline used by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-egorov-namecert}, but the public consumer here reads a continuous approximant row rather than a uniform convergence window.

The density and integral-facing dependency is also explicit. The route through $I$ and $R$ mirrors the eventwise readback discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-radon-nikodym-namecert}, while refusing completed measure algebras and quotient almost-everywhere classes.

Finally $H,Q,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only those displayed rows. Since the excluded host theorem, null-set selector, quotient, choice, and completeness coordinates are absent from the carrier, the five listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
