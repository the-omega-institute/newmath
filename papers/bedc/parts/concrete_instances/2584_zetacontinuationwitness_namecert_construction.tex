\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-zeta-continuation-witness-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ interface packages the zeta-continuation application of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeta-continuation} as a finite witness certificate. It is the BEDC-native handoff object between $\ZetaBasicUp$, $\ZetaContUp$, $\ZetaZerosUp$, and the RH-facing critical-strip chapters. The packet records the eta-continuation route, the pole ledger at $s=1$, the functional-equation route, the trivial-zero ledger, and the Gamma-horizon boundary without importing the RH predicate or a global zero-location conclusion.

\begin{definition}[ZetaContinuationWitness carrier]
\label{def:zeta-continuation-witness-carrier}
A $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,R,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the source $\ZetaBasicUp$ row on the half-plane $\Re(s)>1$;
\item $E$ is the Dirichlet eta route, including the alternating-series row, the overlap row with $B$, and the displayed denominator boundary for $1-2^{1-s}$;
\item $A$ is the analytic-continuation row from the eta route to the punctured positive half-plane;
\item $P$ is the pole ledger at $s=1$, recording the simple-pole row and residue-one readback;
\item $F$ is the functional-equation route, including the exponential, sine, reflected-zeta, and Gamma-factor rows;
\item $T$ is the trivial-zero ledger for negative even inputs, read through the sine-factor row and the nonzero remaining factors;
\item $G$ is the Gamma-horizon boundary row, recording that the Gamma factor is consumed through its displayed interface and not through a closed Gamma chapter;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $B,E,A,P,F,T,G$;
\item $C$ records the finite $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the source zeta row, eta route, pole ledger, functional-equation route, trivial-zero ledger, Gamma boundary, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $R$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ZetaBasicUp$, $\ZetaContUp$, $\AnalyticContUp$, $\HolomorphicUp$, $\ComplexUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no proof of RH, no non-trivial-zero locator, no selected branch outside the displayed continuation routes, and no hidden completion of the Gamma horizon.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationWitness NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:zeta-continuation-witness-carrier} consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $W$;
\item classifier stability for the $\ZetaBasicUp$ source row, eta route, analytic-continuation row, pole ledger, functional-equation route, trivial-zero ledger, and Gamma-horizon row;
\item analytic-continuation exactness, stating that all exported $\ZetaContUp$ reads factor through $B,E,A,F,H,C,R,N$;
\item pole and trivial-zero ledger exactness for $P$ and $T$;
\item Gamma-horizon non-escape, stating that every Gamma-dependent read reaches $G$ unless the read is already one of the displayed functional-equation factors.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite tuple in \autoref{def:zeta-continuation-witness-carrier}. The source row, eta route, analytic-continuation row, pole ledger, functional-equation route, trivial-zero ledger, Gamma boundary, transport routes, continuation routes, package row, and local naming row are explicit coordinates of the same $\BHist$ packet.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The $\ZetaBasicUp$ source row is transported through $H$, the eta and functional-equation routes are transported through their displayed overlap and factor rows, and the pole and trivial-zero ledgers are transported as ledger coordinates rather than as ambient analytic claims.

Analytic-continuation exactness follows because every exported $\ZetaContUp$ read is routed through $C$ after the source, eta, and functional-equation coordinates have been exposed. There is no additional continuation coordinate in $W$, so a consumer cannot bypass $B,E,A,F,H,C,R,N$ to obtain a separate zeta continuation.

The pole and trivial-zero clauses are exact ledger reads. The pole row $P$ records the simple-pole and residue-one slots at $s=1$, while $T$ records the negative-even zero slots produced by the sine factor together with the nonzero-factor side conditions.

Finally, the Gamma boundary is a non-escape row. If a consumer asks for a Gamma fact beyond the displayed functional-equation factor, the request reaches $G$ and remains a boundary entry, not a closed Gamma certificate and not a hidden route to RH.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationWitness continuation handoff]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-continuation-handoff}
Every $\ZetaZerosUp$ or critical-strip consumer of an accepted $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ carrier reads the continued zeta value through the displayed rows $B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,R,N$. In particular, the handoff exposes the $\ZetaContUp$ value surface, the pole boundary at $s=1$, the trivial-zero ledger, and the Gamma-horizon boundary, but it exports no RH conclusion and no zero-location classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the consumer route stored in $C$. If it asks for the basic zeta source, the eta continuation, the analytic-continuation row, or the functional-equation route, the requested coordinate is one of $B,E,A,F$ and is transported through $H$.

If the consumer asks about the pole at $s=1$ or a negative even input, the request lands in the ledger rows $P$ or $T$. These rows expose the relevant boundary or zero witness as ledger data attached to the same packet.

The remaining analytic escape route is Gamma-dependent. That route is absorbed by $G$ unless the requested factor is already one of the displayed functional-equation rows. Therefore the downstream consumer receives only the finite continuation witness packet and does not receive an RH proof, a non-trivial-zero locator, or a completed Gamma certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationWitness route-carrier admission]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-route-carrier-admission}
An accepted $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ packet admits a continuation route only when the route is carried by the displayed eta-continuation row $E$, pole ledger $P$, functional-equation row $F$, trivial-zero ledger $T$, Gamma-horizon boundary $G$, componentwise $\hsame$ transports $H$, $\Cont$ reads $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $R$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. No continuation route is admitted from an unrecorded branch, an RH predicate, a global zero-location classifier, or a completed Gamma certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $W=(B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,R,N)$. The eta-continuation coordinate $E$, the analytic-continuation coordinate $A$, and the functional-equation coordinate $F$ are the only route-bearing analytic rows.

The ledger rows $P$ and $T$ restrict the exceptional reads, while $G$ records the Gamma-horizon boundary. Transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve those displayed rows and do not add a branch.

Finally authenticate the route through $R$ and $N$. Since the carrier contains no RH coordinate, no non-trivial-zero locator, and no closed Gamma chapter, admission is exactly the displayed finite route carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationWitness classifier stability]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-classifier-stability}
The $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ classifier is stable under componentwise $\hsame$ and displayed $\Cont$ transport of the source zeta row, eta-continuation route, pole ledger, functional-equation route, trivial-zero ledger, Gamma-horizon boundary, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows. Transported packets keep the same finite witness surface and do not gain RH, global zero-location, or analytic-continuation data beyond the displayed witness packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare two accepted packets componentwise. The source row $B$, eta route $E$, analytic-continuation row $A$, pole ledger $P$, functional-equation row $F$, trivial-zero ledger $T$, and Gamma boundary $G$ are transported only along the corresponding rows of $H$.

The continuation reads in $C$ replay those transported rows in the same order. They preserve the route carrier and the exceptional ledgers because each read targets a displayed coordinate of the packet.

Package provenance $R$ and local naming $N$ then repack the transported surface. No classifier coordinate mentions RH, a non-trivial-zero locator, a global branch theorem, or a completed Gamma chapter, so stability remains finite-packet stability.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationWitness pole-ledger exactness]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-pole-ledger-exactness}
Every pole-facing read of an accepted $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ packet is exactly the displayed pole ledger $P$ together with the eta-continuation, functional-equation, Gamma-horizon, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows needed to authenticate that ledger. The read does not decide non-trivial zeros, does not prove RH, and does not extend analytic continuation beyond the witness packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the exceptional coordinate $P$ from the carrier. It records the simple-pole row and residue-one readback at $s=1$, so a pole-facing consumer has a named ledger rather than an ambient meromorphic theorem.

The surrounding rows $E,F,G,H,C,R,N$ authenticate the ledger. The eta and functional-equation routes place the pole row inside the witness packet, the Gamma boundary limits Gamma-dependent access, and the transport and continuation rows replay only displayed data.

The trivial-zero ledger $T$ remains separate. Therefore pole exactness does not locate non-trivial zeros, does not assert RH, and does not supply continuation data outside the finite certificate.
\end{proof}
