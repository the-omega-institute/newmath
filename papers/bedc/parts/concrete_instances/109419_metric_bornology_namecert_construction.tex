\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetricBornologyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metric-bornology-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki/Kelley-style bornology plus Bishop--Bridges metric boundedness, the $\MetricBornologyUp$ packet records the metric bornology generated by displayed center-radius bounded-set data. It sits between the bounded-subset carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedset-namecert}, the center-radius row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricball-namecert}, the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the radius comparison surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-alg-order-namecert}, the ordinary bounded-family closure of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bornology-namecert}, and the completion-facing specialization of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-bornology-namecert}. The packet turns metric ball containment and bounded subset evidence into a reusable bornology surface without introducing host suprema, compactness, selected bounds, quotient families, trichotomy, or ambient real completeness.

\begin{definition}[MetricBornology carrier]
\label{def:metric-bornology-carrier}
A $\MetricBornologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(X,S,c,r,B,U,E,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier whose distance and point-classifier rows are the only metric data available to the packet.
\item $S$ is the bounded-subset classifier row read through the $\BoundedSetUp$ packet.
\item $c$ is the displayed center or witness row for the metric bound.
\item $r$ is the radius row, read through $\RealUp$ and the ordered comparison surface of $\RealAlgOrderUp$.
\item $B$ is the $\MetricBallUp$ containment ledger connecting accepted membership reads of $S$ to the displayed ball with center $c$ and radius $r$.
\item $U$ is the finite-union bornology ledger, recording union requests only over previously accepted metric-bounded families.
\item $E$ is the hereditary bounded-family ledger, recording subfamily requests only through a displayed parent family already accepted by $S$ or $U$.
\item $D$ is the consumer handoff row for $\BornologyUp$ and $\CauchyBornologyUp$ readers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,S,c,r,B,U,E,D,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a bounded-family request through metric carrier, classifier, center, radius, ball containment, hereditary closure, finite union, handoff, provenance, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricBornologyUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\BoundedSetUp$, $\MetricBallUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RealAlgOrderUp$, $\BornologyUp$, $\CauchyBornologyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricBornologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier compares metric-bounded families only through these rows. It has no coordinate for a host supremum, selected bound, compactness proof, open-cover compactness, quotient family, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricBornology NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-bornology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetricBornologyUp$ carrier $M=(X,S,c,r,B,U,E,D,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricBornologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the metric row, bounded-subset classifier, center or witness row, radius row, metric-ball containment ledger, finite-union ledger, hereditary ledger, consumer handoff, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Every accepted bounded-family read must first expose the $\MetricSpaceUp$ row $X$, the $\BoundedSetUp$ classifier $S$, the center-radius data $c,r$, and the $\MetricBallUp$ ledger $B$. Hereditary and finite-union bornology reads may then consume only $E$ and $U$, and downstream $\BornologyUp$ or $\CauchyBornologyUp$ consumers may read the packet only through $D,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metric-bornology-carrier}. The metric rows are $X,S,c,r,B$, and the bornology rows are $U,E,D$; the remaining rows only transport, replay, source, and name the same packet.

Carrier admission and boundedness are forced by the sibling routes. The bounded-subset packet supplies $S,c,r,B$, while the metric-ball packet supplies the center-radius comparison over the same metric row $X$ and radius row $r$.

Hereditary and finite-union reads are then confined to $E$ and $U$. Since neither row stores a host powerset, selected bound, supremum, compactness proof, quotient family, or choice schedule, the local NameCert obligations are exactly the displayed metric-bounded-family rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricBornology bounded-set radius handoff]
\label{thm:metric-bornology-bounded-set-radius-handoff}
Let $M=(X,S,c,r,B,U,E,D,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\MetricBornologyUp$ carrier. Every $\BoundedSetUp$ or $\MetricBallUp$ read exported from $M$ follows the route
$$
X,S,c,r,B \longmapsto U,E \longmapsto D,H,C,P,N .
$$
The radius row $r$ is read only through $\RealUp$ and $\RealAlgOrderUp$, the containment row $B$ is read only as a $\MetricBallUp$ ledger, and the resulting bounded-family surface is handed to $\BornologyUp$ and $\CauchyBornologyUp$ consumers before hereditary or finite-union bornology closure can be used downstream.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a bounded-set request. By \autoref{def:metric-bornology-carrier}, it must name the metric carrier $X$, the bounded-subset classifier $S$, the center or witness $c$, the radius row $r$, and the metric-ball containment ledger $B$.

Use the radius anchors next. The row $r$ is available only through the $\RealUp$ and $\RealAlgOrderUp$ comparison surfaces, and $B$ is available only through the $\MetricBallUp$ center-radius ledger. Thus the request cannot replace the displayed radius by a selected supremum or by a compactness argument.

Finally pass to the bornology rows. The hereditary row $E$ and finite-union row $U$ consume the already displayed metric-bounded families, while $D,H,C,P,N$ export the same surface to $\BornologyUp$ and $\CauchyBornologyUp$ readers. No downstream consumer receives a host family, quotient family, selected bound, trichotomy proof, or ambient real completeness datum.
\end{proof}
