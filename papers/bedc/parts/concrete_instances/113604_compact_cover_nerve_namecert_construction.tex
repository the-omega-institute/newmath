\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompactCoverNerveUp$}

Following Bishop--Bridges compactness and the finite nerve construction, the $\CompactCoverNerveUp$ packet records the finite cover-member and overlap ledger behind the compact-uniform-continuity proof route of \autoref{ch:visions-compact-uniform-continuity}. It reads compact metric finite-net data from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, continuous-map source rows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, rational lower-bound folds from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-lower-bound-fold-namecert}, finite-net minimum folds from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitenetminimumfold-namecert}, and the downstream tolerance-only consumer from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}. The packet names the finite nerve object and its rational-fold handoff; it does not assert open-cover compactness, an external Lebesgue number selector, or host quotient equality of metric points.

\begin{definition}[Compact cover nerve carrier]
\label{def:compact-cover-nerve-carrier}
A $\CompactCoverNerveUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
C=(K,U,O,R,F,H,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ finite-net source row, including the compact metric carrier, finite probe spine, and located coverage witnesses.
\item $U$ is the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$-indexed cover-member ledger assigning each displayed probe to the compact source row and the local continuity radius it carries.
\item $O$ is the overlap and nerve-incidence row recording which displayed cover members have certified nonempty overlap or certified adjacency in the finite nerve.
\item $R$ is the $\RatUp$ lower-bound readback row for the positive radius data attached to the cover members and overlap witnesses.
\item $F$ is the $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp/\UniformModulusUp$ handoff row, recording the finite fold that exports a tolerance-only uniform-radius ledger.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact source, cover-member ledger, overlap incidence, rational readback, fold handoff, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactCoverNerveUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\RatUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactCoverNerveUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no external open-cover choice, no host nerve complex, no quotient equality of metric points, no arbitrary Lebesgue number, and no ambient compactness theorem beyond the displayed compact metric source row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact cover nerve NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-cover-nerve-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactCoverNerveUp$ carrier $C=(K,U,O,R,F,H,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactCoverNerveUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed rows; compact-source compatibility for $K,U$; finite cover-member admission for the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledger $U$; overlap-incidence exactness for $O$; rational lower-bound readback for $R$; uniform-modulus handoff exactness for $F$; transport stability through $H$; provenance and local naming through $P,N$; and ledger refusal for external open-cover choice, host nerve complexes, quotient equality of points, arbitrary Lebesgue numbers, and ambient compactness theorems.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:compact-cover-nerve-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes only $K,U,O,R,F,H,P,N$, so every local obligation must be read from these rows.

Compact-source compatibility and cover-member admission are the route through $K$ and $U$. The compact metric row fixes the finite probe spine, and the cover-member ledger attaches each cover row to that same displayed source.

Overlap-incidence exactness is the route through $O$. The finite nerve records only displayed overlap or adjacency evidence among members of $U$, so it cannot introduce a host nerve complex detached from the packet.

The rational fold and uniform-modulus handoff are the route through $R$ and $F$. The lower-bound readback consumes the displayed radius data, and the fold handoff exports only the tolerance-only radius row available to $\UniformModulusUp$ consumers.

Transport, provenance, and naming through $H,P,N$ preserve the same coordinates. Since no row stores external cover choice, quotient point equality, arbitrary Lebesgue-number selection, or ambient compactness, the listed obligations exhaust the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact cover nerve uniform-modulus handoff]
\label{thm:compact-cover-nerve-uniform-modulus-handoff}
Every $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer row exported by an accepted $\CompactCoverNerveUp$ carrier factors through the displayed finite route
$$
K,U,O,R,F \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
The consumer receives compact metric finite-net data, cover-member rows, overlap incidence, rational lower-bound readback, and finite-fold handoff rows, but it receives no external open cover, no host nerve complex, no quotient equality of metric points, no arbitrary Lebesgue number, and no ambient compactness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a uniform-modulus consumer of the fold handoff row $F$. Carrier admission makes $F$ downstream of the compact source $K$, the cover-member ledger $U$, the overlap-incidence row $O$, and the rational readback row $R$.

Replay the finite route. The compact source supplies the finite probe spine, $U$ attaches cover members and local radii to that spine, $O$ records the finite incidence data, and $R$ reads back the positive rational lower-bound data before $F$ exports the uniform-radius handoff.

The structural rows do not widen the export surface. Transport through $H$ preserves the same finite coordinates, while $P,N$ source and name the packet. Thus every downstream $\UniformModulusUp$ read remains on the displayed cover-nerve route and cannot recover any excluded host object or selector.
\end{proof}
