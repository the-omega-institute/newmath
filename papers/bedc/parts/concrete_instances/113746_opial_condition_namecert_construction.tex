\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\OpialConditionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-opial-condition-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Opial's fixed-point tradition, the $\OpialConditionUp$ packet records the finite certificate surface by which a bounded sequence window, a candidate weak-limit row, and a limsup-distance comparison are made available to nonexpansive fixed-point consumers without importing weak compactness, selected weak limits, or countable choice. It depends on the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the Banach carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-banachspace-namecert}, the regular Cauchy nonexpansive transport route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-nonexpansive-map-namecert}, and the L10 sequence/readback route represented by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-sequence-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-limit-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Opial condition carrier]
\label{def:opial-condition-carrier}
An $\OpialConditionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(M,B,S,W,L,D,F,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the source $\MetricSpaceUp$ row on which distance readings are measured.
\item $B$ is the ambient $\BanachSpaceUp$ row, exposing only the linear and norm data needed to read the same metric distances through the Banach carrier.
\item $S$ is the bounded sequence row, displayed through the L10 sequence/readback route rather than through an infinite choice object.
\item $W$ is the finite bounded-sequence window opened around the requested tail and tolerance.
\item $L$ is the candidate limit row. It is a named point read against $M$ and $B$, not a selected weak limit supplied by a compactness principle.
\item $D$ is the displayed limsup-distance comparison ledger
$$
\limsup_n d(S_n,L) < \limsup_n d(S_n,Y)
$$
for each displayed competing point row $Y$ whose carrier classifier is distinct from $L$.
\item $F$ is the nonexpansive-map handoff row. It records that a $\RegularCauchyNonexpansiveMapUp$ consumer may read $S$, $W$, $L$, and $D$ when checking the fixed-point boundary for a displayed map, but may not manufacture a limit or a compactness witness.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ readback row for the distance values used in $D$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric row, Banach row, sequence row, finite window, candidate limit, limsup ledger, nonexpansive handoff, readback rows, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each finite fixed-point request through
$$
M,B,S,W,L,D,F,R \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\OpialConditionUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\BanachSpaceUp$, $\RegularCauchyNonexpansiveMapUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\OpialConditionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for weak compactness, a selected weak cluster point, quotient stream equality, excluded middle, countable choice, a demiclosedness theorem, or an ambient fixed-point theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Opial condition NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:opial-condition-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\OpialConditionUp$ carrier
$$
O=(M,B,S,W,L,D,F,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\OpialConditionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the metric row, Banach row, bounded sequence row, finite window, candidate limit, limsup-distance ledger, nonexpansive handoff, rational and Real readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item sequence exposure, requiring every limsup-distance read to begin with the displayed L10 sequence/readback route and the finite bounded window $W$;
\item candidate discipline, requiring $L$ to be a displayed point row and forbidding replacement of $L$ by a selected weak cluster point;
\item limsup comparison exactness, requiring $D$ to display the strict comparison between the distance tail at $L$ and each displayed competing row $Y$ distinct from $L$;
\item nonexpansive handoff, requiring $F$ to export only the rows needed by a $\RegularCauchyNonexpansiveMapUp$ fixed-point-boundary consumer;
\item provenance and local naming, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed Opial route.
\end{enumerate}
No $\OpialConditionUp$ obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:opial-condition-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the metric, Banach, sequence, finite-window, candidate-limit, comparison, handoff, readback, transport, replay, provenance, and naming coordinates.

The sequence and candidate obligations are forced by $S,W,L$. The sequence is opened only through the finite L10 readback route, and the limit candidate is a displayed point row, so the certificate has no position from which to extract a selected weak cluster point.

The comparison obligation is the role of $D$. Each accepted competing row $Y$ is first displayed as distinct from $L$, and then the limsup-distance inequality is read through the rational and Real readback row $R$.

Finally $F,H,C,P,N$ close the route. The nonexpansive handoff exports the visible rows to fixed-point-boundary consumers, while the structural rows preserve only the displayed coordinates and leave no carrier position for weak compactness, demiclosedness, countable choice, or an ambient fixed-point theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Opial condition nonexpansive handoff]
\label{thm:opial-condition-nonexpansive-handoff}
Every $\RegularCauchyNonexpansiveMapUp$-facing consumer read accepted by an $\OpialConditionUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\MetricSpaceUp,\ \BanachSpaceUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp,\ \RegularCauchyNonexpansiveMapUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
Equivalently, an accepted fixed-point-boundary read may consume the displayed bounded sequence window, candidate limit row, limsup-distance comparison, and nonexpansive handoff, but it cannot bypass them by weak compactness, a selected weak limit, demiclosedness, countable choice, or an ambient fixed-point theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the obligation theorem \autoref{thm:opial-condition-namecert-obligations}. It exposes a nonexpansive handoff only after the metric and Banach rows, the bounded sequence row, the finite window, the candidate limit row, the limsup-distance comparison ledger, and the rational and Real readback row have all been displayed.

The displayed $\Cont$ route then replays each fixed-point-boundary request through the same rows. Thus a nonexpansive-map consumer reads the Opial comparison as finite certificate data rather than as a compactness principle.

Inspecting the carrier definition gives the exclusion. There is no coordinate for weak compactness, selected weak limits, demiclosedness, countable choice, or a detached fixed-point theorem, so any consumer requiring one of those objects is outside the local classifier.
\end{proof}
