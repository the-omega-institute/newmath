Following the Euclidean plane and product-space tradition, the $\RealPlaneUp$ packet names the finite product surface obtained from two displayed $\RealUp$ coordinates rather than from a host ordered pair. It reads the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the product constructor of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prod-namecert}, the metric interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. The packet supplies a human-origin L10 plane object for later one-sphere, metric-plane, and Euclidean constructions while keeping quotient real equality, selected limits, ambient completeness, open-cover compactness, and bridge credentials outside the local carrier.

\begin{definition}[RealPlane carrier]
\label{def:real-plane-carrier}
A $\RealPlaneUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(X,Y,F,M,S,R,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the first $\RealUp$ coordinate seal row, exposing only the named Real observations admitted by the local plane request.
\item $Y$ is the second $\RealUp$ coordinate seal row, disjoint from $X$ except through the shared product, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $F$ is the $\ProdUp$ provenance row, recording that the displayed plane point is read as the finite product of the two Real-coordinate seals.
\item $M$ is the optional $\MetricUp$ distance-witness row, available only as a displayed metric read over the same two coordinate seals and not as a host Euclidean metric.
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation row used to inspect coordinate windows before either coordinate is sealed as a Real read.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the inspected stream windows for the two coordinates.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two coordinate seals, product provenance, metric witness when present, finite observations, regular readback, replay, package provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a plane request through the corresponding Real-coordinate seal rows, product row, metric row when present, observation row, readback row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealPlaneUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ProdUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealPlaneUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,F,M,S,R,H,C,Q,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient real equality, selected limits, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, open-cover compactness, host ordered pairs, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealPlane NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-plane-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealPlaneUp$ carrier $P=(X,Y,F,M,S,R,H,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealPlaneUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the displayed first Real-coordinate seal, second Real-coordinate seal, product provenance, metric-distance witness when present, finite StreamName observations, RegSeqRat readback, transport, replay, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item componentwise classifier stability, requiring any identified packet to preserve $X$ and $Y$ separately before the product row $F$ can classify the pair;
\item projection stability, requiring the first coordinate projection to read only $X$ together with $F,H,C,Q,N$, and the second coordinate projection to read only $Y$ together with $F,H,C,Q,N$;
\item product provenance, requiring every plane read to pass through the finite $\ProdUp$ row $F$ after the two Real-coordinate seals have been displayed;
\item metric locality, requiring any distance read to use $M$ only after the same two coordinate seals and product row have been exposed;
\item ledger nonescape, requiring $H,C,Q,N$ to replay, source, and name no row from which quotient real equality, selected limits, ambient Real completeness, open-cover compactness, host ordered pairs, or bridge credentials can be exported.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:real-plane-carrier}. The tuple displays the two Real-coordinate seals, product provenance, optional metric witness, finite observations, regular readback, and structural rows.

Componentwise classifier stability is forced before product classification. The row $H$ transports $X$ and $Y$ separately, and only after those two transported coordinate seals are fixed does the product row $F$ identify the displayed plane packet.

Projection stability follows from the row discipline. The first projection can inspect $X$ and the shared product, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows, while the second projection can inspect $Y$ and the same shared structural rows. Neither projection receives the other coordinate as an independent source.

The product and metric obligations are downstream. Every plane read passes through $F$ as the finite product provenance row, and any distance read then uses $M$ over that same displayed coordinate pair rather than a host Euclidean carrier.

Finally, $H,C,Q,N$ close the packet without adding analytic content. Since the excluded objects are absent from the carrier, the local ledger cannot export quotient real equality, selected limits, ambient Real completeness, open-cover compactness, host ordered pairs, or bridge credentials.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealPlane coordinate projection stability]
\label{thm:real-plane-coordinate-projection-stability}
Let $P=(X,Y,F,M,S,R,H,C,Q,N)$ be an accepted $\RealPlaneUp$ carrier. The first coordinate projection factors only through
$$
X\longmapsto F,H,C,Q,N
$$
and the second coordinate projection factors only through
$$
Y\longmapsto F,H,C,Q,N .
$$
In particular, each coordinate projection reads the corresponding $\RealUp$ seal plus the shared product, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows, and it exports no quotient real equality, selected limit, ambient Real completeness, open-cover compactness, host ordered pair, or bridge credential.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only coordinate seal available to the first projection is $X$, and the only coordinate seal available to the second projection is $Y$.

Both projections share the structural route. The product row $F$ records the finite product source, $H$ transports the displayed rows componentwise, $C$ replays the same request order, and $Q,N$ package and name the local certificate.

The rows $M,S,R$ are not additional coordinate sources. They give optional metric evidence and finite observation readback already attached to the displayed coordinate seals.

Thus each coordinate projection is exhausted by its own Real seal and the shared product, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows. Since the carrier has no excluded coordinate, neither projection can export quotient real equality, selected limits, ambient Real completeness, open-cover compactness, host ordered pairs, or bridge credentials.
\end{proof}
