\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteChoiceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finitechoice-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis and Troelstra--van Dalen on finite choice, the $\FiniteChoiceUp$ packet records finite index selection over displayed $\ListUp$, $\OptionUp$, and $\BoolUp$ rows and over the L10 $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ windows. It sits beside the countable-choice refusal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-countable-choice-boundary-namecert}, the finite stream-window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet gives Real and Completion arguments a visible finite selector boundary while refusing countable-choice selectors.

\begin{definition}[FiniteChoice carrier]
\label{def:finite-choice-carrier}
A $\FiniteChoiceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(I,O,B,L,W,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the finite index-list row. It records a displayed $\ListUp$ of inspected indices together with the order in which the packet reads them.
\item $O$ is the $\OptionUp$ decidability row, carrying for each displayed index either a selected row or an explicit absence token.
\item $B$ is the $\BoolUp$ decisional ledger attached to $O$, recording the boolean case used for each finite option inspection.
\item $L$ is the selected-row ledger. It contains only the rows selected from the displayed finite list and records no selector outside $I$.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row opened for the selected finite indices.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the selected windows in $W$.
\item $S$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, exposed only after $I,O,B,L,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the index list, option and boolean rows, selected ledger, stream windows, regular readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the finite index-list row through $O,B,L,W,R,S,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteChoiceUp$, $\ListUp$, $\OptionUp$, $\BoolUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteChoiceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these eleven rows. It has no coordinate for countable choice, selected infinite branches, quotient stream equality, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteChoice NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-choice-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteChoiceUp$ carrier
$$
F=(I,O,B,L,W,R,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteChoiceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed index-list row, option decidability row, boolean decision ledger, selected-row ledger, StreamName window row, RegSeqRat readback row, Real seal row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item finite index coverage, requiring every selected row to appear in the displayed $\ListUp$ row $I$;
\item option and boolean case exactness, requiring $O$ and $B$ to account for each selected or absent finite case before the selected-row ledger $L$ is read;
\item window selection, requiring each stream window in $W$ to be opened only for an index accepted by the finite selected-row ledger;
\item rational readback, requiring $R$ to consume the displayed windows in $W$ rather than a detached stream quotient or selected limit;
\item $\RealUp$ seal non-escape, requiring $S$ to remain downstream of $I,O,B,L,W,R$ and to add no countable selector, infinite branch, quotient stream equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness principle, Lean verification, or bridge data;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No local finite-choice obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-choice-carrier}. The visible choice data are exactly the finite index list $I$, the option row $O$, the boolean decision ledger $B$, and the selected-row ledger $L$.

Finite index coverage follows by case analysis over the displayed $\ListUp$ row. In the empty-list case, no selected row can be exported. In the cons case, the head index is inspected by $O$ and $B$, and the tail is handled by the same finite projection.

Option and boolean exactness is local to $O$ and $B$. The present case contributes its selected row to $L$, while the absent case contributes only the explicit absence token. The mixed cases are exhausted because the boolean ledger records one displayed decision for each option row.

The analytic handoff then follows the selected rows. Each accepted selected row opens a finite $\StreamNameUp$ window in $W$, and every regular rational readback in $R$ consumes one of those windows.

Finally, $S$ is terminal and $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the same displayed route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for the excluded objects, the listed rows exhaust the local $\NameCert$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteChoice window selection boundary]
\label{thm:finite-choice-window-selection-boundary}
Let $F=(I,O,B,L,W,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\FiniteChoiceUp$ carrier. Every $\StreamNameUp$ window exported by $F$ is selected by the finite route
$$
I \longmapsto O \longmapsto B \longmapsto L \longmapsto W .
$$
Consequently a window consumer may inspect selected finite windows and their option/boolean decisions, but it receives no countable selector, selected infinite branch, detached quotient stream, or ambient real-completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an exported stream window. By carrier projection, the only window coordinate is $W$.

The row $W$ is not free-standing. It is downstream of the finite index-list row $I$, the option row $O$, the boolean ledger $B$, and the selected-row ledger $L$.

Now split by the displayed option cases. A present option contributes a selected finite row to $L$ and therefore may open the corresponding window in $W$. An absent option contributes no selected row, so no window can be exported from that index.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name this finite route without adding a second selector. Thus every exported window is selected by the displayed finite route, and all countable or completion-level choice data remain outside the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteChoice Real-completion handoff]
\label{thm:finite-choice-real-completion-handoff}
Every L10 Real or Completion consumer of an accepted $\FiniteChoiceUp$ carrier
$$
F=(I,O,B,L,W,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
must read the finite route
$$
\ListUp \longmapsto \OptionUp \longmapsto \BoolUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp
$$
as displayed by $I,O,B,L,W,R,S$. The handoff permits finite selection for the displayed windows and readbacks only; it does not supply countable choice, selected infinite branches, quotient stream equality, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and read the Real-facing coordinate. The only Real seal row is $S$, and it is exposed only after the finite index, option, boolean, selected-row, stream-window, and regular-readback rows have been displayed.

Apply the window boundary of \autoref{thm:finite-choice-window-selection-boundary}. Every window consumed by the Real or Completion route has already passed through the finite $\ListUp$, $\OptionUp$, $\BoolUp$, and selected-ledger rows.

The readback row $R$ then supplies the regular rational data for those windows, and the terminal seal $S$ records the $\RealUp$ handoff. This route is finite because its source is the displayed list $I$ and its case analysis is exhausted by the option and boolean rows.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since no excluded coordinate is present, a Real or Completion consumer receives finite selection data only through $I,O,B,L,W,R,S$ and receives none of the stronger choice or completeness principles.
\end{proof}
