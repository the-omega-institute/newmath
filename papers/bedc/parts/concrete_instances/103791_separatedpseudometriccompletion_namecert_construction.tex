$$

\item $Z$ is the displayed zero-distance row. It records the finite evidence consumed by the separated classifier and does not identify point rows by host equality.
\item $Q$ is the $\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp$ classifier output. It is the only row that turns the zero-distance evidence in $Z$ into separated metric point-classifier evidence.

It has no coordinate for quotient primitives, host equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedPseudometricCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:separated-pseudometric-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SeparatedPseudometricCompletionUp$ carrier $A=(P,Z,Q,M,S,R,D,E,H,C,K,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SeparatedPseudometricCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source $\PseudometricUp$ packet, zero-distance row, $\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp$ classifier output, $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item zero-distance separation, requiring every point-identity read from $Z$ to pass through the separated classifier output $Q$ before a metric or completion consumer may use it;
\item completion handoff, requiring $M$ to consume the separated metric surface from $Q$ rather than the pre-separated pseudometric row $P$ directly;
\item numerical readback ordering, requiring $S,R,D,E$ to be displayed in order before any completion-facing real-distance seal is exported;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the same route under componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay;
\item provenance exactness, requiring $K$ and $N$ to name exactly the displayed BEDC dependencies and the local certificate row;
\item non-escape, excluding quotient primitives, host equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No separated pseudometric completion NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:separated-pseudometric-completion-carrier}. The tuple lists the source pseudometric packet, zero-distance evidence, separated classifier output, completion handoff, finite windows, regular rational readback, dyadic ledger, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Zero-distance separation follows from the order $P,Z,Q$. The source pseudometric row exposes zero-distance evidence, the row $Z$ records the displayed evidence, and the row $Q$ is the first coordinate that can export separated point-classifier evidence.

The completion handoff is then forced through $M$. Since $M$ is downstream of $Q$, a completion-facing consumer cannot read point identity directly from the pre-separated pseudometric row.

Numerical readback is the displayed route $S,R,D,E$. Finite windows are opened first, regular rational data and dyadic comparisons are read next, and the terminal real seal is reached only after those rows have been displayed.

Transport and replay stability are supplied by $H$ and $C$. They move the listed rows componentwise and replay the same finite route for consumers, so the certificate remains inside the displayed dependency surface.

The provenance and non-escape clauses are inventory checks on $K,N$ and on the fields of $A$. None of the excluded principles occurs among the carrier coordinates, hence none can be part of the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedPseudometricCompletion completion route]
\label{thm:separated-pseudometric-completion-completion-route}
Let $A=(P,Z,Q,M,S,R,D,E,H,C,K,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparatedPseudometricCompletionUp$ packet. Every completion-facing consumer factors through the finite route
$$
\PseudometricUp\to Z\to\SeparatedQuotientMetricUp\to\MetricCompletionUp\to\StreamNameUp\to\RegSeqRatUp\to\DyadicRatCoreUp\to\RealUp .
$$
Thus the consumer receives a separated metric completion handoff only after the zero-distance evidence has passed through the separated classifier and the numerical readback rows have been displayed. The packet exports no direct route from pre-separated pseudometric evidence to completion equality, host equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an accepted packet $A$. The source row $P$ supplies pre-separated distance evidence, and the row $Z$ records the zero-distance boundary as displayed data.

The replay row $C$ sends that evidence through $Q$ before the completion handoff $M$ can be read. This makes the separated classifier output the only route from zero distance to the metric point surface consumed by completion.

After $M$, a completion-facing numerical request reads the windows $S$, the regular rational row $R$, the dyadic ledger $D$, and finally the real seal $E$. The transport row $H$ and the provenance rows $K,N$ name the same displayed chain.

The excluded objects are absent from the carrier. There is no quotient primitive, host equality field, selected representative, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy row, open-cover compactness row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row, so no consumer can obtain one from the packet.
\end{proof}
