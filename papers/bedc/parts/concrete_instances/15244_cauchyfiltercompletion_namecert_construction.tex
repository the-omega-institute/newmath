\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bishop--Bridges, the $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ packet records the finite completion boundary generated by Cauchy filters before any completed Real seal is exposed. It sits between the saturated filter surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the limit-facing packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilterlimit-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the Real seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the uniform completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}. The packet exposes a finite completion route, not a quotient filter, selected limit, host equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[CauchyFilterCompletion carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-filter-completion-carrier}
A $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ row naming the saturated Cauchy-filter data consumed by the completion request.
\item $W$ is the finite family of $\StreamNameUp$ windows through which the request observes the filter at displayed precision levels.
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to those windows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row extracted from the same windows and dyadic tolerances.
\item $L$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal handoff reached only after $C,W,T,R$ have been replayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the filter, window, tolerance, readback, seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Cauchy-filter request through finite windows, dyadic tolerance, regular readback, and the Real seal handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyFilterCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for quotient filters, selected limits, host real equality, countable-choice selection, ambient completeness, or a separate uniform-completion object beyond the displayed provenance row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilterCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ carrier $F=(C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyFilterCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of these obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed Cauchy filter, finite windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular readback, Real seal handoff, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming row;
\item filter-to-window exposure, requiring $C$ to be consumed before any completion-facing read inspects $W,T,R$, or $L$;
\item tolerance exactness, requiring every accepted readback in $R$ to use the displayed dyadic ledger $T$ for the same window row $W$;
\item completion handoff, requiring the Real seal row $L$ to remain downstream of $C,W,T,R$ and the structural rows $H,K,P,N$;
\item ledger refusal, excluding quotient filters, selected limits, host real equality, countable-choice selectors, ambient completeness, and detached uniform-completion coordinates.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-filter-completion-carrier}. The public rows are exactly $C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N$, so carrier admission exposes the Cauchy-filter row, the finite observation route, the Real seal handoff, and the structural rows without adding quotient, selector, host-equality, or completeness data.

The filter-to-window and tolerance obligations come from the displayed continuation row $K$. A completion-facing request must first consume $C$, then inspect the finite windows $W$, then read the dyadic ledger $T$ before the regular readback row $R$ is available.

The completion handoff is the remaining public route. The row $L$ is downstream of $C,W,T,R$, while $H$, $P$, and $N$ transport, package, and name only that route. Thus every excluded object lies outside the accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilterCompletion Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-real-seal-handoff}
Let $F=(C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ packet. Every completion-facing or Real-facing consumer that reads the terminal seal factors through the finite route
$$
C,W,T,R \longmapsto L \longmapsto H,K,P,N .
$$
Thus the $\RealUp$ seal handoff is available only after the $\CauchyFilterUp$ row, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback have been displayed. The same route is compatible with the $\CauchyFilterLimitUp$ boundary and the $\UniformCompletionUp$ provenance surface, but it exports no quotient filter, selected limit, host real equality, countable-choice selector, ambient completeness theorem, or detached uniform-completion coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a consumer of the terminal seal. Carrier admission gives access to $L$ only as a row of the accepted packet $F$.

Replay the continuation row $K$. The request reads the saturated Cauchy-filter row $C$, the finite windows $W$, the dyadic tolerance ledger $T$, and the regular rational readback $R$ before the Real seal handoff can be consumed.

The structural rows preserve this order. Transport through $H$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ record the same route, so the consumer has no available coordinate for quotient filters, selected limits, host equality, choice, ambient completeness, or an independent uniform-completion object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilterCompletion located-uniform gluing]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-located-uniform-gluing}
Let $F=(C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ packet. Suppose a $\LocatedCauchyFilterUp$ real-seal read and a $\UniformCauchyFilterUp$ completion handoff are both consumed through this packet. Then the two reads have the same finite BEDC route inside $F$:
$$
C,W,T,R \longmapsto L \longmapsto H,K,P,N .
$$
The located read cannot reach the $\RealUp$ seal by any row other than the displayed $L$, and the uniform handoff cannot reach a completion-facing consumer by any row detached from the displayed $C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N$. Thus the joint $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ consumption glues the located-filter and uniform-filter surfaces through one packet, not through quotient filters, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, or a separate completion object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-filter-completion-carrier}. It displays only the rows $C,W,T,R,L,H,K,P,N$, so both imported reads must be interpreted through those coordinates once they are consumed as $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ data.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-namecert-obligations} to fix the local obligation surface. Filter-to-window exposure and tolerance exactness force any completion-facing read to consume $C$, then the window and tolerance rows $W,T$, before the regular readback row $R$ is available.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-filter-completion-real-seal-handoff}. The terminal seal read is downstream of $C,W,T,R$ and upstream of the structural rows $H,K,P,N$, so the packet exposes exactly the displayed route.

The imported boundary theorems match this route. The $\LocatedCauchyFilterUp$ real-seal read is constrained by \autoref{thm:located-cauchy-filter-real-seal-boundary}, while the $\UniformCauchyFilterUp$ completion handoff is constrained by \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-filter-completion-handoff}. Since both are consumed through the same accepted packet $F$, their public exits coincide at $L$ and then pass through the same transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

No excluded object can mediate the gluing. Quotient filters, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, and detached completion objects are absent from the carrier and refused by the NameCert obligations, so there is no second route available inside the packet.
\end{proof}
