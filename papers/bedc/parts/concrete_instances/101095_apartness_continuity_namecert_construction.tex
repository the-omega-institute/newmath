\begin{theorem}[Apartness continuity NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:apartness-continuity-namecert-obligations}

$$
A=(G,L,R,M,S,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N),

the local $\NameCert_{\ApartnessContinuityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the continuous-map graph, located apartness row, real apartness row, modulus-continuity row, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore ledger, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item apartness-source stability, requiring $L$ and $R$ to expose positive separation only over the same displayed source and target cells opened by $G$;
\item modulus-to-target-separation handoff, requiring $M$ and $D$ to turn every target-separation request into the finite source budget consumed by $S,Q$ before $R$ or $E$ can be read;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to carry only the displayed rows $G,L,R,M,S,Q,D,E,P,N$ through componentwise $\hsame$ and displayed $\Cont$ routes;

\end{enumerate}
No local apartness-continuity obligation is stored outside these rows.

The handoff from modulus to target separation is carried by $M,S,Q,D$. A target-separation request first reads the modulus row $M$, then opens the StreamName windows $S$, the regular readback $Q$, and the dyadic ledger $D$ before the real apartness row $R$ or the terminal seal $E$ is visible.

Transport and replay stability follow from $H$ and $C$. These rows move the displayed packet componentwise and replay the same finite route; they introduce no independent equality, limit, choice, completeness, or semantic-continuity coordinate.

Finally, ledger non-escape is the exhaustion clause for $D,P,N$. The dyadic ledger, provenance, and local NameCert rows name only the displayed route, so the excluded nonconstructive principles cannot become local obligations.
