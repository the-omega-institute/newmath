\chapter{Regular Cauchy Limit Classifier NameCert Construction}

The $\RegularCauchyLimitClassifierUp$ interface is the finite classifier handoff between the diagonal-limit construction of \autoref{def:real-completeness-diagonal-limit} and the limit-existence target of \autoref{thm:real-completeness-limit-exists-unique}. It packages the selected $\RegSeqRatUp$ diagonal extraction, the $\StreamNameUp$ observation windows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rational ledgers, and the $\RealUp$ seal row through which a completion consumer sees the candidate limit. The packet is a classifier surface for the limit handoff; it is not an ambient completeness principle and it contains no host selected real.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy limit classifier carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-limit-classifier-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyLimitClassifierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(X,\mu,\Delta,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular Cauchy input family and $\mu$ is its explicit modulus row;
\item $\Delta$ is the diagonal extraction row assigning to each unary precision request the selected threshold and diagonal read demanded by \autoref{def:real-completeness-diagonal-limit};
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family that realizes the selected reads of the regular rational names;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the rational observations obtained through $W$;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger recording endpoints, radii, comparison rows, and precision rows for those observations;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal handoff row exposing the same diagonal observations as the candidate real-name surface;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the input family, modulus row, diagonal extraction, windows, readbacks, ledger, and seal row;
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ routes for every consumer read that reaches those rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyLimitClassifierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a host real value, a non-displayed limit selector, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy limit classifier stability]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-limit-classifier-stability}
The local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyLimitClassifierUp}$ obligation surface consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger exactness: every exported rational observation is the endpoint, radius, comparison, and precision row recorded by $D$;
\item $\StreamNameUp$ window exactness: each diagonal read uses the finite window named by $W$ and no unrecorded stream access;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ extraction stability: transporting $X$, $\mu$, and $\Delta$ through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows transports the selected regular-sequence readback $R$;
\item $\RealUp$ seal discipline: the seal row $E$ is reached only after the displayed window, readback, and dyadic ledger rows have been produced;
\item package and name closure: $P$ and $N$ record exactly the provenance and local classifier row consumed by downstream Real and Completion certificates.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently classifier transport preserves the finite diagonal-limit handoff from source packets to target packets without adding quotient representatives, hidden tail choices, host real equality tests, or ambient completeness assumptions.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RegularCauchyLimitClassifierUp.RegularCauchyLimitClassifierCarrier\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-limit-classifier-carrier}. The rows $X$, $\mu$, and $\Delta$ determine which regular rational name is read at each precision request, while $W$ records the finite stream window that performs the read.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$ and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$ are coordinates of the same packet. Transport by the stored $\hsame$ rows in $H$ and consumer routes in $C$ therefore moves the selected readback together with its displayed endpoint, radius, comparison, and precision data.

The $\RealUp$ row $E$ consumes only the already displayed $W$, $R$, and $D$ rows. Repacking those rows with $P$ and $N$ gives the transported local classifier surface and leaves no coordinate through which a non-displayed limit selector or quotient equality can enter.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy limit Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-limit-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ classifier read exported by an accepted $\RegularCauchyLimitClassifierUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(X,\mu,\Delta,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
factors through the displayed diagonal extraction row $\Delta$, the $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, and the seal row $E$. This handoff is the finite NameCert surface by which the Real completeness target sees a candidate limit classifier; it exports no quotient stream equality, selected host limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RegularCauchyLimitClassifierUp.RegularCauchyLimitClassifierCarrier\_real\_seal\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the stability surface of \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-limit-classifier-stability}. It identifies the only classifier-visible diagonal observations as the rows produced by $\Delta$, $W$, $R$, and $D$.

The seal row $E$ is downstream of those observations. A consumer read into $\RealUp$ must therefore pass through the finite stream window and dyadic ledger before reaching the real-name handoff.

Finally, the carrier definition lists all coordinates of $Q$. Since none of them is a quotient representative, a host selected limit, a host real equality test, or an ambient completeness field, the exported classifier cannot supply any of those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy limit classifier diagonal seal uniqueness]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-limit-classifier-diagonal-seal-uniqueness}
Let $Q$ and $Q'$ be accepted $\RegularCauchyLimitClassifierUp$ packets. If they share the same diagonal extraction row, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger rows, and $\RealUp$ seal row up to the displayed $\hsame$ transports, then every public completion-facing classifier read factors through the same sealed diagonal-window route.

The conclusion is a uniqueness statement for the public readback route only. It does not assert quotient stream equality, select a host limit, identify host real values, or use ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply classifier stability from \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-limit-classifier-stability}. The shared diagonal extraction, stream-window, regular readback, dyadic ledger, and seal rows remain the stable coordinates visible to both packets.

Next use the Real seal handoff of \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-limit-real-seal-handoff}. It places each real-facing read downstream of the same diagonal extraction, finite windows, readback rows, dyadic ledgers, and seal row.

Now apply the diagonal-window public boundary and public export, \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-limit-classifier-diagonal-window-public-boundary} and \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-limit-classifier-public-export}. They force every completion-facing read through the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ route attached to those shared rows.

Finally use the bridge source packet of \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-limit-classifier-bridge-source-packet}. Since the bridge-facing packet has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a selected host limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness, the shared sealed route is the only public route available to both classifiers.
\end{proof}
