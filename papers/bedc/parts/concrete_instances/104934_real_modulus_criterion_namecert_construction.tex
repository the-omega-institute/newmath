It sits between the convergence schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-convergence-modulus-namecert}, the sequence-limit consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-limit-namecert}, the regular rational names of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Real modulus criterion carrier]
\label{def:real-modulus-criterion-carrier}
A $\RealModulusCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$ row naming the explicit tail schedule.
\item $S$ is the sequence-source row inspected by the modulus.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback exposed by the scheduled tails.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window family used to read each tail comparison.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the modulus requests.
\item $L$ is the $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ consumer row reached only after $M,S,Q,W,D$ have been displayed.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ convergence-readiness seal exported to real-name consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the analytic rows and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the tolerance request through the modulus, windows, regular readback, sequence-limit consumer, and real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealModulusCriterionUp$, $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$, $\RealSequenceLimitUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealModulusCriterionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice, trichotomy, host sequence convergence, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{proof}
