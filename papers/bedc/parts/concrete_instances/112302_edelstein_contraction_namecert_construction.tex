\label{def:edelstein-contraction-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\MetricUp$ row carrying the inspected point and distance classifier;
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ row exposing only the finite compact-metric inspection data used by the strict-decrease consumer;
\item $F$ is the self-map graph row over the same metric carrier;
\item $O$ is the $\PicardIterationUp$-style orbit row generated from the self-map graph and an initial displayed point;
\item $D$ is the strict distance-decrease row, recording that every displayed non-diagonal comparison in the orbit is sent to a strictly smaller displayed distance;

\begin{theorem}[EdelsteinContraction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:edelstein-contraction-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EdelsteinContractionUp$ carrier $E=(X,K,F,O,D,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\EdelsteinContractionUp}$ surface consists exactly of metric carrier admission through $X$, compact-metric finite inspection through $K$, self-map graph exposure through $F$, Picard-style orbit exposure through $O$, strict distance-decrease exposure through $D$, sequential-continuity transport through $S$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:edelstein-contraction-carrier}. The rows $X,K,F,O,D,S,H,C,P,N$ are the only rows available to the local certificate, so each admissible read must begin with the displayed metric, compact-metric, graph, orbit, strict-decrease, sequential-continuity, transport, replay, provenance, and naming coordinates.

The compactness service is read only through $K$, and the distance service is read only through $X$. The self-map and orbit service is therefore constrained to the same source carrier before the strict decrease row $D$ can be consumed.

Finally repack the rows through $H,C,P,N$. Componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming preserve exactly the listed rows and add no endpoint coordinate or host contraction principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EdelsteinContraction compact-metric route]
\label{thm:edelstein-contraction-compactmetric-route}

\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $E=(X,K,F,O,D,S,H,C,P,N)$. The metric and compact-metric rows $X$ and $K$ supply the only distance and compactness reads, so any endpoint-facing consumer first exposes the same source carrier and finite inspection surface.

Since no Banach ratio, selected endpoint, quotient completion representative, host choice operator, or ambient real-completeness row appears in the carrier, none is exported by the route.
\end{proof}
