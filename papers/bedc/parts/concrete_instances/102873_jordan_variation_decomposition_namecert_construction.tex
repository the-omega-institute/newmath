\begin{theorem}[Jordan variation decomposition NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:jordan-variation-decomposition-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\JordanVariationDecompositionUp$ carrier $J=(B,\Pi,X,D,V^{+},V^{-},S,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\JordanVariationDecompositionUp}$ surface consists of exactly five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed tuple $B,\Pi,X,D,V^{+},V^{-},S,R,H,C,P,N$;
\item finite positive-edge and negative-edge ledger formation from the dyadic observations in $D$;
\item signed reconstruction through $S$, returning the displayed endpoint-difference ledger on $\Pi$;
\item common-refinement transport through $R,H,C$ for the source bounded-variation packet, endpoint rows, dyadic observations, signed ledgers, and reconstruction row;
\item no-escape from variation measures, host suprema over all partitions, quotient function spaces, countable choice, hidden partition families, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
These rows are read through the finite route
$$
B,\Pi,X,D\longmapsto V^{+},V^{-}\longmapsto S\longmapsto R
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:jordan-variation-decomposition-carrier}. The bounded-variation source and displayed partition are present before any signed ledger is formed.

Finite signed ledger formation is edgewise. Each adjacent endpoint pair in $\Pi$ is read through $X$, its dyadic difference row is read through $D$, and that row is placed in exactly one of the positive-edge or negative-edge finite ledgers.

The reconstruction row $S$ then forgets the sign split only after both finite ledgers are visible. Thus the public endpoint-difference read returns to the displayed bounded-variation ledger rather than to a measure-valued object.

For common refinements, use $R$ to transport the partition and edge rows, then replay the same signed split through $H$ and $C$. Package and naming provenance are carried componentwise, so no extra comparison coordinate is introduced.

The excluded host objects do not occur among the carrier coordinates. The certificate therefore closes only the finite signed-variation ledger and its displayed transport surface.
\end{proof}
