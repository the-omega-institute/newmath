\begin{definition}[Philosophy governance-export carrier]
\label{def:philosophy-governance-export-carrier}
A $\PhilosophyGovernanceExportUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(P,R,L,S,C,X,T,O,H,K,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the package row, recording the $\BedcPhilosophyPackageUp$ thesis, registry, theorem-map, gap-map, tradition/science-map, closure-status, and audit-policy coordinates consumed by the export.
\item $R$ is the finite cannot-claim registry row for the same package reading.
\item $L$ is the active-label row, recording the current citation target, active formulation, and label discipline for the exported reading.
\item $S$ is the capstone row, recording whether the reading spans the required registered domains and which dependencies are displayed.
\item $C$ is the claim-registry row, recording claim text, admission status, local refusal status, and public route.
\item $X$ is the export-grade row, recording reading export, paper-synthesis export, component-checked export, formal-package target, or formal-package checked status.
\item $T$ is the transport row from $\CannotClaimExportGateUp$, $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, $\PhilosophyMapTargetUp$, and $\GapFailureBridgeAuditUp$ into the same package reading.
\item $O$ is the route and provenance row, recording displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ source data, and package-file provenance for $P,R,L,S,C,X,T,H,N$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for package, registry, active-label, capstone, claim, export-grade, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $K$ is the policy row enforcing finite registry lookup, refusal lookup, active-label lookup, capstone dependency lookup, export-grade lookup, and no hidden checked-target override.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PhilosophyGovernanceExportUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an export whose claim row bypasses the cannot-claim registry, whose active label detaches from the exported formulation, whose capstone dependency list is implicit, whose export grade outruns its certificate, or whose checked formal-package status is supplied by host-side prose.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Philosophy governance-export no override]
\label{thm:philosophy-governance-export-no-override}
Every accepted $\PhilosophyGovernanceExportUp$ export route factors through the package row $P$, cannot-claim registry row $R$, active-label row $L$, capstone row $S$, claim-registry row $C$, export-grade row $X$, transport row $T$, route/provenance row $O$, componentwise transport row $H$, policy row $K$, and local naming row $N$. No consumer can override an active refusal, active label, capstone dependency list, export grade, or package provenance by appealing to prose adjacency in the philosophy package.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet $E=(P,R,L,S,C,X,T,O,H,K,N)$. The package row $P$ names the package reading, while $R,L,S,C,X$ display the refusal, active-label, capstone, claim-registry, and export-grade surfaces before any public route is named.

The transport and route rows then preserve the same order. The row $T$ cites the sibling refusal gate, claim registry layer, philosophy map target, and gap/failure bridge audit, while $O$ replays the displayed route and provenance for the same reading.

Finally $H,K,N$ compare, enforce, and name the packet. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, a consumer has nowhere to obtain a checked-target override, a different active label, or a stronger export grade. The no-override claim is therefore a projection of the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Philosophy governance-export NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:philosophy-governance-export-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:philosophy-governance-export-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\PhilosophyGovernanceExportUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $E=(P,R,L,S,C,X,T,O,H,K,N)$, package exposure through $P$, cannot-claim exposure through $R$, active-label exposure through $L$, capstone exposure through $S$, claim-registry exposure through $C$, export-grade exposure through $X$, sibling transport through $T$, displayed route and provenance through $O$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, finite policy enforcement through $K$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the packet by coordinates. Carrier admission supplies the finite $\BHist$ object, and the rows $P,R,L,S,C,X$ supply the package reading, refusal registry, active label, capstone dependencies, claim entry, and export grade.

The support rows then expose the audit route. The row $T$ records which sibling surfaces are consumed, $O$ records continuation and provenance, $H$ transports only the displayed rows, and $K$ checks the finite policy conditions before the local name $N$ is available.

There is no remaining field in the packet. Hence the listed coordinates exhaust the local NameCert obligations, and any accepted export must be read through those obligations.
\end{proof}
