\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-generatorfixedpoint-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$ packet names the finite certificate surface where an authorized generator returns a displayed output that remains fixed under its own certified continuation boundary. It refines the $\GeneratorClosureUp$ classifier by adding a fixed-output witness while keeping every read inside the constructor-list provenance of the authorized generator. A continuation without that witness is not a fixed point of the generator; it is recorded as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary.

\begin{definition}[Generator fixed-point carrier]
\label{def:generator-fixed-point-carrier}
A $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(G,L,Q,W,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the authorized generator row whose constructor-list closure supplies the only admitted source of generated outputs;
\item $L$ is the constructor-list provenance for $G$, including the displayed witness that the relevant continuation remains inside that list;
\item $Q$ is the $\GeneratorClosureUp$ classifier row that compares generator-internal continuations with boundary attempts;
\item $W$ is the fixed-output witness, recording that the displayed generator continuation returns the same output row under the certified route;
\item $O$ is the displayed output row whose fixed-output status is witnessed by $W$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $G,L,Q,W,O,P,N$ and the local packet shape;
\item $C$ records the finite $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the generator row, constructor-list provenance, classifier row, fixed-output witness, output row, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, authorized generator rows, constructor-list provenance, $\GeneratorClosureUp$, fixed-output witnesses, displayed outputs, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and boundary ledger rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorFixedPointUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no host-level fixed-point theorem, no self-application oracle, and no induction rule beyond the constructor-list provenance displayed in $L$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Generator fixed-point NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:generator-fixed-point-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorFixedPointUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:generator-fixed-point-carrier} consists of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the packet $F=(G,L,Q,W,O,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier stability stating that the $\GeneratorClosureUp$ row $Q$ is preserved by componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the displayed generator, constructor-list provenance, fixed witness, output row, routes, package provenance, and local certificate;
\item fixed-witness persistence stating that $W$ remains attached to $O$ under the displayed generator-internal continuation routes;
\item non-escape stating that any continuation missing the constructor-list witness required by $L$ is recorded as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary rather than as a generator fixed point;
\item ledger exactness stating that every public read of the certificate factors through $G,L,Q,W,O,H,C,P,N$ and no unrecorded fixed-point principle is exported.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:generator-fixed-point-carrier}. The tuple lists the generator row, constructor-list provenance, closure classifier, fixed-output witness, displayed output, transports, routes, package provenance, and local naming row.

Classifier stability is read through $H$ and $C$. Transport preserves the generator row and constructor-list provenance, so the $\GeneratorClosureUp$ row $Q$ continues to classify the same generator-internal boundary.

Fixed-witness persistence is the displayed role of $W$. Since $W$ is a coordinate of the same finite packet as $O$, a consumer route that stays inside $C$ can read the output only together with the witness that certifies it as fixed under the displayed continuation.

The non-escape and ledger-exactness rows are the remaining package clauses. If a continuation lacks the constructor-list witness named by $L$, no carrier coordinate can turn it into a fixed point; the package records it as $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary evidence, and every exported read remains a projection of the listed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Generator fixed-point non-escape]
\label{thm:generator-fixed-point-non-escape}
Let $F$ be an accepted $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$ carrier. A finite continuation read that lacks the constructor-list witness required by $L$ is recorded by the package provenance $P$ as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary. It is not admitted as a fixed point of the generator row $G$, and it cannot export the fixed-output witness $W$ for the displayed output row $O$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the continuation through the displayed route $C$. If the route carries the constructor-list witness from $L$, then it is evaluated against the $\GeneratorClosureUp$ classifier $Q$ and may read the fixed-output witness $W$ for $O$.

If the route lacks the witness from $L$, it has no carrier coordinate that authorizes it as generator-internal. The package provenance $P$ therefore records the attempted read as $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary evidence, and the local naming row $N$ cannot expose it as a fixed point.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Generator fixed-point witness stability]
\label{thm:generator-fixed-point-fixed-witness}
Componentwise $\hsame$ transport preserving $G,L,Q,W,H,C,P,N$ preserves the fixed-output witness for the displayed row $O$. After such transport, the transported packet still reads $O$ through the same authorized generator row, constructor-list provenance, $\GeneratorClosureUp$ classifier, fixed-output witness, continuation routes, package provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorFixedPointUp}$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the transport row $H$. It preserves the generator row $G$, the constructor-list provenance $L$, the classifier $Q$, the fixed-output witness $W$, and the local routing and package rows.

The continuation row $C$ then replays the same generator-internal read of the displayed output. Since the witness $W$ is transported as one of the preserved coordinates, the fixed-output status of $O$ remains attached to the transported packet.

Finally $P$ and $N$ repack the transported rows as the same local certificate surface. No new continuation route is introduced, so a boundary attempt remains outside the fixed-point witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Generator fixed-point admitted packet row]
\label{thm:generator-fixed-point-admitted-packet-row}
Every accepted $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$ packet exposes exactly the rows
$$
G,L,Q,W,O,H,C,P,N
$$
of \autoref{def:generator-fixed-point-carrier}. A public or local read of the packet first projects one of these displayed rows: the authorized generator row $G$, constructor-list provenance $L$, $\GeneratorClosureUp$ classifier row $Q$, fixed-output witness $W$, displayed output row $O$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport row $H$, $\Cont$ route row $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance row $P$, or local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorFixedPointUp}$ row $N$. No accepted packet contains an additional host fixed-point principle, self-application oracle, or unlisted continuation coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. The accepted packet is the finite $\BHist$ tuple displayed there, so admission is projection from the nine named coordinates and not from an ambient fixed-point operator.

The route row $C$ and provenance row $P$ determine how consumers read the tuple. Since every such route is attached to one of the displayed coordinates, any accepted read factors through $G,L,Q,W,O,H,C,P,N$.

The last assertion is the non-escape side of the same inventory. The carrier definition lists no host theorem, oracle, or extra continuation field, and the local naming row $N$ certifies only the displayed packet shape.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Generator fixed-point classifier transport row]
\label{thm:generator-fixed-point-classifier-transport-row}
Let $F=(G,L,Q,W,O,H,C,P,N)$ and $F'=(G',L',Q',W',O',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$ packets related by componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the displayed generator row, constructor-list provenance, classifier row, fixed-output witness, output row, routes, package provenance, and local naming row. Then the transported packet preserves the $\GeneratorClosureUp$ classifier row and the fixed-output witness route: $Q'$ classifies the transported generator-internal continuation under the transported constructor-list provenance, and $W'$ remains attached to the transported output row $O'$ through the transported $\Cont$ routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the transport row $H$ of $F$ together with the matching row $H'$ of $F'$. These rows relate the generator, constructor-list provenance, classifier, witness, output, routes, provenance, and naming coordinates component by component.

The classifier part is the transported $Q$ row. Because the constructor-list provenance $L$ is transported with the generator row $G$, the $\GeneratorClosureUp$ classifier reads the same generator-internal boundary after transport.

The fixed-output part is the transported $W$ row. Since the output row $O$ and continuation routes $C$ are transported in the same packet, the witness route remains attached to $O'$ and does not create a separate boundary read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Generator fixed-point witness ledger row]
\label{thm:generator-fixed-point-witness-ledger-row}
For every accepted $\GeneratorFixedPointUp$ packet, any public witness read factors through the fixed-output witness row $W$, displayed output row $O$, continuation route row $C$, package provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$. A read that does not factor through this witness ledger remains a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary; in particular, non-witness generator routes and continuations outside the constructor-list provenance are not exported as fixed-output witnesses.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read a public witness request through the route row $C$. If the route is a witness route, it must project the displayed witness $W$ together with the displayed output row $O$.

The package provenance $P$ and local naming row $N$ then record that projection as part of the finite certificate surface. Thus a witness consumer receives the same $W,O,C,P,N$ ledger, not an unrecorded fixed-point principle.

If the route is not one of the displayed witness routes, \autoref{thm:generator-fixed-point-non-escape} applies. The attempt lacks the required constructor-list witness and is recorded as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary rather than as a fixed-output export.
\end{proof}
