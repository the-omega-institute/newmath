It sits between the Cauchy-net carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the Moore--Smith completion packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-moore-smith-completion-namecert}, the uniform completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, and the Real readback boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Moore--Smith Cauchy-net/filter bridge carrier]
\label{def:moore-smith-cauchy-net-filter-bridge-carrier}

$$

\item $T$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ tail row, recording eventual containment tests over the directed index surface $D$;
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ base row, recording finite base refinements and Cauchy diameter tests;
\item $K$ is the cofinal net-filter comparison row, displaying the finite route from directed tails to filter bases and back to cofinal tail data;

\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ comparison row for metric-facing completion consumers;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row used by completion-facing readback;

\end{enumerate}
The public carrier exports the finite packet $B$ and no quotient completion, selected subnet, chosen filter limit, countable-choice schedule, or ambient completeness datum.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith Cauchy-net/filter bridge NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:moore-smith-cauchy-net-filter-bridge-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:moore-smith-cauchy-net-filter-bridge-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\MooreSmithCauchyNetFilterBridgeUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $B=(D,T,F,K,U,M,S,R,Y,E,H,C,P,N)$; directed-index admission through $D$; Cauchy-net tail admission through $T$; Cauchy-filter base admission through $F$; cofinal comparison through $K$; uniform and metric completion handoff through $U$ and $M$; StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and Real readback through $S,R,Y,E$; componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$; displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$; provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The rows $D,T,F,K$ expose the directed index surface, the Cauchy-net tails, the Cauchy-filter bases, and the cofinal comparison between them as visible coordinates of one finite packet.

The completion and readback rows are downstream. The uniform and metric completion handoffs $U,M$ are read only after the net/filter comparison row is displayed, and the finite readback rows $S,R,Y,E$ attach the StreamName, RegSeqRat, dyadic, and Real-facing reads to the same displayed route.

The structural rows exhaust the NameCert surface. Componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming are exactly $H,C,P,N$, so a consumer cannot obtain a quotient completion, selected subnet, chosen filter limit, countable-choice schedule, or ambient completion principle from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith Cauchy-net/filter bridge handoff]
\label{thm:moore-smith-cauchy-net-filter-bridge-handoff}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\MooreSmithCauchyNetFilterBridgeUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
D,T,F,K,U,M,S,R,Y,E,H,C,P,N
$$
before reading any $\RealUp$ seal. In particular, a consumer moving between \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-moore-smith-completion-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert} reads the cofinal net/filter rows before it can use the Real-facing readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a completion-facing request. Carrier admission forces the directed index row $D$, Cauchy-net tail row $T$, Cauchy-filter base row $F$, and cofinal comparison row $K$ to be visible before any completion handoff row appears.

The handoff rows $U$ and $M$ then carry only the displayed comparison route. They do not identify completion classes, choose a subnet, or select a filter limit; they pass the finite net/filter comparison to uniform and metric completion consumers.

Finally the readback rows $S,R,Y,E$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ keep the Real seal downstream of the same displayed route. Since the packet has no coordinate for quotient completion, countable choice, selected limits, or ambient completeness, every completion-facing read factors through the named rows before it reaches $\RealUp$.
\end{proof}
