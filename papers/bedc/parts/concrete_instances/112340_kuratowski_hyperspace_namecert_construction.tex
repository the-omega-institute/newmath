\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\KuratowskiHyperspaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-kuratowski-hyperspace-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kuratowski and Hausdorff, the $\KuratowskiHyperspaceUp$ packet names the completion-facing hyperspace whose public rows are compact-subset presentations, finite Hausdorff-distance comparisons, and Kuratowski embedding transport. It sits after the finite compact-subset carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hyperspace-namecert}, the embedding route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kuratowski-embedding-namecert}, and the universal Hausdorff completion handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorff-completion-universal-namecert}. The chapter records the carrier boundary on which later completion consumers may inspect hyperspace data without importing host powersets, quotient compacta, selected Cauchy limits, or ambient equality of closed subsets.

\begin{definition}[Kuratowski hyperspace carrier]
\label{def:kuratowski-hyperspace-carrier}
An accepted $\KuratowskiHyperspaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(H,E,U,F,D,S,C,P,M)
$$
whose rows have the following public roles.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the underlying $\HyperspaceUp$ finite compact-subset carrier, including the compact source, Vietoris finite-net rows, directed Hausdorff ledgers, and tolerance readback.
\item $E$ is the $\KuratowskiEmbeddingUp$ row that sends each compact-subset presentation to its displayed distance-profile history.
\item $U$ is the $\HausdorffCompletionUniversalUp$ handoff row that admits only completion routes already expressed through Hausdorff-distance Cauchy data.
\item $F$ is the compact finite-net readback row used by hyperspace and embedding consumers before any completion read is public.
\item $D$ is the distance-stability ledger comparing the Hausdorff classifier on $H$ with the Kuratowski profile classifier on $E$.
\item $S,C,P,M$ are respectively componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\KuratowskiHyperspaceUp}$ naming row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a host powerset of closed subsets, a quotient completion class, a selected representative, a selected compact limit, or an ambient extensional equality of subsets.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Kuratowski hyperspace distance stability]
\label{thm:kuratowski-hyperspace-distance-stability}
Let $K=(H,E,U,F,D,S,C,P,M)$ be an accepted $\KuratowskiHyperspaceUp$ carrier. Every distance comparison exposed by $K$ is stable under componentwise $\hsame$ transport exactly when the $\HyperspaceUp$ Hausdorff classifier carried by $H$, the $\KuratowskiEmbeddingUp$ distance-profile classifier carried by $E$, and the $\HausdorffCompletionUniversalUp$ Cauchy handoff carried by $U$ are transported through the same $\BHist$ route. The stability ledger $D$ therefore compares finite-net Hausdorff rows with Kuratowski profile rows; it does not identify compact subsets by quotient equality or by host extensional equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier of \autoref{def:kuratowski-hyperspace-carrier}. The only distance rows available to a consumer are the directed Hausdorff ledgers in $H$, the distance-profile route in $E$, and the completion-facing handoff in $U$.

The compact finite-net row $F$ fixes the source data on which both $H$ and $E$ are evaluated. Transport through $S$ is componentwise, so it preserves the displayed source, the compact finite-net schedules, and the tolerance readback before the comparison reaches $D$.

Finally replay the transported comparison through $C$ and name it through $P,M$. Since the carrier lacks quotient representatives and host subset equality coordinates, the only possible stability statement is the finite classifier transport recorded by $D$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Kuratowski hyperspace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:kuratowski-hyperspace-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\KuratowskiHyperspaceUp$ carrier $K=(H,E,U,F,D,S,C,P,M)$, the local $\NameCert_{\KuratowskiHyperspaceUp}$ obligations are exactly the admission of the $\HyperspaceUp$ carrier $H$, the $\KuratowskiEmbeddingUp$ profile row $E$, the $\HausdorffCompletionUniversalUp$ completion handoff $U$, the compact finite-net readback $F$, the distance-stability ledger $D$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $S$, displayed $\Cont$ replay $C$, and $\Pkg$ provenance $P$. These obligations exhaust the public naming surface before any completion consumer can read a hyperspace object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier coordinates listed in \autoref{def:kuratowski-hyperspace-carrier}. Each coordinate is already a displayed BEDC row: hyperspace finite-net data, Kuratowski profile transport, Hausdorff completion handoff, classifier stability, structural transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Use \autoref{thm:kuratowski-hyperspace-distance-stability} for the only comparison row. It pins the Hausdorff and Kuratowski classifiers to the same finite $\BHist$ route and rules out quotient or host equality reads.

It remains to account for completion consumers. They can enter only through $U$, after $H,E,F,D,S,C,P,M$ have been exposed. Thus the listed rows are exactly the NameCert obligations and no extra carrier coordinate remains public.
\end{proof}
