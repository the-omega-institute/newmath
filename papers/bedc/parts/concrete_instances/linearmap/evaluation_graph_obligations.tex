\begin{definition}[LinearMap evaluation-graph carrier]
\label{def:linearmap-evaluation-graph-carrier}
Let $F$ carry a $\FieldUp(F)$ certificate, and let $V$ and $W$ carry $\VecSpaceUp(F,V)$ and $\VecSpaceUp(F,W)$ certificates over the same scalar source. A $\LinearMapUp(V,W)$ evaluation-graph packet is a finite $\BHist$ family with the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item a source vector-space row naming the carrier, addition, zero, additive inverse, scalar action, and classifier of $V$;
  \item a target vector-space row naming the corresponding data of $W$;
  \item an evaluation graph row $G_f$ whose entries record carried source endpoints $x:V$, carried target endpoints $y:W$, and graph witnesses $f(x)\sim_W y$;
  \item an additivity ledger row stating that every carried pair $x,u:V$ has a graph witness for $x+_V u$ whose target endpoint is classified with $f(x)+_W f(u)$;
  \item a scalar-compatibility ledger row stating that every carried scalar $a:F$ and carried $x:V$ have a graph witness for $a\odot_V x$ whose target endpoint is classified with $a\odot_W f(x)$;
  \item a pointwise classifier row comparing two such graph packets by target-classifier comparison at each carried source endpoint;
  \item continuation rows showing that evaluation, additivity, and scalar-compatibility entries remain visible after the relevant $\Cont$ routes and context appends;
  \item a $\Pkg$ provenance row recording the function identifier, the source and target vector-space packages, the three graph ledgers, and the finite history addresses used by the packet;
  \item a $\NameCert$ row asserting reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, carrier transport, and ledger exactness for the pointwise graph classifier.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LinearMap evaluation-graph NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:linearmap-evaluation-graph-namecert-obligations}
Every evaluation-graph packet of \autoref{def:linearmap-evaluation-graph-carrier} supplies the local $\LinearMapUp(V,W)$ NameCert obligation surface: carrier admission for the map-code, pointwise classifier transport along the target $\NameCert$, additivity stability, scalar-compatibility stability, and exact recovery of the displayed graph, additivity, scalar, continuation, $\Pkg$, and naming ledgers. Conversely, any finite $\BHist$ packet carrying precisely those rows is admitted by the evaluation-graph carrier and determines the same pointwise $\LinearMapUp(V,W)$ classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission reads the source and target vector-space rows through the $\VecSpaceUp$ certificates and then reads their module fragments through \autoref{prop:vecspace-module-fragment-projection}. The output-closure part of the graph row gives the carried target endpoint for each carried source input, so the packet has the map-code carrier required by \autoref{def:linearmap-carrier}.

Pointwise classifier transport is inherited from the target $\NameCert$. For each carried source endpoint, the graph row gives a target classifier comparison, and \autoref{def:linearmap-classifier-specification} packages those target comparisons as the $\LinearMapUp(V,W)$ classifier. Reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are the pointwise rows isolated in \autoref{thm:linearmap-pointwise-classifier-equivalence}.

Additivity and scalar compatibility are the two stability ledgers. The additivity row is the same law used in \autoref{def:linearmap-stability-certificate}; the scalar row is the vector-space form of the module certificate in \autoref{def:module-linearmap-certificate-package}. The zero and inverse consequences available in \autoref{thm:module-zero-linearmap-certificate}, \autoref{thm:module-zero-linearmap-classifier-uniqueness}, and \autoref{thm:linearmap-additive-inverse-preservation} show that no separate function-extensional equality is needed.

Finally, the continuation rows are checked by the singleton evaluation and composition continuation surfaces in \autoref{thm:linearmap-singleton-eval-context-continuation-append-target-carrier-iff}, \autoref{thm:linearmap-singleton-eval-context-append-input-result-carrier-iff}, and \autoref{thm:linearmap-singleton-eval-comp-continuation-pair-classifier}. Since every public read is one of the listed $\BHist$ rows, the $\Pkg$ row and the $\NameCert$ row recover exactly the carrier, classifier, stability, and ledger fields of \autoref{def:linearmap-certificate-obligations}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LinearMap evaluation-graph composition closure]
\label{thm:linearmap-evaluation-graph-composition-closure}
Let $F$ carry a $\FieldUp(F)$ certificate, and let $U$, $V$, and $W$ carry $\VecSpaceUp(F,U)$, $\VecSpaceUp(F,V)$, and $\VecSpaceUp(F,W)$ certificates over the same scalar source. If $G_f$ is an accepted evaluation-graph packet for $f:U\to V$ and $G_g$ is an accepted evaluation-graph packet for $g:V\to W$, with the target vector-space row of $G_f$ matching the source vector-space row of $G_g$, then the $\Cont$-spliced graph packet for $g\circ f:U\to W$ is an accepted evaluation-graph packet. Its pointwise classifier is the target classifier of $W$, its additivity ledger is the composite of the additivity ledgers of $G_f$ and $G_g$, and its scalar-compatibility ledger is the composite of their scalar ledgers.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LinearMapUp.LinearMapEvaluationGraph\_composition\_closure}
\begin{proof}
The matching middle row identifies the target classifier and operations of the first packet with the source classifier and operations of the second packet. Thus every carried $u:U$ evaluated by $G_f$ produces a carried middle endpoint in $V$, and the carrier-admission row of $G_g$ evaluates that endpoint in $W$. Splicing the two $\Cont$ routes gives the graph row for $g(f(u))$.

For additivity, the first packet classifies $f(u+_U u')$ with $f(u)+_V f(u')$. The second packet transports that middle classifier comparison through its pointwise descent row and then applies its own additivity ledger to classify $g(f(u)+_V f(u'))$ with $g(f(u))+_W g(f(u'))$. The target $\NameCert$ in $W$ composes these comparisons into the displayed additivity ledger for $g\circ f$.

The scalar row is parallel. The first packet classifies $f(a\odot_U u)$ with $a\odot_V f(u)$. The second packet transports along the shared middle classifier and applies scalar compatibility in the target vector space, yielding a comparison with $a\odot_W g(f(u))$.

All pointwise comparisons are recorded as target-classifier rows in $W$, so \autoref{def:linearmap-classifier-specification} supplies the composite $\LinearMapUp(U,W)$ classifier. The $\Pkg$ provenance is the ordered pair of input packet identifiers together with the spliced continuation address, and the exactness clause follows because no row outside the two accepted graph packets and their shared middle vector-space row is read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LinearMap evaluation-graph composition proof interface]
\label{thm:linearmap-evaluation-graph-composition-closure-proof-interface}
Let $F$ carry a $\FieldUp(F)$ certificate, and let $U$, $V$, and $W$ carry vector-space rows over the same scalar source. Suppose that $G_f$ is an accepted evaluation-graph packet for $f:U\to V$, that $G_g$ is an accepted evaluation-graph packet for $g:V\to W$, that the target vector-space row of $G_f$ is $\hsame$ as the source vector-space row of $G_g$, that target-classifier transport is available in $W$, and that the displayed $\Cont$ provenance row splices the two graph reads through the shared middle row. Then these finite data determine exactly the nine rows of \autoref{def:linearmap-evaluation-graph-carrier} for $g\circ f$: the source vector-space row, the target vector-space row, the evaluation graph row, the additivity row, the scalar row, the pointwise target classifier row, the continuation row, the $\Pkg$ provenance row, and the naming row.

Consequently the composite packet satisfies the local NameCert obligation surface of \autoref{thm:linearmap-evaluation-graph-namecert-obligations}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The two accepted packets are finite $\BHist$ packets, so their source vector-space rows, target vector-space rows, additivity rows, scalar rows, target classifier rows, $\Cont$ rows, $\Pkg$ rows, and naming rows are already present as carried history rows. The $\hsame$ middle hypothesis identifies the target vector-space row of $G_f$ with the source vector-space row of $G_g$, so the composite source row is read from $G_f$ and the composite target row is read from $G_g$.

For the evaluation graph row, follow the $\Cont$ provenance from a carried source endpoint through $G_f$ to the middle vector-space row and then through $G_g$ to the target row of $W$. The target-classifier transport in $W$ records the resulting pointwise classifier row for $g\circ f$.

For the additivity row, read the additivity row of $G_f$ in the middle vector space, transport it across the shared target classifier row, and then read the additivity row of $G_g$ in $W$. The scalar row is obtained by the same route, using the scalar rows of the two packets and the same target classifier row in $W$.

The $\Pkg$ row is the paired provenance of the two input packets together with the spliced $\Cont$ address, and the naming row records that this finite packet is the graph packet for $g\circ f$. These are exactly the nine rows specified by \autoref{def:linearmap-evaluation-graph-carrier}, and \autoref{thm:linearmap-evaluation-graph-composition-closure} supplies the accepted composite packet. Applying \autoref{thm:linearmap-evaluation-graph-namecert-obligations} to those rows gives the stated local NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}
