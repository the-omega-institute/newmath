\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformCauchyBicompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-cauchy-bicompletion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki on uniform structures and Kelley on completions of uniform spaces, the $\UniformCauchyBicompletionUp$ packet records the finite two-sided Cauchy-filter corridor used to approach a separated uniform-completion boundary. It sits over the uniform-space surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the separated comparison row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separated-limit-uniqueness-namecert}, the uniform-completion consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, and the real readback boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names only finite bicompletion data for a displayed left and right Cauchy route; it does not construct a quotient completion, select a limit representative, use choice, or assume ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Uniform Cauchy bicompletion carrier]
\label{def:uniform-cauchy-bicompletion-carrier}
A $\UniformCauchyBicompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(U,L,R,J,S,E,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage row, recording finite entourage membership, diagonal, inverse, refinement, and composition data available to both Cauchy sides.
\item $L$ is the left $\CauchyFilterUp$ row, exposing the finite left filter-base observations and their entourage refinements.
\item $R$ is the right $\CauchyFilterUp$ row, exposing the finite right filter-base observations and their entourage refinements.
\item $J$ is the joint Cauchy row, recording that requested left and right tails share a displayed common entourage refinement before any completion-facing read.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ comparison row, used only to identify two displayed boundary reads through separated zero-distance data.
\item $E$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ handoff row, reached after $U,L,R,J,S$ have been displayed.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ readback row for real-valued observers of the separated boundary, available only downstream of the uniform-completion handoff.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the uniform, left-filter, right-filter, joint-Cauchy, separated, completion, Real, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a bicompletion request through $U,L,R,J,S,E,A,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformCauchyBicompletionUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchyBicompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts exactly that these rows form one finite two-sided Cauchy corridor. It has no coordinate for quotient filters, selected completion points, ambient host equality, choice, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform Cauchy bicompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-bicompletion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformCauchyBicompletionUp$ carrier $B=(U,L,R,J,S,E,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchyBicompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the uniform-space row, two Cauchy-filter rows, joint-Cauchy row, separated comparison row, uniform-completion handoff, Real readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item left and right Cauchy-filter stability under componentwise $\hsame$ transport over the same $\UniformSpaceUp$ row $U$;
\item joint-refinement exactness through $J$, requiring both Cauchy sides to reach every displayed entourage request through finite filter-base refinements;
\item separated-boundary exactness through $S$, requiring any identification of two displayed boundary reads to pass through the separated comparison row;
\item completion and Real nonescape through $E$ and $A$, requiring every $\UniformCompletionUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer to stay downstream of $U,L,R,J,S$;
\item displayed $\Cont$ replay and $\Pkg$ provenance for the whole two-sided corridor;
\item refusal of quotient filters, selected completion points, ambient host equality, choice, and ambient Real completeness as certificate data.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:uniform-cauchy-bicompletion-carrier}. The source rows are $U,L,R,J,S,E,A,H,C,P,N$, so every obligation must be read from those coordinates.

The two Cauchy sides are bound by $L$ and $R$ over the same uniform row $U$. The joint row $J$ records the common finite entourage refinement requested by a bicompletion read, so neither side can be replaced by an independent host filter or a selected representative.

The separated and completion-facing rows are $S,E,A$. The row $S$ is the only comparison route for displayed boundary reads, $E$ is available only after the joint two-sided Cauchy data have been exposed, and $A$ is downstream of that handoff.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, record provenance for, and locally name the same corridor. Since the carrier has no quotient-filter, selected-point, host-equality, choice, or ambient-completeness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform Cauchy bicompletion separated route]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-bicompletion-separated-route}
Every separated $\UniformCompletionUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\UniformCauchyBicompletionUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
U \longmapsto L,R \longmapsto J \longmapsto S \longmapsto E,A,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the consumer first reads the uniform entourage row, then both Cauchy-filter sides, then the joint-refinement row, then the separated comparison row, and only then the completion handoff and Real readback rows. The route exports no quotient completion, selected limit representative, choice schedule, ambient host equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet $B=(U,L,R,J,S,E,A,H,C,P,N)$. The only uniform source is $U$, so every completion-facing read begins with the displayed entourage data.

The two Cauchy inputs are $L$ and $R$. Their common-refinement witness is $J$, which records the finite two-sided response to each requested entourage before a boundary read is available.

The separated boundary is $S$. Any comparison of two displayed completion reads must pass through this row, so host equality or a selected quotient representative cannot enter the route.

The endpoint rows are $E,A,H,C,P,N$. They hand off to the uniform-completion and Real-facing consumers, transport and replay the same data, and locally name the corridor without adding quotient, selection, choice, host-equality, or ambient-completeness data.
\end{proof}
