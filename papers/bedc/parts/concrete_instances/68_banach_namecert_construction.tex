\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BanachUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-banach-namecert}

The Banach-space interface $\BanachUp$ packages a normed vector space carrier together with a regular Cauchy completeness witness in the metric induced by the norm. It depends on $\NameCert_{\NormUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-norm-namecert}) and the real-completeness capstone (\autoref{ch:capstones-real-completeness}), and supplies the substrate for $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ and the operator certificates downstream.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:banach-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Complete normed-history Banach carrier]
\label{def:banach-complete-normed-carrier}
A $\BanachUp$ candidate is a $\VecSpaceUp$ carrier $C_V$ with vector
classifier $\sim_V$, zero endpoint $0_V$, additive difference operation
$x-_V y$, and scalar source read over $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$, together with a
$\NormUp$ norm
$$
\nu:C_V\to \RealUp
$$
whose classifier is the RealUp classifier. Its induced distance predicate is
the RealUp value
$$
d_\nu(x,y):=\nu(x-_V y)
$$
on carried vector endpoints $x,y:C_V$. A regular Cauchy completion witness for
the candidate is a row assigning to every regular Cauchy sequence
$(X,\mu):\mathbb{N}\to C_V$ for $d_\nu$ a carried limit endpoint
$\lim_\nu(X,\mu):C_V$, a convergence modulus, and a uniqueness certificate up
to $\sim_V$.

The BEDC source is $\NameCert_{\NormUp}$ together with the real-completeness
capstone of \autoref{ch:capstones-real-completeness}. The carrier choice is
$C_V$, the classifier is $\sim_V$, the stability obligations are norm transport
and Cauchy-sequence transport across $\sim_V$, the ledger records all moduli and
proof-index data used by the norm and completion rows, and the status is
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:banach-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Banach norm-induced metric obligations]
\label{thm:banach-metric-induced-by-norm}
For any $\BanachUp$ candidate of
\autoref{def:banach-complete-normed-carrier}, the distance predicate
$d_\nu(x,y)=\nu(x-_V y)$ satisfies the $\MetricSpaceUp$ witness obligations on
the vector carrier $C_V$: it is RealUp-valued and non-negative, it is zero
exactly on $\sim_V$-classified endpoints, it is symmetric, and it satisfies the
triangle inequality
$$
d_\nu(x,z)\le d_\nu(x,y)+d_\nu(y,z)
$$
for carried $x,y,z:C_V$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The RealUp carrier and non-negativity rows are exactly the codomain and
non-negativity clauses of the supplied $\NormUp$ norm. For carried endpoints
$x$ and $y$, the vector-space difference $x-_V y$ is carried by the
$\VecSpaceUp$ additive stability rows, so applying $\nu$ gives a carried
RealUp distance.

Zero exactness is the positive-definiteness clause of $\NormUp$ applied to the
difference endpoint $x-_V y$. The vector-space additive inverse and zero laws
identify $x-_V y$ with $0_V$ exactly when $x\sim_V y$, and classifier transport
converts this vector statement into the displayed distance-zero statement.

For symmetry, the vector-space inverse law identifies $x-_V y$ with the
additive inverse of $y-_V x$. The $\NormUp$ absolute-homogeneity row for the
scalar $-1$ and the RealUp absolute-value row $|-1|=1$ show that the two norm
values are classified by the RealUp classifier.

For the triangle inequality, use the vector identity
$x-_V z\sim_V (x-_V y)+_V(y-_V z)$. Norm transport moves $\nu$ across this
classifier comparison, and the triangle-inequality row of $\NormUp$ bounds the
right-hand side by $\nu(x-_V y)+\nu(y-_V z)$. This is the required
$\MetricSpaceUp$ triangle row for $d_\nu$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach classifier transport obligation]
\label{thm:banach-classifier-transport-obligation}
For any $\BanachUp$ candidate of
\autoref{def:banach-complete-normed-carrier}, if $x\sim_V x'$ and
$y\sim_V y'$ with all four endpoints carried by $C_V$, then the norm values and
induced distances transport:
$$
\mathsf{RealClassifier}(\nu(x),\nu(x'))
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{RealClassifier}(d_\nu(x,y),d_\nu(x',y')).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The first displayed row is the norm-transport stability obligation contained
in the $\NormUp$ certificate. It reads the vector classifier comparison
$x\sim_V x'$ and returns the RealUp classifier comparison between the two norm
values.

For the distance row, apply the $\VecSpaceUp$ congruence laws to the two input
classifier comparisons. They transport additive inverse and addition, giving
$x-_V y\sim_V x'-_V y'$ in the vector classifier.

Applying the same norm-transport row to this difference comparison yields
$\mathsf{RealClassifier}(\nu(x-_V y),\nu(x'-_V y'))$. Unfolding the definition
of $d_\nu$ on both sides gives the stated distance transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach regular Cauchy completion obligation]
\label{thm:banach-regular-cauchy-completion-obligation}
For any $\BanachUp$ candidate of
\autoref{def:banach-complete-normed-carrier}, every regular Cauchy sequence
$(X,\mu):\mathbb{N}\to C_V$ for the induced metric $d_\nu$ has the supplied
limit endpoint $\lim_\nu(X,\mu):C_V$. The endpoint comes with an explicit
convergence modulus for $d_\nu$, and any two endpoints satisfying the same
regular Cauchy limit specification are classified by $\sim_V$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The regular Cauchy input contains a carried vector endpoint $X_n$ at each
index and a modulus $\mu$ witnessing that the distances
$d_\nu(X_m,X_n)$ enter every requested positive RealUp tolerance after
$\mu(\varepsilon)$. By
\autoref{thm:banach-metric-induced-by-norm}, these distances are legitimate
$\MetricSpaceUp$ distances.

The completion row in the carrier definition is then applied directly to
$(X,\mu)$. It returns the carried endpoint $\lim_\nu(X,\mu)$, the convergence
modulus, and the ledger entries recording the modulus calls and the norm rows
used to read each distance.

If $p$ and $q$ are two carried endpoints satisfying the same limit
specification, the real-completeness capstone supplies uniqueness of the
RealUp distance-to-zero limit for the two difference sequences
$X_n-_V p$ and $X_n-_V q$. The vector-space subtraction identity rewrites
$p-_V q$ through those two tails, and the positive-definiteness row of
$\NormUp$ reflects distance zero back to $p\sim_V q$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach zero-sequence limit obligation]
\label{thm:banach-zero-sequence-limit-obligation}
For any $\BanachUp$ candidate of
\autoref{def:banach-complete-normed-carrier}, the constant sequence
$Z_n=0_V$ is regular Cauchy for the induced metric $d_\nu$, and its supplied
completion endpoint is classified with the vector zero:
$$
\lim_\nu(Z,\mu_0)\sim_V 0_V
$$
for the constant zero modulus $\mu_0$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The vector zero endpoint is carried by the $\VecSpaceUp$ zero row, so the
constant sequence $Z_n=0_V$ is a sequence in $C_V$. For any pair of indices,
$Z_m-_V Z_n$ is classified with $0_V$ by the additive identity and inverse
laws.

Positive definiteness and norm transport convert that vector zero comparison
into RealUp distance zero. Thus every requested tolerance is met by the
constant modulus $\mu_0$, and the sequence is regular Cauchy for $d_\nu$.

Apply
\autoref{thm:banach-regular-cauchy-completion-obligation} to obtain the
completion endpoint and its convergence modulus. The endpoint $0_V$ itself
satisfies the same limit specification by the preceding distance-zero row, so
the uniqueness part of the completion obligation classifies
$\lim_\nu(Z,\mu_0)$ with $0_V$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach certificate obligation assembly]
\label{thm:banach-certificate-obligation-assembly}
The data of a $\BanachUp$ candidate in
\autoref{def:banach-complete-normed-carrier}, together with the four obligation
rows of
\autoref{thm:banach-metric-induced-by-norm},
\autoref{thm:banach-classifier-transport-obligation},
\autoref{thm:banach-regular-cauchy-completion-obligation}, and
\autoref{thm:banach-zero-sequence-limit-obligation}, assemble the paper-side
$\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ obligation set. Its carrier is $C_V$, its classifier is
$\sim_V$, its metric witness is $d_\nu$, its stability rows are vector
classifier transport through $\nu$ and $d_\nu$, its ledger is the norm ledger
together with all regular Cauchy moduli and proof indices, and its completion
row is the supplied $\lim_\nu$ operation with uniqueness up to $\sim_V$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier fields are copied from the underlying
$\VecSpaceUp$ certificate, and the norm field is copied from the supplied
$\NormUp$ certificate. By
\autoref{thm:banach-metric-induced-by-norm}, the expression
$d_\nu(x,y)=\nu(x-_V y)$ supplies the metric witness expected by
$\MetricSpaceUp$.

The stability fields are the transport rows of
\autoref{thm:banach-classifier-transport-obligation}. They show that both
norm values and induced distances are invariant under the vector classifier,
which is the exact endpoint-respect condition required by the naming
certificate.

The completion fields are supplied by
\autoref{thm:banach-regular-cauchy-completion-obligation}. The special zero
row of \autoref{thm:banach-zero-sequence-limit-obligation} anchors the
certificate at the vector zero and records that the completion operation agrees
with the vector structure on the constant zero sequence.

Combining these rows gives the ten-field fit: traditional target Banach
spaces; BEDC source $\NameCert_{\NormUp}$ plus the RealUp completion capstone;
carrier $C_V$; classifier $\sim_V$; stability norm and distance transport;
ledger norm provenance, moduli, and proof indices; core theorem the completion
obligation; exactness zero distance iff vector classifier equality; standard
bridge ordinary complete normed vector spaces; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{proof}
