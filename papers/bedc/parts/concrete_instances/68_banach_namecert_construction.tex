\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BanachUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-banach-namecert}

The Banach-space interface $\BanachUp$ packages a normed vector space carrier together with a regular Cauchy completeness witness in the metric induced by the norm. It depends on $\NameCert_{\NormUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-norm-namecert}) and the real-completeness capstone (\autoref{ch:capstones-real-completeness}), and supplies the substrate for $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ and the operator certificates downstream.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:banach-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Concrete Banach carrier]\label{def:banach-concrete-carrier}
A concrete Banach carrier is a history predicate $X:\Hist\to\mathsf{Prop}$
together with the $\NormUp$ carrier and classifier data supplied by
$\NameCert_{\NormUp}$, a displayed norm value row for each carried history,
and the metric predicate induced by that norm:
$$
\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X(x,y,d)
$$
means that $x$ and $y$ are carried by $X$, their difference history is a
$\NormUp$ vector-history row, and $d$ is the $\RealUp$ norm value assigned to
that difference. The point classifier of the carrier is not a quotient:
carried representatives are identified by $\hsame$ transport of the displayed
vector, norm-value, and metric rows.

The completeness field is a family which assigns, to every regular Cauchy
$\StreamNameUp$ stream $s$ over $X$ for the norm-induced metric, histories
$\mu_s,\ell_s,L_s$ such that
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu_s,\ell_s,L_s)
$$
holds. Thus the Banach datum is the tuple consisting of the carried
$\NormUp$ structure, the induced metric ledger, the $\hsame$ classifier on
representatives, and the displayed limit-witness family for all regular
Cauchy stream names over $X$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Banach norm-induced metric compatibility]
\label{thm:banach-norm-induced-metric-compatibility}
For the singleton empty-history $\NormUp$ instance of
\autoref{def:singleton-empty-history-norm-instance}, read through the
singleton metric carrier and the relation
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$, the induced Banach metric row is the
constant RealUp zero endpoint row. Concretely, for carried vector histories
$m,n:\mathsf{BHist}$, the Banach distance between $m$ and $n$ is classified by
$0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$ exactly when
$$
\rho_{\emp}(m,n)
$$
holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First identify the singleton vector endpoints. By
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-norm-carrier-classifier}, any two carried
vectors in the singleton norm instance are related by $\rho_{\emp}$, and their
constant norm endpoints are related by the RealUp constant-history
classifier.

The zero side is exact by
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-norm-zero-exactness}. Thus the displayed
norm value of the singleton difference is classified by
$0_{\mathsf{Real}}^{c}$ exactly when the underlying vector endpoint is
classified with the singleton zero vector.

Finally read the same endpoint row through the metric carrier. The metric row
with empty distance is equivalent, by
\autoref{thm:metric-distance-witness-empty-distance-iff}, to both endpoints
being $\hsame$ to $\emp$. The singleton vector classifier is precisely the
collapse relation on those carried endpoints, so the norm-zero row and the
empty-distance metric row classify the same pairs $m,n$.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:banach-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Banach regular Cauchy limit witness]
\label{thm:banach-regular-cauchy-limit-witness}
Every regular Cauchy stream carried by the singleton norm-induced Banach
metric has a row
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(C_{\emp},s,\emp,\emp,L_{\emp})
$$
whose selected limit is $\emp$, whose modulus history is $\emp$, and whose
late-observation ledgers are the empty-distance rows transported through
\autoref{thm:banach-norm-induced-metric-compatibility}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the carried observations of the singleton norm-induced metric. Each
observation of the regular Cauchy stream is a carried singleton vector
history, hence collapses to $\emp$ by the singleton vector classifier.

Use the selected singleton limit. The complete-metric singleton construction
of \autoref{thm:singleton-complete-metric-laws} supplies the carried limit
$\emp$, the empty modulus, and the convergence ledger with rows
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(s(n),\emp,\emp)$ for every late observation.

Transport those metric rows back to the norm-induced presentation. By
\autoref{thm:banach-norm-induced-metric-compatibility}, the empty-distance
metric rows are exactly the rows whose norm endpoint is classified by the
constant RealUp zero endpoint. These transported ledgers are the required
$L_{\emp}$ family, so the displayed package is a
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ for the singleton Banach stream.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach limit classifier uniqueness]
\label{thm:banach-limit-classifier-uniqueness}
For any singleton normed stream $s$, any two Banach limit witnesses
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(C_{\emp},s,\mu_0,\ell_0,L_0)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(C_{\emp},s,\mu_1,\ell_1,L_1)
$$
have limits related by $\hsame(\ell_0,\ell_1)$, and therefore by the
singleton vector classifier $\rho_{\emp}(\ell_0,\ell_1)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-uniqueness} to the two
displayed limit witnesses for the same stream. In the singleton carrier the
separated point classifier is empty-history sameness, so the uniqueness
conclusion gives $\hsame(\ell_0,\ell_1)$.

It remains to read the same relation as a vector classifier row. Both limits
are carried singleton histories in their respective witness rows. Applying
\autoref{thm:singleton-empty-history-vecspace-endpoint-collapse} to the two
carried limits collapses them to the singleton endpoint and repacks their
$\hsame$ relation as $\rho_{\emp}(\ell_0,\ell_1)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Norm-induced metric completeness]
\label{thm:banach-norm-metric-completeness}
For every concrete Banach carrier $X$ of
\autoref{def:banach-concrete-carrier}, the supplied family of
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ rows makes the norm-induced metric on
$X$ complete: every regular Cauchy $\StreamNameUp$ stream over $X$ for
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X$ has a carried limit history, an explicit
modulus history, and late-observation distance ledgers bounded by each
positive $\RatUp$ tolerance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the carrier tuple in \autoref{def:banach-concrete-carrier}. A regular
Cauchy stream over $X$ is already part of the input shape accepted by the
completeness field: its observations are carried by the $\NormUp$ carrier and
its pairwise distance rows are exactly the displayed
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X$ rows obtained from norm values.

Apply the displayed completeness family to the stream. It returns the modulus
history, the carried limit history, and the late-observation ledger family
appearing in
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu_s,\ell_s,L_s)$. By
\autoref{def:completemetric-limit-witness}, those rows are precisely the
complete-metric convergence data for the metric carrier.

The norm side supplies no hidden quotient step. The classifier used at the
limit is $\hsame$ transport on representatives, so the returned rows remain
inside the same BEDC history carrier and are accepted as the completeness
witness for the norm-induced metric.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\hsame$ transport of Banach carriers]
\label{thm:banach-hsame-transport}
Let $X$ be a concrete Banach carrier. If carried representatives, norm rows,
metric rows, regular Cauchy stream observations, modulus histories, limit
histories, and late-observation ledgers are transported componentwise along
$\hsame$, then the transported package is again a concrete Banach carrier.
The transported carrier preserves the $\NormUp$ classifier fields and the
complete-metric limit-witness fields.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the Banach carrier as the tuple of \autoref{def:banach-concrete-carrier}.
The first component is the $\NameCert_{\NormUp}$ carrier package, so its
carrier, classifier, norm value, and algebraic law rows transport along the
given $\hsame$ histories by the stability fields of that certificate.

The metric row is induced from the transported norm row. Transport the two
endpoints, the displayed difference history, and the $\RealUp$ norm value;
then repack these transported ledgers as
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X$ for the transported representatives.

For the completeness component, apply
\autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-stability}. The hypotheses named in
the theorem are exactly the transported stream observations, modulus history,
limit history, thresholds, distance histories, and $\RatUp$ comparison rows.
The result is a transported
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s',\mu',\ell',L')$.

Combining the transported norm package, the induced metric row, and the
transported complete-metric witness family gives the transported concrete
Banach carrier. Since every field was moved by $\hsame$ and then repacked
componentwise, the norm and limit-witness fields are preserved exactly in the
paper-side classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Complete-metric witness exactness for Banach carriers]
\label{thm:banach-complete-metric-witness-exactness}
For a concrete Banach carrier $X$, the displayed predicate
$$
\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu,\ell,L)
$$
is exact for regular Cauchy streams over the norm-induced metric. Coverage
holds because every accepted regular Cauchy stream receives such a row from
the Banach completeness family. Soundness holds because any displayed row
contains carried observations, a carried limit, metric-distance rows, and
$\RatUp$ convergence bounds. Completeness holds because the real-completeness
capstone supplies the diagonal regular-Cauchy limit rows consumed by
$\NameCert_{\CompleteMetricUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For coverage, start with an accepted regular Cauchy stream over $X$. The last
field of \autoref{def:banach-concrete-carrier} assigns the histories
$\mu_s,\ell_s,L_s$ and the row
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}(X,s,\mu_s,\ell_s,L_s)$, so the displayed
predicate covers the stream.

For soundness, unfold \autoref{def:completemetric-limit-witness}. A displayed
row contains the carried-observation ledger, the explicit modulus, the carried
limit, the threshold history for each positive tolerance, and the metric
distance and $\RatUp$ comparison rows for every late observation. Since the
metric row is $\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X$, each distance is a norm value
of a difference history supplied by $\NameCert_{\NormUp}$.

For completeness of the displayed predicate, use the real-completeness
capstone through the $\CompleteMetricUp$ interface. The capstone supplies the
diagonal regular-Cauchy limit construction, while
\autoref{thm:completemetric-limit-witness-namecert-target} packages the
stream, modulus, limit, metric-distance rows, and rational bounds as a
complete-metric witness. Replacing the metric-distance rows by their
norm-induced representatives gives the Banach witness without changing the
BEDC histories being transported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Banach NameCert obligation package]
\label{thm:banach-namecert-obligation-package}
The data of \autoref{def:banach-concrete-carrier}, together with
\autoref{thm:banach-norm-metric-completeness},
\autoref{thm:banach-hsame-transport}, and
\autoref{thm:banach-complete-metric-witness-exactness}, assemble the
paper-side $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ obligation package. Its fields are the
$\NormUp$ carrier, the $\hsame$ classifier on representatives, the
norm-induced metric ledger, the complete-metric limit-witness family, the
transport stability clauses, the exactness clauses for displayed regular
Cauchy limits, and the visible ledger policy inherited from
$\NameCert_{\NormUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\CompleteMetricUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier field is the predicate $X:\Hist\to\mathsf{Prop}$ equipped with
the displayed $\NormUp$ data and the induced
$\mathsf{BanachMetricWitness}_X$ relation of
\autoref{def:banach-concrete-carrier}. The classifier field is $\hsame$
transport on representatives, so the certificate compares histories by
visible representative transport rather than by a host quotient.

The stability field is supplied by
\autoref{thm:banach-hsame-transport}. It moves the norm rows, metric rows,
stream rows, modulus histories, limit histories, and late-observation ledgers
componentwise and then repacks them as the same Banach carrier structure.

The completeness and exactness fields are supplied by
\autoref{thm:banach-norm-metric-completeness} and
\autoref{thm:banach-complete-metric-witness-exactness}. These theorems show
that every regular Cauchy stream over the norm-induced metric has a displayed
$\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ row, and that every such row has exactly
the carried observations, carried limit, norm-distance rows, and rational
comparison bounds demanded by $\NameCert_{\CompleteMetricUp}$.

The ledger field is the union of the visible rows already named by
$\NameCert_{\NormUp}$ with the stream, modulus, threshold, distance, and
comparison rows of $\NameCert_{\CompleteMetricUp}$. The real-completeness
capstone supplies the limit construction consumed by the complete-metric
side, so all fields of the $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ obligation package are
accounted for constructively.
\end{proof}

The ten-field fit is as follows. The traditional target is Banach space. The
BEDC source is the singleton $\NormUp$ carrier, the $\CompleteMetricUp$
singleton limit witness, and the RealUp constant zero endpoint. The carrier is
the singleton normed history carrier. The classifier is $\rho_{\emp}$ on
vectors and $\hsame$ on displayed limit histories. Stability is supplied by
$\NormUp$ transport and $\mathsf{CompleteMetricLimitWitness}$ transport. The
ledger records the norm-zero endpoint, metric-distance rows, the Cauchy
modulus, and the limit rows. The core theorem list is
\autoref{thm:banach-norm-induced-metric-compatibility},
\autoref{thm:banach-regular-cauchy-limit-witness}, and
\autoref{thm:banach-limit-classifier-uniqueness}. Exactness is the equivalence
between zero distance and the singleton classifier. The standard bridge field
is absent. The status is a paper proof target for the future namespace
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.BanachUp}$.
