\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NuclearUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-nuclear-namecert}

The nuclear-operator interface $\NuclearUp$ packages compact operators between Banach spaces representable as absolutely convergent rank-one sums. It depends on $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-banach-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\OperatorIdealUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-operatorideal-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:nuclear-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[NuclearUp carrier obligation]\label{thm:nuclear-carrier-obligation}
The carrier for $\NuclearUp$ is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ predicate packaging a $\BanachUp$ source endpoint, an $\OperatorIdealUp$ compact-operator surface, and a unary rank-index ledger. The carried endpoint records the visible $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows, their $\Pkg$ provenance, and the rank-index observations used by the nuclear expansion; hidden representatives of the Banach spaces, compact operators, and rank-one terms are not carrier equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the carrier field of the ten-field certificate template. The named source object is $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$, the operator surface is $\NameCert_{\OperatorIdealUp}$, and the rank index is represented as a unary $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger row.

Packing these three rows through $\mathsf{BHist}$ and $\Pkg$ gives the visible carrier predicate. Since all remaining data are placed behind the ledger row, the carrier equality is exactly the displayed BEDC package equality and not equality of host representatives.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NuclearUp compact-operator obligation]\label{thm:nuclear-compact-operator-obligation}
The compactness row for $\NuclearUp$ is read through certified $\OperatorIdealUp$ approximation data and $\BanachUp$ classifier transport. It is not an appeal to external norm compactness: compactness is visible only when the $\OperatorIdealUp$ surface supplies the approximation row and the $\BanachUp$ endpoints transport it along the displayed $\hsame$ classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the compact-operator field of the certificate template after \autoref{thm:nuclear-carrier-obligation}. The available objects are the $\NameCert_{\OperatorIdealUp}$ approximation row and the $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$ classifier on source and target histories.

The construction transports approximation observations along the $\BanachUp$ classifier and then repacks them as the compact row of the carried $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoint. No norm-compactness principle is introduced, because the only compactness witness consumed by the template is the certified operator-ideal approximation row.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:nuclear-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[NuclearUp rank-one-sum obligation]\label{thm:nuclear-rank-one-sum-obligation}
Rank-one expansion data for $\NuclearUp$ supplies unary $\mathsf{BHist}$ indices, coefficient and vector observations, a tail modulus, and $\Cont$-compatible partial sums. The partial-sum rows are stable under $\hsame$, so changing the displayed Banach histories by the certified classifier preserves the carried nuclear expansion package.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the expansion field of the ten-field template. Each unary index names one rank-one row, with its scalar coefficient, source vector observation, target vector observation, and tail-modulus entry recorded as $\mathsf{BHist}$ data.

The $\Cont$ row is then applied to the finite partial sums generated by those indices. Stability follows by transporting the coefficient and vector observations along $\hsame$ before repacking the same tail-modulus ledger, so the expansion remains a carried nuclear package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NuclearUp ledger exactness obligation]\label{thm:nuclear-ledger-exactness-obligation}
The $\NuclearUp$ ledger hides exactly rank indices, rank-one projections, and tail-modulus evidence. Its exactness row must supply coverage for every visible nuclear package, soundness for every ledger entry projected to the visible carrier, and completeness saying that no visible compact rank-one-sum package is lost by the hidden ledger representation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the ledger and exactness fields of the certificate template. The hidden rows are precisely the unary rank index, the rank-one projection data named by that index, and the tail-modulus witness used by the expansion row.

Coverage reads a visible nuclear package back into such ledger rows. Soundness projects any ledger row to the $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier of \autoref{thm:nuclear-carrier-obligation}, and completeness states that the projection captures all visible compact rank-one-sum packages admitted by \autoref{thm:nuclear-compact-operator-obligation} and \autoref{thm:nuclear-rank-one-sum-obligation}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NuclearUp dependency boundary]\label{thm:nuclear-dependency-boundary-obligation}
Downstream consumers of $\NuclearUp$ may use only the $\BanachUp$ interface, the $\OperatorIdealUp$ interface, and the rank-one ledger specified in this chapter. They may not use a trace-class principle, a spectral principle, choice, or host compactness as part of the $\NuclearUp$ certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the dependency field of the ten-field template. The declared dependencies are exactly $\NameCert_{\BanachUp}$, $\NameCert_{\OperatorIdealUp}$, and the unary rank-one ledger rows fixed by \autoref{thm:nuclear-rank-one-sum-obligation}.

Every public observation is obtained by projecting those named dependencies through $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\Pkg$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$. Since trace-class, spectral, choice, and host compactness principles are not dependency rows, they cannot be consumed by the certified $\NuclearUp$ surface.
\end{proof}
