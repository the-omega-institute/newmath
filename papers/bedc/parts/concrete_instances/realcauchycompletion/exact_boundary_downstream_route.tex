\begin{theorem}[RealCauchyCompletion exact-boundary downstream route]
\label{thm:real-cauchy-completion-exact-boundary-downstream-route}
Every $\RealCompletionExactBoundaryUp$ consumer of the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ completion route factors through the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ four-face support surface. The consumed route is the finite packet over $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ together with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ support rows.

The downstream consumer exports no quotient stream, no selected host limit, no host real equality, no countable-choice row, and no ambient completeness row. It is a consumer of the four-face completion route, not an isolated completion object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted $\RealCompletionExactBoundaryUp$ consumer of the completion route. By \autoref{thm:real-completion-exact-boundary-four-face-exactness}, the exact-boundary packet exposes exactly the four completion-facing faces $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ after its governing seal, classifier, witness-ledger, and budget rows.

Now read the same four faces on the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ side. The sibling lattice route \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-completion-sibling-lattice-route} places every public seal read on the finite route through the regular-family input, stream window, regular-rational readback, dyadic observation ledger, and exported $\RealUp$ seal.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-completion-four-face-support-nondegeneracy}. It says that the public completion read is nondegenerate exactly when the four faces and the visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows are present. Therefore the exact-boundary consumer can read the completion route only through that support surface.

Finally use \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-completion-non-escape-boundary}. Since the completion carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness, the downstream exact-boundary read cannot export any of those rows. It remains a finite consumer of the displayed four-face route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealCauchyCompletion exact-boundary bidirectional pullback]
\label{thm:real-cauchy-completion-exact-boundary-bidirectional-pullback}
Let an accepted $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ carrier and an accepted $\RealCompletionExactBoundaryUp$ packet display the same finite $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, terminal $\RealUp$ seal, and the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ support rows. Then the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ public completion read factors through the $\RealCompletionExactBoundaryUp$ terminal consumer, and conversely the exact-boundary terminal consumer restricts back to the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ four-face support surface.

The pullback exports no quotient stream, no selected host limit, no host real equality, no countable-choice row, no metric-completeness row, no bridge row, and no ambient completeness row. It is exactly the shared finite four-face support route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the shared-support hypotheses. The downstream route theorem \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-completion-exact-boundary-downstream-route} already places every exact-boundary consumption of the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ completion route on the four-face surface carried by $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, and the displayed structural rows.

For the forward direction, apply \autoref{thm:real-completion-exact-boundary-terminal-consumer-exactness}. Since the exact-boundary packet displays the same four faces and the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows, its terminal consumer is the finite route through those rows. The public $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ read therefore factors through that terminal consumer and not through any separate completion coordinate.

For the reverse direction, apply \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-completion-four-face-support-nondegeneracy}. The terminal exact-boundary consumer can agree with the public completion read only while retaining the same four faces and the same support rows. Thus the exact-boundary terminal consumer restricts back to the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ four-face support surface.

It remains to exclude outside witnesses. The exact-boundary side is bounded by \autoref{thm:real-completion-exact-boundary-no-completeness-escape}, while the completion side is bounded by \autoref{thm:real-cauchy-completion-non-escape-boundary}. Together these remove quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality, countable choice, metric completeness, bridge rows, and ambient completeness from both directions. Hence the two accepted readings meet precisely in the shared finite support pullback.
\end{proof}
