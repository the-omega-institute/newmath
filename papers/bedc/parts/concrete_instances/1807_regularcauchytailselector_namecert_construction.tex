Following Bishop--Bridges constructive Cauchy sequence tail selection, the $\RegularCauchyTailSelectorUp$ packet names only the finite rows needed to select an inspected tail of a regular rational Cauchy name. The packet sits on the $\RealUp$ and completion path: downstream diagonal-limit and finite-window consumers can cite the selected-tail ledger without obtaining a quotient representative, a host real equality, a selected infinite subsequence, or an ambient completeness principle.

The selector is the one-source face of the shared-tail threshold surface in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-tail-meet-namecert}. Its selected-tail witness row $w$ is consumed by the sibling meet packet whenever two regular Cauchy sources must share a dyadic threshold; \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-meet-selector-admission} is the exact sibling dependency that admits this witness into the shared-threshold route.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy tail selector carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-tail-selector-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyTailSelectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(q,s,r,d,e,w,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $q$ is the requested rational precision row, carried by $\RatUp$ and read through the dyadic comparison surface;
\item $s$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ source row whose finite windows are inspected by the selector;
\item $r$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row certifying that the inspected stream windows obey the displayed Cauchy tolerance schedule;
\item $d$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ readback row that turns each requested precision into the dyadic-radius comparison used by the tail ledger;
\item $e$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after the selected tail row and regularity row have both been read;
\item $w$ is the selected-tail witness row, listing the requested precision, the displayed starting index, the finite suffix window, and the dyadic bounds checked on that window;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the rational precision row, stream row, regularity row, dyadic readback row, real seal row, selected-tail witness row, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which diagonal-limit, finite-window, and real-seal consumers may read the packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and the local selector rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailSelectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a quotient of Cauchy streams, a host-level completed real, a globally chosen infinite tail function, or an external real-completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy tail selector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-selector-namecert-obligations}
The carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-tail-selector-carrier} satisfies the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailSelectorUp}$ obligation surface. Its five obligation rows are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite tuple $T=(q,s,r,d,e,w,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier transport by the componentwise $\hsame$ rows stored in $H$ and the consumer routes stored in $C$;
\item tail witness exactness, saying that $w$ names only the displayed precision request, starting index, finite suffix window, and dyadic bounds;
\item regularity compatibility, saying that each selected-tail bound is read through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $r$ and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row $d$ before any $\RealUp$ seal is exposed;
\item seal non-escape, saying that the real-facing route exposes only the finite selected-tail ledger and never a quotient representative, a host real equality, a choice-selected limit, or ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the displayed $\BHist$ tuple. Each coordinate is either a finite row, a transport row, a continuation row, package provenance, or the local naming row.

For classifier transport, move the rational precision, stream window, regularity, dyadic readback, seal, witness, package, and local naming coordinates through $H$, then read them through $C$. Componentwise transport cannot introduce a tail row outside the tuple.

Tail witness exactness is inspection of $w$. It records one requested precision, one displayed starting index, one finite suffix window, and the dyadic bounds checked on that window.

Regularity compatibility follows by reading $r$ and $d$ before $e$. The selected tail is certified by regular rational data and dyadic comparisons, so the real-facing seal is reached only after those rows have been consumed.

The carrier contains no quotient stream, host completed real, global selector, or ambient completeness coordinate. The local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailSelectorUp}$ obligations are therefore exhausted by the displayed finite rows.
\end{proof}
