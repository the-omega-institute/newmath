\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealZeroUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-realzero-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\RealZeroUp$ packet records the terminal zero real not as a host real equality claim but as the finite zero-source route already displayed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-stationary-rational-cauchy-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyzero-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter packages that route as the public zero-real seal consumed by downstream positive-real, absolute-value, order, metric, and completion-facing certificates.

\begin{definition}[RealZero carrier]
\label{def:real-zero-carrier}
A $\RealZeroUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(q,S,Z_0,D,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $q$ is the $\RatUp$ zero seed row.
\item $S$ is the constant $\StreamNameUp$ window whose every admitted finite request reads $q$.
\item $Z_0$ is the $\RegularCauchyZeroUp$ route, including the stationary $\RegSeqRatUp$ zero row and the zero regular-Cauchy source handoff.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ zero ledger recording the endpoint, tolerance, and radius data used by the zero-source route.
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached after the constant window, regular-rational readback, and dyadic zero ledger have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the rational seed, constant window, regular-Cauchy zero route, dyadic ledger, terminal seal, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay routes from $q$ through $S$, from $S$ through $Z_0$ and $D$, and from those rows into the terminal seal $R$.
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealZeroUp$, $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\StationaryRationalCauchyUp$, $\RegularCauchyZeroUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealZeroUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host zero equality, selected limits, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealZero NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-zero-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealZeroUp$ carrier $Z=(q,S,Z_0,D,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealZeroUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the rational zero seed, constant StreamName window, RegularCauchyZero route, DyadicRatCore zero ledger, terminal RealUp seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item stationary zero-source route, requiring the rational seed to be read only through the constant window, the stationary rational Cauchy row, and the RegularCauchyZero handoff;
\item terminal RealUp seal handoff, requiring the zero real-facing row $R$ to be exported only after the StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and RegularCauchyZero rows have been displayed;
\item componentwise transport and replay stability, requiring accepted zero-real packets to move through $H$ and replay through $C$ without changing the displayed zero-source chain;
\item ledger nonescape from quotient streams, host zero equality, selected limits, trichotomy, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these five finite rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-zero-carrier}. The only visible data are the zero seed $q$, the constant window $S$, the regular-Cauchy zero route $Z_0$, the dyadic zero ledger $D$, the terminal seal $R$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The stationary source row is forced by the sibling route. The packet reads $q$ through the constant $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\StationaryRationalCauchyUp$ surface, and the $\RegularCauchyZeroUp$ route before any regular-rational or real-facing consumer can inspect the zero source.

The terminal seal is then read through $R$. Transport and replay are componentwise because $H$ and $C$ are the only rows that move or re-open the chain.

The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host zero equality, selected limits, trichotomy, or ambient completeness. Thus the ledger cannot export any of those principles through the local $\NameCert_{\RealZeroUp}$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealZero terminal seal handoff]
\label{thm:real-zero-terminal-seal-handoff}
Every accepted $\RealZeroUp$ consumer must read the zero seed through the constant $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ zero row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ zero ledger, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal before any downstream $\PositiveRealUp$ or $\RealAbsUp$ consumer may use it. Equivalently, the public zero-real boundary is the finite route
$$
q \longrightarrow S \longrightarrow Z_0 \longrightarrow D \longrightarrow R
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$, and no consumer may replace that route by host zero equality, quotient real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $Z=(q,S,Z_0,D,R,H,C,P,N)$. The seed row $q$ is not exported directly; it is first read through the constant window $S$ and the regular-rational zero row supplied by $Z_0$.

Next the dyadic zero ledger $D$ records the finite endpoint and tolerance data attached to that same route. The real-facing seal $R$ is reached only after those rows are visible.

Downstream $\PositiveRealUp$ and $\RealAbsUp$ consumers therefore receive the zero source as the displayed terminal seal handoff, not as a host value. Since the carrier contains no excluded principle, the handoff cannot provide quotient real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
