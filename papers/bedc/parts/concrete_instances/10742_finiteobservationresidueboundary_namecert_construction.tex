The $\FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp$ packet realizes the hidden-residue and failure-surface branch of the model-audit program in \autoref{sec:constructive-model-audit}. It composes the audit rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-model-audit-namecert}, the finite defeat rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realityconstrainedfailuresurface-namecert}, the hidden-material ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ontic-residue-ledger-namecert}, and the finite source interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-observation-interface-namecert}. Those siblings already expose model audits, failure rows, residue ledgers, and finite observation windows; this packet binds them into one boundary surface for audit exports that cannot claim physical closure unless the hidden residue and defeating observations remain visible.

\begin{definition}[Finite-observation residue-boundary carrier]
\label{def:finite-observation-residue-boundary-carrier}
A $\FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(S,\Sigma,K,T,L,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite source row, read through the finite observation interface and the model-audit source trace.
\item $\Sigma$ is the exposed observation-signature row for the finite audit export.
\item $K$ is the classifier row deciding which source and model signatures are compared.
\item $T$ is the approximation-tower row that routes source, observation, and model-facing signatures through the same finite packet.
\item $L$ is the hidden-residue ledger, including unobserved source content, compression residue, idealization residue, and observer-access limits inherited from the residue-ledger surface.
\item $F$ is the failure-surface row naming the finite defeating observations, domain exits, classifier mismatches, and continuation boundaries relevant to the audit export.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,\Sigma,K,T,L,F,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of source access, signature comparison, tower movement, residue reading, and failure-surface consumption.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp$, $\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$, $\RealityConstrainedFailureSurfaceUp$, $\OnticResidueLedgerUp$, $\FiniteObservationInterfaceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a closed physical audit packet without $L$, no defeat-free model truth export without $F$, no hidden source completion outside $S$, and no classifier route outside $K$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-observation residue boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-observation-residue-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-observation-residue-boundary-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of source exposure, signature exposure, classifier exposure, tower exposure, hidden-residue ledger exactness, failure-surface exposure, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ continuation replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp.FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryNameCert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $R=(S,\Sigma,K,T,L,F,H,C,P,N)$. The first four rows provide the finite source, observation signatures, classifier, and tower needed to read a model-audit export as a bounded packet.

The ledger row $L$ and failure row $F$ supply the two boundary fields. $L$ records hidden residue from source access, compression, idealization, and observer limits; $F$ records the displayed defeating observations, domain exits, mismatches, and continuation boundaries.

The structural rows preserve and replay the same packet. Transport through $H$ is componentwise, replay through $C$ follows the displayed continuation routes, provenance through $P$ records the sibling certificates consumed by the packet, and $N$ supplies the local name.

Since the carrier has no further coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert surface. A model-audit export with the source, signature, classifier, and tower rows but no residue ledger or failure surface is a projection of $R$, not the accepted $\FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-observation residue boundary audit non-closure]
\label{thm:finite-observation-residue-boundary-audit-nonclosure}
Any model-audit export that passes through the finite source row $S$, signature row $\Sigma$, classifier row $K$, and tower row $T$ but omits either the hidden-residue ledger $L$ or the failure-surface row $F$ cannot claim a closed physical audit packet through $\FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp.FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryAuditNonclosure}
\begin{proof}
Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:finite-observation-residue-boundary-namecert-obligations}. The accepted packet exposes $L$ and $F$ as obligations, not as optional annotations on an already closed audit route.

If $L$ is absent, the export has no displayed row for hidden source content, compression residue, idealization residue, or observer-access limits. If $F$ is absent, the export has no displayed row for finite defeats, domain exits, classifier mismatches, or continuation boundaries.

The remaining rows cannot restore the missing boundary. Transport, replay, provenance, and naming preserve the displayed packet; they do not create a hidden-residue ledger or failure surface. Hence omission of $L$ or $F$ leaves the route outside the closed physical audit packet named here.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite-observation residue boundary audit handoff]
\label{thm:finite-observation-residue-boundary-audit-handoff}
An accepted $\FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp$ packet exports a model-audit handoff only by preserving the finite source row $S$, observation-signature row $\Sigma$, classifier row $K$, approximation-tower row $T$, hidden-residue ledger $L$, failure-surface row $F$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$.

The handoff may be consumed by the model-audit, failure-surface, residue-ledger, and finite-observation siblings named at the start of the chapter, but it has no route that drops either the hidden-residue ledger or the failure-surface row while still claiming a closed physical audit export.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp.FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryAuditHandoff}
\begin{proof}
First use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:finite-observation-residue-boundary-namecert-obligations}. They list the finite source, signature, classifier, approximation tower, hidden-residue ledger, failure surface, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows as the complete local surface of the accepted packet.

Next apply audit non-closure, \autoref{thm:finite-observation-residue-boundary-audit-nonclosure}. A model-audit export that retains $S,\Sigma,K,T$ but omits $L$ or $F$ is not a closed physical audit packet through this boundary, so the audit handoff cannot be formed by a projection that hides residue or defeating observations.

Finally read the sibling roles named in the introduction. $\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$ supplies the model-audit rows, $\RealityConstrainedFailureSurfaceUp$ supplies the finite defeat and continuation-boundary rows, $\OnticResidueLedgerUp$ supplies hidden-material residue, and $\FiniteObservationInterfaceUp$ supplies the finite source window. The accepted packet preserves those roles only through $S,\Sigma,K,T,L,F,H,C,P,N$, so downstream audit exports cite this finite handoff rather than a detached model, residue, or failure-surface route.
\end{proof}
