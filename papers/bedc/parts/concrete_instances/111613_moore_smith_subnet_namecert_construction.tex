Following Moore and Smith on nets and Kelley-style subnet reindexing, the $\MooreSmithSubnetUp$ packet records the standard subnet relation as finite BEDC naming data. It sits between the $\DirectedSubnetUp$ cofinal-tail surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-directedsubnet-namecert}, the Cauchy-facing restriction route of $\CauchySubnetUp$ in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-subnet-namecert}, and the convergence interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-netconvergence-namecert}. The packet names cofinal subnet evidence without selecting a host subnet representative, quotienting nets, invoking countable choice, or importing ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Moore--Smith subnet carrier]
\label{def:moore-smith-subnet-carrier}
A $\MooreSmithSubnetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(I,J,\phi,A,T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the directed source-index row for the original net.
\item $J$ is the directed subnet-index row.
\item $\phi$ is the monotone cofinal map row from $J$ into $I$, read through the $\DirectedSubnetUp$ interface.
\item $A$ is the source net-value row, containing only the finite values inspected by the packet.
\item $T$ is the cofinal tail ledger, recording for each displayed source tail a subnet tail whose image lies inside it.
\item $W$ is the finite observation-window row over $\StreamNameUp$.
\item $D$ is the dyadic tolerance row over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$.
\item $R$ is the regular rational readback row over $\RegSeqRatUp$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal-facing row, exposed only after $I,J,\phi,A,T,W,D,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the directed, cofinal, observation, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from subnet requests through the same finite route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MooreSmithSubnetUp$, $\DirectedSubnetUp$, $\CauchySubnetUp$, $\NetConvergenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MooreSmithSubnetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rows $I,J,\phi,A,T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a selected host subnet, quotient-net equality, a chosen limit point, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith subnet NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:moore-smith-subnet-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MooreSmithSubnetUp$ carrier $M=(I,J,\phi,A,T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MooreSmithSubnetUp}$ surface consists exactly of directed source admission, directed subnet-index admission, monotone cofinal map admission, finite net-value observation, cofinal-tail exactness, $\StreamNameUp$ window stability, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance exactness, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, terminal $\RealUp$ seal ordering, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming for the finite carrier of \autoref{def:moore-smith-subnet-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:moore-smith-subnet-carrier}. The directed rows $I,J$ and the map row $\phi$ supply the Moore--Smith reindexing surface, while $A,T$ record the finite source values and the cofinal tail ledger.

The observation and Real-facing rows are read after that subnet route. The packet exposes $W,D,R,E$ as the StreamName window, dyadic tolerance, regular rational readback, and terminal Real seal, with $E$ unavailable before the finite observation and readback rows have been displayed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since no row carries a selected host subnet, quotient-net equality, selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith subnet cofinality route]
\label{thm:moore-smith-subnet-cofinality-route}
Every convergence-facing or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\MooreSmithSubnetUp$ carrier must read the monotone cofinal map row and the cofinal-tail ledger before it can consume the $\NetConvergenceUp$, $\CauchySubnetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ rows. The public route factors through
$$
I,J,\phi,T \longmapsto W,D,R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with $M=(I,J,\phi,A,T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The subnet data are not represented by an external object; the only available reindexing evidence is the displayed directed pair $I,J$ together with the map row $\phi$.

The cofinality condition is then read through $T$. For each finite source-tail request, $T$ supplies the matching subnet-tail evidence that permits a downstream $\NetConvergenceUp$ or $\CauchySubnetUp$ consumer to follow the same tail route.

Only after the cofinal route has been displayed may the packet expose $W,D,R$ and then the terminal seal $E$. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve this order through $H,C,P,N$, so no consumer can bypass cofinality by reading a selected subnet representative or a host convergence predicate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith subnet nonescape]
\label{thm:moore-smith-subnet-nonescape}
An accepted $\MooreSmithSubnetUp$ packet exports no selected host subnet, quotient-net equality, selected limit, countable-choice coordinate, open-cover compactness principle, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness claim. Every public read remains inside the finite rows of \autoref{def:moore-smith-subnet-carrier} and the sibling surfaces $\DirectedSubnetUp$, $\CauchySubnetUp$, $\NetConvergenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:moore-smith-subnet-namecert-obligations}. The obligations list every row of the accepted carrier and identifies the structural rows as componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming.

Apply \autoref{thm:moore-smith-subnet-cofinality-route}. The downstream route keeps convergence and completion reads behind the displayed cofinal map, tail ledger, observation windows, tolerance row, regular readback, and terminal seal.

The carrier has no coordinate for the excluded host objects. Therefore no structural transport or replay step can synthesize a selected subnet, quotient equality, selected limit, choice coordinate, compactness principle, or ambient completeness claim from the finite Moore--Smith subnet rows.
\end{proof}
