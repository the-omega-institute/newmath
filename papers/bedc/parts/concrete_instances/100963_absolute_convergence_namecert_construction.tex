It sits over the real-series surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realseries-namecert}, the fast tail budget of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fastconvergentseries-namecert}, the Cauchy-majorant schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-majorant-series-namecert}, the uniform Cauchy route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-cauchy-series-namecert}, the regular readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream observations of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the endpoint seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Absolute convergence carrier]\label{def:absolute-convergence-carrier}
An $\AbsoluteConvergenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(T,M,B,U,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the real-valued term-stream row, read only through the finite $\RealSeriesUp$ observations selected by the packet.
\item $M$ is the majorant-stream row, recording the nonnegative comparison terms, displayed majorant partial sums, and the tail index attached to each tolerance request.
\item $B$ is the absolute-domination ledger: each selected term observation from $T$ is paired with its displayed majorant observation from $M$ and the dyadic budget witnessing the absolute comparison.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCauchySeriesUp$ route obtained from the displayed domination ledger and the Cauchy-majorant budget.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the selected term, majorant, partial-sum, and tail-window observations opened by the packet.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, visible only after $T,M,B,U,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the term stream, majorant stream, domination ledger, uniform Cauchy route, readback, Real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from term reads to absolute comparisons, from comparisons to the majorant budget, from the budget to the uniform Cauchy route, from that route to regular readback, and from readback to the Real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AbsoluteConvergenceUp$, $\RealSeriesUp$, $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$, $\CauchyMajorantSeriesUp$, $\UniformCauchySeriesUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AbsoluteConvergenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $T,M,B,U,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for selected sums, quotient stream equality, countable choice, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Absolute convergence NameCert obligations]\label{thm:absolute-convergence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\AbsoluteConvergenceUp$ carrier
$$
A=(T,M,B,U,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\AbsoluteConvergenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the term stream, majorant stream, absolute-domination ledger, uniform Cauchy route, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item term and majorant window exactness, requiring $T$ and $M$ to expose only the selected finite observations and displayed partial-sum ledgers;
\item absolute-domination exactness, requiring $B$ to pair each selected term read with its majorant read and its $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison budget;
\item Cauchy-majorant handoff, requiring $U$ to be read only after the domination ledger and majorant-tail budget have been displayed;
\item regular readback, requiring $R$ to expose only the selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows opened by the term, majorant, domination, and uniform-Cauchy rows;
\item $\RealUp$ seal nonescape, requiring $E$ to be terminal with respect to $T,M,B,U,R$ and to export no selected sum, quotient stream equality, countable choice, trichotomy, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge data;
\item componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming for the same route through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No absolute-convergence obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:absolute-convergence-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the term stream $T$, majorant stream $M$, domination ledger $B$, uniform Cauchy route $U$, regular readback $R$, Real seal $E$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The term and majorant rows provide the only series observations. The domination row $B$ then records the absolute comparison between each selected term observation and its displayed majorant observation, using the dyadic budget already visible to the packet.

The Cauchy-facing rows are downstream of that ledger. The row $U$ consumes the domination ledger and the majorant-tail budget before any uniform Cauchy conclusion is available, and $R$ reads back only the finite windows opened by this route.

Finally, $E,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since selected sums, quotient stream equality, countable choice, trichotomy, ambient completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking do not occur as carrier coordinates, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Absolute convergence Cauchy-majorant handoff]\label{thm:absolute-convergence-cauchy-majorant-handoff}

$$

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Cauchy-facing request. The carrier first exposes the term stream $T$ and the majorant stream $M$, so the request has only selected finite observations of the series and the comparison stream.

The row $B$ is then the unique absolute-domination ledger. It pairs the selected term reads with their displayed majorant reads and the dyadic comparison budget, so the request cannot bypass the majorant route.

The handoff to Cauchy control occurs through $U$ and $R$. The row $U$ records the uniform Cauchy route supported by the Cauchy-majorant budget, while $R$ supplies the regular rational readback for the finite windows opened along that route.

Thus every Completion or Real consumer sees the displayed domination ledger, majorant budget, uniform threshold, selected stream windows, and regular readback before any endpoint-facing seal is available.
\end{proof}
