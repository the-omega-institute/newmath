\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedSubstitutionSealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closedsubstitutionseal-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ClosedSubstitutionSealUp$ interface names the finite MetaCIC boundary at which substitution against a closed-at-depth term is inert. It packages the term, the boundary depth, the closedness classifier, the substitution payload, the shift witness, the substitution witness, recursive constructor routes, package provenance, and local naming data. It exports no hidden free variable, no later binder budget, no semantic evaluator, and no host normalization theorem.

\begin{definition}[ClosedSubstitutionSeal carrier]
\label{def:closed-substitution-seal-carrier}
A $\ClosedSubstitutionSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(d,t,v,B,L,I,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $d$ is the MetaCIC depth at which the boundary is inspected;
\item $t$ is the displayed MetaCIC term whose free de Bruijn indices are classified at $d$;
\item $v$ is the displayed substitution payload, recorded but not consumed by the inert boundary;
\item $B$ is the $\mathsf{ClosedAt}(d,t)$ classifier row;
\item $L$ is the closedness ledger recording that every visible variable index of $t$ lies below $d$;
\item $I$ records the inert substitution read $\mathsf{substitute}(d,v,t)=t$;
\item $R$ records the matching shift boundary read $\mathsf{shift}(d,1,t)=t$ when the same closedness row is used as a context-extension certificate;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $d$, $t$, $v$, $B$, $L$, $I$, and $R$;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes through the MetaCIC constructors $\mathsf{var}$, $\mathsf{app}$, $\mathsf{lam}$, $\mathsf{pi}$, and $\mathsf{sort}$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\mathsf{Term}$, $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$, $\mathsf{shift}$, $\mathsf{substitute}$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedSubstitutionSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an unregistered free variable, a future binder budget, an external evaluator state, a host normalization result, or a semantic equality beyond the displayed MetaCIC term equality.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedSubstitutionSeal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-substitution-seal-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ClosedSubstitutionSealUp}$ obligation surface for \autoref{def:closed-substitution-seal-carrier} consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $S=(d,t,v,B,L,I,R,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier exactness, requiring $B$ to be the MetaCIC $\mathsf{ClosedAt}(d,t)$ row and $L$ to record only variable indices below $d$;
\item inert substitution, requiring the substitution read $I$ to identify $\mathsf{substitute}(d,v,t)$ with $t$ without inspecting $v$;
\item recursive generator routing, requiring the proof routes for $\mathsf{var}$, $\mathsf{app}$, $\mathsf{lam}$, $\mathsf{pi}$, and $\mathsf{sort}$ to replay through the displayed $\Cont$ rows;
\item non-escape, excluding hidden free variables, unrecorded binder budgets, host evaluator states, and semantic normalization claims.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the tuple in \autoref{def:closed-substitution-seal-carrier}. The term, depth, payload, classifier, ledger, inert reads, transport rows, recursive routes, provenance row, and local naming row are all displayed coordinates.

Classifier exactness is the MetaCIC closedness row. The ledger $L$ records precisely the variable-index inequalities supplied by $\mathsf{ClosedAt}(d,t)$, so a variable branch has no visible index at or beyond the boundary.

The inert substitution row is then read from the same classifier. Because the boundary site is absent from a closed-at-depth term, the payload $v$ is recorded as part of the attempted generator action but is never consumed by the term.

The recursive route row replays this reasoning through the constructor list. Applications pass the claim to both subterms, binders raise the boundary together with the closedness row, and sorts close immediately. These rows exhaust the certificate surface and leave no coordinate for a hidden name or external evaluator state.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedSubstitutionSeal inert boundary]
\label{thm:closed-substitution-seal-inert-boundary}
For an accepted $\ClosedSubstitutionSealUp$ carrier $S=(d,t,v,B,L,I,R,H,C,P,N)$, every substitution read at the displayed boundary factors through the classifier row $B$, the variable ledger $L$, the inert read $I$, the transport row $H$, the recursive route row $C$, the package row $P$, and the local naming row $N$. The read returns the original term $t$ and cannot export a free variable at depth $d$, a borrowed binder from a later context, or an inspected value of $v$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only rows mentioning a substitution read are $d$, $t$, $v$, the closedness classifier $B$, the ledger $L$, and the inert read $I$.

Use the classifier exactness clause of \autoref{thm:closed-substitution-seal-namecert-obligations}. Any variable visible in $t$ lies below $d$, so neither the equality branch at $d$ nor the decrement branch above $d$ can be used.

Now replay the constructor route in $C$. Compound terms pass the boundary condition to their subterms, binder cases raise the local boundary together with the classifier, and the sort case has no variable branch. Transport and provenance rows $H$, $P$, and $N$ record that the same displayed surface is being read.

Thus the substitution attempt is inert on the classified slice. The payload $v$ remains a recorded parameter of the generator action, not a source of a new name.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedSubstitutionSeal recursive generator route]
\label{thm:closed-substitution-seal-recursive-generator-route}
The recursive generator route of an accepted $\ClosedSubstitutionSealUp$ carrier is exhausted by the five MetaCIC surface constructors $\mathsf{var}$, $\mathsf{app}$, $\mathsf{lam}$, $\mathsf{pi}$, and $\mathsf{sort}$. Along these routes, $\mathsf{shift}$ and $\mathsf{substitute}$ may traverse the term, but every consumer-visible equality is certified by the displayed $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ row and the corresponding continuation route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the constructor route row $C$. Its cases are exactly the displayed MetaCIC term constructors.

In the variable case, the closedness ledger supplies the inequality placing the index below the inspected boundary. This prevents both shift movement at the cutoff and substitution at the boundary.

In the application case, the route splits into the function and argument subterms. In the binder cases, the body or codomain is checked at the raised boundary, matching the closedness classifier. In the sort case, no variable ledger is needed.

These are all constructor routes available to the carrier. Since each route returns to $B$, $L$, $I$, $R$, $H$, $P$, and $N$, traversal cannot add an evaluator state, hidden syntax form, or unregistered binder.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedSubstitutionSeal ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:closed-substitution-seal-ledger-exhaustion}
Every downstream self-compilation use of an accepted $\ClosedSubstitutionSealUp$ carrier reads only the displayed term $t$, boundary $d$, closedness row $B$, variable ledger $L$, inert substitution row $I$, shift boundary row $R$, constructor routes $C$, transport row $H$, package provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. It cannot strengthen the packet into a full evaluator correctness theorem, a normalization theorem, or a certificate for substitution on open terms.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the downstream read. A self-compilation consumer needs only the fact that the evaluator's substitution attempt does not move a closed-at-boundary source fragment outside its registered name ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:closed-substitution-seal-inert-boundary}. The substitution read factors through $B$, $L$, $I$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$ and returns the original term.

Apply \autoref{thm:closed-substitution-seal-recursive-generator-route}. The only generator paths are the five MetaCIC constructors, and the shift row $R$ records the matching context-extension boundary for the same classifier.

The carrier therefore supplies a finite seal for the local self-compilation step. It does not contain a coordinate for full evaluator correctness, host normalization, or substitution behavior on open terms.
\end{proof}
