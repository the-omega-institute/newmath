\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompactMetricContinuousImageUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-compact-metric-continuous-image-namecert}
\origin{human}

Bishop-style compact metric reasoning treats the image of a compact metric source under a continuous map through finite approximations: choose a source net, route the requested target tolerance through the displayed continuity modulus, and read the image centers by evaluating the displayed map on the source centers. The $\CompactMetricContinuousImageUp$ packet records exactly that finite route. It sits over the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the continuous-map graph row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the target metric-space observations of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the uniform-modulus consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}, and the total-bounded image handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-continuous-image-total-bounded-namecert}. The packet names finite image-net witnesses without claiming open-cover compactness, a host image subset, or a selected inverse image.

\begin{definition}[Compact metric continuous image carrier]
\label{def:compact-metric-continuous-image-carrier}
A $\CompactMetricContinuousImageUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(K,F,M,U,E,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, exposing only the displayed finite source-net data, source radii, and compact-metric provenance.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row from the source carrier to the target carrier.
\item $M$ is the target $\MetricSpaceUp$ observation row used to compare the displayed target names.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformModulusUp$ row routing each target tolerance to a source radius for the same displayed map row.
\item $E$ is the finite source-net ledger read from $K$ at the source radius supplied by $U$.
\item $T$ is the finite target-image ledger whose centers are the displayed $F$-readbacks of the centers in $E$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact source, map row, metric observation row, modulus row, source-net ledger, target-image ledger, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a target-image request through $K,F,M,U,E,T,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactMetricContinuousImageUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\CompactContinuousImageTotalBoundedUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactMetricContinuousImageUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rows $K,F,M,U,E,T,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, an ambient image subset, selected preimages, quotient equality of target names, choice-selected centers, or target completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact metric continuous image obligations]
\label{thm:compact-metric-continuous-image-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactMetricContinuousImageUp$ carrier $I=(K,F,M,U,E,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactMetricContinuousImageUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, compact source-net accountability, continuous-map graph routing, target metric observation, uniform-modulus compatibility, finite image-net exactness, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:compact-metric-continuous-image-carrier}. The mathematical rows are $K,F,M,U,E,T$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

The source-net read is governed by $K$ and $U$. A target tolerance is first sent through the uniform-modulus row $U$, and only then may the source-net ledger $E$ be read from the compact metric source $K$.

The target-image ledger is governed by $F,M,E,T$. Each center in $T$ is the displayed value of the continuous-map graph row $F$ at a center already listed in $E$, with target comparisons read through the metric observation row $M$.

Transport and replay preserve that same finite route. Since the excluded open-cover, host-image, selected-preimage, quotient-equality, choice, and completeness objects do not occur among the rows, no local obligation can export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite image-net handoff]
\label{thm:compact-metric-continuous-image-finite-net-handoff}
Every compactness-facing or total-boundedness-facing consumer of an accepted $\CompactMetricContinuousImageUp$ packet receives only the finite route
$$
\CompactMetricUp,\quad
\ContinuousMapUp,\quad
\MetricSpaceUp,\quad
\UniformModulusUp,\quad
\CompactContinuousImageTotalBoundedUp .
$$
The exported witness is the finite target-image ledger $T$ obtained from $E$ along $F$ and $U$; it is not an open-cover compactness theorem, not a host image-set construction, and not a choice-selected family of target centers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a target-image request. The carrier forces the request through $U$, which supplies a source radius for the same displayed continuous-map row $F$.

Read the compact metric source row $K$ at that radius to obtain the finite source-net ledger $E$. No open cover or arbitrary subcover selector is inspected.

Apply the displayed graph row $F$ to the centers in $E$ and compare the resulting target names through $M$. The resulting finite list is exactly the target-image ledger $T$.

Rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name this route without adding another coordinate. Thus the handoff can be consumed by $\CompactContinuousImageTotalBoundedUp$ as finite image-net data, but not as ambient image compactness or a selected inverse-image construction.
\end{proof}
