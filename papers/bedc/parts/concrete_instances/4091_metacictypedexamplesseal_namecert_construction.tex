\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetaCICTypedExamplesSealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-typedexamples-seal-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\MetaCICTypedExamplesSealUp$ packet turns the typed-example audit map of \autoref{ch:metacic-typedexamples-audit-map} into a finite seal that can be read by downstream MetaCIC checker consumers. It sits between the bounded decidability boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicdecidableboundary-namecert} and the bounded normal-equality checker of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-normal-equality-checker-namecert}: those chapters expose the finite checker and normal-comparison rows, while the present packet seals the worked gallery rows that exercise them.

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC TypedExamples seal carrier]
\label{def:metacic-typedexamples-seal-carrier}
A $\MetaCICTypedExamplesSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(E,T,C,B,D,Q,R,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the finite worked-example row selected from the typed-example audit map: Church encodings, identity rows, applications, projections, eliminator examples, constants, Pi rows, and sort-context rows;
\item $T$ is the displayed typing-checker row for the examples that are accepted by the finite gallery;
\item $C$ is the closedness and context-formation row consumed by those checker reads;
\item $B$ is the selected beta and readback row for examples whose evaluation behavior is part of the worked gallery;
\item $D$ is the bounded decidability row imported from the $\MetaCICDecidableBoundaryUp$ surface;
\item $Q$ is the bounded normal-equality checker row imported from the $\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp$ surface;
\item $R$ is the finite seal ledger recording which gallery families are accepted, which computations are named, and which checker or normal-equality rows they are allowed to read;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports across $E,T,C,B,D,Q,R,P,N$;
\item $K$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes for replaying gallery entry reads, checker reads, closedness reads, beta reads, bounded decidability reads, normal-equality reads, and seal-ledger reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:metacic-typedexamples-audit-map}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicdecidableboundary-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-normal-equality-checker-namecert};
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICTypedExamplesSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an arbitrary MetaCIC term, no global subject-reduction theorem, no strong normalization theorem, no global conversion decider, no proof-erasure correctness theorem, and no host evaluator.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC TypedExamples seal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-typedexamples-seal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-typedexamples-seal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICTypedExamplesSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $S=(E,T,C,B,D,Q,R,H,K,P,N)$, gallery-entry visibility through $E$, typing-checker visibility through $T$, closedness and context visibility through $C$, selected computation visibility through $B$, bounded decidability visibility through $D$, bounded normal-equality visibility through $Q$, finite seal-ledger exactness through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $K$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The rows $E,T,C,B,D,Q,R$ are the entire worked-example surface, and the rows $H,K,P,N$ are the entire structural surface available to a consumer.

The gallery-entry obligation reads $E$ only for the families selected by \autoref{ch:metacic-typedexamples-audit-map}. The checker obligation reads $T$ under the closedness and context row $C$, while the computation obligation reads $B$ only for the examples whose beta or readback behavior is named in the finite ledger.

The bounded decidability and normal-equality obligations read $D$ and $Q$ through the sibling packets of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicdecidableboundary-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-normal-equality-checker-namecert}. The seal ledger $R$ records which of those sibling rows each worked example may consume.

Finally, $H,K,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name only the displayed rows. Since the carrier contains no further coordinate, the local NameCert surface is exhausted by the listed obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC TypedExamples seal non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-typedexamples-seal-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\MetaCICTypedExamplesSealUp$ packet reads only the finite rows
$$
E,T,C,B,D,Q,R,H,K,P,N.
$$
The consumer may use those rows as a worked-example seal for the typed-example gallery and may route selected checker or bounded normal-equality reads through the named sibling packets. It receives no theorem about all represented MetaCIC terms, no quotient equality of terms, no unrestricted beta-conversion decision procedure, no selected normalization endpoint, no proof-erased compiler theorem, and no host evaluator.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier definition. The tuple lists all gallery, checker, closedness, computation, bounded decidability, normal-equality, ledger, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows exposed by the packet.

Any checker or normal-equality read must pass through the finite seal ledger $R$. That ledger points to the bounded decidability and bounded normal-equality sibling surfaces, so the consumer cannot replace the selected gallery rows by an arbitrary MetaCIC term or a global conversion oracle.

The structural rows $H,K,P,N$ preserve componentwise agreement, replay the same finite routes, record provenance, and name the packet. They do not add a quotient of terms, a normalization selector, a proof-erased compiler route, or a host evaluator.

Thus the accepted reading is exactly the finite worked-example seal. All stronger metatheoretic endpoints are absent from the carrier and cannot be exported by a downstream consumer.
\end{proof}
