\label{thm:epsilon-delta-limit-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The tolerance part is finite. The epsilon request $E$ and delta witness $D$ are $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows, so the smallness comparison is a dyadic ledger rather than a host real inequality or selected limit object.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since no carrier coordinate stores host real equality, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, choice, or ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
