Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis and Kelley metric-space terminology, the $\LipschitzContinuityHandoffUp$ packet records the textbook handoff from a displayed Lipschitz bound to a uniform continuity modulus before regular-Cauchy or Real-facing consumers read the result. It is grounded in the bound-carrying $\LipschitzMapUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lipschitzmap-namecert}, the metric rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the graph row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the induced modulus row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}, the regular-rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the continuity handoff consumed by L10 Real and Completion routes; it does not duplicate the upstream Lipschitz map carrier or turn that carrier into a complete downstream theorem.

\begin{definition}[Lipschitz continuity handoff carrier]
\label{def:lipschitz-carrier}
A $\LipschitzContinuityHandoffUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(L,X,Y,G,B,U,Q,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the upstream $\LipschitzMapUp$ row, read through the carrier package of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lipschitzmap-namecert}.
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the source and target $\MetricSpaceUp$ rows used by the displayed distance comparison.
\item $G$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row carried by the same Lipschitz packet.
\item $B$ is the displayed rational bound ledger used to compare source and target distances.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformModulusUp$ row induced by the bound ledger.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row available to regular-Cauchy consumers of the mapped readings.
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, consumed only after $L,X,Y,G,B,U,Q$ have fixed the handoff.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the Lipschitz row, metric rows, graph, bound ledger, modulus, regular readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a continuity request through $L,X,Y,G,B,U,Q,R,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LipschitzContinuityHandoffUp$, $\LipschitzMapUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LipschitzContinuityHandoffUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted handoff packets only through $L,X,Y,G,B,U,Q,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host function extensionality, quotient map equality, choice of representatives, selected limits, countable choice, differentiability, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Lipschitz continuity handoff NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:lipschitz-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LipschitzContinuityHandoffUp$ carrier
$$
K=(L,X,Y,G,B,U,Q,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LipschitzContinuityHandoffUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier exposure for the upstream $\LipschitzMapUp$ row, source and target $\MetricSpaceUp$ rows, $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row, rational Lipschitz-bound ledger, induced $\UniformModulusUp$ row, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row, terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:lipschitz-carrier}. The displayed tuple contains one upstream Lipschitz row, two metric rows, one graph row, one bound ledger, one induced modulus row, one regular-rational handoff row, one Real seal row, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The analytic route is inherited from the upstream $\LipschitzMapUp$ carrier rather than duplicated. The source and target metric rows, graph row, and bound ledger are read from $L$, and the modulus row $U$ is the displayed uniform-modulus handoff induced by that same ledger.

The downstream rows $Q$ and $R$ are sealed only after the finite handoff through $L,X,Y,G,B,U$ is visible. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host function extensionality, quotient map equality, representative choice, selected limits, countable choice, differentiability, or ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lipschitz bound to uniform modulus handoff]
\label{thm:lipschitz-uniform-modulus-handoff}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\LipschitzContinuityHandoffUp$ carrier must first read the upstream $\LipschitzMapUp$ row, source and target $\MetricSpaceUp$ rows, $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row, rational bound ledger, and induced $\UniformModulusUp$ row. Only then may it consume the regular-rational handoff row or terminal Real seal row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted handoff packet and project the route in \autoref{def:lipschitz-carrier}. The only entrance to the continuity handoff is the upstream Lipschitz row $L$, and its metric, graph, and bound coordinates are displayed as $X,Y,G,B$.

The uniform modulus row $U$ is downstream of that same bound ledger. Thus a regular-Cauchy consumer receives the modulus as a finite handoff from the Lipschitz bound rather than as a selected host-function modulus.

The rows $Q$ and $R$ are downstream seals. A consumer that reads either row has already exposed $L,X,Y,G,B,U$ and the structural transport and replay rows, so it cannot bypass the route by host function extensionality, quotient map equality, choice of representatives, selected limits, countable choice, differentiability, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}
