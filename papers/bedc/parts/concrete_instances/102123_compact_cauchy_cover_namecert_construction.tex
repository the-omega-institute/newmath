\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompactCauchyCoverUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-compact-cauchy-cover-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on totally bounded metric data and constructive Cauchy approximation, the $\CompactCauchyCoverUp$ packet records the finite cover-window layer that can feed a regular-Cauchy real route without importing open-cover compactness. It reads the compact metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the finite stream-window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the rational regular sequence handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet gives L10 Real and Completion arguments a finite route from displayed cover windows to Cauchy data while leaving selected subcovers, quotient stream equality, choice, and detached $\RealUp$ completeness outside the certificate.

\begin{definition}[Compact Cauchy cover carrier]
\label{def:compact-cauchy-cover-carrier}
A $\CompactCauchyCoverUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(M,W,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, including the carried metric endpoints, metric classifier, finite total-boundedness windows, and visible compact-metric provenance.
\item $W$ is the finite cover-window row. For each inspected precision request it lists finitely many displayed metric windows, their centers, and the local membership probes used by the Cauchy-facing route.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius ledger, storing the dyadic cover radii, shrinkage comparisons, overlap tolerances, and the monotone budget used when windows are refined.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row, exposing the finite order in which accepted cover windows are read as stream data.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, obtained only after $W,D,S$ have fixed the window, radius, and stream schedule data.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached by the regular-Cauchy readback and never as an independent real-completion coordinate.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact metric row, cover windows, dyadic radius ledger, stream schedule, regular readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the finite route
$$
M \longmapsto W \longmapsto D \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactCauchyCoverUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactCauchyCoverUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,W,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected finite subcovers, quotient stream equality, countable choice, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, host metric completion, or selected representatives.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact Cauchy cover NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-cauchy-cover-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactCauchyCoverUp$ carrier
$$
K=(M,W,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CompactCauchyCoverUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the compact metric source row, finite cover-window row, dyadic radius ledger, stream schedule, regular-Cauchy readback, Real seal, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$;
\item cover-window finiteness, requiring each inspected precision request to expose only the finitely listed windows and membership probes stored in $W$;
\item dyadic radius discipline, requiring every refinement, overlap read, and tolerance claim to use the ledger $D$ rather than an ambient compactness principle;
\item stream schedule readiness, requiring $S$ to display the finite order in which cover windows become stream data before $R$ is read;
\item regular-Cauchy handoff, requiring $R$ to be the only route by which the cover-window data becomes a $\RegSeqRatUp$ row;
\item Real-seal factorization, requiring every downstream read of $E$ to pass through $M,W,D,S,R$ and the structural rows;
\item transport, replay, and provenance non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve and name exactly the displayed rows, with no open-cover compactness, selected subcover, quotient stream equality, choice, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, host metric completion, or selected representative.
\end{enumerate}
No compact-Cauchy-cover obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $K=(M,W,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. Carrier admission exposes exactly the compact metric source, the finite cover windows, the dyadic radius ledger, the stream schedule, the regular-Cauchy readback, the Real seal, and the four structural rows.

The cover-window obligation is finite because $W$ is a displayed row of the packet. A precision request may inspect only the centers, windows, and membership probes listed in that row, so it cannot invoke an open-cover compactness theorem or choose a hidden subcover.

The dyadic discipline is the projection to $D$. Each shrinkage comparison, radius bound, and overlap tolerance used by a refinement is read from the dyadic ledger before any stream or regular-Cauchy coordinate is consumed.

The stream and regular obligations are the projections to $S$ and $R$. The stream schedule fixes the finite read order of the windows, and the regular readback is downstream of that schedule rather than a detached representative.

Finally, the Real seal and structural rows exhaust the boundary. The seal $E$ is reached only after $M,W,D,S,R$, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this same finite route. Hence the excluded compactness, selection, quotient, choice, completion, and representative coordinates cannot be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact Cauchy cover regular handoff]
\label{thm:compact-cauchy-cover-regular-handoff}
For every accepted $\CompactCauchyCoverUp$ carrier
$$
K=(M,W,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer exported by $K$ factors through the displayed finite route
$$
\CompactMetricUp \longrightarrow \CompactCauchyCoverUp \longrightarrow \StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \RealUp .
$$
The handoff supplies only cover windows, dyadic radii, stream schedule data, regular readback, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row. It does not supply open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, quotient stream equality, choice, detached $\RealUp$ completeness, host metric completion, or selected representatives.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a downstream consumer of $R$ or $E$. The carrier projection shows that $R$ is available only after the compact metric row $M$, finite cover windows $W$, dyadic ledger $D$, and stream schedule $S$ have been displayed.

The finite route is therefore forced. The compact metric row supplies the metric and total-boundedness surface, $W$ records the windows inspected at the requested precision, $D$ records the radii and tolerance comparisons, and $S$ orders those windows as stream data.

The regular readback row $R$ packages that stream data as the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff. The terminal seal $E$ is downstream of $R$, so a Real-facing consumer receives a sealed Cauchy readback rather than an independent completeness theorem.

Componentwise transport and replay through $H$ and $C$ preserve the same route, while $P$ and $N$ name only the displayed packet. Since the carrier inventory has no open-cover, selected-subcover, quotient-stream, choice, detached-completeness, host-completion, or representative coordinate, none can be consumed downstream.
\end{proof}
