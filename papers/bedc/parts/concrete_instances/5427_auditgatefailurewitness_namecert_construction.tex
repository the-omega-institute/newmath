It sits beside the pass/refusal ledgers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditgateboundary-namecert}, the composed gate surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditgatecomposition-namecert}, the apophatic refusal packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-negativecertificateboundary-namecert}, and the gate-question packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticgatequestion-namecert}. Those siblings expose questions, boundary ledgers, composition routes, or negative certificates; this packet records the concrete diagnostic witness for a gate row whose boundary item fails.

\begin{definition}[Audit-gate failure-witness carrier]
\label{def:audit-gate-failure-witness-carrier}
An $\AuditGateFailureWitnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(G,F,R,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the audit-gate row whose finite boundary check is being read;
\item $F$ is the failed boundary item, recording the source token, proof-ancestry dependency, unresolved target, origin/import crossing, or other gate-visible item that did not pass;
\item $R$ is the refusal target attached to $F$, namely the theorem-content route, host supply, marker resolution, or internalizer that the gate refuses to admit;
\item $D$ is the diagnostic readback row, recording the exact finite reason by which $G$ classifies $F$ as a failed item and attaches the refusal to $R$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the gate row, failed item, refusal target, diagnostic readback, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes from $G$ to $F$, from $F$ to $D$, from $D$ to $R$, and from the local witness to any composed refusal handoff;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance naming $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$, $\AuditGateCompositionUp$, $\NegativeCertificateBoundaryUp$, $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ source packets;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AuditGateFailureWitnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports a failure witness for the displayed gate row. It exports no route by which the failed item becomes theorem content and no replacement diagnostic outside the displayed readback row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Audit-gate failure-witness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:audit-gate-failure-witness-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\AuditGateFailureWitnessUp$ carrier
$$
W=(G,F,R,D,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\AuditGateFailureWitnessUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly the displayed $\BHist$ packet $W$ with gate row, failed boundary item, refusal target, diagnostic readback, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item diagnostic exactness: the row $D$ is the only diagnostic readback for $F$ under $G$, and it records the finite gate-facing reason for failure before any consumer reaches the refusal target;
\item refusal attachment: the row $R$ is attached to the pair $(F,D)$ rather than to an anonymous gate outcome, so the refusal cannot be detached from the concrete failed boundary item;
\item replay visibility: every public replay of the witness factors through the displayed routes in $C$, including the route from $G$ to $F$, the diagnostic route from $F$ to $D$, and the refusal route from $D$ to $R$;
\item non-internalization: the packet exposes the failed verdict and its diagnostic, but it exports no theorem-content object, host supply, marker repair, admitted internalizer, or gate-bypass route for $F$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:audit-gate-failure-witness-carrier}. The tuple exposes exactly the rows $G,F,R,D,H,C,P,N$, so an accepted witness has no hidden item beyond the displayed finite packet.

Diagnostic exactness is read from the role of $D$. The diagnostic is attached to $F$ under the gate row $G$, and the continuation route reaches $D$ before it can reach the refusal target. Thus a consumer cannot read the failure as an anonymous refusal.

Refusal attachment then follows by reading $R$ after $D$. The refused target is paired with the failed item and diagnostic row, so the refusal cannot be moved to a different boundary item without leaving the carrier.

Replay visibility is confined to $C$, while componentwise transport is confined to $H$. The package and local-name rows $P$ and $N$ bind these visible routes as a local NameCert surface.

Finally, non-internalization follows from the carrier inventory. No coordinate of $W$ supplies theorem content for $F$, repairs the marker or host supply, admits the refused internalizer, or bypasses the gate diagnostic.
