\begin{definition}[Audit-gate failure-witness carrier]
\label{def:audit-gate-failure-witness-carrier}
An $\AuditGateFailureWitnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(G,F,R,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the audit-gate row whose finite boundary check is being read;
\item $F$ is the failed boundary item, recording the source token, proof-ancestry dependency, unresolved target, origin/import crossing, or other gate-visible item that did not pass;
\item $R$ is the refusal target attached to $F$, namely the theorem-content route, host supply, marker resolution, or internalizer that the gate refuses to admit;
\item $D$ is the diagnostic readback row, recording the exact finite reason by which $G$ classifies $F$ as a failed item and attaches the refusal to $R$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the gate row, failed item, refusal target, diagnostic readback, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes from $G$ to $F$, from $F$ to $D$, from $D$ to $R$, and from the local witness to any composed refusal handoff;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance naming $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$, $\AuditGateCompositionUp$, $\NegativeCertificateBoundaryUp$, $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ source packets;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AuditGateFailureWitnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports a failure witness for the displayed gate row. It exports no route by which the failed item becomes theorem content and no replacement diagnostic outside the displayed readback row.
\end{definition}
