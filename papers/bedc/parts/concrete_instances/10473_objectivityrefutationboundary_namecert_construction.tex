The $\ObjectivityRefutationBoundaryUp$ packet realizes the negative test implicit in the reality-constrained objectivity vision of \autoref{sec:objectivity-without-privileged-observer}. It sits beside the positive $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-objectivity-namecert}, the relational objectivity carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-relational-objectivity-namecert}, the no-global-synchronization refusal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-noglobalsyncboundary-namecert}, the observer-frame refusal surface, and the inter-history invariant surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interhistinvariant-namecert}. Those siblings certify positive invariant routes or refusal-only boundaries; this packet records the finite failed-invariant and failed-anchor-stability rows that make an asserted objectivity claim refutable.

\begin{definition}[Objectivity refutation boundary carrier]
\label{def:objectivity-refutation-boundary-carrier}
An $\ObjectivityRefutationBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(H,K,A,W,R,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the finite multi-observer packet over which an objectivity claim is asserted;
\item $K$ is the failed inter-observer invariant row, recording that no displayed $\InterHistInvariantUp$ row preserves the asserted claim across the observer packet;
\item $A$ is the failed anchor-stability row, recording that no anchor-change table transports the claim without selecting a privileged observer;
\item $W$ is the witness row naming the concrete claim, observer slots, missing invariant coordinates, and missing anchor-stability coordinates;
\item $R$ is the refusal ledger, routing the failed claim through $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$ and $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$ rather than through a positive objectivity certificate;
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay for the failed-invariant, failed-anchor, witness, refusal, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ObjectivityRefutationBoundaryUp$, $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$, $\RelationalObjectivityUp$, $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$, $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$, $\InterHistInvariantUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObjectivityRefutationBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a positive invariant certificate, anchor-stable objectivity export, global synchronization map, privileged observer, quotient of observer histories, or hidden host-side truth value.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Objectivity refutation boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:objectivity-refutation-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:objectivity-refutation-boundary-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObjectivityRefutationBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
B=(H,K,A,W,R,T,P,N),
$$
failed-invariant exposure through $K$, failed anchor-stability exposure through $A$, concrete witness display through $W$, refusal-ledger routing through $R$, componentwise transport and replay through $T$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$. An asserted objectivity claim over the packet $H$ fails this boundary whenever no inter-observer invariant and no anchor-stability row are displayed.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ObjectivityRefutationBoundaryUp.ObjectivityRefutationBoundaryCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:objectivity-refutation-boundary-carrier}. The rows $H,K,A,W,R,T,P,N$ are the only rows available to the local certificate.

The failed-invariant row $K$ and failed-anchor row $A$ are the decisive certificate data. If both are absent as positive rows, the witness row $W$ names the claim and the observer slots where the attempted objectivity export lacks support.

The refusal ledger $R$ then routes the packet through the no-global-synchronization and observer-frame-refusal surfaces rather than through positive objectivity. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are supplied by $T,P,N$, so every obligation is one of the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Objectivity refutation boundary non-escape]
\label{thm:objectivity-refutation-boundary-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\ObjectivityRefutationBoundaryUp$ packet factors through the finite rows
$$
H,K,A,W,R,T,P,N.
$$
The consumer may report the asserted claim as refuted for the displayed multi-observer packet, but it cannot convert the failed-invariant row or failed-anchor-stability row into a positive $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ export, a $\RelationalObjectivityUp$ certificate, a global synchronization map, a privileged observer frame, a quotient stream equality, or a host-side truth value.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to $B=(H,K,A,W,R,T,P,N)$. The only claim-facing rows are the failed invariant $K$, failed anchor-stability row $A$, concrete witness $W$, and refusal ledger $R$.

Apply the NameCert obligations. They identify a refutation boundary precisely when the asserted objectivity claim has no displayed inter-observer invariant and no displayed anchor-stability row.

No coordinate of the packet is a positive objectivity certificate, global synchronization map, privileged frame, quotient equality, or host truth value. A consumer that exports such data has therefore left the finite packet rather than reading the accepted boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Objectivity refutation boundary sibling independence]
\label{thm:objectivity-refutation-boundary-sibling-independence}
The $\ObjectivityRefutationBoundaryUp$ packet is not bijective to $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$, $\RelationalObjectivityUp$, $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$, $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$, or $\InterHistInvariantUp$. The positive objectivity siblings carry invariant and anchor-stability rows rather than failed rows; the refusal-only siblings carry synchronization or frame refusals without the failed-invariant witness; and $\InterHistInvariantUp$ carries invariant readback rather than the paired absence of invariant and anchor-stability evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the carrier rows. $\RealityConstrainedObjectivityUp$ and $\RelationalObjectivityUp$ include positive invariant and anchor-stability surfaces, while $\ObjectivityRefutationBoundaryUp$ records the failed invariant row $K$, failed anchor-stability row $A$, and concrete witness row $W$.

The no-global-synchronization and observer-frame refusal siblings supply refusal ledgers, but they do not name a failed objectivity claim, the missing invariant coordinates, and the missing anchor-stability coordinates in one packet. They are refusal-only boundaries rather than objectivity-refutation witnesses.

Finally, $\InterHistInvariantUp$ supplies a positive invariant readback surface. Removing that readback and replacing it by the failure rows $K,A,W$ changes the carrier shape and the consumer obligation surface, so no bijection preserving carrier rows, refusal ledger, transport, provenance, and local naming identifies the packets.
\end{proof}
