\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-completionembedding-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, and the Bourbaki treatment of completion by separated metric envelopes, the $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ packet names the dense isometric embedding surface of completion as finite BEDC data. It sits after the metric-completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the displayed isometry route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-isometric-embedding-namecert}, and the regular Cauchy dense embedding boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-dense-embedding-namecert}. The packet binds those sibling surfaces before any downstream consumer treats a source point, regular Cauchy name, or Real seal as a point of a completed host space.

\begin{definition}[CompletionEmbedding carrier]
\label{def:completion-embedding-carrier}
A $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(X,Y,D,I,R,S,A,L,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source metric row whose point classifier and distance witnesses are the domain of the embedding.
\item $Y$ is the completion target row, read through the finite $\MetricCompletionUp$ surface rather than through an ambient complete metric space.
\item $D$ is the dense image row recording that every displayed completion-facing tolerance request is met by a source-side image window.
\item $I$ is the isometry row, read through $\IsometricEmbeddingUp$ distance reflection and not through host function equality.
\item $R$ is the regular-Cauchy source row supplying the finite rational or regular source names used to approach target reads.
\item $S$ is the Hausdorff separated boundary row, comparing two target reads only through displayed zero-distance classifier evidence.
\item $A$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached by the regular Cauchy and distance-reflection routes.
\item $L$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source metric, completion target, dense image, isometry, regular-Cauchy source, Hausdorff boundary, Real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay through $X,Y,D,I,R,S,A,L,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\IsometricEmbeddingUp$, $\RegularCauchyDenseEmbeddingUp$, $\HausdorffCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionEmbeddingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completions, selected representatives, host equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionEmbedding NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:completion-embedding-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ carrier $E=(X,Y,D,I,R,S,A,L,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionEmbeddingUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed source metric, completion target, dense image, isometry, regular-Cauchy source, Hausdorff separated boundary, Real seal, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item dense-image coverage, requiring each completion-facing tolerance request in $Y$ to route through the image row $D$ and a regular-Cauchy source read in $R$;
\item isometric reflection, requiring each source-target distance comparison to pass through $I$ and then through the $\RealUp$ seal row $A$;
\item Hausdorff separated comparison, requiring any identification of two target reads to pass through $S$ after the dense and isometric rows have been displayed;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that no quotient completion, selected representative, host equality, countable-choice schedule, or ambient completeness theorem is exported by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:completion-embedding-carrier}. The tuple lists the source, target, dense-image, isometry, regular-Cauchy, Hausdorff, Real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

Dense-image coverage reads a completion-facing tolerance request in $Y$ through $D$ before a target read is accepted. The row $R$ supplies the finite regular-Cauchy source data used by that image window.

Isometric reflection is then read through $I$ and $A$. The isometry row preserves the source distance route, while the Real seal row records the displayed distance comparison consumed by later completion readers.

Hausdorff separated comparison is local to $S$. The continuation row $C$ replays the already displayed dense and isometric route before $S$ compares target reads, and the package row $P$ and naming row $N$ record that no excluded completion or choice principle is available.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompletionEmbeddingUp.CompletionEmbeddingCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionEmbedding dense isometric handoff]
\label{thm:completion-embedding-dense-isometric-handoff}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
X\longmapsto R\longmapsto D\longmapsto I\longmapsto A\longmapsto Y\longmapsto S\longmapsto(L,C,P,N).
$$
Thus the consumer receives a dense source image and a distance-reflecting route into the completion target, not a quotient-completion point, not a selected representative, not host equality, and not an ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the source metric row $X$ and the regular-Cauchy row $R$. These two rows provide the displayed source-side data from which the completion-facing image can be read.

The dense image row $D$ is the next route component. A consumer cannot read a target point from $Y$ before the dense source image has been displayed through $D$.

The isometry row $I$ and Real seal row $A$ then attach distance preservation to that image. The target row $Y$ is reached only after this dense isometric route has been recorded.

Finally the Hausdorff row $S$ compares target reads through separated zero-distance evidence, while $L,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since the route has no field for quotient classes, selected representatives, host equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness, the handoff exports only the displayed dense isometric surface.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompletionEmbeddingUp.CompletionEmbeddingCarrier\_dense\_isometric\_handoff}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionEmbedding Real seal non-escape]
\label{thm:completion-embedding-real-seal-nonescape}
For every accepted $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ packet $E=(X,Y,D,I,R,S,A,L,C,P,N)$, each $\RealUp$ seal-facing read exported by $E$ factors through the regular-Cauchy source row $R$, the dense image row $D$, the isometry row $I$, and the Hausdorff separated boundary row $S$. The Real seal cannot be used as a detached completed point, quotient-class representative, host equality witness, countable-choice selection, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the Real seal coordinate $A$ from the carrier. It is listed after the regular-Cauchy source row, dense image row, isometry row, and Hausdorff separated boundary row, so the packet exposes it only as part of that displayed route.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:completion-embedding-namecert-obligations}. Dense-image coverage places $R$ and $D$ before any completion-facing read, isometric reflection places $I$ before the Real distance comparison, and separated comparison places $S$ around target-read identification.

The replay and provenance rows $C$ and $P$ record no second Real route. Transport by $L$ preserves the same fields componentwise, and the local naming row $N$ names exactly this packet.

Therefore any exported $\RealUp$ read remains inside $R,D,I,S,A,L,C,P,N$. The excluded detached point, quotient representative, host equality, choice schedule, and ambient completeness data have no coordinate from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompletionEmbeddingUp.CompletionEmbeddingCarrier\_real\_seal\_nonescape}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionEmbedding source-density reflection]
\label{thm:completion-embedding-source-density-reflection}
For every accepted $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ packet $E=(X,Y,D,I,R,S,A,L,C,P,N)$, any completion-target read that the packet classifies back to a source-side approximation factors through the regular-Cauchy source row $R$, dense image row $D$, isometry row $I$, $\RealUp$ seal row $A$, and Hausdorff separated boundary row $S$. The resulting comparison is a local classifier reflection recorded by $L,C,P,N$, not host equality, quotient equality, selected representatives, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompletionEmbeddingUp.CompletionEmbeddingCarrier\_source\_density\_reflection}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the accepted carrier and its NameCert obligations. The dense-image coverage obligation requires a completion-facing tolerance read in $Y$ to pass first through the source-side regular-Cauchy data in $R$ and the dense image window in $D$.

Use the dense isometric handoff of \autoref{thm:completion-embedding-dense-isometric-handoff} to extend that source-density route through $I$ and $A$. The isometry row supplies the distance-reflecting comparison, and the $\RealUp$ seal records the displayed real-valued boundary consumed by completion readers.

The Real-seal non-escape theorem, \autoref{thm:completion-embedding-real-seal-nonescape}, keeps that comparison inside the same finite route. The separated boundary row $S$ is therefore the only target-read comparison surface available after the dense and isometric rows have been replayed.

Finally, $L,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the reflected classifier route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host equality, quotient equality, selected representatives, countable choice, or ambient completeness, the source-density read exports only the local reflection described above.
\end{proof}
