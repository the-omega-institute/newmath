\begin{definition}[Oracle-augmented substrate carrier]
\label{def:oracle-augmented-substrate-carrier}
An $\OracleAugmentedSubstrateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(S,A,T,B,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the universal substrate row, naming the internal finite rule, machine, or simulation surface whose ordinary steps are read as substrate-local records.
\item $A$ is the oracle-call row, recording the finite query shape, query name, and call boundary by which the substrate asks a source not generated by $S$.
\item $T$ is the oracle-transcript row, recording the finite reply ledger used by the consumer, including the reply token and the local point at which it is consumed.
\item $B$ is the open-boundary row, recording that $A$ and $T$ are not closed orbit records of $S$ and cannot be replayed as a cellular ledger.
\item $E$ is the quadrant-evidence row, recording universality from the substrate row and non-closure from the oracle-call and transcript boundary.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,A,T,B,E,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes through substrate-local steps, oracle calls, transcript reads, the open-boundary check, and the final quadrant read.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\OracleAugmentedSubstrateUp$, the quadrant map, the cellular and halting siblings, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\OracleAugmentedSubstrateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for converting the oracle transcript into a closed cellular orbit, no hidden halting oracle outside the displayed call row, no ambient proof of hypercomputation, and no closure certificate for the open boundary.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Oracle-augmented substrate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:oracle-augmented-substrate-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:oracle-augmented-substrate-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\OracleAugmentedSubstrateUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for $O=(S,A,T,B,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item oracle-call accountability, requiring every oracle read to cite $A$ before a reply can be consumed;
\item transcript accountability, requiring every reply token to cite $T$ and its consumption point;
\item open-boundary exactness, requiring $B$ to separate oracle-call and transcript rows from closed orbit-ledger rows;
\item non-escape through $E,H,C,P,N$, excluding hidden oracles, closed cellular replay of the transcript, ambient hypercomputation proofs, and any quadrant read detached from the displayed oracle rows.
\end{enumerate}
Thus a consumer may classify the packet as universal but not closed only after reading the substrate row, oracle-call row, transcript row, open-boundary row, and package-local naming rows in the same finite certificate.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Oracle-augmented substrate oracle non-escape]
\label{thm:oracle-augmented-substrate-oracle-nonescape}
No public consumer of an accepted $\OracleAugmentedSubstrateUp$ packet can erase the oracle boundary. Every oracle-dependent read factors through the finite route
$$
S,A,T,B,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
The route may certify that the displayed substrate belongs to the universal-but-not-closed quadrant, but it exports no closed cellular orbit ledger for the oracle reply, no substrate-internal proof of the oracle answer, no hidden halting oracle, and no closure premise for observations that pass through $A$ and $T$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with carrier projection. The only public coordinates are $S,A,T,B,E,H,C,P,N$, so any oracle-dependent read must cite the oracle-call row and transcript row before it can reach the quadrant-evidence row.

The open-boundary row blocks closed replay. A closed cellular orbit ledger, such as the sibling cellularautomaton and cellulartrustsubstrate surfaces, records substrate-local evolution. The oracle transcript is admitted precisely as a reply not generated by that local evolution, so a consumer cannot identify it with a closed orbit row without leaving the packet.

The haltingobstruction, haltingdiagonal, and haltingdistinctionlimit siblings explain why the oracle row is not merely an obstruction label: the packet stores a call-and-transcript surface, not a proof that the substrate internally solves the obstruction. Therefore hidden oracle strength and substrate-internal oracle correctness remain outside the public projection.

The packaging rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve that boundary under transport and replay. Hence the oracle-open classification is public only as the finite route through the displayed oracle rows, and no closed-observational premise is exported for those rows.
\end{proof}
