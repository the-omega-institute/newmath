Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive analysis, the $\BishopCauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ packet records completion as a finite transformation of metric names rather than as an ambient quotient or selected limit. It sits on the dyadic endpoint discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational sequence row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular-Cauchy completion packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert}, the functor rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionfunctor-namecert}, and the extension-uniqueness surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Bishop Cauchy completion functor carrier]
\label{def:bishop-cauchy-completion-functor-carrier}
A $\BishopCauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S,T,R,E,F,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source metric row, containing the named points, displayed distance observations, and finite tolerance ledger read by the source classifier.
\item $T$ is the target metric row, containing the metric surface into which transported Cauchy data are read.
\item $R$ is the regular-Cauchy family row, exposing the $\RegSeqRatUp$ schedules and stream windows used by the completion handoff.
\item $E$ is the completion seal row, recording the $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ and $\RealUp$ boundary reached from $S$ and $R$.
\item $F$ is the transported map row, storing the displayed map action on source rows and the finite replay by which regular-Cauchy families are sent to target completion rows.
\item $U$ is the extension-uniqueness row, read through the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ surface and tied to the same source, target, regular-Cauchy, and seal rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source, target, regular-Cauchy, completion seal, transported map, extension, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for a completion-facing consumer from $S,T,R$ through $E,F,U$ and back to the local naming row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCauchyCompletionFunctorUp}$ evidence.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient completion, no selected limit, no countable-choice schedule, and no ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop Cauchy completion functor NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-cauchy-completion-functor-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted carrier $Q=(S,T,R,E,F,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCauchyCompletionFunctorUp}$ surface consists of six finite obligation rows: carrier admission for $S,T,R,E,F,U,H,C,P,N$; classifier stability by componentwise $\hsame$ transport; functorial map transport through the displayed source, target, regular-Cauchy, and seal rows; unit handoff from source metric points into the completion seal; extension uniqueness through the row $U$; and non-escape from quotient completions, selected limits, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is coordinate projection from \autoref{def:bishop-cauchy-completion-functor-carrier}. The packet exposes the source and target metric rows, regular-Cauchy family row, completion seal, transported map row, extension row, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ as one finite history.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The row $H$ supplies the only equality-like transport, and $C$ replays the same completion-facing route through $S,T,R,E,F,U$ whenever a consumer replaces one accepted packet by a classified mate.

Functorial map transport and unit handoff are sibling-row compositions. The source row $S$ and regular-Cauchy row $R$ feed the completion seal $E$; the transported map row $F$ carries that route to the target row $T$; and the unit read is the special case in which a displayed source point enters the same seal route without naming a host limit.

Extension uniqueness is read only from $U$ after $E$ and $F$ have been fixed. Since no carrier coordinate stores a quotient completion, selected representative, choice schedule, or ambient Real-completeness theorem, coordinate exhaustion gives the non-escape clauses.
\end{proof}
