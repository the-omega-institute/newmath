\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ConstructiveBaireThreadUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-constructive-baire-thread-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Baire, Oxtoby, and Bishop--Bridges, the $\ConstructiveBaireThreadUp$ packet records the choice-free complete-metric thread used when L10 Real and Completion consumers read a nested dense-open construction through displayed finite data. It sits after the choice-free Baire-category route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-baire-category-choice-free-namecert}, the complete-metric service of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the Specker boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-speckersequence-namecert}. The packet names the thread as finite NameCert data and does not add countable choice, a host generic point, quotient equality of real names, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Constructive Baire thread carrier]
\label{def:constructive-baire-thread-carrier}
A $\ConstructiveBaireThreadUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(B,K,S,R,E,P,H,C,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\BaireCategoryChoiceFreeUp$ source row, exposing dense-open requests only as displayed finite obligations.
\item $K$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ Cauchy-thread row, carrying the nested-ball, radius, and limit-service data visible to the thread.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row that schedules finite dense-open requests, nested-ball inspections, and Cauchy-window reads.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for centers, radii, and tolerance comparisons consumed by the thread.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the Baire, complete-metric, stream, and regular-rational rows have been displayed.
\item $P$ is the $\SpeckerSequenceUp$ boundary row used by consumers that test the thread against a non-located or non-choice escape route.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $B,K,S,R,E,P$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a thread request through the dense-open row, complete-metric service, finite window, regular readback, Real seal, Specker boundary, transport, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ConstructiveBaireThreadUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $B,K,S,R,E,P,H,C,N$. It has no coordinate for a countable-choice selector, a host generic point, an infinite intersection object, quotient equality of real names, a selected Specker limit, or ambient completeness outside the displayed complete-metric service.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Constructive Baire thread NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:constructive-baire-thread-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ConstructiveBaireThreadUp$ carrier $T=(B,K,S,R,E,P,H,C,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ConstructiveBaireThreadUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed $\BaireCategoryChoiceFreeUp$ row, the $\CompleteMetricUp$ Cauchy-thread row, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite schedule, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, the terminal $\RealUp$ seal, the $\SpeckerSequenceUp$ boundary row, componentwise transport, displayed replay, and local naming. Every L10 Real or Completion consumer of the thread must read through these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:constructive-baire-thread-carrier}. The analytic rows are $B,K,S,R,E,P$, and the structural rows are $H,C,N$.

The Baire and complete-metric rows are read before any real-facing endpoint. The row $B$ supplies only the choice-free dense-open obligations, while $K$ supplies the displayed nested Cauchy service used by the thread.

The finite schedule and readback rows then control the observation route. The row $S$ records the finite windows, $R$ records the regular rational centers and radii, and $E$ is available only after those rows have been displayed.

Finally $P,H,C,N$ preserve the same boundary. Since the carrier has no selector, host generic point, infinite intersection object, quotient real equality, selected Specker limit, or ambient completeness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Constructive Baire thread nonescape]
\label{thm:constructive-baire-thread-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, or Specker-facing consumer of an accepted $\ConstructiveBaireThreadUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\BaireCategoryChoiceFreeUp
\longmapsto
\CompleteMetricUp
\longmapsto
\StreamNameUp
\longmapsto
\RegSeqRatUp
\longmapsto
\RealUp
\longmapsto
\SpeckerSequenceUp .
$$
The route exports a displayed thread for L10 Real and Completion consumers, but it does not export countable choice, a chosen generic point, quotient equality of real names, a selected Specker limit, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness beyond the named rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $T=(B,K,S,R,E,P,H,C,N)$. The dense-open side is exactly $B$, so the thread reads the Baire obligation through the choice-free packet rather than through an arbitrary sequence of choices.

The complete-metric side is exactly $K$. It carries the displayed Cauchy-thread service, and the finite schedule $S$ determines which dense-open and nested-ball observations can be replayed.

The real-facing endpoint is downstream of regular rational readback. The row $R$ packages the centers, radii, and tolerance comparisons before $E$ exposes the terminal real-name seal.

The Specker boundary $P$ is read only as a refusal surface for hidden located-limit or choice escape. Transport through $H$, replay through $C$, and naming through $N$ preserve the same finite route, so the consumer cannot reach any excluded selector, quotient, host generic point, or ambient completeness object.
\end{proof}
