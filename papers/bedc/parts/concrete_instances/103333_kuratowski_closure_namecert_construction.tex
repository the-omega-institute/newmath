\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\KuratowskiClosureUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-kuratowski-closure-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kuratowski's closure-operator axioms and standard point-set topology, the $\KuratowskiClosureUp$ packet records topological closure as a finite BEDC NameCert surface. It sits over the generated-open and neighbourhood rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the located distance tests of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, and the certified sequence-limit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sequentialclosure-namecert}. The packet gives topology, metric, compactness, and sequence-facing consumers a canonical closure-operator object without importing host powersets, arbitrary complements, excluded middle, selected convergent subsequences, or an ambient semantic closure theorem.

\begin{definition}[Kuratowski closure carrier]
\label{def:kuratowski-closure-carrier}
A $\KuratowskiClosureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(T,S,N,W,Q,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the ambient $\TopologyUp$ row. It records the indexed open rows, finite-intersection rows, union rows, and neighbourhood readback available to closure consumers.
\item $S$ is the subset or source-membership row whose closure is being named. It records only displayed membership witnesses over the carrier read by $T$.
\item $N$ is the neighbourhood ledger. Each row records a named neighbourhood of a candidate point, the open-row classifier that admits it, and the finite request asking whether the neighbourhood meets $S$.
\item $W$ is the closure-membership witness row. It records, for each accepted candidate point, the finite responses showing that every displayed neighbourhood request in $N$ reaches a displayed member row of $S$.
\item $Q$ is the $\SequentialClosureUp$ comparison row. It is present only as a displayed comparison route from sequence-limit closure evidence to the same neighbourhood ledger, not as a replacement for topological closure.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the topology row, source row, neighbourhood ledger, membership witness rows, sequential comparison row, replay data, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a closure request through $T,S,N,W,Q$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\KuratowskiClosureUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\SequentialClosureUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\KuratowskiClosureUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these nine rows. Its classifier compares accepted packets only through $T,S,N,W,Q,H,C,P,L$ and has no coordinate for host powersets, arbitrary complements, excluded middle, open-cover compactness, selected subsequences, ambient semantic closure, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Kuratowski closure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:kuratowski-closure-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\KuratowskiClosureUp$ carrier
$$
K=(T,S,N,W,Q,H,C,P,L),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\KuratowskiClosureUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the ambient topology row, subset or source row, neighbourhood ledger, closure-membership witness rows, sequential-closure comparison row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item neighbourhood-meets-source exactness, requiring every displayed closure-membership read to pass through $N$ and $W$ before it can claim that a neighbourhood of the candidate point meets $S$;
\item topology-row stability, requiring finite-intersection and union reads used by the Kuratowski laws to remain inside the displayed $\TopologyUp$ row $T$;
\item sequential comparison discipline, requiring the row $Q$ to compare certified sequence-limit closure evidence with the same $T,S,N,W$ ledger rather than replacing the topological closure carrier;
\item classifier transport, requiring $\hsame$ comparison to preserve the topology, source, neighbourhood, witness, comparison, replay, provenance, and local naming rows componentwise;
\item non-escape, excluding host powersets, arbitrary complement choice, excluded middle, open-cover compactness, selected convergent subsequences, and ambient semantic closure.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:kuratowski-closure-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the ambient topology row, source-membership row, neighbourhood ledger, closure-membership witness rows, sequential comparison row, and structural rows $H,C,P,L$.

Neighbourhood-meets-source exactness follows from the route $N,W$. A closure-membership read may answer a candidate point only after the named neighbourhood request in $N$ and the displayed source-meeting response in $W$ have both been read.

Topology-row stability is inherited from $T$. The finite-intersection and union rows used to express the Kuratowski closure laws are the rows already displayed by the topology packet, so no host powerset or complement surface is added.

The comparison row $Q$ is downstream of the same ledger. It can translate certified sequence-limit evidence into neighbourhood-meeting evidence only through $T,S,N,W$, and therefore cannot supply arbitrary subsequence choice or quotient stream equality.

Finally $H,C,P,L$ preserve and name only the displayed coordinates. Since the excluded host objects have no carrier row, no replay edge, and no provenance entry, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Kuratowski closure idempotence]
\label{thm:kuratowski-closure-idempotence}
Let
$$
K=(T,S,N,W,Q,H,C,P,L)
$$
be an accepted $\KuratowskiClosureUp$ carrier, and let $\overline{S}$ denote the closure-membership row named by $W$ inside the same packet. The closure packet generated from $\overline{S}$ over the unchanged topology row $T$ is classified by $\msame_{\KuratowskiClosureUp}$ with the original packet $K$. Equivalently, applying the displayed closure operation twice reads the same finite neighbourhood ledger, the same membership witness rows, the same sequential-comparison route, and the same transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted carrier $K$. A first closure read exposes the neighbourhood ledger $N$ and the witness rows $W$ over the source row $S$.

For the second closure read, the source row is the displayed closure-membership row $\overline{S}$. A neighbourhood request for a point of the second closure is answered by replaying the same neighbourhood row in $N$ and then using the witness row in $W$ that already certifies a meeting point for the first closure read.

The Kuratowski closure law used here is therefore idempotence of the displayed neighbourhood-meeting ledger: the second pass introduces no new topology row, no new source classifier, and no new witness coordinate. It only reuses the finite $T,N,W$ rows.

Componentwise transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ identify the two packets. Provenance and local naming through $P,L$ are unchanged, and the sequential comparison row $Q$ remains attached to the same ledger. Hence the two closure packets are classified by $\msame_{\KuratowskiClosureUp}$.
\end{proof}
