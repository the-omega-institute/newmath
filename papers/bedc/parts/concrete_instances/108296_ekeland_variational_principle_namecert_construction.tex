\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EkelandVariationalPrincipleUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-ekeland-variational-principle-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Ekeland's variational principle and the Bishop-style use of complete-metric descent, the $\EkelandVariationalPrincipleUp$ packet names the finite descent ledger consumed by variational and fixed-point arguments. It sits downstream of the metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the complete-metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the lower-semicontinuity packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lowersemicontinuous-namecert}, the regular-rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet records a finite variational descent certificate; it is not a host minimizer, not a selected choice function, and not an ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Ekeland variational-principle carrier]
\label{def:ekeland-variational-principle-carrier}
An $\EkelandVariationalPrincipleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(X,d,M,L,R,A,T,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the finite metric-domain row read through $\MetricSpaceUp$ addresses.
\item $d$ is the metric-comparison row naming the inspected distance witnesses on $X$.
\item $M$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ descent-readiness row, exposing only the finite Cauchy-tail obligations needed by the packet.
\item $L$ is the $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$ value-stability row for the inspected functional.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational readback row used to compare displayed values and descent budgets.
\item $A$ is the approximation row naming the initial value, positive tolerance, and admissible descent budget.
\item $T$ is the finite transfinite-free descent trace, ordered by the displayed metric and value-readback rows.
\item $W$ is the winner-window row naming the final inspected point and the finite perturbation inequality visible at that point.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for metric, completeness, lower-semicontinuity, rational readback, approximation, trace, and winner-window rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a variational request through $X,d,M,L,R,A,T,W$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EkelandVariationalPrincipleUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\LowerSemicontinuousUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EkelandVariationalPrincipleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports exactly this finite descent packet. It contains no host-level minimizer, no global choice selector, no quotient of Cauchy approximants, and no independent assertion that the ambient metric space is complete.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Ekeland variational-principle NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:ekeland-variational-principle-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EkelandVariationalPrincipleUp$ carrier $E=(X,d,M,L,R,A,T,W,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\EkelandVariationalPrincipleUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed metric-domain, distance, completeness, lower-semicontinuity, rational-readback, approximation, descent-trace, winner-window, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows;
\item metric readability, requiring every distance comparison used by the descent trace to be one of the displayed $d$ rows on addresses in $X$;
\item complete-metric descent readiness, requiring every Cauchy-tail request made by the trace to pass through $M$;
\item lower-semicontinuity readability, requiring every value-stability read to pass through $L$ and its regular-rational row $R$;
\item descent-budget coverage, requiring the initial value, tolerance, and perturbation budget to be named in $A$ before the trace row $T$ is consumed;
\item winner-window exposure, requiring the terminal inspected point and its finite perturbation inequality to be named in $W$;
\item transport, replay, provenance, and naming stability through $H,C,P,N$;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding host minimizers, global choice selectors, quotient-completion objects, and ambient completeness theorems.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier row by row. The metric-domain and distance rows expose the inspected addresses and comparisons, while $M,L,R,A,T,W$ expose the complete-metric descent readiness, lower-semicontinuity reads, rational comparisons, approximation budget, finite trace, and terminal winner window.

The obligation order follows the displayed route. A descent comparison cannot be read before $d$ and $R$ are visible, a Cauchy-tail request cannot be read before $M$ is visible, and the perturbation inequality cannot be read before the approximation row and trace row have been consumed.

Transport and replay are structural rows. They preserve the same metric, value, budget, trace, and winner-window data under $\hsame$ comparison and displayed $\Cont$ replay.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ source and name only the displayed packet. Since no coordinate stores a host minimizer, a global choice selector, a quotient-completion object, or an independent completeness theorem, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Ekeland metric-descent handoff]
\label{thm:ekeland-variational-principle-metric-descent-handoff}
Every variational, complete-metric, or fixed-point consumer reading an accepted $\EkelandVariationalPrincipleUp$ packet factors through the finite descent route
$$
\MetricSpaceUp,\CompleteMetricUp,\LowerSemicontinuousUp,\RegSeqRatUp
\longmapsto
A,T,W,H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer therefore receives a certificate-local descent ledger and terminal perturbation window, not a host minimizer, selected choice function, quotient index, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an accepted carrier $E=(X,d,M,L,R,A,T,W,H,C,P,N)$. The metric rows $X,d$ supply the only addresses and distance comparisons available to the consumer.

The complete-metric and lower-semicontinuity rows then bind the analytic reads. The row $M$ supplies the finite Cauchy-tail obligations used by descent, while $L$ and $R$ supply the value-stability and rational-readback route.

The approximation and trace rows $A,T$ order the descent budget before the winner-window row $W$ is consumed. Thus the terminal perturbation inequality is a displayed row of the packet, not a selected minimizer outside the packet.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this same route. Because no row carries a host choice selector, quotient completion, or independent ambient-completeness assertion, downstream consumers cannot obtain such data from the handoff.
\end{proof}
