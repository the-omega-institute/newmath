\section{Finite Convergence Carrier}
\label{sec:martingale-convergence-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Martingale convergence finite packet]
\label{def:martingale-convergence-carrier}
A $\MartingaleConvergenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
C=(\Omega,X,E,M,T,O,U,D,S,R,L,A,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\Omega$ is the $\ProbSpaceUp$ row naming the probability context.
\item $X$ is the $\RandomVarUp$ endpoint-family row for the displayed martingale window.
\item $E$ is the $\CondExpUp$ row exposing the successor faces used by the martingale law.
\item $M$ is the consumed $\MartingaleUp$ finite adapted-sequence packet.
\item $T$ is the $\StoppingTimeUp$ finite index row for bounded stopping reads.
\item $O$ is the $\OptionalStoppingUp$ stopped-window row.
\item $U$ is the $\UpcrossingUp$ crossing-count ledger for fixed lower and upper $\RealUp$ endpoints.
\item $D$ is the $\DoobUpcrossingUp$ inequality row bounding the displayed crossing-count ledger.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation schedule for finite endpoint prefixes.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational regularity row for displayed approximation windows.
\item $L$ is the $\RealUp$ lower and upper boundary endpoint row, with the comparison row carried as data.
\item $A$ is the convergence-admission ledger recording which finite windows, boundary pairs, rational tolerances, and upcrossing bounds are available to downstream consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the probability, endpoint, conditional-expectation, martingale, stopping, upcrossing, schedule, rational, real-boundary, admission, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $K$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from finite endpoint prefixes through bounded stopped windows, upcrossing counts, Doob bounds, and convergence-admission rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ProbSpaceUp$, $\RandomVarUp$, $\CondExpUp$, $\MartingaleUp$, $\StoppingTimeUp$, $\OptionalStoppingUp$, $\UpcrossingUp$, $\DoobUpcrossingUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MartingaleConvergenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows. In particular, it has no coordinate for almost-sure quotient equality, completed sigma-algebras, Brownian paths, unbounded stopping-time choice, or a bridge theorem.
\end{definition}

\section{Certificate Obligations}
\label{sec:martingale-convergence-obligations}

\begin{theorem}[Martingale convergence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:martingale-convergence-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:martingale-convergence-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\MartingaleConvergenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the rows
$$
\Omega,X,E,M,T,O,U,D,S,R,L,A,H,K,P,N,
$$
martingale endpoint exposure through $X,E,M$, bounded stopped-window exposure through $T,O$, finite upcrossing and Doob-bound exposure through $U,D$, schedule and rational-approximation exposure through $S,R,L$, convergence-admission exactness through $A$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $K$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet of \autoref{def:martingale-convergence-carrier}. The carrier lists exactly the probability, endpoint, conditional-expectation, martingale, stopping, optional-stopping, upcrossing, Doob-bound, schedule, rational, real-boundary, admission, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The martingale-facing rows are $X,E,M$. They supply finite endpoint reads and conditional-expectation faces only through the displayed $\MartingaleUp$ packet.

The stopping-facing rows are $T,O$. They expose bounded stopped windows and do not select an unbounded stopping-time family.

The crossing-facing rows are $U,D$. They consume the finite crossing-count ledger and the finite Doob-bound row, so the convergence packet does not recompute an inequality outside the accepted upcrossing certificates.

The remaining rows $S,R,L,A,H,K,P,N$ record finite prefix scheduling, rational approximation, boundary endpoints, admission, transport, replay, provenance, and naming. Since the carrier contains no quotient-equality, measure-completion, Brownian-path, unbounded-choice, or bridge coordinate, those objects are outside the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Martingale convergence bounded real window]
\label{def:martingale-convergence-bounded-real-window}
For an accepted $\MartingaleConvergenceUp$ carrier
$$
C=(\Omega,X,E,M,T,O,U,D,S,R,L,A,H,K,P,N),
$$
the bounded real window is the finite endpoint packet displayed by $L,S,R$: the row $L$ names the lower and upper $\RealUp$ boundary endpoints, the row $S$ names the finite prefix windows in which those endpoints are inspected, and the row $R$ names the regular rational readbacks used to compare the endpoints with the martingale values. The window is bounded only when the same $L,S,R$ rows are consumed before the upcrossing rows $U,D$ and before the convergence-admission row $A$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Martingale convergence upcrossing window lock]
\label{thm:martingale-convergence-upcrossing-window-lock}
Every convergence-admission read of $A$ in an accepted $\MartingaleConvergenceUp$ carrier first passes through the upcrossing rows $U,D$ together with the bounded real window of \autoref{def:martingale-convergence-bounded-real-window}, the bounded optional-stopping rows $T,O$, the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row $S$, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$. The lock supplies a finite convergence-preparation boundary over bounded endpoints and bounded stopping windows; it supplies no almost-sure quotient equality, no unbounded stopping theorem, no measure completion, and no ambient convergence theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Open the carrier of \autoref{def:martingale-convergence-carrier}. The row $A$ is the convergence-admission ledger, and its available inputs are exactly the displayed probability, endpoint, martingale, stopping, upcrossing, schedule, readback, boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows of the same packet.

Apply the NameCert obligation theorem, \autoref{thm:martingale-convergence-namecert-obligations}. It identifies bounded stopped-window exposure through $T,O$, finite upcrossing and Doob-bound exposure through $U,D$, schedule and rational-approximation exposure through $S,R,L$, and convergence-admission exactness through $A$.

The bounded real window is therefore not an external endpoint object. It is the same $L,S,R$ packet that the upcrossing and Doob rows consume before $A$ can admit a convergence-facing read.

Finally, the carrier contains no coordinate for almost-sure quotient equality, completed measure spaces, unbounded stopping-time choice, or ambient convergence. Hence the admission row can only lock the finite bounded-window route through $T,O,U,D,L,S,R,H,K,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Martingale convergence upcrossing route]
\label{thm:martingale-convergence-upcrossing-route}
Every accepted $\MartingaleConvergenceUp$ carrier consumes upcrossing information only through the displayed finite handoff from $\UpcrossingUp$ and $\DoobUpcrossingUp$: lower and upper $\RealUp$ boundary rows, finite martingale endpoint rows, lower and upper decision ledgers, bounded $\OptionalStoppingUp$ stopped-window rows, crossing-count ledger, and the Doob-bound row. No convergence consumer may replace that handoff by an ambient limiting event, almost-sure quotient, or unbounded stopping theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted carrier and project $U$ and $D$. The row $U$ is the crossing-count ledger supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-upcrossing-namecert}; the row $D$ is the Doob-bound surface supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-doob-upcrossing-namecert}.

The boundary rows and endpoint reads are displayed in $L$ and $X$, while the bounded stopped-window route is displayed in $T$ and $O$. The replay row $K$ records the finite path from endpoint prefixes to stopped windows, crossing counts, and Doob bounds.

Thus a downstream consumer receives exactly the finite upcrossing handoff rows carried by $U,D,L,X,T,O,H,K,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate from which an ambient limiting event, almost-sure quotient, or unbounded stopping theorem could be read.
\end{proof}
