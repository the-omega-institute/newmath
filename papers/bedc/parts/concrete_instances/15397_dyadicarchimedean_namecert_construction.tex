\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicArchimedeanUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicarchimedean-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\DyadicArchimedeanUp$ packet records the finite unary bound that a dyadic observation must expose before a $\RealUp$ seal may consume it. It sits between the dyadic observation carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal seal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is an Archimedean bounding certificate only in the constructive finite sense: the bound is a displayed unary ledger row, not a host-order axiom.

\begin{definition}[DyadicArchimedean carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-archimedean-carrier}
A $\DyadicArchimedeanUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(D,U,L,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation row naming the dyadic numerator, sign, scale, comparison budget, and local arithmetic ledger for the observed value.
\item $U$ is the unary bound row, carried by $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ and read as a $\NatUp$ bound for the magnitude of the dyadic observation in $D$.
\item $L$ is the dyadic scale ledger tying the scale coordinate of $D$ to the unary bound row $U$ by finite comparison data.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row that displays exactly the observation windows for which $D,U,L$ are available.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained only after the stream window $S$ has exposed the same dyadic observation and bound.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the displayed route through $D,U,L,S,R$ has been replayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dyadic observation, unary bound, scale ledger, stream window, regular readback, real seal, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each finite observation request through $D,U,L,S,R,E$ and then through the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicArchimedeanUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\NatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicArchimedeanUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a host Archimedean order, trichotomy, quotient stream equality, selected limit, ambient completeness, or an undisplayed real-number comparison.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Unary dyadic bound visibility]
\label{thm:dyadic-archimedean-unary-bound}
For every accepted $\DyadicArchimedeanUp$ packet $A=(D,U,L,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the unary bound for the dyadic observation is exposed only by the displayed $\BHist$ rows $U$ and $L$, transported only through the componentwise $\hsame$ rows $H$, and replayed only along the displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$. Any consumer that reads the bound must therefore read the same $D,U,L,H,C,P,N$ packet rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-archimedean-carrier}. The only bound coordinate is $U$, and the only scale compatibility coordinate tying it to the dyadic observation is $L$.

Transport cannot introduce a second bound. The row $H$ is componentwise, so it carries the displayed dyadic observation, unary bound, and scale ledger together; it has no host-order or trichotomy coordinate from which another comparison could be read.

Continuation replay is equally constrained. The row $C$ replays the finite route through $D,U,L$ before any downstream window, readback, or seal row is consumed. Since $P$ and $N$ bind exactly this packet, every accepted bound read is exhausted by the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$ rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-archimedean-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\DyadicArchimedeanUp$ packet receives the unary dyadic bound only through the finite chain
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp\longmapsto\StreamNameUp\longmapsto\RegSeqRatUp\longmapsto\RealUp.
$$
More explicitly, the consumer first reads the dyadic observation and bound rows $D,U,L$, then the finite window row $S$, then the regular readback row $R$, and only then the real seal row $E$. The handoff exports no host Archimedean axiom, trichotomy, quotient stream, selected limit, ambient completeness witness, or hidden real-order comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier in route order. The rows $D,U,L$ provide the finite dyadic observation, unary bound, and scale compatibility ledger; the row $S$ records the StreamName window over the same visible observation.

The readback row $R$ is downstream of that finite window. Thus a regular rational consumer receives the bound only after the StreamName row has exposed the same dyadic data, and the terminal row $E$ can be read as a $\RealUp$ seal only after that regular readback.

The excluded principles are absent by inspection of the packet. A host Archimedean order, trichotomy, quotient stream, selected limit, ambient completeness theorem, and hidden real-order comparison are not carrier coordinates and are not named by the provenance row. The Real-facing consumer therefore receives exactly the displayed dyadic-to-window-to-readback-to-seal route.
\end{proof}
