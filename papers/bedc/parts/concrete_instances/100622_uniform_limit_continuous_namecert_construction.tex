\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformLimitContinuousUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-limit-continuous-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges and Kelley-style uniform convergence of continuous maps, the $\UniformLimitContinuousUp$ packet records the standard constructive handoff from a uniformly convergent displayed family to a continuous displayed endpoint. It refines the uniform-limit certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformlimit-namecert} by adding the continuity-modulus row that every endpoint consumer must read before it treats the sealed limit as a $\ContinuousMapUp$ object.

The chapter uses the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window surface, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational handoff, $\RealUp$ seal rows, and the $\ContinuousMapUp$ modulus discipline as anchors. It does not introduce quotient-function equality, selected host limits, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Uniform-limit-continuous carrier]
\label{def:uniform-limit-continuous-carrier}
A $\UniformLimitContinuousUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(F,U,M,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the displayed $\ContinuousMapUp$ family, read only through certified source-window, target-window, and modulus rows;
\item $U$ is the $\UniformLimitUp$ row tying the family to one uniform-limit endpoint surface;
\item $M$ is the shared continuity-modulus ledger used by the family and by the endpoint handoff;
\item $W$ is the finite window schedule containing source requests, target tolerances, and the uniform threshold rows read from $\StreamNameUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained from the same finite windows and uniform-limit comparisons;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ endpoint seal exposed by the uniform-limit route;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the family, uniform-limit row, shared modulus, finite windows, regular-rational handoff, endpoint seal, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading the continuous-family modulus, entering the uniform-limit ledger, applying the shared window threshold, reaching the regular handoff, and exposing the endpoint as a continuous map read;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformLimitUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformLimitContinuousUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows, their transports, their continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming provenance.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform-limit-continuous NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-limit-continuous-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:uniform-limit-continuous-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformLimitContinuousUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the packet $Q=(F,U,M,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item uniform-window stability, requiring every endpoint-facing read to use the same $\UniformLimitUp$ row $U$, finite window schedule $W$, and shared threshold rows;
\item continuity-modulus handoff, requiring the endpoint continuity read to factor through the displayed modulus ledger $M$ before it is exported as a $\ContinuousMapUp$ read;
\item $\RealUp$ seal nonescape, requiring the regular-rational handoff $R$ and endpoint seal $E$ to be reached only through the uniform-limit row and the finite windows;
\item ledger refusal for quotient-function equality, selected limits, open-cover compactness, countable choice, ambient completeness, and any continuity proof not routed through $F,U,M,W,R,E$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:uniform-limit-continuous-carrier}. The packet lists the continuous family, uniform-limit row, shared modulus, finite windows, regular handoff, endpoint seal, transports, continuations, provenance, and local certificate.

Uniform-window stability follows because the only endpoint-facing route in $C$ enters the uniform-limit row $U$ and reads the same window schedule $W$. The transport rows in $H$ move those coordinates componentwise, so the accepted classifier cannot switch to a pointwise or externally selected threshold.

The continuity-modulus handoff is the displayed route through $M$. The packet has no coordinate for an ambient open-cover proof, quotient of functions, selected host limit, countable-choice selector, or completeness theorem, so those objects cannot serve as NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform-limit-continuous continuity handoff]
\label{thm:uniform-limit-continuous-continuity-handoff}
Every continuity read of the endpoint map exported by an accepted $\UniformLimitContinuousUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
F,U,M,W \longmapsto R \longmapsto E.
$$
Thus the endpoint is available as a $\ContinuousMapUp$ read only after the displayed continuous-family row, uniform-limit row, shared continuity modulus, finite window schedule, regular-rational handoff, $\RealUp$ seal, and visible $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance have been consumed. No quotient-function equality, selected limit, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or ambient completeness is exported by the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a consumer asking for continuity of the endpoint. By \autoref{def:uniform-limit-continuous-carrier}, the only continuity-facing entrance is the displayed family row $F$ together with the shared modulus ledger $M$.

Next the request enters the uniform-limit row $U$ and the finite window schedule $W$. These rows supply the uniform threshold and finite comparisons needed before the regular-rational handoff $R$ can be read.

Finally the endpoint seal $E$ is exposed through the same continuation and provenance rows. Applying \autoref{thm:uniform-limit-continuous-namecert-obligations}, there is no exported coordinate for quotient-function equality, selected host limits, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or ambient completeness, so every continuity read has the stated finite factorization.
\end{proof}
