The $\PhysicalNameCertAuditUp$ packet realizes the physical NameCert analogue of \autoref{sec:observation-and-reflection} as one finite BEDC naming certificate. It sits beside \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observationreflectionpacket-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-physical-model-audit-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflective-stability-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflection-gap-boundary-namecert}. Those siblings expose observation routing, audit strength, reflective reuse, and reflection-gap boundaries; this packet binds the certificate-template rows needed before a model signature may be read as a certified physical name.

\begin{definition}[PhysicalNameCertAudit carrier]
\label{def:physical-namecert-audit-carrier}
A $\PhysicalNameCertAuditUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(S,O,M,C,Y,L,F,H,R,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source row recording the finite observation or experiment trace that licenses the naming attempt;
\item $O$ is the observed-signature row extracted from $S$ through the displayed observation-reflection route;
\item $M$ is the model-signature row offered as the physical name candidate;
\item $C$ is the classifier row comparing $O$ and $M$ without importing a hidden model-reality identity;
\item $Y$ is the stability certificate row recording the continuation discipline under which the comparison remains reusable;
\item $L$ is the ledger policy row recording accepted fits, open gaps, and hidden residue that may later become active;
\item $F$ is the failure-surface row on which missing source, classifier, stability, or ledger data are recorded as certificate blockers;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for source, signature, classifier, stability, ledger, and failure reads;
\item $R$ is the displayed $\Cont$ route through $S,O,M,C,Y,L,F,H$;
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PhysicalNameCertAuditUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PhysicalNameCertAuditUp}$ row naming exactly this packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for final truth, source-domain identity, unledgered approximation, host physical law, or an external model-selection oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PhysicalNameCertAudit obligation surface]
\label{thm:physical-namecert-audit-obligation-surface}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:physical-namecert-audit-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\PhysicalNameCertAuditUp}$ surface consists exactly of the finite obligations for source exposure in $S$, observed-signature extraction in $O$, model-signature presentation in $M$, classifier comparison in $C$, stability certification in $Y$, ledger policy in $L$, failure-surface accounting in $F$, componentwise transport in $H$, displayed routing in $R$, provenance in $Q$, and local naming in $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier tuple of \autoref{def:physical-namecert-audit-carrier}. It exposes the source, observed signature, model signature, classifier, stability, ledger, failure, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows as the only packet coordinates.

The observation side is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observationreflectionpacket-namecert}. That sibling supplies the finite source and observed-signature route, but it does not by itself certify a physical model name.

The audit and stability rows are read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-physical-model-audit-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflective-stability-namecert}. These give the classifier-facing model audit and continuation stability surfaces needed before the local naming row can be consumed.

Finally use \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflection-gap-boundary-namecert}. The boundary row keeps the certificate open to later defeating observations, so the ledger and failure rows remain part of the same finite NameCert surface rather than hidden host evidence.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PhysicalNameCertAudit non-escape]
\label{thm:physical-namecert-audit-nonescape}
Every accepted $\PhysicalNameCertAuditUp$ packet exports only the displayed rows
$$
S,O,M,C,Y,L,F,H,R,Q,N.
$$
A consumer may promote a model signature to a certified physical name only through those rows. It cannot replace them by an unrecorded source, a detached pattern, an implicit classifier, an unstated stability principle, an absent ledger policy, a hidden failure surface, final truth, source-domain identity, or host physical-law semantics.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect \autoref{def:physical-namecert-audit-carrier}. The packet has explicit rows for each certificate-template field and no additional coordinate for a hidden source, detached pattern, final truth, or host physical law.

Apply \autoref{thm:physical-namecert-audit-obligation-surface}. Source, observed signature, model signature, classifier, stability, ledger, and failure data must all be read before the route, provenance, and local name are consumed.

The non-escape boundary follows because any missing source, classifier, stability, or ledger datum is recorded in $F$ as a blocker rather than supplied by an external oracle. The local $\NameCert$ row can therefore name only the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PhysicalNameCertAudit certification handoff]
\label{thm:physical-namecert-audit-certification-handoff}
If a $\PhysicalNameCertAuditUp$ packet admits source row $S$, observed-signature row $O$, model-signature row $M$, classifier row $C$, stability row $Y$, ledger row $L$, failure-surface row $F$, transport row $H$, displayed route $R$, provenance row $Q$, and local name $N$, then the only certification handoff available to a downstream physical-model consumer is the displayed route from $O,M,C,Y,L,F$ to $N$. If the source, classifier, stability, or ledger row is absent, the handoff stops at the failure surface and no certified physical name is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:physical-namecert-audit-obligation-surface}. It requires the source, signature, classifier, stability, ledger, and failure rows before the local naming row is available.

Use non-escape from \autoref{thm:physical-namecert-audit-nonescape}. Since no hidden source, classifier, stability principle, ledger policy, or host law can be projected from the packet, the handoff cannot be repaired outside the displayed carrier.

Thus a complete carrier hands the model signature to the local $\NameCert$ through $R,Q,N$. An incomplete carrier reaches $F$ and remains a failed naming attempt rather than a certified physical name.
\end{proof}
