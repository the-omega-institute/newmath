The $\EmptyBoundarySelectorLedgerUp$ packet realizes the selector-ledger branch of \autoref{ch:visions-empty-fable-machine} as finite NameCert data. It refines the one-step boundary selector of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-emptyboundaryselector-namecert}, the finite selector ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-selectorledger-namecert}, the observer-history identity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}, and the inscription event boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}. Its carrier stores the current history, selected mark, displayed $\Ext$ route, selector witness, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming in one packet so that an empty-boundary branch is generated by a visible row rather than by a hidden chooser.

\begin{definition}[EmptyBoundarySelectorLedger carrier]\label{def:empty-boundary-selector-ledger-carrier}
An $\EmptyBoundarySelectorLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(H,M,R,X,S,L,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the current $\Hist$ row whose empty boundary is being inspected.
\item $M$ is the selected-mark row, exposing the mark enacted at that boundary.
\item $R$ is the resulting $\Hist$ row after the selected mark has been enacted.
\item $X$ is the displayed $\Ext(H,M,R)$ route row.
\item $S$ is the selector-witness row, recording that $M$ is the enacted local branch for this packet and not a value returned by an undisplayed choice function.
\item $L$ is the non-reset ledger row. It records the finite selector ledger continuing from $H$ through $M$ to $R$, preserving prior history instead of returning to $\emp$.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $H,M,R,X,S,L$ and for the local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
H,M,R,X,S,L \longmapsto T,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EmptyBoundarySelectorLedgerUp$, $\EmptyBoundarySelectorUp$, $\SelectorLedgerUp$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, $\InscriptionEventUp$, $\BHist$, $\Ext$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EmptyBoundarySelectorLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $H,M,R,X,S,L,T,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for observer substance, global branch choice, a reset-to-Empty state, host trace equality, an unrecorded inscription source, or a truth oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[EmptyBoundarySelectorLedger NameCert obligations]\label{thm:empty-boundary-selector-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EmptyBoundarySelectorLedgerUp$ carrier
$$
E=(H,M,R,X,S,L,T,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\EmptyBoundarySelectorLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the current $\Hist$ row, selected-mark row, resulting $\Hist$ row, displayed $\Ext$ route, selector witness, non-reset ledger, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every branch read must expose both the selected mark $M$ and the route row $X$ before the ledger row $L$ is consumed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier in \autoref{def:empty-boundary-selector-ledger-carrier}. The current history $H$, selected mark $M$, resulting history $R$, extension route $X$, selector witness $S$, and ledger $L$ are the complete branch-facing inventory.

The selected mark and extension route are read before the ledger. Thus a consumer cannot treat $L$ as an unexplained sequence of choices; it receives the enacted local mark and the displayed $\Ext$ route that produced the next history.

The rows $T,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly these displayed rows. Since the carrier has no coordinate for observer substance, global choice, reset-to-Empty semantics, host trace equality, or truth oracles, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EmptyBoundarySelectorLedger branch separation]\label{thm:empty-boundary-selector-ledger-branch-separation}
Let $E=(H,M,R,X,S,L,T,C,P,N)$ and $E'=(H,M',R',X',S',L',T',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\EmptyBoundarySelectorLedgerUp$ carriers over the same current history row. If the selected-mark rows $M$ and $M'$ are not classified together by the displayed transport rows, then the packets cannot be identified by their ledger rows alone. The branch distinction must pass through the selected mark, $\Ext$ route, selector witness, and non-reset ledger rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the two accepted packets. Each exposes the current history row, selected mark, result row, extension route, selector witness, ledger row, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

If $M$ and $M'$ are not classified together by the displayed transport rows, then the branch-facing coordinates already differ before the ledger row is read. The route rows $X$ and $X'$ and selector witnesses $S$ and $S'$ are attached to those selected marks.

Reading only $L$ and $L'$ would erase the branch evidence that the carrier explicitly stores. The classifier therefore preserves the distinction through $M,X,S,L$ and their transported routes, rather than collapsing the packets to a hidden ledger equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EmptyBoundarySelectorLedger non-reset replay]\label{thm:empty-boundary-selector-ledger-nonreset-replay}
Every public replay of an accepted $\EmptyBoundarySelectorLedgerUp$ packet factors through the current history row $H$ before it reaches the selected mark $M$, displayed extension route $X$, selector witness $S$, and ledger row $L$. Consequently the packet records continuation from the current history, not a reset to $\emp$ and not a fresh Empty-as-chooser event.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the replay row $C$. It displays a route whose first row is the current history $H$, followed by the selected mark, result row, extension route, selector witness, and ledger.

Because $L$ is declared as a non-reset ledger row, it records the finite selector ledger continuing from $H$ through $M$ to $R$. The source history is therefore part of the public route, not external memory.

The structural rows preserve that route componentwise. No replay edge starts from $\emp$ unless $H$ itself is the displayed empty history, and no row supplies a hidden chooser at the boundary.
\end{proof}
