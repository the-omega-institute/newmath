\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AxiomDependencyTupleUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-axiom-dependency-tuple-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\AxiomDependencyTupleUp$ packet realizes the dependency-map reading of
\autoref{ch:bedc-axiom-dependency-map} as finite NameCert data. It follows
the audit-map family discipline of \autoref{ch:audit-map-family-methodology}
and sits beside the discharge-socket boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subject-reduction-discharge-socket-namecert}:
the former gives the row family shape, while the latter is a concrete sibling
where missing MetaCIC supply is named rather than hidden.

\begin{definition}[AxiomDependencyTuple carrier]
\label{def:axiom-dependency-tuple-carrier}
An $\AxiomDependencyTupleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(M,W,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is one of the three mode rows, Mode A for zero-supply internal
proof, Mode B for restricted-proof discharge under displayed supply, and
Mode C for counterexample or socket witness against an unstated supply;
\item $W$ is the witness row, recording a proof, restricted proof,
counterexample, or socket witness according to $M$;
\item $S$ is the required-supply ledger, listing exactly the host conversion,
quotient equality, $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$, $\mathsf{propext}$, ambient
classical theorem, or other named supply row that the entry requires;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the mode, witness,
required-supply, route, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads the
mode, witness, and required-supply ledger;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:bedc-axiom-dependency-map},
\autoref{ch:audit-map-family-methodology},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subject-reduction-discharge-socket-namecert},
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AxiomDependencyTupleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts exactly packets whose mode, witness, required-supply
ledger, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows are displayed in
this finite carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AxiomDependencyTuple NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:axiom-dependency-tuple-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:axiom-dependency-tuple-carrier}, the local
$\NameCert_{\AxiomDependencyTupleUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission
for
$$
A=(M,W,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
mode classification, witness transport, required-supply ledger exactness,
displayed $\Cont$ route readback, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. The
packet may name host conversion, quotient equality,
$\mathsf{Classical.choice}$, $\mathsf{propext}$, or an ambient classical CIC
theorem as supply in $S$, but it cannot export any of them as BEDC data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet $A$. The rows $M,W,S$ provide the mathematical
payload: a mode, a witness appropriate to that mode, and the ledger of supply
required by that witness.

Read $H$ and $C$ next. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport preserves the same
mode and witness row, and every consumer route must replay the displayed
ledger before it reads the tuple.

Finally inspect $P$ and $N$. Provenance cites only the dependency-map chapter,
the audit-map methodology, the discharge-socket sibling, and the BEDC kernel
rows. Since no carrier coordinate contains host conversion, quotient equality,
choice, propositional extensionality, or an ambient classical theorem as an
exported BEDC proof, the named supply remains a ledger entry rather than a
hidden rule.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AxiomDependencyTuple mode exhaustion]
\label{thm:axiom-dependency-tuple-mode-exhaustion}
Every accepted $\AxiomDependencyTupleUp$ carrier
$A=(M,W,S,H,C,P,N)$ is exhausted by the three displayed modes: Mode A carries
a zero-supply proof witness, Mode B carries a restricted-proof witness
together with the required-supply ledger $S$, and Mode C carries a
counterexample or socket witness naming the refused supply. Componentwise
$\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local
$\NameCert$ row preserve this trichotomy and do not add a fourth mode.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the mode row $M$ in the carrier of
\autoref{def:axiom-dependency-tuple-carrier}. Its visible constructors are
exactly the zero-supply, restricted-proof, and counterexample-or-socket
alternatives.

In Mode A, the witness row $W$ is read as an internal proof and the ledger
$S$ is empty of external supply. In Mode B, the witness row is read only
together with the named required supply. In Mode C, the witness row is a
counterexample or socket naming the missing supply rather than discharging it.

Transport and routing do not change the case split. The rows $H,C,P,N$ replay
the same mode, witness, and ledger coordinates, so no consumer can introduce
host conversion, quotient equality, $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$,
$\mathsf{propext}$, an ambient classical CIC theorem, or any fourth mode
outside the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AxiomDependencyTuple supply-ledger readback]
\label{thm:axiom-dependency-tuple-supply-ledger-readback}
Every accepted $\AxiomDependencyTupleUp$ packet exposes the required-supply row
$S$ before the witness row $W$ can be consumed. If the tuple names host
conversion, quotient equality, $\mathsf{Classical.choice}$,
$\mathsf{propext}$, an ambient theorem, or any other external supply, that name
remains a ledger row in $S$ and never becomes an exported BEDC rule.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier projection
$$
A=(M,W,S,H,C,P,N).
$$
By \autoref{def:axiom-dependency-tuple-carrier}, the consumer-facing data
contains both the witness coordinate $W$ and the required-supply ledger $S$.

Apply \autoref{thm:axiom-dependency-tuple-namecert-obligations}. The local
$\NameCert_{\AxiomDependencyTupleUp}$ surface includes witness transport and
required-supply ledger exactness, so any route that reads $W$ must also read the
displayed supply row attached to that witness.

Finally use \autoref{thm:axiom-dependency-tuple-mode-exhaustion}. Mode A has no
external supply to export, Mode B consumes $W$ only under the displayed ledger,
and Mode C records a counterexample or socket rather than a discharge. Thus the
named host or classical material remains visible audit supply in $S$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AxiomDependencyTuple route nonexport]
\label{thm:axiom-dependency-tuple-route-nonexport}
Every displayed $\Cont$ route in the coordinate $C$ replays the
$M,W,S,H,P,N$ components of an accepted $\AxiomDependencyTupleUp$ packet
componentwise. The route cannot introduce a fourth mode and cannot convert the
required-supply row $S$ into an internal proof principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the route coordinate $C$ from
\autoref{def:axiom-dependency-tuple-carrier}. Its declared role is readback for
the mode, witness, and required-supply ledger, with the remaining rows supplying
transport, provenance, and naming.

Apply \autoref{thm:axiom-dependency-tuple-namecert-obligations} to the same
carrier. The NameCert obligations include displayed $\Cont$ route readback and
componentwise transport, so a route is checked against the rows already present
in $A$.

The mode alternatives are exhausted by
\autoref{thm:axiom-dependency-tuple-mode-exhaustion}. Since $C$ only replays the
displayed coordinates, it has no constructor by which to create another mode or
turn a required-supply ledger entry into a BEDC proof rule.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AxiomDependencyTuple consumer exhaustion]
\label{thm:axiom-dependency-tuple-consumer-exhaustion}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\AxiomDependencyTupleUp$ tuple is
exhausted by the mode row $M$, witness row $W$, required-supply ledger $S$,
displayed route row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local
$\NameCert_{\AxiomDependencyTupleUp}$ row $N$, together with their
componentwise transport $H$. There is no hidden consumer authority outside this
finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier of \autoref{def:axiom-dependency-tuple-carrier}. The packet
lists the only coordinates available to a consumer:
$$
M,\ W,\ S,\ H,\ C,\ P,\ N.
$$

The obligation theorem
\autoref{thm:axiom-dependency-tuple-namecert-obligations} assigns each
consumer-facing role to one of these coordinates: carrier admission, mode
classification, witness transport, ledger exactness, route readback,
provenance, and local naming.

Mode exhaustion then removes the only remaining possibility for extra consumer
authority. By \autoref{thm:axiom-dependency-tuple-mode-exhaustion}, every
accepted packet lies in Mode A, Mode B, or Mode C, and transport or route
readback preserves that case split. Hence any downstream consumer is confined to
the finite displayed rows.
\end{proof}
