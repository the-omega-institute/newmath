\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionSplit carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-split-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionSplitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(X,U,V,A,I,G,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the displayed source row whose completion-facing split is being named.
\item $U$ is the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ route admitting $X$ into the completion-facing surface.
\item $V$ is the $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ route returning already displayed completion reads to the real-facing boundary.
\item $A$ is the $\CauchyCompletionAdjunctionUp$ row comparing unit-admitted consumers with completion-facing consumers over the same finite source.
\item $I$ is the $\CauchyCompletionIdempotentUp$ row recording that a repeated completion read contributes no further public completion row.
\item $G$ is the $\CauchyCompletionTriangleIdentityUp$ row comparing the unit-counit composite with the direct displayed read.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row opened before any rational observation can be exported.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used by the window and split comparisons.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row consumed by the terminal seal.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached after the split has passed through $W,D,R$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,U,V,A,I,G,W,D,R,E$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for consumers that inspect the unit side, the counit side, the triangle comparison, or the terminal real-facing route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionSplitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionAdjunctionUp$, $\CauchyCompletionIdempotentUp$, $\CauchyCompletionTriangleIdentityUp$, $\CauchyCompletionRetractionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionSplitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient-completion equality, selected completion representatives, countable-choice schedules, host equality of completed objects, ambient completeness, or public bridge credentials.
\end{definition}

\item carrier admission for the source row, unit row, counit row, adjunction row, idempotent row, triangle row, finite-window row, dyadic ledger, regular readback, terminal seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item unit exposure, requiring every completion-facing read exported from $X$ to pass through $U$ before a completion consumer can inspect it;
\item counit return, requiring every displayed completion-facing read to pass through $V$ before it reaches $W,D,R,E$;
\item finite split exactness, requiring the composite through $U,A,I,G,V$ to expose the same terminal route through $W,D,R,E$ as the direct source read;

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionSplit unit-counit route]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-split-unit-counit-route}
Let $S=(X,U,V,A,I,G,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionSplitUp$ carrier. Every consumer of the finite unit-counit split factors through the displayed route
$$
X\longmapsto U\longmapsto A,I,G\longmapsto V\longmapsto W,D,R\longmapsto E\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
After this route is projected, the visible seal-facing read is the same finite route
$$
W,D,R\longmapsto E
$$
that the source row already displays. The split is therefore a finite unit-counit boundary over BEDC rows, not equality of quotient completions, not a selected representative, not host equality, and not countable choice or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer of the split packet and project the source row $X$. The only completion-facing admission exposed by the carrier is $U$, so the consumer first passes through the unit row.

The middle comparison is fixed by $A,I,G$. The adjunction row $A$ records that the completion-facing consumer is a consumer of the same reflected route, the idempotent row $I$ prevents a repeated completion read from adding a public row, and the triangle row $G$ compares the unit-counit composite with the displayed route to the seal.

The return part is then forced by $V$. The counit row routes the already displayed completion-facing read through the finite stream window $W$, the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, and the regular rational readback $R$ before the terminal seal $E$ is inspected.

Transport by $H$ and replay by $C$ preserve this route for equivalent packets, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming. No other carrier coordinate exists, so the split exports exactly the displayed finite route and none of the excluded quotient, representative, host-equality, choice, or completeness data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionSplit nonescape]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-split-nonescape}

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. They add no carrier coordinate beyond the listed rows.

Thus every exported read is generated by \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-split-carrier}. The forbidden quotient, representative, choice, host-equality, ambient-completeness, real-completeness, and bridge data are absent from the carrier and cannot be produced by projection, transport, replay, provenance, or local naming.
