\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionReflectiveLocalization NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-reflective-localization-namecert-obligations}

\item reflector visibility, requiring every completion-facing read to pass through $Q$ after the source row $S$ has displayed the relevant finite windows and tolerance ledger;
\item inclusion exactness, requiring every completed-object consumer to pass through $I$ as the reflective subcategory boundary;
\item universal factorization, requiring every finite-window continuous consumer to reach its extension route only through $U$ after $S,Q,I$ have been projected;
\item localization saturation, requiring repeated completion-facing reads or already-complete reads to return through $F$ to the same included complete-object surface;

\item transport and replay nonescape, requiring $T,H,C,P,N$ to preserve the displayed dependency surface and to export no quotient completion, selected limit, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, or full $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}

The reflector and inclusion obligations are read in order. The row $Q$ supplies the Cauchy completion reflector and functor route from the source data, while $I$ exposes the completed objects only as the reflective-subcategory boundary.

The universal-property and saturation obligations are downstream from those rows. The row $U$ is the sole factorization surface for finite-window continuous consumers, and $F$ records that already-complete or repeated completion-facing reads remain on the same included complete-object surface.

Finally $K,T,H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, ambient completeness, or full $\RealUp$ completeness, the NameCert surface cannot export any of those objects.
