\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ObservabilityGramianUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-observability-gramian-namecert}

\origin{human}
The $\ObservabilityGramianUp$ interface records the finite observability Gramian packet of a finite-dimensional linear control system. Its textbook source is the finite-horizon observability Gramian in Kalman's control-theoretic duality and the matrix-ledger presentation used by Kailath, Sayed, and Hassibi for linear estimation. The BEDC packet is the finite dual certificate read beside $\ReachabilityGramianUp$ in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reachabilitygramian-namecert}; it is not an ambient analytic observability theorem.

The dependency anchors are the finite observation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-controlobservability-namecert}, the reachability dual surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-controlcontrollability-namecert}, the matrix rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-matrix-namecert}, the vector-space rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}, and the linear-map rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-linearmap-namecert}. These anchors force the Gramian to be read as displayed $\MatrixUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, and $\LinMapUp$ data before a control consumer can compare it with the reachability Gramian.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:observability-gramian-carrier}

\begin{definition}[ObservabilityGramianUp finite carrier]
\label{def:observability-gramian-carrier}
An $\ObservabilityGramianUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet with rows
$$
\begin{aligned}
(A,C,n,W,L,R,P,N).
\end{aligned}
$$
Here $A$ is the displayed transition-matrix row, $C$ is the displayed output-matrix row, $n$ is a unary finite-horizon row, $W$ is the displayed finite Gramian-sum ledger with summand rows
$$
\begin{aligned}
C^{\mathsf T}C,\quad A^{\mathsf T}C^{\mathsf T}CA,\quad (A^{\mathsf T})^2C^{\mathsf T}CA^2,\quad \ldots,\quad (A^{\mathsf T})^{n-1}C^{\mathsf T}CA^{n-1},
\end{aligned}
$$
$L$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport ledger for the transition, output, horizon, summand, and Gramian endpoint rows, $R$ is the finite dual-readback row comparing $W$ with the observation-matrix rows $C,CA,\ldots,CA^{n-1}$ of $\ControlObservabilityUp$, $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ObservabilityGramianUp$, $\ControlObservabilityUp$, $\ControlControllabilityUp$, $\ReachabilityGramianUp$, $\MatrixUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\LinMapUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$, and $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObservabilityGramianUp}$ row.

The classifier compares accepted packets only by componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the $A,C,n,W,L,R,P,N$ rows. It does not quotient by a host unobservable subspace, analytic trajectory family, Riccati equation, spectral factorization, infinite-horizon Gramian, or external estimator object.
\end{definition}

\section{Certificate surface}
\label{sec:observability-gramian-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[ObservabilityGramianUp NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observability-gramian-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ObservabilityGramianUp}$ obligation surface has exactly five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed $A,C,n,W,L,R,P,N$ packet of \autoref{def:observability-gramian-carrier};
\item classifier transport under componentwise $\hsame$ of the transition, output, horizon, Gramian, summand-ledger, dual-readback, package, and local certificate rows;
\item finite sum ledger exactness, saying that the Gramian endpoint inside $W$ is read only as the finite sum of the displayed adjoint-output summands inside $W$;
\item observability handoff, saying that the observation-matrix surface of $\ControlObservabilityUp$ can consume $A$, $C$, $n$, $W$, $L$, and $R$ only through the displayed $\MatrixUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\LinMapUp$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows;
\item dual non-escape, saying that no accepted route exports a host unobservable subspace, analytic output trajectory, Riccati solution, spectral factorization, infinite-horizon Gramian, covariance process, or external estimator.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row is \autoref{def:observability-gramian-carrier}. It displays the transition row, output row, unary horizon, Gramian endpoint, summand ledger, transport ledger, dual-readback row, package provenance, and local naming row as finite $\BHist$ coordinates.

Classifier transport is componentwise. Replacing each displayed coordinate by an $\hsame$-matched coordinate leaves the same finite packet shape, so the transported packet is again admitted by the carrier.

For finite sum exactness, inspect $W$. Each summand is a displayed matrix product formed from $A$, $C$, and transposes, and the Gramian endpoint is the finite fold of precisely those rows. The readback row $R$ ties that ledger to the observation rows without introducing a host observability theorem.

The observability handoff is a projection to the shared $A$, $C$, and $n$ rows, to the finite observation rows of $\ControlObservabilityUp$, and to the Gramian sum named by $W$. Because those rows are already packaged with $\MatrixUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, and $\LinMapUp$ provenance, a downstream observability certificate can read them without importing analytic control-system semantics.

Dual non-escape follows from constructor inspection of the carrier. The packet has no coordinate for a host unobservable subspace, analytic output trajectory, Riccati solution, spectral factorization, infinite-horizon Gramian, covariance process, or external estimator, so none of those objects can be projected by a consumer route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObservabilityGramianUp finite dual handoff]
\label{thm:observability-gramian-dual-handoff}
Every $\ControlObservabilityUp$, $\ControlControllabilityUp$, or $\ReachabilityGramianUp$ consumer of an accepted $\ObservabilityGramianUp$ packet can read only the displayed $A$ row, $C$ row, unary horizon row $n$, finite Gramian-summand and endpoint row $W$, $\hsame$ transport ledger $L$, dual-readback row $R$, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row of \autoref{def:observability-gramian-carrier}. The handoff supplies the finite dual route from observation rows to the reachability-side Gramian ledger, but it supplies no host Kalman decomposition, no analytic trajectory semantics, and no infinite-horizon duality theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to the rows $A,C,n,W,L,R,P,N$. The first three rows are the finite control-system coordinates shared with $\ControlObservabilityUp$, while $W$ records the finite adjoint-output Gramian ledger and $L$ records componentwise transport over those same coordinates.

The dual-readback row $R$ ties the summands in $W$ to the observation rows $C,CA,\ldots,CA^{n-1}$. Thus a $\ControlObservabilityUp$ consumer reads the packet through the observation matrix surface, and a $\ControlControllabilityUp$ or $\ReachabilityGramianUp$ consumer reads only the dual comparison route named by the shared package provenance.

The carrier contains no row for a host Kalman decomposition, analytic trajectory semantics, or infinite-horizon duality theorem. Therefore the handoff is exactly the finite BEDC packet projection and does not add a control-theory semantic bridge.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObservabilityGramianUp ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:observability-gramian-ledger-nonescape}
Every exported route from an accepted $\ObservabilityGramianUp$ packet factors through the finite rows $A,C,n,W,L,R,P,N$ of \autoref{def:observability-gramian-carrier}. In particular, the packet exports no ambient unobservable subspace, no analytic output trajectory family, no Riccati solution, no spectral factorization, no stochastic covariance process, no infinite-horizon Gramian, and no external numerical estimator.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Take an exported consumer route and project the carrier. The available coordinates are exactly the transition row, output row, horizon row, finite Gramian-summand and endpoint row, transport ledger, dual-readback row, package provenance, and local certificate row.

The NameCert obligations account for carrier admission, classifier transport, finite sum exactness, observability handoff, and dual non-escape. Each obligation is stated over those displayed coordinates and is preserved by the stored $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows.

Thus every export factors through the listed finite coordinates. Since the carrier contains no ambient unobservable subspace, analytic output trajectory family, Riccati solution, spectral factorization, stochastic covariance process, infinite-horizon Gramian, or external numerical estimator, no accepted route can supply one downstream.
\end{proof}
