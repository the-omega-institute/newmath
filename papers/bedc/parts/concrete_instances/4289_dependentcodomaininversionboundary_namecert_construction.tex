\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DependentCodomainInversionBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dependentcodomaininversionboundary-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\DependentCodomainInversionBoundaryUp$ packet realizes the dependent-codomain barrier named in \autoref{ch:metacic-open-problems}. It sits beside the obstruction packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert}, the beta-substitution packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-betasubstitutionpreservation-namecert}, and the frontier ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-open-frontier-ledger-namecert}. Its role is to name the finite codomain-readback boundary that must be discharged before a subject-reduction consumer may cross from syntactic substitution evidence to dependent application preservation.

\begin{definition}[Dependent-codomain inversion boundary carrier]
\label{def:dependent-codomain-inversion-boundary-carrier}
A $\DependentCodomainInversionBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(\Pi,A,a,a',C_0,C_1,S,R,O,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\Pi$ is the dependent function-type row whose codomain may observe the argument;
\item $A$ is the displayed domain row shared by the argument and the function type;
\item $a$ and $a'$ are the source and reduced argument rows;
\item $C_0$ is the codomain readback row obtained by substituting $a$ into the displayed codomain;
\item $C_1$ is the codomain readback row obtained by substituting $a'$ into the same displayed codomain;
\item $S$ is the syntactic substitution-evidence row, including the beta-substitution route supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-betasubstitutionpreservation-namecert};
\item $R$ is the subject-reduction handoff row pointing to the finite subject-reduction obstruction surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert};
\item $O$ is the open-frontier ledger row recording that no unconditional dependent-codomain inversion is exported;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the function type, domain, arguments, codomain readbacks, substitution row, handoff row, obstruction row, provenance row, and local name;
\item $K$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay routes for codomain readback, substitution evidence, obstruction readback, and subject-reduction handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the open-problem vision, the obstruction packet, the beta-substitution packet, and the open-frontier ledger;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DependentCodomainInversionBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for closed subject reduction, no proof that $C_0$ and $C_1$ are interchangeable, no context-reduction theorem, no inhabited discharge bundle, and no host conversion semantics.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dependent-codomain inversion boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dependent-codomain-inversion-boundary-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\DependentCodomainInversionBoundaryUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:dependent-codomain-inversion-boundary-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission for $D=(\Pi,A,a,a',C_0,C_1,S,R,O,H,K,P,N)$, domain and argument row visibility, paired codomain-readback visibility for $C_0$ and $C_1$, syntactic substitution evidence through $S$, subject-reduction obstruction handoff through $R$, open-frontier ledger exposure through $O$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $K$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The domain, argument, and codomain-readback rows are displayed before any substitution-facing or subject-reduction-facing read is available.

Read the syntactic side through $S$. The beta-substitution packet supplies a finite substitution route, but that route reaches only the displayed codomain readback rows and does not identify $C_0$ with $C_1$.

Read the subject-reduction side through $R$ and $O$. These rows cite the obstruction packet and the open-frontier ledger as boundary surfaces, so the consumer sees exactly where the dependent-codomain discharge would be required.

The structural rows $H,K,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name only the displayed rows. Since the carrier contains no context-reduction theorem, discharge bundle inhabitant, or closed subject-reduction row, the listed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dependent-codomain inversion boundary subject-reduction handoff]
\label{thm:dependent-codomain-inversion-boundary-subject-reduction-handoff}
Every subject-reduction consumer of an accepted $\DependentCodomainInversionBoundaryUp$ packet must pass through the displayed paired codomain rows $C_0$ and $C_1$, the syntactic substitution row $S$, the obstruction handoff row $R$, and the open-frontier ledger row $O$ before it may request dependent-codomain discharge. The packet may hand off this request to the subject-reduction obstruction surface, but it does not close the request and does not export closed subject reduction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a consumer asking for subject-reduction evidence in the dependent-codomain application case. Carrier projection exposes the two argument rows and the two codomain readbacks as separate coordinates.

Apply the NameCert obligation theorem. The consumer can read syntactic substitution evidence only through $S$, and it can read the subject-reduction boundary only through $R$ and $O$.

The handoff is therefore finite and explicit. It records the exact point where codomain readback stability or a compatible discharge surface must enter, while excluding closed subject reduction, host conversion, and context-reduction data from the packet itself.
\end{proof}
