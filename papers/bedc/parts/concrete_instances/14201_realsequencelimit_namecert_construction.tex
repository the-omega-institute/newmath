\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealSequenceLimitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-limit-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ packet records a displayed limit interface for sequences of constructive real names without importing host sequence convergence. It sits after the $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular rational rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the nonquotient finite-window equality boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyequivalence-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[RealSequenceLimit carrier]\label{def:real-sequence-limit-carrier}
A $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
(S,L,W,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is a displayed sequence of $\RealUp$ names, each read only through finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows selected for the packet.
\item $L$ is the candidate limit $\RealUp$ row, again accompanied by the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows made visible to the packet.
\item $W$ is the finite tail-window schedule that records which sequence indices and which observation windows are inspected at each displayed tolerance.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger for the scheduled sequence windows and the scheduled limit windows.
\item $E$ is the displayed convergence classifier, asserting that every scheduled tail window compares the corresponding sequence real to $L$ through the recorded dyadic tolerance.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the sequence rows, limit row, window schedule, tolerance ledger, and convergence classifier.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect the sequence windows, the limit windows, the dyadic ledger, and the classifier.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance for the $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and classifier rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealSequenceLimitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier stores a finite certificate surface for a displayed sequence-limit claim. It has no host sequence equality, no quotient limit, no countable-choice selector, no trichotomy row, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealSequenceLimit NameCert obligation surface]\label{thm:real-sequence-limit-namecert-obligation-surface}
For an accepted $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ carrier $(S,L,W,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealSequenceLimitUp}$ surface consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed sequence of $\RealUp$ names, the candidate limit $\RealUp$ row, the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ observations, the dyadic tolerance ledger, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming row;
\item convergence classification by the finite tail-window schedule $W$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, and the displayed classifier row $E$;
\item tail-window transport, stating that componentwise $\hsame$ transports and displayed $\Cont$ routes preserve the same scheduled sequence-to-limit comparison surface;
\item real-seal boundary through $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$, stating that every equality-facing read of a sequence real against the candidate limit factors through the nonquotient regular-Cauchy comparison boundary before any $\RealUp$ seal is consumed;
\item ledger refusal, stating that host sequence equality, quotient limits, countable choice, trichotomy, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness are not packet coordinates.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealSequenceLimitUp.RealSequenceLimitNameCert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the tuple of \autoref{def:real-sequence-limit-carrier}. The sequence rows, limit row, finite windows, dyadic ledger, classifier, transports, continuations, package provenance, and local naming row are all explicit $\BHist$ coordinates.

The convergence classifier is finite because it reads only the tail windows listed in $W$ and the tolerances listed in $D$. Each displayed comparison is made between scheduled $\RegSeqRatUp$ observations of a sequence element and scheduled $\RegSeqRatUp$ observations of the candidate limit.

Tail-window transport reads the rows $H$ and $C$ componentwise. Since the transported data still contains the same schedule, ledger, and classifier shape, every consumer route remains attached to the displayed finite comparison surface.

The real-seal boundary is inherited from the sibling route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyequivalence-namecert}. Equality-facing reads pass through the regular-Cauchy finite-window classifier before the $\RealUp$ seal rows are consumed.

The refusal row is an inventory check. The carrier has no coordinate for host sequence equality, quotient limits, countable-choice selection, trichotomy, or ambient real completeness, so none of those principles can be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealSequenceLimit observation-tail carrier]\label{thm:realsequencelimit-observation-tail-carrier}
Every accepted $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ packet exposes only scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ observations of the sequence and candidate limit through the finite schedule rows $W$ and $D$. No consumer can read a sequence-to-limit comparison outside those displayed observation windows before the $\RealUp$ seal is reached.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier of \autoref{def:real-sequence-limit-carrier}. The sequence row $S$ and candidate limit row $L$ are visible only through the $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows selected by the packet.

Apply the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:real-sequence-limit-namecert-obligation-surface}. Its carrier-admission and convergence-classification rows force every comparison to use the finite tail-window schedule $W$ and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$ before the real-seal boundary can be consumed.

The sibling references in the chapter introduction identify the available sources: $\RealUp$ contributes the terminal seal, $\RegSeqRatUp$ contributes regular rational readback, $\StreamNameUp$ contributes finite windows, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ contributes the tolerance ledger. None of these routes supplies an unscheduled host sequence observation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealSequenceLimit classifier stability]\label{thm:realsequencelimit-limit-classifier-stability}
For an accepted $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ carrier $(S,L,W,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the convergence classifier $E$ is stable under componentwise $\hsame$ transports of $S$, $L$, $W$, and $D$. Transported consumers therefore inspect the same scheduled sequence windows, candidate-limit windows, and dyadic tolerances before reaching the $\RealUp$ seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition gives $H$ as the componentwise $\hsame$ row for the sequence rows, limit row, window schedule, tolerance ledger, and convergence classifier. Hence a transported packet still has displayed coordinates of the same shape.

Use \autoref{thm:real-sequence-limit-namecert-obligation-surface}. The convergence classification row is finite because it reads only the windows listed in $W$ and the tolerances listed in $D$, and the tail-window transport row preserves that scheduled comparison surface.

The $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ sibling surfaces named in the introduction supply only their displayed rows. Since $H$ transports those rows componentwise, $E$ cannot acquire a host sequence equality, quotient limit, choice-selected tail, trichotomy row, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealSequenceLimit tail-window transport]\label{thm:realsequencelimit-tail-window-transport}
Every consumer read of a $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ sequence-to-limit comparison factors through the finite tail-window schedule $W$ and the dyadic ledger $D$ before reaching the $\RealUp$ seal. The route is
$$
S,L\longmapsto W,D\longmapsto E\longmapsto C\longmapsto P,N
$$
with componentwise transport supplied by $H$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a displayed sequence-to-limit comparison request. By \autoref{def:real-sequence-limit-carrier}, the only sequence and limit coordinates available to the packet are $S$ and $L$, and both are accompanied by scheduled finite observations.

Next apply \autoref{thm:real-sequence-limit-namecert-obligation-surface}. The convergence classifier row requires the request to pass through $W$ and $D$, while the real-seal boundary prevents equality-facing reads from bypassing the regular-Cauchy comparison route named in the introduction.

The continuation row $C$ replays the same scheduled comparison for consumers, and $P,N$ record provenance and local naming for that route. The transport row $H$ preserves the packet componentwise, so every replayed comparison remains attached to the same finite tail-window schedule before the $\RealUp$ seal is consumed.
\end{proof}
