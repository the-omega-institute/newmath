\section{DiffForm root consumer threshold certificate}
\label{sec:diffform-root-consumer-threshold-certificate}

\begin{definition}[Differential-form root consumer threshold certificate]
\label{def:diffform-root-consumer-threshold-certificate}
Fix the $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ source $M$ and the
$\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$ source $T$ used by
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier}. The differential-form root
consumer threshold certificate is the following ten-field surface:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the ambient $BHist$ history carrier for all displayed form rows;
  \item the $\hsame$ transport relation on degree, tensor, scalar endpoint,
  antisymmetry, and source-ledger histories;
  \item the $\Cont$ continuation ledger for ordered tensor concatenation and
  degree bookkeeping;
  \item the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine of tangent-probe names;
  \item the upstream $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ source for tangent probes and
  scalar endpoint data;
  \item the upstream $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$ source for multilinear
  tensor rows;
  \item the six-coordinate carrier
  $\mathsf{DiffFormBHistCarrier}_{M,T}(d,\mathcal{P},\tau,\sigma,\alpha,\lambda)$
  of \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier};
  \item the displayed classifier $\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$ of
  \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier};
  \item the wedge and exterior-input rows consisting of tensor concatenation,
  probe-bundle append, degree bookkeeping, antisymmetry ledgers, scalar
  endpoint transport, and source-ledger projections;
  \item the downstream consumer slots for $\DeRhamUp$, $\ContactUp$, and
  $\SymplecticUp$.
\end{enumerate}
The certificate explicitly excludes $d^2=0$, closedness, nondegeneracy,
contact nonintegrability, and any standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root consumer carrier readback]
\label{thm:diffform-root-consumer-carrier-readback}
Every consumer admitted by the
\autoref{def:diffform-root-consumer-threshold-certificate} reads exactly the
six-coordinate carrier
$$
(d,\mathcal{P},\tau,\sigma,\alpha,\lambda)
$$
and the displayed classifier $\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$ from
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier}. No downstream consumer slot may
read an additional carrier coordinate, replacement classifier, operator law,
or standard bridge from the $\DiffFormUp$ root.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-unblock-carrier-classifier-scope}. It reads the
root surface through \autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-carrier} and
\autoref{def:diffform-bhist-form-classifier}, so the only carrier available
to a consumer is the displayed six-tuple and the only classifier available is
the displayed $\DiffFormUp$ classifier.

Next apply \autoref{thm:diffform-root-export-carrier-classifier-threshold}.
The export threshold states the same carrier and classifier boundary at the
public root table, with no seventh carrier coordinate and no replacement
classifier.

Finally use \autoref{thm:diffform-namecert-obligation-surface}. The exported
NameCert surface lists carrier, classifier, transport, wedge, exterior-input,
and downstream scope rows, and it names no standard bridge or downstream
operator law. Therefore every threshold consumer reads exactly the stated
carrier and classifier surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root consumer wedge input readback]
\label{thm:diffform-root-consumer-wedge-input-readback}
Every accepted wedge or exterior-input consumer row admitted by the
\autoref{def:diffform-root-consumer-threshold-certificate} reads only probe
append, $\Cont$ tensor and degree ledgers, antisymmetry ledgers, scalar
endpoint transport, and source ledgers from the displayed
$\DiffFormUp$ root surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the wedge side. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-unblock-wedge-cont-closure}. It identifies wedge
closure with probe-bundle concatenation, visible tensor concatenation through
$\Cont$, degree bookkeeping, and the displayed antisymmetry ledger.

Next read the exterior-input boundary through
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-unblock-derham-input-consumption} and
\autoref{thm:diffform-derham-input-boundary}. These rows allow
exterior-input consumers to inspect carried input and output rows, degree
shift, wedge-input stability, scalar endpoint transport, and the restricted
source ledger.

The export side contributes no broader input. By
\autoref{thm:diffform-namecert-obligation-surface} and
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-export-wedge-threshold}, the NameCert root exports
the wedge ledger and exterior-input rows only through those displayed
histories. Hence accepted wedge and exterior-input consumers read exactly the
stated probe, continuation, antisymmetry, scalar, and source surfaces.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Differential-form root consumer downstream law separation]
\label{thm:diffform-root-consumer-downstream-law-separation}
The downstream slots of the
\autoref{def:diffform-root-consumer-threshold-certificate} are input slots
only. $\DeRhamUp$, $\ContactUp$, and $\SymplecticUp$ must supply their
operator and geometric laws in their own certificate surfaces; the
$\DiffFormUp$ root certificate does not project $d^2=0$, closedness,
nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability, or any standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-unblock-derham-input-consumption} to identify the
consumer boundary at the root-unblock surface. It permits downstream chapters
to consume degree, wedge, antisymmetry, scalar endpoint, and source-ledger
rows, while leaving their laws outside the DiffForm root.

Then apply \autoref{thm:diffform-namecert-obligation-surface} together with
the boundary rows
\autoref{thm:diffform-exterior-derivative-downstream-scope},
\autoref{thm:diffform-downstream-consumer-boundary}, and
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-export-downstream-law-separation}. These rows give
the same separation at the NameCert and export surfaces.

Thus the consumer threshold is citable by $\DeRhamUp$, $\ContactUp$, and
$\SymplecticUp$ as a root input certificate, but each downstream chapter must
close its own nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, nonintegrability, or
bridge obligations within its own surface.
\end{proof}
