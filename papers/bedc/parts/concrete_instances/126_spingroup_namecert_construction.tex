\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SpinGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-spingroup-namecert}

The Spin-group interface $\SpinGroupUp$ packages the connected double cover of the orthogonal group as a subgroup of the Clifford-algebra units. It depends on $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:spingroup-carrier}

\begin{definition}[SpinGroupUp BHist carrier packet]
\label{def:spingroup-bhist-carrier-packet}
Fix the $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ source of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert} and the
$\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ source of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}. A
$\SpinGroupUp$ carrier packet is the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet whose
visible rows consist of:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the Clifford unit row and its transported $\GroupUp$ identity row;
\item vector rows accepted by the Clifford source and their norm-one
classifier witnesses;
\item finite Clifford product rows closed under the carried group operation;
\item a cover row naming the induced orthogonal action on vector histories;
\item a subgroup row saying the accepted product histories lie inside the
Clifford unit group surface;
\item an action row recording conjugation of vector histories by accepted
spin histories;
\item a classifier row generated by the Clifford classifier, the Group
classifier, $\hsame$ endpoint transport, and finite $\Cont$ product
transport;
\item $\Pkg$ provenance rows for every Clifford source row, Group source row,
cover row, subgroup row, action row, classifier row, and finite ledger row.
\end{enumerate}
The packet is a BEDC carrier presentation. It does not identify spin histories
by a host quotient or by a topological connected-component predicate.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[SpinGroup root BHist carrier]
\label{def:spingroup-root-bhist-carrier}
The concrete $\SpinGroupUp$ root carrier is the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet whose visible rows are all of the following:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item a carried $\CliffordUp$ endpoint row supplied by $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$;
  \item a singleton-history $\GroupUp$ law row supplied by $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ for the same visible endpoint shape;
  \item a unit-like Clifford endpoint row, namely a Clifford carrier row classified with the Clifford unit endpoint;
  \item $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ witnesses recording that the displayed multiplication and inverse rows are transported through the Clifford endpoint classifier.
\end{enumerate}
The packet contains no host orthogonal group, no quotient algebra, and no ambient matrix representation; its carrier evidence is exactly the listed $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\NameCert$, and $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroup source scope]
\label{thm:spingroup-root-carrier-source-scope}
Every row of \autoref{def:spingroup-root-bhist-carrier} is sourced through $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ or $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$. The Clifford endpoint, unit-like endpoint, product transport, and inverse transport rows are read from the Clifford certificate; the singleton-history associativity, unit, and inverse law rows are read from the group certificate over $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$. No additional source row is admitted by the $\SpinGroupUp$ root carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the root carrier packet. Each constructor in the packet names either a Clifford carrier row, a Clifford classifier row, a group singleton-history law row, or a transport witness connecting these rows.

The Clifford rows are discharged by the carrier, unit, product, and classifier clauses of $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$. The group law rows are discharged by the singleton-history law surface of $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$, with the visible endpoint supplied by the same $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ spine.

It remains only to account for the transport witnesses. These are ordinary $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ rows whose endpoints are already present in the two certificates, so repacking them adds no third source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Clifford unit lift for SpinGroup]
\label{thm:spingroup-root-clifford-unit-lift}
If a $\CliffordUp$ carrier row is classified with the Clifford unit endpoint, then the same row lifts to the $\SpinGroupUp$ root carrier by pairing it with the singleton $\GroupUp$ identity law row and the reflexive $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ transport witnesses. The lift preserves the visible Clifford endpoint classifier and records the row as unit-like inside the SpinGroup packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the displayed Clifford carrier row and its classifier witness to the unit endpoint. The carrier clause of $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ supplies the endpoint row, while classifier reflexivity supplies the stable unit comparison needed by the packet.

Next choose the singleton-history identity row from $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$. Since the endpoint spine is the same unary history, the group row and the Clifford unit row share the visible boundary required by the root carrier.

Finally repack the Clifford row, the singleton identity law, and the reflexive $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ witnesses into \autoref{def:spingroup-root-bhist-carrier}. No multiplication or inverse row is consumed in this special case.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroup group-law transport]
\label{thm:spingroup-root-group-law-transport}
Let $a$, $b$, $a'$, and $b'$ be $\SpinGroupUp$ root-carrier rows whose Clifford endpoints are classified by the $\CliffordUp$ endpoint classifier and whose group-law rows are singleton-history $\GroupUp$ rows. If $\hsame(a,a')$ and $\hsame(b,b')$ hold through the Clifford classifier, then multiplication and inverse rows transported by $\Cont$ remain inside the $\SpinGroupUp$ root carrier and carry the corresponding $\GroupUp$ multiplication and inverse laws.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the two root-carrier rows. The Clifford parts expose carried endpoints and classifier witnesses, while the group parts expose the singleton-history multiplication and inverse law rows.

For multiplication, compose the two Clifford endpoint transports with the Clifford product transport supplied by $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$. The group singleton-history multiplication row is then read through $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ and placed over the transported endpoint.

For inverse, use the same endpoint classifier transport with the Clifford inverse endpoint row. The singleton-history inverse law supplies the group side, and the $\Cont$ witness repacks the result as a root-carrier inverse row.

The final packet is again of the form in \autoref{def:spingroup-root-bhist-carrier}: the visible Clifford endpoint has moved only by $\hsame$, and every group-law row remains a $\GroupUp$ row over the same unary history.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroup NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:spingroup-root-namecert-obligation-surface}
The rows in \autoref{def:spingroup-root-bhist-carrier}, together with \autoref{thm:spingroup-root-carrier-source-scope}, \autoref{thm:spingroup-root-clifford-unit-lift}, and \autoref{thm:spingroup-root-group-law-transport}, form the $\SpinGroupUp$ root $\NameCert$ obligation surface. The surface consists precisely of Clifford source scope, unit lift, multiplication transport, inverse transport, singleton-history group laws, and finite ledger exactness for these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the obligation surface row by row. Source scope is supplied by \autoref{thm:spingroup-root-carrier-source-scope}; the unit face is supplied by \autoref{thm:spingroup-root-clifford-unit-lift}; multiplication and inverse transport are supplied by \autoref{thm:spingroup-root-group-law-transport}.

The classifier side is finite because every row is a named component of the carrier packet. Inverting a proposed $\SpinGroupUp$ root obligation therefore yields either a Clifford carrier/classifier row, a singleton-history $\GroupUp$ law row, or a displayed $\Cont$ or $\hsame$ witness between those endpoints.

Repacking these cases gives the stated root $\NameCert$ surface. No Spin or Pin global presentation, orthogonal action, matrix model, or host quotient algebra is used by this obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroup Clifford even-unit carrier obligation]
\label{thm:spingroup-clifford-even-unit-carrier-obligation}
The $\SpinGroupUp$ carrier is the $\mathsf{BHist}$-coded row of Clifford unit
data whose displayed product is even and whose projection lies in the
orthogonal action boundary. Its visible carrier entries are inherited from
$\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$: Clifford unit rows, even
product witnesses, group operation and inverse rows, $\hsame$ transport,
$\Cont$ and $\Pkg$ provenance, and ledger entries naming the projection to the
orthogonal action interface. The carrier does not export a host Clifford
algebra element apart from these certified rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the Clifford unit rows from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}. They provide the visible
$\mathsf{BHist}$ product, unit, inverse, even-product, and projection rows used
by the spin carrier.

Read the group-facing operation boundary from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}. It supplies the multiplication,
unit, inverse, carrier transport, and ledger slots needed to regard the
Clifford unit rows as group rows.

Repack the shared $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and ledger provenance as the
carrier for $\SpinGroupUp$. The repacking keeps the Clifford and group rows
visible and records the orthogonal projection as a boundary row, so it adds no
analytic or host-level carrier beyond the displayed BEDC histories.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:spingroup-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroupUp root obligation surface]
\label{thm:spingroup-root-obligation-surface}
The $\SpinGroupUp$ NameCert obligation surface consists exactly of the carrier
packet of \autoref{def:spingroup-bhist-carrier-packet}, the generated
classifier on its accepted histories, stability under $\hsame$ and finite
$\Cont$ product transport, and the finite ledger rows naming unit, vector,
product, cover, subgroup, action, classifier, and $\Pkg$ provenance data.
In the roadmap ten-field template, the traditional target is the spin group
inside the Clifford units; the BEDC sources are the cited $\CliffordUp$ and
$\GroupUp$ certificates; the carrier, classifier, stability, ledger,
exactness, and status rows are precisely the rows displayed in the packet;
the standard bridge row is empty.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier field from
\autoref{def:spingroup-bhist-carrier-packet}. The unit, vector, and product
rows are Clifford rows, while the identity and product laws are read through
the $\GroupUp$ source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}.

The classifier field is generated only from the two source classifiers,
$\hsame$ endpoint replacement, and finite $\Cont$ product transport. Thus
carrier stability follows componentwise: Clifford transport keeps the
word-level rows inside \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert}, and
Group transport keeps the unit and product rows inside the group source.

The remaining roadmap fields are the finite ledger and exactness fields. The
cover, subgroup, action, and provenance rows are named in the packet, so any
public $\SpinGroupUp$ row is obtained by repacking those rows into the
ten-field certificate surface. No additional row is available for topology,
quotient equality, analytic connectedness, or a standard bridge.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroupUp Clifford unit carrier obligation]
\label{thm:spingroup-clifford-unit-carrier-obligation}
A $\SpinGroupUp$ carrier is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet whose visible rows are a
$\CliffordUp$ unit row, an orthogonal-action endpoint row, and a $\GroupUp$
carrier row. The packet exports no host Lie group, matrix equality, or ambient
Clifford algebra equality beyond the displayed BEDC rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the dependency surface stated at the start of this chapter. The
$\CliffordUp$ certificate supplies the unit row inside the Clifford-algebra
interface, while the $\GroupUp$ certificate supplies the public group-carrier
row read by downstream group consumers.

The $\SpinGroupUp$ carrier is the ten-field certificate packet that places
those two dependency rows beside the orthogonal-action endpoint row. Since the
carrier is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet, all equality and transport visible at the
interface is through $\hsame$ and the named classifier rows, not through a host
matrix or Lie-group equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroupUp Clifford--Group consumer boundary]
\label{thm:spingroup-clifford-group-consumer-boundary}
A downstream consumer of a $\SpinGroupUp$ certificate may read only the
$\CliffordUp$ source rows, the $\GroupUp$ source rows, the cover row, the
subgroup row, the action row, the generated classifier row, the $\hsame$ and
$\Cont$ transport witnesses, and the $\Pkg$ provenance rows appearing in
\autoref{def:spingroup-bhist-carrier-packet}. In particular, every consumer
request name factors through those rows and no request may inspect a host Lie
group, a quotient representative, or an analytic connected-component object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack a consumer request against the displayed carrier packet. The only
source rows named by the packet are the Clifford rows from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert} and the Group rows from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}.

The cover and action requests are finite ledger reads: the cover row names
the induced action on vector histories, while the action row records
conjugation by carried spin histories. Both rows retain their $\Pkg$
provenance and use the generated classifier for transport.

For a product or classifier request, the consumer must use the same finite
$\Cont$ spine and $\hsame$ endpoint transport carried by the packet. Since no
other row is present, the public surface exposes exactly the Clifford source,
Group source, cover, action, subgroup, classifier, transport, and provenance
rows listed in the statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroupUp ledger exclusion]
\label{thm:spingroup-ledger-exclusion}
The public $\SpinGroupUp$ ledger excludes host Lie-group topology, quotient
equality, analytic connectedness, and external double-cover data. Any accepted
spin row must instead exhibit a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet over the
$\CliffordUp$ and $\GroupUp$ sources, with cover, subgroup, action,
classifier, transport, and $\Pkg$ provenance rows as in
\autoref{def:spingroup-bhist-carrier-packet}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the ledger rows of the carrier packet. The visible rows are all
finite BEDC rows: Clifford unit and vector rows, Group unit and product rows,
finite product spines, cover and action rows, subgroup rows, classifier rows,
and provenance rows.

Host Lie-group topology and analytic connectedness have no row in this
packet. A consumer cannot reconstruct them from $\hsame$ or $\Cont$, since
those witnesses only transport endpoints and finite product spines already
present in the BEDC ledger.

Quotient equality and external double-cover data are also absent. The cover
row is a named finite action row on vector histories, not a quotient object;
the subgroup row is a carried Group row over Clifford units, not an imported
subgroup equality. Therefore every public spin certificate is exactly the
finite packet described above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroupUp double-cover ledger obligation]
\label{thm:spingroup-double-cover-ledger-obligation}
The double-cover ledger for a $\SpinGroupUp$ packet records the Clifford unit
endpoint, the projected orthogonal-action endpoint, and the kernel-sign witness
as $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows. No additional covering
space, topology, or host quotient is exported as public carrier data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the same dependency rows through the $\NameCert$ ten-field template. The
$\CliffordUp$ side contributes the unit endpoint, and the projected
orthogonal-action endpoint records the public target of the double-cover map.

The kernel-sign witness is ledger data: it is carried by BEDC rows using
$\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints, $\hsame$ transport, continuation provenance
$\Cont$, and package evidence $\Pkg$. Repacking those rows into the ledger slot
of the $\SpinGroupUp$ certificate yields the stated double-cover ledger without
adding a host quotient or topological covering object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroupUp group interface boundary]
\label{thm:spingroup-group-interface-boundary}
Any $\GroupUp$ consumer of a displayed $\SpinGroupUp$ packet may read only the
multiplication, identity, inverse, and classifier rows supplied by that packet.
The Clifford multiplication rows, orthogonal projection rows, and kernel-sign
witness remain ledger rows and are not exported as extra group operations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the $\GroupUp$ dependency from the carrier packet described in
\autoref{thm:spingroup-clifford-unit-carrier-obligation}. The public group
interface consists of its multiplication, identity, inverse, and classifier
rows, all carried through the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

The double-cover data is read separately through
\autoref{thm:spingroup-double-cover-ledger-obligation}. It supplies provenance
for why the group rows come from Clifford units and the orthogonal projection,
but it stays in the ledger slot of the ten-field $\NameCert$ template. Thus a
$\GroupUp$ consumer receives exactly the group interface and no hidden Clifford
or projection operation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroupUp Clifford-unit source obligation]
\label{thm:spingroup-clifford-unit-source-obligation}
A $\SpinGroupUp$ source row is a displayed $\CliffordUp$ unit endpoint together
with even-product provenance, a $\GroupUp$ multiplication row, and
$\mathsf{BHist}$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ ledgers tying the unit read and
the product read to the same carried source. The row records multiplication
and inverse access only through the inherited Clifford-unit and group
certificates, and it does not assert an uncarried host-level spin subgroup.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the Clifford dependency as a unit endpoint in the $\CliffordUp$ carrier.
The even-product provenance is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger whose factors
are carried Clifford generators and whose product endpoint is the displayed
unit row.

Read the group dependency as the multiplication and inverse package on those
unit endpoints. The $\GroupUp$ row supplies the operation laws only for the
displayed carried endpoints, while $\Cont$ records the product computation and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ records the shared provenance.

Combining the two dependency reads gives the $\SpinGroupUp$ source row. Every
field is inherited from $\CliffordUp$, $\GroupUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\Cont$,
and $\mathsf{Pkg}$, so no ambient subgroup predicate or external Clifford
algebra quotient is added.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroupUp double-cover fiber classifier]
\label{thm:spingroup-double-cover-fiber-classifier}
The double-cover classifier for $\SpinGroupUp$ compares two carried spin
endpoints by their Clifford-unit carrier rows together with the shared
$\mathsf{OrthogonalGroup}^{\uparrow}$ action endpoint. Two spin rows are in the same fiber
exactly when the Clifford-unit comparisons and the displayed orthogonal-action
readback agree in the package ledger; the classifier does not quotient by
host-level signs or by an unrecorded kernel object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack two $\SpinGroupUp$ source rows using
\autoref{thm:spingroup-clifford-unit-source-obligation}. Each row exposes a
carried Clifford unit, even-product provenance, multiplication and inverse
rows, and package provenance.

Compare the Clifford-unit coordinates with the inherited $\CliffordUp$
classifier. Then compare the displayed orthogonal-action endpoint using the
shared action readback recorded in the same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ ledger.

The fiber classifier is the conjunction of these displayed comparisons. Since
the sign ambiguity is represented only by carried Clifford-unit rows and their
action endpoint, the construction never forms a host sign quotient or an
unrecorded double-cover kernel.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroupUp group consumer boundary]
\label{thm:spingroup-group-consumer-boundary}
Downstream $\LieGroupUp$ or $\PinGroupUp$ consumers of $\SpinGroupUp$ may read
only the Clifford unit row, multiplication and inverse rows, the double-cover
classifier of \autoref{thm:spingroup-double-cover-fiber-classifier}, and
$\NameCert$ provenance. They may not inspect a host Lie group, a host sign
quotient, or an uncarried Clifford algebra carrier outside the displayed BEDC
source package.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The source obligation exposes the carried unit and the group operation rows.
These are sufficient for group-style consumers to read multiplication,
inverse, identity provenance, and the finite even-product ledger.

The double-cover classifier exposes exactly the additional fiber data used by
geometry consumers: the Clifford-unit comparison and the shared orthogonal
action endpoint. The consumer therefore reads a package of named
$\mathsf{BHist}$ rows, not an ambient quotient.

The $\NameCert$ provenance binds these rows to the $\SpinGroupUp$ interface.
Any $\LieGroupUp$ or $\PinGroupUp$ consumer that needs more structure must add
its own displayed source rows rather than reading an unrecorded host object
through this boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroup even-product classifier obligation]
\label{thm:spingroup-even-product-classifier-obligation}
The $\SpinGroupUp$ classifier is inherited from the Clifford unit classifier
together with group classifier transport. For carried spin rows it is stable
under multiplication, inverse, and replacement of even-product witnesses, and
it preserves the projection ledger into the orthogonal action boundary. The
classifier surface uses only $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows, $\hsame$ transport,
$\Cont/\Pkg$ provenance, $\NameCert$ fields, and the Clifford and group ledgers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert} to read the Clifford
unit classifier and the even-product witness transport rows. These rows compare
the displayed Clifford products through certified $\mathsf{BHist}$ data.

Apply \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert} to transport the same
classifier through multiplication and inverse. The group boundary supplies the
operation and inverse stability rows without replacing the BEDC comparison by
host equality.

The projection ledger is carried along the same $\Cont$ and $\Pkg$ provenance
used by the carrier theorem. Thus multiplication, inverse, and even-witness
replacement preserve the classifier and leave the orthogonal action boundary
unchanged.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpinGroup NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:spingroup-namecert-obligation-surface}
The carrier row of
\autoref{thm:spingroup-clifford-even-unit-carrier-obligation} and the
classifier row of
\autoref{thm:spingroup-even-product-classifier-obligation} assemble the
$\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$ obligation surface. This surface contains the
Clifford even-unit carrier, group transport rows, classifier stability, inverse
and multiplication stability, even-product witness stability, projection
ledger, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont/\Pkg$ provenance, and the exact visible
ledger boundary. It explicitly does not assert connected Lie topology,
smooth-manifold structure, analytic covering data, or a topological double
cover theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier theorem. It fixes the accepted histories as Clifford
unit rows with even-product witnesses and an orthogonal-action projection
ledger, all read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-clifford-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}.

Then apply the classifier theorem. It supplies classifier transport under
group multiplication and inverse and preserves the even-product and projection
ledger rows.

Repack these rows into the $\NameCert$ fields: source, carrier, classifier,
stability, exactness, and ledger. Since every row is a $\mathsf{BHist}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, or $\Pkg$ row inherited from the Clifford and group
boundaries, the resulting $\SpinGroupUp$ surface is a BEDC certificate surface
only and carries no Lie-topological or analytic covering assertion.
\end{proof}
