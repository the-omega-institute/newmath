\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HolonomyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-holonomy-namecert}

The $\HolonomyUp$ interface names the finite BEDC surface of parallel transport around certified loops. Its traditional differential-geometric source is Kobayashi--Nomizu, \emph{Foundations of Differential Geometry}. Its BEDC sources are the connection certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-connection-namecert}, the curvature certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-curvature-namecert}, the bundle certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bundle-namecert}, and the manifold certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-manifold-namecert}.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:holonomy-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Holonomy finite-loop carrier]
\label{def:holonomy-carrier}
A $\HolonomyUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet displaying a base-point row, a fibre-endpoint row, a certified loop row based at that point, a parallel-transport spine read from $\ConnectionUp$, a curvature-boundary row read from $\CurvatureUp$, bundle and manifold provenance rows, componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons, and a $\Cont$ ledger tying the loop, transport, curvature, and provenance rows to a single readback.

The packet contains no ambient smooth path space, no host connection object, no unbounded loop family, and no external holonomy group. Every visible endpoint comparison is read through the displayed finite loop and transport rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Holonomy classifier]
\label{def:holonomy-classifier}
The $\HolonomyUp$ classifier relates two accepted packets exactly when their base-point, fibre-endpoint, certified loop, parallel-transport spine, curvature-boundary row, bundle provenance, manifold provenance, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ coordinates are related componentwise by displayed $\hsame$ rows. The classifier exports only this finite componentwise comparison.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:holonomy-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Holonomy parallel-transport stability]
\label{thm:holonomy-parallel-transport-stability}
Let $H$ and $H'$ be accepted $\HolonomyUp$ packets. If the base point, fibre endpoint, certified loop, parallel-transport spine, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows of $H$ are transported to the corresponding rows of $H'$ by displayed $\hsame$ comparisons, then the transported packet remains inside the same finite $\HolonomyUp$ carrier surface. The endpoint readback of $H'$ is exactly the transported finite parallel-transport loop row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:holonomy-carrier}. The parallel-transport component is a finite spine read from $\ConnectionUp$ together with the base, fibre, loop, continuation, and package rows.

Transport those coordinates componentwise along the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons. The classifier of \autoref{def:holonomy-classifier} accepts precisely that componentwise transport, so the resulting tuple has the same carrier shape.

Finally read back the endpoint row. Since the carrier has no coordinate for an ambient path space or external connection object, the endpoint readback is exhausted by the transported finite loop spine.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Holonomy curvature-loop boundary]
\label{thm:holonomy-curvature-loop-boundary}
Every curvature contribution visible in an accepted $\HolonomyUp$ packet is read through the displayed certified loop row, the $\CurvatureUp$ boundary row, the $\ConnectionUp$ parallel-transport spine, the $\BundleUp$ provenance row, the $\ManifoldUp$ provenance row, $\hsame$ transport, and the finite $\Cont$ ledger. No downstream consumer can insert an unlogged curvature tensor, ambient surface integral, or host smooth-loop family.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the curvature coordinate from the packet. It is attached to a certified loop row and to the parallel-transport spine already named by the connection source.

Use the displayed $\CurvatureUp$ boundary row to account for the curvature data along that loop. The bundle and manifold provenance rows identify the source of the fibre and base coordinates, while the $\Cont$ ledger records the finite route to the endpoint readback.

Because the packet contains no host tensor, surface integral, or smooth-loop family coordinate, the curvature contribution is exactly the displayed boundary row together with its loop and transport provenance.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Holonomy certificate boundary]
\label{thm:holonomy-certificate-boundary}
The seed $\NameCert_{\HolonomyUp}$ obligation surface consists of five finite rows: carrier inhabitation for \autoref{def:holonomy-carrier}, reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity for the classifier of \autoref{def:holonomy-classifier}, transport stability of finite parallel-transport loops, exactness of the curvature-boundary ledger, and a downstream readback boundary restricting consumers to the displayed loop, transport, curvature, bundle, manifold, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier inhabitation is the constructor that assembles the base, fibre, loop, transport, curvature, provenance, and continuation rows into one finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

The classifier laws are componentwise. Reflexivity uses the displayed row identities, symmetry reverses each $\hsame$ comparison, and transitivity composes the corresponding comparisons while preserving the same finite continuation ledger.

The transport and curvature obligations are exactly the preceding two theorems. The final readback boundary follows by projecting the carrier coordinates: no accepted packet exposes an ambient holonomy group, host smooth-loop space, external transport solver, or extra curvature source.
\end{proof}
