\begin{theorem}[SeparatedMetric zero-distance scope obligations]
\label{thm:separatedmetric-zero-distance-scope-obligations}
Let $S=(X,D,Z,L_0,L_1,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparatedMetricUp$ carrier. Every zero-distance-to-classifier export from $S$ must expose, in order, the $\MetricUp$ distance witness in $X$, the $\RealUp$ zero-distance read through $D$, the $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit rows $L_0,L_1$ when the export is completion-facing, the $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$ comparison route $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and the local $\NameCert$ row $N$ before the separated classifier row $Z$ is consumed.

Thus the zero-distance export is scoped by the displayed finite packet. It is not host equality, quotient completion equality, selected representative data, trichotomy, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier definition \autoref{def:separatedmetric-carrier}. Its zero-distance route begins with the metric row $X$ and the real-valued distance witness interface $D$, while the classifier consequence is stored only in the separation row $Z$.

Apply the NameCert obligation surface of \autoref{thm:separatedmetric-namecert-obligations}. Carrier admission, classifier stability, completion compatibility, zero-distance exactness, and ledger visibility require the same displayed rows before any classifier export is available.

For completion-facing reads, use the completion-limit uniqueness route \autoref{thm:separatedmetric-completion-limit-uniqueness-route}. The limit rows $L_0,L_1$ are compared through the $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$ route $E$ before the metric witness and $\RealUp$ zero-distance read are formed.

Finally use zero-distance consumer exactness, \autoref{thm:separatedmetric-zero-distance-consumer-exactness}. The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same finite route, so no excluded equality, selector, compactness, or ambient completeness principle can enter the export.
\end{proof}
