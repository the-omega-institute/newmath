\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BraidGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-braidgroup-namecert}

The braid-group interface $\BraidGroupUp$ packages the finite Artin-word presentation of the braid group as a BEDC naming surface. Its textbook source is the Artin generator presentation and braid-closure calculus appearing in Birman's braid monograph and in the Kassel--Turaev treatment of braid groups. The BEDC packet keeps the presentation finite: every generator letter, inverse letter, braid relation, inverse cancellation, far-commutation move, Weyl-action read, and knot-closure read is a displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, or $\mathsf{Pkg}$ row.

\section{Carrier And Ledger}
\label{sec:braidgroup-carrier-ledger}

\begin{definition}[BraidGroup finite Artin-word carrier]
\label{def:braidgroup-word-carrier}
A $\BraidGroupUp$ packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$$
\mathcal{B}=(n,w,L,C,\rho,\lambda)
$$
whose source row $n$ is a positive unary strand-count ledger, whose word row $w$ is a finite list of signed adjacent-strand letters $\sigma_i^{\epsilon}$ with $1\leq i<n$ and $\epsilon\in\{-1,+1\}$, and whose classifier ledger $L$ is generated by visible Artin moves:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item inverse cancellation rows $\sigma_i\sigma_i^{-1}\leadsto \emp$ and $\sigma_i^{-1}\sigma_i\leadsto \emp$;
  \item braid rows $\sigma_i\sigma_{i+1}\sigma_i\leadsto\sigma_{i+1}\sigma_i\sigma_{i+1}$ and their signed inverse transports;
  \item far-commutation rows $\sigma_i\sigma_j\leadsto\sigma_j\sigma_i$ when the displayed unary gap records $|i-j|>1$.
\end{enumerate}
The row $C$ is the classifier witness obtained by composing such ledger entries. The row $\rho$ records the consumed $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$, $\NameCert_{\MonoidUp}$, $\NameCert_{\SymGroupUp}$, $\NameCert_{\WeylGroupUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\KnotUp}$ package sources used by downstream consumers. The finite ledger $\lambda$ lists exactly the word letters, move rows, classifier transports, action readbacks, closure readbacks, and provenance rows accepted by the packet.
\end{definition}

\section{Certificate Surface}
\label{sec:braidgroup-certificate-surface}

\begin{theorem}[BraidGroup Artin ledger stability]
\label{thm:braidgroup-artin-ledger-stability}
Let $\mathcal{B}$ and $\mathcal{B}'$ be $\BraidGroupUp$ packets over the same displayed strand-count row. If their Artin words are related by a finite classifier ledger generated by braid relations, inverse cancellation, and far-commutation moves, then every transported word row remains a $\BraidGroupUp$ word row, and the composed ledger remains visible as $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier data with the same $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BraidGroupUp.BraidGroupArtinPacket\_artin\_ledger\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Read the classifier ledger $L$ entry by entry. Each generator of $L$ rewrites a finite subword whose indices are already checked against the unary strand-count row, so the target subword is again a finite list of signed adjacent-strand letters over the same row.

For inverse cancellation, delete only the displayed adjacent inverse pair and keep the surrounding word prefix and suffix unchanged. For the braid and far-commutation rows, replace the named subword by the displayed target subword and attach the corresponding $\Cont$ route to the same prefix and suffix.

Composing the entries gives the transported word row and classifier witness $C$. Since all entries come from the finite ledger and every transport is recorded by $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows, the result is still a $\BraidGroupUp$ packet rather than a quotient of Artin words.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BraidGroupUp.BraidGroupPacket\_artin\_ledger\_stability}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BraidGroupUp.BraidGroupArtinPacket\_ledger\_stability}

\begin{theorem}[BraidGroup NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:braidgroup-namecert-obligation-surface}
The seed $\NameCert_{\BraidGroupUp}$ obligation surface consists of the finite Artin-word carrier of \autoref{def:braidgroup-word-carrier}, the classifier ledger generated by braid relations, inverse cancellation, and far-commutation moves, the inherited monoid concatenation and group inverse rows from $\NameCert_{\MonoidUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$, the permutation boundary consumed by $\NameCert_{\SymGroupUp}$, the braid-row action boundary consumed by $\NameCert_{\WeylGroupUp}$, the closure-ledger boundary consumed by $\NameCert_{\KnotUp}$, and the exact $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows listed by the packet ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet of \autoref{def:braidgroup-word-carrier}. The carrier supplies the strand count, signed adjacent-strand word, classifier ledger, classifier witness, dependency provenance, and finite ledger rows.

The monoid and group faces are the visible concatenation, empty-word, and inverse-word operations on the same finite word lists. Their stability is the Artin ledger stability of \autoref{thm:braidgroup-artin-ledger-stability}, because concatenating a displayed move with fixed prefixes and suffixes keeps the same finite move visible.

The consumer faces are projections of the same packet: $\SymGroupUp$ reads the adjacent transposition shadow, $\WeylGroupUp$ reads the root-action braid rows, and $\KnotUp$ reads braid closure as a finite closure ledger. No face adds host word equality, a presentation quotient, or an unrecorded isotopy class.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BraidGroupUp.BraidGroupPacket\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BraidGroupUp.BraidGroupArtinPacket\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}

\begin{theorem}[BraidGroup Weyl root-action handoff]
\label{thm:braidgroup-weyl-root-action-handoff}
Every $\WeylGroupUp$ braid-row consumer of a $\BraidGroupUp$ packet reads exactly the signed Artin letters, the adjacent-strand index rows, the braid and far-commutation ledger entries, the symmetric-group shadow row, and the $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance exposed by \autoref{def:braidgroup-word-carrier}. The handoff supplies a finite root-action ledger and supplies no Coxeter completion, host reflection equality, or Artin-representation faithfulness claim.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BraidGroupUp.BraidGroupArtinPacket\_weyl\_root\_action\_handoff}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BraidGroupUp.BraidGroupPacket\_weyl\_root\_action\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Project the Artin word to its adjacent-strand letters. Each letter has a displayed index row, so the $\WeylGroupUp$ consumer can read it as the corresponding adjacent simple-reflection action row only through the packet ledger.

Use \autoref{thm:braidgroup-artin-ledger-stability} for transport along braid and far-commutation moves. These are precisely the moves that a finite Weyl braid row consumes when comparing two displayed reduced-action ledgers.

The symmetric-group shadow is read from the same adjacent transposition sequence, while provenance remains the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ row of the packet. Because the packet contains no host reflection equality or faithfulness field, the handoff is only the finite root-action ledger just described.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BraidGroup knot-closure ledger boundary]
\label{thm:braidgroup-knot-closure-ledger-boundary}
Every $\KnotUp$ closure consumer of a $\BraidGroupUp$ packet reads the strand-count row, the finite signed Artin-word row, the crossing ledger induced by those letters, the Artin move ledger, the closure endpoint rows, and the $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance exposed by the braid packet. The boundary records finite braid closures as knot-diagram ledgers and does not claim Markov theorem, knot isotopy completeness, braid group faithfulness, or a standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BraidGroupUp.BraidGroupArtinPacket\_knot\_closure\_empty\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Read the signed Artin word from the carrier. Each positive or negative adjacent-strand letter determines one finite crossing entry with its incident strand indices and over-under orientation recorded in the closure ledger.

Transport of closure diagrams follows the Artin move ledger. Braid relations and far-commutations rewrite local crossing subledgers, while inverse cancellation removes a displayed opposite crossing pair; the closure endpoint rows remain attached to the same strand-count ledger.

The resulting object is the $\KnotUp$ crossing ledger read through the braid packet's $\Cont$ and $\Pkg$ rows. Since the packet does not contain a Markov-move classifier, an isotopy-completeness theorem, a faithfulness theorem, or a standard bridge row, none of those claims is exported by the closure boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BraidGroup scoped Artin source package]
\label{thm:braidgroup-scoped-artin-source-package}
Every consumer-facing $\BraidGroupUp$ read is exhausted by the finite
strand-count row, signed Artin word row, Artin move ledger,
symmetric-group shadow, Weyl root-action handoff, knot-closure ledger, and the
$\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance from the
carrier. The package exports no Markov theorem, braid-group faithfulness, knot
isotopy completeness, or standard bridge data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:braidgroup-word-carrier}. It supplies the
positive unary strand count, signed adjacent-strand word, classifier ledger,
dependency provenance, and finite packet ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:braidgroup-artin-ledger-stability} to identify every
transported word read with the visible Artin move ledger. Then apply
\autoref{thm:braidgroup-namecert-obligation-surface} to account for the
monoid, group, symmetric-group, Weyl-group, and knot dependency rows in the
NameCert surface.

The two downstream faces are the finite projections already isolated by
\autoref{thm:braidgroup-weyl-root-action-handoff} and
\autoref{thm:braidgroup-knot-closure-ledger-boundary}. Their exclusion clauses
remove Markov moves, Artin-representation faithfulness, knot isotopy
completeness, and standard bridge data, leaving exactly the scoped Artin source
package stated above.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BraidGroupUp.BraidGroupArtinPacket\_scoped\_artin\_source\_package}

\begin{theorem}[BraidGroup public Artin interface]
\label{thm:braidgroup-public-artin-interface}
The finite signed Artin-word carrier, Artin ledger stability, NameCert
obligation surface, Weyl root-action handoff, knot-closure ledger boundary, and
scoped Artin source package assemble the public
$\NameCert_{\BraidGroupUp}$ interface. It is readable by $\GroupUp$,
$\SymGroupUp$, $\WeylGroupUp$, and $\KnotUp$ consumers as strand-count rows,
signed adjacent-strand word rows, braid/inverse/far-commutation ledgers,
symmetric-group shadow rows, Weyl action rows, knot-closure ledger rows,
$\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

The interface exports no Markov theorem, braid-group faithfulness, knot isotopy
completeness, presentation quotient, or standard bridge data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite carrier of \autoref{def:braidgroup-word-carrier}. It
provides the strand count, signed Artin word, classifier ledger, dependency
provenance, and packet rows that all four consumer directions read.

Apply \autoref{thm:braidgroup-artin-ledger-stability} to keep braid,
inverse-cancellation, and far-commutation transport inside the displayed Artin
ledger. Then use \autoref{thm:braidgroup-namecert-obligation-surface} to expose
the NameCert rows shared by the group, symmetric-group, Weyl-group, and knot
readers.

The two non-group faces are exactly the finite handoffs isolated by
\autoref{thm:braidgroup-weyl-root-action-handoff} and
\autoref{thm:braidgroup-knot-closure-ledger-boundary}. Finally
\autoref{thm:braidgroup-scoped-artin-source-package} gathers these rows into a
single scoped Artin source package.

Since the cited package has no coordinate for Markov moves, representation
faithfulness, isotopy completeness, presentation quotients, or standard bridge
rows, repacking it as a public $\NameCert_{\BraidGroupUp}$ interface cannot
export any of that data.
\end{proof}
