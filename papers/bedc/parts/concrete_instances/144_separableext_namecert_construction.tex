\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SeparableExtUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-separableext-namecert}

The separable-extension interface $\SeparableExtUp$ packages a field extension whose minimal polynomials all have simple roots. It depends on $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fieldext-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\PolynomialUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polynomial-namecert}).

\begin{theorem}[Separable-extension FieldExt and Polynomial source surface]
\label{thm:separableext-fieldext-polynomial-source}
A $\SeparableExtUp$ source row is the joint BEDC source consisting of a $\FieldExtUp$ extension packet, a $\PolynomialUp$ minimal-polynomial packet over that extension, and shared provenance rows in $\mathsf{BHist}$ and $\mathsf{Pkg}$. The two dependency witnesses are scoped by $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\PolynomialUp}$, and the shared provenance requires the displayed base-field, extension-field, generator, and minimal-polynomial histories to be compared by $\hsame$ inside the same package ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the $\FieldExtUp$ dependency certificate to obtain the base row, extension row, extension classifier, and package provenance. Project the $\PolynomialUp$ dependency certificate to obtain the polynomial carrier, coefficient ledger, and polynomial classifier over the displayed extension row.

The source row for $\SeparableExtUp$ is then the common pullback of these two projections over $\mathsf{BHist}$ and $\mathsf{Pkg}$. The $\hsame$ rows identify the base and extension histories seen by the polynomial packet with the histories supplied by the field-extension packet, so the two dependencies describe one BEDC source rather than two unrelated presentations.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeparableExtUp.SeparableExtJointSource\_fieldext\_polynomial\_source}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeparableExtUp.SeparableExtSourceSurface\_dependency\_ledger\_closure}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeparableExtUp.SeparableExtSourceRow\_classifier\_stability}

\begin{theorem}[Separable-extension carrier and classifier surface]
\label{thm:separableext-carrier-classifier-surface}
The carrier surface for separable-extension histories consists of the joint source row of \autoref{thm:separableext-fieldext-polynomial-source} together with the displayed generator and minimal-polynomial histories. Two such carrier rows are classified by the inherited $\FieldExtUp$ classifier on the extension packet and by the $\PolynomialUp$ equality classifier on the displayed minimal-polynomial rows, with the shared $\mathsf{BHist}$ and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance transported along $\hsame$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack a carried row into its extension packet, polynomial packet, generator history, minimal-polynomial history, and package ledger. The field-extension component supplies the comparison of base and extension histories, while the polynomial component supplies comparison of coefficient and polynomial rows.

For classification, compare the extension projections using the $\FieldExtUp$ classifier and compare the minimal-polynomial projections using the $\PolynomialUp$ equality classifier. The remaining carrier fields are provenance fields, so they move by the $\hsame$ transports recorded in the joint package ledger.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeparableExtUp.SeparableExtSourceSurface\_carrier\_classifier\_surface}

\begin{theorem}[Separable-extension simple-root obligation]
\label{thm:separableext-simple-root-obligation}
The separability row for $\SeparableExtUp$ is a displayed $\PolynomialUp$ witness that the minimal-polynomial row has only simple roots. Concretely, the row records a derivative or gcd-style witness in $\mathsf{BHist}$, continuation evidence in $\Cont$, classifier transports in $\hsame$, and package provenance in $\mathsf{Pkg}$; it does not assert an uncarried host-algebra separability predicate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeparableExtUp.SeparableExtSimpleRoot\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Start from the minimal-polynomial packet supplied by \autoref{thm:separableext-fieldext-polynomial-source}. Its $\PolynomialUp$ ledger exposes the polynomial row, the derivative row or equivalent gcd-style comparison row, and the coefficient histories over the same extension packet.

The simple-root obligation is the package row that stores these displayed histories together with the $\Cont$ evidence computing the derivative or gcd comparison. Soundness is only a BEDC soundness statement: every accepted separability row is one of these displayed polynomial ledgers, transported by $\hsame$ and scoped by the shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeparableExtUp.SeparableExtSourceSurface\_simple\_root\_obligation}

\begin{theorem}[Separable-extension classifier stability]
\label{thm:separableext-classifier-stability}
The simple-root row is stable under the classifier of \autoref{thm:separableext-carrier-classifier-surface}. Transport along the inherited $\FieldExtUp$ classifier and the inherited $\PolynomialUp$ minimal-polynomial classifier carries the derivative or gcd-style witness, the $\Cont$ computation row, the $\hsame$ transports, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance to the classified target carrier row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeparableExtUp.SeparableExtSourceSurface\_classifier\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Transport the field-extension part first. The $\FieldExtUp$ classifier identifies the base, extension, and generator histories seen by the source row, so the minimal-polynomial packet remains over the transported extension packet.

Next transport the polynomial part. The $\PolynomialUp$ equality classifier carries the minimal-polynomial row and its displayed derivative or gcd-style witness to the target packet. The $\Cont$ computation row is stable under the transported input and output histories, and the $\hsame$ witnesses compose with the package provenance to repack the same simple-root obligation at the target.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separable-extension ledger exactness]
\label{thm:separableext-ledger-exactness}
The $\SeparableExtUp$ ledger has coverage, soundness, and completeness for exactly the carried extension row, minimal-polynomial row, simple-root row, classifier-stability row, ledger row, and dependency rows supplied by $\FieldExtUp$, $\PolynomialUp$, $\NameCert$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$. No additional host-field algebra, root set, splitting-field data, or Galois action is part of this certificate surface.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeparableExtUp.SeparableExtSourceRow\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Coverage follows by reading the roadmap ten-field template. The traditional target is separability of a field extension; the BEDC sources are the $\FieldExtUp$ and $\PolynomialUp$ certificates; the carrier, classifier, stability, ledger, theorem-list, exactness, bridge, and status slots are the rows named in the four preceding theorems, with the bridge slot empty.

Soundness reads every accepted public row back to that list. A carried extension row comes from the field-extension dependency, a minimal-polynomial row comes from the polynomial dependency, and the simple-root row is the displayed derivative or gcd-style package with its $\Cont$, $\hsame$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

Completeness is the reverse packaging. Given exactly those dependency rows and provenance rows, the ten-field template assembles a $\SeparableExtUp$ certificate row, and \autoref{thm:separableext-classifier-stability} supplies the classifier transport needed to keep the row stable under certified comparison.
\end{proof}
