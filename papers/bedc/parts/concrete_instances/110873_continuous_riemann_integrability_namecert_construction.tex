\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ContinuousRiemannIntegrabilityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-riemann-integrability-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Riemann and Bishop--Bridges constructive integration, the $\ContinuousRiemannIntegrabilityUp$ packet records the finite route by which a continuous function on a located closed real interval receives a Darboux/Riemann integrability certificate. It consumes the pointwise-modulus surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}, the tag-bearing finite interval route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tagged-real-partition-namecert}, the Darboux upper/lower boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-darboux-sum-namecert}, the integrability carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemann-integrable-namecert}, the weak located interval selector of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-weakly-located-interval-selection-namecert}, and the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet gives integration consumers a finite NameCert surface for continuous functions without importing measure integration, open-cover compactness, quotient equality of real names, selected limiting values, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Continuous Riemann integrability carrier]
\label{def:continuous-riemann-integrability-carrier}
A $\ContinuousRiemannIntegrabilityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(F,T,D,J,W,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousUp$ row exposing the pointwise modulus and graph-stability data of the integrand over the displayed interval source.
\item $T$ is the $\TaggedRealPartitionUp$ row naming the finite partition cells, tag containment, window reads, regular readbacks, and terminal real seals used for integration.
\item $D$ is the $\DarbouxSumUp$ row carrying lower and upper finite sums over the same displayed partition route.
\item $J$ is the $\RiemannIntegrableUp$ handoff row, read only after the Darboux gap row has been displayed.
\item $W$ is the $\WeaklyLocatedIntervalSelectionUp$ row that exposes located cells and endpoint windows for the closed interval source.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window family opened for interval endpoints, partition tags, and pointwise modulus reads.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback family attached to the same finite windows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $F,T,D,J,W,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the continuous row, tagged partition, Darboux rows, integrability handoff, weak interval selector, window and readback rows, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from pointwise modulus requests through located cells, finite tagged partitions, Darboux lower/upper rows, the integrability handoff, and the terminal real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ContinuousRiemannIntegrabilityUp$, $\ContinuousUp$, $\TaggedRealPartitionUp$, $\DarbouxSumUp$, $\RiemannIntegrableUp$, $\WeaklyLocatedIntervalSelectionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ContinuousRiemannIntegrabilityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $F,T,D,J,W,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for full integration theory, measure integration, open-cover compactness, quotient equality, selected limits, or ambient real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous Riemann integrability NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:continuous-riemann-integrability-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ContinuousRiemannIntegrabilityUp$ carrier
$$
I=(F,T,D,J,W,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\ContinuousRiemannIntegrabilityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite $\BHist$ packet and the displayed rows $F,T,D,J,W,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$;
\item interval-source visibility through the weakly located interval row $W$ and its located cell, endpoint-window, and regular-readback route;
\item pointwise modulus exposure through the continuous-function row $F$, with every modulus read tied to displayed interval cells and finite windows;
\item finite tagged partition coverage through $T$, including cell containment, tag containment, and the same $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows;
\item Darboux gap control through $D$, read as finite lower and upper sums over the displayed tagged partition route;
\item integrability handoff through $J$, allowed only after the Darboux gap row and partition coverage rows are visible;
\item Real-seal nonescape through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{enumerate}
No full integration theory, measure integration, open-cover compactness, quotient equality of real names, selected limiting value, Lean verification, or bridge checking is an obligation or output of this packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:continuous-riemann-integrability-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the continuous row, tagged partition, Darboux row, integrability handoff, weak interval selector, window family, regular readback, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

The interval and modulus rows are read first. The row $W$ supplies located cells and endpoint windows, while $F$ supplies pointwise modulus data only over those displayed cells and windows.

The finite partition route is then exposed by $T$. Tags and endpoints are read through $S$ and $R$, so the integration-facing partition is a finite BEDC object rather than a host partition or a selected representative family.

The Darboux and integrability rows are downstream. The row $D$ computes lower and upper finite sums over the displayed partition route, and $J$ exports integrability evidence only after that gap control is visible.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for the excluded analytic objects, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous Riemann integrability Darboux handoff]
\label{thm:continuous-riemann-integrability-darboux-handoff}
Every integration-facing consumer of an accepted $\ContinuousRiemannIntegrabilityUp$ carrier factors through the finite partition and Darboux rows
$$
W,F \longmapsto T,S,R \longmapsto D \longmapsto J \longmapsto E,H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the consumer receives a finite located-cell, pointwise-modulus, tagged-partition, lower/upper Darboux, integrability-handoff, and Real-seal route rather than quotient reals, host equality, choice-selected witnesses, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an integration-facing read. The weak interval selector $W$ and continuous row $F$ force the request through located cells, endpoint windows, and pointwise modulus rows before any partition or sum row can be inspected.

Next read the tagged partition route $T,S,R$. The finite cells and tags are displayed together with their StreamName windows and RegSeqRat readbacks, so later rows cannot detach the partition from the interval source.

The Darboux row $D$ is then read as lower and upper finite sums over those displayed cells and tags. The integrability row $J$ is therefore a handoff from finite Darboux gap control, not from a quotient real or host equality judgment.

The terminal seal and structural rows $E,H,C,P,N$ preserve that route. No excluded object is stored in the carrier, so every consumer factors through the displayed finite partition and Darboux rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous Riemann integrability uniform-modulus handoff]
\label{thm:continuous-riemann-integrability-uniform-modulus-handoff}
Every accepted $\ContinuousRiemannIntegrabilityUp$ carrier whose continuous row factors through a displayed uniform-modulus or composed-modulus route passes the Darboux gap and $\RiemannIntegrableUp$ handoff through the finite chain
$$
\ContinuousUp,\ \UniformContinuityCompositionUp,\ \TaggedRealPartitionUp,\ \DarbouxSumUp,\ \RiemannIntegrableUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
The handoff therefore reads composed modulus data before the Darboux row and exports no host integral selector, quotient function equality, open-cover compactness argument, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:continuous-riemann-integrability-carrier}. The displayed rows are the continuous row $F$, tagged partition row $T$, Darboux row $D$, Riemann-integrable handoff $J$, weak interval selector $W$, StreamName windows $S$, RegSeqRat readbacks $R$, Real seal $E$, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Use \autoref{thm:continuous-riemann-integrability-namecert-obligations} for row exhaustion. When the continuous row is supplied by a uniform-modulus or composed-modulus route, the only admitted modulus source is the displayed $\ContinuousUp$ row together with the $\UniformContinuityCompositionUp$ composed-modulus handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-continuity-composition-namecert}.

Now apply \autoref{thm:continuous-riemann-integrability-darboux-handoff}. The tagged partition, Darboux lower and upper sums, and integrability row must be read after the same StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readbacks, and terminal Real seal, so the composed-modulus source cannot bypass the finite Darboux route.

Since the carrier stores no host integral selector, quotient function equality, open-cover compactness proof, or ambient completeness object, the listed finite rows are the whole uniform-modulus handoff.
\end{proof}
