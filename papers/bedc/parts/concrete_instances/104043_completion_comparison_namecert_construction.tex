\begin{definition}[Completion comparison carrier]
\label{def:completion-comparison-carrier}
A $\CompletionComparisonUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(M,F,T,R,S,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ source row, carrying the metric completion presentation before any comparison consumer reads a completion point.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ source row, carrying the filter presentation over the same finite tolerance requests.
\item $T$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ source row, carrying the net presentation and its directed observation rows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegularCauchyComparisonUp$ row, comparing the sequence-style regular Cauchy route with the filter and net routes through displayed tolerance windows.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ row used to make equality and uniqueness reads pass through separated comparison data rather than host equality.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ row, recording the uniform completion presentation consumed by entourage-facing readers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric, filter, net, regular-comparison, separated, uniform, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the comparison route
$$
M,F,T,R \longmapsto S,U \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompletionComparisonUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\RegularCauchyComparisonUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionComparisonUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, choice of a convergent subnet, host equality of limits, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Completion comparison NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:completion-comparison-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompletionComparisonUp$ carrier $K=(M,F,T,R,S,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionComparisonUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, metric completion source exposure, Cauchy-filter source exposure, Cauchy-net source exposure, regular-Cauchy comparison exposure, separated-metric equality routing, uniform-completion handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, local naming, and non-escape of quotient completion equality, selected representatives, choice-selected subnets, host equality of limits, open-cover compactness, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:completion-comparison-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the metric row $M$, filter row $F$, net row $T$, regular comparison row $R$, separated row $S$, uniform row $U$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The source presentations are read before any equality or uniqueness comparison. Rows $M,F,T,R$ keep metric, filter, net, and regular-Cauchy evidence inside the same finite packet, so a consumer cannot replace the displayed comparison by a quotient object or a selected representative.

The separated and uniform rows then control the target-facing read. Row $S$ supplies the separated comparison boundary, and row $U$ supplies the uniform completion handoff. Rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route.

Since the excluded host-side principles are absent from the carrier, every local obligation is one of the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Completion comparison filter-net correspondence]
\label{thm:completion-comparison-filter-net-correspondence}
Every filter-net completion read of an accepted $\CompletionComparisonUp$ packet factors through the displayed route
$$
\MetricCompletionUp,\ \CauchyFilterUp,\ \CauchyNetUp,\ \RegularCauchyComparisonUp,\ \SeparatedMetricUp,\ \UniformCompletionUp,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
In particular, a consumer comparing Cauchy filters with Cauchy nets must read the shared metric row, the regular-Cauchy comparison row, the separated-metric row, and the uniform-completion handoff from \autoref{def:completion-comparison-carrier}; it cannot import quotient equality of completions, a selected representative sequence, a choice-selected subnet, a host limit theorem, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier $K=(M,F,T,R,S,U,H,C,P,N)$. The filter row $F$ and net row $T$ occur in the same packet as the metric row $M$, so their comparison begins from displayed finite completion data.

The regular comparison row $R$ supplies the common tolerance-window route. The separated row $S$ is the only equality-facing component, and the uniform row $U$ is the only entourage-facing completion handoff.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve that route under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. No extra completion object appears in $K$, so every filter-net read factors through the stated rows.
\end{proof}
