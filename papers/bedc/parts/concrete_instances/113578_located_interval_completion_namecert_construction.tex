\begin{definition}[Located interval completion carrier]
\label{def:located-interval-completion-carrier}
A $\LocatedIntervalCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(E_0,E_1,D,W,Q,M,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E_0$ and $E_1$ are the lower and upper $\RealUp$ endpoint rows. They are read as terminal real-name seals, not as host real representatives.
\item $D$ is the positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, including the interval-width budget, endpoint tolerance budget, and halving ledger used by completion reads.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row opened at the endpoint indices and tolerances displayed by $E_0,E_1,D$.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the same finite windows and dyadic tolerance data.
\item $M$ is the Cauchy modulus row. It records the finite schedule that makes the endpoint window reads Cauchy-compatible inside the located interval route.
\item $R$ is the completion seal row, consumed only after the endpoint rows, dyadic tolerance row, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, and Cauchy modulus row have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $E_0,E_1,D,W,Q,M,R$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for interval-completion requests through the finite route
$$
E_0,E_1,D \longmapsto W,Q,M \longmapsto R,H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedIntervalCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedIntervalCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $E_0,E_1,D,W,Q,M,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located interval completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-interval-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedIntervalCompletionUp$ carrier $L=(E_0,E_1,D,W,Q,M,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedIntervalCompletionUp}$ surface is exhausted by the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item endpoint admission for the two $\RealUp$ rows and their displayed interval order context;
\item dyadic tolerance admission for interval-width and endpoint-read budgets through $D$;
\item finite StreamName window exposure through $W$ at the same endpoint and tolerance requests;
\item RegSeqRat readback through $Q$, attached to the displayed windows rather than to quotient streams;
\item Cauchy modulus compatibility through $M$, requiring the endpoint reads and the interval-width budget to share one finite schedule;
\item completion sealing through $R$, downstream of $E_0,E_1,D,W,Q,M$;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation exports a selected representative, quotient stream equality, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-interval-completion-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the endpoint rows, dyadic tolerance row, finite windows, regular rational readback, Cauchy modulus, completion seal, and structural rows.

The endpoint and tolerance obligations are upstream of every completion read. A consumer cannot inspect $R$ before $E_0,E_1$ and $D$ determine the displayed interval and its finite precision request.

The window and readback obligations then follow the sibling routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. The Cauchy modulus row $M$ binds those finite reads to the same dyadic budget supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}.

The completion seal is therefore terminal only for this displayed route. Since $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet without adding a selector, quotient, choice schedule, trichotomy decision, host equality row, or ambient completeness theorem, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

$$

The route exports no selected limit, quotient stream equality, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{proof}

The finite observation route is fixed by those rows. The StreamName row supplies only the requested finite windows, the RegSeqRat row supplies the regular rational readback attached to those windows, and the Cauchy modulus row binds the readbacks to one completion schedule.

Transport and replay do not create a second completion source. The rows $H$ and $C$ move and replay only the displayed coordinates, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming for the same packet.

Thus every exported Cauchy-facing completion observation factors through $E_0,E_1,D,W,Q,M,R,H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for a selected limit, quotient stream equality, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient completeness, none of those principles is exported by the route.
\end{proof}
