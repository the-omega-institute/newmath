\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\KoszulDualityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-koszulduality-namecert}

The Koszul-duality interface $\KoszulDualityUp$ packages the contravariant derived equivalence between graded algebras of finite global dimension and their Koszul-dual algebras. It depends on $\NameCert_{\DerivedCatUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-derivedcat-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tensorproduct-namecert}), and bridges algebraic and homological certificates through the same Ext-classifier.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:koszulduality-carrier}

\begin{definition}[KoszulDuality BHist Ext carrier]
\label{def:koszulduality-bhist-ext-carrier}
A $\KoszulDualityUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ package consisting
of four visible rows. The first row is a $\DerivedCatUp$ object row naming the
source graded algebra object and its displayed derived-category endpoint. The
second row is a $\TensorProductUp$ graded tensor row naming the tensor surface
used to form the dual algebra side. The third row is the displayed
$\mathrm{Ext}$-classifier row comparing the derived object row with the graded
tensor row through their certificate dependencies. The fourth row is a variance
ledger recording that the dual row reverses source and target order
contravariantly.

The carrier is this finite packet of rows and endpoint data. It contains no
quotient derived category, no chosen representative, no external equivalence
object, and no host-level model of Koszul duality outside the displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.KoszulDualityUp.KoszulDualityBHistExtCarrier}

\begin{theorem}[KoszulDuality Ext classifier stability]
\label{thm:koszulduality-ext-classifier-stability}
Let two $\KoszulDualityUp$ carrier packets have derived-category rows and
graded tensor rows related by displayed $\hsame$ classifiers from
$\NameCert_{\DerivedCatUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$. If the
$\mathrm{Ext}$-classifier row in the first packet is read only through those
two dependency certificates and the visible variance ledger, then the same
$\mathrm{Ext}$-classifier package repacks over the transported rows, and the
contravariant dual row is accepted in the transported packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.KoszulDualityUp.KoszulDualityExtCarrier\_classifier\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Project the derived-category dependency certificate from the source packet.
It transports the source object endpoint and all displayed derived rows through
the listed $\hsame$ comparison.

Project the graded tensor dependency certificate in the same way. The tensor
surface is transported as a certificate row, so the target-side endpoint used
by the dual algebra surface is still available after transport.

Replay the $\mathrm{Ext}$-classifier ledger with these two transported rows.
The classifier has no independent carrier beyond the two projected dependency
rows and the variance tag, so it repacks over the transported endpoints.

Finally read the contravariant tag. Since the tag records only reversal of
source and target order, the transported dual row is accepted by the same
finite ledger with the order reversed.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.KoszulDualityUp.KoszulDualityBHistExtCarrier\_ext\_classifier\_stability}

\begin{theorem}[KoszulDuality derived tensor ledger exactness]
\label{thm:koszulduality-derived-tensor-ledger-exactness}
For every accepted $\KoszulDualityUp$ carrier, the ledger exposes exactly the
$\DerivedCatUp$ object row, the $\TensorProductUp$ graded tensor row, the
displayed $\mathrm{Ext}$-classifier row, and the variance tag recording
contravariant source-target reversal. No quotient category, chosen
representative, external equivalence object, triangulated enhancement, or
model-category localization is exported by the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.KoszulDualityUp.KoszulDualityDerivedTensorLedger\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier of
\autoref{def:koszulduality-bhist-ext-carrier}. Its accepted rows are the
derived object row, graded tensor row, $\mathrm{Ext}$-classifier row, and
variance ledger.

The dependency certificates may be projected only through the rows named in
the packet. The derived side contributes the object endpoint, and the tensor
side contributes the graded tensor endpoint.

The $\mathrm{Ext}$ row is then a displayed classifier between those projected
endpoints. Since no further constructor is present in the packet, none of the
excluded host objects can be read from the accepted carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KoszulDuality NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:koszulduality-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\KoszulDualityUp}$ surface is the package consisting of
the carrier of \autoref{def:koszulduality-bhist-ext-carrier}, the displayed
$\mathrm{Ext}$ classifier, the stability clause of
\autoref{thm:koszulduality-ext-classifier-stability}, the exact ledger of
\autoref{thm:koszulduality-derived-tensor-ledger-exactness}, and the source
coverage assertion that every accepted duality row is read from the displayed
derived-category row, graded tensor row, classifier row, and variance tag.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.KoszulDualityUp.KoszulDualityBHistExtCarrier\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.KoszulDualityUp.KoszulDualityExtCarrier\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier definition as the source predicate for the local certificate.
It supplies precisely the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows that the certificate is
allowed to classify.

The classifier row is the displayed $\mathrm{Ext}$ row. Its transport
obligation is supplied by
\autoref{thm:koszulduality-ext-classifier-stability}, which repacks the same
classifier after the two dependency certificates transport their rows.

The ledger and source-coverage obligations are supplied by
\autoref{thm:koszulduality-derived-tensor-ledger-exactness}. Together they say
that every accepted row is visible in the finite package and that no host
duality structure is added by the certificate boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KoszulDuality bridge boundary exclusions]
\label{thm:koszulduality-bridge-boundary-exclusions}
The $\KoszulDualityUp$ boundary supplies only the BEDC
$\mathrm{Ext}$-classifier correspondence between the displayed
$\DerivedCatUp$ and $\TensorProductUp$ rows. It does not assert a full derived
equivalence, a triangulated enhancement, a model-category localization, or a
standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.KoszulDualityUp.KoszulDualityBHistExtCarrier\_bridge\_boundary\_exclusions}
\begin{proof}
Read the public boundary through
\autoref{thm:koszulduality-namecert-obligation-surface}. The available data
are carrier, classifier, stability, ledger exactness, and source coverage.

Each of those rows factors through the finite
\autoref{def:koszulduality-bhist-ext-carrier} packet. That packet contains the
dependency rows and the variance tag, but it contains no object representing a
host derived equivalence or any localization apparatus.

Therefore the boundary exports the stated BEDC classifier correspondence and
nothing beyond the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KoszulDuality standard derived-tensor bridge]
\label{thm:koszulduality-standard-derived-tensor-bridge}
Any standard bridge for the public $\KoszulDualityUp$ packet is restricted to
the displayed $\DerivedCatUp$ source row, the $\TensorProductUp$ graded tensor
row, the $\mathrm{Ext}$-classifier row, the variance ledger, the $\hsame$
transport, the $\Cont$ routes, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and the
$\NameCert$ surface already isolated by the public certificate. It does not
supply a full derived equivalence, a triangulated enhancement, a
model-category localization, or a host quotient.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Combine \autoref{thm:koszulduality-namecert-obligation-surface} with
\autoref{thm:koszulduality-bridge-boundary-exclusions}. The obligation surface
lists the carrier, classifier, stability, ledger exactness, and source coverage
rows, while the exclusions identify which host structures are absent from that
public packet.

Repack the same finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ and $\mathrm{Ext}$ rows as the standard
bridge boundary. The bridge has no independent coordinate beyond the displayed
derived-category row, tensor row, classifier row, variance ledger, transports,
routes, provenance, and naming surface.

Therefore a full derived equivalence, a triangulated enhancement, a
model-category localization, and a host quotient remain outside the exported
bridge boundary.
\end{proof}
