The $\ObserverHsameClassifierUp$ surface realizes the observer-history identity vision of \autoref{ch:visions-observer-hist-identity} as a finite classifier row. It consumes the single-packet observer surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert} and the pairwise licensed-observation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistoryidentity-namecert}. Its role is narrower than either sibling: it decides only whether displayed, licensed observations agree through $\hsame$-mediated rows, and it has no coordinate for observer-substance or host equality.

\falsifiablePrediction{If an accepted ObserverHsameClassifierUp packet changes only an unlicensed observer-substance row, then every public classifier read is unchanged because no such row is in the carrier.}

\independenceWitness{Nearest siblings: observerhistidentity, observerhistoryidentity, observersubjectboundary, observerkerneltrace, observerfilter. The carrier is not bijective to any of them: it has a classifier-output and licensed-observation ledger row that the identity carriers and trace/filter packets do not have, while it lacks their full trace/filter selector coordinates.}

\begin{definition}[Observer hsame classifier carrier]
\label{def:observer-hsame-classifier-carrier}
An $\ObserverHsameClassifierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(H_0,H_1,A,R,L,D,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H_0$ and $H_1$ are the two observer-history rows being compared;
\item $A$ is the licensed observation ledger, listing exactly the mark, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{SigRel}$, and classifier reads permitted to affect the decision;
\item $R$ stores the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the two histories, licensed observations, classifier-output row, continuation routes, package provenance, and local name;
\item $L$ records the local ledger tying each licensed observation to its source history row and naming boundary;
\item $D$ is the finite classifier-output row, recording agreement, disagreement, or refusal only after the relevant licensed observation row has passed through $R$ and $C$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads the histories, licensed observations, transports, and classifier output;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{SigRel}$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverHsameClassifierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $A,R,L,D,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for unlicensed observation, observer-substance, host equality of observers, quotient history identity, or an observation outside the displayed ledger.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observer hsame classifier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-hsame-classifier-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-hsame-classifier-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverHsameClassifierUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $K=(H_0,H_1,A,R,L,D,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier transport through the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $R$;
\item licensed-observation exactness for every row in $A$ and no row outside $A$;
\item decision-row confinement, requiring every public classifier read to factor through $D,L,R,C,P,N$;
\item non-escape from unlicensed observation, observer-substance, host equality, quotient identity, and hidden classifier state.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ObserverHsameClassifierUp.ObserverHsameClassifierTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:observer-hsame-classifier-carrier}. The packet displays both observer-history rows, the licensed observation ledger, $\hsame$ transports, local ledger, classifier-output row, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row before any public decision is available.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The row $R$ moves each licensed observation and each displayed output row to the common $\hsame$ surface, while $C$ records the only routes along which a consumer may read those transported rows.

Licensed-observation exactness follows by finite inventory of $A$ and $L$. A public classifier decision may use a mark, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{SigRel}$, or prior classifier row only when that row appears in $A$ with its source and boundary entry in $L$.

The remaining obligations follow from absence of coordinates. Since the carrier contains no observer-substance row, host equality row, quotient row, or hidden classifier state, every accepted public read is confined to $A,R,L,D,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observer hsame classifier non-escape]
\label{thm:observer-hsame-classifier-nonescape}
Every public consumer of an accepted $\ObserverHsameClassifierUp$ packet reads only the licensed observation ledger $A$, the componentwise $\hsame$ transports $R$, the local ledger $L$, the classifier-output row $D$, the displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$, package provenance $P$, and local name row $N$. Changing any unlicensed observer-substance datum leaves the public classifier read unchanged, because no such datum is a carrier coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to the rows of \autoref{def:observer-hsame-classifier-carrier}. The only consumer-facing comparison data are the licensed observation ledger, transport row, local ledger, classifier-output row, route row, provenance row, and name row.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:observer-hsame-classifier-namecert-obligations}. They require every public decision to pass through the displayed licensed observations and the $\hsame$-mediated transport surface before the classifier-output row can be read.

There is no observer-substance coordinate and no unlicensed observation coordinate in the carrier. A change confined to such data therefore changes no row in $A,R,L,D,C,P,N$, so every public classifier read remains the same.
\end{proof}
