\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PolishSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-polishspace-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Alexandrov and Urysohn, a Polish space is treated here as a complete
separable metric object whose admissible data are displayed by finite naming
routes rather than by ambient set-theoretic selection. The
$\PolishSpaceUp$ packet synthesizes the metric carrier of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the completion handoff of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the countable dense
surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separablemetric-namecert}, the
finite stream-window interface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the regular rational
readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. Its
role is to expose the L10 complete-separable metric synthesis node without
claiming choice-selected dense points, quotient equality, open-cover
compactness, or a host-level completion principle.

\begin{definition}[Polish-space carrier]
\label{def:polish-space-carrier}
A $\PolishSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ row containing the admitted point-history
carrier, metric readbacks, distance classifier, and local metric
$\NameCert$.
\item $K$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff row; it records the displayed
Cauchy-window and limit-seal route over the same metric carrier.
\item $D$ is the $\SeparableMetricUp$ row; it stores the countable dense
schedule as public finite name data, not as a choice function over arbitrary
balls.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule that enumerates the inspected dense
names and the Cauchy-window requests made by downstream consumers.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row used to observe finite rational
windows on the scheduled dense names and Cauchy approximants.
\item $W$ is the finite observation ledger joining requested tolerances,
stream positions, regular rational windows, metric-radius checks, and
completion handoff rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,K,D,S,R,W$ and for
the local certificate rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from a metric-radius request to
the dense schedule, from a Cauchy-window request to the completion handoff,
and from either request to the regular rational readback.
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricSpaceUp$,
$\CompleteMetricUp$, $\SeparableMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PolishSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only by transporting these displayed
rows to a common metric, complete-metric, separable-metric, stream-name, and
regular-readback surface. It has no coordinate for selected dense points,
quotient equality of completions, arbitrary open covers, or ambient host
sets.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Polish-space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:polish-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ carrier
$P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PolishSpaceUp}$ surface
consists exactly of six obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed metric, complete-metric,
separable-metric, stream-name, regular-readback, observation-ledger,
transport, continuation, provenance, and local-name rows;
\item classifier transport to a common $\MetricSpaceUp$ surface before either
the complete-metric or separable-metric row is consumed;
\item stability of the completion handoff $K$ and the countable dense schedule
$D$ under the componentwise $\hsame$ transports recorded in $H$;
\item exactness of the observation ledger $W$ as the only source of metric
radius checks, Cauchy windows, dense-name inspections, and regular rational
readbacks;
\item $\Cont$ replay from radius or Cauchy-window requests through $S,R,W$ to
the corresponding metric, completion, or dense-schedule row;
\item $\Pkg$ provenance and local $\NameCert$ naming for exactly the displayed
packet.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these six rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:polish-space-carrier}. The projection
exposes the metric row, complete-metric handoff, separable-metric dense
schedule, stream schedule, regular rational readback, observation ledger,
transport rows, continuation rows, provenance row, and local name.

The classifier route begins at the metric surface. The rows in $H$ transport
$M,K,D,S,R,W$ together, so the completion handoff and dense schedule are never
compared over different metric carriers.

The observation exactness row is exhausted by $W$. A metric-radius request, a
Cauchy-window request, and a dense-name inspection all read their finite
stream positions and regular rational observations through this ledger.

Finally replay $C$ and attach $G,N$. These rows route, source, and name the
already displayed packet; they do not introduce selected dense points,
quotient completion equality, arbitrary open covers, or ambient host sets.
Hence the six listed rows exhaust the local Polish-space naming obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Polish-space StreamName dense handoff]
\label{thm:polish-space-streamname-countable-dense-handoff}
Let $P=(M,K,D,S,R,W,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\PolishSpaceUp$ carrier. Every
consumer that reads the countable dense surface must factor through the
displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$, the finite observation ledger $W$, and
the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$ before using the $\SeparableMetricUp$ row
$D$ or the $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff $K$. Consequently the packet exports a
countable dense handoff by finite names and regular rational windows, not by a
choice-selected dense subset or a quotient-completion representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer of the dense surface. Carrier admission exposes $D$
only as a separable-metric row over the metric carrier $M$ and the stream
schedule $S$.

Read the requested dense-name index through $S$ and the finite ledger $W$.
The regular rational observation used by the consumer is then obtained through
$R$, so the handoff has a visible finite window before the dense row is
consumed.

If the same consumer also requests a completion read, the route passes through
$K$ after the same metric surface and observation ledger have been fixed.
The transports in $H$ keep the dense schedule, completion handoff, and metric
classifier on one admitted surface.

The remaining rows $C,G,N$ only replay, source, and name this finite route.
Since the carrier has no row for a choice-selected dense subset or a quotient
completion representative, the dense handoff can export only the displayed
stream-name and regular-readback data.
\end{proof}
