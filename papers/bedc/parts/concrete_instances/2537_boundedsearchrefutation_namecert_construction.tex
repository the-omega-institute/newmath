\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BoundedSearchRefutationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-boundedsearchrefutation-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\BoundedSearchRefutationUp$ surface packages a finite refutation search as BEDC naming data. It records the proposition being inspected, a displayed depth budget, the finite frontier reached by that budget, a refutation witness when one is found or a $\Gap$ row when the budget is exhausted, and the provenance rows through which a consumer may replay the bounded search. The packet is not a global proof-search oracle: every accepted read is bounded by the displayed depth row and remains inside $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

\begin{definition}[BoundedSearchRefutation carrier]
\label{def:bounded-search-refutation-carrier}
A $\BoundedSearchRefutationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(A,K,F,R,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the proposition row being searched for a certificate-level refutation;
\item $K$ is the finite depth budget row, including the displayed start depth, successor ledger, and final bounded depth inspected by the packet;
\item $F$ is the search frontier row, listing exactly the finite proof states reached within the budget $K$ and the local edges by which each frontier entry was generated;
\item $R$ is the displayed refutation witness row, present only when the frontier contains a route from an assumed witness of $A$ to $\bot$;
\item $G$ is the $\Gap$ row, present exactly when the bounded frontier ends without a displayed refutation witness and recording that the packet makes no truth claim beyond the budget;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for proposition rows, depth rows, frontier rows, refutation-witness rows, $\Gap$ rows, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading the proposition row, advancing the bounded frontier, replaying a displayed refutation witness, or exposing the $\Gap$ row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\msame$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Gap$, bounded frontier rows, displayed refutation witnesses, and the surrounding $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedSearchRefutationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no host-level proof search, no unbounded recursion principle, no truth predicate for all naming certificates, no excluded-middle branch, and no witness selected outside the displayed frontier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedSearchRefutation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-search-refutation-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\BoundedSearchRefutationUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:bounded-search-refutation-carrier} consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item proposition row: the accepted packet displays the proposition row $A$ whose refutation is being searched;
\item finite depth budget: every frontier expansion is indexed by the displayed budget row $K$ and stops at the displayed final bounded depth;
\item search frontier: the row $F$ lists the complete finite frontier generated within $K$, including the displayed local edges used to reach each entry;
\item refutation or gap: the packet exposes either a displayed refutation witness row $R$ or the $\Gap$ row $G$, and the two rows are read through the same bounded frontier surface;
\item provenance: all reads of $A,K,F,R,G$ transport through the stored $\hsame$ row $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$, the package row $P$, and the local naming row $N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bounded-search-refutation-carrier}. The proposition row, depth budget, frontier, refutation witness, gap row, transports, continuations, provenance, and local name are all displayed coordinates of the same finite packet.

The depth and frontier obligations are finite because $K$ supplies the only allowed expansion budget. Each frontier entry in $F$ is reached by a displayed local edge before the final bounded depth is reached, and no consumer can ask for a row outside that finite ledger.

The refutation-or-gap obligation reads the same frontier surface in both cases. If $R$ is present, the packet exports the displayed route to $\bot$; if $G$ is present, the packet exports only the bounded absence of such a displayed route. The provenance rows $H,C,P,N$ keep both reads inside the same NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedSearchRefutation depth soundness]
\label{thm:bounded-search-refutation-depth-soundness}
For an accepted $\BoundedSearchRefutationUp$ carrier $B=(A,K,F,R,G,H,C,P,N)$, every refutation witness exported from $R$ is generated by a finite path in the frontier row $F$ whose length is bounded by the displayed depth budget $K$. A consumer that reads $G$ receives only the statement that no refutation witness is displayed inside that same bounded frontier; it receives no claim about depths beyond $K$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer read of the refutation side. The witness row $R$ is not an independent proof object; by the NameCert obligations it is read through the bounded frontier row $F$ and the budget row $K$.

The continuation routes $C$ replay only the local frontier edges recorded in $F$. Since each edge is indexed by the successor ledger in $K$, the replayed route cannot exceed the displayed final bounded depth.

For a gap read, the same bounded ledger is inspected. The row $G$ records the absence of a displayed witness within $F$ at budget $K$, not the absence of all refutations and not a truth value for $A$. Thus depth soundness is exactly the finite replay discipline of the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedSearchRefutation non-escape]
\label{thm:bounded-search-refutation-nonescape}
No accepted $\BoundedSearchRefutationUp$ packet exports a global proof-search oracle. Every consumer read factors through $A,K,F,R,G,H,C,P,N$, and the packet has no coordinate for unbounded search, excluded middle over certificate predicates, host truth, quotient proof equality, or witness selection outside the displayed frontier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates in \autoref{def:bounded-search-refutation-carrier}. They name a proposition row, a finite budget, a finite frontier, a displayed refutation witness or gap row, and the BEDC transport, continuation, package, and naming rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:bounded-search-refutation-depth-soundness}. A witness read is a bounded frontier replay, while a gap read is only a bounded frontier report. Neither read supplies data for a search at a greater depth.

The forbidden oracle shapes require coordinates absent from the packet: an unbounded recursion route, a truth classifier over all naming certificates, an excluded-middle branch, or a selector outside $F$. Since no such row exists, every consumer remains inside the displayed bounded-search certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedSearchRefutation NameCert boundary]
\label{thm:bounded-search-refutation-namecert-boundary}
The $\BoundedSearchRefutationUp$ carrier realizes a finite refutation-search boundary for contradiction and refutation consumers. A consumer may use a displayed witness row $R$ as a bounded certificate-level refutation of $A$, or it may use the $\Gap$ row $G$ as a bounded search report at depth $K$; in both cases the read is routed through the same frontier, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows. The packet never upgrades a $\Gap$ row into a proof of $A$ or into a proof that $A$ has no refutation at greater depth.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First assemble the finite surface from \autoref{thm:bounded-search-refutation-obligations}. The proposition row, budget, frontier, refutation-or-gap row, and provenance rows form one NameCert packet.

If a consumer reads $R$, depth soundness gives a replayable frontier path to $\bot$ bounded by $K$. That is a permitted certificate-level refutation because the assumed witness of $A$ is discharged by displayed $\Cont$ rows inside the carrier.

If a consumer reads $G$, non-escape gives the boundary. The row reports only that the displayed frontier at the displayed depth contains no refutation witness, and it carries no branch that decides $A$ or all deeper searches.

Therefore the public boundary is a finite BEDC search certificate. It supports bounded refutation and bounded gap reporting, while refusing any global proof-search, truth-branch, or unbounded-depth conclusion.
\end{proof}
