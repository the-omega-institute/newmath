\begin{definition}[Closed-normal route certificate carrier]
\label{def:closed-normal-route-certificate-carrier}
A $\ClosedNormalRouteCertificateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(M,E,O,K,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\ClosedNormalConsistencyMainUp$ row naming the checked main-result endpoint as a closed beta-strong false-inhabitation exclusion;
\item $E$ is the $\NormalFalseExclusionUp$ row carrying the already-normal false-endpoint exclusion consumed by the endpoint route;
\item $O$ is the $\MetaCICSubjectReductionObstructionUp$ row recording that arbitrary closed proofs are not transported into the normal fragment by this certificate;
\item $K$ is the $\CandidateEvidenceSNRouteUp$ row naming the candidate-mediated route that supplies the strong-normalization side of the normal endpoint;
\item $R$ is the closed-normal route row from $K,E,O$ to the main-result endpoint $M$;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the sibling rows and the route row;
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ routes from the sibling packets to the route row and from the route row to the main-result consumer;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over the main-result chapter and the four sibling concrete packets;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedNormalRouteCertificateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for full closed consistency, closed subject reduction, unrestricted normalization, quotient proof conversion, host evaluation, ambient consistency, or a proof that every closed false proof reaches the normal endpoint.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-normal route certificate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-normal-route-certificate-namecert-obligations}
Every accepted $\ClosedNormalRouteCertificateUp$ packet binds exactly the finite obligation surface of \autoref{def:closed-normal-route-certificate-carrier}: carrier admission for $Q=(M,E,O,K,R,H,C,P,N)$, sibling-row exactness for the main-result endpoint, normal false exclusion, subject-reduction obstruction, and candidate-evidence route, route-row exactness for $R$, componentwise classifier stability through $H$, displayed continuation stability through $C$, package provenance through $P$, local naming through $N$, and non-escape from full closed consistency or closed subject reduction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. Admission exposes only the finite tuple $Q=(M,E,O,K,R,H,C,P,N)$, so every public read starts from the four sibling rows and the displayed route row.

The positive endpoint is $M$. The rows $E$ and $K$ supply the already-normal false exclusion and the candidate-mediated route that feed the endpoint, while $O$ records the subject-reduction obstruction that prevents the packet from being reread as full closed consistency.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name only this route surface. Since no coordinate supplies closed subject reduction, unrestricted normalization, quotient proof conversion, host evaluation, or ambient consistency, the accepted packet certifies only the closed-normal endpoint route displayed by $R$.
\end{proof}
