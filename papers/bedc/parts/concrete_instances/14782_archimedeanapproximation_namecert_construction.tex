\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ArchimedeanApproximationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-archimedeanapproximation-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\ArchimedeanApproximationUp$ packet records the finite Archimedean approximation boundary used before a rational or dyadic scale computation may be sealed as a real-name observation. It sits over the natural-number bound rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert}, the rational source rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite name windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readbacks of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names approximation as displayed BEDC route data, not as quotient real equality, a selected host limit, countable choice, trichotomy, or a completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[ArchimedeanApproximation carrier]
\label{def:archimedean-approximation-carrier}
An $\ArchimedeanApproximationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(N,Q,D,\epsilon,S,R,E,H,C,P,M)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $N$ is the $\NatUp$ bounding row that records the finite index or scale bound used by the approximation request.
\item $Q$ is the $\RatUp$ rational source row naming the displayed lower and upper rational bounds read at that scale.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row that refines the rational bounds into a dyadic tolerance ledger.
\item $\epsilon$ is the displayed tolerance request, read only through $D$ and the $\NatUp$ bound row $N$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row opened at the bound and tolerance named by $N,D,\epsilon$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row extracted from the same finite window $S$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row that exposes the approximation to a Real-facing consumer after the finite window and readback have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the natural bound, rational source, dyadic tolerance, stream window, regular readback, real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay that opens the finite window, reads the rational and dyadic rows, extracts the regular rational readback, and hands it to the $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NatUp$, $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $M$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanApproximationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for quotient real equality, selected representatives, host limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanApproximation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:archimedean-approximation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ArchimedeanApproximationUp$ carrier
$A=(N,Q,D,\epsilon,S,R,E,H,C,P,M)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanApproximationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the
following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed $\NatUp$ bound, $\RatUp$ rational source, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance, $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item bound compatibility, stating that the requested finite approximation scale is read from the same $N$ row that controls the tolerance request $\epsilon$;
\item rational-bound exposure, stating that the lower and upper rational rows exported to the consumer are the displayed $\RatUp$ data in $Q$;
\item dyadic tolerance exactness, stating that every tolerance comparison used by the packet is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger named by $D$;
\item window and readback compatibility, stating that $S$ and $R$ are the $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows opened at the bound and tolerance already displayed by $N,D,\epsilon$;
\item real-seal handoff, stating that the only Real-facing route is the row $E$ after the displayed finite window and regular rational readback;
\item non-escape, stating that quotient real equality, selected host limits, countable choice, trichotomy, and ambient completeness are absent from the carrier.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:archimedean-approximation-carrier}. The
only available coordinates are the natural bound, rational source, dyadic
tolerance, finite stream window, regular rational readback, real seal, and the
structural support rows.

Bound compatibility and rational-bound exposure are immediate from the rows
$N,Q,\epsilon$. The tolerance request is attached to the displayed natural
bound, and the lower and upper rational readings are the $\RatUp$ data named
by $Q$ rather than host rational choices outside the packet.

Dyadic tolerance exactness is the role of $D$. The finite window $S$ can be
opened only at the bound and tolerance already named by $N,D,\epsilon$, and
the readback $R$ is the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ extraction from that same
window.

The Real-facing consumer then sees only the seal row $E$ transported and
replayed by $H$ and $C$. The provenance $P$ and local naming row $M$ name the
same route and add no further coordinate, so the listed obligation rows
exhaust the local $\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanApproximationUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanApproximation real-seal handoff]
\label{thm:archimedean-approximation-real-seal-handoff}
Every Real-facing read exported by an accepted $\ArchimedeanApproximationUp$
carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\NatUp,\ \RatUp,\ \DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp .
$$
Concretely, a consumer first reads the natural bound and rational source rows,
then the dyadic tolerance row, then the finite stream window and regular
rational readback, and only then the $\RealUp$ seal. The handoff exports no
quotient real equality, selected host limit, countable-choice schedule,
trichotomy, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an accepted carrier and project its Real-facing read. The row $E$
is the only $\RealUp$ seal coordinate, so the read cannot begin from a host
real or a quotient class.

Before $E$ can be consumed, the continuation row $C$ displays the bound,
rational source, tolerance, window, and readback rows. Thus the route first
passes through $N$ and $Q$, then through $D$, then through $S$ and $R$.

The transport row $H$ preserves the same components, while $P$ and $M$ record
only provenance and local naming. Since the carrier has no additional
coordinate, every Real-facing approximation read factors through the stated
finite route and cannot obtain any of the excluded principles.
\end{proof}
