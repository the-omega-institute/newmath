\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier parallel composite determinacy]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-parallel-composite-determinacy}
For BHist histories $source,middle,target,f,g,fg,fg',mod_F,mod_G,mod_{FG},
mod_{FG}',cert_F,cert_G,cert,cert'$, suppose
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,f,middle,mod_F,cert_F),\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(middle,g,target,mod_G,cert_G),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(f,g,fg),\quad \mathsf{Cont}(f,g,fg'),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_F,mod_G,mod_{FG}),\quad
\mathsf{Cont}(mod_F,mod_G,mod_{FG}'),\\
\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_{FG},cert),\quad
\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_{FG}',cert')
\end{array}
$$
hold. Then the two exposed composites are both continuous function carriers
and their graph, modulus, and certificate histories are determined:
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,fg,target,mod_{FG},cert)\land\\
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,fg',target,mod_{FG}',cert')\land\\
\hsame(fg,fg')\land\hsame(mod_{FG},mod_{FG}')\land\hsame(cert,cert').
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First compose through the first visible ledger. The two given base carriers,
together with $\mathsf{Cont}(f,g,fg)$, $\mathsf{Cont}(mod_F,mod_G,mod_{FG})$,
and $\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_{FG},cert)$, satisfy the hypotheses of
Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-comp-closed}. This gives
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,fg,target,mod_{FG},cert)$.

The second displayed ledger has the same two base carriers. Applying the same
composition theorem to $\mathsf{Cont}(f,g,fg')$,
$\mathsf{Cont}(mod_F,mod_G,mod_{FG}')$, and
$\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_{FG}',cert')$ gives
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,fg',target,mod_{FG}',cert')$.

Map determinacy is local to the shared graph rows. Continuation determinacy
for the two rows $\mathsf{Cont}(f,g,fg)$ and $\mathsf{Cont}(f,g,fg')$ gives
$\hsame(fg,fg')$; the same kernel determinacy applied to the two modulus rows
gives $\hsame(mod_{FG},mod_{FG}')$.

Finally compare the terminal certificate rows. They share the target history,
and the recovered modulus sameness transports the row
$\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_{FG}',cert')$ back across
$\mathsf{Cont}(target,mod_{FG},cert)$, yielding $\hsame(cert,cert')$. These
five outputs are exactly the two composite carriers plus the three exposed
history-sameness conclusions.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous function carrier certificate semantic naming certificate]
\label{thm:continuous-function-carrier-certificate-semantic-namecert}
For BHist histories $source,map,target,modulus,cert$, any carrier witness
$\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert)$ induces
the semantic naming certificate
$$
\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(C,C,C,\hsame),
\qquad
C(c) :=
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,c).
$$
Thus the certificate-slot carrier, pattern, and ledger are all the displayed
fiber $c\mapsto\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,c)$,
and the classifier is $\hsame$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The displayed carrier gives the source witness for the semantic certificate at
the point $cert$. This is exactly the inhabited source field for the fiber $C$.

The classifier fields are the standard $\hsame$ rows. Reflexivity classifies
each displayed certificate with itself, symmetry reverses a displayed
classification, and transitivity composes two displayed classifications.

Carrier transport is confined to the certificate slot. Keep $source$, $map$,
$target$, and $modulus$ fixed by $\hsame$ reflexivity, use the supplied
$\hsame$ comparison of certificate histories, and apply
\autoref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-hsame-transport} to transport the
continuous function carrier along that comparison.

The pattern and ledger predicates are the same fiber $C$ as the source
predicate. Their soundness and completeness maps are therefore identity
projections, so the semantic naming certificate is repacked from the preceding
fields.
\end{proof}
