\begin{definition}[DyadicModulus carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-modulus-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row naming dyadic scales, finite comparison budgets, rounding windows, and precision requests.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row whose visible entries are exactly the observations demanded by the tolerance row.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained by reading the stream windows under the dyadic tolerance ledger.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal boundary, available only after $D,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for tolerance, stream-window, regular-readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each dyadic precision request through $D,S,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicModulusUp}$ row.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected representatives, host real equality, countable choice, ambient Real completeness, or a detached host-level modulus function.

\begin{theorem}[DyadicModulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicModulusUp$ carrier $M=(D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, dyadic tolerance exposure, finite-window exposure, regular rational readback, terminal Real seal handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming.

Every Real or Completion consumer must read the displayed route
$$
D \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N
$$
before using the packet as a dyadic tolerance-modulus certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-modulus-carrier}. The tuple displays exactly the dyadic tolerance row, stream-window row, regular-readback row, terminal seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

The analytic route is forced in order. A precision request first reads $D$, then the finite stream observations in $S$, then the regular rational handoff $R$, and only then reaches the seal boundary $E$.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier has no quotient-stream, selected-limit, choice, host-equality, ambient-completeness, or detached-modulus coordinate, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicModulus RegSeqRat handoff]

$$

\begin{proof}

The regular rational readback is the row $R$, and $R$ is downstream of both the dyadic tolerance row $D$ and the stream-window row $S$. Thus a consumer cannot read a regular name, Real seal, or Completion boundary before the finite tolerance and window data have been displayed.
