\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SeparableSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-separable-space-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki's countable-base presentation and Bishop and Bridges'
constructive dense-sequence discipline, the $\SeparableSpaceUp$ packet names a
countable dense schedule as finite BEDC data. It sits over the metric carrier
of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the finite window surface
of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational
readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the
terminal real-name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert},
and the complete-separable consumer of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polishspace-namecert}. The packet exports a
dense schedule visible to L10 completion consumers without host set equality,
selected dense points, countable choice, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Separable-space carrier]
\label{def:separable-space-carrier}
A $\SeparableSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(M,S,R,D,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ row containing the admitted metric carrier,
distance classifier, and metric local $\NameCert$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ row that schedules finite dense-name probes
and radius requests.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row that reads the rational windows attached
to those probes.
\item $D$ is the countable-density ledger, recording that every displayed
metric-radius request reaches an admitted stream probe with a regular rational
distance bound.
\item $W$ is the observation ledger joining stream positions, regular
rational windows, metric-radius requests, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal reads.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,S,R,D,W$ and the
local certificate rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a density request to the
corresponding stream and regular-rational readback rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SeparableSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these rows. It has no
coordinate for a host dense subset, selected nearest point, quotient point
equality, or external enumeration.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Separable-space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:separable-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SeparableSpaceUp$ carrier
$Q=(M,S,R,D,W,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\SeparableSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of six obligation
rows: carrier admission for $M,S,R,D,W,H,C,P,N$; classifier transport to a
common $\MetricSpaceUp$ row before density is read; stability of the stream
schedule and regular rational readback under $H$; exactness of $W$ as the
only route from a radius request to finite stream and rational-window data;
displayed $\Cont$ replay from density requests through $S,R,D,W$; and $\Pkg$
provenance with local naming for precisely this packet. No additional
obligation stores a selected dense point, countable-choice function, host set
equality, or ambient completion datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:separable-space-carrier}. Carrier
admission exposes the metric row, stream schedule, regular rational readback,
density ledger, observation ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local
name.

The classifier route begins at $M$. Componentwise transport in $H$ moves
$M,S,R,D,W$ together, so a dense schedule cannot be compared after changing
the metric source.

The exactness row is $W$. Every radius request reaches a finite stream
position in $S$, reads its rational window through $R$, and records the
density witness in $D$ before any terminal $\RealUp$ seal is consumed.

Finally replay the request through $C$ and source it through $P,N$. Since no
row names a selected point, host dense subset, countable-choice selector, or
ambient completion, the six listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separable-space StreamName dense readback]
\label{thm:separable-space-streamname-dense-readback}
Let $Q=(M,S,R,D,W,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparableSpaceUp$ carrier. Every
dense-readback consumer reads the countable schedule only through
$\StreamNameUp$ row $S$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$, observation ledger $W$, and
density ledger $D$ over the common $\MetricSpaceUp$ row $M$. When the read is
Real-facing it may consume only the terminal $\RealUp$ seal already displayed
by $R,W$; when it is completion-facing, a $\PolishSpaceUp$ or
$\SeparableMetricUp$ consumer receives the same finite schedule route and no
selected dense point, quotient point equality, host enumeration, or ambient
completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Expose the accepted carrier. The only scheduling coordinate is $S$, and the
only regular rational readback coordinate attached to the schedule is $R$.

Use \autoref{thm:separable-space-namecert-obligations}. Observation exactness
forces a dense-readback request through $W$ before the density ledger $D$ is
available, while $H$ keeps the route over the same metric row $M$.

The Real-facing boundary is downstream of the regular rational observations.
Thus any $\RealUp$ seal is read after $S,R,W,D$, not as a separate host-real
shortcut.

Finally pass the displayed route to the complete-separable consumers. The
$\PolishSpaceUp$ and $\SeparableMetricUp$ packets may inspect the same finite
schedule rows, but the carrier contains no coordinate for selected dense
points, quotient equality, host enumeration, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
