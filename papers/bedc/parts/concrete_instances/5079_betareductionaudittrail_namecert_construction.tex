\begin{definition}[Beta-reduction audit-trail carrier]
\label{def:beta-reduction-audit-trail-carrier}
A $\BetaReductionAuditTrailUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(S,V,O,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the ordered $\mathrm{BetaStep}$ ledger, separating the direct
redex row from application, lambda, and Pi congruence rows;
\item $V$ is the $\mathrm{BetaConv}$ common-reduct ledger read after the
single-step ledger and before any typing-boundary row;
\item $O$ is the obstruction-trigger ledger for beta substitution,
application-argument stability, lambda-domain preservation, and Pi-domain
preservation;
\item $D$ is the subject-reduction discharge-socket row that records whether a
typed-preservation discharge is present for the displayed trigger;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the step, conversion,
obstruction, discharge, route, provenance, and name rows;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ route through the ordered stages
$S,V,O,D$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:metacic-beta-audit-map},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedbetacongruencetrigger-namecert},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicbetaaudit-namecert},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-betastepboundary-namecert},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subject-reduction-discharge-socket-namecert},
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BetaReductionAuditTrailUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no direct typing-preservation row, no closed
subject-reduction theorem, no hidden conversion rule for typing, and no
terminal audit escape outside $H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Beta-reduction audit-trail NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:beta-reduction-audit-trail-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:beta-reduction-audit-trail-carrier}, the local
$\NameCert_{\BetaReductionAuditTrailUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier
admission for $R=(S,V,O,D,H,C,P,N)$, beta-step stage readback through $S$,
common-reduct conversion readback through $V$, obstruction-trigger readback
through $O$, subject-reduction socket readback through $D$, componentwise
$\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$,
package provenance through $P$, and terminal naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:beta-reduction-audit-trail-carrier}. Its only stage coordinates
are the beta-step ledger $S$, the conversion ledger $V$, the obstruction
ledger $O$, and the discharge-socket row $D$.

Read the siblings in the displayed order. The typed congruence trigger supplies
the constructor-facing boundary, the MetaCIC beta audit packet separates
reduction and conversion, the beta-step boundary keeps obstruction triggers
visible, and the subject-reduction discharge socket records whether a typed
preservation discharge has been supplied.

Finally inspect $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, source, and name the
same finite trail. Since no coordinate contains an unguarded typing rule, a
closed subject-reduction theorem, or an external audit ledger, the local
NameCert obligations are exhausted by the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Beta-reduction audit-trail Stage F falsifiability]
\label{thm:beta-reduction-audit-trail-stage-falsifiability}
Every accepted $\BetaReductionAuditTrailUp$ packet has a falsifiable terminal
ledger: a consumer that reads a direct typing-preservation row must also read
the subject-reduction discharge socket $D$ for the same ordered trail
$S,V,O,D$. If $D$ is absent, the accepted trail may still expose beta-step,
conversion, and obstruction-trigger rows, but it cannot export typed
preservation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the ordered carrier. A typing-facing read can only occur after the
consumer has passed through $S$, then $V$, then $O$, because those are the
displayed step, conversion, and obstruction stages.

The only row after the obstruction stage that can mention typed-preservation
discharge is $D$. The transport, continuation, package, and name rows
$H,C,P,N$ keep the same coordinate order and do not add a new discharge row.

Thus any accepted trail lacking $D$ lacks a direct typing-preservation read.
The packet remains readable as a beta audit trail, but its terminal ledger
falsifies any attempted escape from reduction and conversion into typed
preservation.
\end{proof}
