The $\SubjectReductionRouteTriangleUp$ packet realizes the discharge-route
triangle of \autoref{ch:sr-discharge-routes} as finite NameCert data. It sits
beside the route certificate of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreductiondischarge-namecert} and the
preservation packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert}:
the first names the discharge rows, the second names the subject-reduction
endpoint, and this chapter records the bundle route, endpoint, and obstruction
boundary in one visible packet.

\begin{definition}[Subject-reduction route-triangle carrier]
\label{def:subject-reduction-route-triangle-carrier}
A $\SubjectReductionRouteTriangleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(B,E,O,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the explicit discharge bundle row carrying the four subject-reduction discharge obligations;
\item $E$ is the represented $\SubjectReductionUp$ endpoint row reached only after the discharge payload has been displayed;
\item $O$ is the obstruction row recording the present closed-CIC non-inhabitation boundary for the missing discharge hypotheses;
\item $L$ is the route-ledger row, recording that the consumer entered through $B$ directly;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the bundle, endpoint, obstruction, ledger, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes from the bundle row to the endpoint and from the obstruction row to the route ledger;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:sr-discharge-routes}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreductiondischarge-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert};
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SubjectReductionRouteTriangleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is only this finite triangle packet. It contains no inhabitant of
the missing discharge hypotheses, no closed subject-reduction theorem for the
present closed-CIC substrate, no host conversion rule, and no route outside the
displayed bundle, obstruction, and ledger rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectReductionRouteTriangle bundle projection]
\label{thm:subject-reduction-route-triangle-bundle-projection}
Every accepted $\SubjectReductionRouteTriangleUp$ carrier projects an explicit
discharge-bundle route
$$
B,H,C,P,N
$$
whose payload is the same four-row discharge surface consumed by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreductiondischarge-namecert}. This
projection may be read as a bundle-facing handoff to the represented
$\SubjectReductionUp$ endpoint $E$, but it does not certify that the four
discharge rows are inhabited for the present closed-CIC substrate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $T=(B,E,O,L,H,C,P,N)$ to its bundle coordinate. The row
$B$ is already a displayed packet row, and the transport, route, provenance,
and local naming coordinates $H,C,P,N$ preserve that row as finite NameCert
data.

The sibling discharge chapter supplies the semantic boundary for the four
discharge obligations. Reading $B$ through
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreductiondischarge-namecert} therefore
exposes the obligation surface, not an inhabitant of the obligations.

Finally pass to $E$ only through the displayed continuation row. Since the
carrier also keeps the obstruction row $O$, the projection cannot be confused
with a closed subject-reduction theorem for the present substrate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectReductionRouteTriangle obstruction boundary]
\label{thm:subject-reduction-route-triangle-obstruction-boundary}
In every accepted $\SubjectReductionRouteTriangleUp$ carrier, the obstruction
row $O$ separates route transport from discharge inhabitation. The packet may
expose the bundle projection and the represented $\SubjectReductionUp$
endpoint row, but any consumer that asks whether the present closed-CIC
substrate supplies the four missing discharge hypotheses must read $O$ and
cannot replace it by $B$ or $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the triangle inventory. The bundle row, endpoint row, and obstruction row
are separate coordinates of $T$.

The projection theorem identifies the route available to a consumer. It
transports the four-field payload to the represented endpoint row, while it
does not claim an inhabitant for the present closed-CIC discharge obligations.

It remains to inspect the obstruction coordinate. The route ledger records
that the bundle route was used, and $O$ records the boundary at which the
closed-CIC substrate still lacks the required inhabitants. Since $O$ is not
definitionally one of $B,E$, no consumer can erase that boundary by choosing a
route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectReductionRouteTriangle NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:subject-reduction-route-triangle-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:subject-reduction-route-triangle-carrier}, the
local $\NameCert_{\SubjectReductionRouteTriangleUp}$ surface has four rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the explicit discharge bundle row $B$;
\item the represented subject-reduction endpoint row $E$;
\item the obstruction and non-inhabitation boundary row $O$;
\item the route ledger row $L$, transported and replayed through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
These four rows exhaust the public route-triangle certificate. The certificate
names how bundle access reaches the subject-reduction endpoint and where the
present obstruction remains, without adding host conversion, hidden discharge
evidence, erased derivation semantics, quotient equality of MetaCIC terms, or
a closed subject-reduction theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First expose the carrier row by row. The rows $B,E,O,L$ are the finite data
being certified, while $H,C,P,N$ provide transport, route replay, provenance,
and local naming for exactly those rows.

The four-row sketch follows by grouping the carrier coordinates by their route
role. The bundle row gives the direct route, the endpoint row records the
represented preservation consumer, the obstruction row records the
missing-inhabitant boundary, and the ledger records that the bundle route has
been followed.

The sibling chapters supply the boundary checks. The discharge packet of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreductiondischarge-namecert} fixes the
obligation surface, and the subject-reduction packet of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert} fixes the endpoint
surface. Since no other coordinate appears in $T$, the listed rows exhaust the
local NameCert obligations and leave the excluded objects outside the packet.
\end{proof}
