Following Kelley and Bishop--Bridges, the $\TopologicalClosureUp$ packet records closure of a displayed subset through finite neighbourhood evidence. It sits after the metric closure certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-closure-namecert}, the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the topology surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the finite ball discipline of $\MetricBallUp$, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a topological closure interface for BEDC neighbourhood reads; it is not a host closure operator, not a powerset completion, not quotient equality, not open-cover compactness, and not an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[TopologicalClosure carrier]
\label{def:topological-closure-carrier}
A $\TopologicalClosureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(T,S,x,U,B,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the ambient $\TopologyUp$ row naming the finite neighbourhood request surface.
\item $S$ is the displayed subset row whose closure is being tested.
\item $x$ is the candidate point row, readable only through the same neighbourhood requests.
\item $U$ is the neighbourhood-hit ledger, pairing each displayed neighbourhood of $x$ with a retained source witness from $S$.
\item $B$ is the optional $\MetricBallUp$ refinement row when the topology is read through metric balls.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row for the retained neighbourhood and witness observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for metric radii and witness distances when a metric refinement is present.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the neighbourhood, ball, window, and readback rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the topology, subset, candidate, neighbourhood ledger, metric-ball refinement, windows, readback, and Real seal rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from neighbourhood requests to source witnesses, through optional metric balls, finite windows, regular readback, and the Real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TopologicalClosureUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\MetricBallUp$, $\MetricClosureUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TopologicalClosureUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts only packets whose topology row, subset row, candidate point, neighbourhood-hit ledger, optional metric-ball refinement, finite windows, rational readbacks, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are all displayed. It has no coordinate for a host closure operator, arbitrary open-set quantification, powersets, quotient equality, countable choice, or ambient completion of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TopologicalClosure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:topological-closure-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\TopologicalClosureUp$ carrier $K=(T,S,x,U,B,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\TopologicalClosureUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the ambient topology row, displayed subset row, candidate point row, neighbourhood-hit ledger, optional metric-ball refinement, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, RealUp sealing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every public closure read must pass through those rows before it can consume the candidate point as closure evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier to the displayed rows $T,S,x,U,B,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. The topology, subset, and candidate rows give the only admissible source data for a closure read.

The ledger row $U$ binds each neighbourhood request around $x$ to a retained source witness from $S$. When the topology is read metrically, the row $B$ refines that same request through finite $\MetricBallUp$ data before the window row $W$ and readback row $R$ are exposed.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name the same displayed packet. Since no row stores a host closure operator, a powerset, quotient equality, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, the NameCert obligations are exhausted by the listed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TopologicalClosure metric neighbourhood route]
\label{thm:topological-closure-metric-neighbourhood-route}
Every $\MetricClosureUp$ or metric-topology consumer of an accepted $\TopologicalClosureUp$ packet reads a candidate closure point through the finite route
$$
T,S,x,U,B,W,R,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
The consumer receives a topology row, displayed subset row, candidate point, neighbourhood-hit ledger, metric-ball refinement, finite StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readbacks, RealUp seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. It receives no ambient closure operator, arbitrary open-set family, quotient-set equality, selected convergent net, or RealUp completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a metric-neighbourhood consumer. The carrier exposes the candidate point $x$ only together with the common topology row $T$ and source subset row $S$, so the consumer cannot detach the candidate from the displayed neighbourhood interface.

Use the ledger $U$ to align each neighbourhood read with a source witness. The metric-ball row $B$, finite window row $W$, regular rational row $R$, and Real seal $E$ then give the same finite evidence surface used by the $\MetricClosureUp$ packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-closure-namecert}.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve that route under transport, replay, provenance, and naming. Thus the handoff is the finite topological closure surface itself, not a stronger host closure, selected net, powerset, quotient, or completeness principle.
\end{proof}
