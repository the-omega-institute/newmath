\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NormalFalseExclusionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-normalfalseexclusion-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\NormalFalseExclusionUp$ packet exposes the closed-normal exclusion row from \autoref{ch:closed-metacic-consistency-assembly} as finite BEDC naming data. It sits beside the subject-reduction obstruction packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert}: the present chapter names the already-normal false-proof exclusion, while the sibling obstruction records why arbitrary closed proofs are not transported into that normal fragment.

\begin{definition}[NormalFalseExclusion carrier]
\label{def:normal-false-exclusion-carrier}
A $\NormalFalseExclusionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(T,C,B,Y,H,R,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the closed MetaCIC proof-term row whose type read is internal False;
\item $C$ records the closedness witness for $T$;
\item $B$ records the $\mathsf{BetaStrong}$ normality witness for $T$;
\item $Y$ is the internal False typing row consumed by the MetaCIC consistency assembly;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the closedness, normality, false typing, theorem row, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $R$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading the closed proof-term row, the normality row, and the false typing row without leaving the packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over MetaCIC typing, closedness, beta-strong normality, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NormalFalseExclusionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for a reduction sequence from an arbitrary closed proof, a subject-reduction theorem, a host consistency theorem, a quotient proof identity, or a hidden normalization selector.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NormalFalseExclusion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:normal-false-exclusion-namecert}
For every accepted $\NormalFalseExclusionUp$ carrier
$$
F=(T,C,B,Y,H,R,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\NormalFalseExclusionUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission, closed proof-term exposure, beta-strong normality exposure, internal False typing exposure, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ route stability, $\Pkg$ provenance, and non-escape from arbitrary closed consistency or subject-reduction transport.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:normal-false-exclusion-carrier}. The proof-term row, closedness row, normality row, false typing row, transport row, route row, provenance row, and local naming row are all displayed coordinates.

The closed-normal obligations are componentwise. The row $C$ supplies closedness, $B$ supplies beta-strong normality, and $Y$ supplies internal False typing for the same proof-term row $T$.

The structural rows then restrict all consumer access. Transports through $H$, routes through $R$, provenance through $P$, and naming through $N$ replay only the displayed rows, so the local certificate has no source for arbitrary reduction, subject reduction, quotient proof identity, or host consistency.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NormalFalseExclusion closed-normal boundary]
\label{thm:normal-false-exclusion-boundary}
An accepted $\NormalFalseExclusionUp$ carrier cannot contain a closed beta-strong normal proof term whose internal type is False. Equivalently, any attempted read of the displayed rows $T,C,B,Y$ is rejected by the MetaCIC closed-normal false-proof exclusion theorem before it can be exported as a consumer-facing proof row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.MetaCIC.no\_closed\_normal\_proof\_of\_false}
\begin{proof}
Read the closedness, beta-strong normality, and internal False typing rows from the carrier. These are exactly the inputs named by the closed-normal exclusion node in \autoref{ch:closed-metacic-consistency-assembly}.

Apply the checked MetaCIC exclusion theorem. It rules out a closed proof term of internal False once beta-strong normality is already part of the input.

The conclusion is a boundary statement for the displayed packet. It rejects the closed-normal false-proof row, but it does not derive a reduction route from arbitrary closed proofs into normal form.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NormalFalseExclusion subject-reduction boundary]
\label{thm:normal-false-exclusion-subject-reduction-boundary}
The $\NormalFalseExclusionUp$ packet names only the already-normal false-proof exclusion. It does not supply the subject-reduction route needed to reduce an arbitrary closed proof of internal False to the normal-form input of \autoref{thm:normal-false-exclusion-boundary}; that route remains outside the packet and is recorded by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. They include a proof-term row, closedness, beta-strong normality, internal False typing, structural transport, routes, provenance, and local naming.

No coordinate records a beta-reduction path from an arbitrary closed proof to $T$. No coordinate records a subject-reduction theorem along such a path.

Thus the packet can invoke the closed-normal exclusion only when normality is already displayed. The neighboring subject-reduction obstruction chapter names the missing route and prevents this packet from being read as a full closed consistency certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NormalFalseExclusion consumer non-escape]
\label{thm:normal-false-exclusion-consumer-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\NormalFalseExclusionUp$ packet can read only the finite rows
$$
T,C,B,Y,H,R,P,N.
$$
The consumer may use the checked closed-normal exclusion on those rows, but it receives no arbitrary closed-proof normalization, no subject-reduction discharge, no host consistency principle, no quotient proof identity, and no hidden proof search oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{def:normal-false-exclusion-carrier}. The tuple $F$ lists the whole consumer surface.

Use \autoref{thm:normal-false-exclusion-namecert} for the certificate obligations. It confines reads to the closed proof-term row, the normality row, the internal False typing row, and the displayed structural rows.

Finally use \autoref{thm:normal-false-exclusion-boundary}. The checked exclusion rejects the closed-normal false-proof input itself, while the absence of arbitrary normalization and subject-reduction coordinates blocks every stronger consistency read.
\end{proof}
