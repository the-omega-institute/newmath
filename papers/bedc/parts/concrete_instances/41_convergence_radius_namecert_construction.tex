\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\ConvergenceRadiusUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-convergence-radius-namecert}

This chapter gives a concrete naming-certificate construction for the convergence radius of a complex power series \(\sum a_n z^n\) at the paper-proof level. The certificate sits above \(\ComplexLimitUp\) (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-limit-namecert}) and the formal power series chapter (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fps-namecert}), and gives an explicit unary-history witness for the radius rather than a real-number supremum. Absolute convergence inside the disk is proved by geometric majorant; the Cauchy-Hadamard characterization is given in constructive form using only explicit unary suprema. The construction is fully constructive and uses no \(\mathsf{Classical.choice}\), no \(\mathsf{Quot.sound}\), and no \(\mathsf{propext}\), so the certificate's Lean targets fall under the project's axiom-purity gate.

\begin{theorem}[ConvergenceRadius public export]
\label{thm:convergence-radius-public-export}
\autoref{thm:conv-rad-semantic-namecert} exports the public
$\ConvergenceRadiusUp$ boundary over the power-series carrier,
geometric-bound rows, convergence-radius witness, radius classifier transport,
endpoint packages, and checked semantic certificate rows. Consumers may cite
this public boundary without treating Cauchy--Hadamard exactness or standard
complex-analysis bridge rows as checked consequences.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Cite the semantic naming certificate of
\autoref{thm:conv-rad-semantic-namecert}. It repacks the checked carrier,
classifier, transport, witness, and append-unary closure rows into the
$\NameCert$ schema for $\ConvergenceRadiusUp$.

Read the same packet through the checked-row reduct of
\autoref{def:convrad-checked-row-reduct}. This identifies the rows available
to consumers: the power-series carrier, geometric-bound data,
convergence-radius witness, radius classifier transport, endpoint packages,
and semantic certificate rows.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:convrad-checked-rows-do-not-force-cauchy-hadamard}. The public
export therefore does not treat Cauchy--Hadamard exactness or standard
complex-analysis bridge rows as checked consequences.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ConvergenceRadius public boundary exactness]
\label{thm:convergence-radius-public-boundary-exactness}
The public $\ConvergenceRadiusUp$ boundary consists exactly of the
power-series carrier, geometric-bound rows, convergence-radius witness, radius
classifier transport, endpoint packages, checked-row reduct, and semantic
certificate rows. Every consumer read of the public export factors through
one of these rows.

Conversely, each listed row is visible through
\autoref{thm:convergence-radius-public-export}. The boundary does not imply
real-number supremum completeness, Cauchy--Hadamard exactness, or standard
complex-analysis bridge rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:convergence-radius-public-export}. Its public export
names the available carrier, geometric-bound, witness, classifier-transport,
endpoint-package, and semantic-certificate rows.

Refine this list through \autoref{thm:conv-rad-semantic-namecert} and
\autoref{def:convrad-checked-row-reduct}. The semantic certificate supplies
the naming packet, while the checked-row reduct gives the exact consumer
boundary through which every public read must factor.

For the converse, read each displayed component through the same public export.
Each listed row is one of the rows repacked by the certificate and retained by
the checked-row reduct.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:convrad-checked-rows-do-not-force-cauchy-hadamard}. Since the
checked rows do not force Cauchy--Hadamard exactness, the boundary also does
not provide real-number supremum completeness or the standard
complex-analysis bridge rows.
\end{proof}
