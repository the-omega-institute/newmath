Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive completions and Bourbaki/Kelley on uniform embeddings, the $\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingReflectionUp$ packet records the finite route by which a dense completion embedding is reflected through the separated completion interface. It sits over the dense completion embedding of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-dense-embedding-namecert}, the dense uniform embedding surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dense-uniform-embedding-namecert}, the functorial completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-functoriality-namecert}, the separated reflection boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separated-completion-reflection-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular-rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the completion-embedding reflection route only; it does not select representatives, form a quotient completion outside the displayed certificate, or import ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionEmbeddingReflection carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-embedding-reflection-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingReflectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
J=(A,G,K,R,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the source approximation row whose metric or uniform histories feed the completion embedding.
\item $G$ is the $\CauchyCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp$ row naming the dense completion embedding and its image-window requests.
\item $K$ is the $\DenseUniformEmbeddingUp$ row that exposes uniform density before the completion target is read as separated.
\item $R$ is the $\CauchyCompletionFunctorialityUp$ row transporting maps and comparison windows through completion.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedCompletionReflectionUp$ row that reflects the target route through the separated completion boundary.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row attached to the same finite approximation windows.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row reached after $A,G,K,R,S,D$ have been displayed.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after the dense embedding, separated reflection, dyadic tolerance, and regular-rational readback rows are fixed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the analytic rows and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the same embedding-reflection route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingReflectionUp$, $\CauchyCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp$, $\DenseUniformEmbeddingUp$, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorialityUp$, $\SeparatedCompletionReflectionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingReflectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient-completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, a detached separated completion, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionEmbeddingReflection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-embedding-reflection-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingReflectionUp$ carrier $J=(A,G,K,R,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingReflectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed approximation row, dense completion embedding row, dense uniform embedding row, completion functoriality row, separated reflection row, dyadic tolerance row, regular-rational readback row, terminal real seal row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, and local naming. Every completion-facing read must pass through $A,G,K,R,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N$ in that order, and no read may bypass the separated reflection boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-embedding-reflection-carrier}. Its analytic rows are $A,G,K,R,S,D,Q,E$, followed by the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The dense route begins with $A$ and $G$. The approximation row supplies the finite source histories, while the $\CauchyCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp$ row records the image-window requests through which completion consumers may inspect the dense embedding.

The rows $K,R,S$ then bind the same route to dense uniform embedding, functorial completion transport, and separated reflection. A completion-facing consumer therefore reaches the separated target only after the dense route and functorial route have been displayed.

The rows $D,Q,E$ provide dyadic tolerance, regular-rational readback, and the terminal real seal. Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet, so carrier exhaustion blocks every excluded quotient, selector, detached completion, and ambient-completeness route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionEmbeddingReflection handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-embedding-reflection-handoff}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingReflectionUp$ packet receives the dense completion embedding only through the finite handoff
$$
\CauchyCompletionDenseEmbeddingUp \longmapsto \DenseUniformEmbeddingUp \longmapsto \CauchyCompletionFunctorialityUp \longmapsto \SeparatedCompletionReflectionUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \DyadicRatCoreUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp .
$$
Thus the consumer obtains a reflected separated-completion route with finite stream windows, dyadic tolerance, regular-rational readback, and a terminal real seal; it does not obtain a quotient-completion representative, a selected dense point, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:cauchy-completion-embedding-reflection-namecert-obligations}. The NameCert surface forces every public read to begin with the approximation row and the dense completion embedding row.

Read the sibling chapters named in the statement along the displayed order. The $\DenseUniformEmbeddingUp$ row supplies the uniform dense image, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorialityUp$ transports the route through completion, and $\SeparatedCompletionReflectionUp$ is the only separated target boundary available to the packet.

The finite observation route is then exposed through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$. These rows are exactly the rows $D,Q,E$ together with the stream-window data carried by $A,G,K,R,S$.

Since the carrier has only $A,G,K,R,S,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N$, the structural rows can preserve and name the handoff but cannot supply a quotient representative, selected dense point, countable-choice schedule, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{proof}
