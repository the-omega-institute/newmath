Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\DyadicTailBallUp$ packet records the finite late-tail enclosure by which a regular Cauchy source enters a displayed dyadic ball before any $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer reads a seal. It binds the center-radius surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicball-namecert}, the threshold and tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadictailbound-namecert}, the late-tail route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-tail-filter-namecert}, and the Real-specific Cauchy consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-cauchy-criterion-namecert}. The packet is a finite induction-friendly bridge from tail control to dyadic-ball containment; it is not a quotient stream, not a selected limit, not countable choice, not host real equality, not trichotomy, and not ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[DyadicTailBall late-tail carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-tail-ball-carrier}
A $\DyadicTailBallUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(B,D,F,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\DyadicBallUp$ center-radius packet, carrying the dyadic center, positive radius, scheduled observation row, and containment ledger read from \autoref{def:dyadicball-carrier}.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ threshold and tolerance ledger, recording the displayed late-tail threshold, dyadic tolerance budget, finite comparison rows, and seal-facing readback route.
\item $F$ is the $\RegularCauchyTailFilterUp$ route that exposes the cofinite late-tail neighbourhoods through the same threshold and tolerance rows before any downstream Cauchy consumer can inspect the packet.
\item $R$ is the $\RealCauchyCriterionUp$ consumer row, reading the late-tail windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal only after $B,D,F$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dyadic ball row, tail-bound ledger, regular Cauchy tail-filter route, Real Cauchy criterion consumer, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a late-tail request through $D$, then through $F$, into the containment row of $B$, and finally into the consumer row $R$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicBallUp$, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, $\RegularCauchyTailFilterUp$, $\RealCauchyCriterionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicTailBallUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows, their componentwise $\hsame$ transports, their displayed $\Cont$ routes, their $\Pkg$ provenance, and their local $\NameCert$ rows. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicTailBall NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-tail-ball-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicTailBallUp$ carrier $T=(B,D,F,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicTailBallUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic center-radius packet, dyadic tail-bound threshold and tolerance ledger, regular Cauchy tail-filter route, Real Cauchy criterion consumer, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item tail-bound exactness, requiring the threshold and tolerance read by $F$ and $R$ to be the same displayed threshold and dyadic budget carried by $D$;
\item ball-containment exactness, requiring the late-tail observations admitted through $F$ to enter the containment ledger of $B$ before the consumer row $R$ is exposed;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$ to preserve the ordered route $D,F,B,R$;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $P$ and $N$ to name only the displayed rows and to export no quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicTailBallUp.DyadicTailBallTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:dyadic-tail-ball-carrier}. The visible packet consists of the dyadic ball row $B$, the tail-bound ledger $D$, the late-tail filter route $F$, the Real Cauchy criterion consumer $R$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Tail-bound exactness is read from the order of $D$ and $F$. The regular Cauchy tail-filter route may expose only the threshold and tolerance ledger already displayed by $D$, so no unrecorded tail selector or separate rational budget can enter the packet.

Ball-containment exactness is supplied by $B$. The late observations admitted through $F$ are replayed through $C$ into the center-radius containment ledger of the $\DyadicBallUp$ row before $R$ can consume the packet.

Transport and replay are structural. Componentwise $\hsame$ in $H$ preserves the four public rows $D,F,B,R$, and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$ repeats the same route from late-tail request to dyadic-ball containment and then to the Real Cauchy criterion consumer.

Finally, provenance is exhausted by $P$ and $N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness, the local certificate cannot export any such object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicTailBall regular Cauchy consumer route]
\label{thm:dyadic-tail-ball-regular-cauchy-consumer-route}
Every $\RealUp$-facing or completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\DyadicTailBallUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
\DyadicTailBoundUp \longmapsto \RegularCauchyTailFilterUp \longmapsto \DyadicBallUp \longmapsto \RealCauchyCriterionUp .
$$
Thus a consumer first reads the displayed late-tail threshold and dyadic tolerance ledger, then the regular Cauchy tail-filter route, then the dyadic center-radius containment packet, and only then the Real Cauchy criterion row with its terminal seal. The exported route contains no quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice selector, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness assertion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet $T=(B,D,F,R,H,C,P,N)$. Carrier projection exposes $D$ as the only threshold and tolerance source available to the late-tail route.

Replay the continuation row $C$. The request first passes through $D$, then through $F$, so the regular Cauchy tail-filter reads the same finite threshold, tolerance, and comparison ledger rather than an external convergence predicate.

The next public row is the dyadic ball packet $B$. Its center-radius containment ledger receives the late-tail observations supplied by $F$, and its classifier reads only the displayed center, radius, schedule, observation, containment, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

Only after this containment row has been replayed can the consumer inspect $R$. The Real Cauchy criterion row therefore receives a finite dyadic-ball late-tail packet rather than a quotient stream or a selected limit.

Transport through $H$ and provenance through $P,N$ preserve the same ordered route. The excluded quotient, choice, host-equality, trichotomy, and completeness objects are absent from the carrier inventory, so no consumer read can export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicTailBall late-window stability]
\label{thm:dyadic-tail-ball-window-stability}
Let $T=(B,D,F,R,H,C,P,N)$ and $T'=(B,D',F',R,H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\DyadicTailBallUp$ packets sharing the same $\DyadicBallUp$ row $B$ and the same $\RealCauchyCriterionUp$ consumer row $R$. Suppose the second $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ row $D'$ refines the threshold and tolerance ledger of $D$ through the $\RegularCauchyTailFilterUp$ routes $F,F'$. Then every $\RealUp$-facing read available from $T$ is available from $T'$ through the same finite route
$$
\DyadicTailBoundUp \longmapsto \RegularCauchyTailFilterUp \longmapsto \DyadicBallUp \longmapsto \RealCauchyCriterionUp .
$$
The refinement exports no quotient stream, selected limit, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two carriers. They share the dyadic ball row $B$ and the Real Cauchy criterion consumer row $R$, so the only public late-tail change is the replacement of $D,F$ by the refined pair $D',F'$.

Use the refinement hypothesis to replay every late-tail request through $D'$ and $F'$. Because the refined threshold and tolerance ledger is served by the same regular Cauchy tail-filter route, each observation admitted by the first packet is still admitted before the shared containment ledger $B$ is read.

The Real-facing consumer remains fixed. After $B$ receives the refined late-tail observations, the same row $R$ consumes the packet, so the public route has the same four stages as in \autoref{thm:dyadic-tail-ball-regular-cauchy-consumer-route}.

Finally, replay $H',C',P',N'$ componentwise. These rows transport, continue, source, and name the refined packet without adding any coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}
