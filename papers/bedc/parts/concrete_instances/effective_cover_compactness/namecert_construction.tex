\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EffectiveCoverCompactnessUp$}

\origin{human}

Following Lebesgue and Bishop--Bridges constructive compactness, the $\EffectiveCoverCompactnessUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface by which a compact metric carrier exposes cover data only through explicit finite subcovers, mesh bounds, and Lebesgue-number handoffs. It sits after the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the finite-cover refinement surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-cover-refinement-namecert}, and the Lebesgue-number finite-cover route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lebesgue-number-finite-cover-namecert}, while supplying the compact-uniform modulus consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-uniform-modulus-namecert} and the vision corridor of \autoref{ch:visions-compact-uniform-continuity}. The chapter names effective finite-cover compactness, not arbitrary open-cover compactness.

\begin{definition}[Effective cover compactness carrier]
\label{def:effective-cover-compactness-carrier}
An $\EffectiveCoverCompactnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(K,O,R,L,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, carrying the inspected compact metric name, metric balls, and finite request windows.
\item $O$ is the cover row, listing the finitely observed basic opens and their metric radii.
\item $R$ is the $\FiniteCoverRefinementUp$ row, recording the finite refinement and subcover choices that remain visible to downstream consumers.
\item $L$ is the $\LebesgueNumberFiniteCoverUp$ row, supplying the displayed Lebesgue-number witness for the same finite cover.
\item $M$ is the compact-uniform handoff row consumed by $\CompactUniformModulusUp$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for compact metric data, cover rows, refinement rows, Lebesgue-number rows, compact-uniform handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a cover or modulus request through $K,O,R,L,M,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EffectiveCoverCompactnessUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\FiniteCoverRefinementUp$, $\LebesgueNumberFiniteCoverUp$, $\CompactUniformModulusUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveCoverCompactnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $K,O,R,L,M,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for arbitrary open-cover compactness, countable subcover extraction, choice of centers outside the displayed cover, ambient completeness, or a selected global modulus.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Effective cover compactness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:effective-cover-compactness-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EffectiveCoverCompactnessUp$ carrier
$$
E=(K,O,R,L,M,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveCoverCompactnessUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the compact metric row, cover row, finite refinement row, Lebesgue-number witness row, compact-uniform handoff row, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming. Every cover-facing consumer must read $K$ and $O$ before $R$, read $R$ before $L$, and read $L$ before the compact-uniform handoff $M$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:effective-cover-compactness-carrier}. The visible coordinates are exactly the compact metric source, cover ledger, finite refinement, Lebesgue-number witness, compact-uniform handoff, and structural rows.

The obligation order is forced by those coordinates. The finite refinement row $R$ is meaningful only over the displayed cover row $O$ and compact metric row $K$, while the Lebesgue-number row $L$ is read from that same finite cover surface.

Finally $M,H,C,P,N$ only hand off, transport, replay, source, and name the displayed finite-cover data. Since no other carrier coordinate exists, the local NameCert surface cannot export arbitrary open-cover compactness, a countable subcover selector, hidden centers, ambient completeness, or a selected global modulus.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Effective cover compactness finite-cover handoff]
\label{thm:effective-cover-compactness-finite-cover-handoff}
Every compact-uniform consumer of an accepted $\EffectiveCoverCompactnessUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
\CompactMetricUp \longmapsto \FiniteCoverRefinementUp \longmapsto \LebesgueNumberFiniteCoverUp \longmapsto \CompactUniformModulusUp .
$$
Thus the consumer sees the compact metric source row, displayed cover row, finite refinement row, Lebesgue-number witness, compact-uniform handoff, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming before any modulus-facing conclusion is available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a compact-uniform consumer and apply \autoref{thm:effective-cover-compactness-namecert-obligations}. The consumer must first display the compact metric source and the finite cover row.

Next it reads the finite-cover refinement and Lebesgue-number rows. These are the existing finite-cover surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-cover-refinement-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lebesgue-number-finite-cover-namecert}, so the handoff remains a finite certificate rather than an arbitrary open-cover principle.

The compact-uniform row $M$ is downstream of the same finite route. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name that route only, so the consumer receives no hidden subcover choice, no countable cover extraction, no ambient completeness, and no selected global modulus outside the displayed certificate.
\end{proof}
