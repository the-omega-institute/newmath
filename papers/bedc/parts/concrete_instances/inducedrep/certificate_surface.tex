\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:inducedrep-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp subgroup source boundary]
\label{thm:inducedrep-subgroup-source-boundary}
For every $\InducedRepUp$ packet of
\autoref{def:inducedrep-induction-restriction-packet}, the subgroup source
visible to downstream consumers is exactly the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ row
$S_{\leq}$ consumed from $\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$, together with its
$\hsame$ classifier row $H_{\leq}$, its $\Cont$ entries inside
$C_{\mathrm{src}}$, and its $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance inside $\Pi$. No
additional subgroup object, ambient group, inclusion witness, or host
restriction datum is part of the public source boundary.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.InducedRepUp.InducedRepSubgroupSource\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is the subgroup inclusion along which induction and
restriction are displayed. The BEDC source is not an ambient group object but
the consumed $\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ row $S_{\leq}$ in the packet.

Project the carrier. The only subgroup-source row in
$\mathcal{P}_{\InducedRepUp}$ is $S_{\leq}$, and the matching classifier
entry is $H_{\leq}$. Stability is therefore componentwise $\hsame$ transport
through $H_{\leq}$ together with the source continuation entries recorded in
$C_{\mathrm{src}}$.

The ledger and exactness clauses are read from $\Pi$ and $C_{\mathrm{src}}$.
Every exported subgroup-source entry occurs in those rows, and every such
entry is named by the packet. Since no $\StdBridge$ row is carried, the
standard bridge is absent at this boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp representation-ring action boundary]
\label{thm:inducedrep-representation-ring-action-boundary}
For every $\InducedRepUp$ packet of
\autoref{def:inducedrep-induction-restriction-packet}, the
representation-ring action visible at the induction and restriction
interface is exactly the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ row $R_{\mathrm{rep}}$
consumed from $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$, the displayed rows
$I_{\uparrow}$ and $R_{\downarrow}$, the $\hsame$ classifier rows
$H_{\mathrm{rep}},H_{\uparrow},H_{\downarrow}$, the continuation rows in
$C_{\mathrm{src}}$ and $C_{\mathrm{fr}}$, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance
row $\Pi$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is an action of representation rings on induction and
restriction. The BEDC source is the consumed
$\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$ row $R_{\mathrm{rep}}$, and the carrier
exposes only the two displayed action endpoints $I_{\uparrow}$ and
$R_{\downarrow}$.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The row $H_{\mathrm{rep}}$ transports
the representation-ring source, while $H_{\uparrow}$ and $H_{\downarrow}$
transport the displayed induction and restriction rows. The $\Cont$ ledgers
record the public continuation entries attached to those rows and to the
Frobenius comparison.

Exactness is obtained by checking the provenance row $\Pi$. Any action row
available to a consumer must be one of
$R_{\mathrm{rep}},I_{\uparrow},R_{\downarrow}$ or a continuation entry named
by the displayed ledgers. No host representation category, functor equality,
or standard bridge is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp Frobenius reciprocity classifier]
\label{thm:inducedrep-frobenius-reciprocity-classifier}
The Frobenius reciprocity data of an $\InducedRepUp$ packet is classified by
the finite comparison row $\Phi$, the endpoint classifier rows
$H_{\uparrow}$ and $H_{\downarrow}$, the source classifier rows $H_{\leq}$
and $H_{\mathrm{rep}}$, the $\Cont$ ledger $C_{\mathrm{fr}}$, and the
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row $\Pi$. Thus $\hsame$ equivalence of Frobenius
rows is a BEDC comparison between displayed induction and restriction rows,
not equality of a host adjunction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is Frobenius reciprocity for induction and
restriction. The BEDC carrier names this target by the row $\Phi$, whose
endpoints are the displayed rows $I_{\uparrow}$ and $R_{\downarrow}$ over the
source rows $S_{\leq}$ and $R_{\mathrm{rep}}$.

The classifier is the tuple of $\hsame$ rows. Source transport uses
$H_{\leq}$ and $H_{\mathrm{rep}}$; endpoint transport uses $H_{\uparrow}$ and
$H_{\downarrow}$. Stability of the Frobenius comparison is the finite
$\Cont$ transport recorded in $C_{\mathrm{fr}}$.

The ledger clause is exactly the combination of $C_{\mathrm{fr}}$ and
$\Pi$. Exactness follows because every Frobenius entry consumed by the public
interface is named by $\Phi$ or by that ledger. The packet carries no
$\StdBridge$ row, so the standard bridge is absent and the classifier remains
inside $\mathsf{BHist}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp public obligation surface]
\label{thm:inducedrep-public-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\InducedRepUp}$ obligation surface consists of the
$\mathsf{BHist}$ packet of
\autoref{def:inducedrep-induction-restriction-packet}, the consumed
$\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$ rows, the
displayed induction and restriction rows, the Frobenius comparison row, all
listed $\hsame$ classifier rows, the $\Cont$ ledgers
$C_{\mathrm{src}}$ and $C_{\mathrm{fr}}$, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance
row $\Pi$. No obligation outside those displayed rows is public.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is the public certificate for an induced
representation interface. The BEDC source is the pair of consumed
certificates $\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$, read through $S_{\leq}$ and
$R_{\mathrm{rep}}$.

The carrier is the finite packet
$\mathcal{P}_{\InducedRepUp}$. Its classifier rows are
$H_{\leq},H_{\mathrm{rep}},H_{\uparrow},H_{\downarrow}$, and stability is the
corresponding componentwise $\hsame$ transport together with the two finite
$\Cont$ ledgers.

The ledger and exactness conditions are public precisely because they are
recorded in $C_{\mathrm{src}}$, $C_{\mathrm{fr}}$, and $\Pi$. Every row in the
surface is a projection of the packet, and every projection repacks into the
same surface. With no $\StdBridge$ row present, the standard bridge is absent.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp consumer boundary]
\label{thm:inducedrep-consumer-boundary}
An $\InducedRepUp$ consumer may inspect exactly the source rows
$S_{\leq}$ and $R_{\mathrm{rep}}$, the displayed induction and restriction
rows $I_{\uparrow}$ and $R_{\downarrow}$, the Frobenius row $\Phi$, the
$\hsame$ classifier rows $H_{\leq},H_{\mathrm{rep}},H_{\uparrow},
H_{\downarrow}$, the $\Cont$ ledgers $C_{\mathrm{src}}$ and
$C_{\mathrm{fr}}$, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ row $\Pi$. Any use of
$\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ or $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$ by that
consumer factors through those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target is a downstream use of induction, restriction, and
Frobenius reciprocity. The BEDC source data are the two consumed
certificates, and the carrier presents them only through $S_{\leq}$ and
$R_{\mathrm{rep}}$.

For classifier stability, the consumer must use the displayed $\hsame$ rows.
Subgroup and representation-ring requests pass through $H_{\leq}$ and
$H_{\mathrm{rep}}$; induction and restriction requests pass through
$H_{\uparrow}$ and $H_{\downarrow}$; Frobenius requests pass through
$\Phi$ and $C_{\mathrm{fr}}$.

The ledger is finite and explicit: $C_{\mathrm{src}}$, $C_{\mathrm{fr}}$,
and $\Pi$ list exactly what the consumer may read. Exactness is the converse
projection from those listed rows back to the carrier. No host carrier,
external representation category, adjunction object, or standard bridge is
available beyond that boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp Frobenius ledger boundary]
\label{thm:inducedrep-frobenius-ledger-boundary}
Every Frobenius row exported by an $\InducedRepUp$ carrier of
\autoref{def:inducedrep-bhist-carrier} is one of the displayed finite rows
$F,\eta,\epsilon,\lambda$. Its endpoints are the displayed induction row $i$
and restriction row $r$, scoped through the consumed
$\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$ packets.
No host adjunction, host functor equality, naturality square, or external
representation category is exported at the Frobenius boundary.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.InducedRepUp.InducedRepFrobeniusLedger\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted packet to
$\mathcal{I}=(S,R,i,r,F,\eta,\epsilon,\rho,\lambda)$. The carrier definition
lists the Frobenius part as the finite ledger $F$ together with the unit row
$\eta$, the counit row $\epsilon$, and the public continuation entries in
$\lambda$.

The displayed endpoints of those rows are $i$ and $r$. Their subgroup source
is read through $S$, and their representation-ring source and target rows are
read through $R$. The source certificates are precisely the SubgroupUp and
RepresentationRingUp $\NameCert$ rows cited in the packet.

Exactness is the reverse projection. A row outside
$F,\eta,\epsilon,\lambda$ is not a Frobenius row of the carrier, and a row
outside $S,R,i,r,\rho$ supplies no public endpoint or provenance for that
Frobenius display. Thus the Frobenius boundary is exactly the finite ledger
shown in the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:inducedrep-namecert-obligation-surface}
The $\NameCert_{\InducedRepUp}$ obligation surface is the package consisting
of the carrier packet of \autoref{def:inducedrep-bhist-carrier}, the
restriction-induction classifier of
\autoref{def:inducedrep-restriction-induction-classifier}, the Frobenius
ledger boundary of \autoref{thm:inducedrep-frobenius-ledger-boundary},
componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ stability for each displayed row, exactness
of the finite provenance and continuation ledgers, and no standard bridge.
The surface exports no row outside the carrier, classifier, and provenance
ledger.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.InducedRepUp.InducedRepCarrierPacket\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.InducedRepUp.InducedRepBHistCarrier\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and classifier. The carrier supplies the finite row
family $S,R,i,r,F,\eta,\epsilon,\rho,\lambda$, while the classifier supplies
componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the consumed certificates, subgroup
inclusion rows, representation-ring rows, induction and restriction endpoints,
Frobenius rows, provenance packet, and continuation ledger.

The ten-field reading is therefore fixed. The traditional target is the
restriction-induction interface with Frobenius reciprocity; the BEDC sources
are $\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$; the
carrier is the displayed finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet; the classifier is
the restricted componentwise relation; stability is the listed $\hsame$ and
$\Cont$ transport; the ledger is $\lambda$ together with the Frobenius rows;
the core theorem list is this surface and the Frobenius ledger boundary;
exactness is projection from the displayed rows; the standard bridge is none;
and the status is a paper-side obligation surface for
$\NameCert_{\InducedRepUp}$.

For stability, transport each component of a packet along the corresponding
classifier row. The SubgroupUp source certificate transports the subgroup
inclusion rows, the RepresentationRingUp source certificate transports the
representation-ring rows, and the endpoint and Frobenius rows are transported
by their displayed $\hsame$ comparisons and the finite $\Cont$ comparison.

Exactness follows because every exported row is named in the carrier,
classifier, or provenance ledger. Conversely, each named row repacks into the
obligation surface by the displayed packet, so no additional host functor,
host category, adjunction, character table, or bridge row is part of the
certificate.
\end{proof}
