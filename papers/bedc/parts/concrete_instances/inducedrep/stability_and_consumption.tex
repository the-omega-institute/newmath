\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp Subgroup and RepresentationRing consumer boundary]
\label{thm:inducedrep-subgroup-representationring-consumer-boundary}
An $\InducedRepUp$ consumer of the source certificates may read exactly the
SubgroupUp rows exposed by $\NameCert_{\SubgroupUp}$ and the
representation-ring rows exposed by $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$,
together with the induction, restriction, Frobenius, provenance, and
continuation rows displayed in \autoref{def:inducedrep-bhist-carrier}. It may
not read a subgroup carrier, representation ring, functor, category, or
Frobenius adjunction row unless that row is present in the carrier,
classifier, or provenance ledger of the $\InducedRepUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the consumer request through the carrier packet. A SubgroupUp request
lands in $S$, and a RepresentationRingUp request lands in $R$. The source
certificates named by those two rows supply their own public carrier,
classifier, stability, exactness, and ledger boundaries.

Next project the $\InducedRepUp$-specific request. The induction endpoint is
$i$, the restriction endpoint is $r$, the Frobenius rows are
$F,\eta,\epsilon$, the provenance packet is $\rho$, and the continuation
ledger is $\lambda$. Transport between two such consumer surfaces is exactly
the componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport listed in the
restriction-induction classifier.

There are no remaining exported rows. Any row not found in the two source
certificates or in $i,r,F,\eta,\epsilon,\rho,\lambda$ is outside the consumer
boundary, so the downstream interface receives a concrete BEDC packet rather
than equality of host-level induction and restriction constructions.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[InducedRepUp stability obligation packet]
\label{def:inducedrep-stability-obligation-packet}
For carrier packets
$$
\mathcal{I}=(S,R,i,r,F,\eta,\epsilon,\rho,\lambda)
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\mathcal{I}'=(S',R',i',r',F',\eta',\epsilon',\rho',\lambda'),
$$
the $\InducedRepUp$ stability obligation packet is the finite datum
consisting of the two displayed carriers, the componentwise $\hsame$ rows
from the restriction-induction classifier of
\autoref{def:inducedrep-restriction-induction-classifier}, and the finite
$\Cont$ comparison ledger from $\lambda$ to $\lambda'$. Its fields are:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\hsame$ rows for the consumed certificate rows $S$ and $R$;
  \item $\hsame$ rows for the induction and restriction endpoints $i$ and
  $r$;
  \item entrywise $\hsame$ rows for the Frobenius display
  $F,\eta,\epsilon$;
  \item an $\hsame$ row for the provenance packet $\rho$;
  \item the finite $\Cont$ comparison ledger relating the source,
  endpoint, Frobenius, provenance, and public continuation entries recorded
  by $\lambda$.
\end{enumerate}
The packet has no field for equality of host categories, host induction
functors, host restriction functors, functorial naturality, or adjunction
objects outside the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp classifier stability]
\label{thm:inducedrep-classifier-stability}
The restriction-induction classifier of
\autoref{def:inducedrep-restriction-induction-classifier} transports every
carrier component of \autoref{def:inducedrep-bhist-carrier}. For any two
packets $\mathcal{I}$ and $\mathcal{I}'$ related by that classifier, the
transport repacks as the stability obligation packet of
\autoref{def:inducedrep-stability-obligation-packet} and preserves the
public $\NameCert_{\InducedRepUp}$ obligation surface of
\autoref{thm:inducedrep-namecert-obligation-surface}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two carriers as
$\mathcal{I}=(S,R,i,r,F,\eta,\epsilon,\rho,\lambda)$ and
$\mathcal{I}'=(S',R',i',r',F',\eta',\epsilon',\rho',\lambda')$. The
classifier is already componentwise: it gives the $\hsame$ rows for the
consumed SubgroupUp and RepresentationRingUp packets, the displayed
subgroup and representation-ring rows read from them, the induction and
restriction endpoints, the Frobenius rows, and the provenance packet.

It remains to repack the continuation part. The same classifier includes
the finite $\Cont$ comparison between $\lambda$ and $\lambda'$, entry by
entry, so the source, endpoint, Frobenius, provenance, and public
continuation reads are transported inside the displayed ledger.

The obligation surface in
\autoref{thm:inducedrep-namecert-obligation-surface} asks for no more data
than those projected rows. Repacking the projected $\hsame$ rows and the
finite $\Cont$ comparison gives the stability obligation packet, and the
surface is preserved because no host category, functor equality, adjunction
row, or bridge row is part of the transported data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp continuation ledger closure]
\label{thm:inducedrep-cont-ledger-closure}
The source reads, induction and restriction endpoint reads, Frobenius reads,
provenance reads, and public continuation reads of an $\InducedRepUp$
carrier close under finite $\Cont$ composition inside the ledger
$\lambda$ of \autoref{def:inducedrep-bhist-carrier}. Consequently every
finite continuation needed by the obligation surface is again a row recorded
by $\lambda$.
\end{theorem}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.InducedRepUp.InducedRepBHistCarrier\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier to the finite packet
$\mathcal{I}=(S,R,i,r,F,\eta,\epsilon,\rho,\lambda)$. By definition,
$\lambda$ contains the consumed SubgroupUp rows, consumed
RepresentationRingUp rows, induction and restriction endpoint rows,
Frobenius rows, provenance rows, and public continuation rows exposed by the
packet.

Now compose only rows already listed in $\lambda$. A source read followed by
an endpoint read, an endpoint read followed by a Frobenius read, or a
Frobenius read followed by a public continuation read is still a finite
read through the same displayed ledger, because the ledger is the complete
$\Cont$ record for the carrier.

Thus the closure statement is a projection-and-repacking argument. The
projection chooses the listed rows of $\lambda$, and the repacking records
their finite composite as another continuation row of the same public
ledger. Nothing in the argument introduces a host functor, host category,
or adjunction object outside the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp package provenance exactness]
\label{thm:inducedrep-pkg-provenance-exactness}
Every row exported by the $\InducedRepUp$ package provenance surface is
exactly one of the rows recorded by $\rho$ or $\lambda$ in
\autoref{def:inducedrep-bhist-carrier}: the consumed SubgroupUp rows, the
consumed RepresentationRingUp rows, the induction row, the restriction row,
and the Frobenius rows $F,\eta,\epsilon$. No host category, host functor
equality, or adjunction row is exported by the provenance surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the package row $\rho$. Its definition records precisely the
consumed package rows from $S$ and $R$ together with the package rows
attached to $i$, $r$, $F$, $\eta$, and $\epsilon$. These are exactly the
SubgroupUp, RepresentationRingUp, induction, restriction, and Frobenius
rows named in the theorem.

Project the continuation ledger $\lambda$ next. It records the corresponding
public reads: source rows, endpoint rows, Frobenius rows, provenance rows,
and public continuation rows. Therefore every public continuation witness
for a provenance row is already present in the same finite carrier.

For the converse, take any row recorded by $\rho$ or $\lambda$ with one of
the listed roles. The carrier definition repacks it as part of the
$\InducedRepUp$ public provenance surface, and the consumer boundary of
\autoref{thm:inducedrep-subgroup-representationring-consumer-boundary}
excludes every row not so recorded. Hence no host category, equality of
induction or restriction functors, or Frobenius adjunction row is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp source-Frobenius exact consumption]
\label{thm:inducedrep-source-frobenius-exact-consumption}
A downstream row consumed from an $\InducedRepUp$ packet is visible exactly
when it is one of the following finite public rows: a SubgroupUp source row
exposed through $S$, a RepresentationRingUp source row exposed through $R$,
an induction or restriction endpoint row, one of the Frobenius rows
$F,\eta,\epsilon$, or a provenance and continuation row recorded by $\rho$ or
$\lambda$. No host subgroup object, host representation category, host
functor equality, or Frobenius adjunction object is visible outside this
finite source--Frobenius ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project a consumed row through the carrier packet
$\mathcal{I}=(S,R,i,r,F,\eta,\epsilon,\rho,\lambda)$. The source rows are
exactly the SubgroupUp and RepresentationRingUp rows allowed by
\autoref{thm:inducedrep-subgroup-representationring-consumer-boundary}; the
endpoint rows are the displayed induction and restriction rows $i$ and $r$.

Project the Frobenius part next. By
\autoref{thm:inducedrep-frobenius-ledger-boundary}, every Frobenius read is
one of $F,\eta,\epsilon$ and has endpoints in the displayed induction and
restriction rows. By \autoref{thm:inducedrep-cont-ledger-closure}, any finite
continuation needed to consume those rows remains inside $\lambda$.

The provenance projection is fixed by
\autoref{thm:inducedrep-pkg-provenance-exactness}: every public provenance
row is recorded by $\rho$ or by the corresponding public continuation row in
$\lambda$. Therefore any downstream row consumed from the packet is on the
listed source, endpoint, Frobenius, provenance, or continuation surface.

Conversely, take any row on that finite list. The source rows repack through
the consumer boundary, the endpoint and Frobenius rows repack through the
Frobenius ledger boundary, and the provenance and continuation rows repack
through $\rho$ and $\lambda$. The four cited boundaries together reconstruct
the same $\InducedRepUp$ public packet, so every listed row is visible to the
consumer and no outside host-level datum is added.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp Frobenius source-row determinacy]
\label{thm:inducedrep-frobenius-source-row-determinacy}
Let
$\mathcal{I}_0=(S,R,i,r,F,\eta,\epsilon,\rho_0,\lambda_0)$ and
$\mathcal{I}_1=(S,R,i,r,F,\eta,\epsilon,\rho_1,\lambda_1)$ be two
$\InducedRepUp$ packets with the same SubgroupUp source row, the same
RepresentationRingUp source row, the same induction and restriction endpoint
rows, the same Frobenius rows $F,\eta,\epsilon$, the same public $\Cont$
ledger rows, and the same $\Pkg$ provenance rows. Then the public classifier
endpoints exported by $\mathcal{I}_0$ and $\mathcal{I}_1$ are related by
$\hsame$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both packets through the restriction-induction classifier. The source
rows are the common SubgroupUp and RepresentationRingUp rows, and the endpoint
rows are the common induction and restriction histories $i$ and $r$.

Apply \autoref{thm:inducedrep-source-frobenius-exact-consumption}. It rules out
any consumed public row outside the common source rows, endpoint rows,
Frobenius rows, $\Cont$ ledger, and $\Pkg$ provenance. Thus both packets expose
the same finite public ledger.

Finally repack that common source-Frobenius ledger through
\autoref{thm:inducedrep-frobenius-ledger-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:inducedrep-pkg-provenance-exactness}. The classifier endpoint is
therefore read from the same carried rows in both packets, so the two exported
endpoints are related by $\hsame$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp source classifier obligation]
\label{thm:inducedrep-source-classifier-obligation}
For an $\InducedRepUp$ carrier packet
$\mathcal{I}=(S,R,i,r,F,\eta,\epsilon,\rho,\lambda)$, the public classifier
is determined by the SubgroupUp source row $S$, the RepresentationRingUp
source row $R$, the induction endpoint $i$, the restriction endpoint $r$,
and the displayed $\hsame$ transport rows of
\autoref{def:inducedrep-restriction-induction-classifier}. No classifier
read may use a host subgroup, representation category, or functor equality
outside those carried rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier through
\autoref{thm:inducedrep-subgroup-representationring-consumer-boundary}. The
consumer sees the SubgroupUp and RepresentationRingUp source rows only
through $S$ and $R$, together with the induction and restriction endpoints.

Apply \autoref{thm:inducedrep-consumer-boundary}. It identifies the visible
classifier rows as $H_{\leq},H_{\mathrm{rep}},H_{\uparrow}$, and
$H_{\downarrow}$ together with the public Frobenius and continuation rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:inducedrep-frobenius-source-row-determinacy}. Once
the source rows, endpoints, Frobenius rows, $\Cont$ ledger, and $\Pkg$
provenance are fixed, the exported classifier endpoints are related by
$\hsame$, so no outside classifier datum is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:inducedrep-ledger-exactness-obligation}
The visible Frobenius ledger reads of an $\InducedRepUp$ carrier are exactly
the rows recorded by the $\Cont$ ledger $\lambda$ and the $\Pkg$ provenance
packet $\rho$ for the consumed source certificates, induction endpoint,
restriction endpoint, and Frobenius rows $F,\eta,\epsilon$. Every visible
read is on that finite ledger, and every listed row repacks into the public
carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:inducedrep-frobenius-ledger-boundary}. It restricts
Frobenius reads to $F,\eta,\epsilon,\lambda$ with endpoints $i$ and $r$ over
the consumed source packets $S$ and $R$.

Use \autoref{thm:inducedrep-cont-ledger-closure}. It shows that finite
continuations among source reads, endpoint reads, Frobenius reads, provenance
reads, and public continuation reads remain inside $\lambda$.

Apply \autoref{thm:inducedrep-pkg-provenance-exactness}. The provenance rows
are precisely those recorded by $\rho$ or $\lambda$ for the source,
endpoint, and Frobenius roles. The reverse projection repacks each listed
row into the carrier, so the ledger is exact.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InducedRepUp NameCert threshold witness]
\label{thm:inducedrep-namecert-threshold-witness}
The dependency-ready $\InducedRepUp$ obligation surface is the finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ packet consisting of the source classifier obligation of
\autoref{thm:inducedrep-source-classifier-obligation}, the ledger exactness
obligation of \autoref{thm:inducedrep-ledger-exactness-obligation}, and the
source-Frobenius consumption boundary of
\autoref{thm:inducedrep-source-frobenius-exact-consumption}. It supplies
downstream consumers exactly the carried source, endpoint, Frobenius,
classifier, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use \autoref{thm:inducedrep-source-classifier-obligation}. It fixes the
public classifier from the two source certificates, the induction endpoint,
the restriction endpoint, and the displayed $\hsame$ transport rows.

Next use \autoref{thm:inducedrep-ledger-exactness-obligation}. It fixes the
visible ledger reads as the rows in $\lambda$ and $\rho$ for the source,
endpoint, and Frobenius roles.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:inducedrep-source-frobenius-exact-consumption}.
It states that downstream consumption is exactly the finite list of source,
endpoint, Frobenius, provenance, and continuation rows. Repacking those
three surfaces gives the dependency-ready $\NameCert_{\InducedRepUp}$
threshold, with no host category, functor equality, adjunction object, or
external bridge row.
\end{proof}
