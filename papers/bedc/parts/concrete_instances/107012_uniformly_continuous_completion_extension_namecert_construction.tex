\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformlyContinuousCompletionExtensionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniformly-continuous-completion-extension-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on uniformly continuous maps between completions, the $\UniformlyContinuousCompletionExtensionUp$ packet records the finite NameCert surface by which a uniformly continuous source map extends across displayed BEDC completion data. It sits after the Cauchy lift route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-lift-namecert}, the uniform completion consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletionfunctor-namecert}, the regular readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the extension route through finite rows; it does not introduce quotient completion classes, selected representatives, host function extensionality, choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Uniformly continuous completion extension carrier]
\label{def:uniformly-continuous-completion-extension-carrier}
A $\UniformlyContinuousCompletionExtensionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(D,T,M,L,U,R,S,Y,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the dense source row, recording the displayed source observations, density witnesses, Cauchy windows, and tolerance requests made before any completed target is inspected.
\item $T$ is the target completion-consumer row, exposing the target-facing completion object only through an accepted consumer interface.
\item $M$ is the uniform modulus and graph row for the source map, carrying finite graph observations and the tolerance-to-precision ledger consumed by the extension.
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyCompletionLiftUp$ handoff row, requiring the source map and its modulus to be read before a lifted completion-facing map is named.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionFunctorUp$ route row, binding the same source, target, graph, and modulus data to the uniform completion surface.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, recording the regular rational approximants opened by the completion consumer.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, recording the finite stream observations used to compare source Cauchy data with target readback data.
\item $Y$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, carrying the dyadic radii and comparison budgets through the modulus handoff.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal, visible only after the dense source, target consumer, modulus, lift, uniform completion, regular readback, stream-window, and dyadic tolerance rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dense source, target consumer, modulus graph, lift, uniform completion route, readback, stream windows, dyadic ledger, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
D,T,M \longmapsto L,U \longmapsto R,S,Y \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformlyContinuousCompletionExtensionUp$, $\CauchyCompletionLiftUp$, $\UniformCompletionFunctorUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformlyContinuousCompletionExtensionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $D,T,M,L,U,R,S,Y,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, host function extensionality, choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniformly continuous completion extension modulus handoff]
\label{thm:uniformly-continuous-completion-extension-modulus-handoff}
Let
$$
X=(D,T,M,L,U,R,S,Y,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\UniformlyContinuousCompletionExtensionUp$ carrier. Every extension consumer of the completed map factors through the uniform modulus and graph row $M$, the $\CauchyCompletionLiftUp$ handoff row $L$, the $\UniformCompletionFunctorUp$ route row $U$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row $S$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $Y$, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$ before the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ name the result.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier projection gives the dense source row $D$, the target completion-consumer row $T$, and the modulus-bearing graph row $M$. The carrier orders these rows before any lift, uniform completion, readback, finite-window, dyadic, or real-seal row.

The completion-facing handoff is then read through $L$ and $U$. The row $L$ records the same uniform-modulus handoff required by the Cauchy completion lift route, while $U$ records the uniform completion consumer surface; neither row supplies a selected completed representative.

Regular readback is downstream of the handoff. The rows $R,S,Y$ expose regular rational approximants, finite stream windows, and dyadic tolerance budgets before $E$ may seal a real-facing read.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed route. Since the packet has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected representatives, host function extensionality, choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, no extension consumer can factor through those alternate surfaces.
\end{proof}
