\begin{theorem}[QuotientGroup singleton semantic naming certificate]
\label{thm:quotientgroup-singleton-semantic-namecert}
The singleton quotient-group carrier and classifier form a semantic naming
certificate whose source, pattern, and ledger predicates are all
$Q_{\emp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The inhabited endpoint is $\emp$, witnessed by reflexive
$\hsame(\emp,\emp)$. Reflexivity of the classifier packages the source carrier
twice with reflexive $\hsame(h,h)$. Symmetry swaps the endpoint carriers and
reverses the $\hsame$ component. Transitivity composes the two $\hsame$
components and keeps the outer endpoint carrier witnesses. Carrier transport
along a classifier edge is the right endpoint carrier field. Since the semantic
source, pattern, and ledger predicates are the same singleton predicate, their
soundness clauses are direct projections of the source witness.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.QuotientGroupUp.QuotientGroupSingleton\_semanticNameCert}
\begin{theorem}[QuotientGroup singleton normalizer orbit classifier collapse]
\label{thm:quotientgroup-singleton-normalizer-orbit-classifier-collapse}

instance of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance} and the
singleton $\QuotientGroupUp$ classifier of
Definition~\ref{def:quotientgroup-singleton-classifier},
$$
x\sim_{N_{\emp}}y \Longleftrightarrow \rho_Q(x,y).
$$

its terminal normalizer action; BEDC source concrete singleton $\GroupUp$ plus
singleton $\QuotientGroupUp$; carrier $Q_{\emp}=A_{\emp}$ by
Definitions~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source},
\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, and
\ref{def:quotientgroup-singleton-carrier}; classifier $\rho_Q$ compared with
$\sim_{N_{\emp}}$; stability from
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-orbit-total-coverage},
\autoref{thm:quotientgroup-singleton-semantic-namecert}, and symmetry and
transitivity for $\hsame$; ledger the hidden conjugator endpoint; core theorem
this block; exactness the displayed iff; downstream consumer
first-isomorphism-style quotient-kernel exactness; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

\autoref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-orbit-total-coverage}. It identifies
the terminal normalizer orbit relation with

Definitions~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source},
\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-group-instance}, and
\ref{def:quotientgroup-singleton-carrier}, this is the same endpoint data as
$Q_{\emp}(x)\land Q_{\emp}(y)$.

$Q_{\emp}(x)$ and $Q_{\emp}(y)$. Unfold

Under the same carrier identification these are

\autoref{thm:group-singleton-normalizer-orbit-total-coverage} yields

\end{proof}

$$

$\mathsf{e0}$ or $\mathsf{e1}$ endpoint on either side:
$$
\rho_Q(\mathsf{e0}(p),k),\quad
\rho_Q(\mathsf{e1}(p),k),\quad
\rho_Q(k,\mathsf{e0}(q)),\quad
\rho_Q(k,\mathsf{e1}(q))

Unfold the classifier. A visible left endpoint gives its left carrier field,
which identifies $\mathsf{e0}(p)$ or $\mathsf{e1}(p)$ with $\emp$. A visible
right endpoint gives the same contradiction from the right carrier field. Each
case is excluded by constructor separation for generated BEDC histories.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer-normalizer QuotientGroupUp semantic certificate]
\label{thm:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-semantic-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$
satisfying the associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence,
inverse-congruence, inverse-product reversal, and two-sided inverse laws used
by \autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}, the tuple
of \autoref{def:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-carrier} satisfies the
$\QuotientGroupUp$ certificate fields:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the representative carrier $N_a$ is inhabited by $e$;
\item $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$ is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive on
carried representatives;
\item represented multiplication descends through
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$;
\item $e$ and $\mathsf{inv}(x)$ are carried by $N_a$ whenever $x$ is carried
by $N_a$;
\item the represented product, unit, and inverse operations respect the same
classifier on all carried representatives.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently the centralizer-normalizer tower supplies a quotient-free
semantic certificate for $\QuotientGroupUp$ whose observable names are
histories in $N_a$ and whose equality test is the relative-kernel predicate
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}. Its lower
and upper subgroup rows give $Z_a(e)$, $N_a(e)$, product and inverse closure
for $N_a$, the inclusion $Z_a\subseteq N_a$, and the normality rows carrying
$Z_a$ through conjugation by elements of $N_a$.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-kernel-congruence} to
the classifier
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$. The first three rows give reflexivity, symmetry,
and transitivity on representatives in $N_a$. The fourth row gives product
descent for $\mathsf{mul}$ and the quotient operation congruence required by
the represented multiplication.

It remains to identify the unit and inverse operation rows. The unit row is
the upper subgroup inhabitance supplied by the tower. For inverse closure, use
the upper subgroup inverse row from
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-tower-certificate}; inverse
descent follows by combining the ambient inverse-congruence and two-sided
inverse laws named there with
\autoref{thm:group-inverse-mul-reverse}. Expanding
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-kernel-relation}, the
kernel witness for inverse representatives is transported from
$Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(x),y))$ to the corresponding relative inverse
word by the $Z_a$ inverse-closure and $\hsame$-transport rows.

These rows are exactly the carrier inhabitance, equivalence, product descent,
unit closure, inverse closure, and operation congruence fields demanded by the
history-level $\QuotientGroupUp$ certificate, with the ledger witness kept as
the relative kernel membership rather than as an element of a host quotient.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer-normalizer quotient identity fiber subgroup package]
\label{thm:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-identity-fiber-subgroup}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
the same hypotheses as
\autoref{thm:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-semantic-certificate}, define
the quotient identity fiber carrier by
$$
K_Q(x) :\Longleftrightarrow Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(\mathsf{e},x).
$$
Then $K_Q$ is the concrete subgroup package carried by the identity fiber of
the centralizer-normalizer quotient classifier:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K_Q(\mathsf{e})$.
\item If $K_Q(x)$ and $K_Q(y)$, then $K_Q(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.
\item If $K_Q(x)$, then $K_Q(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.
\item If $K_Q(x)$ and $x\hsame y$, then $K_Q(y)$.
\item For every history $x:\mathsf{BHist}$,
$$
K_Q(x)\Longleftrightarrow Z_a(x).
$$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-kernel-identity-fiber}.
For each history $x$, it gives the identity-fiber equivalence for
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$. After unfolding the displayed definition of $K_Q$,
this is exactly the equivalence in the final row.

The identity row follows by transporting $Z_a(\mathsf{e})$ from
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup} through the reverse direction of the
fiber equivalence at $\mathsf{e}$. This gives
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(\mathsf{e},\mathsf{e})$, which is exactly
$K_Q(\mathsf{e})$.

For product closure, assume $K_Q(x)$ and $K_Q(y)$. The forward directions of
the fiber equivalence give $Z_a(x)$ and $Z_a(y)$. The product row of
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup} gives
$Z_a(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$, and the reverse direction of the fiber equivalence
returns $K_Q(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.

For inverse closure, assume $K_Q(x)$. Convert it to $Z_a(x)$ by the forward
direction of the fiber equivalence. The inverse row of
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup} gives $Z_a(\mathsf{inv}(x))$, and the
reverse direction of the fiber equivalence gives $K_Q(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.

For carrier transport, assume $K_Q(x)$ and $x\hsame y$. Convert $K_Q(x)$ to
$Z_a(x)$, use the $\hsame$-transport row for the centralizer carrier from
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-subgroup} to obtain $Z_a(y)$, and convert back
to $K_Q(y)$. Thus the classifier inherited from
$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$ has the same concrete identity fiber as the
centralizer carrier.
\end{proof}

\item If $K_Q(y)$ and $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$, then $K_Q(x)$.

Per-object template: traditional target saturated identity fiber of a quotient
classifier; BEDC source $\GroupUp$, $\SubgroupUp$, and $\QuotientGroupUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $K_Q$; classifier $Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}$; ledger

$Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)$ by transitivity to obtain

\begin{enumerate}

\item If $K_Q(x)$ and $K_Q(y)$, then $K_Q(\mathsf{mul}(x,y))$.
\item If $K_Q(x)$, then $K_Q(\mathsf{inv}(x))$.
\item If $K_Q(x)$ and $x\hsame y$, then $K_Q(y)$.
\item If $N_a(x)$ and $K_Q(k)$, then
$K_Q(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(x,k),\mathsf{inv}(x)))$.

\begin{proof}

clauses give the forward and inverse conjugation words listed above.

Together these rows present the identity fiber as the concrete normal kernel of
the representative quotient classifier. The first block supplies the subgroup
package; the second supplies conjugation stability by every carried
normalizer element, which is the combined certificate consumed by

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer coset carrier representative product closure]
\label{thm:quotientgroup-centralizer-coset-carrier-representative-product}

$$
\mathsf{CentralizerCosetCarrier}(\mathsf{mul},a,x,h_1)
\wedge
\mathsf{CentralizerCosetCarrier}(\mathsf{mul},a,y,h_2)
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{CentralizerCosetCarrier}(\mathsf{mul},a,\mathsf{mul}(x,y),
\mathsf{mul}(h_1,h_2)).
$$
\end{theorem}

\autoref{def:quotientgroup-centralizer-coset-carrier} for the representative

\end{proof}
