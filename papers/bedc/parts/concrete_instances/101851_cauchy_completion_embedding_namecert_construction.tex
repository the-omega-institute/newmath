Following Bishop and Bridges constructive completion and Bourbaki/Kelley completion terminology, the packet records the finite BEDC certificate for the canonical entry of a displayed source into its Cauchy completion. It sits after the unit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionunit-namecert}, the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the entry map as finite NameCert data; it does not assert quotient completion equality, select limits, identify host objects, invoke countable choice, or use ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionEmbedding carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-embedding-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
J=(X,U,C,S,R,D,E,H,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the displayed source row whose point, metric, or regular-Cauchy data are admitted into the completion-facing entry.
\item $U$ is the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ route that exposes the source row before any completion seal is read.
\item $C$ is the completion seal row recording that the entry is consumed by the Cauchy-completion surface as displayed finite data.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row through which the source is observed before tolerance or completion-facing reads.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row used by finite consumers of the entry.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger that bounds each readback and entry request.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the route through $S,R,D$ has been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source row, unit route, completion seal, finite-window row, regular readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $Q$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for consumers that inspect the source row, the unit route, the finite readback, or the completion seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected limits, host equality of completed objects, countable-choice schedules, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or public bridge credentials.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionEmbedding NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-embedding-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingUp$ carrier $J=(X,U,C,S,R,D,E,H,Q,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source row, unit route, completion seal, finite-window row, regular readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item entry exposure, requiring every completion-facing consumer to read $X$ through $U$ before the completion seal $C$ is available;
\item finite-window locality, requiring the source observation to pass through $S$ before $R,D,E$ or $C$ can be inspected;
\item tolerance and readback exactness, requiring each finite precision request to be answered by $D$ and then $R$ before the terminal seal or completion seal is consumed;
\item classifier stability, requiring transported packets with the same displayed $X,U,C,S,R,D,E$ route to be identified by the carried $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that the packet exports no quotient completion equality, selected limit, host equality, countable-choice schedule, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-embedding-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the source, unit, completion seal, finite-window, regular-readback, dyadic-ledger, Real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Entry exposure is constructive because the unit route $U$ is the only row connecting $X$ to the completion seal $C$. A consumer starting from the source has no detached completion point to inspect; it must first read the displayed unit route.

Finite-window locality then projects $S$. The regular readback $R$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the Real seal $E$, and the completion seal $C$ all occur after the finite source observation, so no later row can be consumed before the window row is present.

Tolerance and readback exactness use $D$ and $R$. Each finite request is bounded by the dyadic ledger and read back as regular rational data before either seal is exposed.

Classifier stability is componentwise transport along $H$ followed by replay through $Q$. Since the carrier fields are exhausted by $X,U,C,S,R,D,E,H,Q,P,N$, the excluded quotient, selected-limit, host-equality, choice, ambient-completeness, real-completeness, and bridge data have no row from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionEmbedding density handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-embedding-density-handoff}
Let $J=(X,U,C,S,R,D,E,H,Q,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingUp$ carrier. Every density, functoriality, reflector, or universal-property consumer of the source-to-completion entry factors through the finite route
$$
X\longmapsto U\longmapsto S\longmapsto D,R\longmapsto E\longmapsto C\longmapsto H,Q,P,N .
$$
The handoff uses only finite windows and regular readback. It is not equality of quotient completions, not a selected limit, not host equality, and not an appeal to countable choice or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer of the entry packet. Projection gives the displayed source row $X$, and the only completion-facing admission row available from $X$ is the unit route $U$.

The consumer must next read the finite window row $S$. This keeps the handoff local to a bounded observation of the source before any regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, Real seal, or completion seal is exposed.

The tolerance and readback rows $D$ and $R$ answer the finite request. The terminal real-facing boundary $E$ is reached only after that regular route has been displayed, and the completion seal $C$ consumes the same finite route as the entry map.

Transport by $H$ and replay by $Q$ preserve this route for equivalent packets, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming. No carrier coordinate exists for quotient equality, selected limits, host equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness, so downstream density and universal-property consumers receive only the finite handoff described above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionEmbedding Real seal non-escape]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-embedding-real-seal-nonescape}
For an accepted $\CauchyCompletionEmbeddingUp$ carrier $J=(X,U,C,S,R,D,E,H,Q,P,N)$, every $\RealUp$-facing or completion-facing read exported by $J$ is generated by the rows named in \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-embedding-carrier}. In particular, the Real seal cannot be used as a detached completed point, quotient-completion equality witness, selected limit, host equality witness, countable-choice selection, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. A $\RealUp$-facing read must pass through $S,D,R,E$, because those are the only finite observation, tolerance, readback, and terminal seal rows in the tuple.

A completion-facing read must also pass through $U$ and $C$. The unit route records the source entry before the completion seal is consumed, and the completion seal is stored as displayed NameCert data rather than as a quotient point, selected limit, or host equality witness.

The structural rows $H,Q,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. They add no carrier coordinate beyond the listed rows.

Thus every exported read is generated by \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-embedding-carrier}. The forbidden quotient, selected-limit, host-equality, choice, ambient-completeness, real-completeness, and bridge data are absent from the carrier and cannot be produced by projection, transport, replay, provenance, or local naming.
\end{proof}
