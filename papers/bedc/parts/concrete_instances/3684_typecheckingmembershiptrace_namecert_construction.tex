The $\TypeCheckingMembershipTraceUp$ surface packages a type-checking read as finite classifier membership rather than as a host typing judgment. It joins the membership row of $\TypeClassifierMembershipUp$, the classifier typing row of $\ClassifierTypingUp$, the subject-reduction handoff of $\SubjectReductionUp$, the finite derivation route of $\TypeCheckingDerivationTraceUp$, and the proof-erasure readback of $\ProofTermErasureTraceUp$ into one locally named packet.

\begin{definition}[TypeCheckingMembershipTrace carrier]
\label{def:typechecking-membership-trace-carrier}
A $\TypeCheckingMembershipTraceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(M,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\Ext$-membership row read as the local type-classifier decision;
\item $D$ is the finite derivation trace row recording the classifier typing route;
\item $R$ is the displayed $\Cont$ subject-reduction route attached to the same membership read;
\item $S$ is the $\SigRel$ readback row relating the derivation route to the visible classifier name;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,D,R,S$ and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay by which consumers reread membership, derivation, subject-reduction, and readback rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\TypeClassifierMembershipUp$, $\ClassifierTypingUp$, $\SubjectReductionUp$, $\TypeCheckingDerivationTraceUp$, $\ProofTermErasureTraceUp$, $\Ext$, $\Cont$, $\SigRel$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TypeCheckingMembershipTraceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares two accepted carriers only through the displayed membership, derivation, subject-reduction, readback, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TypeCheckingMembershipTrace classifier stability]
\label{thm:typechecking-membership-trace-classifier-stability}
Let
$$
T=(M,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
and
$$
T'=(M',D',R',S',H',C',P',N')
$$
be accepted $\TypeCheckingMembershipTraceUp$ carriers whose classifier rows are related by the displayed local comparison. Then the $\Ext$-membership read, finite derivation route, $\Cont$ subject-reduction handoff, and $\SigRel$ readback are preserved through the componentwise $\hsame$ rows and the displayed $\Cont$ replay. The preserved object is the same finite membership-trace packet; it supplies no host typing judgment, conversion oracle, or quotient proof-term equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both carriers through \autoref{def:typechecking-membership-trace-carrier}. The rows $M,D,R,S$ name the classifier membership, derivation route, subject-reduction handoff, and readback boundary, while $H$ records componentwise transport for those rows.

Use the displayed continuation replay $C$ and $C'$ to reread the same rows after transport. Since replay is attached to the packet coordinates, it cannot introduce an external typing derivation or conversion decision.

Finally repack the transported rows with the provenance and local naming rows $P,N$ and $P',N'$. The result is still the finite $\TypeCheckingMembershipTraceUp$ surface, so quotient proof-term equality and host type equality remain outside the exported classifier evidence.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TypeCheckingMembershipTrace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:typechecking-membership-trace-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\TypeCheckingMembershipTraceUp}$ surface has five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission exposes exactly $T=(M,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)$;
\item membership classification reads accepted type-checking only from the displayed $\Ext$ row $M$;
\item derivation-route exactness keeps the classifier typing route inside $D$ and its $\SigRel$ readback inside $S$;
\item subject-reduction handoff uses only the displayed $\Cont$ route $R$ and its replay through $C$;
\item the non-escape ledger excludes host type equality, global normalization, proof irrelevance, quotient syntax, conversion oracles, and any proof-term equality not displayed in the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite tuple. The membership row, derivation row, subject-reduction row, readback row, transport row, replay row, package row, and local naming row are all coordinates of the same $\BHist$ packet.

The classifier and derivation obligations are read from $M,D,S$. The row $M$ is the only accepted $\Ext$-membership decision, $D$ is the only derivation route, and $S$ is the only signature readback attached to that route.

The subject-reduction handoff is read from $R$ and replayed through $C$. Because every exported row is packaged with $H,P,N$, no host type equality, global normalization theorem, proof irrelevance principle, quotient syntax, conversion oracle, or external proof-term equality can be recovered from the certificate surface.
\end{proof}
