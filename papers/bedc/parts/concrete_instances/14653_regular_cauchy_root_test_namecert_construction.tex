\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-root-test-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$ packet records the finite root-bound test that turns a displayed eventually bounded root ledger into a regular Cauchy tail before the Real-facing seal is consumed. It sits over the fast-tail surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fastconvergentseries-namecert}, the dyadic budget rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadictailbound-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the Real seal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the majorant route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-comparison-test-namecert}, and the geometric-tail route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-ratio-test-namecert}. The packet is a constructive root test for L10 Real and Completion certificates; it is not a host root-limit theorem, not a quotient of streams, not a selected sum, not countable choice, and not an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy root test carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-root-test-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(A,Q,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the series or tail source row whose finite terms are inspected through displayed windows.
\item $Q$ is the root-bound row, recording a dyadic bound strictly inside the unit tolerance scale together with the finite entry index after which the bound is admitted.
\item $M$ is the modulus row assigning each displayed precision request a finite threshold beyond the root-bound entry index.
\item $D$ is the dyadic tail-domination ledger that turns the root-bound row into a geometric majorant consumed by $\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp$ and checked against the geometric-tail discipline of $\RegularCauchyRatioTestUp$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained from the displayed tail-domination ledger and modulus row.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing row reached only through the regular rational handoff.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, root-bound, modulus, dyadic domination, regular handoff, real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through $Q$ and $M$, then through $D$ to the comparison-test surface, the ratio-test geometric budget, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and the $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$, $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp$, $\RegularCauchyRatioTestUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyRootTestUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for host root limits, quotient streams, selected sums, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy root test NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-root-test-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$ carrier $T=(A,Q,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyRootTestUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed ten-row packet;
\item root-bound tail domination, requiring $Q,M,D$ to turn each inspected tail request into a finite dyadic geometric majorant routed through $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, $\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp$, and $\RegularCauchyRatioTestUp$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, requiring $R$ to be read only from the displayed source, root-bound, modulus, and tail-domination rows;
\item $\RealUp$ seal non-escape, requiring $E$ to factor through $R$ and to export no host root limit, quotient stream, selected sum, countable choice, or ambient completeness row;
\item $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ transport for exactly the rows recorded by $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations are finite-row obligations over the sibling surfaces named in the chapter introduction.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $T=(A,Q,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes the source, root-bound, modulus, tail-domination, regular handoff, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

For a displayed precision request, read $Q$ and $M$. The root-bound row supplies the dyadic bound and entry index, while the modulus row chooses a finite threshold beyond that entry index.

The dyadic ledger $D$ performs the tail-domination step. It converts the displayed root bound into a geometric majorant, sends the majorant through the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ and $\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp$ surfaces, and checks the geometric budget against the $\RegularCauchyRatioTestUp$ route.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff $R$ is then obtained from the same displayed route. The seal row $E$ is downstream of $R$, so it cannot expose a host root-limit object, quotient stream, selected sum, countable-choice row, or ambient completeness theorem.

Finally apply the structural rows. The $\hsame$ transports in $H$ preserve the analytic rows componentwise, while $C,P,N$ replay, source, and name exactly the displayed route. No omitted coordinate remains from which an additional NameCert obligation could be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy root test tail-bound handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-root-test-tail-bound-handoff}
Every accepted $\RegularCauchyRootTestUp$ carrier hands a root-bound tail to $\RegSeqRatUp$ and then to $\RealUp$ only through the finite route
$$
A,Q,M\longmapsto D\longmapsto R,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
Consequently a Real-facing consumer can use the root test only after reading the displayed root-bound row, modulus row, dyadic tail-domination ledger, comparison-test handoff, ratio-test geometric budget, regular rational handoff, and local transport package.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier and a displayed precision request. The rows $Q$ and $M$ determine the admitted root bound, entry index, and finite tail threshold.

Read the domination ledger $D$. This ledger is the only row that converts the root-bound data into a geometric majorant, and it names the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, $\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp$, and $\RegularCauchyRatioTestUp$ routes consumed by the handoff.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ is obtained by replaying that displayed domination route. Since $R$ depends on $A,Q,M,D$ and not on a host root-limit theorem, the downstream $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ can only read the regular rational handoff presented by the carrier.

Transport and provenance preserve the same route. The rows $H,C,P,N$ carry the root-bound tail data, replay the comparison and ratio-test handoffs, and name the local certificate without adding quotient streams, selected sums, countable choice, RealUp completeness, or bridge checking.
\end{proof}
