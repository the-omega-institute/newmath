\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchySpeedupUp$}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\CauchySpeedupUp$ packet records the finite route by which an arbitrary displayed Cauchy source is accelerated along a cofinal schedule before it is read as regular rational data. It sits after the regularization interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyregularization-namecert}, the finite acceleration surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fastcauchysubsequence-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-subsequence-namecert}, the window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite L10 speedup certificate; it is not quotient stream equality, a selected limit, countable choice, host equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy speedup carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-speedup-carrier}
A $\CauchySpeedupUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(A,J,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the source Cauchy row, recording the finite requests and source observations available before any speedup schedule is inspected.
\item $J$ is the cofinal speedup schedule row, carrying the monotone tail-access route and the displayed evidence that every downstream tolerance request is eventually reached.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, storing the requested dyadic scales, slack comparisons, and exact tolerance accounting used by the accelerated route.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family opened by the schedule $J$ over the source row $A$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the same scheduled windows and dyadic ledger.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $A,J,D,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source Cauchy row, cofinal schedule, dyadic ledger, finite windows, regular rational readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each Real-facing request through $A,J,D,W,R,E$ and then through $H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchySpeedupUp$, $\CauchyRegularizationUp$, $\FastCauchySubsequenceUp$, $\RegularCauchySubsequenceUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchySpeedupUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted speedup packets only through $A,J,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean evidence, or standard-bridge data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy speedup NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-speedup-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchySpeedupUp$ carrier
$$
S=(A,J,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchySpeedupUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the source Cauchy row $A$, cofinal speedup schedule $J$, dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, scheduled stream windows $W$, regular rational readback $R$, Real seal $E$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$;
\item classifier transport, requiring every accepted replacement to preserve the same componentwise source, schedule, tolerance, window, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and naming rows through $H$ and $C$;
\item cofinal schedule stability, requiring $J$ to remain monotone and cofinal for the displayed tail requests over $A$ under classifier transport;
\item dyadic tolerance exactness, requiring every accelerated tolerance claim to be read through the ledger $D$ and the scheduled windows $W$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, requiring $R$ to be downstream of $A,J,D,W$ and to use no unlisted stream coordinate;
\item $\RealUp$ non-escape, requiring $E$ to be consumed only after the scheduled regular rational route has been displayed and to add no selected limit, quotient equality, trichotomy, choice principle, or ambient completeness claim;
\item ledger exactness, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name exactly the displayed route $A,J,D,W,R,E$.
\end{enumerate}
No local speedup obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchySpeedupUp.CauchySpeedupNameCertObligations}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-speedup-carrier}. The analytic rows are the source Cauchy row $A$, the cofinal schedule $J$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the finite windows $W$, the regular rational readback $R$, and the terminal Real seal $E$.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The row $H$ transports the same source, schedule, tolerance, window, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and naming coordinates, while $C$ replays Real-facing requests through the same ordered route.

Cofinal schedule stability is the role of $J$. Since the carrier has no second schedule coordinate, every accelerated tail request must be read through the displayed schedule, and transport can only preserve that schedule through the corresponding $\hsame$ component.

Dyadic exactness is forced by $D$ and $W$. The ledger stores the requested dyadic scales and slack comparisons, and the windows opened by $J$ are exactly the windows available for the tolerance read.

The handoff to $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ is downstream. The readback row $R$ is built from $A,J,D,W$, the seal $E$ is reached only after $R$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ only preserve and name that route. Because no excluded coordinate appears in the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
