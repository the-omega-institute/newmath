\begin{theorem}[PinGroup source projection obligation]
\label{thm:pingroup-source-projection-obligation}
The $\PinGroupUp$ source projection surface is exactly the pair consisting of
the consumed $\SpinGroupUp$ source row and the consumed $\CliffordUp$ source
row, transported by the displayed $\hsame$ witnesses and finite $\Cont$
ledger of $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$. No public source projection is available
unless it factors through one of those two rows and the corresponding
$\Pkg$ provenance entry.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-ledger-coverage}. It places every
public source read in the Spin or Clifford source ledger and records the
same $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories in the carrier-facing certificate packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-exactness}. The inherited Spin
branch is the Spin projection, while the reflective branch is read through
the Clifford source together with the reflection-generator product ledger.

Now use \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-exhaustion}. It rules out a third source
projection, and the downstream package of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package} repacks the two source
rows with exactly their $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ witnesses.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup parity classifier obligation]
\label{thm:pingroup-parity-classifier-obligation}
The reflection-parity carrier of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-parity-carrier} is the complete public
classifier for $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$: every admitted public row is
classified either by the inherited $\SpinGroupUp$ branch or by the
$\CliffordUp$ reflection branch, and the classifier is exact under the
carried $\hsame$ transport rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project a public row through
\autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-boundary}. The row is read through
one branch of the reflection-parity carrier, except for the Spin overlap
already recorded by the inherited Spin branch.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-exactness}. It identifies the
Spin branch with rows factoring through the Spin-inclusion source and the
reflective branch with rows factoring through the Clifford
reflection-generator source and product ledger.

Finally use \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-exhaustion} and
\autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package}. The first excludes any
outside classifier source, and the second exports the same parity carrier,
transport witnesses, continuation ledger, and provenance rows to downstream
consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup Clifford action ledger obligation]
\label{thm:pingroup-clifford-action-ledger-obligation}
The Clifford action visible at the $\PinGroupUp$ obligation surface is
exhausted by the reflection ledger, Clifford action row, inherited Spin
boundary, finite $\Cont$ ledger, $\hsame$ witnesses, and $\Pkg$ provenance
rows displayed by $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$. The obligation surface exports
no host orthogonal action, matrix action, quotient algebra, or external Pin
object beyond those $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:pingroup-clifford-action-consumer-surface}. It lists
the action rows a consumer may inspect: reflection ledger, Clifford action,
Spin boundary, provenance, continuation transport, and $\hsame$ witnesses.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-ledger-closure} to anchor the
reflection part in carried Clifford rows, and apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-spin-boundary-exhaustion} to anchor the Spin part in
the consumed Spin source and even-grade Clifford rows.

Use \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-exhaustion} to exclude any source outside
the Spin and Clifford rows. Then
\autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package} repacks the same action,
ledger, transport, and provenance rows as the downstream obligation surface.
\end{proof}
