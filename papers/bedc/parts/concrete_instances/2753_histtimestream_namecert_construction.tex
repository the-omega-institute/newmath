\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HistTimeStreamUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-histtimestream-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\HistTimeStreamUp$ interface realizes the local-time-stream reading of
\autoref{ch:visions-observer-hist-identity} as a finite BEDC naming surface. A
stream is not a clock shared by distinct histories; it is the displayed sequence
of $\Cont$ steps by which one source history is generated from $\emp$.

\begin{definition}[HistTimeStream NameCert carrier]
\label{def:histtimestream-namecert-carrier}
A $\HistTimeStreamUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(H,T,S,C,L,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the source $\Hist$ row being read as a local stream;
\item $T$ is the finite sequence of $\Eone$ and $\Ezero$ time-step rows whose
terminal history is $H$;
\item $S$ is the start-at-empty source ledger, recording that the sequence begins
at $\emp$ and reaches only the displayed source history;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ route for each time-step row in $T$;
\item $L$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the displayed rows and
the no-global-clock ledger preventing a second history from importing this
stream as a synchronizing clock;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming $\BHist$, $\Hist$, $\Cont$,
$\hsame$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\Pkg$, and the local stream packet;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HistTimeStreamUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets by the displayed rows
$H,T,S,C,L,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host natural-number index, no
ambient time parameter, and no equality synchronizing the local streams of
distinct Hist values.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[HistTimeStream NameCert obligation sketch]
\label{thm:histtimestream-namecert-obligation-sketch}
The local $\NameCert_{\HistTimeStreamUp}$ obligation surface has five displayed
checks: carrier admission for $Q=(H,T,S,C,L,P,N)$, exactness of the
start-at-empty step ledger $S$, stability of every $\Cont$ route recorded in
$C$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $L$, and no-global-clock
non-escape for the stream named by $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier of
\autoref{def:histtimestream-namecert-carrier}. The row $H$ fixes the source
history, while $T$ and $S$ record the finite step ledger from $\emp$ to that
history.

For each displayed step in $T$, read the corresponding route in $C$. These
routes are the only permitted transitions, so the stream has no hidden branch
outside the finite packet.

Transport is then performed rowwise through $L$. The same ledger also records
that a second Hist cannot import this local stream as a shared clock, because
the packet names only the source row $H$ and its own finite route list.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ repack the source, step, route, transport, and locality
rows as a local $\NameCert$ surface. This is exactly the concrete packet needed
for the observer-history time-as-growth reading.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HistTimeStream observation carrier]
\label{thm:histtimestream-observation-carrier}
Every accepted $\HistTimeStreamUp$ packet exposes the source $\Hist$ row $H$, the finite $\Eone/\Ezero$ schedule $T$, the start-at-empty ledger $S$, and the displayed $\Cont$ replay row $C$ as carrier coordinates of
$$
Q=(H,T,S,C,L,P,N).
$$
The packet has no accepted observation surface apart from these coordinates, the componentwise $\hsame$ ledger $L$, package provenance $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\HistTimeStreamUp}$ row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through \autoref{def:histtimestream-namecert-carrier}. The source row, schedule row, start ledger, continuation replay ledger, transport ledger, package provenance, and local naming row are exactly the displayed coordinates of $Q$.

Apply \autoref{thm:histtimestream-namecert-obligation-sketch}. Its carrier-admission and step-ledger clauses identify $H,T,S,C$ as the public observation rows, while the remaining clauses leave only $L,P,N$ as transport, provenance, and naming data.

No other coordinate is present in the finite tuple. Therefore an accepted packet cannot export a separate observation carrier, ambient clock, or hidden route outside the listed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HistTimeStream schedule classifier stability]
\label{thm:histtimestream-schedule-classifier-stability}
For an accepted $\HistTimeStreamUp$ packet $Q=(H,T,S,C,L,P,N)$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport along the rows stored in $L$ preserves the finite schedule $T$, the start-at-empty ledger $S$, and the displayed $\Cont$ replay row $C$. The transported classifier remains indexed only by the displayed carrier rows and cannot introduce a host-indexed global time coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the classifier description in \autoref{def:histtimestream-namecert-carrier}. It compares packets by the visible rows $H,T,S,C,L,P,N$ and provides no coordinate for a natural-number host index or shared ambient time parameter.

Transport is componentwise through $L$. Each transported schedule entry remains one of the displayed $\Eone/\Ezero$ rows of $T$, the start ledger remains attached to $\emp$ and the terminal source row $H$, and every replayed route is still read through the corresponding row of $C$.

The no-global-clock clause in \autoref{thm:histtimestream-namecert-obligation-sketch} blocks the only possible escape route: importing the local stream into a second Hist as a synchronizing clock. Thus the transported classifier preserves the finite schedule and cannot acquire a host-indexed time row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HistTimeStream NameCert ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:histtimestream-namecert-ledger-exhaustion}
Every public $\NameCert_{\HistTimeStreamUp}$ read factors through the carrier row, the finite step ledger, route stability, componentwise transport, package provenance, and the local naming row:
$$
H,T,S,C,L,P,N.
$$
No public read from the packet yields inter-Hist synchronization, a rate claim, an ambient global clock, or a host time index.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use the observation-carrier theorem to place every public read inside the finite tuple $Q=(H,T,S,C,L,P,N)$. The source, schedule, start ledger, route replay, transport, package, and local naming rows are the complete carrier inventory.

Next use schedule classifier stability. It keeps the step ledger and $\Cont$ replay rows stable under componentwise $\hsame$ transport and prevents the classifier from acquiring a global-time coordinate.

Finally inspect the package and local naming rows. They certify only the displayed $\BHist$, $\Hist$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance for this one local stream, so the finite coordinate list exhausts the public NameCert ledger and leaves no synchronization or rate claim to read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HistTimeStream observer-locality lattice link]
\label{thm:histtimestream-observer-locality-lattice-link}
Every accepted $\HistTimeStreamUp$ read of
$$
Q=(H,T,S,C,L,P,N)
$$
is consumed as the local-time component of the observer-history identity and observer-locality surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-locality-transport-namecert}. The stream supplies the displayed source history, finite step ledger, continuation replay, and no-global-clock transport row, but it supplies no inter-Hist synchronization, shared clock, or imported host-time index.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $Q=(H,T,S,C,L,P,N)$. The rows $H,T,S,C$ give the source history, start-at-empty finite schedule, and displayed $\Cont$ replay that the observer-history identity surface reads as its local growth component.

Use \autoref{thm:histtimestream-namecert-ledger-exhaustion}. Its no-global-clock ledger keeps the read attached to the single source row $H$ and blocks any second Hist from importing $T$ as a synchronizing clock.

The sibling packets of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-locality-transport-namecert} therefore consume this same local stream through $L,P,N$. They may use it to identify observer-history locality and transport surfaces, but they add no inter-Hist clock equality, host-time coordinate, or cross-history synchronization row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HistTimeStream public export]
\label{thm:histtimestream-public-export}
An accepted packet
$$
Q=(H,T,S,C,L,P,N)
$$
exports exactly the local-time stream $\NameCert_{\HistTimeStreamUp}$ surface: the source $\Hist$ row $H$, the finite $\Eone/\Ezero$ schedule $T$, the start-at-empty ledger $S$, the displayed $\Cont$ replay row $C$, the componentwise $\hsame$ and no-global-clock ledger $L$, the $\Pkg$ provenance row $P$, and the local $\NameCert$ row $N$. No public export from $Q$ yields an inter-Hist clock, host time index, global synchronization row, temporal-rate claim, or hidden route outside those displayed coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier of \autoref{def:histtimestream-namecert-carrier}. It lists exactly the rows $H,T,S,C,L,P,N$, with $H$ as the source history, $T$ as the finite $\Eone/\Ezero$ schedule, $S$ as the start-at-empty ledger, $C$ as the displayed replay row, and $L,P,N$ as transport, provenance, and local naming data.

Use the observation carrier theorem, \autoref{thm:histtimestream-observation-carrier}, to identify $H,T,S,C$ as the only observation rows. Then apply schedule classifier stability, \autoref{thm:histtimestream-schedule-classifier-stability}, so componentwise $\hsame$ transport preserves the finite schedule and cannot add a host-indexed global time coordinate.

Next apply ledger exhaustion, \autoref{thm:histtimestream-namecert-ledger-exhaustion}. It forces every public $\NameCert_{\HistTimeStreamUp}$ read through the same carrier, schedule, replay, transport, package, and naming rows.

Finally use the observer-locality lattice link, \autoref{thm:histtimestream-observer-locality-lattice-link}. The downstream observer-history surfaces consume the packet as one local stream, so the public export is precisely the displayed local-time stream surface and no inter-Hist synchronization or hidden route is available.
\end{proof}
