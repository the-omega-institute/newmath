\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\QuantumStateUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-quantumstate-namecert}

The quantum-state interface $\QuantumStateUp$ packages a unit vector in a Hilbert space carrier together with the equivalence under global phase. It depends on $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveSpaceUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-projectivespace-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:quantumstate-carrier}

\begin{definition}[QuantumStateUp Hilbert-projective packet]
\label{def:quantumstate-hilbert-projective-packet}
A $\QuantumStateUp$ packet is the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$(H,P,u,\pi,\phi,\rho,\lambda)$ with the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $H$ is the consumed $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ row, including its carried Hilbert endpoint surface and classifier transport.
  \item $P$ is the consumed $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveSpaceUp}$ row, including its punctured representative surface and scaling-orbit classifier.
  \item $u$ is a Hilbert endpoint over $H$ together with the displayed unit row
  $$
  \mathsf{Unit}_H(u).
  $$
  \item $\pi$ is a punctured projective representative row over $P$.
  \item $\phi$ is the global-phase ledger saying that the Hilbert endpoint $u$ and the punctured representative $\pi$ are related through the $\ProjectiveSpaceUp$ scaling-orbit classifier.
  \item $\rho$ records the $\Pkg$ provenance of the two consumed certificate rows and the chosen endpoint rows.
  \item $\lambda$ is the finite $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ transport ledger carrying the Hilbert endpoint, unit row, projective representative, and phase ledger through accepted history replacements.
\end{enumerate}
No host Hilbert carrier, quotient carrier, or equality of external vectors is added to the tuple; every visible row is read through $H$, $P$, $\Pkg$, $\Cont$, and $\hsame$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[QuantumStateUp phase classifier]
\label{def:quantumstate-phase-classifier}
Let $q=(H,P,u,\pi,\phi,\rho,\lambda)$ and $q'=(H',P',u',\pi',\phi',\rho',\lambda')$ be $\QuantumStateUp$ packets. They classify as the same quantum-state row, written $q\sim_{\QuantumStateUp}q'$, exactly when the Hilbert endpoint rows $u$ and $u'$ transport by the $\hsame$ classifier supplied by the consumed $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ rows, the projective representative rows $\pi$ and $\pi'$ classify by the $\ProjectiveSpaceUp$ scaling-orbit classifier, the unit rows $\mathsf{Unit}_H(u)$ and $\mathsf{Unit}_{H'}(u')$ transport along the same endpoint history comparison, and the phase ledgers $\phi$ and $\phi'$ carry the same $\Pkg$ provenance. The classifier records no host vector equality, no independent Hilbert-space quotient, and no primitive quotient object beyond the projective scaling classifier already present in $P$.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:quantumstate-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[QuantumStateUp Hilbert unit projection]
\label{thm:quantumstate-hilbert-unit-projection}
Every admitted $\QuantumStateUp$ packet projects to the Hilbert carrier endpoint and unit row supplied by its consumed $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ surface. More precisely, if $(H,P,u,\pi,\phi,\rho,\lambda)$ is a carried packet of \autoref{def:quantumstate-hilbert-projective-packet}, then $u$ is an accepted Hilbert endpoint over $H$, the displayed row $\mathsf{Unit}_H(u)$ is present, and both rows are stable under the $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport entries recorded by $\lambda$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the finite tuple of \autoref{def:quantumstate-hilbert-projective-packet}. The first component $H$ is the consumed $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$ row, so its public carrier field supplies the Hilbert endpoint surface on which $u$ lives.

The third component is not an arbitrary endpoint; it is the endpoint $u$ paired with the displayed row $\mathsf{Unit}_H(u)$. Projection of a finite tuple therefore reads back exactly the Hilbert endpoint and its unit witness.

Finally use the last component $\lambda$. Its $\hsame$ entries transport the endpoint row, and its $\Cont$ entries transport the finite continuation ledger. Repacking those transported rows gives the same Hilbert projection for every admitted representative of the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuantumStateUp projective phase projection]
\label{thm:quantumstate-projective-phase-projection}
Every admitted $\QuantumStateUp$ packet projects to the $\ProjectiveSpaceUp$ punctured carrier and scaling-orbit classifier. If $(H,P,u,\pi,\phi,\rho,\lambda)$ is carried, then $\pi$ is a punctured representative over the consumed $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveSpaceUp}$ row $P$, the phase ledger $\phi$ relates $u$ to $\pi$ through the scaling-orbit classifier of \autoref{thm:projectivespace-scaling-orbit-classifier-obligation}, and any representative change remains ledger data rather than public carrier data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the second and fourth components of the packet. The row $P$ is the consumed $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveSpaceUp}$ certificate, and $\pi$ is explicitly one of its punctured representative rows.

Next read the fifth component. The phase ledger $\phi$ records that the Hilbert endpoint and the selected punctured representative are compared through the same scaling-orbit classifier that defines $\ProjectiveSpaceUp$ classification.

For a replacement of projective representative, apply the classifier surface of $P$. The scalar witness and representative route are retained inside $\phi$ and $\lambda$, while the public projection still exposes only punctured-carrier membership and the scaling-orbit classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuantumStateUp public certificate surface]
\label{thm:quantumstate-public-certificate-surface}
The public $\QuantumStateUp$ certificate surface consists exactly of the carrier packet of \autoref{def:quantumstate-hilbert-projective-packet}, the phase classifier of \autoref{def:quantumstate-phase-classifier}, the Hilbert projection of \autoref{thm:quantumstate-hilbert-unit-projection}, the projective projection of \autoref{thm:quantumstate-projective-phase-projection}, stability transport through $\hsame$ and $\Cont$, the phase ledger, and exactness for those rows. It contains no density-matrix row, measurement row, channel row, or spectral row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Coverage follows by unpacking an admitted packet. Its visible fields are exactly $H$, $P$, $u$, $\pi$, the phase ledger $\phi$, the package provenance $\rho$, and the transport ledger $\lambda$; the preceding two projection theorems read back the Hilbert and projective components.

Soundness repacks those same entries. A candidate public row is accepted only when the Hilbert endpoint and unit witness come from $H$, the punctured representative and scaling classifier come from $P$, and the phase ledger relates them through the displayed classifier rows.

Completeness is immediate from the tuple constructor: any finite package with exactly those rows is a $\QuantumStateUp$ packet, and the classifier of \autoref{def:quantumstate-phase-classifier} is the boundary comparison used for packet replacement.

The named dependency certificates expose no density-matrix, measurement, channel, or spectral row at this site. Since the public surface is exhausted by the displayed tuple, classifier, projections, transport, phase ledger, and exactness clauses, none of those additional quantum interfaces is part of the certificate surface.
\end{proof}
