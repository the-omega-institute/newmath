\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FormalBallUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-formalball-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis and Vickers-style formal-ball presentations, the $\FormalBallUp$ packet records a radius-indexed observation interface for constructive completion. It sits between the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the dyadic radius ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the uniform-completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}. The packet gives completion consumers a finite formal-ball window without quotienting Cauchy names, selecting limits, using countable choice, invoking open-cover compactness, or assuming ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[FormalBall carrier]
\label{def:formalball-carrier}
A $\FormalBallUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(M,R,D,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ source row, including the point classifier, distance witness surface, and finite observation histories.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ radius-name row read as a positive rational tolerance through finite requests.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison ledger recording radius refinement, dyadic addition, and the strict comparison budget attached to $R$.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row whose entries display the selected center observations and requested radii before any completion handoff.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, radius row, dyadic ledger, finite window, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from metric observations to radius refinements, window reads, $\RealUp$ tolerance seals, and the $\UniformCompletionUp$ handoff boundary.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FormalBallUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RealUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FormalBallUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FormalBall NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:formalball-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:formalball-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\FormalBallUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $B=(M,R,D,W,H,C,P,N)$, metric source exposure through $M$, radius-name exposure through $R$, dyadic refinement exposure through $D$, finite-window exposure through $W$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

Every completion-facing read of an accepted carrier must pass through the displayed route
$$
M\longmapsto R\longmapsto D\longmapsto W\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
No such read exports quotient completion equality, a chosen Cauchy representative, a selected limit, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is tuple projection from \autoref{def:formalball-carrier}. The tuple displays the metric source, radius name, dyadic ledger, finite window, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

The analytic obligations are exactly $M,R,D,W$. The metric row supplies point and distance reads, the radius row supplies the requested rational scale, the dyadic ledger records the refinement budget, and the stream window exposes the center observations available at that scale.

The structural obligations are exactly $H,C,P,N$. They transport the same rows, replay the same finite route, record package provenance, and bind the local certificate. Since the carrier contains no quotient, selected representative, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, compactness principle, or ambient-completeness coordinate, none can be exported by the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FormalBall radius refinement]
\label{thm:formalball-radius-refinement}
Let $B=(M,R,D,W,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\FormalBallUp$ carrier. A refinement request from radius $r$ to a displayed smaller radius $s$ is admissible only when the dyadic ledger $D$ records the comparison and the replay row $C$ reads the corresponding finite center window $W$ over the same metric source $M$.

The refined formal ball is therefore a smaller window inside the same packet. It is not a second metric, not a quotient of centers, not a selected Cauchy representative, and not an appeal to ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet and read the metric source $M$. Any center comparison used by the refinement is interpreted through the point classifier and distance rows already displayed there.

Next read the radius row $R$ and the dyadic ledger $D$. The ledger is the only coordinate that records the comparison between $r$ and $s$, together with the finite arithmetic budget needed to keep the smaller radius inside the requested tolerance.

Finally replay the finite window through $W$ and $C$. The transport row $H$ preserves the same metric, radius, dyadic, and window rows, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and naming. Thus refinement narrows an existing displayed formal-ball window and introduces no extra metric, quotient, representative choice, selected limit, or completeness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FormalBall radius refinement chain]
\label{thm:formalball-radius-refinement-chain}
Let $B=(M,R,D,W,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\FormalBallUp$ carrier. For any finite displayed chain of radius refinements
$$
r_0 \ge r_1 \ge \cdots \ge r_k ,
$$
whose comparisons are all recorded in the same dyadic ledger $D$ and replayed through the same row $C$, every formal-ball window reached along the chain is read over the same metric source $M$ and the same finite stream-window row $W$.

The chain therefore refines only the displayed radius budget of the accepted packet. It does not identify quotient-completion classes, choose Cauchy representatives, select limits, use countable choice, invoke open-cover compactness, or assume ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the empty chain there is no new refinement step. The accepted packet already exposes the metric source $M$, the radius row $R$, the dyadic ledger $D$, the stream-window row $W$, and the replay row $C$ through \autoref{def:formalball-carrier}.

For a nonempty chain, apply \autoref{thm:formalball-radius-refinement} to the first displayed comparison. The theorem keeps the smaller formal-ball window inside the same accepted packet, with its comparison recorded by $D$ and its finite center read through $W$ and $C$ over $M$.

Iterate the same argument over the remaining displayed comparisons. The transport row $H$ carries the metric, radius, dyadic, window, and replay rows componentwise at each step, while $P$ and $N$ only record provenance and local naming for the same packet.

No step introduces a field for quotient-completion equality, a selected Cauchy representative, a selected limit, a countable-choice schedule, an open-cover compactness principle, or an ambient complete space. Hence the whole finite chain preserves the same finite formal-ball window interface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FormalBall completion-window handoff]
\label{thm:formalball-completion-window-handoff}
Every $\UniformCompletionUp$-facing consumer of an accepted $\FormalBallUp$ carrier $B=(M,R,D,W,H,C,P,N)$ receives only the finite window route through $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\UniformCompletionUp$ rows recorded by $B$. The handoff exposes radius-indexed formal balls as finite observation data before any completion boundary is read.

Consequently the handoff can support constructive completion arguments that use formal balls as observable neighborhoods, but it does not close quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the metric source and radius data. The rows $M$ and $R$ fix the center-observation surface and the requested radius scale before any completion-facing route is available.

Read the dyadic and stream windows next. The row $D$ records the arithmetic budget for the radius, and $W$ records the finite center windows inspected at that budget. Any $\RealUp$ tolerance seal reached by the consumer is read only through these rows.

Now pass to the completion boundary through $C$. The replay route carries the same finite formal-ball data to the $\UniformCompletionUp$ handoff, while $H,P,N$ preserve transport, provenance, and local naming.

The carrier fields are exhausted by this route. Since no field stores quotient equality, a selected limit, a countable-choice schedule, an open-cover compactness principle, or an ambient complete space, the completion-window handoff exports none of those principles.
\end{proof}
