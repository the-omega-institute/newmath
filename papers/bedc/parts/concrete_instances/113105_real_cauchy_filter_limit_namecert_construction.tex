\begin{theorem}[Real Cauchy filter limit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-cauchy-filter-limit-namecert-obligations}

No obligation row can be discharged by quotient filters, selected representatives, choice-selected limits, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. Each row required by the certificate is one of $F,B,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$, and the only analytic source before the terminal seal is the finite filter-base row $B$ under the Cauchy-filter source $F$.

Read the route through the finite windows. The row $W$ exposes the StreamName observations supplied by $B$; the row $R$ packages those observations as regular rational readback; and the row $D$ records the dyadic tolerance comparisons needed before the terminal RealUp seal $E$ is accepted.

Finally transport and replay the same rows. The $\hsame$ row $H$ acts componentwise on the displayed coordinates, $\Cont$ replay $C$ consumes only that displayed continuation, and $P,N$ record provenance and local naming for the same finite packet. Since the carrier has no quotient-filter, selected-representative, choice-limit, trichotomy, host-equality, or completeness coordinate, the certificate cannot export those claims.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

Use the NameCert obligation theorem. It identifies the local surface with the same finite rows and therefore leaves no hidden coordinate through which a consumer could replace $R$ by a selected limit or a quotient-filter representative.
