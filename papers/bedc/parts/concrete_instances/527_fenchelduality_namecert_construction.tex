\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FenchelDualityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-fenchelduality-namecert}

The $\FenchelDualityUp$ interface names the finite certificate surface of convex conjugate duality for displayed convex optimization packets. Its textbook sources are Rockafellar, \emph{Convex Analysis}, and Boyd--Vandenberghe, \emph{Convex Optimization}. The BEDC construction below keeps the duality statement at the visible row level: primal convex rows, affine-functional rows, conjugate epigraph rows, biconjugate comparison rows, scalar $\RealUp$ rows, and provenance through $\ConvexSetUp$, $\LPDualityUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\MatrixUp$, $\SemidefiniteConeUp$, and $\KKTUp$.

\section{Finite conjugate-duality carrier}
\label{sec:fenchelduality-finite-carrier}

\begin{definition}[FenchelDuality BHist carrier]
\label{def:fenchelduality-bhist-carrier}
A $\FenchelDualityUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
F=(P,D,E,B,S,Q,L)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is a primal convex row: a displayed $\VecSpaceUp$ ambient row, a $\ConvexSetUp$ feasible row, and a finite list of scalar $\RealUp$ objective reads;
\item $D$ is a dual affine-functional row: matrix and vector rows through $\MatrixUp$ and $\VecSpaceUp$, affine pairing rows, and scalar comparison rows through $\RealUp$;
\item $E$ is a conjugate epigraph row recording the finite inequality witnesses used to read each displayed affine functional against the primal convex row;
\item $B$ is a biconjugate comparison row recording only the displayed lower-bound and boundary comparison reads between the primal row and its finite biconjugate packet;
\item $S$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ stability surface for the primal, dual, epigraph, biconjugate, matrix, vector, and scalar rows;
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance naming the consumed $\ConvexSetUp$, $\LPDualityUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\MatrixUp$, $\SemidefiniteConeUp$, $\KKTUp$, and $\RealUp$ certificate boundaries;
\item $L$ is a ledger hiding only finite-support choices, omitted comparison bookkeeping, and row-order bookkeeping beyond the displayed packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is $\mathsf{FenchelDualityPacket}(P,D,E,B,S,Q,L)$. Two accepted carriers classify together exactly when their primal, dual, epigraph, biconjugate, stability, provenance, and ledger rows transport componentwise by displayed $\hsame$ and by the same $\Cont$ routes named in $Q$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FenchelDuality conjugate row obligation]
\label{thm:fenchelduality-conjugate-row-obligation}
For every accepted $\FenchelDualityUp$ carrier, the conjugate row is exactly the finite epigraph packet $E$ of \autoref{def:fenchelduality-bhist-carrier}. A consumer may read from $E$ only the displayed affine-functional rows, scalar $\RealUp$ comparison rows, $\ConvexSetUp$ feasible rows, $\MatrixUp$ and $\VecSpaceUp$ pairing provenance, and the componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes recorded in $S$ and $Q$.

The row exports no completed supremum, no separating-hyperplane theorem, no topological closedness assertion, and no host convex-analysis semantics.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:fenchelduality-bhist-carrier}. The conjugate coordinate is the single finite epigraph row $E$, and its readable entries are the affine-functional, scalar comparison, feasible-set, matrix, vector, stability, and provenance rows listed in the carrier.

Transport of the conjugate row is supplied by the stability surface $S$. Each displayed comparison is moved by componentwise $\hsame$ and then read through the same $\Cont$ routes named by $Q$.

No coordinate in the packet contains a completed supremum, a separating-hyperplane certificate, a topological closure witness, or a host convex-analysis object. Therefore the obligation surface of the conjugate row is exactly the finite epigraph packet stated above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FenchelDuality biconjugate boundary]
\label{thm:fenchelduality-biconjugate-boundary}
The biconjugate comparison boundary of an accepted $\FenchelDualityUp$ carrier is the finite row $B$ together with the scalar $\RealUp$ comparisons and dependency provenance named by $S$ and $Q$. It records displayed lower-bound and boundary comparisons between the primal convex row and the finite biconjugate packet.

The boundary does not claim lower semicontinuity, topological closure of epigraphs, equality with a completed biconjugate function, or any separation theorem beyond the rows already present in the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the biconjugate coordinate $B$ from the carrier. Its scalar endpoints are $\RealUp$ rows, and its comparison slots are transported through the same stability and package rows used by the primal and dual faces.

The primal convex row $P$ and the dual affine-functional row $D$ provide the two sides of every displayed comparison. The biconjugate row records only those finite comparisons and their provenance.

The excluded topological and analytic principles would require coordinates outside the carrier: closed epigraph data, completed supremum data, separation witnesses, or host function equality. Since none of those rows are present, the biconjugate boundary is exactly the finite comparison surface described in the statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FenchelDuality certificate obligation surface]
\label{thm:fenchelduality-certificate-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\FenchelDualityUp}$ obligation surface consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier and classifier: the $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet and accepted-row classifier of \autoref{def:fenchelduality-bhist-carrier};
\item conjugate row: the finite epigraph obligation of \autoref{thm:fenchelduality-conjugate-row-obligation};
\item biconjugate boundary: the finite comparison surface of \autoref{thm:fenchelduality-biconjugate-boundary};
\item dependency provenance: the $\Pkg$ row tying the packet to $\ConvexSetUp$, $\LPDualityUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\MatrixUp$, $\SemidefiniteConeUp$, $\KKTUp$, and $\RealUp$;
\item downstream consumer boundary: the finite read boundary that allows optimization consumers to use only the displayed primal, dual, epigraph, comparison, stability, and provenance rows.
\end{enumerate}
These rows are the full seed surface for the finite Fenchel-duality name.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier row supplies the seven displayed coordinates and fixes the accepted readback relation. This determines the source domain of the name.

Apply \autoref{thm:fenchelduality-conjugate-row-obligation} to bind the conjugate face to a finite epigraph packet. Apply \autoref{thm:fenchelduality-biconjugate-boundary} to bind the biconjugate face to finite scalar comparisons.

The provenance row names every consumed BEDC certificate, and the ledger hides only finite-support choices and comparison bookkeeping. Repacking these rows gives the five local $\NameCert_{\FenchelDualityUp}$ obligations and leaves no extra analytic coordinate to export.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FenchelDuality downstream consumer boundary]
\label{thm:fenchelduality-downstream-consumer-boundary}
A downstream $\KKTUp$, $\SemidefiniteConeUp$, or optimization consumer of $\NameCert_{\FenchelDualityUp}$ may read only the finite primal convex row, dual affine-functional row, conjugate epigraph row, biconjugate comparison row, scalar $\RealUp$ rows, componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and finite-support ledger of \autoref{def:fenchelduality-bhist-carrier}.

The consumer boundary does not provide strong duality, constraint qualification, Slater data, completed conjugates, closedness of convex functions, or host optimization semantics.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Take an arbitrary downstream read of the certificate. By \autoref{thm:fenchelduality-certificate-obligation-surface}, the read factors through the carrier, conjugate row, biconjugate boundary, provenance row, and finite ledger.

The named optimization consumers can use those rows as inputs to their own finite packets: $\KKTUp$ reads primal-dual and stationarity-facing rows, while $\SemidefiniteConeUp$ reads matrix, vector, cone, and scalar comparison rows. Each such read remains routed through displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ provenance.

All excluded duality principles require data absent from the carrier. The consumer therefore receives exactly the finite BEDC certificate surface stated in the theorem.
\end{proof}
