The $\RealSealAdmissionCutUp$ packet records the finite boundary at which a Real-completion route becomes admissible. It sits after the observation-budget limiter of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observationbudgetlimiter-namecert}, the finite window carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and before any $\RealUp$ seal or $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff is consumed. The packet is an admission cut: it records the ordered entry boundary for a finite route, not a host completeness principle, quotient stream, selected limit, or independent real equality.

\begin{definition}[Real seal admission-cut carrier]
\label{def:real-seal-admission-cut-carrier}
A $\RealSealAdmissionCutUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(E,B,W,Q,S,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the finite seal-request row whose requested observation is being admitted;
\item $B$ is the $\ObservationBudgetLimiterUp$ row that caps the request before any window read is served;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row admitted under that budget;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained only from the displayed window;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row that may be consumed only after $E,B,W,Q$ have been displayed;
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff row that reads the same admitted route;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the request, budget, window, readback, seal, limit handoff, route, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ route from request to budget admission, from budget admission to window, from window to readback, from readback to Real seal, and from the Real seal to the Cauchy-limit handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\ObservationBudgetLimiterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealSealAdmissionCutUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for an unbounded observation schedule, a window served outside $B$, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback outside $W$, a $\RealUp$ seal outside $Q$, a selected host limit, a quotient stream equality, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real seal admission-cut NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-seal-admission-cut-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:real-seal-admission-cut-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RealSealAdmissionCutUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
A=(E,B,W,Q,S,L,H,C,P,N),

budget-limited request admission, StreamName window locality, RegSeqRat readback locality, Real seal ordering, CauchyLimitSeal handoff ordering, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, local naming, and non-escape of any seal route that bypasses $B$ or $W$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:real-seal-admission-cut-carrier}. The finite packet exposes the request, budget, window, readback, seal, handoff, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows.

Budget-limited request admission uses the sibling limiter row. The request $E$ is admitted only through $B$, and the StreamName window $W$ is served only under that admitted budget.

Readback and seal ordering then follow the displayed route. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $Q$ reads the finite window $W$, the $\RealUp$ seal $S$ reads $Q$, and the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row $L$ consumes the same admitted seal route.

The transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows preserve that order. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a bypassing schedule, host limit, quotient stream equality, independent seal, or ambient completeness theorem, the local NameCert surface is exhausted by the displayed admission cut.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real seal admission-cut route non-escape]
\label{thm:real-seal-admission-cut-route-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$ seal read or $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff accepted through a $\RealSealAdmissionCutUp$ packet factors through the ordered finite boundary
$$
E \to B \to W \to Q \to S \to L .
$$
A route that bypasses either the observation-budget limiter $B$ or the StreamName window $W$ is not admitted by the packet and cannot be consumed as the displayed Real-completion handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:real-seal-admission-cut-namecert-obligations}. They force every admitted read to expose the request, limiter, window, readback, seal, and limit-handoff rows in the displayed order.

Use $H$ and $C$ to transport and replay the same finite route. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport cannot replace the budget row or the window row, and the displayed continuation route has no branch from $E$ directly to $Q$, $S$, or $L$.

It remains to exclude downstream escape. The carrier inventory has no row for an unbounded observation schedule, a selected host limit, quotient stream equality, independent real equality, or ambient completeness. Therefore any downstream $\RealUp$ or $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ consumer receives only the admitted boundary $E,B,W,Q,S,L,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}
