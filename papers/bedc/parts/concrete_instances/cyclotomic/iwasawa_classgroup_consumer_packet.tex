\begin{definition}[Cyclotomic Iwasawa classgroup consumer packet]
\label{def:cyclotomic-iwasawa-classgroup-consumer-packet}
The Cyclotomic Iwasawa classgroup consumer packet is the finite row family
read by an $\IwasawaUp$ classgroup consumer from an accepted
$\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ source. It consists of the cyclotomic layer rows
$\mathcal N,n,p,\mathcal S,\zeta$, the primitive-root endpoint row $\zeta$,
the factorization ledger rows of $\mathcal S$ over the polynomial ledger $p$,
the classifier transport supplied by the cyclotomic rational-polynomial
classifier, the finite $\Cont$ acceptance and factorization provenance row
$\alpha$, and the shared $\Pkg$ provenance row $\rho$.

The packet is only a BEDC consumer surface. It is not a host cyclotomic field,
not a class-field bridge, not analytic Iwasawa data, and not a quotient
carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic Iwasawa classgroup consumer projection]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-classgroup-consumer-projection}
For every accepted $\CyclotomicUp$ source carrier
$$
\mathcal C_{\zeta}=(\mathcal N,n,p,\mathcal S,\zeta,\alpha,\lambda,\rho),
$$
the $\IwasawaUp$ classgroup consumer rows projected from that carrier are
exactly the rows of
\autoref{def:cyclotomic-iwasawa-classgroup-consumer-packet}. The projection
uses the cyclotomic source boundary, the primitive-root endpoint rows, the
factorization ledger rows, the classifier transport, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$
provenance already present in the source carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the cyclotomic source boundary of
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-source-boundary}. It projects the NumField
source row, the positive exponent row, the primitive-root endpoint, the
factorization ledger for $X^n-1$, and the shared package provenance to the
Iwasawa-facing surface.

Next use the root obligation closure rows. The packet of
\autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-obligation-packet} carries the polynomial ledger,
the SplittingField source, the primitive endpoint, the finite $\Cont$
acceptance row, the transported $\hsame$ comparison, and the $\Pkg$ provenance
row. The transport theorem
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-primitive-root-ledger-transport} keeps these rows tied
to the same source carrier.

Repack the projected rows by
\autoref{def:cyclotomic-iwasawa-classgroup-consumer-packet}. None of the cited
source-boundary or root-obligation rows contains a host cyclotomic field,
class-field bridge, analytic Iwasawa datum, or quotient carrier, so none is
exported by the projection.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic Iwasawa classgroup consumer exhaustion]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-classgroup-consumer-exhaustion}
Every Iwasawa-facing cyclotomic read used by a classgroup consumer is one of
the rows of
\autoref{def:cyclotomic-iwasawa-classgroup-consumer-packet}, and every such
row repacks into the same consumer packet. The surface has no host
cyclotomic-field carrier, class-field bridge, analytic Iwasawa row, or
quotient carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project any Iwasawa-facing read through
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-root-source-row-exhaustion}. The source row must be
one of the root carrier, classifier transport, polynomial ledger, primitive
endpoint, threshold, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, or $\Pkg$ rows of the root obligation
packet.

Now restrict to the classgroup consumer view using
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-root-iwasawa-classfield-consumer-boundary}. The
Iwasawa side receives only the source, exponent, primitive endpoint,
factorization, and provenance rows needed for its finite classgroup-facing
input; class-field operations remain outside the cyclotomic packet.

Conversely, each row listed in
\autoref{def:cyclotomic-iwasawa-classgroup-consumer-packet} is a coordinate of
the cyclotomic source boundary or of the root obligation closure package.
Therefore the rows repack into the same consumer packet, and the exclusions
follow because neither cited boundary contains a host field, analytic
Iwasawa object, class-field bridge, or quotient carrier.
\end{proof}
