\begin{definition}[Cyclotomic root downstream consumer surface]
\label{def:cyclotomic-root-unblock-consumer-surface}
The downstream consumer surface of $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ is the tuple of
rows already present in \autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-carrier}: the
$\mathsf{BHist}$ entries $\mathcal{N}$, $n$, $p$, $\mathcal{S}$, and $\zeta$,
the $\Cont$ acceptance row $\alpha$, the $\hsame$ comparison row $\lambda$, and
the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row $\rho$.  A consumer may address this tuple
through the $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$
projections, the carrier/classifier rows of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-carrier}
and \autoref{def:cyclotomic-rational-polynomial-classifier}, and the certificate
surface of \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-namecert-obligation-surface}.  No host
field equality, quotient of roots, valuation row, class-group row, or
class-field operation is part of this surface.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic root carrier classifier scope]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-root-carrier-classifier-scope}
For every accepted $\CyclotomicUp$ carrier, the classifier scope available to
downstream consumers is exactly the componentwise $\hsame$ transport on the
$\mathsf{BHist}$ rows of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-unblock-consumer-surface},
together with the displayed $\Cont$ and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ ledgers.  In particular,
the $\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\SplittingFieldUp}$ projections
remain the only source certificates visible to an $\IwasawaUp$ or
$\ClassFieldUp$ consumer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-carrier}.  The visible
rows are precisely $\mathcal{N}$, $n$, $p$, $\mathcal{S}$, $\zeta$, $\alpha$,
$\lambda$, and $\rho$.

Apply \autoref{def:cyclotomic-rational-polynomial-classifier} and
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-root-layer-classifier-transport}.  They transport the
source, exponent, polynomial, splitting-field, and endpoint rows by $\hsame$
and keep the endpoint acceptance and provenance attached by $\Cont$ and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$.

The two projected source certificates are therefore the only certificates
available at the root boundary.  Since no other coordinate appears in the
carrier or classifier, downstream consumers receive no host equality or
quotient-root datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic root ledger stability package]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-root-ledger-stability-package}
The ledger package on the cyclotomic root surface is stable under classifier
transport: the rational polynomial ledger for $X^n-1$, the primitive endpoint
readback, the splitting-field root and factorization rows, and the
$\NameCert_{\NumFieldUp}$ provenance remain the same public rows after
transport through $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ support.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-polynomial-ledger-exactness} for the polynomial
ledger and \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-primitive-root-readback-exactness} for the
endpoint readback.  These identify the public root rows as projections of the
same accepted carrier.

Next apply \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-splittingfield-stability} and
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-numfield-embedding-ledger}.  The first transports the
splitting-field root and factorization rows, while the second preserves the
NumFieldUp provenance ledger.

Repack those projections into the surface of
\autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-unblock-consumer-surface}.  The repacking uses
only the listed carrier and certificate rows, so it adds no host equality,
valuation, or class-field row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic root downstream unblock package]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-root-downstream-unblock-package}
The pair consisting of \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-root-carrier-classifier-scope}
and \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-root-ledger-stability-package} supplies the complete
root obligation surface needed by downstream $\IwasawaUp$ and $\ClassFieldUp$
fronts: a $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier tuple, $\hsame$ classifier transport, the
$\Cont$ acceptance ledger, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row, and the two source
$\NameCert$ projections for $\NumFieldUp$ and $\SplittingFieldUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier/classifier scope theorem.  It fixes the exported rows
and the only allowed transport mechanism for each projected root component.

Use the ledger stability package to carry the polynomial, primitive endpoint,
splitting-field, factorization, and NumFieldUp provenance rows through that
transport.  These rows are exactly the rows listed in
\autoref{def:cyclotomic-root-unblock-consumer-surface}.

Therefore an $\IwasawaUp$ or $\ClassFieldUp$ consumer can cite the cyclotomic
root surface as a stable source package.  The cited package still contains no
tower transition map, class group, reciprocity map, local symbol, completion
row, quotient root, or equality of ambient fields.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cyclotomic root Iwasawa and ClassField consumer boundary]
\label{thm:cyclotomic-root-iwasawa-classfield-consumer-boundary}
When $\IwasawaUp$ or $\ClassFieldUp$ consumes $\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$, the
consumed data are exactly the root downstream unblock package of
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-root-downstream-unblock-package}.  $\IwasawaUp$ may read
the cyclotomic layer as source data for later tower rows, and $\ClassFieldUp$
may read the same layer as source data for later reciprocity rows, but neither
consumer receives tower maps, class groups, reciprocity maps, local symbols,
completion data, quotient-root carriers, or host-field equality from
$\NameCert_{\CyclotomicUp}$ alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the Iwasawa side, apply \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-source-boundary}
and \autoref{thm:cyclotomic-iwasawa-tower-source-readback}.  They read the
cyclotomic carrier as a layer source while keeping tower transition and measure
rows outside the cyclotomic packet.

For the class-field side, apply
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-classfield-source-interface}.  It reads the same
$\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows as
source data and leaves class-field operations to their own certificate.

Both projections factor through
\autoref{thm:cyclotomic-root-downstream-unblock-package}.  Hence the downstream
boundary is shared and exact, with no extra root quotient or ambient-field
equality introduced by either consumer.
\end{proof}
