\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyCompletionOperatorUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive completion, the $\CauchyCompletionOperatorUp$ packet names the finite operator surface that turns displayed Cauchy data into a completion-facing handoff without selecting quotient representatives. It sits between the metric completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-boundary-namecert}, the uniform completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, the separated limit surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-separated-metric-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The operator is therefore a finite NameCert object over visible StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readbacks, DyadicRatCore tolerances, completion-boundary rows, and Real-facing seals, not a host completion class or a choice of limits.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionOperator carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-operator-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionOperatorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(M,B,U,S,R,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ source row, exposing the metric Cauchy request and its displayed finite tolerance route;
\item $B$ is the $\CauchyCompletionBoundaryUp$ row, recording the handoff from metric and uniform completion reads to finite-window and Real-facing consumers;
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ row, recording the entourage-facing completion presentation consumed by the same operator;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule of finite windows inspected by the completion request;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row extracted from the scheduled windows;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used by the Cauchy request, readback, and seal boundary;
\item $Q$ is the $\CompleteSeparatedMetricUp$ separated-limit row that receives the operator handoff only through the visible completion boundary;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $M,B,U,S,R,D,Q$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, boundary, uniform, window, readback, dyadic, separated-limit, Real-seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;

\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts only packets whose operator handoff is exhausted by $M,B,U,S,R,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable choice, host completion operators, or ambient real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionOperator NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-operator-namecert-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionOperatorUp$ carrier $O=(M,B,U,S,R,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionOperatorUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed twelve-row packet; metric-completion exposure through $M$; completion-boundary exposure through $B$; uniform-completion exposure through $U$; finite-window exposure through $S$; regular-rational readback through $R$; dyadic-tolerance exposure through $D$; separated-limit handoff through $Q$; terminal Real sealing through $E$; componentwise transport through $H$; displayed replay through $C$; package provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-operator-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the metric source row, completion-boundary row, uniform-completion row, finite windows, regular readback, dyadic ledger, separated-limit row, Real seal, and structural rows.

The operator obligations are read in the displayed order. The metric and uniform completion requests first pass through the boundary row $B$; the scheduled windows $S$, readback $R$, and dyadic ledger $D$ then supply the finite evidence needed by both completion and Real-facing consumers.

The separated-limit and Real-seal rows are downstream of the same packet. The row $Q$ is reached only through the visible boundary route, and the terminal seal $E$ is reached only after the finite windows, readback, and tolerance ledger have already been displayed.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since quotient completion equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable choice, host completion operators, and ambient real completeness are not coordinates of $O$, they cannot appear as local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionOperator functorial handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-operator-functorial-handoff}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\CauchyCompletionOperatorUp$ packet factors through the finite handoff
$$
M,U \longmapsto B \longmapsto S,R,D \longmapsto Q,E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the operator may be replayed by later functorial completion surfaces only as a displayed route from metric and uniform completion requests through common finite windows, dyadic tolerances, regular readback, separated-limit handoff, Real sealing, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier. The completion-facing rows $M$ and $U$ are not independent host completions; they are source rows of the same finite packet.

The boundary row $B$ aligns those source rows before any consumer reaches the scheduled windows. The rows $S,R,D$ then expose the common finite observations, regular rational readback, and dyadic tolerance ledger used by downstream completion requests.

The rows $Q$ and $E$ are terminal for the separated-limit and Real-facing sides of the handoff. They do not insert a quotient completion or a selected representative between the finite evidence and the terminal consumer.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve this route under transport, replay it as a displayed continuation, record provenance, and name the local certificate. Therefore any later functorial completion surface can consume only the displayed operator route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionOperator ledger nonescape]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-operator-ledger-nonescape}
No accepted $\CauchyCompletionOperatorUp$ packet exports quotient completion equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable-choice schedules, host completion operators, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness data. Every consumer route to $\CompleteSeparatedMetricUp$ or $\RealUp$ must first read the packet rows $M,B,U,S,R,D,Q,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier inventory in \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-operator-carrier}. Its analytic coordinates are the metric source, completion boundary, uniform source, finite windows, regular readback, dyadic ledger, separated-limit handoff, and Real seal.

The structural coordinates are only $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. They move, replay, source, and name the displayed packet; they do not add analytic data.

The excluded quotient, representative, limit, choice, host-operator, and ambient-completeness objects are absent from both inventories. Hence every separated-limit or Real-facing read is forced through the visible finite ledger and cannot escape the operator packet.
\end{proof}
