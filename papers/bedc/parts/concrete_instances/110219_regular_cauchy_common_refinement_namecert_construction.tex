Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\RegularCauchyCommonRefinementUp$ packet records the finite common-refinement route by which two displayed regular Cauchy names may be inspected through one shared target window before either reaches a $\RealUp$ seal. It sits after the single-schedule reindexing surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-reindexing-namecert}, the finite observation schedules of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy common refinement carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-common-refinement-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyCommonRefinementUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(S_0,S_1,I,M_0,Q_0,M_1,Q_1,W,D,R,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S_0$ and $S_1$ are the two source $\StreamNameUp$ schedule rows for the accepted regular Cauchy names;
\item $I$ is the common target $\StreamNameUp$ window through which both sources are inspected;
\item $M_0,Q_0$ are the monotone and cofinal reindex rows from $I$ to $S_0$;
\item $M_1,Q_1$ are the monotone and cofinal reindex rows from $I$ to $S_1$;
\item $W$ is the common target-window budget row, recording that a single displayed finite request feeds both reindex routes;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row shared by the two reads at the budget named by $W$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row showing that both inspected names reach the same regular rational tolerance surface;
\item $A$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $S_0,S_1,I,M_0,Q_0,M_1,Q_1,W,D,R$ have been consumed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two schedules, the common target window, both reindex routes, the tolerance, readback, Real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a common-window request through the two monotone--cofinal routes and then through the shared tolerance, readback, and Real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyReindexingUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCommonRefinementUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected subsequence limits, countable-choice selectors, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy common refinement obligation surface]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-common-refinement-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyCommonRefinementUp$ carrier $K=(S_0,S_1,I,M_0,Q_0,M_1,Q_1,W,D,R,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCommonRefinementUp}$ surface consists exactly of paired source-schedule admission, common-window admission, two monotone--cofinal reindex rows, shared dyadic tolerance, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal nonescape, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, local naming, and ledger refusal for quotient stream equality, selected subsequence limits, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, and ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-common-refinement-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the two source schedules, the common target window, two monotone--cofinal reindex pairs, the shared tolerance row, the regular readback row, the Real seal, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The common-window row $W$ is the only target-window coordinate. A consumer therefore cannot inspect the two source schedules by unrelated requests; each request must replay through $C$ and then through both displayed reindex routes.

The dyadic and regular rows are shared downstream rows. Since $D$ and $R$ are read only after the two reindex pairs have been displayed, the carrier records one tolerance surface and one regular readback surface rather than two unrelated limit witnesses.

Finally, $A$ is downstream of the same paired route, while $H,P,N$ preserve and package only the listed rows. No row remains from which to read quotient stream equality, a selected subsequence limit, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy common refinement]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-common-refinement}
Let $S_0$ and $S_1$ be accepted regular Cauchy source schedules with displayed finite schedules, and suppose the two schedules admit displayed monotone and cofinal reindex rows into a common target window $I$. Then there is an accepted $\RegularCauchyCommonRefinementUp$ carrier
$$
K=(S_0,S_1,I,M_0,Q_0,M_1,Q_1,W,D,R,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
whose $\RealUp$-facing consumers factor through the same dyadic tolerance row $D$ and the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$. The construction uses only the two displayed schedule rows, the two monotone--cofinal reindex routes, the common target window, finite $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row; it does not use quotient stream equality, selected subsequence limits, countable-choice selectors, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the two accepted regular Cauchy schedules. Each source schedule is available as a finite $\StreamNameUp$ row, and each displayed monotone--cofinal route is the same kind of finite route isolated in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-reindexing-namecert}.

Choose the displayed common target window $I$ as the single inspection surface. The row $W$ records the finite request made at that window, while $M_0,Q_0$ and $M_1,Q_1$ replay the request back to $S_0$ and $S_1$ without choosing a subsequence or comparing host streams.

Read the shared tolerance and regularity rows next. The $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row $D$ records the tolerance budget attached to $W$, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ is consumed after both reindex routes have been displayed. Hence both source reads reach the same finite regular rational surface before any Real-facing seal is inspected.

Transport and replay close the packet. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport identifies the rows that are meant to be shared, and $\Cont$ records the two finite route replays from $I$ through the source schedules and forward to $D,R,A$. The $\Pkg$ and local $\NameCert$ rows then package exactly this surface.

By \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-common-refinement-obligations}, the resulting packet contains no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected subsequence limits, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient completeness. Therefore every $\RealUp$-facing consumer of the accepted common-refinement carrier factors through the displayed common window, the shared dyadic tolerance, and the shared regular readback.
\end{proof}
