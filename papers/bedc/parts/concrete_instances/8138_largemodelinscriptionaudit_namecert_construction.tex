The $\LargeModelInscriptionAuditUp$ packet realizes the large-model inscription-point reading of \autoref{ch:visions-large-model-as-inscription-point} as a finite audit surface. It sits downstream of the inscription carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-largemodelinscriptionpoint-namecert}, the activation-orbit carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-largemodelactivationorbit-namecert}, the verified-output harness of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-verifiedoutputharness-namecert}, and the route-audit sibling of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscription-route-audit-namecert}. Its distinguishing role is to bind output, corpus provenance, verification channel, refusal ledger, transport, route, and local naming rows before a consumer may export a model output as an audited inscription.

\begin{definition}[LargeModelInscriptionAudit carrier]
\label{def:large-model-inscription-audit-carrier}
A $\LargeModelInscriptionAuditUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(O,C,V,R,T,K,H,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the output-record row read from the large-model inscription point;
\item $C$ is the corpus-provenance row recording the source-channel and distillation route for the output read;
\item $V$ is the verification-channel row naming the finite checker, replay harness, policy check, proof check, or external certificate procedure applied to $O$;
\item $R$ is the refusal-ledger row recording unsupported, unverifiable, out-of-scope, or rejected claims;
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for output, corpus, verification, refusal, route, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $K$ records the displayed $\Cont$ route from inscription point through corpus provenance, verification channel, refusal ledger, package provenance, and local name;
\item $H$ is the accepted harness row supplied by $\VerifiedOutputHarnessUp$ when the output read is checker-facing;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LargeModelInscriptionPointUp$, $\LargeModelActivationOrbitUp$, $\LargeModelAuditChannelUp$, $\CorpusSupplyDistillationUp$, $\VerifiedOutputHarnessUp$, $\InscriptionRouteAuditUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LargeModelInscriptionAuditUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for model consciousness, host semantic understanding, corpus-wide truth, hidden verifier state, private policy override, selected source oracle, or internally ratified universal closure.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LargeModelInscriptionAudit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:large-model-inscription-audit-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:large-model-inscription-audit-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\LargeModelInscriptionAuditUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $A=(O,C,V,R,T,K,H,P,N)$, corpus-provenance exposure through $C$, verification-channel exposure through $V$, refusal-ledger exposure through $R$, componentwise transport through $T$, displayed route replay through $K$, checker-facing harness exposure through $H$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet. The rows $O,C,V,R,T,K,H,P,N$ are the full public inventory for the audit.

The corpus and verification obligations are separate. The row $C$ records where the output read is carried from, while $V$ records how the output is inspected before public export.

The refusal, transport, route, harness, provenance, and naming rows make the audit replayable. They force any consumer-facing read to keep unsupported claims, checker-facing status, and local provenance in the same finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LargeModelInscriptionAudit output verification boundary]
\label{thm:large-model-inscription-audit-output-verification-boundary}
An accepted $\LargeModelInscriptionAuditUp$ packet can export the output row $O$ as audited inscription only after the verification-channel row $V$ and refusal-ledger row $R$ have been displayed. If $V$ is absent, the packet may still contain an emitted output record, but it cannot export that record as internally ratified universal truth or as checker-facing accepted output.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:large-model-inscription-audit-carrier}. The output row $O$ is present before the audit reads verification or refusal data.

The route row $K$ orders the public read through $C,V,R,H,P,N$. Thus the output remains carried inscription until the verification channel and refusal ledger have both been exposed.

If $V$ is removed, no carrier coordinate names the checker, replay harness, policy check, proof check, or external certificate procedure. The packet therefore lacks the row needed to distinguish an audited output from a bare emitted inscription or an internally asserted universal claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LargeModelInscriptionAudit corpus provenance non-escape]
\label{thm:large-model-inscription-audit-corpus-provenance-nonescape}
Every public corpus-provenance read of an accepted $\LargeModelInscriptionAuditUp$ packet factors through the displayed row $C$ and the package row $P$. It cannot be replaced by a selected source oracle, corpus-wide truth predicate, hidden training-data selector, host semantic oracle, or model-internal proof of the output.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier contains one corpus-provenance coordinate, namely $C$, and one package-provenance coordinate, namely $P$. A consumer can read source-channel and distillation provenance only through those rows.

The sibling $\CorpusSupplyDistillationUp$ row supplies finite filtering and distillation provenance, while $\LargeModelInscriptionPointUp$ supplies the carried-output boundary. Neither sibling adds an oracle for corpus-wide truth or host semantic interpretation to this packet.

Since the excluded objects have no coordinate in $A=(O,C,V,R,T,K,H,P,N)$, public export of corpus provenance is exhausted by $C$ and $P$.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:large-model-inscription-audit-tastegate-obligation}

\begin{proof}

Field faithfulness must then be componentwise over the same displayed rows. Using $\mathsf{mk}$-derived fields keeps the Lean carrier aligned with the paper packet and prevents hidden verifier state, corpus truth, or internal ratification from entering through an unlisted coordinate.
