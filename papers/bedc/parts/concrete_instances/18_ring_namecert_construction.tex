\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\RingUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-ring-namecert}

The ring interface \(\RingUp\) has certificate obligations over a carrier with two binary operations \(+\) and \(\cdot\) such that \((C, +)\) satisfies \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-abgroup-namecert} and \((C, \cdot)\) satisfies \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monoid-namecert}, together with left and right distributivity of \(\cdot\) over \(+\). It is not a primitive structure; it is admitted only by composing the additive abelian group and multiplicative monoid certificates with the distributive law.

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[Ring carrier]
\label{def:ring-carrier}
Use a setup with carrier \(C\), classifier, addition, zero, additive inverse, multiplication, one, and ring law fields.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[Ring source specification]
\label{def:ring-source-specification}
The source is an additive \(\AbGroupUp\) and multiplicative \(\MonoidUp\) on the same carrier and classifier.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Ring pattern specification]
\label{def:ring-pattern-specification}
The pattern is expression formation from \(0,1,+,-,\cdot\).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Ring classifier specification]
\label{def:ring-classifier-specification}
The classifier is the shared carrier sameness relation, congruent for both operations.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Ring stability certificate]
\label{def:ring-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item additive abelian-group laws
  \item multiplicative monoid laws
  \item addition and multiplication congruence
  \item left distributivity
  \item right distributivity
  \item zero multiplication laws derived or supplied constructively
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Ring ledger policy]
\label{def:ring-ledger-policy}
Record the shared carrier ledger and the operation tree for ring expressions.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[\(\RingUp\) certificate obligations]
\label{def:ring-certificate-obligations}
The carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data listed above are the obligations required for the interface name \(\RingUp\).
\end{definition}

\paragraph{Primitive boundary.}
\(\RingUp\) is not a BEDC primitive. It is admitted only when those obligations are supplied.
