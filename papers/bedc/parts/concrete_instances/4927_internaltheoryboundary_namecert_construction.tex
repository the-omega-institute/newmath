\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\InternalTheoryBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-internaltheoryboundary-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\InternalTheoryBoundaryUp$ packet turns the own-theory reading of \autoref{ch:bedc-is-its-own-theory} into a concrete finite certificate. It sits beside the kernel-acceptance packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelacceptancewitness-namecert}, the forbidden-host-axiom ancestry boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-forbidden-axiom-ancestry-namecert}, and the proposition-transport boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-propexttransportboundary-namecert}. Those sibling chapters expose accepted-declaration rows, strict proof-ancestry rows, and directed proposition-transport rows; this chapter packages the positive BEDC.MetaCIC commitments that make those rows internal rather than borrowed from a comparison calculus.

\begin{definition}[InternalTheoryBoundary carrier]
\label{def:internal-theory-boundary-carrier}
An $\InternalTheoryBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(G,E,R,Z,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the closed-generator row, naming the finite constructors, binder forms, typed example rows, and accepted declaration rows from which BEDC.MetaCIC objects are generated;
\item $E$ is the literal AST equality row, recording syntactic equality, constructor tag equality, and componentwise binder equality as displayed kernel data rather than as host quotient equality;
\item $R$ is the structural recursor row, exposing only the recursors attached to the closed generators in $G$ and the continuation routes that consume their recursive branches;
\item $Z$ is the strict-purity row, recording the empty source-axiom supply, accepted kernel row, and forbidden-host-axiom refusal surface required before a theorem is read as BEDC-internal;
\item $A$ is the apophatic boundary row, recording that comparison with classical CIC is secondary analysis and not a carrier coordinate, definition source, or external equality oracle for BEDC.MetaCIC;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the generator, AST equality, recursor, purity, boundary, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads generators, literal AST comparison, structural recursion, strict-purity audit, and the apophatic boundary;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over BEDC.MetaCIC, $\KernelAcceptanceWitnessUp$, $\ForbiddenAxiomAncestryUp$, $\PropextTransportBoundaryUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\InternalTheoryBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a host subtheory embedding, proposition equality supplied by $\mathsf{propext}$, quotient stream equality, classical-CIC definitional authority, hidden axiom supply, or external completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[InternalTheoryBoundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:internal-theory-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For every $\InternalTheoryBoundaryUp$ carrier
$$
I=(G,E,R,Z,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\InternalTheoryBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of closed-generator exposure, literal AST equality exposure, structural recursor exposure, strict-purity exposure, apophatic boundary exposure, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet of \autoref{def:internal-theory-boundary-carrier}. The rows $G,E,R,Z,A$ expose the five positive commitments named by \autoref{ch:bedc-is-its-own-theory}.

The transport and replay rows $H$ and $C$ are componentwise. A consumer may move the packet or replay one of its reads, but the endpoints remain the displayed generator, AST equality, recursor, purity, and boundary rows.

The package and naming rows $P,N$ bind the same surface to the local certificate. Since no additional coordinate is present, the NameCert obligations cannot import an external equality oracle, hidden host axiom supply, or classical-CIC definitional authority.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InternalTheoryBoundary closed-generator non-escape]
\label{thm:internal-theory-boundary-closed-generator-nonescape}
Every accepted $\InternalTheoryBoundaryUp$ packet reads BEDC.MetaCIC objects through the closed-generator row $G$, the literal AST equality row $E$, and the structural recursor row $R$. A downstream consumer cannot replace those rows by a host syntax quotient, a classical-CIC embedding, an unlisted constructor, or a recursor not generated by $G$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier definition. The only object-generation row is $G$, the only equality row is $E$, and the only recursion row is $R$.

The continuation routes in $C$ read these rows directly. The transport row $H$ moves them componentwise and therefore preserves the same finite generator inventory, AST comparison, and structural recursion surface.

The excluded objects have no coordinate in $I=(G,E,R,Z,A,H,C,P,N)$. Hence a consumer has no route to a host syntax quotient, a classical-CIC embedding, an unlisted constructor, or an ungenerated recursor.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InternalTheoryBoundary comparison secondary]
\label{thm:internal-theory-boundary-comparison-secondary}
Any comparison between BEDC.MetaCIC and classical CIC that is read from an $\InternalTheoryBoundaryUp$ packet is secondary analysis over the displayed rows $G,E,R,Z,A,H,C,P,N$. It may consume the accepted-declaration surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelacceptancewitness-namecert}, the forbidden-axiom refusal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-forbidden-axiom-ancestry-namecert}, and the directed proposition-transport surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-propexttransportboundary-namecert}, but it cannot define BEDC.MetaCIC by a subtheory relation, proposition extensionality, quotient equality, or external completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the apophatic boundary row $A$. It records the comparison as a boundary reading, not as a generator, equality, recursor, or purity source.

The package row $P$ permits references to the three sibling certificates. Kernel acceptance supplies accepted-declaration rows, forbidden-axiom ancestry supplies strict refusal rows, and proposition-transport supplies directed implication rows in place of proposition equality.

Those sibling surfaces remain finite certificate data. None supplies a coordinate that turns classical CIC into the defining substrate of BEDC.MetaCIC, and none adds a host equality or completeness principle to the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InternalTheoryBoundary vision concretization]
\label{thm:internal-theory-boundary-vision-concretization}
The carrier of \autoref{def:internal-theory-boundary-carrier}, the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:internal-theory-boundary-namecert-obligations}, the closed-generator non-escape boundary of \autoref{thm:internal-theory-boundary-closed-generator-nonescape}, and the secondary-comparison boundary of \autoref{thm:internal-theory-boundary-comparison-secondary} realize the five positive commitments of \autoref{ch:bedc-is-its-own-theory}: closed generators, literal AST equality, structural recursors, zero-axiom strict purity, and apophatic external boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The definition assigns the five commitments to the rows $G,E,R,Z,A$. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ then make those commitments readable as one finite local certificate.

The obligation theorem identifies the complete NameCert surface over those rows. The non-escape theorem prevents the closed generator, literal equality, and recursor clauses from being replaced by host objects.

The secondary-comparison theorem handles the apophatic clause: comparison language may be read as analysis over the packet, but it cannot define the packet. The strict-purity row $Z$ supplies the remaining zero-axiom commitment through the accepted-declaration and forbidden-ancestry surfaces named in $P$.
\end{proof}
