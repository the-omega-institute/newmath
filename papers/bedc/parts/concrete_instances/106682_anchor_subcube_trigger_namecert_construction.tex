\begin{theorem}[Anchor subcube trigger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:anchor-subcube-trigger-namecert-obligations}

$$
A,B \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto G,K \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
No obligation is discharged by a host median-closure oracle or by an unrecorded codon-space equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The rows $A,B,S,R,G,K,H,C,P,N$ are the complete packet, so anchor admission and boundary-tile admission are direct finite reads.

The span row $S$ records the axis-aligned subcube generated by the anchor and the outside boundary observations. The rank row $R$ then reads the displayed span dimension, so the trigger threshold is a certificate row rather than a host calculation over all codon subsets.

The trigger-algebra row $G$ replays the finite median operations that generate the subcube, while the blocker row $K$ prevents reads outside the displayed span. Transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve this same route componentwise.

The provenance and local naming rows $P,N$ source the packet in the anchor-subcube trigger theorem. Since no carrier coordinate names a global codon powerset or an ambient closure engine, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}
