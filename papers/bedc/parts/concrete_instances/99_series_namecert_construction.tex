\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SeriesUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-series-namecert}

The series interface $\SeriesUp$ packages the partial-sum sequence of a $\NameCert_{\SeqUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-seq-namecert}) over an additive carrier together with explicit convergence moduli. It is the real-and-general counterpart of $\NameCert_{\ComplexSeriesUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-series-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:series-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[Series source carrier boundary obligation]
\label{thm:series-source-carrier-boundary-obligation}
The $\SeriesUp$ source carrier is the $\SeqUp$ source carrier together with additive-carrier rows over $\mathsf{BHist}$. A carried row consists of a sequence certificate $\mathcal{S}:\NameCert_{\SeqUp}$, a summand stream $a:\mathsf{BHist}$ accepted by that source, an additive carrier row $A:\mathsf{BHist}$, and a displayed action row saying that every unary summand observation of $a$ lands in $A$. The classifier is the restriction of the $\SeqUp$ stream classifier together with $\hsame$ on the additive carrier row; no ambient real-number carrier, host sequence, or external summability predicate is part of the source carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the $\SeqUp$ certificate to obtain the stream carrier, stream classifier, and unary observation rows. Pair these projections with the displayed additive-carrier row over $\mathsf{BHist}$.

The restriction of the classifier is componentwise: the sequence component is transported by the $\SeqUp$ source classifier, and the additive component is transported by $\hsame$. Repacking those two rows gives exactly the announced carrier boundary without importing a host-level series object.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeriesUp.SeriesSourceCarrierBoundary\_obligation}

\begin{theorem}[Series partial-sum obligation]
\label{thm:series-partial-sum-obligation}
For every carried $\SeriesUp$ source row, the partial-sum row is a unary prefix family $s:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}$ generated from the summand stream by finite $\Cont$-compatible prefixes. The zero prefix is the additive zero row, the successor prefix is obtained by appending one summand observation through the additive operation row, and prefix comparison is transported by the $\SeqUp$ stream classifier and by $\hsame$ on the additive carrier. Thus partial sums are BEDC ledger rows, not hidden evaluations of an external infinite sum.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeriesUp.SeriesPartialSumLedger\_step\_transport}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.SeriesUp.SeriesPartialSum\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Start from the unary index witness supplied by the $\SeqUp$ observation surface. The empty prefix is read as the additive zero row, and the successor case appends the next observed summand using the additive operation row.

Compatibility with $\Cont$ is by induction on the unary prefix length. The base case is the zero prefix, and the successor step composes the previous prefix ledger with the next summand observation. Classifier transport follows by applying the $\SeqUp$ stream classifier to the summand slot and $\hsame$ transport to the additive row.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:series-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Series modulus stability obligation]
\label{thm:series-modulus-stability-obligation}
A convergence modulus for a carried $\SeriesUp$ row is an explicit unary bound $\mu:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{BHist}$ such that, for every unary precision row $k$ and every pair of unary indices beyond $\mu(k)$, the corresponding partial-sum rows are classified by the additive carrier at precision $k$. If two summand streams are classified by the $\SeqUp$ stream classifier, the same displayed modulus row transports to the classified stream by the partial-sum classifier of \autoref{thm:series-partial-sum-obligation}. The modulus is therefore stable under the source classifier and remains a visible BEDC row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeriesUp.SeriesModulusStability\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the modulus as a unary history transformer together with its bound witness for pairs of sufficiently long prefixes. The witness refers only to partial-sum rows and the additive classifier, so it is scoped by \autoref{thm:series-source-carrier-boundary-obligation} and \autoref{thm:series-partial-sum-obligation}.

For classifier stability, transport the summand observations along the $\SeqUp$ stream classifier. The partial-sum obligation transports each finite prefix, and the additive classifier transports the comparison at precision $k$. The same unary bound row $\mu(k)$ can therefore be reused after transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Series ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:series-ledger-exactness-obligation}
The $\SeriesUp$ ledger contains exactly the $\SeqUp$ source certificate, the additive-carrier row, the hidden summand-prefix rows, the partial-sum rows, the explicit modulus rows, the stream-classifier transport rows, and the additive-classifier comparison rows. Coverage says every accepted public series row exposes one such ledger; soundness says every ledger row is produced by those BEDC primitives; completeness says any package with precisely those rows is accepted by the $\SeriesUp$ obligation surface. No comparison with $\ComplexSeriesUp$, no host summability theorem, and no standard bridge is included in this ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Coverage is obtained by projecting an accepted public row into its source certificate, additive-carrier row, summand-prefix display, partial-sum display, and modulus display.

Soundness reads each projected component back through the corresponding BEDC primitive: $\SeqUp$ supplies stream observations, $\Cont$ supplies finite prefix composition, $\hsame$ supplies classifier transport, and the additive carrier supplies zero, addition, and comparison rows.

Completeness is the reverse packing. Given exactly those rows, assemble the carrier boundary, the partial-sum family, and the modulus stability witness, then record them as the public $\SeriesUp$ obligation surface. Since every accepted row is accounted for in this package, the dependency surface is exact.
\end{proof}
