\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedCompactCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-located-compact-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\LocatedCompactCompletionUp$ packet records the finite completion-facing route by which located compact metric data supplies Cauchy completion consumers with explicit finite-net and real-seal rows. It sits after the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the Cauchy-thread surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the finite covering route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}. The certificate names only the located finite-net handoff into the RegSeqRat/Real completion route; it does not choose quotient representatives, host limits, or open-cover compactness data.

\begin{definition}[Located compact completion carrier]
\label{def:located-compact-completion-carrier}
A $\LocatedCompactCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(K,T,D,S,Q,R,F,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, exposing the metric, total-boundedness, and complete-metric interfaces read by compact consumers.
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net row, indexed by a dyadic tolerance ledger from $\DyadicRatCoreUp$.
\item $D$ is the dyadic radius and rounding core that measures the requested net precision.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation-window row used to inspect candidate Cauchy threads.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational readback row attached to the same window schedule.
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the regular-rational readback.
\item $F$ records the finite net centers and coverage witnesses carried from $T$ into the completion route.
\item $M$ is the complete-metric Cauchy handoff row, read before any completion consumer treats the finite-net route as convergent data.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all displayed compact, net, tolerance, stream, rational, real, handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a located-compact completion request through $K,T,D,S,Q,R,F,M,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedCompactCompletionUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompactCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only by these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for arbitrary open covers, quotient completion equality, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located compact completion carrier obligations]
\label{thm:located-compact-completion-carrier}
Every accepted $\LocatedCompactCompletionUp$ carrier $L=(K,T,D,S,Q,R,F,M,H,C,P,N)$ exposes exactly the compact metric source, finite total-boundedness net, dyadic tolerance ledger, stream-window surface, regular-rational readback, Real seal, finite-net handoff, complete-metric handoff, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming rows listed in \autoref{def:located-compact-completion-carrier}. No consumer of the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompactCompletionUp}$ row receives an additional open-cover compactness theorem, quotient completion object, selected limit, host real equality, or Real completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $L$. The compact source is $K$, while the finite-net data is the pair of rows $T,F$ measured by the dyadic tolerance row $D$.

The completion-facing readback is not taken directly from compactness. It passes through the window row $S$, the regular-rational row $Q$, the Real seal $R$, and the complete-metric handoff $M$ before a downstream consumer may read the route as completion data.

Transport and replay preserve the displayed packet only. The rows $H,C,P,N$ keep the same finite $\BHist$ data under $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ bookkeeping, so the excluded open-cover, quotient, selected-limit, host-equality, choice, and completeness objects have no row from which they could be recovered.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located compact completion finite-net seal]
\label{thm:located-compact-completion-finite-net-seal}
Every $\LocatedCompactCompletionUp$ consumer that asks for a completion-facing compactness row must first display the finite-net seal
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp
$$
together with the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ net row and the $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff row. The seal exports a finite list of inspected centers, tolerance-indexed coverage, regular-rational readback, and a terminal Real seal; it exports no open-cover subcover selector, quotient completion equality, selected limit, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier rows in order. The finite-net seal begins with $T$ and $D$, so every radius request is measured by dyadic BEDC data before any stream or real row is inspected.

Next the route enters $S,Q,R$. These rows force the completion-facing consumer to pass through StreamName windows, RegSeqRat regular readback, and the Real seal rather than through a host completion or quotient representative.

Finally $F$ and $M$ bind the finite centers to the complete-metric handoff. Since replay and provenance only repackage those rows, the consumer receives the listed seal and none of the excluded compactness, quotient, selected-limit, host-equality, or completeness principles.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located compact completion Bishop handoff]
\label{thm:located-compact-completion-bishop-handoff}
The Bishop-style handoff from compact finite-net data to completion consumers factors through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} after the \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} rows are displayed. Thus a downstream completion packet may cite $\LocatedCompactCompletionUp$ only as a finite NameCert route over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, finite nets, and the RegSeqRat/Real seal rows, not as a host theorem identifying a quotient completion with a selected real limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the compact metric and total-boundedness rows of \autoref{def:located-compact-completion-carrier}. They supply a located compact source and finite centers, but not a host compactness theorem.

The handoff to completion consumers is then routed through dyadic tolerances, stream observations, and regular rational readback. This is exactly the finite route named by the StreamName and RegSeqRat sibling chapters before the terminal Real seal is read.

The complete-metric handoff row records where Cauchy completion consumers may attach their own local obligations. It does not identify quotient representatives, pick limits, or equate the BEDC real seal with a host real object, so the Bishop-style completion use remains a displayed NameCert route.
\end{proof}
