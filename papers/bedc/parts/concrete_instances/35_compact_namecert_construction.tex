\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\CompactUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert}

The compactness interface \(\CompactUp\) is licensed for subsets of metric spaces from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}. In Bishop-style constructive analysis, compact means located + totally bounded, with both fields expressed via explicit witness functions: locatedness gives, for any point and rational \(\epsilon\), a decision whether the point is within \(\epsilon\) of the subset; total boundedness gives, for any rational \(\epsilon\), a finite \(\epsilon\)-net. The classical Heine-Borel formulation (every open cover has a finite subcover) is not directly licensable in BEDC because it requires LEM in general; the located + totally bounded formulation is constructively equivalent for compact metric spaces and is the form licensed here.

\begin{remark}[Roadmap chapter---formalization pending]
\label{rem:concrete-instances-compact-namecert-roadmap}

\end{remark}

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[Compact carrier]
\label{def:compact-carrier}
Use a metric-space subset predicate equipped with locatedness and total-boundedness witness functions.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[Compact source specification]
\label{def:compact-source-specification}
The source is a certified metric space plus a subset predicate over its carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Compact pattern specification]
\label{def:compact-pattern-specification}
The pattern is compact-subset formation by attaching locatedness and finite-net data for each rational precision.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Compact classifier specification]
\label{def:compact-classifier-specification}
The classifier relates compact witnesses when their membership predicates agree by explicit bidirectional evidence under carrier sameness.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Compact stability certificate]
\label{def:compact-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item membership stability under carrier classifier
  \item locatedness witness validity
  \item total-bounded finite-net validity
  \item monotonicity of precision witnesses where required
  \item stability under compact-witness classifier
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Compact ledger policy]
\label{def:compact-ledger-policy}
Record the subset predicate, locatedness decisions, and finite net ledgers.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete \(\CompactUp\) naming certificate]
\label{thm:concrete-compact-namecert}
The located-and-totally-bounded tuple forms a valid NameCert for \(\CompactUp(X)\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[\(\CompactUp\) is licensed, not primitive]
\label{cor:compact-licensed-not-primitive}
\(\CompactUp\) is licensed by constructive metric compactness data.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Zero-axiom encoding note]
\label{rem:compact-zero-axiom-note}
Replaces open-cover compactness and LEM with locatedness plus explicit finite nets.
\end{remark}
