\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DistanceToSetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-distance-to-set-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on located subsets in metric spaces, the $\DistanceToSetUp$ packet records the finite route by which a point-to-set distance request is read through displayed located-radius rows and closed-ball witnesses. It sits after the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the located-set surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedset-namecert}, the metric closed-ball boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricclosedball-namecert}, the located closed-ball handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-closed-ball-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names distance-to-set as finite BEDC data; it is not a host infimum, not a selected nearest point, not quotient real equality, and not a countable-choice construction.

\begin{definition}[Distance-to-set carrier]
\label{def:distance-to-set-carrier}
A $\DistanceToSetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(X,A,x,r,B,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\MetricUp$ source row over which point and subset reads are interpreted.
\item $A$ is the $\LocatedSetUp$ row naming the subset by finite located-radius observations.
\item $x$ is the displayed point row in the metric source.
\item $r$ is the dyadic radius ledger recording the inspected tolerance windows.
\item $B$ is the $\MetricClosedBallUp$ witness row attached to the point and radius ledger.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row for the exported distance name, read only after the located-set and closed-ball rows are displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, located set, point row, dyadic ledger, closed-ball witness, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the metric source through located-set exposure, point inspection, dyadic-radius accounting, closed-ball handoff, and the Real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DistanceToSetUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\LocatedSetUp$, $\MetricClosedBallUp$, $\LocatedClosedBallUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DistanceToSetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,A,x,r,B,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host infimum over all points of $A$, a selected nearest point, quotient real equality, countable choice, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Distance-to-set NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:distance-to-set-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DistanceToSetUp$ carrier
$$
D=(X,A,x,r,B,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\DistanceToSetUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the metric source, located set, point row, dyadic radius ledger, closed-ball witness, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item located-set exposure, requiring every subset read to pass through the $\LocatedSetUp$ row $A$ before a distance row is exported;
\item dyadic-radius monotonicity, requiring the tolerance ledger $r$ to preserve the displayed radius order used by the located-set and closed-ball rows;
\item closed-ball handoff, requiring each ball membership or exclusion read to pass through the $\MetricClosedBallUp$ witness $B$ and the located closed-ball sibling surface before the Real seal is read;
\item Real seal non-escape, requiring $R$ to seal only the displayed distance name and not a host infimum or quotient real;
\item componentwise transport, displayed replay, package provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming for exactly the displayed packet.
\end{enumerate}
No DistanceToSet obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:distance-to-set-carrier}. The metric source is $X$, the located subset is $A$, the inspected point is $x$, and the finite tolerance information is held by $r$.

The closed-ball witness $B$ is read only after those rows are displayed. Its radius requests are therefore attached to the same metric source, located-set row, point row, and dyadic ledger.

The Real seal $R$ comes after the located-radius and closed-ball rows. Transport $H$ and replay $C$ preserve the same order, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming.

Since no coordinate stores a host infimum, a nearest-point selector, quotient real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness, those objects cannot become local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Distance-to-set located-radius handoff]
\label{thm:distance-to-set-located-radius-handoff}
Every consumer of an accepted $\DistanceToSetUp$ packet reads distance-to-set data only through the displayed located-radius row $A$, the dyadic radius ledger $r$, the closed-ball witness $B$, and the Real seal $R$, transported by $H$ and replayed by $C$. The consumer cannot bypass these rows by reading a host infimum, a selected nearest point, quotient real equality, countable choice, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier of \autoref{def:distance-to-set-carrier}. The only subset coordinate is the located-set row $A$, and the only radius coordinate is the dyadic ledger $r$.

The closed-ball witness $B$ supplies the displayed ball boundary before the Real seal $R$ is exposed. Thus a distance consumer reaches the exported real name only through the located-radius and closed-ball rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:distance-to-set-namecert-obligations}. The transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows preserve the same finite packet and add no host infimum, nearest-point selector, quotient real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness data.
\end{proof}
