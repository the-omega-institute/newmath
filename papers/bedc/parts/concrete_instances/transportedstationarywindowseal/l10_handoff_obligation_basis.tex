\begin{theorem}[Transported stationary window-seal L10 handoff obligation basis]
\label{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-l10-handoff-obligation-basis}
Let $T=(R,S,G,W,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\TransportedStationaryWindowSealUp$ packet. Its L10 handoff basis for Real and Completion consumers is the finite route
$$
\begin{aligned}
  \RatUp
  \longrightarrow
  \StreamNameUp
  \longrightarrow
  \RegSeqRatUp
  \longrightarrow
  \DyadicRatCoreUp
  \longrightarrow
  \FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp
  \longrightarrow
  \RealUp ,
\end{aligned}
$$
read through the visible rows $R,S,G,W,E,H,C,P,N$ and through the sibling surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitewindowenvelope-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tailcofinalityschedule-namecert}. The basis is an obligation route for later checked carriers; it is not a host completion theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the local obligation theorem \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-namecert-obligations}. It exposes the accepted packet only through the carrier rows $R,S,G,W,E,H,C,P,N$, with componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming.

Next use \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-real-factorization}. The $\RealUp$ read cannot appear before the regular-rational seal and finite envelope rows; it must pass through the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ source and the same $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ row already displayed by the packet.

Finally attach the completion-facing side by \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-completion-consumer-boundary} and \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-tail-cofinality-handoff}. These place every completion consumer after the transported $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ source, after the dyadic and finite-window ledger rows, and before any off-packet real-completeness datum could be read. The result is exactly the stated finite handoff basis.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Transported stationary window-seal dyadic window ledger totality]
\label{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-dyadic-window-ledger-totality}
For an accepted $\TransportedStationaryWindowSealUp$ packet $T=(R,S,G,W,E,H,C,P,N)$, the dyadic window ledger available to the Real and Completion route is total only through the displayed rows $R,S,G,W,E,H,C,P,N$ and the sibling certificates for $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$. In particular, any accepted dyadic tolerance, finite-window, or regular-readback consumer must read the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert} before it can use $W$ or $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the accepted packet. The obligation theorem \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-namecert-obligations} lists exactly the root rows $R,S,G,W,E,H,C,P,N$ and gives no separate dyadic or window carrier outside them.

The finite window and regular seal are then fixed by \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-real-factorization} and by the sibling StreamName and RegSeqRat chapters. The dyadic ledger is read only as the tolerance and observation surface compatible with those same rows, through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}.

The route boundary \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-completion-consumer-boundary} and the schedule handoff \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-tail-cofinality-handoff} rule out any second window budget, second regular seal, or detached dyadic ledger. Hence the dyadic window ledger is total exactly over the displayed packet and the named sibling certificates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Transported stationary window-seal Real RegSeq consumer exactness]
\label{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-real-regseq-consumer-exactness}
Let $T=(R,S,G,W,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\TransportedStationaryWindowSealUp$ packet. Every $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer of $T$ factors through the finite route from transported $\RatUp$ through $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$, and $\RealUp$ displayed in \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-l10-handoff-obligation-basis}. No such consumer can import quotient streams, host equality, selected limits, choice, hidden tail oracles, or real-completeness data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the Real-facing side. By \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-real-factorization}, every $\RealUp$ read of $E$ factors through the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal row and the finite envelope $W$, so there is no direct host-real read.

Now use \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-completion-consumer-boundary}. It forces every $\CompletionUp$ consumer back to the displayed root packet rather than to quotient streams, host equality, selected limits, choice, or ambient real completeness.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:transported-stationary-window-seal-tail-cofinality-handoff}. The only tail information available to the consumer is the scheduled sibling route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tailcofinalityschedule-namecert}; no hidden tail oracle is introduced. Together with the StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, FiniteWindowEnvelope, and Real sibling chapters, this gives the exact finite consumer factorization claimed above.
\end{proof}
