The MetricSpace compact-continuity source lock is the finite BEDC packet read before the compact-uniform-continuity construction of \autoref{ch:visions-compact-uniform-continuity}. It joins the located distance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the compact finite-net rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the continuous pointwise-modulus rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, and the uniform-modulus consumer boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}. The packet is a source lock: a consumer sees the source rows together or receives no uniform-modulus precision row.

\falsifiablePrediction{If the packet is atomic, no consumer can read a uniform-modulus precision row before the MetricSpaceUp distance row, CompactMetricUp finite-net row, ContinuousMapUp pointwise-modulus row, and RatUp finite-fold row are all displayed.}
\independenceWitness{metric, compactmetric, continuousmap, uniformmodulus, finitenetminimumfold: this packet is not bijective to any one sibling because it carries all four source-lock rows and a consumer non-escape row simultaneously, while each sibling omits at least one required coordinate.}

\begin{definition}[MetricSpace compact-continuity source lock]
\label{def:metricspace-compact-continuity-source-lock}
A MetricSpace compact-continuity source-lock carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(M,K,F,P,R,H,C,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ source row, including the located distance witness and classifier-stable metric comparison used by the compact and continuous inputs.
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ finite-net row, carrying the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of centers and the coverage ledger for the same metric source.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ pointwise-modulus row, carrying the continuous graph, target metric boundary, and local modulus rows over the source metric $M$.
\item $P$ is the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ center-assignment row that aligns bundled probes from $K$ with the pointwise-modulus centers consumed by $F$.
\item $R$ is the $\RatUp$ lower-fold row, carrying the finite lower-bound ledger over the bundled positive rational radius histories before any uniform precision row is exposed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $M,K,F,P,R$, the consumer boundary, and the local naming row.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that read distance, finite-net, pointwise-modulus, probe-assignment, and rational-fold rows before the uniform-modulus consumer route is available.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert$ row for the source-lock packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is finite and source-locked. It contains no host compactness theorem, no open-cover selector, no choice-selected modulus, no quotient metric equality, and no ambient completeness row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricSpace compact-continuity source-lock obligations]
\label{thm:metricspace-compact-continuity-source-lock-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:metricspace-compact-continuity-source-lock}, the local $\NameCert$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure: every accepted packet exposes exactly the rows $L=(M,K,F,P,R,H,C,N)$;
\item metric source compatibility: the finite-net, pointwise-modulus, and rational-fold rows are read over the same $\MetricSpaceUp$ source row $M$;
\item compact-continuous alignment: the probe centers supplied by $K$ and $P$ are the centers at which the pointwise-modulus rows of $F$ are consumed;
\item finite-fold precision: the $\RatUp$ row $R$ is the only lower-bound fold available to the uniform-modulus consumer;
\item consumer non-escape: every uniform-modulus precision read factors through $M,K,F,P,R,H,C,N$ and receives no host compactness, choice-selected modulus, quotient equality, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:metricspace-compact-continuity-source-lock}. The rows $M,K,F,P,R,H,C,N$ are coordinates of one finite $\BHist$ packet, so no consumer can read a hidden source row outside that tuple.

Metric source compatibility is enforced by the shared $M$ coordinate. The compact finite-net row $K$, continuous pointwise-modulus row $F$, probe center assignment $P$, and rational lower fold $R$ are all stored as routes over that displayed source metric.

Compact-continuous alignment is the role of $P$. A bundled center from $K$ becomes a pointwise-modulus center for $F$ only through the displayed probe-assignment row, and the $\hsame$ transports in $H$ replay the same alignment after classifier comparison.

Finite-fold precision is supplied by $R$. The continuation rows in $C$ route a tolerance request through the displayed finite bundle and then through the rational lower-bound fold before a precision row reaches the uniform-modulus consumer.

Finally, consumer non-escape follows from the package inventory. Since $L$ has no coordinate for host compactness, open-cover choice, quotient metric equality, or ambient completeness, none of those principles can be read from the source-lock packet. The later Lean carrier for this same packet must provide $\mathsf{Nontrivial}$ and $\mathsf{FieldFaithful}$ instances in `TasteGate.lean` for the same source carrier, so the formal consumer cannot switch to a different carrier while preserving the source-lock rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricSpace source-lock row exposure]
\label{thm:metricspace-source-lock-row-exposure}
Every accepted MetricSpace compact-continuity source-lock packet exposes exactly the displayed rows
$$
M,K,F,P,R,H,C,N
$$
of \autoref{def:metricspace-compact-continuity-source-lock}. Its public surface contains the $\MetricUp$ source row, the $\CompactMetricUp$ finite-net row, the $\ContinuousMapUp$ pointwise-modulus row, the $\RatUp$ lower-fold row, the $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer boundary, the $\BHist$ carrier ledger, the componentwise $\hsame$ transports, the displayed $\Cont$ routes, and the local $\NameCert$ row. It contains no hidden host compactness, quotient metric equality, open-cover selector, choice modulus, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier tuple in \autoref{def:metricspace-compact-continuity-source-lock}. The only rows named by the packet are $M,K,F,P,R,H,C,N$, and the row-exposure obligation in \autoref{thm:metricspace-compact-continuity-source-lock-obligations} states that every accepted packet displays exactly that tuple.

The sibling anchors determine the interpretation of those rows. The $\MetricUp$ row is the metric source, $\CompactMetricUp$ supplies finite-net data, $\ContinuousMapUp$ supplies pointwise-modulus data, $\RatUp$ supplies the finite lower fold, and $\UniformModulusUp$ is reached only as the downstream consumer boundary.

The excluded principles are absent by carrier inventory. Since no coordinate of $L$ is a host compactness witness, quotient equality proof, open-cover selector, choice-selected modulus, or ambient completeness row, a public read from the packet cannot expose one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricSpace source-lock center alignment]
\label{thm:metricspace-source-lock-center-alignment}
In every accepted MetricSpace compact-continuity source-lock packet, the $K$ finite-net centers, the $P$ $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ assignment rows, and the $F$ pointwise-modulus rows are read over the same $\MetricSpaceUp$ source row $M$. Transport between the $\CompactMetricUp$ finite-net surface, the $\ContinuousMapUp$ pointwise surface, the $\RatUp$ lower-fold surface, and the $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer boundary is permitted only through the displayed $H$ componentwise $\hsame$ rows and the displayed $C$ $\Cont$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the metric source compatibility and compact-continuous alignment clauses of \autoref{thm:metricspace-compact-continuity-source-lock-obligations}. They bind $K,F,P,R$ to the single source coordinate $M$ in \autoref{def:metricspace-compact-continuity-source-lock}.

The center route is therefore unique inside the packet. A center exposed by the $\CompactMetricUp$ row $K$ reaches a $\ContinuousMapUp$ pointwise-modulus row in $F$ only through the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ assignment row $P$.

Transport does not add a second source. The displayed $H$ rows replay componentwise $\hsame$ comparison, and the displayed $C$ rows replay the $\Cont$ routes from $\MetricUp$ through $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\RatUp$, and toward $\UniformModulusUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricSpace source-lock uniform-modulus nonescape]
\label{thm:metricspace-source-lock-uniform-modulus-nonescape}
Any uniform-modulus precision read from an accepted MetricSpace compact-continuity source-lock packet factors through the displayed rows
$$
M,K,F,P,R,H,C,N.
$$
In particular, the $\RatUp$ lower-fold row $R$ must be consumed before any $\UniformModulusUp$ precision row is public, and no precision read can bypass the $\MetricUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows displayed by the source lock.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the finite-fold precision and consumer non-escape clauses of \autoref{thm:metricspace-compact-continuity-source-lock-obligations}. They state that a tolerance-to-precision read is routed through the rational lower-bound fold and through the exposed packet inventory.

The factorization is exactly the carrier route in \autoref{def:metricspace-compact-continuity-source-lock}. The source $\MetricUp$ row $M$, finite-net $\CompactMetricUp$ row $K$, pointwise $\ContinuousMapUp$ row $F$, probe-assignment row $P$, and $\RatUp$ lower-fold row $R$ are read before the continuation boundary reaches $\UniformModulusUp$.

Thus $R$ is not an optional scalar witness. It is the displayed finite lower-fold row that the continuation rows $C$ consume before the precision row becomes public, with $H$ and $N$ preserving the same source-lock identity.
\end{proof}
