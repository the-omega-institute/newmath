\section{Compact positive functional carrier}
\label{sec:riesz-markov-compact-positive-functional-carrier}

\begin{definition}[RieszMarkov compact positive functional carrier]
\label{def:riesz-markov-compact-positive-functional-carrier}
A $\RieszMarkovUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
\mathsf{RieszMarkov}(K,F,L,M,I,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $K$ is the compact source row, read through $\CompactUp$ over the inherited $\RealUp$ scalar boundary;
  \item $F$ is the continuous real-valued observable row, read through $\ContinuousMapUp$ with codomain $\RealUp$;
  \item $L$ is the positive functional ledger, recording linear scalar reads and positivity reads on the displayed observable rows;
  \item $M$ is the representing $\RadonMeasureUp$ row over the same compact source;
  \item $I$ is the integral-readback row comparing each displayed functional value with the corresponding integral against $M$;
  \item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport ledger for $K,F,L,M,I,C,P,N$;
  \item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay ledger for compact source, observable, functional, representing measure, and integral-readback routes;
  \item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RieszMarkovUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CompactUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\RadonMeasureUp$, $\RieszRepresentationUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
  \item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RieszMarkovUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $K,F,L,M,I,H,C,P,N$. It does not identify host functions, host measures, arbitrary sigma-algebras, quotient measure classes, selected representing measures, or ambient functional extensionality.
\end{definition}

\section{Certificate obligations}
\label{sec:riesz-markov-namecert-obligations}

\begin{theorem}[RieszMarkov NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:riesz-markov-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RieszMarkovUp$ carrier
$$
R=\mathsf{RieszMarkov}(K,F,L,M,I,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RieszMarkovUp}$ surface consists exactly of compact-source admission through $K$, real-valued continuity admission through $F$, positive-functional ledger visibility through $L$, representing Radon row visibility through $M$, integral-readback exactness through $I$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

The certificate refuses arbitrary choice of representing measures, host sigma-algebras beyond the displayed $\RadonMeasureUp$ rows, quotient equality of measures, host equality of functionals, and ambient functional extensionality. A consumer may use the representation only by reading the finite rows of $R$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:riesz-markov-compact-positive-functional-carrier}. The compact source and continuous observable are fixed by $K$ and $F$, so the functional ledger $L$ is read on displayed observables rather than on a host function space.

The representing object is the displayed Radon row $M$. The readback row $I$ is the only route from a functional value to an integral value, and it is attached to the same compact source and observable rows.

Transport and replay are componentwise through $H$ and $C$, while $P$ and $N$ source and name the route. Since no carrier coordinate stores a selector for representing measures, a larger host sigma-algebra, a quotient measure equality, host functional equality, or functional extensionality, the stated rows exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
