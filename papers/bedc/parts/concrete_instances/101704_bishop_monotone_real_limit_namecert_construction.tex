\begin{definition}[Bishop monotone real limit carrier]
\label{def:bishop-monotone-real-limit-carrier}
A $\BishopMonotoneRealLimitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(S,B,M,L,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\BoundedRealSequenceUp$ source row, carrying the inspected real sequence windows, regular rational readbacks, real-name seals, and boundedness surface.
\item $B$ is the bound ledger, recording the finite upper-bound and lower-window data read from $S$ by a consumer requesting a monotone convergence witness.
\item $M$ is the monotone comparison data, displaying the finite comparison rows that certify the inspected windows are nondecreasing along the requested index route.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedSupremumUp$ row, reached from the same bound ledger and comparison data and read only as a located upper-bound and lower-approximation surface.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and Cauchy-modulus ledger, recording each requested dyadic precision, the monotone tail window selected for that precision, and the comparison budget used to trap later terms.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the stream windows, monotone comparisons, located-supremum approximants, and dyadic tolerance ledger.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal descent row reached only after $S,B,M,L,D,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the sequence source, bound ledger, monotone comparisons, located supremum, dyadic ledger, regular readback, real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through the bounded source, bound ledger, monotone row, located supremum, dyadic ledger, regular readback, and real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopMonotoneRealLimitUp$, $\BoundedRealSequenceUp$, $\LocatedSupremumUp$, $\MonotoneCauchyUp$, $\BolzanoCauchyUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopMonotoneRealLimitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these rows. It has no coordinate for ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, real trichotomy, selected subsequences, countable choice, quotient stream equality, open-cover compactness, or a host-level supremum operator.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop monotone real limit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-monotone-real-limit-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopMonotoneRealLimitUp$ carrier
$$
T=(S,B,M,L,D,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BishopMonotoneRealLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed bounded sequence source, bound ledger, monotone comparison data, located supremum, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular readback, real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming row;
\item monotone bounded-source routing, requiring every convergence read to begin with $S,B,M$ before any located supremum or real-seal row is exposed;
\item located-supremum routing, requiring lower approximants and upper-bound reads to pass through $L$ with the same bound ledger and comparison data;
\item Cauchy-route routing, requiring each dyadic precision request to pass through $D$ and then through $R,E$ before the limit-facing read is available;
\item structural stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to transport and replay only the displayed packet, with provenance supplied by $P$ and local naming supplied by $N$.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishop-monotone-real-limit-carrier}. The displayed coordinates are exactly the bounded sequence source, bound ledger, monotone comparisons, located supremum, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular readback, real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name.

The first analytic route is forced by the order of the packet. A convergence consumer must read the bounded sequence surface and the monotone comparison data before it can inspect the located supremum row.

The second route is the Cauchy handoff. A precision request enters the dyadic ledger, obtains a finite monotone tail window, passes through the regular rational readback, and reaches the real seal only after those rows have been displayed.

The structural rows exhaust the remaining obligations. The transports and continuations can preserve and replay only the displayed packet, while the provenance and local name add no analytic coordinate. Hence there is no further obligation outside the listed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop monotone real limit Cauchy route]
\label{thm:bishop-monotone-real-limit-cauchy-route}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read of an accepted $\BishopMonotoneRealLimitUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
S \longmapsto B \longmapsto M \longmapsto L \longmapsto D \longmapsto R \longmapsto E
$$
and then through the stored $\hsame$ transport $H$, $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. The route is the bounded monotone sequence read through located supremum data and the Bolzano-Cauchy Cauchy criterion; it exports no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, trichotomy row, selected subsequence, countable-choice operator, quotient stream equality, or open-cover compactness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a real-facing read. By carrier admission, the only sequence source is $S$, and the only bound and monotonicity data are the displayed rows $B$ and $M$.

Read the located supremum row next. The row $L$ supplies the upper-bound and lower-approximation surface already available in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedsupremum-namecert}, but it is attached here to the same bounded monotone source.

The dyadic ledger $D$ then converts a requested precision into a finite monotone tail window. The regular readback $R$ and real seal $E$ consume that same window, matching the Cauchy-route discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bolzano-cauchy-namecert}.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the route. Since the carrier contains none of the excluded analytic objects, the real-facing read cannot leave the displayed path.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop monotone real limit nonescape]
\label{thm:bishop-monotone-real-limit-nonescape}
Every public read of an accepted $\BishopMonotoneRealLimitUp$ carrier is exhausted by the rows $S,B,M,L,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$. In particular, the packet cannot export ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, real trichotomy, a selected subsequence, countable choice, quotient stream equality, open-cover compactness, or a host-level supremum operation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. The analytic rows are the bounded sequence source, the bound ledger, the monotone comparison data, the located supremum row, the dyadic Cauchy ledger, the regular readback, and the real seal.

The only remaining coordinates are structural. Transport $H$ preserves componentwise rows, continuation $C$ replays displayed reads, provenance $P$ records the sibling sources, and $N$ names the local certificate.

No coordinate remains for completeness, trichotomy, selected subsequences, countable choice, quotient equality, open-cover compactness, or a host-level supremum operator. Therefore every public read is confined to the finite packet.
\end{proof}
