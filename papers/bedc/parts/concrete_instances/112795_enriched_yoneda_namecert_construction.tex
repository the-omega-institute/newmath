\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EnrichedYonedaUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-enriched-yoneda-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelly's enriched category theory and Mac Lane's categorical account of
representability, the $\EnrichedYonedaUp$ packet records the enriched
functor-of-points surface as finite NameCert data. It sits over the category
carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-category-namecert}, the ordinary
functor and natural-transformation rows of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-functor-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nattrans-namecert}, the enriched hom-object
surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-enrichedcat-namecert}, and the
monoidal tensor boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monoidalcat-namecert}. The packet names the
finite route by which enriched representables, enriched naturality, and
monoidal coherence can be replayed without importing a host enriched presheaf
category, ambient ends, quotient functor categories, or external universality.

\begin{definition}[EnrichedYoneda seed carrier]
\label{def:enriched-yoneda-seed-carrier}
An $\EnrichedYonedaUp$ seed carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
Y=(C,V,O,H,F,S,U,Q,T,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is the consumed $\CategoryUp$ object-and-hom boundary for the
  underlying ordinary category read by downstream categorical consumers;
\item $V$ is the consumed $\EnrichedCatUp$ row, including the object boundary,
  enriched hom-object rows, identity rows, and enriched composition rows;
\item $O$ records the chosen object row whose enriched representable is being
  named;
\item $H$ is the enriched hom-object row assigning to each displayed object
  $x$ the hom-object $V(x,O)$ in the consumed monoidal category;
\item $F$ is the enriched functor-of-points row, read as the object-indexed
  assignment $x \mapsto V(x,O)$ together with its displayed action on enriched
  hom rows;
\item $S$ records enriched naturality-square rows comparing the action of $F$
  against enriched composition in $V$;
\item $U$ records monoidal-unit rows and the coherence rows needed to read
  identities, associators, left unitors, and right unitors through the consumed
  $\MonoidalCatUp$ boundary;
\item $Q$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the ordinary category,
  enriched category, chosen object, hom-object, functor-of-points, naturality,
  monoidal coherence, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the chosen object through
  enriched hom-object inspection, functor-of-points readback, naturality-square
  comparison, and monoidal coherence;
\item $R$ is the finite readback ledger by which ordinary $\FunctorUp$ and
  $\NatTransUp$ consumers may read the enriched rows without receiving host
  functor-category equality;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EnrichedYonedaUp$, $\CategoryUp$,
  $\FunctorUp$, $\NatTransUp$, $\EnrichedCatUp$, $\MonoidalCatUp$,
  $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EnrichedYonedaUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier relates two accepted packets exactly when these rows transport
componentwise through $Q$ and replay through the same displayed routes in $T$.
It does not contain a coordinate for a host enriched presheaf category, external
end calculus, quotient natural-transformation equality, global enriched
embedding theorem, or ambient universal property.
\end{definition}
