$$

\item $A$ is the $\ApartnessRealUp$ evidence row comparing $X$ with the displayed zero real-name boundary.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite observation window opened for the sign request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback visible on that window.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and endpoint ledger used to compare the readback with zero at the requested precision.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal exposed only after $A,W,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $L$ records the located comparison row: negative-side evidence, positive-side evidence, or a bounded zero-adjacent refusal at the current dyadic tolerance.

\begin{theorem}[RealApartnessSign obligation surface]
\label{thm:real-apartness-sign-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealApartnessSignUp$ carrier $S=(X,A,W,R,D,E,L,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealApartnessSignUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the inspected real row, apartness row, finite window, regular readback, dyadic ledger, real seal, located comparison row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item apartness anchoring, requiring the sign read to inspect $X$ against the zero boundary through the displayed $\ApartnessRealUp$ row $A$ before a positive or negative side is exposed;
\item finite-window readback, requiring every sign request to pass through $W$, $R$, and $D$ before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$ can be consumed;
\item located comparison exactness, requiring $L$ to record exactly the displayed negative-side evidence, positive-side evidence, or zero-adjacent refusal available at the current dyadic tolerance;
\item componentwise stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to transport and replay only the displayed rows $X,A,W,R,D,E,L,P,N$;
\item ledger exhaustion, requiring the packet to export no trichotomy decision, total order, quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{enumerate}
No local sign obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-apartness-sign-carrier}. The accepted tuple displays exactly the inspected real row, apartness row, finite window, regular rational readback, dyadic ledger, terminal seal, located comparison row, and structural support.

Apartness anchoring is forced by the position of $A$. A positive-side or negative-side read cannot be named before the comparison with the zero boundary is visible as $\ApartnessRealUp$ evidence.

The finite-window readback discipline is the role of $W,R,D,E$. The sign request opens a finite StreamName window, reads it as a regular rational row, compares it through the dyadic ledger, and only then exposes the Real seal.

Located comparison exactness follows from $L$. The row records the finite sign information available at the requested tolerance, including the zero-adjacent refusal case when neither side has been witnessed.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name only the displayed packet. Since the carrier contains no further coordinate, it cannot export trichotomy, total order, quotient equality, host equality, choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealApartnessSign located comparison route]
\label{thm:real-apartness-sign-located-comparison-route}
Every located comparison read exported by an accepted $\RealApartnessSignUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
X,A,W,R,D \longmapsto L \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus a Real or Completion consumer can read sign information only after the apartness-to-zero row, finite StreamName window, regular rational readback, and dyadic tolerance ledger have all been displayed. The route may expose positive-side evidence, negative-side evidence, or a zero-adjacent refusal at the current tolerance, but it exports no total sign decision for arbitrary real names.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:real-apartness-sign-carrier}. Projection gives the single displayed route $X,A,W,R,D,E,L,H,C,P,N$.

Read the apartness row first. The sign request is anchored by $A$, so any positive or negative side must be evidence against the zero boundary rather than a host-level order claim.

Next replay the finite observation path through $W,R,D$. The StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, and DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger determine which finite comparison row is available.

The located comparison row $L$ is then consumed before the terminal seal $E$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. Since $L$ has only the three displayed alternatives, the consumer receives finite sign information without a global trichotomy principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealApartnessSign real seal boundary]
\label{thm:real-apartness-sign-real-seal}
