It depends on $\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-s1-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\HomotopyUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-homotopy-namecert}).

\begin{theorem}[KnotUp projection stability obligation]\label{thm:knot-projection-stability-obligation}
Every carried knot projection row over $\mathsf{BHist}$ is stable under $\hsame$ transport of the displayed $\SOneUp$ source row and the ambient $\HomotopyUp$ row. The transported row has the same visible knot packet, and its hidden projection data are carried only through the $\Cont$ ledger and the two cited NameCert source rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Read the carried projection row as a $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet whose visible boundary consists of the $\SOneUp$ source row, the ambient $\HomotopyUp$ row, and the finite projection ledger. The BEDC source field is therefore $\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ together with $\NameCert_{\HomotopyUp}$; the carrier field is the projection packet; the classifier field is the row displayed by the packet; and the stability field is the given $\hsame$ transport.

Transport first along the $\SOneUp$ source row and then along the ambient $\HomotopyUp$ row. The $\Cont$ ledger carries exactly the hidden projection witnesses, so no additional diagram equality or ambient quotient is introduced.

The ledger field is the transported finite projection ledger, the core theorem list is this stability row together with the two dependency rows, and exactness says that the accepted packet is precisely the transported packet. The standard bridge field is none, and the status field is a paper obligation for $\NameCert_{\KnotUp}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KnotUp Reidemeister-move classifier obligation]\label{thm:knot-reidemeister-move-classifier-obligation}
The $\KnotUp$ classifier is generated by finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ ledgers for Reidemeister I, II, and III moves, together with endpoint $\hsame$ transport. Reflexive, symmetric, and transitive classifier rows are supplied respectively by the empty move ledger, the reversed move ledger, and concatenation of finite move ledgers.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The BEDC source field is the projection carrier from \autoref{thm:knot-projection-stability-obligation} with endpoints classified by $\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ and ambient rows classified by $\NameCert_{\HomotopyUp}$. The carrier field is a finite move ledger whose entries are tagged Reidemeister I, Reidemeister II, or Reidemeister III probes inside $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$.

For the classifier field, accept a row exactly when such a finite ledger connects the two visible projection endpoints after endpoint $\hsame$ transport. Stability follows by transporting the endpoints first and then reusing the same tagged move probes over the transported boundary.

The ledger field records the tagged move list. The core theorem list consists of the empty-ledger row, the reverse-ledger row, and the concatenation row, giving reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity of the classifier. Exactness states that every accepted classifier row has one of these finite ledger presentations; the standard bridge field is none, and the status field is a paper obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KnotUp Homotopy source obligation]\label{thm:knot-homotopy-source-obligation}
Every ambient-isotopy row consumed by the $\KnotUp$ certificate factors through the displayed $\NameCert_{\HomotopyUp}$ source, and it consumes only endpoints supplied by the $\SOneUp$ carrier boundary.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The BEDC source field is the pair of source certificates $\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\HomotopyUp}$. The carrier field is the knot packet whose source boundary exposes the circle endpoints and whose ambient boundary exposes the homotopy row.

Factor the ambient row through $\NameCert_{\HomotopyUp}$ before it is used by any Reidemeister ledger. The endpoint checks are then read from the $\SOneUp$ carrier boundary, so the homotopy row cannot introduce a new endpoint source or an untracked ambient endpoint.

The classifier and stability fields are inherited from the factorized homotopy row and the endpoint $\hsame$ transport. The ledger field records only the homotopy source witness and the endpoint boundary witnesses; exactness says that these are the only inputs consumed by the ambient-isotopy row. The standard bridge field is none, and the status field is a paper obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KnotUp SOne boundary ledger]\label{thm:knot-sone-boundary-ledger}
Every diagram endpoint, arc endpoint, and crossing endpoint used by a $\KnotUp$ packet has an $\SOneUp$ boundary source. The boundary ledger records the hidden projection and crossing data while exposing only the $\SOneUp$ endpoint rows and the ambient $\HomotopyUp$ row.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The BEDC source field is the displayed $\SOneUp$ boundary together with the ambient $\HomotopyUp$ boundary. The carrier field is the knot packet with diagram endpoints, arc endpoints, crossing endpoints, hidden projection data, and hidden crossing data.

For each endpoint kind, choose its boundary witness from the $\SOneUp$ carrier row. The classifier field then reads endpoint compatibility through those source witnesses, while the stability field transports them by $\hsame$ and leaves hidden projection and crossing witnesses inside the finite ledger.

The ledger field is the list of endpoint witnesses plus the hidden projection and crossing records. The core theorem list is the diagram-endpoint, arc-endpoint, and crossing-endpoint boundary rows. Exactness states that no endpoint used by the packet is outside the $\SOneUp$ source boundary; the standard bridge field is none, and the status field is a paper obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KnotUp ambient-isotopy ledger exactness]\label{thm:knot-ambient-isotopy-ledger-exactness}
If two knot packets are related by the $\KnotUp$ classifier, then they have compatible visible $\SOneUp$ endpoints and compatible visible $\HomotopyUp$ endpoints, and a finite Reidemeister ledger over those same visible endpoints supplies the accepted classifier row.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The BEDC source field is the pair of endpoint sources from $\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\HomotopyUp}$. The carrier field is the two knot packets together with their displayed boundaries, and the classifier field is the accepted finite Reidemeister ledger from \autoref{thm:knot-reidemeister-move-classifier-obligation}.

Project the accepted classifier row to its endpoint boundary. The $\SOneUp$ projection gives compatible circle endpoints, and the $\HomotopyUp$ projection gives compatible ambient endpoints; both projections use the same visible endpoints as the finite Reidemeister ledger.

The stability field is endpoint $\hsame$ transport, and the ledger field is the shared finite Reidemeister ledger. The core theorem list is projection stability, the Reidemeister classifier, homotopy source factorization, and the $\SOneUp$ boundary ledger. Exactness says that the accepted classifier row contains exactly those visible endpoints and that ledger; the standard bridge field is none, and the status field is a paper obligation.
\end{proof}
