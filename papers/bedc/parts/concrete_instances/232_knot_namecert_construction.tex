\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\KnotUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-knot-namecert}

The knot interface $\KnotUp$ packages an embedding of $S^1$ into $S^3$ up to ambient isotopy, certified by diagrammatic Reidemeister-move equivalence. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-s1-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\HomotopyUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-homotopy-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:knot-carrier}

\begin{definition}[KnotUp diagram source carrier]
\label{def:knot-diagram-source-carrier}
A $\KnotUp$ diagram source carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
\mathcal{K}=(C,S,A,D,E,\gamma,\omega,\rho)
$$
whose rows are displayed in one BEDC history ledger. Here $C$ is the
$\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ source-circle row, $S$ is an ambient $S^3$ or ambient
history-boundary row, $A$ is the finite endpoint ledger for diagram arcs,
$D$ is the finite diagram-crossing ledger, $E$ records the endpoint rows for
the arcs meeting each crossing, $\gamma$ is the $\Cont$ provenance reading arc
endpoints from the source circle into the ambient boundary row, $\omega$ is the
$\mathsf{SigRel}$ ledger recording the crossing orientation and over-under relation,
and $\rho$ is the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance binding the consumed
$\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\HomotopyUp}$ certificates.

The packet is admitted only when every arc endpoint row in $E$ is projected
from $C$ through $\gamma$, every crossing row of $D$ names its two incident arc
endpoint rows in $E$, and every orientation row in $\omega$ is tied to the
corresponding crossing row by $\hsame$. Thus the carrier presents a knot
diagram as a finite certificate packet, not as an untracked ambient subset.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[KnotUp Reidemeister move classifier]
\label{def:knot-reidemeister-move-classifier}
Two $\KnotUp$ diagram source carriers $\mathcal{K}$ and $\mathcal{K}'$ are
classified by the $\KnotUp$ Reidemeister move classifier when there is a finite
ledger $R$ whose rows are Reidemeister I, Reidemeister II, and Reidemeister III
move rows between the displayed diagram ledgers of $\mathcal{K}$ and
$\mathcal{K}'$. Each row of $R$ transports the affected arc endpoints by
$\hsame$, preserves the source-circle row through the same
$\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ provenance, and preserves the ambient deformation row
through the same $\NameCert_{\HomotopyUp}$ provenance.

The classifier is generated only by the finite ledger $R$ and the two retained
provenance rows. It therefore compares diagram packets by named local
Reidemeister transport rather than by quantifying over an external isotopy
relation.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:knot-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[KnotUp SOne domain readback]
\label{thm:knot-sone-domain-readback}
For every admitted $\KnotUp$ diagram source carrier
$\mathcal{K}=(C,S,A,D,E,\gamma,\omega,\rho)$, the circle-domain rows consumed
by $\KnotUp$ are exactly the $\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ carrier endpoint rows in
$E$ together with the $\Cont$ provenance $\gamma$ projected from the packet.
No additional source-circle point, parameter row, or endpoint relation is
available to a downstream consumer outside these displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier packet along its displayed coordinates. The first
coordinate is the $\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ source row $C$, and the endpoint
coordinate $E$ is admitted only when each endpoint row is read from $C$ through
the finite $\Cont$ ledger $\gamma$.

For inclusion from the packet to the readback surface, take any consumed
circle-domain row. By the carrier admission condition it appears as one of the
arc endpoint rows in $E$ or as the provenance row $\gamma$ that reads such an
endpoint from $C$.

For the converse inclusion, each row listed in $E$ and the ledger $\gamma$ are
literal coordinates of $\mathcal{K}$. Repacking those coordinates returns the
same admitted packet fields, so the readback surface is exact.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KnotUp Homotopy isotopy transport]
\label{thm:knot-homotopy-isotopy-transport}
Let $\mathcal{K}$ and $\mathcal{K}'$ be admitted $\KnotUp$ diagram source
carriers. A $\NameCert_{\HomotopyUp}$ ambient-isotopy row between their ambient
boundary rows transports the diagram packet from $\mathcal{K}$ to
$\mathcal{K}'$ and preserves the $\KnotUp$ Reidemeister move classifier
whenever the transported endpoint, crossing, and orientation rows are the rows
displayed by a finite Reidemeister ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the ambient-isotopy row as the HomotopyUp provenance component of the
target packet. The source-circle row is retained through the shared
$\NameCert_{\SOneUp}$ provenance, while endpoint rows are transported by the
displayed $\hsame$ rows of the finite Reidemeister ledger.

Induct on the finite Reidemeister ledger. The empty ledger leaves the endpoint
ledger, crossing ledger, orientation rows, and provenance rows unchanged, so
the original packet already supplies the target carrier data.

For a cons step, handle the leading Reidemeister I, II, or III row by replacing
only the local crossing and endpoint rows named by that move. The admission
conditions for the remaining rows are inherited from the induction hypothesis,
and the local move row supplies the required $\hsame$ transport for the changed
endpoints and crossing-orientation relation.

After the induction, repack the transported endpoint ledger, crossing ledger,
orientation ledger, HomotopyUp provenance, and SOneUp provenance. The same
finite ledger witnesses the classifier comparison, so the classifier is
preserved.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KnotUp diagram ledger exactness]
\label{thm:knot-diagram-ledger-exactness}
The public $\NameCert_{\KnotUp}$ surface exposes exactly the diagram source
carrier of \autoref{def:knot-diagram-source-carrier}, the Reidemeister move
classifier of \autoref{def:knot-reidemeister-move-classifier}, the SOneUp
domain readback of \autoref{thm:knot-sone-domain-readback}, the HomotopyUp
isotopy transport of \autoref{thm:knot-homotopy-isotopy-transport}, the finite
diagram crossing ledger, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance tying the SOneUp
and HomotopyUp certificates. It exposes no further knot invariant, ambient
manifold structure, or host-level isotopy object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Collect the two carrier-side definitions. They account for the source-circle
row, ambient boundary row, endpoint rows, crossing ledger, crossing-orientation
$\mathsf{SigRel}$ rows, Reidemeister ledger, and retained $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

Apply \autoref{thm:knot-sone-domain-readback} to identify the entire
circle-domain consumer surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:knot-homotopy-isotopy-transport} to identify the ambient isotopy
transport surface and the classifier-preservation field.

It remains to check that no public field is left over. Any admitted
$\NameCert_{\KnotUp}$ row is either a projected coordinate of the packet, a
row of the finite Reidemeister ledger, or one of the two provenance rows used
to repack the carrier and classifier. These are precisely the rows listed in
the statement, giving exactness of the public surface.
\end{proof}
