\begin{definition}[Focused multi-Hist ledger carrier]
\label{def:focused-multi-hist-ledger-carrier}
A $\FocusedMultiHistLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is an accepted $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$ row naming the focused current-state surface;
\item $F$ is an accepted $\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp$ row naming the local coordinate frame available at that state;
\item $K$ is an accepted $\ObserverKernelTraceUp$ row exposing the focused observer trace without a subject carrier;
\item $T$ is an accepted $\ObserverTraceSealUp$ row sealing the focused trace and its no-subject refusal ledger;
\item $G$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ and provenance ledger for every compressed, digested, or not-yet-fulfilled row in the finite packet;
\item $R$ is the finite inter-$\Hist$ relation ledger, each relation being read through an $\InterHistLocalityLedgerUp$ row;

The carrier is the records-side realization of the closed focused multi-Hist ledger of \autoref{def:closed-focused-multihist-ledger}; it is not a carrier for a global universe, observer-substance, host spacetime, or external synchronization substrate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Focused multi-Hist ledger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:focused-multi-hist-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:focused-multi-hist-ledger-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\FocusedMultiHistLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $L=(S,F,K,T,G,R,I,D,O,H,C,P,N)$, focused current-state admission through $S$, local frame admission through $F$, focused trace and trace-seal admission through $K,T$, $\mathsf{GAP}$ and provenance accountability through $G$, inter-$\Hist$ relation admission through $R$, invariant and dependence readback through $I,D$, audit refusal through $O$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:focused-multi-hist-ledger-carrier}. The rows $S,F,K,T$ expose the focused state, local frame, trace row, and sealed trace before the ledger reads any relation or invariant data.

The rows $G,R,I,D$ supply the closed records-side body of the packet. The $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger accounts for compressed or not-yet-fulfilled rows, the relation row consumes $\InterHistLocalityLedgerUp$, and the invariant and dependence rows record exactly the finite readback available to a focused multi-history consumer.

The audit and structural rows are $O,H,C,P,N$. They record the refusal of subject parameters, global clocks, hidden choosers, object-level $\mathsf{T}$ fields, quotient history equality, and choice-like services, while transport, replay, provenance, and local naming keep every public read inside the same displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:focused-multi-hist-ledger-public-export}

$$

\begin{proof}

The public export is therefore a repackaging of those rows, not a new carrier. The excluded objects have no coordinate in the carrier and cannot be recovered from the export packet. Hence the public route exposes exactly the focused multi-Hist ledger rows and nothing outside them.
