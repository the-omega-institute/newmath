Following Bishop and Bridges on nested closed intervals, the $\NestedIntervalPointNameUp$ packet records the constructive point-name extracted from a displayed shrinking chain of closed bounded intervals. It sits after the endpoint carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert}, the retained-chain surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nestedinterval-namecert}, the intersection route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nestedintervalintersection-namecert}, and the Cantor-style singleton route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cantor-intersection-namecert}. The packet names the point by finite $\BHist$ rows over $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$; it does not choose a host real, quotient representative, open-cover compactness proof, or countable-choice schedule.

\begin{definition}[Nested interval point-name carrier]
\label{def:nested-interval-point-name-carrier}
A $\NestedIntervalPointNameUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(I,J,K,S,R,E,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ endpoint-and-containment source row for each retained interval window;
\item $J$ is the $\NestedIntervalUp$ chain row, recording inclusion of the next retained interval inside the preceding one;
\item $K$ is the $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$ and $\CantorIntersectionUp$ singleton-intersection route row;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite schedule used to read endpoints and diameter bounds at requested precision;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row extracted from the retained endpoint windows;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal for the named point, reached only after $I,J,K,S,R$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval source, nested chain, intersection row, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, and Real seal;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from closed bounded intervals through nested containment, intersection, finite windows, regular readback, and the Real seal;
\item $Q$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NestedIntervalPointNameUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$, $\CantorIntersectionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NestedIntervalPointNameUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an ambient intersection set, a selected quotient representative, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or an unbounded completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Nested interval point-name regular Cauchy seal]
\label{thm:nested-interval-point-name-regular-cauchy-seal}
Every $\RealUp$ read exported by an accepted $\NestedIntervalPointNameUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp,\ \NestedIntervalUp,\ \NestedIntervalIntersectionUp,\ \CantorIntersectionUp,\ \StreamNameUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
In particular, the regular rational readback row $R$ and the terminal seal row $E$ are downstream of the interval source $I$, nested-chain row $J$, singleton-intersection row $K$, and finite schedule row $S$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:nested-interval-point-name-carrier}. The rows $I,J,K$ are the source, nesting, and intersection rows; the rows $S,R,E$ are the finite schedule, regular readback, and terminal Real seal.

Use the closed bounded interval source route from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert}. That route exposes endpoint, containment, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows as finite $\BHist$ data before any point-facing consumer can read them.

Now apply the nested and Cantor intersection siblings. The $\NestedIntervalUp$ and $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$ rows retain the chain and intersection route, while $\CantorIntersectionUp$ supplies the singleton-facing seal. Since $H,C,Q,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name these displayed rows, the exported $\RealUp$ read factors through the stated route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Nested interval point-name endpoint containment]
\label{thm:nested-interval-point-name-endpoint-containment}
For every accepted $\NestedIntervalPointNameUp$ carrier $P=(I,J,K,S,R,E,H,C,Q,N)$, every finite read of the named point remains contained in each displayed retained $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ window before the $\RealUp$ seal is consumed. The containment read is transported componentwise by $H$ and replayed by $C$ through the same $\NestedIntervalUp$ and $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$ chain.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the interval source row $I$. By the carrier, $I$ is a closed bounded interval packet with lower endpoint, upper endpoint, endpoint-order, rational containment, dyadic containment, StreamName, RegSeqRat, and Real-seal rows.

Next use the nested-chain row $J$. It records that each retained window is included in the preceding one, so a later finite read cannot leave an earlier displayed closed bounded interval without breaking the $\hsame$ transport recorded in $H$.

Finally use the intersection row $K$. The $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$ and $\CantorIntersectionUp$ route supplies the point-facing intersection witness, while $C$ replays the same containment route into $S,R,E$. Hence endpoint containment is preserved before the named point reaches the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Nested interval point-name uniqueness route]
\label{thm:nested-interval-point-name-uniqueness-route}
If two accepted $\NestedIntervalPointNameUp$ carriers are classified together by their displayed $\hsame$ transport rows, then their $\RealUp$ point-name seals agree only through the same closed-interval source, nested-chain, intersection, StreamName, and RegSeqRat rows. The classifier exposes no separate host equality of points and no quotient-real representative selection.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the two carriers componentwise. The classifier can transport the interval source, nested chain, intersection row, finite schedule, regular readback, and Real seal only along displayed $\hsame$ rows.

Apply the endpoint-containment theorem. Each finite read remains inside the same transported retained intervals before the Real seal is consumed, so the uniqueness route is pinned to the displayed closed bounded interval and nested-intersection data.

The terminal comparison is therefore a route comparison, not a host equality principle. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient real equality or representative choice, the only available uniqueness evidence is the transported $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\NestedIntervalIntersectionUp$, $\CantorIntersectionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ route named by the packet.
\end{proof}
