\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HyperspaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-hyperspace-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Vietoris and Kelley on compact hyperspaces, the $\HyperspaceUp$ packet names the finite BEDC surface for nonempty compact subsets of a displayed compact metric source. It sits over the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the compact finite-net row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the total-boundedness schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, the completeness boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the diameter ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-diameter-namecert}, the directed convergence row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-netconvergence-namecert}, and the uniform Cauchy-net route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-uniform-net-namecert}. The packet is a finite Hausdorff-distance ledger for compact-subset presentations; it is not a host powerset, not a quotient compactum, not a selected limit object, not open-cover compactness, and not a choice-selected representative.

\begin{definition}[Hyperspace carrier]
\label{def:hyperspace-carrier}
A $\HyperspaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
H=(X,K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1,D_0,D_1,R,H_s,C,P,M)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\CompactMetricUp$ row, including its $\MetricUp$ distance witnesses, finite-net schedules, total-boundedness row, and completeness boundary.
\item $K_0$ and $K_1$ are compact-subset presentations over $X$, each given by finite net schedules whose centers and radii are readable only through the displayed $\CompactMetricUp$ and $\TotallyBoundedUp$ rows.
\item $N_0$ and $N_1$ are the requested net-resolution ledgers for $K_0$ and $K_1$, recording the finite witness lists used to inspect each subset presentation.
\item $D_0$ is the directed Hausdorff-distance coverage ledger from $K_0$ to $K_1$: every displayed center of $K_0$ is matched to a displayed $K_1$ center within the current tolerance after routing through the source metric row.
\item $D_1$ is the directed Hausdorff-distance coverage ledger from $K_1$ to $K_0$ with the same source row, tolerance budget, and finite-read discipline.
\item $R$ is the dyadic and $\RealUp$ tolerance readback for the two directed ledgers, including the comparison cells that package the maximum of $D_0$ and $D_1$ as the finite Hausdorff-distance request.
\item $H_s$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact source, the two subset presentations, the two net ledgers, the two directed coverage ledgers, the tolerance readback, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a compact-subset request through $X,K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1,D_0,D_1,R$ and then to the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HyperspaceUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\MetricDiameterUp$, $\NetConvergenceUp$, $\CauchyUniformNetUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $M$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HyperspaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host powersets, quotient compacta, selected limits, open-cover compactness, or choice-selected representatives.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hausdorff-distance ledger stability]
\label{thm:hyperspace-hausdorff-distance-stability}
For every accepted $\HyperspaceUp$ carrier $H=(X,K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1,D_0,D_1,R,H_s,C,P,M)$, classifier transport preserves the two-sided finite Hausdorff-distance ledger. More precisely, any componentwise $\hsame$ transport of the displayed source, subset presentations, net ledgers, directed coverage ledgers, and tolerance readback carries the finite route through $\CompactMetricUp$, $\MetricDiameterUp$, $\NetConvergenceUp$, and $\CauchyUniformNetUp$ to a carrier with the same directed $K_0$-to-$K_1$ and $K_1$-to-$K_0$ coverage obligations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:hyperspace-carrier}. The source row $X$ fixes the compact metric surface, while $K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1$ identify the finite subset-net data that any Hausdorff-distance read can inspect.

The two directed ledgers are then transported componentwise. The row $H_s$ compares the source, subset presentations, net schedules, directed coverage rows, and tolerance readback, and the replay row $C$ repeats the same route after transport.

The compact-source part of the route remains inside $\CompactMetricUp$. Diameter and tolerance comparisons are read through $\MetricDiameterUp$ and $R$, and the finite convergence and uniform-net inspections are routed through $\NetConvergenceUp$ and $\CauchyUniformNetUp$.

Thus each transported center match in $D_0$ and $D_1$ still cites the same kind of displayed source-distance witness and the same finite tolerance readback. Since the packet contains no host powerset, quotient compactum, selected limit, open-cover compactness, or choice-selected representative, no external coordinate can alter the two-sided ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hyperspace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hyperspace-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\HyperspaceUp$ carrier $H=(X,K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1,D_0,D_1,R,H_s,C,P,M)$, the local $\NameCert_{\HyperspaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item compact-source admission for the displayed $\CompactMetricUp$ source row $X$ and its $\MetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, and $\CompleteMetricUp$ support;
\item finite subset-net admission for $K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1$, requiring every subset presentation and net resolution to be read through the displayed compact-source schedules;
\item bidirectional distance coverage, requiring both directed ledgers $D_0$ and $D_1$ to be present before the Hausdorff-distance tolerance readback $R$ is public;
\item classifier and transport stability through $H_s$ and $C$, preserving the source, subset-net, coverage, and readback rows under componentwise $\hsame$ and displayed replay;
\item provenance and name exhaustion through $P$ and $M$, excluding host powersets, quotient compacta, selected limits, open-cover compactness, and choice-selected representatives.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HyperspaceUp.HyperspaceTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:hyperspace-carrier}. The packet exposes a compact metric source $X$ and only the displayed finite rows attached to $X$.

Finite subset-net admission follows from the same projection. The rows $K_0,K_1,N_0,N_1$ are the only subset-presentation and resolution coordinates, so every compact-subset inspection passes through them.

Bidirectional coverage is exactly the pair $D_0,D_1$. A Hausdorff-distance read is not public until both directed ledgers have been consumed and the dyadic or $\RealUp$ tolerance readback $R$ has compared them.

Transport is componentwise through $H_s$ and replayed through $C$. These rows preserve the compact source, subset nets, directed coverage witnesses, and tolerance readbacks without adding a second classifier surface.

Finally, $P$ and $M$ name precisely the displayed packet. Because the carrier contains no row for host powersets, quotient compacta, selected limits, open-cover compactness, or choice-selected representatives, the five listed rows exhaust the local naming obligations.
\end{proof}
