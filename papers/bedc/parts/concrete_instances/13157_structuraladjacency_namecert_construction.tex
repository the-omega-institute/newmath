\begin{definition}[Structural adjacency carrier]
\label{def:structural-adjacency-carrier}
A $\StructuralAdjacencyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(G,R,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the genealogy-label row, recording the two inherited claim labels, source families, question surfaces, and local comparison prompts.
\item $R$ is the BEDC role-row classifier, recording the carrier, relation, classifier, ledger, certificate, gap, or $T$-position on which each claim lands.
\item $A$ is the adjacency-witness row, recording that the two labels land on the same BEDC role row without importing doctrinal identity or tradition membership.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for genealogy labels, role rows, adjacency witnesses, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for the route from genealogy labels through role-row classification and adjacency witness inspection.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StructuralAdjacencyUp$, $\TraditionComparisonBoundaryUp$, $\PrimitiveDistinctionLedgerUp$, $\ObserverDistinctionTraceUp$, $\KernelCategoryUp$, $\EpistemicWitnessUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\StructuralAdjacencyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for prose resemblance alone, inherited doctrinal agreement, host-level semantic identity, or adjacency export outside the displayed packet $S=(G,R,A,H,C,P,N)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Structural adjacency NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:structural-adjacency-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:structural-adjacency-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\StructuralAdjacencyUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five rows: carrier admission for $S=(G,R,A,H,C,P,N)$; role-row classifier stability through $R$ under componentwise transport; adjacency exactness through $A$ for shared BEDC landing; genealogy-label non-collapse through $G$; and ledger non-escape through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier. Carrier admission supplies the displayed $\BHist$ packet, while the genealogy row $G$ keeps the two inherited labels present as distinct labels before any BEDC landing is compared.

Read the role classifier and adjacency witness together. The row $R$ records the BEDC role rows available to each label, and $A$ certifies adjacency only when those role rows coincide as a BEDC site. Componentwise transport in $H$ preserves the classifier row instead of replacing it by prose similarity.

Finally apply the route, provenance, and local-name rows. The continuation row $C$, package row $P$, and local NameCert row $N$ name the same packet, so the ledger cannot export adjacency without the displayed role row, genealogy labels, and exact adjacency witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Structural adjacency independence witness]
\label{thm:structural-adjacency-independence-witness}
The nearest siblings of $\StructuralAdjacencyUp$ are $\TraditionComparisonBoundaryUp$, $\PrimitiveDistinctionLedgerUp$, $\ObserverDistinctionTraceUp$, $\KernelCategoryUp$, and $\EpistemicWitnessUp$. No bijection preserves all carrier rows between any one of those siblings and $S=(G,R,A,H,C,P,N)$: each sibling lacks at least one of genealogy-pair labeling, BEDC role-row classification for two claims, adjacency witness exactness, or the local $\NameCert_{\StructuralAdjacencyUp}$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the displayed rows. $\TraditionComparisonBoundaryUp$ records source, landing, rejected surplus, and structural distinction for a comparison; it does not store a two-claim adjacency witness as the public row of the packet.

The primitive distinction ledger and observer distinction trace isolate mark distinction and observer trace structure. The finite kernel category route and epistemic witness packet isolate category-shaped operations and witness admission. None of these carries the pair of genealogy labels, shared BEDC role-row classifier, adjacency witness, transport, route, provenance, and local StructuralAdjacency name in one carrier.

A row-preserving bijection would have to preserve $G,R,A,H,C,P,N$ jointly. Since every listed sibling omits at least one of these rows or assigns it a different obligation role, no such bijection preserves all carrier rows.
\end{proof}
