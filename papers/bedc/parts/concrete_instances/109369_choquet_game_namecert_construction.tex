Following Choquet and Oxtoby on topological games and Baire category, the $\ChoquetGameUp$ packet records the finite nested-open game route used by complete-metric Baire arguments. It sits over the Baire prefix of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairespace-namecert}, the dense-open and category surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairecategory-namecert}, the complete-metric handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the finite schedule discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names the standard Choquet-game interface as BEDC NameCert data without importing a chosen winning strategy, a host intersection point, countable choice, quotient stream equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Choquet-game carrier]
\label{def:choquet-game-carrier}
A $\ChoquetGameUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(B,M,L,S,R,E,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix row naming the inspected basic-open source, finite prefix probes, and legal initial open row.
\item $M$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ metric-ball row, including displayed centers, positive rational radii, inclusion checks, and shrinking-radius obligations for successor moves.
\item $L$ is the nested legal-move ledger, recording each finite play prefix, the predecessor ball, the successor ball, the player tag, and the proof that the successor remains inside the predecessor.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row selecting the finite play prefixes and ball observations visible to the consumer.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the rational centers, radius budgets, and tolerance comparisons used by the nested ball thread.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the Baire prefix, complete-metric ball data, legal-move ledger, stream schedule, and regular rational readback have been displayed.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for Baire prefixes, complete-metric ball rows, legal-move ledgers, stream schedules, regular rational readback, Real seals, replay data, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a game request to the Baire prefix, complete-metric ball route, nested legal-move ledger, finite schedule, rational readback, Real seal, transport, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ChoquetGameUp$, $\BaireSpaceUp$, $\BaireCategoryUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ChoquetGameUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a global winning strategy, a selected limit point, countable choice, quotient equality of streams, ambient completed-real principles, or a hidden dense-open oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Choquet-game NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:choquet-game-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ChoquetGameUp$ carrier $G=(B,M,L,S,R,E,T,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ChoquetGameUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed Baire prefix, complete-metric ball, legal-move ledger, stream schedule, regular rational readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item Baire-prefix coverage, requiring every play prefix to begin with the $\BaireSpaceUp$ row $B$;
\item metric-ball legality, requiring $M$ to supply displayed centers, radii, inclusions, and shrinking obligations before $L$ records a successor move;
\item nested-play exactness, requiring $L$ to store only finite legal play prefixes and no unbounded strategy object;
\item schedule and readback discipline, requiring $S,R,E$ to be read in that order before any terminal Real-facing consumer receives the packet;
\item structural preservation, requiring $T,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed game route;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding global winning strategies, selected limit points, countable choice, quotient stream equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, and hidden dense-open oracles.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:choquet-game-carrier}. The analytic rows are $B,M,L,S,R,E$, and the structural rows are $T,C,P,N$.

Baire-prefix coverage fixes the source of every finite play. Each accepted game request begins at the displayed $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix row and therefore cannot read an external open-set universe.

Metric-ball legality is confined to $M$ and $L$. The complete-metric row supplies centers, radii, inclusion checks, and shrinking obligations, while the legal-move ledger records only the finite nested prefixes admitted by those checks.

The terminal handoff is scheduled. A consumer reads the finite play schedule through $S$, the rational tolerances through $R$, and the Real-facing seal through $E$ only after the earlier rows have appeared.

Finally $T,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same displayed packet. Since the excluded objects have no carrier coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Choquet-game Baire-category handoff]
\label{thm:choquet-game-baire-category-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ seal exported by an accepted $\ChoquetGameUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\BaireSpaceUp
\longmapsto
\CompleteMetricUp
\longmapsto
\ChoquetGameUp
\longmapsto
\StreamNameUp

\longmapsto

$$
The route supplies the Baire-category consumer surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairecategory-namecert} by exposing nested legal move prefixes and complete-metric ball obligations, not by selecting a host intersection point or importing a completed-real principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier. The Baire prefix $B$ names the basic-open source, and the complete-metric row $M$ names the ball data through which legal moves are checked.

The game content is finite. The ledger $L$ records the displayed nested play prefixes and the successor-in-predecessor evidence needed by a Baire-category handoff, while $S$ exposes the finite schedule consumed at the current stage.

Regular rational readback and Real sealing are downstream. The row $R$ records the rational center and radius tolerances, and $E$ seals the terminal read only after $B,M,L,S,R$ have been displayed.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming in $T,C,P,N$ preserve that route componentwise. Thus the handoff supplies Baire-category arguments with the visible nested-ball interface while exporting no global strategy, selected point, countable-choice enumeration, quotient stream equality, or ambient completed-real principle.
\end{proof}
