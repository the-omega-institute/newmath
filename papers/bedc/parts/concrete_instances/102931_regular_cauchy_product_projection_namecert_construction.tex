\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy product projection budget]\label{thm:regular-cauchy-product-projection-budget}
Let
$$
J=(K,L,R,M,W,D,A,B,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\RegularCauchyProductProjectionUp$ carrier. Every left or right projection request from $J$ factors through the displayed product-modulus budget $M$, the finite window family $W$, the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, the selected component readback $L$ or $R$, and the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff $A$ or $B$ before the $\RealUp$ seal $S$ is available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-product-projection-carrier}. A projection request first identifies the product packet $K$ and one of the two projection readback rows $L$ or $R$.

The request then passes through the product-modulus ledger $M$. This row is the only place where the product-facing precision request is assigned to component budgets, so neither component handoff can be read before $M$ is displayed.

Next the finite window family $W$ and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$ supply the actual component observations and tolerance rows. The left handoff $A$ consumes the left readback row $L$, while the right handoff $B$ consumes the right readback row $R$.

The seal $S$ appears only after those regular rational handoffs. Therefore every projected read follows the route through $M,W,D$, the selected component readback, and its $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff before any $\RealUp$ seal can be consumed.
\end{proof}
