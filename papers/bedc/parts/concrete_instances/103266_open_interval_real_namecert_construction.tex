\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\OpenIntervalRealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-open-interval-real-namecert}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\OpenIntervalRealUp$ packet names an open real interval by finite endpoint seals, located order tests, dyadic tolerance ledgers, and regular Cauchy readbacks before any topology or completion-facing consumer sees membership. It sits after the located interval surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-real-interval-namecert}, the located-order test of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-real-located-order-test-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream-window route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a constructive interface for the open interval $(a,b)$ over displayed $\RealUp$ names; it is not host trichotomy, quotient real equality, a chosen representative, countable choice, ambient real completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.

\begin{definition}[OpenIntervalReal carrier]
\label{def:open-interval-real-carrier}
An $\OpenIntervalRealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(A,B,X,L,U,D,S,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are endpoint $\RealUp$ seal rows. They name the lower and upper endpoints only through displayed regular Cauchy data.
\item $X$ is the candidate point $\RealUp$ seal row whose open-interval membership is being read.
\item $L$ is the left located-order row from $\CauchyRealLocatedOrderTestUp$, witnessing that $A$ is located strictly below $X$ at the displayed tolerance.
\item $U$ is the right located-order row from $\CauchyRealLocatedOrderTestUp$, witnessing that $X$ is located strictly below $B$ at the displayed tolerance.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row. It records the dyadic radius, positive slack, endpoint comparison budget, and exhaustion ledger shared by $L$ and $U$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row. It records the finite endpoint and point windows inspected by the two located-order tests.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row. It reads the same finite windows as regular rational observations before the endpoint and point seals are consumed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the endpoint seals, candidate seal, order-test rows, dyadic tolerance row, stream windows, readback row, replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an open-interval membership request through $A,B,X,L,U,D,S,R,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\OpenIntervalRealUp$, $\LocatedRealIntervalUp$, $\CauchyRealLocatedOrderTestUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\OpenIntervalRealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts exactly that these twelve rows form one finite open-interval membership packet. Its classifier reads only the displayed tuple $A,B,X,L,U,D,S,R,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for host trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected representatives, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[OpenIntervalReal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:open-interval-real-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\OpenIntervalRealUp$ carrier $I=(A,B,X,L,U,D,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\OpenIntervalRealUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two endpoint $\RealUp$ seals, candidate $\RealUp$ seal, two located-order rows, dyadic tolerance ledger, stream windows, regular rational readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item endpoint-seal exactness, requiring $A$ and $B$ to be read only after the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks expose the endpoint data used by the order tests;
\item candidate-seal exactness, requiring $X$ to be read through the same window and readback route before a membership verdict is exported;
\item located left boundary, requiring $L$ to display the finite order-test route for $A<X$ over the shared dyadic tolerance row $D$;
\item located right boundary, requiring $U$ to display the finite order-test route for $X<B$ over the same dyadic tolerance row $D$;
\item dyadic tolerance coherence, requiring the positive slack and exhaustion rows in $D$ to be the only tolerance data consumed by both boundary tests;
\item structural stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to transport and replay the endpoint, candidate, order-test, tolerance, window, readback, provenance, and local naming rows componentwise;
\item ledger refusal, excluding host trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected representatives, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation for the open interval is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $I=(A,B,X,L,U,D,S,R,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes exactly the two endpoint seals, candidate seal, two located-order rows, dyadic tolerance ledger, stream windows, regular rational readback, and structural rows.

The readback route is finite. The stream row $S$ supplies the endpoint and point windows, while the row $R$ reads those same windows as regular rational observations before the $\RealUp$ seals are consumed.

The two boundary tests are then read against the same dyadic ledger. The row $L$ records the displayed route for $A<X$, and the row $U$ records the displayed route for $X<B$; both use the positive slack and exhaustion data stored in $D$.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since none of the carrier coordinates stores host trichotomy, quotient equality, representative choice, countable choice, ambient completeness, a Lean theorem, or a bridge, no further local obligation can be read from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OpenIntervalReal located membership route]
\label{thm:open-interval-real-located-membership}
Every accepted $\OpenIntervalRealUp$ membership read for $X$ between endpoints $A$ and $B$ factors through the finite route
$$
A,B,X \longmapsto S,R,D \longmapsto L,U \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus an open-interval consumer may inspect endpoint seals, candidate seal, finite windows, regular rational readbacks, dyadic tolerance data, and the two located-order rows, but it cannot obtain host trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected representatives, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted membership read. The only real-name coordinates are $A,B,X$, so any endpoint or candidate observation must pass through the displayed $\RealUp$ seals.

The finite observation stage comes next. The row $S$ fixes the inspected windows, the row $R$ reads those windows as regular rational data, and the row $D$ supplies the dyadic tolerance, positive slack, and exhaustion ledger shared by the two boundary tests.

The membership verdict is the conjunction of the two located-order rows. The left row $L$ displays $A<X$, and the right row $U$ displays $X<B$, each over the same readback and tolerance data rather than over a host-order primitive.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ close the route by transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Because the route has no coordinate for trichotomy, quotient equality, representative selection, countable choice, completeness, verification, or a bridge, those principles are not exported by the membership read.
\end{proof}
