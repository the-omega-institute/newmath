\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SpectralMeasureUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-spectral-measure-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following von Neumann and standard functional analysis, the $\SpectralMeasureUp$ interface records the finite BEDC certificate surface for a projection-valued spectral measure over a Hilbert carrier, a bounded-operator row, a projection-family ledger, and finite scalar measure windows. Its traditional target is the projection-valued measure used in the spectral theorem, as in Reed and Simon, \emph{Methods of Modern Mathematical Physics I: Functional Analysis} (1980), or Conway, \emph{A Course in Functional Analysis} (1990). The chapter states only the finite certificate surface consumed by $\SpectralTheoremUp$ and $\ObservableUp$: it does not prove a spectral theorem, host sigma-additivity, quotient measure spaces, unrestricted functional calculus, or choice-selected spectral decompositions.

The packet consumes the Hilbert carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}, the bounded operator surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedlinearoperator-namecert}, the operator-ideal projection boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-operatorideal-namecert}, the self-adjoint observable row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observable-namecert}, the event-carrier and finite-additivity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-measure-namecert}, and the spectral-data consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spectraltheorem-namecert}. These are substantive carrier and ledger dependencies, not background references: without them the spectral-measure packet has no Hilbert endpoint for projections, no bounded-operator row whose projections can act, no operator-ideal surface for projection-family transport, no observable row to measure, and no finite event ledger on which projection additivity can be recorded.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:spectralmeasure-carrier}

\begin{definition}[SpectralMeasureUp carrier]
\label{def:spectralmeasure-carrier}
\label{def:spectral-measure-carrier}
A $\SpectralMeasureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(H,B,\Pi,\mu,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the $\HilbertUp$ source row over which all projection endpoints are typed.
\item $B$ is the $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$ row carrying the displayed self-adjoint or normal operator endpoint whose spectral windows are being named.
\item $\Pi$ is the projection-family ledger: each visible event row is assigned a projection endpoint over the same $H$, with orthogonality and finite-additivity rows for disjoint displayed events.
\item $\mu$ is the scalar $\RealUp$ measure-window row read from finite event probes, recording only finite scalar windows attached to the same projection family.
\item $T$ is the classifier-transport row, comparing Hilbert, bounded-operator, event, projection, and scalar-window endpoints only by displayed $\hsame$ rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from event rows through projection endpoints and scalar $\RealUp$ measure windows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SpectralMeasureUp$, $\HilbertUp$, $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$, $\OperatorIdealUp$, $\ObservableUp$, $\MeasureUp$, $\SpectralTheoremUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SpectralMeasureUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}

The carrier exports no ambient Hilbert-space equality, host sigma-additivity, quotient measure space, unrestricted functional calculus, choice-selected spectral decomposition, countable family selector, eigenbasis, or external spectral-theorem object.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[SpectralMeasureUp classifier]
\label{def:spectralmeasure-classifier}
For accepted $\SpectralMeasureUp$ packets $S$ and $S'$, the classifier $S\sim_{\SpectralMeasureUp}S'$ holds exactly when the displayed $\HilbertUp$ carrier rows, $\ObservableUp$ rows, $\MeasureUp$ event rows, projection endpoints, orthogonality and finite-additivity ledgers, $\Cont$ routes, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance compare componentwise by their recorded $\hsame$ rows. The classifier does not compare completed measurable spaces or host projection-valued functions; it compares only the finite rows visible in the certificate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SpectralMeasureUp public consumer boundary]
\label{thm:spectralmeasure-public-consumer-boundary}
Every downstream $\SpectralTheoremUp$ or $\ObservableUp$ consumer of an
accepted $\SpectralMeasureUp$ packet may read only the finite $\HilbertUp$
carrier, the observable row, the $\MeasureUp$ event row, the projection
endpoints, the orthogonality and finite-additivity ledger, the displayed
$\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. Such a consumer
cannot read countable additivity, completed sigma-algebras, host
projection-valued functions, eigenbasis choices, or a spectral theorem proof.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpectralMeasureUp.SpectralMeasureFinitePacket\_public\_consumer\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:spectralmeasure-carrier}. Its exported
coordinates are exactly the Hilbert carrier, observable row, MeasureUp event
row, projection endpoints, finite ledger, transport rows, and provenance rows.

Then project the classifier of \autoref{def:spectralmeasure-classifier}. It
compares only those displayed rows by their recorded $\hsame$ witnesses and
does not introduce a completed sigma-algebra, host projection-valued function,
eigenbasis selector, or spectral theorem object.

The public consumer boundary is therefore the finite list of exported rows.
Every downstream $\SpectralTheoremUp$ or $\ObservableUp$ read factors through
that list and has no coordinate for the excluded host-level data.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:spectralmeasure-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[SpectralMeasureUp hsame stability]
\label{thm:spectralmeasure-hsame-stability}
If accepted $\SpectralMeasureUp$ packets $S$ and $S'$ are related by the classifier of \autoref{def:spectralmeasure-classifier}, then transporting the Hilbert, observable, event, projection, orthogonality, finite-additivity, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows of $S$ along the displayed $\hsame$ witnesses yields the corresponding accepted rows of $S'$. In particular, downstream spectral-theorem and observable consumers may replace a carried spectral-measure packet by a classified packet without reading any unlogged measurable or operator data.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpectralMeasureUp.SpectralMeasureCarrier\_hsame\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier of \autoref{def:spectralmeasure-carrier}. Each accepted row is one of the displayed Hilbert, observable, event, projection, ledger, continuation, or provenance coordinates.

Apply the classifier definition componentwise. The $\hsame$ witnesses transport each source coordinate to its partner coordinate, and the $\Cont$ rows record the same event-to-projection readback after transport.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for completed measurable spaces, host projection-valued functions, or eigenbasis choices, the transported packet is exhausted by the displayed rows and remains an accepted $\SpectralMeasureUp$ packet.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpectralMeasureUp.SpectralMeasurePacket\_hsame\_stability}

\begin{theorem}[SpectralMeasureUp orthogonal finite additivity]
\label{thm:spectralmeasure-orthogonal-additivity}
Let $S$ be an accepted $\SpectralMeasureUp$ packet and let $E$ and $F$ be displayed event rows in its $\MeasureUp$ event carrier. If the packet ledger records $E$ and $F$ as disjoint displayed events, then the projection endpoint row for the displayed union event is the finite-additivity endpoint obtained from the two projection endpoint rows, and the orthogonality row records that the two projections are compatible as Hilbert projections over the same $\HilbertUp$ carrier. This is a finite ledger statement and not a countable-additivity theorem over a completed sigma-algebra.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the two event rows and their disjointness witness from the $\MeasureUp$ side of the carrier. The projection endpoint rows for $E$ and $F$ are displayed Hilbert projection rows with the same $\HilbertUp$ provenance.

The orthogonality ledger supplies the compatibility row for the two projection endpoints. The finite-additivity ledger then names the union-event projection endpoint as the endpoint obtained from those two displayed projection rows.

No countable family is introduced. The proof sketch uses only the finite event pair present in the packet and the ledger rows already displayed by the carrier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpectralMeasureUp.SpectralMeasureOrthogonalFiniteAdditivity\_row}

\begin{theorem}[SpectralMeasureUp projection-ledger finite additivity]
\label{thm:spectralmeasure-projection-ledger-finite-additivity}
Let $S$ be an accepted $\SpectralMeasureUp$ packet and let $E_1,\ldots,E_n$ be a finite displayed family of pairwise-disjoint event rows in its $\MeasureUp$ event carrier. If the packet also displays the finite-union event row $E_1\cup\cdots\cup E_n$, then every projection endpoint row assigned to that union is obtained by iterating the packet's orthogonality and finite-additivity ledger over the same $\HilbertUp$, $\ObservableUp$, and $\MeasureUp$ provenance rows. The read uses no countable additivity, completed sigma-algebra data, eigenbasis choice, or host projection-valued function.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpectralMeasureUp.SpectralMeasurePacket\_projection\_ledger\_finite\_additivity}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpectralMeasureUp.SpectralMeasureFinitePacket\_projection\_ledger\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier and classifier of \autoref{def:spectralmeasure-carrier} and \autoref{def:spectralmeasure-classifier}. The only available event-to-projection data are the displayed event rows, projection endpoint rows, orthogonality ledger, finite-additivity ledger, continuation routes, and source packages for $\HilbertUp$, $\ObservableUp$, and $\MeasureUp$.

Proceed by induction over the finite ledger for the displayed union. The empty or singleton ledger reads back the projection endpoint already stored for the displayed event row.

For the binary step, apply \autoref{thm:spectralmeasure-orthogonal-additivity} to the next disjoint event row and the accumulated displayed union row. The orthogonality row supplies compatibility over the same Hilbert carrier, and the finite-additivity row names the next projection endpoint without changing the observable or measure provenance.

The packet has no coordinate for countable families, completed measurable spaces, eigenvectors, or external projection-valued functions. Thus the finite-union endpoint is exhausted by the projection ledger recorded in the accepted $\SpectralMeasureUp$ packet.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpectralMeasureUp.SpectralMeasureFiniteAdditivityLedger\_projection\_chain}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpectralMeasureUp.SpectralMeasureFinitePacket\_projection\_ledger\_additivity}

\begin{theorem}[SpectralMeasureUp NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:spectralmeasure-namecert-obligation-surface}
\label{thm:spectral-measure-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\SpectralMeasureUp}$ obligation surface consists of the carrier of \autoref{def:spectralmeasure-carrier}, the classifier of \autoref{def:spectralmeasure-classifier}, the $\hsame$ stability theorem, the orthogonal finite-additivity ledger, the projection-family ledger, the scalar $\RealUp$ measure windows, and the provenance rows naming $\HilbertUp$, $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$, $\OperatorIdealUp$, $\ObservableUp$, $\MeasureUp$, and $\SpectralTheoremUp$. Every consumer of $\SpectralMeasureUp$ can read only those finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SpectralMeasureUp.SpectralMeasureFinitePacket\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and classifier definitions. They expose the Hilbert source, observable row, event carrier, projection endpoints, finite ledger, transport rows, and source provenance as the whole packet.

Use \autoref{thm:spectralmeasure-hsame-stability} for classified replacement of a packet and \autoref{thm:spectralmeasure-orthogonal-additivity} for the finite disjoint-event ledger. These two rows are the visible stability and exactness obligations needed by spectral-data consumers.

Finally repack the displayed rows as the local naming-certificate surface. Because no spectral theorem proof, completed sigma-algebra, countable additivity row, or eigenbasis row is part of the carrier, the obligation surface stops at the finite BEDC certificate boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SpectralMeasureUp projection-ledger nonescape]
\label{thm:spectral-measure-projection-ledger-nonescape}
Every $\SpectralTheoremUp$, bounded-operator, or observable consumer of an accepted $\SpectralMeasureUp$ packet reads the projection-family ledger only through
$$
H,B,\Pi,\mu,T,C,P,N .
$$
The read therefore passes through the displayed $\HilbertUp$ source, $\BoundedLinearOperatorUp$ row, projection-family ledger, scalar $\RealUp$ measure windows, classifier transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\SpectralMeasureUp}$ row before it exports spectral data. It cannot expose host sigma-additivity, quotient measure spaces, unrestricted functional calculus, choice-selected spectral decompositions, host projection-valued functions, or eigenbasis choices.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:spectralmeasure-carrier}. The only projection-facing coordinates are $H,B,\Pi,\mu,T,C,P,N$.

The projection-family ledger $\Pi$ is typed over the same Hilbert source $H$ and bounded-operator row $B$, and the scalar measure windows $\mu$ are read only through finite $\RealUp$ windows attached to visible event probes. Thus the consumer receives finite projection and scalar-window data, not a completed host measure space.

The classifier and replay rows close the boundary. The row $T$ transports only the displayed Hilbert, operator, event, projection, and scalar-window endpoints, while $C,P,N$ replay, source, and name that finite route. Since no excluded object appears among these rows, the projection-ledger read has no route to host sigma-additivity, quotient measure spaces, unrestricted functional calculus, choice-selected spectral decompositions, host projection-valued functions, or eigenbasis choices.
\end{proof}
