\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ArzelaAscoliUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-arzelaascoli-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ArzelaAscoliUp$ interface names the constructive compact-metric reading of the Arzela--Ascoli theorem for families of continuous maps. A carrier records a family of $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows from a $\CompactMetricUp$ source into a $\CompleteMetricUp$ target, together with finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ nets, equicontinuity modulus rows, pointwise totally bounded image rows, displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport, $\Pkg$ provenance, and a local $\NameCert$ row. It is a finite compact-continuity discovery target over $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\FanTheoremUp$, and $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$, not an open-cover compactness principle or a subsequence chosen by ambient choice.

The carrier consumes the finite-net source surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert} through its $\CompactMetricUp$ row $X$, the target completion rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert} through its $\CompleteMetricUp$ row $Y$, the family graph rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert} through the finite ledger $F$, and the finite-modulus handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactuniformcontinuity-namecert} through the modulus row $M$. These sibling rows are consumed as carrier coordinates and ledger inputs, not as ambient compactness, function-space, or choice principles.

The family-level equicontinuity rows are read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-equicontinuityfamily-namecert}, while the finite modulus handoff is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactuniformcontinuity-namecert}; both are consumed only as finite net and modulus ledgers and do not add open-cover compactness or choice-selected subsequences.

\begin{definition}[Arzela-Ascoli carrier]
\label{def:arzela-ascoli-carrier}
An $\ArzelaAscoliUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
A=(X,Y,F,\mathcal{P},E,I,M,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\CompactMetricUp$ row, including its $\MetricUp$ source, finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ net rows, and transported $\TotallyBoundedUp$ coverage;
\item $Y$ is the target $\CompleteMetricUp$ row whose distance and regular-Cauchy limit witnesses are read through displayed $\MetricUp$ and completion rows;
\item $F$ is a finite family ledger of $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows from $X$ to $Y$;
\item $\mathcal{P}$ records the finite source-net windows used to test the family;
\item $E$ records equicontinuity modulus rows, each binding a requested target tolerance to one finite source-radius row uniformly over the family ledger;
\item $I$ records pointwise $\TotallyBoundedUp$ image rows for the finite family values at every bundled source-net point;
\item $M$ records the $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$ or $\FanTheoremUp$ finite-modulus handoff rows that turn pointwise windows into a uniform finite source window;
\item $B$ records the finite image-bundle selections and target metric distance witnesses used by the pointwise total-bounded rows;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for source nets, target-completion rows, family rows, equicontinuity rows, pointwise image rows, finite-modulus rows, and bundle selections;
\item $C$ records the visible $\Cont$ routes by which compact-metric, continuous-map, total-bounded, complete-metric, fan, and compact-uniform-continuity consumers read the packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\FanTheoremUp$, $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ArzelaAscoliUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only finite net, modulus, image-bundle, completion-witness, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows. It contains no open-cover compactness row, no sequential compactness row, no unrecorded function-space topology, and no choice-selected subsequence.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Arzela-Ascoli NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:arzela-ascoli-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ArzelaAscoliUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier habitation by the packet of \autoref{def:arzela-ascoli-carrier}; classifier transport of the source compact-metric rows, target complete-metric rows, family of continuous-map rows, equicontinuity rows, pointwise image-bundle rows, and finite-modulus rows; finite-net coverage through $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$; target-side completion readback through $\CompleteMetricUp$; and downstream non-escape through the recorded $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ArzelaAscoliUp.ArzelaAscoliPacket\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is the displayed tuple of \autoref{def:arzela-ascoli-carrier}. Each source-net row, family row, target-completion row, equicontinuity row, pointwise image-bundle row, finite-modulus handoff, transport row, continuation route, package row, and local certificate row is visible before a consumer can read it.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The $\CompactMetricUp$ coordinate transports source finite nets, the $\ContinuousMapUp$ family coordinate transports graph and pointwise modulus rows, the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ coordinate transports finite image bundles, and the $\CompleteMetricUp$ coordinate transports target regular-Cauchy limit witnesses.

The finite-modulus coordinate $M$ is then transported through the same $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes. It may cite $\FanTheoremUp$ or $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$, but only through their finite branch-depth, witness-window, and compact-uniform-modulus ledgers.

These rows exhaust the certificate. Since the carrier has no coordinate for open covers, host function spaces, choice-selected subsequences, or ambient sequential compactness, the local naming certificate cannot export any of those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Arzela-Ascoli carrier habitation]
\label{thm:arzela-ascoli-carrier-habitation}
There is a finite inhabited $\ArzelaAscoliUp$ carrier of the form
$$
A=(X,Y,F,\mathcal{P},E,I,M,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
whose source row is a $\CompactMetricUp$ row, whose target row is a $\CompleteMetricUp$ row, whose family rows are $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows, whose pointwise image rows are $\TotallyBoundedUp$ rows, whose finite handoff rows are read through $\FanTheoremUp$ or $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$, and whose visible bookkeeping is a $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ packet as specified in \autoref{def:arzela-ascoli-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the displayed packet shape of \autoref{def:arzela-ascoli-carrier}. The source coordinate $X$, target coordinate $Y$, finite family ledger $F$, source-net packet $\mathcal{P}$, equicontinuity ledger $E$, image ledger $I$, handoff ledger $M$, image bundle $B$, transport row $H$, continuation row $C$, provenance row $P$, and naming row $N$ form a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

The equicontinuity net handoff of \autoref{thm:arzela-ascoli-equicontinuity-net-handoff} verifies that the source-net, family, pointwise-image, completion, and handoff rows are read as a single finite compact-continuity surface. No open-cover or subsequence coordinate is needed for habitation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Arzela-Ascoli completion-window readback]
\label{thm:arzela-ascoli-completion-window-readback}
Let $A=(X,Y,F,\mathcal{P},E,I,M,B,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\ArzelaAscoliUp$ carrier. Every target-side completion read exported by $A$ factors through the $\CompleteMetricUp$ row $Y$, the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ source windows $\mathcal{P}$, the $\ContinuousMapUp$ family ledger $F$, the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ image rows $I$, the finite handoff row $M$ from $\FanTheoremUp$ or $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$, and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a completion read in the target. The carrier first exposes the target coordinate $Y$, so every limit or regular-Cauchy read is a $\CompleteMetricUp$ read rather than a host completion choice.

Next read the source windows $\mathcal{P}$ and family ledger $F$. By \autoref{thm:arzela-ascoli-equicontinuity-net-handoff}, the selected source-net rows and equicontinuity rows route every family value through the same finite compact-continuity handoff.

The pointwise image rows $I$ and image-bundle rows $B$ then supply the finite totally bounded target packets. The finite handoff row $M$ records whether the branch-depth or compact-uniform-continuity route is used, and $H,C,P,N$ keep that read stable under classifier transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Arzela-Ascoli non-escape obligation]
\label{thm:arzela-ascoli-non-escape-obligation}
The public boundary of an accepted $\ArzelaAscoliUp$ packet exports only the source $\CompactMetricUp$ row, target $\CompleteMetricUp$ row, finite $\ContinuousMapUp$ family ledger, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ source-net rows, equicontinuity rows, pointwise $\TotallyBoundedUp$ image rows, $\FanTheoremUp$ or $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$ handoff rows, image-bundle rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ evidence. It exports no open-cover compactness, no sequential compactness, no ambient function-space topology, and no choice-selected subsequence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the coordinates of \autoref{def:arzela-ascoli-carrier}. They are exactly $X$, $Y$, $F$, $\mathcal{P}$, $E$, $I$, $M$, $B$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$.

The handoff theorem \autoref{thm:arzela-ascoli-equicontinuity-net-handoff} routes compact-continuity consumers through finite source nets, equicontinuity ledgers, pointwise image bundles, target completion rows, and the provenance rows. This accounts for every downstream read named by the carrier.

Since no carrier coordinate names open covers, sequential compactness, a compact-open topology, or a subsequence selector, the local naming certificate cannot export those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Arzela-Ascoli equicontinuity net handoff]
\label{thm:arzela-ascoli-equicontinuity-net-handoff}
Every compact-continuity consumer reading an accepted $\ArzelaAscoliUp$ packet obtains a finite handoff: one displayed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ source net from $\CompactMetricUp$, equicontinuity modulus rows over the $\ContinuousMapUp$ family ledger, pointwise totally bounded image rows in the $\CompleteMetricUp$ target, and the transported $\Cont$ and $\Pkg$ provenance tying those rows to the local $\NameCert_{\ArzelaAscoliUp}$ certificate.

The handoff supplies finite net data for a constructive Arzela--Ascoli argument and does not supply open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, an ambient compact-open topology, or a choice-selected subsequence.
\end{theorem}

Read the family coordinate $F$ together with the equicontinuity ledger $E$. For a requested target tolerance, the ledger selects source-radius rows uniformly over the displayed family entries and routes the corresponding continuous-map modulus reads through the same finite net.

Next project the pointwise image coordinate $I$ and the image-bundle coordinate $B$. At each bundled source-net point, the target values of the displayed family are covered by finite totally bounded image rows and by target metric distance witnesses read inside the $\CompleteMetricUp$ target.

Finally attach the finite-modulus coordinate $M$ and the provenance rows $H,C,P,N$. \begin{proof}

Then combine \autoref{thm:arzela-ascoli-non-escape-obligation} with \autoref{thm:arzela-ascoli-equicontinuity-net-handoff}.
