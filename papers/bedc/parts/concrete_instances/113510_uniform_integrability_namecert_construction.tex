\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformIntegrabilityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-integrability-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Dunford and Schwartz, the $\UniformIntegrabilityUp$ packet records the finite tail-control surface that sits between dominated convergence and Vitali convergence. It reads the measure and integral rows used by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dominated-convergence-namecert}, exposes a family-indexed tail cutoff and uniform tail-bound ledger, and then supplies the uniform-integrability row consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vitali-convergence-namecert}. The packet is a finite BEDC carrier for tail budgets; it is not measure-theoretic choice, full dominated convergence, ambient integration completeness, quotient almost-everywhere equality, or a selected representative theorem.

\begin{definition}[Uniform integrability carrier]
\label{def:uniform-integrability-carrier}
A $\UniformIntegrabilityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(M,I,F,T,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MeasureUp$ source row for the displayed measurable-domain and measurable-set requests.
\item $I$ is the $\IntegralUp$ readback row through which tail integrals and budget comparisons are inspected.
\item $F$ is the finite family row naming the integrands whose tails are being controlled.
\item $T$ is the tail-cutoff row, assigning each displayed tolerance request a finite cutoff window.
\item $B$ is the uniform tail-bound row, recording the shared bound read after $M,I,F,T$ have been exposed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,I,F,T,B$ and the local structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a tail-budget request through the cutoff and bound rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformIntegrabilityUp$, $\MeasureUp$, $\IntegralUp$, $\DominatedConvergenceUp$, $\VitaliConvergenceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformIntegrabilityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for countable choice of representatives, ambient measure completion, full dominated convergence, global integration completeness, quotient almost-everywhere equality, or a detached Vitali theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform integrability NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-integrability-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformIntegrabilityUp$ carrier $U=(M,I,F,T,B,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformIntegrabilityUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the measure row, integral row, displayed family row, tail-cutoff row, uniform tail-bound row, transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row. The cutoff and bound obligations are read only through $T$ and $B$ after $M,I,F$ have been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:uniform-integrability-carrier}. The analytic rows are exactly $M,I,F,T,B$, and the structural rows are exactly $H,C,P,N$.

Tail control is the ordered read through $T$ and $B$. The cutoff row answers the displayed tolerance request, and the uniform bound row records the shared tail estimate after the same measure, integral, and family rows have already been exposed.

The structural rows preserve the same packet. Componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming do not add a selected representative, measure completion, host domination theorem, or integration-completeness coordinate. Hence the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform integrability Vitali consumption]
\label{thm:uniform-integrability-vitali-consumption}
Every $\VitaliConvergenceUp$ consumer of a $\UniformIntegrabilityUp$ packet reads the uniform-integrability budget through the finite route
$$
M \longmapsto I \longmapsto F \longmapsto T \longmapsto B \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
When a dominated-convergence sibling route is present, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dominated-convergence-namecert} may supply the displayed majorant-side evidence, but the Vitali-facing handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vitali-convergence-namecert} still consumes only the tail-cutoff and uniform tail-bound rows listed in \autoref{def:uniform-integrability-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Vitali-facing read. By \autoref{def:uniform-integrability-carrier}, the only family-level analytic rows are $M,I,F,T,B$.

The budget visible to $\VitaliConvergenceUp$ is the tail route $T \longmapsto B$. It records finite cutoffs and a shared tail-bound ledger, not a host theorem about all measurable functions.

If a dominated-convergence handoff is also used, it is sibling evidence attached through the displayed measure, integral, and family rows. It does not replace the uniform-integrability row by full dominated convergence.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport and replay the same finite route. Since the carrier lacks measure-theoretic choice, ambient integration completeness, quotient almost-everywhere equality, and selected representatives, the Vitali consumer cannot read any of those objects from this packet.
\end{proof}
