\begin{theorem}[Unary-continuation category stability law package]
\label{thm:category-unary-continuation-stability-law-package}
For the concrete unary-continuation category on $\mathsf{BHist}$, with object
carrier the unary histories, hom-carrier
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)$, object classifier $\hsame$, and morphism
classifier $\hsame$ on displayed hom histories, the $\CategoryUp$ stability
laws are satisfied. At every unary object the empty history is an identity
hom-carrier, composition of displayed hom-carriers is closed, any two
associations of a threefold displayed composite classify together, the left and
right empty-composition laws hold under the morphism classifier, and morphism
classification is preserved after endpoint $\hsame$ transport.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The identity and binary composition fields are the concrete hom-carrier laws.
The empty history is an identity hom-carrier at each unary object by
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-empty-identity}, and displayed composition
closure is exactly \autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-comp-closed}.

For associativity, use the two-step closure supplied by
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-comp-assoc-closed}. It builds both
associated composites as hom-carriers over the same endpoints and identifies
their morphism histories by the classifier $\hsame$.

The unit laws are supplied by the identity square. Applying
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-identity-square-closed} to a displayed
hom-carrier and the two empty-composition ledgers gives the left and right
unit composites and the required morphism-classifier equality.

It remains to record stability under endpoint transport. Transporting the
source endpoint, target endpoint, and morphism history along $\hsame$ keeps the
displayed hom-carrier witness by
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-hsame-transport}; once the endpoints have
been transported, any two displayed morphism witnesses over classified
endpoints are identified by
\autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-endpoint-hsame-morphism-deterministic}.
\end{proof}
