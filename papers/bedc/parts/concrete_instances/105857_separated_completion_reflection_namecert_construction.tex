Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\SeparatedCompletionReflectionUp$ packet records the finite reflection boundary where separated zero-distance evidence is allowed to feed completion uniqueness. It sits between the separated metric classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the complete-limit rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the metric distance surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the regular-Cauchy comparison route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequenceequivalence-namecert}. The packet names a reflection route for completion consumers without importing quotient completion, selected representatives, trichotomy, countable choice, or a claim that $\RealUp$ is complete.

\begin{definition}[SeparatedCompletionReflection carrier]
\label{def:separated-completion-reflection-carrier}
A $\SeparatedCompletionReflectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(M,S,C,E,Z,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the source $\MetricUp$ row, including the point classifier and the metric-distance witness surface that completion reads may inspect.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ zero-distance classifier row, read only after the metric distance witness has been displayed.
\item $C$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ pair of completion-limit rows for a single regular Cauchy point name, including the displayed limit modulus and convergence-distance ledgers.
\item $E$ is the $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$ comparison route binding the two completion-limit reads to the same finite regular-Cauchy window data.
\item $Z$ is the $\RealUp$ zero-distance read of the metric-distance witness between the two displayed completion limits.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,S,C,E,Z,K,P,N$.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from finite Cauchy windows to completion limits, from those limits to a metric-distance witness, and from the real zero-distance row to the separated classifier.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SeparatedCompletionReflectionUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SeparatedCompletionReflectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a reflection surface: it records how already displayed separated zero-distance evidence is consumed by completion uniqueness. It has no quotient-completion object, no representative selector, no trichotomy row, no countable-choice schedule, and no ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedCompletionReflection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:separated-completion-reflection-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SeparatedCompletionReflectionUp$ carrier $R=(M,S,C,E,Z,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SeparatedCompletionReflectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the $\MetricUp$ source row, the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ zero-distance classifier, the paired $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit rows, the $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$ comparison route, the $\RealUp$ zero-distance read, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item completion-window exactness, requiring the two completion-limit reads in $C$ to be compared only through the finite-window route $E$ before any endpoint classifier is inspected;
\item zero-distance exactness, requiring the metric-distance witness from $M$ to pass through the $\RealUp$ row $Z$ before the separated classifier $S$ is consumed;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $\hsame$ transport through $H$ and $\Cont$ replay through $K$ to preserve the same order $E,M,Z,S$ under local naming;
\item ledger visibility, requiring $P$ and $N$ to expose only the displayed rows and to refuse quotient completion, selected representatives, trichotomy, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:separated-completion-reflection-carrier}. Its fields give exactly the metric source, separated classifier, complete-limit pair, Cauchy comparison route, real zero-distance read, and structural support rows named in the first obligation.

For completion-window exactness, read $C$ through $E$. The two limit rows are thereby tied to the same regular-Cauchy window data before the metric-distance witness is formed, so no quotient completion or selected representative can supply an alternate comparison.

For zero-distance exactness, the metric row $M$ supplies the distance witness, $Z$ reads that witness at zero through the $\RealUp$ seal, and only then may $S$ export the separated classifier. The transport and replay rows $H,K$ preserve this same order componentwise.

The final obligation is the provenance inventory. Since $P$ and $N$ name only the rows listed in the carrier, the local certificate cannot expose trichotomy, countable choice, a quotient completion carrier, selected representatives, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedCompletionReflection route]
\label{thm:separated-completion-reflection-route}
Every completion-uniqueness consumer of an accepted $\SeparatedCompletionReflectionUp$ packet factors through the displayed route
$$
\CompleteMetricUp,\ \CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp,\ \MetricUp,\ \RealUp,\ \SeparatedMetricUp,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
Concretely, the consumer must first read the paired complete-limit rows $C$, then the finite-window comparison route $E$, then the metric-distance witness from $M$, then the $\RealUp$ zero-distance row $Z$, and only afterward the separated classifier row $S$ with support from $H,K,P,N$. The exported conclusion is completion uniqueness as a local separated-classifier route, not quotient completion equality, a selected limit, host equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the paired $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit rows in $C$. The comparison route $E$ binds them to the same regular Cauchy point name through finite windows before any endpoint comparison is available.

Next form the displayed metric-distance witness using $M$. The real-valued zero-distance read $Z$ is then the only row that turns that witness into data consumable by a separated classifier.

Finally apply the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ row $S$ and carry the result through $H,K,P,N$. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming do not add analytic coordinates; they only preserve and name the displayed route. Since the carrier contains no quotient-completion, selected-representative, trichotomy, countable-choice, or ambient-completeness field, the consumer route has no escape from the stated boundary.
\end{proof}
