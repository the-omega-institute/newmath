\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FinitePartitionMeshUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finite-partition-mesh-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive partitions of real intervals, the $\FinitePartitionMeshUp$ packet records the finite dyadic mesh surface used before interval estimates may pass to Darboux or regular-completion consumers. It sits between the real endpoint seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite window discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the closed bounded interval Darboux partition route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-bounded-interval-darboux-partition-namecert}, and the finite Darboux-sum boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-darboux-sum-namecert}. The packet names only displayed endpoint rows, dyadic breakpoints, mesh adjacency, refinement witnesses, and downstream handoff rows; it is not a selected tagged partition, an open-cover compactness theorem, a quotient equality of functions, or an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness assertion.

\begin{definition}[Finite partition mesh carrier]
\label{def:finite-partition-mesh-carrier}
A $\FinitePartitionMeshUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(I,D,W,Q,M,R,B,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\RealUp$ closed-interval endpoint row, exposing the lower endpoint, upper endpoint, endpoint order, and terminal real seal used by the mesh.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ breakpoint ledger naming the dyadic cut points and the dyadic mesh bound.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window family in which endpoint and breakpoint reads are inspected.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the displayed endpoints and dyadic breakpoints.
\item $M$ is the finite mesh row listing the cells, endpoint adjacency, left-to-right order, and boundary inclusion witnesses.
\item $R$ is the displayed common-refinement row comparing $M$ with any accepted finite dyadic submesh over the same interval.
\item $B$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalDarbouxPartitionUp$ handoff row reached only after $I,D,W,Q,M,R$ have been displayed.
\item $S$ is the $\DarbouxSumUp$ consumer row for finite lower and upper sum reads over the cells of $M$ or its displayed refinement.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport of interval endpoints, dyadic breakpoints, windows, readbacks, mesh cells, refinement witnesses, Darboux handoff, sum rows, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from endpoint rows to dyadic breakpoints, from breakpoints to mesh cells, and from mesh refinement to Darboux partition and sum handoffs.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FinitePartitionMeshUp$, $\RealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalDarbouxPartitionUp$, $\DarbouxSumUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FinitePartitionMeshUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an infinite partition, a selected tag family, quotient function equality, measure completion, open-cover compactness, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness beyond the displayed endpoint seal.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite partition mesh refinement obligations]
\label{thm:finite-partition-mesh-refinement-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-partition-mesh-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\FinitePartitionMeshUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $K=(I,D,W,Q,M,R,B,S,H,C,P,N)$; interval endpoint exposure through $I$; dyadic breakpoint and mesh-bound exposure through $D$; finite observation windows through $W$; regular rational readback through $Q$; mesh-cell exposure through $M$; common-refinement evidence through $R$; closed bounded interval Darboux partition handoff through $B$; finite Darboux-sum handoff through $S$; componentwise transport through $H$; displayed continuation replay through $C$; provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.

Every accepted refinement request must therefore route a $\RealUp$ interval consumer, a $\ClosedBoundedIntervalDarbouxPartitionUp$ consumer, or a $\DarbouxSumUp$ consumer through the finite path
$$
I\longmapsto D\longmapsto W\longmapsto Q\longmapsto M\longmapsto R\longmapsto B\longmapsto S\longmapsto(H,C,P,N)
$$
before any lower-sum, upper-sum, or interval estimate can be read. The route exports no selected tags, quotient stream, host partition predicate, countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness row, measure-completion object, or ambient real-completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed packet. The interval row $I$, dyadic ledger $D$, finite windows $W$, regular readback $Q$, and mesh row $M$ are all finite certificate rows, so the carrier exposes a bounded list of cells before a refinement witness can be inspected.

Read the refinement row next. Since $R$ compares mesh cells only through the already displayed endpoint, breakpoint, window, and readback rows, every common refinement remains a finite dyadic submesh over the same interval boundary.

Finally inspect the Darboux handoff rows. The rows $B$ and $S$ are downstream of the finite mesh and its refinement witness, while $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name that route. The excluded tag, quotient, choice, compactness, measure, and ambient-completeness objects have no carrier coordinate, so the listed rows exhaust the refinement obligations.
\end{proof}
