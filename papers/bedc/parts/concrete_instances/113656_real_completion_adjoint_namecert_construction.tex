\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealCompletionAdjointUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-completion-adjoint-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\RealCompletionAdjointUp$ packet records the finite universal-property surface by which regular rational Cauchy data enters the Real-facing completion seal and by which Real-facing maps are read back as regular-Cauchy extensions. It sits over the regular rational source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the finite reflection route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-real-reflection-namecert}, and the adjacent Bishop completion-adjoint packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-real-completion-adjoint-namecert}. The certificate is a displayed adjoint boundary, not a quotient-completion theorem, selected-limit construction, or host equality principle.

\begin{definition}[Real completion adjoint carrier]
\label{def:real-completion-adjoint-carrier}
A $\RealCompletionAdjointUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(D,S,Q,R,E,U,X,K,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the dense-unit row naming the finite unit access from rational data into the completion-facing route.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row used by every unit, reflection, and extension read.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-Cauchy source row.
\item $R$ is the $\RegularCauchyRealReflectionUp$ row that exposes the route from $Q$ and $S$ to the Real-facing seal.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, visible only after $D,S,Q,R$ have been projected.
\item $U$ is the extension row recording the finite continuation by which a Real-facing target reads a regular-Cauchy source through the unit and reflection rows.
\item $X$ is the uniqueness row: two displayed extensions agreeing on the dense-unit and regular-Cauchy source rows are identified only by componentwise certificate data.
\item $K$ records local compatibility between the dense-unit row, the reflection row, the extension row, and the uniqueness row.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $D,S,Q,R,E,U,X,K,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
D,S,Q \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto U,X,K,H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealCompletionAdjointUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RegularCauchyRealReflectionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionAdjointUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The accepted carrier is exactly the displayed packet $A$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a selected limit, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion adjoint NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-completion-adjoint-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealCompletionAdjointUp$ carrier $A=(D,S,Q,R,E,U,X,K,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionAdjointUp}$ obligations are exhausted by carrier admission for the dense unit, finite stream windows, regular-Cauchy source, Real reflection route, terminal Real seal, extension row, uniqueness row, local compatibility, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. No obligation may call on quotient stream equality, a selected representative, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient Real completeness, or an external adjunction theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:real-completion-adjoint-carrier}. The visible analytic rows are $D,S,Q,R,E,U,X,K$, and the visible structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

Read the source side first. The dense-unit row $D$ and finite windows $S$ expose the rational access data, while $Q$ supplies the regular-Cauchy source. The reflection row $R$ is then the only route from that source to the terminal Real seal $E$.

Read the target side next. The extension row $U$ consumes the same displayed unit and reflection route, and the uniqueness row $X$ compares two such extensions only through componentwise displayed data. The compatibility row $K$ records that the unit, reflection, extension, and uniqueness rows share the same finite source route.

Finally transport and replay by $H$ and $C$, and bind provenance and naming by $P,N$. Since no excluded analytic coordinate appears in the carrier, the local certificate has no remaining place to import one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion adjoint reflection]
\label{thm:real-completion-adjoint-reflection}
Let $A=(D,S,Q,R,E,U,X,K,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RealCompletionAdjointUp$ carrier. Every Real-facing read exported by $A$ factors through the displayed finite route
$$
D,S,Q \longmapsto R \longmapsto E .
$$
The extension row $U$ and uniqueness row $X$ may replay this route, but they cannot bypass the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows, or the $\RegularCauchyRealReflectionUp$ reflection row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the source projection. The carrier exposes the dense unit, finite windows, and regular-Cauchy source as $D,S,Q$, so any Real-facing read has an explicit finite source spine.

Apply the reflection row $R$. By construction it is the only row that turns the regular-Cauchy source and finite windows into a terminal $\RealUp$ seal. The seal row $E$ is therefore downstream of $D,S,Q,R$.

Now inspect $U$ and $X$. The extension row reads the same reflected source, and the uniqueness row compares extensions only after that read has occurred. Componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve this order, so no accepted route can jump directly to the Real seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion adjoint uniqueness]
\label{thm:real-completion-adjoint-uniqueness}
Let $A=(D,S,Q,R,E,U,X,K,H,C,P,N)$ and $A'=(D',S',Q',R',E',U',X',K',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\RealCompletionAdjointUp$ carriers. If the dense-unit, finite-window, regular-Cauchy source, reflection, Real seal, extension, compatibility, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows are componentwise $\hsame$, then the uniqueness rows $X$ and $X'$ expose the same completion-adjoint comparison. The comparison is certificate-local and introduces no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, choice schedule, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both carriers and match the source rows. The componentwise $\hsame$ assumptions identify the dense unit, finite windows, regular-Cauchy source, reflection route, and Real seal as displayed certificate data.

Match the extension rows next. Since $U$ and $U'$ read the same reflected source route, their comparison is already constrained by the displayed rows $D,S,Q,R,E$ and their primed counterparts.

The uniqueness rows $X$ and $X'$ therefore have only one local comparison surface: the componentwise certificate comparison replayed through $K,H,C,P,N$ and $K',H',C',P',N'$. Because neither carrier contains an excluded analytic row, the uniqueness comparison remains internal to the NameCert packet.
\end{proof}
