\begin{definition}[Contraction mapping theorem carrier]
\label{def:contraction-mapping-theorem-carrier}

$$

\item $M$ is the metric-distance row, exposing only the finite distance comparisons admitted by the $\MetricUp$ surface.
\item $D$ is the $\ContractiveMapUp$ row, recording the self-map graph, contraction constant ledger, and one-step distance decrease over the same metric source.

\item $L$ is the fixed-point uniqueness ledger, comparing two endpoint reads through the same metric-distance row and the contraction decrease route before local equality is exported.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ContractionMappingTheoremUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}

\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Contraction mapping theorem NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:contraction-mapping-theorem-namecert-obligations}

$$

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The fixed-point equation row $U$ and uniqueness ledger $L$ are downstream rows. They compare only endpoint reads that have traversed $C,M,D,A,O$ and then preserve that route through the structural rows $H,T,P,N$.

Thus the NameCert obligations are precisely the displayed finite route. No carrier coordinate supplies a host fixed-point theorem, quotient completion equality, selected representative, unrestricted choice, trichotomy, or open-cover compactness.
\end{proof}
