$$

with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ source row, including the displayed metric carrier, completion handoff, and finite tolerance readback surface.
\item $C$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit-existence row, recording that every accepted regular Cauchy point name exposes a displayed limit, modulus, and convergence-distance ledger.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ zero-distance identity row, recording that two displayed carried observations with real zero distance are identified by the point classifier.
\item $E$ is the $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$ boundary row by which two Cauchy presentations of the same completion-facing point are compared before any limit equality is read.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ zero-distance readback row used to consume metric-distance witnesses only through the terminal real-name seal.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, complete-limit row, separatedness row, Cauchy-equivalence boundary, real readback, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.

\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

\begin{theorem}[CompleteSeparatedMetric NameCert obligations]\label{thm:complete-separated-metric-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CompleteSeparatedMetricUp$ carrier $Q=(X,C,S,E,R,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompleteSeparatedMetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the $\MetricCompletionUp$ source row, $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit-existence row, $\SeparatedMetricUp$ zero-distance identity row, $\CauchySequenceEquivalenceUp$ boundary row, $\RealUp$ readback row, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item classifier stability, stating that the accepted classifier route is preserved by the $\hsame$ transports in $H$ and replayed by $K$ through the same finite rows;
\item completion handoff, stating that any completion-facing consumer first reads the source completion row $X$ and the complete-limit row $C$ before seeing a displayed limit;
\item separated uniqueness, stating that two displayed limits for the same Cauchy presentation are compared through $E$, read as zero through $R$, and then identified only through the separatedness row $S$;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that the packet exports no quotient completion object, no selected representative, no countable-choice schedule, no host equality of completion points, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:complete-separated-metric-carrier}. The tuple lists the completion source, complete-limit witness, separated identity route, Cauchy-equivalence boundary, real zero-distance readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Classifier stability follows from the componentwise transport row $H$ together with the replay row $K$. Transport moves each displayed row to a row of the same kind, and replay consumes the transported rows in the same order as the accepted classifier route.

Completion handoff is the role of $X$ and $C$. A consumer cannot inspect a limit until the metric-completion source has exposed its completion-facing carrier and the complete-metric row has exposed a displayed limit witness for the regular Cauchy presentation.

Separated uniqueness uses the equivalence boundary $E$ to compare two presentations before the limit comparison is read. The real readback row $R$ supplies the zero-distance observation, and $S$ is the only exported bridge from zero distance to classifier identity.

The ledger statement is an inventory check on the rows of $Q$. None of the displayed coordinates is a quotient completion class, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, host equality proof, or ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{proof}
