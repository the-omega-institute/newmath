Following Kelley and Bishop--Bridges compact-open and uniform convergence arguments, the $\CompactOpenUniformConvergenceUp$ packet records the finite BEDC route by which compact-open function-space rows, compact-metric probes, equicontinuity ledgers, and uniform-modulus rows are read as one convergence surface. It sits beside the compact-open function-space carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-open-function-space-namecert}, the compact-open topology surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-open-topology-namecert}, the compact-metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, and the equicontinuity family route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-equicontinuity-namecert}. The packet is a finite convergence carrier for compact-window consumers; it does not assert host function equality, arbitrary open-cover compactness, selected subnet convergence, quotient maps, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Compact-open uniform convergence carrier]
\label{def:compact-open-uniform-convergence-carrier}
A $\CompactOpenUniformConvergenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(F,K,E,Q,M,G,D,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the $\CompactOpenFunctionSpaceUp$ source row, exposing the finite function-space window to be tested.
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ compact probe row that supplies the displayed compact window.
\item $E$ is the $\EquicontinuityUp$ family row over the same compact probe.
\item $Q$ is the $\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp$ row, recording the shared compact-window radius schedule.
\item $M$ is the $\UniformModulusUp$ convergence threshold consumed by the downstream convergence read.
\item $G$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row for the displayed maps on the compact probe.
\item $D$ is the $\MetricUp$ and $\RealUp$ target-distance ledger used to compare displayed values.
\item $R$ is the compact-open replay row, requiring every compact-window convergence read to pass through $F,K,E,Q,M,G,D$ before any public handoff.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $F,K,E,Q,M,G,D,R,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from compact-open requests through the same finite convergence route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactOpenUniformConvergenceUp$, $\CompactOpenFunctionSpaceUp$, $\CompactOpenTopologyUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\EquicontinuityUp$, $\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactOpenUniformConvergenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host equality of functions, arbitrary compact-open topology construction, selected subnets, quotient maps, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact-open uniform convergence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-open-uniform-convergence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactOpenUniformConvergenceUp$ carrier
$$
U=(F,K,E,Q,M,G,D,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CompactOpenUniformConvergenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the compact-open function-space row, compact-metric probe row, equicontinuity row, uniform-equicontinuity modulus row, uniform-modulus threshold row, continuous-map graph row, metric and real target-distance row, compact-open replay row, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, provenance, and local naming.

Every compact-open convergence consumer must read the compact-window probe $K$ and the shared modulus rows $Q,M$ before reading the target-distance ledger $D$ or the handoff row $R$. No obligation in the packet is discharged by host function equality, selected subnet convergence, quotient map equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:compact-open-uniform-convergence-carrier}. The tuple lists the compact-open source, compact probe, equicontinuity, uniform-modulus, continuous-map, metric, real, replay, transport, provenance, and naming rows.

The compact-window order is fixed by the visible route. A consumer first reads $F$ through the compact probe $K$, then reads the equicontinuity row $E$ and shared modulus rows $Q,M$ before any target-distance comparison in $D$ is available.

The replay row $R$ packages that order for compact-open convergence consumers. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite route by componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming.

Since the carrier contains no coordinate for the excluded host equality, subnet, quotient, open-cover, or completeness data, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact-open uniform convergence finite-net handoff]
\label{thm:compact-open-uniform-convergence-finite-net-handoff}
Every compact-open topology or Arzela--Ascoli-style consumer of an accepted $\CompactOpenUniformConvergenceUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
\CompactOpenFunctionSpaceUp \longrightarrow \CompactMetricUp \longrightarrow \EquicontinuityUp \longrightarrow \UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp \longrightarrow \UniformModulusUp \longrightarrow \ContinuousMapUp \longrightarrow \MetricUp \longrightarrow \RealUp .
$$
The handoff exports only the displayed compact-window convergence surface, with the shared modulus and target-distance rows preserved by $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:compact-open-uniform-convergence-namecert-obligations}. They force the compact-open consumer to read the compact-window probe, equicontinuity row, shared modulus rows, continuous-map graph row, and target-distance ledger before the replay row.

Next read the finite compact source through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert} and the equicontinuity route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-equicontinuity-namecert}. These sibling surfaces supply finite probes and shared modulus data rather than open-cover compactness or selected subnets.

Finally $R,H,C,P,N$ repack the displayed route for compact-open topology and function-space consumers. The carrier contains no host function equality, quotient map, selected subnet, or ambient completeness coordinate, so the public handoff is exactly the stated finite route.
\end{proof}
