$$

\item $Q$ is the $\FormalBallBasisUp$ row, exposing the basis elements and basis-refinement route inspected by the domain surface.
\item $I$ is the directed-ideal row, listing the finite compatible formal-ball windows retained by the rounded domain read.
\item $C$ is the $\FormalBallCompletionUp$ row, reached only after the formal-ball, radius-order, basis, and directed-ideal rows have been displayed.
\item $E$ is the completion endpoint row, read through $\MetricCompletionUp$ and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal rather than through host point equality.

\begin{theorem}[FormalBallDomain NameCert obligations]\label{thm:formal-ball-domain-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FormalBallDomainUp$ carrier $D=(B,R,Q,I,C,E,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FormalBallDomainUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the formal-ball row, radius-order row, basis row, directed-ideal row, formal-ball-completion row, endpoint row, transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item radius-order exactness, requiring every refinement or rounded containment used by the domain read to be recorded in $R$ before any ideal or completion row is consumed;
\item basis coverage, requiring each retained formal-ball window to be inspected through $Q$ and then recorded in the directed row $I$;
\item completion handoff, requiring $C$ and $E$ to remain downstream of $B,R,Q,I$ and to expose only the displayed $\FormalBallCompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$ route;
\item structural stability, requiring $H$ and $T$ to preserve the same component rows under $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay;
\item provenance and local naming through $P$ and $N$, with no obligation row for quotient completion, selected representatives, host point equality, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No $\FormalBallDomainUp$ obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:formal-ball-domain-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the formal-ball, radius-order, basis, directed-ideal, completion, endpoint, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

Radius-order exactness and basis coverage are forced by the row order. A domain read must first inspect $B$ through the finite comparisons in $R$, then use $Q$ to identify the displayed basis route before the retained directed row $I$ is available.

The completion handoff is downstream of those rows. The formal-ball-completion row $C$ and endpoint row $E$ can be read only after $B,R,Q,I$ have been displayed, so they cannot supply a quotient-completion representative or host point equality on their own.

Finally $H$ and $T$ transport and replay the same finite packet, while $P$ and $N$ source and name it. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected representatives, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
