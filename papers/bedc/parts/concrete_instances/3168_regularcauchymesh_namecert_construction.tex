\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-mesh-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$ packet records the shared finite mesh surface used by regular-Cauchy Real-completeness arguments before a diagonal mesh-budget consumer reads a $\RealUp$ seal. It names a finite family of regular rational observations indexed by mesh rows, together with StreamName windows, dyadic tolerance rows, transport, continuation, provenance, and a local naming certificate. The packet is a mesh-indexed observation carrier, not a completed real, a quotient class of regular sequences, or an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy mesh carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(a,j,w,r,d,m,e,h,c,p,n)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $a$ is the finite regular-Cauchy request row, recording the requested precision and the finite observation sites to be inspected;
\item $j$ is the mesh-index row, listing the finite $\DyadicMeshUp$ cells and regular-Cauchy positions selected by the request;
\item $w$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row attached to those mesh cells;
\item $r$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the selected regular rational observations;
\item $d$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, recording dyadic radii, endpoint comparisons, and mesh-cell tolerance bounds;
\item $m$ is the $\DyadicMeshUp$ compatibility row that states the selected mesh cells are exactly the cells inspected by $j$ and $w$;
\item $e$ is the $\RealUp$ handoff row reached only after $a,j,w,r,d,m$ have been displayed;
\item $h$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the request, mesh indices, windows, readbacks, dyadic ledger, mesh compatibility row, handoff row, provenance, and local name;
\item $c$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $a$ to $j$, from $j$ to $w$, from $w$ to $r$, from $r$ to $d$, from $d$ to $m$, and from $m$ to $e$;
\item $p$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyMeshUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no infinite mesh, no quotient equality of regular sequences, no host real equality, no chosen limit representative, and no ambient Real completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy mesh classifier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-classifier}
Two $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$ carriers
$$
G=(a,j,w,r,d,m,e,h,c,p,n)
\quad\text{and}\quad
G'=(a',j',w',r',d',m',e',h',c',p',n')
$$
are classified together exactly when the displayed request rows, mesh-index rows, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readbacks, DyadicRatCore ledgers, DyadicMesh compatibility rows, RealUp handoff rows, componentwise transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local NameCert rows are matched by the corresponding $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ evidence already carried in the two packets.

The classifier is rowwise and finite. It does not identify completed reals, quotient regular-Cauchy streams, or mesh families outside the displayed cells.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy mesh NameCert obligation sketch]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-mesh-namecert-obligation-sketch}
The local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyMeshUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-carrier} has the following five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $G=(a,j,w,r,d,m,e,h,c,p,n)$;
\item finite mesh request exposure through $a$ and mesh-index exposure through $j$;
\item selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows $w$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks $r$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $d$, and $\DyadicMeshUp$ compatibility row $m$;
\item $\RealUp$ handoff through $e$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $h$, and displayed $\Cont$ route replay through $c$;
\item $\Pkg$ provenance through $p$, local naming through $n$, and non-escape for infinite meshes, quotient regular-Cauchy equality, host real equality, selected limits, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RegularCauchyMeshUp.RegularCauchyMeshTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-carrier}. The packet exposes the eleven displayed rows and no other mesh-indexed data.

The request and mesh-index rows $a$ and $j$ choose only finitely many cells. The selected windows $w$, readbacks $r$, dyadic ledger $d$, and mesh compatibility row $m$ then give the finite regular-rational observations attached to those cells.

The handoff row $e$ is reached through the displayed transports and continuations in $h$ and $c$, with provenance and local naming recorded by $p$ and $n$. Thus the five rows exhaust the NameCert surface.

The non-escape clauses follow from the carrier inventory. Since no row supplies an infinite mesh, quotient regular-Cauchy equality, host real equality, a selected limiting representative, or an ambient completeness theorem, none of those objects can be exported by the certificate.
\end{proof}
