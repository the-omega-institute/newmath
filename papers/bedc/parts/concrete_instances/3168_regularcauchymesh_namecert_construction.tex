\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-mesh-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$ packet records the shared finite mesh surface used by regular-Cauchy Real-completeness arguments before a diagonal mesh-budget consumer reads a $\RealUp$ seal. It names a finite family of regular rational observations indexed by mesh rows, together with StreamName windows, dyadic tolerance rows, transport, continuation, provenance, and a local naming certificate. The packet is a mesh-indexed observation carrier, not a completed real, a quotient class of regular sequences, or an ambient completeness principle.

The concrete dependency route is through the finite modulus-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchywitnessschedule-namecert} and the mesh-cell compatibility surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicmesh-namecert}. The $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$ packet refines the schedule packet by replacing a single selected window with a finite mesh-index row while preserving the same $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$ handoff, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy mesh carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(a,j,w,r,d,m,e,h,c,p,n)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $a$ is the finite regular-Cauchy request row, recording the requested precision and the finite observation sites to be inspected;
\item $j$ is the mesh-index row, listing the finite $\DyadicMeshUp$ cells and regular-Cauchy positions selected by the request;
\item $w$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row attached to those mesh cells;
\item $r$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the selected regular rational observations;
\item $d$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, recording dyadic radii, endpoint comparisons, and mesh-cell tolerance bounds;
\item $m$ is the $\DyadicMeshUp$ compatibility row that states the selected mesh cells are exactly the cells inspected by $j$ and $w$;
\item $e$ is the $\RealUp$ handoff row reached only after $a,j,w,r,d,m$ have been displayed;
\item $h$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the request, mesh indices, windows, readbacks, dyadic ledger, mesh compatibility row, handoff row, provenance, and local name;
\item $c$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $a$ to $j$, from $j$ to $w$, from $w$ to $r$, from $r$ to $d$, from $d$ to $m$, and from $m$ to $e$;
\item $p$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyMeshUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no infinite mesh, no quotient equality of regular sequences, no host real equality, no chosen limit representative, and no ambient Real completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy mesh classifier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-classifier}
Two $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$ carriers
$$
G=(a,j,w,r,d,m,e,h,c,p,n)
\quad\text{and}\quad
G'=(a',j',w',r',d',m',e',h',c',p',n')
$$
are classified together exactly when the displayed request rows, mesh-index rows, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readbacks, DyadicRatCore ledgers, DyadicMesh compatibility rows, RealUp handoff rows, componentwise transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local NameCert rows are matched by the corresponding $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ evidence already carried in the two packets.

The classifier is rowwise and finite. It does not identify completed reals, quotient regular-Cauchy streams, or mesh families outside the displayed cells.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy mesh NameCert obligation sketch]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-mesh-namecert-obligation-sketch}
The local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyMeshUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-carrier} has the following five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $G=(a,j,w,r,d,m,e,h,c,p,n)$;
\item finite mesh request exposure through $a$ and mesh-index exposure through $j$;
\item selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows $w$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks $r$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $d$, and $\DyadicMeshUp$ compatibility row $m$;
\item $\RealUp$ handoff through $e$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $h$, and displayed $\Cont$ route replay through $c$;
\item $\Pkg$ provenance through $p$, local naming through $n$, and non-escape for infinite meshes, quotient regular-Cauchy equality, host real equality, selected limits, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RegularCauchyMeshUp.RegularCauchyMeshTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-carrier}. The packet exposes the eleven displayed rows and no other mesh-indexed data.

The request and mesh-index rows $a$ and $j$ choose only finitely many cells. The selected windows $w$, readbacks $r$, dyadic ledger $d$, and mesh compatibility row $m$ then give the finite regular-rational observations attached to those cells.

The handoff row $e$ is reached through the displayed transports and continuations in $h$ and $c$, with provenance and local naming recorded by $p$ and $n$. Thus the five rows exhaust the NameCert surface.

The non-escape clauses follow from the carrier inventory. Since no row supplies an infinite mesh, quotient regular-Cauchy equality, host real equality, a selected limiting representative, or an ambient completeness theorem, none of those objects can be exported by the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy mesh displayed submesh restriction]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-displayed-submesh-restriction}
Let
$$
G=(a,j,w,r,d,m,e,h,c,p,n)
$$
be a $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$ carrier as in \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-carrier}. A displayed finite submesh selector for $G$ is a finite row $q$ equipped with an inclusion read $\iota:q\to j$ whose cells are entries already displayed in the mesh-index row $j$.

The restriction of $G$ along $q$ is the packet
$$
G\vert_q=(a\vert_q,j\vert_q,w\vert_q,r\vert_q,d\vert_q,m\vert_q,e\vert_q,h\vert_q,c\vert_q,p\vert_q,n\vert_q),
$$
where $j\vert_q$ is the row $q$, and every other coordinate is obtained by retaining exactly the entries of the corresponding row of $G$ whose mesh index lies in the image of $\iota$. The handoff row $e\vert_q$, transport row $h\vert_q$, continuation row $c\vert_q$, provenance row $p\vert_q$, and local naming row $n\vert_q$ retain only the components whose source rows are retained by $a\vert_q,j\vert_q,w\vert_q,r\vert_q,d\vert_q,m\vert_q$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy mesh finite submesh restriction]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-mesh-finite-submesh-restriction}
Let
$$
G=(a,j,w,r,d,m,e,h,c,p,n)
$$
be an accepted $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$ carrier, and let $q$ be a displayed finite submesh selector for $G$ in the sense of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-displayed-submesh-restriction}. Suppose the retained StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readbacks, DyadicRatCore tolerance rows, DyadicMesh compatibility row, $\RealUp$ handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows are exactly the rows pulled back along the inclusion read $\iota:q\to j$. Then
$$
G\vert_q=(a\vert_q,j\vert_q,w\vert_q,r\vert_q,d\vert_q,m\vert_q,e\vert_q,h\vert_q,c\vert_q,p\vert_q,n\vert_q)
$$
is an accepted $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$ carrier.

Moreover, every retained consumer read of a mesh cell in $q$ factors through the restricted rows
$$
a\vert_q,j\vert_q,w\vert_q,r\vert_q,d\vert_q,m\vert_q,e\vert_q,h\vert_q,c\vert_q,p\vert_q,n\vert_q.
$$
It cannot recover a cell of $j$ outside $q$, an infinite mesh, quotient regular-Cauchy equality, host real equality, a chosen limiting representative, or an ambient Real-completeness theorem.

The rate/modulus surface is unchanged: every retained Cauchy read uses the same displayed StreamName window row and DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger already present in $G$, restricted to $q$, and no new tail bound or completion modulus is introduced.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier through \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-carrier}. The visible data are exactly the finite request row $a$, mesh-index row $j$, window row $w$, readback row $r$, dyadic ledger $d$, mesh compatibility row $m$, handoff row $e$, transport row $h$, continuation row $c$, provenance row $p$, and local name row $n$.

The selector $q$ is finite and every entry of $q$ is read from the displayed mesh-index row $j$. Therefore $j\vert_q=q$ is a finite mesh-index row, and $a\vert_q$ is the corresponding finite request row obtained by keeping only the requests whose inspected mesh cells lie in $q$.

For each retained mesh cell, the StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore tolerance entry, and DyadicMesh compatibility entry are already rows of the original accepted packet. Pulling those rows back along $\iota$ gives $w\vert_q,r\vert_q,d\vert_q,m\vert_q$ without adding any analytic object. The mesh compatibility row remains a compatibility row because it now states the same cell-window agreement after both sides have been restricted to the same displayed submesh.

The rows $e,h,c,p,n$ are administrative over the displayed finite route. Retaining only the components whose sources survive in $a\vert_q,j\vert_q,w\vert_q,r\vert_q,d\vert_q,m\vert_q$ yields the handoff, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows $e\vert_q,h\vert_q,c\vert_q,p\vert_q,n\vert_q$. These rows have exactly the roles required by \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-carrier}, so the restricted tuple is a $\RegularCauchyMeshUp$ carrier.

Acceptance follows from the local certificate surface of \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-mesh-namecert-obligation-sketch}. Carrier admission is the restricted finite tuple, finite mesh request exposure is through $a\vert_q$ and $j\vert_q$, selected windows and readbacks are $w\vert_q$ and $r\vert_q$, the dyadic and mesh rows are $d\vert_q$ and $m\vert_q$, and the handoff, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows are $e\vert_q,h\vert_q,c\vert_q,p\vert_q,n\vert_q$.

Now let a retained consumer read a cell of $q$. In the original packet such a read factors through the displayed mesh-index row $j$, the attached window row $w$, the readback row $r$, the dyadic ledger $d$, the compatibility row $m$, and then the handoff and certificate rows. Since the cell lies in the image of $\iota$, every one of those entries is present in the restricted tuple. Hence the same read factors through the restricted rows.

No omitted cell can be recovered from this factorization, because every retained row was formed by pullback along $q\to j$ and contains no component indexed outside $q$. The non-escape clauses are inherited from the carrier inventory in \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-mesh-carrier}: neither $G$ nor $G\vert_q$ has a coordinate for an infinite mesh, quotient regular-Cauchy equality, host real equality, a chosen limiting representative, or an ambient Real-completeness theorem.
\end{proof}
