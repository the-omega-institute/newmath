The $\CompilerCompositionTraceUp$ interface packages sequential compiler composition as a finite trace over adjacent $\NameCert$ compiler graphs. It does not assert a host-level compiler category. It records the two displayed compiler packets, their shared middle certificate, the relational composite graph, classifier transport, continuation replay, associativity comparison, package provenance, and the local naming row.

\begin{definition}[CompilerCompositionTrace carrier]
\label{def:compiler-composition-trace-carrier}
A $\CompilerCompositionTraceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S,M,T,K_{SM},K_{MT},J,G,L,H,C,A,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$, $M$, and $T$ are the source, middle, and target $\NameCert$ rows consumed by the two adjacent compiler graphs;
\item $K_{SM}$ is the accepted $\CertificateCompilerUp$ packet from $S$ to $M$;
\item $K_{MT}$ is the accepted $\CertificateCompilerUp$ packet from $M$ to $T$;
\item $J$ is the middle-certificate compatibility row recording that the target endpoint of $K_{SM}$ and the source endpoint of $K_{MT}$ are the same displayed $\NameCert$ row $M$;
\item $G$ is the composite compiler graph row from $S$ to $T$, obtained by finite relational composition through a displayed middle history of $M$;
\item $L$ is the classifier-transport ledger chaining source classifier membership through $K_{SM}$, the middle row $J$, and $K_{MT}$ into target classifier membership;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the endpoint certificates, adjacent compiler packets, middle compatibility row, composite graph, classifier ledger, continuation routes, associativity row, package row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for the first graph edge, middle handoff, second graph edge, composite edge, target readback, and downstream NameCert exposure;
\item $A$ is the associativity comparison row stating that three adjacent accepted compiler packets have the same exported finite packet after either parenthesization of relational graph composition;
\item $P$ names the dependency surface $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, $\NameCertMorphismUp$, $\CertificateCompilerUp$, $\ClassifierMorphismUp$, $\KernelMorphismUp$, and $\CompilerOptimizationEndomorphismUp$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompilerCompositionTraceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a host compiler function, ambient program equality, denotational semantics, optimizer correctness outside the displayed rows, decompiler data, quotient equality, or a global categorical equality not witnessed by $A$.
\end{definition}

\label{thm:compiler-composition-trace-namecert-obligations}

$$

endpoint certificate admission, acceptance of the two adjacent $\CertificateCompilerUp$ packets, middle-certificate compatibility, composite graph formation, classifier transport, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, associativity comparison, dependency provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The packet exposes only the endpoint certificate rows $S,M,T$, the two adjacent compiler packets $K_{SM},K_{MT}$, and the middle compatibility row $J$ before the composite graph is read.

The composite graph obligation is then forced through $G$. A source-to-target edge is accepted only when it factors through a middle history already displayed by $M$ and consumed by both adjacent compiler packets. The remaining certificate data are the transport and replay rows. Componentwise $\hsame$ evidence is read from $H$, continuation compatibility from $C$, associativity from $A$, dependency provenance from $P$, and local naming from $N$. \end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompilerCompositionTrace associativity]
\label{thm:compiler-composition-trace-associativity}
Let
$$
Q_{012}=(S_0,S_1,S_2,K_{01},K_{12},J_{1},G_{02},L_{02},H_{02},C_{02},A_{02},P_{02},N_{02})
$$
and
$$
Q_{123}=(S_1,S_2,S_3,K_{12},K_{23},J_{2},G_{13},L_{13},H_{13},C_{13},A_{13},P_{13},N_{13})
$$
be accepted $\CompilerCompositionTraceUp$ packets sharing the adjacent compiler packet $K_{12}$ and middle certificate rows $S_1,S_2$. For the three accepted compiler packets $K_{01}$, $K_{12}$, and $K_{23}$, the two displayed composites
$$
(K_{23}\circ K_{12})\circ K_{01}
$$
and
$$
K_{23}\circ (K_{12}\circ K_{01})
$$
export the same finite public packet: the same source row $S_0$, target row $S_3$, relational graph through the displayed middle rows, classifier-transport ledger, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, package provenance, and local naming boundary. The equality is witnessed only by the associativity rows $A_{02}$, $A_{13}$ and the componentwise transports recorded in the corresponding $H$ and $C$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two accepted trace packets. The first trace exposes the composite from $S_0$ to $S_2$ through $S_1$, and the second exposes the composite from $S_1$ to $S_3$ through $S_2$. Their overlap is the displayed compiler packet $K_{12}$ together with the middle certificate rows that both traces read.

Form the triple graph by finite relational composition. A displayed edge from $S_0$ to $S_3$ is accepted exactly when there are middle histories in $S_1$ and $S_2$ read by $K_{01}$, $K_{12}$, and $K_{23}$ in that order. Parenthesizing the relational composition on the left or on the right does not introduce another endpoint, graph row, or classifier source; it only changes which binary trace row is consulted first.

The classifier and continuation ledgers replay the same rows in the same order. The first compiler transports source classifier membership into $S_1$, the second transports it into $S_2$, and the third transports it into $S_3$. The $\Cont$ routes in $C_{02}$ and $C_{13}$ record the same ordered handoff, while $H_{02}$ and $H_{13}$ supply the componentwise sameness comparisons needed to identify the public rows.

Finally read the associativity coordinates. The rows $A_{02}$ and $A_{13}$ state that the two parenthesized binary traces repack to the same displayed triple packet. The carrier has no coordinate for an external compiler function, semantic program equality, quotient collapse, or global category law, so the exported associativity evidence is exhausted by the displayed graph, classifier, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\end{proof}
