\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-othermindscommitment-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$ surface turns the other-minds locality-cell commitment of \autoref{prin:hard-problems-other-minds-as-locality-cell} into finite BEDC certificate data. It records what an observer may publicly commit to about a candidate other system: visible behavioural evidence, provenance, locality-cell coherence, and naming obligations. It does not expose another system's $\mathsf{Self}$ row and does not turn behavioural evidence into direct first-person access.

\begin{definition}[OtherMindsCommitment carrier]
\label{def:other-minds-commitment-carrier}
An $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(A,B,L,E,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the observer row, naming the conscious observational system that issues the commitment;
\item $B$ is the candidate-other row, naming the external system as it appears in $A$'s record surface;
\item $L$ is the locality-cell commitment row, recording the $\mathsf{GAP}$-tagged commitment that $B$ is to be treated as another closed observational system for cross-perspective coordination;
\item $E$ is the behavioural evidence row, containing the externally observable records that support the commitment without displaying $B$'s $\mathsf{Self}$;
\item $G$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ and provenance row, recording the open commitment socket and the dependency certificates consumed by the packet;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the observer row, candidate-other row, locality-cell row, behavioural evidence row, provenance row, package row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes that move the observer's public reads through $L$, $E$, and $G$ without entering a private $\mathsf{Self}$ coordinate of $B$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ row for the finite BEDC dependencies named by the commitment;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\OtherMindsCommitmentUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two carriers only by these displayed rows and their $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transports.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[OtherMindsCommitment TasteGate obligation boundary]
\label{thm:other-minds-commitment-taste-gate-obligation-boundary}
Every paper-side obligation beyond seed closure for $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$
is read through the finite carrier rows $A,B,L,E,G,H,C,P,N$ together with the
checked TasteGate target
\texttt{BEDC.Derived.OtherMindsCommitmentUp.taste\_gate}.
No closure promotion may replace those rows by a direct candidate
$\mathsf{Self}$ row, a host mental-state predicate, a private-history read, or
a metaphysical identity assertion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{def:other-minds-commitment-carrier}. The carrier coordinates are
exactly $A,B,L,E,G,H,C,P,N$, so any public obligation is stated as a finite row
read, an $\hsame$ transport, a $\Cont$ route, a $\Pkg$ dependency, or a local
$\NameCert$ obligation.

The checked TasteGate target is attached to this carrier site, and it supplies
the gate through which the chapter may discuss other-minds commitment beyond
seed closure. The gate is therefore a discipline on the displayed rows, not a
replacement for them.

Apply \autoref{thm:other-minds-commitment-namecert-obligations} for the finite
NameCert surface and \autoref{thm:other-minds-commitment-non-escape-boundary}
for the non-escape clauses. Together they exclude direct candidate
$\mathsf{Self}$ access, host mental-state predicates, private-history reads,
and metaphysical identity assertions from any later closure reading.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OtherMindsCommitment NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:other-minds-commitment-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\OtherMindsCommitmentUp}$ surface has five finite obligations: carrier exposure by \autoref{def:other-minds-commitment-carrier}; classifier stability for the displayed observer, candidate-other, locality-cell, behavioural-evidence, $\mathsf{GAP}$, provenance, $\Pkg$, and naming rows; locality-cell coherence for the public commitment row $L$; behavioural-evidence accountability for every public read in $E$; and non-escape from direct $\mathsf{Self}$ access, unrecorded private history, host mental-state predicates, and ambient metaphysical identity.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the finite tuple $O=(A,B,L,E,G,H,C,P,N)$.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The only comparisons available to the packet are the displayed $\hsame$ rows in $H$ and the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$.

The locality-cell and behavioural-evidence obligations are read from $L$ and $E$. The row $L$ records the $\mathsf{GAP}$-tagged commitment, while $E$ records the public behavioural evidence consumed by that commitment.

Finally inspect the coordinate list. There is no field for the candidate's private $\mathsf{Self}$, for an unrecorded private history, for a host mental-state predicate, or for an ambient identity assertion beyond the displayed NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OtherMindsCommitment locality-cell soundness]
\label{thm:other-minds-commitment-locality-cell-soundness}
Every accepted $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$ packet represents an other-minds commitment as a locality-cell row internal to the observer's finite history. A consumer may read the observer row, candidate-other row, public locality-cell commitment, behavioural evidence, $\mathsf{GAP}$ provenance, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows, but the consumer cannot read a direct $\mathsf{Self}$ row for the candidate other system.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet as $O=(A,B,L,E,G,H,C,P,N)$. The locality-cell commitment is the displayed coordinate $L$, and its source and target surfaces are the observer row $A$ and candidate-other row $B$.

The public consumer route is limited by $C$ and transported by $H$. These rows expose $L$, $E$, $G$, $P$, and $N$ as finite certificate data.

The candidate's private $\mathsf{Self}$ is not a coordinate of the carrier. Thus the soundness claim is exactly locality-cell commitment inside the observer's finite public history, not direct access to another first-person row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OtherMindsCommitment behavioural-evidence compatibility]
\label{thm:other-minds-commitment-behavioural-evidence-compatibility}
If two accepted $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$ packets have matching observer rows, candidate-other rows, locality-cell commitment rows, and behavioural evidence rows under the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transports, then they determine the same public other-minds commitment surface. The agreement is behavioural and certificate-local: it does not identify private $\mathsf{Self}$ rows, host mental states, or unrecorded histories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the classifier comparison on the displayed rows. Matching $A$, $B$, $L$, and $E$ fixes the public observer, candidate, locality-cell commitment, and behavioural-evidence coordinates.

Transport along $H$ and $C$ carries the same public evidence into the same $\Pkg$ and local $\NameCert$ rows. Therefore any consumer sees the same finite commitment surface.

The agreement is not a private-state identity. By \autoref{thm:other-minds-commitment-locality-cell-soundness}, the carrier has no direct candidate $\mathsf{Self}$ coordinate and no host mental-state predicate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OtherMindsCommitment public evidence ledger]
\label{thm:other-minds-commitment-public-evidence-ledger}
Every consumer of an accepted $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$ packet must factor an other-minds read through the displayed rows $A,B,L,E,G,H,C,P,N$. In particular, the consumer reaches the candidate-other commitment only through the public evidence row $E$ together with the locality-cell row $L$; no private $\mathsf{Self}$ row, host mental-state predicate, or unrecorded history is available as a consumer route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier as $O=(A,B,L,E,G,H,C,P,N)$. These are the only rows exposed by \autoref{def:other-minds-commitment-carrier}.

Use \autoref{thm:other-minds-commitment-locality-cell-soundness} to locate the candidate-other commitment inside the locality-cell row $L$. A consumer may read that commitment only as a public locality-cell coordinate of the observer's finite history.

Use \autoref{thm:other-minds-commitment-behavioural-evidence-compatibility} to identify the evidence consumed by the read with the displayed behavioural evidence row $E$, transported by $H$ and routed by $C$ into $P$ and $N$.

There is no remaining coordinate for a private $\mathsf{Self}$, a host mental-state predicate, or an unrecorded history. Thus the public evidence ledger is the sole consumer route for the other-minds read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OtherMindsCommitment non-escape boundary]
\label{thm:other-minds-commitment-non-escape-boundary}
An accepted $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$ carrier exports only the observer row, candidate-other public row, locality-cell commitment row, behavioural evidence row, $\mathsf{GAP}$ and provenance row, displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transports, $\Pkg$ row, and local $\NameCert$ row. It exports no direct candidate $\mathsf{Self}$ access, no private-history read, no host mental-state predicate, no metaphysical identity assertion, and no decision procedure for whether every candidate other system is conscious.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates of \autoref{def:other-minds-commitment-carrier}. Every exported row is one of $A$, $B$, $L$, $E$, $G$, $H$, $C$, $P$, or $N$.

The locality-cell and behavioural evidence rows are public to the observer's history. They support a finite commitment surface but do not contain the candidate's private $\mathsf{Self}$ or a private-history projection.

No remaining coordinate names a host mental-state predicate, a metaphysical identity row, or a global consciousness decision procedure. Hence none of those objects can escape from the certificate.
\end{proof}

By \autoref{thm:other-minds-commitment-taste-gate-obligation-boundary}, that gate constrains the displayed rows rather than adding a private candidate coordinate or a host predicate.

The finite obligation surface is then exhausted by the existing theorem chain. Apply \autoref{thm:other-minds-commitment-namecert-obligations} for the carrier and NameCert rows, \autoref{thm:other-minds-commitment-locality-cell-soundness} for the locality-cell reading, \autoref{thm:other-minds-commitment-behavioural-evidence-compatibility} for public evidence compatibility, and \autoref{thm:other-minds-commitment-public-evidence-ledger} for the consumer route through $E$ and $L$.
