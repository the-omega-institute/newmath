\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NetConvergenceUp$}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis and Kelley-style convergence of nets, the $\NetConvergenceUp$ packet names only the finite directed-tail evidence a completion-facing consumer can inspect. It sits between the Moore--Smith net carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-moore-smith-net-namecert}, the directed-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, the finite filterbase handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the entourage discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}, and the L10 real-and-completion route of \autoref{sec:roadmap-l10-construction-order}. The Moore--Smith net row supplies the directed-tail and value-window route that this convergence interface consumes before filter and Real handoff rows are read. The packet is a convergence interface for Real and completion arguments; it is not a selected limit, a quotient of nets, or an ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Net convergence carrier]
\label{def:net-convergence-carrier}
A $\NetConvergenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
N=(D,T,E,A,F,S,R,L,H,C,P,M)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the directed-index window, naming the finite index vertices, the displayed upper-window witnesses, and the finite tail cut used by every convergence read.
\item $T$ is the tail-neighbourhood ledger, recording the neighbourhood requests whose tails are inspected inside $D$.
\item $E$ is the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage row, giving the finite entourage request through which the tail-neighbourhood read is interpreted.
\item $A$ is an optional $\CauchyNetUp$ source row; when present, its common-window classifier is read only through the same $D,T,E$ rows, and when absent the packet records no Cauchy-net source.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ handoff row obtained from the displayed tail-neighbourhood family.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule naming the finite observation windows used by the directed tail.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from those scheduled observations and dyadic comparisons.
\item $L$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after $D,T,E,F,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the directed window, tail-neighbourhood ledger, entourage row, optional source row, filter handoff, stream schedule, regular-rational readback, Real seal, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay for every consumer read through the same coordinates.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NetConvergenceUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $M$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NetConvergenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only these finite rows. It contains no selected limit point, quotient net, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Net convergence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:net-convergence-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted carrier $N=(D,T,E,A,F,S,R,L,H,C,P,M)$ of \autoref{def:net-convergence-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\NetConvergenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the directed-index window, tail-neighbourhood ledger, uniform-entourage row, optional CauchyNet source row, CauchyFilter handoff row, StreamName schedule, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal boundary, and structural rows;
\item tail classification, stating that every convergence read names a displayed tail in $D$ before it can consume a neighbourhood row in $T$;
\item entourage interpretation, stating that the neighbourhood read is routed through the finite $\UniformSpaceUp$ row $E$ rather than a host topology or ambient uniformity;
\item optional-source discipline, stating that a present $\CauchyNetUp$ row is read only through the same directed window and that an absent source row exports no hidden Cauchy-net data;
\item CauchyFilter handoff, stating that the filter-facing consumer sees only the tail-neighbourhood family and refinement rows displayed by $F$;
\item Real seal non-escape, stating that $L$ is consumed only after the stream schedule $S$ and regular-rational readback $R$ have been displayed;
\item componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,M$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet of \autoref{def:net-convergence-carrier}. The analytic coordinates are $D,T,E,A,F,S,R,L$, and the structural coordinates are $H,C,P,M$.

A convergence read first names a directed tail in $D$. The tail-neighbourhood ledger $T$ can then be inspected only at that displayed tail, and the neighbourhood is interpreted by the finite entourage row $E$.

The optional source row does not add a second convergence surface. If $A$ is present, its common-window classifier is read through the same directed window and entourage data; if $A$ is absent, there is no coordinate from which source-net data can be exported.

The downstream routes are likewise fixed by visible rows. The Cauchy-filter consumer reads $F$, while Real-facing consumers read $S$ and $R$ before reaching the seal $L$. Transport, replay, provenance, and naming are componentwise through $H,C,P,M$, so the listed rows exhaust the local naming surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Moore--Smith dependency for net convergence]
\label{thm:net-convergence-moore-smith-dependency}
Every $\NetConvergenceUp$ consumer that is sourced from a $\MooreSmithNetUp$ packet reads the Moore--Smith directed-index row, value-window row, and finite tail ledger before it reads the $\CauchyFilterUp$ handoff row or the $\RealUp$ seal row. The dependency is confined to displayed finite directed-tail evidence and does not introduce selected subnets, cofinal choice, quotient nets, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the Moore--Smith net obligations of \autoref{thm:moore-smith-net-namecert-obligations}. They expose directed indices, value windows, and tail requests as the finite packet consumed by convergence-side readers.

Now apply the NetConvergence carrier discipline of \autoref{def:net-convergence-carrier}. The optional source row $A$ can contribute source-net material only through the same directed window, tail ledger, and entourage rows $D,T,E$.

The downstream rows are read after that directed-tail route. The $\CauchyFilterUp$ handoff row $F$ and the $\RealUp$ seal row $L$ therefore consume displayed Moore--Smith evidence only through the accepted $\NetConvergenceUp$ packet, leaving no coordinate for a selected subnet, cofinal-choice operator, quotient net, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Net convergence CauchyFilter handoff]
\label{thm:net-convergence-cauchy-filter-handoff}
Every $\CauchyFilterUp$ handoff exported by an accepted $\NetConvergenceUp$ carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
D\longmapsto T\longmapsto E\longmapsto F
$$
and every $\RealUp$ read after that handoff factors through the same $F$ row together with the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$. No selected limit, quotient net, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness row can alter the handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:net-convergence-namecert-obligations}. The tail classification obligation places every convergence read on a displayed directed tail in $D$, and the tail-neighbourhood ledger $T$ supplies the finite neighbourhood family inspected at that tail.

The entourage interpretation obligation routes that neighbourhood family through the uniform row $E$. Thus the filter-facing datum is not a host filter; it is exactly the finite family that the handoff row $F$ exposes to $\CauchyFilterUp$ consumers.

For a Real-facing read, the packet must continue through $S$ and $R$ before reaching $L$. These rows are part of the same accepted carrier and are transported only by the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data in $H$ and $C$.

There is no remaining coordinate in $N$ for a selected limit point, a quotient of nets, a countable-choice selector, or an ambient completeness theorem. Consequently the handoff is determined by the displayed route through $D,T,E,F,S,R,L$.
\end{proof}
