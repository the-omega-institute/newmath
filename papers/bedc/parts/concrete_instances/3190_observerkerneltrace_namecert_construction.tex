\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ObserverKernelTraceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-observerkerneltrace-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ObserverKernelTraceUp$ surface realizes observer identity as a finite trace packet rather than as a subject behind the trace. It packages history rows, history-sameness transport, continuation reads, licensed probe and signature observations, provenance, and a local naming row into one $\NameCert$ boundary. A consumer may compare observer traces through the displayed packet, but it receives no observer-substance coordinate and no host equality of subjects.

$\ObserverKernelTraceUp$ supplies the no-subject finite trace surface consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistoryidentity-namecert} before pairwise observer-history comparison is licensed.

\begin{definition}[ObserverKernelTrace carrier]
\label{def:observer-kernel-trace-carrier}
An $\ObserverKernelTraceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(T,H,C,B,S,L,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the finite observer-trace ledger, whose entries are $\BHist$ rows recording the histories available to an observer-facing consumer;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for trace rows, observation rows, licensed readback rows, provenance rows, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads trace rows, transports, probe bundles, signature events, provenance, and local naming;
\item $B$ is the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ observation row attached to the same trace ledger;
\item $S$ is the $\mathsf{SigRel}$ observation row attached to the bundled probe reads;
\item $L$ is the no-subject ledger recording that every exported observer read is one of the displayed trace, transport, route, probe, signature, provenance, or local naming rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $T,H,C,B,S,L$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverKernelTraceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an observer-substance type, subject identity row, hidden owner of the observations, or host equality principle outside $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{SigRel}$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverKernelTrace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-kernel-trace-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-kernel-trace-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverKernelTraceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $O$, trace-ledger stability, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ route replay, licensed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and $\mathsf{SigRel}$ observation exactness, no-subject ledger exactness, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming exactness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ObserverKernelTraceUp.ObserverKernelTraceNameCert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite packet $O$. The trace ledger $T$, transports $H$, routes $C$, probe row $B$, signature row $S$, no-subject ledger $L$, package row $P$, and local naming row $N$ are the displayed coordinates of the carrier.

The stability and replay obligations are componentwise. Transport through $H$ preserves the trace and observation rows, while the routes in $C$ replay only the listed reads.

Probe and signature exactness come from $B$ and $S$, and the no-subject ledger $L$ records every exported observer read before it reaches the consumer. Since the carrier has no coordinate beyond $T,H,C,B,S,L,P,N$, the local naming certificate is exhausted by the stated finite obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverKernelTrace no-subject non-escape]
\label{thm:observer-kernel-trace-non-escape}
Every accepted $\ObserverKernelTraceUp$ packet exposes observer identity only through its finite $\BHist$ trace rows, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ routes, licensed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ readback, licensed $\mathsf{SigRel}$ readback, no-subject ledger, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row. It exports no observer-substance carrier, subject identity coordinate, hidden owner of the trace, or host equality of subjects.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ObserverKernelTraceUp.ObserverKernelTrace\_non\_escape}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.ObserverKernelTraceUp.ObserverKernelTrace\_no\_subject\_non\_escape}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet and inspect the coordinates of \autoref{def:observer-kernel-trace-carrier}. The only rows available to a consumer are $T,H,C,B,S,L,P,N$.

Apply the NameCert obligations from \autoref{thm:observer-kernel-trace-namecert-obligations}. They identify transport, route replay, probe readback, signature readback, no-subject ledgering, package provenance, and local naming as the whole certificate surface.

A requested subject carrier would require a coordinate outside the displayed tuple. Because no such coordinate is part of the packet, the accepted boundary cannot export observer-substance, subject identity, hidden ownership, or host equality data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverKernelTrace public export boundary]
\label{thm:observer-kernel-trace-public-export-boundary}
The public export of an accepted $\ObserverKernelTraceUp$ packet is exactly the finite route
$$
T,H,C,B,S,L,P,N.
$$
It exports trace rows, componentwise transport, displayed continuation routes, probe bundles, signature observations, the no-subject ledger, package provenance, and local naming, and it exports no subject carrier, host equality of subjects, hidden owner, or global observation equivalence.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ObserverKernelTraceUp.ObserverKernelTrace\_public\_export\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-kernel-trace-carrier}. Its displayed rows are precisely $T,H,C,B,S,L,P,N$, so the public surface starts from this finite tuple.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:observer-kernel-trace-namecert-obligations}. They identify trace-ledger stability, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, probe and signature exactness, no-subject ledgering, package provenance, and local naming as the whole certificate route.

Finally use \autoref{thm:observer-kernel-trace-non-escape}. A subject carrier, host equality of subjects, hidden owner, or global observation equivalence would require a coordinate absent from the carrier, so the public export is exactly the displayed route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverKernelTrace public bridge route]
\label{thm:observer-kernel-trace-public-bridge-route}
For an accepted $\ObserverKernelTraceUp$ packet, the public bridge route from the packet to downstream observer-history consumers is exactly
$$
T,H,C,B,S,L,P,N.
$$
The probe-bundle row $B$ and signature row $S$ are visible before any observer-history identity consumer reads the trace, and the route carries no subject carrier, host subject equality, hidden owner, or observer-substance coordinate outside the displayed packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ObserverKernelTraceUp.ObserverKernelTrace\_public\_bridge\_route}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:observer-kernel-trace-carrier}. Its finite rows are $T,H,C,B,S,L,P,N$, and no additional subject coordinate is part of the carrier.

Use \autoref{thm:observer-kernel-trace-public-export-boundary} to identify the public export with the same finite tuple. The probe and signature rows $B$ and $S$ are listed in that export before the no-subject ledger, provenance, and local naming rows are consumed by downstream observers.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:observer-kernel-trace-non-escape}. Any subject carrier, host subject equality, hidden owner, or observer-substance coordinate would have to be a packet row outside $T,H,C,B,S,L,P,N$, which the carrier and public export do not contain.
\end{proof}
