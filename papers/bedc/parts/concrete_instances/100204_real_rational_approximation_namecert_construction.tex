\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealRationalApproximationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-rational-approximation-namecert}
\origin{human}

Bishop and Bridges-style constructive analysis reads a real name through explicit rational approximants at finite precision, not through a host real value or a completed quotient stream. The $\RealRationalApproximationUp$ packet records that finite approximation window as BEDC data between the displayed real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite observation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the rational boundedness interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-archimedeanreal-namecert}. It is a rational-approximation face for Real and Completion consumers; it is not host real equality, a best-approximant selector, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[RealRationalApproximation carrier]
\label{def:real-rational-approximation-carrier}
A $\RealRationalApproximationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(R,Q,D,S,G,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal whose finite rational approximation window is being displayed.
\item $Q$ is the $\RatUp$ approximation row exported at the requested precision.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row naming the dyadic budget under which $Q$ is accepted as an approximant.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window opened from $R$ before the rational approximation is read.
\item $G$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to $S$, recording the regular rational observations used to justify $Q$.
\item $A$ is the approximation ledger comparing $Q$ with the displayed readback rows under the dyadic tolerance budget $D$.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the real seal, rational approximation row, dyadic tolerance row, stream window, regular readback, approximation ledger, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite precision request through $S,G,D,A,Q$ and then into the terminal real seal $R$.
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealRationalApproximationUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\ArchimedeanRealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealRationalApproximationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host real equality, quotient streams, selected best approximants, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealRationalApproximation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-rational-approximation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealRationalApproximationUp$ carrier $W=(R,Q,D,S,G,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealRationalApproximationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed $\RealUp$ seal, $\RatUp$ approximation row, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, approximation ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item window admission, requiring every approximation request to open the finite stream window $S$ from the real seal $R$ before reading any rational row;
\item tolerance admission, requiring the dyadic budget $D$ to be displayed before the rational approximant $Q$ may be accepted;
\item readback comparison, requiring the ledger $A$ to compare $Q$ against the regular rational observations in $G$ under the same dyadic budget $D$;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the ordered route $R,S,G,D,A,Q$ componentwise;
\item ledger nonescape from host real equality, quotient streams, selected best approximants, trichotomy, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealRationalApproximationUp.RealRationalApproximationTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-rational-approximation-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the real seal $R$, rational row $Q$, dyadic tolerance row $D$, stream window $S$, regular readback $G$, approximation ledger $A$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Window admission and tolerance admission are forced by the ordering of the packet. A finite approximation request first opens $S$ from the displayed real seal, reads the regular rational row $G$, and fixes the dyadic budget $D$ before $Q$ can be consumed as an approximant.

The comparison row is the ledger $A$. It records the finite check that the displayed rational row $Q$ matches the readback observations in $G$ at the tolerance named by $D$, so the approximation surface is exhausted by the rows already present in the carrier.

Transport and replay are structural. The $\hsame$ row $H$ moves each displayed coordinate componentwise, and the $\Cont$ row $C$ repeats the same finite route for later consumers.

The nonescape clause follows because the carrier has no row for host real equality, quotient streams, selected best approximants, trichotomy, or ambient completeness. The local $\NameCert$ row $N$ can therefore export only the displayed approximation window.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealRationalApproximation window extraction]
\label{thm:real-rational-approximation-window-extraction}
Every accepted $\RealRationalApproximationUp$ carrier $W=(R,Q,D,S,G,A,H,C,P,N)$ exposes a finite rational approximation window
$$
R \longrightarrow S \longrightarrow G \longrightarrow D \longrightarrow A \longrightarrow Q
$$
for the displayed $\RealUp$ name. A Real or Completion consumer receives the rational approximant only after the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows have been displayed with their transport, replay, provenance, and local naming data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an accepted packet $W=(R,Q,D,S,G,A,H,C,P,N)$. Projection gives the terminal real seal $R$ and the finite stream window $S$ opened from it.

The regular readback row $G$ is then read through the same window. The dyadic tolerance row $D$ supplies the precision budget for the request, and the ledger $A$ compares the candidate rational row $Q$ with the displayed observations under that budget.

Thus the consumer obtains $Q$ only through the ordered finite route $R,S,G,D,A$. The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route, so no detached real value or quotient stream is required to extract the approximation window.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealRationalApproximation Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:real-rational-approximation-real-seal-handoff}
The terminal $\RealUp$ seal of an accepted $\RealRationalApproximationUp$ packet hands off exactly the displayed approximation rows $Q,D,S,G,A,H,C,P,N$. It exports no host real equality, quotient stream, selected best approximant, trichotomy datum, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The real-facing boundary is the coordinate $R$ of \autoref{def:real-rational-approximation-carrier}. Every public approximation read attached to $R$ must pass through the finite window $S$, the regular readback $G$, the dyadic tolerance row $D$, the ledger $A$, and the rational row $Q$.

The structural rows do not enlarge the boundary. They preserve the same displayed route by componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming.

Since no excluded object is a coordinate of the carrier, the terminal seal has no path to host real equality, quotient streams, selected best approximants, trichotomy, or ambient completeness. The handoff is exactly the finite rational-approximation surface.
\end{proof}
