\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DarbouxSumUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-darboux-sum-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Darboux and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\DarbouxSumUp$ packet names the finite upper/lower sum surface over a dyadic partition and step-function brackets. It consumes the dyadic partition ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-partition-namecert}, the bracket rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicstepfunction-namecert}, the tagged finite-sum consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannsum-namecert}, the Darboux integration carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-darbouxintegral-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet supplies the finite $SU/SL$ boundary that later Darboux and Riemann integration chapters can cite without importing open-cover compactness, selected mesh limits, measure-theoretic integration, quotient real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Darboux sum carrier]\label{def:darboux-sum-carrier}
A $\DarbouxSumUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(P,L,U,SL,SU,G,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the $\DyadicPartitionUp$ packet naming the finite ordered dyadic cell list and its common-refinement ledger.
\item $L$ and $U$ are lower and upper $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$ packets over the displayed partition cells, with every bracket value read through the same partition row $P$.
\item $SL$ is the finite lower Darboux-sum row computed from the displayed cell widths of $P$ and the carried lower values of $L$.
\item $SU$ is the finite upper Darboux-sum row computed from the displayed cell widths of $P$ and the carried upper values of $U$.
\item $G$ is the upper-minus-lower gap row, exposing only the finite comparison between $SU$ and $SL$ after the partition and step-function brackets have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the partition row, lower and upper step-function rows, lower and upper finite sum rows, gap row, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from partition cells to bracket reads, from bracket reads to finite sums, and from the two finite sums to the gap comparison.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DarbouxSumUp$, $\DyadicPartitionUp$, $\DyadicStepFunctionUp$, $\RiemannSumUp$, $\DarbouxIntegralUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DarbouxSumUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $P,L,U,SL,SU,G,H,C,Q,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected mesh limits, measure-theoretic integration, quotient real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Darboux sum NameCert obligations]\label{thm:darboux-sum-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DarbouxSumUp$ carrier
$$
S=(P,L,U,SL,SU,G,H,C,Q,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\DarbouxSumUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed dyadic partition packet, lower and upper step-function brackets, finite lower and upper sum rows, gap row, transport and replay ledger, provenance, and local naming row;
\item partition compatibility, requiring every bracket value and every cell width used by $SL$ and $SU$ to be read through the displayed $\DyadicPartitionUp$ row $P$;
\item bracket stability, requiring $\hsame$ transport of $L$ and $U$ to carry the same lower/upper role over any displayed common refinement of $P$;
\item finite sum stability, requiring $SL$ and $SU$ to replay as finite cellwise sums after transport of the partition and bracket rows;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $H,C,Q,N$ to preserve, replay, source, and name only the displayed rows and to exclude open-cover compactness, selected mesh limits, measure-theoretic integration, quotient real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:darboux-sum-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the partition, lower bracket, upper bracket, lower finite sum, upper finite sum, gap, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Partition compatibility is forced before any sum row can be read. The row $P$ supplies the finite ordered dyadic cells and their widths, while $L$ and $U$ supply lower and upper values over those same displayed cells.

Bracket stability and finite sum stability are then replayed through $H$ and $C$. Transport moves the partition and both brackets componentwise, and continuation replay recomputes $SL$ and $SU$ as finite cellwise sums over the transported rows.

Finally, $Q$ and $N$ source and name this same finite route. Since no carrier coordinate stores open-cover compactness, selected mesh limits, measure data, quotient real equality, or ambient completeness, the ledger non-escape row exhausts the remaining obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Darboux upper-lower gap]\label{thm:darboux-sum-upper-lower-gap}
For every accepted $\DarbouxSumUp$ carrier $S=(P,L,U,SL,SU,G,H,C,Q,N)$, the upper-minus-lower gap row $G$ is read only through the displayed finite partition $P$, the displayed lower and upper step-function brackets $L$ and $U$, and the resulting finite sum rows $SL$ and $SU$. Any consumer of $G$ therefore receives a finite partition-and-bracket comparison surface, not open-cover compactness, a selected mesh limit, measure-theoretic integration, quotient real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only rows upstream of $G$ are $P,L,U,SL,SU$ together with the transport and replay structure that preserves them.

The lower and upper finite sums are computed before the gap is visible. The row $SL$ uses the cell widths of $P$ and the carried lower values of $L$, while $SU$ uses the same cell widths and the carried upper values of $U$.

Reading $G$ then compares these two displayed finite sums. Componentwise transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ may move the packet to a displayed common refinement, but the read still factors through the transported partition, transported brackets, and recomputed finite sum rows.

The excluded analytic objects are absent from the carrier. Thus no consumer can obtain them by inspecting the upper-minus-lower gap.
\end{proof}
