Following Bourbaki and Kelley on uniform spaces, a uniformity can be read through a directed family of entourages whose finite refinements control diagonal, symmetry, and composition requests. The $\UniformEntourageBasisUp$ packet records that basis surface as finite BEDC NameCert data. It sits between the ambient uniform-space interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}, the separated boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separated-uniform-space-namecert}, the complete-uniform consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-uniform-space-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, and the metric basis model of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}. The packet gives L10 completion consumers an explicit finite entourage-basis carrier rather than an implicit convention about available uniform neighborhoods.

\begin{definition}[Uniform entourage basis carrier]
\label{def:uniform-entourage-basis-carrier}
A $\UniformEntourageBasisUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(X,B,D,R,S,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the point-source row, carrying the displayed source histories and point classifier read by the ambient $\UniformSpaceUp$ packet.
\item $B$ is the finite entourage-basis ledger, listing the named basic entourage rows available to completion, Cauchy-filter, separated-uniform, and metric consumers.
\item $D$ is the diagonal ledger, recording that each listed basic entourage admits the displayed diagonal point pairs from $X$.
\item $R$ is the pairwise refinement ledger, recording for each two displayed basic entourages a further listed basic entourage refined into both.
\item $S$ is the symmetry ledger, recording for each listed basic entourage a displayed basic entourage whose reversed point-pair reads refine the original row.
\item $Q$ is the composition ledger, recording for each listed basic entourage a displayed basic entourage whose two-step relational composition refines the original row.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the point source, basis ledger, diagonal rows, pairwise refinement rows, symmetry rows, and composition rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from any completion or Cauchy-filter consumer request through $X,B,D,R,S,Q$ in that order.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformEntourageBasisUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\SeparatedUniformSpaceUp$, $\CompleteUniformSpaceUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformEntourageBasisUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no quotient completion, host equality of points, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness principle, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row, Lean verification, or bridge certificate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform entourage basis NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-entourage-basis-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:uniform-entourage-basis-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformEntourageBasisUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $K=(X,B,D,R,S,Q,H,C,P,N)$, point-source admission through $X$, finite basic-entourage admission through $B$, diagonal admission through $D$, pairwise common-refinement admission through $R$, symmetry admission through $S$, composition admission through $Q$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The point-source row and finite basis ledger are displayed before any uniform consumer can ask for a Cauchy, separated, complete, or metric-facing read.

The obligation rows are the uniformity laws restricted to the named basis. The diagonal row $D$, pairwise refinement row $R$, symmetry row $S$, and composition row $Q$ certify exactly the finite requests needed to treat $B$ as an entourage basis.

The structural rows preserve that basis surface. The transport row $H$ moves all six data rows componentwise, the replay row $C$ forces consumers through the same route, the package row $P$ records the sibling dependencies, and the local name $N$ certifies no additional completion or choice coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform entourage basis refinement stability]
\label{thm:uniform-entourage-basis-refinement-stability}
Every accepted $\UniformEntourageBasisUp$ packet keeps completion-facing refinement reads inside the finite route
$$
X,B,D,R,S,Q,H,C,P,N.
$$
A request for a smaller common entourage is discharged by the pairwise refinement row $R$; a request for a reversed entourage is discharged by $S$; and a request for a two-step entourage whose composition remains inside a displayed target entourage is discharged by $Q$. The resulting read is transported by $H$, replayed by $C$, sourced by $P$, and named by $N$, with no quotient completion, host equality, selected limit, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness exposed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a completion-facing or Cauchy-filter request. The consumer can inspect only the point source $X$ and the finite basis ledger $B$ before asking for a refinement row.

There are three refinement forms. Pairwise narrowing is the row $R$, reversed-point stability is the row $S$, and two-step composition stability is the row $Q$; the diagonal row $D$ supplies the identity-pair side condition used by all three forms.

Finally apply the structural route. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport keeps the selected basis rows attached to the same point source, $\Cont$ replay prevents the consumer from skipping the displayed rows, and the $\Pkg$ and local NameCert rows name only this finite basis packet.
\end{proof}
