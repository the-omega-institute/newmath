\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyCompletionCounitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-completion-counit-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive completion and Mac Lane on adjunction counits, the $\RegularCauchyCompletionCounitUp$ packet records the regular-source face of the completion reflector's return map. It sits between the source-side unit of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-completion-unit-namecert}, the general counit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-counit-namecert}, and the monadic completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionmonad-namecert}. Its readback route passes through the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ packets of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the counit face only as finite BEDC data; it is not quotient-stream equality, a chosen regular representative, countable choice, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[RegularCauchyCompletionCounit carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-completion-counit-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyCompletionCounitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(M,U,Q,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the completion-facing regular source row whose accepted read is being returned.
\item $U$ is the $\RegularCauchyCompletionUnitUp$ admission route that records how the regular source entered the reflector surface.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ return row restricting the general counit to the regular source face.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row through which the returned regular name is inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational readback row attached to the same finite windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger accounting for each precision request used by $S$ and $R$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal handoff reached only after $U,Q,S,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the completion-facing source, unit route, counit row, stream windows, regular readback, dyadic ledger, seal handoff, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the completion-facing source through the unit route, counit row, finite windows, dyadic ledger, regular readback, and real seal.
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyCompletionCounitUp$, $\RegularCauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCompletionCounitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected representatives, host sequence equality, countable-choice schedules, analytic closure of the monad, ambient completion, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyCompletionCounit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-counit-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\RegularCauchyCompletionCounitUp$ carrier $K=(M,U,Q,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCompletionCounitUp}$ surface consists exactly of completion-facing source admission through $M$, regular unit admission through $U$, general counit restriction through $Q$, finite $\StreamNameUp$ window exposure through $S$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback through $R$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance accounting through $D$, $\RealUp$ seal handoff through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-completion-counit-carrier}. Its displayed coordinates are exactly $M,U,Q,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$.

The source and adjunction rows are $M,U,Q$. The unit row $U$ records how the regular source entered the completion-facing surface, and the counit row $Q$ records the return route restricted to that same regular face.

The finite observation rows are $S,R,D$. They expose stream windows, regular rational readback, and dyadic tolerance accounting before any terminal real seal is consumed.

The seal handoff $E$ is downstream of those rows, while $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same finite route. No other local obligation is present.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyCompletionCounit real-seal factorization]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-counit-real-seal-factorization}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RegularCauchyCompletionCounitUp$ packet reaches the seal row $E$ only through the displayed route
$$
M\longmapsto U\longmapsto Q\longmapsto S\longmapsto D\longmapsto R\longmapsto E
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$. Thus the regular counit reads the completion return as finite regular Cauchy data with stream windows and dyadic tolerance accounting, not as quotient-stream equality, a selected representative, or an ambient completion principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a real-seal consumer. By the carrier definition, the only completion-facing source row available to that consumer is $M$.

The consumer must next pass through $U$ and $Q$. These rows identify the source admission as regular and restrict the general counit return to that regular source face.

The finite readback is then forced through $S,D,R$. Stream windows name the inspected observations, the dyadic ledger accounts for the requested tolerance, and the regular rational row supplies the readback before $E$ is inspected.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. The seal handoff is therefore the displayed factorization, not a hidden quotient, selector, or completeness witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegularCauchyCompletionCounit non-escape]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-counit-nonescape}
No accepted $\RegularCauchyCompletionCounitUp$ packet exports data outside $M,U,Q,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$. In particular, it cannot be used to recover quotient-stream equality, a chosen regular representative, host sequence equality, countable-choice schedules, analytic closure of $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, ambient completion, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier. The completion-facing source, unit admission, counit restriction, stream-window, regular readback, dyadic ledger, seal handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local-name rows are the only fields.

Apply \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-counit-namecert-obligations}. Every accepted local obligation is a projection of those rows or a structural transport, replay, provenance, or naming row for them.

Use \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-completion-counit-real-seal-factorization}. Every real-seal consumer passes through $M,U,Q,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$, so no excluded quotient, selector, host, monadic closure, ambient completion, or real-completeness object can be exported.
\end{proof}
