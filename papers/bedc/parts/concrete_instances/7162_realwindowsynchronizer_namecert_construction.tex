The $\RealWindowSynchronizerUp$ packet realizes the Real and Completion route of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness} as one finite naming certificate. It consumes the StreamName window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the RegSeqRat tail surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the Cauchy limit seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}, the regular Cauchy tail budget of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-tail-budget-namecert}, and the Real seal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The synchronizer is not a new completion principle; it is the finite packet that keeps the window, tail, budget, limit-seal, and RealUp rows aligned before any completion-facing consumer reads the route.

\begin{definition}[Real window synchronizer carrier]
\label{def:real-window-synchronizer-carrier}
A $\RealWindowSynchronizerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(W,R,T,L,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window service row whose finite windows are visible to downstream consumers;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ tail row that reads the same window service as a regular rational sequence surface;
\item $T$ is the shared tail threshold and $\RegularCauchyTailBudgetUp$ service row through which the window read and the regular tail read are synchronized;
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row consuming the synchronized tail route as a sealed Cauchy-limit handoff;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after $W,R,T,L$ have been replayed through the displayed routes;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the window, tail, threshold, limit-seal, Real seal, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that inspect the StreamName window, pull the RegSeqRat tail to the shared threshold, pass through the CauchyLimitSeal row, and expose the RealUp seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RegularCauchyTailBudgetUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealWindowSynchronizerUp}$ row naming exactly this synchronizer packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a quotient stream, selected host real, ambient completeness theorem, independent seal route, or second window service not transported through $H$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real window synchronizer NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-window-synchronizer-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealWindowSynchronizerUp$ carrier $Z=(W,R,T,L,S,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealWindowSynchronizerUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, StreamName window admission through $W$, RegSeqRat tail admission through $R$, shared-threshold admission through $T$, CauchyLimitSeal route admission through $L$, RealUp seal admission through $S$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed route replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-window-synchronizer-carrier}. Each coordinate is displayed in the finite $\BHist$ packet, so the local naming row can inspect only $W,R,T,L,S,H,C,P,N$.

The obligation rows are then read in route order. The StreamName window and RegSeqRat tail rows are tied by the shared threshold $T$; the CauchyLimitSeal row consumes that synchronized tail route; and the RealUp seal row is exposed only after the same replay path has passed through $H,C,P,N$.

No extra obligation can appear, because the carrier has no quotient, selected host real, independent seal, or second window-service coordinate. Thus the displayed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real window synchronizer budget pullback]
\label{thm:real-window-synchronizer-budget-pullback}
Every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing read exported by an accepted $\RealWindowSynchronizerUp$ packet factors through the same shared threshold row $T$ used by the $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$ and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ tail row $R$. Transporting the window, tail, or threshold rows through $H$ transports the CauchyLimitSeal and RealUp seal reads through the same displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$ and does not create a second budget ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet $Z=(W,R,T,L,S,H,C,P,N)$. The only threshold coordinate is $T$, and both the window route and the tail route are displayed as consumers of that row.

Compose the displayed routes in $C$: first read $W$, then replay $R$ at the shared threshold, then pass through $L$ before exposing $S$. Componentwise transport by $H$ changes representatives of these rows but preserves the same ordered route.

Since no separate budget coordinate is present, a completion-facing read cannot bypass the pullback. It receives the RealUp seal only as the end of the synchronized StreamName, RegSeqRat, CauchyLimitSeal, and local NameCert route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real window synchronizer non-escape]
\label{thm:real-window-synchronizer-nonescape}
An accepted $\RealWindowSynchronizerUp$ carrier exports no route to a Real or completion consumer except the finite synchronized packet $(W,R,T,L,S,H,C,P,N)$. In particular, it cannot export a StreamName window without the RegSeqRat tail threshold, a RegSeqRat tail without the shared StreamName window, a CauchyLimitSeal read detached from the RealUp seal, or a RealUp seal detached from the displayed limit-seal route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. The exported window, tail, threshold, limit seal, Real seal, transports, continuations, provenance, and naming rows are all fields of the same finite packet.

If a consumer removes $W$, the tail row no longer has the displayed StreamName window it claims to read. If it removes $R$ or $T$, the Real-facing route has no regular tail threshold. If it removes $L$, the completion-facing handoff is absent; if it removes $S$, no RealUp seal can be read.

Thus every admitted route is the displayed synchronized route, and every escape route asks for a coordinate not present in the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real window synchronizer limit-seal compatibility]
\label{thm:real-window-synchronizer-limit-seal-compatibility}
For an accepted $\RealWindowSynchronizerUp$ carrier
$$
Z=(W,R,T,L,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row $L$ is consumed only after the same $\StreamNameUp$ window row $W$, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ tail row $R$, and the same shared threshold row $T$ have been replayed through the displayed transport, route, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$. No consumer of $L$ may replace $W$, $R$, or $T$ by a second window service, hidden tail oracle, detached threshold ledger, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to its displayed coordinates. The only entries available before the limit-seal row are the window $W$, tail $R$, threshold $T$, and the shared transport and route surface $H,C$.

The replay through $C$ first reads the StreamName window and then the RegSeqRat tail at the same threshold. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ may rename those rows, but it preserves the ordered route into $L$ rather than creating a second source for the seal.

The provenance and naming rows $P,N$ then certify that the consumed limit seal is the row displayed in the same finite packet. Since the carrier has no hidden tail oracle, detached threshold, second window service, or ambient completeness coordinate, every admitted $L$ read is the synchronized $W,R,T,H,C,P,N$ read.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

Then apply limit-seal compatibility from \autoref{thm:real-window-synchronizer-limit-seal-compatibility}. The CauchyLimitSeal row is available only after the same $W,R,T$ replay has passed through $H,C,P,N$.

Finally expose the Real seal row $S$ as the last displayed consumer row. The non-escape theorem \autoref{thm:real-window-synchronizer-nonescape} rules out quotient streams, selected host reals, ambient completeness, detached seal routes, and second window services because none is present among the carrier coordinates.
