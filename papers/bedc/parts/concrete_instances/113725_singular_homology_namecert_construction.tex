\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SingularHomologyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-singular-homology-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Eilenberg and Steenrod, the $\SingularHomologyUp$ packet records singular homology as a finite NameCert-facing ledger of singular-simplex rows, chain rows, boundary rows, and cycle-boundary classifier rows. It refines the homology carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-homology-namecert}, reads simplex incidence beside the finite combinatorics of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-simplicialcomplex-namecert}, keeps cochain duality separate from the cohomology certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cohomology-namecert}, and attaches all simplex domains and endpoint evaluations to the Topology packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}. The chapter names the local certificate surface for singular chains; it does not assert exactness of long sequences, homotopy invariance, excision, universal coefficients, or de Rham comparison.

\begin{definition}[SingularHomology carrier]\label{def:singular-homology-carrier}
A $\SingularHomologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(X,\Delta,\Sigma,C,B,Z,Q,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\TopologyUp$ carrier row naming the inspected space, its displayed opens, and the finite continuity witnesses used by singular simplices.
\item $\Delta$ is the finite simplex-domain row. It lists dimensions, face inclusions, vertex orders, and endpoint evaluations for the displayed standard simplices.
\item $\Sigma$ is the singular-simplex row. Each entry records a displayed continuous map from a simplex domain in $\Delta$ into the topological space row $X$.
\item $C$ is the chain row. It stores finite formal integer combinations of the displayed singular simplices and records the degree of each chain.
\item $B$ is the boundary row. It records the alternating face ledger for each chain row using only the listed face maps and singular-simplex restrictions.
\item $Z$ is the cycle-boundary classifier row. It separates rows whose boundary is the displayed zero chain from rows displayed as a boundary of a chain in the next degree.
\item $Q$ is the quotient-facing homology-name row. It names the cycle modulo boundary classifier as local BEDC data rather than as host quotient equality.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for topology, simplex-domain, singular-simplex, chain, boundary, cycle-boundary classifier, quotient-facing name, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $T$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from simplex domains to singular maps, from singular maps to chains, from chains to boundaries, and from cycle-boundary classifiers to the local homology name.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SingularHomologyUp$, $\HomologyUp$, $\SimplicialComplexUp$, $\CohomologyUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SingularHomologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It contains no coordinate for ambient abelian-group quotients, choice of representatives, arbitrary topological colimits, exact-sequence machinery, homotopy invariance, excision, or de Rham integration.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SingularHomology NameCert obligations]\label{thm:singular-homology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SingularHomologyUp$ carrier $S=(X,\Delta,\Sigma,C,B,Z,Q,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SingularHomologyUp}$ surface is exhausted by topology admission for $X$, simplex-domain admission for $\Delta$, singular-simplex continuity for $\Sigma$, finite chain admission for $C$, boundary-row computation for $B$, cycle-boundary classification for $Z$, quotient-facing homology naming for $Q$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $T$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier row by row. The topology, simplex-domain, singular-simplex, chain, boundary, classifier, and homology-name coordinates are the only mathematical data visible to the local certificate.

The structural rows preserve that surface. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ can identify only displayed coordinates, the replay row $T$ can traverse only the displayed route from simplex domains to homology names, and $P,N$ record provenance and local naming for that same route.

The cited sibling chapters provide the intended boundaries: \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-homology-namecert} supplies the homology naming pattern, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-simplicialcomplex-namecert} supplies finite face bookkeeping, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cohomology-namecert} remains the separate dual certificate surface, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert} supplies topological continuity data. Since no other row is present, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SingularHomology boundary-square-zero ledger]\label{thm:singular-homology-boundary-square-zero}
For every accepted $\SingularHomologyUp$ carrier $S=(X,\Delta,\Sigma,C,B,Z,Q,H,T,P,N)$, the displayed boundary ledger $B$ contains the local witness that applying the singular boundary route twice reaches the displayed zero chain row. The witness is computed only from the alternating face rows in $\Delta$, the singular-simplex restrictions in $\Sigma$, the chain row $C$, and the replay data in $T$; it does not use host quotient equality, selected chain representatives, homotopy invariance, excision, or a de Rham comparison theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the simplex-domain row $\Delta$. Its face-inclusion ledger lists the two ways each codimension-two face appears in an iterated boundary, with opposite alternating signs.

Next read the singular-simplex row $\Sigma$ and the chain row $C$. Restricting singular simplices along the two displayed face routes produces the same listed simplex face with opposite signs, so the iterated boundary entries cancel inside the finite chain ledger.

Finally replay the result through $B,Z,Q,H,T,P,N$. The boundary row records the cancellation as a displayed zero-chain witness, the classifier row $Z$ consumes it as the cycle side of the cycle-boundary ledger, and the local homology-name row $Q$ receives only that finite witness. No excluded global theorem or external quotient principle is available in the carrier.
\end{proof}
