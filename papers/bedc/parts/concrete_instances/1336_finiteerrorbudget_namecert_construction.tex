\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finiteerrorbudget-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$ interface names the finite precision-budget packet
used on the $\RealUp$ and completion path. Following Bishop and Bridges,
\emph{Constructive Analysis}, it treats accuracy as visible dyadic tolerance
data, diagonal scheduling, tail ledgers, finite budget consumption, rational
regular-sequence readback, and a real-name seal. The packet sits above
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DiagonalModulusUp$,
$\DyadicTailBoundUp$, $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$, $\FastSeriesProductUp$, and
$\RealCauchyCompletionUp$. It never forms quotient streams, chooses a host
limit, compares host real equality, or appeals to ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[FiniteErrorBudget carrier]
\label{def:finite-error-budget-carrier}
A $\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(n,\epsilon,b,d,t,r,e,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $n$ is the unary precision-request row read by downstream real and
completion consumers;
\item $\epsilon$ is the dyadic tolerance row, displayed as a positive
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius for the request $n$;
\item $b$ is the $\DiagonalModulusUp$ selector row naming the finite diagonal
index schedule used to answer the request;
\item $d$ is the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ tail ledger recording the tail estimate
available at the selected schedule;
\item $t$ is the finite budget-consumer row, either a
$\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ packet or a $\FastSeriesProductUp$ packet, whose
summand and convolution windows are bounded by $d$;
\item $r$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that exposes the regular rational
name reached by the selected budget;
\item $e$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exported to $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ after
the finite request is answered;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance naming the dyadic tolerance, diagonal
selector, tail ledger, budget consumer, regular-sequence readback, and real
seal packets;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteErrorBudgetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient of regular streams, no host real equality
test, no selected infinite limit, and no ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[FiniteErrorBudget classifier]
\label{def:finite-error-budget-classifier}
For accepted $\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$ carriers $E$ and $E'$, the classifier
$\mathsf{FiniteErrorBudgetClassifier}(E,E')$ compares only the displayed
precision-request rows, dyadic tolerance rows, $\DiagonalModulusUp$ selector
rows, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ tail ledgers, finite budget-consumer rows,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows, $\RealUp$ seal rows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert$ evidence of \autoref{def:finite-error-budget-carrier}.

Classifier transport is componentwise by $\hsame$ on the visible rows and by
$\Cont$ along the recorded consumer routes. The classifier does not compare
hidden streams, host real values, chosen limits, quotient classes, or ambient
completion objects.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteErrorBudget zero seed]
\label{thm:finite-error-budget-zero-seed}
The unary zero precision request with dyadic tolerance $1$, the initial
$\DiagonalModulusUp$ selector, the zero $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ ledger, the empty
$\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ budget consumer, the constant-zero $\RegSeqRatUp$
readback, and the zero $\RealUp$ seal determine an accepted
$\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$ carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the zero request as the displayed precision row and choose the dyadic unit
row as the tolerance. The initial diagonal selector and the zero tail ledger
give a finite schedule whose tail cost is already below the displayed budget.

The empty fast-convergent-series packet consumes no summands, so its finite
window is compatible with the zero tail ledger. Its regular rational readback
is the constant-zero $\RegSeqRatUp$ name, and the real seal is the corresponding
zero $\RealUp$ row.

All transports and continuations are identity rows on the displayed packet
coordinates. Repacking these rows with the package provenance and local
certificate row gives the required carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteErrorBudget diagonal handoff]
\label{thm:finite-error-budget-diagonal-handoff}
Every accepted $\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$ carrier
$E=(n,\epsilon,b,d,t,r,e,P,N)$ exports a finite handoff into
$\RealCauchyCompletionUp$: the request $n$ is answered through the dyadic
tolerance $\epsilon$, the selector $b$, the tail ledger $d$, the budget
consumer $t$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $r$, and the $\RealUp$ seal $e$ before
any completion consumer can inspect the result.

The handoff is stable under the classifier of
\autoref{def:finite-error-budget-classifier} and exports no quotient stream,
host real equality, selected limit, or ambient completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the visible request row $n$. The dyadic tolerance row $\epsilon$
fixes the precision budget that can be consumed by the downstream completion
read.

The diagonal selector $b$ chooses a finite schedule, and the tail ledger $d$
records the bound available at that schedule. The budget consumer $t$ can be
read only through that displayed schedule and ledger, so every finite summand
or convolution window remains inside the packet.

The readback row $r$ presents the resulting regular rational name, and the seal
row $e$ is the only row handed to $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$. Componentwise
$\hsame$ transport and the displayed $\Cont$ routes preserve each of these
coordinates, so classified replacements give the same handoff surface.

Since the carrier coordinates are exhausted by these visible rows, there is no
field from which a quotient stream, host real equality test, selected infinite
limit, or ambient completeness principle could be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteErrorBudget tail-ledger consumption exactness]
\label{thm:finite-error-budget-tail-ledger-consumption-exactness}
For every accepted $\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$ carrier
$E=(n,\epsilon,b,d,t,r,e,P,N)$, every finite budget-consumer read used by the
$\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ handoff factors through the displayed
$\DyadicTailBoundUp$ ledger $d$, the $\DiagonalModulusUp$ selector $b$, the
dyadic tolerance $\epsilon$, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $r$.

If two accepted carriers classify together by
\autoref{def:finite-error-budget-classifier}, then the consumer-visible
ledger surface is preserved exactly: classified replacement can transport the
same displayed rows through $\hsame$ and $\Cont$, but it cannot replace the
handoff by an unrecorded stream tail, selected limit, host real equality, or
ambient completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the selector row $b$ and the tail ledger $d$ from the carrier. The
selector names the finite schedule for the request $n$, while $d$ records the
tail estimate available at that schedule against the displayed dyadic
tolerance $\epsilon$.

Project the budget-consumer row $t$. By the carrier definition, its summand or
convolution windows are read only inside the schedule and tail bound already
displayed by $b$ and $d$, and the resulting rational name is exposed only
through the readback coordinate $r$.

Classifier transport is componentwise. Therefore a classified carrier can
transport only the precision row, tolerance row, selector, tail ledger,
consumer row, readback row, seal row, package provenance, and certificate row
already present in the packet.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-diagonal-handoff}. The exported completion
surface is the path through $\epsilon$, $b$, $d$, $t$, $r$, and $e$, so no
unrecorded stream tail, selected limit, host equality test, or ambient
completion datum is available to the consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteErrorBudget classifier stability]
\label{thm:finite-error-budget-classifier-stability}
Let $E=(n,\epsilon,b,d,t,r,e,P,N)$ and
$E'=(n',\epsilon',b',d',t',r',e',P',N')$ be accepted
$\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$ carriers. If they classify together by
\autoref{def:finite-error-budget-classifier}, then the consumer-visible rows
$n,\epsilon,b,d,t,r,e,P,N$ transport componentwise to
$n',\epsilon',b',d',t',r',e',P',N'$ through the displayed $\hsame$ rows and the
displayed $\Cont$ routes of the classifier.

The transport preserves the precision request, dyadic tolerance, diagonal
selector, tail ledger, finite budget consumer, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback,
$\RealUp$ seal, package provenance, and local naming row as the complete
visible surface. It introduces no hidden stream coordinate, host equality
coordinate, selected limit, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two accepted carriers. Their coordinates are exactly the rows named
in \autoref{def:finite-error-budget-carrier}, so there is no further coordinate
available for transport.

Now apply the classifier of
\autoref{def:finite-error-budget-classifier}. Its clauses are componentwise
$\hsame$ comparisons together with displayed $\Cont$ routes for the finite
precision-budget reads, so each visible row of $E$ is matched with the
corresponding visible row of $E'$.

The route list is exhausted by
$n,\epsilon,b,d,t,r,e,P,N$. Since neither carrier definition nor classifier
definition contains a stream representative, host equality field, selected
limit, or completeness witness, such data cannot be exported by classifier
transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteErrorBudget zero-budget stability]
\label{thm:finite-error-budget-zero-budget-stability}
The zero-budget seed of \autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-zero-seed} remains an
accepted $\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$ packet under every classifier-preserving
precision-row transport. The transported seed still consists only of the zero
precision request, dyadic unit tolerance, initial diagonal selector, zero tail
ledger, empty budget consumer, constant-zero $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback,
zero $\RealUp$ seal, package provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the seed packet constructed in
\autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-zero-seed}. Each of its coordinates is one of
the visible carrier rows of \autoref{def:finite-error-budget-carrier}.

Apply \autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-classifier-stability} to the transported
precision row. The classifier can move the zero request only through the
displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows, and the remaining tolerance, selector,
ledger, consumer, readback, seal, provenance, and naming coordinates move with
that same finite surface.

The resulting packet is accepted because the carrier predicates are the same
componentwise rows used by the seed construction. No unrecorded stream tail,
host real equality test, selected limit, or completeness witness is introduced
by the transport.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteErrorBudget public finite-packet export]
\label{thm:finite-error-budget-public-export}
For every accepted $\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$ carrier
$E=(n,\epsilon,b,d,t,r,e,P,N)$, the diagonal handoff of
\autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-diagonal-handoff}, the tail-ledger
consumption exactness of
\autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-tail-ledger-consumption-exactness}, and the
local $\NameCert_{\FiniteErrorBudgetUp}$ rows of
\autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-namecert-obligations} assemble a public finite
packet export for $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.

Every downstream read factors through the displayed rows
$n,\epsilon,b,d,t,r,e,P,N$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, and displayed
$\Cont$ routes. The export contains no quotient stream, hidden host equality
coordinate, selected limit, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the carrier rows. The request $n$, tolerance $\epsilon$, selector
$b$, ledger $d$, budget consumer $t$, readback $r$, seal $e$, package row $P$,
and naming row $N$ are the whole finite packet.

Next apply \autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-diagonal-handoff}. It identifies
the completion-facing route through the tolerance, selector, ledger, consumer,
readback, and seal rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-tail-ledger-consumption-exactness} to
the same packet. This makes every finite consumer read pass through the
displayed ledger and prevents replacement by an unrecorded tail surface.

Finally use \autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-namecert-obligations}. Its
carrier, classifier, zero-stability, diagonal-handoff, and non-escape rows
repack the same finite data as the public export consumed by
$\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteErrorBudget NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-error-budget-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\FiniteErrorBudgetUp}$ obligation surface consists of
five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation by the zero-budget seed of
\autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-zero-seed};
\item classifier transport by componentwise $\hsame$ and the displayed $\Cont$
routes of \autoref{def:finite-error-budget-classifier};
\item zero-budget stability, asserting that the zero request, dyadic unit
tolerance, initial selector, zero tail ledger, empty budget consumer,
constant-zero readback, and zero real seal remain inside the carrier under
classified replacement;
\item diagonal handoff into $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ by
\autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-diagonal-handoff};
\item non-escape of quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, and
ambient completeness beyond the displayed precision-budget rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is exactly the packet constructed in
\autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-zero-seed}. It supplies every carrier
coordinate using visible finite rows.

Classifier transport follows from
\autoref{def:finite-error-budget-classifier}. A classified replacement can move
only the precision request, dyadic tolerance, diagonal selector, tail ledger,
budget consumer, readback, seal, provenance, and local certificate rows that
the carrier already displays.

Zero-budget stability is obtained by projecting the same coordinates from the
zero seed after transport. Identity $\hsame$ rows preserve the zero request,
unit tolerance, initial selector, zero tail ledger, empty consumer,
constant-zero readback, and zero real seal.

The diagonal handoff row is supplied by
\autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-diagonal-handoff}. Its exported surface is the
finite path through $\epsilon$, $b$, $d$, $t$, $r$, and $e$, so the completion
consumer sees a real-seal read and not an unrecorded stream.

The non-escape row follows because all accepted carrier coordinates have been
listed. None of them is a quotient constructor, a host equality oracle, a
choice of limit, or an ambient completeness datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}
Project the two carrier tuples using
\autoref{def:finite-error-budget-carrier}. The visible rows are precisely
$n,\epsilon,b,d,t,r,e,P,N$ and their primed counterparts.

Apply the classifier definition of
\autoref{def:finite-error-budget-classifier}. Its transport clauses are
componentwise $\hsame$ rows and displayed $\Cont$ routes for the same finite
rows, so every transported consumer read remains on the listed surface.

The diagonal and tail-consumption rows then rule out hidden enlargement.
\autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-diagonal-handoff} routes completion reads

\autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-tail-ledger-consumption-exactness} keeps the

\begin{theorem}[FiniteErrorBudget diagonal selector exactness]
\label{thm:finite-error-budget-diagonal-selector-exactness}
For every accepted carrier $E=(n,\epsilon,b,d,t,r,e,P,N)$, every diagonal
handoff from $\FiniteErrorBudgetUp$ into $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ factors
through the displayed selector $b$, tail ledger $d$, tolerance $\epsilon$,
finite budget consumer $t$, and readback row $r$ before reaching the
$\RealUp$ seal $e$. The handoff cannot replace any of these rows by an
unrecorded schedule, tail estimate, tolerance, consumer, readback, or seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier coordinates of
\autoref{def:finite-error-budget-carrier}. The request row $n$ exposes only
one dyadic tolerance row, one diagonal selector, one tail ledger, one consumer,
one readback, and one seal.

Apply \autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-diagonal-handoff}. It identifies the
completion-facing path as the chain through $\epsilon$, $b$, $d$, $t$, $r$,
and $e$.

Use \autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-tail-ledger-consumption-exactness} for
the consumer step. The consumer read is bound to the same displayed selector
and ledger, so diagonal handoff exactness is a statement about the packet
itself rather than about an ambient completion object.
\end{proof}

Finally use \autoref{thm:finite-error-budget-namecert-obligations}. Its local
