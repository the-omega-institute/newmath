\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AsymptoticSequenceUp$}

Following Hardy and Littlewood on asymptotic comparison of sequences, the $\AsymptoticSequenceUp$ packet records finite tail-comparison data for displayed sequence names rather than importing host sequence equality or completed real analysis. It sits over the $\StreamNameUp$ window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the limit-facing handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-limit-namecert}, the symmetric tail comparison of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-tail-equivalence-namecert}, and the cofinal route comparison of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cofinaltailequivalence-namecert}. The packet is a named consumer for Real and Completion tail comparisons: it exposes finite windows, thresholds, readbacks, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming, while refusing quotient streams, arbitrary subsequence choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[AsymptoticSequence carrier]
\label{def:asymptotic-sequence-carrier}
An $\AsymptoticSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(S,T,W,M,Q,L,E,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ and $T$ are the two source sequence-name rows, read only through finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows.
\item $W$ is the paired $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger naming the inspected source cells and the corresponding comparison windows.
\item $M$ is the tail-threshold row recording which displayed unary tail must be reached before the comparison classifier is read.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the rational observations attached to $W$ and $M$.
\item $L$ is the $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ handoff row available only after the scheduled windows and rational readbacks are displayed.
\item $E$ is the $\CauchyTailEquivalenceUp$ provenance row for symmetric regular-Cauchy tail comparison.
\item $F$ is the $\CofinalTailEquivalenceUp$ provenance row for cofinal reindexing and tail-route comparison.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source rows, windows, threshold, readback, limit handoff, tail-equivalence rows, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each asymptotic-comparison request through $S,T,W,M,Q,L,E,F,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AsymptoticSequenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealSequenceLimitUp$, $\CauchyTailEquivalenceUp$, $\CofinalTailEquivalenceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AsymptoticSequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no quotient equality of streams, no host asymptotic relation, no selected subsequence, no trichotomy row, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AsymptoticSequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:asymptotic-sequence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\AsymptoticSequenceUp$ carrier
$$
A=(S,T,W,M,Q,L,E,F,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\AsymptoticSequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the two source sequence rows, paired $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the displayed tail threshold, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ handoff, $\CauchyTailEquivalenceUp$ provenance, $\CofinalTailEquivalenceUp$ provenance, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every accepted comparison reads $W$ and $M$ before $Q$, reads $Q$ before $L,E,F$, and reaches $H,C,P,N$ only as structural support for the same finite route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:asymptotic-sequence-carrier}. The source rows $S,T$ expose only the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows stored in $W$, and the comparison cannot inspect a tail before the threshold row $M$ has been displayed.

The readback row $Q$ is then forced by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. It consumes the same windows and threshold data rather than a host sequence value.

The analytic rows are downstream of that readback. The limit-facing handoff $L$ is the finite route supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-sequence-limit-namecert}, while $E$ and $F$ record the symmetric and cofinal tail provenance of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-tail-equivalence-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cofinaltailequivalence-namecert}.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed route. Since no carrier coordinate contains quotient stream equality, host asymptotic comparison, a selected subsequence, trichotomy, or ambient completeness, none can appear as a local NameCert obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AsymptoticSequence tail classifier]
\label{thm:asymptotic-sequence-tail-classifier}
Let $A=(S,T,W,M,Q,L,E,F,H,C,P,N)$ and $A'=(S',T',W',M',Q',L',E',F',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\AsymptoticSequenceUp$ carriers. Their accepted tail classifiers agree exactly when the paired source rows, displayed $\StreamNameUp$ windows, tail-threshold rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, $\RealSequenceLimitUp$ handoff rows, $\CauchyTailEquivalenceUp$ provenance rows, $\CofinalTailEquivalenceUp$ provenance rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ replays, package provenance rows, and local naming rows agree under the displayed transport. The classifier has no further coordinate for host sequence equality, quotient tails, arbitrary subsequence choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, read an accepted classifier agreement through the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:asymptotic-sequence-namecert-obligations}. The agreement must pass through $S,T,W,M,Q,L,E,F,H,C,P,N$, so each corresponding displayed row is preserved under the componentwise transport.

For the reverse direction, assume the listed rows agree under the displayed transport. Carrier admission reconstructs the same paired windows and tail threshold, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and the same limit, Cauchy-tail, and cofinal-tail provenance rows.

Displayed replay through $C$ and $C'$ then gives the same consumer route, while $P,N$ and $P',N'$ record the same source and local naming surface. The absence of quotient, choice, host equality, and completeness coordinates shows that no hidden classifier field remains.
\end{proof}
