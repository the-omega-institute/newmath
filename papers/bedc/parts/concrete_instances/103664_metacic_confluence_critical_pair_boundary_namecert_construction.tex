\begin{definition}[MetaCIC confluence critical-pair boundary carrier]
\label{def:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-boundary-carrier}
A $\MetaCICConfluenceCriticalPairBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$
packet
$$
B=(R,K,J,E,D,S,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\MetaCICRedexFrontierUp$ row from which beta-redex,
application, lambda-domain, and Pi-domain frontier cells are read.
\item $K$ is the critical-pair table row, listing the finite overlapping
frontier cases inspected by the packet.
\item $J$ is the common-reduct table row, listing the displayed join candidates
for the accepted critical-pair rows.
\item $E$ is the $\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp$ row used to compare bounded
normal forms when a join candidate is checked by bounded normal equality.
\item $D$ is the $\MetaCICConfluenceNormalFragmentUp$ row used for closed
normal and atom-shaped source cases.
\item $S$ is the $\SubjectReductionRouteClassifierUp$ separation row, recording
that subject-reduction discharge routes are named separately from confluence
critical-pair rows.
\item $U$ is the boundary row stating that the packet does not prove arbitrary
closed-term Church--Rosser, full parallel diamond, or residual-substitution
compatibility.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the frontier,
critical-pair table, common-reduct table, bounded-checker, normal-fragment,
route-classifier, boundary, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay by which audit consumers read the
critical-pair table and common-reduct table before any bounded checker or
normal-fragment handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetaCICConfluenceCriticalPairBoundaryUp$,
$\MetaCICRedexFrontierUp$, $\MetaCICConfluenceNormalFragmentUp$,
$\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp$, $\SubjectReductionRouteClassifierUp$,
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local
$\NameCert_{\MetaCICConfluenceCriticalPairBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no
coordinate for a global confluence theorem, arbitrary closed-term
Church--Rosser, full parallel diamond, residual-substitution compatibility, or
unchecked subject-reduction discharge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence critical-pair boundary obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-boundary-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetaCICConfluenceCriticalPairBoundaryUp$ carrier
$B=(R,K,J,E,D,S,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\MetaCICConfluenceCriticalPairBoundaryUp}$ surface consists
exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the redex-frontier row, critical-pair table,
common-reduct table, bounded equality checker, closed-normal fragment,
subject-reduction route classifier, boundary row, transport, replay,
provenance, and local naming row;
\item critical-pair display, requiring each accepted pair to be read from the
finite frontier cases in $R$ and $K$;
\item common-reduct display, requiring each accepted join candidate to be read
from $J$ before any bounded checker or normal-fragment row is consumed;
\item bounded and normal handoff, requiring $E$ and $D$ to consume only the
displayed critical-pair and common-reduct rows;
\item subject-reduction separation, requiring $S$ to keep typing-discharge
routes outside the confluence boundary;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $U,P,N$ to export no global confluence
theorem, arbitrary closed-term Church--Rosser, full parallel diamond, or
residual-substitution compatibility.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from
\autoref{def:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-boundary-carrier}. The projection
exposes exactly $R,K,J,E,D,S,U,H,C,P,N$.

The critical-pair display obligation reads $R$ and $K$ first. Thus the packet
can only enumerate finite frontier overlaps that are explicitly recorded in
the carrier.

The common-reduct display obligation is downstream of that enumeration. The
row $J$ supplies the join candidates before the bounded checker $E$ or the
closed-normal fragment $D$ may be consulted.

The subject-reduction row $S$ separates typing-discharge routes from the
confluence boundary, and $U$ records the non-claim surface. Since the remaining
rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the displayed route,
the packet cannot export global confluence or residual-substitution
compatibility.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence critical-pair common-reduct boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-common-reduct-boundary}
Let $B=(R,K,J,E,D,S,U,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted
$\MetaCICConfluenceCriticalPairBoundaryUp$ carrier. Every confluence audit read
from $B$ factors through the displayed route
$$
R \longmapsto K \longmapsto J \longmapsto E,D \longmapsto S,U \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the packet supplies a finite critical-pair and common-reduct
boundary, not a proof of arbitrary closed-term Church--Rosser, full parallel
diamond, residual-substitution compatibility, or subject-reduction discharge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an audit consumer of the packet. The consumer first reads the
redex-frontier row $R$ and then the critical-pair table $K$, so the inspected
cases are finite displayed rows.

The common-reduct table $J$ is read next. Any bounded normal-equality check or
closed-normal confluence fragment receives only the join candidates already
displayed by $J$.

The route-classifier row $S$ and boundary row $U$ then fix the non-claim
surface. They keep subject-reduction discharge and residual-substitution
compatibility outside the packet.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route.
No row in the carrier is a global confluence theorem, full parallel diamond, or
unchecked subject-reduction proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence critical-pair local join]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-local-join}
Let $B=(R,K,J,E,D,S,U,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\MetaCICConfluenceCriticalPairBoundaryUp$ carrier. Any displayed critical pair listed by $K$ may be consumed as joinable only through a corresponding common-reduct row in $J$, then through the bounded normal-equality checker row $E$ or the closed-normal fragment row $D$. It may not be consumed as arbitrary closed-term Church--Rosser evidence, full parallel diamond evidence, or residual-substitution compatibility.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-boundary-carrier}. The critical-pair table is the row $K$, and the only row that can name a join candidate for an entry of $K$ is the common-reduct table $J$.

Apply \autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-common-reduct-boundary}. It forces every confluence audit read through $R,K,J$ before the bounded checker $E$ or closed-normal fragment $D$ is consumed.

The rows $S$ and $U$ record the non-claim separation. They keep subject-reduction discharge, arbitrary closed-term Church--Rosser, full parallel diamond, and residual-substitution compatibility outside the local critical-pair packet, while $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence critical-pair field-faithful prediction]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-fieldfaithful-prediction}
Let $B=(R,K,J,E,D,S,U,H,C,P,N)$ and $B'=(R',K',J',E',D',S',U',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\MetaCICConfluenceCriticalPairBoundaryUp$ carriers. If their displayed field projections agree componentwise on $R,K,J,E,D,S,U,H,C,P,N$, then they expose the same critical-pair and common-reduct boundary route. In particular, the critical-pair table $K$ and common-reduct table $J$ cannot be erased or replaced by aliases of $R,E,D,S,U,H,C,P,N$ without changing the public packet classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both carriers through \autoref{def:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-boundary-carrier}. Componentwise equality of the displayed fields identifies the same redex-frontier row, the same critical-pair table, the same common-reduct table, the same bounded and normal checker rows, and the same boundary classifier route.

Apply \autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-common-reduct-boundary}. The confluence audit route must read $R,K,J$ before it reaches $E,D,S,U$, so agreement of all displayed projections fixes the same critical-pair and common-reduct boundary route.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-local-join}. A displayed critical pair is joinable only through its corresponding row in $J$. Erasing $K$ or $J$ would remove a field used by the local join route, so the public classifier changes rather than preserving a field-faithful packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence critical-pair nontrivial witness surface]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-nontrivial-witness-surface}
There are two classifier-separated $\MetaCICConfluenceCriticalPairBoundaryUp$ packet surfaces: one whose $K,J$ boundary rows are empty and whose $U$ row records that no displayed critical-pair join is available, and one with a displayed critical-pair row in $K$ together with a corresponding common-reduct row in $J$. The separation is witnessed by the $K,J,U$ fields and is visible to the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICConfluenceCriticalPairBoundaryUp}$ classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier definition. The fields $K$ and $J$ are independent displayed rows: $K$ lists inspected critical pairs, and $J$ lists the corresponding common-reduct candidates.

For the empty surface, the classifier reads empty $K,J$ rows together with the boundary row $U$ that denies a displayed local join route. For the inhabited surface, the classifier reads one critical-pair entry in $K$ and the matching common-reduct entry in $J$ before $U$ records the bounded boundary route.

Apply \autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-common-reduct-boundary} and \autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-critical-pair-local-join}. The local join claim cannot be consumed without the displayed $K,J$ rows, so the empty and inhabited surfaces are separated by $K,J,U$ rather than by an external confluence principle.
\end{proof}
