\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HaltingDistinctionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdistinction-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\HaltingDistinctionUp$ surface turns the halting-as-distinction interface of \autoref{ch:visions-halting-as-form-of-distinction-limit} into a finite naming packet. It does not decide halting. It records the visible certificate-history pair, the finite continuation trace being tested, the diagonal self-reference row, the transports, routes, provenance, and local naming rows through which the fixed-point obstruction can be read.

The finite trace row $T$ is the local trace surface refined by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdistinctiontrace-namecert}. $\HaltingDistinctionUp$ consumes that sibling only as a finite continuation-trace row, not as a total halting predicate or host evaluator.

The diagonal pair row is also consumed together with the inscription-event record surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert} and the trace-normal-form route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtracenormalform-namecert}. The finite route $Q,T,Z,H,C,P,N$ supplies the certificate-history pair, trace segment, diagonal read, transports, routes, provenance, and local ledger; the sibling chapters supply the inscription record and normal-form trace surfaces needed by later halting-boundary and cellular closed-substrate consumers.

\begin{definition}[HaltingDistinction carrier]
\label{def:halting-distinction-carrier}
A $\HaltingDistinctionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(Q,T,Z,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the certificate-history pair row, naming a candidate program certificate $\NameCert_P$ together with the history $h$ whose $\Cont_P$ chain is being inspected;
\item $T$ is the finite trace row recording the visible $\Cont_P$ chain segment from $h$ through its displayed marks and intermediate histories;
\item $Z$ is the diagonal self-reference row, naming the self-application read in which the classifier for $Q$ is asked about the same certificate-history pair it is currently classifying;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for certificate-history pair rows, trace rows, diagonal rows, package rows, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading the input pair, replaying the finite trace, entering the diagonal row, and returning the result to the local ledger;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Ext$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, the diagonal read, and the surrounding $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HaltingDistinctionUp}$ ledger.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no global truth predicate, no classical decidability assumption for termination, no oracle for divergence, and no meta-level evaluator outside the displayed $\BHist$, $\Ext$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[HaltingDistinction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:halting-distinction-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\HaltingDistinctionUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:halting-distinction-carrier} consists of five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite packet $D=(Q,T,Z,H,C,P,N)$;
\item certificate-history classifier transport, saying that pair rows classify only through the displayed $\hsame$ transport recorded in $H$;
\item trace readability, saying that a public termination-facing read factors through the finite $\Cont_P$ chain segment $T$ and the read routes in $C$;
\item diagonal boundary exactness, saying that the self-reference row $Z$ records the classifier's self-application point without supplying an external halting decision;
\item non-escape from global truth predicates, classical decidability, divergence oracles, host evaluators, or classifiers not named by $Q$, $T$, $Z$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:halting-distinction-carrier}. The packet names exactly the pair, trace, diagonal, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows.

Classifier transport is componentwise in $H$. Since $Q$ is the only row naming the certificate-history input, any transported public comparison must return to the same visible pair data and its local ledger.

Trace readability is controlled by $T$ and $C$. A consumer may replay the finite $\Cont_P$ segment displayed by $T$, but the carrier has no coordinate that upgrades this finite trace into a total halting predicate.

The diagonal boundary is the row $Z$. It records the self-application read needed by the fixed-point obstruction, while $P$ and $N$ record only provenance and local naming. Thus the packet exhibits the obstruction surface without importing a global decision principle.
\end{proof}

$$

$$
The row $Z$ may identify the classifier question with its own certificate-history input under the recorded $\hsame$ transports, but it does not produce a truth value for all certificate-history pairs and does not decide divergence outside the displayed finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from a public diagonal read. Carrier projection gives the input pair row $Q$, the finite trace row $T$, and the self-reference row $Z$ as the only rows that can name the read.

Use the routes in $C$ to move from $Q$ through $T$ to $Z$. The permitted renamings are exactly the componentwise transports stored in $H$, and the dependency record is exactly $P$ together with the local ledger $N$.

Since no coordinate of $D$ is a total truth predicate, a classical halting decision, or a divergence oracle, the read stops at the displayed diagonal boundary. It exposes where the self-reference pressure occurs without converting that pressure into a global classifier.
\end{proof}
