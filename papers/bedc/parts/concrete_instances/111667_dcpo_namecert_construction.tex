\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DcpoUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dcpo-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Scott domain theory and standard order theory, the $\DcpoUp$ packet records the directed-complete partial-order surface as finite BEDC naming data. It sits after the directed-index substrate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-directedset-namecert}, the separated order surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-poset-namecert}, the Moore--Smith net route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-moore-smith-net-namecert}, the directed-filter corridor of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-directed-filter-namecert}, and the Cauchy-net completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}. It is also the order-theoretic neighbour of the displayed directed-supremum packet in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scott-domain-namecert}. The packet names finite directed-supremum evidence for completion routes; it is not a host powerset lattice, not a quotient order, not a selected lub operator, and not an ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[DCPO carrier]
\label{def:dcpo-carrier}
A $\DcpoUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(O,I,W,S,M,F,Q,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the $\PosetUp$ order row, exposing the displayed comparison relation and the antisymmetry witness accepted by the packet.
\item $I$ is the $\DirectedSetUp$ index row for a directed family whose inspected addresses are available to completion consumers.
\item $W$ is the finite directed-window row, listing the displayed subfamilies of the directed diagram that a consumer may request.
\item $S$ is the supremum row, recording the displayed least-upper-bound read attached to the directed windows in $W$.
\item $M$ is the $\MooreSmithNetUp$ handoff row, reading the same directed family as a finite net route before any convergence or completion consumer is reached.
\item $F$ is the $\DirectedFilterUp$ handoff row, translating the same directed-tail ledger into a filter-base corridor.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ and completion-facing row, available only after $O,I,W,S,M,F$ have been displayed.
\item $L$ is the lower-bound exactness row, recording that a candidate receiving every displayed upper-bound comparison from $S$ is already above the named supremum by the order row $O$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the order, directed-family, supremum, handoff, exactness, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a directed-supremum request through $O,I,W,S,M,F,Q,L,H$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DcpoUp$, $\PosetUp$, $\DirectedSetUp$, $\MooreSmithNetUp$, $\DirectedFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\ScottDomainUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DcpoUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $O,I,W,S,M,F,Q,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for arbitrary joins, quotient-order equality, selected least-upper-bound functions, global choice over directed families, host powersets, or ambient order completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DCPO NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dcpo-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DcpoUp$ carrier $D=(O,I,W,S,M,F,Q,L,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DcpoUp}$ surface consists exactly of order admission, directed-index admission, finite directed-window coverage, directed-supremum readability, Moore--Smith net handoff, directed-filter handoff, CauchyNet and completion-facing handoff, lower-bound exactness, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every directed-supremum consumer must read the rows $O,I,W,S,L$ before treating the supremum as public, and no obligation is stored outside $O,I,W,S,M,F,Q,L,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dcpo-carrier}. The order and directed-family rows are $O,I,W,S,L$, while the completion-facing handoff rows are $M,F,Q$ and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

Order admission is read from $O$. Directed-index admission and window coverage are read from $I$ and $W$, so a consumer cannot ask for a directed supremum before the inspected directed family has been displayed.

The supremum row $S$ supplies the named upper-bound endpoint, and the exactness row $L$ records the leastness comparison back through the same order row $O$. Thus a lower-bound or completion consumer receives a displayed directed-supremum certificate rather than a host lub selector.

The sibling rows $M,F,Q$ expose the same directed family to Moore--Smith nets, directed filters, and Cauchy-net completion routes. Transport and replay through $H$ and $C$ preserve the displayed rows, while $P,N$ source and name the same finite carrier. Since none of the excluded host objects appears as a coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DCPO directed-supremum handoff]
\label{thm:dcpo-directed-supremum-handoff}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\DcpoUp$ carrier must factor its directed-supremum read through
$$
\PosetUp,\DirectedSetUp,W,S,L \longmapsto \MooreSmithNetUp,\DirectedFilterUp,\CauchyNetUp \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The route exposes the order row, the directed index row, finite directed windows, the named supremum, and lower-bound exactness before any Moore--Smith net, directed-filter, or CauchyNet consumer is read. It does not export arbitrary joins, quotient-order equality, global directed-choice data, host powersets, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier $D=(O,I,W,S,M,F,Q,L,H,C,P,N)$. The directed-supremum data are not supplied by an external order-completion object; the only available order and directed-family evidence is the displayed row family $O,I,W,S,L$.

The Moore--Smith route $M$ and directed-filter route $F$ are downstream of those rows. Each therefore receives the same finite directed-tail ledger and cannot replace it by a selected cofinal net, quotient filter, or host-level directed diagram.

The CauchyNet and completion-facing row $Q$ is downstream of $M$ and $F$. It may consume the directed-supremum handoff, but it cannot read an ambient completion witness before the finite order, directed-window, supremum, and leastness rows have been displayed.

Finally, componentwise transport and continuation replay through $H$ and $C$ preserve this row order, and $P,N$ record provenance and local naming for the same packet. Since arbitrary joins, quotient equality, directed-choice data, host powersets, and ambient completeness are absent from the carrier, every public completion read remains inside the displayed handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DCPO Scott-domain compatibility]
\label{thm:dcpo-scott-domain-compatibility}
The $\ScottDomainUp$ consumer of an accepted $\DcpoUp$ carrier reads only the displayed directed-supremum route $O,I,W,S,L$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. Compact-basis, Scott-open, and represented-space observations may be attached downstream by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scott-domain-namecert}, but the $\DcpoUp$ packet itself exports only the directed-complete order substrate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:dcpo-namecert-obligations}. The DCPO obligation surface exposes the order row, directed-index row, directed windows, supremum row, and lower-bound exactness row before any sibling consumer is reached.

The Scott-domain packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scott-domain-namecert} adds basis, Scott-open, and represented-space rows on top of a displayed directed-supremum route. When it consumes a $\DcpoUp$ carrier, those rows can only be attached after $O,I,W,S,L$ have been read.

Apply \autoref{thm:dcpo-directed-supremum-handoff}. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming remain componentwise, so the Scott-facing route cannot turn the DCPO packet into a host domain, quotient order, selected basis enumeration, or ambient semantic-completeness claim.
\end{proof}
