\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompletionAdjointUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-completion-adjoint-namecert}

Following Mac Lane and Bishop--Bridges, the $\CompletionAdjointUp$ packet records the Cauchy completion reflector as a finite adjunction certificate. It sits after the reflector surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionreflector-namecert}, the functorial completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionfunctor-namecert}, the universal extension ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}, the reflective-subcategory boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-reflective-subcategory-namecert}, the regular rational and stream-window interfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names only the displayed unit, counit, and universal-property rows; it is not a quotient completion, a selected limit object, an ambient reflective subcategory theorem, or an appeal to countable choice.

\begin{definition}[Completion adjoint carrier]
\label{def:completion-adjoint-carrier}
A $\CompletionAdjointUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(R,F,U,V,S,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$ row from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionreflector-namecert}, recording the source-to-complete handoff through the Cauchy completion reflector surface.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ row from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionfunctor-namecert}, carrying the functorial source, regular-Cauchy, seal, and classifier data.
\item $U$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ row from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}, recording extension existence, extension uniqueness, and unit compatibility for the same displayed source.
\item $V$ is the $\CauchyCompletionReflectiveSubcategoryUp$ visibility row from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-reflective-subcategory-namecert}: it records that complete objects are read through the reflected completion surface, not through a host subcategory object.
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ schedule for completion requests, approximating maps, unit reads, and counit reads.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the regular Cauchy names inspected by $F$ and by the universal-property row $U$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger for all approximation and triangle reads used by the unit-counit ledger.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal exposed only after $R,F,U,V,S,Q,D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the reflector, functor, universal-property, reflective-subcategory, stream, readback, dyadic, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from any completion-adjunction consumer through the rows $R,F,U,V,S,Q,D,E$ and then through the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompletionAdjointUp$, $\CauchyCompletionReflectorUp$, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\CauchyCompletionReflectiveSubcategoryUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionAdjointUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected limits, host reflective subcategories, functor extensionality, countable choice, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Completion adjoint NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:completion-adjoint-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompletionAdjointUp$ carrier $A=(R,F,U,V,S,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionAdjointUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the twelve displayed rows; reflector exposure through $R$; functorial completion exposure through $F$; universal-property exposure through $U$; reflective-subcategory visibility through $V$; finite request scheduling through $S$; regular rational readback through $Q$; dyadic tolerance accounting through $D$; terminal RealUp sealing through $E$; componentwise transport through $H$; continuation replay through $C$; package provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows. The adjunction-facing data are precisely the reflector row $R$, the functor row $F$, the universal-property row $U$, the reflective-subcategory visibility row $V$, and the finite analytic rows $S,Q,D,E$.

Read the structural rows separately. The transport row $H$ preserves those displayed rows componentwise, while $C$ replays the same route for completion-adjunction consumers.

The provenance and naming rows $P,N$ attach the packet to the cited completion, stream, regular-readback, dyadic, and Real certificates. Since no further coordinate is present, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Completion adjoint unit-counit ledger]
\label{thm:completion-adjoint-unit-counit-ledger}
Every unit or counit read exported by an accepted $\CompletionAdjointUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
R,F,U,V \longrightarrow S,Q,D \longrightarrow E,H,C,P,N .
$$
The unit side reads the source-to-completion handoff through the reflector and functor rows, while the counit side reads the complete-object return through the universal-property and reflective-subcategory visibility rows. Both sides use the same $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows before the structural ledger is inspected.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the rows $R,F,U,V$. They are the only coordinates in the packet that can name the reflector, functor, extension, uniqueness, and reflected-object handoff needed by an adjunction consumer.

Next read $S,Q,D,E$. These rows supply finite request schedules, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance accounting, and the terminal Real seal for both the unit-facing and counit-facing directions.

Finally inspect $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, source, and name the same displayed ledger, so the unit and counit routes cannot escape to a quotient completion, selected limit, host reflective subcategory, functor-extensional equality, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}
