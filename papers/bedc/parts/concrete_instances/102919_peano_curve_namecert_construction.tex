\begin{definition}[PeanoCurve finite dyadic subdivision carrier]
\label{def:peano-curve-carrier}
A $\PeanoCurveUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(I,G,S,X,Y,E,R,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the interval-address row. It records a finite dyadic interval address, its precision level, and the corresponding $\StreamNameUp$ observation window before any planar readback is exposed.
\item $G$ is the grid-subdivision row. For each displayed interval address in $I$, it records the finite dyadic square cell selected by the Peano subdivision rule, including the horizontal and vertical dyadic endpoints.
\item $S$ is the sibling-continuity row. Adjacent dyadic interval cells are required to map to edge-adjacent or coincident dyadic square cells at the same precision, so the graph can be read through the $\ContinuousMapUp$ surface.
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the two coordinate readback rows. They turn the horizontal and vertical dyadic endpoints of $G$ into $\RegSeqRatUp$ and then $\RealUp$ coordinate seals.
\item $E$ is the error-budget row. A request for square precision $2^{-n}$ is answered by an interval precision and a dyadic diameter bound supplied through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$.
\item $R$ is the finite surjection readback row. Every displayed dyadic square cell at precision $n$ is paired with at least one interval address whose grid-subdivision row lands inside that cell.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for interval addresses, grid cells, sibling-continuity witnesses, coordinate readbacks, error budgets, surjection readbacks, replay data, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through
$$

\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PeanoCurveUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PeanoCurveUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,G,S,X,Y,E,R,H,C,Q,N$. It has no coordinate for a completed function space, arbitrary pointwise choice, quotient equality of real names, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or a standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PeanoCurve NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:peano-curve-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\PeanoCurveUp$ carrier
$$
P=(I,G,S,X,Y,E,R,H,C,Q,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\PeanoCurveUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the interval-address, grid-subdivision, sibling-continuity, coordinate readback, error-budget, finite surjection, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item dyadic subdivision exactness, requiring every planar cell read to open $I$ and then $G$ before a coordinate seal is exposed;
\item continuity budget exactness, requiring adjacent interval addresses and square-cell diameters to be checked by $S$ and $E$ before the carrier is consumed through $\ContinuousMapUp$;
\item finite-grid surjection exactness, requiring every displayed dyadic square cell to be met by the readback row $R$ at the requested precision;
\item structural exhaustion, requiring $H,C,Q,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the listed finite dyadic route.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those five rows, and none exports a host-level function space, arbitrary pointwise choice, quotient equality of real names, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or a bridge credential.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:peano-curve-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the interval-address row, grid-subdivision row, sibling-continuity row, coordinate readback rows, error-budget row, finite surjection row, and structural rows.

Dyadic subdivision exactness is read from $I$ and $G$. A consumer cannot inspect planar coordinates until a finite interval address and its selected dyadic square cell have both been displayed.

Continuity budget exactness is read from $S$ and $E$. The sibling row checks that adjacent interval cells have compatible square cells, while the error-budget row supplies the dyadic diameter bound requested by the continuous-map consumer.

Finite-grid surjection exactness is read from $R$. For each displayed square cell at the requested precision, the row supplies an interval address whose grid-subdivision cell lands in that square cell.

Finally $H,C,Q,N$ add no analytic coordinate. They preserve, replay, source, and name the same finite route, so the excluded function-space, choice, quotient, compactness, completeness, verification, and bridge data have no row from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:peano-curve-continuous-surjection-surface}

\begin{proof}

The coordinate readbacks are then forced through $X$ and $Y$. Each coordinate endpoint is read as regular rational data and then as a terminal real-name seal, so the planar point is exposed only through the sibling $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ routes.
