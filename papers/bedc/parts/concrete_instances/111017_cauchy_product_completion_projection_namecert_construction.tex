\begin{theorem}[CauchyProductCompletionProjection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-completion-projection-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted packet
$$
J=(Q,\pi_0,\pi_1,S_0,S_1,D_0,D_1,R_0,R_1,L_0,L_1,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductCompletionProjectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of product-packet admission, selector exposure, coordinate stream-window exposure, dyadic-budget accounting, regular readback, coordinate completion-seal nonescape, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming for the displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-product-completion-projection-carrier}. The row $Q$ supplies the already displayed product-completion packet, while $\pi_0,\pi_1$ determine which coordinate route is being opened.

For each selector, the corresponding stream window is read before any dyadic budget or regular readback is available. Thus $S_0,S_1$ precede $D_0,D_1$, and the regular rows $R_0,R_1$ are exposed only through those windows and budgets.

The completion-seal rows $L_0,L_1$ bind the coordinate read back to $Q$. The transport table $H$ and replay table $C$ preserve the same product packet, selector, window, budget, readback, and seal rows, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming.

Since these are the only carrier coordinates, the NameCert surface has no route to quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, host product equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:cauchy-product-completion-projection-route}

The left and right selectors expose only the displayed coordinate windows and their completion seals; they do not turn the product-completion packet into a host tuple, a quotient-completion equality, or a chosen representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
