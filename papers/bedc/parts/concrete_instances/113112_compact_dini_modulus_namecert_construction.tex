\begin{theorem}[Compact Dini modulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-dini-modulus-namecert-obligations}

$$

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item compact-source admission through $K$;
\item monotone-family admission through $F$ and $M$;
\item finite Dini-fold exactness through $\epsilon$ and $L$;
\item uniform-modulus output through $U$ and regular rational readback through $R$;
\item nonescape from host-selected limits, arbitrary open-cover compactness, quotient real equality, and ambient real completeness through the seal row $E$ and the transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The compact-source obligation is exactly the admission of $K$. The monotone-family obligation is exactly the joint visibility of $F$ and $M$, because no later row contains a separate family selector or host order comparison.

The finite Dini-fold obligation is the route through $\epsilon$ and $L$. The tolerance budget is fixed before the fold is read, and $L$ stores the compact-net lower-bound comparisons together with the finite maximum of the displayed center thresholds.

The output obligation is the handoff through $U$ and $R$. The row $U$ is the only uniform-modulus output, and $R$ is the only regular rational readback exposed before the terminal real seal.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ close the packet boundary. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host-selected limits, arbitrary open covers, selected subcovers, quotient real equality, countable choice, or ambient real completeness, none of those readings can be exported by the local NameCert surface.
