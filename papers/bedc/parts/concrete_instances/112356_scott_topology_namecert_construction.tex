\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ScottTopologyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-scott-topology-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Scott and Gierz--Hofmann--Keimel--Lawson--Mislove--Scott, the $\ScottTopologyUp$ packet records the topology generated by upper sets inaccessible by directed suprema as finite BEDC naming data. It sits beside the directed-complete order surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dcpo-namecert}, the domain packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scott-domain-namecert}, the interval real presentation of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scott-real-namecert}, the way-below interval route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-intervaldomainwaybelow-namecert}, and the indexed-open topology surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}. The packet names Scott-open observations for downstream domain-theoretic consumers; it is not a host powerset topology, quotient-open equality, selected directed supremum operation, arbitrary open lattice, choice principle, or Lean-verified bridge.

\begin{definition}[ScottTopology carrier]
\label{def:scott-topology-carrier}
A $\ScottTopologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(D,S,U,I,B,O,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DcpoUp$ source row, exposing the finite order, directed-family schedule, supremum row, and lower-bound exactness already named by the directed-complete packet.
\item $S$ is the $\ScottDomainUp$ source row, recording the domain-facing compact-basis and represented-space handoff rows that may consume the same directed-supremum route.
\item $U$ is the upper-set row, saying that every accepted open observation is upward closed with respect to the displayed order in $D$.
\item $I$ is the directed-supremum inaccessibility row, requiring membership of a directed supremum to be witnessed by membership at some displayed directed-family stage before the supremum is treated as open.
\item $B$ is the way-below basis row. For interval-domain real consumers it reads the $\IntervalDomainWayBelowUp$ refinements used by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-scott-real-namecert}; for general domain consumers it records the compact-basis approximation row already exposed by $S$.
\item $O$ is the $\TopologyUp$ handoff row, exporting the accepted Scott-open observations as indexed open rows without adding a second open-set carrier.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the DCPO source, Scott-domain source, upper-set row, directed-supremum inaccessibility row, way-below basis row, topology handoff, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Scott-open request through $D,S,U,I,B,O,H$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ScottTopologyUp$, $\DcpoUp$, $\ScottDomainUp$, $\ScottRealUp$, $\IntervalDomainWayBelowUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ScottTopologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $D,S,U,I,B,O,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for arbitrary powersets, quotient-open equality, selected directed suprema, host topological equality, choice, or a Lean theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ScottTopology NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:scott-topology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ScottTopologyUp$ carrier $T=(D,S,U,I,B,O,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ScottTopologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the $\DcpoUp$ and $\ScottDomainUp$ sources, upper-set closure, directed-supremum inaccessibility, way-below basis compatibility, $\TopologyUp$ handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every Scott-open consumer must read $D,S,U,I,B,O,H,C,P,N$ before treating an open observation as public.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:scott-topology-carrier}. The order source and domain source are $D$ and $S$, the Scott-open tests are $U,I,B$, the topology export is $O$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

The upper-set row $U$ is read against the displayed order in $D$. Thus an open observation cannot be consumed independently of the directed-complete order carrier.

The directed-supremum inaccessibility row $I$ is read against the directed-family and supremum rows already named by $\DcpoUp$ and by the $\ScottDomainUp$ source. It requires a displayed stage witness before a supremum membership is exported as open data.

The way-below basis row $B$ supplies the compact-basis route. For real-facing domain consumers this route is the $\IntervalDomainWayBelowUp$ refinement used by $\ScottRealUp$; for general domain consumers it is the compact approximation row of $S$.

Finally $O$ hands the accepted Scott-open observations to $\TopologyUp$, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since quotient opens, arbitrary powersets, host topological equality, selected suprema, choice, and Lean verification are absent from the carrier, these rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
