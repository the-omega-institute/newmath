\chapter{$\ZNormalUp$ NameCert Construction}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-znormal-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ZNormalUp$ packet exposes the structural normal-form boundary used by the closed-normal consistency surface as a finite NameCert object. It sits beside the carry packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zcarry-namecert}, the normal false-exclusion packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normalfalseexclusion-namecert}, the endpoint ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalendpointledger-namecert}, and the main closed-normal consistency packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalconsistencymain-namecert}. Those siblings consume carry, false endpoint, endpoint-ledger, and main-result rows; this packet records the closed normal-form carrier that they may read without importing a Fibonacci value interpretation.

\begin{definition}[$\ZNormalUp$ carrier]
\label{def:z-normal-carrier}
A $\ZNormalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(h,W,C,L,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $h$ is the source history row;
\item $W$ is the closed structural witness $\mathsf{ZNormal}(h)$ generated by empty, zero-extension, one-after-empty, and one-above-zero rows;
\item $C$ is the restricted classifier row, an $\hsame$ comparison between histories already accepted by $\mathsf{ZNormal}$;
\item $L$ is the ledger row recording the displayed normal-form witness behind the visible class;
\item $H$ stores the adjacent-one exclusion row, including the rejection of the displayed word $\mathtt{word\_011}$;
\item $T$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ routes for the source, classifier, ledger, and exclusion rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, and the sibling boundaries cited above;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for Fibonacci value preservation, uniqueness of arithmetic value, or an external number interpretation.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:z-normal-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of the carrier in \autoref{def:z-normal-carrier}, the closed $\mathsf{ZNormal}$ source row, the restricted $\hsame$ classifier row, the displayed stability rows for the four normal-form constructors, the normal-form ledger row, the adjacent-one exclusion row, transport and continuation rows, provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $Z=(h,W,C,L,H,T,P,N)$. The witness $W$ supplies the closed source row, while $C$ and $L$ supply the restricted classifier and ledger rows.

The stability surface is finite because the structural judgment has exactly four generating rows: empty, zero-extension, one-after-empty, and one-above-zero. The exclusion row $H$ records that no accepted one-headed history has a one-headed tail, so the certificate cannot admit an unlisted normal-form constructor.

The rows $T$, $P$, and $N$ then package transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming for that same finite surface. Since the carrier lacks arithmetic-value coordinates, the NameCert obligations stop at structural normal-form data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ public normal-form boundary]
\label{thm:z-normal-public-normal-form-boundary}
Every public read of an accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet factors through the closed $\mathsf{ZNormal}$ source over $\BHist$, the four displayed normal-form constructor rows, the restricted $\hsame$ classifier, the normal-form ledger, and the transport routes for those rows. No public read can extract Fibonacci value preservation, uniqueness of arithmetic value, or an external number interpretation from this packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:z-normal-namecert-obligations}. It identifies the public certificate surface with the structural source, stability, classifier, ledger, transport, provenance, and local naming rows.

The carry sibling \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zcarry-namecert} reads the same structural boundary only after it displays the source non-normal and target normal rows. The closed-normal endpoint siblings \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalendpointledger-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalconsistencymain-namecert} read normality only as an endpoint row, not as an arithmetic value claim.

Thus any consumer route is confined to $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ data. The excluded coordinates are absent from the carrier, so they cannot be exported by the public boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ false-exclusion boundary]
\label{thm:z-normal-false-exclusion-boundary}
When a closed-normal consistency consumer combines a $\ZNormalUp$ packet with the normal false-exclusion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normalfalseexclusion-namecert}, the normality row is available only as a displayed finite endpoint witness. The combination supports the closed-normal boundary but does not supply a subject-reduction route from arbitrary closed false proofs to normal endpoints.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the normal-form side first. By \autoref{thm:z-normal-public-normal-form-boundary}, a $\ZNormalUp$ packet exposes only the structural source, constructor rows, restricted classifier, ledger, and finite transport routes.

Read the false-exclusion side through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normalfalseexclusion-namecert}. That packet consumes an already displayed closed normal false endpoint. It does not create the subject-reduction route needed to turn an arbitrary closed false proof into such an endpoint.

The endpoint ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalendpointledger-namecert} and the main-result packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalconsistencymain-namecert} therefore receive a normality row only at the closed-normal boundary. The joint surface supports that boundary and refuses the stronger closed-consistency read.
\end{proof}
