\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ApophaticSocketUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticsocket-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet realizes apophatic naming as a finite certificate surface. It names the socket kind, the requested external supply shape, the audit gate that keeps the request visible, the site row where the request is made, the transport and replay rows that preserve the boundary, the package provenance, and the local \NameCert{} row. This socket specializes the boundary-name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert}: ApophaticName supplies the negative naming row, while ApophaticSocket adds the requested-supply shape, audit gate, site row, and noninternalization ledger. The packet does not make the requested supply internally available.

\begin{definition}[ApophaticSocket ledger carrier]
\label{def:apophatic-socket-ledger-carrier}
An $\ApophaticSocketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the socket-kind row, recording whether the named boundary concerns an unwitnessed selector, quotient identification, proposition-extensionality collapse, causal-continuity assumption, inscription novelty, outermost $\mathsf{T}$ supply, or another displayed externality class;
\item $S_0$ is the requested-supply-shape row, recording the content requested from the boundary without supplying that content as an internal constructor, definition, theorem, or setup field;
\item $G$ is the audit-gate row, recording the finite question that detects visible socket use and rejects hidden internalization of the requested supply;
\item $A$ is the site row, recording the manuscript, Lean, or governance location at which the socket is invoked and the local claim that depends on it;
\item $L$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,S_0,G,A$, the continuation row, the package row, and the local naming row;
\item $Q$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect the socket kind, requested supply shape, audit gate, site row, provenance, and local certificate;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Hist$, $\mathsf{GAP}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, \NameCert{}, apophatic names, audit-gate rows, site rows, and origin ledgers;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticSocketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains exactly these rows. It has no coordinate for the requested external supply itself, no hidden theorem converting that supply into substrate content, no anonymous import channel, and no route by which the apophatic name becomes a positive internal description.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticSocketUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:apophatic-socket-ledger-carrier} consists of carrier admission for
$$
S=(K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N),
$$
socket-kind exposure, requested-supply-shape display, audit-gate exactness, site-row locality, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and non-internalization for the requested external supply.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApophaticSocketUp.ApophaticSocketCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:apophatic-socket-ledger-carrier}. The packet exposes exactly the rows $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$.

The naming obligations are read row by row. The socket kind is supplied by $K$, the requested supply shape by $S_0$, the gate question by $G$, and the invocation site by $A$.

Transport and consumer access are confined by $L$ and $Q$. These rows replay the displayed socket packet under componentwise $\hsame$ movement and expose only the recorded continuation routes.

Finally $P$ and $N$ record provenance and the local naming certificate. Since the requested external supply has no coordinate in the carrier, the NameCert surface can name the boundary without exporting the supply as internal content.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket gate correspondence]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-gate-correspondence}
For an accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet, the audit-gate row $G$ is the complete public gate corresponding to the socket-kind row $K$ and the requested-supply-shape row $S_0$. A downstream consumer may treat a use of the socket as visible exactly when it factors through the rows $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$ and may reject any use of the same requested supply that has no displayed gate row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApophaticSocketUp.ApophaticSocketCarrier\_gate\_correspondence}
\begin{proof}
Read the socket side first. The rows $K$ and $S_0$ record the kind of externality and the shape of supply being requested.

Read the enforcement side next. The row $G$ records the finite audit question attached to that socket, while $A$ fixes the site at which the question is asked.

The transport and replay rows $L$ and $Q$ preserve the same gate for downstream consumers, and $P,N$ package and name it. Therefore visible socket use is exactly use through the displayed packet, and an un-gated use of the requested supply has no accepted route through this carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket noninternalization boundary]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-noninternalization-boundary}
For an accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet, the requested supply shape $S_0$ remains boundary data. The packet may name the supply shape, expose the site where it is requested, attach an audit gate, transport the displayed rows by $\hsame$, replay them by $\Cont$, and record $\Pkg$ and \NameCert{} provenance, but it cannot produce the requested external supply as an internal constructor, definition, theorem, setup field, or imported host principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApophaticSocketUp.ApophaticSocketCarrier\_noninternalization\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the requested-supply-shape row $S_0$ from the carrier. It records the shape of the requested externality, not an inhabitant or proof of that externality inside the substrate.

The gate and site rows $G$ and $A$ keep the request visible. They classify and locate the boundary use, but they do not add a coordinate for the requested supply itself.

The remaining rows $L,Q,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name the displayed boundary packet. Because the carrier inventory contains no constructor, definition, theorem, setup field, or host-principle coordinate for the requested supply, the packet preserves the noninternalization boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket consumer surface]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-consumer-surface}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet factors through the finite rows
$$
K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N.
$$
Such a consumer may inspect the socket kind, requested supply shape, audit gate, invocation site, transport rows, replay routes, provenance, and local naming certificate. It may not extract a positive description of the external source, a hidden witness for the requested supply, an unmarked dependency, or a theorem object internalizing the socket.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApophaticSocketUp.ApophaticSocketCarrier\_consumer\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Let a downstream consumer route be given. By \autoref{def:apophatic-socket-ledger-carrier}, the source carrier is the finite tuple $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$.

The consumer first reads the socket, supply-shape, gate, and site rows. These rows provide the full public boundary surface.

The rows $L$ and $Q$ supply all permitted transport and replay, and $P,N$ supply provenance and the local certificate. The non-extraction claim follows from the carrier inventory: none of the forbidden objects appears among the displayed rows, so no consumer route out of the packet can project it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket obligation closure package]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-obligation-closure-package}
The $\ApophaticSocketUp$ obligation surface is the finite package consisting of the carrier row of \autoref{def:apophatic-socket-ledger-carrier}, the local NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-namecert-obligations}, the gate correspondence of \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-gate-correspondence}, the noninternalization boundary of \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-noninternalization-boundary}, and the consumer surface of \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-consumer-surface}. Closing $\NameCert_{\ApophaticSocketUp}$ requires exactly carrier admission, socket-kind exposure, requested-supply-shape display, audit-gate exactness, site-row locality, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming exactness for $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition lists all rows available to the package. The NameCert theorem records the local naming obligations on those rows, while the gate, noninternalization, and consumer theorems identify the public uses that must remain visible.

Reading these clauses together gives the obligation surface. Each required proof target is tied to one displayed coordinate of $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$, and no additional supply coordinate is introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket gated supply route exhaustion]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-gated-supply-route-exhaustion}
Every visible use of the requested supply shape in an accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet factors through the finite rows
$$
K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N.
$$
In particular, the socket kind is read through $K$, the requested supply shape through $S_0$, the gate through $G$, the invocation site through $A$, transport and replay through $L,Q$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ApophaticSocketUp.ApophaticSocketCarrier\_gated\_supply\_route\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Let a visible supply use be given. By the gate correspondence theorem, visibility means that the use has the displayed audit-gate row $G$ attached to the socket kind and requested supply shape.

The only carrier coordinates that can support such a use are $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$. The rows $L$ and $Q$ preserve and replay the same displayed packet, and $P,N$ package and name it.

Therefore every visible use is exhausted by these rows. A route outside them has no accepted coordinate in the carrier and so cannot count as a visible socket use.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket boundary noninternalization ledger]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-boundary-noninternalization-ledger}
For an accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet, no requested supply named by $S_0$ is internalized by the ledger. The ledger may record the supply shape, the audit question, the invocation site, permitted transport, replay, provenance, and local naming, but it cannot produce the requested supply as a constructor, definition, theorem, setup field, anonymous import, or positive internal description.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the requested-supply-shape row $S_0$. It records the boundary request as ledger data and not as a substrate object satisfying the request.

The gate and site rows $G,A$ keep the request inspectable. The transport and replay rows $L,Q$ preserve the same visible request, while $P,N$ record provenance and local naming.

Since the carrier inventory contains no row for the requested supply itself, no ledger projection can internalize it. The only available outputs are the displayed boundary rows and their permitted read routes.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket scoped supply ledger]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-scoped-supply-ledger}
Every visible supply request in an accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet is scoped by the concrete rows
$$
K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N.
$$
The socket kind is read through $K$, the requested external supply shape through $S_0$, the audit gate through $G$, the invocation site through $A$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $L$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $Q$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local \NameCert{} naming through $N$. This scoped ledger records the request without internalizing the requested external supply.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:apophatic-socket-ledger-carrier}. Its visible coordinates are exactly $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$, so every accepted packet already carries the supply request as a finite displayed ledger.

Apply the gated supply route exhaustion of \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-gated-supply-route-exhaustion}. Any visible use of the requested supply shape must pass through the socket kind, supply shape, gate, site, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Finally apply the boundary noninternalization ledger of \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-boundary-noninternalization-ledger}. The scoped packet can expose the request and its audit route, but it has no coordinate from which the requested external supply itself can be projected.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket kernel-scope obligation package]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-kernel-scope-obligation-package}
For an accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet, the rows
$$
K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N
$$
are the complete scoped kernel surface for carrier admission, gate correspondence, noninternalization, and consumer factoring. Carrier admission reads exactly these rows; gate correspondence binds $K,S_0,G,A$ to visible socket use; noninternalization is recorded by the absence of a requested-supply coordinate; and every consumer factors through $L,Q,P,N$ together with the same displayed socket rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier and local NameCert obligations. \autoref{def:apophatic-socket-ledger-carrier} lists exactly $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$, and \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-namecert-obligations} reads those rows as carrier admission, socket-kind exposure, requested-supply display, gate exactness, site locality, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Next bind the gate and consumer routes. \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-gate-correspondence} makes $G$ the public gate for the socket kind $K$ and requested supply shape $S_0$, while \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-consumer-surface} forces every downstream read through the displayed packet.

Finally apply the boundary and route ledgers. \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-gated-supply-route-exhaustion} and \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-scoped-supply-ledger} exhaust visible supply use through the same rows, and \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-boundary-noninternalization-ledger} keeps the requested external supply outside the carrier. These clauses give the scoped kernel surface without adding an internal supply coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket positive supply refusal scope]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-positive-supply-refusal-scope}
For an accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet with visible boundary request rows
$$
K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N,
$$
any visible request through these rows remains a request row. It cannot be consumed as a constructor, theorem, setup field, import permission, or positive internal description of the requested supply.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the gate correspondence of \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-gate-correspondence}. Visibility of the request is exposed only through the socket kind $K$, requested supply shape $S_0$, audit gate $G$, and site row $A$.

Next apply the noninternalization boundary of \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-noninternalization-boundary} and the boundary noninternalization ledger of \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-boundary-noninternalization-ledger}. These results say that the carrier records the request shape and its audit route without supplying a constructor, theorem, setup field, import permission, or positive internal description.

Finally use the scoped supply ledger of \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-scoped-supply-ledger} together with the kernel-scope obligation package of \autoref{thm:apophatic-socket-kernel-scope-obligation-package}. They exhaust every visible consumer route through $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$, so no downstream consumer can reinterpret the visible boundary request as positive internal supply.
\end{proof}
