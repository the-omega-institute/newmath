\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ApophaticSocketUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticsocket-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet realizes apophatic naming as a finite certificate surface. It names the socket kind, the requested external supply shape, the audit gate that keeps the request visible, the site row where the request is made, the transport and replay rows that preserve the boundary, the package provenance, and the local \NameCert{} row. The packet does not make the requested supply internally available.

\begin{definition}[ApophaticSocket ledger carrier]
\label{def:apophatic-socket-ledger-carrier}
An $\ApophaticSocketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the socket-kind row, recording whether the named boundary concerns an unwitnessed selector, quotient identification, proposition-extensionality collapse, causal-continuity assumption, inscription novelty, outermost $\mathsf{T}$ supply, or another displayed externality class;
\item $S_0$ is the requested-supply-shape row, recording the content requested from the boundary without supplying that content as an internal constructor, definition, theorem, or setup field;
\item $G$ is the audit-gate row, recording the finite question that detects visible socket use and rejects hidden internalization of the requested supply;
\item $A$ is the site row, recording the manuscript, Lean, or governance location at which the socket is invoked and the local claim that depends on it;
\item $L$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,S_0,G,A$, the continuation row, the package row, and the local naming row;
\item $Q$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect the socket kind, requested supply shape, audit gate, site row, provenance, and local certificate;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Hist$, $\mathsf{GAP}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, \NameCert{}, apophatic names, audit-gate rows, site rows, and origin ledgers;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticSocketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains exactly these rows. It has no coordinate for the requested external supply itself, no hidden theorem converting that supply into substrate content, no anonymous import channel, and no route by which the apophatic name becomes a positive internal description.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ApophaticSocketUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:apophatic-socket-ledger-carrier} consists of carrier admission for
$$
S=(K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N),
$$
socket-kind exposure, requested-supply-shape display, audit-gate exactness, site-row locality, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and non-internalization for the requested external supply.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:apophatic-socket-ledger-carrier}. The packet exposes exactly the rows $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$.

The naming obligations are read row by row. The socket kind is supplied by $K$, the requested supply shape by $S_0$, the gate question by $G$, and the invocation site by $A$.

Transport and consumer access are confined by $L$ and $Q$. These rows replay the displayed socket packet under componentwise $\hsame$ movement and expose only the recorded continuation routes.

Finally $P$ and $N$ record provenance and the local naming certificate. Since the requested external supply has no coordinate in the carrier, the NameCert surface can name the boundary without exporting the supply as internal content.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket gate correspondence]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-gate-correspondence}
For an accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet, the audit-gate row $G$ is the complete public gate corresponding to the socket-kind row $K$ and the requested-supply-shape row $S_0$. A downstream consumer may treat a use of the socket as visible exactly when it factors through the rows $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$ and may reject any use of the same requested supply that has no displayed gate row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the socket side first. The rows $K$ and $S_0$ record the kind of externality and the shape of supply being requested.

Read the enforcement side next. The row $G$ records the finite audit question attached to that socket, while $A$ fixes the site at which the question is asked.

The transport and replay rows $L$ and $Q$ preserve the same gate for downstream consumers, and $P,N$ package and name it. Therefore visible socket use is exactly use through the displayed packet, and an un-gated use of the requested supply has no accepted route through this carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket noninternalization boundary]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-noninternalization-boundary}
For an accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet, the requested supply shape $S_0$ remains boundary data. The packet may name the supply shape, expose the site where it is requested, attach an audit gate, transport the displayed rows by $\hsame$, replay them by $\Cont$, and record $\Pkg$ and \NameCert{} provenance, but it cannot produce the requested external supply as an internal constructor, definition, theorem, setup field, or imported host principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the requested-supply-shape row $S_0$ from the carrier. It records the shape of the requested externality, not an inhabitant or proof of that externality inside the substrate.

The gate and site rows $G$ and $A$ keep the request visible. They classify and locate the boundary use, but they do not add a coordinate for the requested supply itself.

The remaining rows $L,Q,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name the displayed boundary packet. Because the carrier inventory contains no constructor, definition, theorem, setup field, or host-principle coordinate for the requested supply, the packet preserves the noninternalization boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApophaticSocket consumer surface]
\label{thm:apophatic-socket-consumer-surface}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\ApophaticSocketUp$ packet factors through the finite rows
$$
K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N.
$$
Such a consumer may inspect the socket kind, requested supply shape, audit gate, invocation site, transport rows, replay routes, provenance, and local naming certificate. It may not extract a positive description of the external source, a hidden witness for the requested supply, an unmarked dependency, or a theorem object internalizing the socket.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a downstream consumer route be given. By \autoref{def:apophatic-socket-ledger-carrier}, the source carrier is the finite tuple $K,S_0,G,A,L,Q,P,N$.

The consumer first reads the socket, supply-shape, gate, and site rows. These rows provide the full public boundary surface.

The rows $L$ and $Q$ supply all permitted transport and replay, and $P,N$ supply provenance and the local certificate. The non-extraction claim follows from the carrier inventory: none of the forbidden objects appears among the displayed rows, so no consumer route out of the packet can project it.
\end{proof}
