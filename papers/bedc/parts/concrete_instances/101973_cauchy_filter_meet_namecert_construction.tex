\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyFilterMeetUp$}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley filter terminology, the $\CauchyFilterMeetUp$ packet records the finite common-refinement operation by which two displayed Cauchy-filter packets over the same uniform surface are read as one refinement packet before any limit or completion-facing consumer inspects the route. It sits after the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}, the source $\CauchyFilterUp$ packets of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the basis generator of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-filter-basis-namecert}, and the limit boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilterlimit-namecert}; it reads finite windows through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, regular rational rows through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, dyadic tolerances through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and terminal seals through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a binary meet of displayed filter data, not quotient filters, selected limits, countable choice, host equality, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy filter meet carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-filter-meet-carrier}
A $\CauchyFilterMeetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(U,F_0,F_1,B_0,B_1,J,S,R,D,L,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the shared $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage row. It supplies the finite entourage requests, diagonal reads, and refinement reads through which both source filters are interpreted.
\item $F_0$ and $F_1$ are the two source $\CauchyFilterUp$ rows whose displayed requests are to be met.
\item $B_0$ and $B_1$ are the corresponding $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ rows. Each basis row exposes the finite basis requests, window data, regular readback, and dyadic tolerance ledger used by its source filter.
\item $J$ is the binary common-refinement row. For every displayed pair of basis requests from $B_0$ and $B_1$, it names a displayed basis request refining both sources over the same entourage surface $U$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row inspected after the common-refinement row has been read.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row attached to the displayed windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used to verify that the common-refinement request is small enough for both Cauchy-filter sources.
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyFilterLimitUp$ handoff row available only after $U,F_0,F_1,B_0,B_1,J,S,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached by a Real-facing consumer through the same handoff route.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the uniform row, both source-filter rows, both basis rows, the common-refinement row, the window, readback, tolerance, handoff, seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a pair of source-filter requests through $U,F_0,F_1,B_0,B_1,J,S,R,D,L,E$ in that order.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyFilterMeetUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$, $\CauchyFilterLimitUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyFilterMeetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted meet packets only through the displayed route
$$
U,F_0,F_1,B_0,B_1,J,S,R,D,L,E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
No carrier coordinate stores a quotient filter, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, host equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, Lean verification row, or bridge certificate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy filter meet NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-meet-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyFilterMeetUp$ carrier
$$
M=(U,F_0,F_1,B_0,B_1,J,S,R,D,L,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyFilterMeetUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the shared uniform row, two source Cauchy-filter rows, two Cauchy-filter-basis rows, common-refinement row, window row, regular readback row, dyadic tolerance row, limit handoff, terminal seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item same-uniform-source discipline, requiring both source filters and both basis rows to be read over the shared entourage surface $U$ before a meet request is accepted;
\item binary common-refinement coverage, requiring every pair of displayed basis requests from $B_0$ and $B_1$ to pass through $J$ and name a displayed request refining both;
\item finite observation exactness, requiring the refinement request to read only $S,R,D$ before it reaches the limit handoff $L$ and terminal seal $E$;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the route order under componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $P,N$ to name only this finite common-refinement route and to export no quotient filter, selected limit, countable choice, host equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-filter-meet-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the shared uniform row, both source filters, both basis generators, the common-refinement row, the finite observation rows, the limit and seal rows, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The same-uniform-source discipline is fixed by the row order. A meet request first reads $U$, then reads $F_0,F_1$ and $B_0,B_1$ as source data over that same uniform surface before any common-refinement row is available.

Binary common-refinement coverage is the role of $J$. For a displayed pair of basis requests, $J$ names the refining request and the two refinement reads back to the source requests. The row is finite data in the carrier, not an appeal to an ambient filter lattice.

Finite observation exactness follows by replaying $C$ after $J$ is read. The refining request passes through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ windows $S$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row $D$ before it may reach $L$ or $E$.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ cannot enlarge the packet. They transport, replay, source, and name the displayed rows only, so the excluded quotient, choice, host-equality, real-completeness, Lean-verification, and bridge-checking data are outside the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy filter meet common-refinement factorization]
\label{thm:cauchy-filter-meet-common-refinement-factorization}
Let
$$
M=(U,F_0,F_1,B_0,B_1,J,S,R,D,L,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CauchyFilterMeetUp$ carrier. Every completion-facing or Real-facing consumer of the binary meet factors through the finite route
$$
U,F_0,F_1,B_0,B_1 \longmapsto J \longmapsto S,R,D \longmapsto L \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the consumer receives a displayed common refinement of the two Cauchy-filter packets before it receives a $\CauchyFilterLimitUp$ handoff or a terminal $\RealUp$ seal. The factorization exports no quotient filters, selected limits, countable-choice schedule, host equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a completion-facing or Real-facing consumer exposed by $P$. Carrier projection shows that the consumer can enter the meet packet only through the displayed rows of \autoref{def:cauchy-filter-meet-carrier}.

The source part of the route is forced through $U,F_0,F_1,B_0,B_1$. These rows display the shared entourage surface, the two Cauchy-filter packets, and the two finite basis generators whose requests are being compared.

We now replay the common finite-refinement row. For each displayed pair of basis requests, $J$ supplies the refining request and the two reads back to $B_0$ and $B_1$. The replay uses only the stored row $J$ and therefore stays inside the same finite packet.

The observation part of the route is $S,R,D$. The displayed window, regular readback, and dyadic tolerance rows verify that the refining request remains small enough for both Cauchy sources before the limit boundary is inspected.

Only after this route has been replayed can the packet expose $L$, and only after $L$ can it expose $E$. Componentwise transport through $H$ and continuation replay through $C$ preserve the same route, while $P,N$ record provenance and local naming without adding any excluded coordinate.
\end{proof}
