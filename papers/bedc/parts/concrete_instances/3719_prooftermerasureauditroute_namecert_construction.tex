\begin{definition}[Proof-term erasure audit route carrier]
\label{def:proofterm-erasure-audit-route-carrier}
A $\ProofTermErasureAuditRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(P,E,T,C,R,S,H,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the proof-term row whose original type-checking membership is audited;
\item $E$ is the erased-term row exported to downstream consumers;
\item $T$ is the $\Ext$ typing row that admits the proof-term row and names the classifier membership being preserved;
\item $C$ is the erasure $\Cont$ route from the proof-term row to the erased-term row;
\item $R$ is the subject-reduction compatibility row tying the typing membership in $T$ to the erased public read along $C$;
\item $S$ is the accepted audit stamp row stating that the proof object has been consumed by the local checker and is not part of the exported evidence;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the proof-term row, erased-term row, $\Ext$ typing row, erasure route, subject-reduction row, audit stamp, package row, and local naming row;
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Ext$, $\Cont$, $\SubjectReductionUp$, $\ProofTermErasureTraceUp$, $\TypeCheckingDerivationTraceUp$, $\TypeCheckingMembershipTraceUp$, $\TypePreservingCompilerUp$, $\hsame$, and $\NameCert$ source packets;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ProofTermErasureAuditRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares two accepted carriers only through the displayed proof row, erased row, typing row, erasure route, subject-reduction row, audit stamp, componentwise transports, provenance, and local naming row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Proof-term erasure audit route NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:proofterm-erasure-audit-route-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:proofterm-erasure-audit-route-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ProofTermErasureAuditRouteUp}$ surface consists of six finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission exposes exactly $A=(P,E,T,C,R,S,H,Q,N)$;
\item proof-term membership reads the accepted typing evidence only from the displayed $\Ext$ row $T$;
\item erasure replay routes the exported erased row $E$ only along the displayed $\Cont$ row $C$;
\item subject-reduction compatibility uses only the row $R$ to relate the source membership in $T$ to the erased public read;
\item audit-stamp exactness records in $S$ that proof evidence was consumed locally and that no proof object is exported;
\item provenance and naming exactness bind every downstream read to $H,Q,N$ and to the same displayed proof-term, erased-term, typing, erasure, subject-reduction, and audit-stamp rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of the displayed finite tuple. The rows $P,E,T,C,R,S,H,Q,N$ are the only coordinates exposed by the local certificate.

The membership and replay obligations are read from $T$ and $C$. The proof-term row enters the certificate through the classifier membership row $T$, and the erased-term row is exported only after the displayed erasure route $C$ is replayed.

Subject-reduction compatibility is the role of $R$. It binds the source membership already named by $T$ to the erased read reached through $C$, so a consumer cannot replace the route by host conversion or quotient term equality.

The audit-stamp and ledger obligations are then exhausted by $S,H,Q,N$. The stamp records local consumption of proof evidence, the transports replay the same rows componentwise, the package records the dependency surface, and the local naming row fixes the certificate boundary.
\end{proof}
