\begin{definition}[MetaCIC frontier dependency matrix carrier]
\label{def:metacic-frontier-dependency-matrix-carrier}
A $\MetaCICFrontierDependencyMatrixUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(B,A,L,P,Q,N,D,J,R,H,C,K,E)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B,A,L,P$ are the four positive subject-reduction discharge rows: beta substitution, application-argument stability, lambda-domain subject reduction, and Pi-domain subject reduction.
\item $Q$ is the dependent-codomain obstruction row separating application-argument stability from a closed subject-reduction theorem.
\item $N$ is the MetaCIC normalization frontier row, including the reduction and normal-form boundaries read by normalization consumers.
\item $D$ is the decidability frontier row for bounded checking and closed type-checking consumers.
\item $J$ is the confluence and critical-pair obstruction row read by audit-map consumers.
\item $R$ is the route-classifier row assigning each matrix row to positive support, obstruction, or audit consumer.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all positive, obstruction, classifier, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an audit-map request through a selected matrix row and its route-classifier entry.
\item $K$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetaCICFrontierDependencyMatrixUp$, $\SubjectReductionDischargeSocketUp$, $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$, $\SubjectReductionRouteClassifierUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $E$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICFrontierDependencyMatrixUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only by these finite rows. It has no coordinate for a closed subject-reduction theorem, global confluence, full normalization, total decidability, or an audit-map result that bypasses the route classifier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC frontier dependency matrix obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-frontier-dependency-matrix-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetaCICFrontierDependencyMatrixUp$ carrier $M=(B,A,L,P,Q,N,D,J,R,H,C,K,E)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICFrontierDependencyMatrixUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the four positive subject-reduction rows, dependent-codomain obstruction row, normalization frontier, decidability frontier, confluence boundary, route classifier, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item row polarity, requiring each matrix row to be classified by $R$ as positive support, obstruction, or audit consumer;
\item support factorization, requiring every positive row to factor through one of $B,A,L,P$ before subject-reduction consumers may use it;
\item obstruction factorization, requiring every negative frontier row to factor through exactly one of $Q,N,D,J$ before audit-map consumers may cite it;
\item nonescape, excluding closed subject reduction, global confluence, full normalization, total decidability, and audit-map closure outside the displayed rows.
\end{enumerate}
No frontier dependency matrix obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:metacic-frontier-dependency-matrix-carrier}. The tuple displays the positive subject-reduction rows, obstruction rows, classifier, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row.

Row polarity is the job of $R$. Each matrix entry must be read with its route-classifier tag before any consumer treats it as support or obstruction.

Support factorization then follows by exhaustion of the positive rows. The only positive coordinates are $B,A,L,P$, so a subject-reduction consumer cannot obtain a fifth support row from the matrix.

Obstruction factorization is the matching exhaustion over $Q,N,D,J$. These rows name the dependent-codomain, normalization, decidability, and confluence boundaries, and the replay row $C$ preserves their route tags for audit consumers.

The nonescape clause inspects $K,E$ and the carrier fields. Since no closed subject-reduction theorem, global confluence theorem, full normalization theorem, total decidability theorem, or completed audit-map result appears among the coordinates, none is exported by the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC frontier audit-map route]
\label{thm:metacic-frontier-dependency-matrix-audit-route}
Every audit-map consumer of an accepted $\MetaCICFrontierDependencyMatrixUp$ packet first reads the route classifier $R$, then either one positive support row among $B,A,L,P$ or one obstruction row among $Q,N,D,J$, and only then replays the selected dependency through $H,C,K,E$. Thus the matrix is finite row data for frontier accounting, not a proof of closed subject reduction, confluence, normalization, decidability, or audit-map completion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
An audit-map request begins at $R$, because the carrier gives no untagged dependency coordinate. The classifier chooses the polarity and the named row before the consumer can inspect the row content.

In the positive case, row exhaustion gives one of $B,A,L,P$. In the obstruction case, row exhaustion gives one of $Q,N,D,J$. The two cases are disjoint because the route classifier stores their tags before replay.

The structural rows $H,C,K,E$ preserve and name the selected matrix entry. They do not add a closed theorem or a completed audit-map result, so the consumer remains inside finite frontier accounting.
\end{proof}
