The $\FiniteObservationLockUp$ packet realizes the subject-free observation thesis of \autoref{sec:visions-philosophy-observation-subject-time} as a finite NameCert carrier. It sits beside the real observation budget of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-observation-budget-namecert}, the finite observation-budget selector of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finiteobservationbudgetselector-namecert}, and the observation-budget limiter of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observationbudgetlimiter-namecert}. Those siblings expose consumer service, selected finite budgets, and capped window routes; this packet locks the observation itself to a displayed continuation, history-sameness comparison, finite budget, readback, and no-subject ledger before any Real- or Completion-facing route may consume it.

\begin{definition}[Finite observation lock carrier]
\label{def:finite-observation-lock-carrier}
A $\FiniteObservationLockUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(A,D,Z,Q,W,R,B,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the antecedent history row of the observed continuation;
\item $D$ is the displayed distinction or continuation-tail row;
\item $Z$ is the successor history row reached from $A$ through $D$;
\item $Q$ is the subject-free ledger row recording that the observation signature contains no observer-substance parameter, owner map, or separate viewpoint carrier;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window surface through which the observation becomes readable by finite consumers;
\item $R$ is the readback row consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ or another finite observation reader after $W$ is displayed;
\item $B$ is the finite observation-budget row supplied through $\RealObservationBudgetUp$, $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$, or $\ObservationBudgetLimiterUp$;
\item $U$ is the downstream use row for the Real- or Completion-facing consumer;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $A,D,Z,Q,W,R,B,U$ and the local certificate row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from $A$ through $D$ to $Z$, and from the locked observation row through $W,R,B$ to $U$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteObservationLockUp$, $\RealObservationBudgetUp$, $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$, $\ObservationBudgetLimiterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationLockUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a hidden subject, host observation, quotient stream, selected limit, ambient completeness principle, detached readback, or observer identity outside the finite packet $L$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation lock NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-observation-lock-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-observation-lock-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationLockUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $L=(A,D,Z,Q,W,R,B,U,H,C,P,N)$;
\item continuation and history-sameness lock, requiring the observed event to be read through the displayed $\Cont(A,D,Z)$ route and transported only by the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$;
\item finite budget and readback lock, requiring $W,R,B$ to be displayed before the downstream use row $U$ is consumed;
\item no-subject ledger exposure, requiring $Q$ to block subject-substance parameters, owner maps, viewpoint carriers, and host observation rows;
\item non-escape, excluding hidden subjects, quotient streams, selected limits, ambient completeness, detached readbacks, and observer identities not carried by the finite packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the packet in \autoref{def:finite-observation-lock-carrier}. The visible observation rows are $A,D,Z,Q,W,R,B,U,H,C,P,N$ and there is no other coordinate.

The continuation and history-sameness lock reads the observation as the displayed route from $A$ through $D$ to $Z$. The transport row $H$ can only compare the displayed rows componentwise, so it preserves the locked observation rather than creating a hidden observer surface.

The finite budget and readback lock then routes finite consumers through $W,R,B$ before $U$ is available. The sibling budget surfaces provide window service, selected finite budgets, and limiter caps, but the lock packet requires those rows to remain attached to the same continuation event.

The no-subject ledger row $Q$ records the negative side of subject-free observation. It refuses a subject parameter, an owner map, a separate viewpoint carrier, and a host observation row because none is part of the accepted carrier.

Finally, $C,P,N$ replay, source, and name the same rows. Since the carrier contains no quotient stream, selected limit, ambient completeness principle, detached readback, or external observer identity coordinate, the five obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation lock consumer route]
\label{thm:finite-observation-lock-consumer-route}
Every $\RealUp$, completion-facing, finite-budget, or observer-ledger consumer of an accepted $\FiniteObservationLockUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
A,D,Z,Q,W,R,B,U,H,C,P,N.
$$
The consumer receives the displayed continuation, the no-subject ledger row, finite window and readback rows, the finite budget row, the downstream use row, and the structural transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. It receives no hidden subject, host observation, quotient stream, selected limit, ambient completeness datum, detached readback, or observer identity outside the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted carrier $L=(A,D,Z,Q,W,R,B,U,H,C,P,N)$. The continuation route $A,D,Z$ is the only observation event in the carrier, and $Q$ is the only row that records subject-free admissibility.

Apply \autoref{thm:finite-observation-lock-namecert-obligations}. The finite budget and readback lock forces any downstream use to pass through $W,R,B$ before $U$ is read, while the continuation and history-sameness lock keeps those rows attached to the same displayed observation.

The Real observation budget, finite observation-budget selector, and observation-budget limiter siblings may consume the window, readback, and budget rows. They do not supply a second observation carrier, because the packet gives them only $W,R,B,U,H,C,P,N$ downstream of the locked continuation and no-subject ledger.

Thus every accepted consumer route is exhausted by the displayed packet. The excluded objects require coordinates absent from $L$, so they cannot be projected from an accepted $\FiniteObservationLockUp$ read.
\end{proof}
