\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DiniTheoremUp$}

Following Dini's theorem in constructive analysis, the $\DiniTheoremUp$ packet records only finite compact-window and monotone convergence data. It consumes the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the compact-uniform-continuity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactuniformcontinuity-namecert}, the continuous-map rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the located real boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, and the Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite NameCert for a displayed monotone convergence window; it does not import open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, a host supremum, a selected limit, quotient real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[DiniTheorem carrier]\label{def:dini-theorem-carrier}
A $\DiniTheoremUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(X,F,M,W,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the compact metric source row, carrying the $\CompactMetricUp$ name, its finite inspection windows, and the source-side metric classifier used by downstream rows.
\item $F$ is a finite monotone family of $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows over $X$, together with the pointwise direction ledger that records, for each inspected index pair, which displayed real-name comparison is monotone.
\item $M$ is the compact-uniform-continuity ledger imported from $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$ for the displayed rows of $F$ on the windows of $X$.
\item $W$ is the shared tolerance-window ledger: for each requested dyadic tolerance it lists the finite $X$-windows, family indices, continuity moduli, and located real readbacks inspected by the packet.
\item $R$ is the boundary real-seal row, storing the $\LocatedRealUp$ and $\RealUp$ rows reached by the displayed window reads and by no independent host limit coordinate.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the compact source, continuous-map family, monotonicity ledger, uniform-continuity ledger, tolerance windows, and real-seal rows.
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes from compact windows to continuity rows, from monotone family rows to located real comparisons, and from the shared tolerance ledger to the boundary real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DiniTheoremUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DiniTheoremUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an open cover, sequential compactness witness, ambient supremum, selected limit function, quotient equality of real names, countable choice, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DiniTheorem NameCert obligations]\label{thm:dini-theorem-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\DiniTheoremUp$ carrier $D=(X,F,M,W,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DiniTheoremUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed compact source, monotone continuous-map family, uniform-continuity ledger, tolerance-window ledger, boundary real rows, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows;
\item classifier transport along $H$ and $C$, preserving the same compact windows, family indices, monotone comparisons, continuity moduli, and real-seal reads;
\item monotone ledger exactness, requiring every comparison used by a convergence read to occur in the finite family row $F$;
\item shared-window exactness, requiring every tolerance request to be answered by the displayed compact windows and compact-uniform-continuity rows listed in $W$ and $M$;
\item nonescape, requiring every $\LocatedRealUp$ or $\RealUp$ read to pass through the rows $W,R,H,C,P,N$ and through no open-cover, sequential-compactness, host-supremum, selected-limit, quotient-equality, or completeness coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the packet $D=(X,F,M,W,R,H,C,P,N)$. The compact metric row, monotone family, compact-uniform-continuity ledger, shared tolerance windows, real-seal boundary, transports, continuation routes, provenance, and local name are exactly the displayed coordinates of \autoref{def:dini-theorem-carrier}.

Classifier transport reads $H$ componentwise and then replays $C$. A transported consumer sees the same compact source windows, the same finite family indices, the same monotone comparison rows, the same continuity-modulus rows, and the same real-seal boundary route.

Monotone ledger exactness follows because $F$ is the only row that stores family comparison data. A convergence-facing read cannot introduce a private order comparison or a detached tail relation without leaving the accepted carrier.

Shared-window exactness follows from $M$ and $W$. The continuity ledger supplies the compact-uniform-continuity rows, while the tolerance ledger names the finite windows and family indices consumed for each displayed tolerance.

Finally, the nonescape clause is an inventory check on the carrier. The rows $R,H,C,P,N$ expose the located and Real boundary route, and none of the excluded compactness, supremum, selected-limit, quotient-equality, choice, or completeness principles appears as a coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiniTheorem monotone uniform window]\label{thm:dini-theorem-monotone-uniform-window}
Let $D=(X,F,M,W,R,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\DiniTheoremUp$ carrier. For every displayed tolerance request recorded in $W$, every accepted convergence read factors through a single finite route
$$
X,F,M,W,R,H,C,P,N .
$$
The route reads the compact source windows from $X$, the finite monotone family comparisons from $F$, the compact-uniform-continuity rows from $M$, and the shared tolerance window from $W$ before any $\LocatedRealUp$ or $\RealUp$ boundary row in $R$ is consumed. No accepted read may replace this route by open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, a host supremum, a selected limit, quotient real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the tolerance request in $W$. It names the compact source windows, the family indices, and the continuity-modulus rows that are inspected at the requested dyadic scale.

The source windows are read through $X$, and the family rows are read through $F$. Since $F$ includes the monotone ledger, each comparison used by the convergence read is already a displayed family comparison, not an ambient real-order assertion.

The compact-uniform-continuity row $M$ then identifies the finite windows sufficient for all displayed source points in the request. The route remains finite because $M$ and $W$ list the inspected windows explicitly.

Only after these rows are visible can the read reach $R$. The continuation row $C$ records this order, while $H$ preserves it under transport and $P,N$ package and name the same route.

Thus every accepted convergence read factors through $X,F,M,W,R,H,C,P,N$. Any route that inserts open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, host suprema, selected limits, quotient real equality, or ambient completeness uses data absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiniTheorem nonescape boundary]\label{thm:dini-theorem-nonescape-boundary}
Every $\LocatedRealUp$ or $\RealUp$ boundary read exported by an accepted $\DiniTheoremUp$ carrier is downstream of the displayed finite compact-window monotonicity route. Equivalently, if a consumer reaches the boundary row $R$, then the consumer has already read the same compact source row $X$, monotone family row $F$, compact-uniform-continuity ledger $M$, shared tolerance-window row $W$, componentwise transports $H$, continuation replay $C$, package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier. The only boundary coordinate is $R$, and it is stored together with the compact source, monotone family, uniform-continuity ledger, and shared tolerance-window rows.

The continuation row $C$ records the admissible replay into $R$. Its predecessors are the source-window read, the family monotonicity read, the uniform-continuity read, and the tolerance-window read; no separate boundary route is recorded.

Transport cannot create another route. The row $H$ moves the displayed coordinates componentwise, so a transported boundary read still consumes the same $X,F,M,W,R$ surface.

The package and naming rows $P,N$ therefore certify only this route. Because the carrier contains no open-cover, sequential-compactness, host-supremum, selected-limit, quotient-equality, countable-choice, or completeness coordinate, a boundary read using any such datum is outside the accepted $\DiniTheoremUp$ packet.
\end{proof}
