\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HausdorffSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffspace-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Hausdorff and Bourbaki/Kelley general topology, the $\HausdorffSpaceUp$ packet records separated points by disjoint open-neighborhood rows over $\TopologyUp$. When a $\MetricSpaceUp$ row supplies a positive-distance separation witness, the packet stores the resulting open-neighborhood handoff as finite NameCert data rather than as host point equality. The chapter sits between the topology surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, and the separated completion consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Hausdorff space carrier]
\label{def:hausdorff-space-carrier}
A $\HausdorffSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(T,x,y,U,V,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ source row, including the displayed open predicates, finite neighborhood ledger, and classifier route read by topological consumers.
\item $x,y$ are the two point-name rows compared by the separatedness witness. They are point observations inside the same topology source row, not host-level representatives.
\item $U,V$ are the open-neighborhood rows, with $U$ containing the displayed name $x$ and $V$ containing the displayed name $y$.
\item $D$ is the disjointness ledger certifying that the two open rows have no shared displayed point observation under the local topology classifier.
\item $M$ is the optional $\MetricSpaceUp$ distance-separation handoff row. When present, it records the metric route that generates $U,V,D$ from a positive displayed distance.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row used by metric-generated separation reads, exposing only the displayed distance and positivity evidence available through the real-name interface.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $T,x,y,U,V,D,M,E,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a point-pair comparison through the neighborhood and disjointness route
$$
T,x,y\longmapsto U,V,D\longmapsto M,E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HausdorffSpaceUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier of an accepted packet compares separated point pairs only through these rows. It contains no coordinate for quotient point equality, open-cover compactness, selected representatives, ambient completeness, or standard bridge data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[HausdorffSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hausdorff-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\HausdorffSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
S=(T,x,y,U,V,D,M,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the topology source, point pair, open-neighborhood rows, disjointness ledger, optional metric handoff, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item disjoint open-neighborhood separation, requiring $U$ and $V$ to be displayed as open rows in $T$, to contain $x$ and $y$ respectively, and to be separated by $D$;
\item classifier transport, requiring every transported separatedness read to preserve $T,x,y,U,V,D$ through the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$;
\item metric handoff, requiring any $\MetricSpaceUp$ distance-apartness read to pass through $M$ and the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ before it yields $U,V,D$;
\item nonescape, requiring downstream topology, metric, real, and completion consumers to read only $T,x,y,U,V,D,M,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No HausdorffSpace obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HausdorffSpaceUp.HausdorffSpaceNameCert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:hausdorff-space-carrier}. The accepted tuple displays exactly the topology source, point pair, open-neighborhood rows, disjointness ledger, optional metric handoff, real seal, and structural rows.

Disjoint open-neighborhood separation is the role of $U,V,D$. The rows $U$ and $V$ are declared open inside the topology source $T$, the point-name rows $x,y$ are read through those neighborhoods, and $D$ records their disjointness under the local classifier.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The row $H$ transports the same topology source, point pair, neighborhood rows, and disjointness ledger, while $C$ replays the displayed route from the point-pair comparison to the separated-neighborhood witness.

Metric handoff uses only $M$ and $E$. A metric-generated read first exposes the positive displayed distance through the $\RealUp$ seal, then records the open-neighborhood separation route in $M$. It never replaces the separated-neighborhood data by host equality or host apartness of points.

Finally inspect $P,N$. Provenance and local naming mention only the displayed rows, so topology, metric, real, and completion consumers have no coordinate from which to read quotient point equality, open-cover compactness, selected representatives, ambient completeness, or standard bridge closure.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HausdorffSpaceUp.HausdorffSpaceCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}

\begin{theorem}[HausdorffSpace metric separation handoff]
\label{thm:hausdorff-space-metric-separation-handoff}
Let
$$
S=(T,x,y,U,V,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\HausdorffSpaceUp$ carrier. If the optional $\MetricSpaceUp$ row $M$ is present, every displayed positive-distance apartness witness in $M$ factors through the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ and yields the same open-neighborhood rows $U,V$ with disjointness ledger $D$. Thus metric distance-apartness contributes a Hausdorff separation row without importing host equality, quotient point identity, selected representatives, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the metric-generated branch of the carrier. The row $M$ is attached to the same point-name rows $x,y$ and the same topology source $T$ as the displayed neighborhoods.

The distance value is visible only through the $\RealUp$ seal $E$. Positivity therefore supplies a finite separation budget for the neighborhood construction, not an external equality or apartness relation on host representatives.

The handoff then lands in $U,V,D$. These rows are already topology rows in the carrier: $U$ and $V$ are open neighborhoods of $x$ and $y$, and $D$ is their disjointness ledger. Componentwise transport and replay through $H,C$ preserve this factorization for downstream consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HausdorffSpace classifier separation route]
\label{thm:hausdorff-space-classifier-separation-route}
Let
$$
S=(T,x,y,U,V,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\HausdorffSpaceUp$ carrier. Every separated-point classifier read generated from the optional metric row $M$ factors through the $\MetricSpaceUp$ distance witness, the $\RealUp$ positive-distance seal $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffSpaceUp}$ row $N$ before it yields the open-neighborhood rows $U,V$ and disjointness ledger $D$. Thus metric classifier separation is a finite NameCert route over the displayed carrier rows, not a host apartness predicate on points.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HausdorffSpaceUp.HausdorffSpaceCarrier\_classifier\_separation\_route}
\begin{proof}
Start with a metric-generated classifier read. The only metric coordinate in the carrier is $M$, and it is attached to the same topology source $T$ and point-name rows $x,y$ as the displayed neighborhoods.

The distance value and positivity evidence are exposed only through the $\RealUp$ seal $E$. The metric route therefore supplies a finite positive-distance budget, after which the packet returns to the topological output rows $U,V,D$.

Transport and replay are restricted to $H$ and $C$. Provenance and local naming are restricted to $P$ and $N$, so the resulting classifier read has no access to host point apartness, selected representatives, quotient equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HausdorffSpace diagonal nonescape]
\label{thm:hausdorff-space-diagonal-nonescape}
For an accepted $\HausdorffSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
S=(T,x,y,U,V,D,M,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the diagonal equality boundary of the packet is exhausted by the displayed separation rows $T,x,y,U,V,D$ together with optional metric and real handoff rows $M,E$. A downstream topology, metric, or completion consumer cannot replace that boundary by host point equality, quotient topology equality, real trichotomy, open-cover compactness, selected representatives, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier. The point names $x,y$ occur only as displayed rows inside the topology source $T$, and the separation output is the pair of open-neighborhood rows $U,V$ together with the disjointness ledger $D$.

When the optional metric route is present, it passes through $M$ and the real seal $E$ before returning to the same topological rows. This adds a displayed positive-distance source for separation, but it does not add host equality, quotient equality, trichotomy, compactness, representatives, or completeness coordinates.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name only the displayed packet. Hence every consumer remains inside the finite diagonal boundary described by the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HausdorffSpace choice-free point separation]
\label{thm:hausdorff-space-point-separation-choicefree}
Let
$$
S=(T,x,y,U,V,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\HausdorffSpaceUp$ carrier. If the optional metric row $M$ displays a positive-distance witness through the $\RealUp$ seal $E$, then the carrier produces the open-neighborhood rows $U,V$ and the disjointness ledger $D$ as the separatedness output. This construction does not select representatives, quotient points, host point equalities, or ambient completeness data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier of \autoref{def:hausdorff-space-carrier}. The rows $x,y$ are displayed point names inside the topology source $T$, while $U,V,D$ are already the open-neighborhood and disjointness rows of the same packet.

Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:hausdorff-space-namecert-obligations}. The metric handoff obligation requires every metric-generated read to pass through $M$ and the $\RealUp$ seal $E$ before it yields $U,V,D$.

Apply the metric separation handoff of \autoref{thm:hausdorff-space-metric-separation-handoff}. A positive displayed distance supplies only the finite separation budget for the recorded open neighborhoods. Since the carrier has no coordinate for representatives, quotient point identity, host equality, or ambient completeness, none of those objects is selected or consumed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HausdorffSpace open-neighborhood classifier transport]
\label{thm:hausdorff-space-open-neighborhood-classifier-transport}
Let
$$
S=(T,x,y,U,V,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\HausdorffSpaceUp$ carrier. Transporting any accepted separated-neighborhood read along the componentwise row $H$ preserves the topology source $T$, point rows $x,y$, open-neighborhood rows $U,V$, disjointness ledger $D$, optional metric row $M$, and optional $\RealUp$ seal row $E$. Consequently the transported classifier still reads separation through the same displayed open-neighborhood and disjointness rows, and it cannot replace the read by quotient point equality, selected representatives, host point apartness, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:hausdorff-space-carrier}. The only classifier-visible topology data are $T,x,y,U,V,D$, with optional metric and real handoff rows $M,E$ attached to the same packet.

Use the NameCert obligation rows of \autoref{thm:hausdorff-space-namecert-obligations}. They require classifier transport to preserve $T,x,y,U,V,D$ through the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$, and require metric-generated reads to return through $M,E$ before yielding the same neighborhood rows.

The replay row $C$ then repeats the displayed route from the point-pair comparison to $U,V,D$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient point equality, selected representatives, host point apartness, or ambient completeness, transport cannot create one for the classifier to read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HausdorffSpace Real seal nonescape]
\label{thm:hausdorff-space-realseal-nonescape}
Every completion-facing or Real-facing consumer of an accepted $\HausdorffSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
S=(T,x,y,U,V,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
reads only the displayed $\TopologyUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. In particular, such a consumer cannot extract quotient point equality, open-cover compactness, selected representatives, ambient completeness, or standard bridge closure from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. Topology-facing reads use $T,U,V,D$; metric-generated reads additionally pass through $M$; Real-facing reads pass through the seal row $E$ before returning to the same displayed separation rows.

The structural rows do not add hidden data. Transport is restricted to $H$, continuation replay to $C$, provenance to $P$, and local naming to $N$.

Since no other coordinate appears in the carrier, completion-facing and Real-facing consumers can inspect only the displayed topology, metric, Real, history, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows. The excluded quotient, compactness, representative-selection, ambient-completeness, and bridge data have no projection from the packet.
\end{proof}
