Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive metric completeness, the $\LocatedCompleteMetricUp$ packet records a complete metric object whose Cauchy-limit service is located at every finite precision request. It sits after the completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the zero-distance classifier boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the finite observation schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. The packet names a constructive complete-metric carrier with displayed location and limit witnesses; it is not a quotient completion class, a selected representative, countable choice, host equality of points, or an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[LocatedCompleteMetric carrier]\label{def:located-complete-metric-carrier}
A $\LocatedCompleteMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(M,S,R,Q,W,A,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ source row, exposing the metric carrier, completion handoff, and finite tolerance surface.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ classifier row, requiring zero-distance comparisons to pass through the displayed separated metric boundary.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ distance-seal row, used only after finite distance observations have been routed through regular rational readback.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row for Cauchy approximants, limit budgets, and distance witnesses.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row naming the finite observation schedule for Cauchy data and locatedness requests.
\item $A$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric-completion source, separated classifier, real seal, regular rational readback, stream windows, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite Cauchy request through $W,Q,R$ and then through the separated classifier $S$ over the completion source $M$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompleteMetricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports a displayed complete-metric service only through these rows. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected limits, a countable-choice schedule, host equality of carried points, or an unrestricted completeness principle for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCompleteMetric NameCert obligation surface]\label{thm:located-complete-metric-namecert-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\LocatedCompleteMetricUp$ carrier $L=(M,S,R,Q,W,A,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompleteMetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the metric-completion source, separated classifier, real distance seal, regular rational readback, stream-window schedule, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item located request admission, requiring every precision request to be read through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$ before any limit witness is exposed;
\item regular readback stability, requiring Cauchy approximants, limit budgets, and distance witnesses to pass through $Q$ before the $\RealUp$ seal $R$ is consumed;
\item separated classifier stability, requiring zero-distance uniqueness for displayed limits to be compared through $S$ over the same completion source $M$;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding quotient completion classes, selected representatives, countable-choice schedules, host equality of carried points, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-complete-metric-carrier}. The displayed tuple contains exactly the rows $M,S,R,Q,W,A,C,P,N$, so carrier admission and local naming are exhausted by those coordinates.

A located request must enter through $W$. That row supplies the finite observation schedule, and $Q$ turns the scheduled Cauchy approximants and requested budgets into regular rational readback before any terminal real-distance seal is visible.

The separated comparison is then confined to $S$ over the same metric-completion source $M$. Transport through $A$ and replay through $C$ preserve the displayed route, while $P$ records that no excluded quotient, selector, choice, host-equality, or ambient-completeness coordinate is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedCompleteMetric Cauchy-limit witness]\label{thm:located-complete-metric-cauchy-limit-witness}
Let $L=(M,S,R,Q,W,A,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\LocatedCompleteMetricUp$ carrier. Every Cauchy-limit witness served by $L$ factors through the finite route
$$
W \longmapsto Q \longmapsto R \longmapsto M,S \longmapsto A,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the witness is a located finite-readback service over the displayed metric-completion source and separated classifier, not a selected host limit, quotient representative, choice sequence, or ambient completeness assertion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a Cauchy-limit request against $L$. The only observation schedule in the carrier is $W$, so the request first fixes a finite stream window.

The regular rational row $Q$ supplies the finite readback for the Cauchy approximants and the requested limit budget. The real-distance seal $R$ is read after that regular handoff, so a downstream consumer receives only displayed distance information.

The completion source $M$ and separated classifier $S$ package the witness as a complete-metric service and compare any two displayed limits through zero-distance classification. The structural rows $A,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route, so the excluded host-limit and quotient data have no row from which to be read.
\end{proof}
