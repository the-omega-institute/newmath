\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedNormalConfluenceSealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closednormalconfluenceseal-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\ClosedNormalConfluenceSealUp$ packet turns the normal-source branch of
\autoref{ch:metacic-confluence-audit-map} into finite BEDC data. It is placed
after the typed reduction and preservation surfaces of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedreductionnormalform-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert}: the present packet
records the closed normal source, two visible beta-star routes, their displayed
join, and the boundary row that prevents this normal-source seal from being
read as full closed Church--Rosser.

\begin{definition}[Closed normal confluence seal carrier]
\label{def:closed-normal-confluence-seal-carrier}
A $\ClosedNormalConfluenceSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(t,n,r_1,r_2,j,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $t$ is the closed MetaCIC term row serving as the source of both
beta-star routes;
\item $n$ is the beta-normality witness row for $t$;
\item $r_1$ is the first displayed $\mathrm{BetaStarStep}$ route from $t$ to a
target row;
\item $r_2$ is the second displayed $\mathrm{BetaStarStep}$ route from $t$ to a
target row;
\item $j$ is the join row supplied by the normal-source confluence theorem,
recording the common target and the two residual beta-star routes into it;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the source,
normality, route, join, provenance, and local certificate rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the
closedness witness, the normality witness, the two beta-star routes, and the
join row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over MetaCIC closed terms, beta normality,
$\mathrm{BetaStarStep}$, normal-source confluence, the closed-substitution
compatibility obstruction, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and sibling
$\NameCert$ packets;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedNormalConfluenceSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for arbitrary closed-source confluence, no proof
of $\mathrm{BetaParallelSubstitutionCompatibility}$, no closed parallel diamond
for all closed terms, and no hidden subject-reduction or normalization oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed normal confluence seal normal source]
\label{thm:closed-normal-confluence-seal-normal-source}
Every accepted $\ClosedNormalConfluenceSealUp$ carrier exposes confluence only
from the closed beta-normal source row $t$. A consumer may read $t,n,r_1,r_2,j$
through $H,C,P,N$ and obtain the displayed join of the two beta-star routes,
but it cannot use the packet as a confluence theorem for an arbitrary closed
source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:closed-normal-confluence-seal-carrier}. The source row $t$ and the
normality witness row $n$ are not auxiliary decoration: they are the hypotheses
that select the normal-source theorem named in
\autoref{ch:metacic-confluence-audit-map}.

The routes $r_1$ and $r_2$ are the only beta-star routes exposed by the packet,
and the join row $j$ is read only as their common normal-source join. The
transport and route rows $H$ and $C$ replay this finite surface without adding
new sources.

The sibling packets provide the typed-route and preservation discipline around
the read. \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedreductionnormalform-namecert}
keeps terminal route access finite, while
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert} keeps target
membership reads behind the displayed preservation boundary.

Since no coordinate supplies an arbitrary closed-source theorem, a closed
parallel diamond, or a substitution-compatibility discharge, the accepted seal
cannot be repacked into full closed Church--Rosser.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed normal confluence seal substitution boundary]
\label{thm:closed-normal-confluence-seal-substitution-boundary}
The $\ClosedNormalConfluenceSealUp$ carrier separates the available
normal-source join from the substitution-compatibility boundary. Agreement on
$t,n,r_1,r_2,j,H,C,P,N$ records the checked closed-normal confluence read, but
it does not produce $\mathrm{BetaParallelSubstitutionCompatibility}$,
$\mathrm{BetaParallelDiamondClosed}$, or arbitrary closed-term confluence for
$\mathrm{BetaStarStep}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the provenance row $P$. It names the checked normal-source confluence
surface and also names the closed-substitution compatibility obstruction
recorded by \autoref{ch:metacic-confluence-audit-map}.

These two entries have different roles. The normal-source entry justifies the
join row $j$ for the displayed closed beta-normal source, while the obstruction
entry keeps the substitution interface visible as a boundary rather than as a
field filled by the carrier.

The structural rows $H,C,N$ preserve this separation. They transport and replay
the finite seal rows, but no row in the carrier contains the substitution
compatibility proof, the closed parallel diamond, or the general closed
star-confluence endpoint.

Therefore the packet is a local confluence seal for closed normal sources. It
is not a certificate for the substitution-sensitive branch that would be needed
to close arbitrary closed-term Church--Rosser.
\end{proof}
