\label{def:fan-bar-route-carrier}
A $\FanBarRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(T,B,I,W,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the finite fan-tree row. It records the displayed binary prefix tree, prefix closure, finite successor rows, and the source fan surface consumed from $\FanTheoremUp$.
\item $B$ is the decidable bar-depth ledger. It records the displayed barred prefixes, Bool decisions, unary depth bound, and exact branch-prefix coverage rows delivered by the finite fan theorem.
\item $I$ is the barred-spread induction replay row. It records the local backwards replay consumed from $\BrouwerBarInductionUp$, using only displayed children and barred prefixes from $T$ and $B$.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row. It is the only route by which stream-facing consumers may request finite observations of the fan/bar packet.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row. It turns the displayed stream windows into regular rational observations before a Real or Completion consumer can inspect the handoff.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row. It records the dyadic precision, radius, comparison, and refinement ledger attached to the readback route.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $T,B,I,W,Q,D$ and the structural rows have been displayed.

\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from finite fan inspection through bar-depth exactness, barred-spread replay, stream windows, regular-rational readback, dyadic tolerance, and the Real seal.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FanBarRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $T,B,I,W,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a selected infinite branch, open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, quotient stream equality, countable choice, or an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.

\begin{theorem}[FanBarRoute NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:fan-bar-route-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FanBarRouteUp$ carrier $F=(T,B,I,W,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FanBarRouteUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite fan tree, decidable bar-depth ledger, induction replay, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore tolerance, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item finite bar-depth exactness, requiring every branch-prefix request to meet the displayed bar through $T$ and $B$ by the named unary depth;
\item barred-spread replay, requiring every backwards induction step to pass through $I$ and the displayed children of $T$ rather than through a selected infinite branch;
\item stream and readback compatibility, requiring every $\StreamNameUp$ window read to pass through $W$ and every regular-rational read to pass through $Q$ before any dyadic or Real-facing seal is visible;
\item Real seal nonescape, requiring the terminal $\RealUp$ row $E$ to be downstream of $T,B,I,W,Q,D$ and to export no ambient completeness or host real equality;
\item transport and provenance stability, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve the same route under componentwise sameness, displayed continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these six rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:fan-bar-route-carrier}. The projected packet is exactly the tuple $T,B,I,W,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N$.

Finite bar-depth exactness reads the $\FanTheoremUp$ surface. The fan tree $T$ supplies the displayed prefix tree, and $B$ supplies the Bool bar decisions together with the unary depth ledger, so the bar claim is checked over visible prefixes only.

Barred-spread replay reads the $\BrouwerBarInductionUp$ surface. The row $I$ contains the backwards local replay, and its children are the displayed successors already present in $T$, so no branch selector is introduced.

The analytic handoff uses the ordered rows $W,Q,D,E$. A stream request first opens the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$, then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $Q$, then the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row $D$, and only then the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, name, and record the same route. Since the carrier contains no further coordinate, the excluded compactness, choice, quotient, branch-selection, host-equality, and ambient-completeness principles cannot be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FanBarRoute finite bar handoff]
\label{thm:fan-bar-route-finite-bar-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ or Completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\FanBarRouteUp$ carrier factors through
$$
T,B,I,W,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
The consumer receives a finite fan tree, a decidable bar-depth ledger, a barred-spread replay row, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore tolerance, the Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming. It receives no selected infinite branch, open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, quotient stream equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Real or Completion-facing read of the carrier. By \autoref{def:fan-bar-route-carrier}, the only analytic-facing rows are the stream window $W$, the regular-rational readback $Q$, the dyadic tolerance row $D$, and the Real seal $E$.

Replay the continuation row $C$. Before any analytic-facing row is visible, the route exposes the finite fan tree $T$, the decidable bar-depth ledger $B$, and the barred-spread induction replay $I$.

After those rows have been displayed, the consumer can read $W$, then $Q$, then $D$, and then $E$. Componentwise transport and package provenance are fixed by $H$ and $P$, and the local naming row is $N$.

Thus every consumer-visible route factors through the displayed tuple. The carrier has no row for branch selection, open-cover compactness, sequential compactness, quotient stream equality, countable choice, or ambient real completeness, so none of those principles can be extracted from the handoff.
\end{proof}
