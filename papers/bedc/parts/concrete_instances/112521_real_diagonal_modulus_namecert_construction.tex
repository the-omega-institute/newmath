\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealDiagonalModulusUp$}

Following Bishop--Bridges style constructive diagonal arguments, the $\RealDiagonalModulusUp$ packet records the rank-one modulus row used when a regular rational diagonal route is read as a real-name construction. It sits between the finite selector packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-diagonalmodulus-namecert}, the finite stream surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic observation core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet isolates the single displayed modulus schedule consumed by regular-Cauchy and $\RealUp$ completion routes without quotient streams, selected host representatives, countable choice, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Real diagonal modulus carrier]
\label{def:real-diagonal-modulus-carrier}
A $\RealDiagonalModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
P=(n,\mu,S,R,D,E,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $n$ is the unary precision-request row submitted by a $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer;
\item $\mu$ is the accepted $\DiagonalModulusUp$ selector row naming the threshold inspected for that request;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row opened by the selected threshold;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the displayed window $S$;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the dyadic radius, endpoint comparison, and diagonal-budget cells read by $R$;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $S,R,D$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport ledger for $n,\mu,S,R,D,E$ and for their finite subrows;
\item $C$ is the $\Cont$ route ledger from precision request to modulus threshold, stream window, regular readback, dyadic tolerance, and real seal;
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\RealDiagonalModulusUp$, $\DiagonalModulusUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealDiagonalModulusUp}$ naming row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier on such packets is componentwise transport through $H$ with replay along $C$. It never compares hidden streams, quotient representatives, host real endpoints, selected limits, or ambient complete spaces.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealDiagonalModulusUp NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-diagonal-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealDiagonalModulusUp$ carrier $P=(n,\mu,S,R,D,E,H,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealDiagonalModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the ten displayed rows, classifier transport through $H$, route replay through $C$, selector visibility through $\mu$, stream-window visibility through $S$, regular-rational readback through $R$, dyadic tolerance exactness through $D$, real-seal non-escape through $E$, package provenance through $Q$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is \autoref{def:real-diagonal-modulus-carrier}. It lists the precision request, modulus selector, stream window, regular readback, dyadic ledger, real seal, transport ledger, continuation ledger, provenance row, and local naming row.

The selector and readback obligations are inherited by projection. The row $\mu$ is the only $\DiagonalModulusUp$ selector coordinate, $S$ is the only $\StreamNameUp$ window coordinate, $R$ is the only $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback coordinate, and $D$ is the only $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance coordinate.

The transport and route obligations are exactly $H$ and $C$. Any consumer that moves the packet must move each displayed row componentwise and then replay the same route from $n$ through $\mu,S,R,D$ to $E$.

Finally, $Q$ and $N$ close the local naming surface. Since no other coordinate occurs in the carrier, the certificate cannot export quotient stream equality, selected host representatives, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealDiagonalModulusUp real-seal route]
\label{thm:real-diagonal-modulus-real-seal-route}
Let $P=(n,\mu,S,R,D,E,H,C,Q,N)$ be an accepted $\RealDiagonalModulusUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$ consumer of the seal row $E$ first reads the displayed precision request $n$, the $\DiagonalModulusUp$ selector $\mu$, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window $S$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, with transport and provenance supplied by $H,C,Q,N$. Thus the real-facing handoff is a finite diagonal-modulus route rather than a quotient stream, selected representative, choice sequence, host equality proof, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier projection. The only row whose type can feed a terminal real-name read is $E$, and the carrier places it after the displayed diagonal route $n,\mu,S,R,D$.

Replay the route ledger $C$. A precision request opens the $\DiagonalModulusUp$ selector, the selector opens the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window, the window is read back by $R$, and the dyadic tolerance data in $D$ records the comparison budget before $E$ is exposed.

Transport is controlled by $H$, while $Q$ and $N$ record the package and local certificate names. Therefore every real-seal consumer factors through the same finite route and cannot read a quotient stream, selected host representative, choice sequence, host equality proof, or ambient complete space.
\end{proof}
