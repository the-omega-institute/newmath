The $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ packet gives the shared $\mathsf{GAP}$ and $\mathsf{T}$ socket discipline of \autoref{ch:visions-hard-problems-under-bedc} a finite common boundary. It sits beside the causal commitment of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-causalcommitment-namecert}, the finite witnessed refutation of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitewitnessedrefutation-namecert}, the other-minds commitment of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-othermindscommitment-namecert}, the aspect chain of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-aspectchain-namecert}, and the solipsism refusal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-solipsismgaprefusal-namecert}. Those siblings package application faces; the present carrier isolates the common closure boundary consumed by all of them.

\begin{definition}[GapClosureBoundary carrier]
\label{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier}
A $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ request row, naming the forward-binding or socket-facing demand whose source is not generated by the closed substrate;
\item $S$ is the source observation or socket row, displaying the finite record, locality cell, aspect side, or inscription route that the request may lawfully read;
\item $R$ is the refusal and non-escape ledger, recording that an off-source closure request is not internalised;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $G,S,R$ together with continuation, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the request row to the source row, and from every unsupported closure read to the refusal row;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, and the hard-problem sibling packets that consume this boundary;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GapClosureBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts exactly finite packets with these seven rows. It has no coordinate for internal universal closure, private other-minds access, host causal necessity, direct aspect reduction, or a $\mathsf{T}$ supply that bypasses the displayed source route.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\GapClosureBoundaryUp}$ surface has exactly four obligations: carrier exposure for the finite request, source, refusal, and structural rows; source-route exactness for any displayed observation or socket row $S$; refusal-ledger routing for any request that tries to convert $G$ into internal closure without $S$; and structural exhaustion through $H,C,P,N$. These obligations are sufficient for the shared boundary used by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-causalcommitment-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitewitnessedrefutation-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-othermindscommitment-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-aspectchain-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-solipsismgaprefusal-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier}. The rows $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$ are the full inventory visible to a boundary reader, so carrier exposure contributes no hidden coordinate.

Read the source route next. Any accepted use of the $\mathsf{GAP}$ request must show the source observation or socket row $S$ before it can be consumed by a causal, refutation, other-minds, aspect-chain, or solipsism-refusal sibling.

Finally inspect a closure request with no displayed source route. The continuation row sends such a request to $R$, while $H,P,N$ merely transport, package, and name the same finite packet. Since the carrier has no internal-closure coordinate, the four obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary refusal non-escape]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-nonescape}
Any accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ consumer that attempts to turn a $\mathsf{GAP}$ request into substrate-internal closure must first read the displayed source route $S$. If no such route is present, the consumer is routed to the refusal ledger $R$ and receives no universal closure, private other-minds access, host causal necessity, direct aspect reduction, hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, or off-packet continuation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations}. They divide every boundary read into the carrier exposure route, the displayed source route, and the refusal route.

When $S$ is present, the consumer remains a reader of the finite source row and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. The carrier still exports only a named boundary packet, not a substrate-internal closure principle.

When $S$ is absent, the only available continuation is the refusal route into $R$. The refusal row is a ledger entry rather than a supply witness, so the excluded closure, access, necessity, reduction, hidden-source, and off-packet continuation coordinates cannot be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary displayed source precondition]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-displayed-source-precondition}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier

$$
B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N),
$$

any supported closure read of $B$ must expose the displayed source observation or socket row $S$ before a hard-problem sibling consumer reads the packet. The only public precondition for such a read is the finite $\BHist$ packet together with the $\hsame$ transports in $H$, the $\Cont$ source route in $C$, the $\Pkg$ provenance in $P$, and the local $\NameCert$ row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier definition. The packet has exactly the request row $G$, the displayed source row $S$, the refusal ledger $R$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Use the NameCert obligation theorem \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations}. Its source-route exactness clause requires a supported observation or socket-facing read to display $S$ before any sibling application consumes the boundary.

The refusal non-escape theorem \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-nonescape} rules out replacing this precondition by an internal universal closure row. Thus the supported closure read has to expose $S$ and remains inside the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary refusal ledger transport]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-ledger-transport}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, any off-source closure read of the request row $G$ is transported to the refusal ledger $R$ along the displayed structural rows $H,C,P,N$. This transport uses only componentwise $\hsame$, the displayed $\Cont$ route to refusal, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an off-source request. By definition it has no displayed source route through $S$, so it cannot satisfy the source precondition of \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-displayed-source-precondition}.

Apply refusal non-escape. The only available continuation sends the request to $R$, while the rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, route, package, and name the same finite packet.

The structural rows do not add a hidden source. They preserve the refusal ledger through $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$, so the off-source read is transported to $R$ rather than to internal universal closure, private access, causal necessity, aspect reduction, or a hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary sibling consumer exhaustion]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-sibling-consumer-exhaustion}
The hard-problem sibling consumers listed in the $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ packet exhaust the exported boundary surface of $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$. Causal commitment, finite witnessed refutation, other-minds commitment, aspect-chain, and solipsism-refusal consumers may read the common request row $G$, source row $S$, refusal ledger $R$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$, but none gains internal universal closure beyond that finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier and the NameCert obligation surface. The provenance row $P$ records exactly the hard-problem sibling packets that consume this boundary, and the local certificate exposes no row beyond $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$.

Supported sibling reads use \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-displayed-source-precondition}; they must expose $S$ before consuming the packet. Off-source sibling reads use \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-ledger-transport}; they are sent to $R$ through $H,C,P,N$.

Thus every listed sibling consumer is accounted for by either the displayed source route or the refusal ledger route. Since neither route contains an internal universal closure coordinate, the exported boundary is exhausted by the finite packet and no sibling acquires a stronger closure principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary source route exactness]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-source-route-exactness}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, any
consumer of $B$ that reads a displayed source observation or socket row must
route the $\mathsf{GAP}$ request row $G$ through $S$ before any application
sibling consumes the boundary packet. The source route is the only admissible
pre-consumption route for causal commitment, finite witnessed refutation,
other-minds commitment, aspect-chain, and solipsism-refusal consumers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier}. The only
source-facing rows available to a consumer are the $\mathsf{GAP}$ request row
$G$ and the displayed source observation or socket row $S$.

Use the obligation summary of
\autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations}. Its source-route
clause requires an accepted boundary consumer to exhibit $S$ before any
application sibling may consume the packet.

The refusal non-escape row of
\autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-nonescape} rules out bypassing $S$ by
turning $G$ into internal closure. Thus every accepted source-reading consumer
uses the displayed route $G \longrightarrow S$ first.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary refusal ledger routing]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-ledger-routing}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, any
off-source closure request is routed to the refusal ledger $R$. Such a request
cannot export internal closure, a hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, or any
continuation that bypasses the displayed source row $S$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier of \autoref{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier}. It contains one
request row $G$, one displayed source row $S$, one refusal ledger $R$, and the
structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations}. Its
refusal-ledger clause sends every closure read without the displayed source
route to $R$ rather than to a substrate-internal closure coordinate.

Now invoke \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-nonescape}. The refusal
row is a ledger endpoint, not a supply row, so it exports neither internal
closure nor hidden $\mathsf{T}$ data.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-structural-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, the
rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, route, package, and locally name the same

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The obligation summary identifies structural exhaustion as one of the four

repackage the already displayed request, source, and refusal rows.

Finally use refusal non-escape. Since off-source closure is routed to $R$ and
$R$ exports no hidden supply, the structural rows have no coordinate from which
to generate a second source, internal closure, or hidden $\mathsf{T}$ row.
\end{proof}
