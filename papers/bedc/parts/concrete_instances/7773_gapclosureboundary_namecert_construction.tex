\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-gapclosureboundary-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ packet gives the shared $\mathsf{GAP}$ and $\mathsf{T}$ socket discipline of \autoref{ch:visions-hard-problems-under-bedc} a finite common boundary. It sits beside the causal commitment of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-causalcommitment-namecert}, the finite witnessed refutation of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitewitnessedrefutation-namecert}, the other-minds commitment of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-othermindscommitment-namecert}, the aspect chain of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-aspectchain-namecert}, and the solipsism refusal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-solipsismgaprefusal-namecert}. Those siblings package application faces; the present carrier isolates the common closure boundary consumed by all of them.

\begin{definition}[GapClosureBoundary carrier]
\label{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier}
A $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ request row, naming the forward-binding or socket-facing demand whose source is not generated by the closed substrate;
\item $S$ is the source observation or socket row, displaying the finite record, locality cell, aspect side, or inscription route that the request may lawfully read;
\item $R$ is the refusal and non-escape ledger, recording that an off-source closure request is not internalised;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $G,S,R$ together with continuation, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the request row to the source row, and from every unsupported closure read to the refusal row;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, and the hard-problem sibling packets that consume this boundary;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GapClosureBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts exactly finite packets with these seven rows. It has no coordinate for internal universal closure, private other-minds access, host causal necessity, direct aspect reduction, or a $\mathsf{T}$ supply that bypasses the displayed source route.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\GapClosureBoundaryUp}$ surface has exactly four obligations: carrier exposure for the finite request, source, refusal, and structural rows; source-route exactness for any displayed observation or socket row $S$; refusal-ledger routing for any request that tries to convert $G$ into internal closure without $S$; and structural exhaustion through $H,C,P,N$. These obligations are sufficient for the shared boundary used by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-causalcommitment-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitewitnessedrefutation-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-othermindscommitment-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-aspectchain-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-solipsismgaprefusal-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier}. The rows $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$ are the full inventory visible to a boundary reader, so carrier exposure contributes no hidden coordinate.

Read the source route next. Any accepted use of the $\mathsf{GAP}$ request must show the source observation or socket row $S$ before it can be consumed by a causal, refutation, other-minds, aspect-chain, or solipsism-refusal sibling.

Finally inspect a closure request with no displayed source route. The continuation row sends such a request to $R$, while $H,P,N$ merely transport, package, and name the same finite packet. Since the carrier has no internal-closure coordinate, the four obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary refusal non-escape]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-nonescape}
Any accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ consumer that attempts to turn a $\mathsf{GAP}$ request into substrate-internal closure must first read the displayed source route $S$. If no such route is present, the consumer is routed to the refusal ledger $R$ and receives no universal closure, private other-minds access, host causal necessity, direct aspect reduction, hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, or off-packet continuation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations}. They divide every boundary read into the carrier exposure route, the displayed source route, and the refusal route.

When $S$ is present, the consumer remains a reader of the finite source row and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. The carrier still exports only a named boundary packet, not a substrate-internal closure principle.

When $S$ is absent, the only available continuation is the refusal route into $R$. The refusal row is a ledger entry rather than a supply witness, so the excluded closure, access, necessity, reduction, hidden-source, and off-packet continuation coordinates cannot be exported.
\end{proof}

For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, a supported root read must pass through the displayed source row $S$, while an unsupported closure read is routed to the refusal ledger $R$. The two routes are orthogonal at the public boundary: neither $S$ nor $R$ can be reread as a hidden closure supply, and no accepted read obtains both a source witness and a refusal witness as a concealed substitute for internal closure.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the seven-row carrier from \autoref{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier}. The only source-facing coordinate is $S$, and the only off-source endpoint is the refusal ledger $R$.

Apply the NameCert obligations and displayed source precondition, \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations} and \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-displayed-source-precondition}. A supported read of the request row $G$ has to expose $S$ before any downstream consumer can use the boundary.

For an unsupported read, use refusal non-escape, \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-nonescape}. The read is routed to $R$ and receives no internal universal closure, hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, private access row, causal necessity row, direct aspect reduction, or off-packet continuation. Thus the source route and refusal route remain separate public alternatives rather than a combined hidden supply.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary hard-problem consumer factorization]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-hard-problem-consumer-factorization}
Every accepted root read used by $\CausalCommitmentUp$, $\FiniteWitnessedRefutationUp$, $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$, $\AspectChainUp$, or $\SolipsismGapRefusalUp$ factors through the same displayed packet $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$. A supported consumer factors through $G,S,H,C,P,N$, and an unsupported consumer factors through $G,R,H,C,P,N$; no listed consumer receives a consumer-specific carrier outside the $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier definition and the NameCert obligations, \autoref{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier} and \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations}. They expose the shared root packet and record the hard-problem sibling packets only through the provenance row $P$.

For supported consumers, apply the displayed source precondition. The causal, refutation, other-minds, aspect, and solipsism routes can use the boundary only after $G$ has been read through $S$ and transported, replayed, packaged, and named by $H,C,P,N$.

For unsupported consumers, apply refusal non-escape. The same hard-problem request is sent to $R$ through the structural rows, and the excluded closure, private access, causal necessity, direct aspect reduction, hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, and off-packet continuation are absent from the carrier. Hence every listed consumer factors through one of the two displayed finite routes.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary closure-request determinacy]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-closure-request-determinacy}
For accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carriers, agreement on the request row $G$, source row $S$, transport row $H$, continuation row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$ determines whether a root closure request is supported or refusal-routed. If the displayed source route through $S$ is available, the read is supported through $G,S,H,C,P,N$; if that route is absent, the same continuation and naming surface routes the request to $R$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the displayed source precondition, \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-displayed-source-precondition}. It identifies the public support condition as the presence of the source observation or socket row $S$ together with the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ surface.

Use the NameCert obligation theorem to inspect the continuation row. The row $C$ routes supported reads through the source row and routes unsupported closure reads to the refusal row, while $H,P,N$ transport, package, and name that same decision.

Finally apply refusal non-escape. Once the source route is absent, the request cannot escape to an internal closure coordinate or hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, so the public data $G,S,H,C,P,N$ fixes the branch: supported through $S$ when the source route is displayed, and refusal-routed to $R$ otherwise.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary sibling source totality]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-sibling-source-totality}
For every supported read of $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ by $\CausalCommitmentUp$, $\FiniteWitnessedRefutationUp$, $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$, $\AspectChainUp$, or $\SolipsismGapRefusalUp$, the consumer first factors through the displayed source route
$$
G \longrightarrow S.
$$
Only after that route is exposed may the read use the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. Thus the listed sibling consumers receive a common source route before any transport, continuation, provenance, or local naming row is read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$ using \autoref{def:gap-closure-boundary-carrier}. The carrier exposes one request row, one displayed source row, one refusal row, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Apply the NameCert obligations and displayed source precondition, \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-namecert-obligations} and \autoref{thm:gap-closure-boundary-displayed-source-precondition}. A supported root request cannot be read by a sibling consumer until the public source row has been displayed as the route $G \longrightarrow S$.

Now use source-refusal orthogonality, hard-problem consumer factorization, and closure-request determinacy. These theorems keep the supported branch separate from $R$, force each listed sibling through the same boundary packet, and make the source branch the determined route whenever the displayed source is present. The rows $H,C,P,N$ therefore occur only as the transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming surface for that already displayed source route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary refusal route determinacy]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-refusal-route-determinacy}
For every accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(G,S,R,H,C,P,N)$, if a root closure request has no displayed source route through $G \longrightarrow S$, then the request is deterministically routed to the refusal row $R$. The same $H,C,P,N$ rows transport, route, package, and name this refusal; no unsupported request may choose a second source row, hidden closure row, private access row, or hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the displayed source precondition. Its contrapositive boundary reading is that a supported branch must display the route from $G$ to $S$ before any sibling can consume the boundary.

When that route is absent, apply refusal non-escape and source-refusal orthogonality. Refusal non-escape sends the unsupported request to $R$ and excludes internal closure, private access, causal necessity, direct aspect reduction, off-packet continuation, and hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply; orthogonality prevents $R$ from being reread as a concealed source branch.

Finally use hard-problem consumer factorization and closure-request determinacy. Every listed sibling reads the same packet $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$, and the public data determine the branch: displayed source gives the supported route, while absence of the displayed source gives the refusal route $R$ with the same structural rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GapClosureBoundary structural row conservation]
\label{thm:gap-closure-boundary-structural-row-conservation}
For accepted $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ carriers, the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ conserve only the two displayed branches: the source branch $G \longrightarrow S$ and the refusal branch through $R$. These rows cannot introduce internal closure, a hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply, a private access route, causal necessity, direct aspect reduction, or any consumer-specific route outside the displayed boundary packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier and NameCert obligations together. The rows $H,C,P,N$ are respectively transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows for the visible packet $G,S,R$; they are not additional carriers and contain no independent closure coordinate.

For the supported branch, apply sibling source totality. Each listed sibling reaches $H,C,P,N$ only after the route $G \longrightarrow S$ has been displayed, so the structural rows preserve that route rather than supplying a hidden source.

For the unsupported branch, apply refusal route determinacy and refusal non-escape. The same structural rows carry the request to $R$ and exclude hidden closure supply, private access, causal necessity, direct aspect reduction, off-packet continuation, and hidden $\mathsf{T}$ supply.

The source-refusal orthogonality and hard-problem consumer factorization theorems then exhaust the boundary. Since every listed consumer is routed through one of the two displayed branches over $G,S,R,H,C,P,N$, the structural rows conserve the visible source/refusal split and cannot add an internal closure branch.
\end{proof}
