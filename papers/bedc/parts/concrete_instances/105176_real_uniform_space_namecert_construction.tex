\label{def:real-uniform-space-carrier}

$$

\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the endpoint names.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family selected for the displayed endpoint and readback rows.

\begin{theorem}[RealUniformSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-uniform-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealUniformSpaceUp$ carrier $U=(R,Q,W,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealUniformSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the ten displayed rows, endpoint access through $R$, regular rational readback through $Q$, finite window selection through $W$, dyadic tolerance accounting through $D$, metric distance witnessing through $M$, regular Cauchy entourage routing through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:real-uniform-space-carrier}. Each public obligation is attached to one displayed row, so carrier admission supplies precisely the endpoint, readback, window, tolerance, metric, entourage, transport, replay, provenance, and naming data.

The transport row is componentwise. Moving a packet along $H$ changes only the displayed representatives inside their $\hsame$ classes and cannot create a host uniform-space carrier or a quotient-completion equality.

Replay by $C$ follows the same route, and $P,N$ source and name that route locally. Since no accepted row stores a selected representative, countable-choice schedule, open-cover compactness datum, or ambient completeness witness, the NameCert surface is exhausted by the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUniformSpace Cauchy-entourage route]
\label{thm:real-uniform-space-cauchy-entourage-route}
Every $\RealUniformSpaceUp$ consumer that asks for a Cauchy entourage comparison must traverse
$$
R\longmapsto Q\longmapsto W\longmapsto D\longmapsto M\longmapsto E
$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The finite windows are then selected by $W$. Tolerance comparisons are recorded by $D$, and the metric-facing distance witnesses in $M$ are read against the same windows and dyadic ledgers.

Only after those rows are visible may the route reach the entourage row $E$. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this finite route, so no host uniformity, quotient completion, selected representative, or ambient completeness principle enters the handoff.
\end{proof}
