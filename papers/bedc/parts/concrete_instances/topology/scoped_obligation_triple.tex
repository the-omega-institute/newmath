\section{Scoped obligation triple}
\label{sec:topology-scoped-obligation-triple}

The topology certificate has enough indexed-open data for downstream use once its public surface is read as three obligations over visible $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows. The following triple records that surface in the form consumed by chapters that need topology but do not need a standard-space bridge.

\begin{definition}[Topology scoped obligation triple]
\label{def:topology-scoped-obligation-triple}
The scoped obligation triple for $\TopologyUp$ consists of the following three requirements over $\mathsf{BHist}$ indexed-open rows.

\begin{enumerate}
\item Carrier/classifier scope: every accepted source exposes only the carrier $X$, the carrier classifier $\mathsf{same}_X$, the open index $\mathsf{OpenIx}$, the membership row $\mathsf{OpenAt}$, and classifier transport through the indexed-open carrier of \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}.

\item Open-constructor exhaustion: every admitted public open from the metric-generated or singleton source is accounted for by the constructor ledger displayed in \autoref{def:topology-generated-open-exactness} and by the singleton boundary, meet, and union rows.

\item Downstream export certificate: the exported surface for $\BanachUp$, $\ManifoldUp$, $\ComplexTopologyUp$, and continuous-map chapters is the carrier/classifier scope together with the exhausted constructor ledger and the downstream surface of \autoref{thm:topology-downstream-namecert-export-surface}.
\end{enumerate}

The triple is an obligation inventory for the indexed-open certificate. It does not identify opens by equality in an external powerset and does not assert a bridge to a host topological-space object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Topology obligation carrier classifier scope]
\label{thm:topology-obligation-carrier-classifier-scope}
Every accepted $\TopologyUp$ source covered by \autoref{def:topology-scoped-obligation-triple} exposes only the carrier $X$, classifier $\mathsf{same}_X$, open index $\mathsf{OpenIx}$, membership row $\mathsf{OpenAt}$, and classifier transport supplied by \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier}. A downstream proof may transport membership in a carried public open only by passing through the carrying equation for some index $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$ and then using the $\mathsf{same}_X$-stability field of that indexed-open carrier. No external powerset equality is part of the obligation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a carried public open row. By \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carrier} and \autoref{def:topology-indexed-open-carried-predicate}, the row consists of an index $i:\mathsf{OpenIx}$ and a pointwise equation between the public predicate and $\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,-)$.

Given a classifier witness $\mathsf{same}_X(x,y)$, convert membership at $x$ through the carrying equation, apply the indexed-open membership-stability field to move from $\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,x)$ to $\mathsf{OpenAt}(i,y)$, and convert back through the carrying equation at $y$.

This is exactly the route isolated in \autoref{thm:topology-downstream-carrier-classifier-scope}. Since every step mentions only $X$, $\mathsf{same}_X$, $\mathsf{OpenIx}$, $\mathsf{OpenAt}$, and the carried row, the obligation has no place for host powerset equality.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TopologyUp.BHistCarriesOpen\_membership\_transport\_route}

\begin{theorem}[Topology obligation open constructor exhaustion]
\label{thm:topology-obligation-open-constructor-exhaustion}
Every public open admitted by the metric-generated source of \autoref{def:topology-metric-generated-carrier} or by the singleton empty-history source of \autoref{def:topology-singleton-empty-history-carrier} is exhausted by the displayed constructor ledger. For the metric-generated source the possibilities are exactly the six rows of \autoref{def:topology-generated-open-exactness}: singleton metric ball, finite-list intersection, binary generated-open meet, arbitrary union, empty open, and whole open. For the singleton source the public rows reduce to the boundary rows together with the displayed meet and union classifications, so they embed into the same bottom/top and constructor-closure accounting.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TopologyUp.TopologyPublicOpenTree\_constructor\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
For the metric-generated source, apply \autoref{thm:topology-generated-open-six-row-exactness-package}. Its row coverage, classification soundness, and classification completeness clauses range over precisely the six constructor rows listed in \autoref{def:topology-generated-open-exactness}.

The constructive split is the displayed six-case ledger. Singleton metric balls use \autoref{thm:topology-metric-base-open-transport}; finite-list intersections use \autoref{thm:topology-metric-base-intersection}; binary generated-open meets use \autoref{thm:topology-generated-open-binary-meet-row}; arbitrary unions use \autoref{thm:topology-indexed-open-arbitrary-union}; empty and whole opens use \autoref{thm:topology-indexed-open-boundary-closure}.

For the singleton source, inspect \autoref{def:topology-singleton-empty-history-carrier}. The bottom and top rows are supplied by \autoref{thm:topology-singleton-boundary-open-laws}, while finite intersections and arbitrary unions are supplied by \autoref{thm:topology-singleton-finite-intersection-laws} and \autoref{thm:topology-singleton-arbitrary-union-laws}.

These are the cases collected by \autoref{thm:topology-downstream-open-constructor-coverage}. Therefore every admitted public open from either source has a visible constructor record, and there is no seventh public-open route.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TopologyUp.BHistUnaryTopologyLedgerRow\_generated\_open\_exactness}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.TopologyUp.BHistLedgerPublicOpenTree\_constructor\_exhaustion}

\begin{theorem}[Topology obligation downstream export certificate]
\label{thm:topology-obligation-downstream-export-certificate}
The carrier/classifier scope of \autoref{thm:topology-obligation-carrier-classifier-scope}, the constructor exhaustion of \autoref{thm:topology-obligation-open-constructor-exhaustion}, and the export theorem \autoref{thm:topology-downstream-namecert-export-surface} assemble the scoped $\TopologyUp$ surface consumed by $\BanachUp$, $\ManifoldUp$, $\ComplexTopologyUp$, and continuous-map chapters. The exported data are the carrier, carrier classifier, carried-open index, membership row, constructor provenance in $\mathsf{BHist}$, finite-intersection constructor, arbitrary-union constructor, boundary rows, and the binary generated-open meet constructor, with no standard-space bridge claimed.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TopologyUp.TopologyScopedObligation\_downstream\_export\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
First use \autoref{thm:topology-obligation-carrier-classifier-scope} to identify the only membership-transport channel available to dependent chapters. This supplies the carrier, classifier, open index, membership row, and carrying equation.

Next use \autoref{thm:topology-obligation-open-constructor-exhaustion} to attach provenance to each public open. The metric-generated and singleton sources are both reduced to visible $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows, so downstream consumers do not need to inspect an ambient powerset.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:topology-downstream-namecert-export-surface}. That theorem already names the receiving chapters and records the exported finite-intersection, arbitrary-union, boundary, and binary generated-open meet rows. Combining the three clauses gives the scoped export certificate promised by \autoref{def:topology-scoped-obligation-triple}.
\end{proof}
