\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EquicontinuousFamilyUp$}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis and the Arzela--Ascoli finite-family reading, the $\EquicontinuousFamilyUp$ packet records the shared-modulus interface by which a finite family of continuous maps over one compact metric source exposes a single displayed modulus row to downstream compact-uniform-continuity and Ascoli consumers. It sits after the compact source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the graph rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the uniform-modulus consumer surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}, the finite observation schedules of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet does not introduce open-cover compactness, a host function-space topology, quotient equality of maps, or choice-selected pointwise moduli.

\begin{definition}[Equicontinuous family carrier]
\label{def:equicontinuous-family-carrier}
An $\EquicontinuousFamilyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(X,F,J,M,S,R,V,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the shared $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, including the finite metric reads and compact finite-net source used by the family.
\item $F$ is the finite ledger of $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph rows whose source coordinate is $X$ and whose values are read only through displayed graph windows.
\item $J$ is the finite family-index row, naming exactly the family members admitted by $F$.
\item $M$ is the shared $\UniformModulusUp$ row, recording one tolerance-to-radius ledger used uniformly for every index in $J$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation schedule through which the source and graph reads are inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the same tolerance and radius histories.
\item $V$ is the $\RealUp$ value-seal row reached only after the graph, schedule, regular rational, and shared-modulus rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact source, finite graph ledger, family index row, shared modulus row, observation schedule, regular rational readback, Real seal, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from compact-source reads to graph reads, from tolerance requests to the shared modulus row, and from scheduled readback to the Real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EquicontinuousFamilyUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuousFamilyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,F,J,M,S,R,V,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for an open cover, a selected global modulus outside $M$, a host function-space topology, quotient equality of functions, or a choice principle over the family.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuous family NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:equicontinuous-family-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EquicontinuousFamilyUp$ carrier $E=(X,F,J,M,S,R,V,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuousFamilyUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission, compact-source admission, finite continuous-map ledger admission, family-index totality, shared uniform-modulus exposure, StreamName and RegSeqRat readback, Real-seal handoff, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and ledger non-escape. These obligations are discharged entirely by the finite route
$$
X,F,J\longmapsto M\longmapsto S,R\longmapsto V
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:equicontinuous-family-carrier}. The shared compact source row $X$, finite graph ledger $F$, family index row $J$, shared modulus row $M$, schedule row $S$, regular readback row $R$, and Real seal $V$ are all visible coordinates of one finite packet.

The shared-modulus obligation is read before any Ascoli or compact-uniform-continuity consumer inspects the family. Each member named by $J$ is admitted through $F$, and the same tolerance-to-radius ledger $M$ is used for that member over the source row $X$.

The rows $S,R,V$ provide the finite observation and value boundary. They keep the scheduled source and graph reads attached to the same regular rational readback and Real seal, rather than to a host function-space object.

Transport and replay are supplied by $H$ and $C$, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming. Since the carrier has no row for open-cover compactness, quotient function equality, selected pointwise moduli, or ambient topology, the listed obligations exhaust the certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Equicontinuous family uniform-modulus handoff]
\label{thm:equicontinuous-family-uniform-modulus-handoff}
Any $\UniformModulusUp$, compact-uniform-continuity, or Arzela--Ascoli consumer of an accepted $\EquicontinuousFamilyUp$ carrier must receive the shared modulus row $M$ together with the compact source row $X$, finite continuous-map ledger $F$, family index row $J$, observation schedule $S$, regular rational readback $R$, Real seal $V$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$. The handoff exports only the displayed finite shared-modulus route and does not supply open-cover compactness, a function-space topology, quotient equality of maps, or choice-selected moduli.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier of \autoref{def:equicontinuous-family-carrier}. It displays the shared source, finite graph ledger, finite family index, shared modulus, schedule, readback, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows as the whole packet.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:equicontinuous-family-namecert-obligations}. They force every accepted read through $X,F,J,M,S,R,V,H,C,P,N$, with $M$ placed before the consumer can treat the family as equicontinuous.

The downstream consumer may add its own Ascoli, compact-uniform-continuity, or selection rows after this boundary. It cannot replace the displayed shared modulus by an open cover, host topology, quotient function equality, or a choice-selected family of pointwise moduli, because none of those objects is a carrier coordinate.
\end{proof}
