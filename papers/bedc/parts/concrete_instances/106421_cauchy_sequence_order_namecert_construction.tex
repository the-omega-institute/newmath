\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\mathsf{CauchySequenceOrder}^{\uparrow}$}

\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\mathsf{CauchySequenceOrder}^{\uparrow}$ packet records the finite constructive-order route by which two regular rational Cauchy source rows are compared before any $\RealUp$ consumer reads an ordered boundary. It sits over the binary algebra packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-sequence-algebra-namecert}, the regular source surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic comparison ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet gives completion and ordered-Real consumers one finite comparison surface for Cauchy sequence algebra; it does not import trichotomy, quotient Cauchy classes, selected limits, countable choice, host real order, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy sequence order carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-sequence-order-carrier}
A $\mathsf{CauchySequenceOrder}^{\uparrow}$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(X,Y,A,W,D,L,Q,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows whose regular rational observations supply the left and right comparison inputs.
\item $A$ is the upstream $\CauchySequenceAlgebraUp$ row that fixes the shared binary Cauchy-algebra source from which the comparison is read.
\item $W$ is the synchronized $\StreamNameUp$ window row. It records the finite pairs of observations where $X$ and $Y$ are inspected at the same requested tolerance scale.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison-budget row. It stores the displayed dyadic radius, rational endpoint differences, slack conversions, strict-gap tests, and non-strict comparison checks used by the accepted order request.
\item $L$ is the apartness row. It records only displayed positive-gap evidence between the two regular names, with the gap budget read through $D$ and the windows read through $W$.
\item $Q$ is the non-strict order row. It records only displayed tolerance domination evidence, again through the same $W$ and $D$ rows.
\item $R$ is the comparison replay row, exposing whether a downstream consumer requested apartness, non-strict order, or the boundary case where no displayed comparison row is accepted.
\item $S$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal handoff. It is exposed only after the requested comparison row has displayed its regular-source and dyadic-budget dependencies.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two source rows, algebra row, synchronized windows, dyadic budget, apartness row, non-strict order row, replay row, Real seal, continuation route, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a constructive order request through $X,Y,A,W,D,L,Q,R,S,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\mathsf{CauchySequenceOrder}^{\uparrow}$, $\CauchySequenceAlgebraUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\mathsf{CauchySequenceOrder}^{\uparrow}}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,A,W,D,L,Q,R,S,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for trichotomy, quotient-stream equality, a selected limit, host real order, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy sequence order NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-sequence-order-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\mathsf{CauchySequenceOrder}^{\uparrow}$ carrier
$$
O=(X,Y,A,W,D,L,Q,R,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\mathsf{CauchySequenceOrder}^{\uparrow}}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ sources, upstream $\CauchySequenceAlgebraUp$ row, synchronized $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison budget, apartness row, non-strict order row, comparison replay row, $\RealUp$ seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming;
\item source-window synchronization, requiring every comparison request to inspect $X$ and $Y$ through the same displayed window row $W$ before any dyadic comparison is read;
\item dyadic-budget exactness, requiring every apartness or non-strict order row to consume only the displayed radius, difference, slack, strict-gap, and domination entries of $D$;
\item comparison-row separation, requiring $L$ to carry only positive-gap apartness evidence and $Q$ to carry only non-strict domination evidence, while both rows consume the same source, algebra, window, and dyadic-budget rows;
\item Real seal handoff, requiring $S$ to be exposed only after the requested comparison row has displayed its regular-source and dyadic-budget dependencies;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the full route $X,Y,A,W,D,L,Q,R,S,P,N$ without adding trichotomy, quotient equality, selected limits, host real order, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No Cauchy sequence order NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is row projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-sequence-order-carrier}. The displayed coordinates are exactly the two source rows $X,Y$, algebra row $A$, synchronized windows $W$, dyadic budget $D$, comparison rows $L,Q,R$, Real seal $S$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Source-window synchronization is forced by the single window coordinate $W$. Both source reads must pass through that coordinate before the comparison budget is available, so the carrier has no second observation service for either input.

Dyadic-budget exactness and comparison-row separation are read from $D,L,Q$. The row $D$ supplies the finite arithmetic comparisons, the row $L$ consumes those comparisons for positive-gap apartness, and the row $Q$ consumes them for non-strict domination. Since $L$ and $Q$ are distinct rows with the same upstream inputs, neither row can import a hidden order oracle.

Finally, the replay, seal, and structural rows close the finite route. The row $R$ records which comparison request is being replayed, the seal $S$ is downstream of the displayed comparison dependency, and $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name the listed rows. The excluded trichotomy, quotient, selected-limit, host-order, choice, and ambient-completeness data have no carrier coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy sequence order dyadic budget exactness]
\label{thm:cauchy-sequence-order-dyadic-budget-exactness}
Every apartness or non-strict order fact exported from an accepted $\mathsf{CauchySequenceOrder}^{\uparrow}$ carrier reads its comparison budget through the displayed route
$$
X,Y,A \longmapsto W \longmapsto D \longmapsto L,Q,R \longmapsto S \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the exported fact obtains synchronized regular-source observations, dyadic radius and slack data, strict-gap or domination evidence, the terminal Real seal handoff, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming, and obtains no trichotomy datum, quotient Cauchy class, selected limit, host real order, countable-choice datum, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a downstream comparison request. By \autoref{def:cauchy-sequence-order-carrier}, the only source coordinates are $X$ and $Y$, and the upstream algebra row is $A$.

The finite observation surface is then forced through $W$. The dyadic comparison budget is forced through $D$, whose rows record the displayed radius, endpoint differences, slack conversions, strict-gap tests, and domination checks.

Apartness reads the route row $L$, non-strict order reads the route row $Q$, and replay reads the route row $R$. Each of these rows is downstream of the same sources, algebra row, windows, and dyadic budget named in \autoref{thm:cauchy-sequence-order-namecert-obligations}.

The Real-facing handoff is last. A $\RealUp$ consumer can read $S$ only after the requested comparison row has displayed its dependencies, and the rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve that same finite route. No coordinate remains from which trichotomy, quotient equality, selected limits, host order, choice, or ambient completeness could be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy sequence order non-escape]
\label{thm:cauchy-sequence-order-nonescape}
Let $O=(X,Y,A,W,D,L,Q,R,S,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\mathsf{CauchySequenceOrder}^{\uparrow}$ carrier. Any order-facing fact exported from $O$ is a rowwise consequence of the displayed regular source rows, algebra row, synchronized windows, dyadic comparison budget, apartness row, non-strict order row, replay row, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows. It is not a trichotomy principle, quotient Cauchy class, selected limit, host real-order fact, countable-choice datum, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier rows. The data-bearing coordinates are exactly $X,Y,A,W,D,L,Q,R,S$, and the structural coordinates are exactly $H,C,P,N$.

An exported apartness fact must use $L$ after the source, algebra, window, and dyadic-budget rows have been displayed. An exported non-strict order fact must use $Q$ after the same upstream rows have been displayed. A replayed comparison must pass through $R$, and any Real-facing boundary then passes through the seal row $S$.

The structural rows preserve only that route. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay cannot create trichotomy, quotient equality, selected limits, host order, choice, or ambient completeness, while provenance and local naming record only the finite packet. Thus every exported order-facing fact is confined to the listed rows, and each excluded principle has no entry point.
\end{proof}
