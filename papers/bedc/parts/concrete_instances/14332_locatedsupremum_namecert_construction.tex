\begin{definition}[LocatedSupremum carrier]
\label{def:located-supremum-carrier}
A $\LocatedSupremumUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(L,U,A,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is a located-cut or located-real family row, read through the finite locatedness and comparison surfaces of the sibling $\LocatedCutUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, and $\BishopRealComparisonUp$ chapters;
\item $U$ is the upper-bound ledger, displaying the inspected bound claims and the finite comparison rows that certify each requested member of $L$ below the proposed bound surface;
\item $A$ is the lower-approximation and cofinality ledger, displaying the finite rational or dyadic witnesses that approach the bound surface from the family side at the precisions requested by the consumer;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ precision-window row that fixes which finite observations of the family, bound ledger, and approximation ledger are in scope;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row that repacks the windowed observations as regular rational readback data;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only through $L,U,A,W,R$;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the family row, upper-bound ledger, approximation ledger, precision windows, regular handoff, real seal, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming row;

The accepted carrier stores no quotient cut, no selected supremum element, no trichotomy oracle, no host real equality test, and no ambient order-completeness coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedSupremum NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-supremum-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted packet $S=(L,U,A,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedSupremumUp}$ obligations are exactly the following finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is the displayed packet with a located family row, upper-bound ledger, lower-approximation ledger, precision window, regular-rational handoff, real seal, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming data;
\item located upper-bound stability: replacing $L,U,W,R,E$ along the displayed $\hsame$ transports preserves the same upper-bound ledger reads through the $\BishopRealComparisonUp$ comparison surface;
\item lower-approximation cofinality: every requested precision in $W$ receives a displayed approximation row in $A$ whose readback reaches the same bound surface through $R$ and $E$;
\item classifier transport: the componentwise transports in $H$ are sufficient to move the located family, bound ledger, approximation ledger, precision window, regular handoff, and real seal through the same classifier routes;
\item ledger exhaustion: every admissible upper-bound or lower-approximation consumer must read one of the rows $L,U,A,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$ before obtaining any real-facing observation;
\item handoff stability: the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ and $\RealUp$ seal $E$ remain attached to the same displayed precision window and ledgers under the continuation routes in $C$.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation may call on a quotient cut, selected supremum, trichotomy, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient order completeness.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedSupremum real-seal boundary]
\label{thm:located-supremum-real-seal-boundary}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The rows $R$ and $E$ then give the regular-rational and real-name handoff for exactly those displayed observations. The transport and continuation rows can replay this route but cannot add a quotient cut, chosen supremum, trichotomy oracle, host equality test, or order-completeness principle, because none of those rows occurs in the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
