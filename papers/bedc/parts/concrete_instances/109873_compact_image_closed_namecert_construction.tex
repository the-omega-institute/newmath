\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompactImageClosedUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-compact-image-closed-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\CompactImageClosedUp$ packet records the closed-target reading of the image of a compact metric source under a displayed continuous map into a separated metric target. It consumes the compact source surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the finite image-net source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-image-compact-namecert}, and the separated target boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}. The packet names the finite route by which image-net data and target separatedness force closed-boundary reads; it does not introduce open-cover compactness, a host image set, a selected preimage, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Compact image closed carrier]
\label{def:compact-image-closed-carrier}
A $\CompactImageClosedUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(K,F,I,S,C,M,R,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, exposing the compact metric inspection data and finite source-net provenance consumed by the image route.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row from the source surface displayed by $K$ into the target metric surface.
\item $I$ is the $\ContinuousImageCompactUp$ finite image-net row, recording the target centers and tolerances produced from $K$ and $F$.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ target row, used only for zero-distance target identity and boundary comparison.
\item $C$ is the $\ClosedSetUp$ boundary row for the named target-image read.
\item $M$ is the target $\MetricUp$ distance row, exposing only displayed distances between target names.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ distance readback row for zero and positive tolerance comparisons.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,F,I,S,C,M,R,T,P,N$.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a closed-target-boundary request through the compact source, continuous-map row, image-net row, separated target row, metric and real readbacks, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactImageClosedUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\ContinuousImageCompactUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\ClosedSetUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactImageClosedUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rows $K,F,I,S,C,M,R,H,T,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, host image subsets, selected preimages, quotient equality of target names, ambient completeness, or a hidden closure operator.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact image closed NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-image-closed-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactImageClosedUp$ carrier $Q=(K,F,I,S,C,M,R,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactImageClosedUp}$ surface consists exactly of six finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the compact source, continuous-map graph, finite image-net, separated target, closed-boundary, metric, real readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item image-net accountability, requiring every target center and tolerance in $I$ to be read through the $\ContinuousImageCompactUp$ route over the same $K$ and $F$;
\item separated-boundary accountability, requiring target identity at zero distance to pass through $S,M,R$ before the closed-boundary row $C$ is exposed;
\item closed-target exactness, requiring every boundary read of the displayed image to be witnessed by finite image-net approximation together with separated target comparison;
\item componentwise transport and replay stability under $H$ and $T$ for the same displayed rows;
\item ledger refusal, stating that open-cover compactness, host image subsets, selected preimages, quotient equality of target names, ambient completeness, and hidden closure operators are not carrier coordinates.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The mathematical rows are $K,F,I,S,C,M,R$, and the structural rows are $H,T,P,N$.

The image-net obligation is read first. Row $I$ may expose only target centers and tolerances obtained from the compact source row $K$ and the continuous-map row $F$, as in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-image-compact-namecert}.

The target boundary obligation is then read through the separated metric. A zero-distance comparison between a displayed target read and the finite image-net route must pass through $M$ and $R$, and the target identity conclusion is allowed only through $S$.

The closed-boundary row $C$ is forced by this finite route. A boundary read of the target image can cite the finite image-net approximation and the separated zero-distance classifier, but it cannot cite a host image subset, an open cover, or a selected inverse image.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same row inventory. Since the refused objects do not occur among $K,F,I,S,C,M,R,H,T,P,N$, no local NameCert obligation can export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact image closed target route]
\label{thm:compact-image-closed-closed-target-route}
Every accepted $\CompactImageClosedUp$ carrier $Q=(K,F,I,S,C,M,R,H,T,P,N)$ exports closedness of the displayed compact image only through the finite route
$$
\CompactMetricUp,\quad
\ContinuousMapUp,\quad
\ContinuousImageCompactUp,\quad
\SeparatedMetricUp,\quad
\ClosedSetUp .
$$
A downstream closed-set consumer receives the finite image-net row $I$, the target distance readbacks $M,R$, the separated identity row $S$, and the closed-boundary row $C$. It receives no open-cover compactness datum, host image subset, selected preimage, quotient equality of target names, ambient completeness coordinate, or hidden closure operator.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a target-boundary request for the displayed image. The carrier first exposes the compact source row $K$ and the continuous-map row $F$.

Read the finite image-net source through $I$. This gives target centers and tolerances produced by the $\ContinuousImageCompactUp$ packet from the same $K$ and $F$; no open-cover row or host image subset is introduced.

Now compare the requested boundary target with the finite image-net route. The metric and real readbacks $M,R$ provide the zero-distance surface, and the separated target row $S$ converts that displayed zero-distance comparison into target identity.

Finally expose $C$ as the closed-boundary row for the named image read. Rows $H,T,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite route. Thus closedness is exported only as the finite image-net plus separated-boundary route and not as any host image-set or open-cover principle.
\end{proof}
