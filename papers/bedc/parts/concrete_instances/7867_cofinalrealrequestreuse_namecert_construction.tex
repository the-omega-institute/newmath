The $\CofinalRealRequestReuseUp$ packet realizes the Real and Completion route of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness} as a finite request-reuse certificate. It consumes the finite request fusion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-cauchy-completion-namecert}, the window naming surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the regular-rational and dyadic readback rows used by the completion seal. The packet is not a completion theorem by itself; it is the displayed lock that lets repeated cofinal precision bundles reuse one visible route before a $\RealUp$ seal is served.

\begin{definition}[Cofinal real request-reuse carrier]
\label{def:cofinal-real-request-reuse-carrier}
A $\CofinalRealRequestReuseUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(Q,\sigma,W,G,D,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the finite bundle of cofinal Real-completion precision requests;
\item $\sigma$ is the cofinal selector row that chooses the displayed tail service for every request in $Q$;
\item $W$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ window family through which all selected finite observations are read;
\item $G$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row over the same shared windows;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger recording the requested tolerances, endpoints, and finite comparison rows;
\item $S$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after $Q,\sigma,W,G,D$ have been replayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the request bundle, selector, window, readback, dyadic ledger, seal, route, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that replay the cofinal selector, shared StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore observations, and RealUp seal handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, $\RealObservationBudgetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CofinalRealRequestReuseUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no second request-bundle route, no host completion object, no quotient stream equality, no hidden selector, and no RealUp seal outside the displayed shared route.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cofinal real request-reuse NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cofinal-real-request-reuse-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cofinal-real-request-reuse-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CofinalRealRequestReuseUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $R=(Q,\sigma,W,G,D,S,H,C,P,N)$, cofinal request-bundle exposure through $Q$, selector exposure through $\sigma$, shared $\StreamNameUp$ window exposure through $W$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback through $G$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger exactness through $D$, $\RealUp$ seal handoff through $S$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier definition. Each obligation row named in the statement is a displayed coordinate of $R$, so carrier admission fixes the request bundle, selector, shared window, readback, dyadic ledger, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name at once.

Use the finite request fusion supplied by the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ surface. A cofinal precision bundle may be read request by request, but every accepted read must return to the same displayed selector $\sigma$ and the same shared StreamName window family $W$ before reaching $G,D,S$.

The downstream handoff rows then keep the route local. The RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore ledger, RealUp seal, componentwise transports, continuations, package row, and NameCert row are all consumed from the displayed packet, so no unlisted completion or selector coordinate enters the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cofinal real request-reuse fused handoff]
\label{thm:cofinal-real-request-reuse-fused-handoff}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\CofinalRealRequestReuseUp$ packet reads the finite request bundle $Q$ through the same selector $\sigma$ and the same shared window route
$$
W,G,D,S,H,C,P,N.
$$
Repeated precision bundles may therefore reuse the displayed RealCauchyCompletion route, but they cannot split into separate StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, or RealUp seal routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the request-bundle row $Q$ and the cofinal selector $\sigma$. The selector is a coordinate of the packet, so a request cannot choose a tail service outside the displayed carrier.

Apply the finite request fusion route from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-cauchy-completion-namecert}. That route turns finitely many precision reads into a finite completion-facing surface, and here its window side is identified with the single StreamName family $W$.

Now replay the downstream handoff. The RegSeqRat row $G$ reads only the windows in $W$, the DyadicRatCore ledger $D$ records only the displayed observations, and the RealUp seal $S$ is consumed only after those rows pass through $H,C,P,N$. Hence repeated precision bundles reuse one route and do not branch into separate completion-seal paths.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cofinal real request-reuse non-escape]
\label{thm:cofinal-real-request-reuse-nonescape}
An accepted $\CofinalRealRequestReuseUp$ packet exports no host completion carrier, no quotient stream equality, no second cofinal selector, no unshared StreamName window, no RegSeqRat readback detached from the dyadic ledger, and no RealUp seal detached from the displayed packet. Every downstream $\RealUp$ or completion-seal consumer factors through $Q,\sigma,W,G,D,S,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier rows. The only request surface is $Q$, the only selector is $\sigma$, the only window family is $W$, and the only Real-facing row is the seal $S$.

Use the NameCert obligations to force every accepted read through the shared rows $W,G,D,S,H,C,P,N$. The componentwise transport and continuation rows record how the packet is replayed; they do not add another selector or another completion route.

Finally use the fused handoff theorem. Since every completion-facing consumer must reuse the same displayed route, there is no coordinate from which a host completion carrier, quotient stream equality, unshared window, detached readback, or detached RealUp seal can be projected.
\end{proof}
