\begin{theorem}[Diagonal convergence-bound nonchoice boundary]
\label{thm:diagonal-convergence-bound-nonchoice-boundary}
An accepted $\DiagonalConvergenceBoundUp$ packet exports exactly the displayed family row $X$, modulus row $\mu$, request row $n$, diagonal representative row $D$, dyadic tolerance ledger $Q$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, $\RealUp$ seal row $E$, componentwise transport $H$, continuation replay $C$, package provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$. It exports no selected host limit, quotient-stream equality, countable choice row, hidden tail selector, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness proof, or off-packet convergence witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the coordinates of \autoref{def:diagonal-convergence-bound-carrier}. Every coordinate is either a displayed BEDC row or structural support for transport, replay, provenance, and naming.

\autoref{thm:diagonal-convergence-bound-namecert-obligations} accounts for all admissible carrier and classifier reads. The classifier of \autoref{def:diagonal-convergence-bound-classifier} then restricts comparison to those same displayed rows.

The excluded objects require coordinates absent from the carrier. Therefore they cannot be exported by the local naming certificate or recovered by a consumer of the displayed convergence-bound row.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:diagonal-convergence-bound-real-completion-handoff}

\begin{proof}

Next use the dependency route.
