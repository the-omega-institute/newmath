Following Bourbaki, Kelley, and Bishop-style completion functoriality, the $\MetricCompletionFunctorUp$ packet records the L10 route that transports uniformly continuous metric maps across the displayed completion surface. It specializes the metric-completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the functorial transport surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionfunctor-namecert}, and the Cauchy-name functor packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionfunctor-namecert}. Its cofinal evidence is read through the Cauchy-filter branch of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert} or the Cauchy-net branch of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}; the packet itself adds no quotient completion, selected representative, host completed metric space, or ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[MetricCompletionFunctor carrier]
\label{def:metric-completion-functor-carrier}
A $\MetricCompletionFunctorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(M_0,M_1,G,E,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M_0$ is the source $\MetricCompletionUp$ row, including the displayed metric source, its Cauchy-filter or Cauchy-net cofinal evidence, finite readback, and separated-limit comparison row.
\item $M_1$ is the target $\MetricCompletionUp$ row with the same kind of displayed completion evidence and readback surface.
\item $G$ is the metric-map row whose finite modulus and graph ledger send source observations to target observations before completion-facing reads are taken.
\item $E$ is the extension row inherited from the $\CompletionFunctorUp$ and $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ surfaces, routing the displayed map through the two completion rows.
\item $T$ is the functoriality ledger comparing identity and composition reads by finite $\hsame$ transport over $M_0$, $M_1$, $G$, and $E$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two metric-completion rows, the map row, the extension row, the functoriality ledger, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
M_0,G,M_1 \longmapsto E \longmapsto T,H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricCompletionFunctorUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CompletionFunctorUp$, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionFunctorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected limits, host function equality, a host completed metric space, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionFunctor NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-completion-functor-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\MetricCompletionFunctorUp$ carrier $F=(M_0,M_1,G,E,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionFunctorUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, source and target metric-completion exactness, map-modulus compatibility, extension-ledger exactness, identity and composition functoriality, classifier transport, and non-escape of quotient completions, selected limits, host function equality, countable choice, and ambient completeness. Every obligation is read from the displayed rows $M_0,M_1,G,E,T,H,C,P,N$ and their finite $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:metric-completion-functor-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the two metric-completion rows, the displayed map ledger, the extension row, functoriality ledger, transport rows, replay rows, provenance, and local naming row.

The source and target exactness clauses require every completion-facing read to pass through $M_0$ or $M_1$ before the extension row is inspected. The map-modulus clause keeps the graph ledger $G$ attached to the finite source observations and target observations already visible in those rows.

Extension exactness is the coordinate $E$. It is read from the existing completion-functor and Cauchy-completion-functor surfaces, so no second extension route or host function equality is available.

Identity and composition are the public roles of $T$, transported componentwise by $H$ and replayed through $C$. The provenance and local naming rows then exhaust the packet, leaving no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionFunctor L10 route]
\label{thm:metric-completion-functor-route}
Every L10 completion-route consumer of an accepted $\MetricCompletionFunctorUp$ packet factors through the finite chain
$$
M_0,G,M_1 \longmapsto E \longmapsto T,H,C,P,N .
$$
The route first reads the source $\MetricCompletionUp$ packet, then the displayed map ledger, then the target $\MetricCompletionUp$ packet, and only then the extension and functoriality rows supplied by the completion-functor surfaces. It therefore connects $\MetricCompletionUp$ to $\CompletionFunctorUp$ and $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ by displayed finite data rather than by a quotient completion object or a host-level completed metric functor.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metric-completion-functor-carrier}. The first completion-facing coordinate is $M_0$, so the consumer begins with the source metric-completion evidence, including its filter or net branch and finite readback row.

The next analytic coordinate is the map ledger $G$. Its finite modulus and graph rows move displayed source observations to target observations before any extension read is taken.

The target completion row $M_1$ supplies the completion-facing landing surface. The extension row $E$ then repacks the displayed map through the completion-functor and Cauchy-completion-functor interfaces, while the functoriality ledger $T$ records identity and composition comparisons as finite transport.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the same route. Since the carrier has no quotient, selected-limit, host-function-equality, or ambient-completeness coordinate, every L10 consumer receives only the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}
