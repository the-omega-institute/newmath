\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\LatticeUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lattice-namecert}

The lattice interface \(\LatticeUp\) extends \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-poset-namecert} with binary meet \(\wedge\) and join \(\vee\) operations, each classified as the greatest lower bound and least upper bound of two elements under the partial order. Idempotence, commutativity, associativity, and absorption laws are derived from the bound characterizations under the carrier's classifier.

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[Lattice carrier]
\label{def:lattice-carrier}
Use a poset setup extended with meet and join operations carrying bound-characterization proofs.
\end{definition}

\section{Concrete singleton prefix lattice instance}
\label{sec:lattice-concrete-singleton-prefix-instance}

\begin{definition}[Concrete singleton prefix lattice instance]
\label{def:concrete-singleton-prefix-lattice-instance}
Let $A_{\emp}$ be the singleton history source of
Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source} at $a_0=\emp$. Define
$\rho_{\emp}$ by
$$
\rho_{\emp}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow
A_{\emp}(h)\land A_{\emp}(k)\land\hsame(h,k).
$$
Let $h\le_{\emp} k$ mean that $A_{\emp}(h)$ and $A_{\emp}(k)$ hold and that
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}(h,k)$ witnesses the prefix comparison between the
carried endpoints. Define the concrete lattice operations by
$$
\mathsf{meet}(h,k):=\emp,\qquad
\mathsf{join}(h,k):=\emp.
$$
The certificate has ten fields: traditional target the one-point lattice; BEDC
source $\mathsf{BHist}$ plus the singleton source and concrete prefix order;
carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier $\rho_{\emp}$; stability obligations for poset
laws, meet lower bounds, meet greatestness, join upper bounds, join leastness,
and meet and join congruence; ledger singleton endpoint plus prefix witnesses;
core theorem Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-prefix-lattice-laws};
exactness singleton endpoint classification; standard bridge terminal
one-point lattice; and status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete singleton prefix lattice laws]
\label{thm:concrete-singleton-prefix-lattice-laws}
For all carried histories $h,k,z$ in $A_{\emp}$, the endpoints
$\mathsf{meet}(h,k)$ and $\mathsf{join}(h,k)$ are carried by $A_{\emp}$ and
satisfy the meet lower-bound laws, meet greatest-lower-bound law, join
upper-bound laws, join least-upper-bound law, and meet and join congruence laws
for the restricted prefix order $\le_{\emp}$ under the classifier
$\rho_{\emp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The singleton carrier witnesses identify each carried endpoint with $\emp$.
Thus $A_{\emp}(h)$, $A_{\emp}(k)$, and $A_{\emp}(z)$ reduce every endpoint
comparison needed by the order, meet, and join clauses to the empty-history
endpoint.

The restricted prefix comparisons are supplied by
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE\_of\_hsame}$ after the singleton witnesses produce
the relevant $\hsame$ facts. The inherited order boundary is the concrete unary
prefix poset law package of Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-unary-prefix-poset-laws},
with antisymmetry supplied by Theorem~\ref{thm:poset-prefix-antisymmetry}.

The meet and join outputs are both definitionally $\emp$. The lower-bound and
upper-bound clauses therefore use the singleton endpoint witness for $\emp$
and the prefix witnesses from the previous paragraph; the greatest-lower-bound
and least-upper-bound clauses use the same reduction for the third carried
history $z$.

Congruence is immediate because both operations ignore their operands and
return $\emp$. After unfolding, every source and target output is classified by
$\rho_{\emp}(\emp,\emp)$, assembled from two copies of $A_{\emp}(\emp)$ and the
reflexive witness $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonPrefix\_laws}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice order has empty endpoints]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-le-empty-endpoints}
For $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, the restricted singleton prefix order satisfies
$$
h\le_{\emp} k
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
h\hsame\emp\land k\hsame\emp.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction reads the two singleton carrier fields from the
definition of $h\le_{\emp} k$. These fields are exactly $h\hsame\emp$ and
$k\hsame\emp$.

For the reverse direction, the two endpoint witnesses supply the singleton
carrier fields. Their transitive composite through $\emp$ gives $h\hsame k$,
and the prefix comparison follows from the concrete
$\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE\_of\_hsame}$ constructor.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonLE\_empty\_endpoints\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice carrier order collapse]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-carrier-order-collapse}
If $h$ and $k$ are carried by $A_{\emp}$, then both restricted order
comparisons hold, the singleton classifier relates $h$ and $k$, and both
endpoints are internally the same as $\emp$:
$$
h\le_{\emp} k\land k\le_{\emp} h\land \rho_{\emp}(h,k)
\land h\hsame\emp\land k\hsame\emp.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The two carrier witnesses are the endpoint comparisons
$h\hsame\emp$ and $k\hsame\emp$. Their composite through $\emp$ gives
$h\hsame k$, and symmetry gives $k\hsame h$.

The two restricted order comparisons are obtained from these two sameness
witnesses by $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE\_of\_hsame}$, together with the same
carrier fields. The classifier package uses the carrier fields and
$h\hsame k$, and the endpoint clauses are the original carrier witnesses.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonCarrier\_order\_collapse}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice antisymmetry classifier]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-le-antisymm-classifier}
If $h\le_{\emp} k$ and $k\le_{\emp} h$ in the restricted singleton prefix
order, then $\rho_{\emp}(h,k)$, $h\hsame\emp$, and $k\hsame\emp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The two restricted comparisons carry their endpoint fields, so the first
comparison already identifies both $h$ and $k$ with $\emp$. The prefix
components of the two comparisons are opposing $\mathsf{PreorderPrefixLE}$
witnesses, and concrete prefix antisymmetry gives $\hsame(h,k)$. These three
pieces are exactly the classifier data $\rho_{\emp}(h,k)$, together with the
two displayed empty endpoint witnesses.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonLE\_antisymm\_classifier}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice meet-join absorption classifier]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-meet-join-absorption-classifier}
If $h$ and $k$ are carried by $A_{\emp}$, then both absorption composites
$\mathsf{meet}(h,\mathsf{join}(h,k))$ and
$\mathsf{join}(h,\mathsf{meet}(h,k))$ are classified with $h$ by
$\rho_{\emp}$, and both output histories are $\hsame$ to $\emp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Both concrete lattice operations are definitionally $\emp$. Hence each
absorption composite is the empty history, whose singleton carrier witness is
reflexive. The carrier witness for $h$ gives $h\hsame\emp$, so symmetry gives
the classifier component from each absorption output back to $h$. Reassemble
the two $\rho_{\emp}$ classifiers and record the two empty output endpoints.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonMeetJoin\_absorption\_classifier}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice meet-join absorption is empty-classified]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-meet-join-absorption-empty-classified}
For carried singleton endpoints $h,k$, both absorption composites reduce to
the empty endpoint and classify back to $h$:
$$
\begin{aligned}
&\mathsf{meet}(h,\mathsf{join}(h,k))\hsame\emp,\qquad
\mathsf{join}(h,\mathsf{meet}(h,k))\hsame\emp,\\
&\rho_{\emp}(\mathsf{meet}(h,\mathsf{join}(h,k)),h),\qquad
\rho_{\emp}(\mathsf{join}(h,\mathsf{meet}(h,k)),h).
\end{aligned}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Both concrete operations are definitionally the endpoint $\emp$. The two
endpoint equalities are therefore reflexive empty-history sameness. Since $h$
is carried by the singleton carrier, symmetry of its carrier witness classifies
$\emp$ with $h$, and the classifier packages the two carrier witnesses with
that history-sameness witness.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonMeetJoin\_absorption\_empty\_classified}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice meet-join idempotent commutative classifier]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-meet-join-idempotent-comm-classifier}
For carried singleton endpoints $h,k$, meet and join are classified with the
idempotent endpoint and with their swapped arguments:
$$
\rho_{\emp}(\mathsf{meet}(h,h),h)\land
\rho_{\emp}(\mathsf{join}(h,h),h)\land
\rho_{\emp}(\mathsf{meet}(h,k),\mathsf{meet}(k,h))\land
\rho_{\emp}(\mathsf{join}(h,k),\mathsf{join}(k,h)).
$$
Moreover $\mathsf{meet}(h,k)\hsame\emp$ and
$\mathsf{join}(h,k)\hsame\emp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The concrete singleton meet and join operations both return $\emp$. Thus the
swapped meet outputs and swapped join outputs are classified by reflexive
empty-history sameness. Since $h$ is carried by the singleton carrier, symmetry
of $h\hsame\emp$ classifies each idempotent output with $h$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonMeetJoin\_idempotent\_comm\_classifier}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice classifier has empty endpoints]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-classifier-empty-endpoints}
For $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, the singleton lattice classifier satisfies
$$
\rho_{\emp}(h,k)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
h\hsame\emp\land k\hsame\emp.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction projects the two carrier fields from the classifier
tuple. Since the singleton carrier is the predicate $x\hsame\emp$, these fields
are exactly the two endpoint witnesses.

Conversely, the two endpoint witnesses make $h$ and $k$ singleton-carried.
Their composite through $\emp$, using symmetry on the second witness, supplies
$h\hsame k$ and hence the classifier tuple.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonClassifier\_empty\_endpoints\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice classifier continuation result-left iff]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-classifier-continuation-result-left}
For histories $P,Q,R:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\ContR(P,Q,R)$, the singleton
lattice classifier relates $P$ and $Q$ exactly when it relates the
continuation result $R$ to $P$:
$$
\mathsf{LatticeSingletonClassifier}(P,Q)
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{LatticeSingletonClassifier}(R,P).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction uses the singleton carriers of $P$ and $Q$ to transport
the continuation result to the right-unit continuation at $\emp$, so $R$ is
singleton-carried and compares with $P$ through $\emp$. For the reverse
direction, the result-left classifier identifies $R$ with $\emp$; transporting
the continuation to empty result and applying empty-result inversion recovers
the singleton carrier witnesses for both inputs, and hence the input
classifier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonClassifier\_continuation\_result\_left\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton meet--join absorption gives order and classifier data]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-meet-join-absorption-order-classifier}
If $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ are singleton-carried, then the two singleton lattice
absorption endpoints
$$
h\wedge(h\vee k)
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
h\vee(h\wedge k)
$$
are both definitionally the empty history. They satisfy the restricted prefix
order comparisons
$$
h\wedge(h\vee k)\le_{\emp} h
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
h\le_{\emp} h\vee(h\wedge k),
$$
are both classified with $h$, and both read back to $\emp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
In the singleton lattice, meet and join ignore their inputs and return $\emp$.
The carrier witness for $h$ gives $h\hsame\emp$, while reflexivity gives
$\emp\hsame\emp$.

The first order comparison uses symmetry of $h\hsame\emp$ to obtain
$\emp\hsame h$ and then applies the prefix comparison constructor. The second
uses $h\hsame\emp$ directly. The same two $\hsame$ witnesses assemble the
classifier tuples with $h$, and the endpoint readbacks are both reflexivity at
$\emp$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonMeetJoin\_absorption\_order\_classifier}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton meet greatest lower bound]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-meet-greatest-lower-bound}
For carried histories $h,k,z$ in the singleton lattice, the concrete meet output
is the empty history, and the lower-bound condition against that meet is exactly
the pair of lower-bound conditions against the two operands:
$$
\hsame(\mathsf{LatticeSingletonMeet}(h,k),\emp)
\land
\left(
z\le_{\emp}\mathsf{LatticeSingletonMeet}(h,k)
\Longleftrightarrow
z\le_{\emp} h\land z\le_{\emp} k
\right).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The meet operation is definitionally the empty history. Since $h,k,z$ are all
carried by the singleton carrier, the order endpoint theorem
\autoref{thm:lattice-singleton-le-empty-endpoints} reduces every displayed
comparison to the two empty-endpoint witnesses. The forward direction rebuilds
the two operand bounds from the endpoint witnesses for $z,h,k$; the reverse
direction rebuilds the meet bound from the endpoint witness for $z$ and
reflexivity at $\emp$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonMeet\_empty\_output\_greatest\_lower\_bound\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton join least upper bound]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-join-least-upper-bound-empty-iff}
For carried histories $h,k,z$ in the singleton lattice, the concrete join
least-upper-bound comparison is exactly the pair of upper-bound comparisons
against the two operands, together with the empty readback of the join:
$$
\mathsf{LatticeSingletonJoin}(h,k)\le_{\emp} z
\Longleftrightarrow
h\le_{\emp} z\land k\le_{\emp} z\land
\mathsf{LatticeSingletonJoin}(h,k)\hsame\emp.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The join operation is definitionally the empty history. Since $h,k,z$ are all
carried by the singleton carrier, the singleton order comparison between any
two of them is built from the corresponding \(\hsame\) chain through \(\emp\).
The forward direction reconstructs the two operand upper bounds and records
reflexivity of the join endpoint; the reverse direction uses the displayed join
endpoint together with the carrier of \(z\) to rebuild the join bound.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonJoin\_least\_upper\_bound\_empty\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice append tail emptiness]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-le-append-tail-empty}
For $h,t:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $A_{\emp}(h)$ and
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(t)$, then the restricted singleton prefix order
satisfies
$$
\mathsf{append}(h,t)\le_{\emp} h
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
t\hsame\emp.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction projects the underlying prefix comparison from the
restricted order and applies the concrete append-tail backforcing law from
\(\PreorderUp\). A unary tail that returns from $\mathsf{append}(h,t)$ back
to $h$ must be the empty history.

For the reverse direction, the witness $t\hsame\emp$ reduces
$\mathsf{append}(h,t)$ to $h$ by the right continuation unit law. The carried
witness $A_{\emp}(h)$ therefore also carries $\mathsf{append}(h,t)$, and the
prefix comparison is the empty-tail side of the same append-tail equivalence.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonLE\_append\_tail\_empty\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice classifier append tail emptiness]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-classifier-append-tail-empty}
For $h,t:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $A_{\emp}(h)$, then the singleton classifier
satisfies
$$
\mathsf{LatticeSingletonClassifier}(\mathsf{append}(h,t),h)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
t\hsame\emp.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction projects the carried left endpoint from the classifier.
Since $\mathsf{append}(h,t)\hsame\emp$, the concrete append-empty inversion
forces $t\hsame\emp$.

Conversely, assume $t\hsame\emp$. The append endpoint is carried because both
$h$ and $t$ are empty in the singleton sense, and the right continuation unit
identifies $\mathsf{append}(h,t)$ with $h$. Together with the given carrier for
$h$, these witnesses reconstruct the singleton classifier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonClassifier\_append\_tail\_empty\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice append tail upper emptiness]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-le-append-tail-upper-empty}
For $h,t:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $A_{\emp}(h)$ and
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(t)$, then the restricted singleton prefix order
satisfies
$$
h\le_{\emp}\mathsf{append}(h,t)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
t\hsame\emp.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction reads the upper endpoint carrier from the restricted
order. Thus $\mathsf{append}(h,t)$ is history-same to $\emp$, and the BEDC
append-empty inversion forces both endpoints of the append, in particular
$t$, to be empty. Conversely, if $t\hsame\emp$, the right continuation unit
identifies $\mathsf{append}(h,t)$ with $h$, so the given singleton carrier
for $h$ also carries the append endpoint. The prefix witness is the concrete
append-tail continuation.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonLE\_append\_tail\_upper\_empty\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton lattice meet greatest lower bound empty exactness]
\label{thm:lattice-singleton-meet-glb-empty-exactness}
For carried singleton endpoints $h,k,z$, the statement that $z$ is below the
concrete meet of $h$ and $k$ is equivalent to the two endpoint lower-bound
clauses together with the fact that the concrete meet is empty:
$$
z\le_{\emp}\mathsf{meet}(h,k)
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
z\le_{\emp}h\wedge z\le_{\emp}k\wedge \mathsf{meet}(h,k)\hsame\emp.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The singleton carrier identifies $h,k,z$ with $\emp$, and the concrete meet
operation is definitionally $\emp$. Thus the forward direction builds both
lower-bound comparisons from the endpoint sameness witnesses using the
singleton prefix comparison. Conversely, the displayed empty-meet endpoint
and the carrier witness for $z$ build the singleton comparison
$z\le_{\emp}\mathsf{meet}(h,k)$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp.LatticeSingletonMeet\_greatest\_lower\_bound\_empty\_iff}

The $\LatticeUp$ schema is instantiated by a concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier
and operations, so Lean can target $\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp}$ as a
$\mathsf{BHist}$-anchored certificate rather than an abstract operator schema.

\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[Lattice source specification]
\label{def:lattice-source-specification}
The source is a certified poset carrier plus binary meet and join data.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Lattice pattern specification]
\label{def:lattice-pattern-specification}
The pattern is expression formation by \(\wedge\) and \(\vee\) over carrier witnesses.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Lattice classifier specification]
\label{def:lattice-classifier-specification}
The classifier is the poset carrier classifier, with meet and join congruent in both arguments.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Lattice stability certificate]
\label{def:lattice-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item all poset stability fields
  \item meet lower-bound laws
  \item meet greatestness law
  \item join upper-bound laws
  \item join leastness law
  \item meet and join congruence
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Lattice ledger policy]
\label{def:lattice-ledger-policy}
Record operands and the bound-characterization certificate for each meet or join.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[\(\LatticeUp\) certificate obligations]
\label{def:lattice-certificate-obligations}
The carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data listed above are the obligations required for the interface name \(\LatticeUp\).
\end{definition}

\paragraph{Primitive boundary.}
\(\LatticeUp\) is not a BEDC primitive. It is admitted only when those obligations are supplied.
