\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\LatticeUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lattice-namecert}

The lattice interface \(\LatticeUp\) extends \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-poset-namecert} with binary meet \(\wedge\) and join \(\vee\) operations, each classified as the greatest lower bound and least upper bound of two elements under the partial order. Idempotence, commutativity, associativity, and absorption laws are derived from the bound characterizations under the carrier's classifier.

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[Lattice carrier]
\label{def:lattice-carrier}
Use a poset setup extended with meet and join operations carrying bound-characterization proofs.
\end{definition}

\section{Concrete singleton empty-history lattice instance}
\label{sec:lattice-concrete-singleton-instance}

\begin{definition}[Singleton empty-history lattice instance]
\label{def:singleton-empty-history-lattice-instance}
Let $A_{\emp}$ be the singleton history source of
Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source} at $a_0=\emp$. Define the
classifier
$$
\rho_{\emp}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow
A_{\emp}(h)\land A_{\emp}(k)\land\hsame(h,k).
$$
Define the order by $h\le_{\emp} k$ exactly when $\rho_{\emp}(h,k)$ holds.
Define the concrete lattice operations by
$$
\mathsf{meet}_{\emp}(h,k):=\emp,\qquad
\mathsf{join}_{\emp}(h,k):=\emp.
$$
This is a one-element $\mathsf{BHist}$ lattice carried by the empty-history
endpoint class.
The certificate is the ten-field fit with traditional target the one-point
lattice; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier
$\rho_{\emp}$; stability obligations for lower and upper bounds,
greatestness, leastness, meet and join congruence, and absorption; ledger
singleton operand provenance; core theorem
Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-lattice-laws}; exactness the
singleton endpoint classification; standard bridge the terminal one-point
lattice; and status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton empty-history lattice laws]
\label{thm:singleton-empty-history-lattice-laws}
The tuple
$(A_{\emp},\rho_{\emp},\le_{\emp},\mathsf{meet}_{\emp},\mathsf{join}_{\emp})$
satisfies carrier inhabitance, classifier stability, preorder reflexivity and
transitivity, antisymmetry, meet lower-bound laws, meet greatestness, join
upper-bound laws, join leastness, idempotence, commutativity, associativity,
absorption, and meet and join congruence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier inhabitance is the reflexive endpoint witness $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.
By Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source} at $a_0=\emp$, this is
exactly the witness that $\emp$ lies in $A_{\emp}$.

Classifier stability follows from the definition of $\rho_{\emp}$. Each related
pair already carries the two endpoint witnesses and the displayed $\hsame$
component, so reflexivity, symmetry where needed for repackaging, and
transitivity compose only singleton empty-history endpoints.

Reflexivity of $\le_{\emp}$ is the classifier witness
$\rho_{\emp}(h,h)$ obtained from the carrier witness $A_{\emp}(h)$ and
$\hsame(h,h)$. Transitivity of $\le_{\emp}$ is transitivity of $\hsame$ together
with the retained carrier witnesses from the two order premises.

Antisymmetry is immediate under the classifier: the two order premises already
are $\rho_{\emp}(h,k)$ and $\rho_{\emp}(k,h)$. Either one supplies the required
classified equality between the endpoints.

For meet, $\mathsf{meet}_{\emp}(h,k)$ is definitionally $\emp$. The two
lower-bound clauses are therefore the singleton carrier witness for $\emp$,
the input carrier witnesses for $h$ and $k$, and the endpoint identifications
provided by the singleton source.

For meet greatestness, let $l$ be any carried lower witness below both $h$ and
$k$. Since $A_{\emp}(l)$ identifies $l$ with $\emp$, and the meet endpoint is
definitionally $\emp$, the required order $l\le_{\emp}\mathsf{meet}_{\emp}(h,k)$
is exactly a singleton classifier package.

The join upper-bound clauses are dual in the singleton carrier. The join
endpoint is definitionally $\emp$, and the input carrier facts identify $h$ and
$k$ with that same endpoint, so both upper-bound relations are supplied by
$\rho_{\emp}$.

For join leastness, any carried upper witness $u$ is already classified with
$\emp$ by $A_{\emp}(u)$. Since $\mathsf{join}_{\emp}(h,k)$ is $\emp$, the
relation $\mathsf{join}_{\emp}(h,k)\le_{\emp}u$ reduces to the same singleton
classifier package.

Idempotence, commutativity, associativity, and absorption all compare constant
endpoints. After unfolding the operations, both sides are $\emp$, so the
classifier is assembled from two copies of $A_{\emp}(\emp)$ and
$\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.

Meet and join congruence are constant-operation congruences. Both outputs are
$\emp$, so the source and target endpoints have carrier witness
$A_{\emp}(\emp)$ and relation witness $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.
\end{proof}

The $\LatticeUp$ schema is instantiated by a concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier
and concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$-valued order, meet, and join operations, giving
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.LatticeUp}$ a $\mathsf{BHist}$-anchored formal target.

\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[Lattice source specification]
\label{def:lattice-source-specification}
The source is a certified poset carrier plus binary meet and join data.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Lattice pattern specification]
\label{def:lattice-pattern-specification}
The pattern is expression formation by \(\wedge\) and \(\vee\) over carrier witnesses.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Lattice classifier specification]
\label{def:lattice-classifier-specification}
The classifier is the poset carrier classifier, with meet and join congruent in both arguments.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Lattice stability certificate]
\label{def:lattice-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item all poset stability fields
  \item meet lower-bound laws
  \item meet greatestness law
  \item join upper-bound laws
  \item join leastness law
  \item meet and join congruence
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Lattice ledger policy]
\label{def:lattice-ledger-policy}
Record operands and the bound-characterization certificate for each meet or join.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[\(\LatticeUp\) certificate obligations]
\label{def:lattice-certificate-obligations}
The carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data listed above are the obligations required for the interface name \(\LatticeUp\).
\end{definition}

\paragraph{Primitive boundary.}
\(\LatticeUp\) is not a BEDC primitive. It is admitted only when those obligations are supplied.
