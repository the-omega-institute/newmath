\begin{definition}[Antipodal trigger ledger carrier]
\label{def:antipodal-trigger-ledger-carrier}
An $\AntipodalTriggerLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source row for $M^{\star}=\mathrm{NNR}\cup\mathrm{CUY}$, including the saturated $R$ halfspace and the $\mathrm{CUY}$ anchor row.
\item $Y$ is the third-position $Y$ halfspace shell ledger, displaying the shell decomposition $H_{0},H_{1},H_{2},H_{3},H_{4}$.
\item $G$ is the antipodal $\mathrm{AGY}$ row, displaying either $\mathrm{AGU}$ or $\mathrm{AGC}$ as the single trigger.
\item $T$ is the trigger ledger that pairs the $\mathrm{CUY}$ anchor, the $\mathrm{AGY}$ antipode, and the already present $R$-halfspace witness $t_{b}$ for each target $Y$-side codon $b$.
\item $M$ is the median-closure replay row, recording that the coordinatewise median of those three displayed witnesses reaches $b$ and hence fills the whole codon cube in one pass.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source, shell, antipode, trigger, median-closure, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from source presentation through the antipode trigger ledger into the median-closure row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\AntipodalTriggerLedgerUp$, $\DnaBoundaryTriggerClosureUp$, $\SixBoundaryTileCoverageUp$, $\WindowCoverageTriggerAlgebraUp$, $\AnchorSubcubeTriggerUp$, $\RadialWobbleSpectrumUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AntipodalTriggerLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier admits only packets carrying these finite rows and future implementations must provide the corresponding $\Nontrivial$ and $\FieldFaithful$ instances before a Lean-side field interpretation can be claimed.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Antipodal trigger ledger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:antipodal-trigger-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\AntipodalTriggerLedgerUp$ carrier $A=(S,Y,G,T,M,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\AntipodalTriggerLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item source admission for $M^{\star}=\mathrm{NNR}\cup\mathrm{CUY}$ and its median-closed halfspace anchor;
\item shell admission for the displayed $Y$-halfspace ledger and its antipodal $H_{4}=\mathrm{AGY}$ row;
\item trigger exactness, requiring the same $\mathrm{CUY}$ anchor, $\mathrm{AGY}$ antipode, and $R$-halfspace witness row to feed the median calculation;
\item one-pass median replay, requiring the row $M$ to expose every $Y$-side target before the full-cube claim is exported;
\item transport, replay, provenance, and naming stability through $H,C,P,N$;
\item field-read discipline, requiring future $\Nontrivial$ and $\FieldFaithful$ instances before any field-facing interpretation of the trigger ledger is exposed.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:antipodal-trigger-ledger-carrier}. The source row $S$ supplies the $R$ halfspace and the $\mathrm{CUY}$ anchor, while $Y$ supplies the finite shell ledger.

The trigger row $G$ is indispensable. Without a displayed $\mathrm{AGY}$ antipode, the packet contains no finite row pairing the anchor with the antipodal point required by \autoref{thm:philosophy-antipodal-single-trigger}.

The ledger $T$ then names the witness triple for each target $Y$-side codon $b$: the $\mathrm{CUY}$ anchor, the $\mathrm{AGY}$ antipode, and the already present $R$-halfspace point $t_{b}$. The replay row $M$ records the coordinatewise median calculation and exposes the one-pass full-cube result.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve this route under transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming. The field-read clause is only a discipline on later interpretation: until $\Nontrivial$ and $\FieldFaithful$ instances are displayed, the packet exports a finite median-trigger ledger rather than a field theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Antipodal trigger ledger median nonescape]
\label{thm:antipodal-trigger-ledger-median-nonescape}

$$

\mathrm{CUY}\text{ anchor}
\longmapsto
\mathrm{AGY}\text{ antipode}

\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier. The public full-cube read must first expose $S$, so the median source is exactly $M^{\star}$ with the $\mathrm{CUY}$ anchor and the already present $R$ halfspace.

Next the read consumes $Y$ and $G$. These rows place the $\mathrm{AGY}$ point in the antipodal shell $H_{4}$ and identify it as the single trigger rather than as an undifferentiated radial-shell element.

Now apply the trigger ledger $T$ and median replay $M$. For each target $Y$-side codon, the route supplies the anchor, antipode, and matching $R$-halfspace witness, so the coordinatewise median calculation remains inside displayed finite data.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ do not add another closure principle; they preserve the same finite route. Hence a consumer that omits the antipode, witness ledger, or median replay row cannot claim one-pass full-cube median closure from this packet.
\end{proof}
