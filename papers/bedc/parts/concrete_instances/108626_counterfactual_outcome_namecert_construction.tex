\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CounterfactualOutcomeUp$}

Following Pearl, Causality, 2009, the $\CounterfactualOutcomeUp$ packet records paired factual and counterfactual outcome reads as finite causal-inference NameCert data. It sits beside the intervention calculus of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-do-calculus-namecert}, the intervention-row packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-causal-intervention-namecert}, the probability-space carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-probspace-namecert}, the distribution handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-distribution-namecert}, and the Bayesian conditioning route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bayesian-namecert}. The packet gives causal consumers a selected counterfactual-outcome surface without weakening the explicit non-claim boundary of $\DoCalculusUp$.

\begin{definition}[Counterfactual outcome carrier]
\label{def:counterfactual-outcome-carrier}
A $\CounterfactualOutcomeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
C=(U,I,Y_f,Y_{cf},M,R,H,D,E,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the finite unit row, naming the observational unit and the displayed covariate cells opened by the counterfactual query.
\item $I$ is the intervention row inherited from the $\CausalInterventionUp$ route. It records the do-row, intervention target, and intervention value that may be inspected by the counterfactual read.
\item $Y_f$ is the factual outcome row observed for $U$ before the counterfactual branch is exported.
\item $Y_{cf}$ is the counterfactual outcome row selected under $I$ for the same unit row $U$.
\item $M$ is the modified-distribution row, read through the $\DoCalculusUp$, $\ProbSpaceUp$, $\DistributionUp$, and $\BayesianUp$ siblings before any expectation or probability export.
\item $R$ is the consistency row requiring factual and counterfactual reads to agree on the factual branch selected by the displayed intervention route.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for units, interventions, factual outcomes, counterfactual outcomes, modified distributions, consistency rows, distribution exports, expectation rows, replay rows, provenance rows, and local naming rows.
\item $D$ is the distribution handoff row. It exposes only the finite probability surface displayed by $M$ and does not store hidden-world measures.
\item $E$ is the expectation and export row for finite counterfactual queries.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
U,I,Y_f,Y_{cf},M,R \longmapsto D,E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CounterfactualOutcomeUp$, $\DoCalculusUp$, $\CausalInterventionUp$, $\ProbSpaceUp$, $\DistributionUp$, $\BayesianUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CounterfactualOutcomeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $U,I,Y_f,Y_{cf},M,R,H,D,E,T,P,N$. It has no coordinate for hidden structural equations, cross-world equality outside the displayed consistency row, selected counterfactual universes, host structural causal models, or ambient probability semantics.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Counterfactual outcome NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:counterfactual-outcome-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CounterfactualOutcomeUp$ carrier
$$
C=(U,I,Y_f,Y_{cf},M,R,H,D,E,T,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CounterfactualOutcomeUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite unit row, intervention row, factual outcome row, counterfactual outcome row, modified-distribution row, consistency row, transport, distribution handoff, expectation and export row, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item intervention anchoring, requiring every counterfactual read $Y_{cf}$ to cite the displayed $I$ row and the same unit row $U$ before any export through $D$ or $E$;
\item factual and counterfactual consistency, requiring $R$ to bind the factual branch of $Y_{cf}$ to the observed $Y_f$ without adding cross-world equality outside the carrier;
\item probability handoff, requiring $M,D,E$ to pass through the displayed $\ProbSpaceUp$, $\DistributionUp$, and $\BayesianUp$ routes before any finite probability or expectation read is available;
\item non-escape from hidden worlds, host structural causal models, and selected counterfactual universes, requiring $H,T,P,N$ to preserve only the displayed rows and to export no unlisted causal semantics.
\end{enumerate}
No counterfactual-outcome obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $C=(U,I,Y_f,Y_{cf},M,R,H,D,E,T,P,N)$. Carrier admission exposes exactly the finite unit, intervention, factual outcome, counterfactual outcome, modified-distribution, consistency, transport, distribution, export, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The counterfactual branch is anchored before it is exported. The row $I$ supplies the intervention target and value, while $U$ supplies the unit row shared by $Y_f$ and $Y_{cf}$.

Consistency is then read through $R$. This row identifies the factual branch of the counterfactual read with the observed factual outcome, but it does not create an unconstrained cross-world equality or a hidden structural equation.

The probability-facing rows are downstream. The modified distribution $M$, distribution handoff $D$, and expectation row $E$ are available only along the displayed probability, distribution, and Bayesian routes.

Finally $H,T,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite surface. Since no carrier coordinate stores hidden worlds, a host structural causal model, or a selected counterfactual universe, none can escape through the local NameCert.
\end{proof}
