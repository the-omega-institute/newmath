\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicCoverUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadiccover-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, finite covers over
located real data are useful when the finite list, the dyadic mesh, and the
coverage witnesses are carried as explicit data. The $\DyadicCoverUp$
interface packages this information as a finite BEDC certificate for Real and
Completion consumers. It depends on $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$,
$\RationalIntervalUp$, $\RationalBallUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\RealUp$, and $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$, and it does not invoke open-cover
compactness, excluded middle, or a choice of subcover from an arbitrary family.

\begin{definition}[DyadicCover carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-cover-carrier}
A $\DyadicCoverUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(K,R,I,Q,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the finite list of dyadic centers, each read through a
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row;
\item $R$ is the finite list of dyadic radii, each routed through a
$\RationalBallUp$ row with rational nonnegative radius data;
\item $I$ is the finite list of rational interval cells, each routed through a
$\RationalIntervalUp$ row and attached to the corresponding center-radius
entry;
\item $Q$ records the $\DyadicMeshUp$ mesh level and the finite refinement rows
between adjacent mesh levels;
\item $W$ is the displayed $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ window whose points are
covered by the interval cells;
\item $H$ records the $\hsame$ transports for centers, radii, interval cells,
mesh rows, window bounds, stream-name reads, regular-rational sequence reads,
and real rows;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read center membership,
radius bounds, interval containment, mesh refinement, coverage witnesses, and
total-bounded handoff rows;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance for $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicMeshUp$,
$\RationalIntervalUp$, $\RationalBallUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\RealUp$, $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and
$\NameCert$ dependency rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicCoverUp}$ provenance row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts exactly those packets whose finite cell list covers the
displayed finite window and whose refinement rows keep every later mesh cell
inside a named earlier interval or rational ball.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicCover NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-cover-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\DyadicCoverUp}$ seed surface consists of five finite
obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the packet of \autoref{def:dyadic-cover-carrier};
\item classifier: componentwise comparison of centers, radii, interval cells,
mesh rows, finite-window witnesses, transport rows, continuation routes,
package provenance, and local certificate rows;
\item refinement stability: displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport preserves
the dyadic center list, rational radii, interval cells, mesh refinement rows,
and coverage witnesses;
\item coverage exactness: every covered real read factors through a
$\StreamNameUp$ row, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ approximation row, a $\RealUp$ row, and a
finite interval or ball cell in the displayed window;
\item ledger policy: $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert$ expose only the listed dyadic,
rational, real, finite-window, transport, and continuation rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The finite center list, radius list, interval-cell list,
mesh rows, window witnesses, transport rows, continuation routes, package
provenance, and local certificate row are exactly the visible coordinates.

The classifier is componentwise over these rows. Transport stability follows
by moving each coordinate through the stored $\hsame$ rows and then reading it
through the corresponding $\Cont$ route.

Coverage exactness follows from the explicit witness attached to each point in
the displayed finite window: the witness names the stream read, regular
rational approximation, real row, and interval or ball cell used for that
point. The package and certificate rows contain no arbitrary open family or
subcover selector, so the ledger policy is exhausted by the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicCover finite refinement]
\label{thm:dyadic-cover-finite-refinement}
Let $D$ be an accepted $\DyadicCoverUp$ carrier and let $Q'$ be a displayed
later $\DyadicMeshUp$ refinement row of its mesh coordinate $Q$. The refined
packet is obtained by replacing each old cell with the finite list of named
dyadic subcells routed by $Q'$, transporting the corresponding rational
interval and rational ball rows through $\hsame$, and retaining the same
$\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ coverage witnesses after refinement.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the mesh coordinate $Q$ and the displayed refinement row $Q'$. Each old
dyadic cell has a finite named list of subcells, so the refined center, radius,
and interval lists are built by finite list replacement.

Transport each subcell's rational interval and rational ball rows through the
stored $\hsame$ entries, then read the containment route through $\Cont$. The
finite-window coordinate $W$ is unchanged, while each old coverage witness now
points to the named refined subcell contained in the old cell.

Repack the refined rows with the same $\Pkg$ provenance and local
$\NameCert_{\DyadicCoverUp}$ row. No infinite cover or selected subcover is
introduced, because every operation is over the displayed finite mesh lists.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicCover total-bounded handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-cover-total-bounded-handoff}
Every total-bounded or compact-metric consumer of an accepted
$\DyadicCoverUp$ carrier factors through the displayed finite list of dyadic
centers, rational radii, rational interval cells, $\DyadicMeshUp$ refinement
rows, $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ coverage witnesses, and the $\Cont$ routes
that read $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ data into those cells.
The handoff is a finite packet handoff, not an appeal to open-cover compactness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and choose a consumer route. The only data addressed to
total-bounded consumers are the finite center list, radius list, interval-cell
list, mesh refinement rows, and coverage witnesses over the displayed window.

Follow the real-data read route. A covered point is read through its
$\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows into a $\RealUp$ row, then into the
named rational interval or rational ball cell supplied by the coverage
witness.

Thus the consumer receives exactly a finite cover packet with explicit mesh
and radius data. Since the carrier contains no arbitrary indexed open family,
no compactness principle and no choice of subcover can be projected from it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicCover ledger nonescape]
\label{thm:dyadic-cover-ledger-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$, $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$, total-bounded, or completion
consumer of an accepted $\DyadicCoverUp$ carrier factors through the displayed
finite dyadic centers, dyadic radii, rational interval cells, mesh refinement
rows, coverage witnesses, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$
provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row.

No consumer can obtain open-cover compactness, a choice function for arbitrary
covers, a completeness theorem, a quotient of names, or host-level real
extensionality from the $\DyadicCoverUp$ ledger alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the obligation surface of
\autoref{thm:dyadic-cover-namecert-obligations}. It lists the carrier,
classifier, refinement stability, coverage exactness, and ledger rows
available to every local certificate consumer.

For a $\RealUp$ or $\FiniteWindowEnvelopeUp$ consumer, read the stream,
regular-rational, real, and finite-window rows that are already displayed in
the carrier. For a total-bounded or completion consumer, read the finite center
and radius lists together with the mesh refinement and coverage routes.

All of these routes stay inside the finite $\BHist$ packet. The excluded
conclusions require coordinates not present in
\autoref{def:dyadic-cover-carrier}, so they cannot escape from the local
ledger.
\end{proof}
