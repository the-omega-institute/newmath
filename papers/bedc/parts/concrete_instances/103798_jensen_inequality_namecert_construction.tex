\begin{definition}[Jensen inequality carrier]
\label{def:jensen-inequality-carrier}
A $\JensenInequalityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
J=(F,W,A,X,Y,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the convex-function source row, recording the displayed domain, codomain, convexity ledger, and function-read route.
\item $W$ is the finite affine-weight row, recording the rational coefficients, nonnegative coefficient checks, and finite-sum-to-one row read through $\RatUp$.
\item $A$ is the affine-spine row, recording the $\ConvexSetUp$ route that keeps the weighted point inside the displayed convex carrier.
\item $X$ is the finite input-family row, recording the finitely many points consumed by the affine spine and their $\VecSpaceUp$ source reads.
\item $Y$ is the convex-image row, recording the finite image reads $F(x_i)$ and the weighted image expression.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ comparison row, recording the displayed inequality between the function value at the affine point and the weighted image expression.
\item $Q$ is the $\RealMinMaxUp$ endpoint row, recording the comparison and bounding data used when a consumer reads the displayed inequality.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $F,W,A,X,Y,R,Q$ and the local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the source rows through the affine spine, comparison rows, endpoint row, provenance, and local name.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\JensenInequalityUp$, $\ConvexSetUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RatUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\RealMinMaxUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\JensenInequalityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $F,W,A,X,Y,R,Q,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host convex function, quotient equality of functions or real names, a selected supremum, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Jensen inequality NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:jensen-inequality-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $J=(F,W,A,X,Y,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$. The carrier definition exposes exactly the convex-function source, affine weights, affine spine, input family, image family, comparison, endpoint, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The coefficient and affine-spine rows are read before the inequality row. Thus $W$ supplies the nonnegative rational coefficients and finite sum row, while $A$ supplies the displayed $\ConvexSetUp$ route that keeps the affine point in the accepted carrier.

The image and comparison rows then record the Jensen surface. The rows $Y,R,Q$ carry the function images, real comparison, and endpoint comparison data, and $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name that route. Since the excluded host convex-function and quotient-equality objects are absent from the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Jensen inequality affine-spine route]
\label{thm:jensen-inequality-affine-spine-route}

$$
W,X \longmapsto A \longmapsto F,Y \longmapsto R,Q \longmapsto H,C,P,N .

\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer read of the inequality. By \autoref{def:jensen-inequality-carrier}, the only affine-combination data are the coefficient row $W$, input-family row $X$, and affine-spine row $A$.

The convex-function and image rows are downstream of that affine route. The row $F$ supplies the convexity ledger, and $Y$ supplies the displayed image family over the same finite weights.

Finally the comparison rows $R,Q$ expose the real inequality only after those source rows have been displayed. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route, so no consumer can bypass the affine spine by invoking an ambient convex-function theorem.
