\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ObserverRegularUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-observerregular-namecert}
\origin{ai}

Inspired by Omega Project: \texttt{automath/papers/publication/2026\_scan\_error\_prefix\_partitions\_convergence\_rates\_etds}; here re-expressed in the BEDC mathlib-free substrate. The $\ObserverRegularUp$ chapter packages finite prefix-observer data as a BEDC naming certificate for $\RealUp$ and $\CompletionUp$ window schedules. Its public surface is a finite observer ledger over displayed prefixes, resolving states, stream windows, regular-rational readbacks, tolerance rows, transports, continuation routes, provenance, and a local naming row.

The dependency anchors are $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, and $\CompletionUp$.

\begin{definition}[ObserverRegular prefix carrier]
\label{def:observerregular-prefix-carrier}
An $\ObserverRegularUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(A,R,S,W,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the finite alphabet and prefix row, listing the retained symbols, the displayed prefix packets, and the finite prefix-extension ledger inspected by the observer;
\item $R$ is the resolving-state row, listing the accepted observer states and the finite transition evidence by which a prefix reaches such a state;
\item $S$ is the schedule row, naming the $\RealUp$ and $\CompletionUp$ window schedules for which the observer packet is intended;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row exposed by each accepted resolving state;
\item $L$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback ledger, pairing each displayed window with the finite rational readback consumed by downstream schedules;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for alphabet rows, prefix rows, resolving-state rows, window rows, readback rows, tolerance rows, provenance, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes for prefix extension, resolving-state inspection, window display, regular-rational readback, tolerance checking, and the $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CompletionUp$, and the consumed local source certificates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverRegularUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets by first transporting them to a common displayed finite prefix row, then comparing the resolving-state rows, the $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, the $\CauchyModulusUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows, and finally the consumer handoff rows. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, ambient completeness, or choice-selected completion data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverRegular resolving-window soundness]
\label{thm:observerregular-resolving-window-soundness}
For every accepted $\ObserverRegularUp$ carrier $O=(A,R,S,W,L,H,C,P,N)$, each accepted observer state in $R$ determines a displayed finite $\StreamNameUp$ window in $W$ and a finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback in $L$. The readback is reached only through the finite prefix row $A$, the resolving-state row $R$, the displayed window row $W$, the stored $\hsame$ transports, and the displayed $\Cont$ routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:observerregular-prefix-carrier}. An accepted observer state is not a host-level predicate; it is one of the finite rows recorded in $R$ together with the displayed prefix evidence from $A$.

Use the continuation routes in $C$ to inspect that resolving row. The inspection selects a window entry in $W$, and the same displayed route selects the matching regular-rational readback entry in $L$.

The transport row $H$ supplies the componentwise equality witnesses needed when the same state is read after prefix transport. Since the only exposed coordinates are $A$, $R$, $W$, $L$, $H$, and $C$, the soundness claim remains inside the finite prefix-observer packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverRegular NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observerregular-namecert-obligations}
The seed $\NameCert_{\ObserverRegularUp}$ surface has five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation: finite alphabet rows, prefix rows, resolving-state rows, window rows, readback ledgers, tolerance rows, transports, continuations, provenance, and the local naming row inhabit the carrier of \autoref{def:observerregular-prefix-carrier};
\item classifier transport: accepted carriers are compared only after displayed transport to a common finite prefix row and common resolving-state inspection surface;
\item resolving-window soundness: every accepted observer state determines the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback of \autoref{thm:observerregular-resolving-window-soundness};
\item ledger exactness: the $\CauchyModulusUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows are read only through the displayed prefix, resolving-state, window, and readback ledgers;
\item $\RealUp$ non-escape: every $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer read factors through the finite observer ledger and never through quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, or ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is obtained by choosing finite displayed rows for the alphabet, prefixes, resolving states, windows, readbacks, tolerances, transports, continuations, provenance, and local name. These are exactly the coordinates listed in \autoref{def:observerregular-prefix-carrier}.

Classifier transport first moves packets to a common prefix row and then inspects the resolving-state rows through the same displayed continuations. The resolving-window obligation is precisely \autoref{thm:observerregular-resolving-window-soundness}.

The ledger exactness and real-name boundary obligations follow from the absence of any carrier coordinate outside the finite observer packet. The tolerance rows and consumer rows can only be reached by projecting the listed coordinates and applying the stored continuation routes.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverRegular finite ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:observerregular-finite-ledger-non-escape}
Every consumer-facing read of an accepted $\ObserverRegularUp$ carrier is exhausted by the finite alphabet and prefix row, resolving-state row, $\StreamNameUp$ window row, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback ledger, $\CauchyModulusUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row.

The ledger exports no quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected host limit, ambient completeness principle, or choice-selected completion witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a requested downstream read. The only entry point into the carrier is the displayed prefix row $A$, which selects the resolving-state row $R$ and then the window and readback rows $W$ and $L$.

The tolerance rows are reached by the same displayed readback route. The $\CauchyModulusUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ entries therefore certify only the finite observation already selected by the observer ledger.

Finally route the read through $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$. Those coordinates record transport, continuation, provenance, and naming data, not host-level equality, selected limits, or ambient completeness, so no non-displayed object can be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverRegular StreamName handoff]
\label{thm:observerregular-streamname-handoff}
The downstream handoff from an accepted $\ObserverRegularUp$ carrier to $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ consists exactly of the displayed window row, regular-rational readback ledger, tolerance rows, transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming row selected by an accepted resolving state.

For $\CompletionUp$ schedules, the same handoff remains finite: the completion consumer receives the $\StreamNameUp$ window and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback already exposed to the $\RealUp$ boundary, together with the displayed tolerance rows. No quotient stream, host sequence equality, selected limit, or ambient completeness coordinate is added.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:observerregular-resolving-window-soundness} to the accepted resolving state. It supplies the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window and matching $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback.

Apply \autoref{thm:observerregular-finite-ledger-non-escape}. The $\RealUp$ and $\CompletionUp$ consumers can inspect only the selected window, readback, tolerance, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.

Thus the handoff is exactly the finite packet described in the statement. Since the completion-facing schedule receives no coordinate beyond those rows, it cannot introduce quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
