\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulusmeet-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ interface packages the finite rows needed when two regular rational stream names must be read through one shared Cauchy bound. It is a naming certificate for the shared-modulus packet used by diagonal-limit and uniform Cauchy consumers; it does not assert that a quotient stream, a selected limit, or an ambient completeness principle has already been constructed.

\begin{definition}[CauchyModulusMeet carrier]\label{def:cauchy-modulus-meet-carrier}
A $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(S_0,S_1,\mu_0,\mu_1,\mu,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S_0$ and $S_1$ are the two $\StreamNameUp$ rows whose rational windows are read as $\RegSeqRatUp$ data;
\item $\mu_0$ and $\mu_1$ are the two source $\CauchyModulusUp$ rows attached to those regular rational names;
\item $\mu$ is the shared $\CauchyModulusUp$ meet row, displayed with a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger sufficient for both source rows;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the two stream names, two source moduli, shared meet modulus, dyadic tolerance rows, real-facing reads, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect stream windows, regular rational rows, source modulus rows, the shared meet bound, and the real-facing handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusMeetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only after transporting these displayed coordinates to a common stream-name, modulus, and dyadic tolerance surface. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyModulusMeet NameCert obligations]\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusMeetUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-meet-carrier} consists exactly of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite packet $M=(S_0,S_1,\mu_0,\mu_1,\mu,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier transport to a common displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ surface;
\item stability of both source modulus projections and of the shared meet row under componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item exactness of the shared dyadic tolerance ledger as a bound readable by both source modulus rows;
\item ledger rows for $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, $\RealUp$ handoff reads, and the local naming certificate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-meet-carrier}. The displayed coordinates provide the two source stream names, two source moduli, shared meet modulus, transport rows, continuation rows, provenance, and local name.

Classifier transport uses $H$ to move every coordinate to a common finite surface. Once transported, the routes in $C$ are the only ways to read stream windows, regular rational rows, source moduli, the shared meet bound, and the real-facing handoff.

The dyadic tolerance ledger is stored inside the shared row $\mu$ and its associated $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ read. Since no carrier coordinate stores quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, or an ambient completeness proof, the NameCert obligations are exhausted by the five displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyModulusMeet projection stability]\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-projection-stability}
Let $M=(S_0,S_1,\mu_0,\mu_1,\mu,H,C,P,N)$ and $M'=(S'_0,S'_1,\mu'_0,\mu'_1,\mu',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ carriers whose displayed coordinates are related componentwise by the transport rows in $H$ and $H'$. Then the projected source modulus reads $\mu_0,\mu_1$ and the shared meet read $\mu$ remain readable through the transported packet, and their $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger is the same displayed ledger seen by both $\RegSeqRatUp$ stream-name projections.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both accepted carriers and read the componentwise transport rows. The only modulus coordinates in either packet are the two source rows and the shared meet row.

Use the displayed $\hsame$ entries in $H$ and $H'$ to transport $S_0,S_1,\mu_0,\mu_1,\mu$ to the common surface. The continuation rows then read the transported stream windows and regular rational rows before reading the transported source moduli.

It remains to compare the shared bound. The dyadic tolerance ledger is part of the displayed meet row and is routed by $C$ rather than by an external real-completeness object. Hence each projected source modulus remains readable through the meet packet after transport, and both projections see the same displayed shared bound.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyModulusMeet Real handoff boundary]\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-real-handoff-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ diagonal consumer of an accepted $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ carrier may read the shared bound only through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ rows $S_0,S_1$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational readbacks of those rows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger carried by $\mu$, the componentwise $\hsame$ transports, the displayed $\Cont$ routes, the $\Pkg$ provenance row, and the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusMeetUp}$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a $\RealUp$ diagonal consumer route. The carrier has no real-facing coordinate other than the handoff reads reached through the stream-name, regular rational, and shared modulus routes.

The consumer first reads $S_0$ and $S_1$, then their regular rational windows, and then the shared dyadic tolerance ledger inside $\mu$. The two source moduli $\mu_0$ and $\mu_1$ are available only as displayed projections through the same finite packet.

All remaining movement is supplied by the rows $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, route, record provenance, and name the packet; they do not add quotient streams, selected limits, ambient completeness, or host real equality. Therefore the real-facing handoff is bounded by the displayed certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyModulusMeet swap stability]\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-swap-stability}
Let $M=(S_0,S_1,\mu_0,\mu_1,\mu,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ carrier, and let
$$
M^{\mathrm{sw}}=(S_1,S_0,\mu_1,\mu_0,\mu,H^{\mathrm{sw}},C^{\mathrm{sw}},P,N)
$$
be the packet obtained by exchanging the two displayed source stream/modulus rows and transporting every affected row componentwise by the $\hsame$ entries recorded in $H$. Then $M^{\mathrm{sw}}$ is accepted by the same carrier surface, and it presents the same shared meet ledger $\mu$ and the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows to every displayed $\Cont$ route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-meet-carrier}. The first two stream coordinates and the first two source modulus coordinates are the only ordered source-side entries of the carrier.

Exchange these two pairs and read the corresponding componentwise transport rows from $H$. The transported packet still has two $\StreamNameUp$ rows, two $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, two source $\CauchyModulusUp$ rows, one shared meet row, the same provenance row, and the same local name row.

The shared row $\mu$ is not duplicated or replaced by the swap. Its dyadic tolerance ledger remains the displayed ledger routed by $C$, while $H^{\mathrm{sw}}$ and $C^{\mathrm{sw}}$ only retarget the source-side reads. Hence the swapped packet is accepted and exposes the same shared meet surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyModulusMeet shared-bound exhaustion]\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-shared-bound-exhaustion}
For an accepted $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ carrier $M=(S_0,S_1,\mu_0,\mu_1,\mu,H,C,P,N)$, every readable shared bound exported by the packet is one of the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows carried by $\mu$. No exported shared-bound route can use quotient stream equality, a selected limit, host real equality, or an ambient completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an exported shared-bound route. By the carrier definition, the only bound coordinate common to the two source modulus rows is the meet row $\mu$ together with its displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger.

The continuation row $C$ reads source stream windows, regular rational rows, source modulus rows, the shared meet bound, and the real-facing handoff. The transport row $H$ only moves these displayed coordinates to a common surface.

There is no carrier coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, or ambient completeness. Therefore a readable shared bound must be a displayed tolerance row inside $\mu$, recorded through $H,C,P,N$ and nothing outside the finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyModulusMeet consumer factorization]\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-consumer-factorization}
Every $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer route out of an accepted $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ carrier factors through the ordered rows
$$
S_0,S_1,\mu_0,\mu_1,\mu,H,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, such a consumer first reads the displayed stream-name rows, the source modulus rows, and the shared meet row, and then uses only the componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusMeetUp}$ row supplied by the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer route be given. Project its source carrier to the tuple of \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-meet-carrier}.

For a $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer, the stream-name and rational readback surface is reached through $S_0$ and $S_1$, with source bounds supplied by $\mu_0,\mu_1$ and their common bound supplied by $\mu$. For a $\RealUp$ consumer, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-real-handoff-boundary} forces the real-facing read through the same rows before any handoff is visible.

The remaining route data are exactly $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, route, record provenance, and name the packet; they do not provide an independent consumer object. Thus every such route factors through the displayed ordered carrier rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyModulusMeet obligation surface]\label{thm:cauchymodulusmeet-obligation-surface}
The carrier, projection stability, swap stability, shared-bound exhaustion, $\RealUp$ handoff boundary, and consumer factorization of the $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ packet constitute the full obligation surface for meeting two displayed $\CauchyModulusUp$ rows over a shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger. Downstream $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ consumers may use only this finite surface.

The surface exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, ambient completeness datum, host real equality, or public standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-meet-carrier}. It lists the two $\StreamNameUp$ rows, their two source $\CauchyModulusUp$ rows, the shared meet row, the transport row, continuation row, provenance row, and local name.

Projection stability is supplied by \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-projection-stability}, and source-row exchange is supplied by \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-swap-stability}. Together these bind the two source moduli and their shared meet row under the permitted componentwise $\hsame$ transports.

The shared-bound and consumer-facing parts are then fixed by \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-shared-bound-exhaustion}, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-real-handoff-boundary}, and \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-consumer-factorization}. These three results force $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance reads, $\RegSeqRatUp$ route reads, and $\RealUp$ handoff reads through the displayed meet packet.

Finally the NameCert obligations in \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-meet-namecert-obligations} record the same rows as the local naming surface. Since none of the cited blocks introduces quotient stream equality, selected limits, ambient completeness, host real equality, or a standard bridge, the listed blocks are the full obligation surface.
\end{proof}
