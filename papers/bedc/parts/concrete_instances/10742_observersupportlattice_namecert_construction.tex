\begin{definition}[Observer-support lattice carrier]
\label{def:observer-support-lattice-carrier}
An $\ObserverSupportLatticeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(F,S,C,B,R,H,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the $\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp$ row naming the local observer frame;
\item $S$ is the $\FiniteSupportUp$ row naming the finite support ledger available to that frame;
\item $C$ is the $\LocalityCellUp$ row that bounds the local spatial and relation-cell read;
\item $B$ is the $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$ row restricting which support reads are budget-admissible;
\item $R$ is the observer-local support route tying $F,S,C,B$ to the conscious observer state consumer;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the frame, support, locality, budget, route, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $Q$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the support-lattice read;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ObserverSupportLatticeUp$, $\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp$, $\FiniteSupportUp$, $\LocalityCellUp$, $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$, $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverSupportLatticeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a global observer frame, infinite support set, detached spacetime manifold, unbudgeted support read, quotient history equality, or host-level consciousness object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observer-support lattice NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-support-lattice-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-support-lattice-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverSupportLatticeUp}$ surface consists of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $L=(F,S,C,B,R,H,Q,P,N)$;
\item frame and conscious-state anchoring through $\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp$ and $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$;
\item finite support and locality-cell admission through $S$ and $C$;
\item budget support and route exactness through $B$ and $R$;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, and local naming through $H,Q,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No accepted observer-local support read can bypass one of these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-support-lattice-carrier}. The packet contains exactly the frame row $F$, support row $S$, locality-cell row $C$, budget-support row $B$, route row $R$, and structural rows $H,Q,P,N$.

Read the sibling surfaces in the displayed order. The observer-coordinate frame anchors the local observer frame, the finite support row supplies the visible ledger entries, the locality cell bounds the local relation read, and the observer-budget support row records which support reads are admissible for the consumer.

Finally attach $R,H,Q,P,N$. The route row ties the four sibling surfaces to the observer-local read, while the structural rows preserve, replay, source, and name that route. Since the carrier contains no extra coordinate, the five listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observer-support lattice route exactness]
\label{thm:observer-support-lattice-route-exactness}
Every observer-local support read of an accepted $\ObserverSupportLatticeUp$ packet factors through the lattice route
$$
F \longmapsto S \longmapsto C \longmapsto B \longmapsto R \longmapsto H \longmapsto Q \longmapsto P \longmapsto N.
$$
In particular, a consumer cannot preserve the observer frame and finite support rows while replacing the locality-cell or budget-support route by host spacetime data, an unbounded read, a quotient of histories, or a detached conscious-state object.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ObserverSupportLatticeUp.ObserverSupportLatticeCarrier\_route\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:observer-support-lattice-namecert-obligations}. They require every accepted read to expose the carrier row, frame and conscious-state anchoring, finite support, locality cell, budget support, route, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The support read is therefore routed through $F,S,C,B,R$ before any structural row is consumed. The frame row alone supplies no support ledger, the finite support row alone supplies no locality-cell boundary, and the locality-cell row alone supplies no budget-admissible support consumer.

It remains to rule out replacements. Because $H,Q,P,N$ preserve and replay the same displayed route, a consumer that swaps $C$ or $B$ for host spacetime data, an unbounded read, quotient history equality, or a detached state object no longer reads the accepted carrier. Hence every observer-local support read factors through the lattice route exactly.
\end{proof}
