\begin{theorem}[Signed dyadic normal-form NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:signed-dyadic-normal-form-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SignedDyadicNormalFormUp$ carrier $S=(M,E,L,C,K,D,T,H,R,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SignedDyadicNormalFormUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the signed mantissa, positive unary exponent, denominator ledger, common scale, normalized comparison, DyadicRatCore provenance, completion handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item signed-mantissa exactness, requiring each mantissa read to pass through the displayed $\RatUp$ and $\IntUp$ rows before any dyadic comparison is exported;
\item denominator-ledger exactness, requiring every denominator and scale request to be discharged by $E$ and $L$ before the common-scale row is opened;
\item common-scale classifier stability, requiring $C$ and $K$ to be preserved componentwise under $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay;
\item completion-handoff nonescape, requiring the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ consumers in $T$ to read only the displayed normal-form route.
\end{enumerate}
No signed dyadic normal-form obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:signed-dyadic-normal-form-carrier}. The finite packet exposes exactly the signed mantissa, positive unary exponent, denominator ledger, common scale, normalized comparison, DyadicRatCore provenance, handoff, and structural rows.

The mantissa and denominator obligations are forced by the order of the displayed rows. A comparison cannot read the mantissa without passing through $M$, and it cannot read a denominator scale without the exponent row $E$ and ledger row $L$.

Common-scale stability is the role of $C,K,H,R$. The common-scale row fixes the scale, the normalized comparison row records the comparison at that scale, the transport row preserves those coordinates componentwise, and the replay row repeats the same route.

The downstream handoff is then only a reader of the normal form. Since $T$ is downstream of $M,E,L,C,K,D$ and the structural rows contain no excluded coordinate, the local NameCert surface exports no quotient rational equality, host integer normal form, quotient stream equality, host real equality, trichotomy, choice, ambient real completeness, Lean credential, or bridge datum.

\begin{theorem}[Signed dyadic common-scale canonicality]
\label{thm:signed-dyadic-normal-form-common-scale-canonicality}

$$
M,E,L \longmapsto C \longmapsto K \longmapsto D,H,R,P,N .
$$
Thus the comparison is canonical at the displayed common scale, and two accepted replays with componentwise $\hsame$ rows export the same normalized comparison surface without appealing to quotient equality or trichotomy.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an exported normalized comparison. Carrier admission places the only comparison coordinate at $K$, and $K$ is downstream of the common-scale row $C$.

The row $C$ is downstream of the signed mantissa, positive unary exponent, and denominator ledger rows. Hence the compared signed dyadics are first read through $M,E,L$, and the common comparison scale is part of the finite packet before $K$ can be inspected.

Componentwise transport through $H$ preserves $M,E,L,C,K$ as displayed rows, while replay through $R$ repeats the same access path. Provenance and local naming through $P,N$ source and name that route but do not add a second comparison rule.

Therefore a downstream reader receives the normalized comparison at the displayed common scale. The conclusion is canonical for this finite carrier route and uses neither quotient equality nor a trichotomy principle.
\end{proof}
