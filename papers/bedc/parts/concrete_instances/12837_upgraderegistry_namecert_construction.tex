\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UpgradeRegistryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-upgraderegistry-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\UpgradeRegistryUp$ packet realizes the upgrade-registry table of \autoref{subsec:philosophy-upgrade-registry-table} as a finite NameCert surface for promotion claims. It sits beside the claim-entry discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-claimregistrylayer-namecert}, the public export gate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registry-export-surface-namecert}, and the refusal axis of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-axiszeckendorfcannotclaim-namecert}. Those siblings admit a claim row, expose registry export, and block overstrong readings; this packet binds the current-status, next-status, blocker, formal-target, provenance, transport, and local naming rows before any promotion may be read as accepted.

\begin{definition}[UpgradeRegistry carrier]
\label{def:upgrade-registry-carrier}
An $\UpgradeRegistryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(T,S,Nx,F,B,A,R,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the target row naming the chapter, package, theorem site, registry family, or bridge surface whose status may be promoted;
\item $S$ is the current-status row, recording the theory grade, verification grade, and bridge-status grade presently exported by the target;
\item $Nx$ is the next-status row, naming the immediate stronger status that may be accepted only after the required rows are discharged;
\item $F$ is the required-field row, listing the carrier, classifier, stability, exactness, ledger, bridge, or proof fields that must become public packet data;
\item $B$ is the blocker row, recording the open gap, refused reading, missing proof, missing bridge, or unresolved dependency that prevents immediate completion export;
\item $A$ is the audit row, recording the registry-consistency, writing-discipline, and cannot-claim checks consumed before the promotion is exported;
\item $R$ is the formal-target row, naming the Lean declaration, statement-only target, bridge obligation, or absence of such a target as public registry data;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the target, current-status, next-status, field, blocker, audit, formal-target, continuation, package, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes by which claim consumers, export consumers, and refusal consumers read the same promotion packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$, $\AxisZeckendorfCannotClaimUp$, $\AuditWritingDisciplineUp$, $\RegistryConsistencyAuditUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local certificate data;
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UpgradeRegistryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier for two accepted upgrade-registry packets compares only the displayed target, current-status, next-status, required-field, blocker, audit, formal-target, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UpgradeRegistry NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:upgrade-registry-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:upgrade-registry-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\UpgradeRegistryUp}$ surface consists of six finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure: every accepted registry promotion packet exposes exactly $U=(T,S,Nx,F,B,A,R,H,C,P,L)$;
\item status locality: public comparison reads the current-status row $S$ and next-status row $Nx$ before any promotion claim is exported;
\item blocker accountability: every accepted blocker row $B$ remains visible to the claim registry and cannot-claim registry until the required-field row $F$ is discharged;
\item formal-target exposure: the formal-target row $R$ is public packet data and cannot be replaced by prose expectation or hidden tooling state;
\item audit preservation: registry-consistency and writing-discipline checks are carried through the audit row $A$ and the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$;
\item naming locality: componentwise $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance preserve the same promotion packet for all public consumers.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:upgrade-registry-carrier}. The tuple displays the target, status rows, required fields, blockers, audit route, formal target, transports, continuations, package provenance, and local naming row.

Status locality and blocker accountability follow from the classifier. Since $S,Nx,F,B,A,R,H,C,P,L$ are the only compared rows, a consumer cannot read a promotion without also reading the current status and the blocker surface that prevents completion export.

Formal-target exposure and audit preservation are the $R,A,C$ coordinates. The claim layer admits the row only through the public registry packet, while the export surface and cannot-claim sibling read the same displayed continuation routes. Componentwise transport and package provenance then repack the rows as the local NameCert packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UpgradeRegistry status preservation]
\label{thm:upgrade-registry-status-preservation}
If an accepted $\UpgradeRegistryUp$ packet has current-status row $S$, next-status row $Nx$, required-field row $F$, blocker row $B$, audit row $A$, and formal-target row $R$, then every public export route preserves the distinction between current status and next status. A promotion claim may be exported only through the displayed route $S,Nx,F,B,A,R,H,C,P,L$; it cannot be exported from the next-status row alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:upgrade-registry-namecert-obligations}. The status rows are adjacent coordinates in the finite carrier, and the NameCert obligations force both to remain visible to the public comparison.

The blocker and required-field rows are also displayed. Thus a route that attempts to read $Nx$ as already accepted must also expose $F$ and $B$, where the unmet field or blocker remains part of the same packet.

Finally, the export route factors through $A,R,H,C,P,L$. These rows preserve audit, formal-target, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming data, so no consumer route can erase the current-status row while retaining the promotion claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UpgradeRegistry noncompletion surface]
\label{thm:upgrade-registry-noncompletion-surface}
An accepted $\UpgradeRegistryUp$ packet with no current-status row or no blocker row cannot export a completion claim. The packet may still expose a target row, next-status row, required-field row, audit row, or formal-target row, but those rows do not constitute a completed promotion without the displayed current-status and blocker accounting.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier definition. A valid packet exposes $S$ and $B$ as coordinates, so a route without either coordinate is not the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:upgrade-registry-carrier}.

Use status preservation from \autoref{thm:upgrade-registry-status-preservation}. Completion export requires a route that preserves the distinction between current status and next status, and that route also carries the blocker surface. Without $S$ or $B$, the route may at most display a target, desired status, field list, audit row, or formal target; it cannot display the complete promotion packet.
\end{proof}
