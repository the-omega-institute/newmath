sibling of the subject-reduction boundary packets

packet separates the residual substitution boundary for MetaCIC confluence.

\begin{definition}[MetaCIC confluence audit packet carrier]
\label{def:metacic-confluence-audit-packet-carrier}
A $\MetaCICConfluenceAuditPacketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(D,C,N,A_0,S,R,H,T,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the conditional $\mathsf{BetaParallel}$ diamond row, naming the
substitution-compatibility input and the parallel diamond obtained from it;
\item $C$ is the closed-diamond-to-star-confluence row, naming the route from a
closed parallel diamond to closed $\mathsf{BetaStarStep}$ confluence;
\item $N$ records the unconditional closed-normal confluence fragment;
\item $A_0$ records the atom, sort, closed-variable-impossible, and
non-lambda-application shape fragments that do not consume the full
substitution input;
\item $S$ is the substitution-compatibility boundary row, stating that the
packet names the input and the obstruction without proving the input;
\item $R$ is the subject-reduction separation row, linking the confluence
boundary to the sibling subject-reduction packets without merging the two
interfaces;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the displayed rows;
\item $T$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the
conditional bridges, the unconditional fragments, and the non-claim boundary;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over MetaCIC reduction, $\mathsf{BetaParallel}$,
$\mathsf{BetaStarStep}$, closedness, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and
$\NameCert$;
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICConfluenceAuditPacketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier relates two carriers exactly when their ten displayed
rows compare componentwise through $H$ and are replayed through the routes in
$T$. The carrier contains no coordinate proving
$\mathsf{BetaParallelSubstitutionCompatibility}$, no coordinate proving
$\mathsf{BetaParallelDiamondClosed}$, and no coordinate for arbitrary
closed-term Church--Rosser.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence audit packet obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-audit-packet-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-confluence-audit-packet-carrier}, the
local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICConfluenceAuditPacketUp}$ surface consists exactly of
carrier admission for $A=(D,C,N,A_0,S,R,H,T,P,L)$, conditional parallel-diamond
exposure, closed-diamond-to-star-confluence exposure, closed-normal and atom
fragment exposure, substitution-boundary exposure, subject-reduction separation,
componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ route replay, $\Pkg$
provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through
\autoref{def:metacic-confluence-audit-packet-carrier}. The rows $D$ and $C$
give the conditional bridge surface: $D$ exposes the substitution-sensitive
parallel diamond, and $C$ exposes the route from a closed parallel diamond to
closed star confluence.

The rows $N$ and $A_0$ give the unconditional fragment surface. They record
closed-normal confluence and the atom or impossible-source cases without adding
the full substitution-compatibility row as a proved theorem.

The boundary rows $S$ and $R$ then fix the negative reading. The first names the
substitution compatibility input as a boundary, and the second keeps the
confluence audit separate from the sibling subject-reduction discharge packets.
Transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming are exactly the rows
$H,T,P,L$, so the displayed coordinates exhaust the obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence audit packet conditional boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-audit-packet-conditional-boundary}
An accepted $\MetaCICConfluenceAuditPacketUp$ carrier may be cited for the
conditional route from substitution compatibility to a parallel diamond and for
the conditional route from a closed parallel diamond to closed
$\mathsf{BetaStarStep}$ confluence. It may not be cited as a proof of
$\mathsf{BetaParallelSubstitutionCompatibility}$, as a proof of
$\mathsf{BetaParallelDiamondClosed}$, or as arbitrary closed-term
Church--Rosser.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the first two rows of the carrier. The row $D$ records a bridge that
consumes the substitution-compatibility input, and the row $C$ records a bridge
that consumes the closed parallel-diamond input.

The row $S$ is the explicit substitution-boundary row. It states that the input
is named and routed, not generated by the packet.

Since the carrier has no coordinate whose projection is the input itself, no
consumer can turn the displayed bridges into unconditional endpoints. The
accepted readings are therefore conditional bridge citations and the listed
unconditional fragments, not the full confluence claims.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence audit packet closed substitution boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-audit-packet-closed-substitution-boundary}
An accepted $\MetaCICConfluenceAuditPacketUp$ carrier may expose
$\mathsf{BetaParallelClosedSubstitutionCompatibility}$ only as a closed-face
boundary whose inputs include $\mathsf{ClosedAt}\ 0\ a$ and
$\mathsf{ClosedAt}\ 1\ b$. The unclosed substitution obstruction remains
outside that face, so the packet may not cite the obstruction as a
closed-diamond proof or as a discharge of the closed Church--Rosser
substitution input.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the row $S$. It records substitution compatibility as a named boundary,
and \autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-audit-packet-conditional-boundary} already
states that the generic substitution input is not produced by the packet.

The closed face is narrower than the generic input. Its stated compatibility
requires the closedness hypotheses $\mathsf{ClosedAt}\ 0\ a$ and
$\mathsf{ClosedAt}\ 1\ b$, while the Lean-side theorem
\texttt{betaParallel\_closed\_substitution\_compatibility\_obstruction} records
the obstruction that prevents the unclosed path from supplying those closed
inputs.

Therefore a downstream closed Church--Rosser consumer still needs an explicit
closed compatibility row. The audit packet can route the boundary and can name
the obstruction, but it cannot convert the obstruction into the closed
substitution premise demanded by the closed diamond route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence audit packet subject-reduction separation]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-audit-packet-subject-reduction-separation}
The subject-reduction separation row $R$ of an accepted
$\MetaCICConfluenceAuditPacketUp$ carrier permits downstream chapters to cite
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subject-reduction-discharge-socket-namecert},
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-trace-namecert}, and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-discharge-obligation-ledger-namecert}
as neighboring MetaCIC audit packets. It does not identify the confluence
substitution boundary with the four subject-reduction discharge obligations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the row $R$. Its job is referential: it names the sibling
subject-reduction packets as separate finite surfaces available to downstream
MetaCIC audits.

Now project the confluence rows $D,C,N,A_0,S$. These rows concern parallel
reduction, closed star confluence, closed-normal and atom fragments, and the
residual substitution boundary. They do not contain beta, application-argument,
lambda-domain, or Pi-domain typing-discharge coordinates.

Thus the packet records a horizontal dependency rather than a fusion of
interfaces. A downstream consumer may cite both families, but each obligation
must be discharged on its own carrier surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence audit packet non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-audit-packet-nonescape}
Every consumer read of an accepted $\MetaCICConfluenceAuditPacketUp$ carrier
factors through the finite rows
$$
D,C,N,A_0,S,R,H,T,P,L.
$$
The packet exports no hidden confluence theorem, no unrecorded substitution
lemma, no host quotient over reductions, and no conversion from conditional
bridges into an unconditional closed Church--Rosser result.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. A consumer can project only the named rows
$D,C,N,A_0,S,R,H,T,P,L$.

The positive rows expose conditional bridges and unconditional fragments. The
transport and route rows explain how those entries are read componentwise, and
the provenance and local naming rows identify the packet boundary.

The substitution boundary row blocks the only possible escape from the
conditional bridge to a full endpoint. Because no further coordinate exists,
there is no hidden confluence theorem, unrecorded substitution lemma, host
quotient over reductions, or conversion into arbitrary closed Church--Rosser.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence audit candidate-mediated strong normalization]
\label{thm:metacic-confluence-audit-candidate-mediated-sn}

attached to $S$ and replayed through $T$. The packet may record that such a

name $L$. It may not convert the candidate witness into an unconditional proof
of $\mathsf{BetaParallelSubstitutionCompatibility}$, arbitrary strong
normalization, or closed Church--Rosser.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

names the obstruction, so any candidate-mediated witness relevant to the

Next replay the candidate row through $T$ and transport it through $H$. The

\autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-audit-packet-closed-substitution-boundary}, but
it remains a row of the same finite packet with provenance $P$ and local name
$L$.

The conditional-boundary theorem then prevents the final step from becoming
unconditional. Since $A$ has no coordinate for full substitution compatibility,
arbitrary strong normalization, or closed Church--Rosser, the candidate witness
is an audit row and not an exported MetaCIC theorem.
\end{proof}
