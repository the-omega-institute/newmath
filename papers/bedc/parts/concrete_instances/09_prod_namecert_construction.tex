\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\ProdUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-prod-namecert}

The product interface $\ProdUp$ is licensed over two source carriers each endowed with a $\NameCert$. Its explicit typed classifier is the conjunction of the component classifiers on the two coordinates. Its checked history-envelope classifier records product-carrier witnesses at both endpoints together with endpoint $\hsame$. It is not a primitive cartesian product; it is licensed only as a derived constructor over the two sources.

\section{Roadmap template status}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Traditional target}: ordered product data with one left component and one right component.
  \item \textbf{BEDC source}: two licensed source carriers $A$ and $B$, each with its own name certificate.
  \item \textbf{Carrier choice}: the paired history carrier records a left history, a right history, and the continuation that combines them.
  \item \textbf{Classifier choice}: $\mathsf{ProdClassifierSpec}$ is the typed componentwise classifier; $\mathsf{ProdHistoryClassifier}$ is the checked history-envelope classifier consisting of two $\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}$ endpoint witnesses and endpoint $\hsame$.
  \item \textbf{Stability obligations}: component transport, continuation transport, unary closure from components, history-envelope classifier symmetry and transitivity, typed classifier symmetry and transitivity, and typed coordinate inversion.
  \item \textbf{Ledger}: no product quotient is used; pairing records both component witnesses and their generated continuation.
  \item \textbf{Core theorem list}: append introduction, continuation introduction, carrier transport, unary closure, classifier equivalence, and the semantic name certificate.
  \item \textbf{Exactness predicate}: typed classification holds exactly when both coordinates classify in their respective sources; history-envelope classification holds when both endpoints lie in $\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R)$ and are related by endpoint $\hsame$.
  \item \textbf{Standard bridge}: the licensed pair reads back as the ordinary ordered pair $(a,b)$.
  \item \textbf{Status}: the product-history semantic envelope is checked in Lean; the explicit ordered-product interface uses the typed componentwise classifier and the projection fields checked around $\mathsf{prodClassifier\_iff}$.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[Prod carrier]
\label{def:prod-carrier}
For licensed source carriers \(A\) and \(B\), define a pair carrier with constructor \(\mathsf{pair}(a,b)\).
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdCarrier}

\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[Prod source specification]
\label{def:prod-source-specification}
The source is the ordered product of the two source certificates.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryCarrier}

\begin{theorem}[Product history carrier append introduction]
\label{thm:prod-history-carrier-append-intro}
If \(l\) lies in the left source carrier and \(r\) lies in the right source
carrier, then \(\mathsf{append}(l,r)\) lies in the product history carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \(l\) and \(r\) as the two component witnesses. The BEDC continuation
judgment is witnessed by reflexivity of the defining append result, so the
product history carrier is rebuilt from the two source proofs and that
continuation witness.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryCarrier\_append\_intro}

\begin{theorem}[Product history carrier continuation introduction]
\label{thm:prod-history-carrier-cont-intro}
If \(l\) lies in the left source carrier, \(r\) lies in the right source
carrier, and \(\Cont(l,r,h)\), then \(h\) lies in the product history carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \(l\) and \(r\) as the component witnesses. The supplied continuation
proof is exactly the result witness required by the product history carrier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryCarrier\_cont\_intro}

\begin{theorem}[Product unary history closure]
\label{thm:prod-history-carrier-unary}
If a product history is generated from unary left and right histories, then the
resulting product history is unary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Invert the product-history carrier into its left history, right history, unary
component witnesses, and continuation proof. Unary closure under continuation
turns the two unary component witnesses and the stored continuation proof into
unary membership of the result history.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryCarrier\_unary}

\begin{theorem}[Product history carrier transport]
\label{thm:prod-history-carrier-hsame-transport}
If two product histories are internally the same and the first is generated from a left witness, a right witness, and a continuation proof, then the second is generated from the same component witnesses and the transported continuation proof.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Invert the product-history carrier into its left history, right history, component witnesses, and continuation proof. Transport the continuation result across \(\hsame\), and rebuild the product-history carrier with the same component witnesses.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryCarrier\_hsame\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Product history carrier unary from components]
\label{thm:prod-history-carrier-unary-of-components}
If the left and right source predicates each determine unary histories, then
every product-history carrier generated from those predicates is unary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the product-history carrier into component histories \(l\) and \(r\),
their source witnesses, and the BEDC continuation witness for the result.
The component assumptions give unary witnesses for \(l\) and \(r\), and BEDC
unary continuation closure gives unary membership of the product result.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryCarrier\_unary\_of\_components}

\begin{definition}[Prod pattern specification]
\label{def:prod-pattern-specification}
The pattern is paired generation from one \(A\)-witness and one \(B\)-witness.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Prod classifier specification]
\label{def:prod-classifier-specification}
The typed classifier $\mathsf{ProdClassifierSpec}$ relates pairs componentwise using the classifiers of $A$ and $B$. The typed wrapper $\mathsf{ProdClassifier}$ has the same componentwise content. Separately, the checked history-level scope is $\mathsf{ProdHistoryClassifier}(L,R)$: it relates histories $h$ and $k$ exactly when $h$ lies in $\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R)$, $k$ lies in $\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R)$, and $\hsame(h,k)$ holds.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdClassifierSpec}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryClassifier}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.prodClassifier\_iff}

\begin{theorem}[Product history classifier right transport]
\label{thm:prod-history-classifier-right-hsame-transport}
If a product-history classifier relates $h$ to $k$, and $k$ is internally the same as $k'$, then the classifier relates $h$ to $k'$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Invert the product-history classifier into the left carrier, the right carrier, and the stored $\hsame(h,k)$ witness. Transport the right carrier across $\hsame(k,k')$, then compose $\hsame(h,k)$ with $\hsame(k,k')$ for the classifier witness.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryClassifier\_right\_hsame\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Product history classifier endpoint transport]
\label{thm:prod-history-classifier-hsame-transport}
If a product-history classifier relates $h$ to $k$, and $h$ is internally the same as $h'$ while $k$ is internally the same as $k'$, then the classifier relates $h'$ to $k'$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Invert the product-history classifier into endpoint carriers and the stored $\hsame(h,k)$ witness. Transport both endpoint carriers across their respective $\hsame$ witnesses, then compose symmetry of $\hsame(h,h')$, the stored link, and $\hsame(k,k')$ to obtain $\hsame(h',k')$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryClassifier\_hsame\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Product history classifier symmetry]
\label{thm:prod-history-classifier-symmetry}
If a product-history classifier relates \(h\) to \(k\), then it relates
\(k\) to \(h\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the classifier into the two endpoint product carriers and the stored
\(\hsame(h,k)\) link. Exchange the endpoint carriers and reverse the stored
history-sameness link by symmetry.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryClassifier\_symm}

\begin{theorem}[Product history classifier left transport]
\label{thm:prod-history-classifier-left-hsame-transport}
If a product-history classifier relates $h$ to $k$, and $h$ is internally the
same as $h'$, then the classifier relates $h'$ to $k$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Invert the classifier witness. Transport the left carrier through the
$\hsame(h,h')$ witness, keep the right carrier, and compose the symmetric
$\hsame(h',h)$ link with the stored $\hsame(h,k)$ classifier witness.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryClassifier\_left\_hsame\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Product history classifier transitivity]
\label{thm:prod-history-classifier-transitivity}
For source-history predicates $L$ and $R$ on $\mathsf{BHist}$, if $\mathsf{ProdHistoryClassifier}(L,R,h,k)$ and $\mathsf{ProdHistoryClassifier}(L,R,k,u)$, then $\mathsf{ProdHistoryClassifier}(L,R,h,u)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack both classifier witnesses. Keep the left carrier from the first witness and the right carrier from the second witness, then compose the stored $\hsame(h,k)$ and $\hsame(k,u)$ witnesses by transitivity to obtain the classifier link from $h$ to $u$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryClassifier\_trans}

\begin{definition}[Prod stability certificate]
\label{def:prod-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item componentwise reflexivity
  \item componentwise transitivity
  \item closure under pair construction
  \item projection stability for first and second components
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.prod\_stability\_certificate\_fields}

\begin{theorem}[Product classifier transitivity at the typed level]
\label{thm:prod-classifier-transitivity}
Let $R_A : A \to A \to \mathsf{Prop}$ and
$R_B : B \to B \to \mathsf{Prop}$ be source classifier relations.  If both
$R_A$ and $R_B$ are transitive, then for product carriers $x$, $y$, and $z$,
$$
  \mathsf{ProdClassifierSpec}(R_A,R_B,x,y)
  \quad\text{and}\quad
  \mathsf{ProdClassifierSpec}(R_A,R_B,y,z)
$$
imply $\mathsf{ProdClassifierSpec}(R_A,R_B,x,z)$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdClassifierSpec\_trans}
\begin{proof}
Invert the product classifier witnesses by their constructor. The first
components compose by transitivity of $R_A$, the second components compose by
transitivity of $R_B$, and the paired typed witness rebuilds the product
classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[History product classifier is symmetric]
\label{thm:prod-classifier-hsame-symm}
For pairs of finite-kernel histories, if the product classifier relates \(x\) to \(y\) componentwise by \(\hsame\), then it relates \(y\) to \(x\).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Invert the componentwise product classifier. Each component witness reverses by symmetry of \(\hsame\), and the reversed witnesses rebuild the product classifier in the opposite direction.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdClassifierSpec\_hsame\_symm}

\begin{definition}[Prod ledger policy]
\label{def:prod-ledger-policy}
Record a raw product history carrying both component ledgers together with an
\(\hsame\) link to the visible history exposed by the ledger.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryLedgerPolicy}

\begin{theorem}[Prod ledger visible carrier]
\label{thm:prod-ledger-visible-carrier}
If a visible history is linked by \(\hsame\) to a raw product-ledger history,
then the visible history is itself a product carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the raw product carrier and transport it across the stored \(\hsame\)
link by product-carrier transport.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryLedgerPolicy\_visible\_carrier}

\begin{theorem}[Prod ledger raw-visible classifier]
\label{thm:prod-ledger-raw-visible-classifier}
If $\mathsf{ProdHistoryLedgerPolicy}(L,R,\rho,v)$ holds, then
$\mathsf{ProdHistoryClassifier}(L,R,\rho,v)$ holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the ledger into the raw product carrier for $\rho$ and the stored
$\hsame(\rho,v)$ link. The visible-carrier theorem supplies the product
carrier for $v$. These three witnesses are exactly the product classifier
fields for the raw ledger history and the visible history.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.ProdHistoryLedgerPolicy\_raw\_visible\_classifier}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete \(\ProdUp\) naming certificate]
\label{thm:concrete-prod-namecert}
For source-history predicates $L$ and $R$ on $\mathsf{BHist}$, assume there
are carrier witnesses $l$ and $r$ with $L(l)$ and $R(r)$. Equivalently, the
witnesses may be supplied by source certificates whose carrier-inhabited
fields provide them. Then the checked certificate is for
$\mathsf{ProdHistoryCarrier}(L,R)$ and the history-envelope classifier
$\mathsf{ProdHistoryClassifier}(L,R)$ under these explicit left and right
carrier witnesses. The explicit ordered-product interface additionally uses
the typed componentwise classifier and projection fields.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Take the left and right witnesses from the two source certificates, or from
the explicit carrier-witness assumptions. BEDC continuation joins them into a
product history. The history-envelope classifier stores the two endpoint carrier
witnesses and an $\hsame$ witness between product histories, so the certificate fields follow
by $\hsame$ reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and carrier transport.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProdUp.prod\_history\_semantic\_name\_certificate}

\begin{corollary}[\(\ProdUp\) is licensed, not primitive]
\label{cor:prod-licensed-not-primitive}
\(\ProdUp\) is licensed as a derived binary constructor over two sources.
\end{corollary}
