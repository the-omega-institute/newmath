It consumes the compact metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the continuous-map pointwise rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the rational lower-bound machinery of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitenetminimumfold-namecert}, the uniform-modulus consumer row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}, the rational carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, and the ProbeBundle finite-net selectors of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactnetmodulusselector-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Compact uniform modulus extractor carrier]
\label{def:compact-uniform-modulus-extractor-carrier}
A $\CompactUniformModulusExtractorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(K,F,T,P,L,M,U,H,C,G,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ finite-net source row, including the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of centers at the requested rational tolerance.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph and pointwise-modulus row over the same compact metric source.
\item $T$ is the positive $\RatUp$ tolerance row requested by the downstream uniform-modulus consumer.
\item $P$ is the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ readback row listing the centers and coverage radii supplied by $K$.
\item $L$ is the center-indexed lower-bound ledger whose entries are rational radii certified by the pointwise-modulus rows of $F$.
\item $M$ is the finite rational-fold row that takes the lower bound of the displayed $L$ entries without a host minimum selector.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformModulusUp$ handoff row produced from $T,P,L,M$ and the source and target metric triangle ledgers.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact source, continuous map, tolerance row, ProbeBundle readback, lower-bound ledger, rational fold, uniform-modulus handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a uniform-modulus request through $K,F,T,P,L,M,U,H,G,N$.
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactUniformModulusExtractorUp$, $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\RatUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactUniformModulusExtractorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $K,F,T,P,L,M,U,H,C,G,N$. No open cover, selected subcover, sequential compactness argument, host lower-bound function, choice-selected Lebesgue number, or ambient completeness coordinate is part of the extractor.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact uniform modulus extractor obligations]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-modulus-extractor-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactUniformModulusExtractorUp$ carrier $E=(K,F,T,P,L,M,U,H,C,G,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactUniformModulusExtractorUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the compact metric finite-net row, continuous-map pointwise-modulus row, rational tolerance row, ProbeBundle readback, lower-bound ledger, rational fold, uniform-modulus handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item compact-source lock, requiring $K$ and the source row of $F$ to name the same metric carrier before the center bundle $P$ is read;
\item pointwise-radius binding, requiring every entry of $L$ to be attached to a center of $P$ and to a displayed pointwise-modulus row of $F$;
\item rational-fold exactness, requiring $M$ to be the finite $\RatUp$ lower-bound fold over $L$ rather than a host minimum selector;
\item uniform-modulus handoff, requiring $U$ to be read only after $T,P,L,M$ and the metric triangle rows have been displayed;
\item structural transport, requiring $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows to replay the same finite extractor route;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding open-cover compactness, selected Lebesgue numbers, sequential compactness, choice, and ambient completeness from the local packet.
\end{enumerate}
No compact uniform modulus extractor obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:compact-uniform-modulus-extractor-carrier}. The compact source is $K$, the pointwise-modulus source is $F$, the requested tolerance is $T$, and the finite center bundle is $P$.

The source lock is required before the fold begins. The center rows in $P$ and the pointwise-modulus rows in $F$ are read over the same compact metric carrier, so the lower-bound ledger $L$ cannot mix centers from one source with moduli from another.

The finite extractor step is the transition from $L$ to $M$. Since $M$ is a rational fold over the displayed finite ledger, it is classifier-stable data in the packet rather than a host-selected minimum.

Finally $U$ is read only after the fold and the triangle rows are visible. The transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows follow this same route, so open-cover compactness, selected Lebesgue numbers, sequential compactness, choice, and ambient completeness have no coordinate from which to enter the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact uniform modulus finite-fold handoff]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-modulus-extractor-finite-fold-handoff}
Every $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer reading an accepted $\CompactUniformModulusExtractorUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
K,F,T \longmapsto P,L,M \longmapsto U,H,C,G,N .
$$
Thus the consumer receives compact finite-net rows, continuous-map pointwise-modulus rows, the $\RatUp$ lower-bound fold over the displayed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ radii, and the structural handoff rows before any tolerance-only modulus is public. The packet exports no open-cover compactness, selected subcover, selected Lebesgue number, host minimum selector, sequential compactness argument, choice principle, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a uniform-modulus consumer of $E$. The carrier of \autoref{def:compact-uniform-modulus-extractor-carrier} exposes the compact metric source $K$, continuous-map row $F$, and rational tolerance $T$ as the only input rows.

The finite center data are then read through $P$. Each center contributes a pointwise-modulus radius to $L$, and the fold row $M$ computes the displayed rational lower bound over that finite ledger.

The handoff row $U$ packages the folded radius as a tolerance-only uniform modulus, while $H,C,G,N$ transport, replay, record provenance for, and name the same route. Applying \autoref{thm:compact-uniform-modulus-extractor-obligations}, no consumer can replace this finite path by an open-cover proof, selected subcover, selected Lebesgue number, host minimum selector, sequential compactness argument, choice principle, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{proof}
