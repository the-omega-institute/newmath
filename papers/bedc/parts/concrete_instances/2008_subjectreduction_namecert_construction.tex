\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SubjectReductionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-subjectreduction-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\SubjectReductionUp$ surface isolates subject reduction from the broader type-classifier packet. It records the finite data by which an accepted source membership row, a visible reduction row, and a target membership row form one $\Ext$-$\Cont$ closure packet inside $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

The packet consumes the derivation-row surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typecheckingderivationtrace-namecert} and the normal-form boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedreductionnormalform-namecert}. The source membership row $E_0$, displayed reduction row $R$, and target membership row $E_1$ are therefore ledgered rows routed through those sibling packets, not freestanding typing semantics.

\begin{definition}[SubjectReduction carrier]
\label{def:subject-reduction-namecert-carrier}
A $\SubjectReductionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(A,B,E_0,R,E_1,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the source term-history row whose type membership is being consumed;
\item $B$ is the target term-history row whose type membership is being produced;
\item $E_0$ is the source $\Ext$ membership row for $A$;
\item $R$ is the $\Cont$ reduction row from $A$ to $B$ through the displayed mark and local read route;
\item $E_1$ is the target $\Ext$ membership row for $B$;
\item $L$ is the finite subject-reduction ledger tying $E_0$, $R$, and $E_1$ to the same typing packet;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the source row, target row, source membership row, reduction row, target membership row, ledger row, package row, and local certificate row;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ read routes for source membership, typed reduction, target membership, and replay of the closure packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Ext$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, the ledger $L$, and the surrounding $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SubjectReductionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no host reduction semantics, no semantic equality of terms, no unrecorded conversion procedure, and no global normalization theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectReduction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:subject-reduction-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\SubjectReductionUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:subject-reduction-namecert-carrier} consists of four obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite packet $S=(A,B,E_0,R,E_1,L,H,C,P,N)$;
\item $\Ext$-$\Cont$ closure, saying that the accepted source membership row $E_0$ and displayed reduction row $R$ expose only the target membership row $E_1$;
\item ledger exactness, saying that every public subject-reduction read is recorded in $L$ with its source membership, reduction, target membership, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows;
\item non-escape from host reduction semantics, semantic equality of terms, unrecorded conversion, hidden normalization, or any classifier not named by the carrier.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SubjectReductionUp.SubjectReductionTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the finite packet displayed in \autoref{def:subject-reduction-namecert-carrier}. Its rows name the source and target histories, the source and target $\Ext$ memberships, the reduction route, the local ledger, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and certificate row.

The closure obligation is the only permitted bridge from $E_0$ and $R$ to $E_1$. Since $C$ supplies the read routes and $H$ supplies only componentwise $\hsame$ transport, a consumer can replay the subject-reduction packet but cannot replace it by an ambient typing theorem.

The ledger and non-escape obligations follow from the same inventory. Every usable read is first recorded in $L$, and no carrier coordinate names host reduction semantics, semantic equality, conversion outside the packet, or a global normalization result.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectReduction $\Ext$-$\Cont$ closure]
\label{thm:subject-reduction-ext-cont-closure}
Let $S=(A,B,E_0,R,E_1,L,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SubjectReductionUp$ carrier. Any public read that starts from the source membership row $E_0$ and the displayed reduction row $R$ reaches target membership only as the recorded row $E_1$, transported through the displayed $\hsame$ rows and replayed through the recorded $\Cont$ routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The source membership data are exactly $E_0$, and the reduction data are exactly $R$.

Use the read routes in $C$ to replay the closure packet. The only target membership row named by the packet is $E_1$, and the only permitted renamings are the componentwise $\hsame$ transports in $H$.

Thus the closure read is an internal $\Ext$-$\Cont$ packet. It cannot appeal to semantic equality, host reduction, or an unrecorded conversion procedure.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectReduction ledger exactness]
\label{thm:subject-reduction-ledger-exactness}
Every accepted subject-reduction read exported by a $\SubjectReductionUp$ carrier is recorded in the ledger $L$ before it is consumed. The ledger records the source row $A$, target row $B$, source membership $E_0$, reduction row $R$, target membership $E_1$, the relevant $\hsame$ transport rows, the $\Cont$ read routes, the $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a public subject-reduction read. Carrier projection places the source membership in $E_0$, the reduction in $R$, and the target membership in $E_1$.

The ledger $L$ records these rows together with their source and target histories. It also records the $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows needed to transport and replay the read.

Since $P$ and $N$ are the only package and naming rows, every exported read has the stated provenance. There is no remaining channel through which an unledgered subject-reduction assertion can enter the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectReduction non-escape]
\label{thm:subject-reduction-non-escape}
An accepted $\SubjectReductionUp$ packet exports only the displayed $\BHist$ rows, $\Ext$ membership rows, $\Cont$ reduction row, subject-reduction ledger, $\hsame$ transports, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row of \autoref{def:subject-reduction-namecert-carrier}. It exports no host reduction semantics, semantic equality of terms, hidden conversion relation, erased typing derivation, or global normalization theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. They are exactly $A,B,E_0,R,E_1,L,H,C,P,N$, and each coordinate is a displayed history, membership row, reduction row, ledger row, transport row, route row, package row, or naming row.

Apply \autoref{thm:subject-reduction-ext-cont-closure} for reduction-facing consumers and \autoref{thm:subject-reduction-ledger-exactness} for provenance-facing consumers. Both routes remain inside the finite carrier inventory.

Therefore the public boundary has no coordinate from which host reduction semantics, semantic equality, hidden conversion, erased typing derivations, or global normalization can be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectReduction bridge packet scope]
\label{thm:subject-reduction-bridge-packet-scope}
The public carrier, $\Ext$-$\Cont$ closure, ledger exactness, and non-escape rows form the bridge packet available to standard subject-reduction consumers. The packet exports the finite surface $S=(A,B,E_0,R,E_1,L,H,C,P,N)$ and still exports no host type checker, semantic term equality, global normalization, or erased derivation semantics.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{def:subject-reduction-namecert-carrier}. It displays the public packet with source and target histories, source and target $\Ext$ rows, the $\Cont$ reduction row, ledger, transports, continuations, provenance, and local certificate.

Apply \autoref{thm:subject-reduction-ext-cont-closure} to obtain the consumer route from $E_0$ and $R$ to $E_1$. Then apply \autoref{thm:subject-reduction-ledger-exactness} to record that route in $L$ with its transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows.

The bridge packet boundary is closed by \autoref{thm:subject-reduction-non-escape}. Since the carrier has no host type-checker row, semantic equality row, global normalization row, or erased-derivation row, standard subject-reduction consumers receive only the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}
