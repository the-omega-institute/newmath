\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BishopRegularCutEquivalenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cut-equivalence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\BishopRegularCutEquivalenceUp$ packet records the finite comparison certificate by which located Dedekind cut language and regular Cauchy real names meet inside the existing $\LocatedRealUp$ and $\RealUp$ routes. It is a NameCert reconstruction over the concrete rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-real-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dedekindcut-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The packet is deliberately finite. It records a displayed cut row, a located-real enclosure row, a regular rational readback, a dyadic bracket ledger, and a terminal real seal, but it does not add quotient reals, trichotomy, countable choice, or an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Bishop regular-cut equivalence carrier]
\label{def:bishop-regular-cut-equivalence-carrier}
A $\BishopRegularCutEquivalenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(C,L,R,D,Q,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is a displayed Dedekind or located cut row whose lower and upper sides are read as positive and negative rational evidence rather than as quotient-real data;
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedRealUp$ enclosure row that records the finite located interval information associated to the cut row $C$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the located enclosures, giving the regular Cauchy name visible to completion consumers;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ bracket ledger that records the dyadic lower and upper rational brackets used to compare the cut and regular-name presentations;
\item $Q$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached from $R$ through the existing real-name route;
\item $H$ stores the displayed $\hsame$ transports for $C,L,R,D,Q$;
\item $T$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes that replay the cut-to-enclosure, enclosure-to-readback, bracket-compatibility, and real-seal handoffs;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DedekindCutUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopRegularCutEquivalenceUp}$ row for the finite comparison packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is $\mathsf{BishopRegularCutEquivalencePacket}(E)$. Its public rows are exactly the displayed cut, located enclosure, regular-rational readback, dyadic bracket ledger, real seal, transports, provenance, and local naming row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop regular-cut equivalence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-regular-cut-equivalence-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier
$$
E=(C,L,R,D,Q,H,T,P,N)
$$
of \autoref{def:bishop-regular-cut-equivalence-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\BishopRegularCutEquivalenceUp}$ surface has five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed finite packet;
\item bracket and readback compatibility, requiring the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ brackets in $D$ to support both the located cut row $C$ and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$;
\item located-real handoff, requiring the enclosure row $L$ to be the only route from $C$ to $R$;
\item classifier transport, requiring accepted carriers to compare only through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes in $H,T$ together with the package row $P$;
\item $\RealUp$ seal nonescape, requiring every real-facing read through $Q$ to factor through $C,L,R,D,H,T,P,N$ and no outside quotient, trichotomy, choice, or completeness datum.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite packet $E$. The rows list the cut display, located enclosure, regular readback, dyadic bracket ledger, real seal, transports, provenance, and local naming certificate as one visible history.

Bracket and readback compatibility is the joint routing condition on $D$ and $R$. The same dyadic bracket ledger that locates the cut side also supplies the rational bounds used by the regular Cauchy readback, so the comparison has no hidden quotient-real coordinate.

The located-real handoff is the row $L$. Any passage from the cut row $C$ to the regular name $R$ must pass through the displayed located enclosure, and any transported comparison must replay through $H,T$ with provenance $P$.

Finally the real-facing row $Q$ is a terminal seal, not a new completeness principle. Since $Q$ is reached only after the displayed cut, enclosure, readback, bracket, transport, package, and local certificate rows have been consumed, the packet exports the finite comparison surface and no stronger real-analysis principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop regular-cut equivalence located-real handoff]
\label{thm:bishop-regular-cut-equivalence-located-real-handoff}
For every accepted $\BishopRegularCutEquivalenceUp$ carrier
$$
E=(C,L,R,D,Q,H,T,P,N),
$$
the public comparison between the located cut row $C$ and the regular Cauchy row $R$ factors through the $\LocatedRealUp$ enclosure row $L$ and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ bracket ledger $D$. The $\RealUp$ seal row $Q$ may be consumed only after that finite route has been replayed through $H,T,P,N$.

Consequently the packet gives a finite L10 comparison certificate between Bishop located cuts and regular Cauchy real names without asserting Dedekind completeness, quotient-real extensionality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier through \autoref{def:bishop-regular-cut-equivalence-carrier}. The row $C$ is the displayed cut surface, $L$ is the located enclosure row attached to it, and $R$ is the regular rational readback.

Apply \autoref{thm:bishop-regular-cut-equivalence-namecert-obligations}. The bracket and readback obligation forces $D$ to be the shared finite dyadic ledger for both sides of the comparison, while the located-real handoff obligation prevents a direct jump from $C$ to $R$.

The classifier transport obligation then carries the same route through $H,T,P,N$. The real-seal nonescape obligation leaves $Q$ as the terminal consumer of that route, so no unlisted quotient, trichotomy, choice, or completeness datum can be read from the carrier.
\end{proof}
