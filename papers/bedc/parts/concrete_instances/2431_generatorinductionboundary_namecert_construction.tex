\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GeneratorInductionBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-generatorinductionboundary-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\GeneratorInductionBoundaryUp$ packet is the concrete NameCert surface for separating authorized generator-internal induction from free-floating induction. It packages the generator signature, closure witness, continuation route, GAP socket, transport row, package provenance, and local naming row into one finite carrier, so that every induction read remains attached to the generator that licenses it.

\begin{definition}[GeneratorInductionBoundary carrier]
\label{def:generator-induction-boundary-carrier}
A $\GeneratorInductionBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(S,W,C,G,H,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the authorized generator signature row, recording the generator name, its closed constructor list, and the local induction pattern licensed by that constructor list;
\item $W$ is the closure witness row, recording that the displayed induction step ranges only over the closure generated by $S$;
\item $C$ is the continuation route row, recording the $\Cont$ paths by which a consumer may replay the generator-internal induction step and transport its conclusion to later finite histories;
\item $G$ is the GAP socket row, recording attempted reads that would move from the generator closure to a free-floating universal induction principle;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,W,C,G,P,N$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, authorized generator signatures, closure witnesses, continuation routes, GAP sockets, $\hsame$, and local naming;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorInductionBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a universal induction rule, an unbounded object domain, a hidden host-CIC evaluator, an oracle continuation, or an induction conclusion detached from the authorized generator signature.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorInductionBoundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:generator-induction-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\GeneratorInductionBoundaryUp$ carrier
$$
B=(S,W,C,G,H,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorInductionBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $B$, authorized generator exposure through $S$, closure-witness exposure through $W$, continuation routing through $C$, GAP socket accounting through $G$, componentwise transport through $H$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:generator-induction-boundary-carrier}. The packet displays the authorized generator signature, closure witness, continuation route, GAP socket, transport, provenance, and naming rows as finite coordinates.

The generator and closure obligations are the rows $S$ and $W$. They identify the constructor list being used and bind the induction read to the closure generated by that list.

The continuation obligation is the row $C$. Every replay of the induction step must pass through the displayed route, so the consumer receives a generator-internal conclusion rather than a detached induction rule.

Finally $G,H,P,N$ preserve the same boundary object under socket accounting, transport, provenance, and naming. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for universal induction or hidden host evaluation, the local $\NameCert_{\GeneratorInductionBoundaryUp}$ surface is exactly the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GeneratorInductionBoundary GAP non-escape]
\label{thm:generator-induction-boundary-gap-non-escape}
Every accepted $\GeneratorInductionBoundaryUp$ continuation route factors through the finite rows
$$
(S,W,C,G,H,P,N).
$$
The route may export that an induction step is authorized inside the generator named by $S$, carried by the closure witness $W$, replayed through $C$, transported by $H$, packaged by $P$, named by $N$, and separated from free-floating induction by the GAP socket $G$. It cannot export a universal induction principle, a conclusion over an unbounded object domain, or a host-CIC delegation that has no displayed row in the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the route through the carrier. The only available coordinates are $S,W,C,G,H,P,N$.

Use \autoref{thm:generator-induction-boundary-namecert-obligations} to account for the authorized generator signature, closure witness, continuation route, GAP socket, transport, provenance, and local naming rows.

The row $G$ records attempted movement from generator-internal induction to a free-floating induction rule. Because such movement is represented only as a socket row and not as an exported conclusion, it cannot become a new induction coordinate.

Thus any exported route remains inside the displayed generator boundary. A universal induction principle, an unbounded object domain, or an unrecorded host-CIC delegation would require an extra row outside $B$, and the carrier has no such row.
\end{proof}
