\begin{definition}[Bishop regular-sequence carrier]
\label{def:bishop-regular-sequence-carrier}
A $\BishopRegularSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(D,W,R,M,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation row, naming the dyadic rational values and tolerance comparisons available to the sequence.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row, exposing the inspected prefixes and tail windows of the lawlike sequence.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row attached to the same windows and dyadic observations.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ schedule row, recording the finite tail threshold supplied for each dyadic tolerance request.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal handoff, available only after $D,W,R,M$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the observation, window, regularity, modulus, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from observations and windows through regularity and modulus data to the seal handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopRegularSequenceUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.

\begin{theorem}[Bishop regular-sequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-regular-sequence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopRegularSequenceUp$ carrier $B=(D,W,R,M,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BishopRegularSequenceUp}$ surface consists of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $D,W,R,M,E,H,C,P,N$;
\item observation and window exposure through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and $\StreamNameUp$;
\item regularity and tail-modulus exposure through $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\CauchyModulusUp$;
\item terminal $\RealUp$ seal handoff through $E$ after the observation, window, regularity, and modulus rows are visible;
\item structural stability through $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and the local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{enumerate}
No accepted obligation row may be discharged by quotient streams, a selected limit, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishop-regular-sequence-carrier}. The analytic rows are exactly $D,W,R,M,E$, and the structural rows are exactly $H,C,P,N$.

The observation and window rows are read first. The $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row supplies dyadic values and tolerance comparisons, while the $\StreamNameUp$ row supplies the finite inspected windows.

Next read the regularity and modulus rows. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row states the displayed regularity of the same windows, and the $\CauchyModulusUp$ row supplies the finite tail threshold for each dyadic tolerance request.

Finally the seal handoff and structural rows are consumed. The $\RealUp$ row $E$ is downstream of $D,W,R,M$, and $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same finite route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for the refused host-level data, the five obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop regular-sequence tail-modulus induction]
\label{thm:bishop-regular-sequence-tail-modulus-induction}
For every accepted $\BishopRegularSequenceUp$ carrier $B=(D,W,R,M,E,H,C,P,N)$ and every finite dyadic tolerance request read through $D$, primitive induction on the displayed tail schedule $M$ produces a window threshold in $W$ such that all later inspected windows preserve the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity row $R$ before the $\RealUp$ seal handoff $E$ is exposed. The induction stays inside the route
$$
D \longmapsto M \longmapsto W \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
It does not select an external infinite tail, quotient two streams, choose a representative, or invoke ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a dyadic tolerance request in $D$. The request is finite BEDC data, so it can be passed to the displayed $\CauchyModulusUp$ schedule row $M$ without invoking an external selector.

Induct on the finite tail schedule carried by $M$. The base case exposes the first admissible window in $W$ for the requested tolerance. The successor step refines the inspected window while preserving the same dyadic tolerance row and the same $\StreamNameUp$ route.

Use the regularity row $R$ after the window threshold is displayed. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row is attached to $D$ and $W$, so every later inspected window remains a regular rational readback inside the same packet.

Finally expose $E,H,C,P,N$. The $\RealUp$ seal is reached only after the induced tail threshold and regularity row are visible, and the structural rows replay the same finite route. Thus the induction provides a tail-modulus handoff for the public seal without quotient streams, selected limits, hidden representatives, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
