\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PicardContractionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-picardcontraction-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\PicardContractionUp$ interface names the finite contraction-iteration
packet used by $\OdeUp$ and $\NewtonIterationUp$ consumers when they read a
displayed Picard step, a Lipschitz ledger, or an explicit Cauchy modulus. In
standard functional analysis this surface is motivated by the Banach
contraction principle as presented, for example, in Kreyszig,
\emph{Introductory Functional Analysis with Applications}. BEDC records only
the finite rows consumed by downstream certificates: a Banach source row, a
contraction-map row, a rational Lipschitz-ratio ledger, finite iterate rows,
an explicit modulus window, transport routes, provenance, and a local
$\NameCert$ row. It does not add a host fixed point, ambient completeness
theorem, or selected limit.

\begin{definition}[PicardContraction BHist carrier]
\label{def:picardcontraction-bhist-carrier}
A $\PicardContractionUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
P=(B,T,L,I,M,E,H,C,G,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\BanachUp$ source row whose carried histories provide the
state endpoints inspected by the finite iteration packet;
\item $T$ is the contraction-map row, displaying the map-code history and the
source-target $\Cont$ route through which a carried state history is read as
its next Picard state;
\item $L$ is the Lipschitz-ratio ledger, a positive $\RatUp$ row below the
unit bound together with the displayed metric comparison rows used by the
contraction read;
\item $I$ is the finite Picard-iterate ledger, listing the visible state rows
$x_0,\ldots,x_n$ and the application rows reading $x_{k+1}$ from $T$ and
$x_k$;
\item $M$ is the Cauchy-modulus row, giving an explicit finite window after
which the displayed iterate distances are bounded by the requested rational
tolerance;
\item $E$ is the endpoint and seal row through which downstream $\OdeUp$,
$\NewtonIterationUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumers read the last
finite iterate and the modulus window;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the Banach source,
map-code row, Lipschitz ledger, finite iterate ledger, modulus row, endpoint
row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes for Banach-state reads,
contraction-map application, finite iterate concatenation, modulus readback,
and endpoint sealing;
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the consumed $\BanachUp$,
$RatUp$, metric, iterate, modulus, and downstream certificate surfaces;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PicardContractionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a finite packet. It has no coordinate for a completed orbit, a
host fixed point, an ambient completeness theorem, an external contraction
principle, or a choice-selected limit.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[PicardContraction finite classifier]
\label{def:picardcontraction-classifier}
Two accepted $\PicardContractionUp$ carriers are classified as the same
finite contraction packet exactly when the visible rows of
\autoref{def:picardcontraction-bhist-carrier} compare componentwise: their
$\BanachUp$ source rows are classified by the Banach carrier classifier, their
contraction-map rows are transported through the displayed $\Cont$ route,
their Lipschitz-ratio ledgers compare through the $\RatUp$ order rows, their
finite iterate ledgers are related by $\hsame$ at every retained endpoint,
their Cauchy-modulus windows and endpoint seals are the same after the
displayed transport rows, and their $\Pkg$ and local $\NameCert$ rows name the
same visible packet.

The classifier has no generator for comparing host functions, no generator
for a fixed-point value, and no generator for an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PicardContraction finite iterate closure]
\label{thm:picardcontraction-finite-iterate-closure}
Let $P$ and $Q$ be accepted $\PicardContractionUp$ carriers over the same
$\BanachUp$ source row, contraction-map row, and Lipschitz-ratio ledger. If
the endpoint row of $P$ is related by $\hsame$ to the initial iterate row of
$Q$, and the displayed iterate ledgers concatenate by a finite continuation
row, then the concatenated packet is again an accepted
$\PicardContractionUp$ carrier. Its Lipschitz-ratio ledger is the transported
ledger shared by $P$ and $Q$, its finite Picard-iterate ledger is the
concatenated $\mathsf{BHist}$ row, and its Cauchy-modulus and endpoint rows
are read through the displayed $\Cont$ routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows of $P$ and $Q$. Both packets expose the same
Banach source, contraction-map row, and Lipschitz-ratio ledger, while their
iterate coordinates are finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ lists.

Use the endpoint comparison to splice the last visible state of $P$ to the
first visible state of $Q$. The componentwise $\hsame$ rows transport only the
displayed endpoint histories and do not introduce an external orbit or limit.

Concatenate the two finite iterate ledgers by the given $\Cont$ row. The
application rows of the first segment and the transported application rows of
the second segment remain contraction-map reads under the same $T$ row, and
the Lipschitz-ratio ledger is reused through the transported $L$ coordinate.

The modulus and endpoint coordinates of the concatenated packet are then
read through the same displayed continuation routes. Repacking the Banach
source, contraction row, ratio ledger, concatenated iterate ledger, modulus
row, endpoint seal, transports, provenance, and local name gives exactly the
carrier of \autoref{def:picardcontraction-bhist-carrier}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PicardContraction Banach ODE boundary]
\label{thm:picardcontraction-banach-ode-boundary}
Every $\OdeUp$ or $\NewtonIterationUp$ consumer read of a
$\PicardContractionUp$ packet factors through the finite Picard rows, the
$\BanachUp$ source row, the contraction-map ledger, the Lipschitz-ratio
ledger, the explicit Cauchy-modulus row, and the displayed endpoint/seal
routes of \autoref{def:picardcontraction-bhist-carrier}. The consumer may not
read a host fixed point, a completed Picard orbit, an ambient completeness
theorem, a Banach fixed-point theorem, or a choice-selected limit from this
packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an $\OdeUp$ or $\NewtonIterationUp$ read. The only Banach-facing
coordinate is $B$, and the only iteration-facing coordinates are the
contraction-map row $T$, the Lipschitz ledger $L$, the finite iterate ledger
$I$, and the endpoint/modulus rows $M$ and $E$.

The classifier of \autoref{def:picardcontraction-classifier} can transport
only those rows. Banach-state movement is read through the Banach classifier,
map application through the displayed $\Cont$ route, rational contraction
bounds through $L$, and finite iterate comparison through the endpoint
$\hsame$ rows.

No remaining carrier coordinate contains a fixed-point value or a completed
limit. Therefore downstream ODE and Newton consumers receive exactly the
finite contraction surface named in the statement, with full fixed-point
convergence and ambient completeness outside the BEDC packet.
\end{proof}

consumer to factor through the displayed Banach, contraction, ratio, iterate,
modulus, endpoint, transport, provenance, and local-name rows.

\begin{proof}
The carrier row gives the finite inventory. It lists the Banach source,
contraction map, Lipschitz ledger, iterate ledger, modulus row, endpoint seal,

The classifier row gives the only permitted transports, so every accepted

exactly \autoref{thm:picardcontraction-finite-iterate-closure}.

The modulus and readback row is the pair $(M,E)$ together with its displayed
continuation routes. Finally,
\autoref{thm:picardcontraction-banach-ode-boundary} supplies ledger
non-escape for the ODE and Newton consumers. These five rows constitute the
local naming-certificate surface and leave fixed-point convergence outside
the seed packet.
\end{proof}
