Following Brouwer and Troelstra--van Dalen on fan functionals and continuous choice-sequence functionals, the $\FanContinuityPrincipleUp$ packet records the constructive fan-continuity principle as finite NameCert data rather than as host function-space continuity. Its dependency surface joins the finite bar depth of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fantheorem-namecert}, the prefix-cylinder context of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairespace-namecert}, the finite observation row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-choice-sequence-namecert}, the window schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet gives later Real and Completion consumers one finite route for extracting a common prefix modulus from fan-bar data, without asserting open-cover compactness, host equality of functionals, countable choice, quotient streams, selected real limits, trichotomy, or ambient real completeness.

\begin{definition}[Fan continuity principle carrier]
\label{def:fan-continuity-principle-carrier}
A $\FanContinuityPrincipleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\FanTheoremUp$ finite bar-depth row, carrying a binary spread, a decidable bar classifier, and the displayed depth ledger that bounds the fan read.
\item $A$ is the $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix-cylinder row, exposing the finite prefix context in which a Baire-name observation is inspected.
\item $Q$ is the $\ChoiceSequenceUp$ finite observation row, listing the already displayed choice-sequence answers whose common prefix controls the read.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window schedule opened for the fan prefix, Baire prefix, and choice-sequence observation rows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row used when the finite prefix data are consumed by a regular-rational route.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $B,A,Q,S,R$ have been replayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the bar-depth, prefix-cylinder, finite-observation, stream-window, readback, real-seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$

\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Fan continuity principle modulus extraction]
\label{thm:fan-continuity-principle-modulus-extraction}
Every accepted $\FanContinuityPrincipleUp$ carrier
$$
F=(B,A,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
exports a finite prefix modulus for the displayed real-facing read: the modulus is the depth ledger supplied by the $\FanTheoremUp$ row $B$, restricted to the prefix-cylinder row $A$, witnessed by the finite $\ChoiceSequenceUp$ observation row $Q$, scheduled through $S$, and read back through $R$ before reaching the $\RealUp$ seal $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only depth datum in the packet is the finite bar-depth row $B$, and the only prefix context is the $\BaireSpaceUp$ row $A$.

Use the displayed choice-sequence observation row $Q$ next. Its entries are finite answers already visible through the stream window schedule $S$, so any two inspected choice sequences agreeing on the displayed $B$-depth have the same $Q$-prefix inside the $A$-cylinder.

The readback step is then forced through $R$. Since the continuation route is displayed as $B,A,Q,S \longmapsto R \longmapsto E$, the real-facing seal $E$ can be reached only after the finite prefix data have been read back.

Thus the depth ledger in $B$, viewed inside $A$ and witnessed by $Q,S,R$, is the exported modulus. No host function-space continuity or selected infinite branch is used.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Fan continuity principle bar stability]
\label{thm:fan-continuity-principle-bar-stability}
Let
$$
F=(B,A,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
and
$$
F'=(B',A',Q',S',R',E',H',C',P',N')
$$
be accepted $\FanContinuityPrincipleUp$ carriers whose displayed rows are related by componentwise $\hsame$ transport and whose $\Cont$ routes replay the same order. Then the prefix modulus extracted in \autoref{thm:fan-continuity-principle-modulus-extraction} transports from $F$ to $F'$ by carrying the fan bar-depth, Baire prefix-cylinder, choice-sequence observation, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, and RealUp seal rows componentwise.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:fan-continuity-principle-modulus-extraction} to $F$. The extracted modulus is exactly the depth row $B$ together with the surrounding prefix-cylinder, observation, window, readback, and seal rows.

Now inspect the transport rows $H$ and $H'$. They compare $B,A,Q,S,R,E$ with $B',A',Q',S',R',E'$ componentwise, so the displayed depth ledger and every row used to read it are carried to the second packet.

Finally use the $\Cont$ replay rows $C$ and $C'$. They preserve the order $B,A,Q,S \longmapsto R \longmapsto E$, so the transported packet still reads the same finite bar before opening the real-facing seal.

Therefore the modulus is stable under the displayed carrier classifier. The argument has no place to introduce open-cover compactness, host function equality, countable choice, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, ambient real completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Fan continuity principle NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:fan-continuity-principle-namecert}

\item modulus extraction, requiring the exported prefix modulus to be the finite depth ledger of $B$ read through $A,Q,S,R$ before $E$;

\item real-seal nonescape, requiring every $\RealUp$ read to pass through the displayed route $B,A,Q,S \longmapsto R \longmapsto E$;
\item provenance exactness, requiring $P$ and $N$ to name only the listed sibling chapters and the local certificate row.
\end{enumerate}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

For modulus extraction, use \autoref{thm:fan-continuity-principle-modulus-extraction}. It identifies the exported prefix modulus with the finite bar-depth row $B$ after restriction to the prefix and observation rows $A,Q,S,R$.

For stability, use \autoref{thm:fan-continuity-principle-bar-stability}. The componentwise transport rows preserve the depth ledger and every sibling row required for its replay.

The nonescape and provenance obligations are the remaining projections. The continuation row displays the only route to the real seal, and $P,N$ record only package provenance and local naming for the same finite carrier. Since the carrier inventory contains no further coordinates, the five listed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
