\begin{definition}[ApartnessTopology carrier]
\label{def:apartnesstopology-carrier}
An $\ApartnessTopologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(G,O,T,M,S,W,R,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the $\LocatedApartnessUp$ positive-gap row, including the two located sources, the shared finite window, the dyadic endpoint ledgers, and the gap evidence.
\item $O$ is the $\LocatedOpenUp$ neighbourhood-window row that records the finite interval-entry or positive-radius witnesses read by a local neighbourhood consumer.
\item $T$ is the $\MetricTopologyUp$ basis row through which the generated neighbourhood packet is admitted as an indexed topology row.
\item $M$ is the $\LocatedMetricUp$ comparison row tying the same observations to finite metric-distance and dyadic locatedness reads.
\item $S$ is the $\LocatedSetUp$ membership row exposing the displayed set admission and located-distance route used by neighbourhood membership checks.
\item $W$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row on which the apartness, located-open, located-metric, and located-set reads are aligned.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the rational observations inspected by $G,O,M,S$.
\item $Q$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger containing the positive gap, endpoint, radius, and tolerance rows used by the neighbourhood request.
\item $D$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the stream, regular-rational, and dyadic rows have been displayed.
\item $E$ records the local neighbourhood packet generated from the transported apartness gap and the located-open window.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the apartness row, located-open row, metric-topology row, located-metric row, located-set row, readbacks, generated neighbourhood packet, continuation, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from apartness gap requests to located neighbourhood windows, then to metric-topology basis rows and located-set membership reads.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ApartnessTopologyUp$, $\LocatedApartnessUp$, $\LocatedOpenUp$, $\MetricTopologyUp$, $\LocatedMetricUp$, $\LocatedSetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ApartnessTopologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $G,O,T,M,S,W,R,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host powersets, quotient opens, classical trichotomy, selected limits, open-cover compactness, or an independent $\RealUp$ completeness principle.

\begin{theorem}[ApartnessTopology NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:apartnesstopology-namecert-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\ApartnessTopologyUp$ carrier $A=(G,O,T,M,S,W,R,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ApartnessTopologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier visibility: the packet displays the located-apartness gap, located-open window, metric-topology basis route, located-metric comparison row, located-set membership row, readbacks, generated neighbourhood packet, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item gap-to-neighbourhood generation: the positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ gap row of $G$ is aligned over $W,R,Q,D$ and repacked as the neighbourhood packet $E$ through the located-open row $O$;
\item topology admission: the generated neighbourhood packet is admitted only through the indexed basis route $T$ and the comparison and membership rows $M,S$;
\item transport stability: componentwise $\hsame$ transport preserves the apartness gap, located window, metric basis, located metric comparison, located set membership, readbacks, and generated neighbourhood packet together;
\item boundary non-escape: the packet exports no host powerset, quotient open, trichotomy row, selected limit, open-cover compactness datum, or standalone $\RealUp$ completeness field.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these five finite rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier visibility is projection from \autoref{def:apartnesstopology-carrier}. The displayed tuple lists the apartness, neighbourhood, topology, located-metric, located-set, readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The generation row uses the shared finite window. The positive gap of $G$ is transported over $W,R,Q,D$ and then read by the located-open row $O$, so the generated neighbourhood packet $E$ is determined by finite apartness evidence and the displayed neighbourhood window.

Topology admission is forced through $T,M,S$. The metric-topology row supplies the indexed basis route, while the located-metric and located-set rows supply the comparison and membership reads required by the same neighbourhood packet.

Transport stability follows componentwise from $H$ and replay from $C$. These rows move the gap, window, basis, comparison, membership, readback, and generated-neighbourhood data together, so no detached witness can enter the local certificate.

The negative boundary is immediate from the carrier coordinates. Since no host powerset, quotient-open representative, trichotomy row, selected limit, compactness witness, or independent completeness field appears in $A$, the five listed rows exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Apartness neighbourhood transport]
\label{thm:apartnesstopology-neighborhood-apartness-transport}
Let $A=(G,O,T,M,S,W,R,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\ApartnessTopologyUp$ packet. If the positive apartness gap row of $G$ is transported componentwise along $H$ over the shared finite window and readbacks $W,R,Q,D$, then the transported gap generates the same located neighbourhood packet $E$ through $O,T,M,S$ and the replay row $C$. This generation uses only the finite $\BHist$ route named by the carrier; it does not invoke host powersets, trichotomy, quotient opens, selected limits, open-cover compactness, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the positive gap row inside $G$. The row is already attached to the shared finite window, regular-rational readbacks, dyadic endpoint and radius ledgers, and the real seal listed as $W,R,Q,D$.

Apply the transport row $H$ componentwise. The transported packet still carries the same finite window indices, readback rows, dyadic gap evidence, and real-facing seal, so the transported apartness evidence remains aligned with the located-open window $O$.

Replay the neighbourhood request through $C$. The replay first reads $O$ to form the located neighbourhood packet, then uses $T$ for indexed topology admission, $M$ for located metric comparison, and $S$ for located set membership.

The resulting neighbourhood packet is therefore the same $E$ named in the carrier, transported along the same finite rows. The construction never leaves $G,O,T,M,S,W,R,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N$, and hence cannot use any excluded powerset, quotient, trichotomy, selection, compactness, or completeness datum.
\end{proof}
