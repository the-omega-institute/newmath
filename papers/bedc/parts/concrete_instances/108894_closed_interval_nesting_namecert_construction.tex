Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\ClosedIntervalNestingUp$ packet records the finite nested-closed-interval route used before a retained interval family is read as regular rational Cauchy data. It sits over the dyadic endpoint core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream-window discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the interval certificate surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interval-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Closed interval nesting carrier]
\label{def:closed-interval-nesting-carrier}
A $\ClosedIntervalNestingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(E,I,S,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the dyadic closed-endpoint row. It records left and right endpoints through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, endpoint order, midpoint arithmetic cells, and the dyadic comparison budget for each inspected interval.
\item $I$ is the nesting-inclusion row. It displays, for every inspected successor stage, the finite evidence that the successor closed interval is contained in its predecessor.
\item $S$ is the shrinking-width schedule. It records the requested precision, the displayed stage bound, and the dyadic inequality that makes the inspected interval width smaller than the requested tolerance.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row for endpoint, inclusion, width, and stage-bound observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained from the chosen endpoint stream and the width schedule after $E,I,S,W$ have been displayed.
\item $Q$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row. It is opened only after the dyadic endpoints, nesting inclusions, shrinking-width schedule, stream windows, and regular rational readback have been replayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the endpoint row, inclusion row, width schedule, stream windows, regular readback, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion-facing or real-name request through $E,I,S,W,R,Q,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClosedIntervalNestingUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, interval certificates, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedIntervalNestingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted nesting carriers only through $E,I,S,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for Dedekind cuts, trichotomy, quotient equality of real names, selected limits, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed interval nesting NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-interval-nesting-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ClosedIntervalNestingUp$ carrier $K=(E,I,S,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedIntervalNestingUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, dyadic closed-endpoint exposure, successor nesting inclusion, shrinking-width scheduling, finite $\StreamNameUp$ window exposure, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, terminal $\RealUp$ sealing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming for the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-interval-nesting-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:closed-interval-nesting-carrier}. The endpoint row $E$ and inclusion row $I$ are the only rows that expose closed-interval data, so every local naming obligation begins with displayed dyadic endpoints and finite successor containment.

The width and window obligations are consecutive. The schedule row $S$ supplies the stage bound and dyadic width inequality, while $W$ exposes the finite endpoint, inclusion, width, and stage observations used by that schedule.

The readback rows $R,Q$ are downstream of those displayed observations. The regular rational row repacks the endpoint stream under the shrinking-width bound, and the real seal is available only after that regular readback has been replayed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for Dedekind cuts, trichotomy, quotient equality, selected limits, choice, or ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed interval nesting RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:closed-interval-nesting-regseqrat-handoff}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\ClosedIntervalNestingUp$ carrier reaches regular rational data only through the displayed finite route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp
\longmapsto
\StreamNameUp
\longmapsto
\RegSeqRatUp
\longmapsto

$$
The handoff may be consumed by completion and real-name routes as a nested closed-interval certificate, but it exports no Dedekind-cut construction, trichotomy row, quotient equality of real names, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $K=(E,I,S,W,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$. The endpoint row $E$ and nesting row $I$ expose the only closed-interval information available to a consumer.

Read the shrinking-width schedule $S$ next. It ties every requested tolerance to a displayed stage bound and a dyadic width inequality, so the route cannot be interpreted as a selected intersection point or a hidden completed limit.

The stream and regular rows $W,R$ then turn those finite endpoint windows into regular rational readback. The terminal row $Q$ is a real-name seal only after the dyadic endpoint, inclusion, width, and stream rows have been displayed.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same order under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Because no stronger object appears among the carrier coordinates, every $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff remains the displayed finite nested-interval route.
\end{proof}
