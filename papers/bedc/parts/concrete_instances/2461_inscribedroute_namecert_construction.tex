\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\InscribedRouteUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-inscribedroute-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\InscribedRouteUp$ surface isolates the reusable proof route carried by an inscription event. It names the finite path from a visible gap through a public statement, a displayed proof route, an accepted record row, and a downstream consumer, while leaving private discovery, host trust, and unrecorded choice outside the packet.

\begin{definition}[InscribedRoute carrier]
\label{def:inscribed-route-carrier}
An $\InscribedRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S,G,M,R,A,U,L,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the prior $\BHist$ source scope from which the route is read;
\item $G$ is the visible gap row naming the local theorem, certificate, or consumer boundary that still requires a route;
\item $M$ is the named statement row, fixing the public binders, hypotheses, and conclusion of the reusable route;
\item $R$ is the proof-route row, recording the displayed constructor, projection, inversion, transitivity, symmetry, or transport steps that move from $S$ and $G$ to $M$;
\item $A$ is the acceptance row, recording that the route is admitted as public record data;
\item $U$ is the downstream consumer row naming the theorem site, citation, audit surface, or certificate boundary that may read the route;
\item $L$ is the public $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger row tying the visible gap, named statement, route, acceptance row, and consumer row to the same finite route packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, route-checking data, $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger data, and local certificate dependencies;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\InscribedRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier for accepted carriers compares only $S,G,M,R,A,U,L,P,N$ and their displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transports. It has no coordinate for hidden psychology, host trust, or an unrecorded route selector.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[InscribedRoute NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:inscribed-route-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\InscribedRouteUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:inscribed-route-carrier} consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: every accepted route exposes exactly the packet $Q=(S,G,M,R,A,U,L,P,N)$;
\item route classifier stability: public comparison is restricted to the source scope, visible gap, named statement, proof route, acceptance row, downstream consumer, ledger, package provenance, local naming row, and displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes;
\item proof-route determinacy: the route row $R$ reaches $M$ from $S$ and $G$ only through the displayed $\Cont$ path and the matching $\hsame$ rows;
\item acceptance-ledger exactness: the acceptance row $A$ and $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger row $L$ record exactly the admitted route and the downstream consumer boundary that may cite it;
\item non-escape: the packet exports no hidden psychology, host trust predicate, or unrecorded choice of route.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:inscribed-route-carrier}. The source scope, visible gap, named statement, proof route, acceptance row, downstream consumer, ledger, package provenance, and local naming row are all displayed before the route can be consumed.

Classifier stability and proof-route determinacy are read from the displayed comparison surface. The classifier has access only to $S,G,M,R,A,U,L,P,N$, and the route from $R$ to $M$ is replayed through the recorded $\Cont$ path together with the matching $\hsame$ rows.

Acceptance-ledger exactness follows because $A$ and $L$ are coordinates of the same finite packet. The accepted route and the downstream consumer boundary are therefore ledgered in public data rather than inferred from a host-side process.

The non-escape clause is immediate from the carrier inventory. No row stores hidden psychology, host trust, or an unrecorded choice, so none of these can be exported by the local $\NameCert_{\InscribedRouteUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}
