\begin{definition}[Monotone sequence convergence carrier]
\label{def:monotone-sequence-convergence-carrier}
A $\MonotoneSequenceConvergenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
J=(S,W,Q,R,B,M,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the inspected sequence schedule, listing the finite index windows used by the packet.
\item $W$ is the finite family of $\StreamNameUp$ windows observed at the indices in $S$.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to those windows and their dyadic tolerances.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the stream windows and regular rational readbacks have been displayed.
\item $B$ is the bounded interval or bounded net row supplied by the bounded real sequence surface.
\item $M$ is the monotonicity ledger, recording the displayed order direction and the finite comparison rows used by the inspected windows.
\item $T$ is the tail-Cauchy modulus and threshold row; it names the finite threshold beyond which the $Q$-readbacks are forced into the displayed tolerance.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the schedule, windows, readbacks, real seals, boundedness row, monotonicity ledger, tail threshold, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from inspected indices to windows, from windows to regular readback, from readback to real seals, and from the bounded monotone ledger to the tail-Cauchy threshold.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MonotoneSequenceConvergenceUp$, $\BoundedRealSequenceUp$, $\MonotoneCauchyUp$, $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local naming.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MonotoneSequenceConvergenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier classifier reads only
$$
S,W,Q,R,B,M,T,H,C,P,N.
$$
It has no coordinate for trichotomy, host real equality, quotient equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, open-cover compactness, a Bolzano--Weierstrass subsequence selector, or a choice-selected limit.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Monotone sequence tail-Cauchy route]
\label{thm:monotone-sequence-convergence-tail-cauchy}
For every accepted $\MonotoneSequenceConvergenceUp$ carrier $J=(S,W,Q,R,B,M,T,H,C,P,N)$, bounded monotone finite-window rows produce a regular Cauchy tail route through the displayed threshold $T$ and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier $Q$. Every tail comparison factors through
$$
S\longmapsto W\longmapsto Q\longmapsto B,M\longmapsto T
$$
together with $R,H,C,P,N$, and no comparison uses trichotomy, host completeness, quotient equality, choice, or a selected limit representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the inspected schedule $S$. The continuation row $C$ exposes only the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$, and the readback row $Q$ classifies those windows as regular rational observations before any $\RealUp$ seal row is consumed.

The boundedness and monotonicity rows are then read together. The row $B$ supplies the bounded real sequence interval or net surface, while $M$ supplies the displayed monotone ledger for the same inspected windows. Their joint use is finite: the packet never asks for a host set of all sequence values.

The threshold row $T$ is the tail-Cauchy output of that bounded monotone read. It records the displayed tail index and tolerance route through the $Q$-classifier, so downstream regular Cauchy consumers receive a route already expressed in $\RegSeqRatUp$ data.

Transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ preserve the same route. Since the carrier contains no trichotomy, host-completeness, quotient-equality, choice, or selected-limit coordinate, the tail-Cauchy comparison cannot leave the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Monotone sequence convergence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:monotone-sequence-convergence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MonotoneSequenceConvergenceUp$ carrier, the local $\NameCert_{\MonotoneSequenceConvergenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, finite-window classification, boundedness transport, monotone-ledger transport, tail-Cauchy extraction, $\RealUp$ seal non-escape, and package provenance for the displayed rows $S,W,Q,R,B,M,T,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the tuple of \autoref{def:monotone-sequence-convergence-carrier}. The finite-window classifier reads the schedule $S$, the $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks $Q$; these rows are the only source of sequence observations.

Boundedness transport uses $B$ together with the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$ and the displayed replay row $C$. Monotone-ledger transport is parallel: the same $H$ and $C$ rows move the finite order ledger $M$ without adding a host order or trichotomy field.

Tail-Cauchy extraction is exactly the row $T$ produced from the bounded monotone finite-window data. The real-seal boundary is the row $R$, which is readable only after the $W$ and $Q$ route has been displayed and therefore cannot introduce quotient real equality or ambient completeness.

Finally $P$ records the package provenance and $N$ records the local naming row. These rows name no data outside $S,W,Q,R,B,M,T,H,C,P,N$, so the seven listed obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}
