\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ThreeManifoldUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-threemanifold-namecert}

The 3-manifold interface $\ThreeManifoldUp$ packages a closed orientable manifold of dimension three together with the JSJ and geometric decompositions licensed by Thurston-Perelman geometrisation. It depends on $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-manifold-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\TopologyUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:threemanifold-carrier}

\begin{definition}[ThreeManifold finite carrier]
\label{def:threemanifold-carrier}
A $\ThreeManifoldUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet displaying a closed orientable three-dimensional $\ManifoldUp$ row, a $\TopologyUp$ source row, a decomposition ledger row for the visible JSJ and geometric pieces, componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons, $\Cont$ rows connecting the decomposition ledger to the source rows, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance for the manifold and topology certificates. The carrier exports no checked geometrisation bridge, no host smooth manifold, and no unlogged decomposition object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ThreeManifold carrier obligation]
\label{thm:threemanifold-carrier-obligation}
Every accepted $\ThreeManifoldUp$ packet is generated by the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier of \autoref{def:threemanifold-carrier}: its manifold row, topology row, decomposition ledger, $\hsame$ classifier rows, $\Cont$ continuation rows, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance are all displayed, and the dependency rows from $\ManifoldUp$ and $\TopologyUp$ are the only source rows available to downstream consumers.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ThreeManifoldUp.ThreeManifoldFiniteCarrier\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier definition. The packet records the $\ManifoldUp$ and $\TopologyUp$ dependencies as explicit source rows and stores the decomposition data as a finite ledger attached to them.

The classifier rows are componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons over the displayed coordinates. The continuation rows tie those coordinates to the packet readback, and the package rows identify the two dependency certificates.

Since no other coordinate is present in the $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet, every accepted readback is confined to the displayed carrier rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ThreeManifold topology source scope]
\label{thm:threemanifold-topology-source-scope}
The topology source of a $\ThreeManifoldUp$ packet is exactly the displayed $\TopologyUp$ row together with its $\mathsf{BHist}$ data, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ ledger, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and the paired $\ManifoldUp$ dependency row. It cannot be replaced by a host topological space, an external atlas, or an unlogged equivalence relation.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ThreeManifoldUp.ThreeManifoldFiniteCarrier\_topology\_source\_scope}
\begin{proof}
Read the topology coordinate from the carrier. It is paired with the displayed manifold row and with the finite continuation ledger that carries the topology data into the decomposition readback.

Transport along the classifier uses only the named $\hsame$ rows. The provenance of the topology source is recorded by $\mathsf{Pkg}$ and is not inferred from an ambient topological model.

Thus the topology source scope is exhausted by the displayed $\TopologyUp$ and $\ManifoldUp$ dependency rows.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:threemanifold-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[ThreeManifold JSJ ledger exactness]
\label{thm:threemanifold-jsj-ledger-exactness}
Every JSJ or geometric-decomposition entry visible in a $\ThreeManifoldUp$ packet is read from the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ decomposition ledger, the $\ManifoldUp$ source row, the $\TopologyUp$ source row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, the $\Cont$ continuation rows, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. The ledger does not assert a checked geometrisation bridge and does not expose a host decomposition theorem.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ThreeManifoldUp.ThreeManifoldFiniteCarrier\_jsj\_ledger\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Inspect a decomposition entry. Its inputs are the displayed manifold and topology rows, and its output is a finite ledger row inside the same packet.

Transport of that entry follows the componentwise classifier. The $\hsame$ rows move the manifold, topology, decomposition, continuation, and package coordinates together.

The readback therefore has no route to a host JSJ theorem or checked geometrisation bridge; it contains exactly the displayed ledger entry and its dependency provenance.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ThreeManifold chart-transition ledger obligation]
\label{thm:threemanifold-chart-transition-ledger-obligation}
Every chart-transition row or piece-boundary row consumed by the visible
JSJ/geometric decomposition ledger of a $\ThreeManifoldUp$ packet is read
through the displayed $\ManifoldUp$ chart row, the $\TopologyUp$ open-set row,
componentwise $\hsame$ transport, finite $\Cont$ routing, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$
provenance. The ledger cannot introduce a host atlas, unlogged triangulation,
or external geometrisation object.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ThreeManifoldUp.ThreeManifoldFiniteCarrier\_chart\_transition\_ledger\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier. A chart-transition or piece-boundary row appears
only as a coordinate of the displayed decomposition ledger, and the carrier
places that ledger beside the $\ManifoldUp$ and $\TopologyUp$ source rows.

Route the transition row through those two displayed sources. The manifold row
supplies the chart coordinate, the topology row supplies the open-set
coordinate, and the finite $\Cont$ rows carry the resulting readback into the
visible decomposition ledger.

Componentwise $\hsame$ transport compares only the displayed coordinates, while
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ records the provenance of the two dependency certificates. Row
exhaustion from \autoref{thm:threemanifold-certificate-boundary} then rules out
any host atlas, unlogged triangulation, or external geometrisation coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ThreeManifold certificate boundary]
\label{thm:threemanifold-certificate-boundary}
Every downstream consumer of $\ThreeManifoldUp$ reads only the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier, $\ManifoldUp$ dependency row, $\TopologyUp$ dependency row, decomposition ledger, $\hsame$ classifier rows, $\Cont$ continuation rows, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance of \autoref{def:threemanifold-carrier}. The certificate boundary excludes unlogged smooth, topological, or geometrisation data.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ThreeManifoldUp.ThreeManifoldFiniteCarrier\_certificate\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a downstream readback. The carrier obligation theorem confines the readback to the displayed finite packet rows.

Use the topology source theorem for the topology coordinate and the JSJ ledger theorem for the decomposition coordinate. Together they account for every dependency and ledger row that the consumer can inspect.

Because the packet contains no additional smooth, topological, or geometrisation coordinate, the downstream boundary is exactly the displayed BEDC surface.
\end{proof}
