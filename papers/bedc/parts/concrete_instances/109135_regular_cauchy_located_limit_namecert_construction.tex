\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy located-limit carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-located-limit-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyLocatedLimitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(W,D,Q,I,A,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row naming the inspected regular Cauchy segment and the requested precision.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the same window.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational readback row obtained from $W$ and $D$.
\item $I$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ row naming the displayed rational interval that traps the requested tail window.
\item $A$ is the $\CauchyRealLocatorUp$ row that relates the regular Cauchy tail to the located interval at the same dyadic tolerance.
\item $R$ is the downstream $\RealUp$ seal row, available only after $W,D,Q,I,A$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the window, dyadic ledger, rational readback, located interval, locator, and Real-seal rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through the regular Cauchy window, located interval, and locator rows to the Real-facing seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyLocatedLimitUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\CauchyRealLocatorUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyLocatedLimitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $W,D,Q,I,A,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a selected host limit, quotient real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, metric completeness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy located-limit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-located-limit-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyLocatedLimitUp$ carrier $L=(W,D,Q,I,A,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyLocatedLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of finite window exposure, dyadic tolerance admission, regular rational readback, located-interval trapping, Cauchy-real locator admission, Real-seal ordering, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. Every consumer must read the finite route
$$
W,D \longmapsto Q \longmapsto I,A \longmapsto R
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$ before treating the packet as a located limit of a regular Cauchy name.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-located-limit-carrier}. The rows $W,D,Q,I,A,R,H,C,P,N$ are the only coordinates visible to the local classifier.

The regular Cauchy side is read first. The window row $W$ supplies a finite observation, the dyadic row $D$ supplies the requested tolerance, and the readback row $Q$ is downstream of those two rows.

The located-limit side is then forced through $I$ and $A$. The interval row $I$ names the displayed trap for the requested tail window, while the locator row $A$ relates that trap to the same regular Cauchy data already used by $Q$.

Finally $R$ is reached through the displayed route, and $H,C,P,N$ preserve the route by transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since the excluded quotient, choice, host-limit, and completeness objects are absent from the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy located-limit real handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-located-limit-real-handoff}
An accepted $\RegularCauchyLocatedLimitUp$ carrier may expose its $\RealUp$ seal row $R$ only after the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $Q$, the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ trapping row $I$, and the $\CauchyRealLocatorUp$ row $A$ have been displayed through $H$ and $C$. Thus a Real or Completion consumer receives a finite located-limit handoff for the regular Cauchy name, not a quotient stream, a selected representative, a host limit, metric completeness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier projection. The Real-seal row $R$ is a coordinate of $L$, but it is downstream of $W,D,Q,I,A$ in the displayed route.

The rows $W,D,Q$ bind the regular Cauchy readback to finite windows and dyadic tolerances. The rows $I,A$ then bind the same data to a located interval and a locator witness, so the Real-facing read is not independent of the regular rational readback.

Transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve this ordering, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming for the same packet. No carrier coordinate can supply quotient equality, a chosen representative, a selected host limit, countable choice, trichotomy, metric completeness, or ambient completeness; the Real seal therefore exports only the displayed finite located-limit handoff.
\end{proof}
