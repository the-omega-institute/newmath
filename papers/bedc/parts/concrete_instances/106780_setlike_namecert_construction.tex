\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SetLikeUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-setlike-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Russell and Bourbaki-style set-membership grounding, the $\SetLikeUp$ packet records the finite BEDC carrier by which membership, subset implication, restricted comprehension, and extensional boundary reads are exposed without importing an ambient powerset, host quotient, or impredicative universe. It sits before the displayed family discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typelike-namecert}, the syntactic carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-firstorder-namecert}, and the satisfaction carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-modeltheory-namecert}. The packet names only finite membership source, classifier, implication, bounded comprehension, extensionality boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

\begin{definition}[SetLike carrier]
\label{def:setlike-carrier}
A $\SetLikeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(M,Q,I,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the membership-source row, naming the finite source from which element and set-read markers are admitted;
\item $Q$ is the classifier row for displayed membership decisions and membership-compatible reads;
\item $I$ is the subset-implication row, recording finite implications from one membership classifier to another;
\item $R$ is the restricted-comprehension row, admitting only classifiers already routed through $M,Q,I$;
\item $E$ is the extensionality-boundary ledger, recording when two displayed set rows may be compared by the carried membership classifier;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,Q,I,R,E$ together with the structural rows;
\item $C$ records $\Cont$ replay through which a consumer reads membership source, classifier, subset implication, restricted comprehension, and extensionality boundary data;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SetLikeUp$, $\TypeLikeUp$, $\FirstOrderUp$, $\ModelTheoryUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SetLikeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an ambient powerset, unrestricted separation, quotient equality of sets, selected representatives, global choice of elements, or a host universe of all sets.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SetLike NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:setlike-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SetLikeUp$ carrier
$$
S=(M,Q,I,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\SetLikeUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $S$, membership-source admission through $M$, classifier exposure through $Q$, subset-implication exposure through $I$, restricted-comprehension exposure through $R$, extensionality-boundary exposure through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $H$, $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:setlike-carrier}. The membership source $M$ and classifier $Q$ provide the only entry points for element-in-set reads.

The subset row $I$ records implication between displayed classifiers, while the restricted-comprehension row $R$ may bind only predicates already exposed through the same membership source and classifier route. The extensionality row $E$ records the comparison boundary for displayed set rows; it does not identify host sets outside the packet.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since no further coordinate is present, the NameCert obligations cannot require unrestricted separation, ambient powersets, quotient equality of sets, selected representatives, global element choice, or a universe of all sets.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SetLike membership route]
\label{thm:setlike-membership-route}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\SetLikeUp$ packet that reads membership, subset implication, restricted comprehension, or extensional comparison must factor through the finite route
$$
M,Q,I,R,E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently sibling reads by $\TypeLikeUp$, $\FirstOrderUp$, or $\ModelTheoryUp$ may use $\SetLikeUp$ only through the carried membership route, and cannot replace it by an ambient powerset, unrestricted comprehension principle, host quotient of sets, or external satisfaction relation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier $S=(M,Q,I,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. A membership consumer can reach an element-in-set assertion only by reading the source row $M$ and the classifier row $Q$.

Subset and comprehension reads then remain inside the same boundary. The row $I$ supplies finite implication between displayed membership classifiers, and $R$ admits restricted comprehension only when the classifier has already passed through $M,Q,I$.

Extensional reads are likewise bounded. The row $E$ permits comparison only through the displayed membership classifier, so it does not introduce quotient equality or an ambient host set identity.

Finally replay the structural rows. The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same finite route, while $\TypeLikeUp$, $\FirstOrderUp$, and $\ModelTheoryUp$ can cite the packet only through $P$ and $C$. No sibling read therefore bypasses $M,Q,I,R,E$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SetLike subset implication obligation]
\label{thm:setlike-subset-implication-obligation}
For every accepted $\SetLikeUp$ carrier $S=(M,Q,I,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, an accepted subset read is an implication row $I$ over the displayed membership classifier $Q$ and the membership-source row $M$. The read is admitted as finite $\BHist$ data only when the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay row $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance row $P$, and local $\NameCert_{\SetLikeUp}$ row $N$ all keep the same implication surface visible; it cannot import host subset equality or a global inclusion relation outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and isolate the subset row $I$. The only membership facts available to $I$ are the source row $M$ and the displayed classifier row $Q$.

Route the implication through the existing membership classifier. A consumer may read that an accepted membership assertion under one displayed row implies an accepted membership assertion under another, but the route is still the finite $M,Q,I$ ledger.

The structural rows close the boundary. Transport by $H$, replay by $C$, provenance by $P$, and local naming by $N$ preserve the same finite implication row, so no host subset equality or external inclusion predicate enters the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SetLike bounded comprehension obligation]
\label{thm:setlike-bounded-comprehension-obligation}
For every accepted $\SetLikeUp$ carrier $S=(M,Q,I,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, a comprehension read is admitted only through the restricted-comprehension row $R$ bounded by a displayed certified family over $M,Q,I$. The resulting $\BHist$ row remains tied to componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert_{\SetLikeUp}$ naming $N$, and therefore does not create a powerset or unrestricted separation principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the restricted-comprehension coordinate $R$. By \autoref{def:setlike-carrier}, $R$ is not an ambient class former; it is one row of the accepted finite packet.

The bound is supplied by the existing membership route. The source row $M$, classifier row $Q$, and implication row $I$ determine which displayed family may be read before $R$ is exposed.

Finally replay the structural rows. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same bounded family row, so the comprehension read remains finite BEDC data and never becomes a powerset, unrestricted separation rule, or host universe of subsets.
\end{proof}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SetLike subset-comprehension stability]
\label{thm:setlike-subset-comprehension-stability}
For every accepted $\SetLikeUp$ carrier $S=(M,Q,I,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, subset implication through $I$ and restricted comprehension through $R$ share the same membership classifier $Q$ over the membership source $M$. Any consumer that reads both rows must keep the common classifier visible through $H,C,P,N$, and the read cannot introduce a powerset row, unrestricted separation row, host subset equality, or an external comprehension principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted carrier and its subcarrier $S_{\partial}=(M,Q,I,R,E)$. The rows $I$ and $R$ both sit inside the same finite boundary and have access only to the membership source $M$ and classifier $Q$.

The subset implication row $I$ reads implication between displayed membership classifiers. The restricted-comprehension row $R$ may bind only a classifier already routed through $M,Q,I$; it therefore cannot choose a second classifier outside the packet.

The structural rows close the stability claim. Componentwise transport $H$, replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming $N$ preserve the same classifier route for both $I$ and $R$, so the combined read remains finite $\BHist$ data and does not become a powerset or unrestricted separation surface.
\end{proof}
