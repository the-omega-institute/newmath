\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton source lock]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-source-lock}
The singleton $\LieGroupUp$ source uses the single predicate
$L_{\emp}(h):\Longleftrightarrow \hsame(h,\emp)$ as the $\GroupUp$ carrier,
the $\ManifoldUp$ point and chart-domain carrier, and the $\LieGroupUp$
carrier. Its classifier is the same restricted $\hsame$ row
$R_{\emp}(h,k)$ from \autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-smooth-group-carrier};
no transported second source, auxiliary carrier, or duplicate classifier is
introduced.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-smooth-group-carrier}. The displayed
predicate $L_{\emp}$ is the only carrier predicate named there, and the
classifier is the displayed restricted row $R_{\emp}$.

Project the singleton group rows from
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-history-semantic-namecert}. These rows use
$\mathsf{BHist}$, $\emp$, $\mathsf{append}$, constant inverse, and the
restricted $\hsame$ classifier; their checked source is
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.GroupSingletonHistory\_laws}$.

Project the singleton chart rows from
\autoref{thm:manifold-singleton-namecert-obligation-package}. The chart
domain is the same predicate $\hsame(h,\emp)$, the chart value is constant,
and the overlap row is the singleton transition row.

Thus both projected packages identify their visible carrier with
$L_{\emp}$. The $\LieGroupUp$ package is assembled by sharing that predicate
and classifier rather than by transporting data across a second source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton exact ledger]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-exact-ledger}
For the singleton $\LieGroupUp$ package, the accepted ledger consists exactly
of $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\emp$, $\mathsf{append}/\Cont$, the singleton
$\GroupUp$ laws, the singleton chart domain and chart value, and the
singleton constant transition row. It excludes any standard bridge, any host
smooth-manifold equality, any extra carrier, and any external topology row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The source-lock theorem fixes the boundary of the ledger: all carrier and
classifier rows are projections of the one history predicate $L_{\emp}$ and
the restricted $\hsame$ relation.

The algebraic entries are exactly the singleton $\GroupUp$ rows. They are
supplied by \autoref{thm:group-singleton-history-semantic-namecert} and
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.GroupSingletonHistory\_laws}$, namely
$\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\emp$, $\mathsf{append}$, $\Cont$, and the
singleton group laws.

The smooth entries are exactly the singleton $\ManifoldUp$ rows. They are
supplied by \autoref{thm:manifold-singleton-namecert-obligation-package},
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.ManifoldUp.ManifoldSingleton\_semanticNameCert}$, and
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.ManifoldUp.ManifoldSingleton\_transition\_smoothness}$:
the singleton chart domain, constant chart value, and constant transition.

Finally,
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-operation-smoothness-obligation} accounts for
the operation rows by reading multiplication and inverse through the same
singleton atlas. No cited row provides a $\StdBridge$, host equality of smooth
manifolds, an additional carrier predicate, or an external topology, so none
belongs to the accepted ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton downstream export]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-downstream-export}
The packages $\ExpMapUp$, $\AdjointRepUp$, $\RootSystemUp$, and
representation certificates may consume the singleton $\LieGroupUp$ package
only through the exported carrier, classifier, operation, chart, and
transition rows. The singleton package makes no downstream claim of
connectedness, completeness, nontrivial Lie algebra, or standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-source-lock}, every visible carrier and
classifier row of the singleton $\LieGroupUp$ package is a projection of the
shared history predicate $L_{\emp}$ and the restricted $\hsame$ classifier.
These are the only source rows a dependent certificate can name.

By \autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-exact-ledger}, the remaining visible rows
are precisely the group operation rows, singleton chart rows, and singleton
transition row. The NameCert surface in
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-namecert-obligation} repacks those rows as the
public obligation surface for $\LieGroupUp$.

Therefore any $\ExpMapUp$, $\AdjointRepUp$, $\RootSystemUp$, or
representation certificate built over this singleton source can project only
those exported rows. Since the ledger contains no connectedness, completeness,
nontrivial Lie-algebra, or $\StdBridge$ row, no downstream consumer receives
such a claim from this package.
\end{proof}
