\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformlyLocatedFiniteCoverUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniformly-located-finite-cover-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\UniformlyLocatedFiniteCoverUp$ packet records the finite-cover substrate used by located compact-uniform-continuity arguments on closed bounded interval data. It refines the closed interval source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedinterval-namecert}, the finite ball-cover carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-real-cover-namecert}, the positive-radius ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-cover-lebesgue-number-namecert}, the compact uniform-continuity route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-uniform-continuity-modulus-namecert}, and the metric-ball observation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricball-namecert}. The packet names only finite located cover rows, local membership witnesses, Lebesgue-number handoff data, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming evidence.

\begin{definition}[Uniformly located finite cover carrier]
\label{def:uniformly-located-finite-cover-carrier}
A $\UniformlyLocatedFiniteCoverUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(I,F,L,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ source row, including endpoint order, rational and dyadic containment ledgers, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, and the $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $F$ is the $\FiniteRealCoverUp$ row whose ball cells, centers, dyadic radii, coverage witnesses, and refinement ledger cover the inspected interval windows.
\item $L$ is the $\FiniteCoverLebesgueNumberUp$ ledger row that records the positive-radius handoff for the displayed finite cover.
\item $M$ is the metric-ball and local locatedness membership row: each inspected interval window is paired with an admitted $\MetricBallUp$ cell and a finite locatedness witness for membership in that cell.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $I,F,L,M,C,P,N$ and for the endpoint, cover, radius, ball, membership, and local naming coordinates exposed by those rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from interval containment rows to finite cover coverage checks, from coverage checks to local locatedness membership, and from the cover ledger to the Lebesgue-number handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$, $\FiniteRealCoverUp$, $\FiniteCoverLebesgueNumberUp$, $\CompactUniformContinuityModulusUp$, $\MetricBallUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformlyLocatedFiniteCoverUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for arbitrary open-cover compactness, a choice-selected subcover, a host-set membership oracle, quotient real equality, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Uniformly located finite cover NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniformly-located-finite-cover-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\UniformlyLocatedFiniteCoverUp$ carrier $U=(I,F,L,M,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformlyLocatedFiniteCoverUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed tuple; finite cover coverage through $F$ over the interval source $I$; local locatedness preservation through the metric-ball membership row $M$; Lebesgue-number handoff through $L$; componentwise transport stability through $H$; displayed replay through $C$; provenance through $P$; local naming through $N$; and ledger nonescape from open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, host membership oracles, quotient real equality, trichotomy, and ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:uniformly-located-finite-cover-carrier}. The displayed tuple exposes the closed bounded interval source, finite cover row, positive-radius ledger, local metric-ball membership row, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Coverage is read only through $F$ after $I$ has supplied its endpoint, containment, window, readback, and Real-seal data. Locatedness preservation is read through $M$, where each finite cell membership remains attached to an admitted $\MetricBallUp$ row and to the same interval window.

The Lebesgue-number handoff is downstream of the displayed finite cover: $L$ may be consumed only after the cover row and local membership ledger have been replayed. The transport and replay rows then preserve the same interval-cover-membership route componentwise.

No excluded object is a coordinate of $U$. The local certificate therefore exports only the displayed finite located cover route, with $P$ and $N$ recording provenance and naming for that route.
\end{proof}
