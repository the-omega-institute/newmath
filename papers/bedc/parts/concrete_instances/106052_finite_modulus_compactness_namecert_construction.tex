\begin{theorem}[Finite modulus compactness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-modulus-compactness-namecert-obligations}

Every public finite-modulus compactness read must therefore pass through the displayed compact metric, finite-net equicontinuity, compact uniform-continuity modulus, and Ascoli handoff rows before it can export a downstream compactness certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The row $W$ is the only place where requested tolerances, compact-net radii, family indices, and dyadic support are tied together. Rows $K,E,U,A$ supply the four named chapter interfaces read by that schedule, while $H,C,P,N$ preserve transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

No further coordinate appears in the carrier. Hence the local NameCert obligations are exhausted by exactly the displayed rows, and every public read must traverse those rows before it can be consumed by a compactness or Ascoli-facing packet.
\end{proof}
