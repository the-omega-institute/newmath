\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CalculusUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-calculus-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Cartan's local differential calculus and the Bishop--Bridges constructive treatment of limits, derivatives, and integrals, the $\CalculusUp$ packet records the finite analysis root used by later derivative and integration chapters. It sits beside the real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the generic limit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-limit-namecert}, the continuous-map surface used throughout the analysis region, the derivative carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-derivative-namecert}, the integral carrier, and the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. The packet names the common calculus substrate without importing a host function space, a global limit operator, quotient equality of functions, or an ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Calculus local-linearization carrier]
\label{def:calculus-local-linearization-carrier}
A $\CalculusUp$ local-linearization carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
\Lambda=(E,R,D,L,J,H,C,P,N)
$$
whose rows are the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback
row $R$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, the $\DerivativeUp$
finite difference-quotient row $L$, the $\RiemannIntegralUp$ finite
tagged-sum row $J$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed $\Cont$
replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and the local
$\NameCert_{\CalculusUp}$ row $N$. The classifier compares accepted packets
only through these rows. It has no coordinate for a host differentiability
theorem, selected antiderivative, quotient real equality, or ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Calculus derivative continuation obligation]
\label{thm:calculus-derivative-continuation-obligation}
Every accepted $\CalculusUp$ local-linearization carrier
$\Lambda=(E,R,D,L,J,H,C,P,N)$ exposes a derivative-facing continuation only
after the $\RealUp$ seal $E$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, $\DerivativeUp$ difference-quotient
row $L$, $\hsame$ transport $H$, $\Cont$ replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local
NameCert row $N$ have been displayed. The read cannot export a host
differentiability theorem, quotient real equality, or ambient real
completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:calculus-local-linearization-carrier}. The derivative-facing row
is $L$, and its tolerance and value data are visible only together with the
real seal $E$, regular-rational readback $R$, and dyadic ledger $D$.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same
finite packet. Since none of those rows contains a host differentiability
theorem or quotient real equality, the continuation remains a displayed
finite difference-quotient route.

The Riemann row $J$ may coexist in the packet, but it does not supply
derivative data. Thus every derivative consumer must read through
$E,R,D,L,H,C,P,N$ before any derivative-facing conclusion is public.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Calculus integral finite-sum obligation]
\label{thm:calculus-integral-finite-sum-obligation}
Every accepted $\CalculusUp$ local-linearization carrier
$\Lambda=(E,R,D,L,J,H,C,P,N)$ exposes an integral-facing read only through the
$\RiemannIntegralUp$ finite tagged-sum row $J$ together with the $\RealUp$
seal $E$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger
$D$, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. The read
does not select an antiderivative and does not import quotient real equality
or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $J$ from the carrier. It records the partition, tags, finite sum, and
endpoint handoff as displayed calculus data, so an integral consumer cannot
bypass the finite-sum route.

The rows $E,R,D$ provide the real-facing seal, regular-rational comparison,
and dyadic tolerance ledger used to interpret that sum. The structural rows
$H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite route under accepted transport and replay.

The derivative row $L$ remains separate. Because no coordinate of
$\Lambda$ contains a selected antiderivative, host integration functional,
quotient real equality, or ambient completeness theorem, the integral
obligation is exactly the finite-sum route through $J$ and the displayed
support rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Calculus limit real-seal obligation]
\label{thm:calculus-limit-real-seal-obligation}
Every $\RealUp$-facing limit read exported by an accepted $\CalculusUp$
local-linearization carrier factors through the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$
readback row $R$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, derivative row $L$
or Riemann finite-sum row $J$, terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$, $\hsame$
transport, $\Cont$ replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows. No such read
may use a host limit theorem, quotient real equality, selected antiderivative,
or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness as local data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from a Real-facing calculus read. The carrier exposes $E$ as the only
terminal real seal, so every such read must arrive at $E$ through the displayed
regular-rational and dyadic rows $R,D$.

There are two calculus sources for that seal. A derivative read uses $L$ and
the finite difference-quotient route; an integral read uses $J$ and the
finite tagged-sum route. Both sources are transported and replayed only by
$H$ and $C$, with provenance and local naming recorded by $P$ and $N$.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for host limits, quotient real equality,
selected antiderivatives, or ambient completeness, the real-seal obligation is
confined to the displayed BEDC rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Calculus carrier]
\label{def:calculus-carrier}
A $\CalculusUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(R,L,C,D,I,Q,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ scalar boundary used by all endpoint values, tolerances, finite sums, and error estimates.
\item $L$ is the $\LimitUp$ route for displayed convergence windows and downstream real seals.
\item $C$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ row naming the finite graph, pointwise continuity, and replay windows available to calculus consumers.
\item $D$ is the $\DerivativeUp$ local difference-quotient row, including the source point, target read, and remainder ledger.
\item $I$ is the $\IntegralUp$ finite-sum row, exposing the partition, tagged-read, and endpoint handoff data used by integration consumers.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row shared by the limit, derivative, and integral ledgers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for real, limit, continuous-map, derivative, integral, regular-rational, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from real reads to limits, from limits to continuous maps, from finite difference quotients to derivative reads, and from finite sums to integral handoffs.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CalculusUp$, $\RealUp$, $\LimitUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\DerivativeUp$, $\IntegralUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CalculusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier reads only $R,L,C,D,I,Q,H,T,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host function space, a selected global derivative operator, an unbounded integration functional, quotient equality of functions, an open-cover compactness principle, or ambient real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Calculus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:calculus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CalculusUp$ carrier $K=(R,L,C,D,I,Q,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CalculusUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, real-boundary exposure, limit-window readback, continuous-map replay, local-linear approximation routing, finite difference quotient readback, integral finite-sum handoff, regular-rational readback, componentwise transport, displayed replay, package provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:calculus-carrier}. The rows $R,L,C,D,I,Q,H,T,P,N$ are the full finite inventory available to a calculus consumer.

The local-linear approximation route is carried by $D$ and is read only after the real boundary $R$, limit route $L$, continuous-map row $C$, and regular-rational readback $Q$ have supplied the displayed point, tolerance, and remainder data. Thus a derivative-facing consumer sees a finite difference quotient route rather than a hidden global derivative operator.

The integral handoff is carried by $I$. Its partition, finite-sum, and endpoint rows are exposed before any real-facing value is consumed, and the same $Q$ row supplies the rational readback needed to compare finite sums with limit and derivative windows.

Finally $H,T,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly the displayed rows. Since no coordinate contains host function-space equality, ambient completeness, a selected derivative operator, or an unbounded integral functional, every accepted read remains inside the finite calculus packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Calculus continuous-map graph replay obligation]
\label{thm:calculus-continuous-map-graph-replay-obligation}
For every accepted $\CalculusUp$ carrier $K=(R,L,C,D,I,Q,H,T,P,N)$, a continuous-map read is public only after the consumer has displayed the $\RealUp$ boundary $R$, the $\LimitUp$ window route $L$, the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row $C$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $Q$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay $T$, provenance $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\CalculusUp}$ row $N$. A derivative or integral consumer using that continuous-map read must therefore factor through $R,L,C,Q,H,T,P,N$ before reading $D$ or $I$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:calculus-carrier}. The only row that names a continuous-map graph is $C$, and its endpoint values are typed by the real boundary $R$ and compared through the regular-rational readback $Q$.

The limit route $L$ supplies the finite convergence windows through which the graph row is replayed. The rows $H,T,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same displayed graph data.

Now apply \autoref{thm:calculus-namecert-obligations}. Since the derivative row $D$ and integral row $I$ are later consumers of the continuous-map graph, neither can use $C$ until the route through $R,L,C,Q,H,T,P,N$ is visible.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Calculus derivative-integral separation obligation]
\label{thm:calculus-derivative-integral-separation-obligation}
For every accepted $\CalculusUp$ carrier $K=(R,L,C,D,I,Q,H,T,P,N)$, the derivative row $D$ and the integral row $I$ are separate finite rows. The row $D$ may expose local difference-quotient and remainder data only through $R,L,C,Q,H,T,P,N$, while the row $I$ may expose finite-sum and endpoint handoff data only through those same support rows. Neither row supplies the other's conclusion, and the carrier contains no host fundamental theorem connecting them.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project \autoref{def:calculus-carrier}. The derivative data is stored in $D$, and the integral data is stored in $I$; they are distinct coordinates of the finite packet.

Use \autoref{thm:calculus-derivative-continuation-obligation} for the derivative-facing route and \autoref{thm:calculus-integral-finite-sum-obligation} for the integral-facing route. Each theorem reads its own calculus row together with the real, limit, continuous-map, regular-rational, transport, replay, provenance, and naming support.

\autoref{thm:calculus-namecert-obligations} lists the full local surface and includes no coordinate for a host fundamental theorem. Hence a consumer cannot derive an integral conclusion from $D$, cannot derive a derivative conclusion from $I$, and cannot import a global theorem to identify the two reads.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Calculus RegSeqRat readback root obligation]
\label{thm:calculus-regseqrat-readback-root-obligation}
Every real-facing value exported by an accepted $\CalculusUp$ carrier $K=(R,L,C,D,I,Q,H,T,P,N)$ must display the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $Q$ together with the $\RealUp$ boundary $R$ and the dyadic tolerance route inherited by the regular-rational readback. This readback is required before any limit, derivative, or integral read is exported from $L$, $D$, or $I$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier of \autoref{def:calculus-carrier}. The terminal real boundary is $R$, and the common rational readback row is $Q$; no other coordinate converts limit, derivative, or integral data into a real-facing value.

The earlier real-seal theorem \autoref{thm:calculus-limit-real-seal-obligation} already forces Real-facing limit reads through regular-rational and dyadic tolerance data. The derivative and integral obligations similarly keep their finite quotient and finite-sum reads attached to the displayed real and rational support rows.

Finally use \autoref{thm:calculus-namecert-obligations}. The local NameCert surface lists regular-rational readback as a root obligation, so every export from $L,D,I$ must first pass through $Q$ with $R$ and the underlying dyadic tolerance route visible.
\end{proof}
