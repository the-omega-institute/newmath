Following Cartan's local differential calculus and the Bishop--Bridges constructive treatment of limits, derivatives, and integrals, the $\CalculusUp$ packet records the finite analysis root used by later derivative and integration chapters. It sits beside the real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the generic limit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-limit-namecert}, the continuous-map surface used throughout the analysis region, the derivative carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-derivative-namecert}, the integral carrier, and the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. The packet names the common calculus substrate without importing a host function space, a global limit operator, quotient equality of functions, or an ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Calculus carrier]
\label{def:calculus-carrier}
A $\CalculusUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(R,L,C,D,I,Q,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ scalar boundary used by all endpoint values, tolerances, finite sums, and error estimates.
\item $L$ is the $\LimitUp$ route for displayed convergence windows and downstream real seals.
\item $C$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ row naming the finite graph, pointwise continuity, and replay windows available to calculus consumers.
\item $D$ is the $\DerivativeUp$ local difference-quotient row, including the source point, target read, and remainder ledger.
\item $I$ is the $\IntegralUp$ finite-sum row, exposing the partition, tagged-read, and endpoint handoff data used by integration consumers.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row shared by the limit, derivative, and integral ledgers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for real, limit, continuous-map, derivative, integral, regular-rational, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from real reads to limits, from limits to continuous maps, from finite difference quotients to derivative reads, and from finite sums to integral handoffs.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CalculusUp$, $\RealUp$, $\LimitUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\DerivativeUp$, $\IntegralUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CalculusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier reads only $R,L,C,D,I,Q,H,T,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host function space, a selected global derivative operator, an unbounded integration functional, quotient equality of functions, an open-cover compactness principle, or ambient real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Calculus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:calculus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CalculusUp$ carrier $K=(R,L,C,D,I,Q,H,T,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CalculusUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, real-boundary exposure, limit-window readback, continuous-map replay, local-linear approximation routing, finite difference quotient readback, integral finite-sum handoff, regular-rational readback, componentwise transport, displayed replay, package provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:calculus-carrier}. The rows $R,L,C,D,I,Q,H,T,P,N$ are the full finite inventory available to a calculus consumer.

The local-linear approximation route is carried by $D$ and is read only after the real boundary $R$, limit route $L$, continuous-map row $C$, and regular-rational readback $Q$ have supplied the displayed point, tolerance, and remainder data. Thus a derivative-facing consumer sees a finite difference quotient route rather than a hidden global derivative operator.

The integral handoff is carried by $I$. Its partition, finite-sum, and endpoint rows are exposed before any real-facing value is consumed, and the same $Q$ row supplies the rational readback needed to compare finite sums with limit and derivative windows.

Finally $H,T,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly the displayed rows. Since no coordinate contains host function-space equality, ambient completeness, a selected derivative operator, or an unbounded integral functional, every accepted read remains inside the finite calculus packet.
\end{proof}
