\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompactUniformContinuityModulusUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-compact-uniform-continuity-modulus-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges compact metric continuity, the $\CompactUniformContinuityModulusUp$ packet records the concrete modulus extracted from compact finite-net data and displayed pointwise continuity rows. It consumes the compact metric finite-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the graph and pointwise-radius surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the finite compact-uniform-continuity routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-uniform-continuity-scheme-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-uniform-modulus-extractor-namecert}. The packet names the modulus object itself, not an ambient open-cover theorem or a host-chosen Lebesgue number.

\begin{definition}[Compact uniform continuity modulus carrier]
\label{def:compact-uniform-continuity-modulus-carrier}
A $\CompactUniformContinuityModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(K,F,L,D,R,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the compact finite-net row, including the displayed center bundle and covering radii used by the compact source.
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph row with pointwise continuity radii attached to the same compact metric carrier.
\item $L$ is the local modulus ledger, assigning to each center of $K$ a displayed source radius and target tolerance comparison read from $F$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger for the requested target tolerance, the local radii, the finite lower-bound comparison, and the final source tolerance.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row used only to read the rational-radius and target-distance comparisons after the dyadic ledger has been displayed.
\item $U$ is the uniform-modulus output row, recording the source tolerance produced by the finite compact-net and local-modulus fold.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact source, continuous-map graph, local modulus rows, dyadic ledger, real seal, uniform-modulus output, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a tolerance request through $K,F,L,D,R,U,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactUniformContinuityModulusUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactUniformContinuityModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, selected Lebesgue numbers, sequential compactness, host modulus functions, choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact uniform continuity modulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-continuity-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CompactUniformContinuityModulusUp$ carrier $M=(K,F,L,D,R,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactUniformContinuityModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the compact finite-net row, continuous-map graph row, local modulus ledger, dyadic tolerance ledger, real-seal comparison row, uniform-modulus output, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item source lock, requiring $K$ and the source of $F$ to name the same compact metric carrier before any center radius or graph row is read;
\item local modulus coverage, requiring every center selected by $K$ to have a corresponding row in $L$ and every row of $L$ to be read from the pointwise continuity surface of $F$;
\item dyadic fold exactness, requiring the source tolerance to be computed through $D$ from the displayed finite local-radius ledger rather than by a host minimum or selected Lebesgue number;
\item real-seal boundary, requiring $R$ only after the dyadic tolerance route has exposed the rational comparisons needed by the output row $U$;
\item structural replay, requiring $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows to preserve the same modulus extraction route;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, selected Lebesgue numbers, sequential compactness, host modulus functions, choice, and ambient completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No compact uniform continuity modulus obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:compact-uniform-continuity-modulus-carrier}. The compact source row $K$ supplies only a finite center bundle and covering radii, while the continuous-map row $F$ supplies graph reads and pointwise continuity radii over the same source.

The local modulus ledger $L$ attaches the pointwise radius information to the displayed centers. Thus each center used by the compact-net row has an explicit continuity row, and no center outside the finite bundle can affect the extracted modulus.

The dyadic fold is carried by $D$. It compares the requested target tolerance with the centerwise radii and records the finite lower-bound route to the source tolerance. The $\RealUp$ seal row $R$ is read only after these dyadic comparisons have been displayed.

The output row $U$ is therefore a tolerance-only modulus obtained from finite packet data. The transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows preserve this route, so open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, selected Lebesgue numbers, sequential compactness, host modulus functions, choice, and ambient completeness have no coordinate in the certificate.
\end{proof}
