\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore finite-tail scale-window pullback exactness]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-scale-window-pullback-exactness}
Let two finite-tail $\RealUp$ L10 reads have $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ faces obtained from displayed DyadicRatCore packets by finite positive-unary exponent widenings. Suppose the two reads share the same terminal scale-window packet of \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-scale-window-packet} and the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-request section. The exactness is only the finite BEDC pullback exactness of the displayed route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp.
$$
It exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host rational equality, ambient completeness row, or $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply scale-window terminal fusion, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-terminal-fusion}. The common terminal scale-window packet identifies the widened DyadicRatCore comparison face through displayed scale transports, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ data.

Next apply finite-tail window factorization, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-window-factorization}. Use finite-tail window confluence, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-window-confluence}, on the two factorizations. The shared terminal scale-window packet already fixes the DyadicRatCore and StreamName faces, while the hypothesis fixes the RegSeqRat finite-request section.

Finally invoke the StreamName and Real completion pullback rows, \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback}, \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-classifier-pullback}, and \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}. These rows identify the Real-facing boundary exactly from the common four faces and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

The cited exclusion clauses remove quotient stream equality, selected limits, host rational equality, ambient completeness, and Real completeness from each step. Therefore the two reads determine the same finite pullback route and nothing outside the displayed BEDC rows.
\end{proof}
