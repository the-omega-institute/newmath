\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore finite-tail common scale-window reduction]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-common-scale-window-reduction}

The reduction exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host rational equality, ambient completeness row, or $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}

Now apply RegSeqRat bridge handoff exactness, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-bridge-handoff-exactness}. The exclusion clauses of terminal fusion, factorization, and bridge handoff are preserved by this reduction. Hence the argument does not assert quotient stream equality, selected limits, host rational equality, ambient completeness, or any $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{proof}
