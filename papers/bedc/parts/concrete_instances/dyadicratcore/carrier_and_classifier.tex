\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicRatCoreUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}

The $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ surface names the dyadic rational source layer used by
the Phase Real and Completion interface in \autoref{sec:roadmap-l10-construction-order}.
Following the Bishop--Bridges constructive-analysis treatment of dyadic
rational approximants, the chapter consumes the signed integer and rational
certificate surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-int-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert} through its mantissa and
denominator ledger. It keeps each denominator exponent as visible unary
history data and never identifies dyadics by a quotient carrier.

\section{Carrier and classifier}
\label{sec:dyadicratcore-carrier}

\begin{definition}[DyadicRatCore signed mantissa carrier]
\label{def:dyadicratcore-carrier}
A $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ carrier row is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$(m,e,\ell,P)$ where $m$ is a signed $\RatUp$ mantissa row, $e$ is a positive
$\UnaryHistory$ exponent row, $\ell$ is the denominator ledger displaying the
unary tower for $2^e$, and $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the
$\IntUp$ and $\RatUp$ source certificates. The intended value of the packet is
the dyadic rational $m\cdot 2^{-e}$, but the carrier stores only the visible
mantissa row, positive unary exponent row, denominator ledger, and provenance.

The carrier contains no quotient class of rational pairs, no hidden integer
normal form, and no imported real-number endpoint. A consumer can read a
dyadic value only by projecting the displayed $\RatUp$ mantissa and unary
denominator rows through the visible $\Cont$ ledger.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore Int/Rat sibling route]
\label{thm:dyadic-rat-core-rat-int-sibling-route}
Every public read of an accepted $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ packet $D=(m,e,\ell,P)$ factors the signed mantissa through the consumed $\RatUp$ row, the sign and integer provenance through the consumed $\IntUp$ row, and the exponent and denominator ledger through the visible unary-history route. The read has no coordinate for a quotient rational, host integer normal form, or $\RealUp$ endpoint.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the four carrier fields in \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-carrier}. The mantissa field $m$ is already a signed $\RatUp$ row, while the provenance field $P$ records the attached $\IntUp$ and $\RatUp$ source certificates cited at the start of this chapter.

The exponent field $e$ and ledger field $\ell$ give the unary-history route for the displayed denominator. A public read can therefore inspect the dyadic packet only by following the mantissa, integer-provenance, rational-provenance, exponent, and ledger rows that are already part of $D$.

Finally use the carrier exclusion in \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-carrier}. Since the carrier contains no quotient class of rational pairs, no host integer normal form, and no imported real-number endpoint, the sibling route through $\IntUp$ and $\RatUp$ is the complete public route for the finite dyadic source layer.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[DyadicRatCore common-exponent classifier]
\label{def:dyadicratcore-classifier}
For two accepted dyadic packets
$D=(m,e,\ell,P)$ and $D'=(m',e',\ell',P')$, the predicate
$\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreClassifier}(D,D')$ is the common-exponent scaling
classifier: it supplies a positive unary exponent row $E$ above both $e$ and
$e'$, transported denominator ledgers from $\ell$ and $\ell'$ to $E$, and a
$\RatUp$ classifier row between the two scaled mantissas
$m\cdot 2^{E-e}$ and $m'\cdot 2^{E-e'}$.

The classifier is a BEDC predicate over displayed scaling rows. It is not
quotient equality of rational pairs, and it does not collapse the two packets
to a host rational number.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCoreClassifier}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore denominator ledger transport]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-denominator-ledger}
Let $D=(m,e,\ell,P)$ be an accepted $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ packet, and let
$D'=(m',e',\ell',P')$ be obtained from $D$ by displayed $\hsame$ transport of
the mantissa, exponent, denominator ledger, and provenance rows. Then $e'$ is
again a positive unary exponent row, $\ell'$ is the transported denominator
ledger for $2^{e'}$, and every finite consumer window that read the denominator
of $D$ can read the denominator of $D'$ through the transported $\Cont$ route.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCoreCarrier\_denominator\_ledger\_transport}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCorePacket\_denominator\_ledger\_transport}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCore\_denominator\_ledger\_transport\_window}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCoreCarrier\_denominator\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier row of \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-carrier}. The
positivity of $e$ is a displayed unary-history row, so transporting it along
the supplied $\hsame$ comparison gives the corresponding positive row for
$e'$.

The denominator ledger is finite and is indexed by the same unary exponent
tower. Transport each ledger entry along the displayed exponent comparison and
repack the entries with the transported $\Pkg$ provenance.

Finally transport the finite $\Cont$ route used by a consumer window. The
window reads only the visible denominator ledger entries, so the transported
route reads exactly the denominator rows of $D'$ and introduces no quotient or
real endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore common-exponent window exactness]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-common-exponent-window-exactness}
Let $D=(m,e,\ell,P)$ and $D'=(m',e',\ell',P')$ be accepted
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ packets. Any displayed common-exponent witness for
$\mathsf{DyadicRatCoreClassifier}(D,D')$ determines a finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ window whose rows are exactly the common unary exponent row,
the two transported denominator ledgers, the two scaled mantissa rows, the
$\RatUp$ classifier row comparing $m\cdot 2^{E-e}$ with
$m'\cdot 2^{E-e'}$, and the attached $\Cont$ and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.
Conversely, every consumer-visible classifier read of the two packets factors
through such a window. It has no row from which to read a quotient rational, a
host integer normal form, or a $\RealUp$ endpoint.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCoreCommonExponentWindow\_exactness}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCoreClassifier\_common\_exponent\_transport}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCoreCarrier\_common\_exponent\_window\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Unpack \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-classifier}. A classifier witness displays
the common unary exponent $E$, the two scaling rows from $e$ and $e'$ to $E$,
and the two denominator ledgers transported along those scaling rows.

The same witness also displays the scaled mantissas and the single $\RatUp$
classifier row comparing them. Repack these rows with the displayed $\Cont$
routes and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance inherited from the two accepted
packets.

For the reverse direction, inspect the carrier inventory in
\autoref{def:dyadicratcore-carrier}. A consumer-visible classifier read can
project only the packet rows named there and the classifier rows named in
\autoref{def:dyadicratcore-classifier}. Those rows are precisely the common
exponent, denominator-ledger, scaled-mantissa, $\RatUp$, $\Cont$, and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows above, so the read factors through the same finite window
and cannot access any quotient rational, host integer normal form, or
$\RealUp$ endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore distance-window stability]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-distance-window-stability}
Let $D_0,D_1,D'_0,D'_1$ be accepted $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ packets such that
$D_i$ is transported to $D'_i$ by displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows
for $i=0,1$. Any finite observation window that computes the dyadic distance
$|D_0-D_1|$ or the dyadic absolute value $|D_0|$ from common-exponent mantissa
scaling is stable under this transport: the transported window computes
$|D'_0-D'_1|$ or $|D'_0|$ from the transported mantissa, exponent, denominator,
and provenance rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCoreCarrier\_distance\_window\_stability}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCoreCarrier\_distance\_window\_transport}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCoreCarrier\_distance\_window\_transport\_closure}
\begin{proof}
Choose the common exponent displayed by the finite observation window. The
classifier of \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-classifier} makes the scaling rows
visible, so the mantissa difference and sign split are ordinary $\RatUp$ rows
inside the same window.

Transport the two input packets componentwise through the supplied $\hsame$
rows and transport the scaling ledger through the corresponding $\Cont$ routes.
The $\Pkg$ rows keep the same $\IntUp$ and $\RatUp$ source certificates attached
to the transported packet.

The distance and absolute-value observations therefore recompute the same
finite rational expression at the transported exponent. Since the window has
no access to a quotient carrier or real completion endpoint, stability is
exactly the repacking of those displayed rows.
\end{proof}
