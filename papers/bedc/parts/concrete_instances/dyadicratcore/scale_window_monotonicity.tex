\begin{definition}[DyadicRatCore scale-window packet]
\label{def:dyadicratcore-scale-window-packet}
The DyadicRatCore scale-window packet is the finite dyadic observation packet consisting of a signed $\RatUp$ mantissa row, a positive $\UnaryHistory$ exponent row, the comparison window at that exponent, the scale transport row to any displayed wider exponent window, the componentwise $\hsame$ rows comparing the transported dyadic observations, the displayed $\Cont$ replay row, the $\Pkg$ provenance row, and the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ naming row.

The packet is consumed by $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ as a finite dyadic source. It carries no host rational order and no quotient equality coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore scale-window monotonicity]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-monotonicity}
Widening the positive unary exponent window of a DyadicRatCore scale-window packet preserves the dyadic comparison row consumed by $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$. The preserved row is transported only by the displayed scale transport, componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ rows of \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-scale-window-packet}.

No host rational order, quotient equality, completed Real endpoint, or ambient completeness row is imported by the widened window.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-standard-bridge-denominator-refinement-stability}. It transports the signed mantissa, common-exponent classifier, distance window, radius row, carrier data, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ readback, and provenance across a displayed positive-unary denominator refinement.

Next apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-standard-bridge-denominator-refinement-composition}. Repeated widening of the exponent window is therefore the composite of the same displayed finite transports, not a comparison in an ambient host order.

Use \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-bridge-handoff-exactness}. It identifies the dyadic comparison rows consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ with exactly the public DyadicRatCore envelope rows, and the same finite source surface is available to $\StreamNameUp$ through the public bridge consumer boundary.

The rows left by these references are precisely the rows of \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-scale-window-packet}. Their exclusion clauses remove host rational order, quotient equality, completed Real endpoints, and ambient completeness from the widened window.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore scale-window cofinality]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-cofinality}
Any finite chain of positive-unary exponent-window widenings of a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ scale-window packet remains on the same cofinal dyadic comparison route consumed by $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$. The terminal packet of the chain is reached only through displayed scale transports, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ data.

The cofinal route imports no host rational order, quotient equality, completed $\RealUp$ endpoint, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply scale-window monotonicity, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-monotonicity}, to each displayed widening in the finite chain. Each step preserves the dyadic comparison row consumed by $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$.

Next use \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-standard-bridge-denominator-refinement-composition}. It composes the finite positive-unary denominator refinements into one displayed scale-transport route from the initial packet to the terminal packet.

Now apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-bridge-handoff-exactness}. The terminal comparison row remains the same public DyadicRatCore envelope read by $\RegSeqRatUp$, with the same finite source surface available to $\StreamNameUp$.

Therefore the whole finite widening chain is cofinal in the displayed dyadic comparison route, and the exclusion clauses from the cited theorems remove host rational order, quotient equality, completed Real endpoints, and ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore scale-window terminal fusion]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-terminal-fusion}
If two finite chains of positive-unary exponent-window widenings of a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ scale-window packet have a common terminal exponent, then they fuse into the same terminal DyadicRatCore comparison packet for $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumers. The fused packet is reached only through the displayed scale transports, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ rows of \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-scale-window-packet}.

No host rational order, quotient equality, completed $\RealUp$ endpoint, hidden denominator selector, or ambient completeness row is imported by either chain.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply scale-window cofinality, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-cofinality}, to both finite widening chains. Each chain reaches its terminal comparison row through the same displayed scale transports, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local NameCert rows.

Use denominator-refinement composition, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-standard-bridge-denominator-refinement-composition}. Since the terminal exponent is common, the two composed refinement routes identify the same terminal positive-unary denominator window rather than two ambient host-order comparisons.

Now apply RegSeqRat bridge handoff exactness, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-bridge-handoff-exactness}. The terminal DyadicRatCore comparison packet handed to $\RegSeqRatUp$ is exactly the public envelope row, and the same finite source packet is available to $\StreamNameUp$.

The non-escape exclusions in the packet and the cited bridge theorems remove host rational order, quotient equality, completed Real endpoints, hidden denominator selectors, and ambient completeness. Therefore the two chains fuse at one terminal comparison packet for both consumers.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore scale-window consumer face determinacy]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-consumer-face-determinacy}
Let an accepted $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer read expose its dyadic face through a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ scale-window packet and a finite chain of positive-unary exponent-window widenings. Then the consumer-facing dyadic face is determined by the terminal scale-window packet: the signed mantissa row, exponent window, denominator ledger, common-exponent comparison, distance and radius window, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ row.

A second widening chain, $\RealUp$ seal read, or $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite request cannot introduce another dyadic face with different displayed rows over the same terminal packet. The consumer boundary therefore reads one dyadic source surface through $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$, without quotient stream equality, selected limits, host rational order, hidden denominator selectors, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply scale-window cofinality, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-cofinality}, to the finite positive-unary widening chain used by the consumer read. The read reaches its terminal packet only through displayed scale transports, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ row.

For two displayed widening chains, apply terminal fusion, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-terminal-fusion}. A common terminal packet fuses the signed mantissa row, exponent window, denominator ledger, common-exponent comparison, distance and radius window, and the displayed support rows into one terminal DyadicRatCore comparison packet.

For the $\RegSeqRatUp$ face, use \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-bridge-handoff-exactness}. The finite request consumes exactly the public DyadicRatCore envelope delivered by that terminal packet, so it has no independent dyadic row from which to read a different face.

For the $\RealUp$ face, apply the dependency route \autoref{thm:real-dyadic-streamname-regseqrat-dependency-route}. The Real seal-visible read factors through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer faces, so it inherits the same terminal DyadicRatCore packet rather than adding a separate dyadic source.

The exclusion clauses in the scale-window packet, terminal fusion, bridge handoff, and dependency route remove quotient stream equality, selected limits, host rational order, hidden denominator selectors, and ambient completeness. Hence every accepted consumer read over the terminal scale-window packet exposes the same dyadic face.
\end{proof}
