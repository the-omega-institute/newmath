\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore L10 sibling dependency totality]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-l10-sibling-dependency-totality}
Every accepted Phase Real or completion-facing consumer that reaches
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$ through a dyadic tolerance face
consumes exactly the signed $\RatUp$ mantissa row, positive $\UnaryHistory$
exponent row, denominator ledger, common-exponent classifier,
radius/distance windows, and displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$,

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the consumer-to-dyadic direction, apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-public-bridge-consumer-exactness}. It identifies
the public dyadic consumer inventory with the signed mantissa, positive unary
exponent, denominator ledger, common-exponent classifier, finite windows,
displayed history and sameness rows, readback, package provenance, and
certificate support already supplied by $\DyadicRatCoreUp$.

The standard-source bridge of
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-standard-source-bridge} keeps that inventory fixed
when the dyadic face is presented to sibling consumers. Then
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-bridge-handoff-exactness} gives the
RegSeqRat handoff exactness for the same endpoint, radius, and finite-window
rows.

For the reverse direction, start with precisely those $\DyadicRatCoreUp$

\autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback}
places the DyadicRatCore face, the finite StreamName schedule, the RegSeqRat

Finally apply

ordered route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
and excludes quotient rational or stream equality, host real equality,
selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness
rows, and $\RealUp$ completeness from the route. These exclusions are exactly
the non-rows listed in the theorem statement.
\end{proof}
