by \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-public-readback-envelope}. Conversely, every
public envelope row with precisely those coordinates supplies the finite

The bridge exports no quotient rational, host equality row, completion object,
or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DyadicRatCoreUp.DyadicRatCoreCarrier\_public\_bridge\_consumer\_exactness}
\begin{proof}

\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-public-readback-envelope}. It lists exactly the
mantissa, exponent, denominator, classifier, finite-window, carrier,
transport, readback, and provenance rows visible to a public
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ read.

\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scoped-source-boundary} to restrict the downstream

\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-public-observation-standard-bridge-boundary} for the

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore finite radius-window cofinality]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-radius-window-cofinality}
Every finite family of public $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ packets whose $\RegSeqRatUp$
or $\RealUp$ consumers read them through displayed directed-refinement radius
handoffs factors through one common monotone unary radius window. The common
window carries the shared refined exponent, denominator ledger, common-exponent
classifier, distance rows, $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier rows, $\hsame$ transports,
$\Cont$ readbacks, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance for the whole finite family.

The common window exports no quotient rational, host equality row, completed
$\RealUp$ endpoint, countable-choice selector, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the finite family of displayed directed-refinement handoffs. For the
empty family, take the vacuous finite window with no consumer rows. For a
singleton, apply
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-radius-monotone-window-extraction} to the
single public handoff and read its monotone unary radius window.

For the induction step, assume the first finite subfamily has already been
placed on one common monotone unary radius window. Compose that accumulated
window with the next directed-refinement handoff by
\autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-standard-bridge-denominator-refinement-composition}
and \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-radius-window-composition}. The
composition keeps the refined exponent, denominator ledger, classifier,
distance rows, carrier data, transports, readbacks, and provenance inside the
same displayed dyadic bridge inventory.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-directed-refinement-common-window} to identify
the composed bridge packet with a common monotone unary radius window for the
accumulated subfamily and the new handoff. The induction invariant is preserved
because the window is enlarged only by displayed denominator-refinement rows and
finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows.

Finally use \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-directed-refinement-radius-handoff} at the
$\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ consumer boundary. Its exclusion clause, together
with the exclusions in the bridge and radius-window composition results, rules
out quotient rationals, host equality rows, completed real endpoints,
countable-choice selectors, and real-completeness fields from the cofinal
window.
\end{proof}
