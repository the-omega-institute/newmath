\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore finite-tail window factorization]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-window-factorization}
Every finite-tail $\RealUp$ L10 window read whose $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ face is obtained from a displayed DyadicRatCore packet by a finite positive-unary exponent widening factors through the scale-window packet of \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-scale-window-packet}. The factorization first reads the widened $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation rows, then the fused $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window packet supplied by \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-terminal-fusion}, then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-request section supplied by \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-bridge-handoff-exactness}, and finally the $\RealUp$ L10 finite-window boundary of \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}.

The visible route is exactly the four-face finite-tail packet
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
with the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows. It exports no host rational order, quotient equality, selected limit, ambient completeness row, or $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the displayed finite positive-unary widening on the DyadicRatCore face. By scale-window cofinality, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-cofinality}, the widened observation remains on the same cofinal dyadic comparison route carried by the scale-window packet.

Next apply terminal fusion, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-terminal-fusion}. It identifies the terminal widened comparison packet as one fused $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window source, using only displayed scale transports, componentwise $\hsame$ rows, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ data.

Now use RegSeqRat bridge handoff exactness, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-regseqrat-bridge-handoff-exactness}. The fused terminal packet is read by $\RegSeqRatUp$ as the same public DyadicRatCore envelope, so the finite request section does not add an ambient order coordinate or a quotient-equality coordinate.

Finally pass to the Real L10 finite-window determinacy surface, \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}. That theorem consumes exactly the fused $\StreamNameUp$ packet, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and radius ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-request section, and the local $\RealUp$ seal over the displayed support rows.

The exclusion clauses of the cited DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and Real L10 theorems are stable under this composition. Hence the finite-tail read has the stated four-face factorization and cannot read host rational order, quotient equality, selected limits, ambient completeness, or Real completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore finite-tail window confluence]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-window-confluence}
Let two finite-tail $\RealUp$ L10 window reads have $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ faces obtained from displayed DyadicRatCore packets by finite positive-unary exponent widenings. Assume the two widened DyadicRatCore faces fuse to the same terminal scale-window packet of \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-scale-window-packet}. Then the two four-face routes
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
determine the same terminal finite pullback through the DyadicRatCore, StreamName, RegSeqRat, and Real boundary.

The equality is only equality over the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows. It asserts no quotient stream equality, no host rational equality, no selected limit, no ambient completeness, no metric-completeness datum, and no $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use terminal fusion, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-terminal-fusion}. The two finite positive-unary widenings have the same terminal scale-window packet, so their DyadicRatCore comparison faces expose one common terminal dyadic packet with the same displayed scale transports, $\hsame$ rows, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ rows.

Apply the finite-tail factorization theorem, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-tail-window-factorization}, to each read. Both reads therefore pass through the same four displayed faces: the common widened $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ packet, the fused $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window source, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-request section, and the $\RealUp$ L10 finite-window boundary.

Next use the StreamName terminal pullback, \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-four-object-terminal-pullback}. Since the StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and Real faces and the displayed support rows agree after terminal fusion, the two accepted routes factor through one terminal finite BEDC pullback.

Finally apply the classifier pullback, \autoref{thm:streamname-real-completion-classifier-pullback}, together with the Real L10 finite-window witness determinacy theorem, \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}. The public classifier and the Real L10 boundary are determined by exactly the common terminal StreamName packet, the DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledger, the RegSeqRat finite-request section, the local Real seal, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

The exclusion clauses in the cited DyadicRatCore, StreamName, RegSeqRat, and Real L10 boundary theorems are stable under this common terminal pullback. Thus confluence holds only on the displayed BEDC rows and cannot be read as quotient stream equality, selected limits, ambient completeness, metric completeness, host rational equality, or Real completeness.
\end{proof}
