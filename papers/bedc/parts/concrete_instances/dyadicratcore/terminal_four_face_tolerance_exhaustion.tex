\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore terminal four-face tolerance exhaustion]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-exhaustion}
Let $P$ be an accepted L10 terminal route whose four visible faces are the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and classifier row, and the local $\RealUp$ seal row over the same displayed
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
support. Then every public tolerance read of $P$ factors through exactly that $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger together with the three downstream faces and the displayed support rows.

No quotient rational, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, bridge row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row is an additional tolerance coordinate for the terminal route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-common-scale-window-four-face-pullback-correspondence}. It identifies the terminal route with exactly the four visible faces: the DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, the StreamName finite window, the RegSeqRat classifier and readback surface, and the Real seal row, all over the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

Next use \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-real-exit-no-fifth-source}. Once the four faces and support rows are fixed, the exit inventory has no fifth source from which another tolerance coordinate could be read.

The StreamName side keeps the tolerance coordinate inside the same finite packet. Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-ledger-exactness} and \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-normal-form}; these results say that the accepted open-phase read exposes the ledger through the four faces themselves, not through quotient stream equality, selected limits, ambient completion, or standard bridge data.

Now read the RegSeqRat and Real faces. The non-escape theorem \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-nonescape} confines the readback consumer to the displayed dyadic observation rows, StreamName window rows, RegSeqRat budget rows, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}. The terminal Real boundary determines exactly the fused StreamName packet, the DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledger, the RegSeqRat finite-request section, the local Real seal, and the displayed support. Hence every public tolerance read of $P$ factors through the DyadicRatCore ledger already present in the four-face packet, and none of the excluded quotient, selected-limit, choice, completeness, metric-completeness, bridge, or ambient $\RealUp$ rows can be added as a separate tolerance coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore terminal tolerance finite-support readback]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-tolerance-finite-support-readback}
Let $P$ be an accepted L10 terminal route whose visible faces are $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the local $\RealUp$ face over the same displayed
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
support. Then any public tolerance read of $P$ is determined by the displayed finite support rows: the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ signed mantissa, exponent, denominator, comparison, distance, and radius rows; the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows; the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and readback rows; the local $\RealUp$ seal row; and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

The readback exports no quotient rational equality, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, bridge row, aggregate formalstatus row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-exhaustion}. Every public tolerance read of $P$ factors through the four visible faces and the shared support rows, so it is enough to identify the finite support carried by those faces.

The DyadicRatCore support is finite. Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-l10-exit-ledger-finite-support-coverage}; the accepted four-face packet reads the signed mantissa, exponent, denominator, comparison, distance, radius, StreamName, RegSeqRat, Real, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows from the displayed finite inventory.

Now read the three downstream faces on the same inventory. The StreamName face is supplied by \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-section}. The RegSeqRat classifier and readback face is supplied by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-support-induction}. The local Real seal is supplied by \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}.

Finally use the no-fifth-source clause from \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-exhaustion}. Since all public tolerance reads already factor through the displayed four faces and those faces have just been read from the finite support rows, none of the excluded quotient, selected-limit, countable-choice, metric-completeness, bridge, aggregate formalstatus, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness rows can be exported by the readback.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore terminal tolerance projection]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-projection}
Let $P$ and $Q$ be accepted L10 terminal routes whose visible four-face packets expose the same $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and classifier row, the same local $\RealUp$ seal row, and the same displayed
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
support. Then the public tolerance reads of $P$ and $Q$ agree if and only if their $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger rows agree.

No quotient rational, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, bridge row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row can mediate a different tolerance read between the two terminal routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-exhaustion} to $P$ and to $Q$. Each public tolerance read is forced through the displayed four-face packet: the DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, the StreamName finite window, the RegSeqRat classifier and readback row, the local Real seal row, and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

For the forward direction, suppose the public tolerance reads agree. Use \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-common-scale-window-four-face-pullback-correspondence}. Since the StreamName, RegSeqRat, Real, and support faces are already the same, the pullback has only one remaining tolerance coordinate; the agreement of the public reads therefore identifies the DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger row.

For the reverse direction, suppose the DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger rows agree. The other three faces and all displayed support rows agree by hypothesis, so the same pullback theorem gives one common four-face packet for both routes. Reading the tolerance face through that common packet gives the same public tolerance read.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-real-exit-no-fifth-source}. The exit inventory has no fifth source that could alter the tolerance coordinate after the four visible faces and support rows have been identified. The StreamName rows \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-ledger-exactness} and \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-normal-form}, together with the RegSeqRat nonescape row \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-nonescape}, rule out external completion, quotient stream, quotient rational, selected-limit, countable-choice, bridge, metric-completeness, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore terminal tolerance pullback initiality]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-tolerance-pullback-initiality}
Let $P$ be an accepted L10 terminal route whose visible four-face packet exposes the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, a $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and readback row, a local $\RealUp$ seal row, and the displayed
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
support. Let $Q$ be any accepted L10 terminal route presenting the same $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and readback row, the same local $\RealUp$ seal row, and the same displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support. Then $Q$ factors uniquely through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger of $P$ if and only if the public tolerance read of $Q$ agrees with the terminal projection of $P$ supplied by \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-projection}.

This initiality exports no quotient rational equality, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, bridge row, aggregate formalstatus row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read both terminal routes through the same visible support. Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-exhaustion} to $P$ and $Q$; every public tolerance read is forced through the displayed four-face packet and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-tolerance-finite-support-readback} to replace each such read by the finite DyadicRatCore ledger, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, local Real seal, and support rows.

For the forward direction, assume $Q$ factors through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger of $P$. The StreamName face is already the same finite section, so \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness} identifies the two finite-window pullbacks. The RegSeqRat and Real faces are then locked by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-terminal-four-face-exactness} and \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-pullback-coherence}. With all four faces fixed, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-projection} gives agreement with the terminal projection of $P$.

For the reverse direction, assume the public tolerance read of $Q$ agrees with the terminal projection of $P$. By \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-projection}, agreement of public tolerance reads over the already shared StreamName, RegSeqRat, Real, and support faces is equivalent to agreement of the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger rows. The finite-support readback theorem identifies the only ledger rows available to $Q$, so the factorization through the DyadicRatCore ledger of $P$ is unique.

It remains to rule out external witnesses for a second factorization. The exhaustion theorem supplies no fifth source beyond the four visible faces, the finite-support readback supplies no hidden row outside the displayed inventory, the StreamName pullback exactness supplies no quotient stream coordinate, the RegSeqRat-Real terminal exactness supplies no quotient rational or selected-limit coordinate, and the Real terminal pullback coherence supplies no ambient completeness coordinate. Hence quotient rational equality, quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, metric completeness, bridge rows, aggregate formalstatus rows, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness cannot mediate another terminal tolerance factorization.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore terminal tolerance cofinal-refinement finality]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-tolerance-cofinal-refinement-finality}
Let $P$ be an accepted L10 terminal route whose visible faces are $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the local $\RealUp$ face over the same displayed
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
support. Let $R$ be any accepted cofinal refinement of $P$ preserving the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and readback row, the local $\RealUp$ seal row, the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support, and the public terminal status of the route. Then the public tolerance read of $R$ factors through the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger as $P$.

Equivalently, no accepted cofinal refinement with those preserved visible faces can introduce a new tolerance coordinate. The finality statement exports no quotient rational equality, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, bridge row, aggregate formalstatus row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read $R$ as a terminal route over the preserved visible support. Since the refinement keeps the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and readback row, the local $\RealUp$ seal row, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-tolerance-pullback-initiality} applies to $P$ and $R$.

The StreamName preservation clause identifies the finite-window side of the comparison. Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness}; the refinement has the same finite StreamName section and therefore cannot supply a second window through which a tolerance row could be read.

Next lock the RegSeqRat and Real faces. Theorem~\ref{thm:regseqrat-real-terminal-four-face-exactness} reads the preserved classifier, readback, and local Real rows as the same four-face terminal surface, and \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-pullback-coherence} rules out an ambient $\RealUp$ completion coordinate.

With all non-dyadic faces fixed, use \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-projection}. Equality of public tolerance reads is now exactly equality of the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger rows. The pullback initiality theorem then gives the desired factorization of the public tolerance read of $R$ through the ledger of $P$.

Finally apply the non-export clauses in the exhaustion, projection, and initiality results. Quotient rational equality, quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, metric completeness, bridge rows, aggregate formalstatus rows, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness are not visible tolerance coordinates for the terminal route or for its accepted cofinal refinements.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore terminal tolerance support minimality]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-tolerance-support-minimality}
Let $P$ be an accepted L10 terminal route whose visible faces are $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the local $\RealUp$ face over the displayed
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
support. Preserving the public terminal tolerance read of $P$ is equivalent to preserving exactly the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and readback row, the local $\RealUp$ seal row, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows.

Moreover, deleting any one of these rows cannot be repaired by quotient rational equality, quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, metric completeness, bridge rows, aggregate formalstatus, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the tolerance through its finite support. Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-tolerance-finite-support-readback}; every public tolerance read of $P$ is determined by the displayed DyadicRatCore ledger, the three downstream faces, and the shared support rows.

For the forward direction, suppose a presentation preserves the public terminal tolerance read. Use \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-projection}; over the already displayed StreamName, RegSeqRat, Real, and support faces, equality of public reads is exactly equality of the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger. Then apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-tolerance-pullback-initiality} to identify the unique terminal factorization through that ledger.

For the reverse direction, suppose the presentation preserves exactly the listed rows. The finite-support readback theorem gives the same public tolerance read from those rows, and \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-tolerance-cofinal-refinement-finality} keeps accepted cofinal refinements on the same ledger whenever the three downstream faces and displayed support are preserved.

It remains to show that no listed row is dispensable. The StreamName finite section is fixed by \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-section-pullback-exactness}; without it there is no finite-window coordinate through which the route can read the same tolerance packet. The RegSeqRat and Real faces are fixed by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-terminal-four-face-exactness} and \autoref{thm:real-l10-terminal-pullback-coherence}; without them the classifier, readback, and local seal no longer present the same terminal four-face surface.

Finally apply the no-fifth-source clauses from the exhaustion, projection, initiality, and cofinal-refinement finality theorems above. Quotient rational equality, quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, metric completeness, bridge rows, aggregate formalstatus, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness are not replacement coordinates for any deleted support row, so the displayed support is minimal for the public terminal tolerance read.
\end{proof}
