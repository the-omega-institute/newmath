\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore terminal four-face tolerance exhaustion]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-exhaustion}
Let $P$ be an accepted L10 terminal route whose four visible faces are the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and classifier row, and the local $\RealUp$ seal row over the same displayed
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
support. Then every public tolerance read of $P$ factors through exactly that $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger together with the three downstream faces and the displayed support rows.

No quotient rational, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, bridge row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row is an additional tolerance coordinate for the terminal route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-common-scale-window-four-face-pullback-correspondence}. It identifies the terminal route with exactly the four visible faces: the DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, the StreamName finite window, the RegSeqRat classifier and readback surface, and the Real seal row, all over the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

Next use \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-real-exit-no-fifth-source}. Once the four faces and support rows are fixed, the exit inventory has no fifth source from which another tolerance coordinate could be read.

The StreamName side keeps the tolerance coordinate inside the same finite packet. Apply \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-ledger-exactness} and \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-normal-form}; these results say that the accepted open-phase read exposes the ledger through the four faces themselves, not through quotient stream equality, selected limits, ambient completion, or standard bridge data.

Now read the RegSeqRat and Real faces. The non-escape theorem \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-nonescape} confines the readback consumer to the displayed dyadic observation rows, StreamName window rows, RegSeqRat budget rows, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}. The terminal Real boundary determines exactly the fused StreamName packet, the DyadicRatCore endpoint and radius ledger, the RegSeqRat finite-request section, the local Real seal, and the displayed support. Hence every public tolerance read of $P$ factors through the DyadicRatCore ledger already present in the four-face packet, and none of the excluded quotient, selected-limit, choice, completeness, metric-completeness, bridge, or ambient $\RealUp$ rows can be added as a separate tolerance coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore terminal tolerance finite-support readback]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-tolerance-finite-support-readback}
Let $P$ be an accepted L10 terminal route whose visible faces are $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the local $\RealUp$ face over the same displayed
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
support. Then any public tolerance read of $P$ is determined by the displayed finite support rows: the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ signed mantissa, exponent, denominator, comparison, distance, and radius rows; the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows; the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier and readback rows; the local $\RealUp$ seal row; and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

The readback exports no quotient rational equality, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, bridge row, aggregate formalstatus row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-exhaustion}. Every public tolerance read of $P$ factors through the four visible faces and the shared support rows, so it is enough to identify the finite support carried by those faces.

The DyadicRatCore support is finite. Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-l10-exit-ledger-finite-support-coverage}; the accepted four-face packet reads the signed mantissa, exponent, denominator, comparison, distance, radius, StreamName, RegSeqRat, Real, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows from the displayed finite inventory.

Now read the three downstream faces on the same inventory. The StreamName face is supplied by \autoref{thm:streamname-finite-observation-section}. The RegSeqRat classifier and readback face is supplied by \autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-tail-support-induction}. The local Real seal is supplied by \autoref{thm:real-l10-finite-window-witness-determinacy}.

Finally use the no-fifth-source clause from \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-exhaustion}. Since all public tolerance reads already factor through the displayed four faces and those faces have just been read from the finite support rows, none of the excluded quotient, selected-limit, countable-choice, metric-completeness, bridge, aggregate formalstatus, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness rows can be exported by the readback.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore terminal tolerance projection]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-projection}
Let $P$ and $Q$ be accepted L10 terminal routes whose visible four-face packets expose the same $\StreamNameUp$ finite window, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and classifier row, the same local $\RealUp$ seal row, and the same displayed
$$
\BHist,\quad \hsame,\quad \Cont,\quad \Pkg,\quad \NameCert
$$
support. Then the public tolerance reads of $P$ and $Q$ agree if and only if their $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger rows agree.

No quotient rational, quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, bridge row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row can mediate a different tolerance read between the two terminal routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-four-face-tolerance-exhaustion} to $P$ and to $Q$. Each public tolerance read is forced through the displayed four-face packet: the DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, the StreamName finite window, the RegSeqRat classifier and readback row, the local Real seal row, and the shared $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support.

For the forward direction, suppose the public tolerance reads agree. Use \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-common-scale-window-four-face-pullback-correspondence}. Since the StreamName, RegSeqRat, Real, and support faces are already the same, the pullback has only one remaining tolerance coordinate; the agreement of the public reads therefore identifies the DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger row.

For the reverse direction, suppose the DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger rows agree. The other three faces and all displayed support rows agree by hypothesis, so the same pullback theorem gives one common four-face packet for both routes. Reading the tolerance face through that common packet gives the same public tolerance read.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-terminal-real-exit-no-fifth-source}. The exit inventory has no fifth source that could alter the tolerance coordinate after the four visible faces and support rows have been identified. The StreamName rows \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-ledger-exactness} and \autoref{thm:streamname-open-phase-four-face-normal-form}, together with the RegSeqRat nonescape row \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-nonescape}, rule out external completion, quotient stream, quotient rational, selected-limit, countable-choice, bridge, metric-completeness, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinates.
\end{proof}
