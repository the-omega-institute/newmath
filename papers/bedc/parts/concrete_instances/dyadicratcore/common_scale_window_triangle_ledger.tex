\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore common scale-window triangle ledger]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-common-scale-window-triangle-ledger}
Let three accepted L10 consumers among the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ routes expose dyadic faces that each factor through pairwise $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ scale-window packets. Then the three faces admit one common finite directed-meet scale window. That window carries the signed mantissa row, refined positive-unary exponent row, denominator ledger, common-exponent comparison row, distance and radius rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ rows for all three consumer faces.

The triangle ledger exports no quotient rational, host epsilon equality, selected $\RealUp$ endpoint, countable-choice selector, metric-completeness row, or $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First place each pairwise dyadic face on a terminal scale-window packet by applying \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-terminal-fusion}. The pairwise packets therefore expose the same kind of signed mantissa, positive-unary exponent, denominator, comparison, transport, readback, provenance, and local NameCert rows.

Next apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-radius-window-directed-meet} to the finite family consisting of the three pairwise terminal packets. The directed meet supplies one common finite monotone radius window carrying the shared refined exponent, denominator ledger, common-exponent classifier, distance rows, carrier rows, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ readbacks, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local NameCert row.

Use \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-directed-refinement-radius-handoff} at each consumer boundary. It forces the $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ read to pass through the displayed radius handoff before any terminal seal can consume the dyadic packet, so the three L10 faces cannot split into separate ambient dyadic sources.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-l10-sibling-dependency-totality}. It reads the same finite dyadic rows through the route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp
$$
and identifies those rows as the dyadic source consumed by the L10 real-and-completion boundary.

The exclusion clauses of the terminal fusion, directed meet, directed-refinement handoff, and L10 route leave no row for quotient rationals, host epsilon equality, selected real endpoints, countable-choice selectors, metric-completeness data, or $\RealUp$ completeness. Hence the common window is exactly the finite triangle ledger stated above.
\end{proof}
