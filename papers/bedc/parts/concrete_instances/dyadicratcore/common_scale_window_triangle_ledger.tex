\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore common scale-window triangle ledger]

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First place each pairwise dyadic face on a terminal scale-window packet by applying \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-scale-window-terminal-fusion}. Next apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-finite-radius-window-directed-meet} to the finite family consisting of the three pairwise terminal packets. It forces the $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RealUp$ read to pass through the displayed radius handoff before any terminal seal can consume the dyadic packet, so the three L10 faces cannot split into separate ambient dyadic sources.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-l10-sibling-dependency-totality}. It reads the same finite dyadic rows through the route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \RealUp

The exclusion clauses of the terminal fusion, directed meet, directed-refinement handoff, and L10 route leave no row for quotient rationals, host epsilon equality, selected real endpoints, countable-choice selectors, metric-completeness data, or $\RealUp$ completeness. Hence the common window is exactly the finite triangle ledger stated above.
\end{proof}
