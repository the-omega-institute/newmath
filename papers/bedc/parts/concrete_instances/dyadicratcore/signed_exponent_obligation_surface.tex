\begin{definition}[DyadicRatCore signed-exponent carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-rat-core-signed-exponent-carrier}
The signed-exponent $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ carrier refines the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-carrier} to the finite packet
$$
Q=(m,e,\ell,W,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
where $m$ is the signed $\RatUp$ mantissa row, $e$ is the positive unary exponent row, $\ell$ is the denominator ledger for $2^e$, $W$ is the common-exponent comparison window of \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-classifier}, $R$ is the dyadic tolerance-radius row, $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay, $P$ records the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\IntUp$, $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ consumers, and $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ row. The packet has no quotient rational, host equality row, completed $\RealUp$ endpoint, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore common-exponent stability]
\label{thm:dyadic-rat-core-common-exponent-stability}
Let $Q$ and $Q'$ be signed-exponent $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ carriers whose mantissa, exponent, denominator ledger, tolerance-radius row, transport, replay, provenance, and local name are related by displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows. Any common-exponent comparison window for $Q$ factors through the same finite rows as the transported common-exponent comparison window for $Q'$, namely the signed mantissa rows, positive unary exponent rows, transported denominator ledgers, scaled $\RatUp$ mantissas, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the signed-exponent carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-signed-exponent-carrier}. Its comparison row $W$ is already the common-exponent classifier window of \autoref{def:dyadicratcore-classifier}.

Apply denominator ledger transport, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-denominator-ledger}, to the exponent and denominator rows. This transports the positive unary exponent and the ledger for $2^e$ without introducing a quotient rational or host normal form.

Use common-exponent window exactness, \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-common-exponent-window-exactness}. The transported classifier is determined by the transported exponent, ledgers, scaled mantissas, $\RatUp$ classifier row, $\Cont$ replay, and $\Pkg$ provenance. The displayed $\hsame$ rows therefore carry the same common-exponent comparison surface from $Q$ to $Q'$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore tolerance-radius monotonicity]
\label{thm:dyadic-rat-core-tolerance-radius-monotonicity}
For a signed-exponent $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ carrier $Q$, every displayed positive-unary refinement of the exponent row induces a monotone refinement of the tolerance-radius row $R$. The refined radius window remains a finite BEDC row over the denominator ledger, common-exponent comparison window, distance window, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ data, and it exports no host epsilon equality or completed $\RealUp$ endpoint.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the radius row $R$ through the signed-exponent carrier. It is attached to the same positive unary exponent and denominator ledger that define the common-exponent comparison window.

Apply the monotone-radius obligation of \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-monotone-radius-obligation}. A positive-unary refinement widens the denominator scale and transports the dyadic radius through displayed ledger rows.

Then apply the distance-window stability of \autoref{thm:dyadicratcore-distance-window-stability}. The distance and absolute-value observations remain inside the transported signed mantissa, common exponent, denominator ledger, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows. Hence the tolerance-radius refinement is monotone on the displayed finite packet and cannot read host epsilon equality or a completed real endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DyadicRatCore signed-exponent denominator-chain determinacy]
\label{thm:dyadicratcore-signed-exponent-denominator-chain-determinacy}
Let $Q=(m,e,\ell,W,R,H,C,P,N)$ be a signed-exponent $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ carrier. For any finite chain $Q=Q_0,Q_1,\ldots,Q_n$ in which each adjacent step is a displayed positive-unary refinement of the exponent row and transports the signed mantissa, denominator ledger, common-exponent comparison window, tolerance-radius row, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\DyadicRatCoreUp}$ row, the terminal denominator ledger, common-exponent comparison row, tolerance-radius row, and L10 dyadic face are determined by $Q$ alone. The same terminal surface is obtained for every subdivision and parenthesization of the finite refinement chain. This determinacy is a statement only about the finite BEDC rows of the signed-exponent carrier; it excludes quotient rational equality, host normalization, selected $\RealUp$ endpoints, countable choice, metric completeness, and ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by induction on the finite refinement chain. In the empty chain the terminal rows are exactly $\ell$, $W$, $R$, and the displayed L10 dyadic face of the carrier in \autoref{def:dyadic-rat-core-signed-exponent-carrier}.

For a successor step, apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-common-exponent-stability} to the adjacent carriers. This identifies the transported common-exponent comparison row with the same finite mantissa, exponent, denominator-ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-tolerance-radius-monotonicity} to the same adjacent refinement. The radius row is transported monotonically along the displayed positive-unary exponent refinement and remains attached to the denominator ledger and common-exponent window already fixed by the induction hypothesis.

Associativity of finite chain concatenation is therefore harmless at the paper level: subdividing a step or reparenthesizing consecutive steps only repeats the same adjacent comparison and radius transports. The terminal denominator ledger, comparison row, and radius row remain the rows forced by the original carrier.

Finally use \autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-signed-exponent-source-determinacy} and \autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-namecert-obligation-surface}. They identify the downstream $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ L10 dyadic face with exactly the displayed signed mantissa, positive unary exponent, denominator ledger, comparison, radius, transport, replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows, leaving no quotient rational, host normalization, selected endpoint, countable choice, metric-completeness, or ambient-completeness coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

The comparison and radius coordinates are fixed by \autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-common-exponent-stability} and \autoref{thm:dyadic-rat-core-tolerance-radius-monotonicity}. They say that the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ reads consume exactly the public dyadic source rows already displayed, so an accepted downstream read determines exactly those rows.

For the converse, start with any finite packet exposing precisely $m$, $e$, $\ell$, $W$, $R$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$. The carrier projection repacks these rows as the signed-exponent surface, and the NameCert obligation theorem supplies the same public source boundary for the three consumers.

The correspondence exports no quotient rational, host equality row, completed $\RealUp$ endpoint, ambient completeness principle, or selected host limit.
