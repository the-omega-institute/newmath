\section{DiffForm root degree-classifier package}
\label{sec:diffform-root-degree-classifier-package}

\begin{definition}[DiffForm root degree-classifier package]
\label{def:diffform-root-degree-classifier-package}
Fix a $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ source $M$ and a
$\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$ source $T$. The root degree-classifier package
for $\DiffFormUp$ is the citable surface
$$
\mathsf{DiffFormRootDegreeClassifier}_{M,T}.
$$
Its BEDC source is the tuple consisting of $BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$,
$\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\TensorProductUp}$.

Its carrier is the pair of the concrete carried row
$\mathsf{DiffFormBHistCarrier}_{M,T}$ and the alignment witness
$\mathsf{DegreeProbeAligned}(d,\mathcal{P})$ for the displayed unary degree
row $d$ and visible probe bundle $\mathcal{P}$. Its classifier is equality of
the transported unary degree row and equality of the transported visible probe
arity under $\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$; the remaining transported carrier rows are
read only through that same $BHist$ classifier surface.

Its ledger consists exactly of the displayed degree history $d$, the probe
bundle $\mathcal{P}$, the tensor row $\tau$, the scalar endpoint $\sigma$, the
antisymmetry row $\alpha$, the source ledger $\lambda$, and the continuation
route that records the degree endpoint used by wedge and exterior-input
routing. The package contains no row for nilpotence, closedness,
nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability, quotienting, or a standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DiffForm root degree-classifier coverage]
\label{thm:diffform-root-degree-classifier-coverage}
Every root $BHist$ surface row of
\autoref{def:diffform-root-bhist-namecert-surface} supplies the package
$\mathsf{DiffFormRootDegreeClassifier}_{M,T}$. In particular, the carrier
part is the concrete $\mathsf{DiffFormBHistCarrier}_{M,T}$ row, the arity
part is the visible $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine aligned with its unary
degree row, and the ledger part is exhausted by the displayed degree, probe,
tensor, scalar endpoint, antisymmetry, source, and continuation rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the carrier field of the root $BHist$ surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-bhist-carrier-obligation}. It supplies the
concrete carrier row together with the manifold and tensor source rows named
by the package.

Next bind the degree and arity interface. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-degree-probe-alignment-carrier-refinement}; the carried
row exposes the unary degree history and the visible probe bundle as an
aligned pair, with no second arity counter outside the displayed bundle.

Finally read the ledger. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-bhist-ledger-exactness-obligation}. Its exactness
statement lists precisely the degree histories, probe bundles, tensor source
rows, manifold source rows, wedge rows, exterior-input rows, and $\Cont$
route histories available at the root surface. Repacking those rows gives the
announced degree-classifier package.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiffFormUp.DiffFormRootDegreeClassifier\_coverage}

\begin{theorem}[DiffForm root degree-classifier transport]
\label{thm:diffform-root-degree-classifier-transport}
Transport along $\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$ preserves
$\mathsf{DiffFormRootDegreeClassifier}_{M,T}$. If a carried row with degree
$d$ and probe bundle $\mathcal{P}$ supplies the package, then every
$\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$-equivalent carried row supplies the transported package
with the transported unary degree row and the same visible probe arity.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with classifier stability for the root surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-bhist-classifier-stability-obligation}. The
transported endpoint remains in the concrete carrier surface and keeps the
root classifier rows available.

Now transport the aligned pair. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-degree-probe-alignment-transport}. The degree
comparison carried by $\hsame$ and the transported probe bundle rebuild
$\mathsf{DegreeProbeAligned}$ at the target endpoint.

It remains to identify the arity read. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-probe-slot-arity-transport}. The visible number of
probe slots is preserved by the transported bundle, so the classifier
interface is exactly equality of transported degree rows together with
equality of transported visible probe arity under $\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiffForm root degree-classifier downstream consumption]
\label{thm:diffform-root-degree-classifier-downstream-consumption}
The downstream chapters $\DeRhamUp$, $\SymplecticUp$, and $\ContactUp$
consume the $\DiffFormUp$ degree and arity data only through
$\mathsf{DiffFormRootDegreeClassifier}_{M,T}$. Their reads of degree rows and
probe-slot arity factor through the root $BHist$ surface, the aligned
degree-probe pair, and the root consumer face classifier; they do not read
nilpotence, closedness, nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability, quotienting,
or a standard bridge from this package.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from a downstream read by one of the three consumer chapters. Apply
\autoref{thm:diffform-root-bhist-downstream-boundary}. It factors every such
read through the root carrier row, classifier row, degree-probe alignment,
wedge routing, exterior-input routing, source NameCert rows, and $\Cont$
route histories.

Next apply \autoref{thm:diffform-root-consumer-face-coverage}. The consumer
face classifier assigns every exported row to the de Rham, symplectic,
contact, or shared root face, with no row outside those faces.

The shared degree and arity interface is therefore exactly the package
surface: transported unary degree equality and transported visible
probe-arity equality under $\sim_{\DiffFormUp}$. The consumer-specific laws
remain in their own faces, so this package carries no nilpotence, closedness,
nondegeneracy, contact nonintegrability, quotienting, or standard bridge
claim.
\end{proof}
