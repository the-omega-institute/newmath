\begin{definition}[Dyadic Cauchy approximation carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-cauchy-approximation-carrier}

$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, recording the dyadic radius, comparison, and refinement data requested by the approximation.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row exposing the inspected stream prefixes associated with the dyadic tolerance request.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the same dyadic tolerances and stream windows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal available only after $D,W,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyRealLocatorUp$ handoff row, recording the locator-facing use of the same finite approximation data.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the tolerance, window, readback, Real seal, locator handoff, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an approximation request through $D,W,Q,E,L$ to the named consumer rows.

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic Cauchy approximation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-cauchy-approximation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DyadicCauchyApproximationUp$ carrier $K=(D,W,Q,E,L,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicCauchyApproximationUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the nine displayed rows, dyadic tolerance admission through $D$, finite stream-window exposure through $W$, regular rational readback through $Q$, RealUp sealing through $E$, CauchyRealLocator handoff through $L$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-cauchy-approximation-carrier}. The only rows available to an approximation consumer are $D,W,Q,E,L,H,C,P,N$, so tolerance, window, readback, seal, locator, transport, replay, provenance, and naming obligations must be read there.

The dyadic and stream obligations are bound by $D$ and $W$. The dyadic radius, comparison, and refinement data come from the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row, while the inspected prefixes are the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows attached to the same request.

The readback obligations are bound by $Q,E,L$. The regular rational row reads exactly the dyadic-window data, the Real seal is downstream of that row, and the locator handoff reuses the same finite approximation surface.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this route. Since the carrier has no quotient-stream, selected-limit, trichotomy, open-cover, choice, or ambient completeness coordinate, the listed obligations exhaust the certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic Cauchy approximation RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-cauchy-approximation-regseqrat-handoff}

$$

$$
Thus the consumer first receives the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row and finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and only then the terminal $\RealUp$ seal or $\CauchyRealLocatorUp$ handoff. The route exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, countable-choice schedule, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.

\begin{proof}

The regular rational stage is $Q$. It reads precisely the windows and dyadic tolerances already displayed by $D$ and $W$, so no separate stream quotient or selected limit can enter the handoff.

The endpoint stage is $E,L,H,C,P,N$. The $\RealUp$ seal and $\CauchyRealLocatorUp$ handoff are downstream of the regular readback, while transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same finite route.
