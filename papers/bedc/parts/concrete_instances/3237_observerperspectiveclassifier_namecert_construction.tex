\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ObserverPerspectiveClassifierUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-observerperspectiveclassifier-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ObserverPerspectiveClassifierUp$ surface packages comparison between two observers as a finite public certificate. Each observer keeps its own universe-for-observer row; the classifier compares only the displayed rows that pass through locality cells, $\mathsf{GAP}$ commitments, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming row. It is not a global view over both worlds.

The packet consumes \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-consciousobserverstate-namecert} for the two observer-state rows $O_0,O_1$. Its locality evidence is read from the public cell surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-localitycell-namecert} or \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-locality-cell-namecert}, and the comparison is checked against the public alignment surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crossperspectivealignment-namecert} rather than against an ambient shared world.

\begin{definition}[Observer-perspective-classifier carrier]
\label{def:observer-perspective-classifier-carrier}
An $\ObserverPerspectiveClassifierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(O_0,O_1,U_0,U_1,L,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O_0$ and $O_1$ are accepted $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$ rows naming the two conscious-observer state surfaces being compared;
\item $U_0$ and $U_1$ are the two universe-for-observer record surfaces, each read only as the records and closure rules accumulated by the corresponding observer row;
\item $L$ is the locality-cell evidence row, displayed as a $\LocalityCellUp$ or $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ public coordination surface for the two observer rows;
\item $G$ is the paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ commitment ledger recording the two-sided coherence commitments by which $U_0$ and $U_1$ can be compared without identifying them as one host world;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $O_0,O_1,U_0,U_1,L,G$ and for the package and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that let a consumer read each observer row, its universe-for-observer surface, the locality-cell evidence, and the paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ commitments in that order;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$, universe-for-observer record surfaces, $\LocalityCellUp$, $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$, $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledgers, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverPerspectiveClassifierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two accepted carriers only through the displayed rows $O_0,O_1,U_0,U_1,L,G,H,C,P,N$ and their recorded transports and routes.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverPerspectiveClassifier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-perspective-classifier-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverPerspectiveClassifierUp}$ surface has exactly five obligation rows: carrier exposure for $Q=(O_0,O_1,U_0,U_1,L,G,H,C,P,N)$; observer anchoring through $O_0,O_1$; universe-for-observer exposure through $U_0,U_1$; locality and coherence comparison through $L,G,H,C$; and non-escape from a god's-eye observer, host equality of worlds, private $\mathsf{Self}$ access, global spacetime carriers, hidden synchronization maps, and unrecorded cross-perspective evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the finite packet $Q=(O_0,O_1,U_0,U_1,L,G,H,C,P,N)$.

Observer anchoring is read from $O_0$ and $O_1$. Universe-for-observer exposure is read from $U_0$ and $U_1$, with each universe surface attached to its own observer row.

The comparison obligation is then read from $L,G,H,C$. The locality-cell row $L$ supplies the public coordination surface, the paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger $G$ records the coherence commitments, the transport row $H$ moves only displayed coordinates, and the continuation row $C$ replays only the displayed reading order.

Finally inspect the carrier list. No coordinate names a god's-eye observer, host equality of the two worlds, private $\mathsf{Self}$ access, global spacetime carrier, hidden synchronization map, or unrecorded evidence row, so none of those objects is exported by the local naming surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverPerspectiveClassifier locality-cell comparison]
\label{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-locality-cell-comparison}
Every accepted $\ObserverPerspectiveClassifierUp$ packet compares $U_0$ and $U_1$ only after the observer rows $O_0,O_1$, the locality-cell evidence $L$, and the paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ commitments $G$ have been displayed in the same finite carrier. A consumer may classify the two perspectives through $H$ and $C$, but it cannot replace the locality-cell comparison by a single ambient world, a shared external spacetime, or direct access to either observer's private $\mathsf{Self}$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet and read the two observer rows $O_0,O_1$ together with their universe-for-observer surfaces $U_0,U_1$.

The classifier is not applied to those universe surfaces in isolation. It first uses the locality-cell evidence $L$ and the paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger $G$, then replays those rows through the displayed transports $H$ and continuations $C$.

Since the packet contains no ambient world coordinate, shared external spacetime row, or private $\mathsf{Self}$ coordinate, the comparison remains a finite locality-cell comparison between displayed observer perspectives.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverPerspectiveClassifier ledger exactness]
\label{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-ledger-exactness}
Every public read exported by an accepted $\ObserverPerspectiveClassifierUp$ carrier factors through the finite ledger
$$
O_0,O_1,U_0,U_1,L,G,H,C,P,N.
$$
The ledger is exact: observer rows, universe-for-observer surfaces, locality-cell evidence, paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ commitments, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row are available, and no other comparison primitive is available.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition displays all rows of the packet. The mathematical comparison data are $O_0,O_1,U_0,U_1,L,G$, and the structural data are $H,C,P,N$.

Any consumer read must pass through the displayed continuation routes in $C$. Those routes expose the two observer rows, their universe surfaces, the locality evidence, and the paired commitments before the package and naming rows are read.

There is no remaining coordinate for a second comparison primitive. Thus every exported read factors through the listed ledger and the ledger is exact for public consumers.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-nonescape}
An accepted $\ObserverPerspectiveClassifierUp$ carrier exports only the two conscious-observer state rows, the two universe-for-observer record surfaces, the locality-cell evidence row, the paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ commitment ledger, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row. It exports no god's-eye observer, no host equality of worlds, no private $\mathsf{Self}$ access, and no global spacetime carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates of \autoref{def:observer-perspective-classifier-carrier}. Every exported row is one of $O_0,O_1,U_0,U_1,L,G,H,C,P,N$.

The positive comparison data remain public and finite: observer rows, universe-for-observer surfaces, locality-cell evidence, and paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ commitments. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, route, package, and name those same displayed coordinates.

No listed coordinate is a god's-eye observer, a host equality witness between worlds, a private $\mathsf{Self}$ row, or a global spacetime carrier. Therefore an accepted classifier cannot export any of those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverPerspectiveClassifier anchor-change stability]
\label{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-anchor-change-stability}
In an accepted $\ObserverPerspectiveClassifierUp$ carrier, changing the observer anchor from the $O_0$ side to the $O_1$ side, or from the $O_1$ side to the $O_0$ side, preserves a comparison verdict only through the displayed rows
$$
O_0,O_1,U_0,U_1,L,G,H,C,P,N.
$$
The stability route may use the two observer rows, the two universe-for-observer surfaces, locality evidence, paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ commitments, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row, but it cannot pass through a privileged observer or any unlisted observer-neutral witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the carrier description in \autoref{def:observer-perspective-classifier-carrier}. The only named anchors are $O_0$ and $O_1$, and each universe-for-observer surface is attached to one of those rows.

Apply the obligation theorem \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-namecert-obligations}. Anchor comparison is part of the finite local naming surface, so an anchor change must be replayed through the displayed locality, commitment, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows.

The locality comparison theorem \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-locality-cell-comparison} supplies the admissible comparison route, while \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-ledger-exactness} says that the ledger has no further comparison coordinate. Finally \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-nonescape} rules out a god's-eye observer and any unlisted observer-neutral witness, so the comparison is stable only through the displayed packet rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObserverPerspectiveClassifier GAP commitment readback]
\label{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-gap-commitment-readback}
Every cross-perspective comparison exported by an accepted $\ObserverPerspectiveClassifierUp$ carrier reads the paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ row $G$ before it exports a comparison verdict. A verdict based only on $O_0,O_1,U_0,U_1$ or only on the locality row $L$ is not a public classifier verdict for this carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier in \autoref{def:observer-perspective-classifier-carrier} displays $G$ as the paired commitment ledger by which $U_0$ and $U_1$ can be compared without identifying them as one host world.

By \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-namecert-obligations}, the comparison obligation is not just observer exposure or universe exposure; it is the locality and coherence comparison through $L,G,H,C$. The locality-cell comparison theorem \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-locality-cell-comparison} makes the same ordering explicit: the classifier compares after the observer rows, the locality-cell evidence, and the paired $\mathsf{GAP}$ commitments have been displayed in the same finite carrier.

The exactness theorem \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-ledger-exactness} then prevents a consumer from dropping $G$ while still claiming the public verdict. The non-escape theorem \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-nonescape} prevents replacing $G$ by hidden synchronization or host equality, so the paired commitment row must be read before the verdict is exported.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-comparison-route-totality}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Apply \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-namecert-obligations} to read those layers as the local naming obligations. Apply \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-locality-cell-comparison} to place the comparison after the observer rows, universe surfaces, locality evidence, and paired commitment ledger.

It remains to account for transport, routing, provenance, and local naming. The exactness theorem \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-ledger-exactness} says that public reads factor through $H,C,P,N$ after the mathematical comparison rows, and \autoref{thm:observer-perspective-classifier-nonescape} says that no hidden comparison route remains outside this finite list. \end{proof}
