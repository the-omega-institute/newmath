\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finiteobservationbudgetselector-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ packet names the finite observation budget used by the Real completeness route of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}. It sits between the precision-to-window schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-choice-free-limit-schedule-namecert}, the selected-tail surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchytailselector-namecert}, and the diagonal completion packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-diagonal-completion-namecert}; its role is to expose the bounded rows a diagonal consumer may inspect before the $\RealUp$ seal is reached.

\begin{definition}[Finite observation-budget selector carrier]
\label{def:finite-observation-budget-selector-carrier}
A $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(B,S,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the precision-budget row naming the finite set of tolerances and diagonal indices a Real-completion consumer may request;
\item $S$ is the selected schedule row assigning each budget entry of $B$ to one finite observation route;
\item $W$ is the finite family of $\StreamNameUp$ window rows selected by the schedule;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger of rational observations, radii, and tolerance comparisons read from the windows in $W$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row reached after the dyadic ledger has been checked;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after the displayed handoff;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the budget, schedule, windows, dyadic ledger, regular-sequence handoff, seal, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $B$ to $S$, from $S$ to $W$, from $W$ to $D$, from $D$ to $R$, and from $R$ to $E$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, countable choice, a hidden limit selector, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation-budget selector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:finite-observation-budget-selector-carrier} consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure, requiring every accepted object to display the packet $F=(B,S,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item budget-to-schedule exactness, requiring each requested budget entry in $B$ to reach the selected schedule row $S$ only through the displayed route in $C$;
\item window and dyadic ledger coverage, requiring $S$ to select only the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows in $W$ and requiring every exported $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation in $D$ to be read from those windows;
\item handoff and seal discipline, requiring the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ and $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ to be reached through $D$ and then transported by the displayed $H,C,P,N$ rows;
\item non-escape, requiring every public read to stay inside the displayed packet and to export no quotient stream, host real equality, countable-choice row, hidden limit selector, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the tuple in \autoref{def:finite-observation-budget-selector-carrier}. The packet lists the budget, selected schedule, selected windows, dyadic observations, regular-sequence handoff, real seal, transports, routes, provenance, and local naming row.

Budget-to-schedule exactness is the first displayed route in $C$. Since the carrier has one selected schedule row $S$ for the budget $B$, a consumer has no alternate coordinate from which to obtain a different schedule for the same requested observation budget.

Window and dyadic ledger coverage use the next two route segments. The route from $S$ to $W$ exposes only the finite window family, and the route from $W$ to $D$ makes the dyadic observation ledger depend on those windows rather than on an unbounded stream object.

Handoff and seal discipline are supplied by $R,E,H,C,P,N$. The regular-sequence handoff is reached after the dyadic ledger, the real seal is reached after the handoff, and transport or repacking must replay the same rows through $H,C,P,N$.

The non-escape clause follows from the carrier inventory. None of the displayed rows is a quotient stream, host real equality test, countable-choice row, hidden selector, or ambient completeness theorem, so the local naming certificate has no route that can export one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation-budget selector schedule admission]
\label{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-schedule-admission}
Every accepted $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ packet admits its selected schedule as a finite consumer of the $\ChoiceFreeLimitScheduleUp$ route: the budget row $B$ is read as the precision request surface, the schedule row $S$ is read as the finite route selector, and the rows $W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$ supply exactly the window, dyadic-ledger, handoff, seal, transport, route, provenance, and local naming surface needed by a choice-free limit-schedule consumer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the accepted carrier $F=(B,S,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The first two coordinates display the finite precision budget and the selected schedule row that answers each budget entry.

Read the schedule through the $\ChoiceFreeLimitScheduleUp$ dependency named in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-choice-free-limit-schedule-namecert}. Its public route expects a request row, a schedule row, selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows, a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, a $\RealUp$ seal, and the same transport, route, provenance, and naming rows.

These rows are exactly the coordinates of $F$. The admission therefore adds no selected representative, quotient stream, or host-completeness datum; it only reuses the displayed finite schedule-to-seal route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation-budget selector RealUp seal handoff]
\label{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-real-seal-handoff}
For every accepted $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ packet, each $\RealUp$ seal read through $E$ factors through the finite route
$$
B \to S \to W \to D \to R.
$$
The handoff is compatible with the selected-tail route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchytailselector-namecert} and with the diagonal-completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-diagonal-completion-namecert}, because all three surfaces read only displayed $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledgers, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\NameCert$ rows before exposing a $\RealUp$ seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the seal coordinate from the carrier. The definition places $E$ after the budget, selected schedule, selected windows, dyadic ledger, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and the continuation coordinate records the route into $E$.

Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-namecert-obligations}. They require the seal row to be reached through $D$ and $R$ and then repacked only through the displayed $H,C,P,N$ rows.

The selected-tail and diagonal-completion siblings consume the same kind of visible rows. Since $F$ exposes exactly those rows and no hidden analytic coordinate, the $\RealUp$ handoff can be read by those consumers without changing the finite budget route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation-budget selector non-escape]
\label{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-nonescape}
Every Real-completion, diagonal-limit, tail-selection, or schedule consumer of an accepted $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ packet factors through the displayed finite packet
$$
B,S,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
The consumer may inspect the precision budget, selected schedule, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row. It cannot inspect quotient streams, host equality of completed reals, countable choice, hidden limit selectors, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier and NameCert obligations. Carrier exposure fixes the displayed packet, budget-to-schedule exactness fixes the segment from $B$ to $S$, window and dyadic ledger coverage fixes the segments from $S$ to $W$ and from $W$ to $D$, and handoff and seal discipline fixes the segments from $D$ to $R$ and from $R$ to $E$ under the same $H,C,P,N$ rows.

Use schedule admission to keep schedule-facing reads on those rows. By \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-schedule-admission}, the packet supplies the choice-free schedule surface without adding an alternate selector.

Use the seal handoff to keep Real-facing reads on the same route. By \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-real-seal-handoff}, the $\RealUp$ seal is reached only after the finite budget, schedule, windows, dyadic ledger, and regular-sequence handoff have been read.

The excluded analytic objects do not occur as coordinates of $F$. Therefore no public consumer read can export quotient streams, host equality of completed reals, countable choice, hidden limit selectors, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation-budget selector route determinacy]
\label{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-budget-route-determinacy}
Let $F$ and $F'$ be accepted $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ packets with the same displayed $B,S,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$ rows. Then they determine the same finite budget-to-seal route
$$
B \to S \to W \to D \to R \to E.
$$
In particular, a Real-completion consumer cannot obtain two distinct routes from the same displayed rows: schedule admission fixes $B \to S$, window and dyadic coverage fix $S \to W \to D$, and seal handoff fixes $D \to R \to E$ under the shared $H,C,P,N$ surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the common carrier rows. Both packets expose the same budget row, schedule row, window family, dyadic ledger, regular-sequence handoff, real seal, transports, routes, provenance, and local naming row.

The first segment is determined by schedule admission. By \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-schedule-admission}, the budget row $B$ is read only through the displayed selected schedule row $S$.

The middle segment is determined by the NameCert coverage obligations. Window selection uses the displayed route from $S$ to $W$, and every dyadic observation exported by $D$ is read through the displayed windows in $W$.

The final segment is determined by the seal handoff. By \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-real-seal-handoff}, the $\RealUp$ seal is reached only after the dyadic ledger and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and the same $H,C,P,N$ rows repack the result. Thus two accepted packets with the same displayed rows have the same finite route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation-budget selector dyadic-window exhaustion]
\label{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-dyadic-window-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ packet, each dyadic observation exported by the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$ is exhausted by the selected finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows in $W$. No exported dyadic observation has a route to quotient streams, host equality of completed reals, countable choice, hidden limit selectors, or any unbounded observation surface outside the displayed packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the carrier exposure clause to identify the finite window family $W$ and the dyadic ledger $D$ inside the packet. The carrier contains no additional stream coordinate and no ambient selector from which an observation could be read.

Apply the window and dyadic ledger coverage obligation from \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-namecert-obligations}. It requires the schedule row $S$ to select only the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows in $W$ and requires every exported $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation in $D$ to be read from those windows.

The non-escape theorem removes the remaining alternatives. By \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-nonescape}, public reads cannot export quotient streams, host real equality, countable-choice data, hidden limit selectors, or an ambient completeness theorem. Hence the selected finite windows exhaust the dyadic observations exported by $D$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation-budget selector diagonal consumer boundary]
\label{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-diagonal-consumer-boundary}
Every diagonal-limit or Real-completion consumer that uses an accepted $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ packet factors through the displayed route
$$
B \to S \to W \to D \to R \to E
$$
together with the $H,C,P,N$ rows. This is the boundary needed by the Real-completeness vision of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}, the selected-tail surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchytailselector-namecert}, and the diagonal-completion packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-diagonal-completion-namecert}: each such consumer may use the finite budget route, but it receives no quotient stream, host real equality, countable choice, hidden selector, or independent completeness oracle from this selector.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the non-escape theorem. It already states that Real-completion, diagonal-limit, tail-selection, and schedule consumers of an accepted packet factor through the displayed finite packet and cannot inspect excluded analytic objects.

Refine the factorization by route determinacy. By \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-budget-route-determinacy}, the displayed rows determine one finite route from the budget to the seal, so a downstream consumer cannot branch into an alternate completion path while using the same selector.

Use dyadic-window exhaustion for the observation boundary. By \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-dyadic-window-exhaustion}, every dyadic observation supplied to the diagonal route is already read from the selected finite windows.

The sibling selected-tail and diagonal-completion chapters named in the introduction consume exactly these visible rows before reaching a $\RealUp$ seal. Therefore any such downstream consumer factors through $B \to S \to W \to D \to R \to E$ together with $H,C,P,N$, and the selector contributes no further completion principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation-budget selector primitive scope]
\label{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-primitive-scope}
For every accepted $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ packet $F=(B,S,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the scoped source of every Real-completion, diagonal-limit, selected-tail, and schedule consumer is exactly the displayed BEDC primitive surface: the $\BHist$ packet rows $B,S,W,D,R,E$, the componentwise $\hsame$ transports $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$, the $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp}$ row $N$, over $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$. The accepted packet exports no quotient stream, host real equality, countable-choice row, hidden selector, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The row inventory is fixed by \autoref{def:finite-observation-budget-selector-carrier}: an accepted packet has exactly the displayed tuple $F=(B,S,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ over $\BHist$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$. The five local NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-namecert-obligations} make those rows the only carrier exposure, budget route, window coverage, seal handoff, and non-escape surface.

The route is then forced rather than chosen from an ambient source. By \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-budget-route-determinacy}, the displayed rows determine the unique finite path $B \to S \to W \to D \to R \to E$. By \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-dyadic-window-exhaustion}, the $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger exhaust the observations available to the consumer.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-diagonal-consumer-boundary}. It identifies Real-completion, diagonal-limit, selected-tail, and schedule consumers as consumers of precisely this finite route together with $H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier and obligations contain no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, countable choice, hidden selectors, or ambient completeness, no such object is exported from the scoped primitive surface.
\end{proof}
