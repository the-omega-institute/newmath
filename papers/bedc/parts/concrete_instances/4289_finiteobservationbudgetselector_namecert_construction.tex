The $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ packet names the finite observation budget used by the Real completeness route of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}. It sits between the precision-to-window schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-choice-free-limit-schedule-namecert}, the selected-tail surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchytailselector-namecert}, and the diagonal completion packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-diagonal-completion-namecert}; its role is to expose the bounded rows a diagonal consumer may inspect before the $\RealUp$ seal is reached.

\begin{definition}[Finite observation-budget selector carrier]
\label{def:finite-observation-budget-selector-carrier}
A $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(B,S,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the precision-budget row naming the finite set of tolerances and diagonal indices a Real-completion consumer may request;
\item $S$ is the selected schedule row assigning each budget entry of $B$ to one finite observation route;
\item $W$ is the finite family of $\StreamNameUp$ window rows selected by the schedule;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger of rational observations, radii, and tolerance comparisons read from the windows in $W$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row reached after the dyadic ledger has been checked;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after the displayed handoff;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the budget, schedule, windows, dyadic ledger, regular-sequence handoff, seal, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $B$ to $S$, from $S$ to $W$, from $W$ to $D$, from $D$ to $R$, and from $R$ to $E$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, host real equality, countable choice, a hidden limit selector, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite observation-budget selector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-observation-budget-selector-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:finite-observation-budget-selector-carrier} consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure, requiring every accepted object to display the packet $F=(B,S,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item budget-to-schedule exactness, requiring each requested budget entry in $B$ to reach the selected schedule row $S$ only through the displayed route in $C$;
\item window and dyadic ledger coverage, requiring $S$ to select only the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows in $W$ and requiring every exported $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation in $D$ to be read from those windows;
\item handoff and seal discipline, requiring the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ and $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ to be reached through $D$ and then transported by the displayed $H,C,P,N$ rows;
\item non-escape, requiring every public read to stay inside the displayed packet and to export no quotient stream, host real equality, countable-choice row, hidden limit selector, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp.FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the tuple in \autoref{def:finite-observation-budget-selector-carrier}. The packet lists the budget, selected schedule, selected windows, dyadic observations, regular-sequence handoff, real seal, transports, routes, provenance, and local naming row.

Budget-to-schedule exactness is the first displayed route in $C$. Since the carrier has one selected schedule row $S$ for the budget $B$, a consumer has no alternate coordinate from which to obtain a different schedule for the same requested observation budget.

Window and dyadic ledger coverage use the next two route segments. The route from $S$ to $W$ exposes only the finite window family, and the route from $W$ to $D$ makes the dyadic observation ledger depend on those windows rather than on an unbounded stream object.

Handoff and seal discipline are supplied by $R,E,H,C,P,N$. The regular-sequence handoff is reached after the dyadic ledger, the real seal is reached after the handoff, and transport or repacking must replay the same rows through $H,C,P,N$.
