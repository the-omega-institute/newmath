It sits over the finite window carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the shared budget route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-cauchy-modulus-namecert}, the symmetric comparison surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-tail-equivalence-namecert}, and the diameter witness of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-tail-diameter-namecert}. The packet gives Real and Completion consumers one finite tail-composition surface instead of a hidden cofinal tail, quotient stream, selected limit, or choice-like threshold selector.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy tail composition carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-tail-composition-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyTailCompositionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(T_1,T_2,B,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T_1$ is the first regular-Cauchy tail threshold row, naming the displayed point after which the source observations may be read inside the first budget.
\item $T_2$ is the second regular-Cauchy tail threshold row, naming the displayed point after which the refinement observations may be read inside the second budget.
\item $B$ is the composed tail-budget row; it records the finite threshold obtained by reading $T_1$ and $T_2$ in order and taking the displayed common tail region exposed by the packet.
\item $W$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ window row, requiring all source, intermediate, and refined reads to use the same finite window family.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, recording the requested tolerance, the intermediate slack, and the final comparison budget.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row that connects the window reads and dyadic tolerances to the regular rational sequence surface.
\item $E$ is the tail-evidence row; it records that every accepted read beyond $B$ is admitted through the two displayed tail refinements rather than through a quotient stream or a hidden limit.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two thresholds, composed budget, windows, dyadic ledger, regular-rational handoff, evidence, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from $T_1$ to $T_2$, from the two thresholds to $B$, from $B$ to $W,D,R,E$, and then to the provenance and local naming rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyTailCompositionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\UniformCauchyModulusUp$, $\CauchyTailEquivalenceUp$, $\CauchyTailDiameterUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailCompositionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $T_1,T_2,B,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, selected limits, countable choice, or a standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy tail composition NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-composition-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyTailCompositionUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(T_1,T_2,B,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailCompositionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two displayed tail thresholds, composed tail budget, shared window row, dyadic ledger, regular-rational handoff, tail-evidence row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item threshold ordering, requiring $T_1$ to be read before $T_2$ and requiring the composed budget $B$ to be downstream of both threshold rows;
\item dyadic budget exactness, requiring the tolerance ledger $D$ to record the requested tolerance, the intermediate slack, and the final comparison budget used by $B$;
\item window stability, requiring $W$ to keep the source, intermediate, and refined observations inside one finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family;
\item regular-rational handoff, requiring $R$ to expose the same readback route used by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer before any $\RealUp$ seal is read;
\item componentwise transport and replay, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the two thresholds, composed budget, windows, dyadic ledger, handoff, evidence, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item refusal of quotient stream equality, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, selected limits, countable choice, Lean verification, and bridge checking as local obligations.
\end{enumerate}
No tail-composition obligation is stored outside these displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. Its analytic rows are exactly the two thresholds $T_1,T_2$, composed budget $B$, shared windows $W$, dyadic ledger $D$, regular-rational handoff $R$, and tail evidence $E$.

The threshold obligations are read in order. A consumer first opens $T_1$, then opens the refinement threshold $T_2$, and only then may read the composed budget $B$ as the displayed common tail region.

The numerical and window obligations are finite. The tolerance ledger $D$ records the requested tolerance together with the intermediate slack, while $W$ requires the source, intermediate, and refined observations to remain in one StreamName window family.

The structural rows preserve only this route. Componentwise transport $H$ and displayed replay $C$ carry the same coordinates through provenance $P$ and local naming $N$. Since none of the excluded objects is a coordinate of $Q$, none can become a local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailCompositionUp}$ obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy tail-budget associativity]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-composition-tail-budget-associativity}
Let
$$
Q=(T_1,T_2,B,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\RegularCauchyTailCompositionUp$ carrier. Two successive finite tail refinements through $T_1$ and then $T_2$ compose into the single displayed threshold $B$. Consequently, any third finite tail read that consumes the result may read the composed budget either as the already displayed row $B$ or as the replayed route
$$
T_1 \longrightarrow T_2 \longrightarrow B,
$$
and the two readings preserve the same $W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$ rows. The comparison is a finite threshold-composition statement; it is not a quotient equality of streams, a selected limit, a countable-choice schedule, trichotomy, or an appeal to ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the successive refinement route. Carrier admission exposes $T_1$ and $T_2$ as two threshold rows in one packet, so the second refinement is not a detached cofinal tail.

Replay the continuation row $C$. Its threshold part reads $T_1$ before $T_2$ and then exposes the single composed budget row $B$. Thus a consumer that asks for the combined tail sees one displayed threshold after the two finite inputs have been read.

Now compare with the direct read of $B$. The transport row $H$ preserves $T_1,T_2,B,W,D,R,E,P,N$ componentwise, and the dyadic ledger $D$ and window row $W$ are shared by both readings.

Therefore the two readings are the same local NameCert route through the carrier. Since the route never opens a quotient coordinate, selected limit, choice schedule, trichotomy row, or completeness theorem, associativity is only the finite composition of displayed tail thresholds.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy tail composition Real route]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-composition-real-route}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read admitted by an accepted $\RegularCauchyTailCompositionUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
(T_1,T_2) \longmapsto B \longmapsto W \longmapsto D \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N \longmapsto \RealUp .
$$
Thus a Real or Completion consumer must first read the two displayed tail thresholds, the composed tail budget, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and the tail-evidence row. No such read bypasses this route through quotient stream equality, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, selected limits, countable choice, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Real-facing request. The carrier gives no immediate Real seal; the request must first identify the two threshold rows $T_1,T_2$ and the composed budget $B$.

The finite observation route then passes through $W,D,R,E$. The shared StreamName windows determine which source and refined observations are visible, the DyadicRatCore ledger fixes the tolerance budget, and the RegSeqRat handoff supplies the regular rational readback used by the tail-evidence row.

The structural rows finish the route. Componentwise transport and displayed replay preserve the same data into provenance and local naming before the terminal $\RealUp$ consumer may read the result.

The nonescape clause follows from the carrier inventory. Quotient equality, trichotomy, ambient completeness, selected limits, countable choice, Lean verification, and bridge checking are not coordinates of $Q$, so no Real-facing read can extract them from this packet.
\end{proof}
