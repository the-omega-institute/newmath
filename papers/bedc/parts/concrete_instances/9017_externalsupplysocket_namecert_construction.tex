\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ExternalSupplySocketUp$}

The $\ExternalSupplySocketUp$ packet realizes the socket side of \autoref{ch:visions-external-supply-without-axioms} as a finite NameCert carrier. It sits between the boundary packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-external-supply-boundary-namecert}, the apophatic naming surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert}, the kind router of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supply-kind-router-namecert}, and the classifier packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-socketkindclassifier-namecert}. The socket packet keeps the requested supply visible as a refused boundary row while preserving the ledger rows that downstream audit consumers read.

\begin{definition}[ExternalSupplySocket carrier]
\label{def:external-supply-socket-carrier}
An $\ExternalSupplySocketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(B,A,K,R,F,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\ExternalSupplyBoundaryUp$ row naming the boundary site and requested supply shape;
\item $A$ is the $\ApophaticNameUp$ row naming the supply position without supplying the far end;
\item $K$ is the $\SocketKindClassifierUp$ row naming the socket kind that governs the request;
\item $R$ is the $\SupplyKindRouterUp$ row routing the request through the chosen kind and requested-shape surface;
\item $F$ is the refusal-gate row recording that the requested supply is not available as substrate truth;
\item $L$ is the socket ledger row recording the GAP pressure, visible use site, and refusal readback for the same request;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $B,A,K,R,F,L$;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes from the boundary row to the apophatic name, classifier, router, refusal gate, and ledger rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ExternalSupplyBoundaryUp$, $\ApophaticNameUp$, $\SupplyKindRouterUp$, $\SocketKindClassifierUp$, the socket ledger, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ExternalSupplySocketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for the requested outside supply, no hidden selector, no quotient representative, no proposition-equality token, no host equality, and no theorem asserting that the requested supply has become internal.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ExternalSupplySocket NameCert surface]
\label{thm:external-supply-socket-namecert-surface}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:external-supply-socket-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ExternalSupplySocketUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
X=(B,A,K,R,F,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
boundary exposure through $B$, apophatic-name exposure through $A$, socket-kind classification through $K$, kind routing through $R$, refusal-gate readback through $F$, socket-ledger readback through $L$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

Consequently every accepted public read of the socket must pass through the boundary row, name row, classifier row, router row, refusal-gate row, and ledger row before it may cite the socket as an external-supply boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The packet lists $B,A,K,R,F,L,H,C,P,N$, so the certificate has no coordinate from which to manufacture another boundary name, kind classifier, refusal gate, ledger, transport, route, provenance, or local name.

Read the dependency rows in order. The boundary row $B$ gives the requested supply shape, the apophatic row $A$ names the same unsupplied position, the classifier row $K$ fixes the socket kind, and the router row $R$ places that kind before the requested-shape and refusal reads.

Finally read $F,L,H,C,P,N$. The refusal gate and ledger record the non-internalizing readback, while transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming preserve the same finite packet. Thus the NameCert surface is exhausted by the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ExternalSupplySocket noninternalization]
\label{thm:external-supply-socket-noninternalization}
Every accepted $\ExternalSupplySocketUp$ packet keeps its requested supply external. A consumer may read the boundary row, apophatic name, socket-kind classifier, kind router, refusal gate, socket ledger, transport, route, provenance, and local name, but it receives no supplied witness, quotient representative, proposition-equality token, host equality, hidden selector, unrestricted induction principle, or theorem turning the requested supply into substrate truth.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the boundary packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-external-supply-boundary-namecert}. It records the requested supply and non-internalization row before any socket consumer reads the request.

Use the apophatic name and classifier rows next. The apophatic surface names the external position without a far-end object, and the socket-kind classifier fixes the local kind row that must be routed before refusal data is available.

Apply the kind router and the refusal-gate row. The router forces the requested-shape read through the displayed kind route, while $F$ records that this route ends in refusal rather than supplied content.

Finally use the ledger and structural rows. The row $L$ records GAP pressure and refusal readback, and $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name the same packet. Since no carrier coordinate stores a supplied witness or host-level replacement, the requested supply remains external.
\end{proof}
