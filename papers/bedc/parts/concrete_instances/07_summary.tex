\chapter{Summary of the v1.4 Concrete-Instance Layer}

The v1.4 increment upgrades the proof-standing layer in two ways.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item It constructs the first concrete finite-kernel globalization instance: packages generated from answer signatures, with gaps given by signature fibers.
  \item It constructs the first two concrete naming certificates: \(\NameCert_{\NatUp}\) and \(\NameCert_{\AddUp}\).
\end{enumerate}

Thus the project now has not only abstract policies, but first inhabitants of those policies. The concrete globalization instance remains conditional on \(\AskPol(\Pi,D)\), and the naming certificates remain paper-level constructions. Machine verification is still pending.

\begin{corollary}[Current live edge]
After v1.4, the next mathematically meaningful step is not another governance increment. It is to turn the concrete signature package, concrete gap policy, and the two naming certificates into checked proof artifacts.
\end{corollary}
