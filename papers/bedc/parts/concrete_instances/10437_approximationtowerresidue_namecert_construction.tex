It is adjacent to the indexed tower carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-approximation-tower-namecert}, the finite tower packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-tower-packet-namecert}, the audit surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-physical-model-audit-namecert}, the certified truth surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-certified-physical-truth-namecert}, and the explanatory surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-explanation-namecert}. Those siblings expose approximation, packet routing, physical audit, certified truth, and explanation rows; this carrier isolates the residue row that prevents a hidden source or failed compression from being read as physical adequacy.

\begin{definition}[ApproximationTowerResidue carrier]
\label{def:approximation-tower-residue-carrier}
An $\ApproximationTowerResidueUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(S,T,C,L,F,U,D,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the hidden-source residue row left by the tower compression;
\item $T$ is the approximation-tower packet row whose compression produced the residue;
\item $C$ is the classifier row recording the physical-signature comparison that remains under inspection;
\item $L$ is the ledger row naming the GAP-admissible compression and every omitted source coordinate;
\item $F$ is the failure-surface row naming finite defeating observations, signature gaps, or classifier mismatches;
\item $U$ is the recovery-route row, recording which later observation or audit route may reopen the residue;
\item $D$ is the descent-survivor row, recording which tower operations remain valid after the residue is exposed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source, tower, classifier, ledger, failure, recovery, and descent rows;
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the same rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ApproximationTowerResidueUp$, the five cited sibling chapters, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ApproximationTowerResidueUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for promoting a hidden source, failed classifier, or unledgered residue into a certified physical adequacy claim.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[ApproximationTowerResidue classifier]
\label{def:approximation-tower-residue-classifier}
For accepted packets
$$
R=(S,T,C,L,F,U,D,H,K,P,N)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
R'=(S',T',C',L',F',U',D',H',K',P',N'),
$$
the predicate $\mathsf{ApproximationTowerResidueClassifier}(R,R')$ holds when the hidden-source residue, tower packet, classifier, ledger, failure surface, recovery route, and descent-survivor rows are transported componentwise by $H,H'$ and replayed by $K,K'$ without changing which residue coordinates are still exposed.

Classifier agreement therefore preserves the residue surface itself. It does not identify two tower endpoints, certify physical truth, or erase a failure row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ApproximationTowerResidue NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:approximation-tower-residue-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:approximation-tower-residue-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ApproximationTowerResidueUp}$ surface is exhausted by five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $R=(S,T,C,L,F,U,D,H,K,P,N)$;
\item hidden-source residue exposure through $S$ together with the tower packet row $T$;
\item classifier and GAP-ledger accountability through $C,L$;
\item failure-surface and recovery-route exposure through $F,U$;
\item descent-survivor, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $D,H,K,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
Every consumer that reads a residue, failure surface, recovery route, or descent survivor from an approximation tower must display these rows before it can export its conclusion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:approximation-tower-residue-carrier}. The source residue $S$, tower row $T$, classifier $C$, ledger $L$, failure surface $F$, recovery route $U$, and descent survivor $D$ are the only positive content rows.

The structural rows do not add another source. Transport through $H$, replay through $K$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ preserve the same displayed residue packet.

Thus the NameCert surface is exactly the five listed obligation rows. A consumer that asks for hidden source material, failure evidence, recovery, or descent outside those rows has left the accepted $\ApproximationTowerResidueUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApproximationTowerResidue non-escape]
\label{thm:approximation-tower-residue-nonescape}
An accepted $\ApproximationTowerResidueUp$ packet cannot promote a hidden-source residue, omitted physical signature, failed classifier row, or unrecovered descent gap into physical adequacy. Any such promotion must first pass through
$$
S,T,C,L,F,U,D,H,K,P,N.
$$
In particular, the packet exports no tower endpoint equality, certified physical truth, host-level model adequacy, unbounded validation route, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:approximation-tower-residue-namecert-obligations}. They force every residue-facing read to expose the hidden-source row, tower packet, classifier, ledger, failure surface, recovery route, and descent survivor before any conclusion is exported.

The five sibling chapters supply larger consumer surfaces, but none supplies permission to erase the residue row. The tower and packet chapters expose approximation routes, the audit chapter reads physical fit, the certified-truth chapter reads a finite truth certificate, and the explanation chapter reads explanatory descent; each route still requires the local residue packet when the hidden-source surface is under inspection.

Therefore a hidden source, failure row, or recovery gap cannot be reinterpreted as physical adequacy by structural transport. The rows $H,K,P,N$ only preserve and name the displayed packet, so all stronger claims are outside the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ApproximationTowerResidue recovery-route determinacy]
\label{thm:approximation-tower-residue-recovery-route-determinacy}
For an accepted $\ApproximationTowerResidueUp$ carrier $R=(S,T,C,L,F,U,D,H,K,P,N)$, every recovery-route consumer factors through the same residue ledger rows $S,T,C,L,F,U,D$ and the same structural rows $H,K,P,N$. A recovery read may reopen the finite source or failure surface named by $U$, but it cannot add a private source row, second classifier, unledgered descent operation, certified-truth row, or physical-audit acceptance row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the classifier of \autoref{def:approximation-tower-residue-classifier} to keep the source residue, tower packet, classifier, ledger, failure surface, recovery route, and descent survivor componentwise aligned.

Apply \autoref{thm:approximation-tower-residue-namecert-obligations}. The recovery route is one of the local obligation rows, so a consumer sees $U$ only together with $S,T,C,L,F,D,H,K,P,N$.

Finally use the non-escape theorem. Since certified truth and physical-audit acceptance require their own displayed carriers, the residue recovery route cannot supply those rows by itself. It only names the finite route along which the residue can be inspected again.
\end{proof}
