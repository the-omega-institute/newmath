\section{Located Complete Interval Carrier}
\label{sec:located-complete-interval-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Located complete interval carrier]
\label{def:located-complete-interval-carrier}
A $\LocatedCompleteIntervalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(B,L,N,Q,D,S,R,E,K,H,C,P,M)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the accepted $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ source row, exposing lower and upper endpoint reads, endpoint order, rational containment, dyadic containment, finite windows, regular readback, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedRealIntervalUp$ row that answers finite membership, containment, separation, and refinement requests through endpoint-radius locatedness data.
\item $N$ is the $\NestedIntervalCauchyUp$ row carrying a descending bracket family, diameter budget, and finite Cauchy extraction route.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for rational centers and endpoint witnesses inspected by the bracket family.
\item $D$ is the positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to measure bracket diameter, endpoint slack, and refinement depth.
\item $S$ is the finite observation schedule for bracket requests and Cauchy reads.
\item $R$ is the completion-read row that sends each nested bracket request to a sealed $\RealUp$ output through $B,L,N,Q,D,S$.
\item $E$ records the interval-membership evidence for the sealed output, again read only through the closed bounded and located interval rows.
\item $K$ is the Cauchy-bracket induction ledger, recording the base bracket, successor refinement, diameter decrease, and endpoint containment rows for the displayed finite family.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all rows above.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from bracket refinement requests to the same completion-read and membership rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedCompleteIntervalUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$, $\LocatedRealIntervalUp$, $\NestedIntervalCauchyUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $M$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompleteIntervalUp}$ row binding the packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no host closed interval object, no quotient equality of real names, no selected representative stream, no trichotomy row, no countable choice schedule, and no ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located complete interval NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-complete-interval-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:located-complete-interval-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCompleteIntervalUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $I=(B,L,N,Q,D,S,R,E,K,H,C,P,M)$ as one $\BHist$ packet;
\item source-interval exactness, requiring endpoint, containment, and Real-seal reads to pass through the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ row $B$;
\item located-boundary exactness, requiring membership and separation reads to pass through the $\LocatedRealIntervalUp$ row $L$;
\item nested-bracket exactness, requiring each Cauchy bracket read to pass through the $\NestedIntervalCauchyUp$ row $N$ and the induction ledger $K$;
\item rational and dyadic budget exactness, requiring all centers, endpoint witnesses, diameters, and tolerances to be read through $Q$ and $D$;
\item completion-read confinement, requiring the sealed $\RealUp$ output and its interval-membership evidence to be exported only through $R$ and $E$;
\item transport and replay stability through $H,C,P,M$.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations name located interval completeness as a finite certificate surface. They do not assert host completeness, quotient construction, trichotomy, selected limits, countable choice, compactness beyond the displayed bracket route, or a public standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-complete-interval-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the closed bounded source row, located interval row, nested Cauchy bracket row, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, schedule, completion read, membership evidence, induction ledger, and structural rows.

Source-interval and located-boundary exactness are immediate from the rows $B$ and $L$. Every endpoint, containment, membership, separation, and refinement read is already routed through those two interval surfaces before a completion-facing consumer can inspect $R$ or $E$.

Nested-bracket exactness is supplied by $N$ and $K$. The bracket family, diameter decrease, and endpoint containment facts are displayed as finite rows, and the rational and dyadic budgets used by those rows are exactly $Q$ and $D$.

Finally the structural rows close the obligation surface. The $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ replay routes, provenance, and local naming row preserve the listed packet without adding a host completeness coordinate, quotient, trichotomy decision, selected limit, or choice schedule.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located complete interval Cauchy bracket induction]
\label{thm:located-complete-interval-cauchy-bracket-induction}
Let $I=(B,L,N,Q,D,S,R,E,K,H,C,P,M)$ be an accepted $\LocatedCompleteIntervalUp$ carrier. Every nested-bracket Cauchy read exported by $I$ is obtained by induction over the displayed bracket ledger $K$: the base step reads the source interval through $B$ and $L$; the successor step refines the current bracket through $N$, $Q$, $D$, and $S$; and the terminal read exports a sealed $\RealUp$ row through $R$ together with interval-membership evidence through $E$. The induction is finite-window BEDC data, not an appeal to ambient completeness or choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the base bracket. The closed bounded source row $B$ supplies the endpoint and containment packet, while the located interval row $L$ supplies the finite membership and separation service for the same endpoints.

For the successor step, read the nested interval row $N$. Each refinement consumes a rational center or endpoint witness from $Q$, a positive dyadic tolerance from $D$, and an observation window from $S$. The ledger $K$ records that the next bracket remains inside the previous bracket and has the displayed smaller diameter budget.

Iterating this finite ledger yields the Cauchy route named by the packet. The completion-read row $R$ seals the resulting read as a $\RealUp$ output only after the bracket data have passed through $B,L,N,Q,D,S,K$.

The membership row $E$ then reads the sealed output back into the same interval carrier. Since the construction has used only the displayed bracket induction rows and the structural transports $H,C,P,M$, no host completeness principle, quotient equality, selected representative, trichotomy, or choice schedule enters the export.
\end{proof}
