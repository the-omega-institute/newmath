The $\TailModulusAlignmentUp$ packet realizes the shared tail-modulus route used by the Real-completion surface of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}. It is structurally composite over the refinement route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-modulus-refinement-namecert}, the tail schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchytailschedule-namecert}, the tail-fusion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-realmodulusfusion-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular-rational readbacks of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the seal rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names one finite alignment certificate for a shared tail threshold, not a quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, countable-choice selector, or ambient Real-completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Tail-modulus alignment carrier]
\label{def:tail-modulus-alignment-carrier}
A $\TailModulusAlignmentUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(M,S,F,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ row exposing the source moduli, shared meet, selector-budget route, and selected-window tolerance surface;
\item $S$ is the $\RegularCauchyTailScheduleUp$ row exposing the precision request, cofinal tail bound, and scheduled finite tail witness;
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyTailModulusFusionUp$ or $\RealModulusFusionUp$ row that joins the refinement and schedule surfaces before a seal-facing consumer reads them;
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family common to the refinement, schedule, and fusion rows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the scheduled windows;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and arithmetic ledger attached to the common window readback;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $M,S,F,W,R,D$ have been displayed on the same packet;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,S,F,W,R,D,E$ and the local certificate row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the refinement request through the schedule, fusion, window readback, dyadic ledger, and Real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TailModulusAlignmentUp$, $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$, $\RegularCauchyTailScheduleUp$, $\CauchyTailModulusFusionUp$, $\RealModulusFusionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TailModulusAlignmentUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the displayed alignment rows and their transport, replay, provenance, and naming support.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Tail-modulus alignment obligation surface]
\label{thm:tail-modulus-alignment-obligation-surface}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:tail-modulus-alignment-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\TailModulusAlignmentUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, refinement-source exposure through $M$, tail-schedule exposure through $S$, fusion-route exposure through $F$, common $\StreamNameUp$ window admission through $W$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback through $R$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and arithmetic exposure through $D$, $\RealUp$ seal exposure through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $A$. Its coordinates list the refinement, schedule, fusion, stream-window, regular-rational readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The data-bearing obligations are inherited in order. The refinement row exposes the source moduli and selected-window tolerance route, the schedule row exposes the cofinal tail request and finite tail witness, and the fusion row forces those two surfaces onto the same tail-modulus route before any seal-facing read is available.

The remaining rows are the common BEDC support rows. The $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, and $\RealUp$ seal are consumed along the displayed route, while $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name that route. Thus the listed rows exhaust the local naming obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tail-modulus alignment sibling dependency]
\label{thm:tail-modulus-alignment-sibling-dependency}
Every accepted $\TailModulusAlignmentUp$ packet depends essentially on both the refinement sibling and the schedule sibling: deleting $M$ removes the displayed source-modulus and selector-budget route, while deleting $S$ removes the displayed precision-to-cofinal-tail schedule. In either deletion, the remaining rows cannot determine the common $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ handoff through $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First remove the refinement row. The schedule row still displays a precision request and a cofinal tail witness, but it no longer carries the source-modulus meet, selector-budget route, or selected-window tolerance readback needed to identify the common window $W$ as the refinement window.

Next remove the schedule row. The refinement row still displays source moduli and a selected-window route, but it no longer carries the cofinal-tail request and finite tail witness that make the route usable as a Real-completion tail schedule.

The structural rows cannot repair either absence. The $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows transport and replay displayed data; $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert$ record provenance and naming for displayed data. None of them creates a missing refinement or schedule coordinate, so both siblings are genuine dependencies of the alignment packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tail-modulus alignment non-escape]
\label{thm:tail-modulus-alignment-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$ or Completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\TailModulusAlignmentUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
M,S,F,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
The consumer receives a shared tail-modulus alignment over displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. It receives no quotient stream, host sequence equality, selected host limit, hidden tail oracle, countable-choice selector, independent Real seal, or ambient Real-completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the accepted carrier. The only Real-facing coordinate is the seal row $E$, and it is downstream of the refinement row $M$, the schedule row $S$, the fusion row $F$, the common stream windows $W$, the regular-rational readback $R$, and the dyadic ledger $D$.

Apply \autoref{thm:tail-modulus-alignment-obligation-surface}. It forces every accepted consumer to read the refinement, schedule, fusion, window, readback, ledger, and seal rows through the displayed continuation route before the local NameCert row can be used.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:tail-modulus-alignment-sibling-dependency}. Since neither sibling route can be omitted, the packet has no alternate route to a Real-facing output. The excluded objects are not coordinates of the carrier and are not introduced by $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, or $\NameCert$, so no consumer can project them from the alignment certificate.
\end{proof}
