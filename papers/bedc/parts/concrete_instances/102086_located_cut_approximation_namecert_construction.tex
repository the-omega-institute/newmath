$$

\item $U$ is the upper located dyadic row read from $\LocatedCutUp$, recording finite rational witnesses above the cut.
\item $W$ is the located window row binding the lower and upper dyadic observations to one inspected precision request.
\item $A$ is the approximation row, storing the finite midpoint, endpoint, or bracket readback exported by $L,U,W$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window family through which the approximation row is read as finite stream data.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained after the displayed stream windows and approximation row have been fixed.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, storing dyadic radii, slack comparisons, and endpoint budgets used by $W,A,S,R$.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $L,U,W,A,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient cuts, selected suprema, trichotomy, choice, $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$, propext, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.

\begin{theorem}[Located cut approximation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-cut-approximation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedCutApproximationUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(L,U,W,A,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCutApproximationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed lower row, upper row, located window, approximation row, stream windows, regular-Cauchy handoff, dyadic tolerance ledger, Real seal, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$;
\item located-window exposure, requiring every approximation request to display both $L$ and $U$ through the common window row $W$ before any stream or regular-Cauchy read can be consumed;
\item approximation exactness, requiring $A$ to store only finite dyadic readback data obtained from $L,U,W$ and the tolerance ledger $D$;
\item stream-window readiness, requiring $S$ to expose the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows used by the approximation row before $R$ is read;
\item regular-Cauchy handoff, requiring $R$ to be the only route by which the located-window approximation becomes a $\RegSeqRatUp$ row;
\item Real-seal factorization, requiring every downstream read of $E$ to pass through $L,U,W,A,S,R,D$ and the structural rows;
\item transport, replay, and provenance non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve and name exactly the displayed rows, with no Dedekind construction of $\RealUp$, quotient cut, selected supremum, trichotomy, choice, $\mathsf{Quot.sound}$, propext, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No located-cut approximation obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $Q=(L,U,W,A,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$. Carrier admission exposes exactly the lower and upper located dyadic rows, the common located window, the finite approximation row, the stream windows, the regular-Cauchy handoff, the dyadic tolerance ledger, the Real seal, and the four structural rows.

The located-window obligation is the projection to $W$ together with its source rows $L$ and $U$. A precision request cannot inspect a lower row or an upper row separately and then enter the Cauchy route; the common window row records the finite bracket in which both sides are read.

Approximation exactness is then the projection to $A$ and $D$. The approximation row stores only the displayed dyadic readback supported by the tolerance ledger, so it cannot introduce quotient-cut equality, a selected supremum, trichotomy, or choice.

The stream and regular handoff obligations are the projections to $S$ and $R$. The stream row exposes the finite windows used by the approximation, and the regular row is downstream of those windows rather than a detached regular representative.

Finally, the Real seal and structural rows exhaust the boundary. The seal $E$ is downstream of $L,U,W,A,S,R,D$, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, name, and source only those displayed rows. \end{proof}
