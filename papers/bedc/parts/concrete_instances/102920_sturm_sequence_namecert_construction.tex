\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SturmSequenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-sturm-sequence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, a Sturm chain is read as a finite Euclidean division ledger whose endpoint sign rows can be inspected without choosing roots in an ambient ordered field. The $\SturmSequenceUp$ packet names that finite sign-variation surface: its polynomial source is the coefficient and pseudo-remainder route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polynomial-namecert}, its bracket consumer is \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-root-bracket-namecert}, and its Real-facing windows pass through the completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The certificate exposes a chain and its variation counts, not a host real-root theorem.

\begin{definition}[SturmSequence Real carrier]
\label{def:sturm-sequence-real-carrier}
A $\SturmSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(P,D,Q,E,V,B,W,R,H,C,K,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the $\PolynomialUp$ source row, carrying the coefficient ledger, the degree bound, the formal derivative row, and the polynomial identity under inspection.
\item $D$ is the Euclidean-division ledger for the Sturm chain. It records each displayed quotient, remainder, sign normalization, and terminal-zero decision used to construct the next chain entry.
\item $Q$ is the finite chain table, indexed by the entries produced from $P$ and $D$, with no appeal to factorization over a host field.
\item $E$ is the endpoint-evaluation row. It stores the rational or dyadic endpoint windows and the displayed sign rows for every chain entry at each inspected endpoint.
\item $V$ is the sign-variation row. It records the deletion of zero signs by displayed neighbor rows and the resulting variation counts at the two endpoints.
\item $B$ is the bracket interface row consumed by $\RealRootBracketUp$: it stores only the endpoint cells, endpoint signs, and variation-count difference exposed by $V$.
\item $W$ is the dyadic window handoff row through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, retaining the requested endpoint precision and comparison rows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row used to expose the same endpoint windows at unary precision before any $\RealUp$ seal is read.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the polynomial, division, chain, endpoint, variation, bracket, dyadic, regular-rational, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a requested sign-variation read through polynomial inspection, chain construction, endpoint evaluation, zero deletion, variation counting, bracket handoff, and completion-route readback.
\item $K$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PolynomialUp$, $\RealRootBracketUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SturmSequenceUp}$ registration row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no coordinate for a selected root, algebraic closure, real trichotomy, quotient real equality, factorization theorem, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SturmSequence sign-variation window]
\label{thm:sturm-sequence-sign-variation-window}
For every accepted $\SturmSequenceUp$ carrier $S=(P,D,Q,E,V,B,W,R,H,C,K,N)$ and every inspected endpoint pair, the visible sign-variation window factors through the displayed route
$$
P,D \longmapsto Q \longmapsto E \longmapsto V \longmapsto B,W,R,H,C,K,N .
$$
The window exposes the finite chain entries, endpoint signs, zero-deletion witnesses, and endpoint variation counts. It exports no selected root, no host factorization theorem, and no $\RealUp$ equality principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Open the carrier of \autoref{def:sturm-sequence-real-carrier}. The polynomial row $P$ and division ledger $D$ are the only inputs from which the chain table $Q$ is admitted, so the chain cannot be replaced by an external factorization or by a root enumeration.

Endpoint inspection is then recorded in $E$. Each sign row is attached to a displayed chain entry and a displayed rational or dyadic endpoint window, and the zero rows used by the variation count remain visible.

The variation row $V$ reads those endpoint signs and records the finite zero-deletion and sign-change count. The bracket interface $B$ receives only the endpoint cells and the displayed difference of the two counts.

Finally, $W$ and $R$ expose the same endpoint windows through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$, while $H,C,K,N$ provide transport, replay, provenance, and naming. Since no listed coordinate stores a selected root, a factorization theorem, or a quotient real equality proof, the exported window remains finite Sturm-sequence data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SturmSequence NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:sturm-sequence-namecert-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\SturmSequenceUp$ carrier, the local $\NameCert_{\SturmSequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the rows listed in \autoref{def:sturm-sequence-real-carrier};
\item polynomial-source admission through the coefficient, derivative, degree, and polynomial-identity rows of $P$;
\item chain-construction exactness through the displayed quotient, remainder, sign-normalization, and terminal-zero rows of $D$ and $Q$;
\item endpoint-sign exactness through the endpoint windows and displayed sign rows of $E$;
\item variation-count exactness through the zero-deletion witnesses and sign-change counts of $V$;
\item bracket and completion-route discipline through $B,W,R$ before any $\RealUp$-facing consumer is available;
\item componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,K,N$.
\end{enumerate}
There is no further NameCert obligation for algebraic closure, selected roots, quotient real equality, real trichotomy, factorization, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row gives the admission surface. Its coordinates name exactly the polynomial source, division ledger, chain table, endpoint signs, variation counts, bracket interface, dyadic and regular-rational readbacks, and structural BEDC rows.

The chain-construction obligations are finite because $D$ and $Q$ list the quotient, remainder, sign-normalization, and terminal-zero rows used to produce the table. No obligation asks for a host factorization or for a completed list of roots.

The endpoint and variation obligations are also finite. The row $E$ attaches each sign to an endpoint window, and the row $V$ records the zero-deletion witnesses and variation counts consumed by the bracket interface.

The completion-route obligations are downstream of that interface. Rows $W$ and $R$ expose the retained endpoint windows through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$, while $H,C,K,N$ replay and name the same route. Thus the local certificate has no remaining slot from which to export algebraic closure, selected roots, quotient real equality, real trichotomy, factorization, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
