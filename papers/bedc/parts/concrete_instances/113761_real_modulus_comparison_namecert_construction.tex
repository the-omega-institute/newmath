\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealModulusComparisonUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive real numbers with explicit moduli of convergence, the $\RealModulusComparisonUp$ packet records how two Real-facing convergence-modulus certificates are compared without quotienting streams or selecting representatives. It sits after the comparison surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymoduluscomparison-namecert}, the convergence ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-modulus-of-convergence-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite NameCert bridge between rate certificates; it is not a selected limit, a quotient equality of real names, a countable-choice schedule, or an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Real modulus comparison carrier]
\label{def:real-modulus-comparison-carrier}
A $\RealModulusComparisonUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(C,M_0,M_1,S,R,D,E,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is the $\CauchyModulusComparisonUp$ comparison row naming the cofinal comparison between the two displayed schedules.
\item $M_0$ and $M_1$ are $\RealModulusOfConvergenceUp$ ledger rows for the two Real-facing convergence certificates under comparison.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule shared by the requested comparison read.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational readback row reached from the same finite windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row that names the requested precision and the dyadic comparison budget.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, available only after the two modulus ledgers, finite windows, readback row, tolerance row, and comparison row have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the comparison row, both modulus ledgers, stream schedule, readback row, dyadic tolerance row, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a comparison request through $S,R,D,M_0,M_1,C,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealModulusComparisonUp$, $\CauchyModulusComparisonUp$, $\RealModulusOfConvergenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealModulusComparisonUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
Accepted packets are compared only through $C,M_0,M_1,S,R,D,E,H,T,P,N$. There is no coordinate for selected limits, quotient stream equality, countable choice, host Real equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real modulus comparison NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-modulus-comparison-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealModulusComparisonUp$ carrier
$$
K=(C,M_0,M_1,S,R,D,E,H,T,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RealModulusComparisonUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $K$, dyadic tolerance exposure through $D$, finite-window handoff through $S$, regular-rational readback through $R$, cofinal modulus comparison through $C,M_0,M_1$, Real-seal nonescape through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $T$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$. Every Real or Completion consumer must read these rows before it may use comparison data between convergence-modulus packets.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:real-modulus-comparison-carrier}. The rows $M_0,M_1$ are the only Real-facing convergence-modulus ledgers, and $C$ is the only comparison row between them.

The finite observation route is ordered by $S,R,D$. A comparison request first opens the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ windows, then reads regular rational data through $R$, and then names the requested dyadic tolerance through $D$ before the cofinal comparison row can be consumed.

The Real seal $E$ is terminal for this packet. It is reached only after the two modulus ledgers and their comparison row have been displayed, while $H,T,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same route.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for selected limits, quotient stream equality, countable choice, host Real equality, or ambient Real completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
