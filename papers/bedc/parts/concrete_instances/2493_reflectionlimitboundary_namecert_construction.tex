\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-reflectionlimitboundary-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp$ interface packages the reflection-limit boundary of \autoref{ch:visions-reflection-and-limits} as a finite naming certificate. It separates the rows that close the primitive ground loop from the rows that record structural meta-description, and it forces every truth-facing meta row to remain a boundary entry rather than an admitted ground-loop closure. The structural meta-description ledger is read against the self-compilation boundary of \autoref{sec:self-comp-metacic}, while the ground-loop side stays inside the visible $\BMark$, $\BHist$, $\msame$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

The dependency row is horizontal across the concrete-instance lattice: the $G$ row is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-groundloopboundary-namecert}, the $T$ row is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-truthtotalreflection-namecert}, and the local naming refusal is aligned with \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert}. These siblings supply the closed ground-loop surface, the truth-total refusal boundary, and the apophatic naming discipline consumed by $\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp$.

\begin{definition}[Reflection-limit boundary carrier]
\label{def:reflection-limit-boundary-carrier}
A $\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(G,M,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the ground-loop ledger containing the raw $\BMark$ constructors, the primitive $\msame$ rows, and the closed constructor no-confusion boundary;
\item $M$ is the structural meta-description ledger for coded inductive formation, dependent formation, recursor shape, and classifier closure rows over the internal $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ presentation;
\item $T$ is the truth-boundary ledger recording every attempted truth-total, runtime-total, or validation-as-row query as an $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary entry;
\item $H$ stores displayed $\hsame$ transports for the ground, meta-description, and truth-boundary rows;
\item $C$ stores the finite $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the closed ground rows, the structural meta rows, and the boundary entries;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BMark$, $\BHist$, $\msame$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, and $\mathsf{GAP}$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports a closed ground-loop packet and an open meta-loop packet at once. It contains no BEDC truth predicate, no self-validating runtime, and no row that classifies all internal sentence codes by their truth value.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Reflection-limit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:reflection-limit-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp$ carrier $L=(G,M,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed $\BHist$ packet of \autoref{def:reflection-limit-boundary-carrier};
\item ground-loop closure, requiring every exported primitive distinction read to factor through $G$;
\item meta-loop openness, requiring every structural self-description read to factor through $M$ without becoming a truth predicate;
\item boundary exactness, requiring each truth-total, runtime-total, or validation-as-row query to be recorded in $T$ as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary entry;
\item package closure, requiring all transports, continuations, provenance, and naming rows to be exactly the displayed rows $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus a consumer may read the finite ground closure and the finite structural reflection surface, but it cannot export a closed meta-level truth row through the same packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of the tuple in \autoref{def:reflection-limit-boundary-carrier}. The ground ledger $G$, meta-description ledger $M$, truth-boundary ledger $T$, transport rows $H$, continuation rows $C$, package row $P$, and naming row $N$ are the complete classifier-visible packet.

The ground-loop clause reads only $G$. The primitive constructors, primitive sameness rows, and constructor no-confusion boundary are finite rows of the packet, so closing the ground loop does not require a truth predicate or runtime validation row.

The meta-loop clause reads only $M$. The structural copy of formation and recursor behavior remains a description ledger over internal codes; it is not a classifier deciding the truth of every represented BEDC sentence.

For any truth-total, runtime-total, or validation-as-row query, the carrier exposes only $T$. That row records the query as a boundary entry, and $H,C,P,N$ transport and name the same boundary entry without turning it into an admitted truth row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reflection-limit meta-loop openness obligation]
\label{thm:reflection-limit-meta-loop-openness-obligation}
For an accepted $\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp$ carrier $L=(G,M,T,H,C,P,N)$, the row $M$ remains a structural description ledger for coded formation, dependent formation, recursor shape, and classifier closure. It is never a truth-classification row and never decides represented BEDC sentence codes by truth value.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $M$ from \autoref{def:reflection-limit-boundary-carrier}. Its declared role is structural meta-description over the internal $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ presentation.

Apply the meta-loop openness clause of \autoref{thm:reflection-limit-namecert-obligations}. That clause permits consumers to read structural self-description through $M$ while excluding the conversion of that row into a truth predicate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reflection-limit boundary exactness obligation]
\label{thm:reflection-limit-boundary-exactness-obligation}
For an accepted $\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp$ carrier $L=(G,M,T,H,C,P,N)$, every request for a truth-total row, runtime-total row, or validation-as-row judgment is recorded in $T$ as a $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary entry. No row of $G$ or $M$ discharges that request as a closed ground-loop or closed meta-loop fact.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the truth-boundary coordinate $T$ in the carrier. The carrier statement assigns exactly the truth-total, runtime-total, and validation-as-row requests to this boundary ledger.

Use the boundary exactness clause of \autoref{thm:reflection-limit-namecert-obligations}. It keeps those requests in $T$ as $\mathsf{GAP}$ entries, while $G$ remains a closed ground-loop ledger and $M$ remains structural description only.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reflection-limit package closure obligation]
\label{thm:reflection-limit-package-closure-obligation}
For an accepted $\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp$ carrier $L=(G,M,T,H,C,P,N)$, the rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, route, package, and name the same finite packet. They add no truth predicate, no self-validating runtime row, and no validation row beyond the boundary entries already recorded in $T$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ from the carrier. Their roles are componentwise transport, finite consumer routing, package provenance, and local naming.

Apply the package-closure clause of \autoref{thm:reflection-limit-namecert-obligations}. The clause identifies those rows with the displayed packet and gives them no extra target beyond $G,M,T$ and the boundary entries already visible there.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reflection-limit truth-boundary non-escape]
\label{thm:reflection-limit-truth-boundary-nonescape}
Let $L=(G,M,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp$ carrier. Every consumer query asking for a truth-total row, a runtime-total row, or a validation-as-row judgment factors through the displayed boundary ledger $T$. No projection from $G,M,H,C,P,N$ yields a BEDC truth predicate or a row classifying all represented BEDC sentence codes by truth value.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted carrier through \autoref{def:reflection-limit-boundary-carrier}. The only coordinate whose declared role contains truth-facing, runtime-facing, or validation-facing queries is $T$; the carrier statement identifies those entries as $\mathsf{GAP}$ boundary rows.

The remaining coordinates have disjoint public roles. The $G$ row contains primitive ground-loop data, $M$ contains structural meta-description data, and $H,C,P,N$ transport, route, package, and name already displayed rows. Projecting any of those coordinates therefore returns one of the listed finite rows, not a truth predicate over all represented BEDC sentence codes.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reflection-limit package-route closure]
\label{thm:reflection-limit-package-route-closure}
Let $L=(G,M,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp$ carrier. Every accepted consumer route for $L$ is exhausted by the displayed rows $G,M,T,H,C,P,N$: ground-loop reads pass through $G$, structural meta-description reads pass through $M$, boundary reads pass through $T$, and the rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name those same rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the package-closure clause of \autoref{thm:reflection-limit-namecert-obligations}. It states that all transports, continuations, provenance, and naming rows are exactly the displayed rows $H,C,P,N$.

The carrier definition supplies the source rows $G,M,T$. Since the continuation row $C$ has no consumer target outside the closed ground rows, structural meta rows, and boundary entries, every accepted route is a route to one of $G,M,T$ followed by the displayed transport, package, provenance, and naming data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reflection-limit TasteGate route alignment]
\label{thm:reflection-limit-tastegate-route-alignment}
The paper carrier $L=(G,M,T,H,C,P,N)$ is the single-carrier surface used by the checked Lean TasteGate theorem
$\mathrm{BEDC.Derived.ReflectionLimitBoundaryUp.ReflectionLimitBoundaryTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}$.
Thus the ground row, structural meta row, truth-boundary row, transport row, continuation row, provenance row, and naming row are the rows compared by the gate, with no additional paper-side carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The Lean theorem is stated for the inductive carrier with seven visible coordinates: ground, meta row, truth boundary, transport, continuation, provenance, and name. These are the same seven coordinates displayed in \autoref{def:reflection-limit-boundary-carrier}.

The paper route therefore reads the checked gate as an alignment statement for the displayed packet itself. The theorem adds no second carrier and no extra truth or runtime coordinate; it records that the gate compares exactly the carrier rows already used by the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
