\begin{theorem}[Bool finite-data bridge interface]
\label{thm:bool-finite-data-bridge-interface}
The endpoint bridge of \autoref{def:bool-endpoint-bridge}, endpoint coverage,
classifier endpoint exactness, constructor separation, semantic fields, and the
certificate of \autoref{thm:bool-concrete-semantic-namecert} form the
bridge-ready finite-data interface from $\BoolUp$ to the standard two-endpoint
Bool surface. Concretely, the interface consists of
\autoref{thm:bool-endpoint-bridge-exactness},
\autoref{thm:bool-history-carrier-endpoint-coverage},
\autoref{thm:bool-history-classifier-endpoint-exactness},
\autoref{thm:bool-history-classifier-constructor-separation},
\autoref{def:bool-history-semantic-fields}, and
\autoref{thm:bool-concrete-semantic-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The endpoint bridge supplies the two visible endpoints, and
\autoref{thm:bool-endpoint-bridge-exactness} identifies endpoint agreement
with the kernel mark equality read by the Bool bridge.

Endpoint coverage follows from
\autoref{thm:bool-history-carrier-endpoint-coverage}. Classifier exactness and
constructor separation are supplied by
\autoref{thm:bool-history-classifier-endpoint-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:bool-history-classifier-constructor-separation}, so the two
constructors are both covered and separated.

The semantic fields of \autoref{def:bool-history-semantic-fields} and the
public certificate of \autoref{thm:bool-concrete-semantic-namecert} package the
carrier, pattern, and ledger rows into the finite Bool surface. These are
exactly the finite data a bridge layer may consume, without asserting a
machine-checked standard bridge in this chapter.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Bool finite-data classifier alignment boundary]
\label{lem:bool-finite-data-classifier-alignment-boundary}
For histories $h,k:\BHist$, the classifier side of the Bool finite-data
bridge interface has the following exact alignment boundary:
$$
  \mathsf{BoolHistoryClassifier}(h,k)
  \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
  \exists v,w:\BMark,\
    \mathsf{BoolSourceSpec}(v)
    \land
    \mathsf{BoolSourceSpec}(w)
    \land
    \hsame(h,\mathsf{BoolEndpoint}(v))
    \land
    \hsame(k,\mathsf{BoolEndpoint}(w))
    \land
    \msame(v,w).
$$
Under this equivalence, the transported displayed rows are precisely the two
endpoint readback rows
$\hsame(h,\mathsf{BoolEndpoint}(v))$ and
$\hsame(k,\mathsf{BoolEndpoint}(w))$, together with the mark-comparison row
$\msame(v,w)$. The retained certificate rows are precisely the source,
pattern, and ledger rows of \autoref{def:bool-history-semantic-fields}, all
read as $\mathsf{BoolHistoryCarrier}$. Every row outside the interface listed
in \autoref{thm:bool-finite-data-bridge-interface} is forgotten by this
alignment boundary; in particular, it exports no host proposition, decidable
predicate, eliminator, truth-table-completeness row, payload carrier, payload
classifier, or option-packaging row.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The displayed equivalence is exactly
\autoref{thm:bool-source-qualified-classifier-readback-exactness}. Its forward
direction says that a classifier comparison between $h$ and $k$ exposes only
source-qualified endpoint marks $v,w$, the two endpoint readback rows, and the
mark comparison $\msame(v,w)$. Its reverse direction says that those rows are
already sufficient to rebuild the Bool history classifier comparison, so no
additional classifier coordinate is consumed.

By \autoref{def:bool-endpoint-bridge}, the endpoint map has only the two
visible target rows $\mathsf{BoolEndpoint}(\bzero)=\emp$ and
$\mathsf{BoolEndpoint}(\bone)=\Eone(\emp)$. Endpoint coverage in
\autoref{thm:bool-history-carrier-endpoint-coverage} supplies no third
readback case, and constructor separation in
\autoref{thm:bool-history-classifier-constructor-separation} prevents the two
constructor rows from being identified by the classifier. Thus the readback
part of the boundary transports exactly the displayed endpoint rows and their
$\msame$ comparison.

The certificate part is fixed by \autoref{def:bool-history-semantic-fields}:
source, pattern, and ledger are all the same predicate
$\mathsf{BoolHistoryCarrier}$. The public semantic certificate in
\autoref{thm:bool-concrete-semantic-namecert} therefore retains only the
carrier-classifier ledger named in \autoref{thm:bool-finite-data-bridge-interface}.
Finally, \autoref{thm:bool-public-surface-no-hidden-leakage} rules out host
propositions, decidable predicates, eliminators, and truth-table completeness,
and the finite-data interface list contains no payload carrier, payload
classifier, or option-packaging row. Those nonlisted rows are therefore
forgotten rather than transported.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Bool finite-data domain-codomain mirror boundary]
\label{lem:bool-finite-data-domain-codomain-mirror-boundary}
In the finite-data bridge interface of \autoref{thm:bool-finite-data-bridge-interface}, the codomain-facing Bool read is the endpoint mirror of the domain-facing carrier read. Concretely, every public codomain read of the standard two-endpoint Bool surface is forced through one of the two rows
$\mathsf{BoolEndpoint}(\bzero)=\emp$ or $\mathsf{BoolEndpoint}(\bone)=\Eone(\emp)$, and every such endpoint row is already a domain-side $\mathsf{BoolHistoryCarrier}$ row. Its observation, result, termination, and certificate data are therefore exactly the endpoint exactness row, endpoint coverage row, classifier endpoint exactness row, constructor separation row, semantic field row, and concrete semantic certificate row cited in \autoref{thm:bool-finite-data-bridge-interface}. There is no separate codomain carrier, codomain classifier, or codomain certificate row in this Bool finite-data bridge.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Start with a public codomain read of the standard two-endpoint Bool surface. By \autoref{def:bool-public-endpoint-constructors}, the only public constructors of that surface are the two endpoint rows $\mathsf{BoolEndpoint}(\bzero)=\emp$ and $\mathsf{BoolEndpoint}(\bone)=\Eone(\emp)$. The readback theorem \autoref{thm:bool-public-constructor-case-readback} says that every public endpoint read is classified by exactly one of those rows, and that agreement of such rows is exactly mark agreement through $\msame$.

Each of the two displayed endpoint rows is also a carrier row on the domain side. This is precisely the reverse direction of \autoref{thm:bool-history-carrier-endpoint-coverage}: an endpoint witness $\hsame(h,\mathsf{BoolEndpoint}(v))$ repacks as $\mathsf{BoolHistoryCarrier}(h)$. The agreement comparison is not a codomain-specific classifier, because \autoref{thm:bool-endpoint-bridge-exactness} identifies endpoint agreement with $\msame$ on the underlying marks, while \autoref{thm:bool-history-classifier-endpoint-exactness} and \autoref{thm:bool-history-classifier-constructor-separation} say that the history classifier has exactly the two endpoint classes and cannot mix them.

It remains to identify the certificate side. The semantic fields in \autoref{def:bool-history-semantic-fields} read source, pattern, and ledger all as $\mathsf{BoolHistoryCarrier}$, and \autoref{thm:bool-concrete-semantic-namecert} supplies the public semantic certificate for that same carrier and classifier. Thus reversing attention from the domain read to the codomain read only renames the endpoint row being inspected. It does not create any extra carrier, classifier, ledger, result, termination, or certificate row beyond the finite packet already listed in \autoref{thm:bool-finite-data-bridge-interface}.
\end{proof}
