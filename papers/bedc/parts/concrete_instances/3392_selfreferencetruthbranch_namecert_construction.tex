\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SelfReferenceTruthBranchUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-selfreferencetruthbranch-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\SelfReferenceTruthBranchUp$ packet realizes the Type VII reflection socket as a finite NameCert surface. It records the structural self-description a system may expose, the requested truth-total branch it must refuse, the certificate index at which the request would be consumed, and the audit rows that keep structural self-description separate from internal truth classification.

\begin{definition}[Self-reference truth-branch carrier]
\label{def:self-reference-truth-branch-carrier}
A $\SelfReferenceTruthBranchUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ tuple
$$
B=(S,D,Q,F,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the structural self-description row, naming the object-language syntax, certificate grammar, and replay surface being described;
\item $D$ is the diagonal request row, naming the self-application route by which the description would be read back against itself;
\item $Q$ is the certificate-indexed truth-branch query, naming the finite certificate row whose truth-total classification is requested;
\item $F$ is the forbidden-branch row, recording that a certificate-indexed internal truth predicate or runtime-total validator is not a carrier coordinate;
\item $R$ is the socket report row separating admitted structural description from refused truth-totalization;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for structural descriptions, diagonal requests, branch queries, refusal rows, socket reports, replay routes, provenance, and local names;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from structural description through diagonal request, branch query, refusal exposure, and socket report;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, structural syntax description, certificate indices, Type VII socket reports, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ source rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SelfReferenceTruthBranchUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an internal predicate classifying all sentences of the described system, no runtime validator contained in the object language it validates, no branch that converts a certificate index into its own truth token, and no collapse of structural self-description into truth-total reflection.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Self-reference truth-branch NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:self-reference-truth-branch-namecert-obligations}
For every $\SelfReferenceTruthBranchUp$ carrier
$$
B=(S,D,Q,F,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\SelfReferenceTruthBranchUp}$ surface consists exactly of structural self-description exposure through $S$, diagonal-request exposure through $D$, certificate-indexed query exposure through $Q$, forbidden-branch exposure through $F$, socket-report exposure through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:self-reference-truth-branch-carrier}. The finite tuple lists the structural description, diagonal request, branch query, refusal row, socket report, transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local name.

The Type VII obligation is the separation between $S$ and $Q,F,R$. The row $S$ may describe syntax, certificate grammar, and replay shape, while $Q$ records the requested truth-total branch and $F,R$ record the refusal and report that keep the branch outside the carrier.

Transport and replay are confined to $H$ and $C$, with provenance and local naming confined to $P$ and $N$. Thus every consumer read passes through the displayed rows before it can discuss the self-reference socket.

No remaining coordinate can act as an internal truth predicate, a runtime-total validator, or a certificate-indexed truth branch. The local $\NameCert$ obligations are therefore exhausted by the stated finite surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Self-reference truth-branch non-escape]
\label{thm:self-reference-truth-branch-nonescape}
Every accepted $\SelfReferenceTruthBranchUp$ packet exports Type VII reflection information only through the finite rows
$$
S,D,Q,F,R,H,C,P,N.
$$
It exports structural self-description, diagonal-request reporting, forbidden-branch reporting, componentwise transport, replay provenance, and local naming, but it exports no internal truth predicate for its own sentences, no runtime-total self-validator, no certificate-indexed truth token, and no route that treats structural self-description as truth-total reflection.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet as $B=(S,D,Q,F,R,H,C,P,N)$. The carrier definition lists every row available to a downstream reader.

Structural information is exhausted by $S$ and the replay route through $C$. The diagonal pressure and certificate-indexed query are exhausted by $D$ and $Q$, while the refusal and socket report are exhausted by $F$ and $R$.

The transport and packaging side is exhausted by $H,C,P,N$. These rows preserve and replay the same displayed certificate surface; they do not add a second truth branch or a validator coordinate.

Since the packet has no other exported row, a consumer cannot bypass the forbidden-branch report to obtain a truth-total classifier. The only admitted public reading is the finite socket report separating structural self-description from the refused Type VII branch.
\end{proof}
