\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ZetaContinuationSocketUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-zeta-continuation-socket-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ZetaContinuationSocketUp$ packet turns the Riemann-zeta continuation narrative into a finite local certificate. It reads the already displayed $\ZetaBasicUp$ source, the eta half-plane handoff, the $\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp$ operation boundary, the pole and functional-equation ledgers, the $\GammaUp$ factor interface, and the trivial-zero ledger as named rows of one $\BHist$ packet. It does not assert RH, global zero classification, a complete constructive Gamma chapter, or a branch selector outside the displayed continuation routes.

\begin{definition}[ZetaContinuationSocket carrier]
\label{def:zeta-continuation-socket-carrier}
A $\ZetaContinuationSocketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\ZetaBasicUp$ source row on the half-plane $\Re(s)>1$;
\item $E$ is the eta half-plane row, including the eta-series value, the denominator boundary $1-2^{1-s}$, and the overlap agreement with $B$ where both rows are defined;
\item $A$ is the $\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp$ handoff row that reads $B,E$ and the finite overlap evidence as a local continuation socket;
\item $P$ is the pole-residue row at $s=1$, recording the simple-pole boundary and residue $\Eone(\emp)$ without exporting a regular value there;
\item $F$ is the finite functional-equation factor ledger, containing the rows for $2^s$, $\pi^{s-1}$, $\sin(\pi s/2)$, the reflected zeta input $1-s$, and their displayed product route;
\item $T$ is the trivial-zero ledger, recording the negative-even inputs whose sine factor is zero and the companion rows that remain finite at those inputs;
\item $G$ is the $\GammaUp$ factor interface row, naming the input boundary for $\Gamma(1-s)$ and recording poles as ledger entries rather than as zeta values;
\item $H$ stores the displayed $\hsame$ transports among the zeta-basic source, eta row, analytic-continuation socket row, pole row, functional-equation factor rows, trivial-zero ledger, Gamma factor interface, and local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the finite $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the source half-plane, eta handoff, pole boundary, functional-equation factorization, Gamma interface, trivial-zero ledger, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ZetaContinuationSocketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exposes exactly this finite socket. It contains no unrestricted meromorphic-continuation principle, no proof that all nontrivial zeros lie on the critical line, no complete $\GammaUp$ construction, and no hidden analytic branch choice.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationSocket NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-socket-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ZetaContinuationSocketUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:zeta-continuation-socket-carrier} consists of five finite rows: carrier admission for $Z$; classifier stability for $B,E,A,P,F,T,G$ under the displayed $\hsame$ transports in $H$; ledger exactness for the pole, branch, Gamma-boundary, and trivial-zero coordinates; $\Pkg$ provenance through the continuation routes in $C$; and local naming exactness for $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of the tuple $Z$. Each analytic ingredient named by the socket is a visible coordinate, so the certificate never reads an unlisted continuation object.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The displayed $\hsame$ transports in $H$ preserve the zeta-basic source, the eta handoff, the analytic-continuation socket boundary, the pole-residue row, the functional-equation factor rows, the trivial-zero ledger, and the Gamma-boundary row.

Ledger exactness is also coordinatewise. Requests at $s=1$ land in $P$, Gamma inputs at a pole land in $G$, negative-even zero requests land in $T$, and branch requests outside the finite routes land in the obstruction part of the analytic-continuation socket row $A$.

The continuation routes in $C$ and the local naming row $N$ then repack the same coordinates as a finite $\NameCert$ surface. No row introduces a global continuation theorem or an RH-facing zero-location conclusion.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationSocket ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-socket-ledger-nonescape}
Every consumer read exported by an accepted $\ZetaContinuationSocketUp$ carrier factors through the displayed rows $B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,N$. A read that asks for a zeta value at the pole, a Gamma value at a Gamma pole, a continuation route outside the displayed eta and functional-equation routes, or a nontrivial-zero location claim is recorded in the corresponding ledger coordinate and is not exported as a zeta-continuation value.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the consumer request through the finite routes in $C$. If it lands in the half-plane source row, eta handoff, analytic-continuation socket row, pole row, functional-equation factor ledger, trivial-zero ledger, Gamma-boundary row, transport row, or local naming row, the carrier returns that coordinate.

It remains to inspect requests outside those coordinates. A value at $s=1$ is blocked by $P$, a Gamma-pole request is blocked by $G$, a branch request outside the displayed continuation routes is blocked by $A$, and a nontrivial-zero location assertion has no carrier coordinate. Each case is therefore a ledger read rather than an exported zeta value.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationSocket analytic handoff]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-socket-analytic-handoff}
For an accepted $\ZetaContinuationSocketUp$ carrier $Z=(B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,N)$, every downstream consumer of the continued zeta row also reads the $\ZetaBasicUp$ source row $B$, the eta half-plane row $E$, the $\AnalyticContinuationSocketUp$ handoff row $A$, the pole-residue row $P$, the functional-equation factor ledger $F$, the trivial-zero ledger $T$, the $\GammaUp$ interface $G$, and the transport, continuation, and naming rows $H,C,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a downstream continued-zeta read. By the carrier definition, such a read reaches the output only through the continuation socket coordinate $A$ and its finite route in $C$.

The source side of $A$ is the pair of rows $B,E$ together with their overlap evidence. Transport through $H$ preserves this source boundary, so the downstream read keeps the zeta-basic and eta inputs visible.

The remaining analytic boundary is supplied by $P,F,T,G$. These coordinates record the pole at $1$, the functional-equation factors, the trivial-zero sine cancellations, and the Gamma-boundary interface. Thus the handoff exports a finite packet, not a standalone meromorphic function or a hidden branch choice.
\end{proof}

The analytic handoff theorem identifies the exact rows a downstream zeta-continuation consumer must read. These clauses realize the zeta continuation application as a finite BEDC socket certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationSocket witness carrier bridge]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-socket-witness-carrier-bridge}
Let $Z=(B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,N)$ be an accepted $\ZetaContinuationSocketUp$ carrier as in \autoref{def:zeta-continuation-socket-carrier}. Suppose $R$ is a displayed $\Pkg$ provenance row over the same zeta source, eta handoff, pole ledger, functional-equation ledger, trivial-zero ledger, Gamma-boundary row, transport row, route row, and local naming row, with package scope
$$
\ZetaBasicUp,\ \ZetaContUp,\ \AnalyticContUp,\ \HolomorphicUp,\ \ComplexUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \NameCert.
$$
Then
$$
W=(B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,R,N)
$$
is an accepted $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ carrier as in \autoref{def:zeta-continuation-witness-carrier}. Its downstream non-escape boundary is the same as the boundary of $Z$: pole requests land in $P$, negative-even trivial-zero requests land in $T$, Gamma-dependent requests outside the displayed functional-equation factors land in $G$, branch requests outside the displayed continuation routes land in $A$, and RH or non-trivial-zero-location requests have no exported carrier coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the socket carrier. The accepted packet $Z$ already supplies the source row $B$, eta row $E$, analytic-continuation socket row $A$, pole row $P$, functional-equation factor ledger $F$, trivial-zero ledger $T$, Gamma interface $G$, componentwise transports $H$, finite continuation routes $C$, and local naming row $N$.

The only coordinate required by \autoref{def:zeta-continuation-witness-carrier} and not already present in the socket tuple is the package-provenance coordinate $R$. By hypothesis, $R$ is displayed over exactly the same zeta, eta, pole, functional-equation, trivial-zero, Gamma-boundary, transport, route, and naming coordinates, and its package scope is the witness scope listed in \autoref{def:zeta-continuation-witness-carrier}. Inserting this row between $C$ and $N$ therefore gives the witness tuple $W=(B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,R,N)$ with every required coordinate visible.

It remains to compare the exported boundaries. The provenance row $R$ is a package row; it records how the already displayed coordinates are named through $\Pkg$, and it is not an analytic continuation row, a Gamma completion row, a pole-regularization row, or a zero-location row. Thus adding $R$ cannot create a route that bypasses $A,P,T,G,H$ or $C$.

Now apply the socket non-escape statement of \autoref{thm:zeta-continuation-socket-ledger-nonescape}. The pole boundary is still the coordinate $P$, the negative-even trivial-zero ledger is still $T$, Gamma escape is still absorbed by $G$, and branch escape is still absorbed by $A$. The witness handoff statement of \autoref{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-continuation-handoff} reads these same coordinates through $W$ and exports no RH conclusion and no zero-location classifier. Hence the constructed witness has the same downstream non-escape boundary as the original socket carrier.
\end{proof}
