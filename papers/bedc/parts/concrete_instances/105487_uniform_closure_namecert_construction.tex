\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformClosureUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-closure-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop-style metric and uniform-space practice, the $\UniformClosureUp$ packet records the closure interface whose tests are uniform-neighbourhood rows rather than host open covers. It sits between the metric closure surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-closure-namecert}, the completion handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the separation boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the regular-rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. The packet names closure by finite entourage, window, and seal data; it is not open-cover compactness, quotient closure, selected limits, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[UniformClosure carrier]
\label{def:uniform-closure-carrier}
A $\UniformClosureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(M,Q,S,L,E,W,R,Z,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricClosureUp$ row naming the metric closure surface whose point, source, and candidate-limit data are being read.
\item $Q$ is the uniform-neighbourhood ledger, recording the finite entourage requests admitted for the same carrier.
\item $S$ is the source subset or located-source row being closed under those uniform requests.
\item $L$ is the candidate closure point, read only through the displayed uniform and metric rows.
\item $E$ is the entourage-to-metric comparison row that routes uniform requests into the metric tests already visible in $M$.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window surface used to inspect the approximating observations and the candidate point.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the same windows, tolerances, and comparison requests.
\item $Z$ is the $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $M,Q,S,L,E,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric-closure, uniform-ledger, source, candidate, comparison, window, readback, and seal rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an entourage request through the metric-closure test, finite windows, regular-rational readback, and real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformClosureUp$, $\MetricClosureUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformClosureUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts only packets whose uniform requests, metric closure route, candidate point, finite windows, regular readbacks, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are displayed. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, quotient closure, selected limits, a quotient completion object, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UniformClosure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-closure-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformClosureUp$ carrier $U=(M,Q,S,L,E,W,R,Z,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformClosureUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the $\MetricClosureUp$ row, uniform-neighbourhood ledger, source row, candidate closure point, entourage-to-metric comparison row, finite window row, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\RealUp$ seal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming.

Every public uniform-closure read must factor through
$$
M,Q,S,L,E,W,R,Z,H,C,P,N
$$
before the candidate closure point can be consumed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:uniform-closure-carrier}. The only admissible source data are the metric-closure row $M$, uniform ledger $Q$, source row $S$, and candidate closure point $L$.

The comparison row $E$ then binds each uniform request to the metric tests already exposed by $M$. The finite window row $W$, regular-rational readback $R$, and real seal $Z$ give the displayed numerical route for those tests.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since no row stores open-cover compactness, quotient closure, selected limits, a quotient completion object, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformClosure completion handoff]
\label{thm:uniform-closure-completion-handoff}
Every $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumer of an accepted $\UniformClosureUp$ carrier reads the closure candidate only through the finite route
$$
\MetricClosureUp,\ \SeparatedMetricUp,\ \RegSeqRatUp,\ \RealUp,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
The consumer receives the displayed metric-closure row, uniform-neighbourhood ledger, entourage-to-metric comparison, regular-rational readback, real seal, separation-facing identity boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. It receives no selected completion limit, quotient closure object, open-cover compactness principle, host equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the public route of \autoref{thm:uniform-closure-namecert-obligations}. It places the uniform ledger and entourage-to-metric comparison before any completion-facing consumer can read the candidate point.

Use the metric-closure handoff of \autoref{thm:metric-closure-sequential-closure-handoff}. The same finite windows and regular-rational readback rows feed the completion-facing side, so the consumer cannot detach the candidate from the displayed metric closure surface.

The separated-metric boundary of \autoref{thm:separatedmetric-bridged-closure-readiness} supplies the identity discipline for zero-distance reads. Transport, replay, provenance, and naming through $H,C,P,N$ preserve that route without adding selected limits, quotient closure, compactness, or ambient real completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformClosure separated stability]
\label{thm:uniform-closure-separated-stability}
Let $U=(M,Q,S,L,E,W,R,Z,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\UniformClosureUp$ carrier. If a downstream separated-metric consumer compares two transported closure candidates from $U$, the comparison must pass through the displayed metric-closure row $M$, uniform ledger $Q$, comparison row $E$, finite windows $W$, regular-rational readback $R$, real seal $Z$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ before the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ classifier can be read.

Thus separated stability is a finite transport-and-seal obligation over the displayed rows, not a quotient closure equality or host identity theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two transported candidates through the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$. The transport preserves the metric-closure row, uniform ledger, comparison row, windows, readback, and real seal as displayed rows of the same packet.

Replay the comparison through $C$. The route reaches the $\RealUp$ seal only after $M,Q,E,W,R$ are fixed, and then uses the separated-metric identity boundary rather than host equality.

The provenance and local naming rows $P,N$ expose this route as the public certificate. Since no quotient closure object, selected limit, open-cover compactness row, or ambient real-completeness coordinate occurs in $U$, the separated-stability conclusion remains confined to the displayed finite route.
\end{proof}
