It sits over the nested interval surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nestedinterval-namecert}, the dyadic interval nesting route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-interval-nesting-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the metric Cantor-intersection packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cantor-intersection-metric-namecert}. \begin{definition}[CantorIntersectionUniformWindow carrier]
\label{def:cantor-intersection-uniform-window-carrier}
A $\CantorIntersectionUniformWindowUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(N,D,M,W,R,E,J,H,C,P,K)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $N$ is the $\NestedIntervalUp$ source row, exposing the nested compact interval family and the requested finite depth.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicIntervalNestingUp$ row, recording dyadic endpoint budgets and containment checks for the same depth.
\item $M$ is the $\CantorIntersectionMetricUp$ metric row, read only as a bounded diameter and overlap witness for the displayed interval family.
\item $W$ is the uniform finite-window row, assigning one inspection window to every precision request visible in $N,D,M$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the dyadic endpoints and the uniform window family.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, available only after $N,D,M,W,R$ have been displayed.

\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the nested source, dyadic ledger, metric row, windows, readbacks, seal, handoff, provenance, and local name.

$$

\item $K$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CantorIntersectionUniformWindowUp}$ row.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $N,D,M,W,R,E,J,H,C,P,K$. It has no coordinate for a selected intersection point, unrestricted compactness, quotient stream equality, countable choice, host endpoint equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CantorIntersectionUniformWindow NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cantor-intersection-uniform-window-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CantorIntersectionUniformWindowUp$ carrier
$$
U=(N,D,M,W,R,E,J,H,C,P,K),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CantorIntersectionUniformWindowUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following seed obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed nested source, dyadic ledger, metric Cantor row, finite uniform windows, regular rational readback, terminal real seal, L10 handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item nested-source exposure, requiring every interval read to pass through $N$ before a dyadic or metric row is consumed;
\item dyadic containment visibility, requiring $D$ to supply the finite endpoint and containment checks used by $W$ and $R$;
\item uniform-window exactness, requiring the same finite window family $W$ to serve every visible precision request before the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff is read;
\item real-seal nonescape, requiring $E$ and $J$ to remain downstream of $N,D,M,W,R$;
\item structural transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,K$.
\end{enumerate}
No Cantor intersection uniform-window obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cantor-intersection-uniform-window-carrier}. The displayed tuple lists the nested source, dyadic ledger, metric Cantor row, uniform windows, regular rational readback, real seal, L10 handoff, and structural rows.

The nested and dyadic obligations are read in order. A consumer first sees $N$, then $D$, and only then the metric row $M$ and window row $W$; none of these rows exports a selected point or an unrestricted compactness theorem.

The uniform-window exactness obligation is the route through $W$ and $R$. The row $W$ fixes the finite inspection family, and $R$ reads only the rational data exposed by that family and the dyadic endpoint ledger.

Finally, $E,J,H,C,P,K$ preserve the same route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for the excluded principles, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CantorIntersectionUniformWindow L10 handoff]
\label{thm:cantor-intersection-uniform-window-l10-handoff}

It cannot bypass this route to obtain general Heine--Borel compactness, a selected intersection point, quotient streams, countable choice, host equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The rows $H,C,P,K$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Therefore no consumer can extract the excluded compactness, choice, quotient, equality, or ambient completeness data from the packet.
