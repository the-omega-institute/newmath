The $\CauchySharedTailModulusUp$ packet realizes the Real and Completion route of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness} as a finite shared-tail threshold certificate. It consumes the explicit modulus extraction surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulusextraction-namecert}, the dyadic tail-radius ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadictailradiusledger-namecert}, the finite window service of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the Real seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Its purpose is to synchronize two regular-Cauchy tail requests through one displayed dyadic threshold before any $\RealUp$ completion-facing seal is exposed.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy shared-tail modulus carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-shared-tail-modulus-carrier}
A $\CauchySharedTailModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(R_0,R_1,M_0,M_1,\tau,D,W_0,W_1,Q_0,Q_1,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R_0$ and $R_1$ are the two accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ sources whose tails are being synchronized for a Real-completion consumer;
\item $M_0$ and $M_1$ are the displayed $\CauchyModulusExtractionUp$ rows supplying the two finite tail requests;
\item $\tau$ is the single shared dyadic tail-threshold row, read through the $\DyadicTailRadiusLedgerUp$ service;
\item $D$ is the dyadic tolerance ledger witnessing that both requests are served at the same threshold $\tau$;
\item $W_0$ and $W_1$ are the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows opened by $\tau$ for the two sources;
\item $Q_0$ and $Q_1$ are the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows obtained from the two windows and the shared dyadic ledger;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exposed only after the two readbacks have passed through the same shared threshold;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the sources, extracted moduli, shared threshold, dyadic ledger, windows, readbacks, Real seal, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from each extracted modulus to $\tau$, from $\tau$ to the two windows, from the windows to the readbacks, and from the readbacks to the Real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyModulusExtractionUp$, $\DyadicTailRadiusLedgerUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchySharedTailModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, independent per-source seal exposure, hidden threshold replacement, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy shared-tail modulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-shared-tail-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-shared-tail-modulus-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchySharedTailModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $S$, paired request exposure through $M_0,M_1$, shared-threshold exposure through $\tau,D$, window service through $W_0,W_1$, regular readback through $Q_0,Q_1$, Real seal ordering through $E$, componentwise stability through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$. A completion-facing consumer obtains no $\RealUp$ seal read until both requests have been routed through the shared dyadic threshold.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The rows $R_0,R_1,M_0,M_1,\tau,D,W_0,W_1,Q_0,Q_1,E,H,C,P,N$ are all of the public packet, so every tail request, threshold, window, readback, seal, transport, route, provenance, and local name is visible before the certificate is consumed.

The sibling modulus and dyadic rows supply the request boundary. The extracted modulus rows come from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulusextraction-namecert}, while the shared dyadic tolerance row comes from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadictailradiusledger-namecert}; together they force both tail requests to meet at the same finite threshold $\tau$.

The window and readback rows then route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}. The Real seal row is exposed only after those finite rows have been replayed through $C$ and transported by $H$.

Since the carrier contains no quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, hidden threshold replacement, or ambient completeness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy shared-tail modulus tail synchronization]
\label{thm:cauchy-shared-tail-modulus-tail-synchronization}
Every accepted $\CauchySharedTailModulusUp$ packet synchronizes the two tail requests through the same row $\tau$. In particular, any $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\StreamNameUp$ consumer of the packet reads $M_0$ and $M_1$ before $\tau$, reads both windows $W_0,W_1$ only through $\tau$, and reads the two regular outputs $Q_0,Q_1$ only after the shared dyadic ledger $D$ has been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier definition. The two request rows $M_0,M_1$ and the single threshold row $\tau$ are separate displayed coordinates, but there is no second threshold coordinate.

Use the local obligations of \autoref{thm:cauchy-shared-tail-modulus-namecert-obligations}. Paired request exposure forces both requests to be visible before the threshold is consumed, and shared-threshold exposure forces both window services through the same $\tau,D$ rows.

The sibling $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$ route reads the same threshold as its shared-tail row. Thus every tail-meet, StreamName, or RegSeqRat consumer remains on the finite route through $M_0,M_1,\tau,D,W_0,W_1,Q_0,Q_1$.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:cauchy-shared-tail-modulus-real-seal-handoff}
