\begin{definition}[Cellular orbit substrate carrier]
\label{def:cellular-orbit-substrate-carrier}
A $\CellularOrbitSubstrateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(R,I,W,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the local cellular rule-table row, including the finite alphabet, the neighborhood radius, and every local transition entry used by the packet.
\item $I$ is the initial row, displayed as a finite spatial window with boundary convention and origin marker.
\item $W$ is the finite orbit-window ledger recording the displayed rows obtained by iterating $R$ from $I$ for the accepted time budget and spatial window.
\item $L$ is the local-neighborhood ledger listing, for each accepted successor cell in $W$, the visible predecessor neighborhood and the rule-table entry used to compute it.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for rule tables, initial rows, orbit windows, local-neighborhood ledgers, provenance rows, and local names.
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ replay route from $R$ and $I$ through each displayed row of $W$ and each neighborhood read in $L$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the cellular substrate reading, the sibling computational certificates, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CellularOrbitSubstrateUp}$ row for carrier admission, local determinacy, observation non-escape, and public name exposure.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rule table, hsame initial row, finite orbit window, local-neighborhood ledger, transport row, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row. It has no coordinate for an infinite orbit, a hidden global evaluator, a quotient stream, host equality between cellular histories, or an external cellular oracle.

\begin{theorem}[Cellular orbit substrate local determinacy]
\label{thm:cellular-orbit-substrate-local-determinacy}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cellular-orbit-substrate-carrier}, if two accepted packets expose the same rule-table row $R$ and $\hsame$ initial rows $I$ and $I'$, then every displayed next-row entry accepted by the finite orbit-window ledger is determined by the same local-neighborhood read in $L$. In particular, the public classifier cannot expose two distinct next-row ledgers under the same rule table and hsame initial row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both accepted packets to their rule-table, initial-row, orbit-window, and local-neighborhood ledger rows. The rule table $R$ fixes a single successor value for each displayed predecessor neighborhood, and the $\hsame$ initial rows identify the first visible neighborhoods componentwise.

Use the continuation replay row $C$ to step through the finite window ledger. At each displayed successor cell, the ledger $L$ records the predecessor neighborhood and the rule-table entry used to compute it, so two accepted ledgers with the same $R$ and transported initial row must read the same entry.

The classifier has no coordinate for a detached evaluator or hidden cellular history. Therefore a claimed second next-row ledger would need a displayed neighborhood read absent from $L$ or a second value for the same entry of $R$, neither of which belongs to the carrier.
\end{proof}
