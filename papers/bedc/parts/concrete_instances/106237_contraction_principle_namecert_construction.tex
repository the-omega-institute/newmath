\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ContractionPrincipleUp$}

Following Banach and Bishop--Bridges constructive metric analysis, the $\ContractionPrincipleUp$ packet records the theorem-level completion-neighbour object that connects the contraction-map surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-contractionmapping-namecert}, the finite iteration ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fixed-point-iteration-namecert}, the complete-metric handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, and the endpoint-facing consumer discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-banach-fixed-point-namecert}. The packet names the finite route through which contraction data becomes completion-facing evidence; it does not itself choose a fixed point or assert ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[ContractionPrinciple carrier]
\label{def:contraction-principle-carrier}
A $\ContractionPrincipleUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
R=(X,d,T,q,I,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\MetricUp$ carrier row whose point histories and classifier are read by the contraction route.
\item $d$ is the metric-distance row used for all finite source, target, and iterate-distance comparisons.
\item $T$ is the $\ContractionMappingUp$ graph row, exposing the self-map application surface without host function extensionality.
\item $q$ is the rational contraction-bound ledger, including the displayed nonnegative row and strict unit-bound row consumed by finite estimates.
\item $I$ is the $\FixedPointIterationUp$ route, displaying the initial row, finite iterate window, and residual-budget rows generated from $T$ and $q$.
\item $L$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff row, naming the completion-facing service that may consume the finite Cauchy evidence without being stored as ambient completeness in the contraction packet.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, distance row, contraction graph, rational bound, finite iteration route, completion handoff, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for metric reads, contraction graph inspection, bound comparison, finite iteration extension, completion handoff, and consumer readback.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\MetricUp$, $\ContractionMappingUp$, $\FixedPointIterationUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\BanachFixedPointUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ContractionPrincipleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a theorem-level route packet. It contains no selected fixed point, no completed endpoint row, no host equality of maps, and no completeness assertion outside the displayed handoff row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ContractionPrinciple NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:contraction-principle-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\ContractionPrincipleUp$ carrier $R=(X,d,T,q,I,L,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ContractionPrincipleUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission, contraction-bound stability, iterate visibility, complete-metric handoff visibility, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance stability, and local naming for the packet of \autoref{def:contraction-principle-carrier}. Every accepted read of the principle is routed through these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission projects the displayed tuple $X,d,T,q,I,L,H,C,P,N$. The contraction graph row and rational bound ledger supply the map-level comparison surface, while the metric row is the only source of distance reads.

Iterate visibility is supplied by $I$. It records only finite Picard windows and residual budgets generated from $T$ and $q$, so it cannot introduce a completed orbit or a selected endpoint.

The completion handoff is supplied by $L$, transported by $H$, and replayed by $C$. Provenance and local naming are then read from $P$ and $N$, giving exactly the listed NameCert obligations and no additional analytic coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ContractionPrinciple completion handoff]
\label{thm:contraction-principle-completion-handoff}
Every $\BanachFixedPointUp$ or $\CompleteMetricUp$ consumer of an accepted $\ContractionPrincipleUp$ carrier must pass through the displayed route
$$
\ContractionMappingUp,\quad
\FixedPointIterationUp,\quad
\CompleteMetricUp,\quad
\MetricUp,\quad
\NameCert_{\ContractionPrincipleUp}.
$$
Equivalently, the consumer reads the rows $X,d,T,q,I,L,H,C,P,N$ before any endpoint-facing fixed-point seal can be consumed. The handoff exports contraction-map evidence, finite iterate evidence, metric-distance reads, complete-metric service visibility, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming; it does not export a selected fixed point, a completed orbit endpoint, host fixed-point equality, quotient equality of maps, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the contraction surface. The rows $T$ and $q$ are read as the $\ContractionMappingUp$ graph and rational bound ledger over the $\MetricUp$ source $X,d$.

Next read the finite iteration route $I$. It exposes the $\FixedPointIterationUp$ orbit window and residual budget that a completion-facing consumer may inspect before any endpoint row is available.

Finally pass through $L,H,C,P,N$. The row $L$ is only a $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff, while $H$ and $C$ preserve the same componentwise route under transport and replay. Therefore a $\BanachFixedPointUp$ or $\CompleteMetricUp$ consumer receives the displayed theorem-level route but must provide its own endpoint or completeness discipline downstream.
\end{proof}
