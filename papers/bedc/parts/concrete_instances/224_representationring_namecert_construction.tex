\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RepresentationRingUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-representationring-namecert}

The representation-ring interface $\RepresentationRingUp$ packages the Grothendieck ring of finite-dimensional representations of a group under direct sum and tensor product. It depends on $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\RingUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ring-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:representationring-carrier}

\begin{definition}[RepresentationRingUp BHist representation packet]
\label{def:representationring-bhist-representation-packet}
A $\RepresentationRingUp$ representation packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$
tuple
$$
\mathcal{K}=(G,R,V,S,T,P,\rho,\lambda).
$$
The row $G$ is the consumed $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ packet, and $R$ is the
consumed $\NameCert_{\RingUp}$ packet. The row $V$ is the finite list of
representation endpoints carried as $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows: each endpoint has
its group action source read through $G$ and its additive and multiplicative
target rows read through $R$. The row $S$ records the displayed direct-sum
faces, and $T$ records the displayed tensor-product faces. Both are finite
$\Cont$ ledgers whose endpoints are members of $V$.

The row $P$ is the provenance packet listing the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows consumed
from $G$ and $R$ and the package rows attached to $S$ and $T$. The row $\rho$
is the Grothendieck classifier row naming formal direct-sum balance among the
displayed representation endpoints. The finite ledger $\lambda$ is the
$\Cont$ list containing exactly the consumed GroupUp rows, consumed RingUp
rows, representation rows, direct-sum rows, tensor rows, classifier row, and
provenance rows exposed by the public packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[RepresentationRingUp Grothendieck ledger classifier]
\label{def:representationring-grothendieck-ledger-classifier}
For two representation packets
$$
\mathcal{K}=(G,R,V,S,T,P,\rho,\lambda)
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\mathcal{K}'=(G',R',V',S',T',P',\rho',\lambda'),
$$
the Grothendieck ledger classifier
$\mathcal{K}\sim_{\RepresentationRingUp}^{\mathrm{gr}}\mathcal{K}'$
is the finite componentwise transport datum consisting of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item an $\hsame$ transport row between the consumed $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$
  packets $G$ and $G'$;
  \item an $\hsame$ transport row between the consumed $\NameCert_{\RingUp}$
  packets $R$ and $R'$;
  \item an entrywise $\hsame$ transport row between the displayed
  representation endpoint lists $V$ and $V'$;
  \item entrywise $\hsame$ transport rows between the direct-sum ledgers $S$
  and $S'$ and between the tensor-product ledgers $T$ and $T'$;
  \item an $\hsame$ transport row between the provenance packets $P$ and
  $P'$;
  \item an $\hsame$ transport row between the Grothendieck classifier rows
  $\rho$ and $\rho'$;
  \item a finite $\Cont$ ledger comparison between $\lambda$ and $\lambda'$,
  entry by entry.
\end{enumerate}
Thus equality in the public representation-ring carrier is not host equality
of representation categories. It is the displayed $\hsame$ transport of the
consumed GroupUp packet, consumed RingUp packet, representation rows,
direct-sum rows, tensor rows, provenance, and finite continuation ledger.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:representationring-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentationRingUp carrier BHist boundary]
\label{thm:representationring-carrier-bhist-boundary}
Every public endpoint of a $\RepresentationRingUp$ carrier packet of
\autoref{def:representationring-bhist-representation-packet} is one of the
displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows in
$G,R,V,S,T,P,\rho,\lambda$. In particular, every representation endpoint is
scoped through the consumed $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\RingUp}$ certificates, and no additional group carrier, ring
carrier, module carrier, or host representation object is exported at the
boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted packet to the tuple
$\mathcal{K}=(G,R,V,S,T,P,\rho,\lambda)$. The carrier definition lists all
public rows in that tuple, so a public endpoint can only be the consumed
GroupUp row, the consumed RingUp row, a representation endpoint from $V$, a
direct-sum row from $S$, a tensor row from $T$, a provenance row from $P$, the
Grothendieck classifier row $\rho$, or an entry of the finite ledger
$\lambda$.

For a representation endpoint in $V$, the same projection reads its action
source through $G$ and its target algebra rows through $R$. The direct-sum
and tensor rows are ledger faces whose endpoints are members of $V$, so they
also remain scoped by those two consumed certificates.

Conversely, any row from the displayed tuple repacks into the public carrier
surface by the constructor pattern of
\autoref{def:representationring-bhist-representation-packet}. No external
representation object is needed, because the boundary is exactly the
finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentationRingUp direct-sum tensor ledger stability]
\label{thm:representationring-direct-sum-tensor-ledger-stability}
If two packets of
\autoref{def:representationring-bhist-representation-packet} are related by
the classifier of
\autoref{def:representationring-grothendieck-ledger-classifier}, then the
direct-sum faces and tensor-product faces of the first packet transport to
the corresponding faces of the second packet through $\hsame$ and the finite
$\Cont$ ledger comparison. The transported packet preserves the consumed
$\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\RingUp}$ sources and the
Grothendieck classifier row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the classifier between
$\mathcal{K}=(G,R,V,S,T,P,\rho,\lambda)$ and
$\mathcal{K}'=(G',R',V',S',T',P',\rho',\lambda')$. It supplies transported
GroupUp and RingUp dependency rows, entrywise endpoint transport between
$V$ and $V'$, entrywise ledger transport between $S$ and $S'$ and between
$T$ and $T'$, provenance transport, classifier-row transport, and a finite
comparison between $\lambda$ and $\lambda'$.

Read a direct-sum face from $S$. Its endpoints are displayed members of
$V$, so the endpoint comparison in the classifier carries them to the
matching members of $V'$. The ledger comparison then carries the $\Cont$
row witnessing the direct-sum face to the corresponding entry of $S'$.

The tensor-product case is identical except that the ledger entry is read
from $T$. The transported endpoints still read their action source through
$G'$ and their target rows through $R'$, because the classifier transported
the consumed GroupUp and RingUp packets at the same time.

Finally transport $\rho$ to $\rho'$ and $P$ to $P'$. Repacking these rows
gives a representation-ring packet with stable direct-sum and tensor faces
over the transported finite ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentationRingUp NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:representationring-namecert-obligation-surface}
The $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$ obligation surface is the package
consisting of the carrier packet of
\autoref{def:representationring-bhist-representation-packet}, the
Grothendieck ledger classifier of
\autoref{def:representationring-grothendieck-ledger-classifier}, the carrier
boundary of \autoref{thm:representationring-carrier-bhist-boundary}, the
direct-sum and tensor stability row of
\autoref{thm:representationring-direct-sum-tensor-ledger-stability}, exactness
of the finite $\Cont$ and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ ledger rows, and the consumer
boundary used by $\InducedRepUp$ through $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$.
The consumer may read the consumed GroupUp packet, consumed RingUp packet,
representation endpoints, direct-sum faces, tensor faces, Grothendieck
classifier row, provenance rows, and finite ledger rows, but it receives no
additional induction functor, restriction functor, character row, or
host-level representation category.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and classifier definitions. The carrier supplies the
finite row family, while the classifier specifies componentwise $\hsame$
transport for each dependency, endpoint list, operation face, provenance
packet, classifier row, and ledger entry.

Apply the carrier boundary theorem to identify the public endpoints. This
shows that every exported representation-ring row is already one of the
displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows and that the representation endpoints remain
scoped by the consumed GroupUp and RingUp certificates.

Next apply the direct-sum and tensor stability theorem. It transports the two
operation faces through the same endpoint comparisons and the same finite
$\Cont$ ledger comparison, so the Grothendieck classifier row remains attached
to the transported packet.

Exactness follows by inspecting the displayed rows. The only provenance rows
are the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows in $P$, and the only continuation rows are the
entries of $\lambda$ together with the displayed direct-sum and tensor faces.
Thus every public row is accounted for by projection from the packet.

For the downstream boundary, an $\InducedRepUp$ consumer reads precisely this
public surface: representation endpoints together with direct-sum, tensor,
classifier, provenance, and dependency rows. Induction, restriction,
Frobenius reciprocity, and character-theoretic data are not fields of
$\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$ and must be supplied by their own
certificates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentationRingUp InducedRep consumer boundary]
\label{thm:representationring-consumer-boundary}
An $\InducedRepUp$ consumer of $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$ may read
only the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows displayed in the carrier packet
$\mathcal{K}=(G,R,V,S,T,P,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:representationring-bhist-representation-packet}: the consumed
$\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ row $G$, the consumed $\NameCert_{\RingUp}$ row $R$,
the representation endpoint list $V$, the direct-sum ledger $S$, the
tensor-product ledger $T$, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance packet $P$, the
Grothendieck classifier row $\rho$, and the finite $\Cont$ ledger
$\lambda$. Any induction, restriction, Frobenius, subgroup-inclusion, or
character-theoretic row required by $\InducedRepUp$ lies outside this
RepresentationRingUp boundary unless it is separately supplied by the
$\InducedRepUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the consumer request to the carrier packet $\mathcal{K}$. The carrier
definition lists exactly the rows $G,R,V,S,T,P,\rho,\lambda$, and each
representation endpoint in $V$ reads its action source through $G$ and its
algebra target through $R$.

The operation rows are also already named. A direct-sum request lands in the
finite $\Cont$ ledger $S$, a tensor-product request lands in the finite
$\Cont$ ledger $T$, and every public continuation read is an entry of
$\lambda$. Provenance is read through the displayed $\mathsf{Pkg}$ packet
$P$.

Repack these projections as the representation-ring part of an
$\InducedRepUp$ input. The repacking gives a concrete BEDC source boundary:
the downstream packet receives the rows named above and no host
representation category, induction functor, restriction functor, Frobenius
adjunction, or character row from $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentationRingUp classifier exactness]
\label{thm:representationring-classifier-exactness}
For packets $\mathcal{K}$ and $\mathcal{K}'$ of
\autoref{def:representationring-bhist-representation-packet}, the
Grothendieck ledger classifier
$\mathcal{K}\sim_{\RepresentationRingUp}^{\mathrm{gr}}\mathcal{K}'$ of
\autoref{def:representationring-grothendieck-ledger-classifier} is exact for
the public carrier: its projections are precisely the $\hsame$ rows for the
consumed $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\RingUp}$ packets, the
entrywise representation rows, the direct-sum and tensor $\Cont$ ledgers, the
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance packet, the Grothendieck row $\rho$, and the
finite ledger comparison for $\lambda$. Conversely, those displayed
projections repack into the classifier without using host equality of
representation categories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the classifier between
$\mathcal{K}=(G,R,V,S,T,P,\rho,\lambda)$ and
$\mathcal{K}'=(G',R',V',S',T',P',\rho',\lambda')$. Its fields are the two
dependency comparisons, endpoint-list comparison, operation-ledger
comparisons, provenance comparison, Grothendieck-row comparison, and finite
ledger comparison.

Each field is a projection of a public row of the carrier packet. The GroupUp
and RingUp comparisons account for the dependency rows; the endpoint and
operation comparisons account for $V,S,T$; the provenance comparison accounts
for $P$; the Grothendieck comparison accounts for $\rho$; and the finite
ledger comparison accounts for $\lambda$.

For the converse direction, begin with exactly those displayed projections.
Place them in the same ordered slots required by
\autoref{def:representationring-grothendieck-ledger-classifier}. This
constructs the classifier row by repacking finite BEDC data, so no
source-level equivalence of representation categories is invoked.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentationRingUp operation ledger coverage]
\label{thm:representationring-operation-ledger-coverage}
Every public operation row of a $\RepresentationRingUp$ carrier packet is
covered by the finite ledgers $S$, $T$, and $\lambda$ of
\autoref{def:representationring-bhist-representation-packet}. Direct-sum
operations are exactly the rows of $S$, tensor-product operations are exactly
the rows of $T$, their endpoints are members of the representation endpoint
list $V$, and their dependency and provenance reads factor through the
consumed $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ row $G$, the consumed $\NameCert_{\RingUp}$
row $R$, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ packet $P$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project a public operation row from the packet
$\mathcal{K}=(G,R,V,S,T,P,\rho,\lambda)$. The carrier definition has only two
operation ledgers: $S$ for direct sums and $T$ for tensor products. Thus the
row is read from one of those finite $\Cont$ ledgers.

In the direct-sum case, the endpoints of the selected row are displayed
members of $V$. Their group-action source is obtained by projecting $G$, and
their additive target rows are obtained by projecting $R$. The relevant
continuation entry is also listed in $\lambda$, and its package provenance is
listed in $P$.

The tensor-product case is parallel. The selected row lies in $T$, its
endpoints are members of $V$, its dependency rows are again $G$ and $R$, and
its public continuation and package reads are entries of $\lambda$ and $P$.
Repacking these projections covers every operation row and leaves no
unlisted operation at the boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentationRingUp finite BHist source exhaustion]
\label{thm:representationring-bhist-finite-source-exhaustion}
The finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ source of a $\RepresentationRingUp$ public packet
is exhausted by the displayed tuple
$G,R,V,S,T,P,\rho,\lambda$. Every public carrier, classifier, operation,
ledger, provenance, and downstream-consumer row is a projection of that tuple
or a row named by one of its finite $\Cont$ ledgers. No ambient group object,
ambient ring object, module category, character table, or representation
category is a separate source row of the public certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier packet. Its dependency sources are exactly $G$ and $R$,
its representation endpoints are exactly the finite list $V$, and its
operation sources are exactly the finite ledgers $S$ and $T$. The classifier
source is the row $\rho$, provenance is the packet $P$, and public
continuation data is the ledger $\lambda$.

Now consider any public read. A carrier read is one of those fields. A
classifier read is a projection of
\autoref{def:representationring-grothendieck-ledger-classifier}. An operation
read is covered by \autoref{thm:representationring-operation-ledger-coverage}.
A consumer read for $\InducedRepUp$ is covered by
\autoref{thm:representationring-consumer-boundary}.

These cases exhaust the finite packet. Repacking the selected projections
returns a row of the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ source, and there is no remaining
slot where an ambient host object or representation category could enter as a
public source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RepresentationRingUp public NameCert surface]
\label{thm:representationring-public-namecert-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$ surface consists exactly of
the $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier packet of
\autoref{def:representationring-bhist-representation-packet}, the
Grothendieck ledger classifier of
\autoref{def:representationring-grothendieck-ledger-classifier}, the
consumer boundary of \autoref{thm:representationring-consumer-boundary}, the
classifier exactness of \autoref{thm:representationring-classifier-exactness},
the operation-ledger coverage of
\autoref{thm:representationring-operation-ledger-coverage}, and the finite
source exhaustion of
\autoref{thm:representationring-bhist-finite-source-exhaustion}. This surface
exports the GroupUp and RingUp dependency rows, finite representation
endpoint rows, finite $\Cont$ ledgers, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and the
Grothendieck classifier row, and it exports nothing outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier packet and classifier. They provide the finite BEDC
rows $G,R,V,S,T,P,\rho,\lambda$ and the componentwise comparisons between
two such packets. The classifier exactness theorem shows that these
comparisons are precisely the public classifier fields.

Next use the operation-ledger coverage theorem. It accounts for all direct
sum and tensor product rows through $S$, $T$, $\lambda$, and $P$, with
endpoints in $V$ and dependencies through $G$ and $R$.

The consumer boundary theorem supplies the downstream side: an
$\InducedRepUp$ packet may consume the same finite surface but receives no
induction, restriction, Frobenius, subgroup-inclusion, or character data from
the representation-ring certificate.

Finally apply finite source exhaustion. Every public row named by the
certificate is already a projection of the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
or of one of its finite $\Cont$ ledgers. Repacking those projections gives
the public $\NameCert_{\RepresentationRingUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}
