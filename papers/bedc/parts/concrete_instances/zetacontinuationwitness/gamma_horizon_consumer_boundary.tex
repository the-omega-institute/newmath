\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationWitness Gamma-horizon consumer boundary]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-gamma-horizon-consumer-boundary}
Every Gamma-dependent read of an accepted $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ packet terminates at the Gamma-horizon boundary row $G$ unless the requested datum is already one of the displayed functional-equation factors carried by $F$. The packet therefore exposes the Gamma factor only through $F,G,H,C,R,N$ and does not close a Gamma chapter, select a hidden branch, or supply an RH-facing analytic shortcut.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZetaContinuationWitnessUp.ZetaContinuationWitnessPacket\_gamma\_horizon\_consumer\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $W=(B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,R,N)$. The only Gamma-facing coordinates are the functional-equation route $F$ and the Gamma-horizon boundary row $G$.

Apply the NameCert obligation surface. Its Gamma-horizon clause states that a Gamma-dependent read reaches $G$ unless it is already a displayed functional-equation factor.

Apply the continuation handoff. The read is replayed through $C$, transported through $H$, and packaged and named by $R,N$, so the consumer receives the same finite boundary rather than a completed Gamma certificate.

Coordinate exhaustion excludes the analytic escape. The carrier has no hidden branch selector, closed Gamma theorem, RH predicate, or zero-location coordinate, so Gamma-dependent reads stop at the displayed horizon boundary.
\end{proof}
