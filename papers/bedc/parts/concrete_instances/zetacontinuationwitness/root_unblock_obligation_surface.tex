\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationWitness zero-facing source totality]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-zero-facing-source-totality}

\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZetaContinuationWitnessUp.ZetaContinuationWitnessPacket\_zero\_facing\_source\_totality}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet as $W=(B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,R,N)$. The dependency ledger identifies the $\ZetaBasicUp$, $\ZetaContUp$, $\AnalyticContUp$, $\HolomorphicUp$, $\ComplexUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows that a zero-facing consumer must meet before reading the packet.

Apply source-lock coverage. It fixes $B,E,A,F$ as the source, eta, analytic-continuation, and functional-equation face of the continuation route, while $H,C,R,N$ authenticate the transport, continuation, provenance, and local-name rows of that same route.

The pole, trivial-zero, and Gamma rows $P,T,G$ remain visible ledger coordinates, but they do not replace the source face. Thus every zero-facing consumer reaches the packet only after the displayed source and authentication rows have been exposed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationWitness Gamma functional boundary totality]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-gamma-functional-boundary-totality}
For an accepted $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ packet, every Gamma-dependent consumer read is exhausted by one of two displayed routes: it either uses the Gamma factor already present in the functional-equation route $F$, or it stops at the Gamma-horizon boundary row $G$. No such read exports a completed Gamma certificate, a hidden analytic branch, or a Gamma route outside $F,G,H,C,R,N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZetaContinuationWitnessUp.ZetaContinuationWitnessPacket\_gamma\_functional\_boundary\_totality}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligation surface. The surface lists the functional-equation route $F$ and Gamma-horizon boundary $G$ as explicit packet rows, and it treats Gamma-horizon non-escape as part of the accepted certificate.

Apply the pole-Gamma boundary theorem. A Gamma request that is already one of the displayed functional-equation factors is read through $F$; every other Gamma-dependent request reaches $G$ and remains a boundary row.

Finally use coordinate exhaustion for $W$. Since the packet contains no Gamma coordinate other than the displayed functional-equation route and boundary row, and since $H,C,R,N$ only transport and authenticate those coordinates, no completed Gamma certificate or hidden branch can be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationWitness critical-strip consumer non-escape]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-critical-strip-consumer-nonescape}
Every critical-strip consumer of an accepted $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ packet receives only the finite packet rows
$$
B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,R,N.
$$
The consumer receives no RH predicate, no non-trivial-zero locator, no global zero classifier, and no completed Gamma certificate as packet input.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier row. The accepted witness is the finite tuple with coordinates $B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,R,N$, so every packet read by a critical-strip consumer is a read from that displayed list.

Apply critical-strip input refusal and route separation. These rows exclude RH predicates, non-trivial-zero locators, global zero classifiers, and completed Gamma certificates from the packet inventory while keeping the consumer route inside the displayed source lock.

The Gamma functional boundary closes the remaining escape route. Gamma-dependent reads either use $F$ or stop at $G$, so the critical-strip consumer obtains no completed Gamma object and no hidden route to an RH-facing or zero-location predicate.
\end{proof}
