\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationWitness carrier row]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-carrier-row}
Every accepted $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ witness exposes the rows
$$
B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,R,N
$$
as $\BHist$-carrier coordinates before any zeta-zero consumer reads the packet. The rows are the source, eta route, analytic-continuation row, pole ledger, functional-equation route, trivial-zero ledger, Gamma boundary, transports, continuation routes, provenance, and local name of the same finite witness carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:zeta-continuation-witness-carrier}. The accepted witness is the displayed packet $W=(B,E,A,P,F,T,G,H,C,R,N)$, so each listed row is a carrier coordinate.

Apply \autoref{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-critical-strip-consumer-scope}. Every critical-strip or zeta-zero-facing consumer receives only the displayed rows of that packet, and it receives them before any downstream read is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ZetaContinuationWitness contour route]
\label{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-contour-route}
Every contour-facing analytic read of an accepted $\ZetaContinuationWitnessUp$ packet factors through the displayed eta, analytic-continuation, functional-equation, pole, Gamma-boundary, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. It does not factor through a hidden analytic branch, an RH predicate, a non-trivial-zero locator, or a completed Gamma certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with source-lock coverage. By \autoref{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-source-lock-coverage}, a continuation read exposes $B,E,A,F$ together with the exceptional ledgers before the packet is consumed.

Use the functional-boundary and Gamma-ledger theorems for the contour side. \autoref{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-functional-boundary-scoped-exactness} confines functional, pole, and Gamma reads to $P,F,G,H,C,R,N$, and \autoref{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-gamma-ledger-exhaustion} rules out a completed Gamma or hidden analytic branch as an additional contour coordinate.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:zeta-continuation-witness-no-rh-nonescape}. The contour-facing route stays inside the same displayed witness package and therefore cannot export RH data or a non-trivial-zero locator.
\end{proof}
