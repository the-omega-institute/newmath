\item $G$ records the generator-closure rows for \texttt{closed\_under\_shift\_classifier} and \texttt{closed\_under\_substitute\_classifier};

\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no typing preservation theorem, no subject-reduction discharge inhabitant, no confluence theorem, no normalization theorem, no hidden kernel soundness premise, and no generator outside the displayed shift, substitution, beta-step, and finite beta-chain rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCICClosureTrace substitution and shift generator package]
\label{thm:metacicclosuretrace-substitution-shift-generator-package}
For every accepted $\MetaCICClosureTraceUp$ carrier $T=(S,U,V,B,R,G,K,H,C,P,N)$, the rows $S,U,V,G$ form a finite generator package for the closedness classifier. The shift row $S$ authorizes the one-step lift at the successor boundary, the variable row $U$ and full-substitution row $V$ authorize substitution at the receiving boundary, and the generator row $G$ records exactly those two generator-closure classifier witnesses as the package exported to downstream closure consumers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed packet $T$. The shift row and the two substitution rows are already separated as $S,U,V$, so the carrier has a finite inventory before any downstream consumer is considered.

Read the generator row $G$. Its shift coordinate is backed by the same closedness-preservation theorem named by $S$, and its substitution coordinate is backed by the variable and full-substitution theorem rows $U,V$.

Finally inspect $H,C,P,N$. Transport and continuation replay preserve the displayed package rows, while provenance and local naming bind them to the ClosurePreservation audit map. Since no other generator coordinate occurs in $T$, the exported generator package is exhausted by $S,U,V,G$.
\end{proof}
