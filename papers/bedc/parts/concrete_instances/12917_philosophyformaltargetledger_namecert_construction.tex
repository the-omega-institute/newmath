\begin{definition}[Philosophy formal-target ledger carrier]
\label{def:philosophy-formal-target-ledger-carrier}
A $\PhilosophyFormalTargetLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(R,T,G,D,S,U,L,M,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the concept-registry row, recording the concept entry, carrier or boundary position, classifier status, ledger status, forbidden readings, and cannot-claim conflicts.
\item $T$ is the theorem-map row, recording the theorem or statement site, its claim-entry row, its expected machine namespace, and its dependency surface.
\item $G$ is the gap-map row, recording the typed open boundary, refusal branch, or target socket associated with the claim.
\item $D$ is the tradition-map row, recording preserved structure, rejected surplus, and tradition-comparison boundary rows.
\item $S$ is the scientific-map row, recording empirical record, invariant, bridge, value, and prediction-gap rows when the target touches science-facing prose.
\item $U$ is the cannot-claim registry row, recording the exact claim forms refused by the packet.
\item $L$ is the closure-status row, recording the paper-side closure grade, formal-verification grade, bridge status, not-claimed surface, and next obligation for the target.
\item $M$ is the formal-target skeleton row, recording name, namespace, binder shape, statement outline, dependency list, expected status, and queue status.
\item $B$ is the refusal-boundary and no-smuggling row, recording absence of host truth predicates, subject-substance, quotient equality, Classical choice, external philosophy authority, and any hidden solution claim.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $R,T,G,D,S,U,L,M,B$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the registry, theorem, gap, tradition, science, refusal, closure-status, target-skeleton, and no-smuggling rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$, $\MachineInterfaceFormalizationTargetUp$, $\WritingItemAuditUp$, $\AxiomPurityGateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PhilosophyFormalTargetLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a host truth predicate, subject-substance, quotient equality, Classical choice, external philosophical authority, proof content for the target skeleton, or export beyond the displayed refusal boundary.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Philosophy formal-target ledger nonescape]
\label{thm:philosophy-formal-target-ledger-nonescape}
Every machine-readable audit consumer of an accepted $\PhilosophyFormalTargetLedgerUp$ packet reads a claim-entry row, target-skeleton row, no-smuggling row, closure-status row, and refusal-boundary row before it can read the philosophy-package formal target. If any one of these rows is absent, the consumer leaves the accepted carrier and cannot cite the packet as a formal-target ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:philosophy-formal-target-ledger-namecert-obligations}. The obligations expose claim-entry coverage through $R,T$, target-skeleton coverage through $M$, no-smuggling and refusal-boundary coverage through $B,U$, and closure-status coverage through $L$.

The audit consumer has no alternate route to those data. The continuation row $C$ only replays the displayed path, the transport row $H$ only preserves componentwise rows, and the provenance row $P$ only records the sibling surfaces already named by the packet.

Therefore removing a claim-entry row, target-skeleton row, no-smuggling row, closure-status row, or refusal-boundary row changes the carrier being read. The resulting object may be a partial registry or target record, but it is not an accepted $\PhilosophyFormalTargetLedgerUp$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

\end{proof}
