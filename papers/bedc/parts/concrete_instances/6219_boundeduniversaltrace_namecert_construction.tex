\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-universal-trace-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$ packet is the finite host-side trace surface for the strong-normalisation boundary of \autoref{ch:visions-strong-normalization-vs-turing-completeness}. It combines the bounded machine substrate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-turingmachine-namecert}, the cellular substrate row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cellularautomaton-namecert}, and the finite continuation ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtrace-namecert}. The object does not assert that an unbounded computation halts; it exposes exactly the fuel-indexed rows from which a total host may read a terminal row.

\falsifiablePrediction{Removing the fuel row permits a consumer to read terminal output without a displayed finite trace ledger, which the local classifier rejects.}

\independenceWitness{turingmachine, continuationtrace, cellularautomaton, continuationbigstep, haltingdistinctiontrace: none is bijective because $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$ carries the fuel-bound, substrate row, and trace ledger in one packet, while each sibling lacks at least one of those coordinates or replaces the bounded packet by a different semantic surface.}

\begin{definition}[BoundedUniversalTrace carrier]
\label{def:bounded-universal-trace-carrier}
A $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(F,M,S,T,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the fuel row, naming the finite step bound used by the total host read;
\item $M$ is a substrate row drawn from the displayed $\TuringMachineUp$ or $\CellularAutomatonUp$ interface;
\item $S$ is the finite source row containing the initial configuration, initial cellular window, or encoded substrate input;
\item $T$ is the finite $\ContinuationTraceUp$ ledger listing the step rows admitted below the bound $F$;
\item $R$ is the terminal or readback row selected from the displayed ledger $T$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $F,M,S,T,R,P,N$;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes that replay the bounded step ledger and the terminal readback;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$, $\TuringMachineUp$, $\CellularAutomatonUp$, $\ContinuationTraceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedUniversalTraceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted packets only by the displayed fuel, substrate, source, trace, readback, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows. It has no coordinate for an unbounded halting predicate, host evaluator equality, quotient behaviour, selected nontermination evidence, or universal-machine semantic completion.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedUniversalTrace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-universal-trace-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:bounded-universal-trace-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedUniversalTraceUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for $U=(F,M,S,T,R,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier transport by componentwise $\hsame$ over the displayed fuel, substrate, source, trace, readback, package, and naming rows;
\item step-indexed readback through $F$, $T$, and $R$;
\item non-escape excluding unbounded halting, host evaluator equality, quotient behaviour, selected nontermination evidence, and universal-machine semantic completion;
\item public export of the same finite packet for Lean formalization under $\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.BoundedUniversalTraceUp}$.
\end{enumerate}
No consumer can cite a total-host read of a universal substrate computation unless the read passes through these five rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:bounded-universal-trace-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the fuel, substrate, source, finite continuation ledger, readback, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows.

Classifier transport is componentwise. A comparison between accepted packets may transport rows through $H$ and replay routes through $C$, but it cannot compare hidden host evaluators, unbounded runs, quotient behaviours, or semantic completions because no such coordinate is carried.

The remaining rows are the bounded readback, non-escape, and public-export statements proved below. Together they exhaust the NameCert surface because every consumer-facing read is either a projection from the packet, a displayed route through the finite ledger, or a refusal caused by an absent coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedUniversalTrace step-indexed readback]
\label{thm:bounded-universal-trace-step-index-readback}
Every public read of the terminal row $R$ in an accepted $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$ carrier factors through the fuel row $F$ and the finite trace ledger $T$. The read is admitted only when $T$ displays a bounded route from the source row $S$ over the substrate row $M$ and $R$ is the terminal or readback row selected by that same finite ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet through \autoref{def:bounded-universal-trace-carrier}. The only computation-facing coordinates are the substrate row $M$, source row $S$, fuel row $F$, trace ledger $T$, readback row $R$, and route row $C$.

Apply the continuation-trace reading of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtrace-namecert}. A step read is public only as a finite ledger row, so selecting $R$ requires the displayed ledger entry in $T$ and the replay route in $C$.

The fuel row $F$ bounds the ledger inspected by the host. Since there is no coordinate for an unbounded trace, a consumer cannot read $R$ by bypassing $F$ or by replacing $T$ with a host-level run.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedUniversalTrace non-escape]
\label{thm:bounded-universal-trace-non-escape}
An accepted $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$ packet exports no unbounded halting predicate, no equality between host evaluators, no quotient behaviour, no selected nontermination witness, and no universal-machine semantic completion. Its visible computational content is exactly the finite packet $F,M,S,T,R,H,C,P,N$ of \autoref{def:bounded-universal-trace-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. The substrate row $M$ may name a $\TuringMachineUp$ or $\CellularAutomatonUp$ surface, but the packet stores only the bounded substrate row selected for this trace.

The trace row $T$ is finite and is read through the continuation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtrace-namecert}. The readback theorem above forces the terminal row through $F$ and $T$, so it supplies a step-indexed host read rather than an unbounded semantic claim.

The excluded objects require coordinates not present in the packet. Unbounded halting needs a global run predicate, host evaluator equality needs a host semantic equality row, quotient behaviour needs a quotient classifier, selected nontermination needs an infinite negative witness, and universal-machine semantic completion needs an all-input semantic surface. None is among $F,M,S,T,R,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedUniversalTrace public export]
\label{thm:bounded-universal-trace-public-export}
The public $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$ export is the finite carrier of \autoref{def:bounded-universal-trace-carrier} together with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:bounded-universal-trace-namecert-obligations}, the step-indexed readback of \autoref{thm:bounded-universal-trace-step-index-readback}, and the non-escape boundary of \autoref{thm:bounded-universal-trace-non-escape}. This export is the finite bridge surface by which the total host of \autoref{ch:visions-strong-normalization-vs-turing-completeness} may inspect universal-substrate traces without importing halting, partiality, host evaluator equality, quotient behaviour, or semantic completion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier supplies the displayed packet $F,M,S,T,R,H,C,P,N$. The NameCert theorem binds carrier exposure, classifier transport, readback, non-escape, and public export to those rows.

The readback theorem accounts for the only public terminal read. It must pass through the fuel row and finite trace ledger, so the host-facing surface remains step-indexed.

The non-escape theorem records what the packet cannot export. Repacking the carrier, readback, and refusal clauses gives a public certificate surface over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$, with the substrate-side anchors supplied by $\TuringMachineUp$, $\CellularAutomatonUp$, and $\ContinuationTraceUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedUniversalTrace fuel determinacy]
\label{thm:bounded-universal-trace-fuel-determinacy}
For every accepted $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$ carrier $U=(F,M,S,T,R,H,C,P,N)$, the terminal readback row $R$ is determined by the displayed fuel row $F$ together with the finite trace ledger $T$. Two accepted reads with the same $F$, the same substrate and source rows $M,S$, and the same ledger $T$ have the same public terminal row after the componentwise transports and routes $H,C,P,N$ are applied; no unbounded halting predicate or host evaluator equality contributes to the determination.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier through \autoref{def:bounded-universal-trace-carrier}. The only computation-facing coordinates are $F,M,S,T,R,H,C,P,N$.

Use the public export of \autoref{thm:bounded-universal-trace-public-export}. It exposes terminal readback through the same finite packet, so a read of $R$ must pass through the fuel row $F$ and the finite trace ledger $T$.

If two reads share $F,M,S,T$, the remaining comparison is componentwise transport and replay through $H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier includes no unbounded run row and no host evaluator equality row, those absent objects cannot distinguish the terminal readback.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedUniversalTrace substrate readback]
\label{thm:bounded-universal-trace-substrate-readback}
For every accepted $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$ carrier $U=(F,M,S,T,R,H,C,P,N)$, every substrate read factors through the displayed $\TuringMachineUp$ or $\CellularAutomatonUp$ row $M$ and the finite $\ContinuationTraceUp$ ledger $T$ before reaching the terminal row $R$. The packet exports a bounded substrate read, not a host evaluator for the unbounded substrate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the substrate coordinate in \autoref{def:bounded-universal-trace-carrier}. The row $M$ is explicitly drawn from the $\TuringMachineUp$ or $\CellularAutomatonUp$ interface, while $T$ is the finite continuation ledger below the fuel bound.

Apply \autoref{thm:bounded-universal-trace-step-index-readback} and \autoref{thm:bounded-universal-trace-public-export}. The terminal row is public only when the displayed continuation ledger routes the source row $S$ over the substrate row $M$ within the fuel row $F$.

Thus substrate information reaches consumers through $M,T,R,H,C,P,N$. No consumer receives an evaluator equality, an unbounded transition closure, or a semantic completion of the substrate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedUniversalTrace ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:bounded-universal-trace-ledger-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$ carrier $U=(F,M,S,T,R,H,C,P,N)$, the finite trace ledger $T$ exhausts every step row visible below the fuel bound $F$. A public consumer can inspect only the source row $S$, substrate row $M$, ledger rows in $T$, terminal readback $R$, and the displayed transport, route, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$; it cannot add hidden steps, appeal to unbounded halting, or identify traces by host evaluator equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted packet as the finite carrier $U=(F,M,S,T,R,H,C,P,N)$. The trace rows available below the bound are exactly the entries displayed in $T$.

Use \autoref{thm:bounded-universal-trace-step-index-readback}. Any terminal read must route through the same ledger $T$ and the same fuel row $F$, so every visible step used by the read is already part of the displayed finite trace.

Finally apply the non-escape boundary of \autoref{thm:bounded-universal-trace-non-escape}. Hidden steps, unbounded halting, quotient behaviour, and host evaluator equality require coordinates absent from the carrier, so the ledger $T$ exhausts the accepted bounded trace surface.
\end{proof}
