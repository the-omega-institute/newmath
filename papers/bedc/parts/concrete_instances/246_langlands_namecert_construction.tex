\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LanglandsUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-langlands-namecert}

The Langlands-correspondence interface $\LanglandsUp$ packages the conjectural bijection between $n$-dimensional Galois representations of a global field and irreducible automorphic representations of $GL_n$ matching local L-factors at almost all places. It depends on $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-galoisgroup-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\AutomorphicUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-automorphic-namecert}), and bridges Galois and automorphic certificates through a common $L$-factor classifier.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:langlands-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Langlands correspondence ledger carrier]
\label{def:langlands-bhist-correspondence-carrier}
Fix a $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ source row, a $\NameCert_{\AutomorphicUp}$ source row, and a finite list of observed places at which both sources expose local factor answers. A $\LanglandsUp$ correspondence carrier row is the $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger row consisting of the two source certificates, the visible Galois and automorphic answer histories at the selected places, the local $L$-factor package rows read from those answers, and the $\Cont$ witnesses that place the paired local rows inside one finite observation packet.

The carrier records only the observed packet. It does not assert an all-place correspondence, irreducibility, analytic continuation, functoriality, or uniqueness beyond the displayed finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Langlands local factor classifier]
\label{def:langlands-lfactor-classifier}
Two rows of \autoref{def:langlands-bhist-correspondence-carrier} are classified as the same $\LanglandsUp$ packet exactly when their visible local $L$-factor packages are matched place by place, their source certificate rows are transported through the corresponding $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\AutomorphicUp}$ classifiers, and their answer histories are related by the available $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ witnesses.

Equivalently, the classifier is the least relation generated by source-certificate transport, answer-history transport, local package agreement, and finite concatenation of the observed place rows. No hidden global correspondence row is a generator of the classifier.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:langlands-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Langlands source certificate scope]
\label{thm:langlands-source-certificate-scope}
Every carried $\LanglandsUp$ packet of \autoref{def:langlands-bhist-correspondence-carrier} has exactly two external source certificate dependencies: one $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ row and one $\NameCert_{\AutomorphicUp}$ row. All remaining data in the packet are finite answer histories, local $L$-factor package rows, and the $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ witnesses that connect those rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LanglandsUp.LanglandsCorrespondenceLedger\_source\_certificate\_scope}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier row. Its first two fields are the Galois and automorphic source certificates, and each observed place contributes only the two visible answer histories and the local package rows read from them.

The classifier of \autoref{def:langlands-lfactor-classifier} transports these fields through the two source $\NameCert$ classifiers and through the displayed history and package witnesses. Since no constructor introduces a third source certificate or a global host theorem, the stated scope is exact.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Langlands local factor stability]
\label{thm:langlands-local-factor-stability}
If two carried $\LanglandsUp$ packets are classified by \autoref{def:langlands-lfactor-classifier}, then replacing either source representative by a $\NameCert$-classified representative and replacing any visible local answer by an $\hsame$-related answer preserves the classified local $L$-factor packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LanglandsUp.LanglandsLocalFactor\_stability}
\begin{proof}
First transport the source side. The $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\AutomorphicUp}$ rows supply the certificate-level replacement witnesses, and the carrier keeps the transported rows inside the same finite observation packet by $\Cont$.

Next transport the local factor rows. At each observed place, the classifier stores the package comparison as a $\Pkg$ row and the answer-history comparison as an $\hsame$ row. Finite concatenation of these rows gives a classifier witness for the whole packet after the replacement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Langlands correspondence obligation package]
\label{thm:langlands-correspondence-obligation-package}
The $\LanglandsUp$ chapter exports a finite correspondence obligation package: a carrier row, a local factor classifier row, the two source certificate rows, and the ledger exactness statement that the packet hides only the finite observed matching row. It does not claim a global Langlands correspondence theorem.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LanglandsUp.LanglandsLFactorClassifier\_endpoint\_confluence}
\begin{proof}
Build the package from a carried row and its classifier witness. The carrier supplies the observed Galois answer rows, automorphic answer rows, local package rows, and $\Cont$ links. The classifier supplies the source transport and the visible place-by-place package comparisons.

Ledger exactness is obtained by inverting these two displayed structures. Every accepted datum is either one of the two source certificates, a finite answer history, a local factor package row, or a transport witness already named by $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, or $\NameCert$. Hence the package contains no hidden all-place bijection or analytic assertion.
\end{proof}
