\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SymGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-symgroup-namecert}

The symmetric-group interface $\SymGroupUp$ packages the group structure on $\NameCert_{\PermutationUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-permutation-namecert}) under composition. It is a canonical instance of $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}) and the substrate for representation-theoretic certificates.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:symgroup-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[SymGroupUp carrier obligation]
\label{thm:symgroup-carrier-obligation}
The $\SymGroupUp$ carrier is the certified permutation carrier over
$\NameCert_{\PermutationUp}$ together with the $\GroupUp$ source boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}. A carried row is therefore a
$\PermutationUp$ source row whose visible operation is certified composition,
whose displayed unit is the certified identity permutation, and whose displayed
inverse is the certified inverse permutation. The carrier boundary records only
the $\mathsf{BHist}$ graph rows, inverse rows, action row, $\Pkg$ provenance,
and ledger entries already exposed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-permutation-namecert};
it does not expose a host enumeration or a host function as the group object.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SymGroupUp.SymGroupCarrier\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Read the permutation source row from \autoref{def:permutation-bijection-source-row}.
Its carrier consists of graph, inverse graph, inverse-witness, classifier,
action, and ledger rows over the fixed $\NameCert_{\FinSetUp}$ source.

The $\GroupUp$ boundary from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}
asks for a carried operation, unit, inverse, classifier, and ledger. Use
\autoref{thm:permutation-composition-inverse-action-obligation} to supply
composition, identity, inverse, and action compatibility from the same certified
finite-position rows.

Thus the $\SymGroupUp$ carrier is obtained by repacking the already certified
$\PermutationUp$ carrier as a group-facing source row. All comparisons remain
$\hsame$ and classifier transport through $\Pkg$ provenance, so no host
function equality or quotient is introduced.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SymGroupUp.SymGroupPermutationCarrier\_group\_surface}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SymGroupUp.SymGroupPermutationCarrier\_carrier\_obligation}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:symgroup-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[SymGroupUp classifier obligation]
\label{thm:symgroup-classifier-obligation}
The $\SymGroupUp$ classifier is the permutation classifier transported through
the group carrier of \autoref{thm:symgroup-carrier-obligation}. For carried
permutation rows $G,H,K$, it must satisfy reflexivity on $G$, symmetry from
$G\sim H$ to $H\sim G$, transitivity from $G\sim H$ and $H\sim K$ to
$G\sim K$, and carrier transport across the $\GroupUp$ source rows for
composition, identity, and inverse. These fields are exactly the
$\PermutationUp$ classifier rows of \autoref{thm:permutation-classifier-obligation}
read through the group-facing carrier boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the pointwise action classifier of
\autoref{thm:permutation-classifier-obligation}. It compares two certified
self-bijection rows by reading their action outputs through the fixed
$\FinSetUp$ classifier.

Reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are inherited from the finite-set
classifier rows named in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-permutation-namecert}.
Carrier transport is the same $\hsame$ and $\Pkg$ transport used to move graph
and endpoint rows in \autoref{thm:permutation-classifier-obligation}.

Repack those rows under the $\GroupUp$ boundary named in
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}. Since the classifier observes
only certified action outputs and package provenance, the construction never
identifies host functions and never forms a quotient of presentations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SymGroupUp composition, inverse, and action obligations]
\label{thm:symgroup-composition-inverse-action-obligations}
For the carrier and classifier of \autoref{thm:symgroup-carrier-obligation}
and \autoref{thm:symgroup-classifier-obligation}, the certificate rows required
for $\SymGroupUp$ are the following.
\begin{enumerate}
\item Composition closure: if $G$ and $H$ are carried permutations, then the
composite $H\circ G$ is a carried permutation.
\item Inverse closure: if $G$ is carried, then $G^{-1}$ is carried.
\item Unit laws: the certified identity permutation is carried and classifies
as the left and right unit for composition.
\item Associativity: the two certified composites $(K\circ H)\circ G$ and
$K\circ(H\circ G)$ classify together.
\item Action compatibility: the action row of a composite classifies with the
successive action rows, and the action row of the inverse is the inverse graph
readback.
\item Exactness: graph coverage, inverse-witness coverage, and classifier
transport are exactly the visible rows of the permutation source package.
\item Ledger boundary: enumeration choices, position names, order witnesses,
probe reads, and package provenance remain in the ledger and are not promoted
to public carrier data.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Composition closure, inverse closure, unit rows, and action compatibility are
the four group-facing readings of
\autoref{thm:permutation-composition-inverse-action-obligation}. The theorem
already states these rows as finite-position bijection facts whose graph
readings are carried by the $\PermutationUp$ ledger.

Associativity is read constructively from the same certified composition row:
both parenthesized composites have the same sequence of finite-position graph
reads, and the comparison is recorded as classifier transport over the
$\mathsf{BHist}$ graph rows. The proof uses the $\GroupUp$ source boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert} only to name the operation row,
not to add a host-level equality of functions.

Exactness and the ledger boundary are inherited from
\autoref{def:permutation-bijection-source-row}. Graph coverage, inverse
coverage, and action readback stay visible; enumeration choices, order choices,
position names, probe logs, and $\Pkg$ provenance stay in the ledger. Hence the
certificate surface is constructive and introduces no quotient of permutation
presentations.
\end{proof}
