\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\OnticStateUp$}

The $\OnticStateUp$ packet realizes the stable-signature source of \autoref{ch:visions-reality-constrained-ontic-state} as a finite NameCert surface. It sits beside the observer-local packet $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$ of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-consciousobserverstate-namecert}, the model-state packet $\StateSpaceModelUp$ of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-statespacemodel-namecert}, the support-and-budget packet $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$ of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-budget-support-namecert}, the observer-history identity packet $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$ of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}, and the quantum-state packet $\QuantumStateUp$ of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-quantumstate-namecert}. Its role is narrower than a total ontology and wider than a model row: it binds the reality-facing signature source, the admissible observer-access route, and the residue ledger in one accepted carrier before any consumer treats a model as ontically adequate.

\begin{definition}[OnticState carrier]
\label{def:onticstate-carrier}
An $\OnticStateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(S,A,K,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite reality-facing signature-source row, naming the stable signatures exposed by the accepted bundle family.
\item $A$ is the observer-access route row, recording which $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$ and $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$ surfaces are allowed to inspect the signatures.
\item $K$ is the classifier row comparing exposed signatures under the admissible observer bundle.
\item $R$ is the residue ledger, recording reality-facing content not captured by the displayed $\StateSpaceModelUp$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, or $\QuantumStateUp$ sibling rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the signature source, observer-access route, classifier, residue ledger, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through $S,A,K,R,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\OnticStateUp$, $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$, $\StateSpaceModelUp$, $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, $\QuantumStateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\OnticStateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier for $O$ reads only the displayed signature source, observer-access route, signature classifier, residue ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local name. It has no coordinate for a total physical object, hidden global observer, unregistered model state, quotient observer identity, or host-level quantum state.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[OnticState classifier]
\label{def:onticstate-classifier}
The $\OnticStateUp$ classifier identifies two accepted packets only when their displayed signature-source rows agree through $K$, their observer-access routes agree through the same admitted observer and support surfaces, and their residue ledgers preserve the same hidden or unqueried reality-facing rows under componentwise $\hsame$ transport. Agreement of model coordinates alone is not enough to classify two $\OnticStateUp$ packets as the same.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[OnticState signature stability]
\label{thm:onticstate-signature-stability}
Let $O=(S,A,K,R,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\OnticStateUp$ carrier. If the displayed signature source $S$ is transported along the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$ and replayed through the route $C$, then the resulting packet remains classified by the same $\OnticStateUp$ surface exactly when $A$, $K$, and $R$ are transported with it. In particular, signature stability is not a property of $S$ alone; it is a property of the source together with observer access, classifier, and residue ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:onticstate-carrier}. The signature source $S$ is admitted only as part of the packet $O=(S,A,K,R,H,C,P,N)$.

Transport through $H$ is componentwise. Thus a transported source row must be accompanied by the transported observer-access row $A$, classifier row $K$, residue ledger $R$, replay row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local name $N$.

The classifier of \autoref{def:onticstate-classifier} then reads the transported packet through all four content rows $S,A,K,R$. A consumer that keeps $S$ while dropping $A$, $K$, or $R$ has left the accepted $\OnticStateUp$ classifier surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OnticState residue-ledger exactness]
\label{thm:onticstate-residue-ledger-exactness}
Every accepted $\OnticStateUp$ public read factors through the finite route
$$
S,A,K,R,H,C,P,N.
$$
The route may expose the reality-facing signature source, admitted observer access, classifier comparison, and residue ledger. It cannot replace $R$ by a bare $\StateSpaceModelUp$ state, a $\ConsciousObserverStateUp$ state, an $\ObserverBudgetSupportUp$ support row, an $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$ identity row, or a $\QuantumStateUp$ state.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the displayed carrier $O=(S,A,K,R,H,C,P,N)$. Public reads reach the local name only after the signature source, access route, classifier, residue ledger, transport row, replay row, and provenance row have been displayed.

The residue ledger $R$ is a content row of the carrier. It records what is not captured by the displayed model, observer, support, history-identity, or quantum-state sibling rows.

Replacing $R$ by any one sibling would delete the ledger coordinate that distinguishes ontic source from model interface. Such a replacement has no row in $O$, so it is outside the accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OnticState NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:onticstate-namecert-obligation-surface}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:onticstate-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\OnticStateUp}$ surface consists exactly of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $O=(S,A,K,R,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier exposure through the signature-source row $S$, observer-access row $A$, and classifier row $K$;
\item signature stability through componentwise transport and displayed replay of $S,A,K$;
\item residue-ledger exactness through $R$ and its componentwise transport;
\item public obligation surface through $H,C,P,N$, with no hidden total-state, global-observer, quotient-identity, or host quantum-state coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
These five rows are the complete public obligation surface for using $\OnticStateUp$ as an ontic-state certificate.
\end{theorem}
