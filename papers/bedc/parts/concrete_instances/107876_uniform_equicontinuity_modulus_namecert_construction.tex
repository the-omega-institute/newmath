\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-equicontinuity-modulus-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Arzela--Ascoli and Bishop--Bridges equicontinuity arguments, the $\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp$ packet records the finite-net route by which a compact source and a finite family of continuous maps expose one shared modulus before any open-cover compactness or choice principle is read. It consumes compact-metric data from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, total-bounded finite nets from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, continuous-map graph and pointwise modulus rows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, compact uniform-continuity handoff data from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-heine-cantor-namecert}, and the downstream modulus surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}. The certificate is a finite uniform-equicontinuity route for compact-window consumers, not a selected subcover, not a family-choice functional, and not ambient completeness of the codomain.

\begin{definition}[UniformEquicontinuityModulus carrier]
\label{def:uniform-equicontinuity-modulus-carrier}
A $\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(K,T,F,W,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, exposing only the compact metric carrier, distance ledger, and compact finite-net surface used by the family.
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net row over the same source, listing inspected centers and dyadic source-radius budgets.
\item $F$ is the finite family of $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows whose pointwise modulus and graph rows are inspected at the centers named by $T$.
\item $W$ is the finite target-window row, recording the common tolerance window and the family indices admitted by the packet.
\item $M$ is the exported $\UniformModulusUp$ row, obtained by a finite fold over the centerwise and familywise modulus witnesses displayed by $F$ against $T$ and $W$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact source, finite-net, continuous-family, window, folded-modulus, replay, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a compact-window consumer through $K,T,F,W,M$ and then through the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\HeineCantorUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for arbitrary open covers, selected finite subcovers, countable choice over the family, quotient equality of maps, host real trichotomy, or ambient codomain completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UniformEquicontinuityModulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-equicontinuity-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp$ carrier $U=(K,T,F,W,M,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformEquicontinuityModulusUp}$ surface is exhausted by the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly the finite packet $K,T,F,W,M,H,C,P,N$;
\item finite-window classifier: every family and target-tolerance read is confined to the displayed window row $W$ and the listed $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows in $F$;
\item compact finite-net exactness: the only compactness data read by the classifier are the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row $K$ and the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net row $T$;
\item uniform-modulus export: the shared modulus row $M$ is obtained by the finite fold over the centerwise pointwise-modulus rows in $F$ and the compact finite-net route already exposed by $K,T$;
\item non-escape: no accepted consumer receives an open-cover compactness object, selected subcover, family-choice functional, quotient map equality, host real trichotomy, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier row by row. The analytic data available before the exported modulus are exactly the compact source $K$, the finite-net inventory $T$, the finite continuous-family rows $F$, and the common target window $W$.

The compact finite-net exactness clause follows from the route through $K$ and $T$. Since $T$ lists the centers and radius budgets as finite data, the classifier never needs an open-cover subcover or a choice over covers.

The uniform-modulus export is then the finite fold stored in $M$. Each fold input is a pointwise modulus row from $F$ at a center named by $T$ and a tolerance named by $W$; the Heine--Cantor handoff supplies the compact-window reading of those rows, while the final row is presented to the $\UniformModulusUp$ surface.

Finally read the structural rows. The transport row $H$, replay row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$ preserve the same finite route and contain no coordinate for the excluded cover, choice, equality, trichotomy, or completeness data.
\end{proof}
