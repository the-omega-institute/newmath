\begin{theorem}[DensityMatrix carrier obligation]
\label{thm:densitymatrix-carrier-obligation}
Let $H$ be a $\HilbertUp$ carrier supplied by $\NameCert_{\HilbertUp}$. The carrier row of $\DensityMatrixUp(H)$ is exactly the positive trace-class trace-one subcarrier
$$
\mathsf{Dens}_H(\rho)
:\Longleftrightarrow
\rho\in\mathsf{TC}(H)\land \mathsf{Pos}_H(\rho)\land \operatorname{Tr}_H(\rho)=1.
$$
The trace-class membership row $\rho\in\mathsf{TC}(H)$ is read from the $\OperatorIdealUp$ trace-class surface of \autoref{def:densitymatrix-trace-class-carrier}, while the Hilbert carrier, positivity predicate, and trace functional are read over the $\HilbertUp$ endpoint named in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hilbert-namecert}. No object is carried by $\DensityMatrixUp(H)$ unless all three displayed rows are present, and any endpoint with all three rows is a carried density matrix.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DensityMatrixUp.DensityMatrixTraceLedgerCarrier\_rows}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the density carrier named in \autoref{def:densitymatrix-trace-class-carrier}. Its carrier field is the intersection of the trace-class carrier with the positivity row and the trace-one equation, so the forward direction projects the three displayed witnesses.

For the reverse direction, repack the same three witnesses in the order required by the carrier field. The trace-class witness is supplied by the $\OperatorIdealUp$ dependency surface, while positivity and trace are rows over the underlying $\HilbertUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DensityMatrix restricted classifier obligation]
\label{thm:densitymatrix-restricted-classifier-obligation}
Let $H$ be a $\HilbertUp$ carrier, and write $\sim_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}$ for the trace-class classifier consumed from the $\OperatorIdealUp$ boundary. The classifier row of $\DensityMatrixUp(H)$ is the restricted classifier of \autoref{def:densitymatrix-restricted-classifier}:
$$
T\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}S
:\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{Dens}_H(T)\land\mathsf{Dens}_H(S)\land T\sim_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}S.
$$
Thus every density classifier comparison carries both density endpoints and the inherited trace-class comparison. Its reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and transport rows are inherited from the trace-class classifier together with transport of the carrier rows of \autoref{thm:densitymatrix-carrier-obligation}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack \autoref{def:densitymatrix-restricted-classifier}. The comparison is a restricted trace-class comparison, so projection gives the two density endpoints and the underlying trace-class classifier witness.

Reflexivity uses density-carrier reflexivity together with trace-class classifier reflexivity. Symmetry and transitivity keep the endpoint carrier witnesses and apply the corresponding inherited trace-class classifier rows.

For transport, use the carrier description of \autoref{thm:densitymatrix-carrier-obligation}. Trace-class membership is transported by the $\OperatorIdealUp$ classifier row, while positivity and trace-one evidence are transported over the Hilbert endpoint; repacking gives the transported density-carrier endpoint required by the restricted classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DensityMatrix positivity and trace ledger obligation]
\label{thm:densitymatrix-positivity-trace-ledger-obligation}
For a $\HilbertUp$ carrier $H$, the ledger hidden by the $\DensityMatrixUp(H)$ seal consists exactly of the trace-class membership witness $\rho\in\mathsf{TC}(H)$, positivity evidence $\mathsf{Pos}_H(\rho)$, trace-one evidence $\operatorname{Tr}_H(\rho)=1$, the restricted trace-class classifier comparison of \autoref{def:densitymatrix-restricted-classifier}, and the finite affine-mixture weight and bracketing data of \autoref{def:densitymatrix-affine-mixture-spine} when that operation is invoked. The seal contains no spectral decomposition, purification, channel action, dilation, or measurement-interface data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier ledger is fixed by \autoref{thm:densitymatrix-carrier-obligation}: one trace-class witness, one positivity witness, and one trace-one witness. The classifier ledger is fixed by \autoref{thm:densitymatrix-restricted-classifier-obligation}: two carried density endpoints together with the inherited trace-class comparison.

When an affine mixture is used, \autoref{def:densitymatrix-affine-mixture-spine} adds only the finite list of density entries, the finite real-weight list, nonnegative unit-sum evidence, and the local bracketing endpoint. These data are read over the trace-class vector surface supplied by $\OperatorIdealUp$ and the scalar rows supplied through the $\HilbertUp$ dependency.

The named dependency surfaces do not expose spectral decompositions, purifications, channel actions, dilations, or measurement rows at this paper site. Therefore none of those structures can be part of the hidden ledger of the $\DensityMatrixUp(H)$ seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[DensityMatrix affine mixture spine]
\label{def:densitymatrix-affine-mixture-spine}
Let $H$ be a $\HilbertUp$ carrier, and let $\mathsf{TC}(H)$, $\mathsf{Pos}_H$, $\operatorname{Tr}_H$, and $\mathsf{Dens}_H$ be as in \autoref{def:densitymatrix-trace-class-carrier}. Use the real trace-class scalar action $\odot_H$ and trace-class addition $+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}$ inherited from the $\OperatorIdealUp$ and $\HilbertUp$ dependency surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-densitymatrix-namecert}. For a finite trace-class spine $xs:\mathsf{ListCarrier}(\mathsf{TC}(H))$, a finite real-weight spine $\lambda:\mathsf{ListCarrier}(\RealUp)$, and an endpoint $\rho_{\mathrm{mix}}\in\mathsf{TC}(H)$, define
$$
\mathsf{DensAffSpine}_H(xs,\lambda,\rho_{\mathrm{mix}})
:\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{ConvAffSpine}_{\mathsf{Dens}_H}(xs,\lambda,\rho_{\mathrm{mix}})
$$
where the right side is the finite affine-spine datum of \autoref{def:convexset-finite-affine-spine-data} read with carrier $C=\mathsf{Dens}_H$, scalar action $\odot_H$, and vector addition $+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}$. Thus the datum records a position match between density-matrix entries and real weights, nonnegative weights whose finite sum is classified as $1_{\RealUp}$, and a recursive bracketing endpoint for the displayed affine mixture.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[DensityMatrix binary convex closure]
\label{lem:densitymatrix-binary-convex-closure}
Let $H$ be a $\HilbertUp$ carrier. Let $\rho,\sigma\in\mathsf{TC}(H)$ and let $a,b$ be real scalar coefficients. If
$$
\mathsf{Dens}_H(\rho)\land \mathsf{Dens}_H(\sigma)
\land \mathsf{NonNeg}_{\RealUp}(a)\land \mathsf{NonNeg}_{\RealUp}(b)
\land a+b=1,
$$
then the binary affine mixture is a carried density matrix:
$$
\mathsf{Dens}_H\bigl((a\odot_H\rho)+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}(b\odot_H\sigma)\bigr).
$$
\end{lemma}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DensityMatrixUp.DensityMatrixAffineMixtureSpine\_binary\_convex\_closure}
\begin{proof}
Unpack $\mathsf{Dens}_H$ by \autoref{def:densitymatrix-trace-class-carrier}. The two input hypotheses give
$$
\rho,\sigma\in\mathsf{TC}(H),
\qquad
\mathsf{Pos}_H(\rho),\mathsf{Pos}_H(\sigma),
\qquad
\operatorname{Tr}_H(\rho)=\operatorname{Tr}_H(\sigma)=1.
$$
The trace-class carrier is closed under real scalar action and addition by the vector-space surface inherited from the $\OperatorIdealUp$ and $\HilbertUp$ dependencies, so
$$
(a\odot_H\rho)+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}(b\odot_H\sigma)\in\mathsf{TC}(H).
$$
The positivity cone on trace-class operators is closed under multiplication by nonnegative real scalars and under addition. The two nonnegativity hypotheses therefore give
$$
\mathsf{Pos}_H\bigl((a\odot_H\rho)+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}(b\odot_H\sigma)\bigr).
$$
Trace linearity over the same trace-class vector carrier gives
$$
\operatorname{Tr}_H\bigl((a\odot_H\rho)+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}(b\odot_H\sigma)\bigr)
=
a\operatorname{Tr}_H(\rho)+b\operatorname{Tr}_H(\sigma)
=
a+b
=
1.
$$
These are exactly the trace-class, positivity, and trace-one clauses of \autoref{def:densitymatrix-trace-class-carrier} for the displayed affine mixture.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DensityMatrix finite affine mixture closure]
\label{thm:densitymatrix-finite-affine-mixture-closure}
Let $H$ be a $\HilbertUp$ carrier. For every finite trace-class spine $xs:\mathsf{ListCarrier}(\mathsf{TC}(H))$, finite real-weight spine $\lambda:\mathsf{ListCarrier}(\RealUp)$, and endpoint $\rho_{\mathrm{mix}}\in\mathsf{TC}(H)$, if
$$
\mathsf{DensAffSpine}_H(xs,\lambda,\rho_{\mathrm{mix}}),
$$
then
$$
\mathsf{Dens}_H(\rho_{\mathrm{mix}}).
$$
Equivalently, any finite affine mixture of carried $\DensityMatrixUp(H)$ entries with nonnegative real weights whose finite sum is classified as one is again a carried $\DensityMatrixUp(H)$ entry.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DensityMatrixUp.DensityMatrixAffineMixtureSpine\_finite\_closure}
\begin{proof}
Unpack \autoref{def:densitymatrix-affine-mixture-spine}. The hypothesis is the finite affine-spine datum of \autoref{def:convexset-finite-affine-spine-data} specialized to the concrete carrier $C=\mathsf{Dens}_H$ on the trace-class vector carrier $\mathsf{TC}(H)$. It contains both the finite readback of carried entries and nonnegative unit-sum weights, and a local bracketing witness for the endpoint $\rho_{\mathrm{mix}}$.

Induct on that local bracketing witness. In the singleton clause, the endpoint is the displayed trace-class operator $\rho$, and the clause itself supplies $\mathsf{Dens}_H(\rho)$.

In the binary clause, the induction hypothesis gives that the tail endpoint $\tau$ is carried by $\mathsf{Dens}_H$. The same clause supplies a carried head $\rho$, nonnegative real coefficients $a,b$, and the local unit-sum identity $a+b=1$. Applying \autoref{lem:densitymatrix-binary-convex-closure} to $\rho$, $\tau$, $a$, and $b$ gives
$$
\mathsf{Dens}_H\bigl((a\odot_H\rho)+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}(b\odot_H\tau)\bigr),
$$
which is exactly the endpoint named by the binary bracketing clause.

The induction covers every recursive affine bracketing packaged by $\mathsf{DensAffSpine}_H(xs,\lambda,\rho_{\mathrm{mix}})$. Hence the finite mixture endpoint $\rho_{\mathrm{mix}}$ satisfies $\mathsf{Dens}_H(\rho_{\mathrm{mix}})$.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[DensityMatrix restricted classifier]
\label{def:densitymatrix-restricted-classifier}
Let $H$ be a $\HilbertUp$ carrier. Write $\sim_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}$ for the trace-class classifier inherited from the $\OperatorIdealUp$ boundary of \autoref{thm:operatorideal-densitymatrix-trace-class-boundary}. The density-matrix classifier is the restricted trace-class classifier
$$
T\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}S
:\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{Dens}_H(T)\land\mathsf{Dens}_H(S)\land
T\sim_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}S.
$$
Thus a density classifier comparison records both density-carrier endpoints and the underlying trace-class classifier comparison.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[DensityMatrix binary constant affine exactness]
\label{lem:densitymatrix-binary-constant-affine-exactness}
Let $H$ be a $\HilbertUp$ carrier, and use the notation of \autoref{def:densitymatrix-restricted-classifier}. Let $\rho,\sigma,\rho_0\in\mathsf{TC}(H)$ and let $a,b$ be real scalar coefficients. Put
$$
E:=(a\odot_H\rho)+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}(b\odot_H\sigma).
$$
If
$$
\mathsf{Dens}_H(E)
\land
\rho\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}\rho_0
\land
\sigma\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}\rho_0
\land
(a+b)\sim_{\RealUp}1_{\RealUp},
$$
then
$$
E\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}\rho_0.
$$
\end{lemma}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DensityMatrixUp.DensityMatrixAffineMixtureSpine\_binary\_constant\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Unpack \autoref{def:densitymatrix-restricted-classifier} for the two input density-classifier comparisons. They give the density carrier of $\rho_0$ and trace-class classifier comparisons
$$
\rho\sim_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}\rho_0,
\qquad
\sigma\sim_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}\rho_0.
$$
By \autoref{thm:operatorideal-densitymatrix-trace-class-boundary}, the trace-class carrier inherits the real scalar action, vector addition, and trace-class classifier from the operator-ideal and Hilbert module surface. Reading that surface through \autoref{def:vecspace-stability-certificate} and \autoref{def:module-stability-certificate}, scalar-action congruence and additive congruence give
$$
E\sim_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}
(a\odot_H\rho_0)+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}(b\odot_H\rho_0).
$$
The scalar-distributivity row over scalar addition gives the reverse comparison from the displayed sum to the single scalar action:
$$
(a\odot_H\rho_0)+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}(b\odot_H\rho_0)
\sim_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}
((a+b)\odot_H\rho_0).
$$
Scalar-action congruence applied to $(a+b)\sim_{\RealUp}1_{\RealUp}$ and classifier reflexivity of the carried endpoint $\rho_0$ gives
$$
((a+b)\odot_H\rho_0)
\sim_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}
(1_{\RealUp}\odot_H\rho_0).
$$
The scalar unit law in the same module surface gives
$$
(1_{\RealUp}\odot_H\rho_0)\sim_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}\rho_0.
$$
Transitivity of the trace-class classifier composes the four displayed comparisons into
$$
E\sim_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}\rho_0.
$$
Pair this comparison with the assumed carrier $\mathsf{Dens}_H(E)$ and the carrier of $\rho_0$ obtained from either input density-classifier comparison. Folding \autoref{def:densitymatrix-restricted-classifier} gives $E\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}\rho_0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DensityMatrix constant affine-spine exactness]
\label{thm:densitymatrix-constant-affine-spine-exactness}
Let $H$ be a $\HilbertUp$ carrier, and use the notation of \autoref{def:densitymatrix-restricted-classifier}. Let $xs:\mathsf{ListCarrier}(\mathsf{TC}(H))$ be a finite trace-class spine, let $\lambda:\mathsf{ListCarrier}(\RealUp)$ be a finite real-weight spine, and let $\rho_{\mathrm{mix}},\rho_0\in\mathsf{TC}(H)$. If
$$
\mathsf{Dens}_H(\rho_0)
\land
\mathsf{DensAffSpine}_H(xs,\lambda,\rho_{\mathrm{mix}})
\land
\Bigl(\forall i:\mathsf{Pos}(xs).\;
\mathsf{entry}_{xs}(i)\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}\rho_0\Bigr),
$$
then
$$
\rho_{\mathrm{mix}}\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}\rho_0.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack $\mathsf{DensAffSpine}_H(xs,\lambda,\rho_{\mathrm{mix}})$ by \autoref{def:densitymatrix-affine-mixture-spine}. This is the finite affine-spine datum of \autoref{def:convexset-finite-affine-spine-data} specialized to the concrete density carrier $\mathsf{Dens}_H$, the trace-class scalar action $\odot_H$, and trace-class addition $+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}$. It contains a local bracketing witness $\mathsf{Bracket}_{\mathsf{Dens}_H}(xs,\lambda,\rho_{\mathrm{mix}})$.

Prove the stronger statement for every displayed sub-bracketing: if every leaf displayed in that sub-spine is density-classifier equal to $\rho_0$, then the sub-bracketing endpoint is density-classifier equal to $\rho_0$. Induct on the local bracketing witness.

In the singleton clause, the endpoint is the displayed leaf $\rho$. The finite-position hypothesis applied to the unique displayed position gives
$$
\rho\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}\rho_0,
$$
which is exactly the endpoint claim.

In the binary-extension clause, write the endpoint as
$$
E=(a\odot_H\rho)+_{\mathsf{TC}(H)}(b\odot_H\tau).
$$
The head position of the displayed cons spine gives
$$
\rho\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}\rho_0.
$$
The tail positions inherit the same leafwise hypothesis, so the induction hypothesis gives
$$
\tau\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}\rho_0.
$$
The binary bracketing clause supplies $\mathsf{Dens}_H(\rho)$, the nonnegative coefficient rows, the local unit-sum comparison $(a+b)\sim_{\RealUp}1_{\RealUp}$, and the tail bracketing witness. Since the induction hypothesis includes $\mathsf{Dens}_H(\tau)$ through \autoref{def:densitymatrix-restricted-classifier}, the specialized binary affine-combination row of \autoref{def:convexset-binary-affine-combination-row}, concretely proved for density matrices in \autoref{lem:densitymatrix-binary-convex-closure}, gives
$$
\mathsf{Dens}_H(E).
$$
Now apply \autoref{lem:densitymatrix-binary-constant-affine-exactness} to $\rho$, $\tau$, $\rho_0$, $a$, and $b$. The result is
$$
E\sim_{\mathsf{Dens}(H)}\rho_0,
$$
which is the endpoint claim for the binary node.

The induction applies to the full bracketing witness packaged by the original affine-spine datum. Therefore the endpoint $\rho_{\mathrm{mix}}$ is density-classifier equal to the fixed carried density matrix $\rho_0$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DensityMatrixUp.DensityMatrixAffineMixtureSpine\_restricted\_constant\_exactness}
