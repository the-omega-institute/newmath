\begin{theorem}[Substitution generator-audit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:substitution-generator-audit-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:substitution-generator-audit-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\SubstitutionGeneratorAuditUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$

$$
with the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $G=(T,C,R,S,K,W,H,Q,P,N)$;
\item closed-boundary exposure through $T,C$;
\item closed shift exposure through $R$;
\item closed substitution exposure through $S$;
\item closed-composition exposure through $K$;
\item generator-route replay and obstruction-ledger exposure through $W$;
\item componentwise transport through $H$;
\item displayed continuation through $Q$;
\item package provenance through $P$;
\item local naming through $N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:substitution-generator-audit-carrier}. The rows $T,C,R,S,K,W,H,Q,P,N$ are the complete finite packet.

Read $T,C$ first. They expose the MetaCIC term and the closed boundary at which the generator actions are admitted, matching the closed slice recorded in \autoref{ch:metacic-substitution-audit-map}.

Read $R,S,K,W$ next. The shift, substitution, and composition rows record the checked closed generator fragments, while $W$ fixes the replay route and obstruction ledger through which a consumer may inspect them before any subject-reduction handoff boundary is named.

Finally inspect $H,Q,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, package, and name the same finite packet. Since no additional coordinate is present, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
