\begin{theorem}[Principal empty filter carrier semantic certificate]
\label{thm:filter-principal-empty-carrier-semantic-namecert}
The BEDC unary carrier restricted to the empty history,
$$
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(h)\wedge h\hsame \emp,
$$
has a semantic naming certificate with source, pattern, and ledger predicates
all equal to this principal empty carrier, and with classifier $\hsame$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The witness is the empty history, which is unary and classifies to itself.
Reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are the finite-kernel laws for
$\hsame$. If $h$ lies in the principal empty carrier and $h\hsame k$, unary
transport moves the unary carrier from $h$ to $k$, while symmetry followed by
transitivity sends the displayed comparison $h\hsame\emp$ to
$k\hsame\emp$.
\end{proof}
