Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\CauchyMatrixUp$ packet records a two-index Cauchy matrix certificate for the L10 Real and Completion route. It sits after the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream-window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names row and column regularity, diagonal extraction, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming as finite $\BHist$ data; it does not assert quotient equality of completed real names, select a limiting row, use countable choice, or invoke ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy matrix carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-matrix-carrier}
A $\CauchyMatrixUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(R,C,W,D,Q,E,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the row-regularity ledger, recording the finite row windows whose tails are read as regular rational Cauchy data.
\item $C$ is the column-regularity ledger, recording the finite column windows whose tails satisfy the same tolerance discipline.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ two-index window schedule, listing every inspected pair $(i,j)$ together with the row, column, and diagonal slices visible to the consumer.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used for every row, column, and diagonal comparison.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the diagonal schedule of $W$ and the tolerance row $D$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $R,C,W,D,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for row regularity, column regularity, windows, dyadic tolerances, diagonal readback, Real sealing, replay, provenance, and naming.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion request through $R,C,W,D,Q,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyMatrixUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyMatrixUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $R,C,W,D,Q,E,H,K,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a quotient real, a chosen limiting row or column, countable choice over rows, detached completion data, host equality of streams, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy matrix NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-matrix-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyMatrixUp$ carrier
$$
M=(R,C,W,D,Q,E,H,K,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyMatrixUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, row regularity, column regularity, finite two-index window scheduling, dyadic tolerance accounting, diagonal regular rational readback, terminal Real sealing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every L10 Real or Completion consumer must read $R,C,W,D,Q,E$ before consuming $H,K,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-matrix-carrier}. Its analytic data are exactly the row ledger $R$, the column ledger $C$, the two-index window schedule $W$, the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, the diagonal readback row $Q$, and the Real seal $E$.

The structural rows do not add new analytic content. Rows $H$ and $K$ transport and replay the displayed route, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient equality, selected limiting rows, countable choice, detached completion data, host stream equality, or ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy matrix row and column regularity]
\label{thm:cauchy-matrix-row-column-regularity}
For every accepted $\CauchyMatrixUp$ carrier $M=(R,C,W,D,Q,E,H,K,P,N)$, a completion-facing read of the matrix can inspect row tails and column tails only through the finite schedule $W$ and the dyadic tolerance row $D$. Thus row regularity and column regularity are synchronized as displayed packet data before any diagonal readback or Real seal is visible.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:cauchy-matrix-namecert-obligations}, every consumer reads the row ledger, column ledger, window schedule, and dyadic tolerance row before the diagonal and seal rows.

The row route is therefore $R,W,D$, and the column route is $C,W,D$. Both routes share the same finite two-index schedule and the same tolerance ledger.

The diagonal row $Q$ and terminal seal $E$ occur only after those routes. Hence neither row regularity nor column regularity can be consumed as an external host property; both are part of the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy matrix diagonal Real handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-matrix-diagonal-real-handoff}
For every accepted $\CauchyMatrixUp$ carrier $M=(R,C,W,D,Q,E,H,K,P,N)$, the diagonal handoff to $\RealUp$ factors through the finite route
$$
R,C,W,D \longmapsto Q \longmapsto E .
$$
Consequently the packet exports a regular rational diagonal name and then a terminal Real seal, without exporting quotient stream equality, a selected limit, countable choice over rows, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:cauchy-matrix-row-column-regularity}. The row and column ledgers are synchronized through the displayed two-index windows and dyadic tolerance row before the diagonal readback is visible.

The diagonal readback row $Q$ is a $\RegSeqRatUp$ row over that same window-and-tolerance surface. It is not an independent sequence chosen outside the matrix packet.

Finally the Real seal $E$ is downstream of $Q$. Rows $H,K,P,N$ preserve and name this route, so a consumer receives only the displayed diagonal handoff $R,C,W,D \mapsto Q \mapsto E$ and no excluded host completion principle.
\end{proof}
