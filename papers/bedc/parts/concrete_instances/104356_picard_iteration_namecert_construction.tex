\label{def:picard-iteration-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the complete metric carrier and distance-read row available to the contraction route.
\item $T$ is the contraction self-map graph row, including the displayed contraction-bound row used by adjacent iterate comparisons.
\item $x_0$ is the initial-point row from which the orbit is generated.
\item $I$ is the finite iterate ledger, recording the visible rows $x_0,x_1,\ldots,x_n$ obtained by repeated reads of $T$.
\item $r$ is the residual-distance row, storing the adjacent distances and contraction-ratio comparisons read from the iterate ledger.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ row extracted from $I$ and $r$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row used when the orbit is consumed as a regular rational approximation stream.

The carrier exposes a finite contraction-iteration route. It contains no coordinate for a Banach fixed point, a selected limit, quotient equality of completed reals, host function extensionality, or an ambient completeness theorem beyond the displayed complete-metric row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PicardIteration NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:picard-iteration-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\PicardIterationUp$ carrier $P=(X,T,x_0,I,r,M,R,E,H,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PicardIterationUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission, complete-metric visibility, contraction-map visibility, initial-point visibility, finite iterate-ledger visibility, residual-distance stability, Cauchy-modulus exposure, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff visibility, $\RealUp$ seal separation, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for the finite packet of \autoref{def:picard-iteration-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission projects the displayed tuple. The rows $X,T,x_0,I$ expose the complete metric, contraction graph, initial point, and finite orbit before any convergence-facing row is consumed.

The residual-distance row $r$ is then read from the same finite iterate ledger. Its adjacent-distance and contraction-ratio comparisons determine the modulus row $M$; no infinite stream, chosen endpoint, or host limit is consulted.

The handoff rows $R$ and $E$ remain downstream boundaries. They mark where a regular rational sequence consumer and a real-seal consumer may read the orbit after the finite modulus route has been displayed, but they do not add quotient equality or a selected real value.

Finally $H,C,Q,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly these rows. The certificate therefore exports the contraction-iteration surface and not a Banach fixed-point theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PicardIteration contraction route]
\label{thm:picard-iteration-contraction-route}
Every accepted $\PicardIterationUp$ carrier factors its iterate and residual reads through
$$
\ContractionMappingUp \longmapsto \PicardContractionUp \longmapsto \CompleteMetricUp \longmapsto \CauchyModulusUp .
$$
The self-map row $T$ and initial row $x_0$ generate the finite ledger $I$, the adjacent-distance comparisons in $r$ are read through the contraction route, and the modulus row $M$ is visible only after those finite rows have been displayed. No iterate read bypasses the contraction-map graph, the Picard-step ledger, or the complete-metric distance row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the contraction-map surface. The graph row $T$ is the only source for the displayed successor rows in $I$, and the complete-metric row $X$ is the only source for the distance comparisons used by $r$.

The Picard-step route is finite. Each successor row in $I$ is obtained by replaying the same accepted graph row, so the residual-distance comparisons remain attached to the same contraction certificate rather than to an ambient function space.

The modulus row $M$ is downstream of those reads. It is therefore a Cauchy-modulus handoff for a displayed contraction ledger, not an endpoint selector, quotient-equality claim, or host extensionality principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PicardIteration completion handoff]
\label{thm:picard-iteration-completion-handoff}
Every completion-facing read of an accepted $\PicardIterationUp$ carrier passes through the displayed rows
$$

\begin{proof}
First expose the finite orbit and residual rows. The rows $I$ and $r$ are generated inside the displayed metric and contraction route, so a completion-facing consumer must first read $X,T,x_0,I,r$.

Next expose the Cauchy-modulus and regular-sequence handoff. The row $M$ records the finite modulus extracted from the residual ledger, and $R$ is the only regular rational sequence boundary available to downstream consumers.
