\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyCategoryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-category-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges' regular-Cauchy real construction and Mac Lane's categorical language, the $\RegularCauchyCategoryUp$ packet records the finite route by which displayed regular-Cauchy objects and tail-compatible morphisms are admitted, identified, composed, and sealed before any completion or universal-property consumer reads them. It sits over the finite observation surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the shift route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-shift-namecert}, the equivalence surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequenceequivalence-namecert}, and the fast-equivalence comparison of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fast-regular-cauchy-equivalence-namecert}. Its purpose is to expose the categorical object, identity, and composition ledgers as finite BEDC data rather than as host functions, quotient streams, or ambient completeness arguments.

\begin{definition}[Regular-Cauchy category carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-category-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyCategoryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(O,M,I,Q,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the object ledger. Each object row exposes a regular-Cauchy name only through finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ reads, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance data, and a terminal $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $M$ is the morphism ledger. Each morphism row displays its source object, target object, finite window schedule, tolerance-preserving read route, and tail-compatibility evidence.
\item $I$ is the identity ledger. For every displayed object row in $O$, it stores the identity morphism row whose source and target classifiers are that object row and whose reads replay the same finite windows and dyadic tolerances.
\item $Q$ is the composition ledger. For every composable pair of displayed morphism rows, it stores the composite source row, middle row, target row, composed window schedule, composed tolerance ledger, and replay route.
\item $S$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger consumed by object, morphism, identity, and composition rows.
\item $R$ is the shared $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback ledger, recording each finite rational read used by object rows and by morphism action rows.
\item $D$ is the shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, including the object tolerances, morphism tolerances, identity tolerances, and composition tolerances.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal ledger exposed only after the corresponding object or morphism route through $S,R,D$ has been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for object rows, morphism rows, identity rows, composition rows, window schedules, rational reads, dyadic ledgers, real seals, replay rows, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for finite categorical reads through
$$
O,M,I,Q,S,R,D,E,H \longmapsto P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyCategoryUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCategoryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $O,M,I,Q,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a quotient stream, host function equality, selected limit, countable choice principle, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, global category of sets, or external categorical quotient.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular-Cauchy category NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-category-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyCategoryUp$ carrier
$$
K=(O,M,I,Q,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyCategoryUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the object ledger, morphism ledger, identity ledger, composition ledger, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat reads, DyadicRatCore tolerances, Real seals, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item object classifier stability, requiring accepted object rows to be compared only by their displayed windows, rational reads, dyadic tolerances, terminal seals, and local transport;
\item morphism classifier stability, requiring accepted morphism rows to preserve the displayed source row, target row, finite window schedule, tolerance ledger, and replay route;
\item identity route, requiring every identity row in $I$ to replay the object row it names without adding a quotient stream, selected limit, host equality proof, or ambient completeness argument;
\item composition route, requiring every row in $Q$ to display its source morphism, middle object, target morphism, composed window schedule, dyadic tolerance ledger, and $\Cont$ replay witness;
\item RegSeqRat--DyadicRatCore--Real handoff, requiring every categorical read that reaches a $\RealUp$ seal to pass through the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row and the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the rows listed in the carrier.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows, and none exports quotient stream equality, host function equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, a global category of sets, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-category-carrier}. The packet exposes object, morphism, identity, composition, window, readback, tolerance, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows as finite coordinates of one $\BHist$ carrier.

Object classifier stability is read from $O,S,R,D,E,H$. Since an object row can be inspected only through those coordinates, the classifier has no access to an external stream quotient or a selected limit.

Morphism classifier stability is read from $M$ together with the shared analytic ledgers $S,R,D,E$. A morphism row must display its source and target object rows and its finite tolerance-preserving read route before it can be accepted.

The identity route is the projection to $I$. Each identity row is attached to a displayed object row and replays that row's windows, rational reads, dyadic tolerances, and seal, so it cannot introduce a host equality proof or a separate completeness theorem.

The composition route is the projection to $Q$. A composable pair is accepted only when the two morphism rows, middle object row, composed finite windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, and continuation replay have all been displayed.

The handoff row is $S,R,D,E$. Any read that reaches a $\RealUp$ seal must first pass through the StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, and DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger stored in the same packet.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ are structural rows. They transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed carrier coordinates, leaving no row from which a consumer could extract quotient stream equality, host function equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular-Cauchy category composition route]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-category-composition-route}
Let
$$
K=(O,M,I,Q,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\RegularCauchyCategoryUp$ carrier. Every composite morphism read accepted by $K$ factors through the displayed source row, middle row, target row, finite window schedule, dyadic tolerance ledger, and $\Cont$ replay route recorded in $Q,S,R,D,E,H,C$. Consequently the composite exports no quotient stream, host function equality, selected limit, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted composite morphism read. By the composition obligation in \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-category-namecert-obligations}, the read cannot be accepted until the two input morphism rows, their shared middle object row, and the resulting source and target object rows have been displayed.

The finite window schedule is then read from $S$ through the composition ledger $Q$. The schedule records exactly the windows needed by the source morphism, the target morphism, and the composite read; it is not a function space or an infinite selector.

The rational and dyadic data are read next. The row $R$ supplies the regular rational observations, and $D$ supplies the tolerance ledger used by both component morphisms and by the composed route.

The seal-facing step passes through $E$. A terminal $\RealUp$ seal is visible only after the source, middle, target, window, readback, and tolerance rows have been displayed, so the seal cannot detach from the finite categorical route.

Finally the continuation row $C$, transported by $H$ and sourced by $P,N$, replays the displayed composite. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for quotient streams, host function equality, selected limits, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness, none of those principles can be exported by the composite read.
\end{proof}
