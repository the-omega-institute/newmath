\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AtiyahSingerUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-atiyahsinger-namecert}

The Atiyah-Singer-index interface $\AtiyahSingerUp$ packages the equality between the analytic index of an elliptic operator on a closed manifold and a topological index defined via Chern characters and Todd classes. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spectraltheorem-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\ChernWeilUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-chernweil-namecert}), and bridges spectral and topological certificates through the same index pairing.

\begin{definition}[Atiyah-Singer index-pairing carrier packet]
\label{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}
An $\AtiyahSingerUp$ index-pairing carrier packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$$
\mathcal{A}=(m,D,\sigma_D,\mathcal{S},\alpha,\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau,\eta,\rho)
$$
with the following rows. The row $m:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the closed-manifold endpoint. The row $D:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the elliptic-operator endpoint over $m$, and $\sigma_D:\mathsf{BHist}$ is its symbol row. The packet $\mathcal{S}$ is a $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$ source whose operator row is $D$ and whose spectral-data ledger is read over the same endpoint $m$. The row $\alpha:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the analytic-index endpoint projected from that spectral source. The packet $\mathcal{W}$ is a $\ChernWeilUp$ source packet over the same endpoint $m$. The row $\theta:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the Chern-character and Todd characteristic-class row projected from $\mathcal{W}$, and $\tau:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the topological-index endpoint obtained by the displayed characteristic-class pairing with $\sigma_D$ over $m$. The row $\eta:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the index-pairing equality obligation, and $\rho$ records the $\Pkg$ provenance of the spectral, Chern-Weil, symbol, endpoint, and pairing rows.

The packet is admitted only when the analytic side and the topological side are displayed over the identical closed-manifold endpoint row $m$. Its public classifier row is the index-pairing predicate
$$
\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau)
:\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)\land
\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau)\land
\hsame(\alpha,\tau).
$$
Here $\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)$ names the analytic-index projection from the $\SpectralTheoremUp$ dependency surface, while $\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau)$ names the Chern-Weil characteristic-class projection and its displayed pairing endpoint. Thus the packet carries an equality obligation between two named index endpoints, not a host-level Fredholm theorem or an ambient equality of characteristic forms.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Atiyah-Singer index-pairing carrier surface]
\label{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-surface}
Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an accepted packet of \autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. If its $\SpectralTheoremUp$ component supplies the analytic-index row $\alpha$ for the elliptic-operator row $D$ over the closed-manifold endpoint $m$, and its $\ChernWeilUp$ component supplies the characteristic-class row $\theta$ and topological-index endpoint $\tau$ for the symbol row $\sigma_D$ over the same endpoint $m$, then $\mathcal{A}$ exposes the public index-pairing classifier row $\eta$ exactly as
$$
\eta=\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau).
$$
The equality obligation in this row is scoped exactly to the two dependency reads
$$
\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau),
$$
together with the endpoint comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$. No further analytic spectrum row, coordinate connection form, ambient cohomology quotient, external symbol class, or completed Atiyah-Singer theorem is exported by this carrier surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted packet of \autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. Projection gives the closed-manifold endpoint row $m$, the elliptic-operator row $D$, the symbol row $\sigma_D$, the spectral packet $\mathcal{S}$, the analytic-index endpoint $\alpha$, the Chern-Weil packet $\mathcal{W}$, the characteristic-class row $\theta$, the topological-index endpoint $\tau$, the equality-obligation row $\eta$, and the provenance row $\rho$.

The spectral component is bounded by the $\SpectralTheoremUp$ surface. By \autoref{thm:spectraltheorem-obligation-carrier-classifier}, the carried operator row and its classifier are read through the certified operator side rather than through host equality. By \autoref{thm:spectraltheorem-obligation-spectrum-pvm-functional-calculus}, the spectrum, projection-valued-measure, and functional-calculus rows are the visible spectral-data rows. By \autoref{thm:spectraltheorem-obligation-stability-ledger-exactness} and \autoref{thm:spectraltheorem-public-certificate}, a downstream consumer of $\mathcal{S}$ may use only the displayed carrier, classifier, spectral-data, stability, ledger, exactness, and dependency rows. The Atiyah-Singer packet consumes this surface only through the analytic-index projection named by $\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)$.

The topological component is bounded by the $\ChernWeilUp$ surface. The source packet of \autoref{def:chernweil-curvature-derham-source} supplies the curvature-to-de~Rham rows, invariant-polynomial row, evaluated characteristic row, endpoint row, exact-boundary row, ledger, and provenance. The classifier of \autoref{def:chernweil-characteristic-class-classifier} compares characteristic classes only after the displayed de~Rham transport. The carrier and exactness theorems \autoref{thm:chernweil-invariant-polynomial-carrier-obligation}, \autoref{thm:chernweil-closed-form-derham-class-obligation}, and \autoref{thm:chernweil-root-namecert-obligation-surface} exclude coordinate connection forms, ambient polynomial algebras, and uncarried cohomology quotients from the public surface. The Atiyah-Singer packet consumes this surface only through the characteristic-class row $\theta$ and the topological-index endpoint $\tau$ named by $\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau)$.

The shared endpoint condition is part of the admission rule in \autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. Both dependency reads cite the identical row $m$ before the equality obligation is formed. Therefore there is no hidden transport from one manifold endpoint to another inside $\eta$; any such transport would have to appear as an additional displayed ledger before a packet of this definition could be admitted.

It remains to identify the public classifier row. The definition assigns $\eta$ to the predicate $\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau)$, whose three clauses are exactly the spectral analytic-index read, the Chern-Weil topological-index read, and the comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$. Coverage follows by projecting $\eta$ from the accepted packet. Soundness follows because any public index-pairing comparison must unpack this displayed $\eta$ and hence must supply those two dependency reads and that endpoint comparison. Exactness follows from the tuple inventory: after the listed projections and the provenance row $\rho$, the packet contains no field from which an additional analytic, topological, or host-theoretic equality could be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Atiyah-Singer index-pairing provenance exactness]
\label{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-provenance-exactness}
Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an accepted packet of
\autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. Every public read of
the analytic-index endpoint $\alpha$, the Chern-Weil topological endpoint
$\tau$, the symbol row $\sigma_D$, the shared closed-manifold endpoint $m$, and
the comparison row $\eta$ factors through the displayed provenance row $\rho$,
the dependency packets $\mathcal{S}$ and $\mathcal{W}$, and the endpoint
comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$.

Equivalently, the exact public inventory of the packet is the spectral
analytic-index read
$\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)$, the Chern-Weil
topological-index read
$\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau)$, the shared
symbol and closed-manifold rows, the displayed comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$,
and the $\Pkg$ provenance recorded by $\rho$. The packet exports no host
Fredholm index equality, coordinate characteristic form equality, ambient
cohomology quotient, or uncarried equality between analytic and topological
models.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted tuple. Its fields give exactly $m$, $D$, $\sigma_D$,
$\mathcal{S}$, $\alpha$, $\mathcal{W}$, $\theta$, $\tau$, $\eta$, and $\rho$,
with the shared endpoint condition imposed before the classifier row is read.

Apply \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-surface}. It identifies
the classifier row $\eta$ with
$\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau)$, so every public
comparison read must pass through the spectral analytic-index read, the
Chern-Weil topological-index read, and $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$.

The endpoint and symbol reads are then covered by the same tuple inventory.
The analytic endpoint $\alpha$ is read only through the dependency packet
$\mathcal{S}$, the topological endpoint $\tau$ only through $\mathcal{W}$, and
the shared rows $m$ and $\sigma_D$ are the displayed coordinates used by both
dependency clauses.

Finally, the provenance row $\rho$ records precisely the $\Pkg$ source of these
dependency, endpoint, symbol, and comparison rows. Since the packet has no
field beyond the projected inventory, no host Fredholm equality, coordinate
form equality, ambient cohomology quotient, or uncarried analytic-topological
identity can be exported by a public read of $\mathcal{A}$.
\end{proof}
