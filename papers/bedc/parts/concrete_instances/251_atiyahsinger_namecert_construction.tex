\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AtiyahSingerUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-atiyahsinger-namecert}

The Atiyah-Singer-index interface $\AtiyahSingerUp$ packages the equality between the analytic index of an elliptic operator on a closed manifold and a topological index defined via Chern characters and Todd classes. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-spectraltheorem-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\ChernWeilUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-chernweil-namecert}), and bridges spectral and topological certificates through the same index pairing.

\begin{definition}[Atiyah-Singer index-pairing carrier packet]
\label{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}
An $\AtiyahSingerUp$ index-pairing carrier packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$$
\mathcal{A}=(m,D,\sigma_D,\mathcal{S},\alpha,\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau,\eta,\rho)
$$
with the following rows. The row $m:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the closed-manifold endpoint. The row $D:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the elliptic-operator endpoint over $m$, and $\sigma_D:\mathsf{BHist}$ is its symbol row. The packet $\mathcal{S}$ is a $\NameCert_{\SpectralTheoremUp}$ source whose operator row is $D$ and whose spectral-data ledger is read over the same endpoint $m$. The row $\alpha:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the analytic-index endpoint projected from that spectral source. The packet $\mathcal{W}$ is a $\ChernWeilUp$ source packet over the same endpoint $m$. The row $\theta:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the Chern-character and Todd characteristic-class row projected from $\mathcal{W}$, and $\tau:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the topological-index endpoint obtained by the displayed characteristic-class pairing with $\sigma_D$ over $m$. The row $\eta:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the index-pairing equality obligation, and $\rho$ records the $\Pkg$ provenance of the spectral, Chern-Weil, symbol, endpoint, and pairing rows.

The packet is admitted only when the analytic side and the topological side are displayed over the identical closed-manifold endpoint row $m$. Its public classifier row is the index-pairing predicate
$$
\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau)
:\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)\land
\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau)\land
\hsame(\alpha,\tau).
$$
Here $\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)$ names the analytic-index projection from the $\SpectralTheoremUp$ dependency surface, while $\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau)$ names the Chern-Weil characteristic-class projection and its displayed pairing endpoint. Thus the packet carries an equality obligation between two named index endpoints, not a host-level Fredholm theorem or an ambient equality of characteristic forms.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Atiyah-Singer index-pairing carrier surface]
\label{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-surface}
Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an accepted packet of \autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. If its $\SpectralTheoremUp$ component supplies the analytic-index row $\alpha$ for the elliptic-operator row $D$ over the closed-manifold endpoint $m$, and its $\ChernWeilUp$ component supplies the characteristic-class row $\theta$ and topological-index endpoint $\tau$ for the symbol row $\sigma_D$ over the same endpoint $m$, then $\mathcal{A}$ exposes the public index-pairing classifier row $\eta$ exactly as
$$
\eta=\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau).
$$
The equality obligation in this row is scoped exactly to the two dependency reads
$$
\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau),
$$
together with the endpoint comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$. No further analytic spectrum row, coordinate connection form, ambient cohomology quotient, external symbol class, or completed Atiyah-Singer theorem is exported by this carrier surface.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AtiyahSingerUp.AtiyahSingerIndexPairingCarrierPacket\_carrier\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted packet of \autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. Projection gives the closed-manifold endpoint row $m$, the elliptic-operator row $D$, the symbol row $\sigma_D$, the spectral packet $\mathcal{S}$, the analytic-index endpoint $\alpha$, the Chern-Weil packet $\mathcal{W}$, the characteristic-class row $\theta$, the topological-index endpoint $\tau$, the equality-obligation row $\eta$, and the provenance row $\rho$.

The spectral component is bounded by the $\SpectralTheoremUp$ surface. By \autoref{thm:spectraltheorem-obligation-carrier-classifier}, the carried operator row and its classifier are read through the certified operator side rather than through host equality. By \autoref{thm:spectraltheorem-obligation-spectrum-pvm-functional-calculus}, the spectrum, projection-valued-measure, and functional-calculus rows are the visible spectral-data rows. By \autoref{thm:spectraltheorem-obligation-stability-ledger-exactness} and \autoref{thm:spectraltheorem-public-certificate}, a downstream consumer of $\mathcal{S}$ may use only the displayed carrier, classifier, spectral-data, stability, ledger, exactness, and dependency rows. The Atiyah-Singer packet consumes this surface only through the analytic-index projection named by $\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)$.

The topological component is bounded by the $\ChernWeilUp$ surface. The source packet of \autoref{def:chernweil-curvature-derham-source} supplies the curvature-to-de~Rham rows, invariant-polynomial row, evaluated characteristic row, endpoint row, exact-boundary row, ledger, and provenance. The classifier of \autoref{def:chernweil-characteristic-class-classifier} compares characteristic classes only after the displayed de~Rham transport. The carrier and exactness theorems \autoref{thm:chernweil-invariant-polynomial-carrier-obligation}, \autoref{thm:chernweil-closed-form-derham-class-obligation}, and \autoref{thm:chernweil-root-namecert-obligation-surface} exclude coordinate connection forms, ambient polynomial algebras, and uncarried cohomology quotients from the public surface. The Atiyah-Singer packet consumes this surface only through the characteristic-class row $\theta$ and the topological-index endpoint $\tau$ named by $\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau)$.

The shared endpoint condition is part of the admission rule in \autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. Both dependency reads cite the identical row $m$ before the equality obligation is formed. Therefore there is no hidden transport from one manifold endpoint to another inside $\eta$; any such transport would have to appear as an additional displayed ledger before a packet of this definition could be admitted.

It remains to identify the public classifier row. The definition assigns $\eta$ to the predicate $\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau)$, whose three clauses are exactly the spectral analytic-index read, the Chern-Weil topological-index read, and the comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$. Coverage follows by projecting $\eta$ from the accepted packet. Soundness follows because any public index-pairing comparison must unpack this displayed $\eta$ and hence must supply those two dependency reads and that endpoint comparison. Exactness follows from the tuple inventory: after the listed projections and the provenance row $\rho$, the packet contains no field from which an additional analytic, topological, or host-theoretic equality could be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Atiyah-Singer index-pairing provenance exactness]
\label{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-provenance-exactness}
Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an accepted packet of
\autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. Every public read of
the analytic-index endpoint $\alpha$, the Chern-Weil topological endpoint
$\tau$, the symbol row $\sigma_D$, the shared closed-manifold endpoint $m$, and
the comparison row $\eta$ factors through the displayed provenance row $\rho$,
the dependency packets $\mathcal{S}$ and $\mathcal{W}$, and the endpoint
comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$.

Equivalently, the exact public inventory of the packet is the spectral
analytic-index read
$\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)$, the Chern-Weil
topological-index read
$\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau)$, the shared
symbol and closed-manifold rows, the displayed comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$,
and the $\Pkg$ provenance recorded by $\rho$. The packet exports no host
Fredholm index equality, coordinate characteristic form equality, ambient
cohomology quotient, or uncarried equality between analytic and topological
models.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AtiyahSingerUp.AtiyahSingerIndexPairingCarrierPacket\_provenance\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted tuple. Its fields give exactly $m$, $D$, $\sigma_D$,
$\mathcal{S}$, $\alpha$, $\mathcal{W}$, $\theta$, $\tau$, $\eta$, and $\rho$,
with the shared endpoint condition imposed before the classifier row is read.

Apply \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-surface}. It identifies
the classifier row $\eta$ with
$\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau)$, so every public
comparison read must pass through the spectral analytic-index read, the
Chern-Weil topological-index read, and $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$.

The endpoint and symbol reads are then covered by the same tuple inventory.
The analytic endpoint $\alpha$ is read only through the dependency packet
$\mathcal{S}$, the topological endpoint $\tau$ only through $\mathcal{W}$, and
the shared rows $m$ and $\sigma_D$ are the displayed coordinates used by both
dependency clauses.

Finally, the provenance row $\rho$ records precisely the $\Pkg$ source of these
dependency, endpoint, symbol, and comparison rows. Since the packet has no
field beyond the projected inventory, no host Fredholm equality, coordinate
form equality, ambient cohomology quotient, or uncarried analytic-topological
identity can be exported by a public read of $\mathcal{A}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Atiyah-Singer shared index-source boundary]
\label{thm:atiyahsinger-shared-index-source-boundary}
Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an accepted packet of
\autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. The spectral
analytic-index read $\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)$
and the Chern-Weil topological-index read
$\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau)$ are
admitted only over the same closed-manifold endpoint row $m$. Consequently the
comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$ in the public classifier row never compares
index endpoints whose dependency reads were formed over distinct manifold
endpoints.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted packet of
\autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. Its admission clause
states that the analytic side and the topological side are displayed over the
identical closed-manifold endpoint row $m$ before the classifier row is formed.

The spectral read is bounded by the $\SpectralTheoremUp$ public surface cited
in \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-surface}: the operator row
$D$ and the analytic endpoint $\alpha$ are read through the displayed spectral
dependency packet $\mathcal{S}$. The Chern-Weil read is bounded by the
$\ChernWeilUp$ surface cited in the same theorem: the symbol row $\sigma_D$,
the characteristic row $\theta$, and the topological endpoint $\tau$ are read
through the displayed packet $\mathcal{W}$.

Apply \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-provenance-exactness}. Every
public read of $\alpha$, $\tau$, $\sigma_D$, $m$, and $\eta$ factors through
the two dependency packets, the endpoint comparison, and the provenance row
$\rho$. Since the only endpoint row available to both dependency reads is the
projected row $m$, a comparison formed from distinct manifold endpoints cannot
be read from an accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Atiyah-Singer root endpoint exactness frontier]
\label{thm:atiyahsinger-root-endpoint-exactness-frontier}
Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an accepted packet of \autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. Every public Atiyah-Singer endpoint read is one of the carried closed-manifold endpoint $m$, elliptic-operator row $D$, symbol row $\sigma_D$, spectral analytic-index row $\alpha$, Chern-Weil topological-index row $\tau$ together with its characteristic-class row $\theta$, endpoint comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$, or $\Pkg$ provenance row $\rho$ from that packet. Conversely, these rows repack into the public index-pairing classifier
$$
\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau).
$$
No host Fredholm equality, coordinate characteristic-form equality, or cohomology quotient equality is exported by this frontier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted tuple of \autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. Its visible endpoint inventory is exactly $m$, $D$, $\sigma_D$, $\mathcal{S}$, $\alpha$, $\mathcal{W}$, $\theta$, $\tau$, $\eta$, and $\rho$.

Apply \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-surface}. It identifies the public classifier row $\eta$ with the displayed predicate $\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau)$, whose clauses are the spectral analytic-index read, the Chern-Weil topological-index read, and the comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$.

Apply \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-provenance-exactness}. It restricts every public read of $\alpha$, $\tau$, $\sigma_D$, $m$, and $\eta$ to the dependency packets, the endpoint comparison, and the provenance row $\rho$.

Finally use \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-shared-index-source-boundary}. Both dependency reads are admitted over the same closed-manifold endpoint $m$, so the endpoint frontier has no second manifold row and no host-level equality between analytic and topological models. Repacking the listed rows yields the public classifier and excludes Fredholm equality, coordinate characteristic-form equality, and cohomology quotient equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Atiyah-Singer spectral Chern-Weil consumer exhaustion]
\label{thm:atiyahsinger-spectral-chernweil-consumer-exhaustion}
Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an accepted packet of
\autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. Any downstream
consumer of the public Atiyah-Singer bridge surface sees exactly the spectral
analytic-index read, the Chern-Weil topological-index read, the shared symbol
row $\sigma_D$, the shared endpoint row $m$, the comparison
$\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$, and the $\Pkg$ provenance row $\rho$. No additional
consumer-visible datum is supplied by the carrier packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet. The tuple inventory gives the endpoint row $m$,
the elliptic row $D$, the symbol row $\sigma_D$, the dependency packets
$\mathcal{S}$ and $\mathcal{W}$, the endpoints $\alpha$ and $\tau$, the
characteristic row $\theta$, the classifier row $\eta$, and the provenance row
$\rho$.

Apply \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-surface}. It identifies
$\eta$ with the public predicate whose clauses are precisely
$\mathsf{SpecAnalyticIndex}_{m,D}(\mathcal{S},\alpha)$,
$\mathsf{CWTopologicalIndex}_{m,\sigma_D}(\mathcal{W},\theta,\tau)$, and
$\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$.

Apply \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-provenance-exactness}. It
states that every public read of the analytic endpoint, topological endpoint,
symbol row, endpoint row, and comparison row factors through those dependency
reads and the $\Pkg$ provenance $\rho$.

The $\SpectralTheoremUp$ citations in
\autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-surface} bound the spectral
consumer to the carrier, classifier, spectral-data, stability, ledger,
exactness, and dependency rows exposed by $\mathcal{S}$. The $\ChernWeilUp$
citations there bound the topological consumer to the displayed
curvature-to-de~Rham, invariant-polynomial, characteristic-class, ledger, and
provenance rows exposed by $\mathcal{W}$. Thus the listed reads exhaust the
public bridge surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Atiyah-Singer index-pairing host exclusion]
\label{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-host-exclusion}
Let $\mathcal{A}$ be an accepted packet of
\autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}. The bridge surface
exported by $\mathcal{A}$ does not export a host Fredholm equality, coordinate
form equality, cohomology quotient, or ambient analytic-topological identity.
Its only equality-bearing public row is the displayed comparison
$\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$ inside the classifier
$\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier packet. The displayed classifier in
\autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet} contains the spectral
analytic-index read, the Chern-Weil topological-index read, and
$\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$; it does not contain a host Fredholm row, a coordinate
form row, a quotient row, or an ambient identity row.

Apply \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-surface} to identify the
public classifier row $\eta$ with that displayed predicate. Apply
\autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-provenance-exactness} to identify the
complete public inventory as the two dependency reads, the shared symbol and
endpoint rows, the endpoint comparison, and the provenance row $\rho$.

The $\SpectralTheoremUp$ surface used by the spectral dependency excludes host
operator equality from the public spectral read. The $\ChernWeilUp$ surface
used by the topological dependency excludes coordinate connection forms,
ambient polynomial algebras, and uncarried cohomology quotients from the
public characteristic-class read. Since $\mathcal{A}$ exports only those two
bounded reads and their displayed comparison, none of the excluded host-level
objects can be recovered from the bridge surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Atiyah-Singer NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:atiyahsinger-namecert-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\AtiyahSingerUp}$ obligation surface for the
Atiyah-Singer index-pairing certificate consists exactly of the carrier packet
of \autoref{def:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-packet}, the classifier row
$\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau)$, the shared endpoint
and source-compatibility rows, the spectral and Chern-Weil dependency reads, the
endpoint comparison $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$, and the $\Pkg$ provenance row. The
surface excludes host Fredholm equality, coordinate forms, cohomology quotients,
and uncarried analytic-topological identities.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carried packet. Apply
\autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-carrier-surface} to identify the public
classifier as
$\mathsf{ASIndexPair}_{m,D,\sigma_D}(\alpha,\theta,\tau)$ and to bound the
equality-bearing row by the displayed spectral read, Chern-Weil read, and
$\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$.

Next apply \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-provenance-exactness}. This
records that every public endpoint, symbol, comparison, and dependency read
factors through the two dependency packets and the $\Pkg$ provenance row.

The endpoint and source boundary is supplied by
\autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-shared-index-source-boundary}. The dependency reads
are then exhausted by
\autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-spectral-chernweil-consumer-exhaustion}, which leaves
only the spectral analytic-index read and the Chern-Weil topological-index read
as public consumers of the two source certificates.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:atiyahsinger-index-pairing-host-exclusion}. It
removes host Fredholm equality, coordinate forms, cohomology quotients, and
uncarried analytic-topological identities from the public surface. The remaining
rows are precisely the carrier packet, the classifier, the shared compatibility
rows, the two dependency reads, $\hsame(\alpha,\tau)$, and $\Pkg$ provenance.
\end{proof}
