\begin{definition}[Real seal congruence carrier]
\label{def:real-seal-congruence-carrier}

$$
G=(R_0,W_0,E_0,R_1,W_1,E_1,A,J,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R_0$ and $R_1$ are the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows whose Real-facing routes are being compared;
\item $W_0$ and $W_1$ are the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window ledgers used to inspect the two readbacks;
\item $E_0$ and $E_1$ are the two $\RealUp$ seal rows reached from those readbacks and windows;
\item $A$ is the shared displayed $\RealUp$ seal classifier row, read after $E_0$ and $E_1$ are both present;
\item $J$ is the seal-congruence row stating that a consumer may replace the route $R_0,W_0,E_0$ by the route $R_1,W_1,E_1$ only under the shared classifier row $A$;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the two regular readbacks, the two window ledgers, the two seals, the shared classifier, the congruence row, provenance, and local name;

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealSealCongruenceUp}$ row.

The carrier contains no quotient stream, no host real equality, no selected limit representative, no ambient completion object, and no direct route from either readback to a consumer outside the displayed seal classifier.

\begin{theorem}[Real seal congruence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-seal-congruence-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RealSealCongruenceUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:real-seal-congruence-carrier} consists of seven finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the packet $G=(R_0,W_0,E_0,R_1,W_1,E_1,A,J,H,C,P,N)$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback admission for both $R_0$ and $R_1$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert};
\item finite $\StreamNameUp$ window coverage for $W_0$ and $W_1$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert};
\item $\RealUp$ seal admission for $E_0$ and $E_1$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert};
\item shared seal-classifier exactness for the row $A$, with the separated comparison discipline supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-limit-uniqueness-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert};
\item congruence replacement through $J$, stating that a consumer may exchange the two displayed readback routes only after $A$ has been read;
\item ledger non-escape excluding quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, ambient completion, and direct readback-to-consumer shortcuts.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-seal-congruence-carrier}. The tuple exposes exactly the two regular-rational readbacks, two finite window ledgers, two Real seal rows, shared classifier row, congruence row, transport, route, provenance, and local naming data.

The two readback routes are the sibling routes named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Each route is read from a displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ row through a displayed finite $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger before it reaches its $\RealUp$ seal.

The shared classifier row $A$ is the only comparison point between the two routes. The refinement schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-modulus-refinement-namecert} supplies the finite-window discipline, while the separated comparison boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-limit-uniqueness-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert} keeps the comparison at the displayed seal rather than at a quotient real.

Finally, the congruence row $J$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ make replacement consumer-visible only after the shared classifier has been read. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a host equality, selected limit, quotient stream, or ambient completion, no consumer can bypass $A,J$ to distinguish the two accepted routes.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real seal congruence classifier exactness]
\label{thm:real-seal-congruence-classifier-exactness}
For every accepted $\RealSealCongruenceUp$ packet
$$
G=(R_0,W_0,E_0,R_1,W_1,E_1,A,J,H,C,P,N),
$$
every consumer-visible replacement of the first readback route by the second factors through the finite route
$$
R_0,W_0,E_0,A,J,E_1,W_1,R_1,H,C,P,N.
$$
Thus the classifier row $A$ is exact: if two accepted packets display the same $A$ and the same transported structural rows $H,C,P,N$, a consumer cannot observe whether the $\RealUp$ seal was reached through $R_0,W_0,E_0$ or through $R_1,W_1,E_1$. Conversely, without the displayed $A$ and $J$ rows, the carrier exports no replacement claim between the two readback routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier first. The only comparison row shared by the two sides is $A$, and the only replacement row is $J$; the routes into $A$ are the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, and $\RealUp$ seals.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:real-seal-congruence-namecert-obligations}. They force each side to be admitted through the same finite readback and seal discipline, then force replacement to pass through $J$ after $A$.

Consumer exactness is therefore coordinate exhaustion. The rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, record provenance, and name the same displayed packet. None of them adds quotient equality, a selected completed representative, host real equality, or a direct read from one route to the other.

For the converse boundary, remove $A$ or $J$ from the displayed route. The remaining coordinates contain two seal-reaching routes but no row that compares them or licenses replacement. Such a packet may show two Real-facing reads, but it is not a $\RealSealCongruenceUp$ replacement packet.
\end{proof}
