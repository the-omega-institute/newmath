\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BishopIntervalCompactUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-interval-compact-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\BishopIntervalCompactUp$ packet records the finite-net compact interval surface for displayed real-name intervals. It is anchored in the dyadic tolerance arithmetic of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular-rational readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the visible real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the comparison boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishoprealcomparison-namecert}, and the rational witness route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rational-density-real-namecert}. The chapter treats compactness as a finite-net packet over those rows, not as open-cover compactness or a choice-bearing completeness principle.

A $\BishopIntervalCompactUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(A,B,W,T,G,Q,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the lower and upper endpoint names, each read through $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows, finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, and a visible $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $W$ is the bounded interval window ledger naming the endpoint windows inspected at each dyadic tolerance.
\item $T$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance scale used to compare endpoint windows and interval mesh points.
\item $G$ is the finite grid row listing the rational mesh points visible at the chosen dyadic scale.
\item $Q$ is the rational-density witness ledger that routes each mesh cell through $\RationalDensityRealUp$ rather than through host real order.
\item $R$ is the Bishop comparison row recording the same endpoint, readback, and seal surface as a $\BishopRealComparisonUp$ packet.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for endpoints, windows, tolerance, grid, witness, comparison, continuation, package, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay from endpoint windows to dyadic scale, from dyadic scale to grid, and from grid cells to rational witnesses.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopIntervalCompactUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BishopRealComparisonUp$, $\RationalDensityRealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopIntervalCompactUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}

The packet gives a finite interval-net surface: every consumer read factors through the displayed endpoint windows, dyadic tolerance, finite grid, rational-density witnesses, comparison row, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming. It does not supply a cover selector, a subsequence selector, an ambient Heine--Borel theorem, quotient equality of real names, or a host-real equality test.
