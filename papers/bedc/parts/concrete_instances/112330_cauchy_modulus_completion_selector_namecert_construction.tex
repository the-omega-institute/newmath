\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyModulusCompletionSelectorUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-modulus-completion-selector-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges and Weihrauch on effective completion, the
$\CauchyModulusCompletionSelectorUp$ packet records the finite selector surface
that passes an explicit Cauchy modulus into a completion-facing readback. It
sits after the explicit rate rows of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, the finite observation
windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular
rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the
dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert},
and the effective completion boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-effective-cauchy-completion-namecert}. The
packet names a displayed selector for modulus evidence only; it does not choose
a Cauchy representative, quotient streams, assert ambient real completeness, or
export host equality of completed objects.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy-modulus completion selector carrier]\label{def:cauchy-modulus-completion-selector-carrier}
A $\CauchyModulusCompletionSelectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(M,W,Q,D,E,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ rate row, recording the requested precision
and the displayed threshold selected for the completion query.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row read at that threshold.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the same windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used by the selected window
and by the regular readback.
\item $E$ is the $\EffectiveCauchyCompletionUp$ completion-facing boundary row.
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached through $M,W,Q,D,E$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the displayed rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion request through
$M,W,Q,D,E,R,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over
$\CauchyModulusCompletionSelectorUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\EffectiveCauchyCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$,
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusCompletionSelectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these rows. It has no
coordinate for a hidden threshold choice, quotient stream equality, selected
limit, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient completion principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy-modulus completion selector obligations]\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-completion-selector-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusCompletionSelectorUp$ carrier
$$
S=(M,W,Q,D,E,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusCompletionSelectorUp}$ surface consists
exactly of carrier admission for the explicit modulus row, finite stream-window
row, regular rational readback row, dyadic tolerance row, effective completion
boundary row, terminal real seal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed
$\Cont$ replay, provenance, and local naming. Every completion-facing selector
read must expose $M,W,Q,D,E,R$ before $H,C,P,N$ can be consumed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-completion-selector-carrier}. Its coordinates are
precisely the modulus, window, readback, tolerance, completion boundary, real
seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The selector route is read through $M,W,Q,D,E,R$. The modulus row supplies the
threshold for the query, the window row identifies the inspected observations,
the regular readback row records the Cauchy data, the dyadic row records the
precision budget, the effective completion row exposes the completion-facing
boundary, and the real row seals that same route.

The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the route
already displayed. Since hidden threshold choice, quotient streams, selected
limits, countable choice, host equality, and ambient completeness have no
carrier coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy-modulus selector effective readback]\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-completion-selector-effective-readback}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusCompletionSelectorUp$ carrier
$S=(M,W,Q,D,E,R,H,C,P,N)$, any completion-facing query that consumes the
effective boundary row $E$ must first read the $\CauchyModulusUp$ row $M$, the
$\StreamNameUp$ window $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $Q$, and the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row $D$. Thus the selector exposes a finite,
modulus-indexed readback before the completion boundary is visible.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with
\autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-completion-selector-namecert-obligations}. It
requires the selector read to expose the modulus, window, readback, tolerance,
completion, and seal rows before the structural rows are consumed.

The effective boundary row $E$ is therefore not read from a completed object in
isolation. It is reached only after $M$ selects the threshold, $W$ displays the
finite observations, $Q$ records the regular rational readback, and $D$ records
the dyadic budget used by that readback.

Consequently the completion-facing route factors through the same finite
selector surface. The carrier has no coordinate for bypassing $M,W,Q,D$ by a
host-level limit, quotient stream, or selected representative.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy-modulus selector real-seal nonescape]\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-completion-selector-real-seal-nonescape}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusCompletionSelectorUp$ carrier
$S=(M,W,Q,D,E,R,H,C,P,N)$, the $\RealUp$ seal row $R$ is scoped to the displayed
selector route $M,W,Q,D,E$. No downstream Real or Completion consumer can use
$R$ as evidence for an unrelated Cauchy name, a quotient-completion equality,
a selected limit, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-completion-selector-effective-readback}. Before
the effective boundary is visible, the consumer has already read the explicit
modulus, finite window, regular readback, and dyadic tolerance rows.

The real seal row $R$ is listed in the carrier only after that same boundary.
Thus any read of $R$ is a read of the displayed selector route
$M,W,Q,D,E$, not of an independent completed-real object.

Finally use
\autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-completion-selector-namecert-obligations}. The
transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows preserve the same route and add
no second seal surface. The excluded host equalities, quotient completions,
selected limits, and ambient completeness claims therefore cannot escape the
finite carrier boundary.
\end{proof}
