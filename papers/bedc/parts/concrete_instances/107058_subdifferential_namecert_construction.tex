\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SubdifferentialUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-subdifferential-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Rockafellar and Boyd--Vandenberghe, the $\SubdifferentialUp$ packet records the finite supporting-hyperplane data that makes a convex subgradient usable inside BEDC without importing a separation theorem. It sits beside the convex-set carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-convexset-namecert}, the conjugacy rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-convex-conjugate-namecert}, the duality handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fenchelduality-namecert}, the order boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-positive-cone-namecert}, and the stationarity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kkt-namecert}. The chapter names the local subgradient certificate only; it does not assert Hahn--Banach separation, quotient function-space duality, selected suprema, or choice-selected subgradients.

\section{Subdifferential Carrier}
\label{sec:subdifferential-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Subdifferential carrier]
\label{def:subdifferential-carrier}
A $\SubdifferentialUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(F,x,\xi,E,L,G,K,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the displayed convex functional row, including its finite convex-domain, convex-combination, value-comparison, and lower-support ledgers;
\item $x$ is the displayed base point row inside the carrier domain of $F$;
\item $\xi$ is the displayed dual covector row, read only through the finite pairing tests carried by the packet;
\item $E$ is the pairing-evaluation ledger recording the finite values $\langle \xi,y-x\rangle$ that are actually inspected;
\item $L$ is the supporting-inequality ledger asserting, for each displayed comparison point $y$, the row $F(y)\geq F(x)+\langle \xi,y-x\rangle$;
\item $G$ is the epigraph row that reads the same support inequality as a displayed lower supporting face of the epigraph carrier;
\item $K$ is the cone-boundary row connecting the finite support inequality to the $\PositiveConeUp$ order boundary and to the stationarity rows later consumed by $\KKTUp$;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the functional, base point, covector, pairing ledger, support ledger, epigraph row, cone boundary, continuation row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a subgradient request through the convex functional, pairing, support, epigraph, cone-boundary, and downstream handoff rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SubdifferentialUp$, $\ConvexSetUp$, $\ConvexConjugateUp$, $\FenchelDualityUp$, $\PositiveConeUp$, $\KKTUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SubdifferentialUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no ambient locally convex dual, no quotient function space, no selected separating hyperplane, no selected supremum in a conjugate formula, and no rule choosing a subgradient when the displayed support ledger is absent.
\end{definition}

\section{Subdifferential Certificate}
\label{sec:subdifferential-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Subdifferential NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:subdifferential-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SubdifferentialUp$ carrier $S=(F,x,\xi,E,L,G,K,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SubdifferentialUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the convex functional row, base point row, dual covector row, finite pairing-evaluation ledger, supporting-inequality ledger, epigraph row, cone boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item support-inequality exactness, requiring every exported subgradient read to pass through the displayed row $F(y)\geq F(x)+\langle \xi,y-x\rangle$ for the listed comparison point;
\item epigraph compatibility, requiring the support ledger and epigraph row to name the same lower supporting face and the same base point;
\item Fenchel handoff, requiring any conjugacy or duality consumer to pass through the displayed $\ConvexConjugateUp$ and $\FenchelDualityUp$ rows before reading a dual certificate;
\item KKT and normal-cone consumer boundary, requiring stationarity consumers to read the cone-boundary row and refusing any normal-cone claim not carried by the finite support ledger;
\item non-escape from Hahn--Banach, quotient function spaces, selected suprema, and choice-selected subgradients.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The rows $F,x,\xi,E,L,G,K$ are the entire analytic surface, and the rows $H,C,P,N$ are the entire structural surface.

Support exactness is a ledger condition. A consumer can call $\xi$ a subgradient only at a displayed comparison point whose support inequality is present in $L$ and whose pairing value is present in $E$.

Epigraph compatibility and the Fenchel handoff are then read through $G$ and the provenance row $P$. The same lower support row is available to epigraph, conjugacy, and duality consumers, but no hidden supremum or separating functional is added by that handoff.

Finally the stationarity boundary passes through $K$. Since no coordinate of $S$ names Hahn--Banach separation, a quotient dual space, a selected supremum, or a choice functional for subgradients, those principles cannot be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}
