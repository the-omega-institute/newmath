\begin{definition}[Closure-universality carrier]
\label{def:closure-universality-carrier}
A $\ClosureUniversalityQuadrantUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(U,D,G,S,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the universality-axis row, recording whether the accepted substrate read is universal, sub-universal, or refused for lack of displayed evidence.
\item $D$ is the closure-axis row, recording whether the accepted observation channel is closed, open, or refused for lack of displayed evidence.
\item $G$ is the quadrant-tag row determined by the displayed pair $(U,D)$.
\item $S$ is the substrate-witness row, naming the finite sibling surface from which the substrate read is consumed.
\item $A$ is the sibling-anchor ledger, containing the relevant $\CellularAutomatonUp$, $\CellularSubstrateUp$, $\HaltingObstructionUp$, and $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$ rows used by this packet.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $U,D,G,S,A,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes through the two axis rows, the quadrant tag, the substrate witness, and the sibling-anchor ledger.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClosureUniversalityQuadrantUp$, the quadrant vision, the sibling concrete chapters, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosureUniversalityQuadrantUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted packets only by these rows. It has no coordinate for a host proof of arbitrary universality, an ambient closure oracle, a hidden substrate evaluator, or a quadrant tag detached from the displayed axis pair.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closure-universality quadrant NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closure-universality-quadrant-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:closure-universality-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosureUniversalityQuadrantUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier exposure for $Q=(U,D,G,S,A,H,C,P,N)$, universality-axis accountability through $U$, closure-axis accountability through $D$, quadrant-tag determinacy through $G$, substrate-witness exposure through $S$, sibling-anchor replay through $A$, componentwise stability through $H$, displayed route replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$. A consumer cannot read a closed quadrant, an open quadrant, a universal quadrant, or a sub-universal quadrant unless the two axis rows and their determined tag are both present.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:closure-universality-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the two axis rows, quadrant tag, substrate witness, sibling-anchor ledger, transports, continuations, provenance, and local name as its only coordinates.

Axis accountability is componentwise. The universality read is taken from $U$, the closure read is taken from $D$, and the tag row $G$ is admitted only as the finite classifier row for that displayed pair.

The sibling ledger $A$ and substrate witness $S$ connect the abstract quadrant entry to concrete surfaces already present in the dossier. The consumer may replay those anchors through $C$ and transport them through $H$, but it cannot replace them with a hidden universality proof, closure oracle, or host substrate evaluator.

Finally $P$ and $N$ package the same finite route as the public naming surface. Since no detached quadrant tag or ambient axis oracle is carried, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closure-universality axis independence boundary]
\label{thm:closure-universality-axis-independence-boundary}
An accepted $\ClosureUniversalityQuadrantUp$ packet exposes the universality-axis row $U$ and the closure-axis row $D$ as separate displayed coordinates, and the quadrant tag $G$ is read only after both coordinates are present. Thus a consumer may transport or compare the two axes componentwise, but it may not infer either axis from the other, collapse the pair to a single sibling certificate, or replace the tag by a host-level substrate classification.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier of \autoref{def:closure-universality-carrier}. The universality row, closure row, and quadrant tag are distinct coordinates, so projection of one axis does not project the other.

The tag row $G$ is a classifier read from the displayed pair $(U,D)$. Therefore a tag comparison may use the componentwise transports in $H$ and the route replay in $C$, but it must keep both axis rows visible.

The sibling rows in $A$ witness non-bijection rather than collapse. $\CellularAutomatonUp$ supplies rule and orbit rows, $\CellularSubstrateUp$ supplies carrier rows, $\HaltingObstructionUp$ supplies finite obstruction rows, and $\BoundedUniversalTraceUp$ supplies bounded trace rows. None supplies both axis rows and the quadrant tag as one finite packet.
\end{proof}
