\begin{definition}[Shared tail normalizer carrier]
\label{def:shared-tail-normalizer-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two incoming tail-schedule rows, each already displayed as a finite regular-Cauchy source route;
\item $M$ is the shared threshold row that records the common finite bound serving both schedules;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window opened at the shared threshold;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the shared window;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the window and dyadic ledger;

\label{thm:shared-tail-normalizer-namecert-obligations}

\begin{proof}

The source schedules are exposed first. The shared threshold $M$ is then the only row that can serve both schedules, and the window $W$ is opened through that same threshold before any dyadic ledger, regular readback, fiber, or Real-facing seal is available.

\label{thm:shared-tail-normalizer-common-threshold-factorization}
