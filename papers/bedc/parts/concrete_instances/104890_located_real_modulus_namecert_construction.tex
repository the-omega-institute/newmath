\begin{definition}[LocatedRealModulus carrier]
\label{def:located-real-modulus-carrier}
A $\LocatedRealModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(L,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedRealUp$ row whose located interval and endpoint enclosure data answer the precision request.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ precision ledger recording the requested radius, endpoint slack, and comparison budget.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row opened from the located real at the displayed precision.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the same finite window and dyadic tolerance ledger.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $L,D,W,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the located-real row, dyadic ledger, finite window, regular readback, real seal, replay route, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay from a precision request through $L,D,W,R$ to the seal row $E$ and to downstream completion consumers.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedRealModulusUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RealSeparabilityUp$, $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient real equality, trichotomy, a selected limiting representative, countable choice, host completeness, or an ambient order oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedRealModulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-real-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\LocatedRealModulusUp$ carrier $M=(L,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, located-real row exposure, dyadic precision-ledger exactness, finite stream-window coverage, regular rational readback compatibility, real-seal non-escape, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for the packet of \autoref{def:located-real-modulus-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed tuple. The analytic rows are $L,D,W,R,E$, and the structural rows are $H,C,P,N$.

The located-real row $L$ supplies the enclosure surface, while $D$ records the radius and comparison budget for the request. The stream row $W$ and readback row $R$ are opened only at that displayed budget, so the window coverage and rational readback are tied to the same dyadic ledger.

The seal row $E$ is downstream of those reads. Transport and replay preserve the same order through $H$ and $C$, while $P$ and $N$ add provenance and naming without introducing quotient equality, trichotomy, selected limits, choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedRealModulus dyadic-window coverage]
\label{thm:located-real-modulus-dyadic-window-coverage}
Every precision request accepted by a $\LocatedRealModulusUp$ packet must display the dyadic ledger $D$ before opening the finite stream window $W$, and must display the window $W$ before reading the regular rational row $R$. Thus a $\RealSeparabilityUp$ or $\CauchyConvergenceModulusUp$ consumer receives a covered dyadic-window route
$$
D\longmapsto W\longmapsto R
$$
inside the same $\BHist$ packet, not a detached density choice, an infinite selector, or a host convergence proof.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier inventory of \autoref{def:located-real-modulus-carrier}. The dyadic ledger precedes the finite-window and readback rows in the accepted packet.

Use the local obligations of \autoref{thm:located-real-modulus-namecert-obligations}. They require dyadic precision exactness, window coverage, and regular rational readback compatibility to be certified for the same packet.

The separability and convergence-modulus consumers can therefore cite the displayed route only as finite coverage data. Since the packet contains no density selector, infinite choice schedule, quotient equality, or host convergence coordinate, none can be exported with the covered window.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedRealModulus real-seal handoff]
\label{thm:located-real-modulus-real-seal-handoff}

Next read the dyadic-window route of \autoref{thm:located-real-modulus-dyadic-window-coverage}. The precision ledger, finite stream window, and regular rational readback are already fixed before the seal row is available.

Finally replay the handoff through $C$ and transport it through $H$. The provenance and local naming rows record the same packet, so the terminal $\RealUp$ seal is a downstream read of finite modulus data rather than an appeal to quotient completion, choice, trichotomy, selected representatives, host equality, or ambient completeness.
