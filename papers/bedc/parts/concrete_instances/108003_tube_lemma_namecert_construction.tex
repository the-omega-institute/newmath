\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TubeLemmaUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-tube-lemma-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Munkres and Kelley on product topology and compact neighbourhoods, the $\TubeLemmaUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface for the tube lemma: when a slice around a compact factor lies inside an open product-neighbourhood row, a displayed neighbourhood in the other factor suffices for the whole compact slice. It sits over the product topology certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-product-topology-namecert}, the generated-open topology rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the compact finite-cover surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert}, and the finite-cover siblings such as \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interval-finite-cover-certificate-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-cover-refinement-namecert}. The chapter names only the finite tube-neighbourhood carrier and its local obligations; it does not assert product compactness, quotient equality of product points, selected neighbourhood bases, or ambient open-cover compactness beyond the displayed compact row.

\begin{definition}[Tube lemma carrier]
\label{def:tube-lemma-carrier}
A $\TubeLemmaUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(X,Y,K,O,U,V,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the source $\TopologyUp$ row naming the first factor.
\item $Y$ is the source $\TopologyUp$ row naming the second factor.
\item $K$ is the $\CompactUp$ row for the compact slice inside the second factor.
\item $O$ is the $\ProductUp$ open-neighbourhood row containing the displayed slice.
\item $U$ is the finite family of product-open rows read from $O$ along the compact slice.
\item $V$ is the resulting neighbourhood row in the first factor.
\item $F$ is the finite-cover row over $K$ that justifies replacing the family $U$ by one displayed tube neighbourhood.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two topology rows, compact row, product-open row, finite family, tube neighbourhood, finite-cover row, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a slice-neighbourhood request through $X,Y,K,O,U,V,F,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TubeLemmaUp$, $\ProductUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\CompactUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TubeLemmaUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,K,O,U,V,F,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for global product compactness, a host topological theorem, quotient equality of product points, an arbitrary open-cover selector, or a detached neighbourhood basis.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Tube lemma NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:tube-lemma-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\TubeLemmaUp$ carrier $T=(X,Y,K,O,U,V,F,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\TubeLemmaUp}$ surface is exhausted by carrier admission, topology-row exposure for both factors, compact-slice exposure through $K$, product-open exposure through $O$, finite product-neighbourhood family exposure through $U$, tube-neighbourhood exposure through $V$, finite-cover justification through $F$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:tube-lemma-carrier}. The accepted tuple exposes the two topology rows, the compact slice row, the product-open row, the finite product-neighbourhood family, the resulting tube neighbourhood, and the finite-cover row.

The compactness use is local to $K$ and $F$. The finite family $U$ is read from the displayed product-open neighbourhood $O$ along the compact slice, and $F$ records the finite-cover handoff that permits one neighbourhood row $V$ in the first factor to cover the whole slice.

Finally transport and replay preserve the same finite route. Since $H,C,P,N$ contain only componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and naming, they cannot add product compactness, quotient product equality, selected bases, or an arbitrary open-cover selector. The listed rows therefore exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
