\label{def:regulated-measure-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $V$ is the $\BoundedVariationUp$ control row, recording the partition-variation budget that bounds all displayed interval contributions.
\item $\Pi$ is the $\DyadicPartitionUp$ row, naming the ordered cells and refinement witnesses on which the packet is read.

\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RegulatedMeasure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regulated-measure-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegulatedMeasureUp$ carrier
$$
M=(F,V,\Pi,\Delta,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegulatedMeasureUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the regulated source $F$, bounded-variation control through $V$, dyadic partition exposure through $\Pi$, interval-mass exactness through $\Delta$, regular rational readback through $R$, terminal real sealing through $E$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regulated-measure-carrier}. The source rows $F,V,\Pi$ are finite regulated-function, bounded-variation, and dyadic partition data, so carrier admission begins from displayed rows rather than from an ambient measure space.

The interval-mass obligation is the dyadic ledger $\Delta$. Its cells are exactly the cells of $\Pi$, and its allowed values are constrained by the variation budget $V$ before any downstream readback is visible.

The readback and seal obligations are $R$ and $E$. They route the finite mass ledger through regular rational windows and then through the terminal real seal, while $H,C,P,N$ preserve transport, replay, provenance, and local naming for the same packet.

Since no omitted object is a carrier coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert surface and exclude sigma-measure completion, host quotient equality, selected limits, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}
