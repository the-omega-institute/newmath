\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealRootExistenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-real-root-existence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive intermediate-value reasoning, the $\RealRootExistenceUp$ packet turns a displayed sign-change bracket and its bisection-to-Cauchy seal into a public finite BEDC root-existence surface. It is grounded by the intermediate-value row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-intermediatevalue-namecert}, the sign-change bracket of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-root-bracket-namecert}, the bisection seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bisection-root-cauchy-seal-namecert}, the finite stream interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational and dyadic readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names the public existence certificate; it does not select a root.

\begin{definition}[RealRootExistence carrier]\label{def:real-root-existence-carrier}
A $\RealRootExistenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(I,F,S,B,R,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the located interval row. It records the lower and upper endpoint windows, endpoint order evidence, interval identity, and tolerance requests consumed by the public existence read.
\item $F$ is the endpoint sign row. It stores the displayed opposite-sign endpoint data and the sign orientation used by the root-existence packet.
\item $S$ is the continuity modulus row. It records the source tolerance demanded by each endpoint, midpoint, retained-window, and Real-facing read.
\item $B$ is the $\IntermediateValueUp$ ledger row. It stores the finite intermediate-value obligation surface attached to the displayed interval, endpoint signs, and modulus data.
\item $R$ is the $\RealRootBracketUp$ row. It records the finite bracket surface that keeps the sign-change interval visible as a BEDC certificate rather than as a host theorem.
\item $Q$ is the $\BisectionRootCauchySealUp$ row. It records the finite bisection branch ledger, retained windows, regular Cauchy readback, and public seal handoff.
\item $D$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ readback row. It contains the regular rational observations, dyadic tolerances, endpoint comparisons, branch radii, and rounding rows used by the seal.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row. It is visible only after the sign-change bracket, bisection branch, retained windows, and regular readback have been exposed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the interval, signs, modulus, intermediate-value ledger, bracket row, bisection seal, regular and dyadic readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a public root-existence request through located interval inspection, endpoint sign inspection, modulus use, intermediate-value admission, bracket exposure, bisection sealing, regular and dyadic readback, and Real-seal consumption.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealRootExistenceUp$, $\IntermediateValueUp$, $\RealRootBracketUp$, $\BisectionRootCauchySealUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealRootExistenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,F,S,B,R,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a selected root, quotient stream equality, real trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or host root equality.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealRootExistence NameCert obligations]\label{thm:real-root-existence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealRootExistenceUp$ carrier
$$
X=(I,F,S,B,R,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RealRootExistenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the located interval, endpoint signs, continuity modulus, intermediate-value ledger, real-root bracket, bisection-root Cauchy seal, regular and dyadic readback, Real seal, transports, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item sign-change stability, requiring $I$ and $F$ to expose the same located interval and opposite displayed endpoint signs before any public existence row is available;
\item continuity and modulus compatibility, requiring $S$ to answer each bracket, bisection, and seal request by source precision consumed by the same finite route;
\item bisection branch regularity, requiring $B,R,Q$ to keep the intermediate-value ledger, bracket row, branch prefix, midpoint sign rows, retained windows, and Cauchy readback attached to the displayed sign-change packet;
\item Real-seal non-escape, requiring $D,E,H,C,P,N$ to export only the displayed existence route and no selected root, quotient real equality, real trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or host root equality.
\end{enumerate}
No RealRootExistence obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealRootExistenceUp.RealRootExistenceTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-root-existence-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the located interval, endpoint signs, modulus row, intermediate-value ledger, bracket row, bisection seal, regular and dyadic readback, Real seal, and structural rows.

Sign-change stability is the entry stage. The rows $I$ and $F$ must be opened together, so the public existence read cannot replace the displayed interval and endpoint signs by an ambient real interval or by a host sign theorem.

Continuity and modulus compatibility is the precision stage. The row $S$ supplies the source precision used by endpoint inspection, midpoint inspection, retained-window requests, and the terminal seal, so all later rows consume one displayed continuity route.

Bisection branch regularity is the middle stage. The row $B$ admits the intermediate-value ledger, $R$ exposes the bracket surface, and $Q$ records the finite branch prefix, midpoint sign rows, retained windows, and regular Cauchy handoff.

Finally, $D,E,H,C,P,N$ perform readback, sealing, transport, replay, provenance, and naming for the same route. Since selected roots, quotient real equality, real trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient Real completeness, and host root equality are absent from the carrier, none of the five obligation rows can export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealRootExistence bisection seal handoff]\label{thm:real-root-existence-bisection-seal-handoff}
Every accepted $\RealRootExistenceUp$ read factors through $\IntermediateValueUp$, $\RealRootBracketUp$, $\BisectionRootCauchySealUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ before any public root seal is visible. In row form the route is
$$
I,F,S \longmapsto B \longmapsto R \longmapsto Q \longmapsto D \longmapsto E,H,C,P,N .
$$
The handoff exports a finite public existence certificate and a terminal seal boundary, not a selected root, quotient stream, trichotomy decision, or ambient Real-completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $X=(I,F,S,B,R,Q,D,E,H,C,P,N)$. The only entry rows are the located interval, endpoint sign, and modulus rows $I,F,S$.

The intermediate-value and bracket stages are forced next. The row $B$ records the $\IntermediateValueUp$ obligation surface attached to $I,F,S$, and the row $R$ records the $\RealRootBracketUp$ surface that keeps the sign-change bracket visible as finite data.

The bisection seal stage is $Q$. It records the $\BisectionRootCauchySealUp$ branch ledger, retained windows, StreamName-facing observations, and regular Cauchy handoff, all downstream of the same intermediate-value and bracket rows.

The public seal stage is terminal. The row $D$ reads the route as $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ data, the row $E$ seals the resulting boundary as $\RealUp$ data, and $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name that same route.

Thus every accepted read factors through the listed siblings before the public seal is visible. Because the carrier has no coordinate for a selected root, quotient stream, trichotomy row, or ambient Real-completeness theorem, the handoff cannot export any of those principles.
\end{proof}
