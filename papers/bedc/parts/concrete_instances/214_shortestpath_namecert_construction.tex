\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ShortestPathUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-shortestpath-namecert}

The shortest-path interface $\ShortestPathUp$ packages, on a weighted graph carrier with nonnegative weights, the path of minimum total weight between two vertices together with Dijkstra and Bellman-Ford correctness certificates. It depends on $\NameCert_{\GraphUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-graph-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\RealUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:shortestpath-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Shortest-path weighted graph carrier]
\label{def:shortestpath-bhist-weighted-graph-carrier}
A $\ShortestPathUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ weighted-graph
packet. Its visible rows consist of vertex rows, directed or undirected edge
rows, nonnegative $\RealUp$ weight rows attached to the displayed edges, source
and target endpoint rows, a finite path ledger, and the $\Pkg$ provenance row
that records which graph and weight packet the ledger reads from.

The carrier does not include a completed metric space, an infinite graph, an
unstated host ordering on vertices, or an external minimization oracle. Every
comparison row is read from the finite graph packet, the $\RealUp$ weight
certificates, the endpoint rows, and the displayed path ledger.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:shortestpath-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Shortest-path visible path ledger]
\label{thm:shortestpath-visible-path-ledger}
For any accepted $\ShortestPathUp$ packet, the readable path data are exactly
the finite vertex sequence in the path ledger, the edge-incidence rows
certifying that consecutive vertices are connected, the nonnegative $\RealUp$
weight rows for those edges, the source and target endpoint rows, and the
$\Pkg$ provenance row tying the ledger to the weighted-graph carrier.

No row of the certificate asserts an unseen edge, a hidden lower-weight path,
a completed real metric, or a host-index equality between vertices.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ShortestPathUp.ShortestPathWeightedGraphCarrier\_visible\_path\_ledger}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.ShortestPathUp.ShortestPathVisiblePathLedger}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the weighted-graph carrier of
\autoref{def:shortestpath-bhist-weighted-graph-carrier}. The only graph rows it
exports are the finite vertex rows, edge rows, endpoint rows, weight rows,
path-ledger rows, and package provenance.

Read the path ledger pointwise. Each adjacent pair in the displayed vertex
sequence is checked by one visible edge-incidence row, and each such edge
contributes its displayed nonnegative $\RealUp$ weight row.

The source and target constraints are the endpoint rows at the first and last
ledger positions. Since the carrier has no generator for unseen edges,
completed metrics, host-index equalities, or external minimization oracles, the
listed rows are exactly the visible path ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Shortest-path relaxation certificate scope]
\label{thm:shortestpath-relaxation-certificate-scope}
Dijkstra and Bellman-Ford readback for $\ShortestPathUp$ remains a finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger obligation. The certificate surface consists of the
weighted-graph carrier, the source and target endpoint rows, nonnegative
$\RealUp$ edge-weight rows, finite relaxation rows comparing displayed path
weights, predecessor or path-ledger rows, and the $\Pkg$ provenance row.

The certificate does not assert an ambient choice of shortest path, a total
ordering of all host vertices, an infinite relaxation process, or a completed
real-analysis object.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ShortestPathUp.ShortestPathWeightedGraphCarrier\_relaxation\_certificate\_scope}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.ShortestPathUp.ShortestPathRelaxationCertificate\_scope}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. It fixes a finite graph packet, a finite
family of nonnegative $\RealUp$ weight rows, and the package provenance used by
the path ledger.

For a Dijkstra readback, each settled-step claim is a row comparing visible
candidate path weights over the finite frontier named by the packet. For a
Bellman-Ford readback, each relaxation claim is a finite edge row together
with the displayed before-and-after path-weight comparison.

In both readings the predecessor or path-ledger row supplies the reconstructed
path, and \autoref{thm:shortestpath-visible-path-ledger} identifies the
readable endpoint and incidence rows. Thus the certificate surface is a finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger obligation over the named graph and weight rows, with
no ambient choice principle, host-vertex ordering, infinite process, or
completed real object.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Shortest-path relaxation ledger carrier]
\label{def:shortestpath-relaxation-ledger-carrier}
A $\ShortestPathUp$ relaxation ledger carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$
packet over the weighted-graph carrier of
\autoref{def:shortestpath-bhist-weighted-graph-carrier}. Its displayed rows
are the $\GraphUp$ vertex and edge rows, nonnegative $\RealUp$ edge-weight
rows, before-and-after path-weight rows, predecessor rows, visible $\Cont$
relaxation rows, the $\hsame$ comparisons recording the transported packet
equalities, and the $\Pkg$ provenance tying the relaxation ledger to the named
graph and weight packet.

The carrier records only finite relaxation evidence. It does not contain a
host minimizer, a hidden vertex order, an infinite relaxation stream, or a
completed metric assumption.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.ShortestPathUp.ShortestPathRelaxationLedgerCarrier}

\begin{theorem}[Shortest-path relaxation continuation closure]
\label{thm:shortestpath-relaxation-cont-closure}
Let a $\ShortestPathUp$ weighted-graph carrier display finite vertex rows,
edge rows, nonnegative $\RealUp$ weight rows, endpoint rows, and path-ledger
rows. If a transported relaxation packet also displays the before-and-after
path-weight rows, predecessor rows, visible $\Cont$ relaxation rows, matching
$\hsame$ comparisons, and the same $\Pkg$ provenance, then those rows repack
as an accepted relaxation ledger carrier over the same weighted graph.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ShortestPathUp.ShortestPathWeightedGraphCarrier\_relaxation\_cont\_closure}
\begin{proof}
Project the weighted-graph carrier rows. They give the finite vertex, edge,
weight, endpoint, path-ledger, and provenance rows named in
\autoref{def:shortestpath-bhist-weighted-graph-carrier}.

Next read the transported relaxation packet. Its visible $\Cont$ rows produce
the before-and-after path-weight rows and the predecessor rows, while the
$\hsame$ comparisons identify the transported packets with the displayed
source graph and weight rows.

The required carrier is obtained by repacking exactly these projected and
transported rows into
\autoref{def:shortestpath-relaxation-ledger-carrier}. The $\Pkg$ provenance is
unchanged, so the relaxation ledger remains attached to the original
weighted-graph packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Shortest-path visible relaxation NameCert surface]
\label{thm:shortestpath-visible-relaxation-namecert-surface}
The visible path ledger of
\autoref{thm:shortestpath-visible-path-ledger} together with an accepted
relaxation ledger carrier of
\autoref{def:shortestpath-relaxation-ledger-carrier} supplies the finite
$\NameCert_{\ShortestPathUp}$ surface for displayed Dijkstra or Bellman-Ford
readback. The surface consists of endpoint visibility, edge-incidence
visibility, nonnegative weight visibility, finite before-and-after
path-weight comparisons, predecessor visibility, relaxation $\Cont$ rows,
$\hsame$ transport comparisons, and $\Pkg$ provenance.

It does not expose a host minimization principle, a hidden vertex order, an
infinite relaxation process, or a completed metric-space assumption.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ShortestPathUp.ShortestPathWeightedGraphCarrier\_visible\_relaxation\_namecert\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:shortestpath-visible-path-ledger} to obtain the endpoint,
incidence, nonnegative weight, path-ledger, and provenance rows from the
visible path certificate.

Apply \autoref{thm:shortestpath-relaxation-cont-closure} to the transported
relaxation rows. This supplies the finite before-and-after path-weight rows,
predecessor rows, visible $\Cont$ relaxation rows, and $\hsame$ transport
comparisons over the same weighted graph.

Combining the two finite ledgers gives exactly the displayed
$\NameCert_{\ShortestPathUp}$ surface. Since neither carrier has a generator
for host minimizers, hidden vertex orderings, infinite relaxation streams, or
completed metric objects, none of those assumptions is part of the certificate
surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Shortest-path finite edge exhaustion]
\label{thm:shortestpath-finite-edge-exhaustion}
Every accepted $\ShortestPathUp$ path-ledger step is exhausted by visible
finite data: a displayed edge-incidence row, the source and target endpoint
rows it connects, a finite $\Cont$ route through the carrier rows, and the
$\Pkg$ provenance row that ties the step to the weighted-graph packet.

Consequently the certificate has no hidden edge source. Any edge used by the
accepted path is read from the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier of
\autoref{def:shortestpath-bhist-weighted-graph-carrier}, not from a host graph
outside the certificate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ShortestPathUp.ShortestPathWeightedGraphCarrier\_finite\_edge\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier constructors in
\autoref{def:shortestpath-bhist-weighted-graph-carrier}. The carrier exports
only vertex rows, edge rows, nonnegative weight rows, endpoint rows,
path-ledger rows, and the package provenance row.

Apply \autoref{thm:shortestpath-visible-path-ledger} to a single adjacent
ledger step. The step supplies its two displayed vertices, the incidence row
between them, and the endpoint rows used by the ledger.

It remains to account for routing and provenance. The finite $\Cont$ route is
the row-by-row traversal through those displayed carrier rows, and the $\Pkg$
row records that the traversal reads this weighted-graph packet. Since the
carrier has no constructor for an unseen edge source, the listed rows exhaust
the edge obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Shortest-path weight readback]
\label{thm:shortestpath-weight-readback}
For any accepted $\ShortestPathUp$ packet, the total weight of the displayed
path is read only from the visible nonnegative $\RealUp$ edge-weight rows and
the finite path-ledger summation rows.

No total path weight may be supplied by an ambient metric, an unlisted edge
weight, a host real-number accumulator, or an implicit minimization oracle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ShortestPathUp.ShortestPathWeightedGraphCarrier\_weight\_readback}
\begin{proof}
Start with the visible ledger theorem. By
\autoref{thm:shortestpath-visible-path-ledger}, every adjacent pair in the
accepted vertex sequence has one displayed edge-incidence row and one
displayed nonnegative $\RealUp$ weight row.

The summation rows are finite because the path ledger is finite in the
weighted-graph carrier. Reading them in ledger order gives the total weight as
the certificate's displayed finite sum of those edge-weight rows.

The carrier described in
\autoref{def:shortestpath-bhist-weighted-graph-carrier} contains no completed
metric object, no unlisted edge-weight generator, and no host accumulator.
Thus the readback of total weight is exactly the finite $\RealUp$ row
summation named by the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Shortest-path relaxation soundness]
\label{thm:shortestpath-relaxation-soundness}
A Dijkstra or Bellman-Ford relaxation row in an accepted $\ShortestPathUp$
packet can lower or preserve a displayed path-weight candidate only through
visible rows: the relaxed edge row, its predecessor row, its endpoint rows,
the relevant $\hsame$ identifications, the finite $\Cont$ route through the
ledger, and the $\Pkg$ provenance row for the weighted-graph carrier.

The relaxation certificate therefore cannot justify a candidate change by a
hidden predecessor, a host-side vertex equality, an unrecorded edge weight, or
an infinite relaxation process.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ShortestPathUp.ShortestPathWeightedGraphCarrier\_relaxation\_soundness}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:shortestpath-relaxation-certificate-scope}. It identifies a
relaxation claim as a finite row comparing displayed before-and-after
path-weight candidates over the named graph packet.

The row can mention an edge only through the carrier of
\autoref{def:shortestpath-bhist-weighted-graph-carrier}. Its predecessor and
endpoint data are therefore the displayed predecessor row, endpoint rows, and
edge-incidence row already present in the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:shortestpath-visible-path-ledger} to reconstruct the
candidate path segment. The required $\hsame$ identifications and finite
$\Cont$ route are the visible transports that keep the predecessor, edge, and
endpoint rows in the same ledger packet, while the $\Pkg$ row supplies
provenance.

No remaining constructor can lower or preserve the displayed candidate: the
certificate has no host-side equality source, no hidden predecessor row, no
unrecorded weight row, and no infinite relaxation process. The relaxation is
therefore sound exactly over the visible finite rows.
\end{proof}
