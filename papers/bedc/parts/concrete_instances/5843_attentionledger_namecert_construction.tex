The $\AttentionLedgerUp$ packet concretizes the observer-history vision of \autoref{ch:visions-observer-hist-identity} as a selected-row ledger with an explicit omitted-row boundary. It consumes the observer-history carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistoryidentity-namecert}, refines the filter surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-filter-namecert}, and hands only selected public rows to the StreamName surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. The packet is a NameCert object for attention as finite disclosure: the selected rows are public, the omitted rows remain certified boundary data, and the StreamName handoff cannot erase the split.

\begin{definition}[Attention ledger carrier]
\label{def:attention-ledger-carrier}
An $\AttentionLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(H,F,S,O,U,W,R,L,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the source $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$ row whose observation identity is being filtered;
\item $F$ is the $\ObserverFilterUp$ row that records the local selection decision;
\item $S$ is the selected-row ledger, listing the finite $\BHist$ rows admitted to public attention;
\item $O$ is the omitted-row ledger, listing the finite source rows not admitted to public attention together with their boundary reasons;
\item $U$ is the public attention row, naming the selected surface that a downstream consumer may inspect;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ handoff row served only from $S$ and $U$;
\item $R$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the source, filter, selected, omitted, public attention, StreamName, route, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $L$ is the ledger exactness row tying every source observation either to $S$ or to $O$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read the source identity, filter, selected rows, omitted boundary, public attention row, and StreamName handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$, $\ObserverFilterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AttentionLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these displayed coordinates. It has no coordinate for host observer equality, quotient stream equality, hidden attention state, or deletion of an omitted source row.
\end{definition}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The ledger rows split the source observations. The selected ledger $S$ contains exactly the rows admitted to public attention, while the omitted ledger $O$ contains the rows stopped at the boundary. The row $L$ records that every source observation has one of these two ledger readings.

\end{proof}

\label{thm:attention-ledger-non-escape}

No consumer route may turn an omitted row into public attention data, StreamName data, host observer equality, quotient stream equality, or hidden attention state.

\begin{theorem}[Attention ledger ObserverFilter handoff]
\label{thm:attention-ledger-observer-filter-handoff}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The attention packet records that separation as $S$ and $O$. The row $U$ is built from the selected side, and the StreamName row $W$ is routed through $S,U,R,C,P,N$.

The omitted ledger remains visible but non-exporting. It certifies what the filter did not select, so it cannot provide a StreamName row or replace the selected public attention row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Attention ledger public boundary]
\label{thm:attention-ledger-public-boundary}
An accepted $\AttentionLedgerUp$ consumer cannot export a StreamName-facing row unless both the selected-row ledger $S$ and the omitted-row boundary $O$ are present in the same carrier. The public attention row $U$ is therefore a boundary between visible selected data and certified omitted data, not a collapse of the two ledgers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume a consumer exports the StreamName-facing row $W$. Carrier projection exposes $W$ together with $S,O,U,R,L,C,P,N$ in the same finite packet.

The route to $W$ passes through the public attention row $U$, and $U$ is licensed by the exactness row $L$. That exactness row accounts for source observations only by the selected ledger $S$ and the omitted ledger $O$.

If either ledger is absent, the route lacks the displayed certificate separating public attention from boundary refusal. Hence an accepted export of $W$ requires both ledgers, and the public row $U$ remains the visible boundary rather than a hidden merger of selected and omitted data.
\end{proof}
