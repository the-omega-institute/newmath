The quasi-uniform completion route also has a distance-presented sibling edge. A consumer that asks for a directed ball, rather than a primitive entourage, must first read the $\QuasiMetricUp$ carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-quasi-metric-namecert}; only after the directed distance, zero diagonal, forward triangle, directed-ball, and Cauchy rows have been displayed may the consumer reflect to $\QuasiUniformSpaceUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, or $\MetricSpaceUp$.

\begin{theorem}[QuasiMetric forward link for quasi-uniform consumers]
\label{thm:quasi-uniform-space-quasi-metric-forward-link}
Every $\QuasiUniformSpaceUp$ completion-facing consumer that asks for a distance-presented directed ball factors through the $\QuasiMetricUp$ carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-quasi-metric-namecert} before reflecting to $\UniformSpaceUp$ or $\MetricSpaceUp$. The reflected quasi-uniform route may consume directed-ball and Cauchy rows, but it does not turn those rows into primitive symmetry, quotient completion, selected limits, or host metric equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:quasi-metric-directed-ball-handoff}. The distance-presented directed-ball request first reads the $\QuasiMetricUp$ source, point, directed distance, zero diagonal, forward triangle, directed-ball, Cauchy filter, Cauchy net, uniform-reflection, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Only after that route is displayed can the quasi-uniform carrier of \autoref{def:quasi-uniform-space-carrier} receive a directed entourage or completion-facing request. The reflected $\UniformSpaceUp$ or $\MetricSpaceUp$ consumer is therefore downstream of the quasi-metric route, not a replacement for it.

The non-escape clause of \autoref{thm:quasi-metric-nonescape} excludes primitive symmetry, quotient completion, selected limits, and host equality. The quasi-uniform packet adds no coordinate that reintroduces them.
\end{proof}
