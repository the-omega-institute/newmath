\begin{theorem}[Uniform-space sibling route for quasi-uniform data]
\label{thm:quasi-uniform-space-uniform-sibling-route}
Let
$$
Q=(U,D,F,N,E,H,C,P,L)
$$
be an accepted $\QuasiUniformSpaceUp$ carrier whose forward and reverse entourage ledgers agree as displayed finite rows. Then every symmetric quasi-uniform packet routes to the $\UniformSpaceUp$ sibling surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert} through the same finite entourage, $\hsame$-transport, $\Cont$-replay, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

The route is local to the displayed carrier:
$$
U,D \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,L .
$$
It does not claim completion, Hausdorff separation, host topological equality, selected limits, or quotient uniformity.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:quasi-uniform-space-carrier}. The directed entourage row $U$ and direction ledger $D$ are displayed before the reflected row $E$.

The agreement assumption says that the forward and reverse entourage ledgers are the same finite data when read as a symmetric packet. Thus the reflected row $E$ can expose the $\UniformSpaceUp$ sibling surface without adding primitive symmetric uniformity to the quasi-uniform carrier.

The structural rows $H,C,P,L$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since no further coordinate is present, the theorem records only the local sibling passage and leaves completion, Hausdorff separation, host topological equality, selected limits, and quotient uniformity outside the packet.
\end{proof}
