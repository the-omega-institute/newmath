\begin{theorem}[QuasiUniformSpace entourage-direction sibling route]
\label{thm:quasi-uniform-space-entourage-direction-sibling-route}
Let
$$
Q=(U,D,F,N,E,H,C,P,L)
$$
be an accepted $\QuasiUniformSpaceUp$ carrier. A directed-entourage read of $Q$ sits between the distance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-quasi-metric-namecert}, the symmetric-entourage rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}, the convergence routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, and the completion consumers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}. The directed entourage classifier is not a symmetric metric, selected limit, quotient completion, or open-cover compactness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:quasi-uniform-space-carrier}. The rows $U$ and $D$ expose directed entourage data and the direction ledger before the filter, net, reflection, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows can be consumed.

Use \autoref{thm:quasi-uniform-space-quasi-metric-forward-link}. Distance-presented directed balls must first pass through the $\QuasiMetricUp$ route, while metric-facing consumers remain reflected readers rather than primitive quasi-uniform coordinates.

Apply \autoref{thm:quasi-uniform-space-uniform-sibling-route} and \autoref{thm:quasi-uniform-space-directed-uniform-space-dependency}. Symmetric $\UniformSpaceUp$ data are downstream of the displayed directed rows and cannot replace them.

Finally use \autoref{thm:quasi-uniform-space-cauchy-filter-boundary} and \autoref{thm:quasi-uniform-space-uniform-completion-dependency-route}. The $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyNetUp$, and $\UniformCompletionUp$ routes read the same directed boundary, so completion-facing consumers receive no selected limit, quotient completion, symmetric metric, or open-cover compactness coordinate.
\end{proof}
