\begin{theorem}[Quasi-uniform uniform-completion dependency route]
\label{thm:quasi-uniform-space-uniform-completion-dependency-route}
Every $\UniformCompletionUp$ consumer of a $\QuasiUniformSpaceUp$ packet first reads the directed entourage row, the direction ledger, the $\CauchyFilterUp$ and $\CauchyNetUp$ boundary rows, and the reflected $\UniformSpaceUp$ row before it reaches the completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}. The route is mediated by the Cauchy-filter and Cauchy-net siblings of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, so uniform completion is a downstream reader of directed quasi-uniform data rather than a primitive coordinate of the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:quasi-uniform-space-carrier}. The directed entourage row and direction ledger are exposed before any filter, net, or reflected uniform consumer is visible.

The $\CauchyFilterUp$ and $\CauchyNetUp$ rows then bind completion-facing reads to the displayed directed boundary. The reflected $\UniformSpaceUp$ row can be read only after those boundary rows have been displayed.

Thus every uniform-completion request reaches \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert} through the same directed entourage, direction-ledger, Cauchy-filter, Cauchy-net, and reflected-uniform route, and no quotient completion or selected limit is promoted to primitive quasi-uniform data.
\end{proof}
