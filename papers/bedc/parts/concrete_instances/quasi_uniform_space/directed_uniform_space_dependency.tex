\begin{theorem}[Directed uniform-space dependency for quasi-uniform packets]
\label{thm:quasi-uniform-space-directed-uniform-space-dependency}
Let
$$
Q=(U,D,F,N,E,H,C,P,L)
$$
be an accepted $\QuasiUniformSpaceUp$ carrier. Any route from $Q$ to the $\UniformSpaceUp$ sibling of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert} must first display the directed entourage row $U$ and the direction ledger $D$. Only after those rows are visible may the reflected row $E$ name a symmetric uniform-space reader; the quasi-uniform packet itself still exports directed entourage data rather than primitive symmetric uniformity, quotient completion, selected limits, or host equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:quasi-uniform-space-carrier}. The directed entourage row $U$ and direction ledger $D$ precede every Cauchy-facing, reflected-uniform, transport, replay, provenance, or naming row.

Apply the uniform reflection route of \autoref{thm:quasi-uniform-space-uniform-reflection-route}. The reflected row $E$ names $\UniformSpaceUp$ only as a downstream reader of the displayed directed data.

Finally use the uniform-space sibling route of \autoref{thm:quasi-uniform-space-uniform-sibling-route}. It identifies the sibling passage through $U,D,E,H,C,P,L$, so the dependency edge to \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert} is real, but it does not promote symmetric uniformity or completion data to primitive quasi-uniform coordinates.
\end{proof}
