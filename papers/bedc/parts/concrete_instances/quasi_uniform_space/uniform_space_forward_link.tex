\begin{theorem}[Quasi-uniform forward uniform-space link]
\label{thm:quasi-uniform-space-uniform-space-forward-link}
Let
$$
Q=(U,D,F,N,E,H,C,P,L)
$$
be an accepted $\QuasiUniformSpaceUp$ carrier whose forward and reverse entourage requests are both displayed in the directed ledger $D$. Then every symmetric uniform consumer of $Q$ factors through the $\UniformSpaceUp$ sibling of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert} before the completion-facing source row consumed by $\CompleteUniformSpaceUp$ in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-uniform-space-namecert} is available.

The route is
$$
U,D \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,L,
$$
where $E$ reads the reflected $\UniformSpaceUp$ row only after both directions have been displayed. The resulting completion source is therefore a finite reflected uniform row, not primitive symmetric data inside the quasi-uniform carrier and not a selected quotient completion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:quasi-uniform-space-carrier}. The directed entourage row $U$ and the ledger $D$ are read before the reflected row $E$, so a symmetric consumer must see both the forward and reverse displayed requests before it can cite a uniform-space row.

Use the uniform reflection route of \autoref{thm:quasi-uniform-space-uniform-reflection-route}. Once both directions are present, $E$ supplies the reflected $\UniformSpaceUp$ sibling surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}, while $H,C,P,L$ only transport, replay, source, and name that same reflected route.

Finally read the complete-uniform-space carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-uniform-space-namecert}. Its completion source row consumes a displayed $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage surface, so the quasi-uniform packet reaches it only through the reflected uniform row and not through primitive symmetric uniformity, quotient completion, or a selected limit.
\end{proof}
