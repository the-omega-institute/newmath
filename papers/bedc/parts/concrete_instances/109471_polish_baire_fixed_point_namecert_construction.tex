Following Banach's fixed-point theorem and Baire's complete-metric category theorem, the $\PolishBaireFixedPointUp$ packet records the finite route by which a complete separable metric carrier, a Baire surface, and a contraction-iteration ledger are read together before any endpoint is treated as a host object. It routes through the complete-separable carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polishspace-namecert}, the ultrametric Baire window of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-baire-ultrametric-namecert}, the complete-metric Baire surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-metric-baire-surface-namecert}, the Picard step route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-picardcontraction-namecert}, and the finite iteration packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fixed-point-iteration-namecert}. The chapter names the displayed fixed-point route without claiming open-cover compactness, countable choice, a selected host fixed point, or any fixed-point primitive outside the carried rows.

\begin{definition}[Polish-Baire fixed-point carrier]
\label{def:polish-baire-fixed-point-carrier}
A $\PolishBaireFixedPointUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(P,B,U,C,I,E,H,R,G,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the $\PolishSpaceUp$ row exposing the complete separable metric carrier, stream-name schedule, regular rational readback, observation ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $B$ is the $\BaireUltrametricUp$ row that records the finite nested-window Baire route consumed by the packet.
\item $U$ is the $\CompleteMetricBaireSurfaceUp$ row joining a dense-open schedule, complete-metric Cauchy thread, metric carrier, finite windows, rational readback, and terminal real seal.
\item $C$ is the $\PicardContractionUp$ row carrying the one-step contraction route over the displayed metric source.
\item $I$ is the $\FixedPointIterationUp$ row carrying the finite orbit ledger, residual budget, completion handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local name for the same route.
\item $E$ is the endpoint-candidate seal row. It is readable only after $P,B,U,C,I$ have been displayed, and it records no selected host point or host fixed-point equality.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the Polish-space, Baire-ultrametric, complete-metric Baire, Picard-contraction, iteration, endpoint-seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $R$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a contraction request through the Baire windows, complete-metric surface, Picard step, finite iteration ledger, residual budget, and endpoint-candidate seal.
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PolishBaireFixedPointUp$, $\PolishSpaceUp$, $\BaireUltrametricUp$, $\CompleteMetricBaireSurfaceUp$, $\PicardContractionUp$, $\FixedPointIterationUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PolishBaireFixedPointUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, countable-choice selection, quotient completion equality, host fixed-point equality, or a primitive fixed-point theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Polish-Baire fixed-point NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:polish-baire-fixed-point-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PolishBaireFixedPointUp$ carrier
$$
F=(P,B,U,C,I,E,H,R,G,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\PolishBaireFixedPointUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, Polish complete-separable source exposure, Baire-ultrametric window exposure, complete-metric Baire-surface routing, Picard one-step readability, finite fixed-point iteration ledger exposure, endpoint-candidate sealing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. No NameCert obligation is stored outside the rows of \autoref{def:polish-baire-fixed-point-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The rows $P,B,U,C,I,E$ are the only analytic rows available to the local certificate: complete separable source, Baire window, complete-metric Baire surface, Picard step, finite iteration ledger, and endpoint-candidate seal.

The source and Baire obligations are read before any endpoint row. The Polish row supplies the common complete-separable metric surface, while the two Baire rows expose only finite nested-window and complete-metric schedule data.

The fixed-point route is then read through $C$ and $I$. The Picard row supplies the one-step contraction route, and the iteration row supplies the finite orbit ledger and residual budget without selecting a completed fixed point.

Finally $H,R,G,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier contains no coordinate beyond these rows, the listed surface exhausts the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Polish-Baire fixed-point iteration nonescape]
\label{thm:polish-baire-fixed-point-iteration-nonescape}
Every fixed-point-facing read from an accepted $\PolishBaireFixedPointUp$ carrier must pass through
$$
\PolishSpaceUp,\ \BaireUltrametricUp,\ \CompleteMetricBaireSurfaceUp,\ \PicardContractionUp,\ \FixedPointIterationUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
In particular, the endpoint-candidate row $E$ is consumable only after the complete-separable source, Baire windows, complete-metric Baire surface, Picard step, finite orbit ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows have been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. The endpoint-candidate seal $E$ is stored after the Polish source $P$, Baire rows $B,U$, Picard route $C$, and finite iteration ledger $I$.

Apply the NameCert obligation theorem. It makes the complete-separable source, Baire schedule, Picard step, finite orbit ledger, and residual budget part of the public local surface before the endpoint-candidate seal can be read.

The structural rows $H,R,G,N$ then preserve the same route under transport, replay it for downstream consumers, record provenance, and attach the local name. Because no separate coordinate stores open-cover compactness, countable choice, host fixed-point equality, or a fixed-point primitive, any fixed-point-facing read remains inside the displayed route.
\end{proof}
