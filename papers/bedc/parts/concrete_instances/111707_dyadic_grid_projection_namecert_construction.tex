\begin{definition}[Dyadic grid projection carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-grid-projection}
A $\DyadicGridProjectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $x$ is the source observation row, carrying the finite real-name or regular Cauchy read requested by the consumer.
\item $n$ is the unary precision row, naming the dyadic scale at which the grid is inspected.
\item $a$ and $b$ are adjacent $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint rows at precision $n$.
\item $I$ is the interval-containment row, displaying that the finite observation of $x$ lies inside the closed dyadic window bounded by $a$ and $b$.
\item $E$ is the finite error-budget row, recording the tolerance consumed by the projection and the remaining budget available to downstream readback.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source observations, unary precision rows, dyadic endpoints, containment windows, error budgets, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$

$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicGridProjectionUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\DyadicRoundingUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicGridProjectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $x,n,a,b,I,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host rounding, trichotomy on rationals, quotient real equality, selected Cauchy limits, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic grid projection endpoint coverage]
\label{thm:dyadic-grid-projection-endpoint-coverage}

Hence a consumer receives a finite dyadic window covering the displayed source observation $x$ without a host rounding operator or trichotomy oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-grid-projection}. The only endpoint coordinates are $a$ and $b$, and their role is fixed by the displayed unary precision row $n$.

The containment row $I$ is listed after the endpoint rows, so it can be read only as a finite interval statement over the same adjacent dyadic pair. The finite error budget $E$ records the tolerated projection loss but does not add a selector for an exact host representative.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same rows. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host rounding, rational trichotomy, quotient real equality, selected limits, or ambient completeness, the coverage exported by the packet is exactly the displayed finite endpoint window.
\end{proof}
