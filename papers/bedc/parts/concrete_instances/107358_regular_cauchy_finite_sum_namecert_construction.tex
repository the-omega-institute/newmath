It sits after the regular rational source surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic arithmetic core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the binary addition packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-addition-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the finite-product sibling of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-finite-product-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy finite sum carrier]\label{def:regular-cauchy-finite-sum-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyFiniteSumUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(I,S,W,D,A,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the finite unary index row. It lists exactly the summand slots opened by the request and the displayed fold order used by the addition route.
\item $S$ is the component $\RegSeqRatUp$ source family, one regular rational Cauchy name for each slot of $I$.
\item $W$ is the synchronized $\StreamNameUp$ window family for component reads, intermediate addition reads, and the final sum-facing read.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ sum-budget ledger, storing component observations, intermediate sum observations, requested radii, and slack cells.
\item $A$ is the folded $\RegularCauchyAdditionUp$ route row. It records the adjacent binary additions named by $I,W,D$ and exposes no host finite-sum operation.
\item $R$ is the final $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained only after the source, window, dyadic, and folded-addition rows have been displayed.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached after the regular readback of the folded sum has been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the index row, summand sources, synchronized windows, dyadic ledger, folded-addition route, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from any finite-sum request through the same rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyFiniteSumUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegularCauchyAdditionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegularCauchyFiniteProductUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyFiniteSumUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,S,W,D,A,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host finite sum, quotient stream equality, selected limit, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy finite sum NameCert obligations]\label{thm:regular-cauchy-finite-sum-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyFiniteSumUp$ carrier
$$
K=(I,S,W,D,A,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyFiniteSumUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite unary index row, component $\RegSeqRatUp$ summands, synchronized $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ budget ledger, folded $\RegularCauchyAdditionUp$ route, final readback, $\RealUp$ seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item finite tuple-window coverage, requiring every summand slot in $I$ to open the matching source row and all synchronized windows needed by the fold;
\item dyadic sum-budget exactness, requiring every intermediate addition observation to consume its assigned radius and slack cells from $D$;
\item RegSeqRat readback stability, requiring the final readback row $R$ to depend only on the displayed sources, windows, dyadic ledger, and folded-addition route;
\item Real seal non-escape, requiring $E,H,C,P,N$ to expose no host finite sum, quotient stream equality, selected limit, trichotomy, ambient completeness theorem, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No Regular Cauchy finite sum obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-finite-sum-carrier}. The displayed tuple $I,S,W,D,A,R,E,H,C,P,N$ is the whole public surface, so carrier admission is exactly the admission of those rows.

Tuple-window coverage is forced by the index row. Each slot named by $I$ has one component $\RegSeqRatUp$ source in $S$, and the synchronized window row $W$ is the only place where component, intermediate, and final sum reads are opened.

The dyadic budget is read through $D$. Each intermediate binary addition request consumes a displayed radius and slack cell from that ledger before the corresponding folded $\RegularCauchyAdditionUp$ row $A$ can be inspected.

The final regular readback row $R$ is downstream of $S,W,D,A$. Therefore readback stability follows from the carrier order: no sum-facing $\RegSeqRatUp$ data can be inspected until the synchronized windows, dyadic budget, and folded addition route have already been displayed.

Finally, the seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name rows preserve only this route. Since none of the excluded objects is a coordinate of the carrier, the listed five rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy finite sum budget route]\label{thm:regular-cauchy-finite-sum-budget-route}
Let
$$
K=(I,S,W,D,A,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\RegularCauchyFiniteSumUp$ carrier. Every finite sum read from $K$ factors through
$$
I,S \longmapsto W \longmapsto D,A \longmapsto R \longmapsto E,H,C,P,N .
$$
In particular, regrouping displayed adjacent additions may change only the fold order named by $I$ and $A$; it preserves the same synchronized windows $W$, dyadic sum-budget ledger $D$, final $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, and $\RealUp$ seal $E$ when those rows are fixed by the accepted carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a finite sum request. The request first names the finite unary index row $I$ and the component source family $S$, because these are the only carrier rows that identify which regular Cauchy names participate in the fold.

The requested component and intermediate observations then pass through $W$. This synchronized window row aligns component reads with the intermediate addition reads used by the displayed fold.

The dyadic part of the request is read next through $D$, and the adjacent binary additions are read through $A$. The proof uses the displayed $\RegularCauchyAdditionUp$ rows rather than host associativity of sums.

Only after those rows are displayed can the final regular rational readback $R$ be consumed. The downstream rows $E,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name the same route, so the finite sum read cannot bypass the synchronized windows, the dyadic ledger, or the folded-addition route.
\end{proof}
