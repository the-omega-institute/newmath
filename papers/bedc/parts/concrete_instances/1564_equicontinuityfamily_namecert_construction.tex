\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-equicontinuityfamily-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$ interface names the constructive finite-ledger
surface for a family of continuous maps with a shared source metric, shared
target metric, finite center probes, and rational tolerance histories. It is
the BEDC reading of the equicontinuity object used in Kelley, \emph{General
Topology}, and Bishop--Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, re-expressed
as finite rows over $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$,
$\UniformContinuityUp$, and $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$. The packet does
not claim open-cover compactness, host function extensionality, or a chosen
function-space topology.

The finite-center modulus ledger consumes the compact-uniform continuity packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactuniformcontinuity-namecert} before exposing the family-level equicontinuity row.

\begin{definition}[EquicontinuityFamily carrier]
\label{def:equicontinuity-family-carrier}
An $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
E=(X,Y,F,\mathcal{P},R,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the shared source $\MetricUp$ or $\CompactMetricUp$ row whose
finite source reads are routed through displayed metric and compact-metric
data;
\item $Y$ is the shared target $\MetricUp$ row whose distance comparisons
are routed through displayed metric-distance witnesses;
\item $F$ is a finite family ledger of $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph rows from
$X$ to $Y$;
\item $\mathcal{P}$ is a finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of source centers
used as the displayed testing window for the family;
\item $R$ records the requested $\RatUp$ tolerance histories and the
source-radius histories offered in response;
\item $M$ records pointwise modulus rows indexed by the centers in
$\mathcal{P}$, uniformly over the finite family ledger $F$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the source metric,
target metric, graph rows, center bundle, rational tolerance rows, modulus
rows, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records the visible $\Cont$ routes by which metric,
compact-metric, continuous-map, rational-tolerance, modulus, and
equicontinuity-family consumers read the packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$,
$\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformContinuityUp$,
$\CompactUniformContinuityUp$, $\RatUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$,
$\hsame$, and $\Cont$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuityFamilyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the displayed source and target metric rows, finite
family rows, rational tolerance rows, pointwise finite-center modulus rows,
transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming
provenance. It contains no host function equality, no open-cover
compactness, no selected modulus function, and no ambient function-space
topology.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[EquicontinuityFamily NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-family-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\EquicontinuityFamilyUp}$ surface consists exactly of
five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation by the finite packet of
\autoref{def:equicontinuity-family-carrier};
\item classifier transport comparing the shared source and target metric
rows, the family graph rows, the pointwise modulus rows, the finite center
bundle, the rational tolerance rows, the transport routes, and the
provenance componentwise;
\item shared finite-center modulus coverage, stating that every displayed
family read used by the packet factors through one center row of
$\mathcal{P}$ and the corresponding modulus row in $M$;
\item rational tolerance stability, stating that tolerance and radius rows
are transported through the $\RatUp$ classifier and the stored $\hsame$ and
$\Cont$ routes;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that every downstream read remains inside
the displayed metric, continuous-map, modulus, package, and local naming
rows.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EquicontinuityFamilyUp.EquicontinuityFamilyPacket\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is projection of
\autoref{def:equicontinuity-family-carrier}. The shared source row, shared
target row, family ledger, finite center bundle, rational tolerance ledger,
pointwise modulus rows, transport rows, continuation routes, package
provenance, and local naming row are all visible coordinates of the same
finite packet.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The source and target metric rows
transport their displayed distance witnesses, the $\ContinuousMapUp$ rows
transport the graph and pointwise modulus reads, the center bundle transports
the finite probe indices, and the rational rows transport requested
tolerances together with their offered source radii.

For shared finite-center coverage, read a family member through $F$ and a
requested tolerance through $R$. The packet routes that read to a displayed
center of $\mathcal{P}$ and then to the corresponding row of $M$, where the
same source-radius history is used uniformly over the finite family ledger.

Rational tolerance stability follows from the same transported $R$ rows and
the displayed $\RatUp$ comparisons. Replaying the $\Cont$ routes preserves
which tolerance request, which radius response, and which center-indexed
modulus row are being consumed.

These rows exhaust the certificate surface. Because the carrier has no
coordinate for host function equality, open-cover compactness, selected
modulus functions, or ambient function-space topology, no classifier or
consumer route can export those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EquicontinuityFamily shared-center coverage]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-family-shared-center-coverage}
Every displayed family read from an accepted $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$ packet factors through one finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ center in $\mathcal{P}$ and the corresponding shared modulus row in $M$. The read uses the shared source row $X$, shared target row $Y$, finite $\ContinuousMapUp$ family ledger $F$, rational tolerance row $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ rows $H$, displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$ of \autoref{def:equicontinuity-family-carrier}.

The coverage statement exports no open-cover compactness, host function extensionality, selected global modulus function, or ambient function-space topology.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EquicontinuityFamilyUp.EquicontinuityFamilyPacket\_shared\_center\_coverage}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:equicontinuity-family-carrier}. Its only center data are the finite probe bundle $\mathcal{P}$, and its only modulus data are the pointwise rows in $M$ indexed by those centers.

Read a displayed family member through the finite ledger $F$ and a tolerance through $R$. The NameCert obligation theorem routes that read to one center of $\mathcal{P}$ and the matching shared modulus row, uniformly over the finite family ledger.

The transport rows $H$ and continuation routes $C$ preserve the selected center and modulus row as finite packet data. Since the carrier has no coordinates for open covers, host function equality, selected modulus functions, or function-space topology, the shared-center read cannot escape to those objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EquicontinuityFamily rational-radius transport]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-family-rational-radius-transport}
The $\RatUp$ tolerance and radius rows in an accepted $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$ packet are transported only through the stored $\hsame$ rows and displayed $\Cont$ routes. Transport preserves the requested tolerance row, the offered rational source-radius row, the selected probe-center row, and the corresponding modulus row without selecting a modulus function outside the finite packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.EquicontinuityFamilyUp.EquicontinuityFamilyPacket\_rational\_radius\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the tolerance ledger $R$ in \autoref{def:equicontinuity-family-carrier}. It records requested rational tolerances and offered source radii as displayed $\RatUp$ histories.

Apply the classifier transport row of \autoref{thm:equicontinuity-family-namecert-obligations}. The $\RatUp$ rows move componentwise through $H$, and the read routes are replayed through $C$.

Use shared-center coverage to keep the transported radius attached to the same finite probe-center and modulus-row pattern. The operation is therefore transport of stored rational rows, not selection of a hidden modulus function.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EquicontinuityFamily ledger non-escape package]
\label{thm:equicontinuity-family-ledger-non-escape-package}
The carrier of \autoref{def:equicontinuity-family-carrier}, classifier transport in \autoref{thm:equicontinuity-family-namecert-obligations}, shared-center coverage in \autoref{thm:equicontinuity-family-shared-center-coverage}, and rational-radius transport in \autoref{thm:equicontinuity-family-rational-radius-transport} assemble the ledger non-escape package for $\EquicontinuityFamilyUp$. Every consumer read remains within the displayed $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformContinuityUp$, $\CompactUniformContinuityUp$, $\RatUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

The package excludes open-cover compactness, host function extensionality, selected modulus functions, and ambient function-space topology.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First bind the packet rows. The carrier lists the shared metric rows, finite family ledger, probe bundle, rational histories, modulus rows, transports, routes, provenance, and local name row.

Next bind the coverage and transport rows. Shared-center coverage forces each family read through one displayed probe center and matching modulus row, while rational-radius transport keeps tolerance and radius data inside $\RatUp$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$ rows.

Finally apply the ledger non-escape clause of the NameCert obligation theorem. It states that downstream reads stay inside the displayed metric, continuous-map, modulus, package, and local naming rows. Repacking these rows gives the announced non-escape package, with no route to open-cover compactness, host function extensionality, selected modulus functions, or function-space topology.
\end{proof}
