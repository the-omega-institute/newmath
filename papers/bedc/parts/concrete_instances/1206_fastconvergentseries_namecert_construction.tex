\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-fastconvergentseries-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ interface names the finite certificate surface for a constructive series whose partial sums come with an explicit fast tail schedule. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, where convergence of series is carried by effective Cauchy information, the packet records only displayed summand observations, partial-sum readbacks, tail bounds, real-seal rows, transport routes, and provenance. It does not form a quotient of streams, select an infinite sum, or import host completeness for real numbers.

Its fast tail schedule and finite comparison ledger consume the displayed dyadic tail-bound surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadictailbound-namecert} before the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff and the $\RealUp$ seal. Thus the tail-budget carrier is read through a concrete dyadic bound rather than an ambient convergence predicate.

The fast-tail packet is a consumer of the regular rational readback and real-seal surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. It does not create a real endpoint: partial-sum rows $Q$, the tail schedule $\mu$, and the tail-bound ledger $T$ are routed into the existing $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff $R$ and then into the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$.

\begin{definition}[FastConvergentSeries BHist carrier]
\label{def:fastconvergentseries-bhist-carrier}
A $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
F=(A,S,Q,\mu,T,R,E,L,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the $\SeriesUp$ source row naming the summand stream and its finite partial-sum ledger;
\item $S$ is the $\SeqUp$ observation surface through which displayed summand rows and prefix rows are read;
\item $Q$ is the finite family of partial-sum observations displayed at requested unary indices;
\item $\mu$ is a $\CauchyModulusUp$ fast tail schedule assigning each precision row a finite threshold after which tails are bounded by the requested tolerance;
\item $T$ is the tail-bound ledger comparing displayed partial sums past $\mu$ and recording the resulting rational tolerance rows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ partial-sum readback surface obtained from $Q$, $\mu$, and $T$;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing ledger for the real name represented by the partial-sum readbacks;
\item $L$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for summand observations, partial sums, tail bounds, schedules, and seal-facing rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ read routes through which finite consumers reach the summand, partial-sum, tail-bound, readback, and seal rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SeriesUp$, $\SeqUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and the rational source rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FastConvergentSeriesUp}$ provenance row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a finite fast-convergence series packet. It contains no quotient stream, no chosen infinite sum, and no ambient completeness proof.
\end{definition}
