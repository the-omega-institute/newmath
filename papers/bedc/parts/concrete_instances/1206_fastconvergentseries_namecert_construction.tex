\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-fastconvergentseries-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ interface names the finite certificate surface for a constructive series whose partial sums come with an explicit fast tail schedule. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, where convergence of series is carried by effective Cauchy information, the packet records only displayed summand observations, partial-sum readbacks, tail bounds, real-seal rows, transport routes, and provenance. It does not form a quotient of streams, select an infinite sum, or import host completeness for real numbers.

\begin{definition}[FastConvergentSeries BHist carrier]
\label{def:fastconvergentseries-bhist-carrier}
A $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
F=(A,S,Q,\mu,T,R,E,L,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the $\SeriesUp$ source row naming the summand stream and its finite partial-sum ledger;
\item $S$ is the $\SeqUp$ observation surface through which displayed summand rows and prefix rows are read;
\item $Q$ is the finite family of partial-sum observations displayed at requested unary indices;
\item $\mu$ is a $\CauchyModulusUp$ fast tail schedule assigning each precision row a finite threshold after which tails are bounded by the requested tolerance;
\item $T$ is the tail-bound ledger comparing displayed partial sums past $\mu$ and recording the resulting rational tolerance rows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ partial-sum readback surface obtained from $Q$, $\mu$, and $T$;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing ledger for the real name represented by the partial-sum readbacks;
\item $L$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for summand observations, partial sums, tail bounds, schedules, and seal-facing rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ read routes through which finite consumers reach the summand, partial-sum, tail-bound, readback, and seal rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SeriesUp$, $\SeqUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, and the rational source rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FastConvergentSeriesUp}$ provenance row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a finite fast-convergence series packet. It contains no quotient stream, no chosen infinite sum, and no ambient completeness proof.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[FastConvergentSeries classifier]
\label{def:fastconvergentseries-classifier}
For accepted $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ packets $F$ and $F'$, the classifier $\mathsf{FastConvergentSeriesClassifier}(F,F')$ compares only the displayed partial-sum rows, tail-bound ledgers, fast tail schedules, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, $\RealUp$ seal-facing rows, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ read routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

It does not compare hidden summability predicates, host real endpoints, infinite stream quotients, or choice-selected sums. Any accepted transport must therefore preserve the finite rows listed in \autoref{def:fastconvergentseries-bhist-carrier}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FastConvergentSeries constant zero seed]
\label{thm:fastconvergentseries-constant-zero-seed}
The stationary zero summand stream determines an accepted $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ carrier. Its $\SeriesUp$ and $\SeqUp$ rows display only zero summand observations, every displayed partial sum is the zero row, the fast tail schedule may use the zero threshold for every precision request, the tail-bound ledger records zero tails, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback is the constant zero regular sequence, and the $\RealUp$ seal-facing row is the constant zero real name.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the zero summand row in the source carrier and read it through the $\SeqUp$ observation surface. Every finite prefix has the same zero partial-sum row, so the $\SeriesUp$ partial-sum ledger is inhabited by the displayed zero rows.

Choose the fast tail schedule that assigns the zero threshold to every precision request. Since every later partial sum is again zero, the tail-bound ledger compares identical displayed rows and records the requested tolerance as satisfied.

Repack the same zero rows as the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback and the $\RealUp$ seal-facing ledger. The $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ routes are componentwise identities on the displayed finite rows, and the package provenance names only the source certificates and the local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FastConvergentSeries tail-sum boundary]
\label{thm:fastconvergentseries-tail-sum-boundary}
For every accepted $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ packet and every displayed precision request, any $\RealUp$ consumer read of the represented sum factors through one fast tail schedule row, the displayed partial-sum rows past that threshold, the tail-bound ledger comparing those rows, the resulting $\RegSeqRatUp$ partial-sum readback, and the seal-facing $\RealUp$ row. The consumer receives no coordinate for a host infinite sum, quotient equality of streams, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FastConvergentSeriesUp.FastConvergentSeriesCarrier\_tail\_sum\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier coordinates of \autoref{def:fastconvergentseries-bhist-carrier}. A precision request first reads the schedule row $\mu$, which supplies the finite threshold used by the tail-bound ledger.

The consumer then reaches the displayed partial sums through the $\SeqUp$ and $\SeriesUp$ rows. The tail-bound ledger compares only those finite partial-sum rows past the threshold and records the rational tolerance evidence visible at that precision.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff reads the same partial-sum ledger as a regular rational sequence, and the $\RealUp$ seal-facing row is attached to that handoff. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a host sum, quotient stream, or completeness proof, none can be exported by the read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FastConvergentSeries regular rational tail-bound handoff]
\label{thm:fastconvergentseries-regseqrat-tail-bound-obligations}
For an accepted $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ packet, the displayed partial-sum rows, fast tail schedule, and finite tail-bound ledger determine the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row used by the real-seal ledger. Classifier transport of the schedule rows and tail-bound rows transports that handoff row componentwise.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FastConvergentSeriesUp.FastConvergentSeriesTailBoundPacket\_regseqrat\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows $Q$, $\mu$, $T$, $R$, and $E$ from \autoref{def:fastconvergentseries-bhist-carrier}. The partial-sum rows $Q$ supply the displayed rational approximants, the schedule $\mu$ supplies the finite threshold for each requested precision, and the tail-bound ledger $T$ records the comparison rows past that threshold.

Use \autoref{thm:fastconvergentseries-tail-sum-boundary} to factor every real-facing read through the same schedule and finite comparison ledger. Thus the regular rational readback row $R$ is the repacking of $Q$, $\mu$, and $T$ that the seal-facing row $E$ is allowed to consume.

Under classifier transport, the schedule rows and tail-bound rows move by the stored componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons. Repack the transported finite rows as the transported $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row. No host infinite sum, quotient stream, or ambient completeness object is introduced in this repacking.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FastConvergentSeries NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:fastconvergentseries-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\FastConvergentSeriesUp}$ obligation surface consists of five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier inhabitation: \autoref{thm:fastconvergentseries-constant-zero-seed} supplies a concrete accepted packet for the stationary zero series;
\item classifier transport: \autoref{def:fastconvergentseries-classifier} transports displayed partial sums, tail-bound rows, schedules, readbacks, seal-facing rows, $\hsame$ routes, $\Cont$ routes, package provenance, and local provenance componentwise;
\item tail-sum boundary: \autoref{thm:fastconvergentseries-tail-sum-boundary} forces every real-facing read through the displayed fast tail schedule and finite partial-sum comparison ledger;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff: the partial-sum rows, schedule rows, and tail-bound ledger repack as the visible regular rational readback row $R$ of \autoref{def:fastconvergentseries-bhist-carrier};
\item $\RealUp$ seal non-escape: the seal-facing ledger $E$ may read only the $\SeriesUp$, $\SeqUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows displayed by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
These rows are exactly the finite naming-certificate surface for fast convergent series.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The first row is the explicit zero-series inhabitant. It provides summand observations, partial sums, tail bounds, regular readbacks, and the real seal without importing any completion object.

The classifier row is componentwise by definition. Each permitted transport is one of the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons or $\Cont$ read routes, so transported packets preserve the same partial-sum, tail-bound, schedule, readback, and seal-facing coordinates.

The boundary row is \autoref{thm:fastconvergentseries-tail-sum-boundary}. It shows that every real-facing read must pass through the finite schedule and partial-sum ledger before reaching the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ rows.

The final two rows are projections of the same carrier. The regular readback forgets no tail evidence needed by $\RegSeqRatUp$, and the real seal has no field from which a quotient stream, selected sum, or ambient completeness principle could be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FastConvergentSeries scoped kernel factorization]
\label{thm:fastconvergentseries-scoped-kernel-factorization}
For every accepted $\FastConvergentSeriesUp$ packet, the consumer route to a real-seal read is exhausted by the displayed $\SeriesUp$ source row, $\SeqUp$ observation surface, $\CauchyModulusUp$ fast tail schedule, finite tail-bound ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ partial-sum handoff, $\RealUp$ seal row, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ read routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row.

No real-seal consumer can factor the read through an ambient summability predicate, a quotient of summand streams, a selected host sum, or a completeness theorem outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the tail boundary. By \autoref{thm:fastconvergentseries-tail-sum-boundary}, every precision request reaches the fast tail schedule, the partial-sum rows past the displayed threshold, and the finite comparison ledger before it reaches any real-facing row.

Next identify the regular rational handoff. By \autoref{thm:fastconvergentseries-regseqrat-tail-bound-obligations}, the same partial-sum rows, schedule rows, and tail-bound ledger determine the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback consumed by the seal-facing ledger.

Finally bind the local certificate surface. By \autoref{thm:fastconvergentseries-namecert-obligation-surface}, the only remaining routes are the displayed $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ reads, package provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row attached to the same carrier.

Combining these three projections exhausts the kernel route for the real-seal read. Since none of the cited rows contains an ambient summability predicate, quotient stream, selected sum, or completeness theorem, no such object can be exported by the scoped packet.
\end{proof}
