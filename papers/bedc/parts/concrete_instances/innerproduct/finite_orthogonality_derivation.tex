\begin{theorem}[InnerProduct finite orthogonality derivation]
\label{thm:innerproduct-finite-orthogonality-derivation}
For every accepted $\InnerProductUp$ packet, the zero-left orthogonality row is derived by finite inspection of the $\VecSpaceUp$ carrier, the scalar endpoint classifier, the sesquilinear rows, the conjugate-symmetry row, the positivity row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row. The derivation is the BEDC route through \autoref{def:innerproduct-bhist-carrier}, \autoref{def:innerproduct-bhist-source}, \autoref{thm:innerproduct-symmetry-positivity-row}, \autoref{thm:innerproduct-vecspace-linearity-row}, \autoref{def:innerproduct-orthogonality-row}, and \autoref{thm:innerproduct-zero-left-orthogonal-row}; it is not Hilbert completeness, Banach completion, spectral theory, or a host inner-product-space theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the displayed carrier and source rows. The $\VecSpaceUp$ carrier and scalar endpoint classifier are already visible in \autoref{def:innerproduct-bhist-carrier} and \autoref{def:innerproduct-bhist-source}, so the zero-left claim is read inside the accepted BEDC packet rather than in a host vector space.

Next inspect the form laws. The sesquilinear source row of \autoref{thm:innerproduct-vecspace-linearity-row} and the conjugate-symmetry and positivity rows of \autoref{thm:innerproduct-symmetry-positivity-row} expose exactly the scalar-form data used by the orthogonality classifier.

Finally apply the orthogonality rows. The definition \autoref{def:innerproduct-orthogonality-row} names the finite classifier surface, and \autoref{thm:innerproduct-zero-left-orthogonal-row} derives the zero-left row from that surface. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming keep the same rows attached to the accepted packet, so the result is a finite BEDC route and exports no Hilbert, Banach, spectral, or host-completeness principle.
\end{proof}
