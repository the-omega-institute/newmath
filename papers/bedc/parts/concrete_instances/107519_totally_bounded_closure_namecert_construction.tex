\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TotallyBoundedClosureUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-totally-bounded-closure-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on total boundedness and Kelley and Bourbaki on metric closure, the $\TotallyBoundedClosureUp$ packet records the finite BEDC route by which a displayed closure window of a totally bounded metric carrier remains available to finite-net consumers. It sits after the finite-net source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, the metric-distance surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the compact metric consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the closed-subspace completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-subspace-completion-namecert}, and the precompact bridge of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-precompactmetric-namecert}. The packet names only displayed closure-window data and finite rational readbacks; it does not import host closure, selected limits, quotient completion, open-cover compactness, or ambient real completeness.

\begin{definition}[TotallyBoundedClosure carrier]
\label{def:totally-bounded-closure-carrier}
A $\TotallyBoundedClosureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(T,M,F,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net source row, including the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ center families, $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, center-carrier rows, distance witnesses, and rational bound ledgers of \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier};
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ distance row for the same source, exposing endpoint classifiers, distance-witness reads, triangle ledgers, and the exact comparison rows used by closure requests;
\item $F$ is the finite closure-window ledger, naming the displayed approximating source points, their tolerance windows, and the inclusion route from each window point into the closure carrier;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row that records only the finite observation prefixes needed by a closure consumer before a net center is inspected;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the rational tails attached to the closure windows and the finite-net tolerances;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row for endpoint distance bounds and closure radii already exposed by $M,F,W,R$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all rows $T,M,F,W,R,E$ and for every center, window point, rational tail, real seal, and metric-distance history read from them;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay route from closure-window reads to the source finite-net row, then back through the metric distance ledger to the closure carrier;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TotallyBoundedClosureUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ClosedSubspaceCompletionUp$, $\PrecompactMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TotallyBoundedClosureUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier on such packets is componentwise transport through $H$ together with replay of the finite routes in $C$. The carrier has no coordinate for a host closure operator, a choice-selected convergent sequence, quotient completion equality, an open-cover compactness principle, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TotallyBoundedClosure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:totally-bounded-closure-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\TotallyBoundedClosureUp$ carrier $K=(T,M,F,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\TotallyBoundedClosureUp}$ surface consists exactly of six obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: every row exposed to a consumer is one of the ten displayed rows or a finite subrow of the totally bounded source, metric distance ledger, closure-window ledger, stream window, rational readback, or real seal;
\item classifier transport: packet equality is componentwise $\hsame$ transport of centers, source points, closure-window points, stream prefixes, rational tails, real seals, metric distances, provenance rows, and local naming rows;
\item finite-net inheritance: each positive tolerance read reaches the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, membership constructors, center histories, distance witnesses, and rational comparison ledgers already named by $T$;
\item closure-window exactness: each closure point read first passes through a finite window in $F,W,R,E$ before the metric row $M$ compares it with a displayed source point;
\item replay discipline: every handoff from a closure-window point to a finite net factors through the displayed $\Cont$ route in $C$ and the same source metric rows in $M$;
\item provenance and non-escape: $P$ and $N$ name only the listed source certificates, and the packet exports no host closure, selected limit, quotient completion, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness coordinate.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations make total boundedness under displayed closure a finite-row interface rather than a semantic closure theorem in an ambient metric space.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:totally-bounded-closure-carrier}. The ten rows list the finite-net source, metric distance row, closure-window ledger, stream windows, regular rational readbacks, real seals, transport ledger, replay ledger, provenance row, and local naming row.

Classifier transport is the role of $H$. It moves the source centers, closure-window points, rational tails, real seals, and metric-distance histories by $\hsame$, while the point and distance classifiers in $M$ keep the source and closure endpoint reads aligned.

Finite-net inheritance is read from $T$. A positive tolerance request reaches exactly the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine, its $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership constructors, center-carrier rows, coverage-distance witnesses, and rational comparison ledgers.

Closure-window exactness is supplied by $F,W,R,E$. The packet never asks for a host closure membership proof; it displays the finite windows, the stream prefixes, the rational tail reads, and the real endpoint seals that justify each closure read before any finite net is consumed.

Finally, $C,P,N$ package the handoff. The replay route sends a closure-window point to a displayed source approximation, reads the totally bounded center through $T$, compares the closure point through $M,R,E$, and returns only the named finite rows to compact, completion, or precompact consumers. Since no other coordinate is present, host closure, selected limits, quotient completion, open-cover compactness, countable choice, and ambient real completeness cannot be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TotallyBoundedClosure finite-net closure handoff]
\label{thm:totally-bounded-closure-finite-net-closure-handoff}
Every $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ClosedSubspaceCompletionUp$, or $\PrecompactMetricUp$ consumer of an accepted $\TotallyBoundedClosureUp$ packet $K=(T,M,F,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ reads total boundedness of the closure only through the finite route
$$
\TotallyBoundedUp,\MetricUp,F,W,R,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
For each positive tolerance history, the consumer receives a displayed source net from $T$, a closure-window approximation from $F,W,R,E$, and the metric comparison row from $M$ that transfers the source-net bound to the closure point. The route exports no host closure operator, no chosen dense sequence, no quotient completion, no selected subcover, and no ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a positive tolerance request made by one of the listed consumers. The request is first routed by $C$ to the finite-net source row $T$, so the available centers and coverage witnesses are exactly the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}.

For a closure point, the packet then reads a finite window in $F$ together with the prefix row in $W$, the regular rational tail row in $R$, and the real seal in $E$. These rows display a source approximation close enough for the requested tolerance; no closure predicate outside the packet is inspected.

The metric row $M$ compares the closure point, the displayed source approximation, and the selected net center through the same endpoint classifiers and distance ledgers. Transport by $H$ and replay by $C$ make the comparison stable under the packet classifier.

Thus any compact-metric, closed-subspace-completion, or precompact consumer sees only the finite-net source, closure windows, rational readbacks, real seals, metric comparisons, transport, replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows. The excluded host closure, selected dense sequence, quotient completion, selected subcover, and ambient completeness data have no carrier coordinate from which they could be read.
\end{proof}
