\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finitetraceevaluator-namecert}

The $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ packet realizes the runtime-candidate surface of \autoref{def:reflection-runtime-candidate} inside the reflection-limit boundary of \autoref{ch:visions-reflection-and-limits}. It sits beside the runtime-candidate boundary packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-runtimecandidateboundary-namecert}, the diagonal finite-trace packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdistinctiontrace-namecert}, and the finite-trace GAP socket of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-trace-gap-socket-namecert}. Those chapters expose, respectively, object-level runtime rows, a certificate-level diagonal trace, and the finite-window boundary; the present packet records a bounded evaluator trace whose accepted result is visible while host validation of the evaluator remains outside the packet.

\begin{definition}[Finite trace evaluator carrier]
\label{def:finite-trace-evaluator-carrier}
A $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the input row of coded $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ expressions, proof objects, and states admitted to the bounded evaluator;
\item $T$ is the finite trace-step row, listing the displayed evaluator steps from the input row $I$ without adding an unbounded runtime;
\item $A$ is the accepted-result classifier row, grouping exactly the results reached by the displayed trace-step row $T$;
\item $V$ is the validation-boundary row, recording that host validation of the evaluator is not a packet coordinate;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the input, trace-step, accepted-result, validation-boundary, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads $I$, replays $T$, reaches $A$, checks the boundary row $V$, and returns through provenance and local naming;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, the structural $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ coding interface, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-runtimecandidateboundary-namecert};
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts exactly packets whose visible coordinates are $I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for a host evaluator, a total truth predicate, an unbounded execution stream, or the judgment that the bounded evaluator validates itself.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: every accepted object exposes the displayed packet $E=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N)$;
\item finite trace-step exposure: every evaluator step readable by a consumer is a row of the finite trace-step coordinate $T$ reached from the input coordinate $I$;
\item accepted-result classifier: every accepted result is classified by $A$ only after the corresponding trace-step row in $T$ has been displayed;
\item runtime-validation non-escape: the boundary row $V$ keeps host validation outside $I,T,A,H,C,P,N$ and prevents a packet row from serving as its own validator;
\item ledger exhaustion: the provenance and local naming rows $P,N$ exhaust the available dependencies and expose no unlisted evaluator, truth predicate, total halting predicate, or infinite trace.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently the certificate names a bounded object-level evaluator trace and its accepted-result ledger, but it does not internalize the host judgment that licenses the evaluator.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is tuple projection. The packet $E=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N)$ is finite, and every visible object-level coordinate is one of the displayed rows.

Trace-step exposure begins with $I$ and then reads $T$. Since $T$ is the only trace coordinate, a consumer can inspect only the finitely displayed evaluator steps and cannot replace them by an ambient runtime.

The accepted-result classifier is reached only after that trace read. A result belongs to $A$ because the packet displays the corresponding step in $T$; no accepted result is supplied by a hidden classifier or by host execution.

Runtime validation is separated by $V$. The argument of \autoref{thm:reflection-structure-existence-boundary} and the sibling packet \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-runtimecandidateboundary-namecert} place validation of a runtime candidate outside its source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger fields. Thus the finite trace evaluator records a boundary row rather than a self-validating checker.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ bind provenance and local naming without adding new evaluator content. Ledger exhaustion follows because every available consumer route has already passed through $I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N$, so an unlisted evaluator, truth predicate, total halting predicate, or infinite trace would require an extra coordinate absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator object-level boundary]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-object-level-boundary}
Every consumer read of an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
I \to T \to A \to V \to H \to C \to P \to N.
$$
The consumer may inspect the coded input, replay the displayed finite trace, and read the accepted-result classifier, but it cannot extract host validation, total halting information, truth for $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentences, or an unbounded evaluator from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-carrier}. The input, trace-step, accepted-result, and validation-boundary information is exhausted by $I,T,A,V$.

Apply \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-namecert-obligations}. Its trace-step exposure and accepted-result classifier clauses force every evaluator read through $T$ and then through $A$, while its runtime-validation non-escape clause keeps the host validating judgment outside the packet.

The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, route, cite, and name the same finite packet. Hence every consumer read factors through the displayed route, and no unbounded evaluator or internal truth classifier is available from the finite trace evaluator.
\end{proof}
