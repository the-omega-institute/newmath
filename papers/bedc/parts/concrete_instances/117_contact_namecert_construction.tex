\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ContactUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-contact-namecert}

The contact-structure interface $\ContactUp$ packages a maximally nonintegrable hyperplane field on an odd-dimensional manifold carrier, recorded as a $1$-form whose top-degree wedge is nondegenerate. It depends on $\NameCert_{\DiffFormUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-diffform-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-manifold-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:contact-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[ContactUp carrier and classifier obligations]
\label{thm:contact-carrier-classifier-obligation}
A $\ContactUp$ candidate must expose four carrier rows. The first row is a $\ManifoldUp$ carrier. The second row is an odd-dimensional hyperplane-field carrier over the same manifold row. The third row is a contact one-form row over $\DiffFormUp$. The fourth row is a classifier whose $\hsame$ transport preserves both the manifold carrier and the hyperplane-field row.

Equivalently, the carrier part of the $\NameCert_{\ContactUp}$ obligation surface is not a free ambient smooth object: it is the displayed BEDC package consisting of the $\ManifoldUp$ source row, the hyperplane-field row, the $\DiffFormUp$ one-form row, and the restricted classifier relating those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the seed scope for $\ContactUp$. Its named dependencies are the $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\DiffFormUp}$ sources, so the candidate carrier can only project manifold and differential-form rows licensed by those two sources.

The roadmap ten-field template then fixes the public carrier and classifier slots. The carrier slot records the odd-dimensional manifold, the hyperplane-field row, and the one-form row. The classifier slot is the restriction of $\hsame$ to these rows, so carrier transport keeps the manifold and hyperplane-field entries in the same displayed package.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:contact-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[ContactUp contact-form and nondegeneracy obligations]
\label{thm:contact-form-nondegeneracy-obligation}
For a $\ContactUp$ candidate, the $\DiffFormUp$ one-form row must supply the top-degree wedge witness on the same $\ManifoldUp$ carrier. If the manifold has contact dimension $2n+1$, the ledger records the row for $\alpha\wedge(d\alpha)^n$ and a nondegeneracy witness for that top-degree row on the displayed manifold carrier.

This witness cannot be replaced by an external smooth-structure primitive. The obligation is exact only when the one-form row, its exterior-differential row, the wedge exponent, and the top-degree nondegeneracy row all appear inside the $\ContactUp$ certificate ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the seed description as a maximally nonintegrable hyperplane field recorded by a $1$-form. The same seed names the manifold carrier and the $\DiffFormUp$ source, so the wedge expression must be built from rows already visible in those two sources.

The roadmap ten-field template places nondegeneracy in the core theorem and exactness slots. Therefore the top-degree wedge row and its witness are ledger data for the displayed one-form, not an imported analytic primitive. Exactness follows by allowing only those rows to certify the contact-form condition.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ContactUp stability ledger and exactness obligations]
\label{thm:contact-stability-ledger-exactness-obligation}
The stability surface of $\ContactUp$ consists of four transport obligations: $\DiffFormUp$ transport for the contact one-form and exterior-differential rows, $\ManifoldUp$ transport for the underlying manifold carrier, wedge-nondegeneracy transport for the top-degree row, and classifier transport for the hyperplane field under $\hsame$.

The exact ledger records exactly the one-form row, the hyperplane-field row, the wedge exponent, and the nondegeneracy witness. A certified $\ContactUp$ object may expose no additional hidden smooth-structure row as part of its contact obligation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the stability row in the roadmap ten-field template. Dependency transport comes from $\NameCert_{\DiffFormUp}$ for differential-form rows and from $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ for the manifold row, while $\hsame$ carries the classifier comparison for the hyperplane-field entry.

It remains to identify the ledger. The seed scope already lists the one-form, hyperplane field, top-degree wedge, nondegeneracy row, stability obligations, and ledger obligations. Repacking those rows gives the exact ledger stated in the theorem, and excluding any further smooth-structure primitive is precisely the exactness clause.
\end{proof}
