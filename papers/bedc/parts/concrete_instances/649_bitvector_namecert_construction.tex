\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BitVectorUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bitvector-namecert}

The $\BitVectorUp$ interface names fixed-length bit strings as finite BEDC
data rather than as host-level binary strings. Knuth, \emph{The Art of
Computer Programming}, Vol. 4A, treats fixed-length bit strings as finite
combinatorial objects; the BEDC packet below keeps that reading at the
$\mathsf{BHist}$ level by using $\BoolUp$, $\ListUp$, and $\NatUp$ rows with
explicit length and provenance ledgers.

\begin{definition}[BitVector BHist source]\label{def:bitvector-bhist-source}
A $\BitVectorUp$ source packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ record
$$
B=(n,s,l,p)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $n$ is a unary $\NatUp$ length row;
\item $s$ is a $\ListUp$ spine whose entries are $\BoolUp$ histories and whose
displayed list length is transported from $n$;
\item $l$ is the length ledger recording that every public bit read of $s$
lies at an index below $n$ and that no row beyond the displayed length is read;
\item $p$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance naming the consumed $\BoolUp$, $\ListUp$,
$\NatUp$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$ certificate rows.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is $\mathsf{BitVectorSource}(n,s,l,p)$. The classifier
on accepted packets compares the unary length rows by $\hsame$, compares the
list spines by the $\ListUp$ classifier, compares each visible bit by the
$\BoolUp$ classifier, and requires the same $\Cont$ routes and $\Pkg$
provenance. No host bit-string equality, machine word, numeric binary
encoding, or hidden padding convention is exported.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BitVector carrier stability]\label{thm:bitvector-carrier-stability}
Let $B=(n,s,l,p)$ be an accepted $\BitVectorUp$ source packet. If the unary
length row, the list-of-bool spine, the length ledger, and the provenance row
are transported by displayed $\hsame$ along the same $\Cont$ routes named by
$p$, then the transported packet $B'=(n',s',l',p')$ is again an accepted
$\BitVectorUp$ source packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the four coordinates of \autoref{def:bitvector-bhist-source}. The
$\NatUp$ length row transports first, so the displayed target length is fixed
before any bit read is considered.

Transport the $\ListUp$ spine along that length row. Each visible component of
the spine is a $\BoolUp$ history, so the component reads transport by the
componentwise $\BoolUp$ classifier and the displayed $\hsame$ rows.

Finally transport the length ledger and $\Pkg$ provenance through the same
$\Cont$ routes. The ledger still permits exactly the positions below the
transported length and hides every position beyond it, so the transported
packet is accepted.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BitVector classifier laws]\label{thm:bitvector-classifier-laws}
The $\BitVectorUp$ classifier is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive on
accepted packets by componentwise use of the $\NatUp$ length classifier, the
$\ListUp$ spine classifier, and the $\BoolUp$ entry classifier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BitVectorUp.BitVectorSourceClassifier\_laws}
\begin{proof}
For reflexivity, take the reflexive $\hsame$ row on the displayed unary length
and the reflexive $\ListUp$ comparison of the list spine. Each entry read is a
$\BoolUp$ row and hence compares with itself by the boolean classifier.

For symmetry, reverse the displayed length comparison, then reverse the
$\ListUp$ spine comparison and every visible $\BoolUp$ entry comparison. The
same ledger and provenance rows are read in the opposite order.

For transitivity, compose the two displayed length comparisons, compose the two
list-spine comparisons, and compose the entrywise boolean comparisons at each
transported index. The length ledger keeps the index set fixed under the
transport, so no hidden position enters the composite classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BitVector ledger coverage]\label{thm:bitvector-ledger-coverage}
Every public read of an accepted $\BitVectorUp$ packet factors through the
finite ledger $(n,s,l,p)$. The packet exposes exactly the unary length row, the
list-of-bool spine, the length exactness ledger, the componentwise $\BoolUp$
classifier rows, the $\Cont$ routes, and the $\Pkg$ provenance.

Consequently the packet exports no host binary-string primitive, no numeric
interpretation of the bits, no padding policy, and no machine-word overflow
rule.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. Its tuple lists exactly four coordinates,
and each coordinate is a finite row over the existing BEDC kernel and finite
data certificates.

A length-facing consumer reads $n$. A bit-facing consumer reads $s$ only
through the positions admitted by $l$. A transport-facing consumer reads the
componentwise $\BoolUp$ and $\ListUp$ classifier rows together with the
displayed $\Cont$ routes and provenance named by $p$.

No other coordinate is present in the packet. Therefore every public consumer
projection is one of the displayed rows or a projection through those route and
provenance rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BitVector NameCert obligation surface]\label{thm:bitvector-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\BitVectorUp}$ obligation surface consists of five
finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation: the empty vector is carried by the zero $\NatUp$
length row, the empty $\ListUp$ boolean spine, the empty length ledger, and the
corresponding $\Pkg$ provenance;
\item classifier laws: reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are inherited
componentwise from the length, list, and boolean classifiers of
\autoref{thm:bitvector-classifier-laws};
\item length-ledger exactness: the public bit positions are exactly the
positions below the displayed unary length row;
\item transport under $\hsame$: the carrier stability of
\autoref{thm:bitvector-carrier-stability} repacks every transported length,
spine, ledger, and provenance row as another accepted packet;
\item downstream finite-data consumption: list, product, matrix-index, and
error-code chapters may read only the displayed finite bit spine, length row,
componentwise classifier rows, continuation routes, and provenance ledger.
\end{enumerate}
These rows are the complete seed obligation for the fixed-length bit-vector
name.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For carrier habitation, choose the zero unary length row and the empty
$\ListUp$ spine of $\BoolUp$ entries. The length ledger has no public bit
position to expose, and the provenance row records only the consumed finite
data certificates.

For the classifier laws, apply \autoref{thm:bitvector-classifier-laws}. The
classifier is built from the displayed length comparison, list-spine
comparison, and entrywise boolean comparisons, so the three equivalence laws
are inherited componentwise.

For length-ledger exactness, unfold the carrier. A public bit read must be a
read of the list spine at an index admitted by $l$, and $l$ admits exactly the
indices below $n$.

For transport, apply \autoref{thm:bitvector-carrier-stability}. The same
$\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes transport the length row, the boolean spine, the
ledger, and the provenance without adding a hidden binary-string comparison.

For downstream use, every listed consumer receives only the rows displayed in
\autoref{thm:bitvector-ledger-coverage}. Thus a list, product, matrix-index,
or error-code construction can consume fixed-length bit data without treating
bit strings as host primitives.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BitVector finite-data anchor]\label{thm:bitvector-finite-data-anchor}
The $\BitVectorUp$ source packet is a finite-data anchor between $\ListUp$ and
later finite algebraic encodings. It can be consumed as a length-indexed
boolean list with explicit $\NatUp$, $\ListUp$, $\BoolUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
and $\Pkg$ rows, and it supplies no additional arithmetic, hashing, coding, or
matrix operation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the packet through \autoref{def:bitvector-bhist-source}. The only payload
row is a $\ListUp$ spine of $\BoolUp$ entries, and the only shape row is the
unary $\NatUp$ length with its ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:bitvector-ledger-coverage}. It identifies every public
consumer route with the finite length, boolean spine, classifier rows,
continuations, and provenance.

The exclusions follow by projection: the packet has no coordinate for a
numeric interpretation, hash evaluation, error-correction metric, matrix
indexing operation, or host machine word. Those later chapters may consume the
finite bit-vector source, but they must add their own operation rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BitVector public finite-algebra export]\label{thm:bitvector-public-finite-algebra-export}
Every downstream finite algebraic encoding consuming $\BitVectorUp$ receives
only the unary $\NatUp$ length row, the $\ListUp$ spine of $\BoolUp$ entries,
the length ledger, componentwise classifier rows, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$
routes, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance from
\autoref{def:bitvector-bhist-source}. It receives no host bit-string equality,
numeric binary primitive, padding policy, hash evaluator, or machine-word
overflow rule.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The source of every public read is \autoref{def:bitvector-bhist-source}. Carrier
stability is supplied by \autoref{thm:bitvector-carrier-stability}, and the
componentwise classifier laws are supplied by
\autoref{thm:bitvector-classifier-laws}.

Apply \autoref{thm:bitvector-ledger-coverage} to identify the visible length
ledger and its admitted list positions. Apply
\autoref{thm:bitvector-namecert-obligation-surface} to package the carrier,
classifier, stability, ledger, transport, and provenance rows as the public
certificate surface.

Finally use \autoref{thm:bitvector-finite-data-anchor}. It states that finite
algebraic consumers may read the fixed-length boolean list surface but must add
their own arithmetic, coding, hash, matrix, or machine-word operation rows.

Thus the public export is exactly the displayed finite-data packet. Since the
packet has no coordinate for host bit-string equality, binary arithmetic,
padding, hashing, or overflow, none of those structures can be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BitVector scoped dependency binding]\label{thm:bitvector-scoped-dependency-binding}
The $\BitVectorUp$ source packet, carrier stability, classifier laws, ledger
coverage, $\NameCert$ obligation surface, and finite-data anchor are scoped
exactly over $\BoolUp$, $\ListUp$, $\NatUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$,
$\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

The scoped packet exposes no host bit-string equality, numeric binary
interpretation, padding convention, machine-word overflow, hashing semantics,
or error-correction metric.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The source rows are the rows of \autoref{def:bitvector-bhist-source}. Carrier
stability, classifier laws, and ledger coverage are fixed by
\autoref{thm:bitvector-carrier-stability},
\autoref{thm:bitvector-classifier-laws}, and
\autoref{thm:bitvector-ledger-coverage}.

The accepted certificate surface is
\autoref{thm:bitvector-namecert-obligation-surface}, and the downstream
finite-data role is \autoref{thm:bitvector-finite-data-anchor}. Together these
results list all consumer projections: length, boolean spine, classifier
rows, transport rows, finite ledger rows, package provenance, and certificate
registration.

Every projection is one of the displayed BEDC rows over $\BoolUp$, $\ListUp$,
$\NatUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, and
$\NameCert$. There is no coordinate for host bit-string equality, binary
numeric interpretation, padding, overflow, hashing, or an error-correction
metric, so none can be exported by the scoped package.
\end{proof}
