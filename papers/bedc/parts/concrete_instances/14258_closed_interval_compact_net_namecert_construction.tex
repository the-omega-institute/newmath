It consumes the endpoint-cell surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the mesh surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedintervalnet-namecert}, the finite-net interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, the complete-limit interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the finite-window rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is an explicit finite-net compactness certificate for displayed closed intervals; it is not general Heine--Borel compactness, not open-cover compactness, not trichotomy, not quotient real equality, and not a selected subcover choice.

\begin{definition}[ClosedIntervalCompactNet carrier]
\label{def:closed-interval-compact-net-carrier}
A $\ClosedIntervalCompactNetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(I,M,T,B,S,R,E,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ endpoint-cell row naming the displayed closed interval and its lower and upper located endpoints;
\item $M$ is the $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$ mesh row over $I$, including its dyadic cell ledger, center rows, and finite coverage ledger;
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net handoff row obtained from the same mesh and read at the requested positive tolerance;
\item $B$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ boundary row used only to keep regular Cauchy endpoint and center reads inside the displayed metric-completion ledger;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row for the endpoint, center, and cell observations used by the mesh;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the same finite-window observations;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached through $I,M,S,R$ and through no detached real equality coordinate;
\item $L$ is the local compact-net ledger stating that every accepted interval read is covered by one of the displayed mesh cells and its bundled center;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for interval endpoints, mesh rows, total-bounded handoff rows, completion-boundary rows, windows, readbacks, real seals, and the compact-net ledger;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect the interval, mesh, finite net, completion boundary, windows, readbacks, real seal, and compact-net ledger;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClosedIntervalCompactNetUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedIntervalCompactNetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the displayed finite-net compactness route for the located closed interval. It has no coordinate for an arbitrary open family, open-cover compactness, selected finite subcover, trichotomy on real names, quotient real equality, host endpoint choice, or ambient Heine--Borel theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedIntervalCompactNet NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-interval-compact-net-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\ClosedIntervalCompactNetUp$ carrier $K=(I,M,T,B,S,R,E,L,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedIntervalCompactNetUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for those twelve rows, mesh-to-net coverage through $M,T,L$, completion-bounded real-name readback through $B,S,R,E$, componentwise classifier transport through $H$, consumer replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the tuple of \autoref{def:closed-interval-compact-net-carrier}. The interval row, mesh row, finite-net handoff, completion boundary, finite windows, readbacks, real seal, compact-net ledger, transports, continuations, package provenance, and local naming row are all explicit $\BHist$ coordinates.

The coverage side is read through $M,T,L$. The closed bounded interval net supplies the dyadic cells and centers, the totally bounded row packages those centers as the finite net at the requested tolerance, and the local ledger records that interval reads enter one of those displayed cells.

The real-name side is read through $B,S,R,E$. The complete-metric boundary is only a ledger for regular Cauchy reads of the displayed endpoint and center names; the terminal $\RealUp$ seal is reached after the finite windows and regular rational readbacks have already been exposed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for open covers, selected subcovers, trichotomy, quotient real equality, host endpoint choice, or a general Heine--Borel theorem, none of those objects can appear as local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedIntervalCompactNet finite-subcover route]
\label{thm:closed-interval-compact-net-finite-subcover-route}
Every compactness consumer of an accepted $\ClosedIntervalCompactNetUp$ packet receives the displayed finite route
$$
I,M,T,L,H,C,P,N.
$$
The route may be read as a finite cover by mesh cells and bundled centers for the displayed interval, but it is already finite data and is not selected from an arbitrary open cover.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the interval row $I$. The only coverage information attached to it in the carrier is the closed bounded interval net $M$ and the compact-net ledger $L$.

Project the total-bounded handoff $T$. It repacks the mesh cells and centers of $M$ as the finite-net row consumed by compactness-facing clients, with the requested tolerance still visible in the displayed ledger.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ then transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Because the carrier contains no arbitrary open family and no selected subcover coordinate, a consumer cannot reinterpret this finite net as a choice from an open cover.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedIntervalCompactNet totally bounded handoff]
\label{thm:closed-interval-compact-net-totally-bounded-handoff}
For every accepted $\ClosedIntervalCompactNetUp$ carrier $K=(I,M,T,B,S,R,E,L,H,C,P,N)$, the row $T$ is the only $\TotallyBoundedUp$ handoff exported by the packet. It is determined by the located interval row $I$, the closed bounded interval net row $M$, the compact-net ledger $L$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and isolate the total-bounded coordinate. The packet contains a single row $T$ for the finite-net handoff, and it appears downstream of the interval row $I$ and mesh row $M$.

The compact-net ledger $L$ fixes the coverage read used by $T$. Thus the totally bounded export is the mesh-derived finite-net row, not a separate ambient total-boundedness principle.

Transport and replay are supplied only by $H$ and $C$, with provenance and naming supplied by $P$ and $N$. No additional $\TotallyBoundedUp$ coordinate or hidden center selector is present.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedIntervalCompactNet complete metric handoff]
\label{thm:closed-interval-compact-net-complete-metric-handoff}
Every completion-facing or real-seal-facing consumer of an accepted $\ClosedIntervalCompactNetUp$ packet factors through
$$
B,S,R,E,H,C,P,N
$$
after the interval and mesh rows $I,M$ have been displayed. The handoff uses $\CompleteMetricUp$ only as the visible regular Cauchy readback boundary for endpoint and center observations; it does not assert ambient real completeness, quotient real equality, or a selected real endpoint.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the displayed interval and mesh. Endpoint and center observations are already fixed by $I$ and $M$ before any completion-facing read is available.

Read the finite-window and regular-rational rows next. The rows $S$ and $R$ expose the endpoint and center names through $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ data, and the complete-metric boundary row $B$ keeps those reads inside the displayed regular Cauchy ledger.

Finally the real seal $E$ and structural rows $H,C,P,N$ carry that same route to consumers. Since the carrier has no quotient real equality coordinate, selected endpoint coordinate, or ambient completeness coordinate, the completion-facing handoff cannot export those principles.
\end{proof}
