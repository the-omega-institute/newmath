\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DiffGaloisUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-diffgalois-namecert}

The differential-Galois interface $\DiffGaloisUp$ packages the Picard-Vessiot Galois group of a linear differential equation over a $\NumFieldUp$-base differential field. It depends on $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fieldext-namecert}), $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-galoisgroup-namecert}), $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ode-namecert}), and $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-liegroup-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:diffgalois-carrier}

A $\DiffGaloisUp$ token carries: a differential field $(K,\partial)$ over a $\NumFieldUp$ subfield, a linear differential operator $L=\sum a_i\partial^i$ with coefficients in $K$, a $\SeqUp$-typed solution-space row $V$ of dimension equal to the order of $L$, and the differential-Galois group $G$ acting on $V$ that fixes $K^\partial$ pointwise. The carrier accepts a Picard-Vessiot certificate when the solution space exists, the differential-Galois group $G$ acts faithfully on $V$, and the constants $K^\partial$ are exactly the $G$-fixed elements. The classifier is $\hsame$ on the solution-space rows; the ledger keeps the differential operator, the solution-space row, the Galois action, and the constant-field witness visible.

\begin{theorem}[DiffGalois Picard-Vessiot obligation]
\label{thm:diffgalois-picard-vessiot-obligation}
An accepted $\DiffGaloisUp$ Picard-Vessiot row exposes the solution-space,
differential-operator, and fundamental-solution rows as finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ data over $\SeqUp$, $\FieldExtUp$, and $\OdeUp$. The
differential field $(K,\partial)$ is read through the displayed
$\FieldExtUp$ row, the coefficient rows of the linear operator
$L=\sum a_i\partial^i$ are read through the displayed $\OdeUp$ row, and the
solution-space basis and fundamental-solution matrix are read through the
$\SeqUp$ row.

The obligation is local to the displayed Picard-Vessiot ledger. It does not
assert algebraic-group classification, Liouvillian solvability, or an
integration-by-quadratures bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to its differential-field and operator
coordinates. These rows provide $(K,\partial)$ and the coefficient list of
$L$ as finite source data.

Next read the solution-space coordinate. The $\SeqUp$ basis row and the
fundamental-solution row are the only witnesses consumed by the carrier, and
the classifier compares solution-space histories by the fixed $\hsame$ row.

Repacking these coordinates gives the stated obligation surface. No step adds
an algebraic-group classification row, a Liouvillian criterion, or an external
analytic solution method.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiffGaloisUp.DiffGaloisPicardVessiotPacket\_solution\_space\_obligation}

\begin{theorem}[DiffGalois faithful action and constant-field exactness]
\label{thm:diffgalois-faithful-action-constant-field-exactness}
For an accepted $\DiffGaloisUp$ packet, the displayed
$\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ action row is faithful on the visible
solution-space basis and transports along the fixed solution-space classifier.
Two action histories that agree on every displayed basis row are identified by
the same $\hsame$ solution-space classifier. The constant-field row is exactly
the fixed-point ledger for this action: a displayed row is accepted as a
constant precisely when the ledger records it as fixed, and every displayed
fixed row is read back through the same $K^\partial$ coordinate.

This obligation is a finite action-and-fixed-row statement. It does not
classify differential-Galois groups, assert Liouvillian solvability, or supply
a standard model-theoretic bridge.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiffGaloisUp.DiffGaloisFaithfulActionLedger\_basis\_fold\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Start from the action coordinate of the accepted packet. It is already typed
as a $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ row acting on the visible solution-space
basis.

Faithfulness is read at the displayed basis rows: if two action histories have
the same effect on each basis coordinate, the ledger identifies their action
rows through the fixed classifier. Transport along $\hsame$ changes only the
solution-space presentation and keeps the action row attached to the same
basis surface.

Now read the constant-field coordinate from the same packet. A displayed
constant row is fixed by the action ledger, and a displayed fixed row is read
back through $K^\partial$, so exactness is an $\hsame$ transport comparison
between two visible projections of the same Picard-Vessiot data.

Thus the obligation is witnessed by finite projection from the accepted
Picard-Vessiot packet rather than by a classification theorem for the ambient
group.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiffGalois public certificate export]
\label{thm:diffgalois-public-certificate-export}
The Picard-Vessiot obligation and the faithful-action constant-field exactness
obligation repack into the public $\NameCert_{\DiffGaloisUp}$ boundary. The
exported certificate exposes the $\FieldExtUp$ differential-field row, the
$\OdeUp$ operator row, the $\SeqUp$ solution-space and fundamental-solution
rows, the faithful $\GaloisGroupUp$ action row, and the constant-field
fixed-row ledger. It excludes Liouvillian solvability, algebraic-group
classification, integration-by-quadratures, and standard model-theoretic
bridge claims.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiffGaloisUp.DiffGaloisPublicCertificate\_export}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:diffgalois-picard-vessiot-obligation}. This supplies the
differential-field, operator, solution-space, and fundamental-solution rows as
the visible Picard-Vessiot ledger.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:diffgalois-faithful-action-constant-field-exactness}. This adds
the faithful action row and identifies the constant-field coordinate with the
fixed-row ledger by $\hsame$ transport.

Repacking these rows gives the public $\NameCert_{\DiffGaloisUp}$ boundary.
No field of the export supplies a Liouvillian criterion, an algebraic-group
classification theorem, integration-by-quadratures, or a standard
model-theoretic bridge.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiffGalois scoped certificate surface]
\label{thm:diffgalois-scoped-certificate-surface}
The Picard-Vessiot packet, faithful action ledger, constant-field fixed-point
exactness, solution-space row, and displayed Galois action rows form the closed
scoped $\DiffGaloisUp$ certificate surface over $\FieldExtUp$,
$\GaloisGroupUp$, $\OdeUp$, $\SeqUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and
$\Pkg$. Its carrier is the displayed differential-field, operator,
solution-space, fundamental-solution, action, and constant-field ledger; its
classifier is $\hsame$ on the solution-space row together with transported
Galois action rows; and its visible ledger is exactly the public
$\NameCert_{\DiffGaloisUp}$ boundary of
\autoref{thm:diffgalois-public-certificate-export}.

This scoped surface closes only the finite Picard-Vessiot certificate rows. It
does not add algebraic-group classification, Liouvillian solvability,
integration-by-quadratures, or a standard model-theoretic differential-Galois
bridge.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DiffGaloisUp.DiffGaloisPicardVessiotPacket\_scoped\_certificate\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:diffgalois-picard-vessiot-obligation}. It supplies the
differential-field row, operator row, solution-space row, and
fundamental-solution row as finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ data over the displayed
$\FieldExtUp$, $\OdeUp$, and $\SeqUp$ sources.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:diffgalois-faithful-action-constant-field-exactness}. This adds
the faithful $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ action ledger, transports it through
the solution-space classifier, and identifies the constant-field coordinate
with the fixed-point ledger.

Finally use \autoref{thm:diffgalois-public-certificate-export}. It repacks the
Picard-Vessiot rows and faithful-action rows into the public
$\NameCert_{\DiffGaloisUp}$ boundary. Reading that export as a scoped
carrier/classifier/ledger package gives the stated surface over
$\FieldExtUp$, $\GaloisGroupUp$, $\OdeUp$, $\SeqUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$, with no additional classification, solvability,
quadrature, or model-theoretic bridge row.
\end{proof}
