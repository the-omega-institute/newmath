\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DiffGaloisUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-diffgalois-namecert}

The differential-Galois interface $\DiffGaloisUp$ packages the Picard-Vessiot Galois group of a linear differential equation over a $\NumFieldUp$-base differential field. It depends on $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fieldext-namecert}), $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-galoisgroup-namecert}), $\NameCert_{\OdeUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ode-namecert}), and $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-liegroup-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:diffgalois-carrier}

A $\DiffGaloisUp$ token carries: a differential field $(K,\partial)$ over a $\NumFieldUp$ subfield, a linear differential operator $L=\sum a_i\partial^i$ with coefficients in $K$, a $\SeqUp$-typed solution-space row $V$ of dimension equal to the order of $L$, and the differential-Galois group $G$ acting on $V$ that fixes $K^\partial$ pointwise. The carrier accepts a Picard-Vessiot certificate when the solution space exists, the differential-Galois group $G$ acts faithfully on $V$, and the constants $K^\partial$ are exactly the $G$-fixed elements. The classifier is $\hsame$ on the solution-space rows; the ledger keeps the differential operator, the solution-space row, the Galois action, and the constant-field witness visible.

\begin{theorem}[DiffGalois solution-space obligation]
\label{thm:diffgalois-solution-space-obligation}
An accepted $\DiffGaloisUp$ Picard-Vessiot row exposes the differential field
$(K,\partial)$, the coefficient rows of the displayed linear operator
$L=\sum a_i\partial^i$, a $\SeqUp$ solution-space basis, a
fundamental-solution row, and the fixed $\hsame$ solution-space classifier as
finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ data. The carrier reads only these visible rows when
it certifies solution-space admissibility.

The obligation is local to the displayed Picard-Vessiot ledger. It does not
assert algebraic-group classification, Liouvillian solvability, or an
integration-by-quadratures bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to its differential-field and operator
coordinates. These rows provide $(K,\partial)$ and the coefficient list of
$L$ as finite source data.

Next read the solution-space coordinate. The $\SeqUp$ basis row and the
fundamental-solution row are the only witnesses consumed by the carrier, and
the classifier compares solution-space histories by the fixed $\hsame$ row.

Repacking these coordinates gives the stated obligation surface. No step adds
an algebraic-group classification row, a Liouvillian criterion, or an external
analytic solution method.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiffGalois faithful-action obligation]
\label{thm:diffgalois-faithful-action-obligation}
For an accepted $\DiffGaloisUp$ packet, the displayed
$\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ action row is faithful on the visible
solution-space basis and transports along the fixed solution-space classifier.
Two action histories that agree on every displayed basis row are identified by
the same $\hsame$ solution-space classifier, and the ledger records the action
only through the Picard-Vessiot basis already exposed by the carrier.

This obligation is a finite action-surface statement. It does not classify
differential-Galois groups or supply a standard model-theoretic bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the action coordinate of the accepted packet. It is already typed
as a $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ row acting on the visible solution-space
basis.

Faithfulness is read at the displayed basis rows: if two action histories have
the same effect on each basis coordinate, the ledger identifies their action
rows through the fixed classifier. Transport along $\hsame$ changes only the
solution-space presentation and keeps the action row attached to the same
basis surface.

Thus the obligation is witnessed by finite projection from the accepted
Picard-Vessiot packet rather than by a classification theorem for the ambient
group.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DiffGalois constant-field exactness obligation]
\label{thm:diffgalois-constant-field-exactness}
In an accepted $\DiffGaloisUp$ packet, the constant-field row is exactly the
displayed fixed-point ledger for the $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ action on
the visible solution-space basis. A row is accepted as a constant precisely
when the ledger records it as fixed by the displayed action, and every
displayed fixed row is read back through the same $K^\partial$ coordinate.

The statement closes only this constant-field fixed-point ledger. It does not
assert algebraic-group classification, Liouvillian solvability, or an
integration-by-quadratures bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the constant-field coordinate and the action coordinate from the same
accepted packet. Both are finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows attached to the visible
solution-space basis.

For a displayed constant row, the ledger records that the action fixes it.
Conversely, a displayed fixed row is read back through the constant-field
coordinate, so exactness identifies the two rows inside the packet.

The proof therefore compares two visible ledger projections. It never invokes
classification of algebraic groups, Liouvillian theory, or an external
differential-algebra bridge.
\end{proof}
