\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ArchimedeanOrderedFieldUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-archimedeanorderedfield-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\ArchimedeanOrderedFieldUp$ packet records a traditional ordered-field comparison certificate over displayed constructive real names. It sits between the algebra/order surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-alg-order-namecert}, the rational-bound route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-archimedeanreal-namecert}, the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the rational source rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}. The packet is a comparison carrier for downstream L10 and real-algebra consumers, not an ambient ordered field object.

\begin{definition}[ArchimedeanOrderedField carrier]
\label{def:archimedean-ordered-field-carrier}
An $\ArchimedeanOrderedFieldUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
F=(R,A,Q,O,B,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the family of $\RealUp$ endpoint rows whose algebra and order reads are being compared;
\item $A$ is the $\RealAlgOrderUp$ row that supplies displayed addition, negation, multiplication, absolute-value, nonnegative-order, and apartness evidence for those endpoints;
\item $Q$ is the finite $\RatUp$ source family used by constant-rational witnesses and Archimedean comparison bounds;
\item $O$ is the $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ rational-bound row attached to the displayed real endpoints;
\item $B$ is the local comparison ledger recording the field-operation, order, rational-embedding, and Archimedean-bound reads that the packet exports;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the real endpoints, algebra/order rows, rational source rows, Archimedean bound rows, and local ledger rows;
\item $C$ stores the $\Cont$ routes that expose the displayed operation, order, rational-bound, and rational-embedding rows to downstream consumers;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealUp$, $\RealAlgOrderUp$, $\ArchimedeanRealUp$, $\RatUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanOrderedFieldUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two accepted packets only by replaying these finite coordinates. It has no coordinate for real trichotomy, quotient real equality, a host ordered-field carrier, a quotient-reals construction, a selected least bound, or a standard bridge theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanOrderedField NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:archimedean-ordered-field-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanOrderedFieldUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:archimedean-ordered-field-carrier} consists of five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly the displayed packet $F=(R,A,Q,O,B,H,C,P,N)$ with real endpoint, algebra/order, rational source, Archimedean bound, comparison ledger, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming coordinates;
\item classifier transport: classification transports every displayed $\RealUp$ endpoint, $\RealAlgOrderUp$ operation or order row, $\RatUp$ source row, $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ bound row, and local ledger row componentwise through the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes;
\item field-row stability: addition, negation, multiplication, unit, zero, and absolute-value reads remain inside the $\RealAlgOrderUp$ coordinate $A$ after endpoint replacement by classified $\RealUp$ rows;
\item Archimedean comparison stability: every rational-bound or rational-embedding comparison exposed by $O$ and $Q$ remains tied to the same displayed real endpoints and the same local ledger rows after transport;
\item ledger exactness: every downstream ordered-field or Archimedean read is one of the displayed rows $R,A,Q,O,B,H,C,P,N$ before any consumer receives it.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ArchimedeanOrderedFieldUp.ArchimedeanOrderedFieldTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:archimedean-ordered-field-carrier}. The real endpoint family, algebra/order row, rational source family, Archimedean bound row, local comparison ledger, transport table, continuation table, provenance row, and local naming row are exactly the listed coordinates.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The $\RealUp$ endpoints move with their $\RealAlgOrderUp$ operation and order rows, while the rational source rows and Archimedean bound rows move through their own displayed sibling classifiers and the stored $\hsame$ routes.

The field-row and Archimedean stability clauses then repack the transported rows into the same ledger shape $B$. Algebraic reads use only $A$ and the endpoint rows in $R$; rational-bound reads use only $Q,O$ and the endpoint rows in $R$.

Ledger exactness follows because every public read is a coordinate of $F$ or a row of $B$ generated from those coordinates. Since no coordinate stores trichotomy, quotient real equality, a host ordered-field carrier, quotient reals, selected least bounds, or bridge-checked data, none of those principles is exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanOrderedField RealAlgOrder handoff]
\label{thm:archimedean-ordered-field-realalgorder-handoff}
Every ordered-field or Archimedean comparison read exported by an accepted $\ArchimedeanOrderedFieldUp$ packet factors through the $\RealAlgOrderUp$ row $A$ and the $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ row $O$ over the displayed $\RealUp$ endpoints $R$. The $\RealAlgOrderUp$ side supplies the finite operation and order rows, the $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ side supplies explicit rational-bound rows, and the $\RatUp$ coordinate $Q$ supplies the rational source data used by both comparison routes.

Consequently the packet is a handoff between the existing real algebra/order and Archimedean-bound surfaces. It does not identify those surfaces with a host ordered field, quotient-reals carrier, complete real field, trichotomy principle, selected least-bound operation, or bridge-checked standard structure.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier tuple $F=(R,A,Q,O,B,H,C,P,N)$. The only operation and order coordinate is $A$, and the only Archimedean-bound coordinate is $O$.

Read the algebra/order route first. By \autoref{thm:real-alg-order-namecert-obligations}, the rows exposed by $A$ are finite endpoint, operation, order, apartness, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows over displayed $\RealUp$ data.

Read the Archimedean route next. By \autoref{thm:archimedean-real-namecert-obligations}, the rows exposed by $O$ are finite rational-bound, dyadic-bound, stream-window, regular-Cauchy handoff, ledger, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows for displayed $\RealUp$ data.

The rational source coordinate $Q$ is shared by the two routes. The local ledger $B$ records only those field-operation, order, rational-embedding, and rational-bound reads, and the transports $H,C$ replay them componentwise for consumers.

Thus every exported comparison factors through $A$, $O$, and $Q$ over $R$. The excluded host ordered-field, quotient-reals, trichotomy, completeness, least-bound, and bridge-checked objects have no carrier coordinate and no route through the local ledger.
\end{proof}
