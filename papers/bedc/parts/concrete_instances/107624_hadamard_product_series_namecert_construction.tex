\label{def:hadamard-product-series-carrier}

$$

\item $W$ is the synchronized $\StreamNameUp$ window row selecting matching
coefficient positions from $A$ and $B$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ coefficient-product ledger; for every

\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row repacking the coefficientwise

\begin{theorem}[Hadamard product Cauchy-product handoff]
\label{thm:hadamard-product-series-cauchyproduct-handoff}

$\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\HadamardProductSeriesUp$ carrier must

$$

\longmapsto E
$$

handoff therefore supplies coefficientwise-product data as synchronized

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a consumer request for product-series data. The two source streams
are readable only as $A$ and $B$, and the matching of coefficient positions is
stored only in $W$.

The product coefficient cannot be read before the dyadic ledger $D$ is
displayed. Its regular-rational readback is $R$, and the derived real-series
row $S$ is assembled only from those finite product windows.

The convergence-control row $M$ is the unique handoff to the named
$\CauchyProductUp$ or $\WeierstrassMTestUp$ surface, and $E$ is downstream of
that handoff. Since $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same route and no additional
analytic coordinate is present, every public consumer factors through the
displayed finite coefficientwise-product path.
\end{proof}
