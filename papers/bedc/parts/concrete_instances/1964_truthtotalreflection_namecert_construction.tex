\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TruthTotalReflectionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-truthtotalreflection-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\TruthTotalReflectionUp$ interface records the finite boundary certificate associated with the truth-total reflection obstruction of \autoref{def:truth-total-reflection-certificate} and \autoref{thm:reflection-boundary-exact}. It packages sentence-code rows for $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$, attempted reflection rows, diagonal obstruction rows, transport data, package provenance, and a local naming row. The truth-total reflection packet reads its extension-attempt and refusal boundary against the accepted and refused declaration surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelacceptancewitness-namecert}, while adding the diagonal obstruction row that prevents a total internal truth classifier. The packet is a boundary witness: it records why a truth-total classifier cannot be exported as an internal BEDC object.

\begin{definition}[Truth-total reflection NameCert carrier]
\label{def:truth-total-reflection-namecert-carrier}
A $\TruthTotalReflectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(S,A,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is a finite row of $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentence codes, including the code rows inspected by a candidate reflection consumer;
\item $A$ is the finite extension-attempt row recording the proposed $\Ext_T$ classifier reads against the sentence-code row $S$;
\item $D$ is the diagonal obstruction row displaying the code for the sentence that reads the negation of its own attempted $\Ext_T$ classification;
\item $H$ stores the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the sentence-code, extension-attempt, and diagonal rows;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads those rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentence coding, diagonal formation, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TruthTotalReflectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier relates two such packets exactly when their displayed sentence-code, extension-attempt, diagonal, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows compare componentwise through $H$ and $C$. It has no coordinate for a BEDC-internal truth predicate, a total truth classifier, or a metalanguage able to validate BEDC from inside BEDC.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Truth-total reflection boundary certificate]
\label{thm:truth-total-reflection-namecert-boundary}
Every accepted $\TruthTotalReflectionUp$ packet records only the finite boundary evidence for truth-total reflection over $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentence codes. Its public surface consists of the rows $S,A,D,H,C,P,N$ of \autoref{def:truth-total-reflection-namecert-carrier}; therefore any consumer can read the attempted extension classifier and the diagonal obstruction through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes, but cannot export an internal BEDC truth classifier for all BEDC sentence codes.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TruthTotalReflectionUp.TruthTotalReflectionUp\_taste\_gate\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to the tuple $T=(S,A,D,H,C,P,N)$. The sentence-code row $S$ supplies the finite $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ codes under inspection, and the extension-attempt row $A$ records only the proposed classifier reads attached to those codes.

The diagonal row $D$ is read through the same finite boundary. It records the obstruction used in \autoref{thm:reflection-boundary-exact}: any truth-total reading of the attempted classifier can be turned against its own diagonal code.

Transport and consumer access are exhausted by $H$ and $C$. These rows move and read the displayed sentence-code, extension-attempt, and diagonal coordinates, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming.

No remaining coordinate stores a BEDC-internal truth predicate or a total classifier over all represented BEDC sentences. Thus the accepted packet is a finite boundary certificate, not an internal truth-total reflection object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Truth-total reflection attempt-row stability]
\label{thm:truth-total-reflection-attempt-row-stability}
For every accepted $\TruthTotalReflectionUp$ packet
$$
T=(S,A,D,H,C,P,N),
$$
componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$ preserves the sentence-code row $S$, the extension-attempt row $A$, and the diagonal row $D$ as rows of the same finite boundary certificate. The transport may replay the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$, but it does not add a coordinate for a BEDC-internal truth predicate, a total truth classifier, or host validation of BEDC.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet to its displayed rows. The transport row $H$ is part of the carrier inventory and is specified only for the sentence-code, extension-attempt, diagonal, route, provenance, and naming coordinates.

Transporting $S,A,D$ therefore keeps the same finite row classes and merely changes their presented history representatives. The continuation row $C$ can replay reads of those transported rows, but it has no source for a classifier outside the packet.

Since the carrier definition contains no coordinate for internal truth, total classification, or host validation, componentwise transport cannot create one. The stabilized surface remains the finite attempted-reflection boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Truth-total reflection diagonal obstruction exactness]
\label{thm:truth-total-reflection-diagonal-obstruction-exactness}
Every read of the diagonal row $D$ in an accepted $\TruthTotalReflectionUp$ packet factors through the displayed route
$$
S,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
The obstruction is exact for the finite attempted reflection row $A$: it blocks only the displayed attempt to classify the sentence codes in $S$, and it does not assert a global theorem about all possible BEDC truth predicates or all host interpretations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $D$ from the carrier only after the sentence-code row $S$ and the extension-attempt row $A$ have been fixed. The diagonal row is the obstruction attached to that displayed attempt.

The rows $H$ and $C$ give the only transport and consumer routes by which the diagonal row can be moved or inspected. The provenance and naming rows $P,N$ record the package in which that read occurs.

Thus a consumer reaches $D$ through the route $S,A,H,C,P,N$. Because no other classifier row is present, the obstruction applies exactly to the finite attempt recorded by $A$ and carries no broader host-level or total-truth conclusion.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Truth-total reflection consumer surface exhaustion]
\label{thm:truth-total-reflection-consumer-surface-exhaustion}
Every consumer of an accepted $\TruthTotalReflectionUp$ packet factors through
$$
S,A,D,H,C,P,N.
$$
Such a consumer may inspect the sentence-code row, the attempted extension-classifier row, the diagonal obstruction row, componentwise transport, displayed continuation reads, provenance, and local naming. It cannot export BEDC-internal truth, total truth classification, host validation, or any classifier not recorded in $A$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier inventory lists exactly the rows $S,A,D,H,C,P,N$. Any accepted consumer read must therefore begin by projecting one of those coordinates and then using the displayed transport and continuation data.

The boundary theorem identifies $A$ as the only attempted classifier row and $D$ as the corresponding diagonal obstruction row. The stability theorem keeps these rows fixed under $\hsame$ transport, and the exactness theorem confines diagonal reads to the displayed attempt.

No remaining coordinate stores internal truth, total classification, host validation, or another classifier. Hence the consumer surface is exhausted by the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}
