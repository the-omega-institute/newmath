\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteMetricCoverUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finite-metric-cover-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive compact metric spaces and finite covers, the $\FiniteMetricCoverUp$ packet records a finite family of metric balls together with the displayed radius and coverage witnesses needed by compactness consumers. It sits between the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the compact source rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the finite-net coverage surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, the closed-ball boundary rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricclosedball-namecert}, and the real-radius seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names finite cover data only; it does not assert open-cover compactness, choice of subcovers, quotient point equality, host compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.

\begin{definition}[FiniteMetricCover carrier]
\label{def:finite-metric-cover-carrier}
A $\FiniteMetricCoverUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(M,K,T,B,R,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ source row carrying the point carrier and distance classifier.
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row whose compactness consumers read the finite cover through this packet.
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net row that supplies center admission and tolerance scheduling.
\item $B$ is the finite $\ClosedMetricBallUp$ row family, with one displayed center and positive radius ledger for each cover cell.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ or dyadic-real radius seal row attached to the ball radii and requested tolerance.
\item $G$ is the finite cover membership bundle, naming the displayed cells and their coverage witnesses as a closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,K,T,B,R,G$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the metric source through the finite cover bundle to downstream compactness consumers.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteMetricCoverUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\ClosedMetricBallUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteMetricCoverUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for arbitrary open covers, selected subcovers outside $G$, quotient point equality, host compactness, or ambient real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteMetricCover NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-metric-cover-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteMetricCoverUp$ carrier
$$
F=(M,K,T,B,R,G,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteMetricCoverUp}$ surface consists exactly of metric source admission, compact-metric source exposure, total-bounded center scheduling, closed metric ball boundary rows, positive radius sealing, finite membership-bundle coverage, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-metric-cover-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes only the metric source, compact source, total-bounded finite-net schedule, closed ball family, radius seal, finite membership bundle, and structural BEDC rows.

The ball family $B$ is read together with the radius row $R$. Thus each cover cell has a displayed center, a displayed radius, and a closed metric boundary row before it can be consumed by compactness-facing routes.

The membership bundle $G$ supplies the finite coverage witnesses. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same displayed route, so no arbitrary open cover, detached selected subcover, quotient equality, host compactness, or ambient completeness coordinate is introduced.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteMetricCover refinement stability]
\label{thm:finite-metric-cover-refinement-stability}
Let
$$
F=(M,K,T,B,R,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
and
$$
F'=(M',K',T',B',R',G',H',C',P',N')
$$
be accepted $\FiniteMetricCoverUp$ carriers. If componentwise $\hsame$ transport identifies $M,K,T,B,R,G$ with $M',K',T',B',R',G'$ and the second cover bundle refines the first by displayed membership witnesses, then every $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\ClosedMetricBallUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer of $F'$ factors through the same finite cover route as a consumer of $F$.

In particular, refinement may split cells and decrease displayed radii, but it cannot add a host open-cover object, choice-selected subcover, quotient point equality, ambient compactness theorem, or detached real-completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the two accepted carriers and read their common transported rows. The $\hsame$ hypotheses identify the metric source, compact source, total-bounded schedule, closed-ball family, radius seal, and finite membership bundle before any consumer reads a refinement.

The displayed refinement witnesses attach each cell of $G'$ to a cell of $G$. A consumer of the refined packet therefore reaches metric and compact data through $M,K,T$, reads ball boundaries through $B'$ and $R'$, and then returns to the original finite cover route through the displayed membership map into $G$.

Finally use the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ and $H',C',P',N'$ to replay and name the same route. Since both carriers contain only finite bundle data and structural BEDC rows, the refinement cannot export any excluded open-cover, choice, quotient, host-compactness, or real-completeness coordinate.
\end{proof}
