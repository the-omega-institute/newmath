\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp$ packet records the universal property of separated reflection as finite BEDC NameCert data. It reads the separated completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedcompletion-namecert}, the separated metric classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, and the Hausdorff reflection boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorff-reflection-namecert}. The packet is a universal-property surface for factorization through separated targets; it does not introduce quotient objects, choice-selected representatives, host equality of reflected points, or ambient classical Hausdorff completion.

\begin{definition}[Separated reflection universal carrier]
\label{def:separated-reflection-universal-carrier}
A $\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(S,M,H,F,Q,T,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source premetric or uniform source row whose indistinguishability classifier is being reflected.
\item $M$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ classifier row exposing the separated target comparison used by the reflection.
\item $H$ is the $\HausdorffReflectionUp$ row recording the Hausdorff boundary through which reflected equality is read.
\item $F$ is the factorization row: every displayed map from $S$ into a separated target factors through the reflected classifier row.
\item $Q$ is the uniqueness row, comparing two displayed factorizations only by the separated target classifier supplied by $M$ and $H$.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source, classifier, Hausdorff boundary, factorization, uniqueness, provenance, replay, and naming rows.
\item $R$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a separated-target request through $S,M,H,F,Q,T$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp$, $\SeparatedCompletionUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\HausdorffReflectionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,M,H,F,Q,T,R,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient carriers, selected representatives, host point equality, countable choice, hidden separated completions, or ambient Hausdorff replacement.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Separated reflection universal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:separated-reflection-universal-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp$ carrier
$$
U=(S,M,H,F,Q,T,R,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp}$ surface consists exactly of source admission, separated classifier exposure, Hausdorff boundary readback, displayed factorization, uniqueness by separated comparison, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, provenance, and local naming through $S,M,H,F,Q,T,R,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:separated-reflection-universal-carrier}. The rows $S,M,H,F,Q,T,R,P,N$ are the entire public packet.

Read $S,M,H$ first. The source row supplies the pre-reflection indistinguishability data, while the separated metric and Hausdorff rows expose the target comparison used by every separated read.

The factorization and uniqueness rows are then forced through $F$ and $Q$. Transport, replay, provenance, and naming preserve that same route through $T,R,P,N$.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient carriers, selected representatives, host equality, choice, hidden separated completions, or ambient Hausdorff replacement, those objects cannot appear in the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separated reflection universal factorization]
\label{thm:separated-reflection-universal-factorization}
Let $U=(S,M,H,F,Q,T,R,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp$ carrier. Every displayed map from the source row $S$ into a separated target whose comparison is read through $M$ and $H$ factors through the row $F$, and every consumer of the factorization must read the route
$$
S,M,H\longmapsto F
$$
before it can inspect transport, replay, provenance, or local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the NameCert obligation theorem, \autoref{thm:separated-reflection-universal-namecert-obligations}. It identifies $S,M,H,F$ as the whole factorization-facing prefix of the packet.

The source row is read before the separated target classifier, and the Hausdorff boundary is read before any reflected equality is consumed. The factorization row $F$ therefore has no independent source of map data beyond $S,M,H$.

The structural rows $T,R,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name that route. They cannot add a second factorization object or a choice-selected representative.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separated reflection universal uniqueness]
\label{thm:separated-reflection-universal-uniqueness}
For every accepted $\SeparatedReflectionUniversalUp$ carrier $U=(S,M,H,F,Q,T,R,P,N)$, two displayed factorizations out of $S$ are publicly equal only through the uniqueness row $Q$ over the separated comparison supplied by $M$ and $H$. The uniqueness read exports no quotient equality, host point equality, selected representative equality, choice principle, or hidden completion equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the factorization theorem, \autoref{thm:separated-reflection-universal-factorization}. It places both displayed factorizations on the same $S,M,H\mapsto F$ route.

Now read the uniqueness row $Q$. Its classifier is the separated target comparison supplied by $M$ and $H$, so the equality of factorization witnesses is a displayed separated comparison, not a host equality claim.

Finally use the absence clauses in \autoref{def:separated-reflection-universal-carrier}. No row carries quotient equality, selected representatives, choice, or hidden completion equality, so the stated separated uniqueness route exhausts the public claim.
\end{proof}
