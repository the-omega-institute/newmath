\begin{definition}[Observation-logic boundary carrier]
\label{def:observation-logic-boundary-carrier}
An $\ObservationLogicBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(O,L,M,K,G,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the subject-free observation row, recording observation as displayed $\Cont$ replay over $\BHist$ and $\hsame$ rather than as a hidden observer substance.
\item $L$ is the proof-discipline row, recording constructive witness formation, local refutation, and refusal of unwitnessed positive supply.
\item $M$ is the open-meta-loop row, refusing total truth, unrestricted choice, quotient collapse, global proof search, halting closure, and self-validation of the certificate surface.
\item $K$ is the phase-refusal row, recording whether the attempted proof-boundary export is in phase, blocked by a dependency, or off-table relative to the displayed programme surface.
\item $G$ is the gap/failure/bridge audit row, tying typed gaps, failure branches, bridge evidence, no-host-leak evidence, and axis separation to the same boundary read.
\item $R$ is the finite observation residue row, recording hidden-residue and failure-surface exposure for finite observation exports.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for observation, proof discipline, open meta-loop refusal, phase refusal, gap audit, residue, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from observation through proof-discipline inspection, open-meta-loop refusal, phase classification, audit routing, residue exposure, provenance, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SubjectFreeObservationUp$, $\LogicContradictionMetaLoopUp$, $\KernelPhaseRefusalUp$, $\GapFailureBridgeAuditUp$, $\FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObservationLogicBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares carriers only through $O,L,M,K,G,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a subject-substance, a total truth predicate, an unrestricted choice supplier, quotient equality elimination, a halting oracle, a closed meta-loop classifier, or a proof-boundary export detached from finite observation residue.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observation-logic boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observation-logic-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observation-logic-boundary-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObservationLogicBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of subject-free observation through $O$, proof-discipline exposure through $L$, open-meta-loop refusal through $M$, phase-refusal classification through $K$, gap/failure/bridge audit through $G$, finite observation residue through $R$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $B=(O,L,M,K,G,R,H,C,P,N)$. The tuple exposes observation, proof discipline, meta-loop refusal, phase refusal, gap audit, residue, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming as distinct rows.

Use \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-subject-free-observation-namecert} for $O$ and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-logiccontradictionmetaloop-namecert} for $L,M$. The first supplies the observation row without a subject-substance coordinate; the second supplies constructive proof discipline and open meta-loop refusal without total truth, choice, quotient collapse, or halting closure.

Use \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelphaserefusal-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-gap-failure-bridge-audit-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-observation-residue-boundary-namecert} for $K,G,R$. These rows record the phase boundary, the gap/failure/bridge audit surface, and the finite residue surface that must remain visible before a proof-boundary reading is exported.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since the carrier contains no further coordinate, the displayed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observation-logic boundary axis separation]
\label{thm:observation-logic-boundary-axis-separation}
An accepted $\ObservationLogicBoundaryUp$ packet keeps observation, proof discipline, open meta-loop refusal, phase refusal, gap/failure/bridge audit, and finite observation residue as separate classifier axes. No accepted consumer route may read observation as proof, proof discipline as total truth, bridge status as theory closure, phase refusal as blocker discharge, or finite residue as closed physical audit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:observation-logic-boundary-carrier}. The rows $O,L,M,K,G,R$ are separate coordinates before transport, continuation replay, provenance, or local naming is consulted.

Apply \autoref{thm:observation-logic-boundary-namecert-obligations}. Each axis is admitted through its own row and then preserved by $H,C,P,N$; none of the structural rows computes one axis from another.

The sibling packets enforce the same separation. $\SubjectFreeObservationUp$ refuses a hidden subject, $\LogicContradictionMetaLoopUp$ refuses total meta closure, $\KernelPhaseRefusalUp$ keeps blocker rows visible, $\GapFailureBridgeAuditUp$ separates bridge, failure, and theory axes, and $\FiniteObservationResidueBoundaryUp$ keeps finite residue visible. Thus every listed collapsed reading lies outside the accepted boundary carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observation-logic boundary non-escape]
\label{thm:observation-logic-boundary-nonescape}
Every BEDC-valid proof-boundary reading exported by an accepted $\ObservationLogicBoundaryUp$ packet factors through
$$
O,L,M,K,G,R,H,C,P,N.
$$
If the open-meta-loop row $M$ is deleted, the remaining rows may still describe observation, local proof discipline, phase refusal, audit routing, and finite residue, but they cannot export a BEDC-valid proof-boundary reading.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet. A public proof-boundary read must preserve the observation row, proof-discipline row, open-meta-loop row, phase-refusal row, gap/failure/bridge audit row, finite residue row, transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local name.

The open-meta-loop row is not recoverable from the observation row or the proof-discipline row. Observation exposes a finite $\Cont$ read, and proof discipline exposes constructive and local-refutation behavior; neither refuses total truth, choice, quotient collapse, global proof search, halting closure, or self-validation on its own.

The remaining rows also cannot reconstruct $M$. Phase refusal records target admissibility, the gap/failure/bridge row records audit coordinates, and the residue row records finite observation limits. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve displayed rows, so deleting $M$ prevents export of the named proof-boundary reading.
\end{proof}
