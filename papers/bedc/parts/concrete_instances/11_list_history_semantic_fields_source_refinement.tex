$$

$$
and
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)
$$
hold, then $ys=\mathsf{nil}$. Symmetrically, for a finite BEDC-history spine
$xs$, if $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,xs)$,
$\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(k,\mathsf{nil})$, and

\begin{proof}

$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,\mathsf{nil},ys).
$$

Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness}. The symmetric clause applies
the same alignment theorem to obtain
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,\mathsf{nil})

and then case-analyzes $xs$; the nil branch is immediate, and the cons branch is
the symmetric mixed case ruled out by the same no-confusion lemma and case
exactness theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List represented-spine classifier constructor exactness]
\label{thm:list-represented-spine-classifier-constructor-exactness}
Let $A:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a BEDC history source carrier, let
$\sim_A$ be a source classifier, let $\mathsf{cert}:\NameCert(A,\sim_A)$, and
assume $\mathsf{ListSpineRepCoherent}_{A,\sim_A}$. The ten-field fit is:
traditional target finite-list readback over $\mathsf{BHist}$; BEDC source $A$
with $\NameCert(A,\sim_A)$; carrier $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A$; classifier
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}$; stability from
$\mathsf{cert}$ and spine coherence; ledger finite spines; core theorem
represented-spine classifier constructor exactness; exactness by
displayed-spine classifier exactness; standard bridge finite spines; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$. For histories $h,k,a,b:\mathsf{BHist}$ and finite
BEDC-history spines $xs,ys$, the public classifier has the following
represented-spine constructor behavior:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item if $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{nil})$,
  $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(k,\mathsf{cons}(b,ys))$, and
  $\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$ hold, then $\bot$;
  \item if $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs))$,
  $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(k,\mathsf{nil})$, and
  $\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$ hold, then $\bot$;
  \item if $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(h,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs))$ and
  $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(k,\mathsf{cons}(b,ys))$ hold, then
  $$
  \mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)
  \Longleftrightarrow
  (a\sim_A b)\land \mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the nil-cons clause, apply the forward direction of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-represented-spine-classifier-displayed-exactness} to the
two represented endpoints and the public classifier witness. This gives
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,\mathsf{nil},\mathsf{cons}(b,ys)).
$$
Lemma~\ref{lem:list-classifier-nil-cons-no-confusion} eliminates that mixed
finite-spine classifier. The cons-nil clause uses the same displayed exactness
theorem to obtain
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs),\mathsf{nil}),
$$
and Lemma~\ref{lem:list-classifier-nil-cons-no-confusion} eliminates this mixed
shape as well. For the cons-cons clause, apply the same theorem to obtain
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs),\mathsf{cons}(b,ys)).
$$
The cons-cons branch of Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness} then
projects the source-head classifier $a\sim_A b$ and the recursive tail
classifier $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys)$. Conversely, suppose the
source-head classifier $a\sim_A b$ and the recursive tail classifier
$\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,xs,ys)$ are given. The cons-cons branch of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-classifier-case-exactness} rebuilds
$$
\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim_A,\mathsf{cons}(a,xs),\mathsf{cons}(b,ys)).
$$
With the two displayed representation witnesses, the reverse direction of
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-represented-spine-classifier-displayed-exactness} repacks
this finite-spine classifier as
$\mathsf{ListSpineHistoryClassifier}_{A,\sim_A}(h,k)$.
\end{proof}
