\begin{theorem}[List history semantic fields source refinement]
\label{thm:list-history-semantic-fields-source-refinement}
Let $A,B:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source carriers. The
ten-field fit is: traditional target finite-list source refinement; BEDC source
$\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}$, pattern
$\mathsf{ListHistoryPattern}$, and ledger $\mathsf{ListHistoryLedger}$ as the
three list semantic fields; classifier unused; stability source inclusion;
ledger finite spine; core theorem the three displayed implications below;
exactness endpoint preservation; standard bridge finite spines; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$. If the source carrier of $A$ is included in the source
carrier of $B$,
$$
\forall h:\mathsf{BHist}.\; A(h)\to B(h),
$$
then for every history $h$,
$$
\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_A(h)\to\mathsf{ListHistoryCarrier}_B(h),
$$
$$
\mathsf{ListHistoryPattern}_A(h)\to\mathsf{ListHistoryPattern}_B(h),
$$
and
$$
\mathsf{ListHistoryLedger}_A(h)\to\mathsf{ListHistoryLedger}_B(h).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the displayed generation of the carrier and ledger witnesses. The nil
case reuses the empty endpoint witness. The cons case applies the source
inclusion to the head witness, applies the induction hypothesis to the tail,
and rebuilds the same continuation endpoint. For the pattern field, use the
displayed nil and cons cases: the nil pattern is unchanged, and the cons pattern
uses the source inclusion on the head together with the carrier-refinement
clause for the tail.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[List spine coherence source restriction]
\label{thm:list-spine-coherence-source-restriction}
Let $A,B:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be BEDC history source carriers, and
let $\sim$ be a source classifier. The ten-field fit is: traditional target
finite-list source restriction; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$\mathsf{ListSpineRep}$; classifier $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim)$;
stability source inclusion; ledger finite spine; core theorem coherence
restriction; exactness inherited from ambient spine coherence; standard bridge
finite-spine readback; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$. If the restricted source
is included in the ambient source,
$$
\forall h:\mathsf{BHist}.\; A(h)\to B(h),
$$
and the ambient source has coherent spine presentations,
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineRepCoherent}_{B,\sim},
$$
then the restricted source has coherent spine presentations:
$$
\mathsf{ListSpineRepCoherent}_{A,\sim}.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Take two $A$-spine presentations $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(m,xs)$ and
$\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_A(m,ys)$ of the same history. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:list-source-classifier-weakening} with the displayed source
inclusion and identity classifier weakening
$\forall x\,y:\mathsf{BHist}.\;x\sim y\to x\sim y$. Its spine-representation
clause transports both presentations to $\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_B(m,xs)$ and
$\mathsf{ListSpineRep}_B(m,ys)$. The ambient coherence hypothesis
$\mathsf{ListSpineRepCoherent}_{B,\sim}$ applied to those two transported
presentations yields $\mathsf{ListClassifierSpec}(\sim,xs,ys)$, which is exactly
the coherence datum required for $\mathsf{ListSpineRepCoherent}_{A,\sim}$.
\end{proof}
