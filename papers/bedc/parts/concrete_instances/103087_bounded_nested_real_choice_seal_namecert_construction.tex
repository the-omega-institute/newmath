\begin{definition}[Bounded nested real choice-seal carrier]
\label{def:bounded-nested-real-choice-seal-carrier}
A $\BoundedNestedRealChoiceSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(I,L,C,W,R,D,E,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the bounded nested interval row, exposing the initial bounded source, endpoint names, nested containment ledger, and finite diameter request.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedRealNestedIntervalsUp$ row, attaching located endpoint comparisons and interval-membership reads to the same nested source.
\item $C$ is the $\CantorIntersectionUp$ handoff row, available only after the bounded source and located rows have been displayed.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window family read from the nested interval route, with one visible window for each finite precision request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the displayed windows and their regular rational approximants.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, recording the dyadic endpoint budgets, diameter bounds, and comparison tolerances consumed by $W$ and $R$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $I,L,C,W,R,D$ have been exposed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for bounded source data, located nested data, Cantor handoff, windows, readback, tolerances, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the bounded nested route
$$
I,L,C,D \longmapsto W \longmapsto R \longmapsto E .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BoundedNestedRealChoiceSealUp$, $\CantorIntersectionUp$, $\LocatedRealNestedIntervalsUp$, $\NestedIntervalConvergenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedNestedRealChoiceSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,L,C,W,R,D,E,H,T,P,N$. It has no coordinate for ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, countable choice, a selected host point, quotient stream equality, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or host real equality.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedNestedRealChoiceSeal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-nested-real-choice-seal-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BoundedNestedRealChoiceSealUp$ carrier
$$
B=(I,L,C,W,R,D,E,H,T,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedNestedRealChoiceSealUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed bounded source, located nested data, Cantor handoff, finite windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerances, terminal seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item bounded nested-source exposure, requiring every interval read to pass through $I$ before any located or Cantor-facing row is consumed;
\item Cantor handoff, requiring $C$ to be read only after $I$ and $L$ and before any Real-facing seal request;
\item finite-window readback, requiring every consumer to pass through $W,R,D$ before reaching $E$;
\item Real seal nonescape, requiring $E$ to remain attached to $I,L,C,W,R,D,H,T,P,N$;
\item transport, requiring $H$ to preserve the displayed rows componentwise;
\item replay, requiring $T$ to replay only the displayed route from bounded nested data to the terminal seal;
\item provenance, requiring $P$ to source the packet from the named BEDC rows listed in the carrier;
\item local naming, requiring $N$ to name exactly this finite packet.
\end{enumerate}
No bounded nested real choice-seal obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bounded-nested-real-choice-seal-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the bounded interval row, located nested row, Cantor handoff, finite windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerances, terminal seal, and structural rows.

The bounded-source and Cantor-handoff obligations are read in order. A consumer first sees $I$, then the located row $L$, and only then the handoff row $C$; the handoff cannot be read as an ambient completion theorem or as a selected point.

The finite-window readback obligation is the route through $W,R,D$. The StreamName windows expose finite observations, the RegSeqRat row records the regular rational readback of those observations, and the DyadicRatCore row supplies the endpoint and tolerance comparisons used by both.

The structural obligations are $H,T,P,N$. They transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed coordinates. Since the carrier has no excluded coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
