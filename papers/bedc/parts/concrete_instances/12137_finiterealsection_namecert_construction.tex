The $\FiniteRealSectionUp$ packet realizes one finite section of the diagonal-limit representative of \autoref{def:real-completeness-diagonal-limit}. It sits between the representative packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-diagonallimitrepresentative-namecert} and the tail-alignment packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tail-modulus-alignment-namecert}, and it consumes the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular-rational readbacks of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the seal rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a single displayed precision request and its section route; it is not a quotient stream, selected host limit, countable-choice selector, ambient Real-completeness principle, or standard bridge.

\begin{definition}[Finite real section carrier]
\label{def:finite-real-section-carrier}
A $\FiniteRealSectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(q,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $q$ is the finite precision request for the diagonal-limit section;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window exposing the selected stream read for that request;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row read through $W$;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the rational arithmetic and error budget attached to $R$;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached after the selected regular-rational readback has been repacked through $D$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $q,W,R,D,E$ and the local certificate row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the request through the window, readback, dyadic ledger, and Real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteRealSectionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteRealSectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the displayed finite-section route and its transport, replay, provenance, and naming support.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Finite real section obligation surface]
\label{thm:finite-real-section-obligation-surface}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-real-section-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteRealSectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, classifier stability for the displayed tuple $(q,W,R,D,E)$, window-readback compatibility between $W$ and $R$, dyadic-tolerance exposure through $D$, $\RealUp$ seal exposure through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $S$. Its coordinates list one precision request, one stream window, one regular-rational readback, one dyadic ledger, one Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The data-bearing obligations are inherited in order. Carrier admission exposes $q$; the classifier is stable precisely because accepted packets compare the displayed tuple $(q,W,R,D,E)$ componentwise before any support row is read; the window-readback obligation then forces $R$ to be the readback attached to $W$.

The remaining rows are downstream support. The dyadic ledger $D$ records the tolerance row for the readback, the seal row $E$ is reachable only after that ledger is displayed, and $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Thus the listed obligations exhaust the local naming surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite real section non-escape]
\label{thm:finite-real-section-nonescape}
Every Real- or Completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\FiniteRealSectionUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
q,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
The consumer receives a single precision request, one $\StreamNameUp$ window, one $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, one $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, one $\RealUp$ seal, and the displayed transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. It receives no quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, selected host limit, countable-choice selector, ambient Real completeness, or standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{def:finite-real-section-carrier}. The only Real-facing coordinate is the seal row $E$, and it is downstream of the finite request $q$, stream window $W$, regular-rational readback $R$, and dyadic tolerance ledger $D$.

Apply \autoref{thm:finite-real-section-obligation-surface}. It forces every consumer to pass through carrier admission, classifier stability, window-readback compatibility, dyadic-tolerance exposure, seal exposure, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming before using the packet.

The excluded objects require coordinates that the carrier does not contain. The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name displayed data, but they do not create quotient stream equality, host sequence equality, a selected host limit, a countable-choice selector, ambient Real completeness, or a standard bridge. Hence none of those objects can be projected from the finite-section certificate.
\end{proof}
