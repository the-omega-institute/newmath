It sits beside the finite covering ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-vitali-cover-namecert} and the terminal real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}; the packet names one displayed maximal domination route without importing measure completion, countable choice, selected suprema, quotient function equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Hardy-Littlewood maximal carrier]
\label{def:hardy-littlewood-maximal-carrier}
A $\HardyLittlewoodMaximalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(X,B,R,A,V,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the metric-source row. It records the carried points, the displayed distance reads, and the finite ball names inspected by the maximal request.
\item $B$ is the metric-ball row. Each ball record stores a center, a positive radius witness, the membership probes read by the packet, and the carrier-local inclusion comparisons used downstream.
\item $R$ is the positive-radius window row. It stores only finite radius candidates and rational comparison budgets; it has no coordinate for a selected optimizing radius.
\item $A$ is the local-average row. For every displayed ball window it records the finite readback route for the corresponding average value and the rational tolerance by which that value is compared.
\item $V$ is the finite Vitali-cover ledger, read through $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$. It records the selected disjoint subfamily, the overlap budget, and the cover cells used to dominate the requested window.
\item $D$ is the domination witness row. It records the finite comparison that bounds each displayed local average by the exported maximal value read through the same radius and cover windows.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row. It exposes only the finite real-name windows reached from $A$ and $D$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, ball windows, radius budgets, local averages, Vitali ledger, domination witness, real seal, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$

\longmapsto

$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HardyLittlewoodMaximalUp$, $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HardyLittlewoodMaximalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these eleven rows. Its classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,B,R,A,V,D,E,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for a selected supremum, a countable ball family, a measure-completion object, quotient function equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hardy-Littlewood maximal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hardy-littlewood-maximal-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\HardyLittlewoodMaximalUp$ carrier
$$
M=(X,B,R,A,V,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\HardyLittlewoodMaximalUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the metric source, metric-ball windows, positive-radius budgets, local average rows, finite Vitali-cover ledger, domination witness, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item positive-radius visibility, requiring every local average request to read a displayed radius witness from $R$ and the matching ball data from $B$ before any average row in $A$ is inspected;
\item finite Vitali-cover discipline, requiring domination to pass through the ledger $V$ and its overlap budget before the witness row $D$ is exported;
\item real-seal discipline, requiring every numerical output to pass through $E$ after the local average and domination rows have been displayed;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve exactly the rows $X,B,R,A,V,D,E$ and to export no selected supremum, no countable-choice ball selection, no measure completion, no quotient function equality, and no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:hardy-littlewood-maximal-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the metric source, ball windows, positive-radius budgets, local average rows, finite Vitali ledger, domination witness, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The radius and average obligations are ordered by the carrier itself. A local average read from $A$ is meaningful only after the matching ball data from $B$ and positive radius witness from $R$ have been displayed, so the packet cannot replace finite windows by an optimizing host radius.

The covering step is the row $V$. Its $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$ ledger supplies the finite selected subfamily and overlap budget used by $D$, and the domination witness can only cite those displayed cells.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since there is no carrier coordinate for a selected supremum, countable-choice ball family, measure completion, quotient function equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, none of those principles appears in the local $\NameCert_{\HardyLittlewoodMaximalUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}
