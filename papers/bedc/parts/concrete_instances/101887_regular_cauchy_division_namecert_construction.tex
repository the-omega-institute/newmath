\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy division carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-division-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyDivisionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(X,Y,A,I,P,W,R,E,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular Cauchy source rows for the numerator and denominator.
\item $A$ is the denominator apartness guard exposing the finite nonzero-domain evidence for $Y$.
\item $I$ is the $\RegularCauchyReciprocalUp$ route that reads $Y$ only through $A$ and returns the reciprocal readback surface.
\item $P$ is the $\RegularCauchyProductUp$ route that multiplies $X$ by the reciprocal route $I$.
\item $W$ records the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows opened for $X$, $Y$, the reciprocal readback, and the product readback.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the guarded quotient candidate produced by the product route.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal receiving the guarded quotient packet.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,Y,A,I,P,W,R,E,C,Q,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for source inspection, guard exposure, reciprocal routing, product routing, finite-window handoff, readback, and seal handoff.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyDivisionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only through the displayed rows $X,Y,A,I,P,W,R,E,H,C,Q,N$.
\end{definition}

\label{thm:regular-cauchy-division-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $D=(X,Y,A,I,P,W,R,E,H,C,Q,N)$. Its coordinates list the two regular Cauchy sources, the denominator guard, reciprocal route, product route, finite windows, quotient readback, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

The analytic rows are ordered by dependency. The reciprocal route $I$ reads $Y$ only after the apartness guard $A$, while the product route $P$ reads $X$ together with the reciprocal readback exposed by $I$.

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy division reciprocal-product handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-division-reciprocal-product-handoff}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyDivisionUp$ carrier $D=(X,Y,A,I,P,W,R,E,H,C,Q,N)$, every quotient readback factors through
$$
(Y,A)\longmapsto I,\qquad (X,I)\longmapsto P,\qquad P\longmapsto R.
$$
No product read can consume the denominator as a divisor until the reciprocal route has exposed the apartness-guarded reciprocal surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the denominator side first. The row $A$ is the only coordinate licensing reciprocal use of $Y$, and the reciprocal route $I$ is downstream of that guard.

Next read the product row $P$. Its left input is the numerator source $X$, and its right input is the reciprocal surface supplied by $I$; the carrier contains no separate division operation row.

Finally project the readback row $R$. Because $R$ is downstream of $P$, every quotient read has already passed through the denominator guard, reciprocal route, and product route displayed in the statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy division Real seal non-escape]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-division-real-seal-nonescape}

The seal exports no total division, trichotomy, quotient stream equality, selected representative, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Use the reciprocal-product handoff. It places $I$ after the denominator guard and places $P$ after the numerator source and reciprocal route, so the readback $R$ cannot be reached by a direct host division route.

The Real seal row $E$ is terminal. Since the carrier has no additional coordinate for total division, trichotomy, quotient stream equality, selected representatives, or ambient completeness, the seal can export only the guarded finite quotient route already displayed.
\end{proof}
