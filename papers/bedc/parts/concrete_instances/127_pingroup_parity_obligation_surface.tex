\begin{theorem}[PinGroup parity carrier inversion]
\label{thm:pingroup-parity-carrier-inversion}
Every accepted row of the reflection-parity carrier of
\autoref{def:pingroup-reflection-parity-carrier} has a finite inversion record
inside the public $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ surface. The record is a
$\mathsf{BHist}$ row whose source projection is either the inherited
$\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$ Spin row or the $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ Clifford
reflection row, together with the public parity classifier, the displayed
$\Cont$ transport ledger, the matching $\hsame$ witness, and the $\Pkg$
provenance row. No accepted parity-carrier row has a third source projection.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-boundary}. It factors every
public $\PinGroupUp$ row through the inherited Spin branch or the Clifford
reflection branch of the displayed parity carrier.

Use \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-exactness}. The exactness theorem
identifies the accepted branch with the public parity classifier and keeps the
transported row inside the $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier data.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-projection-obligation} and
\autoref{thm:pingroup-source-projection-no-confusion}. These results attach
the $\Cont$, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$ rows to the same source projection and exclude
any source beyond the consumed $\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup parity transport ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:pingroup-parity-transport-ledger-exhaustion}
The finite transport ledger visible to the $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$
reflection-parity surface is exhausted by the inherited $\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$
inclusion row, the $\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ Clifford reflection product row,
the parity classifier row, the source-projection rows, and the consumer rows
exported by the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet. Its public witnesses are
exactly the listed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-ledger-coverage}. It places each public
source read in the Spin or Clifford source ledger and records the same
$\mathsf{BHist}$ histories for the carrier-facing packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-spin-subrow-classifier-readback} to identify the
Spin inclusion row, and apply
\autoref{thm:pingroup-clifford-action-ledger-obligation} to identify the
Clifford reflection product and action rows.

Next use \autoref{thm:pingroup-parity-classifier-obligation} and
\autoref{thm:pingroup-source-projection-obligation}. They fix the parity
classifier and source projections as the only classifier-facing source rows.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package}. It exports
the same consumer rows with their $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\Pkg$ witnesses, so
no further transport ledger remains.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PinGroup parity NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:pingroup-parity-namecert-obligation-surface}
The $\PinGroupUp$ obligationClosure surface is the finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ package obtained from the parity carrier inversion of
\autoref{thm:pingroup-parity-carrier-inversion}, the transport-ledger
exhaustion of \autoref{thm:pingroup-parity-transport-ledger-exhaustion}, the
source ledger coverage of \autoref{thm:pingroup-source-ledger-coverage}, and
the parity exactness of \autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-exactness}.
It consists exactly of the inherited $\NameCert_{\SpinGroupUp}$ rows, the
$\NameCert_{\CliffordUp}$ reflection rows, the parity classifier, the displayed
$\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport rows, the $\Pkg$ provenance rows, and the
exported consumer rows of $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use \autoref{thm:pingroup-parity-carrier-inversion}. It gives the
row-wise inversion data and restricts every accepted parity-carrier row to the
Spin or Clifford source projections.

Then use \autoref{thm:pingroup-parity-transport-ledger-exhaustion}. This fixes
the finite ledger as the Spin inclusion, Clifford reflection product, parity
classifier, source projections, and exported consumer rows.

Combine those two statements with
\autoref{thm:pingroup-source-ledger-coverage} and
\autoref{thm:pingroup-reflection-parity-exactness}. The source theorem fixes
the two certificate inputs, while the exactness theorem fixes the branch
classifier and its transport behavior.

Apply \autoref{thm:pingroup-downstream-unblock-package} to repack the same
$\mathsf{BHist}$ rows as the public $\NameCert_{\PinGroupUp}$ consumer surface.
The resulting package is the full obligation surface and contains no host
orthogonal group, quotient algebra, matrix model, or third source.
\end{proof}
