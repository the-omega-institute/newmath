\begin{definition}[BishopRegularCauchyPair carrier]
\label{def:bishopregularcauchypair-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two regular Cauchy real-name source rows being compared.
\item $W$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule used to expose both sources at the same precision requests.
\item $D_A$ and $D_B$ are the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledgers read from $A$ and $B$ along $W$.
\item $R_A$ and $R_B$ are the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows produced from the same window and tolerance data.
\item $C$ is the $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ admission row witnessing that the displayed reads are regular Cauchy reads at the inspected tolerances.
\item $T$ is the $\RealNameClassifierUp$ finite comparison row comparing the two displayed regular rational readbacks before the real seal is consumed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $A,B,W,D_A,D_B,R_A,R_B,C,T$ and the structural support rows.
\item $K$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from the paired sources through the common windows, dyadic tolerance ledgers, regular rational readbacks, convergence criterion, classifier row, and real seal.

\item $S$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after both regular rational readbacks and the finite classifier row have been displayed.

The carrier stores no quotient Cauchy class, no selected host limit, no trichotomy witness, no open-cover compactness principle, and no host $\RealUp$ equality coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BishopRegularCauchyPair RegSeqRat factorization]
\label{thm:bishopregularcauchypair-regseqrat-factorization}
For an accepted $\BishopRegularCauchyPairUp$ carrier $Q=(A,B,W,D_A,D_B,R_A,R_B,C,T,H,K,P,S,N)$, every comparison read factors through the finite route
$$
A,B \longmapsto W \longmapsto D_A,D_B \longmapsto R_A,R_B .
$$
Thus the paired comparison reaches $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks only by using the common $\StreamNameUp$ window schedule and the two $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledgers, and it has no independent route to host sequence equality or quotient Cauchy classes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishopregularcauchypair-carrier}. The only shared observation schedule for the two source names is $W$, so neither source has a separate precision route.

The tolerance rows $D_A$ and $D_B$ are attached to that same schedule. The replay row $K$ therefore carries both sources through one displayed window route before any regular rational readback is visible.

The readback rows $R_A$ and $R_B$ occur after $W,D_A,D_B$ in the carrier, and the transport row $H$ preserves the same component order. Since no carrier coordinate contains host sequence equality or quotient classes, the displayed route is the whole $\RegSeqRatUp$ factorization surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BishopRegularCauchyPair Real classifier handoff]
\label{thm:bishopregularcauchypair-real-classifier-handoff}

Any $\RealNameClassifierUp$ consumer of $Q$ reads the classifier row $T$ only after the two regular Cauchy sources, the common finite-window schedule, the dyadic tolerance ledgers, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, and the $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ admission row have been displayed. The subsequent $\RealUp$ seal row $S$ exports only this finite classifier handoff, not quotient Cauchy equality, selected limits, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or host real equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a classifier-facing consumer. Carrier admission displays $A,B,W,D_A,D_B,R_A,R_B,C,T$ in the stated dependency order, and the continuation row $K$ replays the same order for every accepted read.

The classifier row $T$ is downstream of the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows and the convergence row $C$. Hence it compares the displayed regular rational readbacks under the common finite window and dyadic tolerance data, rather than comparing host-level completed objects.

The seal row $S$ is later than $T$. The package provenance $P$ and local name $N$ record the same packet, while the componentwise transport $H$ moves the displayed rows without adding a new equality principle.

The carrier explicitly omits quotient Cauchy classes, selected limits, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, and host $\RealUp$ equality. Consequently the real-facing export is exactly the finite classifier handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BishopRegularCauchyPair common-window induction]
\label{thm:bishopregularcauchypair-common-window-induction}

At each step, both source reads advance through the same $W$-coordinate before $D_A,D_B$, $R_A,R_B$, $C$, $T$, or $S$ can be consumed.

Consequently no side-specific window, hidden tail oracle, quotient Cauchy equality, selected limit, trichotomy, or ambient completeness row can enter between the two sources and the $\RealNameClassifierUp$ handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the finite displayed window rows in $W$. In the empty-window case, carrier admission exposes only the source rows, provenance rows, and local naming data, so no tolerance ledger, readback, classifier, or seal row has yet been consumed.

For the step case, extend the inspected window by one displayed coordinate. The componentwise transport row $H$ keeps the new coordinate shared by the two sources, and the continuation replay row $K$ advances both source reads through that same coordinate before any downstream row is replayed.

Projecting \autoref{def:bishopregularcauchypair-carrier} then fixes the remaining order: the dyadic ledgers $D_A,D_B$ feed the readbacks $R_A,R_B$, the convergence row $C$ admits the displayed reads, the classifier row $T$ follows those rows, and the seal $S$ is terminal.

Composing this induction with \autoref{thm:bishopregularcauchypair-regseqrat-factorization} and \autoref{thm:bishopregularcauchypair-real-classifier-handoff} leaves no carrier coordinate through which a side-specific precision route, hidden tail oracle, quotient equality, selected limit, trichotomy, or ambient completeness witness could intervene.
\end{proof}
