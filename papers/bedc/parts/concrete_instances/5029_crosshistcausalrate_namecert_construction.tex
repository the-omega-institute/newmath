$$

\item $D$ is the displayed causal-dependence route family, whose entries are finite $\CrossHistCausalUp$ rows over the ordered pair $A,B$ and selected unary slots;

\item $S$ is the observer-symmetry row, recording that swapping the two Hist slots preserves the same maximum-rate read after displayed transport;

\begin{theorem}[Cross-Hist causal-rate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cross-hist-causal-rate-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cross-hist-causal-rate-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CrossHistCausalRateUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
R=(A,B,D,M,S,G,H,C,P,N),
$$
two observer-history slots, finite causal-dependence route replay, maximum-rate row exactness, observer-symmetry preservation, no-global-frame refusal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cross-hist-causal-rate-carrier}. The rows $A,B,D,M,S,G,H,C,P,N$ expose every public coordinate.

The observer slots are read through $A$ and $B$, with observer-Hist identity supplied by the sibling surface. The dependence routes in $D$ are finite $\CrossHistCausalUp$ rows, and the continuation routes in $C$ replay those rows without adding a hidden causal witness.

The maximum-rate read is the row $M$. It is attached to exactly the displayed dependence route family $D$, so the rate surface cannot detach from the two histories or from the selected unary slots.

The observer-symmetry row $S$ and the no-global-frame ledger $G$ provide the two public gates for a maximum-rate read. The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same packet, so the NameCert obligations are exhausted by the displayed carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cross-Hist causal-rate invariant boundary]
\label{thm:cross-hist-causal-rate-invariant-boundary}
Every accepted $\CrossHistCausalRateUp$ packet reads its maximum-rate row as an observer-symmetric inter-Hist invariant over the two displayed history slots. A consumer may reach the rate only through $A,B,D,M,S,G,H,C,P,N$, and it may not read a maximum-rate value when either the observer-symmetry row $S$ or the no-global-frame ledger $G$ is absent.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The two histories are the rows $A$ and $B$, and all causal content is the finite route family $D$.

Read the maximum-rate row $M$ through the same route family. Since $M$ is specified over the displayed dependence routes, it is not a host real-valued speed and not a metric row.

Now apply the observer-symmetry row $S$. It records that swapping $A$ and $B$ preserves the same maximum-rate read after componentwise transport, matching the finite invariant surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interhistinvariant-namecert}.

Finally inspect the no-global-frame ledger $G$. Without $G$, the read could not certify that the rate is independent of a privileged synchronization surface; without $S$, it could not certify observer symmetry. Hence a public maximum-rate read requires both rows.
\end{proof}
