\begin{definition}[Cross-Hist causal-rate carrier]
\label{def:cross-hist-causal-rate-carrier}

$$

\begin{enumerate}

\item $D$ is the displayed causal-dependence route family, whose entries are finite $\CrossHistCausalUp$ rows over the ordered pair $A,B$ and selected unary slots;
\item $M$ is the maximum-rate row, read as a $\MaxCausalRateUp$ unary-bound packet over exactly the dependence routes in $D$;
\item $S$ is the observer-symmetry row, recording that swapping the two Hist slots preserves the same maximum-rate read after displayed transport;
\item $G$ is the no-global-frame ledger, recording absence of a global synchronization row, shared clock row, metric tensor row, or privileged observer-frame row;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two Hist slots, dependence routes, maximum-rate row, observer-symmetry row, no-global-frame ledger, continuation routes, provenance row, and local naming row;

The carrier contains no coordinate for a global observer frame, synchronized clock, metric tensor, host real-valued speed, unlisted causal witness, or maximum-rate read detached from the displayed dependence routes.
\end{definition}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cross-hist-causal-rate-carrier}. The rows $A,B,D,M,S,G,H,C,P,N$ expose every public coordinate.

The observer slots are read through $A$ and $B$, with observer-Hist identity supplied by the sibling surface. The dependence routes in $D$ are finite $\CrossHistCausalUp$ rows, and the continuation routes in $C$ replay those rows without adding a hidden causal witness.

The maximum-rate read is the row $M$. It is attached to exactly the displayed dependence route family $D$, so the rate surface cannot detach from the two histories or from the selected unary slots.

The observer-symmetry row $S$ and the no-global-frame ledger $G$ provide the two public gates for a maximum-rate read. The remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same packet, so the NameCert obligations are exhausted by the displayed carrier.
