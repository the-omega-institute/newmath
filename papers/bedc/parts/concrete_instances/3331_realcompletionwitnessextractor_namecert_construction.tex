\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RealCompletionWitnessExtractorUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-realcompletionwitnessextractor-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\RealCompletionWitnessExtractorUp$ interface packages the diagonal-limit
witness surface used by the Real Completeness route. It starts from the
finite dyadic tolerance ledger, selected stream windows, regular-rational
readback rows, and RealUp seal rows already exposed by the Real and Completion
pipeline, then records the witness packet that a downstream completion
consumer may inspect. It is a finite NameCert packet; it is not a selected
host limit, quotient stream equality, or ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Real completion witness-extractor carrier]
\label{def:real-completion-witness-extractor-carrier}
A $\RealCompletionWitnessExtractorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
X=(D,W,M,Q,L,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger for the diagonal
regular-Cauchy route;
\item $W$ is the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window family used to read the
finite diagonal observations;
\item $M$ is the modulus schedule that chooses the displayed diagonal windows
and records the finite comparison budget for each requested precision;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row assembled from the selected
windows and dyadic tolerance rows;
\item $L$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached after the regular-rational
readback has passed through the displayed diagonal schedule;
\item $S$ is the completion-facing seal or handoff row consumed by Real
Completeness and Cauchy-limit consumers;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $D,W,M,Q,L,S$ and the
local structural rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from tolerance requests through
the selected windows, modulus schedule, regular-rational readback, RealUp
seal, and completion-facing handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionWitnessExtractorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts exactly those packets whose completion-facing handoff is
read through the same finite dyadic ledger, selected windows, modulus schedule,
regular-rational witness, RealUp seal, transport rows, continuation rows, and
local certificate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion witness-extractor NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-completion-witness-extractor-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionWitnessExtractorUp}$ surface consists of
carrier admission for the packet $X=(D,W,M,Q,L,S,H,C,P,N)$, selected-window
admission through $D,W,M$, regular-rational readback through $Q$, RealUp seal
handoff through $L$, completion-facing handoff through $S$, componentwise
transport through $H$, displayed route replay through $C$, package provenance
through $P$, local naming through $N$, and non-escape for quotient streams,
selected host limits, host real equality, choice data, and ambient
completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The displayed packet lists the dyadic tolerance ledger,
selected window family, modulus schedule, regular-rational readback, RealUp
seal, completion-facing handoff, transports, routes, provenance, and local
certificate.

The selected-window obligation is read first. The route from $D$ through
$W$ and $M$ fixes the finite observations and the precision schedule before
any regular-rational witness can be exported.

The readback and seal obligations then factor through $Q,L,S$ in that order.
The rows $H$ and $C$ preserve this order componentwise, while $P$ and $N$
record the same finite packet as the local NameCert surface.

No coordinate of the carrier names quotient stream equality, a selected host
limit, host real equality, choice data, or ambient completeness. Those
conclusions therefore cannot be read from the witness extractor.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion witness-extractor diagonal handoff]
\label{thm:real-completion-witness-extractor-diagonal-handoff}
Every Real Completeness diagonal-limit consumer of an accepted
$\RealCompletionWitnessExtractorUp$ packet receives exactly the finite handoff
$$
D,W,M,Q,L,S,H,C,P,N.
$$
The handoff presents the dyadic tolerance ledger, selected stream windows,
modulus schedule, regular-rational witness row, RealUp seal, completion-facing
seal, displayed $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, package
provenance, and local naming row. It supplies no independent completion
operator and no equality of quotient real names.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the selected-window side of the carrier. The diagonal route can
inspect $D,W,M$ only as the displayed finite schedule for the requested
precision.

Next read the regular-rational witness $Q$. It is attached to the same
selected windows and dyadic ledger, so the consumer cannot choose a second
window family or a second modulus schedule.

Finally pass through the RealUp seal $L$ and the completion-facing handoff
$S$. The transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows keep the
handoff inside the same finite packet, and the carrier has no quotient-real
or ambient-completion coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion witness-extractor non-escape]
\label{thm:real-completion-witness-extractor-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, or completion-facing
consumer of an accepted $\RealCompletionWitnessExtractorUp$ packet factors
through $D,W,M,Q,L,S,H,C,P,N$. The packet exports no quotient stream equality,
no selected host limit, no host real equality, no arbitrary choice of a
diagonal subsequence, and no ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the NameCert obligations. They place every consumer-visible read on the
same carrier coordinates: dyadic tolerance ledger, selected windows, modulus
schedule, regular-rational witness, RealUp seal, completion-facing handoff,
transport rows, route rows, provenance, and local name.

For a stream or regular-rational consumer, the route stops at $W,M,Q$. For a
RealUp or completion-facing consumer, the route continues through $L$ and
$S$ after the same window and tolerance rows have already been fixed.

The excluded conclusions require rows not present in
\autoref{def:real-completion-witness-extractor-carrier}. Since $H,C,P,N$
only transport, replay, package, and name the displayed coordinates, no
consumer can escape the finite witness packet.
\end{proof}
