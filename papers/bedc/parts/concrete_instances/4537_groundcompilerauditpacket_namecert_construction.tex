The $\GroundCompilerAuditPacketUp$ carrier realizes the compiler-layer audit map of \autoref{ch:bedc-groundcompiler-audit-map} as a finite naming certificate. It sits beside the source-channel ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelsourcechannelledger-namecert}: that sibling packet records the acceptance-facing channel around kernel declarations, while this packet records the GroundCompiler event-flow, source-channel, recognizer, certificate-gate, report-boundary, and no-hidden-input rows that feed compiler-audit consumers.

\begin{definition}[GroundCompiler audit packet carrier]
\label{def:groundcompiler-audit-packet-carrier}
A $\GroundCompilerAuditPacketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(E,S,R,C,Q,M,B,X,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the GroundCompiler event-flow row naming the formal event input and its finite flow image;
\item $S$ is the source-channel row recording legal encoding, decoding, and source/channel separation for the same flow image;
\item $R$ is the generated recognizer row for package, name-certificate, derivation-certificate, theorem, chapter, or motif-facing classifier output;
\item $C$ is the certificate-gate row through which recognized compiler artifacts become export candidates;
\item $Q$ is the report-boundary row separating metric reports, motif reports, theorem code, chapter code, and source reports from proof or certificate evidence;
\item $M$ is the module-inventory row naming the GroundCompiler submodule ledger used by \autoref{ch:bedc-groundcompiler-audit-map};
\item $B$ is the no-hidden-input row grounded by the streaming compiler theorem and its legal event-flow, source-channel, recognizer, gate, quotient-limit, source-separation, metric-conservativity, self-hosting, bootstrap, and global-conservativity clauses;
\item $X$ is the source-channel export row linking this packet to \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelsourcechannelledger-namecert} without identifying compiler evidence with kernel acceptance evidence;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for event-flow, channel, recognizer, gate, report, module, no-hidden-input, export, route, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes by which compiler-audit consumers inspect $E,S,R,C,Q,M,B,X$ in finite order;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:bedc-groundcompiler-audit-map}, the sibling source-channel ledger, $\GroundCompiler$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GroundCompilerAuditPacketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host compiler trust, hidden host input, source text outside the legal channel, report-as-proof conversion, motif-as-object identity, raw equality beyond classifier limits, kernel acceptance evidence, or MetaCIC proof evidence.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[GroundCompiler audit packet classifier]
\label{def:groundcompiler-audit-packet-classifier}
The $\GroundCompilerAuditPacketUp$ classifier accepts exactly the carriers of \autoref{def:groundcompiler-audit-packet-carrier} whose rows satisfy the GroundCompiler audit-map reading of \autoref{ch:bedc-groundcompiler-audit-map}: the event-flow row is formal and finite, the source-channel row is legal and separated, generated recognizers mediate recognition evidence, certificate gates mediate exports, report rows remain non-evidence rows, the module-inventory row names the inspected GroundCompiler ledger, the no-hidden-input row is read through the streaming compiler theorem, and the source-channel export row is routed through the sibling kernel source-channel ledger.

It rejects any packet that reads report output as proof, treats source substrings as decoded event data without the legal channel row, treats classifier quotient data as raw equality, imports kernel acceptance evidence into the compiler packet, or imports MetaCIC proof evidence into the compiler packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[GroundCompiler audit packet NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:groundcompiler-audit-packet-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\GroundCompilerAuditPacketUp$ carrier
$$
G=(E,S,R,C,Q,M,B,X,H,T,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\GroundCompilerAuditPacketUp}$ surface consists exactly of event-flow readback through $E$, source-channel readback through $S$, recognizer readback through $R$, certificate-gate readback through $C$, report-boundary readback through $Q$, module-inventory readback through $M$, no-hidden-input readback through $B$, source-channel export readback through $X$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $T$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier projection. The rows $E,S,R,C,Q,M,B,X,H,T,P,N$ are the only public coordinates available to the local certificate.

Read the compiler evidence rows first. The event-flow row $E$ supplies the finite formal input surface, $S$ supplies legal source-channel encoding and separation, $R$ supplies generated recognition evidence, and $C$ supplies the certificate-mediated export gate.

Read the boundary rows next. The report row $Q$ records which report-like artifacts are not proof or certificate evidence. The module row $M$ anchors the packet in the inspected GroundCompiler ledger, while $B$ records the no-hidden-input streaming compiler surface grounded by the checked compiler theorem.

Finally inspect $X,H,T,P,N$. The export row links the compiler packet to the source-channel ledger without turning that ledger into compiler proof evidence. Transport, continuation, provenance, and naming replay the same finite rows, so the local NameCert obligations are exhausted by the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GroundCompiler audit packet export non-escape]
\label{thm:groundcompiler-audit-packet-export-non-escape}
Every public export from an accepted $\GroundCompilerAuditPacketUp$ packet factors through the displayed event-flow, source-channel, recognizer, certificate-gate, report-boundary, module-inventory, no-hidden-input, source-channel export, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows of \autoref{def:groundcompiler-audit-packet-carrier}. The export surface carries no hidden compiler input, no host source scan, no report-as-proof conversion, no motif-as-object identity, no classifier-quotient collapse into raw equality, and no kernel acceptance claim outside the sibling ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted carrier $G$. By \autoref{thm:groundcompiler-audit-packet-namecert-obligations}, every public export must pass through the listed NameCert rows.

The compiler-facing rows $E,S,R,C$ account for event inputs, source channels, generated recognizers, and certificate gates before anything is exported. The report row $Q$ keeps reports, motifs, theorem code, and chapter code on the non-evidence side of the boundary.

The no-hidden-input row $B$ supplies the streaming compiler audit surface, and the export row $X$ routes source-channel consumers toward the sibling kernel source-channel ledger. Neither row adds a host compiler trust coordinate or a kernel acceptance proof coordinate to this packet.

Thus the only export path is the finite displayed path through $H,T,P,N$. Since no carrier field names hidden input, host source scans, report-to-proof conversion, motif identity, raw equality collapse, or kernel acceptance beyond $X$, those routes cannot be read from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GroundCompiler audit packet MetaCIC separation]
\label{thm:groundcompiler-audit-packet-metacic-separation}
An accepted $\GroundCompilerAuditPacketUp$ packet may cite the compiler-layer audit map of \autoref{ch:bedc-groundcompiler-audit-map} and the source-channel ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelsourcechannelledger-namecert}, but it does not export MetaCIC reduction, typing, confluence, normalization, closure-preservation, or subject-reduction evidence. Any consumer requiring MetaCIC proof evidence must read it from the relevant MetaCIC packet rather than from the GroundCompiler audit packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Separate the cited origins. The origin chapter \autoref{ch:bedc-groundcompiler-audit-map} records compiler-layer facts: event-flow conservativity, legal channels, recognizer generatedness, certificate-mediated exports, report boundaries, and no-hidden-input streaming compilation.

The sibling ledger \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kernelsourcechannelledger-namecert} records source-channel rows around kernel acceptance events. Its use by $X$ is a channel-routing use, not a transfer of kernel acceptance or MetaCIC proof evidence into the GroundCompiler packet.

Now inspect the carrier of \autoref{def:groundcompiler-audit-packet-carrier}. None of $E,S,R,C,Q,M,B,X,H,T,P,N$ is a MetaCIC term, reduction, typing, confluence, normalization, closure-preservation, or subject-reduction proof row.

Therefore a MetaCIC consumer has no projection from this packet to the proof evidence it needs. It must instead read a dedicated MetaCIC packet, while the GroundCompiler packet remains a finite compiler-audit certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GroundCompiler audit packet refusal separation]
\label{thm:groundcompiler-audit-packet-refusal-separation}
In an accepted $\GroundCompilerAuditPacketUp$ packet, report rows, motif rows, host source scans, and kernel acceptance evidence cannot be exported as compiler proof evidence. The $\BHist$ carrier records report separation through $Q$, generated classifier and motif-facing recognition through $R$, no-hidden-input refusal through $B$, source-channel routing through $S$ and $X$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $T$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local $\NameCert$ naming through $N$; none of these rows converts a report, motif, host scan, or kernel acceptance row into proof evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the report and recognition rows first. The row $Q$ places metric reports, motif reports, theorem code, chapter code, and source reports on the report-boundary side, while $R$ records generated recognizer output rather than a proof object.

Next inspect the channel and refusal rows. The source-channel row $S$ and the export row $X$ route legal source-channel information, and $B$ records the no-hidden-input boundary. These rows do not add a host source scan coordinate or a kernel acceptance proof coordinate to the compiler packet.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:groundcompiler-audit-packet-metacic-separation} together with \autoref{thm:groundcompiler-audit-packet-carrier-exposure}. The only remaining rows are $H,T,P,N$, which preserve, replay, package, and name the same finite carrier; they do not turn report rows, motif rows, host scans, or kernel acceptance evidence into compiler proof evidence.
