\item $M$ is the $\LocatedRealIntervalPartitionUp$ row over the same interval endpoints, exposing the finite cell mesh, cell adjacency, dyadic refinement, rational-cell witnesses, and located endpoint readbacks;

The carrier has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected tag families, quotient function equality, measure completion, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness. Its public object is the finite closed-interval partition route from $I$ through $M,L,U,S,D,J,R,H,C,P,N$.

\begin{theorem}[Closed bounded interval Darboux partition NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-bounded-interval-darboux-partition-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-bounded-interval-darboux-partition-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedBoundedIntervalDarbouxPartitionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $K=(I,M,L,U,S,D,J,R,H,C,P,N)$; source-interval exactness through $I$; located partition exactness through $M$; lower and upper Darboux bracket exposure through $L,U$; finite Darboux-sum exactness and gap visibility through $S$; Darboux handoff through $D$; Riemann handoff through $J$; $\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ sealing through $R$; componentwise transport through $H$; continuation replay through $C$; provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The interval row $I$ fixes the lower and upper located endpoints, endpoint order, containment ledgers, finite readbacks, and terminal real seal before any partition row can be consumed.

Next read the located partition row $M$. Since $M$ is attached to the same endpoint and containment data, every Darboux cell and refinement read remains a finite closed-interval read rather than a host interval partition.

The lower and upper bracket rows $L,U$ and the sum row $S$ are then inspected through the Darboux-sum surface. They record finite lower and upper sums over displayed cells, and the gap ledger is visible before either integration handoff row can be used.

Finally the handoff rows $D,J,R$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name exactly this route. None of these rows contains open-cover compactness, a selected tag family, quotient function equality, measure completion, or ambient real completeness, so the listed obligations exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed bounded interval Darboux partition refinement stability]
\label{thm:closed-bounded-interval-darboux-partition-refinement-stability}
Let $K=(I,M,L,U,S,D,J,R,H,C,P,N)$ and $K'=(I',M',L',U',S',D',J',R',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\ClosedBoundedIntervalDarbouxPartitionUp$ carriers classified together by componentwise $\hsame$ transport. If $M'$ is read as a displayed common refinement of $M$ over the transported closed bounded interval row, then the transported lower bracket, upper bracket, finite sum gap, Darboux handoff, Riemann handoff, and $\RegSeqRatUp/\RealUp$ seal are all read through the same finite refinement route.

The refinement therefore preserves the Darboux partition certificate without adding selected tags, quotient equality of integrands, measure completion, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the source interval row. Componentwise transport carries $I$ to $I'$, including endpoint order, containment ledgers, finite windows, readbacks, and the real seal, before any refined cell can be read.

Read the common refinement through $M'$. Its cells are located relative to the transported endpoints and containment ledgers, so each refined cell points back to the same closed bounded interval route.

The Darboux bracket and sum rows transport next. The rows $L',U',S'$ compute lower and upper sums only from the displayed refined cells and carried values, hence the visible gap is preserved as a finite Darboux comparison.

The integration handoff rows $D',J',R'$ are downstream of that comparison. Since $H'$ and $C'$ replay the same interval-to-partition-to-gap route and $P',N'$ only source and name it, refinement stability cannot introduce the excluded host-side objects.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed bounded interval Darboux partition Riemann handoff]
\label{thm:closed-bounded-interval-darboux-partition-riemann-handoff}
Every accepted $\RiemannIntegralUp$ consumer of a $\ClosedBoundedIntervalDarbouxPartitionUp$ packet receives the closed interval only through $I$, the located partition only through $M$, the Darboux lower and upper bracket rows only through $L,U,S,D$, and the real-facing seal only through $R$. The handoff row $J$ may place a tagged-mesh consumer over that displayed partition route, but it does not supply an independent tag-selection family, quotient equality of integrands, measure completion, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:closed-bounded-interval-darboux-partition-namecert-obligations}. It makes the interval, partition, bracket, sum, gap, Darboux handoff, Riemann handoff, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows the entire public surface.

Apply \autoref{thm:closed-bounded-interval-darboux-partition-refinement-stability}. Any consumer-facing refinement of the partition must preserve the same endpoint, containment, cell, bracket, sum, and gap route before the handoff rows are read.

Now compare with the Riemann handoff boundary of \autoref{thm:riemann-integral-real-seal-boundary}. The Riemann consumer may add its own tag-containment and finite-sum rows, but those rows sit downstream of the displayed closed-interval Darboux partition route.

Thus $J$ is a consumer handoff for the finite route already named by $I,M,L,U,S,D,R,H,C,P,N$. It cannot export an independent selected tag family, quotient integrand equality, measure-completion object, open-cover compactness proof, or ambient real-completeness witness.
