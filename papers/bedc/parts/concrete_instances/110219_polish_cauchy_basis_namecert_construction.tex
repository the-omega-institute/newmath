\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PolishCauchyBasisUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-polish-cauchy-basis-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Alexandrov--Urysohn, Bourbaki, Kelley, and Bishop--Bridges, the
$\PolishCauchyBasisUp$ packet records the basis-level handoff by which a
complete separable metric source exposes Cauchy-filter basis data before any
completion consumer reads a limit. It sits between the $\PolishSpaceUp$ source
of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polishspace-namecert}, the
$\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ generator of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-filter-basis-namecert}, the
$\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, and the
$\SeparableMetricUp$ dense schedule of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separablemetric-namecert}. The packet is a
finite BEDC handoff surface for Polish-space Cauchy bases, not a quotient
completion, not a choice-selected dense sequence, and not an ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Polish Cauchy basis carrier]
\label{def:polish-cauchy-basis-carrier}
A $\PolishCauchyBasisUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(X,B,K,D,S,R,O,H,C,G,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\PolishSpaceUp$ source row, carrying the complete separable
metric packet over which every later basis read is interpreted.
\item $B$ is the $\CauchyFilterBasisUp$ basis row, recording the directed
finite basis generator used by Cauchy-filter consumers.
\item $K$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff row attached to the same metric
surface as $X$; it records the completion-facing route without selecting a
limit point.
\item $D$ is the $\SeparableMetricUp$ dense schedule row, stored as public
finite name data rather than as a choice function over all balls.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row used to inspect dense names,
basis requests, and Cauchy-window demands.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to those stream
windows, giving regular rational observations before any basis comparison is
made.
\item $O$ is the observation ledger joining dense-window requests,
regular-rational readbacks, Cauchy-basis refinements, and completion-handoff
checks.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,B,K,D,S,R,O$ and
for the local certificate rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Polish-source request
through dense schedules, stream windows, regular readbacks, Cauchy-basis
refinement, and the completion handoff.
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PolishSpaceUp$,
$\CauchyFilterBasisUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\SeparableMetricUp$,
$\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and
$\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PolishCauchyBasisUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two accepted packets only after transporting these
displayed rows to a common Polish source, Cauchy-basis generator,
completion-handoff row, dense schedule, stream-window row, and regular
readback ledger. It has no coordinate for choice-selected dense points,
quotient completion equality, host point equality, or ambient real
completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Polish Cauchy basis NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:polish-cauchy-basis-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PolishCauchyBasisUp$ carrier
$P=(X,B,K,D,S,R,O,H,C,G,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\PolishCauchyBasisUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following
obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the Polish source, Cauchy-filter basis,
complete-metric handoff, separable dense schedule, stream windows, regular
rational readback, observation ledger, transport, continuation, provenance,
and local naming rows;
\item common-source classification, requiring the $B,K,D,S,R,O$ rows to be
read over the same transported $\PolishSpaceUp$ source row $X$;
\item basis-directedness exposure, requiring every Cauchy-basis refinement
consumed by $B$ to be visible in $S,R,O$ before the completion handoff $K$ is
read;
\item dense-schedule compatibility, requiring the separable row $D$ and the
Cauchy-basis row $B$ to share the same stream-window and regular-readback
surface;
\item completion-handoff non-escape, requiring every completion-facing
consumer to pass through $B,S,R,O$ before reaching $K$;
\item package provenance and local $\NameCert$ naming for exactly the
displayed packet.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:polish-cauchy-basis-carrier}. The
projection exposes the Polish source, Cauchy-basis generator, complete-metric
handoff, dense schedule, stream windows, regular readbacks, observation
ledger, transport rows, continuation rows, provenance row, and local name.

The classifier route starts at $X$. Componentwise transport in $H$ moves
$B,K,D,S,R,O$ together, so a consumer never compares a basis row over one
Polish source with a completion handoff or dense schedule over another.

The observation ledger $O$ fixes the finite read order. Dense requests and
basis refinements are observed through $S$ and $R$ before either the
Cauchy-filter basis or complete-metric handoff can be consumed.

Finally replay $C$ and attach $G,N$. These rows route, source, and name the
same displayed packet, with no additional coordinate for selected dense
points, quotient completion equality, host point equality, or ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness. Hence the listed rows exhaust the local naming
surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Polish Cauchy basis completion handoff]
\label{thm:polish-cauchy-basis-completion-handoff}
Let $P=(X,B,K,D,S,R,O,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted
$\PolishCauchyBasisUp$ packet. Every completion-facing consumer of the Polish
source $X$ reaches the $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff $K$ only through the
displayed Cauchy-filter basis row $B$, the separable dense schedule $D$, the
$\StreamNameUp$ windows $S$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$, and the
observation ledger $O$, transported by $H$ and replayed by $C$.

The visible route is
$$
X,D,S,R,B,O \longmapsto K \longmapsto H,C,G,N .
$$
It exports a finite completion handoff for downstream completion, Baire, and
fixed-point packets; it does not export a quotient completion class, a
selected dense sequence, host point equality, or an ambient real-completeness
principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $X$ from the carrier in \autoref{def:polish-cauchy-basis-carrier}. The
Polish source supplies the common complete separable metric surface, while
$D,S,R$ display the finite dense-window observations available to the packet.

Use the NameCert obligations of
\autoref{thm:polish-cauchy-basis-namecert-obligations}. The basis-directedness
and dense-schedule compatibility clauses force the Cauchy-filter basis row
$B$ to be read through the same stream windows, regular readbacks, and
observation ledger as the dense schedule.

The completion-handoff clause then places $K$ after $B,S,R,O$. Transport by
$H$ and replay by $C$ preserve that order, while $G,N$ package and name the
same route. Therefore every completion-facing consumer receives only the
displayed finite handoff and cannot bypass it through choice, quotient
equality, host point equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}
