Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp$ packet records the finite comparison test that lets a displayed majorant regular Cauchy source certify a target stream before the Real-facing route is consumed. It sits over the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite window discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the explicit completion-modulus route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishopcompletionmodulus-namecert}. The packet is a constructive comparison principle for the open L10 real-completion path; it is not a quotient of streams, a selected limit, a host real comparison, countable choice, or an ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy comparison test carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-comparison-test-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(A,B,M,D,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the majorant regular Cauchy source row whose finite tolerances bound the comparison.
\item $B$ is the target stream or source row to be tested against the displayed majorant.
\item $M$ is the explicit modulus row assigning each displayed tolerance request a finite threshold.
\item $D$ is the dyadic domination and tolerance ledger, including the dyadic scale, comparison endpoint rows, and displayed domination certificates at each inspected tolerance.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family on which the source row, target row, modulus row, and dyadic ledger are all read.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the majorant source, target source, modulus, dyadic ledger, stream windows, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each tolerance request through $M$ to the window in $W$, then through $D$ to the target Cauchy row and the downstream regular rational handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\BishopCompletionModulusUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through the displayed rows above. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a hidden infinite tail selector, a host real value, a selected completed limit, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy comparison test NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-comparison-test-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp$ carrier $T=(A,B,M,D,W,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed nine-row packet;
\item majorant-source exposure for $A$ and target-source exposure for $B$ on the same finite window family $W$;
\item modulus threshold readback, stating that every inspected tolerance request reads its threshold from the displayed row $M$;
\item dyadic domination compatibility, stating that each displayed tolerance in $D$ bounds the target row $B$ by the majorant row $A$ on the requested window;
\item transport stability, stating that the threshold and domination rows are preserved by the componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$;
\item continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for exactly the route recorded by $C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations are finite-row obligations over the sibling surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishopcompletionmodulus-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $T=(A,B,M,D,W,H,C,P,N)$. The projection exposes exactly the majorant source, target source, modulus row, dyadic ledger, finite window family, transport rows, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming row.

For a displayed tolerance request, read the modulus row $M$ to obtain the finite threshold. The same threshold selects the relevant finite windows in $W$, so both $A$ and $B$ are inspected only through displayed data.

The dyadic ledger then supplies domination at that tolerance. Since the comparison row in $D$ is attached to the same threshold and window, the target Cauchy row is obtained by finite-row projection from the displayed domination certificate rather than from a hidden tail argument.

Finally apply the structural rows. The $\hsame$ rows in $H$ preserve the threshold and domination data componentwise, while $C,P,N$ only replay, source, and name the displayed route. No omitted coordinate remains from which an additional NameCert obligation could be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy comparison test RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-comparison-test-regseqrat-handoff}
Every accepted $\RegularCauchyComparisonTestUp$ carrier hands the target row to $\RegSeqRatUp$ only through the finite route
$$
A,B,M\longmapsto W,D\longmapsto H,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, a completion-facing consumer can reach the downstream Real-completion path only after reading the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ domination ledger, and the $\BishopCompletionModulusUp$ threshold route that bind the target row to the majorant source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier and fix a displayed tolerance request. The modulus row $M$ provides the threshold, and the finite windows in $W$ are the only windows admitted for that request.

Read the target row through the domination ledger. At each displayed tolerance, the dyadic row $D$ compares the target source $B$ with the majorant regular Cauchy source $A$ on the selected window. This supplies the target Cauchy row as a finite consequence of displayed domination.

Transport preserves the same route. The componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $H$ carry the modulus threshold, the selected window, and the dyadic domination certificate together, so a transported carrier cannot change the RegSeqRat handoff while keeping the same comparison test.

The remaining rows are downstream bookkeeping. The $\Cont$ replay in $C$, the provenance row $P$, and the local naming row $N$ send the displayed comparison route to the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff and then to Real-completion consumers only through the named sibling rows. Thus $\RealUp$ is reachable along this path only after the StreamName, DyadicRatCore, and BishopCompletionModulus data have been displayed.
\end{proof}
