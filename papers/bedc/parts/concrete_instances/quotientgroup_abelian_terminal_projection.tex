\begin{theorem}[Abelian centralizer-normalizer quotient terminal projection]
\label{thm:abelian-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-terminal-projection}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$
satisfying the hypotheses of
\autoref{thm:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-semantic-certificate},
and additionally the $\AbGroupUp$ commutativity hypothesis used by
\autoref{thm:abgroup-centralizer-commutator-collapse}, fix
$a:\mathsf{BHist}$. For every carried endpoint $x$ of $N_a$, define the
constant representative projection to the singleton quotient carrier by
$$
\pi_Q(x):=\emp .
$$
For any $x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $N_a(x)$ and $N_a(y)$, the abelian
centralizer-normalizer quotient classifier is the pullback of the singleton
quotient classifier along this projection:
$$
Q_{Z_a\triangleleft N_a}(x,y)\Longleftrightarrow
\rho_Q(\pi_Q(x),\pi_Q(y)).
$$
Moreover the projected product, inverse, and unit endpoints are already
singleton-classifier related:
$$
\rho_Q(\pi_Q(\mathsf{mul}(x,y)),\pi_Q(x)),\quad
\rho_Q(\pi_Q(\mathsf{inv}(x)),\pi_Q(x)),\quad
\rho_Q(\pi_Q(e),\emp).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the displayed equivalence, the left classifier is total under the
commutativity hypothesis by
\autoref{thm:abelian-centralizer-normalizer-quotient-classifier-totality}.
Thus its truth value is $\mathsf{True}$ on every carried endpoint pair.

The right classifier is also inhabited on every displayed endpoint pair.
Indeed, each projected endpoint is definitionally $\emp$, so unfolding the
projection reduces it to $\rho_Q(\emp,\emp)$. This is the reflexive singleton
classifier edge packaged by
\autoref{thm:quotientgroup-singleton-semantic-namecert}, using the singleton
carrier and classifier of Definitions~\ref{def:quotientgroup-singleton-carrier}
and \ref{def:quotientgroup-singleton-classifier}.

The product, inverse, and unit claims use the same endpoint computation. The
semantic certificate of
\autoref{thm:quotientgroup-centralizer-normalizer-semantic-certificate}
supplies the carried product, inverse, and unit endpoints in $N_a$; applying
$\pi_Q$ sends each one to $\emp$. Hence all three displayed singleton
classifier statements again reduce to $\rho_Q(\emp,\emp)$.
\end{proof}
