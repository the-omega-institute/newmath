Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\UniformCauchySequenceUp$ packet records the finite NameCert surface for one regular rational sequence whose Cauchy comparisons are governed by a shared modulus schedule. It sits on the L10 route from the stream-window source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the explicit threshold surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-modulusofconvergence-namecert}, and the terminal seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the sequence-level carrier consumed by completion routes; it does not select a limit, quotient streams, host real equality, countable choice, or an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[UniformCauchySequence carrier]
\label{def:uniform-cauchy-sequence-carrier}
A $\UniformCauchySequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(S,Q,D,M,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window surface for the sequence observations;
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row assigning rational names to the displayed stream windows;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used by every displayed Cauchy comparison;
\item $M$ is the $\ModulusOfConvergenceUp$ shared threshold row that binds all late-window comparisons;
\item $R$ is the finite uniform Cauchy comparison row, reading pairs of sufficiently late windows through $S,Q,D,M$;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal boundary reached only after the shared modulus row and comparison row have been displayed;
\item $H$ records $\hsame$ transport for the stream windows, regular readbacks, dyadic ledger, shared modulus, comparison row, real seal, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from stream inspection through regular readback, dyadic tolerance, shared threshold, comparison, and real-seal delivery;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformCauchySequenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\ModulusOfConvergenceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchySequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier stores no quotient stream, no selected host limit, no host equality of real names, no countable-choice selector, and no ambient completeness field.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchySequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-sequence-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\UniformCauchySequenceUp$ carrier $U=(S,Q,D,M,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchySequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed stream windows, regular rational readbacks, dyadic tolerance ledger, shared modulus row, comparison row, real seal, transports, replay route, provenance, and local naming row;
\item shared-modulus exactness, requiring every late-window Cauchy comparison in $R$ to use the same $\ModulusOfConvergenceUp$ threshold row $M$ and the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$;
\item readback compatibility, requiring the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $Q$ to be the only rational readback consumed by the comparison row $R$;
\item route ordering, requiring $R$ to be displayed only after $S,Q,D,M$ and requiring $E$ to be displayed only after $R$;
\item transport stability, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the route $S,Q,D,M,R,E,P,N$ without adding a hidden threshold, independent rational stream, quotient stream, or selected limit;
\item ledger exhaustion, stating that no quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle is exported.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:uniform-cauchy-sequence-carrier}. The tuple displays the stream window row, regular readback row, dyadic tolerance ledger, shared modulus row, comparison row, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

Shared-modulus exactness follows from the single coordinates $M$ and $D$. Since every comparison row in $R$ is read through those coordinates, the packet has no second threshold schedule and no hidden tolerance ledger.

Readback compatibility and route ordering are forced by the displayed coordinate order. The comparison row $R$ consumes $S,Q,D,M$, while the real seal $E$ is downstream of $R$.

Transport stability and ledger exhaustion are the role of $H,C,P,N$. These rows replay and name the same finite route, so they cannot introduce quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchySequence RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-sequence-regseqrat-handoff}
Let $U=(S,Q,D,M,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\UniformCauchySequenceUp$ carrier. Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback used by a Cauchy comparison in $R$ factors through the visible handoff
$$
S,Q,D,M,R,H,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, a comparison cannot replace $Q$ by an independent rational stream, cannot replace $M$ by a local threshold choice, and cannot replace $D$ by a hidden host tolerance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier coordinates. The only regular rational readback coordinate is $Q$, and the only shared threshold coordinate is $M$.

Apply the readback compatibility and shared-modulus clauses of \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-sequence-namecert-obligations}. They force every comparison in $R$ to read $Q$ together with $S,D,M$ before the comparison is accepted.

Finally use the transport rows $H$ and $C$. They preserve the same displayed route with provenance $P$ and local name $N$, so the handoff has no access to an independent rational stream, local threshold choice, or hidden host tolerance.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchySequence Real seal boundary]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-sequence-real-seal-boundary}
For an accepted $\UniformCauchySequenceUp$ carrier $U=(S,Q,D,M,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, every $\RealUp$ seal read reaches $E$ only after passing through the finite stream row $S$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $Q$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, $\ModulusOfConvergenceUp$ shared threshold $M$, and uniform Cauchy comparison row $R$. The seal boundary exports no quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a $\RealUp$ seal read of $U$. By the carrier definition, the only terminal seal coordinate is $E$.

The NameCert obligations place $E$ downstream of the comparison row $R$, and $R$ downstream of $S,Q,D,M$. Hence the seal read must traverse the stream windows, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, shared modulus, and comparison row first.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name the same route. Since the carrier lists no quotient-stream coordinate, selected-limit coordinate, countable-choice selector, host real equality, or ambient completeness row, the real seal boundary is exhausted by the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}
