\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GaloisGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-galoisgroup-namecert}

The Galois-group interface $\GaloisGroupUp$ packages the group of field automorphisms of a Galois extension fixing the base field pointwise. It depends on $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-galoisext-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:galoisgroup-carrier}

\begin{definition}[GaloisGroupUp automorphism carrier]
\label{def:galoisgroup-automorphism-carrier}
Fix a source packet for $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ as in
\autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary}. The
$\GaloisGroupUp$ automorphism carrier is the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
whose rows consist of:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the chosen $\GaloisExtUp$ source packet;
\item a displayed field-automorphism row over the source field-extension
carrier;
\item a fixed-base witness row saying that the base-field source endpoint is
preserved pointwise by the displayed automorphism;
\item source and target endpoint rows for the automorphism display;
\item finite $\Cont$ composition ledger rows for composing displayed
automorphisms;
\item $\hsame$ classifier rows comparing source, target, fixed-base, and
composition endpoints; and
\item $\Pkg$ provenance rows naming the $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ and
$\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ dependency certificates that scope the displayed
automorphism data.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts a row only when every endpoint, fixed-base witness,
composition ledger entry, classifier comparison, and provenance row is one of
these finite BEDC rows. It exports no equality of host fields and no quotient
of automorphisms outside the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:galoisgroup-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp fixed-base classifier]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-fixed-base-classifier}
For two accepted rows of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-automorphism-carrier}, the fixed-base
automorphism classifier is the conjunction of the inherited
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ classifier on the source packet and the
$\hsame$ comparisons of the displayed source endpoint, target endpoint, and
fixed-base witness rows. In particular, two rows are classified only through
the Galois-extension carrier, normality, separability, ledger, and exactness
rows exposed by \autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary}, together
with endpoint comparison inside the finite automorphism packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project each accepted automorphism packet to its $\GaloisExtUp$ source row.
\autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary} says that the available
source information is precisely the carrier, normality, separability,
classifier, stability, ledger, and exactness surface of the extension packet.
Thus no comparison of automorphism rows can read more source data than that
surface.

Now inspect the automorphism-specific rows. The displayed source endpoint,
target endpoint, and fixed-base witness are all finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows
inside \autoref{def:galoisgroup-automorphism-carrier}. Their comparisons are
therefore exactly the restricted $\hsame$ rows recorded in the carrier packet.

Combining the projected GaloisExtUp classifier with these endpoint
comparisons gives the stated classifier. Reflexive, symmetric, and transitive
uses are inherited from the dependency classifier and from the structural
$\hsame$ laws, and repacking those rows returns an accepted fixed-base
automorphism packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp composition closure]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-composition-closure}
Let $a$ and $b$ be accepted fixed-base automorphism rows of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-automorphism-carrier}. If the target endpoint of
$a$ is $\hsame$-classified with the source endpoint of $b$, and the carrier
contains a displayed $\Cont$ row composing their automorphism ledgers, then
the composed row is again accepted by the $\GaloisGroupUp$ automorphism
carrier. Its fixed-base witness is obtained by composing the two fixed-base
witness rows and transporting the middle endpoint through $\hsame$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the two accepted packets. Each supplies the same kind of
$\GaloisExtUp$ source projection, a displayed field-automorphism row, endpoint
rows, a fixed-base witness, classifier rows, and $\Pkg$ provenance through
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-automorphism-carrier}.

The displayed $\Cont$ row concatenates the two automorphism ledgers. The
middle endpoint comparison supplies the transport needed to align the target
of $a$ with the source of $b$, so the concatenated ledger has the source
endpoint of $a$ and the target endpoint of $b$.

For the fixed-base face, compose the two fixed-base witness rows. Since each
row fixes the base endpoint pointwise inside the inherited GaloisExtUp
surface, the composite fixes the same base endpoint after transporting the
middle row by $\hsame$.

Finally, repack the source projection, composed display, endpoints,
fixed-base witness, $\Cont$ ledger, classifier rows, and $\Pkg$ provenance.
The result is one of the finite packets accepted by the carrier, so
composition is closed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp inverse closure]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-closure}
Every accepted automorphism row of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-automorphism-carrier} exposes an inverse row through
the $\GroupUp$ dependency surface. The inverse row reverses the displayed
source and target endpoints, carries the inverse automorphism ledger supplied
by $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$, preserves the same fixed-base endpoint up to
$\hsame$, and is accepted by the same $\GaloisGroupUp$ carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted automorphism row into its source GaloisExtUp packet,
displayed field-automorphism row, endpoints, fixed-base witness, classifier
rows, composition ledger, and $\Pkg$ provenance.

Use the $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ dependency cited in
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}. Its inverse surface supplies
the inverse operation row together with the left-unit and right-unit
classification rows for the automorphism ledger. These rows reverse the
displayed source and target endpoints.

The fixed-base witness is stable under this reversal because the original row
fixes the base endpoint and the inverse ledger is classified with the identity
row on that endpoint by the GroupUp inverse laws. Transport the displayed
base comparison by $\hsame$ to obtain the fixed-base row for the inverse.

Repack the reversed endpoints, inverse ledger, fixed-base witness, inherited
GaloisExtUp source projection, classifier rows, and $\Pkg$ provenance. This
is again a finite packet of the form required by
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-automorphism-carrier}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp public certificate surface]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-public-certificate-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ surface consists exactly of the
carrier of \autoref{def:galoisgroup-automorphism-carrier}, the fixed-base
classifier of \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-fixed-base-classifier}, the
composition closure of \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-closure}, the
inverse closure of \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-closure}, the identity
row inherited from $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$, the finite $\Cont$ and $\hsame$
ledger rows displayed in the carrier, and the exactness rows inherited from
$\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Soundness is by projection. Every public row listed in the statement is read
directly from \autoref{def:galoisgroup-automorphism-carrier} or from one of
the two dependency certificates named by its $\Pkg$ provenance rows. The
GaloisExtUp side is limited by
\autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary}; the GroupUp side supplies
only identity, composition, inverse, and law rows for the displayed
automorphism ledger.

Completeness is by repacking. Given a row accepted by the public
$\GaloisGroupUp$ certificate, inspect whether it is a carrier row, classifier
row, composition row, inverse row, identity row, ledger row, or exactness row.
Each case is one of the fields of the automorphism packet or one of the named
dependency rows.

Thus the certificate surface has no hidden host-field equality, no external
automorphism quotient, and no additional algebraic obligation beyond the
finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet, $\Cont$ ledgers, $\hsame$ classifiers,
$\Pkg$ provenance, and the two cited NameCert dependency surfaces.
\end{proof}
