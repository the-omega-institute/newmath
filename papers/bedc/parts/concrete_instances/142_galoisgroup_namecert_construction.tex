\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GaloisGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-galoisgroup-namecert}

The Galois-group interface $\GaloisGroupUp$ packages the group of field automorphisms of a Galois extension fixing the base field pointwise. It depends on $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-galoisext-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}).

\begin{closurestatus}{\GaloisGroupUp}
  \constructivestory{A Galois group here is not a host automorphism set; it is a finite automorphism-action packet built from a $\GaloisExtUp$ source row, a $\GroupUp$ law row, fixed-base rows, action rows, composition and inverse rows, and the continuation ledgers that concatenate these rows to a public endpoint. The carrier accepts exactly those packets whose extension, group, fixed-base, action, composition, and inverse faces are unary histories tied by $\Cont$ to provenance, classifier, ledger, and endpoint rows, while quotient automorphism carriers and arbitrary ambient groups are outside the packet. Two accepted action packets classify together when the fixed-base row, action row, composition row, inverse row, provenance row, and endpoint row move by $\hsame$ through the same finite readback ledgers. The source specification is the accepted automorphism-action packet, the pattern specification records base-fixing, action, composition, inverse, identity, and group-law rows inherited from $\GaloisExtUp$ and $\GroupUp$, and the ledger keeps the raw action data visible as the same provenance and law endpoints. The five naming-certificate obligations come from the displayed packet endpoint for inhabitedness, the equivalence laws of $\hsame$, and the transport theorems that rebuild a carrier packet after componentwise classifier movement. A history $h$ lies in the signature GAP of a $\GaloisGroupUp$ token $p$ when the source accepts $h$, the signature relation pairs it to an answer action packet and evidence ledger, and token introduction identifies that answer with $p$; concretely, $h$ must be the endpoint produced by the extension-plus-group provenance row and the composition-plus-inverse ledger for the same base-fixed action surface. The chapter registers this construction as $\GaloisGroupUp$ without supplying a host automorphism group, an automorphism quotient, arbitrary Galois-group carriers, or a standard bridge.}
