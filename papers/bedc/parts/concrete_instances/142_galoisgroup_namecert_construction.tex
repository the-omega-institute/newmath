\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GaloisGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-galoisgroup-namecert}

The Galois-group interface $\GaloisGroupUp$ packages the group of field automorphisms of a Galois extension fixing the base field pointwise. It depends on $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-galoisext-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:galoisgroup-carrier}

\begin{definition}[GaloisGroupUp BHist automorphism-action packet]
\label{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}
A $\GaloisGroupUp$ automorphism-action packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$
tuple $(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ over two consumed dependency packets. The
row $E$ is a $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ packet, read through its public
extension carrier, normality, separability, classifier, exactness, and ledger
surface. The row $G$ is a $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ packet, read through its
operation, unit, inverse, associativity, congruence, and inverse-law surface.

The row $b$ is the fixed-base row. It records, for each displayed base-field
endpoint of $E$, that the automorphism endpoint leaves that base endpoint in
the same $\hsame$ class. The row $a$ is the automorphism-action row: source
and target extension endpoints are finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ endpoints from
$E$, and the action is carried only by $\mathsf{Cont}$ rows and the displayed
classifier of $E$. The row $m$ is the composition row, aligning the
$\GroupUp$ multiplication operation of $G$ with composition of the displayed
automorphism-action rows. The row $i$ is the inverse row, aligning the
$\GroupUp$ inverse operation of $G$ with inverse automorphism-action rows.

The row $c$ is the classifier row. It compares automorphism packets by
$\hsame$ on their finite extension endpoints, fixed-base rows, action rows,
composition rows, and inverse rows, without requiring equality of host fields,
ambient function spaces, or quotient representatives. The row $\rho$ is the
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row assigning every displayed face to either the
Galois-extension packet $E$, the group packet $G$, or the Galois-group
automorphism surface. The finite ledger $\lambda$ is the $\mathsf{Cont}$ list
containing exactly the dependency rows, fixed-base rows, action rows,
composition rows, inverse rows, classifier rows, provenance rows, and
exactness rows consumed by the public $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ interface.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:galoisgroup-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp fixed-base carrier obligation]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-fixed-base-carrier-obligation}
For an automorphism-action packet $(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}, carrier
acceptance for $\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ is exactly acceptance of
fixed-base automorphism rows whose extension endpoints are read from the
public $\NameCert_{\GaloisExtUp}$ surface of $E$ and whose operation endpoints
are read from the $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ surface of $G$. No carrier clause
asks for equality of host fields, ambient endomorphism sets, or uncarried
base-field elements.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet to $E$ and $G$. The Galois-extension public boundary of
\autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary} supplies the extension
carrier, classifier, exactness, normality, separability, and finite ledger
rows. The group certificate supplies the operation surface, inverse surface,
congruence rows, and inverse-law rows through the $\GroupUp$ public packet.

For the forward direction, carrier acceptance exposes a fixed-base
automorphism row. Reading the row through
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet} projects each
extension endpoint to $E$, each multiplication or inverse endpoint to $G$, and
each fixed-base comparison to an $\hsame$ row in the finite ledger
$\lambda$. Thus the accepted carrier is only a displayed BEDC row family over
the two consumed packets.

For the reverse direction, start with fixed-base automorphism rows whose
endpoints are already read from $E$ and $G$ with the stated ledger and package
provenance. Repack those rows as $b$ and $a$, attach the classifier row $c$,
and retain the same $\mathsf{Pkg}$ row $\rho$ and $\mathsf{Cont}$ ledger
$\lambda$. This constructs the carrier face of
$\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ without adding any host-field equality clause.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp composition and inverse obligation]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-composition-and-inverse-obligation}
For an automorphism-action packet $(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}, the composition
row $m$ and inverse row $i$ remain in the same Galois-group classifier exactly
when their displayed endpoints are transported by finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ rows,
$\hsame$ comparisons, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance from the Galois-extension
packet $E$ and the group-operation packet $G$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First isolate the composition row. The operation endpoint in $m$ is the
$\GroupUp$ multiplication row, while the acted-on endpoints are extension
histories from $E$. Group congruence transports the multiplication endpoint,
and the Galois-extension classifier transports the acted-on endpoints. The
ledger $\lambda$ records both transports as finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ rows.

Next isolate the inverse row. The inverse endpoint in $i$ is the $\GroupUp$
inverse row, and the inverse action endpoints are again displayed extension
histories from $E$. The inverse congruence and inverse laws from the group
surface keep the operation in the same classifier, while the fixed-base row
$b$ keeps the base endpoints in their original $\hsame$ classes.

Combining the two rows gives the stated equivalence. If composition and
inverse endpoints are transported by $\mathsf{Cont}$, $\hsame$, and
$\mathsf{Pkg}$, then $m$ and $i$ repack into the classifier row $c$.
Conversely, projecting $c$ exposes precisely those finite transports and no
additional ambient automorphism equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp public NameCert obligation boundary]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-public-namecert-obligation-boundary}
For an automorphism-action packet $(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}, the public
$\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ obligation surface is exactly the following
finite row family: the Galois-extension dependency packet $E$, the group
dependency packet $G$, the fixed-base row $b$, the automorphism-action row
$a$, the composition row $m$, the inverse row $i$, the classifier row $c$, the
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row $\rho$, the finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ ledger
$\lambda$, and the exactness rows named by those packets. This is the public
obligation boundary consumed by downstream Galois and representation-facing
chapters.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet to its dependency surfaces. The Galois-extension side is
the public boundary of
\autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary}; the group side is the
operation and inverse surface named by the $\GroupUp$ certificate. These two
dependency projections account for the extension carrier, group operation,
classifier, stability, ledger, and exactness rows available to the
Galois-group packet.

The fixed-base carrier obligation is supplied by
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-fixed-base-carrier-obligation}. The composition and
inverse obligation is supplied by
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-and-inverse-obligation}. Therefore every
public row is a projection of $E$, $G$, $b$, $a$, $m$, $i$, $c$, $\rho$, or
$\lambda$.

Conversely, each listed row is a field of the
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet} packet and is named
by the public $\GaloisGroupUp$ interface. Repacking those rows gives the
carrier, operation, inverse, classifier, provenance, ledger, and exactness
surface needed by downstream Galois and representation-facing uses, so no
hidden host automorphism datum is part of the public boundary.
\end{proof}
