\begin{theorem}[RealBall radius transport]
\label{thm:realball-radius-transport}
Let $B=(c,r,W,P,L)$ be an accepted $\RealBallUp$ carrier, and let $B'=(c',r',W',P',L')$ be obtained by displayed $\hsame$ transport of the $\RealUp$ center row, the positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius row, the visible $\DyadicIntervalUp$ window rows, and the provenance and ledger rows. If the same $\Cont$ routes named by $P$ and $P'$ preserve the $\RegSeqRat$ and $\StreamNameUp$ reads of the center, then $B'$ is again an accepted $\RealBallUp$ carrier.

The transported classifier reads the same finite dyadic-radius neighborhood surface: it changes only the displayed representatives of $c$, $r$, $W$, $P$, and $L$, and it introduces no Dedekind cut, trichotomy row, completeness datum, host real ball, or open-cover topology.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
