\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EgorovUp$}

Following Egorov and Bishop--Bridges constructive measure theory, the $\EgorovUp$ packet records almost-everywhere convergence as finite exceptional-set and finite-window uniformity data rather than as a host measure-theoretic selector. It sits after the measurable carrier discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-measure-namecert}, the probability-space ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-probspace-namecert}, the random-variable preimage surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-randomvar-namecert}, the stream observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular-rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The carrier keeps every convergence claim inside a displayed finite budget and exports no selected exceptional set, ambient almost-everywhere equality predicate, or completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Egorov carrier]\label{def:egorov-carrier}
An $\EgorovUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(M,\Omega,F,X,S,R,A,W,U,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the ambient $\MeasureUp$ row whose measurable-set ledger supplies the finite exceptional-set budget.
\item $\Omega$ is the $\ProbSpaceUp$ normalization row used to read the budget as a probability-bound request.
\item $F$ is the finite family of $\RandomVarUp$ rows whose pointwise convergence boundary is being inspected.
\item $X$ is the limiting $\RandomVarUp$ row, kept as a named random-variable packet rather than as a host almost-everywhere equivalence class.
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation schedule for the family $F$ and the limit row $X$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback route that turns each observed real value into a regular-rational comparison surface.
\item $A$ is the finite exceptional-window row. It records the displayed measurable approximants, their finite measure budget, and the containment checks used by later uniformity reads.
\item $W$ is the finite convergence window of indices, precision requests, and admissible observation sites outside $A$.
\item $U$ is the uniformity row asserting that all reads in $W$ meet the displayed regular-rational tolerance route outside the recorded exceptional window.
\item $L$ is the measure ledger connecting $A$ to $M$ and $\Omega$ without selecting a countable exceptional set.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the measure, probability, random-variable, stream, regular-rational, exceptional-window, uniformity, ledger, replay, provenance, and local-name rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the finite route
$$
M,\Omega,F,X,S,R,A,W,U,L \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EgorovUp$, $\MeasureUp$, $\ProbSpaceUp$, $\RandomVarUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EgorovUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,\Omega,F,X,S,R,A,W,U,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for full measure convergence theorems, countable choice, selected exceptional sets, host almost-everywhere equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Egorov NameCert obligations]\label{thm:egorov-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EgorovUp$ carrier
$$
E=(M,\Omega,F,X,S,R,A,W,U,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\EgorovUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the measure row, probability row, finite random-variable family, limit random-variable row, finite stream observation schedule, regular-rational readback route, exceptional-window row, finite convergence window, uniformity row, measure ledger, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every public convergence read must pass through $A,W,U,L$ before it can be exported as a local name.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier in \autoref{def:egorov-carrier}. The only convergence-facing rows are the exceptional-window row $A$, the finite convergence window $W$, the uniformity row $U$, and the measure ledger $L$.

The rows $M,\Omega,F,X,S,R$ supply the measurable, probabilistic, random-variable, stream, and regular-rational dependency surface on which those four rows are interpreted. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly the same finite route.

Because no row stores a selected countable exceptional set, a host almost-everywhere equality predicate, a full measure convergence theorem, or an ambient completeness principle, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Egorov finite-window uniformity]\label{thm:egorov-finite-window-uniformity}
For every accepted $\EgorovUp$ carrier $E$, any public uniform-convergence read of $E$ is determined by the displayed finite convergence window $W$ together with the regular-rational readback route $R$ and the uniformity row $U$. Refining the observation schedule through the same $R,W,U$ leaves the public verdict unchanged outside the exceptional window $A$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The finite convergence window $W$ lists the only indices, precision requests, and observation sites that the classifier may inspect. Each such request is evaluated through the regular-rational readback row $R$ and then compared by the uniformity row $U$.

If the observation schedule is refined while the same $R,W,U$ remain displayed, the classifier receives the same finite comparison data for every public read. The exceptional-window row $A$ fixes the sites that are not part of the uniformity read, so the verdict outside $A$ is unchanged.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Egorov measure ledger nonescape]\label{thm:egorov-measure-ledger-nonescape}
For every accepted $\EgorovUp$ carrier $E$, the measure information exported by the packet is exactly the finite ledger $L$ attached to the exceptional-window row $A$ and the ambient rows $M,\Omega$. No consumer of the local $\NameCert_{\EgorovUp}$ may replace $L$ by a selected exceptional set, a countable null-set union, host almost-everywhere equality, or an ambient $\RealUp$ completeness argument.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The measure-facing coordinates of the carrier are $M,\Omega,A,L$. The ledger $L$ records the finite budget and containment checks for the displayed exceptional window $A$ against the ambient measure and probability rows.

All replay into the public certificate passes through $C$, whose source includes $L$ but contains no row for a selected exceptional set, countable null-set union, host almost-everywhere equality, or completeness of $\RealUp$. Thus the local name can export only the finite measure ledger already present in the carrier.
\end{proof}
