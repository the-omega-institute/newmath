\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ProperMetricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-propermetric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Burago--Burago--Ivanov, the $\ProperMetricUp$ packet records constructive properness as compactness of displayed closed metric balls, not as open-cover compactness or choice-selected compactness. It reads the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the center-radius boundary rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricclosedball-namecert}, the compact certificate surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the completion handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, and the located compact boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedcompact-namecert}. The chapter fixes the traditional proper-metric interface needed by compactness and completion consumers while keeping every witness inside finite BEDC carrier rows.

\begin{definition}[ProperMetric carrier]
\label{def:proper-metric-carrier}
A $\ProperMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(X,B,K,L,T,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ row, including the point classifier, distance-witness source rows, metric-law ledger, and finite observation histories used by all downstream closed-ball reads.
\item $B$ is a radius-indexed family of $\MetricClosedBallUp$ rows. For every displayed center row $c$ and every displayed nonnegative $\RatUp$ or $\RealUp$ radius row $r$, the entry $B(c,r)$ records the closed membership comparison $d(c,x)\leq_{\mathsf R} r$ through the center-radius surface of \autoref{def:metric-closed-ball-carrier}.
\item $K$ assigns to each displayed closed ball $B(c,r)$ a $\CompactMetricUp$ witness row over the induced metric surface of that ball. This is the constructive properness datum: compactness is carried by explicit compact-metric rows for the shown balls.
\item $L$ records the $\LocatedCompactUp$ row for each displayed compact closed ball, exposing finite-net and located-distance data when a consumer needs located compactness rather than only compactness.
\item $T$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff row. It states that the complete-metric consumer of a proper closed-ball window reads only the metric source row $X$, the closed-ball row $B(c,r)$, and the compact witness $K(c,r)$ before forming its completion-facing route.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the metric carrier, centers, radii, closed-ball membership rows, compact witnesses, located compact rows, and complete-metric handoff rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from metric distance evaluation to closed-ball membership, from closed-ball membership to compact-metric witnesses, from compact witnesses to located compact reads, and from those rows to complete-metric handoff.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ProperMetricUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\MetricClosedBallUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\LocatedCompactUp$, $\RatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ProperMetricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The accepted carrier contains no open-cover compactness field, no host-selected center or radius, no quotient equality test on metric points, and no ambient real-completeness coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ProperMetric NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:proper-metric-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted packet $P=(X,B,K,L,T,H,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ProperMetricUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of carrier admission, displayed radius-window indexing, closed-ball membership transport, compact witness transport for every displayed closed ball, located compact handoff, complete-metric handoff, provenance non-escape, and local naming for the rows $X,B,K,L,T,H,C,Q,N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProperMetricUp.ProperMetricCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the displayed packet of \autoref{def:proper-metric-carrier}. The metric row $X$ supplies the point classifier and distance-witness rows, while $B$ exposes only closed-ball membership comparisons indexed by shown centers and radii.

Radius-window indexing is finite and internal to the packet. Induct over the displayed list of center-radius windows. At each window, project the corresponding $\MetricClosedBallUp$ row and its compact witness $K(c,r)$; no host choice of centers or radii is used.

Transport is componentwise. The ledger $H$ moves $X$, each $B(c,r)$, each $K(c,r)$, each located compact row $L(c,r)$, and the handoff row $T(c,r)$ along the displayed $\hsame$ routes, while $C$ replays the required continuation routes.

Provenance and naming are exhausted by $Q$ and $N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, quotient equality, selected centers, or ambient real completeness, none of those principles can appear in the local obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ProperMetric closed-ball compactness]
\label{thm:proper-metric-closed-ball-compactness}
Every displayed closed ball in an accepted $\ProperMetricUp$ packet carries its compactness through the compact witness row already indexed by that ball:
$$
B(c,r)\longmapsto K(c,r).
$$
Thus a closed-ball compactness consumer reads the $\MetricSpaceUp$ source row, the $\MetricClosedBallUp$ membership row, the $\CompactMetricUp$ witness row, and their $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transports, and it receives no open-cover compactness proof, selected center, quotient point equality, or ambient real-completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProperMetricUp.ProperMetricCarrier\_closed\_ball\_compactness}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by induction over the displayed finite radius-window list in the carrier. The empty list exports no closed-ball compactness claim.

For a cons window $(c,r)$, project the row $B(c,r)$ and then project the paired compact witness $K(c,r)$. The route from $B(c,r)$ to $K(c,r)$ is one of the displayed $\Cont$ entries in $C$, and the equality-sensitive movement of the same rows is one of the componentwise $\hsame$ entries in $H$.

The induction hypothesis covers the remaining windows. Repacking the head route with the recursive routes gives exactly the family of displayed closed-ball compactness witnesses and no additional compactness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ProperMetric complete and located handoff]
\label{thm:proper-metric-complete-located-handoff}
For an accepted $\ProperMetricUp$ packet, every complete-metric or located-compact consumer of a displayed closed ball factors through
$$
X,\quad B(c,r),\quad K(c,r),\quad L(c,r),\quad T(c,r),\quad H,\quad C,\quad Q,\quad N .
$$
The handoff is therefore componentwise over the displayed closed-ball compact witnesses and does not import general Heine--Borel compactness, open-cover compactness, arbitrary properness beyond the shown windows, host choice of centers, quotient equality, or ambient real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a displayed closed-ball window $(c,r)$. The metric source row $X$ supplies the distance data, and $B(c,r)$ supplies the non-strict center-radius membership comparison.

Next read the compact witness $K(c,r)$. A located-compact consumer continues through $L(c,r)$, while a complete-metric consumer continues through $T(c,r)$. Both routes are already named in $C$ and transported by $H$.

The handoff is exact because $Q$ names only the cited sibling certificates and $N$ names only the local $\ProperMetricUp$ packet. No other row is available from the carrier, so the excluded classical and ambient principles cannot be recovered by a downstream consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[ProperMetric radius-window root]
\label{def:proper-metric-radius-window-root}
For an accepted $\ProperMetricUp$ packet $P=(X,B,K,L,T,H,C,Q,N)$, the radius-window root surface is the finite row family
$$
R_P(c,r)=\bigl(X,\ B(c,r),\ K(c,r),\ L(c,r),\ T(c,r),\ H,\ C,\ Q,\ N\bigr)
$$
indexed by the displayed center-radius windows of the carrier. A root row is accepted only when $B(c,r)$ is the $\MetricClosedBallUp$ row over the $\MetricUp$ source $X$, $K(c,r)$ is the paired $\CompactMetricUp$ witness, $L(c,r)$ is the paired $\LocatedCompactUp$ handoff, $T(c,r)$ is the paired $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff, and the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows certify the componentwise route.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ProperMetric radius-window root soundness]
\label{thm:proper-metric-radius-window-root-soundness}
For every accepted $\ProperMetricUp$ packet $P=(X,B,K,L,T,H,C,Q,N)$ and every displayed radius-window row $(c,r)$, the root row $R_P(c,r)$ is a valid finite obligation surface for closed-ball properness. Its consumers may read the $\MetricUp$ source, the $\MetricClosedBallUp$ membership row, the $\CompactMetricUp$ witness, the $\LocatedCompactUp$ handoff, the $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff, and the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, and no other properness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:proper-metric-carrier}. The displayed window $(c,r)$ supplies $B(c,r)$, and the carrier pairs that row with $K(c,r)$, $L(c,r)$, and $T(c,r)$.

Apply \autoref{thm:proper-metric-closed-ball-compactness}. It makes the route from the $\MetricClosedBallUp$ membership row to the $\CompactMetricUp$ witness a displayed finite route, transported by $H$ and replayed by $C$.

Apply \autoref{thm:proper-metric-complete-located-handoff}. The located compact and complete metric consumers remain attached to the same closed-ball and compact-witness rows. The provenance row $Q$ and local naming row $N$ then exhaust the accepted root row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ProperMetric radius-window non-escape]
\label{thm:proper-metric-radius-window-nonescape}
No accepted radius-window root row $R_P(c,r)$ exports arbitrary properness beyond the displayed window. In particular, it does not provide an open-cover compactness theorem, a general Heine--Borel theorem, host choice of centers or radii, quotient equality on metric points, or ambient real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{def:proper-metric-radius-window-root}. The root row contains only $X,B(c,r),K(c,r),L(c,r),T(c,r),H,C,Q,N$, all indexed by the displayed center-radius window.

Use \autoref{thm:proper-metric-radius-window-root-soundness}. It identifies exactly which rows a consumer may read from the root surface, and all of them are already components of the accepted $\ProperMetricUp$ packet.

The carrier of \autoref{def:proper-metric-carrier} explicitly lacks open-cover compactness, host-selected centers, quotient equality, and ambient real-completeness coordinates. Since $Q$ and $N$ name only the same finite rows, none of the excluded principles can escape from the radius-window surface.
\end{proof}
