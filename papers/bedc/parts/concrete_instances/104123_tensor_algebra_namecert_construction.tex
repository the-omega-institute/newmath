\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TensorAlgebraUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-tensor-algebra-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Mac Lane, the $\TensorAlgebraUp$ packet records the free associative algebra generated by a displayed module or vector-space carrier through finite tensor words. It is grounded in the scalar ring rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ring-namecert}, the module carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-module-namecert}, the vector-space specialization of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}, and the bilinear tensor-product surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tensorproduct-namecert}. The packet is a BEDC NameCert carrier for finite words and universal handoff rows; it is not a quotient primitive, a host free algebra, an infinite direct sum, or a selected basis construction.

\begin{definition}[Tensor algebra carrier]
\label{def:tensor-algebra-carrier}
A $\TensorAlgebraUp$ carrier over a displayed $\ModuleUp$ row $V$ and scalar $\RingUp$ row $R$ is a finite $\BHist$ tensor-word packet
$$
T=(R,V,W,Q,U,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the scalar $\RingUp$ source row, and $V$ is the $\ModuleUp$ source row; when the scalar source is a field, the same packet may be read through $\VecSpaceUp$.
\item $W$ is the finite word ledger, whose entries are displayed tensor words of lengths $0,1,2,\ldots,n$ over carried endpoints of $V$.
\item $Q$ is the tensor-product row family: each positive-length word is admitted by iterated $\TensorProductUp$ rows, and each adjacent concatenation step displays the corresponding bilinear factorization.
\item $U$ is the unit row, naming the empty tensor word as the scalar unit from $R$.
\item $A$ is the concatenation row, sending two displayed words to their concatenated tensor word and recording associativity only as displayed word reassociation.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for scalar rows, module or vector endpoints, tensor-product rows, unit rows, concatenation rows, and word classifiers.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from source endpoints through tensor-product formation, unit insertion, and word concatenation.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TensorAlgebraUp$, $\TensorProductUp$, $\ModuleUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\RingUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TensorAlgebraUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only finite tensor-word data and the displayed universal handoff rows below. It does not add a quotient primitive, a host free algebra, a basis selector, an infinite coproduct, or an ambient algebra library object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Tensor algebra NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:tensor-algebra-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:tensor-algebra-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\TensorAlgebraUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $T=(R,V,W,Q,U,A,H,C,P,N)$, scalar admission through $R$, module or vector-space endpoint admission through $V$, finite word admission through $W$, iterated tensor-product admission through $Q$, unit admission through $U$, concatenation admission through $A$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows of \autoref{def:tensor-algebra-carrier}. The scalar source, module or vector source, finite word ledger, tensor-product family, unit row, and concatenation row are displayed before any consumer sees an algebra operation.

The tensor-product obligations come from the visible $Q$ row. Each nonempty word is built by finite iteration of the already named $\TensorProductUp$ surface, so bilinear handoff data are read at every adjacent tensoring step.

The structural rows close the certificate. Transport by $H$, replay by $C$, provenance by $P$, and naming by $N$ preserve only the displayed word packet; no quotient carrier, host free algebra, selected basis, or infinite direct-sum row is exposed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tensor algebra tensor-product universal handoff]
\label{thm:tensor-algebra-tensorproduct-universal-handoff}
Every multilinear or algebra consumer accepted by a $\TensorAlgebraUp$ packet must read a finite tensor word through
$$
R,V,W,Q,U,A,H,C,P,N.
$$
The read first consumes the scalar $\RingUp$ row and the $\ModuleUp$ or $\VecSpaceUp$ source row, then the finite word ledger, then the iterated $\TensorProductUp$ rows and the unit or concatenation row needed for the displayed word. The consumer receives the universal tensor-product handoff carried by $Q$ and $A$, not a quotient primitive, host free algebra equality, selected basis expansion, infinite direct sum, or ambient algebra object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:tensor-algebra-namecert-obligations}. It places $Q$ after the scalar and module or vector endpoints and before the unit and concatenation rows that form algebra words.

For a length-one word, the handoff is just the source row $V$ with transport and replay supplied by $H$ and $C$. For a longer word, repeatedly read the adjacent $\TensorProductUp$ rows in $Q$ and then use $A$ to expose the concatenated tensor word.

The universal surface is therefore the displayed finite factorization route. Since the carrier has no quotient primitive, host free algebra object, basis selector, or infinite direct-sum coordinate, the consumer cannot recover any of those structures from the local name $N$.
\end{proof}
