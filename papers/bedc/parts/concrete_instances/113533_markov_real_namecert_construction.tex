\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MarkovRealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-markov-real-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Markov and Kushner on constructive recursive real analysis, the $\MarkovRealUp$ packet records a Markov-school effective real inspection boundary as finite BEDC data. It is read beside the effective source rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-computablereal-namecert}, the Markov-sensitive refusal row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-markov-principle-boundary-namecert}, the constructive trichotomy boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-constructive-real-trichotomy-boundary-namecert}, the regular-rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the stream-window discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet exposes the finite rows through which a Markov-school inspection may be replayed, while keeping Markov's principle, real trichotomy, stream quotients, selected representatives, countable choice, and ambient completeness outside the BEDC kernel.

\begin{definition}[MarkovReal carrier]
\label{def:markov-real-carrier}
A $\MarkovRealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(C,B,A,S,R,D,E,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is the $\ComputableRealUp$ source row naming the effective real presentation whose finite observations are being inspected.
\item $B$ is the $\MarkovPrincipleBoundaryUp$ inspection/refusal row that bounds any use of negative evidence.
\item $A$ is the apartness-or-trichotomy-boundary row supplied by $\ConstructiveRealTrichotomyBoundaryUp$ for displayed comparison evidence and refusal.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window through which the inspection reads the effective source.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row exposed by the window.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger naming the endpoint and margin rows used by the readback.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the effective source, boundary, window, readback, and tolerance rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the effective source, Markov boundary, trichotomy boundary, stream window, regular readback, dyadic ledger, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Markov-school inspection request through $C,B,A,S,R,D,E$.
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MarkovRealUp$, $\ComputableRealUp$, $\MarkovPrincipleBoundaryUp$, $\ConstructiveRealTrichotomyBoundaryUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MarkovRealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for Markov's principle as an imported axiom, constructive real trichotomy, quotient stream equality, selected representatives, countable choice, selected limits, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MarkovReal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:markov-real-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MarkovRealUp$ carrier
$$
M=(C,B,A,S,R,D,E,H,K,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\MarkovRealUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed effective source, Markov boundary, trichotomy boundary, finite window, readback, tolerance ledger, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item effective-window exposure, requiring every public observation of $C$ to pass through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$;
\item Markov-boundary non-escape, requiring every use of negative evidence to cite $B$ rather than a global Markov rule;
\item apartness/refusal compatibility, requiring every comparison-facing inspection to pass through the displayed row $A$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, requiring each inspected rational value to be exposed through $R$ before any real-facing conclusion is public;
\item $\RealUp$ seal non-escape, requiring the seal $E$ to consume only the displayed readback and tolerance rows;
\item componentwise transport through $H$;
\item displayed continuation replay through $K$;
\item package provenance through $P$;
\item local naming through $N$.
\end{enumerate}
No Markov-real naming obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:markov-real-carrier}. The tuple displays the effective source, the two boundary rows, the finite window, the regular-rational readback, the dyadic tolerance ledger, and the real seal before it lists transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

The effective-window and readback obligations follow from the positions of $S$ and $R$. A consumer cannot inspect the effective source $C$ without first naming a finite stream window $S$, and the rational data read from that window is public only through the regular-sequence row $R$ and the dyadic ledger $D$.

The Markov-boundary and apartness/refusal obligations are the boundary rows $B$ and $A$. They record the finite evidence or refusal that an inspection may cite, but they do not add Markov's principle or trichotomy as ambient rules.

The remaining rows are componentwise. Transport is exactly $H$, replay is exactly $K$, provenance is exactly $P$, and the local public name is exactly $N$. Since the carrier contains no additional coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local $\NameCert_{\MarkovRealUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MarkovReal RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:markov-real-regseqrat-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing $\MarkovRealUp$ read factors through the displayed route
$$
\ComputableRealUp \to \MarkovPrincipleBoundaryUp \to \StreamNameUp \to \RegSeqRatUp \to \DyadicRatCoreUp \to \RealUp
$$
before any inspection conclusion is public. In particular, the $\MarkovRealUp$ packet exposes no direct path from an effective real source to a real-facing Markov conclusion that bypasses the boundary, finite window, regular-rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, and real seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let $M=(C,B,A,S,R,D,E,H,K,P,N)$ be an accepted carrier. The source row $C$ names the effective real presentation, while $B$ bounds the Markov-sensitive inspection that may be performed on it.

Any actual observation must next pass through the finite window $S$. The read values are not public as a completed real object; they are exposed through the regular-rational row $R$ and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$.

Only after those rows have been displayed may the real seal $E$ be cited. Transport $H$ and replay $K$ preserve the same route componentwise, while $P$ and $N$ repackage it as the public NameCert surface. Thus every Real-facing read factors through the stated chain before it can support an inspection conclusion.
\end{proof}
