\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FastCauchySubsequenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-fastcauchysubsequence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\FastCauchySubsequenceUp$ packet records the finite extraction route from inspected Cauchy-modulus data to a fast regular subsequence. It reads the modulus surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, the fast-rate route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fastcauchy-namecert}, the selector ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cofinalsubsequence-namecert}, the regular subsequence handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-subsequence-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The bridge is a local L10 NameCert object. It turns displayed Cauchy-modulus inspections into a cofinal fast subsequence route, then hands that route to the existing regular-subsequence and regular-rational surfaces without importing host subsequence equality, arbitrary countable choice, quotient Cauchy classes, selected limits, trichotomy, or real completeness.

\begin{definition}[FastCauchySubsequence carrier]
\label{def:fast-cauchy-subsequence-carrier}
A $\FastCauchySubsequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(S,M,Q,F,R,W,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source $\StreamNameUp$ or Cauchy-name row whose windows are inspected before any subsequence is read;
\item $M$ is the accepted $\CauchyModulusUp$ row recording the inspected modulus bounds for $S$;
\item $Q$ is the $\CofinalSubsequenceUp$ selector row, including the finite monotone-cofinal ledger used to choose later windows;
\item $F$ is the $\FastCauchyUp$ fast-rate row obtained from the inspected modulus data along $Q$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegularCauchySubsequenceUp$ handoff row that repacks the selected fast windows as a regular subsequence surface;
\item $W$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row determined by the same selected windows, radius data, and fast-rate ledger;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $R$ and $W$ have been projected;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,M,Q,F,R,W,E$ and the naming support rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from source windows to modulus inspection, cofinal selection, fast-rate routing, regular-subsequence handoff, regular rational readback, and real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\CofinalSubsequenceUp$, $\FastCauchyUp$, $\RegularCauchySubsequenceUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FastCauchySubsequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two carriers only through these displayed rows and stores no host subsequence equality, arbitrary countable-choice functional, quotient Cauchy class, selected limit, trichotomy row, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FastCauchySubsequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:fast-cauchy-subsequence-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\FastCauchySubsequenceUp$ carrier $A=(S,M,Q,F,R,W,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FastCauchySubsequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed source row, modulus row, selector row, fast-rate row, regular-subsequence row, regular rational readback, real seal, transports, replay routes, provenance, and local naming row;
\item modulus coverage, requiring every selected window used by the downstream route to be covered by the inspected bounds in $M$;
\item cofinal selector admission for $Q$, including the displayed monotone-cofinal ledger and the absence of a host-level choice selector;
\item fast-rate exactness, requiring $F$ to be read from the modulus coverage row along the selected cofinal windows;
\item regular-subsequence window handoff, requiring $R$ to consume the selected windows and transported dyadic radius data exposed by $Q$ and $F$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, requiring $W$ to be obtained from the same selected regular windows and not from an independent rational sequence;
\item $\RealUp$ seal delivery, requiring $E$ to be reached only after $R$ and $W$ have been projected;
\item $\hsame$ transport through $H$ for the source, modulus, selector, fast-rate, regular-subsequence, readback, seal, and support rows;
\item $\Cont$ replay through $C$ for the same dependency route;
\item $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$ over the cited sibling certificates and BEDC support rows;
\item local $\NameCert_{\FastCauchySubsequenceUp}$ admission through $N$.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation row exports host subsequence equality, arbitrary countable choice, quotient Cauchy classes, selected limits, trichotomy, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The tuple displays exactly the source, inspected modulus, cofinal selector, fast-rate row, regular-subsequence handoff, regular rational readback, real seal, transports, replay routes, provenance, and local naming row.

Use the modulus and selector rows first. The row $M$ supplies only inspected Cauchy-modulus bounds, and $Q$ supplies only a finite monotone-cofinal selection ledger. Thus selected downstream windows must be covered by displayed modulus data rather than by a host-level choice functional.

Next use the fast-rate and regular-subsequence rows. The row $F$ reads the chosen windows through the modulus coverage route, and $R$ repacks exactly those windows and their transported dyadic radius data as a $\RegularCauchySubsequenceUp$ handoff.

Finally read the regular rational and real-facing rows. The row $W$ is tied to the same selected windows, while $E$ is placed after $R$ and $W$ in the carrier. The transport, replay, package, and local naming rows preserve that order componentwise, and the carrier has no remaining coordinate for host equality, countable choice, quotient classes, selected limits, trichotomy, or completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FastCauchySubsequence regular route]
\label{thm:fast-cauchy-subsequence-regular-route}
Every accepted $\FastCauchySubsequenceUp$ packet factors any $\RealUp$-facing read through the inspected modulus row, cofinal selector row, fast-rate row, $\RegularCauchySubsequenceUp$ handoff, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback before the $\RealUp$ seal is exposed. In the notation of \autoref{def:fast-cauchy-subsequence-carrier}, every such read follows
$$
S,\quad M,\quad Q,\quad F,\quad R,\quad W,\quad E
$$
with the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier order. The real seal row $E$ occurs only after the source row $S$, the inspected modulus row $M$, the cofinal selector row $Q$, the fast-rate row $F$, the regular-subsequence row $R$, and the regular rational row $W$.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:fast-cauchy-subsequence-namecert-obligations}. They force modulus coverage before selection, selector admission before fast-rate readback, fast-rate exactness before the regular-subsequence handoff, and the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback before any real-facing seal is consumed.

The rows $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$ carry the same dependency order under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since no carrier coordinate stores host subsequence equality, arbitrary countable choice, quotient Cauchy classes, selected limits, trichotomy, or real completeness, every $\RealUp$-facing read factors through the displayed route.
\end{proof}
