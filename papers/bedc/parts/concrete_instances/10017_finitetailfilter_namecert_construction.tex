\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteTailFilterUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finite-tail-filter-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\FiniteTailFilterUp$ packet is the finite-filter surface shared by the Real and Completion routes. It packages the cofinal-tail information that is read by the stream-name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic rational core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational sequence surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the RealUp seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the tail budget of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadictailbound-namecert}, and the common-tail comparison packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-tail-comparison-namecert}. The packet is the BEDC finite-filter analogue of the constructive Cauchy tail-filter interface: a consumer receives a finite set of admitted tail windows, the dyadic and regular-sequence rows attached to those windows, and the seal-facing route that preserves the same cofinal-tail packet.

\begin{definition}[FiniteTailFilter carrier]
\label{def:finite-tail-filter-carrier}
A $\FiniteTailFilterUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(S,D,R,B,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger whose entries form the admitted tail filter base;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the admitted windows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the same admitted windows and dyadic ledger;
\item $B$ is the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ tail-budget row that records the finite threshold and tolerance data used by the filter;
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyTailComparisonUp$ common-tail comparison row read through the same window ledger and dyadic ledger;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached after the window, dyadic, regular-sequence, tail-budget, and common-tail comparison rows have all been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,D,R,B,Q,E,C,P,N$;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes for window readback, dyadic tolerance use, regular-rational readback, tail-budget consumption, common-tail comparison, and RealUp seal consumption;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicTailBoundUp$, $\CauchyTailComparisonUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteTailFilterUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through the displayed rows $S,D,R,B,Q,E,H,C,P,N$ and their recorded read routes.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTailFilter NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-tail-filter-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-tail-filter-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteTailFilterUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
F=(S,D,R,B,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
window-filter exposure through $S$, dyadic tolerance exposure through $D$, regular-sequence readback through $R$, tail-budget exposure through $B$, common-tail comparison through $Q$, RealUp seal exposure through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The rows $S,D,R,B,Q,E,H,C,P,N$ are the only coordinates of the finite packet, so carrier admission is the assertion that these rows are assembled as one $\BHist$ surface.

Read the analytic rows in order. The window ledger $S$ supplies the finite filter base, the dyadic ledger $D$ supplies the tolerance rows attached to that base, and the regular readback $R$ is the regular rational interpretation of the same finite windows.

The tail-budget row $B$ and the common-tail comparison row $Q$ then read the same $S,D,R$ packet rather than separate windows or separate tolerances. The RealUp seal $E$ is exposed only after those rows have been displayed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, authenticate, and name the same packet. Hence the listed coordinates exhaust the local naming surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTailFilter non-escape]
\label{thm:finite-tail-filter-nonescape}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\FiniteTailFilterUp$ packet receives only the finite surface
$$
S,D,R,B,Q,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
It receives no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, ambient completeness datum, metric-completeness datum, countable-choice row, or standard bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-tail-filter-carrier}. The analytic coordinates are the finite window ledger, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular-sequence readback, tail-budget row, common-tail comparison row, and RealUp seal row. The remaining coordinates are transport, continuation, package provenance, and local naming rows for that same packet.

Apply the NameCert obligation theorem. Any consumer route must pass through the displayed coordinates and their recorded $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ witnesses.

None of the excluded objects is a coordinate of the carrier or a row certified by the local naming surface. Therefore an accepted consumer cannot recover quotient stream equality, a selected limit, host real equality, ambient completeness, metric-completeness data, countable choice, or a standard bridge row from the finite filter packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTailFilter tail-window exactness]
\label{thm:finite-tail-filter-tail-window-exactness}
For every accepted $\FiniteTailFilterUp$ carrier
$$
F=(S,D,R,B,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the tail windows consumed by the $\DyadicTailBoundUp$ row $B$, the $\CauchyTailComparisonUp$ row $Q$, and the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ are exactly the windows displayed in $S$ with the dyadic tolerances displayed in $D$. No accepted read may replace the filter base by an unrecorded window selector, split the tail-budget row from the common-tail comparison row, or substitute a separate dyadic tolerance ledger.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FiniteTailFilterUp.FiniteTailFilterCarrier\_tail\_window\_exactness}
\begin{proof}
Project $S$ and $D$ from the carrier. These are the only coordinates that display tail windows and dyadic tolerances.

Use the NameCert obligations to locate $B$ and $Q$. The tail-budget row and the common-tail comparison row are accepted only as rows of the same packet, and both are read after the $S,D,R$ surface has been displayed.

The RealUp seal row $E$ is downstream of that same packet. Its continuation route in $C$ can replay the displayed rows, but it has no coordinate for a second filter base or a second tolerance ledger.

Thus the visible tail-window packet is exact: all accepted budget, comparison, and seal reads use $S$ and $D$ as displayed.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

Apply tail-window exactness. The tail-budget and common-tail comparison rows use the same $S,D$ window packet, so a seal-facing read cannot detach the RealUp row from the cofinal-tail filter base.

Use the NameCert obligation theorem to read the remaining route data. The $\hsame$ rows in $H$, the $\Cont$ routes in $C$, the package row $P$, and the local naming row $N$ certify the displayed handoff and no larger completion structure.

Finally apply non-escape. Since the carrier has no excluded coordinate, the Real and Completion consumers receive the finite route $S,D,R,B,Q,H,C,P,N$ before reaching $E$ and nothing outside that route.
\end{proof}

Use tail-window exactness, \autoref{thm:finite-tail-filter-tail-window-exactness}, to identify the common window and tolerance packet consumed by the budget, comparison, and seal-facing rows.
