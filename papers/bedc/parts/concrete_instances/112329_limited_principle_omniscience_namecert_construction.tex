Following Brouwer, Bishop--Bridges, and Troelstra--van Dalen on constructive omniscience boundaries, the $\LimitedPrincipleOmniscienceUp$ packet names the finite alternative-inspection surface that may be discussed without adopting global LPO. It sits next to the $\LesserLimitedOmniscienceUp$ boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lesser-limited-omniscience-namecert}, the $\SpeckerSequenceUp$ obstruction surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-speckersequence-namecert}, the $\MarkovPrincipleBoundaryUp$ row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-markov-principle-boundary-namecert}, the $\FanTheoremUp$ bar-inspection boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fantheorem-namecert}, and the L10 readback faces $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$. The packet records a named boundary object only: it is not global LPO, not trichotomy for arbitrary reals, not a selected limit principle, not countable choice, not quotient equality, and not ambient Real completeness.

\begin{definition}[Limited principle of omniscience carrier]
\label{def:limited-principle-omniscience-carrier}
A $\LimitedPrincipleOmniscienceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(A,S,R,D,E,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the finite alternative row, recording only the finitely displayed binary alternatives inspected by the packet.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ row whose finite source windows are read before any alternative is compared.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row that converts the inspected stream windows into regular rational readback data.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row used by the regular rational readback and by the finite comparison budget.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached after $S,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $T$ records transport from the finite alternative row to the sibling boundaries $\LesserLimitedOmniscienceUp$, $\SpeckerSequenceUp$, $\MarkovPrincipleBoundaryUp$, and $\FanTheoremUp$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $A,S,R,D,E,T$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for stream-window inspection, regular rational readback, dyadic comparison, Real sealing, and sibling-boundary transport.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LimitedPrincipleOmniscienceUp$, $\LesserLimitedOmniscienceUp$, $\SpeckerSequenceUp$, $\MarkovPrincipleBoundaryUp$, $\FanTheoremUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LimitedPrincipleOmniscienceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $A,S,R,D,E,T,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for global LPO, WLPO, LLPO as an adopted principle, trichotomy for arbitrary reals, selected limits, countable choice, quotient equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Limited principle of omniscience NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:limited-principle-omniscience-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LimitedPrincipleOmniscienceUp$ carrier
$$
L=(A,S,R,D,E,T,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LimitedPrincipleOmniscienceUp}$ surface consists exactly of finite rows for alternative admission, $\StreamNameUp$ window inspection, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ comparison support, $\RealUp$ sealing, sibling-boundary transport through $\LesserLimitedOmniscienceUp$, $\SpeckerSequenceUp$, $\MarkovPrincipleBoundaryUp$, and $\FanTheoremUp$, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. No obligation row may be discharged by global LPO, WLPO, LLPO, trichotomy, selected limits, countable choice, quotient equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:limited-principle-omniscience-carrier}. The packet exposes the finite alternative row, the three readback rows $S,R,D$, the terminal seal $E$, sibling transport $T$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The sibling references are boundary reads, not imported omniscience principles. The rows named by $\LesserLimitedOmniscienceUp$, $\SpeckerSequenceUp$, $\MarkovPrincipleBoundaryUp$, and $\FanTheoremUp$ record where finite alternative inspection is compared with known constructive obstructions.

The L10 route is also finite. A consumer reads stream windows through $S$, regular rational data through $R$, dyadic comparison data through $D$, and only then the $\RealUp$ seal $E$.

Since no row stores global LPO, WLPO, LLPO, trichotomy, selected limits, countable choice, quotient equality, or ambient Real completeness, the NameCert obligations are exhausted by the displayed carrier coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Limited principle of omniscience Real-completion refusal]
\label{thm:limited-principle-omniscience-real-completion-refusal}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\LimitedPrincipleOmniscienceUp$ carrier must route through the finite display
$$
\StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
together with the finite alternative row $A$ and the sibling-boundary row $T$. The route may inspect named alternatives and compare them with $\LesserLimitedOmniscienceUp$, $\SpeckerSequenceUp$, $\MarkovPrincipleBoundaryUp$, and $\FanTheoremUp$, but it cannot export global LPO, real trichotomy, selected limits, countable choice, quotient equality, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the NameCert obligation theorem. It fixes the completion-facing route to $S,R,D,E$ and keeps the alternative and sibling-boundary rows visible as packet data.

The regular rational and dyadic rows are read before the Real seal. Thus the $\RealUp$ endpoint is reached only after finite stream-window inspection and finite rational comparison support have been displayed.

The refused principles are not hidden in the structural rows. Transport by $H$, replay by $C$, provenance by $P$, and naming by $N$ preserve the same displayed route and add no coordinate for global omniscience, choice, quotient equality, selected limits, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
