\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PortmanteauUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-portmanteau-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Billingsley and Prokhorov, the $\PortmanteauUp$ packet records the weak-convergence test surface that becomes visible only after tight probability data have already been exposed. It sits after the probability source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-probspace-namecert}, the pushforward distribution row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-distribution-namecert}, the event-surface measure row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-measure-namecert}, the real comparison boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the tight probability handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prokhorov-tight-probability-namecert}, and the compact metric test surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}. The chapter names a finite BEDC test packet for bounded-continuous probes; it does not assert weak compactness, choose subsequences, use countable choice, export host measures, quotient events, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Portmanteau carrier]
\label{def:portmanteau-carrier}
A $\PortmanteauUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(P,D,M,T,B,R,A,E,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ is the $\ProbSpaceUp$ source row carrying the probability surface.
\item $D$ is the $\DistributionUp$ row whose pushforward reads supply the tested laws.
\item $M$ is the $\MeasureUp$ event-surface row through which measured test preimages are inspected.
\item $T$ is the $\ProkhorovTightProbabilityUp$ handoff row already exposing tightness data before any weak-convergence test is read.
\item $B$ is the bounded-continuous test row, recorded as a displayed $\mathsf{BoundedContinuousTest}$ surface over the compact or metric test carrier.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ comparison row for the two finite test expectations or inequalities observed by the packet.
\item $A$ is the transport row aligning probability, distribution, measure, tightness, bounded-continuous test, and real-comparison reads.
\item $E$ is the finite endpoint or tolerance ledger that records which bounded-continuous probe and real comparison window are being inspected.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for every displayed row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of source admission, pushforward readback, measure inspection, tightness exposure, bounded-continuous test evaluation, real comparison, and tolerance readback.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PortmanteauUp$, $\ProbSpaceUp$, $\DistributionUp$, $\MeasureUp$, $\ProkhorovTightProbabilityUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PortmanteauUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for weak compactness, selected subsequences, countable choice, host measures, quotient events, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Portmanteau NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:portmanteau-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PortmanteauUp$ carrier $W=(P,D,M,T,B,R,A,E,H,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\PortmanteauUp}$ surface consists exactly of six obligation groups:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the probability source, distribution pushforward, event-surface measure, tightness handoff, bounded-continuous test, real comparison, transport, tolerance, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item tightness-before-testing exposure, requiring each weak-convergence test read to display the $\ProkhorovTightProbabilityUp$ row $T$ before the bounded-continuous probe is consumed;
\item bounded-continuous test admission, requiring the probe row $B$ to be read through the displayed compact or metric test surface rather than through a host function space;
\item real comparison exactness, requiring the comparison row $R$ to be read through $\RealUp$ and the tolerance ledger $E$;
\item distribution and measure compatibility, requiring $D$ and $M$ to name the same tested event or pushforward observation under the transport row $A$;
\item non-escape from weak compactness, selected subsequences, countable choice, host measures, quotient events, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No additional NameCert obligation is stored outside these finite rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:portmanteau-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the probability, distribution, measure, tightness, bounded-continuous test, real comparison, transport, tolerance, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The tightness side is already finite. A consumer must inspect $T$ before the probe row $B$ can be evaluated, so the weak-convergence test is downstream of the Prokhorov tightness handoff rather than a source of compactness by itself.

The comparison side is real-name data. The row $R$ and tolerance ledger $E$ provide the displayed real comparison, while $D,M,A$ keep pushforward and measured-event observations on the same route.

Since no carrier coordinate stores weak compactness, selected subsequences, countable choice, host measures, quotient events, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, the six displayed groups exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
