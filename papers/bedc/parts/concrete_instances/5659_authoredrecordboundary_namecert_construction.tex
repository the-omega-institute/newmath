\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AuthoredRecordBoundaryUp$}

The $\AuthoredRecordBoundaryUp$ surface makes the inscription-event vision of \autoref{ch:visions-inscription-event-as-concrete-instance} into a finite authored-record carrier. It sits after the observer-history identity row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistoryidentity-namecert}, the finite prefix scheduling row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-countableobservationschedule-namecert}, and the stream naming row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. Those siblings supply the public history, schedule, and finite name rows; this chapter records the boundary at which a mismatch notice is accepted as an authored route and then exported as a public record.

\begin{definition}[Authored-record boundary carrier]
\label{def:authored-record-boundary-carrier}
An $\AuthoredRecordBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(O,S,M,R,W,F,T,Q,V,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the observer-history row consumed from $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$;
\item $S$ is the countable finite-prefix schedule row consumed from $\CountableObservationScheduleUp$;
\item $M$ is the mismatch-notice row that identifies the finite gap between the prior record surface and the proposed authored record;
\item $R$ is the accepted-route row that binds the mismatch notice to a reusable public record route;
\item $W$ is the written public-record row;
\item $F$ is the refusal row excluding bypass reads that skip $M$ or $R$;
\item $T$ is the transport row for moving $O,S,M,R,W,F$ componentwise through displayed $\hsame$ comparisons;
\item $Q$ is the route-replay row of displayed $\Cont$ steps from prior record, through mismatch notice and accepted route, to public record;
\item $V$ is the provenance row naming the source vision, observer-history, schedule, stream-name, closed-observation-system, and inscription-acceptance-budget dependencies;
\item $H$ records the local handoff to $\ClosedObservationSystemUp$ and $\InscriptionAcceptanceBudgetUp$ consumers;
\item $C$ records the public consumer routes for authored-record reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$, $\CountableObservationScheduleUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\ClosedObservationSystemUp$, $\InscriptionAcceptanceBudgetUp$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AuthoredRecordBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier for accepted carriers compares only the displayed prior-record, mismatch-notice, accepted-route, public-record, refusal, transport, route, provenance, handoff, consumer, package, and local naming rows. It has no coordinate for private intention, raw byte authorship, host psychological identity, hidden checker state, or a record route that bypasses $M$ or $R$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Authored-record boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:authored-record-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:authored-record-boundary-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\AuthoredRecordBoundaryUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure: every accepted packet exposes exactly $A=(O,S,M,R,W,F,T,Q,V,H,C,P,N)$;
\item mismatch accountability: the public record row $W$ is admitted only after the mismatch-notice row $M$ is displayed against the prior observer-history and schedule rows $O,S$;
\item accepted-route accountability: every authored-record read passes through the accepted-route row $R$ and the route-replay row $Q$;
\item refusal stability: the refusal row $F$ is transported by $T$ and remains visible to every consumer route in $C$;
\item provenance exactness: observer-history, finite schedule, stream-name, closed-observation-system, inscription-acceptance-budget, package, and local naming dependencies are exhausted by $V,H,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuthoredRecordBoundaryUp.AuthoredRecordBoundaryTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:authored-record-boundary-carrier}. The displayed tuple lists the prior record rows, mismatch notice, accepted route, public record, refusal, transport, route replay, provenance, handoff, consumer, package, and local naming rows.

Mismatch accountability reads $M$ beside $O$ and $S$. Since $W$ is not a standalone row but the public record reached after the mismatch and route rows are displayed, an authored-record read cannot begin at the written record alone.

Accepted-route accountability reads $R$ and $Q$. The route row names the accepted articulation, and the continuation row replays the finite path from prior record through mismatch notice to the public record.

Refusal stability is the role of $F$ and $T$. The refusal row records that bypassing $M$ or $R$ is outside the carrier, and transport moves that refusal together with the other displayed rows.

Finally, provenance exactness is obtained by inspecting $V,H,P,N$. These rows name the observer-history, schedule, stream-name, closed-observation-system, inscription-acceptance-budget, package, and local certificate surfaces consumed by the boundary, with no extra source coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Authored-record boundary non-escape]
\label{thm:authored-record-boundary-nonescape}
Every public read of an accepted $\AuthoredRecordBoundaryUp$ carrier factors through the finite rows
$$
O,S,M,R,W,F,T,Q,V,H,C,P,N.
$$
A read that bypasses the mismatch-notice row $M$, bypasses the accepted-route row $R$, imports private intention, treats raw byte equality as authorship, or replaces the displayed provenance rows by a hidden host process has left the accepted carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. Its public-record coordinate is $W$, but its admission route is the displayed chain through $O,S,M,R,Q$ with refusal, transport, provenance, and consumer rows $F,T,V,H,C,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:authored-record-boundary-namecert-obligations}. The mismatch-accountability and accepted-route clauses force every authored-record read through $M$ and $R$, while the refusal-stability clause records the boundary for bypass attempts.

No coordinate of the packet stores private intention, byte-level authorship, hidden checker state, or a host process trace. Therefore every public read is exhausted by the displayed rows listed above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Authored-record boundary vision handoff]
\label{thm:authored-record-boundary-vision-handoff}
The authored-record boundary realizes the inscription-event reading of \autoref{ch:visions-inscription-event-as-concrete-instance} as a finite NameCert packet: prior record rows are supplied by $\ObserverHistoryIdentityUp$ and $\CountableObservationScheduleUp$, finite naming is supplied by $\StreamNameUp$, the closed observation side is bounded by $\ClosedObservationSystemUp$, the accepted-route budget is bounded by $\InscriptionAcceptanceBudgetUp$, and the public authored record is exported only through $M,R,W,F,T,Q,V,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the source vision. Its inscription event requires a prior-record surface, a mismatch or gap notice, an accepted route, and a public named record. These are exactly the $O,S,M,R,W$ coordinates of \autoref{def:authored-record-boundary-carrier}.

The sibling dependencies supply the finite inputs. Observer-history identity gives the public history row, countable observation schedules give finite prefix windows, StreamName gives the nameable stream surface, closed observation systems bound the prior-record side, and inscription-acceptance budgets bound the accepted route and downstream consumer side.

The non-escape theorem \autoref{thm:authored-record-boundary-nonescape} then supplies the handoff boundary. A public authored-record consumer receives only the displayed mismatch, accepted-route, record, refusal, transport, route, provenance, package, and local naming rows.
\end{proof}
