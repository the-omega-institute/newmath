\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LyapunovUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lyapunov-namecert}

The $\LyapunovUp$ interface records the finite stability ledger used by
Lyapunov arguments for displayed dynamical systems and quadratic matrix
certificates. In the classical presentation of Khalil, \emph{Nonlinear
Systems} (2002), the proof reads a positive function and a one-step or
derivative decrease condition to conclude stability in a stated neighborhood.
The BEDC certificate below keeps only the finite rows needed to name that
surface: state rows, transition rows, quadratic certificate rows,
positive-definite witnesses, one-step decrease rows, transports, continuation
routes, package provenance, and a local $\NameCert$ row.

\begin{definition}[Lyapunov finite stability carrier]
\label{def:lyapunov-bhist-carrier}
A $\LyapunovUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
(X,T,Q,P,D,H,C,G,N)
$$
with the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is a displayed state-row packet read through $\DynSystemUp$ and
$\VecSpaceUp$;
\item $T$ is a displayed transition-row packet for the sampled or one-step
state motion, with its source and target slots named by $X$;
\item $Q$ is a quadratic certificate row read through $\MatrixUp$ and the
scalar boundary of $\RealAlgOrder$;
\item $P$ is a positive-definite witness row for the displayed quadratic
certificate on the state rows carried by the packet;
\item $D$ is a one-step decrease ledger saying that the displayed successor row
has a nonincreasing, or strictly decreasing when named, quadratic value relative
to the displayed predecessor row;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for state, transition,
quadratic, positivity, and decrease rows;
\item $C$ records the $\Cont$ routes from predecessor states to successor
states and from both states to the quadratic-value comparison row;
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance naming $\DynSystemUp$, $\MatrixUp$,
$\VecSpaceUp$, $\RealAlgOrder$, and $\RiccatiUp$ as the only imported
certificate boundaries;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert$ row for the resulting finite stability
surface.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is
$\mathsf{LyapunovLedger}(X,T,Q,P,D,H,C,G,N)$. Two accepted packets classify
together only when all nine rows transport by the displayed $\hsame$ data and
follow the same $\Cont$ routes named in $G$.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LyapunovUp.taste\_gate}

\begin{theorem}[Lyapunov decrease ledger]
\label{thm:lyapunov-decrease-ledger}
Let $L=(X,T,Q,P,D,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\LyapunovUp$ carrier. The decrease
information exported by $L$ is exactly the finite row $D$: a displayed
comparison between the quadratic value of the predecessor state row and the
quadratic value of the successor state row, routed through $C$ and typed by
$\RealAlgOrder$. The packet exports no differentiability assumption, no
infinite-time convergence assertion, no analytic limit, and no external
optimization solver semantics.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:lyapunov-bhist-carrier}. The predecessor
and successor state rows are the visible endpoints of the transition row $T$.

Use the continuation routes in $C$ to read the two quadratic values from the
same displayed certificate row $Q$. The order comparison between those two
values is precisely the row $D$, and the positive-definite row $P$ supplies only
the local sign witness for $Q$ on the named state rows.

The remaining coordinates are transport, provenance, and the local name row.
None of them contains a derivative, an infinite trajectory, a limiting
convergence theorem, or a solver state, so the decrease surface is exhausted by
the finite comparison row described above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lyapunov NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:lyapunov-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\LyapunovUp}$ obligation surface consists of five finite
rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet of
\autoref{def:lyapunov-bhist-carrier};
\item classifier and stability: the componentwise $\hsame$ transport rule for
state, transition, quadratic, positivity, decrease, continuation, provenance,
and local name rows;
\item certificate ledger: the positive-definite witness row $P$ and the
one-step decrease row $D$ of
\autoref{thm:lyapunov-decrease-ledger};
\item consumer boundary: the finite stability read permitted to downstream
control packets by \autoref{thm:lyapunov-consumer-boundary};
\item provenance: the $\Pkg$ row tying the packet to $\DynSystemUp$,
$\MatrixUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\RealAlgOrder$, and $\RiccatiUp$.
\end{enumerate}
These rows form the full public seed surface for the Lyapunov certificate name.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LyapunovUp.LyapunovLedger\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row fixes every coordinate that can be named by the certificate.
The classifier is the finite membership test on those coordinates, and the
stability clause is componentwise $\hsame$ transport along the same $\Cont$
routes.

The certificate ledger consists only of the positive-definite row and the
decrease row. The consumer boundary then determines which downstream reads are
available from those rows.

Finally, the package row names every imported BEDC certificate boundary. Since
the carrier has no coordinate for analytic trajectory convergence, smooth
derivatives, basin computation, or solver output, the five listed rows exhaust
the local $\NameCert_{\LyapunovUp}$ obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lyapunov consumer boundary]
\label{thm:lyapunov-consumer-boundary}
Every consumer of $\NameCert_{\LyapunovUp}$ may read only the displayed state
rows, transition rows, quadratic certificate row, positive-definite witness,
one-step decrease ledger, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$
provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row of
\autoref{def:lyapunov-bhist-carrier}. A $\DynSystemUp$ consumer may use those
rows as a finite stability certificate, and a $\RiccatiUp$ or matrix-control
consumer may reuse the quadratic and order rows, but no consumer obtains a
basin-of-attraction computation, asymptotic convergence theorem, differentiable
flow, or external synthesis solver from the certificate alone.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read a consumer map as a projection from the accepted carrier. The
$\DynSystemUp$ projection returns the displayed state and transition rows
together with the decrease comparison.

The matrix-control projection returns the quadratic row, its positive-definite
witness, and the $\RealAlgOrder$ comparison row. The $\RiccatiUp$ projection
can reuse the same finite matrix and order coordinates as a named quadratic
ledger, without adding any Riccati recursion step.

All other objects named in the statement are absent from the carrier and from
the package provenance. They therefore cannot be read through
$\NameCert_{\LyapunovUp}$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LyapunovUp.LyapunovPacket\_consumer\_boundary}

\begin{theorem}[Lyapunov stability transport]
\label{thm:lyapunov-stability-transport}
Let $L=(X,T,Q,P,D,H,C,G,N)$ be an accepted $\LyapunovUp$ carrier and let
$L'=(X',T',Q',P',D',H',C',G',N')$ be obtained by displayed $\hsame$ transport of
all carrier rows, preserving the $\Cont$ routes named by the provenance row.
Then $L'$ is again an accepted $\LyapunovUp$ carrier, and its finite stability
read is the transported positive-definite and one-step decrease ledger over
$X'$, $T'$, and $Q'$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LyapunovUp.LyapunovPacket\_stability\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Transport the state and transition rows first. The preserved continuation
routes identify the successor and predecessor slots of $T'$ with the
transported state rows in $X'$.

Next transport the quadratic certificate and its positive-definite witness.
Because the order boundary is the same $\RealAlgOrder$ row carried by the
provenance, the transported sign witness remains attached to $Q'$ on the
transported state rows.

Finally transport the decrease ledger, package row, and local name row. Repack
the nine transported coordinates as
$\mathsf{LyapunovLedger}(X',T',Q',P',D',H',C',G',N')$. The stability read of
the new packet is exactly the transported positive-definite and decrease rows,
since the carrier has no additional stability coordinate.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LyapunovUp.LyapunovLedger\_stability\_transport}

\begin{theorem}[Lyapunov public export]
\label{thm:lyapunov-public-export}
The decrease ledger, $\NameCert_{\LyapunovUp}$ obligation surface, consumer
boundary, and stability transport export exactly the finite state, transition,
positivity, decrease, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows of
$\LyapunovUp$.

The public export contains no minimizer-existence principle, no global
asymptotic completeness theorem, no analytic regularity assertion, and no
standard-model dynamics claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier of \autoref{def:lyapunov-bhist-carrier}. The only
state and transition data are the rows $X$ and $T$, and the only certificate
data are the quadratic row, the positivity witness, and the decrease ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:lyapunov-decrease-ledger} and
\autoref{thm:lyapunov-namecert-obligation-surface}. These identify the
decrease row and the local naming surface over the displayed finite packet.

Use \autoref{thm:lyapunov-consumer-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:lyapunov-stability-transport} to expose the consumer-facing
rows and their displayed transport behavior. Since all cited rows are
projections from the same finite carrier, the excluded analytic and
standard-model claims have no coordinate in the public export.
\end{proof}
