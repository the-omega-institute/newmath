\begin{definition}[Observer approximation tower carrier]
\label{def:observer-approximation-tower-carrier}
An $\ObserverApproximationTowerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the observer-source row, recording the finite observation history whose reality-facing signature is being approximated;
\item $A$ is the mathematical approximation row, naming the visible approximation layer selected from the tower of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-approximation-tower};
\item $B$ is the $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ row that bounds Real-facing finite reads for the approximation;
\item $W$ is the $\FiniteWindowObserverUp$ row that exposes the observer's finite window before any terminal read;
\item $R$ is the $\FiniteObservationBudgetSelectorUp$ row that chooses the budgeted observation schedule;
\item $S$ is the reality-facing signature row, comparing the approximation output with the physical signature discipline of \autoref{sec:reality-constrained-signatures};
\item $G$ is the $\GapClosureBoundaryUp$ ledger row recording which approximation gaps are closed and which remain boundary data;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the observer source, approximation row, budget row, window row, selector row, signature row, gap ledger, continuation row, provenance row, and local naming row;

The classifier reads only these eleven rows. It has no coordinate for host physical identity, detached mathematical equivalence, unbounded observation, invisible smoothness transfer, quotient stream equality, or a reality-facing signature outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observer approximation tower NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-approximation-tower-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-approximation-tower-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverApproximationTowerUp}$ surface consists of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $T=(O,A,B,W,R,S,G,H,C,P,N)$;
\item observer-source and approximation-layer admission through $O$ and $A$;
\item budgeted finite observation admission through $B,W,R$;
\item reality-signature and gap-ledger accountability through $S$ and $G$;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed replay, package provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
Every terminal observer or Real-facing consumer of the tower must read through these rows before it may classify the approximation as reality-constrained.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-approximation-tower-carrier}. The admitted packet displays the observer source, approximation layer, observation budget, finite window, selector, signature, gap ledger, and structural rows.

Read the observation side next. The Real observation budget, finite-window observer, and finite observation-budget selector are separate rows of the same packet, so the terminal consumer cannot replace the finite schedule by an unbounded observation route.

Now read the reality-facing side. The signature row $S$ is present only with the gap ledger $G$, so a mathematical approximation row $A$ is not enough to certify physical adequacy unless the displayed signature and ledger rows travel with it.

Finally use $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since no other coordinate is admitted, host physical identity, detached mathematical equivalence, invisible smoothness transfer, quotient stream equality, and unregistered signature rows cannot be exported.
\end{proof}
