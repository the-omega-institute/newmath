Following Kelley and Bishop--Bridges, the $\RegularCauchyFilterSpaceUp$ packet records a regular Cauchy filter space as finite NameCert data for the L10 completion route. It sits between the Cauchy filter carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the regular Cauchy filter rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyfilter-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet feeds completion consumers by displaying regular filter-space rows before any quotient of filters, selected limit, or countable-choice principle can be invoked.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy filter space carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-filter-space-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyFilterSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(X,B,W,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the admitted point and basic-filter carrier row, retaining classifier-distinct point and filter names without quotienting them by convergence or equality;
\item $B$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ basis row, exposing the finite filter-membership and directedness observations consumed by the packet;
\item $W$ is the $\RegularCauchyFilterUp$ regularity window, recording the shrinking, containment, and Cauchy-modulus rows used to read the filter as regular;
\item $Q$ is the classifier/window exactness row, tying every accepted window request to the same point and filter classifier rows in $X,B,W$;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ inspection row for finite request windows;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rational readback row for regular window moduli and Cauchy bounds;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ sealing row reached only after $S,R,W$ have displayed the finite regular-filter readback;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all carrier, basis, regularity, exactness, window, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the same finite route;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyFilterSpaceUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\RegularCauchyFilterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local naming rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyFilterSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient filter equality, a selected representative filter, a selected limit point, countable choice, ambient separated completion, or host equality of point names.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy filter space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-filter-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyFilterSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
F=(X,B,W,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyFilterSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the point/filter carrier $X$, Cauchy filter basis $B$, regularity window $W$, classifier/window exactness row $Q$, stream windows $S$, regular rational readback $R$, real seal $E$, transport $H$, replay $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming $N$;
\item classifier/window exactness, requiring every filter-space read to use the displayed classifier rows of $X,B,W$ and the same finite request windows recorded in $Q,S$;
\item stability and transport, requiring regularity, Cauchy bounds, rational readback, and real sealing to move only componentwise through $H$ and to replay through $C$;
\item ledger nonescape, excluding quotient filters, host equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable choice, and ambient separated completion from the packet;
\item completion-consumer handoff, exporting only $X,B,W,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$ to a $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ consumer before that consumer adds its own completion rows.
\end{enumerate}
No other NameCert obligation is available from the regular Cauchy filter space packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-filter-space-carrier}. The tuple lists the point/filter carrier, Cauchy filter basis, regularity window, exactness row, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Classifier/window exactness is carried by $Q$ and $S$. A consumer first fixes the point and filter classifiers in $X,B,W$, then reads the finite request windows through $Q,S$ before asking for rational or real data.

Stability and transport are componentwise. The row $H$ transports the displayed coordinates, and the row $C$ replays the same route for consumers, so regularity and Cauchy bounds cannot be replaced by host equality or by a detached separated-completion fact.

Ledger nonescape follows from the carrier inventory. There is no coordinate for quotient filter equality, selected representatives, selected limits, countable choice, or ambient separated completion.

Finally the completion handoff is only the displayed tuple $X,B,W,Q,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$. A $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ consumer may use that finite regular-filter-space surface as input, but any completion object, quotient, or limit belongs to the consumer packet rather than to this carrier.
\end{proof}
