\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FilterLimitBasisUp$}

Following Kelley and Bourbaki on filters, convergence, and completion by directed neighbourhood data, the $\FilterLimitBasisUp$ packet records the finite basis interface used when a completion consumer asks for limit evidence before reading a terminal real-name seal. It sits after the Cauchy-filter completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfiltercompletion-namecert}, the Cauchy-filter surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}, the limit boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilterlimit-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names a traditional finite filter-basis route for limit reads; it is not a quotient filter, selected limit, host real equality, ultrafilter argument, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[FilterLimitBasis carrier]
\label{def:filter-limit-basis-carrier}
A $\FilterLimitBasisUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(Q,F,L,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ completion-boundary row whose accepted reads are still tied to finite Cauchy-filter data.
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ row exposing the saturated finite-filter surface consumed by the basis request.
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyFilterLimitUp$ limit-boundary row, read only after the same filter and basis data have been displayed.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row inspected by the basis request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the displayed windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare the readback at the requested precision.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row available only after $Q,F,L,W,R,D$ have been replayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for completion, filter, limit, window, readback, tolerance, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a limit-basis request through $Q,F,L,W,R,D,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FilterLimitBasisUp$, $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\CauchyFilterLimitUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FilterLimitBasisUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $Q,F,L,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$. There is no coordinate for quotient filters, host filter equality, ultrafilters, selected limits, countable-choice schedules, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FilterLimitBasis NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:filter-limit-basis-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FilterLimitBasisUp$ carrier $B=(Q,F,L,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FilterLimitBasisUp}$ surface consists exactly of these obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed Cauchy-filter completion row, Cauchy-filter row, limit-boundary row, finite stream window, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, terminal Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item completion-basis exposure, requiring every completion-facing read to consume $Q$ and $F$ before it consults $L,W,R,D$, or $E$;
\item limit compatibility, requiring the $\CauchyFilterLimitUp$ row $L$ to use the same displayed filter, window, readback, and dyadic tolerance route;
\item Real handoff, requiring the terminal seal $E$ to remain downstream of $Q,F,L,W,R,D$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$;
\item ledger refusal, excluding quotient filters, host filter equality, ultrafilters, selected limits, countable-choice schedules, ambient completeness, and bridge certificates.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:filter-limit-basis-carrier}. The displayed rows are exactly $Q,F,L,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$, so carrier admission exposes only finite completion, filter, limit, window, readback, tolerance, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and naming data.

The completion-basis route starts with $Q$ and $F$. These rows force the request to pass through the accepted Cauchy-filter completion boundary and the saturated Cauchy-filter surface before any limit-facing row can be inspected.

Next read $L,W,R,D$. The limit boundary consumes the same finite stream windows, regular rational readback, and dyadic tolerance ledger, so the packet has one displayed basis route rather than two unrelated filter presentations.

Finally the terminal seal $E$ and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same route. Since quotient filters, host equality, ultrafilters, selected limits, countable-choice schedules, ambient completeness, and bridge certificates are absent from the carrier, the listed obligations exhaust the local surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FilterLimitBasis CauchyFilterCompletion handoff]
\label{thm:filter-limit-basis-cauchyfiltercompletion-handoff}
Every $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\FilterLimitBasisUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
Q,F,L,W,R,D \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus a completion-facing read receives the displayed Cauchy-filter completion boundary, the saturated filter surface, the limit boundary, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance accounting, the terminal real-name seal, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. It receives no quotient filter, ultrafilter, selected limit, host real equality, countable-choice selector, ambient completeness theorem, or bridge certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a completion-facing consumer of $B$. The carrier admits the $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ row $Q$ only together with the finite filter and limit rows $F,L$.

Replay the displayed continuation row $C$. The request consumes $Q$ and $F$, then passes through the limit row $L$, finite windows $W$, regular readback $R$, and dyadic tolerance ledger $D$ before the terminal seal $E$ is available.

The structural rows do not create an additional completion object. Transport through $H$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ record the same finite route, so every $\CauchyFilterCompletionUp$ or $\RealUp$ consumer receives only that route.
\end{proof}
