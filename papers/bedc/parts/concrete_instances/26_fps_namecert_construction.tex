\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\FormalPowerSeriesUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-fps-namecert}

The formal power series interface \(\FormalPowerSeriesUp\) has certificate obligations over a scalar ring from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ring-namecert} as functions \(\mathbb{N} \to R\), interpreted as coefficient sequences without termination. The classifier is pointwise sameness on every index. Formal series addition and Cauchy product are derived operations governed by the ring's certificate fields. Convergence is not asserted: this is the formal carrier, not analytic series.

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[FormalPowerSeries carrier]
\label{def:fps-carrier}
Use coefficient functions \(\NatUp\to R\) over a certified scalar ring \(R\).
\end{definition}

\section{Concrete singleton empty-history formal power series instance}
\label{sec:fps-concrete-singleton-instance}

\begin{definition}[Singleton empty-history formal power series instance]
\label{def:singleton-empty-history-fps-instance}
Let the series-code carrier be the singleton history source $A_{\emp}$ of
Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source} at $a_0=\emp$. Let the
scalar source be the singleton empty-history ring of
Definition~\ref{def:singleton-empty-history-ring-instance}. Use the classifier
$$
\rho_{\emp}(h,k)\Longleftrightarrow
A_{\emp}(h)\land A_{\emp}(k)\land\hsame(h,k).
$$
Define coefficient observation, zero series, series addition, and Cauchy
product as the concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$-valued constant operations
$$
\mathsf{coef}(S,n):=\emp,\qquad
0_F:=\emp,\qquad
S\oplus T:=\emp,\qquad
S\star T:=\emp.
$$
The certificate fit has ten fields:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Traditional target: the terminal formal power series algebra.
  \item BEDC source: $\mathsf{BHist}$ with the singleton scalar ring.
  \item Carrier choice: $A_{\emp}$ on series codes.
  \item Classifier choice: $\rho_{\emp}$.
  \item Stability obligations: pointwise reflexivity, addition closure,
  Cauchy-product closure, and congruence.
  \item Ledger: singleton coefficient provenance.
  \item Core theorem:
  Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-fps-laws}.
  \item Exactness predicate: singleton endpoint classification.
  \item Standard bridge: the terminal formal series object.
  \item Status: paper certificate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton empty-history formal power series laws]
\label{thm:singleton-empty-history-fps-laws}
For the singleton instance of
Definition~\ref{def:singleton-empty-history-fps-instance}, the displayed
$\FormalPowerSeriesUp$ carrier, classifier, zero series, addition operation,
and Cauchy-product operation satisfy the concrete law package:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $0_F$ is carried by $A_{\emp}$.
  \item Every coefficient query $\mathsf{coef}(S,n)$ is carried by the
  singleton scalar ring.
  \item If $A_{\emp}(S)$ and $A_{\emp}(T)$, then
  $A_{\emp}(S\oplus T)$ and $A_{\emp}(S\star T)$.
  \item $\rho_{\emp}$ is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive on carried series
  codes.
  \item $\oplus$ and $\star$ respect $\rho_{\emp}$ on carried inputs.
\end{enumerate}
No convergence field is required.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier endpoint is supplied by the singleton source certificate from
Definition~\ref{def:list-singleton-history-source} at $a_0=\emp$. The zero
series is definitionally $\emp$, so its carrier witness is the same singleton
endpoint witness.

For coefficient queries, every value of $\mathsf{coef}(S,n)$ is
definitionally $\emp$. The scalar side is the singleton empty-history ring of
Definition~\ref{def:singleton-empty-history-ring-instance}, whose laws are
given by Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-ring-laws}. Hence each
coefficient observation lands in the certified scalar carrier.

For addition and Cauchy-product closure, the displayed operations return
$\emp$ for every pair of inputs. The singleton carrier witness therefore
discharges both closure obligations without inspecting the input series or the
index used in a coefficient view.

Classifier reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are inherited from the
$\hsame$ reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity laws in the FKernel, together
with the two singleton endpoint witnesses required by $\rho_{\emp}$. Operation
congruence reduces both operation results to $\emp$, so the relation component
is again $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.

The Cauchy-product certificate has no analytic convergence clause. Its
coefficient-side finite-sum reading is terminal here because every scalar
operation and every coefficient query is the singleton endpoint, already
covered by Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-ring-laws}.
\end{proof}

The $\FormalPowerSeriesUp$ schema is instantiated by this concrete
$\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier and these concrete operations, so Lean formalization
can target BHist-anchored laws rather than abstract operator fields.

\section{The certificate}

\begin{definition}[FormalPowerSeries source specification]
\label{def:fps-source-specification}
The source is the natural index source and scalar ring source.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[FormalPowerSeries pattern specification]
\label{def:fps-pattern-specification}
The pattern is infinite coefficient assignment indexed by unary natural histories.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[FormalPowerSeries classifier specification]
\label{def:fps-classifier-specification}
The classifier relates two series when all corresponding coefficients are scalar-classified the same.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[FormalPowerSeries stability certificate]
\label{def:fps-stability-certificate}
The stability certificate consists of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item pointwise reflexivity
  \item pointwise transitivity
  \item addition closure pointwise
  \item Cauchy product closure by finite sums at each coefficient index
  \item no convergence field is required
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[FormalPowerSeries ledger policy]
\label{def:fps-ledger-policy}
Record the coefficient rule and scalar ledgers at queried indices.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[\(\FormalPowerSeriesUp\) certificate obligations]
\label{def:fps-certificate-obligations}
The carrier, source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data listed above are the obligations required for the interface name \(\FormalPowerSeriesUp\).
\end{definition}

\paragraph{Primitive boundary.}
\(\FormalPowerSeriesUp\) is not a BEDC primitive. It is admitted only when those obligations are supplied.

\begin{remark}[Zero-axiom encoding note]
\label{rem:fps-zero-axiom-note}
Avoids funext by using pointwise classifier sameness.
\end{remark}
