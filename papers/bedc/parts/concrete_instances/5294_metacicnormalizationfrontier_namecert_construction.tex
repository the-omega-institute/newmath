\begin{definition}[MetaCIC normalization frontier carrier]
\label{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-carrier}
A $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $C$ is the candidate-evidence row for the inspected empty-context typed closed MetaCIC terms;
\item $K$ is the closed-candidate evidence row, recording where the closed term is actually placed into the candidate interface;
\item $E$ is the finished-normal evidence row available after bounded reduction or displayed normal-form inspection;
\item $N$ is the normal-endpoint use row consumed by closed-normal consistency routes;
\item $S$ is the subject-reduction obstruction row, kept visible whenever candidate evidence or finished-normal evidence does not erase the preservation boundary;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $C,K,E,N,S,R,P,L$ and the local packet shape;
\item $R$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes by which downstream consumers read candidate evidence, finished-normal evidence, normal endpoints, and obstruction rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:metacic-open-problems}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-open-frontier-ledger-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicnormalizationaudit-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicnormalizationauditseal-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closed-normal-route-certificate-namecert};
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is finite and obstruction-preserving. It records where a closed-normal consumer may read normalization evidence, but it does not turn the normalization frontier into a blanket closed strong-normalization theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-namecert-obligations}
Every accepted $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ packet with carrier \autoref{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-carrier} carries exactly the following local NameCert obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $F=(C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L)$ as one finite $\BHist$ packet;
\item candidate-evidence boundary: a read of candidate-mediated strong-normalization content must pass through $C$ and the closed-candidate row $K$;
\item finished-normal boundary: a read of finished-normal content must pass through $E$ before it can feed the normal-endpoint row $N$;
\item closed-normal consumer handoff: any consistency-facing read of $N$ must replay through $R$ with provenance $P$ and the local naming row $L$;
\item subject-reduction-obstruction separation: the obstruction row $S$ remains distinct from $C,K,E,N$ and is not discharged by the normalization packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:metacic-normalization-frontier-carrier}. The nine rows $C,K,E,N,S,H,R,P,L$ are the complete finite packet.

For the candidate boundary, inspect $C$ and $K$. The candidate row records the available normalization evidence, while the closed-candidate row records the point at which an empty-context closed term is admitted to that interface.

For the finished-normal boundary, inspect $E$ and $N$. The endpoint row is available to a closed-normal consumer only after the finished-normal evidence row has been read through the displayed replay route.

The handoff and separation clauses use the structural rows. The replay row $R$, provenance row $P$, and local row $L$ expose only the displayed normalization frontier, and the obstruction coordinate $S$ remains a named coordinate rather than a hidden subject-reduction theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization frontier non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-frontier-nonescape}
An accepted $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ packet exports no blanket strong-normalization theorem for all empty-context typed closed MetaCIC terms, no full closed consistency theorem, and no erased subject-reduction transport. Any closed-normal consistency consumer route must factor through the candidate-evidence rows $C,K$ or through the finished-normal row $E$ before it reads $N$; a route that bypasses both is outside the carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The only normalization evidence rows are $C,K$ and $E$, and the only closed-normal endpoint row is $N$.

The replay and provenance rows $R$ and $P$ do not add new normalization evidence. They only expose the recorded candidate-evidence route, the finished-normal route, and the local endpoint route to a consumer.

The obstruction row $S$ is separate from those positive rows. Since the packet has no coordinate for global closed strong normalization, full closed consistency, or erased subject-reduction transport, none of those objects can be exported by its local certificate.
\end{proof}
