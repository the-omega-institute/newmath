\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CandidateEvidenceSNRouteUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-candidate-evidence-sn-route-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CandidateEvidenceSNRouteUp$ packet realizes the candidate-mediated route of \autoref{ch:metacic-normalization-audit-map} as finite NameCert data. It sits beside the typed reduction normal-form packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedreductionnormalform-namecert}, the normal-form consistency seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normal-form-consistency-seal-namecert}, and the normal false exclusion packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normalfalseexclusion-namecert}: those siblings consume normal-form and consistency-facing rows, while this packet keeps the strong-normalization route tied to displayed candidate evidence.

\begin{definition}[Candidate evidence SN route carrier]
\label{def:candidate-evidence-sn-route-carrier}
A $\CandidateEvidenceSNRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(E,K,M,A,I,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the candidate evidence row naming $\mathrm{Candidate}$ witnesses and their candidate-to-strong-normalization read;
\item $K$ is the closed-candidate row naming closed type, closed term, and closed candidate evidence;
\item $M$ is the candidate-set membership row that exposes the finite membership and soundness reads used by closed typed terms;
\item $A$ is the Pi and application adequacy row, including function candidate evidence, argument candidate evidence, and the application candidate readback;
\item $I$ is the no-infinite-reduction projection row attached to the strong-normalization evidence already displayed in $E,K,M,A$;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for candidate evidence, closed-candidate evidence, membership evidence, adequacy rows, and no-infinite projections;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes from candidate evidence to strong normalization and from strong normalization to no-infinite-reduction boundaries;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over the normalization audit map, typed reduction normal forms, normal-form consistency seals, normal false exclusion, and the MetaCIC candidate evidence rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CandidateEvidenceSNRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for evidence-free global strong normalization, an unconditional application-normalization theorem, a host evaluator, quotient equality over reduction traces, or a hidden tail of reduction steps.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Candidate evidence SN route obligation surface]
\label{thm:candidate-evidence-sn-route-obligation-surface}
Every accepted $\CandidateEvidenceSNRouteUp$ packet binds exactly the obligation surface displayed in \autoref{def:candidate-evidence-sn-route-carrier}: candidate evidence through $E$, closed-candidate evidence through $K$, candidate-set membership through $M$, Pi and application adequacy through $A$, no-infinite-reduction projection through $I$, componentwise transport through $H$, route replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier coordinates. The positive route begins with $E,K,M,A$: a consumer first reads candidate evidence, closed-candidate evidence, candidate-set membership, or Pi and application adequacy before any strong-normalization consequence is visible.

The row $I$ is downstream from those evidence rows. It records no-infinite-reduction only as a projection from displayed strong-normalization evidence, so the packet never creates a second route that skips the candidate surface.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, package, and name only the listed coordinates. Since the carrier contains no further row, the displayed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Candidate evidence SN route no-infinite boundary]
\label{thm:candidate-evidence-sn-route-no-infinite-boundary}
Every no-infinite-reduction read exported by an accepted $\CandidateEvidenceSNRouteUp$ packet factors through one of the displayed candidate-mediated strong-normalization rows $E,K,M,A$ before it reaches $I$. Thus the route can feed $\TypedReductionNormalFormUp$, $\NormalFormConsistencySealUp$, and $\NormalFalseExclusionUp$ consumers, but it cannot be reread as blanket strong normalization for every closed typed MetaCIC term.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from a no-infinite-reduction read of the packet. The only no-infinite coordinate is $I$, and its input is the strong-normalization evidence already supplied by $E,K,M,A$.

Use the route replay row $C$ to trace the read backward to candidate evidence, closed-candidate evidence, candidate-set membership, or Pi and application adequacy. The transport row $H$ preserves those evidence coordinates under accepted packet agreement.

Finally send the resulting boundary to the normal-form consumers named in $P$. Each consumer receives a finite candidate-mediated route, not a host evaluator, quotient reduction equality, unconditional application-normalization theorem, or evidence-free global strong-normalization principle.
\end{proof}
