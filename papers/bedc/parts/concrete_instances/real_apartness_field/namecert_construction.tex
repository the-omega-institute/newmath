\begin{theorem}[Real apartness field NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-apartness-field-namecert-obligations}

$$

$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RealApartnessFieldUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the real seal, positive apartness row, located order row, field operation row, dyadic ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item algebraic projection, requiring every addition, multiplication, negation, zero, and one read to pass through the displayed $\FieldUp$ row $K$ and the real seal row $R$;
\item inverse admission, requiring an inverse read to consume the $\CauchyRealApartnessUp$ row $A$ and the positive dyadic gap data in $D$ before the field row $K$ exports the reciprocal branch;
\item located order compatibility, requiring the $\LocatedRealOrderUp$ row $O$ to read the same real-name and dyadic comparison data used by the apartness row;

The algebraic projection reads the $\FieldUp$ row $K$ only over the displayed real seal $R$. Thus the operation surface is the carried field row, not host real arithmetic or a quotient-real operation.

The inverse projection first reads $A$ and $D$. The apartness row supplies positive separation from zero, and the dyadic row supplies the gap and reciprocal budget; without those rows the inverse request remains at the boundary and cannot be consumed as a field operation.

Finally, $O,H,C,P,N$ keep comparison, transport, replay, provenance, and naming inside the same finite packet.
