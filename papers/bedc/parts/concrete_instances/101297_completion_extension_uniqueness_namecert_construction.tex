Following Bishop--Bridges and Kelley constructive completion, the $\CompletionExtensionUniquenessUp$ packet records the separated uniqueness boundary for uniformly continuous extensions across displayed constructive completions. It reads the source-completion evidence of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completion-namecert}, the dense route isolated by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-adjunction-namecert}, the handoff surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-continuous-extension-namecert}, the universal extension ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}, the target convergence row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, and the separated target boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}. The packet names the finite condition under which two accepted extension rows are the same extension for BEDC consumers; it does not construct quotient completions or identify host functions.

\begin{definition}[CompletionExtensionUniqueness carrier]
\label{def:completion-extension-uniqueness-carrier}
A $\CompletionExtensionUniquenessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(S,D,F,V,W,T,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source completion evidence row, exposing the $\CompletionUp$ and $\MetricCompletionUp$ data from which completion-facing source names are read.
\item $D$ is the dense source route, recording the displayed inclusion of source observations into the completion surface and the dense-row comparison used by completion consumers.
\item $F$ is the $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$ handoff row for the dense source map and its finite uniform-modulus transport.
\item $V$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ extension row attached to the same source completion evidence, dense route, and target route.
\item $W$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ target convergence row, storing the carried limit evidence reached after the extension handoff.
\item $T$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ target boundary, exposing only the finite zero-distance-to-classifier route used to compare the two carried target reads.
\item $L$ is the uniqueness ledger comparing two accepted extension packets componentwise over $S,D,F,V,W,T$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source completion evidence, dense route, extension handoff, universal-property row, complete-target convergence row, separated target boundary, uniqueness ledger, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
S,D\longmapsto F,V\longmapsto W,T\longmapsto L .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompletionExtensionUniquenessUp$, $\CompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CauchyCompletionAdjunctionUp$, $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionExtensionUniquenessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $S,D,F,V,W,T,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, host function extensionality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionExtensionUniqueness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:completion-extension-uniqueness-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompletionExtensionUniquenessUp$ carrier
$$
U=(S,D,F,V,W,T,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionExtensionUniquenessUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source completion evidence, dense source route, uniform-extension handoff, universal-property extension row, complete-target convergence row, separated target boundary, uniqueness ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item source-completion compatibility, requiring the dense route $D$ and extension handoff $F$ to read the same source completion evidence $S$ before any target-side comparison is available;
\item uniform-extension handoff, requiring each candidate extension packet to reach its target read only through the $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$ row $F$;
\item universal-property row exactness, requiring the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ row $V$ to be attached to the same source completion evidence, dense route, and target route as $F$;
\item separated uniqueness, requiring comparison of two candidate target reads to pass through the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ boundary $T$ over the complete target row $W$;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $L,H,C,P,N$ to expose no quotient completion equality, selected representatives, host function extensionality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{enumerate}
No local uniqueness obligation is stored outside these six rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:completion-extension-uniqueness-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the source completion evidence, dense route, extension handoff, universal-property row, complete-target convergence row, separated target boundary, uniqueness ledger, and structural rows.

Source-completion compatibility follows from the order of the carrier. A candidate extension read first consumes $S$ and $D$; the rows $F$ and $V$ are not available as independent target-side data, because both are recorded as handoffs from the same source completion evidence and dense route.

The uniform-extension and universal-property obligations are then read from $F$ and $V$. The row $F$ supplies the finite uniform-modulus handoff, while $V$ records the universal extension surface attached to that same route. Thus the two sibling surfaces are composed by shared carrier coordinates rather than by host-level function equality.

Separated uniqueness is the projection to $W,T,L$. The complete-target row $W$ stores the convergence evidence for the carried target reads, and $T$ is the only boundary that compares those reads. The ledger $L$ records that two accepted extension packets agreeing on $D,F,V$ are compared through this same separated target boundary.

Finally inspect $H,C,P,N$. Transport is componentwise, replay follows the displayed route, and provenance plus local naming mention only the rows listed in the carrier. Hence the local NameCert obligations export no quotient completion equality, selected representatives, host function extensionality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompletionExtensionUniqueness separated determinacy]
\label{thm:completion-extension-uniqueness-separated-determinacy}
Let
$$
U=(S,D,F,V,W,T,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
and
$$
U'=(S',D',F',V',W',T',L',H',C',P',N')
$$
be accepted $\CompletionExtensionUniquenessUp$ packets. Suppose the two packets agree on the dense source route and on the $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$ and $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ rows through the displayed componentwise $\hsame$ ledger:
$$
\hsame(D,D'),\qquad \hsame(F,F'),\qquad \hsame(V,V').
$$
Then any two exported extension reads from $U$ and $U'$ are identified only by the same $\SeparatedMetricUp$ target boundary over the carried complete-target convergence rows $W,W'$. In particular, the determinacy route does not use quotient completion equality, selected representatives, host function extensionality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the shared dense route. The hypotheses compare $D$ with $D'$ and force both accepted packets to read their source-completion data through the same displayed dense source route before any extension row is inspected.

Next compose the sibling extension surfaces. The comparison of $F$ with $F'$ aligns the $\UniformContinuousExtensionUp$ handoff, while the comparison of $V$ with $V'$ aligns the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ row attached to that handoff. Thus the source-to-target extension route is fixed at the finite ledger level.

The only remaining target-side comparison is the separated boundary. The rows $W,W'$ carry the complete-target convergence evidence reached by the aligned extension route, and $T,T'$ expose the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ comparison used by the uniqueness ledgers $L,L'$. The proof therefore identifies exported extension reads through $T,T'$ and through no other equality principle.

The exclusions follow by inspection of the carrier. No coordinate stores quotient completion equality, selected representatives, host function extensionality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$; the transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows preserve only the displayed route.
\end{proof}
