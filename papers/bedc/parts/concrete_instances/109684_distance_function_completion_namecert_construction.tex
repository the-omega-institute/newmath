\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DistanceFunctionCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-distance-function-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley on metric completion by distance functions, the $\DistanceFunctionCompletionUp$ packet records the L10 route where a metric source is read through its distance-to-source observations before later completion and separated-completion consumers inspect it. It sits after the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the metric-completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the Kuratowski embedding route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-kuratowski-embedding-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the distance-function completion route as finite BEDC data; it is not a quotient completion, selected representative, host equality of completed points, countable-choice schedule, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.

\begin{definition}[DistanceFunctionCompletion carrier]
\label{def:distance-function-completion-carrier}
A $\DistanceFunctionCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(M,C,K,S,R,D,A,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ source row whose distances are observed.
\item $C$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumer row reached only after the source metric observations have been displayed.
\item $K$ is the $\KuratowskiEmbeddingUp$ distance-function handoff row from the source metric into the completion-facing observation space.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row naming the inspected distance observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the regular rational values exposed by those windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare requested distances and finite approximants.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row for each displayed distance value.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, completion row, Kuratowski handoff, stream windows, regular readback, dyadic ledger, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
M,K,S,R,D \longmapsto A \longmapsto C \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DistanceFunctionCompletionUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\KuratowskiEmbeddingUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DistanceFunctionCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $M,C,K,S,R,D,A,H,T,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion classes, selected completed points, host equality of distance functions, countable choice over source points, ambient completeness of $\RealUp$, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DistanceFunctionCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:distance-function-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DistanceFunctionCompletionUp$ carrier
$$
F=(M,C,K,S,R,D,A,H,T,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\DistanceFunctionCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the metric source, metric-completion consumer, Kuratowski embedding, stream-window, regular-rational, dyadic-tolerance, real-seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item distance-function exposure, requiring every completion-facing read to pass through $M$ and $K$ before $C$ may be consumed;
\item L10 readback, requiring each displayed distance observation to pass through $S,R,D,A$ before it is available to a completion consumer;
\item transport stability, requiring $\hsame$ comparison to preserve the source metric, distance-function handoff, readback route, completion consumer, replay, provenance, and local naming rows componentwise;
\item replay locality, requiring $\Cont$ replay to use only the displayed route from $M,K,S,R,D$ through $A$ and $C$;
\item non-escape, excluding quotient completion classes, selected completed points, host equality of completed points or distance functions, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:distance-function-completion-carrier}. The displayed packet supplies exactly the source metric row, the distance-function handoff, the finite observation route, the completion consumer, and the structural rows.

Distance-function exposure starts with $M$ and $K$. A completion-facing read has no access to $C$ until the source metric and its Kuratowski distance observations have both been displayed.

The L10 readback is the route $S,R,D,A$. Stream windows name the inspected observations, regular rational rows read them back, dyadic tolerance rows compare requested scales, and the real seal records the terminal value visible to completion consumers.

Finally $H,T,P,N$ preserve and name the same route. Since no excluded quotient, selector, host-equality, choice, completeness, verification, or bridge object is a carrier row, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
