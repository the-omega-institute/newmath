Following Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\LocatedModulusCompletionUp$ interface records a completion-side read of a located real name only through explicit regular Cauchy data, a displayed modulus, and a finite limit seal. It belongs to the same Real and Completion phase as $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, and $\LocatedRealUp$: the packet exposes finite readback and seal rows, not a quotient of Cauchy streams or a selected host real value.

\begin{definition}[LocatedModulusCompletion carrier]
\label{def:located-modulus-completion-carrier}
A $\LocatedModulusCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
L=(R,Q,M,S,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row whose rational observations supply the regular Cauchy name;
\item $Q$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row read from that regular source;
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ row assigning each requested precision a finite threshold for the same source row;
\item $S$ is the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row recording the finite limit-facing seal consumed by completion readers;
\item $E$ is the $\LocatedRealUp$ evidence row, listing the located read windows and rational separation or enclosure rows attached to the same real-name read;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the regular source, real seal, modulus row, limit seal, located evidence row, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading the source windows, applying the modulus threshold, reaching the limit seal, and exposing the located real evidence;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedModulusCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The packet is a finite completion-side read certificate. It carries no quotient of Cauchy names, no selected completed value, no host equality of real numbers, and no ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedModulusCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-modulus-completion-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:located-modulus-completion-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedModulusCompletionUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $L=(R,Q,M,S,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier transport by componentwise $\hsame$ over the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source, $\RealUp$ seal, $\CauchyModulusUp$ row, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row, and $\LocatedRealUp$ evidence;
\item regular-seal routing, which requires every completion-facing read to pass through the displayed regular source $R$, modulus row $M$, and limit seal $S$ before reaching $Q$ or $E$;
\item ledger exactness, which records that the precision threshold used by a read is exactly the threshold displayed by $M$ for that request;
\item non-escape, which excludes quotient streams, selected host limits, host real equality, trichotomy, and ambient completeness from the packet surface.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.LocatedModulusCompletionUp.LocatedModulusCompletionCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the direct projection of the finite tuple in \autoref{def:located-modulus-completion-carrier}. The listed rows are all displayed in the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet and are named by the local certificate row.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The regular source, real seal, modulus row, limit seal, and located evidence each move through the stored $\hsame$ rows and are read through the corresponding $\Cont$ route.

For routing and exactness, inspect a requested precision. The continuation first reads the threshold row of $M$, then uses that finite threshold to reach the source window in $R$, the limit-facing row in $S$, and the visible real or located rows $Q$ and $E$.

The non-escape clause follows from the carrier inventory. Since no coordinate stores a quotient stream, selected limit, host equality, trichotomy, or completeness theorem, no consumer route can project one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedModulusCompletion regular seal]
\label{thm:located-modulus-completion-regular-seal}
Every $\RealUp$ or $\LocatedRealUp$ read exported by an accepted $\LocatedModulusCompletionUp$ carrier factors through the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row $R$, the same $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row $M$, and the same $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ limit-facing row $S$. The real seal $Q$ and located evidence $E$ therefore share one finite completion route over $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the consumer route to $Q$ or $E$. By the carrier definition, both routes are stored in $C$ and both are packaged with the same provenance row $P$.

Read the requested precision through $M$. The threshold selected by the modulus row determines which finite source window in $R$ is inspected before any real-seal or located-evidence row is exposed.

Finally read the limit-facing row $S$. It is the completion-side seal attached to the same source and threshold, so the exported real and located reads share the same finite route and do not choose an independent completed value.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedModulusCompletion classifier stability]
\label{thm:located-modulus-completion-classifier-stability}
Let $L=(R,Q,M,S,E,H,C,P,N)$ and $L'=(R',Q',M',S',E',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\LocatedModulusCompletionUp$ carriers whose displayed rows are related componentwise by the stored $\hsame$ transports. If the transported modulus rows assign matching threshold reads and the transported limit seals remain attached to the transported regular sources, then every $\RealUp$ and $\LocatedRealUp$ consumer route from $L$ transports to the corresponding route from $L'$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the componentwise transports. The rows $R,Q,M,S,E$ have separate $\hsame$ comparisons, and the package and naming rows record that those comparisons belong to one carrier.

Use the modulus comparison first. Matching threshold reads ensure that a requested precision reaches corresponding source windows in $R$ and $R'$.

Next use the seal comparison. The transported $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row remains attached to the transported regular source, so the real-seal and located-evidence routes keep the same source-threshold-limit order.

Repack the transported rows with $C',P',N'$. The resulting consumer route is exactly the corresponding route from $L'$, and no hidden equality of completed real values is used.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocatedModulusCompletion ledger exactness]
\label{thm:located-modulus-completion-ledger-exactness}
For every accepted $\LocatedModulusCompletionUp$ carrier and every displayed precision request, the threshold consumed by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source read, the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ row, the $\RealUp$ seal row, and the $\LocatedRealUp$ evidence row is the threshold recorded by the carrier's $\CauchyModulusUp$ row for that request. No downstream consumer may replace it by an unrecorded modulus, a host convergence rate, or a choice-selected tail.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the precision request in the carrier. The only threshold coordinate is the displayed row $M$, and the continuation route $C$ reads that row before it reaches any source, seal, or located-evidence coordinate.

The regular source route consumes the threshold selected by $M$. The limit seal, real seal, and located evidence are downstream of that same source read, so their tolerance ledger is the one already selected by the modulus row.

An unrecorded modulus or host convergence rate would require an additional carrier coordinate. Since \autoref{def:located-modulus-completion-carrier} lists none, the displayed ledger is exact for every accepted route.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

Apply the regular-seal theorem to route real and located reads through the same source, modulus, and limit-seal rows. Apply classifier stability to transport those rows componentwise, and apply ledger exactness to identify the threshold consumed by each precision request.

\begin{theorem}[LocatedModulusCompletion located-real handoff]
\label{thm:located-modulus-completion-located-real-handoff}

$$
(R,M,S)
$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Use \autoref{thm:located-modulus-completion-modulus-seal-ledger-scope}. It forces any route to either consumer through the same modulus row $M$ and limit-seal row $S$, with the same regular source row $R$ read before the consumer boundary.

The located evidence row $E$ therefore extends the real-seal route by visible located-read data only. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a selected completed value or an alternate source/modulus packet, the handoff preserves the same finite packet $(R,M,S)$ for both consumers.
\end{proof}
