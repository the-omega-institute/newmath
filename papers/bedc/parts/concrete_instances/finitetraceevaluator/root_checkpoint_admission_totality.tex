\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator root checkpoint admission totality]
\label{thm:finitetraceevaluator-root-checkpoint-admission-totality}
For an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ packet with carrier
$E=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N)$, checkpoint admission is total only along the displayed
finite route
$$
I \to T \to A \to V \to H \to C \to P \to N.
$$
The admission surface is a finite $\BHist$ read transported by $\hsame$,
replayed by $\Cont$, packaged by $\Pkg$, and named by
$\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$. It has no coordinate for host
validation, total truth, total halting, unbounded evaluation, or an infinite
trace.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-carrier}. The
accepted evaluator packet displays exactly $I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N$, with $T$ as the
finite $\BHist$ trace row, $H$ as the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row, $C$
as the $\Cont$ replay row, $P$ as $\Pkg$ provenance, and $N$ as the local
$\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$ row.

Apply the local NameCert obligations and bounded trace coverage,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-namecert-obligations} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-bounded-trace-coverage}. They force every
accepted-result read through $I \to T \to A$ before checkpoint admission can
read validation, transport, continuation, provenance, or naming rows.

Finally attach the host-validation refusal ledger and root runtime admission
surface, \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-host-validation-refusal-ledger}
and \autoref{thm:finitetrace-evaluator-root-runtime-admission}. The refusal
ledger blocks host validation, total truth, total halting, unbounded
evaluation, and infinite traces, while the runtime admission surface keeps the
consumer on the same finite route through $V,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator root trace-validation separation]
\label{thm:finitetraceevaluator-root-trace-validation-separation}
For an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ packet $E=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N)$,
accepted-result reads and validation-boundary reads remain separated at the
rows $A$ and $V$. The accepted-result side is read only through
$$
I \to T \to A,
$$
while the validation-boundary side is read only through
$$
V \to H \to C \to P \to N.
$$
The two sides may be transported, replayed, packaged, and named inside the same
$\BHist/\hsame/\Cont/\Pkg/\NameCert$ packet, but neither side becomes host
validation or an infinite trace checker.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from result-validation separation,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-result-validation-separation}. It places
accepted results behind the finite route $I \to T \to A$ and records validation
only at the boundary row $V$.

Use the checkpoint source lock and validation nonescape totality,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-source-lock} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-validation-nonescape-totality}. They keep
checkpoint-facing accepted-result reads on $A$ and validation-facing reads on
$V,H,C,P,N$ under the displayed $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ replay routes.

The local NameCert obligations then exhaust the packet. Since $P$ and $N$ add
only provenance and local naming, the separated reads cannot import host
validation, total truth, total halting, unbounded evaluation, or infinite trace
semantics.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator root reflection checkpoint handoff]
\label{thm:finitetraceevaluator-root-reflection-checkpoint-handoff}
Every $\ReflectionRuntimeCheckpointUp$ handoff that consumes an accepted
$\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ packet consumes the finite evaluator rows
$I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N$. The handoff reads bounded trace coverage, accepted-result
classification, validation-boundary refusal, componentwise $\hsame$ transport,
displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row, and
it cannot import host validation, total truth, total halting, an unbounded
runtime, or an infinite trace.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply the runtime boundary handoff,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-runtime-boundary-handoff}. It sends every
$\ReflectionRuntimeCheckpointUp$ handoff through the finite evaluator carrier,
bounded trace coverage, validation non-escape, result-validation separation,
root trace-result totality, root validation boundary, and root boundary
package.

Next use checkpoint admission totality above. The runtime admission surface is
already the displayed route $I \to T \to A \to V \to H \to C \to P \to N$, so
the handoff receives no extra host row before reading the evaluator packet.

Finally combine root consumer readiness with runtime admission boundary,
\autoref{thm:finitetraceevaluator-root-consumer-readiness} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-runtime-admission-boundary}. Downstream
checkpoint consumers receive the same finite $\BHist/\hsame/\Cont/\Pkg/
\NameCert$ packet, and the refusal surface blocks host validation, total truth,
total halting, unbounded runtime state, and infinite trace imports.
\end{proof}
