\begin{definition}[FiniteTraceEvaluator BHist step carrier]
\label{def:finitetraceevaluator-bhist-step-carrier}
For an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ packet, the root $\BHist$ step carrier is the finite evaluator packet
$$
B_{\mathrm{step}}=(I,T,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
where $I$ is the source trace row, $T$ is the checkpoint row, $K$ is the runtime-state row, $R$ is the result image, $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $C$ is the displayed continuation route, $P$ is the package provenance, and $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$ row. The carrier is read inside the root evaluator surface of \autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-carrier} and the root exactness surface of \autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-root-exactness-surface}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator route totality]
\label{thm:finitetraceevaluator-route-totality}
Every accepted evaluator read of the step carrier in \autoref{def:finitetraceevaluator-bhist-step-carrier} passes through the displayed $\BHist/\hsame/\Cont/\Pkg/\NameCert$ rows
$$
I,T,K,R,H,C,P,N.
$$
The source trace, checkpoint, runtime state, result image, componentwise transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows therefore form the complete finite route for root evaluator consumers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the checkpoint route of \autoref{thm:finitetraceevaluator-root-checkpoint-totality}. It sends checkpoint-facing reads through the displayed finite evaluator rows before any downstream checkpoint consumer can inspect the accepted result.

Next use the runtime surface of \autoref{thm:finitetraceevaluator-root-runtime-totality} and the checkpoint source lock of \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-source-lock}. These identify the source trace, checkpoint row, runtime-state boundary, and accepted-result image as one finite route inside the root evaluator packet.

Finally attach the root NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:finitetrace-evaluator-root-namecert-obligations}. The local naming row, package provenance, continuation, and componentwise $\hsame$ transport are part of the same accepted carrier, so every evaluator read factors through $I,T,K,R,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator nonescape boundary]
\label{thm:finitetraceevaluator-nonescape-boundary}
No accepted read of the step carrier in \autoref{def:finitetraceevaluator-bhist-step-carrier} exports host runtime validation, hidden interpreter state, quotient trace equality, or unbounded execution evidence. Every checkpoint or runtime consumer remains on the displayed finite route
$$
I,T,K,R,H,C,P,N.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:finitetraceevaluator-root-validation-refusal}. Host validation requests stop at the displayed validation and naming boundary of the accepted evaluator packet and do not become host runtime evidence.

Next use bounded replay ledger exhaustion and checkpoint consumer nonescape, \autoref{thm:finitetraceevaluator-bounded-replay-ledger-exhaustion} and \autoref{thm:finitetraceevaluator-checkpoint-consumer-nonescape}. They exclude hidden runtime state, infinite trace evidence, unchecked compiler data, and second checkpoint sources from checkpoint-facing reads.

Finally combine route totality with the root exactness scope, \autoref{thm:finitetraceevaluator-route-totality} and \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-exactness-scope}. Since every read has already been forced through $I,T,K,R,H,C,P,N$, quotient trace equality and unbounded execution evidence have no carrier coordinate and no continuation route from the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
