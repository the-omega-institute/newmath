\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator terminal route totality]
\label{thm:finitetrace-evaluator-terminal-route-totality}
Every terminal route of an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ surface
$$
E=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N)
$$
from \autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-root-surface} factors
through the accepted-result spine
$$
I \to T \to A.
$$
The rows $V,H,C,P,N$ may then supply validation-boundary refusal,
componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$
provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$ naming, but no
terminal route begins from a host evaluator, hidden runtime state, total truth
predicate, total halting classifier, unbounded evaluator, or second checkpoint
source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the terminal surface definition. It displays exactly the packet
$E=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N)$ and identifies $I$ as the coded input row, $T$ as the
terminal finite trace row, and $A$ as the accepted-result classifier.

Apply the terminal trace-source lock and terminal accepted-result readback,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-trace-source-lock} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-accepted-result-readback}. These
place the terminal source on $I \to T$ and make access to $A$ pass through the
same finite route.

It remains to exclude alternate terminal sources. Use terminal validation
nonescape, root exactness scope, and the checkpoint ledger cut,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-validation-nonescape},
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-exactness-scope}, and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-ledger-cut}. The sibling
runtime checkpoint chapter then consumes the displayed packet rather than a
hidden evaluator or second checkpoint source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator terminal source stability]
\label{thm:finitetrace-evaluator-terminal-source-stability}
Let two terminal reads of accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ surfaces display
the same packet
$$
E=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N)
$$
up to the componentwise $\hsame$ transports recorded in $H$ and the displayed
$\Cont$ replay routes recorded in $C$. Then both reads preserve the same
terminal source route
$$
I \to T \to A.
$$
Transport may replay validation, provenance, and naming access through
$V,H,C,P,N$, but it cannot move the terminal source to a host evaluator,
hidden runtime state, total truth predicate, total halting classifier,
unbounded evaluator, or second checkpoint source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First expose the common terminal packet through
\autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-root-surface}. The only source,
trace, and accepted-result rows of the packet are $I$, $T$, and $A$.

Use \autoref{thm:finitetrace-evaluator-terminal-route-totality}. Each terminal
read already factors through $I \to T \to A$ before the validation,
transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows are consumed.

Now apply the bounded replay stability and acceptance row stability results,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-bounded-replay-stability} and
\autoref{thm:finitetrace-evaluator-acceptance-row-stability}. Componentwise
$\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay preserve the finite trace row
and the accepted-result row of the same route.

Finally combine terminal validation nonescape with the checkpoint ledger cut,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-validation-nonescape} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-ledger-cut}. Since the ledger
contains no alternate source coordinate, transport cannot create one for the
runtime checkpoint consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator terminal ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:finitetrace-evaluator-terminal-ledger-exhaustion}
The terminal ledger exported by an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ surface
is exhausted by the displayed packet
$$
E=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N).
$$
It exports no host evaluator, hidden runtime state, total truth predicate,
total halting classifier, unbounded evaluator, or second checkpoint source.
Every downstream runtime checkpoint read therefore receives the terminal
route $I \to T \to A$ together with the boundary, transport, continuation,
provenance, and naming rows $V,H,C,P,N$, and receives no further evaluator
coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-root-surface}. The
terminal ledger has exactly the eight displayed rows of $E$.

Apply terminal route totality and terminal source stability,
\autoref{thm:finitetrace-evaluator-terminal-route-totality} and
\autoref{thm:finitetrace-evaluator-terminal-source-stability}. The
accepted-result side is fixed at $I \to T \to A$, and componentwise transport
keeps terminal reads on that route.

Use root exactness scope and checkpoint ledger cut,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-exactness-scope} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-ledger-cut}. These results
separate the accepted-result spine from $V,H,C,P,N$ and remove host
validation, total truth, total halting, unbounded evaluation, infinite traces,
and second checkpoint sources from the ledger.

Finally read the handoff through the runtime checkpoint sibling chapter,
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflection-runtime-checkpoint-namecert}. Its
root carrier and ledger exactness rows consume the finite checkpoint packet
without importing hidden runtime state or an additional evaluator coordinate.
\end{proof}
