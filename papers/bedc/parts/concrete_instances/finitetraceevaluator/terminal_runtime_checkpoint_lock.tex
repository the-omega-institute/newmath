\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator terminal runtime checkpoint lock]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-runtime-checkpoint-lock}
For every accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ terminal checkpoint packet
$$
E=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N),
$$
every terminal checkpoint read remains on the displayed route
$$
I \to T \to A \to V \to H \to C \to P \to N.
$$
Here $T$ is the finite $\BHist$ trace row, $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$
transport row, $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay row, $P$ is the $\Pkg$
provenance row, and $N$ is the local
$\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$ row. The lock exposes no host evaluator,
hidden acceptance predicate, total truth predicate, unbounded runtime, or
infinite trace semantics.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier and terminal root surface,
\autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-carrier} and
\autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-root-surface}. They display
exactly $I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N$ as the terminal checkpoint packet and provide no
coordinate for a host evaluator or infinite trace.

Apply checkpoint-budget exhaustion,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-budget-exhaustion}. It forces
every checkpoint-budget read consumed by the reflection-runtime sibling to be
exhausted by the same finite evaluator rows.

Finally use terminal route exposure and root checkpoint admission totality,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-route-exposure} and
\autoref{thm:finitetraceevaluator-root-checkpoint-admission-totality}. These
results identify the terminal read and root admission route with the displayed
finite spine from $I$ through $N$, so no off-route checkpoint source remains.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator terminal admission totality]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-admission-totality}
Terminal admission for an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ checkpoint packet
is total only through the finite
$\BHist/\hsame/\Cont/\Pkg/\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$ packet
$$
I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N.
$$
The admission surface reads trace evidence from $T$, transports it through
$H$, replays it through $C$, packages provenance through $P$, and names the
local certificate through $N$. It does not admit a host evaluator, hidden
acceptance predicate, total truth predicate, total halting classifier,
unbounded evaluator, or infinite trace semantics.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the terminal packet through
\autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-root-surface}. The admission
surface is already finite: $I$ names the coded input, $T$ names the finite
$\BHist$ trace, $A$ names accepted-result classification, and $V$ names the
validation-boundary refusal row.

Next use the root checkpoint admission totality theorem. It makes admission
total only along $I \to T \to A \to V \to H \to C \to P \to N$, with $H,C,P,N$
as the displayed transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The remaining terminal clauses are exhausted by the terminal NameCert
obligation surface,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-namecert-terminal-obligation-surface}.
Since that surface lists only finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$ data, terminal admission has no further
truth, halting, host-runtime, or infinite-trace gate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator terminal reflection handoff]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-reflection-handoff}
Every reflection-runtime consumer of an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$
terminal checkpoint receives only the finite checkpoint packet
$$
I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N.
$$
The handoff exposes the finite $\BHist$ route, componentwise $\hsame$
transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$ naming surface. It provides no host
evaluator, hidden acceptance predicate, total truth predicate, total halting
classifier, infinite trace semantics, or alternate runtime evaluator.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the reflection-runtime route,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-reflection-runtime-route}. It sends every
$\ReflectionRuntimeCheckpointUp$ read through the displayed sequence
$I \to T \to A \to V \to H \to C \to P \to N$.

Use terminal runtime-candidate bridge,
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-terminal-runtime-candidate-bridge}. That
bridge factors runtime-candidate provenance through the sibling
$\RuntimeCandidateBoundaryUp$ and $\ReflectionRuntimeCheckpointUp$ surfaces
before returning to the displayed terminal rows.

Finally combine terminal admission totality above with checkpoint-budget
exhaustion. The admissible checkpoint packet is already exhausted by finite
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$ data, so a reflection-runtime consumer
receives no hidden acceptance predicate, total truth predicate, host
evaluator, total halting classifier, alternate evaluator, or infinite trace
semantics.
\end{proof}
