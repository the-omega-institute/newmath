\begin{definition}[FiniteTraceEvaluator root exactness surface]
\label{def:finite-trace-evaluator-root-exactness-surface}
For an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ packet, the root exactness surface is
the finite packet
$$
E_{\mathrm{root}}=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N).
$$
Its rows are the carrier of \autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-carrier}, the
local $\NameCert_{\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp}$ obligations of
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-namecert-obligations}, the trace-result
route of \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-trace-result-totality}, the
validation-boundary route of
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-validation-boundary}, and the
checkpoint-consumer route of
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-consumer-route}. The surface has
no row outside $I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator root exactness scope]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-exactness-scope}
Every root checkpoint read of the surface in
\autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-root-exactness-surface} factors either
through the accepted-result route
$$
I \to T \to A
$$
or through the validation and ledger route $V,H,C,P,N$. No root checkpoint read
can mix those routes into a host validator, total truth predicate, total halting
classifier, unbounded evaluator, infinite trace, or second checkpoint source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-trace-result-totality}. It
places every accepted-result read on the route $I \to T \to A$ before a root
consumer can inspect the result classifier.

Next use \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-validation-boundary}. It
sends validation-facing reads to $V,H,C,P,N$ and keeps host validation, total
truth, total halting, and unbounded evaluation outside the packet.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-source-lock}. The
checkpoint source remains locked to the accepted-result route and the
validation boundary, so the root read factors through one of the displayed
routes and cannot create a second checkpoint source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator checkpoint ledger cut]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-ledger-cut}
The checkpoint ledger of the surface in
\autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-root-exactness-surface} has no coordinate
for host validation, total truth for $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentences, total
halting, unbounded evaluation, an infinite trace, or a second checkpoint
source. Its complete checkpoint-facing ledger is the displayed packet
$I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-boundary-package}. The root
boundary package exposes accepted results only through $I \to T \to A$ and
validation-facing data only through $V,H,C,P,N$.

Use \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-runtime-boundary-handoff}. The runtime
handoff receives the same finite evaluator rows and no extra coordinate for
host validation, total truth, total halting, unbounded evaluation, or an
infinite trace.

Combining the two package facts cuts the checkpoint ledger at the displayed
surface. A second checkpoint source would require a row not present in
$I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N$, so it is not part of the ledger.
\end{proof}
