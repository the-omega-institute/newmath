\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator checkpoint source lock]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-source-lock}
Every root-facing checkpoint consumer of an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$
packet locks its accepted-result source to the displayed finite route
$$
I \to T \to A
$$
and its validation-facing boundary to $V,H,C,P,N$. The consumer may read the
root checkpoint package, but it cannot replace the coded input, finite trace,
accepted-result classifier, or validation boundary by host validation, total
truth, total halting, unbounded execution, or a second source ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier and NameCert obligations,
\autoref{def:finite-trace-evaluator-carrier} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-namecert-obligations}. They display exactly
the rows $I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N$ and make trace-step exposure, accepted-result
classification, validation non-escape, transport, continuation, provenance, and
local naming part of one packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-bounded-trace-coverage} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-trace-result-totality}. These force
every accepted-result read through $I \to T \to A$ before a root-facing
checkpoint consumer can continue.

Finally use \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-validation-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-boundary-package}. The validation read
stays on $V,H,C,P,N$, so no host validator, total truth predicate, total
halting classifier, unbounded evaluator, or second source ledger is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator validation nonescape totality]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-validation-nonescape-totality}
For an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ packet, every validation-facing root
read is total only through the displayed boundary row $V$ together with
$H,C,P,N$. The accepted-result route $I \to T \to A$ remains separate from the
validation boundary, and neither route can certify host validation, total truth
for $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentences, self-validation, total halting, or unbounded
evaluation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-validation-row-nonescape}. It
identifies $V$ as a boundary row and refuses host validation, total truth,
self-validation, and unbounded execution as packet coordinates.

Next use \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-result-validation-separation}.
The accepted-result route through $I,T,A$ and the validation-facing route
through $V$ remain distinct rows of the same evaluator packet.

Attach the root boundary with
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-validation-boundary} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-boundary-package}. The surrounding
transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows are exactly
$H,C,P,N$, so validation cannot escape to a host judgment or total classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator runtime boundary handoff]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-runtime-boundary-handoff}
Every $\ReflectionRuntimeCheckpointUp$ handoff from an accepted
$\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$ packet factors through the finite trace evaluator
carrier, bounded trace coverage, validation non-escape, result-validation
separation, root trace-result totality, root validation boundary, and root
boundary package. The handoff exposes only the source-locked evaluator rows
$I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the checkpoint consumer route from
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-consumer-route} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-runtime-checkpoint-boundary}. The runtime
checkpoint receives the same evaluator rows and no extra validation or
unbounded-execution coordinate.

Use \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-checkpoint-source-lock}. It locks the
accepted-result source to $I \to T \to A$ and the validation-facing surface to
$V,H,C,P,N$.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-validation-nonescape-totality}. The handoff
therefore stays inside the displayed evaluator carrier and refuses host
validation, total truth, total halting, self-validation, and unbounded
evaluation.
\end{proof}
