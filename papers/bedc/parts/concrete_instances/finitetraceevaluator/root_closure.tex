\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator root trace-result totality]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-trace-result-totality}
Every root-level downstream consumer of an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$
packet that reads accepted-result data first factors through
$$
I \to T \to A.
$$
The consumer may then continue through the remaining rows of
$E=(I,T,A,V,H,C,P,N)$, but it cannot read an accepted result before the coded
input and finite trace-step rows have been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the bounded trace coverage of
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-bounded-trace-coverage}. It sends every
accepted evaluator result through the displayed route $I \to T \to A$.

Use \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-trace-result-route} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-result-classifier-exactness}. They make
$A$ exact for accepted results only after the finite trace-step row in $T$ has
been read from $I$.

Any root-level downstream consumer is still a consumer of the same displayed
packet. By \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-public-consumer-package} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-runtime-checkpoint-boundary}, the public
and checkpoint routes continue through $V,H,C,P,N$ without adding a second
accepted-result channel.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteTraceEvaluator root validation boundary]
\label{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-root-validation-boundary}
Every root-level validation read of an accepted $\FiniteTraceEvaluatorUp$
packet terminates at the displayed boundary, transport, continuation,
provenance, and local naming rows $V,H,C,P,N$. Such a read cannot become host
validation, total truth for $\mathrm{CIC}^{*}$ sentences, total halting, or
unbounded evaluation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-validation-row-nonescape}. It
states that $V$ is a boundary row and refuses host validation, total truth,
self-validation, and unbounded execution as packet coordinates.

Attach the surrounding transport and naming rows by
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-boundary-exhaustion} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-validation-refusal-package}. These keep
the validation read on the route through $V,H,C,P,N$.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-provenance-exhaustion} and
\autoref{thm:finite-trace-evaluator-accepted-result-ledger-exhaustion}. The
ledger is exhausted by the displayed finite packet, so no root-level read can
recover a host validator, total truth predicate, total halting predicate, or
unbounded evaluator from the validation boundary.
\end{proof}
