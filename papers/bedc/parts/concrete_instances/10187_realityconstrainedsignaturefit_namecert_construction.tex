\begin{definition}[Reality-constrained signature-fit carrier]
\label{def:reality-constrained-signature-fit-carrier}
A $\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(S,A,M,G,P,C,H,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation schedule row, naming the finite stream of reality-facing probes used by the signature comparison.
\item $A$ is the $\RealUp$ approximation row, recording the mathematical endpoint, tolerance ledger, and finite approximation tower layer read by the classifier.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricUp$ comparison row, recording the distance or discrepancy surface on which the approximation claim is evaluated.
\item $G$ is the GAP-policy row, recording which physical signatures remain unrepresented, withheld, or outside the admitted comparison.
\item $P$ is the physical-representation-certificate row, recording source, pattern, classifier, stability, and ledger data for the claimed physical representation.
\item $C$ is the reality-constrained classifier row, comparing the mathematical signature induced by $A,M$ with the physical signature exposed by $S,P,G$.
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for $S,A,M,G,P,C$.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row over $\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RealUp$, $\MetricUp$, GAP policy, physical-representation certificates, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for identifying mathematical approximation with physical adequacy, no hidden route around the GAP-policy row, and no operation descent that omits the representation certificate.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Reality-constrained signature-fit classifier]
\label{def:reality-constrained-signature-fit-classifier}
For accepted packets
$$
R=(S,A,M,G,P,C,H,Q,N)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
R'=(S',A',M',G',P',C',H',Q',N'),
$$
the classifier $\mathsf{RealityConstrainedSignatureFitClassifier}(R,R')$ holds precisely when the displayed $\hsame$ data transports the stream schedules, real approximation rows, metric comparison rows, GAP-policy rows, physical-representation certificates, and local classifier rows componentwise, and when the transported classifier preserves the distinction between mathematical approximation and physical-signature compatibility.

Thus classifier agreement is not host equality of physical systems and not equality of Real endpoints alone. It is agreement of the finite stream, real, metric, GAP, representation-certificate, package, and local naming rows as a single BEDC packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained signature-fit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-signature-fit-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-signature-fit-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly $R=(S,A,M,G,P,C,H,Q,N)$;
\item classifier stability: every accepted comparison uses the classifier of \autoref{def:reality-constrained-signature-fit-classifier};
\item physical-signature exposure: a physical-fit read must display the stream schedule $S$, GAP-policy row $G$, and representation certificate $P$ before the classifier row $C$ is public;
\item mathematical-approximation exposure: every mathematical approximation read must display the real endpoint row $A$ and metric comparison row $M$ before it is compared with a physical signature;
\item non-escape: no consumer may export physical adequacy, operation descent, or signature compatibility from rows outside $S,A,M,G,P,C,H,Q,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:reality-constrained-signature-fit-carrier}. The finite packet lists the only available rows, so a consumer cannot add a hidden physical source, metric comparison, GAP exception, or representation certificate after admission.

Classifier stability follows from \autoref{def:reality-constrained-signature-fit-classifier}. The classifier compares transported stream, real, metric, GAP, representation-certificate, package, and naming rows rather than a bare Real endpoint.

The two exposure clauses are the two sides of the carrier. Physical-signature compatibility is read through $S,G,P,C$, while mathematical approximation is read through $A,M,C$. Both sides meet only in the displayed classifier and componentwise transport row.

The non-escape clause is the finite-packet conclusion. Since the local certificate names only $S,A,M,G,P,C,H,Q,N$, adequacy or descent depending on any absent row is not a route in the accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained signature-fit no-free descent]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-signature-fit-no-free-descent}
Let $R=(S,A,M,G,P,C,H,Q,N)$ be an accepted $\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp$ packet. An operation transported through the mathematical classifier descends to a physical-signature claim only when the same route displays the stream schedule, GAP policy, and physical-representation certificate rows. If the route lacks any of $S,G,P$, then the transported mathematical operation does not descend as a physical-fit operation in $R$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:reality-constrained-signature-fit-namecert-obligations}. Physical-signature exposure requires $S,G,P$ before the classifier row is public as a physical-fit comparison.

The mathematical approximation side may still display $A$ and $M$. That display licenses an approximation read, not a physical-signature descent, because the classifier definition keeps approximation compatibility distinct from physical-signature compatibility.

If one of $S,G,P$ is absent from the route, then the route lacks a required physical-signature coordinate of the accepted packet. The operation remains a mathematical classifier transport and has no accepted descent as a physical-fit operation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained signature-fit finite non-escape]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-signature-fit-nonescape}
Every accepted $\RealityConstrainedSignatureFitUp$ consumer route factors through the finite rows
$$
S,A,M,G,P,C,H,Q,N.
$$
In particular, no consumer can replace the displayed stream schedule by an unobserved source, replace the GAP-policy row by silent omission, replace the representation certificate by a bare equation, or identify the real approximation row with physical adequacy.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition exposes exactly the nine rows $S,A,M,G,P,C,H,Q,N$. The classifier definition transports those rows componentwise and preserves the distinction between mathematical approximation and physical-signature compatibility.

Apply the NameCert obligations. Physical-signature exposure forces $S,G,P$ into every fit read, mathematical-approximation exposure forces $A,M$ into every approximation read, and classifier stability forces both sides to meet only through $C$ and $H$ inside the same package.

Therefore a consumer route through an unobserved source, silent GAP omission, bare equation, or host adequacy judgment has no coordinate in the packet. The accepted route is exhausted by the displayed finite rows.
\end{proof}
