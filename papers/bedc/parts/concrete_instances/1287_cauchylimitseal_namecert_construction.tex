\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyLimitSealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ interface records the visible seal boundary between regular rational Cauchy data and the consumer-facing $\RealUp$ row. In the Bishop--Bridges constructive-analysis reading of Cauchy completion, a limit is presented by finite regular-name observations and an explicit schedule, not by quotienting streams or selecting a host limit. The chapter isolates that boundary as a concrete packet so later $\RealUp$ consumers can read only the displayed finite windows that pass through the seal.

\begin{definition}[CauchyLimitSeal carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-limit-seal-carrier}
A $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is a $\RegSeqRatUp$ family row presenting the regular rational Cauchy source to be sealed;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule that lists the finite windows a consumer may inspect;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCore$ observation ledger attached to those scheduled windows;
\item $G$ is the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ diagonal row that routes the regular family through its displayed completion packet;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row exposed to downstream real consumers;
\item $T$ is the finite collection of $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport rows connecting the source family, scheduled windows, diagonal row, observation ledger, and seal row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$ dependency certificates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyLimitSealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares two carriers only by componentwise transport of these visible rows. It does not compare quotient representatives, host limits, or ambient completeness data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-namecert-obligations}
The $\NameCert_{\CauchyLimitSealUp}$ surface has four finite obligations: carrier habitation by \autoref{def:cauchy-limit-seal-carrier}; classifier stability under the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport rows; exact factorization of every $\RealUp$ consumer read through the $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCore$, and $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ rows; and non-escape of quotient stream equality, selected host limits, uniqueness theorems, and ambient real completeness data.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyLimitSealUp.CauchyLimitSealPacket\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is the finite packet assembly. The regular family, schedule, observation ledger, completion row, seal row, transport rows, package provenance, and local certificate are all coordinates of the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

Classifier stability is componentwise. Each row is transported only by the stored $\hsame$ comparison and the corresponding $\Cont$ route, so an accepted packet stays inside the same visible dependency surface.

The exactness and boundary obligations follow from the shape of the carrier. Every $\RealUp$ read must pass through the scheduled finite windows and their dyadic observation ledger before reaching the completion row and seal row; no field contains a quotient stream, a selected host limit, a uniqueness theorem, or an ambient real-completeness datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal window factorization]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-window-factorization}
For every accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier $L$ and every finite $\RealUp$ consumer request, the read factors through a visible $\StreamNameUp$ window from $S$, the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row in $X$, the matching $\DyadicRatCore$ observation ledger in $D$, the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ diagonal row $G$, and the displayed transport rows in $T$ before it reaches the seal row $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-limit-seal-carrier}. A consumer request first selects one of the scheduled finite windows because the seal row has no independent read channel.

Read the selected window through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source family. The rational observation is recorded in the $\DyadicRatCore$ ledger, and the same scheduled row is passed to the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ diagonal coordinate.

Finally apply the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows in $T$ to transport the read to the $\RealUp$ seal. The path is therefore the stated finite factorization, with every consumer-facing value mediated by visible packet data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal non-escape boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-non-escape}
An accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier exports no quotient stream, no selected host limit, no uniqueness theorem for limits, and no ambient real completeness datum. Its public surface is exhausted by the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ source family, the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, the $\DyadicRatCore$ observation ledger, the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ diagonal row, the $\RealUp$ seal row, and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates. Each coordinate is either a named dependency certificate row, a finite transport row, a package provenance row, or the local certificate row.

The completion coordinate is consumed only through its displayed diagonal row, and the real-facing coordinate is the seal row attached to that diagonal route. Neither coordinate adds a host real carrier or a quotient of regular streams.

Thus a downstream consumer can cite exactly the listed rows. Since no other coordinate exists in the packet, uniqueness theorems, selected limits, and ambient real completeness data cannot be exported by the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal RealUp consumer boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-realup-consumer-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier may read only the sealed finite-window route of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-window-factorization}. In particular, the consumer boundary is the displayed packet surface over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCore$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$, and it does not expose an independent completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the factorization theorem. It forces each consumer read through the scheduled window, regular rational source row, dyadic observation ledger, completion diagonal row, and transport route before the read reaches the $\RealUp$ seal.

Use the non-escape boundary to identify what remains visible after the seal is reached. The package provenance and local certificate name the dependency rows, but they do not add quotient, host-limit, uniqueness, or ambient-completeness fields.

Therefore the $\RealUp$ consumer boundary is precisely the finite route listed in the statement. The seal is a public endpoint for displayed regular-Cauchy data, not a source of an independent real-completeness principle.
\end{proof}
