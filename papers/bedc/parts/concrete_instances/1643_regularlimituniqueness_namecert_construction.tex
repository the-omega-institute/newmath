The $\RegularLimitUniquenessUp$ interface records the separated uniqueness side of regular-Cauchy completion. In the Bishop--Bridges constructive-analysis presentation, a completed real is read through finite regular-name windows and explicit moduli; uniqueness is therefore a visible separated-seal certificate over two accepted completion candidates, not a quotient of streams or a selected host limit. The packet depends on $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$, $\RegularCauchyFamilyUp$, $\HausdorffCompletionUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows.

\begin{definition}[RegularLimitUniqueness carrier]
\label{def:regular-limit-uniqueness-carrier}
A $\RegularLimitUniquenessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(F,D_0,D_1,\mu,R_0,R_1,E_0,E_1,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the shared $\RegularCauchyFamilyUp$ input row;
\item $D_0$ and $D_1$ are two $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$ candidate limit rows for the same $F$;
\item $\mu$ is the shared $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row controlling the tail windows compared by both candidates;
\item $R_0$ and $R_1$ are the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows exposed by the two diagonal candidates at the threshold selected by $\mu$;
\item $E_0$ and $E_1$ are the two $\RealUp$ seal rows reached from those readbacks;
\item $S$ is the separated comparison row, read through the $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ boundary, stating that $E_0$ and $E_1$ classify at the same separated real seal;
\item $H$ records the displayed $\hsame$ transports tying $F$, $\mu$, the two diagonal rows, the two readbacks, the two seals, and the separated comparison together;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through the shared family, threshold row, diagonal candidates, readback rows, real seals, and separated comparison;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyFamilyUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RealDiagonalCompletionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\HausdorffCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularLimitUniquenessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, a chosen host limit, host real equality, or choice. Its public data are exactly the displayed finite rows above.
\end{definition}
