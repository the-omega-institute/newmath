It sits between the bounded comparison boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicdecidableboundary-namecert}, the candidate-mediated normalisation frontier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicnormalizationfrontier-namecert}, and the closed-normal endpoint seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normal-form-consistency-seal-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Bounded normal conversion bridge carrier]
\label{def:bounded-normal-conversion-bridge-carrier}
A $\BoundedNormalConversionBridgeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(D,F,O,E,S,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\MetaCICDecidableBoundaryUp$ row, exposing the bounded normal-equality checker and its refusal boundary.
\item $F$ is the $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$ row, exposing the candidate-mediated route by which normal computation is allowed to reach a finished endpoint.
\item $O$ is the bounded normal-output row, naming the finite fuel, closedness window, and two normalizer outputs inspected by the checker.
\item $E$ is the finished-normal evidence row, requiring the compared outputs to be closed normal forms before conversion evidence is exported.
\item $S$ is the $\NormalFormConsistencySealUp$ endpoint row, recording that the finished-normal endpoint can be consumed only at the normal-form boundary.
\item $R$ is the refusal row, recording that a read bypassing either $O$ or $E$ is not a conversion bridge read.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the decidability boundary, normalisation frontier, bounded output, finished evidence, normal-form seal, refusal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a conversion request through $D,F,O,E,S,R,H$ and back to the local certificate row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BoundedNormalConversionBridgeUp$, $\MetaCICDecidableBoundaryUp$, $\MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp$, $\NormalFormConsistencySealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedNormalConversionBridgeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a global conversion decider, fuel-erased normalization, quotient equality over represented terms, host evaluator equality, unrestricted closedness decision, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded normal conversion bridge NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-normal-conversion-bridge-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BoundedNormalConversionBridgeUp$ carrier
$$
B=(D,F,O,E,S,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedNormalConversionBridgeUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the decidability boundary, normalisation frontier, bounded normal-output row, finished-normal evidence row, endpoint seal, refusal row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item bounded-output visibility, requiring every conversion read to cite the finite output row $O$ before checker equality is consumed;
\item finished-normal endpoint visibility, requiring $E$ to certify that the compared outputs are finished closed normal forms before the normal-form seal $S$ is read;
\item frontier compatibility, requiring $D$ and $F$ to be read together so bounded decidability is not separated from the normalisation route that supplies its endpoint evidence;
\item refusal stability, requiring $R$ to reject any read that bypasses bounded normal output or finished-normal evidence;
\item structural stability, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed rows.
\end{enumerate}
No bounded normal conversion bridge obligation is stored outside $B$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bounded-normal-conversion-bridge-carrier}. The tuple exposes the decidability boundary, normalisation frontier, bounded output, finished evidence, normal-form seal, refusal, and structural rows.

The bounded-output and finished-endpoint obligations are the rows $O$ and $E$. A conversion request first fixes the finite normalizer outputs and then supplies evidence that those outputs are finished closed normal forms.

The frontier compatibility obligation is stored in $D,F,S$. The bounded checker is read through the decidability boundary, the endpoint evidence is routed through the normalisation frontier, and the normal-form seal records the finished endpoint without creating a global conversion decision procedure.

The refusal and structural rows $R,H,C,P,N$ preserve that order. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for fuel-erased normalization, quotient equality, host evaluator equality, unrestricted closedness decision, Lean verification, or bridge checking, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded normal conversion endpoint handoff]
\label{thm:bounded-normal-conversion-endpoint-handoff}
Every accepted $\BoundedNormalConversionBridgeUp$ conversion read factors through the finite route
$$
\MetaCICDecidableBoundaryUp \longrightarrow \MetaCICNormalizationFrontierUp \longrightarrow \NormalFormConsistencySealUp
$$
with bounded normal outputs and finished closed-normal evidence displayed before any conversion conclusion is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let $B=(D,F,O,E,S,R,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted carrier. Projection exposes $D,F,O,E,S,R,H,C,P,N$ as the only rows available to a conversion read.

The row $O$ fixes the bounded normalizer outputs inspected by the decidability boundary $D$. The row $E$ then supplies the finished-normal evidence required before those outputs can be consumed as closed normal forms.

The row $F$ supplies the normalisation-frontier route, and $S$ places the endpoint at the normal-form consistency seal. The refusal row $R$ rejects any path that omits $O$ or $E$, while $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same row order under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Thus the packet supplies an endpoint handoff from bounded normal comparison to finished normal forms, not a global conversion decider or a fuel-erased normalization theorem.
\end{proof}
