\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LieGroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-liegroup-namecert}

The Lie-group interface $\LieGroupUp$ packages a group carrier whose underlying set is a manifold and whose multiplication and inverse are constructively smooth. It depends on $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-manifold-namecert}), and is the substrate for representation, exponential, and root-system certificates.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:liegroup-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Singleton smooth group carrier]
\label{def:liegroup-singleton-smooth-group-carrier}
The concrete $\LieGroupUp$ carrier is the shared singleton history carrier
$$
L_{\emp}(h):\Longleftrightarrow \hsame(h,\emp)
$$
on $\mathsf{BHist}$. Its group rows are the singleton $\GroupUp$ rows
$$
\mathsf{mul}(h,k):=\mathsf{append}(h,k),\qquad
\mathsf{inv}(h):=\emp,\qquad
e:=\emp,
$$
with classifier
$$
R_{\emp}(h,k):\Longleftrightarrow
L_{\emp}(h)\wedge L_{\emp}(k)\wedge \hsame(h,k).
$$
Its smooth-manifold rows are the singleton $\ManifoldUp$ chart rows with the
one chart $\chi_{\emp}$, top domain $L_{\emp}$, constant value $0_{\RealUp}$,
and the self-overlap constant transition. Thus the algebraic carrier and the
manifold carrier are the same predicate on histories, not two transported
copies.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:liegroup-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton carrier obligation]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-carrier-obligation}
The carrier $L_{\emp}$ of
\autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-smooth-group-carrier} simultaneously supplies
the $\GroupUp$ singleton carrier and classifier rows and the $\ManifoldUp$
singleton chart rows. Concretely, every accepted history is classified with
$\emp$ by $R_{\emp}$, multiplication and inverse stay inside $L_{\emp}$, and
the sole chart domain is exactly the same carried predicate $L_{\emp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the algebraic side, unfold
\autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-smooth-group-carrier}. The carrier,
classifier, multiplication, unit, and inverse rows are exactly the rows used by
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-history-semantic-namecert}; the checked Lean row
is
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.GroupSingletonHistory\_laws}$.

For the manifold side, the chart-domain row is the singleton empty-chart row
of \autoref{thm:manifold-singleton-chart-coverage}. Its carried predicate is
$\hsame(h,\emp)$, which is definitionally the same predicate as $L_{\emp}$.

The two sides therefore share the same history carrier and the same restricted
$\hsame$ classifier. Projection from the group row gives the algebraic carrier
and classifier fields, while projection from the manifold row gives the chart
domain, chart value, and singleton atlas fields.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton operation smoothness obligation]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-operation-smoothness-obligation}
For histories carried by $L_{\emp}$, both $\mathsf{mul}$ and $\mathsf{inv}$ of
\autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-smooth-group-carrier} are smooth operations in
the singleton atlas. Every accepted multiplication result and every accepted
inverse result is classifier-equal to $\emp$, so each operation is read by the
sole chart as a constant transition on the singleton overlap.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with multiplication. If $L_{\emp}(h)$ and $L_{\emp}(k)$ hold, the group
singleton laws identify $\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ with $\emp$ and keep the result
inside the singleton carrier. This is the multiplication-closure row of
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.GroupSingletonHistory\_laws}$.

For inverse, $\mathsf{inv}(h)$ is definitionally $\emp$. The inverse-closure
row of the same group theorem gives the carrier witness, and the classifier
row identifies the result with the unit endpoint.

Now read both operations through the manifold atlas. By
\autoref{thm:manifold-singleton-chart-coverage}, every carried input and every
accepted output lies in the only chart domain and has the constant chart value
$0_{\RealUp}$. The overlap transition is therefore the singleton constant
transition of \autoref{thm:manifold-singleton-transition-smoothness}, whose
checked Lean row is
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.ManifoldUp.ManifoldSingleton\_transition\_smoothness}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton shared source scope]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-shared-source-scope}
For the carrier of
\autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-smooth-group-carrier}, every displayed
$\LieGroupUp$ row is scoped by the same BEDC history source. The group carrier,
manifold chart domain, restricted classifier, unit, multiplication, inverse,
chart value, and transition rows are all given by $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$,
$\emp$, $\mathsf{append}$, $\Cont$, the singleton $\GroupUp$ laws, and the
singleton $\ManifoldUp$ rows. No row requires equality of host-language
functions; the only equality test exposed to the certificate is the restricted
$\hsame$ classifier on carried histories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the carrier definition. The algebraic carrier is the predicate
$L_{\emp}(h)$, and the chart domain is the same predicate rather than a second
copy. The classifier row is therefore the displayed relation $R_{\emp}$, which
mentions only $L_{\emp}$ and $\hsame$.

The group operation rows are the singleton rows: the unit is $\emp$,
multiplication is $\mathsf{append}$, and inverse is the constant map to $\emp$.
Their closure and law witnesses are exactly the singleton $\GroupUp$ laws.

The manifold rows use the singleton chart, constant value $0_{\RealUp}$, and
the self-overlap transition controlled by $\Cont$. Since both sides name the
same $\mathsf{BHist}$ source, every interface projection is scoped by
$\mathsf{BHist}$ and by the restricted classifier, with no appeal to host
function equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton smooth operation readback]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-smooth-operation-readback}
For histories carried by $L_{\emp}$, the multiplication output
$\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ and the inverse output $\emp$ are classifier-equal to
$\emp$. Reading either output through the sole singleton chart gives the
constant value $0_{\RealUp}$, and every operation overlap is the constant
self-transition of the singleton atlas.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:group-singleton-history-semantic-namecert}. Its singleton
history laws give closure of $\mathsf{append}(h,k)$, closure of $\emp$ as the
inverse output, and classifier equality of both accepted outputs with the unit
endpoint.

Apply \autoref{thm:manifold-singleton-chart-coverage}. Every accepted input and
accepted output lies in the only chart domain, and the chart value on that
domain is the constant $0_{\RealUp}$.

Apply \autoref{thm:manifold-singleton-transition-smoothness}. The only overlap
available in the singleton atlas is the self-overlap, so the operation readback
through the chart is the constant transition row already supplied by the
manifold certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton dependent interface package]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-dependent-interface-package}
Any downstream $\mathsf{ExpMap}$, $\mathsf{AdjointRep}$, or
$\mathsf{RootSystem}$ consumer of the
singleton $\LieGroupUp$ package may project only the displayed unit,
multiplication, inverse, chart, transition, and ledger rows. These projections
are stable under the restricted $\hsame$ classifier: replacing an accepted
history by a classifier-equal accepted history preserves the projected carrier
witness, operation witness, chart-domain witness, chart value, and transition
witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The allowed projections are exactly the rows named in
\autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-smooth-group-carrier}. They expose the unit,
the append multiplication, the constant inverse, the singleton chart, the
constant transition, and the ledger rows $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\emp$,
$\mathsf{append}$, and $\Cont$.

Stability follows from the shared source scope of
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-shared-source-scope}. Every projected row is
defined over $L_{\emp}$ and $R_{\emp}$, so classifier-equal accepted histories
remain in the same carried predicate and have the same singleton chart value.

The smooth readback theorem supplies the operation-facing part of the
interface. Multiplication and inverse outputs return to the singleton endpoint,
and the only chart transition available to the consumer is the constant
self-transition.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton downstream unblock package]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-downstream-unblock-package}
The singleton $\LieGroupUp$ certificate consists of the following ten-field
roadmap package.
\begin{description}
\item[Traditional target.] The one-point Lie group.
\item[BEDC source.] $\mathsf{BHist}$ with distinguished empty history $\emp$.
\item[Carrier.] The predicate $L_{\emp}(h)$, equivalently $\hsame(h,\emp)$.
\item[Classifier.] The restricted classifier $R_{\emp}$ on accepted histories.
\item[Stability obligations.] Carrier reuse, operation closure, chart-domain
reuse, chart-value constancy, transition constancy, and classifier transport
under $\hsame$.
\item[Ledger.] $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\emp$, $\mathsf{append}$, $\Cont$,
the singleton $\GroupUp$ laws, and the singleton $\ManifoldUp$ chart and
transition rows.
\item[Core theorem list.]
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-carrier-obligation},
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-operation-smoothness-obligation},
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-shared-source-scope},
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-smooth-operation-readback},
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-dependent-interface-package}, and
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-namecert-obligation}.
\item[Exactness predicate.] A projected Lie-group row is accepted exactly when
it factors through the displayed carrier, classifier, operation, chart, and
transition rows.
\item[Standard bridge absent.] No $\StdBridge$ row is part of this package.
\item[Status.] A paper-side $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$ target for
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.LieGroupUp}$.
\end{description}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The traditional target, BEDC source, carrier, and classifier fields are the
surface rows of \autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-smooth-group-carrier} and
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-carrier-obligation}. They identify the
algebraic and manifold carriers with the same singleton history predicate.

The stability and ledger fields are supplied by
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-shared-source-scope} and
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-smooth-operation-readback}. These theorems name
the shared $\mathsf{BHist}$ source, the restricted $\hsame$ classifier, the
append operation, the constant inverse, and the constant chart transition.

The dependent-interface field is supplied by
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-dependent-interface-package}. It records the
rows that later $\mathsf{ExpMap}$, $\mathsf{AdjointRep}$, and
$\mathsf{RootSystem}$ packages may
project, and it states their stability under the restricted classifier.

The exactness, bridge, and status fields are bookkeeping consequences of those
rows: a row belongs to the singleton package only when it factors through the
displayed carrier and operation data, no standard bridge is included, and the
target namespace is $\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.LieGroupUp}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LieGroup singleton NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:liegroup-singleton-namecert-obligation}
The rows of
\autoref{def:liegroup-singleton-smooth-group-carrier},
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-carrier-obligation}, and
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-operation-smoothness-obligation},
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-shared-source-scope},
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-smooth-operation-readback}, and
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-dependent-interface-package} assemble the
paper-side $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$ obligation surface for the singleton
smooth group carrier.
\begin{description}
\item[Traditional target.] The one-point Lie group.
\item[BEDC source.] $\mathsf{BHist}$ with distinguished empty history $\emp$.
\item[Carrier.] The predicate $L_{\emp}(h)$, equivalently $\hsame(h,\emp)$.
\item[Classifier.] The restricted singleton classifier $R_{\emp}$.
\item[Stability.] Carrier transport, group operation closure, chart-domain
transport, chart-value constancy, and transition-row constancy.
\item[Ledger.] The rows $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{append}$, $\emp$, the singleton $\GroupUp$ laws, the singleton
$\ManifoldUp$ chart, and the singleton constant transition.
\item[Core theorem list.]
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-history-semantic-namecert},
\autoref{thm:manifold-singleton-namecert-obligation-package},
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-carrier-obligation}, and
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-operation-smoothness-obligation},
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-shared-source-scope},
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-smooth-operation-readback}, and
\autoref{thm:liegroup-singleton-dependent-interface-package}.
\item[Exactness.] A Lie-group operation row is accepted only when its carrier,
classifier, chart-domain, and transition witnesses all reduce to the displayed
singleton rows.
\item[No standard bridge.] The surface contains no $\StdBridge$ row.
\item[Status.] A paper-side $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$ obligation target under
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.LieGroupUp}$.
\end{description}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier theorem supplies the shared object boundary: one predicate
$L_{\emp}$ is both the group carrier and the manifold point carrier. This
prevents any separate transport problem between algebraic and smooth rows.

The group rows are inherited from
\autoref{thm:group-singleton-history-semantic-namecert}, backed by
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.GroupUp.GroupSingletonHistory\_laws}$. They give
inhabitance, multiplication closure, inverse closure, unit laws, and inverse
laws for the append operation with constant inverse.

The manifold rows are inherited from
\autoref{thm:manifold-singleton-namecert-obligation-package}, backed by
$\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.ManifoldUp.ManifoldSingleton\_semanticNameCert}$ and
the singleton transition smoothness row. They give the sole chart domain,
constant chart value, and constant self-overlap transition.

The operation-smoothness theorem ties these projections together. Once a group
operation result is accepted, it is again classifier-equal to $\emp$, hence it
is read by the only chart as the same constant endpoint. Repacking the listed
fields gives exactly the ten rows displayed in the statement, with no standard
bridge component claimed.
\end{proof}
