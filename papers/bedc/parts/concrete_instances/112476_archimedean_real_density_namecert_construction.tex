\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ArchimedeanRealDensityUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-archimedean-real-density-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the
$\ArchimedeanRealDensityUp$ packet records the finite route by which a
displayed $\RealUp$ name exposes density data through Archimedean bounds,
dyadic brackets, finite stream windows, regular-rational readbacks, and a
terminal Real seal. It sits over the dyadic arithmetic of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite windows of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular sequence
readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the Real seal
of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the apartness gap boundary of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apartnessreal-namecert}, and the dyadic
density route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-density-real-namecert}.
The certificate names a density handoff around displayed Real names; it does
not import classical trichotomy, quotient equality of real names, a selected
representative stream, countable choice, host least-upper-bound machinery, or
ambient Real completeness.

\begin{definition}[ArchimedeanRealDensity carrier]
\label{def:archimedean-real-density-carrier}
An $\ArchimedeanRealDensityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(A,B,W,R,E,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ bound row for the displayed
$\RealUp$ name and the local rational scale at which density is read.
\item $B$ is the $\DyadicDensityRealUp$ bracket row naming the dyadic witness
inside the displayed interval surface.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family opened from the Real
name at the precisions requested by $A$ and $B$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to those finite windows.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row consumed only after the bound, bracket,
window, and readback rows have been displayed.
\item $G$ is the positive-gap and apartness row, read through
$\ApartnessRealUp$, that keeps the bracket separated at the displayed scale.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the bound, bracket,
window, readback, Real seal, gap, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from an Archimedean-density request
through $A,B,W,R,E,G,H$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ArchimedeanRealUp$,
$\DyadicDensityRealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\RealUp$, $\ApartnessRealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanRealDensityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains only finite displayed rows. It stores no trichotomy row,
no quotient equality of real names, no selected representative stream, no
host least upper bound, no countable-choice selector, and no Real completeness
coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanRealDensity NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:archimedean-real-density-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ArchimedeanRealDensityUp$ carrier
$D=(A,B,W,R,E,G,H,C,P,N)$, the local
$\NameCert_{\ArchimedeanRealDensityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the
following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the Archimedean bound, dyadic bracket, finite
window, regular readback, Real seal, apartness gap, transport, replay,
provenance, and local naming rows;
\item bound-bracket compatibility, stating that the dyadic bracket row $B$ is
opened only at a scale admitted by the Archimedean row $A$;
\item window-readback exactness, stating that every density observation uses
the finite windows $W$ and the regular-rational readback $R$ before the Real
seal $E$ is consumed;
\item gap stability, stating that the positive apartness row $G$ transports
componentwise with $A,B,W,R,E$ through the stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes;
\item non-escape, stating that trichotomy, quotient equality of real names,
selected representatives, host least upper bounds, countable choice, and Real
completeness are absent from the carrier.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:archimedean-real-density-carrier}. The bound, bracket, finite
windows, regular readback, Real seal, positive gap, transport table, replay
table, provenance row, and local naming row are exactly the available
coordinates.

The bound-bracket compatibility row reads $A$ before $B$. Thus an accepted
density request cannot open a dyadic bracket at a scale not already admitted
by the Archimedean bound surface.

The window-readback and gap-stability rows then read the same finite route in
two directions. The Real seal $E$ is reached only after $W$ and $R$, while the
apartness row $G$ is transported beside those rows through $H$ and $C$.

Finally inspect the carrier inventory. Since none of the refused host or
quotient objects is a coordinate of $D$, the listed rows exhaust the local
NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanRealDensity dyadic-bracket handoff]
\label{thm:archimedean-real-density-dyadic-bracket-handoff}
Every density read exported by an accepted $\ArchimedeanRealDensityUp$
carrier factors through the displayed route
$$
A,B,W,R,E
$$
before any Real-facing consumer receives it. In particular, the read first
uses the $\ArchimedeanRealUp$ bound row $A$, then the
$\DyadicDensityRealUp$ bracket row $B$, then the finite $\StreamNameUp$
windows $W$, then the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row $R$, and only then the
$\RealUp$ seal row $E$, with $G,H,C,P,N$ preserving the positive gap,
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an exported density read and project the accepted carrier. The
only Archimedean scale coordinate is $A$, so the read begins at the displayed
bound row.

The dyadic bracket is then the row $B$. By the NameCert obligations, $B$ is
opened only at a scale admitted by $A$, and no separate bracket coordinate is
available.

The Real-facing part of the read passes through $W$ and $R$ before $E$. The
carrier lists no route from $A$ or $B$ directly to a Real consumer, so the
finite window and regular readback rows are mandatory.

The remaining rows $G,H,C,P,N$ keep this path inside the displayed positive
gap, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming surface. Therefore every
density read factors through $A,B,W,R,E$ before reaching the Real boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanRealDensity Real boundary]
\label{thm:archimedean-real-density-real-boundary}
An accepted $\ArchimedeanRealDensityUp$ carrier gives its Real-facing
consumer only the finite displayed rows
$$
A,B,W,R,E,G,H,C,P,N.
$$
It does not export trichotomy, quotient equality of real names, a selected
representative stream, a host least-upper-bound object, countable choice, or a
Real completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the dyadic-bracket handoff theorem. Any exported density read reaches a
Real-facing consumer only after passing through the Archimedean bound, dyadic
bracket, finite window, regular readback, and Real seal rows.

The positive-gap row and structural rows do not add a host-side object. They
only transport and replay the same displayed carrier surface through
$G,H,C,P,N$.

Inspecting \autoref{def:archimedean-real-density-carrier} shows that the
refused objects are not carrier coordinates. Hence no Real-facing consumer of
the accepted packet can receive trichotomy, quotient equality, a selected
representative, a host least upper bound, countable choice, or Real
completeness from this certificate.
\end{proof}
