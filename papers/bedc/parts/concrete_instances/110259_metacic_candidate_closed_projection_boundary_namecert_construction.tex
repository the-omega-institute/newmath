\begin{definition}[MetaCIC candidate closed-projection boundary carrier]
\label{def:metacic-candidate-closed-projection-boundary-carrier}
A $\MetaCICCandidateClosedProjectionBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(T,K,R,E,NF,O,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the empty-context typed closed-term row whose projection into the candidate interface is being tested.
\item $K$ is the candidate-set evidence row, read through $\CandidateSetNormalizationUp$ when the projection is admitted.
\item $R$ is the router row, read through $\MetaCICNormalizationCertificateRouterUp$ and recording which candidate, closed-candidate, or candidate-set route is exposed.
\item $E$ is the bounded-normal endpoint seal row, recording the finite normal-output evidence available after candidate admission.
\item $NF$ is the no-infinite-reduction row exported only downstream of $K,R,E$.
\item $O$ is the obstruction row recording refusal when the closed term cannot be placed into the displayed candidate interface.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $T,K,R,E,NF,O,C,P,L$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the closed-term row through candidate admission, router selection, endpoint seal, no-infinite-reduction read, and obstruction retention.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetaCICCandidateClosedProjectionBoundaryUp$, $\CandidateSetNormalizationUp$, $\MetaCICNormalizationCertificateRouterUp$, $\MetaCICOpenProblemLedgerUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICCandidateClosedProjectionBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $T,K,R,E,NF,O,H,C,P,L$. It has no coordinate for evidence-free closed-term strong normalization, unrestricted subject reduction, host reduction equality, quotient conversion, or a global normalizer.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC candidate-set closed projection frontier]
\label{thm:metacic-candidate-set-closed-projection-frontier}
For every accepted $\MetaCICCandidateClosedProjectionBoundaryUp$ carrier
$$
B=(T,K,R,E,NF,O,H,C,P,L),
$$
the closed-term row $T$ reaches a strong-normalization-facing read only through candidate evidence $K$, router selection $R$, and bounded-normal endpoint seal $E$. If those rows do not place $T$ inside the candidate interface, the obstruction row $O$ is the only exported boundary datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The closed-term row $T$ is present as a finite syntactic and typing packet, but it is not itself a normalization theorem.

The positive route first reads $K$ through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-candidate-set-normalization-namecert}. The router row $R$ then records the candidate, closed-candidate, or candidate-set branch through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicnormalizationcertificaterouter-namecert}. Only after those rows are displayed can the endpoint seal $E$ and no-infinite-reduction row $NF$ be consumed.

If candidate admission is absent, $O$ records the refusal. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,L$ preserve that same finite boundary, so the packet exports no evidence-free closed-term strong-normalization claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC candidate-set SN handoff boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-candidate-set-sn-handoff-boundary}
Every strong-normalization handoff exported by an accepted $\MetaCICCandidateClosedProjectionBoundaryUp$ packet is a handoff for rows already inside the displayed candidate interface. It cannot be read as a blanket theorem that all empty-context typed closed MetaCIC terms are strongly normalizing.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the frontier theorem. It places the normalization-facing route behind $K,R,E$ and keeps the obstruction row $O$ visible when the candidate interface is not supplied.

For an admitted row, the handoff consumes candidate evidence from $\CandidateSetNormalizationUp$ and route evidence from $\MetaCICNormalizationCertificateRouterUp$. The no-infinite-reduction row $NF$ is therefore downstream of the admitted candidate branch rather than a second source of normalization evidence.

For a closed term outside that interface, the carrier has only $T$ and the obstruction row $O$ on the boundary. The structural rows $H,C,P,L$ transport, replay, source, and name the boundary, but they do not add subject reduction, host conversion equality, quotient reduction equality, or a global normalizer. Hence the handoff is candidate-scoped.
\end{proof}
