\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteReflectionTupleUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finitereflectiontuple-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\FiniteReflectionTupleUp$ packet realizes the TasteGate stability discipline of \autoref{ch:visions-theory-stability-taste-gate} as a finite reflection tuple. It consumes the stability packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tastegatestability-namecert}, the candidate-admission surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tastegateadmission-namecert}, and the compiler-witness route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tastegatecompilerwitness-namecert}. The packet is narrower than these siblings: it records the fixed displayed tuple image, the readback rows, the componentwise $\NameCert$ transports, and the reflection-request rows that a downstream bridge request may inspect.

\begin{definition}[FiniteReflectionTuple carrier]
\label{def:finite-reflection-tuple-carrier}
A $\FiniteReflectionTupleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(X,S,A,W,I,R,H,C,P,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the candidate chapter identity row, recording the chapter label, origin row, and concrete-instance site being reflected;
\item $S$ is the consumed $\TasteGateStabilityUp$ row, carrying the candidate carrier, $\ChapterTasteGate$ obligations, admission ledger, and non-escape boundary;
\item $A$ is the consumed $\TasteGateAdmissionUp$ row, exposing the candidate-admission surface and refusal ledger for the same $X$;
\item $W$ is the consumed $\TasteGateCompilerWitnessUp$ row, exposing the ground-compiler event-flow image, replay rows, dependency ledger, and audit result;
\item $I$ is the displayed tuple-image row, listing the finite image of $X,S,A,W$ as the exact tuple requested by the reflection route;
\item $R$ is the readback row, recovering $X,S,A,W$ componentwise from $I$ without importing a second carrier or host semantic judgment;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $X,S,A,W,I,R,C,P,Q,N$ and componentwise $\NameCert$ transport for the three consumed TasteGate packets;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that replay tuple formation, image reading, readback, and componentwise NameCert transport;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:visions-theory-stability-taste-gate}, the three consumed TasteGate packets, tuple-image rows, readback rows, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $Q$ is the reflection-request row, recording which $\StdBridge$-facing request may inspect the tuple image and readback;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteReflectionTupleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for hidden prompt state, oracle taste, unlisted compiler facts, a host-side approval row, a second tuple image, or a reflection request detached from $I$ and $R$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteReflectionTuple NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:finite-reflection-tuple-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteReflectionTupleUp$ carrier
$$
F=(X,S,A,W,I,R,H,C,P,Q,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteReflectionTupleUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier exposure for $F$, candidate identity through $X$, consumed stability data through $S$, consumed admission data through $A$, consumed compiler-witness data through $W$, tuple-image exposure through $I$, readback exposure through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ and $\NameCert$ transport through $H$, replay routes through $C$, package provenance through $P$, reflection-request exposure through $Q$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:finite-reflection-tuple-carrier}. The packet lists every row available to a consumer of the finite reflection tuple.

The TasteGate rows are supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tastegatestability-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tastegateadmission-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tastegatecompilerwitness-namecert}. The stability row supplies the carrier and non-escape boundary, the admission row supplies the candidate row being tested, and the compiler witness supplies the ground-compiler evidence read by $W$ and replayed through $C$.

The reflection-specific obligations are $I,R,H,C,P,Q,N$. The tuple image $I$ is the only displayed image, $R$ is the only readback, $H$ gives componentwise transport for both equality and local certificates, and $Q$ records the bridge-facing request that may inspect the tuple.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for hidden prompt state, oracle taste, unlisted compiler facts, host-side approval, a second tuple image, or a request detached from $I$ and $R$, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteReflectionTuple field-faithful prediction]
\label{thm:finite-reflection-tuple-fieldfaithful-prediction}
Every accepted $\FiniteReflectionTupleUp$ packet is field-faithful to its displayed tuple image. A consumer that asks for the reflected candidate, stability packet, admission packet, compiler witness, componentwise transport, or $\StdBridge$ request must read that field through $I$ and recover it through $R$. A packet lacking either the displayed image row or the readback row is not a public reflection request over the accepted TasteGate route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier definition. The only image coordinate is $I$, and the only row that recovers fields from the image is $R$.

Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:finite-reflection-tuple-namecert-obligations}. They force candidate identity, stability, admission, compiler witness, transport, replay, provenance, and request reads through the displayed tuple surface.

The reflection-request row $Q$ is therefore request data for the same image and readback. If either $I$ or $R$ is absent, a consumer may still name some TasteGate packet, but it cannot present the fixed tuple image/readback surface required by this carrier.

Thus every accepted field read is faithful to the displayed tuple, and a request without both image and readback rows is outside the public reflection packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteReflectionTuple image-readback exactness]
\label{thm:finite-reflection-tuple-image-readback-exactness}
For every accepted $\FiniteReflectionTupleUp$ packet
$$
F=(X,S,A,W,I,R,H,C,P,Q,N),

every public field read of $X$, $S$, $A$, $W$, $C$, $P$, $Q$, or $N$ factors through the displayed tuple-image row $I$ and the readback row $R$. No public read of the reflected candidate, consumed TasteGate packet, replay route, provenance row, request row, or local naming row may bypass $I$ or recover its component from a second readback surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier definition gives exactly one image coordinate, namely $I$, and exactly one readback coordinate, namely $R$. The public rows of $F$ are therefore exposed through the displayed finite tuple rather than through a separate host-side table.

Apply \autoref{thm:finite-reflection-tuple-namecert-obligations}. Its certificate surface lists candidate identity, consumed stability data, consumed admission data, compiler-witness data, replay routes, provenance, request exposure, and local naming as rows of the same finite packet.

Now apply \autoref{thm:finite-reflection-tuple-fieldfaithful-prediction}. A consumer that asks for any reflected field must read that field through $I$ and recover it through $R$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a second image or second readback, the exactness claim follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteReflectionTuple reflection-request exhaustion]
\label{thm:finite-reflection-tuple-request-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\FiniteReflectionTupleUp$ packet
$$
F=(X,S,A,W,I,R,H,C,P,Q,N),
$$
any $\StdBridge$-facing request that inspects this finite reflection tuple is the displayed request row $Q$ attached to the same $I,R,H,C,P,N$ packet. A request detached from the displayed tuple image, readback row, componentwise transport row, replay route, provenance row, or local naming row is not a request over $F$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The definition of the carrier assigns request exposure to the single row $Q$. The same definition places $Q$ in one tuple with $I$, $R$, $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$.

The NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:finite-reflection-tuple-namecert-obligations} make request exposure part of the local certificate surface. Thus the request visible to a bridge-facing consumer is not an extra coordinate outside the accepted packet.

Use \autoref{thm:finite-reflection-tuple-fieldfaithful-prediction} to bind that request back to the displayed image and readback. If a proposed request is not the displayed row $Q$ for the same packet, or if it is detached from $I$, $R$, $H$, $C$, $P$, or $N$, it lies outside the public reflection route.
\end{proof}
