The $\SocketKindClassifierUp$ packet realizes the socket-kind taxonomy of \autoref{ch:visions-transcendental-supply-taxonomy} as a finite BEDC carrier. It sits beside the socket ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supplysocketledger-namecert}, the refusal surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-negative-name-boundary-namecert}, and the audit gate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditgateboundary-namecert}. Those siblings consume, refuse, or police sockets; this packet records the kind row that tells downstream consumers which supply aspect is being handled.

\begin{definition}[SocketKindClassifier carrier]
\label{def:socket-kind-classifier-carrier}
A $\SocketKindClassifierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(S,K_D,R,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the socket-site row naming the visible $\mathsf{GAP}$ or supply socket under classification;
\item $K_D$ is the socket-kind row naming the Type I, Type II, Type III, or later taxonomy row from \autoref{ch:visions-transcendental-supply-taxonomy};
\item $R$ is the requested-supply row recording the exact shortcut the site asks to consume;
\item $G$ is the gate-locality row naming the audit gate that must reject or admit that kind at the local site;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the socket-site, kind, requested-supply, gate-locality, route, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes through $S,K_D,R,G$ before a consumer reads the classification;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$, $\NegativeNameBoundaryUp$, $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$, $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$, $\QuotientSoundnessBoundaryUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SocketKindClassifierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a supplied witness, quotient equality, host-side harmlessness proof, anonymous socket kind, or global taxonomy oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SocketKindClassifier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:socket-kind-classifier-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:socket-kind-classifier-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\SocketKindClassifierUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $K=(S,K_D,R,G,H,C,P,N)$, socket-site exposure through $S$, kind exposure through $K_D$, requested-supply exactness through $R$, gate-locality exactness through $G$, componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

Equivalently, the standard five-row NameCert surface is carrier admission, classifier-kind exactness, gate-locality stability, displayed route/provenance readback, and non-escape for omitted supply coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The accepted packet lists $S,K_D,R,G,H,C,P,N$, so the local certificate cannot introduce another socket site, kind row, requested-supply row, audit gate, transport, route, provenance, or local name.

Read the classification rows in order. The socket-site row $S$ is displayed before the kind row $K_D$, and the requested-supply row $R$ is read before the gate-locality row $G$ can certify where the classification is policed.

Finally inspect $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, package, and name only the same finite packet. Therefore the NameCert obligations are exhausted by the displayed carrier rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SocketKindClassifier gate-locality exactness]
\label{thm:socket-kind-classifier-gate-locality-exactness}
Every accepted $\SocketKindClassifierUp$ packet binds its socket-kind row to the local audit gate displayed in $G$. A consumer that identifies a Type II witness socket with a Type III extensional-collapse socket fails this gate-locality row, because witness visibility and extensional-collapse refusal are distinct audit gates in \autoref{ch:visions-transcendental-supply-taxonomy}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the socket-kind row $K_D$ and requested-supply row $R$. Together they record which shortcut is requested: a missing witness, an extensional equality token, or another named taxonomy route.

Use the gate-locality row $G$. It ties the classification to the audit gate appropriate to that requested supply, so a witness-visibility gate cannot be read as an extensional-collapse gate.

The support rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport and package the displayed classification. Since no row supplies a host-side coercion between Type II and Type III, the mixed identification is rejected by the local gate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SocketKindClassifier sibling separation]
\label{thm:socket-kind-classifier-sibling-separation}
The $\SocketKindClassifierUp$ carrier is not bijective to $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$, $\NegativeNameBoundaryUp$, $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$, $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$, or $\QuotientSoundnessBoundaryUp$. The classifier has an explicit kind row $K_D$ and gate-locality row $G$ that these siblings respectively consume, refuse, gate, question, or specialize, so no row-preserving bijection can preserve all displayed NameCert fields.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the socket ledger first. $\SupplySocketLedgerUp$ records visible socket use, while the classifier records the kind row and its gate-locality row before use is consumed.

Compare the refusal and gate siblings next. $\NegativeNameBoundaryUp$ refuses internalization, $\AuditGateBoundaryUp$ records audit verdict surfaces, and $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$ asks the gate question; none of these carries the classifier's simultaneous socket-site, kind, requested-supply, and local-gate tuple.

Finally compare $\QuotientSoundnessBoundaryUp$. It specializes the Type III extensional-collapse refusal, while $\SocketKindClassifierUp$ must also distinguish Type I and Type II rows and later taxonomy rows. The displayed fields therefore cannot be matched by a bijection preserving all NameCert rows.
\end{proof}
