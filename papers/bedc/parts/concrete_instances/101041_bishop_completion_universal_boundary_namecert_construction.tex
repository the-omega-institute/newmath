Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive treatment of completion, the $\BishopCompletionUniversalBoundaryUp$ packet isolates the finite L10 boundary at which a Bishop regular Cauchy completion route can be consumed by a universal-property extension and uniqueness surface. It reads the completion carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert}, the extension and uniqueness face of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}, and the regular-Cauchy universal seed of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-completion-universal-namecert}. Its source observations remain the $\StreamNameUp$ windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The packet is only a boundary certificate. It names the finite rows that let a completion consumer move from regular Cauchy data to an extension route and a uniqueness ledger, while excluding quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, and any bridge credential.

\begin{definition}[Bishop completion universal boundary carrier]
\label{def:bishop-completion-universal-boundary-carrier}
A $\BishopCompletionUniversalBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(Q,S,R,D,A,U,E,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ row, exposing the regular Cauchy completion carrier and its common-tail route.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row read by the completion source and by downstream extension consumers.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the windows in $S$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used by the regular Cauchy route, the extension request, and the uniqueness comparison.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after $Q,S,R,D$ have been displayed.
\item $U$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ face restricted to the displayed Bishop regular Cauchy route.
\item $E$ is the extension route row, read through $U$ after the rows $Q,S,R,D,A$ have fixed the source and seal surface.
\item $L$ is the uniqueness ledger, a displayed $\hsame$ comparison between two extension rows over the same $Q,S,R,D,A,U,E$ route.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the completion row, stream windows, regular readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, universal-property face, extension route, uniqueness ledger, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion-universal request through $Q,S,R,D,A,U,E,L,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopCompletionUniversalBoundaryUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\RegularCauchyCompletionUniversalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCompletionUniversalBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $Q,S,R,D,A,U,E,L,H,C,P,N$. The carrier contains no quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice selector, host real equality row, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness datum, or standard-bridge credential.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop completion universal extension route]
\label{thm:bishop-completion-universal-extension-route}
Let $B=(Q,S,R,D,A,U,E,L,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\BishopCompletionUniversalBoundaryUp$ carrier. Every extension read exported by $E$ first displays the $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$ row $Q$, then the $\StreamNameUp$ windows $S$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $R$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, and the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $A$, and only then reads the restricted $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ face $U$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishop-completion-universal-boundary-carrier}. The extension row $E$ is downstream of the completion carrier $Q$, the finite source windows $S$, the regular rational readback $R$, the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, the terminal seal $A$, and the universal-property face $U$.

The sibling completion chapters supply exactly these rows. The Bishop regular Cauchy completion packet fixes the completion-facing source, while the StreamName, RegSeqRat, DyadicRatCore, and Real chapters expose the finite observation route consumed by the universal-property face.

The continuation row $C$ can replay only the displayed route, and the provenance row $P$ names no selected limit, quotient stream, host real equality, choice schedule, or ambient completeness object. Therefore every extension read factors through $Q,S,R,D,A,U$ before $E$ is exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop completion universal uniqueness ledger]
\label{thm:bishop-completion-universal-uniqueness-ledger}
Let
$$
B=(Q,S,R,D,A,U,E,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
and
$$
B'=(Q,S,R,D,A,U,E',L',H',C',P,N')
$$
be accepted $\BishopCompletionUniversalBoundaryUp$ carriers over the same completion row, stream windows, regular readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, universal-property face, and provenance. Any comparison between the exported extension rows is exhausted by the displayed uniqueness ledgers $L,L'$ together with their componentwise $\hsame$ transports and displayed $\Cont$ replays. No quotient-stream equality, selected limit, countable choice, host real equality, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness datum, or bridge credential can contribute to the comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The shared coordinates $Q,S,R,D,A,U,P$ fix the completion source, finite windows, readback, tolerance ledger, terminal seal, universal-property face, and provenance available to both packets.

The only extension-sensitive coordinates are $E,L,H,C,N$ and $E',L',H',C',N'$. The ledgers $L$ and $L'$ compare extension rows over the fixed displayed source route, while $H,H'$ and $C,C'$ provide the permitted transports and replays.

Since neither accepted carrier contains any excluded quotient, selected-limit, choice, host-equality, completeness, or bridge coordinate, the comparison cannot leave the displayed uniqueness surface. It is exactly the local $\hsame$ ledger over the Bishop regular Cauchy universal boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop completion universal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-completion-universal-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopCompletionUniversalBoundaryUp$ carrier $B=(Q,S,R,D,A,U,E,L,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCompletionUniversalBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the Bishop regular Cauchy completion row, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, Real seal, CompletionUniversalProperty face, extension route, uniqueness ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item source-route exactness, requiring every universal-property request to pass through $Q,S,R,D,A$ before $U,E,L$ can be inspected;
\item extension-route exactness, requiring $E$ to be read only as the extension route attached to the displayed $Q,S,R,D,A,U$ surface;
\item uniqueness-ledger exactness, requiring every comparison of extension rows to use $L$ over the same displayed source, seal, universal-property, and extension coordinates;
\item regular-Cauchy compatibility, requiring the boundary to reuse the $\RegularCauchyCompletionUniversalUp$ extension and uniqueness seed without adding a second completion carrier;
\item non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name no row from which quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, ambient Real completeness, or bridge credentials can be exported.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bishop-completion-universal-boundary-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the completion carrier, finite windows, readback, tolerance ledger, Real seal, universal-property face, extension route, uniqueness ledger, and structural rows.

Source-route exactness follows from the order of the carrier. A universal-property request first reads $Q$ and then the finite observation route $S,R,D,A$ before it can inspect the rows $U,E,L$.

Extension-route exactness is \autoref{thm:bishop-completion-universal-extension-route}. The extension row $E$ is attached to the displayed Bishop regular Cauchy source and cannot be read as a detached host completion map.

Uniqueness-ledger exactness is \autoref{thm:bishop-completion-universal-uniqueness-ledger}. The comparison is carried by the displayed ledgers $L,L'$ and their transports and replays, over the same visible source and extension coordinates.

Regular-Cauchy compatibility is the provenance constraint in $P$. The packet cites the $\RegularCauchyCompletionUniversalUp$ seed as a source for the extension and uniqueness shape, but it stores no second completion carrier beyond the displayed row $Q$.

Finally, non-escape is exhausted by $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, source, and name the displayed route only, and none contains quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, ambient Real completeness, or bridge credentials.
\end{proof}
