The finite-data sibling lattice reads $\BoolUp$ as the two-tag truth classifier consumed by constructor interfaces, not as a global proposition oracle. In \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-option-namecert}, the empty and present branches use the two boolean endpoint rows as constructor tags; in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prod-namecert}, component truth readback uses the same finite classifier to decide which displayed component rows have been exposed. The dependence is therefore a constructor-tag and component-readback dependence on the two $\BoolUp$ endpoints, while $\OptionUp$, $\SumUp$, $\ProdUp$, and $\ListUp$ still own their payload carriers, product rows, list spines, and local naming data.

\begin{proposition}[Bool finite-data sibling readback]
\label{prop:bool-finite-data-sibling-readback}
Every finite-data sibling read of $\OptionUp$, $\SumUp$, $\ProdUp$, or $\ListUp$ that cites $\BoolUp$ uses only the two endpoint rows of $\BoolUp$ as constructor tags or component truth readback. It does not import a payload carrier, product classifier, list spine, or external truth predicate from the boolean chapter.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Read the sibling dependency through the hub inputs. The option and sum surfaces consume the two endpoints as branch tags, the product surface consumes them as component-exposure readback, and the list surface consumes them only through finite constructor routing.

The payload, product, and spine data are then supplied by the receiving chapters. Since the Bool carrier contains only the two endpoint histories and their classifier, no additional payload or external truth predicate can be projected from the Bool row.
\end{proof}
