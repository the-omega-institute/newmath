\begin{theorem}[Bool finite-data product sibling route]
\label{thm:bool-prod-sibling-route}
The $\BoolUp$ finite truth rows are consumed by the $\ProdUp$ paired component classifier through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prod-namecert}. A product consumer may read a Boolean component only as one of the two displayed endpoint rows named by the Bool certificate, then package it with the other product coordinate through the product sibling route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the Bool carrier to its two finite truth endpoints. The product sibling route reads those endpoints as component classifier data, while the paired coordinate and product envelope remain supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prod-namecert}.

Thus the Boolean row contributes only its displayed finite endpoint and its local naming evidence. It cannot replace the product classifier, introduce an ambient Boolean algebra, or hide product-coordinate data outside the sibling certificate.
\end{proof}
