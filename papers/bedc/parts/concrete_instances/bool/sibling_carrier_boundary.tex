\begin{theorem}[$\BoolUp$ sibling carrier boundary]
\label{thm:bool-sibling-carrier-boundary}
The two endpoint histories exported by $\BoolUp$ are consumed by $\OptionUp$ as the nullable discriminator of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-option-namecert} and by $\SumUp$ as the finite branch discriminator of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sum-namecert}. The $\BoolUp$ packet exports only the two endpoint rows and their displayed classifier boundary; it exports no option payload carrier, sum payload carrier, nullable payload classifier, or tagged-sum classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the public endpoint surface of $\BoolUp$. Its exported rows are exactly the empty endpoint and the one-step endpoint, together with the classifier rows that distinguish those two histories.

The $\OptionUp$ consumer reads the same two histories as absent and present tags before any payload row is inspected. The $\SumUp$ consumer reads them as left and right branch tags before any branch payload row is inspected.

Because payload carriers and payload classifiers are supplied by the option and sum sibling chapters, no accepted $\BoolUp$ read can own those rows. The boundary therefore stops at the two endpoint histories and their finite discriminator role.
\end{proof}
