\begin{theorem}[Bool finite-truth sibling dependency route]
\label{thm:bool-finite-truth-sibling-dependency-route}
The $\BoolUp$ finite-truth surface is consumed downstream only through displayed sibling NameCert routes. Equality endpoints and no-confusion reads cite the $\EqUp$ boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-eqtype-namecert}, while finite carrier scans cite the unary and list surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-list-namecert}. Thus a consumer may use Boolean truth rows as explicit endpoint data, but it may not replace those rows by ambient equality reflection, hidden finite enumeration, or list recursion outside the sibling certificates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted $\BoolUp$ consumer route. Any equality or no-confusion read is routed through the displayed EqType sibling, so the endpoint comparison is read as a finite NameCert dependency rather than as ambient equality reflection.

For carrier scans, the only displayed finite traversal rows are the unary and list surfaces supplied by the Nat and List siblings. A downstream consumer therefore receives Boolean truth data through those certificates, not through an unrecorded enumeration of all Boolean values.
\end{proof}
