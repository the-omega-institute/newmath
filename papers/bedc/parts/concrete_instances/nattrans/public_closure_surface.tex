\begin{theorem}[NatTrans prefix standard bridge]
\label{thm:nattrans-prefix-standard-bridge}
For the concrete prefix realization, the carrier
$\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentCarrier}(p,q,a,\eta)$ is exactly the component
row of a natural transformation from the prefix functor at $p$ to the prefix
functor at $q$ over unary object $a$. Its source and target are the functor
images $\mathsf{append}(p,a)$ and $\mathsf{append}(q,a)$, and its displayed
component is the concrete morphism
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(p,a),\mathsf{append}(q,a),\eta)$.
The public $\NatTransUp$ classifier is the componentwise $\hsame$ comparison
packaged by $\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentClassifier}_{p,q,a}$.

The bridge fields are as follows. The traditional target is ordinary natural
transformations between the two fixed-prefix functors. The BEDC source is
$\CategoryUp$, $\FunctorUp$, $\NatTransUp$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, and
$\Cont$. The carrier is $\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentCarrier}$, the
classifier is $\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentClassifier}_{p,q,a}$, stability
is given by component transport and vertical composition rows, the ledger is
the pair of functor ledgers plus the component morphism ledgers, exactness is
the displayed carrier/classifier correspondence, the standard bridge is this
theorem, and the status is a paper bridge target awaiting Lean formalization.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row is read first. By
\autoref{thm:nattrans-prefix-component-carrier-unary-object-suffix-iff}, the
prefix component carrier is precisely unary membership of $a$ together with
the target-category hom carrier from $p$ to $q$ after the shared object suffix
is exposed. Reading that row through the source and target object maps of
\autoref{thm:functor-prefix-standard-bridge} identifies the endpoints as
$\mathsf{append}(p,a)$ and $\mathsf{append}(q,a)$, which are the ordinary
component source and target for the two prefix functors at object $a$.

The classifier row is then fixed componentwise. The definition
\autoref{def:nattrans-prefix-component-classifier} says that two displayed
components over the same prefixes and object are classified exactly when both
component carriers are present and their component histories are related by
$\hsame$. The semantic certificate of
\autoref{thm:nattrans-prefix-component-classifier-semantic-namecert} packages
that component classifier as the public NameCert classifier for the displayed
component carrier.

Finally, the public natural-transformation classifier is the componentwise row
specified in \autoref{def:nattrans-classifier-specification}. Substituting the
prefix carrier and classifier rows above gives ordinary natural
transformations between the two prefix functors, with no extra source data:
the functor ledgers come from \autoref{thm:functor-prefix-standard-bridge},
the component morphism ledger is the displayed
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(p,a),\mathsf{append}(q,a),\eta)$,
and stability is exactly the existing component transport and vertical
composition package. These are the listed bridge fields.
\end{proof}
