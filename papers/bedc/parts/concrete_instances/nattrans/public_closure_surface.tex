\begin{theorem}[NatTrans prefix standard bridge]
\label{thm:nattrans-prefix-standard-bridge}
For the concrete prefix realization, the carrier
$\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentCarrier}(p,q,a,\eta)$ is exactly the component
row of a natural transformation from the prefix functor at $p$ to the prefix
functor at $q$ over unary object $a$. Its source and target are the functor
images $\mathsf{append}(p,a)$ and $\mathsf{append}(q,a)$, and its displayed
component is the concrete morphism
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(p,a),\mathsf{append}(q,a),\eta)$.
The public $\NatTransUp$ classifier is the componentwise $\hsame$ comparison
packaged by $\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentClassifier}_{p,q,a}$.

The bridge fields are as follows. The traditional target is ordinary natural
transformations between the two fixed-prefix functors. The BEDC source is
$\CategoryUp$, $\FunctorUp$, $\NatTransUp$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, and
$\Cont$. The carrier is $\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentCarrier}$, the
classifier is $\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentClassifier}_{p,q,a}$, stability
is given by component transport and vertical composition rows, the ledger is
the pair of functor ledgers plus the component morphism ledgers, exactness is
the displayed carrier/classifier correspondence, the standard bridge is this
theorem, and the status is a paper bridge target awaiting Lean formalization.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row is read first. By
\autoref{thm:nattrans-prefix-component-carrier-unary-object-suffix-iff}, the
prefix component carrier is precisely unary membership of $a$ together with
the target-category hom carrier from $p$ to $q$ after the shared object suffix
is exposed. Reading that row through the source and target object maps of
\autoref{thm:functor-prefix-standard-bridge} identifies the endpoints as
$\mathsf{append}(p,a)$ and $\mathsf{append}(q,a)$, which are the ordinary
component source and target for the two prefix functors at object $a$.

The classifier row is then fixed componentwise. The definition
\autoref{def:nattrans-prefix-component-classifier} says that two displayed
components over the same prefixes and object are classified exactly when both
component carriers are present and their component histories are related by
$\hsame$. The semantic certificate of
\autoref{thm:nattrans-prefix-component-classifier-semantic-namecert} packages
that component classifier as the public NameCert classifier for the displayed
component carrier.

Finally, the public natural-transformation classifier is the componentwise row
specified in \autoref{def:nattrans-classifier-specification}. Substituting the
prefix carrier and classifier rows above gives ordinary natural
transformations between the two prefix functors, with no extra source data:
the functor ledgers come from \autoref{thm:functor-prefix-standard-bridge},
the component morphism ledger is the displayed
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(p,a),\mathsf{append}(q,a),\eta)$,
and stability is exactly the existing component transport and vertical
composition package. These are the listed bridge fields.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NatTransUp.NatTransPrefixComponentClassifier\_standard\_bridge\_fields}

\begin{definition}[NatTrans functor-category prefix carrier]
\label{def:nattrans-functor-category-prefix-carrier}
The prefix functor-category carrier for $\NatTransUp$ has unary prefix
histories as objects. A hom row from object prefix $p$ to object prefix $q$
is the component family
$\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentCarrier}(p,q,a,\eta_a)$, one row for every
unary object $a$. The displayed source and target of the $a$-component are
$\mathsf{append}(p,a)$ and $\mathsf{append}(q,a)$, and the component morphism
is the concrete target-category row
$\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(p,a),\mathsf{append}(q,a),\eta_a)$.
The hom classifier is the componentwise
$\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentClassifier}_{p,q,a}$ row, so equality of hom
rows is read by $\hsame$ on every displayed component history.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NatTrans functor-category identity and composition]
\label{thm:nattrans-functor-category-identity-and-composition}
For the prefix functor-category carrier of
\autoref{def:nattrans-functor-category-prefix-carrier}, each prefix object
$p$ carries an identity hom row whose $a$-component is the empty
component/identity semantic certificate at
$\mathsf{append}(p,a)$. If $\eta:p\Rightarrow q$ and $\theta:q\Rightarrow r$
are hom rows, their composite is the componentwise vertical composite
$\theta_a \circ \eta_a$. Associativity and the left and right unit laws are
the componentwise laws inherited from the checked prefix component carrier and
the classifier rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The object row is already a unary prefix history, and the identity component
at $a$ is the identity morphism row for
$\mathsf{append}(p,a)$. The semantic NameCert certificate for the prefix
component classifier reads this row as the empty identity component, so every
component belongs to the hom carrier from $p$ to $p$.

For composition, take the vertical composite in the target-category hom
carrier at each unary object $a$. The source and target boundaries line up
because the two component rows end and begin at the shared prefix
$q$ after the same object suffix $a$ is appended.

The associativity and unit equations are then checked componentwise. The
classifier for hom rows is componentwise $\hsame$, and the component carrier
already supplies vertical composition closure, so the category laws reduce to
the corresponding checked classifier rows at every unary object.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NatTransUp.NatTransPrefixFunctorCategory\_identity\_and\_composition}

\begin{definition}[NatTrans prefix natural-isomorphism carrier]
\label{def:nattrans-prefix-natural-isomorphism-carrier}
A prefix natural isomorphism from $p$ to $q$ is a pair of component families
$\eta:p\Rightarrow q$ and $\epsilon:q\Rightarrow p$ in the prefix
functor-category carrier such that, for every unary object $a$, both vertical
composites $\epsilon_a \circ \eta_a$ and $\eta_a \circ \epsilon_a$ classify
as the empty identity component at the corresponding appended histories
$\mathsf{append}(p,a)$ and $\mathsf{append}(q,a)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NatTrans natural-isomorphism obligation package]
\label{thm:nattrans-natural-isomorphism-obligation-package}
The prefix natural-isomorphism surface carries the NameCert obligation rows
for carrier, classifier, stability, inverse, ledger, exactness, and
no-host-leak. Its carrier is
\autoref{def:nattrans-prefix-natural-isomorphism-carrier}; its classifier is
the componentwise prefix component classifier in both directions; stability is
given by component transport and vertical composition; the inverse row swaps
the two component families; the ledger is the two functor ledgers together
with the forward and reverse component morphism ledgers; exactness is the
componentwise identity-composite condition; and no-host-leak follows because
all rows are built from $\CategoryUp$, $\FunctorUp$, $\NatTransUp$,
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\Cont$, and the displayed component histories.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.NatTransUp.NatTransPrefixNaturalIso\_component\_cycle\_empty\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier rows are obtained by unpacking
\autoref{def:nattrans-prefix-natural-isomorphism-carrier}. Each direction is
a prefix natural-transformation hom row, and each component is classified by
$\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentClassifier}_{p,q,a}$ or
$\mathsf{NatTransPrefixComponentClassifier}_{q,p,a}$.

Stability is componentwise. Transport preserves the displayed component
carrier, and vertical composition closes the composites used in the identity
conditions. Swapping the forward and reverse families gives the inverse row,
because the two identity-composite requirements are symmetric in the displayed
definition.

The ledger and exactness rows are the public rows already present in the
component-family surface. The only histories recorded are the two prefix
functor ledgers and the forward and reverse component morphism ledgers, and
the exactness condition says precisely that the two displayed vertical
composites classify as empty identity components at every unary object. No
additional host datum is introduced.
\end{proof}
