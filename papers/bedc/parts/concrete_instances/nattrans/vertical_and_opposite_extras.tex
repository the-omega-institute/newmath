\begin{theorem}[NatTrans vertical composition naturality]
\label{thm:nattrans-vertical-composition-naturality}
Let \(F\), \(G\), and \(H\) be certified \(\FunctorUp\) carriers with the same certified source category and target category. Let \(\alpha:F\Rightarrow G\) and \(\beta:G\Rightarrow H\) be admitted \(\NatTransUp\) carriers. For every source morphism \(f:C\to C'\), the vertical component family \((\beta\circ\alpha)_X=\beta_X\circ\alpha_X\) satisfies the target morphism-classifier naturality square
\[
  H(f)\circ(\beta\circ\alpha)_C
  \sim
  (\beta\circ\alpha)_{C'}\circ F(f).
\]
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The shared source and target categories are part of the \(\NatTransUp\) source specification in \autoref{def:nattrans-source-specification}. By the functor carrier definition in \autoref{def:functor-carrier}, the symbols \(F(f)\), \(G(f)\), and \(H(f)\) denote the corresponding target-category morphism images. By \autoref{def:nattrans-carrier}, each admitted natural transformation is an object-indexed component family with a naturality witness for every source morphism.

The \(\NatTransUp\) stability certificate in \autoref{def:nattrans-stability-certificate} includes vertical composition closure. Applying that field to \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) gives an admitted \(\NatTransUp\) carrier from \(F\) to \(H\) whose component at \(X\) is \(\beta_X\circ\alpha_X\). The same stability certificate lists naturality square validity for every source morphism, so applying that naturality field to this composite carrier and to \(f:C\to C'\) gives
\[
  H(f)\circ(\beta\circ\alpha)_C
  \sim
  (\beta\circ\alpha)_{C'}\circ F(f).
\]
This is the displayed classifier witness for the composite component family.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Opposite natural transformation certificate data]
\label{def:opposite-nattrans-certificate-data}
Let $\mathcal C$ and $\mathcal D$ be $\CategoryUp$ certificates, let $F,G:\mathcal C\to\mathcal D$ be $\FunctorUp$ certificates, and let
$\alpha:F\Rightarrow G$ be a $\NatTransUp$ certificate with component family $\alpha_X:F_0(X)\to G_0(X)$ in $\mathcal D$. Over the
opposite-functor certificates of \autoref{def:opposite-functor-certificate-data}, define $\alpha^{\mathrm{op}}:G^{\mathrm{op}}\Rightarrow F^{\mathrm{op}}$ by the same raw component family
$$
(\alpha^{\mathrm{op}})_X:=\alpha_X.
$$
The component is typed in $\mathcal D^{\mathrm{op}}$ because the hom-carrier of \autoref{def:opposite-category-certificate-data} reads the original
morphism $\alpha_X:F_0(X)\to G_0(X)$ as a displayed morphism $G_0(X)\to F_0(X)$. For a displayed morphism $u:X\to Y$ in
$\mathcal C^{\mathrm{op}}$, the naturality ledger is the classifier-symmetric form of the original naturality ledger for the same raw morphism
$u:Y\to X$ in $\mathcal C$, with both target composites read through opposite composition.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Opposite natural transformation certificate]
\label{thm:opposite-natural-transformation-certificate}
If $\mathcal C$ and $\mathcal D$ satisfy the $\CategoryUp$ certificate obligations, $F,G:\mathcal C\to\mathcal D$ satisfy the $\FunctorUp$
certificate obligations, and $\alpha:F\Rightarrow G$ satisfies the $\NatTransUp$ certificate obligations of \autoref{def:nattrans-certificate-obligations},
then the data of \autoref{def:opposite-nattrans-certificate-data} satisfy the $\NatTransUp$ certificate obligations from $G^{\mathrm{op}}$ to
$F^{\mathrm{op}}$ over $\mathcal C^{\mathrm{op}}$ and $\mathcal D^{\mathrm{op}}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By \autoref{thm:opposite-category-certificate}, the source and target opposite data are certified categories. By \autoref{thm:opposite-functor-certificate},
$G^{\mathrm{op}}$ and $F^{\mathrm{op}}$ are certified functors with common source $\mathcal C^{\mathrm{op}}$ and common target $\mathcal D^{\mathrm{op}}$.
This supplies the source specification required by \autoref{def:nattrans-source-specification}.

For the carrier field, let $X$ be a source object. The component field of $\alpha$ gives $\alpha_X:F_0(X)\to G_0(X)$ in $\mathcal D$.
Since the opposite hom-carrier reverses endpoints and leaves the raw morphism classifier unchanged, the same raw morphism is a component
$(\alpha^{\mathrm{op}})_X:G^{\mathrm{op}}_0(X)\to F^{\mathrm{op}}_0(X)$ in $\mathcal D^{\mathrm{op}}$. Thus the carrier and pattern fields of
\autoref{def:nattrans-carrier} and \autoref{def:nattrans-pattern-specification} are the original component family with the endpoints read oppositely.

The classifier field is inherited componentwise. The target morphism classifier of $\mathcal D^{\mathrm{op}}$ is the target morphism classifier
of $\mathcal D$ on the same raw component representatives, so componentwise reflexivity, transitivity, and classifier replacement are exactly the
corresponding fields listed in \autoref{def:nattrans-classifier-specification} and \autoref{def:nattrans-stability-certificate} for $\alpha$.

It remains to check naturality. Let $u:X\to Y$ be displayed in $\mathcal C^{\mathrm{op}}$. The same raw morphism is $u:Y\to X$ in
$\mathcal C$. Naturality of $\alpha$ in $\mathcal D$ gives the target morphism-classifier comparison
$$
G_1(u)\circ_{\mathcal D}\alpha_Y \sim_{\mathcal D} \alpha_X\circ_{\mathcal D}F_1(u).
$$
Applying symmetry of the codomain morphism classifier gives
$$
\alpha_X\circ_{\mathcal D}F_1(u) \sim_{\mathcal D} G_1(u)\circ_{\mathcal D}\alpha_Y.
$$
By \autoref{def:opposite-category-certificate-data} and \autoref{def:opposite-functor-certificate-data}, these two original composites are exactly
$$
F^{\mathrm{op}}_1(u)\circ_{\mathcal D^{\mathrm{op}}}(\alpha^{\mathrm{op}})_X
$$
and
$$
(\alpha^{\mathrm{op}})_Y\circ_{\mathcal D^{\mathrm{op}}}G^{\mathrm{op}}_1(u),
$$
respectively. Since the morphism classifier is unchanged by the opposite category data, this is the required naturality square in $\mathcal D^{\mathrm{op}}$.

The remaining stability and ledger clauses are inherited without changing raw data. Identity components and vertical component composites are the same raw
component histories, with their endpoints read in the opposite target category; the naturality argument above applies to each displayed source morphism. The
ledger policy of \autoref{def:nattrans-ledger-policy} therefore records the same functor ledgers, component morphism ledgers, and naturality derivations,
now indexed by the two opposite-functor certificates.
\end{proof}
