\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyCompletionProductUp$}

Following Bourbaki product uniformities, Kelley completion terminology, and Bishop--Bridges constructive metric completion, the $\CauchyCompletionProductUp$ packet records the finite BEDC certificate for forming the completion-facing product of two displayed Cauchy-completion routes. It consumes the unit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionunit-namecert}, the counit route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completion-counit-namecert}, the monadic completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionmonad-namecert}, the reflection packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionreflection-namecert}, the regular readback surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite product handoff for two coordinate routes; it is not a quotient completion product, not a selected pair of representatives, not countable choice, not a host product theorem, and not an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionProduct carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-product-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionProductUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(L,R,U_L,U_R,K_L,K_R,M,F,S_L,S_R,D_L,D_R,G_L,G_R,E_L,E_R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ and $R$ are the left and right completion-facing source rows whose product read is being exposed.
\item $U_L$ and $U_R$ are the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ routes admitting the two displayed source rows into completion-facing consumers.
\item $K_L$ and $K_R$ are the $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ routes returning coordinate reads to their terminal real seals when the coordinates are already displayed.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ bind-and-return ledger tying the two coordinate routes to a single completion-facing product packet.
\item $F$ is the $\CompletionReflectionUp$ seal that keeps the product read on the displayed completion boundary rather than on an external host product.
\item $S_L$ and $S_R$ are the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows for the two coordinates.
\item $D_L$ and $D_R$ are the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledgers used by the two coordinate window reads.
\item $G_L$ and $G_R$ are the coordinate $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows assembled from the corresponding window and dyadic ledger rows.
\item $E_L$ and $E_R$ are the $\RealUp$ terminal seal rows reached after the coordinate readbacks.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for all coordinate, monad, reflection, window, dyadic, seal, continuation, provenance, and naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the product read through the coordinate unit routes, coordinate counit routes, monad ledger, reflection seal, windows, dyadic ledgers, regular readbacks, and real seals.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyCompletionProductUp$, $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\CompletionReflectionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionProductUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, host product equality, selected representatives, countable-choice schedules, open-cover compactness, product completeness of $\RealUp$, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionProduct NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-product-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CauchyCompletionProductUp$ carrier $Z$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionProductUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two completion-facing source rows, the two unit routes, the two counit routes, the monad ledger, the reflection seal, the two stream-window rows, the two dyadic ledgers, the coordinate regular readbacks, the two real seals, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item coordinatewise completion handoff, requiring each product read to split through the left and right $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ routes, return through the corresponding $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ routes when a coordinate is already displayed, and expose the two $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RealUp$ routes before the product read is consumed;
\item product seal compatibility, requiring the $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ ledger and $\CompletionReflectionUp$ seal to bind the two coordinate routes into a single product-facing packet without adding a host product equality or quotient-completion equality;
\item transport stability, requiring $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay to preserve the same coordinate routes, monad ledger, reflection seal, package provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row;
\item ledger exactness, stating that the packet exports exactly the rows sourced by $\Pkg$ over $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$, $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\CompletionReflectionUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$, and exports no selected representatives, countable-choice schedule, product-completeness theorem for $\RealUp$, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-product-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the two source rows, the unit and counit coordinate routes, the monad ledger, the reflection seal, the window, tolerance, readback, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The coordinate handoff reads the product packet one coordinate at a time. The left coordinate must pass through $U_L,K_L,S_L,D_L,E_L$, and the right coordinate must pass through $U_R,K_R,S_R,D_R,E_R$, with the regular readback route named by the surrounding $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows before either terminal seal is consumed.

The product seal is then the finite bind-and-reflect step. The monad ledger $M$ ties the two coordinate routes into one product-facing route, and the reflection row $F$ keeps that route inside the displayed completion boundary rather than creating an external host product.

Transport stability is componentwise transport through $H$ followed by replay through $C$. Since the provenance row $P$ and local naming row $N$ source only the displayed rows, the excluded quotient, representative-choice, product-completeness, and ambient-completeness principles have no row from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionProduct coordinate handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-product-coordinate-handoff}
For an accepted $\CauchyCompletionProductUp$ carrier
$$
Z=(L,R,U_L,U_R,K_L,K_R,M,F,S_L,S_R,D_L,D_R,G_L,G_R,E_L,E_R,H,C,P,N),
$$
every completion-facing product consumer factors through the coordinate route
$$
L,R\longmapsto U_L,U_R\longmapsto K_L,K_R\longmapsto S_L,S_R\longmapsto D_L,D_R\longmapsto G_L,G_R\longmapsto E_L,E_R\longmapsto M,F,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the product handoff is coordinatewise finite BEDC data, not a quotient product, not a host product equality, not a selected pair of representatives, and not countable choice or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and begin with the two source rows $L$ and $R$. A product-facing consumer cannot inspect a product point before the unit rows $U_L$ and $U_R$ have admitted the two displayed coordinate routes.

For each coordinate, the counit row returns already displayed data to the real-facing boundary. The stream-window rows $S_L,S_R$ and dyadic ledgers $D_L,D_R$ account for the finite precision requests that produce the corresponding regular readbacks and terminal real seals $E_L,E_R$.

The monad row $M$ binds these two coordinate reads into one completion-facing packet, and the reflection row $F$ records that the packet remains on the displayed completion boundary. Transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming preserve the same route through $H,C,P,N$.

No further coordinate occurs in the carrier. Hence every product consumer factors through the displayed coordinate handoff and cannot obtain quotient equality, host product equality, selected representatives, countable choice, or ambient completeness from this packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionProduct non-escape]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-product-nonescape}
An accepted $\CauchyCompletionProductUp$ carrier exports no read outside the finite packet
$$
(L,R,U_L,U_R,K_L,K_R,M,F,S_L,S_R,D_L,D_R,G_L,G_R,E_L,E_R,H,C,P,N).
$$
In particular, it cannot expose a product-completion consumer without the two coordinate unit routes, the two coordinate counit routes, the monad ledger, the completion-reflection seal, the stream-window rows, the dyadic ledgers, the regular readback rows, the real seals, componentwise transport, displayed continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume a consumer reads an accepted product packet. By carrier admission, the only available rows are the fields displayed in \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-product-carrier}.

If the consumer drops a coordinate unit row, one source has not entered the completion-facing surface. If it drops a coordinate counit row, an already displayed coordinate cannot return to its real seal. If it drops a window, dyadic ledger, regular readback, or real seal, the corresponding coordinate read is not displayed at the requested precision.

If the consumer drops $M$ or $F$, the two coordinate routes are no longer bound and reflected as a single completion-facing product route. If it drops $H,C,P$, or $N$, the packet loses transport, replay, provenance, or local certificate evidence.

The carrier has no additional row. Therefore no product-completion read escapes the displayed packet, and none of the excluded quotient, host-product, representative-choice, countable-choice, or ambient-completeness principles is exported.
\end{proof}
