\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SequentialClosureUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-sequentialclosure-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\SequentialClosureUp$ packet records the finite route by which a displayed sequence-limit witness may be consumed as closure evidence without importing host topological closure. It sits over the topology surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the finite windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the located-set boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedset-namecert}, and the sequence-consumer interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sequentialcontinuity-namecert}. The packet is a finite NameCert surface for certified sequence-limit closure; it is not an ambient closure operator, not a compactness theorem, not quotient stream equality, not arbitrary subsequence choice, and not completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[SequentialClosure carrier]
\label{def:sequential-closure-carrier}
A $\SequentialClosureUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(T,M,S,Q,L,U,W,R,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the ambient $\TopologyUp$ row, including the named neighbourhood indices and the classifier transport available to topology-facing consumers.
\item $M$ is the ambient $\MetricUp$ row over the same visible carrier, including the distance classifier and metric comparison rows used when neighbourhood tests are read metrically.
\item $S$ is the displayed subset or $\LocatedSetUp$ row whose admitted points form the source surface for the sequence.
\item $Q$ is the sequence source row. It records a finite $\StreamNameUp$ schedule of admitted sequence entries over $S$ together with the point-name rows inspected by the packet.
\item $L$ is the candidate limit row, naming the target point over the common topology and metric source without asserting that it is host-closure equivalent to $S$.
\item $U$ is the neighbourhood and metric test ledger. Each displayed request records either a topology neighbourhood row around $L$ or a metric tolerance row around $L$, together with the sequence-entry test that answers it.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window ledger naming the inspected sequence windows and the index budgets consumed by $U$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row translating the displayed metric tolerance windows into regular rational readback data before the Real distance boundary is consulted.
\item $A$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row certifying the accepted distance comparison downstream of $R$ and $U$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the topology row, metric row, subset or located-set row, sequence source, candidate limit, test ledger, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat handoff, Real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a closure-consumer request through $T,M,S,Q,L,U,W,R,A$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TopologyUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\LocatedSetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialClosureUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host topological closure, arbitrary subsequence choice, quotient stream equality, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or a detached $\SequentialContinuityUp$ theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SequentialClosure NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:sequential-closure-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SequentialClosureUp$ carrier $K=(T,M,S,Q,L,U,W,R,A,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialClosureUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the ambient topology, ambient metric, subset or located-set row, sequence source, candidate limit, neighbourhood and metric tests, finite StreamName windows, RegSeqRat handoff, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming row;
\item neighbourhood-test stability, requiring each topology or metric request around $L$ to replay through the displayed test ledger $U$ over the same $T,M,S,Q,L$ rows;
\item sequence-window coverage, requiring every accepted closure read to expose the finite StreamName windows $W$ that name the inspected sequence entries and index budgets;
\item limit-row handoff, requiring the RegSeqRat row $R$ and Real seal $A$ to be read downstream of the displayed metric test ledger before a distance conclusion is exported;
\item classifier transport, requiring all $\hsame$ comparison of closure packets to preserve the topology, metric, subset, sequence, limit, test, window, handoff, seal, continuation, provenance, and local NameCert rows componentwise;
\item non-escape from the host closure boundary, excluding host topological closure, arbitrary subsequence choice, quotient stream equality, open-cover compactness, and detached $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:sequential-closure-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the topology row, metric row, subset or located-set row, sequence source, candidate limit, test ledger, finite StreamName windows, RegSeqRat handoff, Real seal, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Neighbourhood-test stability is forced by the continuation row $C$. A closure-facing request reaches a neighbourhood or metric conclusion only by replaying the common $T,M,S,Q,L$ source rows and then consulting the displayed ledger $U$.

Sequence-window coverage is the next step in the same replay. The ledger $W$ is the only place where inspected sequence windows and index budgets are stored, so an accepted read cannot replace the displayed sequence by an arbitrary subsequence or by quotient stream equality.

The limit-row handoff is downstream of those windows. The RegSeqRat row $R$ translates the metric tolerance read into regular rational data, and the Real seal $A$ certifies the accepted distance comparison only after $U$ and $W$ have been consumed.

Finally, componentwise transport by $H$ preserves every displayed coordinate, while $P$ and $N$ package only that finite route. Since host topological closure, arbitrary subsequence choice, quotient stream equality, open-cover compactness, and detached Real completeness have no carrier row, no continuation edge, and no provenance entry, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SequentialClosure sequence-limit handoff]
\label{thm:sequential-closure-sequence-limit-handoff}
Let $K=(T,M,S,Q,L,U,W,R,A,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SequentialClosureUp$ packet. Every closure consumer of the displayed candidate limit factors through
$$
T,M,S,Q,L \longmapsto U,W \longmapsto R,A \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the consumer receives closure evidence only through the displayed sequence windows, the RegSeqRat handoff, the Real seal, the topology and metric tests, and the local NameCert rows; no host closure operator, arbitrary sequence selection, quotient stream equality, compactness principle, or $\RealUp$ completeness theorem is exported by the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a closure consumer of the candidate limit row $L$. Carrier admission gives access to $L$ only together with the common topology row $T$, metric row $M$, subset or located-set row $S$, and sequence source row $Q$.

Replay the continuation row $C$. The consumer must pass through the neighbourhood and metric test ledger $U$ and then through the finite StreamName window ledger $W$, so the inspected sequence entries and their index budgets remain displayed throughout the read.

The metric side of the request then factors through $R$ and $A$. The RegSeqRat handoff records the regular rational readback for the tolerance window, and the Real seal records the accepted distance comparison after the topology and metric tests have already been displayed.

Transport, provenance, and local naming through $H,P,N$ preserve the same dependency order. This proves the stated factorization and leaves no available row from which a consumer could extract host closure, arbitrary sequence selection, quotient stream equality, compactness, or detached completeness.
\end{proof}
