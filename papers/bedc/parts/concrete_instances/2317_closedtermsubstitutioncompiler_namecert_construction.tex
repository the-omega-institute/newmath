\chapter{ClosedTermSubstitutionCompiler NameCert Construction}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closedtermsubstitutioncompiler-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$ surface packages the MetaCIC closed-term substitution boundary as a finite compiler-facing naming certificate. Its carrier is not a full evaluator correctness theorem. It records the concrete rows by which $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$, $\mathsf{shift}$, $\mathsf{substitute}$, $\mathsf{substitute\_closed}$, $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ form the local boundary used by self-compilation consumers.

The compiler-facing packet consumes the finite closed-term boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedtermsubstitutionboundary-namecert} before it exposes the $\mathsf{substitute\_closed}$ and $\mathsf{shift\_closed}$ witness rows. This dependency is boundary reuse: it records that the compiler schedule reads the closed substitution and shift surface through the registered packet, not that the packet proves evaluator correctness or subject reduction.

\begin{definition}[ClosedTermSubstitutionCompiler carrier]
\label{def:closed-term-substitution-compiler}
A $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(T,D,V,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the MetaCIC term-generator row, recording the closed constructor surface for variables, applications, binders, and universes;
\item $D$ is the $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ boundary row, recording the depth classifier used to admit closed or context-closed source terms;
\item $V$ is the operation row containing the syntactic $\mathsf{shift}$ and $\mathsf{substitute}$ traversals;
\item $F$ is the fixed-boundary witness row recording $\mathsf{shift\_closed}$ and $\mathsf{substitute\_closed}$ as inertness facts on the classified slice;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $T,D,V,F$, the continuation row, the package row, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a compiler consumer reads the closedness boundary, applies the syntactic operation, and returns to the fixed-boundary witness;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over MetaCIC terms, $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$, $\mathsf{shift}$, $\mathsf{substitute}$, $\mathsf{substitute\_closed}$, $\BHist$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no subject-reduction theorem for open evaluation, no semantic substitution theorem for arbitrary host syntax, no truth predicate for CIC, and no unrecorded binder budget.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedTermSubstitutionCompiler boundary]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-boundary}
Every accepted $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$ packet exposes the closed-term substitution boundary as the finite route
$$
\begin{aligned}
  \mathsf{ClosedAt}(d,t)
  &\longrightarrow
  \mathsf{substitute}(d,v,t)=t.
\end{aligned}
$$
The same packet also exposes the companion shift boundary
$$
\begin{aligned}
  \mathsf{ClosedAt}(n,t)
  &\longrightarrow
  \mathsf{shift}(n,1,t)=t.
\end{aligned}
$$
Both routes are read through the displayed $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, and neither route may be replaced by semantic evaluation correctness, a host binder convention, or an ambient compiler theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $K=(T,D,V,F,H,C,P,N)$. The term row $T$ supplies the authorized MetaCIC constructors, the boundary row $D$ supplies the displayed $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ classifier, and the operation row $V$ supplies only the syntactic traversals $\mathsf{shift}$ and $\mathsf{substitute}$.

Read the witness row $F$ through the continuation row $C$. The substitution witness is the fixed-boundary equality $\mathsf{substitute}(d,v,t)=t$ under $\mathsf{ClosedAt}(d,t)$, and the shift witness is the matching equality at the shift cutoff. The arbitrary value $v$ is not consumed because the classifier shows that the target variable is absent at the boundary.

The non-escape clause is carrier exactness. Since no coordinate stores evaluator correctness, host-level binder semantics, or a global compiler theorem, a consumer can extract only the displayed closed-boundary route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedTermSubstitutionCompiler NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-compiler}, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $K=(T,D,V,F,H,C,P,N)$;
\item classifier transport for the $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ row and the operation-witness rows under componentwise $\hsame$;
\item substitution-boundary exactness, requiring every accepted substitution read to cite the displayed $\mathsf{substitute\_closed}$ witness before exposing a compiler-facing boundary claim;
\item shift-boundary compatibility, requiring binder-depth movement to cite the displayed $\mathsf{shift\_closed}$ witness before a deeper-context read is accepted;
\item non-escape for open-term semantics, subject reduction, host CIC truth, unregistered variables, and any compiler correctness theorem not stored in the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp.ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerPacket\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-compiler}. The packet lists the term generator, closedness classifier, operation row, fixed-boundary witnesses, transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local certificate row.

Transport is componentwise. The rows $T,D,V,F$ move only through the displayed $\hsame$ entries, and the continuation row replays the same reads after transport.

For exactness, inspect a substitution consumer. The route must read the $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ row and then the $\mathsf{substitute\_closed}$ witness before it may expose the compiler-facing equality. The corresponding shift consumer reads the same boundary classifier and the $\mathsf{shift\_closed}$ witness before using binder-depth movement.

The excluded principles have no carrier coordinate. Thus the local $\NameCert$ admits only the five displayed obligations and cannot project an unrecorded semantic theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedTermSubstitutionCompiler self-compile route]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-self-compile-route}
In any compiler-facing read of an accepted $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$ packet, the self-compilation substitution step passes through the schedule
$$
\begin{gathered}
\mathsf{ClosedAt}\ \longrightarrow\ \mathsf{substitute}\ \longrightarrow\ \mathsf{substitute\_closed}\ \longrightarrow\ \NameCert_{\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp}.
\end{gathered}
$$
The route keeps the source term inside the registered boundary ledger. It may be composed with the displayed $\mathsf{shift\_closed}$ row for binder movement, but it does not close evaluator soundness, subject reduction, or total self-compilation.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp.ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerPacket\_self\_compile\_route}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the compiler consumer's closed source row. The carrier exposes this row only through $D$, so the consumer first reads the displayed $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ boundary.

Next read the operation row $V$ and the witness row $F$. The syntactic operation is $\mathsf{substitute}$, and the accepted boundary witness is $\mathsf{substitute\_closed}$, so the attempted substitution returns the same registered term at the boundary.

Finally repack the route through $C,P,N$. The continuation, provenance, and local naming rows record a self-compilation boundary step, while the absence of evaluator-soundness and subject-reduction coordinates fixes the stated scope.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedTermSubstitutionCompiler shift-substitute compatibility]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-shift-substitute-compatibility}
In every accepted $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$ packet, the displayed $\mathsf{shift\_closed}$ witness and the displayed $\mathsf{substitute\_closed}$ witness share the same closed-boundary classifier before any compiler-facing read may consume either equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier row $D$ before the witness row $F$. By \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-boundary}, the substitution and shift routes are both exposed as closed-boundary routes rather than as evaluator facts.

The NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-namecert-obligations} require substitution-boundary exactness and shift-boundary compatibility. Thus both witnesses are admitted only after the same displayed $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ classifier has been read.

The self-compile route of \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-self-compile-route} may compose with the shift row, but the composition stays inside the shared classifier boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedTermSubstitutionCompiler public non-escape]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-public-nonescape}
No public consumer of an accepted $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$ packet can read evaluator soundness, subject reduction, host CIC truth, or open-term semantics from the packet. Public reads are limited to the displayed $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$, $\mathsf{shift}$, $\mathsf{substitute}$, witness, route, provenance, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-compiler}. It contains rows for the MetaCIC term generator, closedness classifier, syntactic operations, fixed-boundary witnesses, transports, routes, provenance, and local naming.

\autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-boundary} and \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-self-compile-route} describe exactly the public routes: closedness, syntactic operation, fixed-boundary witness, and local NameCert.

The excluded principles are absent from the carrier and are rejected by the non-escape obligation in \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-namecert-obligations}. Hence no public projection can recover evaluator soundness, subject reduction, host CIC truth, or open-term semantics.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedTermSubstitutionCompiler boundary-ledger exactness]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-boundary-ledger-exactness}
In every accepted $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$ packet, each compiler-facing substitution read and shift read passes through the displayed boundary ledger
$$
\mathsf{ClosedAt},\ \mathsf{shift},\ \mathsf{substitute},\ \mathsf{shift\_closed},\ \mathsf{substitute\_closed}.
$$
No read may expose a substitution equality, a shift equality, or a self-compilation boundary step unless it first cites the $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ classifier row and then the corresponding operation and witness row stored in the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $K=(T,D,V,F,H,C,P,N)$ of \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-compiler}. The row $D$ is the only displayed $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ source, $V$ is the only operation row for $\mathsf{shift}$ and $\mathsf{substitute}$, and $F$ is the only row containing $\mathsf{shift\_closed}$ and $\mathsf{substitute\_closed}$.

For a substitution read, the route described in \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-self-compile-route} must enter through $D$, read $\mathsf{substitute}$ from $V$, and consume $\mathsf{substitute\_closed}$ from $F$ before reaching the local $\NameCert$ row.

For a shift read, \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-shift-substitute-compatibility} forces the same closed-boundary classifier before $\mathsf{shift}$ and $\mathsf{shift\_closed}$ are consumed. The continuation and package rows then record only that displayed route.

Since no other coordinate stores substitution or shift evidence, any compiler-facing equality that skips this ledger is outside the accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ClosedTermSubstitutionCompiler binder-budget non-escape]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-binder-budget-nonescape}
An accepted $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$ packet does not export binder-budget semantics, open-term substitution semantics, evaluator soundness, subject reduction, host CIC truth, or any unregistered variable convention. Public reads are confined to the displayed $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$, $\mathsf{shift}$, $\mathsf{substitute}$, $\mathsf{shift\_closed}$, $\mathsf{substitute\_closed}$, $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-compiler}. It names the term generator, closedness classifier, syntactic operation row, fixed-boundary witness row, transports, routes, provenance, and local certificate row.

Binder-budget semantics would require a separate row describing how budgets are allocated or consumed across arbitrary open binders. Open-term substitution semantics would require a row for variables not admitted by the displayed $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$ boundary. Neither row occurs in $K$.

\autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-public-nonescape} already excludes evaluator soundness, subject reduction, host CIC truth, and open-term semantics. The boundary-ledger exactness of \autoref{thm:closed-term-substitution-compiler-boundary-ledger-exactness} further confines every public read to the displayed closed-term ledger.

Thus a consumer may use the packet as a closed substitution compiler boundary, but it cannot project binder-budget or open-term semantics from the same certificate.
\end{proof}
