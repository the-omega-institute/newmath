\begin{definition}[Sequential real completion carrier]
\label{def:sequential-real-completion-carrier}
A $\SequentialRealCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row exposing the inspected observations of the candidate regular Cauchy name.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row naming the regular rational approximants visible in those windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used to compare window observations against the requested precision.
\item $Q$ is the regular-Cauchy coherence row, recording tail agreement and modulus coverage only through displayed finite windows.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached after $W,R,D,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff row, recording that the same regular route is available to completion-facing consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the stream windows, regular readback, dyadic ledger, coherence row, real seal, limit seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $W,R,D,Q,E,L,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient representatives of Cauchy sequences, host equality of completed objects, countable choice over tails, an external limit selector, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{theorem}[Sequential real completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:sequential-real-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SequentialRealCompletionUp$ carrier
$$

the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialRealCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, StreamName window admission through $W$, RegSeqRat readback through $R$, DyadicRatCore tolerance admission through $D$, regular-Cauchy coherence through $Q$, RealUp seal admission through $E$, CauchyLimitSeal handoff through $L$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:sequential-real-completion-carrier}. The rows $W,R,D,Q$ are the regular Cauchy source route, while $E$ and $L$ are the only real-facing and completion-facing endpoints.

The finite route starts with windows. A precision request reads the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ row $W$, extracts the regular rational observations through $R$, and checks the dyadic tolerance data through $D$ before the coherence row $Q$ may be consumed.

The endpoint rows are downstream of that same route. The $\RealUp$ seal $E$ and the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff $L$ read only the displayed regular-Cauchy packet, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same packet under transport, replay, source, and naming.

Since no carrier coordinate contains quotient representatives, host equality, countable-choice schedules, external limit selectors, or ambient real completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
