$$

\item $A$ is the $\ArchimedeanOrderedFieldUp$ evidence row that supplies the ordered-field comparison route over the displayed real endpoint.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicFloorUp$ scale row that binds the request to a finite dyadic lower grid point and its displayed dyadic successor.
\item $Z$ is the $\IntUp$ integer candidate row, sourced through the $\NatUp$, $\RatUp$, and sign data required to read it as an integer bracket endpoint.
\item $S$ is the successor bracket row, recording the displayed integer successor of $Z$ and the two comparisons $Z\leq R$ and $R<S$ through the rows $A,D$.

\item real-read exposure through the $\RealUp$ or regular-Cauchy rows in $R$;

\begin{theorem}[ArchimedeanIntegerPart bracket route]
\label{thm:archimedean-integer-part-bracket-route}

\begin{proof}

The successor row $S$ packages the bracket $Z\leq R<S$ for downstream decimal and real-approximation consumers. Because $S$ is downstream of $R,A,D,Z$, every bracket read factors through the displayed finite route.
