\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\QuotientRingUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-quotientring-namecert}

The quotient-ring interface $\QuotientRingUp$ packages the carrier of cosets of an ideal in a ring. It depends on $\NameCert_{\IdealUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ideal-namecert}) and lifts $\NameCert_{\QuotientGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-quotientgroup-namecert}) to the multiplicative structure.

\begin{definition}[Ideal coset relation for quotient rings]
\label{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, zero $0_R$, and multiplication $\cdot_R$. Let $I$ be an $\IdealUp$ object over $R$, written as a carried predicate $I(x)$. Write $x-_R y$ for $x+_R(-_R y)$. The ideal-coset relation used by the $\QuotientRingUp$ carrier is
$$
a\equiv_I a'\Longleftrightarrow C(a)\land C(a')\land I(a-_R a').
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[QuotientRing multiplication descends to ideal cosets]
\label{thm:quotientring-multiplication-descends-to-cosets}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate and let $I$ be an $\IdealUp$ object over $R$. Assume the ideal certificate supplies additive closure, classifier transport, and two-sided absorption by every carried ring endpoint, as required by \autoref{sec:ideal-certificate}. For carried endpoints $a,a',b,b'$, if
$$
a\equiv_I a'\qquad\text{and}\qquad b\equiv_I b',
$$
then
$$
(a\cdot_R b)\equiv_I(a'\cdot_R b').
$$
Thus the multiplication operation on the $\QuotientRingUp$ coset carrier is representative-independent.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.QuotientRingUp.QuotientRingIdealCoset\_mul\_descends}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.QuotientRingUp.QuotientRingIdealCoset\_multiplication\_descent}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}. Set $d_a:=a-_R a'$ and $d_b:=b-_R b'$. The two coset hypotheses give $I(d_a)$ and $I(d_b)$, together with the carrier witnesses for $a,a',b,b'$.

By the right absorption field of the $\IdealUp$ certificate, $I(d_a)$ and the carrier witness for $b$ give
$$
I(d_a\cdot_R b).
$$
By the left absorption field, $I(d_b)$ and the carrier witness for $a'$ give
$$
I(a'\cdot_R d_b).
$$
Additive closure in the ideal therefore gives
$$
I((d_a\cdot_R b)+_R(a'\cdot_R d_b)).
$$

It remains to identify this ideal element with the difference of the two products. By \autoref{thm:ring-right-distributivity-over-subtraction},
$$
d_a\cdot_R b\sim_R(a\cdot_R b)-_R(a'\cdot_R b),
$$
and by \autoref{thm:ring-left-distributivity-over-subtraction},
$$
a'\cdot_R d_b\sim_R(a'\cdot_R b)-_R(a'\cdot_R b').
$$
Addition congruence transports the sum to
$$
((a\cdot_R b)-_R(a'\cdot_R b))+_R((a'\cdot_R b)-_R(a'\cdot_R b')).
$$
The additive abelian-group rows of \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}, with the right inverse law from \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse} and the right zero law from \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-zero}, cancel the middle pair $-_R(a'\cdot_R b)$ and $a'\cdot_R b$. Hence
$$
(d_a\cdot_R b)+_R(a'\cdot_R d_b)\sim_R(a\cdot_R b)-_R(a'\cdot_R b').
$$
Classifier transport for the ideal moves the displayed membership across this classifier edge, giving
$$
I((a\cdot_R b)-_R(a'\cdot_R b')).
$$
Multiplication closure in the $\RingUp$ certificate supplies the carrier witnesses for $a\cdot_R b$ and $a'\cdot_R b'$. These two carrier witnesses together with the displayed ideal-membership statement are exactly
$$
(a\cdot_R b)\equiv_I(a'\cdot_R b')
$$
by \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuotientRing additive descent to ideal cosets]
\label{thm:quotientring-additive-descent}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, zero $0_R$, and multiplication $\cdot_R$. Let $I$ be an $\IdealUp$ object over $R$, written as a carried predicate $I(x)$. Assume the ideal certificate supplies additive closure, additive inverse closure, and classifier transport for $I$, as specified in \autoref{sec:ideal-certificate}. If
$$
a\equiv_I a' \qquad \text{and} \qquad b\equiv_I b',
$$
then the additive and inverse operations respect ideal cosets:
$$
(a+_R b)\equiv_I(a'+_R b')
$$
and
$$
(-_R a)\equiv_I(-_R a').
$$
Thus the additive part of the $\QuotientRingUp$ coset carrier is representative-independent.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.QuotientRingUp.QuotientRingIdealCoset\_additive\_descent}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.QuotientRingUp.QuotientRingIdealCoset\_add\_descends}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}. Set
$$
d_a:=a-_R a', \qquad d_b:=b-_R b'.
$$
The two coset hypotheses give $C(a)$, $C(a')$, $C(b)$, $C(b')$, together with $I(d_a)$ and $I(d_b)$.

For addition, ideal additive closure gives
$$
I(d_a+_R d_b).
$$
The additive abelian-group component of the ring certificate, using \autoref{thm:abgroup-middle-four-interchange} for the operation $+_R$, identifies
$$
(a+_R(-_R a'))+_R(b+_R(-_R b'))
\sim_R
(a+_R b)+_R((-_R a')+_R(-_R b')).
$$
By \autoref{thm:ring-neg-add}, read in the additive component of $R$,
$$
-_R(a'+_R b')\sim_R(-_R a')+_R(-_R b').
$$
Addition congruence, symmetry, and transitivity of $\sim_R$ therefore give
$$
d_a+_R d_b\sim_R(a+_R b)-_R(a'+_R b').
$$
Classifier transport for the ideal moves the displayed membership across this classifier edge, so
$$
I((a+_R b)-_R(a'+_R b')).
$$
Ring additive closure supplies $C(a+_R b)$ and $C(a'+_R b')$. These carrier witnesses and the displayed ideal-membership statement are exactly
$$
(a+_R b)\equiv_I(a'+_R b')
$$
by \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}.

For additive inverse, ideal inverse closure applied to $I(d_a)$ gives
$$
I(-_R d_a).
$$
Since $d_a=a+_R(-_R a')$, \autoref{thm:ring-neg-add} gives the classifier comparison
$$
-_R d_a\sim_R(-_R a)+_R(-_R(-_R a')),
$$
and the right-hand side is $(-_R a)-_R(-_R a')$ by the notation of \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}. Classifier transport for the ideal yields
$$
I((-_R a)-_R(-_R a')).
$$
Ring additive inverse closure supplies $C(-_R a)$ and $C(-_R a')$. Repacking these two carrier witnesses with the displayed ideal-membership statement gives
$$
(-_R a)\equiv_I(-_R a')
$$
by \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuotientRing ideal coset relation equivalence]
\label{thm:quotientring-ideal-coset-equivalence}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, zero $0_R$, and multiplication $\cdot_R$. Let $I$ be an $\IdealUp$ object over $R$, written as a carried predicate $I(x)$, and write $x-_R y$ for $x+_R(-_R y)$ as in \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}. Assume the ideal certificate rows named in \autoref{sec:ideal-certificate} supply zero closure, additive-inverse closure, additive closure, and classifier transport:
$$
I(0_R),\qquad
\forall x,\; I(x)\Longrightarrow I(-_R x),\qquad
\forall x,y,\; I(x)\land I(y)\Longrightarrow I(x+_R y),
$$
and
$$
\forall x,y,\; I(x)\land x\sim_R y\Longrightarrow I(y).
$$
Then the relation $\equiv_I$ of \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation} is an equivalence relation on carried ring endpoints: for every $a,b,c$,
$$
C(a)\Longrightarrow a\equiv_I a,
$$
$$
a\equiv_I b\Longrightarrow b\equiv_I a,
$$
and
$$
(a\equiv_I b\land b\equiv_I c)\Longrightarrow a\equiv_I c.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}.

For reflexivity, assume $C(a)$. The zero row gives $I(0_R)$. The additive right-inverse theorem \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-inverse}, read in the additive component of $R$, gives
$$
a-_R a\sim_R0_R.
$$
Classifier symmetry from \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate} reverses this edge, and classifier transport for $I$ gives $I(a-_R a)$. Together with the two copies of $C(a)$, this is $a\equiv_I a$.

For symmetry, assume $a\equiv_I b$. The unfolded hypothesis supplies $C(a)$, $C(b)$, and $I(a-_R b)$. Additive-inverse closure gives
$$
I(-_R(a-_R b)).
$$
The additive abelian-group rows in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} identify the inverse of a difference with the reversed difference:
$$
-_R(a-_R b)\sim_R b-_R a.
$$
Indeed, \autoref{thm:ring-neg-add} rewrites the left endpoint to $(-_R a)+_R(-_R(-_R b))$; \autoref{thm:group-left-inverse-involutive}, read in the additive group of $R$, identifies $-_R(-_R b)$ with $b$; additive commutativity then gives $b+_R(-_R a)$. The classifier edges are composed by \autoref{thm:namecert-equivalence-transitivity}. Classifier transport for $I$ yields $I(b-_R a)$. Repacking $C(b)$, $C(a)$, and this membership gives $b\equiv_I a$.

For transitivity, assume $a\equiv_I b$ and $b\equiv_I c$. Unfolding gives $C(a)$, $C(b)$, $C(c)$, $I(a-_R b)$, and $I(b-_R c)$. Additive closure gives
$$
I((a-_R b)+_R(b-_R c)).
$$
The additive associativity, left-inverse, left-zero, and addition-congruence rows in \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate} give
$$
(a-_R b)+_R(b-_R c)\sim_R a-_R c.
$$
After expanding the two differences, reassociate
$$
(a+_R(-_R b))+_R(b+_R(-_R c))
$$
to
$$
a+_R(((-_R b)+_R b)+_R(-_R c)).
$$
The middle pair collapses to $0_R$ by the additive left-inverse row, and the additive left-zero row reduces the tail to $-_R c$. Thus the expression is classified with $a+_R(-_R c)$, namely $a-_R c$. Classifier transport for $I$ gives $I(a-_R c)$. Repacking this with $C(a)$ and $C(c)$ gives $a\equiv_I c$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuotientRing zero coset classification]
\label{thm:quotientring-zero-coset-classification}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, zero $0_R$, and multiplication $\cdot_R$, with carrier row supplying $C(0_R)$. Let $I$ be an $\IdealUp$ object over $R$, written as a carried predicate $I(x)$, and write $x-_R y$ for $x+_R(-_R y)$ as in \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}. Assume the ideal certificate includes classifier transport as specified in \autoref{sec:ideal-certificate}:
$$
\forall x,y,\; I(x)\land x\sim_R y\Longrightarrow I(y).
$$
Then for every endpoint $a$,
$$
a\equiv_I 0_R \Longleftrightarrow C(a)\land I(a).
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.QuotientRingUp.QuotientRingIdealCoset\_zero\_classification}
\begin{proof}
Fix $a$. We first record the conditional comparison with the zero difference. Suppose $C(a)$ and set $d_0:=a-_R 0_R$. The additive inverse of zero comparison from \autoref{thm:ring-neg-zero}, read in the additive component of $R$, gives
$$
-_R 0_R\sim_R 0_R.
$$
Addition congruence from \autoref{def:ring-stability-certificate}, with the left summand $a$ fixed, gives
$$
a+_R(-_R 0_R)\sim_R a+_R0_R.
$$
The additive right-zero law \autoref{thm:ring-add-right-zero} gives
$$
a+_R0_R\sim_R a.
$$
By transitivity of the carrier classifier from \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate},
$$
d_0\sim_R a.
$$
Thus, whenever $C(a)$ is available, $a-_R 0_R\sim_R a$.

For the forward implication, assume $a\equiv_I 0_R$. Unfolding \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation} gives $C(a)$, $C(0_R)$, and $I(a-_R 0_R)$. Applying the comparison just proved under this $C(a)$ and then the ideal classifier-transport row gives $I(a)$. Hence $C(a)\land I(a)$.

For the reverse implication, assume $C(a)\land I(a)$. The carrier row gives $C(0_R)$. Apply classifier symmetry from \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate} to the displayed comparison $d_0\sim_R a$, obtaining
$$
a\sim_R d_0.
$$
The ideal classifier-transport row carries $I(a)$ across this edge, so $I(d_0)$, equivalently $I(a-_R 0_R)$. Repacking $C(a)$, $C(0_R)$, and this membership through \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation} yields $a\equiv_I 0_R$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[QuotientRingUp public coset certificate]
\label{thm:quotientring-public-coset-certificate}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, zero $0_R$, and multiplication $\cdot_R$. Let $I$ be an $\IdealUp$ object over $R$, written as a carried predicate $I(x)$, and let $\equiv_I$ be the ideal-coset relation of \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}. Then the public $\NameCert_{\QuotientRingUp}$ coset surface is assembled from \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}, \autoref{thm:quotientring-multiplication-descends-to-cosets}, \autoref{thm:quotientring-additive-descent}, \autoref{thm:quotientring-ideal-coset-equivalence}, and \autoref{thm:quotientring-zero-coset-classification}.

Its carrier is the relation $a\equiv_I b$. Its classifier is stable under the ideal-coset relation. Its additive and multiplicative operations are independent of representatives. Its zero coset is exact:
$$
a\equiv_I 0_R\Longleftrightarrow C(a)\land I(a).
$$
Its ledger is limited to $\RingUp$ operations, $\IdealUp$ closure and transport rows, and $\QuotientGroupUp$ coset inheritance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier component is \autoref{def:quotientring-ideal-coset-relation}: a quotient-ring endpoint is represented by the relation $a\equiv_I b$, namely carrier evidence for both endpoints together with ideal membership of the difference $a-_R b$.

The classifier rows are supplied by \autoref{thm:quotientring-ideal-coset-equivalence}. Reflexivity gives the carried self-coset, symmetry reverses representatives, and transitivity composes two representative changes. These rows make classifier stability a property of the ideal-coset relation itself.

The operation rows come from the two descent results. Apply \autoref{thm:quotientring-additive-descent} for addition and additive inverse, and apply \autoref{thm:quotientring-multiplication-descends-to-cosets} for multiplication. The hypotheses of those theorems are exactly the $\RingUp$ operation rows and the $\IdealUp$ closure, absorption, and classifier-transport rows already recorded in the chapter ledger.

The zero row is \autoref{thm:quotientring-zero-coset-classification}. It identifies the zero coset with carried ideal membership, so the certificate exposes no extra zero classifier beyond $C(a)\land I(a)$.

Repacking these rows gives the public $\NameCert_{\QuotientRingUp}$ coset surface. No further ring, ideal, or quotient-group clauses are added: the ledger remains bounded by the listed $\RingUp$ operations, $\IdealUp$ closure and transport data, and the inherited coset infrastructure from $\QuotientGroupUp$.
\end{proof}
