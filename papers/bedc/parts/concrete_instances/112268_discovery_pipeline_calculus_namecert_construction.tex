\begin{theorem}[DiscoveryPipelineCalculus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:discovery-pipeline-calculus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DiscoveryPipelineCalculusUp$ carrier $D=(T,A,G,S,L,Q,K,R,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DiscoveryPipelineCalculusUp}$ surface consists exactly of certified theory-state admission through $T$, automation-record exposure through $A$, theorem-genome extraction through $G$, score-certificate exposure through $S$, lineage-ledger exposure through $L$, classifier-shift evidence through $Q$, discovery-gate admission through $K$, refusal and demotion through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$. A discovery claim is admitted only after classifier-shift evidence and the gate row are both visible.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:discovery-pipeline-calculus-carrier}. The certified theory-state row $T$ fixes the state coordinates, while $A$ records generated material without changing the classifier by itself.

The theorem-genome and scoring rows $G,S$ expose the theorem-DNA completeness and public weight account needed for scored claims. The lineage row $L$ records the parent-child route, so selection cannot be read as an unledgered assertion.

The classifier-shift row $Q$ is the discovery-facing boundary. The gate row $K$ consumes $Q$ together with $G,S,L$ and the certified state data before a discovery claim is admitted, while $R$ records the non-admitted cases.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since no row identifies raw record growth, score reduction, or prose novelty with discovery, the local NameCert surface is exactly the listed finite ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Discovery admission requires classifier shift]
\label{thm:discovery-pipeline-calculus-classifier-shift-admission}
Every accepted discovery read from a $\DiscoveryPipelineCalculusUp$ packet factors through
$$
A,G,S,L,Q,K,R \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
In particular, classifier-shift rows $Q$, not raw automation-record growth in $A$, are necessary for discovery admission.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the automation-record row $A$ first. It lists generated material, but by \autoref{def:discovery-pipeline-calculus-carrier} it has no coordinate that changes the certified classifier state.

The genome, score, and lineage rows $G,S,L$ supply the audit account for theorem-like objects and their transitions. These rows make a claim reproducible, but they still do not identify record growth with discovery.

The discovery-facing test reaches the classifier-shift row $Q$ and then the gate row $K$. Only after $Q$ is visible can $K$ admit a discovery claim; the refusal row $R$ records attempts that omit the shift, score, lineage, genome, certificate, or boundary evidence.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve this dependency order. Thus discovery admission requires classifier-shift evidence and cannot be obtained from raw record growth alone.
\end{proof}
