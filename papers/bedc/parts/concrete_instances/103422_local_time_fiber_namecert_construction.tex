\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocalTimeFiberUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-local-time-fiber-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\LocalTimeFiberUp$ packet realizes the observer-local reading of \autoref{sec:visions-philosophy-observation-subject-time} as finite NameCert data. It sits between the observer-history identity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}, the local time-stream claims of \autoref{def:philosophy-local-time-stream}, the no-global-clock boundary of \autoref{thm:philosophy-no-kernel-global-clock}, the local clock budget of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-localclockbudget-namecert}, and the synchronization certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-synchronization-certificate-namecert}. The packet exposes observer-local Hist growth as finite source, Cont-step, transport, refusal, provenance, and NameCert rows rather than as a global time axis.

\begin{definition}[LocalTimeFiber carrier]
\label{def:local-time-fiber-carrier}
A $\LocalTimeFiberUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(O,S,K,B,R,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$ source row naming the observer as a displayed history surface.
\item $S$ is the hist-time-stream row recording the local sequence of $\Hist$ constructor growth for that observer.
\item $K$ is the local-clock-budget row bounding which local steps may be read by a consumer.
\item $B$ is the $\SynchronizationCertificateUp$ boundary row, present only as a certified comparison surface and not as a global clock.
\item $R$ is the refusal row excluding a global present, absolute clock, detached observer frame, and host equality of time fibers.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for observer identity, local stream, local budget, synchronization boundary, refusal, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the observer-local step route
$$
O,S,K \longmapsto B,R \longmapsto T,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocalTimeFiberUp$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, hist-time-stream, local-clock-budget, $\SynchronizationCertificateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocalTimeFiberUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these nine rows. Its classifier compares accepted local time fibers only through $O,S,K,B,R,T,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for a global clock, absolute present, detached observer substance, unbudgeted step selector, host equality of histories, Lean verification beyond the encoded carrier, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LocalTimeFiber NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:local-time-fiber-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\LocalTimeFiberUp$ carrier
$$
L=(O,S,K,B,R,T,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocalTimeFiberUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the observer-history source, local Hist time stream, local clock budget, synchronization boundary, refusal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item observer-time-step exactness, requiring every time read to project the observer-history row $O$ before it projects a local step from $S$;
\item hist-time-stream locality, requiring each step read to stay within the displayed local stream rather than a shared external index;
\item local-clock-budget boundedness, requiring $K$ before any consumer can request a local step window;
\item synchronizationcertificate confinement, allowing $B$ only as a finite comparison certificate and never as a global clock or absolute present;
\item observerhistidentity transport, requiring $\hsame$ comparison to preserve the observer, stream, budget, synchronization, refusal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows componentwise;
\item non-escape, excluding a global clock, detached observer frame, host equality of histories, unbudgeted local-step selection, Lean verification beyond the encoded carrier, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:local-time-fiber-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the observer-history source, local stream, budget, synchronization boundary, refusal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Observer-time-step exactness starts at $O$. A consumer cannot inspect a local time step until the observer-history source has been displayed, so the time fiber is attached to an observer history rather than to a detached clock carrier.

The stream and budget obligations are confined to $S$ and $K$. The stream row supplies local Hist growth, and the budget row bounds which local windows may be read by downstream consumers.

The synchronization and refusal obligations are supplied by $B$ and $R$. A synchronization certificate may compare displayed local fibers, but the refusal row blocks any reading of that comparison as a global present, absolute clock, detached frame, or host equality.

Finally $T,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite route. Since no excluded host-side time principle has a carrier row, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LocalTimeFiber no-global-clock boundary]
\label{thm:local-time-fiber-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\LocalTimeFiberUp$ carrier reads observer-local time only through
$$
\ObserverHistIdentityUp,\ \text{hist-time-stream},\ \text{local-clock-budget},\ \SynchronizationCertificateUp,\ \BHist,\ \hsame,\ \Cont,\ \Pkg,\ \NameCert .
$$
It cannot read a global clock, absolute present, detached observer frame, host equality of histories, unbudgeted local-step selector, Lean verification beyond the encoded carrier, or standard bridge from this packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The boundary is field exclusion. The carrier of \autoref{def:local-time-fiber-carrier} lists the only rows admitted by the packet, and the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:local-time-fiber-namecert-obligations} force every accepted read to project through those rows.

A global clock, absolute present, detached observer frame, host equality of histories, unbudgeted step selector, or standard bridge would require an additional coordinate. Since no such coordinate occurs in $L$, the local classifier has no accepted read for that request.
\end{proof}
