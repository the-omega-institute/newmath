\begin{proposition}[Uniform continuity modulus restriction along subset inclusion]
\label{prop:uniform-continuity-modulus-restricts-along-subset-inclusion}
Let $Y$ and $Z$ be certified $\MetricSpaceUp$ objects with the metric data of \autoref{def:metric-carrier}. Let $T$ and $U$ be certified source-subset witnesses in the totally bounded domain discipline of \autoref{def:continuous-source-specification}, and let
$$
i:U\subseteq T
$$
be a certified inclusion, meaning that each membership ledger $\nu:U(u)$ is transported to a membership ledger $i(\nu):T(u)$. Let $f:T\to Z$ be a certified graph witness with totality and single-valuedness as in \autoref{def:continuous-carrier}, and let $f\!\restriction_U$ be the same graph relation read with the source classifier restricted through $i$. If a unary precision ledger $\delta$ satisfies the modulus-validity field of \autoref{def:continuous-stability-certificate} for $f$ on $T$, then the same $\delta$ satisfies the modulus-validity field for $f\!\restriction_U$ on $U$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Fix a target precision request $n$ and the source precision returned by the unary ledger $\delta$. To verify the restricted modulus field, take source histories $u_0,u_1$ admitted by $U$, graph outputs $z_0,z_1$ for $f\!\restriction_U$, and the source closeness judgment required at the returned source precision.

The inclusion certificate transports the two source-admission ledgers:
$$
\nu_j:U(u_j)\Longrightarrow i(\nu_j):T(u_j)\qquad(j=0,1).
$$

By \autoref{def:continuous-pattern-specification} and \autoref{def:continuous-ledger-policy}, restricting the source subset does not alter the graph relation, the codomain classifier ledger, the precision ledger, or the modulus ledger. Thus the graph witnesses for $f\!\restriction_U$ are the same graph witnesses for $f$ after the source admissions are read in $T$.

Apply the modulus-validity field for $f$ on $T$ to the transported memberships, the same source closeness judgment, and the same two graph outputs. It yields the target metric closeness conclusion at precision $n$. The well-formedness and representative stability of this conclusion are exactly the classifier and stability data of \autoref{def:metric-classifier-specification} and \autoref{def:metric-stability-certificate}.

This is the entire obligation for the restricted witness: only the source-admission hypotheses have been narrowed from $T$ to $U$, and those hypotheses enter the original modulus field through the certified inclusion. The precision ledger $\delta$ is unchanged.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier\_empty\_source\_restriction\_modulus\_chain}

\begin{theorem}[Continuous empty-source restriction public package]
\label{thm:continuous-empty-source-restriction-public-package}
Let $\mathit{restricted},\mathit{source},\mathit{map},\mathit{target},\mathit{target}',\mathit{oldMod},\mathit{oldCert},\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta,\mathit{cert},\mathit{cert}' : \mathsf{BHist}$ be histories. Assume
$$
\ContR(\mathit{restricted},\emp,\mathit{source}),
$$
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(\mathit{source},\mathit{map},\mathit{target},\mathit{oldMod},\mathit{oldCert}),
$$
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusChain}(\mathit{target},\delta_1,\delta_2,\mathit{cert}),
$$
$$
\ContR(\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta),
$$
and a displayed restricted carrier
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(\mathit{restricted},\mathit{map},\mathit{target}',\delta,\mathit{cert}').
$$
Then the empty-source restriction has the public package
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(\mathit{restricted},\mathit{map},\mathit{target},\delta,\mathit{cert}),
$$
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(\mathit{target},\delta,\mathit{cert}),
$$
and
$$
\hsame(\mathit{target},\mathit{target}')\land\hsame(\mathit{cert},\mathit{cert}').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the preceding checked empty-source restriction-chain theorem to the source restriction, the original carrier, the modulus chain, and the composite modulus relation. This produces the restricted $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ over $(\mathit{restricted},\mathit{map},\mathit{target},\delta,\mathit{cert})$ and the $\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}$ over $(\mathit{target},\delta,\mathit{cert})$.

It remains to read back the displayed public target and certificate. The Lean-side theorem $\mathsf{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier\_empty\_source\_restriction\_public\_readback}$ applies to the same hypotheses together with the displayed restricted carrier, and returns $\hsame(\mathit{target},\mathit{target}')\land\hsame(\mathit{cert},\mathit{cert}')$.

Ten-field fit: traditional target restriction of uniformly continuous maps along an empty-source inclusion; BEDC source BHist, Cont, ContinuousFunctionCarrier, and ContinuousModulusChain; carrier the restricted ContinuousFunctionCarrier; classifier hsame on target and certificate histories; stability hsame transport and modulus-chain composition; ledger unchanged graph with replaced modulus/certificate chain; core theorem this public package; exactness the target/certificate readback; standard bridge ordinary restricted-map readback; status ProofTarget.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform continuity modulus restriction is transitive]
\label{thm:uniform-continuity-modulus-restriction-transitive}
Let $Y$ and $Z$ be certified $\MetricSpaceUp$ objects with the metric data of \autoref{def:metric-carrier}. Let $V$, $U$, and $T$ be certified source-subset witnesses in the totally bounded domain discipline of \autoref{def:continuous-source-specification}, with certified inclusions
$$
j:V\subseteq U
$$
and
$$
i:U\subseteq T.
$$
For a certified graph witness $f:T\to Z$, let $f\!\restriction_U$ be the restriction along $i$, let $(f\!\restriction_U)\!\restriction_V$ be the successive restriction along $j$, and let $f\!\restriction_V$ be the direct restriction along the composite inclusion $i\circ j:V\subseteq T$. The composite inclusion carries the same history-level graph relation, codomain classifier ledger, unary precision ledger, and modulus ledger as the successive restriction. If a unary precision ledger $\delta$ satisfies the modulus-validity field of \autoref{def:continuous-stability-certificate} for $f$ over $T$, then $\delta$ satisfies that field for both $f\!\restriction_V$ and $(f\!\restriction_U)\!\restriction_V$, and the two restricted witnesses are related by the $\ContinuousMapUp$ classifier of \autoref{def:continuous-classifier-specification}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Proposition~\ref{prop:uniform-continuity-modulus-restricts-along-subset-inclusion} first to $i:U\subseteq T$. This transports the modulus-validity field for $f$ over $T$ to the restriction $f\!\restriction_U$ over $U$, with the graph relation and all precision data left unchanged.

Apply the same proposition to $j:V\subseteq U$ and the already restricted witness $f\!\restriction_U$. The result is the modulus-validity field for $(f\!\restriction_U)\!\restriction_V$ over $V$, with the same history-level graph relation, codomain classifier ledger, unary precision ledger, and modulus ledger.

Apply Proposition~\ref{prop:uniform-continuity-modulus-restricts-along-subset-inclusion} once more to the composite inclusion $i\circ j:V\subseteq T$. This gives the direct restricted witness $f\!\restriction_V$ and its modulus-validity field over $V$ using the original witness $f$ and the same unary precision ledger $\delta$.

It remains to compare the direct and successive restrictions. By \autoref{def:continuous-pattern-specification}, restriction changes only the source-admission reading of the graph; by \autoref{def:continuous-ledger-policy}, the graph relation, codomain classifier ledger, unary precision ledger, and modulus ledger are recorded as the same ledgers. Thus the direct and successive witnesses present the same carrier data to the classifier, and \autoref{def:continuous-classifier-specification} relates them as $\ContinuousMapUp$ witnesses.

Ten-field fit: traditional target functorial restriction of uniformly continuous maps; BEDC source BHist with certified source predicates and ContinuousFunctionCarrier; carrier restricted graph witness; classifier ContinuousMap classifier; stability nested source-admission transport; ledger unchanged graph/modulus ledgers; core theorem this block; exactness direct-versus-successive restriction agreement; standard bridge restriction functoriality for uniformly continuous maps; status ProofTarget.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniform continuity restriction commutes with terminal modulus extension]
\label{thm:uniform-continuity-restriction-terminal-modulus-commutes}
Let $Y$ and $Z$ be certified $\MetricSpaceUp$ objects with the metric data of \autoref{def:metric-carrier}. Let $T$ and $U$ be certified source-subset witnesses in the totally bounded domain discipline of \autoref{def:continuous-source-specification}, with certified inclusion $i:U\subseteq T$. Let $f:T\to Z$ be a certified graph witness, and let
$$
source,map,target,modulus,cert,extra,modulus',cert':\mathsf{BHist}
$$
be the BEDC histories recording the source ledger for $T$, the graph ledger of $f$, the codomain classifier ledger, the original unary modulus, the terminal certificate, the additional terminal modulus, the composite modulus, and the terminal certificate after composition. Assume that $f$ satisfies the ContinuousMap carrier, totality, single-valuedness, and modulus-validity fields of \autoref{def:continuous-carrier} and \autoref{def:continuous-stability-certificate} over $T$, and assume
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}(source,map,target,modulus,cert),\\
\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(cert,extra,cert'),\\
\ContR(modulus,extra,modulus').
\end{array}
$$
Then the $\ContinuousMapUp$ witness obtained by restricting $f$ along $i$ and then applying terminal modulus extension with $extra$, $modulus'$, and $cert'$ is related by the classifier of \autoref{def:continuous-classifier-specification} to the witness obtained by first applying terminal modulus extension over $T$ and then restricting along $i$. Both witnesses present the restricted graph $f\!\restriction_U$, the source-admission transport through $i$, the codomain classifier ledger for $Z$, the composite modulus ledger $modulus'$, and the terminal certificate ledger $cert'$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-terminal-modulus-extension} to the displayed carrier, the terminal modulus witness, and the composite continuation ledger. This gives a continuous-function carrier over $T$ with histories $source,map,target,modulus',cert'$.

Restrict this extended carrier along $i:U\subseteq T$. Proposition~\ref{prop:uniform-continuity-modulus-restricts-along-subset-inclusion} transports the modulus-validity field to $U$, while \autoref{def:continuous-pattern-specification} and \autoref{def:continuous-ledger-policy} keep the graph relation, target classifier, precision ledger, and modulus ledger fixed.

For the other route, first apply Proposition~\ref{prop:uniform-continuity-modulus-restricts-along-subset-inclusion} to the original witness $f$. The restricted witness over $U$ carries the same graph ledger $map$, the same target ledger $target$, and the same modulus and certificate ledgers $modulus,cert$, with only the source-admission hypotheses narrowed through $i$.

Now apply Theorem~\ref{thm:continuous-function-carrier-terminal-modulus-extension} to this restricted carrier using the same $\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}(cert,extra,cert')$ and the same $\ContR(modulus,extra,modulus')$. The resulting restricted-then-extended witness has composite modulus $modulus'$ and terminal certificate $cert'$.

It remains to compare the two route results. Their graph relation is the same relation read on $U$, source histories admitted by $U$ are transported to $T$ by the same inclusion certificate, the codomain classifier ledger is the same $Z$ ledger, and the composite modulus and terminal certificate ledgers are the same displayed histories. Therefore \autoref{def:continuous-classifier-specification} relates the two witnesses as $\ContinuousMapUp$ representatives.

Ten-field fit: traditional target commutation of source restriction with terminal modulus extension; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ with certified source predicates, $\ContR$, and $\mathsf{ContinuousModulusWitness}$; carrier $\mathsf{ContinuousFunctionCarrier}$ with restricted graph witness; classifier ContinuousMap classifier; stability source-admission transport plus terminal modulus preservation; ledger unchanged graph relation, codomain classifier ledger, composite modulus ledger, and terminal certificate ledger; core theorem this block; exactness equality of the two route witnesses under \autoref{def:continuous-classifier-specification}; standard bridge restriction and modulus-normalization order independence for uniformly continuous maps; status ProofTarget.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContinuousUp.ContinuousFunctionCarrier\_empty\_source\_restriction\_terminal\_modulus\_commutes}
