\begin{theorem}[MarkovTriggerDynamics NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:markov-trigger-dynamics-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The state and support rows $S,K$ determine the finite rank-trigger situation. The transition-count row $A$ counts rank-preserving and rank-increasing next draws from that finite support surface.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve the same coordinates. Since the excluded ambient and hidden objects are not coordinates of $T$, no consumer can read them from the packet.
