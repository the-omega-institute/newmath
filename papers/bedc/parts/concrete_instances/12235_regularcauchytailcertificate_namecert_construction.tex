The $\RegularCauchyTailCertificateUp$ packet realizes the shared tail-window witness used by the Real-and-Completion route of \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}. It sits between the regular Cauchy data of \autoref{sec:real-completeness-regular-cauchy} and the Real-facing seal consumers, and it reuses the finite dyadic rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the schedule rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular-rational readbacks of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal naming seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter is composite: it assembles already named Real and Completion rows into one reusable tail certificate rather than claiming a new atomic carrier.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy tail-certificate carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-tail-certificate-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyTailCertificateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(X,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular Cauchy source row whose tail is being witnessed;
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ shared tail-window row naming the finite observation window selected for the displayed precision request;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the window $W$;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and arithmetic ledger for the windowed readback;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reachable from $X,W,R,D$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $X,W,R,D,E$ and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes from the regular Cauchy source through the shared tail window, readback, dyadic ledger, and Real seal;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailCertificateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares carriers only by the rows $X,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, choice rows, standard-bridge rows, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy tail-certificate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-certificate-namecert-obligations}

\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the packet $T=(X,W,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$;
\item shared tail-window selector coverage through the $\StreamNameUp$ row $W$;
\item dyadic readback exactness through the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$;
\item Real-seal factorization through the row $E$ and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ support rows;
\item non-escape for quotient streams, host sequence equality, selected limits, choice rows, standard-bridge rows, and ambient Real-completion principles.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\end{theorem}

The route to $E$ is therefore ordered by the displayed packet: source, shared window, readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, and then the transport, continuation, provenance, and naming support rows. No second selector or hidden tail oracle is available.
\end{proof}
