\section{Finite adjacency windows}
\label{sec:subshift-finite-type-adjacency-windows}

\begin{definition}[Subshift of finite type carrier]
\label{def:subshift-finite-type-carrier}
A $\SubshiftOfFiniteTypeUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
S=(A,W,F,L,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the finite visible alphabet row, specialized here to the displayed zero and one bit rows;
\item $W$ is a finite word row over $A$, recorded as a list of visible $\mathsf{BHist}$ bit rows;
\item $F$ is the finite forbidden-adjacency ledger whose rows are adjacent bit pairs disallowed inside $W$;
\item $L$ is the acceptance ledger recording, for every neighboring pair of $W$, that the pair is absent from $F$;
\item $P$ records the finite prefix, restriction, and row-transport provenance through $\Cont$ and $\Pkg$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SubshiftOfFiniteTypeUp}$ row for the componentwise visible-word classifier.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two carriers componentwise by $\hsame$ on the alphabet row, each visible word row, the forbidden-adjacency ledger, the acceptance ledger, the provenance row, and the local certificate row. Acceptance is exactly finite ledger inspection: every adjacent pair displayed by $W$ must be rejected by no row of $F$ and certified in $L$ as allowed.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Subshift of finite type NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:subshift-finite-type-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\SubshiftOfFiniteTypeUp}$ obligation surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation: the empty word and every one-letter visible word over $A$ are accepted, because they display no forbidden adjacent pair;
\item classifier laws: reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are inherited componentwise from $\hsame$ on visible alphabet rows, word rows, forbidden-ledger rows, acceptance-ledger rows, provenance rows, and certificate rows;
\item adjacency-ledger transport: displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ routes transport accepted carriers exactly when transported neighboring pairs remain outside the transported forbidden ledger;
\item finite-prefix restriction: every displayed prefix or finite restriction of $W$ retains the corresponding subledger of $F,L,P,N$ and remains accepted;
\item non-escape: every accepted row is justified only by the finite visible alphabet, word, forbidden-adjacency ledger, acceptance ledger, provenance, and local certificate rows.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the certificate is a BEDC-native finite-window object over $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For habitation, choose the empty word or a one-letter word. There is no adjacent position to inspect, so the acceptance ledger is empty and no forbidden pair can be witnessed.

For the classifier laws, read each coordinate of \autoref{def:subshift-finite-type-carrier} componentwise. The visible rows are compared by $\hsame$, and the packet has no further coordinate on which another classifier could act.

For adjacency-ledger transport, move the alphabet, word, forbidden-ledger, acceptance-ledger, provenance, and certificate rows through the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ routes. The transported carrier is accepted exactly when every transported neighboring pair remains absent from the transported forbidden ledger, because acceptance is defined by that finite inspection.

For finite-prefix restriction, select an initial segment of the word through the recorded $\Cont$ route and restrict the ledgers to neighboring pairs that still appear in the segment. A retained neighbor was already accepted in the original word, so the restricted acceptance ledger certifies it again.

Non-escape follows by projection of the carrier coordinates. The packet exposes no entropy row, inverse-limit object, quotient coordinate, infinite sequence carrier, or host symbolic-dynamics theorem, so a downstream $\NameCert$ consumer can cite only the displayed finite ledger rows and their routes.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Golden mean instance]
\label{thm:subshift-finite-type-golden-mean-instance}
The $\GoldenMeanShiftUp$ finite-window carrier is the one-forbidden-word instance of $\SubshiftOfFiniteTypeUp$ obtained by taking the visible alphabet row $A=\{0,1\}$ and the forbidden-adjacency ledger
$$
F=\{11\}.
$$
For this instance, a finite word is accepted precisely when every neighboring pair is zero-zero, zero-one, or one-zero, and the inherited $\NameCert$ obligations are the GoldenMeanShift carrier habitation, local stability, fold-prefix closure, adjacency-ledger exhaustion, and non-escape obligations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Specialize \autoref{def:subshift-finite-type-carrier} to the binary alphabet row and the singleton forbidden ledger whose only row is the adjacent pair $11$. A neighboring pair is accepted exactly when the finite ledger inspection does not find that row.

The remaining adjacent binary pairs are zero-zero, zero-one, and one-zero. These are exactly the accepted adjacency rows used by the $\GoldenMeanShiftUp$ carrier, and the componentwise $\hsame$ classifier is the same visible-word classifier.

The NameCert obligations restrict along the same finite ledgers. Habitation is witnessed by the empty word, one-letter words, and the all-zero word; local transport preserves acceptance by transporting the singleton forbidden row; prefix restriction deletes neighboring checks without adding any; and non-escape is inherited from the absence of any coordinate beyond the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ word and ledger rows.
\end{proof}
