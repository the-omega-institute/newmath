\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DistanceToSetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-distance-to-set-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges and Kelley, the $\DistanceToSetUp$ packet records the finite located metric route for distance from a displayed point to a displayed located source. It refines the request boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedspace-namecert} through the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the located-real handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedreal-namecert}, the apartness-real gap surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apartnessreal-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet presents distance-to-source data without selecting a nearest point, forming an arbitrary infimum, or importing completion data from an ambient real object.

\begin{definition}[DistanceToSet carrier]
\label{def:distance-to-set-carrier}
A $\DistanceToSetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(M,x,L,T,G,W,Q,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the source metric row, carrying the finite point names, distance request interface, and metric comparison rows used by the packet.
\item $x$ is the queried point row whose distance to the displayed source is being named.
\item $L$ is the located source request, consisting of the source row, the displayed query point, and the finite locatedness question opened at the requested tolerance.
\item $T$ is the dyadic tolerance row used to ask for an upper approximation and to bound every displayed comparison.
\item $G$ is the gap row used for lower-bound apartness reads against the located source.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite window through which the query point, source observations, tolerance row, and gap row are inspected.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the windows in $W$ and to every rational comparison exported by the distance request.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal reached by the distance read only after $L,T,G,W,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric row, queried point, located source request, tolerance row, gap row, stream window, regular readback, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of every distance-to-source consumer route through
$$
M,x,L,T,G,W,Q \longmapsto R .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DistanceToSetUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\LocatedSpaceUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\ApartnessRealUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DistanceToSetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,x,L,T,G,W,Q,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for selected nearest points, arbitrary infima, quotient representatives, trichotomy, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DistanceToSet NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:distance-to-set-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DistanceToSetUp$ carrier
$$
D=(M,x,L,T,G,W,Q,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\DistanceToSetUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed metric row, queried point row, located source request, dyadic tolerance row, gap row, stream window, regular readback, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item request stability, requiring every transported or replayed distance read to keep the same metric row $M$, queried point $x$, located request $L$, tolerance $T$, gap row $G$, window $W$, and readback $Q$ before exposing the real seal $R$;
\item lower-bound apartness exactness, requiring every certified lower separation to use the same gap row $G$ and the same regular readback $Q$ displayed by the located request;
\item upper-approximation route, requiring every finite upper distance approximation to pass through the source request $L$, dyadic tolerance $T$, stream window $W$, and regular readback $Q$;
\item Real seal handoff, requiring the terminal real row $R$ to be named only after the located request, tolerance, gap, stream, and readback rows have been displayed;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed rows, with no selected nearest point, arbitrary infimum, quotient representative, trichotomy, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No distance-to-set NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:distance-to-set-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the metric row, queried point, located source request, tolerance row, gap row, finite stream window, regular readback, real seal, and the four structural rows.

Request stability is componentwise. Transport or replay preserves the same $M,x,L,T,G,W,Q$ inventory, so a downstream consumer cannot replace the displayed located request by an ambient subset predicate or a completed-space assertion.

Lower-bound exactness is the shared role of $G$ and $Q$. The gap row names the apartness budget used by lower separation reads, and the regular rational readback supplies the inspected comparisons for that same budget.

Upper approximations use the other side of the located request. The tolerance row $T$ bounds the requested approximation, while $W$ and $Q$ expose the finite observations that make the approximation inspectable.

The real seal is therefore downstream of the displayed route $L,T,G,W,Q$. The rows $H,C,P,N$ only preserve, replay, source, and name that inventory, and the excluded nearest-point, infimum, quotient, trichotomy, choice, compactness, ambient-completeness, Lean, and bridge data are absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DistanceToSet located-real handoff]
\label{thm:distance-to-set-located-real-handoff}
Every distance-to-set read exported by an accepted $\DistanceToSetUp$ carrier
$$
D=(M,x,L,T,G,W,Q,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
reaches $\RealUp$ only through the displayed located request $L$, dyadic tolerance row $T$, gap row $G$, $\StreamNameUp$ finite window $W$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback $Q$, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal $R$. The handoff exports no selected nearest point, arbitrary infimum, quotient representative, trichotomy, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier and a distance-to-set read. Projection from \autoref{def:distance-to-set-carrier} gives the rows $M,x,L,T,G,W,Q,R,H,C,P,N$ and no additional real-completion object.

The read first opens the located request $L$ against the metric row $M$ and the queried point $x$. Lower-bound use passes through the gap row $G$, while upper-approximation use passes through the tolerance row $T$.

Both sides of the request are inspected through the finite window $W$ and regular readback $Q$. These rows are the only exported source of rational observations for the distance comparison.

Only after those rows have been displayed can the route name the real seal $R$. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve this order under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Thus every distance-to-set read reaches $\RealUp$ through the displayed located-real handoff. The excluded nearest-point, infimum, quotient, trichotomy, choice, compactness, ambient-completeness, Lean, and bridge data cannot be exported because the accepted packet contains no such coordinate.
\end{proof}
