Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\OneSidedLimitUp$ packet records the left- and right-hand real-limit interface as finite BEDC data. It sits next to the $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the $\StreamNameUp$ observation schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, and the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. The packet names only displayed side-tagged approach windows, regular rational reads, dyadic tolerance rows, transport, replay, provenance, and a terminal Real seal; it does not import two-sided completeness, host real equality, quotient streams, trichotomy, or a selected limiting point.

\begin{definition}[One-sided limit carrier]\label{def:one-sided-limit-carrier}
A $\OneSidedLimitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(A,L,W,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the approach-anchor row, naming the source point or endpoint cell at which the one-sided read is taken.
\item $L$ is the side classifier row, with each visible approach window tagged as left-hand or right-hand before any limiting seal is inspected.
\item $W$ is the finite approach-window row, listing only windows compatible with the tag in $L$ and the anchor in $A$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ observation schedule opened by the side-tagged windows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the regular rational data observed on $S$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger for the displayed one-sided comparisons.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached from $A,L,W,S,R,D$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the anchor, side classifier, windows, stream schedule, regular readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the side-tagged approach windows through the stream, readback, dyadic, and Real-seal rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\OneSidedLimitUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\OneSidedLimitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rows $A,L,W,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a two-sided completion theorem, quotient real equality, host order trichotomy, countable choice, or a selected limit outside the named side-tagged windows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[One-sided limit NameCert obligations]\label{thm:one-sided-limit-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\OneSidedLimitUp$ carrier $K=(A,L,W,S,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\OneSidedLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the approach anchor, side classifier, approach windows, stream schedule, regular readback, dyadic ledger, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item side classifier stability, requiring each transported window to retain its left-hand or right-hand tag before a readback row is consumed;
\item dyadic tolerance ledger exactness, requiring $D$ to expose only the tolerance rows demanded by the side-tagged windows in $W$;
\item $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, requiring $S$ and $R$ to read only the finite observations selected by $A,L,W$;
\item Real seal nonescape, requiring $E$ to remain downstream of $A,L,W,S,R,D$ and never to serve as a two-sided completeness witness or host real equality row;
\item componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No $\OneSidedLimitUp$ obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:one-sided-limit-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the approach anchor $A$, side classifier $L$, finite approach windows $W$, stream schedule $S$, regular readback $R$, dyadic ledger $D$, Real seal $E$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Side classifier stability is componentwise. A transported window may be read only with the left-hand or right-hand tag already recorded in $L$, and the replay row $C$ keeps the same tag attached while the stream and regular-rational rows are inspected.

The tolerance and readback rows are downstream of the same finite windows. The ledger $D$ records the dyadic comparisons demanded by $W$, the schedule $S$ opens only those observations, and $R$ records the regular rational readback.

Finally, the Real seal $E$ is terminal for the route. Since the carrier contains no row for two-sided completeness, quotient real equality, host order trichotomy, countable choice, or a selected limit outside the displayed windows, those objects cannot become local obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[One-sided limit Real-seal handoff]\label{thm:one-sided-limit-realseal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing consumer of an accepted $\OneSidedLimitUp$ packet reads the proposed one-sided limit only through the displayed finite route
$$

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The finite approach windows determine the side of approach before readback begins. Thus a consumer that asks for a Real seal must already have read the side classifier, the compatible windows, the stream schedule, the regular rational row, and the dyadic tolerance ledger.

The remaining coordinates transport and name the same route. Because no carrier row stores a two-sided completion principle, quotient equality, host equality, trichotomy, choice, or an independent selected limit, the Real-seal handoff cannot export any of those objects.
\end{proof}
