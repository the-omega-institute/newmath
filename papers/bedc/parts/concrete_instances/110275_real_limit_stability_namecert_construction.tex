\begin{theorem}[Real limit stability seal stability]
\label{thm:real-limit-stability-seal-stability}

$$

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer that reads the paired Real seals. The only Real-facing terminal coordinates in the carrier are $E_0$ and $E_1$, and both appear after the two presentation rows, shared finite window, regular rational readback, and dyadic ledger.

The consumer must therefore pass through $W,R,D$ before either seal can be used. These rows give the finite observations, regular Cauchy readback, and common tolerance budget that make the two terminal seals comparable as displayed BEDC data.

Finally read $H,C,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, source, and name the same route, so they preserve seal stability without adding a selected host limit or quotient stream equality.
