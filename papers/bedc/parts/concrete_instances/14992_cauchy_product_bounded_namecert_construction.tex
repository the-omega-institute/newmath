\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyProductBoundedUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\CauchyProductBoundedUp$ packet records the bounded-product surface for two bounded Cauchy sequence packets before the product is exposed as a regular rational sequence or a real seal. It refines the product route of $\CauchyProductUp$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyproduct-namecert} with the two bounded-source ledgers of $\CauchySequenceBoundedUp$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-sequence-bounded-namecert}, then routes the result through $\RegSeqRatUp$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} and $\RealUp$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet contains no host multiplication on completed reals, quotient stream representative, selected product limit, countable choice, or ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy product bounded carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-product-bounded-carrier}
A $\CauchyProductBoundedUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(X,Y,P,W,D,R,E,B,H,C,L,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are accepted $\CauchySequenceBoundedUp$ packets, carrying the two stream schedules, Cauchy-modulus rows, dyadic tolerance ledgers, regular-rational handoffs, real-seal rows, and explicit bound ledgers for the factors.
\item $P$ is the accepted $\CauchyProductUp$ route whose product observation is read only from the two factor schedules and dyadic product ledger.
\item $W$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ schedule aligning the two bounded factor windows with the product window used by $P$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance and magnitude ledger containing the two factor bounds, the product tolerance row, and the finite cross-error budget used to compare the product read.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier row that receives the product readback after $X,Y,P,W,D$ have been displayed.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row for the product, consumed only after the bounded-source rows, product route, dyadic ledger, and regular-rational classifier row are present.
\item $B$ is the product-bound ledger, recording the two source bounds, the product-bound row, and the reconciliation row saying that the product read remains inside the displayed dyadic or rational bound.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,Y,P,W,D,R,E,B,C,L,N$ and the local packet shape.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from bounded-source reads to product windows, from product windows to dyadic tolerances, from tolerances to the product bound, and from the bound ledger to the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ rows.
\item $L$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyProductBoundedUp$, $\CauchySequenceBoundedUp$, $\CauchyProductUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductBoundedUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,P,W,D,R,E,B,H,C,L,N$. It has no coordinate for host real multiplication, quotient-stream equality, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy product bounded NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-bounded-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CauchyProductBoundedUp$ carrier $Z=(X,Y,P,W,D,R,E,B,H,C,L,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductBoundedUp}$ obligations are exactly carrier admission, bounded-source admission for $X$ and $Y$, product-route admission for $P$, shared-window exactness for $W$, dyadic product-bound stability for $D$ and $B$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier handoff for $R$, $\RealUp$ seal handoff for $E$, componentwise transport and continuation replay through $H$ and $C$, provenance through $L$, and ledger non-escape through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of the displayed tuple in \autoref{def:cauchy-product-bounded-carrier}. The first two coordinates are accepted $\CauchySequenceBoundedUp$ packets, so each factor already exposes a stream schedule, modulus row, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular-rational handoff, real seal row, and explicit bound ledger.

The product-route admission is the row $P$. It is read through the $\CauchyProductUp$ sibling surface, so the product observation comes from the paired schedules and dyadic product ledger rather than from host multiplication over completed reals.

The shared-window row $W$ aligns the factor reads with the product read. The dyadic ledger $D$ and product-bound ledger $B$ then record the finite magnitude and tolerance data needed to keep the product read inside the displayed bound.

The rows $R$ and $E$ are downstream handoffs. They receive only the bounded product packet assembled from $X,Y,P,W,D,B$; the transport rows $H$, continuation rows $C$, provenance row $L$, and local naming row $N$ preserve and name that same route.

No coordinate in the carrier supplies host real multiplication, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness. Hence the listed obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy product bounded regular-sequence route]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-bounded-regseqrat-product-route}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ read exported by an accepted $\CauchyProductBoundedUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
X,Y\longmapsto W\longmapsto P\longmapsto D\longmapsto B\longmapsto R .
$$
The two source rows are consumed as $\CauchySequenceBoundedUp$ packets, the product row is consumed as the $\CauchyProductUp$ sibling route, and the regular-sequence classifier receives no quotient stream, selected product limit, host multiplication primitive, trichotomy row, countable-choice row, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a consumer that asks for the regular rational product readback. By \autoref{def:cauchy-product-bounded-carrier}, the only regular-sequence classifier row is $R$.

The row $R$ is not source data. It is downstream of the bounded factor rows $X,Y$, the aligned window row $W$, the product route $P$, and the dyadic product-bound ledger $D,B$.

The sibling $\CauchySequenceBoundedUp$ packets supply the two factor bounds, while $\CauchyProductUp$ supplies the product observation route. Combining those rows gives the displayed finite path to $R$.

Since no other row in the carrier is a regular-sequence classifier, every exported $\RegSeqRatUp$ read factors through that path and cannot obtain any excluded object from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy product bounded real-seal boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-bounded-real-seal-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ seal read of an accepted $\CauchyProductBoundedUp$ carrier factors through
$$
X,Y\longmapsto W\longmapsto P\longmapsto D\longmapsto B\longmapsto R\longmapsto E
$$
together with the displayed $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ replays, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductBoundedUp}$ row. Thus the real-facing product boundary is a finite bounded-source product certificate, not a host multiplication operation, quotient stream, selected limit, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The real-seal consumer can read only the row $E$ as a $\RealUp$ boundary. In the carrier, $E$ is declared downstream of the bounded-source packets, shared window row, product route, dyadic ledger, product-bound ledger, and regular-rational classifier.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-product-bounded-regseqrat-product-route}. The classifier row $R$ is reached only after $X,Y,W,P,D,B$ are displayed, so the seal row $E$ inherits the same source and product-bound route.

The structural rows $H,C,L,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this route without adding a second seal source. Therefore any real-facing product read is exhausted by $X,Y,P,W,D,R,E,B,H,C,L,N$.

Because the carrier has no coordinate for host multiplication, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness, none of those objects can be exported by the seal boundary.
\end{proof}
