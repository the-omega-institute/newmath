Following Painleve and Kuratowski on set convergence, the $\PainleveKuratowskiConvergenceUp$ packet records finite-window closed-set convergence by separating lower-hit witnesses from upper-miss finite-cover witnesses. It sits over the hyperspace carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hyperspace-namecert}, the finite Hausdorff comparison boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorff-metric-namecert}, the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the compact metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the hit/miss topology of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vietoris-topology-namecert}, the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names only displayed hit/miss windows and their readback rows; it is not a host powerset convergence theorem or a quotient subset equality principle.

\begin{definition}[Painleve--Kuratowski convergence carrier]
\label{def:painleve-kuratowski-convergence-carrier}
A $\PainleveKuratowskiConvergenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(L,U,K,H,V,W,R,E,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the lower-hit witness ledger, listing the finite inspected hits by which points or probes of a displayed closed set are met by later closed-set rows.
\item $U$ is the upper-miss finite-cover witness ledger, listing finite open or ball-cover exclusions for the inspected windows.
\item $K$ is the inspected compact or hyperspace source row, read through $\HyperspaceUp$ and the relevant $\CompactMetricUp$ carrier.
\item $H$ is the $\HausdorffMetricUp$ comparison boundary available only for the displayed compact windows and finite tolerances.
\item $V$ is the $\VietorisTopologyUp$ hit/miss row that records the lower-hit and upper-miss subbasic tests used by the packet.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row that schedules the inspected convergence stages and tolerance requests.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the rational schedules and metric tolerances seen in $W$ and $H$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal reached after the stream, regular-rational, Hausdorff, and hit/miss rows have been displayed.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the lower-hit ledger, upper-miss ledger, hyperspace source, Hausdorff boundary, Vietoris row, stream window, regular readback, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite hit/miss request through $K,H,V,W,R,E,T,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PainleveKuratowskiConvergenceUp$, $\HyperspaceUp$, $\HausdorffMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\VietorisTopologyUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PainleveKuratowskiConvergenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $L,U,K,H,V,W,R,E,T,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host powerset, quotient subset equality, arbitrary supremum or infimum, selected nearest point, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Painleve--Kuratowski convergence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:painleve-kuratowski-convergence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PainleveKuratowskiConvergenceUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(L,U,K,H,V,W,R,E,T,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\PainleveKuratowskiConvergenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the lower-hit ledger, upper-miss ledger, hyperspace or compact source, Hausdorff comparison boundary, Vietoris row, stream window, regular readback, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item lower-hit coverage, requiring every asserted hit to be witnessed in the displayed ledger $L$ before a convergence read is available;
\item upper-miss coverage, requiring every asserted miss to be witnessed by the finite-cover ledger $U$ before exclusion data is exported;
\item finite-window compatibility, requiring $W$ and $R$ to schedule exactly the inspected stages and tolerances used by $L,U,H,V$;
\item metric and hyperspace handoff, requiring $K,H,V$ to expose the compact-source, Hausdorff-boundary, and Vietoris hit/miss rows before the real seal is read;
\item componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming for the same $L,U,K,H,V,W,R,E$ route.
\end{enumerate}
No Painleve--Kuratowski convergence obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:painleve-kuratowski-convergence-carrier}. The lower-hit coordinate is $L$, the upper-miss coordinate is $U$, and the inspected source and topology data are $K,H,V$.

Lower-hit coverage is read from $L$. A consumer may cite a hit only after the displayed witness appears in the finite ledger, so the packet does not infer pointwise contact from an ambient subset relation.

Upper-miss coverage is read from $U$. Each miss is paired with a finite cover or ball-exclusion window before any convergence-facing exclusion is exported, so no arbitrary complement or open-cover compactness theorem is used.

The finite-window route is then checked through $W$ and $R$. These rows schedule the inspected stages and regular rational tolerances, while $H,V$ attach the Hausdorff and Vietoris readings to the same windows.

Finally, $E,T,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name only that route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host powersets, quotient subset equality, arbitrary suprema or infima, selected nearest points, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness, the listed obligations exhaust the certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Painleve--Kuratowski convergence finite-window boundary]
\label{thm:painleve-kuratowski-convergence-finite-window-boundary}

$$
L,U \longmapsto K,H,V \longmapsto W,R \longmapsto E \longmapsto T,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the chapter names finite hit/miss windows for closed-set convergence; it does not export host powersets, quotient subset equality, arbitrary suprema or infima, selected nearest points, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a convergence-facing read. By \autoref{def:painleve-kuratowski-convergence-carrier}, the only lower and upper convergence data are the finite ledgers $L$ and $U$.

Use \autoref{thm:painleve-kuratowski-convergence-namecert-obligations}. The consumer must pass through the hyperspace or compact source row $K$, the Hausdorff comparison boundary $H$, and the Vietoris hit/miss row $V$ before it may read the finite schedules $W,R$ and the terminal seal $E$.

The structural rows $T,C,P,N$ preserve the same displayed dependency order. Because the excluded host-level principles have no carrier coordinate and no replay edge, the packet can export only the named finite-window boundary.
\end{proof}
