\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TychonoffFiniteProductUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-tychonoff-finite-product-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bishop--Bridges, the $\TychonoffFiniteProductUp$ packet records the finite compact-product theorem as explicit NameCert data over a finite index list. It consumes the compact-metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the finite-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, the limit-witness surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, and the product-distance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-productmetric-namecert}. The packet is finite throughout: it does not claim arbitrary Tychonoff products, countable products, choice-selected subcovers, or open-cover compactness beyond the displayed compact-metric rows.

\begin{definition}[Tychonoff finite-product carrier]
\label{def:tychonoff-finite-product-carrier}
A $\TychonoffFiniteProductUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
(I,X,K,T,L,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the finite index-list row, including the displayed membership rows for every coordinate used by the product;
\item $X$ assigns to each listed index a $\MetricUp$ carrier with its local distance classifier;
\item $K$ assigns to each listed index a $\CompactMetricUp$ row whose finite-net and completeness witnesses are readable through the same coordinate name;
\item $T$ is the coordinatewise $\TotallyBoundedUp$ handoff, exposing finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ nets for each positive product tolerance request;
\item $L$ is the coordinatewise $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff for product Cauchy streams and their displayed limit witnesses;
\item $M$ is the $\ProductMetricUp$ ledger that folds the finite list of coordinate distances into the product-distance row;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ row for $I,X,K,T,L,M$;
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ route replaying coordinate compactness, finite-net extraction, completion, and product-distance rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\TychonoffFiniteProductUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\ProductMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TychonoffFiniteProductUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for an arbitrary product family, countable choice, selected subcover, quotient product equality, or host open-cover compactness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Tychonoff finite-product NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:tychonoff-finite-product-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\TychonoffFiniteProductUp}$ surface of \autoref{def:tychonoff-finite-product-carrier} consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed tuple $(I,X,K,T,L,M,H,C,P,N)$;
\item coordinate compactness, requiring every listed coordinate to expose its $\CompactMetricUp$ row before product compactness may be read;
\item finite-net product assembly, requiring product total boundedness to be obtained by finite folding of the coordinate $\TotallyBoundedUp$ rows through $M$;
\item Cauchy-limit product assembly, requiring product completeness to be obtained by coordinate $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit witnesses and the same product metric ledger;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding arbitrary products, countable choice, open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, and host product equality from the exported packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:tychonoff-finite-product-carrier}. The finite index row $I$ fixes the only coordinates that can be inspected, and the rows $X,K,T,L,M$ attach metric, compactness, total boundedness, completeness, and product-distance data to exactly those coordinates.

For the finite-net side, read each coordinate compact row $K$ through the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ handoff $T$. The product request is then folded by the $\ProductMetricUp$ ledger $M$, so the product net is assembled from the displayed coordinate bundles rather than from a selected open subcover.

For the limit side, read each coordinate compact row through the $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff $L$. A product Cauchy stream supplies coordinate Cauchy streams, each coordinate limit witness is displayed in $L$, and $M$ repacks the coordinate bounds as a product-distance bound.

Finally, $H$ and $C$ transport and replay the same finite rows, while $P$ and $N$ source and name the packet. Since no other coordinate is present, the listed non-finite and choice-based data cannot be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tychonoff finite-product net-product route]
\label{thm:tychonoff-finite-product-net-product-route}
For every accepted $\TychonoffFiniteProductUp$ carrier
$$
(I,X,K,T,L,M,H,C,P,N),
$$
each product compactness consumer factors through the finite route
$$
I,K,T,L \longmapsto M \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The route first reads the coordinate $\CompactMetricUp$ rows, obtains finite product nets from the coordinate $\TotallyBoundedUp$ rows, obtains coordinate limits from the $\CompleteMetricUp$ rows, and then repacks both witnesses through the displayed $\ProductMetricUp$ ledger. It exports no arbitrary Tychonoff product theorem, no countable product compactness, no choice-selected subcover, and no host open-cover compactness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier and project its finite index row. A consumer can ask only for coordinates listed in $I$, and every such coordinate has its compact-metric evidence in $K$.

The finite-net component is obtained from $T$. For each positive product tolerance, the product metric ledger $M$ distributes the request across the listed coordinates, reads the corresponding finite bundles from $T$, and folds them back into a product bundle.

The limit component is obtained from $L$. Coordinate Cauchy streams and limit witnesses are read only through the listed $\CompleteMetricUp$ rows, and $M$ converts the coordinate bounds into the product-distance estimate required by the product compactness consumer.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ then preserve, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Because the route never leaves $I,K,T,L,M,H,C,P,N$, it cannot supply arbitrary-product compactness, countable choice, selected subcovers, or open-cover compactness.
\end{proof}
