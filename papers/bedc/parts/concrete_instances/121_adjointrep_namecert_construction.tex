\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AdjointRepUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-adjointrep-namecert}

The adjoint-representation interface $\AdjointRepUp$ packages the conjugation action $\mathrm{Ad}\colon G \to \mathrm{Aut}(\mathfrak{g})$ together with its differential $\mathrm{ad}$. It depends on $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-liegroup-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\LieAlgebraUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-liealgebra-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:adjointrep-carrier}

\begin{definition}[AdjointRep source packet]
\label{def:adjointrep-source-packet}
An $\AdjointRepUp$ source packet is a BEDC package whose visible source is the
licensed $\LieGroupUp$ public surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-liegroup-namecert} together with the
$\LieAlgebraUp$ bracket and endomap surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-liealgebra-namecert}. The packet contains
$\mathsf{BHist}$-indexed rows
$\mathrm{Ad}(g,x)$ and $\mathrm{ad}(u,x)$, where the first row reads carried
group inputs as classifier-respecting endomaps of the carried Lie-algebra
carrier and the second row is the differential row at the identity group
endpoint.

Classifier transport is performed only through the source classifiers and
$\hsame$: carried group histories are classified by the $\LieGroupUp$ source,
carried algebra histories are classified by the $\LieAlgebraUp$ source, and the
endomap rows are stable under those classifiers. The public ledger exposes the
source packages, the action row, the differential row, the automorphism-target
row, the classifier-stability rows, and exactness rows; it hides only the
chosen conjugator representative and the tangent-source representative used to
construct the displayed rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[AdjointRep carrier obligation]
\label{thm:adjointrep-carrier-obligation}
The $\AdjointRepUp$ carrier is admitted only when the following data are
packaged as BEDC $\Pkg$ or $\NameCert$ rows: the $\LieGroupUp$ source package,
the $\LieAlgebraUp$ source package, the conjugation-action row
$\mathrm{Ad}$, the differential row $\mathrm{ad}$, and the automorphism-target
row identifying each carried action endpoint as a classifier-respecting
endomap of the Lie-algebra carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the two source certificates named in the chapter introduction. The
$\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$ dependency supplies the carried group histories,
their classifier, the identity endpoint, multiplication, inverse, and the
conjugation-action source row. The $\NameCert_{\LieAlgebraUp}$ dependency
supplies the carried algebra histories, bracket classifier, and endomap
surface.

Package these rows with the BEDC $\Pkg$ constructor discipline. Carrier
transport is then allowed only along the displayed source classifiers and
$\hsame$, so a group input can act on an algebra input only after both sources
have accepted their histories.

The automorphism-target row is the final admission check. It repacks the
transported endpoint as a classifier-respecting endomap of the
$\LieAlgebraUp$ carrier. Without this row the action is merely a hidden
construction over representatives, not a visible $\AdjointRepUp$ carrier row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AdjointRep action and differential obligation]
\label{thm:adjointrep-action-differential-obligation}
For carried inputs admitted by the source packet of
\autoref{def:adjointrep-source-packet}, $\mathrm{Ad}$ sends each carried group
endpoint to a classifier-respecting Lie-algebra endomap. The identity group
endpoint acts as the identity endomap, group multiplication composes the action
rows, and $\mathrm{ad}$ is the differential row compatible with the
$\LieAlgebraUp$ bracket classifier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the $\LieGroupUp$ action surface. Its carried identity and
multiplication rows, supplied by $\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$, determine the two
action laws: the identity endpoint leaves every accepted algebra endpoint in
the same $\hsame$ classifier class, and multiplication of carried group
endpoints transports to composition of the corresponding action rows.

Next apply the $\LieAlgebraUp$ bracket and endomap surface. The action endpoint
is admitted as an endomap only after classifier stability shows that
$\hsame$-equivalent algebra representatives have $\mathrm{Ad}$-images in the
same algebra classifier class.

Finally read the tangent row at the identity endpoint. The differential row
$\mathrm{ad}$ is accepted precisely when it is transported from the same source
classifiers and its bracket interaction is checked against the
$\LieAlgebraUp$ classifier, so no separate host derivative or host equality is
introduced into the public action law. These rows are then repacked as the
$\Pkg$ action and differential clauses named by the $\AdjointRepUp$
$\NameCert$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AdjointRep ledger exactness obligation]
\label{thm:adjointrep-ledger-exactness-obligation}
Exactness for the $\AdjointRepUp$ source packet is the conjunction of the
$\LieGroupUp$ source classifier exactness, the $\LieAlgebraUp$ source
classifier exactness, the action-row exactness, the differential-row exactness,
and the named ledger of \autoref{def:adjointrep-source-packet}. The public
surface contains no host functions and no host equality.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project exactness from the two dependencies named in the introduction. The
$\NameCert_{\LieGroupUp}$ projection accounts for every visible group source
row, and the $\NameCert_{\LieAlgebraUp}$ projection accounts for every visible
algebra source row. Both projections speak through their BEDC classifiers and
$\hsame$.

The action-row and differential-row clauses then add exactly the rows that
make $\AdjointRepUp$ distinct from its sources. Carrier transport and
classifier stability ensure that changing a representative changes neither the
accepted action endpoint nor the accepted differential endpoint.

It remains to compare the ledger. The ledger in
\autoref{def:adjointrep-source-packet} lists the source packages, action row,
differential row, automorphism target, stability rows, and exactness rows, and
it hides only the conjugator representative and tangent-source representative.
Repacking this list as a $\Pkg$ under the $\AdjointRepUp$ $\NameCert$ surface
therefore makes exactness exactly the displayed conjunction, with no public row
left for host functions or host equality.
\end{proof}
