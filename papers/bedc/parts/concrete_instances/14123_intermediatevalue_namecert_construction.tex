\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\IntermediateValueUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-intermediatevalue-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive intermediate-value reading, the $\IntermediateValueUp$ packet records the finite bisection route by which opposite endpoint signs, a displayed continuity modulus, and located interval windows generate a real-facing root-location seal. The certificate is a NameCert reconstruction of the bisection argument: it consumes the real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the finite branch ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicbisection-namecert}, and the nested-window carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nestedinterval-namecert}, but it does not claim open-cover compactness, quotient reals, host equality of real values, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

The packet also reads its endpoint cells through the located interval surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert} and its function data through the pointwise-modulus carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}. Thus the intermediate-value route is a finite ledger over displayed source rows, not a global theorem about all continuous maps on a host interval.

\begin{definition}[IntermediateValue located-sign carrier]
\label{def:intermediate-value-located-sign-carrier}
An $\IntermediateValueUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
V=(J,A,B,F,M,D,N,E,H,C,P,Q)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $J$ is the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ row naming the inspected endpoint interval and its rational and dyadic endpoint cells;
\item $A$ and $B$ are the endpoint sign rows, one recording the displayed negative-side read and one recording the displayed positive-side read after both endpoints have been transported into the same located interval packet;
\item $F$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ row carrying the map graph, pointwise modulus, and metric-distance witness used by the sign inspections;
\item $M$ is the finite modulus budget row selecting the dyadic precision at which the sign reads and midpoint tests are performed;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicBisectionUp$ branch ledger whose midpoint choices retain an interval with opposite displayed signs at the requested finite window;
\item $N$ is the $\NestedIntervalUp$ row obtained by threading the retained intervals of the bisection ledger;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row offered to downstream root-location consumers;
\item $H$ stores the $\hsame$ transports for the located interval, sign rows, continuous-map row, modulus budget, bisection ledger, nested interval row, and real seal;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ read routes by which a consumer reaches exactly those rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicBisectionUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $Q$ is the local $\NameCert_{\IntermediateValueUp}$ evidence row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts that these rows form one finite located-sign bisection packet. The packet has no field for a host real root, a quotient of real names, an order-completeness principle, a real trichotomy oracle, or an open-cover compactness argument.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[IntermediateValue dyadic bisection obligations]
\label{thm:intermediate-value-dyadic-bisection-obligations}
The seed $\NameCert_{\IntermediateValueUp}$ surface has five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: admission of the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet of \autoref{def:intermediate-value-located-sign-carrier};
\item endpoint sign exactness: the displayed negative and positive endpoint reads are routed through $J$, $F$, $M$, $H$, and $C$, and every transported sign read remains attached to the same located interval row;
\item bisection ledger exactness: each midpoint decision in $D$ is the displayed branch row whose retained subinterval preserves the transported opposite-sign boundary;
\item nested-window stability: the retained branch prefix in $D$ repacks as the $\NestedIntervalUp$ row $N$ through the displayed $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ routes;
\item real-seal handoff: the $\RealUp$ seal $E$ is exposed only after the located interval row, endpoint signs, continuous-map modulus row, bisection ledger, nested interval row, provenance row, and local NameCert row have all been read.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations are internal to the displayed packet and do not use compactness, quotient equality of real names, host real trichotomy, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IntermediateValueUp.IntermediateValueCarrier\_dyadic\_bisection\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the tuple $V$. Its coordinates are exactly the located interval, endpoint sign, continuous-map, modulus, bisection, nested-window, real-seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows listed in \autoref{def:intermediate-value-located-sign-carrier}.

Endpoint sign exactness reads the two endpoint signs only through the located interval and continuous-map rows. The stored $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data move both endpoint reads into the same finite window before any midpoint branch is inspected.

Bisection ledger exactness is the finite branch invariant inherited from the displayed $\DyadicBisectionUp$ row. Each retained subinterval is selected by a visible midpoint row, and the opposite-sign boundary is carried forward as part of the same packet.

Nested-window stability repacks the retained branch prefix as the $\NestedIntervalUp$ row. The repacking uses only the branch ledger, endpoint cells, and transport routes already present in $V$.

The real-seal handoff then exposes $E$ through the same displayed provenance. Since no coordinate of $V$ contains compactness data, quotient-real equality, a trichotomy oracle, or a completeness theorem, none of these can participate in the seed certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[IntermediateValue real seal handoff]
\label{thm:intermediate-value-real-seal-handoff}
For every accepted $\IntermediateValueUp$ carrier, every $\RealUp$ or root-location consumer factors through the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ endpoint row, the $\DyadicBisectionUp$ branch ledger, the $\NestedIntervalUp$ retained window, the $\ContinuousMapUp$ modulus row, the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet, the $\hsame$ transport rows, the $\Cont$ read routes, the $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\IntermediateValueUp}$ row.

Consequently the consumer route cannot bypass the finite located-sign bisection packet by reading a host root, an infinite nested intersection, quotient equality of real names, real trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or an ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted carrier $V=(J,A,B,F,M,D,N,E,H,C,P,Q)$. A root-location consumer can reach the real-facing row only through the continuation route $C$, so its first readable data are the located interval row $J$, the endpoint sign rows $A,B$, and the continuous-map row $F$ with its finite modulus budget $M$.

Next route the midpoint decisions through the bisection ledger. The ledger $D$ supplies the retained branch prefix, and \autoref{thm:intermediate-value-dyadic-bisection-obligations} keeps every branch choice attached to the transported endpoint signs and the displayed modulus row.

Then read the nested window. The retained branch prefix repacks as the $\NestedIntervalUp$ row $N$, so the real-facing request reaches a finite nested interval window before it reaches the seal row.

Finally expose $E$ with its structural rows. The transports $H$, routes $C$, provenance $P$, and local certificate row $Q$ bind the $\RealUp$ read to the finite packet. Every named consumer therefore factors through the stated rows, and there is no available coordinate for a host root, infinite intersection, quotient equality, trichotomy decision, compactness theorem, or completeness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[IntermediateValue bisection convergence handoff]
\label{thm:intermediate-value-bisection-convergence-handoff}
For every accepted $\IntermediateValueUp$ carrier
$$
V=(J,A,B,F,M,D,N,E,H,C,P,Q),
$$
if the retained branch prefix $D$ and nested window $N$ are read through the $\BisectionConvergenceUp$ packet supplied by \autoref{def:bisection-convergence-carrier}, then every root-location or $\RealUp$ consumer factors through $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicBisectionUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\BisectionConvergenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ before reading $E$.

Consequently the intermediate-value route can consume the named finite convergence certificate without introducing a selected root, quotient real equality, real trichotomy, countable choice, compactness, or an ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $V=(J,A,B,F,M,D,N,E,H,C,P,Q)$. By \autoref{thm:intermediate-value-real-seal-handoff}, any root-location or $\RealUp$ consumer first reaches the located interval row $J$, the dyadic branch ledger $D$, the retained nested window $N$, the displayed packet rows, and the local $\NameCert_{\IntermediateValueUp}$ evidence before it can read the seal row $E$.

Now read $D$ and $N$ through the $\BisectionConvergenceUp$ carrier of \autoref{def:bisection-convergence-carrier}. The carrier places the located interval source, dyadic branch ledger, retained nested windows, tail-diameter schedule, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, and terminal Real seal in one displayed finite route.

Apply \autoref{thm:bisection-convergence-tail-diameter} to the retained branch prefix and nested window. The convergence packet forces every tolerance request to pass through the displayed branch depth and retained-window diameter bound before any $\StreamNameUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback is exposed.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:bisection-convergence-real-seal-handoff}. The StreamName and RegSeqRat stages precede the terminal $\RealUp$ seal, so the original intermediate-value consumer factors through the stated $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicBisectionUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\BisectionConvergenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ route before reading $E$. Since neither carrier has a coordinate for a selected root, quotient real equality, trichotomy, choice, compactness, or ambient completeness, the composite handoff exports none of them.
\end{proof}
