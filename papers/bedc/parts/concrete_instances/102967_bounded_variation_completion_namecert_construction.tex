\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BoundedVariationCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-variation-completion-namecert}

Following Jordan's bounded-variation decomposition and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\BoundedVariationCompletionUp$ packet names the finite completion-facing surface by which bounded-variation data may be consumed as Real/Completion rows. It sits over the bounded-variation carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-boundedvariation-namecert}, the regulated-function approximation schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regulated-function-namecert}, the Darboux finite-sum boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-darboux-sum-namecert}, the Riemann--Stieltjes tagged-sum handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannstieltjes-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite certificate surface for BV completion reads; it does not introduce host function equality, a quotient of completed functions, a selected limit, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Bounded-variation completion carrier]
\label{def:bounded-variation-completion-carrier}
A $\BoundedVariationCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(V,R,D,I,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $V$ is the source $\BoundedVariationUp$ packet, including its finite partition-variation evidence, endpoint transports, common-refinement rows, and local $\NameCert_{\BoundedVariationUp}$ provenance.
\item $R$ is the $\RegulatedFunctionUp$ representative row carrying the displayed step-approximation schedule, tolerance-tail ledger, and finite-sum handoff through the regulated-function surface.
\item $D$ is the Darboux ledger row, read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-darboux-sum-namecert}, recording the upper and lower finite-sum windows used by completion-facing approximation requests.
\item $I$ is the Riemann--Stieltjes handoff row, read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-riemannstieltjes-namecert}, connecting the regulated representative and bounded-variation integrator rows to a finite tagged-sum route.
\item $S$ records the $\StreamNameUp$ read windows used to expose the requested finite approximation stages.
\item $Q$ records the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows by which the stream windows are returned as regular rational names.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the bounded-variation, regulated, Darboux, Riemann--Stieltjes, stream, and regular-rational rows have all been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the bounded-variation source, regulated representative, Darboux ledger, Riemann--Stieltjes handoff, stream windows, regular-rational readback, real seal, package, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a bounded-variation completion request to the regulated representative, from the representative to finite integration ledgers, from those ledgers to stream and regular-rational readback, and from readback to the real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BoundedVariationCompletionUp$, $\BoundedVariationUp$, $\RegulatedFunctionUp$, $\DarbouxSumUp$, $\RiemannStieltjesUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedVariationCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $V,R,D,I,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host function equality predicate, quotient completed function, selected completion limit, countable-choice selector, open-cover compactness proof, or ambient completeness witness for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded-variation completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-variation-completion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BoundedVariationCompletionUp$ carrier $K=(V,R,D,I,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedVariationCompletionUp}$ surface consists of exactly six obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed tuple $V,R,D,I,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N$;
\item variation-Cauchy compatibility, requiring each completion-facing tolerance request to be witnessed by finite bounded-variation partition rows and transported common-refinement ledgers from $V$;
\item regulated representative handoff, requiring $R$ to expose a step-approximation schedule and tolerance-tail row before any completion read reaches $D$ or $I$;
\item integration-ledger compatibility, requiring the Darboux row $D$ and the Riemann--Stieltjes row $I$ to consume the same displayed representative, partition, and bounded-variation evidence;
\item classifier transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ for every bounded-variation, regulated, integration, stream, regular-rational, real-seal, package, and naming row;
\item ledger nonescape from host function equality, quotient completed functions, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
These obligations are read through the finite route
$$
V,R \longmapsto D,I \longmapsto S,Q \longmapsto E
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection of \autoref{def:bounded-variation-completion-carrier}. The rows $V$ and $R$ expose the bounded-variation source and regulated representative before any integration or completion-facing row can be read.

Variation-Cauchy compatibility is finite at each tolerance request. The request first selects displayed partition and common-refinement rows from $V$, then uses the transported endpoint and variation ledgers to bound the requested window without invoking a completed function object.

The regulated representative handoff is supplied by $R$. Its step schedule and tolerance-tail row must be present before the Darboux and Riemann--Stieltjes ledgers can be exposed, so the integration route is attached to a visible regulated-function approximation rather than to a host quotient of functions.

For integration compatibility, $D$ and $I$ consume the same displayed representative and bounded-variation evidence. The Darboux row records finite upper and lower sum windows, while the Riemann--Stieltjes row records the tagged finite handoff; neither row adds a hidden selector or measure-valued carrier.

Transport and replay are exactly the rows $H$ and $C$. They move each displayed coordinate componentwise and preserve the route from $V,R$ through $D,I,S,Q$ to $E$.

The excluded host objects do not occur among the carrier coordinates. Hence the certificate obligations close only the displayed finite Real/Completion packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded-variation completion real-seal route]
\label{thm:bounded-variation-completion-real-seal-route}
Every Real/Completion consumer route for an accepted $\BoundedVariationCompletionUp$ packet factors through the displayed bounded-variation row $V$, regulated representative row $R$, Darboux and Riemann--Stieltjes rows $D$ and $I$, $\StreamNameUp$ windows $S$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows $Q$, and terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$. The route exposes only those rows and their visible $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance.

No consumer route in this scope uses host function equality, a quotient completed function, a selected limit, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a Real/Completion consumer request. The continuation row $C$ first replays the bounded-variation source $V$ and the regulated representative $R$, so the request is already attached to finite partition-variation evidence and a displayed approximation schedule.

The request then reaches the integration ledgers. The Darboux row $D$ supplies finite upper and lower approximation windows, while the Riemann--Stieltjes row $I$ supplies the tagged-sum handoff against the same bounded-variation source and regulated representative.

Only after those rows are present does the stream and regular-rational readback route appear. The windows in $S$ expose finite stream stages, and $Q$ returns the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows.

Finally the terminal row $E$ supplies the $\RealUp$ seal. Componentwise transport through $H$ and replay through $C$ preserve this order, while package and naming provenance add no bypass coordinate.

Since the route has displayed every coordinate used by the carrier, no host function equality, quotient completed function, selected limit, countable-choice selector, open-cover compactness proof, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness witness can enter the consumer boundary.
\end{proof}
