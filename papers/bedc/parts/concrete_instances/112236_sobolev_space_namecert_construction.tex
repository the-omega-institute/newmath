Following Sobolev and Adams--Fournier, the $\SobolevSpaceUp$ packet records a finite constructive-analysis surface for functions whose weak derivatives and norm budgets are visible as named BEDC rows. It sits downstream of the weak-derivative interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-weak-derivative-namecert}, the norm carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-norm-namecert}, the metric-completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, and the real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet supplies PDE, variational, and compactness-facing consumers with a displayed Sobolev-space carrier rather than a host function quotient, an ambient Banach theorem, or a selected completion point.

\begin{definition}[SobolevSpace carrier]
\label{def:sobolev-space-carrier}
A $\SobolevSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(F,D,N_0,I,M,R,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the source-function row, listing the finite function-name windows, domain probes, boundary tags, and scalar readbacks exposed to this Sobolev-space packet.
\item $D$ is the $\WeakDerivativeUp$ row, recording the displayed weak derivative actions, derivative-coordinate tags, test-action windows, and candidate derivative readbacks attached to $F$.
\item $N_0$ is the $\NormUp$ budget row. It binds the source-function row and each displayed weak-derivative row to the finite integrability, exponent, and seminorm ledger used by the carrier.
\item $I$ is the integrability and local-domain budget row, naming the finite measurable-window, power-budget, and comparison data that justify the accepted norm reads.
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff row, available only after $F,D,N_0,I$ have been displayed and only for the completion route named by this carrier.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ readback row for norm values, integrability budgets, weak-derivative action values, and completion-facing estimates.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for source-function, weak-derivative, norm, integrability, completion, Real readback, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from every Sobolev-space request through $F,D,N_0,I,M,R,H,P,L$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SobolevSpaceUp$, $\WeakDerivativeUp$, $\NormUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SobolevSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $F,D,N_0,I,M,R,H,C,P,L$. It has no coordinate for quotient equality of functions, a host distribution space, global measurability completion, countable choice, compact embedding, trace theory, PDE solvability, or an ambient Banach-space theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SobolevSpace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:sobolev-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SobolevSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
S=(F,D,N_0,I,M,R,H,C,P,L),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\SobolevSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source-function, weak-derivative, norm-budget, integrability, completion-handoff, Real readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item weak-derivative coverage, requiring every derivative read to pass through the displayed $\WeakDerivativeUp$ row $D$ before it can contribute to a Sobolev-space norm budget;
\item norm and integrability exactness, requiring every norm, exponent, and local-domain budget read to factor through $N_0,I$ before it reaches the $\RealUp$ row $R$;
\item completion nonescape, requiring the $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff row $M$ to be downstream of $F,D,N_0,I,R$ and not detachable as a selected completion point;
\item structural stability under $H,C,P,L$, which transports, replays, sources, and names only the displayed finite route.
\end{enumerate}
No $\SobolevSpaceUp$ obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:sobolev-space-carrier}. The analytic data visible to the classifier are exactly the source row $F$, weak-derivative row $D$, norm-budget row $N_0$, integrability row $I$, completion handoff $M$, and Real readback $R$.

The weak-derivative obligation is forced before any norm read. A consumer cannot inspect a derivative budget unless it first reads $D$, and $D$ exposes only the finite test-action and candidate derivative surface inherited from $\WeakDerivativeUp$.

The norm and integrability obligations are then attached to $N_0,I,R$. Norm values, exponent budgets, and local-domain comparisons reach the Real seal only through those displayed rows, so they cannot be replaced by quotient function equality or global measurability completion.

Finally read $H,C,P,L$. These rows transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet, while $M$ remains downstream of the displayed analytic rows. Hence the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SobolevSpace completion handoff]
\label{thm:sobolev-space-completion-handoff}
Every completion-facing read exported by an accepted $\SobolevSpaceUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
F,D,N_0,I \longmapsto R \longmapsto M \longmapsto H,C,P,L.
$$
The handoff supplies only displayed source-function windows, weak-derivative actions, norm and integrability budgets, Real readbacks, completion routing, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. It does not supply a quotient of functions, a selected equivalence class, a global distribution dual, compact embedding, trace theory, PDE solvability, countable choice, or an ambient Banach-space completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a completion-facing request for an accepted carrier. The request must first expose the source-function row $F$ and the weak-derivative row $D$, because those rows determine which function windows and derivative actions can enter the Sobolev norm.

Next the request reads $N_0$ and $I$. These rows bind the norm, exponent, and local integrability budgets to the same displayed function and derivative data, and the Real readback row $R$ is the only scalar seal available to the route.

The completion handoff $M$ is read after those rows. Transport and replay through $H,C$ preserve the route, while $P,L$ record provenance and local naming. Since the excluded host objects are not carrier coordinates, no completion-facing consumer can bypass the displayed route.
\end{proof}
