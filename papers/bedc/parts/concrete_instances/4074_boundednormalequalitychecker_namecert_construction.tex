\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-normal-equality-checker-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp$ packet records the normal-equality side of
\autoref{ch:metacic-decidable-audit-map} as a finite naming certificate. It
sits between the already-normal exclusion packet of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normalfalseexclusion-namecert} and the
closed-normal confluence fragment of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacicconfluencenormalfragment-namecert}: the
present packet names a bounded comparison surface, while those sibling packets
keep false-proof exclusion and confluence scopes separate.

The packet is not a global beta-conversion oracle. It exposes bounded
normalizer output rows, structural \texttt{Term.eq} readback,
finished-normalization witnesses when they are supplied, closedness rows,
componentwise $\hsame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$
provenance, and a local $\NameCert$ row.

\begin{definition}[BoundedNormalEqualityChecker carrier]
\label{def:bounded-normal-equality-checker-carrier}
A $\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(L,R,F,N_L,N_R,E,W,C,H,K,P,A)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ and $R$ are the two closed MetaCIC input term rows being compared;
\item $F$ is the shared unary fuel row for the bounded normalizer;
\item $N_L$ and $N_R$ are the bounded normalizer output rows obtained from
the two input terms at fuel $F$;
\item $E$ is the structural \texttt{Term.eq} readback row comparing $N_L$ and
$N_R$;
\item $W$ stores the finished-normalization witnesses for the two outputs
when such witnesses are part of the input surface;
\item $C$ records the closedness rows for the inputs and for the named normal
forms that a finished-soundness consumer may read;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the input terms,
fuel, normalizer outputs, equality readback, finished witnesses, closedness
rows, package row, and local naming row;
\item $K$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for running the bounded normalizer,
reading the structural equality result, replaying finished witnesses, and
transporting closedness rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over MetaCIC terms, bounded normalization,
\texttt{Term.eq}, finished-normalization witnesses, closedness, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $A$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for a fuel-erased normalizer, no proof that
every closed term reaches a finished normal form, no arbitrary closed-term
beta-conversion decider, and no host equality oracle.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp.BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}

\begin{definition}[BoundedNormalEqualityChecker classifier]
\label{def:bounded-normal-equality-checker-classifier}
Two accepted $\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp$ carriers are classified as the
same bounded normal-equality packet exactly when their displayed rows compare
componentwise. Their input rows agree by the closed MetaCIC term classifiers,
their fuel rows agree by the unary fuel classifier, their bounded normalizer
outputs agree by the displayed output rows, their equality decision agrees by
\texttt{Term.eq} readback, their finished-normalization and closedness rows
agree when those rows are present, and their $\Pkg$ provenance and local
$\NameCert$ rows name the same packet.

The classifier has no generator for comparing arbitrary beta-conversion
classes, no generator for choosing a fuel that finishes all closed terms, and
no generator for replacing structural \texttt{Term.eq} by host equality.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedNormalEqualityChecker NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-normal-equality-checker-namecert-obligations}
The carrier and classifier of
\autoref{def:bounded-normal-equality-checker-carrier} and
\autoref{def:bounded-normal-equality-checker-classifier} satisfy the local
$\NameCert_{\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp}$ obligation surface for bounded
normal equality. Carrier admission reads only the two input term rows, shared
fuel, bounded normalizer outputs, structural equality readback, optional
finished-normalization witnesses, closedness rows, $\hsame$ transports,
displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming row. Classifier
stability is componentwise over exactly those rows, and ledger exactness
rejects every consumer read outside them.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp.BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
For carrier admission, project the tuple $Q$. The rows $L,R,F,N_L,N_R,E,W,C$
are the entire computational surface, and the rows $H,K,P,A$ are the entire
structural replay surface.

For classifier stability, compare accepted packets row by row. Input terms,
fuel, normalizer outputs, equality readback, finished witnesses, and
closedness rows are transported only through the displayed $\hsame$ and
$\Cont$ rows.

For ledger exactness, inspect a downstream read. It can project a bounded
normalizer output, a \texttt{Term.eq} result, a supplied finished witness, a
closedness row, or a structural replay row. Since no carrier coordinate names a
global conversion oracle or a fuel-selection theorem, such a read cannot
escape the bounded packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedNormalEqualityChecker finished soundness boundary]
\label{thm:bounded-normal-equality-checker-finished-soundness-boundary}
If an accepted $\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp$ packet carries finished
closed-normalization witnesses for both bounded normalizer outputs, then a
true structural \texttt{Term.eq} readback may be consumed as equality of those
named finished normal forms and as beta-convertibility between those named
normal forms. The same readback does not prove that arbitrary closed terms
finish at some fuel, and it does not decide unrestricted beta conversion.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp.BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerCarrier\_finished\_soundness\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Read the bounded outputs $N_L$ and $N_R$ through the carrier, and read the
structural equality result through $E$. The equality claim is tied to those two
displayed normalizer outputs at the displayed fuel $F$.

When the finished rows in $W$ and the closedness rows in $C$ are present, the
consumer may replay the finished-soundness route recorded in $K$. That route
identifies the equality as a statement about the named finished normal forms,
not about all possible reductions from arbitrary closed terms.

The boundary follows by coordinate exhaustion. The packet has no row proving
fuel existence for every closed input and no row deciding beta conversion
outside the displayed bounded outputs.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedNormalEqualityChecker consumer non-escape]
\label{thm:bounded-normal-equality-checker-consumer-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp$ packet reads
only the finite rows
$$
L,R,F,N_L,N_R,E,W,C,H,K,P,A.
$$
The consumer may use the bounded normalizer outputs, the structural
\texttt{Term.eq} result, and the finished-normalization bridge when its
witness rows are displayed, but it receives no unrestricted beta-conversion
decision procedure, no hidden normalizer termination theorem, no quotient
equality of terms, and no host equality oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. The displayed tuple lists all computational,
structural, provenance, and local naming rows available to a consumer.

Use the NameCert obligations to force each read through the same finite packet.
Normalizer outputs pass through $N_L$ and $N_R$, equality readback passes
through $E$, and conditional finished soundness passes through $W,C,H,K,P,A$.

Apply the finished soundness boundary. It permits equality and
beta-convertibility only for the named finished normal forms supplied by the
packet, while the absent fuel-existence and global conversion coordinates block
every stronger read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedNormalEqualityChecker classifier stability row]
\label{thm:bounded-normal-equality-checker-classifier-stability-row}
For accepted $\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp$ packets
$$
Q=(L,R,F,N_L,N_R,E,W,C,H,K,P,A)
$$
and
$$
Q'=(L',R',F',N'_L,N'_R,E',W',C',H',K',P',A'),
$$
the classifier is stable exactly componentwise over the displayed rows
$L,R,F,N_L,N_R,E,W,C,H,K,P,A$. In particular, the comparison transports
the two input term rows, shared fuel row, bounded normalizer outputs,
structural \texttt{Term.eq} readback, supplied finished witnesses,
closedness rows, componentwise $\hsame$ data, displayed $\Cont$ routes,
$\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp}$
row. It has no additional coordinate for host equality, fuel erasure, or
global beta conversion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both accepted carriers through
\autoref{def:bounded-normal-equality-checker-carrier}. The classifier of
\autoref{def:bounded-normal-equality-checker-classifier} lists the same
coordinates and compares them row by row.

The NameCert obligations of
\autoref{thm:bounded-normal-equality-checker-namecert-obligations} force
each computational comparison through the displayed bounded normalizer,
structural readback, witness, and closedness rows. The consumer non-escape
result of \autoref{thm:bounded-normal-equality-checker-consumer-nonescape}
then rules out any stabilizing coordinate beyond the finite tuple.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BoundedNormalEqualityChecker ledger exactness row]
\label{thm:bounded-normal-equality-checker-ledger-exactness-row}
Every consumer read from an accepted $\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp$ packet
is exhausted by the bounded normalizer outputs $N_L,N_R$, the structural
\texttt{Term.eq} row $E$, the supplied finished witness row $W$, the
closedness row $C$, the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$, the displayed
$\Cont$ row $K$, the $\Pkg$ row $P$, and the local
$\NameCert_{\BoundedNormalEqualityCheckerUp}$ row $A$. The ledger therefore
contains no global beta-conversion procedure, no hidden termination theorem,
no quotient equality of terms, and no host equality oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the finite tuple in the order fixed by the carrier definition. The only
normalization outputs are $N_L$ and $N_R$, and the only equality readback is
the structural \texttt{Term.eq} row $E$.

Finished soundness is conditional on the displayed rows $W$ and $C$ and is
bounded by \autoref{thm:bounded-normal-equality-checker-finished-soundness-boundary}.
Transport, replay, provenance, and naming then pass through $H,K,P,A$.

Finally apply consumer non-escape from
\autoref{thm:bounded-normal-equality-checker-consumer-nonescape}. Since every
consumer read is a projection from the displayed ledger, no global
conversion, hidden termination, quotient equality, or host equality row can
be read.
\end{proof}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

\autoref{thm:bounded-normal-equality-checker-classifier-stability-row}.

The finished-soundness boundary is exactly
\autoref{thm:bounded-normal-equality-checker-finished-soundness-boundary}, and

The remaining ledger clause is
