\begin{definition}[Subject-reduction route equivalence carrier]
\label{def:subject-reduction-route-equivalence-carrier}

$$

with the following displayed rows:

\item $B$ is the bundle-route row, recording explicit construction of the four-field subject-reduction discharge bundle;
\item $T$ is the $\mathsf{SubjectReductionSetup}$ route row, recording the four setup fields as the typeclass-facing discharge source;
\item $D$ is the toBundle conversion row, recording the finite handoff from the setup row $T$ to the same bundle-shaped socket surface read from $B$;
\item $H$ gives componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the four discharge coordinates, the bundle row, the setup row, the conversion row, the consumer routes, provenance, and local naming;

\item $P$ gives $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Ext$, $\Cont$, $\SubjectReductionDischargeSocketUp$, $\SubjectReductionRouteClassifierUp$, the bundle route, the setup route, toBundle conversion, componentwise $\hsame$, and $\NameCert$ rows;

\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no inhabitant of the four discharge hypotheses, no closed subject-reduction theorem, no global setup instance for the present closed-CIC substrate, and no route equality outside the displayed finite rows.

\begin{definition}[Subject-reduction route equivalence classifier]
\label{def:subject-reduction-route-equivalence-classifier}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectReductionRouteEquivalence bundle-socket handoff]
\label{thm:subject-reduction-route-equivalence-bundle-socket-handoff}
For every accepted $\SubjectReductionRouteEquivalenceUp$ carrier $S=(B,T,D,H,C,P,N)$, the bundle route $B$ and the setup-to-bundle route $D$ feed the same $\SubjectReductionDischargeSocketUp$ surface. A consumer reaching the socket through $B$ reads the four displayed discharge coordinates in bundle order; a consumer reaching the socket through $T$ first passes through $D$ and then reads the same four coordinates through the componentwise transport rows in $H$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows. The row $B$ already has the bundle-shaped socket order, while $T$ exposes the same four discharge coordinates as setup fields.

Read the conversion row $D$. It is the only permitted route from $T$ to the socket-facing bundle shape, so any setup-side consumer must enter the socket through that displayed handoff.

Finally use the transport rows $H$ and the consumer routes $C$. They identify the four coordinates componentwise and replay them to the socket surface. Since the packet has no further route coordinate, both reads terminate at the same displayed socket rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectReductionRouteEquivalence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:subject-reduction-route-equivalence-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:subject-reduction-route-equivalence-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\SubjectReductionRouteEquivalenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite packet $S=(B,T,D,H,C,P,N)$;
\item row classification for the bundle row $B$, setup row $T$, and toBundle conversion row $D$;
\item componentwise stability through the $\hsame$ transport ledger $H$;
\item consumer exactness through the displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$;
\item package provenance and local naming through $P$ and $N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the first projection of the displayed packet $S$. The next projection reads the three route rows $B,T,D$ and therefore supplies the complete classifier surface for bundle, setup, and conversion coordinates.

The transport obligation is exactly the row $H$. It compares the four discharge coordinates and the route metadata componentwise, without adding another discharge source.

The remaining public obligations are the consumer routes and provenance rows. The row $C$ gives the only consumer reads, while $P$ and $N$ bind package origin and local naming. These five groups exhaust the carrier coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubjectReductionRouteEquivalence consumer non-escape]
\label{thm:subject-reduction-route-equivalence-consumer-nonescape}
Every consumer read of an accepted $\SubjectReductionRouteEquivalenceUp$ carrier receives route agreement only as the finite rows
$$

It cannot infer an inhabited discharge bundle, a closed subject-reduction theorem, a global $\mathsf{SubjectReductionSetup}$ instance for the present substrate, host equality of route implementations, or any discharge evidence not present in the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

For every stronger request, the packet has no coordinate to answer it. In particular, no row proves any of the four discharge hypotheses, installs a setup instance for the present substrate, or exports a closed subject-reduction theorem. The consumer therefore remains confined to finite route agreement.
\end{proof}
