\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LebesgueCoveringDimensionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lebesgue-covering-dimension-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Lebesgue and Hurewicz--Wallman, the $\LebesgueCoveringDimensionUp$
packet records covering dimension through finite open-cover refinements with
bounded overlap. It sits beside the finite refinement carrier of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-coveringdimension-namecert}, the topology row of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the metric-ball observation
surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, and the finite
radius ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-lebesgue-number-namecert}.
The packet names only displayed cover rows, refinement rows, and order ledgers;
it does not invoke arbitrary open-cover compactness, a selected subcover, or a
host dimension theorem.

\begin{definition}[Lebesgue covering dimension carrier]
\label{def:lebesgue-covering-dimension-carrier}
A $\LebesgueCoveringDimensionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(X,U,V,R,O,B,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the $\TopologyUp$ source row together with the metric observation
surface needed to read finite $\MetricBallUp$ cover cells.
\item $U$ is a finite open-cover request row, displayed as named topology
opens and local metric-ball witnesses.
\item $V$ is a finite refinement row whose entries are open rows over the same
source $X$.
\item $R$ is the refinement-containment ledger, recording for each row of $V$
the named row of $U$ that contains it.
\item $O$ is the order-at-most-$n$ overlap ledger, recording that every
displayed point-history read meets at most $n+1$ refinement rows.
\item $B$ is the finite ball-inspection row tying the topology opens to
$\MetricBallUp$ observations.
\item $F$ is the $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ radius ledger for the displayed
finite cover window.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,U,V,R,O,B,F$ and
for the certificate rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an open-cover request through
finite ball inspection, finite-radius readback, refinement containment, and
overlap checking.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LebesgueCoveringDimensionUp$,
$\TopologyUp$, $\MetricBallUp$, $\CoveringDimensionUp$,
$\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LebesgueCoveringDimensionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only by transporting these displayed
rows. It has no coordinate for a host open-cover family, a selected refinement,
an external multiplicity oracle, or a classical covering-dimension theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Lebesgue covering dimension NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:lebesgue-covering-dimension-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LebesgueCoveringDimensionUp$ carrier
$$
L=(X,U,V,R,O,B,F,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LebesgueCoveringDimensionUp}$ surface consists exactly
of topology-source admission through $X$, finite open-cover admission through
$U$, refinement-row admission through $V$, refinement containment through $R$,
order-at-most-$n$ overlap checking through $O$, metric-ball inspection through
$B$, finite Lebesgue-number radius readback through $F$, componentwise
$\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$,
$\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:lebesgue-covering-dimension-carrier}. The topology, cover,
refinement, containment, overlap, ball-inspection, finite-radius, transport,
replay, provenance, and naming rows are precisely the displayed coordinates.

The finite refinement obligation reads $U,V,R,O$ over the source row $X$.
Containment is stored as a row-to-row ledger, and the order bound is stored as
the visible overlap count for finite point-history inspections.

The metric side is only observational. The rows $B$ and $F$ route finite open
cells through $\MetricBallUp$ and $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$ before a consumer
may compare the packet with the sibling $\CoveringDimensionUp$ surface.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same route by transport, replay, provenance,
and local naming. Since no excluded host object occurs as a carrier coordinate,
the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lebesgue finite-refinement route]
\label{thm:lebesgue-covering-dimension-finite-refinement-route}
Every $\CoveringDimensionUp$ consumer of an accepted
$\LebesgueCoveringDimensionUp$ packet receives only the finite route
$$
\TopologyUp,\ \MetricBallUp,\ \FiniteLebesgueNumberUp
\longmapsto
U,V,R,O,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the dimension assertion is a displayed open-cover refinement with
order-at-most-$n$ overlap and finite radius readback. It exports no arbitrary
open-cover compactness principle, selected subcover, host refinement theorem,
or detached multiplicity oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligations of
\autoref{thm:lebesgue-covering-dimension-namecert-obligations}. They force a
consumer to read the source topology, the finite cover request, the refinement
rows, the containment ledger, and the overlap ledger before any dimension
claim can be exported.

Read the metric observation side next. The ball row $B$ supplies only the
displayed $\MetricBallUp$ inspections, while $F$ supplies only the finite
radius ledger already exposed by $\FiniteLebesgueNumberUp$.

The comparison with $\CoveringDimensionUp$ is therefore horizontal: both
packets share finite cover-refinement and overlap rows, but this chapter keeps
the Lebesgue finite-radius route explicit. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$
transport, replay, source, and name that finite route, so no host compactness
or selected-refinement object can be read from the packet.
\end{proof}
