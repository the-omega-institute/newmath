\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closed-normal-consistency-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$ packet gives the closed-normal branch of
\autoref{ch:closed-metacic-consistency-assembly} a finite BEDC landing.
It is deliberately downstream of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert}:
the packet records the normal-fragment consistency object that is already
assembled from closed typing, closedness, and beta-normality, while keeping the
subject-reduction obstruction as a separate boundary row.

\begin{definition}[Closed normal consistency carrier]
\label{def:closed-normal-consistency-carrier}
A $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(F,T,C,S,O,H,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the closed proof-of-false target row, naming the false encoding and
the empty-context typing boundary;
\item $T$ is the closed-term row, recording that the inspected term is closed at
the empty context;
\item $C$ is the closed-normal consistency statement row, naming
$\mathsf{BEDC.MetaCIC.ClosedNormalConsistencyAssemblyStatement}$;
\item $S$ is the beta-strong normality row consumed by the closed-normal
consistency statement;
\item $O$ is the subject-reduction obstruction handoff row, read from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert};
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the target, closed-term,
statement, normality, obstruction, package, and local naming rows;
\item $R$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consistency-audit
consumers read the finite packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over MetaCIC typing, closed terms,
beta-strong normality, the subject-reduction obstruction boundary, $\BHist$,
$\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for full closed consistency, no unblocked
closed subject-reduction theorem, no proof of the four subject-reduction
discharge obligations, and no ambient normalization principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed normal consistency subject-reduction obstruction handoff]
\label{thm:closed-normal-consistency-subject-reduction-obstruction-handoff}
Every consumer of an accepted $\ClosedNormalConsistencyUp$ packet reads the
normal-fragment consistency row through
$$
F,T,C,S,H,R,P,N
$$
and reads the subject-reduction boundary only through the separate obstruction
row $O$ supplied by
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert}.
The handoff permits citation of the closed-normal consistency statement for
already-normal closed terms, but it does not convert the obstruction row into
full closed subject reduction, closed reduction consistency, or a discharge of
the beta, application-argument, lambda-domain, and Pi-domain obligations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-normal-consistency-carrier}. The
normal-fragment side consists of the false target row $F$, the closed-term row
$T$, the statement row $C$, and the beta-strong row $S$, transported and routed
only through $H,R,P,N$.

The obstruction side is the single row $O$. By
\autoref{thm:metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert-obligations}, that
row exposes the parameterized subject-reduction theorem, the four discharge
obligations, and the checked obstruction readback as a finite packet rather
than as a closed subject-reduction theorem.

The consumer boundary is therefore separated into two reads. The
closed-normal consistency row may eliminate a closed normal proof-of-false
candidate, while the subject-reduction obstruction row records why a closed
reduction route to such a normal candidate is not part of this packet.

Since no carrier coordinate supplies the four discharge obligations, an
unparameterized closed subject-reduction proof, or closed reduction
consistency, the handoff cannot be repacked into a stronger MetaCIC consistency
claim.
\end{proof}
