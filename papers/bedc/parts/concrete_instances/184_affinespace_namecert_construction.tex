\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AffineSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-affinespace-namecert}

The affine-space interface $\AffineSpaceUp$ packages a torsor over a vector-space carrier: a set with a free transitive action of vector translations. It depends on $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:affinespace-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Affine history torsor carrier]
\label{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}
An $\AffineSpaceUp$ carried datum is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ package
$$
\mathcal{A}=(\mathcal{G},\mathcal{V},p,\tau,\eta_{\mathcal{G}},\eta_{\mathcal{V}},\lambda)
$$
where $\mathcal{G}$ is the $\GroupUp$ additive-translation source, $\mathcal{V}$ is the $\VecSpaceUp$ vector source, $p:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the point row, $\tau:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the vector-translation row, $\eta_{\mathcal{G}}$ and $\eta_{\mathcal{V}}$ are the displayed source $\NameCert$ witnesses, and $\lambda$ records that the translation row is read through the vector carrier of $\mathcal{V}$ and the additive group operation exposed by $\mathcal{G}$.

The affine classifier on two carried rows $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{A}'$ is the componentwise relation saying that the point rows are $\hsame$-compatible and that the translation rows are classified by the $\VecSpaceUp$ vector classifier:
$$
\mathsf{AffCls}(\mathcal{A},\mathcal{A}') :=
\hsame(p,p')\land \mathsf{VecCls}_{\mathcal{V}}(\tau,\tau').
$$
The affine action of a certified vector translation $v:\mathsf{BHist}$ on a carried point row $p$ is the $\Cont$-append row
$$
\mathsf{act}(p,v):=\Cont(p,\mathsf{trans}(v)),
$$
where $\mathsf{trans}(v)$ is the certified vector-translation packet scoped by the $\VecSpaceUp$ and $\GroupUp$ source certificates. Thus the carrier is a torsor surface over $\VecSpaceUp$ whose visible rows are only the point row, the vector-translation row, the source $\NameCert$ witnesses, and the finite $\Cont$ action ledger.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceHistoryTorsorCarrier}

\begin{definition}[AffineSpaceUp translation classifier]
\label{def:affinespace-translation-classifier}
For point rows $p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the carrier of \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}, the $\AffineSpaceUp$ translation classifier declares
$$
p \sim_{\mathsf{Aff}} q
$$
exactly when there are translation witnesses $v,w:\mathsf{BHist}$ licensed by the $\VecSpaceUp$ dependency certificate and the $\GroupUp$ dependency certificate such that the displayed action routes from $p$ to $q$ are equal up to $\mathsf{hsame}$. Equivalently, the classifier is equality of translation witnesses after transport through the source classifiers of $\mathcal{V}$ and $\mathcal{G}$ and through the $\mathsf{hsame}$ comparison on the $\Cont$ action rows.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceTranslationClassifier}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:affinespace-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp action closure obligation]
\label{thm:affinespace-action-closure-obligation}
Let $\mathcal{V}$ be a $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ dependency certificate and let $\mathcal{G}$ be a $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ dependency certificate. If $p:\mathsf{BHist}$ is carried by \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier} and $v:\mathsf{BHist}$ is a vector row accepted by $\mathcal{V}$ and by the translation row of $\mathcal{G}$, then every certified $\Cont$ action witness from $(p,v)$ has an endpoint $q:\mathsf{BHist}$ carried by the same $\AffineSpaceUp$ carrier, and the endpoint is stable under $\mathsf{hsame}$ transport of the action witness.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceHistoryTorsorCarrier\_action\_closure\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier row of $p$. It contains the $\VecSpaceUp$ source certificate, the $\GroupUp$ source certificate, and the finite ledger slot reserved for translation actions.

Insert the displayed $\Cont$ witness into that ledger. The $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ dependency scopes the vector endpoint, while the $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ dependency scopes the translation row that acts on the point. Therefore the endpoint $q$ is generated by the same carrier predicate over the same two certificates.

If the action witness is transported by $\mathsf{hsame}$, use the carrier transport fields of the two dependency certificates on the vector and group endpoints. Repacking the transported rows with the same point-source data gives the required carried endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp action coverage obligation]
\label{thm:affinespace-action-coverage-obligation}
For any carried point rows $p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the $\AffineSpaceUp$ carrier over $\mathcal{V}:\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and $\mathcal{G}:\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$, the certificate obligation surface contains a displayed vector row $v:\mathsf{BHist}$ and a $\Cont$ action witness carrying $p$ to a row $\mathsf{hsame}$-classified with $q$. The vector witness is accepted by the $\VecSpaceUp$ dependency certificate, its translation reading is accepted by the $\GroupUp$ dependency certificate, and the witness is unique only up to the classifier of \autoref{def:affinespace-translation-classifier}.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceTranslationClassifier\_action\_coverage\_obligation}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceHistoryTorsorCarrier\_action\_coverage\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Read the two point rows through the torsor carrier. The carrier records both rows against the same $\VecSpaceUp$ and $\GroupUp$ dependency certificates, so the only admissible route between them must be a translation row licensed by those dependencies.

The obligation is then stated by choosing the displayed vector row and the $\Cont$ action witness in the ledger. The endpoint comparison with $q$ is not host equality of points; it is the $\mathsf{hsame}$ row required by the carrier and classifier definitions.

Transporting the chosen witness along either dependency classifier gives an equivalent witness. Thus coverage is recorded as existence of a BEDC translation route, while uniqueness is deferred to the translation classifier rather than exposed as an extra carrier field.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp vector difference obligation]
\label{thm:affinespace-vector-difference-obligation}
For carried point rows $p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ over $\mathcal{V}:\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and $\mathcal{G}:\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$, any two certified vector-difference witnesses from $p$ to $q$ are identified by the $\AffineSpaceUp$ translation classifier. The comparison uses the $\VecSpaceUp$ classifier on vector histories, the $\GroupUp$ classifier on translation histories, and $\mathsf{hsame}$ transport along the two $\Cont$ action rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceTranslationClassifier\_witnesses\_identified}
\begin{proof}
Take two displayed difference witnesses. Each witness consists of a vector row, its translation-group reading, and a $\Cont$ action row from $p$ to an endpoint classified with $q$.

Apply the source classifier field of $\mathcal{V}$ to compare the two vector rows and the source classifier field of $\mathcal{G}$ to compare their translation readings. The two endpoint comparisons to $q$ are then concatenated by $\mathsf{hsame}$ symmetry and transitivity.

The resulting comparison is exactly the relation in \autoref{def:affinespace-translation-classifier}. Hence the difference row is determined at the $\AffineSpaceUp$ boundary only up to the BEDC classifier, with all chosen action ledgers remaining internal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp separation obligation]
\label{thm:affinespace-separation-obligation}
If carried point rows $p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ admit a certified zero-translation action from $p$ to a row $\mathsf{hsame}$-classified with $q$, then $p$ and $q$ are classified by the identity case of \autoref{def:affinespace-translation-classifier}. The zero row is the vector zero supplied by $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and the identity translation is the group identity supplied by $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceTranslationClassifier\_separation\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Project the zero vector row from the $\VecSpaceUp$ dependency certificate and the identity translation row from the $\GroupUp$ dependency certificate. These rows are the two dependency endpoints of the zero-translation action.

The action ledger says that acting by this identity translation is a $\Cont$ row from $p$ to an endpoint classified with $q$. The identity action law is therefore recorded as an $\mathsf{hsame}$ transport between the original point row and the endpoint row.

Combine that transport with the displayed endpoint comparison to $q$. Repacking the zero vector, identity translation, and composed $\mathsf{hsame}$ row gives the identity case of the translation classifier, so the carrier separates point rows exactly at the certified zero-translation boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Affine vector-action stability]
\label{thm:affinespace-vector-action-stability}
Let $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{A}'$ be carried $\AffineSpaceUp$ rows of \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}. If their point rows are related by $\hsame$ and their vector translations are related by the $\VecSpaceUp$ classifier, then appending any certified vector-translation packet by $\Cont$ preserves the affine classifier. Equivalently, for carried point rows $p,p'$ and vector rows $v,v'$,
$$
\hsame(p,p')\land \mathsf{VecCls}_{\mathcal{V}}(v,v')
$$
implies that the action endpoints $\Cont(p,\mathsf{trans}(v))$ and $\Cont(p',\mathsf{trans}(v'))$ are classified by $\mathsf{AffCls}$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceHistoryTorsorCarrier\_vector\_action\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Use the $\VecSpaceUp$ certificate to transport $v$ to $v'$ through its vector classifier. The $\GroupUp$ dependency reads the transported vector row as the same certified additive-translation packet in the translation source.

Apply $\Cont$ endpoint transport to the point comparison $\hsame(p,p')$ and to the transported translation packet. The $\Cont$ associativity and unit rows keep the appended action ledger in the same finite-history surface, so the resulting endpoint pair satisfies the displayed affine classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Affine free-action obligation]
\label{thm:affinespace-free-action-obligation}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}, the free-action obligation is the following BEDC row: if two certified vector translations $v,w$ act on the same carried point row $p$ and the resulting point endpoints are related by $\hsame$, then the vector rows $v$ and $w$ are related by the $\VecSpaceUp$ classifier. The only accepted witnesses are the shared point row, the two vector-translation packets, the two $\Cont$ action ledgers, and the source $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceTranslationClassifier\_free\_action\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Read both action endpoints as $\Cont$ appends from the same point row $p$. The left unit and right unit behavior of $\Cont$ identifies the empty translation packet as the neutral action row supplied by the additive $\GroupUp$ source.

Cancel the shared point ledger by composing the first action with the inverse translation packet supplied by the $\GroupUp$ certificate. Associativity of $\Cont$ moves the inverse packet next to the original packet, and the group inverse and unit rows reduce the visible endpoint comparison to a comparison of the two translation rows.

Finally, the $\VecSpaceUp$ classifier is the public classifier for translation rows in this carrier. Transport through the $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ witness repacks the endpoint comparison as $\mathsf{VecCls}_{\mathcal{V}}(v,w)$, with no host-level point equality used.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Affine transitive-action obligation]
\label{thm:affinespace-transitive-action-obligation}
For any two carried point rows $p$ and $q$ in the $\AffineSpaceUp$ carrier, the transitive-action obligation records a certified vector row $v$ whose translation packet carries $p$ to a point row classified with $q$. The witness ledger consists exactly of the source $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ row for $v$, the additive $\GroupUp$ row interpreting $v$ as a translation packet, and the $\Cont$ action ledger from $p$ to $q$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceHistoryTorsorCarrier\_transitive\_action\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Use the torsor source row in the carrier definition to read the difference from $p$ to $q$ as a vector-translation row in the $\VecSpaceUp$ carrier. This is a named $\mathsf{BHist}$ row, not a quotient of host points.

The $\GroupUp$ certificate interprets that vector row as an additive translation packet. Appending the packet to $p$ by $\Cont$ gives the action endpoint, and the stored torsor ledger supplies the $\hsame$ comparison between this endpoint and $q$.

The witness is therefore the displayed vector row together with its two source certificates and the finite action ledger. Exactness of the ledger prevents any additional choice of ambient affine coordinate system from entering the obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Affine classifier transport]
\label{thm:affinespace-classifier-transport}
The affine classifier of \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier} is stable under $\hsame$ transport of point rows and under $\VecSpaceUp$ classifier transport of vector-translation rows. If $\mathsf{AffCls}(\mathcal{A},\mathcal{A}')$ and the source $\NameCert$ rows transport $\mathcal{A}$ to $\mathcal{B}$ and $\mathcal{A}'$ to $\mathcal{B}'$, then $\mathsf{AffCls}(\mathcal{B},\mathcal{B}')$ holds.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the affine classifier into its point and translation components. The point component is an $\hsame$ comparison, so reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and endpoint transport are exactly the corresponding $\hsame$ rows.

For the translation component, apply the $\VecSpaceUp$ certificate transport field to the two vector rows. The $\GroupUp$ source is used only to keep the transported vector rows accepted as additive translation packets.

Recombine the transported point comparison and transported vector comparison. Since the classifier contains no hidden field beyond those two components, the recombined pair is precisely the target affine classifier.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceTranslationClassifier\_classifier\_transport}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceTranslationClassifier\_transport}
\begin{theorem}[Affine ledger exactness]
\label{thm:affinespace-ledger-exactness}
The $\AffineSpaceUp$ obligation ledger contains exactly the $\GroupUp$ source row, the $\VecSpaceUp$ source row, the carried point $\mathsf{BHist}$ row, the vector-translation $\mathsf{BHist}$ row, the $\hsame$ point classifier component, the $\VecSpaceUp$ translation classifier component, the $\Cont$ action rows, and the two source $\NameCert$ witnesses. No coordinate chart, host torsor equality, quotient representative, or standard bridge row is part of this obligation surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Soundness is by projection from the carrier tuple. Each accepted affine row exposes the two source certificates, the point row, the vector-translation row, and the two source $\NameCert$ witnesses. The classifier definition exposes only $\hsame$ on point rows and the $\VecSpaceUp$ classifier on vector rows.

The action rows are sound because they are built by appending the certified translation packet with $\Cont$. Associativity and the unit behavior of $\Cont$ account for changing parenthesization and for the neutral translation row, while the $\GroupUp$ certificate supplies the additive inverse and unit packets used by the free-action ledger.

Exactness is the reverse reading. Any row used by stability, freeness, transitivity, or classifier transport is one of the projected carrier rows, one of the named source-certificate fields, or one finite $\Cont$ action row. Hence the public obligation surface is exactly the listed $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ data.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceTranslationClassifier\_ledger\_exactness}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:affinespace-namecert-obligation-surface}
The $\AffineSpaceUp$ certificate obligation surface consists exactly of the $\mathsf{BHist}$ affine history torsor carrier of \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}, the translation classifier of \autoref{def:affinespace-translation-classifier}, the action closure, action coverage, vector-difference, separation, vector-action stability, free-action, transitive-action, classifier-transport, and ledger-exactness obligations of \autoref{thm:affinespace-action-closure-obligation}, \autoref{thm:affinespace-action-coverage-obligation}, \autoref{thm:affinespace-vector-difference-obligation}, \autoref{thm:affinespace-separation-obligation}, \autoref{thm:affinespace-vector-action-stability}, \autoref{thm:affinespace-free-action-obligation}, \autoref{thm:affinespace-transitive-action-obligation}, \autoref{thm:affinespace-classifier-transport}, and \autoref{thm:affinespace-ledger-exactness}, together with the two dependency certificates $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$. No host affine-space carrier, chosen origin, coordinate chart, quotient representative, or external vector-difference function is exported as a public field.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceHistoryTorsorCarrier\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Soundness is obtained by unpacking the carrier and classifier rows. Every visible point row is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ row scoped by the two dependency certificates, and every visible comparison is a translation witness read through $\Cont$ and $\mathsf{hsame}$.

The obligation theorems supply the action rows consumed by the certificate: closure gives carried endpoints, coverage gives a route between carried points, vector difference identifies parallel witnesses, separation fixes the identity route, stability and transport keep classifier rows invariant under accepted motion, freeness and transitivity record the torsor laws, and ledger exactness fixes the public surface. Each row is constructed only from the dependency $\NameCert$ fields and the finite action ledger.

For exactness, read any accepted ledger row in reverse. It must be either a point row, a vector or group dependency row, a $\Cont$ action witness, or an $\mathsf{hsame}$ transport used by the classifier. Therefore the visible $\NameCert_{\AffineSpaceUp}$ boundary is exactly the listed surface and no further public datum is claimed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp action additivity]
\label{thm:affinespace-action-additivity}
Let $\mathcal{V}:\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and $\mathcal{G}:\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ be the dependency certificates of \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}. Let $p:\mathsf{BHist}$ be a carried point row, and let $v,w:\mathsf{BHist}$ be vector rows accepted by $\mathcal{V}$ whose translation readings are accepted by $\mathcal{G}$. Write $v+_V w$ for the vector-addition row supplied by $\mathcal{V}$. Then the one-step action endpoint $\mathsf{act}(p,v+_V w)$ and the iterated action endpoint $\mathsf{act}(\mathsf{act}(p,v),w)$ admit carried affine repackings over the same source certificates and the same cumulative translation row $v+_V w$, and those repackings are classified by $\mathsf{AffCls}$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceHistoryTorsorCarrier\_action\_additivity}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:affinespace-action-closure-obligation} to the carried point row $p$ and the accepted vector row $v+_V w$. This produces a carried affine endpoint whose point component is $\mathsf{act}(p,v+_V w)$.

Apply the same closure theorem first to $(p,v)$ and then to the carried endpoint obtained from that first action together with $w$. This produces a carried affine endpoint whose point component is $\mathsf{act}(\mathsf{act}(p,v),w)$.

Unfold the action definition from \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}. The one-step endpoint is obtained by appending to $p$ the translation packet read from $v+_V w$. The iterated endpoint is obtained by first appending the packet read from $v$ and then appending the packet read from $w$.

Now unpack the field $\lambda$ from \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}. It records that the translation row attached to the vector-sum $v+_V w$ is read through the vector carrier of $\mathcal{V}$ and the additive translation operation exposed by $\mathcal{G}$. Therefore the tail used by the one-step action and the composite tail used by the iterated action are two certified readbacks of the same cumulative translation row.

Apply \autoref{thm:continuation-associativity-up-to-hsame-proof-standing} to the continuation ledger starting at $p$ with these two bracketings. It yields
$$
\hsame\bigl(\mathsf{act}(p,v+_V w),\mathsf{act}(\mathsf{act}(p,v),w)\bigr).
$$

Finally repack both endpoints as affine rows over the same two source certificates and the common cumulative translation row $v+_V w$. The point components are related by the displayed $\hsame$ row, and the translation components are the same vector-sum row, hence are classified by the $\VecSpaceUp$ classifier. By the definition of $\mathsf{AffCls}$ in \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}, the two repackings are classified together.
\end{proof}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.AffineSpaceUp.AffineSpaceHistoryTorsorCarrier\_action\_additivity}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp zero action classifies the carried point]
\label{thm:affinespace-zero-action-classifies-carried-point}
Let $\mathcal{V}:\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and $\mathcal{G}:\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ be the dependency certificates of \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}. Let $p:\mathsf{BHist}$ be a carried point row, and let $0_V:\mathsf{BHist}$ be the vector zero row supplied by $\mathcal{V}$ whose translation reading is the identity translation row supplied by $\mathcal{G}$. Then the zero-action endpoint $\mathsf{act}(p,0_V)$ and the original point row $p$ admit carried affine repackings over the same two source certificates and the same zero translation row, and those repackings are classified by $\mathsf{AffCls}$. Equivalently, the displayed zero action gives the identity case of the translation classifier between $p$ and $\mathsf{act}(p,0_V)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the action clause in \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}. There exist histories $t,u:\mathsf{BHist}$ such that $t$ is the certified translation packet read from the vector zero row $0_V$ through the $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ dependencies, $u$ is the appended endpoint witnessing
$$
\Cont(p,t,u),
$$
and $u$ is classified by $\hsame$ with $\mathsf{act}(p,0_V)$. By the hypothesis on $0_V$, the packet $t$ is the identity translation row exposed by the $\GroupUp$ dependency certificate.

Apply \autoref{thm:affinespace-action-closure-obligation} to the carried point row $p$ and the accepted vector row $0_V$. The endpoint $u$ is therefore carried by the same $\AffineSpaceUp$ carrier over $\mathcal{V}$ and $\mathcal{G}$.

The same displayed action ledger is a certified zero-translation action from $p$ to the endpoint $u$. Its endpoint comparison with $u$ is the reflexive $\hsame$ row. Thus the hypotheses of \autoref{thm:affinespace-separation-obligation} hold with $q=u$, and the separation obligation repacks the zero vector row, the identity translation row, and the endpoint comparison as the identity case of \autoref{def:affinespace-translation-classifier}.

It remains only to read this identity classification in the affine carrier language. Repack $p$ and $u$ as affine rows over the same source certificates and with the same zero translation row $0_V$. The point component is the $\hsame$ comparison supplied by the identity action case, and the translation component is the reflexive $\VecSpaceUp$ classifier comparison of $0_V$ with itself. By the definition of $\mathsf{AffCls}$ in \autoref{def:affinespace-history-torsor-carrier}, the two carried repackings are classified together.

Finally transport the carried repacking of $u$ along the recorded $\hsame$ comparison between $u$ and $\mathsf{act}(p,0_V)$. This gives the stated classification of the original point row $p$ with the zero-action endpoint $\mathsf{act}(p,0_V)$ over the same two source certificates and the same zero translation row.
\end{proof}
