\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\AffineSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-affinespace-namecert}

The affine-space interface $\AffineSpaceUp$ packages a torsor over a vector-space carrier: a set with a free transitive action of vector translations. It depends on $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-group-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:affinespace-carrier}

\begin{definition}[AffineSpaceUp torsor point carrier]
\label{def:affinespace-torsor-point-carrier}
An $\AffineSpaceUp$ point carrier is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ predicate
$$
\mathsf{AffPt}_{\mathcal{V},\mathcal{G}}(p)
$$
scoped by a $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ dependency certificate $\mathcal{V}$ and a $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ dependency certificate $\mathcal{G}$. A carried point row consists of a point history $p:\mathsf{BHist}$ together with a vector-row source from $\mathcal{V}$, a translation-group source from $\mathcal{G}$, and a finite ledger saying that every displayed translation of $p$ is obtained by a $\Cont$ action witness whose vector endpoint is accepted by $\mathcal{V}$ and whose translation endpoint is accepted by $\mathcal{G}$. The carrier exposes only the point row and the dependency certificates; the chosen origin, chosen translation witness, and action ledger remain internal certificate data.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[AffineSpaceUp translation classifier]
\label{def:affinespace-translation-classifier}
For point rows $p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the carrier of \autoref{def:affinespace-torsor-point-carrier}, the $\AffineSpaceUp$ classifier declares
$$
p \sim_{\mathsf{Aff}} q
$$
exactly when there are translation witnesses $v,w:\mathsf{BHist}$ licensed by the $\VecSpaceUp$ dependency certificate and the $\GroupUp$ dependency certificate such that the two displayed action routes from $p$ to $q$ are equal up to $\mathsf{hsame}$. Equivalently, the classifier is equality of translation witnesses after transport through the source classifiers of $\mathcal{V}$ and $\mathcal{G}$ and through the $\mathsf{hsame}$ comparison on the $\Cont$ action rows.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:affinespace-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp action closure obligation]
\label{thm:affinespace-action-closure-obligation}
Let $\mathcal{V}$ be a $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ dependency certificate and let $\mathcal{G}$ be a $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ dependency certificate. If $p:\mathsf{BHist}$ is carried by \autoref{def:affinespace-torsor-point-carrier} and $v:\mathsf{BHist}$ is a vector row accepted by $\mathcal{V}$ and by the translation row of $\mathcal{G}$, then every certified $\Cont$ action witness from $(p,v)$ has an endpoint $q:\mathsf{BHist}$ carried by the same $\AffineSpaceUp$ carrier, and the endpoint is stable under $\mathsf{hsame}$ transport of the action witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the carrier row of $p$. It contains the $\VecSpaceUp$ source certificate, the $\GroupUp$ source certificate, and the finite ledger slot reserved for translation actions.

Insert the displayed $\Cont$ witness into that ledger. The $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ dependency scopes the vector endpoint, while the $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$ dependency scopes the translation row that acts on the point. Therefore the endpoint $q$ is generated by the same carrier predicate over the same two certificates.

If the action witness is transported by $\mathsf{hsame}$, use the carrier transport fields of the two dependency certificates on the vector and group endpoints. Repacking the transported rows with the same point-source data gives the required carried endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp action coverage obligation]
\label{thm:affinespace-action-coverage-obligation}
For any carried point rows $p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ in the $\AffineSpaceUp$ carrier over $\mathcal{V}:\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and $\mathcal{G}:\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$, the certificate obligation surface contains a displayed vector row $v:\mathsf{BHist}$ and a $\Cont$ action witness carrying $p$ to a row $\mathsf{hsame}$-classified with $q$. The vector witness is accepted by the $\VecSpaceUp$ dependency certificate, its translation reading is accepted by the $\GroupUp$ dependency certificate, and the witness is unique only up to the classifier of \autoref{def:affinespace-translation-classifier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the two point rows through the torsor carrier. The carrier records both rows against the same $\VecSpaceUp$ and $\GroupUp$ dependency certificates, so the only admissible route between them must be a translation row licensed by those dependencies.

The obligation is then stated by choosing the displayed vector row and the $\Cont$ action witness in the ledger. The endpoint comparison with $q$ is not host equality of points; it is the $\mathsf{hsame}$ row required by the carrier and classifier definitions.

Transporting the chosen witness along either dependency classifier gives an equivalent witness. Thus coverage is recorded as existence of a BEDC translation route, while uniqueness is deferred to the translation classifier rather than exposed as an extra carrier field.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp vector difference obligation]
\label{thm:affinespace-vector-difference-obligation}
For carried point rows $p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ over $\mathcal{V}:\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and $\mathcal{G}:\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$, any two certified vector-difference witnesses from $p$ to $q$ are identified by the $\AffineSpaceUp$ translation classifier. The comparison uses the $\VecSpaceUp$ classifier on vector histories, the $\GroupUp$ classifier on translation histories, and $\mathsf{hsame}$ transport along the two $\Cont$ action rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Take two displayed difference witnesses. Each witness consists of a vector row, its translation-group reading, and a $\Cont$ action row from $p$ to an endpoint classified with $q$.

Apply the source classifier field of $\mathcal{V}$ to compare the two vector rows and the source classifier field of $\mathcal{G}$ to compare their translation readings. The two endpoint comparisons to $q$ are then concatenated by $\mathsf{hsame}$ symmetry and transitivity.

The resulting comparison is exactly the relation in \autoref{def:affinespace-translation-classifier}. Hence the difference row is determined at the $\AffineSpaceUp$ boundary only up to the BEDC classifier, with all chosen action ledgers remaining internal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp separation obligation]
\label{thm:affinespace-separation-obligation}
If carried point rows $p,q:\mathsf{BHist}$ admit a certified zero-translation action from $p$ to a row $\mathsf{hsame}$-classified with $q$, then $p$ and $q$ are classified by the identity case of \autoref{def:affinespace-translation-classifier}. The zero row is the vector zero supplied by $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and the identity translation is the group identity supplied by $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the zero vector row from the $\VecSpaceUp$ dependency certificate and the identity translation row from the $\GroupUp$ dependency certificate. These rows are the two dependency endpoints of the zero-translation action.

The action ledger says that acting by this identity translation is a $\Cont$ row from $p$ to an endpoint classified with $q$. The identity action law is therefore recorded as an $\mathsf{hsame}$ transport between the original point row and the endpoint row.

Combine that transport with the displayed endpoint comparison to $q$. Repacking the zero vector, identity translation, and composed $\mathsf{hsame}$ row gives the identity case of the translation classifier, so the carrier separates point rows exactly at the certified zero-translation boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[AffineSpaceUp NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:affinespace-namecert-obligation-surface}
The $\AffineSpaceUp$ certificate obligation surface consists exactly of the $\mathsf{BHist}$ point carrier of \autoref{def:affinespace-torsor-point-carrier}, the translation classifier of \autoref{def:affinespace-translation-classifier}, the action closure, action coverage, vector-difference, and separation obligations of \autoref{thm:affinespace-action-closure-obligation}, \autoref{thm:affinespace-action-coverage-obligation}, \autoref{thm:affinespace-vector-difference-obligation}, and \autoref{thm:affinespace-separation-obligation}, together with the two dependency certificates $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\GroupUp}$. No host affine-space carrier, chosen origin, or external vector-difference function is exported as a public field.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Soundness is obtained by unpacking the carrier and classifier rows. Every visible point row is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ row scoped by the two dependency certificates, and every visible comparison is a translation witness read through $\Cont$ and $\mathsf{hsame}$.

The four obligation theorems supply the action rows consumed by the certificate: closure gives carried endpoints, coverage gives a route between carried points, vector difference identifies parallel witnesses, and separation fixes the identity route. Each row is constructed only from the dependency $\NameCert$ fields and the finite action ledger.

For exactness, read any accepted ledger row in reverse. It must be either a point row, a vector or group dependency row, a $\Cont$ action witness, or an $\mathsf{hsame}$ transport used by the classifier. Therefore the visible $\NameCert_{\AffineSpaceUp}$ boundary is exactly the listed surface and no further public datum is claimed.
\end{proof}
