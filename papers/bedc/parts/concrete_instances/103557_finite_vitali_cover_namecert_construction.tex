\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-finite-vitali-cover-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Vitali's finite covering argument, Bishop--Bridges located interval reasoning, and the dyadic-radius bookkeeping used by constructive real analysis, the $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$ packet records the finite subcover-selection surface as BEDC NameCert data. It sits after the located interval carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the dyadic rational core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the real-value seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names finite interval rows, radius rows, a disjoint selected subfamily, shadow-coverage rows, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming only; it does not import measure, arbitrary open-cover compactness, countable choice, or host set equality.

\begin{definition}[FiniteVitaliCover carrier]\label{def:finite-vitali-cover-carrier}
A $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
V=(I,R,S,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the finite located-interval row. It lists the candidate intervals by their $\LocatedIntervalUp$ endpoints and records the displayed membership windows through which a later real consumer may inspect each interval.
\item $R$ is the dyadic radius row. It records the positive $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius attached to each interval, together with the finite comparison windows used to order candidate radii.
\item $S$ is the selected subfamily row. It records a finite list of chosen interval indices and the pairwise disjointness checks between the selected located intervals.
\item $W$ is the shadow-coverage row. It records, for every candidate interval not selected, a selected interval with comparable or larger dyadic radius whose controlled enlargement covers the candidate's displayed located window.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the located-interval row, dyadic radius row, selected disjoint subfamily row, shadow-coverage row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the finite route
$$
I,R \longmapsto S,W \longmapsto H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteVitaliCoverUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,R,S,W,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for Lebesgue measure, a countable choice sequence, open-cover compactness, a host set equality predicate, or a global family of balls.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FiniteVitaliCover NameCert obligations]\label{thm:finite-vitali-cover-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FiniteVitaliCoverUp$ carrier
$$
V=(I,R,S,W,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\FiniteVitaliCoverUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the finite located-interval row, dyadic radius row, selected disjoint subfamily row, shadow-coverage row, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. The shadow row must witness that every unselected candidate interval is covered by the controlled enlargement of a selected interval whose dyadic radius is comparable or larger, and every public cover read must pass through the route displayed by $C$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier in \autoref{def:finite-vitali-cover-carrier}. The only mathematical data exposed to a cover consumer are the located interval row $I$, dyadic radius row $R$, selected subfamily row $S$, and shadow-coverage row $W$.

The selected row records pairwise disjointness directly, while the shadow row records the finite witness from each unselected interval to a selected controlling interval. Thus the usual finite Vitali conclusion is read as a bounded finite shadow of the selected subfamily rather than as a measure-theoretic covering theorem.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly this finite surface. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for measure, countable choice, arbitrary open-cover compactness, host set equality, or an infinite ball family, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
