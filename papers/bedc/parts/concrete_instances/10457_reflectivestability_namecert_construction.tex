The $\ReflectiveStabilityUp$ packet realizes the reflective stability certificate of \autoref{ch:visions-reality-constrained-approximation-towers} as a finite NameCert surface. It consumes the source and classifier rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observationreflectionpacket-namecert}, the checkpoint boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflection-runtime-checkpoint-namecert}, the model-audit row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-model-audit-namecert}, the closed observation carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedobservationsystem-namecert}, and the finite observation interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-observation-interface-namecert}. Its role is to certify reuse under displayed continuations without exporting final truth or source-domain identity.

\begin{definition}[ReflectiveStability carrier]
\label{def:reflective-stability-carrier}
A $\ReflectiveStabilityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(S,K,A,Q,O,F,T,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the finite source row inherited from an $\ObservationReflectionPacketUp$ inquiry;
\item $K$ is the checkpoint row naming the visible $\ReflectionRuntimeCheckpointUp$ reuse boundary;
\item $A$ is the classifier and audit row inherited from $\RealityConstrainedModelAuditUp$;
\item $Q$ is the open-continuation row under which the self-description may be reused;
\item $O$ is the closed observation row inherited from $\ClosedObservationSystemUp$;
\item $F$ is the finite interface row inherited from $\FiniteObservationInterfaceUp$;
\item $T$ is the transport row carrying componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons among the displayed source, checkpoint, classifier, observation, and interface rows;
\item $L$ is the ledger row recording hidden source material, rejected finality claims, and source-domain separation;
\item $H$ is the finite $\BHist$ carrier row for the reuse trace;
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ route through the accepted reuse continuation;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the same source, checkpoint, classifier, continuation, observation, interface, transport, and ledger rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ReflectiveStabilityUp}$ row naming exactly this packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a final truth predicate, an identity between physical source and formal source, a host validation oracle, or an undisplayed checkpoint.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ReflectiveStability NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:reflective-stability-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:reflective-stability-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ReflectiveStabilityUp}$ surface consists exactly of seven finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $R=(S,K,A,Q,O,F,T,L,H,C,P,N)$;
\item source exposure, requiring every reuse read to begin from the displayed $\ObservationReflectionPacketUp$ source row $S$;
\item checkpoint exposure, requiring every reflected self-description to pass through the visible checkpoint row $K$;
\item classifier and audit exposure, requiring reuse to preserve the displayed audit row $A$ before any continuation read;
\item open-continuation stability, requiring $Q$ and $C$ to expose the continuation under which reuse is licensed;
\item observation and interface stability, requiring $O$ and $F$ to keep the closed observation and finite interface rows visible;
\item transport, ledger, provenance, history, and local naming closure through $T,L,H,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus reflective stability is certified as finite reuse under displayed rows, not as a total self-truth principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:reflective-stability-carrier}. The twelve rows $S,K,A,Q,O,F,T,L,H,C,P,N$ are the complete finite packet visible to a consumer.

Source, checkpoint, and classifier exposure come from the inbound packets. The source row is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observationreflectionpacket-namecert}; the checkpoint row is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reflection-runtime-checkpoint-namecert}; the classifier and audit row is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-reality-constrained-model-audit-namecert}. These rows describe reuse of a self-description, not identity with the source domain.

The continuation and observation rows then delimit the reuse claim. The open-continuation row $Q$ and displayed route $C$ state where the certificate may be replayed, while $O$ and $F$ keep the closed observation and finite interface surfaces visible.

Finally the transport, ledger, history, provenance, and local naming rows close the certificate. Since $T,L,H,P,N$ add no final truth coordinate and no hidden checkpoint coordinate, the seven obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ReflectiveStability anchor stability]
\label{thm:reflective-stability-anchor-stability}
Every accepted $\ReflectiveStabilityUp$ packet preserves the same displayed anchor rows $S,K,A,O,F$ whenever its self-description is reused under $Q$ through the displayed $\Cont$ route $C$. A consumer may transport these anchors through $T$ and record the reuse in $L,H,P,N$, but it cannot replace them by a detached source, an undisplayed checkpoint, a hidden classifier, an unbounded observation stream, or a host validation route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:reflective-stability-carrier}. The only anchor rows available for reuse are $S,K,A,O,F$, and the only continuation route available for reuse is $Q$ through $C$.

Apply the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:reflective-stability-namecert-obligations}. It requires source exposure, checkpoint exposure, classifier and audit exposure, open-continuation stability, and observation-interface stability before any consumer may read the local NameCert row.

The remaining structural rows only preserve the displayed anchors. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport at $T$, ledgering at $L$, history at $H$, package provenance at $P$, and local naming at $N$ record the same finite route. None of these rows contains a detached source, hidden classifier, unbounded observation stream, or host validation route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ReflectiveStability nonescape]
\label{thm:reflective-stability-nonescape}
No accepted $\ReflectiveStabilityUp$ packet exports final truth, source-domain identity, host validation, or an undisplayed reflection checkpoint beyond the rows
$$
S,K,A,Q,O,F,T,L,H,C,P,N.
$$
Every reflective reuse read must remain inside the displayed finite source, checkpoint, classifier, open-continuation, transport, ledger, provenance, and local NameCert packet.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier. Its only truth-facing row is the finite classifier and audit row $A$, its only reflection-facing row is the checkpoint row $K$, and its only future-facing rows are the open continuation $Q$ and displayed route $C$.

The ledger row records the excluded demands as non-exported material. Final truth, source-domain identity, host validation, and undisplayed checkpoints therefore have no carrier coordinate through which they can be read.
\end{proof}
