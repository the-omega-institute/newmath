\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy lattice operations carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-lattice-operations-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyLatticeOperationsUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(X,Y,T,D,M,A,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the two accepted $\RealUp$ rows presented by regular Cauchy names, exposing only their finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks and terminal real seals.
\item $T$ is the shared tolerance row used to align the lower-member and upper-member requests.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger for the displayed comparison scale, branch budgets, and readback arithmetic consumed by both operation rows.
\item $M$ is the $\RegularCauchyMinimumUp$ lower-member row.
\item $A$ is the $\RegularCauchyMaximumUp$ upper-member row.
\item $R$ is the paired regular rational readback row for the two operation outputs.
\item $E$ is the paired $\RealUp$ seal row, reached only after $M,A,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two input names, tolerance, dyadic arithmetic, minimum row, maximum row, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a lattice-operation request through $X,Y,T,D,M,A,R,E,H,P,N$.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyLatticeOperationsUp}$ row.

The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,T,D,M,A,R,E,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for trichotomy, quotient real equality, host order completeness, selected limits, countable choice, a host lattice structure, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy lattice operations NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-lattice-operations-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyLatticeOperationsUp$ carrier
$$
L=(X,Y,T,D,M,A,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyLatticeOperationsUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two regular Cauchy real rows, shared tolerance row, dyadic arithmetic ledger, minimum row, maximum row, paired readback, paired seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item shared-scale exposure, requiring $T$ and $D$ to be displayed before either $M$ or $A$ may be consumed;
\item lower-member routing, requiring the minimum output to be read through the $\RegularCauchyMinimumUp$ row $M$;
\item upper-member routing, requiring the maximum output to be read through the $\RegularCauchyMaximumUp$ row $A$;
\item readback and seal ordering, requiring $R$ to expose the paired regular rational outputs before $E$ exposes the paired $\RealUp$ seals;
\item classifier stability, requiring componentwise $\hsame$ transport of $X,Y,T,D,M,A,R,E,C,P,N$ to preserve the accepted operation route;
\item non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name no trichotomy, quotient real equality, host order completeness, selected limit, countable choice, host lattice structure, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-lattice-operations-carrier}. Its displayed rows are exactly the two regular Cauchy real inputs, the shared tolerance row, the dyadic ledger, the two operation rows, the paired readback and seal rows, and the structural rows.

Shared-scale exposure follows because $M$ and $A$ have no independent tolerance coordinate inside this packet. Both operation rows read the same $T,D$ surface before any output row can be consumed.

The lower-member and upper-member obligations are inherited as named rows, not as host operations. The packet can read the lower member only through $M$ and the upper member only through $A$.

Readback and seal ordering is forced by $R,E$. The real-facing consumer first receives the paired regular rational readback and only then the paired $\RealUp$ seal. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are exhausted by $H,C,P,N$, so no excluded host or quotient object is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy lattice operations min-max readback]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-lattice-operations-minmax-readback}
Let $L=(X,Y,T,D,M,A,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RegularCauchyLatticeOperationsUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$ consumer of the lattice-operation output factors through the finite route
$$
X,Y,T,D \longmapsto M,A \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the packet exports the lower and upper regular Cauchy operation outputs only as paired $\RegularCauchyMinimumUp$ and $\RegularCauchyMaximumUp$ readbacks over the same dyadic scale. It exports no trichotomy, quotient real equality, host order completeness, selected limit, countable choice, or host lattice operation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a real-facing read of the carrier. The only input rows are $X,Y$, and the only scale rows are $T,D$, so the consumer must read those coordinates before either operation row is available.

The operation rows are $M$ and $A$. By \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-lattice-operations-carrier}, $M$ names the lower-member route and $A$ names the upper-member route; neither row carries a separate host order principle.

After the operation rows are displayed, the readback row $R$ exposes the paired regular rational outputs and the seal row $E$ exposes the paired $\RealUp$ boundary. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve and name this route without adding any coordinate beyond it.
\end{proof}
