\begin{definition}[Kuratowski completion naturality carrier]
\label{def:kuratowski-completion-naturality-carrier}
A $\KuratowskiCompletionNaturalityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(M,E,C,F,R,S,D,A,H,T,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the source metric row, carrying the finite endpoint, distance, and Cauchy-window requests inspected by both completion routes.
\item $E$ is the $\KuratowskiCompletionUp$ row that sends the source metric observations through the Kuratowski embedding and completion seal.
\item $C$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumer row reached by the Kuratowski route after the displayed completion seal is exposed.
\item $F$ is the $\CompletionReflectionUp$ witness row that displays the reflection route for the same source metric observations.
\item $R$ is the $\RealCompletionReflectionUp$ row that returns the reflection route to the public real-completion boundary.
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row shared by the Kuratowski and reflection reads.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the shared finite windows.
\item $A$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that converts the finite dyadic observations into the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source metric, Kuratowski completion, metric-completion consumer, reflection witness, real-reflection seal, stream window, dyadic ledger, regular readback, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $T$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the two routes
$$
M \longmapsto E,C \longmapsto S,D,A \quad\text{and}\quad M \longmapsto F,R \longmapsto S,D,A .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\KuratowskiCompletionNaturalityUp$, $\KuratowskiCompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\CompletionReflectionUp$, $\RealCompletionReflectionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\KuratowskiCompletionNaturalityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,E,C,F,R,S,D,A,H,T,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, host function equality, selected representatives, countable choice, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Kuratowski completion naturality obligations]
\label{thm:kuratowski-completion-naturality-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\KuratowskiCompletionNaturalityUp$ carrier
$$
K=(M,E,C,F,R,S,D,A,H,T,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\KuratowskiCompletionNaturalityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source metric row, Kuratowski completion row, metric-completion consumer row, completion-reflection witness, real-reflection seal, StreamName window, DyadicRatCore ledger, RegSeqRat readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item Kuratowski-completion exposure, requiring $E$ and $C$ to display the source metric read through the Kuratowski completion route before any completion-facing consumer receives it;
\item reflection-square routing, requiring $F$ and $R$ to display the same source metric read through the completion-reflection and real-reflection route before it reaches the shared analytic rows;
\item Real-seal nonescape, requiring $S,D,A$ to be the only finite observation path from either route to the terminal $\RealUp$ seal;
\item transport, replay, and provenance exhaustion, requiring $H,T,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the listed rows.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation is stored outside these rows, and none exports quotient completions, host function equality, selected representatives, countable choice, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:kuratowski-completion-naturality-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the source metric row, the two completion-facing routes, the shared finite observation rows, and the structural rows.

Kuratowski-completion exposure is read from $E$ and $C$. The row $E$ places the source metric observations inside the Kuratowski completion route, while $C$ records the metric-completion consumer surface available after that route is displayed.

Reflection-square routing is read from $F$ and $R$. The reflection witness and real-reflection seal expose the parallel route for the same source metric row before either side can inspect the shared stream, dyadic, and regular-readback rows.

Real-seal nonescape is the row inventory $S,D,A$. Both routes must pass through the same finite StreamName window, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, and RegSeqRat readback before reaching the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.

Finally $H,T,P,N$ add only componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since the carrier contains no quotient, host-equality, representative-choice, countable-choice, trichotomy, ambient-completeness, Lean, or bridge coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Kuratowski completion naturality square]
\label{thm:kuratowski-completion-naturality-square}
Let
$$
K=(M,E,C,F,R,S,D,A,H,T,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\KuratowskiCompletionNaturalityUp$ carrier. Every completion-facing consumer comparing the Kuratowski route with the reflection route factors through the finite square
$$
\begin{gathered}
M \longmapsto E,C \longmapsto S,D,A \longmapsto H,T,P,N,\\
M \longmapsto F,R \longmapsto S,D,A \longmapsto H,T,P,N .
\end{gathered}
$$
Consequently the consumer receives only the displayed source metric row, Kuratowski completion row, metric-completion consumer row, completion-reflection witness, real-reflection seal, shared finite window, dyadic ledger, regular readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local name. It exports no quotient completion, host function equality, selected representative, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a completion-facing consumer of the packet. Projection gives the source row $M$, so both compared routes begin from the same displayed endpoint, distance, and Cauchy-window requests.

The Kuratowski side then uses $E$ and $C$. These rows expose the Kuratowski completion route and the metric-completion consumer surface before any terminal real seal is available.

The reflection side uses $F$ and $R$. These rows expose the completion-reflection route and the real-reflection seal for the same source row, not for a separately chosen representative.

Both sides are forced through $S,D,A$. The shared finite stream window, dyadic tolerance ledger, and regular rational readback form the only observation route to the public $\RealUp$ seal.

Transport through $H$, replay through $T$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ preserve exactly that square. Since no excluded coordinate occurs in the carrier, no completion-facing consumer can export quotient completion data, host function equality, a selected representative, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}
