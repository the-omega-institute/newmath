\begin{theorem}[Compact uniform radius-choice NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-uniform-radius-choice-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompactUniformRadiusChoiceUp$ carrier $R=(K,M,Q,F,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CompactUniformRadiusChoiceUp}$ surface consists exactly of compact finite-net admission through $K$, pointwise-modulus admission through $M$, local positive-radius exposure through $Q$, finite ProbeBundle lower-bound folding through $F$, tolerance-only $\UniformModulusUp$ output through $U$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:compact-uniform-radius-choice-carrier}. The rows $K,M,Q,F,U,H,C,P,N$ are the complete displayed inventory.

The compact finite-net and pointwise-modulus rows determine the only local radii that may be inspected. The row $Q$ records those radii as positive BEDC histories attached to bundled probes, not as host choices.

The fold row $F$ then recurses over the displayed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ and returns a positive lower-bound history below every radius named by $Q$. Since $F$ is a finite rational-order fold, the exported radius is classifier-stable without invoking a global minimum operator.

Finally $U,H,C,P,N$ export, transport, replay, source, and name exactly this radius-choice route. The omitted open-cover, sequential, quotient, and choice-selected objects are absent from the carrier, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

$$

For a fixed tolerance history, the compact row supplies only a finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ of center histories. The pointwise row supplies the local radii attached to those centers, and the radius-choice row records them as displayed positive histories.

The lower-bound fold produces the single radius consumed by $U$. Because all preceding rows are tied by $H$ and replayed by $C$, the exported uniform-modulus read depends only on the displayed tolerance and finite packet, not on any external selector.
\end{proof}
