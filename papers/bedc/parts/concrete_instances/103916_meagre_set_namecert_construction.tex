\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MeagreSetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-meagre-set-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Baire and Oxtoby, the $\MeagreSetUp$ packet records the finite BEDC surface for first-category evidence inside a displayed complete-metric Baire space. It sits next to the dense-open packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairecategory-namecert}, reads the prefix windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bairespace-namecert}, and consumes the completeness rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert} through the metric-completion interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}. The packet names displayed nowhere-dense covers by finite windows and regular-rational readbacks; it does not assert a host set-theoretic category theorem, a countable-choice selector, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[MeagreSet carrier]\label{def:meagre-set-carrier}
A $\MeagreSetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(B,Q,K,W,R,E,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\BaireSpaceUp$ prefix packet supplying the finite observation windows in which basic balls, dense-open complements, and tested points are named.
\item $Q$ is the displayed $\CompleteMetricUp/\MetricCompletionUp$ completeness route over the same metric source, including the limit-witness and completion-facing rows needed by Baire-category consumers.
\item $K$ is the finite nowhere-dense cover ledger. Each listed row names a closed-frontier inspection, an empty-interior certificate for a displayed basic ball request, and the complementary dense-open row consumed downstream.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule used to inspect the cover rows and the tested point name.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the radii, shrinkage requests, and comparison tolerances opened by $W$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the displayed regular-rational readback has been opened.
\item $T$ is the $\BaireCategoryUp$ downstream route sending the complementary dense-open rows of $K$ to the finite dense-open intersection packet.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $B,Q,K,W,R,E,T$ and the structural rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay of the prefix windows, completeness route, nowhere-dense cover ledger, readback rows, real seals, and Baire-category route.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MeagreSetUp$, $\BaireSpaceUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BaireCategoryUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MeagreSetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for an arbitrary host subset, a selected point outside a countable union, a quotient completion, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MeagreSet NameCert obligations]\label{thm:meagre-set-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\MeagreSetUp$ carrier $M=(B,Q,K,W,R,E,T,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MeagreSetUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the Baire-space prefix, complete-metric and metric-completion route, nowhere-dense cover ledger, finite stream windows, regular-rational readbacks, real seal, Baire-category downstream route, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item nowhere-dense ledger exposure, requiring each row of $K$ to provide a displayed basic-ball request, an empty-interior certificate for the covered fragment, and the complementary dense-open row read by $T$;
\item window-readback compatibility, requiring $W,R,E$ to open finite point and radius observations before any cover or dense-open decision is exported;
\item Baire-category consumer compatibility, requiring $T$ to consume only the complementary dense-open rows already displayed by $K$ over the same $B,Q,W,R,E$ prefix;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding arbitrary host subsets, selected outside points, countable-choice schedules, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:meagre-set-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the prefix, completeness, cover, window, readback, seal, downstream, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The nowhere-dense ledger is the row $K$. Each entry is opened only as a finite basic-ball inspection together with an empty-interior certificate and a complementary dense-open row; no host subset predicate is exported.

Window-readback compatibility follows from the order $W,R,E$. The finite stream window opens the relevant point and radius observations, the regular-rational row supplies the tolerance readback, and the real seal is reached only after those rows are displayed.

The downstream consumer route is $T$. It reads the complementary dense-open rows already named in $K$ and hands them to the $\BaireCategoryUp$ packet over the same prefix and completeness surface. The support rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same route.

No remaining carrier coordinate names a selected point outside a cover, a countable-choice schedule, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, ambient completeness, or completeness of $\RealUp$. The listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MeagreSet BaireCategory consumer route]\label{thm:meagre-set-bairecategory-consumer-route}
Let $M=(B,Q,K,W,R,E,T,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\MeagreSetUp$ packet. Every $\BaireCategoryUp$ consumer of $M$ factors through the displayed route
$$
B,Q,K,W,R,E \longmapsto T .
$$
The consumer receives the Baire-space prefix, the complete-metric and metric-completion route, the finite nowhere-dense cover ledger, stream windows, regular-rational readbacks, real seals, and the complementary dense-open handoff. It receives no arbitrary subset classifier, selected generic point, quotient completion, countable-choice selector, open-cover compactness theorem, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier inventory. The prefix row $B$ and completeness route $Q$ determine the displayed complete-metric surface on which the cover rows can be interpreted.

Read the cover ledger $K$ through the finite windows $W$. Each inspected cover row is tied to the same point and ball observations, and each radius or shrinkage request is read back through $R$ before reaching the real seal $E$.

The route $T$ then repacks the complementary dense-open rows for the $\BaireCategoryUp$ consumer. This handoff is exactly the finite dense-open schedule required by the neighboring Baire-category packet; it is not a host intersection or a selection operation.

Finally use the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. They preserve the same route under componentwise transport, replay it as displayed continuation data, record its provenance, and name it locally. Since the carrier has no arbitrary subset classifier, selected generic point, quotient completion, choice schedule, open-cover compactness row, or ambient completeness row, no consumer can read one from the packet.
\end{proof}
