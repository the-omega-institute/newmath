The $\HankelVandermondeUp$ packet is a BEDC-native finite certificate surface. It records a finite determinant ledger for displayed Hankel moment rows and their Vandermonde-square readback. It is consumable by $\FoldMomentKernelUp$ and $\CollisionKernelSpectrumUp$ without importing host matrices, analytic spectra, quotient moment spaces, or external field algebra.
The determinant and Vandermonde-square rows form a finite ledger consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fold-moment-kernel-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-collisionkernelspectrum-namecert}: $\FoldMomentKernelUp$ supplies the finite fold-moment rows, while $\CollisionKernelSpectrumUp$ reads the spectral-shadow consumer boundary through that finite ledger, and $\HankelVandermondeUp$ itself exports no analytic spectrum or host matrix carrier.

\begin{definition}[Hankel-Vandermonde carrier]
\label{def:hankel-vandermonde-carrier}
A $\HankelVandermondeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
V=(A,W,M,D,\Delta,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the displayed atom row, listing the finite atom names whose pairwise differences are read by the determinant ledger;
\item $W$ is the weight row, recording the finite coefficient attached to each displayed atom;
\item $M$ is the Hankel moment row, whose entries are displayed finite sums over the atom and weight rows;
\item $D$ is the determinant-expansion ledger row, listing the finite permutation terms used to read the selected Hankel determinant;
\item $\Delta$ is the pairwise-difference row, containing only displayed differences between atoms from $A$;
\item $Q$ is the Vandermonde-square product row, recording the finite product of the displayed pairwise differences after squaring and weighting;
\item $H$ stores the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for atom, weight, moment, determinant-ledger, pairwise-difference, and Vandermonde-square rows;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which determinant and product consumers read the same finite rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance naming $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\FoldMomentKernelUp$, $\CollisionKernelSpectrumUp$, finite determinant ledgers, and local naming evidence;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HankelVandermondeUp}$ evidence row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two carriers only by the displayed rows $A,W,M,D,\Delta,Q,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host matrices, analytic spectra, quotient moment spaces, imported external theorems, or external field algebra.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Hankel-Vandermonde NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hankel-vandermonde-namecert-obligations}
Every accepted $\HankelVandermondeUp$ packet with carrier \autoref{def:hankel-vandermonde-carrier} carries five local naming obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation: the tuple $V=(A,W,M,D,\Delta,Q,H,C,P,N)$ is admitted as one finite $\BHist$ packet;
\item classifier transport: componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport preserves the displayed atom, weight, moment, determinant-ledger, pairwise-difference, Vandermonde-square, package, and naming rows;
\item determinant-ledger exactness: every determinant read passes through the finite permutation ledger $D$ and the displayed Hankel moment row $M$;
\item Vandermonde-square readback: every product read passes through the finite pairwise-difference row $\Delta$ and the Vandermonde-square row $Q$;
\item non-escape: accepted carriers export no host matrix object, analytic spectrum, quotient moment space, imported external theorem, or external field algebra.
\end{enumerate}
These five clauses are the complete local $\NameCert_{\HankelVandermondeUp}$ surface for finite Hankel-Vandermonde determinant ledgers.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HankelVandermondeUp.HankelVandermondeCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is the displayed tuple $V=(A,W,M,D,\Delta,Q,H,C,P,N)$. Each coordinate is a finite $\BHist$ row or a finite route between such rows, so the packet is admitted without a host matrix carrier.

For classifier transport, move each visible coordinate through its stored $\hsame$ row and read it through the corresponding $\Cont$ route. Because the classifier is componentwise, transport cannot create a new atom, weight, moment, determinant term, pairwise difference, product factor, package row, or naming row.

Determinant-ledger exactness is finite ledger inspection. The selected Hankel determinant is read only by expanding along the displayed permutation terms in $D$, and each term cites entries already present in the moment row $M$.

The Vandermonde-square readback then projects $\Delta$ and $Q$. Pairwise differences are available only between atoms listed in $A$, and the squared product row records only the factors displayed by $\Delta$ together with the weights from $W$.

Non-escape follows from the carrier definition. Since no coordinate names a host matrix space, analytic spectral object, quotient moment space, external theorem, or external field algebra, downstream $\FoldMomentKernelUp$ and $\CollisionKernelSpectrumUp$ consumers can cite only the finite rows and routes in the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Hankel-Vandermonde spectral-shadow bridge]
\label{thm:hankel-vandermonde-spectral-shadow-bridge}
Let
$$
V_H=(A,W,M,D,\Delta,Q,H_H,C_H,P_H,N_H)
$$
be an accepted $\HankelVandermondeUp$ carrier. Let
$$
K_{\Omega}=(W_{\Omega},F_{\Omega},L_{\Omega},I_{\Omega},Q_{\Omega},T_{\Omega},C_{\Omega},P_{\Omega},N_{\Omega})
$$
be an accepted $\FoldMomentKernelUp$ finite-window carrier, and let
$$
C_S=(G,F,V,I,K,S,H_S,T_S,P_S,N_S)
$$
be an accepted $\CollisionKernelSpectrumUp$ carrier. Suppose the shared $\FoldMomentKernelUp$ moment surface identifies the collision moment-index row $I$ with the retained row $I_{\Omega}$, and suppose every displayed spectral-shadow request in $S$ is read at the same finite moment address of the Hankel row $M$. Suppose also that, at those addresses, the displayed atom row $A$, weight row $W$, determinant ledger $D$, pairwise-difference row $\Delta$, and Vandermonde-square row $Q$ are the rows recorded by the $\CollisionKernelSpectrumUp$ provenance through $P_S$ and by the $\FoldMomentKernelUp$ provenance through $P_{\Omega}$.

Then every downstream consumer route that reads the spectral-shadow row $S$ factors through the finite $\HankelVandermondeUp$ determinant surface
$$
A,W,M,D,\Delta,Q,H_H,C_H,P_H,N_H.
$$
More explicitly, each such read first reaches $S$ as a finite row-sum, diagonal weight, or selected moment shadow of the displayed kernel row $K$, then passes through the common finite moment-index row $I=I_{\Omega}$, then reads the Hankel moment row $M$, and finally reaches the determinant ledger $D$ and the Vandermonde-square readback rows $\Delta,Q$. No such consumer route exports a host matrix, analytic spectrum, quotient moment space, selected eigenbasis, external field algebra, or ambient linear-algebra object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted $\CollisionKernelSpectrumUp$ carrier through \autoref{def:collision-kernel-spectrum-carrier}. By \autoref{thm:collision-kernel-spectrum-finite-window-spectral-shadow}, the row $S$ is not an independent analytic spectrum: each of its displayed entries is a row-sum, diagonal weight, or selected moment shadow of the finite kernel entries in $K$. By \autoref{thm:collision-kernel-spectrum-moment-index-projection}, any exported spectral-shadow read must pass through the finite moment-index row $I$ together with the visible rows $G,F,V,K,H_S,T_S,P_S,N_S$.

The hypothesis identifies that moment-index row with the retained $\FoldMomentKernelUp$ row $I_{\Omega}$. Apply \autoref{thm:fold-moment-kernel-standard-bridge-surface} to the accepted finite-window carrier $K_{\Omega}$. A moment read over this surface can inspect only the displayed window, fold-source, fiber-ledger, moment-index, collision-count, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows. If the route is restricted to the retained window, \autoref{thm:fold-moment-kernel-finite-window-restriction-carrier} keeps the same conclusion inside the packet $K_{\Omega}$. Thus the spectral-shadow consumer cannot leave the finite moment surface before it reaches the shared Hankel moment address.

At that shared address, the second hypothesis names the Hankel-Vandermonde rows $A,W,M,D,\Delta,Q$ as the rows carried by the collision-spectrum provenance and by the fold-moment provenance. Now apply \autoref{thm:hankel-vandermonde-namecert-obligations}. Determinant-ledger exactness forces every determinant read of $M$ through the finite permutation ledger $D$, and Vandermonde-square readback forces the product read through the displayed pairwise-difference row $\Delta$ and the Vandermonde-square row $Q$, with componentwise transport and continuation stored in $H_H$ and $C_H$.

Combining the three projections gives the announced factorization. A read of $S$ factors through $K$, then through the common finite moment-index row $I=I_{\Omega}$, then through the Hankel moment row $M$, and finally through $D,\Delta,Q$ inside the carrier $V_H$. The non-escape clauses of \autoref{thm:collision-kernel-spectrum-ledger-non-escape}, \autoref{thm:fold-moment-kernel-standard-bridge-surface}, and \autoref{thm:hankel-vandermonde-namecert-obligations} exclude exactly the remaining host matrix, analytic spectrum, quotient moment, selected eigenbasis, external field-algebra, and ambient linear-algebra exports.
\end{proof}
