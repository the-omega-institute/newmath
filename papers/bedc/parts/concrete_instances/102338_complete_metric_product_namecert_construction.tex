Following Bishop--Bridges constructive completion and the Bourbaki--Kelley
binary product metric construction, the $\CompleteMetricProductUp$ packet
records the standard product-stability route for complete metric spaces as
finite BEDC NameCert data. It reads the displayed product metric of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-productmetric-namecert}, the two component
complete-metric limit witnesses of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the finite
stream-name windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert},
the regular rational readback of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real seal of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the binary product carrier
of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prod-namecert}. The packet names the
constructive route from a product Cauchy source to component limits and then
back to a displayed product limit, without quotient products, selected
limits, countable choice, host equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Complete metric product carrier]
\label{def:complete-metric-product-carrier}
A $\CompleteMetricProductUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(M,X,Y,S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y,A_X,A_Y,L_X,L_Y,\Pi,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\ProductMetricUp$ row on the binary product carrier. It
stores the two projection routes, the two component distance reads, and the
product-distance ledger.
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the first and second $\CompleteMetricUp$ source rows.
Each source is read only as a displayed component limit-witness surface.
\item $S_X$ and $S_Y$ are the component $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window rows
opened by projecting a product Cauchy source through the two projection routes
of $M$.
\item $R_X$ and $R_Y$ are the component $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows obtained
from those windows and from the product Cauchy tolerance ledger.
\item $A_X$ and $A_Y$ are the terminal component $\RealUp$ seal rows reached
after the corresponding $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows have been
displayed.
\item $L_X$ and $L_Y$ are the two component limit rows supplied by the
displayed $\CompleteMetricUp$ witnesses $X$ and $Y$.
\item $\Pi$ is the $\ProdUp$ pairing row that repacks the two component limit
rows as the displayed product endpoint.
\item $L$ is the product-limit route ledger from the product Cauchy source
through $M$, then through $S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y,A_X,A_Y,L_X,L_Y$, and finally
through $\Pi$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the product metric,
the two complete-metric sources, the component stream windows, regular
readbacks, real seals, component limits, product endpoint, route ledger,
replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completeness request through
$$
M,X,Y \longmapsto S_X,S_Y \longmapsto R_X,R_Y
\longmapsto A_X,A_Y \longmapsto L_X,L_Y,\Pi,L,H,P,N .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompleteMetricProductUp$,
$\ProductMetricUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\RealUp$, $\ProdUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompleteMetricProductUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through
$M,X,Y,S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y,A_X,A_Y,L_X,L_Y,\Pi,L,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no
coordinate for quotient products, selected limits, countable-choice
selectors, host pair equality, quotient equality of Cauchy sources, or
ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Complete metric product NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:complete-metric-product-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CompleteMetricProductUp$ carrier
$$
Q=(M,X,Y,S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y,A_X,A_Y,L_X,L_Y,\Pi,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CompleteMetricProductUp}$ surface consists exactly of
six finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure, requiring the displayed packet
$M,X,Y,S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y,A_X,A_Y,L_X,L_Y,\Pi,L,H,C,P,N$ to be the whole public
carrier;
\item product-metric exactness, requiring every product distance and
projection read to pass through the displayed $\ProductMetricUp$ row $M$;
\item component Cauchy readback, requiring every projected Cauchy source to
open the matching $\StreamNameUp$ window and then the matching $\RegSeqRatUp$
regular rational readback before a component limit witness is visible;
\item component limit admission, requiring $L_X$ and $L_Y$ to be obtained
from the two displayed $\CompleteMetricUp$ rows $X$ and $Y$ after the terminal
$\RealUp$ seals $A_X$ and $A_Y$ have been displayed;
\item product endpoint pairing, requiring the product limit to be the
$\ProdUp$ pairing row $\Pi$ applied to the two component limit rows and
recorded by the route ledger $L$;
\item non-escape provenance, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay,
source, and name only the displayed rows and never a quotient product,
selected limit, countable-choice schedule, host equality, quotient equality,
or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
No CompleteMetricProduct obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is tuple inspection of
\autoref{def:complete-metric-product-carrier}. The product-facing metric
route is fixed by $M$, so every product distance and projection read starts
from the displayed $\ProductMetricUp$ packet.

The component Cauchy readback obligations follow by projecting the product
Cauchy source along the two projection routes stored in $M$. Each projected
source opens its own finite $\StreamNameUp$ window and then its
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback before any terminal real seal is available.

The component limit obligations are the two applications of the displayed
$\CompleteMetricUp$ rows $X$ and $Y$. They consume the corresponding
stream-name, regular readback, and real-seal rows and return the component
limit rows $L_X$ and $L_Y$.

The product endpoint obligation is then finite pairing. The row $\Pi$ pairs
$L_X$ and $L_Y$, while $L$ records the route from the original product Cauchy
source through the two component reads and back to that product endpoint.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the rows already
displayed. Because none of the carrier coordinates stores quotient products,
selected limits, countable choice, host equality, quotient equality, or
ambient completeness, the local NameCert cannot export such data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Complete metric product limit pairing]
\label{thm:complete-metric-product-limit-pairing}
Let
$$
Q=(M,X,Y,S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y,A_X,A_Y,L_X,L_Y,\Pi,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CompleteMetricProductUp$ carrier. Every componentwise Cauchy
source completed in the two factors determines the product-limit route through
the displayed $\ProductMetricUp$ row $M$: project the product source through
$M$, read the two component $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ routes, consume
the two $\CompleteMetricUp$ limit witnesses $X$ and $Y$, and pair the resulting
limit rows through $\Pi$.

The resulting product-limit certificate is exactly the finite route ledger
$L$. It introduces no quotient product, selected limit, countable-choice
selector, host equality, quotient equality of Cauchy sources, or ambient
$\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Start with a product Cauchy source visible to the packet. The only product
metric coordinate is $M$, so both component observations are obtained by the
projection routes and distance ledger stored in that $\ProductMetricUp$ row.

The first projection opens $S_X$, then $R_X$, and reaches the terminal
$\RealUp$ seal $A_X$. The second projection opens $S_Y$, then $R_Y$, and
reaches $A_Y$. Thus both component Cauchy sources are read through finite
StreamName and RegSeqRat data before a completion witness is consulted.

Apply the two displayed complete-metric witnesses. The first witness $X$
returns $L_X$ for the first component route, and the second witness $Y$
returns $L_Y$ for the second component route. These are component limit rows,
not selected host limits.

Pair the two component limits through $\Pi$. The ledger $L$ records exactly
this route from the original product Cauchy source to the paired endpoint, so
the product-limit certificate is componentwise before it is product-facing.

The exclusions follow from the carrier boundary. Since $Q$ contains no
quotient product, no chosen limit selector, no countable-choice schedule, no
host equality row, no quotient equality row, and no ambient completeness
coordinate, none can be recovered from the route ledger.
\end{proof}
