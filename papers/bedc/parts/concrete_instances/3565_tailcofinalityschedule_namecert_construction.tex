The $\TailCofinalityScheduleUp$ packet records the finite shared-threshold schedule used when a diagonal $\RealUp$ consumer must pass from dyadic tolerances and stream windows to $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows and a $\RealUp$ seal. It names the schedule as an inspectable $\BHist$ surface, not as a host limit selector, quotient-stream equality, selected real, or ambient completeness principle.

The schedule is the consumer-facing route from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} through the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ rows, so its carrier records the finite L10 handoff rather than an isolated tail oracle.

\begin{definition}[Tail cofinality schedule carrier]
\label{def:tail-cofinality-schedule-carrier}
A $\TailCofinalityScheduleUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(E,W,D,R,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $E$ is the unary precision request row naming the tolerance level demanded by the diagonal consumer;
\item $W$ is the cofinal finite-window schedule selected for that request over the relevant $\StreamNameUp$ rows;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger proving that the windows in $W$ meet the requested dyadic bound;
\item $R$ is the family of $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows made available after the window and dyadic ledgers have been consumed;
\item $L$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row through which the diagonal consumer receives the scheduled regular-rational handoff;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $E,W,D,R,L$ and the structural rows of the packet;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $E$ to $W$, from $W$ to $D$, from $D$ to $R$, and from $R$ to $L$;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TailCofinalityScheduleUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a host-selected limit, ambient completeness, a hidden tail oracle, or an unbounded schedule.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Tail cofinality schedule NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:tail-cofinality-schedule-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\TailCofinalityScheduleUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:tail-cofinality-schedule-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission for $T=(E,W,D,R,L,H,C,P,N)$, unary precision exposure through $E$, cofinal finite-window coverage through $W$, threshold sufficiency through the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff through $R$, $\RealUp$ seal boundary through $L$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ route replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, local naming through $N$, and non-escape from quotient streams, selected host limits, ambient completeness, hidden tail oracles, and unbounded schedules.

\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet. The rows $E,W,D,R,L$ expose the precision request, finite cofinal schedule, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular-rational readback, and real-facing seal.

The schedule and threshold obligations are read in order. The route from $E$ to $W$ selects the finite cofinal windows, and the route from $W$ to $D$ records the dyadic sufficiency ledger for those same windows.

The handoff and seal obligations then consume $R$ and $L$. Componentwise transport, route replay, provenance, and naming are exactly the rows $H,C,P,N$. Since no further analytic coordinate occurs in $T$, the listed rows exhaust the certificate surface.
\end{proof}

The rows $H,C,P,N$ replay and name the same route under transport and provenance.
