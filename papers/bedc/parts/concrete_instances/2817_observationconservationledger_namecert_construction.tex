\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ObservationConservationLedgerUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-observation-conservation-ledger-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ObservationConservationLedgerUp$ interface names the finite monotone ledger packet that realises closed observation inside the BEDC kernel vocabulary. It binds observation states to displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ records, uses $\hsame$ to transport old records into later states, routes every allowed read through $\Cont$, records provenance through $\Pkg$, and marks every non-recorded universal demand as a $\Gap$ row. The packet is a concrete NameCert carrier for the conservation side of closed observation; it is not an induction principle, not a universal quantifier over all future observations, and not an external oracle for unseen records.

The ledger consumes the observer-history comparison surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistoryidentity-namecert}: old records are preserved only by transporting the displayed earlier record rows into the later observation state. That preservation row is the dependency edge read by the inscription-event packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert}, where an accepted inscription can cite the finite record that already survived the ledger transition rather than a host memory store or an unrecorded event.

\begin{definition}[ObservationConservationLedger carrier]
\label{def:observation-conservation-ledger-carrier}
An $\ObservationConservationLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
L=(O_t,O_{t+1},R_t,R_{t+1},A,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O_t$ and $O_{t+1}$ are the earlier and later observation-state rows;
\item $R_t$ is the finite ledger of records already visible at $O_t$;
\item $R_{t+1}$ is the finite ledger visible at $O_{t+1}$;
\item $A$ is the admission row saying which finite records are appended between the two states;
\item $G$ is the $\Gap$ row assigned to any universal or future-facing assertion not witnessed by $R_t$, $R_{t+1}$, or $A$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the earlier observation state, later observation state, old records, appended records, and $\Gap$ rows;
\item $C$ records the visible $\Cont$ routes by which ledger consumers read old records, appended records, transport rows, and non-escape rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row for the observation-state, record-ledger, admission, $\Gap$, $\hsame$, and $\Cont$ coordinates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObservationConservationLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the two observation-state rows, the two finite record ledgers, the displayed admission row, the $\Gap$ row, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows. It contains no route for deleting old records, rewriting old records, manufacturing a universal record, or reading a witness absent from the displayed finite ledgers.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ObservationConservationLedger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observation-conservation-ledger-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ObservationConservationLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation by the finite packet of \autoref{def:observation-conservation-ledger-carrier};
\item old-record preservation from $R_t$ into $R_{t+1}$ through the displayed admission row $A$;
\item appended-record visibility for every record admitted by $A$;
\item componentwise $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ stability for the observation-state rows, record-ledger rows, admission rows, and $\Gap$ rows;
\item provenance and naming closure through $P$ and $N$, forcing every consumer read to remain inside the displayed finite packet.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation exports a universal quantifier over future observations, an induction principle, a deletion route for old records, or an oracle for unseen witnesses.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ObservationConservationLedgerUp.ObservationConservationLedgerCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is exactly \autoref{def:observation-conservation-ledger-carrier}. The two observation states, both record ledgers, the admission row, the $\Gap$ row, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows are all displayed coordinates of one finite packet.

Old-record preservation is read by comparing $R_t$ with $R_{t+1}$ through $A$. The admission row can append finite records, and the transport row $H$ moves the earlier ledger componentwise into the later packet without changing its visible record identities.

Appended-record visibility is supplied by the same admission row. Any newly admitted record must appear in $R_{t+1}$ and must be routed through $C$, $P$, and $N$ before a consumer can read it.

The remaining demands are non-escape demands. If a consumer asks for a universal or future-facing assertion not witnessed by the displayed rows, the request is assigned to $G$ rather than accepted as a record, so the local NameCert surface contains exactly the listed finite obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ObservationConservationLedger non-escape]
\label{thm:observation-conservation-ledger-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\ObservationConservationLedgerUp$ carrier reads only the displayed rows $O_t,O_{t+1},R_t,R_{t+1},A,G,H,C,P,N$. A consumer may recover preserved old records, newly appended finite records, componentwise transports, continuation routes, provenance, and the local naming row. It cannot recover an unrecorded witness, a deletion or alteration of an old record, a universal assertion over all future observations, or an induction principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ObservationConservationLedgerUp.ObservationConservationLedgerCarrier\_gap\_read\_nonescape}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier of \autoref{def:observation-conservation-ledger-carrier}. Its only record coordinates are the finite ledgers $R_t$ and $R_{t+1}$ together with the finite admission row $A$.

Apply the NameCert obligation surface of \autoref{thm:observation-conservation-ledger-namecert-obligations}. Old records persist through $H$, appended records become visible through $A$, and every legal consumer read is routed through $C$, $P$, and $N$.

It remains to rule out non-displayed reads. A request for a universal or future-facing assertion has no witness coordinate in $R_t$, $R_{t+1}$, or $A$, so it is routed to the $\Gap$ row $G$. Since $G$ is a tag rather than an accepted record, the carrier has no route from closed observation to an unrecorded witness, record deletion, universal closure, or induction.
\end{proof}
