\begin{theorem}[Rat history semantic name certificate]
\label{thm:rat-history-semantic-name-certificate}
The Rat history carrier with its history classifier supplies the semantic name
certificate
$$
\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}(\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier},
\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier},\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier},
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier is inhabited by the one-extension of the empty denominator tail.
This is the positive unary denominator witness used by the checked core.

For the core certificate relation, reflexivity is the self-classifier supplied
by carrier membership and $\hsame$ reflexivity. Symmetry reverses the
$\hsame$ component of a classifier witness. Transitivity composes the two
$\hsame$ components and uses carrier transport along $\hsame$ to recover the
right endpoint carrier. The same carrier transport gives respect of the
carrier under a classifier edge.

The semantic source, pattern, and ledger rows are all
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$. Hence the source-to-pattern and source-to-ledger
clauses are identity rows: each source witness is reused unchanged as the
pattern witness and as the ledger witness. These are exactly the rows assembled
by the Lean theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rat ledger two-sided endpoint classifier equivalence]
\label{thm:rat-ledger-two-sided-endpoint-classifier-equivalence}
For histories $\rho,v,w:\mathsf{BHist}$, if
$\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}(\rho,v)$ holds, then
$$
(\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(\rho,w)\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w))
\land
(\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,\rho)\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,v))
\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(\rho)
\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(v)
\land
(\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w)\to
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(w))
\land
(\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,v)\to
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(w)).
$$

Its ten-field fit is: traditional target two-sided $\RatUp$ ledger endpoint
classifier exactness with conditional endpoint readback; BEDC source $\RatUp$
ledger over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$;
classifier $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$; stability from ledger endpoint
equivalence, classifier symmetry, and classifier positive-denominator
readback; ledger $\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}$; core theorem this block;
exactness the two iff rows plus conditional $w$-readback; downstream consumer
the $\RatUp$ semantic certificate boundary and $\RealUp$ rational observation
streams; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:rat-history-ledger-classifier-endpoint-equivalence} to the
ledger witness. This gives the classifier endpoint equivalence from $\rho$ and
from $v$ toward every target history $w$.

For the reversed-argument equivalence, use
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-symmetry} on both sides. A classifier
witness from $w$ to $\rho$ becomes one from $\rho$ to $w$, crosses the endpoint
equivalence just obtained, and is then reversed to a classifier witness from
$w$ to $v$. The converse direction is the same argument with $v$ and $\rho$
exchanged.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:rat-ledger-raw-visible-classifier} to the ledger
witness, obtaining $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(\rho,v)$. Applying
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-positive-denominators} to that classifier
supplies the positive unary denominator witnesses for $\rho$ and $v$.

For the first conditional $w$-readback, apply
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-positive-denominators} to a witness of
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w)$ and project the right endpoint. This gives
$\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(w)$ under the visible-target classifier
hypothesis.

For the second conditional $w$-readback, apply the same theorem to a witness of
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,v)$ and project the left endpoint. This gives
$\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(w)$ under the reversed visible-endpoint
classifier hypothesis.

The two equivalences, the positive $\rho$ and $v$ endpoint witnesses, and the
two implication rows assemble the displayed conjunction.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:rat-ledger-bidirectional-target-positive-endpoint-package}

$\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}(\rho,v)$ holds, then
$$
(\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(\rho,w)\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w))
\land
(\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,\rho)\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,v))
\land
(\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w)\Rightarrow
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(\rho)\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(v)\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(w))
\land
(\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,v)\Rightarrow
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(\rho)\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(v)\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(w)).
$$

Its ten-field fit is: traditional target rational ledger bidirectional endpoint
package; BEDC source $\RatUp$ ledger over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$; classifier $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$;
stability from ledger classifier extension plus classifier symmetry and
transitivity; ledger the raw/visible denominator edge; core theorem this block;
exactness the two displayed fibers plus positivity; downstream consumer
$\RealUp$ stream-prefix and the $\RatUp$ semantic certificate boundary; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:rat-history-ledger-classifier-endpoint-equivalence} to the
ledger witness. This gives the forward-orientation classifier fiber between
$\rho$ and $v$ at the common target $w$.

For the reversed-argument fiber, apply
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-symmetry} before and after the forward
fiber. A witness $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,\rho)$ becomes a witness from
$\rho$ to $w$, crosses the endpoint equivalence, and reverses to a witness from
$w$ to $v$; the converse direction follows by the same symmetric transport.

It remains to package endpoint positivity. Assume
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w)$. Apply
\autoref{thm:rat-ledger-classifier-extension} to the ledger witness and this
visible-target classifier, obtaining
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(\rho,w)$.

Apply \autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-positive-denominators} to the lifted
raw-target classifier for the $\rho$ and $w$ witnesses, and apply the same
positive-denominator theorem to the original visible-target classifier for the
$v$ and $w$ witnesses. These assemble the first implication.

For $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,v)$, first reverse it by
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-symmetry}. The resulting
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w)$ case is exactly the positivity package just
constructed, so the second implication follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rat ledger classifier saturation unary package]
\label{thm:rat-ledger-classifier-saturation-unary-package}
For histories $\rho,v,w:\mathsf{BHist}$, assume
$\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}(\rho,v)$ and at least one of
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(\rho,w)$,
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w)$,
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,\rho)$, or
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,v)$. Then
$$
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(\rho,w)
\land
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w)
\land
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,\rho)
\land
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,v)
\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(\rho)
\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(v)
\land
\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(w)
\land
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(\rho)
\land
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(v)
\land
\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(w).
$$

Its ten-field fit is: traditional target ledger-saturated rational classifier
endpoint package; BEDC source $\RatUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$; classifier $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$;
stability from classifier symmetry/transitivity and ledger endpoint
equivalence; ledger $\mathsf{RatHistoryLedgerPolicy}$; core theorem this block;
exactness the four-orientation saturated package plus unary endpoint readback;
downstream consumer the $\RatUp$ semantic certificate boundary and
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ denominator-unit rows; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:rat-ledger-two-sided-endpoint-classifier-equivalence} to the
ledger witness. This gives the forward endpoint equivalence
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(\rho,w)\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w)$, the reversed endpoint equivalence
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,\rho)\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,v)$.

For the ledger endpoint denominators, apply
\autoref{thm:rat-ledger-raw-visible-classifier} to the ledger witness and then
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-positive-denominators} to the obtained
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(\rho,v)$. This supplies positive unary
denominator witnesses for $\rho$ and $v$.

It remains to turn the supplied orientation into a single classifier involving
$w$ and one ledger endpoint. If the supplied classifier is
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(\rho,w)$, transport it across the forward
endpoint equivalence to obtain $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w)$, and apply
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-symmetry} to obtain both reversed
orientations.

If the supplied classifier is $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(v,w)$, transport
it back across the same endpoint equivalence to obtain
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(\rho,w)$, and again apply
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-symmetry} to obtain the two reversed
orientations.

If the supplied classifier is $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,\rho)$,
transport it across the reversed endpoint equivalence to obtain
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,v)$, and apply
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-symmetry} to obtain the two forward
orientations.

If the supplied classifier is $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,v)$, transport
it back across the reversed endpoint equivalence to obtain
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(w,\rho)$, and apply
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-symmetry} to obtain the two forward
orientations.

In every case one of the assembled classifiers has $w$ as an endpoint. Apply
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-positive-denominators} to that classifier
to obtain $\mathsf{PositiveUnaryDenominator}(w)$, keeping the ledger endpoint
witnesses for $\rho$ and $v$. Apply
\autoref{thm:positive-unary-denominator-unary-and-nonempty} to each of the
three positive denominator witnesses and project the unary row. The four
classifiers, three positive unary denominator witnesses, and three unary
endpoint rows assemble the displayed package.
\end{proof}
