\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BishopCompleteRealUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-complete-real-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\BishopCompleteRealUp$ packet records the textbook complete-real interface as a finite BEDC boundary rather than as an ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$. It sits on the dyadic endpoint ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite observation schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the separated completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedcompletion-namecert}, and the completion reflection boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionreflection-namecert}. The packet exposes the L10 completion interface currently needed by Real and Completion consumers; it does not assert that $\RealUp$ is complete, select limits, quotient streams, or import countable choice.

\begin{definition}[Bishop complete real carrier]
\label{def:bishop-complete-real-carrier}
A $\BishopCompleteRealUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(D,W,R,E,S,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint and tolerance ledger used to measure regular Cauchy requests.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite schedule row opened by the same tolerance request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational readback row attached to $D$ and $W$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the dyadic, schedule, and regular readback rows have been displayed.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedCompletionUp$ comparison row, recording that two completion-facing reads are compared through separated finite evidence.
\item $F$ is the $\CompletionReflectionUp$ handoff row, replaying the same regular-rational source surface into the completion-facing interface.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $D,W,R,E,S,F,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a completion-facing request through the same dyadic, schedule, regular, real-seal, separated, and reflection rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopCompleteRealUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\SeparatedCompletionUp$, $\CompletionReflectionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCompleteRealUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host real equality, quotient stream equality, a selected limit, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or a theorem that $\RealUp$ is complete.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop complete real NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-complete-real-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopCompleteRealUp$ carrier
$$
B=(D,W,R,E,S,F,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCompleteRealUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the dyadic endpoint ledger, finite schedule, regular rational readback, terminal Real seal, separated completion comparison, completion reflection handoff, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows, together with the obligation that every completion-facing read passes through $D,W,R,E,S,F,H,C,P,N$ in that order.

No obligation in the packet is discharged by host real equality, quotient streams, selected representatives, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or a completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bishop-complete-real-carrier}. The tuple lists the dyadic, schedule, regular readback, Real seal, separated comparison, completion reflection, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The completion-facing order is fixed by the visible rows. A request first reads a dyadic endpoint and tolerance ledger in $D$, then opens the finite schedule row $W$, and then passes through the regular rational readback row $R$ before the terminal Real seal $E$ is available.

The separated and reflection rows then consume that same source surface. The comparison row $S$ records separated evidence, while $F$ replays the regular-rational source into the completion-facing boundary.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name only these displayed rows. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for the excluded host, quotient, choice, compactness, or completeness data, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop complete real L10 handoff]
\label{thm:bishop-complete-real-l10-handoff}
Every Real and Completion consumer of an accepted $\BishopCompleteRealUp$ packet factors through the finite L10 route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \RealUp \longrightarrow \SeparatedCompletionUp \longrightarrow \CompletionReflectionUp .
$$
The handoff exports a displayed regular-rational completion interface with separated comparison and reflection rows. It does not export completed $\RealUp$ equality, quotient Cauchy classes, selected host limits, countable-choice coordinates, open-cover compactness, or an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:bishop-complete-real-namecert-obligations}. They force every accepted read to pass through the dyadic ledger, finite schedule, regular rational readback, and terminal Real seal before any completion-facing row is consumed.

Next read the separated comparison row. The row $S$ compares completion-facing observations only through displayed separated evidence, so it cannot introduce quotient classes or host equality.

The completion reflection row $F$ then replays the same source surface into the completion-facing interface. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve that route componentwise and add only transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Thus the consumer receives exactly the displayed L10 handoff from dyadic data to schedules, regular rational readback, Real seal, separated comparison, and completion reflection. None of the excluded completeness, choice, quotient, compactness, or selected-limit data has an entry point in the packet.
\end{proof}
