\begin{theorem}[Modulus of uniform continuity NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:modulus-of-uniform-continuity-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ModulusOfUniformContinuityUp$ carrier $M=(X,Y,F,D,S,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ModulusOfUniformContinuityUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:

\item carrier admission for the source $\MetricSpaceUp$ row, target $\MetricSpaceUp$ row, $\ContinuousMapUp$ row, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance request, modulus schedule, $\RealUp$ seal boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item tolerance-source exactness, requiring every requested target tolerance to be read from the displayed dyadic ledger $D$ before the schedule row $S$ is inspected;
\item source-radius schedule exactness, requiring $S$ to return only a source-radius row over $X$ and to reuse that row for each accepted source-pair read of the same tolerance;
\item continuous-map handoff, requiring the source-radius evidence to pass through the pointwise $\ContinuousMapUp$ route $F$ before the target $\MetricSpaceUp$ row $Y$ and the Real seal row $E$ are exposed;
\item compact-metric compatibility, requiring any downstream $\CompactMetricUp$ consumer to read finite-net or compact-metric data only as an external consumer of the displayed schedule, not as a hidden source of $S$;
\item transport and replay stability, requiring componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$ to preserve the ordered route $D,S,X,F,Y,E$;

No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. Its analytic rows are the source metric $X$, target metric $Y$, continuous-map route $F$, dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, modulus schedule $S$, and Real seal boundary $E$; the remaining rows transport, replay, source, and name that same packet.

Read the tolerance route in order. A target-radius request first enters the dyadic ledger $D$, then the modulus schedule $S$, and only then the source metric row $X$ where the displayed source radius is used.

The map and target estimates are downstream of that schedule. The continuous-map row $F$ consumes the source-radius evidence before the target metric row $Y$ and the terminal $\RealUp$ boundary $E$ can be inspected.

Compact-metric data is therefore only a consumer-side row. A $\CompactMetricUp$ finite-net argument may cite the displayed schedule, but it cannot supply an unrecorded open cover, selected subcover, or choice principle inside the $\ModulusOfUniformContinuityUp$ carrier.

Finally, componentwise transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$ preserve the same ordered rows. Since the excluded quotient, selection, trichotomy, and completeness objects are absent from the carrier inventory, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
