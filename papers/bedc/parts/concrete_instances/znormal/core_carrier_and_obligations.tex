\begin{definition}[$\ZNormalUp$ carrier]
\label{def:znormal-carrier}
A $\ZNormalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the typed reduction normal-form row read through $\TypedReductionNormalFormUp$;
\item $F$ is the finite fuel row naming the bounded host reduction segment;
\item $V$ is the terminal-normality row asserting that the segment ends at a normal form;
\item $K$ is the Zeckendorf and carry-facing normal-form comparison row read through $\ZeckendorfNormalUp$ and $\ZCarryUp$ where a finite word boundary is present;
\item $R$ is the continuation terminal-read row read through $\ContinuationTraceNormalFormUp$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for typed endpoint, fuel, terminal normality, finite word comparison, continuation read, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from typed endpoint to fuel replay, from fuel replay to terminal normality, from terminal normality to the continuation read, and from those rows to the consumer-facing packet boundary;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:visions-strong-normalization-vs-turing-completeness}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedreductionnormalform-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeckendorf-normal-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtracenormalform-namecert}, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts only packets whose normal-form claim is read through a finite terminal row. It has no coordinate for a divergence oracle, host conversion row, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluator, or ambient proof that every external computation halts.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:znormal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
typed endpoint readback through $T$, finite fuel exposure through $F$, terminal-normality exposure through $V$, finite normal-word comparison through $K$, continuation terminal-read exposure through $R$, componentwise transport through $H$, displayed route replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The rows $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$ are the only fields of an accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet.

Read the endpoint rows first. The typed reduction row $T$ is supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedreductionnormalform-namecert}; the finite fuel row $F$ and terminal-normality row $V$ bind that endpoint to a displayed total-host reduction segment.

Read the sibling normal-form rows next. The row $K$ may cite the finite word discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeckendorf-normal-namecert}, while $R$ may cite the terminal trace surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtracenormalform-namecert}. These rows are auxiliary certificate coordinates, not routes to host conversion or general normalization.

Finally inspect $H,C,P,N$. They transport, replay, package, and name the same finite surface. Since no carrier coordinate names divergence, quotient computation, host conversion, or an unrestricted evaluator, the listed obligations exhaust the packet.
\end{proof}
