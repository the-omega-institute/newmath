\begin{definition}[$\ZNormalUp$ root display packet]\label{def:znormal-root-display-packet}
The root display packet of an accepted $\ZNormalUp$ certificate is the finite tuple
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N).
$$
Here $T$ is the typed endpoint row, $F$ is the finite fuel row, $V$ is the terminal-normality row, $K$ is the Zeckendorf and carry-facing normal-word row, $R$ is the terminal continuation-trace row, $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row, $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay row, $P$ is the local $\Pkg$ provenance row, and $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ naming row. The packet is read as one finite $\BHist$ surface: every admitted root consumer must enter through these rows and their displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ root total-host bridge components]\label{thm:znormal-root-total-host-bridge-components}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ root display packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
each total-host read factors through the typed endpoint row $T$, the Zeckendorf and carry-facing normal row $K$, the terminal continuation-trace row $R$, and the local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ rows $P,N$. The read may use the finite fuel, terminal-normality, transport, and replay rows $F,V,H,C$, but it cannot bypass the displayed bridge components by a host conversion row, divergence oracle, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluator, or global normalizer.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalRootTotalHostBridgeComponents}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalPacket\_root\_total\_host\_bridge\_components}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{def:znormal-root-display-packet}. It exposes the accepted root surface as the finite tuple $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$, so there is no second total-host coordinate outside the displayed $\BHist$ packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-root-bhist-source} and \autoref{thm:znormal-root-classifier-stability}. The first pins root reads to the single $\BHist$ source, and the second keeps the Zeckendorf and carry-facing normal row $K$ and the terminal continuation-trace row $R$ under the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows $H,C$.

The sibling chapters then supply only component surfaces. The typed endpoint route is the one named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedreductionnormalform-namecert}; the finite normal-word route is the one named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeckendorf-normal-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zcarry-namecert}; and the terminal continuation route is the one named by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtracenormalform-namecert}.

Finally use \autoref{thm:znormal-root-ledger-exhaustion}. Since the ledger accounts for every root consumer through $P$ and $N$, the total-host read has exactly the displayed bridge components and no exported host conversion, divergence, quotient, evaluator, or global-normalization row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ root continuation replay closure]\label{thm:znormal-root-cont-replay-closure}
Every accepted replay of an accepted $\ZNormalUp$ root display packet is exhausted by the displayed $\Cont$ row $C$ together with the terminal continuation-trace row $R$, transport row $H$, provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$. The replay cannot export a divergence oracle, host conversion row, or global normalization procedure, and it cannot manufacture a continuation trace outside the finite packet $Z$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalRootContReplayClosure}
\begin{proof}
Read the replay surface through \autoref{def:znormal-root-display-packet}. The only replay coordinate is $C$, and it is attached to $R,H,P,N$ inside the same finite packet.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-root-classifier-stability}. It keeps the $R$ read replayed only by $C$ and transported only by $H$, with the ContinuationTraceNormalForm sibling surface supplying the terminal trace rather than an ambient evaluator.

Next apply \autoref{thm:znormal-root-total-host-source} and \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-route-field-exhaustion}. These force terminal and total-host consumers to pass through the displayed rows before any replay can be accepted.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:znormal-root-ledger-exhaustion}. The package and local naming ledger has no coordinate for divergence, host conversion, or global normalization, so replay closure is exactly the displayed $\Cont$ row over $Z$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ root NameCert component exhaustion]\label{thm:znormal-root-namecert-component-exhaustion}
The local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ surface of an accepted root display packet is exactly the displayed carrier, classifier, transport, replay, provenance, and naming data
$$
(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N).
$$
Its carrier is the finite $\BHist$ packet $Z$; its classifier reads the typed endpoint, finite fuel, terminal normality, Zeckendorf and carry-facing normal row, and terminal continuation trace; its transport and replay rows are $H$ and $C$; its provenance and local name rows are $P$ and $N$. No additional carrier, classifier, transport, replay, provenance, naming, host conversion, divergence, or global-normalization component belongs to the local NameCert surface.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalRootNameCertComponentExhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier from \autoref{def:znormal-root-display-packet} and \autoref{def:znormal-carrier}. Both descriptions display the same finite coordinates $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$ and give no external NameCert component.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations}. It binds carrier admission, classifier reads, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming to the rows already visible in the accepted packet.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-root-classifier-stability} to account for the classifier side and \autoref{thm:znormal-root-ledger-exhaustion} to account for provenance and local naming. These two results close the component list through $K,R,H,C,P,N$ without opening a host-facing route.

The sibling packets remain inputs to the displayed rows only: \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedreductionnormalform-namecert} supplies the typed endpoint surface, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeckendorf-normal-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zcarry-namecert} supply the finite normal-word surface, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtracenormalform-namecert} supplies the terminal continuation surface.

Thus the local NameCert surface is exhausted by the carrier, classifier, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows of $Z$, with no additional host conversion, divergence, or global-normalization component.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ root display packet bridge readback exactness]\label{thm:znormal-root-display-packet-bridge-readback-exactness}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ root display packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
any accepted total-host or terminal-normal consumer read of $Z$ factors through exactly the displayed typed endpoint row $T$, finite fuel row $F$, terminal-normality row $V$, Zeckendorf and carry-facing normal-word row $K$, terminal continuation-trace row $R$, componentwise transport row $H$, replay row $C$, provenance row $P$, and local name row $N$. The typed endpoint, normal-word, and continuation-trace sibling surfaces may be consumed only as displayed rows of this packet. No host conversion, divergence oracle, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluator, or global normalizer participates in the readback.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalRootDisplayPacketBridgeReadbackExactness}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet by \autoref{def:znormal-root-display-packet}. The accepted root surface is the finite $\BHist$ display $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$, so a consumer read has no ambient coordinate apart from these rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-root-total-host-bridge-components}. It identifies the typed endpoint read with $T$, the Zeckendorf and carry-facing normal read with $K$, the terminal continuation-trace read with $R$, and the local provenance and naming read with $P,N$, while keeping $F,V,H,C$ inside the same packet.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-root-cont-replay-closure} for the replay coordinate. This confines every accepted continuation replay to $C$ together with $R,H,P,N$, and it prevents the readback from exporting a host conversion, divergence oracle, or global-normalization procedure.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:znormal-root-namecert-component-exhaustion}. Its component list exhausts the local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ surface by the carrier, classifier, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows of $Z$. The sibling chapters \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedreductionnormalform-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeckendorf-normal-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zcarry-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtracenormalform-namecert} are therefore consumed only as displayed endpoint, normal-word, and continuation-trace surfaces, giving exact readback with no quotient computation, unrestricted evaluator, or global normalizer.
\end{proof}
