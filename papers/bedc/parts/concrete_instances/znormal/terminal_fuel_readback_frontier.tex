\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ terminal fuel readback frontier]
\label{thm:znormal-terminal-fuel-readback-frontier}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
every terminal fuel read factors through the displayed typed endpoint $T$,
finite fuel row $F$, terminal-normality row $V$, continuation readback row
$R$, componentwise transport $H$, displayed route $C$, provenance $P$, and
local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ row $N$. The sibling normal-word row $K$ may be
consulted only as finite sibling evidence after $V$ is displayed.

The frontier exports no divergence oracle, host conversion row, quotient
computation, unrestricted evaluator, global normalizer, or total-host claim
outside $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:znormal-carrier}. Its terminal-facing
coordinates are the typed endpoint, finite fuel, terminal-normality,
continuation readback, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations} to bind those coordinates to
the local certificate surface. The finite fuel row and terminal-normality row
are therefore read before any terminal continuation row is consumed.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-fuel-normality-nonescape},
\autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-exactness}, and
\autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-fuel-route-admission}. These results force the
terminal read through $T,F,V,R,H,C,P,N$ and keep $K$ as a finite sibling row.

Finally use \autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff} for the only
allowed $K$ consumption. The carrier has no coordinate for divergence, host
conversion, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluation, global
normalization, or total-host escape, so the displayed frontier is exhaustive.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ total-host frontier lock]
\label{thm:znormal-total-host-frontier-lock}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the total-host boundary is locked to the finite rows
$T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$. A consumer may not reread that boundary as a divergence
row, host conversion row, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluator,
global normalization procedure, or substrate halting statement.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{def:znormal-carrier} and
\autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations}. Together they list the complete
accepted packet and attach every public read to the local $\NameCert$ surface.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-total-host-boundary}. It identifies the packet as a
finite interface for the total-host side and refuses host Turing completeness
or substrate halting content beyond the accepted rows.

Now use \autoref{thm:znormal-fuel-normality-nonescape} and
\autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-exactness}. Terminal normality is read
through finite fuel and continuation readback, not through a host conversion or
unrestricted evaluator.

The route admission theorem,
\autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-fuel-route-admission}, keeps the readback route
inside $H,C,P,N$, while
\autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff} keeps the sibling row finite.
Thus no excluded total-host or quotient object can be recovered from the
frontier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ downstream consumer boundary]
\label{thm:znormal-downstream-consumer-boundary}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet factors only
through the typed endpoint $T$, finite fuel $F$, terminal normality $V$,
sibling normal-word handoff $K$, continuation readback $R$, componentwise
transport $H$, displayed route $C$, provenance $P$, and local
$\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ row $N$.

Consequently downstream normal-form consumers receive the finite terminal
frontier and no divergence oracle, host conversion row, quotient computation,
unrestricted evaluator, global normalizer, or total-host computation row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First expose the finite packet with \autoref{def:znormal-carrier}. The
downstream-facing rows are exactly $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$.

Next bind the local certificate. By
\autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations}, carrier admission, typed endpoint
readback, fuel, terminal normality, sibling handoff, continuation readback,
transport, route, provenance, and naming all belong to that same finite
surface.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-fuel-readback-frontier} to force terminal
fuel readback through $T,F,V,R,H,C,P,N$. Then use
\autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff} to identify the only allowed
$K$ consumption as finite sibling normal-word evidence.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:znormal-total-host-frontier-lock}. Since the locked
frontier refuses divergence, host conversion, quotient computation,
unrestricted evaluation, global normalization, and total-host computation,
every downstream consumer remains inside the displayed terminal-fuel boundary.
\end{proof}
