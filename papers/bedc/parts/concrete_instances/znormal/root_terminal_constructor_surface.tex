\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ terminal row constructor exhaustion]
\label{thm:znormal-terminal-row-constructor-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the terminal row constructor surface is exhausted by typed endpoint $T$,
finite fuel $F$, terminal-normality $V$, sibling normal-word evidence $K$,
terminal readback $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed
$\Cont$ replay $C$, package provenance $P$, and local
$\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ row $N$. It constructs no host conversion, quotient
normalization, unrestricted evaluation, divergence detection, or global
normalization row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalTerminalRowConstructorExhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:znormal-carrier}. The accepted packet
lists exactly $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$ as the root terminal constructor inventory.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations}. Carrier admission, typed
endpoint readback, finite fuel, terminal normality, sibling normal-word
evidence, continuation readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local
naming are the displayed NameCert obligations for the same packet.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff}. Terminal readback is routed
through the finite terminal rows, and $K$ is only the finite sibling
normal-word handoff.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:znormal-root-tastegate-field-surface}. The
TasteGate-facing field surface is the same packet surface, so no constructor
row can be reinterpreted as host conversion, quotient normalization,
unrestricted evaluation, divergence detection, or global normalization.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ fuel replay nondivergence boundary]
\label{thm:znormal-fuel-replay-nondivergence-boundary}
For an accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the fuel replay boundary is the finite path from $T$ through $F$ to $V$ and
$R$, transported and replayed by $H,C,P,N$, with $K$ available only as finite
sibling normal-word evidence. This boundary records nondivergence of the
displayed replay route without exporting divergence detection, unrestricted
evaluation, host conversion, quotient normalization, or global normalization.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{def:znormal-carrier} and
\autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations}. Together they expose the finite
fuel row $F$ beside the typed endpoint, terminal-normality, continuation,
transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-exactness}. The replayed terminal
read is the displayed route through $T,F,V,R,H,C,P,N$, not a host evaluator.

Attach the sibling side by
\autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff}. The row $K$ remains finite
normal-word evidence and does not become a quotient normalizer or a global
normalizer.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:znormal-root-tastegate-field-surface}. Since the
TasteGate-facing packet has exactly the rows $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$, the fuel
replay boundary can certify only the displayed nondivergence route and cannot
decide arbitrary divergence or unrestricted evaluation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ total host consumer factorization]
\label{thm:znormal-total-host-consumer-factorization}
Every total-host consumer of an accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet factors through
the finite packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N).
$$
The factorization first reads typed endpoint, fuel, terminal-normality,
sibling normal-word, and terminal readback rows, and then moves only through
$H,C,P,N$. It has no route to host conversion, quotient normalization,
unrestricted evaluation, divergence detection, or global normalization.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and local NameCert surface using
\autoref{def:znormal-carrier} and
\autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations}. These rows give the complete
consumer inventory and bind it to one local naming certificate.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-exactness}. Any terminal read of
the consumer must pass through the finite terminal route, including $R$ and
the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff} to account for $K$. The
normal-word component is the sibling handoff surface, not an additional host
normalization channel.

Finally use \autoref{thm:znormal-root-tastegate-field-surface}. The
TasteGate-facing field surface is already the same tuple, so a total-host
consumer factors through the packet and cannot extract host conversion,
quotient normalization, unrestricted evaluation, divergence detection, or
global normalization.
\end{proof}
