\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ total-host readback triad]
\label{thm:znormal-root-total-host-readback-triad}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
total-host readback exposes exactly the terminal-normality row $V$, the
bounded fuel replay row $F$, and the non-escape row witnessed by the local
carrier and naming surface. The exposure is a finite $\BHist$ route transported
by $\hsame$, read by $\Cont$, packaged by $\Pkg$, and named by
$\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ through $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$.

No accepted total-host readback supplies a detached host conversion row,
quotient computation, divergence oracle, unrestricted evaluator, global
normalizer, or substrate-halting certificate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalRootTotalHostReadbackTriad}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier by \autoref{def:znormal-carrier}. The packet
shows the terminal-normality coordinate $V$, the fuel coordinate $F$, the
readback coordinate $R$, and the support rows $H,C,P,N$ inside the single
finite tuple $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations}. The local
$\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ row binds carrier admission, terminal normality,
bounded fuel replay, sibling normal-word evidence, readback, transport,
continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming to the same packet.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-exactness} together with
\autoref{thm:znormal-fuel-normality-nonescape}. Terminal readback reaches $V$
only through the bounded fuel route, while the non-escape theorem excludes
host conversion, quotient computation, divergence detection, unrestricted
evaluation, global normalization, and substrate halting.

Finally combine \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-host-root-factorization},
\autoref{thm:znormal-fuel-replay-root-exhaustion}, and
\autoref{thm:znormal-normal-output-root-nonescape}. These root rows identify
the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ surface, so the
total-host readback triad is exactly terminal normality, bounded fuel replay,
and non-escape.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ fuel route non-escape]
\label{thm:znormal-root-fuel-route-nonescape}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the bounded fuel route from $T$ through $F$ to $V$ and $R$ cannot export
divergence detection, host conversion, quotient computation, unrestricted
evaluation, global normalization, or substrate halting. Its whole observable
surface is the finite $\BHist$ packet with $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and
$\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ support.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:znormal-fuel-normality-nonescape}. It already places
terminal normality behind finite fuel replay and rules out the host-side and
global-normalization objects listed in the statement.

Next apply \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-exactness}. The readback row
$R$ is reached only after the endpoint, fuel, and terminal-normality rows have
been displayed, so no consumer can detach $R$ into an ambient host conversion
or quotient-computation route.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-host-root-factorization}. The route remains
inside the same $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$ packet, with $H,C,P,N$ giving transport,
continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming.

The sibling row is harmless by \autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff}:
$K$ contributes finite normal-word evidence only. Hence the bounded fuel route
has no coordinate from which divergence, host conversion, global
normalization, or substrate halting could be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ downstream normal-form readiness]
\label{thm:znormal-root-downstream-normal-form-readiness}
Every downstream normal-form consumer of an accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
must factor through the terminal-host exposure, the terminal-host obligation
rows, and the local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ route. The consumer may read only
the finite $\BHist$ surface carried by $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$, transported by
$\hsame$, replayed by $\Cont$, and packaged by $\Pkg$.

Consequently downstream readiness is a finite normal-form boundary: it does
not provide host conversion, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluation,
divergence detection, global normalization, or substrate-halting evidence.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalRootDownstreamNormalFormReadiness}
\begin{proof}
First expose the terminal-host surface with
\autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-normality-carrier-exposure} and
\autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-host-root-factorization}. These theorems show
that every terminal-host consumer reads the displayed packet and no second
host-side coordinate.

Next apply \autoref{thm:znormal-fuel-replay-root-exhaustion} and
\autoref{thm:znormal-normal-output-root-nonescape}. These obligation rows bind
terminal normality, bounded fuel replay, readback, transport, continuation
replay, package provenance, and naming to the same finite carrier.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations} to repack those rows as the
local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ route. The support objects are precisely
$\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and the local name certificate over
$T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:znormal-root-fuel-route-nonescape}. Since the fuel
route cannot export host conversion, divergence detection, unrestricted
evaluation, quotient computation, global normalization, or substrate halting,
downstream normal-form readiness factors through the terminal host rows and
does not exceed them.
\end{proof}
