\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ terminal normality carrier exposure]
\label{thm:znormal-terminal-normality-carrier-exposure}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
terminal normality is exposed only through the displayed typed endpoint row
$T$, finite fuel row $F$, terminal-normality row $V$, terminal read row $R$,
componentwise transport row $H$, continuation replay row $C$, package row $P$,
and local naming row $N$. The row $K$ contributes only finite sibling
normal-word evidence, and no accepted carrier can reread $K$ as host
conversion, quotient computation, an unrestricted evaluator, a divergence
oracle, or a global normalizer.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalPacket\_terminal\_normality\_carrier\_exposure}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:znormal-carrier}. Its accepted packet is
the finite row list $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$, with $V$ singled out as the
terminal-normality coordinate and $K$ listed separately as sibling
normal-word evidence.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations}. The local
$\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ obligations bind carrier admission, typed endpoint,
finite fuel, terminal normality, finite normal-word comparison, terminal
readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming to this same packet.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-fuel-normality-nonescape} and
\autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-exactness}. These theorems route every
terminal read through $T,F,V,R,H,C,P,N$ and keep the normal-word coordinate
$K$ outside the terminal-read source.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff}. The $K$ row
is finite sibling evidence only, so terminal normality remains exposed through
the displayed carrier rows and cannot become any excluded host-side or
global-normalization object.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ total-host fuel replay boundary]
\label{thm:znormal-total-host-fuel-replay-boundary}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
total-host fuel replay is the finite route from typed endpoint $T$ through
fuel $F$ to terminal row $V$ and terminal continuation read $R$. The route is
transported by $H$, replayed by $C$, packaged by $P$, and named by $N$; it
does not export a host conversion row, quotient computation, unrestricted
evaluator, divergence oracle, substrate halting claim, or global normalizer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{def:znormal-carrier}. The carrier displays the endpoint,
fuel, terminal normality, sibling normal-word, continuation read, transport,
replay, package, and naming rows as the single finite packet
$T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations} to read the same rows as the
local certificate surface. The fuel coordinate $F$ and terminal-normality
coordinate $V$ are therefore packet rows, not ambient host computation
relations.

Next use \autoref{thm:znormal-fuel-normality-nonescape} and
\autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-exactness}. They force terminal fuel
replay along $T,F,V,R$ before any consumer can read the terminal continuation
surface.

The structural route is then exactly $H,C,P,N$. By
\autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff}, the sibling row $K$ remains
finite evidence beside that route, so the fuel replay boundary cannot escape
to any excluded host, quotient, divergence, substrate-halting, or
global-normalization row.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalPacket\_total\_host\_fuel\_replay\_boundary}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ consumer divergence-oracle nonescape]
\label{thm:znormal-consumer-divergence-oracle-nonescape}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\ZNormalUp$ packet factors through
the displayed rows
$$
T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N.
$$
Such a consumer may use typed endpoint evidence, finite fuel replay, terminal
normality, finite sibling normal-word evidence, terminal continuation read,
transport, replay, package provenance, and local naming, but it cannot obtain
a divergence oracle, host conversion row, quotient computation, unrestricted
evaluator, global normalizer, or substrate halting claim.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalPacket\_consumer\_divergence\_oracle\_nonescape}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier by \autoref{def:znormal-carrier}. The consumer
surface is the finite packet $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$, and there is no second
consumer coordinate outside that packet.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations}. It identifies the permitted
reads as carrier admission, typed endpoint, finite fuel, terminal normality,
sibling normal-word comparison, continuation read, transport, replay,
provenance, and local naming.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-fuel-normality-nonescape} and
\autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-exactness}. These route terminal
normal-form consumption through $T,F,V,R,H,C,P,N$ and prevent the consumer from
detaching a host computation or unbounded evaluator from the finite replay.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff}. The remaining
row $K$ is finite sibling evidence only. Hence every downstream consumer
factors through the displayed packet and has no route to divergence detection,
host conversion, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluation, global
normalization, or substrate-halting evidence.
\end{proof}
