\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ total-host readback display]
\label{thm:znormal-total-host-readback-display}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ root packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the root-facing total-host readback surface exposes exactly the rows $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$. Here $T$ is the typed endpoint row, $F$ is the finite fuel row, $V$ is the terminal-normality row, $K$ is the finite ZeckendorfNormal and $\ZCarryUp$ normal-word evidence row, $R$ is the continuation readback row, and $H,C,P,N$ are the displayed transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows.

No root consumer receives host conversion, quotient normalization, unrestricted evaluation, divergence, or global normalization data from this display.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:znormal-carrier} and the root boundary of \autoref{def:znormal-root-carrier-boundary}. Both expose the same finite packet $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$ before any root-facing terminal claim can be read.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations} and \autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-root-obligations}. These obligations bind carrier admission, terminal-normality exposure, sibling evidence, bounded fuel, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming to the displayed root packet.

The row list contains no host conversion, quotient normalization, unrestricted evaluator, divergence oracle, or global normalizer. Thus the total-host readback display is exactly the finite root surface $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ terminal fuel route exhaustion]
\label{thm:znormal-terminal-fuel-route-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ root packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
any terminal-fuel consumer factors through the displayed finite route
$$
T \longmapsto F \longmapsto V \longmapsto R
$$
together with $H,C,P,N$. The sibling row $K$ may be read only as finite normal-word evidence attached to that route, and it cannot replace the finite fuel route by host conversion, quotient normalization, unrestricted evaluation, divergence, or global normalization.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the total-host readback display of \autoref{thm:znormal-total-host-readback-display}. It exposes the root packet as a finite $\BHist$ surface and leaves no unbounded evaluator row outside the packet.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-fuel-readback-frontier} and \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-fuel-route-admission}. They place every terminal fuel read behind the typed endpoint $T$, finite fuel $F$, terminal-normality row $V$, and continuation readback row $R$.

Next apply \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-boundary-nonescape}. The boundary keeps the read inside the displayed transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows $H,C,P,N$.

Finally inspect $K$. By \autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff}, $K$ is finite sibling normal-word evidence only. It cannot supply host conversion, quotient normalization, unrestricted evaluation, divergence, or global normalization in place of the terminal fuel route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ normal-word sibling separation]
\label{thm:znormal-normal-word-sibling-separation}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ root packet
$$
Z=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the ZeckendorfNormal and $\ZCarryUp$ normal-word evidence carried by $K$ remains sibling evidence for the finite route $T,F,V,R,H,C,P,N$. It is separate from host conversion, quotient normalization, unrestricted evaluation, divergence, and global normalization, and no root consumer may identify those excluded host-side objects with the normal-word evidence row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $K$ from the accepted root packet. The carrier records $K$ beside $T,F,V,R,H,C,P,N$, so the normal-word evidence is a displayed sibling row rather than a replacement for the terminal route.

Apply \autoref{thm:znormal-carry-normalization-obligation} and \autoref{thm:znormal-sibling-normalword-handoff}. These theorems read $K$ through the finite ZeckendorfNormal and $\ZCarryUp$ surfaces and attach it to the same $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows as the rest of the packet.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-total-host-frontier-lock} for the excluded host-side objects. The frontier refuses host conversion, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluation, divergence, and global normalization as exported reads.

Therefore the $K$ row remains finite normal-word sibling evidence only. The terminal route remains $T,F,V,R,H,C,P,N$, and no accepted root consumer can turn $K$ into a host conversion, quotient normalization, unrestricted evaluator, divergence oracle, or global normalizer.
\end{proof}
