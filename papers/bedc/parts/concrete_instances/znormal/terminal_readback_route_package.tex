\begin{definition}[$\ZNormalUp$ terminal readback route carrier]
\label{def:znormal-terminal-readback-route-carrier}
The $\ZNormalUp$ terminal readback route carrier is the finite packet
$$
Q=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
read from an accepted $\ZNormalUp$ carrier as follows: $T$ is the typed endpoint row, $F$ is the bounded fuel replay row, $V$ is the terminal-normality row, $K$ is the finite normal-word comparison row, $R$ is the continuation readback row, $H$ is componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $C$ is displayed $\Cont$ replay, $P$ is $\Pkg$ provenance, and $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ row.

The route carrier is terminal only when the readback passes through the rows displayed in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-typedreductionnormalform-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-zeckendorf-normal-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuationtracenormalform-namecert}. It has no coordinate for a divergence oracle, host conversion row, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluator, global normalizer, or substrate-halting certificate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ terminal readback route exhaustion]
\label{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-route-exhaustion}
Every downstream read of an accepted $\ZNormalUp$ terminal readback route carrier $Q=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N)$ factors through terminal normality $V$, bounded fuel replay $F$, continuation readback $R$, componentwise transport $H$, displayed replay $C$, provenance $P$, the local naming row $N$, and the sibling finite normal-word row $K$. The factorization is through $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ only.

Consequently no downstream normal-route consumer can obtain a detached host conversion, quotient computation, divergence oracle, unrestricted evaluator, global normalization procedure, or substrate-halting theorem from this route.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalPacket\_terminal\_readback\_route\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{def:znormal-terminal-readback-route-carrier}. The route carrier lists only $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$, so every downstream read begins inside the same finite packet.

Apply the carrier and obligation rows of \autoref{def:znormal-carrier} and \autoref{thm:znormal-namecert-obligations}. They identify typed endpoint readback, bounded fuel replay, terminal normality, normal-word comparison, continuation readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming as one $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$ surface.

Next use \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-host-root-factorization}, \autoref{thm:znormal-fuel-replay-root-exhaustion}, and \autoref{thm:znormal-normal-output-root-nonescape}. These root rows keep terminal-host consumption, fuel replay, and normal-output reads inside the displayed packet.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:znormal-root-total-host-readback-triad} and \autoref{thm:znormal-root-downstream-normal-form-readiness}. The typed endpoint, Zeckendorf normal-word, and continuation trace references already present in the chapter index supply the sibling readback boundaries, while the terminal-host rows exclude host conversion, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluation, divergence detection, global normalization, and substrate halting.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\ZNormalUp$ total-host refusal nonescape]
\label{thm:znormal-total-host-refusal-nonescape}
For every accepted $\ZNormalUp$ terminal readback route carrier $Q=(T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N)$, the total-host refusal surface is exhausted by terminal normality, bounded fuel replay, continuation readback, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows. The packet exports no divergence oracle, host conversion row, global normalization procedure, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluator, or substrate-halting certificate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ZnormalUp.ZnormalTotalHostRefusalNonescape}
\begin{proof}
Project the route carrier of \autoref{def:znormal-terminal-readback-route-carrier}. The rows $T,F,V,K,R,H,C,P,N$ expose a finite terminal readback route, not a host evaluator or global normalizer.

Use \autoref{thm:znormal-fuel-normality-nonescape}, \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-exactness}, and \autoref{thm:znormal-root-fuel-route-nonescape}. These theorems place every terminal read behind bounded fuel and remove divergence detection, host conversion, quotient computation, unrestricted evaluation, global normalization, and substrate halting from the fuel route.

Finally combine \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-host-root-factorization}, \autoref{thm:znormal-root-downstream-normal-form-readiness}, and \autoref{thm:znormal-terminal-readback-route-exhaustion}. The resulting route is a finite $\BHist$ packet transported by $\hsame$, replayed by $\Cont$, packaged by $\Pkg$, and named by $\NameCert_{\ZNormalUp}$, so the listed host-side objects have no remaining export coordinate.
\end{proof}
