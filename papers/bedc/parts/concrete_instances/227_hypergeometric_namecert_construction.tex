\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HypergeometricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-hypergeometric-namecert}

The hypergeometric-function interface $\HypergeometricUp$ packages the analytic continuation of $_pF_q$ series defined by ascending-factorial coefficient ratios. It depends on $\NameCert_{\ComplexAnalyticUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-analytic-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\GammaUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-gamma-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:hypergeometric-carrier}

\begin{definition}[HypergeometricUp BHist source packet]
\label{def:hypergeometric-bhist-source-packet}
A $\HypergeometricUp$ source packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$(C,G,A,B,R,S,\rho,\lambda)$. The row $C$ is the consumed
$\ComplexAnalyticUp$ dependency packet, and the row $G$ is the consumed
$\GammaUp$ dependency packet. The rows $A$ and $B$ record the visible numerator
and denominator parameter lists for the $_pF_q$ series, the row $R$ records the
ascending-factorial coefficient-ratio boundary, and the row $S$ records the
finite series-readback boundary over the displayed complex-analytic carrier.
The row $\rho$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance linking the two dependency packets to
the hypergeometric name, and $\lambda$ is the finite $\Cont$ ledger storing the
$\hsame$ transports among parameters, coefficient ratios, and readback rows.

Acceptance is exactly this finite packet. It carries no analytic continuation
theorem, no host power-series object, no monodromy data, no differential
equation, and no external equality of special functions.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[HypergeometricUp root carrier]
\label{thm:hypergeometric-root-carrier}
Every accepted $\HypergeometricUp$ carrier row projects to the
$\ComplexAnalyticUp$ dependency row, the $\GammaUp$ dependency row, the
parameter rows $A$ and $B$, the coefficient-ratio row $R$, the finite readback
row $S$, the $\Pkg$ provenance $\rho$, and the $\Cont$ ledger $\lambda$ of
\autoref{def:hypergeometric-bhist-source-packet}. Conversely, those rows repack
as an accepted carrier packet when their displayed $\hsame$ transports are the
ones recorded by $\lambda$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project an accepted packet by its displayed tuple coordinates. The first two
coordinates expose the complex-analytic and Gamma dependency packets, while the
parameter, coefficient-ratio, readback, provenance, and ledger coordinates are
read directly from the finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.

For the converse, place the same displayed rows back into the tuple constructor
and use the stored $\Cont$ ledger as the acceptance evidence. The construction
does not inspect a host series or an analytic continuation theorem, because no
such coordinate occurs in the packet.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HypergeometricUp.HypergeometricBHistSourcePacket\_root\_carrier}

\begin{theorem}[HypergeometricUp root classifier]
\label{thm:hypergeometric-root-classifier}
For two $\HypergeometricUp$ source packets, the root classifier compares only
the consumed $\ComplexAnalyticUp$ and $\GammaUp$ dependency rows, the numerator
and denominator parameter rows, the coefficient-ratio row, the finite readback
row, the $\Pkg$ provenance row, and the finite $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ ledger.
Transport along those rows preserves the accepted carrier and supplies no
analytic-continuation classifier outside the displayed packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the dependency comparisons. The carried
$\ComplexAnalyticUp$ and $\GammaUp$ rows supply the only classifiers available
for the analytic carrier and Gamma-derived parameter faces.

Next transport the parameter rows, the coefficient-ratio row, and the finite
series-readback row through the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons stored in
$\lambda$. The $\Pkg$ row records that the transported faces belong to the same
hypergeometric packet.

Repack the transported rows by
\autoref{thm:hypergeometric-root-carrier}. Since the classifier has compared
every packet coordinate and no continuation classifier is present, the result is
exactly the root classifier surface.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HypergeometricUp.HypergeometricBHistSourcePacket\_root\_classifier}

\begin{theorem}[HypergeometricUp root ledger]
\label{thm:hypergeometric-root-ledger}
The $\HypergeometricUp$ root ledger is exact: its rows are precisely the
$\ComplexAnalyticUp$ dependency, the $\GammaUp$ dependency, the two parameter
lists, the coefficient-ratio boundary, the finite $\Cont$ readback, the
$\hsame$ transports, and the $\Pkg$ provenance of
\autoref{def:hypergeometric-bhist-source-packet}. No analytic continuation,
singularity classification, monodromy representation, differential-equation
solution, or host-function equality is part of this ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Coverage follows by projecting the accepted source packet. Each ledger entry is
one of the displayed tuple coordinates or one of the transport rows stored in
the finite $\Cont$ ledger.

Soundness is the reverse projection. Given exactly those rows, the source packet
constructor rebuilds the same accepted $\HypergeometricUp$ carrier and the same
classifier surface.

Completeness follows from the packet inventory. There is no coordinate for
analytic continuation, singularity data, monodromy, a host differential
equation, or external function equality, so none of those rows can be consumed
from the root ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HypergeometricUp root stability]
\label{thm:hypergeometric-root-stability}
If a $\HypergeometricUp$ source packet is transported by the displayed
$\hsame$ rows on its $\ComplexAnalyticUp$ dependency, $\GammaUp$ dependency,
parameter lists, coefficient-ratio boundary, finite readback row, and
$\Pkg$ provenance, then the transported packet is again accepted by the same
root carrier and classified by the same root classifier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.HypergeometricUp.HypergeometricBHistSourcePacket\_root\_stability}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier rows using
\autoref{thm:hypergeometric-root-carrier}. The hypotheses give an $\hsame$
transport for every visible coordinate of the packet.

Use the finite $\Cont$ ledger to move the coefficient-ratio and readback rows
with the parameter transports. The dependency rows move through their own
displayed $\NameCert$ classifiers, and the $\Pkg$ provenance remains attached
to the transported packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:hypergeometric-root-classifier} to the transported row
inventory. It compares exactly the rows just transported, so the accepted packet
and its classifier are stable without using analytic continuation as an
uncarried host theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HypergeometricUp root consumer boundary]
\label{thm:hypergeometric-root-consumer-boundary}
A downstream consumer of the $\HypergeometricUp$ root may read exactly the
$\mathsf{BHist}$ source packet of
\autoref{def:hypergeometric-bhist-source-packet}, the carrier projection of
\autoref{thm:hypergeometric-root-carrier}, the classifier stability of
\autoref{thm:hypergeometric-root-classifier}, the exact ledger of
\autoref{thm:hypergeometric-root-ledger}, and the transport stability of
\autoref{thm:hypergeometric-root-stability}. It may consume the displayed
complex-analytic dependency, Gamma dependency, parameter rows,
coefficient-ratio row, finite readback, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, and $\Pkg$
provenance; it may not consume analytic continuation as an uncarried host
theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the source packet. The carrier theorem lists every row available
to the consumer and repacks exactly those rows as the accepted
$\HypergeometricUp$ root.

Next apply the classifier and stability theorems. They show that all comparisons
and transports remain inside the same dependency, parameter, coefficient-ratio,
readback, provenance, and ledger surface.

Finally apply ledger exactness. Since the ledger has no coordinate for analytic
continuation or for any external special-function equality, the consumer
boundary is precisely the displayed finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet.
\end{proof}
