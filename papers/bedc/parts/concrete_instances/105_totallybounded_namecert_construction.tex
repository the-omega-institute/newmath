\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TotallyBoundedUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}

The totally-bounded interface $\TotallyBoundedUp$ packages a metric carrier admitting, for every $\varepsilon > 0$, an explicit finite $\varepsilon$-net. It depends on $\NameCert_{\MetricSpaceUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}) and is the prerequisite for $\NameCert_{\CompactMetricUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:totallybounded-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Singleton metric totally-bounded instance]
\label{def:singleton-metric-totally-bounded-instance}
The singleton metric totally-bounded instance is the $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier
on histories
$$
T_{\emp}(x):=\hsame(x,\emp).
$$
Its classifier is
$$
x\sim_T y \quad:\!\!\Longleftrightarrow\quad
T_{\emp}(x)\land T_{\emp}(y)\land \hsame(x,y),
$$
and its distance witness is the concrete metric row
$$
d_T(x,y,\delta):=\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,y,\delta).
$$
For every positive tolerance history $\varepsilon$, the finite net is the
one-point closed bundle $\Bcons(\emp,\Bnil)$ with ledger center $\emp$. The
bundle membership row is the head $\mathsf{InBundle}$ constructor, and the
coverage ledger for a carried point $x$ uses the empty-distance witness
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,\emp,\emp)$ together with the carried endpoint
row $T_{\emp}(x)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton metric totally-bounded laws]
\label{thm:singleton-metric-totally-bounded-laws}
The instance of
\autoref{def:singleton-metric-totally-bounded-instance} satisfies the concrete
finite-net laws required by $\TotallyBoundedUp$: $\emp$ inhabits the carrier,
the unique net center $\emp$ lies in the carrier, every carried point is covered
by that center at empty distance, the net witness is stable under the singleton
classifier $\sim_T$, and the coverage row is compatible with metric
empty-distance exactness from
\autoref{thm:metric-distance-witness-empty-distance-iff}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier inhabitation and center membership are both the reflexive $\hsame$ row
on $\emp$. Thus the ledger center named by the one-point
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ is accepted by $T_{\emp}$.

For coverage, fix a carried point $x$. The row $T_{\emp}(x)$ is exactly
$\hsame(x,\emp)$, so the singleton classifier records $x\sim_T \emp$ using the
carried endpoint row, reflexivity of $\emp$, and the same endpoint
$\hsame$ witness.

Apply \autoref{thm:metric-distance-witness-empty-distance-iff} to identify the
empty-distance metric row with the two endpoint classifiers to $\emp$. This
packages $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,\emp,\emp)$ as the coverage witness
for the unique center, and the head $\mathsf{InBundle}$ constructor records
that the center belongs to $\Bcons(\emp,\Bnil)$.

Stability under $\sim_T$ follows by unpacking the classifier on the source and
target endpoints. Both histories classify with $\emp$, so the empty-distance
exactness row supplies the same metric coverage after transport, and the closed
bundle branch is unchanged.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TotallyBoundedUp.SingletonMetricTotallyBounded\_laws}

The ten-field fit is: traditional target total boundedness by finite
$\varepsilon$-nets; BEDC source $\MetricSpaceUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$T_{\emp}$; classifier $\sim_T$; stability from $\hsame$ and
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ empty-distance exactness; ledger one center
and one $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ branch; core theorem
\autoref{thm:singleton-metric-totally-bounded-laws}; exactness singleton
coverage; standard bridge terminal metric space; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{definition}[Totally bounded ProbeBundle net carrier]
\label{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier}
Let $X$ be a metric BEDC carrier whose distance rows are encoded by
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$. A totally bounded ProbeBundle net carrier
at tolerance history $\varepsilon$ is the history relation
$$
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P},net)
$$
whose accepted row consists only of $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger data:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item a positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$;
  \item a closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ $\mathcal{P}$ of center names;
  \item for every probe row $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P})$, a center
  history $c_p$ admitted by the metric carrier $X$;
  \item for every such bundled probe, a positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ radius
  history $r_p$;
  \item for every source-point history $x$ admitted by $X$, a bundled probe
  row $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P})$, a distance history $d_p$, and a
  row $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,c_p,d_p)$ whose $\RatUp$ comparison
  ledger certifies $d_p \leq \varepsilon$.
\end{enumerate}
The finite carrier is the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ inductive together
with the displayed $\mathsf{InBundle}$ coverage rows. It is not an external
enumeration of centers, and no host-level list is exposed as certificate data.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.TotallyBoundedUp.TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}

\begin{theorem}[Totally bounded ProbeBundle net coverage stability]
\label{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability}
Let $X$ be a metric BEDC carrier with $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$
classifier transport. Suppose
$$
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P},net)
$$
holds. If the tolerance history $\varepsilon$, the bundled center histories
$c_p$, the positive radius histories $r_p$, and all displayed distance
histories $d_p$ are transported by $\hsame$, and if the positive-tolerance,
positive-radius, and bound comparisons are transported by the $\RatUp$
classifier, then the same $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ $\mathcal{P}$ with the
transported ledgers is again a valid totally bounded net for the transported
tolerance history.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TotallyBoundedUp.TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet\_coverage\_hsame\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Proceed by induction on the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine. In the
$\Bnil$ case there is no bundled probe row to transport, and any accepted
coverage row is absent by inversion on $\mathsf{InBundle}$.

For a cons bundle $\Bcons(p,\mathcal{P}_0)$, the head probe is handled by the
head constructor of $\mathsf{InBundle}$ and the tail probes are handled by the
induction hypothesis through the tail constructor. The center history and the
positive radius history attached to each case are moved along the supplied
$\hsame$ rows, while the $\RatUp$ classifier transports positivity of the
radius ledger.

Now fix a transported source-point history $x'$. The original coverage row for
an $\hsame$ representative $x$ supplies a bundled probe $p$, a center $c_p$,
and a distance row $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,c_p,d_p)$ with
$d_p \leq \varepsilon$. Metric $\hsame$ transport carries this row to
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x',c'_p,d'_p)$ for the transported center and
distance histories.

It remains to repack the bound. The $\RatUp$ classifier transports the
comparison $d_p \leq \varepsilon$ to the comparison
$d'_p \leq \varepsilon'$, and the same $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P})$
constructor records that the chosen probe still belongs to the finite carrier.
Thus the transported ledgers satisfy the carrier relation for the same closed
bundle.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:totallybounded-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[TotallyBoundedUp ProbeBundle NameCert target]
\label{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-namecert-target}
The carrier of \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier}, the
classifier given by transported tolerance and bundled ledger histories, the
coverage-stability theorem
\autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability}, and the
ledger policy that records the tolerance, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$,
$\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, center histories, positive radius
histories, $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ rows, and $\RatUp$ bound rows form
the paper-level $\NameCert$ target for $\TotallyBoundedUp$ over a
$\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TotallyBoundedUp.TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
The source field is the underlying $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier together with its
accepted point histories and metric-distance classifier. The pattern field is
the relation
$\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P},net)$ for
each positive $\RatUp$ tolerance history.

The classifier acts on the visible tolerance and on every bundled ledger row:
centers, radii, distance histories, and comparison proofs are transported by
the displayed $\hsame$ and $\RatUp$ classifier rows. The finite part of the
certificate is unchanged because $\mathcal{P}$ is the closed
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ inductive and membership is witnessed by
$\mathsf{InBundle}$ constructors.

The stability obligation is exactly
\autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability}. The ledger
policy records the same rows named in the carrier definition, so source
soundness, pattern soundness, classifier stability, and ledger soundness are
all witnessed by the displayed carrier and stability theorem. This is the
required current paper target for $\NameCert_{\TotallyBoundedUp}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Totally bounded ProbeBundle net tolerance weakening]
\label{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-tolerance-weakening}
Let $X$ be a metric BEDC carrier whose distance rows are encoded by
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$, and write $\delta\le_{\RatUp}\eta$ for a
visible non-strict $\RatUp$ comparison ledger. Suppose
$$
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P},net)
$$
holds in the sense of \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier}.
If $\varepsilon'$ is a positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ tolerance history and
$\theta:\varepsilon\le_{\RatUp}\varepsilon'$ is supplied, then the row
$net_{\varepsilon'}$ obtained from $net$ by reusing the same closed
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine, bundled centers, radius histories,
$\mathsf{InBundle}$ rows, source-point probe selections, and
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ rows, and by replacing each displayed bound
$d\le_{\RatUp}\varepsilon$ with its transitive composite
$d\le_{\RatUp}\varepsilon'$, satisfies
$$
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon',\mathcal{P},net_{\varepsilon'}).
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TotallyBoundedUp.TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet\_tolerance\_weakening}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted row for $net$. It contains the positive tolerance row for
$\varepsilon$, the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ carrier $\mathcal{P}$, the
center and positive-radius ledgers attached to each bundled probe, and, for
each source-point history $x$ carried by $X$, a bundled probe $p$, a distance
history $d$, a row $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,c_p,d)$, and a comparison
ledger $d\le_{\RatUp}\varepsilon$.

To build the row at $\varepsilon'$, use the supplied positive tolerance row for
$\varepsilon'$. The closed bundle, the $\mathsf{InBundle}$ membership rows, the
center histories, and the radius histories are copied verbatim from $net$.
These clauses of \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier} do not
mention the upper tolerance except through its positivity row.

Now fix a carried source-point history $x$. Read from $net$ the same bundled
probe $p$, the same center $c_p$, the same distance history $d$, and the same
metric row $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,c_p,d)$. The original coverage
comparison gives $d\le_{\RatUp}\varepsilon$. Compose this comparison on the
right with $\theta:\varepsilon\le_{\RatUp}\varepsilon'$ by the transitivity
field of the non-strict $\RatUp$ comparison ledger. The resulting row is
$d\le_{\RatUp}\varepsilon'$.

Thus every source point keeps its original bundled probe and metric-distance
witness, while only the final rational upper-bound ledger is weakened from
$\varepsilon$ to $\varepsilon'$. All fields required by the totally bounded
ProbeBundle net carrier are therefore present for
$\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon',\mathcal{P},net_{\varepsilon'})$.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Subcarrier-restricted TotallyBounded ProbeBundle net]
\label{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-subcarrier-restriction}
Let $X:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a metric BEDC carrier with
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ rows, and let
$Y:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a subcarrier of $X$ with inclusion
$$
\iota_Y:\forall y:\mathsf{BHist}.\;Y(y)\to X(y).
$$
Given a row
$$
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P},net)
$$
in the sense of \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier}, the
subcarrier-restricted row
$$
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}_{X\downarrow Y}(\varepsilon,\mathcal{P},net|_Y)
$$
keeps the positive tolerance history, the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$
$\mathcal{P}$, every bundled center history $c_p$ carried by $X$, every
positive radius history $r_p$, and every $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P})$
row from $net$, while replacing the source-point coverage clause by the same
clause quantified only over histories carried by $Y$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Totally bounded ProbeBundle net subcarrier restriction]
\label{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-subcarrier-restriction}
Let $X$ be a metric BEDC carrier whose distance rows are encoded by
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$, and let $Y$ be a classifier-stable subcarrier
of $X$. Thus there are rows
$$
\iota_Y:\forall y:\mathsf{BHist}.\;Y(y)\to X(y)
$$
and
$$
\sigma_Y:\forall y y':\mathsf{BHist}.\;Y(y)\to y\sim_X y'\to Y(y'),
$$
where $\sim_X$ is the metric classifier of \autoref{def:metric-classifier-specification}.
If
$$
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P},net)
$$
holds, then the row obtained by restricting only the source-point coverage
quantifier satisfies
$$
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}_{X\downarrow Y}(\varepsilon,\mathcal{P},net|_Y).
$$
Moreover, if every bundled center is also carried by $Y$,
$$
\forall p:\mathsf{BHist}.\;\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P})\to Y(c_p),
$$
then the same restricted row is an ordinary
$\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(Y,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P},net|_Y)$ for
the induced metric subcarrier.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TotallyBoundedUp.TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet\_subcarrier\_restriction}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the accepted row for
$\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P},net)$ using
\autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier}. It supplies the positive
$\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$, the closed
$\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine $\mathcal{P}$, the bundled center histories
$c_p$ carried by $X$, the positive radius histories $r_p$, and the coverage row
for every source-point history carried by $X$.

The subcarrier-restricted row keeps all bundle-indexed data unchanged. The
positive tolerance row, the closed bundle spine, the
$\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P})$ witnesses, the ambient center rows
$X(c_p)$, and the radius rows are exactly the rows already present in $net$.
These clauses do not inspect whether the covered source point is taken from
$X$ or from the subcarrier $Y$.

It remains to verify coverage for a carried point of $Y$. Fix
$y:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $Y(y)$. The inclusion row gives $X(y)$. Applying the
coverage clause of $net$ to this ambient source point gives a bundled probe
$p$, a membership row $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P})$, a distance history
$d_p$, a metric row
$$
\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(y,c_p,d_p),
$$
and a rational comparison ledger
$$
d_p\leq_{\RatUp}\varepsilon.
$$
The restricted row uses precisely these witnesses for the same point $y$.
Thus every $Y$-carried point is covered by the same closed bundle
$\mathcal{P}$ with the same distance and bound ledgers.

The classifier-stability row $\sigma_Y$ records that the induced carrier is a
legitimate classifier-stable subcarrier: replacing a $Y$-carried endpoint by an
ambient-classified endpoint keeps it in $Y$. No center or distance ledger is
altered by this restriction step.

If the additional center-carrier rows
$\forall p.\;\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P})\to Y(c_p)$ are supplied, then the
only remaining difference between the relative row and the ordinary
$\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}$ row over $Y$ disappears. Replace each
ambient center clause $X(c_p)$ by the supplied subcarrier center clause
$Y(c_p)$, and keep the same positive tolerance, bundle spine, radius rows,
coverage witnesses, metric-distance rows, and rational bound ledgers. These are
exactly the fields required for
$\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(Y,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P},net|_Y)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[ProbeBundle append membership row separation]
\label{lem:probebundle-append-membership-row-separation}
Let $\mathcal{P}_X$ and $\mathcal{P}_Y$ be closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spines. For every probe history $p$, a visible membership row
$$
\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathsf{bundleAppend}(\mathcal{P}_X,\mathcal{P}_Y))
$$
determines either a left membership row $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P}_X)$ or a right membership row $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P}_Y)$. Conversely, every left or right membership row injects into the appended bundle. The alternatives are row alternatives: if the same probe name occurs on both sides, the left and right ledger branches remain distinct membership rows.
\end{lemma}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TotallyBoundedUp.TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet\_append\_membership\_separation}
\begin{proof}
Induct on the closed spine $\mathcal{P}_X$. If $\mathcal{P}_X=\Bnil$, then $\mathsf{bundleAppend}(\Bnil,\mathcal{P}_Y)$ is $\mathcal{P}_Y$. Thus an appended membership row is exactly a right membership row, and the converse right injection is the identity. There is no left membership row in the empty bundle.

For the cons case write $\mathcal{P}_X=\Bcons(q,\mathcal{P}_0)$. A membership row in
$$
\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathsf{bundleAppend}(\Bcons(q,\mathcal{P}_0),\mathcal{P}_Y))
$$
is either the head equality row $p=q$ or a tail row in $\mathsf{bundleAppend}(\mathcal{P}_0,\mathcal{P}_Y)$. The head equality gives the left membership row in $\Bcons(q,\mathcal{P}_0)$. For a tail row, apply the induction hypothesis. A left-tail result is reinserted by the tail constructor of $\mathsf{InBundle}$ for $\Bcons(q,\mathcal{P}_0)$, and a right result remains a right membership row in $\mathcal{P}_Y$.

The converse injections are obtained by the same constructors. A left head row maps to the appended head row. A left tail row first injects into $\mathsf{bundleAppend}(\mathcal{P}_0,\mathcal{P}_Y)$ by the induction hypothesis and then into the appended cons tail. A right row first injects into $\mathsf{bundleAppend}(\mathcal{P}_0,\mathcal{P}_Y)$ and then into the appended cons tail. This proves both directions and keeps duplicated probe names separated by their visible branch rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite union of totally bounded ProbeBundle nets]
\label{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-finite-union}
Let $X,Y:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be metric BEDC carriers over the same $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ rows and the same metric classifier of \autoref{def:metric-classifier-specification}. Define the union carrier by
$$
(X\cup Y)(z):\Longleftrightarrow X(z)\vee Y(z).
$$
Assume
$$
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P}_X,net_X)
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(Y,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P}_Y,net_Y)
$$
in the sense of \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier}. Let
$$
\mathcal{P}_{X\cup Y}:=\mathsf{bundleAppend}(\mathcal{P}_X,\mathcal{P}_Y).
$$
Then the union carrier $X\cup Y$ carries a totally bounded ProbeBundle net at the same tolerance $\varepsilon$ with probe bundle $\mathcal{P}_{X\cup Y}$. Its ledger $net_{X\cup Y}$ is obtained by reading each appended membership row through \autoref{lem:probebundle-append-membership-row-separation}: left rows use the corresponding fields of $net_X$, and right rows use the corresponding fields of $net_Y$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the two accepted rows by \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier}. The row $net_X$ supplies a positive $\NameCert_{\RatUp}$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$, the closed bundle $\mathcal{P}_X$, center histories, positive radius histories, and coverage rows for all points carried by $X$. The row $net_Y$ supplies the same fields over $Y$ and $\mathcal{P}_Y$ at the same tolerance history. Use either positive-tolerance row for the union net.

The finite carrier of the union net is the closed spine $\mathsf{bundleAppend}(\mathcal{P}_X,\mathcal{P}_Y)$. Since $\mathsf{bundleAppend}$ is generated by recursion on the left closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine, the result is again a closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$.

Fix an appended probe row
$$
\alpha:\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P}_{X\cup Y}).
$$
By \autoref{lem:probebundle-append-membership-row-separation}, $\alpha$ is read as either a left row $\alpha_X:\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P}_X)$ or a right row $\alpha_Y:\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P}_Y)$. In the left case, copy from $net_X$ the center history, positive radius history, and center-carrier row attached to $\alpha_X$; the center is carried by $X\cup Y$ by the left union injection. In the right case, copy the corresponding fields from $net_Y$ and use the right union injection. If the same probe name appears in both bundles, these are still two ledger branches selected by the visible membership row, so no side-disjointness assumption is required.

It remains to verify coverage. Let $z$ be a history carried by $X\cup Y$. If $X(z)$ holds, the coverage clause of $net_X$ supplies a probe $p$, a row $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P}_X)$, a center history, a distance history $d$, a metric row
$$
\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(z,c_p,d),
$$
and a rational comparison ledger $d\leq_{\RatUp}\varepsilon$. Inject the left membership row into $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{P}_{X\cup Y})$ using \autoref{lem:probebundle-append-membership-row-separation}, and keep the same center, distance, metric witness, and bound ledger. The center-carrier row is promoted from $X$ to $X\cup Y$ by the left union injection.

If $Y(z)$ holds, apply the same argument to $net_Y$, inject the right membership row into the appended bundle, and promote the center-carrier row by the right union injection. Thus every source point of $X\cup Y$ is covered by a bundled center in $\mathcal{P}_{X\cup Y}$ at distance bounded by the same tolerance $\varepsilon$. All fields required by \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier} are present for
$$
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X\cup Y,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P}_{X\cup Y},net_{X\cup Y}).
$$
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TotallyBoundedUp.TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet\_finite\_union}

\begin{theorem}[TotallyBoundedUp compact-metric component bridge]
\label{thm:totallybounded-compactmetric-component-bridge}
Let $X$ be a certified metric carrier, and suppose that each positive
$\RatUp$ tolerance history $\varepsilon$ is assigned a row
$$
\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P}_{\varepsilon},net_{\varepsilon})
$$
as in \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier}. This family supplies exactly the totally-bounded component required by
$$
\mathsf{CompactMetricCertificate}(X)
$$
in \autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}: the exported carrier is the same carrier $X$; the finite carrier at tolerance $\varepsilon$ is the closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine $\mathcal{P}_{\varepsilon}$; membership is read only through rows of $\mathsf{InBundle}$; coverage is witnessed by the displayed $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ rows in $net_{\varepsilon}$; and every bound is one of the displayed $\RatUp$ comparison ledgers against $\varepsilon$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier component is fixed before the net is read. By \autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}, the compact-metric certificate asks for a totally-bounded field over the same certified metric carrier $X$. By \autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-namecert-target}, each accepted $\mathsf{TotallyBoundedProbeBundleNet}(X,\varepsilon,\mathcal{P}_{\varepsilon},net_{\varepsilon})$ row exports the same target carrier $X$, so no carrier translation or quotienting step is present.

The finite component is the closed probe spine already carried by the totally-bounded row. The unpacking specified in \autoref{def:totallybounded-probebundle-net-carrier} exposes $\mathcal{P}_{\varepsilon}$ as a closed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, and every center or probe lookup is keyed by a visible $\mathsf{InBundle}$ row. Thus the compact-metric field consumes the bundle ledger rather than an ambient finite set or an untracked list of representatives.

The coverage and bound components are also copied without changing their meaning. For each point history carried by $X$, \autoref{thm:totallybounded-probebundle-net-coverage-stability} keeps the coverage witness stable under the stated metric carrier interface: the same center row, the same $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ distance row, and the same $\RatUp$ comparison ledger against $\varepsilon$ are the rows consumed by the totally-bounded clause of $\mathsf{CompactMetricCertificate}(X)$.

Therefore the bridge row contributes only the totally-bounded component named in \autoref{def:compactmetric-certificate-carrier}. It does not assert open-cover compactness, does not select a finite subcover, and does not introduce any host-level choice principle.
\end{proof}
