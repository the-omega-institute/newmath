\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyFilterUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CauchyFilterUp$ interface names the finite filterbase surface used by
the real-completion boundary. Following Bishop-style Cauchy completion and
the Kelley--Bourbaki language of filters and directed bases, the BEDC object
keeps only explicit finite rows: directed finite-tail dyadic neighbourhood
evidence over regular Cauchy observations, refinement ledgers, modulus rows,
and handoff rows for $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$,
$\RealBallUp$, and $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[CauchyFilter finite-filterbase carrier]
\label{def:cauchyfilter-finite-filterbase-carrier}
A $\CauchyFilterUp$ finite-filterbase carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$
packet
$$
F=(B,R,M,L,P)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is a finite list of directed finite-tail neighbourhood packets,
  each packet carrying a $\StreamNameUp$ scheduled tail window, a
  $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular Cauchy observation row, and a nonempty finite family
  of $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ dyadic neighbourhood witnesses;
\item $R$ is a refinement ledger whose row for two displayed base packets
  names a third displayed tail packet refining both requested packets through
  a smaller dyadic tolerance window;
\item $M$ is a $\CauchyModulusUp$ row assigning to each requested precision a
  finite tail packet after which all displayed dyadic neighbourhood witnesses
  around the regular Cauchy observations are within the requested radius;
\item $L$ is the ledger exposing the base-packet rows, the refinement rows,
  the modulus rows, the componentwise $\hsame$ comparisons, the dyadic
  tolerance inclusions, and the displayed $\Cont$ routes used by refinements
  and precision reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\RegSeqRatUp$,
  $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealBallUp$, and $\RealUp$ certificate
  boundaries consumed by the packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts only these finite rows. Classification is componentwise
transport of $B$, $R$, $M$, $L$, and $P$ through the displayed $\hsame$ and
$\Cont$ data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilter directed refinement obligation]
\label{thm:cauchyfilter-directed-refinement-obligation}
For every accepted $\CauchyFilterUp$ carrier $F=(B,R,M,L,P)$ and every two
tail packets displayed in $B$, the refinement row in $R$ is a displayed
$\Cont$ and $\hsame$ obligation: it names a finite tail packet in the same
carrier whose dyadic neighbourhood rows refine both inputs through the
continuation routes recorded by $L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier through
\autoref{def:cauchyfilter-finite-filterbase-carrier}. The row $R$ is stored
with the tail packets it refines, so the two input packets and the refined
packet are all visible finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows over the same regular
Cauchy observation surface.

Read the continuation routes from $L$. Each route records how the refined
packet restricts to the requested input packet, and the accompanying $\hsame$
rows compare the displayed dyadic neighbourhood witnesses componentwise.

Thus directedness is not a global existence assertion. It is exactly the
finite refinement row already stored in the carrier, together with its
displayed $\Cont$ and $\hsame$ witnesses.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilter Cauchy modulus obligation]
\label{thm:cauchyfilter-cauchy-modulus-obligation}
Let $F=(B,R,M,L,P)$ be an accepted $\CauchyFilterUp$ carrier. The modulus row
$M$ makes $F$ stable under smaller dyadic tail windows: for each displayed
precision request, $M$ selects a finite tail packet, and every later
refinement row in $R$ carries regular Cauchy observations whose
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ neighbourhood ledger is included in the requested radius.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a precision request and project the selected row of $M$. It names a tail
packet in $B$ and records the requested dyadic radius through the
$\CauchyModulusUp$ boundary.

Now take any later refinement row displayed by $R$. By
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-directed-refinement-obligation}, the later packet
refines the selected packet through explicit $\Cont$ routes and componentwise
$\hsame$ comparisons.

The dyadic distance bound is read from the same finite ledger $L$. Since the
bound is attached to the selected precision and transported along the displayed
refinement rows, the carrier supplies a constructive Cauchy witness using only
its finite tail, modulus, regular observation, and dyadic neighbourhood rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilter NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:cauchyfilter-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyFilterUp}$ obligation surface consists of five
finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the finite-filterbase carrier of
\autoref{def:cauchyfilter-finite-filterbase-carrier};
\item classifier transport: accepted carriers compare by componentwise
$\hsame$ transport of the tail packets, regular observation rows, dyadic
neighbourhood witnesses, refinement ledgers, modulus rows, evidence ledgers,
and provenance rows, with reads routed through displayed $\Cont$ data;
\item directedness: the displayed refinement row of
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-directed-refinement-obligation};
\item Cauchy modulus: the precision-to-base selection and dyadic-radius
readback of \autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-cauchy-modulus-obligation};
\item downstream handoff: $\RealUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumers read only the
tail packets, stream schedules, regular Cauchy observations, dyadic
neighbourhood witnesses, real-ball bounds, refinement rows, modulus rows,
ledger rows, and provenance rows of the same finite packet.
\end{enumerate}
These five rows form the seed obligation for naming completion-facing Cauchy
filterbase data over displayed histories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row is
\autoref{def:cauchyfilter-finite-filterbase-carrier}. It supplies the finite
tail packets, regular observation rows, dyadic neighbourhood witnesses,
refinement ledger, Cauchy-modulus row, evidence ledger, and provenance row.

Classifier transport is componentwise. Transporting the five carrier
coordinates through their displayed $\hsame$ rows and following the recorded
$\Cont$ routes repacks the same finite-filterbase shape.

Directedness and the Cauchy property are the two distinguished projections of
the carrier. The first projection is
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-directed-refinement-obligation}; the second is
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-cauchy-modulus-obligation}.

For downstream readback, a $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer can cite only
the rows named by $P$ and exposed by $L$. Those rows are exactly the base
packets, stream schedules, regular Cauchy observations, dyadic neighbourhood
witnesses, real-ball bounds, refinement rows, modulus rows, ledger rows, and
provenance rows of the carrier, so the surface is exhausted by the five listed
obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilter carrier transport obligation]
\label{thm:cauchyfilter-carrier-transport-obligation}
Let $F=(B,R,M,L,P)$ and $F'=(B',R',M',L',P')$ be displayed
$\CauchyFilterUp$ finite-filterbase carriers. Suppose the evidence ledger
exhibits componentwise $\hsame$ rows for $B$ with $B'$, $R$ with $R'$, $M$
with $M'$, $L$ with $L'$, and $P$ with $P'$, and suppose every read used by a
refinement or precision request is routed through the displayed $\Cont$ rows.
Then classifier transport preserves the accepted carrier tuple: $F$ is
accepted exactly when the transported tuple $F'$ is accepted, and all accepted
reads remain inside the displayed finite rows $(B',R',M',L',P')$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the finite carrier of
\autoref{def:cauchyfilter-finite-filterbase-carrier}. Acceptance is expressed
only through the five displayed coordinates $B$, $R$, $M$, $L$, and $P$.

Transport is componentwise. The tail packets, regular observation rows, dyadic
neighbourhood witnesses, refinement ledger, modulus row, evidence ledger, and
provenance row are compared by the named $\hsame$ witnesses, while each
refinement or precision read follows a displayed $\Cont$ route recorded in the
ledger.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-namecert-obligation-surface} to identify the
classifier row as the transport row of the local $\NameCert$ surface. The
transported carrier therefore has exactly the same finite-filterbase shape,
with rows read from $(B',R',M',L',P')$.

No host filter, selected limit, quotient real, or ambient completeness object
is introduced by this transport. The construction moves only the displayed
finite rows and their recorded $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ witnesses.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilter refinement directedness package]
\label{thm:cauchyfilter-refinement-directedness-package}
For an accepted $\CauchyFilterUp$ carrier $F=(B,R,M,L,P)$, the directedness
obligation is the finite package consisting of the two input tail packets in
$B$, the selected third tail packet named by the row of $R$, the two displayed
$\Cont$ routes from that selected packet to the inputs, the componentwise
$\hsame$ witnesses for the transported dyadic neighbourhood rows, and the
modulus row of $M$ that reads the selected packet at every requested precision.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Choose two displayed tail packets in $B$. By
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-directed-refinement-obligation}, the corresponding
row of $R$ names a third displayed tail packet together with the continuation
routes and componentwise comparison rows needed to refine both inputs.

The selected packet is still a tail packet of the same finite carrier, so the
modulus row can be read without leaving the displayed surface. Apply
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-cauchy-modulus-obligation} to attach the requested
precision row and its dyadic-radius ledger to the same refinement package.

The resulting object is a directedness package, not an ambient filter
principle. Every component is one of the rows already listed in
\autoref{def:cauchyfilter-finite-filterbase-carrier} and exposed by
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-namecert-obligation-surface}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilter Real and RegSeqRat handoff non-escape]
\label{thm:cauchyfilter-real-regseq-handoff-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer of an accepted $\CauchyFilterUp$
carrier $F=(B,R,M,L,P)$ reads only the displayed tail packets, the
$\StreamNameUp$ schedules, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular observation rows, the
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ neighbourhood rows, the
$\RealBallUp$ bounds, the refinement rows, the Cauchy-modulus rows, the ledger
rows, and the $\Pkg$ provenance rows named by $L$ and $P$. The handoff does
not read a host filter, a selected limit, a quotient real, or an ambient
completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the downstream handoff row from
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-namecert-obligation-surface}. It lists the rows
available to $\RealUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumers: tail packets, stream
schedules, regular Cauchy observations, dyadic neighbourhood rows, real-ball
bounds, refinement rows, modulus rows, ledger rows, and provenance rows.

The carrier definition fixes where these rows live. By
\autoref{def:cauchyfilter-finite-filterbase-carrier}, the base packets are in
$B$, refinement data is in $R$, precision data is in $M$, read evidence is in
$L$, and downstream provenance is in $P$.

Directed refinement and precision readback do not add further carrier data.
They are the displayed rows supplied by
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-directed-refinement-obligation} and
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-cauchy-modulus-obligation}.

Thus the Real and regular rational sequence handoff is closed under the named
finite rows. Since no step constructs or selects an external filter limit, the
handoff cannot escape through host filters, selected limits, quotient reals,
or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilter L10 dependency handoff lattice]
\label{thm:cauchyfilter-l10-dependency-handoff-lattice}
Every accepted $\CauchyFilterUp$ handoff is a horizontal L10 dependency
lattice for the real-completion surface. Its tail packets are read through
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, its dyadic neighbourhood
witnesses through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, its
regular readbacks through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert},
and its final seal through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The
lattice is exhausted by the finite carrier rows, directed refinement rows,
Cauchy-modulus rows, ledger evidence, and provenance rows of the accepted
carrier; it contains no host filter, selected limit, quotient real, or ambient
completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $F=(B,R,M,L,P)$. The carrier definition
\autoref{def:cauchyfilter-finite-filterbase-carrier} places every tail packet
in the displayed finite row $B$, and each such packet carries its
$\BHist$ evidence, $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular
observation row, dyadic neighbourhood rows, real-ball bounds, ledger reads,
and downstream provenance. Hence the horizontal tail layer of the handoff is
read through the StreamName and DyadicRatCore concrete-instance chapters cited
in the statement.

The middle layer is forced by the two obligations of the accepted carrier.
Apply \autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-directed-refinement-obligation} to read a
displayed refinement row of $R$ whenever two tail packets are compared, and
apply \autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-cauchy-modulus-obligation} to read the
displayed precision row of $M$ together with its retained dyadic-radius ledger.
These rows are still entries of $B$, $R$, $M$, and $L$, so they add refinement
and Cauchy control without introducing a new completion object.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-real-regseq-handoff-nonescape}. It identifies the
regular-sequence and real handoff boundary as exactly the displayed tail,
stream, regular observation, dyadic, real-ball, refinement, modulus, ledger,
and $\Pkg$ provenance rows. Therefore the RegSeqRat readback layer and the
Real sealing layer are the cited sibling concrete-instance dependencies, and
the whole L10 handoff is closed before any host filter, selected limit,
quotient real, or ambient completeness principle can be read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilter uniform-space completion handoff]
\label{thm:cauchyfilter-uniformspace-completion-handoff}
Let $F=(B,R,M,L,P)$ be an accepted $\CauchyFilterUp$ finite-filterbase
carrier whose provenance row exposes the same finite entourage and
filterbase surface consumed by $\UniformSpaceUp$. Every $\CompletionUp$ or
$\RealUp$ read admitted from $F$ factors first through the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ neighbourhood witnesses,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows, $\RealBallUp$ bounds, and the finite $\UniformSpaceUp$
entourage rows before any completion-facing seal is available. Consequently
the handoff described in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}
and consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completion-namecert} and
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert} receives only the finite
ledger displayed by the Cauchy filterbase carrier, not a selected limit,
quotient stream, host filter, or ambient completeness object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $F=(B,R,M,L,P)$. The base row $B$ supplies the displayed
tail packets, $R$ supplies directed refinement, $M$ supplies Cauchy
precision, $L$ supplies ledger reads, and $P$ supplies provenance. By
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-l10-dependency-handoff-lattice}, these rows already
identify the StreamName windows, DyadicRatCore neighbourhood witnesses,
RegSeqRat readback rows, RealBall bounds, ledger evidence, and real-facing
provenance.

Next route the finite filterbase as uniform entourage data. Apply
\autoref{thm:uniformspace-cauchy-completion-handoff}: the UniformSpaceUp
interface exposes only finite entourage rows, diagonal rows, refinement rows,
symmetry rows, composition rows, endpoint $\hsame$ transport, and $\Pkg$
provenance. Since the provenance row of $F$ exposes precisely this surface,
the CauchyFilterUp read may be consumed by the UniformSpaceUp handoff without
adding a further carrier coordinate.

The completion-facing consumer therefore receives the displayed finite
ledger in the stated order: stream windows, dyadic observations, regular
readback, real-ball bounds, and uniform entourage rows. None of the preceding
projections constructs a chosen limit, quotient stream equality, host filter,
or ambient completeness witness, so no such object can be read before the
completion-facing seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[CauchyFilter finite subfilterbase restriction datum]
\label{def:cauchyfilter-finite-subfilterbase-restriction-datum}
Let $F=(B,R,M,L,P)$ be an accepted $\CauchyFilterUp$ finite-filterbase
carrier in the sense of \autoref{def:cauchyfilter-finite-filterbase-carrier}.
A finite subfilterbase restriction datum for $F$ is a displayed finite sublist
$B'\subseteq B$ together with two preservation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item refinement preservation: whenever $b_0$ and $b_1$ are displayed in $B'$
and the row of $R$ for $(b_0,b_1)$ names a refined tail packet $b_2$, the
packet $b_2$ is displayed in $B'$ and the corresponding $\Cont$ and $\hsame$
witnesses in $L$ are retained in the restricted ledger;
\item modulus preservation: for every precision request admitted by the
carrier, the tail packet selected by the row $M$ is displayed in $B'$, and the
associated dyadic-radius readback row of $L$ is retained in the restricted
ledger.
\end{enumerate}
The restricted packet is
$$
F|_{B'}=(B',R|_{B'},M|_{B'},L|_{B'},P),
$$
where $R|_{B'}$ keeps exactly the refinement rows whose two inputs lie in
$B'$, $M|_{B'}$ keeps the same precision-to-base selections, $L|_{B'}$ keeps
only the ledger rows used by those tail, regular observation, dyadic
neighbourhood, refinement, modulus, $\Cont$, and $\hsame$ reads, and $P$ is
the unchanged downstream provenance row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyFilter finite subfilterbase restriction]
\label{thm:cauchyfilter-finite-subfilterbase-restriction}
Let $F=(B,R,M,L,P)$ be an accepted $\CauchyFilterUp$ finite-filterbase carrier.
If $B'\subseteq B$ is a finite subfilterbase restriction datum for $F$ in the
sense of \autoref{def:cauchyfilter-finite-subfilterbase-restriction-datum},
then the restricted packet
$$
F|_{B'}=(B',R|_{B'},M|_{B'},L|_{B'},P)
$$
is an accepted $\CauchyFilterUp$ finite-filterbase carrier. Its downstream
handoff boundary is the same finite $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealBallUp$, and $\RealUp$ provenance boundary carried by
$F$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier rows are finite because $B'$ is a displayed finite sublist of the
finite tail row $B$, while $R|_{B'}$, $M|_{B'}$, and $L|_{B'}$ are obtained by
retaining displayed rows from the finite ledgers $R$, $M$, and $L$ of
\autoref{def:cauchyfilter-finite-filterbase-carrier}. Each tail packet in
$B'$ is already a tail packet of $B$, hence it carries the same $\BHist$ row,
$\StreamNameUp$ scheduled window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular observation row, and
nonempty finite family of $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ neighbourhood rows that it carried
in $F$.

Directedness is inherited row by row. Take two tail packets displayed in
$B'$. In the accepted carrier $F$, \autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-directed-refinement-obligation}
reads the corresponding row of $R$ as a displayed refinement packet together
with its $\Cont$ routes and componentwise $\hsame$ witnesses. Refinement
preservation in the restriction datum says that this refined packet is again
displayed in $B'$ and that the required continuation and comparison witnesses
remain in $L|_{B'}$. Therefore $R|_{B'}$ supplies exactly the directed
refinement obligation required of the restricted carrier.

The Cauchy modulus row is also inherited without changing the selected bases.
For a displayed precision request, \autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-cauchy-modulus-obligation}
reads the row of $M$ in $F$ as a selected tail packet together with the dyadic
radius ledger controlling every later displayed refinement. Modulus
preservation says that the selected packet lies in $B'$ and that the associated
radius readback row remains in $L|_{B'}$. If a later refinement is read inside
$R|_{B'}$, it is one of the refinement rows of $R$, so the same dyadic bound
from $L$ applies to it, and the retained row in $L|_{B'}$ records that bound.
Thus $M|_{B'}$ is a constructive Cauchy modulus for the restricted packet.

It remains to identify the handoff boundary. The restriction does not replace
$P$; it only removes base, refinement, modulus, and ledger rows not used by
$B'$. Hence a downstream $\RealUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer can still read
exactly the provenance categories listed in
\autoref{thm:cauchyfilter-namecert-obligation-surface}: tail packets, stream
schedules, regular Cauchy observations, dyadic neighbourhood rows, real-ball
bounds, refinement rows, modulus rows, ledger rows, and the same $\Pkg$
provenance boundary. Since every retained row is a displayed row of the
accepted carrier and the two preservation conditions keep all refinement and
modulus rows needed for the CauchyFilter obligations, $F|_{B'}$ repacks as an
accepted $\CauchyFilterUp$ carrier with the same finite downstream handoff
boundary.
\end{proof}
