\begin{definition}[Located sequence carrier]
\label{def:located-sequence-carrier}
A $\LocatedSequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(S,R,D,L,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ row, exposing exactly the finite sequence windows that may be inspected by a consumer.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, repacking the inspected stream windows as regular rational sequence data.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used when located windows are compared, tightened, or handed to a modulus consumer.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedRealUp$ window row, recording the located interval or ball evidence available at the displayed sequence indices.
\item $Q$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the stream, regular rational, dyadic, and located-real rows have been projected.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for stream windows, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerances, located-real windows, real seals, continuation, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite sequence request through
$$
S,D,L \longmapsto R \longmapsto Q .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedSequenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedSequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts only packets in which the displayed stream windows, dyadic tolerance budgets, located-real windows, regular rational readback, and real seal describe one finite route. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected representatives, host real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located sequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-sequence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedSequenceUp$ carrier $A=(S,R,D,L,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedSequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of stream-window admission, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance accounting, located-real window coverage, real-seal routing, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming through the rows $S,R,D,L,Q,H,C,P,N$. Consumers from $\LocatedSequenceModulusUp$, $\LocatedSequenceLimitUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, and $\RealUp$ may read the packet only through those displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-sequence-carrier}. The sequence is visible only through $S$, and every tolerance-facing comparison is visible only through the dyadic row $D$ and the located-real row $L$.

The readback row $R$ repacks the same inspected windows as $\RegSeqRatUp$ data. The real seal $Q$ is downstream of $S,D,L,R$, so a real-facing consumer reaches it only after replaying the displayed located route.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are supplied by $H,C,P,N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected representatives, host equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness, the listed NameCert rows are exhaustive.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located sequence regular rational readback]
\label{thm:located-sequence-regseqrat-readback}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\LocatedSequenceUp$ carrier must read
$$
S,D,L \longmapsto R \longmapsto Q \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus regular rational readback is a displayed finite route from stream windows and located dyadic tolerances to the real seal, not a selected quotient stream, not host real equality, not a trichotomy principle, and not ambient real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The row $R$ is the only $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback surface. Therefore a located-real or real-facing consumer reaches the terminal seal $Q$ only after the finite route through $S,D,L,R$ has been displayed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve that route by transport, replay, source, and local naming. No excluded analytic object is present in the tuple, so no downstream consumer can extract one from the packet.
\end{proof}
