\begin{theorem}[RealSeries finite partial-sum carrier]
\label{thm:realseries-root-unblock-carrier}
For an accepted $\RealSeriesUp$ carrier $K=(T,S,W,Q,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)$, every downstream root-facing read of finite partial sums is already determined by the displayed $\BHist$ rows $T,S,W,Q,D,M,E,H,C,P,N$. The row $T$ supplies the term stream, $S$ supplies the finite partial-sum windows, $W$ supplies selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $Q$ supplies $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, $D$ supplies the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, $M$ supplies the shared threshold, $E$ supplies the endpoint seal, and $H,C,P,N$ provide componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:real-series-carrier}. The finite partial-sum data is stored in $S$ and is read only after the term stream $T$ has been admitted.

Route those finite sums through the selected windows $W$ and regular readback $Q$. The dyadic ledger $D$ and shared threshold $M$ then account for the comparisons before the endpoint row $E$ is available.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this same finite route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host infinite sums, selected limits, quotient equality, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, no root-facing read can bypass the displayed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealSeries tail ledger]
\label{thm:realseries-tail-ledger}
For every accepted $\RealSeriesUp$ carrier $K=(T,S,W,Q,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)$, each tail comparison exported to a $\RealSeriesCauchyCriterionUp$, $\UniformCauchySeriesUp$, $\CauchyMajorantSeriesUp$, or $\CauchyProductUp$ consumer factors through the single ledger chain
$$
S\longmapsto W\longmapsto Q\longmapsto D\longmapsto M
$$
together with $T,E,H,C,P,N$. In particular, the threshold row $M$ is shared across the exported tail requests and cannot be replaced by a family of selected tail indices.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealSeriesUp.RealSeriesTailLedger}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite partial-sum windows $S$. A tail request selects only finitely many windows through $W$ and reads the corresponding rational data through $Q$.

The comparisons are then recorded in the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$. The shared threshold $M$ is the only row admitted after $D$, so all exported tail bounds use the same displayed threshold surface.

Endpoint access through $E$ and structural access through $H,C,P,N$ preserve that route. The downstream consumers receive a tail ledger over finite rows, not a host infinite-sum principle or a chosen sequence of tail witnesses.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealSeries endpoint readback]
\label{thm:realseries-endpoint-readback}
Every endpoint read of an accepted $\RealSeriesUp$ carrier $K=(T,S,W,Q,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)$ reaches $\RealUp$ through $E$ only after the finite rows $T,S,W,Q,D,M$ have been displayed. The readback route is therefore
$$
T,S,W,Q,D,M \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N,
$$
with $\BHist$ admission, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming attached to the same packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the endpoint row from the carrier only after the term stream, finite partial sums, selected windows, regular readback, dyadic ledger, and shared threshold have been projected.

Apply \autoref{thm:real-series-endpoint-nonescape-obligation}. It places every $\RealUp$ endpoint seal behind $T,S,W,Q,D,M$ and refuses selected limits, quotient equality of partial-sum streams, countable choice, and ambient completeness.

Finally replay the endpoint read through $H,C,P,N$. These rows preserve and name the finite packet; they do not provide a second endpoint channel outside the displayed $\RealSeriesUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealSeries root tail factorization]
\label{thm:realseries-root-tail-factorization}
For an accepted $\RealSeriesUp$ carrier $K=(T,S,W,Q,D,M,E,H,C,P,N)$, every root-facing Cauchy consumer factors partial sums through finite tail windows before any convergence claim is visible. The forced route is
$$
S\longmapsto W\longmapsto Q\longmapsto D\longmapsto M\longmapsto E,
$$
where $S$ supplies the partial-sum ledger, $W$ supplies the selected $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $Q$ supplies $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback for terms and partial sums, $D$ supplies the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, $M$ supplies the shared tail threshold, and $E$ supplies the $\RealUp$ seal only after those rows have been displayed.

Thus a downstream $\RealSeriesCauchyCriterionUp$, $\UniformCauchySeriesUp$, $\CauchyMajorantSeriesUp$, or $\CauchyProductUp$ consumer receives a finite BEDC tail-window certificate. It receives no host infinite sum, selected limit, quotient equality of partial-sum streams, countable-choice tail selector, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the partial-sum row $S$. It names the finite windows of partial sums that a root-facing consumer may inspect, and it is already tied to the term stream $T$ by the carrier of \autoref{def:real-series-carrier}.

Move from $S$ to selected windows and regular readback. The row $W$ records the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows, and $Q$ records the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback for each displayed term and partial-sum window.

The Cauchy comparison is then ledgered. The row $D$ records dyadic tolerances and finite addition errors, while the shared threshold row $M$ is the only admitted tail gate after those comparisons.

Finally the endpoint seal $E$ is reached only after the finite route has been displayed. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route, so no convergence-facing consumer can bypass finite tail windows for a host infinite-sum or completeness principle.
\end{proof}
