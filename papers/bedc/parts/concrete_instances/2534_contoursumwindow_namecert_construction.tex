\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ContourSumWindowUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-contoursumwindow-namecert}

The $\ContourSumWindowUp$ interface gives the contour-integral operation a finite window packet that downstream analytic chapters can inspect without importing a host integration operator. It records one piecewise-linear contour row, one holomorphic input row, a finite subdivision and sample ledger, the resulting Riemann-sum value, the output complex-history row, transport and continuation rows, package provenance, and a local naming row.

\begin{definition}[ContourSumWindow carrier]
\label{def:contour-sum-window-carrier}
A $\ContourSumWindowUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(\gamma,f,S,R,I,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\gamma$ is the $\mathsf{PLContour}$ row naming the finite endpoint list and segment history used by the contour read;
\item $f$ is the $\HolomorphicUp$ input row on the displayed neighbourhood of $\gamma$;
\item $S$ is the finite subdivision and sample row, listing the segment subdivision, sample point, and local mesh witness for every segment;
\item $R$ is the Riemann-sum ledger, recording the finite summands, the mesh comparison, and the value computed from $f$ on the samples of $S$;
\item $I$ is the output $\ComplexUp$ history row classified as the window value associated to the displayed ledger $R$;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row for $\gamma$, $f$, $S$, $R$, and $I$;
\item $C$ is the finite $\Cont$ route row carrying subdivision refinement, contour endpoint transport, holomorphic-input transport, and output readback;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\mathsf{PLContour}$, $\HolomorphicUp$, finite subdivision rows, Riemann-sum rows, $\ComplexUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ContourSumWindowUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ContourSumWindow NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:contour-sum-window-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ContourSumWindowUp$ carrier $W=(\gamma,f,S,R,I,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ContourSumWindowUp}$ surface consists exactly of finite carrier admission, subdivision ledger exactness, contour route stability, output readback through $I$, package provenance through $P$, and non-escape from host integration, open-cover compactness, selected limits, quotient values, or unlisted analytic function-space objects.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:contour-sum-window-carrier}. Carrier admission is the display of $\gamma,f,S,R,I,H,C,P,N$ as rows of one finite $\BHist$ packet, so no consumer can read a contour, integrand, subdivision, sum, or output row outside that list.

Read the subdivision and Riemann-sum coordinates next. The row $S$ supplies the finite segment subdivision and sample data, while $R$ supplies the summand ledger and mesh comparison attached to those samples. Exactness is therefore ledger exactness for the listed rows, not convergence in an ambient integration theory.

Finally apply the displayed transport and continuation rows. The $\hsame$ row transports the contour, input, ledger, and output componentwise, and the $\Cont$ row records only the finite routes named in the packet. Since no coordinate names host integration, compactness, selected limits, or quotient values, the local $\NameCert$ exports only the finite window surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ContourSumWindow Cauchy boundary]
\label{thm:contour-sum-window-cauchy-boundary}
Every Cauchy-facing read of an accepted $\ContourSumWindowUp$ carrier factors through the same $\mathsf{PLContour}$ row $\gamma$, the same $\HolomorphicUp$ row $f$, the displayed subdivision row $S$, the Riemann-sum ledger $R$, the $\ComplexUp$ output row $I$, and the visible $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. The boundary exports only finite contour-window data and does not assert a host Cauchy integral theorem, a host contour integral, open-cover compactness, selected limiting values, quotient complex histories, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ContourSumWindowUp.ContourSumWindowTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite window packet. The only route from the contour and holomorphic input to an output row passes through the subdivision row $S$ and the Riemann-sum ledger $R$ before reaching the $\ComplexUp$ row $I$.

The Cauchy-facing consumer can therefore inspect the displayed ledger and its transports, but it cannot replace the finite ledger by a host integral or by a selected limiting process. Those objects are not coordinates of the carrier.

The non-escape clauses of \autoref{thm:contour-sum-window-namecert-obligations} close the boundary. They keep Cauchy-style cancellation and boundary reads inside the displayed contour, subdivision, Riemann-sum, output, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows, with no open-cover compactness or quotient-value surface added.
\end{proof}
