\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ArensFortSpaceUp$}

Following Arens and Fort, the $\ArensFortSpaceUp$ packet records the classical grid space with one non-isolated accumulation point as finite BEDC topology data. It sits beside the indexed-open carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, the two-point observable handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-sierpinski-space-namecert}, the metric-generated topology boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-topology-namecert}, and the finite observation windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}. The packet is a topology-generated boundary object: it names neighbourhood filters and observable truth reads without importing a metric presentation, a host powerset of opens, choice, or a completed ambient space.

\begin{definition}[Arens--Fort space carrier]
\label{def:arens-fort-space-carrier}
An $\ArensFortSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A_{\mathsf{af}}=(G,S,T,O,W,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the finite grid-point ledger. It records the distinguished point $\infty$ and the finitely inspected isolated points $(m,n)$, together with the local classifier that separates $\infty$ from the displayed grid rows.
\item $S$ is the special-point neighbourhood filter ledger. A row of $S$ names a finite stem and a tail threshold for each displayed column, so a neighbourhood of $\infty$ contains all but finitely many points in every named column and contains $\infty$ itself.
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ indexed-open carrier generated by the isolated singleton rows of $G$ and the special-point neighbourhood rows of $S$.
\item $O$ is the observable truth handoff into $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$: an open-membership read is exposed only as a carried observable truth row after the relevant isolated singleton or special-neighbourhood witness has been displayed.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family used to inspect finitely many grid coordinates, special-neighbourhood thresholds, and observable truth requests.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for grid rows, special-neighbourhood filters, indexed opens, observable truth rows, finite windows, replay rows, provenance, and the local certificate.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a public open-membership request through $W$, then through $G$ or $S$, then through $T$, and finally through the observable handoff $O$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ArensFortSpaceUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$, $\MetricTopologyUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ArensFortSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier admits packets only through these nine rows. It has no coordinate for a host powerset of grid points, a metric ball basis, a selected convergent sequence, an ambient compactness or completeness witness, or a choice principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Arens--Fort NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:arens-fort-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\ArensFortSpaceUp$ carrier $A_{\mathsf{af}}=(G,S,T,O,W,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\ArensFortSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed grid ledger, special-point neighbourhood filter, indexed-open carrier, observable truth handoff, finite windows, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming row;
\item isolated-point stability, requiring each displayed grid point $(m,n)$ to remain open by its singleton row under the componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay;
\item special-point neighbourhood exactness, requiring every open read at $\infty$ to exhibit the finite stem and column thresholds named by $S$ before the read is admitted by $T$;
\item topology-open transport, requiring the $\TopologyUp$ indexed-open row to move only through the isolated singleton constructors and special-neighbourhood constructors named in $G$ and $S$;
\item observable-open handoff, requiring every $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ truth read to factor through the same open-membership witness already carried by $T$ and inspected by $W$;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding host powersets, metric-generated ball bases, selected convergent sequences, ambient compactness, ambient completeness, choice, and any open classifier outside $G,S,T,O,W,H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:arens-fort-space-carrier}. The packet exposes only the grid ledger $G$, the special-neighbourhood filter $S$, the indexed topology row $T$, the observable handoff $O$, the finite windows $W$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Isolated-point stability is read directly from the singleton rows of $G$. Transport along $H$ preserves the displayed grid coordinate, and replay along $C$ keeps the singleton-open request attached to the same coordinate before $T$ admits it as an open.

For the special point, the only neighbourhood source is $S$. Thus every open read at $\infty$ must present the displayed finite stem and column-threshold data before the topology row $T$ can expose the neighbourhood as open.

Topology-open transport follows by constructor analysis on $T$. Its generators are exactly the isolated singleton rows and the special-neighbourhood rows, and the replay route never introduces a metric-ball basis or a host powerset of points.

The observable handoff is forced by $O$ and $W$. A $\SierpinskiSpaceUp$ truth value is readable only after the same finite window and the same carried open-membership witness have been displayed.

Finally, the non-escape clause is an inventory check on the carrier. Since metric balls, selected sequences, compactness, completeness, choice, host powersets, and detached open classifiers are not coordinates of $A_{\mathsf{af}}$, no local NameCert obligation can export them.
\end{proof}
