The trace packet sits between the obstruction boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-subject-reduction-obstruction-namecert} and the discharge-obligation ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-discharge-obligation-ledger-namecert}. \begin{definition}[MetaCIC subject-reduction trace carrier]
\label{def:metacic-subject-reduction-trace-carrier}
A $\MetaCICSubjectReductionTraceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(P,B,A,L,I,O,H,C,G,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P$ records the parameterized theorem row for $\mathsf{BEDC.MetaCIC.subject\_reduction}$, including the four hypothesis sockets it consumes;
\item $B$ records the beta-substitution obligation row $\mathrm{BetaSubstitutionPreservation}$;
\item $A$ records the application-argument-stability obligation row $\mathrm{AppArgTypeStable}$;
\item $L$ records the lambda-domain obligation row $\mathrm{LamDomainSubjectReduction}$;
\item $I$ records the Pi-domain obligation row $\mathrm{PiDomainSubjectReduction}$;
\item $O$ records the obstruction theorem row for $\mathsf{BEDC.MetaCIC.closed\_consistency\_reduction\_obstruction\_not\_available}$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the theorem row, the four obligation rows, the obstruction row, replay routes, provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that replay consumers through the parameterized theorem row, the four obligation rows, and the obstruction row;
\item $G$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$, and the MetaCIC subject-reduction audit rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICSubjectReductionTraceUp}$ row.

The carrier has no coordinate for an unparameterized closed subject-reduction theorem, a hidden proof of any of the four obligations, an implicit host-calculus preservation theorem, or an axiom that discharges the obstruction.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC subject-reduction trace NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-subject-reduction-trace-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetaCICSubjectReductionTraceUp$ carrier
$$
T=(P,B,A,L,I,O,H,C,G,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICSubjectReductionTraceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the parameterized theorem row $P$, the four discharge-obligation rows $B,A,L,I$, the checked obstruction row $O$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $G$, and local naming through $N$.

The packet records the four discharge obligations without proving them. A consumer that needs subject reduction must route through the parameterized theorem row $P$ and supply $B,A,L,I$; a consumer that asks for the closed statement is routed to the obstruction row $O$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet of \autoref{def:metacic-subject-reduction-trace-carrier}. The rows $P,B,A,L,I,O,H,C,G,N$ are the complete displayed inventory, so carrier admission and local naming are read directly from the packet.

The subject-reduction side is exhausted by $P$ together with $B,A,L,I$. The row $P$ names the checked parameterized theorem, while $B,A,L,I$ name the beta-substitution, application-argument-stability, lambda-domain, and Pi-domain sockets that must be supplied before that theorem yields a preservation step.

The obstruction side is exhausted by $O$. Since $O$ records the checked obstruction for the closed consistency-reduction proposition, the packet does not convert the four obligation rows into proofs and does not export a closed subject-reduction theorem.

Transport, replay, provenance, and naming are confined to $H,C,G,N$. Thus the local $\NameCert$ surface is exactly the displayed trace packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC subject-reduction trace non-escape]
\label{thm:metacic-subject-reduction-trace-nonescape}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\MetaCICSubjectReductionTraceUp$ packet factors through
$$
P,B,A,L,I,O,H,C,G,N.
$$
A preservation consumer may read the parameterized theorem row and the four named obligation rows. An audit consumer may read the checked obstruction row. No consumer can read a closed subject-reduction theorem, a hidden proof of any obligation, or an unrecorded host-calculus preservation principle from this packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the route row $C$. By the carrier definition, every public continuation route is displayed there and has endpoints among $P,B,A,L,I,O,H,G,N$.

Use the NameCert obligation surface of \autoref{thm:metacic-subject-reduction-trace-namecert-obligations}. It separates the parameterized theorem row from the four obligation rows and from the obstruction row, so a consumer cannot collapse the rows into a stronger theorem.

Componentwise transport through $H$ preserves the same row inventory. Package provenance through $G$ and local naming through $N$ bind the route to the finite packet, leaving no coordinate for a closed subject-reduction theorem or hidden discharge proof.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC subject-reduction trace discharge frontier]
\label{thm:metacic-subject-reduction-trace-discharge-frontier}
An accepted $\MetaCICSubjectReductionTraceUp$ packet excludes any closed subject-reduction theorem unless all four obligation rows
$$
B,A,L,I
$$
are supplied as discharge witnesses for the parameterized theorem row $P$. The obstruction row $O$ remains the consumer-facing answer for the closed statement whenever any one of $B,A,L,I$ is only a named obligation row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $P$ as the parameterized subject-reduction theorem row. Its visible interface consumes exactly the beta-substitution, application-argument-stability, lambda-domain, and Pi-domain discharge rows.

Read $B,A,L,I$ as obligation rows rather than proof rows. The carrier records their names and positions, but it does not add a witness that any one of them is discharged.

Now read $O$ as the checked obstruction row. If a consumer requests the closed theorem while any one of the four rows remains only an obligation, the replay route sends that request to $O$ rather than to a closed subject-reduction theorem.

Finally, $H,C,G,N$ preserve and package the same frontier. They transport, replay, and name the displayed rows, but they introduce no extra discharge coordinate.
\end{proof}
