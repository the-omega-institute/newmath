\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HaltingObstructionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-haltingobstruction-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\HaltingObstructionUp$ surface realizes the halting fixed-point pressure of \autoref{ch:visions-halting-as-form-of-distinction-limit} as a finite naming certificate. It records only positive finite traces, self-reference rows, diagonal policy rows, and certificate provenance. It is not a global halting classifier and it carries no decision procedure for arbitrary continuation chains.

The $\HaltingObstructionUp$ packet supplies the finite obstruction ledger consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdistinction-namecert} and the local fixed-point packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdiagonal-namecert}. This dependency is limited to finite trace, self-reference, diagonal-policy, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

\begin{definition}[Halting obstruction carrier]
\label{def:halting-obstruction-carrier}
A $\HaltingObstructionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the certificate row naming the local program-or-certificate surface whose continuation behavior is being inspected;
\item $I$ is the input-history row naming the starting history and its finite observation window;
\item $T$ is the finite $\Cont$ trace row recording the positive continuation steps actually witnessed from $I$;
\item $R$ is the self-reference row that identifies the history being fed back into the same certificate surface;
\item $D$ is the diagonal policy row recording the local non-escape ledger attached to that self-application;
\item $H$ stores the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the certificate, input history, finite trace, self-reference row, diagonal policy row, package row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reaches the trace, self-reference, and diagonal rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HaltingObstructionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for deciding whether an arbitrary chain halts, no total classifier over all certificates and histories, and no host-level oracle for divergence.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Halting obstruction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:halting-obstruction-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\HaltingObstructionUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:halting-obstruction-carrier} consists of five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation by the finite packet $O=(S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N)$;
\item trace exactness, saying that every positive continuation fact exported by the packet is one of the finite $\Cont$ steps recorded in $T$;
\item self-reference readability, saying that the feedback row $R$ is reached only through the displayed input-history and certificate rows $I$ and $S$;
\item diagonal policy locality, saying that each obstruction claim is the ledger entry named by $D$ after the displayed self-application route;
\item package and name closure, saying that all public reads pass through the stored $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{enumerate}
Thus the packet certifies a finite obstruction ledger for a displayed self-reference route, not a halting decision for every certificate-history pair.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:halting-obstruction-carrier}. The rows $S$, $I$, and $T$ give the only continuation facts that can be read: each exported step is one displayed finite $\Cont$ transition in the trace row.

Next read the self-reference coordinate $R$. It is not a classifier over all histories; it is a recorded feedback row tied to the same certificate and input-history surface by the transports in $H$ and the routes in $C$.

The diagonal row $D$ is then local to that feedback route. A consumer can read its obstruction ledger only after following the displayed $\Cont$ routes through $T$ and $R$.

Finally, repack the visible rows with $P$ and $N$. Since no carrier coordinate names a total halting predicate or a divergence oracle, the NameCert obligations expose only the finite trace and diagonal ledger listed above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Halting obstruction diagonal non-escape]
\label{thm:halting-obstruction-diagonal-nonescape}
Let $O=(S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\HaltingObstructionUp$ carrier. Every public attempt to turn the displayed self-reference route into a halting decision must factor through the finite trace row $T$, the self-reference row $R$, the diagonal policy row $D$, and the recorded $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance. The exported surface therefore cannot escape into a global halting classifier, an unbounded search over continuation histories, a host divergence oracle, or quotient equality of certificate names.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-namecert-obligations}. Trace exactness forces every positive continuation read to be one of the finite steps recorded in $T$.

Self-reference readability then routes the feedback claim through $R$ together with the input-history and certificate rows. The consumer receives a displayed self-application route, not an external enumeration of all possible histories.

Apply diagonal policy locality. The obstruction ledger is exactly the row $D$ attached to that route, transported only by $H$ and read only through the continuations in $C$.

The exclusions follow by carrier inspection. Since $O$ contains no total halting predicate, no unbounded search coordinate, no host divergence oracle, and no quotient equality of names, none of those objects can be exported from the public surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Halting obstruction distinction boundary]
\label{thm:halting-obstruction-distinction-boundary}
Every accepted $\HaltingObstructionUp$ diagonal read consumes the HaltingDistinction certificate-history pair, finite trace, and diagonal self-reference boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdistinction-namecert} before it can expose the obstruction ledger $D$. The obstruction surface is therefore a downstream finite ledger over $S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N$, not a global halting classifier and not a duplicate distinction principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the obstruction carrier of \autoref{def:halting-obstruction-carrier}. Its diagonal read is available only after the certificate row $S$, input-history row $I$, finite trace row $T$, self-reference row $R$, and diagonal policy row $D$ have been displayed.

Apply the local NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-namecert-obligations}. Trace exactness, self-reference readability, diagonal policy locality, and package closure force any public obstruction read through the finite rows $T,R,D,H,C,P,N$.

Now read the sibling boundary \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-haltingdistinction-namecert}. The certificate-history pair, finite trace, and diagonal self-reference packet supplied there are the distinction data consumed before the obstruction ledger is exposed.

Finally use diagonal non-escape from \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-diagonal-nonescape}. Since the obstruction packet has no total halting predicate, unbounded search, host divergence oracle, or quotient equality of certificate names, it remains a downstream finite ledger rather than a global classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Halting obstruction trace coverage]
\label{thm:halting-obstruction-trace-coverage}
For an accepted $\HaltingObstructionUp$ packet $O=(S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N)$, every positive continuation fact exported by the packet is a displayed member of the finite trace row $T$. Such a fact is read through $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\Pkg$, and the local $\NameCert$ rows, and it is not supplied by an unbounded search over histories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:halting-obstruction-carrier}. The only row that records positive continuation facts is the finite trace row $T$.

Apply trace exactness from \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-namecert-obligations}. Any exported continuation fact must be one of the displayed $\Cont$ steps stored in $T$, transported by $H$ and reached by the route row $C$.

The package and local naming rows $P,N$ only repack that same finite evidence. Since the carrier has no coordinate for an unbounded continuation search, the public trace surface is exactly the displayed membership surface of $T$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Halting obstruction self-reference route determinacy]
\label{thm:halting-obstruction-self-reference-route-determinacy}
For an accepted $\HaltingObstructionUp$ packet $O=(S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N)$, the self-reference row $R$ is readable only through the certificate row $S$, input-history row $I$, finite trace row $T$, componentwise transports $H$, and displayed continuations $C$. The rows $P$ and $N$ certify that route, and no second route can read $R$ without passing through those displayed coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the self-reference coordinate in \autoref{def:halting-obstruction-carrier}. It is a row of the same finite packet, not a free-standing self-application operator.

Use self-reference readability from \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-namecert-obligations}. A read of $R$ must first name the certificate surface $S$ and input-history row $I$, and it must consume the finite trace row $T$ along the displayed routes.

The transports $H$ and continuations $C$ determine the only admissible path to the row. The package provenance $P$ and local $\NameCert$ row $N$ certify that path rather than adding an alternate route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Halting obstruction diagonal policy ledger exactness]
\label{thm:halting-obstruction-diagonal-policy-ledger-exactness}
For an accepted $\HaltingObstructionUp$ packet $O=(S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N)$, the row $D$ is the unique diagonal policy ledger row exported by the packet. It records the local obstruction attached to the displayed self-reference route and cannot supply a total halting classifier for arbitrary certificate-history pairs.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier in \autoref{def:halting-obstruction-carrier}. Among its coordinates, only $D$ is a diagonal policy row; the remaining rows name the certificate, input history, finite trace, self-reference route, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming surface.

Apply diagonal policy locality from \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-namecert-obligations}. A public diagonal read reaches $D$ only after following the displayed self-reference route through $S,I,T,R,H,C,P,N$.

Finally use \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-diagonal-nonescape}. Since the packet has no coordinate for a total halting predicate, unbounded search, host divergence oracle, or quotient equality of names, the diagonal ledger remains local and cannot become a classifier for arbitrary inputs.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Halting obstruction positive trace ledger]
\label{thm:halting-obstruction-positive-trace-ledger}
For an accepted $\HaltingObstructionUp$ packet $O=(S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N)$, every positive continuation fact exported by the public obstruction surface is one of the finite rows displayed in $T$. The exported fact is reached through the carrier, transport, route, package, and local naming coordinates of $O$, and it cannot be replaced by an unbounded search over continuation histories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier of \autoref{def:halting-obstruction-carrier}. It names $T$ as the finite $\Cont$ trace row and gives no separate coordinate for a search procedure over all histories.

Apply the trace exactness obligation in \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-namecert-obligations}. Any positive continuation fact exposed by the packet must already be one of the displayed finite $\Cont$ steps in $T$.

The non-escape statement of \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-diagonal-nonescape} rules out reading this trace as a global halting classifier or as an unbounded continuation oracle. The distinction boundary of \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-distinction-boundary} then identifies the downstream object as the same finite obstruction ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Halting obstruction finite route non-escape]
\label{thm:halting-obstruction-finite-route-nonescape}
For an accepted $\HaltingObstructionUp$ packet $O=(S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N)$, every public diagonal read factors only through the displayed rows $S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N$. In particular, no consumer can obtain a diagonal obstruction read by bypassing the finite trace, self-reference, policy, transport, route, package, and local naming rows of $O$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the coordinates in \autoref{def:halting-obstruction-carrier}. The public packet lists exactly the certificate row, input-history row, trace row, self-reference row, diagonal policy row, transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local NameCert row.

Use package and name closure from \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-namecert-obligations}. Public reads must pass through the stored $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, while self-reference readability and diagonal policy locality force the route through $R$ and $D$.

Apply \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-diagonal-nonescape}. Since the carrier contains no global classifier, unbounded search coordinate, host oracle, or quotient equality of names, the only public diagonal route is the finite route through $S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Halting obstruction public read boundary]
\label{thm:halting-obstruction-public-read-boundary}
Every downstream HaltingDistinction or HaltingDiagonal consumer of an accepted $\HaltingObstructionUp$ packet receives the finite obstruction ledger displayed by $S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N$. The consumer receives trace exactness, self-reference readability, diagonal policy locality, and package/name closure for that ledger, not a global classifier for arbitrary certificate-history pairs.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier from \autoref{def:halting-obstruction-carrier}. Its public payload is the finite obstruction packet $S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N$.

Apply the five NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-namecert-obligations}. They provide the carrier row, the exact finite positive trace, the readable self-reference route, the local diagonal policy ledger, and the package/name closure surface.

Now use \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-distinction-boundary}. Downstream HaltingDistinction and HaltingDiagonal uses consume the finite ledger after the displayed certificate-history and self-reference boundary have been read, so the public object is not a global halting classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Halting obstruction local NameCert repackaging]
\label{thm:halting-obstruction-local-namecert-repackaging}
For an accepted $\HaltingObstructionUp$ packet $O=(S,I,T,R,D,H,C,P,N)$, the package row $P$ and local naming row $N$ repackage only the carrier exposure, trace exactness, self-reference readability, diagonal policy locality, and package/name closure already displayed by the packet. \end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $P$ and $N$ against the carrier in \autoref{def:halting-obstruction-carrier}. They are package provenance and local NameCert rows for the same finite obstruction packet, not independent computational operators.

Apply \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-namecert-obligations}. The content available for repackaging is exactly carrier habitation, trace exactness, self-reference readability, diagonal policy locality, and package/name closure.

Finally combine \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-diagonal-nonescape} with \autoref{thm:halting-obstruction-distinction-boundary}. The repackaged public surface remains the same finite obstruction ledger and therefore cannot expose a global classifier, unbounded search, host oracle, quotient equality, or second diagonal route.
\end{proof}
