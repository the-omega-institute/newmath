\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyRegularizationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyregularization-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ interface names the finite certificate surface
that turns a rational Cauchy stream with displayed modulus windows into the
regular rational sequence data consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ and then by
$\RealUp$. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the
regularizing operation is carried by explicit tail moduli and finite
approximants. The packet therefore records only the rational stream windows,
dyadic modulus observations, finite-window regularized approximants, seal
provenance, and $\Pkg/\NameCert$ ledgers visible to BEDC consumers.

\begin{definition}[CauchyRegularization finite carrier]
\label{def:cauchyregularization-finite-carrier}
A $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
G=(S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\RationalStreamUp$ source row naming the inspected rational
Cauchy stream through finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation windows;
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ row naming, for each requested precision,
the displayed tail threshold used by the regularization;
\item $D$ is the finite family of $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ modulus-window packets
that records the rational tolerance, dyadic radius, and comparison rows read
at the inspected thresholds;
\item $W$ is the finite window ledger that pairs each inspected source window
with the modulus row that justifies reading it as a late Cauchy window;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained by repacking those late
finite-window approximants as a regular rational sequence surface;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-provenance row attached to the same regular
sequence handoff;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for source windows,
modulus rows, dyadic observations, regularized approximants, and seal rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers reach the
stream, modulus, dyadic-window, regular-sequence, and seal coordinates;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RationalStreamUp$,
$\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
$\RealUp$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRegularizationUp}$ ledger.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient of Cauchy streams, no chosen host real, no
ambient completeness theorem, and no hidden tail selector outside the
displayed modulus row.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[CauchyRegularization modulus classifier]
\label{def:cauchyregularization-modulus-classifier}
For accepted $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ carriers $G$ and $G'$, the classifier
$\mathsf{CauchyRegularizationClassifier}(G,G')$ compares only the displayed
$\RationalStreamUp$ windows, $\CauchyModulusUp$ thresholds,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ modulus-window ledgers, finite-window approximants,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows, $\RealUp$ seal-provenance rows, componentwise
$\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ read routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert$ ledgers.

In particular, two carriers classify together only when their regularized
finite windows are read from the same transported source windows and the same
transported modulus thresholds. The classifier does not inspect host limits,
completion quotients, or any convergence predicate not represented by the
listed rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRegularizationUp}$ surface consists of five
obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: an accepted packet is exactly the finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ package of
\autoref{def:cauchyregularization-finite-carrier};
\item modulus-window classifier stability:
\autoref{def:cauchyregularization-modulus-classifier} is preserved by the
displayed $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ routes on source windows, modulus
thresholds, dyadic observations, approximants, and seal rows;
\item regular-sequence handoff: the finite approximants selected by the
modulus windows repack as the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$;
\item $\RealUp$ seal compatibility: the seal-provenance row $E$ reads only
the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff and the finite stream, modulus, dyadic, transport,
and package rows carried by the packet;
\item ledger exactness: every consumer-visible source window, modulus
threshold, dyadic radius, approximant, handoff, seal, transport, continuation,
package, and local certificate row occurs in the displayed carrier.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is just the finite packet of
\autoref{def:cauchyregularization-finite-carrier}. Each coordinate is a
visible history row, and the provenance row names exactly the source
certificates that own those rows.

Classifier stability is componentwise. Transport a source window, its modulus
threshold, and the dyadic observation ledger along the displayed $\hsame$
rows, then replay the recorded $\Cont$ routes. The regularized approximant is
still read from the transported late window justified by the transported
modulus threshold.

The regular-sequence handoff is obtained by projecting the row $R$. It
forgets no evidence needed by $\RegSeqRatUp$, because the source window,
modulus threshold, dyadic radius, and comparison rows remain in $S,M,D,W,H,C$.

Finally the $\RealUp$ seal reads the same handoff through $E$, and the ledger
rows $P$ and $N$ account for package and local naming provenance. Since every
consumer-visible coordinate is listed in the carrier, there is no extra host
limit, quotient, hidden selector, or ambient completeness object to export.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization regular-sequence handoff]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-regular-sequence-handoff}
For every accepted $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ carrier and every displayed
precision request, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer read factors through one
$\RationalStreamUp$ finite observation window, one $\CauchyModulusUp$
threshold row for that precision, the corresponding $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
modulus-window ledger, and the finite-window approximant recorded in $W$.
No $\RegSeqRatUp$ read can bypass those rows by using an unrecorded tail
choice or a quotient of rational streams.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier
$G=(S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. A precision request first reaches the source
stream through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ window in $S$ and then reads the
threshold supplied by $M$.

The dyadic tolerance and comparison evidence are exactly the rows recorded in
$D$. The window ledger $W$ pairs those rows with the inspected late source
window, so the regularized approximant has a visible origin in the same
finite packet.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ is attached to that approximant ledger. Transport
changes only the displayed routes in $H$ and $C$; it does not add a hidden tail
selector or a quotient stream coordinate. Hence every regular-sequence read
factors through the stated finite rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization RealUp seal boundary]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer read of an accepted $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ packet
is mediated by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row, the displayed rational-stream
window, the modulus threshold, the dyadic modulus-window ledger, the
regularized finite approximant, the $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport rows, and
the $\Pkg/\NameCert$ provenance. The packet exports no classical
completeness claim, no host real equality, no selected limit, and no quotient
of Cauchy streams.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a real-facing read. The only $\RealUp$ coordinate in the carrier is
the seal-provenance row $E$, and $E$ is attached to the regular rational
handoff $R$.

Use \autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-regular-sequence-handoff} to expand the
source of $R$. The read must pass through the finite rational observation
window, the displayed modulus threshold, the dyadic tolerance ledger, and the
approximant row justified by that window.

The remaining coordinates are transport and provenance rows. They preserve
the displayed route and name the dependency certificates, but they contain no
field for a host real value, a quotient representative, a selected limit, or
an ambient completeness theorem. Thus the seal boundary is exactly the
finite BEDC surface stated above.
\end{proof}
