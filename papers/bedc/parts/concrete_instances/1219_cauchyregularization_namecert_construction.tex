\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyRegularizationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyregularization-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ interface names the finite certificate surface
that turns a rational Cauchy stream with displayed modulus windows into the
regular rational sequence data consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$ and then by
$\RealUp$. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the
regularizing operation is carried by explicit tail moduli and finite
approximants. The packet therefore records only the rational stream windows,
dyadic modulus observations, finite-window regularized approximants, seal
provenance, and $\Pkg/\NameCert$ ledgers visible to BEDC consumers.

This packet consumes the modulus threshold surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert} and the finite
observation-window surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} before repacking the
regular-sequence handoff. Row $M$ is read through $\CauchyModulusUp$ and row
$S$ is read through $\StreamNameUp$, not through a host sequence or quotient
stream.

\begin{definition}[CauchyRegularization finite carrier]
\label{def:cauchyregularization-finite-carrier}
A $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
G=(S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\RationalStreamUp$ source row naming the inspected rational
Cauchy stream through finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation windows;
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ row naming, for each requested precision,
the displayed tail threshold used by the regularization;
\item $D$ is the finite family of $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ modulus-window packets
that records the rational tolerance, dyadic radius, and comparison rows read
at the inspected thresholds;
\item $W$ is the finite window ledger that pairs each inspected source window
with the modulus row that justifies reading it as a late Cauchy window;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained by repacking those late
finite-window approximants as a regular rational sequence surface;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-provenance row attached to the same regular
sequence handoff;
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for source windows,
modulus rows, dyadic observations, regularized approximants, and seal rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers reach the
stream, modulus, dyadic-window, regular-sequence, and seal coordinates;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RationalStreamUp$,
$\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and
$\RealUp$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRegularizationUp}$ ledger.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient of Cauchy streams, no chosen host real, no
ambient completeness theorem, and no hidden tail selector outside the
displayed modulus row.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[CauchyRegularization modulus classifier]
\label{def:cauchyregularization-modulus-classifier}
For accepted $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ carriers $G$ and $G'$, the classifier
$\mathsf{CauchyRegularizationClassifier}(G,G')$ compares only the displayed
$\RationalStreamUp$ windows, $\CauchyModulusUp$ thresholds,
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ modulus-window ledgers, finite-window approximants,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff rows, $\RealUp$ seal-provenance rows, componentwise
$\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ read routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local
$\NameCert$ ledgers.

In particular, two carriers classify together only when their regularized
finite windows are read from the same transported source windows and the same
transported modulus thresholds. The classifier does not inspect host limits,
completion quotients, or any convergence predicate not represented by the
listed rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\CauchyRegularizationUp}$ surface consists of five
obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: an accepted packet is exactly the finite
$\mathsf{BHist}$ package of
\autoref{def:cauchyregularization-finite-carrier};
\item modulus-window classifier stability:
\autoref{def:cauchyregularization-modulus-classifier} is preserved by the
displayed $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ routes on source windows, modulus
thresholds, dyadic observations, approximants, and seal rows;
\item regular-sequence handoff: the finite approximants selected by the
modulus windows repack as the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$;
\item $\RealUp$ seal compatibility: the seal-provenance row $E$ reads only
the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff and the finite stream, modulus, dyadic, transport,
and package rows carried by the packet;
\item ledger exactness: every consumer-visible source window, modulus
threshold, dyadic radius, approximant, handoff, seal, transport, continuation,
package, and local certificate row occurs in the displayed carrier.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is just the finite packet of
\autoref{def:cauchyregularization-finite-carrier}. Each coordinate is a
visible history row, and the provenance row names exactly the source
certificates that own those rows.

Classifier stability is componentwise. Transport a source window, its modulus
threshold, and the dyadic observation ledger along the displayed $\hsame$
rows, then replay the recorded $\Cont$ routes. The regularized approximant is
still read from the transported late window justified by the transported
modulus threshold.

The regular-sequence handoff is obtained by projecting the row $R$. It
forgets no evidence needed by $\RegSeqRatUp$, because the source window,
modulus threshold, dyadic radius, and comparison rows remain in $S,M,D,W,H,C$.

Finally the $\RealUp$ seal reads the same handoff through $E$, and the ledger
rows $P$ and $N$ account for package and local naming provenance. Since every
consumer-visible coordinate is listed in the carrier, there is no extra host
limit, quotient, hidden selector, or ambient completeness object to export.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization regular-sequence handoff]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-regular-sequence-handoff}
For every accepted $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ carrier and every displayed
precision request, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer read factors through one
$\RationalStreamUp$ finite observation window, one $\CauchyModulusUp$
threshold row for that precision, the corresponding $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
modulus-window ledger, and the finite-window approximant recorded in $W$.
No $\RegSeqRatUp$ read can bypass those rows by using an unrecorded tail
choice or a quotient of rational streams.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier
$G=(S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. A precision request first reaches the source
stream through the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ window in $S$ and then reads the
threshold supplied by $M$.

The dyadic tolerance and comparison evidence are exactly the rows recorded in
$D$. The window ledger $W$ pairs those rows with the inspected late source
window, so the regularized approximant has a visible origin in the same
finite packet.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ is attached to that approximant ledger. Transport
changes only the displayed routes in $H$ and $C$; it does not add a hidden tail
selector or a quotient stream coordinate. Hence every regular-sequence read
factors through the stated finite rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization RealUp seal boundary]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer read of an accepted $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ packet
is mediated by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row, the displayed rational-stream
window, the modulus threshold, the dyadic modulus-window ledger, the
regularized finite approximant, the $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ transport rows, and
the $\Pkg/\NameCert$ provenance. The packet exports no classical
completeness claim, no host real equality, no selected limit, and no quotient
of Cauchy streams.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyRegularizationUp.CauchyRegularizationSealRoute\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a real-facing read. The only $\RealUp$ coordinate in the carrier is
the seal-provenance row $E$, and $E$ is attached to the regular rational
handoff $R$.

Use \autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-regular-sequence-handoff} to expand the
source of $R$. The read must pass through the finite rational observation
window, the displayed modulus threshold, the dyadic tolerance ledger, and the
approximant row justified by that window.

The remaining coordinates are transport and provenance rows. They preserve
the displayed route and name the dependency certificates, but they contain no
field for a host real value, a quotient representative, a selected limit, or
an ambient completeness theorem. Thus the seal boundary is exactly the
finite BEDC surface stated above.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization common-modulus idempotence]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-common-modulus-idempotence}
For accepted $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ carriers regularized through the same
transported $\RationalStreamUp$ observation windows, $\CauchyModulusUp$
threshold rows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ modulus-window ledgers, $\hsame$ and
$\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ rows, and $\NameCert$ ledger, a second
$\CauchyRegularizationUp$ read through those same displayed rows yields the
same $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff classifier and the same $\RealUp$ seal-provenance
row. The repeated read introduces no hidden tail selector, quotient stream,
host limit, or completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyRegularizationUp.CauchyRegularizationFiniteCarrier\_common\_modulus\_idempotence}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier
$G=(S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The common modulus classifier identifies the
displayed source window in $S$, the threshold row in $M$, the dyadic ledger in
$D$, and the finite approximant ledger in $W$ for the first read and for the
second read.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-regular-sequence-handoff} to both
reads. Since the two reads use the same transported observation windows,
modulus thresholds, dyadic rows, transport routes, package rows, and naming
ledger, the handoff route can only be the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier
row $R$.

It remains to compare the real-facing rows. Apply
\autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-boundary}. The $\RealUp$
seal-provenance row is mediated by that same handoff, the same transport
routes, and the same $\Pkg/\NameCert$ provenance, so the repeated read has the
identical displayed BEDC seal route and no additional selector or host-limit
coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization RealUp seal idempotent route]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-idempotent-route}
For accepted $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ packets whose first and second
regularization reads share the transported $\RationalStreamUp$ windows,
$\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold rows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger rows,
$\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row, $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$
provenance, and $\NameCert$ ledger, the $\RealUp$ consumer seal route after
the second read is the same displayed route as after the first read. The
repeated real-facing read introduces no quotient stream, selected limit,
host real equality, hidden tail selector, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier
$G=(S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The shared carrier rows fix the source windows,
modulus thresholds, dyadic ledgers, regularized approximants, handoff row,
transport routes, package provenance, and local naming ledger for both reads.

Apply
\autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-common-modulus-idempotence}. The
$\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff classifier and the $\RealUp$ seal-provenance row are
locked to the displayed rows $R$ and $E$ for the first read and for the
second read.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-boundary} to expand both
real-facing reads. Each read passes through the same handoff, transported
rational-stream window, continuation route, package row, and local naming
row, and the boundary lists no further consumer-visible coordinate.

Therefore the second $\RealUp$ seal route is the same displayed route as the
first. Since the carrier and the boundary exclusions contain no quotient
stream, selected limit, host real equality, hidden tail selector, or ambient
completeness coordinate, repeated regularization through the same finite rows
adds no Real-facing escape route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization Real seal source-modulus factorization]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-source-modulus-factorization}
For an accepted $\CauchyRegularizationUp$ packet
$G=(S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, every $\RealUp$ seal read factors through exactly
the displayed finite $\StreamNameUp$ source window carried by $S$, the
$\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row $M$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
modulus-window ledger $D$, the approximant ledger $W$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$
handoff row $R$, the seal provenance $E$, and the structural rows
$H,C,P,N$. Conversely, those rows repack as the same Real-facing seal route.
No quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, hidden tail selector,
ambient completeness theorem, or unrecorded modulus choice contributes to the
read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier of
\autoref{def:cauchyregularization-finite-carrier}. Its displayed rows are
precisely $S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$, so any Real-facing route must first account
for the source window, modulus threshold, dyadic ledger, approximant ledger,
handoff, seal provenance, transports, continuation routes, package row, and
local naming ledger named there.

For the forward direction, apply
\autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-regular-sequence-handoff}. The
$\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row $R$ is reached only through the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$ source window in $S$, the threshold row $M$, the dyadic
modulus-window ledger $D$, and the approximant ledger $W$.

Then apply \autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-boundary}. The
$\RealUp$ seal reads that handoff through the seal-provenance row $E$ and the
structural rows $H,C,P,N$, and the boundary exposes no further
consumer-visible coordinate.

For the converse direction, start from the displayed rows
$S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. The handoff theorem repacks the source-modulus data as
the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ route, and the seal-boundary theorem attaches exactly
the Real-facing provenance row $E$ with the same structural routes and
ledgers.

Finally use \autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-idempotent-route}.
If another Real-facing route were available from the same source,
modulus, dyadic, approximant, handoff, seal, transport, package, and naming
rows, the second read would differ from the first. Idempotence rules this out,
so the factorization is exactly the displayed route and excludes quotient
streams, selected limits, host real equality, hidden tail selectors, ambient
completeness, and unrecorded modulus choices.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization Real seal normalization retract]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-normalization-retract}
Let $G=(S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted
$\CauchyRegularizationUp$ packet. Suppose a second regularization packet reads
the same transported $\RationalStreamUp$ window row $S$, the same
$\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row $M$, the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$,
the same approximant ledger $W$, the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff $R$, the same
seal row $E$, and the same structural rows $H,C,P,N$. Then the $\RealUp$
consumer route of the second read retracts to the original seal route $E$.

The retract exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real
equality, hidden tail selector, unrecorded modulus choice, or ambient
completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier of
\autoref{def:cauchyregularization-finite-carrier} for the original packet and
for the second packet. The two projections list the same source window,
modulus threshold, dyadic ledger, approximant ledger, handoff, seal,
transport, continuation, package, and naming rows.

Apply
\autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-common-modulus-idempotence}. The repeated
read cannot introduce a new $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row or a new
$\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff; the handoff and seal provenance remain the displayed
rows $R$ and $E$.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-idempotent-route}. With the
same Real-facing rows on both reads, the second consumer route is identified
with the first consumer route at the seal surface.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-source-modulus-factorization}. The
first route has only the displayed source-modulus-seal factorization through
$S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$. Hence the second route retracts to the same seal row
$E$, and the listed quotient, limit, equality, selector, modulus-choice, and
ambient-completeness data have no exported coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization public real-regularization export]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-public-real-regularization-export}
The finite carrier, modulus classifier, regular-sequence handoff, $\RealUp$
seal boundary, and Real-facing idempotent route assemble a public
$\CauchyRegularizationUp$ export over $\RationalStreamUp$,
$\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$,
$\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
The export exposes only displayed rational-stream windows, modulus
thresholds, dyadic comparison rows, regularized approximants, the
regular-sequence handoff, the real-seal provenance, and the named transport
and package rows.

It exposes no quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, hidden tail selector, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and classifier. The carrier of \autoref{def:cauchyregularization-finite-carrier} lists exactly the finite rows available to a consumer, and the classifier of \autoref{def:cauchyregularization-modulus-classifier} transports only those rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-namecert-obligation-surface} to bind carrier admission, classifier stability, regular-sequence handoff, real-seal compatibility, and ledger exactness into the local naming surface.

Next use \autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-regular-sequence-handoff}. It routes every $\RegSeqRatUp$ read through a displayed rational window, a displayed modulus threshold, the dyadic ledger, and the finite approximant row.

Use \autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-boundary}. The $\RealUp$
read is mediated by the same handoff, transport, continuation, package, and
local naming provenance.

Finally use
\autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-idempotent-route}. Repeated
regularization through the same finite rows preserves that Real-facing route.
The finite-row assembly therefore gives a public real-regularization export
without quotient streams, host equality, selected limits, hidden tail
selectors, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyRegularization public normalization retract export]
\label{thm:cauchyregularization-public-normalization-retract-export}
Let $G=(S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted
$\CauchyRegularizationUp$ packet, and suppose a second accepted
$\CauchyRegularizationUp$ packet has the same displayed source row $S$,
modulus row $M$, dyadic ledger $D$, approximant ledger $W$,
regular-sequence handoff $R$, seal row $E$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.
Then the public real-regularization export of the second read retracts to the
original public export surface determined by $S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$, not only
to the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row.

This public retract exports no quotient stream, selected limit, host real
equality, hidden tail selector, unrecorded modulus choice, or ambient
completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-public-real-regularization-export} to the
original packet. Its public export surface contains exactly the displayed
rational-stream windows, modulus thresholds, dyadic comparison rows,
regularized approximants, handoff row, real-seal provenance, transport rows,
package row, and naming ledger.

Apply the same public export theorem to the second packet. Since the second
accepted packet shares $S,M,D,W,R,E,H,C,P,N$, its public export has the same
displayed carrier, classifier, handoff, seal, transport, package, and naming
coordinates.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-normalization-retract}. The
Real-facing consumer route of the second read retracts to the original seal
route $E$ under the shared displayed rows.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:cauchyregularization-real-seal-source-modulus-factorization}.
The seal row $E$ is not an isolated terminal coordinate: it is reached only
through the source window $S$, modulus row $M$, dyadic ledger $D$,
approximant ledger $W$, handoff row $R$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.
Thus the retract returns the second public read to the original public export
surface itself. The excluded quotient stream, selected-limit, host-equality,
tail-selector, modulus-choice, and ambient-completeness data have no public
coordinate available for the retract.
\end{proof}
