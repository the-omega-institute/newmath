\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HeineCantorModulusUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-heine-cantor-modulus-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\HeineCantorModulusUp$ packet records the
finite-net form of Heine--Cantor as explicit BEDC modulus data. It reads the
compact-metric finite net of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the finite
compact-uniform-continuity route of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactuniformcontinuity-namecert}, the
pointwise rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, the
consumer modulus surface of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}, and the final
triangle handoff of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-triangle-uniform-estimate-namecert}. The
chapter names the finite certificate route that turns local pointwise modulus
rows on a displayed compact net into one tolerance-indexed uniform modulus.

\begin{definition}[Heine-Cantor modulus carrier]
\label{def:heine-cantor-modulus-carrier}
A $\HeineCantorModulusUp$ carrier is a finite BEDC packet
$$
H=(X,Y,F,\varepsilon,P,C,R,M,T,Q,S,K,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the compact metric source row, read through
$\NameCert_{\CompactMetricUp}$, with its finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ net
family at the target precision scale.
\item $Y$ is the target metric row whose triangle ledger receives the final
estimate.
\item $F$ is the continuous-map row from $X$ to $Y$, read through
$\NameCert_{\ContinuousMapUp}$, with pointwise modulus rows at the listed net
centers.
\item $\varepsilon$ is the positive target dyadic precision history whose
uniform modulus is being named.
\item $P$ is the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ net selected from the compact
metric certificate for the source scale required by $\varepsilon$.
\item $C$ lists the center histories in $P$, together with their membership
constructors and coverage rows.
\item $R$ lists the pointwise continuity radii attached to those centers and
the rational lower-bound rows used to fold them into one positive source
precision.
\item $M$ is the exported source precision history, classified componentwise
by the $\RatUp$ and source-metric classifiers.
\item $T$ is the metric triangle handoff row, read through
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-triangle-uniform-estimate-namecert},
which carries the compact-net source estimates and the two pointwise target
estimates to the final $\varepsilon$ bound.
\item $Q$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,Y,F,\varepsilon,P,C,R,M,T$.
\item $S$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from compact metric data,
pointwise continuity data, finite-fold data, and triangle data to the
$\UniformModulusUp$ consumer.
\item $K$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactMetricUp$,
$\CompactUniformContinuityUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\UniformModulusUp$,
$\MetricTriangleUniformEstimateUp$, $\RatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, and
$\Cont$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HeineCantorModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier accepts only these finite rows. Its displayed continuity condition
is the continuation assertion
$$
\Cont(H,\NameCert_{\UniformModulusUp})
$$
through the rows $P,C,R,M,T,Q,S,K,N$; it has no coordinate for open-cover
compactness, sequential compactness, choice-selected centers, trichotomy, or
host real equality.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Heine-Cantor modulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:heine-cantor-modulus-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\HeineCantorModulusUp}$ obligation surface consists
exactly of the finite carrier exposure of
\autoref{def:heine-cantor-modulus-carrier}, componentwise $\hsame$ transport
for all displayed rows, the finite-net extraction row, the folded positive
source-precision row, the metric triangle handoff row, and the
$\UniformModulusUp$ consumer route. No accepted obligation may cite an
open-cover compactness theorem, a sequential compactness principle, a choice
operation, a trichotomy split on host reals, or host equality of real points.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier packet $H$. The source, target, continuous-map,
precision, net, center, radius, folded-modulus, triangle, transport,
continuation, provenance, and local naming rows are the whole displayed
surface.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The row $Q$ transports each displayed
history by the corresponding source, target, rational, finite-bundle, and
triangle classifier, while $S$ replays the same $\Cont$ route after transport.

The remaining obligations are the three constructive readings: extract the
finite net from $\CompactMetricUp$, fold the pointwise radii in the finite
list to obtain $M$, and apply the metric triangle handoff to deliver the
target $\varepsilon$ estimate. Since every reading uses a named coordinate of
$H$, the excluded compactness and choice-theoretic routes have no readable
slot in the local $\NameCert$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Heine-Cantor finite-net extraction]
\label{thm:heine-cantor-modulus-finite-net-extraction}
For an accepted $\HeineCantorModulusUp$ carrier $H$, the net rows
$P$ and $C$ determine the finite list of centers used by the modulus
construction. Each listed center is a source history carried by $X$, each
membership row is a displayed $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ constructor, and each
coverage row is a compact-metric distance ledger at the precision scale read
from $\varepsilon$. The extraction is by finite-row projection and induction
over the displayed net spine.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read $P$ from the carrier. It is already a finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ row
from the compact-metric certificate, so the only available centers are the
histories listed in its finite spine.

Induct over that spine. At the empty boundary no center is produced and no
coverage row is exported. At a successor row, project the displayed center
history, its membership constructor, and the attached compact-metric coverage
ledger, then append those rows to the extracted list.

The induction uses no cover refinement or external compactness principle.
Every step reads the next stored row of $P$ and $C$, and componentwise
$\hsame$ transport in $Q$ carries the extracted center and coverage rows to
classified representatives.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Heine-Cantor triangle handoff]
\label{thm:heine-cantor-modulus-triangle-handoff}
For an accepted $\HeineCantorModulusUp$ carrier $H$, the folded source
precision $M$, the compact-net center rows $C$, and the pointwise continuity
rows $R$ determine the triangle handoff row $T$. Consequently the
$\UniformModulusUp$ consumer receives a single positive source precision whose
validity for the target tolerance $\varepsilon$ is witnessed by finite-net
coverage, two pointwise target estimates at the selected center, and the
target metric triangle ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite-net extraction theorem. It gives the selected center
route and compact-metric source coverage rows from the displayed net packet.

Next read the folded precision $M$ from the rows $R$. The fold is finite:
each pointwise radius attached to a center is compared through the rational
ledger, and the accumulator remains a positive history classified by the
source and rational classifiers.

Finally apply the triangle handoff row $T$. The source metric ledger places
both source points inside the pointwise radius of the selected center, the
continuous-map row supplies the two target estimates at that center, and the
target metric triangle ledger returns the displayed $\varepsilon$ estimate.
The continuation row $S$ sends exactly this finite package to
$\UniformModulusUp$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Heine-Cantor radius fold]
\label{thm:heine-cantor-modulus-radius-fold}
For an accepted $\HeineCantorModulusUp$ carrier $H$, the folded source
precision $M$ is determined only by the finite center rows $C$, the pointwise
radius rows $R$, and the rational lower-bound fold displayed in those rows.
The extraction has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, a host minimum
operator, a selector for centers, trichotomy on host reals, or host equality of
real points.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $C$, $R$, and $M$ from the carrier. The finite-net extraction theorem
identifies the center spine, so each source radius available to the fold is
already attached to a displayed center row.

Induct over that finite center spine. At the empty boundary the fold reads the
displayed positive source-precision base row. At a successor row, project the
next center, its pointwise continuity radius, and its rational lower-bound
certificate, then combine it with the accumulated positive history by the
finite rational fold.

Componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $Q$ carries the center row, radius row, and
fold accumulator through the source-metric and rational classifiers. Thus the
exported $M$ is a finite-row object of $C$ and $R$ alone, with no appeal to
open-cover data or host-side order case splits.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Heine-Cantor UniformModulus export]
\label{thm:heine-cantor-modulus-uniformmodulus-export}
Every $\UniformModulusUp$ consumer accepted by the
$\HeineCantorModulusUp$ chapter factors through the finite rows
$P,C,R,M,T,Q,S,K,N$. The route is finite-net extraction, radius fold, and
metric triangle handoff, rather than an open-cover compactness argument,
choice-selected center data, or host-side real comparison.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First project the carrier rows. The finite-net extraction theorem reads
$P$ and $C$ as the complete center and coverage surface for the source
precision scale determined by $\varepsilon$.

Next apply the radius-fold theorem. It turns the pointwise rows $R$ into the
single positive source precision $M$ by a finite rational lower-bound fold, and
$Q$ transports the resulting history componentwise across classified
representatives.

Finally apply the triangle handoff theorem. The row $T$ combines compact-net
coverage, the two pointwise target estimates, and the target metric triangle
ledger; the continuation row $S$ presents exactly this package to the
$\UniformModulusUp$ consumer. The provenance row $K$ and local row $N$ record
that no further compactness, selector, or host comparison input is part of the
accepted route.
\end{proof}
