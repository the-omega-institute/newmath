\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SubtypeUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-subtype-namecert}

The subtype interface $\SubtypeUp$ packages the carrier of elements of a parent $\NameCert_{\SetUp}$ satisfying a decidable predicate. It depends on $\NameCert_{\SetUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-set-namecert}) and supplies the kernel-licit refinement type used throughout the algebra ladder for sub-objects.

\begin{definition}[Subtype restricted classifier]
\label{def:subtype-restricted-classifier}
Let $S$ be the certified point type of a parent $\NameCert_{\SetUp}$, with
parent classifier $\sim_S$. Let $P:S\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a decidable predicate
stable under the parent classifier, meaning that for $x,y:S$,
$$
x\sim_S y\Longrightarrow P(x)\Longrightarrow P(y).
$$
The $\SubtypeUp$ carrier cut out by $P$ is
$$
S_P=\{(x,p)\mid x:S\ \text{and}\ p:P(x)\}.
$$
For subtype elements $(x,p_x)$ and $(y,p_y)$, the subtype classifier is the
restriction of the parent classifier:
$$
(x,p_x)\sim_{S_P}(y,p_y)\quad:\Longleftrightarrow\quad x\sim_S y.
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Subtype classifier reflects parent classifier]
\label{thm:subtype-classifier-reflects-parent-classifier}
Let $S$ be a parent $\NameCert_{\SetUp}$ with classifier $\sim_S$, and let
$P:S\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a decidable predicate stable under $\sim_S$. For all
$x,y:S$ and witnesses $p_x:P(x)$ and $p_y:P(y)$, the classifier of
\autoref{def:subtype-restricted-classifier} satisfies
$$
(x,p_x)\sim_{S_P}(y,p_y)
\Longleftrightarrow
x\sim_S y.
$$
Equivalently, a parent classifier witness between predicate-bearing endpoints
induces the subtype classifier witness, and every subtype classifier witness
projects to the parent classifier witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the classifier clause in \autoref{def:subtype-restricted-classifier}.
The forward implication is projection: a subtype classifier witness is a parent
classifier witness between the first components. The reverse implication is
inclusion: a witness of $x\sim_S y$ is, by the displayed definition, a witness
of $(x,p_x)\sim_{S_P}(y,p_y)$ once the two subtype endpoints have been formed.
The decidability and stability assumptions are the formation and transport rows
for the $\SubtypeUp$ carrier; the restricted classifier itself carries no data
beyond the parent comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Subtype carrier obligation row]
\label{thm:subtype-carrier-obligation-row}
Let $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be the carrier of a parent $\NameCert_{\SetUp}$, and let $P:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a decidable predicate. In the predicate presentation of \autoref{def:subtype-restricted-classifier}, the carrier obligation for $\SubtypeUp$ is exactly
$$
S_P(h)\Longleftrightarrow S(h)\land P(h).
$$
Consequently a displayed endpoint $s_0:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $S(s_0)$ and $P(s_0)$ supplies the inhabitedness row
$$
S_P(s_0).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the carrier clause of \autoref{def:subtype-restricted-classifier}. A subtype endpoint is formed by pairing a parent-carrier witness with a predicate witness, so its predicate presentation is the conjunction $S(h)\land P(h)$.

For inhabitedness, apply the same clause at $s_0$. The two displayed witnesses $S(s_0)$ and $P(s_0)$ are precisely the two components required to form $S_P(s_0)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Subtype classifier projection obligation]
\label{thm:subtype-classifier-projection-obligation}
Let $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be the carrier of a parent $\NameCert_{\SetUp}$ with classifier $\sim_S$, and let $P:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$. For all predicate-bearing endpoints $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, the classifier row for $\SubtypeUp$ is exactly
$$
h\sim_{S_P}k\Longleftrightarrow h\sim_S k.
$$
Thus parent projection and restricted-classifier introduction are the two classifier rows: a subtype classifier witness projects to the parent comparison, and a parent comparison between already formed subtype endpoints introduces the restricted classifier witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the classifier clause in \autoref{def:subtype-restricted-classifier}. The restricted classifier stores no comparison datum apart from the parent comparison $h\sim_S k$.

The forward direction is projection from the restricted witness to that parent witness. The reverse direction is introduction: once the endpoints are already certified by the carrier row, the parent witness is read as the restricted-classifier witness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Subtype NameCert transport obligation]
\label{thm:subtype-namecert-transport-obligation}
Let $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be the carrier of a parent $\NameCert_{\SetUp}$ with classifier $\sim_S$, and let $\mathsf{cert}:\NameCert(S,\sim_S)$. Let $P:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be stable under the parent classifier, meaning that for all $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$,
$$
S(h)\land P(h)\land h\sim_S k\Longrightarrow P(k).
$$
In the predicate presentation of \autoref{def:subtype-restricted-classifier}, parent carrier transport from $\mathsf{cert}$ together with predicate stability gives
$$
S_P(h)\to h\sim_{S_P}k\to S_P(k).
$$
This is the transport row consumed by \autoref{thm:subtype-restricted-classifier-namecert}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $S_P(h)$ by \autoref{thm:subtype-carrier-obligation-row}. This gives $S(h)$ and $P(h)$. Unfold the restricted classifier by \autoref{thm:subtype-classifier-projection-obligation}; the comparison is the parent witness $h\sim_S k$.

The carrier-respect field of the parent $\NameCert(S,\sim_S)$ transports $S(h)$ along $h\sim_S k$ and gives $S(k)$. Apply the displayed predicate-stability hypothesis to $S(h)$, $P(h)$, and the same parent comparison to obtain $P(k)$.

Pair $S(k)$ with $P(k)$ using the carrier row for $S_P(k)$. The resulting witness is the required transport row for the restricted certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Subtype carrier closed under restricted classifier]
\label{lem:subtype-carrier-restricted-classifier-closure}
Let $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be the carrier of a parent $\NameCert_{\SetUp}$ with classifier $\sim_S$, and let $\mathsf{cert}:\NameCert(S,\sim_S)$. Let $P:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a decidable predicate stable under the parent classifier, in the sense that for all $x,y:\mathsf{BHist}$,
$$
S(x)\land P(x)\land x\sim_S y\Longrightarrow P(y).
$$
In the predicate presentation of \autoref{def:subtype-restricted-classifier}, write
$$
S_P(h)\Longleftrightarrow S(h)\land P(h),
\qquad
h\sim_{S_P}k\Longleftrightarrow h\sim_S k.
$$
If $S_P(h)$ and $h\sim_{S_P}k$, then $S_P(k)$.
\end{lemma}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SubtypeUp.SubtypeRestrictedCarrier\_classifier\_closed}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $S_P(h)$ to obtain $S(h)$ and $P(h)$. Unfold the restricted classifier to read the comparison as $h\sim_S k$. The carrier-transport field of $\mathsf{cert}$, one of the fields listed in \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}, transports $S(h)$ along this comparison and gives $S(k)$. Stability of $P$ under the same parent comparison gives $P(k)$. Pairing $S(k)$ with $P(k)$ gives $S_P(k)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Subtype restricted classifier NameCert]
\label{thm:subtype-restricted-classifier-namecert}
Under the hypotheses and notation of \autoref{lem:subtype-carrier-restricted-classifier-closure}, assume that the subtype carrier has a displayed endpoint, so there is an $s_0:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $S(s_0)$ and $P(s_0)$. Then the $\SubtypeUp$ carrier $S_P$ with the restricted classifier $\sim_{S_P}$ forms a Lean carrier naming certificate:
$$
\NameCert(S_P,\sim_{S_P}).
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SubtypeUp.SubtypeRestrictedClassifier\_nameCert}
\begin{proof}
Inhabitedness is witnessed by $s_0$, since $S(s_0)$ and $P(s_0)$ are exactly the two components of $S_P(s_0)$.

For reflexivity, let $h$ be carried by $S_P$. Then $S(h)$ holds, so the reflexivity field of $\mathsf{cert}$ gives $h\sim_S h$. By the classifier clause of \autoref{def:subtype-restricted-classifier}, this is $h\sim_{S_P}h$.

For symmetry, a restricted witness $h\sim_{S_P}k$ is the parent witness $h\sim_S k$. The symmetry field of $\mathsf{cert}$ gives $k\sim_S h$, hence $k\sim_{S_P}h$.

For transitivity, restricted witnesses $h\sim_{S_P}k$ and $k\sim_{S_P}r$ are parent witnesses $h\sim_S k$ and $k\sim_S r$. The transitivity field of $\mathsf{cert}$ composes them to $h\sim_S r$, which is the restricted witness $h\sim_{S_P}r$.

Carrier transport for $S_P$ is exactly \autoref{lem:subtype-carrier-restricted-classifier-closure}. These five rows are the inhabitedness, reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity, and carrier-transport fields required by \autoref{def:lean-carrier-naming-certificate}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubtypeUp carrier-source obligation]
\label{thm:subtype-obligation-carrier-source}
Let $S:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be the carrier of a parent $\NameCert(S,\sim_S)$, let $P:\mathsf{BHist}\to\mathsf{Prop}$ be a predicate stable under the parent classifier, and let $s_0:\mathsf{BHist}$ be a displayed endpoint with $S(s_0)\land P(s_0)$. The carrier row for $\SubtypeUp$ is exactly
$$
S_P(h)\Longleftrightarrow S(h)\land P(h).
$$
The source data are the parent certificate $\NameCert(S,\sim_S)$, the predicate $P$, the displayed endpoint $s_0$, and the stability of $P$ under $\sim_S$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold \autoref{def:subtype-restricted-classifier}. The subtype carrier is introduced as the predicate cut of the parent carrier by $P$, so membership in $S_P$ is precisely the pair consisting of parent membership in $S$ and the predicate witness.

The endpoint row is supplied by $s_0$: its two witnesses are the two components needed for $S_P(s_0)$. Predicate stability is not extra carrier data; it is the transport hypothesis that makes the carrier row usable across the restricted classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubtypeUp restricted-classifier obligation]
\label{thm:subtype-obligation-restricted-classifier}
Under the hypotheses of \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-carrier-source}, the restricted classifier row for carried subtype endpoints is exactly
$$
h\sim_{S_P}k\Longleftrightarrow h\sim_S k.
$$
The parent projection row sends a restricted classifier witness to the parent witness, and the inclusion row sends a parent witness between carried subtype endpoints to the restricted witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:subtype-classifier-reflects-parent-classifier}. Its forward direction is the parent projection row: a restricted comparison contains the parent comparison between the displayed endpoints.

Its reverse direction is the inclusion row: once the two endpoints are carried by $S_P$, a parent classifier witness is already the restricted classifier witness by the definition of the subtype classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubtypeUp predicate-transport obligation]
\label{thm:subtype-obligation-predicate-transport}
Under the hypotheses of \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-carrier-source}, parent carrier transport together with stability of $P$ transports the subtype carrier across the restricted classifier: if $S_P(h)$ and $h\sim_{S_P}k$, then $S_P(k)$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{lem:subtype-carrier-restricted-classifier-closure}. That lemma separates the two transported components: the parent certificate transports $S(h)$ to $S(k)$ along the parent classifier, and stability of $P$ transports the predicate witness.

By \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-restricted-classifier}, the restricted comparison supplies exactly the parent comparison needed by both transports. Pairing the transported parent carrier witness with the transported predicate witness gives $S_P(k)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubtypeUp NameCert surface obligation]
\label{thm:subtype-obligation-namecert-surface}
The carrier row of \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-carrier-source}, the restricted-classifier rows of \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-restricted-classifier}, the endpoint row, and the transport row of \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-predicate-transport} assemble the displayed $\NameCert(S_P,\sim_{S_P})$ surface for $\SubtypeUp$. The ten-field template entries are: parent carrier $S$, parent classifier $\sim_S$, parent certificate $\NameCert(S,\sim_S)$, predicate $P$, predicate-stability row, restricted carrier $S_P$, restricted classifier $\sim_{S_P}$, displayed endpoint $s_0$, parent-projection row, and carrier-transport row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-carrier-source} for the carrier and endpoint rows, \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-restricted-classifier} for the parent-projection and inclusion rows, and \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-predicate-transport} for carrier transport.

These are exactly the rows consumed by \autoref{thm:subtype-restricted-classifier-namecert}. Applying that theorem repacks the rows into the public $\NameCert(S_P,\sim_{S_P})$ surface, with the ten displayed entries recording the source data and the exactness maps used by the construction.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubtypeUp public NameCert export]
\label{thm:subtype-public-namecert-export}
Under the hypotheses of \autoref{thm:subtype-restricted-classifier-namecert}, the public interface exported by $\SubtypeUp$ is the certificate
$$
\NameCert(S_P,\sim_{S_P}).
$$
Its public rows are the restricted carrier $S_P$, the restricted classifier $\sim_{S_P}$, the displayed endpoint, the parent-projection and inclusion rows, the predicate-stability transport row, and the assembled carrier-transport row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row is \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-carrier-source}: public membership in $S_P$ is exactly parent membership in $S$ together with the predicate witness $P$.

The restricted-classifier rows are \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-restricted-classifier}. They identify a public comparison in $\sim_{S_P}$ with the parent classifier comparison in $\sim_S$, with the forward parent-projection row and reverse inclusion row both displayed on carried subtype endpoints.

The predicate-transport row is \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-predicate-transport}. It combines parent carrier transport with stability of $P$ and produces the transported public carrier witness for $S_P$.

Apply \autoref{thm:subtype-restricted-classifier-namecert}. The preceding rows provide exactly the carrier, classifier, endpoint, and transport data consumed by that theorem, so the exported public surface is the certificate $\NameCert(S_P,\sim_{S_P})$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SubtypeUp standard bridge surface]
\label{thm:subtype-standard-bridge-surface}
Under the hypotheses of \autoref{thm:subtype-restricted-classifier-namecert}, the concrete restricted carrier $S_P$ and restricted classifier $\sim_{S_P}$ form the paper-side standard subtype bridge. Its rows are exactly:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier membership in the restricted endpoint is $S_P(h)$, equivalently $S(h)\land P(h)$;
\item classifier comparison between carried restricted endpoints is $h\sim_{S_P}k$, equivalently the parent comparison $h\sim_S k$;
\item the projection to the parent certificate preserves the carrier row by forgetting only the predicate component and preserves the classifier row by returning the same parent comparison;
\item predicate stability reflects transported parent endpoints back into $S_P$, so every transported parent endpoint with the transported predicate witness is again carried by the restricted carrier.
\end{enumerate}
Consequently the public $\NameCert(S_P,\sim_{S_P})$ surface exported by $\SubtypeUp$ is equipped with the bridge rows needed for the standard subtype bridge, without adding host subtype equality, quotient extensionality, or an abstract source-equivalence chain.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier row is exactly \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-carrier-source}. It identifies membership in the concrete restricted carrier with the pair of parent membership and predicate witness, and it records the displayed endpoint data used by the certificate.

The classifier row is exactly \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-restricted-classifier}. Its forward direction is the projection from a restricted comparison to the parent comparison, and its reverse direction is the inclusion of a parent comparison between carried restricted endpoints.

The transport row is \autoref{thm:subtype-obligation-predicate-transport}. Parent carrier transport supplies the transported parent endpoint, and predicate stability supplies the transported predicate witness; pairing them gives membership back in $S_P$.

Finally, \autoref{thm:subtype-public-namecert-export} repacks these rows into the public $\NameCert(S_P,\sim_{S_P})$ export. The bridge therefore uses only the concrete carrier, concrete classifier, parent projection, inclusion row, and predicate-stability transport already displayed in the $\SubtypeUp$ construction.
\end{proof}
