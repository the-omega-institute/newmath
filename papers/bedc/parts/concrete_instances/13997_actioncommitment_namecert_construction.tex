\begin{definition}[ActionCommitment carrier]
\label{def:action-commitment-carrier}
An $\ActionCommitmentUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(I,R,L,G,T,C,H,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the inscription-event row, consumed through $\InscriptionEventUp$ and $\FreeWillInscriptionCommitmentUp$;
\item $R$ is the selected route row, naming the finite route by which the event is accepted as the action-facing record rather than as an unselected future row;
\item $L$ is the local actualization row, recording that the selected route is read at the local inscription point;
\item $G$ is the gap and commitment row, binding the route to $\mathsf{GAP}$ provenance, $\CausalCommitmentUp$, $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$, and $\HardProblemClosureUp$ without adding a hidden agency source;
\item $T$ records classifier transport for the inscription, route, local actualization, and gap-commitment rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ route from focused ledger support through inscription event, selected route, local actualization, and commitment boundary;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $I,R,L,G,T,C,P,N$;
\item $Q$ is the replay row, requiring every downstream action read to replay the displayed route before reading the local NameCert row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ActionCommitmentUp$, $\HardProblemClosureUp$, $\FreeWillInscriptionCommitmentUp$, $\CausalCommitmentUp$, $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$, $\FocusedMultiHistLedgerUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ActionCommitmentUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is exactly this packet. It contains no hidden faculty of will, no selected future row outside $R$, no unledgered agency primitive, no host psychology predicate, and no off-packet causal necessity row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[ActionCommitment NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:action-commitment-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:action-commitment-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ActionCommitmentUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier exposure for $A=(I,R,L,G,T,C,H,Q,P,N)$, inscription-event accountability for $I$, selected-route accountability for $R$, local-actualization accountability for $L$, gap and sibling-commitment accountability for $G$, classifier stability through $T$ and $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$ and $Q$, package provenance through $P$, local naming through $N$, and non-escape from hidden free-will faculties, selected future rows, and unledgered agency primitives.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:action-commitment-carrier}. The rows $I,R,L,G,T,C,H,Q,P,N$ are the complete displayed inventory before a consumer may read the action commitment.

Read the action-facing content in order. The inscription-event row $I$ supplies the event surface, the selected route row $R$ names the finite route, and the local actualization row $L$ records the inscription point at which that route is read as action.

The gap and commitment row $G$ then attaches the sibling boundaries. $\FreeWillInscriptionCommitmentUp$ keeps the event from becoming a hidden faculty, $\CausalCommitmentUp$ keeps causal reading on the forward-GAP route, $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$ keeps public agency evidence in locality-cell form, and $\HardProblemClosureUp$ keeps closure pressure exposed as a boundary row.

Finally use $T,C,H,Q,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[ActionCommitment inscription route]
\label{thm:action-commitment-inscription-route}
Every accepted $\ActionCommitmentUp$ packet routes an action read through the displayed inscription-event path
$$
I,R,L,G,T,C,H,Q,P,N.
$$
The route begins with the inscription event, passes through the selected route and local actualization rows, exposes the gap and commitment boundary, and then replays only the displayed transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows. It exports no hidden free-will faculty, no selected future row, and no unledgered agency primitive outside that route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the inscription row. The packet contains a single action-facing event coordinate $I$, and it is read through the inscription-event discipline already used by $\FreeWillInscriptionCommitmentUp$.

The route is then selected and localized. The row $R$ names the finite route that becomes public record, while $L$ records the local actualization point. Neither row contains a future selector outside the accepted packet.

Next expose the commitment boundary. The row $G$ records the GAP and sibling-commitment handoff to $\CausalCommitmentUp$, $\OtherMindsCommitmentUp$, and $\HardProblemClosureUp$ without replacing those siblings by an agency substance.

The structural rows close the route. Componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming preserve $I,R,L,G$ as displayed rows; because no carrier coordinate names a hidden faculty, selected future row, or unledgered agency primitive, no consumer can extract one from the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
