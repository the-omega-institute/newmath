\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyAccelerationUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\CauchyAccelerationUp$ packet records the finite acceleration step by which an ordinary displayed Cauchy name is replaced by a cofinal regular rational name without changing the represented real. It sits after the criterion surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycriterion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-cauchy-criterion-namecert}, the regularization route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyregularization-namecert}, the windowed name surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet makes acceleration a named finite ledger: it does not select an ambient subsequence, quotient a Cauchy representative, or appeal to host completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy acceleration carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-acceleration-carrier}
A $\CauchyAccelerationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(S,T,\alpha,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source $\StreamNameUp$ row whose finite windows display the original rational Cauchy name.
\item $T$ is the dyadic tolerance schedule, read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, assigning the requested precision budget to each inspected window.
\item $\alpha$ is the cofinal acceleration map, stored as a finite monotone ledger saying which source window is read for each accelerated precision request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ proof row for the accelerated rational name, including the regularity inequalities obtained by reading $S$ through $\alpha$ and $T$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row, recording that the accelerated regular name reaches the same Real-facing boundary as the source name.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source stream, tolerance schedule, cofinality map, regular proof row, Real seal, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from an acceleration request through $S,T,\alpha,R,E$ and then to the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyAccelerationUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyAccelerationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a quotient representative of the original Cauchy name, a choice-selected subsequence, an ambient completeness theorem, or a host-side equality oracle for real numbers.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy acceleration NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-acceleration-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyAccelerationUp$ carrier $A=(S,T,\alpha,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyAccelerationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ source row $S$, dyadic tolerance schedule $T$, cofinal map $\alpha$, regular rational proof row $R$, and Real seal row $E$;
\item monotone cofinality, requiring $\alpha$ to preserve request order and to eventually reach every inspected source precision window;
\item dyadic tolerance preservation, requiring each accelerated request to read the same displayed tolerance budget through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ before it reaches the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row;
\item classifier transport, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the source stream, tolerance schedule, cofinality ledger, regular proof row, and Real seal under componentwise $\hsame$ and displayed replay;
\item ledger exhaustion through $P$ and $N$, excluding quotient representatives, choice-selected subsequences, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, and host real equality.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-acceleration-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes only the source stream $S$, tolerance schedule $T$, acceleration ledger $\alpha$, regular readback $R$, Real seal $E$, and the structural rows.

Monotone cofinality is the public content of $\alpha$. Since $\alpha$ is stored as a finite ledger over the inspected request window, each accelerated precision read names the source window it consumes and records that later requests do not move backward.

Dyadic tolerance preservation follows by reading $T$ before the regular rational proof row. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ receives exactly the tolerance entries displayed by $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, so the acceleration cannot import an unrecorded modulus or external precision oracle.

Classifier transport is componentwise through $H$ and replayed through $C$. These rows preserve $S,T,\alpha,R,E$ without adding a second classifier surface.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ name precisely the displayed packet. Because the carrier contains no quotient representative, choice-selected subsequence, ambient completeness coordinate, or host real equality oracle, the five listed rows exhaust the local naming obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy acceleration L10 handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-acceleration-l10-handoff}
Every accepted $\CauchyAccelerationUp$ carrier $A=(S,T,\alpha,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ exports only the finite route
$$
\StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \RealUp
$$
displayed by $S,T,\alpha,R,E,H,C,P,N$. Consequently every L10 consumer receives a $\RegSeqRatUp$-compatible and $\RealUp$-compatible accelerated name, while quotient representatives of the original Cauchy name, choice-selected subsequences, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness remain unavailable.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier and apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-acceleration-namecert-obligations}. The only public data are $S,T,\alpha,R,E,H,C,P,N$.

The source read begins at the $\StreamNameUp$ row $S$. The tolerance read then passes through $T$ and $\alpha$, so every accelerated precision request is attached to an explicit dyadic budget and a displayed source window.

The regular rational handoff is exactly $R$, and the Real-facing boundary is exactly $E$. Transport and replay through $H$ and $C$ preserve that route for downstream consumers.

Since $P$ and $N$ exhaust the packet and no forbidden coordinate appears in \autoref{def:cauchy-acceleration-carrier}, the exported object is only the displayed accelerated name compatible with $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$.
\end{proof}
