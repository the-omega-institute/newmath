\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\UniformCauchyFamilyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-uniform-cauchy-family-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\UniformCauchyFamilyUp$ packet records the finite NameCert surface for a displayed finite family of regular Cauchy rational names whose Cauchy control is witnessed by one shared threshold schedule. It sits between the stream-window source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readbacks of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the shared-tail criterion of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcauchycriterion-namecert}, the Cauchy-name consumer surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchysequencespace-namecert}, and the terminal real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names the family-level carrier consumed by uniform-tail and completion routes; it does not select a limit, quotient streams, host real equality, countable choice, or an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[UniformCauchyFamily carrier]
\label{def:uniform-cauchy-family-carrier}
A $\UniformCauchyFamilyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(F,\sigma,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $F$ is the finite family row, listing the inspected family members and their visible component identifiers;
\item $\sigma$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ schedule that selects one threshold window surface for the whole family;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ family readback row, assigning to each displayed component the rational readbacks inspected at the scheduled windows;
\item $D$ is the single $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used by every componentwise comparison in the family;
\item $T$ is an accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ row whose shared threshold consumes the same family row, schedule, regular readbacks, and dyadic tolerance ledger;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal handoff reached only after the shared-threshold criterion and regular readbacks have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the family row, shared schedule, regular readbacks, dyadic ledger, uniform criterion row, real seal, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from family inspection through the shared schedule, regular readbacks, dyadic tolerance ledger, uniform Cauchy criterion, and real-seal delivery;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UniformCauchyFamilyUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$, $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchyFamilyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier stores no separate threshold per component, no quotient stream, no selected host limit, no host equality of real names, no countable-choice selector, and no ambient completeness field.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyFamily NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-family-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\UniformCauchyFamilyUp$ carrier $U=(F,\sigma,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchyFamilyUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed family row, shared stream schedule, regular rational readbacks, dyadic tolerance ledger, accepted uniform Cauchy criterion row, real seal, transports, replay route, provenance, and local naming row;
\item shared-threshold exactness, requiring every componentwise Cauchy comparison to use the same $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $\sigma$ and the same $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$;
\item readback compatibility, requiring the $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows in $R$ to be the only rational names consumed by the shared criterion $T$;
\item criterion transport, requiring $T$ to remain attached to $F,\sigma,R,D$ under the componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay $C$;
\item real-seal non-escape, requiring the handoff $E$ to be reached only after $T$ and the componentwise readbacks in $R$ have been displayed;
\item CauchySequenceSpace compatibility, requiring any $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ consumer to read the family through the same $F,\sigma,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N$ route;
\item ledger exhaustion, stating that no per-component hidden threshold, quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness principle is exported.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:uniform-cauchy-family-carrier}. The tuple displays the finite family row, shared schedule, regular rational readbacks, dyadic tolerance ledger, uniform criterion row, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

Shared-threshold exactness is forced by the single schedule coordinate $\sigma$ and the single tolerance coordinate $D$. Every componentwise comparison named by the packet must therefore read the same window and tolerance ledger before consulting the uniform criterion row.

Readback compatibility and criterion transport are the role of $R,T,H,C$. The criterion row $T$ consumes the displayed regular readbacks in $R$, while $H$ and $C$ move and replay only those rows together with the family and tolerance data.

The real-seal and CauchySequenceSpace boundaries are downstream of the same route. Since $E$ and any $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ consumer are named after $F,\sigma,R,D,T,H,C,P,N$ have been displayed, neither can import a selected limit, a quotient stream, host real equality, countable choice, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyFamily common-window extraction]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-family-common-window-extraction}
Let $U=(F,\sigma,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\UniformCauchyFamilyUp$ packet. Every componentwise Cauchy comparison extracted from $U$ factors through the same finite route
$$
F,\sigma,R,D,T,H,C,P,N.
$$
In particular, the extracted comparison cannot replace the shared $\StreamNameUp$ schedule by a component-specific threshold, cannot replace the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks by an independent rational stream, and cannot replace the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger by a hidden host tolerance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The family row $F$ has only one schedule row $\sigma$, one regular readback family $R$, and one dyadic tolerance ledger $D$.

Use the shared-threshold and readback clauses of \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-family-namecert-obligations}. They force each componentwise comparison to read $\sigma,R,D$ before it reaches the accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ row $T$.

Transport and replay do not add coordinates. The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite route, so the extracted comparison has no access to a component-specific threshold, independent rational stream, or hidden host tolerance.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyFamily Real seal boundary]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-family-real-seal-boundary}
For an accepted $\UniformCauchyFamilyUp$ carrier $U=(F,\sigma,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)$, every $\RealUp$ seal read and every completion-facing consumer read of the family reaches $E$ only after passing through the finite family row $F$, shared $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $\sigma$, $\RegSeqRatUp$ family readbacks $R$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, and accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ row $T$. The boundary exports no quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, countable choice, or ambient completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a real-seal or completion-facing read of $U$. By the carrier definition, the only terminal seal coordinate is $E$.

The NameCert obligations place $E$ downstream of the shared-threshold criterion $T$ and the regular readback row $R$. Hence the read must first traverse $F,\sigma,R,D,T$.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same displayed route for equivalent packets and consumer replays. The carrier lists no quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, countable-choice selector, or ambient completeness coordinate, so the real seal boundary is exhausted by the stated finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyFamily shared-threshold transport]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-family-shared-threshold-transport}
Let $U=(F,\sigma,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\UniformCauchyFamilyUp$ carrier. The transport rows $H$ and $C$ move the single shared $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $\sigma$ and the single $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$ together with the displayed family row $F$ and the accepted criterion row $T$. Thus a transported component cannot acquire its own hidden threshold, tolerance ledger, or per-component Cauchy witness outside the route
$$
F,\sigma,R,D,T,H,C,P,N.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier coordinates. The only schedule coordinate available to the family is $\sigma$, and the only tolerance ledger coordinate available to componentwise comparisons is $D$.

The transport row $H$ is componentwise $\hsame$ over the displayed family row, shared schedule, regular readbacks, dyadic ledger, criterion row, real seal, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row. It therefore transports the criterion surface with $\sigma$ and $D$ still attached.

The replay row $C$ follows the same displayed route from family inspection through the shared schedule, regular readbacks, dyadic tolerance ledger, uniform criterion, and real seal. Since neither $H$ nor $C$ adds a threshold coordinate, every transported component remains bound to the shared schedule and ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyFamily Real seal factorization]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-family-real-seal-factorization}
For an accepted $\UniformCauchyFamilyUp$ carrier $U=(F,\sigma,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)$, every $\RealUp$ seal read factors through the finite packet
$$
F,\sigma,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
The seal coordinate $E$ is therefore readable only as the terminal handoff of the displayed family row, shared $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, $\RegSeqRatUp$ family readbacks, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ row, transport rows, replay row, provenance, and local $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchyFamilyUp}$ row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a $\RealUp$ seal read of $U$. By the carrier definition, the only seal coordinate is $E$, and it is listed after the family row, schedule, readbacks, tolerance ledger, and accepted criterion row.

The NameCert obligations require real-seal non-escape. Thus the read cannot reach $E$ before the shared criterion $T$ and the regular readback family $R$ have been displayed over $F,\sigma,D$.

Transport and replay preserve the factorization. The rows $H$ and $C$ move the same displayed data, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming for the packet; no extra seal source is available.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[UniformCauchyFamily CauchySequenceSpace compatibility]
\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-family-cauchy-sequence-space-compatibility}
Let $U=(F,\sigma,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\UniformCauchyFamilyUp$ carrier. Any $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ consumer of the family receives exactly the finite route
$$
F,\sigma,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N
$$
through the $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$, and $\RealUp$ surfaces. Such a consumer cannot import quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the consumer through the compatibility clause of \autoref{thm:uniform-cauchy-family-namecert-obligations}. The clause names the same carrier route $F,\sigma,R,D,T,E,H,C,P,N$ for every $\CauchySequenceSpaceUp$ consumer.

The upstream rows are finite and displayed. They pass through the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $\sigma$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks $R$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger $D$, the accepted $\UniformCauchyCriterionUp$ row $T$, and the $\RealUp$ handoff $E$.

The exclusion follows from ledger exhaustion. Since the carrier has no quotient-stream coordinate, selected-limit coordinate, countable-choice selector, host real equality, or ambient completeness row, the consumer cannot import any of those structures.
\end{proof}
