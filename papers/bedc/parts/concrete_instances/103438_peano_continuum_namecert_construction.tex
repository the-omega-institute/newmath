\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PeanoContinuumUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-peano-continuum-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Hahn and Mazurkiewicz, the $\PeanoContinuumUp$ packet records a compact, connected, locally connected metric continuum as finite BEDC NameCert data. It sits over the compact-metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the chain-connected route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-chain-connected-metric-namecert}, the complete separable metric boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-separable-metric-namecert}, the Polish-space readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polishspace-namecert}, and the continuous-map surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}. The packet names the finite continuum interface needed by interval-image and compact-metric consumers without importing host point-set topology, quotient point equality, open-cover compactness, countable choice, or the full Hahn--Mazurkiewicz theorem.

\begin{definition}[Peano continuum carrier]
\label{def:peano-continuum-carrier}
A $\PeanoContinuumUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(X,K,S,L,I,R,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the compact-metric source row, including the displayed metric carrier, finite compactness data, total-boundedness ledger, and complete metric handoff read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}.
\item $K$ is the connectedness row. It is either a directly displayed connectedness witness or a $\ChainConnectedMetricUp$ route whose finite chain ledgers are admitted as the connectedness-facing evidence for the same metric source.
\item $S$ is the complete-separable and Polish readback row. It records the common dense-window and completion boundary consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-separable-metric-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-polishspace-namecert}.
\item $L$ is the finite local-connectedness refinement ledger. For each displayed compact-window request, it records a finite family of connected neighbourhood rows, inclusion refinements, overlap certificates, and radius comparisons over the source metric.
\item $I$ is the interval and continuum readback row. It records the finite interval-source windows and continuum-facing observations that later consumers read before asking for an interval image or a compact continuum boundary.
\item $R$ is the continuous readback row, exposing only the $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph and modulus rows that connect the interval windows in $I$ to the compact-metric source row $X$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the compact-metric source, connectedness route, complete-separable and Polish readback, local-connectedness ledger, interval and continuum readback, continuous-map rows, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
X,K,S,L,I \longmapsto R \longmapsto H,Q,N .
$$
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PeanoContinuumUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ChainConnectedMetricUp$, $\CompleteSeparableMetricUp$, $\PolishSpaceUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PeanoContinuumUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,K,S,L,I,R,H,C,Q,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for host point-set topology, open-cover compactness, quotient point equality, countable choice, a selected space-filling map, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Peano continuum NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:peano-continuum-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\PeanoContinuumUp$ carrier
$$
P=(X,K,S,L,I,R,H,C,Q,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\PeanoContinuumUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the compact-metric source, connectedness route, complete-separable and Polish readback, local-connectedness ledger, interval and continuum readback, continuous readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item compact-metric handoff, requiring every metric, compactness, finite-net, and completion-facing read to pass through the single source row $X$;
\item connectedness visibility, requiring $K$ to expose either the displayed connectedness evidence or the finite $\ChainConnectedMetricUp$ route before a continuum-facing consumer may read the packet;
\item local-connectedness refinement, requiring $L$ to record finite connected-neighbourhood refinements, inclusion rows, overlap certificates, and radius comparisons for each displayed compact-window request;
\item continuous-image readback nonescape, requiring $I$ and $R$ to be displayed before any interval-image or continuum read is exported;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $H,C,Q,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name exactly the rows listed above and no host point-set topology, open-cover compactness, quotient point equality, countable-choice selector, full Hahn--Mazurkiewicz theorem, Lean verification, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No Peano-continuum obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $P=(X,K,S,L,I,R,H,C,Q,N)$. The row list in \autoref{def:peano-continuum-carrier} is the whole visible packet, so carrier admission is projection from the displayed finite $\BHist$ data.

The compact-metric handoff is forced by the source coordinate. Every metric, compactness, finite-net, and completion-facing request first reads $X$, and the complete-separable and Polish row $S$ is only a readback of that same displayed source.

Connectedness is visible through $K$. If the packet uses direct connectedness evidence, the row is read as displayed data; if it uses chain-connectedness, the finite $\ChainConnectedMetricUp$ route supplies the endpoint, chain, radius, and tolerance ledgers over the same source $X$.

Local connectedness is confined to $L$. Each refinement request is answered by a finite ledger of connected neighbourhood rows, inclusion refinements, overlap certificates, and radius comparisons, so no host open-set lattice or point-set topology is introduced.

The interval and continuous-image rows are downstream. A consumer must read $I$ and then $R$ before receiving an interval-image or continuum observation, and $R$ exposes only the graph and modulus rows admitted by the continuous-map surface.

Finally $H,C,Q,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same rows componentwise. Since the refused principles have no carrier coordinate and no replay edge, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Peano continuum locally connected handoff]
\label{thm:peano-continuum-locally-connected-handoff}
Every continuum-facing consumer read of an accepted $\PeanoContinuumUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
X \longmapsto K \longmapsto S \longmapsto L \longmapsto I \longmapsto R \longmapsto H,C,Q,N .
$$
Thus the consumer receives the compact-metric row, connectedness row, finite local-connectedness refinement ledger, interval and continuum readback, continuous readback, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming. It receives no host point-set topology, open-cover compactness, quotient point equality, countable choice, selected space-filling map, full Hahn--Mazurkiewicz theorem, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Start with a continuum-facing read of the accepted packet. The compact-metric source row $X$ is the first admissible row because every later interval, local-connectedness, and completion-facing observation is measured over that source.

The connectedness and complete-separable boundaries are then fixed. The row $K$ supplies the displayed connectedness or chain-connectedness route, while $S$ records the complete-separable and Polish readback for the same metric data rather than for a second host space.

Next read the local-connectedness ledger $L$. Its finite refinement rows give the connected neighbourhood, inclusion, overlap, and radius-comparison data required by the consumer before the interval readback is exposed.

The interval and continuous readback rows complete the functional handoff. The row $I$ supplies the interval and continuum observations, and $R$ supplies the continuous-map graph and modulus rows through which those observations are replayed.

The structural rows close the route. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming preserve exactly this dependency order, so every consumer read factors through the compact-metric row, connectedness row, finite local-connectedness ledger, continuous readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\end{proof}
