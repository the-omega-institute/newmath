\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NormalizationByEvaluationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-normalization-by-evaluation-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Berger and Schwichtenberg, normalization by evaluation is read as a finite semantic-evaluation and reification packet for typed terms. The $\NormalizationByEvaluationUp$ carrier sits beside the lambda-calculus certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lambdacalc-namecert}, the first-order syntax interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-firstorder-namecert}, and the candidate-mediated MetaCIC normalization audit of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-candidate-strong-normalization-namecert}. It names only the displayed typed-term syntax, environment, semantic-value, neutral-reflection, readback, evaluation-ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local NameCert rows needed for a readback consumer. It does not assert a host normalization function, unrestricted subject reduction, evidence-free MetaCIC strong normalization, quotient syntax equality, or a completed semantic model.

\begin{definition}[NormalizationByEvaluation carrier]
\label{def:normalization-by-evaluation-carrier}
A $\NormalizationByEvaluationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(T,G,V,NF,R,Ev,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the typed-term row, carrying the source term, its type, and the binder context visible to the readback consumer.
\item $G$ is the environment row, recording finite semantic assignments for the free variables and context entries named by $T$.
\item $V$ is the semantic-value row obtained by evaluating the displayed term under $G$ through the packet ledger.
\item $NF$ is the neutral-reflection row, separating variables and neutral applications that are reflected into semantic values from already reified normal forms.
\item $R$ is the normal-form readback row, turning accepted semantic values into displayed beta-normal eta-long syntax when the packet admits such readback.
\item $Ev$ is the evaluation ledger, recording each semantic-evaluation step, reification step, substitution replay, and refusal boundary used by the carrier.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for typed terms, environments, semantic values, neutral-reflection rows, readback rows, evaluation ledgers, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a requested typed-term read through $T,G,V,NF,R,Ev$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LambdaCalcUp$, $\FirstOrderUp$, $\MetaCICCandidateStrongNormalizationUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NormalizationByEvaluationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier accepts the packet only when semantic evaluation and readback are both recorded as finite rows. No coordinate may be a host-language evaluator, a global normalization oracle, a quotient of syntax trees, or an unlogged semantic model.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NormalizationByEvaluation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:normalization-by-evaluation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\NormalizationByEvaluationUp$ carrier $E=(T,G,V,NF,R,Ev,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\NormalizationByEvaluationUp}$ surface consists of the following six obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the typed-term row, environment row, semantic-value row, neutral-reflection row, readback row, evaluation ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item semantic evaluation, requiring $V$ to be obtained from the displayed term and environment through the finite ledger $Ev$ rather than a hidden evaluator;
\item neutral reflection, requiring reflected variables and neutral applications in $NF$ to remain tied to the binder context and semantic assignments named by $T,G$;
\item reification and readback, requiring $R$ to read only accepted semantic values and neutral reflections before producing a displayed normal form;
\item substitution stability, requiring context extension and substitution replay to pass through the same $T,G,V,NF,R,Ev$ route under componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item no host normalization oracle, excluding external evaluators, quotient syntax equality, completed semantic models, unrestricted subject reduction, and evidence-free MetaCIC strong normalization.
\end{enumerate}
No accepted NameCert row is stored outside these finite coordinates.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:normalization-by-evaluation-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the typed-term, environment, semantic-value, neutral-reflection, readback, ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local-name rows.

Semantic evaluation is confined to the ledger $Ev$. A consumer may inspect $V$ only after the displayed term $T$ and environment $G$ have been replayed, so the packet cannot consult a separate evaluator.

Neutral reflection and readback are then separated by $NF$ and $R$. Reflection keeps variables and neutral applications attached to the visible context, while readback consumes accepted semantic values and neutral rows before producing normal-form syntax.

Substitution stability is recorded by the same route. Componentwise transport $H$ and continuation replay $C$ move the typed-term context, environment assignments, semantic values, neutral rows, readback rows, and ledger rows together, so substitution cannot bypass the displayed evaluation packet.

The provenance and local naming rows restrict the sibling imports to the lambda-calculus, first-order syntax, and candidate-mediated MetaCIC normalization surfaces cited above. Since no carrier coordinate stores a host evaluator, quotient syntax equality, completed semantic model, or evidence-free strong-normalization theorem, the listed rows are the whole NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}
