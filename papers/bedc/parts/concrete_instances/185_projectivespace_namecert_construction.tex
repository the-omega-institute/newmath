\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ProjectiveSpaceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-projectivespace-namecert}

The projective-space interface $\ProjectiveSpaceUp$ packages the quotient of a punctured vector-space carrier by the scaling action of the underlying field's multiplicative group. It depends on $\NameCert_{\VecSpaceUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\FieldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-field-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:projectivespace-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[ProjectiveSpaceUp punctured carrier obligation]
\label{thm:projectivespace-punctured-carrier-obligation}
The $\ProjectiveSpaceUp$ carrier is the $\VecSpaceUp$ carrier of
\autoref{def:vecspace-carrier} equipped with the $\FieldUp$ nonzero-vector
guard supplied by \autoref{def:field-source-specification} and the certified
scalar-action rows required by \autoref{def:vecspace-certificate-obligations}.
A carried projective endpoint is therefore a vector-space row whose
representative is guarded as nonzero over the field source, together with the
ledger entries recording the scalar source, vector source, and scalar-action
certificate used to read that row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProjectiveSpaceUp.ProjectiveSpacePuncturedCarrier\_obligation}
\begin{proof}
Start with the vector carrier row from \autoref{def:vecspace-carrier}. The
certificate obligations in \autoref{def:vecspace-certificate-obligations}
provide the scalar-action closure and congruence rows over the same scalar
source.

The field source specification in \autoref{def:field-source-specification}
supplies the nonzero guard and the proof-indexed inverse operations available
for guarded scalars. Attaching that guard to the vector representative removes
the zero row from the visible projective carrier while leaving the underlying
$\VecSpaceUp$ provenance unchanged.

Thus the carrier predicate is exactly the punctured vector-space row together
with the field and scalar-action certificate rows. No host quotient carrier or
external projective-point object is introduced.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProjectiveSpaceUp.ProjectiveSpacePuncturedCarrier\_empty\_boundary}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProjectiveSpaceUp.ProjectiveSpaceSingletonPuncturedCarrier\_obligation}

\begin{theorem}[ProjectiveSpaceUp scaling-orbit classifier obligation]
\label{thm:projectivespace-scaling-orbit-classifier-obligation}
Two carried $\ProjectiveSpaceUp$ rows classify exactly when a certified
nonzero $\FieldUp$ scalar transports one guarded representative to the other
by the $\VecSpaceUp$ scalar action. The classifier is reflexive by scalar
one, symmetric by the proof-indexed inverse of the scaling scalar, and
transitive by field multiplication together with scalar-action associativity.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For reflexivity, use the scalar one row from the field source and the
$\VecSpaceUp$ scalar unit law from
\autoref{def:vecspace-certificate-obligations}. Acting by one leaves the
guarded representative in the same vector classifier row.

For symmetry, suppose a nonzero scalar transports representative $v$ to
representative $w$. The field source supplies the proof-indexed inverse for
that scalar, and the vector-space scalar-action rows identify inverse action
as the return route from $w$ to $v$ inside the same carried surface.

For transitivity, compose two certified nonzero scaling witnesses by field
multiplication. The scalar-action associativity row in the vector-space
certificate identifies the double action with action by the product scalar,
so the resulting witness transports the first representative to the third.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ProjectiveSpaceUp.ProjectiveSpaceSingletonScalingOrbitClassifier\_obligation}

\begin{theorem}[ProjectiveSpaceUp ledger and exactness obligation]
\label{thm:projectivespace-ledger-and-exactness-obligation}
For $\ProjectiveSpaceUp$, representative choice, nonzero scalar witnesses,
and scaling-chain provenance remain ledger data. The visible exactness
predicate is exactly the scaling-orbit classifier on punctured
$\VecSpaceUp$ rows: consumers may see the carrier of
\autoref{thm:projectivespace-punctured-carrier-obligation} and the classifier
of \autoref{thm:projectivespace-scaling-orbit-classifier-obligation}, but
they cannot inspect a chosen representative or a chosen scaling chain as
public carrier data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the certificate ledger into three hidden fields: the selected
punctured representative, each nonzero scalar witness used to compare
representatives, and the chain of scalar-action steps witnessing a composite
comparison. These entries are consumed by the carrier and classifier rows but
are not themselves part of the visible endpoint predicate.

Exactness is then read at the boundary. Soundness says every visible
classification comes from a certified nonzero scalar-action route between
punctured vector rows. Completeness says every such certified route repacks as
the displayed projective classifier.

Hence the public surface exposes precisely punctured-carrier membership and
scaling-orbit classification. All representative and route choices remain
ledger provenance.
\end{proof}
