\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetricCompletionUniquenessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metric-completion-uniqueness-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges separated completion uniqueness and the Bourbaki presentation of completions by universal dense maps, the $\MetricCompletionUniquenessUp$ packet records the finite BEDC boundary at which two completion presentations over the same metric source may be compared. It consumes the dense embedding route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionembedding-namecert}, the finite-window completion handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletionfinite-namecert}, the separated classifier route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetricreflection-namecert}, and the general separated-completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedcompletion-namecert}. The packet names uniqueness only as a routed comparison between presentations; it does not introduce quotient completion objects, selected representatives, or host equality of completed points.

\begin{definition}[MetricCompletionUniqueness carrier]
\label{def:metric-completion-uniqueness-carrier}
A $\MetricCompletionUniquenessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(M,E_0,F_0,R_0,S_0,E_1,F_1,R_1,S_1,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the common $\MetricUp$ source row whose point classifier and distance witnesses are shared by both completion-facing presentations.
\item $E_i$ is the $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ dense row for presentation $i\in\{0,1\}$, recording how source observations enter that presentation.
\item $F_i$ is the $\MetricCompletionFiniteUp$ handoff row for presentation $i$, exposing only finite-window completion reads downstream of $M$ and $E_i$.
\item $R_i$ is the $\SeparatedMetricReflectionUp$ classifier route for presentation $i$, comparing completion-facing reads by separated zero-distance evidence.
\item $S_i$ is the $\SeparatedCompletionUp$ uniqueness boundary for presentation $i$, read over the same $M,E_i,F_i,R_i$ route.
\item $Q$ is the local comparison ledger. It records that both presentations read the same source row $M$, that dense rows are consumed before finite handoff rows, that classifier routes are consumed before uniqueness rows, and that the comparison never leaves the displayed separated routes.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M$, the two dense rows, the two finite handoff rows, the two separated-reflection rows, the two separated-completion rows, the comparison ledger, and the structural rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from source metric observations through each dense row, finite handoff, separated classifier, separated-completion boundary, and then through $Q$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricUp$, $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$, $\MetricCompletionFiniteUp$, $\SeparatedMetricReflectionUp$, $\SeparatedCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionUniquenessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion, selected representatives, host equality of completed points, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionUniqueness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-completion-uniqueness-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetricCompletionUniquenessUp$ carrier
$$
U=(M,E_0,F_0,R_0,S_0,E_1,F_1,R_1,S_1,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\MetricCompletionUniquenessUp}$ surface consists exactly of these finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the common source metric row, the two dense embedding rows, the two finite completion handoff rows, the two separated-reflection classifier routes, the two separated-completion uniqueness boundaries, the comparison ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item common-source agreement, requiring both presentations to read the same $M$ before any completion-facing comparison is admitted;
\item dense-to-finite order, requiring each $E_i$ to precede the corresponding finite handoff $F_i$;
\item classifier-before-uniqueness order, requiring each separated-reflection route $R_i$ to precede the corresponding separated-completion boundary $S_i$;
\item comparison exactness, requiring $Q$ to compare only the two displayed routes $M,E_i,F_i,R_i,S_i$ and their structural rows;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding quotient completion, selected representatives, host equality of completed points, countable choice, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetricCompletionUniquenessUp.TasteGate.MetricCompletionUniquenessTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:metric-completion-uniqueness-carrier}. The tuple lists the common source, the two completion-facing routes, the comparison ledger, and the structural rows.

Common-source agreement is the first dependency. Both presentations expose the same $M$, so a comparison cannot be formed from unrelated metric sources or from an external completed host space.

Dense-to-finite order is read separately on the two sides. For each $i$, the dense row $E_i$ is displayed before the finite handoff $F_i$, so no selected representative or quotient point can replace the source-to-completion route.

Classifier-before-uniqueness order is also sidewise. The separated-reflection route $R_i$ supplies the zero-distance classifier boundary before the separated-completion row $S_i$ is read as a uniqueness boundary.

Finally $Q,H,C,P,N$ compare, transport, replay, source, and name only those displayed rows. Since the carrier has no quotient, representative, host-equality, choice, compactness, ambient-completeness, Lean, or bridge coordinate, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionUniqueness separated route]
\label{thm:metric-completion-uniqueness-separated-route}
Every comparison exported by an accepted $\MetricCompletionUniquenessUp$ carrier factors through the finite separated route
$$
M\longmapsto (E_0,F_0,R_0,S_0)\quad\text{and}\quad M\longmapsto(E_1,F_1,R_1,S_1)\longmapsto Q,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus separated uniqueness is read only after both completion presentations have consumed the same source metric row, dense embedding route, finite completion handoff, separated classifier route, and separated-completion boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a consumer comparison admitted by the packet. Carrier admission exposes the two routes and the common source row $M$.

On each side, the dense row precedes the finite handoff. The consumer therefore reaches $F_i$ only through $E_i$ and never through a selected representative, quotient class, or host completion point.

The separated-reflection row $R_i$ then supplies the classifier route used by the corresponding separated-completion row $S_i$. This places zero-distance classifier evidence before any uniqueness read.

The comparison ledger $Q$ is downstream of both sidewise routes. The transport and continuation rows $H,C$ preserve the same route, while $P,N$ record provenance and local naming. Hence the exported comparison factors through the displayed separated route and nothing else.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetricCompletionUniqueness non-escape]
\label{thm:metric-completion-uniqueness-nonescape}
An accepted $\MetricCompletionUniquenessUp$ packet exports no quotient completion, no selected representative, no host equality of completed points, no countable-choice schedule, no open-cover compactness row, no ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, no Lean verification, and no bridge checking. Every uniqueness-facing read is confined to the displayed rows
$$
M,E_0,F_0,R_0,S_0,E_1,F_1,R_1,S_1,Q,H,C,P,N .
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:metric-completion-uniqueness-namecert-obligations}. They enumerate the common source row, the two dense-to-finite completion routes, the two separated classifier and uniqueness routes, the comparison ledger, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming as the whole public surface.

Use the separated route theorem, \autoref{thm:metric-completion-uniqueness-separated-route}, for any uniqueness-facing comparison. It forces the comparison through $M,E_i,F_i,R_i,S_i$ on both sides before $Q,H,C,P,N$ can be consumed.

No remaining coordinate can supply quotient completion data, representatives, host equality, choice, compactness, ambient completeness, Lean evidence, or bridge evidence. Therefore those excluded objects cannot be projected from an accepted packet.
\end{proof}
