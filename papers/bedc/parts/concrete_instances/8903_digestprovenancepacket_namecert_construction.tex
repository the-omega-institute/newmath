The $\DigestProvenancePacketUp$ carrier is the concrete NameCert realization of the digest-provenance discipline from \autoref{ch:visions-hash-like-apophatic-fixed-point}. It sits downstream of the finite hash transcript surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hash-namecert}, the negative socket naming discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert}, and the visible supply refusal ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supplysocketledger-namecert}. Its purpose is narrow: a digest may be used as a visible token only after the packet displays the source row, fiber-membership row, GAP ledger, exactness or refusal row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

\falsifiablePrediction{Every accepted DigestProvenancePacketUp consumer that reads source content must pass through the displayed fiber-membership and GAP ledger rows before any source equality claim.}

\independenceWitness{hash, apophaticname, apophaticsocket, transcendentalsupplyledger, supplysocketledger: none is bijective to $\DigestProvenancePacketUp$ because this carrier binds the visible digest token, source row, fiber-membership row, GAP ledger, exactness or refusal row, hsame transport, Cont replay, provenance, and local NameCert row in one packet, while the siblings isolate hash evaluation, negative naming, socket request, supply taxonomy, or supply-kind ledger surfaces without the source-fiber discipline.}

\begin{definition}[DigestProvenancePacket carrier]
\label{def:digest-provenance-packet-carrier}
A $\DigestProvenancePacketUp$ carrier is a finite packet
$$
D=(V,S,F,G,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
over the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $V$ is the visible digest-token row, naming the token that downstream consumers may compare, store, or route;
\item $S$ is the source row, recording the finite source class and the local source-channel handle without identifying the digest with the source;
\item $F$ is the fiber-membership row, recording the generated relation that the source row belongs to the visible digest fiber;
\item $G$ is the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger row, recording any far-end or missing-source supply required by the fiber reading;
\item $E$ is the exactness or refusal row, recording whether the packet has an exactness certificate for a particular source claim or refuses source equality;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the visible digest, source, fiber, ledger, exactness, replay, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes by which consumers inspect $V,S,F,G,E,H,P,N$ in order;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the digest token, source row, fiber relation, GAP ledger, exactness or refusal row, transports, replay routes, and local naming surface;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DigestProvenancePacketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a global universe digest, digest-as-source equality, object-level $\mathsf{T}$, hidden preimage oracle, quotient source equality, or host hash-function semantics.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[DigestProvenancePacket NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:digest-provenance-packet-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\DigestProvenancePacketUp$ carrier
$$
D=(V,S,F,G,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\DigestProvenancePacketUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the nine displayed rows, token visibility through $V$, source-channel display through $S$, fiber-membership exactness through $F$, GAP ledger exposure through $G$, exactness or refusal through $E$, componentwise transport through $H$, replay locality through $C$, package provenance through $P$, local naming through $N$, and non-escape from the refused source and far-end supplies.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DigestProvenancePacketUp.DigestProvenancePacketCarrier\_namecert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:digest-provenance-packet-carrier}. The visible inventory is exactly $V,S,F,G,E,H,C,P,N$, so carrier admission and local naming are attached to these rows and to no hidden digest substrate.

Token visibility is read through $V$, while source-channel display is read through $S$. The hash-facing comparison surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hash-namecert} may supply digest transcripts, but the packet does not let such transcripts replace the displayed source row.

Fiber exactness and ledger exposure are the rows $F$ and $G$. They force every source-sensitive read to name both the fiber-membership relation and the GAP entry that records missing far-end or source supply.

The exactness row $E$ then separates certified source claims from refused ones. Transports and replay through $H$ and $C$ preserve this separation, and provenance through $P$ records which local packet is being consumed.

Thus the NameCert surface refuses a global universe digest, digest-as-source equality, object-level $\mathsf{T}$, hidden preimage oracle, quotient source equality, and host hash-function semantics. The apophatic naming discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert} and the supply ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supplysocketledger-namecert} are consumed only as visible boundary rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DigestProvenancePacket no hidden source consumption]
\label{thm:digest-provenance-packet-no-hidden-source-consumption}
Every accepted consumer of a $\DigestProvenancePacketUp$ carrier that reads source content must factor its read through the displayed source row $S$, fiber-membership row $F$, GAP ledger $G$, exactness or refusal row $E$, transport row $H$, replay route $C$, provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$ before making any source equality claim. A consumer that reads only the digest token $V$ obtains a visible digest token and no source content.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DigestProvenancePacketUp.DigestProvenancePacketCarrier\_no\_hidden\_source\_consumption}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer read of an accepted packet $D=(V,S,F,G,E,H,C,P,N)$. If the read only asks for the digest token, \autoref{def:digest-provenance-packet-carrier} exposes $V$ as a visible token and provides no source row outside the packet.

Now suppose the read asks for source content. The source channel is $S$, and the source may be attached to the token only through the fiber-membership row $F$. Without $F$, the read has a digest token and a displayed source row but no generated relation between them.

The next required row is the GAP ledger $G$. It records whether the fiber far end, source supply, or missing exactness data has been socketed rather than silently consumed. This is the same refusal posture enforced by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert} and by the supply-ledger surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supplysocketledger-namecert}.

The exactness or refusal row $E$ controls equality-facing claims. If $E$ contains a local exactness certificate, the claim remains scoped to the displayed packet; if $E$ is a refusal row, the source equality claim is unavailable. In neither case does the digest become the source.

Finally route the read through $H,C,P,N$. Componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming preserve the same packet and do not add a preimage oracle, quotient representative, host hash semantics, global digest universe, or object-level $\mathsf{T}$ coordinate. Therefore no source content is consumed unless the displayed fiber and GAP ledger rows have already been opened.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[DigestProvenancePacket source-fiber ledger order]
\label{thm:digest-provenance-packet-source-fiber-ledger-order}
For every accepted $\DigestProvenancePacketUp$ carrier
$$
D=(V,S,F,G,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
each source-sensitive read must expose the source row $S$, then the fiber-membership row $F$, then the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger row $G$, before the exactness or refusal row $E$ may authorize or refuse an equality-facing source claim. A read that has not passed through $S,F,G$ has no packet-local authority to assert source equality, digest-as-source equality, quotient source equality, hidden preimage recovery, or host hash-function semantics.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.DigestProvenancePacketUp.DigestProvenancePacketCarrier\_source\_fiber\_ledger\_order}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier definition. The rows of \autoref{def:digest-provenance-packet-carrier} place the visible digest token $V$ beside a source row $S$, then a fiber-membership row $F$, then the $\mathsf{GAP}$ ledger $G$, with $E$ recording only the exactness or refusal status that follows those displayed rows.

Now use no hidden source consumption. \autoref{thm:digest-provenance-packet-no-hidden-source-consumption} forces every source-content read through $S,F,G,E,H,C,P,N$ before any source equality claim, while a token-only read exposes $V$ and no source content.

It remains to identify the order relevant to equality-facing claims. The carrier gives no coordinate that can replace $S$ by a hidden source, no coordinate that can replace $F$ by a host preimage oracle, and no coordinate that can replace $G$ by silent far-end supply. Therefore $E$ can authorize or refuse only after the source, fiber, and ledger rows have been opened in that order.
\end{proof}
