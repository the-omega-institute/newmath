The $\AuditWritingDisciplineUp$ packet realizes the audit-methodology discipline of \autoref{sec:visions-philosophy-audit-methodology} inside the auditable-generativity programme of \autoref{ch:visions-philosophy-auditable-generativity}. It sits beside the registry export surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registry-export-surface-namecert}, the claim registry layer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-claimregistrylayer-namecert}, the machine-interface socket of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-machine-interface-socket-namecert}, the standard bridge audit packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-standardbridgeauditpacket-namecert}, and the proof trace audit witness of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prooftraceauditwitness-namecert}. Those siblings expose registry export, claim admission, machine routing, bridge audit, and proof-trace readback; this carrier binds them into one writing packet before a public claim may be read as auditable prose.

\begin{definition}[Audit writing discipline carrier]
\label{def:audit-writing-discipline-carrier}
An $\AuditWritingDisciplineUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(W,C,K,L,T,F,G,R,H,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is the writing-item row, naming the claim, paragraph, theorem, definition, or closure-status site being exported.
\item $C$ is the carrier row, recording the BEDC carrier or packet surface to which the writing item is attached.
\item $K$ is the classifier row, recording the classifier, membership, or exactness predicate that licenses the writing item.
\item $L$ is the ledger row, recording the finite audit ledger entries consumed by the writing item.
\item $T$ is the theory-axis row, recording the theory-closure grade as a paper-side coordinate.
\item $F$ is the formal-axis row, recording the formal-verification grade as an independent Lean-side coordinate.
\item $G$ is the gap/refusal row, recording missing bridge data, refused readings, blocked host resources, and unsupported consumer routes.
\item $R$ is the registry row, recording how the claim is admitted, blocked, or handed to the registry export surface.
\item $H$ is the transport row, recording machine-interface routing, standard-bridge audit transport, and proof-trace readback through the sibling surfaces.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$, $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, $\MachineInterfaceSocketUp$, $\StandardBridgeAuditPacketUp$, $\ProofTraceAuditWitnessUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\AuditWritingDisciplineUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a prose-only claim, a collapsed theory/formal status, a hidden registry override, a proof-trace read without refusal data, or a host bridge imported as BEDC evidence.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Audit writing discipline obligation surface]
\label{thm:audit-writing-discipline-obligation-surface}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:audit-writing-discipline-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\AuditWritingDisciplineUp}$ surface consists exactly of six obligation rows: writing-item admission through $W$; carrier and classifier exposure through $C,K$; ledger exactness through $L$; independent theory and formal status reporting through $T,F$; gap and refusal exposure through $G$; and registry, transport, provenance, and local naming through $R,H,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.AuditWritingDisciplineUp.AuditWritingDisciplineUp\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed packet of \autoref{def:audit-writing-discipline-carrier}. The rows $W,C,K,L,T,F,G,R,H,P,N$ are the only available coordinates, so the writing item must first name its carrier, classifier, and ledger route.

Read the status rows next. The theory-axis row $T$ records paper closure, while the formal-axis row $F$ records Lean verification status. Since they are separate rows, the carrier has no rule that turns one status coordinate into the other.

Finally attach gap/refusal, registry, transport, provenance, and local naming. Rows $G,R,H,P,N$ keep unsupported readings, claim export, machine routing, bridge audit, and proof-trace readback visible in the same packet, giving exactly the six local obligation rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Audit writing discipline nonescape]
\label{thm:audit-writing-discipline-nonescape}
Let
$$
A=(W,C,K,L,T,F,G,R,H,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\AuditWritingDisciplineUp$ packet. Every public claim read factors through the displayed writing item, carrier, classifier, ledger, theory axis, formal axis, gap/refusal, registry, transport, provenance, and local naming rows. No public consumer may replace those rows by prose emphasis, host equality, unchecked bridge evidence, private registry state, hidden proof trace, or a single status coordinate that merges $T$ and $F$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the obligation surface of \autoref{thm:audit-writing-discipline-obligation-surface}. It forces the public writing item to expose its carrier, classifier, ledger, status axes, refusal surface, registry route, transport, provenance, and local name together.

If the route omits $T$ or $F$, the consumer no longer knows which axis is being cited. If the route omits $G$, unsupported readings and refusal boundaries are erased. If it omits $R$ or $H$, the claim no longer passes through registry export or machine-facing transport.

Thus every accepted claim remains inside the displayed finite packet. The excluded prose, host, bridge, registry, proof-trace, and collapsed-status readings have no coordinate from which to enter the $\AuditWritingDisciplineUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Audit writing discipline registry handoff]
\label{thm:audit-writing-discipline-registry-handoff}
For every accepted $\AuditWritingDisciplineUp$ packet $A=(W,C,K,L,T,F,G,R,H,P,N)$, the claim admitted by $W$ is exported only through the registry row $R$ and the sibling surfaces $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$ and $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$. The machine-facing route in $H$ may cite $\MachineInterfaceSocketUp$, $\StandardBridgeAuditPacketUp$, and $\ProofTraceAuditWitnessUp$ only after the registry row has preserved the distinct carrier, classifier, ledger, theory-axis, formal-axis, and refusal rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read registry admission through $R$. By the obligation surface, $R$ is attached to the same writing item, carrier, classifier, ledger, theory axis, formal axis, refusal surface, provenance, and local name.

Next use the sibling registry surfaces. $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$ records whether the claim is admitted or refused, while $\RegistryExportSurfaceUp$ records whether it may leave the registry as a public export.

Only after those rows are displayed may the transport row $H$ route the claim toward $\MachineInterfaceSocketUp$, $\StandardBridgeAuditPacketUp$, or $\ProofTraceAuditWitnessUp$. Since $H$ is part of the same packet, it cannot erase $C,K,L,T,F,G$ or replace them by a hidden bridge or proof-trace reading.
\end{proof}
