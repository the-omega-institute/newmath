The $\TwinSubstrateAuditHandshakeUp$ packet realizes the twin-substrate synthesis boundary of \autoref{ch:twin-substrate-synthesis} as a finite NameCert handshake. It sits beside the GroundCompiler audit map of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-groundcompilerauditmap-namecert} and the twin-substrate audit coupling of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-twinsubstrateauditcoupling-namecert}: those chapters expose the compiler-facing audit map and the paired audit coupling, while this chapter names the narrower handshake that lets a reader inspect both audit surfaces without converting one substrate into the other.

\begin{definition}[Twin-substrate audit-handshake carrier]
\label{def:twin-substrate-audit-handshake-carrier}
A $\TwinSubstrateAuditHandshakeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(M,G,B,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the MetaCIC audit row, carrying closed-CIC theorem, reduction, substitution, normalisation, confluence, decidability, consistency, and obstruction-boundary references;
\item $G$ is the GroundCompiler audit row, carrying event-flow, channel legality, generated recognizer, report-boundary, hidden-input exclusion, and non-evidence references;
\item $B$ is the handshake boundary row, naming the finite public claim that requires both audit surfaces while preserving their separate subject matter;
\item $R$ is the route classifier row, separating MetaCIC-facing reads, GroundCompiler-facing reads, and paired handshake reads;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the MetaCIC row, GroundCompiler row, handshake boundary, route classifier, continuation rows, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a public consumer reads $M$, reads $G$, and then reads $B$ without adding a cross-substrate conversion route;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:twin-substrate-synthesis}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-groundcompilerauditmap-namecert}, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TwinSubstrateAuditHandshakeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for turning a GroundCompiler report into MetaCIC proof content, no coordinate for turning a MetaCIC theorem into compiler evidence, and no hidden equality that identifies $M$ with $G$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Twin-substrate audit-handshake separation]
\label{thm:twin-substrate-audit-handshake-separation}
For every accepted $\TwinSubstrateAuditHandshakeUp$ carrier
$$
U=(M,G,B,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the packet exposes both audit rows $M$ and $G$ through the displayed boundary row $B$, route classifier $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ rows $H$, displayed $\Cont$ routes $C$, package provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. A public handshake read may cite both substrates as audit context, but it cannot convert a compiler report into MetaCIC proof content and cannot convert a MetaCIC theorem into compiler evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite carrier. The MetaCIC row $M$ and the GroundCompiler row $G$ are distinct coordinates, and the handshake boundary row $B$ records only the public claim that needs both of them.

Inspect the route classifier $R$. A MetaCIC-facing read keeps proof-theoretic content attached to $M$, and a GroundCompiler-facing read keeps compiler-pipeline content attached to $G$. A paired handshake read may mention both rows only after $R$ has recorded that it is paired.

Transport and replay are componentwise through $H$ and $C$. These rows preserve the displayed coordinates and provide no route that maps compiler reports to proof content or maps theorem rows to compiler evidence.

Finally read the provenance and naming rows $P,N$. They package the finite handshake over the cited audit surfaces, so any conversion between the two substrates would require a coordinate outside $U$. Since the carrier has no such coordinate, the two audit surfaces are exposed together without collapse.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Twin-substrate audit-handshake route-classifier trichotomy]
\label{thm:twin-substrate-audit-handshake-route-classifier-trichotomy}
Let
$$
U=(M,G,B,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\TwinSubstrateAuditHandshakeUp$ carrier. If a public audit read $q$ is exposed by $U$, then the route classifier row $R$ places $q$ in exactly one of the following three displayed route classes:
\begin{enumerate}
\item a MetaCIC-facing route, whose readable substrate row is $M$ and whose public content remains closed-CIC audit content;
\item a GroundCompiler-facing route, whose readable substrate row is $G$ and whose public content remains compiler audit content;
\item a paired handshake route, whose readable boundary is $B$ and whose context may cite both $M$ and $G$ without converting either row into the other.
\end{enumerate}
In all three cases, the transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows $H,C,P,N$ replay the selected classifier case componentwise. No accepted public read can pass from the $G$ row to MetaCIC proof content or from the $M$ row to compiler evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier using \autoref{def:twin-substrate-audit-handshake-carrier}. The public rows available to a read are the MetaCIC audit row $M$, the GroundCompiler audit row $G$, the handshake boundary row $B$, the route classifier row $R$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

The row $R$ is displayed as the finite classifier separating MetaCIC-facing reads, GroundCompiler-facing reads, and paired handshake reads. Therefore a public audit read $q$ must be inspected by one of those three classifier entries. The first entry reads $M$ as closed-CIC audit data; the second reads $G$ as compiler audit data; the third reads $B$ as the boundary that permits a public handshake claim to cite both audit surfaces.

The three entries are exact because no other route-classifier coordinate is present in the carrier. If $q$ is read through the MetaCIC-facing entry, the GroundCompiler row may not be used as proof content, since the carrier has no coordinate turning a GroundCompiler report into MetaCIC proof content. If $q$ is read through the GroundCompiler-facing entry, the MetaCIC row may not be used as compiler evidence, since the carrier has no coordinate turning a MetaCIC theorem into compiler evidence. If $q$ is read through the paired handshake entry, the boundary row $B$ records only the public claim that needs both audit surfaces, and it preserves their separate subject matter.

Finally apply the separation theorem, \autoref{thm:twin-substrate-audit-handshake-separation}. It states that public handshake reads expose both audit rows through $B,R,H,C,P,N$ while preserving the non-conversion boundary. Thus $H,C,P,N$ replay the chosen classifier case componentwise, and any route outside the three displayed cases would require a carrier coordinate outside $U$.
\end{proof}
