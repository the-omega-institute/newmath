\item source stability, requiring accepted comparisons to preserve the two regular Cauchy source rows componentwise;

\item null-sequence consumption, requiring $Z$ to consume precisely the displayed difference row $D$ with the tolerance ledger $T$ before any equality seal is read;

The two $\RegSeqRatUp$ rows $X,Y$ are read first; the $\RegularCauchyDifferenceUp$ row $D$ is the only coordinate that can display their pointwise difference; and the windows $W$ are the finite observations shared by the source and difference reads.

The null-sequence clause is the next step. The row $Z$ consumes $D$ together with the dyadic tolerance ledger $T$, so the zero-shrinkage evidence is visible before the endpoint row $E$ is inspected.

Finally inspect the carrier inventory. Since quotient stream equality, host equality of reals, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, and bridge data are not coordinates of $C$, none can become local NameCert obligations.
