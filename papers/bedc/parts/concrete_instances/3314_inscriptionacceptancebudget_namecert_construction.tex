The $\InscriptionAcceptanceBudgetUp$ surface isolates the accepted-check and downstream consumer-budget part of an inscription event. It names the finite public packet by which a prior source state reaches a named record through a route witness, receives an accepted check, spends only the displayed consumer budget, and leaves the remaining $\mathsf{GAP}$ residue visible to later audit.

This packet consumes the route-witness and accepted-record rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscriptionevent-namecert} together with the visible GAP-residue ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-inscription-gap-ledger-namecert}. The source, name, route, and check rows come from $\InscriptionEventUp$, while the residue row $Q$ is the $\InscriptionGapLedgerUp$ boundary consumed by this budget packet.

\begin{definition}[InscriptionAcceptanceBudget carrier]
\label{def:inscription-acceptance-budget-carrier}
An $\InscriptionAcceptanceBudgetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(S,M,R,K,U,B,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the prior source-state row from which the inscription route is read;
\item $M$ is the named statement, theorem site, citation row, or public record admitted by the check;
\item $R$ is the displayed route witness from $S$ to $M$;
\item $K$ is the accepted check row that admits $R$ as public record data for $M$;
\item $U$ is the intended downstream consumer row;
\item $B$ is the finite consumer-budget row naming the uses of $K$ and $M$ available to $U$;
\item $Q$ is the remaining $\mathsf{GAP}$ residue row after the accepted route and consumer budget have been exposed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $S,M,R,K,U,B,Q$ together with package and naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which consumers read the source, name, route, check, budget, and residue rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, accepted-check data, consumer-budget data, $\mathsf{GAP}$ residue data, and local certificate dependencies;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\InscriptionAcceptanceBudgetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier for accepted carriers compares only $S,M,R,K,U,B,Q,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for private intention, host trust, unbudgeted downstream use, unchecked route replay, hidden mutation, or a complete inscription-event ontology.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[InscriptionAcceptanceBudget check row exactness]
\label{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-check-row-exactness}
For every accepted $\InscriptionAcceptanceBudgetUp$ carrier $A=(S,M,R,K,U,B,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the accepted-check row $K$ is exactly the public route by which $R$ is admitted from $S$ to the named record $M$. Every public comparison of check rows is transported componentwise by $H$ and read through the displayed routes in $C$, and the carrier exports no second checker, hidden host acceptance predicate, unchecked route, or unrecorded proof replay.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:inscription-acceptance-budget-carrier}. The only row whose role is route admission is $K$, and it is displayed beside the source row $S$, named record $M$, and route witness $R$.

The comparison surface is componentwise. The $\hsame$ rows in $H$ transport $S,M,R,K$ together with the budget, residue, package, and local naming rows, while the $\Cont$ routes in $C$ replay the same public route into the accepted check.

No coordinate of $A$ names another checker, a host acceptance predicate, or an unchecked replay route. Thus every accepted-check consumer reads exactly $K$ through the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InscriptionAcceptanceBudget consumer boundary]
\label{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-consumer-boundary}
Every downstream use of an accepted $\InscriptionAcceptanceBudgetUp$ carrier is bounded by the intended consumer row $U$ and the finite consumer-budget row $B$. A theorem site, citation row, audit surface, or certificate consumer may read the named record $M$, route witness $R$, accepted check $K$, budget $B$, and residue $Q$ only through $U,H,C,P,N$; it receives no unbudgeted consumer right, private agency predicate, hidden host trace, or replacement for the $\mathsf{GAP}$ residue.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a public downstream request. The intended consumer is the row $U$, and the finite budget of permitted reads is the row $B$.

The route into the accepted material then passes through $C$. It exposes $M,R,K$ together with $Q$ under the componentwise transports in $H$ and the provenance rows $P,N$.

Because the carrier contains no unbudgeted consumer coordinate and no hidden host trace, the consumer boundary is exhausted by $U$ and $B$. The remaining residue is still the displayed row $Q$, not a private explanation outside the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InscriptionAcceptanceBudget NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\InscriptionAcceptanceBudgetUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:inscription-acceptance-budget-carrier} consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure: every accepted packet exposes exactly $A=(S,M,R,K,U,B,Q,H,C,P,N)$;
\item check-row exactness: the accepted check is the displayed row $K$ relating $S,R$, and $M$;
\item consumer-budget exhaustion: every downstream use routes through $U$ and spends only the finite row $B$;
\item $\mathsf{GAP}$ residue non-escape: the residue is the displayed row $Q$ and cannot be replaced by private intention, hidden agency, raw byte identity, or host process trace;
\item downstream route stability: all public reads are preserved by the componentwise $\hsame$ transports in $H$ and the $\Cont$ routes in $C$ over the same package and local naming rows $P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from the displayed finite tuple. The source, name, route, check, consumer, budget, residue, transport, continuation, package, and local naming rows are all coordinates of one $\BHist$ packet.

Check-row exactness is exactly \autoref{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-check-row-exactness}. Consumer-budget exhaustion is exactly \autoref{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-consumer-boundary}; both read the same rows $S,M,R,K,U,B,Q,H,C,P,N$.

For residue non-escape and route stability, inspect the remaining carrier inventory. The only residue coordinate is $Q$, and the only public comparison and consumer routes are the displayed rows $H$ and $C$ with provenance $P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InscriptionAcceptanceBudget GAP residue exposure]
\label{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-gap-residue-exposure}
For every accepted $\InscriptionAcceptanceBudgetUp$ carrier $A=(S,M,R,K,U,B,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the $\mathsf{GAP}$ residue row $Q$ is the only residue coordinate exposed to downstream consumers. It cannot be replaced by private intention, hidden agency, raw byte identity, or host process trace.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The check-row exactness theorem, \autoref{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-check-row-exactness}, confines acceptance to $K$ over $S,R$, and $M$. The consumer boundary theorem, \autoref{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-consumer-boundary}, lets downstream readers see residue only through the public packet and its displayed routes.

Finally use \autoref{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-namecert-obligations}. Its residue non-escape obligation states that private intention, hidden agency, raw byte identity, and host process trace are not replacement rows for $Q$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InscriptionAcceptanceBudget route stability]
\label{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-route-stability}
Accepted route reads of $S,M,R,K,U,B,Q$ are stable under the componentwise $H$ transports and the displayed $C$ routes. Thus any downstream comparison that preserves the $H$ $\hsame$ rows and follows the $C$ $\Cont$ routes reads the same accepted source, name, route witness, check row, consumer row, budget row, and residue row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier of \autoref{def:inscription-acceptance-budget-carrier}. The rows $H$ and $C$ are part of the same finite packet as $S,M,R,K,U,B,Q$, so public transport and route reading are not external operations.

Apply \autoref{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-check-row-exactness}. It transports public comparisons of $S,M,R,K$ componentwise by $H$ and reads them through the displayed routes in $C$.

Use \autoref{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-consumer-boundary} for $U,B,Q$. Downstream reads of the consumer budget and residue pass through the same $H,C,P,N$ surface.

The route-stability obligation in \autoref{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-namecert-obligations} then covers the whole accepted tuple $S,M,R,K,U,B,Q$ over the same package and local naming rows.
\end{proof}

The packet exports no unbudgeted consumer right, no hidden route replay, and no consumer authority outside the displayed $\Pkg$ and $\NameCert$ surface.

\begin{proof}

Apply \autoref{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-consumer-boundary}. It states that every downstream use is bounded by $U$ and $B$, and that a theorem site, citation row, audit surface, or certificate consumer reads the accepted material only through $U,H,C,P,N$.

The check-row exactness theorem, \autoref{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-check-row-exactness}, keeps admitted route use tied to the displayed row $K$ rather than to a hidden replay route. The NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:inscription-acceptance-budget-namecert-obligations} then identify consumer-budget exhaustion as a local finite obligation.

Therefore downstream consumption spends exactly the displayed budget row $B$ through $U$ and the local $P,N$ rows, with no additional consumer authority outside the packet.
\end{proof}
