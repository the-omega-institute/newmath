The $\UseProcessBoundaryUp$ packet realizes the certified-use and process-priority surface of \autoref{subsec:philosophy-wittgenstein-use} and \autoref{subsec:philosophy-whitehead-process} as one finite NameCert boundary. It sits beside $\TraditionComparisonBoundaryUp$, $\TraditionRegistryUp$, $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, and $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$: those siblings compare inherited tradition rows, register tradition entries, admit package claims, and check registry export consistency, while this packet binds certified use to process priority before either a tradition comparison or a registry export can read meaning as a stable claim.

\begin{definition}[Use-process-boundary carrier]
\label{def:use-process-boundary-carrier}
A $\UseProcessBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(U,P,R,E,H,C,K,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the certified-use row, recording the public use of the name together with the certificate route that licenses that use;
\item $P$ is the process-priority row, recording the $\Cont$ process through which use is replayed before any object-facing or registry-facing conclusion is read;
\item $R$ is the rule-audit row, recording the finite distinction between licensed use, bare habit, private image, and unregistered philosophical claim;
\item $E$ is the export-boundary row, recording the routes by which $\TraditionComparisonBoundaryUp$, $\TraditionRegistryUp$, $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, and $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$ may consume the packet;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for certified use, process priority, rule audit, export boundary, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the same certified-use and process-priority rows for downstream consumers;
\item $K$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\UseProcessBoundaryUp$, $\TraditionComparisonBoundaryUp$, $\TraditionRegistryUp$, $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\UseProcessBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for private meaning, social habit as an oracle, host equality, doctrine identity, total truth, or registry export without the displayed use and process rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Use-process-boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:use-process-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:use-process-boundary-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\UseProcessBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $B=(U,P,R,E,H,C,K,N)$, certified-use exposure through $U$, process-priority exposure through $P$, rule-audit exposure through $R$, export-boundary exposure through $E$, componentwise transport and replay through $H,C$, provenance through $K$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet of \autoref{def:use-process-boundary-carrier}. Its rows display certified use, process priority, rule audit, export boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

The first four rows are the obligation surface before any downstream registry or tradition consumer can read the packet. The rows $H$ and $C$ preserve that same surface under componentwise transport and displayed replay.

Finally $K$ and $N$ cite and name the same displayed rows. Since the carrier contains no further coordinate, these obligations exhaust the local $\NameCert_{\UseProcessBoundaryUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Use-process-boundary certified-use nonescape]
\label{thm:use-process-boundary-certified-use-nonescape}
Every accepted $\UseProcessBoundaryUp$ consumer that exports meaning, tradition comparison, claim admission, or registry consistency must expose the certified-use row $U$, process-priority row $P$, rule-audit row $R$, export-boundary row $E$, transport row $H$, replay row $C$, provenance row $K$, and local naming row $N$. No accepted consumer receives private meaning, bare social habit, doctrine identity, host equality, total truth, or registry export without those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the consumer through the carrier. The certified-use row $U$ licenses the use, while the process-priority row $P$ keeps the use on a displayed continuation before export.

The rule-audit and export-boundary rows $R,E$ determine which downstream tradition and registry siblings may consume the packet. The structural rows $H,C,K,N$ transport, replay, source, and locally name exactly that route.

The excluded readings are absent from the coordinate list of \autoref{def:use-process-boundary-carrier}. A consumer claiming one of them would therefore require a field that the accepted boundary packet does not contain.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Use-process-boundary process priority]
\label{thm:use-process-boundary-process-priority}
Every accepted $\UseProcessBoundaryUp$ export reaches a tradition or registry consumer only after the certified-use row $U$ has been replayed through the process-priority row $P$ and the displayed $\Cont$ row $C$. Thus meaning-as-certified-use is process-mediated before $\TraditionComparisonBoundaryUp$, $\TraditionRegistryUp$, $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, or $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$ may consume it.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the order of the carrier rows. The use row $U$ is not followed by an immediate object-export row; it is paired with the process-priority row $P$ and the replay row $C$.

The export-boundary row $E$ names only consumers of that mediated route. It cannot bypass $P$ or $C$ without ceasing to be the export row of the displayed packet.

Consequently each sibling consumer reads certified use through a process route. The theorem asserts process priority for the finite NameCert boundary, not an ambient metaphysical completeness claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Use-process-boundary sibling independence]
\label{thm:use-process-boundary-sibling-independence}
The $\UseProcessBoundaryUp$ carrier is not bijective, under NameCert-row preservation, to $\TraditionComparisonBoundaryUp$, $\TraditionRegistryUp$, $\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$, or $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$. Each sibling is consumed through the export-boundary row $E$, but none carries the joint certified-use, process-priority, rule-audit, export-boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming packet of $\UseProcessBoundaryUp$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Compare the displayed rows. $\TraditionComparisonBoundaryUp$ separates inherited tradition rows from BEDC landing rows, and $\TraditionRegistryUp$ records admitted tradition entries.

$\ClaimRegistryLayerUp$ controls package-level claim admission, while $\RegistryExportConsistencyGateUp$ checks status consistency before machine-facing registry export. These siblings supply downstream or adjacent routes, not the certified-use and process-priority boundary itself.

The $\UseProcessBoundaryUp$ packet binds $U,P,R,E,H,C,K,N$ in one finite carrier. A row-preserving bijection would have to preserve certified use, process priority, rule audit, export boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming simultaneously, which none of the four siblings displays as its carrier surface.
\end{proof}
