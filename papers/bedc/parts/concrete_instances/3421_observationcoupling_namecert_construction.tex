\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ObservationCouplingUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-observationcoupling-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ObservationCouplingUp$ packet realizes inter-history locality as a finite two-history naming surface. It records two displayed $\BHist$ rows, paired observation routes, componentwise transports, a symmetric coupling ledger, package provenance, and a local $\NameCert$ row. The packet gives downstream multi-observer consumers a bounded coupling object without adding a global frame, synchronized clock, host metric, or observer-independent carrier outside the displayed histories.

As a sibling lattice row, $\ObservationCouplingUp$ is the paired-history coupling surface consumed by the inter-Hist invariant of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interhistinvariant-namecert} and the maximum-rate chain of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-maxcausalrate-namecert}. Those consumers may read the coupling ledger only after the two $\BHist$ rows, paired $\Cont$ routes, componentwise $\hsame$ transports, and symmetric ledger have been displayed.

\begin{definition}[Observation coupling carrier]
\label{def:observation-coupling-carrier}
An $\ObservationCouplingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(h_a,h_b,r_a,r_b,H,C,L,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $h_a$ and $h_b$ are the two displayed $\BHist$ history rows being compared;
\item $r_a$ and $r_b$ are paired $\Cont$ routes that expose the inspected observation windows from $h_a$ and $h_b$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for both history rows, both route rows, the coupling ledger, package provenance, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed consumer routes that read the two history windows and replay the coupling comparison;
\item $L$ is the symmetric coupling ledger, storing the same finite boundary when the two history slots are exchanged;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the two $\BHist$ rows, paired $\Cont$ routes, componentwise transports, coupling ledger, and local naming row;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObservationCouplingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no global frame, synchronized clock, ambient metric, cross-history equality oracle, host causal-rate coordinate, or observer-substance beyond the two displayed history rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observation coupling locality obligations]
\label{thm:observation-coupling-locality-obligations}
Every accepted $\ObservationCouplingUp$ packet exposes inter-history comparison only through the finite rows
$$
h_a,h_b,r_a,r_b,H,C,L,P,N.
$$
A positive coupling witness must first read the two displayed $\BHist$ rows, then the paired $\Cont$ routes, then the componentwise $\hsame$ transports and symmetric ledger. No consumer may project a comparison from a global synchronization row, ambient frame, host metric, or hidden equality oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:observation-coupling-carrier}. The only history data are $h_a$ and $h_b$, and the only observation accessors are the paired route rows $r_a$ and $r_b$.

The locality obligation reads the histories through those routes before any comparison is consumed. The transport row $H$ keeps the comparison componentwise over the same displayed histories, while $C$ records the consumer replay paths.

The ledger row $L$ is the only cross-history coupling row. Since the carrier has no global frame, synchronized clock, ambient metric, host causal-rate coordinate, or equality oracle, every accepted locality witness remains inside the finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observation coupling symmetric rate boundary]
\label{thm:observation-coupling-symmetric-rate-boundary}
The coupling ledger $L$ of an accepted $\ObservationCouplingUp$ packet is observer-symmetric: exchanging $h_a,r_a$ with $h_b,r_b$ reads the same finite boundary through the transported rows $H$ and the displayed routes $C$. Any maximum cross-history causal-rate consumer can receive only this symmetric ledger boundary and cannot choose a preferred observer slot, global clock, or host-side metric ratio.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the displayed tuple $O=(h_a,h_b,r_a,r_b,H,C,L,P,N)$. The two observation sides are represented by matching history and route rows, and the carrier gives no distinguished anchor outside those rows.

Use the ledger row $L$. By definition it stores the coupling boundary in a form that is replayed after either slot exchange, with $H$ carrying the componentwise $\hsame$ transports and $C$ carrying the consumer routes.

A maximum-rate consumer therefore receives a symmetric finite ledger boundary rather than a host-side rate object. The absence of a preferred observer slot, global clock, and ambient metric prevents any asymmetric projection from the accepted packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observation coupling NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observation-coupling-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ObservationCouplingUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $O=(h_a,h_b,r_a,r_b,H,C,L,P,N)$, two displayed $\BHist$ source rows, paired $\Cont$ route exposure, componentwise $\hsame$ stability, symmetric ledger replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, local naming, and no-global-frame exclusion.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:observation-coupling-carrier}. The two source rows, paired route rows, transport row, route replay row, ledger row, package row, and local naming row are all displayed coordinates.

The locality obligations supply the reading order for the two histories and their paired routes. The symmetric rate boundary supplies the slot-exchange clause for the ledger $L$.

The remaining obligation is exclusion. Because the carrier lists no global frame, synchronized clock, ambient metric, cross-history equality oracle, or observer-substance coordinate, the local certificate surface is exhausted by the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observation coupling ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:observation-coupling-ledger-nonescape}
Every downstream consumer of an accepted $\ObservationCouplingUp$ packet factors through $h_a,h_b,r_a,r_b,H,C,L,P,N$. The packet exports no global synchronization value, no host observer frame, no ambient spacetime coordinate, no selected maximum-rate witness, no quotient equality between histories, and no causal relation outside the displayed symmetric ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier projection. The consumer-visible rows are exactly $h_a,h_b,r_a,r_b,H,C,L,P,N$.

Apply the locality obligations to force any inter-history read through the two displayed histories and paired $\Cont$ routes. Apply the NameCert obligations to keep the read attached to the componentwise transports, replay routes, package provenance, and local name.

Finally apply the symmetric boundary theorem. The only cross-history content exported to downstream rate or invariant consumers is the ledger $L$, so no global synchronization value, host observer frame, ambient spacetime coordinate, selected maximum-rate witness, quotient equality, or undisplayed causal relation can be projected from the packet.
\end{proof}
