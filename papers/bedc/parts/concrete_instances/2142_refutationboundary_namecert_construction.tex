\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RefutationBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-refutationboundary-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\RefutationBoundaryUp$ surface records the exact BEDC boundary between permitted refutation and forbidden truth-branch extraction. It is a finite NameCert carrier for consumers that need to use contradiction as a local refutation rule while preserving the open meta loop of \autoref{ch:visions-reasoning-by-contradiction-boundary}. The packet carries enough data to replay permitted negation routes, to consume a decidability row only when one is displayed, and to refuse any export of host excluded middle, nonconstructive existence, quotient truth predicates, or RealUp completeness.

The permitted-refutation route row $F$, optional decidability row $D$, and truth-branch exclusion ledger are the boundary consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-groundrefutation-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-refutationwitness-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finiterefutationboundary-namecert}. The dependency is only finite $\BHist$, $\Cont$, $\hsame$, and $\NameCert$ data, not host excluded middle or truth-branch extraction.

When the optional decidability row is absent, the permitted-refutation route consumes the stable negation boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-stable-negation-boundary-namecert}, while $\RefutationBoundaryUp$ keeps the consumer-facing packet finite through $A,F,D,S,T,H,C,P,N$.

\begin{definition}[RefutationBoundary carrier]
\label{def:refutation-boundary-carrier}
A $\RefutationBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(A,F,D,S,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the inspected proposition row, read as a certificate-visible predicate over the current finite history;
\item $F$ is the permitted-refutation route from an assumed witness of $A$ to the displayed bottom row, using only $\Cont$, $\hsame$, $\msame$, and the local naming rows;
\item $D$ is an optional decidability row for $A$, present only when the same packet displays a constructive branch for $A$;
\item $S$ is the forbidden-schema descriptor, naming the three blocked shapes of \autoref{def:reasoning-forbidden-pattern}: unwitnessed existential extraction, double-negation elimination without $D$, and excluded-middle branching over the carrier of all naming certificates;
\item $T$ is the truth-branch exclusion ledger, recording that no coordinate of the packet exports a family $Q(N)\vee\neg Q(N)$ over all naming certificates;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the proposition row, refutation route, optional decidability row, forbidden-schema descriptor, truth-branch ledger, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading $A$, replaying $F$, consuming $D$ when present, checking $S$, and transporting $T$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Hist$, $\Ext$, $\Cont$, $\msame$, $\hsame$, the local refutation rows, and the surrounding $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RefutationBoundaryUp}$ ledger.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is only this finite packet. It has no host truth predicate, no quotient over truth values, no global excluded-middle coordinate, no witness selector for an existential obtained by contradiction, and no completeness certificate for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Permitted negation is sound inside the packet]
\label{thm:refutation-boundary-permitted-negation-sound}
Every accepted $\RefutationBoundaryUp$ packet supports the permitted BEDC rule that a displayed route from an assumed witness of $A$ to bottom yields the local negation of $A$. The rule is stable under the packet's $\hsame$ transports and displayed $\Cont$ routes.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier as $B=(A,F,D,S,T,H,C,P,N)$. The row $F$ is not an ambient proof search result; it is a displayed route from an assumed witness of $A$ through the stored continuation rows to the bottom row.

Transport stability is supplied by $H$ and $C$. Moving the proposition row and the route along the componentwise $\hsame$ data, then replaying the displayed $\Cont$ chain, gives the same local map from an admitted witness of $A$ to bottom.

Thus the only negation exported by the packet is the one accounted for by its displayed route. This is exactly the permitted pattern of \autoref{def:reasoning-permitted-pattern}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Forbidden truth branches are excluded]
\label{thm:refutation-boundary-forbidden-truth-branch-exclusion}
No consumer read of an accepted $\RefutationBoundaryUp$ packet can produce a certificate-indexed truth branch for predicates over the carrier of all naming certificates. In particular, the packet does not implement any forbidden schema of \autoref{def:reasoning-forbidden-pattern}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The only classifier-visible coordinates of the carrier are $A,F,D,S,T,H,C,P,N$. None of them ranges over all naming certificates, and none is a family choosing $Q(N)\vee\neg Q(N)$ for arbitrary $Q$ and arbitrary certificate $N$.

The descriptor $S$ names the blocked schemas, while the ledger $T$ records their non-export condition. A consumer may inspect those rows, transport them through $H$, and replay their checks through $C$, but that yields only the finite exclusion ledger already present in the packet.

The forbidden schemas require data outside the carrier inventory: a witness selected from $\neg\forall\neg$, a double-negation eliminator with no displayed decidability row, or excluded middle over naming certificates. Since no such coordinate exists, the truth branch of \autoref{def:reasoning-certificate-indexed-truth-branch} cannot be extracted.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RefutationBoundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:refutation-boundary-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\RefutationBoundaryUp}$ surface consists of five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the finite packet $B=(A,F,D,S,T,H,C,P,N)$ of \autoref{def:refutation-boundary-carrier};
\item classifier stability for the visible proposition row, permitted-refutation route, optional decidability row, forbidden-schema descriptor, truth-branch ledger, transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item permitted-negation soundness, namely the transport-stable route of \autoref{thm:refutation-boundary-permitted-negation-sound};
\item decidability accountability, saying that $\neg\neg A$ may be consumed only when the row $D$ displays a constructive decision for the same proposition row;
\item truth-branch exclusion, namely the non-export statement of \autoref{thm:refutation-boundary-forbidden-truth-branch-exclusion}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RefutationBoundaryUp.RefutationBoundaryCarrier\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:refutation-boundary-carrier}. Every accepted object exposes exactly the displayed finite tuple and no hidden ambient coordinate.

Classifier stability follows by comparing only the visible rows of the tuple. The proposition, refutation route, optional decidability row, forbidden-schema descriptor, truth-branch ledger, provenance, and local naming row are transported by $H$ and read through $C$.

Permitted-negation soundness is \autoref{thm:refutation-boundary-permitted-negation-sound}. The proof route is finite, displayed, and stable under the same componentwise transports.

For decidability accountability, inspect $D$. If $D$ is present, it is consumed as the displayed constructive branch for $A$; if it is absent, $S$ and $T$ record that no undecided double-negation eliminator is exported.

Finally, truth-branch exclusion is \autoref{thm:refutation-boundary-forbidden-truth-branch-exclusion}. The NameCert ledger records the boundary by carrying the exclusion row rather than a truth predicate over all naming certificates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealUp consumers receive only boundary data]
\label{thm:refutation-boundary-realup-consumer-scope}
A $\RealUp$ or completion consumer that reads an accepted $\RefutationBoundaryUp$ packet receives only the proposition row, displayed refutation route, optional decidability row, forbidden-schema descriptor, truth-branch exclusion ledger, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming row. It receives no completeness theorem, no quotient truth predicate, and no host excluded-middle principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Any consumer read factors through the carrier coordinates $A,F,D,S,T,H,C,P,N$. These coordinates are finite $\BHist$ and ledger rows, so their projections remain inside the displayed NameCert surface.

The refutation data available to the consumer is the route $F$ together with its transports and continuations. If the consumer also needs double-negation elimination, the only available route is the optional row $D$, and only for the same proposition row $A$.

The completion-side exclusions are exactly the absent coordinates of \autoref{def:refutation-boundary-carrier}. The packet does not contain a RealUp completeness witness, a quotient truth predicate, or a branch over all naming certificates, so a RealUp consumer cannot obtain any of those principles from this certificate.
\end{proof}
