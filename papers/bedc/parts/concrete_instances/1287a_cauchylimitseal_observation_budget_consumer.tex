\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal observation-budget pullback]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-observation-budget-pullback}
Let $L=(X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet, and let $B=(E_B,W_B,D_B,R_B,S_B,H_B,C_B,P_B,N_B)$ be an accepted $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ packet. If the seal row $S_B$ is transported to the seal row $E$ of $L$, and the regular handoff rows $R_B$ cover the source row $X$, then every budget read of $B$ pulls back to the public route packet of $L$. The pulled-back route reads only the finite precision request, windows, dyadic ledger, regular handoff, seal row, $\mathsf{BHist}$ surface, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ coordinates already displayed by $B$ and $L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite budget packet. Window coverage from \autoref{thm:real-observation-budget-window-coverage} places each budget read over the displayed precision request $E_B$, finite windows $W_B$, and dyadic ledger $D_B$.

Next apply the regular handoff and seal boundary. The handoff theorem \autoref{thm:real-observation-budget-regseq-handoff} routes the regular rows $R_B$ through the same finite windows, while \autoref{thm:real-observation-budget-real-seal-boundary} makes $S_B$ the real-facing seal boundary of the budget packet.

Transport the seal row to $E$ and the handoff rows to $X$. The public route boundary of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-public-route-boundary} then identifies the resulting read with the route packet $X,S,D,G,E,T,P,N$ of the accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal budgeted tail comparison admission]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budgeted-tail-comparison-admission}
A family-tail comparison row is admitted through an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet only when the same finite $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ packet displays the precision request, finite windows, dyadic ledger, regular handoff, seal row, transports, routes, provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row consumed by the seal packet. A comparison row that lacks any one of those displayed budget coordinates is not an admitted CauchyLimitSeal family-tail comparison row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use family-tail admission from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-family-tail-admission}. It requires the source family, schedule, tail modulus, null-tail comparison, diagonal completion row, seal row, transports, continuation route, provenance, and local naming row to be displayed in the accepted seal packet.

Next use seal-window ledger compatibility from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-seal-window-ledger-compatibility}. This ties the tail comparison to the same scheduled window and dyadic observation row used by the seal route, so the comparison cannot be read from a different finite budget.

Finally apply $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ consumer exactness from \autoref{thm:real-observation-budget-consumer-exactness}. Exactness says the regular and real-facing consumers read precisely the budget packet, so an admitted tail comparison must carry the same precision, window, ledger, handoff, seal, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal RealObservationBudget consumer package]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-real-observation-budget-consumer-package}
Every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing consumer that enters through an accepted $\RealObservationBudgetUp$ packet and exits through an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet reads exactly the union of the budget rows and the seal rows. The union is taken over the shared $\mathsf{BHist}$ surface, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\NameCert$ rows, and it exposes no quotient stream, selected host limit, ambient completeness principle, hidden family limit, or unbounded schedule.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the observation-budget pullback of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-observation-budget-pullback}. It sends every finite budget read into the public route packet of the accepted seal carrier, preserving the displayed budget rows and the shared transport, route, provenance, and naming data.

Attach the tail comparison by \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budgeted-tail-comparison-admission}. A family-tail comparison is admitted only when it is read through the same finite budget surface consumed by the seal route.

Now compose with consumer ledger totality from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-consumer-ledger-totality} and real consumer family exhaustion from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-real-consumer-family-exhaustion}. The budget route and the seal route already exhaust the consumer-visible rows, leaving no coordinate for quotient streams, selected host limits, ambient completeness, hidden family limits, or unbounded schedules.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal tail extraction]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-tail-extraction}
Every admitted tail read of an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet is projected from the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ family, $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ diagonal row, and visible $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ ledger. The projection is the same public route consumed by the seal packet, not a tail chosen from a quotient stream, host limit, hidden family limit, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with family-tail admission. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-family-tail-admission}, a tail read is admitted only with the displayed source family, schedule, tail modulus, null-tail comparison, completion diagonal, seal row, transports, route, provenance, and local naming row.

Next attach seal-window ledger compatibility from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-seal-window-ledger-compatibility}. The admitted tail therefore uses the same scheduled window and dyadic observation ledger as the seal route.

Finally pull the observation budget back along \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-observation-budget-pullback}. The tail read is projected through the displayed regular family, schedule, dyadic ledger, diagonal row, and visible $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ route, with no remaining coordinate for an external tail source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal uniqueness boundary]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-uniqueness-boundary}
Every equality or uniqueness-facing consumer of an accepted $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ packet may use only the visible null-sequence and tail-comparison route. It cannot import a host uniqueness theorem, quotient-stream equality, selected limit, hidden family limit, or ambient completeness datum.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the non-escape boundary of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-non-escape}. The accepted packet contains no host uniqueness theorem, quotient representative, selected limit, or ambient completeness coordinate.

For the comparison itself, use null-sequence factorization from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-nullsequence-factorization}. Equality-facing reads can add only the displayed $\NullSequenceUp$ tolerance-window-threshold row to the already fixed seal route.

Then apply budgeted tail comparison admission from \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-budgeted-tail-comparison-admission}. A uniqueness-facing read is therefore admitted only through the same finite budget, scheduled window, dyadic ledger, regular handoff, seal, transport, route, provenance, and naming rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyLimitSeal consumer readback]
\label{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-consumer-readback}
A downstream $\RealUp$ or completion consumer reads back exactly the sealed route assembled by the root-unblock package, together with the tail-extraction row of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-tail-extraction} and the uniqueness boundary of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-uniqueness-boundary}. The readback exports only the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the root-unblock package of \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-root-unblock-package}. It gives the consumer one route from the accepted seal packet to the downstream $\RealUp$ or completion boundary.

Attach tail extraction. The tail row is already projected from the same displayed regular family, schedule, observation ledger, completion diagonal, and transport ledger, so it does not add a second consumer route.

Finally attach the uniqueness boundary. Equality-facing reads remain on the null-sequence and tail-comparison route admitted by the packet, and the readback has no coordinate for quotient completion, host uniqueness, selected limits, hidden family limits, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
