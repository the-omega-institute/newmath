\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\OptimalTransportUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-optimaltransport-namecert}

The $\OptimalTransportUp$ interface names the finite-support transport surface used by the classical optimal-transport presentation of Villani 2009. The BEDC chapter below restricts that surface to displayed finite source and target distribution rows, a finite cost ledger, a finite coupling table, and a linear-programming dual certificate; it does not import measure completion, arbitrary-measure minimizers, quotient spaces, or infinite Kantorovich duality.

\section{Finite-support transport packets}
\label{sec:optimaltransport-finite-support-packets}

\begin{definition}[OptimalTransport finite coupling carrier]
\label{def:optimaltransport-finite-coupling-carrier}
An $\OptimalTransportUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
T=(X,Y,\mu,\nu,C,\Gamma,M,S,L)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are finite support ledgers whose entries are carried by displayed source certificates;
\item $\mu$ and $\nu$ are $\DistributionUp$ rows on those supports, with finite mass entries and displayed summation ledgers;
\item $C$ is either a displayed cost table row or a cost row read through $\MetricUp$ on the paired support entries;
\item $\Gamma$ is a finite nonnegative coupling table indexed by the displayed support pairs;
\item $M$ is the pair of marginal-sum ledgers asserting that row sums of $\Gamma$ read as $\mu$ and column sums read as $\nu$;
\item $S$ is the cost-summation ledger for the displayed finite sum of $C(x,y)\Gamma(x,y)$ over the support pairs;
\item $L$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance tying the support, distribution, cost, coupling, marginal, convex, polytope, and dual rows to the cited source certificates.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two accepted packets componentwise by $\hsame$ on support entries, distribution masses, cost entries, coupling entries, marginal ledgers, cost ledgers, $\ConvexSetUp$ and $\PolytopeUp$ feasible-region rows, and $\LPDualityUp$ dual rows. No coordinate stores a host probability space, a completed measure, a quotient of couplings, or a selected optimizer over arbitrary measures.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[OptimalTransport coupling marginal ledger]
\label{thm:optimaltransport-coupling-marginal-ledger}
For an accepted carrier $T=(X,Y,\mu,\nu,C,\Gamma,M,S,L)$, every marginal claim read by an $\OptimalTransportUp$ consumer is a projection of the finite ledger $M$. The source marginal at a support row $x$ is the displayed finite sum of $\Gamma(x,y)$ over entries $y$ of $Y$, and the target marginal at a support row $y$ is the displayed finite sum of $\Gamma(x,y)$ over entries $x$ of $X$. These two families of displayed sums are the only marginal data exported by the carrier.

The ledger exports no sigma-additivity statement, no completion of $\mu$ or $\nu$, and no selection principle for couplings outside the displayed finite support.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptimalTransportUp.OptimalTransportFiniteCouplingPacket\_marginal\_ledger}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:optimaltransport-finite-coupling-carrier}. The only coordinates that mention marginals are the finite distribution rows $\mu,\nu$, the coupling table $\Gamma$, and the marginal ledger $M$.

For a source support entry $x$, read the row of $\Gamma$ indexed by $x$ and the finite summation row in $M$ attached to that row. This gives the displayed equality between the row sum and the corresponding $\mu$ entry. The target case is identical after projecting the column summation row attached to $y$.

Since the proof uses only finite table projections and the displayed summation rows, there is no operation that can create an ambient measure, complete a sigma algebra, or search over couplings that are not already present in the packet.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptimalTransportUp.OptimalTransportFiniteCouplingCarrier\_marginal\_ledger}

\begin{theorem}[OptimalTransport cost summation ledger]
\label{thm:optimaltransport-cost-summation-ledger}
For an accepted $\OptimalTransportUp$ carrier, the transport cost exposed to a consumer is exactly the finite ledger $S$ summing the displayed pair costs against the displayed coupling entries. If the cost row is read through $\MetricUp$, then each summand uses only the metric-distance row named for that support pair; if the cost row is displayed directly, then each summand uses only that table entry.

The cost surface contains no integral over a completed product measure, no measurable-selection argument, and no comparison against an unlisted transport plan.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptimalTransportUp.OptimalTransportFiniteCouplingCarrier\_cost\_summation\_ledger}
\begin{proof}
Read the cost coordinate $C$, the coupling table $\Gamma$, and the cost ledger $S$. The carrier has no other coordinate from which a scalar cost can be computed.

If $C$ is metric-backed, each summand is obtained by the displayed $\MetricUp$ row for its support pair. If $C$ is table-backed, each summand is the visible table entry. In both cases the finite summation ledger $S$ records the multiplication by the corresponding coupling entry and the finite accumulation over support pairs.

Thus the exported cost is a repacking of listed rows. Product measures, measurable functions, and unlisted transport plans are not available to the projection.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OptimalTransport LP-duality feasible surface]
\label{thm:optimaltransport-lpduality-feasible-surface}
The feasible surface of an accepted $\OptimalTransportUp$ packet is the finite polytope determined by the nonnegativity rows for $\Gamma$, the two marginal ledgers of \autoref{thm:optimaltransport-coupling-marginal-ledger}, and the displayed cost objective of \autoref{thm:optimaltransport-cost-summation-ledger}. A dual-feasibility certificate is an $\LPDualityUp$ row whose inequalities are indexed only by the displayed support pairs and whose objective rows read only the displayed $\mu$ and $\nu$ masses.

The theorem names the finite linear-programming surface only; it does not assert infinite-dimensional Kantorovich duality, analytic compactness, or existence of minimizers outside the displayed polytope.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptimalTransportUp.OptimalTransportFiniteCouplingCarrier\_lpduality\_feasible\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Build the primal feasible region from the coordinates of the carrier. Nonnegativity of the table entries gives the half-space rows, and the marginal ledgers give the equality rows. These rows form the finite $\ConvexSetUp$ and $\PolytopeUp$ surface for the coupling table.

The objective is the finite cost ledger from \autoref{thm:optimaltransport-cost-summation-ledger}. A dual certificate is then read as an $\LPDualityUp$ packet over the same finite index set: one dual potential row for each displayed source or target support entry and one inequality row for each displayed support pair.

Because every primal and dual constraint is indexed by a listed support entry, the surface remains a finite linear program. Nothing in the construction invokes topological compactness, arbitrary-measure duality, or a minimizer-selection principle.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptimalTransportUp.OptimalTransportFiniteCouplingPacket\_lpduality\_feasible\_surface}

\begin{theorem}[OptimalTransport finite-support dual-certificate exhaustion]
\label{thm:optimaltransport-dual-certificate-finite-support-exhaustion}
Every $\OptimalTransportUp$ dual certificate read by a consumer is indexed only
by the displayed source support rows, target support rows, visible mass rows,
and cost inequalities of the finite LP surface of
\autoref{thm:optimaltransport-lpduality-feasible-surface}. Conversely, each
listed dual row is read through the finite coupling carrier of
\autoref{def:optimaltransport-finite-coupling-carrier}.

The certificate surface is exhausted by those finite support rows. It contains
no sigma-additivity principle, arbitrary-measure minimizer, or
infinite-dimensional Kantorovich duality datum.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OptimalTransportUp.OptimalTransportFiniteCouplingPacket\_dual\_certificate\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite coupling carrier. Its visible coordinates are the source
and target support rows, the two finite distribution rows, the cost row, the
coupling table, the marginal ledgers, the objective ledger, the feasible-region
rows, the dual-certificate rows, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.

Use \autoref{thm:optimaltransport-coupling-marginal-ledger} to identify the
only consumer-readable mass constraints. Use
\autoref{thm:optimaltransport-cost-summation-ledger} to identify the only cost
summation rows over the displayed coupling table.

Apply \autoref{thm:optimaltransport-lpduality-feasible-surface}. That theorem
places each dual potential and each dual inequality over the same finite source
and target supports and over the same displayed cost inequalities. Hence every
dual certificate read by a consumer factors through the finite carrier rows,
and every listed dual row is read back through that carrier.

No coordinate of the carrier stores a sigma-additive measure space, an
arbitrary-measure optimizer, or an infinite-dimensional Kantorovich dual
principle. The displayed finite LP rows therefore exhaust the dual-certificate
surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OptimalTransport NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:optimaltransport-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\OptimalTransportUp}$ seed surface consists of the following finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet of \autoref{def:optimaltransport-finite-coupling-carrier};
\item classifier: componentwise $\hsame$ on support, distribution, cost, coupling, marginal, cost-summation, feasible-region, and dual rows;
\item marginal ledger: the two finite summation families of \autoref{thm:optimaltransport-coupling-marginal-ledger};
\item cost ledger: the finite objective row of \autoref{thm:optimaltransport-cost-summation-ledger};
\item feasible and dual surface: the $\ConvexSetUp$, $\PolytopeUp$, and $\LPDualityUp$ rows of \autoref{thm:optimaltransport-lpduality-feasible-surface}.
\end{enumerate}
These rows are sufficient to name the finite-support optimal-transport certificate surface and deliberately exclude measure completion, arbitrary-measure optimizer selection, quotient spaces, and Kantorovich duality beyond the finite linear-programming boundary.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The carrier row fixes the finite support entries, source and target distributions, cost rows, coupling table, marginal ledgers, objective ledger, and provenance. These are precisely the coordinates available to an $\OptimalTransportUp$ consumer.

The classifier is componentwise because each coordinate already lives in a displayed BEDC row. Transport along $\hsame$ repacks support entries, masses, costs, coupling entries, marginal sums, and dual inequalities without changing the finite index set named by the packet.

The two ledger theorems account for the only marginal and objective reads. The feasible-surface theorem accounts for the only convex, polytope, and dual rows. Since no remaining coordinate contains a completed measure, a quotient coupling, an infinite support, or a global optimizer, the five rows exhaust the local seed certificate.
\end{proof}
