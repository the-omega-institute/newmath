The $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ packet records the functorial completion surface obtained from a source metric presentation and a regular-Cauchy family. Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the packet treats completion as a visible transformation of names: it consumes the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ seal rows, the $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ unit-and-bind schedule, and the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ extension-uniqueness rows, while its classifier remains confined to finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ evidence.

\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionFunctor carrier]
\label{def:cauchycompletionfunctor-carrier}
A $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(M,R,S,U,B,E,P,T,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the source metric presentation row, including the displayed points, distance observations, and finite tolerance ledger read by the source classifier;
\item $R$ is the regular-Cauchy family row, exposing the $\RegSeqRatUp$ or $\RegularCauchyFamilyUp$ schedules that approximate each source point;
\item $S$ is the $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$ seal row reached from the same source metric and regular-Cauchy family data;
\item $U$ records the $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ unit schedule for source points and the bind schedule for completion-facing maps;
\item $B$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ extension-and-uniqueness row attached to the same unit and bind routes;
\item $E$ is the classifier-transport ledger comparing two accepted packets only through displayed source rows, regular-Cauchy windows, completion seals, unit schedules, bind schedules, and extension-uniqueness rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RegularCauchyFamilyUp$, $\RealCauchyCompletionUp$, $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $T$ is the transport row recording the componentwise $\hsame$ and displayed $\Cont$ routes for $M$, $R$, $S$, $U$, $B$, and $E$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp}$ ledger.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient of Cauchy names, no selected limit object, no host-level completed metric space, and no ambient completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionFunctor unit laws]
\label{thm:cauchycompletionfunctor-unit-laws}
For every accepted $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ packet $F=(M,R,S,U,B,E,P,T,N)$, the unit part of $U$ sends each displayed source metric point of $M$ to the completion seal row $S$ by the regular-Cauchy family route $R$. The left and right unit readings available to consumers are therefore the same finite route through $M$, $R$, $S$, $U$, $P$, $T$, and $N$, not an equality of host completion objects.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the unit schedule from $U$. Its source is the displayed metric row $M$, and its target is the seal row $S$ reached through the regular-Cauchy family row $R$.

The left-unit read begins with a source point and immediately follows the unit schedule into the displayed completion seal. The right-unit read begins with the same displayed completion-facing row and reads back through the stored source and regular-Cauchy schedule.

Both reads use the same package and transport evidence, namely $P$, $T$, and $N$. Thus the unit laws are certificate-level route equalities inside the packet, with no appeal to quotient completion equality or selected limits.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

The universal-property coordinate $B$ records the extension route used by each bind. The classifier ledger $E$ keeps both binds attached to the same source metric and regular-Cauchy windows, and the transport row $T$ moves those rows componentwise.
