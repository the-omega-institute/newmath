\section{CommRing quadratic polarization package}
\label{sec:commring-quadratic-polarization-package}

\begin{theorem}[CommRing quadratic polarization package]
\label{thm:commring-quadratic-polarization-package}
For BEDC history operations $add$, $mul$, $neg$, and $zero$ satisfying the
shared hypotheses of
\autoref{thm:commring-parallelogram-square-sum},
\autoref{thm:commring-square-cross-term-extraction-pair}, and
\autoref{thm:commring-signed-square-zero-exact}, every
$a,b : \mathsf{BHist}$ carries the concrete quadratic-polarization package
$$
\Bigl(
((a+b)\cdot(a+b))+((a+(-b))\cdot(a+(-b)))\hsame
(a\cdot a+a\cdot a)+(b\cdot b+b\cdot b)
\Bigr)
\land
\Bigl(
((a+b)\cdot(a+b))+((-(a\cdot a))+(-(b\cdot b)))\hsame
(a\cdot b)+(a\cdot b)
\Bigr)
\land
\Bigl(
((a+(-b))\cdot(a+(-b)))+((-(a\cdot a))+(-(b\cdot b)))\hsame
(-(a\cdot b))+(-(a\cdot b))
\Bigr)
\land
\Bigl(
(a+(-b))\cdot(a+(-b))\hsame0
\Longleftrightarrow
a\cdot a+b\cdot b\hsame a\cdot b+a\cdot b
\Bigr).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:commring-parallelogram-square-sum} to the shared hypotheses
and the selected histories $a,b$. This gives the parallelogram square-sum
identity. Apply
\autoref{thm:commring-square-cross-term-extraction-pair} to the same data; its
two components give the positive and signed cross-term extraction identities.

Apply \autoref{thm:commring-signed-square-zero-exact} to the same histories.
This gives the signed-square zero exactness iff. Introduce conjunctions in the
displayed order, using the parallelogram identity first, the two extraction
identities next, and the exactness iff as the final component.
\end{proof}

The ten-field fit is: traditional target a commutative-ring quadratic
polarization interface; BEDC source $\RingUp$ plus $\CommRingUp$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $\mathsf{BHist}$; classifier $\hsame$; stability from
the shared distributivity, congruence, commutativity, and negative-product
laws; ledger the parallelogram square-sum, two cross-term extractions, and
signed-square zero exactness witnesses; core theorem this block; exactness the
displayed conjunction; standard bridge ordinary quadratic polarization in a
commutative ring; downstream use $\FieldUp$ quadratic normalization and
equal-square/zero-product reasoning; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
