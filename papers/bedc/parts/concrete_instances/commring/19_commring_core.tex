\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for \(\CommRingUp\)}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-commring-namecert}

The commutative ring interface \(\CommRingUp\) extends \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-ring-namecert} with the commutativity field \(a \cdot b = b \cdot a\) for the multiplicative operation under the carrier's classifier. The integer ring from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-int-namecert} is the canonical concrete instance.

\section{Carrier definition}

\begin{definition}[CommRing carrier]
\label{def:commring-carrier}
Use a ring setup extended with commutativity of multiplication.
\end{definition}

\section{Concrete singleton history commutative ring instance}
\label{sec:commring-concrete-singleton-history-instance}

\begin{definition}[Concrete singleton history commutative ring instance]
\label{def:concrete-singleton-history-commring-instance}
On the concrete $\mathsf{BHist}$ carrier, define
$A_{\emp}(h):=\hsame(h,\emp)$ and
$$
R_{\emp}(h,k):=A_{\emp}(h)\land A_{\emp}(k)\land\hsame(h,k).
$$
The commutative-ring operation tuple is
$$
\mathsf{add}(h,k):=\mathsf{append}(h,k),\qquad
\mathsf{neg}(h):=\emp,\qquad
0:=\emp,
$$
with multiplication and unit
$$
\mathsf{mul}(h,k):=\mathsf{append}(h,k),\qquad
1:=\emp.
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete singleton history commutative ring laws]
\label{thm:concrete-singleton-history-commring-laws}
The concrete singleton history commutative ring instance of
Definition~\ref{def:concrete-singleton-history-commring-instance} has carrier
containing $0$, is closed under addition, negation, and multiplication, has
left and right additive zero laws under $R_{\emp}$, and satisfies
multiplicative commutativity under $R_{\emp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier witness $A_{\emp}(0)$ is reflexive $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$. For
addition and multiplication closure, if $A_{\emp}(x)$ and $A_{\emp}(y)$ hold,
then both inputs are the empty history, so
$\mathsf{append}(x,y)$ reduces to $\emp$. The negation operation is constantly
$\emp$, so its closure is immediate from the same reflexive endpoint witness.

For the two additive zero laws, the singleton input witness identifies $x$
with $\emp$; after that identification, both
$\mathsf{append}(\emp,x)$ and $\mathsf{append}(x,\emp)$ are the empty history,
and the classifier packages the two carrier endpoints with reflexive
$\hsame$. Multiplicative commutativity is identical: two singleton carrier
witnesses identify $x$ and $y$ with $\emp$, so the two append products are
both $\emp$ and are related by $R_{\emp}$.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:concrete-singleton-history-commring-full-schema-laws}
For the concrete singleton history commutative ring instance of

$(A_{\emp},R_{\emp},\mathsf{add},0,\mathsf{neg},\mathsf{mul},1)$ satisfies

Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-commring-laws}, and stability of
the commutativity row under $R_{\emp}$-classified replacement of carried

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The $\RingUp$ part is exactly
Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-ring-laws} for the displayed
append-operation tuple. It supplies the additive abelian-group laws,
multiplicative monoid laws, addition and multiplication congruence, and the
two distributive laws over $A_{\emp}$ and $R_{\emp}$.

The commutativity row is exactly
Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-commring-laws}, read at the same
append multiplication. For carried inputs $x$ and $y$, the singleton endpoint
witnesses identify both products $\mathsf{mul}(x,y)$ and
$\mathsf{mul}(y,x)$ with $\emp$, so the displayed row is an
$R_{\emp}$-classification.

For compatibility of that row, suppose the two carried inputs are replaced by
$R_{\emp}$-classified inputs. Multiplication congruence from the $\RingUp$
package transports $\mathsf{mul}(x,y)$ to the replacement product, and the
same congruence transports $\mathsf{mul}(y,x)$ to the reversed replacement
product. Compose these transported relations with the commutativity row by
symmetry and transitivity of $\hsame$; the carrier fields are preserved by the
same singleton endpoint classification, so the commutativity row remains
$R_{\emp}$-classified after replacement.
\end{proof}

$$

  \item BEDC source: $\mathsf{BHist}$ with the singleton empty-history ring instance;

\begin{theorem}[Singleton empty-history commutative ring laws]
\label{thm:singleton-empty-history-commring-laws}
The singleton empty-history commutative-ring tuple supplies a concrete
$\mathsf{SemanticNameCert}$ for the carrier $A_{\emp}$ and classifier
$\rho_{\emp}$. Its operation rows include the displayed constant
$\mathsf{add}$, $\mathsf{neg}$, and $\mathsf{mul}$ endpoints, multiplicative
commutativity, both distributive laws, and the left unit row for
$1$ under $\rho_{\emp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier-aware classifier $\rho_{\emp}$ is a semantic naming certificate.
Carrier inhabitance is witnessed by $\emp$. Reflexivity uses the given carrier
witness and $\hsame(h,h)$; symmetry swaps the endpoint carrier witnesses and
uses symmetry of $\hsame$; transitivity composes the $\hsame$ components and
keeps the outer carrier witnesses. Carrier transport is the right endpoint
carrier field of the classifier.

The concrete $\mathsf{add}$, $\mathsf{neg}$, and $\mathsf{mul}$ endpoint rows
close because all three displayed operations are constant at $\emp$. The two
carrier witnesses are therefore singleton witnesses for $\emp$, and the
relation component is reflexive $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.

The ring-law part is exactly Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-ring-laws}
applied to the displayed singleton empty-history operation tuple. This supplies
carrier inhabitance, additive abelian-group laws, multiplicative monoid laws,
operation congruence, both distributive laws, the unit laws, and zero
multiplication laws.

For multiplicative commutativity, both $\mathsf{mul}(x,y)$ and
$\mathsf{mul}(y,x)$ are definitionally $\emp$. The two endpoint witnesses for
$\rho_{\emp}$ are the singleton carrier witnesses for $\emp$, and the relation
component is $\hsame(\emp,\emp)$.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:commring-singleton-classifier-append-context-cancel}

$$
\rho_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(L,Q),\mathsf{append}(R,S))
\Longleftrightarrow
\rho_{\emp}(Q,S).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Conversely, if $\rho_{\emp}(Q,S)$ holds and both contexts are singleton
carriers, empty-result construction for append gives singleton carrier
witnesses for $\mathsf{append}(L,Q)$ and $\mathsf{append}(R,S)$. Composing
the two append carrier witnesses with symmetry gives the classifier relation
component between the contextual endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CommRing singleton classifier append associativity carrier exactness]
\label{thm:commring-singleton-classifier-append-assoc-carrier-iff}
For histories $a,b,c:\mathsf{BHist}$,
$$
\rho_{\emp}(\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(a,b),c),
\mathsf{append}(a,\mathsf{append}(b,c)))
\Longleftrightarrow
A_{\emp}(a)\land A_{\emp}(b)\land A_{\emp}(c).
$$
The ten-field fit is: traditional target singleton commutative-ring bracketing
exactness; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ with the singleton $\CommRingUp$ tuple;
carrier $A_{\emp}$; classifier $\rho_{\emp}$; stability from nested append
empty-result inversion and singleton classifier packaging; ledger the three
append factors and the two bracketings; core theorem this block; exactness the
displayed iff; standard bridge one-element commutative-ring bracketing; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
In the forward direction, project the source carrier field from the classifier.
This gives the carrier witness for
$\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(a,b),c)$. Empty-result inversion for append
splits it into carrier witnesses for $\mathsf{append}(a,b)$ and $c$.

Apply empty-result inversion again to the witness for
$\mathsf{append}(a,b)$. The resulting two carrier witnesses, together with the
witness for $c$, give $A_{\emp}(a)\land A_{\emp}(b)\land A_{\emp}(c)$ by
conjunction introduction.

Conversely, assume carrier witnesses for $a$, $b$, and $c$. Empty-result
construction first builds a carrier witness for $\mathsf{append}(a,b)$, and then
one for $\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(a,b),c)$.

The same construction applied to $b$ and $c$, followed by the witness for $a$,
builds the carrier witness for $\mathsf{append}(a,\mathsf{append}(b,c))$. Since
both bracketed endpoints are classified through $\emp$, symmetry and
transitivity of $\hsame$ package the displayed singleton classifier.
\end{proof}
