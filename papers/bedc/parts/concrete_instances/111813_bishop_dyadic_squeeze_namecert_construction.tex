It sits over the dyadic denominator discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the countable-window presentation of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-separability-namecert}. \begin{definition}[Bishop dyadic squeeze carrier]
\label{def:bishop-dyadic-squeeze-carrier}
A $\BishopDyadicSqueezeUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(\ell,u,n,W,L,U,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\ell$ and $u$ are lower and upper $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint rows.
\item $n$ is the unary precision row naming the dyadic scale at which the squeeze is inspected.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window through which the source regular rational observations are read.
\item $L$ records the lower-bound comparison between the inspected window and $\ell$.
\item $U$ records the upper-bound comparison between the inspected window and $u$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row assembled from $W,L,U$ and the displayed dyadic tolerance scale.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached from $R$ after the squeeze window has been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $\ell,u,n,W,L,U,R,E$ and the local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a squeeze request through dyadic endpoints, finite stream windows, lower and upper comparisons, regular rational readback, and the Real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopDyadicSqueezeUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopDyadicSqueezeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted squeeze packets only through $\ell,u,n,W,L,U,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a quotient real representative, selected limit, trichotomy oracle, countable-choice schedule, hidden infinite stream, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness witness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop dyadic squeeze NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-dyadic-squeeze-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopDyadicSqueezeUp$ carrier
$$
S=(\ell,u,n,W,L,U,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BishopDyadicSqueezeUp}$ surface consists exactly of dyadic endpoint exposure, unary precision exposure, finite $\StreamNameUp$ window exposure, lower-bound comparison exposure, upper-bound comparison exposure, $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, terminal $\RealUp$ seal exposure, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. Every Real or Completion consumer must expose $\ell,u,n,W,L,U,R,E$ before the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ can be consumed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishop-dyadic-squeeze-carrier}. The analytic rows are exactly $\ell,u,n,W,L,U,R,E$, while $H,C,P,N$ are the structural transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The endpoint rows $\ell,u$ and the precision row $n$ determine the displayed dyadic scale. The stream row $W$ is therefore the only observation surface through which the source regular rational data can enter the squeeze.

The comparison rows $L$ and $U$ bind that same window below and above. Hence the packet does not export a detached lower route or a detached upper route; both comparisons are attached to the same finite window and the same dyadic scale.

Finally $R$ and $E$ are downstream of the squeeze rows. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient representatives, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, hidden streams, or ambient completeness, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop dyadic squeeze Real-seal handoff]
\label{thm:bishop-dyadic-squeeze-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ or Completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\BishopDyadicSqueezeUp$ packet reaches the terminal Real seal only through the displayed finite route
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp
\longmapsto
\StreamNameUp
\longmapsto
\RegSeqRatUp
\longmapsto

$$
The handoff may use the dyadic endpoint rows, unary precision row, finite stream window, lower and upper comparison rows, and regular rational readback, but it cannot replace them by quotient real equality, a selected limit, trichotomy, countable choice, an uninspected infinite stream, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the Real or Completion-facing read of $S$. The dyadic endpoints $\ell,u$ and precision row $n$ are exposed before the stream window $W$, so the consumer reads a finite dyadic scale before any rational replay is available.

The lower and upper comparison rows $L,U$ are both attached to $W$. Thus the regular rational readback $R$ is assembled from a displayed squeeze window rather than from a selected representative or an external limit operation.

The terminal seal $E$ is downstream of $R$. It therefore receives only the regular rational route produced by the finite dyadic squeeze data, and the excluded host principles remain absent from the carrier.
\end{proof}
