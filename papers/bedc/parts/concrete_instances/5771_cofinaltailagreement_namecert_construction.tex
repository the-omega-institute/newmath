\label{def:cofinal-tail-agreement-carrier}

$$

\item $F$ is the $\RegularCauchyTailFusionUp$ row that fuses the paired cofinal tail witnesses through the shared threshold;
\item $B$ is the $\RegularCauchySelectorBudgetUp$ row that records the finite selector budget used by the paired route;
\item $S$ is the $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$ row that receives the paired agreement boundary;
\item $D$ is the dyadic readback row consumed before the seal handoff is visible;

\item $L$ is the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ handoff row consumed by completion-facing readers;

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CofinalTailAgreementUp}$ row.

The carrier contains no quotient stream equality, selected host limit, external tail oracle, countable-choice row, or ambient Real-completeness theorem.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cofinal tail-agreement NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cofinal-tail-agreement-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The dependency rows are read in order. The shared threshold $M$ supplies the common tail bound, $F$ fuses the paired cofinal witnesses through that bound, and $B$ records the finite selector budget that the paired route may use.

The consumer-facing rows are then forced. The dyadic readback $D$ and regular readbacks $R$ feed the Real agreement seal $S$, and the completion-facing reader reaches the Cauchy-limit handoff $L$ only through the same displayed route. Since $H,C,P,N$ name all transports, continuations, provenance, and local certificate rows, no hidden tail selector or ambient completeness principle is exported.

\begin{proof}
