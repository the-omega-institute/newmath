\begin{definition}[Located Cauchy embedding carrier]
\label{def:located-cauchy-embedding-carrier}
A $\LocatedCauchyEmbeddingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(X,S,R,D,E,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the located Cauchy source row, including the accepted approximation schedule, Cauchy modulus evidence, and locatedness witnesses that the embedding route may inspect.
\item $S$ is a finite $\StreamNameUp$ window over the source schedule, recording the precision requests and observation slots used by the embedding read.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to $S$, turning the displayed windows into regular rational observations.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row that budgets the endpoint, radius, and comparison tolerances used by the located Cauchy source and the completion handoff.
\item $E$ is the $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$ or $\CompletionDensityUp$ handoff row by which the displayed source observations enter the completion-facing boundary.
\item $Q$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal boundary. It accepts only the route already displayed through $X,S,R,D,E$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source row, stream window, readback row, dyadic ledger, embedding handoff, real seal, continuation row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a located Cauchy precision request through $S,R,D,E,Q$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedCauchyUp$, $\LocatedRealUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\CompletionEmbeddingUp$, $\CompletionDensityUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCauchyEmbeddingUp}$ row naming the displayed embedding route and the permitted reads from it.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier on such packets is componentwise transport through $H$ together with replay through $C$. No coordinate exports quotient completion, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located Cauchy embedding NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-cauchy-embedding-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedCauchyEmbeddingUp$ carrier
$L=(X,S,R,D,E,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCauchyEmbeddingUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed located Cauchy source, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, completion handoff, Real seal, transports, replay route, provenance, and local naming row;
\item located-source exactness, requiring every embedding read to start from $X$ and to use only the approximation schedule, Cauchy modulus evidence, and locatedness witnesses displayed there;
\item window and readback compatibility, requiring the finite window $S$ and the regular rational readback $R$ to remain attached to the same source requests;
\item dyadic tolerance compatibility, requiring the tolerance row $D$ to budget the located-source comparisons and the completion-facing handoff without introducing a host real comparison;
\item embedding handoff, requiring the row $E$ to be the only passage from the located Cauchy source route into the completion-facing surface;
\item Real-seal non-escape, requiring the terminal row $Q$ to read only the already displayed route $X,S,R,D,E$;
\item provenance exhaustion, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, package, and name only these rows.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside this displayed packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:located-cauchy-embedding-carrier}. The tuple displays the source row, finite window, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, completion handoff, Real seal, transports, replay, provenance, and local name.

Located-source exactness is forced by the first coordinate. Any embedding read begins with $X$, so its approximation schedule, Cauchy modulus evidence, and locatedness witnesses are the only source-side data available.

Window and readback compatibility is projection from $S$ and $R$. The stream window records the finite requests, and the regular rational row reads exactly those requests before any dyadic or completion-facing row can be inspected.

Dyadic tolerance compatibility and the embedding handoff are then read from $D$ and $E$. The tolerance row budgets the displayed comparisons, while the handoff row is the unique passage from the source route into the completion boundary.

The Real seal and structural rows close the surface. The row $Q$ is downstream of $X,S,R,D,E$, and $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, package, and name that same route; hence every listed obligation is a row projection and no further obligation is stored outside the packet.
\end{proof}
