\begin{theorem}[Scientific model bridge NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:scientific-model-bridge-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to its displayed rows. The scientific-object row $O$ supplies the object-facing record, gap, audit, and bridge support; the rows $A,B,R,F$ supply the physical audit, standard-bridge audit, reality-constrained model audit, and open-fit surfaces.

Read the ledger and prediction route next. The row $L$ records empirical support, failed predictions, open gaps, and falsification exposure, while $T$ records the consumer-facing prediction transport. These rows make the bridge a finite BEDC carrier rather than a detached model identifier.

Finally apply componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. The rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve exactly the displayed object, audit, bridge, model-audit, open-fit, ledger, and prediction surfaces. Since the carrier has no further coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local $\NameCert$ obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Scientific model bridge falsifiable prediction]
\label{thm:scientific-model-bridge-falsifiable-prediction}

\begin{proof}
