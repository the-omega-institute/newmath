\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EllipticCurveUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-ellipticcurve-namecert}

The elliptic-curve interface $\EllipticCurveUp$ packages a smooth projective genus-one curve over a field carrier together with a chosen base point. It depends on $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveVarUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-projectivevar-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\FieldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-field-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:ellipticcurve-carrier}

\begin{definition}[EllipticCurveUp carrier packet]\label{def:ellipticcurve-carrier-packet}
An $\EllipticCurveUp$ carrier packet is a finite displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ tuple
$$
(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{P},W,S,G,O,\pi)
$$
where $\mathcal{F}$ is a $\NameCert_{\FieldUp}$ source certificate, $\mathcal{P}$ is a $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveVarUp}$ source certificate, $W:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the Weierstrass or projective equation row, $S:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the smoothness witness row, $G:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the genus-one witness row, $O:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the chosen base-point row, and $\pi:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row. The equation coefficients and scalar comparisons are read only through $\mathcal{F}$, while the carrier, incidence, smoothness, genus, and base-point rows are read only through $\mathcal{P}$ together with the displayed provenance ledger.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Base-point readback obligation]\label{thm:ellipticcurve-basepoint-readback-obligation}
For every $\EllipticCurveUp$ carrier packet of \autoref{def:ellipticcurve-carrier-packet}, the base-point row $O$ is readable as a point row of the carried $\ProjectiveVarUp$ surface. If $O\hsame O'$ and the $\ProjectiveVarUp$ classifier transports the point row from $O$ to $O'$, then replacing $O$ by $O'$ gives a carrier packet with the same equation, smoothness, genus-one, field, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the $\ProjectiveVarUp$ source certificate $\mathcal{P}$ from the carrier packet. Its point readback clause supplies the carrier row containing $O$, and the incidence ledger records that $O$ lies on the displayed equation row $W$.

Now apply the $\hsame$ transport hypothesis to the point row. The $\ProjectiveVarUp$ classifier transports incidence along this row, so the replacement point $O'$ remains attached to the same projective carrier and the same equation row.

The remaining rows are unchanged projections of the original packet. Repacking them with $O'$ yields the transported $\EllipticCurveUp$ packet, and no field coefficient, smoothness witness, genus-one witness, or provenance row is altered.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Smooth projective carrier obligation]\label{thm:ellipticcurve-smooth-projective-carrier-obligation}
An accepted $\EllipticCurveUp$ carrier is accepted only through the $\ProjectiveVarUp$ projective-carrier, incidence, smoothness, genus-one, and point rows together with the $\FieldUp$ scalar rows for the displayed equation coefficients. In particular, smoothness and genus-one status are certificate rows over the carried projective variety and field source, not host-level assertions about an unrecorded curve.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack an accepted carrier packet. The projective carrier and incidence rows are projections of $\mathcal{P}$, while the coefficient and scalar rows used by $W$ are projections of $\mathcal{F}$.

The smoothness row $S$ and genus-one row $G$ are then checked against those same projections. Their scope is the displayed projective carrier over the displayed field coefficients, so there is no separate ambient curve object to validate.

Finally attach the base-point row by \autoref{thm:ellipticcurve-basepoint-readback-obligation}. The accepted carrier is therefore exactly the smooth genus-one projective carrier with the named base point and the named field dependency rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Field dependency ledger obligation]\label{thm:ellipticcurve-field-dependency-ledger-obligation}
All coefficient and scalar comparisons in an $\EllipticCurveUp$ carrier packet descend through the $\FieldUp$ classifier and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance ledger. If two packets have $\hsame$-transported equation rows and the $\FieldUp$ source certificate classifies their coefficient rows as the same scalar data, then every scalar comparison used by the projective incidence, smoothness, genus-one, and base-point clauses is transported by the same finite ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the coefficient rows from the equation row $W$. By \autoref{def:ellipticcurve-carrier-packet}, those rows are not interpreted directly; they are projected through the $\NameCert_{\FieldUp}$ source certificate $\mathcal{F}$.

Use the $\FieldUp$ classifier to transport each scalar comparison along the displayed $\hsame$ relation on equation rows. The finite $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row records the order in which these transported scalar rows feed the projective incidence, smoothness, genus-one, and base-point checks.

Repack the transported scalar rows with the unchanged projective certificate rows. The result is the same dependency ledger for the elliptic-curve packet, now read over the transported equation row.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:ellipticcurve-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[EllipticCurveUp NameCert exactness obligation]\label{thm:ellipticcurve-namecert-exactness-obligation}
The public $\NameCert_{\EllipticCurveUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of the carrier readback of \autoref{def:ellipticcurve-carrier-packet}, the base-point readback of \autoref{thm:ellipticcurve-basepoint-readback-obligation}, the smooth projective validation of \autoref{thm:ellipticcurve-smooth-projective-carrier-obligation}, the field dependency transport of \autoref{thm:ellipticcurve-field-dependency-ledger-obligation}, and the ledger exactness asserting that no row outside $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{Pkg}$, $\NameCert_{\FieldUp}$, and $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveVarUp}$ is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Soundness is obtained by projecting a public elliptic-curve certificate to its listed packet rows. The carrier definition supplies the field source, projective-variety source, equation, smoothness, genus-one, base-point, and provenance rows.

Completeness goes in the reverse direction. Given exactly those rows with the base-point readback, smooth projective validation, and field dependency transport clauses, assemble the tuple of \autoref{def:ellipticcurve-carrier-packet} and read it as the public $\EllipticCurveUp$ surface.

The exclusion clause follows from the same projection list. Since every accepted row is one of the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ rows or one of the two source $\NameCert$ projections, the certificate exports no unrecorded host curve, no external equation equality, and no standard bridge row.
\end{proof}
