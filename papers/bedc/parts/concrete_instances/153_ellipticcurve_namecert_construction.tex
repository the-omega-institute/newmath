\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EllipticCurveUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-ellipticcurve-namecert}

The elliptic-curve interface $\EllipticCurveUp$ packages a smooth projective genus-one curve over a field carrier together with a chosen base point. It depends on $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveVarUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-projectivevar-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\FieldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-field-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:ellipticcurve-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Elliptic-curve BHist carrier packet]
\label{def:ellipticcurve-bhist-carrier-packet}
An $\EllipticCurveUp$ carrier is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
\mathcal{E}=(\mathcal{F},\mathcal{P},a_1,a_2,a_3,a_4,a_6,\gamma,\sigma,o,\lambda_F,\lambda_P,\rho)
$$
where $\mathcal{F}$ is a source $\NameCert_{\FieldUp}$ row, $\mathcal{P}$ is a source $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveVarUp}$ row over the same field row, $a_1,a_2,a_3,a_4,a_6:\mathsf{BHist}$ are field-coefficient rows, $\gamma:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the homogeneous cubic and projective-incidence row, $\sigma:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the smooth genus-one boundary row, $o:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the chosen base-point row, $\lambda_F$ and $\lambda_P$ are the field and projective classifier rows witnessed through $\hsame$, and $\rho$ records the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance of the two source certificates and the displayed rows.

The accepted endpoint is exactly the displayed history packet. It contains no ambient curve equality, no external point set, and no host discriminant predicate; all coefficient, incidence, smoothness, base-point, classifier, continuation, and provenance data are rows of $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$.
\end{definition}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:ellipticcurve-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Elliptic-curve field source obligation]
\label{thm:ellipticcurve-field-source-obligation}
In every carrier packet of \autoref{def:ellipticcurve-bhist-carrier-packet}, the coefficient rows $a_1,a_2,a_3,a_4,a_6$, the field operation rows used to form the Weierstrass cubic, and the nonzero-discriminant row are projected from the $\NameCert_{\FieldUp}$ dependency. The $\EllipticCurveUp$ boundary may read these rows only as $\mathsf{BHist}$ data with the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance inherited from $\mathcal{F}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier packet to its $\mathcal{F}$ component and to the five displayed coefficient rows. The FieldUp certificate supplies the addition, multiplication, negation, unit, and nonzero rows needed to interpret the coefficients and the discriminant expression.

The Weierstrass cubic row is then formed by reading those operations through the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ ledger. The discriminant row is not a host-side assertion about a curve; it is the nonzero field row reached through the projected FieldUp source.

Repack the coefficient rows, operation rows, and nonzero-discriminant row with the original $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. This gives the exact field-source part of the elliptic-curve certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Elliptic-curve projective genus-one obligation]
\label{thm:ellipticcurve-projective-genus-one-obligation}
In every carrier packet of \autoref{def:ellipticcurve-bhist-carrier-packet}, the projective equation row, projective-incidence row, smoothness row, and genus-one boundary row are carried by the $\NameCert_{\ProjectiveVarUp}$ dependency as BEDC rows. They are not imported as host curves, external schemes, or ambient geometric equalities.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet to its $\mathcal{P}$ component and to the homogeneous cubic row $\gamma$. The ProjectiveVarUp source supplies the projective carrier, homogeneous zero-locus, incidence, classifier, and ledger rows needed to read $\gamma$ as a projective BEDC row.

Next project the smooth genus-one boundary row $\sigma$. Its smoothness and genus-one readings are attached to the same projective package, so the row is routed through the ProjectiveVarUp carrier and exactness surface rather than through an external curve object.

Repacking $\mathcal{P}$, $\gamma$, and $\sigma$ records the projective equation, incidence, smoothness, and genus-one data as the projective dependency portion of the $\EllipticCurveUp$ carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Elliptic-curve base-point stability obligation]
\label{thm:ellipticcurve-basepoint-stability-obligation}
The chosen base-point row $o$ in \autoref{def:ellipticcurve-bhist-carrier-packet} is stable under the inherited projective classifier and the inherited field classifier. If the field source is transported by $\lambda_F$ and the projective source is transported by $\lambda_P$, the transported packet carries a base-point row connected to $o$ by $\hsame$ and accepted by the same $\Cont$ provenance surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the base-point row $o$ and the projective classifier row $\lambda_P$. Projective transport preserves the projective incidence row, so the image of $o$ remains accepted by the transported projective carrier.

Use the field classifier row $\lambda_F$ to transport the coordinate and coefficient readings used by the base-point incidence test. The same FieldUp source rows supply the operation and nonzero data needed after transport.

The continuation row records that the transported endpoint is read through the same base-point provenance route. Repack the transported field source, projective source, incidence row, and base-point row to obtain the stable $\EllipticCurveUp$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Elliptic-curve classifier transport obligation]
\label{thm:ellipticcurve-classifier-transport-obligation}
Classified replacement of the field source and the projective source transports the whole carrier packet of \autoref{def:ellipticcurve-bhist-carrier-packet}. Componentwise $\hsame$ transports the coefficient, cubic, smoothness, genus-one, and base-point rows, while the displayed $\Cont$ rows preserve their acceptance and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ row preserves their provenance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project a classifier witness into its field part $\lambda_F$ and projective part $\lambda_P$. The field part transports the coefficient and operation rows; the projective part transports the homogeneous cubic, incidence, smoothness, and genus-one rows.

Apply componentwise $\hsame$ to the displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$ entries. Each transported entry is accepted through its original $\Cont$ row after replacing the source endpoint by the classified endpoint.

Finally carry the provenance row $\rho$ along the same componentwise route. Repacking the transported entries gives the full replacement carrier packet, with no additional quotient or host equality inserted at the boundary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Elliptic-curve NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:ellipticcurve-namecert-obligation-surface}
The public $\NameCert_{\EllipticCurveUp}$ obligation surface consists of the carrier packet of \autoref{def:ellipticcurve-bhist-carrier-packet}, the field-source rows of \autoref{thm:ellipticcurve-field-source-obligation}, the projective genus-one rows of \autoref{thm:ellipticcurve-projective-genus-one-obligation}, the base-point stability row of \autoref{thm:ellipticcurve-basepoint-stability-obligation}, and the classifier transport row of \autoref{thm:ellipticcurve-classifier-transport-obligation}. Downstream consumers may read exactly these $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ rows from the elliptic-curve certificate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Collect the displayed carrier packet first. It supplies the FieldUp source, ProjectiveVarUp source, coefficient rows, cubic row, smooth genus-one boundary row, base-point row, classifier rows, continuation readings, and provenance ledger.

Add the four obligation rows above. The field-source row provides the coefficient and discriminant surface, the projective row provides the incidence and smooth genus-one surface, the base-point row provides stability of the chosen point, and the classifier row provides transport of the whole packet.

Exactness of the public surface is by projection from this list. Any downstream use of $\NameCert_{\EllipticCurveUp}$ must pass through one of the displayed rows, so the certificate exports no stronger ambient field, curve, point-set, or geometric equality principle.
\end{proof}
