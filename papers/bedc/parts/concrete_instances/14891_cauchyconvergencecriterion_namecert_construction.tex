Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ packet records the constructive Cauchy convergence criterion as finite BEDC route data. It sits between the finite stream schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the $\RealUp$ seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the modulus surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, and the limit-seal route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}. The certificate names the finite rows that make a convergence read admissible; it is not host completeness, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, or open-cover compactness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy convergence criterion carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-convergence-criterion-carrier}
A $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule whose finite windows are the only sequence observations visible to the carrier.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ row assigning each dyadic tolerance request a displayed finite threshold for the windows in $S$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the dyadic scales, rational comparisons, and exhaustion budget used by the criterion.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained from the scheduled windows under the threshold and tolerance data $M,D$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $S,M,D,R$ have been displayed and the local convergence criterion has been admitted.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the stream schedule, modulus, dyadic ledger, regular-rational handoff, Real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each tolerance request through $S,M,D,R,E$ and then through the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows. It contains no coordinate for host completeness, quotient-stream equality, a selected host limit, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or an unbounded stream read.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy convergence criterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ carrier $G=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following six obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed stream schedule, Cauchy modulus, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular-rational handoff, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name;
\item finite-window classification, requiring every sequence read to pass through the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$;
\item modulus stability, requiring the threshold row $M$ to be preserved by the componentwise $\hsame$ transports in $H$ and replayed by $C$ for the same tolerance requests;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, requiring the regular-rational row $R$ to be read only after the scheduled windows, modulus thresholds, and dyadic tolerance ledger have been displayed;
\item $\RealUp$ seal nonescape, requiring the seal row $E$ to remain downstream of $S,M,D,R$ and the stored structural rows;
\item ledger exhaustion, requiring every accepted tolerance request to be accounted for by the dyadic ledger $D$ and the provenance row $P$ before any convergence-facing consumer can read the seal.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-convergence-criterion-carrier}. Its visible coordinates are exactly the stream schedule, modulus, dyadic ledger, regular-rational handoff, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

The finite-window classifier is forced by the first coordinate. A sequence read has no source except the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$, and the continuation row can replay only the windows attached to that schedule.

Modulus stability and ledger exhaustion are read next. The threshold row $M$ and the dyadic ledger $D$ answer the same tolerance requests, and the transport row $H$ preserves those answers before the handoff $R$ is available.

The Real-facing rows are downstream. The regular-rational handoff $R$ is admitted only from $S,M,D$, and the seal row $E$ is consumed only after that handoff. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host completeness, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or an unbounded stream read, the six listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy convergence criterion Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-real-seal-handoff}
Every accepted $\RealUp$-facing convergence read of a $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ carrier $G=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ factors through the displayed finite route
$$
S \longmapsto M \longmapsto D \longmapsto R \longmapsto E
$$
together with the stored $\hsame$ transport $H$, $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. Before reaching the $\RealUp$ seal, the read is compatible with the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundary and exports no host completeness theorem, quotient stream, selected limit, trichotomy row, open-cover compactness row, or detached seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted Real-facing convergence read. Carrier admission gives only the finite schedule $S$ as sequence data, so the read first factors through the displayed stream windows.

The tolerance part of the route is then forced. The modulus row $M$ supplies thresholds for the requests, while the dyadic ledger $D$ records the scales and comparisons consumed at those thresholds. The continuation row $C$ replays this same request path.

Next pass to regular-rational data and the seal. The row $R$ is the only $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff in the carrier, and the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ is downstream of $R$. The transport and provenance rows $H$ and $P$ preserve the same packet, while the local name $N$ records the route as a $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ certificate.

The route has no extra coordinate after $E$. Thus compatibility with the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundary is witnessed by the same finite handoff, and the read cannot export host completeness, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or a detached seal.
\end{proof}
