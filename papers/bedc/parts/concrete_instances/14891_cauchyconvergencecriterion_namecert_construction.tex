\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-convergence-criterion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ packet records the constructive Cauchy convergence criterion as finite BEDC route data. It sits between the finite stream schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the $\RealUp$ seal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the modulus surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, and the limit-seal route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchylimitseal-namecert}. The certificate names the finite rows that make a convergence read admissible; it is not host completeness, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, or open-cover compactness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy convergence criterion carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-convergence-criterion-carrier}
A $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule whose finite windows are the only sequence observations visible to the carrier.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ row assigning each dyadic tolerance request a displayed finite threshold for the windows in $S$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the dyadic scales, rational comparisons, and exhaustion budget used by the criterion.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained from the scheduled windows under the threshold and tolerance data $M,D$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $S,M,D,R$ have been displayed and the local convergence criterion has been admitted.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the stream schedule, modulus, dyadic ledger, regular-rational handoff, Real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from each tolerance request through $S,M,D,R,E$ and then through the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows. It contains no coordinate for host completeness, quotient-stream equality, a selected host limit, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or an unbounded stream read.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy convergence criterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ carrier $G=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following six obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed stream schedule, Cauchy modulus, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular-rational handoff, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name;
\item finite-window classification, requiring every sequence read to pass through the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$;
\item modulus stability, requiring the threshold row $M$ to be preserved by the componentwise $\hsame$ transports in $H$ and replayed by $C$ for the same tolerance requests;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, requiring the regular-rational row $R$ to be read only after the scheduled windows, modulus thresholds, and dyadic tolerance ledger have been displayed;
\item $\RealUp$ seal nonescape, requiring the seal row $E$ to remain downstream of $S,M,D,R$ and the stored structural rows;
\item ledger exhaustion, requiring every accepted tolerance request to be accounted for by the dyadic ledger $D$ and the provenance row $P$ before any convergence-facing consumer can read the seal.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-convergence-criterion-carrier}. Its visible coordinates are exactly the stream schedule, modulus, dyadic ledger, regular-rational handoff, Real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

The finite-window classifier is forced by the first coordinate. A sequence read has no source except the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$, and the continuation row can replay only the windows attached to that schedule.

Modulus stability and ledger exhaustion are read next. The threshold row $M$ and the dyadic ledger $D$ answer the same tolerance requests, and the transport row $H$ preserves those answers before the handoff $R$ is available.

The Real-facing rows are downstream. The regular-rational handoff $R$ is admitted only from $S,M,D$, and the seal row $E$ is consumed only after that handoff. Since the carrier has no coordinate for host completeness, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or an unbounded stream read, the six listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy convergence criterion Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-real-seal-handoff}
Every accepted $\RealUp$-facing convergence read of a $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ carrier $G=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ factors through the displayed finite route
$$
S \longmapsto M \longmapsto D \longmapsto R \longmapsto E
$$
together with the stored $\hsame$ transport $H$, $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. Before reaching the $\RealUp$ seal, the read is compatible with the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundary and exports no host completeness theorem, quotient stream, selected limit, trichotomy row, open-cover compactness row, or detached seal.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted Real-facing convergence read. Carrier admission gives only the finite schedule $S$ as sequence data, so the read first factors through the displayed stream windows.

The tolerance part of the route is then forced. The modulus row $M$ supplies thresholds for the requests, while the dyadic ledger $D$ records the scales and comparisons consumed at those thresholds. The continuation row $C$ replays this same request path.

Next pass to regular-rational data and the seal. The row $R$ is the only $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff in the carrier, and the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ is downstream of $R$. The transport and provenance rows $H$ and $P$ preserve the same packet, while the local name $N$ records the route as a $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ certificate.

The route has no extra coordinate after $E$. Thus compatibility with the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundary is witnessed by the same finite handoff, and the read cannot export host completeness, quotient streams, selected limits, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or a detached seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy convergence criterion limit-seal determinacy]
\label{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-limit-seal-determinacy}
Let $G=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ and $G'=(S,M,D,R,E',H,C,P,N)$ be accepted $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ carriers sharing the same displayed stream schedule, Cauchy modulus, dyadic ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows. If their Real-facing convergence reads are compatible with the same $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundary over the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$ seal, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows, then the two reads determine the same seal route.

The comparison cannot introduce a detached seal, quotient stream, selected host limit, trichotomy row, open-cover compactness row, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-real-seal-handoff} to each Real-facing convergence read. Both reads are forced through the displayed finite route
$$
S \longmapsto M \longmapsto D \longmapsto R \longmapsto E
$$
or through the corresponding notational endpoint $E'$ for the second carrier, with the same transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

The shared $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundary now supplies the seal comparison. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-limit-seal-seal-route-determinacy}, once the $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\RegSeqRatUp$ row, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\RealUp$ seal boundary, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and $\NameCert$ rows agree, there is no second seal route available to a completion or $\RealUp$ consumer.

Finally return to the non-escape clauses in the handoff theorem. The carrier has no coordinate for a detached seal, quotient stream, selected host limit, trichotomy row, open-cover compactness row, or ambient completeness theorem, so the identified $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ route is determined entirely by the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy convergence criterion NullSequence factorization]
\label{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-nullsequence-factorization}
Let $G=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ carrier. Any $\RealUp$-facing convergence read of $G$ whose comparison row is a zero-difference read factors through a displayed $\NullSequenceUp$ packet before the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundary may consume it.

The displayed $\NullSequenceUp$ packet uses the same $\RegSeqRatUp$ difference row $R$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $D$, $\StreamNameUp$ window schedule $S$, $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold row $M$, $\RealUp$ seal row $E$, and provenance rows $H,C,P,N$ already carried by $G$. No second stream representative, quotient stream, host limit, trichotomy row, choice principle, open-cover compactness object, or ambient completeness coordinate is introduced by this zero-difference handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier $G$. The coordinates $S,M,D,R$ give the finite tolerance-window route: stream observations are read only through $S$, tolerance requests are bounded by $M$, dyadic scales and comparisons are recorded by $D$, and the regular-rational comparison row is $R$.

Now impose the zero-difference condition on the comparison read. By \autoref{thm:null-sequence-window-threshold-exactness}, the tolerance request must pass through the displayed dyadic tolerance row, observation window, and threshold row before any $\RealUp$ seal read. In the present carrier these rows are exactly $D,S,M$, with $R$ as the displayed difference row.

Apply \autoref{thm:null-sequence-real-seal-factorization}. It makes the $\NullSequenceUp$ packet the finite consumer boundary for the $\RealUp$ equality read, so the seal row $E$ is reached only through the same difference, tolerance, window, threshold, transport, continuation, package, and local certificate rows.

Finally return to the remaining coordinates of $G$. The rows $H,C,P,N$ record componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming for the same packet, while the $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundary can consume only the finite handoff already displayed. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a quotient stream, host limit, trichotomy, choice, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness theorem, the zero-difference read factors through $\NullSequenceUp$ and exports nothing beyond that route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy convergence criterion source-triad exhaustion]
\label{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-source-triad-exhaustion}
Let $G=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$- or $\CauchyLimitSealUp$-facing read of $G$ factors through the displayed source triad
$$
S,M,D,R
$$
before the $\RealUp$ seal row $E$ is available.

The read has no bypass through quotient streams, host completeness, selected limits, trichotomy, or open-cover compactness. In particular, every convergence-facing consumer sees the stream schedule, modulus threshold row, dyadic tolerance ledger, and regular-rational handoff before it can consume a seal boundary.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:cauchy-convergence-criterion-carrier}. The only sequence observations are the finite windows named by $S$, the only tolerance thresholds are carried by $M$, the only scale and comparison ledger is $D$, and the only regular-rational handoff is $R$.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-namecert-obligations}. Its finite-window classification, modulus stability, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, Real-seal nonescape, and ledger-exhaustion rows require the consumer to traverse $S,M,D,R$ before any convergence-facing seal read.

For a $\RealUp$ consumer, \autoref{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-real-seal-handoff} identifies the displayed route from the stream schedule through the threshold, ledger, and handoff rows to $E$. For a $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ consumer, \autoref{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-limit-seal-determinacy} reads the same boundary data and admits no second seal route.

The final exclusion is structural. The carrier definition contains no coordinate for quotient streams, host completeness, selected limits, trichotomy, or open-cover compactness, and \autoref{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-nullsequence-factorization} keeps zero-difference reads inside the same finite packet. Hence every accepted read is exhausted by the displayed source triad before $E$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy convergence criterion window-threshold coverage]
\label{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-window-threshold-coverage}
Let $G=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ carrier. Every finite $\StreamNameUp$ window consumed by the convergence criterion has a matching $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold in $M$ and a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row in $D$ in the same packet. Thus no unbounded stream read is accepted by the local classifier route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from a finite window read admitted by the local classifier. By \autoref{def:cauchy-convergence-criterion-carrier}, the window can only come from the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$.

The threshold and tolerance data are then forced by the same carrier projection. The row $M$ assigns the relevant $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold for the tolerance request, while $D$ records the dyadic scale, rational comparison, and exhaustion budget consumed by that threshold.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-namecert-obligations}. Finite-window classification keeps the read on $S$, modulus stability preserves the threshold row under $H$ and $C$, and ledger exhaustion accounts for the request in $D$ and $P$ before the handoff $R$ or seal row $E$ can be consumed.

The handoff and seal results do not add a stream source. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-real-seal-handoff} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-nullsequence-factorization}, downstream $\RealUp$, $\CauchyLimitSealUp$, and zero-difference consumers reuse the same $S,M,D$ rows. Since the carrier has no unbounded stream coordinate, the classifier accepts only covered finite windows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy convergence criterion ledger-classifier exactness]
\label{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-ledger-classifier-exactness}
Let
$$
G=(S,M,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
and
$$
G'=(S,M,D,R,E',H,C,P,N)
$$
be accepted $\CauchyConvergenceCriterionUp$ carriers with the same displayed $\StreamNameUp$ schedule, $\CauchyModulusUp$ thresholds, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ ledger, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local name rows. If their $\RealUp$ and $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundary reads are compatible, then the two carriers determine the same local classifier route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project both carriers. The displayed classifier data agree on $S,M,D,R,H,C,P,N$, so their finite windows, threshold assignments, dyadic tolerance rows, regular-rational handoff, transports, replays, provenance, and local names are the same.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-source-triad-exhaustion}. Any $\RealUp$- or $\CauchyLimitSealUp$-facing read from either carrier must pass through the same source triad $S,M,D,R$ before reaching its seal row.

Next use \autoref{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-window-threshold-coverage}. The shared ledger covers exactly the stream windows accepted by the classifier, so there is no residual unbounded stream read that could distinguish the two carriers.

The compatible boundary reads identify the remaining endpoint. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-convergence-criterion-limit-seal-determinacy}, compatible $\CauchyLimitSealUp$ boundaries over the same displayed schedule, handoff, dyadic ledger, seal boundary, transport, continuation, provenance, and name rows determine the same seal route. The Real handoff theorem supplies the corresponding $\RealUp$ route, and the NullSequence factorization theorem keeps zero-difference comparisons inside the same packet. Therefore the accepted carriers determine the same local classifier route.
\end{proof}
