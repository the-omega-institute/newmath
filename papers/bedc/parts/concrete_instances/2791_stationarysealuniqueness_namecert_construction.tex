\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\StationarySealUniquenessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-stationary-seal-uniqueness-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\StationarySealUniquenessUp$ surface isolates the finite seal packet obtained when a stationary rational classifier is transported through the constant $\StreamNameUp$ row, the finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumption row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger, and the $\RealUp$ finite-window boundary. It is a naming certificate for the four public readings of the same stationary seed, not a host-real equality assertion or a completeness principle.

As a concrete-instance dependency, $\StationarySealUniquenessUp$ is the four-reading finite-window specialization of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-stationaryembeddingclassifier-namecert}. It consumes the $\RatUp$ seed classifier, constant $\StreamNameUp$ row, stationary $\RegSeqRatUp$ diagonal row, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger, and $\RealUp$ finite-window classifier before exposing the same selected-window seal.

\begin{definition}[Stationary seal uniqueness carrier]
\label{def:stationary-seal-uniqueness-carrier}
A $\StationarySealUniquenessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(Q,S,G,D,R,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the $\RatUp$ stationary seed row, recording two rational history carriers and their transported $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row;
\item $S$ is the constant $\StreamNameUp$ row read from $Q$;
\item $G$ is the finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal-consumption row that reads the same constant stream through a displayed observation window;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger for the finite window;
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ finite-window classifier row obtained from the same stationary constant-stream data;
\item $F$ records the selected finite window and the four public projections from $U$ to $D,S,G,R$;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the rational seed, stream row, regular-sequence seal row, dyadic ledger, real classifier row, window selector, continuation routes, package provenance, and local name;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes from $Q$ to $S$, from $S$ to $G$, from $S$ to $D$, and from $D,S,G$ to the real-facing row $R$;
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\StationarySealUniquenessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient-real equality, selected host limits, non-stationary tails, ambient completeness, or an external comparison relation beyond the displayed four readings.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary seal uniqueness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:stationary-seal-uniqueness-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\StationarySealUniquenessUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:stationary-seal-uniqueness-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission for $U=(Q,S,G,D,R,F,H,C,P,N)$, $\RatUp$ seed exposure, constant $\StreamNameUp$ readback, finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal consumption, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation-ledger exposure, $\RealUp$ finite-window classifier exposure, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet $U$. The rows $Q,S,G,D,R,F$ supply the rational seed, constant stream, regular-sequence seal consumption, dyadic ledger, real finite-window classifier, and selected window.

The stationary seed obligation is read from $Q$: it contains the two rational carriers and the $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row. The constant $\StreamNameUp$ obligation is read from $S$ by replaying the continuation route from $Q$ to the constant stream.

The finite-window obligations are then forced by the packet order. Row $G$ consumes the selected window as a $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal row, $D$ records the dyadic observations read from that same window, and $R$ exposes the real-facing classifier only after $D,S,G$ have already been displayed.

The remaining data are routing and naming data. Rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the listed coordinates, replay the finite reads, record the dependency packages, and name the stationary seal packet without adding quotient-real equality, host completeness, or an external real comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary seal four-row exhaustion]
\label{thm:stationary-seal-uniqueness-four-row-exhaustion}
Let $U=(Q,S,G,D,R,F,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\StationarySealUniquenessUp$ carrier. Every certified finite observation window exported by $U$ has exactly the four public readings $D,S,G,R$: the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ observation ledger, the constant $\StreamNameUp$ classifier, the finite $\RegSeqRatUp$ seal-consumption row, and the $\RealUp$ finite-window classifier. These four readings are all transported from the same $\RatUp$ seed row $Q$, and any two accepted seal packets with the same $Q,F,H,C,P,N$ expose the same four-row public surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the accepted carrier and project the rows $Q,S,G,D,R,F,H,C,P,N$. The selected window is recorded in $F$, and the only continuation routes out of the stationary seed are the routes displayed in $C$.

Read the stream-facing boundary first. The route from $Q$ to $S$ exposes the constant $\StreamNameUp$ classifier induced by the rational seed, so every later finite-window read is already tied to the same $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ row.

Next read the two finite observation rows. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $G$ consumes the selected constant-stream window, while the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ row $D$ records the dyadic ledger for that same window. Since both rows are reached through the same selector $F$ and the same continuation routes $C$, neither can introduce an independent seal coordinate.

Finally read the real-facing row $R$. The $\RealUp$ finite-window classifier receives only $D,S,G$ together with the transport, provenance, and local name rows $H,P,N$. Thus the public surface of the seal is exhausted by $D,S,G,R$, and two carriers agreeing on $Q,F,H,C,P,N$ replay the same four readings.
\end{proof}
