It packages the requested precision, dyadic window family, selected cofinal tail, RegSeqRat readback, RealUp seal handoff, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming in one displayed $\BHist$ carrier. \begin{definition}[Dyadic cofinal tail selector carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-cofinal-tail-selector-carrier}
A $\DyadicCofinalTailSelectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(e,\mathcal{D},t,w,r,a,h,c,p,n)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $e$ is the positive $\RatUp$ precision request made by the diagonal-limit consumer;
\item $\mathcal{D}$ is the finite dyadic cofinal-window family opened at that request;
\item $t$ is the selected cofinal tail index, displayed as a row in the same finite packet;
\item $w$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation window determined by $t$ and $\mathcal{D}$;
\item $r$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row assembled from the selected dyadic window;
\item $a$ is the $\RealUp$ seal handoff reached only through $w$ and $r$;
\item $h$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the request, dyadic family, selected tail, window, readback, seal, and structural rows;
\item $c$ records the displayed $\Cont$ route from $e$ through $\mathcal{D},t,w,r$ into $a$;
\item $p$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local certificate rows;
\item $n$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicCofinalTailSelectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only through the visible rows $e,\mathcal{D},t,w,r,a,h,c,p,n$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic cofinal tail selector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-cofinal-tail-selector-namecert-obligations}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The selector obligations are read in order. The request row $e$ opens only the finite dyadic family $\mathcal{D}$, the selected tail $t$ is recorded before any window row can be consumed, and the window row $w$ is the only source for the RegSeqRat handoff $r$.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for host-selected limits, quotient-stream equality, countable choice, or hidden selectors, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic cofinal-window exhaustion]
\label{thm:dyadic-cofinal-tail-selector-cofinal-window-exhaustion}
For an accepted $\DyadicCofinalTailSelectorUp$ carrier
$$
S=(e,\mathcal{D},t,w,r,a,h,c,p,n),
$$
every cofinal-window read made by a Real and Completion diagonal-limit consumer is exhausted by the displayed finite dyadic family $\mathcal{D}$, the selected tail row $t$, the finite StreamName window $w$, the RegSeqRat readback row $r$, and the structural rows $h,c,p,n$. No consumer can replace this route by an unbounded window search, countable choice selector, host supremum argument, or external tail-selection function.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only rows that carry cofinal-window data are $\mathcal{D}$, $t$, and $w$, and they are all displayed before the RegSeqRat and RealUp handoff rows.

The continuation row $c$ routes the precision request through that finite family and selected tail before any readback is available. Componentwise transport along $h$ preserves the same displayed rows, while $p$ and $n$ record provenance and local naming for the same packet.

Thus every cofinal-window read is already inside $e,\mathcal{D},t,w,r,a,h,c,p,n$. The carrier provides no coordinate for an unbounded search, countable choice selector, host supremum, or external tail-selection function.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic cofinal tail selector Real seal handoff]
\label{thm:dyadic-cofinal-tail-selector-real-seal-handoff}
For an accepted $\DyadicCofinalTailSelectorUp$ carrier, the $\RealUp$ seal row $a$ is reached only after the selected cofinal tail $t$ has fixed the finite dyadic window family $\mathcal{D}$, the StreamName window row $w$, and the RegSeqRat readback row $r$. The real-facing handoff therefore factors through the displayed route
$$
e,\mathcal{D},t,w,r,h,c,p,n
$$
before it reaches $a$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier rows in order. The seal row $a$ appears after the selected tail, finite window, and RegSeqRat readback rows, so a real-facing consumer cannot inspect the seal before the finite dyadic route has been fixed.

Use \autoref{thm:dyadic-cofinal-tail-selector-cofinal-window-exhaustion}. It confines the cofinal-window read to $\mathcal{D},t,w,r,h,c,p,n$ and removes any hidden selector route.

The remaining structural rows transport and replay the same packet. Hence the RealUp handoff reaches $a$ only through the displayed finite route and cannot import an ambient limit or quotient-stream comparison.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic cofinal tail selector non-escape]
\label{thm:dyadic-cofinal-tail-selector-nonescape}
An accepted $\DyadicCofinalTailSelectorUp$ carrier cannot be used to recover data outside the displayed tuple $e,\mathcal{D},t,w,r,a,h,c,p,n$. In particular, it exports no unbounded dyadic window family, second tail selector, quotient of regular sequences, host real limit, countable choice rule, or RealUp completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The NameCert obligations identify every row available to a downstream consumer. The request, dyadic family, selected tail, window, readback, and seal rows are the only content rows, and $h,c,p,n$ only transport, replay, package, and name those rows.

Apply the cofinal-window exhaustion theorem to any attempted window read. It forces the read through $\mathcal{D},t,w,r,h,c,p,n$.

Apply the Real seal handoff theorem to any real-facing read. It forces the read through the selected dyadic window and RegSeqRat handoff before the seal row $a$ can be consumed.

Since no additional coordinate is present, the packet has no source for unbounded windows, a second selector, quotient regular sequences, host real limits, countable choice, or a RealUp completeness principle.
\end{proof}
