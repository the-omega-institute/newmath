\begin{definition}[Localized Cauchy rate carrier]
\label{def:localized-cauchy-rate-carrier}
A $\LocalizedCauchyRateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(W,D,T,R,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row for the inspected rational stream segment.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger naming the local dyadic budgets used by the packet.
\item $T$ is the $\CauchyTailBoundUp$ row displaying the tail-bound witness for the requested local tolerance window.
\item $R$ is the $\RegularCauchyTailBoundUp$ row recording the regular tail-bound strengthening consumed by regular Cauchy readers.
\item $S$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, tying the window and tail-bound rows to regular rational sequence data.
\item $Q$ is the $\CauchyOscillationUp$ row, recording the local oscillation bound checked against the same window and dyadic budget.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row available only after $W,D,T,R,S,Q$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for windows, tolerances, tail bounds, regular readback, oscillation, real seal, replay, provenance, and naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a localized rate request through $W,D,T,R,S,Q,E,H,P,N$.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocalizedCauchyRateUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $W,D,T,R,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for a selected rate outside $T$ and $R$, quotient stream equality, an external limit selector, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Localized Cauchy rate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:localized-cauchy-rate-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocalizedCauchyRateUp$ carrier $L=(W,D,T,R,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocalizedCauchyRateUp}$ surface is exhausted by the following obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: the accepted object is exactly the displayed packet $W,D,T,R,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N$;
\item window localization: every rate read starts with the finite $\StreamNameUp$ row $W$ and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$;
\item tail-bound visibility: the $\CauchyTailBoundUp$ row $T$ and $\RegularCauchyTailBoundUp$ row $R$ are displayed before any regular rational readback is consumed;
\item regular readback: the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $S$ reads the same window, dyadic budget, and tail-bound rows;
\item oscillation compatibility: the $\CauchyOscillationUp$ row $Q$ checks the local oscillation bound against the same budget and tail window;
\item real handoff: the $\RealUp$ seal $E$ is reachable only after $W,D,T,R,S,Q$ have been displayed;
\item ledger nonescape: $H,C,P,N$ export no selected rate, quotient representative, host limit, trichotomy decision, countable-choice schedule, ambient completeness theorem, or bridge certificate.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside those rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:localized-cauchy-rate-carrier}. The accepted rows are exactly $W,D,T,R,S,Q,E,H,C,P,N$, so carrier admission exposes only finite windows, dyadic budgets, tail bounds, regular readback, oscillation, a real seal, and structural rows.

The localized rate route begins with $W$ and $D$. These rows name the finite stream segment and the tolerance budget, so the tail-bound rows cannot be read as global convergence-speed functions.

Next read $T$ and $R$. The ordinary Cauchy tail-bound row supplies the requested local tail witness, while the regular tail-bound row records the strengthening needed by a regular Cauchy consumer. The readback row $S$ consumes those same displayed rows, and $Q$ checks the matching oscillation bound.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ seal and transport the route. Since none of these rows adds a selector, quotient representative, trichotomy decision, countable-choice schedule, ambient completeness theorem, or bridge certificate, the listed obligations exhaust the local surface.
\end{proof}
