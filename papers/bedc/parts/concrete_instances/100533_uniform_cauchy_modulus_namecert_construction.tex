\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording every requested rational radius and comparison budget;

\begin{theorem}[Uniform Cauchy modulus NameCert obligations]\label{thm:uniform-cauchy-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\UniformCauchyModulusUp$ carrier $U=(S,R,D,M,W,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchyModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed selected-window, regular-readback, dyadic-ledger, shared-modulus, witness, handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows;
\item classifier transport, requiring accepted comparisons to preserve $S,R,D,M,W,E,H,C,P,N$ componentwise and to preserve no hidden completion datum;
\item shared-window budget exactness, requiring the single row $M$ to govern every displayed Cauchy comparison requested by $D$;
\item finite witness replay, requiring each accepted comparison beyond the budget in $M$ to pass through $S$, $R$, $D$, and $W$ before any endpoint-facing handoff is read;
\item $\RealUp$ handoff nonescape, requiring $E$ to consume only the displayed finite route and never quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, trichotomy, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:uniform-cauchy-modulus-carrier}. The displayed packet has exactly the rows $S,R,D,M,W,E,H,C,P,N$, so the local certificate has no detached limit selector, quotient stream coordinate, or ambient completeness witness.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The row $H$ stores the permitted $\hsame$ comparisons, while $C$ replays only the displayed route from selected windows through regular readback, dyadic budgets, the shared modulus row, the finite witness row, and the endpoint handoff.

The shared-window budget is unique inside the packet. A tolerance request may ask for many finite window comparisons, but those comparisons all read the same displayed modulus row $M$ before the witness row $W$ can be exported.

Finally, the $\RealUp$ handoff row $E$ is downstream of the same finite route. Since none of the excluded principles is a coordinate of the carrier, none can be exported by the local $\NameCert_{\UniformCauchyModulusUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

Thus every $\RealUp$ or completion-facing read must pass through the finite carrier surface before reaching $E$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for the excluded principles, no such principle can be extracted from this handoff.
\end{proof}
