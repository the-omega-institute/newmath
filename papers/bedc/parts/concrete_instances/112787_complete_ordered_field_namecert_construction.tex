\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompleteOrderedFieldUp$}

Following Bourbaki and Bishop--Bridges on complete ordered fields, the $\CompleteOrderedFieldUp$ packet records the traditional complete ordered-field interface as finite BEDC NameCert data rather than as an ambient real line. It sits next to the terminal Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular rational sequence route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the dyadic precision core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}; it packages the completion obligations those siblings expose without claiming that $\RealUp$ has already discharged them.

\begin{definition}[Complete ordered-field carrier]
\label{def:complete-ordered-field-carrier}
A $\CompleteOrderedFieldUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(O,Q,D,E,L,T,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the ordered-field source row naming addition, multiplication, zero, one, positivity, order comparison, and compatibility only as finite $\BHist$ data.
\item $Q$ is the regular-Cauchy completion interface read through $\RegSeqRatUp$ and the terminal $\RealUp$ naming seal.
\item $D$ is the dyadic density and Archimedean readback row read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$.
\item $E$ is the order classifier, comparing accepted ordered-field reads through the displayed algebra, order, dyadic, and regular-Cauchy rows.
\item $L$ is the Cauchy-limit seal, naming only those limit requests whose precision, regular sequence, and order rows have all been displayed.
\item $T$ is the transport row for componentwise $\hsame$ comparison of ordered-field, completion, dyadic, classifier, and limit-seal rows.
\item $H$ records the local $\hsame$ ledger for replaying equality-facing component transport without quotient equality.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from ordered-field source rows through completion, dyadic readback, order classification, and limit sealing.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompleteOrderedFieldUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompleteOrderedFieldUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier of accepted packets compares only $O,Q,D,E,L,T,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a proof that $\RealUp$ is complete, for Dedekind-cut completeness, for quotient equality of real names, for a selected limit operator, or for a host complete ordered field.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CompleteOrderedField sibling route]
\label{thm:complete-ordered-field-sibling-route}
Every accepted $\CompleteOrderedFieldUp$ carrier routes its Real-facing completion interface through the siblings already named in the L10 completion lattice. The completion read $Q$ is a consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} before it reaches \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}; the dyadic density and Archimedean readback row $D$ is a consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}; and the order classifier $E$ may compare only those displayed rows together with the ordered-field source $O$ and Cauchy-limit seal $L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:complete-ordered-field-carrier}. Its completion-facing coordinates are $Q,D,E,L$ together with structural rows $T,H,C,P,N$.

The regular-Cauchy row $Q$ supplies the route through RegSeqRat and then Real naming. The dyadic row $D$ supplies the finite density and Archimedean readback data used by that route.

The order classifier $E$ can compare only the displayed ordered-field source $O$, the regular-Cauchy route $Q$, the dyadic readback row $D$, and the Cauchy-limit seal $L$. Transport and replay preserve those rows through $T,H,C,P,N$, so the packet supplies a sibling route and not an ambient completeness theorem.
\end{proof}
