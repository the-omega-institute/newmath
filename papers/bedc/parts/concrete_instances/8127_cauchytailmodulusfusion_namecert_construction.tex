\label{thm:cauchy-tail-modulus-fusion-namecert-obligations}

\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier. The packet lists the two tail schedules, two modulus rows, shared threshold, modulus comparison, selector windows, dyadic tolerances, regular readbacks, Real seal, transport, route, provenance, and local name as public coordinates.

The sibling tail and modulus routes supply the two source boundaries. The finite observation rows then pass through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ before the $\RealUp$ seal is exposed. Since the carrier has no quotient stream, host real equality, selected external limit, hidden modulus comparison, or ambient completeness coordinate, these rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
