\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DirichletApproximationUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dirichlet-approximation-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Hardy--Wright's presentation of Dirichlet rational approximation, the $\DirichletApproximationUp$ packet names the finite denominator-bounded route that sends a real-facing rational request to a displayed approximant before any host real equality or best-approximant selector is visible. It sits over the rational-tree source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-stern-brocot-namecert}, the interval boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite rational-readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-rational-approximation-namecert}, and the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet also consumes $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback rows so the approximation certificate remains a finite NameCert surface rather than an ambient theorem about completed reals.

\begin{definition}[Dirichlet approximation carrier]
\label{def:dirichlet-approximation-carrier}
A $\DirichletApproximationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
A=(Q,B,M,D,S,G,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Q$ is the rational source row. It records the $\RatUp$ input rationals and interval endpoints that the approximation request may inspect.
\item $B$ is the denominator-bound row. It stores the positive finite bound, the visible denominator budget, and the comparison rows used to certify that the exported denominator stays within the bound.
\item $M$ is the Stern--Brocot or Farey mediant route. It records the finite branch sequence, mediant bracketing data, and rational candidate selected by displayed tree search rather than by an external choice principle.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row. It stores the requested dyadic error, the slack ledger, and the endpoint comparisons used to validate the candidate against the displayed rational interval.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row. It records the finite real-name windows that may be read by the approximation request.
\item $G$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row. It reads the windows in $S$ as regular rational data before any real-facing handoff is exposed.
\item $R$ is the $\RealRationalApproximationUp$ handoff row. It packages the rational candidate, denominator bound, dyadic tolerance, StreamName window, and RegSeqRat readback as the finite approximation surface consumed downstream.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row. It can be read only after $Q,B,M,D,S,G,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the rational source, denominator bound, mediant route, dyadic tolerance, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, real-rational handoff, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Dirichlet approximation request through $Q,B,M,D,S,G,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DirichletApproximationUp$, $\RatUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\SternBrocotUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealRationalApproximationUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DirichletApproximationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts exactly that these rows form one finite denominator-bounded rational-approximation packet. It has no coordinate for host real equality, quotient representatives, choice-selected best approximants, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet approximation NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dirichlet-approximation-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DirichletApproximationUp$ carrier $A=(Q,B,M,D,S,G,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\DirichletApproximationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the rational source, denominator bound, Stern--Brocot or Farey mediant route, dyadic tolerance, StreamName window, RegSeqRat readback, RealRationalApproximation handoff, RealUp seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item rational-source admission, requiring every exported approximant and interval endpoint to be read from the displayed row $Q$;
\item denominator-bound admission, requiring the denominator comparison to pass through the finite row $B$ before the candidate can be exported;
\item mediant-route admission, requiring the rational candidate to be produced by the displayed Stern--Brocot or Farey route $M$;
\item dyadic-tolerance admission, requiring the approximation error and slack comparisons to be read from $D$;
\item window-readback admission, requiring $S$ and $G$ to be fixed before the real-rational handoff $R$ is visible;
\item Real-seal admission, requiring $E$ to remain terminal and to expose no host real equality, quotient representative, best-approximant selector, trichotomy branch, or ambient completeness theorem;
\item transport, replay, provenance, and naming exhaustion, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve and source exactly the displayed packet.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

The mediant and tolerance obligations use the next two rows. The row $M$ supplies the Stern--Brocot or Farey path that names the rational candidate, and $D$ supplies the dyadic error, slack, and comparison ledger used to validate it.

\begin{theorem}[Dirichlet approximation finite handoff]
\label{thm:dirichlet-approximation-finite-handoff}
Every $\RealRationalApproximationUp$ or $\RealUp$ read exported by an accepted $\DirichletApproximationUp$ carrier $A=(Q,B,M,D,S,G,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ factors through the finite route
$$
Q,B,M,D,S,G \longmapsto R,E
$$
before transport, replay, provenance, or local naming can be read. Consequently the exported approximation is witnessed by a rational source row, a denominator-bound row, a displayed mediant route, a dyadic tolerance ledger, StreamName windows, and RegSeqRat readback, not by host real equality or by choosing a best representative.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an exported real-rational approximation or Real-facing read. The carrier has only the handoff row $R$ and the terminal seal row $E$ for such reads, and both occur after $Q,B,M,D,S,G$.

The source and bound rows fix the rational data. The row $Q$ supplies the visible rational source and interval endpoints, while $B$ records the finite denominator budget and its comparison evidence.

The candidate route is then displayed. The row $M$ names the Stern--Brocot or Farey mediant path, and $D$ records the dyadic tolerance and slack comparisons that certify the approximation request.

The real-facing input is read through windows. The row $S$ supplies the finite StreamName window, and $G$ converts that window into RegSeqRat rational readback before $R$ may package the handoff.

Only after these rows are present can $R$ and then $E$ be read. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming preserve this route, so the exported approximation cannot depend on host real equality, quotient representatives, choice-selected best approximants, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}
