\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RiemannianMetricUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-riemannianmetric-namecert}

The Riemannian-metric interface $\RiemannianMetricUp$ packages a smooth field of positive-definite inner products on the tangent fibres of a manifold carrier. It depends on $\NameCert_{\ManifoldUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-manifold-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\InnerProductUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-innerproduct-namecert}), and supplies the metric substrate for length, geodesic, and curvature certificates.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:riemannianmetric-carrier}

\begin{theorem}[RiemannianMetricUp source fibre carrier row]
\label{thm:riemannianmetric-source-fibre-carrier-row}
A $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$ certificate carries the following
source-fibre rows. Its base carrier is a $\ManifoldUp$ point carrier over
$\mathsf{BHist}$. For each carried point history $x$, it displays a tangent
fibre row $T_x$ over $x$. For each displayed fibre $T_x$, it displays an
$\InnerProductUp$ row
$$
g_x:T_x\times T_x\to \mathsf{BHist}
$$
on that fibre. The visible ledger records only the manifold point provenance,
the tangent-witness provenance over the point, and the fibre inner-product
provenance; it contains no length, geodesic, curvature, or bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RiemannianMetricUp.RiemannianMetricSingleton\_source\_fibre\_carrier\_row}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RiemannianMetricUp.RiemannianMetricSingletonFibreSurface\_carrier\_rows}
\begin{proof}
Read the certificate through the ten-field $\NameCert$ template. The only
outer carrier named by the template is the $\ManifoldUp$ carrier, so every
base point is a $\mathsf{BHist}$ point accepted by that source.

For a displayed point $x$, the fibre entry is a dependent row over the same
$\mathsf{BHist}$ witness. The row has no independent endpoint: its source is
the carried $\ManifoldUp$ point and its classifier is inherited through the
point row.

On each such fibre, the metric entry is exactly an $\InnerProductUp$ row. The
ledger projection of the ten-field template therefore has three visible
provenance classes: the $\ManifoldUp$ point row, the tangent row over that
point, and the $\InnerProductUp$ row on the displayed fibre.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RiemannianMetricUp classifier stability obligation]
\label{thm:riemannianmetric-classifier-stability-obligation}
For a $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$ certificate, classifier stability has
the following obligation shape. If point histories $x$ and $y$ are related by
$\hsame$ and by the $\ManifoldUp$ point classifier, then every tangent row over
$x$ transports to a tangent row over $y$. If tangent witnesses $u,v$ over $x$
transport to tangent witnesses $u',v'$ over $y$, then the $\InnerProductUp$
classifier transports the fibrewise metric endpoint
$$
g_x(u,v)
$$
to
$$
g_y(u',v').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the ten-field $\NameCert$ classifier field. Its base comparison is
the $\hsame$ comparison on $\mathsf{BHist}$ histories together with the
$\ManifoldUp$ point classifier, so the transported endpoint remains inside the
same carried point surface.

The tangent row is dependent on that transported point. Applying the
$\NameCert$ stability field moves the displayed tangent witness from the
source point to the target point without adding any classifier beyond
$\hsame$ and the $\ManifoldUp$ point classifier.

It remains to compare metric endpoints. The metric row on each fibre is an
$\InnerProductUp$ row, so its own classifier transports the endpoint through
the transported tangent witnesses and yields the displayed target endpoint.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RiemannianMetricUp.RiemannianMetricSingletonFibreSurface\_classifier\_stability}

\begin{theorem}[RiemannianMetricUp fibrewise positivity and chart compatibility obligation]
\label{thm:riemannianmetric-fibrewise-positive-compatibility-obligation}
A $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$ certificate carries the following
fibrewise metric obligations. For every displayed point $x$ and nonzero
tangent witness $u:T_x$, the fibre row has a positive-definite entry
$$
g_x(u,u)>0.
$$
For every displayed fibre, symmetry and bilinearity are inherited from the
$\InnerProductUp$ row on that fibre. For every overlap in the $\ManifoldUp$
atlas, chart-change transport carries tangent witnesses through the atlas
classifier and preserves the displayed fibre metric endpoint under the
$\InnerProductUp$ classifier. These obligations do not assert length,
geodesic, curvature, or a standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the ten-field $\NameCert$ template at a displayed $\mathsf{BHist}$
point. The point is supplied only by $\ManifoldUp$, and the tangent witness is
supplied only as a fibre row over that point.

The metric row on the fibre is an $\InnerProductUp$ row. Its positive,
symmetric, and bilinear entries give the positive-definite, symmetry, and
bilinearity obligations after restricting all arguments to the displayed
tangent fibre.

For a chart overlap, use the $\ManifoldUp$ atlas classifier to transport the
point and tangent witnesses across the overlap. Then apply the
$\InnerProductUp$ classifier on the target fibre to identify the metric
endpoint after transport.

The template has no row whose endpoint is a length functional, geodesic
equation, curvature tensor, or standard bridge. Hence those interfaces are
outside the stated obligation scope.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RiemannianMetricUp.RiemannianMetricSingletonFibreSurface\_fibrewise\_compatibility\_scope}

\begin{theorem}[RiemannianMetricUp public source-fibre NameCert surface]
\label{thm:riemannianmetric-public-source-fibre-namecert-surface}
The public exported $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$ source-fibre surface is
exactly the conjunction of the source-fibre carrier row of
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-source-fibre-carrier-row}, the classifier
stability obligation of
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-classifier-stability-obligation}, and the
fibrewise positivity and chart-compatibility obligation of
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-fibrewise-positive-compatibility-obligation}.
Downstream length, geodesic, and curvature consumers may cite this theorem as
the single $\RiemannianMetricUp$ surface, but the surface does not assert those
downstream interfaces or a standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RiemannianMetricUp.RiemannianMetricPublicSourceFibre\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Project the public $\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$ surface onto its
source-fibre carrier component. This gives exactly the manifold point row, the
tangent row over each displayed point, and the $\InnerProductUp$ metric row of
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-source-fibre-carrier-row}.

Project the same surface onto its classifier component. The ten-field roadmap
template exposes precisely the point transport, tangent-row transport, and
fibrewise metric endpoint transport recorded in
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-classifier-stability-obligation}.

Finally project the fibrewise metric obligation component. The resulting
positive-definite, symmetric, bilinear, and chart-compatible fibre rows are
exactly those of
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-fibrewise-positive-compatibility-obligation}.
Repacking these three projections under the roadmap ten-field template gives a
single exported source-fibre surface, and the absence of length, geodesic,
curvature, and bridge rows is preserved by the same repacking.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RiemannianMetricUp source-fibre standard bridge]
\label{thm:riemannianmetric-source-fibre-standard-bridge}
The public source-fibre surface of
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-public-source-fibre-namecert-surface} gives a
conservative paper-level bridge from
$\NameCert_{\RiemannianMetricUp}$ to the ordinary fibrewise metric
presentation. Forward readback exposes exactly the $\ManifoldUp$ point row,
the tangent-fibre row, and the $\InnerProductUp$ metric row of
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-source-fibre-carrier-row}. Reverse admission
accepts only such displayed rows. Classifier preservation is exactly
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-classifier-stability-obligation}, and fibre
positivity together with chart compatibility is exactly
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-fibrewise-positive-compatibility-obligation}.
The bridge exports no length, geodesic, curvature, or host metric object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the public export theorem
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-public-source-fibre-namecert-surface}. Its
carrier projection is precisely the source-fibre carrier row of
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-source-fibre-carrier-row}, so forward readback
has only the manifold point row, the tangent-fibre row over that point, and
the displayed $\InnerProductUp$ metric row.

For reverse admission, use the same public export theorem in the opposite
direction of packaging. A displayed ordinary fibrewise metric row is admitted
only when it has the three rows named by
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-source-fibre-carrier-row}; no additional row is
available in the exported surface.

The transport component is supplied by
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-classifier-stability-obligation}, while the
positive-definite and chart-compatible fibre obligations are supplied by
\autoref{thm:riemannianmetric-fibrewise-positive-compatibility-obligation}.
Composing these two obligations with the public export theorem gives the
conservative bridge, and the same boundary excludes length, geodesic,
curvature, and host metric objects.
\end{proof}
