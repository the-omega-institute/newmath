\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyTailBasisUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-tail-basis-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop--Bridges constructive Cauchy terminology and Kelley's filter-basis terminology, the $\CauchyTailBasisUp$ packet names the finite tail-basis surface that sits between tail-index requests and the L10 Real and Completion route. It refines the finite cofinal-tail filter of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finite-tail-filter-namecert}, reads finite windows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, uses dyadic tolerance ledgers from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, hands regular rational readback to \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and remains upstream of the terminal Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The packet is a basis certificate rather than a completion theorem. It gives downstream Cauchy and Real consumers one named finite route for tail-index refinement, window comparison, tolerance budgeting, and regular readback, while excluding quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.

\begin{definition}[CauchyTailBasis carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-tail-basis-carrier}
A $\CauchyTailBasisUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the tail-index basis row, listing the finite tail requests, tail refinements, and common-tail meets allowed to act as basis elements.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row read over the displayed tail-index basis.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the same tail requests and finite windows.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, carrying only the regular rational observations supported by $T,W,D$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, read only after the tail basis, finite windows, dyadic tolerances, and regular readback have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the tail-index basis, finite-window, dyadic-ledger, regular-readback, Real-seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a tail-basis request through $T,W,D,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyTailBasisUp$, $\FiniteTailFilterUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyTailBasisUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$. The carrier contains no quotient stream equality, selected limit, countable-choice selector, host real equality row, trichotomy row, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness datum, Lean verification credential, or standard bridge.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyTailBasis NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-tail-basis-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyTailBasisUp$ carrier $B=(T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyTailBasisUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed tail-index basis, finite StreamName windows, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item tail-basis locality, requiring every request to enter through a displayed element of $T$ before it can inspect $W,D,R$, or $E$;
\item finite-window exactness, requiring $W$ to expose only windows supported by the admitted tail basis;
\item tolerance-ledger exactness, requiring $D$ to record only the dyadic budgets attached to those admitted windows;
\item regular-readback attachment, requiring $R$ to reuse the same $T,W,D$ surface rather than selecting a second stream representative;
\item Real-seal boundary, requiring $E$ to remain downstream of $T,W,D,R$;
\item non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name no row from which quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, ambient Real completeness, Lean verification, or bridge credentials can be exported.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-tail-basis-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the tail-index basis, finite-window, dyadic-ledger, regular-readback, Real-seal, and structural rows.

Tail-basis locality is forced by the row order. A consumer reads $T$ before it can inspect a StreamName window, a dyadic tolerance, a regular rational readback, or a Real seal.

The window, tolerance, and readback clauses are then component projections. The row $W$ contains only finite windows supported by $T$, the row $D$ contains only budgets attached to those windows, and the row $R$ is tied to $T,W,D$ rather than to a detached representative.

The Real-seal boundary is the projection to $E$. Since $E$ is downstream of $T,W,D,R$, it cannot introduce a quotient stream, a selected limit, a choice schedule, host equality, trichotomy, or ambient completeness.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same displayed packet. These rows have no coordinate outside $T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$, so the local NameCert obligations are exhausted by the listed finite rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyTailBasis window directedness]
\label{thm:cauchy-tail-basis-window-directedness}
Let $B=(T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyTailBasisUp$ carrier. Any two finite window reads exported by $W$ and admitted over tail-basis elements of $T$ have a displayed common-tail read in $T$ whose dyadic tolerance ledger in $D$ supports both windows before the route reaches $R$ or $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with two exported finite-window reads. Since $W$ is read only after the tail-index basis row, each window has an admitted tail-basis element in $T$.

The row $T$ is a basis row, so its displayed data include finite tail refinements and common-tail meets for the admitted requests. Project the meet row supplied for the two chosen tail elements.

Read $D$ at the displayed meet. The tolerance ledger is attached to the same tail-basis and window rows, so the dyadic budgets for both windows are recorded before any regular readback or Real seal can be inspected.

The structural rows preserve this directed route. Componentwise $\hsame$ transport and displayed $\Cont$ replay may move the accepted packet along the recorded comparison, but they do not add a second window source or an external tail oracle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyTailBasis RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-tail-basis-regseqrat-handoff}
Every regular rational readback exported by an accepted $\CauchyTailBasisUp$ carrier $B=(T,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ factors through the finite route
$$
T,W,D \longmapsto R \longmapsto E
$$
before any Real-facing consumer can read the terminal seal. Thus the packet hands tail-basis Cauchy data to $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ consumers without quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real equality, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an exported regular rational readback. Carrier admission provides no analytic row before $T$, so the read first chooses an admitted finite tail-basis element.

The route next reads the finite-window row $W$ and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$. These two rows fix the observed stream entries and the tolerance budget for the admitted tail-basis element.

The regular readback row $R$ is attached to the displayed $T,W,D$ data. It therefore cannot select a second stream representative, a quotient class, a detached limit, or a countable-choice schedule.

The Real seal row $E$ is downstream of the same readback. Since the structural rows $H,C,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name this finite route, every Real-facing consumer receives the displayed handoff and no ambient completeness, trichotomy, or host equality coordinate.
\end{proof}
