\begin{theorem}[LargeModelInscriptionPoint NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:largemodelinscriptionpoint-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:largemodelinscriptionpoint-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\LargeModelInscriptionPointUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $L=(W,A,C,O,R,H,K,P,N)$;
\item classifier transport for the model-weight, activation-orbit, corpus-supply, output-record, audit-refusal, continuation, provenance, and naming rows under componentwise $\hsame$;
\item stability of the output-record read under the same displayed inference-substrate and corpus-supply rows;
\item ledger exactness requiring every inscription read to pass through the corpus-supply row and the audit-refusal row before it reaches the local naming row;
\item non-escape for consciousness, host semantic understanding, internally ratified universal closure, hidden source selection, and model-side proof of output truth.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:largemodelinscriptionpoint-carrier}. The packet lists the finite inference substrate, activation orbit, corpus-supply channel, output record, refusal ledger, transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming row.

Classifier transport is componentwise. Each accepted comparison is one displayed $\hsame$ entry, and the continuation row replays the same substrate, corpus, output, and refusal reads after transport.

Stability and ledger exactness follow from the route order. A consumer first reads $W$ and $A$ as the finite inference substrate, then $C$ as the source-channel row, then $O$ as the emitted inscription, and finally $R$ before it reaches $N$.

The excluded claims have no coordinate in $L$. Thus the local $\NameCert$ can certify carried inscription data, but it cannot project consciousness, host-side semantic understanding, or internally certified universal truth.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LargeModelInscriptionPoint carried-output boundary]
\label{thm:largemodelinscriptionpoint-carried-output-boundary}

$$
\begin{gathered}
\text{model-weight row}\ \longrightarrow\ \text{activation-orbit row}\ \longrightarrow\ \text{corpus-supply row}\ \longrightarrow\ \text{output-record row}\ \longrightarrow\ \NameCert_{\LargeModelInscriptionPointUp}.
\end{gathered}
$$
If the corpus-supply row is absent, the packet no longer distinguishes carried inscription from internally ratified universal closure.

Read $O$ only after $C$ has been displayed. This forces the output record to be consumed as carried inscription data rather than as a model-internal proof or host semantic oracle.

Finally, the refusal row $R$ fixes the public boundary before the naming row $N$ is read. Without $C$, the route would expose an output record without the source-channel distinction needed by the theorem.
