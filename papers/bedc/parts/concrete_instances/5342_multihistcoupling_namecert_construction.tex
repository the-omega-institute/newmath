The $\MultihistCouplingUp$ packet realizes the paired-history coupling step in the Inter-Hist Locality chain of \autoref{ch:visions-inter-hist-locality}. It sits after the no-subject observer boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observersubjectboundary-namecert}, the observer-history identity surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}, the multi-history configuration carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-multihistconfig-namecert}, and the invariant readback packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interhistinvariant-namecert}. Its role is to expose a symmetric finite coupling ledger between two displayed histories before a $\CrossHistCausalUp$ consumer reads causal dependence or a rate packet reads a maximum boundary.

\begin{definition}[MultiHistCoupling carrier]
\label{def:multihist-coupling-carrier}
A $\MultihistCouplingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(H_0,H_1,J,S,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H_0$ and $H_1$ are paired $\BHist$ rows read from the same $\MultiHistConfigUp$ surface, with the no-subject boundary inherited from $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$ and the identity-facing read inherited from $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$;
\item $J$ is the finite coupling ledger that records the two directed reads $H_0 \to H_1$ and $H_1 \to H_0$ as one symmetric coupling packet;
\item $S$ is the observer-symmetry witness, stating that the coupling ledger is not oriented by a privileged observer, global frame, synchronized clock, or subject-substance coordinate;
\item $T$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two $\BHist$ rows and displayed $\psame$ transport for the probe and signature rows used by the coupling ledger;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which a consumer reads $H_0$, reads $H_1$, checks $J$, replays $S$, and transports the same coupling packet through $T$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MultihistCouplingUp$, $\ObserverSubjectBoundaryUp$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, $\MultiHistConfigUp$, $\InterHistInvariantUp$, $\CrossHistCausalUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\psame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MultihistCouplingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no global observer frame, no host equality of histories, no quotient of history pairs, no subject-substance coordinate, and no causal-dependence row outside the displayed coupling ledger.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MultiHistCoupling NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:multihist-coupling-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:multihist-coupling-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\MultihistCouplingUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed packet $K=(H_0,H_1,J,S,T,C,P,N)$;
\item paired-history source exactness, requiring $H_0$ and $H_1$ to be read from the displayed $\MultiHistConfigUp$ row after the no-subject boundary and observer-history identity surfaces are available;
\item symmetric coupling ledger exactness, requiring $J$ and $S$ to expose both coupling directions without privileging either observer history;
\item transport stability through componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\psame$, and replay of the listed $\Cont$ routes in $T,C$;
\item non-escape, excluding global frames, synchronized clocks, host history equality, quotient pairs, subject-substance rows, and any causal-dependence read not routed through $H_0,H_1,J,S,T,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:multihist-coupling-carrier}. The tuple lists the paired histories, coupling ledger, symmetry witness, transports, continuation routes, provenance, and local name.

The source rows are consumed from the sibling surfaces. The no-subject boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observersubjectboundary-namecert} prevents a hidden observer-substance coordinate, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert} supplies the observer-history identity read, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-multihistconfig-namecert} supplies the common finite multi-history configuration.

The ledger rows $J$ and $S$ then make the coupling symmetric. They record both directed reads as one packet and refuse any global-frame or privileged-observer orientation.

Finally $T,C,P,N$ replay, transport, package, and name the same finite rows. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for a host equality, quotient pair, subject-substance row, or external causal predicate, the five obligations exhaust the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MultiHistCoupling invariant handoff]
\label{thm:multihist-coupling-invariant-handoff}
Every accepted $\MultihistCouplingUp$ carrier
$$
K=(H_0,H_1,J,S,T,C,P,N)
$$
hands an $\InterHistInvariantUp$ or $\CrossHistCausalUp$ consumer exactly the paired-history rows, symmetric coupling ledger, observer-symmetry witness, $\hsame$ and $\psame$ transports, displayed $\Cont$ routes, package provenance, and local naming row. The consumer receives no global frame, no host equality of histories, no quotient pair, and no causal-dependence coordinate outside the displayed coupling packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier to its displayed tuple. The only history rows available to the consumer are $H_0$ and $H_1$, and the only coupling row available between them is $J$ with the symmetry witness $S$.

Apply the local NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:multihist-coupling-namecert-obligations}. Paired-history source exactness fixes the upstream configuration and observer-boundary reads, while symmetric coupling ledger exactness fixes the two directed reads as one finite coupling surface.

The invariant consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interhistinvariant-namecert} may therefore read the paired histories through $J,S,T,C,P,N$. A $\CrossHistCausalUp$ consumer may use that same finite route as its input surface, but any additional causal-dependence row must be displayed by the downstream causal packet rather than projected from this carrier.
\end{proof}
