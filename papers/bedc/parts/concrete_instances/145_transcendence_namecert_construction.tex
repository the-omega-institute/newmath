\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TranscendenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-transcendence-namecert}

The transcendence interface $\TranscendenceUp$ packages the supremum of cardinalities of algebraically independent subsets of a field extension. It depends on $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fieldext-namecert}).

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:transcendence-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Transcendence BHist carrier]\label{def:transcendence-bhist-carrier}
A $\TranscendenceUp$ carrier packet is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet over
the $\FieldExtUp$ source rows. Its displayed rows consist of a certified field
extension packet, a finite family row $S=(s_1,\ldots,s_n)$ in the extension
endpoint, a base-field coefficient ledger, a finite list of polynomial-relation
test rows for the family, $\hsame$ transports for the field-extension and family
rows, $\Cont$ readbacks for each test row, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance pointing
back to the $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ source packet.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.TranscendenceUp.TranscendenceCarrierPacket}

\begin{definition}[Transcendence classifier]\label{def:transcendence-classifier}
Two $\TranscendenceUp$ carrier packets are classified as the same when their
$\FieldExtUp$ source packets are matched by the displayed $\FieldExtUp$
classifier, their finite family rows are paired coordinatewise by $\hsame$, and
their polynomial-relation test ledgers have the same finite readback through
$\Cont$. The classifier compares only the listed rows; it does not compare
supremum cardinalities, quotient fields, chosen transcendence bases, or maximal
independent subsets.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.TranscendenceUp.TranscendenceCarrierPacket\_classifier}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.TranscendenceUp.TranscendenceClassifier}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.TranscendenceUp.TranscendenceCarrierPacketClassifier}

\section{The certificate}
\label{sec:transcendence-certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Transcendence algebraic-independence ledger]\label{thm:transcendence-algebraic-independence-ledger}
For a $\TranscendenceUp$ carrier packet, the finite family row $S$ is accepted
as algebraically independent over the displayed $\FieldExtUp$ base exactly when
every polynomial-relation test row in the packet reads through $\Cont$ as the
zero relation only at the zero coefficient ledger. This certificate is a finite
ledger statement over the displayed family and coefficient rows, not a statement
about the supremum of all independent cardinalities.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TranscendenceUp.TranscendenceCarrierPacket\_algebraic\_independence\_ledger}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier of
\autoref{def:transcendence-bhist-carrier}. It supplies a finite family row, a
finite coefficient ledger, and a finite list of polynomial-relation test rows,
all sourced from the same $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ packet.

For each displayed test row, the $\Cont$ readback records the evaluated
relation endpoint. Ledger exactness says that the relation endpoint is zero
only when the coefficient ledger is the zero ledger, so each listed polynomial
test witnesses the independence condition for the finite family.

The converse uses the same finite rows. If the packet records the zero-only
outcome for every displayed polynomial-relation test, repacking those readbacks
gives the algebraic-independence ledger for $S$. No step selects a basis,
maximizes a family, forms a quotient field, or invokes a Zorn-style argument.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Transcendence FieldExt source boundary]\label{thm:transcendence-fieldext-source-boundary}
Every source read used by a $\TranscendenceUp$ carrier packet factors through
the displayed $\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ rows: base-field endpoint,
extension-field endpoint, inclusion row, field-operation rows, finite family
membership rows, polynomial coefficient rows, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$
readbacks, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. The boundary exports no ambient
quotient-field construction, algebraic closure, transcendence basis choice, or
maximality principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TranscendenceUp.TranscendenceCarrierPacket\_fieldext\_source\_transport\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Start from the source component of
\autoref{def:transcendence-bhist-carrier}. Each row is tagged by the
$\NameCert_{\FieldExtUp}$ provenance, so the base endpoint, extension endpoint,
inclusion row, and field-operation rows are inherited as finite displayed
source data.

The family and polynomial-test rows consume only those inherited endpoints and
the finite coefficient ledger. Their stability is read by the $\hsame$
transports named in the carrier, and their observations are returned by the
listed $\Cont$ rows.

Constructor inspection of the source boundary leaves no slot for a quotient
field, algebraic closure, chosen transcendence basis, or maximality witness.
Thus downstream $\TranscendenceUp$ consumers receive only the displayed
$\FieldExtUp$ packet and the finite independence ledger.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TranscendenceUp.TranscendenceCarrierPacket\_fieldext\_source\_boundary}

\begin{theorem}[Transcendence NameCert obligation surface]\label{thm:transcendence-namecert-obligation-surface}
The carrier of \autoref{def:transcendence-bhist-carrier} and the classifier of
\autoref{def:transcendence-classifier} determine the $\NameCert$ obligation
surface for $\TranscendenceUp$: carrier membership, classifier reflexivity,
classifier symmetry, classifier transitivity, and carrier stability under the
classifier are all witnessed by the finite $\FieldExtUp$ source packet,
coordinatewise $\hsame$ transports, polynomial-test ledger exactness, $\Cont$
readbacks, and $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TranscendenceUp.TranscendenceCarrierPacket\_namecert\_obligation\_surface}
\begin{proof}
Carrier membership is the finite packet described in
\autoref{def:transcendence-bhist-carrier}. The family row, coefficient ledger,
polynomial tests, transports, readbacks, and provenance are all explicit rows,
so no extra host-level algebraic object is needed to admit the packet.

Reflexivity uses reflexive $\hsame$ rows for the displayed $\FieldExtUp$ source
and each family coordinate, together with identical polynomial-test readbacks.
Symmetry reverses the same coordinatewise transports. Transitivity composes the
source and family transports and then reuses the finite ledger exactness rows
for the polynomial tests.

For carrier stability, transport the $\FieldExtUp$ packet and finite family row
through the classifier, then carry each polynomial-relation test row along the
named $\hsame$ transport. The $\Cont$ readbacks preserve the zero-only
coefficient ledger, so the transported packet is again accepted by the
transcendence carrier. The resulting surface is the displayed
$\NameCert_{\TranscendenceUp}$ obligation packet, with no appeal to supremum
cardinalities, quotient fields, basis choice, or maximality.
\end{proof}
