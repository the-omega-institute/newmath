It sits after the witness ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-witness-ledger-namecert}, the tail-witness envelope of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-tail-witness-envelope-namecert}, the seal-budget synchronizer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-seal-budget-synchronizer-namecert}, and the completion-seal classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-completion-seal-classifier-namecert}. Those sibling packets expose the witness, tail, synchronizer, and completion-seal rows separately; this packet glues them into the single consumer surface through which a Real-completion reader may reach $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[CauchyWitnessGluing carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-witness-gluing-carrier}
A $\CauchyWitnessGluingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(L,T,S,K,U,V,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ row recording the diagonal budget, observation-budget, seal-synchronizer, and completion-seal witness ledger;
\item $T$ is the $\CauchyTailWitnessEnvelopeUp$ row recording the finite tail envelope and its Real-facing tail agreement route;
\item $S$ is the $\CauchySealBudgetSynchronizerUp$ row aligning the selector budget, observation budget, shared threshold, and seal request;
\item $K$ is the $\RealCompletionSealClassifierUp$ row classifying the completion-seal read visible to the Real consumer;
\item $U$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window-family row exposed by the glued route;
\item $V$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-sequence handoff row reached after the stream windows are read;
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ dyadic tolerance ledger consumed by the stream and regular-sequence rows;
\item $R$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row served to the completion-facing consumer;
\item $E$ is the Real-completion witness edge stating that $L,T,S,K,U,V,D,R$ are read as one gluing packet;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for every displayed row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes from the ledger to the tail envelope, from the synchronizer to the completion-seal classifier, from stream and dyadic reads to the regular handoff, and from the regular handoff to $\RealUp$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-witness-ledger-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-tail-witness-envelope-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-seal-budget-synchronizer-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-completion-seal-classifier-namecert}, \autoref{ch:visions-real-completeness}, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyWitnessGluingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected external limit, countable-choice row, detached Cauchy witness, or ambient completeness principle beyond the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyWitnessGluing budget compatibility]
\label{thm:cauchy-witness-gluing-budget-compatibility}
Let
$$
G=(L,T,S,K,U,V,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CauchyWitnessGluingUp$ packet. Any Real-completion consumer that preserves the witness ledger $L$, tail envelope $T$, seal synchronizer $S$, completion-seal classifier $K$, stream window row $U$, regular handoff $V$, dyadic ledger $D$, Real seal $R$, witness edge $E$, transports $H$, continuations $C$, provenance $P$, and local name $N$ preserves the same finite budget route. Replacing the synchronizer row while keeping the ledger and envelope fixed is not a refinement of this packet; it is a different candidate for acceptance.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the witness ledger. The row $L$ records which diagonal, observation-budget, synchronizer, and completion-seal witnesses are present before the Real-facing consumer reads the packet.

Next attach the tail and seal rows. The envelope $T$ supplies the finite tail request seen by the Real route, while $S$ aligns that request with the selected observation budget and shared threshold. The classifier $K$ then reads the completion-seal side of the same synchronized route.

The lower observation rows cannot be varied independently. The stream windows $U$, dyadic ledger $D$, and regular handoff $V$ are routed together by the displayed $\Cont$ row, and their componentwise $\hsame$ transports are recorded in $H$ before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $R$ is served.

Thus a consumer preserving all displayed rows preserves the budget route as a whole. Changing $S$ changes the coordinate that binds the ledger to the completion-seal classifier, so the resulting data may form another accepted packet, but it is not the same $\CauchyWitnessGluingUp$ carrier read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyWitnessGluing witness ledger exactness]
\label{thm:cauchy-witness-gluing-witness-ledger-exactness}
In an accepted $\CauchyWitnessGluingUp$ packet, the witness ledger row $L$ is exact only together with the tail envelope $T$, seal synchronizer $S$, completion classifier $K$, and witness edge $E$. The ledger read is therefore the $\CauchyWitnessLedgerUp$ certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-witness-ledger-namecert} synchronized through the seal-budget route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-seal-budget-synchronizer-namecert}, not a detached witness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The row $L$ supplies the diagonal, observation-budget, synchronizer, and completion-seal witness ledger, but the gluing carrier does not expose $L$ as an independent terminal object. Its exactness is tested inside the same packet that contains $T,S,K$, and $E$.

The tail envelope $T$ determines the Real-facing tail request, while $S$ aligns that request with the shared budget and seal request. The classifier $K$ reads the completion-seal side of the synchronized route, and the edge $E$ states that these rows are consumed as one gluing packet.

Thus a consumer that keeps $L$ while dropping any of $T,S,K$, or $E$ no longer reads the displayed $\CauchyWitnessGluingUp$ surface. It reads a detached ledger candidate, which is outside the accepted packet described here.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

The continuation row $C$ then records the displayed path from stream and dyadic reads to the regular handoff and from the regular handoff to the terminal Real seal.

Therefore any tail-envelope handoff to $R$ factors through those finite BEDC rows. A direct appeal to Real completeness or a hidden quotient-stream map would bypass $U,D,V$, and so would not be a read of this gluing packet.
