\begin{definition}[Frechet space carrier]
\label{def:frechet-space-carrier}
A $\FrechetSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(V,L,M,C,Q,H,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $V$ is the $\TopVecSpaceUp$ source row, exposing the vector carrier, topology, addition-continuity ledger, and scalar-continuity ledger.
\item $L$ is the $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$ row, recording the finite seminorm and convex balanced neighbourhood evidence consumed by local convexity reads.
\item $M$ is the compatible $\MetricUp$ row, recording the finite distance witnesses and the topology-compatibility comparison used by metrizable consumers.
\item $C$ is the completeness ledger, read through Cauchy windows, Cauchy-filter or Cauchy-net evidence, and displayed convergence rows without selecting a completed point.
\item $Q$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff row, used only to expose the completion-facing boundary of the compatible metric.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $V,L,M,C,Q$ and for the replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $R$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the topological-vector source, locally convex source, compatible metric, completeness ledger, metric-completion handoff, transport, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FrechetSpaceUp$, $\TopVecSpaceUp$, $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FrechetSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $V,L,M,C,Q,H,R,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host Frechet space, an external complete metric, a selected translation-invariant metric, quotient completion equality, countable choice, or an infinite family of seminorms.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Frechet space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:frechet-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\FrechetSpaceUp$ carrier $F=(V,L,M,C,Q,H,R,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\FrechetSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of topological-vector source exposure through $V$; locally convex source exposure through $L$; compatible metric exposure through $M$; completeness evidence through $C$; metric-completion handoff through $Q$; componentwise transport through $H$; displayed replay through $R$; provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:frechet-space-carrier}. Its mathematical rows are the topological-vector source $V$, the locally convex row $L$, the compatible metric row $M$, the completeness ledger $C$, and the metric-completion handoff $Q$.

The topological and locally convex rows are consumed before the metric row. Thus a Frechet-space consumer first reads vector operations, topology, continuity, seminorm probes, and convex balanced neighbourhood evidence through the already named $\TopVecSpaceUp$ and $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$ surfaces.

The compatible metric and completeness rows then give the metrizable complete handoff. The row $M$ supplies only displayed distance and topology-compatibility evidence, while $C$ records Cauchy and convergence checks without adding a selected completion point.

Finally $Q,H,R,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier contains no host Frechet object, selected metric, quotient-completion equality, countable-choice schedule, or infinite seminorm enumeration, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Frechet space metrizable complete handoff]
\label{thm:frechet-space-metrizable-complete-handoff}
Let $F=(V,L,M,C,Q,H,R,P,N)$ be an accepted $\FrechetSpaceUp$ carrier. Every downstream functional-analysis, completion, or locally convex consumer that asks for metrizability and completeness must factor through
$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a downstream request for a Frechet-space read. The vector and topology data are not independent host structure; they are obtained from the $\TopVecSpaceUp$ row $V$.

The structural rows $H,R,P,N$ preserve that same route under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Therefore a consumer receives a metrizable complete locally convex carrier only as the displayed finite packet above, not as a host Frechet space, selected metric, quotient completion, choice principle, or infinite seminorm family.
