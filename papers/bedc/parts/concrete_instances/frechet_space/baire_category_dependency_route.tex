\begin{theorem}[Frechet Baire dependency route]
\label{thm:frechet-space-baire-dependency-route}
Every accepted $\FrechetSpaceUp$ read that asks for Baire-category evidence factors first through the complete metric row $C$ and the $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff $Q$ of \autoref{def:frechet-space-carrier}, and then through the $\BaireCategoryTheoremUp$ and $\CompleteMetricBaireSurfaceUp$ chapters cited above. The route adds no Hahn--Banach separation, selected generic point, countable-choice schedule, host intersection object, or ambient complete locally convex space to the Frechet carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the Frechet carrier as $F=(V,L,M,C,Q,H,R,P,N)$. The rows $V,L,M,C,Q$ expose topological-vector structure, local convexity, compatible metric data, completeness evidence, and the metric-completion boundary before any theorem consumer reads the packet.

A Baire-category request is not one of those Frechet rows. Route it through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complete-metric-baire-surface-namecert} for the inspected complete-metric Baire surface and through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-baire-category-theorem-namecert} for the theorem-level dense-open package.

The remaining rows $H,R,P,N$ only transport, replay, source, and name the displayed route. Since no row stores Hahn--Banach separation, a selected generic point, a countable-choice schedule, a host intersection object, or an ambient complete locally convex space, none can be exported by the dependency route.
\end{proof}
