\begin{definition}[Large-model output verifier carrier]
\label{def:large-model-output-verifier-carrier}
A $\LargeModelOutputVerifierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
V=(H,A,M,T,I,Q,C,F,R,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H$ is the $\VerifiedOutputHarnessUp$ row carrying the checker-facing accepted-output harness;
\item $A$ is the $\LargeModelAuditChannelUp$ row carrying the consumer-facing audit channel;
\item $M$ is the $\ModelTraceUp$ row recording the finite inference-output trace;
\item $T$ is the $\PromptResponseTraceUp$ row recording the prompt and response boundary;
\item $I$ is the $\LargeModelInscriptionAuditUp$ row binding the output to inscription-audit provenance;
\item $Q$ is the verifier row naming the external checker, checker input, and displayed checker result;
\item $C$ is the proof-check row recording the proof object, proof checker, or finite validation transcript consumed by $Q$;
\item $F$ is the refusal row recording failed verification, unavailable evidence, policy refusal, or obstruction disclosure;
\item $R$ is the route row through which an accepted output is exported to a public consumer;
\item $K$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay through $H,A,M,T,I,Q,C,F,R$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\VerifiedOutputHarnessUp$, $\LargeModelAuditChannelUp$, $\ModelTraceUp$, $\PromptResponseTraceUp$, $\LargeModelInscriptionAuditUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LargeModelOutputVerifierUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for hidden model truth, private verifier correctness, semantic omniscience, or output export without the verifier and proof-check rows.

\begin{theorem}[Large-model output verifier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:large-model-output-verifier-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:large-model-output-verifier-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\LargeModelOutputVerifierUp}$ surface consists exactly of six obligation rows: carrier admission for $V=(H,A,M,T,I,Q,C,F,R,K,P,N)$; sibling exposure through $H,A,M,T,I$; verifier and proof-check exposure through $Q,C$; refusal and route exactness through $F,R$; replay and provenance exactness through $K,P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:large-model-output-verifier-carrier}. The admitted packet displays the five sibling rows $H,A,M,T,I$ together with verifier, proof-check, refusal, route, replay, provenance, and local-name rows.

The sibling exposure row binds the accepted output to the harness, audit channel, model trace, prompt-response trace, and inscription audit. These are exactly the finite surfaces supplied by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-verifiedoutputharness-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-largemodelauditchannel-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-modeltrace-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-prompt-response-trace-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-largemodelinscriptionaudit-namecert}.

The verifier row $Q$ and proof-check row $C$ are then read before route export. Refusal $F$ remains visible whether verification succeeds or blocks, so the packet cannot erase failed checking as if it were accepted evidence.

Finally $K$, $P$, and $N$ replay the same displayed rows as continuation, package provenance, and local naming. This gives exactly the six obligation rows and no hidden checker or model-truth coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Large-model output verifier public acceptance boundary]
\label{thm:large-model-output-verifier-public-acceptance-boundary}
Every public export from an accepted $\LargeModelOutputVerifierUp$ packet must pass through the verifier row $Q$ and proof-check row $C$ after the sibling rows $H,A,M,T,I$ and before the route row $R$. A route that exports the output without $Q$ and $C$ is not a $\LargeModelOutputVerifierUp$ acceptance route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:large-model-output-verifier-namecert-obligations}. They require sibling exposure, verifier exposure, proof-check exposure, refusal exposure, route exactness, replay, provenance, and local naming before a public output is exported.

The route row $R$ is therefore downstream of both $Q$ and $C$. If either row is absent, the route still may describe an emitted model output, but it does not describe an externally checked acceptance packet.

Because the carrier has no coordinate for private verifier correctness or hidden semantic truth, no alternative row can replace $Q$ and $C$. Public acceptance is exactly the displayed verifier/proof-check route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Large-model output verifier ledger non-escape]
\label{thm:large-model-output-verifier-ledger-nonescape}
Let
$$
V=(H,A,M,T,I,Q,C,F,R,K,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\LargeModelOutputVerifierUp$ packet. Any consumer that reads the exported model output through $R$ must also read the sibling rows $H,A,M,T,I$, the verifier and proof-check rows $Q,C$, the refusal row $F$, the replay row $K$, the provenance row $P$, and the local name $N$. Omitting one of these rows exits the verifier packet rather than exporting an accepted output.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:large-model-output-verifier-namecert-obligations}. The local NameCert surface lists all rows required for carrier admission and public export.

The proof-check row cannot be detached from the verifier row, since $C$ records the finite checking transcript consumed by $Q$. The refusal row cannot be detached from the route, since failed or unavailable evidence must remain visible at the same boundary.

Replay and provenance then bind the consumer route to the displayed packet. Thus an export that omits a sibling row, verifier row, proof-check row, refusal row, replay row, provenance row, or local name is not an accepted $\LargeModelOutputVerifierUp$ export.
\end{proof}
