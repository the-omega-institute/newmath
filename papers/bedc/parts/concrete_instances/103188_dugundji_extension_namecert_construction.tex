\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DugundjiExtensionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dugundji-extension-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Dugundji's extension theorem and the Kelley--Bourbaki topology tradition, the $\DugundjiExtensionUp$ packet records only a finite extension-operator certificate: it names the route by which a closed metric subspace map into a convex target is extended through displayed affine coefficients, not a host-level selection of covers or functions. Its normality side is anchored in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normalspace-namecert}, its real-valued seed in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-tietze-extension-namecert}, its exported map surface in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, its closed-domain exposure in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-subspace-namecert}, its affine target in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-convexset-namecert}, its scalar seal in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and its componentwise linear handoff in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}. The packet therefore marks the L10 extension-operator boundary where a Tietze-style real extension is repacked as convex-valued continuous extension data while remaining inside finite $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

\begin{definition}[Dugundji extension carrier]
\label{def:dugundji-extension-carrier}
A $\DugundjiExtensionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(A,S,V,T,L,G,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the ambient metric/topology source row. It records the $\NormalSpaceUp$ and metric-topology admission data through which neighborhoods, continuity tests, and closed-set exposure are read.
\item $S$ is the closed subspace row. It records the $\MetricSubspaceUp$ inclusion, the closed-domain classifier, and the local restriction route for the partial map being extended.
\item $V$ is the convex target row. It records the $\ConvexSetUp$ carrier, the compatible $\VecSpaceUp$ affine-combination surface, and the target classifier used by convex-valued reads.
\item $T$ is the Tietze seed map row. It records the real-valued $\TietzeExtensionUp$ route used to obtain scalar coordinate extensions before any convex-valued graph is exposed.
\item $L$ is the local affine-coefficient ledger. It records the finite coefficient rows, index support, scalar $\RealUp$ seals, convex-sum budget, and compatibility with the target row $V$.
\item $G$ is the extension graph row. It records the continuous graph read from ambient points through the coefficient ledger $L$ and the convex target row $V$.
\item $R$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row. It records the scalar readback used by the Tietze seed and by every displayed affine coefficient.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the ambient source, closed subspace, convex target, Tietze seed, affine-coefficient ledger, extension graph, real seal, replay, provenance, and local name.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of every consumer route through
$$
A,S,V,T,L,R \longmapsto G .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DugundjiExtensionUp$, $\NormalSpaceUp$, $\TietzeExtensionUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\MetricSubspaceUp$, $\ConvexSetUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DugundjiExtensionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $A,S,V,T,L,G,R,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host choice function, a selected locally finite cover, function extensionality, quotient map equality, open-cover compactness, or an ambient extension theorem outside the displayed packet.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dugundji extension NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dugundji-extension-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\DugundjiExtensionUp$ carrier
$$
D=(A,S,V,T,L,G,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\DugundjiExtensionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the ambient metric/topology source, closed subspace, convex target, Tietze seed map, affine-coefficient ledger, extension graph, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item closed-subspace exposure, requiring every restriction read to pass through the $\MetricSubspaceUp$ row $S$ and the ambient source row $A$ before it can compare the original partial map with the extension graph;
\item Tietze seed compatibility, requiring scalar reads in $T$ and $R$ to be the only real-valued extension data consumed by the affine-coefficient ledger $L$;
\item convex affine-combination handoff, requiring every value read of $G$ to be assembled through the $\ConvexSetUp$ and $\VecSpaceUp$ rows in $V$ using the displayed coefficient support in $L$;
\item continuous-map export, requiring the exported extension to pass through the $\ContinuousMapUp$ surface named by $G$ and replayed by $C$;
\item non-escape from host choice and function extensionality, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed finite rows and to export no selected cover, host choice function, function-extensional equality, quotient map equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient extension theorem.
\end{enumerate}
No Dugundji extension NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:dugundji-extension-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the ambient source row, closed subspace row, convex target row, Tietze seed row, affine-coefficient ledger, extension graph, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Closed-subspace exposure follows from the order of the carrier rows. The original partial map can be compared with the extension graph only by reading $S$ inside $A$, so no unrecorded host subset or external restriction operator can be inserted.

Tietze seed compatibility is confined to $T$ and $R$. These rows provide the scalar extension and scalar seals consumed by $L$, and the coefficient ledger has no other real-valued input coordinate.

For the affine handoff, the target row $V$ supplies the convex and vector-space operations, while $L$ supplies the finite coefficient support and scalar budget. The graph row $G$ is exposed only after those rows have been displayed.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same displayed route. Since the packet contains no coordinate for a selected cover, host choice function, function-extensional equality, quotient map equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient extension theorem, none of those principles can escape through the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dugundji extension affine handoff]
\label{thm:dugundji-extension-affine-handoff}
Let
$$
D=(A,S,V,T,L,G,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\DugundjiExtensionUp$ carrier. Every extension read exported by $D$ factors through the displayed route
$$
\NormalSpaceUp \longrightarrow \TietzeExtensionUp \longrightarrow \ConvexSetUp/\VecSpaceUp \longrightarrow \ContinuousMapUp \longrightarrow \RealUp
$$
before the local $\NameCert_{\DugundjiExtensionUp}$ row is exposed. Equivalently, every consumer of the extension graph first reads the normal ambient source $A$, the closed subspace $S$, the Tietze seed $T$, the convex and vector-space target $V$, the affine ledger $L$, and the real seal $R$, then reaches $G,C,P,N$. The exported data includes no host choice function, no function-extensional equality, no selected cover, no quotient map equality, and no open-cover compactness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The ambient and closed-domain side is $A$ together with $S$, so the route begins with the normal metric/topology source and its displayed closed subspace.

The scalar seed is $T$ together with the real seal $R$. Thus any coefficient read entering the affine ledger is already a displayed Tietze-style scalar extension read and not a host-level real-valued function.

The target handoff is $V$ followed by $L$. The row $V$ supplies the $\ConvexSetUp$ and $\VecSpaceUp$ operations, while $L$ supplies the finite coefficient support and the convex-sum budget used to assemble the value.

Only after these rows are displayed does the graph row $G$ export a $\ContinuousMapUp$ read. Componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and the local row $N$ preserve this order, so every extension read reaches the local NameCert through the stated factorization and through no excluded host principle.
\end{proof}
