\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\PrincipalFilterUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-principal-filter-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bourbaki and Kelley on filters, the $\PrincipalFilterUp$ packet records the principal filter generated by one displayed finite base packet. It sits after the finite filter-base surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-filterbase-namecert}, beside the cofinite route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-frechetfilter-namecert}, and before completion-facing consumers read the Cauchy-filter boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyfilter-namecert} or the metric-completion route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}. The packet is a finite human-origin base case for L10 filter completion routes; it is not quotient filter equality, an ultrafilter principle, a selected limit, countable choice, ambient completion, or $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[PrincipalFilter carrier]
\label{def:principal-filter-carrier}
A $\PrincipalFilterUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(G,U,M,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $G$ is the generator packet. It records the single displayed base element and the finite membership witnesses saying which reads contain it.
\item $U$ is the upward-closure row. It records each accepted superset read only by displaying the generator containment already carried by $G$.
\item $M$ is the finite meet and refinement row. It records that two accepted reads refine back to the same generator packet, so their common read stays inside the displayed finite base.
\item $R$ is the $\CauchyFilterUp$ anchor row. It exposes the principal-filter data only as a finite anchored base for completion-facing consumers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the generator, upward-closure, finite meet, Cauchy-filter anchor, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
G \longmapsto U,M \longmapsto R .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PrincipalFilterUp$, $\FilterBaseUp$, $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\PrincipalFilterUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these eight rows. Its classifier compares accepted packets only through $G,U,M,R,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for quotient filter equality, ultrafilters, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completion, $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[PrincipalFilter NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:principal-filter-namecert-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\PrincipalFilterUp$ carrier
$$
F=(G,U,M,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\PrincipalFilterUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the generator packet, upward-closure row, finite meet and refinement row, Cauchy-filter anchor row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item upward closure, requiring each accepted read to display containment of the same generator packet;
\item finite-meet closure, requiring two accepted reads to refine through the displayed generator into an accepted common read;
\item generator stability, requiring componentwise $\hsame$ transport to preserve the generator row before any downstream row is read;
\item handoff, requiring every CauchyFilter or MetricCompletion consumer to receive only the finite anchored base row $R$;
\item ledger refusal, excluding quotient filter equality, ultrafilters, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completion, and $\RealUp$ completeness from the carrier inventory.
\end{enumerate}
No principal-filter NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $F=(G,U,M,R,H,C,P,N)$. Carrier admission is exactly the display of these eight rows, so no host filter object is introduced.

The upward-closure row reads every accepted superset through the generator packet $G$. The finite-meet row then uses the same generator as the common displayed refinement for any two accepted reads.

Generator stability is componentwise. The $\hsame$ row first transports $G$ and then transports $U,M,R,C,P,N$ over the same generator coordinate.

The handoff row is $R$. It is downstream of $G,U,M$ and therefore exposes the principal filter only as a finite anchored base for CauchyFilter and MetricCompletion consumers.

Finally the carrier has no row for quotient filters, ultrafilters, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completion, or real completeness. The ledger refusal follows by inspection of the displayed inventory.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrincipalFilter refinement handoff]
\label{thm:principal-filter-filterbase-refinement-handoff}
Every accepted $\PrincipalFilterUp$ read factors through the displayed generator packet into the $\FilterBaseUp$ finite directed-base surface. The exported route is
$$
G \longmapsto U,M \longmapsto R \longmapsto H,C,P,N ,
$$
where $G$ supplies the single base element, $U$ supplies upward closure, $M$ supplies finite common refinement through that same generator, and $R$ supplies the CauchyFilter anchor. Thus the handoff is a finite FilterBase refinement route, not quotient filter equality, not an ultrafilter, not a selected limit, and not countable choice.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted read. By carrier admission it has a generator row $G$, and every upward read in $U$ records containment of that generator.

For two accepted reads, the finite-meet row $M$ returns to the same generator packet. Hence the common refinement is already displayed inside the finite $\FilterBaseUp$ surface, rather than supplied by a host filter equality.

The anchor row $R$ exports this directed-base data to the CauchyFilter side. The transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows preserve exactly the same route, so no consumer can bypass the displayed generator.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[PrincipalFilter completion anchor]
\label{thm:principal-filter-cauchyfilter-anchor}
Every CauchyFilter or MetricCompletion consumer of an accepted $\PrincipalFilterUp$ carrier receives the packet only as a finite anchored base:
$$
G,U,M \longmapsto R \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer may read generator containment, upward closure, finite common refinement, the CauchyFilter anchor, componentwise transport, displayed replay, package provenance, and local naming. It receives no quotient filter equality, ultrafilter extension, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, ambient completion, or $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a completion-facing consumer read the packet. The route first displays $G,U,M$, so the consumer sees the single generator, all upward reads, and the finite common-refinement evidence before any CauchyFilter anchor is available.

The row $R$ is the only completion-facing anchor. It is therefore a finite base handoff to CauchyFilter and MetricCompletion consumers, not a quotient-filter carrier or a selected completion point.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name that same route. Since the carrier inventory contains no quotient equality, ultrafilter, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, ambient completion, or real-completeness row, the consumer cannot extract any of those principles.
\end{proof}
