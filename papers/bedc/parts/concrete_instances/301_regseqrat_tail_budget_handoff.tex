\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-budget meet handoff]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-meet-handoff}
Let $P$ be an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ carrier and let a tail-budget consumer of
$P$ request a completion-facing read. The consumer first meets the displayed
$\StreamNameUp$ pointwise schedule from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, then reads the matching
$\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint, radius, and distance row from
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, then passes through the
regular-rational classifier rows of $P$, and only then reaches the $\RealUp$
seal row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Thus every
tail-budget read factors through one regular-Cauchy route before any
completion-facing surface is visible.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the L10 source lattice pointer of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-l10-source-lattice-pointer}. It places the accepted
regular-rational packet on the displayed route
$\StreamNameUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow
\RealUp$.

Next apply the common dyadic tail ledger of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-common-dyadic-tail-ledger}. A tail-budget consumer must
read the cofinal finite ledger consisting of the stream schedule, dyadic
endpoint and radius rows, distance rows, regularity rows, classifier rows,
transport rows, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming.

Finally use the exact handoff of
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-exactness}. The
completion-facing read is therefore downstream of the same finite
regular-Cauchy route and cannot bypass the StreamName schedule, the
DyadicRatCore tolerance row, or the RealUp seal row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat StreamName dyadic source lock]
\label{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-source-lock}
For an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet, the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ pointwise
name and the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row are locked to the same
finite source. Any two consumers that present the same tail budget, the same
stream schedule row, and the same dyadic endpoint and radius ledger must read
the same regular-rational classifier route before reaching a $\RealUp$ seal.
No separate quotient stream, host real equality, selected limit, ambient
completion datum, or second dyadic source can be inserted between those reads.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-real-handoff-single-source-lock}.
It identifies public seal reads through displayed StreamName schedules and
DyadicRatCore ledgers as reads of one finite source route.

Then apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-l10-source-lattice-pointer}. The source route
already contains the schedule rows, endpoint rows, radius rows, distance rows,
classifier rows, regularity rows, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

The same route is the input to the RealUp handoff by
\autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-handoff}. Since the carrier exposes no row for
the excluded quotient, host equality, selected limit, completion datum, or
second dyadic source, the two consumers remain locked to the displayed
StreamName and DyadicRatCore source.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat Real seal consumer factorization]
\label{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-consumer-factorization}
Every completion-facing $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet
factors through the three visible rows named by the regular-Cauchy route:
the $\StreamNameUp$ pointwise schedule row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance
row, and the $\RealUp$ seal row that consumes the regular-rational classifier
surface. The factorization preserves the displayed $\hsame$ comparisons,
$\Cont$ routes, $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row, and it
has no route to a hidden completion carrier or an ambient completeness
principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-meet-handoff}. It orders every
tail-budget read through StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and RealUp
before any completion-facing surface is visible.

Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-source-lock}. The StreamName
schedule and DyadicRatCore tolerance row cannot be separated into two sources
once the accepted regular-rational packet is fixed.

Finally use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-public-handoff-determinacy}. The
public RealUp consumer is determined by the finite regular-rational source
surface and its displayed transport, continuation, package, and naming rows.
Thus the consumer factorization is exactly the regular-Cauchy route stated
above, with no hidden completion or ambient completeness read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RegSeqRat tail-budget Real seal nonescape]
\label{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-real-seal-nonescape}
A completion-facing $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RegSeqRatUp$ packet
cannot bypass the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$
tolerance row, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ classifier surface, or the displayed
$\RealUp$ seal row at the tail-budget boundary. Every such consumer factors
through the same regular-Cauchy route with its displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$,
$\mathsf{Pkg}$, and local $\NameCert$ rows.

The boundary has no hidden completion carrier, selected limit, quotient stream,
ambient completeness principle, or second dyadic source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-tail-budget-meet-handoff}. It orders the
completion-facing read through the StreamName schedule, DyadicRatCore tolerance
row, RegSeqRat classifier rows, and RealUp seal row before any completion-facing
surface is visible.

Next apply \autoref{thm:regseqrat-streamname-dyadic-source-lock}. The stream
schedule and dyadic tolerance row are locked to one finite source attached to
the accepted regular-rational packet.

Finally use \autoref{thm:regseqrat-real-seal-consumer-factorization}. It
identifies the completion-facing consumer with the displayed regular-Cauchy
route and its transport, continuation, package, and naming rows. Since that
route has no coordinate for a hidden completion carrier, selected limit,
quotient stream, ambient completeness principle, or second dyadic source, the
consumer cannot escape the stated boundary.
\end{proof}
