\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\FastCauchySeqUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-fastcauchyseq-namecert}

The $\FastCauchySeqUp$ interface names explicit-rate Cauchy sequence packets over dyadic-rational observations. Bishop and Bridges treat a displayed Cauchy rate as constructive data; the BEDC packet records the finite ledger version of that data, connecting $\CauchyModulusUp$ to $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ without quotient completion or a selected limit.

\section{Explicit-rate carrier and classifier}
\label{sec:fastcauchyseq-explicit-rate-carrier}

\begin{definition}[FastCauchySeq carrier]
\label{def:fastcauchyseq-carrier}
A $\FastCauchySeqUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
F=(s,\mu,E,R,L,T,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $s$ is a scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ dyadic-rational observation stream;
\item $\mu$ is a $\CauchyModulusUp$ precision-threshold table, displaying for each requested precision row a finite index bound after which the stream is required to be close;
\item $E$ lists the dyadic endpoint and dyadic radius rows read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ at the visible precisions;
\item $R$ is the late-pair comparison ledger recording that any two displayed observations beyond the threshold named by $\mu$ fall inside the displayed dyadic radius row for that precision;
\item $L$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the stream, threshold, endpoint, radius, and comparison rows;
\item $T$ records the displayed $\Cont$ windows through which the finite reads of $s$, $\mu$, $E$, and $R$ are consumed;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NatUp$, $\RatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\FastCauchySeqUp}$ row for this packet.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is the finite explicit-rate sequence surface only. It contains no completed real, no quotient equality of Cauchy names, and no selected limiting value.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[FastCauchySeq classifier]
\label{def:fastcauchyseq-classifier}
For accepted $\FastCauchySeqUp$ packets $F$ and $F'$, the classifier $\mathsf{FastCauchySeqClassifier}(F,F')$ holds when the scheduled stream rows, Cauchy-modulus threshold rows, dyadic endpoint and radius rows, late-pair comparison ledgers, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ windows, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ rows transport componentwise through the displayed classifiers of their source chapters.

The classifier identifies two packets only at the level of the visible explicit-rate ledger. It does not assert equality of quotient real numbers, does not choose a limit, and does not add a completeness principle to $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[FastCauchySeq NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:fastcauchyseq-namecert-obligation-surface}
The local $\NameCert_{\FastCauchySeqUp}$ obligation surface has five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier: the $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet of \autoref{def:fastcauchyseq-carrier};
\item classifier: the componentwise classifier of \autoref{def:fastcauchyseq-classifier};
\item stability: the $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows transporting the scheduled stream, threshold table, dyadic endpoint and radius rows, and late-pair comparison ledger;
\item handoff: the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity window obtained by forgetting the local row $N$ and retaining the stream, modulus, dyadic, and comparison rows;
\item seal boundary: the $\RealUp$ consumer boundary that can read only the displayed stream, modulus, dyadic, and provenance rows.
\end{enumerate}
These rows are exactly the explicit-rate naming certificate for $\FastCauchySeqUp$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FastCauchySeqUp.FastCauchySeqPacket\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier coordinates from \autoref{def:fastcauchyseq-carrier}. The first two rows expose the scheduled dyadic observation stream and the precision-threshold table, while the dyadic and late-pair rows expose the finite comparison data at the requested precisions.

The stability row is obtained by transporting each projected coordinate through the displayed $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ routes recorded by $L$ and $T$. Because each route is componentwise, the transported packet has the same finite obligation shape.

The final two rows are projections of the same packet through the named source boundaries. Forgetting $N$ gives the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity window, and restricting to the $\RealUp$ seal boundary removes every coordinate not listed in the carrier, classifier, stability, and provenance rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[FastCauchySeq forgetful handoff to RegSeqRat]
\label{thm:fastcauchyseq-regseqrat-forgetful-handoff}
Every accepted $\FastCauchySeqUp$ packet forgets to a $\RegSeqRatUp$ regularity window. The forgotten packet keeps the scheduled $\StreamNameUp$ dyadic-rational observation stream, the visible $\CauchyModulusUp$ threshold rows, the dyadic endpoint and radius rows from $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, the late-pair comparison ledger, and the provenance over $\NatUp$ and $\RatUp$.

The handoff drops only the local $\FastCauchySeqUp$ certificate row. It does not drop the finite regularity evidence required by $\RegSeqRatUp$, and it does not produce a quotient real or a selected limit.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier tuple of \autoref{def:fastcauchyseq-carrier}. Remove the local row $N$ and keep the stream, modulus, dyadic endpoint, dyadic radius, comparison, transport, window, and provenance coordinates.

The $\CauchyModulusUp$ rows close the finite threshold requests: for each displayed precision, the row supplies the index bound used by the late-pair comparison ledger. The ledger then shows that the visible stream values beyond that bound lie inside the corresponding dyadic radius window.

These are precisely the finite regularity rows consumed by $\RegSeqRatUp$. Repacking them yields the regular sequence window, while the absence of any completed-real coordinate prevents the handoff from selecting a limit or asserting quotient equality.
\end{proof}
