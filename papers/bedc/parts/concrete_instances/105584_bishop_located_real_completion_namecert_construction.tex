\begin{theorem}[Bishop located-real completion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-located-real-completion-namecert-obligations}

Every completion-facing consumer reads only the displayed route
$$
D\longmapsto S\longmapsto R\longmapsto L\longmapsto E\longmapsto H\longmapsto C\longmapsto P\longmapsto N.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishop-located-real-completion-carrier}. Its analytic rows are the dyadic tolerance ledger, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, locatedness row, and Real seal.

Read the route in order. The tolerance row $D$ fixes the finite request, $S$ opens the corresponding window, $R$ repacks that window as regular rational data, and $L$ supplies the located lower--upper evidence attached to the same displayed readback.

The seal row $E$ is downstream of those four rows. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the route, while quotient streams, selected limits, host equality, choice, trichotomy, compactness, and ambient completeness have no carrier coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}

The locatedness row is then forced to share the same finite windows. Since $L$ is read after $R$, a consumer cannot substitute a host located real, a quotient stream class, or a selected representative for the displayed regular readback.
