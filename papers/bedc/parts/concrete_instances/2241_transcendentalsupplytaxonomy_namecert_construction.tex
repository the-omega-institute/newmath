The $\TranscendentalSupplyTaxonomyUp$ surface turns the seven socket-kind table of \autoref{ch:visions-transcendental-supply-taxonomy} into a finite NameCert packet. It records the socket kind, requested supply shape, audit gate, site row, and local routing data for a reported $\Gap$ boundary. The taxonomy packet is consumed through the concrete ledger route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-supplysocketledger-namecert}, which records the finite socket kind, audit gate, provenance, and continuation rows before any boundary classifier is read.

\begin{definition}[TranscendentalSupplyTaxonomy carrier]
\label{def:transcendental-supply-taxonomy-carrier}
A $\TranscendentalSupplyTaxonomyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(K,S,G,A,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is one of the seven socket-kind rows: free-floating induction, unwitnessed witness, extensional identification, causal continuity, inter-history coordination, inscription novelty, or self-reference closure;
\item $S$ is the requested supply shape, stated as the exact missing item that the local construction would consume;
\item $G$ is the visible $\Gap$ tag that keeps the request outside the closed substrate;
\item $A$ is the audit-gate row: generator closure, witness visibility, extensional-collapse refusal, records-versus-prediction separation, locality discipline, aspect separation, or reflection stratification;
\item $L$ is the concrete site row naming the paper or formal location where the request would be consumed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $K,S,G,A,L$, package provenance, and the local naming row;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that read the site, expose the $\Gap$ tag, classify the requested supply, and return the audit gate to downstream consumers;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\Gap$, the seven socket-kind rows, requested supply shapes, audit gates, site rows, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\TranscendentalSupplyTaxonomyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for a supplied witness, unrestricted induction principle, quotient equality, future empirical law, shared first-person history, agency explanation, or truth-total self-validator.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[TranscendentalSupplyTaxonomy NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:transcendental-supply-taxonomy-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:transcendental-supply-taxonomy-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\TranscendentalSupplyTaxonomyUp}$ surface consists exactly of socket-kind exposure, requested-supply accountability, audit-gate routing, site-row locality, classifier transport, continuation locality, package provenance, and non-discharge of the requested supply. A consumer accepts the taxonomy row only by projecting
$$
(K,S,G,A,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
and checking that $K$ is one of the seven displayed socket kinds, that $S$ names the missing shape consumed at $L$, and that $A$ is the gate preventing $S$ from being internalised as a substrate theorem.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.TranscendentalSupplyTaxonomyUp.TranscendentalSupplyTaxonomyNameCert\_obligations}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:transcendental-supply-taxonomy-carrier}. The packet lists the socket kind, requested supply shape, $\Gap$ tag, audit gate, site row, transport ledger, continuation ledger, provenance, and local naming row.

Requested-supply accountability is the pair $(S,L)$. The site row states where the missing item would be consumed, and the requested-shape row states what the missing item is.

Audit-gate routing is the pair $(G,A)$. The $\Gap$ tag keeps the request outside the closed substrate, while the audit gate names the discipline that blocks silent internalisation of the requested shape.

Classifier transport is componentwise transport through $H$, and continuation locality is replay through $C$. These rows move the displayed taxonomy packet without replacing its socket kind or adding a discharged supply coordinate.

Package provenance and non-discharge follow from $P$ and from the carrier inventory. Since the tuple has no row for the requested witness, induction principle, equality token, future law, shared locality cell, inscription source, or truth-total validator, the NameCert surface classifies the socket without closing it.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:transcendental-supply-taxonomy-boundary-exhaustion}

Any downstream consumer can read the socket kind, requested supply shape, audit gate, site row, transport data, continuation routes, provenance, and local name, but cannot extract from the packet the supply whose absence the $\Gap$ tag records.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}

Use the carrier inventory to inspect the remaining rows. The $\Gap$ tag $G$ and audit gate $A$ explain why $S$ is outside the closed substrate, while $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite packet.
