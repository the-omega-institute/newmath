\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SeparatedReflectionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-separated-reflection-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\SeparatedReflectionUp$ packet records the boundary where a pseudometric source exposes zero-distance evidence before any consumer treats that evidence as point identity. It starts from the displayed source rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-pseudometric-namecert}, routes the zero-distance boundary through the separated classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-separatedmetric-namecert}, and hands only that routed surface to the completion consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}. Its finite readback remains inside the stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet therefore names the constructive pseudometric-to-separated boundary without quotient primitives, selected representatives, host equality, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$.

\begin{definition}[SeparatedReflection carrier]
\label{def:separated-reflection-carrier}
A $\SeparatedReflectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
P=(P_0,Z,S,M,R,E,H,C,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $P_0$ is the source $\PseudometricUp$ packet, including point admission, displayed distance witnesses, dyadic tolerance and triangle ledgers, stream windows, regular rational readback, a real-distance seal, and the zero-distance boundary available before separation.
\item $Z$ records the zero-distance boundary as evidence read from $P_0$. It does not identify source point rows and does not introduce a quotient class.
\item $S$ is the $\SeparatedMetricUp$ classifier handoff. It is the first row allowed to turn zero-distance evidence into separated point-classifier evidence.
\item $M$ is the metric-completion readback row. It hands the separated classifier surface to $\MetricCompletionUp$ only after $S$ has been displayed.
\item $R$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ finite-window route available to completion and real-readback consumers.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal reached from the displayed finite and regular rational rows.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the pseudometric source, zero-distance boundary, separated classifier, completion readback, finite-window route, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay from pseudometric inspection through zero-distance evidence, separated classifier handoff, completion readback, finite windows, regular rational readback, and real sealing.
\item $N$ is both the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\PseudometricUp$, $\SeparatedMetricUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows and the local $\NameCert_{\SeparatedReflectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is a finite reflection-boundary certificate. It has no quotient-completion coordinate, no host-equality coordinate, no selected representative, no countable-choice selector, no trichotomy row, no open-cover compactness principle, and no ambient completeness theorem for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedReflection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:separated-reflection-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SeparatedReflectionUp$ carrier $P=(P_0,Z,S,M,R,E,H,C,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SeparatedReflectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the displayed rows for source pseudometric admission, zero-distance boundary exposure, separated carrier handoff, metric-completion readback, StreamName-RegSeqRat-Real sealing, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. No obligation may be discharged by a quotient completion, host equality, selected representative, countable-choice selector, trichotomy split, open-cover compactness datum, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, Lean verification row, or bridge row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:separated-reflection-carrier}. The tuple displays the source pseudometric packet, zero-distance row, separated classifier, completion readback, finite-window and regular-rational route, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The obligation order is the order of those displayed rows. The zero-distance evidence is first held in $Z$, the separated carrier handoff is $S$, and completion-facing readback is delayed until $M$ reads the routed separated surface.

The finite numerical readback is confined to $R$ and $E$, while $H,C,N$ provide transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since none of the excluded principles is a field of the carrier, none can serve as a local NameCert obligation.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedReflection zero-distance boundary]
\label{thm:separated-reflection-zero-distance-boundary}
Let $P=(P_0,Z,S,M,R,E,H,C,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparatedReflectionUp$ packet. Every consumer that reads zero-distance evidence from the pseudometric source receives that evidence first as the displayed boundary row $Z$. If the consumer asks for point identity, the request must pass through the separated classifier row $S$ before any metric, completion, regular-rational, or real-readback row can consume it.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the source row $P_0$. It supplies the pseudometric distance surface and exposes zero-distance evidence without identifying source point histories.

The boundary row $Z$ records exactly that evidence. It is not a point-identity row, so a consumer that needs identity has no route inside $P_0$ or $Z$.

The first identity-facing coordinate is $S$. Therefore every downstream metric, completion, regular-rational, or real-readback consumer receives the zero-distance claim only after the separated classifier handoff has been displayed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SeparatedReflection completion handoff]
\label{thm:separated-reflection-completion-handoff}
Every $\MetricCompletionUp$ consumer of an accepted $\SeparatedReflectionUp$ packet reads the route
$$
\PseudometricUp\to Z\to\SeparatedMetricUp\to\MetricCompletionUp\to\StreamNameUp\to\RegSeqRatUp\to\RealUp .
$$
Thus completion consumers obtain only the separated carrier handoff and its finite readback; they do not obtain quotient completion, host equality, selected representatives, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness from the reflection packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the displayed carrier order. The pseudometric source precedes the zero-distance row, the zero-distance row precedes the separated classifier, and the separated classifier precedes the completion readback.

The numerical part of the read is then forced through $R$ and $E$. Stream windows and regular rational approximants are displayed before the real seal, so completion-facing consumers see a finite readback route rather than a detached real object.

The exclusions are inventory facts. No quotient completion, host-equality proof, selected representative, countable-choice selector, trichotomy split, open-cover compactness datum, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem is present in $P$, so no completion handoff can export one.
\end{proof}
