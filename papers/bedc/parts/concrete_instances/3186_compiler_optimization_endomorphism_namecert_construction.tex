\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CompilerOptimizationEndomorphismUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-compiler-optimization-endomorphism-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\CompilerOptimizationEndomorphismUp$ interface packages source-to-source compiler optimisation as a finite accepted endomorphism over one local $\NameCert$ row. It realizes the Cook compile-frontier route of \autoref{ch:visions-cook-compile-frontier-engineering-path} as a before/after compiler trace whose accepted rows can be read by the certificate compiler of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-certificatecompiler-namecert}, the composition trace of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compilercompositiontrace-namecert}, the closed-term substitution compiler of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedtermsubstitutioncompiler-namecert}, and the cellular audit surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cellularautomaton-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cellulartrustsubstrate-namecert}. It consumes the compiler-morphism, certificate-compiler, classifier-morphism, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local naming rows already used by compiler and decompiler certificates, but it does not import host program equality or a semantic optimisation theorem.

\falsifiablePrediction{If the semantic-preservation row is removed, a frontier-improving packet can no longer certify that the before and after compiler traces decode to the same substrate observation row.}

\independenceWitness{certificatecompiler, compilercompositiontrace, closedtermsubstitutioncompiler, cellularautomaton, and cellulartrustsubstrate: none carries the joint before/after optimization endomorphism plus frontier-coordinate effect. CertificateCompiler exposes one compiler edge surface, CompilerCompositionTrace exposes sequential composition, ClosedTermSubstitutionCompiler exposes the closed syntactic boundary, CellularAutomaton exposes the local orbit ledger, and CellularTrustSubstrate exposes substrate trust rows; the optimization endomorphism alone binds the before trace, after trace, semantic-preservation row, and frontier-coordinate improvement in one NameCert packet.}

\begin{definition}[CompilerOptimizationEndomorphism carrier]
\label{def:compiler-optimization-endomorphism-carrier}
A $\CompilerOptimizationEndomorphismUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
O=(S,B,A,G,E,L,F,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source $\NameCert$ row that is also the target naming row of the optimisation;
\item $B$ is the before compiler trace row, read through the same stage order as the Cook pipeline stages of \autoref{sec:cook-frontier-pipeline-stages};
\item $A$ is the after compiler trace row, displaying the optimized trace over the same source and target naming row;
\item $G$ is an accepted endomorphism graph on $S$, displaying source-to-source compiler edges from $B$ to $A$ rather than a host optimiser function;
\item $E$ is the same-source landing row recording that every displayed graph edge starts in the carrier of $S$ and lands back in the same carrier;
\item $L$ is the classifier-preservation row recording that source classifier membership is transported along the displayed endomorphism graph;
\item $F$ is the frontier-coordinate row recording that the after trace improves a displayed compile-frontier coordinate while still decoding to the same substrate observation row as the before trace;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ preservation for the source row, before and after trace rows, graph row, same-source landing row, classifier row, frontier row, continuation rows, package row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ route replay row, pairing every source continuation read used by $G$ with its routed post-optimisation continuation inside the same certificate $S$;
\item $P$ names the dependency surface $\NameCertMorphismUp$, $\CertificateCompilerUp$, $\CompilerCompositionTraceUp$, $\ClosedTermSubstitutionCompilerUp$, $\ClassifierMorphismUp$, $\CellularAutomatonUp$, $\CellularTrustSubstrateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompilerOptimizationEndomorphismUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is an accepted NameCert endomorphism packet. It has no coordinate for host program equality, denotational semantics, hidden cost improvement, global category laws, or a semantic optimiser correctness theorem outside the displayed semantic-preservation row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CompilerOptimizationEndomorphism NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compiler-optimization-endomorphism-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CompilerOptimizationEndomorphismUp}$ surface for \autoref{def:compiler-optimization-endomorphism-carrier} consists exactly of carrier admission, before-trace exposure through $B$, after-trace exposure through $A$, accepted same-source graph edges on $S$, source-to-source landing through $E$, classifier preservation through $L$, semantic-preservation and frontier-coordinate exposure through $F$, $\hsame$ preservation through $H$, displayed continuation replay through $C$, dependency provenance through $P$, and the local naming row $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the finite packet $O$. The source row, before trace, after trace, graph, same-source landing row, classifier-preservation row, frontier row, transport row, continuation replay row, provenance row, and local name are all displayed coordinates.

The endomorphism condition is also a projection. Both the edge source and edge target are read from the same $\NameCert$ row $S$, so no additional target certificate can be introduced by the obligation surface.

Classifier, semantic-preservation, $\hsame$, and continuation obligations are read from $L$, $F$, $H$, and $C$. The package row $P$ records the compiler, composition, closed-substitution, cellular, and trust-substrate dependencies, and $N$ names the local certificate. Since no other coordinate is present, these rows exhaust the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompilerOptimizationEndomorphism composition]
\label{thm:compiler-optimization-endomorphism-composition}
Let
$$
O_1=(S,B_1,A_1,G_1,E_1,L_1,F_1,H_1,C_1,P_1,N_1)
$$
and
$$
O_2=(S,B_2,A_2,G_2,E_2,L_2,F_2,H_2,C_2,P_2,N_2)
$$
be accepted $\CompilerOptimizationEndomorphismUp$ packets over the same source $\NameCert$ row $S$. Their displayed composite is the finite packet whose before trace is $B_1$, whose middle trace is $A_1=B_2$ as a displayed compiler-composition handoff, whose after trace is $A_2$, whose graph row is the relational composite of $G_1$ and $G_2$, whose same-source landing row chains $E_1$ with $E_2$, whose classifier-preservation row chains $L_1$ with $L_2$, whose semantic-preservation and frontier-coordinate row chains $F_1$ with $F_2$, and whose $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local naming rows are obtained by replaying the displayed rows of $O_1$ and $O_2$ in order.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two accepted packets. Both expose the same source and target naming row $S$, so the middle landing of the first graph is exactly the source row consumed by the second graph.

Compose the graph rows relationally. A displayed source row is related to a displayed final row when a middle row of $S$ is named by an edge of $G_1$ followed by an edge of $G_2$.

The landing, classifier, and frontier rows compose along the same middle row. The $E_i$ rows keep both edges inside $S$, the $L_i$ rows transport classifier membership first across $G_1$ and then across $G_2$, and the $F_i$ rows keep the before and after traces semantically pinned to the same substrate observation while recording the displayed frontier-coordinate effect.

Finally replay the $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local naming rows in the displayed order. The result is again a finite endomorphism packet over $S$, with no appeal to an ambient optimiser semantics.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CompilerOptimizationEndomorphism non-escape]
\label{thm:compiler-optimization-endomorphism-nonescape}
Every consumer read of an accepted $\CompilerOptimizationEndomorphismUp$ packet factors through
$$
S,\ B,\ A,\ G,\ E,\ L,\ F,\ H,\ C,\ P,\ N.
$$
A request for host optimisation semantics, host program equality, denotational equivalence beyond the displayed semantic-preservation row, hidden cost improvement, a global compiler category law, or an undisplayed target certificate is outside the carrier and is not exported as NameCert evidence.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the request through the displayed continuation route $C$. If it asks for the source certificate, before trace, after trace, graph, same-source landing row, classifier-preservation row, semantic-preservation and frontier-coordinate row, $\hsame$ transport, continuation replay, package provenance, or local name, the carrier supplies exactly the corresponding coordinate.

Any other request leaves the tuple $O$. The carrier contains no coordinate for a semantic optimiser theorem outside $F$, host equality of programs, denotational model, hidden cost comparison, ambient category law, or separate target certificate.

Thus the accepted packet exports only the finite NameCert endomorphism surface. Optimisation correctness available to downstream compiler or decompiler certificates is the displayed preservation route through $B,A,G,E,L,F,H,C,P,N$, not a host-level theorem.
\end{proof}
