\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\GoursatUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-goursat-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Goursat and constructive complex analysis, the $\GoursatUp$ packet names the finite triangle-subdivision boundary used by the triangle form of the Cauchy integral theorem. It consumes holomorphic disk data from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-holomorphic-namecert}, complex analytic bounds from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-analytic-namecert}, complex limit moduli from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-limit-namecert}, complex coordinate histories from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-complex-namecert}, and the contour ledger rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-contourintegraloperation-namecert}. The packet is a finite certificate for one displayed holomorphic triangle and its subdivision ledger; it is not a host path integral theorem, not open-cover compactness, not a selected primitive, and not a quotient equality principle.

\begin{definition}[Goursat carrier]
\label{def:goursat-carrier}
A $\GoursatUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
G=(T,H,E,S,M,I,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the triangle-vertex row. It records three $\ComplexUp$ vertex histories, their oriented edge histories, and the nondegenerate triangle window inspected by later rows.
\item $H$ is the holomorphic disk witness. It is consumed from $\HolomorphicUp$ and displays a disk containing the triangle window together with the local modulus and complex analytic bounds needed for the subdivision estimate.
\item $E$ is the edge-contour row. It lists the three oriented boundary contour reads and routes each read through the finite contour-integral operation surface.
\item $S$ is the subdivision tree. Each node stores a displayed triangle, its four child triangles, the inherited orientation rows, and the parent-to-child boundary ledger.
\item $M$ is the modulus and mesh budget. It records the unary refinement level, diameter estimates, perimeter estimates, and the holomorphic error bound used at the leaves.
\item $I$ is the integral-cancellation ledger. It records boundary-edge persistence and opposite-edge cancellation for every internal edge of the subdivision tree.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the triangle request through the holomorphic disk row, edge-contour rows, subdivision tree, mesh budget, cancellation ledger, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\GoursatUp$, $\HolomorphicUp$, $\ComplexAnalyticUp$, $\ComplexLimitUp$, $\ComplexUp$, $\ContourIntegralOperationUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\GoursatUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $T,H,E,S,M,I,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a host path integral, open-cover compactness theorem, selected primitive, quotient equality of paths, or classical-choice coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Goursat NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:goursat-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\GoursatUp$ carrier
$$
G=(T,H,E,S,M,I,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\GoursatUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows.
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the displayed triangle vertices, holomorphic disk witness, edge-contour reads, subdivision tree, mesh budget, cancellation ledger, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item classifier transport through the componentwise rows listed in \autoref{def:goursat-carrier};
\item subdivision stability, requiring every child triangle in $S$ to inherit its vertex, orientation, holomorphic-disk, and mesh rows from the parent packet;
\item boundary-edge cancellation, requiring every internal edge created by the subdivision to appear twice in $I$ with opposite orientations and the same contour-integral read;
\item ledger nonescape, excluding host path integrals, open-cover compactness, selected primitives, quotient path equality, and classical-choice coordinates from the local certificate surface.
\end{enumerate}
No local obligation is stored outside the displayed packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:goursat-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the triangle, holomorphic disk, contour-edge, subdivision, mesh, cancellation, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

Classifier transport is componentwise. Two accepted packets compare first through the $\ComplexUp$ vertex histories in $T$, then through the holomorphic disk row $H$, the edge-contour row $E$, the subdivision tree $S$, and the mesh and cancellation ledgers $M,I$. The structural rows $C,P,N$ replay and name that same finite comparison.

Subdivision stability is inherited from the rows stored at each node of $S$. A child triangle has no independent source of vertices, orientations, disk witnesses, or mesh budgets; each of those entries is read from its parent row and transported through the same $\hsame$ comparison.

Boundary-edge cancellation is a ledger property. An internal edge is created only as a shared edge of two sibling triangles, and $I$ records the two appearances with opposite orientations and the same contour-integral row from $E$. External edges are the original boundary rows and are not cancelled.

Coordinate exhaustion gives ledger nonescape. Since the carrier has no slot for a host path integral, open-cover compactness theorem, selected primitive, quotient path equality, or classical-choice coordinate, none of those objects can be exported by the local $\NameCert_{\GoursatUp}$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Goursat triangle integral boundary]
\label{thm:goursat-triangle-integral-boundary}
Let $G=(T,H,E,S,M,I,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\GoursatUp$ carrier. Every accepted triangle-integral read factors through the displayed subdivision tree $S$ and the opposite-edge cancellation ledger $I$. Its observable route is
$$
T,H,E \longmapsto S \longmapsto M \longmapsto I \longmapsto C,P,N .
$$
The read exposes only boundary contour rows, child-triangle rows, leaf mesh estimates, and opposite-edge cancellations. It exposes no host path integral, open-cover compactness argument, selected primitive, quotient equality of paths, or classical-choice coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted triangle-integral read. By \autoref{def:goursat-carrier}, the read enters through the displayed triangle row $T$, the holomorphic disk row $H$, and the edge-contour row $E$.

The route then passes through $S$. Each subdivision node records its four children and its inherited orientations, so every internal edge is visible as a row shared by two sibling child triangles.

The mesh budget $M$ supplies the leaf estimates used by the holomorphic disk witness. These estimates are attached to the displayed leaves; they do not select a primitive and do not invoke compactness outside the finite subdivision tree.

The cancellation ledger $I$ removes exactly the internal opposite-edge pairs. The remaining contour reads are the original boundary edges from $E$, and the structural rows $C,P,N$ replay that finite route. Because no excluded object is a carrier coordinate, the factorization is exhaustive.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Goursat holomorphic handoff]
\label{thm:goursat-holomorphic-handoff}
Every $\HolomorphicUp$ disk packet consumed by an accepted $\GoursatUp$ carrier reaches only the finite triangle-subdivision integral boundary before any downstream Cauchy-integral theorem may use it. The handoff order is
$$
\HolomorphicUp,\ \ComplexAnalyticUp,\ \ComplexLimitUp,\ \ComplexUp
\longmapsto
T,H,E,S,M,I
\longmapsto
\NameCert_{\GoursatUp}.
$$
Thus a downstream Cauchy-integral consumer may inspect the Goursat boundary only as a finite NameCert row over the displayed triangle, subdivision, mesh, and cancellation data.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the consumed holomorphic disk row $H$ from the carrier. Its domain, modulus, and analytic-bound data are read through the holomorphic, complex analytic, complex limit, and complex coordinate chapters cited above.

The disk row is then restricted by the triangle row $T$. No downstream consumer can read $H$ directly as a global analytic theorem, because the continuation row first routes it through the edge-contour data $E$ and the subdivision tree $S$.

The mesh budget $M$ and cancellation ledger $I$ are the last non-structural rows before the local name. They expose finite estimates and opposite-edge cancellation only. The local $\NameCert_{\GoursatUp}$ row therefore presents a finite triangle-subdivision boundary, and any later Cauchy-integral theorem must consume that boundary rather than a host path integral or selected primitive.
\end{proof}
