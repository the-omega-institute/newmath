Following Bishop and Bridges-style separated completion uniqueness, the $\CompletionUniquenessUp$ packet records the finite BEDC route by which two completion-facing presentations are identified only through a displayed separated seal and a universal-property uniqueness ledger. It sits downstream of the separated surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert}, the extension-and-uniqueness surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completionuniversalproperty-namecert}, and the functorial route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchycompletionfunctor-namecert}. The packet also reads the regular real-name spine through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$, without turning those rows into quotient completion data.

\begin{definition}[Completion uniqueness carrier]
\label{def:completion-uniqueness-carrier}
A $\CompletionUniquenessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(D,S,R,E,H,F,L,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used by the compared regular names;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule read by both sides;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational source row carried by the shared schedule and tolerance ledger;
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row reached from $D,S,R$;
\item $H$ is the $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ separated seal that makes the comparison a separated-completion read rather than a quotient-class read;
\item $F$ is the $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ functorial handoff that transports the same regular source into each completion-facing presentation;
\item $L$ is the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ uniqueness ledger comparing the two transported extension rows over the same $D,S,R,E,H,F$ spine;
\item $T$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the displayed dyadic, stream, regular, Real, separated, functorial, and uniqueness rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes that replay the separated-limit and uniqueness reads;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\HausdorffCompletionUp$, $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$, $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompletionUniquenessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient completion object, no selected host limit, no ambient Real completeness field, and no host equality row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Completion uniqueness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:completion-uniqueness-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CompletionUniquenessUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:completion-uniqueness-carrier} consists of five finite rows: carrier admission for the displayed packet $U$; classifier stability under the componentwise $\hsame$ transports $T$ and $\Cont$ routes $C$; separated-seal compatibility through the $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ row $H$; functorial source compatibility through the $\CauchyCompletionFunctorUp$ row $F$; and universal-property ledger exactness through the $\CompletionUniversalPropertyUp$ row $L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection of \autoref{def:completion-uniqueness-carrier}. The dyadic tolerance ledger, finite stream schedule, regular-rational source, Real seal, separated-completion seal, functorial handoff, uniqueness ledger, transport rows, continuation rows, package provenance, and local naming row are displayed coordinates of one finite packet.

Classifier stability is componentwise. The transports in $T$ move $D,S,R,E,H,F,L,N$ without changing the dependency order, and the continuation rows in $C$ replay only the separated-limit and uniqueness reads already exposed by those coordinates.

The remaining obligation rows are sibling handoffs. The separated surface is read through $H$, the functorial source route is read through $F$, and the uniqueness ledger is read through $L$ after the shared $D,S,R,E$ spine has been projected. Since no carrier coordinate contains a quotient completion, selected host limit, ambient Real completeness field, or host equality row, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Completion uniqueness separated limit]
\label{thm:completion-uniqueness-separated-limit}
Let $U=(D,S,R,E,H,F,L,T,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CompletionUniquenessUp$ carrier. Any comparison between the two completion-facing presentations exported by $F$ and $L$ first passes through the displayed route
$$
D,\quad S,\quad R,\quad E,\quad H.
$$
Thus the comparison is a separated-limit read over the dyadic, stream, regular-rational, Real-seal, and Hausdorff-completion rows, not a host equality of completed points.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The rows $D,S,R,E$ give the finite regular-name spine, and the row $H$ is the only separated-completion seal attached to that spine.

Read the functorial and universal-property rows after the separated seal. The functorial handoff $F$ transports the same regular source into the two completion-facing presentations, while the ledger $L$ compares their extension rows only over the already displayed source and seal data.

The continuation row $C$ can replay the comparison only along that route, and the provenance row $P$ records no host completion object. Therefore every comparison is a separated-limit read over the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Completion uniqueness ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:completion-uniqueness-ledger-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\CompletionUniquenessUp$ carrier, the rows $D,S,R,E,H,F,L,T,C,P,N$ exhaust the proof surface for constructive separated completion uniqueness. A consumer may inspect dyadic tolerances, stream windows, regular rational source data, the Real seal, the Hausdorff separated seal, the Cauchy-completion functor handoff, the universal-property uniqueness ledger, componentwise transports, continuation routes, package provenance, and the local NameCert row. It obtains no quotient completion, selected host limit, ambient Real completeness theorem, or host equality witness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the carrier decomposition of \autoref{def:completion-uniqueness-carrier}. Every coordinate available to a consumer is one of $D,S,R,E,H,F,L,T,C,P,N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:completion-uniqueness-namecert-obligations} to bind classifier stability and obligation exactness to those rows. Apply \autoref{thm:completion-uniqueness-separated-limit} to route every completion-facing comparison through the separated finite spine before the uniqueness ledger is read.

No additional field remains in the packet. In particular, there is no coordinate for quotient completion construction, selected host limits, ambient Real completeness, or host equality. The displayed rows therefore exhaust the constructive uniqueness ledger.
\end{proof}
