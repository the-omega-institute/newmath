\begin{definition}[InterHistLocalityLedger carrier]
\label{def:inter-hist-locality-ledger-carrier}
An $\InterHistLocalityLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(A,B,H,C,O,I,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two source $\BHist$ rows whose local relation is being certified;
\item $H$ stores symmetric componentwise $\hsame$ transport between the two source-history presentations and their transported rows;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which the inspected finite data are read from $A$ and $B$ and replayed across the ledger;
\item $O$ is the $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ row that supplies the two-observer locality cell consumed by the ledger;
\item $I$ is the $\InterHistInvariantUp$ row that records the observer-symmetric inter-Hist invariant readback;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$, $\InterHistInvariantUp$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\InterHistLocalityLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for a global frame, shared clock, selected synchronization history, host-side equality of histories, quotient of observer records, or ambient spacetime.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[InterHistLocalityLedger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:inter-hist-locality-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:inter-hist-locality-ledger-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\InterHistLocalityLedgerUp}$ surface consists of carrier admission for $L=(A,B,H,C,O,I,P,N)$, symmetric $\hsame$ transport for the two source histories, displayed $\Cont$ route replay, observer-locality-cell consumption, inter-Hist invariant readback, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed tuple $L$. The rows $A$ and $B$ give the two source histories, while $H$ and $C$ give the transport and continuation data used to compare their finite observations.

The rows $O$ and $I$ supply the two local consumers. The observer-locality-cell row records the finite two-observer surface, and the invariant row records the observer-symmetric readback over the same pair.

The provenance and naming clauses are $P$ and $N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a global frame, shared clock, synchronization history, quotient of observers, host-side history equality, or ambient spacetime, these displayed rows exhaust the local certificate obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InterHistLocalityLedger symmetric route]
\label{thm:inter-hist-locality-ledger-symmetric-route}
Let $L=(A,B,H,C,O,I,P,N)$ be an accepted $\InterHistLocalityLedgerUp$ carrier. Swapping the source-history rows $A$ and $B$ yields the same public locality ledger after replaying the symmetric $\hsame$ transport in $H$ and the displayed $\Cont$ routes in $C$; the observer-locality cell and inter-Hist invariant rows are read through the same finite packet.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only source-history coordinates are $A$ and $B$, and the only transport coordinate relating their presentations is $H$.

Replay the continuation routes stored in $C$. These routes attach the inspected finite data from both source histories to the same locality-cell row $O$ and invariant row $I$.

The NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:inter-hist-locality-ledger-namecert-obligations} then identify the swapped read as a route through the same $H,C,O,I,P,N$ surface. No global synchronization coordinate is available to distinguish one source history as the privileged anchor.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InterHistLocalityLedger non-escape]
\label{thm:inter-hist-locality-ledger-nonescape}
Every public consumer of an accepted $\InterHistLocalityLedgerUp$ carrier may inspect only the source-history rows $A,B$, the symmetric $\hsame$ transport row $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ route row $C$, the $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ row $O$, the $\InterHistInvariantUp$ row $I$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$. It receives no global frame, shared clock, selected synchronization history, host-side history equality, quotient of observer records, metric spacetime, or all-observer synchronization map.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect the carrier coordinates in \autoref{def:inter-hist-locality-ledger-carrier}. The visible rows are exactly $A,B,H,C,O,I,P,N$.

The observer-locality-cell and invariant rows are downstream reads of that finite packet, not extra ambient structures. They preserve the same two-history boundary and do not add a frame, clock, quotient, or host equality coordinate.

Therefore every consumer is exhausted by the displayed rows. Any request for global synchronization, ambient spacetime, host-side equality of histories, or an all-observer map would require a carrier coordinate that is absent from $L$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[InterHistLocalityLedger observer-locality handoff]
\label{thm:inter-hist-locality-ledger-observer-locality-handoff}
An accepted $\InterHistLocalityLedgerUp$ carrier hands the two source histories to an $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$ consumer and an $\InterHistInvariantUp$ consumer through one finite route packet
$$
A,B,H,C,O,I,P,N.
$$
The handoff preserves the symmetric locality relation of the ledger and does not add a cross-Hist causal predicate, maximum-rate bound, global frame, or metric geometry.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier definition. The rows $A$ and $B$ are the only source histories, $O$ is the observer-locality-cell row, and $I$ is the inter-Hist invariant row.

Use the symmetric route theorem to transport the two source histories through $H$ and replay their inspected finite data through $C$. This makes the locality-cell and invariant consumers read the same two-history ledger.

Apply non-escape for the boundary. Since $L$ has no coordinate for cross-Hist causal predicates, maximum-rate bounds, global frames, metric geometry, or host-side equality, the observer-locality handoff is exactly the finite packet $A,B,H,C,O,I,P,N$.
\end{proof}
