\section{Standard bridge}
\label{sec:ideal-quotient-kernel-standard-bridge}

\begin{theorem}[IdealUp quotient-kernel standard bridge]
\label{thm:idealup-quotient-kernel-standard-bridge}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, zero $0_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and subtraction $x-_R y:=x+_R(-_R y)$. Let $I$ be an $\IdealUp$ carried predicate over $R$, and let $Q_I$ be the quotient-kernel source package of \autoref{def:ideal-quotient-kernel-source-package}. Then $Q_I$ interprets as the standard quotient-kernel comparison consumed by $\QuotientRingUp$ and $\IdealClassUp$: two carried representatives are compared exactly by the ambient carrier rows together with membership of the displayed difference in $I$. Conversely, every standard kernel comparison used by those consumers is represented by the BEDC rows exported in \autoref{thm:ideal-quotientring-export-surface}. This bridge adds no host quotient carrier, host quotient primitive, or classifier-changing host equality to the BEDC surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the source package of \autoref{def:ideal-quotient-kernel-source-package}. Its comparison data are precisely the two carrier witnesses $C(x)$ and $C(y)$ and the ideal membership row $I(x-_R y)$. The diagonal comparison is supplied by \autoref{thm:ideal-quotient-kernel-diagonal-exactness}, so each carried representative compares with itself through the BEDC difference row rather than through a host quotient object.

Endpoint invariance is exactly \autoref{thm:ideal-quotient-kernel-transport-surface}. Thus changing representatives along the ambient classifier preserves the same kernel package, and no additional equality relation is inserted between the BEDC carrier and the consumer.

For the multiplicative consumer row, apply \autoref{thm:ideal-quotient-kernel-multiplication-descent}. The product comparison is again expressed by membership of the product difference in $I$, using only the ambient ring rows and the two-sided absorption rows of the ideal predicate.

Finally, \autoref{thm:ideal-quotientring-export-surface} repacks the diagonal, endpoint-transport, additive-descent, negation-descent, and multiplication-descent rows into the full quotient-free surface read by $\QuotientRingUp$. The same exported rows are the standard kernel comparison boundary available to $\IdealClassUp$ through its quotient consumer. Since the package exports only carried endpoints, classifier transport, and difference-membership rows, the bridge has no host quotient carrier or host quotient primitive to leak into the BEDC surface.
\end{proof}
