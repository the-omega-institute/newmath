\section{Standard bridge}
\label{sec:ideal-quotient-kernel-standard-bridge}

\begin{theorem}[Ideal public surface standard bridge]
\label{thm:ideal-public-surface-standard-bridge}
Let $R$ carry a $\RingUp$ certificate with carrier $C$, classifier $\sim_R$, addition $+_R$, additive inverse $-_R$, zero $0_R$, multiplication $\cdot_R$, and subtraction $x-_R y:=x+_R(-_R y)$. Assume the ambient $\RingUp$ certificate has a standard bridge to an ordinary ring surface. Then the public $\IdealUp$ surface interprets over that standard ring as the ordinary ideal-predicate surface and re-encodes through the BEDC rows: carried predicates, zero membership, additive closure, additive inverse closure, internal multiplication, two-sided ambient absorption, finite meet, sum, ring-map preimage, principal generated ideals, and the quotient-kernel export surface. The bridge exports no host quotient carrier, host quotient primitive, or classifier-changing host equality to BEDC.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.IdealUp.IdealUp\_StdBridge}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the $\IdealUp$ carrier inventory of \autoref{def:ideal-carried-predicate-rows}. The ordinary surface reads the BEDC predicate as a carried subset of the standard ring supplied by the ambient $\RingUp$ bridge. Carrier support, zero membership, addition, additive inverse, internal multiplication, classifier transport, and left and right absorption are exactly the rows displayed in \autoref{sec:ideal-certificate}.

The finite-meet constructor is interpreted pointwise. Apply \autoref{thm:finite-ideal-intersection-closure}: every indexed ordinary ideal row is carried back to the finite BEDC family, and the carrier conjunct supplies the empty-spine case. The sum constructor is interpreted by the additive decomposition ledger of \autoref{thm:ideal-sum-closure}; its least-upper-bound surface is the ordinary inclusion property recorded in the same public BEDC rows.

The preimage constructor uses the ambient bridges on the source and target $\RingUp$ certificates. Apply \autoref{thm:ring-map-ideal-preimage-ideal-closure}: carrier preservation, classifier preservation, and operation preservation turn the standard inverse-image ideal into the BEDC source predicate. Principal generated ideals are supplied by \autoref{thm:ideal-principal-generated-full-ideal-closure}, whose generator ledger is again expressed only by carried endpoints, classifier transport, and ideal rows.

For the quotient-kernel part, unfold \autoref{def:ideal-quotient-kernel-source-package}. Its comparison data are precisely the two carrier witnesses $C(x)$ and $C(y)$ and membership of the displayed difference in the ideal predicate. Apply \autoref{thm:ideal-quotientring-export-surface}; the exported diagonal, endpoint-transport, additive-descent, negation-descent, and multiplication-descent rows are the quotient-free standard comparison boundary.

Thus each public constructor is interpreted through the ambient standard ring bridge and then repacked by the BEDC certificate rows already cited in this chapter. Since the quotient-kernel surface exports only carried endpoints, classifier transport, and difference-membership rows, no host quotient carrier or host quotient primitive enters the BEDC interface.
\end{proof}
