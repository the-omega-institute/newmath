\begin{definition}[Centralizer-normalizer SubgroupUp pair]
\label{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-pair}
Fix the centralizer carrier $Z_a$ from
\autoref{def:group-centralizer-carrier} and the centralizer normalizer carrier
$N_a$ from \autoref{def:group-centralizer-normalizer-carrier}. The
centralizer-normalizer $\SubgroupUp$ pair has lower carrier $Z_a$ and upper
carrier $N_a$, both classified by the restriction of $\hsame$ to carried
histories. The operation, unit, and inverse are inherited from the ambient
history-level $\GroupUp$ operation $\mathsf{mul}$, $e$, and $\mathsf{inv}$.

The pair ledger records the fixed element $a$, the conjugation word
$\mathsf{Conj}_s(x)$, the witnesses that histories carried by $Z_a$ commute
with $a$, and the forward and inverse preservation witnesses appearing in the
definition of $N_a$. Thus a carried normalizer history acts on the carried
centralizer histories by the restricted conjugation word while keeping every
endpoint inside the same $\SubgroupUp$ lower carrier.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Centralizer-normalizer SubgroupUp pair certificate]
\label{thm:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-pair-certificate}
For a BEDC history-level $\GroupUp$ operation over $\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying
the same associativity, unit, multiplication-congruence, and two-sided inverse
laws as in
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup}, the pair of
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-pair} satisfies the following
concrete $\SubgroupUp$ certificate rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $Z_a$ is the centralizer $\SubgroupUp$ certificate target of
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}.
\item $N_a$ is a $\SubgroupUp$ certificate target over the same ambient
operation, with unit, product, inverse, and classifier-transport rows.
\item The lower carrier is included in the upper carrier: if $Z_a(s)$, then
$N_a(s)$.
\item If $N_a(s)$ and $Z_a(x)$, then
$Z_a(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x))$ and
$Z_a(\mathsf{Conj}_{\mathsf{inv}(s)}(x))$.
\item For every $s$ with $N_a(s)$, the restricted normalizer action on $Z_a$
is classifier-exact, its orbit classifier is stable under $\hsame$ replacement
of carried endpoints, and its fixed-action kernel satisfies
$$
(\forall x:\mathsf{BHist},\ Z_a(x)\Rightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{Conj}_s(x),x))
\Longleftrightarrow
(\forall x:\mathsf{BHist},\ Z_a(x)\Rightarrow
\hsame(\mathsf{mul}(s,x),\mathsf{mul}(x,s))).
$$
\end{enumerate}
Hence the centralizer and its normalizer are available as one concrete
$\SubgroupUp$ pair over $\mathsf{BHist}$, with the restricted conjugation
action and orbit classifier attached to the same certificate object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The lower certificate is
\autoref{thm:subgroup-centralizer-certificate-target}. It gives the unit,
product, inverse, classifier-transport, and commutator-fiber rows for $Z_a$
with the operation, unit, inverse, and restricted $\hsame$ classifier inherited
from the ambient $\GroupUp$.

The upper certificate and the carrier inclusion are supplied by
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-subgroup}. Its first four rows give
unit, product, inverse, and classifier transport for $N_a$, and its final row
places each centralizer element in the normalizer.

The action rows are the defining preservation clauses of $N_a$, recorded in
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-action-automorphism-kernel-exactness}.
That theorem also gives the inverse-action identities and the displayed
fixed-action kernel equivalence, so the restricted action is exact on the
centralizer carrier.

Finally,
\autoref{thm:group-centralizer-normalizer-orbit-classifier-certificate}
supplies the endpoint-transport row for the orbit classifier generated by the
same normalizer action. Repacking these cited rows with the ledger of
\autoref{def:subgroup-centralizer-normalizer-pair} gives one concrete
$\SubgroupUp$ pair over $\mathsf{BHist}$.
\end{proof}
