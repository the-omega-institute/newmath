\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path public route certificate]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-public-route-certificate}
Every public read of an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet factors through the displayed finite route certificate
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L).
$$
The factorization reads closed-normalization through $S$, normal-form consistency through $N$, the subject-reduction obstruction through $O$, decidability and handoff data through $U$, the discharge socket through $D$, componentwise transport through $H$, route replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICCriticalPathUp}$ naming through $L$. It has no public row for closed subject reduction, full closed consistency, global beta-conversion decidability, a hidden dependency edge, or an exported theorem outside the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the obligation surface. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-namecert-obligation-surface} lists the public packet rows and identifies the local NameCert obligations with the finite tuple $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$.

The positive consistency route is fixed by the handoff and route inventory. Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-consistency-handoff}, Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-route-replay}, and Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-route-exhaustion}. These results keep the closed-normalization row, normal-form endpoint, handoff row, continuation row, transport row, provenance row, and local naming row inside the displayed carrier.

It remains to exclude public escape routes. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-obstruction-boundary} keeps the subject-reduction obstruction visible, while Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-root-unblock-consumer-readiness} restricts downstream consumers to the same displayed rows. Hence every public read is a read of the finite route certificate and not a read of an undisplayed theorem export.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path obstruction scope separation]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-obstruction-scope-separation}
For every accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet $K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)$, the obstruction row $O$ remains a scoped subject-reduction boundary. The consistency row $N$, the closed-normalization route $S$, the handoff row $U$, route replay through $C$, componentwise transport through $H$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $L$ cannot convert $O$ or the socket $D$ into closed subject reduction or full closed consistency.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the obstruction row from the packet carrier. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-obstruction-boundary} states that $O$ remains visible under the handoff row and componentwise transport, and that agreement on the other rows cannot erase the obstruction.

The consistency and normalization side stays on its own route. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-consistency-handoff} lets a consumer travel from $S$ to $N$ through the displayed rows, but it stops at the normal-form endpoint unless the separate discharge socket is filled.

Finally use route exhaustion and obligation coverage. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-route-exhaustion} prevents replay from leaving the displayed route, and Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-namecert-obligation-surface} leaves no extra coordinate that could identify $O$ with a supplied theorem. The obstruction row therefore remains a scoped boundary rather than a theorem produced by the consistency or normalization rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path downstream consumer readiness]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-downstream-consumer-readiness}
Downstream MetaCIC consumers may use an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet only as a finite public route certificate over $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$. They may cite it for carrier admission, closed-normalization readback, normal-form consistency handoff, obstruction visibility, decidability handoff, discharge-socket locality, route replay, transport stability, provenance, and local NameCert naming. They may not cite it as closed subject reduction, full normalization, internal consistency for MetaCIC, or a global decision procedure.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the root-unblock surface. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-root-unblock-consumer-readiness} already confines downstream consumers to the displayed packet rows and excludes host scheduler state, hidden route closures, closed subject reduction, and full consistency.

The public route certificate supplies the usable rows. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-public-route-certificate} packages the normalization route, normal-form endpoint, obstruction row, handoff data, discharge socket, transport, continuation, provenance, and local NameCert row as the complete public read surface.

The obstruction boundary fixes what is not available. By Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-obstruction-scope-separation}, the subject-reduction obstruction and discharge socket remain separated from the consistency and normalization rows. A downstream consumer can therefore depend on the packet as a finite route certificate, but not as subject reduction, full normalization, internal consistency, or a global decision procedure.
\end{proof}
