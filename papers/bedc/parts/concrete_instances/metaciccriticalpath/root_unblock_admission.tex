\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC root-unblock admission]
\label{thm:metaciccriticalpath-root-unblock-admission}
Any $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ admission for candidate-mediated strong-normalization, confluence, or decidability work must first expose the four L10 source rows $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ through the open-phase ledger of \autoref{def:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-ledger} before a candidate checker row is consumed. The admission may then read the candidate-mediated frontier of \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-sn-frontier}, the Phase Real root-unblock route of \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-root-unblock-admission}, and the candidate-mediated obligation surface of \autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-candidate-mediated-sn-obligation-surface}; it exports no closed subject-reduction row, global confluence theorem, global beta-conversion decision procedure, quotient term equality, selected limit, host real equality, or $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the open-phase source ledger. Definition~\ref{def:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-ledger} displays the packet rows together with the four L10 source faces, so a MetaCIC admission cannot consume a candidate checker row before those faces are visible.

Next apply the Phase Real root-unblock admission theorem, \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-root-unblock-admission}. It identifies the admitted root-unblock route with the same packet rows and the separate $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ face records.

Finally attach the candidate-mediated surface. The frontier theorem \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-sn-frontier} and the obligation surface theorem \autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-candidate-mediated-sn-obligation-surface} allow confluence-facing and decidability-facing consumers to inspect the candidate row only after the displayed source ledger is present. Since the cited surfaces contain none of the excluded metatheoretic or completion coordinates, the root-unblock admission is exactly the stated L10-ledger-first route.
\end{proof}
