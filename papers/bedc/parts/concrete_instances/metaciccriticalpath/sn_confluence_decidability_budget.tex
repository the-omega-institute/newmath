\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC candidate SN confluence-decidability budget]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-sn-confluence-budget}
For every accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L),
$$
whose candidate-mediated strong-normalization budget is displayed by the finite socket route, every confluence-facing or decidability-facing handoff must pass through the existing budget rows: the $\BHist$ packet rows $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$, the candidate budget surface over the four local faces $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$, the confluence handoff row $U$, the decidability handoff read of the same row, the componentwise $\Cont$ replay $C$, and the $\NameCert$ provenance $P,L$. The route exports no separate Church--Rosser theorem, no global beta-conversion decision procedure, no hidden discharge of $D$, and no host normalization theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the candidate-mediated SN finite socket normal form, \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-sn-finite-socket-normal-form}. It identifies the displayed candidate budget as the socket-aware finite rows $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$ together with the four local Phase Real faces, supported only by $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

Next route confluence and decidability through the existing handoff surface. The handoff exhaustion theorem, \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-confluence-decidability-handoff-exhaustion}, confines every confluence-facing or decidability-facing read of $U$ to the rows $O,D,H,C,P,L$ and the remaining packet coordinates $S,N,U$. The budget theorem, \autoref{thm:metaciccriticalpath-confluence-decidability-budget}, then forces those reads to pass through the open source ledger and its displayed BEDC support rows.

Finally compose these two inventories. The candidate budget contributes only the four local faces and the socket-aware packet route; the handoff budget contributes only $U$ and the same transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows. Since neither surface contains a coordinate for an independent confluence theorem, a global decidability procedure, a discharge witness for $D$, or a host normalization theorem, every downstream confluence or decidability use remains inside the displayed budget rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC SN discharge-socket readiness]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-sn-discharge-socket-readiness}

$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L),
$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

It forces every confluence-facing or decidability-facing read using candidate-mediated strong normalization to pass through the displayed packet rows and the four L10 faces before the handoff row can be consumed.

Next apply the candidate-mediated obligation surface, \autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-candidate-mediated-sn-obligation-surface}, and the normalization-frontier dependency exposure, \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-normalization-frontier-dependency-exposure}. Now attach the confluence and substitution boundaries. Finally use the candidate-discharge frontier coverage, \autoref{thm:metaciccriticalpath-candidate-discharge-frontier-coverage}. It preserves the obstruction and socket rows while candidate-mediated SN, confluence-facing, bounded-decidability, and L10 readiness rows are consumed. Thus a consumer reaches the socket only after the named readiness surface is visible, and no consumed row fills $D$ without a separate subject-reduction certificate.
\end{proof}
