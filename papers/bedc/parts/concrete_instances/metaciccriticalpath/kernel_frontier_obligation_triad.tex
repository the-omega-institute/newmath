\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path kernel frontier exposure]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-kernel-frontier-exposure}
For every accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ root-unblock packet
$$

$$
the kernel-facing frontier exposes exactly the finite $\BHist$ rows for closed normalization-frontier source data $S$, the normal-form endpoint $N$, the consistency-facing refusal rows $O,D$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and local $\NameCert_{\MetaCICCriticalPathUp}$ naming $L$. The frontier has no row for closed subject reduction, full closed consistency, hidden scheduler state, or a host reachability edge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier inventory of \autoref{def:metacic-critical-path-packet}. The displayed tuple is the only packet read available to the kernel frontier, and its coordinates already name the closed normalization-frontier rows, the normal-form endpoint, the refusal rows, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Use the public route certificate and obstruction scope separation. The route certificate reads every public kernel-facing path through $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$, while the obstruction separation keeps the consistency-facing refusal rows $O,D$ visible rather than turning them into subject reduction or full consistency.

Thus exposure is a projection from the finite packet followed by route replay. Since the carrier has no additional coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the kernel frontier.
\end{proof}
