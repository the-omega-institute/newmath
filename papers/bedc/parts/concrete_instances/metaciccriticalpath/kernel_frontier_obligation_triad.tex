\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-kernel-frontier-exposure}

$$

$$

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier inventory of \autoref{def:metacic-critical-path-packet}. Use the public route certificate and obstruction scope separation. The route certificate reads every public kernel-facing path through $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$, while the obstruction separation keeps the consistency-facing refusal rows $O,D$ visible rather than turning them into subject reduction or full consistency.

Thus exposure is a projection from the finite packet followed by route replay. Since the carrier has no additional coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the kernel frontier.
\end{proof}

\end{proof}
