\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path real-completion source budget]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-confluence-decidability-source-budget}
Any $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ consumer attempting a candidate-mediated strong-normalization, confluence, or decidability handoff must first display the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ source rows named by the current Phase Real and Completion ledger. \end{theorem}

Begin with root-unblock admission. Theorem~\ref{thm:metaciccriticalpath-root-unblock-admission} requires any candidate-mediated strong-normalization, confluence, or decidability admission to expose the four L10 source rows before a candidate checker row is consumed.

Next read the Phase Real source obligations. The existing source ledger then supplies the finite route. Definition~\ref{def:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-ledger} and Theorems~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-closure} and~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-consumer-totality} keep every current Phase Real and Completion read inside the packet rows and the four displayed source faces.

Finally attach the MetaCIC side. The confluence-decidability budget and handoff-exhaustion theorems require the closure trace, critical-pair ledger, normalization frontier, discharge socket, and budget rows to remain downstream of the source ledger. Since the route has no additional coordinate, the excluded metatheoretic and completion claims cannot be exported.
\end{proof}
