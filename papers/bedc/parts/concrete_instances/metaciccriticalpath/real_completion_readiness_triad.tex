\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path StreamName source readiness]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-real-streamname-source-readiness}
For every Real-facing or Completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ critical-path packet
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L),
$$
the $\StreamNameUp$ source schedule may be exposed only after the consumer has displayed the MetaCIC packet rows $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$ and the Phase Real route budget rows attached to that packet. In particular, the source schedule is not a host scheduler coordinate, an undisplayed stream source, a hidden completion selector, closed subject reduction, or full consistency.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the root-unblock carrier and route surfaces. Theorems~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-root-unblock-carrier-readiness} and~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-root-unblock-route-readiness} expose $K$ as the finite displayed packet and keep route replay inside the same rows.

Next apply the Phase Real route budget. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-route-budget} requires every Real-facing and Completion-facing consumer to read $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$ before it can use a StreamName schedule row.

Finally use consumer readiness and the two Phase Real locks. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-root-unblock-consumer-readiness} excludes host scheduler state and hidden route closures, while Theorems~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-normal-form-handoff} and~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-obstruction-ledger-lock} keep the normal-form and obstruction rows attached to the route. Thus the $\StreamNameUp$ source schedule is exposed only as a displayed dependency row after the MetaCIC packet rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path RegSeqRat readback readiness]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-regseqrat-readback-readiness}
For every Real-facing or Completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet $K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)$, a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback can be consumed only as a displayed regular-rational dependency row attached to the same packet. The readback cannot be detached into quotient stream equality, selected limit data, hidden Real completeness, closed subject reduction, or full consistency.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the root-unblock packet. Theorems~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-root-unblock-carrier-readiness} and~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-root-unblock-route-readiness} make the carrier and replay rows visible as $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$.

The Phase Real route budget then fixes the readback order. By Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-route-budget}, the regular-rational dependency row appears only after the displayed MetaCIC packet rows have been admitted.

The normal-form handoff and obstruction ledger lock preserve the same attachment downstream. Theorems~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-normal-form-handoff} and~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-obstruction-ledger-lock} keep $N,O,D,H,C,P,L$ present at the Real and Completion boundary, and Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-root-unblock-consumer-readiness} refuses undisplayed dependency edges. Hence the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback is consumed only as a displayed row of the same critical-path route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path Real completion readiness package]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-real-completion-readiness}
For an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet $K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)$, the Real-facing and Completion-facing root-unblock package is exactly the combination of the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ source schedule readiness, the displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback readiness, and the $\RealUp$ seal route read after the Phase Real budget rows. The package exports no ambient Real completeness, quotient stream equality, selected limit, closed subject reduction, full consistency, or hidden completion selector.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Build the StreamName side first. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-real-streamname-source-readiness} confines the source schedule to a displayed row admitted after $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$.

Build the regular-rational side next. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-regseqrat-readback-readiness} confines the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback to the same critical-path packet and refuses quotient-stream, selected-limit, and hidden-completeness exits.

Finally attach the Real-facing seal route. The Phase Real route budget, normal-form handoff, and obstruction ledger lock from Theorems~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-route-budget},~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-normal-form-handoff}, and~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-obstruction-ledger-lock} keep the $\RealUp$ route downstream of the displayed packet while preserving $N,O,D,H,C,P,L$. With root consumer readiness from Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-root-unblock-consumer-readiness}, the resulting package is a finite root-unblock surface for Phase Real and Completion consumers, not a closure of any excluded metatheoretic or completion principle.
\end{proof}
