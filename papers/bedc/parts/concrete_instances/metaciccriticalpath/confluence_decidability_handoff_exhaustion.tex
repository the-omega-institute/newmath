\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path confluence-decidability handoff exhaustion]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-confluence-decidability-handoff-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L),
$$
every confluence-facing or decidability-facing downstream read of $U$ factors through the displayed rows $O,D,H,C,P,L$ together with the finite packet rows $S,N,U$. The handoff exports no arbitrary Church--Rosser theorem, no global beta-conversion decision procedure, no closed subject reduction, and no full closed consistency.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the socket theorem. By \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-confluence-decidability-socket}, every confluence-facing or decidability-facing use of the handoff row is mediated by the obstruction, discharge socket, transport, route, provenance, and local naming rows $O,D,H,C,P,L$.

Next exhaust the packet route. The handoff exhaustion theorem \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-handoff-exhaustion} says that the finite route has only the rows $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$ and contains no coordinate for closed subject reduction, full closed consistency, global beta-conversion decidability, or quotient term equality.

The local socket boundary supplies the negative side. Apply \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-discharge-socket-locality} to keep $D$ local to $H,C,P,L$ and separate from any hidden discharge, then apply \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-normalization-obstruction-route-coverage} to keep the obstruction row visible along normalization-to-normal-form reads.

Therefore a downstream read of $U$ has only the displayed finite data available: $O,D,H,C,P,L$ for the socket-mediated handoff and $S,N,U$ for the remaining packet coordinates. Since no further theorem-export coordinate is present, the four excluded metatheoretic claims remain outside the handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path candidate-mediated SN boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-sn-boundary}
For every accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L),
$$
any candidate-mediated strong-normalization-facing read of the handoff row factors through the displayed packet rows $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$ together with the four Phase Real faces $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ recorded by Definition~\ref{def:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-ledger}. The read exports no arbitrary strong-normalization theorem, no Church--Rosser theorem, no beta-conversion decision procedure, no quotient term equality, no host real equality, no selected limit, and no $\RealUp$ completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply the confluence-decidability handoff exhaustion theorem, Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-confluence-decidability-handoff-exhaustion}. It bounds every confluence-facing or decidability-facing use of the handoff row by the mediated rows $O,D,H,C,P,L$ and the remaining packet coordinates $S,N,U$.

Next attach the current Phase Real source surface. The open-phase source closure theorem, Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-closure}, says that admitted source reads factor through the displayed packet rows and one of the four rows $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, or $\RealUp$. The open-phase consumer totality theorem, Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-consumer-totality}, says that current Phase Real and Completion consumers receive the ledger as their whole source surface.

Finally use the nonescape side of the ledger. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-root-unblock-soundness} and Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-nonescape} exclude quotient real data, host equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, $\RealUp$ completeness, closed subject reduction, and full consistency from the admitted route. Since a candidate-mediated strong-normalization-facing read has no additional coordinate beyond the same packet rows and four ledger faces, the listed SN, Church--Rosser, beta-conversion, quotient, host-equality, selected-limit, and completeness claims remain outside the handoff.
\end{proof}
