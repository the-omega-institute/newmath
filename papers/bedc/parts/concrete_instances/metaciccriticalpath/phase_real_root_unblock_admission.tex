\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path Phase Real root-unblock admission]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-root-unblock-admission}
For every accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet $K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)$, the packet is admissible as a Phase Real root-unblock route exactly when it displays the nine packet rows $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$ together with the four objectwise face records for $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ at the local closure and formal thresholds required by Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-four-face-audit-floor}. The admitted route is read through the open-phase source ledger of Definition~\ref{def:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-ledger}; no aggregate MetaCIC credential may replace any one of the four face records.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For admission from displayed data, start with the packet rows $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-l10-four-object-exit-certificate} attaches the dyadic budget, StreamName schedule, RegSeqRat readback, and Real seal rows to the same accepted packet, and Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-four-face-audit-floor} raises those four rows to the local threshold required by the current Phase Real route.

The open-phase ledger then supplies the route surface. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-closure} says that every admitted source read factors through the displayed packet rows and one of the four named face rows, so the displayed data are sufficient for the root-unblock admission interface.

Conversely, suppose that the packet is admitted as a Phase Real root-unblock route. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-four-face-obligation-surface} forces the four object rows to be presented separately, and Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-status-product-readiness} requires their local status records. The audit floor of Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-four-face-audit-floor} then gives exactly the stated threshold, while Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-four-face-deficiency-refusal} rules out admission with a missing or substituted face.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path Phase Real root-unblock soundness]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-root-unblock-soundness}
Any route admitted by Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-root-unblock-admission} exports only the open-phase source ledger of Definition~\ref{def:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-ledger}. It exports no quotient stream equality, no selected host limit, no countable choice, no trichotomy, no open-cover compactness, no $\RealUp$ completeness, no closed subject reduction, and no full consistency claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the admission theorem. It places every admitted route inside the displayed packet rows and the four objectwise face records at the audit floor, with the open-phase source ledger as the route surface.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-nonescape}. That theorem says that no claim outside the ledger can be exported by the current Phase Real and Completion route, including quotient real data, host equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, and $\RealUp$ completeness.

The remaining exclusions are already part of the finite route boundary. Theorems~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-exit-refusal-boundary} and~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-real-seal-frontier-exactness} keep the route at the four finite source rows and refuse closed subject reduction, full consistency, hidden completion selectors, and theorem rows outside the displayed packet. Hence an admitted root-unblock route exports only the open-phase ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path Phase Real root-unblock completeness]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-root-unblock-completeness}
Every current Phase Real or Completion consumer that displays the packet rows $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$ together with the four local face records for $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ at the threshold of Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-four-face-audit-floor} factors through Definition~\ref{def:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-ledger}. Such a consumer receives no fifth source row and no completion principle beyond the displayed four-face ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First read the consumer through the audit floor. The displayed packet rows and four local face records satisfy the same threshold used by Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-root-unblock-admission}, so the consumer is an admitted Phase Real root-unblock consumer.

Now apply totality of the open-phase ledger. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-consumer-totality} states that every current Phase Real and Completion consumer of an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet reads the ledger as its whole source surface.

Finally use nonescape to close the factorization. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-nonescape} prevents any outside completion principle from being exported, and Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-open-phase-source-closure} identifies every admitted source read with one of the packet rows or one of the four displayed face rows. Therefore the consumer factors through the ledger and receives no fifth source row.
\end{proof}
