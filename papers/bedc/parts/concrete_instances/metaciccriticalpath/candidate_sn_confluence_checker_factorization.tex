\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC candidate SN confluence checker factorization]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-sn-confluence-checker-factorization}
Every consumer that combines candidate-mediated strong normalization, the confluence handoff, and bounded checker rows for an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)
$$
must factor through the concrete rows $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$ and the L10 four-face ledger
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \StreamNameUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp .
$$
The factorization may read candidate-mediated normal-form evidence, the confluence handoff, and the bounded checker budget only along that route. It cannot discharge subject reduction, fill the open discharge socket $D$, assert global beta-conversion decidability, introduce quotient term equality, select a limit, or import ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the candidate-mediated strong-normalization surface. By \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-sn-confluence-budget} and \autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-candidate-mediated-sn-obligation-surface}, the consumer must display $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$ together with the four L10 faces before any confluence-facing or decidability-facing read is available.

Next read the confluence handoff. The row $U$ is available only as part of the same packet route, while $D$ remains an open discharge socket rather than a subject-reduction proof or a closed conversion theorem.

Finally read the bounded checker budget. The checker rows are downstream of the displayed handoff and replay rows, so they can compare only the finite ledger already carried by $K$ and the L10 faces. Since the carrier has no coordinate for discharged subject reduction, global beta-conversion decidability, quotient equality, selected limits, or ambient Real completeness, the combined consumer factors through the stated route.
\end{proof}
