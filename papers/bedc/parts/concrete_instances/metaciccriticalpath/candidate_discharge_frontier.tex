\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path candidate-discharge frontier]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-discharge-frontier}
Let
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)
$$
be an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ root-unblock packet. Candidate-mediated strong-normalization rows, confluence rows, and bounded-decidability rows may be consumed by the root-unblock route only while the subject-reduction obstruction row $O$ and the discharge socket $D$ remain visible through the same packet. These candidate rows do not fill $D$ unless a separate subject-reduction certificate row is displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the consistency handoff of \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-consistency-handoff}. It permits the positive read from the strong-normalization row $S$ to the normal-form row $N$ only through the displayed route, transport, provenance, and local naming rows.

Next keep the blocked side visible. The obstruction boundary \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-obstruction-boundary}, the NameCert obligation surface \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-namecert-obligation-surface}, and discharge-socket nonescape \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-discharge-socket-nonescape} keep $O$ and $D$ as separate rows of the same accepted packet.

Now consume the candidate evidence. The candidate-mediated SN frontier \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-sn-frontier}, the candidate-mediated SN obligation surface \autoref{thm:metacic-confluence-candidate-mediated-sn-obligation-surface}, and the candidate-mediated subject-reduction boundary \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-sn-subject-reduction-boundary} allow strong-normalization, confluence-facing, and bounded-decidability consumers to inspect the finite candidate rows, but only with $O$ and $D$ still exposed.

Finally apply discharge-socket coverage, \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-discharge-socket-coverage}. A downstream socket read remains inside $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$ unless a separate subject-reduction input is displayed. Hence candidate-mediated SN, confluence, and bounded-decidability rows sharpen the frontier but do not discharge the socket by themselves.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC candidate frontier L10 discharge boundary]
\label{thm:metaciccriticalpath-candidate-frontier-l10-discharge-boundary}
Let
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)
$$
be an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ root-unblock packet. Candidate-mediated strong-normalization and confluence rows may consume the displayed L10 readiness rows $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ as source evidence for the current Phase Real and Completion route. Those four rows do not fill the subject-reduction discharge socket $D$ unless a separate subject-reduction certificate is displayed in the same packet route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the candidate-discharge frontier, \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-discharge-frontier}. It keeps candidate-mediated strong-normalization, confluence-facing, and bounded-decidability rows downstream of the visible obstruction row $O$ and discharge socket $D$.

Read the candidate-mediated SN surface next. Theorems~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-sn-frontier} and~\ref{thm:metacic-confluence-candidate-mediated-sn-obligation-surface} allow the candidate rows to support normalization and confluence consumers, but only as displayed rows inside the accepted packet.

Now attach the L10 four-object route. Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-l10-four-object-exit-certificate} and the source-obligation surface of Theorem~\ref{thm:metacic-critical-path-phase-real-source-obligations} make $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ available as readiness rows for the Phase Real and Completion route.

Finally apply discharge-socket coverage, \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-discharge-socket-coverage}. The four L10 rows supply source readiness, not a subject-reduction certificate. Therefore the socket $D$ remains open until the separate certificate is displayed, and no Real or Completion readiness row can be read as a hidden discharge.
\end{proof}
