\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path substitution-normalization boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-substitution-normalization-boundary}
For every accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L),
$$
any substitution-normalization read accepted through $K$ factors through the displayed packet rows $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$, the obstruction row $O$, and the discharge socket $D$. The read may consume the existing normalization-frontier and route-replay rows, including the substitution handoff carried by $U$, transport through $H$, replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $L$. It does not export beta-substitution preservation, closed subject reduction, full consistency, quotient term equality, or a hidden scheduler route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet carrier from \autoref{def:metacic-critical-path-packet}. The accepted surface is the finite row list $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$, so a substitution-normalization read begins with the displayed handoff row $U$ rather than with an ambient substitution theorem.

The normalization-frontier and replay rows give the permitted route. Apply \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-normalization-frontier-factorization}, \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-route-replay-coverage}, and \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-substitution-handoff-visibility}. These keep the read on the source and normal-form rows, route it through $H,C,P,L$, and identify substitution only as the bounded handoff coordinate $U$.

The blocked side remains visible. By \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-consumer-refusal-exactness} and \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-certificate-row-order}, omitting $O$ or $D$ refuses the stronger read while leaving the bounded route to $N$ intact.

Finally use the discharge-obligation and subject-reduction boundaries, \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-discharge-socket-nonescape} and \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-obstruction-boundary}. They prevent the substitution-normalization route from filling $D$, erasing $O$, or becoming beta-substitution preservation, closed subject reduction, full consistency, quotient term equality, or a hidden scheduler route.
\end{proof}
