\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC critical-path normalization source visibility]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-normalization-source-visibility}
Every consistency-facing read of an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet $K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)$ must expose the closed-strong-normalization source row $S$ before it reads the normal-form row $N$. A read that starts at $N$ without $S$ is not a read of the accepted root-unblock packet.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.MetaCICCriticalPathUp.MetaCICCriticalPathPacket\_normalization\_source\_visibility}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier through \autoref{def:metacic-critical-path-packet}. The packet displays $S$ as the closed-strong-normalization route and $N$ as the normal-form consistency row, with no abbreviated carrier containing only $N$.

Apply \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-consistency-handoff} and \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-normal-form-readback-boundary}. The handoff routes consistency-facing consumers from $S$ to $N$, while the readback boundary stops the exported claim at $N$.

The obstruction and socket rows stay attached by \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-obstruction-boundary} and \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-discharge-socket-nonescape}. Hence the read of $N$ is visible only after $S$ has been displayed inside the same packet.
\end{proof}
