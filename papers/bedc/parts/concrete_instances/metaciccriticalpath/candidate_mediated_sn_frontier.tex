\begin{definition}[MetaCIC candidate-mediated SN frontier]
\label{def:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-frontier}
For an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L),
$$
the candidate-mediated strong-normalization frontier is the finite subroute that displays the strong-normalization row $S$, the normal-form endpoint $N$, the handoff row $U$, the discharge socket $D$, the componentwise transport row $H$, the continuation row $C$, the provenance row $P$, and the local naming row $L$, together with the four local source faces $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ already recorded for the open-phase source ledger. The frontier may be consumed only as a boundary between the candidate-mediated SN evidence and the confluence-decidability handoff; it contains no coordinate for closed subject reduction, arbitrary strong normalization, global confluence, global beta-conversion decidability, quotient term equality, host equality, selected limits, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC candidate-mediated SN frontier]\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-sn-frontier}
Every candidate-mediated strong-normalization read of an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet that is passed to the confluence-decidability handoff must factor through the frontier of \autoref{def:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-frontier}. In particular, the read must display $S,N,U,D,H,C,P,L$, keep the obstruction row $O$ and discharge socket $D$ available to the surrounding packet, and expose the four local faces $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ before any confluence-facing or decidability-facing consumer reads the handoff row $U$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the candidate-mediated SN finite socket normal form, \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-sn-finite-socket-normal-form}. It confines the candidate-mediated read to the socket-aware packet rows $S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L$ together with the four local source faces.

Next apply the SN confluence-decidability budget theorem, \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-sn-confluence-budget}. It requires any confluence-facing or decidability-facing consumer to read the same candidate budget surface before it consumes the handoff row $U$.

The frontier definition simply names the common boundary needed by these two routes: the candidate-mediated read supplies $S,N,U,D,H,C,P,L$ and the four local faces, while the enclosing packet still retains $O$ and $D$ as visible blocked-route data. Since neither cited theorem supplies a coordinate for closed subject reduction, arbitrary strong normalization, global confluence, global beta-conversion decidability, quotient equality, host equality, selected limits, or $\RealUp$ completeness, the factorization cannot export any of those claims.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC SN confluence decidability handshake]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-sn-confluence-decidability-handshake}
Let
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)
$$
be an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet. If a closed-normal-form membership read first uses candidate-mediated strong normalization and then passes to the confluence-decidability handoff, the whole read factors through $S,N,U,H,C,P,L$ together with the four L10 faces
$$
\DyadicRatCoreUp,\qquad
\StreamNameUp,\qquad
\RegSeqRatUp,\qquad
\RealUp .
$$
The obstruction row $O$ and discharge socket $D$ remain visible and unfilled unless the same packet also displays a separate closed subject-reduction row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the candidate-mediated frontier. By \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-sn-frontier}, the strong-normalization read is confined to the displayed frontier rows and the four L10 faces before any confluence-facing or decidability-facing consumer can inspect the handoff row $U$.

Next apply \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-confluence-decidability-consumer-boundary}. The consumer may use the existing handoff row and route closed-normal-form membership through $C,P,L$, but it receives no closed subject-reduction coordinate and no global beta-conversion decision procedure.

The discharge boundary is preserved by \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-sn-discharge-row} and \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-discharge-socket-induction}. These results keep $O$ and $D$ visible while finite frontier rows are replayed and prevent the SN evidence from filling the socket by itself.

Finally use \autoref{thm:metacic-critical-path-candidate-normalization-socket-exhaustion}. The exhausted read surface is exactly the displayed $S,N,U,H,C,P,L$ route plus $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$. Hence the handshake factors through those rows and leaves $O,D$ open unless a separate closed subject-reduction row is present.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC confluence-decidability consumer boundary]\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-confluence-decidability-consumer-boundary}

\begin{proof}

Finally keep the socket and obstruction boundaries visible. The frontier does not fill $D$, and the handoff theorem does not erase $O$ or convert $U$ into a global theorem. Therefore the consumer obtains only the finite candidate-mediated boundary plus the existing handoff route, while the excluded subject-reduction, consistency, Church--Rosser, beta-decidability, socket-discharge, and completeness claims remain outside the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC discharge-socket induction]
\label{thm:metacic-critical-path-discharge-socket-induction}
Let
$$
K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)
$$
be an accepted $\MetaCICCriticalPathUp$ packet, and let $F$ be a finite closed-term frontier whose rows replay the candidate-mediated strong-normalization row $S$, the normal-form endpoint $N$, the handoff row $U$, the componentwise transport row $H$, the displayed continuation row $C$, the provenance row $P$, and the local naming row $L$. If every row of $F$ is consumed through the frontier of \autoref{def:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-frontier}, then induction over $F$ preserves the obstruction row $O$ and keeps the discharge socket $D$ unfilled unless the packet also contains an explicit closed subject-reduction row. In particular, candidate-mediated strong normalization, confluence-facing replay, and decidability-facing replay do not by themselves introduce a closed subject-reduction coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct over the finite frontier rows. The empty frontier exposes no consumer row, so the accepted packet remains exactly $K=(S,N,O,U,D,H,C,P,L)$ with $O$ and $D$ still visible.

For the induction step, project the next closed-term row and the accumulated packet surface. The row is allowed to replay only the displayed $S,N,U,H,C,P,L$ route named by \autoref{def:metacic-critical-path-candidate-mediated-frontier}; it has no coordinate for closed subject reduction.

Use the candidate-mediated SN frontier theorem and the confluence-decidability consumer boundary to keep the replayed row on the same frontier surface. These results route the SN, confluence-facing, and decidability-facing reads through $U$ and $C$ while leaving the obstruction row $O$ and the discharge socket $D$ in the surrounding packet.

The inductive hypothesis applies to the remaining frontier. Since neither the current replay row nor the previous frontier rows add an explicit closed subject-reduction coordinate, no constructor fills $D$. Thus the obstruction row is preserved and the discharge socket stays open until a separate subject-reduction row is supplied.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}
