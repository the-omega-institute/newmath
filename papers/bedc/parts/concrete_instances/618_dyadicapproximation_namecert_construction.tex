\section{Finite dyadic observation packets}
\label{sec:dyadicapproximation-finite-packets}

\begin{definition}[DyadicApproximation carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-approximation-carrier}
A $\DyadicApproximationUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
A=(n,d,w,l,p)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $n$ is a unary precision row naming the requested dyadic accuracy;
\item $d$ is a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ endpoint row whose denominator ledger is readable at the precision named by $n$;
\item $w$ is a certified finite observation window from $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$, displaying the scheduled stream read that produced the endpoint row;
\item $l$ is a ledger tying the finite window to a $\RealUp$ seal candidate by recording the regular-sequence source rows, dyadic-radius budget, and continuation route consumed at the real-name boundary;
\item $p$ is the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance naming the consumed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ certificate surfaces.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares two accepted packets at the same precision when the precision rows are related by displayed $\hsame$, the dyadic endpoint rows are related by the transported $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ classifier, and the finite-window readbacks through $\StreamNameUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ are equivalent after transporting their ledgers componentwise. The carrier contains no quotient of regular sequences, no ambient real endpoint, no hidden completion object, and no selected limit.
\end{definition}
