\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LipschitzRetractionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-lipschitz-retraction-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Banach and Bishop--Bridges metric fixed-point and retraction theory, the $\LipschitzRetractionUp$ packet records the finite certificate by which a metric source retracts onto a displayed subcarrier through a Lipschitz graph and an identity-on-subcarrier row. It sits on the L10 metric and complete-space boundary beside the metric source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the complete-service surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, the continuous-map graph route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuousmap-namecert}, and the located-apartness Real comparison route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-apartness-real-bridge-namecert}. The packet names a constructive finite retraction certificate; it does not assert a selected nearest point, quotient equality of subspaces, ambient completeness of the source, or a classical choice principle.

\begin{definition}[Lipschitz retraction carrier]
\label{def:lipschitz-retraction-carrier}
A $\LipschitzRetractionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(X,A,K,G,I,M,L,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the ambient $\MetricSpaceUp$ source row supplying all source-distance witnesses.
\item $A$ is the subcarrier row, displaying membership, inclusion into $X$, and the inherited distance reads used by the retraction.
\item $K$ is the Lipschitz-constant ledger, recording a rational bound together with the source and target distance reads compared by that bound.
\item $G$ is the retraction graph row, whose source observations lie in $X$ and whose values are admitted by $A$.
\item $I$ is the retraction-identity row, requiring every point already admitted by $A$ to replay through $G$ as the same displayed subcarrier point.
\item $M$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ service row available to consumers that later read Cauchy or endpoint data on the subcarrier.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedApartnessRealBridgeUp$ comparison row used when a distance inequality is inspected through located Real and completion readbacks.
\item $E$ is the $\ContinuousMapUp$ replay row tying the graph $G$ to the finite continuity route induced by the Lipschitz ledger $K$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric source, subcarrier, Lipschitz ledger, graph, identity, complete-metric service, located-apartness comparison, continuous-map replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a retraction request through $X,A,K,G,I,M,L,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LipschitzRetractionUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\LocatedApartnessRealBridgeUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LipschitzRetractionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for a selected best approximation, quotient subspace equality, ambient completeness theorem, global fixed-point theorem, or classical real-order decision.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Lipschitz retraction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:lipschitz-retraction-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LipschitzRetractionUp$ carrier $R=(X,A,K,G,I,M,L,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LipschitzRetractionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed tuple, metric-source visibility through $X$, subcarrier admission and inherited-distance visibility through $A$, Lipschitz-constant readback through $K$, graph admission through $G$, retraction identity through $I$, complete-metric service access through $M$, located-apartness Real comparison through $L$, continuous-map replay through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:lipschitz-retraction-carrier}. The displayed tuple lists exactly the metric source, subcarrier, Lipschitz ledger, graph, identity, complete-service, located-apartness, continuous-map, transport, replay, provenance, and naming rows.

The source-facing obligations are exhausted by $X,A,K,G,I$. The metric row supplies distance witnesses, the subcarrier row supplies admissible values, the Lipschitz ledger supplies the bound, the graph row supplies the finite retraction observations, and the identity row forces admitted subcarrier points to replay unchanged.

The consumer-facing obligations are exhausted by $M,L,E$. A complete-space consumer can inspect only the displayed $\CompleteMetricUp$ service row, a Real comparison can inspect only the $\LocatedApartnessRealBridgeUp$ row, and a continuity consumer can inspect only the $\ContinuousMapUp$ replay induced by the Lipschitz ledger.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name this same finite route. Since the carrier has no selected nearest point, quotient equality, ambient completeness theorem, global fixed-point theorem, or classical comparison branch, no such obligation is present in the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Lipschitz retraction metric factorization]
\label{thm:lipschitz-retraction-metric-factorization}
Every $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, or $\LocatedApartnessRealBridgeUp$ consumer of an accepted $\LipschitzRetractionUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
X,A \longmapsto K,G,I \longmapsto E,L,M \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus source distances are read in the ambient metric row $X$, subcarrier values are admitted through $A$, the Lipschitz graph and identity rows are checked before any continuous-map or complete-service handoff, and located Real inequalities are exposed only through the displayed $\LocatedApartnessRealBridgeUp$ comparison route. The factorization exports a Lipschitz retraction certificate, not a choice-selected projection, quotient subspace equality, or ambient completeness claim.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:lipschitz-retraction-namecert-obligations}. Any consumer first receives the metric source row $X$ and the subcarrier row $A$, since these are the only rows that can supply source distances and admitted retraction values.

The retraction data then pass through $K,G,I$. The Lipschitz ledger bounds graph distances, the graph row records the finite observations, and the identity row verifies that points already in the subcarrier replay as themselves.

Only after those rows are displayed can a consumer read $E,L,M$. The continuous-map row is induced by the same Lipschitz ledger, the located-apartness comparison row reads Real-facing inequalities through the finite readback bridge, and the complete-metric service row is a displayed downstream service rather than an ambient completeness theorem.

Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming are then carried by $H,C,P,N$. These rows preserve the factorization but add no selected projection, quotient equality, host equality, or classical branch.
\end{proof}
