\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger methodology root triad]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-methodology-root-triad}
For every accepted root methodology read of a
$\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ packet
$L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)$, the read exposes the sibling rows
$X,A,O,T,I$, the scope-refusal-upgrade rows $S,D,U$, and the
failure-consumer-provenance-name rows $F,C,Q,N$ inside the same finite carrier.
The transport row $H$ remains part of the same $\BHist/\hsame/\Cont/\Pkg$
support, so the root read cannot replace these rows by a hidden methodology
source, final truth route, observer-privilege route, or off-ledger NameCert.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier and certificate,
\autoref{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-carrier} and
\autoref{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-certificate}. They display
$L$ as a single finite packet and make its classifier read the sibling,
methodology, failure, transport, consumer, provenance, and naming rows through
the same local support.

Apply the NameCert obligations and root audit source lock,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-namecert-obligations} and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-root-audit-source-lock}.
These obligations expose carrier admission, sibling accountability, source
lock, provenance, and local naming before a root methodology read can be
accepted.

Now add the route constraints. Scope-export exactness and domain-leakage
refusal,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-scope-export-exactness}
and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-domain-leakage-refusal},
force the $S,D$ surface to be visible, while upgrade-route determinacy,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-upgrade-route-determinacy},
keeps $U$ inside the same carrier.

Finally use failure-surface exhaustion and consumer-route exactness,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-failure-surface-exhaustion}
and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-consumer-route-exactness}.
They force $F$ and $C$ to be read with the provenance and naming rows already
carried by the packet, so the root methodology triad is a row-wise readback of
$L$ rather than a second authority.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger scope-refusal cover]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-scope-refusal-cover}
For every accepted $\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ packet
$L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)$, every beyond-scope methodology export is
covered by the displayed scope row $S$, refusal row $D$, and upgrade row $U$
before any consumer route $C$ may treat the export as methodology authority.
No consumer route can read a beyond-scope export directly from the sibling,
failure, provenance, or local $\NameCert$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with scope-export exactness,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-scope-export-exactness}.
It requires the displayed scope row $S$ before a methodology export can be
read.

Next apply domain-leakage refusal and upgrade-route determinacy,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-domain-leakage-refusal}
and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-upgrade-route-determinacy}.
They place a beyond-scope attempt in $D$ and any admitted continuation in $U$,
with both rows still belonging to the same packet.

Use the NameCert obligations to keep the consumer read local to the carrier.
The certificate does not provide a separate consumer authority outside
$S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N$.

Finally apply failure-surface exhaustion, consumer-route exactness, and root
export coverage,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-failure-surface-exhaustion},
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-consumer-route-exactness},
and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-root-export-coverage}.
These results require $F$ before $C$ and identify the root export package with
the displayed refusal, upgrade, failure, consumer, provenance, and naming rows.
Hence a consumer route can treat a beyond-scope export as methodology authority
only after the $S,D,U$ cover has already been exposed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger failure-upgrade readback]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-failure-upgrade-readback}
For every accepted $\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ packet
$L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)$, failure rows and upgrade rows are read back
through the same finite carrier. The readback cannot split $F$ into hidden
truth, cannot split $U$ into observer privilege, and cannot route either row
through an off-ledger methodology object before consumer access.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet through the carrier and certificate. The rows $F$ and $U$
are coordinates of the same $\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ object,
with $H,C,Q,N$ supplying the transport, consumer, provenance, and local naming
support.

Apply failure-surface exhaustion and failure-route locality,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-failure-surface-exhaustion}
and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-failure-route-locality}.
They keep defeating tests in $F$ and prevent a consumer-facing route from
turning those tests into hidden truth.

Next apply upgrade-route determinacy and upgrade scope lock,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-upgrade-route-determinacy}
and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-upgrade-scope-lock}. They
keep any admitted upgrade inside $U$ and tie it to the same displayed scope and
refusal surface, not to observer privilege.

Finally combine consumer-route exactness with root audit source lock,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-consumer-route-exactness}
and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-root-audit-source-lock}.
The consumer reads the visible failure and upgrade rows through the same
carrier, provenance, and local NameCert surface, so no split into a hidden
truth route or observer-privilege route is available.
\end{proof}
