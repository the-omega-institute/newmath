\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger refusal upgrade totality]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-refusal-upgrade-totality}
Let $L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)$ be an accepted
$\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ packet. Every refusal-facing or
upgrade-facing methodology read anchored in the finite $\BHist$ carrier is
exhausted by the displayed scope row $S$, refusal row $D$, upgrade row $U$,
componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, consumer route $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance
$Q$, and local $\NameCert$ row $N$. No such read can pass through unbounded
explanation, final model truth, hidden observer privilege, closed empirical
fit, endpoint identity, or an off-ledger methodology source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the carrier and local obligation surface in
\autoref{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-carrier} and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-namecert-obligations}. They
place $S,D,U,H,C,Q,N$ inside the same finite $\BHist$ packet and require scope
exactness, domain-leakage refusal, failure exposure, and ledger non-escape
before methodology authority is consumed.

Next use scope-export exactness, domain-leakage refusal, upgrade-route
determinacy, and upgrade scope lock. These theorems force every beyond-scope
movement to be either refused in $D$ or routed through $U$, while keeping the
route attached to the same $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$
rows.

Finally apply the root refusal and export coverage theorems. They identify the
root read with the displayed carrier surface rather than with a hidden external
methodology source. Since the carrier has no coordinate for unbounded
explanation, final truth, hidden observer privilege, closed fit, or endpoint
identity, the refusal and upgrade rows are total for the accepted read.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger failure consumer coverage]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-failure-consumer-coverage}
Let $L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)$ be an accepted
$\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ packet. Every consumer route $C$ that
reads methodology authority from the finite $\BHist$ ledger must expose the
failure row $F$, the sibling rows $X,A,O,T,I$, the refusal and upgrade rows
$D,U$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, $\Pkg$ provenance $Q$, and local
$\NameCert$ row $N$ before the consumer is accepted.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-carrier}. The explanation,
audit, objectivity, tower-compression, open-fit, refusal, upgrade, failure,
transport, consumer, provenance, and naming rows are displayed together in one
packet.

Apply the NameCert obligations of
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-namecert-obligations}. The
sibling-accountability and failure-surface clauses put the defeating tests in
$F$ before the consumer route is accepted, while ledger non-escape prevents
$C$ from importing hidden authority.

Now use failure-surface exhaustion, failure-route locality, and consumer-route

$\BHist/\hsame/\Cont/\Pkg/\NameCert$ support and route them through

The public failure-consumer and root export coverage theorems close the root

so every accepted consumer route is covered by the displayed failure and
methodology rows.
\end{proof}
