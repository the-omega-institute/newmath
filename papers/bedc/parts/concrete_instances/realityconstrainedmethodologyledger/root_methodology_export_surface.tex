\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger export accountability]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-export-accountability}
For every accepted $\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ packet
$L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)$, any methodology export from $L$ must expose
the sibling rows $X,A,O,T,I$, the scope row $S$, the refusal and upgrade rows
$D,U$, the failure row $F$, and the support rows $H,C,Q,N$. The export is
therefore a read of the displayed $\BHist/\hsame/\Cont/\Pkg/\NameCert$
surface, not a read of an omitted methodology coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of
\autoref{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-carrier}. It displays the
five sibling rows, the scope-refusal-upgrade surface, the failure row, and the
transport, consumer, provenance, and naming rows in one finite packet.

Apply the NameCert obligations of
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-namecert-obligations}.
Carrier admission, sibling accountability, scope exactness, domain-leakage
refusal, failure exposure, and ledger non-escape all bind the same displayed
rows before a methodology claim is accepted.

Finally use public export readiness and sibling independence,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-public-export-readiness}
and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-sibling-independence}.
They keep the export on the public carrier surface and prevent any single
sibling from replacing the full methodology ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger upgrade-refusal totality]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-upgrade-refusal-totality}
Let $L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)$ be an accepted
$\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ packet. Every methodology export
beyond the displayed scope row $S$ is either refused in the domain-leakage row
$D$ or routed through the upgrade row $U$ before any consumer route $C$ may
treat it as methodology authority.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with scope-export exactness and domain-leakage refusal,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-scope-export-exactness}
and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-domain-leakage-refusal}.
They require the scope row before export and reject a beyond-scope claim that
lacks the displayed refusal and extension surface.

Use source-refusal boundary next,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-source-refusal-boundary}.
A beyond-scope read of the sibling source rows must be witnessed in $D$ before
the consumer route may accept it.

Finally apply upgrade-route determinacy and root export coverage,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-upgrade-route-determinacy}
and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-root-export-coverage}.
They rule out deleting, replacing, or bypassing the carrier, leaving only the
displayed refusal row $D$ and upgrade row $U$ as accepted beyond-scope routes.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained methodology-ledger authority nonescape]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-authority-nonescape}
For every accepted $\RealityConstrainedMethodologyLedgerUp$ packet
$L=(X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N)$, no unbounded explanation, final model truth,
hidden observer privilege, closed empirical fit, endpoint/source identity, or
off-ledger authority can be read from the packet. Any accepted methodology
authority must factor through the displayed rows
$X,A,O,T,I,S,D,U,F,H,C,Q,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier and certificate,
\autoref{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-carrier} and
\autoref{def:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-certificate}. The carrier
has no coordinate for unbounded explanation, final model truth, hidden observer
privilege, closed empirical fit, endpoint/source identity, or off-ledger
methodology authority, and the certificate preserves only the displayed rows.

Apply consumer-route exactness and failure-route locality,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-consumer-route-exactness}
and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-methodology-ledger-failure-route-locality}.
They keep consumer authority and defeating tests inside the visible carrier
instead of turning either one into a hidden truth or observer-privilege route.

Finally use the export-accountability and upgrade-refusal-totality surfaces of
this file. Every methodology export exposes the packet rows, and every
beyond-scope export is covered by $D$ or $U$, so there is no remaining route
through which off-ledger authority can escape the finite NameCert packet.
\end{proof}
