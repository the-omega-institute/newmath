\begin{definition}[Helly--Bray selection carrier]
\label{def:helly-bray-selection-carrier}
A $\HellyBraySelectionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
H=(B,J,S,R,E,T,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\BoundedVariationUp$ source row, carrying the bounded-variation object, finite variation ledger, and endpoint budget exposed to the selection request.
\item $J$ is the $\JordanVariationDecompositionUp$ row, displaying the signed-variation split and the positive and negative variation rows read from $B$.
\item $S$ is the family of $\StreamNameUp$ finite windows opened by the inspected variation rows and by the downstream Real-facing request.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to those windows, recording the rational comparisons used before any Real seal is visible.
\item $E$ is the finite family of $\RealUp$ seal rows reached only after $B,J,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $T$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the bounded-variation source, Jordan rows, finite windows, readbacks, Real seals, continuation, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from bounded-variation data through Jordan signed-variation rows, finite windows, regular rational readback, Real seals, transport, provenance, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\HellyBraySelectionUp$, $\BoundedVariationUp$, $\JordanVariationDecompositionUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HellyBraySelectionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $B,J,S,R,E,T,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for host weak convergence, measure completion, quotient function equality, selected subsequences, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Helly--Bray selection NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:helly-bray-selection-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\HellyBraySelectionUp$ carrier
$$
H=(B,J,S,R,E,T,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\HellyBraySelectionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the bounded-variation source row, Jordan signed-variation decomposition row, StreamName finite windows, RegSeqRat readback row, Real seal rows, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item variation-source exposure, requiring every selection-facing read to begin with $B$ and then pass through the signed-variation rows $J$ rather than through a host weak-convergence theorem;
\item finite-window locality, requiring $S$ to list the exact stream windows opened by the variation rows and the Real-facing request;
\item readback ordering, requiring $R$ to consume the displayed windows in $S$ before any row of $E$ is exposed;
\item componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming for the same rows through $T,C,P,N$;
\item nonescape, excluding host weak convergence, measure completion, quotient function equality, selected subsequences, countable choice, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{enumerate}
No Helly--Bray selection obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The only source coordinate is the bounded-variation row $B$, and the only signed-variation coordinate is the Jordan row $J$.

The finite observation surface is then forced by row order. The StreamName windows $S$ are opened from the displayed variation rows, the RegSeqRat readback $R$ reads only those windows, and the Real seals $E$ appear only after that readback has been displayed.

Transport and replay preserve the same route componentwise through $T$ and $C$. Provenance $P$ and local naming $N$ record that route as the local certificate surface.

Since none of the excluded objects occurs as a carrier coordinate, a consumer cannot add weak convergence, measure completion, quotient function equality, selected subsequences, countable choice, or ambient Real completeness to the local NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:helly-bray-selection-finite-window-route}

$$
\BoundedVariationUp \longmapsto \JordanVariationDecompositionUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp \longmapsto \RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp \longmapsto T,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the consumer receives bounded-variation source data, Jordan signed-variation rows, finite StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, Real seals, componentwise transport, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming. The route supplies no host weak convergence, measure completion, quotient function equality, selected subsequence, countable-choice schedule, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

The finite-window part is local. The windows $S$ are the only observation rows available to the packet, and $R$ is the only regular rational readback attached to those windows.

The Real-facing boundary is reached only at $E$. The remaining rows $T,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route; they do not add a new selection principle.

Because the carrier omits host weak convergence, measure completion, quotient function equality, selected subsequences, countable choice, and ambient Real completeness, every accepted consumer read is exhausted by the displayed finite-window route.
