\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement selector-budget meet exhaustion]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-selector-budget-meet-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet
$$
R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
whose two source schedules share the meet row $U$ and selector-budget row $T$, every $\RealUp$-facing or completion-facing read is exhausted by the route
$$
M_0,M_1 \longrightarrow U \longrightarrow V \longrightarrow T \longrightarrow W \longrightarrow Q \longrightarrow E.
$$
No accepted consumer may replace $U$ by a second meet, replace $T$ by a hidden selector, or replace $Q,E$ by a quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyModulusRefinementUp.CauchyModulusRefinementCarrier\_selector\_budget\_meet\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
First bind the source side. The shared-meet dependency row \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-shared-meet-dependency} forces both source schedules $M_0,M_1$ to pass through the displayed meet row $U$ before the extraction row $V$ can be read.

Next bind the selector side. The selector-budget dependency row \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-selector-budget-dependency} places the finite selector budget $T$ between $V$ and the selected-window and tolerance-readback rows $W,Q$.

Now attach the Real-facing route. The Real seal route row \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-seal-route} places $E$ only after $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q$, and the non-escape theorem \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-nonescape} removes every unlisted meet, selector, quotient stream, host equality, selected limit, and ambient completeness coordinate from the accepted packet.
\end{proof}
