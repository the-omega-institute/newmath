Hence none of the excluded coordinates can provide just one of the RegSeqRat, StreamName, or Real projections while omitting the other two.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement RegSeqRat--Real selector lockstep]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-regseq-real-selector-lockstep}
For an accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ route displaying
$$
M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N,

\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}

They bind the displayed route to the source rows $M_0,M_1$, the shared meet $U$, the extraction row $V$, and the selector-budget row $T$ before any of the three consumer projections can be read.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-selector-nonempty-route}. The selected-window and tolerance-facing reads must expose the nonempty selector-budget route through $T,W,Q$ before a projected tail or seal can be consumed.

Finally invoke \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-regseq-real-selector-exactness}. Since the RegSeqRat tail read, StreamName selected-window read, and Real seal read are already exact projections of the same selector route through $U,V,T,W,Q,E$, changing one projection would change the shared route and therefore the other two projections as well.
\end{proof}
