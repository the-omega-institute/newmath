\section{L10 window lattice pointer}
\label{sec:cauchymodulusrefinement-l10-window-lattice-pointer}

The $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ L10 ledger is the shared route consumed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-completion-frontier-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. Its source side is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulusmeet-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulusextraction-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyselectorbudget-namecert}; its finite-window and rational-readback side is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}.

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement L10 window lattice exactness]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-window-lattice-exactness}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet
$$
R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
every $\RealUp$-facing or Completion-facing route through $R$ factors first through the source meet, extraction, and selector-budget rows $U,V,T$, then through the four L10 faces
$$
\StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \RealUp,
$$
and then only through the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows $H,C,P,N$. The route exports no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, hidden selector, second meet row, countable-choice row, bridge aggregation, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or ambient completion principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyModulusRefinementUp.CauchyModulusRefinementL10WindowLatticeExactness}
\begin{proof}
Start with the L10 sibling route of \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-sibling-route}. It places every Phase Real and Completion consumer after the source comparison, meet, extraction, and selector-budget rows, and orders the four downstream faces as $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.

Next pull the consumer back along the finite packet rows. The completion pullback theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-completion-pullback}, reads the route first through the source-window front $M_0,M_1,U,V$, then through $T,W,Q,E$, and finally through the structural rows $H,C,P,N$. The one-line ledger anchors above identify those rows with the CauchyModulusMeet, CauchyModulusExtraction, RegularCauchySelectorBudget, StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and Real sibling surfaces.

Finally apply L10 row exactness, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-row-exactness}. It identifies the public inventory with the selector path, the four L10 faces, and the displayed support rows. Since the accepted packet and those anchors contain no quotient stream equality, selected limit, host real equality, hidden selector, second meet row, countable-choice row, bridge aggregation, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or ambient completion principle, no additional Real-facing or Completion-facing lattice route remains.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement L10 root-window pullback exhaustion]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-root-window-pullback-exhaustion}
For every Phase Real or Completion consumer of an accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet
$$
R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the consumer route is exhausted by the following finite pullback spine. It first passes through the root source-window front $M_0,M_1,U,V$ of \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-window-exhaustion}; it then passes through the selector and L10 face rows $T,W,Q,E$ governed by \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-exit-criterion}; and it finally passes through the support rows $H,C,P,N$ fixed by \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-window-lattice-exactness} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-completion-pullback}. Conversely, any displayed packet with exactly those rows repacks as the same L10 consumer route, with no additional quotient-stream, selected-host-limit, countable-choice, ambient-completeness, metric-completeness, bridge-aggregation, or hidden-selector row.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyModulusRefinementUp.CauchyModulusRefinementCarrier\_l10\_root\_window\_pullback\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
The forward direction begins at the accepted packet $R$. Root-window exhaustion forces the source side through $M_0,M_1,U,V$, so a consumer cannot enter by a second meet row, an unstated extraction row, or an ambient completion premise.

After that source window has been fixed, the L10 four-face exit criterion determines the only Real-facing exit surface. The selector row and the StreamName, DyadicRatCore, RegSeqRat, and Real rows are therefore exactly $T,W,Q,E$, and there is no room for quotient streams, selected host limits, countable choice, host $\RealUp$ equality, or a hidden selector.

The completion pullback and the window-lattice exactness theorem then identify the remaining support as $H,C,P,N$. These rows account for the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support data, so metric-completeness rows and bridge aggregation are not part of the consumer route.

Conversely, suppose a displayed packet carries exactly $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$. Read the first four rows as the root source-window front, the next four as the selector and four-face L10 exit, and the last four as the support ledger. The cited exactness and pullback results repack these finite rows into the same L10 route, and the excluded rows have no constructor in the displayed inventory.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement L10 finite-request route monotonicity]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-finite-request-route-monotonicity}
Let
$$
R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
and
$$
R'=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T',W',Q',E',H,C,P,N)
$$
be accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packets with the same displayed source-meet, extraction, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ support rows. If the finite request displayed by $T'$ refines the finite request displayed by $T$ along the selector row over the same $M_0,M_1,U,V,H,C,P,N$, then the L10 route for $R$ is included in the L10 route for $R'$ on the same support: the $\StreamNameUp$ window row $W$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $Q$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, and the $\RealUp$ seal route $E$ are preserved by inclusion through the displayed $T \leq T'$, $W \leq W'$, $Q \leq Q'$, and $E \leq E'$ rows. No quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice row, ambient completeness row, or host equality supplies a second route between the two requests.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyModulusRefinementUp.CauchyModulusRefinementCarrier\_l10\_finite\_request\_route\_monotonicity}
\leanvariant{BEDC.Derived.CauchyModulusRefinementUp.CauchyModulusRefinementL10FiniteRequestRouteMonotonicity}
\begin{proof}
First fix the common source side. L10 window lattice exactness, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-window-lattice-exactness}, makes every Real-facing or Completion-facing read pass through $M_0,M_1,U,V$ and then through the same displayed support rows $H,C,P,N$.

Now compare the selector rows. Root-window pullback exhaustion, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-root-window-pullback-exhaustion}, says that the consumer route has no source other than the finite spine $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$. Replacing $T$ by a displayed refinement $T'$ over the same source rows can therefore only enlarge the finite request already present in that spine.

Window-tolerance exactness, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-window-tolerance-exactness}, transports this selector inclusion to the StreamName window and DyadicRatCore tolerance rows. The same displayed history and classifier support then carries the regular-rational readback row, so the route through $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ is preserved by inclusion.

Finally apply Real seal factorization, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-seal-factorization}. The $\RealUp$ seal is downstream of the selected window and rational readback route, so the seal route is included along $E \leq E'$ and cannot be replaced by a quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice row, ambient completeness row, or host equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement L10 face-order rigidity]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-face-order-rigidity}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet
$$
R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
any $\RealUp$-facing or Completion-facing consumer of $R$ must read the L10 faces, after the source rows $U,V,T$, in the displayed dependency order
$$
\StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \RealUp.
$$
A proposed consumer that swaps the stream schedule, dyadic tolerance, regular readback, or $\RealUp$ seal face is not an accepted pullback of the refinement packet. In particular, such a consumer cannot be completed by quotient streams, host real equality, selected limits, countable choice, ambient completeness, metric-completeness rows, bridge aggregation, or hidden selectors.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyModulusRefinementUp.CauchyModulusRefinementL10FaceOrderRigidity}
\begin{proof}
The window-lattice exactness theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-window-lattice-exactness}, fixes the only Real-facing and Completion-facing route through $R$: the source meet, extraction, and selector-budget rows precede the L10 faces, and those faces are displayed in the order $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$.

Root-window pullback exhaustion, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-root-window-pullback-exhaustion}, identifies every accepted consumer with that finite pullback spine. The root-window theorem \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-window-exhaustion} supplies the same source-window front, while \autoref{thm:real-l10-four-face-exit-criterion} supplies the four-face exit criterion. Thus the stream schedule must be available before dyadic tolerance, dyadic tolerance before regular readback, and regular readback before the $\RealUp$ seal.

Suppose a proposed consumer swaps one of these four faces. Then its entry point is not the source-window front followed by the four-face exit specified above, so it is not one of the pullbacks exhausted by the accepted packet rows. The excluded resources have no constructor in the packet inventory: quotient streams and host real equality would bypass the StreamName and Real seal faces; selected limits and countable choice would bypass the dyadic and regular-readback rows; ambient completeness, metric-completeness rows, bridge aggregation, and hidden selectors would add rows absent from $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement L10 support row minimality]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-support-row-minimality}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet
$$
R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
let $F$ be an accepted $\RealUp$-facing or Completion-facing consumer of $R$ that already factors through the source rows $M_0,M_1,U,V$ and the L10 face rows $T,W,Q,E$. Then the remaining support of $F$ is exactly $H,C,P,N$: $H$ supplies componentwise $\hsame$ transport along the displayed histories, $C$ supplies the displayed $\Cont$ replay, $P$ supplies the $\Pkg$ provenance row, and $N$ supplies the local $\NameCert$ row. Removing $H$, $C$, $P$, or $N$ loses respectively the history-transport, continuity-replay, package-source, or local-name obligation. Adding any further support coordinate gives an off-packet route, hence is excluded by L10 window lattice exactness and root-window pullback exhaustion.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyModulusRefinementUp.CauchyModulusRefinementL10SupportRowMinimality}
\begin{proof}
Fix the accepted packet $R$ and the consumer $F$. The hypotheses have already placed $F$ through the source rows and through the L10 face rows, so the only undecided part of the route is its support ledger.

By L10 window lattice exactness, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-window-lattice-exactness}, every such consumer reaches support only through the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. Root-window pullback exhaustion, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-root-window-pullback-exhaustion}, then identifies the whole consumer with the finite spine $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$ and leaves no second support coordinate outside that spine.

Project the four support rows. The row $H$ is the componentwise history and $\hsame$ transport needed to carry source equality through the route; without it the consumer has no displayed transport witness. The row $C$ is the continuity replay row; without it the route has no displayed $\Cont$ replay for the completion-facing read. The row $P$ records the $\Pkg$ provenance of the source data; without it the consumer cannot cite the package source. The row $N$ is the local $\NameCert$ row; without it the consumer has no certified name for the packet instance.

Finally apply face-order rigidity, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-face-order-rigidity}, to keep the support ledger downstream of the fixed source and face order. Dropping any one of $H,C,P,N$ removes the corresponding obligation just projected, while adding a further coordinate would be a route not present in the exact finite pullback inventory. Hence the accepted support ledger is precisely $H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Cauchy modulus refinement L10 window lattice downstream coverage]
\label{lem:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-window-lattice-downstream-coverage}
Let $c$ be an accepted local consumer of an accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet with displayed L10 window lattice
$$
R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N).
$$
Then the consumer-facing read made by $c$ is exhausted by the finite alternatives, ledger rows, and coverage rows already displayed in $R$. More precisely, the source-facing alternatives are only $M_0,M_1,U,V$; the selector and L10 ledger rows are only $T,W,Q,E$ in the downstream order
$$
\StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \RealUp;
$$
and the coverage rows are only the displayed $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ support carried by $H,C,P,N$. Consequently a quotient stream equality, selected host limit, host real equality, hidden selector, second meet row, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, bridge aggregation, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or ambient completion principle has no accepted local consumer coordinate in the $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Fix the accepted local consumer $c$ and the displayed packet $R$. By \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-window-lattice-exactness}, any $\RealUp$-facing or Completion-facing route through $R$ must first read the source meet, extraction, and selector-budget rows, then the four L10 faces, and then the support rows $H,C,P,N$. Thus the consumer-facing route of $c$ has no starting coordinate outside the displayed source, face, and support surfaces.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-root-window-pullback-exhaustion} to identify the whole route with the finite pullback spine. The first segment is the root source-window front $M_0,M_1,U,V$; the second segment is the selector and L10 face block $T,W,Q,E$; and the terminal support segment is $H,C,P,N$. The converse part of that theorem says that a displayed packet with exactly these rows repacks as the same L10 consumer route, so the listed alternatives are not merely sufficient rows but the complete accepted inventory for $c$.

The three parts of the read are therefore forced. A source-facing demand can only land on $M_0,M_1,U,V$. A downstream analytic demand can only pass through the selector row and the four displayed faces $T,W,Q,E$ in the order fixed by the window lattice. A coverage demand can only use the displayed history, transport, continuation, provenance, and naming rows carried by $H,C,P,N$.

It remains to inspect the excluded alternatives. Each excluded object named in the statement would require either an additional source coordinate, an alternate L10 face, or an additional support row beyond $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$. The exactness theorem excludes such additions from every $\RealUp$-facing or Completion-facing route, and the pullback exhaustion theorem excludes them from the repacked finite spine. Hence every accepted local consumer is covered by the displayed finite rows, and every excluded alternative remains outside the accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement formal target surface]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-formal-target-surface}
Every accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ window-lattice consumer reaches the $\RealUp$ seal only through the displayed finite route
$$
\CauchyModulusMeetUp,\CauchyModulusExtractionUp,\RegularCauchySelectorBudgetUp
\longmapsto
\StreamNameUp,\DyadicRatCoreUp,\RegSeqRatUp,\RealUp
\longmapsto
\hsame,\Cont,\Pkg,\NameCert .
$$
Equivalently, for a packet $R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)$, the consumer route is exhausted by the modulus-meet rows $M_0,M_1,U$, extraction row $V$, selector-budget row $T$, StreamName window row $W$, DyadicRatCore tolerance row $Q$, RegSeqRat readback and Real seal row $E$, and the support rows $H,C,P,N$. No quotient stream equality, selected host limit, host real equality, hidden selector, second meet row, countable-choice row, metric-completeness row, bridge aggregation, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, or ambient completion principle supplies an alternate formal target surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use L10 window lattice exactness. It places every Real-facing or Completion-facing read through the source meet, extraction, and selector-budget rows before the four L10 faces and support rows are available.

Next apply root-window pullback exhaustion and finite-request route monotonicity. The pullback theorem identifies the finite spine $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$, while monotonicity says refinements enlarge only the displayed request route and do not create a second source of Real-facing data.

Then use face-order rigidity and support-row minimality. The faces must be read in the order $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$, and the remaining support is exactly $H,C,P,N$ for $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$.

Finally apply downstream coverage. Every accepted local consumer is already covered by those source, face, and support rows, so the excluded quotient, selection, host-equality, hidden-selector, second-meet, choice, completeness, bridge, and ambient-completion alternatives have no coordinate in the formal target surface.
\end{proof}
