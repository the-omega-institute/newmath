\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement root-window selector uniqueness]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-window-selector-uniqueness}
Let
$$
R=(M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
and
$$
R'=(M_0',M_1',U',V',T',W',Q',E',H',C',P',N')
$$
be accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ packets. Suppose the two packets expose the same source schedules, shared meet, selector budget, finite $\StreamNameUp$ window, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ readback, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and structural rows along the displayed root route. Then every $\RealUp$ or Completion-facing consumer classifies $E$ and $E'$ as the same consumer-visible Real seal under that route.

No Real completeness principle, quotient stream, selected host limit, countable-choice row, host real equality, hidden selector, or second meet row is used in the classification.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply root route determinacy, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-route-determinacy}. Agreement on the source schedules and shared meet places both accepted packets on the same displayed route through $U,V,T$ before a finite window or Real seal can be read.

Next use the Real-window budget pullback and terminal consumer theorems, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-window-budget-pullback} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-window-terminal-consumer}. These force every $\RealUp$ or Completion-facing consumer to read the same finite request, window, dyadic readback, regular handoff, selector, disclosure, and support rows before it reaches the seal.

Finally invoke L10 row exactness, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-row-exactness}. The L10 consumer inventory is exactly the displayed route with $H,C,P,N$, so two accepted packets agreeing on those route rows expose one consumer-visible Real seal surface. Since none of the excluded rows is present in the carrier, the uniqueness claim does not use Real completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement selector threshold exhaustion]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-window-selector-threshold-exhaustion}
Every accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ route whose public consumer reaches a $\RealUp$ or Completion seal through the displayed root-window selector is exhausted by the finite rows
$$
M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
Deleting $U$, $T$, $W$, or $Q$ breaks the route before the seal can be consumed: $U$ supplies the selector budget, $T$ fixes the public threshold handoff, $W$ exposes the finite window, and $Q$ exposes the dyadic readback required by the public consumer.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyModulusRefinementUp.CauchyModulusRefinementPacket\_window\_selector\_threshold\_exhaustion}
\begin{proof}
First apply root route determinacy, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-route-determinacy}. It places every accepted route to a Real or Completion seal on the displayed root route through $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$.

Next use root budget consumer exhaustion, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-budget-consumer-exhaustion}. The budget-facing consumer cannot reach the seal before reading the selector budget and its finite-window support, so deleting $U$ or $W$ interrupts the route before any public endpoint is available.

Then apply root threshold public exhaustion, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-threshold-public-exhaustion}. The public threshold leg must pass through $T$ and the dyadic readback row $Q$ before it can expose the seal; removing either row leaves only a partial route.

Finally invoke the local selector uniqueness theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-window-selector-uniqueness}. Once the route rows are fixed, all Real or Completion-facing consumers read the same displayed seal surface. Thus the listed rows exhaust the accepted public route, and the four named deletions break it before seal consumption.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement Real-seal route nonescape]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-seal-route-nonescape}
Every accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ route that reaches a $\RealUp$ or Completion seal through the root-window selector is confined to the rows
$$
M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
Preserving the seal preserves the selector budget $U$, the threshold handoff $T$, the finite window $W$, and the dyadic readback $Q$. Such a route cannot read a Real completeness principle, quotient stream, selected host limit, countable-choice row, host real equality, hidden selector, or second meet row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply root route determinacy, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-route-determinacy}. It places every accepted route to a Real or Completion seal on the displayed root route before any terminal consumer can inspect the seal.

Next use real-window budget pullback and the real-window terminal consumer theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-window-budget-pullback} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-window-terminal-consumer}. These force the consumer-facing seal to retain the selector budget, finite window, dyadic readback, and public threshold handoff as displayed rows.

Now invoke L10 row exactness, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-l10-row-exactness}. The L10 inventory is exactly the carrier rows $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$, so no hidden completeness, quotient, host-limit, choice, equality, selector, or second-meet coordinate can be read.

Finally apply selector threshold exhaustion, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-window-selector-threshold-exhaustion}. Deleting $U$, $T$, $W$, or $Q$ breaks the route before the seal can be consumed, hence preserving the seal preserves those four rows and the accepted route is nonescaping.
\end{proof}
