\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus refinement bridged-route surface]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-bridged-route-surface}
Every standard bridge consumer of an accepted $\CauchyModulusRefinementUp$ refinement packet factors through the same public route
$$
M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
The route first reads the shared $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$ meet, the $\CauchyModulusExtractionUp$ witness, the $\RegularCauchySelectorBudgetUp$ selector budget, the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and the $\RealUp$ seal. It then uses only the primitive $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows together with the sibling dependency lattice recorded by the packet. No bridge consumer receives a hidden selector, quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, second meet row, side-channel completion object, or ambient completeness coordinate outside that route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the root-unblock package of \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-unblock-package}. It assembles the local naming scope, the source-locked selector budget, and the diagonal-consumer boundary into one public entry route for downstream Real-completion consumers.

Next apply the diagonal-consumer boundary and the Real-completeness handoff, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-diagonal-consumer-boundary} and \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-completeness-handoff}. These force every standard bridge read to pass through the shared meet, extraction witness, selector budget, selected window, dyadic tolerance, regular readback, and Real seal before it can consume the completion-facing surface.

The root readback stability theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-root-readback-stability}, keeps the selected window and tolerance readbacks on the same $W,Q,H,C,P,N$ route under the componentwise transport and continuation rows. The real-consumer coverage theorem, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-consumer-coverage}, then exhausts the Real and Completion consumers by that displayed route.

Finally use the primitive scope and sibling dependency lattice already carried by the chapter. They identify the only admissible bridge coordinates as the primitive $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, $\NameCert$ rows and the named $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$, $\CauchyModulusExtractionUp$, $\RegularCauchySelectorBudgetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ siblings. Since non-escape excludes the listed hidden and ambient coordinates, every standard bridge consumer factors through the stated public route.
\end{proof}
