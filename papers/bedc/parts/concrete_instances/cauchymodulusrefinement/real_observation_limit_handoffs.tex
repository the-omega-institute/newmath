\label{cor:cauchy-modulus-refinement-diagonal-budget-cofinality-determinacy}

$$

$$

$$
M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N
$$
and then through the two-seal packet rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-limit-uniqueness-seal-namecert}. No alternate hidden coordinate, duplicate classifier source, selected host limit, quotient stream equality, hidden selector, second meet, or ambient completeness row can supply a different accepted route, normal form, or exactness decision.
\end{corollary}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CauchyModulusRefinementUp.CauchyModulusRefinementCarrier\_diagonal\_budget\_cofinality\_determinacy}
\begin{proof}
Start with diagonal budget cofinality, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-diagonal-budget-cofinality}. Once the shared-meet and selector-budget rows have been read, every diagonal Real-completion consumer is already on the cofinal finite budget route through $M_0,M_1,U,V,T,W,Q,E,H,C,P,N$ and through the displayed $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\CauchyModulusMeetUp$, $\CauchySharedTailModulusUp$, $\RegularCauchySelectorBudgetUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ surfaces. That route exports no choice-selected tail, quotient stream, or ambient completeness theorem.

Now apply the RealLimitUniqueness handoff, \autoref{thm:cauchy-modulus-refinement-real-limit-uniqueness-handoff}. A consumer that preserves the same selected finite windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular readback, and Real seal must first factor through the displayed refinement packet and only then through the two-seal uniqueness packet. The handoff leaves no selected host limit, quotient stream equality, hidden selector, second meet, or ambient completeness row for the consumer to read.

Fix the displayed local rows. A reported route selection is accepted only after the cofinal finite route has supplied the meet, extraction witness, selector budget, selected windows, tolerance ledger, readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name. A reported normal-form readback is the same ordered read, because the RealLimitUniqueness-facing continuation consumes the fixed windows, ledger, readback, and seal before it reaches the two-seal packet. A reported exactness decision is therefore the decision that the consumer use is accepted exactly when it factors through those fixed rows and the downstream two-seal rows.

It remains to rule out duplicate sources for that decision. Any proposed duplicate source is either one of the displayed rows, in which case it is already identified by the fixed cofinal route and the handoff, or it is an unlisted coordinate. The cofinality theorem rejects unlisted diagonal-budget coordinates, and the handoff rejects unlisted uniqueness-facing coordinates. Hence every accepted consumer-facing use chooses the same finite route, normal form, and exactness decision named by the chapter.
\end{proof}
