\begin{definition}[MetaCIC normalization-confluence bridge carrier]
\label{def:metacic-normalization-confluence-bridge-carrier}
A \MetacicNormalizationConfluenceBridgeUp{} carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(N,Q,D,A,T,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $N$ is the $\MetacicNormalizationAuditUp$ row carrying candidate, closed-candidate, candidate-set, application adequacy, Pi adequacy, and no-infinite-reduction reads;
\item $Q$ is the $\MetaCICConfluenceAuditUp$ row carrying checked confluence fragments and the named confluence-boundary inputs;
\item $D$ is the $\SubjectReductionDischargeLedgerUp$ row carrying the beta-substitution, application-argument, lambda-domain, and Pi-domain discharge surface;
\item $A$ is the $\ClosedConsistencyAssemblyUp$ row carrying closedness, typed-normal endpoint, normal false-proof exclusion, and subject-reduction boundary exposure;
\item $T$ is the closed typed term route-correspondence classifier, recording that the normalization row and confluence row speak about the same displayed closed typed term before any consistency consumer reads the packet;
\item $H$ is the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row over $N,Q,D,A,T$;
\item $C$ is the displayed $\Cont$ replay route for normalization, confluence, discharge-ledger, and assembly consumers;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the four sibling packets and the audit-map source route;
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetacicNormalizationConfluenceBridgeUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no host evaluator, quotient equality over reductions, hidden typed-term selector, unconditional confluence theorem, unconditional closed subject-reduction theorem, or closed consistency proof detached from $N,Q,D,A,T,H,C,P,L$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization-confluence bridge NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-confluence-bridge-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted \MetacicNormalizationConfluenceBridgeUp{} carrier, the local $\NameCert$ surface consists of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $B=(N,Q,D,A,T,H,C,P,L)$ as a $\BHist$ packet;
\item normalization-audit row readback through $N$, including Candidate, closed-candidate, candidate-set, App adequacy, and Pi adequacy entries;
\item confluence-audit row readback through $Q$, including checked fragment rows and named confluence-boundary inputs;
\item subject-reduction discharge-ledger readback through $D$, preserving the four preservation-obligation rows without turning them into a closed theorem;
\item closed-consistency assembly readback through $A$, preserving the normal false-proof exclusion and subject-reduction boundary rows;
\item closed typed term route correspondence through $T$, with agreement transported componentwise by $H$;
\item displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $L$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:metacic-normalization-confluence-bridge-carrier}. The rows $N,Q,D,A$ are already distinct sibling packets, and $T$ is the only classifier row that compares their closed typed term routes.

Use the normalization audit obligation surface, the confluence audit obligation surface, the subject-reduction discharge-ledger obligations, and the closed-consistency assembly obligations. These sibling results supply the rows consumed by the bridge, while $H,C,P,L$ preserve transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Because the bridge has no further coordinate, every accepted read is exhausted by the displayed finite packet. In particular the read cannot pass through host complex analysis, quotient equality, a hidden typed-term selector, an unconditional confluence theorem, or an unconditional closed subject-reduction theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[MetaCIC normalization-confluence bridge route boundary]
\label{thm:metacic-normalization-confluence-bridge-route-boundary}
Any accepted bridge packet whose normalization and confluence rows agree on the closed typed term through $T,H,C,P,L$ has no route to an unconditional closed subject-reduction theorem. The packet may hand the displayed discharge-ledger row $D$ to the closed-consistency assembly row $A$, but every such handoff remains inside $N,Q,D,A,T,H,C,P,L$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the agreement first through $T$. It compares the normalization and confluence routes only as displayed closed typed term rows; it does not add a preservation theorem.

Next route the preservation side through $D$. The discharge ledger contains the four named preservation-obligation rows, so a consumer may cite those rows but cannot erase their boundary and obtain closed subject reduction for arbitrary closed proofs.

Finally pass to $A$. The closed-consistency assembly can read the normal false-proof exclusion and the subject-reduction boundary, but it receives them as finite sibling rows under $H,C,P,L$. Thus the bridge remains a route-correspondence certificate rather than an unconditional theorem.
\end{proof}
