\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyNullEquivalenceUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynullequivalence-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\CauchyNullEquivalenceUp$ packet records the constructive criterion that two regular Cauchy real names agree exactly when their displayed pointwise difference is certified by a null sequence. The packet is a finite NameCert boundary: it reads the source regular names of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the difference row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchydifference-namecert}, the zero-tail row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nullsequence-namecert}, the finite equivalence boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyequivalence-namecert}, and the seal rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

It does not add a quotient of Cauchy streams, a selected host limit, a host equality of real numbers, a trichotomy branch, or an ambient completeness theorem. Its role is to name the reusable finite bridge between a displayed difference-null packet and the equality-facing read of regular Cauchy real names.

\begin{definition}[CauchyNullEquivalence carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-null-equivalence-carrier}
A $\CauchyNullEquivalenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
(X,Y,D,Z,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows whose regular Cauchy real names are being compared.
\item $D$ is the accepted $\RegularCauchyDifferenceUp$ row that displays the pointwise difference of $X$ and $Y$, including its aligned $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ subtraction ledger.
\item $Z$ is the $\NullSequenceUp$ zero-tail packet consuming the displayed difference row exported by $D$.
\item $E$ is the equality-facing row, either the $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$ finite-window classifier or the $\RealUp$ seal/equality row that reads the same zero-tail evidence.
\item $H$ records the componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the two source rows, the difference row, the null-sequence row, and the equality-facing row.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers inspect the source rows, the difference packet, the zero-tail packet, and the equality-facing handoff.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RegularCauchyDifferenceUp$, $\NullSequenceUp$, $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyNullEquivalenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier stores one finite criterion surface: equality of the two displayed regular Cauchy real names is read through the same difference row and the same null-sequence packet. It stores no quotient stream, no selected limit, no host real equality, and no ambient completeness coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyNullEquivalence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-null-equivalence-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CauchyNullEquivalenceUp$ carrier $(X,Y,D,Z,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyNullEquivalenceUp}$ surface consists of six finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows, the displayed $\RegularCauchyDifferenceUp$ row, the $\NullSequenceUp$ zero-tail row, the equality-facing $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$ or $\RealUp$ row, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming data;
\item difference exactness, stating that $D$ is the pointwise difference packet for the displayed source rows $X$ and $Y$;
\item zero-tail consumption, stating that $Z$ consumes exactly the difference row exposed by $D$;
\item equality handoff, stating that $E$ reads equality of the two regular Cauchy real names only through the same displayed difference-null route;
\item classifier transport, stating that the source rows, difference row, zero-tail row, and equality-facing row transport componentwise through the stored $\hsame$ rows and displayed $\Cont$ routes;
\item ledger refusal, stating that quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, and ambient real completeness are not packet coordinates.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the tuple of \autoref{def:cauchy-null-equivalence-carrier}. It exposes exactly two regular Cauchy source rows, their displayed difference packet, the zero-tail packet for that difference, the equality-facing classifier or seal row, and the finite transport, continuation, package, and naming rows.

Difference exactness is read from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchydifference-namecert}. The row $D$ already binds the source rows $X,Y$, the aligned $\StreamNameUp$ windows, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ subtraction ledger, and the tolerance ledger for the displayed difference.

Zero-tail consumption is read from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nullsequence-namecert}. The row $Z$ consumes the same difference stream and exposes only the tolerance-window-threshold data allowed by the null-sequence packet.

The equality handoff is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyequivalence-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The equality-facing row $E$ may inspect the finite equivalence classifier or the real seal, but only after the difference row and null-sequence row have been displayed.

Classifier transport is componentwise over $H$ and $C$. Since the carrier has no quotient, selected limit, host equality, trichotomy, choice, or completeness coordinate, the refusal row follows from the inventory.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyNullEquivalence difference-zero route]
\label{thm:cauchy-null-equivalence-difference-zero-route}
For accepted $\CauchyNullEquivalenceUp$ carriers, the equality-facing read of the two regular Cauchy real names and the zero-tail read of their displayed difference are the same finite route. More precisely, a consumer may pass from the source rows $X,Y$ to the equality row $E$ only by first reading the $\RegularCauchyDifferenceUp$ packet $D$ and then the $\NullSequenceUp$ packet $Z$ for the displayed difference row; conversely, any such displayed difference-null route supplies exactly the finite equality handoff recorded by $E$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For the forward direction, start with an equality-facing consumer of $E$. By the carrier definition, $E$ is stored after the source rows $X,Y$, the difference packet $D$, and the zero-tail packet $Z$.

Apply the real-seal and finite-window boundaries of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyequivalence-namecert}. They force the consumer to read equality of regular Cauchy names through the finite comparison surface rather than through a quotient stream or host real equality.

Next use the difference surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchydifference-namecert}. The comparison surface is therefore the displayed pointwise difference of $X$ and $Y$, with its windows and dyadic subtraction ledger supplied by $D$.

Finally apply the zero-tail criterion of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nullsequence-namecert}. The equality read reaches $E$ through the $\NullSequenceUp$ row $Z$ that consumes that same displayed difference.

For the reverse direction, begin with the displayed difference-null route $D,Z$. The row $D$ names the pointwise difference of the two source regular names, and $Z$ certifies that this displayed difference is null.

Read that zero-tail packet through the regular-Cauchy equivalence and real-seal boundaries of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyequivalence-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. This produces exactly the equality-facing handoff row $E$ in the carrier.

No additional analytic row is introduced in either direction. The route remains the finite packet $(X,Y,D,Z,E,H,C,P,N)$, so quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, trichotomy, choice, and ambient completeness remain outside the claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyNullEquivalence downstream equality surface]
\label{thm:cauchy-null-equivalence-downstream-equality-surface}
Any downstream completion or limit consumer that cites a $\CauchyNullEquivalenceUp$ packet receives only the finite equality criterion
$$
(X,Y,D,Z,E,H,C,P,N).
$$
The consumer may use the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ source rows, the $\RegularCauchyDifferenceUp$ displayed difference, the $\NullSequenceUp$ zero-tail row, and the equality-facing $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$ or $\RealUp$ handoff, but it receives no quotient stream, selected host limit, host real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet to the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-null-equivalence-carrier}. The only equality-facing analytic data are the displayed difference row $D$, the null-sequence row $Z$, and the handoff row $E$.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-null-equivalence-namecert-obligations}. The obligation package binds carrier admission, difference exactness, zero-tail consumption, equality handoff, componentwise transport, and ledger refusal inside the same finite packet.

Apply \autoref{thm:cauchy-null-equivalence-difference-zero-route}. It identifies the two directions of the criterion without adding an external real equality principle. Thus downstream consumers inherit one reusable finite route, not a stronger completion or quotient object.
\end{proof}
