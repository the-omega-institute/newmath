It sits over the finite StreamName interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. \begin{definition}[StreamName extensionality carrier]
\label{def:streamname-extensionality-carrier}
A $\StreamNameExtensionalityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(X,Y,W,D,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the two $\StreamNameUp$ source rows being compared.
\item $W$ is the shared finite-window row listing the observed indices and values inspected from both sources.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to the window request when the stream entries feed rational or real-name consumers.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row saying that the paired finite windows give the same regular rational data for the requested precision.
\item $Q$ is the terminal comparison row consumed by a $\RealUp$ seal route after the StreamName and RegSeqRat reads have matched pointwise on the displayed window.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two stream rows, the shared window, dyadic ledger, regular readback, terminal comparison, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite equality request through $W,D,R,Q$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\StreamNameExtensionalityUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\StreamNameExtensionalityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,W,D,R,Q,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host function extensionality, selected infinite streams, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName extensionality NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:streamname-extensionality-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\StreamNameExtensionalityUp$ carrier
$$
E=(X,Y,W,D,R,Q,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\StreamNameExtensionalityUp}$ surface consists exactly of paired StreamName admission for $X$ and $Y$, pointwise finite-window agreement for $W$, dyadic tolerance ledger visibility for $D$, RegSeqRat readback agreement for $R$, terminal Real-facing comparison for $Q$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, sibling provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

No StreamName extensionality obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The two stream names enter only as $X$ and $Y$, and the only equality-facing observation is the displayed finite window $W$.

The dyadic ledger $D$ and regular rational readback $R$ consume that same window before any Real-facing comparison can be read. Thus the packet records pointwise finite-window agreement, not quotient equality of streams.

The terminal row $Q$ is downstream of the StreamName, DyadicRatCore, and RegSeqRat rows. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same route, so the displayed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[StreamName extensionality seal route]
\label{thm:streamname-extensionality-seal-route}
Every accepted $\StreamNameExtensionalityUp$ packet exports equality evidence to $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\RealUp$ consumers only through the finite route
$$
\StreamNameUp \longrightarrow \DyadicRatCoreUp \longrightarrow \RegSeqRatUp \longrightarrow \RealUp .
$$
The route reads paired stream sources, a shared finite window, dyadic tolerance data, regular rational readback, and the terminal comparison row, then preserves the same packet through $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and the local $\NameCert_{\StreamNameExtensionalityUp}$ row. It exports no quotient stream equality, host function extensionality, selected infinite stream, countable-choice selector, or ambient Real completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the paired stream rows $X,Y$. A comparison request can inspect them only through the finite window row $W$, so the equality surface is pointwise and bounded by the displayed request.

The tolerance ledger $D$ and regular readback $R$ turn the same finite window into the data consumed by RegSeqRat-facing routes. The terminal comparison row $Q$ is therefore a seal over a displayed finite-window readback, not an independent infinite-stream equality.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that route. Since the excluded quotient, function-extensionality, selected-stream, choice, and completeness coordinates are absent from the carrier, every RegSeqRat or RealUp equality read factors through the stated finite route.
\end{proof}
