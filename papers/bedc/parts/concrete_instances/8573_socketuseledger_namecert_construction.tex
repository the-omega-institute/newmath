\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SocketUseLedgerUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-socket-use-ledger-namecert}

The $\SocketUseLedgerUp$ packet records visible consumption of an apophatic socket as a finite NameCert event surface. It sits under the name-to-gate discipline of \autoref{ch:visions-apophatic-naming-as-discipline} and consumes the sibling surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticsocket-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-externalitygate-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-negative-name-boundary-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-apophaticgatequestion-namecert}. Those chapters name the socket, attach the gate, refuse hidden supply, and ask the audit question; this chapter records the finite event by which a consumer visibly uses that socket.

\begin{definition}[SocketUseLedger carrier]
\label{def:socket-use-ledger-carrier}
A $\SocketUseLedgerUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
U=(A,S,Q,V,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the $\ApophaticNameUp$ row naming the external supply position;
\item $S$ is the $\ApophaticSocketUp$ row naming the requested socket and its visible use site;
\item $Q$ is the $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$ row asking whether the socket use remained marked;
\item $V$ is the visible-use row recording the accepted consumer read through the socket;
\item $R$ is the refusal ledger for unmarked, off-gate, or internalizing uses of the same requested supply;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for $A,S,Q,V,R$ together with continuation, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which audit consumers read the name, socket, question, visible-use, and refusal rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ApophaticNameUp$, $\ApophaticSocketUp$, $\ExternalityGateUp$, $\NegativeNameBoundaryUp$, $\ApophaticGateQuestionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SocketUseLedgerUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for the requested outside supply itself, no anonymous host import, no hidden theorem premise, and no route by which a visible socket use becomes a positive internal description of the external source.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[SocketUseLedger NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:socket-use-ledger-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:socket-use-ledger-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\SocketUseLedgerUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
U=(A,S,Q,V,R,H,C,P,N),
$$
apophatic-name exposure through $A$, socket exposure through $S$, gate-question exactness through $Q$, visible-use readback through $V$, refusal-ledger coverage through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ route replay through $C$, dependency provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the displayed packet. The tuple $U$ lists the apophatic name, socket, gate question, visible-use row, refusal ledger, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

The name-to-gate portion is read through $A,S,Q$. The apophatic name gives the external supply position, the socket row gives the use site, and the gate question records the public test for marked use.

The event portion is read through $V$ and $R$. A visible use is accepted only when it passes through the named socket and gate question; an unmarked or internalizing request is sent to the refusal ledger.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ provide the only transport, replay, provenance, and naming routes. Since the carrier contains no requested outside supply, the local certificate records use of the socket without internalizing the supply.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[SocketUseLedger visible-use non-escape]
\label{thm:socket-use-ledger-visible-use-nonescape}
Every accepted socket-use read of a $\SocketUseLedgerUp$ packet factors through $\ApophaticNameUp$, $\ApophaticSocketUp$, $\ExternalityGateUp$, and the local visible/refusal ledger before any audit consumer sees it. The exported read remains inside $A,S,Q,V,R,H,C,P,N$ and cannot export the requested supply, an unmarked selector, a hidden axiom channel, a quotient collapse, proposition-extensionality collapse, or a host-level replacement for the gate answer.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:socket-use-ledger-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes the name row $A$, socket row $S$, gate-question row $Q$, visible-use row $V$, refusal row $R$, and structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:socket-use-ledger-namecert-obligations}. A consumer read reaches $V$ only after the name, socket, and gate question have been displayed, and any off-gate request is routed to $R$.

Now read the audit-facing rows. The continuation row $C$ exposes the finite use/refusal result, while $H,P,N$ transport, package, and name the same packet. No excluded supply or host principle appears among the coordinates, so the accepted socket-use read cannot escape the ledger.
\end{proof}
