\begin{theorem}[Locally convex functional-analysis forward link]
\label{thm:locally-convex-space-functional-analysis-forward-link}
Every $\FunctionalAnalysisUp$ consumer that cites the $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$ packet must first read the vector-space source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-vecspace-namecert}, the topological-vector-space source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topvecspace-namecert}, the norm-comparison row of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-norm-namecert}, and the functional-analysis boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-functionalanalysis-namecert} through the displayed seminorm ledger $S$ and convex-balanced neighbourhood ledger $B$. The route exports finite local-convexity evidence only through $V,T,S,B,M,H,C,P,N$; it does not export a selected dual, a Hahn--Banach principle, weak-star compactness, or an ambient locally convex topology predicate.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:locally-convex-space-carrier}. Its rows $V,T,M$ are the displayed vector, topological-vector, and norm-comparison sources, while $S$ and $B$ are the only local-convexity ledgers.

The functional-analysis consumer can therefore reach local convexity only after those source rows have been read through the seminorm and convex-balanced neighbourhood data. Transport, replay, provenance, and naming through $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite route, so no dual selector, Hahn--Banach row, weak-star compactness row, or host topology predicate is introduced.
\end{proof}
