\begin{definition}[Locally convex space carrier]
\label{def:locally-convex-space-carrier}
A $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(V,T,S,B,M,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $V$ is the $\VecSpaceUp$ source row, including the additive carrier, scalar action, and vector-space laws used by downstream linear consumers.
\item $T$ is the $\TopVecSpaceUp$ source row, recording the topology and the displayed continuity of addition and scalar multiplication.
\item $S$ is the finite seminorm-family ledger, listing the seminorm names, their vector-space source, triangle and scalar rows, and the generated neighbourhood probes accepted in the packet.
\item $B$ is the convex balanced neighbourhood ledger, recording the finite neighbourhood rows certified as convex, balanced, and compatible with the topology read through $T$.
\item $M$ is the $\NormUp$ comparison row, used only when a named normed source is present and only as a comparison into the seminorm ledger rather than as a replacement for local convexity.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $V,T,S,B,M$ and the local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay through the vector source, topological-vector-space source, seminorm ledger, convex balanced neighbourhood ledger, norm comparison, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$, $\VecSpaceUp$, $\TopVecSpaceUp$, $\NormUp$, $\FunctionalAnalysisUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocallyConvexSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through $V,T,S,B,M,H,C,P,N$. It contains no coordinate for an arbitrary dual selector, a host locally convex topology predicate, an unrestricted separation theorem, weak-star compactness, quotient completion, or a global choice principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Locally convex space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:locally-convex-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$ carrier $L=(V,T,S,B,M,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocallyConvexSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of vector-space source exposure through $V$; topological-vector-space source exposure through $T$; finite seminorm-family evidence through $S$; convex balanced neighbourhood evidence through $B$; optional norm comparison through $M$; componentwise transport through $H$; displayed replay through $C$; provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier in \autoref{def:locally-convex-space-carrier}. The rows $V,T,S,B,M,H,C,P,N$ are exactly the vector, topology, seminorm, neighbourhood, norm-comparison, transport, replay, provenance, and naming coordinates.

The vector and topological-vector rows are consumed first. They supply the additive carrier, scalar action, topology, and continuity obligations before any seminorm or neighbourhood evidence can be read.

The local-convexity evidence is then split across $S$ and $B$. The seminorm ledger records finite probe families, while the neighbourhood ledger records the convex balanced neighbourhood rows generated from those probes.

Finally $M,H,C,P,N$ compare, transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Since no dual selector, separation theorem, weak-star compactness row, or host topology predicate occurs in the carrier, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Locally convex space seminorm-filter stability]
\label{thm:locally-convex-space-seminorm-filter-stability}
Let $L=(V,T,S,B,M,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\LocallyConvexSpaceUp$ carrier. Any consumer that reads a finite seminorm probe from $S$ and then passes to the neighbourhood filter generated by $B$ must still factor through the same rows $V,T,S,B,M,H,C,P,N$. The handoff exports no candidate-free normed-space structure, no selected continuous dual, and no ambient weak topology beyond the displayed finite filter rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with a seminorm probe in $S$. Its vector-space source is the same $V$ read by the carrier, and its topological compatibility is checked against the same $T$ used to admit the packet.

Pass from the probe to the neighbourhood side through $B$. The rows in $B$ record convexity, balancedness, and filter membership as displayed finite evidence, so the handoff remains inside the packet rather than introducing a host filter object.

The comparison row $M$ may relate the probe to a norm row, but it is only a comparison coordinate. Transport and replay through $H$ and $C$, with provenance and naming through $P$ and $N$, preserve the same route. Thus any accepted seminorm-filter read remains mediated by $V,T,S,B,M,H,C,P,N$ and cannot export a selected dual or ambient weak topology.
\end{proof}
