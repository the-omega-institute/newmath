\begin{theorem}[Real tail-agreement seal root window-budget determinacy]
\label{thm:real-tail-agreement-seal-root-window-budget-determinacy}
Let $Q=(R,S,W,D,A,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$ packet. The displayed finite window row $W$ determines the budgeted dyadic agreement route through $D$ and $A$: every root-facing read that has reached $W$ through the admitted observation limiter, regular tail-budget, and shared-threshold surface can reach the agreement row only along
$$
W \to D \to A.
$$
There is no second dyadic readback row, no hidden agreement coordinate, and no selected tail outside the displayed packet route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier $Q$. The only paired window coordinate is $W$, the only dyadic readback coordinate attached to it is $D$, and the only agreement coordinate downstream of that readback is $A$.

Use root budget-selector admission and root budget shared-threshold factorization. They put the root read behind the admitted limiter, tail-budget, selector-budget, and shared-threshold rows before $W$ can be consumed.

Finally apply dyadic readback route totality. Once $W$ is displayed, $D$ is the unique dyadic readback supplied to the local agreement edge, and $A$ is reached only through that readback. Since the remaining rows $H,C,P,N$ provide transport, replay, provenance, and naming, they add no alternate dyadic or agreement route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real tail-agreement seal root criterion handoff]
\label{thm:real-tail-agreement-seal-root-criterion-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ criterion handoff consuming an accepted $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$ packet $Q=(R,S,W,D,A,H,C,P,N)$ factors through the sibling routes $\RegularCauchyTailBudgetUp$, $\ObservationBudgetLimiterUp$, $\RegularCauchyTailMeetUp$, and $\RegularCauchyTailFusionUp$ before it reaches the local route $W \to D \to A$. The handoff is therefore a finite BEDC route through budget, limiter, meet, fusion, paired window, dyadic readback, agreement, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the root budget shared-threshold factorization. It records the observation limiter, regular tail-budget, and tail-meet route as the root-facing entrance to the local paired-window surface.

Attach the tail-fusion consumer through the same shared threshold. The fusion row consumes the already displayed tail-meet and budget rows, so it cannot introduce a second tail source or a separate threshold for the Real or Completion criterion.

Now use root window-budget determinacy. The local criterion handoff continues through $W$, then $D$, then $A$, with $H,C,P,N$ supplying only componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and naming. Thus the criterion handoff factors through exactly the named sibling routes and the local finite packet rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real tail-agreement seal root public consumer non-escape]
\label{thm:real-tail-agreement-seal-root-public-consumer-nonescape}
No public $\RealUp$ or $\CompletionUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RealTailAgreementSealUp$ packet $Q=(R,S,W,D,A,H,C,P,N)$ can bypass the finite route
$$
W \to D \to A
$$
to obtain selected tails, host convergence, quotient stream equality, or ambient completeness. All such consumers receive only the displayed rows $R,S,W,D,A,H,C,P,N$ and the named budget, limiter, meet, fusion, Cauchy-limit, Real, and Completion sibling surfaces.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the root criterion handoff. It forces every Real or Completion criterion read through the budget, limiter, meet, and fusion surfaces before the local paired-window route is public.

Use root window-budget determinacy to identify the local route. The packet exposes $A$ only after $W$ and $D$ have been read, so a consumer cannot jump directly to an agreement row or replace the readback by a quotient stream equality.

Finally apply root budget Completion-consumer exhaustion. The listed sibling surfaces and the displayed carrier rows exhaust the public consumer boundary. Since no row stores a selected tail, host convergence predicate, quotient equality, or ambient completeness principle, no public consumer can bypass $W \to D \to A$ to recover one.
\end{proof}
