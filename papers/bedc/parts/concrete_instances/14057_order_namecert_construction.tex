\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\OrderUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-order-namecert}
\origin{human}

The order interface $\OrderUp$ is the unary comparison face read by the Full Nat Arithmetic boundary. It sits between the $\NatUp$ spine and the $\AddUp$, $\MulUp$, $\IntUp$, and $\RatUp$ arithmetic consumers, exposing finite comparison packets over histories rather than a host natural-number order.

The unary comparison carrier consumes the $\NatUp$ spine of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert}. Its public consumer boundary is read by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-add-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-mul-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-int-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, so the later arithmetic packets depend on $\OrderUp$ only through finite comparison rows over the shared unary history substrate.

\section{Unary comparison packets}

\begin{definition}[OrderUp unary comparison carrier]
\label{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}
The carrier for $\OrderUp$ consists of finite unary comparison packets
$$
\mathsf{OrdRow}(h,k,\beta,\lambda)
$$
where $h$ and $k$ are $\NatUp$ unary histories, $\beta$ is one displayed constructive comparison branch, and $\lambda$ is the finite ledger that justifies that branch. The branch $\beta$ is either a left-prefix witness from $h$ to $k$, a right-prefix witness from $k$ to $h$, or a classifier row $h\hsame k$. The ledger $\lambda$ records only $\mathsf{BHist}$ constructors, $\hsame$ transport, and $\Cont$ prefix rows over the two unary histories.
\end{definition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.OrderUp.TasteGate.OrderUpTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}

\begin{definition}[OrderUp unary comparison classifier]
\label{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier}
Two $\OrderUp$ comparison packets $\mathsf{OrdRow}(h,k,\beta,\lambda)$ and $\mathsf{OrdRow}(h',k',\beta',\lambda')$ are classified together when $h\hsame h'$ and $k\hsame k'$, and the comparison branch transported along those two endpoint classifiers is the displayed branch $\beta'$. The ledger component is compared by replaying the same finite prefix and endpoint rows after this $\hsame$ transport.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp unary comparison NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:order-unary-comparison-namecert-obligations}
The carrier and classifier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier} and \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier} supply the seed $\NameCert_{\OrderUp}$ obligation surface over $\mathsf{BHist}$. The carrier is inhabited by the reflexive packet on $\emp$, classifier reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are inherited from the endpoint $\hsame$ rows together with finite ledger replay, stability follows by transporting prefix and equality branches along $\hsame$, and the ledger records exactly the displayed carrier, branch, transport, and prefix rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For inhabitance, take the packet with $h=k=\emp$, the equality branch $\emp\hsame\emp$, and the empty ledger.

The classifier laws are componentwise. Reflexivity uses the same two endpoints and the same comparison branch. Symmetry reverses the endpoint classifiers and replays the transported branch in the opposite packet direction. Transitivity composes the endpoint $\hsame$ rows and concatenates the finite ledger replays.

For stability, transport the two unary endpoints through the supplied $\hsame$ rows. A left-prefix branch remains a left-prefix branch after endpoint transport, a right-prefix branch remains right-prefix, and an equality branch remains an $\hsame$ branch. Since every ledger entry is one of these finite rows, the transported packet is again an $\OrderUp$ carrier row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp Full Nat Arithmetic handoff]
\label{thm:order-full-nat-arithmetic-handoff}
The unary comparison packet of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}, its classifier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier}, and the seed obligations of \autoref{thm:order-unary-comparison-namecert-obligations} form the $\OrderUp$ face consumed by the Full Nat Arithmetic phase. Thus the $\NatUp$, $\AddUp$, $\MulUp$, $\IntUp$, and $\RatUp$ consumers read order information only as finite unary comparison rows over the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ spine.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier first. Its only endpoints are $\NatUp$ unary histories, and its comparison branch is a finite prefix-or-classifier row over those histories.

Project the classifier next. It transports comparison rows along $\hsame$ on the same two endpoints, so an arithmetic consumer receives a stable comparison interface rather than a second carrier or a host relation.

The seed obligations then provide the NameCert surface needed by the phase boundary: one inhabited carrier row, componentwise classifier laws, transport stability, and a ledger policy. Repacking these projections gives exactly the order face available to the Full Nat Arithmetic consumers listed in the statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp AddMul consumer boundary]
\label{thm:order-add-mul-consumer-boundary}
Every $\AddUp$ or $\MulUp$ consumer of $\OrderUp$ receives only finite unary comparison rows of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier} and classifier transport rows of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier}. In particular, monotonicity or divisibility-order uses may read the displayed prefix branch and its finite ledger, but they do not receive host natural-number order, classical trichotomy, quotient order, or any ordered semiring or ordered ring structure from this chapter.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
An $\AddUp$ consumer reads the two endpoint histories through the additive continuation surface and may attach a comparison packet only after those endpoints are already present as unary histories. The packet contributes the branch and finite ledger, not a host order predicate.

A $\MulUp$ consumer reads the repeated-addition ledger and may attach comparison packets to the displayed factors, partial products, or final product. Each such attachment is again an $\mathsf{OrdRow}$ over unary endpoints with componentwise classifier transport.

Since neither consumer projection introduces a semiring carrier, a ring carrier, a quotient relation, or a classical three-way decision principle, the only order data crossing this boundary are the finite prefix-or-equality rows explicitly stored in the $\OrderUp$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp endpoint transport obligation]
\label{thm:order-endpoint-transport-obligation}
Any accepted $\OrderUp$ packet
$$
\mathsf{OrdRow}(h,k,\beta,\lambda)
$$
transported along endpoint rows $h\hsame h'$ and $k\hsame k'$ remains an $\OrderUp$ packet. Its transported branch is still one of the three branches of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}: a left-prefix witness, a right-prefix witness, or an equality classifier row. Its ledger still contains only $\BHist$ provenance rows, endpoint $\hsame$ rows, and $\Cont$ prefix rows, as required by \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier} and \autoref{thm:order-unary-comparison-namecert-obligations}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier} stores exactly two unary endpoints, one displayed comparison branch, and a finite ledger. The classifier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier} allows transport only through the two endpoint $\hsame$ rows, so the endpoints become $h'$ and $k'$ and no new comparison carrier is introduced.

A left-prefix branch is replayed as a left-prefix branch over the transported endpoints, a right-prefix branch is replayed symmetrically, and an equality branch is replayed by the transported endpoint classifier. This is precisely the stability clause in \autoref{thm:order-unary-comparison-namecert-obligations}.

The ledger replay uses only the original $\BHist$ rows, the two endpoint $\hsame$ rows, and the $\Cont$ rows witnessing the prefix branch. Hence the transported row is again an $\OrderUp$ packet with the same finite ledger vocabulary.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp prefix branch antisymmetry]\label{thm:order-prefix-branch-antisymmetry}
Let $h$ and $k$ be unary $\NatUp$ histories. Suppose one accepted $\OrderUp$ packet displays a left-prefix branch from $h$ to $k$, and another accepted $\OrderUp$ packet displays a right-prefix branch from $k$ to $h$. Then the two endpoints are classified by $h\hsame k$. Equivalently, an arithmetic consumer of the public $\OrderUp$ face cannot use opposite strict-prefix branches as two independent order facts; the paired finite ledgers collapse to the equality classifier row of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read both packets through the carrier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}. The only data available are the endpoints, the displayed branch, and the finite $\BHist$ ledger of $\Cont$ prefix rows and endpoint $\hsame$ rows. By \autoref{thm:order-unary-comparison-namecert-obligations}, no host order or external trichotomy row can enter the comparison.

Induct on the paired unary prefix ledgers. At the empty-prefix boundary, the two displayed branches have no remaining strict $\Cont$ step on either side. The classifier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier} therefore identifies the endpoints by the equality branch $h\hsame k$.

In the successor-prefix case, both opposite branches begin by exposing one shared $\Cont$ step through the unary history spine. Strip that step from the left-prefix ledger and from the right-prefix ledger, apply the induction hypothesis to the two tails, and obtain the tail endpoint classifier. Finally, transport this classifier back across the stripped endpoint rows using \autoref{thm:order-endpoint-transport-obligation}. The transported equality classifier is exactly $h\hsame k$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp Int consumer boundary]
\label{thm:order-int-consumer-boundary}
Every $\IntUp$ order-facing read of $\OrderUp$ factors through signed-pair endpoint comparisons inherited from the $\NatUp$ spine and the $\AddUp$ continuation rows. In the signed-pair chapter \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-int-namecert}, an integer comparison may consume only the $\mathsf{OrdRow}$ packets attached to the displayed positive and negative unary components and their endpoint transport rows. It never imports host integer order, quotient order, ordered ring structure, or a trichotomy principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The $\IntUp$ surface exposes signed pairs built from unary histories, so an order-facing read must first choose the displayed endpoints in those positive and negative components. By \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}, the only comparison datum available on such endpoints is an $\mathsf{OrdRow}$ packet.

The endpoint movement is governed by \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier} and \autoref{thm:order-endpoint-transport-obligation}. Thus the signed-pair consumer can replay comparison rows along endpoint $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ rows inherited from the $\NatUp$ and $\AddUp$ ledgers.

No clause in \autoref{thm:order-unary-comparison-namecert-obligations} exports a host integer relation or quotient order. Therefore the $\IntUp$ consumer boundary is exhausted by finite signed-pair endpoint comparisons.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp Rat consumer boundary]
\label{thm:order-rat-consumer-boundary}
Every $\RatUp$ order-facing read of $\OrderUp$ factors through numerator and positive-denominator unary comparison packets, together with the denominator ledger rows displayed in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}. A rational comparison may consume only $\mathsf{OrdRow}$ packets on the numerator and positive-denominator unary components, endpoint $\hsame$ transport, and denominator-positivity ledger rows. It never imports host rational order, field order, quotient order, ordered-field structure, or classical trichotomy.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The $\RatUp$ carrier presents numerator data and a positive-denominator record as finite unary rows. Hence an order-facing rational consumer must attach comparison information to those displayed unary components rather than to an ambient rational carrier.

By \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}, each such comparison is an $\mathsf{OrdRow}$ with a prefix-or-equality branch. By \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier} and \autoref{thm:order-endpoint-transport-obligation}, transport of that packet uses only endpoint $\hsame$ rows and finite $\Cont$ replay.

The NameCert obligations in \autoref{thm:order-unary-comparison-namecert-obligations} leave no ledger slot for a field order or a host rational trichotomy selector. The denominator contribution is therefore only the finite positivity ledger needed to route the unary denominator endpoint.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp obligation ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:order-obligation-prefix-ledger-exhaustion}
The $\OrderUp$ obligation ledger is exhausted by the two unary endpoints, the prefix-or-equality comparison branch, the endpoint classifier rows, the $\Cont$ replay rows, and the provenance rows naming the originating $\NatUp$ histories. Every $\AddUp$, $\MulUp$, $\IntUp$, or $\RatUp$ consumer read of $\OrderUp$ factors through this finite inventory and no further order datum is present in the NameCert packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}. Its stored data are exactly two unary histories, one displayed branch, and the finite ledger justifying that branch.

The classifier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier} adds only endpoint transport. Once the two endpoint $\hsame$ rows are fixed, the branch replay is forced by the corresponding $\Cont$ prefix rows or by the transported equality row.

The provenance rows record which $\NatUp$ histories the endpoints came from. Therefore any consumer replay that claims to use $\OrderUp$ can be checked against this list: endpoints, branch, endpoint classifier rows, continuation replay, and provenance. There is no remaining slot in the NameCert ledger for a host order, quotient order, trichotomy selector, or ordered-ring import.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp public unary comparison export]
\label{thm:order-public-unary-comparison-export}
The carrier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}, the classifier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier}, the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:order-unary-comparison-namecert-obligations}, the Full Nat Arithmetic handoff of \autoref{thm:order-full-nat-arithmetic-handoff}, the $\AddUp$ and $\MulUp$ boundary of \autoref{thm:order-add-mul-consumer-boundary}, the endpoint transport obligation of \autoref{thm:order-endpoint-transport-obligation}, the prefix branch antisymmetry theorem of \autoref{thm:order-prefix-branch-antisymmetry}, the $\IntUp$ boundary of \autoref{thm:order-int-consumer-boundary}, the $\RatUp$ boundary of \autoref{thm:order-rat-consumer-boundary}, and the ledger exhaustion of \autoref{thm:order-obligation-prefix-ledger-exhaustion} together form the public $\OrderUp$ unary-comparison export. A public arithmetic consumer receives only finite $\BHist$ unary comparison rows with endpoint $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ prefix replay, and equality collapse for opposite prefix ledgers, not host order, quotient order, ordered algebraic structure, or classical trichotomy.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier and classifier first determine the public object being exported: an $\mathsf{OrdRow}$ stores two $\NatUp$ unary histories, one prefix-or-equality branch, and a finite ledger, while classifier transport moves only the two endpoints and replays the displayed branch.

The NameCert obligations and Full Nat Arithmetic handoff then make this packet consumable. They provide the inhabited row, classifier laws, stability clause, and ledger policy needed by the arithmetic phase, without adding a second order carrier or a host relation.

The endpoint transport and prefix branch antisymmetry theorems control branch movement. Endpoint transport preserves the displayed branch under $\hsame$, while antisymmetry collapses opposite finite prefix ledgers to the equality classifier row.

The consumer-boundary theorems identify the public readers. The $\AddUp$ and $\MulUp$ boundary, the $\IntUp$ boundary, and the $\RatUp$ boundary all route their order-facing uses through the same finite packet, endpoint transport obligation, and opposite-prefix collapse.

Finally, ledger exhaustion proves that the exported inventory is complete. Since every accepted consumer read is checked against the endpoints, branch, endpoint classifier rows, continuation replay, and provenance rows, the public surface contains only finite $\BHist$ unary comparison data and excludes host order, quotient order, ordered algebraic structure, and classical trichotomy.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp finite arithmetic mature consumer package]
\label{thm:order-finite-arithmetic-mature-consumer-package}
Every mature Full Nat Arithmetic consumer of $\OrderUp$ factors through the finite unary comparison carrier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}, the endpoint transport obligation of \autoref{thm:order-endpoint-transport-obligation}, the prefix branch antisymmetry theorem of \autoref{thm:order-prefix-branch-antisymmetry}, the $\AddUp$ and $\MulUp$ consumer boundary of \autoref{thm:order-add-mul-consumer-boundary}, the $\IntUp$ consumer boundary of \autoref{thm:order-int-consumer-boundary}, the $\RatUp$ consumer boundary of \autoref{thm:order-rat-consumer-boundary}, and the ledger exhaustion theorem of \autoref{thm:order-obligation-prefix-ledger-exhaustion}. The mature package exposes only finite $\BHist$ unary comparison rows, endpoint $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ prefix replay, equality collapse for opposite prefix ledgers, provenance rows, and the arithmetic consumer routes named above. It does not export host natural order, classical trichotomy, quotient order, ordered semiring structure, ordered ring structure, or field order.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply the public export theorem \autoref{thm:order-public-unary-comparison-export} to identify the available $\OrderUp$ surface. It supplies the unary comparison carrier, classifier obligations, Full Nat Arithmetic handoff, endpoint transport obligation, prefix branch antisymmetry, consumer boundaries, and exhausted finite ledger as one public packet.

The arithmetic consumers are then read through their boundary theorems. Apply \autoref{thm:order-add-mul-consumer-boundary} for the $\AddUp$ and $\MulUp$ routes, \autoref{thm:order-int-consumer-boundary} for signed-pair comparison reads, and \autoref{thm:order-rat-consumer-boundary} for numerator and positive-denominator comparison reads.

Finally, apply \autoref{thm:order-obligation-prefix-ledger-exhaustion}. Since every accepted consumer read is already accounted for by endpoints, branch data, endpoint classifier rows, continuation replay, and provenance, there is no remaining NameCert slot for host natural order, trichotomy, quotient order, ordered semiring or ring structure, or field order.
\end{proof}
