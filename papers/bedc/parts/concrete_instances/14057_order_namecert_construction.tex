\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\OrderUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-order-namecert}
\origin{human}

The order interface $\OrderUp$ is the unary comparison face read by the Full Nat Arithmetic boundary. It sits between the $\NatUp$ spine and the $\AddUp$, $\MulUp$, $\IntUp$, and $\RatUp$ arithmetic consumers, exposing finite comparison packets over histories rather than a host natural-number order.

The unary comparison carrier consumes the $\NatUp$ spine of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nat-namecert}. Its public consumer boundary is read by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-add-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-mul-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-int-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, so the later arithmetic packets depend on $\OrderUp$ only through finite comparison rows over the shared unary history substrate.

\section{Unary comparison packets}

\begin{definition}[OrderUp unary comparison carrier]
\label{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}
The carrier for $\OrderUp$ consists of finite unary comparison packets
$$
\mathsf{OrdRow}(h,k,\beta,\lambda)
$$
where $h$ and $k$ are $\NatUp$ unary histories, $\beta$ is one displayed constructive comparison branch, and $\lambda$ is the finite ledger that justifies that branch. The branch $\beta$ is either a left-prefix witness from $h$ to $k$, a right-prefix witness from $k$ to $h$, or a classifier row $h\hsame k$. The ledger $\lambda$ records only $\mathsf{BHist}$ constructors, $\hsame$ transport, and $\Cont$ prefix rows over the two unary histories.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[OrderUp unary comparison classifier]
\label{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier}
Two $\OrderUp$ comparison packets $\mathsf{OrdRow}(h,k,\beta,\lambda)$ and $\mathsf{OrdRow}(h',k',\beta',\lambda')$ are classified together when $h\hsame h'$ and $k\hsame k'$, and the comparison branch transported along those two endpoint classifiers is the displayed branch $\beta'$. The ledger component is compared by replaying the same finite prefix and endpoint rows after this $\hsame$ transport.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp unary comparison NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:order-unary-comparison-namecert-obligations}
The carrier and classifier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier} and \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier} supply the seed $\NameCert_{\OrderUp}$ obligation surface over $\mathsf{BHist}$. The carrier is inhabited by the reflexive packet on $\emp$, classifier reflexivity, symmetry, and transitivity are inherited from the endpoint $\hsame$ rows together with finite ledger replay, stability follows by transporting prefix and equality branches along $\hsame$, and the ledger records exactly the displayed carrier, branch, transport, and prefix rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For inhabitance, take the packet with $h=k=\emp$, the equality branch $\emp\hsame\emp$, and the empty ledger.

The classifier laws are componentwise. Reflexivity uses the same two endpoints and the same comparison branch. Symmetry reverses the endpoint classifiers and replays the transported branch in the opposite packet direction. Transitivity composes the endpoint $\hsame$ rows and concatenates the finite ledger replays.

For stability, transport the two unary endpoints through the supplied $\hsame$ rows. A left-prefix branch remains a left-prefix branch after endpoint transport, a right-prefix branch remains right-prefix, and an equality branch remains an $\hsame$ branch. Since every ledger entry is one of these finite rows, the transported packet is again an $\OrderUp$ carrier row.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp Full Nat Arithmetic handoff]
\label{thm:order-full-nat-arithmetic-handoff}
The unary comparison packet of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}, its classifier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier}, and the seed obligations of \autoref{thm:order-unary-comparison-namecert-obligations} form the $\OrderUp$ face consumed by the Full Nat Arithmetic phase. Thus the $\NatUp$, $\AddUp$, $\MulUp$, $\IntUp$, and $\RatUp$ consumers read order information only as finite unary comparison rows over the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ spine.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier first. Its only endpoints are $\NatUp$ unary histories, and its comparison branch is a finite prefix-or-classifier row over those histories.

Project the classifier next. It transports comparison rows along $\hsame$ on the same two endpoints, so an arithmetic consumer receives a stable comparison interface rather than a second carrier or a host relation.

The seed obligations then provide the NameCert surface needed by the phase boundary: one inhabited carrier row, componentwise classifier laws, transport stability, and a ledger policy. Repacking these projections gives exactly the order face available to the Full Nat Arithmetic consumers listed in the statement.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp AddMul consumer boundary]
\label{thm:order-add-mul-consumer-boundary}
Every $\AddUp$ or $\MulUp$ consumer of $\OrderUp$ receives only finite unary comparison rows of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier} and classifier transport rows of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier}. In particular, monotonicity or divisibility-order uses may read the displayed prefix branch and its finite ledger, but they do not receive host natural-number order, classical trichotomy, quotient order, or any ordered semiring or ordered ring structure from this chapter.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
An $\AddUp$ consumer reads the two endpoint histories through the additive continuation surface and may attach a comparison packet only after those endpoints are already present as unary histories. The packet contributes the branch and finite ledger, not a host order predicate.

A $\MulUp$ consumer reads the repeated-addition ledger and may attach comparison packets to the displayed factors, partial products, or final product. Each such attachment is again an $\mathsf{OrdRow}$ over unary endpoints with componentwise classifier transport.

Since neither consumer projection introduces a semiring carrier, a ring carrier, a quotient relation, or a classical three-way decision principle, the only order data crossing this boundary are the finite prefix-or-equality rows explicitly stored in the $\OrderUp$ packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp consumer transport obligations]
\label{thm:order-obligation-consumer-transport}
Every $\AddUp$, $\MulUp$, $\IntUp$, or $\RatUp$ consumer that transports an $\OrderUp$ packet may transport it only along the two endpoint classifier rows and the displayed continuation prefix ledgers. Concretely, if the source packet is
$$
\mathsf{OrdRow}(h,k,\beta,\lambda)
$$
and the consumer reads endpoint rows $h\hsame h'$ and $k\hsame k'$, then the transported packet has endpoints $h'$ and $k'$, a branch obtained from $\beta$ by replaying those two $\hsame$ rows, and a ledger obtained by prefixing the finite $\Cont$ rows that witness the consumer route. No consumer receives an additional order carrier, a hidden comparison relation, or an ambient arithmetic order.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The only transport input in \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier} is an endpoint pair of $\hsame$ rows. Thus a consumer cannot change the comparison packet by naming a host predicate; it must first exhibit the two endpoint classifiers.

After the endpoint classifiers are fixed, each displayed branch has a finite replay. A left-prefix branch is replayed by the supplied $\Cont$ prefix rows from the transported left endpoint to the transported right endpoint, a right-prefix branch is replayed symmetrically, and an equality branch is replayed as the transported $\hsame$ row.

The consumer ledger is therefore exhausted by these endpoint and prefix entries. The $\AddUp$ and $\MulUp$ cases read the same finite route through addition and repeated addition, while the $\IntUp$ and $\RatUp$ cases can only attach their signed-pair or denominator records to the already transported unary endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp IntRat comparison boundary]
\label{thm:order-obligation-int-rat-boundary}
Every $\IntUp$ or $\RatUp$ order-facing read is mediated by finite unary comparison packets. An $\IntUp$ signed-pair comparison may read only the comparison packets on the displayed positive and negative unary components, and a $\RatUp$ positive-denominator comparison may read only the comparison packets on the displayed numerator and denominator unary components together with the denominator-positivity ledger. Neither read imports host order, classical trichotomy, quotient order, ordered semiring structure, or ordered-ring structure.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For $\IntUp$, the signed-pair surface exposes the two unary components from which the integer packet is assembled. Any comparison requested by a signed-pair consumer must therefore be routed through the corresponding $\mathsf{OrdRow}$ packets on those components and their endpoint classifiers.

For $\RatUp$, the positive-denominator surface exposes numerator and denominator components together with the finite positivity record for the denominator. A comparison request may combine these displayed rows, but each order-facing step is still an $\OrderUp$ unary comparison packet with a finite ledger.

Since both consumers enter through displayed unary components, neither consumer obtains a quotient comparison, an ambient ordered algebraic structure, or a decision principle outside the stored prefix-or-equality rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[OrderUp obligation ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:order-obligation-prefix-ledger-exhaustion}
The $\OrderUp$ obligation ledger is exhausted by the two unary endpoints, the prefix-or-equality comparison branch, the endpoint classifier rows, the $\Cont$ replay rows, and the provenance rows naming the originating $\NatUp$ histories. Every $\AddUp$, $\MulUp$, $\IntUp$, or $\RatUp$ consumer read of $\OrderUp$ factors through this finite inventory and no further order datum is present in the NameCert packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-carrier}. Its stored data are exactly two unary histories, one displayed branch, and the finite ledger justifying that branch.

The classifier of \autoref{def:order-unary-comparison-classifier} adds only endpoint transport. Once the two endpoint $\hsame$ rows are fixed, the branch replay is forced by the corresponding $\Cont$ prefix rows or by the transported equality row.

The provenance rows record which $\NatUp$ histories the endpoints came from. Therefore any consumer replay that claims to use $\OrderUp$ can be checked against this list: endpoints, branch, endpoint classifier rows, continuation replay, and provenance. There is no remaining slot in the NameCert ledger for a host order, quotient order, trichotomy selector, or ordered-ring import.
\end{proof}
