\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\EffectiveMetricCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-effective-metric-completion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive metric completion, the $\EffectiveMetricCompletionUp$ packet records the finite BEDC route by which located metric data is completed through explicit approximation schedules rather than quotient names or selected representatives. It sits between the metric-completion surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, the located metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedmetric-namecert}, and the Real-and-Completion route through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Effective metric completion carrier]
\label{def:effective-metric-completion-carrier}
An $\EffectiveMetricCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Y=(M,L,A,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ branch consumed from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}, read only as a displayed completion route.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedMetricUp$ source row from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedmetric-namecert}, including the located distance comparisons visible to the packet.
\item $A$ is an effective approximation and modulus ledger, listing finite precision requests, response windows, and the monotone schedule by which later requests refine earlier windows.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ row naming the finite observation windows inspected by the completion route.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for rational regular-sequence data emitted by the approximation ledger.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row used for distance and convergence comparisons after $S$ and $R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the completion branch, located metric source, approximation ledger, stream windows, readback, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay from located metric admission through effective approximation, stream inspection, readback, and terminal Real sealing.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EffectiveMetricCompletionUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\LocatedMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveMetricCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient completion equality, a selected Cauchy representative, host metric equality, countable choice, open-cover compactness, or completeness of $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[EffectiveMetricCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:effective-metric-completion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\EffectiveMetricCompletionUp$ carrier $Y=(M,L,A,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveMetricCompletionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the metric-completion branch and located metric source, effective approximation ledger admission, stream-window readiness, regular-rational readback, terminal Real sealing, componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, local naming, and ledger non-escape for the excluded host-side principles.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:effective-metric-completion-carrier}. The packet lists the completion branch, located metric source, approximation ledger, stream windows, readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The effective approximation row $A$ is downstream of both $M$ and $L$. A consumer must therefore read the completion request through the displayed metric-completion branch and the located metric source before it can inspect any finite precision window.

The numerical route is then forced through $S,R,E$. Stream names provide the finite windows, regular rational sequences provide the readback, and the Real row supplies only the terminal seal for comparisons already exposed by the earlier rows.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same displayed route under transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Since the carrier has no remaining row, quotient completion equality, selected representatives, countable choice, host equality, open-cover compactness, and $\RealUp$ completeness cannot be exported.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[EffectiveMetricCompletion Real-seal handoff]
\label{thm:effective-metric-completion-real-seal-handoff}
Every Real-facing consumer of an accepted $\EffectiveMetricCompletionUp$ carrier factors through the finite handoff
$$
M\longmapsto L\longmapsto A\longmapsto S\longmapsto R\longmapsto E\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
In particular, the terminal $\RealUp$ seal is read only after the metric-completion branch, located metric source, effective approximation ledger, StreamName windows, and RegSeqRat readback have all been displayed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the completion branch $M$ and located source $L$. The carrier exposes no completion-facing request unless those two rows have already been displayed in the same finite history packet.

Next route through the approximation ledger $A$. Its finite modulus entries bind each requested precision to a displayed window, so the consumer cannot choose a hidden representative or import a countable-choice schedule.

The handoff to Real data proceeds through $S$ and $R$. The stream row names the inspected window, and the regular rational row supplies the finite readback consumed by the terminal Real seal.

It remains to check non-escape. Transport, replay, provenance, and local naming keep the same row order, so route exhaustion leaves no coordinate for quotient completion equality, selected representatives, host equality, open-cover compactness, or $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{proof}
