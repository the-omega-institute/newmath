\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BanachFixedPointUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-banach-fixed-point-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\BanachFixedPointUp$ interface names the finite BEDC contraction packet that carries a Banach fixed-point construction as displayed data. Its human reference is Banach's contraction principle, but the certificate records only the rows consumed by BEDC: a Banach carrier row, a complete-metric distance row, a contraction-map row, a rational Lipschitz ledger below the unit bound, a finite Picard ledger, an explicit Cauchy modulus, a regular-rational convergence handoff, a seal-facing real-name endpoint row, transport and continuation rows, package provenance, and a local $\NameCert$ row.

Its dependency list is $\BanachUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\PicardContractionUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$. The finite Picard ledger row $I$ is consumed through the $\PicardContractionUp$ carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-picardcontraction-namecert}, so the fixed-point packet exposes its actual contraction-iteration sibling before any completion handoff is read. The Picard iteration ledger is the concrete contraction-iteration sibling, and the fixed-point packet reaches completion only after consuming \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-picardcontraction-namecert} together with \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert} and the metric carrier.

\begin{definition}[BanachFixedPoint carrier]
\label{def:banach-fixed-point-carrier}
A $\BanachFixedPointUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
F=(B,d,T,\lambda,I,M,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible coordinates:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the $\BanachUp$ carrier row whose state histories provide the domain inspected by the fixed-point packet;
\item $d$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ and $\MetricSpaceUp$ distance row used to read finite iterate distances and the carried completeness surface;
\item $T$ is the contraction-map row displaying the map-code history and the continuation route from a carried state row to the next Picard state;
\item $\lambda$ is a rational Lipschitz ledger with a displayed positivity row and a displayed unit-bound row;
\item $I$ is the finite Picard-iterate ledger, recording $x_0,\ldots,x_n$ together with the application rows for $x_{k+1}=T(x_k)$;
\item $M$ is the explicit $\CauchyModulusUp$ row built from the iterate ledger and the Lipschitz-ratio row;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ and $\CompleteMetricUp$ convergence handoff, exposing the regular rational name and the carried completion route used by consumers;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing endpoint row attached to the handoff;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the Banach, distance, contraction, ratio, iterate, modulus, handoff, endpoint, package, and naming rows;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes for metric reads, contraction application, finite Picard concatenation, modulus readback, completion handoff, and endpoint sealing;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance row naming the $\BanachUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\PicardContractionUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, and $\RealUp$ dependencies;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BanachFixedPointUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host real equality, a choice-selected limit, a global nonlinear-analysis theorem, or ambient completeness outside the carried $\CompleteMetricUp$ row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BanachFixedPoint NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:banach-fixed-point-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\BanachFixedPointUp}$ obligation surface consists of carrier habitation, componentwise classifier transport, finite Picard-iterate stability, explicit Cauchy-modulus construction from the rational contraction ledger, $\CompleteMetricUp$ and $\RegSeqRatUp$ convergence handoff, $\RealUp$ seal non-escape, endpoint determinacy, package dependency stability, and local naming for the finite carrier of \autoref{def:banach-fixed-point-carrier}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet $F$. Carrier habitation is exactly the displayed tuple of Banach, metric, contraction, ratio, iterate, modulus, handoff, endpoint, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows.

Classifier transport is componentwise. The $\hsame$ rows move the displayed histories, while the $\Cont$ rows preserve the metric read, contraction application, Picard concatenation, modulus readback, handoff, and endpoint seal.

The Picard, modulus, handoff, uniqueness-boundary, and export obligations are the visible theorem rows of this chapter. Repacking those rows gives the local naming-certificate surface without adding a hidden fixed point or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BanachFixedPoint iteration ledger]
\label{thm:banach-fixed-point-iteration-ledger}
Every accepted $\BanachFixedPointUp$ carrier exposes a finite $\PicardContractionUp$ ledger $I$ over the carried $\BanachUp$ row $B$, distance row $d$, contraction row $T$, and rational Lipschitz ledger $\lambda$. For each displayed adjacent pair $x_k,x_{k+1}$ in $I$, the application row reads $x_{k+1}$ from $T$ and $x_k$, and the distance comparison is read through $d$ and $\lambda$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BanachFixedPointUp.BanachFixedPointPacket\_iteration\_ledger}
\begin{proof}
Read the $I$ coordinate of \autoref{def:banach-fixed-point-carrier}. It is a finite Picard ledger, so every adjacent pair in the ledger is a visible $\mathsf{BHist}$ row rather than a completed orbit.

The contraction-map coordinate $T$ supplies the application route for each step. The distance row $d$ supplies the metric read, and the rational ledger $\lambda$ supplies the displayed Lipschitz comparison below the unit bound.

Thus the finite iteration surface is exactly the $\PicardContractionUp$ surface carried inside $F$. No infinite sequence object or host fixed-point value is read from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BanachFixedPoint Cauchy handoff]
\label{thm:banach-fixed-point-cauchy-handoff}
For an accepted $\BanachFixedPointUp$ carrier, the finite Picard ledger $I$ and the rational Lipschitz ledger $\lambda$ determine the displayed $\CauchyModulusUp$ row $M$. The convergence handoff $R$ routes this modulus through $\RegSeqRatUp$ and the carried $\CompleteMetricUp$ row $d$, and the endpoint seal $E$ exposes only the resulting $\RealUp$-facing name boundary.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BanachFixedPointUp.BanachFixedPointPacket\_cauchy\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Start with the finite iteration ledger supplied by \autoref{thm:banach-fixed-point-iteration-ledger}. The rational ledger $\lambda$ is stored with a displayed unit-bound row, so repeated contraction estimates produce a finite threshold for each requested rational tolerance.

That threshold is the visible $\CauchyModulusUp$ coordinate $M$. It is constructed from displayed iterate distances and the rational ratio ledger, not from a host convergence theorem.

The handoff row $R$ then records the regular rational name and the carried complete-metric route. The endpoint row $E$ is seal-facing: it is consumable by $\RealUp$ readers only after the modulus and handoff rows have been displayed.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BanachFixedPoint uniqueness boundary]
\label{thm:banach-fixed-point-uniqueness-boundary}
If two accepted $\BanachFixedPointUp$ carriers have the same displayed $\BanachUp$ row, distance row, contraction row, rational Lipschitz ledger, Cauchy-modulus row, and convergence handoff up to the recorded classifier transports, then their $\RealUp$ seal-facing endpoint rows are classifier-determinate. This uniqueness boundary is endpoint determinacy inside the BEDC classifier; it is not a claim of host real equality.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BanachFixedPointUp.BanachFixedPointPacket\_uniqueness\_boundary}
\begin{proof}
Compare the two packets componentwise through their $H$ and $C$ rows. The shared Banach, distance, contraction, ratio, iterate, modulus, and handoff data force the same route to the seal-facing endpoint row.

The usual Banach uniqueness argument is used only as a finite classifier pattern: the contraction ratio below the unit bound collapses two displayed endpoint routes to the same certificate boundary. The conclusion is therefore a statement about the $\RealUp$ seal row after BEDC transport.

Because the carrier never stores a host real object, the theorem cannot export host equality. It records only determinacy of the endpoint row seen by the local classifier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BanachFixedPoint public export]
\label{thm:banach-fixed-point-public-export}
Every public consumer of a $\BanachFixedPointUp$ certificate must factor through the carrier rows

\autoref{thm:banach-fixed-point-iteration-ledger} identifies the finite Picard surface. \autoref{thm:banach-fixed-point-cauchy-handoff} identifies the modulus and convergence handoff. \autoref{thm:banach-fixed-point-uniqueness-boundary} identifies the endpoint determinacy available to the classifier.

These rows repack as the public $\BanachFixedPointUp$ surface. Since no other coordinate is present, the exported certificate cannot be used to read host fixed-point equality, an externally selected limit, global nonlinear-analysis structure, or ambient completeness beyond $d$.

\begin{theorem}[BanachFixedPoint completion handoff scope]
\label{thm:banach-fixed-point-completion-handoff-scope}
The handoff from finite Picard data to the seal-facing endpoint of an accepted $\BanachFixedPointUp$ carrier passes, in order, through the rational Lipschitz ledger $\lambda$, the $\CauchyModulusUp$ row $M$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row $R$, the carried $\CompleteMetricUp$ distance row $d$, and only then the $\RealUp$ endpoint row $E$. This scoped handoff does not expose host fixed-point equality, a choice-selected limit, or ambient completeness beyond the carried row $d$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the finite Picard ledger from \autoref{thm:banach-fixed-point-iteration-ledger}. Its adjacent application rows are read through $T$, while all distance comparisons are read through $d$ and the rational Lipschitz ledger $\lambda$.

Next apply \autoref{thm:banach-fixed-point-cauchy-handoff}. The ratio ledger and finite Picard rows determine the explicit modulus $M$, and the handoff row $R$ routes that modulus through the regular rational name and the carried complete-metric surface.

The endpoint row $E$ is reached only after this handoff route is present. By \autoref{thm:banach-fixed-point-public-export}, there is no exported coordinate for host fixed-point equality, selected limits, or an ambient completeness theorem, so the completion handoff remains confined to the displayed BEDC rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BanachFixedPoint endpoint fixedness]
\label{thm:banach-fixed-point-endpoint-fixedness}
Let
$$
F=(B,d,T,\lambda,I,M,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\BanachFixedPointUp$ carrier. The endpoint row $E$ is classifier-fixed under the carried contraction row $T$: reading $T$ at the endpoint route supplied by the completion handoff $R$, and then sealing the result through the displayed rows $R,E,H,C,P,N$, returns the same $\RealUp$ seal-facing endpoint classifier as $E$. This is a fixed-point statement internal to the BEDC classifier; it does not assert host fixed-point equality or select a limit outside the displayed packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the finite contraction surface. By \autoref{thm:banach-fixed-point-iteration-ledger}, every application row in the Picard ledger is read through the same carried contraction row $T$, with distance comparisons read only through $d$ and $\lambda$.

Use \autoref{thm:banach-fixed-point-cauchy-handoff} to pass from the finite ledger to the displayed modulus $M$ and the handoff row $R$. The route to $E$ is therefore already a route through the contraction ledger, the modulus, and the carried complete-metric surface rather than through an ambient convergence object.

Now compare the endpoint route with the route obtained by one more displayed read of $T$ at that endpoint. The uniqueness boundary of \autoref{thm:banach-fixed-point-uniqueness-boundary} identifies these two seal-facing routes inside the classifier, because they share the same $B,d,T,\lambda,M,R$ data and the recorded componentwise transports.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:banach-fixed-point-completion-handoff-scope}. The sealing operation can pass only through $R,E,H,C,P,N$, so the comparison returns the same $\RealUp$ endpoint classifier and cannot be upgraded into host equality, a choice-selected limit, or an external completeness assertion.
\end{proof}
