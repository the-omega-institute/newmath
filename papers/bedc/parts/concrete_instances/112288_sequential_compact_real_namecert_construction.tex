\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row, recording the finite source windows, requested index windows, and observation schedule.

\begin{theorem}[SequentialCompactReal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:sequential-compact-real-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\SequentialCompactRealUp$ carrier
$$
R=(A,W,Q,I,S,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\SequentialCompactRealUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item real-sequence source admission through $A$ and the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows in $W$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback through $Q$, with rational observations restricted to the displayed windows and tail budgets;
\item compact interval and finite-net admission through $I$, before any cluster read can be public;
\item subsequence-window and cluster comparison through $S$, followed by the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $E$;
\item componentwise transport, displayed replay, provenance, and local naming through $H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation row may be discharged by quotient stream equality, countable choice, a host-selected infinite subsequence, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:sequential-compact-real-carrier}. The packet exposes exactly the real-sequence source, stream windows, regular rational readback, compact interval net, subsequence window, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The source and rational data are finite. The rows $A,W,Q$ name only requested sequence windows and their rational readbacks, so a consumer cannot replace them by a quotient stream or a full sequence object.

The compactness data enter through $I$ and $S$. The interval and finite-net row is displayed before any cluster comparison, and the subsequence row records only finite selected windows with a monotone index ledger.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name that same packet. Since the excluded host principles are absent from the carrier, the five displayed obligation rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}
