\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BishopCompletionModulusUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bishopcompletionmodulus-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\BishopCompletionModulusUp$ packet names the finite completion-modulus route that turns a displayed regular rational Cauchy input into a Real-facing seal. It refines the modulus surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchymodulus-namecert}, reads finite observation windows through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, uses the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, records dyadic thresholds through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and reaches the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet supplies an L10 completion-modulus object without quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Bishop completion modulus carrier]
\label{def:bishop-completion-modulus-carrier}
A $\BishopCompletionModulusUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(M,S,n,k,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusUp$ row, read as a monotone finite modulus interface for rational Cauchy observations.
\item $S$ is the underlying finite $\StreamNameUp$ schedule whose requested windows are admitted by the modulus row.
\item $n$ is the unary precision request and $k$ is the displayed threshold row selected by $M$ for that request.
\item $W$ is the finite stream window of $S$ read at and beyond the threshold $k$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ threshold ledger containing the dyadic tolerance, endpoint, comparison, and scale rows used by the selected window.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row obtained by repacking $W$ with the dyadic threshold ledger $D$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $M,S,n,k,W,D,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the modulus row, stream schedule, request, threshold, window, dyadic ledger, regular handoff, real seal, continuation, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from the request through the modulus row to the window, from the window through the dyadic ledger to the regular handoff, and from the handoff to the real seal.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopCompletionModulusUp$, $\CauchyModulusUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCompletionModulusUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only through these rows. It contains no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a selected completed limit, countable choice, a host real value, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop completion modulus NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-completion-modulus-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopCompletionModulusUp$ carrier $Q=(M,S,n,k,W,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCompletionModulusUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed twelve-row packet;
\item monotone finite modulus readback, stating that the threshold $k$ is read from the displayed $\CauchyModulusUp$ row $M$ at the precision request $n$ and is preserved by the stored transport rows;
\item dyadic threshold compatibility, stating that the window $W$ and ledger $D$ use the same threshold and tolerance scale;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, stating that $R$ is obtained only from the displayed stream window and dyadic threshold ledger;
\item $\RealUp$ seal non-escape, stating that the seal row $E$ is reachable only after the regular handoff and exports no quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice row, host real, or ambient completeness witness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.BishopCompletionModulusUp.BishopCompletionModulusTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishop-completion-modulus-carrier}. The displayed modulus, stream schedule, request, threshold, window, dyadic ledger, regular handoff, real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows are the only classifier-visible coordinates.

The modulus readback is finite. The row $M$ supplies the threshold $k$ for the request $n$, and the transport rows in $H$ preserve that same threshold when the packet is compared with another accepted carrier.

The dyadic and regular faces are forced to use the same source data. The ledger $D$ records the tolerance scale for the window $W$, and the handoff row $R$ is read from $W$ together with $D$ rather than from a hidden rational sequence.

Finally the real-facing route is downstream from $R$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, a selected completed limit, countable choice, a host real value, or ambient completeness, the five listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop completion modulus L10 handoff]
\label{thm:bishop-completion-modulus-l10-handoff}
Every accepted $\BishopCompletionModulusUp$ carrier hands its completion-modulus data to the L10 real-completion route through
$$
M,n\longmapsto k\longmapsto W,D\longmapsto R\longmapsto E
$$
together with the displayed $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows. No consumer of the packet can reach a $\RealUp$ seal without first reading the monotone modulus threshold, the finite stream window, the dyadic threshold ledger, and the regular rational handoff.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted carrier and read the modulus face. The request $n$ is sent through the displayed $\CauchyModulusUp$ row $M$, producing the threshold row $k$ named in the carrier.

Next read the finite observation face. The stream schedule $S$ supplies the window $W$ at the selected threshold, while $D$ records the dyadic tolerance and comparison rows attached to that same window.

The regular handoff is then fixed by the packet. The row $R$ repacks the displayed window and dyadic ledger as the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, and the continuation routes record no alternate path to the seal.

Finally the $\RealUp$ row $E$ is read only after $R$. The non-escape clause of \autoref{thm:bishop-completion-modulus-namecert-obligations} excludes quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, host real values, and ambient completeness from the exported L10 handoff.
\end{proof}
