\begin{theorem}[AxisZeckendorf cannot-claim registry obligations]
\label{thm:axis-zeckendorf-cannot-claim-registry-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\AxisZeckendorfCannotClaimUp}$ surface for the carrier of \autoref{def:axis-zeckendorf-cannot-claim-registry-packet} consists exactly of four finite obligation families:

\item negative theorem coverage for $\mathtt{word\_011}$ versus $\mathtt{word\_100}$, carry-not-$\HSame$, $\mathtt{word\_011}$ non-normality, and boundary-zero-spine exclusion;
\item horizon refusal coverage for $\FullAxisUp$ as $\RealUp$, $\AxisNatUp$ as $\NatUp$, and primitive dimension lift;
\item componentwise transport and continuation routing for every visible refusal row;
\item ledger exactness through the displayed $\Pkg$ provenance and local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The negative theorem rows are precisely $a,b,c,d$ in the registry packet. Each row names an already stated AxisZeckendorf refusal and therefore supplies a finite citation target rather than a new classifier.

The horizon refusal rows are $e,f,g$. They expose only the three named non-claims: no $\FullAxisUp$ field or completion route is read as $\RealUp$, no $\AxisNatUp$ carrier is read as $\NatUp$, and no $\DimLiftUp$ seed is read as a primitive geometric dimension.

Finally, $p$ and $n$ record the package and local name boundary. Every public read of the registry passes through the seven refusal rows and these provenance coordinates, so the obligation surface is exhausted by the displayed packet.
