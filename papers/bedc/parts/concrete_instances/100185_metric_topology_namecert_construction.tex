\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\MetricTopologyUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-metric-topology-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Kelley and Bishop--Bridges, the $\MetricTopologyUp$ packet names the metric-generated topology obtained from center-radius ball rows. It sits between the metric carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the center-radius ball surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metricball-namecert}, the indexed-open topology carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, and the radius ledgers of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}. The packet records the public bridge from metric data to topology data as finite NameCert rows; it does not select opens from a host powerset and does not quotient generated opens by ambient equality.

\begin{definition}[Metric topology carrier]
\label{def:metric-topology-carrier}
A $\MetricTopologyUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G_{\mathsf{mtop}}=(M,B,T,R,Q,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the certified $\MetricSpaceUp$ carrier with its point classifier, distance witness, and displayed metric-law rows.
\item $B$ is the finite $\MetricBallUp$ basis ledger whose singleton rows are center-radius ball data compatible with the metric carrier $M$.
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ indexed-open carrier $\mathcal{T}_{d,\mathsf{ball}}$ generated from the finite ball indices of \autoref{def:metric-finite-ball-indexed-topology}.
\item $R$ records the $\RealUp$ strict-comparison and positive-radius ledgers used by membership tests of the form $d(c,x)<_{\mathsf R}r$.
\item $Q$ records the $\RatUp$ radius ledger and its transport into the displayed positive $\RealUp$ radius rows.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for metric points, ball centers, radius ledgers, singleton-ball predicates, finite ball lists, indexed-open witnesses, replay rows, provenance, and the local certificate.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a public open request through the metric carrier $M$, the ball ledger $B$, the real and rational radius ledgers $R,Q$, and the indexed topology carrier $T$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\MetricTopologyUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\MetricBallUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RatUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\MetricTopologyUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,B,T,R,Q,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for an ambient powerset of $M$, quotient equality of opens, hidden open selectors, selected centers outside $B$, or radius data outside $R,Q$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Metric topology NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:metric-topology-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\MetricTopologyUp$ carrier $G_{\mathsf{mtop}}=(M,B,T,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\MetricTopologyUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed metric carrier, ball basis ledger, indexed topology carrier, real and rational radius ledgers, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item classifier transport for metric-basic opens, requiring each singleton ball predicate to move along the point classifier of $M$ through the displayed comparison and radius ledgers;
\item singleton-ball coverage, requiring every public singleton metric-ball open of $T$ to be read through a row of $B$ before it is admitted as an indexed open;
\item finite intersection closure by ball-list concatenation, requiring the intersection of two finite ball-list opens to use the concatenated finite-list index named by the metric-generated topology source;
\item arbitrary-union ledger admission, requiring every union row to present an indexed family of already carried generated opens together with the supplied union witness of $T$;
\item boundary rows for the empty and whole opens, requiring the distinguished boundary indices of $T$ and their visible $\BHist$ ledger rows;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring public topology consumers to read only $M,B,T,R,Q,H,C,P,N$ and never an ambient powerset, quotient-open representative, hidden open selector, or radius row outside the displayed ledgers.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these finite rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:metric-topology-carrier}. The only available public rows are the metric carrier $M$, the ball basis ledger $B$, the indexed topology carrier $T$, the radius ledgers $R,Q$, and the structural rows $H,C,P,N$.

Classifier transport and singleton coverage are the displayed route from $\MetricBallUp$ into $\TopologyUp$. The singleton data are the ball rows of $B$, and \autoref{thm:metric-balls-instantiate-indexed-topology-opens} supplies the indexed-open carrier and its membership transport along the point classifier of $M$.

Finite intersections are read through finite-list concatenation. The source of \autoref{def:topology-metric-generated-carrier} uses finite lists of ball data as indices, and \autoref{thm:topology-metric-base-intersection} identifies the concatenated list with the carried intersection predicate.

Arbitrary unions and boundary opens are the remaining displayed topology rows. The union witness and the empty and whole indices are part of $T$, so every public open admitted by the packet has a visible constructor ledger row.

Finally, provenance is exhausted by $P$ and $N$. Since ambient powersets, quotient-open representatives, hidden open selectors, and detached radius rows are not coordinates of $G_{\mathsf{mtop}}$, no local NameCert obligation can export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Metric topology ball-basis scope]
\label{thm:metric-topology-ball-basis-scope}
Every public $\MetricTopologyUp$ open carried by an accepted packet $G_{\mathsf{mtop}}=(M,B,T,R,Q,H,C,P,N)$ is read through the displayed $\MetricBallUp$ and $\TopologyUp$ rows. More precisely, each public open is witnessed by one of the constructor rows of the metric-generated topology source: a singleton metric ball from $B$, a finite-list intersection of such balls, a binary generated-open meet, an indexed arbitrary union of already carried generated opens, the empty open, or the whole open. Thus every downstream consumer of the metric topology receives only the route
$$
\MetricSpaceUp,\MetricBallUp,\TopologyUp,\RealUp,\RatUp,\BHist,\hsame,\Cont,\Pkg,\NameCert .
$$
The packet exports no host powerset of metric points, quotient open, hidden open selector, or radius datum outside the displayed ledgers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let a public open be carried by $G_{\mathsf{mtop}}$. By \autoref{def:metric-topology-carrier}, the open can be read only through the indexed topology row $T$ generated from the ball basis ledger $B$.

The constructor analysis is the metric-generated exactness surface of \autoref{def:topology-metric-generated-carrier}. Singleton rows come from $B$; finite intersections are finite-list rows; binary meets, arbitrary unions, and boundary opens are the corresponding constructor rows of $T$.

Each constructor row preserves the same public route. The metric carrier $M$ supplies points and distance witnesses, the ledgers $R,Q$ supply positive-radius comparison data, $H$ and $C$ transport and replay the route, and $P,N$ record provenance and local naming.

No other coordinate is present in the packet. Therefore a consumer cannot recover a host powerset, quotient open, hidden selector, or detached radius datum from a public $\MetricTopologyUp$ open.
\end{proof}
