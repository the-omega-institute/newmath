certified affine automorphism:

\iota_F(\iota_F(T))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}T.

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp affine commutator criterion]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-commutator-trivial-iff-commutes}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, let
$A,B:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ be certified affine automorphisms in the concrete
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object. Let $\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$, $\iota_F(-)$,
$\mathrm{id}_F$, and $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$ be the affine composition,
explicit inverse, identity, and classifier supplied by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-exit-action-inverse-package}. Define
the concrete affine commutator by
$$
\mathsf{Comm}_F(A,B):=(A\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}B)\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}
(\iota_F(A)\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}\iota_F(B)).
$$
Then the affine commutator is classifier-trivial exactly when the two affine
automorphisms commute:
$$
\mathsf{Comm}_F(A,B)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}\mathrm{id}_F
\Longleftrightarrow
A\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}B\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}B\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}A.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix the canonical affine exit package from
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-exit-action-inverse-package}. This fixes
the carrier $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$, affine product $\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$, unit
$\mathrm{id}_F$, inverse row $\iota_F(-)$, and classifier
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$.

Instantiate \autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-group-certificate} on this
package. Its associativity, identity, inverse, and classifier-congruence rows
make the displayed affine data a concrete $\GroupUp$ object.

Apply \autoref{thm:group-commutator-trivial-iff-commutes} in that concrete
group with $a=A$ and $b=B$. The resulting group statement says that
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(A,B),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(A),\mathsf{inv}(B)))$ is classifier-equal to the
unit exactly when $\mathsf{mul}(A,B)$ is classifier-equal to
$\mathsf{mul}(B,A)$.

It remains to match the displayed affine notation with this group notation.
The operation, unit, and classifier are the same rows of the fixed package.
For the inverse factors, use
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inverse-antihom-exit} together with affine
associativity and classifier congruence to identify the grouped product of
explicit inverses with the inverse-product spine used by the group
commutator theorem.

Reading the group equivalence through this notation gives the forward
direction from a trivial displayed $\mathsf{Comm}_F(A,B)$ to affine
commutation.

Reading the same equivalence in reverse gives the converse: affine
commutation transports through the classifier-congruence rows to the displayed
commutator relation
$\mathsf{Comm}_F(A,B)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}\mathrm{id}_F$.
\end{proof}
