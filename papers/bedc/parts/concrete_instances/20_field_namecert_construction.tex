\begin{theorem}[Field two-sided affine automorphisms compose]
\label{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism-composition}
For a BEDC history-level $\FieldUp$ candidate with the operations and laws used
by \autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism}, and with the
distributivity laws and zero-apartness maps used by
\autoref{thm:field-product-apartness-inverse-product-reverse}, define
$$
T_1(x)=\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b),d)
$$
and
$$
T_2(x)=\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',x),b'),d').
$$
Given apartness witnesses for $a,b,a',b'$, the product-apartness theorem supplies
nonzero indices for $\mathsf{mul}(a',a)$ and $\mathsf{mul}(b,b')$. For every
$x:\mathsf{BHist}$ the composite is classified by
$$
T_2(T_1(x))\hsame
\mathsf{add}(
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',a),x),\mathsf{mul}(b,b')),
\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',d),b'),d')).
$$
Therefore $T_2\circ T_1$ is the two-sided affine map with left factor
$\mathsf{mul}(a',a)$, right factor $\mathsf{mul}(b,b')$, and offset
$\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',d),b'),d')$, and this composite map
is a certified automorphism by
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Expand $T_2(T_1(x))$ by the two displayed definitions. The outer affine map
applies $a'$ on the left and $b'$ on the right to the sum
$\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b),d)$, then adds $d'$.

Use the distributivity laws to separate the variable contribution from the
offset contribution. This gives one term containing
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b)$ and one term containing $d$, both still
transported through the outer factors $a'$ and $b'$.

Apply multiplication associativity and multiplication congruence to collect the
variable term as
$$
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',a),x),\mathsf{mul}(b,b')).
$$
The remaining transported offset term is
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',d),b')$, and the final outer offset is $d'$.

Apply
\autoref{thm:field-product-apartness-inverse-product-reverse} to the apartness
witnesses for $a',a$ and again to the apartness witnesses for $b,b'$. These two
applications provide the nonzero indices required for the collected left and
right composite factors.

With these two indices and the displayed composite offset, invoke
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism}. The classifier
identity above identifies $T_2(T_1(x))$ with that certified two-sided affine map,
so the composite is a certified affine automorphism.
\end{proof}

Per-object certificate: traditional target affine automorphism composition; BEDC
source $\FieldUp$ over $\mathsf{BHist}$ with RingUp distributivity; carrier
$\mathsf{BHist}$; classifier $\hsame$; stability obligations affine exactness,
map classifier exactness, product apartness, associativity, and distributivity;
ledger the four factor witnesses and the two offsets; core theorem
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism-composition};
exactness predicate composite affine classifier iff; standard bridge deferred;
status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[Field affine composite inverse agrees with reverse-composed inverses]
\label{thm:field-affine-composite-inverse-reverse-coherence}
For a BEDC history-level $\FieldUp$ candidate with the operations and laws used
by
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism-composition}, let
$p_a:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(a)$, $p_b:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(b)$,
$p_{a'}:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(a')$, and $p_{b'}:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(b')$ be the
factor-apartness witnesses for the two component affine maps
$$
T_1(x)=\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b),d)
$$
and
$$
T_2(x)=\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',x),b'),d').
$$
Put
$A=\mathsf{mul}(a',a)$, $B=\mathsf{mul}(b,b')$, and
$D=\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',d),b'),d')$, and let
$p_A:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(A)$ and $p_B:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(B)$ be the
product-apartness witnesses supplied by
\autoref{thm:field-product-apartness-inverse-product-reverse}. For each
$c:\mathsf{BHist}$ define
$$
s_1(c)=
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(a,p_a),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{add}(c,\mathsf{neg}(d)),\mathsf{inv}(b,p_b)))
$$
and
$$
s_2(c)=
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(a',p_{a'}),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{add}(c,\mathsf{neg}(d')),\mathsf{inv}(b',p_{b'}))).
$$
For each $q:\mathsf{BHist}$ define the composite explicit inverse
$$
s_{21}(q)=
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{inv}(A,p_A),
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{add}(q,\mathsf{neg}(D)),\mathsf{inv}(B,p_B))).
$$
Then every $q:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfies
$$
s_{21}(q)\hsame s_1(s_2(q)).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let
$$
C(x)=\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(A,x),B),D).
$$
Apply
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-explicit-inverse} to the composite
coefficients $A,B,D$ with witnesses $p_A,p_B$. Its left inverse clause gives
$C(s_{21}(q))\hsame q$.

Next instantiate
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism} for the first
component with witnesses $p_a,p_b$ and offset $d$, and again for the second
component with witnesses $p_{a'},p_{b'}$ and offset $d'$. The first certificate
gives $T_1(s_1(s_2(q)))\hsame s_2(q)$, and the second gives
$T_2(s_2(q))\hsame q$.

Use the classifier-preservation direction of the second certificate to
transport the first displayed equality through $T_2$. Transitivity with
$T_2(s_2(q))\hsame q$ yields
$T_2(T_1(s_1(s_2(q))))\hsame q$.

By
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism-composition}, the
left side of this equation is classified by $C(s_1(s_2(q)))$ with the collected
coefficients $A,B,D$. Hence $s_1(s_2(q))$ also solves the same composite affine
equation $C(x)\hsame q$.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-equation-solution-unique-from-apartness}
to the two solutions $s_{21}(q)$ and $s_1(s_2(q))$ of $C(x)\hsame q$. The
unique-solution conclusion is exactly
$s_{21}(q)\hsame s_1(s_2(q))$.
\end{proof}

$\mathsf{BHist}$; classifier $\hsame$; stability obligations explicit inverse
exactness, certified component automorphisms, composite coefficient collection,
product apartness, and affine equation uniqueness; ledger the four component
factor witnesses, the two product witnesses, and the two offsets; core theorem
\autoref{thm:field-affine-composite-inverse-reverse-coherence}; exactness
predicate reverse-composed explicit inverse equals the collected-coefficient
explicit inverse; standard bridge deferred; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
