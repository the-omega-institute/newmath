\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp contextual action composition support laws]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-contextual-action-composition-support-laws}
For the concrete denominator tuple
$(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u,N_D^u)$ and the contextual action $\Gamma$ of
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-denominator-contextual-action-instance}, let
$p,q,p',q',h,l,r,l',r':\mathsf{BHist}$. Suppose
$p\hsame\emp$, $q\hsame\emp$, $p'\hsame\emp$, and $q'\hsame\emp$, and suppose
$C_D^u(h)$, $C_D^u(l)$, $C_D^u(r)$, $C_D^u(l')$, and $C_D^u(r')$. Then the
nested contextual action is carried:
$$
C_D^u(\Gamma_{p',q',l',r'}(\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h))).
$$
It is denominator-classified by the single contextual action with collected
append contexts:
$$
R_D^u(\Gamma_{p',q',l',r'}(\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h)),
\Gamma_{p',q',\mathsf{append}(l',l),\mathsf{append}(r,r')}(h)).
$$
Its strict support is exactly the five visible support sites:
$$
N_D^u(\Gamma_{p',q',l',r'}(\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h)))
\Longleftrightarrow
N_D^u(l')\lor N_D^u(l)\lor N_D^u(h)\lor N_D^u(r)\lor N_D^u(r').
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-contextual-action-composition} to the
empty-endpoint nested actions. Its classifier and support clauses identify the
two-layer action with the corresponding collected action once the inner and
outer carried contexts are available.

The carrier inputs are supplied by denominator-unit carrier closure under
$\mathsf{append}$. First close $l$ with $h$ and then with $r$; transport the
result along the two endpoint equalities $p\hsame\emp$ and $q\hsame\emp$ to get
the carried inner action. Close $l'$ with that carried inner action and then
with $r'$; transport along $p'\hsame\emp$ and $q'\hsame\emp$ to get the carried
nested action.

The same append-closure rows also carry
$\mathsf{append}(l',l)$ and $\mathsf{append}(r,r')$, so the collected single
action is a legitimate denominator action. Carrier transport along $\hsame$
keeps the concrete action endpoints inside $C_D^u$ throughout the comparison.

For strict support, apply bilateral support exactness first to the inner action:
its support is read as $N_D^u(l)\lor N_D^u(h)\lor N_D^u(r)$. Apply the same
exactness theorem to the outer action and substitute the inner readback. The
finite disjunction reassociates to
$N_D^u(l')\lor N_D^u(l)\lor N_D^u(h)\lor N_D^u(r)\lor N_D^u(r')$, which is the
displayed support law.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-contextual-action-pair-full-transport-package}

\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp contextual action three-pair reverse-order coherence]
\label{thm:rat-denom-unit-contextual-action-three-pair-reverse-order-coherence}

$(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u,N_D^u)$ and the contextual action $\Gamma$ of
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-denominator-contextual-action-instance}, let
$h,l_0,r_0,l_1,r_1,l_2,r_2:\mathsf{BHist}$. Suppose
$C_D^u(h)$, $C_D^u(l_0)$, $C_D^u(r_0)$, $C_D^u(l_1)$, $C_D^u(r_1)$,
$C_D^u(l_2)$, and $C_D^u(r_2)$. Then the empty-endpoint contextual actions

and reverse order are denominator-classified:
$$
R_D^u(\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_2,r_2}(\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_1,r_1}(\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_0,r_0}(h))),
\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_0,r_0}(\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_1,r_1}(\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_2,r_2}(h)))).
$$
Their strict-support predicates are equivalent:
$$
N_D^u(\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_2,r_2}(\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_1,r_1}(\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_0,r_0}(h))))
\Longleftrightarrow
N_D^u(\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_0,r_0}(\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_1,r_1}(\Gamma_{\emp,\emp,l_2,r_2}(h)))).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First move the carried pair $(l_0,r_0)$ past $(l_1,r_1)$ while the outer
$(l_2,r_2)$ action is fixed. Apply
\autoref{thm:rat-denom-unit-contextual-action-empty-pair-commutes} to the
inner empty-endpoint pair, and use
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-contextual-action-composition-support-laws}
to supply the carried intermediate action needed by the outer context.

Next move $(l_2,r_2)$ past $(l_0,r_0)$ across the carried middle action. The
same two-pair commutation theorem gives the classifier for the exposed pair
swap, and the composition support laws keep the middle contextual action inside
$C_D^u$. Classifier transitivity composes this row with the first one.

Finally move $(l_1,r_1)$ past $(l_2,r_2)$ under the outer $(l_0,r_0)$ action.
Another application of the empty-pair commutation theorem, followed by the same
composition support laws, gives the third classifier row. Transitivity of
$R_D^u$ along the three displayed classifier rows yields the reverse-order
classifier.

For strict support, apply
\autoref{thm:rat-denom-unit-contextual-action-empty-pair-strict-support-commutes}
along each of the three pair swaps just described. The composition support laws
provide the carried intermediate actions at every stage, and support transport
along the same classifier chain repacks the three local equivalences into the
displayed strict-support equivalence.
\end{proof}

For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, the canonical
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit target simultaneously exports the following rows as one
concrete exit package.

\begin{enumerate}
\item The algebra-reduct compatibility rows of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-algebra-reduct-compatibility}.
\item The inverse-product classifier exactness row of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-inverse-product-classifier-exit-row}.
\item The affine automorphism action certificate of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-automorphism-action-certificate}.
\end{enumerate}

Thus downstream $\FieldUp$ consumers may cite this one canonical package for
reduct rows, inverse-product exactness, and affine action support without
reassembling the three certificate rows from their separate locations.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First invoke
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-machine-checkable-exit}. This fixes
Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object} as the concrete
$\mathsf{BHist}$-anchored source object with carrier
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$, classifier $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$,
denominator data, and contextual action $\Gamma$.

Apply \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-algebra-reduct-compatibility} to the fixed
source anchor. This supplies the denominator continuation reduct, singleton
empty obstruction reducts, singleton zero/one ring rows, and commutative-ring
base coordinate as fields of the same concrete exit object.

Next apply \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-inverse-product-classifier-exit-row}. Its
row is carried by the same $\mathsf{BHist}$ source and classifier $\hsame$, so
the inverse-product classifier exactness field is collected without changing
the carrier or source tuple.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-automorphism-action-certificate}. Its affine
carrier $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$, classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$, group laws,
fiber singleton exactness, and cancellation exactness become the action fields
of the same exit object.

This is not a single-theorem variant of any one cited result. It composes three
separately located exit rows into one canonical package whose fields are all
anchored by Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target canonical $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit action
and inverse package; BEDC source $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$ over
$\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ together with
$\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ for the action row; classifier
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$, $\hsame$, and $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$ on their
displayed rows; stability obligations algebra-reduct compatibility,
inverse-product classifier exactness, affine group laws, affine fiber
singleton exactness, and affine cancellation exactness; ledger denominator
continuation reducts, singleton obstruction reducts, zero/one boundary rows,
commutative-ring base coordinate, certified nonzero factor witnesses, product
witnesses, affine coefficients, offsets, and inverse witnesses; core theorem
list \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-machine-checkable-exit},
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-algebra-reduct-compatibility},
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-inverse-product-classifier-exit-row}, and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-automorphism-action-certificate}; exactness
predicate inverse-product classifier equivalence plus affine fiber and
cancellation exactness; standard bridge ordinary field reducts with ordinary
affine action; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp contextual pair transport full noncollapse invariance]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-contextual-pair-transport-full-noncollapse-invariance}
For the concrete denominator tuple $(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u,N_D^u)$ and the
contextual action $\Gamma$ of
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-denominator-contextual-action-instance}, let
$$
x_L:=\Gamma_{p',q',l',r'}(\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h))
$$
and
$$
x_R:=\Gamma_{p',q',s',t'}(\Gamma_{p,q,s,t}(h)).
$$
Suppose $p\hsame\emp$, $q\hsame\emp$, $p'\hsame\emp$, and $q'\hsame\emp$;
suppose $C_D^u(h)$; suppose the four denominator context comparisons
$R_D^u(l,s)$, $R_D^u(r,t)$, $R_D^u(l',s')$, and $R_D^u(r',t')$ hold; and
suppose $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(x_L)$. Then the transported endpoint $x_R$
has the following concrete noncollapse package:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $R_D^u(x_L,x_R)$;
  \item $C_D^u(x_R)$;
  \item $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(x_R)$;
  \item $\mathsf{fieldSingletonEmptyCarrier}(x_R)\to\bot$;
  \item $x_R\hsame\emp\to\bot$;
  \item for every $r:\mathsf{BHist}$,
  $\mathsf{fieldSingletonEmptyCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(r,x_R))\to\bot$;
  \item for every $r:\mathsf{BHist}$,
  $\mathsf{fieldSingletonEmptyCarrier}(\mathsf{append}(x_R,r))\to\bot$;
  \item for every $r:\mathsf{BHist}$,
  $\mathsf{fieldSingletonEmptyNonZero}(\mathsf{append}(r,x_R))$;
  \item for every $r:\mathsf{BHist}$,
  $\mathsf{fieldSingletonEmptyNonZero}(\mathsf{append}(x_R,r))$;
  \item for all $L,R:\mathsf{BHist}$,
  $$
  \mathsf{fieldSingletonEmptyClassifier}(\mathsf{append}(L,x_R),
  \mathsf{append}(R,x_R))\to\bot.
  $$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply
\autoref{thm:rat-denom-unit-contextual-action-pair-transport-classifier} to the
four denominator classifier rows and the two nested contextual actions. This
gives the transported denominator classifier $R_D^u(x_L,x_R)$.

Next apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-contextual-action-pair-transport-carrier-support}
to the same empty endpoints, carried source, and classifier rows. Transporting
the assumed $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(x_L)$ witness gives
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(x_R)$, and the carrier component gives $C_D^u(x_R)$.

The endpoint noncollapse clauses come from
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-transported-strict-support-separation-package} with
the same data. This supplies the singleton-empty rejection at $x_R$ and the
direct empty-history rejection $x_R\hsame\emp\to\bot$.

For contextual singleton-carrier rejection, apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-transported-strict-support-contextual-singleton-exclusion}.
For each context history $r$, it rejects both
$\mathsf{append}(r,x_R)$ and $\mathsf{append}(x_R,r)$ as singleton-empty
carriers.

Then apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-transported-strict-support-contextual-nonzero-package}
to the same transported endpoint. It repacks the two contextual carrier
rejections as the proof-indexed nonzero witnesses for both append orders.

Finally apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-transported-strict-support-singleton-classifier-exclusion}.
For all $L,R:\mathsf{BHist}$, this rules out a singleton-empty classifier
between $\mathsf{append}(L,x_R)$ and $\mathsf{append}(R,x_R)$, completing the
displayed package.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp affine inverse uniqueness exit]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inverse-uniqueness-exit}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, let
$T:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ be a certified affine automorphism in the concrete
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object. Let $S:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ be the explicit affine
inverse of $T$ supplied by
Theorem~\ref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-identity-and-inverse-package}, and
let $U:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ be any certified affine automorphism whose left and
right affine composites with $T$ classify as the affine identity under
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$:
$$
(U\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}} I
$$
and
$$
(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}} I.
$$
Then $U$ is the same affine inverse as $S$ under the concrete action classifier:
$$
U\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}S.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object-complete} to
fix the concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-automorphism-action-certificate} to attach
the carrier $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$, the affine composition
$\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$, the affine identity $I$, and the classifier
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$ to that same object.

The explicit inverse package
Theorem~\ref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-identity-and-inverse-package} supplies
the displayed inverse witness $S$ for $T$. The hypotheses supply the matching
left and right inverse equations for $U$ against the same $T$ and under the same
affine classifier.

Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-inverse-unique}. Its conclusion
is the group-classifier equality between the arbitrary inverse candidate $U$ and
the explicit inverse $S$. The action certificate identifies that group
classifier with the displayed affine classifier, so the returned relation is
exactly $U\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}S$.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target affine inverse uniqueness over a field
action; BEDC source concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit rows plus
$\mathsf{AffAut}_F$; carrier certified affine automorphism histories; classifier
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$; stability obligations affine group certificate and
inverse equations; ledger inverse witnesses and composite witnesses; core
theorem
Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inverse-uniqueness-exit}; exactness
predicate uniqueness of the affine inverse under the action classifier; standard
bridge ordinary uniqueness of inverse in an affine automorphism group; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp affine inverse determinacy exit]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inverse-determinacy-exit}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, let
$T,U,V:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ be certified affine automorphisms in the concrete
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object. Suppose that both $U$ and $V$ have left and
right affine composites with $T$ classified as the affine identity under
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$:
$$
(U\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}} I
$$
and
$$
(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}U)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}} I
$$
and
$$
(V\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}} I
$$
and
$$
(T\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}V)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}} I.
$$
Then the two inverse candidates are equal under the concrete affine classifier:
$$
U\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}V.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-automorphism-action-certificate}
to the concrete exit object. This supplies the carrier $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$, the
affine identity $I$, composition $\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$, and the classifier
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$ together with its symmetry and transitivity rules.

Take the explicit inverse $S$ of $T$ from
Theorem~\ref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-identity-and-inverse-package}. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inverse-uniqueness-exit} to the two
composite witnesses for $U$. This gives
$$
U\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}S.
$$

Apply the same theorem to the two composite witnesses for $V$. This gives
$$
V\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}S.
$$

Use symmetry for the affine classifier to turn the second relation into
$S\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}V$. Transitivity of the same classifier then composes
$U\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}S$ with $S\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}V$, yielding
$U\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}V$.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target inverse determinacy in the affine
automorphism group; BEDC source concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit rows plus
$\mathsf{AffAut}_F$; carrier certified affine automorphisms; classifier
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$; stability obligations affine classifier symmetry and
transitivity; ledger two inverse-candidate composite witnesses; core theorem
Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inverse-determinacy-exit}; exactness
predicate uniqueness of two-sided inverse candidates; standard bridge ordinary
inverse determinacy in a group action; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
