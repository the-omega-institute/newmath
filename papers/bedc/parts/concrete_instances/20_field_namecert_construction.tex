\section{RatUp boundary for the singleton field schema}
\label{sec:field-singleton-rat-boundary}

\begin{theorem}[Singleton field schema is disjoint from the RatUp history carrier]
\label{thm:field-singleton-schema-disjoint-from-rat-history-carrier}
For every $h:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $A_{\emp}(h)$ is the singleton source from
Definition~\ref{def:singleton-empty-history-field-schema-instance}, then
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)$ implies $\mathsf{False}$. Equivalently, no
RatUp history-carrier endpoint is an endpoint of the singleton empty-history
$\FieldUp$ schema.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume $A_{\emp}(h)$ and $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)$. Unfolding the
singleton carrier component gives the endpoint equality $h\hsame\emp$.

The RatUp carrier witness is separated from the empty denominator endpoint by
Theorem~\ref{thm:rat-history-carrier-not-empty}. Applying that theorem to the
carrier witness and the equality $h\hsame\emp$ gives the required
contradiction.
\end{proof}

Traditional target terminal singleton field boundary; BEDC source singleton
$\mathsf{BHist}$ $\FieldUp$ schema plus RatUp history carrier; carrier choices
$A_{\emp}$ and $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$; classifier choices $\rho_{\emp}$
and Rat history classifier; stability obligation nonempty denominator
separation; ledger empty endpoint versus positive-denominator evidence; core
theorem Theorem~\ref{thm:field-singleton-schema-disjoint-from-rat-history-carrier};
exactness predicate no shared endpoint; standard bridge terminal schema is not
the rational field; status ProofTarget.

\begin{theorem}[Field equal-squares signed factors cannot both be nonzero]
\label{thm:field-equal-squares-signed-factors-not-both-nonzero}
For a BEDC history-level $\FieldUp$ candidate with the hypotheses of
\autoref{thm:field-equal-squares-signed-factor-exclusion} and
\autoref{thm:field-nonzero-factors-exclude-zero-product}, every
$a,b:\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfies
$$
a\cdot a\hsame b\cdot b
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\mathsf{NonZero}_F(a+b)\to
\mathsf{NonZero}_F(a+(-b))\to\mathsf{False}.
$$
The traditional target is constructive signed-factor square reasoning; the
BEDC source is $\FieldUp$ together with $\CommRingUp$; the carrier is
$\mathsf{BHist}$; the classifier is $\hsame$; the ledger consists of the two
explicit signed-factor nonzero witnesses; the core theorem is this block; the
exactness predicate is impossibility of simultaneous nonzero signed factors
under equal squares; the status is $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume $a\cdot a\hsame b\cdot b$ and witnesses
$p_+:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(a+b)$ and
$p_-:\mathsf{NonZero}_F(a+(-b))$.

First apply \autoref{thm:commring-difference-of-squares-zero-exact} to the
square equality. The signed-factor product is classified as zero:
$$
(a+b)\cdot(a+(-b))\hsame0.
$$

Now apply \autoref{thm:field-nonzero-factors-exclude-zero-product} to the two
nonzero witnesses $p_+$ and $p_-$ together with this displayed zero product.
That theorem says that such a zero product is impossible when both factors are
explicitly nonzero, so the assumed pair of signed-factor witnesses yields
$\mathsf{False}$.
\end{proof}
