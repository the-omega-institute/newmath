\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator FieldUp multiplicative exactness boundary]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-exactness-boundary}
The concrete instance
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-exactness-instance}
satisfies the Rat denominator FieldUp multiplicative certificate boundary. In
addition, for all $h,k,t:\mathsf{BHist}$, if $C_D^\ast(h)$, $C_D^\ast(k)$,
and $N_D^\ast(t)$, then
$$
R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(h,t),\mu_D^\ast(k,t))
\Longleftrightarrow R_D^\ast(h,k),
$$
and
$$
R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(t,h),\mu_D^\ast(t,k))
\Longleftrightarrow R_D^\ast(h,k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The displayed tuple is the same concrete carrier, classifier, multiplication,
unit, and strict support used by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-certificate-boundary}.
That theorem supplies the packaged carrier, classifier, monoid, commutativity,
empty-unit uniqueness, and carried-endpoint clauses for the denominator-side
FieldUp boundary.

The strict-support component is the predicate
$N_D^\ast(h):=\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)$. The transport and product-support
parts of the boundary are therefore the corresponding clauses of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-strict-support-laws}.

It remains to identify strict common-factor cancellation at the same concrete
classifier. Since $N_D^\ast(t)$ is by definition
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(t)$, \autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope}
places $t$ in $C_D^\ast$ through the carried alternative.

Under $C_D^\ast(h)$, $C_D^\ast(k)$, and this derived carrier witness for $t$,
the right and left cancellation equivalences are exactly the two displayed
clauses of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-cancellation-exactness}. Hence
the concrete tuple carries both the certificate boundary and the strict
factor-cancellation exactness required for the denominator side of
$\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator bilateral strict factor cancellation]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-bilateral-strict-factor-cancellation}
For the concrete tuple
$(C_D^\ast,R_D^\ast,\mu_D^\ast,e_D^\ast,N_D^\ast)$ of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-exactness-instance},
let $h,k,l,r:\mathsf{BHist}$. If $C_D^\ast(h)$, $C_D^\ast(k)$,
$C_D^\ast(l)$, $C_D^\ast(r)$, $N_D^\ast(l)$, and $N_D^\ast(r)$, then
$$
R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,\mu_D^\ast(h,r)),
\mu_D^\ast(l,\mu_D^\ast(k,r)))
\Longleftrightarrow R_D^\ast(h,k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First build the products to which cancellation will be applied. The
strict-support product closure and carrier closure supplied by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-fieldup-multiplicative-certificate-boundary}
place $\mu_D^\ast(l,h)$ and $\mu_D^\ast(l,k)$ in $C_D^\ast$, and keep the
larger products with the strict factor $r$ inside the same concrete carrier.
The associativity and congruence fields of that boundary identify the displayed
comparison with
$R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,h),r),
\mu_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,k),r))$.

Apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-cancellation-exactness} with
the strict common right factor $r$. Since $N_D^\ast(r)$ and the two left
products are carried by the preceding closure step, this gives
$$
R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,h),r),
\mu_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,k),r))
\Longleftrightarrow
R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,h),\mu_D^\ast(l,k)).
$$

Apply the same cancellation theorem with the strict common left factor $l$.
The hypotheses $C_D^\ast(h)$, $C_D^\ast(k)$, $C_D^\ast(l)$, and
$N_D^\ast(l)$ are exactly its input hypotheses for the left-cancellation
clause, so
$$
R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(l,h),\mu_D^\ast(l,k))
\Longleftrightarrow R_D^\ast(h,k).
$$
Composing the two equivalences gives the bilateral strict-factor cancellation
claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp denominator contextual action certificate]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-denominator-contextual-action-certificate}

$C_D^u(h)$, $C_D^u(k)$, $C_D^u(l)$, $C_D^u(r)$, and
$p\hsame\emp$, $q\hsame\emp$, $p'\hsame\emp$, and $q'\hsame\emp$. Then the
contextual action instance of
\autoref{def:ratup-fieldup-denominator-contextual-action-instance} satisfies
the following classifier and strict-support laws:
$$
R_D^u(\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h),\Gamma_{p',q',l,r}(k))
\Longleftrightarrow
R_D^u(h,k),
$$
$$
N_D^u(\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h))
\Longleftrightarrow
N_D^u(l)\lor N_D^u(h)\lor N_D^u(r),
$$
and
$$
N_D^u(\Gamma_{p',q',l,r}(k))
\Longleftrightarrow
N_D^u(l)\lor N_D^u(k)\lor N_D^u(r).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First use closure for the carried bilateral product. From $C_D^u(l)$,
$C_D^u(h)$, and $C_D^u(r)$, the denominator continuation monoid laws put
$B_{l,r}(h)$ inside $C_D^u$; the same argument using $k$ gives
$C_D^u(B_{l,r}(k))$.

For classifier exactness, strip the empty external endpoint contexts with
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-classifier-empty-context-exactness}.
The endpoint equalities $p\hsame\emp$, $q\hsame\emp$, $p'\hsame\emp$, and
$q'\hsame\emp$ reduce the displayed classifier comparison to
$R_D^u(B_{l,r}(h),B_{l,r}(k))$.

Apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-bilateral-multiplication-classifier-exactness}
to the carried histories $h$, $k$, $l$, and $r$. This gives exactly
$R_D^u(h,k)$, and the reverse direction follows by the same two equivalences in
the opposite order.

For strict support at $h$, use
\autoref{thm:rat-denom-unit-carrier-empty-context-iff} to identify the support
of $\Gamma_{p,q,l,r}(h)$ with the support of $B_{l,r}(h)$ under the empty
