\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp Rat-classified factors exclude empty product]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-rat-classified-factors-exclude-empty-product}
For a BEDC history-level $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ candidate satisfying the hypotheses
of \autoref{thm:field-nonzero-factors-exclude-zero-product}, specialize the
nonzero index to
$$
\mathsf{ApartZero}_F(h):=(h\hsame0\to\bot).
$$
Assume the RatUp concrete exit object identifies the field-zero endpoint with
the empty history, and includes classifier transport for
$\mathsf{ApartZero}_F$ together with contradiction of
$\mathsf{ApartZero}_F(0)$. Then for all histories
$a,a',b,b':\mathsf{BHist}$,
$$
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(a,a')
\land
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(b,b')
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
(\mathsf{mul}(a,b)\hsame0\to\bot)
\land
(\mathsf{mul}(a',b')\hsame0\to\bot).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the two classifier witnesses. Apply
\autoref{thm:rat-history-classifier-endpoints-not-empty} to
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(a,a')$ and to
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(b,b')$. The four endpoint exclusions, transported
across the concrete identification of the field-zero endpoint with $\emp$,
give witnesses
$p_a:\mathsf{ApartZero}_F(a)$, $p_{a'}:\mathsf{ApartZero}_F(a')$,
$p_b:\mathsf{ApartZero}_F(b)$, and $p_{b'}:\mathsf{ApartZero}_F(b')$.

The stability field for $\mathsf{ApartZero}_F$ is transport along $\hsame$:
if $h\hsame k$ and $h\hsame0\to\bot$, then any $k\hsame0$ composes back to
$h\hsame0$ and gives contradiction. The zero contradiction clause is immediate
because $\mathsf{ApartZero}_F(0)$ applied to reflexivity of $0\hsame0$ yields
$\bot$.

Instantiate \autoref{thm:field-nonzero-factors-exclude-zero-product} first at
$(a,b)$ with witnesses $p_a$ and $p_b$, obtaining
$\mathsf{mul}(a,b)\hsame0\to\bot$. Instantiate the same theorem at
$(a',b')$ with witnesses $p_{a'}$ and $p_{b'}$, obtaining
$\mathsf{mul}(a',b')\hsame0\to\bot$. Conjunction introduction packages the two
Rat-classified product exclusions.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target Rat-classified nonzero product in
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$,
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$, and the FieldUp apart-zero certificate;
carrier RatHistoryClassifier endpoints; classifier $\hsame$; stability Rat
endpoint nonemptiness plus FieldUp zero-product exclusion; ledger the two
classifier witnesses; exactness predicate no empty product for classified Rat
endpoints; downstream consumer Phase Algebra Hierarchy $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit
certificate; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator carried continuation unit obstruction]
\label{thm:rat-denominator-carried-continuation-unit-obstruction}
For every history $u:\mathsf{BHist}$, a carried Rat denominator cannot itself be
the continuation left unit for all carried denominators. Concretely,
$$
\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(u)
\to
\left(
\forall d,r:\mathsf{BHist}.\;
\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(d)\to
\Cont(u,d,r)\to
\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}(r,d)
\right)
\to
\mathsf{False}.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Assume a carrier witness $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(u)$ and the displayed
left-unit classifier law. Apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-left-unit-endpoint-iff-empty}
to the law; the forward implication yields $u\hsame\emp$.

Now apply \autoref{thm:rat-history-carrier-not-empty} to the same carrier
witness and the endpoint comparison $u\hsame\emp$. This contradicts the
nonempty Rat denominator condition, giving $\mathsf{False}$.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target carried-unit obstruction for the
Rat-denominator side of $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$,
$\Cont$, and $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$; carrier
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$; classifier $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$;
stability obligation left-unit endpoint exactness; ledger external unit versus
carried denominator; core theorem
\autoref{thm:rat-denominator-carried-continuation-unit-obstruction}; exactness
predicate no carried denominator is the continuation unit; downstream consumer
the concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit certificate; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp final row inventory coverage]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-final-row-inventory-coverage}
For $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$, let $\mathcal E_F$ be the concrete exit
object of Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}. For the
concrete denominator tuple $(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u,N_D^u)$, histories
$h,k,\ell:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $C_D^u(h)$, $C_D^u(k)$, and $C_D^u(\ell)$,
selectors $s,t,u,v,w,z:\mathsf{BMark}$, and contexts
$L,R,r:\mathsf{BHist}$, let $E_q(h,k,\ell)$ be the bracketing endpoint
selector of Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-bracketing-endpoint-selector}
for each $q:\mathsf{BMark}$. If $\mathsf{ThreeSupp}_D^u(h,k,\ell)$ holds, then
the final row inventory of $\mathcal E_F$ contains all of the following rows as
one consumable Phase Algebra Hierarchy package:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the selector inverse-domain coverage row of
  \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-selector-inverse-domain-coverage}
  for the selected pair $(s,t)$ and contexts $L,R$;
  \item for every primed denominator triple
  $h',k',\ell':\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying $C_D^u(h')$, $C_D^u(k')$,
  $C_D^u(\ell')$, $\mathsf{ThreeSupp}_D^u(h',k',\ell')$,
  $R_D^u(h,h')$, $R_D^u(k,k')$, and $R_D^u(\ell,\ell')$, and for every
  $L',R':\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\hsame(L,L')$ and $\hsame(R,R')$, the selector
  row-stability row of
  \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-selector-row-stability};
  \item the complete selector exit obstruction certificate of
  \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-complete-selector-exit-obstruction-certificate}
  for the selector quadruple $(s,t,u,v)$ and contexts $L,R,r$;
  \item the selector-chain final boundary rows of
  \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-selector-chain-final-boundary} for the selector
  pairs $(s,t)$, $(u,v)$, and $(w,z)$.
\end{enumerate}
Equivalently, the one concrete FieldUp exit object supplies selector
inverse-domain coverage, selector row transport, complete obstruction, and
chain boundary rows to the $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit certificate without changing
the underlying $\mathsf{BHist}$ source, $\mathsf{BMark}$ selectors, or
$\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$ package.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First instantiate
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-selector-inverse-domain-coverage} at
the displayed concrete denominator tuple, the histories $h,k,\ell$, their
carrier witnesses, the support witness, the selector pair $(s,t)$, and the
contexts $L,R$. This gives the strict inverse-domain selector row for
$\mathcal E_F$.

Next take an arbitrary primed denominator triple $h',k',\ell'$ with the
displayed carrier, support, and $R_D^u$ comparison hypotheses, and take
$L',R'$ with $\hsame(L,L')$ and $\hsame(R,R')$. Apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-selector-row-stability} to these
data. The result is the selector row-stability transport row for the same
concrete exit object.

We then instantiate
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-complete-selector-exit-obstruction-certificate} with
the same denominator tuple, histories, carrier witnesses, support witness,
selectors $s,t,u,v$, and contexts $L,R,r$. This supplies the complete selector
exit obstruction certificate inside the concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ package.

Finally instantiate \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-selector-chain-final-boundary}
with the same denominator tuple, histories, carrier witnesses, support witness,
selectors $s,t,u,v,w,z$, and contexts $L,R,r$. It supplies the three
selector-pair final boundary row families and the chained endpoint classifier
rows.

All four cited results refer to Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}
and to the concrete tuple $(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u,N_D^u)$. Conjunction
introduction packages their row families as one final row inventory for the
Phase Algebra Hierarchy consumer.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target final FieldUp row inventory; BEDC
source $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\mathsf{BMark}$, and
$\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$; carrier $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ and
$C_D^u$; classifier $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ and $R_D^u$; stability
obligations selector coverage, selector row transport, complete obstruction,
and chain boundary; ledger selectors $s,t,u,v,w,z$ plus contexts $L,R,r$; core
theorem this block; exactness final row inventory coverage; downstream consumer
Phase Algebra Hierarchy $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit certificate; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
