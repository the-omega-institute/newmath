\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator empty-unit commutative monoid laws]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-commutative-monoid-laws}
For the tuple $(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u)$ of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-instance},
the five laws of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-laws} hold,
and the multiplication is commutative up to the endpoint classifier: if
$C_D^u(h)$ and $C_D^u(k)$, then
$$
R_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,k),\mu_D^u(k,h)).
$$
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.FieldUp.field\_rat\_denominator\_empty\_unit\_continuation\_commutative\_monoid\_laws}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the concrete data of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-instance}.
The first five clauses are precisely
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-laws}.

It remains to prove the displayed commutativity classifier. Split the two
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$ witnesses into their empty endpoint and genuine
Rat denominator endpoint alternatives.

In the genuine/genuine case, the two products are the canonical append
continuations $\Cont(h,k,\mathsf{append}(h,k))$ and
$\Cont(k,h,\mathsf{append}(k,h))$. Apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-commutative-classifier} to
these two continuations and the two Rat denominator carriers. The resulting
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ supplies the carried endpoints and the
$\hsame$ comparison required to pack
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}(\mathsf{append}(h,k),\mathsf{append}(k,h))$.

In a mixed case, one endpoint is $\hsame\emp$ and the other is a genuine Rat
denominator endpoint. The append unit law on the empty side identifies both
products with the genuine endpoint, one directly and one after symmetry of
$\hsame$. Transitivity of $\hsame$ gives
$\mathsf{append}(h,k)\hsame\mathsf{append}(k,h)$, and append closure gives the
two empty-unit carrier witnesses. These data pack the required
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}$.

In the empty/empty case, both inputs are $\hsame\emp$. The left and right
append unit laws identify each product with $\emp$; symmetry and transitivity
of $\hsame$ then identify the two products with each other. The empty branch
of $\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$ carries both products, so the classifier
packing again yields
$R_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,k),\mu_D^u(k,h))$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator empty-unit identity uniqueness]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-identity-unique}
For the tuple $(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u)$ of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-instance},
let $u:\mathsf{BHist}$ be a carried endpoint with $C_D^u(u)$. If $u$
satisfies the two one-sided unit laws
$$
\forall h:\mathsf{BHist}.\; C_D^u(h)\to R_D^u(\mu_D^u(u,h),h)
$$
and
$$
\forall h:\mathsf{BHist}.\; C_D^u(h)\to R_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,u),h),
$$
then
$$
R_D^u(u,e_D^u).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-laws}. Its
carried-unit clause gives $C_D^u(e_D^u)$, and its right-unit law with right
factor $e_D^u$ gives $R_D^u(\mu_D^u(u,e_D^u),u)$ from the assumed carrier
witness $C_D^u(u)$.

Now apply the assumed left-unit law for $u$ at the carried endpoint $e_D^u$.
This gives $R_D^u(\mu_D^u(u,e_D^u),e_D^u)$.

Project the two $R_D^u$ classifier witnesses to their $\hsame$ components. The
first comparison identifies $\mu_D^u(u,e_D^u)$ with $u$, and the second
comparison identifies the same product with $e_D^u$. Symmetry and transitivity
of $\hsame$ therefore give $u\hsame e_D^u$.

Finally, repack the carrier witnesses $C_D^u(u)$ and $C_D^u(e_D^u)$ together
with this endpoint comparison. The result is exactly $R_D^u(u,e_D^u)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator empty-unit product nonempty iff]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-product-nonempty-iff}
For histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $C_D^u(h)$ and $C_D^u(k)$, the
empty-unit product is nonempty exactly when at least one input has strict Rat
denominator support:
$$
(\mu_D^u(h,k)\hsame\emp\to\mathsf{False})
\Longleftrightarrow
N_D^u(h)\lor N_D^u(k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $C_D^u$, $\mu_D^u$, and $N_D^u$. The product
$\mu_D^u(h,k)$ is $\mathsf{append}(h,k)$, with canonical continuation witness
$\Cont(h,k,\mathsf{append}(h,k))$.

For the forward direction, apply
\autoref{thm:rat-denom-unit-continuation-nonempty-rat-endpoint} to the two
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$ witnesses and the canonical continuation. The
assumption that $\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ is not $\hsame$ to $\emp$ is exactly the
nonempty result condition, so the theorem returns
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)\lor\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(k)$.

For the reverse direction, split the strict-support disjunction. In the left
case, split the carried endpoint $k$ into its empty and genuine alternatives.
If $k\hsame\emp$, the right append unit law identifies
$\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ with $h$, so any empty product would make $h\hsame\emp$,
contradicting $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier\_not\_empty}$. If
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(k)$, apply Rat denominator continuation result
nonemptiness to the canonical continuation.

In the right strict-support case, split the carried endpoint $h$ in the same
way. If $h\hsame\emp$, the left append unit law identifies
$\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ with $k$, and
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier\_not\_empty}$ rejects an empty product. If
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)$, the same Rat denominator continuation result
nonemptiness theorem applies to the canonical continuation.
\end{proof}

\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-strict-product-support-exactness}

$$
N_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,k))

\begin{proof}

$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(\mu_D^u(h,k))$. By
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier\_not\_empty}$, the product cannot be
$\hsame\emp$. Apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-product-nonempty-iff} to this

direction of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-product-nonempty-iff} gives
that $\mu_D^u(h,k)$ is not $\hsame\emp$.

\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-laws}

$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier\_nonempty\_rat}$ to this carried-product witness
and the nonempty product witness. The result is
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(\mu_D^u(h,k))$, equivalently
$N_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,k))$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator empty-unit multiplicative substrate laws]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-multiplicative-substrate-laws}
For the substrate
$(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u,N_D^u)$ of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-multiplicative-substrate}, the
following concrete laws hold.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The unit is carried: $C_D^u(e_D^u)$.
  \item The carrier is closed under multiplication: if $C_D^u(h)$ and
  $C_D^u(k)$, then $C_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,k))$.
  \item The unit laws hold: if $C_D^u(h)$, then
  $R_D^u(\mu_D^u(e_D^u,h),h)$ and $R_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,e_D^u),h)$.
  \item Multiplication is associative up to the classifier: if $C_D^u(h)$,
  $C_D^u(k)$, and $C_D^u(\ell)$, then
  $R_D^u(\mu_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,k),\ell),\mu_D^u(h,\mu_D^u(k,\ell)))$.
  \item The operation respects binary classifier input: if $R_D^u(h,h')$ and
  $R_D^u(k,k')$, then $R_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,k),\mu_D^u(h',k'))$.
  \item Multiplication is commutative up to the classifier: if $C_D^u(h)$ and
  $C_D^u(k)$, then $R_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,k),\mu_D^u(k,h))$.
  \item Strict support is exact for products: if $C_D^u(h)$ and $C_D^u(k)$,
  then
  $$
  (\mu_D^u(h,k)\hsame\emp\to\mathsf{False})
  \Longleftrightarrow
  N_D^u(h)\lor N_D^u(k).
  $$
  \item Empty endpoint contexts preserve and reflect the classifier: if
  $p\hsame\emp$, $q\hsame\emp$, $p'\hsame\emp$, and $q'\hsame\emp$, then
  $$
  R_D^u(\mathsf{append}(p,\mathsf{append}(h,q)),
  \mathsf{append}(p',\mathsf{append}(k,q')))
  \Longleftrightarrow
  R_D^u(h,k).
  $$
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the concrete substrate of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-multiplicative-substrate}. The
carried unit, closure, left and right unit laws, associativity, and binary
classifier congruence are the five laws supplied by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-laws}.

Commutativity is the additional classifier law of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-commutative-monoid-laws}; it uses
the same carried endpoints and the concrete append multiplication of the
substrate.

The strict support clause is exactly
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-product-nonempty-iff}, because
$N_D^u$ is $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ and the product is
$\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ with its canonical continuation witness.

For the empty-context classifier clause, replace $R_D^u$ by
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}$ and apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-classifier-empty-context-exactness}.
This proves preservation and reflection for the same concrete classifier used
by the substrate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator empty-unit classifier empty-context exactness]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-classifier-empty-context-exactness}
For histories $p,q,p',q',h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, if
$p\hsame\emp$, $q\hsame\emp$, $p'\hsame\emp$, and $q'\hsame\emp$, then
empty endpoint contexts do not change the empty-unit denominator classifier:
$$
\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}(\mathsf{append}(p,\mathsf{append}(h,q)),
\mathsf{append}(p',\mathsf{append}(k,q')))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}(h,k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}$ into the two
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$ endpoints and the endpoint comparison by
$\hsame$. The carrier endpoints are handled by
\autoref{thm:rat-denom-unit-carrier-empty-context-iff}, once for
$(p,q,h)$ and once for $(p',q',k)$.

For the forward direction, the contextual classifier supplies the two
contextual carrier witnesses and the comparison between the contextual
endpoints. The two carrier equivalences transport the carrier witnesses back
to $h$ and $k$. The empty left and right append unit laws identify
$\mathsf{append}(p,\mathsf{append}(h,q))$ with $h$ and
$\mathsf{append}(p',\mathsf{append}(k,q'))$ with $k$; composing these
identifications by symmetry and transitivity with the contextual comparison
gives $h\hsame k$. These three pieces repack
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}(h,k)$.

For the reverse direction, start from the carrier witnesses for $h$ and $k$
and from $h\hsame k$. The same two carrier equivalences transport membership
to the contextual endpoints. The empty append identifications transport
$h\hsame k$ to the contextual endpoint comparison, again using symmetry and
transitivity of $\hsame$. The transported carriers and transported endpoint
comparison pack the contextual classifier.

This is not a variant of the carrier exactness theorem: it proves full
classifier preservation by combining carrier exactness at both endpoints with
transport of the endpoint $\hsame$ relation. Consequently the
$\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ denominator envelope can read an empty external
unit context by the normalized classifier on the core endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator external-unit envelope strict support laws]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-strict-support-laws}

strict support predicate of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-strict-support} satisfies:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $R_D^\ast(h,k)\to
  (N_D^\ast(h)\Longleftrightarrow N_D^\ast(k))$.
  \item $N_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(h,k))\Longleftrightarrow
  N_D^\ast(h)\lor N_D^\ast(k)$.
  \item Together with
  \autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-commutative-monoid-laws},

\end{enumerate}

assumptions split each input into a genuine Rat denominator endpoint or an
external empty endpoint.

For support transport along the classifier, split the defining alternatives of
$R_D^\ast(h,k)$. In the guarded carried branch, both strict-support predicates
are exactly the displayed $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ witnesses, so the two
directions are projections. In the empty-empty branch, any strict-support
witness for either endpoint contradicts $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier\_not\_empty}$
after composing its endpoint comparison with the branch comparison to $\emp$.
