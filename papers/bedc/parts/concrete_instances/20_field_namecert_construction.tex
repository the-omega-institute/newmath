\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator empty-unit commutative monoid laws]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-commutative-monoid-laws}
For the tuple $(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u)$ of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-instance},
the five laws of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-laws} hold,
and the multiplication is commutative up to the endpoint classifier: if
$C_D^u(h)$ and $C_D^u(k)$, then
$$
R_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,k),\mu_D^u(k,h)).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold the concrete data of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-instance}.
The first five clauses are precisely
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-laws}.

It remains to prove the displayed commutativity classifier. Split the two
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$ witnesses into their empty endpoint and genuine
Rat denominator endpoint alternatives.

In the genuine/genuine case, the two products are the canonical append
continuations $\Cont(h,k,\mathsf{append}(h,k))$ and
$\Cont(k,h,\mathsf{append}(k,h))$. Apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-commutative-classifier} to
these two continuations and the two Rat denominator carriers. The resulting
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ supplies the carried endpoints and the
$\hsame$ comparison required to pack
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}(\mathsf{append}(h,k),\mathsf{append}(k,h))$.

In a mixed case, one endpoint is $\hsame\emp$ and the other is a genuine Rat
denominator endpoint. The append unit law on the empty side identifies both
products with the genuine endpoint, one directly and one after symmetry of
$\hsame$. Transitivity of $\hsame$ gives
$\mathsf{append}(h,k)\hsame\mathsf{append}(k,h)$, and append closure gives the
two empty-unit carrier witnesses. These data pack the required
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}$.

In the empty/empty case, both inputs are $\hsame\emp$. The left and right
append unit laws identify each product with $\emp$; symmetry and transitivity
of $\hsame$ then identify the two products with each other. The empty branch
of $\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$ carries both products, so the classifier
packing again yields
$R_D^u(\mu_D^u(h,k),\mu_D^u(k,h))$.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Rat denominator empty-unit strict product support]
\label{def:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-strict-product-support}
For the tuple $(C_D^u,R_D^u,\mu_D^u,e_D^u)$ of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-continuation-monoid-instance},
the strict product support predicate is the genuine Rat denominator endpoint
predicate
$$
N_D^u(h)\Longleftrightarrow\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h).
$$
Thus the empty endpoint is carried by $C_D^u$ but is not in the strict support.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator empty-unit product nonempty iff]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-product-nonempty-iff}
For histories $h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$ with $C_D^u(h)$ and $C_D^u(k)$, the
empty-unit product is nonempty exactly when at least one input has strict Rat
denominator support:
$$
(\mu_D^u(h,k)\hsame\emp\to\mathsf{False})
\Longleftrightarrow
N_D^u(h)\lor N_D^u(k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $C_D^u$, $\mu_D^u$, and $N_D^u$. The product
$\mu_D^u(h,k)$ is $\mathsf{append}(h,k)$, with canonical continuation witness
$\Cont(h,k,\mathsf{append}(h,k))$.

For the forward direction, apply
\autoref{thm:rat-denom-unit-continuation-nonempty-rat-endpoint} to the two
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$ witnesses and the canonical continuation. The
assumption that $\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ is not $\hsame$ to $\emp$ is exactly the
nonempty result condition, so the theorem returns
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)\lor\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(k)$.

For the reverse direction, split the strict-support disjunction. In the left
case, split the carried endpoint $k$ into its empty and genuine alternatives.
If $k\hsame\emp$, the right append unit law identifies
$\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ with $h$, so any empty product would make $h\hsame\emp$,
contradicting $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier\_not\_empty}$. If
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(k)$, apply Rat denominator continuation result
nonemptiness to the canonical continuation.

In the right strict-support case, split the carried endpoint $h$ in the same
way. If $h\hsame\emp$, the left append unit law identifies
$\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ with $k$, and
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier\_not\_empty}$ rejects an empty product. If
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}(h)$, the same Rat denominator continuation result
nonemptiness theorem applies to the canonical continuation.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target strict support for the empty-unit
denominator multiplication; BEDC source $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$,
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$, and $\Cont$; carrier $C_D^u$; classifier
$R_D^u$; stability obligation product support exactness; ledger continuation
witness; core theorem
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-empty-unit-product-nonempty-iff}; exactness
predicate nonempty product iff genuine denominator input; standard bridge
ordinary denominator monoid support; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator empty-unit classifier empty-context exactness]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-classifier-empty-context-exactness}
For histories $p,q,p',q',h,k:\mathsf{BHist}$, if
$p\hsame\emp$, $q\hsame\emp$, $p'\hsame\emp$, and $q'\hsame\emp$, then
empty endpoint contexts do not change the empty-unit denominator classifier:
$$
\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}(\mathsf{append}(p,\mathsf{append}(h,q)),
\mathsf{append}(p',\mathsf{append}(k,q')))
\Longleftrightarrow
\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}(h,k).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unfold $\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}$ into the two
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$ endpoints and the endpoint comparison by
$\hsame$. The carrier endpoints are handled by
\autoref{thm:rat-denom-unit-carrier-empty-context-iff}, once for
$(p,q,h)$ and once for $(p',q',k)$.

For the forward direction, the contextual classifier supplies the two
contextual carrier witnesses and the comparison between the contextual
endpoints. The two carrier equivalences transport the carrier witnesses back
to $h$ and $k$. The empty left and right append unit laws identify
$\mathsf{append}(p,\mathsf{append}(h,q))$ with $h$ and
$\mathsf{append}(p',\mathsf{append}(k,q'))$ with $k$; composing these
identifications by symmetry and transitivity with the contextual comparison
gives $h\hsame k$. These three pieces repack
$\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}(h,k)$.

For the reverse direction, start from the carrier witnesses for $h$ and $k$
and from $h\hsame k$. The same two carrier equivalences transport membership
to the contextual endpoints. The empty append identifications transport
$h\hsame k$ to the contextual endpoint comparison, again using symmetry and
transitivity of $\hsame$. The transported carriers and transported endpoint
comparison pack the contextual classifier.

This is not a variant of the carrier exactness theorem: it proves full
classifier preservation by combining carrier exactness at both endpoints with
transport of the endpoint $\hsame$ relation. Consequently the
$\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$ denominator envelope can read an empty external
unit context by the normalized classifier on the core endpoints.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Rat denominator external-unit envelope commutative monoid laws]
\label{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-commutative-monoid-laws}
For the tuple $(C_D^\ast,R_D^\ast,\mu_D^\ast,e_D^\ast)$ of
\autoref{def:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope}, all laws of
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-monoid-laws} hold, and the
envelope multiplication is commutative up to its endpoint classifier: whenever
$C_D^\ast(h)$ and $C_D^\ast(k)$,
$$
R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(h,k),\mu_D^\ast(k,h)).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier inhabitance, closure, associativity, left and right unit laws,
binary classifier congruence, carried restriction, and carried one-sided unit
obstruction are exactly the fields supplied by
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-monoid-laws}.

It remains to prove the displayed commutativity classifier. Split the two
carrier witnesses $C_D^\ast(h)$ and $C_D^\ast(k)$ into their carried Rat
denominator alternatives and their external-empty alternatives.

In the carried/carried case, $\mu_D^\ast(h,k)$ and $\mu_D^\ast(k,h)$ are the
canonical append continuations of the two carried denominators in opposite
orders. Apply
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-continuation-commutative-classifier} to these
two continuations. The carried classifier exactness theorem
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-carried-classifier-exactness}
then repacks the resulting $\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ as the required
$R_D^\ast$ comparison.

In the carried/empty case, the append right-unit and left-unit laws identify
$\mu_D^\ast(h,k)$ and $\mu_D^\ast(k,h)$ with the same carried endpoint $h$,
up to $\hsame$. The envelope classifier normal form,
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-classifier-normal-form},
turns those two endpoint comparisons and the transported carried witness into
$R_D^\ast(\mu_D^\ast(h,k),\mu_D^\ast(k,h))$.

The empty/carried case is symmetric. The same append unit laws identify the two
products with the carried endpoint $k$, and the same normal form converts the
two transported carrier witnesses and the resulting $\hsame$ comparison into
the envelope classifier.

In the empty/empty case, both products are $\hsame$ to $\emp$ by the append unit
laws and transitivity of $\hsame$. Since $e_D^\ast=\emp$, the empty branch of
$R_D^\ast$ classifies the two products directly.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target commutative denominator monoid boundary
for $\FieldUp(\mathsf{RatUp})$; BEDC source $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$,
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$, $\Cont$, $\mathsf{append}$, and $\emp$;
carrier $C_D^\ast$; classifier $R_D^\ast$; stability obligation monoid laws
plus commutativity; ledger external-unit bit plus continuation witness; core
theorem
\autoref{thm:field-rat-denominator-unit-envelope-commutative-monoid-laws};
exactness predicate envelope commutativity over the strict carried restriction;
standard bridge deferred; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[Field affine automorphism inverse is involutive]
\label{thm:field-affine-automorphism-inverse-involutive}
For a BEDC history-level $\FieldUp+\CommRingUp$ candidate over
$\mathsf{BHist}$ satisfying the hypotheses of
\autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-group-certificate}, let
$T=(a,b,d,p_a,p_b)$ be a certified affine automorphism and let
$T^{-1}_{\mathrm{aff}}$ be the explicit inverse certificate supplied by
\autoref{thm:field-affine-inverse-is-certified-affine}. Then applying the same
inverse construction again returns the original affine certificate under the
affine pointwise classifier:
$$
(T^{-1}_{\mathrm{aff}})^{-1}_{\mathrm{aff}}\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}T.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The inverse certificate $T^{-1}_{\mathrm{aff}}$ is closed among certified
affine automorphisms by
\autoref{thm:field-affine-inverse-is-certified-affine}. The identity and
inverse operations of the affine package are the ones recorded in
\autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-identity-and-inverse-package}, so both
$T$ and $T^{-1}_{\mathrm{aff}}$ inhabit the same $\GroupUp$ carrier of
certified affine automorphism histories.

The two inverse equations for $T$ are interpreted through the affine pointwise
classifier. Operation congruence from
\autoref{thm:field-affine-pointwise-classifier-operation-congruence}
transports those equations across classifier-equivalent representatives, so the
candidate obtained by inverting $T^{-1}_{\mathrm{aff}}$ is a left inverse of
$T^{-1}_{\mathrm{aff}}$ in the same group package.

Now apply the $\GroupUp$ inverse involutivity law from
\autoref{thm:group-left-inverse-involutive}; equivalently, use the
left-right inverse uniqueness principle of
\autoref{thm:group-left-right-inverse-uniqueness} to identify the second
inverse with the original certificate. Re-reading that identity through the
affine classifier gives the displayed conclusion.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target affine automorphism group inverse
involutivity; BEDC source $\FieldUp+\CommRingUp$, affine package, and
$\GroupUp$ inverse laws; carrier certified affine automorphism histories;
classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$; stability obligation inverse closure and
inverse uniqueness; ledger nonzero coefficient witnesses and
inverse-certificate provenance; core theorem
\autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-inverse-involutive}; exactness predicate
double-inverse pointwise classifier; standard bridge ordinary affine
automorphism group; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
