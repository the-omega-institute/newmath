\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp algebra reduct compatibility]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-algebra-reduct-compatibility}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, the concrete
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ certificate is compatible with the already licensed algebraic
reducts over BEDC histories. Its denominator continuation product uses the same
empty-unit and associativity data as the unary append $\MonoidUp$ certificate;
its singleton empty obstruction rows restrict to the concrete singleton
$\GroupUp$ and $\AbGroupUp$ certificates; its zero/one singleton boundary
restricts to the singleton empty-history $\RingUp$ laws; and
Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object-complete} consumes these
reduct rows without introducing schematic operation parameters.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:unary-append-monoid-semantic-name-certificate} to the
denominator continuation product. The empty source, left and right unit laws,
append associativity, and append congruence are exactly the rows used by the
denominator carrier and classifier package.

Next apply Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-abgroup-laws}. The singleton empty
obstruction rows have carrier $A_{\emp}$ and classifier $\rho_{\emp}$, so their
group and abelian-group reducts are the corresponding singleton certificates.

For the zero/one boundary, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-ring-laws}. Its singleton
empty-history ring tuple supplies the additive, multiplicative, zero, and one
laws required by the boundary rows.

Finally apply Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object-complete}.
That theorem packages the displayed denominator, singleton, and ring reduct
rows into the concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object, and its conclusion states
that no schematic $\mathsf{mul}$, $\mathsf{add}$, or $\mathsf{neg}$ parameter is
part of the certificate.
\end{proof}
