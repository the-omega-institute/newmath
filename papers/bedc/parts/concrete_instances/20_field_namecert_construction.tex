\begin{theorem}[Field two-sided affine automorphisms compose]
\label{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism-composition}
For a BEDC history-level $\FieldUp$ candidate with the operations and laws used
by \autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism}, and with the
distributivity laws and zero-apartness maps used by
\autoref{thm:field-product-apartness-inverse-product-reverse}, define
$$
T_1(x)=\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b),d)
$$
and
$$
T_2(x)=\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',x),b'),d').
$$
Given apartness witnesses for $a,b,a',b'$, the product-apartness theorem supplies
nonzero indices for $\mathsf{mul}(a',a)$ and $\mathsf{mul}(b,b')$. For every
$x:\mathsf{BHist}$ the composite is classified by
$$
T_2(T_1(x))\hsame
\mathsf{add}(
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',a),x),\mathsf{mul}(b,b')),
\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',d),b'),d')).
$$
Therefore $T_2\circ T_1$ is the two-sided affine map with left factor
$\mathsf{mul}(a',a)$, right factor $\mathsf{mul}(b,b')$, and offset
$\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',d),b'),d')$, and this composite map
is a certified automorphism by
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Expand $T_2(T_1(x))$ by the two displayed definitions. The outer affine map
applies $a'$ on the left and $b'$ on the right to the sum
$\mathsf{add}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b),d)$, then adds $d'$.

Use the distributivity laws to separate the variable contribution from the
offset contribution. This gives one term containing
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a,x),b)$ and one term containing $d$, both still
transported through the outer factors $a'$ and $b'$.

Apply multiplication associativity and multiplication congruence to collect the
variable term as
$$
\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',a),x),\mathsf{mul}(b,b')).
$$
The remaining transported offset term is
$\mathsf{mul}(\mathsf{mul}(a',d),b')$, and the final outer offset is $d'$.

Apply
\autoref{thm:field-product-apartness-inverse-product-reverse} to the apartness
witnesses for $a',a$ and again to the apartness witnesses for $b,b'$. These two
applications provide the nonzero indices required for the collected left and
right composite factors.

With these two indices and the displayed composite offset, invoke
\autoref{thm:field-affine-two-sided-certified-automorphism}. The classifier
identity above identifies $T_2(T_1(x))$ with that certified two-sided affine map,
so the composite is a certified affine automorphism.
\end{proof}
