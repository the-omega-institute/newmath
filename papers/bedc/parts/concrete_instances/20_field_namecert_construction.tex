\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp canonical machine-checkable exit]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-machine-checkable-exit}
For $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$ and
Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, the canonical
phase-exit target is the single $\mathsf{BHist}$-anchored certificate whose
carrier is $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$, whose classifier is
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$, whose denominator tuple is
$(\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier},\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier},
\mathsf{append},\emp,\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier})$, and whose contextual action
is $\Gamma$.

Its operation rows are exactly the rows covered by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-schema-instance},
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-operation-exit-exhaustion},
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-algebra-hierarchy-exit-witness}, and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object-complete}. Therefore the
canonical machine-checkable exit is not a schematic algebra interface, but the
concrete certificate object carried by the displayed RatUp history source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the concrete source tuple $\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$ and
Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}. The carrier,
classifier, denominator tuple, and contextual action are the displayed
$\mathsf{BHist}$ rows, so the target has one concrete source anchor.

Apply \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-schema-instance} to identify the
source, carrier, classifier, denominator, contextual-action, context-pair, and
affine coordinates. Apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-operation-exit-exhaustion} to cover the
operation rows, including denominator histories, context pairs, affine
witnesses, inverse and nonzero witnesses, product exactness, and selector
endpoint rows.

Apply \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-algebra-hierarchy-exit-witness} to package
those rows as the Phase Algebra Hierarchy paper-certificate object. Finally
apply \autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object-complete} to identify this
package with Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}. The formal
target therefore belongs naturally to the namespace
\texttt{BEDC.Derived.FieldUp}.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target constructive
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ phase exit; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\RatUp$,
$\CommRingUp$, denominator, contextual-action, context-pair, and affine rows;
carrier $\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$; classifier
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$; stability and exactness rows from
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-schema-instance},
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-operation-exit-exhaustion},
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-algebra-hierarchy-exit-witness}, and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object-complete}; ledger denominator
histories, context pairs, affine witnesses, inverse/nonzero witnesses, and
selector endpoint rows; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp algebra reduct compatibility]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-algebra-reduct-compatibility}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, the concrete
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ certificate is compatible with the already licensed algebraic
reducts over BEDC histories. Its denominator continuation product uses the same
empty-unit and associativity data as the unary append $\MonoidUp$ certificate;
its singleton empty obstruction rows restrict to the concrete singleton
$\GroupUp$ and $\AbGroupUp$ certificates; its zero/one singleton boundary
restricts to the singleton empty-history $\RingUp$ laws; and
Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object-complete} consumes these
reduct rows without introducing schematic operation parameters.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:unary-append-monoid-semantic-name-certificate} to the
denominator continuation product. The empty source, left and right unit laws,
append associativity, and append congruence are exactly the rows used by the
denominator carrier and classifier package.

Next apply Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-abgroup-laws}. The singleton empty
obstruction rows have carrier $A_{\emp}$ and classifier $\rho_{\emp}$, so their
group and abelian-group reducts are the corresponding singleton certificates.

For the zero/one boundary, apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-ring-laws}. Its singleton
empty-history ring tuple supplies the additive, multiplicative, zero, and one
laws required by the boundary rows.

Finally apply Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object-complete}.
That theorem packages the displayed denominator, singleton, and ring reduct
rows into the concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object, and its conclusion states
that no schematic $\mathsf{mul}$, $\mathsf{add}$, or $\mathsf{neg}$ parameter is
part of the certificate.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target algebra-hierarchy reduct compatibility
for $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$; BEDC source $\mathsf{BHist}$ with the concrete
$\mathsf{RatUpFieldSource}_F$ rows; carrier rows
$\mathsf{RatHistoryCarrier}$ and $\mathsf{RatDenomUnitCarrier}$; classifier rows
$\mathsf{RatHistoryClassifier}$ and $\mathsf{RatDenomUnitClassifier}$; ledger
the denominator continuation reduct, singleton empty obstruction reducts, and
singleton zero/one ring reduct; core theorem list
Theorem~\ref{thm:unary-append-monoid-semantic-name-certificate},
Theorem~\ref{thm:concrete-singleton-history-group-laws},
Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-abgroup-laws},
Theorem~\ref{thm:singleton-empty-history-ring-laws}, and
Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object-complete}; exactness
predicate reduct-row coverage for
Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}; standard bridge
deferred; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp affine automorphism action certificate]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-automorphism-action-certificate}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, the concrete
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object exports the affine automorphism action certificate
on $\mathsf{BHist}$ required by downstream algebra. The action carrier is
$\mathsf{AffAut}_F$; its classifier is $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$; its identity,
composition, and inverse laws are those of
Theorem~\ref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-group-certificate}; each affine
fiber is singleton-classified by
Theorem~\ref{thm:field-affine-certified-fiber-singleton}; and common affine
factors cancel exactly by
Theorem~\ref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-cancellation-exactness}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First apply Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object-complete} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-algebra-reduct-compatibility}. These fix the
concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ operations and the reduct rows over
$\mathsf{BHist}$, so the action is attached to the same exit object rather than
to a schematic field package.

Next apply Theorem~\ref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-group-certificate} to
the fixed operations. Its carrier $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ and classifier
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$ supply the identity, composition, and inverse fields of
the affine action certificate.

Finally project Theorem~\ref{thm:field-affine-certified-fiber-singleton} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-cancellation-exactness}. The first
projection supplies singleton classification of every affine fiber, and the
second supplies exact cancellation for common affine factors, which are the
action exactness fields consumed downstream.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target affine group action over a field; BEDC
source concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit rows over $\mathsf{BHist}$; carrier
$\mathsf{AffAut}_F$; classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$; stability obligations
group laws, fiber singleton exactness, and cancellation exactness; ledger
nonzero coefficient witnesses, product witnesses, offsets, and inverse
witnesses; core theorem
Theorem~\ref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-automorphism-action-certificate};
exactness predicate fiber and cancellation exactness for the action; standard
bridge ordinary affine action; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
