\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp affine inverse antihomomorphism exit]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inverse-antihom-exit}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, let
$T,U:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ be certified affine automorphisms in the concrete
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object. Let $U\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T$ be the concrete
affine composite supplied by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-exit-action-inverse-package}, and let
$\iota_F(-)$ denote the explicit inverse witness supplied by
\autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-identity-and-inverse-package} under the
same concrete action classifier. Then the inverse of a composite is the reverse
composite of the explicit inverses under the concrete affine classifier:
$$
\iota_F(U\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}
\iota_F(T)\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}\iota_F(U).
$$
The same classifier also identifies the double inverse with the original
certified affine automorphism:
$$
\iota_F(\iota_F(T))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}T.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First fix the concrete exit action by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-exit-action-inverse-package}. This fixes
the carrier $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$, the concrete affine composite
$\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$, the explicit inverse row, and the classifier
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$ inside
Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}.

For the composite inverse law, apply
\autoref{thm:field-affine-composite-inverse-reverse-coherence} pointwise to the
two certified affine automorphisms. The theorem identifies the collected
inverse of $U\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T$ with the pointwise reverse composite
$\iota_F(T)\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}\iota_F(U)$ under $\hsame$.

The affine action classifier from the fixed exit package lifts this pointwise
$\hsame$ relation to the displayed relation
$$
\iota_F(U\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}T)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}
\iota_F(T)\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}\iota_F(U).
$$

For the double-inverse clause, view $T$ as a two-sided inverse candidate for
$\iota_F(T)$ by the identity and inverse fields of
\autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-identity-and-inverse-package}. Apply
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inverse-determinacy-exit} to compare this
candidate with the explicit inverse $\iota_F(\iota_F(T))$ of $\iota_F(T)$.
The resulting classifier equality is exactly
$\iota_F(\iota_F(T))\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}T$.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target affine inverse antihomomorphism; BEDC
source concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit rows plus $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$; carrier
certified affine automorphisms; classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$; stability
obligations concrete composition, inverse uniqueness, and reverse-composite
inverse coherence; ledger factor witnesses, product witnesses, inverse
witnesses, and offsets; core theorem list
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-exit-action-inverse-package},
\autoref{thm:field-affine-composite-inverse-reverse-coherence}, and
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inverse-determinacy-exit}; exactness
predicate inverse antihomomorphism plus double-inverse law; standard bridge
deferred; status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.

\begin{theorem}[RatUp FieldUp affine conjugation exactness]
\label{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-conjugation-exactness}
For Definition~\ref{def:ratup-fieldup-concrete-exit-object}, let
$A,S,T:\mathsf{AffAut}_F$ be certified affine automorphisms in the concrete
$\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object. Let $\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$ be the concrete
affine composite, let $\iota_F(-)$ be the explicit inverse witness, and let
$\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$ be the affine classifier supplied by
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-exit-action-inverse-package}. Define
$$
\mathrm{Conj}_A(S)=A\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}S\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}\iota_F(A).
$$
Then conjugation by $A$ is classifier-exact:
$$
\mathrm{Conj}_A(S)\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}\mathrm{Conj}_A(T)
\Longleftrightarrow S\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}T.
$$
Its inverse conjugation action is conjugation by the explicit inverse:
$$
\mathrm{Conj}_{\iota_F(A)}(\mathrm{Conj}_A(S))
\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}S
\quad\text{and}\quad
\mathrm{Conj}_A(\mathrm{Conj}_{\iota_F(A)}(S))
\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}S.
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
First fix the concrete affine group package from
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-canonical-exit-action-inverse-package}. This supplies
the carrier $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$, the operation $\circ_{\mathrm{aff}}$, the
classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$, and the explicit inverse row inside the
concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit object.

Apply \autoref{thm:group-conjugation-equation-exactness} to this affine group
package with $a=A$ and with the equation target specialized to
$\mathrm{Conj}_A(T)$. The group theorem gives the middle-factor equation
equivalent to the displayed conjugation equation. Use the two cancellation
clauses and operation congruence in
\autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-cancellation-exactness} to identify that
middle-factor equation with $S\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}T$.

For the inverse-action clause, expand
$\mathrm{Conj}_{\iota_F(A)}(\mathrm{Conj}_A(S))$ and
$\mathrm{Conj}_A(\mathrm{Conj}_{\iota_F(A)}(S))$. The reverse-composite inverse
law and double-inverse clause of
\autoref{thm:ratup-fieldup-affine-inverse-antihom-exit} identify the conjugating
factors as mutual inverse witnesses. The remaining outer factors cancel by
\autoref{thm:field-affine-automorphism-cancellation-exactness}, leaving exactly
$S$ under $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$ in both composites.
\end{proof}

Per-object template: traditional target inner automorphism of the affine
automorphism group; BEDC source concrete $\FieldUp(\RatUp)$ exit rows plus
$\GroupUp$; carrier $\mathsf{AffAut}_F$; classifier $\equiv_{\mathrm{aff}}$;
ledger conjugation factors and inverse witnesses; exactness predicate
classifier-exact conjugation; standard bridge ordinary inner automorphism;
status $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
