Following Kelley and Engelking, the $\RegularTopologicalSpaceUp$ packet records the textbook separation surface in which a displayed point and a displayed closed-set row are separated by open neighbourhoods. It sits over the indexed-open carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-topology-namecert}, beside the separation-function route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-urysohn-lemma-namecert}, before the metrization route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-urysohn-metrization-namecert}, and beside the metric consumer surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}. The packet names only a local regularity read over displayed open and closed rows; it does not import host powersets, classical complements, arbitrary normality, selected functions, or a host metric.

\begin{definition}[Regular topological space carrier]
\label{def:regular-topological-space-carrier}
A $\RegularTopologicalSpaceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(T,O,F,M,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the $\TopologyUp$ source row, carrying the open-index classifier, open-neighbourhood membership reads, and finite closure witnesses available to the packet.
\item $O$ is the open-neighbourhood row for the displayed point, recording the named open containing the point before any separation request is consumed.
\item $F$ is the closed-set row disjoint from the displayed point, presented as a finite ledger over the same topology source rather than as a host complement.
\item $M$ is the point-membership row, tying the inspected point to $O$ and excluding it from $F$ by displayed membership and nonmembership reads.
\item $S$ is the separation-witness row, recording open rows $U,V$ with the point in $U$, the closed row $F$ contained in $V$, and no accepted overlap between $U$ and $V$.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the topology source, open-neighbourhood row, closed-set row, point-membership row, separation witnesses, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a regularity request through $T,O,F,M,S,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularTopologicalSpaceUp$, $\TopologyUp$, $\UrysohnLemmaUp$, $\UrysohnMetrizationUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularTopologicalSpaceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $T,O,F,M,S,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for host complements, external powersets, selected Urysohn functions, normal-space separation, metrization outside a displayed route, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular topological space NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-topological-space-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularTopologicalSpaceUp$ carrier
$$
R=(T,O,F,M,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularTopologicalSpaceUp}$ surface consists exactly of topology-source admission through $T$; open-neighbourhood admission through $O$; closed-set row admission through $F$; point-membership and point-nonmembership readback through $M$; regular separation exactness through $S$; componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$; displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$; provenance through $P$; and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-topological-space-carrier}. The accepted packet displays the topology source, the point neighbourhood, the closed-set row, the point-membership row, the separation witness, and the structural rows.

The open-neighbourhood and closed-set obligations are read before separation. The rows $O,F,M$ identify the inspected point, the containing open, and the closed row avoided by that point over the same $\TopologyUp$ source.

Regular separation exactness is the row $S$. It supplies the two separating open rows and their disjointness read, so the certificate has no need for a selected complement or an ambient powerset argument.

The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite route. Since no further coordinate is present, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular topological space neighbourhood separation]
\label{thm:regular-topological-space-neighborhood-separation}
Every neighbourhood-separation read exported by an accepted $\RegularTopologicalSpaceUp$ packet must first display a $\TopologyUp$ source row, a point-open row, a closed-set row disjoint from the displayed point, and the point-membership row tying them together. Only after those rows are visible may a consumer read the separating open-neighbourhood pair, and the later $\UrysohnLemmaUp$, $\UrysohnMetrizationUp$, or $\MetricSpaceUp$ consumer receives only that displayed finite route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the topology source $T$. It supplies indexed open rows, but no regularity assertion is available until the packet displays the point-open row $O$, the closed-set row $F$, and the membership row $M$.

The separation witness is downstream of those rows. The row $S$ records the two separating open neighbourhoods, the containment of the closed-set row in one of them, the displayed point membership in the other, and the disjointness read between the two open rows.

Transport and replay preserve the same order. The componentwise $\hsame$ row $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ row $C$ reread $T,O,F,M,S$ before provenance or local naming can be inspected.

Thus a later Urysohn, metrization, or metric consumer receives a finite regularity route over the displayed topology source. It receives no host complement, external powerset, selected separating function, normal-space theorem, or host metric from the regular-space packet.
\end{proof}
