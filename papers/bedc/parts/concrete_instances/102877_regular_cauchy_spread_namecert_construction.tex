\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchySpreadUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-spread-namecert}

Following Brouwer on spreads and Bishop--Bridges on regular Cauchy real names, the $\RegularCauchySpreadUp$ packet records the finite spread-law windows from which a $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer and then a $\RealUp$ seal may be read. It is downstream of the finite-prefix discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-brouwer-spread-namecert}, the finite request schedule of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal seal discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names finite spread observations and regular Cauchy handoff data; it is not a completed branch, quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice route, host real equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy spread carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-spread-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchySpreadUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(S,W,T,R,L,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the spread-law row. It records the decidable admitted-prefix tree and the finite extension checks inherited from the $\BrouwerSpreadUp$ surface.
\item $W$ is the finite window row. Each displayed request is a StreamName-scheduled spread prefix and carries only the observations listed in the packet.
\item $T$ is the dyadic tolerance row. It records the requested precision, denominator budget, comparison witnesses, and tolerance refinements through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row. It turns each accepted finite spread window and tolerance row into the corresponding regular rational observation without selecting an infinite branch.
\item $L$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row. It records the terminal finite-window seal exposed after the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row has been displayed and after the dyadic tolerance checks have been replayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the spread law, finite windows, dyadic tolerances, regular rational readback, Real seal, continuation replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay from a finite precision request through $S,W,T,R,L,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchySpreadUp$, $\BrouwerSpreadUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchySpreadUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed tuple $S,W,T,R,L,H,C,P,N$. The carrier has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected branches, countable choice, host real equality, open-cover compactness, ambient Real completeness, or equality of unseen tails.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy spread NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-spread-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchySpreadUp$ carrier
$$
G=(S,W,T,R,L,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchySpreadUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the spread law, finite windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular rational readback, Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item spread-window admissibility, requiring every window in $W$ to be licensed by the finite spread-law row $S$ and by the StreamName schedule visible in the packet;
\item dyadic regularity, requiring every precision request in $T$ to provide the finite denominator budget and comparison witnesses needed by the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$;
\item seal handoff, requiring every public $\RealUp$ seal in $L$ to be read only after $S,W,T$ and $R$ have been displayed and replayed;
\item structural non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve the same finite route under $\hsame$, replay it under $\Cont$, record exact provenance, and export no quotient stream, selected branch, countable-choice selector, host real equality, open-cover compactness, ambient Real completeness, Lean verification, or standard bridge.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is the projection from \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-spread-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the rows $S,W,T,R,L,H,C,P,N$.

Spread-window admissibility is internal to $S$ and $W$. A displayed request first checks the decidable spread-law row, then opens only the finite StreamName-scheduled observations present in the window row.

Dyadic regularity is internal to $T$ and $R$. The tolerance row supplies the denominator budget, comparison data, and refinements that the regular rational readback uses; no infinite branch or quotient stream is introduced.

The seal handoff is downstream of those rows. A public $\RealUp$ consumer reaches $L$ only after the spread window, tolerance ledger, and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback have been replayed.

The remaining clauses are structural. The row $H$ transports each displayed coordinate componentwise, $C$ replays the same finite route, and $P,N$ source and name the packet. Since the excluded objects are not coordinates of $G$, the five rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy spread RegSeqRat--Real handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-spread-regseqrat-real-handoff}
Every accepted $\RegularCauchySpreadUp$ packet exposes a $\RegSeqRatUp$ consumer through the displayed finite spread windows and dyadic tolerance rows
$$
S,W,T,H,C,P,N,
$$
and exposes a downstream $\RealUp$ seal only through the already displayed regular rational readback row $R$ and seal row $L$. Consequently the packet cannot export a quotient stream, selected branch, countable-choice selector, host real equality, open-cover compactness, ambient Real completeness, Lean verification, or standard bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted packet $G=(S,W,T,R,L,H,C,P,N)$. The only route to a regular rational observation is through the spread-law row $S$, the finite window row $W$, and the dyadic tolerance row $T$.

Replay the continuation row $C$. It checks the admitted spread prefix, opens the finite StreamName-scheduled window, and then reads the denominator budget and comparison witnesses recorded in $T$.

The $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ is therefore a consumer of displayed finite data rather than a selector for an infinite branch. Componentwise transport in $H$ preserves that route under $\hsame$.

The $\RealUp$ seal is later in the packet. A public seal read reaches $L$ only after the regular rational row $R$ has been displayed, and provenance through $P,N$ names the same finite route. There is no separate coordinate from which the excluded quotient, choice, equality, compactness, completeness, verification, or bridge principles can be read.
\end{proof}
