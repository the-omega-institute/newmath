\begin{proposition}[Uniformly continuous image of a totally bounded source]
\label{prop:uniformly-continuous-image-totally-bounded-source}
Let $X$ and $Y$ be certified $\MetricSpaceUp$ objects with the metric data of
\autoref{def:metric-carrier}. Let $S$ be a certified $\TotallyBoundedUp$ source
subset of $X$ in the domain discipline of
\autoref{def:continuous-source-specification}. Write
$\mathsf{TotBdd}_X(S)$ for its finite-net field, in the internal form that each
source precision $\delta$ has a finite list
$A_\delta=(a_1,\ldots,a_m)$ of $S$-admitted centers such that every
$x\in S$ satisfies $\mathsf{Close}_X^{\delta}(x,a_i)$ for some $i$.
Let $G$ be a certified $\ContinuousMapUp$ graph from $S$ to $Y$, with graph
totality and single-valuedness as in \autoref{def:continuous-carrier}. Suppose
that a unary modulus ledger $\delta_G$ satisfies the modulus-validity field of
\autoref{def:continuous-stability-certificate} on $S$: for every target
precision $n$, it supplies a source precision $\delta_G(n)$ such that
$$
\mathsf{Close}_X^{\delta_G(n)}(x,x')
\Longrightarrow
\mathsf{Close}_Y^n(y,y')
$$
whenever $x,x'\in S$ and $G(x,y)$ and $G(x',y')$ are graph witnesses. Define
the image predicate by
$$
G[S](y)\Longleftrightarrow \exists x.\;S(x)\wedge G(x,y).
$$
Then $G[S]$ is certified totally bounded in $Y$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Fix a target precision $n$, and let $\delta=\delta_G(n)$ be the source
precision supplied by the certified modulus ledger. The finite-net field of
$S$ returns a finite $\delta$-net
$A_\delta=(a_1,\ldots,a_m)$ whose centers carry $S$-membership certificates and
whose cover field says that every $x\in S$ is
$\mathsf{Close}_X^{\delta}$ to one of the centers.

Apply graph totality to each admitted center $a_i$. Induction on the finite
source list produces a finite target list $B_n=(b_1,\ldots,b_m)$ with graph
witnesses $G(a_i,b_i)$ for all $i$. This is a finite certificate construction:
the empty list gives the empty target list, and the cons step uses one instance
of graph totality and the induction hypothesis. Each $b_i$ lies in the image
predicate, witnessed by $a_i\in S$ together with $G(a_i,b_i)$.

It remains to prove that $B_n$ covers $G[S]$ at precision $n$. Let $y$ satisfy
$G[S](y)$. Unpack the image witness to obtain $x\in S$ and $G(x,y)$. The
finite $\delta$-net for $S$ gives an index $i$ such that
$\mathsf{Close}_X^{\delta}(x,a_i)$. Since both $x$ and $a_i$ are admitted by
$S$, the modulus-validity field applies to the graph witnesses $G(x,y)$ and
$G(a_i,b_i)$ and yields
$$
\mathsf{Close}_Y^n(y,b_i).
$$
Thus every image point is covered by one of the finitely many image centers.
Single-valuedness of the graph certificate makes the chosen $b_i$ stable under
alternative graph outputs, and the classifier and distance-stability fields of
\autoref{def:metric-classifier-specification} and
\autoref{def:metric-stability-certificate} transport the cover judgment across
source and target representatives. The target precision $n$ was arbitrary, so
the finite lists $B_n$ certify $\mathsf{TotBdd}_Y(G[S])$.
\end{proof}
