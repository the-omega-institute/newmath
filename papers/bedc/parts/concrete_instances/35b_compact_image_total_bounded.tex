\begin{definition}[BEDC image finite-net ledger]
\label{def:compact-image-finite-net-ledger}
Let $source$, $\mathsf{map}$, $target$, $\delta$, and $\varepsilon$ be histories
belonging to the metric and continuous-map naming certificates. A BEDC image
finite-net ledger is the history relation
$$
\mathsf{ImageFiniteNetLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\delta,\varepsilon,
\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},\mathsf{ledger})
$$
whose accepted row contains a source $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ $\mathcal{A}$, a
target $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ $\mathcal{B}$, and only BEDC rows over histories.
For each bundled source center row $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\mathcal{A})$ there is
a center history $a$ admitted by the source finite-net ledger at precision
$\delta$. For each such center, the graph ledger supplies a bundled target row
$\mathsf{InBundle}(q,\mathcal{B})$ carrying a history $b$ and a row
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(a,\mathsf{map},b,\mathsf{modulus}_a,
\mathsf{cert}_a,\mathsf{distance}_a).
$$

The target-distance component is recorded by $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$:
for every image witness row
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(x,\mathsf{map},y,\mathsf{modulus}_x,
\mathsf{cert}_x,\mathsf{distance}_x),
$$
the source finite-net coverage row gives a bundled center $a$ and a source
distance witness below $\delta$, and the image ledger returns a bundled target
center $b$ together with a target row
$$
\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(y,b,d_y)
$$
certified below $\varepsilon$. This coverage row is stable under $\hsame$ in
the source point, the image point, the bundled center, and the displayed
distance histories; graph single-valuedness and metric-distance classifier
transport are part of the ledger data. No external finite list, image predicate,
or host function is a field of the relation.
\end{definition}
\leandef{BEDC.Derived.CompactUp.ImageFiniteNetLedger}

\begin{proposition}[Uniformly continuous image of a BEDC finite-net source]
\label{prop:uniformly-continuous-image-totally-bounded-source}
Let $source$ and $target$ be metric BEDC carriers whose distance rows are
encoded by $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$, and let
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\mathsf{modulus},
\mathsf{cert},\mathsf{distance})
$$
be a $\ContinuousMapUp$ carrier with graph totality, graph single-valuedness,
modulus validity, and classifier transport. For a target precision history
$\varepsilon$, suppose an accepted modulus-validity row of this carrier selects
a source precision history $\delta$. If the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ source
certificate supplies a finite-net ledger at precision $\delta$, with source
bundle $\mathcal{A}$ and classifier-stable source coverage, then there exist a
target $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ $\mathcal{B}$ and a history
$\mathsf{ledger}$ such that
$$
\mathsf{ImageFiniteNetLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\delta,\varepsilon,
\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},\mathsf{ledger})
$$
holds for the image carrier determined by the displayed
$\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}$ rows.

The ten-field certificate template is as follows.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item traditional target: a uniformly continuous image of a totally bounded
  metric source is totally bounded;
  \item BEDC source: $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, and
  $\ContinuousMapUp$ histories over $\mathsf{BHist}$;
  \item carrier: the image finite-net relation of
  \autoref{def:compact-image-finite-net-ledger};
  \item classifier: $\hsame$ on source histories, target histories, graph rows,
  and distance rows;
  \item stability obligations: graph single-valuedness, modulus-validity
  transport, and $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ classifier transport;
  \item ledger: the source $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, the constructed target
  $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, graph rows at bundled source centers, and target
  distance rows;
  \item core theorem list: this proposition together with the image finite-net
  ledger definition;
  \item exactness predicate: classifier-stable target coverage for every
  displayed image witness row;
  \item standard bridge: the ordinary metric theorem that uniformly continuous
  maps preserve total boundedness;
  \item status: $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompactUp.ImageFiniteNetLedger\_singleton\_target\_bundle}
\begin{proof}
Construct the target bundle by induction on the source $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$
constructors. In the $\Bnil$ case, use $\Bnil$ as the target bundle; the source
coverage row has no admitted center to enumerate, and any image witness is
handled by the source coverage contradiction supplied by the finite-net ledger.

For a cons source bundle $\Bcons(p,\mathcal{A}_0)$, first apply the induction
hypothesis to the tail. The head row $\mathsf{InBundle}(p,\Bcons(p,\mathcal{A}_0))$
carries a source center history $a$. Graph totality from the displayed
$\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}$ supplies a target history $b$ with
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(a,\mathsf{map},b,\mathsf{modulus}_a,
\mathsf{cert}_a,\mathsf{distance}_a).
$$
Adjoin the corresponding target probe to the recursively built target bundle,
and record this graph row in the image ledger.

Now take an arbitrary image witness
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(x,\mathsf{map},y,\mathsf{modulus}_x,
\mathsf{cert}_x,\mathsf{distance}_x).
$$
The source finite-net coverage row at precision $\delta$ gives a bundled
center $a$ and a source distance row
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(x,a,d_x)$ certified below $\delta$. If this
center lies in the tail bundle, use the recursive ledger. If it is the head
center, use the graph row for $a$ and the modulus-validity row selected by
$\varepsilon$ to transport the source distance witness to a target distance
row $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(y,b,d_y)$ certified below $\varepsilon$.

It remains to show the row is classifier-stable. If the image witness is
presented with an $\hsame$ source representative, source coverage stability
transports the chosen center and source distance witness. If the graph row at a
center is presented with another target output, graph single-valuedness gives
the corresponding $\hsame$ target history. The metric-distance classifier
transport then carries $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}(y,b,d_y)$ across the
target representative and the displayed distance history, so the same target
bundle certifies coverage for every equivalent image row.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[BEDC image located-refinement ledger]
\label{def:compact-image-located-refinement-ledger}
Let $source$, $\mathsf{map}$, $target$, $\mathsf{modulus}$, $\mathsf{cert}$, and $\mathsf{distance}$ be histories belonging to the metric and continuous-map certificates, and let $\varepsilon$ and $\delta$ be target and source precision histories related by the modulus-validity row. A BEDC image located-refinement ledger is the relation
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ImageLocatedRefinementLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\mathsf{modulus},
\varepsilon,\delta,\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},\\
\qquad imageSubset,imageLocated,imageFinite,imageIntermediate,imageCompact,
finalImageLocated,finalImageIntermediate,finalImageCompact)
\end{array}
$$
whose accepted row contains:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item the graph-totality, graph single-valuedness, modulus-validity, and metric-distance classifier rows of the displayed $\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}$;
  \item the finite-net image row
  $$
  \mathsf{ImageFiniteNetLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\delta,\varepsilon,
  \mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},imageFinite);
  $$
  \item unary histories $imageSubset$, $imageLocated$, and $imageFinite$, together with continuation ledgers
  $$
  \Cont(imageSubset,imageLocated,imageIntermediate)
  \quad\hbox{and}\quad
  \Cont(imageIntermediate,imageFinite,imageCompact);
  $$
  \item the transported target located-refinement chain
  $$
  \mathsf{CompactLocatedRefinementChain}(imageFinite,imageLocated,imageIntermediate,
  imageCompact,finalImageLocated,finalImageIntermediate,finalImageCompact);
  $$
  \item classifier stability under $\hsame$ in the image point, the displayed graph row, the target finite-net center, and the target metric-distance histories.
\end{enumerate}
The subset history $imageSubset$ is the graph image of the source subset: a target history is admitted exactly when it is related by a displayed continuous-map row to a source history admitted by the source compact carrier. No external set of target points or host-level function is a field of the ledger.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact image ledger classifier stability]
\label{thm:compact-image-ledger-classifier-stability}
Let $source$, $\mathsf{map}$, $target$, $\mathsf{modulus}$, $\varepsilon$,
$\delta$, $\mathcal{A}$, and $\mathcal{B}$ be the source and target histories,
precision histories, and probe bundles of the compact image construction. Assume
the finite-net image row
$$
\mathsf{ImageFiniteNetLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\delta,\varepsilon,
\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},imageFinite)
$$
and the located-refinement image row
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ImageLocatedRefinementLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\mathsf{modulus},
\varepsilon,\delta,\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},\\
\qquad imageSubset,imageLocated,imageFinite,imageIntermediate,imageCompact,
finalImageLocated,finalImageIntermediate,finalImageCompact)
\end{array}
$$
hold. Suppose the same image witness is presented through transported histories:
an $\hsame$ image point, an $\hsame$ graph row, an $\hsame$ target finite-net
center, $\hsame$ target metric-distance histories, and $\hsame$ transports for
the five image carrier ledgers
$$
\begin{array}{c}
imageSubset \hsame imageSubset',
\qquad finalImageLocated \hsame finalImageLocated',
\qquad imageFinite \hsame imageFinite',\\
finalImageIntermediate \hsame finalImageIntermediate',
\qquad finalImageCompact \hsame finalImageCompact'.
\end{array}
$$
Then the unchanged source bundle $\mathcal{A}$ and target bundle $\mathcal{B}$
repack the transported rows
$$
\mathsf{ImageFiniteNetLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\delta,\varepsilon,
\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},imageFinite')
$$
and
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ImageLocatedRefinementLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\mathsf{modulus},
\varepsilon,\delta,\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},\\
\qquad imageSubset',imageLocated',imageFinite',imageIntermediate',imageCompact',
finalImageLocated',finalImageIntermediate',finalImageCompact')
\end{array}
$$
for the transported image rows. Moreover, whenever
$$
\mathsf{CompactWitnessCarrier}(imageSubset,finalImageLocated,imageFinite,
finalImageIntermediate,finalImageCompact)
$$
holds, the transported compact witness carrier
$$
\mathsf{CompactWitnessCarrier}(imageSubset',finalImageLocated',imageFinite',
finalImageIntermediate',finalImageCompact')
$$
also holds.

The ten-field certificate fit is as follows.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item traditional target: uniformly continuous images preserve compact witness
  data under classifier-stable target coverage;
  \item BEDC source: $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, and $\CompactUp$
  histories over $\mathsf{BHist}$;
  \item carrier: the image finite-net ledger and the image located-refinement
  ledger;
  \item classifier: $\hsame$ on image points, graph rows, bundled target centers,
  metric-distance histories, and image carrier ledgers;
  \item stability obligations: graph single-valuedness together with
  $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ classifier transport;
  \item ledger: the source and target $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ data, the graph rows
  at source centers, the target distance rows, and the compact image continuation
  rows;
  \item core theorem list: this theorem;
  \item exactness predicate: transported classifier-stable target coverage;
  \item standard bridge: uniformly continuous images preserve compact witness
  data;
  \item status: $\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.CompactUp.ImageLocatedRefinementLedger\_hsame\_transport}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the finite-net image ledger. For a transported image witness, source
coverage stability transports the chosen source center and its source
$\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ row along the displayed $\hsame$ source and
distance data.

The graph row at that source center is stable because graph single-valuedness
identifies the transported target output with the original bundled target
history. Apply $\mathsf{MetricDistanceWitness}$ classifier transport to the
target output, bundled center, and displayed target distance history. The same
target bundle $\mathcal{B}$ therefore gives the transported coverage row, and
the finite-net ledger repacks with $imageFinite'$.

Next unpack the image located-refinement ledger. Its continuation rows and
located-refinement chain are classifier data over the same graph image and the
same target finite-net ledger; the supplied $\hsame$ transports for
$imageSubset$, $imageFinite$, $finalImageLocated$, $finalImageIntermediate$, and
$finalImageCompact$ carry those rows to the primed histories. Repacking these
transported rows gives the stated
$\mathsf{ImageLocatedRefinementLedger}$ with the same source and target bundles.

Finally unpack the original compact witness carrier. Its fields are exactly the
subset ledger, final located ledger, finite-net ledger, final intermediate
ledger, and final compact ledger. Applying the carrier classifier transport to
the five displayed $\hsame$ rows repacks the compact witness carrier over the
primed image histories.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Uniformly continuous image of a BEDC compact source]
\label{thm:uniformly-continuous-image-compact-source}
Let $source$ and $target$ be metric BEDC carriers, and let
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\mathsf{modulus},
\mathsf{cert},\mathsf{distance})
$$
be a $\ContinuousMapUp$ carrier with graph totality, graph single-valuedness, modulus validity, and classifier transport. Assume the source carries the compact witness
$$
\mathsf{CompactWitnessCarrier}(sourceSubset,sourceLocated,sourceFinite,
sourceIntermediate,sourceCompact).
$$
For a target precision history $\varepsilon$, let the accepted modulus-validity row select a source precision history $\delta$. If the source finite-net certificate supplies a bundle $\mathcal{A}$ at $\delta$, with classifier-stable source coverage, then the image carrier determined by the displayed graph rows admits histories $\mathcal{B}$, $imageSubset$, $imageLocated$, $imageFinite$, $imageIntermediate$, $imageCompact$, $finalImageLocated$, $finalImageIntermediate$, and $finalImageCompact$ such that
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ImageFiniteNetLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\delta,\varepsilon,
\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},imageFinite)\\
\land\
\mathsf{ImageLocatedRefinementLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\mathsf{modulus},
\varepsilon,\delta,\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},imageSubset,imageLocated,imageFinite,
imageIntermediate,imageCompact,\\
\qquad finalImageLocated,finalImageIntermediate,finalImageCompact)\\
\land\
\mathsf{CompactWitnessCarrier}(imageSubset,finalImageLocated,imageFinite,
finalImageIntermediate,finalImageCompact).
\end{array}
$$
Thus the continuous image inherits a full compact witness carrier, with the finite-net image field and the located refinement chain carried through the same modulus ledger.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{prop:uniformly-continuous-image-totally-bounded-source} to the target precision $\varepsilon$, the selected source precision $\delta$, and the source finite-net row at $\delta$. This produces the target bundle $\mathcal{B}$ and the finite-net history $imageFinite$ satisfying
$$
\mathsf{ImageFiniteNetLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\delta,\varepsilon,
\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},imageFinite).
$$
That row is the totally-bounded part of the image certificate: for every displayed image witness, source coverage gives a bundled source center, graph totality maps that center to a bundled target center, and modulus validity turns the source distance bound below $\delta$ into the target distance bound below $\varepsilon$.

Define $imageSubset$ by the graph image of $sourceSubset$. Graph single-valuedness makes this subset independent of the chosen source representative, and the metric-distance classifier transport in the continuous-map carrier makes the displayed distance rows stable under $\hsame$ in the image point and target center.

The located component is transported step by step. The source compact witness supplies the continuations
$$
\Cont(sourceSubset,sourceLocated,sourceIntermediate)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
\Cont(sourceIntermediate,sourceFinite,sourceCompact).
$$
For each unary locatedness refinement tail in the source chain, the modulus-validity row assigns the corresponding target precision tail. The same tail extends the image locatedness ledger and the image intermediate ledger, while the fixed image finite-net history closes the compactness endpoint by the continuation
$$
\Cont(finalImageIntermediate,imageFinite,finalImageCompact).
$$
This is exactly the step constructor of \autoref{def:compact-located-refinement-chain}; the base constructor is the initial image locatedness package. Induction over the source located refinement chain therefore gives
$$
\mathsf{CompactLocatedRefinementChain}(imageFinite,imageLocated,imageIntermediate,
imageCompact,finalImageLocated,finalImageIntermediate,finalImageCompact).
$$
Together with the unary image histories and the two initial continuation ledgers, these rows form the image located-refinement ledger of \autoref{def:compact-image-located-refinement-ledger}.

The unary histories $imageSubset$, $imageLocated$, and $imageFinite$, together with
$$
\Cont(imageSubset,imageLocated,imageIntermediate)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
\Cont(imageIntermediate,imageFinite,imageCompact),
$$
assemble the initial compact witness carrier
$$
\mathsf{CompactWitnessCarrier}(imageSubset,imageLocated,imageFinite,imageIntermediate,
imageCompact).
$$
Apply \autoref{thm:compact-witness-carrier-located-refinement-chain-closed} to this carrier and the transported target located-refinement chain. The conclusion is
$$
\mathsf{CompactWitnessCarrier}(imageSubset,finalImageLocated,imageFinite,
finalImageIntermediate,finalImageCompact),
$$
while the finite-net image ledger remains the same displayed $imageFinite$ field. Hence the image certificate contains both the totally-bounded image data and the located refinement chain required by the compact carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Finite image nets produce image locatedness ledgers]
\label{thm:finite-image-nets-produce-image-locatedness-ledgers}
Let $source$ and $target$ be metric BEDC carriers, and let
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\mathsf{modulus},
\mathsf{cert},\mathsf{distance})
$$
be a $\ContinuousMapUp$ carrier with graph totality, graph single-valuedness,
modulus validity, and metric-distance classifier transport. Fix target and
source precision histories $\varepsilon$ and $\delta$, source and target
bundles $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$, and image histories
$imageSubset$, $imageLocated$, $imageFinite$, $imageIntermediate$,
$imageCompact$, $finalImageLocated$, $finalImageIntermediate$, and
$finalImageCompact$. Assume
$$
\mathsf{ImageFiniteNetLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\delta,\varepsilon,
\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},imageFinite).
$$
Assume also that the target metric-distance finite comparison data over
$\mathcal{B}$ supplies the following BEDC rows:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $imageSubset$ is the graph image of the source subset through the
  displayed continuous-map carrier;
  \item $imageSubset$, $imageLocated$, and $imageFinite$ are unary histories,
  and
  $$
  \Cont(imageSubset,imageLocated,imageIntermediate)
  \quad\hbox{and}\quad
  \Cont(imageIntermediate,imageFinite,imageCompact)
  $$
  hold;
  \item the finite comparison fold of the target distance rows below
  $\varepsilon$ generates the located-refinement chain
  $$
  \mathsf{CompactLocatedRefinementChain}(imageFinite,imageLocated,
  imageIntermediate,imageCompact,finalImageLocated,finalImageIntermediate,
  finalImageCompact);
  $$
  \item the comparison rows are stable under $\hsame$ in the image point, the
  displayed graph row, the target finite-net center, and the target
  metric-distance histories.
\end{enumerate}
Then the graph image predicate admits the image located-refinement ledger
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ImageLocatedRefinementLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\mathsf{modulus},
\varepsilon,\delta,\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},imageSubset,imageLocated,imageFinite,\\
\qquad imageIntermediate,imageCompact,finalImageLocated,finalImageIntermediate,
finalImageCompact).
\end{array}
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack \autoref{def:compact-image-located-refinement-ledger}. Its accepted row
has five components. The continuous-map carrier supplies graph totality, graph
single-valuedness, modulus validity, and metric-distance classifier transport.
The displayed image finite-net hypothesis is exactly the finite-net component
from \autoref{def:compact-image-finite-net-ledger}.

The target comparison package supplies the graph-image reading of
$imageSubset$, the unary histories $imageSubset$, $imageLocated$, and
$imageFinite$, and the two initial continuation ledgers through
$imageIntermediate$ and $imageCompact$. Its finite fold over the target bundle
$\mathcal{B}$ supplies the compact located-refinement chain required by
\autoref{def:compact-located-refinement-chain}. Finally, graph single-valuedness
and metric-distance classifier transport give the stated stability under
$\hsame$ for the image point, graph row, bundled target center, and displayed
distance histories.

These are precisely the fields listed in
\autoref{def:compact-image-located-refinement-ledger}, so they assemble the
claimed image located-refinement ledger.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Image located-refinement ledger compact carrier]
\label{thm:image-located-refinement-ledger-compact-carrier}
Let $source$ and $target$ be metric BEDC carriers, and let
$$
\mathsf{ContinuousMapCarrier}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\mathsf{modulus},
\mathsf{cert},\mathsf{distance})
$$
be a $\ContinuousMapUp$ carrier with graph totality, graph single-valuedness,
modulus validity, and metric-distance classifier transport. Fix target and
source precision histories $\varepsilon$ and $\delta$, source and target
bundles $\mathcal{A}$ and $\mathcal{B}$, and image histories
$imageSubset$, $imageLocated$, $imageFinite$, $imageIntermediate$,
$imageCompact$, $finalImageLocated$, $finalImageIntermediate$, and
$finalImageCompact$. Assume that $imageSubset$, $imageLocated$, and
$imageFinite$ are unary histories, and that
$$
\Cont(imageSubset,imageLocated,imageIntermediate)
\quad\hbox{and}\quad
\Cont(imageIntermediate,imageFinite,imageCompact)
$$
hold. If
$$
\begin{array}{l}
\mathsf{ImageLocatedRefinementLedger}(source,\mathsf{map},target,\mathsf{modulus},
\varepsilon,\delta,\mathcal{A},\mathcal{B},imageSubset,imageLocated,imageFinite,\\
\qquad imageIntermediate,imageCompact,finalImageLocated,finalImageIntermediate,
finalImageCompact)
\end{array}
$$
supplies the compact located-refinement chain to $finalImageLocated$,
$finalImageIntermediate$, and $finalImageCompact$, then
$$
\mathsf{CompactWitnessCarrier}(imageSubset,finalImageLocated,imageFinite,
finalImageIntermediate,finalImageCompact).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack the image located-refinement ledger. Its accepted row contains the compact
located-refinement chain
$$
\mathsf{CompactLocatedRefinementChain}(imageFinite,imageLocated,
imageIntermediate,imageCompact,finalImageLocated,finalImageIntermediate,
finalImageCompact).
$$

The unary histories $imageSubset$, $imageLocated$, and $imageFinite$, together
with the two displayed continuations, assemble the initial compact witness
carrier
$$
\mathsf{CompactWitnessCarrier}(imageSubset,imageLocated,imageFinite,
imageIntermediate,imageCompact).
$$

Apply \autoref{thm:compact-witness-carrier-located-refinement-chain-closed} to
this carrier and the located-refinement chain extracted from the ledger. The
result replaces the located, intermediate, and compact fields by
$finalImageLocated$, $finalImageIntermediate$, and $finalImageCompact$ while
leaving the subset and finite-net fields fixed, which is exactly the claimed
compact witness carrier.
\end{proof}
