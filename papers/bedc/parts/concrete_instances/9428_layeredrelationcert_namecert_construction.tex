\begin{definition}[LayeredRelationCert carrier]
\label{def:layered-relation-cert-carrier}
A $\LayeredRelationCertUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(A,B,M,Q,R,U,E,F,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two refinement-chain rows, each carrying source, layer, obstruction, carrier, and local classifier data;
\item $M$ is the displayed layer-map row pairing layers of $A$ with layers of $B$;
\item $Q$ is the relation-classifier row, accepting same-schema reads only through $A,B,M$;
\item $R$ is the preserved-structure row;
\item $U$ is the not-preserved row, listing the object content, empirical content, proof content, bridge content, or subject-side content refused by the comparison;
\item $E$ is the exactness row, recording the public meaning of same-schema export;
\item $F$ is the failure-boundary row, naming the condition under which the relation weakens or cannot be exported;
\item $S$ is the strength row, separating paper certificate, checked certificate, and bridge certificate readings;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for chain, layer-map, classifier, preserved, not-preserved, exactness, failure-boundary, strength, route, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for source-chain projection, layer-map inspection, classifier admission, refusal inspection, exactness export, and public consumer handoff;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \autoref{ch:visions-layered-relation-certificate}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-classifiermorphism-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interhistinvariant-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-groundcompilerrecognizerflow-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitereflectiontuple-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-auditgatecomposition-namecert}, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LayeredRelationCertUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for object identity between the two domains, hidden empirical law, unmediated proof transfer, subject-substance transfer, bridge certificate, global frame, or unlisted relation strength.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[LayeredRelationCert classifier]
\label{def:layered-relation-cert-classifier}
The $\LayeredRelationCertUp$ classifier accepts a carrier $L=(A,B,M,Q,R,U,E,F,S,H,C,P,N)$ only when all public relation reads factor through the chain rows $A,B$, the layer-map row $M$, the relation-classifier row $Q$, the preserved row $R$, the not-preserved row $U$, the exactness row $E$, the failure-boundary row $F$, and the strength row $S$, with transport, continuation, provenance, and naming supplied by $H,C,P,N$.

Two accepted carriers are classifier-same only when they present the same source-chain boundaries, the same displayed layer-map boundary, the same preserved and not-preserved rows, the same exactness boundary, the same failure-boundary row, and the same strength row after componentwise $\hsame$ transport. The classifier never compares the two domains as objects.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[LayeredRelationCert field exhaustion]
\label{thm:layered-relation-cert-field-exhaustion}
For every accepted $\LayeredRelationCertUp$ carrier
$$
L=(A,B,M,Q,R,U,E,F,S,H,C,P,N),
$$
every public relation read is exhausted by the displayed rows $A,B,M,Q,R,U,E,F,S,H,C,P,N$. In particular, a same-schema export can read preserved structure only through $R$, refused content only through $U$, public exactness only through $E$, failure behavior only through $F$, and certification strength only through $S$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:layered-relation-cert-carrier}. The tuple displays the two chain rows, layer map, classifier, preserved row, not-preserved row, exactness row, failure-boundary row, strength row, transport, continuation, provenance, and local name.

Use the classifier of \autoref{def:layered-relation-cert-classifier}. It admits public reads only after those rows are present and transported to the same finite surface, so the relation cannot acquire a second source for preservation, refusal, exactness, failure, or strength.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ replay and name the same finite packet. Since they do not add a domain-identity coordinate, bridge coordinate, proof-transfer coordinate, or empirical-law coordinate, the row inventory exhausts the public relation surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LayeredRelationCert refusal non-escape]
\label{thm:layered-relation-cert-refusal-nonescape}
For every accepted $\LayeredRelationCertUp$ carrier, all non-preserved content is confined to the row $U$ and the failure-boundary row $F$. The packet cannot export refused content as preserved structure, cannot promote same-schema exactness to object identity, and cannot turn paper-certificate strength into checked or bridge strength.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with the carrier inventory. The only row assigned to not-preserved content is $U$, and the only row assigned to failure behavior is $F$.

Apply \autoref{thm:layered-relation-cert-field-exhaustion}. A public read of preserved structure must pass through $R$, a public exactness read must pass through $E$, and a public strength read must pass through $S$. None of those rows is a constructor from refused content to preserved content.

Finally inspect the classifier row $Q$. It accepts same-schema reads through the displayed chain and layer-map surface, not object identity between the domains. Hence refusal remains visible to every consumer and cannot escape through transport, continuation, provenance, local naming, or strength language.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[LayeredRelationCert public exactness test]
\label{thm:layered-relation-cert-public-exactness-test}
An accepted $\LayeredRelationCertUp$ packet exports public exactness only when the not-preserved row $U$ is present before the exactness row $E$ is read. If the packet lacks $U$, then the exactness export has no displayed refusal surface and fails the public exactness export test.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet and read the exactness route through $C$. The route reaches $E$ only after the carrier has displayed the chain rows, layer map, classifier row, preserved row, and not-preserved row.

The role of $U$ is fixed by \autoref{def:layered-relation-cert-carrier}: it lists the content that the relation refuses to preserve. Without that row, same-schema language has no public boundary separating relation exactness from identity.

Apply \autoref{thm:layered-relation-cert-refusal-nonescape}. Since refusal is the guard against over-reading, an exactness export without $U$ lacks the row needed to keep non-preserved content from escaping as preserved content.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[LayeredRelationCert strict local obstruction]
\label{cor:layered-relation-cert-strict-local-obstruction}
Let
$$
L=(A,B,M,Q,R,U,E,F,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\LayeredRelationCertUp$ carrier. Suppose a displayed failure, refusal, separation, or countermodel row is used by a consumer against a named converse or reuse route for $L$. Then that row is a strict local obstruction: it blocks only the named route whose read asks to add, replace, or bypass one of the displayed relation coordinates
$$
A,B,M,Q,R,U,E,F,S,H,C,P,N.
$$
Equivalently, every consumer-facing use must factor through the two source-chain rows $A,B$, the layer-map row $M$, the classifier row $Q$, the preserved row $R$, the not-preserved row $U$, the exactness row $E$, the failure-boundary row $F$, the strength row $S$, the componentwise $\hsame$ transport row $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ route row $C$, the provenance row $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\LayeredRelationCertUp}$ row $N$. The obstruction cannot be reinterpreted as a global impossibility, a host theorem, hidden external validation, object identity between the domains, unmediated proof transfer, subject-substance transfer, bridge certification, or an unlisted relation strength.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Read the accepted carrier through \autoref{def:layered-relation-cert-carrier}. The finite row inventory is exactly $A,B,M,Q,R,U,E,F,S,H,C,P,N$. The same definition states that the carrier has no coordinate for object identity between the two domains, hidden empirical law, unmediated proof transfer, subject-substance transfer, bridge certificate, global frame, or unlisted relation strength.

Use the classifier of \autoref{def:layered-relation-cert-classifier}. A public relation read is admitted only when it factors through the chain rows $A,B$, the layer-map row $M$, the relation-classifier row $Q$, the preserved row $R$, the not-preserved row $U$, the exactness row $E$, the failure-boundary row $F$, and the strength row $S$, with transport, continuation, provenance, and naming supplied by $H,C,P,N$. Thus a consumer-facing obstruction can meet a named converse or reuse route only at one of those displayed coordinates.

Now apply \autoref{thm:layered-relation-cert-refusal-nonescape}. Refused content is confined to $U$ and $F$; it cannot escape as preserved structure through $R$, cannot promote same-schema exactness through $E$ into object identity, and cannot promote paper-certificate strength through $S$ into checked or bridge strength. The route row $C$ only displays source-chain projection, layer-map inspection, classifier admission, refusal inspection, exactness export, and public consumer handoff, while $H,P,N$ only transport, cite, and name the same finite surface. None of these rows adds a second route around the displayed obstruction.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:layered-relation-cert-public-exactness-test}. Public exactness is readable only after the not-preserved row $U$ has been displayed before $E$ is read. Hence a converse or reuse route that tries to consume exactness without the refusal surface fails at the local $U$-then-$E$ boundary; it does not become a theorem about all relations, a host-side impossibility result, or a hidden validation channel. If the attempted route asks for any unlisted coordinate, the carrier and classifier provide no readback for it. Therefore the displayed negative row has exactly the local blocking force of the named route and no wider export surface.
\end{proof}
