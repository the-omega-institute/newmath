\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\DyadicCompactnessUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-dyadic-compactness-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive compactness, the $\DyadicCompactnessUp$ packet records the finite dyadic net surface for a located closed bounded real interval before any Real or Completion consumer reads a compactness-shaped seal. It sits after the interval-net carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedintervalnet-namecert}, the dyadic cell surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicinterval-namecert}, the rational interval ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}, the finite stream-window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter names finite-net compactness data only; it does not assert Heine--Borel open-cover compactness, select subcovers, quotient streams, host real equality, host limits, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Dyadic compactness carrier]
\label{def:dyadic-compactness-carrier}
A $\DyadicCompactnessUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(L,Q,D,M,Z,F,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the located closed bounded interval-net row supplied by $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$, including lower and upper located endpoint windows;
\item $Q$ is the finite $\RationalIntervalUp$ cell ledger attached to the located endpoint row;
\item $D$ is the finite $\DyadicIntervalUp$ cell ledger refining the rational cells of $Q$;
\item $M$ is the unary mesh scale naming the dyadic precision at which coverage is read;
\item $Z$ is the $\ProbeBundle$ center ledger, with one displayed center for each accepted dyadic cell;
\item $F$ is the finite coverage ledger saying that each displayed interval read enters a rational cell in $Q$, its dyadic refinement in $D$, and the corresponding center row in $Z$;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row used to inspect endpoints, cells, and centers at mesh scale $M$;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the same endpoint, cell, and center windows;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $L,Q,D,M,Z,F,S,R$ have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for located endpoints, rational cells, dyadic cells, mesh scale, centers, coverage rows, stream windows, regular-rational readbacks, and the Real seal;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from the closed bounded interval net through rational cells, dyadic cells, mesh scale, center rows, finite coverage, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, and RealUp sealing;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicCompactnessUp$, $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\DyadicIntervalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ProbeBundle$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicCompactnessUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no open cover, chosen finite subcover, quotient stream representative, host real equality, selected host limit, hidden compactness principle, or ambient Real-completeness row.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic compactness NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:dyadic-compactness-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:dyadic-compactness-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\DyadicCompactnessUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $K=(L,Q,D,M,Z,F,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, closed bounded interval-net admission through $L$, rational interval cell admission through $Q$, dyadic cell admission through $D$, unary mesh admission through $M$, ProbeBundle center admission through $Z$, finite coverage admission through $F$, StreamName and RegSeqRat readback through $S$ and $R$, RealUp sealing through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier row by row. The first block displays a closed bounded interval net, rational interval cells, dyadic refinements, a unary mesh scale, ProbeBundle centers, and a finite coverage ledger.

Read the analytic handoff only after the finite rows have been fixed. The $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks inspect endpoints, cells, and centers at the named mesh scale, and the $\RealUp$ seal is downstream of those windows.

The structural rows close the NameCert surface. The transport row $H$, replay row $C$, package row $P$, and local name $N$ preserve only the displayed finite packet, so no further obligation can enter from an open cover, a chosen subcover, a quotient stream, a host real equality test, a selected limit, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic compactness finite-net coverage]
\label{thm:dyadic-compactness-finite-net-coverage}
Every accepted containment read of a $\DyadicCompactnessUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
L,Q,D,M,Z,F,H,C,P,N.
$$
The read consumes the closed bounded interval-net row, the rational interval cell ledger, the dyadic refinement ledger, the unary mesh scale, the matching ProbeBundle center row, and the finite coverage row. It receives no open-cover compactness principle, hidden subcover choice, quotient stream representative, host real equality, selected host limit, or ambient Real-completeness row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Induct over the finite mesh cells displayed by $Q$ and $D$. In the base cell, the located endpoint read is admitted by $L$, refined by the first dyadic cell in $D$, and routed to its displayed ProbeBundle center in $Z$ at mesh scale $M$.

For the successor step, keep the previously admitted cells fixed and read the next rational cell with its dyadic refinement. The coverage row $F$ appends only that displayed cell-center route, while $H$ transports the same finite rows componentwise.

Finally replay the accumulated route through $C$ and package it through $P,N$. Since the carrier has no coordinate for open covers, subcover choices, quotient stream representatives, host real equality, selected limits, or ambient completeness, the induction cannot leave the finite dyadic net.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Dyadic compactness Real seal nonescape]
\label{thm:dyadic-compactness-real-seal-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$ or Completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\DyadicCompactnessUp$ packet reads the compactness data through the finite route
$$
L,Q,D,M,Z,F,S,R,E,H,C,P,N.
$$
The Real seal row $E$ is exposed only after the closed bounded interval net, rational cells, dyadic cells, unary mesh, ProbeBundle centers, finite coverage rows, StreamName windows, and RegSeqRat readbacks have been displayed. Consequently the downstream Real and Completion route receives a finite dyadic net packet and receives no Heine--Borel open-cover theorem, hidden finite-subcover selection, quotient stream equality, selected host real limit, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness premise.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with \autoref{thm:dyadic-compactness-namecert-obligations}. It places $E$ after the finite rows $L,Q,D,M,Z,F,S,R$, so a Real-facing consumer cannot inspect a seal before the mesh, center, coverage, and readback data have been displayed.

Use \autoref{thm:dyadic-compactness-finite-net-coverage} for the containment side. The coverage read is already confined to rational cells, dyadic refinements, a unary mesh scale, and ProbeBundle centers, so replacing it by an open cover or by a chosen finite subcover would require a row absent from the carrier.

The remaining structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite packet. Therefore every Real and Completion consumer receives the displayed dyadic net route and no quotient stream equality, selected host real limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness premise.
\end{proof}
