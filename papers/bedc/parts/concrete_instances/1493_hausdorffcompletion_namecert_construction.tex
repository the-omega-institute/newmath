\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\HausdorffCompletionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-hausdorffcompletion-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ interface records the separated completion seal used when completion data must remain visible as named finite rows rather than becoming quotient-by-class data. Following Bourbaki, \emph{General Topology}, and Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the packet treats separatedness as displayed seal evidence over Cauchy and uniform data. It depends on $\CauchyFilterUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\UniformSpaceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows.

\begin{definition}[HausdorffCompletion carrier]
\label{def:hausdorff-completion-carrier}
A $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
H=(S,U,A,M,L,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is a $\CauchyFilterUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row;
\item $U$ is the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage route controlling the compared source windows;
\item $A$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing separated classifier row;
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ diagonal handoff row;
\item $L$ records the displayed $\hsame$ transports for source, entourage, separated-seal, and completion-handoff rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through the source, entourage, separated classifier, and handoff ledgers;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over the visible dependency certificates;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffCompletionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no quotient class of Cauchy filters, no host completion object, no selected representative, and no ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[HausdorffCompletion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:hausdorff-completion-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\HausdorffCompletionUp}$ surface consists of five finite rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier habitation by a displayed $\CauchyFilterUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ source, a $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage route, a separated $\RealUp$ classifier row, and a $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ handoff;
\item separated classifier stability under the stored $\hsame$ transports and displayed $\Cont$ routes;
\item Cauchy-filter handoff from the source row through the entourage route into the completion handoff row;
\item completion-seal non-escape, stating that the $\RealUp$ read is reached only through the separated classifier and the finite handoff ledger;
\item ledger exactness for the $\Pkg$ provenance and local $\NameCert$ row.
\end{enumerate}
These rows name separated completion data without turning the packet into a quotient of Cauchy classes or an ambient complete space.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier habitation is projection of \autoref{def:hausdorff-completion-carrier}. The source, entourage, separated classifier, handoff, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows are finite coordinates of the same $\BHist$ packet.

Classifier stability is componentwise. Move the source row, entourage route, separated seal row, and completion handoff along the stored $\hsame$ transports, then replay the displayed $\Cont$ routes for the same coordinates.

The Cauchy-filter handoff uses $S$, $U$, and $M$. A consumer first reads the Cauchy or regular rational source, then the entourage route controlling the displayed windows, and only then the completion handoff row.

The non-escape and exactness rows follow from the absence of any quotient-class, host-completion, selected-representative, or ambient-completeness coordinate. The package and local naming rows are therefore the complete provenance surface for the separated seal.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[HausdorffCompletion separated seal boundary]
\label{thm:hausdorff-completion-separated-seal-boundary}
Every $\RealUp$ seal-facing read exported by an accepted $\HausdorffCompletionUp$ carrier factors through the $\CauchyFilterUp$ or $\RegSeqRatUp$ source row, the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage route, the separated classifier row, the $\CauchyCompletionMonadUp$ diagonal handoff, and the displayed $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ provenance rows. Thus separated completion is exported as finite seal data, not as quotient-by-class data, host completion equality, selected representatives, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $H=(S,U,A,M,L,C,P,N)$. The only source coordinate is $S$, and the only uniform-control coordinate is the entourage route $U$.

Use the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:hausdorff-completion-namecert-obligations}. They force the separated classifier and completion handoff to remain attached to the same source, entourage, transport, continuation, package, and naming rows.

The $\RealUp$ read reaches the separated classifier row $A$ and the completion handoff row $M$ only through that finite route. Since the carrier has no quotient, host completion, selected representative, or ambient completeness coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the public seal boundary.
\end{proof}
