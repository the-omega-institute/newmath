It sits beside the closure-status discipline of \autoref{subsec:philosophy-closure-status-blocks}, the registry layer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registrylayer-namecert}, the object-knowledge certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-objectknowledgecertificate-namecert}, the registry-consistency audit of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-registry-consistency-audit-namecert}, and the machine-readable audit interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-machine-readable-audit-interface-namecert}. Those siblings bind chapter scope, registry admission, object-witness evidence, consistency checking, and machine-facing report reads; this packet names the finite writing item before a consumer may read prose as an auditable BEDC claim.

\begin{definition}[Writing-item audit packet carrier]
\label{def:writing-item-audit-packet-carrier}
A $\WritingItemAuditPacketUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
W=(K,C,R,L,T,F,G,Q,H,A,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the kind row, recording whether the item is a definition, theorem, corollary, proof, reading, schema, roadmap, or refusal;
\item $C$ is the carrier row, recording the BEDC object, packet, registry entry, or refusal surface on which the item lives;
\item $R$ is the classifier row, recording the displayed identity, equivalence, or classification relation by which the item is compared;
\item $L$ is the ledger row, recording hidden material, package provenance, consumed certificates, and refusal handoffs visible to the item;
\item $T$ is the theory-closure row, recording the paper-side closure grade and exact scope licensed by the item;
\item $F$ is the formal-status row, recording the Lean-side or audit-side status for the relevant declaration, target, encoded carrier, or explicit absence;
\item $G$ is the gap-report row, recording open gaps, refused gaps, closed gaps, and cannot-claim boundaries attached to the item;
\item $Q$ is the permitted-claim row, recording the strongest sentence the item authorizes a reader to believe;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for kind, carrier, classifier, ledger, theory closure, formal status, gap report, and permitted claim text;
\item $A$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from item admission through classifier readback, ledger inspection, status readback, gap exposure, and claim publication;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\WritingItemAuditPacketUp$, $\RegistryLayerUp$, $\ObjectKnowledgeCertificateUp$, $\RegistryConsistencyAuditUp$, $\MachineReadableAuditInterfaceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\WritingItemAuditPacketUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no coordinate for a prose override, unledgered carrier, hidden classifier, status-free claim, gap-erasing read, or claim text stronger than $Q$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Writing-item audit packet NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:writing-item-audit-packet-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:writing-item-audit-packet-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\WritingItemAuditPacketUp}$ surface consists exactly of eight obligation groups:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for $W=(K,C,R,L,T,F,G,Q,H,A,P,N)$;
\item kind and carrier readback through $K$ and $C$;
\item classifier exactness through $R$;
\item ledger coverage through $L$;
\item theory-closure and formal-status readback through $T$ and $F$;
\item gap-report exposure through $G$;
\item permitted-claim readback through $Q$;
\item componentwise transport, replay, package provenance, and local naming through $H,A,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
Thus a legal writing item is a finite NameCert surface over kind, carrier, classifier, ledger, closure status, formal status, gap report, and permitted claim text, not a bare prose assertion.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Writing-item audit packet closure bound]
\label{thm:writing-item-audit-packet-closure-bound}
No accepted $\WritingItemAuditPacketUp$ packet authorizes a consumer claim stronger than the conjunction of its displayed theory-closure row $T$, formal-status row $F$, gap-report row $G$, and permitted-claim row $Q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted packet $W=(K,C,R,L,T,F,G,Q,H,A,P,N)$. The rows $T$ and $F$ report the two independent axes described by \autoref{thm:philosophy-theory-formal-independent}, while $G$ records the visible gap or refusal boundary.

Inspect $Q$. By \autoref{def:writing-item-audit-packet-carrier}, $Q$ is the strongest sentence the item authorizes a reader to believe, and the carrier has no coordinate for a stronger prose override.

Finally use $H,A,P,N$. These rows transport, replay, package, and name the same displayed packet; they do not introduce an additional status grade or a second permitted-claim coordinate. Therefore any stronger consumer claim exits the accepted carrier.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Writing-item audit packet gap exposure]
\label{thm:writing-item-audit-packet-gap-exposure}
If the gap-report row $G$ of an accepted $\WritingItemAuditPacketUp$ packet records an open gap, refused gap, or cannot-claim boundary, every permitted consumer read of the packet exposes that row before publishing the permitted claim text $Q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:writing-item-audit-packet-namecert-obligations}. Gap-report exposure is one of the eight local NameCert obligation groups, so a consumer read cannot reach the local name while ignoring $G$.

The replay row $A$ orders the public read through item admission, classifier readback, ledger inspection, status readback, gap exposure, and claim publication. Thus $Q$ is not a free-standing prose sentence; it is read after the displayed gap report.

If a consumer omits the open-gap, refused-gap, or cannot-claim content of $G$, the read no longer follows the displayed replay row and no longer preserves the local NameCert surface. Such a read is not a permitted consumer read of the packet.
\end{proof}
