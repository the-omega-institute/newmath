\begin{definition}[Cellular substrate carrier]
\label{def:cellular-substrate-carrier}
A $\CellularSubstrateUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(S,N,U,G,H,C,P,L)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the cell-state row, naming the finite row segment visible to the carrier.
\item $N$ is the neighbourhood-window row, naming the finite cells read around each displayed cell.
\item $U$ is the local update route, recording the stated local transition from a neighbourhood window to the next cell-state row.
\item $G$ is the global-step ledger, listing the finite sequence of substrate steps admitted by the carrier.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the cell-state, neighbourhood, local-update, global-ledger, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes through the cell-state row, neighbourhood row, local update route, global-step ledger, and local NameCert row.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CellularSubstrateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, the cellular-carrier vision, and the sibling cellular NameCert chapters.
\item $L$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CellularSubstrateUp}$ row for carrier admission, classifier accountability, stability, exactness, ledger replay, and non-escape.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted carriers only by these displayed rows. It has no coordinate for host cellular automaton equality, an infinite grid, a hidden scheduler, a detached global evaluator, or an unbounded update oracle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cellular substrate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cellular-substrate-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:cellular-substrate-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\CellularSubstrateUp}$ surface consists exactly of five obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for $Q=(S,N,U,G,H,C,P,L)$;
\item classifier accountability for reads that compare cell-state rows, neighbourhood windows, and local update routes only through $S,N,U$;
\item stability under the componentwise $\hsame$ transports displayed in $H$;
\item exactness of local update and global-step replay through $U,G,C$;
\item ledger and provenance closure through $G,P,L$, with no export of hidden host equality, infinite-grid reasoning, scheduler state, or unbounded update oracle.
\end{enumerate}
Every accepted $\CellularSubstrateUp$ consumer must pass through these five rows before citing a cellular carrier read.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:cellular-substrate-carrier}. The tuple displays the cell-state row, neighbourhood window, local update route, global-step ledger, transports, continuations, provenance, and local naming row as its only coordinates.

Classifier accountability is local to $S,N,U$. A cell-state comparison or update read is accepted only when the read names the displayed neighbourhood window and the displayed local transition route.

Stability is supplied by $H$, while exactness is supplied by $U,G,C$. The transport row compares only the visible coordinates, and the continuation row replays only the finite local update route and finite global-step ledger.

Finally $P$ and $L$ package the same finite route as the public naming surface. Since the carrier lacks coordinates for host equality, an infinite grid, a hidden scheduler, and an unbounded update oracle, the five obligation rows exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cellular substrate local-update non-escape]
\label{thm:cellular-substrate-local-update-nonescape}
Every accepted local-update read exported by a $\CellularSubstrateUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
S \longmapsto N \longmapsto U \longmapsto G \longmapsto H \longmapsto C \longmapsto P \longmapsto L.
$$
In particular, an accepted packet exposes no host cellular automaton equality, infinite grid, hidden scheduler, or unbounded update oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted local-update read. By \autoref{def:cellular-substrate-carrier}, the only available rows are $S,N,U,G,H,C,P,L$.

Apply \autoref{thm:cellular-substrate-namecert-obligations}. The read must use the displayed neighbourhood window, the displayed local update route, and the finite global-step ledger before it can be named by the local $\NameCert_{\CellularSubstrateUp}$ row.

The route has no coordinate for host cellular automaton equality, infinite grid data, hidden scheduler state, or an unbounded update oracle. Thus every accepted read remains a finite cellular-substrate carrier read.
\end{proof}
