Following Mertens and Bishop--Bridges constructive analysis, the $\CauchyProductConvergenceUp$ packet records the finite Real-and-completion route by which two displayed regular Cauchy or summable rows expose a Cauchy product name. It sits over the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the completion witness route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-completeness-witness-namecert}, and the product theorem surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-mertens-cauchy-product-theorem-namecert}. The chapter names only the finite certificate route for product convergence; it does not assert quotient stream equality, selected infinite limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy product convergence carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-product-convergence-carrier}
A $\CauchyProductConvergenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(A,B,P,T,R,D,W,E,H,C,Q,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ and $B$ are the two source rows, each presented either as a regular Cauchy row or as a summable row with the finite windows needed by the product route.
\item $P$ is the Cauchy product window. It records the finite convolution band, the summand-pair reads, and the displayed partial product windows used by the convergence claim.
\item $T$ is the tail-control row. It records the finite majorant, Mertens, or Bishop--Bridges tail ledger that bounds the product tail after the window $P$ has been fixed.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the product windows determined by $A,B,P,T$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger for the source reads, product-window reads, and tail-control inequalities.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window data obtained from the same product windows and tolerance requests.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the regular readback, stream windows, and dyadic tolerance ledger have been exposed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the source rows, Cauchy product window, tail-control row, readback, tolerance ledger, stream windows, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a product-convergence request through $A,B,P,T,R,D,W,E,H,Q,N$.
\item $Q$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyProductConvergenceUp$, $\MertensCauchyProductTheoremUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\CauchyCompletenessWitnessUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductConvergenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate asserts exactly that these rows form one finite product-convergence history. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy product convergence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-convergence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyProductConvergenceUp$ carrier $K=(A,B,P,T,R,D,W,E,H,C,Q,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductConvergenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two source rows, the product window, the tail-control row, the regular readback, the dyadic tolerance ledger, stream windows, the Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming;
\item product-window stability under componentwise $\hsame$ transport of $A,B,P,T$;
\item tail-control compatibility between the Cauchy product window $P$, the tail row $T$, and the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback for the product windows exposed by $P,T,D$;
\item $\StreamNameUp$ window exposure using the same finite product windows and tolerance rows;
\item $\RealUp$ seal nonescape through $R,D,W,E$;
\item displayed $\Cont$ replay and $\Pkg$ provenance for the whole route;
\item ledger refusal for any product-convergence consumer that omits a source row, product window, tail-control row, tolerance ledger, readback row, stream window, Real seal, transport row, replay row, provenance row, or local naming row.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-product-convergence-carrier}. The projection exposes exactly the two source rows, the Cauchy product window, tail-control ledger, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, stream windows, Real seal, and structural support rows.

The product-window and tail obligations are bound by $P,T,D$. A consumer can read a product window only together with the corresponding source-window reads and dyadic tolerance request, and it can claim tail control only through the displayed row $T$.

The readback and seal obligations are bound by $R,W,E$. These rows repack the same finite product windows as regular rational and stream-name data before the terminal Real seal is available.

Finally $H,C,Q,N$ transport, replay, package, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier has no quotient-stream, selected-limit, choice, trichotomy, or ambient completeness coordinate, the obligation list is exhaustive.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy product convergence tail handoff]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-convergence-tail-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$-facing read of an accepted $\CauchyProductConvergenceUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
A,B \longmapsto P,T,D \longmapsto R,W \longmapsto E,H,C,Q,N .
$$
Thus the read first consumes the two source rows, then the displayed Cauchy product window, tail-control row, and dyadic tolerance ledger, then the regular rational and stream-name readbacks, and only then the terminal $\RealUp$ seal. The route exports no quotient stream semantics, selected product limit, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted packet $K=(A,B,P,T,R,D,W,E,H,C,Q,N)$. The only source coordinates are $A$ and $B$, so all source regularity or summability reads begin there.

The next stage is $P,T,D$. The product window $P$ fixes the finite convolution band, the tail-control row $T$ supplies the displayed convergence bound for the omitted band, and the dyadic ledger $D$ records the tolerance requests used by both.

The readback stage is $R,W$. The regular rational row and stream-name row use the same product windows fixed by $P,T,D$, so there is no separate selected-limit coordinate.

Finally the seal stage is $E,H,C,Q,N$. The $\RealUp$ seal is reached only after the readback rows, and the structural rows transport, replay, package, and name the same finite route.
\end{proof}
