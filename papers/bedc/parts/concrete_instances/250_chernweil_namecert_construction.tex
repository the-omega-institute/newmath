\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ChernWeilUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-chernweil-namecert}

The Chern-Weil interface $\ChernWeilUp$ packages the cohomology-class invariants of a principal bundle as polynomial expressions in the curvature form of any chosen connection. It depends on $\NameCert_{\CurvatureUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-curvature-namecert}) and $\NameCert_{\DeRhamUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-deRham-namecert}), and bridges differential and topological certificates through the same characteristic-class classifier.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:chernweil-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Chern-Weil source envelope]
\label{def:chernweil-source-envelope}
A $\ChernWeilUp$ source envelope is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
\mathcal{W}=(\mathcal{R},\mathcal{D},p,\gamma,\lambda)
$$
where $\mathcal{R}$ is a public $\NameCert_{\CurvatureUp}$ packet carrying the curvature boundary row and provenance exported by \autoref{thm:curvature-namecert-scope-obligation}, $\mathcal{D}$ is a $\NameCert_{\DeRhamUp}$ packet carrying the cohomology row into which the characteristic class is read, $p:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the shared $\Cont$ provenance row connecting the curvature source to the de~Rham projection, $\gamma:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the ledger face recording the chosen connection used to read the curvature polynomial, and $\lambda:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the characteristic-class row returned by the de~Rham classifier after the $\Cont$ projection.

The envelope is admitted only when the curvature boundary, the de~Rham cohomology row, the shared $\Cont$ provenance, the chosen-connection ledger face, and the characteristic-class row all occur as rows of the same finite history packet. Thus $\ChernWeilUp$ carries the concrete curvature-to-cohomology passage rather than an ambient characteristic-class operation.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Chern-Weil characteristic classifier]
\label{def:chernweil-characteristic-classifier}
Two $\ChernWeilUp$ source envelopes $\mathcal{W}$ and $\mathcal{W}'$ classify the same Chern-Weil object exactly when $\hsame$ preserves their $\NameCert_{\CurvatureUp}$ source rows and the $\NameCert_{\DeRhamUp}$ classifier returns the same characteristic-class row after projecting both packets through their carried $\Cont$ provenance rows. Equivalently, the classifier ignores only the hidden connection ledger face $\gamma$ and compares the exported curvature source row together with the de~Rham row $\lambda$ read from it.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Chern-Weil curvature-to-de~Rham source packet]
\label{def:chernweil-curvature-derham-source}
A $\ChernWeilUp$ source packet is a finite BEDC package
$$
\mathcal{W}=(\mathcal{R},\mathcal{D},p,\chi,c,\epsilon,\lambda,\pi)
$$
where $\mathcal{R}$ is a $\NameCert_{\CurvatureUp}$ carrier packet, $\mathcal{D}$ is a $\NameCert_{\DeRhamUp}$ carrier packet, $p:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the invariant-polynomial row, $\chi:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the evaluated curvature-polynomial row, $c:\mathsf{BHist}$ is the de~Rham cohomology-class endpoint, $\epsilon$ is the exact-boundary row witnessing connection replacement, $\lambda$ is the finite $\Cont$ ledger from the curvature Bianchi row to the closed de~Rham row, and $\pi$ records the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance of the curvature, de~Rham, and polynomial rows.

The packet is accepted only when the source curvature rows, the de~Rham endpoint rows, the $\hsame$ comparisons, the $\Cont$ ledgers, the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ observations, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance all project from the same displayed history package. Thus the Chern-Weil source is a CurvatureUp-to-DeRhamUp row package, not a host equality between differential forms.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Chern-Weil characteristic-class classifier]
\label{def:chernweil-characteristic-class-classifier}
Two packets of \autoref{def:chernweil-curvature-derham-source} have the same Chern-Weil characteristic class when their invariant-polynomial rows classify together, their evaluated curvature rows transport by the carried $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ ledgers, and the transported endpoints determine the same de~Rham cohomology-class row in the $\NameCert_{\DeRhamUp}$ classifier. The classifier is therefore agreement of invariant-polynomial curvature rows after de~Rham cohomology-class transport; it is not equality of ambient forms in a host calculus.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Chern-Weil invariant-polynomial carrier obligation]
\label{thm:chernweil-invariant-polynomial-carrier-obligation}
Every accepted Chern-Weil row exposes only the source curvature packet, the invariant-polynomial evaluation history, the de~Rham class endpoint, the finite transport ledgers, the $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ observations, and the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. In particular, the public carrier does not expose a coordinate connection form, an ambient polynomial algebra, or an external cohomology quotient beyond the displayed $\NameCert_{\CurvatureUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\DeRhamUp}$ rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Unpack an accepted packet of \autoref{def:chernweil-curvature-derham-source}. The tuple projections give exactly the source curvature certificate, the source de~Rham certificate, the polynomial row, the evaluated curvature-polynomial row, the class endpoint, the exact-boundary row, the finite ledger, and the provenance row.

The classifier of \autoref{def:chernweil-characteristic-class-classifier} consumes only these projections. It compares the polynomial and evaluated rows by $\hsame$, transports them through the finite $\Cont$ ledger, and then reads the resulting endpoint through the de~Rham classifier row.

Repacking those projections gives the stated public carrier surface. Since no projection returns a host connection form, an external polynomial algebra, or an uncarried quotient, no accepted Chern-Weil row can expose such data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Chern-Weil connection-change cocycle obligation]
\label{thm:chernweil-connection-change-cocycle-obligation}
If two packets of \autoref{def:chernweil-curvature-derham-source} differ only by replacing the $\NameCert_{\ConnectionUp}$ representative inside their $\NameCert_{\CurvatureUp}$ source while preserving the carried base, fibre, section, invariant-polynomial, and provenance rows, then the two evaluated curvature-polynomial endpoints are related in $\NameCert_{\DeRhamUp}$ by the exact-boundary row $\epsilon$. Consequently the characteristic-class classifier transports across the connection change.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two Chern-Weil packets to their curvature sources. The curvature tensorial transport row carries the section and tangent replacement data through $\hsame$, while preserving the shared base, fibre, invariant-polynomial, and provenance projections.

Evaluate the invariant-polynomial row against the transported curvature boundary row. The evaluation history is part of the Chern-Weil packet, so the two evaluated endpoints are connected by the displayed finite $\Cont$ ledger rather than by an unstated analytic homotopy.

The exact-boundary row $\epsilon$ is then projected as a de~Rham boundary witness from the first endpoint to the second. Reading this witness through the de~Rham classifier gives the same cohomology-class endpoint, and repacking the transported rows proves classifier stability under the connection change.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Chern-Weil closed-form de~Rham class obligation]
\label{thm:chernweil-closed-form-derham-class-obligation}
For every accepted Chern-Weil packet, the Bianchi-style curvature ledger supplies a closed de~Rham class endpoint consumable by the $\NameCert_{\DeRhamUp}$ classifier. The closedness row is the finite $\Cont$ consequence of the curvature cyclic ledger, the invariant-polynomial evaluation row, and the transported de~Rham endpoint row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the curvature component of the source packet. Its Bianchi ledger is a finite cyclic $\Cont$ row among displayed curvature boundaries, and the Chern-Weil packet records the invariant-polynomial evaluation of those boundaries.

Transport the evaluated row along the carried $\hsame$ comparisons into the de~Rham source packet. The ledger $\lambda$ records this transport as a BEDC continuation from the Bianchi row to the closed de~Rham endpoint.

The de~Rham classifier consumes the transported endpoint as a closed class row. Thus closedness is not asserted as an external differential identity; it is the class endpoint projected from the finite curvature ledger and repacked inside $\NameCert_{\DeRhamUp}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Chern-Weil NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:chernweil-namecert-obligation-surface}
The $\ChernWeilUp$ obligation surface consists exactly of the source packet of \autoref{def:chernweil-curvature-derham-source}, the characteristic-class classifier of \autoref{def:chernweil-characteristic-class-classifier}, the invariant-polynomial carrier row of \autoref{thm:chernweil-invariant-polynomial-carrier-obligation}, the connection-change exact-boundary row of \autoref{thm:chernweil-connection-change-cocycle-obligation}, the closed de~Rham endpoint row of \autoref{thm:chernweil-closed-form-derham-class-obligation}, the finite ledgers, and the dependency provenance over $\NameCert_{\CurvatureUp}$ and $\NameCert_{\DeRhamUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Collect the two definitions and the three obligation rows above. They provide the carrier packet, classifier, exposed public fields, connection-change transport, closedness endpoint, ledgers, exact-boundary row, and dependency provenance.

Each field has a consumer: the carrier exposes the source histories, the classifier reads the transported de~Rham endpoint, the connection-change row supplies exact-boundary transport, and the closedness row supplies the de~Rham class endpoint. Conversely, every consumer reads one of these displayed fields.

Therefore the surface is exact. A downstream $\NameCert_{\ChernWeilUp}$ consumer may project the listed CurvatureUp-to-DeRhamUp rows, but it may not assume a stronger equality of forms, a coordinate curvature model, or a standard bridge absent from the packet.
\end{proof}

\section{The certificate}

\begin{theorem}[Chern-Weil curvature-de Rham obligation surface]
\label{thm:chernweil-curvature-derham-obligation-surface}
The public obligation surface of a $\ChernWeilUp$ source envelope consists of the $\NameCert_{\CurvatureUp}$ export row of \autoref{thm:curvature-namecert-scope-obligation}, the $\NameCert_{\DeRhamUp}$ cohomology row carried by the de~Rham certificate, the shared $\Cont$ provenance row, the chosen-connection ledger face, and the characteristic-class row returned by the de~Rham classifier. No connection-dependent differential form is exported except through the curvature source row and the hidden ledger face.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the envelope of \autoref{def:chernweil-source-envelope}. Its curvature component exposes the public boundary, Bianchi, transport, and provenance rows supplied by $\NameCert_{\CurvatureUp}$, and \autoref{thm:curvature-namecert-scope-obligation} is the export rule for those rows.

Project the de~Rham component next. The de~Rham packet supplies the cohomology row into which the characteristic-class row $\lambda$ is read, while the carried $\Cont$ provenance row records that this readout is made from the same finite history packet as the curvature source.

The remaining row is the connection ledger face $\gamma$. It is listed as a hidden face because the envelope needs it to witness the chosen connection, but the classifier of \autoref{def:chernweil-characteristic-classifier} never exports it as part of the public characteristic class.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Chern-Weil connection-choice stability]
\label{thm:chernweil-connection-choice-stability}
Changing the selected connection in a $\ChernWeilUp$ source envelope changes only the hidden ledger face $\gamma$ when the transported curvature source rows are related by $\hsame$ and the de~Rham classifier returns the same characteristic-class row after $\Cont$ projection. Under those hypotheses the two envelopes classify the same $\ChernWeilUp$ object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with two envelopes whose visible curvature source rows are related by $\hsame$. The public curvature certificate transports the boundary and provenance rows through the same $\NameCert_{\CurvatureUp}$ export surface used in \autoref{thm:chernweil-curvature-derham-obligation-surface}.

Apply the shared $\Cont$ projections to the transported rows. By hypothesis the de~Rham classifier returns the same characteristic-class row after those projections, so the visible cohomology row $\lambda$ is unchanged.

The chosen connection can therefore differ only in the ledger face $\gamma$. Since \autoref{def:chernweil-characteristic-classifier} compares the curvature source and the de~Rham class while ignoring that hidden face, the two envelopes have the same classifier value.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Chern-Weil characteristic ledger exactness]
\label{thm:chernweil-characteristic-ledger-exactness}
The $\ChernWeilUp$ ledger is exact: every admitted source envelope is covered by a curvature source row, a de~Rham cohomology row, a shared $\Cont$ provenance row, a selected-connection ledger face, and a characteristic-class row; and every public classifier comparison is sound exactly when it is generated by $\hsame$ on the curvature source and equality of the de~Rham characteristic-class row after $\Cont$ projection.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Coverage is by projection from the displayed envelope. Each admitted packet contains the curvature certificate row, the de~Rham certificate row, the shared provenance row, the connection ledger face, and the characteristic-class row as finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ data.

For soundness, take a public classifier comparison. The definition requires $\hsame$ on the curvature source row and agreement of the de~Rham characteristic-class row after the carried $\Cont$ projections, so every accepted comparison is generated by listed envelope data.

Conversely, if those listed rows are present and the two classifier conditions hold, repack them as the comparison of \autoref{def:chernweil-characteristic-classifier}. No unlisted host connection, differential-form representative, or external characteristic-class operator is needed.
\end{proof}
