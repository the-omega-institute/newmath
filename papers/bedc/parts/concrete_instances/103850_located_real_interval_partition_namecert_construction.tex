\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\LocatedRealIntervalPartitionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-located-real-interval-partition-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges on constructive located intervals, the $\LocatedRealIntervalPartitionUp$ packet records a finite dyadic partition of a displayed located $\RealUp$ interval as NameCert data. It sits after the located endpoint surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the dyadic refinement surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicinterval-namecert}, the rational interval cell ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}, the finite StreamName windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a finite mesh ledger for closed-bounded interval reads; it does not import open-cover compactness, a host interval partition, quotient real equality, selected endpoint values, countable choice, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Located real interval partition carrier]
\label{def:located-real-interval-partition-carrier}
A $\LocatedRealIntervalPartitionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(J,M,Q,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $J$ is the source $\LocatedIntervalUp$ row whose lower and upper endpoint names have already exposed located real endpoint windows.
\item $M$ is the finite dyadic mesh row, listing the ordered mesh cells and their common precision.
\item $Q$ is the $\RationalIntervalUp$ witness family assigning each mesh cell a rational containment row inside the located interval ledger.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicIntervalUp$ refinement family attached to the rational cells of $Q$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window family opened for the endpoint and mesh-cell reads.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback family for the same finite windows and dyadic mesh precision.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal family reached only after the located endpoint, rational cell, dyadic mesh, window, and readback rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the located interval row, dyadic mesh cells, rational witnesses, dyadic refinements, stream windows, regular readbacks, real seals, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a partition read through $J,M,Q,D,S,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\LocatedRealIntervalPartitionUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\DyadicIntervalUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealIntervalPartitionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only by the displayed endpoint, mesh, rational-cell, dyadic-refinement, window, readback, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows. It has no coordinate for a host partition object, an open-cover subcover, quotient real equality, selected completed endpoints, countable choice, or ambient compactness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located real interval partition NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-real-interval-partition-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\LocatedRealIntervalPartitionUp$ carrier $T=(J,M,Q,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedRealIntervalPartitionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, located endpoint exposure through $J$, finite dyadic mesh exposure through $M$, rational interval witness exposure through $Q$, dyadic cell refinement through $D$, finite StreamName window exposure through $S$, RegSeqRat readback through $R$, terminal Real-seal exposure through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.

Every partition read of the located interval must therefore pass through the displayed finite mesh and rational witness rows before any StreamName, RegSeqRat, or Real seal row is consumed.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-real-interval-partition-carrier}. The tuple lists the only rows available to a partition consumer: the source located interval, mesh cells, rational witnesses, dyadic refinements, stream windows, regular readbacks, real seals, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The located endpoint stage is $J$. By the located interval certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, endpoint reads enter through displayed located real windows and not through chosen completed endpoint values.

The partition stage is $M,Q,D$. The dyadic mesh row gives the finite cell order and common precision, the rational interval witness family records containment for each cell, and the dyadic refinement family attaches the mesh cells to those rational witnesses.

The readback and seal stage is $S,R,E$. A consumer reaches the finite StreamName windows only after the mesh and rational witness rows are visible, then reads the regular rational rows and finally the terminal Real seal.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and name exactly this route. Since no host partition object, open-cover subcover, quotient real equality, chosen endpoint, choice schedule, or compactness row occurs in the carrier, the displayed NameCert surface is exhaustive.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Located real interval partition mesh handoff]
\label{thm:located-real-interval-partition-mesh-handoff}
Every accepted $\LocatedRealIntervalPartitionUp$ partition read hands its mesh to downstream closed-bounded interval consumers through the finite route
$$
\LocatedIntervalUp \longmapsto \RationalIntervalUp,\DyadicIntervalUp \longmapsto \StreamNameUp,\RegSeqRatUp \longmapsto \RealUp .
$$
The handoff exposes the located endpoint rows, dyadic mesh cells, rational containment witnesses, finite windows, regular readbacks, and terminal real seals, together with $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ provenance. It exports no open-cover compactness principle, no countable-choice subnet, no quotient interval, and no ambient complete ordered field row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $T=(J,M,Q,D,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The only source interval row is $J$, so every endpoint and containment read begins at the displayed located interval surface.

The mesh handoff is stored in $M,Q,D$. The dyadic mesh row $M$ gives the finite partition cells, the rational witness family $Q$ certifies their containment in the located interval ledger, and the dyadic refinement family $D$ attaches the cells to the rational interval witnesses.

The downstream read is stored in $S,R,E$. StreamName windows are opened at the displayed mesh precision, RegSeqRat rows read back the same windows, and the Real seal is reached only after those rows have been displayed.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ preserve the handoff under componentwise transport and replay. There is no additional coordinate in \autoref{def:located-real-interval-partition-carrier}, so a closed-bounded interval consumer receives exactly the displayed finite mesh route and none of the excluded compactness, choice, quotient, or ambient-completeness data.
\end{proof}
