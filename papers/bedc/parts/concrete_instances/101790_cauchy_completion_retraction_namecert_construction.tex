\begin{definition}[CauchyCompletionRetraction carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-completion-retraction-carrier}

$$

with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the displayed source row whose completion-facing admission is being split.
\item $U$ is the $\CauchyCompletionUnitUp$ route admitting $X$ into the completion-facing surface.
\item $V$ is the $\CauchyCompletionCounitUp$ route returning already displayed completion reads to the terminal real-seal boundary.
\item $A$ is the $\CauchyCompletionAdjunctionUp$ universal route that compares unit-admitted consumers with completion-facing consumers over the same finite source.
\item $I$ is the $\CauchyCompletionIdempotentUp$ row recording that a repeated completion read contributes no new public completion row.
\item $G$ is the $\CauchyCompletionTriangleIdentityUp$ row comparing the unit-then-counit route with the direct displayed real route.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row read before the split can expose any rational observation.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger used by the stream windows and by the split comparison.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row consumed by the terminal seal.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal through which the retracted read is observed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $X,U,V,A,I,G,S,D,R,E$ and the structural rows.

\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionRetractionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient-completion equality, selected completion representatives, countable-choice schedules, host equality of completed objects, ambient completeness, or public bridge credentials.

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionRetraction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-retraction-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\CauchyCompletionRetractionUp$ carrier $T=(X,U,V,A,I,G,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyCompletionRetractionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source row, unit row, counit row, adjunction row, idempotent row, triangle row, finite-window row, dyadic ledger, regular readback, terminal seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item unit-side exposure, requiring every completion-facing read exported from $X$ to pass through $U$ before any completion consumer can inspect it;
\item counit-side return, requiring every already displayed completion-facing read to pass through $V$ before reaching $S,D,R,E$;
\item split exactness, requiring the composite through $U,V,A,I,G$ to expose exactly the same displayed route to $E$ as the direct finite route through $S,D,R$;
\item classifier stability, requiring transported packets with the same displayed rows to be identified by the carried $\hsame$ and $\Cont$ data;
\item ledger non-escape, stating that the packet exports no quotient-completion equality, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, host equality, ambient-completeness theorem, $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-retraction-carrier}. The tuple lists exactly the source, unit, counit, adjunction, idempotent, triangle, finite-window, dyadic, readback, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

The unit-side exposure is read from $U$. A completion-facing consumer starting at $X$ receives no direct completion object; the carrier first displays the route by which $X$ enters the completion-facing surface.

The counit-side return is read from $V$. Once a completion-facing read is already displayed, the packet forces the return route through the finite observation rows $S,D,R$ before the terminal seal $E$ can be consumed.

Split exactness is the shared comparison supplied by $A,I,G$. The adjunction row fixes the universal consumer route, the idempotent row states that a repeated completion read contributes no new public row, and the triangle row compares the unit-then-counit route with the direct route to the same seal.

Classifier stability is componentwise transport along $H$ followed by replay through $C$. Since the carrier fields are exhausted by $X,U,V,A,I,G,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,N$, the excluded quotient, representative-choice, countable-choice, host-equality, ambient-completeness, real-completeness, and bridge data have no row from which a consumer could read them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionRetraction unit-counit split exactness]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-retraction-unit-counit-split}
Let $T=(X,U,V,A,I,G,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\CauchyCompletionRetractionUp$ carrier. Every consumer of the completion-facing split factors through the displayed finite route
$$
X\longmapsto U\longmapsto A,I,G\longmapsto V\longmapsto S,D,R\longmapsto E\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
After this route is projected, the visible seal-facing read is the same direct route
$$
S,D,R\longmapsto E
$$
that the source row already displays. The split is therefore a unit-counit retraction over finite BEDC rows, not equality of quotient completions, not a selected representative, not host equality, and not countable choice or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer of the split packet and project the source row $X$. The only completion-facing admission exposed by the carrier is $U$, so the consumer first passes through the unit row.

The middle comparison is fixed by $A,I,G$. The adjunction row $A$ records that the completion-facing consumer is a consumer of the same reflected route, the idempotent row $I$ prevents a second completion read from adding a new public row, and the triangle row $G$ identifies the unit-counit composite with the displayed route to the seal.

The return part is then forced by $V$. The counit row routes the already displayed completion-facing read through the finite stream window $S$, the dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, and the regular rational readback $R$ before the terminal seal $E$ is inspected.

Transport by $H$ and replay by $C$ preserve this route for equivalent packets, while $P$ and $N$ record provenance and local naming. No other carrier coordinate exists, so the split exports exactly the displayed finite route and none of the excluded quotient, representative, host-equality, choice, or completeness data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyCompletionRetraction nonescape]
\label{thm:cauchy-completion-retraction-nonescape}
For an accepted $\CauchyCompletionRetractionUp$ carrier $T=(X,U,V,A,I,G,S,D,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, every $\RealUp$-facing, $\RegSeqRatUp$-facing, or completion-facing read exported by $T$ is generated by the rows named in \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-retraction-carrier}. In particular, the packet exports no quotient-completion equality, selected completion representative, countable-choice schedule, host equality of completed objects, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness theorem, or bridge credential.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. A $\RealUp$-facing or $\RegSeqRatUp$-facing read must pass through $S,D,R,E$, because these are the only finite observation and terminal seal rows in the tuple.

A completion-facing read must pass through $U,V,A,I,G$. These are the only completion-route rows in the tuple, and each is stored as displayed NameCert data rather than as a quotient point or selected representative.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. They add no carrier coordinate beyond the listed rows.

Thus every exported read is generated by \autoref{def:cauchy-completion-retraction-carrier}. The forbidden quotient, representative, choice, host-equality, ambient-completeness, real-completeness, and bridge data are absent from the carrier and cannot be produced by projection, transport, replay, provenance, or local naming.
\end{proof}
