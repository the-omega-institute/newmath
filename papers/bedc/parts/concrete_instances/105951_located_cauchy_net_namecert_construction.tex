Following Moore--Smith, Kelley, and constructive net convergence, the $\LocatedCauchyNetUp$ packet records the located directed-tail Cauchy evidence needed by uniform-completion arguments. It sits between the Moore--Smith carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-moore-smith-net-namecert}, the convergence interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-netconvergence-namecert}, the Cauchy-net surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchynet-namecert}, the filter-base handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-filterbase-namecert}, the uniform-space entourage rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformspace-namecert}, and the completion consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}. The packet supplies finite located-Cauchy windows for the L10 directed-net completion corridor; it does not introduce quotient nets, selected limits, cofinal choice, or host completeness.

\begin{definition}[Located Cauchy net carrier]
\label{def:located-cauchy-net-carrier}
A $\LocatedCauchyNetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$

$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DirectedSetUp$ row naming finite directed vertices, preorder comparisons, and displayed upper-window witnesses.
\item $M$ is the $\MooreSmithNetUp$ row exposing net-value windows and the finite tail ledger over $D$.
\item $A$ is the $\CauchyNetUp$ row recording common-tail Cauchy evidence on the same directed surface.
\item $N$ is the $\NetConvergenceUp$ row naming the convergence-facing route that may inspect $D,M,A$ before any Real or filter handoff is read.
\item $U$ is the $\UniformCompletionUp$ consumer row that reads only the located Cauchy data displayed by $D,M,A,N,F,S$.
\item $F$ is the $\FilterBaseUp$ handoff row obtained from the displayed tail filters of $M$ and the Cauchy windows of $A$.
\item $S$ is the $\UniformSpaceUp$ entourage row used to compare Cauchy windows, located neighbourhood requests, and completion-facing refinements.
\item $W$ is the located-window ledger, listing the finite neighbourhood requests, positive tolerance names, and displayed tail witnesses used to locate each Cauchy read.

\item $K$ is the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCauchyNetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rows $D,M,A,N,U,F,S,W,H,C,P,K$. It has no coordinate for quotienting nets, selecting a limit, selecting a cofinal subnet, invoking countable choice, or importing ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Located Cauchy net NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:located-cauchy-net-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\LocatedCauchyNetUp$ carrier
$$
L=(D,M,A,N,U,F,S,W,H,C,P,K),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\LocatedCauchyNetUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, directed-index exposure, Moore--Smith tail exposure, Cauchy-net common-window exposure, NetConvergence handoff, UniformCompletion consumption, FilterBase handoff, UniformSpace entourage comparison, located-window evidence, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, package provenance, and local naming.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:located-cauchy-net-carrier}. The directed and Cauchy-facing rows are $D,M,A,W$, so every located read first fixes an index window, a Moore--Smith tail, common-tail Cauchy evidence, and a finite located-window witness.

The handoff rows are then $N,F,S,U$. The convergence route can inspect only the displayed tail evidence, the filter-base route is obtained from the same tails, the uniform-space row supplies the entourage comparisons, and the completion row consumes these data after they have been displayed.

Finally $H,C,P,K$ transport, replay, source, and name the same packet. Since the carrier contains no additional coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert obligation surface.
\end{proof}
