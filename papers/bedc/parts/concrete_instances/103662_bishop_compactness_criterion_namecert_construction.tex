\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BishopCompactnessCriterionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-compactness-criterion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges constructive analysis, the $\BishopCompactnessCriterionUp$ packet records the compactness criterion for metric spaces as a finite-net plus complete-limit BEDC route. It consumes the compact metric surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the total-bounded finite-net rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-totallybounded-namecert}, the complete-metric limit-readback rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}, and the completion-facing landing surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metriccompletion-namecert}. The packet gives L10 compact metric consumers a constructive criterion surface without invoking open-cover compactness, host sequential compactness, countable choice, or an ambient selected completion.

\begin{definition}[Bishop compactness criterion carrier]
\label{def:bishop-compactness-criterion-carrier}
A $\BishopCompactnessCriterionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(M,T,F,C,L,Q,H,R,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, exposing the metric carrier and the public compact-metric certificate surface.
\item $T$ is the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ finite-net row, including the requested tolerance, finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ centers, membership constructors, coverage distances, and rational comparison ledgers.
\item $F$ is the finite-net handoff row reading the centers and coverage witnesses from $T$ as the compactness criterion input rather than as an external subcover.
\item $C$ is the $\CompleteMetricUp$ row for regular Cauchy streams over the same metric carrier.
\item $L$ is the limit-readback row supplied by $C$, including the stream name, modulus, displayed limit history, late-observation thresholds, metric-distance witnesses, and rational comparison ledgers.
\item $Q$ is the $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff row used by completion-facing consumers of the same finite-net and complete-limit data.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $M,T,F,C,L,Q$ and the local certificate row.
\item $R$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a compactness request through finite-net extraction, complete-limit readback, metric-completion handoff, transport, provenance, and local naming.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BishopCompactnessCriterionUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\TotallyBoundedUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\MetricCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCompactnessCriterionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $M,T,F,C,L,Q,H,R,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, a selected subsequence, countable choice, quotient completion equality, host compactness, or an ambient complete metric space.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop compactness criterion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bishop-compactness-criterion-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BishopCompactnessCriterionUp$ carrier
$$
K=(M,T,F,C,L,Q,H,R,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BishopCompactnessCriterionUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the compact metric source, total-bounded finite-net row, finite-net handoff, complete-metric row, limit-readback row, metric-completion handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item compact-source lock, requiring $M$ to be the common metric carrier read by both the $\TotallyBoundedUp$ and $\CompleteMetricUp$ faces;
\item finite-net exactness, requiring $T$ and $F$ to expose only the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ centers, membership constructors, coverage distances, and rational comparison rows supplied by total boundedness;
\item complete-limit readback, requiring $C$ and $L$ to expose regular Cauchy stream, modulus, limit, threshold, distance-witness, and rational-bound rows over the same metric source;
\item metric-completion handoff, requiring completion-facing consumers to read $Q$ only after the finite-net and complete-limit rows have been displayed;
\item nonescape, requiring $H,R,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name exactly this route and no open-cover, choice, quotient, host compactness, or ambient-completion datum.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bishop-compactness-criterion-carrier}. The visible inventory is $M,T,F,C,L,Q,H,R,P,N$, so every local obligation must be read from those rows.

The compact-source lock is forced first. The finite-net row and the complete-limit row both attach to the metric carrier named by $M$; otherwise the criterion would compare total boundedness and completeness over different sources.

The finite-net obligations are the rows $T$ and $F$. They expose the tolerance, centers, membership constructors, coverage distances, and rational ledgers already provided by $\TotallyBoundedUp$, and they do not create an open cover or a selected finite subcover.

The complete-limit obligations are the rows $C$ and $L$. They expose the regular Cauchy name, modulus, displayed limit, threshold histories, metric-distance witnesses, and rational comparison ledgers already provided by $\CompleteMetricUp$.

Finally $Q,H,R,P,N$ provide the completion-facing handoff, transport, replay, provenance, and naming for the same route. Since no other coordinate occurs in the carrier, the excluded open-cover, choice, quotient, host compactness, and ambient-completion principles cannot enter the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop compactness criterion net-completion handoff]
\label{thm:bishop-compactness-criterion-net-completion-handoff}
Every completion-facing consumer of an accepted $\BishopCompactnessCriterionUp$ carrier factors through the ordered route
$$
\CompactMetricUp \longmapsto \TotallyBoundedUp \longmapsto \CompleteMetricUp \longmapsto \MetricCompletionUp .
$$
The finite-net side is read through $T,F$, the complete-limit side is read through $C,L$, and the terminal completion handoff is $Q$. No consumer reaches $\MetricCompletionUp$ from this packet before the finite-net and complete-limit rows have both been displayed over the same compact metric source.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the compact metric source row $M$. It names the metric carrier that both the total-bounded and complete-metric faces must use.

The finite-net side is read next through $T$ and $F$. These rows expose the finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ centers and coverage ledgers needed by the constructive compactness criterion.

The complete-limit side is read through $C$ and $L$. These rows expose the regular Cauchy limit-readback data over the same metric source, including the modulus, displayed limit, late thresholds, and metric-distance witnesses.

Only after those two faces are visible does $Q$ supply the metric-completion handoff. The transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows preserve this order, so a completion-facing consumer receives no selected representative, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, countable-choice schedule, or host complete-space datum.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bishop compactness criterion compact metric scope]
\label{thm:bishop-compactness-criterion-compact-metric-scope}
The $\BishopCompactnessCriterionUp$ packet closes exactly the constructive compact-metric criterion surface obtained from a displayed compact metric carrier, total-bounded finite-net data, complete-metric limit-readback data, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, local $\NameCert$ rows, and the $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff. It does not claim open-cover compactness, host sequential compactness, a selected convergent subsequence, countable choice, quotient completion equality, or completeness of an ambient metric space outside the displayed rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Apply \autoref{thm:bishop-compactness-criterion-namecert-obligations}. The obligation surface lists the compact source, finite-net face, complete-limit face, completion handoff, and structural support rows as the entire local certificate.

Apply \autoref{thm:bishop-compactness-criterion-net-completion-handoff}. It fixes the only completion-facing route and requires the finite-net and complete-limit rows to be displayed before the $\MetricCompletionUp$ handoff is consumed.

The remaining scope claim is boundary discipline. Open covers, host sequential compactness, selected subsequences, countable choice, quotient completion equality, and ambient complete spaces all require coordinates absent from $K$. The rows $H,R,P,N$ preserve and name the displayed route rather than adding any of those data, so the compact metric scope is exactly the stated constructive criterion surface.
\end{proof}
