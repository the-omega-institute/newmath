\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\TreeUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-tree-namecert}

The tree interface $\TreeUp$ packages a connected acyclic $\NameCert_{\GraphUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-graph-namecert}) together with a designated root or root-free witness. It is the constructive substrate for inductive carriers and for the syntactic representation of derivations.

\begin{theorem}[TreeUp carrier and classifier obligations]
\label{thm:tree-obligation-carrier-classifier}
The $\TreeUp$ carrier data refine the $\GraphUp$ $\Cont$-adjacency carrier of
\autoref{def:graph-cont-adjacency-carrier} to displayed finite $\Cont$-path
rows over $\mathsf{BHist}$ whose vertices satisfy $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$. Its
classifier is inherited from the $\GraphUp$ endpoint/path classifier and is
transported componentwise by $\hsame$: two displayed tree-path rows are
admissibly compared only when their source vertex, target vertex, visible path
ledger, and every exposed $\mathsf{GraphEdge}$ step are identified through the
same $\hsame$ classifier used by $\NameCert_{\GraphUp}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The obligation is the carrier-and-classifier portion of the roadmap ten-field
template.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Traditional target: a finite tree path carrier for $\TreeUp$.
  \item BEDC source: $\mathsf{BHist}$ with $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$ vertices,
  $\Cont$ rows, and the $\GraphUp$ $\NameCert$ edge predicate.
  \item Carrier: displayed finite rows of $\mathsf{GraphEdge}$ from
  \autoref{def:graph-cont-adjacency-carrier}, read as finite $\Cont$-path
  ledgers.
  \item Classifier: the $\GraphUp$ endpoint/path classifier, transported
  componentwise by $\hsame$ on vertices, edge results, and path ledgers.
  \item Stability: transport of every displayed $\Cont$ row through the
  $\hsame$ transport surface already carried by $\NameCert_{\GraphUp}$.
  \item Ledger: the source and target histories, the finite list of exposed
  $\mathsf{GraphEdge}$ rows, and the visible path-result history.
  \item Core theorem list: carrier refinement, classifier inheritance, and
  path-row transport.
  \item Exactness: a carried tree path is exactly its finite $\GraphUp$ row
  display over $\mathsf{BHist}$.
  \item Standard bridge: none.
  \item Status: paper obligation.
\end{enumerate}

Thus $\TreeUp$ does not introduce a second carrier beside $\GraphUp$. It
selects the finite displayed $\Cont$-path rows and reads their classifier
through the same $\hsame$-based $\NameCert$ surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TreeUp connectedness and acyclicity obligations]
\label{thm:tree-obligation-connected-acyclic}
For $\TreeUp$, connectedness is the obligation to supply, for every pair of
carried vertices, a finite $\mathsf{GraphEdge}$ path package between them.
Acyclicity is the obligation that every nontrivial closed finite path row reads
through $\hsame$ as impossible at the tree classifier: any displayed closed
path must collapse to the trivial unit loop supplied by $\GraphUp$. The
connected and acyclic rows are scoped by the $\GraphUp$ unit-loop laws, the
two-step composition surface, and the three-step path reassociation surface.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The obligation is the path-law portion of the roadmap ten-field template.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Traditional target: connected acyclic graph data.
  \item BEDC source: $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\Cont$, and
  the $\GraphUp$ $\NameCert$ rows.
  \item Carrier: the finite $\mathsf{GraphEdge}$ path packages named in
  \autoref{thm:tree-obligation-carrier-classifier}.
  \item Classifier: $\hsame$ on endpoints and visible path ledgers, inherited
  from the $\GraphUp$ endpoint/path classifier.
  \item Stability: path extension, transport, and reassociation through
  $\hsame$.
  \item Ledger: the chosen finite path between two carried vertices, or the
  displayed closed path whose nontriviality is being tested.
  \item Core theorem list: connected path existence, unit-loop collapse,
  two-step composition, and three-step reassociation.
  \item Exactness: the connectedness row exposes exactly a finite
  $\mathsf{GraphEdge}$ path, and the acyclicity row rejects exactly the
  nontrivial closed path rows.
  \item Standard bridge: none.
  \item Status: paper obligation.
\end{enumerate}

The unit case is read through
\autoref{thm:graph-cont-unit-loop}. Composite paths are assembled through
\autoref{thm:graph-cont-two-step-composition}, and adjacent reassociations are
compared through \autoref{thm:graph-cont-three-step-path-reassociation}.

The acyclicity obligation then says that any closed finite row whose source and
target are identified by $\hsame$ must be the unit-loop row in the displayed
$\NameCert_{\GraphUp}$ ledger. Every nontrivial closed row is therefore outside
the accepted $\TreeUp$ certificate surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[TreeUp root, ledger, and exactness obligations]
\label{thm:tree-obligation-root-ledger-exactness}
The $\TreeUp$ certificate must expose exactly the following rows: a
root-or-root-free witness, a syntactic-representation carrier row, the finite
path witnesses of \autoref{thm:tree-obligation-connected-acyclic}, the
acyclicity witnesses of \autoref{thm:tree-obligation-connected-acyclic}, and
the dependency rows inherited from $\GraphUp$. These rows are precisely the
ledger and exactness surface for $\TreeUp$: every accepted tree certificate is
read back to those displayed rows, and every displayed row is classified by
the $\GraphUp$ $\hsame$ endpoint/path classifier over $\mathsf{BHist}$ and
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The obligation is the provenance-and-exactness portion of the roadmap
ten-field template.
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Traditional target: a rooted or root-free tree certificate with
  syntactic representation.
  \item BEDC source: $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, $\Cont$, and
  the dependency surface of $\NameCert_{\GraphUp}$.
  \item Carrier: the finite tree-path carrier of
  \autoref{thm:tree-obligation-carrier-classifier}, augmented by a
  root-or-root-free witness and a syntactic-representation carrier row.
  \item Classifier: the $\GraphUp$ classifier transported through $\hsame$ on
  roots, endpoints, path rows, and representation ledgers.
  \item Stability: transport of root status, path witnesses, acyclicity
  witnesses, and representation rows along the inherited classifier.
  \item Ledger: the root witness, representation row, connectedness paths,
  acyclicity witnesses, and dependency rows.
  \item Core theorem list: root coverage, root-free coverage, ledger
  readback, path exactness, acyclicity exactness, and dependency exactness.
  \item Exactness: no additional tree datum is accepted beyond the displayed
  ledger rows, and each displayed ledger row is sufficient for the certificate
  boundary.
  \item Standard bridge: none.
  \item Status: paper obligation.
\end{enumerate}

The root-or-root-free witness determines the public orientation of the tree
carrier, while the syntactic-representation carrier row records the derivation
syntax surface that consumes $\TreeUp$.

The path witnesses and acyclicity witnesses are exactly the rows named in
\autoref{thm:tree-obligation-connected-acyclic}. The remaining dependency
rows are the $\GraphUp$ carrier, classifier, unit-loop, two-step composition,
and three-step reassociation rows. Repacking this list gives the full
$\TreeUp$ ledger, and exactness says that the certificate contains no hidden
data outside this displayed $\hsame$-classified $\NameCert$ boundary.
\end{proof}
