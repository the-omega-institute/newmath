\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-noglobalsyncboundary-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$ packet makes the no-global-synchronization row of \autoref{def:no-global-sync} into a concrete finite carrier. It is not a multi-Hist configuration, an observer-frame packet, a coupling ledger, an invariant value, or a causal-rate route. It stores the refusal row itself, together with the displayed histories, locality rows, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ routes, package provenance, and local naming data that let downstream consumers cite the boundary without treating it as prose.

\begin{definition}[NoGlobalSyncBoundary carrier]
\label{def:no-global-sync-boundary-carrier}
A $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
G=(H,L,R,S,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $H=(H_1,\ldots,H_n)$ is the finite row of displayed observer histories;
\item $L=(L_1,\ldots,L_n)$ is the locality ledger, with $L_i$ naming the local rows of $H_i$ available to consumers;
\item $R$ is the refusal row, recording that the packet admits no privileged shared clock, global observer frame, total observer order, or selected synchronization map;
\item $S$ records the displayed $\hsame$ transports for the observer-history, locality-ledger, refusal, route, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading the histories, locality ledgers, refusal row, transport rows, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$, $\FiniteMultiHistPacketUp$, $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$, $\ObservationCouplingUp$, $\InterHistInvariantUp$, $\CrossHistCausalRateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a shared clock, privileged frame, host-side observer order, synchronization selector, causal relation, invariant value, maximum-rate witness, metric row, or quotient of histories.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NoGlobalSyncBoundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:no-global-sync-boundary-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:no-global-sync-boundary-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
G=(H,L,R,S,C,P,N),
$$
displayed observer-history rows through $H$, locality-ledger exactness through $L$, explicit refusal of shared clocks, privileged frames, total observer orders, and synchronization selectors through $R$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $S$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:no-global-sync-boundary-carrier}. The rows $H,L,R,S,C,P,N$ account for the observer histories, locality ledgers, refusal ledger, transport table, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming surface.

The positive rows $H$ and $L$ say which histories and locality rows may be read. The negative row $R$ says which global synchronization data are refused. The structural rows $S,C,P,N$ transport, replay, name, and record provenance for exactly those displayed rows.

No further row is available in the carrier. Thus every local NameCert obligation is either a projection from one of the displayed rows or the boundary clause that the missing global clock, frame, order, and selector are not admitted coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NoGlobalSyncBoundary frame-refusal handoff]
\label{thm:no-global-sync-boundary-frame-refusal-handoff}
Every accepted $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$ packet supplies the refusal row needed by an $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$ consumer. The consumer may read the displayed histories $H$, locality ledgers $L$, refusal row $R$, transport rows $S$, routes $C$, provenance $P$, and local naming row $N$, but it may not promote any of them to a shared clock, privileged observer frame, total observer order, or selected synchronization map.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the carrier coordinates from \autoref{def:no-global-sync-boundary-carrier}. The refusal row $R$ is explicitly part of the packet, and the rows $S,C,P,N$ give the transport, route, provenance, and naming data by which an observer-frame consumer can cite it.

Apply \autoref{thm:no-global-sync-boundary-namecert-obligations}. The same local NameCert surface that admits $R$ also records the refusal of shared clocks, privileged frames, total observer orders, and synchronization selectors.

The handoff therefore carries negative boundary data, not an admitted global frame. Any accepted $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$ use can consume $R$ as the displayed refusal row, while the missing synchronization objects remain outside the public packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NoGlobalSyncBoundary finite-packet non-escape]
\label{thm:no-global-sync-boundary-finite-packet-nonescape}
Every accepted $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$ consumer route factors through the finite rows
$$
H,L,R,S,C,P,N.
$$
A consumer that exports a shared clock, privileged frame, total observer order, selected synchronization map, causal-rate route, invariant value, coupling ledger, metric row, or quotient of histories without displaying it as refused data has left the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier in \autoref{def:no-global-sync-boundary-carrier} is finite and lists exactly seven rows. The rows $H$ and $L$ provide local history and locality access, $R$ provides the refusal boundary, and $S,C,P,N$ provide transport, replay, provenance, and naming.

No coordinate of this tuple is a shared clock, privileged frame, total observer order, selected synchronization map, causal-rate route, invariant value, coupling ledger, metric row, or quotient of histories. These objects cannot be projected from the accepted packet as admitted data.

It remains only the refusal case. If such an object is mentioned by an accepted consumer, it must be mentioned through $R$ as excluded boundary data and replayed through the displayed routes in $C$. Otherwise the consumer has introduced a row that is not part of $G=(H,L,R,S,C,P,N)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NoGlobalSyncBoundary multi-Hist lattice link]
\label{thm:no-global-sync-boundary-multihist-lattice-link}
Every accepted $\NoGlobalSyncBoundaryUp$ consumer reads the refusal row as a downstream lattice edge from the multi-history configuration surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-multihistconfig-namecert} through the invariant surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-interhistinvariant-namecert}, the causal-rate surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausal-namecert}, and the maximum-rate boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-maxcausalrate-namecert}. The consumer may cite those sibling carriers only as displayed dependency rows, and it still exports no shared clock, privileged frame, selected synchronization map, causal-rate route, metric row, or quotient of histories.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:no-global-sync-boundary-carrier}. Its coordinates expose $H,L,R,S,C,P,N$: displayed histories, locality ledgers, the refusal row, componentwise transport, continuation routes, provenance, and local naming.

Apply the local NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:no-global-sync-boundary-namecert-obligations}. The provenance row $P$ may name the multi-Hist, inter-Hist invariant, cross-Hist causal, and maximum-rate sibling surfaces as dependency rows, while the refusal row $R$ records that no global synchronization object is admitted.

Finally apply \autoref{thm:no-global-sync-boundary-finite-packet-nonescape}. A consumer route that reads the cited siblings still factors through the finite packet $G=(H,L,R,S,C,P,N)$. Thus the sibling rows are provenance and consumer routes for the Inter-Hist locality lattice, not hidden synchronization data, causal-rate data, metric data, or a quotient of histories.
\end{proof}
