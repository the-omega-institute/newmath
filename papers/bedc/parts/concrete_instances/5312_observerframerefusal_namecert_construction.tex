\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-observerframerefusal-namecert}

The $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$ packet gives the no-global-frame observer direction a concrete NameCert carrier. It packages the refusal surface implicit in \autoref{ch:visions-observer-hist-identity} as finite rows: an observer-history row, local coordinate-frame row, locality-cell row, rate row, synchronization-refusal ledger, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming row. It sits after \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-coordinate-frame-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-locality-cell-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausalrate-namecert}: those chapters supply the identity, local frame, locality-cell, and maximum-rate surfaces, while this chapter records that none of them may be promoted to a synchronized external observer frame.

\begin{definition}[Observer-frame refusal carrier]
\label{def:observer-frame-refusal-carrier}
An $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(O,Q,L,R,G,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the observer-history identity row consumed from $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$;
\item $Q$ is the local coordinate-frame row consumed from $\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp$ as an observer-local coordinate packet, not as a global frame;
\item $L$ is the locality-cell row consumed from $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$;
\item $R$ is the cross-Hist causal-rate row consumed from $\CrossHistCausalRateUp$;
\item $G$ is the refusal ledger, recording that no synchronized clock, privileged observer frame, global coordinate chart, metric tensor, or all-observer synchronization row is admitted;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the observer-history, coordinate-frame, locality-cell, causal-rate, refusal-ledger, route, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes for reading the local observer rows, checking the refusal ledger, and replaying the accepted consumer route;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$, $\ObserverHistIdentityUp$, $\ObserverCoordinateFrameUp$, $\ObserverLocalityCellUp$, $\HistTimeStreamUp$, $\CrossHistCausalRateUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverFrameRefusalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for a synchronized clock, privileged observer-frame row, global frame, metric tensor, quotient observer identity, host equality of histories, or unlisted external coordinate.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Observer-frame refusal NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:observer-frame-refusal-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:observer-frame-refusal-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ObserverFrameRefusalUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for
$$
F=(O,Q,L,R,G,H,C,P,N),
$$
observer-Hist identity through $O$, local coordinate-frame use through $Q$, locality-cell anchoring through $L$, cross-Hist causal-rate use through $R$, explicit refusal of synchronized clocks and privileged external frames through $G$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:observer-frame-refusal-carrier}. The rows $O,Q,L,R,G,H,C,P,N$ account for the observer-history identity, local frame, locality cell, causal-rate row, refusal ledger, transport, route replay, provenance, and local naming surface.

The first four analytic rows are consumed from the sibling chapters. The observer-Hist identity row comes from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observerhistidentity-namecert}; the local coordinate-frame row comes from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-coordinate-frame-namecert}; the locality cell comes from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-observer-locality-cell-namecert}; and the causal-rate row comes from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-crosshistcausalrate-namecert}.

The refusal ledger $G$ is the row that blocks promotion of those local rows into a synchronized external frame. The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ replay and name the same refusal route, so every accepted read remains inside the finite packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observer-frame refusal no-global-sync boundary]
\label{thm:observer-frame-refusal-no-global-sync-boundary}
Every accepted $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$ packet $F=(O,Q,L,R,G,H,C,P,N)$ admits observer-facing reads only through the displayed local rows $O,Q,L,R$ and the refusal ledger $G$, replayed through $H,C,P,N$. A consumer that reads a synchronized clock, privileged observer-frame row, global frame, metric tensor, quotient observer identity, host equality of histories, or unlisted external coordinate has left the accepted packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. The only observer-facing rows are $O,Q,L,R$, and the only row that records the refusal of external synchronization data is $G$.

Apply \autoref{thm:observer-frame-refusal-namecert-obligations}. The local NameCert surface requires the identity, coordinate-frame, locality-cell, causal-rate, and refusal rows to be consumed together with $H,C,P,N$.

Finally inspect the excluded coordinates in \autoref{def:observer-frame-refusal-carrier}. No synchronized clock, privileged observer frame, global coordinate chart, metric tensor, quotient observer identity, host equality of histories, or unlisted external coordinate is present. Therefore an accepted consumer route can only display such a row as refused external data, never as an admitted packet coordinate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Observer-frame refusal TasteGate obligation]
\label{thm:observer-frame-refusal-tastegate-obligation}
The later Lean \texttt{TasteGate.lean} development for $\ObserverFrameRefusalUp$ must include $\mathsf{Nontrivial}\ \ObserverFrameRefusalUp$ with explicit distinct witnesses and $\mathsf{FieldFaithful}\ \ObserverFrameRefusalUp$ by field projection and injection. These gate-facing instances must preserve the observer-history row, local coordinate-frame row, locality-cell row, cross-Hist causal-rate row, refusal ledger, transport rows, continuation routes, provenance row, and local naming row.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The carrier has separate rows for observer identity, local coordinate use, locality-cell anchoring, causal-rate reading, and refusal of external synchronization. A singleton collapse would erase the difference between local observer data and refused external frame data.

Field faithfulness is the projection discipline for the displayed tuple. Each row of $F=(O,Q,L,R,G,H,C,P,N)$ has a distinct role, and the refusal ledger $G$ must remain distinguishable from the local coordinate-frame row $Q$ and the causal-rate row $R$.
\end{proof}
