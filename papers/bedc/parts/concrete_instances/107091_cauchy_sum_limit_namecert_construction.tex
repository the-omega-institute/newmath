\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchySumLimitUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-sum-limit-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\CauchySumLimitUp$ packet records the finite route by which two displayed regular Cauchy summands produce a regular Cauchy sum and then a terminal real-name seal. It sits over the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter packages the familiar algebraic limit route for sums as NameCert data rather than as quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[CauchySumLimit carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-sum-limit-carrier}
A $\CauchySumLimitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L_{\Sigma}=(X,Y,S,Q,D,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the two regular Cauchy summand rows, each exposing only its displayed finite regularity surface.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row, listing the finite read windows opened for both summands and for the sum-read request.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, recording the rational readbacks of the two summands and the pointwise sum readback obtained from the same windows.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, splitting every requested sum tolerance into displayed summand tolerances and recording the dyadic addition comparisons used by the readback row.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal for the sum, available only after $S,Q,D$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the summand rows, stream windows, regular readbacks, dyadic tolerance ledger, Real seal, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from any Cauchy-sum-limit request through $X,Y,S,Q,D,A$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchySumLimitUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchySumLimitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,S,Q,D,A,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchySumLimit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-sum-limit-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchySumLimitUp$ carrier
$$
L_{\Sigma}=(X,Y,S,Q,D,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchySumLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the two regular Cauchy summands, finite stream-window display, regular rational sum readback, dyadic tolerance splitting, terminal $\RealUp$ seal routing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, provenance, local naming, and non-escape from quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-sum-limit-carrier}. The only summand data are $X$ and $Y$, and the only observation windows available to consumers are the finite windows recorded by $S$.

The readback stage is finite. The row $Q$ records the rational observations of both summands and the pointwise sum readback obtained from those same windows, while $D$ records the dyadic tolerance split and the addition comparisons that justify the requested sum tolerance.

The seal stage is terminal. The row $A$ can be consumed only after the summand windows, readbacks, and tolerance ledger have been displayed, so a Real-facing consumer cannot bypass $S,Q,D$.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name exactly the displayed route. Since the packet has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness, none of those objects can be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchySumLimit Real-seal route]
\label{thm:cauchy-sum-limit-real-seal-route}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer read exported by an accepted $\CauchySumLimitUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
X,Y \longmapsto S \longmapsto Q,D \longmapsto A,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the sum limit is presented as a displayed regular-Cauchy algebra route to a Real seal, not as a quotient stream, selected limit, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy principle, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted packet $L_{\Sigma}=(X,Y,S,Q,D,A,H,C,P,N)$. A consumer first reads the displayed summand rows $X$ and $Y$; no host sequence equality or quotient representative is available at this stage.

Next the stream windows in $S$ determine the finite observations used for the request. The row $Q$ reads both summands on those windows and records the pointwise rational sum, while $D$ supplies the dyadic tolerance split and the corresponding addition comparisons.

The terminal Real seal is $A$. Since the continuation row $C$ replays only the route through $X,Y,S,Q,D,A$, every Real-facing read reaches $A$ after the finite regular readback and tolerance ledger have been displayed.

The transport and provenance rows preserve the same route under componentwise $\hsame$. The local naming row $N$ therefore exports the sum-limit seal without adding quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}
