\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BoundedCauchyIntervalUp$}

Bishop--Bridges style constructive interval and Cauchy data enter this chapter through a finite packet: a closed rational interval bracket, a regular rational sequence, a finite observation schedule, and a dyadic tolerance ledger are displayed before any $\RealUp$ seal or completion-facing consumer can read an endpoint. The $\BoundedCauchyIntervalUp$ packet sits over the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the finite window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the located regular route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-regseqrat-namecert}, and the finite located interval net of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-heine-borel-located-interval-namecert}. It supplies Real and Completion routes with a bounded interval-level Cauchy packet, not quotient streams, selected limits, choice, or ambient completeness.

\begin{definition}[Bounded Cauchy interval carrier]
\label{def:bounded-cauchy-interval-carrier}
A $\BoundedCauchyIntervalUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
I=(B,S,R,D,L,F,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is a bounded rational interval bracket, recording two rational endpoints, an order witness, and the interval membership window used by every later read.
\item $S$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ observation schedule for the Cauchy name inside the bracket.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from $S$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the dyadic radii, endpoint slack, and bracket-preserving comparison rows.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedRegSeqRatUp$ locatedness row for the same regular rational readback.
\item $F$ is the $\HeineBorelLocatedIntervalUp$ finite-net handoff row for the displayed bracket and located readback.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row read only after $B,S,R,D,L,F$ are displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the bracket, stream schedule, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, locatedness row, finite-net handoff, and real seal.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a bounded Cauchy interval request through $B,S,R,D,L,F,E$ and then to the structural rows.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BoundedCauchyIntervalUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\LocatedRegSeqRatUp$, $\HeineBorelLocatedIntervalUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedCauchyIntervalUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded Cauchy interval NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-cauchy-interval-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BoundedCauchyIntervalUp$ carrier $I=(B,S,R,D,L,F,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedCauchyIntervalUp}$ surface consists exactly of bracket admission, finite stream-window admission, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance exposure, located regular-sequence handoff, finite located-interval net handoff, terminal $\RealUp$ seal admission, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming.

Consequently every Real or Completion consumer of the packet must read the route
$$
\begin{gathered}
B,S,R,D\\
\longrightarrow L,F\\
\longrightarrow E,H,C,P,N
\end{gathered}
$$
and cannot replace it by quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, open-cover compactness, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:bounded-cauchy-interval-carrier}. The only available rows are the bracket, stream schedule, regular readback, dyadic tolerance ledger, locatedness row, finite-net handoff, terminal real seal, and structural BEDC rows.

Use the sibling surfaces named in \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. They force each finite Cauchy request to pass through a displayed window, regular readback, and dyadic tolerance ledger before a real seal is visible.

Then apply the located and interval handoff routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-regseqrat-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-heine-borel-located-interval-namecert}. These rows bind locatedness and finite interval-net coverage to the same displayed bracket and readback. The final $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} is therefore reached only through $B,S,R,D,L,F,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded Cauchy interval regular-seal handoff]
\label{thm:bounded-cauchy-interval-regular-seal-handoff}
Every $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\LocatedRegSeqRatUp$, $\HeineBorelLocatedIntervalUp$, or $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\BoundedCauchyIntervalUp$ carrier $I=(B,S,R,D,L,F,E,H,C,P,N)$ receives the same bounded regular-seal surface: finite observations through $S$, regular readback through $R$, dyadic tolerance and bracket slack through $D$, locatedness through $L$, finite interval-net handoff through $F$, and the terminal real seal through $E$.

The handoff exports no completion carrier beyond these rows. In particular, a consumer cannot read a selected limit, quotient stream representative, global compactness theorem, host real equality, countable-choice schedule, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness proof from the packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with \autoref{thm:bounded-cauchy-interval-namecert-obligations}. It fixes the public route through $B,S,R,D,L,F,E,H,C,P,N$.

For regular-sequence consumers, the route is already displayed by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert} after the finite window and dyadic ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}. For located consumers, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-located-regseqrat-namecert} uses the same regular readback and tolerance data.

For interval and Real consumers, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-heine-borel-located-interval-namecert} supplies the finite interval-net row and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert} supplies the terminal seal. Since the carrier contains no coordinate outside the displayed finite route, the handoff cannot introduce quotient streams, selected limits, global compactness, countable choice, host equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{proof}
