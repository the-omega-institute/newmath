\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\NaturalDeductionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-natural-deduction-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Gentzen and Prawitz, the $\NaturalDeductionUp$ packet records a finite proof-tree calculus whose rows expose assumptions, rule applications, discharge, conclusion readback, and proof-term boundary data without importing host proof equality. It sits beside the syntax-and-deduction surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-firstorder-namecert}, the proof-term carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-lambdacalc-namecert}, the bounded computation packet of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-computable-namecert}, and the run-trace surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-turingmachine-namecert}. The packet is a finite NameCert neighbour: it names proof-tree evidence, but it does not identify proofs by normalization, beta equality, semantic validity, or machine execution.

\begin{definition}[NaturalDeduction carrier]
\label{def:natural-deduction-carrier}
A $\NaturalDeductionUp$ carrier is a finite proof-tree $\BHist$ packet
$$
D=(A,R,S,Q,B,T,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $A$ is the assumption row, listing the open formula endpoints admitted at the leaves;
\item $R$ is the rule row, recording introduction, elimination, weakening, and structural rule instances as finite local steps;
\item $S$ is the discharge schedule, pairing discharged assumption occurrences with the rule rows that close them;
\item $Q$ is the conclusion row, exposing the formula endpoint read at the root of the tree;
\item $B$ is the proof-term boundary row, recording when a proof tree may be compared with a $\LambdaCalcUp$ term without quotienting proof identity;
\item $T$ is the transport row, comparing proof-tree nodes only by displayed $\hsame$ routes over formula, assumption, rule, discharge, and conclusion rows;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ stability for the assumption, rule, discharge, conclusion, proof-term boundary, transport, replay, provenance, and local name rows;
\item $K$ records $\Cont$ replay from assumptions through rule rows and discharge rows to the conclusion and proof-term boundary;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\FirstOrderUp$, $\LambdaCalcUp$, $\ComputableUp$, $\TuringMachineUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\NaturalDeductionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier has no coordinate for host proof equality, global proof normalization, semantic validity, executable machine trace, selected proof representative, or quotient proof identity.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[NaturalDeduction NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:natural-deduction-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:natural-deduction-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\NaturalDeductionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $D=(A,R,S,Q,B,T,H,K,P,N)$, assumption exposure through $A$, rule-step exposure through $R$, discharge exposure through $S$, conclusion readback through $Q$, proof-term boundary exposure through $B$, transport through $T$, componentwise $\hsame$ stability through $H$, $\Cont$ replay through $K$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the displayed carrier of \autoref{def:natural-deduction-carrier}. The rows $A,R,S,Q,B,T,H,K,P,N$ are the only coordinates from which the local certificate may read proof-tree data.

The proof-tree content is exposed by the assumption, rule, discharge, and conclusion rows. A consumer can inspect open hypotheses, local rule instances, discharged occurrences, and the root conclusion, but those reads remain inside the finite tree packet.

The proof-term boundary row is separate from proof identity. It records the visible point at which a $\LambdaCalcUp$ term may be compared with a tree boundary, while forbidding any quotient that identifies proof trees by normalization or beta equality.

The structural rows then provide transport, replay, provenance, and naming. Since the carrier contains no further field, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NaturalDeduction FirstOrder consumer route]
\label{thm:natural-deduction-firstorder-consumer-route}
Every $\FirstOrderUp$ consumer of an accepted $\NaturalDeductionUp$ packet reads only the displayed formula endpoints, assumption rows, rule rows, discharge schedule, conclusion row, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row of \autoref{def:natural-deduction-carrier}. It cannot replace the proof-tree route by host syntax equality, semantic validity, model-theoretic completeness, or an external proof checker.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a $\FirstOrderUp$ read of the accepted carrier $D$. The package row $P$ names $\FirstOrderUp$ as the syntax and formula source, so the formula endpoints available to the consumer are exactly the endpoints carried by the assumption and conclusion rows.

Rule and discharge reads pass through $R$ and $S$. These rows record finite local proof steps and the assumptions closed by those steps; they do not add a semantic model, a completeness theorem, or a host proof oracle.

Finally replay $T,H,K,P,N$. Transport and continuation preserve the same displayed proof-tree rows, and the local name row registers that route as the $\NaturalDeductionUp$ certificate. No external proof checker has a coordinate from which it could be projected.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[NaturalDeduction LambdaCalc proof-term boundary]
\label{thm:natural-deduction-lambdacalc-proof-term-boundary}
The $\LambdaCalcUp$ comparison surface of an accepted $\NaturalDeductionUp$ packet is exactly the proof-term boundary row $B$ together with the assumption, rule, discharge, conclusion, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows of \autoref{def:natural-deduction-carrier}. It supplies a boundary for Curry--Howard-style comparison, but it does not identify natural-deduction proof trees with lambda terms, alpha classes, beta-normal forms, or machine traces.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the boundary row $B$ from the carrier. Its purpose is to name the visible point where a proof tree may be compared with the $\LambdaCalcUp$ term surface.

The comparison still depends on the proof-tree rows. The assumption row $A$, rule row $R$, discharge row $S$, and conclusion row $Q$ determine which proof boundary is being compared, while $T,H,K$ preserve and replay those rows componentwise.

The provenance row $P$ may cite $\LambdaCalcUp$, $\ComputableUp$, and $\TuringMachineUp$ as nearby surfaces. This citation does not add alpha quotienting, beta-normal equality, host program equality, or a machine run trace to the natural-deduction packet.

Thus the chapter supplies a boundary for proof-term comparison only. Any stronger identification would require a separate carrier coordinate, and none is present in \autoref{def:natural-deduction-carrier}.
\end{proof}

\falsifiablePrediction{If a proof-tree packet claims $\NaturalDeductionUp$ acceptance while omitting assumption rows, rule rows, discharge schedule, conclusion readback, proof-term boundary, transport, replay, provenance, or local NameCert rows, it refutes the carrier of \autoref{def:natural-deduction-carrier}.}

\independenceWitness{firstorder, lambdacalc, computable, turingmachine: $\NaturalDeductionUp$ is not bijective to any listed sibling because it carries assumption rows, rule rows, discharge schedule, conclusion readback, and a proof-term boundary as a single proof-tree certificate. FirstOrderUp carries syntax and deduction endpoints without the separate proof-term boundary; LambdaCalcUp carries terms and beta-substitution ledgers without discharged proof assumptions; ComputableUp and TuringMachineUp carry bounded computation and run traces rather than finite proof-tree discharge.}
