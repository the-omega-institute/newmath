\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\WhiteheadProductUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-whitehead-product-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following J. H. C. Whitehead on products in homotopy groups, the $\WhiteheadProductUp$ packet names the bracket of two pointed sphere maps by finite BEDC rows. It sits beside the circle source of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-s1-namecert}, the deformation classifier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-homotopy-namecert}, and the horn-and-face bookkeeping of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-simplicialset-namecert}. The certificate records pointed sphere maps, a commutator attachment, a bracket classifier, componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming; it does not import a completed homotopy group, a CW attaching theorem, or a host quotient of maps.

\begin{definition}[$\WhiteheadProductUp$ finite carrier]
\label{def:whitehead-product-carrier}
A $\WhiteheadProductUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
\Omega=(S_m,S_n,a,b,\partial,\kappa,\beta,H,C,P,N).
$$
Its displayed rows are as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S_m$ and $S_n$ are pointed sphere-source rows. Each records a finite basepoint, boundary, and face ledger read through the sphere source surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-s1-namecert} and the simplicial face discipline of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-simplicialset-namecert}.
\item $a$ and $b$ are pointed sphere-map rows into the same displayed target packet. Their basepoint rows agree with the target basepoint by visible $\hsame$ transport, and their homotopy admissibility is read through \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-homotopy-namecert}.
\item $\partial$ is the attachment row for the boundary of the product cell. It records the two coordinate-face routes, the basepoint collar, and the finite source-face identifications used to read the commutator boundary.
\item $\kappa$ is the commutator row. It lists the ordered route $a b a^{-1} b^{-1}$ along the displayed boundary faces, together with the face-by-face signs and basepoint returns needed to keep the pointed condition visible.
\item $\beta$ is the homotopy-bracket classifier row. It classifies two accepted packets by componentwise $\HomotopyUp$ comparison of $a$ and $b$, by $\hsame$ comparison of the sphere-source rows, and by equality of the displayed attachment and commutator ledgers.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S_m,S_n,a,b,\partial,\kappa,\beta,C,P,N$.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for the two pointed map rows, the boundary attachment route, the commutator readback, and the bracket classifier readback.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\WhiteheadProductUp$, $\SOneUp$, $\HomotopyUp$, $\SimplicialSetUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\WhiteheadProductUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier is accepted only when all rows occur in the same displayed finite packet. Thus the Whitehead product is presented as an inspectable sphere-attachment and commutator ledger rather than as an element of an ambient homotopy group.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[$\WhiteheadProductUp$ NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:whitehead-product-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\WhiteheadProductUp$ carrier
$$
\Omega=(S_m,S_n,a,b,\partial,\kappa,\beta,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\WhiteheadProductUp}$ surface consists exactly of sphere-source admission, pointed map admission, attachment admission, commutator ledger exactness, homotopy-bracket classification, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. A downstream homotopy consumer may inspect $S_m,S_n,a,b,\partial,\kappa,\beta,H,C,P,N$ as the finite bracket packet, but it cannot replace the packet by a host homotopy-group element or by an uncarried attaching map.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:whitehead-product-carrier}. The rows $S_m,S_n,a,b,\partial,\kappa,\beta,H,C,P,N$ already list the two pointed source spheres, the two pointed maps, the attachment boundary, the commutator route, the classifier, transport, replay, provenance, and local name.

The NameCert comparison is the classifier row $\beta$. It compares the two pointed map rows through $\HomotopyUp$, compares the sphere-source rows through their displayed source classifiers, and then checks that the attachment and commutator rows have the same finite face itinerary. No comparison step quantifies over all maps from a product cell or over a completed homotopy group.

The stability fields are exactly $H$ and $C$. Transport along $H$ keeps the component rows in the same packet positions, and replay along $C$ keeps the boundary attachment, commutator readback, and bracket classifier visible under the same provenance $P$.

The local name $N$ is therefore attached to the finite bracket packet itself. Since each public coordinate has been accounted for by admission, classification, transport, replay, and provenance, there is no remaining route through which a consumer can demand an ambient Whitehead-product theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$\WhiteheadProductUp$ sphere-attachment route]
\label{thm:whitehead-product-sphere-attachment-route}
Let $\Omega=(S_m,S_n,a,b,\partial,\kappa,\beta,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\WhiteheadProductUp$ carrier. Any consumer that reads the bracket as a sphere-attachment factors through the pointed sphere rows $S_m,S_n$, the pointed maps $a,b$, the attachment row $\partial$, the commutator row $\kappa$, the homotopy-bracket classifier $\beta$, the componentwise transport $H$, the displayed replay $C$, the provenance $P$, and the local name $N$. The consumer may read the ordered boundary route and the homotopy classifier, but it cannot substitute a quotient of maps, an unbounded cell complex, or a completed homotopy-group operation.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the two pointed sphere rows and the two pointed map rows. Their basepoints and boundary faces are already displayed by $S_m,S_n,a,b$, and their admissibility is routed through the sibling sphere, simplicial-set, and homotopy certificates named in the carrier.

Next project the attachment row $\partial$. It exposes only the finite boundary itinerary: coordinate faces, basepoint collar, and source-face identifications. The commutator row $\kappa$ reads that itinerary in the order $a b a^{-1} b^{-1}$ and records the signs and basepoint returns needed for a pointed bracket.

Finally apply the classifier and stability rows. The row $\beta$ classifies the bracket by the transported pointed maps and the identical finite attachment itinerary, while $H$ and $C$ preserve that itinerary under componentwise transport and replay.

Thus the sphere-attachment consumer sees precisely the finite $\BHist$ bracket route with $\Pkg$ provenance and local $\NameCert$ naming. No step introduces a host quotient, a global CW-complex construction, or a stronger homotopy-group operation.
\end{proof}
