Following Bishop--Bridges constructive completion, the $\RealCompletionFiniteRouteUp$ packet records the finite composite admission route from dyadic tolerance data to a $\RealUp$-facing completion read. It ties together the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the terminal $\RealUp$ seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the threshold handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-modulus-selection-namecert}, the nested interval route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-nested-interval-real-namecert}, and the universal boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-completion-universal-boundary-namecert}. The certificate is a finite route record only; it is not quotient stream equality, a selected limit, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.

\begin{definition}[Real completion finite-route carrier]
\label{def:real-completion-finite-route-carrier}
A $\RealCompletionFiniteRouteUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
F=(D,W,R,A,M,J,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row, displaying the requested dyadic scale, slack, comparison, and radius data used by the completion read.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window family opened for the displayed tolerance request and for every rational observation consumed downstream.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row obtained from $D$ and $W$.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the dyadic tolerance, finite windows, and regular readback rows have been displayed.
\item $M$ is the $\CauchyModulusSelectionUp$ row binding the displayed tolerance request to a finite threshold and exactness ledger before $A$ is exported.
\item $J$ is the $\NestedIntervalRealUp$ route row, recording the nested-interval source, dyadic diameter ledger, endpoint seals, finite windows, regular readback, and terminal Real seal used by the same completion read.
\item $U$ is the $\BishopCompletionUniversalBoundaryUp$ face, recording the extension and uniqueness boundary available to consumers of the displayed completion route.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the dyadic tolerance row, finite windows, regular readback, terminal Real seal, modulus-selection row, nested-interval route, universal-boundary face, replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from every Real-facing completion request through $D,W,R,A,M,J,U,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealCompletionFiniteRouteUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CauchyModulusSelectionUp$, $\NestedIntervalRealUp$, $\BishopCompletionUniversalBoundaryUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionFiniteRouteUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $D,W,R,A,M,J,U,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or standard bridge data.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion finite-route NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-completion-finite-route-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealCompletionFiniteRouteUp$ carrier
$$
F=(D,W,R,A,M,J,U,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\RealCompletionFiniteRouteUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the dyadic tolerance row, finite stream windows, regular rational readback, terminal Real seal, modulus-selection row, nested-interval route, universal-boundary face, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item tolerance exactness, requiring every completion request to expose its $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ radius, slack, scale, and comparison data in $D$ before any regular readback or Real seal is inspected;
\item finite-window exactness, requiring every rational observation used by the readback, modulus selector, nested route, or universal-boundary consumer to occur inside the displayed $\StreamNameUp$ family $W$;
\item regular-readback ordering, requiring the $\RegSeqRatUp$ row $R$ to be generated from $D,W$ before the terminal $\RealUp$ seal $A$ is exported;
\item modulus-selection compatibility, requiring $M$ to bind the same tolerance row $D$ and window family $W$ to a finite threshold ledger for the Real-facing request;
\item nested-route compatibility, requiring $J$ to reuse the same dyadic, stream, regular-readback, and Real-seal surface instead of introducing an ambient intersection point or selected limit;
\item universal-boundary compatibility, requiring $U$ to expose extension and uniqueness reads only after $D,W,R,A,M,J$ have fixed the displayed completion route;
\item non-escape, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name no quotient stream, selected limit, host real equality, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy branch, open-cover compactness row, ambient Real completeness theorem, Lean verification marker, or bridge credential.
\end{enumerate}
No Real completion finite-route obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-completion-finite-route-carrier}. The accepted packet displays one dyadic tolerance row, one finite window family, one regular rational readback, one terminal Real seal, one modulus-selection row, one nested-interval route, one universal-boundary face, and the structural rows that transport, replay, source, and name them.

Tolerance exactness is read from $D$. Since the carrier has no second tolerance coordinate, every scale, slack, radius, and comparison used by the completion read must be the displayed $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ data.

Finite-window exactness is read from $W$. The regular readback $R$, the selected threshold row $M$, the nested-interval route $J$, and the universal-boundary face $U$ all consume the same finite observations, so no hidden stream or quotient representative can supply additional data.

The ordering of the analytic route is fixed by $R,A,M,J,U$. The readback $R$ is downstream of $D,W$, the Real seal $A$ is downstream of $R$, the modulus row $M$ binds the tolerance request before that seal is exported, the nested route $J$ records the same read as interval data, and the boundary face $U$ is available only after the completion route has been displayed.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ preserve, replay, source, and name the same finite route. The excluded quotient, selected-limit, host-equality, choice, trichotomy, open-cover, ambient-completeness, Lean-verification, and bridge objects are absent from the carrier, so the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real completion finite-route determinacy]
\label{thm:real-completion-finite-route-determinacy}
Let
$$
F=(D,W,R,A,M,J,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\RealCompletionFiniteRouteUp$ carrier. Every $\RealUp$-facing completion read exported by $F$ factors through exactly the displayed finite route
$$
D \longrightarrow W \longrightarrow R \longrightarrow M \longrightarrow J \longrightarrow U \longrightarrow A
$$
with componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and local naming supplied only by $H,C,P,N$. Consequently the exported read cannot use quotient streams, selected limits, host real equality, countable choice, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Fix a Real-facing completion read exported by $F$. The only Real-seal coordinate in the carrier is $A$, so the read must reach the Real boundary through the displayed terminal seal.

Read the dependencies of $A$ inside the carrier. The seal is downstream of the regular readback $R$, and $R$ is generated from the dyadic tolerance row $D$ and the finite stream windows $W$.

The same request is constrained by the local route certificates. The row $M$ records the finite modulus-selection handoff for the displayed tolerance and windows, $J$ records the nested-interval realization of the same readback and seal surface, and $U$ records the Bishop completion universal boundary visible to extension and uniqueness consumers.

The structural rows do not add analytic data. The $\hsame$ row $H$ transports the displayed coordinates componentwise, the $\Cont$ row $C$ replays the displayed route, and $P,N$ source and name it.

There are no remaining coordinates from which a different route could be read. In particular the carrier contains no quotient stream, selected-limit row, host equality, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy branch, open-cover compactness object, or ambient completeness theorem. Thus every exported Real-facing completion read factors through exactly the displayed finite route and through no excluded object.
\end{proof}
