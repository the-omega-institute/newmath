\begin{theorem}[Reality-constrained TruthCert root ledger nonescape]
\label{thm:reality-constrained-truth-cert-root-ledger-nonescape}
No root-facing export may expose the local name $N$ while bypassing the ledger
row $L$ or the failure row $F$. The accepted export of
$S,\Sigma,K,T,U,D,I,L,F,N$ therefore records compression, provenance, hidden
residue, finite defeat, domain exit, classifier mismatch, and later observation
before $N$ is readable.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp.RealityConstrainedTruthCertRootLedgerNonescape}
\begin{proof}
Apply ledger exactness and failure-surface exactness,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-truth-cert-ledger-exactness} and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-truth-cert-failure-surface-exactness}. They
make $L$ and $F$ exact displayed rows, not optional annotations.

Finally use export readiness and sibling handoff totality,
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-truth-cert-export-readiness} and
\autoref{thm:reality-constrained-truth-cert-sibling-handoff-totality}. The local
name $N$ is readable only after all displayed rows and sibling routes are
consumed, so an export that bypasses $L$ or $F$ cannot be the root-ready
$\RealityConstrainedTruthCertUp$ packet.
\end{proof}
