Following Bishop and Bridges, the $\CauchyModulusSubsequenceUp$ packet records the finite route by which a bounded regular Cauchy source exposes a modulus-indexed subsequence schedule without exporting an infinite selector. It sits between the bounded subsequence envelope of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-regular-cauchy-subsequence-namecert}, the regular subsequence route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-subsequence-namecert}, the finite stream-window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance core of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet is a constructive completion-facing certificate: it supplies a monotone cofinal schedule, finite windows, tolerance accounting, and a Real seal, but not countable choice, quotient stream equality, a selected infinite subsequence, host equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[Cauchy modulus subsequence carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-modulus-subsequence-carrier}
A $\CauchyModulusSubsequenceUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(B,M,T,W,D,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $B$ is the accepted $\BoundedRegularCauchySubsequenceUp$ source row whose bounded regular Cauchy data are available before any subsequence-modulus request is read.
\item $M$ is the modulus row. It records the requested Cauchy tolerance discipline and the finite comparison budget used by the subsequence schedule.
\item $T$ is the monotone cofinal schedule row. It records the finite evidence that requested indices are sent to later source windows and that the schedule is cofinal in the displayed source.
\item $W$ records the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows selected by the schedule row $T$ at the requested modulus levels.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger attached to those windows.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the displayed windows and dyadic tolerances.
\item $R$ is the $\RegularCauchySubsequenceUp$ handoff row reached from $B,M,T,W,D,Q$.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal reached only after the bounded source, modulus, schedule, windows, tolerance ledger, regular readback, and regular subsequence handoff have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the bounded source, modulus, cofinal schedule, windows, dyadic ledger, regular readback, regular subsequence handoff, terminal seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a subsequence-modulus request through $B,M,T,W,D,Q,R,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyModulusSubsequenceUp$, $\BoundedRegularCauchySubsequenceUp$, $\RegularCauchySubsequenceUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusSubsequenceUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $B,M,T,W,D,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a selected infinite subsequence, quotient stream equality, countable choice, host real equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus subsequence NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-subsequence-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyModulusSubsequenceUp$ carrier $S=(B,M,T,W,D,Q,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyModulusSubsequenceUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission, bounded-source admission through $B$, modulus monotonicity through $M$, monotone cofinal schedule admission through $T$, finite-window determinacy through $W$, dyadic tolerance accounting through $D$, regular rational readback through $Q$, $\RegularCauchySubsequenceUp$ handoff through $R$, Real-seal nonescape through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, package provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-subsequence-carrier}. Its analytic coordinates are exactly the bounded source $B$, modulus row $M$, monotone cofinal schedule $T$, finite windows $W$, dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, regular readback $Q$, regular subsequence handoff $R$, and terminal Real seal $E$.

The modulus and schedule obligations are displayed before any support row is inspected. The row $M$ supplies the tolerance discipline, while $T$ records monotonicity and cofinality for the finite requested indices. The window row $W$ and tolerance row $D$ then determine which $\StreamNameUp$ windows and dyadic bounds are visible at those requests.

The readback and seal obligations remain downstream. The row $Q$ reads the visible windows as $\RegSeqRatUp$ data, $R$ records the $\RegularCauchySubsequenceUp$ handoff, and $E$ is reachable only after the preceding rows have been displayed. Rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that same finite route, so no further NameCert obligation remains outside the listed coordinates.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Cauchy modulus subsequence window determinacy]
\label{thm:cauchy-modulus-subsequence-window-determinacy}
Every consumer admitted by an accepted $\CauchyModulusSubsequenceUp$ carrier must read the bounded source $B$, modulus row $M$, monotone cofinal schedule $T$, finite windows $W$, dyadic tolerance ledger $D$, regular rational readback $Q$, regular subsequence handoff $R$, and terminal Real seal $E$ before reading the transport, replay, provenance, or local naming rows. The packet exports no selected infinite subsequence, quotient stream equality, countable choice, host equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a consumer read. By \autoref{def:cauchy-modulus-subsequence-carrier}, the only consumer-facing route before the support rows is the ordered list $B,M,T,W,D,Q,R,E$.

The rows $B,M,T$ fix the source, tolerance request, and monotone cofinal schedule. The row $W$ then supplies only the finite windows named by that schedule, and $D$ supplies the dyadic tolerance ledger attached to those windows. Thus the visible subsequence data are determined by displayed finite requests, not by an externally selected infinite object.

The remaining analytic rows are also fixed by the displayed windows. The readback row $Q$ converts those windows into $\RegSeqRatUp$ data, $R$ records the regular subsequence handoff, and $E$ is the terminal Real seal for that route. Since the carrier contains no coordinate for quotient stream equality, countable choice, host equality, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, the support rows $H,C,P,N$ can only preserve the displayed finite packet.
\end{proof}
