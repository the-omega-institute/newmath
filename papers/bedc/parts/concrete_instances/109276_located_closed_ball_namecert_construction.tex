\begin{theorem}[Located closed-ball membership transport]
\label{thm:located-closed-ball-membership-transport}
Let $B=(M,c,r,D,I,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ and $B'=(M',c',r',D',I',R',E',H',C',P',N')$ be accepted $\LocatedClosedBallUp$ carriers whose metric source, centre, radius, distance, interval, membership, and seal rows are paired by the displayed $\hsame$ transports. Then the located closed-ball membership row $R$ transports to $R'$ through the same located-metric distance row and located-interval comparison row, with $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ naming preserved.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:located-closed-ball-carrier}. The membership row $R$ is downstream of the located metric source $M$, centre $c$, radius $r$, distance row $D$, located interval row $I$, and terminal seal $E$.

Transport the displayed rows componentwise by $H$ and $H'$. The centre and radius rows move together with the distance row, and the located interval comparison remains attached to that same transported distance and radius.

Replay the comparison by $C$ and $C'$. Since $P,P'$ and $N,N'$ source and name only the displayed rows, the transported membership row is still the $\MetricClosedBallUp$ comparison read through $\LocatedMetricUp$ and $\LocatedIntervalUp$, not a host subset or quotient point equality.
\end{proof}
