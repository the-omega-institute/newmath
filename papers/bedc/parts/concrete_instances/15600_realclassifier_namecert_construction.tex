\begin{definition}[RealClassifier carrier]
\label{def:real-classifier-carrier}
A $\RealClassifierUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
Q=(X,Y,S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y,W,D,C,E,H,K,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the two terminal $\RealUp$ seal rows whose public classifier relation is being exposed.
\item $S_X$ and $S_Y$ are the regular rational source rows read back from the two seals.
\item $R_X$ and $R_Y$ are the corresponding $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular-rational readback rows.
\item $W$ is the common $\StreamNameUp$ finite window through which both readbacks are inspected.
\item $D$ is the shared $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger recording the finite precision, radius, and comparison budget used by the classifier read.
\item $C$ is the $\RealNameClassifierUp$ row comparing the two regular names through $W$ and $D$.
\item $E$ is the $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$ row that records the regular-Cauchy equivalence boundary visible before the terminal real seals are consumed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the two seals, readbacks, common window, dyadic ledger, classifier rows, provenance, and local name.
\item $K$ stores displayed $\Cont$ replay routes from $X,Y$ through $S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y,W,D,C,E$ and back to the public seal boundary.
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealClassifierUp$, $\RealUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealNameClassifierUp$, $\RegularCauchyEquivalenceUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.

\end{enumerate}
The packet has no coordinate for quotient equality of streams, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[RealClassifier NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-classifier-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealClassifierUp$ carrier $Q=(X,Y,S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y,W,D,C,E,H,K,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealClassifierUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following five rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two $\RealUp$ seals, regular rational readbacks, common window, dyadic tolerance ledger, classifier rows, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item tolerance classifier stability, requiring every classifier read to pass through the common window $W$, the dyadic ledger $D$, and the $\RealNameClassifierUp$ row $C$;
\item $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff, requiring every regular-rational consumer to factor through $S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y,W,D,C,E$ before reaching the public classifier boundary;
\item real seal non-escape, requiring $X$ and $Y$ to be consumed only as displayed terminal seals rather than as host real values or quotient classes;
\item ledger exhaustion, requiring $H,K,P,N$ to transport, replay, source, and name only the displayed finite packet.
\end{enumerate}
No local NameCert obligation is stored outside these five rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-classifier-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes exactly the two seal rows, the two regular rational source rows, the two $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks, the common window, the dyadic ledger, the two classifier rows, and the structural rows $H,K,P,N$.

Tolerance classifier stability is the route through $W,D,C$. Since $W$ is the only common inspection schedule and $D$ is the only tolerance ledger, a classifier read cannot inspect either side independently of those rows.

The regular-rational handoff uses $S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y$ before it reaches $C$ and $E$. The real seal row is then read only after that finite handoff has been displayed.

The remaining rows transport, replay, source, and locally name the same packet. Because the carrier omits quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected limits, trichotomy, countable choice, and ambient completeness, no NameCert obligation can export them.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[RealClassifier RegSeqRat handoff]
\label{thm:real-classifier-regseqrat-handoff}

Thus the public real-classifier read is a finite route through regular rational data, not a direct equality test on terminal real seals.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $Q=(X,Y,S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y,W,D,C,E,H,K,P,N)$. The two seal rows $X$ and $Y$ expose regular rational readbacks only through $S_X,S_Y,R_X,R_Y$.

The common window $W$ supplies the shared finite requests, and the ledger $D$ records the dyadic tolerance data for those requests. The classifier row $C$ compares the two regular names through exactly those displayed rows.

The row $E$ records the regular-Cauchy equivalence boundary before any real-facing consumer reaches the terminal seals. Hence every $\RegSeqRatUp$-facing read factors through the displayed finite chain and cannot be replaced by a host equality test on $X$ and $Y$.
\end{proof}
