\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-regular-cauchy-interval-selector-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp$ packet names the finite interval selector surface used when a regular Cauchy rational name is read through nested located intervals. In Bishop-style constructive analysis, nested rational or located intervals are data that can be inspected at any requested finite precision; the limiting real is never obtained by selecting a quotient representative or invoking an ambient completeness oracle. The packet therefore sits between the finite observation surfaces of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}.

\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy interval selector carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
Q=(D,S,R,J,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ denominator and radius ledger naming the requested precision;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ schedule row that selects the finite observation windows inspected at that precision;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the selected rational observations;
\item $J$ is the finite $\LocatedIntervalUp$ nesting ledger whose cells contain the selected regular Cauchy observations and whose successive cells refine the previous visible cell;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal-facing row reached only after the selected schedule, regular-rational readback, and located-interval nesting ledger have been displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for denominator ledgers, schedules, regular-rational readbacks, located interval cells, the seal row, provenance, and local naming;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ replay routes from $D$ to $S$, from $S$ to $R$, from $R$ to $J$, and from $J$ to $E$;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\RealUp$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares packets only by these displayed rows and their componentwise transports. It has no coordinate for a selected completed real, quotient equality of Cauchy names, a detached interval limit, or an ambient completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy interval selector nesting extraction]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-nesting-extraction}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp$ packet
$$
Q=(D,S,R,J,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the finite nesting route extracted for a precision request is exactly
$$
D \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto J.
$$
The extracted route exposes the denominator and radius ledger, the selected $\StreamNameUp$ window, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and the $\LocatedIntervalUp$ nesting ledger, all transported by the displayed $\hsame$ rows and replayed by the displayed $\Cont$ rows. No extracted object is a quotient real, a chosen limit point, or a host interval.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp.RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorCarrier\_nesting\_extraction}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-carrier}. The first four rows are precisely the precision ledger $D$, the selected schedule $S$, the regular-rational readback $R$, and the located-interval nesting ledger $J$.

Read the route in dependency order. The schedule row is available only after the precision ledger is displayed, the regular-rational readback is available only through the selected schedule, and the nesting ledger records only the located interval cells reached from that readback.

Transport is componentwise. The rows in $H$ compare the four projected coordinates by $\hsame$, while the rows in $C$ replay the same finite route through $\Cont$. The package row $P$ and the local naming row $N$ record provenance for that route rather than supplying an additional interval object.

The excluded objects are absent from the carrier. Since $Q$ contains no quotient-real coordinate, no chosen limit point, and no host interval coordinate, the extraction can return only the displayed finite nesting route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy interval selector RealUp seal handoff]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-real-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
D \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto J \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N.
$$
Thus the consumer may read the dyadic precision ledger, selected stream window, regular-rational readback, located interval nesting ledger, terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, componentwise transports, continuation replays, package provenance, and local $\NameCert$ row. It receives no quotient equality of regular Cauchy names, selected completed real, detached interval limit, or ambient completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp.RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorCarrier\_real\_seal\_handoff}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the nesting extraction of \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-nesting-extraction}. It fixes the only route from a precision request to a displayed located-interval nesting ledger as $D \mapsto S \mapsto R \mapsto J$.

Now inspect the seal-facing coordinate of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-carrier}. The row $E$ is reachable only after the selected schedule, regular-rational readback, and located-interval nesting ledger have been displayed. Hence a $\RealUp$ consumer reaches $E$ through the same finite route.

The structural rows do not widen the route. The $\hsame$ rows in $H$ move the displayed coordinates componentwise, the $\Cont$ rows in $C$ replay the displayed route, the package row $P$ records provenance for that route, and $N$ names the local certificate surface.

Finally, the carrier has no field for quotient equality of regular Cauchy names, a selected completed real, a detached interval limit, or an ambient completeness principle. A $\RealUp$ consumer therefore receives only the displayed selector-to-seal handoff.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy interval selector four-source route locality]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-four-source-route-locality}

$$
Q=(D,S,R,J,E,H,C,P,N),

Every consumer of $Q$ factors through those rows together with the componentwise transport row $H$, the continuation replay row $C$, the provenance row $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp}$ row $N$. Consequently the packet cannot infer quotient equality of regular Cauchy names, a selected completed real, a detached interval limit, host real equality, or ambient completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-carrier}. Its visible read coordinates are $D,S,R,J,E$, and they are ordered by the selector route already fixed by \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-nesting-extraction} and \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-real-seal-handoff}.

Now inspect the structural rows. The row $H$ supplies only componentwise $\hsame$ transport for displayed coordinates, while $C$ replays the finite $\Cont$ route from the precision ledger to the seal-facing row. These rows can move along the displayed selector route, but they do not add a completion coordinate.

The remaining rows are bookkeeping rather than selection. The provenance row $P$ records the package sources, and the local row $N$ records the $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp}$ surface. Since the carrier definition contains no coordinate for quotient equality, selected limits, host real equality, or ambient completeness, every consumer remains local to the displayed four-source route.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy interval selector schedule exposure]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-schedule-exposure}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp$ packet
$$
Q=(D,S,R,J,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the selector exposes the dyadic precision ledger $D$ before the selected $\StreamNameUp$ schedule $S$. The schedule row $S$ is readable only together with the displayed $\hsame$ rows in $H$, the displayed $\Cont$ replay from $D$ to $S$ in $C$, the provenance row $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp}$ row $N$. No consumer may read $S$ as an ambient stream-choice oracle detached from the displayed precision ledger and certificate rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-carrier}. Its first coordinate is the dyadic denominator and radius ledger $D$, while its second coordinate is the selected stream schedule $S$.

The dependency order is part of the displayed carrier surface. Row $C$ contains the replay route from $D$ to $S$, row $H$ supplies the componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the same displayed coordinates, and the rows $P$ and $N$ bind provenance and local naming for the selector.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for a detached schedule oracle, the only readable schedule is the row $S$ reached through $D,H,C,P,N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy interval selector located nesting transport]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-located-nesting-transport}
Let
$$
Q=(D,S,R,J,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\RegularCauchyIntervalSelectorUp$ packet. Replacing the displayed located-interval ledger $J$ by a transported refinement $J'$ preserves the selector route only when the precision ledger $D$, selected schedule $S$, regular-rational readback $R$, seal row $E$, componentwise transports $H$, continuation rows $C$, provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$ are transported with it. A detached interval limit, quotient real, or unrecorded refinement cannot replace this route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Use \autoref{thm:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-nesting-extraction}. The nesting route visible to the selector is exactly $D \mapsto S \mapsto R \mapsto J$, so any change of the located ledger must be read as a change inside that finite route.

Now inspect the remaining carrier rows in \autoref{def:regular-cauchy-interval-selector-carrier}. The row $E$ is reached only after $J$, while $H$, $C$, $P$, and $N$ record the transports, replay routes, provenance, and local certificate that make the selected route readable.

Therefore a transported refinement $J'$ preserves the selector route only as part of the transported packet. The excluded detached limit, quotient real, and unrecorded refinement have no row in the carrier and cannot serve as replacements.
\end{proof}
