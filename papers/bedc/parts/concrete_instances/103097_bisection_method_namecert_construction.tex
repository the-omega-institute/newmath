\begin{definition}[BisectionMethod carrier]
\label{def:bisection-method-carrier}
A $\BisectionMethodUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
M=(I,D,L,W,T,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the located interval source row, exposing the endpoint names, locator reads, sign or bracket tests, and the initial tolerance request consumed by the method.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicBisectionUp$ branch ledger, recording the finite dyadic midpoint decisions and the branch labels inspected at each visible stage.
\item $L$ is the $\LocatedIntervalBisectionUp$ route, transporting the displayed branch decision to the retained child interval without adding a host-order trichotomy row.
\item $W$ is the retained $\NestedIntervalUp$ window row, listing the finite interval windows that remain available to downstream consumers.
\item $T$ is the finite termination and tolerance budget row, binding requested precision to a displayed bisection depth, diameter estimate, and ledger-exhaustion check.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row exposed from $W$ and $T$, with no completed infinite branch or global selector.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row obtained from the retained stream windows and their dyadic tolerance witnesses.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, reached only after $I,D,L,W,T,S,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for located interval source, dyadic branch ledger, located bisection route, retained nested windows, termination budget, stream windows, regular rational readback, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the method route
$$
I,D,L,W,T \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto E .

\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BisectionMethodUp$, $\DyadicBisectionUp$, $\LocatedIntervalBisectionUp$, $\NestedIntervalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BisectionMethodUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $I,D,L,W,T,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for a selected root, completed intersection, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, quotient equality of real names, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BisectionMethod NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bisection-method-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\BisectionMethodUp$ carrier
$$
M=(I,D,L,W,T,S,R,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BisectionMethodUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier exposure for the located interval source, dyadic branch ledger, located bisection route, retained nested window, finite termination budget, stream windows, regular rational readback, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local name;
\item classifier transport, requiring $H$ and $C$ to preserve the displayed method rows componentwise and to replay only the route shown by the carrier;
\item branch-retention stability, requiring every retained child interval in $W$ to be justified by the dyadic branch ledger $D$ and the located route $L$;
\item finite-window termination, requiring the budget row $T$ to bind each precision request to a displayed finite depth, diameter witness, and stream-window exposure;
\item real-seal nonescape, requiring $E$ to be read only after $S$ and $R$ have displayed finite windows and regular rational readback;
\item ledger exhaustion, requiring every exported branch decision, retained window, readback, and seal request to occur in $I,D,L,W,T,S,R,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{enumerate}
No BisectionMethod obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier exposure is projection from \autoref{def:bisection-method-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the source interval, branch ledger, located bisection route, retained windows, termination budget, finite stream windows, regular readback, terminal seal, and structural rows.

Classifier transport is forced by $H$ and $C$. These rows preserve and replay only the displayed coordinates, so an accepted comparison cannot introduce a second route to branch data, tolerance data, readback, or the Real-facing seal.

Branch-retention stability is read from $D,L,W$. A retained child interval appears in $W$ only after the dyadic branch ledger $D$ has recorded the visible decision and the located route $L$ has transported that decision to the displayed child interval.

Finite-window termination is internal to $T$. The row binds each request to a finite depth, diameter estimate, and ledger-exhaustion check before the stream window row $S$ is available to the regular rational readback $R$.

The real-seal clause follows because $E$ is downstream of $S$ and $R$. A Real-facing consumer must first consume the displayed finite windows and regular rational readback, so the seal cannot be reached through a hidden branch selector or completed intersection.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ only source and name the same packet. Since the carrier contains no excluded coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BisectionMethod Real seal route]
\label{thm:bisection-method-real-seal-route}
Every root-location or intermediate-value consumer of an accepted $\BisectionMethodUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
I,D,L,W,T \longmapsto S \longmapsto R \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N
$$

Consequently the consumer receives only the displayed located interval source, dyadic branch ledger, located bisection route, retained nested windows, finite termination budget, stream windows, regular rational readback, terminal real seal, componentwise transport, continuation replay, package provenance, and local naming data. It exports no selected root, completed intersection, trichotomy, open-cover compactness, quotient equality, countable-choice schedule, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier $M=(I,D,L,W,T,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The source interval coordinate is $I$, so every method request begins with displayed endpoint, locator, bracket, and tolerance data.

The dyadic and located bisection rows form the visible branch step. The ledger $D$ records the branch decision, and the route $L$ transports that decision to the retained interval window exposed by $W$.

The finite termination row $T$ supplies the quantitative stop condition. It records the requested precision, the finite depth, the diameter witness, and the ledger-exhaustion check before any stream-name window is exported.

The row $S$ is therefore downstream of $I,D,L,W,T$, and the row $R$ is downstream of $S$. The terminal real seal $E$ is read only after the regular rational readback has been exposed.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve this displayed route under componentwise transport, replay, provenance, and naming. Because no carrier coordinate stores a selected root, completed intersection, trichotomy, compactness principle, quotient equality, choice schedule, or ambient completeness witness, no such object can be exported by a consumer of the packet.
\end{proof}
