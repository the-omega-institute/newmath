\begin{definition}[Compact Cauchy equicontinuity carrier]
\label{def:compact-cauchy-equicontinuity-carrier}
A $\CompactCauchyEquicontinuityUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
E=(K,F,M,W,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $K$ is the $\CompactMetricUp$ source row, including the finite-net centers, cover radii, and source metric observations used by the packet.
\item $F$ is the finite $\ContinuousMapUp$ family row, with each inspected member routed through a displayed graph and pointwise-modulus packet.
\item $M$ is the pointwise family-modulus ledger, assigning only displayed center/tolerance requests to local source-radius rows.
\item $W$ is the finite window row for the compact-net centers and inspected family members.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger, carrying positive dyadic requests, slack comparisons, and radius refinements.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row that turns the displayed Cauchy/equicontinuity window into regular rational tolerance data.
\item $S$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row, available only after $K,F,M,W,D,R$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for compact source, family, pointwise modulus, windows, dyadic tolerance, regular readback, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for every compact-uniform-continuity consumer route through $K,F,M,W,D,R,S,H$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactCauchyEquicontinuityUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\ContinuousMapUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactCauchyEquicontinuityUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares packets only through $K,F,M,W,D,R,S,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, selected subcovers, selected limiting functions, quotient stream equality, countable choice, host equicontinuity, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact Cauchy equicontinuity finite route]
\label{thm:compact-cauchy-equicontinuity-finite-route}
For every accepted $\CompactCauchyEquicontinuityUp$ carrier $E=(K,F,M,W,D,R,S,H,C,P,N)$, any compact-uniform-continuity consumer that requests a later Cauchy tolerance must first read the finite route
$$
\CompactMetricUp,\quad \ContinuousMapUp,\quad \DyadicRatCoreUp,\quad \RegSeqRatUp,\quad \RealUp
$$
through the displayed rows $K,F,M,W,D,R,S$. The consumer receives a finite-net, pointwise-modulus, regular-tolerance, and real-seal handoff, and it receives no open-cover compactness, selected family limit, quotient stream equality, countable choice, host equicontinuity theorem, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the accepted carrier. The compact source row $K$ supplies only finite-net centers, cover radii, and source metric observations, while the family row $F$ supplies only the displayed $\ContinuousMapUp$ members available to the packet.

The equicontinuity request is then forced through $M,W,D$. The row $M$ records the local pointwise-radius ledger, $W$ restricts the request to displayed finite windows, and $D$ carries the dyadic tolerance and slack comparisons that refine the requested budget.

The Cauchy and Real-facing handoff is downstream. The row $R$ repacks the displayed window and dyadic tolerance as a $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback, and $S$ may be consumed only after that readback is present.

Finally, $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same route. Since no row stores open-cover compactness, a selected family limit, quotient stream equality, countable choice, host equicontinuity, or ambient real completeness, the finite route cannot export any of those objects.
\end{proof}
