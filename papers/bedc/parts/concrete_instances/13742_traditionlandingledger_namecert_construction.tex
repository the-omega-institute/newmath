\begin{theorem}[TraditionLandingLedger nonidentity]
\label{thm:tradition-landing-ledger-nonidentity}
No accepted $\TraditionLandingLedgerUp$ packet identifies a source tradition with its BEDC landing. Every accepted packet must display both the rejected-surplus row $R$ and the structural-distinction row $D$ before the landing row $T$ may be exported through the registry-facing provenance row $P$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let an accepted packet be
$$
L=(S,T,R,D,H,C,P,N).
$$
The landing row $T$ records where the structural reading lands; it is not a proof that the source tradition row $S$ is identical to the BEDC object named by $T$.

The rejected-surplus row $R$ records what is not imported, and the structural-distinction row $D$ records that the landing is a translation boundary. Removing either row leaves the carrier of \autoref{def:tradition-landing-ledger-carrier}, so the packet no longer distinguishes structural translation from doctrinal identity.

The support rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same separation through transport, replay, provenance, and local naming. Therefore a registry consumer may export the landing only as a finite structural-translation ledger, not as an identity claim.
\end{proof}
