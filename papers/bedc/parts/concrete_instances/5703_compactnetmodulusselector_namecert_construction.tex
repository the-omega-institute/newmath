The $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ interface is the concrete finite selector behind the compact-uniform-continuity construction of \autoref{ch:visions-compact-uniform-continuity}. It consumes the compact finite-net rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert}, the pointwise-modulus rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}, the metric-distance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the positive rational order rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, and the finite lower-bound fold surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitenetminimumfold-namecert}. Its output is the single finite packet a uniform-modulus consumer reads when a tolerance history is converted into a precision history by a compact net.

The selected precision row is assembled from sibling concrete packets: the finite cover and budget surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmoduluscover-namecert}, the fold output of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitenetminimumfold-namecert}, and the downstream precision packet read by \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformmodulus-namecert}. The selector therefore records a horizontal dependency route from compact cover data through a finite lower-bound fold to the uniform-modulus consumer boundary.

\begin{definition}[Compact net modulus selector carrier]
\label{def:compact-net-modulus-selector-carrier}
A $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
S=(X,Y,E,\mathcal{P},C,R,M,F,D,H,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the displayed compact metric source row, including the located finite-net coverage data for the requested tolerance history;
\item $Y$ is the displayed target metric row whose distance comparisons are consumed after the pointwise-modulus reads;
\item $E$ is the positive $\RatUp$ tolerance history requested by the uniform-modulus consumer;
\item $\mathcal{P}$ is the nonempty finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine of compact-net centers;
\item $C$ records bundled center histories and half-radius coverage rows for the source metric;
\item $R$ records the positive $\RatUp$ subordinate radius rows attached to the bundled centers;
\item $M$ records the $\ContinuousFunctionUp$ pointwise-modulus rows at the centers of $\mathcal{P}$ for the tolerance history $E$;
\item $F$ is the accepted $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$ lower-bound row over $\mathcal{P}$ and $R$;
\item $D$ is the selected positive precision history exported by the fold row $F$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric, compact-net, tolerance, radius, pointwise-modulus, fold, precision, route, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $Q$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which compact, continuous, metric, rational, fold, and uniform-modulus consumers read the packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactUp$, $\ContinuousFunctionUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RatUp$, $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local naming rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the compact-net input, pointwise-modulus radii, selected lower-bound fold row, tolerance-to-precision handoff, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming row. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, a host minimum, a choice-selected center, a quotient of point histories, or an external modulus function.
\end{definition}

\label{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-namecert-obligations}

The non-escape obligation is the carrier inventory together with the displayed routes. Since no excluded object is a coordinate of $S$, no local naming projection or continuation route can export one.
