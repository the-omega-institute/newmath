The $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ interface is the concrete finite selector behind the compact-uniform-continuity construction of \autoref{ch:visions-compact-uniform-continuity}. It consumes the compact finite-net rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compact-namecert}, the pointwise-modulus rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-continuous-namecert}, the metric-distance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the positive rational order rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rat-namecert}, and the finite lower-bound fold surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-finitenetminimumfold-namecert}. Its output is the single finite packet a uniform-modulus consumer reads when a tolerance history is converted into a precision history by a compact net.

\begin{definition}[Compact net modulus selector carrier]
\label{def:compact-net-modulus-selector-carrier}
A $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ carrier is a finite $\mathsf{BHist}$ packet
$$
S=(X,Y,E,\mathcal{P},C,R,M,F,D,H,Q,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the displayed compact metric source row, including the located finite-net coverage data for the requested tolerance history;
\item $Y$ is the displayed target metric row whose distance comparisons are consumed after the pointwise-modulus reads;
\item $E$ is the positive $\RatUp$ tolerance history requested by the uniform-modulus consumer;
\item $\mathcal{P}$ is the nonempty finite $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$ spine of compact-net centers;
\item $C$ records bundled center histories and half-radius coverage rows for the source metric;
\item $R$ records the positive $\RatUp$ subordinate radius rows attached to the bundled centers;
\item $M$ records the $\ContinuousFunctionUp$ pointwise-modulus rows at the centers of $\mathcal{P}$ for the tolerance history $E$;
\item $F$ is the accepted $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$ lower-bound row over $\mathcal{P}$ and $R$;
\item $D$ is the selected positive precision history exported by the fold row $F$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric, compact-net, tolerance, radius, pointwise-modulus, fold, precision, route, provenance, and naming rows;
\item $Q$ records the displayed $\Cont$ routes through which compact, continuous, metric, rational, fold, and uniform-modulus consumers read the packet;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CompactUp$, $\ContinuousFunctionUp$, $\MetricSpaceUp$, $\RatUp$, $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$, $\mathsf{ProbeBundle}$, $\mathsf{BHist}$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and local naming rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports only the compact-net input, pointwise-modulus radii, selected lower-bound fold row, tolerance-to-precision handoff, transport rows, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming row. It has no coordinate for open-cover compactness, a host minimum, a choice-selected center, a quotient of point histories, or an external modulus function.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Compact net modulus selector NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp}$ surface consists exactly of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the packet of \autoref{def:compact-net-modulus-selector-carrier};
\item compact-net exactness, requiring $X,\mathcal{P},C,R$ to display the located finite $\varepsilon$-net rows read from the compact source certificate;
\item pointwise-modulus exactness, requiring every row of $M$ to be attached to a bundled center and to the displayed tolerance history $E$;
\item lower-bound fold exactness, requiring $F$ to be the $\FiniteNetMinimumFoldUp$ row over the same $\mathcal{P}$ and $R$ and requiring $D$ to be its exported positive lower-bound history;
\item non-escape, requiring every consumer read of $D$ to pass through $X,Y,E,\mathcal{P},C,R,M,F,H,Q,P,N$ and forbidding open-cover compactness, host minima, choice-selected centers, quotient point equality, and external modulus functions.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:compact-net-modulus-selector-carrier}. The packet displays the source and target metric rows, tolerance history, finite probe spine, compact coverage rows, subordinate radius rows, pointwise-modulus rows, fold row, selected precision, transports, routes, provenance, and local name.

Compact-net exactness reads the finite coverage surface from $X,\mathcal{P},C,R$. The center and radius rows are not reconstructed by the selector; they are the displayed input rows supplied by the compact naming certificate.

Pointwise-modulus exactness then reads $M$ only at bundled centers. Each local row is tied to the same tolerance history $E$ and to the source and target metric rows already displayed in the carrier.

Lower-bound fold exactness reads $F$ over the same probe spine and radius ledger. The selected precision $D$ is accepted only as the positive lower-bound output of that finite fold.

The non-escape obligation is the carrier inventory together with the displayed routes. Since no excluded object is a coordinate of $S$, no local naming projection or continuation route can export one.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact net modulus selector fold soundness]
\label{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-fold-soundness}
Let
$$
S=(X,Y,E,\mathcal{P},C,R,M,F,D,H,Q,P,N)
$$
be an accepted $\CompactNetModulusSelectorUp$ carrier. If a source-distance read is certified below the selected precision history $D$, then the compact-continuous handoff for tolerance $E$ factors through one bundled center of $\mathcal{P}$, the corresponding pointwise-modulus row in $M$, and the same lower-bound fold certificate $F$.

The handoff uses the selected row only as a finite $\RatUp$ lower bound below every displayed subordinate radius row in $R$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Begin with the compact-net coordinate. The accepted carrier exposes $\mathcal{P}$ as a nonempty finite probe spine and $C$ as the coverage row assigning each source-point history to a bundled center with a displayed half-radius comparison.

Read the selected precision through $F$. By the lower-bound fold exactness obligation, $D$ is below every radius ledger in $R$ through the $\RatUp$ classifier and the stored transport rows in $H$.

Use the metric rows of $X$ to route a source-distance read below $D$ through the bundled center supplied by $C$. The comparison $D \le R_p$ keeps the second point inside the local ball named by the same probe.

Finally apply the corresponding pointwise-modulus row of $M$ and the target metric row $Y$. The output distance estimate is therefore read from $X,Y,E,\mathcal{P},C,R,M,F,D,H,Q,P,N$, not from an external modulus selector.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Compact net modulus selector independence boundary]
\label{thm:compact-net-modulus-selector-independence-boundary}

It is the joint packet that stores the compact-net input, pointwise-modulus radii, selected lower-bound fold row, and tolerance-only handoff together.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The compact sibling supplies finite-net coverage but has no pointwise-modulus row, no selected lower-bound fold output, and no target-metric handoff.

The continuous sibling supplies pointwise-modulus rows but has no compact probe spine and no finite lower-bound fold over a bundled radius ledger.

The metric and rational siblings supply distance and order readbacks only. They do not carry the compact coverage row, the bundled center ledger, or the tolerance-to-precision handoff.

The finite-net-minimum-fold sibling supplies the lower-bound fold but not the compact coverage input, pointwise-modulus rows, or target-metric consumer route. The uniform-modulus sibling reads the resulting precision packet, while this selector records the compact-net and pointwise-modulus source rows that justify that precision. Hence no bijection preserves the listed NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}
