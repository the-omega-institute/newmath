\begin{definition}[Interval finite cover certificate carrier]
\label{def:interval-finite-cover-certificate-carrier}

$$

$$

\begin{enumerate}
\item $I$ is the accepted $\ClosedBoundedIntervalUp$ row, including its endpoint-order and containment ledgers;
\item $M$ is the unary mesh scale naming the dyadic precision at which the cover is read;
\item $C$ is the finite rational cell ledger attached to $I$ at scale $M$;
\item $D$ is the finite $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ cell ledger refining the rational cells of $C$;
\item $Z$ is the displayed center row, with one rational or dyadic center for each accepted cell;
\item $F$ is the finite coverage row saying that every displayed interval containment read enters one of the cells in $C$ together with its dyadic refinement in $D$;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ window row used to read the endpoints, centers, and cell boundaries at the mesh scale;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row attached to the same windows;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $I,M,C,D,Z,F,S,R$ have been displayed;

Its public data are exactly the finite interval, mesh, cell, center, coverage, readback, seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

\begin{theorem}[Interval finite cover certificate NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:interval-finite-cover-certificate-namecert-obligations}

\begin{proof}

Read the analytic rows before the seal. The interval row $I$, mesh row $M$, cell rows $C,D$, center row $Z$, and coverage row $F$ are all exposed before the $\RealUp$ seal $E$, while $S$ and $R$ record the finite name windows and rational readbacks used by the same cells.

The remaining rows close the naming surface. The transport row $H$, replay row $K$, provenance row $P$, and local name $N$ preserve exactly this finite route, so there is no additional NameCert obligation outside the listed rows.
\end{proof}
