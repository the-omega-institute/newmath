\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\CauchyProductLimitUp$}

Following Bishop and Bridges, \emph{Constructive Analysis}, the $\CauchyProductLimitUp$ packet records the finite route by which two displayed regular Cauchy factor rows produce a regular Cauchy product and then a terminal real-name seal. It sits over the finite stream windows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic product-tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the product descent surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regularcauchyproduct-namecert}, and the real-name boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The chapter is the product analogue of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchy-sum-limit-namecert}: it packages a finite regular-Cauchy algebra route to a Real seal rather than quotient streams, selected product limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host multiplication of completed reals, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.

\begin{definition}[CauchyProductLimit carrier]
\label{def:cauchy-product-limit-carrier}
A $\CauchyProductLimitUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L_{\Pi}=(X,Y,S,Q,D,M,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the two regular Cauchy factor rows, each exposing only its displayed finite regularity surface.
\item $S$ is the shared $\StreamNameUp$ window row, listing the finite read windows opened for both factors and for the product-read request.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, recording the rational readbacks of both factors and the pointwise product readback obtained from the same shared window.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ product tolerance ledger, recording factor bounds, product-error splitting, dyadic multiplication comparisons, and the tolerance entries consumed by the product readback.
\item $M$ is the $\RegularCauchyProductUp$ descent row. It can be read only after $X,Y,S,Q,D$ have been displayed.
\item $A$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal for the product, available only after the regular product descent has reached the displayed readback boundary.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the factor rows, shared windows, regular readbacks, dyadic product ledger, product descent row, Real seal, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from any product-limit request through $X,Y,S,Q,D,M,A$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\CauchyProductLimitUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RegularCauchyProductUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductLimitUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,S,Q,D,M,A,H,C,P,N$. It has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected product limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host multiplication of completed reals, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyProductLimit NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-limit-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\CauchyProductLimitUp$ carrier
$$
L_{\Pi}=(X,Y,S,Q,D,M,A,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\CauchyProductLimitUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the two regular Cauchy factors, shared finite stream-window display, regular rational product readback, dyadic product-tolerance accounting, $\RegularCauchyProductUp$ descent, terminal $\RealUp$ seal routing, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, provenance, local naming, and non-escape from quotient streams, selected product limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host multiplication of completed reals, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:cauchy-product-limit-carrier}. The only factor data are $X$ and $Y$, and the only observation windows available to consumers are the finite shared windows recorded by $S$.

The readback stage is finite. The row $Q$ records the rational observations of both factors and the pointwise product readback obtained from those same windows, while $D$ records the dyadic factor bounds, product-error split, and multiplication comparisons that justify the requested product tolerance.

The descent stage is forced by row order. The row $M$ is downstream of $X,Y,S,Q,D$, so a product-limit consumer cannot read $\RegularCauchyProductUp$ evidence before the factor windows, product readback, and dyadic ledger have been displayed.

Finally $A,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name the same displayed route. Since the packet has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected product limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host multiplication of completed reals, or ambient Real completeness, none of those objects can be exported by the local certificate.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[CauchyProductLimit Real-seal route]
\label{thm:cauchy-product-limit-real-seal-route}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer read exported by an accepted $\CauchyProductLimitUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
X,Y \longmapsto S \longmapsto Q,D \longmapsto M \longmapsto A,H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the product limit is presented as a displayed regular-Cauchy algebra route to a Real seal, not as a quotient stream, selected product limit, countable-choice schedule, trichotomy principle, host multiplication of completed reals, or ambient completeness theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with an accepted packet $L_{\Pi}=(X,Y,S,Q,D,M,A,H,C,P,N)$. A consumer first reads the displayed factor rows $X$ and $Y$; no host sequence equality, quotient representative, or completed-real multiplication primitive is available at this stage.

Next the shared stream windows in $S$ determine the finite observations used for the request. The row $Q$ reads both factors on those windows and records the pointwise rational product, while $D$ supplies the dyadic factor bounds and product-tolerance ledger.

The regular product descent is $M$. Since $M$ is downstream of the factor windows, readbacks, and tolerance ledger, the terminal Real seal $A$ is reached only after the finite product route has already been displayed.

The transport and provenance rows preserve the same route under componentwise $\hsame$. The local naming row $N$ therefore exports the product-limit seal without adding quotient streams, selected limits, countable choice, trichotomy, host multiplication of completed reals, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}
