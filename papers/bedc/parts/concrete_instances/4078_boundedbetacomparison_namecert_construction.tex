\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BoundedBetaComparisonUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bounded-beta-comparison-namecert}
\origin{ai}

The $\BoundedBetaComparisonUp$ packet turns the normal-equality surface of
\autoref{ch:metacic-decidable-audit-map} into concrete finite BEDC data. It
sits next to the normal-form seal of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-normal-form-consistency-seal-namecert} and the
confluence audit packet of
\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metacic-confluence-audit-namecert}: all three
chapters name bounded or conditional MetaCIC evidence without promoting it to
an unrestricted closed-term theorem.

\begin{definition}[Bounded beta-comparison carrier]
\label{def:bounded-beta-comparison-carrier}
A $\BoundedBetaComparisonUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(f,t,u,n_t,n_u,e,w_t,w_u,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $f$ is the fuel row used by the bounded normalizer;
\item $t,u$ are the closed MetaCIC term rows being compared;
\item $n_t,n_u$ are the bounded-normal output rows computed from $t$ and $u$
at fuel $f$;
\item $e$ is the structural $\mathsf{Term.eq}$ comparison row applied to
$n_t$ and $n_u$;
\item $w_t,w_u$ are the finished-normalisation witness rows when the fuel is
known to finish on $t$ and $u$;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the fuel, term,
bounded-normal, equality, witness, provenance, and local naming rows;
\item $C$ records the displayed $\Cont$ route by which a consumer reads the
bounded comparison and any finished-normalisation boundary data;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over \texttt{NormalEqDecide},
\texttt{Term.eq}, closed MetaCIC syntax, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and
sibling $\NameCert$ rows;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BoundedBetaComparisonUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The local classifier compares accepted packets only through the displayed rows
$f,t,u,n_t,n_u,e,w_t,w_u,H,C,P,N$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded beta-comparison NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bounded-beta-comparison-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\BoundedBetaComparisonUp}$ surface for
\autoref{def:bounded-beta-comparison-carrier} consists of carrier admission,
bounded-normal output readback, structural $\mathsf{Term.eq}$ readback,
componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ route replay, provenance,
and non-escape from any fuel-erased beta-conversion theorem.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from the finite tuple
$B=(f,t,u,n_t,n_u,e,w_t,w_u,H,C,P,N)$. The fuel, closed term rows,
bounded-normal outputs, equality comparison, optional witness rows, transport,
route, provenance, and local naming row are all displayed coordinates.

The bounded-normal readback and $\mathsf{Term.eq}$ readback inspect only
$f,t,u,n_t,n_u,e$. Thus a successful comparison is a row about the displayed
bounded-normal outputs, not a row about unrestricted closed-term conversion.

The structural rows $H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite packet under transport,
consumer routing, provenance, and local naming. Since none of these rows
contains a fuel-erased normalisation theorem, the local NameCert exports no
stronger beta-conversion surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded beta-comparison finished soundness boundary]
\label{thm:bounded-beta-comparison-finished-soundness-boundary}
For an accepted $\BoundedBetaComparisonUp$ packet, a true bounded comparison
identifies only the displayed bounded-normal outputs $n_t$ and $n_u$. When the
finished-normalisation witness rows $w_t$ and $w_u$ are present, the same
packet also exposes the finished-normal-form boundary for those named outputs.
Without those witness rows, the packet exports no conclusion that $t$ and $u$
have fuel-independent normal forms.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the comparison row $e$ through the carrier. The row is the
$\mathsf{Term.eq}$ comparison of $n_t$ and $n_u$, so its positive content is
equality of the two bounded-normal outputs displayed at fuel $f$.

If $w_t$ and $w_u$ are present, they supply the extra endpoint data consumed by
the finished-normalisation soundness theorems named in
\autoref{ch:metacic-decidable-audit-map}. The boundary is therefore tied to
the particular fuel, terms, bounded outputs, and witness rows carried by the
packet.

If either witness row is absent, the carrier still records the bounded
comparison row, but it has no coordinate witnessing that the bounded outputs
are final normal forms. The consumer cannot erase the fuel or infer a global
normalisation endpoint from the packet alone.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Bounded beta-comparison non-escape]
\label{thm:bounded-beta-comparison-nonescape}
Every consumer of an accepted $\BoundedBetaComparisonUp$ packet is exhausted by
the carrier of \autoref{def:bounded-beta-comparison-carrier}, the local
NameCert obligations of
\autoref{thm:bounded-beta-comparison-namecert-obligations}, and the
finished-normal-form boundary of
\autoref{thm:bounded-beta-comparison-finished-soundness-boundary}. The packet
exports no global closed-term beta-conversion decider, no fuel-erased
normalisation theorem, no quotient equality, and no host conversion oracle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the displayed carrier rows. They provide exactly the fuel, two
closed terms, two bounded-normal outputs, one structural equality comparison,
optional finished-normalisation witnesses, and the structural BEDC rows.

Apply the NameCert obligations. They route every consumer through the same
bounded-normal and $\mathsf{Term.eq}$ rows before any comparison result is
read.

Finally apply the finished soundness boundary. The only strengthening available
from a true comparison comes from the explicit witness rows $w_t,w_u$ when
they are present. Since the packet has no coordinate for fuel erasure,
quotient equality, host conversion, or arbitrary closed-term normalisation,
none of those readings can be exported.
\end{proof}
