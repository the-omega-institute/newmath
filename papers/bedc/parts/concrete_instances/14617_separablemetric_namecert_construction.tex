\chapter{SeparableMetricUp NameCert Construction}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-separablemetric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, and the classical Bourbaki presentation of separable metric spaces, the $\SeparableMetricUp$ packet records countable density as displayed finite name data rather than as a choice principle over arbitrary balls. It sits beside the distance certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the real-facing seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the completion consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}. Its role is to expose the L10 countable-name surface consumed by completion and compact-metric chapters without importing selected dense points, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.

\begin{definition}[Separable metric carrier surface]
\label{def:separable-metric-carrier-surface}
A $\SeparableMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the underlying $\MetricUp$ carrier and its public distance-witness surface.
\item $A$ is the countable dense-name family, represented as a unary index schedule together with the finite rows naming the indexed dense candidates inspected by a request.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row for requests of the form ``indices below a displayed bound and tolerance below a displayed scale''.
\item $T$ is the tolerance ledger, read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows and through the $\RealUp$ comparison rows already exposed by the metric distance certificate.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row that packages the inspected finite windows and tolerances as regular rational observation data.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ distance-seal row reached only after the metric carrier, dense-name window, tolerance ledger, and regular rational handoff have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric carrier, dense-name family, finite windows, tolerance ledger, regular handoff, real seal, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay route from finite requests to dense-name windows, from windows to dyadic or real tolerance rows, and from those rows to the regular rational and distance-seal consumers.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SeparableMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SeparableMetricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier compares accepted separable-metric packets only through these rows. It contains no coordinate for choosing a dense point from every inhabited ball, no quotient equality for completion points, no open-cover compactness certificate, and no ambient Real-completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Separable metric NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:separable-metric-namecert-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\SeparableMetricUp$ carrier $S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SeparableMetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following six obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed metric carrier, countable dense-name family, finite request windows, tolerance ledger, regular rational handoff, real distance seal, transport, continuation, package provenance, and local naming rows;
\item metric compatibility, stating that all distance reads are inherited from the displayed $\MetricUp$ certificate $M$ and not from a second host metric;
\item countable-name visibility, stating that dense candidates are inspected only through the indexed family $A$ and the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$;
\item tolerance discipline, stating that every density request carries its tolerance through $T$ as a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ or $\RealUp$ row before any handoff is consumed;
\item regular handoff, stating that $R$ is obtained only from the displayed dense-name windows and tolerance rows;
\item nonescape, stating that $E,H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite dependency surface and export no choice-selected dense point, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeparableMetricUp.SeparableMetricTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:separable-metric-carrier-surface}. The tuple already lists the only coordinates available to the classifier: the metric certificate, the countable dense-name family, finite windows, tolerance ledger, regular rational handoff, real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Metric compatibility is inherited from $M$. A separability consumer may ask for distances from indexed candidates to a target row only through the public $\MetricUp$ distance-witness surface, so no second host metric or ambient equality test is introduced.

Countable-name visibility is the role of $A$ and $W$. The indexed family supplies candidates only when a finite request window is displayed, and $\hsame$ transport in $H$ preserves that same windowed family under classifier replacement.

The tolerance and regular faces are downstream from the same window. The ledger $T$ records the dyadic or real tolerance rows read by the request, and $R$ repacks exactly those finite observations as the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff.

Finally, continuation and package provenance do not add analytic strength. The rows $E,C,P,N$ only replay and name the displayed dependency chain, so selected dense points, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, and ambient Real completeness are outside the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separable metric dense-window stability]
\label{thm:separable-metric-dense-window-stability}
Let $S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparableMetricUp$ carrier. If two finite density requests read the same indexed dense-name window through $W$ and the same tolerance row through $T$, then every $\CompleteMetricUp$-facing or $\RealUp$-distance consumer receives the same displayed route
$$
A,W,T\longmapsto R\longmapsto E
$$
transported by $H$ and replayed by $C$ under the package provenance $P$ and the local naming row $N$. The stable window route contains no choice-selected dense point, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the common finite window. Since both requests expose the same indexed rows of $A$ through $W$, a consumer sees the same finite dense-name candidates before any distance or completion-facing read is available.

Read the tolerance ledger next. The shared row $T$ fixes the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ or $\RealUp$ tolerance data attached to that finite window, and the metric compatibility obligation keeps those reads inside the displayed $\MetricUp$ distance surface.

The regular handoff is therefore fixed. The row $R$ is built from the same dense-name window and tolerance ledger in both requests, and the terminal seal $E$ is downstream from $R$.

Transport and replay preserve the route rather than choosing a new candidate. The rows $H$ and $C$ carry the same finite dependency chain to $\CompleteMetricUp$ and $\RealUp$ consumers, while $P$ and $N$ record package provenance and local naming. Thus the stable route exports no selected dense point, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}
