\chapter{SeparableMetricUp NameCert Construction}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-separablemetric-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Bishop and Bridges, and the classical Bourbaki presentation of separable metric spaces, the $\SeparableMetricUp$ packet records countable density as displayed finite name data rather than as a choice principle over arbitrary balls. It sits beside the distance certificate of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-metric-namecert}, the stream-window surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational handoff of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance rows of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the real-facing seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the completion consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-completemetric-namecert}. Its role is to expose the L10 countable-name surface consumed by completion and compact-metric chapters without importing selected dense points, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.

\begin{definition}[Separable metric carrier surface]
\label{def:separable-metric-carrier-surface}
A $\SeparableMetricUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $M$ is the underlying $\MetricUp$ carrier and its public distance-witness surface.
\item $A$ is the countable dense-name family, represented as a unary index schedule together with the finite rows naming the indexed dense candidates inspected by a request.
\item $W$ is the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window row for requests of the form ``indices below a displayed bound and tolerance below a displayed scale''.
\item $T$ is the tolerance ledger, read through $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ rows and through the $\RealUp$ comparison rows already exposed by the metric distance certificate.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row that packages the inspected finite windows and tolerances as regular rational observation data.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ distance-seal row reached only after the metric carrier, dense-name window, tolerance ledger, and regular rational handoff have been displayed.
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the metric carrier, dense-name family, finite windows, tolerance ledger, regular handoff, real seal, continuation routes, package provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ replay route from finite requests to dense-name windows, from windows to dyadic or real tolerance rows, and from those rows to the regular rational and distance-seal consumers.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SeparableMetricUp$, $\MetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SeparableMetricUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier compares accepted separable-metric packets only through these rows. It contains no coordinate for choosing a dense point from every inhabited ball, no quotient equality for completion points, no open-cover compactness certificate, and no ambient Real-completeness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Separable metric NameCert obligation surface]
\label{thm:separable-metric-namecert-obligation-surface}
For every accepted $\SeparableMetricUp$ carrier $S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\SeparableMetricUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following six obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the displayed metric carrier, countable dense-name family, finite request windows, tolerance ledger, regular rational handoff, real distance seal, transport, continuation, package provenance, and local naming rows;
\item metric compatibility, stating that all distance reads are inherited from the displayed $\MetricUp$ certificate $M$ and not from a second host metric;
\item countable-name visibility, stating that dense candidates are inspected only through the indexed family $A$ and the finite $\StreamNameUp$ windows $W$;
\item tolerance discipline, stating that every density request carries its tolerance through $T$ as a $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ or $\RealUp$ row before any handoff is consumed;
\item regular handoff, stating that $R$ is obtained only from the displayed dense-name windows and tolerance rows;
\item nonescape, stating that $E,H,C,P,N$ preserve the same finite dependency surface and export no choice-selected dense point, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SeparableMetricUp.SeparableMetricTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier of \autoref{def:separable-metric-carrier-surface}. The tuple already lists the only coordinates available to the classifier: the metric certificate, the countable dense-name family, finite windows, tolerance ledger, regular rational handoff, real seal, transport, continuation, provenance, and local naming rows.

Metric compatibility is inherited from $M$. A separability consumer may ask for distances from indexed candidates to a target row only through the public $\MetricUp$ distance-witness surface, so no second host metric or ambient equality test is introduced.

Countable-name visibility is the role of $A$ and $W$. The indexed family supplies candidates only when a finite request window is displayed, and $\hsame$ transport in $H$ preserves that same windowed family under classifier replacement.

The tolerance and regular faces are downstream from the same window. The ledger $T$ records the dyadic or real tolerance rows read by the request, and $R$ repacks exactly those finite observations as the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff.

Finally, continuation and package provenance do not add analytic strength. The rows $E,C,P,N$ only replay and name the displayed dependency chain, so selected dense points, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, and ambient Real completeness are outside the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separable metric dense-window stability]
\label{thm:separable-metric-dense-window-stability}
Let $S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparableMetricUp$ carrier. If two finite density requests read the same indexed dense-name window through $W$ and the same tolerance row through $T$, then every $\CompleteMetricUp$-facing or $\RealUp$-distance consumer receives the same displayed route
$$
A,W,T\longmapsto R\longmapsto E
$$
transported by $H$ and replayed by $C$ under the package provenance $P$ and the local naming row $N$. The stable window route contains no choice-selected dense point, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the common finite window. Since both requests expose the same indexed rows of $A$ through $W$, a consumer sees the same finite dense-name candidates before any distance or completion-facing read is available.

Read the tolerance ledger next. The shared row $T$ fixes the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ or $\RealUp$ tolerance data attached to that finite window, and the metric compatibility obligation keeps those reads inside the displayed $\MetricUp$ distance surface.

The regular handoff is therefore fixed. The row $R$ is built from the same dense-name window and tolerance ledger in both requests, and the terminal seal $E$ is downstream from $R$.

Transport and replay preserve the route rather than choosing a new candidate. The rows $H$ and $C$ carry the same finite dependency chain to $\CompleteMetricUp$ and $\RealUp$ consumers, while $P$ and $N$ record package provenance and local naming. Thus the stable route exports no selected dense point, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, or ambient Real completeness.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separable metric countable-density carrier admission]
\label{thm:separablemetric-countable-density-carrier-admission}
Let $S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be the displayed $\SeparableMetricUp$ packet of \autoref{def:separable-metric-carrier-surface}. Carrier admission for $S$ is exactly the finite $\BHist$ admission of the $\MetricUp$ distance row $M$, the indexed dense-name row $A$, the $\StreamNameUp$ request window $W$, the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger $T$, the $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row $R$, the $\RealUp$ distance seal $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport $H$, $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and the local $\NameCert_{\SeparableMetricUp}$ naming row $N$. No admitted coordinate chooses a dense point from an arbitrary inhabited ball, identifies quotient-completion classes, or imports ambient real completeness.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier tuple from \autoref{def:separable-metric-carrier-surface}. Each admissible component is already named by a finite $\BHist$ row: the metric distance row, dense-name index row, window row, tolerance row, regular handoff, real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local certificate.

The admission test is therefore componentwise. The $\MetricUp$ row supplies the only distance surface, $A$ and $W$ supply the only countable-density observations, $T$ supplies the dyadic or real tolerance data, and $R$ and $E$ are downstream of those displayed rows.

Transport, continuation, provenance, and naming do not add a selection principle. The rows $H,C,P,N$ only preserve and package the finite route, so a selected dense point, quotient-completion equality, or ambient Real-completeness witness would require a coordinate not present in $S$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separable metric dense-window metric compatibility]
\label{thm:separablemetric-dense-window-metric-compatibility}
For an accepted $\SeparableMetricUp$ packet $S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, every finite density request that reads an indexed candidate through the $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$ must compute its distance comparison through the displayed $\MetricUp$ distance row $M$ and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ or $\RealUp$ tolerance row $T$ before it can be handed to $R$ or sealed by $E$. The $\hsame$ transport $H$, $\Cont$ replay $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance $P$, and $\NameCert_{\SeparableMetricUp}$ row $N$ preserve that route for later consumers.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with a finite density request. The only candidate names visible to the request are the indexed rows of $A$ admitted through the window $W$, so the request has no direct access to a host dense set or to a separately chosen point.

Metric compatibility is forced by the available distance row. Before a candidate can be compared with a tolerance, the request must pass through $M$ and then through the displayed tolerance ledger $T$, read as $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ or $\RealUp$ data.

Only after those rows are displayed can the request reach the regular handoff $R$ and the real-facing seal $E$. The rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, package, and locally name the same route, so the metric comparison cannot be replaced by a second metric or by quotient-completion equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separable metric regular-handoff obligation]
\label{thm:separablemetric-regular-handoff-obligation}
Let $S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparableMetricUp$ packet. The $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff row $R$ is obligated to use only the dense-name observations displayed by $A$ and $W$, the $\MetricUp$ distance row $M$, and the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ or $\RealUp$ tolerance rows in $T$. Its export to $E,H,C,P,N$ is a finite NameCert-local route, not a selected Cauchy subsequence, quotient-completion equality, open-cover compactness witness, or ambient completeness proof.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Read the handoff row in the order exposed by the carrier. The dense observations come from $A$ through the finite $\StreamNameUp$ window $W$, and all metric comparisons have already been routed through $M$ and the tolerance ledger $T$.

The row $R$ repackages exactly those observations as regular rational data. It has no independent coordinate for selecting a subsequence, choosing a dense point, or asserting equality of completion representatives.

The export surface is then the displayed seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows $E,H,C,P,N$. These rows make the handoff available to $\CompleteMetricUp$, $\RealUp$, and later NameCert consumers while preserving the finite packet boundary and refusing open-cover compactness or ambient Real-completeness data.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separable metric tolerance-net replay]
\label{thm:separable-metric-tolerance-net-replay}
Let $S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparableMetricUp$ carrier. Every finite density request whose indexed window is displayed by $W$ and whose tolerance is displayed by $T$ replays through the finite route
$$
M,A,W,T\longmapsto R\longmapsto E\longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
In particular, the request reads $\MetricUp$ distance data, $\StreamNameUp$ windows, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance rows, $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff data, and $\RealUp$ distance seals only through the carrier rows of $S$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier tuple of \autoref{def:separable-metric-carrier-surface}. The rows $M,A,W,T$ display the metric certificate, indexed dense-name family, finite request window, and tolerance ledger before any downstream handoff is available.

Use the NameCert obligation surface of \autoref{thm:separable-metric-namecert-obligation-surface}. Metric compatibility keeps all distance reads inside $M$, countable-name visibility keeps the request inside $A$ and $W$, and tolerance discipline keeps the precision data inside $T$.

The regular and real-facing rows are then forced. The row $R$ repacks the same inspected window and tolerance ledger as $\RegSeqRatUp$ data, while $E$ supplies the terminal $\RealUp$ seal.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name that route. No step introduces a selected dense point or a second tolerance witness outside the displayed packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separable metric completion-consumer handoff]
\label{thm:separable-metric-completion-consumer-handoff}
Let $S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparableMetricUp$ carrier. Every $\CompleteMetricUp$ consumer of $S$ receives only indexed dense-name windows, tolerance rows, regular handoff rows, and real distance seals displayed by
$$
A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N .
$$
The consumer receives no selected dense point, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness certificate, or ambient Real completeness principle.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the tuple $S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$. The only separability coordinates available to a completion consumer are the indexed dense-name family, the finite windows, the tolerance ledger, the regular handoff, and the real distance seal.

Apply \autoref{thm:separable-metric-namecert-obligation-surface}. Countable-name visibility prevents the consumer from replacing $A$ by a selector over balls, and tolerance discipline prevents the consumer from reading a precision datum before $T$ is displayed.

Apply \autoref{thm:separable-metric-dense-window-stability}. If two completion-facing reads share the same finite window and tolerance row, then the route through $R$ and $E$ is the same under $H$ and $C$.

Thus the $\CompleteMetricUp$ handoff is windowed and indexed. Since selected points, quotient completion equality, open-cover compactness, and ambient Real completeness are absent from the carrier, they cannot be exported to the consumer.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Separable metric RegSeqRat density handoff]
\label{thm:separable-metric-regseqrat-density-handoff}
Let $S=(M,A,W,T,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\SeparableMetricUp$ carrier. Every $\RegSeqRatUp$ handoff exported by the separability packet is downstream of the same countable dense-name family $A$ and the same tolerance ledger $T$ that feed the request window $W$. The handoff cannot be formed from a different dense family, an untracked tolerance, or an ambient real-completeness argument.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project $A,W,T,R$ from \autoref{def:separable-metric-carrier-surface}. The row $W$ displays the finite indices inspected from $A$, and the row $T$ displays the dyadic or real tolerance data attached to that same request.

Use \autoref{thm:separable-metric-namecert-obligation-surface}. The regular handoff obligation states that $R$ is obtained only from the displayed dense-name windows and tolerance rows, while the nonescape obligation keeps all downstream rows on the same finite dependency surface.

The transport and replay rows preserve the source of the handoff. The rows $H$ and $C$ cannot replace the dense-name family or tolerance ledger; they only carry the displayed $A,W,T,R,E$ route to later consumers.

Therefore every $\RegSeqRatUp$ density handoff is downstream of the same countable dense-name family and tolerance ledger. A different dense family, untracked tolerance, or ambient real-completeness argument has no carrier coordinate from which it could be read.
\end{proof}
