Following Weihrauch computable analysis, the $\EffectiveLimitOperatorUp$ packet records the represented-space limit operation as a finite BEDC handoff from Cauchy names to a sealed represented output. It sits over represented-space source rows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-representedspace-namecert}, Cauchy representation rows from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-cauchyrepresentation-namecert}, stream-window observations from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, regular rational readback from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, dyadic tolerance control from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, and the terminal real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet makes the limit operator visible as displayed finite convergence data, not as an ambient completeness principle.

\begin{definition}[EffectiveLimitOperator carrier]
\label{def:effective-limit-operator-carrier}
An $\EffectiveLimitOperatorUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
L=(X,A,S,M,R,D,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ is the represented-space source row, including the representation interface through which names are read.
\item $A$ is the $\CauchyRepresentationUp$ source row presenting the input name as a Cauchy object over $X$.
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ row of inspected name windows.
\item $M$ is the effective convergence and modulus ledger assigning each requested tolerance to a finite tail-window obligation.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the regular rational observations made through the windows in $S$.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance row used to compare the requested precision with the displayed tail budget.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after the represented source, Cauchy representation, stream windows, modulus ledger, regular readback, and dyadic tolerance rows have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the represented source, Cauchy source, stream windows, modulus ledger, readback, tolerance, seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a tolerance request through $A,S,M,R,D$ to the sealed output row $E$.
\item $P$ records $\Pkg$ provenance over $\EffectiveLimitOperatorUp$, $\RepresentedSpaceUp$, $\CauchyRepresentationUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveLimitOperatorUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no selected limit, quotient stream equality, countable-choice selector, host real equality, or ambient completeness row for $\RealUp$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[EffectiveLimitOperator NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:effective-limit-operator-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\EffectiveLimitOperatorUp$ carrier
$$
L=(X,A,S,M,R,D,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\EffectiveLimitOperatorUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item represented-source admission for the displayed $\RepresentedSpaceUp$ and $\CauchyRepresentationUp$ rows;
\item stream-window coverage, requiring every tolerance request admitted by $M$ to name a finite inspected window in $S$;
\item effective-modulus replay, requiring the convergence ledger $M$ to route each requested tolerance to the displayed regular rational readback $R$;
\item dyadic tolerance exactness, requiring every comparison used by the limit handoff to read exactly the row $D$ beside $R$;
\item real-seal handoff, requiring the $\RealUp$ seal $E$ to be consumed only after $X,A,S,M,R,D$ have been displayed;
\item structural stability through the componentwise $\hsame$, displayed $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and local $\NameCert$ rows;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding selected limits, quotient stream equality, countable choice, host real equality, and ambient $\RealUp$ completeness from the packet.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier in \autoref{def:effective-limit-operator-carrier}. The rows $X,A,S,M,R,D,E$ display the represented source, Cauchy presentation, stream windows, effective convergence ledger, regular rational readback, dyadic tolerance comparison, and terminal real seal.

A tolerance request first reads the ledger $M$. The ledger names a finite window in $S$, sends the observed entries through the regular readback row $R$, and compares the requested budget against the dyadic tolerance row $D$.

The seal row $E$ is therefore reachable only after this displayed route has been followed. The transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows $H,C,P,N$ attach the same route to classifier-stable BEDC data.

Since the carrier has no coordinate for a selected limit, quotient stream equality, countable-choice selector, host real equality, or ambient completeness of $\RealUp$, a consumer cannot obtain any of those objects from the local certificate surface.
\end{proof}
