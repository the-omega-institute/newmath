\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\KernelCategoryUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-kernelcategory-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\KernelCategoryUp$ packet records the categorical surface already present in the finite BEDC kernel. It uses unary $\BHist$ objects, displayed $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}$ hom rows, $\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}$ tail witnesses, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ composition routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and a local $\NameCert$ ledger. It is a concrete NameCert reading of the kernel-as-category vision, not a host category object and not a bridge to an external category library.

\begin{definition}[KernelCategory NameCert source]
\label{def:kernel-category-namecert-source}
A $\KernelCategoryUp$ source is a finite packet
$$
K=(O,H,R,I,M,T,F,A,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $O$ is the object row, accepting exactly the unary histories $a:\BHist$ with $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(a)$.
\item $H$ is the hom row, accepting triples $(a,b,f)$ exactly when $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)$ holds.
\item $R$ is the continuation-tail row, storing for every accepted hom row a displayed $\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b)$ whose tail is classified with $f$ by $\hsame$.
\item $I$ is the identity row, storing the empty-tail continuation route from each accepted object to itself.
\item $M$ is the composition row, storing the displayed $\Cont$ route that composes accepted hom histories $f:a\to b$ and $g:b\to c$ into an accepted hom history $fg:a\to c$.
\item $T$ is the transport row, storing object, endpoint, and hom-tail replacement only by componentwise $\hsame$.
\item $F$ is the $\FunctorUp$ provenance row for prefix functor reads over the same unary-continuation hom carrier.
\item $A$ is the $\NatTransUp$ provenance row for component-family reads over the same unary-continuation hom carrier.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ row naming the object, hom, route, identity, composition, functor, and natural-transformation ledgers.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\KernelCategoryUp}$ row for carrier admission, classifier stability, ledger exactness, and boundary evidence.
\end{enumerate}
The source is therefore a ten-row BEDC packet. Every row is a finite history, transport, route, provenance, or naming row already visible to $\CategoryUp$, $\FunctorUp$, and $\NatTransUp$ consumers.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[KernelCategory certificate obligation surface]
\label{thm:kernel-category-certificate-obligation-surface}
For a source packet $K=(O,H,R,I,M,T,F,A,P,N)$, the $\NameCert_{\KernelCategoryUp}$ obligation surface consists exactly of these rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for unary object histories and displayed $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}$ hom histories;
\item classifier laws for objects and hom histories, both read as componentwise $\hsame$ rows in $T$;
\item identity and binary-composition closure through the empty-tail row $I$ and the displayed $\Cont$ composition row $M$;
\item hom exactness through $R$, relating each $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)$ row to unary endpoints, a unary tail, and a $\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b)$ whose tail is $\hsame$ to $f$;
\item prefix-functor and natural-transformation provenance through $F$ and $A$, using the existing $\FunctorUp$ and $\NatTransUp$ certificate sites over the same $\CategoryUp$ carrier;
\item ledger exhaustiveness through $P$ and $N$.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation quantifies over an abstract source-equivalence relation, a host morphism type, or a separate category carrier.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier rows of \autoref{def:kernel-category-namecert-source}. The object and hom coordinates are already the concrete BEDC rows $O$ and $H$, so carrier admission is a finite history check.

The classifier part is also rowwise. Object replacement and hom replacement use the displayed $\hsame$ rows in $T$, with endpoint transport supplied by the unary-continuation stability package of \autoref{thm:category-unary-continuation-stability-law-package}.

Identity and composition are read from $I$ and $M$. The empty-tail identity row supplies identities, while $M$ records the displayed $\Cont$ route and the resulting accepted hom row.

The functor and natural-transformation coordinates are provenance rows, not extra structure. They point to the existing $\FunctorUp$ and $\NatTransUp$ certificate surfaces, both already built over the same unary-continuation $\CategoryUp$ carrier.

The local package row $P$ and naming ledger $N$ collect these visible obligations. Since every listed coordinate is one of $O,H,R,I,M,T,F,A,P,N$, the obligation surface is exhausted by the concrete BEDC packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KernelCategory unary-continuation hom exactness]
\label{thm:kernel-category-unary-continuation-hom-exactness}
Let $K=(O,H,R,I,M,T,F,A,P,N)$ be an accepted $\KernelCategoryUp$ source. For unary histories $a,b,f:\BHist$, an accepted hom row in $H$ from $a$ to $b$ with displayed history $f$ is exactly the BEDC unary-continuation row consisting of unary endpoints, unary displayed tail, and a continuation morphism $m:\mathsf{ContinuationMorphism}(a,b)$ with $\hsame(\mathsf{tail}(m),f)$.

The exactness is read through $H$, $R$, and $T$: $H$ supplies $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)$, $R$ supplies the continuation morphism and tail row, and $T$ supplies the required $\hsame$ transport. No source-equivalence transport or host arrow equality is used.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from an accepted hom row in $H$. By the carrier definition, this row is precisely a displayed $\mathsf{CategoryHomCarrier}(a,b,f)$ row.

Apply the continuation-tail correspondence of \autoref{thm:category-hom-carrier-continuation-morphism-tail-correspondence}. Its forward direction extracts unary endpoints, a unary displayed tail, and a continuation morphism whose tail is classified with $f$ by $\hsame$.

For the reverse direction, use the same correspondence and then apply the transport row $T$ when the displayed tail is replaced by an $\hsame$-classified tail. The resulting row is again accepted by $H$ because $H$ is the concrete hom-carrier coordinate of $K$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KernelCategory NameCert ledger exhaustion]
\label{thm:kernel-category-namecert-ledger-exhaustion}
Every consumer route exported by an accepted $\KernelCategoryUp$ packet factors through the finite ledger
$$
O \longmapsto H \longmapsto R \longmapsto I \longmapsto M \longmapsto T \longmapsto F \longmapsto A \longmapsto P \longmapsto N.
$$
Objects, homs, identity arrows, composites, functor reads, and natural-transformation reads are therefore exhausted by unary histories, displayed hom histories, continuation-tail rows, $\hsame$ transports, $\Cont$ routes, $\Pkg$ provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row. The packet exports no quotient of histories, no host equality of morphisms, no arbitrary category object, no limit structure, and no standard category bridge.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Inspect each exported operation. Object reads use $O$, hom reads use $H$ and $R$, identity reads use $I$, and composition reads use $M$ together with the transport row $T$.

Functor and natural-transformation reads are not independent exits from the packet. The rows $F$ and $A$ record the existing $\FunctorUp$ and $\NatTransUp$ provenance over the same unary-continuation hom carrier, so their consumer routes still pass through $O,H,R,I,M,T$ before reaching $P$ and $N$.

The package row $P$ and naming row $N$ are the final ledgers. Since the carrier has no coordinate for quotients, host morphism equality, arbitrary categories, limits, or an external bridge, no exported consumer route can obtain one of those objects from the packet.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[KernelCategory TasteGate boundary]
\label{thm:kernel-category-tastegate-boundary}
Any $\ChapterTasteGate$ inspection of a $\KernelCategoryUp$ packet is bounded by the same finite rows $O,H,R,I,M,T,F,A,P,N$. Conservativity can inspect only the carrier admission, hom exactness, continuation composition, $\FunctorUp$ provenance, $\NatTransUp$ provenance, and local $\NameCert$ ledger. Layer separation forbids the inspection from treating $\KernelCategoryUp$ as a host category, a mathlib bridge, a quotient of histories, or a source of categorical limits and adjunctions.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The inspection has a single visible carrier, namely the source packet of \autoref{def:kernel-category-namecert-source}. Thus every carrier event is one of the ten displayed rows.

Conservativity follows by replaying the certificate obligation surface of \autoref{thm:kernel-category-certificate-obligation-surface}. Hom exactness, identity, composition, functor provenance, and natural-transformation provenance are all read through rows already present in $K$.

Layer separation is the non-escape part of \autoref{thm:kernel-category-namecert-ledger-exhaustion}. The packet has no coordinate from which a host category object, quotient history, external bridge, limit, or adjunction can be projected.
\end{proof}
