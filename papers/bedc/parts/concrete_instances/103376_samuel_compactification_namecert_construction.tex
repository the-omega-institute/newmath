\begin{definition}[Samuel compactification carrier]
\label{def:samuel-compactification-carrier}
A $\SamuelCompactificationUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(U,B,D,K,W,R,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $U$ is the uniform-entourage source row. It records the finite entourage requests and refinement witnesses from which every compactification-facing read begins.
\item $B$ is the bounded uniformly continuous readout row. It records only displayed bounded readouts whose uniform continuity has been inspected through $U$.
\item $D$ is the dense uniform embedding row. It records the source embedding, dense image windows, and pullback evidence required before a compact boundary read can be exposed.
\item $K$ is the compact-metric boundary row. It records the compact metric recipient, boundary-window requests, and finite compactness witnesses consumed by compactification-facing observers.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window schedule through which dense-source observations, bounded readouts, and compact-boundary requests are inspected.
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ regular rational readback row attached to the displayed stream windows, bounded-readout budgets, and compact-boundary tolerances.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for uniform entourages, bounded readouts, dense embeddings, compact-boundary rows, stream windows, regular readbacks, replay data, provenance, and local naming.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
U,B,D,W \longmapsto R \longmapsto K .
$$
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\SamuelCompactificationUp$, $\SamuelCompletionUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\DenseUniformEmbeddingUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\SamuelCompactificationUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these ten rows. Its classifier compares accepted packets only through $U,B,D,K,W,R,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for a host compactification space, selected ultrafilters, quotient stream equality, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Samuel compactification NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:samuel-compactification-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:samuel-compactification-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\SamuelCompactificationUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the packet $S=(U,B,D,K,W,R,H,C,P,N)$;
\item bounded-readout stability, requiring every bounded uniformly continuous read to remain tied to the displayed entourage row $U$ and the displayed readout row $B$ under componentwise $\hsame$ transport;
\item dense-source handoff, requiring every compactification-facing consumer to pass through the dense uniform embedding row $D$ before reading a compact boundary;
\item compact-boundary sealing, requiring compact-boundary observations to be exposed only through $K$ after the stream-window schedule $W$ and regular rational readback row $R$ have been replayed;
\item ledger non-escape, excluding host compactification spaces, selected ultrafilters, quotient stream equality, countable-choice schedules, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No obligation is stored outside the displayed carrier rows.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:samuel-compactification-carrier}. The accepted packet displays exactly the uniform source, bounded readout, dense embedding, compact boundary, stream-window, regular readback, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.

Bounded-readout stability is componentwise. The row $H$ compares $U$ and $B$ together, so a readout cannot be transported without the entourage data that witnesses its uniform continuity.

Dense-source handoff is the placement of $D$ before the boundary row in every replay route. A compactification-facing consumer therefore reads the displayed dense embedding and its image-window evidence before it can request a compact boundary observation.

Compact-boundary sealing follows from the route $U,B,D,W \longmapsto R \longmapsto K$. Boundary observations are downstream of the finite windows and regular rational readback, so they cannot appear as an independent host compactification coordinate.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ name only the displayed rows. Since the excluded host-side principles have no carrier coordinate, no replay edge, and no provenance entry, the five obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Samuel compactification uniform handoff]
\label{thm:samuel-compactification-uniform-handoff}
Every compactification-facing read exported by an accepted $\SamuelCompactificationUp$ carrier factors through $\SamuelCompletionUp$, $\UniformCompletionUp$, $\DenseUniformEmbeddingUp$, $\CompactMetricUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$. More precisely, the read begins with the uniform-entourage source row $U$, inspects bounded uniformly continuous readouts through $B$, passes through the dense uniform embedding row $D$, replays finite windows through $W$, reads regular rational data through $R$, and only then reaches the compact-metric boundary row $K$ named by $P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start with an accepted carrier and project its displayed rows. The provenance row $P$ records the allowed sibling surfaces, namely the Samuel and uniform completion routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-samuel-completion-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-uniformcompletion-namecert}, the dense embedding route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dense-uniform-embedding-namecert}, the compact metric boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactmetric-namecert}, the stream-window and regular rational readback routes of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert} and \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the real-name seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}.

The replay row $C$ orders those surfaces. A compactification-facing read first consumes $U,B,D,W$, then reaches $R$, and only after that reaches $K$.

The transport row $H$ preserves the same order under $\hsame$, and $N$ names no row outside the displayed packet. Hence every exported read factors through the listed BEDC surfaces and cannot expose a host compactification space, selected ultrafilter, quotient stream equality, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{proof}
