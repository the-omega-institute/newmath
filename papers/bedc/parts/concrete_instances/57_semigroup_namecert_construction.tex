\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\SemigroupUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-semigroup-namecert}

The semigroup interface $\SemigroupUp$ adds associativity to $\MagmaUp$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-magma-namecert}). It sits between the magma layer and $\NameCert_{\MonoidUp}$ (\autoref{ch:concrete-instances-monoid-namecert}), exposing the closure law that distinguishes free semigroups from arbitrary binary structures.

\section{Carrier definition}
\label{sec:semigroup-carrier}

\begin{definition}[Concrete unary-history semigroup instance]
\label{def:concrete-unary-history-semigroup-instance}
The concrete unary-history semigroup instance reuses the magma carrier,
operation, and classifier from
\autoref{def:concrete-unary-history-magma-instance}. Thus the carrier is
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, multiplication is
$\mathsf{mul}_U(h,k):=\mathsf{append}(h,k)$, the continuation witness is
$\Cont(h,k,\mathsf{append}(h,k))$, and endpoint comparison is the
carrier-aware relation
$$
R_U(h,k):=\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(h)\land\mathsf{UnaryHistory}(k)\land\hsame(h,k).
$$
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Concrete unary-history semigroup laws]
\label{thm:concrete-unary-history-semigroup-laws}
The instance of \autoref{def:concrete-unary-history-semigroup-instance}
satisfies the magma closure and multiplication congruence laws of
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-history-magma-laws}. In addition, for unary
histories $h,k,l:\mathsf{BHist}$,
$$
R_U(\mathsf{mul}_U(\mathsf{mul}_U(h,k),l),
\mathsf{mul}_U(h,\mathsf{mul}_U(k,l))).
$$
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The closure and multiplication congruence clauses are the magma laws from
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-history-magma-laws}, applied to the same carrier
$\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$, operation $\mathsf{append}$, and classifier $R_U$.

For associativity, first apply unary append closure to $h$ and $k$, and again
to $k$ and $l$. These witnesses place the intermediate products
$\mathsf{append}(h,k)$ and $\mathsf{append}(k,l)$ in the carrier, so the two
outer products are also unary by the same closure theorem.

The four continuation witnesses
$\Cont(h,k,\mathsf{append}(h,k))$,
$\Cont(\mathsf{append}(h,k),l,\mathsf{append}(\mathsf{append}(h,k),l))$,
$\Cont(k,l,\mathsf{append}(k,l))$, and
$\Cont(h,\mathsf{append}(k,l),\mathsf{append}(h,\mathsf{append}(k,l)))$
are then fed to \texttt{BEDC.FKernel.Cont.cont\_assoc\_hsame}. The result is
the endpoint $\hsame$ proof between the two parenthesizations, and the carrier
witnesses already built above package it as $R_U$.
\end{proof}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.SemigroupUp.concrete\_unary\_history\_semigroup\_cont\_assoc\_classifier}

The ten-field per-object fit is: traditional target semigroup; BEDC source
$\mathsf{BHist}$ restricted to unary histories; carrier $\mathsf{UnaryHistory}$;
classifier carrier-aware $\hsame$ on unary endpoints; stability closure,
congruence, and associativity for unary append; ledger append provenance
through $\Cont$; core theorem
\autoref{thm:concrete-unary-history-semigroup-laws}; exactness endpoint
classification by $R_U$; standard bridge free unary append semigroup; status
$\mathsf{ProofTarget}$.
