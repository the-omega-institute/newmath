Following Bishop and Bridges' constructive Cauchy completion and the Bourbaki-Kelley uniform-space terminology, the $\RealUniformEmbeddingUp$ packet records the finite uniform embedding by which a regular Cauchy name enters the $\RealUp$ seal without becoming a quotient stream or a host real. It binds the finite window interface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the regular rational readback of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, the dyadic tolerance ledger of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicratcore-namecert}, the terminal seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, the dense uniform route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dense-uniform-embedding-namecert}, and the regular completion boundary of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-bishop-regular-cauchy-completion-namecert}. The packet is the Real and Completion handoff for finite regular Cauchy data; it does not assert ambient completeness of $\RealUp$, select representatives, or compare host real values.

\begin{definition}[Real uniform embedding carrier]
\label{def:real-uniform-embedding-carrier}
A $\RealUniformEmbeddingUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
R=(S,W,D,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the source regular Cauchy name row, read as a finite regular rational stream rather than as a quotient class.
\item $W$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window family recording the inspected prefixes and common refinements available to the embedding route.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger that names dyadic radii, tail budgets, and comparison obligations for the displayed windows.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row extracting regular rational sequence data from $S$ through $W$ and $D$.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached after the finite regular Cauchy data have passed through the dyadic and readback route.
\item $U$ is the uniform-control compatibility row stating that the source row, finite windows, dyadic tolerances, readback row, and Real seal are read over one displayed uniform embedding path.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for $S,W,D,Q,E,U$ and for the structural rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay from a Real or completion tolerance request through $W,D,Q,U$ and into the terminal $\RealUp$ seal row $E$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RealUniformEmbeddingUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\RealUp$, $\DenseUniformEmbeddingUp$, $\BishopRegularCauchyCompletionUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$ rows.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RealUniformEmbeddingUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate is exactly the assertion that these rows form one finite regular Cauchy uniform embedding packet. It contains no coordinate for quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected representatives, countable choice, ambient completeness, or a detached Real object.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Real uniform embedding NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:real-uniform-embedding-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RealUniformEmbeddingUp$ carrier $R=(S,W,D,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N)$, the local $\NameCert_{\RealUniformEmbeddingUp}$ surface consists exactly of the following finite obligation rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the source regular Cauchy row, StreamName window row, DyadicRatCore tolerance ledger, RegSeqRat readback row, Real seal row, uniform-control compatibility row, transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row;
\item uniform-window compatibility, requiring every inspected finite window in $W$ to be read over the same source row $S$ and dyadic tolerance ledger $D$ before it reaches the embedding route;
\item RegSeqRat readback stability, requiring $Q$ to preserve the regular rational reading of $S$ across the displayed windows and dyadic comparison budgets;
\item Real seal handoff, requiring every $\RealUp$ read to pass through $S,W,D,Q,U$ before the seal row $E$ is exposed;
\item classifier transport, requiring componentwise $\hsame$ transport in $H$ and displayed $\Cont$ replay in $C$ to preserve the same ordered route;
\item ledger non-escape, requiring $P$ and $N$ to export no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected representative, countable choice, ambient completeness, or detached Real carrier.
\end{enumerate}
No NameCert obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.RealUniformEmbeddingUp.RealUniformEmbeddingUp\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:real-uniform-embedding-carrier}. The tuple lists the source row, finite windows, dyadic tolerance ledger, regular rational readback, Real seal, compatibility row, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming.

Uniform-window compatibility is the row $U$ read together with $W$ and $D$. A finite window cannot enter the embedding route until it has been tied to the same source regular Cauchy row and the same dyadic comparison budget.

RegSeqRat readback stability is the row $Q$. It reads $S$ only through the displayed windows and tolerance ledger, so refinements of $W$ preserve the same regular rational data rather than replacing the source by a quotient representative.

The Real seal handoff is ordered by $C$. Replay sends a Real or completion request through $W,D,Q,U$ before exposing $E$, and $H$ transports only the displayed rows along the same route.

Finally, $P$ and $N$ package exactly the named dependencies and local certificate. Since the carrier contains no excluded quotient, host-equality, representative, choice, completeness, or detached-Real coordinate, the listed rows exhaust the NameCert surface.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Real uniform embedding seal handoff]
\label{thm:real-uniform-embedding-seal-handoff}
Every $\RealUp$ consumer of an accepted $\RealUniformEmbeddingUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
S \longmapsto W \longmapsto D \longmapsto Q \longmapsto U \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Consequently the consumer receives a uniformly controlled regular Cauchy entry into the Real seal, and receives no quotient stream equality, host real equality, selected representative, countable-choice schedule, ambient completeness theorem, or detached Real object.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Let $R=(S,W,D,Q,E,U,H,C,P,N)$ be accepted. Carrier projection gives the consumer only the displayed rows of \autoref{def:real-uniform-embedding-carrier}.

The route begins with the source regular Cauchy row $S$ and the finite windows $W$. The dyadic tolerance ledger $D$ fixes the comparison budget for each inspected window before any Real-facing read is available.

The readback row $Q$ then exposes regular rational data through the same window and tolerance route. The compatibility row $U$ binds that readback to the uniform-control surface used by dense uniform embedding and Bishop regular Cauchy completion consumers.

Only after those rows have been displayed does the replay row $C$ expose the Real seal row $E$. The transport row $H$, provenance row $P$, and local naming row $N$ preserve and name the same finite path.

There is no remaining carrier coordinate from which a consumer could project quotient stream equality, host real equality, a selected representative, countable choice, ambient completeness, or a detached Real object. Hence every Real consumer factors through the displayed route.
\end{proof}
