\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-closedboundedintervalnet-namecert}
\origin{human}

The $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$ packet names the finite dyadic mesh used by Bishop-style constructive compactness arguments for located closed bounded intervals. It consumes the endpoint and cell route of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-locatedinterval-namecert}, the dyadic refinement surface of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-dyadicinterval-namecert}, the rational cell carrier of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-rationalinterval-namecert}, the finite Real seal of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}, and the downstream compact-uniform-continuity consumer of \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-compactuniformcontinuity-namecert}. The construction is an explicit finite net: the interval is read through located endpoints, rational cells, dyadic cells, unary mesh scale, displayed centers, finite coverage rows, StreamName and RegSeqRat windows, RealUp seal rows, $\hsame$ transport, $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and a local NameCert row. It does not use open-cover compactness, choice of subcovers, quotient streams, host real equality, or an ambient completeness primitive.

\begin{definition}[Closed bounded interval net carrier]
\label{def:closed-bounded-interval-net-carrier}
A $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
K=(L,M,Q,D,Z,F,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $L$ is the located closed bounded interval row supplied by $\LocatedIntervalUp$, including lower and upper located endpoint windows;
\item $M$ is the unary mesh scale naming the dyadic precision used for the net;
\item $Q$ is the finite $\RationalIntervalUp$ cell ledger attached to $L$ at scale $M$;
\item $D$ is the finite $\DyadicIntervalUp$ refinement ledger for the rational cells of $Q$;
\item $Z$ is the $\ProbeBundle$ center-row ledger, with one displayed dyadic or rational center for each accepted cell;
\item $F$ is the finite coverage ledger saying that every displayed containment read of $L$ enters one of the cells in $Q$ and its attached dyadic refinement in $D$;
\item $S$ is the $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row used to read the endpoint and center names at the mesh scale;
\item $R$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row for the same endpoint and center windows;
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row reached only after $L,M,Q,D,Z,F,S,R$ are displayed;
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for endpoints, cells, dyadic refinements, centers, coverage rows, windows, readbacks, and the Real seal;
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ routes from the located interval row through mesh selection, finite cell coverage, StreamName windows, RegSeqRat readback, and RealUp sealing;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$, $\LocatedIntervalUp$, $\RationalIntervalUp$, $\DyadicIntervalUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\ProbeBundle$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier exports no open-cover object, selected finite subcover, quotient stream, host real equality, hidden center chooser, or ambient compactness principle.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed bounded interval net NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-bounded-interval-net-namecert-obligations}
For the carrier of \autoref{def:closed-bounded-interval-net-carrier}, the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for $K=(L,M,Q,D,Z,F,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$, located endpoint admission through $L$, unary mesh admission through $M$, rational cell admission through $Q$, dyadic refinement admission through $D$, ProbeBundle center-row admission through $Z$, finite coverage admission through $F$, StreamName and RegSeqRat readback through $S$ and $R$, RealUp sealing through $E$, componentwise $\hsame$ transport through $H$, displayed $\Cont$ replay through $C$, $\Pkg$ provenance through $P$, and local naming through $N$.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp.ClosedBoundedIntervalNetTasteGate\_single\_carrier\_alignment}
\begin{proof}
Project the carrier. Its first rows display a located interval, a unary dyadic mesh scale, rational cells, dyadic refinements, center rows, and finite coverage rows before any Real seal is exposed.

Read the analytic rows next. The $\StreamNameUp$ windows and $\RegSeqRatUp$ readbacks are attached to the same endpoint and center rows, and the $\RealUp$ seal is downstream of those finite windows rather than a completed host interval.

The structural rows close the certificate surface. The transport row $H$, replay row $C$, package row $P$, and local name $N$ preserve and name only the displayed mesh packet, so there is no further NameCert obligation outside the listed rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed bounded interval net dyadic coverage]
\label{thm:closed-bounded-interval-net-dyadic-coverage}
Every containment read of an accepted $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$ packet factors through the finite route
$$
L,M,Q,D,Z,F,H,C,P,N.
$$
The read first consumes the located endpoint row $L$, then the mesh scale $M$, then a displayed rational cell in $Q$, a displayed dyadic refinement in $D$, the corresponding center row in $Z$, and the coverage row $F$. It receives no open cover, subcover choice, hidden center selector, or host interval order outside that route.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp.ClosedBoundedIntervalNetCoverageRoute\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Begin with the located interval row. By the carrier definition, every containment read starts from $L$ and is admitted only at the unary mesh scale $M$.

The coverage row $F$ names a finite cell route. It sends the read into a rational cell from $Q$, the attached dyadic refinement from $D$, and the matching ProbeBundle center row from $Z$.

Finally the rows $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name this finite route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for an open cover, a chosen subcover, a hidden selector, or a host interval order, the dyadic coverage read cannot escape the displayed net.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Closed bounded interval net unary mesh monotonicity]
\label{thm:closed-bounded-interval-net-unary-mesh-monotonicity}
Let $K=(L,M,Q,D,Z,F,S,R,E,H,C,P,N)$ be an accepted $\ClosedBoundedIntervalNetUp$ packet, and let $M'$ be a unary mesh scale obtained by extending $M$ by displayed successor rows. The refined packet keeps the source row $L$ and replaces $Q,D,Z,F,S,R,E$ by rows at scale $M'$ so that every rational cell in $Q$ is subdivided by rational cells in the refined ledger, every dyadic cell in $D$ is subdivided by dyadic cells in the refined ledger, and every coverage route displayed by $F$ factors through the refined $\ProbeBundle$ center rows. Transport by $H$, replay by $C$, provenance by $P$, and local naming by $N$ preserve the same coverage route.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Start from the dyadic coverage theorem \autoref{thm:closed-bounded-interval-net-dyadic-coverage}. It places every containment read on the finite route $L,M,Q,D,Z,F,H,C,P,N$, so the mesh coordinate is the only row being extended.

The successor extension of $M$ refines the rational cell row $Q$ and the dyadic refinement row $D$ by finite subdivision. The center row $Z$ is then read from the refined cells, and the coverage row $F$ factors the old containment read through the new center ledger rather than through a different compactness object.

Finally apply the scope ledger of \autoref{def:closed-bounded-interval-net-scope-ledger}. The rows $S,R,E,H,C,P,N$ are downstream of the displayed mesh rows, so they replay the refined route and do not introduce an open-cover, quotient, hidden-choice, or ambient-completeness coordinate.
\end{proof}
