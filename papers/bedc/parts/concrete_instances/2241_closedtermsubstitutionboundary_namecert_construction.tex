The $\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$ packet records the finite naming surface by which MetaCIC closed terms pass through shift and substitution without exporting a free-variable leak. It packages $\mathsf{Term}$, $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$, $\mathsf{shift}$, $\mathsf{substitute}$, closedness ledgers, audit-visible non-escape rows, transport data, continuation routes, package provenance, and the local $\NameCert$ row. The packet is not a host compiler theorem and does not assert subject reduction beyond the displayed closed-term boundary.

\begin{definition}[Closed-term substitution boundary carrier]
\label{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-carrier}
A $\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
S=(T,V,d,C_{\mathsf{src}},C_{\mathsf{val}},Q,R,L,A,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following visible rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $T$ is the displayed $\mathsf{Term}$ row for the term being substituted into;
\item $V$ is the displayed $\mathsf{Term}$ row for the closed substituting value;
\item $d$ is the de Bruijn depth row at which the boundary is inspected;
\item $C_{\mathsf{src}}$ is the $\mathsf{ClosedAt}(d,T)$ source-closedness witness row;
\item $C_{\mathsf{val}}$ is the $\mathsf{ClosedAt}(0,V)$ value-closedness witness row;
\item $Q$ is the displayed $\mathsf{shift}$ boundary row recording that closed terms spend no future depth budget at the inspected boundary;
\item $R$ is the displayed $\mathsf{substitute}$ boundary row recording that substitution at a closed boundary returns the same closed source row;
\item $L$ is the finite closedness ledger recording which source, value, shift, and substitution rows have been read;
\item $A$ is the audit-visible non-escape row recording that no free variable, host equality witness, or untracked compiler state is exported by the packet;
\item $H$ stores componentwise $\hsame$ transports for the term rows, depth row, closedness rows, boundary-operation rows, ledger, audit row, package row, and local naming row;
\item $C$ stores the displayed $\Cont$ routes by which consumers read those finite rows;
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\mathsf{Term}$, $\mathsf{ClosedAt}$, $\mathsf{shift}$, $\mathsf{substitute}$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, $\Pkg$, and $\NameCert$;
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier contains no hidden typing derivation, no unrestricted compiler correctness theorem, no host substitution semantics, and no coordinate for an unregistered free variable.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Closed-term substitution boundary NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:closed-term-substitution-boundary-namecert-obligations}
The local $\NameCert_{\ClosedTermSubstitutionBoundaryUp}$ surface consists of five finite obligations:
\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission: accepted packets are exactly the finite $\BHist$ carriers of \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-carrier};
\item classifier stability: \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-classifier} is preserved by displayed $\hsame$ transports and $\Cont$ routes on the term rows, depth row, closedness witnesses, shift row, substitution row, ledger, audit row, package provenance, and local naming row;
\item shift boundary exactness: the $\mathsf{shift}$ row may be consumed only after the source closedness witness has been displayed, and it records that the closed boundary does not spend a future depth budget;
\item substitution boundary exactness: the $\mathsf{substitute}$ row may be consumed only after the source and value closedness witnesses have been displayed, and it records that substitution cannot manufacture a free-variable coordinate;
\item audit non-escape: the ledger and audit rows expose every closedness dependency required by the packet and export no unregistered free variable, hidden host equality, unchecked compiler state, or ambient subject-reduction theorem.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-carrier}. The tuple displays the term rows, depth row, closedness witnesses, operation rows, ledger, audit row, transport and continuation data, package provenance, and local naming certificate.

Classifier stability follows from \autoref{def:closed-term-substitution-boundary-classifier}. The classifier is defined only on the displayed rows, so componentwise $\hsame$ movement and recorded $\Cont$ reads cannot introduce a second substitution semantics or an untracked compiler state.

The shift and substitution obligations are the two operation rows $Q$ and $R$. Their prerequisites are recorded in $C_{\mathsf{src}}$ and $C_{\mathsf{val}}$, and the finite ledger $L$ records that those witnesses have been read before the operation boundary is consumed.

Finally the audit row $A$ fixes the non-escape claim. Since the carrier has no coordinate for a free-variable leak, hidden host equality, or ambient subject-reduction theorem, no consumer route can export such data from the local certificate.
\end{proof}
