\chapter{A Concrete Naming Certificate for $\BarRecursionUp$}
\label{ch:concrete-instances-bar-recursion-namecert}
\origin{human}

Following Spector and Troelstra--van Dalen, the $\BarRecursionUp$ packet records finite bar-recursive witnesses on the Real and Completion route. It reads the bar discipline of $\BrouwerBarInductionUp$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-brouwer-bar-induction-namecert}, the finite-cover boundary of $\FanTheoremUp$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-fantheorem-namecert}, the inspected stream windows of $\StreamNameUp$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-streamname-namecert}, the rational readback of $\RegSeqRatUp$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-regseqrat-namecert}, and the terminal seal of $\RealUp$ from \autoref{ch:concrete-instances-real-namecert}. The packet names finite witness production without adding countable choice, a selected infinite branch, quotient stream equality, host real equality, or ambient Real completeness.

\begin{definition}[BarRecursion carrier]
\label{def:bar-recursion-carrier}
A $\BarRecursionUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
B=(S,R,W,K,Q,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $S$ is the barred-spread source row, recording the $\BrouwerBarInductionUp$ or $\FanTheoremUp$ finite bar surface on which the recursion is allowed to descend.
\item $R$ is the recursion step ledger, recording the finite predecessor, stopping, and successor requests used by the bar-recursive witness.
\item $W$ is the finite branch window, exposing only the $\StreamNameUp$ prefixes inspected before the bar row accepts.
\item $K$ is the control-functional read, recording the finite query schedule by which the recursion asks for further prefix observations.
\item $Q$ is the $\RegSeqRatUp$ readback row, displaying the rational data produced by the accepted finite recursion route.
\item $E$ is the $\RealUp$ seal row, available only after the bar source, step ledger, branch window, control read, and rational readback have been accepted.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the barred-spread source, recursion ledger, finite window, control read, rational readback, Real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming rows.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay for all consumer reads of $S,R,W,K,Q,E,H,P,N$.
\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\BarRecursionUp$, $\BrouwerBarInductionUp$, $\FanTheoremUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\BarRecursionUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The classifier compares accepted carriers only by these rows, their componentwise $\hsame$ transports, their displayed $\Cont$ replays, their package provenance, and their local naming rows.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[BarRecursion NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:bar-recursion-namecert-obligations}
For an accepted $\BarRecursionUp$ carrier
$$
B=(S,R,W,K,Q,E,H,C,P,N),
$$
the local $\NameCert_{\BarRecursionUp}$ surface consists exactly of carrier admission for the displayed rows, barred-source exposure, recursion-step exactness, finite-window discipline, control-functional read discipline, rational readback, Real-seal handoff, componentwise $\hsame$ transport, displayed $\Cont$ replay, $\Pkg$ provenance, and local naming. No obligation is stored outside $S,R,W,K,Q,E,H,C,P,N$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Carrier admission is projection from \autoref{def:bar-recursion-carrier}. The accepted packet exposes the barred-spread source, recursion ledger, finite branch window, control-functional read, regular rational readback, Real seal, transport row, replay row, provenance row, and local naming row.

The barred-source and step obligations are read from $S$ and $R$. The source row fixes the finite bar surface supplied by $\BrouwerBarInductionUp$ or $\FanTheoremUp$, while the recursion ledger records only the predecessor, stopping, and successor requests displayed by that finite surface.

The witness-read obligations are then projected from $W,K,Q,E$. The branch window $W$ bounds the inspected stream prefixes, $K$ records the finite control queries, $Q$ records the regular rational readback, and $E$ is reachable only after those rows have been accepted.

Finally $H,C,P,N$ transport, replay, source, and name the same finite route. Since the carrier has no coordinate for countable choice, a selected infinite branch, quotient stream equality, host real equality, or ambient Real completeness, the listed rows are exactly the NameCert obligations.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[BarRecursion Real-completion non-escape]
\label{thm:bar-recursion-real-completion-nonescape}
Every $\RealUp$ or Completion-facing consumer read of an accepted $\BarRecursionUp$ carrier factors through the finite route
$$
S,R,W,K,Q \longmapsto E \longmapsto H,C,P,N .
$$
Thus the consumer may read the barred source, recursion step ledger, finite branch window, control-functional schedule, regular rational readback, terminal Real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming rows, but it cannot read countable choice, a selected infinite branch, quotient stream equality, host real equality, or ambient Real completeness from the $\BarRecursionUp$ packet.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Open the carrier of \autoref{def:bar-recursion-carrier}. A Real-facing read has access only to the displayed rows $S,R,W,K,Q,E,H,C,P,N$, and the terminal seal row $E$ is placed after the barred source, recursion ledger, window, control read, and rational readback.

Apply the NameCert obligations of \autoref{thm:bar-recursion-namecert-obligations}. They force consumer reads to respect the same row order: the recursion step ledger is tied to the barred source, the branch window bounds the inspected prefixes, and the rational readback is produced before the Real seal can be exposed.

Transport and replay do not add new objects. The rows $H$ and $C$ preserve the displayed finite route, while $P$ and $N$ only source and name it. Hence every Real or Completion consumer factors through the stated finite prefix and the refused ambient objects remain outside the public surface.
\end{proof}
