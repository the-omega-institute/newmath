\begin{definition}[Regular Cauchy tail-comparison carrier]
\label{def:regular-cauchy-tail-comparison-carrier}
A $\RegularCauchyTailComparisonUp$ carrier is a finite $\BHist$ packet
$$
T=(X,Y,W,D,I,Z,E,H,C,P,N)
$$
with the following displayed rows:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $X$ and $Y$ are the two regular Cauchy source rows. Each is read only through its displayed $\RegSeqRatUp$ surface before a real seal or comparison answer is available.
\item $W$ is the common $\StreamNameUp$ finite-window row. It records the shared late cut, inspected indices, synchronized endpoint windows, and comparison requests used for both sources.
\item $D$ is the $\DyadicRatCoreUp$ tolerance ledger. It records the requested equality or endpoint-comparison radius, comparison slack, dyadic enclosure budget, and exhausted finite windows.
\item $I$ is the $\CauchyReindexingUp$ route row. It records the finite reindexing handoff that places the $X$ and $Y$ observations in the same route order before any comparison read is exported.
\item $Z$ is the $\NullSequenceUp$ zero-tail handoff row. It is present exactly when an equality request is being read, and it records the displayed tail-difference route used to certify that the two late windows collapse to a null sequence.
\item $E$ is the terminal $\RealUp$ seal boundary for the comparison read. It becomes available only after $X,Y,W,D,I$ and, for equality reads, $Z$ have been displayed.
\item $H$ records componentwise $\hsame$ transport for the two source rows, shared window row, dyadic ledger, reindexing route, zero-tail row, real seal, replay, provenance, and local naming row.
\item $C$ records displayed $\Cont$ replay of the route
$$
X,Y,W,D \longmapsto I \longmapsto Z \longmapsto E .
$$

\item $P$ is the $\Pkg$ provenance over $\RegularCauchyTailComparisonUp$, $\RegSeqRatUp$, $\StreamNameUp$, $\DyadicRatCoreUp$, $\CauchyReindexingUp$, $\NullSequenceUp$, $\RealUp$, $\BHist$, $\hsame$, $\Cont$, and $\NameCert$.
\item $N$ is the local $\NameCert_{\RegularCauchyTailComparisonUp}$ row.
\end{enumerate}
The carrier predicate admits exactly these eleven rows. Its classifier compares accepted packets only through $X,Y,W,D,I,Z,E,H,C,P,N$ and has no coordinate for quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, or bridge checking.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Regular Cauchy tail-comparison NameCert obligations]
\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-comparison-namecert-obligations}
For every accepted $\RegularCauchyTailComparisonUp$ carrier
$$
T=(X,Y,W,D,I,Z,E,H,C,P,N),

\begin{enumerate}
\item carrier admission for the two regular Cauchy source rows, common stream-window row, dyadic tolerance ledger, reindexing route, zero-tail handoff row, terminal real seal, transport, replay, provenance, and local naming row;
\item common-window discipline, requiring both source rows to be read through the same $W$ row before either equality or endpoint-comparison evidence is exported;
\item dyadic tolerance exactness, requiring $D$ to provide the requested radius, slack, enclosure budget, and finite-window exhaustion consumed by the comparison read;
\item route-order discipline, requiring $I$ to align the displayed $X$ and $Y$ observations before any $\RealUp$ seal boundary or equality branch can be read;
\item zero-tail confinement, requiring $Z$ to appear only for equality requests and to record the displayed $\NullSequenceUp$ handoff rather than quotient stream equality;
\item seal-boundary discipline, requiring $E$ to remain downstream of $X,Y,W,D,I$ and the equality branch $Z$ when that branch is requested;
\item transport, replay, provenance, and naming stability, requiring $H,C,P,N$ to preserve and source exactly the displayed rows componentwise;
\item non-escape, excluding quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, ambient $\RealUp$ completeness, Lean verification, and bridge checking.
\end{enumerate}
No regular Cauchy tail-comparison obligation is stored outside these rows.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Project the accepted packet $T=(X,Y,W,D,I,Z,E,H,C,P,N)$. The carrier exposes exactly the two source rows, one shared finite-window row, the dyadic tolerance ledger, the route-order handoff, the zero-tail branch, the terminal real seal, and the structural rows.

The comparison route starts with a common window. The row $W$ fixes the late cut and inspected indices for both $X$ and $Y$, so a comparison read cannot introduce two unrelated StreamName schedules.

The dyadic and route-order data are then forced. The row $D$ supplies the precision budget read by the comparison, and $I$ reorders the displayed observations into the common Cauchy route before the seal row can be reached.

For equality reads, the only equality-specific coordinate is $Z$. It records a $\NullSequenceUp$ zero-tail handoff from the displayed late-window differences; for endpoint comparisons, the same carrier exposes the empty equality branch and therefore no quotient stream-equality coordinate.

Finally $E,H,C,P,N$ seal, transport, replay, source, and name the same finite rows. Since none of the excluded host objects has a carrier coordinate, replay edge, provenance entry, or local naming row, the listed obligations exhaust the local NameCert surface.

\label{thm:regular-cauchy-tail-comparison-common-window-handoff}

\begin{proof}

Because there is no coordinate outside this route, the consumer cannot obtain quotient stream equality, selected limits, host real equality, trichotomy, countable choice, or ambient $\RealUp$ completeness from the packet.
