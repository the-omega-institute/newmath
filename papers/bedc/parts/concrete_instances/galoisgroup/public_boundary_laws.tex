\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp action ledger exactness]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-action-ledger-exactness}
For an automorphism-action packet $(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}, the public action
ledger is exactly the fixed-base row, identity row, composition row, inverse
row, classifier row, dependency projections to $E$ and $G$, finite
$\mathsf{Cont}$ readbacks, and shared $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance. No host
automorphism function space, quotient of automorphisms, or uncarried equality
of field presentations is exported.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet along
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}. The projection
exposes only $E$, $G$, $b$, $a$, $m$, $i$, $c$, $\rho$, and $\lambda$ together
with the dependency rows recorded inside the two consumed NameCert packets.

The Galois-extension public boundary
\autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary} accounts for all extension
carrier, classifier, exactness, and ledger rows from $E$. The fixed-base carrier
obligation \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-fixed-base-carrier-obligation} accounts for
the base-fixing face of the action ledger.

The identity row is the unit action row supplied above. The composition and
inverse faces are exactly the rows controlled by
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-closure},
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-inverse-closure}, and
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-and-inverse-obligation}. These references
exhaust the accepted action, association, and reversal data available in the
finite packet.

Conversely, every listed row is a displayed packet row or a dependency
projection already carried by $\lambda$ and tagged by $\rho$. Repacking those
rows gives the same public $\GaloisGroupUp$ automorphism-action packet, so the
ledger is exact and has no additional ambient automorphism object behind it.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[GaloisGroupUp public NameCert obligation boundary]
\label{thm:galoisgroup-public-namecert-obligation-boundary}
For an automorphism-action packet $(E,G,b,a,m,i,c,\rho,\lambda)$ of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet}, the public
$\NameCert_{\GaloisGroupUp}$ obligation surface is exactly the following
finite row family: the Galois-extension dependency packet $E$, the group
dependency packet $G$, the fixed-base row $b$, the automorphism-action row
$a$, the identity row read from the unit of $G$, the composition row $m$, the
inverse row $i$, the classifier row $c$, the $\mathsf{Pkg}$ provenance row
$\rho$, the finite $\mathsf{Cont}$ ledger $\lambda$, and the exactness rows
named by those packets. This is the public
obligation boundary consumed by downstream Galois and representation-facing
chapters.
\end{theorem}
\leanchecked{BEDC.Derived.GaloisGroupUp.GaloisGroupAutomorphismActionPacket\_semantic\_name\_certificate}
\begin{proof}
Project the packet to its dependency surfaces. The Galois-extension side is
the public boundary of
\autoref{thm:galoisext-public-obligation-boundary}; the group side is the
operation and inverse surface named by the $\GroupUp$ certificate. These two
dependency projections account for the extension carrier, group operation,
classifier, stability, ledger, and exactness rows available to the
Galois-group packet.

The fixed-base carrier obligation is supplied by
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-fixed-base-carrier-obligation}. The identity row is
supplied by \autoref{thm:galoisgroup-identity-row-closure}; classifier-stable
composition is supplied by
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-classifier-congruence}; and the
composition and inverse obligation is supplied by
\autoref{thm:galoisgroup-composition-and-inverse-obligation}. Therefore every
public row is a projection of $E$, $G$, $b$, $a$, the identity row, $m$, $i$,
$c$, $\rho$, or $\lambda$.

Conversely, each listed row is either a coordinate of
\autoref{def:galoisgroup-bhist-automorphism-action-packet} or a projected row
read from those coordinates. Repacking those rows gives the carrier, identity,
operation, inverse, classifier, provenance, ledger, and exactness surface needed
by downstream Galois and representation-facing uses, so no hidden host
automorphism datum is part of the public boundary.
\end{proof}
